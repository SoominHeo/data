The White House is finest prison in the world: President Harry Truman
Harry Truman was the 33rd U.S. president and was in office from 1945 to 1953. He rose to the presidency when his predecessor Franklin Roosevelt died in 1945. President Harry Truman left many remarks about how tough a president’s life is. Now, let’s take a look at some of such words he said to the public in separate occasions. - Ed.
 Being a president is like a tiger. A man has to keep on riding or he is swallowed.
  A president either is constantly on top of events or, if he hesitates, events will soon be on top of him.
  I would rather have peace in the world than be president.
  A president cannot always be popular.
  The White House is the finest prison in the world.
  The president is always abused. If he isn’t, he isn’t doing anything.
