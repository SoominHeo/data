Fans pick Lee Chung-yong top Korean footballer of 2013
Bolton Wanderers’ midfielder Lee Chung-yong was voted the top South Korean footballer of 2013 in a national survey released on Dec. 26, the Korea Football Association said.
 According to the association, Lee garnered 1,369 out of 2,776 votes cast by fans in the annual survey.
 Lee Chung-yong started playing soccer at the age of 11 and his international career began as part of the South Korea national under-20 football team in the 2007 FIFA U20 World Cup. He was also part of the 2008 Beijing Olympics team. 
 The 25-year-old joined Premier League side Bolton Wanderers in July 2009 and at the end of his first season, he won a series of awards including Bolton’s Player of the Year, Players’ Player of the Year, and best newcomer awards. 
 Though Lee suffered a broken leg during pre-season friendly against Newport County in July 2011, he managed to make a successful comeback.
 Lee was at the center of the Korean National Soccer Team campaign to qualify for the 2014 FIFA World Cup. He will be representing Korea in Brazil, where the country will face soccer strongholds Belgium, Russia and Algeria in the group stage.
 Others who made the list include Son Heung-min, a forward for Bayer Leverkusen in the Bundesliga, with 810 votes, and Kim Shin-wook, the reigning MVP in the domestic K League, earning 302 votes. Kim scored 19 goals in 36 league matches last season.
