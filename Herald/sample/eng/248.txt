Cable TV operators cut relaying terrestrial programs
Local TV broadcasters KBS2, MBC, and SBS cut high-definition (HD) programs to households nationwide after negotiations over the cost of digital broadcasting retransmissions failed on Nov. 28.
 The three regular TV operators replaced their HD programs with analog programs with less high-quality visuals. The incident brought about inconvenience in every corner of the country as there are approximately 4 million digital cable TV subscribers.
 Cable TV system operators and three major broadcasting corporations have been at odds over the cost of high-definition broadcasts of terrestrial TV programs in Korea.
