U.S. beats record for natural disasters
This year, the U.S. broke the record for billion-dollar weather disasters, as at least a dozen catastrophic incidents and counting have hit the nation, AP reports.
According to AP, the U.S. has seen more deadly weather disasters in 2011 than the entirety of the 1980s combined.
According to the National Weather Service Director, Jack Hayes, the extreme weather in the U.S. this year has killed more than 1,000 people, adding up to $52 billion in damages.
The previous record for billion-dollar disasters was nine in 2008.
