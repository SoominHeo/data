South Korean leads governing board of U.N. Women
Kim Sook, the South Korean ambassador to the United Nations, was elected president of the 41-nation governing body for U.N. Women, Yonhap News reported. 
U.N. Women, officially known as the U.N. Entity for Gender Equality and the Empowerment of Women, was formed last year to improve the lives of women and girls around the world.
 The group is currently headed by former Chilean president Michelle Bachelet. Upon hearing the news, Bachelet released a speech stating, “I am very much looking forward to continuing the excellent collaboration between U.N. Women and the board under your[Kim’s] leadership."
