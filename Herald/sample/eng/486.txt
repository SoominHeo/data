HBO cancels new series after horse death
American premium cable television network HBO abruptly canceled its new drama series “Luck” after news that a third horse had died during production on March 14. 
“Luck”, a horse racing series starring Dustin Hoffman, was in the midst of filming its second season when the incident occurred. According to TMZ, crew members were trying to get a horse out of its stable when “it reared and fell back, suffering a head injury.” The horse was euthanized and without skipping a beat, the crew continued shooting. 
The first season finale was broadcast on Mar. 25.
