Legal services to solve dispute between teachers and students, parents
The government said on April 10 that it will provide legal services to school teachers involved in disputes with their students or parents as part of its fight to reduce school violence, Yonhap News reported.
 At a weekly Cabinet meeting, the administration announced that it would hire a full-time lawyer and form a legal support group in every municipal and provincial education office to give legal advice to teachers.
 These days, bullying has emerged as a major social problem in the nation. More than 12 percent of children in grades four through 12 were reported to have experienced school abuse by their peers, including verbal threats, beatings and robbery.
