‘Where the Wild Things Are’ author, Maurice Sendak dies at 83
Maurice Sendak, author of the classic children’s book, “Where the Wild Things Are,” died on May 8 in Danbury, Connecticut. He was 83.
 Sendak was widely considered one of the most important children’s book artists of the 20th century. 
Known for more than a dozen picture books that he wrote and illustrated himself, Sendak’s work was the subject of critical studies as well as major exhibitions. 
He gained international acclaim after writing and illustrating his most famous book, “Where the Wild Things Are,” which was first published in 1963. A feature film version of the book was released in 2009.
