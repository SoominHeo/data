Korea becoming medical-tourism hub
The number of people visiting Korea for medical purposes has risen steeply, a report by the Ministry of Health and Welfare noted.
 According to the ministry, about 122,000 foreign patients received treatment in Korean hospitals in 2011, a whopping 49 percent increase from the previous year. During that period, hospitals saw their earnings surge by more than 75 percent to over 180 billion won. 
 U.S. citizens made up the largest portion of patients with 27 percent, followed by Japanese, Chinese and Russian patients. Among the patients, 15 percent received internal treatment while 13 percent of patients had dermatological care or plastic surgeries, added the report.
