Korean adoptee named digital economy minister of France
South Korean adoptee Fleur Pellerin was appointed the minister of industry, energy and digital economy under France’s newly inaugurated Francois Hollande government, AP reported on May 16.
 With her appointment, the 38-year-old became the first Korean to reach a ministerial post in European nations. 
 Pellerin was born in Korea in 1973 but was adopted by a French family when she was six months old. She graduated from the Paris Institute of Political Studies, one of France’s most prestigious schools. Previously, she served as a digital economy adviser at the Hollande camp of the Socialist Party, the report said.
