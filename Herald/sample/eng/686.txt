Seoul hit by prolonged drought
Seoul is struggling to cope with its worst drought in a century, according to the Seoul Metropolitan Government.
 Between May 1 and June 19 this year, the amount of rainfall in Seoul area was estimated at 10.6 mm, only 6.1 percent of the previous yearsâ€™ average amount of 173.9 mm. It is the lowest figure in 104 years, says the Metropolitan government.
 To tackle the dreadful drought, 444 water tankers and 55 private vehicles have been mobilized to spray water onto the city areas that lack water. 
 Nationwide, some 3,600 hectares of farm land in Gyeonggi, South Chuncheong and Jeolla provinces has been affected, the Agriculture Ministry noted.
