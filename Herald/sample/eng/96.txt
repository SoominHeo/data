Mother Nature strikes Nepal and India
At 6:10 p.m. on Sept. 18, a strong earthquake devastated northern India and Nepal. The earthquake caused landslides, destroyed buildings and sent citizens fleeing from their homes onto the streets. 
Initial reports claim that at least 16 people were killed in and around Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal. According to news reports, the earthquake struck a remote part of the Himalayas on the eastern end of Nepal, registering a magnitude of 6.9 on the Richter scale. The following two aftershocks registered 6.1 and 5.3, respectively.
