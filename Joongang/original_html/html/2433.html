Spouses deserve respect, courtesy

아내의 응징은 최후의 순간 돌이킬 수 없이 이루어진다  

Feb 12,2013
It is a fight between a shameless husband and a fearsome wife. Former British Energy Secretary Chris Huhne resigned because of a speeding offense.

Huhne, a member of parliament, had a promising political future ahead of him, considered as the next leader of the Liberal Democrats. However, it was revealed by the end of last year that he had his wife take penalty points for his traffic violation 10 years ago. He is now awaiting a verdict. His offense was disclosed by his former wife, who divorced Huhne for his extramarital affairs.

When I first learned of the scandal, the first thing that came into my mind was, “What a shameless man.” However, many men responded that it was a revenge of a fearsome wife. Former International Monetary Fund director Dominique Strauss-Kahn once was considered the Socialist presidential candidate until he was indicted for allegedly sexually assaulting a hotel maid in New York.

When Strauss-Kahn and his wife separated the following year, some men said, “The wife left the husband when his prospect to become president disappeared.”

But I want to credit her for trying to save the marriage by defending her husband during the scandal. Men and women have different perspectives on marriage, and this difference often get
amplified into greater discords. On JTBC’s drama “Best Life Is Without Children,”

an octogenarian woman threatens to divorce to her husband. The husband always scolds her, but he is so full of his “right values” that he does not see his rudeness and oppression. One of his sons also openly criticizes his own wife’s stinginess in front of other people. Soon enough, his wife will likely abandon him.

Nobody wants to be treated without respect. And no one deserves to be scolded, criticized and humiliated.

However, some men mistakenly think that belittling their wives is a reflection of their own modesty because they see their wives as their alter egos. But a wife is not subordinate to her husband.

She is an independent human being. When you don’t have respect and are rude to your partner, the relationship cannot last. Of course, most wives choose to endure for a long time. However, the moment she decides not to take the humiliation any more, the marriage ends right there. Huhne’s promising political career has ended as well.

The holiday season is also the time of year when couples are most likely to fight over trivial matters.

The war of nerves escalates when strangers gather in the name of a family get-together. If the tension is elevating between the couple in the holiday season, we all need to take a hard look at our attitudes.
Do I treat my spouse with respect and courtesy?


*The author is an editorial writer of the JoongAng Ilbo


By Yang Sunny
