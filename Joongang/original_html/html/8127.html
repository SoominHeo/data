Inchon City To Develop Inter-Korean Trade Center

June 18,2000

Inchon City plans to create a forward base to promote trade and to provide an area for reunions of separated families. Inchon city plans to name the center ＂Peace Island＂ and will build the center on Kyodong Island in Kanghwa County.

On June 16 the city of Inchon disclosed that it had plans to secure a 165,000-330,000 square-meter lot upon which to build a facility to support reunions of separated families. Additionally, the city of Inchon will also build a distribution center on Kyodong Island to promote inter-Korean trade. Kyodong Island is located of the coast of Inchon about three kilometers from Yonbaek County in North Korea＇s Hwanghae Province.

The envisioned facility for separated families will house a meeting place as well as a transfer base for letters and packages, while the distribution center will be a place where inter-Korean agricultural, marine, and industrial goods will be stored, collected and quarantined.

According to the plan, full-fledged construction in Kyodong Island may be launched as early as the first half of next year.

The city will ask the central government to designate Kyodong Island as a special tourist zone, expand the maritime transportation infrastructure, and to build a bridge connecting Kanghwa Island with Kyondong Island by extending Route 48 (Seoul-Kwanghwa) all the way to Kyodong Island.

Kyodong Island is located about 3.2 kilometers from Kanghwa Island. Inchon City Hall plans to make it a center linking the South＇s key West Coast sea route (Kanghwa-Inch＇on-Kunsan-Mokp＇o) with the Northern ports of Haeju and Pyongyang. Many experts agree that Kyodong Island is the most appropriate location from which to promote tourism and trade with North Korea.

Kyodong Island is 46.2 square kilometers in size and is home to 3,800 islanders. It was a maritime transportation hub during the Choson Dynasty (1392-1910), and because of its strategic position at the mouth of the Han, Yesong, Imjin rivers.

In addition, Inchon plans to open a reunion center for separated families on Paeknyong Island, the northernmost island in South Korea, and to also form a sister city relationship with North Korea＇s Namp＇o City, to which a direct navigation route exists.





























인천시, 강화도에 남북 교류 기지 조성

인천시가　강화군　교동도에　남북한　이산가족　상봉　및　교역　전진기지인　'평화의　섬'을　조성하게　된다．
　인천시는　남북한　화해조치로　인한　교역　증대　등에　대비해　북한　황해도　연백군과　３㎞가량　떨어진　강화　교동도에　부지　５만∼１０만평을　마련해　이산가족상봉지원센터와　물류교역단지를　설치하는　계획을　수립　중이라고　１６일　밝혔다．
　이산가족상봉지원센터에는　면회소와　서신·물품　중간기지가　들어서며，물류교역단지는　농수산물과　공산품　등　남북한　교역상품　집산　및　검역　기능을　담당하게　된다．
　시는　이를　위해　내년　１월　중　완료되는　강화종합발전계획안에　교동도　평화의　섬　조성방안을　포함시켰다．
　이에　따라　이르면　내년　상반기부터　본격적인　조성사업에　들어갈　수　있을　것으로　기대된다．　　　　
　시는　또　정부에　＾교동도　관광특구지정　및　해상교통　기반시설　확충＾국도　４８번（서울∼강화）을　교동도까지　연장하기　위한　강화도∼교동도　교량건설　등을　적극　요청할　방침이다．
　교동도는　강화도에서　３．２㎞　가량　떨어져　있으며　국토　간선도로망인　남북　１축（강화∼인천∼군산∼목포）을　해주와　평양으로　연장할　수　있는　교통중심지여서　대북　관광·교역의　최적지로　평가받고　있다．
　총　면적　１천４백여만평인　교동도에는　현재　１천４백여가구　３천８백여명의　주민들이　살고　있다．이곳은　한강과　예성강，임진강　하류에　위치해　조선시대때부터　해상교통의　요충지로　지목받아　왔다．
　한편　인천시는　서해　최북단　백령도에　이산가족상봉센터를，직항로가　개설돼　있는　북한　남포시와는　자매결연을　적극　추진할　방침이다．
