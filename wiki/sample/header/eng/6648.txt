Bugak is a variety of twigim , or fried Korean traditional dishes, made by deep frying dried vegetables or seaweed that have been coated with chapssalpul  for preservation.
The culinary technique is rare along with twigak  in Korean cuisine, all of which are regarded as specialty of Korean Buddhist cuisine.
The ingredients for making bugak are kombu, gim , perilla leaves, chili pepper leaves, ueong , shoots of Ailanthus altissima, camellia leaves, chrysanthemum leaves, sliced potato and so forth.
Vegetable oils such as perilla oil or soybean oil are used for frying.
