<p><b>Mary, Crown Princess of Denmark, Countess of Monpezat</b>, <span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Elephant" title="Order of the Elephant">R.E.</a></span> (Mary Elizabeth; <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%A9e" title="NÃ©e">nÃ©e</a></i> Donaldson; born 5 February 1972) is the wife of <a href="/wiki/Frederik,_Crown_Prince_of_Denmark" title="Frederik, Crown Prince of Denmark">Frederik, Crown Prince of Denmark</a>. Frederik is the heir apparent to the throne of Denmark, which means that should Frederik inherit the throne, Mary will automatically become <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">Queen consort</a> of Denmark.</p>
<p>The couple met at the Slip Inn, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_pubs" title="Australian pubs">pub</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> when the prince was visiting Australia during the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a>. Their official engagement in 2003 and their marriage the following year was the subject of extensive attention from Australian and European news media, which portrayed the marriage as a modern "fairytale" romance between a prince and a commoner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mary Elizabeth Donaldson was born the youngest of four children to Scottish parents,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Henrietta (nÃ©e Horne), an executive assistant to the vice-chancellor of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tasmania" title="University of Tasmania">University of Tasmania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Dalgleish_Donaldson" title="John Dalgleish Donaldson">Dr John Dalgleish Donaldson</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> a mathematics professor. She was named after her grandmothers, Mary Dalgleish and Elizabeth Gibson Melrose. Her mother died on 20 November 1997. In 2001, her father married the British author and novelist <a href="/wiki/Susan_Moody" title="Susan Moody">Susan Horwood</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Donaldson was born and raised in Hobart, Australia. During her childhood, she was involved in sports and other extracurricular activities both at school and elsewhere. She studied music â playing piano, flute, and clarinet â and played basketball and hockey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1974, Donaldson started schooling in Clear Lake City Elementary School in Houston, Texas (where her father was working) and moved to <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Bay,_Tasmania" title="Sandy Bay, Tasmania">Sandy Bay, Tasmania</a> from 1975 to 1977. Her primary education, from 1978 to 1983, was at Waimea Heights with her secondary schooling (1984-1987) being at <a href="/wiki/Taroona_High_School" title="Taroona High School">Taroona High School</a>, and matriculation (1988-1989) at <a href="/wiki/Hobart_College,_Tasmania" title="Hobart College, Tasmania">Hobart College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Donaldson studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tasmania" title="University of Tasmania">University of Tasmania</a> from 1990 to 1994,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crprincess_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-crprincess-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> graduating with a combined <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Commerce" title="Bachelor of Commerce">Bachelor of Commerce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Laws" title="Bachelor of Laws">Bachelor of Laws</a> degree on 27 May 1995. Between 1994 and 1996, she attended a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graduate_program" title="Graduate program">graduate program</a> and qualified with certificates in advertising from the Advertising Federation of Australia (AFA) and direct marketing from the Australian Direct Marketing Association (ADMA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crprincess_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-crprincess-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The crown princess' native language is English. She studied French during her secondary education. In 2002, she briefly tutored in English in Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crprincess_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-crprincess-7">[7]</a></sup> After meeting <a href="/wiki/Frederik,_Crown_Prince_of_Denmark" title="Frederik, Crown Prince of Denmark">Frederik</a> at the Slip Inn during the 2000 Olympic Games in Sydney, Donaldson studied Danish as a foreign language at <a href="/wiki/Studieskolen" title="Studieskolen">Studieskolen</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While studying in Tasmania, Donaldson reportedly had a joint business interest in a company called Kingcash, with Brent Annells, with whom she had a seven-year relationship in the 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>She worked for Australian and global advertising agencies after graduating in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crprincess_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-crprincess-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Upon graduation Mary moved to <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> to work in advertising. She became a trainee in marketing and communications with the Melbourne office of <a href="/wiki/DDB_Worldwide" title="DDB Worldwide">DDB Needham</a>, taking a position of <a href="/wiki/Account_executive" title="Account executive">account executive</a>. In 1996, Mary was employed by <a href="/wiki/Mojo_(advertising)" title="Mojo (advertising)">Mojo Partners</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Account_manager" title="Account manager">account manager</a>. In 1998, six months after her mother's death, she resigned and travelled to America and Europe. In <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, she worked for three months as an account manager with Rapp Collins Worldwide; then, in early 1999, she was appointed as an <a href="/wiki/Advertising_account_executive" title="Advertising account executive">account director</a> with the international advertising agency <a href="/wiki/Young_%26_Rubicam" title="Young &amp; Rubicam">Young &amp; Rubicam</a> in Sydney.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crprincess_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-crprincess-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2000, she moved to a smaller Australian agency, Love Branding, working for a short time as the company's first <a href="/wiki/Advertising_account_executive" title="Advertising account executive">account director</a>. However, in the (Australian) spring of 2000 until December 2001, she became sales director and a member of the management team of Belle Property, a real estate firm specialising in luxury property. In the first half of 2002 Mary taught English at a business school in Paris but, on moving to Denmark permanently, she was employed by <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> Business Solutions (5 September 2002 â 24 September 2003) near <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> as a project consultant for business development, communications and marketing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crprincess_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-crprincess-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Courtship_and_engagement">Courtship and engagement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Courtship and engagement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mary Donaldson met Crown Prince Frederik of Denmark at the Slip Inn<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> during the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> on 16 September in Sydney. He was not identified by her friends as the Crown Prince of Denmark until after they met.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> They conducted a long-distance relationship by phone, email and letter and Frederik made a number of discreet visits to Australia. On 15 November 2001 the Danish weekly magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Billed_Bladet" title="Billed Bladet">Billed Bladet</a></i> named Mary as Frederik's girlfriend. She then moved from Australia to Denmark in December 2001, while she was working as an English tutor in Paris. On 24 September 2003 the Danish court announced that <a href="/wiki/Margrethe_II_of_Denmark" title="Margrethe II of Denmark">Queen Margrethe II</a> intended to give her consent to the marriage at the State Council meeting scheduled for 8 October 2003.</p>
<p>Frederik presented Mary with an engagement ring featuring an emerald-cut diamond and two emerald-cut ruby baguettes, which are similar to the colour of Denmark's flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The couple became officially engaged on 8 October 2003.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_Frederik,_Crown_Prince_of_Denmark,_and_Mary_Donaldson" title="Wedding of Frederik, Crown Prince of Denmark, and Mary Donaldson">Wedding of Frederik, Crown Prince of Denmark, and Mary Donaldson</a></div>
<p>Mary Donaldson and Crown Prince Frederik married on 14 May 2004 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Cathedral" title="Copenhagen Cathedral">Copenhagen Cathedral</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Mary wore a wedding gown designed by Danish designer Uffe Frank and had a small bridal party which included her two sisters and her friend Amber Petty, a radio announcer on commercial radio in Australia. Frederik was supported by his brother <a href="/wiki/Prince_Joachim_of_Denmark" title="Prince Joachim of Denmark">Prince Joachim</a>. Three of Mary's nieces, Erin and Kate Stephens and Madisson Woods, were flower girls; Frederik's nephew <a href="/wiki/Prince_Nikolai_of_Denmark" title="Prince Nikolai of Denmark">Prince Nikolai of Denmark</a> and first cousin once removed, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Count_Richard_von_Pfeil_und_Klein-Ellguth" title="Count Richard von Pfeil und Klein-Ellguth">Count Richard von Pfeil und Klein-Ellguth</a> were pageboys. The wedding<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> was celebrated in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> and at <a href="/wiki/Fredensborg_Palace" title="Fredensborg Palace">Fredensborg Palace</a>. The couple reportedly spent their honeymoon in Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The couple have four children:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_Christian_of_Denmark" title="Prince Christian of Denmark">Prince <b>Christian</b> Valdemar Henri John</a>, born 15 October 2005</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Princess_Isabella_of_Denmark" title="Princess Isabella of Denmark">Princess <b>Isabella</b> Henrietta Ingrid Margrethe</a>, born 21 April 2007</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_Vincent_of_Denmark" title="Prince Vincent of Denmark">Prince <b>Vincent</b> Frederik Minik Alexander</a>, born 8 January 2011</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Princess_Josephine_of_Denmark" title="Princess Josephine of Denmark">Princess <b>Josephine</b> Sophia Ivalo Mathilda</a>, born 8 January 2011</li>
</ul>
<p>The Danish <a href="/wiki/Folketing" title="Folketing">Folketing</a> (parliament) passed a special law (Mary's Law)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> giving Mary Donaldson Danish <a href="/wiki/Citizenship" title="Citizenship">citizenship</a> upon her marriage, a standard procedure for new foreign members of the royal family; she was previously a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dual_citizenship" title="Dual citizenship">dual citizen</a> of Australia and the United Kingdom. Formerly a <a href="/wiki/Presbyterianism" title="Presbyterianism">Presbyterian</a>, Mary converted to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelical_Lutheran" title="Evangelical Lutheran">Evangelical Lutheran</a> <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Denmark" title="Church of Denmark">Church of Denmark</a> upon marriage.</p>
<p>The Crown Princess and her family currently reside at Frederik VIII's Palace, one of the four palaces that make up the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amalienborg_Palace" title="Amalienborg Palace">Amalienborg Palace</a> complex. From May 2004 they have also resided at The Chancellery House, a building in the park at <a href="/wiki/Fredensborg_Palace" title="Fredensborg Palace">Fredensborg Palace</a>.</p>

<p>Following the wedding, the couple embarked upon a summer working tour of mainland Denmark aboard the royal yacht <a href="/wiki/HDMY_Dannebrog_(A540)" title="HDMY Dannebrog (A540)"><i>Dannebrog</i></a>, and then to <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and later the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_Athens_Olympics" title="2004 Athens Olympics">2004 Athens Olympics</a>. In 2005, during the celebrations for the 200th anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Hans_Christian_Andersen" title="Hans Christian Andersen">Hans Christian Andersen</a>, the royal family was involved in related events throughout the year. Frederik and Mary marked the anniversary in London, New York and in Australia, where Mary was made Honorary Hans Christian Andersen Ambassador to Australia in the Utzon Room of the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Opera_House" title="Sydney Opera House">Sydney Opera House</a>. In 2005 the royal family visited the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a>.</p>
<p>Since becoming Crown Princess of Denmark, Mary has made a number of international visits<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and Frederik and Mary participated in the reburial ceremonies for Empress <a href="/wiki/Maria_Feodorovna_(Dagmar_of_Denmark)" title="Maria Feodorovna (Dagmar of Denmark)">Maria Feodorovna</a> in Denmark and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>. In November 2009, Mary made a surprise visit to Danish soldiers in <a href="/wiki/Helmand_Province" title="Helmand Province">Helmand Province</a>, Afghanistan. One of the Crown Princess's stops was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forward_Operating_Base_Armadillo" title="Forward Operating Base Armadillo">FOB Armadillo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the context of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Refugees,_Immigrants_and_Integration_(Denmark)" title="Minister for Refugees, Immigrants and Integration (Denmark)">immigrant issues</a> in Denmark, Mary has visited the disadvantaged migrant areas of Vollsmose (2006),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Gellerup (2007),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and Viborg (2010),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and has participated in integration projects and to teach the Danish language to refugees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fyens1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-fyens1-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> As patron of the Danish <a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">Refugee</a> Council, Mary visited Uganda (2008)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and East Africa (2011)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and supports fundraising for the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mary has played an active role in promoting an anti-bullying program based on an Australian model through the auspices of Denmark's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Save_the_Children_Alliance" title="International Save the Children Alliance">Save the Children</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Mary is also involved in a new campaign to raise awareness and safe practices among Danes about skin cancer through The Danish Cancer Society. In September 2007 Mary formally established the Mary Foundation, with capital from public and private donations, to advance cultural diversity and encourage a sense of the right to belong and contribute to society for those who are socially isolated or excluded.</p>
<p>Mary was voted Woman of the Year 2008 by a Danish magazine, <i>Alt for damerne</i>. The Princess donated her cash reward to charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> She was interviewed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parade_Magazine" title="Parade Magazine">Parade Magazine</a> (US)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and television programs of <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Denton" title="Andrew Denton">Andrew Denton</a> (Australia)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-36">[36]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a> (USA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fyens1_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-fyens1-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a native English-speaker, Mary's main priority from the time of her engagement was to become fluent in the <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish language</a>. Mary has acknowledged that this was a challenge for her in several interviews at the time of her engagement and marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> If Frederik ascends the throne, Mary will be the first Australian-born woman to be a queen consort.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>( The first Australian woman to be a Queen Consort was the wife of King Leka, their late Majesty's spent most of their lives in exile, but HM Queen Susan died in Tirana. Mary's situation highlights the question of what defines a Queen Consort. Does the title of Queen Consort not apply to the wives of Monarchs who never had a coronation, if that is the case, the wife of King Constantine would qualify, but not the wife of King Leka.)</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion">Fashion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fashion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mary is an active patron of Denmark's third-highest-earning export industry, the fashion industry and is Patron of the Copenhagen Fashion Summit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has been named one of the world's most fashionable people in Vanity Fair's annual International Best-Dressed List<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> and has posed and given interviews for magazines including <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue Australia</a></i> (where she used pieces of foreign designers, such as Hugo Boss, Prada, Louis Vuitton or Gaultier, and Danish designers, as Malene Birger and Georg Jensen), <i>Dansk</i> (Danish Magazine, dedicated to Danish fashion), German Vogue (where she was photographed between pieces of Danish modern art in Amalienborg Palace).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Mary also posed for other magazines during her life as a royal, such as Women's Weekly Australia magazine (to which she spoke on several occasions about her life as a royal and her family) and Parade Magazine.</p>
<p>Her elegance was praised by designer <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hilfiger" title="Tommy Hilfiger">Tommy Hilfiger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Patronages">Patronages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Patronages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2004 Crown Princess Mary has steadily worked to establish her relationships with various organisations, their issues, missions, programmes and staff. Mary's patronages range across areas of culture, the fashion industry, humanitarian aid, support for research and science, social and health patronages and sport (golf and swimming). The organisations for which she is patron have reported positive outcomes through their relationship with Mary and there are various reports in the Danish media and on some of the websites of the organisations themselves about Mary being quite involved in her working relationship with them. Mary is currently involved in supporting anti-obesity programs through the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a>, Regional Office for Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mary's current patronages include cultural organisations,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> the Danish fashion industry<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> humanitarian aid,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> research and science,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> social, health and humanitarian organisations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> and sporting organisations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>Crown Princess Mary is also an Honorary Life Governor of the <a href="/wiki/Victor_Chang_Cardiac_Research_Institute" title="Victor Chang Cardiac Research Institute">Victor Chang Cardiac Research Institute</a> based at the Garvan Institute/<a href="/wiki/St_Vincent%27s_Hospital,_Sydney" title="St Vincent's Hospital, Sydney">St Vincent's Hospital, Sydney</a>, a member of the International Committee of Women Leaders for Mental Health and a member of various sporting clubs (riding, golf and yachting). In June 2010, it was announced that Crown Princess Mary has become Patron of UNFPA, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a>, "to support the agency's work to promote maternal health and safer motherhood in more than 150 developing nations".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Mary lends her support to a number of other 'one-off' Danish causes, industry events and international conferences. In 2011, the Westmead Cancer Centre at <a href="/wiki/Westmead_Hospital" title="Westmead Hospital">Westmead Hospital</a> in Sydney was renamed the Crown Princess Mary Cancer Care Centre Westmead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mary_Foundation">Mary Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Mary Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 September 2007 Crown Princess Mary announced the establishment of the Mary Foundation<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> at the inaugural meeting at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amalienborg_Palace" title="Amalienborg Palace">Amalienborg Palace</a>. The initial funds of DKK 1.1 million were collected in Denmark and Greenland and donated to Frederik and Mary as a wedding gift in 2004. Crown Princess Mary is the chairwoman of eight trustees. The Mary Foundation aims to improve lives compromised by environment, heredity, illness or other circumstances which can isolate or exclude people socially. In 2014, Mary received a <a href="/wiki/Bambi_Award" title="Bambi Award">Bambi Award</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="LGBT_rights">LGBT rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: LGBT rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Day_Against_Homophobia_and_Transphobia" title="International Day Against Homophobia and Transphobia">International Day Against Homophobia and Transphobia</a>, the Crown Princess gave a speech on LGBT rights at a forum in Copenhagen hosted by the Danish government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> She called for an end to discrimination, oppression, and violence against people because of their sexual orientation and gender identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles_and_styles">Titles and styles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Titles and styles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mary is styled as <i>Her Royal Highness</i> The Crown Princess of Denmark, Countess of Monpezat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mary has been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_Prince_of_Denmark" title="Crown Prince of Denmark">Crown Princess of Denmark</a> since her marriage and also <a href="/wiki/Count_of_Monpezat" title="Count of Monpezat">Countess of Monpezat</a> by marriage since 29 April 2008, when Queen Margrethe II granted the title to her male-line descendants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mary_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-mary-72">[72]</a></sup> She also holds the rank of first lieutenant in the <a href="/wiki/Home_Guard_(Denmark)" title="Home Guard (Denmark)">Home Guard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kongehuset.dk_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-kongehuset.dk-73">[73]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arms">Arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="695" data-file-width="682" height="224" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg/220px-Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg/330px-Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg/440px-Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_Crown_Princess_Mary_of_Denmark.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Coat of arms of Crown Princess Mary of Denmark.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>With the marriage in 2004, the Crown Princess Mary was honoured with the Order of the Elephant, and her father <a href="/wiki/John_Dalgleish_Donaldson" title="John Dalgleish Donaldson">John Dalgleish Donaldson</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Dannebrog" title="Order of the Dannebrog">Order of the Dannebrog</a>. In accordance with the statutes of the Danish Royal Orders, both the Crown Princess and her father were granted a personal <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>, this for display in the Chapel of the Royal Orders at <a href="/wiki/Frederiksborg_Castle" title="Frederiksborg Castle">Frederiksborg Castle</a>. The main field of the Crown Princess' coat of arms is <a href="/wiki/Or_(heraldry)" title="Or (heraldry)">or</a> <a href="/wiki/Tincture_(heraldry)" title="Tincture (heraldry)">tinctured</a> and shows a <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">gules</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MacDonald" title="MacDonald">MacDonald</a> eagle and a <a href="/wiki/Sable_(heraldry)" title="Sable (heraldry)">Sable</a> tinctured boat both symbolising her Scottish ancestry. The chief field is <a href="/wiki/Azure_(heraldry)" title="Azure (heraldry)">azure</a> tinctured and shows two gold <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Star" title="Commonwealth Star">Commonwealth Stars</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Australia" title="Coat of arms of Australia">Coat of arms of Australia</a>, and a gold <a href="/wiki/Rose_(heraldry)" title="Rose (heraldry)">rose</a> in between, depicted as her personal symbol. Above the shield is placed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heraldic_crown" title="Heraldic crown">heraldic crown</a> of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_Prince_of_Denmark" title="Crown Prince of Denmark">Crown Prince of Denmark</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heraldics_103-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heraldics-103">[103]</a></sup></p>
<p>The coat of arms of her father is almost identical to that of the Crown Princess, but a gold <a href="/wiki/Infinity_symbol" title="Infinity symbol">infinity symbol</a> is depicted (symbolising his career as an Australian mathematician), instead of the gold Rose. Above his shield is instead placed a <a href="/wiki/Helmet_(heraldry)" title="Helmet (heraldry)">barred helmet</a> topped with a gules <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rampant_lion" title="Rampant lion">rampant lion</a>, which is turned outward. The lion is derived from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_coat_of_arms" title="Scottish coat of arms">Scottish coat of arms</a> and also from the arms of <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hobart" title="Hobart">Hobart</a>. Both coats of arms were approved in 2006 and placed in the Chapel of the Royal Orders in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heraldics_103-1"><a href="#cite_note-Heraldics-103">[103]</a></sup></p>
