<p>The four <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <b>lion tamarins</b> make up the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><b>Leontopithecus</b></i>. They are small <a href="/wiki/New_World_monkey" title="New World monkey">New World monkeys</a> named for the mane surrounding their face similar to the mane of a <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lion</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lion_tamarin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Living in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Forest" title="Atlantic Forest">eastern rainforests</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, like all other <a href="/wiki/Callitrichidae" title="Callitrichidae">callitrichids</a> they are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arboreal" title="Arboreal">arboreal</a>. Lion tamarins weigh up to 900 grams (32 oz) and are about 30 cm (12 in) long, with tails about 45 cm (18 in) long. They jump through trees using their fingers to hold on to branches and their claws to dig under the bark to search for insects to eat. They also eat some snakes, small lizards and small fruits. All are endangered or critically endangered.</p>
<p>Lion tamarins tend to live in family groups, with both parents sharing different tasks of child-rearing the yearly twins. The mother nurses her young every two to three hours, and the father carries the babies on his back.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diurnal_animal" title="Diurnal animal">Diurnal</a> tree-dwellers, they sleep in tree cavities at night, and also seek shelter during the hottest part of the day.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Species_list">Species list</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lion_tamarin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Species list">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The different species of lion tamarins are easily discernible from each other, based upon the coloration of their fur:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msw3_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-msw3-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Golden_lion_tamarin" title="Golden lion tamarin">Golden lion tamarin</a>, <i>L. rosalia</i> — golden fur all over, mane sometimes darkening or black</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Golden-headed_lion_tamarin" title="Golden-headed lion tamarin">Golden-headed lion tamarin</a>, <i>L. chrysomelas</i> — black fur with golden face, arms, and tail</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Black_lion_tamarin" title="Black lion tamarin">Black lion tamarin</a> or golden-rumped lion tamarin, <i>L. chrysopygus</i> — black fur with a dark gold rump</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Superagui_lion_tamarin" title="Superagui lion tamarin">Superagui lion tamarin</a> or black-faced lion tamarin, <i>L. caissara</i> — golden fur with black face, arms, and tails</li>
</ul>
