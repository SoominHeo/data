<p><b>Abhishek Bachchan</b> (born 5 February 1976) is an Indian actor, producer and playback singer best known for his work in <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Hindi films</a>. He is the son of veteran actors <a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaya_Bachchan" title="Jaya Bachchan">Jaya Bachchan</a>. He made his film debut in the war drama <i><a href="/wiki/Refugee_(2000_film)" title="Refugee (2000 film)">Refugee</a></i> (2000), which did not perform well at the box office, but had earned him appreciation from critics. For the next few years, Bachchan starred in a series of unsuccessful films that failed to propel his career forward. Bachchan then established himself as a leading actor of Hindi cinema with the blockbuster action thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom" title="Dhoom">Dhoom</a></i> (2004).</p>
<p>Since then, he has starred in several successful films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Bunty_Aur_Babli" title="Bunty Aur Babli">Bunty Aur Babli</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom_2" title="Dhoom 2">Dhoom 2</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Guru_(2007_film)" title="Guru (2007 film)">Guru</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Dostana_(2008_film)" title="Dostana (2008 film)">Dostana</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Bol_Bachchan" title="Bol Bachchan">Bol Bachchan</a></i> (2012) and <i><a href="/wiki/Housefull_3" title="Housefull 3">Housefull 3</a></i> (2016). He has also featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom_3" title="Dhoom 3">Dhoom 3</a></i> (2013) and <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(2014_film)" title="Happy New Year (2014 film)">Happy New Year</a></i> (2014), which both rank among the <a href="/wiki/List_of_highest-grossing_Indian_films" title="List of highest-grossing Indian films">highest-grossing Indian films</a> of all time. In addition to three consecutive <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Awards" title="Filmfare Awards">Filmfare Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor">Best Supporting Actor</a> for his critically acclaimed performances in <i><a href="/wiki/Yuva" title="Yuva">Yuva</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Sarkar_(film)" title="Sarkar (film)">Sarkar</a></i> (2005), and <i><a href="/wiki/Kabhi_Alvida_Naa_Kehna" title="Kabhi Alvida Naa Kehna">Kabhi Alvida Naa Kehna</a></i> (2006), Bachchan has garnered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Film_Awards_(India)" title="National Film Awards (India)">National Film Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Award_for_Best_Feature_Film_in_Hindi" title="National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi">Best Feature Film in Hindi</a> for producing the family dramedy <i><a href="/wiki/Paa_(film)" title="Paa (film)">Paa</a></i> (2009). Since 2007, Bachchan has been married to actress <a href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai" title="Aishwarya Rai">Aishwarya Rai</a> and they have a daughter named Aaradhya born on 16 November 2011.</p>

<p>Abhishek Bachchan was born on 5 February 1976 to veteran actors of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindi_cinema" title="Hindi cinema">Hindi cinema</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jaya_Bhaduri_Bachchan" title="Jaya Bhaduri Bachchan">Jaya Bhaduri Bachchan</a>, an accomplished actress in her own right. He is married to <a href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai" title="Aishwarya Rai">Aishwarya Rai</a>, a former Miss World and leading Bollywood actress. His grandfather, <a href="/wiki/Harivansh_Rai_Bachchan" title="Harivansh Rai Bachchan">Harivansh Rai Bachchan</a>, was a noted poet of the Nayi Kavita Literary Movement of the <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a> literature and professor at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allahabad_University" title="Allahabad University">Allahabad University</a> in Uttar Pradesh. The original surname of his family is Srivastava, Bachchan being the pen name used by his grandfather. However, when Amitabh entered films, he did so under his father's pen name. Bachchan is of <a href="/wiki/Kayastha" title="Kayastha">Kayastha</a> heritage on his father's side,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bengali_people" title="Bengali people">Bengali</a> from his mother's side<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punjabi_people" title="Punjabi people">Punjabi</a> from his grandmother's side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Teji_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Teji-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Time magazine listed Amitabh Bachchan and Aishwarya Rai on its list of most influential Indians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Bachchan was <a href="/wiki/Dyslexia" title="Dyslexia">dyslexic</a> as a child; this was referred to by <a href="/wiki/Aamir_Khan" title="Aamir Khan">Aamir Khan</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Taare_Zameen_Par" title="Taare Zameen Par">Taare Zameen Par</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Jamnabai_Narsee_School" title="Jamnabai Narsee School">Jamnabai Narsee School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Scottish_School,_Mahim" title="Bombay Scottish School, Mahim">Bombay Scottish School</a> in Mumbai, <a href="/wiki/Modern_School_(New_Delhi)" title="Modern School (New Delhi)">Modern School</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasant_Vihar" title="Vasant Vihar">Vasant Vihar</a>, New Delhi, and <a href="/wiki/Aiglon_College" title="Aiglon College">Aiglon College</a> in Switzerland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He then attended <a href="/wiki/Boston_University" title="Boston University">Boston University</a>, and subsequently moved to <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_debut_and_initial_struggle_.282000.E2.80.932003.29">Film debut and initial struggle (2000–2003)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film debut and initial struggle (2000–2003)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, Bachchan made his film debut playing the male lead in <a href="/wiki/J._P._Dutta" title="J. P. Dutta">J. P. Dutta</a>'s war drama <i><a href="/wiki/Refugee_(2000_film)" title="Refugee (2000 film)">Refugee</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Kareena_Kapoor" title="Kareena Kapoor">Kareena Kapoor</a>, who was also making her debut in the film. Although the film did not fare very well at the box office, both Bachchan and Kapoor received positive reviews for their performances. Film critic Taran Adarsh wrote that he "has all it takes to emerge a competent actor in years to come. Even in his debut-making film, Abhishek comes across as a fine actor and lives up to his family name."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <i><a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">Refugee</a></i>, Bachchan starred in a series of poorly received films that failed to make a mark at the box office. However, his performance in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sooraj_R._Barjatya" title="Sooraj R. Barjatya">Sooraj R. Barjatya</a>'s romantic dramedy <i><a href="/wiki/Main_Prem_Ki_Diwani_Hoon" title="Main Prem Ki Diwani Hoon">Main Prem Ki Diwani Hoon</a></i> (2003) earned him his first nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor">Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>, and he went on to win the award the following year for his performance in <a href="/wiki/Mani_Ratnam" title="Mani Ratnam">Mani Ratnam</a>'s political drama <i><a href="/wiki/Yuva" title="Yuva">Yuva</a></i> (2004).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Widespread_success_.282004.E2.80.932008.29">Widespread success (2004–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Widespread success (2004–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bachchan better established himself in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindi_cinema" title="Hindi cinema">Hindi cinema</a> playing a no-nonsense Mumbai police officer in the blockbuster <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Thriller_film" title="Thriller film">thriller</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom" title="Dhoom">Dhoom</a></i> (2004), which also starred <a href="/wiki/Uday_Chopra" title="Uday Chopra">Uday Chopra</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Abraham_(actor)" title="John Abraham (actor)">John Abraham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esha_Deol" title="Esha Deol">Esha Deol</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rimi_Sen" title="Rimi Sen">Rimi Sen</a> in pivotal roles. His next two films were <i><a href="/wiki/Phir_Milenge" title="Phir Milenge">Phir Milenge</a></i> (2004) and <i><a href="/wiki/Naach_(2004_film)" title="Naach (2004 film)">Naach</a></i> (2004), neither of which performed very well at the box office.</p>
<p>Bachchan's first release of 2005 was the crime comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Bunty_Aur_Babli" title="Bunty Aur Babli">Bunty Aur Babli</a></i>, which featured him and <a href="/wiki/Rani_Mukerji" title="Rani Mukerji">Rani Mukerji</a> as the titular con artist duo who perform several successful heists and fall in love along the way. The film emerged as the second highest-grossing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bollywood_films_of_2005" title="Bollywood films of 2005">Bollywood film of 2005</a>, and earned him a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Filmfare Award for Best Actor">Filmfare Award for Best Actor</a>. This film also marked his first professional collaboration with his father <a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a>, who played a police officer closely following the lead pair.</p>
<p>Bachchan was next seen in <a href="/wiki/Ram_Gopal_Varma" title="Ram Gopal Varma">Ram Gopal Varma</a>'s political drama <i><a href="/wiki/Sarkar_(film)" title="Sarkar (film)">Sarkar</a></i>, which was a moderate success at the box office. His performance as the morally upright son of a troubled politician (played by his father <a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a>) earned him rave reviews from critics as well as his second consecutive <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor">Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>. His next two releases <i><a href="/wiki/Dus" title="Dus">Dus</a></i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/Bluffmaster!" title="Bluffmaster!">Bluffmaster!</a></i> (2005) were both moderately successful, but did not earn much critical appreciation.</p>
<p>Bachchan won his third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor">Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor</a> for his performance in <a href="/wiki/Karan_Johar" title="Karan Johar">Karan Johar</a>'s critically acclaimed romantic drama <i><a href="/wiki/Kabhi_Alvida_Naa_Kehna" title="Kabhi Alvida Naa Kehna">Kabhi Alvida Naa Kehna</a></i> (2006), which emerged as a moderate commercial success despite the fact that it dealt with the controversial subject of marital infidelity. Bachchan played the role of a man whose wife (<a href="/wiki/Rani_Mukerji" title="Rani Mukerji">Rani Mukerji</a>) is unhappy with their marriage, and so begins an affair with a friend (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shahrukh_Khan" title="Shahrukh Khan">Shahrukh Khan</a>) who is also unhappy with his marriage to his wife (<a href="/wiki/Preity_Zinta" title="Preity Zinta">Preity Zinta</a>).</p>
<p>Bachchan then starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai" title="Aishwarya Rai">Aishwarya Rai</a> in the period romance <i><a href="/wiki/Umrao_Jaan_(2006_film)" title="Umrao Jaan (2006 film)">Umrao Jaan</a></i>, a remake of the <a href="/wiki/Umrao_Jaan_(1981_film)" title="Umrao Jaan (1981 film)">1981 film of the same name</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Rekha" title="Rekha">Rekha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Farooq_Sheikh" title="Farooq Sheikh">Farooq Sheikh</a> in the lead roles. The film failed to earn the same level of appreciation as the original, and was also a commercial failure. He was next seen in <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom_2" title="Dhoom 2">Dhoom 2</a></i> (2006), which featured him and Uday Chopra reprising their roles from the original <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom" title="Dhoom">Dhoom</a></i> (2004) joined by new cast members <a href="/wiki/Hrithik_Roshan" title="Hrithik Roshan">Hrithik Roshan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai" title="Aishwarya Rai">Aishwarya Rai</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bipasha_Basu" title="Bipasha Basu">Bipasha Basu</a>. The film emerged as the highest-grossing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bollywood_films_of_2006" title="Bollywood films of 2006">Bollywood film of 2006</a>, but some critics commented that Bachchan was "reduced to a mere supporting player"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> to his co-star Roshan.</p>
<p>Bachchan earned much critical acclaim for his performance in Mani Ratnam's <i><a href="/wiki/Guru_(2007_film)" title="Guru (2007 film)">Guru</a></i> (2007), loosely based on the life of business magnate <a href="/wiki/Dhirubhai_Ambani" title="Dhirubhai Ambani">Dhirubhai Ambani</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff2007_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-rediff2007-12">[12]</a></sup> The film starred him alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai_Bachchan" title="Aishwarya Rai Bachchan">Aishwarya Rai Bachchan</a> (their first professional collaboration after marriage), <a href="/wiki/R._Madhavan" title="R. Madhavan">R. Madhavan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mithun_Chakraborty" title="Mithun Chakraborty">Mithun Chakraborty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vidya_Balan" title="Vidya Balan">Vidya Balan</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arya_Babbar" title="Arya Babbar">Arya Babbar</a>.</p>
<p>His next release was the dance comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Jhoom_Barabar_Jhoom" title="Jhoom Barabar Jhoom">Jhoom Barabar Jhoom</a></i> (2007) alongside <a href="/wiki/Preity_Zinta" title="Preity Zinta">Preity Zinta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Deol" title="Bobby Deol">Bobby Deol</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lara_Dutta" title="Lara Dutta">Lara Dutta</a>. Although the film failed at the Indian box office, it was a moderate success overseas. He was then seen playing a minor role in the female-centric drama <i><a href="/wiki/Laaga_Chunari_Mein_Daag" title="Laaga Chunari Mein Daag">Laaga Chunari Mein Daag</a></i> (2007) as the love interest of the film's lead actress <a href="/wiki/Rani_Mukerji" title="Rani Mukerji">Rani Mukerji</a>.</p>
<p>Bachchan's first release of 2008 was <a href="/wiki/Ram_Gopal_Varma" title="Ram Gopal Varma">Ram Gopal Varma</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Sarkar_Raj" title="Sarkar Raj">Sarkar Raj</a></i>, which featured him and his father <a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a> reprising their roles from <i><a href="/wiki/Sarkar_(film)" title="Sarkar (film)">Sarkar</a></i> (2005) while his wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai_Bachchan" title="Aishwarya Rai Bachchan">Aishwarya Rai Bachchan</a> entered the cast as a new addition. This film proved to be a major success at the box office, and earned him a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor">Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>. His next release was the <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">fantasy</a> <a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Drona_(2008_film)" title="Drona (2008 film)">Drona</a></i> (2008), which was a major failure both critically and commercially.</p>
<p>Bachchan was next seen in <i><a href="/wiki/Dostana_(2008_film)" title="Dostana (2008 film)">Dostana</a></i> (2008), a romantic comedy about two men (Bachchan and <a href="/wiki/John_Abraham_(actor)" title="John Abraham (actor)">John Abraham</a>) who pretend to be gay in order to be allowed to live with a girl (<a href="/wiki/Priyanka_Chopra" title="Priyanka Chopra">Priyanka Chopra</a>), but then find that they have both fallen in love with her. The film was a major success at the box office, and earned him a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Filmfare Award for Best Actor">Filmfare Award for Best Actor</a>.</p>
<p>Bachchan's first release of 2009 was <i><a href="/wiki/Delhi-6" title="Delhi-6">Delhi-6</a></i>, which received a lukewarm response from critics. Later that year he was seen in the family dramedy <i><a href="/wiki/Paa_(film)" title="Paa (film)">Paa</a></i>, which he also produced. The film's plot centered on a boy (<a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a>) who suffers from <a href="/wiki/Progeria" title="Progeria">progeria</a> whose parents were played by Abhishek Bachchan and <a href="/wiki/Vidya_Balan" title="Vidya Balan">Vidya Balan</a>. For his work as a producer on the film, Bachchan earned the <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Award_for_Best_Feature_Film_in_Hindi" title="National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi">National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brief_setback_and_recent_success_.282010.E2.80.93present.29">Brief setback and recent success (2010–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Brief setback and recent success (2010–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bachchan experienced a brief setback in his career<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> with five films that failed critically and commercially. These films were the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Raavan" title="Raavan">Raavan</a></i> (2010), the social drama <i><a href="/wiki/Khelein_Hum_Jee_Jaan_Sey" title="Khelein Hum Jee Jaan Sey">Khelein Hum Jee Jaan Sey</a></i> (2010), the action thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Game_(2011_film)" title="Game (2011 film)">Game</a></i> (2011), the crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Dum_Maaro_Dum_(film)" title="Dum Maaro Dum (film)">Dum Maaro Dum</a></i> (2011), and the heist thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Players_(film)" title="Players (film)">Players</a></i> (2012).</p>
<p>Bachchan then played a supporting role alongside <a href="/wiki/Ajay_Devgan" title="Ajay Devgan">Ajay Devgan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asin" title="Asin">Asin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Prachi_Desai" title="Prachi Desai">Prachi Desai</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rohit_Shetty" title="Rohit Shetty">Rohit Shetty</a>'s comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Bol_Bachchan" title="Bol Bachchan">Bol Bachchan</a></i> (2012), which was a major blockbuster at the box office despite receiving mixed reviews from critics. Film critic Anupama Chopra called Bachchan a "good part of the film" and also commented that he "manages to sparkle even in a script that is lazy and determinedly lowbrow".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Bachchan received several comedian award for his role (Including <a href="/wiki/Screen_Awards" title="Screen Awards">Screen</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IIFA_Awards" title="IIFA Awards">IIFA</a>).</p>
<p>Bachchan then appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom_3" title="Dhoom 3">Dhoom 3</a></i> (2013), which featured him and <a href="/wiki/Uday_Chopra" title="Uday Chopra">Uday Chopra</a> reprising their roles from the previous two <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom_(film_series)" title="Dhoom (film series)">Dhoom</a></i> films. In this installment of the series, they were joined by <a href="/wiki/Aamir_Khan" title="Aamir Khan">Aamir Khan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katrina_Kaif" title="Katrina Kaif">Katrina Kaif</a>. While the film became a major blockbuster, most critics commented that Bachchan's performance was overshadowed by Khan's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bachchan was next seen in <a href="/wiki/Farah_Khan" title="Farah Khan">Farah Khan</a>'s blockbuster <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(2014_film)" title="Happy New Year (2014 film)">Happy New Year</a></i>, a musical heist film which featured an ensemble cast of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shahrukh_Khan" title="Shahrukh Khan">Shahrukh Khan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deepika_Padukone" title="Deepika Padukone">Deepika Padukone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonu_Sood" title="Sonu Sood">Sonu Sood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boman_Irani" title="Boman Irani">Boman Irani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vivaan_Shah" title="Vivaan Shah">Vivaan Shah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Shroff" title="Jackie Shroff">Jackie Shroff</a>. Bachchan's performance in a double role earned him many positive reviews from critics, who called his performance a "surprising standout" and "his loosest, funniest performance in years, if not ever."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Both <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom_3" title="Dhoom 3">Dhoom 3</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(2014_film)" title="Happy New Year (2014 film)">Happy New Year</a></i> rank among the <a href="/wiki/List_of_highest-grossing_Indian_films#Highest-grossing_Hindi_films" title="List of highest-grossing Indian films">highest-grossing Hindi films</a> of all time.</p>
<p>Bachchan was then seen in the 2015 family-drama <a href="/wiki/All_Is_Well_(film)" title="All Is Well (film)">All Is Well</a>, which failed at the box office. Bachchan's first release of 2016 was the comedy <a href="/wiki/Housefull_3" title="Housefull 3">Housefull 3</a>, a sequel of the 2012 film <a href="/wiki/Housefull_2" title="Housefull 2">Housefull 2</a>. The film was successful at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exp_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-exp-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_ventures">Sports ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sports ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Abhishek Bachchan bought the <a href="/wiki/Pro_Kabaddi_League" title="Pro Kabaddi League">Pro Kabaddi League</a> franchise team <i><a href="/wiki/Jaipur_Pink_Panthers" title="Jaipur Pink Panthers">Jaipur Pink Panthers</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and co-bought the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Super_League" title="Indian Super League">Indian Super League</a> football team <i><a href="/wiki/Chennaiyin_FC" title="Chennaiyin FC">Chennaiyin FC</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> in 2014. <a href="/wiki/Jaipur_Pink_Panthers" title="Jaipur Pink Panthers">Jaipur Pink Panthers</a> won the first ever championships held in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chennaiyin_FC" title="Chennaiyin FC">Chennaiyin FC</a> won the ISL 2015.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005, he was a part of Tamil director <a href="/wiki/Mani_Ratnam" title="Mani Ratnam">Mani Ratnam</a>'s stage show, <a href="/wiki/Netru,_Indru,_Naalai" title="Netru, Indru, Naalai">Netru, Indru, Naalai</a>, an event which sought to raise funds for The Banyan, a voluntary organisation which rehabilitates homeless women with mental illness in <a href="/wiki/Chennai" title="Chennai">Chennai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer of 2008, Bachchan, his wife, his father, and actors <a href="/wiki/Preity_Zinta" title="Preity Zinta">Preity Zinta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riteish_Deshmukh" title="Riteish Deshmukh">Riteish Deshmukh</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Madhuri_Dixit" title="Madhuri Dixit">Madhuri Dixit</a> starred in the "Unforgettable World Tour" stage production. The first leg covered the USA, Canada, <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a>, and London. Bachchan is also involved in the functional and administrative operations of his father's company, originally known as ABCL, and renamed as AB Corp. Ltd. That company, along with Wizcraft International Entertainment Pvt. Ltd., developed the Unforgettable production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Bachchan has promoted awareness of <a href="/wiki/Drug" title="Drug">drug</a> <a href="/wiki/Abuse" title="Abuse">abuse</a> in India as part of a citizen <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a> <a href="/wiki/Advocacy" title="Advocacy">campaign</a>. The actor launched the Awareness Day race, which celebrated the silver jubilee of the country's <a href="/wiki/Narcotics_Control_Bureau" title="Narcotics Control Bureau">Narcotics Control Bureau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Brand_ambassador">Brand ambassador</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Brand ambassador">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bachchan has been brand ambassador for products like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG" title="LG">LG</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_appliances" title="Home appliances">Home appliances</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> American Express credit cards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Videocon" title="Videocon">Videocon</a> <a href="/wiki/Direct-broadcast_satellite_television" title="Direct-broadcast satellite television">DTH</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Motorola" title="Motorola">Motorola</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobiles</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ford_Fiesta" title="Ford Fiesta">Ford Fiesta</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Idea" title="Idea">Idea</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobiles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Abhishek Bachchan was announced the winner of the 'Best Brand Ambassador of the Year' award at the <a href="/wiki/NDTV" title="NDTV">NDTV</a> Techlife Awards 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>AdEx India, a division of TAM Media Research conducted a study on celebrity brand endorsements for the period of Jan–Dec 2010 in which Abhishek Bachchan eats the pie with a 4.7% share ad volume out of the 41.5% film actors on the endorsement circuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Abhishek Bachchan became the brand ambassador for TTK Prestige, part of the <a href="/wiki/TTK_Group" title="TTK Group">TTK Group</a> in October 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> along with his wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai_Bachchan" title="Aishwarya Rai Bachchan">Aishwarya Rai Bachchan</a>.</p>
<p>In 2014, he became the brand ambassador for the END7 campaign of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Network_for_Neglected_Tropical_Diseases" title="Global Network for Neglected Tropical Diseases">Global Network for Neglected Tropical Diseases</a>. The goal of the campaign is to eradicate seven different tropical diseases by 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abhishek_Bachchan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In October 2002, at his father <a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a>'s 60th birthday celebration, Abhishek and actress <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karishma_Kapoor" title="Karishma Kapoor">Karishma Kapoor</a> announced their engagement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-intoday_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-intoday-37">[37]</a></sup> The engagement was called off in January 2003. Bachchan fell in love with <a href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai" title="Aishwarya Rai">Aishwarya Rai</a>, who is three years older than him, whilst filming for <i><a href="/wiki/Dhoom_2" title="Dhoom 2">Dhoom 2</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> though both of them had already appeared together in <i><a href="/wiki/Dhai_Akshar_Prem_Ke" title="Dhai Akshar Prem Ke">Dhai Akshar Prem Ke</a></i> (in which her then longtime boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/Salman_Khan" title="Salman Khan">Salman Khan</a>, made a brief cameo) and <i><a href="/wiki/Kuch_Naa_Kaho" title="Kuch Naa Kaho">Kuch Naa Kaho</a></i>. Bachchan and Rai announced their engagement on 14 January 2007 later confirmed by his father, Amitabh Bachchan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The couple was married on 20 April 2007, according to traditional Hindu rituals of the <a href="/wiki/Bunt_(community)" title="Bunt (community)">Bunt</a> community, to which Rai belongs. The wedding took place in a private ceremony at the Bachchan residence, <i>Prateeksha</i>, in <a href="/wiki/Juhu" title="Juhu">Juhu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> but was heavily covered by the entertainment media. The couple appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show" title="The Oprah Winfrey Show">The Oprah Winfrey Show</a></i> in September 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> and were described as more famous than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brangelina" title="Brangelina">Brangelina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> They have been described as a <a href="/wiki/Supercouple" title="Supercouple">supercouple</a> in the Indian media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Rai gave birth to a baby girl on 16 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beti-B_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beti-B-46">[46]</a></sup> Her daughter became quite popular by the name of "Beti B" which was named by the fans and the media, since the couple took over four months to name their daughter. The baby girl was finally named as Aaradhya Bachchan in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
