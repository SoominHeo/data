<p><b>Shoichi "Sho" Funaki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">船木 勝一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Funaki Shōichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-oww-1">[1]</a></sup> (born August 24, 1968)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-oww-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a>/<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Color_commentator" title="Color commentator">color commentator</a> signed by <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>, where he is a former <a href="/wiki/List_of_WWE_Cruiserweight_Champions_(1991%E2%80%932007)" title="List of WWE Cruiserweight Champions (1991–2007)">one-time</a> <a href="/wiki/WWE_Cruiserweight_Championship_(1991%E2%80%932007)" title="WWE Cruiserweight Championship (1991–2007)">Cruiserweight Champion</a> and a <a href="/wiki/List_of_WWE_Hardcore_Champions" title="List of WWE Hardcore Champions">one-time</a> <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hardcore_Championship" title="WWE Hardcore Championship">Hardcore Champion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cruiser_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-cruiser-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hardcore_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-hardcore-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.281990.E2.80.931998.29">Early years (1990–1998)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years (1990–1998)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Funaki started as a <a href="/wiki/Shoot_wrestling" title="Shoot wrestling">shoot style</a> wrestler in <a href="/wiki/Yoshiaki_Fujiwara" title="Yoshiaki Fujiwara">Yoshiaki Fujiwara</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Fujiwara_Gumi" title="Pro Wrestling Fujiwara Gumi">Pro Wrestling Fujiwara Gumi</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_promotion" title="Professional wrestling promotion">promotion</a> and moved to its successor promotion, <a href="/wiki/Battlarts" title="Battlarts">Battlarts</a>. He later found the <a href="/wiki/Lucha_libre" title="Lucha libre">lucha libre</a> style more to his liking, so he moved to <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Sasuke" title="The Great Sasuke">The Great Sasuke</a>'s promotion, <a href="/wiki/Michinoku_Pro_Wrestling" title="Michinoku Pro Wrestling">Michinoku Pro Wrestling</a>. In that promotion, he joined with <a href="/wiki/Taka_Michinoku" title="Taka Michinoku">Taka Michinoku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dick_Togo" title="Dick Togo">Dick Togo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_Teioh" title="Men's Teioh">Men's Teioh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kaz_Hayashi" title="Kaz Hayashi">Shiryu</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kai_En_Tai" title="Kai En Tai">Kai En Tai</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#S" title="List of professional wrestling terms">stable</a>. Funaki also wrestled in the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Wrestling_Association" title="Universal Wrestling Association">Universal Wrestling Association</a> (UWA) where he won the <a href="/wiki/UWA_World_Middleweight_Championship" title="UWA World Middleweight Championship">UWA World Middleweight Championship</a> from <a href="/wiki/Pantera_(wrestler)" title="Pantera (wrestler)">El Pantera</a> on March 19, 1997, in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uwa_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-uwa-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Federation.2FEntertainment">World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Kai_En_Tai_.281998.E2.80.932001.29">Kai En Tai (1998–2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kai En Tai (1998–2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kai_En_Tai" title="Kai En Tai">Kai En Tai</a></div>
<p>In March 1998, Funaki, along with Teioh and Togo, joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">World Wrestling Federation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-oww-1">[1]</a></sup> Initially, they feuded with Taka Michinoku and defeated him and his partner <a href="/wiki/John_Layfield" title="John Layfield">Justin Bradshaw</a> in a 3-on-2 <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Handicap_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">handicap match</a> at <a href="/wiki/Over_the_Edge:_In_Your_House" title="Over the Edge: In Your House">Over the Edge: In Your House</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They lost to Michinoku and <a href="/wiki/The_Headbangers" title="The Headbangers">The Headbangers</a> at <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Ring_(1998)" title="King of the Ring (1998)">King of the Ring 1998</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">six-man tag team match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Michinoku eventually <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Turn" title="List of professional wrestling terms">turned</a> <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">villainous</a> and joined the group. At <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam_(1998)" title="SummerSlam (1998)">SummerSlam 1998</a>, Kai En Tai lost to <a href="/wiki/The_Oddities_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Oddities (professional wrestling)">Oddities</a> members <a href="/wiki/Robert_Maillet" title="Robert Maillet">Kurrgan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paulo_C%C3%A9sar_da_Silva_(fighter)" title="Paulo César da Silva (fighter)">Giant Silva</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Tenta" title="John Tenta">Golga</a> in a handicap match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Over time, the stable turned into a tag team, as in 1999, Teioh and Togo (and manager Yamaguchi-san) were given their releases. Michinoku and Funaki were often used for comedic purposes, often having their pre-match promos dubbed—while Michinoku would "deliver" the majority of dialogue for the duo, Funaki would "respond" strongly with a simple "INDEED".</p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_2000" title="WrestleMania 2000">WrestleMania 2000</a>, Funaki participated in a 15-minute <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_wrestling#Hardcore_battle_royal" title="Hardcore wrestling">hardcore battle royal</a> for the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hardcore_Championship" title="WWE Hardcore Championship">Hardcore Championship</a>. Funaki pinned Viscera after a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_aerial_techniques#Diving_shoulder_block" title="Professional wrestling aerial techniques">diving shoulder block</a> from Bradshaw but he was later pinned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rodney_Leinhardt" title="Rodney Leinhardt">Rodney</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bob_Holly" title="Bob Holly">Hardcore Holly</a> ultimately won the match and became the official champion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hardcore_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-hardcore-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Unforgiven_(2000)" title="Unforgiven (2000)">Unforgiven 2000</a>, Funaki once again participated in a hardcore battle royal which was won by the defending champion <a href="/wiki/Steve_Blackman" title="Steve Blackman">Steve Blackman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, Kai En Tai started participating in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Dark_match" title="List of professional wrestling terms">non-televised matches</a> before pay-per-view events such as <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(2001)" title="Royal Rumble (2001)">Royal Rumble 2001</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2001)" title="Judgment Day (2001)">Judgment Day 2001</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SmackDown.21.27s_.231_Announcer_.282001.E2.80.932003.29"><i>SmackDown!'</i>s #1 Announcer (2001–2003)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: SmackDown!'s #1 Announcer (2001–2003)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Taka Michinoku left the WWF in the fall of 2001, resulting in Funaki moving to singles competition. At <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series_(2001)" title="Survivor Series (2001)">Survivor Series</a>, Funaki participated in an Immunity Battle Royal which was won by <a href="/wiki/Test_(wrestler)" title="Test (wrestler)">Test</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2002, Funaki moved to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown!</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Brand_Extension" title="WWE Brand Extension">brand</a> after WWF roster was split into two brands. He turned <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">face</a> and found himself as a lower card wrestler and adopted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#G" title="List of professional wrestling terms">gimmick</a> of "SmackDown!'s number one announcer", performing backstage interviews between matches, while competing in the <a href="/wiki/Cruiserweight_(professional_wrestling)" title="Cruiserweight (professional wrestling)">cruiserweight division</a>. As part of SmackDown! brand, Funaki wrestled for most of the time on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Velocity" title="WWE Velocity">Velocity</a></i>. At <a href="/wiki/WWE_Rebellion#2002" title="WWE Rebellion">Rebellion</a>, Funaki defeated <a href="/wiki/Crash_Holly" title="Crash Holly">Crash Holly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Vengeance_(2003)" title="Vengeance (2003)">Vengeance 2003</a>, Funaki participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acolytes_Protection_Agency" title="Acolytes Protection Agency">APA</a> Invitational Bar Room Brawl which was won by Bradshaw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cruiserweight_Champion_.282004.E2.80.932005.29">Cruiserweight Champion (2004–2005)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cruiserweight Champion (2004–2005)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 4, 2004 episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, Funaki lost to <a href="/wiki/WWE_Cruiserweight_Championship_(1991%E2%80%932007)" title="WWE Cruiserweight Championship (1991–2007)">Cruiserweight Champion</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chavo_Guerrero,_Jr." title="Chavo Guerrero, Jr.">Chavo Guerrero</a> in a non-title match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On the March 11 episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, Funaki participated in an 8-man cruiserweight tag team match teaming with fellow cruiserweights <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rey_Mysterio,_Jr." title="Rey Mysterio, Jr.">Rey Mysterio</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Altimo_Drag%C3%B3n" title="Último Dragón">Último Dragón</a> and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Kidman" title="Billy Kidman">Billy Kidman</a> against <a href="/wiki/Yoshihiro_Tajiri" title="Yoshihiro Tajiri">Tajiri</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Yun" title="James Yun">Akio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Sakoda" title="Ryan Sakoda">Sakoda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Noble" title="Jamie Noble">Jamie Noble</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XX" title="WrestleMania XX">WrestleMania XX</a>, Funaki participated in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Gauntlet_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Cruiserweight Open</a> for the Cruiserweight Championship but was pinned by Jamie Noble.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the December 9 episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, Funaki won a cruiserweight over the top rope number one contender's battle royal which included Chavo Guerrero, <a href="/wiki/Paul_London" title="Paul London">Paul London</a>, Billy Kidman, Akio, <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Moore" title="Shannon Moore">Shannon Moore</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Maritato" title="James Maritato">Nunzio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Armageddon_(2004)" title="Armageddon (2004)">Armageddon 2004</a>, Funaki defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_Hyson" title="Matt Hyson">Spike Dudley</a> to win the Cruiserweight Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Funaki successfully defended the title against Spike Dudley,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Akio<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and Nunzio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> His last successful title defense was against Chavo Guerrero on the February 10, 2005 episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, notably held in Funaki's birth country, Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cruiserweight_Division_.282005.E2.80.932008.29">Cruiserweight Division (2005–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cruiserweight Division (2005–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Funaki lost the championship to Chavo Guerrero in a six-man Cruiserweight Open match at <a href="/wiki/No_Way_Out_(2005)" title="No Way Out (2005)">No Way Out 2005</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Armageddon_(2005)" title="Armageddon (2005)">Armageddon 2005</a>, he lost to Jamie Noble in a match on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Heat" title="WWE Heat">Heat</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> During an interview with then Cruiserweight Champion <a href="/wiki/Kid_Kash" title="Kid Kash">Kid Kash</a> on an episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Friday_Night_SmackDown" title="WWE Friday Night SmackDown">SmackDown!</a></i>, Funaki was assaulted by Kash, who gave him a <a href="/wiki/Brainbuster" title="Brainbuster">brainbuster</a> onto the interview platform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(2006)" title="Royal Rumble (2006)">2006 Royal Rumble</a>, Funaki faced Kid Kash in a match for the title, when he was entered into a Cruiserweight Open along with four other former Cruiserweight champions, including the champion Kash. Funaki was pinned in this one fall match by then <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a> superstar <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Helms" title="Gregory Helms">Gregory Helms</a>, who won the match and title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/No_Way_Out_(2006)" title="No Way Out (2006)">No Way Out 2006</a>, Funaki participated in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Basic_non-elimination_matches" title="Professional wrestling match types">Nine Man Cruiserweight Match</a> for the WWE Cruiserweight Championship but Helms retained the title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Since that time Funaki, would regularly team with <a href="/wiki/Scotty_2_Hotty" title="Scotty 2 Hotty">Scotty 2 Hotty</a> during episodes of <i>Velocity</i> and <i>SmackDown!</i>. During one such <i>Velocity</i> taping, however, Funaki suffered a <a href="/wiki/Concussion#Grades" title="Concussion">2nd Grade concussion</a>, where he was unconscious for just under two minutes. Funaki would resume wrestling later that year, forming a tag team of sorts with Scotty 2 Hotty, which was mainly used to put over debuting wrestlers or tag teams.</p>
<p>Funaki made a one off appearance at the Puroresu King Indy Summit, on December 31, 2006, reuniting with <a href="/wiki/Kai_En_Tai" title="Kai En Tai">Kai En Tai DX</a> in what was being billed as a Kai En Tai DX Revival Ten-Man Tag Match; Funaki reteamed with Taka Michinoku, <a href="/wiki/Kaz_Hayashi" title="Kaz Hayashi">Kaz Hayashi</a>, Dick Togo and Men's Teioh to face five other wrestlers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Funaki returned to WWE TV on February 16, 2007 on <i>SmackDown!</i> in a brawl that involved the other <i>SmackDown!</i> cruiserweights and the then-Cruiserweight Champion Gregory Helms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/No_Way_Out_(2007)" title="No Way Out (2007)">No Way Out 2007</a>, Funaki participated in a Cruiserweight Open match for the WWE Cruiserweight Championship but was pinned by the champion Helms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On May 18 episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, Funaki was made short work of by recently returned superstar <a href="/wiki/Mark_Henry" title="Mark Henry">Mark Henry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> On the June 29 episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, Funaki got a shot at the Cruiserweight title against Cruiserweight Champion Chavo Guerrero and Jamie Noble, but came up short.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/The_Great_American_Bash_(2007)" title="The Great American Bash (2007)">The Great American Bash</a>, he participated in a match for the Cruiserweight Championship which <a href="/wiki/Hornswoggle" title="Hornswoggle">Hornswoggle</a> won.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On December 14, on an episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, he wrestled in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squash_(professional_wrestling)" title="Squash (professional wrestling)">squash</a> match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_Copeland" title="Adam Copeland">Edge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> He also made a televised appearance on January 4, 2008 episode of <i>SmackDown!</i> where he lost to Chavo Guerrero in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Beat_the_Clock_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Beat the Clock match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Funaki <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legit_(professional_wrestling)" title="Legit (professional wrestling)">legitimately</a> broke his nose at a joint SmackDown/ECW taping in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston, Texas</a> during a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#D" title="List of professional wrestling terms">dark match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kozlov" title="Vladimir Kozlov">Vladimir Kozlov</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Kung_Fu_Naki_.282008.E2.80.932010.29">Kung Fu Naki (2008–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Kung Fu Naki (2008–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On October 10, 2008, Funaki revealed his full name to be "Kung Fu Naki" during a backstage segment with <a href="/wiki/Ron_Killings" title="Ron Killings">R-Truth</a>. This was used as the basis for a gimmick change for Funaki; he then came to the ring dressed in a <a href="/wiki/Keikogi" title="Keikogi">gi</a> and tweaked his moveset to incorporate theatrical martial arts moves. His first match as Kung Fu Naki saw him scoring a pinfall win over <a href="/wiki/Montel_Vontavious_Porter" title="Montel Vontavious Porter">Montel Vontavious Porter</a> (MVP) and <a href="/wiki/Shelton_Benjamin" title="Shelton Benjamin">Shelton Benjamin</a> in a tag team match with R-Truth. Kung Fu Naki then defeated MVP in a singles match on the November 14, 2008 episode of <i>SmackDown</i> when <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a> interfered and distracted MVP. His first loss in the character was against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWE Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Edge_(wrestler)" title="Edge (wrestler)">Edge</a> on the December 5, 2008 episode of <i>SmackDown</i>. He was inexplicably absent from WWE action until <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XXVI" title="WrestleMania XXVI">WrestleMania XXVI</a>, where he took part in the 26 Man <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_Royal" title="Battle Royal">Battle Royal</a> match, which was won by Yoshi Tatsu. On April 22, 2010, WWE released Funaki, ending his 12-year stint with the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independent_circuit_.282010.E2.80.932014.29">Independent circuit (2010–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Independent circuit (2010–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his WWE release, <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Zero1" title="Pro Wrestling Zero1">Pro Wrestling Zero1</a> announced they had signed Funaki to return to Japan and team with his former student <a href="/wiki/Ikuto_Hidaka" title="Ikuto Hidaka">Ikuto Hidaka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zero1_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-zero1-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 4, 2010, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Antonio,_Texas" title="San Antonio, Texas">San Antonio, Texas</a>–based Branded Outlaw Wrestling (BOW) announced that it had reached a working agreement with Funaki to host his new professional wrestling school, named the Funaki Dojo. The school's first class started on January 15, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 6, 2013 at TNA One Night Only: World Cup of Wrestling, as a part of Team International, Funaki and <a href="/wiki/Petey_Williams" title="Petey Williams">Petey Williams</a> lost to Team <a href="/wiki/Aces_%26_Eights" title="Aces &amp; Eights">Aces &amp; Eights</a>' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drew_Hankinson" title="Drew Hankinson">D.O.C</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Knox" title="Mike Knox">Knux</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_WWE">Return to WWE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Return to WWE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sporadic_appearances_.282011.E2.88.922016.29">Sporadic appearances (2011−2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sporadic appearances (2011−2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Funaki cameoed for WWE as a special guest referee during their <a href="/wiki/House_show" title="House show">house shows</a> in Japan in 2011 and 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>Funaki made a special appearance in a video as a translator for <a href="/wiki/Kenta_Kobayashi" title="Kenta Kobayashi">Kenta</a>'s first WWE interview with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renee_Paquette" title="Renee Paquette">Renee Young</a>. In September, Funaki returned to WWE's developmental program <a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT" title="WWE NXT">NXT</a> to feud with <a href="/wiki/The_Ascension_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Ascension (professional wrestling)">The Ascension</a> alongside Kenta, who was now going by the name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hideo_Itami" title="Hideo Itami">Hideo Itami</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Commentator_.282016.E2.80.93present.29">Commentator (2016–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Commentator (2016–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Funaki makes an appearance at the start of the <a href="/wiki/Edge_and_Christian" title="Edge and Christian">Edge and Christian</a> Show by introducing the hosts, however they use a voice over from <a href="/wiki/Howard_Finkel" title="Howard Finkel">Howard Finkel</a>.</p>
<p>On the April 1, 2016 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NXT_Takeover:_Dallas" title="NXT Takeover: Dallas">NXT Takeover: Dallas</a> special on the WWE Network, Funaki appeared in a backstage segment with <a href="/wiki/Shinsuke_Nakamura" title="Shinsuke Nakamura">Shinsuke Nakamura</a> as he was making his way into the arena. He later appeared at ringside that same night sitting next to International Japan star <a href="/wiki/Kota_Ibushi" title="Kota Ibushi">Kota Ibushi</a>.</p>
<p>Funaki currently provides <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese language</a> commentary for WWE Pay-Per-Views, beginning with <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_32" title="WrestleMania 32">WrestleMania 32</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shoichi_Funaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along with Japanese and English, Funaki is fluent in French, German, Portuguese, and Spanish.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. Funaki was said to have received <a href="/wiki/Growth_hormone" title="Growth hormone">somatropin</a> in March 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
