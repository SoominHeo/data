<p><b>Operation Hong Kil Dong</b> was the largest South Korean operation of the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>. The 48-day-long operation was claimed by South Korea as a major success as they claimed to have forces thwarted VPA/NLF infiltration into friendly areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The results of the operation were a kill ratio of 24:1 in the Korean's report: 638 Vietnamese to 26 South Koreans. 98 crew-served and 359 individual weapons were captured in the aftermath.</p>
