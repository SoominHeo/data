<p>The <b>National Museum of Natural History</b> (<b>NMNH</b>) was a museum focusing on nature, located in New Delhi, India. Established in 1972 and opened in 1978, the museum functioned under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Environment_and_Forests_(India)" title="Ministry of Environment and Forests (India)">Ministry of Environment and Forests</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">government of India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-1">[1]</a></sup> The museum was situated on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barakhamba_Road" title="Barakhamba Road">Barakhamba Road</a> at Tansen Marg in central New Delhi,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zee_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-zee-2">[2]</a></sup> across from the Embassy of Nepal, near the <a href="/wiki/Connaught_Place,_New_Delhi" title="Connaught Place, New Delhi">Connaught Place</a> metro station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-info_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-info-3">[3]</a></sup> On 26 April 2016, the museum building and its entire collection were destroyed by a fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fire_guts_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-fire_guts-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">Mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Museum_of_Natural_History,_New_Delhi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The NMNH's mission was to promote environmental education, both at NMNH in the capital and at Regional Museums of Natural History in other parts of the country; to provide resources such as school loan kits for schools to use in environmental education; to co-ordinate natural history projects with other agencies and organisations, both nationally and internationally; and to conduct natural history research.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The NMNH's director, B Venugopal, also emphasised "<a href="/wiki/Masterpieces_of_the_Oral_and_Intangible_Heritage_of_Humanity" title="Masterpieces of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity">intangible natural heritage</a>" (a concept invented by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>) at the museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hindu_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-hindu-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Museum_of_Natural_History,_New_Delhi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The NMNH was created in 1972 as part of the celebration of the 25th year of <a href="/wiki/Indian_independence_movement" title="Indian independence movement">India's independence</a>. <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi" title="Indira Gandhi">Indira Gandhi</a>, then Prime Minister of India, said that India needed such a museum "to promote environmental awareness".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-about-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zee_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-zee-2">[2]</a></sup> After several years of development of its building and exhibits, the NMNH opened in 1978, on <a href="/wiki/World_Environment_Day" title="World Environment Day">World Environment Day</a> (5 June).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zee_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-zee-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following a negative government report in 2012 regarding the building's poor state of maintenance,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fire_guts_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-fire_guts-4">[4]</a></sup> in July 2015, plans were made to relocate the museum to a <a href="/wiki/Indian_rupee" title="Indian rupee">₹</a> 2.50 billion ($39.4M, €36.4M, or £25.3M as of July 2015) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_certified" title="Green certified">green-certified</a> building at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bhairon_Marg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bhairon Marg (page does not exist)">Bhairon Marg</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zee_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-zee-2">[2]</a></sup> where "The <a href="/wiki/National_Zoological_Park_Delhi" title="National Zoological Park Delhi">zoological park</a> is close by, [and] so are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Crafts_Museum" title="National Crafts Museum">National Crafts Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Purana_Qila" title="Purana Qila">Purana Qila</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Centre,_Delhi" title="National Science Centre, Delhi">National Science Centre</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-times-7">[7]</a></sup> Regional Museums of Natural History are located in Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Gangtok, Mysuru, and Sawai Madhopur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hindu_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-hindu-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April_2016_fire">April 2016 fire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Museum_of_Natural_History,_New_Delhi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April 2016 fire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the early morning of 26 April 2016, a fire broke out in the museum and destroyed the entire collection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fire_guts_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-fire_guts-4">[4]</a></sup> The fire, which began around 01:30 on the sixth floor of the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Indian_Chambers_of_Commerce_%26_Industry" title="Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce &amp; Industry">Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce &amp; Industry</a> (FICCI) building where the museum had been housed, eventually reached the second floor and destroyed all of the museum's exhibits before fire services were able to bring the flames under control. Around 200 firefighters and 35 fire engines were called; they took more than three and a half hours to extinguish the fire. Six people who became trapped in the building were later sent to a hospital suffering from smoke inhalation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IE_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-IE-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The cause of the fire is still unknown, although the fire's spread was fuelled by museum specimens and the woodwork holding them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IE_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-IE-8">[8]</a></sup> Reports indicate that the devastating effect of the blaze was due to the building's sprinkler system being out of order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fire_guts_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-fire_guts-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exhibits">Exhibits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Museum_of_Natural_History,_New_Delhi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Exhibits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Exhibits at the NMNH were focused on India's plants, animals and mineral wealth, and were divided into four main exhibit galleries: "Cell: The Basic Unit of Life", "Conservation", "Introduction to Natural History", and "Nature's Network: Ecology". The museum had an extensive collection of films on wildlife, ecology, conservation and the environment in general, as well as rare biological specimens, including <a href="/wiki/Herpetology" title="Herpetology">herpetological</a> specimens, dinosaur fossils and mounted animals. There were also galleries on the origin and evolution of life, conservation of nature, the food chain and exhibits of flora and fauna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IT_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-IT-10">[10]</a></sup> A 160 million-year-old fossil of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauropod" title="Sauropod">sauropod</a> and stuffed animals by renowned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mysuru" title="Mysuru">Mysuru</a>-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxidermist" title="Taxidermist">taxidermists</a> <a href="/wiki/Van_Ingen_%26_Van_Ingen" title="Van Ingen &amp; Van Ingen">Van Ingen &amp; Van Ingen</a> were part of the museum collection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TOI_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-TOI-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The museum also featured a Discovery Room and Activity Room, specifically designed for children, where designated specimens could be handled by visitors. Guided tours, and specially designed resources for disabled visitors, were also available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-info_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-info-3">[3]</a></sup> Specific exhibits included a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauropod" title="Sauropod">sauropod</a> fossil, "a collection of bird eggs including those of the <a href="/wiki/Ostrich" title="Ostrich">ostrich</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_vulture" title="Indian vulture">long-billed vulture</a>", and "stuffed animals including various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_cats" title="Big cats">big cats</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-times-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Plans for future exhibitions, as of 2015, had included "climate change, global warming, combating <a href="/wiki/Desertification" title="Desertification">desertification</a>, tsunami, [and] depletion of [the] <a href="/wiki/Ozone_layer" title="Ozone layer">ozone layer</a>" as well as "more digital displays ... [a]nd an <a href="/wiki/IMAX" title="IMAX">IMAX</a> theatre".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-times-7">[7]</a></sup> The museum was also acquiring dinosaur egg fossils from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narmada_Valley" title="Narmada Valley">Narmada Valley</a>, Gujarat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whale_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-whale-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
