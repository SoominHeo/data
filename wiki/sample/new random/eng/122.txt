<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Weifeng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Li Weifeng started his football career with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Locomotive" title="Tianjin Locomotive">Tianjin Locomotive</a>'s youth academy before he was spotted by the Chinese national youth program to study football abroad in a training program sponsored by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shenzhen_Shangqingyin" title="Shenzhen Shangqingyin">Jianlibao</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This then saw him called up to the <a href="/wiki/China_national_under-20_football_team" title="China national under-20 football team">Chinese under-20 national team</a> and given a chance to play in the <a href="/wiki/1997_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="1997 FIFA World Youth Championship">1997 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>. Upon his return from the tournament Li was returned to his initial youth team of Tianjin Locomotive until top tier club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shenzhen_Asia_Travel_F.C." title="Shenzhen Asia Travel F.C.">Shenzhen Ping'an</a> showed an interest in him, not wanting to lose a promising young player a contract dispute would arise until a fee of 800,000 yuan was reportedly agree upon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Jia-A_League_1998" title="Chinese Jia-A League 1998">1998 league season</a> he made his debut for the club and quickly established himself at the heart of the teams defense, guiding them to a 12th-place finish and doing enough to avoid relegation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the following seasons with Shenzhen, Li establish himself as an integral member of the team and would go on to represent his country in the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a>. With the exposure of the tournament, a short trial at <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> immediately followed as part of an Everton deal with Chinese sponsor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kejian" title="Kejian">Kejian</a>. His time at Everton he only made two appearances, playing once in the league against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> and once in the <a href="/wiki/EFL_Cup" title="EFL Cup">League Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Wrexham_A.F.C." title="Wrexham A.F.C.">Wrexham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was unable to make an impact at the club and returned to Shenzhen after the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_FA_Premier_League" title="2002–03 FA Premier League">2002-03 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Jia-A_League_2003" title="Chinese Jia-A League 2003">2003 league season</a>, Li returned from his loan spell at Everton and was immediately incorporated back into the team. He was soon made club captain by then manager <a href="/wiki/Zhu_Guanghu" title="Zhu Guanghu">Zhu Guanghu</a> and within his second spell at the club he would lead them to the <a href="/wiki/2004_Chinese_Super_League" title="2004 Chinese Super League">2004 Chinese Super League</a> title for the first time in the clubs history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After that success, Zhu Guanghu was offered the <a href="/wiki/China_national_football_team" title="China national football team">Chinese Head coach</a> position and <a href="/wiki/Chi_Shangbin" title="Chi Shangbin">Chi Shangbin</a> came in as his replacement. Unfortunately for Chi, results significantly deteriorated under his reign and Li along with several other members of the team in <a href="/wiki/Li_Yi_(footballer)" title="Li Yi (footballer)">Li Yi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yang_Chen_(footballer,_born_1974)" title="Yang Chen (footballer, born 1974)">Yang Chen</a> publicly criticized his management which resulted in his resignation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the beginning of the 2006 season, Li left Shenzhen due to the club's financial difficulties and followed many of his teammates out of the club. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanghai_Shenhua_F.C." title="Shanghai Shenhua F.C.">Shanghai Shenhua</a> bought him for 6 million yuan despite reported interest from <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> side <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2008, Li transferred from Shanghai to <a href="/wiki/Wuhan_Optics_Valley_F.C." title="Wuhan Optics Valley F.C.">Wuhan Guanggu</a> due to a lack of playing time for the league runners-up, reuniting him with his former manager Zhu Guanghu who also coached him in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_PR_national_football_team" title="China PR national football team">Chinese national team</a> as well as in Shenzhen. Soon after the transfer, Li was involved in an on-the-field incident which resulted in him being suspended for eight games by the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Football_Association" title="Chinese Football Association">Chinese Football Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Already on the verge of relegation, Wuhan amounted protests against this ruling which the club deemed to be unjust and extremely damaging to its chance to survive in the top flight. After its efforts were proven to be futile, Wuhan withdrew from the league and was disbanded and Li did not appear in any games for the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because of Wuhan's withdrawal, most of its non-local and highly paid players were put on the transfer list at the end of the season with Li among one of them. Because of his reputation, high wage demands, gigantic transfer fee, and the unserved eight game suspension, Li was a hard commodity to move despite being perceived as heads and shoulders above the rest of the Chinese defenders. But the new <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> rule came to his rescue as it allowed all tournament participants to have one foreign Asian player. Attracting heavy interest from both the Japanese and Korean leagues, Li moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-League" title="K-League">K-League</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suwon_Samsung_Bluewings_F.C." title="Suwon Samsung Bluewings F.C.">Suwon Samsung Bluewings</a> in January 2009, signing a two-year contract for a reported $400,000 and reuniting him with another one of his former managers. Some pundits suggested that Li moved on a free transfer because Wuhan's withdrawal made all of its players free agents under FIFA's rules. However, Wuhan immediately released statements announcing its intention to obstruct the move if it was not at least partly remunerated and media reports stated that Li would pay his former club himself in order to play for the Korean outfit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In his debut appearance, he was sent off against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sparta_Prague" title="Sparta Prague">Sparta Prague</a> in a friendly match in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>; however, Li redeemed himself in his first official match for Suwon in an AFC Champions League match in a 4-1 win against <a href="/wiki/Kashima_Antlers" title="Kashima Antlers">Kashima Antlers</a> by scoring the opening goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 January 2011, Li signed with <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Teda_F.C." title="Tianjin Teda F.C.">Tianjin Teda</a> and was named as the club captain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On 20 July 2015, Li was released by the club midway through the 2015 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Li was appointed as the vice managing director of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Quanjian" title="Tianjin Quanjian">Tianjin Quanjian</a> in September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Weifeng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Li made his first appearance for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_PR_national_football_team" title="China PR national football team">Chinese national team</a> on 22 November 1998 in a friendly match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korea_Republic_national_football_team" title="Korea Republic national football team">South Korea</a>, which ended in a 0-0 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> His performances for the national team would see him called up to China's squads for the <a href="/wiki/2000_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2000 AFC Asian Cup">2000 AFC Asian Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a>. In 2003, he was promoted to team captain by then manager <a href="/wiki/Arie_Haan" title="Arie Haan">Arie Haan</a> and would lead China to a runners-up position at the <a href="/wiki/2004_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2004 AFC Asian Cup">2004 AFC Asian Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In September 2006, he was banned from the national team for attacking an opposing player and hence earning his sixth red card in fourteen months during an <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> game with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanghai_Shenhua_F.C." title="Shanghai Shenhua F.C.">Shanghai Shenhua</a>. His position as captain of the national team was stripped and later assigned to <a href="/wiki/Zheng_Zhi" title="Zheng Zhi">Zheng Zhi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The ban was removed after a year, but since then he never had any significant role in the national team anymore. He returned as Captain during the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(AFC)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)">2014 World Cup qualifying</a>, but his return could not help China pass through the Third Round, after two shocking losses to <a href="/wiki/Iraq_national_football_team" title="Iraq national football team">Iraq</a>.</p>
