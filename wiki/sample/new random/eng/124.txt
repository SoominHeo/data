<p><b>Jung San</b> (Korean: 정산; born <span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1985-01-23</span>)</span>January 23, 1985), more commonly known by his stage name <b>San E</b> (Korean: 산이), is a South Korean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a> contracted under <a href="/wiki/Brand_New_Music" title="Brand New Music">Brand New Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> He was formerly signed to <a href="/wiki/JYP_Entertainment" title="JYP Entertainment">JYP Entertainment</a>, where he was the label's first solo rapper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-2">[2]</a></sup> He is one of the most successful rappers in mainstream Korean music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>San E was born on January 23, 1985 in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. He moved with his family to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia,_USA" title="Atlanta, Georgia, USA">Atlanta, Georgia, USA</a>, when he was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_school_student_(United_States)" title="Middle school student (United States)">middle school student</a> because his parents had been struggling financially as a result of the <a href="/wiki/1997_Asian_financial_crisis" title="1997 Asian financial crisis">1997 Asian financial crisis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a>, where he majored in graphic design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008-2009:_Mixtapes_and_underground_fame">2008-2009: Mixtapes and underground fame</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008-2009: Mixtapes and underground fame">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>San E released his first mixtapes, <i>Ready To Be Signed</i> and <i>Ready To Be Famous</i> in 2008 and 2009, respectively. As a way to draw attention to his self-released music, San E jokingly dissed established underground rapper <a href="/wiki/Verbal_Jint" title="Verbal Jint">Verbal Jint</a> in one of his songs. The diss brought him attention from the Korean hip hop community and from Verbal Jint himself, who invited San E to join his hip hop crew, Overclass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-2012:_JYP_Entertainment_and_Everybody_Ready.3F">2010-2012: JYP Entertainment and <i>Everybody Ready?</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010-2012: JYP Entertainment and Everybody Ready?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Still unsigned, San E won Best Hip Hop Song at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Korean_Music_Awards" title="Korean Music Awards">Korean Music Awards</a> for the track "Rap Genius."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Soon after, he became the first solo rapper to sign with <a href="/wiki/JYP_Entertainment" title="JYP Entertainment">JYP Entertainment</a>, one of the "big three" records labels in the k-pop industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-2">[2]</a></sup> That September, he released his first mini-album, <i>Everybody Ready?,</i> which featured fellow JYP Entertainment artists <a href="/wiki/Min_(singer)" title="Min (singer)">Min</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Miss_A" title="Miss A">Miss A</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Joo_(singer)" title="Joo (singer)">Joo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yenny" title="Yenny">Yeeun</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girls" title="Wonder Girls">Wonder Girls</a>). He debuted several days later on the television music show <i><a href="/wiki/M_Countdown" title="M Countdown">M Countdown</a></i> with his single "Tasty San," featuring Min.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In November, San E started another round of promotions for the single, "LoveSick," the music video for which starred label mate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sohee" title="Sohee">Sohee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girls" title="Wonder Girls">Wonder Girls</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, San E released several singles, including “Please Don’t Go," a collaboration with rapper <a href="/wiki/Outsider_(rapper)" title="Outsider (rapper)">Outsider</a> and singer <a href="/wiki/Lee_Chang-min_(singer)" title="Lee Chang-min (singer)">Lee Changmin</a> (of <a href="/wiki/2AM_(band)" title="2AM (band)">2AM</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The song reached #14 on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Digital_Chart" title="Gaon Digital Chart">Gaon Digital Chart</a>, making it his highest charted song while contracted under JYP Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He also collaborated that year with <a href="/wiki/Verbal_Jint" title="Verbal Jint">Verbal Jint</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beenzino" title="Beenzino">Beenzino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swings_(rapper)" title="Swings (rapper)">Swings</a>, and other artists on the single "Stand Up, Japan!" which benefited Japan’s relief efforts after the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">Tohoku Tsunami</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.932014:_Brand_New_Music_and_top_ten_success">2013–2014: Brand New Music and top ten success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–2014: Brand New Music and top ten success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2013, San E ended his contract with JYP Entertainment, saying that he was leaving on good terms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> That June, he joined hip hop label <a href="/wiki/Brand_New_Music" title="Brand New Music">Brand New Music</a>, home to his Overclass crew mate <a href="/wiki/Verbal_Jint" title="Verbal Jint">Verbal Jint</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> San E experienced his first #1 hit on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Digital_Chart" title="Gaon Digital Chart">Gaon Digital Chart</a> with the song, "Story of Someone I Know," from the album <i>'Not' Based on the True Story.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> His next three singles, "Where Did You Sleep?" (feat. <a href="/wiki/Verbal_Jint" title="Verbal Jint">Verbal Jint</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swings_(rapper)" title="Swings (rapper)">Swings</a>), "Break-Up Dinner" (feat. Sanchez of <a href="/wiki/Phantom_(band)" title="Phantom (band)">Phantom</a>), and "What's Wrong With Me" (feat. Kang Min Hee), all reached the top ten of the Gaon Digital Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>San E's success continued with the single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Sweetness_(song)" title="A Midsummer Night's Sweetness (song)">A Midsummer Night's Sweetness</a>," a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Raina_(singer)" title="Raina (singer)">Raina</a> of the k-pop group <a href="/wiki/After_School_(band)" title="After School (band)">After School</a>. The song was an instant hit and achieved #1 on ten music charts shortly after its debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The song went on to win awards that year at the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Chart_K-Pop_Awards" title="Gaon Chart K-Pop Awards">Gaon Chart K-Pop Awards</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MelOn_Music_Awards" title="MelOn Music Awards">MelOn Music Awards</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Music_Awards" title="Seoul Music Awards">Seoul Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> His next single, "Body Language" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Bumkey" title="Bumkey">Bumkey</a>), was also a #1 hit, topping both the Gaon Digital Chart and seven Korean real-time music charts shortly after its release, despite its 19+ rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
