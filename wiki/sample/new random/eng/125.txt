<p><i><b>Half-Life 2: Deathmatch</b></i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiplayer" title="Multiplayer">multiplayer</a> <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Valve_Corporation" title="Valve Corporation">Valve Corporation</a>. Released on <a href="/wiki/Steam_(software)" title="Steam (software)">Steam</a> on November 30, 2004, it uses many of the assets from <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_2" title="Half-Life 2">Half-Life 2</a></i> and its <a href="/wiki/Source_(game_engine)" title="Source (game engine)">Source engine</a>. It features new levels, optimized for multiplayer arena play, and a few new weapons. Also included are portions of the game's source code, which were the basis for many early Source-based multiplayer modifications. The game is the successor to the popular <a href="/wiki/Half-Life_(video_game)#Multiplayer" title="Half-Life (video game)">multiplayer component of the original <i>Half-Life</i></a>, but is offered as a separate product from <i>Half-Life 2</i>. <i>Deathmatch</i>, like <i>Half-Life</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s multiplayer, does not develop any part of the plot or story of the <a href="/wiki/Half-Life_(series)" title="Half-Life (series)"><i>Half-Life</i> series</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Half-Life_2:_Deathmatch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Deathmatch">Deathmatch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Half-Life_2:_Deathmatch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Deathmatch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="384" data-file-width="683" height="112" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png/200px-Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png/300px-Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png/400px-Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Half_Life_2-_Deathmatch_Gravity_Gun.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Gravity gun in <i>Half Life 2: Deathmatch</i>.</div>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deathmatch_(gaming)" title="Deathmatch (gaming)">Deathmatch</a> includes some notable features such as instant respawn; weapons that have specific spawn points; and special abilities (sprint, flashlight, etc.). The aim of the deathmatch is simple: the player must kill other players to score points. If the player accidentally self-kills, they lose points. If a player is killed, they will respawn with 100 health points and the default spawn weapons, but will lose all the weapons and ammunition acquired before being killed.</p>
<p>The game is server based and each server contains different rules for each round therefore there is no default time limit or kill limit for each round.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_Deathmatch">Team Deathmatch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Half-Life_2:_Deathmatch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Team Deathmatch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deathmatch_(gaming)#Variations" title="Deathmatch (gaming)">team deathmatch</a> mode, players are organized in two teams, Rebels and <a href="/wiki/Combine_(Half-Life)" title="Combine (Half-Life)">Combine</a>, both of them with different characters that appear exactly as they do in <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_2" title="Half-Life 2">Half-Life 2</a></i>. In the gaming aspect, team deathmatch has almost the same rules as deathmatch mode, except for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Instead of only one player winning the round, the team with the higher score wins.</li>
<li>If <a href="/wiki/Friendly_fire" title="Friendly fire">friendly fire</a> is enabled, one point is deducted each time a teammate is killed.</li>
<li>If the player selected a Rebel model for his character, and they are taken to the Combine team, they will get a random model from the list of Combine models. Nevertheless, if the player dislikes that character, they are able to choose another one from the list.</li>
<li>If the teams are unbalanced, the team with fewer players won't get players from the other team, (unless the server owner had 'Auto-Balance' enabled) instead, they will get new players connected to the server.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Half-Life_2:_Deathmatch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon the release and subsequent critical reception of <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_2" title="Half-Life 2">Half-Life 2</a></i>, reviewers expressed disappointment with the game's lack of multiplayer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Two weeks after the initial release of <i>Half-Life 2</i>, Valve revealed and released <i>Half-Life 2: Deathmatch</i> on <a href="/wiki/Steam_(software)" title="Steam (software)">Steam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steamann_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-steamann-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Deathmatch</i> was released simultaneously with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Source_SDK" title="Source SDK">Source SDK</a> as a means of promoting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_modification" title="Game modification">game modifications</a> built upon the platform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Post-release, the game was supported with new maps from Valve as well as updates to the game and its engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Valve's <a href="/wiki/The_Orange_Box" title="The Orange Box">The <i>Orange Box</i></a> does not include <i>Half-Life 2: Deathmatch</i>, however, the game was upgraded to use the Orange Box version of the Source engine in September 2010. Valve announced a free promotional offer on January 10, 2008, which allowed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NVIDIA" title="NVIDIA">NVIDIA</a> graphics card users to download and play <i>Half-Life 2: Deathmatch</i> along with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portal:First_Slice" title="Portal:First Slice">Portal: First Slice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_2:_Lost_Coast" title="Half-Life 2: Lost Coast">Half-Life 2: Lost Coast</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peggle_Extreme" title="Peggle Extreme">Peggle Extreme</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eurogamer_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eurogamer-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2010, the game was released via Steam for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OS_X" title="OS X">OS X</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> version came more than two years later, in March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Half-Life_2:_Deathmatch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Half Life 2: Deathmatch</i> was well-received upon its release, with reviewers praising its inclusion of the gravity gun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a> lauded the game for the Source engine's "impressive use of physics" and the available selection of maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> paid similar compliments to the title, also speaking highly of the "fast play style that [<i>Half-Life 2: Deathmatch</i>] produces."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Multiple reviews stated that the game suffered from large amount of lag upon release, but <a href="/wiki/GameSpy" title="GameSpy">GameSpy</a> noted that this was remedied soon after as more servers came online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
