<p><b>Georginio Gregion Emile "Gini" Wijnaldum</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[dÍ¡ÊÉrËdÍ¡ÊiÉ²oË ÊÉiËnÉldÉµm]</a></span>; <i><a href="/wiki/Given_name#Name_at_birth" title="Given name">nÃ©</a></i>, born 11 November 1990) is a Dutch <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a>.</p>
<p>A youth product of <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a>, he made his professional debut in 2007 as the youngest player ever to represent the club, and played 134 matches for them in a five-year period. He also played four seasons at <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>, winning the <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a> in the first and the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> in the last. In 2015 he joined <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> for Â£14.5 million, before departing after the club's relegation a year later to join Liverpool in a Â£23 million deal after Rafa Benitez agreed to his departure.</p>
<p>A full international with over 30 caps since making his debut in 2011, Wijnaldum was a member of the Dutch squad which came third at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>

<p>Georginio Wijnaldum was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Holland" title="South Holland">South Holland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know.21_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know.21-3">[3]</a></sup> At the age of six his parents divorced and his mother moved to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know.21_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know.21-3">[3]</a></sup> Wijnaldum decided to stay in Rotterdam and moved in with his grandmother, where he lived the remaining part of his childhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10_things_you_need_to_know_about_Georginio_Wijnaldum_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-10_things_you_need_to_know_about_Georginio_Wijnaldum-4">[4]</a></sup> Both his parents are of <a href="/wiki/Afro-Surinamese" title="Afro-Surinamese">Afro-Surinamese</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Wijnaldum's younger years he never showed interest in football. He never played with a ball or watched football on TV. His ambition was to become a gymnast or acrobat. This changed when Wijnaldum's nephew asked him to come to <a href="/wiki/Sparta_Rotterdam" title="Sparta Rotterdam">Sparta Rotterdam</a>'s opening day with him; then he got invited to the Sparta Rotterdam youth academy and his love for football started to grow slowly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kameraadjes_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-kameraadjes-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wijnaldum has two younger brothers, one of whom, <a href="/wiki/Giliano_Wijnaldum" title="Giliano Wijnaldum">Giliano Wijnaldum</a> currently plays for <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Union" title="Philadelphia Union">Philadelphia Union</a>, as well as a half-brother, <a href="/wiki/Rajiv_van_La_Parra" title="Rajiv van La Parra">Rajiv van La Parra</a>, who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Wijnaldum was formerly known as Georginio Boateng, but following his mother's divorce, he took her maiden name (Wijnaldum).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ad_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ad-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the age of six, Wijnaldum developed himself rapidly at Sparta Rotterdam, winning two championship titles in his first two seasons. Soon <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>, <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> showed interest in the youngster, but Wijnaldum declined all offers: "I didn't watch football on TV and I didn't know any of the first team players of Sparta or any of the top clubs. I only knew the real famous players of Oranje, so the offers didn't impress me much. I had a good time at Sparta, I wanted to stay." After playing for Sparta Rotterdam for seven seasons and representing the Netherlands at various youth levels, Wijnaldum decided to accept a new Feyenoord offer. He was convinced playing for Feyenoord was better for his development as a football player and believed in Feyenoord's vision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kameraadjes_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-kameraadjes-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At Feyenoord, Wijnaldum joined a successful generation with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Leroy_Fer" title="Leroy Fer">Leroy Fer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Pedro" title="LuÃ­s Pedro">LuÃ­s Pedro</a>. Wijnaldum stood out as an exceptional talent. In January 2007, a few weeks after turning 16, Wijnaldum was invited to the first team's training camp in <a href="/wiki/Belek" title="Belek">Belek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, by Feyenoord manager <a href="/wiki/Erwin_Koeman" title="Erwin Koeman">Erwin Koeman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 8 April 2007, Wijnaldum made his official debut in Feyenoord's starting line-up in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> home match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Groningen" title="FC Groningen">FC Groningen</a> (0â4).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At the age of 16 and 148 days, Wijnaldum became the youngest player ever to play in Feyenoord's first team and was chosen Feyenoord's man of the match. On 2 December 2007, Wijnaldum scored his first Eredivisie goal for Feyenoord against <a href="/wiki/Heracles_Almelo" title="Heracles Almelo">Heracles Almelo</a> in a 6â0 home win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the season <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eredivisie_2008-09" title="Eredivisie 2008-09">2008â09</a> Wijnaldum made his official European debut. On 18 September 2008, Wijnaldum was named in the starting line-up in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> home match against <a href="/wiki/Kalmar_FF" title="Kalmar FF">Kalmar FF</a> (0â1).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 2 October 2008, Wijnaldum scored his first European goal for Feyenoord in the return match against Kalmar FF, which resulted in a 1â2 win and a place in the group stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 March 2009, Wijnaldum signed a new contract at Feyenoord which keeps him in <a href="/wiki/De_Kuip" title="De Kuip">de Kuip</a> until summer 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On 27 February 2011 Wijnaldum helped his struggling Feyenoord to a 5â1 demolition of <a href="/wiki/FC_Groningen" title="FC Groningen">FC Groningen</a>, scoring four goals. He scored two goals from open play in the first half and two more from the penalty spot to add his total for the season to 8 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

Since joining the club, Wijnaldum established himself in the starting eleven as he scores goals and develops assists from his attacking role in midfield in Eredivisie, KNVB Beker and Europa League. After the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Mark_van_Bommel" title="Mark van Bommel">Mark van Bommel</a> and the departure of <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Strootman" title="Kevin Strootman">Kevin Strootman</a> in the summer of 2013, Wijnaldum was named PSV's captain for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Eredivisie" title="2013â14 Eredivisie">2013â14 Eredivisie</a> season. For the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Eredivisie" title="2014â15 Eredivisie">2014â15 Eredivisie</a> season Wijnaldum returned to full fitness and captained PSV to their first title since 2008.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 July 2016, Wijnaldum returned to the Premier League, signing for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> on a five-year contract, for an initial Â£23 million with a further Â£2 million in conditional add-ons. He was handed the number 5 shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He made his <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> on 14 August 2016 where he played 80 minutes and provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lallana" title="Adam Lallana">Adam Lallana</a> before being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Stewart_(footballer)" title="Kevin Stewart (footballer)">Kevin Stewart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club in a 6-1 thrashing of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watford_A.F.C." title="Watford A.F.C.">Watford</a> on 6 November 2016. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On 31 December 2016 he scored the only goal in Liverpool's 1-0 win over Manchester City to take them four points clear in second. He was praised highly for his goal. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<p>Wijnaldum was one of the key players of the Netherlands U17 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a> in Belgium. The team finished third in their group behind England and Belgium, failing to qualify for the knock-out stage. Quickly after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a>, Wijnaldum got selected for the Netherlands U19. Despite excellent individual performances, the team underachieved and failed to qualify for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2008 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">2008 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship</a> in Czech Republic and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship</a> in Ukraine.</p>
<p>On 12 November 2008, the young midfielder got invited to the Netherlands B by manager <a href="/wiki/Johan_Neeskens" title="Johan Neeskens">Johan Neeskens</a>. Wijnaldum had to deny the invitation due to an injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wijnaldum had to deny his first Netherlands U21 invitation on 9 August 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Wijnaldum received an invitation for the friendly match against <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England U21</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Cor_Pot" title="Cor Pot">Cor Pot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Wijnaldum made his official Netherlands U21 debut on 4 September 2009, in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2011 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2011 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship</a> qualification match against <a href="/wiki/Finland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Finland national under-21 football team">Finland U21</a> (2â0).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 May 2011, Wijnaldum was named in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> squad for friendlies against <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a>, but was not selected in either match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He made his international debut in the match against <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a> on 2 September 2011, appearing as a substitute in the 86th minute and promptly scoring the final goal in an 11â0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wijnaldum was a member of the Netherlands squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, and scored his second goal for the nation in a 3â0 win against Brazil in the third-place play-off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
