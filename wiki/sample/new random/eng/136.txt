<p>The <b>Scolecophidia</b> are an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infraorder" title="Infraorder">infraorder</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITIS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ITIS-2">[2]</a></sup> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snakes" title="Snakes">snakes</a>. They range in length from 10 to 100 cm (3.9 to 39.4 in). All are <a href="/wiki/Fossorial" title="Fossorial">fossorial</a> (adapted for burrowing).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> There are five families and 15 genera recognized,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITIS_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ITIS-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biogeographic_history">Biogeographic history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scolecophidia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biogeographic history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scolecophidians are believed to have originated on <a href="/wiki/Gondwana" title="Gondwana">Gondwana</a>, with anomalepidids and leptotyphlopids evolving in west Gondwana (South America and Africa) and typhlopids, gerrhopilids and xenotyphlopids on east Gondwana, initially on the combined India/Madagascar land mass, during the <a href="/wiki/Mesozoic" title="Mesozoic">Mesozoic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vidal-2010_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Vidal-2010-6">[6]</a></sup> Typhlopids then dispersed to Africa and Eurasia. South American typhlopids apparently evolved from African typhlopids that <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_dispersal" title="Oceanic dispersal">rafted</a> across the Atlantic about 60 million years ago; they in turn dispersed to the Caribbean about 33 million years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vidal-2010_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Vidal-2010-6">[6]</a></sup> Similarly, typhlopids apparently reached Australia from Southeast Asia or Indonesia about 28 million years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vidal-2010_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-Vidal-2010-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
