<p><i><b>Mito Kōmon</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">水戸黄門</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <i><a href="/wiki/Jidaigeki" title="Jidaigeki">jidaigeki</a></i> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Period_drama" title="Period drama">period drama</a> that has been on prime-time television since 1969. The title character is the historic <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Mitsukuni" title="Tokugawa Mitsukuni">Tokugawa Mitsukuni</a>, former vice-<a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">shogun</a> and retired second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">daimyo</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Mito_Domain" title="Mito Domain">Mito Domain</a>. In the guise of Mitsuemon, a retired crêpe merchant from <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Province" title="Echigo Province">Echigo</a>, he roams the realm with two <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> retainers, fun-loving Sasaki Sukesaburō (Suke-san) and studious Atsumi Kakunoshin (Kaku-san). An episode typically starts with some injustice perpetrated by a corrupt official, wealthy merchant or gangster; the travelers arrive incognito, discover the injustice and quietly investigate it; and the episode concludes with a brawl in which the unarmed, disguised protagonists better a crowd of samurai and gangsters, culminating with the presentation of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inro" title="Inro">inrō</a></i> that reveals the hero's identity. Afterwards, the hero passes judgement upon the villains, and sets things straight with fair comments and encouragements, and then moves on to the journey.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_characters">History and characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mito_K%C5%8Dmon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Five actors have portrayed the lead character in the series. <a href="/wiki/Eijir%C5%8D_T%C5%8Dno" title="Eijirō Tōno">Eijirō Tōno</a> created the part and appeared in 13 seasons. His successor was <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8D_Nishimura" title="Kō Nishimura">Kō Nishimura</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Asao_Sano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Asao Sano (page does not exist)">Asao Sano</a> followed, and <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dji_Ishizaka" title="Kōji Ishizaka">Kōji Ishizaka</a> took the role in two seasons, quitting for cancer treatments. <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dtar%C5%8D_Satomi" title="Kōtarō Satomi">Kōtarō Satomi</a>, has played Mitsuemon since 2002 until the end of the series.</p>
<p>The character normally sports a pointed white beard. He wears the lavish garb of a wealthy retiree, and carries a walking stick (a staff), which also serves as his defensive weapon in combat.</p>
<p>Singer-actor <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%8Dtar%C5%8D_Sugi" title="Ryōtarō Sugi">Ryōtarō Sugi</a> was the series' first Suke-san, followed by Kōtarō Satomi (who many years later took over the lead role). <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Teruhiko_Aoi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Teruhiko Aoi (page does not exist)">Teruhiko Aoi</a> was next, then <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Y%C5%ABji_Kishimoto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yūji Kishimoto (page does not exist)">Yūji Kishimoto</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ry%C5%ABji_Harada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ryūji Harada (page does not exist)">Ryūji Harada</a>. Opposite them, <a href="/wiki/Tadashi_Yokouchi" title="Tadashi Yokouchi">Tadashi Yokouchi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shin%27ya_%C5%8Cwada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shin'ya Ōwada (page does not exist)">Shin'ya Ōwada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goro_Ibuki" title="Goro Ibuki">Goro Ibuki</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jundai_Yamada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jundai Yamada (page does not exist)">Jundai Yamada</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Masashi_G%C5%8Dda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Masashi Gōda (page does not exist)">Masashi Gōda</a> have played Kaku-san. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ichiro_Nakatani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ichiro Nakatani (page does not exist)">Ichiro Nakatani</a> played Kazaguruma no Yashichi, a <a href="/wiki/Ninja" title="Ninja">ninja</a> who helped Mitsuemon. Evil characters who tried to kill Mitsuemon were played by such actors as <a href="/wiki/Shigeru_Tsuyuguchi" title="Shigeru Tsuyuguchi">Shigeru Tsuyuguchi</a> (1st season) and <a href="/wiki/Mikio_Narita" title="Mikio Narita">Mikio Narita</a> (3rd season).</p>
<p>In each of the show's 42 seasons, various other regular characters have joined the retinue. For many years, it was the food-loving commoner Hachibei; the reformed thieves Kazaguruma no Yashichi (a former ninja whose main weapon is a <a href="/wiki/Shuriken" title="Shuriken">shuriken</a> with pinwheel, with a dagger as his sidearm) and his wife Kasumi no Oshin; the ninja Tsuge no Tobizaru; and <a href="/wiki/Kunoichi" title="Kunoichi">kunoichi</a> Kagerō Ogin. Portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Kaoru_Yumi" title="Kaoru Yumi">Kaoru Yumi</a>, Ogin was the lead character in a light-hearted spinoff, <i>Mito Kōmon Gaiden Kagerō Ninpō-chō</i>. The same actress took on a new role, Hayate no Oen, in which she continues as of 2007.</p>
<p>Many former regular actors appeared in the show's gala 1000th special episode, which aired on December 15, 2003. A 1978 film distributed by <a href="/wiki/Toei_Company" title="Toei Company">Toei Company</a> shared the cast, production crew, and title.</p>
<p>Since its inception, the program has been a mainstay of the <i>Panasonic Drama Theater</i> (formerly known as the National Theater), sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Panasonic</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS</a> network airs the show throughout Japan, and shows reruns on both its analogue stations and its <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">CS satellite</a> channel. For nearly three decades, <i>Mito Kōmon</i> shared the time slot with <i><a href="/wiki/%C5%8Coka_Echizen" title="Ōoka Echizen">Ōoka Echizen</a></i>, and various actors and actresses appeared in both series.</p>
<p>An unusual feature of the series is that the credits list the maker of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inro" title="Inro"><i>inrō</i></a>. The last episode aired on December 19, 2011.</p>
<p>There was also an animated adaptation called "Manga Mito Kōmon" (まんが水戸黄門) that aired during 1981-1982, animated by <a href="/wiki/Knack_Productions" title="Knack Productions">Knack Productions</a>.</p>
