<p><b>Oxfordshire</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="/ər/ 'er' in 'finger'">ər</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ər/ 'er' in 'finger'">ər</span></span>/</a></span></span> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="/ər/ 'er' in 'finger'">ər</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ɪər/ 'ear' in 'near'">ɪər</span></span>/</a></span></span>; abbreviated <b>Oxon</b>) is a county in <a href="/wiki/South_East_England" title="South East England">South East England</a> bordering on <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire" title="Warwickshire">Warwickshire</a> (to the north/north-west), <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a> (to the north/north-east), <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a> (to the east), <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a> (to the south), <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a> (to the south-west) and <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a> (to the west).</p>
<p>The county has major education and tourist industries and is noted for the concentration of performance motorsport companies and facilities. <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Press" title="Oxford University Press">Oxford University Press</a> is the largest firm among a concentration of print and publishing firms; the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a> is also linked to the concentration of local <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a> companies.</p>
<p>The main centre of population is the city of <a href="/wiki/Oxford" title="Oxford">Oxford</a>. Other significant settlements are <a href="/wiki/Banbury" title="Banbury">Banbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bicester" title="Bicester">Bicester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kidlington" title="Kidlington">Kidlington</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chipping_Norton,_Oxfordshire" title="Chipping Norton, Oxfordshire">Chipping Norton</a> to the north of Oxford; <a href="/wiki/Carterton,_Oxfordshire" title="Carterton, Oxfordshire">Carterton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Witney" title="Witney">Witney</a> to the west; <a href="/wiki/Thame" title="Thame">Thame</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinnor" title="Chinnor">Chinnor</a> to the east; and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abingdon,_England" title="Abingdon, England">Abingdon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wantage" title="Wantage">Wantage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Didcot" title="Didcot">Didcot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallingford,_Oxfordshire" title="Wallingford, Oxfordshire">Wallingford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henley-on-Thames" title="Henley-on-Thames">Henley-on-Thames</a> to the south.</p>
<p>The highest point is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Horse_Hill" title="White Horse Hill">White Horse Hill</a>, in the Vale of White Horse, reaching 261 metres (856 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Oxfordshire's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_flower" title="County flower">county flower</a> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snake%27s-head_Fritillary" title="Snake's-head Fritillary">Snake's-head Fritillary</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-countyflower_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-countyflower-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oxfordshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Oxfordshire" title="History of Oxfordshire">History of Oxfordshire</a></div>
<p>Oxfordshire was recorded as a county in the early years of the 10th century and is situated on land between the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> to the south, the <a href="/wiki/Cotswolds" title="Cotswolds">Cotswolds</a> to the west, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chilterns" title="Chilterns">Chilterns</a> to the east and the Midlands to the north, with spurs running south to <a href="/wiki/Henley-on-Thames" title="Henley-on-Thames">Henley-on-Thames</a> and north to <a href="/wiki/Banbury" title="Banbury">Banbury</a>.</p>
<p>Historically the area has always had some importance, since it contains valuable agricultural land in the centre of the county. Largely ignored by the Romans, it was not until the formation of a settlement at Oxford in the eighth century that the area grew in importance. <a href="/wiki/Alfred_the_Great" title="Alfred the Great">Alfred the Great</a> was born across the Thames in Wantage, Vale of White Horse. The University of Oxford was founded in 1096, though its collegiate structure did not develop until later on. The university in the county town of <a href="/wiki/Oxford" title="Oxford">Oxford</a> (whose name came from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_language" title="Anglo-Saxon language">Anglo-Saxon</a> <i>Oxenaford</i> = "ford for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxen" title="Oxen">oxen</a>") grew in importance during the Middle Ages and early modern period. The area was part of the <a href="/wiki/Cotswolds" title="Cotswolds">Cotswolds</a> wool trade from the 13th century, generating much wealth, particularly in the western portions of the county in the Oxfordshire Cotswolds. <a href="/wiki/Morris_Motors" title="Morris Motors">Morris Motors</a> was founded in Oxford in 1912, bringing heavy industry to an otherwise agricultural county. The importance of agriculture as an employer has declined rapidly in the 20th century though; currently under one percent of the county's population are involved due to high mechanisation. Nonetheless, Oxfordshire remains a very agricultural county by land use, with a lower population than neighbouring Berkshire and Buckinghamshire, which are both smaller.</p>
<p>Throughout most of its history the county was divided into fourteen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hundred_(division)" title="Hundred (division)">hundreds</a>, namely <a href="/wiki/Bampton,_Oxfordshire" title="Bampton, Oxfordshire">Bampton</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banbury_(hundred)" title="Banbury (hundred)">Banbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Binfield" title="Binfield">Binfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bloxham" title="Bloxham">Bloxham</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bullingdon_(hundred)" title="Bullingdon (hundred)">Bullingdon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chadlington_(hundred)" title="Chadlington (hundred)">Chadlington</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dorchester_(hundred)" title="Dorchester (hundred)">Dorchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ewelme" title="Ewelme">Ewelme</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Langtree_(hundred)" title="Langtree (hundred)">Langtree</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lewknor" title="Lewknor">Lewknor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pyrton" title="Pyrton">Pyrton</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ploughley" title="Ploughley">Ploughley</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thame_(hundred)" title="Thame (hundred)">Thame</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wootton,_West_Oxfordshire" title="Wootton, West Oxfordshire">Wootton</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire_and_Buckinghamshire_Light_Infantry" title="Oxfordshire and Buckinghamshire Light Infantry">Oxfordshire and Buckinghamshire Light Infantry</a>, the main army unit in the area, was based at <a href="/wiki/Cowley_Barracks" title="Cowley Barracks">Cowley Barracks</a> on Bullingdon Green, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cowley,_Oxford" title="Cowley, Oxford">Cowley</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_White_Horse" title="Vale of White Horse">Vale of White Horse</a> district and parts of the <a href="/wiki/South_Oxfordshire" title="South Oxfordshire">South Oxfordshire</a> administrative district south of the River Thames were historically part of <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a>, but were added to the administrative county of Oxfordshire in 1974. Conversely, the <a href="/wiki/Caversham,_Berkshire" title="Caversham, Berkshire">Caversham</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">Reading</a>, now administratively in Berkshire, was historically part of Oxfordshire as was the parish of <a href="/wiki/Stokenchurch" title="Stokenchurch">Stokenchurch</a>, now administratively in Buckinghamshire.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oxfordshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire_County_Council" title="Oxfordshire County Council">Oxfordshire County Council</a>, currently controlled by a Conservative Independent Alliance, is responsible for the most strategic <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local government</a> functions, including schools, county roads, and <a href="/wiki/Social_services" title="Social services">social services</a> The county is divided into five <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government districts</a>: <a href="/wiki/Oxford" title="Oxford">Oxford</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cherwell_(district)" title="Cherwell (district)">Cherwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_White_Horse" title="Vale of White Horse">Vale of White Horse</a> (after the <a href="/wiki/Uffington_White_Horse" title="Uffington White Horse">Uffington White Horse</a>), <a href="/wiki/West_Oxfordshire" title="West Oxfordshire">West Oxfordshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Oxfordshire" title="South Oxfordshire">South Oxfordshire</a>, which deal with such matters as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_and_country_planning" title="Town and country planning">town and country planning</a>, waste collection, and housing.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum,_2016" title="United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016">2016 European Union referendum</a>, Oxfordshire was the only English county as a whole to vote to remain in the European Union by a significant margin, at 57.06% (70.27% in the City of Oxford), despite Cherwell (barely) voting to leave at 50.31%.</p>

<p>Oxfordshire has a completely comprehensive education system with 23 independent schools and 35 state secondary schools. Only eight schools do not have a <a href="/wiki/Sixth_form" title="Sixth form">sixth form</a>; these are mostly in South Oxfordshire and Cherwell districts.</p>
<p>The county has two universities: the ancient <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a> and the modern <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Brookes_University" title="Oxford Brookes University">Oxford Brookes University</a>, both located in Oxford. In addition, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wroxton_College" title="Wroxton College">Wroxton College</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Banbury" title="Banbury">Banbury</a>, is affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Fairleigh_Dickinson_University" title="Fairleigh Dickinson University">Fairleigh Dickinson University</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings">Buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oxfordshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "dreaming spires" of the buildings of the University of Oxford are among the reasons for Oxford being the sixth most visited city in the United Kingdom for international visitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Among many notable University buildings are the <a href="/wiki/Sheldonian_Theatre" title="Sheldonian Theatre">Sheldonian Theatre</a>, built 1664–68 to the design of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Christopher_Wren" title="Sir Christopher Wren">Sir Christopher Wren</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_Camera" title="Radcliffe Camera">Radcliffe Camera</a>, built 1737–49 to the design of <a href="/wiki/James_Gibbs" title="James Gibbs">James Gibbs</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Blenheim_Palace" title="Blenheim Palace">Blenheim Palace</a> close to <a href="/wiki/Woodstock,_Oxfordshire" title="Woodstock, Oxfordshire">Woodstock</a> was built by the great architect <a href="/wiki/John_Vanbrugh" title="John Vanbrugh">John Vanbrugh</a> for <a href="/wiki/John_Churchill,_1st_Duke_of_Marlborough" title="John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough">John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough</a>, after he had won the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Blenheim" title="Battle of Blenheim">battle of Blenheim</a>. The gardens, which can be visited, were designed by the landscape gardener <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancelot_%22Capability%22_Brown" title='Lancelot "Capability" Brown'>"Capability Brown"</a>, who planted the trees in the battle formation of the victorious army. In the palace, which can also be visited by the public, Sir <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> was born in 1874.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chastleton_House" title="Chastleton House">Chastleton House</a>, on the Gloucestershire and Warwickshire borders, is a great <a href="/wiki/English_country_house" title="English country house">country mansion</a> built on property bought from <a href="/wiki/Robert_Catesby" title="Robert Catesby">Robert Catesby</a>, who was one of the men involved in the <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder_Plot" title="Gunpowder Plot">Gunpowder Plot</a> with <a href="/wiki/Guy_Fawkes" title="Guy Fawkes">Guy Fawkes</a>. <a href="/wiki/Stonor_Park" title="Stonor Park">Stonor Park</a>, another country mansion, has belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Recusancy" title="Recusancy">recusant</a> Stonor family for centuries.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mapledurham_House" title="Mapledurham House">Mapledurham House</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Elizabethan_era" title="Elizabethan era">Elizabethan</a> stately home in the far south-east of the county, close to <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">Reading</a>.</p>
