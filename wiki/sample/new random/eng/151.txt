<p><b>Kyū-Shirataki Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">旧白滝駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kyū-Shirataki-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "Old Shirataki")</span> was a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Sekihoku_Main_Line" title="Sekihoku Main Line">Sekihoku Main Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Engaru,_Hokkaido" title="Engaru, Hokkaido">Engaru, Hokkaido</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido_Railway_Company" title="Hokkaido Railway Company">Hokkaido Railway Company</a> (JR Hokkaido). Opened in 1947, the station closed in March 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ky%C5%AB-Shirataki_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shimo-Shirataki Station was served by the single-track <a href="/wiki/Sekihoku_Main_Line" title="Sekihoku Main Line">Sekihoku Main Line</a>, lying 88.3 km from the official starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Shin-Asahikawa_Station" title="Shin-Asahikawa Station">Shin-Asahikawa</a>. The station was numbered "A46".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ky%C5%AB-Shirataki_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station had a <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> serving the otherwise single-track line. The station was unstaffed, but had a station structure and waiting room.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ky%C5%AB-Shirataki_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 11 February 1947.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-1">[1]</a></sup> With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR Hokkaido.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2015, JR Hokkaido announced that it would be closing the station along with three others on the line (<a href="/wiki/Shimo-Shirataki_Station" title="Shimo-Shirataki Station">Shimo-Shirataki Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kami-Shirataki_Station" title="Kami-Shirataki Station">Kami-Shirataki Station</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kanehana_Station" title="Kanehana Station">Kanehana Station</a>) in March 2016, due to low passenger usage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doshin20150722_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-doshin20150722-2">[2]</a></sup> According to a news report, as of January 2016, there was only one passenger using the station regularly;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> later reports suggest that there were in fact over 10 additional passengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-straitstimes20160109_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-straitstimes20160109-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The station closed following the last day of services on 25 March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doshin20160326_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-doshin20160326-5">[5]</a></sup> The station structure and platform was demolished in October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doshin20161018_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-doshin20161018-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
