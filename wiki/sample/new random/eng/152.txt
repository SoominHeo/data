<p><b>Ross Barkley</b> (born 5 December 1993) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attacking_midfielder" title="Attacking midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> and the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England national team</a>. He has been at <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> his whole career but has played on <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> when he was younger.</p>
<p>Barkley made his full international debut in 2013. He played for England at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and was selected in <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a>'s 23-man squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a> but didn't appear in any of England's four matches. Managers and teammates have compared his style of play to those of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ballack" title="Michael Ballack">Michael Ballack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gascoigne" title="Paul Gascoigne">Paul Gascoigne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Barkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Barkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Barkley was born in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merseyside" title="Merseyside">Merseyside</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman-1">[1]</a></sup> He joined <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> as an 11-year-old and played for the club's youth teams. He was named as a substitute in a first team league game in early 2010–11, and was expected to make his <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> before his leg was broken in three places after a collision with <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Andre_Wisdom" title="Andre Wisdom">Andre Wisdom</a> during an England under-19 match in October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He recovered from his injury in time to join the first team for pre-season training before the beginning of 2011–12. During pre-season, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Cahill" title="Tim Cahill">Tim Cahill</a> heralded Barkley as the most talented footballer he had worked with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He made his debut in Everton's first home game of the season, a 1–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>, and was named man of the match by Radio City Sport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> His early performances were highly praised, with <a href="/wiki/Martin_Keown" title="Martin Keown">Martin Keown</a> predicting that "[Barkley will] be one of the best players we'll ever see in this country."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He signed a new four-and-a-half-year contract in December 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 September 2012, Barkley joined <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> on a one-month <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> deal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> making his debut the same day in an away defeat against <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club, a penalty, against <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His loan was extended before he was recalled by Everton after playing 13 games for Wednesday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He again went out on loan, this time to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> for an "initial one-month stint", in January 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and made his debut for Leeds in a derby match against <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Everton">Everton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Barkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Everton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DinamoKiev-Everton_(8).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="543" data-file-width="506" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/DinamoKiev-Everton_%288%29.jpg/170px-DinamoKiev-Everton_%288%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/DinamoKiev-Everton_%288%29.jpg/255px-DinamoKiev-Everton_%288%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/DinamoKiev-Everton_%288%29.jpg/340px-DinamoKiev-Everton_%288%29.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DinamoKiev-Everton_(8).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Barkley playing for <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> in 2015</div>

<p>Barkley rejoined the Everton first team for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Everton_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Everton F.C. season">2013–14 season</a>. He scored his first goal for the club on the opening day in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>, and was later named man of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> During the first half of the season, Barkley put in several man-of-the-match performances, notably against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a> – the latter whom he scored the winner against.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He received praise from many pundits including <a href="/wiki/Alan_Hansen" title="Alan Hansen">Alan Hansen</a> who said Barkley has absolutely everything and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Lineker" title="Gary Lineker">Gary Lineker</a> who claimed he is a star in the making and a prodigious talent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barkley's first FA Cup goal came on 4 January 2014, opening a 4–0 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers" title="Queens Park Rangers">Queens Park Rangers</a> in the third round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In March, Barkley scored a superb solo goal against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> in which he ran from his own half to net the opener in a 3–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 18 April 2014, he was named as one of the six players on the shortlist for the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a> award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He scored a total of 6 goals in 34 Premier League matches throughout the season, including a goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_City" title="Manchester City">Manchester City</a> on 3 May which BBC Sport described as "spectacular" and was awarded goal of the season at Everton's end of year awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 July 2014, Barkley signed a new contract with Everton, keeping him at the club until 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Before the start of the 2014–15 season he suffered an injury to his <a href="/wiki/Medial_collateral_ligament" title="Medial collateral ligament">medial collateral ligament</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and did not play until 18 October 2014, setting up <a href="/wiki/Romelu_Lukaku" title="Romelu Lukaku">Romelu Lukaku</a>'s goal in a 3–0 home league win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a>. He scored his first goal of the season in a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> on 15 December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barkley started the 2015–16 season with goals in Everton's first two league games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Barkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Barkley_Ross.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2553" data-file-width="1701" height="255" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Barkley_Ross.jpg/170px-Barkley_Ross.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Barkley_Ross.jpg/255px-Barkley_Ross.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Barkley_Ross.jpg/340px-Barkley_Ross.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Barkley_Ross.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Barkley playing for <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-19_football_team" title="England national under-19 football team">England under-19s</a> in 2012</div>

<p>Barkley was eligible to represent both England and Nigeria as he has a <a href="/wiki/Nigerians" title="Nigerians">Nigerian</a> grandfather. Barkley opted to represent his country of birth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> He has represented England at under-16, under-17, under-19, under-20, under-21 and senior level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thefa-profile_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-thefa-profile-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was first called up for the under-16 level in September 2008, aged 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He was selected to play in the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Montaigu_Tournament" title="Montaigu Tournament">Montaigu Tournament</a> and captained the team to success, beating Germany 2–1 on penalties in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He also helped England win the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2010 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2010 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a> with two goals en route to the final where England beat Spain 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Barkley was called up to the England U-21 squad in August 2011. On 28 May 2013, he was named in manager <a href="/wiki/Peter_Taylor_(footballer,_born_1953)" title="Peter Taylor (footballer, born 1953)">Peter Taylor</a>'s 21-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He made his debut on 16 June, in a 3–0 win in a warm-up game against <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_under-20_football_team" title="Uruguay national under-20 football team">Uruguay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21's</a> on 13 August 2013, in a 6–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Scotland national under-21 football team">Scotland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barkley was called up to the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">senior England squad</a> in August 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> and earned his first senior cap the following month, coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wilshere" title="Jack Wilshere">Jack Wilshere</a> in a 4–0 win in a <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification">World Cup qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Moldova_national_football_team" title="Moldova national football team">Moldova</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> He was named in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/England_world_cup_squad" title="England world cup squad">England squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">World Cup Finals</a> in Brazil,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> and made his World Cup debut as a 61st-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Danny_Welbeck" title="Danny Welbeck">Danny Welbeck</a> in England's 2–1 opening defeat to <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manaus" title="Manaus">Manaus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> He played the full match in England's final group game, a 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Costa Rica</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the senior team in a 6–0 win against <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a> in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying</a> fixture in September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-San_Marino_0.E2.80.936_England_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-San_Marino_0.E2.80.936_England-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Barkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Former Everton manager <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Roberto Martínez">Roberto Martínez</a> described Barkley as a mix of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gascoigne" title="Paul Gascoigne">Paul Gascoigne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ballack" title="Michael Ballack">Michael Ballack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ballack_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-ballack-45">[45]</a></sup> England manager <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a> has said that Barkley's "pace and power" as well as his "excellent technique" are reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gascoigne" title="Paul Gascoigne">Paul Gascoigne</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gascoigne_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-gascoigne-46">[46]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lampard" title="Frank Lampard">Frank Lampard</a> stated that Barkley reminds him of a young <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rooney_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-rooney-47">[47]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> midfielder <a href="/wiki/Xavi" title="Xavi">Xavi</a> said in November 2014 that Barkley is good enough to play for the <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> club, praising his physical and technical qualities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
