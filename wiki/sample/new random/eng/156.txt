<p>The city became famous for its resistance during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Stalingrad" title="Battle of Stalingrad">Battle of Stalingrad</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">German Army</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. It is often regarded as the largest and bloodiest battle in the history of warfare.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tsaritsyn">Tsaritsyn</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tsaritsyn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although the city may have originated in 1555, documented evidence of <i>Tsaritsyn</i> located at the <a href="/wiki/Confluence" title="Confluence">confluence</a> of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tsaritsa_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tsaritsa River (page does not exist)">Tsaritsa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volga_River" title="Volga River">Volga Rivers</a> dates only from 1589.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-gr-9">[9]</a></sup> Grigori Zasekin established the fortress <i>Sary Su</i> (a local <a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Tatar-language</a> name meaning: <i>yellow water/river</i>) as part of the defences of the unstable southern border of the <a href="/wiki/Tsardom_of_Russia" title="Tsardom of Russia">Tsardom of Russia</a>. The structure stood slightly above the mouth of the Tsaritsa River on the right bank. It soon became the nucleus of a trading settlement.</p>
<p>In 1607 the fortress garrison rebelled against the troops of Tsar <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasili_Shuisky" title="Vasili Shuisky">Vasili Shuisky</a> for six months. In 1608 the city acquired its first stone church, St. John the Baptist. At the beginning of the 17th century, the garrison consisted of 350 to 400 people.</p>
<p>In 1670 troops of <a href="/wiki/Stepan_Razin" title="Stepan Razin">Stepan Razin</a> captured the fortress; they left after a month. In 1708 the insurgent Cossack <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kondraty_Bulavin" title="Kondraty Bulavin">Kondraty Bulavin</a> (died July 1708) held the fortress. In 1717 in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kuban_pogrom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kuban pogrom (page does not exist)">Kuban pogrom</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9A%D1%83%D0%B1%D0%B0%D0%BD%D1%81%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B9_%D0%BF%D0%BE%D0%B3%D1%80%D0%BE%D0%BC" title="ru:Кубанский погром">ru</a>)</span>, raiders from the <a href="/wiki/Kuban_River" title="Kuban River">Kuban</a> under the command of the <a href="/wiki/Crimean_Tatars" title="Crimean Tatars">Crimean Tatar</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bakhti_Gerai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bakhti Gerai (page does not exist)">Bakhti Gerai</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%91%D0%B0%D1%85%D1%82%D0%B8_%D0%93%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B0%D0%B9" title="ru:Бахти Герай">ru</a>)</span> blockaded the town and enslaved thousands in the area. In August 1774 <a href="/wiki/Yemelyan_Pugachev" title="Yemelyan Pugachev">Yemelyan Pugachev</a> unsuccessfully attempted to storm the city.</p>
<p>In 1691, Tsaritsyn established <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a>. In 1708 Tsaritsyn was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/Kazan_Governorate" title="Kazan Governorate">Kazan Governorate</a>; in 1719<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> to the <a href="/wiki/Astrakhan_Governorate" title="Astrakhan Governorate">Astrakhan Governorate</a>. According to the census in 1720, the city had a population of 408 people. In 1773 the city became the provincial and district town. From 1779 it belonged to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saratov_Viceroyalty&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saratov Viceroyalty (page does not exist)">Saratov Viceroyalty</a>. In 1780 the city came under the newly established <a href="/wiki/Saratov_Governorate" title="Saratov Governorate">Saratov Governorate</a>.</p>
<p>In the 19th century Tsaritsyn became an important river-port and commercial center. The population expanded rapidly, increasing from fewer than 3,000 people in 1807 to about 84,000 in 1900. The first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railroad" title="Railroad">railroad</a> reached the town in 1862. The first <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatre</a> opened in 1872, the first <a href="/wiki/Movie_theater" title="Movie theater">cinema</a> in 1907. In 1913 Tsaritsyn got its first tram-line, and the city's first electric lights were installed in the city center.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a> of 1917-1923, Tsaritsyn came under Soviet control from November 1917. In 1918 White troops under the <a href="/wiki/Ataman" title="Ataman">Ataman</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_Cossack_Host" title="Don Cossack Host">Don Cossack Host</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pyotr_Krasnov" title="Pyotr Krasnov">Pyotr Krasnov</a>, besieged Tsaritsyn. The Reds repulsed three assaults by the Whites. However, in June 1919 the White <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_South_Russia" title="Armed Forces of South Russia">Armed Forces of South Russia</a> under the command of General <a href="/wiki/Anton_Denikin" title="Anton Denikin">Denikin</a> captured Tsaritsyn, which they held until January 1920. The fighting from July 1918 to January 1920 became known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_for_Tsaritsyn" title="Battle for Tsaritsyn">Battle for Tsaritsyn</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stalingrad">Stalingrad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stalingrad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city was renamed Stalingrad after <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a> on April 10, 1925.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This was officially to recognize the city's and Stalin's role in its defense against the <a href="/wiki/White_movement" title="White movement">Whites</a> between 1918 and 1920.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 1931, the German settlement-colony <a href="/wiki/Old_Sarepta" title="Old Sarepta">Old Sarepta</a> (founded in 1765) became a district of Stalingrad. Renamed Krasnoarmeysky Rayon, it became the largest area of the city.</p>
<p>The first institute was opened in 1930. A year later, the Stalingrad Industrial Pedagogical Institute, now <a href="/wiki/Volgograd_State_Pedagogical_University" title="Volgograd State Pedagogical University">Volgograd State Pedagogical University</a>, was opened.</p>
<p>Under Stalin, the city became a center of heavy industry and <a href="/wiki/Transshipment" title="Transshipment">transshipment</a> by rail and river. It was attacked by German and Axis forces during World War II. In 1942, the city became the site of one of the pivotal battles of the war. The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Stalingrad" title="Battle of Stalingrad">Battle of Stalingrad</a> had perhaps the <a href="/wiki/List_of_battles_by_casualties" title="List of battles by casualties">greatest casualty figures</a> of any single battle in the history of warfare (estimates are between 1,250,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and 1,798,619<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>). The Battle became a titanic struggle between Hitler and Stalin as both saw it of great propaganda value, each keenly aware of the namesake of the city, and each poured hundreds of thousands of men into the battle.</p>
<p>The battle began on August 23, 1942, and on the same day, the city suffered heavy aerial bombardment that reduced most of it to rubble. By September, the fighting reached the city center. The fighting was of unprecedented intensity; the central railway station of the city changed hands thirteen times, and the <a href="/wiki/Mamayev_Kurgan" title="Mamayev Kurgan">Mamayev Kurgan</a> (one of the highest points of the city) was captured and recaptured eight times. By early November, the German forces controlled 90 percent of the city and had cornered the Soviets in two narrow pockets, but they were unable to eliminate the last pockets of Soviet resistance in time. On November 19, Soviet forces launched a <a href="/wiki/Operation_Uranus" title="Operation Uranus">huge counterattack</a>. This led to the encirclement of the <a href="/wiki/6th_Army_(Wehrmacht)" title="6th Army (Wehrmacht)">German Sixth Army and other Axis units</a>. On January 31, 1943 the Sixth Army's commander, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Field_Marshal" title="Field Marshal">Field Marshal</a> <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Paulus" title="Friedrich Paulus">Friedrich Paulus</a>, surrendered, and by February 2, with the elimination of straggling German troops, the Battle of Stalingrad was over. In 1945 the Soviet Union awarded Stalingrad the title <a href="/wiki/Hero_City" title="Hero City">Hero City</a> for its resistance. Great Britain's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_VI_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George VI of the United Kingdom">King George VI</a> awarded the citizens of Stalingrad the jeweled "<a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Stalingrad" title="Sword of Stalingrad">Sword of Stalingrad</a>" in recognition of their bravery. As Stalingrad was destroyed during the war, in 1946, the construction of the modern city started. It included the memorial complex on the Mamayev Kurgan.</p>
<p>A number of cities around the world (especially those that had suffered similar wartime devastation) established sister, friendship and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_twinning" title="Town twinning">twinning</a> links (see list below) in the spirit of solidarity or reconciliation. One of the first "sister city" projects was that established during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> between Stalingrad and <a href="/wiki/Coventry" title="Coventry">Coventry</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> – both suffered extensive devastation from aerial bombardment.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Volgograd">Volgograd</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Volgograd">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 1961, <a href="/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev" title="Nikita Khrushchev">Nikita Khrushchev</a>'s administration changed the name of the city to Volgograd ("Volga City") as part of his programme of <a href="/wiki/De-Stalinization" title="De-Stalinization">de-Stalinization</a> following Stalin's death, as he was trying to reduce the "cult of personality". This action was and remains somewhat controversial, given Stalingrad's importance as a symbol of resistance during the war. During <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Chernenko" title="Konstantin Chernenko">Konstantin Chernenko</a>'s brief administration in 1984, proposals were floated to revive its historic name. There remains a strong degree of local support for a reversion but intermittent proposals have yet to be accepted by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_government" title="Russian government">Russian government</a>.</p>
<p>On May 21, 2007, the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Communist Party of the Russian Federation">Communist Party of the Russian Federation</a> obtained an important success in the Volgograd mayoral election. Communist candidate <a href="/wiki/Roman_Grebennikov" title="Roman Grebennikov">Roman Grebennikov</a> was elected as mayor with 32.47% of the vote. Grebennikov is Russia's youngest mayor of a <a href="/wiki/Federal_subjects_of_Russia" title="Federal subjects of Russia">federal subject</a> administrative center.</p>
<p>In 2010, Russian monarchists and leaders of the Orthodox organizations demanded that the city should return to its original name Tsaritsyn, but the authorities rejected their proposal.</p>
<p>On January 30, 2013, the Volgograd City Council passed a measure to use the title "<a href="/wiki/Hero_City" title="Hero City">Hero City</a> Stalingrad" in city statements on nine specific dates annually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StalingradName_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-StalingradName-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torygraf_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-torygraf-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On the following dates the title "Hero City Stalingrad" can officially be used in celebrations: February 2 (end of the Battle of Stalingrad), February 23 (<a href="/wiki/Defender_of_the_Fatherland_Day" title="Defender of the Fatherland Day">Defender of the Fatherland Day</a>), May 8, May 9 (<a href="/wiki/Victory_Day_(9_May)" title="Victory Day (9 May)">Victory Day</a>), June 22 (start of <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a>), August 23 (start of the Battle of Stalingrad), September 2 (<a href="/wiki/Victory_over_Japan_Day" title="Victory over Japan Day">Victory over Japan Day</a>), November 19 (start of <a href="/wiki/Operation_Uranus" title="Operation Uranus">Operation Uranus</a>), and December 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StalingradName_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-StalingradName-19">[19]</a></sup> In addition, 50,000 people signed a petition to <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>, asking that the city's name be permanently changed to Stalingrad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torygraf_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-torygraf-20">[20]</a></sup> President Putin has replied that such a move should be preceded by a local referendum and that the Russian authorities will look into how to bring about such a referendum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-en.itar-tass.com_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-en.itar-tass.com-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terrorist_attacks">Terrorist attacks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Terrorist attacks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/2004_Russian_aircraft_bombings" title="2004 Russian aircraft bombings">2004 Russian aircraft bombings</a>, <a href="/wiki/October_2013_Volgograd_bus_bombing" title="October 2013 Volgograd bus bombing">October 2013 Volgograd bus bombing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/December_2013_Volgograd_bombings" title="December 2013 Volgograd bombings">December 2013 Volgograd bombings</a></div>
<p>On August 24, 2004, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volga-AviaExpress" title="Volga-AviaExpress">Volga-AviaExpress</a> Flight 1353,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rwkckb07_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-rwkckb07-23">[23]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-134" title="Tupolev Tu-134">Tupolev Tu-134</a> aircraft flying from <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> to Volgograd, exploded in mid-air and crashed as a result of suicide terrorist attack. 34 passengers and 9 crew members were on board the aircraft, all of whom died in the crash. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siberia_Airlines" title="Siberia Airlines">Siberia Airlines</a> flight bound for <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a> that day was also bombed, killing all 46 who were on board.</p>
<p>At approximately 2:00 p.m. on Monday 21 October 2013 Russian intelligence officers reported a bomb carried by a female suicide bomber exploded on a passenger bus carrying 40 people while stopped at the Lesobaza bus stop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Irina Gogolyeva, a spokesperson from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Emergency_Situations_Ministry" title="Russian Emergency Situations Ministry">Russian Emergency Situations Ministry</a>, reported that at least five people died in the blast and seventeen others were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On October 22, 2013, Vladimir Markin from Russia's <a href="/wiki/Investigative_Committee_of_Russia" title="Investigative Committee of Russia">investigative Committee</a> reported that the suicide bomber had been identified as 30-year-old Naida Asiyalova of <a href="/wiki/Dagestan" title="Dagestan">Dagestan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 29, 2013, a suicide bomb attack occurred at the <a href="/wiki/Volgograd_railway_station" title="Volgograd railway station">Volgograd railway station</a>, killing at least seventeen people. It is not clear how many bombers were involved or who they were.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The following day a suicide bombing on a trolleybus killed at least fifteen people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-graphic_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-graphic-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, the City Duma canceled direct election of the mayor and confirmed the position of City Manager. This was short-lived, as in March 2012, Volgograd residents voted for relevant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amendments" title="Amendments">amendments</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_charter" title="City charter">city charter</a> to reinstate the direct mayoral elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Volgograd is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, it is incorporated as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">city of oblast significance</a> of Volgograd</b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Volgograd_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Volgograd Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref151_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Ref151-3">[3]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the city of oblast significance of Volgograd is incorporated as <b>Volgograd Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref921_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref921-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Modern Volgograd remains an important industrial city. Industries include <a href="/wiki/Shipbuilding" title="Shipbuilding">shipbuilding</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_refining" title="Oil refining">oil refining</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aluminum" title="Aluminum">aluminum</a> production, manufacture of machinery and vehicles, and chemical production. A large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volgograd_Hydroelectric_Plant" title="Volgograd Hydroelectric Plant">Volgograd Hydroelectric Plant</a> is located a short distance to the north of Volgograd.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Volgograd is a major railway junction served by the <a href="/wiki/Privolzhskaya_Railway" title="Privolzhskaya Railway">Privolzhskaya Railway</a>. Rail links from the <a href="/wiki/Volgograd_railway_station" title="Volgograd railway station">Volgograd railway station</a> include Moscow; Saratov; Astrakhan; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donbas" title="Donbas">Donbas</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. It stands at the east end of the <a href="/wiki/Volga%E2%80%93Don_Canal" title="Volga–Don Canal">Volga–Don Canal</a>, opened in 1952 to link the two great rivers of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Federal_District" title="Southern Federal District">Southern Russia</a>. <a href="/wiki/European_route_E40" title="European route E40">European route E40</a>, the longest European route connecting <a href="/wiki/Calais" title="Calais">Calais</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> with <a href="/wiki/Ridder,_Kazakhstan" title="Ridder, Kazakhstan">Ridder</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, passes through Volgograd. The <a href="/wiki/M6_highway_(Russia)" title="M6 highway (Russia)">M6 highway</a> between Moscow and the Caspian Sea also passes through the city. The <a href="/wiki/Volgograd_Bridge" title="Volgograd Bridge">Volgograd Bridge</a>, under construction since 1995, was inaugurated in October 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The city river terminal is the center for local passenger shipping along the Volga River.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Volgograd_International_Airport" title="Volgograd International Airport">Volgograd International Airport</a> provides air links to major Russian cities as well as <a href="/wiki/Antalya" title="Antalya">Antalya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aktau" title="Aktau">Aktau</a>.</p>
<p>Volgograd's public transport system includes a <a href="/wiki/Light_rail" title="Light rail">light rail</a> service known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volgograd_metrotram" title="Volgograd metrotram">Volgograd metrotram</a>. Local public transport is provided by buses, trolleybuses and trams.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Volga_River" title="Volga River">Volga River</a> still is a very important communication channel.</p>
<p>Volgograd hosts one of the few floating churches in the world:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> the floating church of Saint Vladimir of Volgograd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="City_name">City name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: City name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beginning in 2013, for nine days every year, the city may be officially referred to as "Stalingrad".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StalingradName_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-StalingradName-19">[19]</a></sup> The city became famous for its resistance during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Stalingrad" title="Battle of Stalingrad">Battle of Stalingrad</a> (1942–43). Some residents have suggested that the city be permanently renamed "Stalingrad"; president <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> has stated that such a move should be preceded by a local referendum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-en.itar-tass.com_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-en.itar-tass.com-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volgograd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>A memorial complex commemorating the battle of Stalingrad, dominated by an immense allegorical sculpture <i><a href="/wiki/The_Motherland_Calls" title="The Motherland Calls">The Motherland Calls</a></i>, was erected on the <a href="/wiki/Mamayev_Kurgan" title="Mamayev Kurgan">Mamayev Kurgan</a>, the hill that saw some of the most intense fighting during the battle. With its 85 meters it is almost twice the height of the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">Statue of Liberty</a>; it is the tallest statue of a <a href="/wiki/Woman" title="Woman">woman</a> in the world.</p>
<p>The <i>Panorama Museum</i> sited on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volga" title="Volga">Volga</a> contains artifacts from World War II. These include a <a href="/wiki/Panoramic_painting" title="Panoramic painting">panoramic painting</a> of the battlefield from the location of the monument on Mamayev Kurgan. A <a href="/wiki/Rifle" title="Rifle">rifle</a> of the famous <a href="/wiki/Sniper" title="Sniper">sniper</a> <a href="/wiki/Vasily_Zaytsev" title="Vasily Zaytsev">Vasily Zaytsev</a> (popularized in Western media in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Enemy_at_the_Gates" title="Enemy at the Gates">Enemy at the Gates</a></i>) is also on display.</p>
