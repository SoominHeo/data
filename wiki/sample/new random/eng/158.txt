<p><b>Matthew John Anderson</b> (born April 18, 1987) is an American <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> player, a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_volleyball_team" title="United States men's national volleyball team">United States men's national volleyball team</a> and Russian club <a href="/wiki/VC_Zenit-Kazan" title="VC Zenit-Kazan">Zenit Kazan</a>, a participant of the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> (<a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2016</a>), bronze medalist of the <a href="/wiki/Volleyball_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Volleyball at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2016 Olympic Games</a>, <a href="/wiki/2013_Men%27s_NORCECA_Volleyball_Championship" title="2013 Men's NORCECA Volleyball Championship">2013 NORCECA Champion</a>, a gold medalist of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Men%27s_Pan-American_Volleyball_Cup" title="2008 Men's Pan-American Volleyball Cup">2008 Pan American Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/2014_FIVB_Volleyball_World_League" title="2014 FIVB Volleyball World League">2014 World League</a> and <a href="/wiki/2015_FIVB_Volleyball_Men%27s_World_Cup" title="2015 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Cup">2015 World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Anderson_(volleyball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anderson was born in <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a> to Michael and Nancy Anderson. His father, Michael Anderson, died in January 2010. He has three older sisters named Jennifer, Joelle and Amy and one older brother named Joshua. Joelle and Amy both played volleyball in their college years. Has a tattoo on his wrist in honor of his nephew Tristin, who is autistic. Also had a tattoo in honor of his late father. Matt graduated from <a href="/wiki/West_Seneca_West_Senior_High_School" title="West Seneca West Senior High School">West Seneca West Senior High School</a> in 2005.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Anderson_(volleyball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Clubs">Clubs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Anderson_(volleyball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Clubs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In his senior year, he led the West Seneca boys' volleyball team to a 17-0 regular-season record and a Sectional Finals appearance, losing to eventual state champion, Hamburg, in 3 sets. In 2004, the team were the 2004 Division I State Champions. He played club volleyball for Eden Volleyball Club in <a href="/wiki/Eden,_New_York" title="Eden, New York">Eden, New York</a>. Anderson followed an uncharacteristic growth path in his youth – only reaching his full height (6'10") as a Sophomore at Penn State. His original position was middle – playing that in his Sophomore and Junior seasons of high school. As he became more experienced in the game, he spent more time in the Outside and Right-side for Eden VBC. He ranks amongst the top alumni of the distinguished club to this day.</p>
<p>Anderson finished his career in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penn_State" title="Penn State">Penn State</a> with 1,212 kills and 82 aces, both which rank in the top 13 in Penn State history. He had a decorated career, especially in 2008 as he helped lead the Nittany Lions to their second ever <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Volleyball_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Volleyball Championship">NCAA Men's Volleyball Championship</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pepperdine" title="Pepperdine">Pepperdine</a>. He was named the NCAA Championship Most Outstanding Player for his 29 kill performance against the Waves. Other awards for 2008 include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EIVA" title="EIVA">EIVA</a> Player of the Year, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AVCA" title="AVCA">AVCA</a> National Player of the Year, AVCA First Team All-America, and All-EIVA First Team.</p>
<p>On July 1, 2008, he announced he would forgo his 2009 senior year at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penn_State" title="Penn State">Penn State</a> and signed a professional contract with <a href="/wiki/Cheonan_Hyundai_Capital_Skywalkers" title="Cheonan Hyundai Capital Skywalkers">Hyundai Capital Skywalkers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In his debut season, Anderson helped the Skywalkers claim first in the regular season before losing 3-1 in the championship series. Season 2010–2011 spent in the Italian League as a player of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Callipo_Volley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Callipo Volley (page does not exist)">Tonno Callipo Vibo Valentia</a>. Then he moved to <a href="/wiki/Modena_Volley" title="Modena Volley">Casa Modena</a>. Since 2012, he has played for Russian club, <a href="/wiki/VC_Zenit-Kazan" title="VC Zenit-Kazan">Zenit Kazan</a>. With this club, he won a bronze medal in the Russian Championship and <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_CEV_Champions_League" title="2012–13 CEV Champions League">CEV Champions League</a> in 2012–2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In the season 2013–2014, his team won the title of Russian Champion and Anderson was the <i>Most Valuable Player</i> of the Russian League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 29, 2014, Anderson suspended his volleyball career. He asked for the termination of the contract with <a href="/wiki/VC_Zenit-Kazan" title="VC Zenit-Kazan">Zenit Kazan</a> because of depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On December 26, 2014 Anderson announced his return to <a href="/wiki/VC_Zenit-Kazan" title="VC Zenit-Kazan">Zenit Kazan</a>, but only in <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_CEV_Champions_League" title="2014–15 CEV Champions League">CEV Champions League</a> matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Anderson_(volleyball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2013 <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_volleyball_team" title="United States men's national volleyball team">USA</a>, including Anderson, achieved title of <a href="/wiki/2013_Men%27s_NORCECA_Volleyball_Championship" title="2013 Men's NORCECA Volleyball Championship">NORCECA Champion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On July 20, 2014 he was of the team winning the gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIVB_Volleyball_World_League" title="2014 FIVB Volleyball World League">World League 2014</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
