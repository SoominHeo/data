<p>The <b>President of the Republic of South Africa</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_South_Africa" title="Constitution of South Africa">Constitution of South Africa</a>. From 1961 to 1994, the head of state was called the <a href="/wiki/State_President_of_South_Africa" title="State President of South Africa">State President</a>.</p>
<p>The President is elected by the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_South_Africa" title="National Assembly of South Africa">National Assembly</a>, the lower house of <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_South_Africa" title="Parliament of South Africa">Parliament</a>, and is usually the leader of the largest party, which has been the <a href="/wiki/African_National_Congress" title="African National Congress">African National Congress</a> since the first non-racial elections were held on 27 April 1994. The role was founded to be distinct from the now defunct role of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_South_Africa" title="Prime Minister of South Africa">prime minister</a>, but the two roles were merged in the 1983 constitution which specified a four-year term of office. The 1993 and later constitutions limits the president's time in office to two five-year terms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-info.gov.za_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-info.gov.za-2">[2]</a></sup> The first President to be elected under the new constitution was <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a>, and the incumbent president is <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Zuma" title="Jacob Zuma">Jacob Zuma</a>.</p>
<p>Under the interim constitution (valid from 1994 to 1996), there was a <a href="/wiki/Government_of_National_Unity_(South_Africa)" title="Government of National Unity (South Africa)">Government of National Unity</a>, in which a Member of Parliament (MP) from the largest opposition party was entitled to a position as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deputy_President" title="Deputy President">Deputy President</a>. Along with Thabo Mbeki, the last State President, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederik_Willem_de_Klerk" title="Frederik Willem de Klerk">F.W. De Klerk</a> also served as Deputy President, in his capacity as the leader of the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_(South_Africa)" title="National Party (South Africa)">National Party</a> which was the second-largest party in the new Parliament. But De Klerk later resigned and went into opposition with his party. A voluntary coalition government continues to exist under the new constitution (adopted in 1996), although there have been no appointments of opposition politicians to the post of Deputy President.</p>
<p>The President is required to be a member of the National Assembly at the time of his election. Upon his election, he immediately resigns his seat for the duration of his term. The President may be removed either by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motion_of_no-confidence" title="Motion of no-confidence">motion of no-confidence</a> or an <a href="/wiki/Impeachment" title="Impeachment">impeachment trial</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The office of the President, and the roles that come with it, were established by Chapter Five of the Constitution of South Africa which was formed by a Constituent Assembly upon the dissolution of apartheid as state policy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-info.gov.za_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-info.gov.za-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A number of manifestations of the office have existed. Aspects of these offices exist within the presidency today. The executive leadership of the British colonies of Natal and of the Cape of Good Hope were invested in their Governors, likewise was invested in the Presidents of the Boer republics of the Transvaal and the Orange Free State. Alternating sovereignty as a result of wars culminated in the Vereeniging Treaty signed in which concluded the South African War.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_Africa" title="Union of South Africa">Union of South Africa</a>, a British Dominion, was established on 31 May 1910<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and the head of state was invested in the apolitical and ceremonial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monarch_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarch of the United Kingdom">Monarch of the United Kingdom</a> (who was represented by a viceroy, the Governor-General).</p>
<p>Upon the declaration of South Africa as a republic on 31 May 1961, the State President was created,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and a realignment of power took place in subsequent years until the dissolution of the Office of the Prime Minister.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_system">Electoral system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electoral system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>South Africa has an almost unique system for the election of its president. Unlike other former British colonies and dominions who have adopted a parliamentary republican form of government and those that follow the <a href="/wiki/Westminster_system" title="Westminster system">Westminster system</a>, South Africa's President is both <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> and Commander-in-Chief of the <a href="/wiki/South_African_National_Defence_Force" title="South African National Defence Force">South African National Defence Force</a> (SANDF). Contrary to presidential systems around the world, the President of South Africa is elected by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_South_Africa" title="Parliament of South Africa">Parliament of South Africa</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> rather than by the people directly or an Electoral College,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> thus answerable to it in theory and able to influence legislation in practice as head of the majority party (presently the ANC).</p>
<p>The president is elected at the first sitting of parliament after an election, and whenever a vacancy arises. The president must be elected from the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_South_Africa" title="National Assembly of South Africa">national assembly</a>, the lower house of parliament. The <a href="/wiki/Chief_Justice_of_South_Africa" title="Chief Justice of South Africa">chief justice</a> must oversee the election. Once elected, a person is no longer a member of the national assembly. They must then be sworn in as president within five days of the election. Should a vacancy arise, the date of a new election must be set by the chief justice, but not more than 30 days after the vacancy occurs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Constitution has thus prescribed a system combining both parliamentary and presidential systems in a unique manner. Only <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_system_of_government#Mixed_republican_systems" title="List of countries by system of government">a few other countries</a> use a similar system. Between 1996 and 2003 <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> combined the two systems in an opposite way, with an elected prime minister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the presidency is the key institution, it is hedged about with numerous checks and balances that prevent its total dominance over the government, as was the case in many African countries. The presidential term is five years, and is limited to two consecutive terms. Thus the electoral system attempts (at least on paper) to prevent the accumulation of power in the president as was during Apartheid or in many other African countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oath_or_Affirmation">Oath or Affirmation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Oath or Affirmation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The President-elect will, before formally assuming office, make and subscribe an <a href="/wiki/Oath" title="Oath">oath</a> or <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Solem" title="Solem">solem</a> affirmation in the terms st out in Schedule 3 before the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Justice" title="Chief Justice">Chief Justice</a> or a Judge of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supreme_Court" title="Supreme Court">Supreme Court</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tenure_of_Office">Tenure of Office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tenure of Office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The President elected in terms of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Section_77_(1)_(a)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Section 77 (1) (a) (page does not exist)">section 77 (1) (a)</a> is subjected to sections 87 and 93 (2), hold office-</p>
<p>(a) for the period terminating on a date five years as from the date of the date of the first sitting of the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly" title="National Assembly">National Assembly</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">Constitution</a>; or</p>
<p>(b) if the <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">Parliament</a> is dissolved during such period, for the period until a President has been elected in terms of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Section_77_(1)_(b)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Section 77 (1) (b) (page does not exist)">section 77 (1) (b)</a> after such dissolution and has assumed office.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibilities_of_President">Responsibilities of President</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Responsibilities of President">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>(1) The President is responsible for the observance of the provision of the Constitution as the supreme law of the land</p>
<p>(2) The president provides with dignity the executive leadership in the interest of national unity in accordance with the Constitution and the law of the country.</p>
<p>(3) The president cannot hold any other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_office" title="Public office">public office</a> and cannot perform remunerative work outside the duties of the president's office.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Succession">Succession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Succession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to chapter five of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_South_Africa" title="Constitution of South Africa">constitution</a>, the president can only exercise the powers of his or her office while within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_South_Africa" title="Republic of South Africa">Republic of South Africa</a>. Should the president be outside of the country, or unable to fulfil the duties of the office, they may appoint an <a href="/wiki/Acting_president" title="Acting president">acting president</a>.</p>
<p>The presidential vacancy should be filled first by the <a href="/wiki/Deputy_President_of_South_Africa" title="Deputy President of South Africa">deputy president</a>, then cabinet minister selected by the president, then a cabinet minister selected by the cabinet, and finally by the <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_National_Assembly_of_South_Africa" title="Speaker of the National Assembly of South Africa">speaker of the national assembly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Presidential_powers">Presidential powers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Presidential powers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The President is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>, <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commander-in-chief" title="Commander-in-chief">commander-in-chief</a> of the armed forces of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_South_Africa" title="Republic of South Africa">Republic of South Africa</a>. The rights, responsibilities and remuneration of the President are enumerated in Chapter V of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_South_Africa" title="Constitution of South Africa">Constitution of South Africa</a> and subsequent amendments and laws passed by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_South_Africa" title="Parliament of South Africa">Parliament of South Africa</a>.</p>
<p>The executive power of the Republic is invested in the president. He appoints various officials to positions listed in the Constitution however the most significant are the ministers and justices of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Appeal_of_South_Africa" title="Supreme Court of Appeal of South Africa">Supreme Court of Appeal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Court_of_South_Africa" title="Constitutional Court of South Africa">Constitutional Court</a>. Through the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_South_Africa" title="Cabinet of South Africa">Cabinet</a>, the president implements and enforces the constitution and laws and enforces his or her political objectives. Judges are appointed on the advice of the <a href="/wiki/Judicial_Service_Commission_(South_Africa)" title="Judicial Service Commission (South Africa)">Judicial Service Commission</a>.</p>
<p>The president plays a role in the formation of legislation. He or she signs bills into laws and can do the opposite, veto them (although subject to an override), refers bills back to Parliament or to the Constitutional Court or can call for a referendum. The president summons parliament, often delivering his or her objectives and agenda in a State of the Nation Address at the beginning of each session.</p>
<p>The president is the commander-in-chief of the <a href="/wiki/South_African_National_Defence_Force" title="South African National Defence Force">South African National Defence Force</a> thereby possessing influence or control over foreign and security policy. He or she is accorded the constitutional powers to declare war and make peace, negotiate and sign (although not ratify) treaties (and the alliances that may come with them), and receives and appoints diplomatic officials, confers honours and grants pardons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_Functions_of_South_African_President">Powers and Functions of South African President</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_of_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Powers and Functions of South African President">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>(1) The president performs the following powers and functions:</p>
<p>(a) Sign bills duly passes by parliament.</p>
<p>(b) Convey meetings with Cabinet</p>
<p>(c) Refer disputes of a constitutional nature between parties represented in Parliament or between organs of state at any level of government to the Constitutional Court.</p>
<p>(d) Confer honours.</p>
<p>(e) Appoint, accredit, receive and recognise ambassadors, diplomatic representatives and other diplomatic officials.</p>
<p>(f) Appoint commissions of inquiry.</p>
<p>(g) Negotiate and sign international agreements</p>
<p>(2) The president shall consult the Executive Deputy President.</p>
<p>(a) In development and execution of the policies of the international government.</p>
<p>(b) All matters relating to the management of the Cabinet and performance of Cabinet business.</p>
<p>(c) Assign and allocation of functions contemplated in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Section_84_(5)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Section 84 (5) (page does not exist)">section 84 (5)</a> to an Executive Deputy President.</p>
<p>(3) The President exercises and performs all powers and functions assigned to him by the Constitution or any other law, except those specified in subsections (1) and (2) or where otherwise expressly or by implication provided in this Constitution in consultation with the cabinet. Provided that the cabinet may delegate it's consultation functions in terms of the subsection, with reference to any particular power or function of the president, to any Minister of Ministers.</p>
<p>(4) (a) The President in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief" title="Commander-in-Chief">Commander-in-Chief</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/National_Defence_Force" title="National Defence Force">National Defence Force</a></p>
<p>(b) The President may -</p>
<p>(ii) With approval of the Parliament, declare a state of national defence.</p>
<p>(iii) Employ the national defence force in accordance with and subject to sections 227 and 228.</p>
<p><br/>
The President is addressed as "Your Excellency" or "Mr/Madam President" and referred to as "The Honourable (name)".</p>
<p>The official seat of the President are the <a href="/wiki/Union_Buildings" title="Union Buildings">Union Buildings</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tuynhuys" title="Tuynhuys">Tuynhuys</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>. His living residences are <a href="/wiki/Mahlamba_Ndlopfu" title="Mahlamba Ndlopfu">Mahlamba Ndlopfu</a> in Pretoria, <a href="/wiki/Genadendal_Residence" title="Genadendal Residence">Genadendal</a> in Cape Town and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dr_John_L_Dube_House&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dr John L Dube House (page does not exist)">Dr John L Dube House</a> in Durban.</p>
