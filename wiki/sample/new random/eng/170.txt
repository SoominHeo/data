<p><b>FirstGroup</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SC157176_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SC157176-1">[1]</a></sup> is a British transport group, registered and operating in the United Kingdom. The company also operates transport services in Ireland, Canada and the United States. It is listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> and is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_250_Index" title="FTSE 250 Index">FTSE 250 Index</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FirstGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FirstGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg/220px-First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg/330px-First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg/440px-First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:First_Games_Transport_36222_BD12_SZY.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Wright_Eclipse_Gemini" title="Wright Eclipse Gemini">Wright Eclipse Gemini</a> bodied <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B9TL" title="Volvo B9TL">Volvo B9TL</a> in September 2012</div>

</div>
<p>FirstGroup originated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bus_deregulation_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Bus deregulation in the United Kingdom">deregulation of bus services</a> in the United Kingdom in 1986, whereby private companies purchased nationalised and municipal bus operators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 1986 the <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a> based services of the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Omnibus_Company" title="Bristol Omnibus Company">Bristol Omnibus Company</a> that were rebranded in 1985 as <a href="/wiki/Badgerline" title="Badgerline">Badgerline</a> were purchased by a <a href="/wiki/Management_buyout" title="Management buyout">management buyout</a>. As Badgerline Group, it expanded through acquisition purchasing other formerly nationalised bus companies in England and Wales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 1989 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grampian_Regional_Transport" title="Grampian Regional Transport">Grampian Regional Transport</a>, the bus operator in <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a> owned by <a href="/wiki/Grampian" title="Grampian">Grampian Regional Council</a>, was privatised in a management buyout led by its then general manager <a href="/wiki/Moir_Lockhead" title="Moir Lockhead">Moir Lockhead</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoirLockhead11April2006_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-MoirLockhead11April2006-5">[5]</a></sup> As <a href="/wiki/GRT_Group" title="GRT Group">GRT Bus Group</a>, it expanded through acquisition purchasing six former nationalised bus companies in England and Scotland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 1995 FirstBus was formed through the merger of the Badgerline and GRT Bus Groups, with fleets in England, Wales and Scotland. <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a> was selected as the headquarters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the time of the merger, FirstBus had 5,600 buses, 4,000 of which came from Badgerline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BM648_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-BM648-8">[8]</a></sup> Badgerline's Trevor Smallwood became chairman of FirstBus, while GRT head Moir Lockhead became deputy chairman and chief executive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BM648_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-BM648-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion">Expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FirstGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FirstBus continued the policy of growth by acquisition acquiring <a href="/wiki/List_of_former_municipal_bus_companies_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of former municipal bus companies of the United Kingdom">former council owned operations</a> and companies formerly owned by <a href="/wiki/National_Bus_Company_(UK)" title="National Bus Company (UK)">English, Welsh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Bus_Group" title="Scottish Bus Group">Scottish</a> nationalised operators. FirstBus went on to acquire larger urban metropolitan operators by taking advantage of the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_PTE_bus_operations#Privatisation" title="History of the PTE bus operations">privatisation of the PTE bus operations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Privatisation_of_London_bus_services" title="Privatisation of London bus services">privatisation of London bus services</a>. FirstBus acquired <a href="/wiki/GM_Buses" title="GM Buses">GM Buses North</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strathclyde_Partnership_for_Transport" title="Strathclyde Partnership for Transport">Strathclyde Buses</a> in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> in 1996, <a href="/wiki/First_South_Yorkshire" title="First South Yorkshire">Mainline</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_London" title="First London">CentreWest</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> in 1997, and <a href="/wiki/Capital_Citybus" title="Capital Citybus">Capital Citybus</a> in London in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BM648_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-BM648-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>The company was renamed FirstGroup in December 1997<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SC157176_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-SC157176-1">[1]</a></sup> after the company moved into railways in February 1996 with the <a href="/wiki/Privatisation_of_British_Rail" title="Privatisation of British Rail">Privatisation of British Rail</a>, through a 24.5% shareholding in <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Holdings" title="Great Western Holdings">Great Western Holdings</a> that won the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Western_franchise" title="Greater Western franchise">Great Western</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_North_Western" title="First North Western">North Western</a> franchises, and a 100% shareholding in <a href="/wiki/First_Great_Eastern" title="First Great Eastern">First Great Eastern</a> that ran the Great Eastern franchise from January 1997. In March 1998 FirstGroup purchased the 75.5% shares in Great Western Holdings it did not already own and rebranded the franchises <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway_(train_operating_company)" title="Great Western Railway (train operating company)">First Great Western</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_North_Western" title="First North Western">First North Western</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 1998 FirstGroup made its first overseas foray when <a href="/wiki/New_World_First_Bus" title="New World First Bus">New World First Bus</a> commenced operating bus services in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> formerly operated by <a href="/wiki/China_Motor_Bus" title="China Motor Bus">China Motor Bus</a>. FirstGroup had a 26% shareholding in the joint venture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In May 2000 FirstGroup sold its shares to joint venture partner <a href="/wiki/New_World_Development" title="New World Development">New World Development</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 1999 FirstGroup purchased Ryder Public Transport Services, a provider of school bus transportation in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2000 FirstGroup began operating the <a href="/wiki/Tramlink" title="Tramlink">London Tramlink</a> concession under contract to <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_London" title="Transport for London">Transport for London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2003 FirstGroup purchased <a href="/wiki/GB_Railways" title="GB Railways">GB Railways</a> which owned <a href="/wiki/Anglia_Railways" title="Anglia Railways">Anglia Railways</a> and <a href="/wiki/GB_Railfreight" title="GB Railfreight">GB Railfreight</a> and held 80% of the shares in <a href="/wiki/Hull_Trains" title="Hull Trains">Hull Trains</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Having not been shortlisted for the <a href="/wiki/East_Anglia_franchise" title="East Anglia franchise">Greater Anglia franchise</a>, this gave FirstGroup another chance to bid. However it was unsuccessful and the franchise was awarded to <a href="/wiki/National_Express" title="National Express">National Express</a> from April 2004 including the services operated by First Great Eastern.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2003 FirstGroup purchased a 90% shareholding in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> coach operator <a href="/wiki/Aircoach" title="Aircoach">Aircoach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2004 FirstGroup's joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Keolis" title="Keolis">Keolis</a> commenced operating the <a href="/wiki/First_TransPennine_Express" title="First TransPennine Express">First TransPennine Express</a> rail franchise, FirstGroup having a 55% shareholding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In April 2004 FirstGroup commenced operating the <a href="/wiki/First_Great_Western_Link" title="First Great Western Link">First Great Western Link</a> franchise<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and in October 2004 the <a href="/wiki/First_ScotRail" title="First ScotRail">First ScotRail</a> franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In December 2004 the remainder of <a href="/wiki/First_North_Western" title="First North Western">First North Western</a> passed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Rail" title="Northern Rail">Northern Rail</a>, some services having already been transferred to <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Trains_Wales" title="Arriva Trains Wales">Arriva Trains Wales</a> and FirstTranspennine Express.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2006 FirstGroup commenced operating the <a href="/wiki/First_Capital_Connect" title="First Capital Connect">First Capital Connect</a> franchise<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and a renewed <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway_(train_operating_company)" title="Great Western Railway (train operating company)">First Great Western</a> franchise that had been expanded to include the <a href="/wiki/First_Great_Western_Link" title="First Great Western Link">Thames Trains</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wessex_Trains" title="Wessex Trains">Wessex Trains</a> franchises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<p>In February 2007 FirstGroup agreed to buy the US-based firm <a href="/wiki/Laidlaw" title="Laidlaw">Laidlaw</a>, an operator of inter-city coaches and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yellow_school_bus" title="Yellow school bus">yellow school buses</a> across North America, for £1.9 billion (US$3.7 billion). This also gave it a controlling stake in <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound Lines</a>, the largest bus operator in North America. The Greyhound name and the names of Canadian subsidiaries of <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Canada" title="Greyhound Canada">Greyhound Canada</a> were retained, and all other Laidlaw-owned services in the United States and Canada were rebranded under the First or Greyhound names, except for <a href="/wiki/Voyageur_Colonial_Bus_Lines" title="Voyageur Colonial Bus Lines">Voyageur Colonial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grey_Goose_Bus_Lines" title="Grey Goose Bus Lines">Grey Goose</a> in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2009 <a href="/wiki/DSBFirst" title="DSBFirst">DSBFirst</a>, FirstGroup's joint venture with <a href="/wiki/DSB_(railway_company)" title="DSB (railway company)">Danish State Railways</a> commenced operating the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oresundtrain" title="Oresundtrain">Oresundtrain</a> rail franchise from <a href="/wiki/Helsing%C3%B8r" title="Helsingør">Helsingør</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niv%C3%A5" title="Nivå">Nivå</a> in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> along the <a href="/wiki/Coast_Line_(Denmark)" title="Coast Line (Denmark)">Kystbanen</a> line and over the <a href="/wiki/%C3%98resund_Bridge" title="Øresund Bridge">Øresund Bridge</a> to <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6_Central_Station" title="Malmö Central Station">Malmö</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4xj%C3%B6" title="Växjö">Växjö</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalmar" title="Kalmar">Kalmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karlskrona" title="Karlskrona">Karlskrona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg_Central_Station" title="Gothenburg Central Station">Gothenburg</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. FirstGroup had a 25% shareholding in the Danish business and 20% in the Swedish business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> By March 2011 this was 30%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnnualReport2011_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-AnnualReport2011-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2009 FirstGroup made a takeover offer for fellow transport operator <a href="/wiki/National_Express" title="National Express">National Express</a>, which was struggling with debt at the time and was struggling to hold onto its <a href="/wiki/National_Express_East_Coast" title="National Express East Coast">National Express East Coast</a> rail franchise. This was rejected, National Express saying it did not "consider it appropriate" at the time to discuss a takeover. FirstGroup believed that there was "significant industrial and commercial logic" for a merger, but National Express wished to focus on its own initiatives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatExDeclinesBid_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-NatExDeclinesBid-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_years">Recent years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FirstGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recent years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2010 FirstGroup sold its railfreight business <a href="/wiki/GB_Railfreight" title="GB Railfreight">First GBRf</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eurotunnel_Group" title="Eurotunnel Group">Eurotunnel Group</a> for £31 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> ending the group's involvement in rail freight transport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gbrf_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-gbrf-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2010 former <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a> managing director <a href="/wiki/Tim_O%27Toole_(businessman)" title="Tim O'Toole (businessman)">Tim O'Toole</a>, already a board member since May 2009 and Chief Operating Officer and Deputy Chief Executive since June 2010, was announced as the successor to retiring group Chief Executive Officer Moir Lockhead with effect from 31 March 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2011 FirstGroup's German bus operations were sold to Marwyn European Transport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2011 <a href="/wiki/DSBFirst" title="DSBFirst">DSBFirst</a> ceased operating the Swedish part of the operation after difficulties encountered by Danish State Railways over cross subsidies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2012 FirstGroup provided bus and coach services for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">London 2012 Olympic Games</a>. This involved the provision of venue shuttle and park and ride services, services connecting peripheral park and ride sites on the <a href="/wiki/M25_motorway" title="M25 motorway">M25</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Park,_London" title="Olympic Park, London">Olympic Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ebbsfleet_Valley" title="Ebbsfleet Valley">Ebbsfleet</a>, and a nationwide network of express coaches to the Olympic Park and the <a href="/wiki/Weymouth_and_Portland_National_Sailing_Academy" title="Weymouth and Portland National Sailing Academy">Weymouth and Portland sailing venue</a>. The services required around 900 vehicles in total, although some were sub-contracted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2013 most of the <a href="/wiki/First_London" title="First London">First London</a> bus operations were sold to <a href="/wiki/Go-Ahead_London" title="Go-Ahead London">Go-Ahead London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metroline" title="Metroline">Metroline</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tower_Transit" title="Tower Transit">Tower Transit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2015 FirstGroup was unsuccessful in bidding for the <a href="/wiki/ScotRail_(brand)" title="ScotRail (brand)">ScotRail franchise</a>, which is now run by <a href="/wiki/Abellio_ScotRail" title="Abellio ScotRail">Abellio ScotRail</a>. In December 2015, FirstGroup was awarded the next <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgi20151209_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-rgi20151209-42">[42]</a></sup> The new franchise commenced on 1 April 2016 with a commitment to introduce new trains, routes and faster journey times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgi20151209_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-rgi20151209-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2016 the Department for Transport announced that FirstGroup had been shortlisted to operate the <a href="/wiki/South_Western_franchise" title="South Western franchise">South Western franchise</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgi20160204_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-rgi20160204-43">[43]</a></sup> The bidding process is currently underway with the winner due to be announced in early 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgi20160204_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-rgi20160204-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2016 <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Trains" title="East Coast Trains">East Coast Trains</a> was granted a 10 track-access agreement to operate services on the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a> from 2021.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In October 2016 First Transit commenced operating the <a href="/wiki/A-train_(Denton_County_Transportation_Authority)" title="A-train (Denton County Transportation Authority)">A-train</a> under contract to the <a href="/wiki/Denton_County_Transportation_Authority" title="Denton County Transportation Authority">Denton County Transportation Authority</a>, its first rail operation in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FirstGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>FirstGroup is Britain's largest bus operator, running more than 20% of all local bus services. A fleet of nearly 9,000 buses carries some 2.9 million passengers a day in more than 40 major towns and cities. FirstGroup also runs passenger rail services in the UK. Passenger rail franchises consist of <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway_(train_operating_company)" title="Great Western Railway (train operating company)">Great Western Railway</a> and <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>. It also operates <a href="/wiki/Hull_Trains" title="Hull Trains">Hull Trains</a>, a non-franchised open access passenger train service. FirstGroup operates tram services on the <a href="/wiki/Tramlink" title="Tramlink">London Tramlink</a> network carrying approximately 24 million passengers a year on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_London" title="Transport for London">Transport for London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>In North America, FirstGroup has several divisions: <a href="/wiki/First_Student_(United_States)" title="First Student (United States)">First Student</a>, which runs school bus routes;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/First_Transit" title="First Transit">First Transit</a>, which holds many city and county public transport contracts;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> and First Vehicle Services, which maintains vehicles for many corporations, organisations and local governments, including the other First divisions and Greyhound lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Canadian operations are provided through its <a href="/wiki/First_Student_Canada" title="First Student Canada">First Student Canada</a> subsidiary, which operates school buses and fulfills public transport contracts.</p>
<p>FirstGroup owns and operates the <a href="/wiki/Aircoach" title="Aircoach">Aircoach</a> service in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fglta_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-fglta-51">[51]</a></sup> linking <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin Airport</a> with the city centre, the south side of Dublin, Greystones and <a href="/wiki/Bray" title="Bray">Bray</a> as well as long-distance express services runs to <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">Cork</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>. Aircoach also operates car park shuttles at Dublin Airport. Until 2009 Aircoach did not carry corporate branding. Buses introduced since January 2009 on Aircoach carry a FirstGroup logo alongside the Aircoach logo.</p>

<p>FirstGroup has always had a consistent brand and uses the First brand for most of its operations. FirstBus began to apply a standard corporate typeface to its fleet names in the late 1990s, introducing the stylised <i>f</i> logo depicting a road. A corporate white, pink and blue livery was introduced, nicknamed "<a href="/wiki/Barbie" title="Barbie">Barbie</a>", to new buses, while further bus company acquisitions continued. Inherited bus fleets were initially left in their original colours with First fleet names, with the intention that the Barbie scheme would stand for a set service quality. Later older buses received a modified "Barbie 2" livery.</p>
<p>As part of its corporate branding, First subsequently removed all local branding for its bus services, buses simply carried the 'First' brand, although each company still operated independently. In 2012, the group began to introduce a new purple, white and lilac livery to its bus fleets, which also reinstated local branding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> In January 2014, the company rebranded its <a href="/wiki/First_West_of_England" title="First West of England">First Somerset &amp; Avon</a> operations in <a href="/wiki/Bridgwater" title="Bridgwater">Bridgwater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taunton" title="Taunton">Taunton</a> as <a href="/wiki/The_Buses_of_Somerset" title="The Buses of Somerset">The Buses of Somerset</a>, using a two-tone green livery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/First_TransPennine_Express" title="First TransPennine Express">First TransPennine Express</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hull_Trains" title="Hull Trains">Hull Trains</a> carry a predominantly blue livery, including white, pink and purple. This was also used by First Great Western until 20 September 2015, when the First Great Western operation was re-branded as Great Western Railway, with a new logo and dark green livery paying homage to the original <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">Great Western Railway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tramlink" title="Tramlink">London Tramlink</a> operations are painted in white, green and blue as per Transport for London requirements. In Scotland, <a href="/wiki/First_ScotRail" title="First ScotRail">First ScotRail</a> operated with a blue livery with white <a href="/wiki/Saltire" title="Saltire">saltire</a> markings on the carriage ends, as mandated by the Scottish Government's transport agency <a href="/wiki/Transport_Scotland" title="Transport Scotland">Transport Scotland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Similarly, in North America, all owned and operated services are operated under the First or Greyhound brands except in the Canadian provinces of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> for regulatory reasons, and vehicles operated under contract to public agencies, which carry the branding of the agency that owns the particular bus. All American subsidiaries of <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound Lines</a> are now operated under the Greyhound name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_UK" title="Greyhound UK">Greyhound UK</a> services in the United Kingdom were painted in the same navy blue and grey livery introduced to the American Greyhound fleet in 2009.</p>
