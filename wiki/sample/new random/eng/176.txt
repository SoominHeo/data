<p><i><b>Rampage</b></i> is a 1986 <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> by <a href="/wiki/Midway_Games" title="Midway Games">Bally Midway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Players take control of gigantic monsters trying to survive against onslaughts of military forces. Each round is completed when a particular city is completely reduced to rubble. <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> currently owns all rights to the property.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rampage_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Up to three simultaneous players control gigantic monsters who were formerly human. The game's protagonists are George, a <a href="/wiki/King_Kong" title="King Kong">King Kong</a>-like gigantic gorilla transformed by an experimental vitamin, Lizzie, a <a href="/wiki/Godzilla" title="Godzilla">Godzilla</a>-like dinosaur/lizard transformed by a radioactive lake, and Ralph, a giant <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> transformed by a food additive. As monsters, they need to raze all buildings in a high-rise city to advance to the next level, eating people and destroying helicopters, tanks, taxis, police cars, boats, and trolleys along the way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The monsters can climb the buildings, punching them to pieces on the way down which will eventually reduce them to rubble. The various people can also be punched or grabbed and food items can be eaten. The player's monster receives damage from enemy bullets, sticks of dynamite, shells, punches from other monsters and falls. Damage is recovered by eating the various food items such as fruit, roast chicken, or even the soldiers. If a monster takes too much damage, it reverts into a naked human and starts walking off the screen sideways, covering its modesty with its hands (and in this state, can be eaten by another monster). If the player continues, the human mutates back into the monster or (if the human walked off the screen) flies in on a blimp (but has lost their score), with a full life bar.</p>
<p>Smashing open windows generally reveals an item or person of interest, which may be helpful or harmful. Helpful items include food or money, while dangerous ones include bombs, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_appliance" title="Electrical appliance">electrical appliances</a>, and cigarettes. Some items can be both; for example, a toaster is dangerous until the toast pops up, and a photographer must be eaten quickly before he dazzles the player's monster with his flash, causing it to fall. When a civilian is present waving their hands at a window signaling for help, a player's points rapidly increase when the person is grabbed. Each monster can hold only one type of person: George can hold women, Lizzy can hold middle-aged men, and Ralph can hold businessmen.</p>
<p><i>Rampage</i> is set over the course of 128 days in cities across North America. The game starts in <a href="/wiki/Peoria,_Illinois" title="Peoria, Illinois">Peoria, Illinois</a> and ends in <a href="/wiki/Plano,_Illinois" title="Plano, Illinois">Plano, Illinois</a>. In Plano, players receive a "mega vitamin bonus" which heals all the monsters and provides a large point bonus. After this, the cycle of cities repeats five times. After 768 days, the game resets back to Day 1.</p>
<p>Some of the home port versions of the game start in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a> and end in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a> after going all around North America. The rampage travels through two Canadian provinces and forty-three United States states. Only <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> are spared.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_Ports">Release and Ports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rampage_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Release and Ports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally released for the arcades in 1986, <i>Rampage</i> was ported to most <a href="/wiki/Home_computer" title="Home computer">home computers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Video_game_console" title="Video game console">video game consoles</a> of its time, including the <a href="/wiki/Atari_2600" title="Atari 2600">Atari 2600</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atari_7800" title="Atari 7800">Atari 7800</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atari_Lynx" title="Atari Lynx">Atari Lynx</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atari_8-bit" title="Atari 8-bit">Atari 8-bit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atari_ST" title="Atari ST">Atari ST</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">Commodore 64</a>, <a href="/wiki/MS-DOS" title="MS-DOS">MS-DOS</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBM_PC" title="IBM PC">IBM PC</a>, <a href="/wiki/ZX_Spectrum" title="ZX Spectrum">ZX Spectrum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amstrad_CPC" title="Amstrad CPC">Amstrad CPC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">NES</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fangame" title="Fangame">Fangame</a> For <a href="/wiki/Super_Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Super Nintendo Entertainment System">SNES</a> was to but <a href="/wiki/Homebrew_(video_games)" title="Homebrew (video games)">Homebrew</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sega_Master_System" title="Sega Master System">Sega Master System</a>. The Atari Lynx version adds a special fourth character named Larry, a giant <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">rat</a>. The NES version excludes Ralph, reducing the number of monsters to two. In 1997 <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Electronics" title="Tiger Electronics">Tiger Electronics</a> released a handheld LCD version of the game</p>
<p>The original arcade version of <i>Rampage</i> was included in various compilation games. In 1999 it was included in <i><a href="/wiki/Arcade_Party_Pak" title="Arcade Party Pak">Arcade Party Pak</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation">PlayStation</a>. In 2003 it was included in <i><a href="/wiki/Midway_Arcade_Treasures" title="Midway Arcade Treasures">Midway Arcade Treasures</a></i>, a compilation of arcade games for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nintendo_GameCube" title="Nintendo GameCube">Nintendo GameCube</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a>. In 2005 it was included in <i><a href="/wiki/Midway_Arcade_Treasures:_Extended_Play" title="Midway Arcade Treasures: Extended Play">Midway Arcade Treasures: Extended Play</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a>. In 2006 it was a bonus feature in <i><a href="/wiki/Rampage:_Total_Destruction" title="Rampage: Total Destruction">Rampage: Total Destruction</a></i>. Finally in 2012, it was included in the compilation <i><a href="/wiki/Midway_Arcade_Origins" title="Midway Arcade Origins">Midway Arcade Origins</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The game was also an unlockable in the 2015 game <i><a href="/wiki/Lego_Dimensions" title="Lego Dimensions">Lego Dimensions</a></i>. In that game, a human sized version of George the ape appears in one of the sidequests. In the <i>Midway Arcade World</i> level of the game, the player aids him in wrecking the area and protecting him from civilians to get a higher score than Ralph and Lizzie.</p>
<p>In July 2000, <a href="/wiki/Midway_Games" title="Midway Games">Midway</a> licensed <i>Rampage</i>, along with other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Williams_Electronics" title="Williams Electronics">Williams Electronics</a> games, to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macromedia_Shockwave" title="Macromedia Shockwave">Shockwave</a> for use in an online applet to demonstrate the power of the shockwave web content platform, entitled <i>Shockwave Arcade Collection</i>. The conversion was created by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_Eclipse" title="Digital Eclipse">Digital Eclipse</a>. <i>Rampage</i> was also ported to <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Midway_Games" title="Midway Games">Midway</a> arcade app.</p>
<p>Unlike the original arcade game, most of the home ports (such as the NES, Sega, and Atari Lynx versions) actually end, rather than repeating levels endlessly. The <a href="/wiki/Amstrad_CPC" title="Amstrad CPC">Amstrad CPC</a> port (published by Activision) has in-game music ripped from the basement level of the arcade game <i><a href="/wiki/Trojan_(video_game)" title="Trojan (video game)">Trojan</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> though <i>Trojan</i> was never ported to the Amstrad, a finished version was completed for the <a href="/wiki/ZX_Spectrum" title="ZX Spectrum">ZX Spectrum</a> by Clive Townsend and was to be published by <a href="/wiki/Elite_Systems" title="Elite Systems">Elite Systems</a>, but was never officially released.</p>

<p><i><a href="/wiki/Computer_Gaming_World" title="Computer Gaming World">Computer Gaming World</a></i> approved of the MS-DOS adaptation of <i>Rampage</i>, especially for those with computers faster than the original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBM_PC" title="IBM PC">IBM PC</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Enhanced_Graphics_Adapter" title="Enhanced Graphics Adapter">EGA</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_card" title="Video card">video card</a>. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rampage_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequels">Sequels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rampage_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sequels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>About a decade later, a remake was released entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Rampage_World_Tour" title="Rampage World Tour">Rampage World Tour</a></i>, later followed by console-exclusive games including <i><a href="/wiki/Rampage_2:_Universal_Tour" title="Rampage 2: Universal Tour">Rampage 2: Universal Tour</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rampage_Through_Time" title="Rampage Through Time">Rampage Through Time</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Rampage_Puzzle_Attack" title="Rampage Puzzle Attack">Rampage Puzzle Attack</a></i>. The latest game in the series is <i><a href="/wiki/Rampage:_Total_Destruction" title="Rampage: Total Destruction">Rampage: Total Destruction</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_adaptation">Film adaptation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rampage_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Film adaptation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 18, 2011 it was announced that a theatrical film adaptation based on the game is in tentative development by <a href="/wiki/New_Line_Cinema" title="New Line Cinema">New Line Cinema</a> with John Rickard set to direct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On June 22, 2015 it was announced that actor <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">Dwayne Johnson</a> and producer <a href="/wiki/Beau_Flynn" title="Beau Flynn">Beau Flynn</a> were attached to the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On July 22, 2015 it was announced that <i><a href="/wiki/San_Andreas_(film)" title="San Andreas (film)">San Andreas</a></i> director <a href="/wiki/Brad_Peyton" title="Brad Peyton">Brad Peyton</a> will direct the film <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On July 20, 2016, it was reported that production on the film will begin in March 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
