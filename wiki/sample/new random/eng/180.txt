<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sonja was born in 4 July 1937 in Red Cross Clinic in Oslo, the daughter of clothing merchant Karl August Haraldsen (1889–1959) and Dagny Ulrichsen (1896–1994).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her siblings were Haakon Haraldsen (1919–2016),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Gry Henriksen (1924–1971) and Karl Herman Haraldsen (1929–1936). She grew up at Tuengen Allé 1B in the district of <a href="/wiki/Vinderen" title="Vinderen">Vinderen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> and completed her lower secondary schooling in 1954. She received a diploma in dressmaking and tailoring at the Oslo Vocational School, and a diploma from École Professionnelle des Jeunes Filles (a <a href="/wiki/Finishing_school" title="Finishing school">finishing school</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Lausanne" title="Lausanne">Lausanne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. There, she studied accounting, fashion design, and social science. She returned to Norway for further studies and received an undergraduate degree (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_History" title="Art History">Art History</a>) from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oslo" title="University of Oslo">University of Oslo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_as_Crown_Princess">Marriage and as Crown Princess</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriage and as Crown Princess">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sonja became engaged to then Crown Prince Harald in March 1968. They had been dating for nine years, although their relationship had been kept secret because of her non-royal status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The Crown Prince made it clear to his father, King <a href="/wiki/Olav_V_of_Norway" title="Olav V of Norway">Olav V</a>, that he would remain unmarried for life unless he could marry her. This would in effect have put an end to the rule of his family, and likely to the monarchy in Norway, as Harald was the sole heir to the throne. Faced with having to choose one of his relatives from the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Royal_Family" title="Danish Royal Family">Danish Royal Family</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dukes_of_Schleswig-Holstein" title="Dukes of Schleswig-Holstein">Dukes of Schleswig-Holstein</a> or even the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Dukes_of_Oldenburg" title="Grand Dukes of Oldenburg">Grand Dukes of Oldenburg</a> as his new heir in place of his son, Olav V consulted the government for advice and, as a result, the couple was wed on 29 August 1968, at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oslo_Domkirke" title="Oslo Domkirke">Oslo Domkirke</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>. She thus acquired the style of <i><a href="/wiki/Royal_Highness" title="Royal Highness">Royal Highness</a></i> and the title of Crown Princess of Norway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Immediately after the wedding, the new Crown Princess began to carry out her royal duties, traveling extensively in Norway and abroad. In 1972 she was involved in establishing Princess Märtha Louise’s Fund, which provides assistance to disabled children in Norway. She has taken active part in large-scale initiatives to raise funds for international refugees and spent time in the 1970s visiting <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_people" title="Vietnamese people">Vietnamese</a> boat refugees in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1987 to 1990, Crown Princess Sonja served as Vice President of the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Red_Cross" title="Norwegian Red Cross">Norwegian Red Cross</a>. She was responsible for the organisation’s international activities. She took part in a Red Cross delegation to <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Queen_Sonja_International_Music_Competition">The Queen Sonja International Music Competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Queen Sonja International Music Competition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The then-Crown Princess Sonja established the <a href="/wiki/Queen_Sonja_International_Music_Competition" title="Queen Sonja International Music Competition">Queen Sonja International Music Competition</a> in 1988. It was originally for pianists, but in 1995 the competition became only for singers. The jury consists of diverse authoritative figures in opera and the winners receive a cash amount and prestigious engagements at Norwegian music institutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kongehuset.no_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-kongehuset.no-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Queen">As Queen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: As Queen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Following the death of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Olav_V" title="King Olav V">King Olav V</a> on 17 January 1991, Sonja became Norway's first <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">queen consort</a> in 53 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Queen Sonja accompanied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Harald_V" title="King Harald V">King Harald V</a> when he swore his oath to uphold the Constitution in the Storting on 21 January 1991. It was the first time in 69 years that a Norwegian queen had been present in the Storting. Since his accession, Queen Sonja has accompanied the King to the formal opening of the autumn session of the Storting and the reading of the Speech from the Throne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In accordance with their own wishes, the King and Queen were <a href="/wiki/Consecration" title="Consecration">consecrated</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nidaros_Cathedral" title="Nidaros Cathedral">Nidaros Cathedral</a> in Trondheim on 23 June 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Following the consecration, the King and Queen conducted a 10-day tour of Southern Norway. In 1992, the entire Royal Family conducted a 22-day tour of Norway’s four northernmost counties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Queen accompanies the King on official state visits abroad. She acts as the hostess when foreign heads of state officially visit Norway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The Queen has also given lectures on Norway as a tourist destination on several occasions during official state visits abroad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Queen Sonja became the first queen ever to visit <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The Queen was there to open the Norwegian <a href="/wiki/Troll_(research_station)" title="Troll (research station)">Troll research station</a> in the country's Antarctic dependency, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Maud_Land" title="Queen Maud Land">Queen Maud Land</a>. The Queen flew in on one of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Norwegian_Air_Force" title="Royal Norwegian Air Force">Royal Norwegian Air Force's</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-130" title="C-130">C-130H Hercules</a> <a href="/wiki/Military_transport_aircraft" title="Military transport aircraft">transport aircraft</a>, landing at <a href="/wiki/Troll_Airfield" title="Troll Airfield">Troll Airfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Queen is appointed a Rear Admiral in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Norwegian_Navy" title="Royal Norwegian Navy">Royal Norwegian Navy</a> and a Brigadier in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_army" title="Norwegian army">Norwegian army</a>. She has undergone a basic officer training course and has participated in exercises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Queen_Sonja.E2.80.99s_School_Award">Queen Sonja’s School Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Queen Sonja’s School Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Queen Sonja’s School Award was established in 2006 and is awarded to schools who have "demonstrated excellence in its efforts to promote inclusion and equality".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Art">Art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Queen is a longtime avid photographer and has a keen interest in art.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> She is a <a href="/wiki/Printmaking" title="Printmaking">printmaker</a>, and held exhibitions with artists <a href="/wiki/Kjell_Nupen" title="Kjell Nupen">Kjell Nupen</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%98rnulf_Opdahl" title="Ørnulf Opdahl">Ørnulf Opdahl</a> in 2011 and 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Queen Sonja Nordic Art Award was established in 2011 with Tiina Kivinen from Finland being the first recipient in 2012. The prize will be awarded every other year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles.2C_styles.2C_honours_and_arms">Titles, styles, honours and arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Titles, styles, honours and arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles_and_styles">Titles and styles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Titles and styles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>4 July 1937 – 29 August 1968:</b> <i>Miss</i> Sonja Haraldsen.</li>
<li><b>29 August 1968 – 17 January 1991: </b><i>Her Royal Highness</i> The Crown Princess of Norway.</li>
<li><b>17 January 1991 - present: </b><i>Her Majesty</i> The Queen of Norway.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Sonja_of_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_honours_of_the_Norwegian_Royal_Family_by_country" title="List of honours of the Norwegian Royal Family by country">List of honours of the Norwegian Royal Family by country</a></div>
<p>In 1982 she was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Nansen_Refugee_Award" title="Nansen Refugee Award">Nansen Refugee Award</a>. In 2007, she received the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holmenkollen_medal" title="Holmenkollen medal">Holmenkollen medal</a> with <a href="/wiki/Simon_Ammann" title="Simon Ammann">Simon Ammann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frode_Estil" title="Frode Estil">Frode Estil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Odd-Bj%C3%B8rn_Hjelmeset" title="Odd-Bjørn Hjelmeset">Odd-Bjørn Hjelmeset</a>, and her husband, King Harald V.</p>
<p>Queen Sonja also received an Honorary Doctorate from <a href="/wiki/Heriot-Watt_University" title="Heriot-Watt University">Heriot-Watt University</a> in 1994 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
