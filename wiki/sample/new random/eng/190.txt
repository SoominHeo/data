<p><i><b>Super Rookie</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">신입사원</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Sin-ip Sa-won</span></i>; lit. "New Employee") is a 2005 <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> television series starring <a href="/wiki/Eric_Mun" title="Eric Mun">Eric Mun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Han_Ga-in" title="Han Ga-in">Han Ga-in</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oh_Ji-ho" title="Oh Ji-ho">Oh Ji-ho</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lee_So-yeon_(actress)" title="Lee So-yeon (actress)">Lee So-yeon</a>. It aired on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> from March 23 to May 26, 2005 on Wednesdays and Thursdays at 21:55 for 20 episodes. The workplace comedy is a <a href="/wiki/Satire" title="Satire">satire</a> on Korea's corporate culture and unemployment among the country's younger generation. It scored solid viewership ratings in the 20% range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Rookie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kang Ho (<a href="/wiki/Eric_Mun" title="Eric Mun">Eric Mun</a>) is just an average guy who got a college degree in physical education from a second-rate, provincial university. Disheveled, unskilled, and unemployed, Kang Ho has wasted most of his life kickboxing and reading comic books, much to the frustration of his family and friends who think he's just a freeloader. He sends his resumes to many companies, but nobody gives him a shot because he has no experience and no apparent prospects. One day, Kang Ho decides to apply to the same prestigious company that his handsome, successful classmate Bong-sam (<a href="/wiki/Oh_Ji-ho" title="Oh Ji-ho">Oh Ji-ho</a>) works at. He completely bungles the interview process, but thanks to a computer error, he gets hired as the number one recruit. Struggling in a job he's totally unqualified for, Kang Ho gets by with his enthusiasm and street smarts. His co-workers include Bong-sam, an overachiever trapped by his own ambitions; Mi-ok, a mousy, bespectacled contract worker (or "<a href="/wiki/Temporary_work" title="Temporary work">temp</a>") who was dumped by Bong-sam; and Hyun-ah, the privileged daughter of a company executive.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcast">International broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Rookie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fuji_Television" title="Fuji Television">Fuji TV</a> reportedly paid ₩2 billion for the drama's broadcasting rights in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It was well received by Japanese viewers when it began airing on October 27, 2005, which contributed to <a href="/wiki/Eric_Mun" title="Eric Mun">Eric Mun</a>'s increased popularity in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It re-aired on cable channel KNTV from January 19 to February 24, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Thailand first aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBTV_Channel_7" title="BBTV Channel 7">Channel 7</a> from March 25 to May 7, 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
