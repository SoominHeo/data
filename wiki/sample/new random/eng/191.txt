<p><b>Le Mans Football Club</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ləmɑ̃]</a></span>; commonly referred to as <b>Le MUC</b> or simply <b>Le Mans</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Football_in_France" title="Football in France">French association football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Le_Mans" title="Le Mans">Le Mans</a>. The club was founded in 1985 as a result of a merger under the name <b>Le Mans Union Club 72</b>. In 2010, Le Mans changed its name to Le Mans FC to coincide with the re-modeling of the club, which includes moving into a new stadium, <a href="/wiki/MMArena" title="MMArena">MMArena</a>, which opened in January 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The club played in <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a>, the second level of <a href="/wiki/Football_in_France" title="Football in France">French football</a> having suffered relegation from <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> following the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Ligue_1" title="2009–10 Ligue 1">2009–10 season</a>. Due to financial difficulties, the club lost its professional status in 2013, went bankrupt, and reformed in the <a href="/wiki/French_football_league_system" title="French football league system">Division d'Honneur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maine_(province)" title="Maine (province)">Maine</a> for the 2013–14 season. The club won promotion back to <a href="/wiki/Championnat_de_France_Amateur_2" title="Championnat de France Amateur 2">CFA 2</a> at the first time of asking, and still play in that league.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Le_Mans_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Le Mans Sports Club were founded in 1900, but it wasn't until 1908 that a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club existed within it. In 1910, Le Mans qualified for the Championnat de la France in 1910, but were heavily overturned by Saint-Servan. Gaining a huge reputation up to World War I, Le Mans SC plunged into obscurity by World War II before joining the a war league in 1942.</p>
<p>The football section of Union Sportive du Mans was founded in 1903.</p>
<p>The current club was formed as a result of a merger between Union Sportive du Mans and Le Mans Sports Club, on 12 June 1985. Upon its foundation, former football player Bernard Deferrez was installed as manager. Le Mans UC spent the majority of its infancy in Ligue 2. In the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Ligue_1" title="2003–04 Ligue 1">2003–04 season</a>, the club achieved promotion to <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> for the first time, but were immediately relegated. Le Mans returned to the first division for the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Ligue_1" title="2005–06 Ligue 1">2005–06 season</a> and successfully remained in the league for the next four seasons. The club suffered relegated back to Ligue 2 in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Ligue_1" title="2009–10 Ligue 1">2009–10 season</a>. Midway through the campaign, on 2 December 2009, Le Mans announced that it was changing its name from Le Mans Union Club 72 to Le Mans FC.</p>
<p>Le Mans moved to the MMArena midway through the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ligue_2" title="2010–11 Ligue 2">2010–11 season</a>, comfortably in the promotion spots for a return to Ligue 1, but a bad run sees them finish 4th, missing promotion on goal difference. The failure to achieve promotion is costly, as the club sees it's payroll limited by the DNCG. Many players leave, and relegation is only narrowly avoided. The club survives by appeal an attempt by DNCG to relegate them to <a href="/wiki/Championnat_National" title="Championnat National">Championnat National</a>. The following <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Ligue_2" title="2012–13 Ligue 2">season</a> they are relegated on the field, and a long summer of legal battles sees them liqudated and reforming in <a href="/wiki/Maine_(province)" title="Maine (province)">Maine (province)</a> Division d'Honneur as an amateur club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Promotion to <a href="/wiki/Championnat_de_France_Amateur_2" title="Championnat de France Amateur 2">Championnat de France Amateur 2</a> was achieved at the first attempt, and promotion from that division was only narrowly missed in 2014–15 and 2015–16.</p>
