<p><b>Sara Gilbert</b> (born <b>Sara Rebecca Abeles</b>; January 29, 1975) is an American actress, best known for her role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darlene_Conner" title="Darlene Conner">Darlene Conner</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Situation_comedy" title="Situation comedy">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Roseanne" title="Roseanne">Roseanne</a></i> from 1988 to 1997, for which she received two <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She is also known as co-host and creator of the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> daytime talk show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Talk_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Talk (U.S. TV series)">The Talk</a></i> and for her recurring role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leslie_Winkle" title="Leslie Winkle">Leslie Winkle</a> on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Bang_Theory" title="The Big Bang Theory">The Big Bang Theory</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sara_Gilbert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gilbert was born Sara Rebecca Abeles in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-2">[2]</a></sup> the daughter of Barbara Cowan (née Crane) and Harold Abeles. Both her parents are <a href="/wiki/American_Jews" title="American Jews">Jewish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her mother is the daughter of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Honeymooners" title="The Honeymooners">The Honeymooners</a></i> creator <a href="/wiki/Harry_Crane" title="Harry Crane">Harry Crane</a>. Gilbert has four older siblings. Her two older siblings on her mother's side, <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Gilbert" title="Melissa Gilbert">Melissa Gilbert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Gilbert" title="Jonathan Gilbert">Jonathan Gilbert</a>, were stars of <i><a href="/wiki/Little_House_on_the_Prairie_(TV_series)" title="Little House on the Prairie (TV series)">Little House on the Prairie</a></i>. In 1984, Sara took the last name Gilbert for <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gilbert_(actor)" title="Paul Gilbert (actor)">Paul Gilbert</a>, her mother's first husband. Gilbert also has two older siblings on her father's side, sister Patrice and brother Joseph.</p>
<p>Gilbert graduated from <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> in 1997 with honors, majoring in Art with an emphasis on Photography.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_career">Television career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sara_Gilbert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Television career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gilbert decided at age six that she wanted to be an actress after her older sister, <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Gilbert" title="Melissa Gilbert">Melissa Gilbert</a>, got a plaque on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Following appearances in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_movie" title="Television movie">television movies</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">commercial</a> for <a href="/wiki/Kool-Aid" title="Kool-Aid">Kool-Aid</a>, at 13 she landed the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darlene_Conner" title="Darlene Conner">Darlene Conner</a>, the sarcastic middle child, in <i><a href="/wiki/Roseanne" title="Roseanne">Roseanne</a></i>. Sara was a cast member in the show's nine-year run (1988–97), for which she wrote a fourth-season episode story (the teleplay was written by the Guild Writers for the show) called "Don't Make Me Over". Her contribution was considered so important to <i>Roseanne</i> that the show's producers juggled storylines and taping schedules to allow her to study at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> while remaining in the cast, shooting remote segments of Darlene at a soundstage in New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gilbert has appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/24_(TV_series)" title="24 (TV series)">24</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Will_%26_Grace" title="Will &amp; Grace">Will &amp; Grace</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Practice_(TV_series)" title="Private Practice (TV series)">Private Practice</a></i>. Starting in 2004, she had a recurring role as smart but sarcastic medical student Jane Figler on the long-running hospital drama <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>; her appearances spanned seasons 11-13.</p>

<p>Gilbert returned to <a href="/wiki/Prime_time" title="Prime time">prime time</a> television in the fall of 2005 in the <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Twins_(TV_series)" title="Twins (TV series)">Twins</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">The WB</a> network. The sitcom was canceled after one season, when the WB and <a href="/wiki/UPN" title="UPN">UPN</a> networks merged as <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a> and dropped a number of shows. She also had a recurring role on the short-lived <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/The_Class_(TV_series)" title="The Class (TV series)">The Class</a></i> as Fern Velch.</p>
<p>From 2007 to 2010, Gilbert has appeared in episodes of the CBS TV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Bang_Theory" title="The Big Bang Theory">The Big Bang Theory</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leslie_Winkle" title="Leslie Winkle">Leslie Winkle</a>, a scientist friend of <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Hofstadter" title="Leonard Hofstadter">Leonard Hofstadter</a> played by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Galecki" title="Johnny Galecki">Johnny Galecki</a>, who had previously played Gilbert's love interest David Healy on <i><a href="/wiki/Roseanne" title="Roseanne">Roseanne</a></i>. In the second episode of the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Bang_Theory" title="The Big Bang Theory">The Big Bang Theory</a></i>, Gilbert was elevated to the show's main cast, portraying again a potential love interest of Galecki's character, and at one point a love interest to <a href="/wiki/Howard_Wolowitz" title="Howard Wolowitz">Howard Wolowitz</a> (<a href="/wiki/Simon_Helberg" title="Simon Helberg">Simon Helberg</a>). By January 2009, it was announced that the writers did not know how to write for her character full-time and her status was reduced from regular to recurring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gilbert is a co-host and executive producer of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Talk_(TV_series)" title="The Talk (TV series)">The Talk</a></i>, a talk show that premiered October 18, 2010. Gilbert appears alongside <a href="/wiki/Julie_Chen" title="Julie Chen">Julie Chen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Osbourne" title="Sharon Osbourne">Sharon Osbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Underwood" title="Sheryl Underwood">Sheryl Underwood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aisha_Tyler" title="Aisha Tyler">Aisha Tyler</a>. Gilbert won a Daytime Emmy in 2016 for Outstanding Entertainment Talk Show as producer and creator of the show.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_career">Film career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sara_Gilbert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gilbert appeared in two films alongside <a href="/wiki/Drew_Barrymore" title="Drew Barrymore">Drew Barrymore</a>, in <i><a href="/wiki/Poison_Ivy_(1992_film)" title="Poison Ivy (1992 film)">Poison Ivy</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/1992_in_film" title="1992 in film">1992</a> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riding_In_Cars_With_Boys" title="Riding In Cars With Boys">Riding In Cars With Boys</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/2001_in_film" title="2001 in film">2001</a>. Gilbert also had a feature role in <i><a href="/wiki/Light_It_Up_(film)" title="Light It Up (film)">Light It Up</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/1999_in_film" title="1999 in film">1999</a>. She has had several minor roles following <i>Roseanne</i>, including the short film <i>$30</i> (aka <i>30 Bucks</i>) as part of the <i><a href="/wiki/Boys_Life_3" title="Boys Life 3">Boys Life 3</a></i> feature, and <i><a href="/wiki/High_Fidelity_(film)" title="High Fidelity (film)">High Fidelity</a></i>. She directed her short film <i>Persona Non Grata</i> in <a href="/wiki/1998_in_film" title="1998 in film">1998</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sara_Gilbert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>As a teenager, Gilbert dated her <i><a href="/wiki/Roseanne" title="Roseanne">Roseanne</a></i> (and later <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Bang_Theory" title="The Big Bang Theory">The Big Bang Theory</a></i>) co-star <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Galecki" title="Johnny Galecki">Johnny Galecki</a>. During their relationship she realized she was a <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a>. She remains close friends with Galecki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, Gilbert began a same-sex relationship with television producer <a href="/wiki/Allison_Adler" title="Allison Adler">Allison Adler</a>. They have two children—a son, Levi Hank, born to Adler in October 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-People-9">[9]</a></sup> and a daughter, Sawyer Jane, born to Gilbert in August 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Celebrity_Babies_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Celebrity_Babies-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> For many years, Gilbert remained private about her sexuality, choosing not to publicly discuss her personal life. In July 2010, as she prepared to launch her talk show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Talk_(TV_series)" title="The Talk (TV series)">The Talk</a></i>, Gilbert confirmed that she was a lesbian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In August 2011, Gilbert announced that she and Adler had separated amicably.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the break-up, Gilbert began a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, music producer and former <a href="/wiki/4_Non_Blondes" title="4 Non Blondes">4 Non Blondes</a> frontwoman <a href="/wiki/Linda_Perry" title="Linda Perry">Linda Perry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Gilbert announced their engagement in April 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and the pair married on March 30, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Gilbert gave birth to their son, Rhodes Emilio Gilbert Perry, on February 28, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
