<p><b>David Finch</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a>-born <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_book_artist" title="Comic book artist">comic book artist</a> known for his work on <a href="/wiki/Top_Cow_Productions" title="Top Cow Productions">Top Cow Productions</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Cyberforce" title="Cyberforce">Cyberforce</a></i>, as well as numerous subsequent titles for <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> and <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a>, such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Avengers_(comics)" title="The New Avengers (comics)">The New Avengers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Moon_Knight" title="Moon Knight">Moon Knight</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ultimatum_(Ultimate_Marvel)" title="Ultimatum (Ultimate Marvel)">Ultimatum</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Brightest_Day" title="Brightest Day">Brightest Day</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WonderCon_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-WonderCon-1">[1]</a></sup> He has provided album cover art for the band <a href="/wiki/Disturbed_(band)" title="Disturbed (band)">Disturbed</a>, and done concept art for films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Watchmen_(film)" title="Watchmen (film)">Watchmen</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WonderCon_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-WonderCon-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Finch_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Finch started his comics career drawing <a href="/wiki/Top_Cow_Productions" title="Top Cow Productions">Top Cow Productions</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Cyberforce" title="Cyberforce">Cyberforce</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-2">[2]</a></sup> after series creator and studio founder <a href="/wiki/Marc_Silvestri" title="Marc Silvestri">Marc Silvestri</a> ceased his run as writer/artist on that book. Finch co-created <i><a href="/wiki/Ascension_(comics)" title="Ascension (comics)">Ascension</a></i> with Matt "Batt" Banning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He later worked on the first three issues of <i><a href="/wiki/Aphrodite_IX" title="Aphrodite IX">Aphrodite IX</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/David_Wohl" title="David Wohl">David Wohl</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2003, Finch returned to comics for a year-long arc on <i><a href="/wiki/Ultimate_X-Men" title="Ultimate X-Men">Ultimate X-Men</a></i> with writer <a href="/wiki/Brian_Michael_Bendis" title="Brian Michael Bendis">Brian Michael Bendis</a>. Following that, the duo moved on to <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a></i>, where they destroyed Marvel's premiere superhero team<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and then relaunched it as <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Avengers_(comics)" title="The New Avengers (comics)">The New Avengers</a></i> featuring a radically different cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Finch worked on the revamped <i><a href="/wiki/Moon_Knight" title="Moon Knight">Moon Knight</a></i> series with novelist <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Huston" title="Charlie Huston">Charlie Huston</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-2">[2]</a></sup> and then illustrated <i><a href="/wiki/Fallen_Son:_The_Death_of_Captain_America" title="Fallen Son: The Death of Captain America">Fallen Son: The Death of Captain America</a></i> #4, featuring <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a>. This was followed by the crossover event "<a href="/wiki/Ultimatum_(Ultimate_Marvel)" title="Ultimatum (Ultimate Marvel)">Ultimatum</a>" for the <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Marvel" title="Ultimate Marvel">Ultimate Marvel</a> line. In addition to interior comics work, he has drawn several covers, including those of "<a href="/wiki/World_War_Hulk" title="World War Hulk">World War Hulk</a>"; <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men" title="X-Men">X-Men</a></i> #200 and the "<a href="/wiki/X-Men:_Messiah_Complex" title="X-Men: Messiah Complex">X-Men: Messiah Complex</a>" <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(fiction)" title="Crossover (fiction)">storyline</a>; and the <i><a href="/wiki/X-Infernus" title="X-Infernus">X-Infernus</a></i> miniseries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finch illustrated the cover to <a href="/wiki/Disturbed_(band)" title="Disturbed (band)">Disturbed</a>'s 2008 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Indestructible_(Disturbed_album)" title="Indestructible (Disturbed album)">Indestructible</a></i>, as well as doing concept design for the film adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Moore" title="Alan Moore">Alan Moore</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Watchmen_(film)" title="Watchmen (film)">Watchmen</a></i>.</p>
<p>In January 2010, Finch left Marvel and became a DC exclusive artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Finch collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Grant_Morrison" title="Grant Morrison">Grant Morrison</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(comic_book)" title="Batman (comic book)">Batman</a></i> #700 (Aug. 2010) an oversized anniversary issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In July 2010 DC announced that Finch would be writing and drawing a new ongoing series entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Dark_Knight" title="Batman: The Dark Knight">Batman: The Dark Knight</a></i>, the first story arc of which deals with the detective's more supernatural cases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The series launched with a January 2011 cover date<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> but was relaunched in November of that same year as part of the company-wide reboot <a href="/wiki/The_New_52" title="The New 52">The New 52</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Finch and <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Johns" title="Geoff Johns">Geoff Johns</a> launched a new <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League" title="Justice League">Justice League</a> of America</i> series<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_Evil" title="Forever Evil">Forever Evil</a></i> limited series in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Finch and his wife, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Finch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Meredith Finch (page does not exist)">Meredith Finch</a>, took over the creative duties on <i><a href="/wiki/Wonder_Woman" title="Wonder Woman">Wonder Woman</a></i>, beginning with issue #36 (Jan. 2015), their first collaborative effort.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> As part of the <a href="/wiki/DC_Rebirth" title="DC Rebirth">DC Rebirth</a> relaunch of DC's titles, Finch teamed with writer <a href="/wiki/Tom_King_(comics)" title="Tom King (comics)">Tom King</a> to launch the <i>Batman</i> vol. 3 series in June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Finch_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
