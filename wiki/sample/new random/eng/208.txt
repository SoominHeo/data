<p><i><b>Pitch Perfect 2</b></i> is a 2015 American <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> directed and co-produced by <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Banks" title="Elizabeth Banks">Elizabeth Banks</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Kay_Cannon" title="Kay Cannon">Kay Cannon</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> to the 2012 film <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect" title="Pitch Perfect">Pitch Perfect</a></i> and the second installment in the <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_(film_series)" title="Pitch Perfect (film series)">Pitch Perfect</a></i> series. The film centers on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional" title="Fictional">fictional</a> Barden University and The Bellas, an all-female <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> singing group. The film features an <a href="/wiki/Ensemble_cast" title="Ensemble cast">ensemble cast</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kendrick" title="Anna Kendrick">Anna Kendrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Wilson" title="Rebel Wilson">Rebel Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hailee_Steinfeld" title="Hailee Steinfeld">Hailee Steinfeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brittany_Snow" title="Brittany Snow">Brittany Snow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Knapp" title="Alexis Knapp">Alexis Knapp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hana_Mae_Lee" title="Hana Mae Lee">Hana Mae Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ester_Dean" title="Ester Dean">Ester Dean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chrissie_Fit" title="Chrissie Fit">Chrissie Fit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelley_Jakle" title="Kelley Jakle">Kelley Jakle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shelley_Regner" title="Shelley Regner">Shelley Regner</a> as The Bellas. It was released on May 15, 2015 by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Pictures</a>.</p>
<p>The film grossed over $287 million and surpassed the total gross of the original film ($115.4 million) in five days, and also became the highest-grossing music comedy film of all-time, overtaking <i><a href="/wiki/School_of_Rock" title="School of Rock">School of Rock</a></i> ($131.3 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boxofficemojo1984_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-boxofficemojo1984-4">[4]</a></sup> A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_3" title="Pitch Perfect 3">Pitch Perfect 3</a></i>, is set to be released on December 22, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anitabusch_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-anitabusch-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.27Pitch_Perfect_3.27_Pushed_Back_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-.27Pitch_Perfect_3.27_Pushed_Back-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Three years after the events of <a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect" title="Pitch Perfect">the previous film</a>, the Barden Bellas are now led by Beca Mitchell (<a href="/wiki/Anna_Kendrick" title="Anna Kendrick">Anna Kendrick</a>) and three-time <a href="/wiki/Super_senior" title="Super senior">super senior</a> Chloe Beale (<a href="/wiki/Brittany_Snow" title="Brittany Snow">Brittany Snow</a>). The Bellas have become <a href="/wiki/International_Championship_of_Collegiate_A_Cappella" title="International Championship of Collegiate A Cappella">ICCA</a> champions each of these three years. However, the group becomes involved in a national scandal called Muffgate when a wardrobe malfunction causes Fat Amy's (<a href="/wiki/Rebel_Wilson" title="Rebel Wilson">Rebel Wilson</a>) pants to rip while performing at President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s birthday gala. This leads to the Bellas' suspension from the ICCAs, meaning they are banned from holding auditions, and damages the group's reputation significantly. Beca makes a deal to allow the Bellas to be reinstated should they win the World Championship of a cappella. A cappella commentators John Smith (<a href="/wiki/John_Michael_Higgins" title="John Michael Higgins">John Michael Higgins</a>) and Gail Abernathy-McKadden-Feinberger (<a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Banks" title="Elizabeth Banks">Elizabeth Banks</a>) doubt the group's chances, as no American team has ever won the world title.</p>
<p>Freshman Emily Junk (<a href="/wiki/Hailee_Steinfeld" title="Hailee Steinfeld">Hailee Steinfeld</a>) starts her college career, hoping to follow in her mother Katherine's (<a href="/wiki/Katey_Sagal" title="Katey Sagal">Katey Sagal</a>) footsteps by being a Bella. At orientation, she watches an a cappella performance by the Treblemakers, now led by Beca's boyfriend Jesse Swanson (<a href="/wiki/Skylar_Astin" title="Skylar Astin">Skylar Astin</a>). Emily meets him and tells him her dream of joining the Bellas. Benjamin "Benji" Applebaum (<a href="/wiki/Ben_Platt_(actor)" title="Ben Platt (actor)">Ben Platt</a>), Jesse's best friend, overhears this and develops an awkward crush on her.</p>
<p>The Bellas learn that German a cappella group Das Sound Machine (who are also the current World Champions) have replaced the Bellas on their victory tour. Additionally, Beca has started an internship at recording studio Residual Heat, something only Jesse knows about so that the Bellas won't question her commitment. On her first day she watches her egocentric boss (<a href="/wiki/Keegan-Michael_Key" title="Keegan-Michael Key">Keegan-Michael Key</a>) degrade his assistant, causing her to realize the negative points of a career in producing music.</p>
<p>Emily goes to the Bellas' sorority house to audition for them because of their absence from the auditions. She is initially rejected by Fat Amy, but in a desperate attempt, she announces her Legacy Bella relation to her mother, who is very well known in the a cappella community. When she sings her unfinished song "Flashlight," she succeeds in joining because of the rules of suspension stating that the Bellas can't hold any auditions, but Emily technically came to them. At the initiation party, Benji nervously tries to ask Emily out, but she kindly rejects him with the reason being that it's her first day of college. Bumper Allen (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_Devine" title="Adam Devine">Adam Devine</a>) returns, from being backup to John Mayer, as Barden security and starts hooking up with Amy.</p>
<p>At a car show where the ICCA winners are due to perform, the Bellas scout their replacements, German powerhouse "Das Sound Machine" (DSM) - led by intimidating duo Pieter Krämer (<a href="/wiki/Flula_Borg" title="Flula Borg">Flula Borg</a>) and Kommissar (<a href="/wiki/Birgitte_Hjort_S%C3%B8rensen" title="Birgitte Hjort Sørensen">Birgitte Hjort Sørensen</a>), who take delight in mocking the all-girl group. To her own surprise, Beca is too dazzled by Kommissar's striking appearance to win any verbal spars she engages her in. Determined to beat DSM at Worlds, the Bellas rehearse a new set that is similar to DSM's style. After one rehearsal, Beca shows off her mashup skills to her boss by helping produce <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s Christmas album. In turn, her boss offers her a chance to become a producer by listening to her demos. Later, the Bellas are invited to an exclusive riff-off, but end up losing to DSM in the final round when a nervous Emily flubs an attempt to sing "Flashlight," an original song she also performed for her Bellas audition. Not only was the song not from the right category ("90's Hip-Hop Jamz"), but original songs are frowned upon by the a cappella groups in attendance.</p>
<p>Having gone through Beca's demo samples, her boss expresses disappointment that all of her songs are mashups. He gives her another chance to add to her covering experience with the Bellas and come forth with original content. Because of this with the current events, Beca is becoming stressed out with both careers. Fat Amy reassures her confidence after revealing her knowledge of her internship. The next night, Bumper makes a romantic attempt to get serious with Amy, but she rejects him as she claims to be "A free range pony, that cannot be tamed." The next day at the warmups, the Bellas' chance of winning end in disaster when the performance makes John and Gail question the Bellas' identity and sets Cynthia-Rose's (<a href="/wiki/Ester_Dean" title="Ester Dean">Ester Dean</a>) hair on fire by pyrotechnics. In order to regain harmony and sync, Chloe takes them to a retreat led by former leader Aubrey Posen (<a href="/wiki/Anna_Camp" title="Anna Camp">Anna Camp</a>). Before going, Emily goes to Katherine for help. She worries about the Bellas' relationships and her possibility of soloing at the ICCAs. Katherine then reassures her that going to the Worlds is bigger than the ICCAs and that the Bellas won't end now.</p>
<p>The boot camp gives the group physically challenging tasks in order to help with their harmony, but to no avail. Beca shows her frustration over the camp activities not helping the group and is pressured by Amy to reveal her internship. She starts a heated argument with Chloe over herself being the only one thinking about life beyond the Bellas and Chloe's obsession with winning Worlds. Beca storms off but is captured by a bear net. Chloe sees this as a win but becomes concerned for her safety when the net starts to rip and fall. Lily (<a href="/wiki/Hana_Mae_Lee" title="Hana Mae Lee">Hana Mae Lee</a>) manages to get her down, claiming that "[she] sleeps upside down like a bat".</p>
<p>The girls calm down and talk about their dreams and future. Beca reveals that her stress was caused by her independence in doing everything under extreme pressure and wishes that she can have Emily's confidence. Emily returns those feelings about her being intimidatingly good and reveals that just wants to be a Bella, not a Legacy. Beca then offers her to produce "Flashlight" at the studio. When the group realizes that they may not see each other after graduation, they regain harmony by singing "Cups (When I'm Gone)." Fat Amy realizes that she made a mistake with Bumper and goes to win him back, before getting trapped by a net.</p>
<p>She rows across the lake to him, singing "We Belong" to him, with the Trebles and Bellas watching from their houses. Bumper initially rejects her, but he ends up joining her in the song. At the studio, Emily and Beca present their song to Beca's boss, in which he envies their talent and looks forward to producing with them.</p>
<p>The senior Bellas graduate and they all head off to Copenhagen for the World Finals, with Jesse and Benji to cheer them on. Benji wishes Emily luck before the start of Worlds and she kisses him. The performance of DSM and the crowd's reaction discourage the Bellas, but Fat Amy gives them one of her pep talks and brings back the spirit to the group. Their performance starts with a handclap introduction and they rock the Worlds with their real sound. Suddenly, they perform a harmonized version of "Flashlight" with Aubrey, Katherine, and other past Bellas joining in. The Bellas win the championship and repair the damaged legacy. As the senior Bellas leave Barden, they give Emily a belated proper initiation with Fat Amy showing her how to do the last tradition: christening the house by sliding down the staircase.</p>
<p>In a <a href="/wiki/Post-credits_scene" title="Post-credits scene">mid-credits scene</a>, Bumper performs on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i>, receiving a reluctant attitude from Adam and choosing Christina as his coach. He then makes her uncomfortable when he lengthens his hug to her.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In December 2012, <a href="/wiki/Skylar_Astin" title="Skylar Astin">Skylar Astin</a> revealed that he and <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Wilson" title="Rebel Wilson">Rebel Wilson</a> had had meetings with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a> about the potential sequel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sequel_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sequel-7">[7]</a></sup> In April 2013, it was confirmed that a sequel would be released in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Banks" title="Elizabeth Banks">Elizabeth Banks</a> directed the sequel, and <a href="/wiki/Kay_Cannon" title="Kay Cannon">Kay Cannon</a> returned as screenwriter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-1-27_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1-27-9">[9]</a></sup> Paul Brooks produced for Gold Circle Films, alongside Banks and <a href="/wiki/Max_Handelman" title="Max Handelman">Max Handelman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-1-27_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1-27-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Deke_Sharon" title="Deke Sharon">Deke Sharon</a> returned as vocal producer, and has a brief cameo as a German reporter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> As in the first movie, they ran a month of "a cappella boot camp" before filming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was released on May 7, 2015 in Australia and New Zealand, and May 15 in the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A still from the rehearsals for the film was revealed on May 16, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The poster was released on November 18, 2014 and the <a href="/wiki/Trailer_(promotion)" title="Trailer (promotion)">trailer</a> came out the following day. A second trailer was aired during the Super Bowl on February 1, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Director <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Banks" title="Elizabeth Banks">Elizabeth Banks</a> stated an additional performance by the Treblemakers was filmed specifically for the DVD release. Also included on the DVD will be deleted scenes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> On May 20, 2015, it was announced that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FX_Networks" title="FX Networks">FX Networks</a> had acquired U.S. television broadcasting rights to the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> and DVD editions of <i>Pitch Perfect 2</i> were released on September 22, 2015 in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> with a <a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target</a>-exclusive edition containing additional bonus features being released the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Pitch Perfect 2</i> grossed $184.2 million in North America and $103.2 million in other territories for a worldwide total of $287.5 million, against a budget of $29 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-3">[3]</a></sup> In its first five days, the film surpassed the total gross of the original ($115.4 million), and also overtook <i><a href="/wiki/School_of_Rock" title="School of Rock">School of Rock</a></i> ($131.3 million) for the highest grossing musical-comedy of all-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boxofficemojo1984_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-boxofficemojo1984-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Deadline.com" title="Deadline.com">Deadline.com</a> calculated the net profit of the film to be $139.6 million, when factoring together all expenses and revenues for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United States and Canada, <i>Pitch Perfect 2</i> grossed $4.6 million from Thursday night showings and $27.8 million on its opening day, increasing the opening weekend projections from $40 million to $64 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In its opening weekend, the film grossed $69.2 million, finishing first at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The opening weekend gross was more than the entire North American total gross of the first film ($65 million), is the third biggest PG-13 comedy opening of all-time (behind <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons_Movie" title="The Simpsons Movie">The Simpsons Movie</a>'s</i> $74 million in 2007 and <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers_in_Goldmember" title="Austin Powers in Goldmember">Austin Powers in Goldmember</a>'</i>s $73 million in 2002) and the highest grossing opening ever for a musical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outside North America, the film opened at number one in Australia and New Zealand, earning $7.6 million and $1.2 million respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PP2_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-PP2-53">[53]</a></sup> The film grossed $22.1 million in Australia, $27.3 million in the United Kingdom and $2.7 million in New Zealand.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Pitch Perfect 2</i> received positive reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film has an approval rating of 66% based on 183 reviews, with an average rating of 6/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "<i>Pitch Perfect 2</i> sings in sweet comedic harmony, even if it doesn't hit quite as many high notes as its predecessor."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film holds score of 63 out of 100, based on 39 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> polls conducted during the opening weekend, cinema audiences gave <i>Pitch Perfect 2</i> an average grade of "A–" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitch_Perfect_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_3" title="Pitch Perfect 3">Pitch Perfect 3</a></div>
<p>On April 11, 2015, a month before the release of the film, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Wilson" title="Rebel Wilson">Rebel Wilson</a> would return for a third film, although she stated that she did not know if <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kendrick" title="Anna Kendrick">Anna Kendrick</a> or any of the other cast members would also be reprising their roles. She added that she would be "up for a Fat Amy spin-off," although nothing has been confirmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>Director, star, and producer <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Banks" title="Elizabeth Banks">Elizabeth Banks</a> acknowledged the possibility of a third film, saying, "I will say, it would be disingenuous to say that no one's talking about a <i>Pitch Perfect 3</i>; the possibility of it. We are really focused on getting as many butts in seats for this one. If fans embrace it, we are going to seriously think about what the continuing journey would look like, but we don’t know what that is yet".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 10, 2015, a third film was officially confirmed, with <a href="/wiki/Kay_Cannon" title="Kay Cannon">Kay Cannon</a> returning to write the script.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Several days later it was announced both Kendrick and Wilson would reprise their roles, and later <a href="/wiki/Brittany_Snow" title="Brittany Snow">Brittany Snow</a> was also confirmed to return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anitabusch_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-anitabusch-5">[5]</a></sup> Banks will also return to produce, but in June 2016 it was announced she would not be directing the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was originally slated for a July 21, 2017 release, and was later pushed back to August 4, 2017 before moving back to the July 21 slot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.27Pitch_Perfect_3.27_Pushed_Back_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-.27Pitch_Perfect_3.27_Pushed_Back-6">[6]</a></sup> In May 2016 it was moved again, this time being pushed back to December 22, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of January 2, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kendrick" title="Anna Kendrick">Anna Kendrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Wilson" title="Rebel Wilson">Rebel Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Camp" title="Anna Camp">Anna Camp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brittany_Snow" title="Brittany Snow">Brittany Snow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ester_Dean" title="Ester Dean">Ester Dean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Knapp" title="Alexis Knapp">Alexis Knapp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hana_Mae_Lee" title="Hana Mae Lee">Hana Mae Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelley_Jakle" title="Kelley Jakle">Kelley Jakle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shelley_Regner" title="Shelley Regner">Shelley Regner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hailee_Steinfeld" title="Hailee Steinfeld">Hailee Steinfeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chrissie_Fit" title="Chrissie Fit">Chrissie Fit</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Banks" title="Elizabeth Banks">Elizabeth Banks</a> have been confirmed to be reprising their roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
