<p><b>Tryavna</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg">Трявна</span>) <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_and_towns_in_Bulgaria" title="List of cities and towns in Bulgaria">is a town</a> in central <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, situated in the north slopes of the Balkan range, on the Tryavna river valley, near <a href="/wiki/Gabrovo" title="Gabrovo">Gabrovo</a>. It is famous for its <a href="/wiki/Textile_industry" title="Textile industry">textile industry</a> and typical <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_National_Revival" title="Bulgarian National Revival">National Revival</a> architecture, featuring 140 cultural monuments, museums and expositions. Tryavna is the birthplace of Bulgarian writer <a href="/wiki/Pencho_Slaveykov" title="Pencho Slaveykov">Pencho Slaveykov</a> and revolutionary <a href="/wiki/Angel_Kanchev" title="Angel Kanchev">Angel Kanchev</a>.</p>
<p>The town is the administrative centre of the eponymous <a href="/wiki/Tryavna_Municipality" title="Tryavna Municipality">Tryavna Municipality</a>. As of December 2009, it has a population of 9,831 inhabitants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statistika_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-statistika-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-population_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-population-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop-stat_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-pop-stat-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tryavna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village was founded as early as the <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thracian</a> Era. However, the first documents of its existence date back to the 12th century. During <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Bulgaria" title="Ottoman Bulgaria">Ottoman Bulgaria</a> period locals defended the pass and enjoyed privileges for this reason. Only Bulgarians lived in the town;. During the period of Bulgarian National Revival, the town was heavily involved in the development of crafts. Houses from this period feature their own architectural design. The ground floors had irregular forms and housed craftsmen and traders. The upper floors featured wooden bow-windows, the roofs were covered with well arranged rocks.</p>
<p>The town square, named <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Capitan_Diado_Nikola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Capitan Diado Nikola (page does not exist)">Capitan Diado Nikola</a>, with the clock tower, dating from 1814, are among the landmarks. Another tourist attraction is the "kivgireniyat" bridge, built above the river behind the clock tower. One of the first secular schools in Bulgaria was built on Capitan Diado Nikola Square. Another symbol of Tryavna is the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=St._Archangel_Mihael&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="St. Archangel Mihael (page does not exist)">St. Archangel Mihael</a> Church, situated in the town center and built in the late 12th century after Bulgarian tzar <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Asen_I_of_Bulgaria" title="Ivan Asen I of Bulgaria">Asen</a> defeated Byzantine emperor <a href="/wiki/Isaac_II_Angelos" title="Isaac II Angelos">Isaac II</a>. The wood-carved iconostasis and the bishop's throne in the church are real masterpieces of the woodcarving in Tryavna. Next to the school, is the Raykov house with its ethnographic collection, which is the birthplace of the first Bulgarian chemist, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pencho_Nikolov_Raikov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pencho Nikolov Raikov (page does not exist)">Pencho Nikolov Raikov</a>. Not far away from it is the museum-house of <a href="/wiki/Angel_Kanchev" title="Angel Kanchev">Angel Kanchev</a> who was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vassil_Levski" title="Vassil Levski">Vassil Levski</a>'s compatriot. One of the most visited museums in Tryavna is the Daskalov house built in 1808. The museum features the famous wood carved suns. The house is a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Museum_of_woodcarving_and_icon_painting_Tryavna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Museum of woodcarving and icon painting Tryavna (page does not exist)">museum of woodcarving and icon painting art</a>. The remarkable ceilings were made after a bet between two of the best masters of wood carving — <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitar_Oshanetsa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dimitar Oshanetsa (page does not exist)">Dimitar Oshanetsa</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ivan_Bochukovetsa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ivan Bochukovetsa (page does not exist)">Ivan Bochukovetsa</a>. Тhe two masters worked hard for six months in the two largest rooms in the house without having a possibility to see each other's work.</p>
<p>The Petko and <a href="/wiki/Pencho_Slaveykov" title="Pencho Slaveykov">Pencho Slaveykov</a> museum-house, situated in the old part of the town, features an exposition of the poetical works of the two authors. The son, Pencho, was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> but died before having a chance to win it. The Kalinchev house, also in the old part, has been transformed into an art gallery, offering the collection, donated by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Totio_Gybenski&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Totio Gybenski (page does not exist)">Totio Gybenski</a>. The Museum of icon painting and woodcarving with its collection of over 160 original icons, painted by well-known masters, is housed in the Tsar's chapel at a distance of the town center.</p>
<p>The unique Museum of Asian and African Art is housed in the old public bath, on the riverside. Tryavna is proud with its great samples of the woodcarving, icon painting and original architecture.</p>
<p>Today the town is a preferable tourist spot, featuring modern hotels, private lodgings and villas, restaurants and taverns.</p>
<p>There is also an Art school where successors of the old icon painters and woodcarvers master these crafts.</p>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Voneshta_voda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Voneshta voda (page does not exist)">Voneshta voda</a> village resort, famous for its healing mineral springs, is located 20 kilometres (12 miles) away from Tryavna. The <a href="/wiki/Bulgarka_Nature_Park" title="Bulgarka Nature Park">Bulgarka Nature Park</a> is located in the Starina Planina, south on the city.</p>
<p>The town is well connected with the whole country thanks to its excellent road network and railway station.</p>
<p>Besides <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_National_Revival" title="Bulgarian National Revival">Bulgarian National Revival</a> figures, another native is former Bulgarian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA">NBA</a> player <a href="/wiki/Georgi_Glouchkov" title="Georgi Glouchkov">Georgi Glouchkov</a>.</p>
