<p><b>Katherine Reutter</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɔɪ/ 'oy' in 'boy'">ɔɪ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ər/ 'er' in 'finger'">ər</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born July 30, 1988)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is a former American <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating" title="Short track speed skating">short track speed skater</a>. She is a two-time medalist (one silver, one bronze) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympics</a>, 2011 overall world silver medalist and the 2010–2011 overall <a href="/wiki/ISU_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_World_Cup" title="ISU Short Track Speed Skating World Cup">ISU Short Track Speed Skating World Cup</a> champion.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics" title="2010 Winter Olympics">2010 Winter Olympics</a>, Reutter won silver in the 1000 m and bronze in the 3000 m relay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She has won one gold, two silvers, and four bronze medals at the <a href="/wiki/World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">World Championships</a>, including overall silver medal at the 2011 World Championships. She has also won two bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Team_Championships" title="World Short Track Speed Skating Team Championships">World Team Championships</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reutter was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Champaign,_Illinois" title="Champaign, Illinois">Champaign, Illinois</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Reutter was inspired to become a speed skater after meeting five-time Olympic gold medalist <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Blair" title="Bonnie Blair">Bonnie Blair</a> at her high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazette_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-6">[6]</a></sup> She learned to skate with her mother during a figure skating class when she was four years old, but immediately found she was more interested in speed skating. Reutter first started speed skating professionally in 2005 at the age of 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dey_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dey-7">[7]</a></sup> She began training at the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Olympic_Training_Center" title="United States Olympic Training Center">Olympic Training Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Marquette,_Michigan" title="Marquette, Michigan">Marquette, Michigan</a> that year. Reutter graduated from <a href="/wiki/Centennial_High_School_(Champaign,_Illinois)" title="Centennial High School (Champaign, Illinois)">Centennial High School</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She has trained in <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a> since 2007, where she has paid for room, board, and training at her own expense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.932009">2008–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2008_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2008 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">2008 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships</a>, Reutter won a bronze medal in the 3000 m with a time of 5:46.518, finishing behind <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Yang_(speed_skater)" title="Zhou Yang (speed skater)">Zhou Yang</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jung_Eun-Ju" title="Jung Eun-Ju">Jung Eun-Ju</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2009, Reutter completed a 1000 m race in 1:29.667 in <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It was less than 0.2 of a second short of the world record set the previous year by <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> short track speed skater <a href="/wiki/Wang_Meng_(speed_skater)" title="Wang Meng (speed skater)">Wang Meng</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazette_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> At the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2009_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Team_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2009 World Short Track Speed Skating Team Championships (page does not exist)">2009 World Short Track Speed Skating Team Championships</a>, in March 2009, Reutter won a bronze medal along with <a href="/wiki/Kimberly_Derrick" title="Kimberly Derrick">Kimberly Derrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alyson_Dudek" title="Alyson Dudek">Alyson Dudek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lana_Gehring" title="Lana Gehring">Lana Gehring</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Smith_(speed_skater)" title="Jessica Smith (speed skater)">Jessica Smith</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reutter won four silver medals at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_World_Cup" title="2009 Short Track Speed Skating World Cup">2009 Short Track Speed Skating World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazette_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-6">[6]</a></sup> She won the women's 1500 m at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_World_Cup" title="2009–10 Short Track Speed Skating World Cup">2009–10 Short Track Speed Skating World Cup</a> on November 7, 2009, finishing in 2:23.275, beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cho_Ha-Ri" title="Cho Ha-Ri">Cho Ha-Ri</a> of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Reutter made the 2010 U.S. Olympic team after winning the 1000 and 1500 m at the 2010 U.S. Olympic Team Trials in September 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_Winter_Olympics">2010 Winter Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 Winter Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics" title="Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics">Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics</a></div>
<p>Reutter ranked 1st in her qualifying heats for the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_500_metres" title="Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Women's 500 metres">500 m</a> with a time of 44.187.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> She then set an Olympic record in the first quarterfinal with a time of 43.834 but the record was beaten by <a href="/wiki/Wang_Meng_(speed_skater)" title="Wang Meng (speed skater)">Wang Meng</a> of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> in the next heat, with a time of 43.284.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kuc_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-kuc-17">[17]</a></sup> Reutter started slow in the semifinals, resulting in a fourth-place finish, eliminating her from medal contention. She finished seventh overall in the women's 500 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kuc_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-kuc-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reutter competed in the semifinals for the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_3000_metre_relay" title="Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Women's 3000 metre relay">3000 m relay</a> with teammates <a href="/wiki/Alyson_Dudek" title="Alyson Dudek">Alyson Dudek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allison_Baver" title="Allison Baver">Allison Baver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimberly_Derrick" title="Kimberly Derrick">Kimberly Derrick</a>. The U.S. team qualified for the final in second place behind <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> with a time of 4:15.376.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Reutter finished in fourth place in the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_1500_metres" title="Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Women's 1500 metres">1500 m</a> final, behind <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Yang_(speed_skater)" title="Zhou Yang (speed skater)">Zhou Yang</a> of China, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Eun-Byul" title="Lee Eun-Byul">Lee Eun-Byul</a> of South Korea in second, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Seung-Hi" title="Park Seung-Hi">Park Seung-Hi</a> of South Korea in third. Reutter got tangled up with Wang Meng in the semifinal, causing both skaters and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cho_Ha-Ri" title="Cho Ha-Ri">Cho Ha-Ri</a> of South Korea to fall, resulting in Meng being disqualified from the final. Reutter repeated the same move in the final, bumping into Park, and later expressed remorse for both mistakes, stating "I think the room was there but maybe I just wasn't there. I don't know. But … I messed up and messed up some people around me, which I'm very sorry for." In the heats of the 1000 m, Reutter set an <a href="/wiki/List_of_Olympic_records_in_short_track_speed_skating" title="List of Olympic records in short track speed skating">Olympic record</a> and finished first overall in the heats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> About an hour and a half later, Reutter competed in the 3000 m relay (with Allison Baver, Alyson Dudek, <a href="/wiki/Lana_Gehring" title="Lana Gehring">Lana Gehring</a>) and won bronze behind China and Canada due to the disqualification of South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In the final of the 1000 m, Reutter was edged out for the gold medal by Wang Meng, finishing behind her 1:29.324 to 1:29.213.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships">2010 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2010 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">2010 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships</a></div>
<p>At the 2010 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sofia,_Bulgaria" title="Sofia, Bulgaria">Sofia, Bulgaria</a>, Reutter, who was fighting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flu" title="Flu">flu</a>, won bronze medals in the 1000 m and the 3000 m relay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In her first event, the 1000 m, Reutter placed third with a time of 1:31.747, finishing behind <a href="/wiki/Wang_Meng_(speed_skater)" title="Wang Meng (speed skater)">Wang Meng</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cho_Ha-Ri" title="Cho Ha-Ri">Cho Ha-Ri</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Reutter then combined with <a href="/wiki/Alyson_Dudek" title="Alyson Dudek">Alyson Dudek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimberly_Derrick" title="Kimberly Derrick">Kimberly Derrick</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lana_Gehring" title="Lana Gehring">Lana Gehring</a> in the 3000 m relay to finish third behind <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> with a time of 4:14.231.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Reutter also placed 6th in the 500 m, 4th in the 1500 m, and 4th in the 3000 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships">2011 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2011 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">2011 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships</a></div>
<p>At the 2011 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, Reutter won one gold, two silvers, and one bronze. She finished second overall with 68 points. In her first event, the 1500 m, Reutter won the gold ahead of Koreans <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Seung-Hi" title="Park Seung-Hi">Park Seung-Hi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cho_Ha-Ri" title="Cho Ha-Ri">Cho Ha-Ri</a> in a time of 2:33.978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In winning the gold, Reutter became the first American woman to win a gold medal at a World Short Track Speed Skating Championships since <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Blair" title="Bonnie Blair">Bonnie Blair</a> did in 1986. After the 1500 m, Reutter competed in the 3000 m relay semifinals but fell during the race, resulting in the United States not advancing to the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In her next event, the 500 m, Reutter did not advance past the semifinals, finishing 6th overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In the 1000 m, Reutter finished in third place behind Cho Ha-Ri and Italian <a href="/wiki/Arianna_Fontana" title="Arianna Fontana">Arianna Fontana</a> in a time of 2:23.268 even though she tripped on a lane block.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In her last event, the 3000 m, Reutter just missed the gold medal, finishing behind Cho Ha-Ri 5:13.677 to 5:13.353.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 2013 due to constant injuries, Reutter announced her retirement at the age of 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> After that, she became a coach at the <a href="/wiki/Pettit_National_Ice_Center" title="Pettit National Ice Center">Pettit National Ice Center</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances_and_endorsements">Appearances and endorsements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katherine_Reutter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Appearances and endorsements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reutter appeared as a guest on <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Colbert_Report" title="The Colbert Report">The Colbert Report</a></i> on December 14, 2009. Host <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Colbert" title="Stephen Colbert">Stephen Colbert</a> autographed her thigh, an idea by Reutter that initially caught the comedian off guard (Although Reutter's website suggest that it was her manager's idea and they had discussed it with Colbert beforehand).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Colbert began raising funds for the U.S. Speedskating team when their sponsor, the Dutch <a href="/wiki/DSB_Bank" title="DSB Bank">DSB Bank</a>, went bankrupt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reutter was sponsored by U.S. Speedskating, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verizon" title="Verizon">Verizon</a>, EyeCare 20/20, Bioenergy Ribose, <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oakley,_Inc." title="Oakley, Inc.">Oakley</a> and the Champaign Police Department, who helped pay the costs of her training in Utah.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
