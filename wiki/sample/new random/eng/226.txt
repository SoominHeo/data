<p><b>Guido Wald Rüdiger, count of Starhemberg</b>; (Graz, November 11 1657 – Vienna, 7 March 1737) was an <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> military officer.</p>
<p>He was a cousin of <a href="/wiki/Ernst_R%C3%BCdiger_von_Starhemberg" title="Ernst Rüdiger von Starhemberg">Ernst Rüdiger von Starhemberg</a> (1638-1701), the famous commander of <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Turkish</a> siege of 1683, and acted as his <a href="/wiki/Aide-de-camp" title="Aide-de-camp">aide-de-camp</a> during that siege. Guido followed his cousin, and later <a href="/wiki/Prince_Eugene_of_Savoy" title="Prince Eugene of Savoy">Prince Eugene of Savoy</a>, in battles against the Turks.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a>, Starhemberg fought in Italy and Spain. Between 1706 and 1708 he was the commander-in-chief of the imperial army in Hungary, leading military operations against the insurgents of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francis_II_R%C3%A1k%C3%B3czi" title="Francis II Rákóczi">Francis II Rákóczi</a>. In 1708, he was appointed Supreme Commander of the Austrians in Spain.</p>
<p>Together with <a href="/wiki/James_Stanhope,_1st_Earl_Stanhope" title="James Stanhope, 1st Earl Stanhope">James Stanhope</a> he succeeded in conquering <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> in 1710, after previously gaining victories at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Almenar" title="Battle of Almenar">Almenar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Saragossa" title="Battle of Saragossa">Saragossa</a>. In December, however, he was forced to leave the city by the lack of support by its inhabitants for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habsburg" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> pretender. After the subsequent defeats at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Brihuega" title="Battle of Brihuega">Battle of Brihuega</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Villaviciosa" title="Battle of Villaviciosa">Battle of Villaviciosa</a> (1710), he had to pull back to <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, where he was made viceroy when <a href="/wiki/Charles_VI,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor">Archduke Charles</a> returned to Austria.</p>
<p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peace_of_Utrecht" title="Peace of Utrecht">Peace of Utrecht</a> (1713), archduke Charles, now Emperor Charles VI, ordered him to abandon Catalonia. He pulled back with his troops to <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a> on English ships.</p>
<p>When he died in 1737, he was Governor of <a href="/wiki/Slavonia" title="Slavonia">Slavonia</a>.</p>
