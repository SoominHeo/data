<p><b>Sidus</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 싸이더스 픽쳐스, formerly called <b>Uno Film</b>, <b>Sidus</b> and <b>Sidus FNH</b>) is a film production and distribution company based in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. Established in 1995, the company has distributed and produced over 70 films since their founding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sidus_Pictures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sidus was founded by Mr. James Hyung Soon Kim after taking over Uno Films, which began in 1995. The company changed its name to Sidus in 2000 and merged with EBM, a talent management firm. EBM would split from Sidus in 2005 and be renamed iHQ. Also in 2005, Sidus was acquired by KT corporation, under which Sidus began investing in, and distributing domestic and foreign films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Korean_Producer_Sidus_Distributes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Korean_Producer_Sidus_Distributes-2">[2]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Korean_Sidus_Sets_Film_Fund_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Korean_Sidus_Sets_Film_Fund-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2014, Sidus was acquired by Locus Corporation, which is headed by Sidus's original founder Mr. Kim.</p>
<p>Throughout its history, Sidus has produced over 70 films and continues to work in film production, distribution, and investment on an international scale. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
