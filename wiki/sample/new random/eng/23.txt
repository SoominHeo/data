<p><b>Juan Cespedes Uribe Tena</b> (born March 22, 1979) is a <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Third_baseman" title="Third baseman">third baseman</a> who is currently a free agent. He stands 6 feet (1.8 m) tall and weighs 235 pounds (107 kg). He previously played in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) for the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>. He bats and throws right-handed.</p>
<p>Uribe began his professional career in 1997 when he was signed by the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>. After advancing through the minors, he made his MLB debut with the Rockies in 2001. He became their shortstop in 2001 and spent all of 2002 in that capacity. He missed part of 2003 with an injury and was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> following the season. After one season as a utility player, Uribe became the starting shortstop for the White Sox in 2005; he held that position for the next three years. While Uribe was with the White Sox, the team won the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Series" title="2005 World Series">2005 World Series</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>. Uribe hit 21 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_runs" title="Home runs">home runs</a> in 2006 but had a low <a href="/wiki/On-base_percentage" title="On-base percentage">on-base percentage</a>. He hit 20 home runs in 2007 but had a low <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batting_average_with_runners_in_scoring_position" title="Batting average with runners in scoring position">batting average with runners in scoring position</a> (RISP). In 2008, Uribe lost his starting shortstop role to <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Cabrera" title="Orlando Cabrera">Orlando Cabrera</a> and shifted to the starter at second, but then lost that job to rookie <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Ram%C3%ADrez" title="Alexei Ramírez">Alexei Ramírez</a>. He eventually ended the season as the team's third baseman due to an injury to <a href="/wiki/Joe_Crede" title="Joe Crede">Joe Crede</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, Uribe signed with the Giants and was again used as utility player. He spent most of 2010 as the Giants' shortstop, hit a career-high 24 home runs, and had several key hits in the playoffs as the Giants won the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Series" title="2010 World Series">2010 World Series</a>. Following that season, he signed a three-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>. The next two years were plagued by injuries and poor hitting, but he regained his starting role at third base and made strong contributions to the team in 2013 and 2014. Uribe was traded to the Braves in late May of the 2015 season and again to the Mets in July of the 2015 season. Before the start of spring training in 2016, he signed a one-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>. He was injured by a ground ball on June 12, 2016 and was later diagnosed with a <a href="/wiki/Testicle" title="Testicle">testicular</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contusion" title="Contusion">contusion</a>. The Indians designated him for assignment on August 1 and released him on August 5.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Uribe was born in <a href="/wiki/Ban%C3%AD" title="Baní">Baní</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. Growing up, he became interested in baseball partly due to <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Uribe" title="José Uribe">José Uribe</a>. José was Juan's second cousin, although Juan calls him his "uncle" because of their age difference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Giants.2C_fans_embracing_do-it-all_infielder_.27Ooooh.21_..._Ree-Bay.21_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Giants.2C_fans_embracing_do-it-all_infielder_.27Ooooh.21_..._Ree-Bay.21-1">[1]</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) was not in season, José would come home to the Dominican Republic and tell Juan about his time in the majors playing <a href="/wiki/Shortstop" title="Shortstop">shortstop</a> for the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>. Juan proved to have baseball ability too. In 1997, <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a> scout Jorge Posada, Sr., spotted Uribe while he was scouting <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> Enemencio Pacheco. Posada offered him a $5,000 contract, but Uribe and his father tried to get a higher amount from agent Bob Michelin. Eventually, Uribe settled for $5,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pacheco_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pacheco-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_baseball_career">Professional baseball career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional baseball career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_league_career">Minor league career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minor league career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Uribe started his professional career playing in the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Summer_League" title="Dominican Summer League">Dominican Summer League</a> (DSL) for the DSL Rockies. In 1998 and 1999, he played for the rookie-level <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arizona_Rookie_League" title="Arizona Rookie League">Arizona Rookie League</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Single-A_(baseball)" title="Single-A (baseball)">Single-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Asheville_Tourists" title="Asheville Tourists">Asheville Tourists</a> in the <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_League" title="South Atlantic League">South Atlantic League</a>. While in Asheville, he <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batted</a> .392 with 28 <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">doubles</a>, compiling a 15-game <a href="/wiki/Hitting_streak" title="Hitting streak">hitting streak</a> at one point in the season. Uribe played for the Single-A advanced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salem_Avalanche" title="Salem Avalanche">Salem Avalanche</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Carolina_League" title="Carolina League">Carolina League</a> in 2000, batting .256 with 13 home runs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> Following the season, <i><a href="/wiki/Baseball_America" title="Baseball America">Baseball America</a></i> named him the number two prospect in the Rockies' organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2001, he was called up by the Rockies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Colorado_Rockies">Colorado Rockies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Colorado Rockies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2001:_Rookie_season">2001: Rookie season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2001: Rookie season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Uribe played 72 games for the Rockies in his rookie season of 2001. He batted .300, had eight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_runs" title="Home runs">home runs</a>, and had 53 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_batted_in" title="Runs batted in">runs batted in</a> (RBIs). He also had 11 <a href="/wiki/Triple_(baseball)" title="Triple (baseball)">triples</a>, tying a club record held by <a href="/wiki/Neifi_P%C3%A9rez" title="Neifi Pérez">Neifi Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pierre" title="Juan Pierre">Juan Pierre</a>. He made his major league debut on April 8, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinch-hitting" title="Pinch-hitting">pinch-hitting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">striking out</a> against <a href="/wiki/Jay_Witasick" title="Jay Witasick">Jay Witasick</a> in an 11–3 loss to the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Three days later, he had his first major league hit against <a href="/wiki/Matt_Morris_(baseball)" title="Matt Morris (baseball)">Matt Morris</a> in a 3–1 loss to the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was sent down to the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Springs_Sky_Sox" title="Colorado Springs Sky Sox">Colorado Springs Sky Sox</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> a few times during the season. However, after July 25 he took over the shortstop position for the Rockies when they traded Pérez. On August 2, he hit his first major league home run against <a href="/wiki/Dave_Coggin" title="Dave Coggin">Dave Coggin</a> in a 4–2 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He tied three other franchise records with two <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hits</a>, two extra base hits, and six total bases in a single inning during an October 7 game against the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2002">2002</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2002">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Uribe played his first full season in the majors for the Rockies in 2002. He started 150 games at shortstop, posting a .240 average, six home runs, 49 RBIs, and 25 doubles. He had a 17-game hitting streak during the months of April and May, a career high. However, his offensive production slumped from May through August, and he also played poor defensive, leading the team with 27 <a href="/wiki/Error_(baseball)" title="Error (baseball)">errors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rockies_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rockies-9">[9]</a></sup> Uribe saw a little improvement offensively late in the season, though, as he tied a career high for hits in a game with four on September 17 in a game against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim</a>; he finished that game a home run short of the <a href="/wiki/Hitting_for_the_cycle" title="Hitting for the cycle">cycle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2003">2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2003, Uribe did not play until June 3 because of an injury suffered when he was rounding the bases during a spring training game on March 1. In his first game back, he homered twice against <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(baseball)" title="Ricardo Rodríguez (baseball)">Ricardo Rodríguez</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He began to play in the outfield for the Rockies, starting at center field on June 15. However, he returned to the shortstop position when <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Hern%C3%A1ndez_(baseball)" title="José Hernández (baseball)">José Hernández</a> was traded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rockies_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rockies-9">[9]</a></sup> In 87 games, he batted .253 with 80 hits, 19 doubles, 10 home runs, and 33 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> Uribe was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> on December 2, 2003, for <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Miles" title="Aaron Miles">Aaron Miles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rockies_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rockies-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicago_White_Sox">Chicago White Sox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Chicago White Sox">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2004">2004</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2004">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Although Uribe did not have a starting role at the beginning of his tenure with the White Sox, he wound up appearing in 134 games for them in 2004, playing second base, shortstop, and third base.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On June 19, he had seven RBIs (one short of the White Sox single-game record) in a 17–14 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He set many career highs in batting categories, including batting average with the bases loaded (.556), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batting_average_with_runners_in_scoring_position" title="Batting average with runners in scoring position">RISP</a> (.340), batting average (.283), home runs (23), RBIs (74), hits (142), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_scored" title="Runs scored">runs</a> (82). He played much better at home, batting .315 with six home runs compared to .248 with seven home runs on the road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> On December 16, he agreed to a contract extension through 2008 with the White Sox.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2005:_World_Series">2005: World Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2005: World Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2005, Uribe spent the entire season playing shortstop after the White Sox did not re-sign <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Valent%C3%ADn" title="José Valentín">José Valentín</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 146 games, a number that would have been higher had it not been for minor injuries, Uribe batted .252 with 121 hits, 23 doubles, 16 home runs, and 74 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> In Game 1 of the <a href="/wiki/2005_American_League_Division_Series" title="2005 American League Division Series">American League Division Series (ALDS)</a>, Uribe hit a home run and had three RBIs as the White Sox defeated the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> 14–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Uribe made two memorable defensive plays for the final two outs of the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Series" title="2005 World Series">2005 World Series</a>, first catching a ball while crashing into the third base stands and then ranging over the middle and firing an off-balance throw on a slow grounder to beat <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astro</a> <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Palmeiro" title="Orlando Palmeiro">Orlando Palmeiro</a> to give the White Sox their first title in 88 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2006">2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On July 2, 2006, Uribe had five RBIs in a 15–11 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> two days later, he again had five RBIs in a 13–0 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Uribe had one of his best power seasons in 2006, batting .235 with 21 home runs and 71 RBIs in 132 games. However, he had the lowest <a href="/wiki/On-base_percentage" title="On-base percentage">on-base percentage</a> of his career, at .257.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> Injuries prevented him from appearing 10 games, and he also lost some starts at shortstop to <a href="/wiki/Alex_Cintr%C3%B3n" title="Alex Cintrón">Alex Cintrón</a> because of his low on-base percentage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He led the White Sox in sacrifice hits and ranked fourth in the AL with a .977 fielding percentage. He also joined <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jeter" title="Derek Jeter">Derek Jeter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Tejada" title="Miguel Tejada">Miguel Tejada</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edgar_Renter%C3%ADa" title="Edgar Rentería">Edgar Rentería</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Young_(baseball)" title="Michael Young (baseball)">Michael Young</a> as the only shortstops to top 70 RBIs in '04, '05, and '06.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2007">2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On September 29, 2007, Uribe had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walkoff" title="Walkoff">walkoff</a> RBI single against <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Rodney" title="Fernando Rodney">Fernando Rodney</a> to give the White Sox a 3–2 win over the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 150 games in 2007, Uribe batted .234 with 120 hits, 18 doubles, 20 home runs, and 68 RBIs. He was second in the league in fielding percentage (.976), but he batted .198 with runners in scoring position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-11"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> The White Sox declined Uribe's option, but on November 7, 2007, they signed him to a one-year, $4.5 million deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The White Sox acquired <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Cabrera" title="Orlando Cabrera">Orlando Cabrera</a> to play shortstop in 2008, forcing Uribe to compete with <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Ram%C3%ADrez" title="Alexei Ramírez">Alexei Ramírez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Richar" title="Danny Richar">Danny Richar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Ozuna" title="Pablo Ozuna">Pablo Ozuna</a> for the position in spring training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2008q_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-2008q-22">[22]</a></sup> Uribe won the second base job, but after an injury in May, Uribe lost his spot in the White Sox starting lineup to Ramírez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2008_Uribe_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-2008_Uribe-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> However, Uribe took over third base from <a href="/wiki/Josh_Fields_(infielder)" title="Josh Fields (infielder)">Josh Fields</a> (who had spent a week filling in for an injured <a href="/wiki/Joe_Crede" title="Joe Crede">Joe Crede</a>) on August 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2008_Uribe_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-2008_Uribe-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 110 games (324 at-bats), Uribe had 80 hits, 22 doubles, seven home runs, and 40 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-12"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> On October 30, Uribe filed for free agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>While with the White Sox, Uribe was always regarded as a talented player. He was especially adept at fielding and hitting home runs. However, <a href="/wiki/Ozzie_Guill%C3%A9n" title="Ozzie Guillén">Ozzie Guillén</a> had issues with Uribe's low on-base percentage and his conditioning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2008q_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-2008q-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="San_Francisco_Giants">San Francisco Giants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: San Francisco Giants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>On January 29, 2009, Uribe signed a minor league deal with the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> It was announced on April 4 that Uribe had made the Giants final roster as a utility player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Uribe wound up making a lot of starts at second base, shortstop, and third base throughout the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On July 10, Uribe bobbled a ground ball with one out in the eighth inning of <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Jonathan Sánchez">Jonathan Sánchez</a>'s <a href="/wiki/No-hitter" title="No-hitter">no-hitter</a> against San Diego, allowing <a href="/wiki/Chase_Headley" title="Chase Headley">Chase Headley</a> to reach first base. The effort had been perfect up to that point, and Sánchez went on to retire each of the remaining five batters, meaning this error lost the first perfect game since <a href="/wiki/Randy_Johnson" title="Randy Johnson">Randy Johnson</a> <a href="/wiki/Perfect_game#Randy_Johnson" title="Perfect game">'s</a> in 2004. This was the first <a href="/wiki/Perfect_game#No-hit.2C_no-walk.2C_no.E2.80.93hit_batsman_games" title="Perfect game">no-hit, no-walk, no–hit batsman game</a> that was not also a perfect game since <a href="/wiki/Terry_Mulholland" title="Terry Mulholland">Terry Mulholland</a>'s no-hitter in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 122 games (398 at-bats), Uribe batted .289 (his highest average since 2001) with 115 hits, 26 doubles, 16 home runs, and 55 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-13"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010:_World_Series">2010: World Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2010: World Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2010, Uribe re-signed a one-year, $3.25 million contract with the Giants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He began 2010 as the Giants' second baseman because of an injury to <a href="/wiki/Freddy_Sanchez" title="Freddy Sanchez">Freddy Sanchez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> At the beginning of May, he moved over to shortstop to replace the injured Rentería; for most of the remainder of the season, he was the Giants' starting shortstop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He hit a game-winning two-run home run on September 5 against <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Broxton" title="Jonathan Broxton">Jonathan Broxton</a>, turning a 4–3 deficit into a 5–4 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On September 23, Uribe hit a <a href="/wiki/Grand_slam_(baseball)" title="Grand slam (baseball)">grand slam</a> against <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Dempster" title="Ryan Dempster">Ryan Dempster</a> and a two-run home run in the second inning against the Cubs for a total of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_leaders_in_RBI_in_one_inning" title="List of Major League Baseball leaders in RBI in one inning">six RBIs</a>, helping the Giants win 13–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>He finished the season batting .256 with a career-high 24 home runs and a career-high 85 RBIs. He had 129 hits on the year in 521 at-bats, bringing his career hit total above the 1,000 mark. He had 24 doubles and 2 triples.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-14"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> In Game 4 of the <a href="/wiki/2010_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2010 National League Championship Series">National League Championship Series</a> against the Phillies on October 20, Uribe had a walk-off <a href="/wiki/Sacrifice_fly" title="Sacrifice fly">sacrifice fly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Roy_Oswalt" title="Roy Oswalt">Roy Oswalt</a> in a 6–5 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In Game 6, on October 23, he hit a go-ahead solo home run against <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Madson" title="Ryan Madson">Ryan Madson</a> in the top of the eighth, which gave the Giants a 3–2 victory, sending them to the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Series" title="2010 World Series">World Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In Game 1 of the World Series on October 27, he hit a three-run home run against <a href="/wiki/Darren_O%27Day" title="Darren O'Day">Darren O'Day</a> which proved decisive in the Giants' 11–7 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> He produced an RBI that made it 2–0 against <a href="/wiki/C._J._Wilson" title="C. J. Wilson">C. J. Wilson</a> in the seventh inning of Game 2; he added another RBI in the eighth as the Giants won 9–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The Giants won the series in five games, and Uribe earned his second World Series championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Los_Angeles_Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Los Angeles Dodgers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the 2010 season, Uribe reached an agreement on a three-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> His first season with the Dodgers was one of his worst seasons as he made two trips to the disabled list and only managed to appear in 77 of 162 games, his fewest amount of games since 2001. His production was the weakest of his career, as he hit a subpar .204 with four home runs and 28 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-15"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> He was placed on the disabled list on July 30, with what was described as abdominal pain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> He was expected to return after 15 days, but the injury didn't get better and was recharacterized as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sports_hernia" title="Sports hernia">sports hernia</a>, which needed season-ending surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the 2012 season, Uribe mostly stayed healthy but his production remained similar to his 2011 totals. From May 14 to June 10, he was on the disabled list with a left wrist injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> After June, he lost his starting role and was relegated to pinch-hitting duties over the second half of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> He played in just 66 games and hit only .191 (his lowest career total) with two home runs and 17 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-16"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Uribe began 2013 as a utility player. However, he made frequent starts at third base, and by June, he had taken over the position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> On July 5, Uribe had seven RBIs and was a <a href="/wiki/Single_(baseball)" title="Single (baseball)">single</a> short of hitting for the cycle in a 10–2 victory over the Giants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 9, 2013, Uribe went four for four and homered in each of his first three trips to the plate against the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>. It was his first 3 home run game in the Major Leagues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> He played in 132 games for the Dodgers in 2013, almost as many as his first two seasons with them combined (143). He hit .278 with 12 homers and 50 RBI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-17"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> On October 7, during Game 4 of the <a href="/wiki/2013_National_League_Division_Series" title="2013 National League Division Series">National League Division Series</a>, Uribe hit a two-run home run in the bottom of the 8th inning to put Los Angeles ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> 4–3. The Dodgers held on to defeat the Braves and took the series 3–1.</p>
<p>On December 14, 2013, Uribe agreed to a 2-year, $15 million contract extension with the Dodgers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Uribe continued his resurgence in 2014, hitting .311 in 103 games. He also had 9 home runs and 54 RBIs. Uribe was well-liked by his teammates, and hailed as a key part of maintaining clubhouse chemistry. <a href="/wiki/Don_Mattingly" title="Don Mattingly">Don Mattingly</a> let him manage the Dodgers for the final game of the regular season, a 10–5 Dodgers win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Uribe started slowly in 2015 and wound up falling behind both <a href="/wiki/Justin_Turner" title="Justin Turner">Justin Turner</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Guerrero" title="Alexander Guerrero">Alex Guerrero</a> on the depth chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In 29 games he hit .247 with one homer and six RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlanta_Braves">Atlanta Braves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Atlanta Braves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 27, 2015, Uribe was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Withrow" title="Chris Withrow">Chris Withrow</a>) in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Callaspo" title="Alberto Callaspo">Alberto Callaspo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Stults" title="Eric Stults">Eric Stults</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ian_Thomas_(baseball)" title="Ian Thomas (baseball)">Ian Thomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Jaime" title="Juan Jaime">Juan Jaime</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> The trade was finalized during the Braves' three-game series at <a href="/wiki/Dodger_Stadium" title="Dodger Stadium">Dodger Stadium</a>, and Uribe appeared in his new team's uniform in the third game, going 0 for 3 in the Atlanta win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> When asked for an opinion on the trade, Uribe commented, "I would've felt bad if they traded me to a soccer, basketball or football team. But it's another baseball team, so I'm happy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Uribe's popularity as a Dodger extended past his trade, as a scheduled <a href="/wiki/Bobblehead" title="Bobblehead">bobblehead</a> night went ahead as planned after he had left the team for the Braves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lat_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-lat-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_Mets">New York Mets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: New York Mets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Juan_Uribe_on_July_25,_2015.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2050" data-file-width="1640" height="275" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Juan_Uribe_on_July_25%2C_2015.jpg/220px-Juan_Uribe_on_July_25%2C_2015.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Juan_Uribe_on_July_25%2C_2015.jpg/330px-Juan_Uribe_on_July_25%2C_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Juan_Uribe_on_July_25%2C_2015.jpg/440px-Juan_Uribe_on_July_25%2C_2015.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>On July 24, 2015, the Braves traded Uribe and <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Johnson_(baseball)" title="Kelly Johnson (baseball)">Kelly Johnson</a> to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> for <a href="/wiki/John_Gant" title="John Gant">John Gant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rob_Whalen" title="Rob Whalen">Rob Whalen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> During his second game with New York, Uribe hit the walk-off single in the tenth inning to win the game for the Mets, 3-2 against the Los Angeles Dodgers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> Uribe missed the <a href="/wiki/2015_National_League_Division_Series" title="2015 National League Division Series">National League Division Series</a> and <a href="/wiki/2015_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2015 National League Championship Series">National League Championship Series</a> due to a chest contusion in September,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> but was added to the Mets' <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Series" title="2015 World Series">World Series</a> roster and came off the bench in the sixth inning of Game 3 to deliver a pinch-hit single, driving in a run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cleveland_Indians">Cleveland Indians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Cleveland Indians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Just before the start of spring training 2016, Uribe signed a one-year, $4 million deal with the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> On June 12, 2016 in Anaheim, Uribe had to be carted off the field after being hit in the crotch by a ground ball which came off the bat of <a href="/wiki/Mike_Trout" title="Mike Trout">Mike Trout</a> at 106 miles per hour (171 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> He was later diagnosed with a <a href="/wiki/Testicle" title="Testicle">testicular</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contusion" title="Contusion">contusion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> He was designated for assignment on August 1, 2016 and released on August 5.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Uribe and his wife Ana have four children: Juan Luis, Juanny, Janny, and Johanny.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-18"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup> He also has a brother named Elpidio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brother_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-brother-69">[69]</a></sup> Uribe does not speak English well and has used a translator throughout his career when conducting interviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pacheco_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pacheco-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2008q_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-2008q-22">[22]</a></sup> He plays winter baseball in the Dominican Republic every year. He has done work for many different charity organizations. In 2004, he read to children on the Golden Apple Foundation Bus. He also helped with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_military" title="United States military">United States military</a> children's coloring contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_3-19"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_shooting_allegations">2006 shooting allegations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Uribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: 2006 shooting allegations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2006, Uribe was alleged to have been involved in a shooting in his native <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. A farmer named Antonio Gonzalez Perez accused Uribe of wounding him with a pellet gun. Despite claims by the <a href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_Province" title="San Cristóbal Province">San Cristóbal Province</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/District_Attorney" title="District Attorney">District Attorney</a> that there was no firm evidence that Uribe was involved in the incident, a judge decided to press forward with the case. Although a defense motion to bar him from leaving the country was rejected, it was ruled on January 5, 2007, that Uribe must appear before a court on the 15 and the 30 of every month until the case was resolved. Uribe, who denied any involvement in the case, suggested that he might not play baseball until the legal proceedings were concluded. He was confident that he would win in court, and the White Sox expected him to be free for spring training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> In February, his name was cleared of any involvement with the shooting, and the court ruled he no longer had to appear on the 15 and 30 of every month. Uribe called the incident "blackmail", saying it was an attempt by Perez to get money from him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brother_69-1"><a href="#cite_note-brother-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
