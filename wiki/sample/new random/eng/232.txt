<p>The <b>Keisei Electric Railway Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">京成電鉄株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Keisei Dentetsu Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (branded as <b>K'SEI</b> since 2001) is a major private railway in <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Prefecture" title="Chiba Prefecture">Chiba Prefecture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, Japan. The name <i>Keisei</i> is the combination of the <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a> 京 from Tokyo <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">東京</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōkyō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and 成 from <a href="/wiki/Narita,_Chiba" title="Narita, Chiba">Narita</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">成田</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Narita</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, which the railway's main line connects. The combination uses different <a href="/wiki/Kanji#Readings" title="Kanji">readings</a> than the ones used in the city names. The railway's main line runs from Tokyo to Narita and the eastern suburb cities of <a href="/wiki/Funabashi,_Chiba" title="Funabashi, Chiba">Funabashi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narashino,_Chiba" title="Narashino, Chiba">Narashino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yachiyo,_Chiba" title="Yachiyo, Chiba">Yachiyo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sakura,_Chiba" title="Sakura, Chiba">Sakura</a>. Keisei runs an airport express train called the <i><a href="/wiki/Skyliner" title="Skyliner">Skyliner</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Keisei_Ueno_Station" title="Keisei Ueno Station">Ueno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nippori_Station" title="Nippori Station">Nippori</a> to <a href="/wiki/Narita_International_Airport" title="Narita International Airport">Narita International Airport</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to its railway business, the Keisei Electric Railway Company owns large bus and taxi services and some real estate holdings. It owns a large share of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oriental_Land_Company" title="Oriental Land Company">Oriental Land Company</a> which owns and manages the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Disney_Resort" title="Tokyo Disney Resort">Tokyo Disney Resort</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keisei_Electric_Railway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Keisei was founded on June 30, 1909 and began services on November 3, 1912, initially operating local train service in eastern Tokyo. Its main line reached Narita in 1930 and Ueno in 1933.</p>
<p>Originally a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge_railway" title="Narrow gauge railway">narrow gauge</a> (<a href="/wiki/Toden_Arakawa_Line" title="Toden Arakawa Line"><span class="nowrap">1,372 mm</span></a> or <span class="nowrap">4 ft 6 in</span> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scotch_gauge" title="Scotch gauge">Scotch gauge</a>) operator, Keisei <a href="/wiki/Track_gauge_conversion" title="Track gauge conversion">converted</a> to <span class="nowrap">1,435 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">4 ft <span class="frac">8<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> in</span>) <span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Standard_gauge" title="Standard gauge">standard gauge</a></span> in 1959. In 1960, Keisei began through service with the <a href="/wiki/Toei_Asakusa_Line" title="Toei Asakusa Line">Toei Asakusa Line</a>, the first interline through service arrangement in Japan.</p>
<p><i>Skyliner</i> service began in 1973 and started serving the airport in 1978, when the first Narita Airport Station opened (today's Higashi-Narita station). A new underground station was opened in 1991 to provide a more direct connection to terminal 1, and in 1992 service began to terminal 2. On July 17, 2010, <i>Skyliner</i> switched its route to the newly built <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narita_Sky_Access" title="Narita Sky Access">Narita Sky Access</a> and reduced the travel time by 15 minutes.</p>
