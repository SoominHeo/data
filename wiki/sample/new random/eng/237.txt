<p>The <b>near-open front unrounded vowel</b>, or <b>near-low front unrounded vowel</b>, is a type of <a href="/wiki/Vowel" title="Vowel">vowel</a> sound, used in some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Speech_communication" title="Speech communication">spoken</a> <a href="/wiki/Language" title="Language">languages</a>. Acoustically it is simply an <i>open</i> or <i>low front unrounded vowel</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The symbol in the <a href="/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet" title="International Phonetic Alphabet">International Phonetic Alphabet</a> that represents this sound is â¨<span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">Ã¦</span>â©, a lowercase of the â¨<a href="/wiki/%C3%86" title="Ã">Ã</a>â© <a href="/wiki/Typographic_ligature" title="Typographic ligature">ligature</a>. Both the symbol and the sound are commonly referred to as "ash".</p>
<p>The rounded counterpart of <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[Ã¦]</span>, the <b>near-open front rounded vowel</b> (for which the IPA provides no separate symbol) has been reported to occur allophonically in <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr100_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gr100-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> see <a href="/wiki/Open_front_rounded_vowel" title="Open front rounded vowel">open front rounded vowel</a> for more information.</p>
<p>The IPA prefers the terms "close" and "open" for vowels, and the name of this article follows this preference. However, a large number of linguists, perhaps a majority, prefer the terms "high" and "low".</p>
<p>In practice, â¨<span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">Ã¦</span>â© is sometimes used to represent the <a href="/wiki/Open_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Open front unrounded vowel">open front unrounded vowel</a>; see the introduction to that page for more information.</p>
