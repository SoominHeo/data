<p><b>Bupyeong District (Bupyeong-gu)</b> is one of the 10 administrative divisions (eight wards and two counties) that comprise <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a>, South Korea. Bupyeong-gu comprises an area of 12.35 square miles (31.98 square km), and has a population of 553,961.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is located north of <a href="/wiki/Namdong_District" title="Namdong District">Namdong-gu</a>, east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seo-gu,_Incheon" title="Seo-gu, Incheon">Seo-gu</a>, and south of <a href="/wiki/Gyeyang_District" title="Gyeyang District">Gyeyang-gu</a>. The city of <a href="/wiki/Bucheon" title="Bucheon">Bucheon</a>, in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Gyeonggi_Province" title="Gyeonggi Province">Gyeonggi Province</a>, comprises its eastern limit.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bupyeong_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bupyeong-gu was created as its own ward in 1995 when 'Buk-gu' was split into 'Gyeyang-gu' and 'Bupyeong-gu' due to rapid growth in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Before the 1970s, much of the area was rich farmland. However, with rapid industrial development and the construction of large apartment complexes, the farmland quickly disappeared leaving what is today a large urban district.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_.22old_Bupyeong.22">History of "old Bupyeong"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bupyeong_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title='Edit section: History of "old Bupyeong"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Old Bupyeong was an administrative district, distinctive from Incheon, before 1914, when the Japanese colonial government merged it with outer parts of old <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a> into Bucheon County. Bupyeong Metropolitan Prefecture (i.e. Buyeong Dohobu, 부평도호부) consisted of today's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bupyeong-gu" title="Bupyeong-gu">Bupyeong-gu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeyang-gu" title="Gyeyang-gu">Gyeyang-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seo_District,_Incheon" title="Seo District, Incheon">Seo-gu</a>(excepting Geomdan) in Incheon, <a href="/wiki/Bucheon" title="Bucheon">Bucheon</a> City in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeonggi" title="Gyeonggi">Gyeonggi</a>, and western part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guro-gu" title="Guro-gu">Guro-gu</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. In 1895, Bupyeong Metropolitan Prefecture was degraded to Bupyeong County. The city centre was in Gyesan-dong, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeyang-gu" title="Gyeyang-gu">Gyeyang-gu</a>. The two remaining buildings of the Bupyeong prefecture office are located in Bupyeong Elementary School.</p>
<p>It should be noted that only Sipjeong-dong was part of Juan township of old Incheon, other than old Bupyeong.</p>
<p>Old Bupyeong was traditionally a district of higher hierarchy than old Incheon in the administrative district system before Incheon became a metropolitan prefecture in early <a href="/wiki/Joseon" title="Joseon">Joseon</a>, and was a military centre for coastal Gyeonggi area, covering old Incheon, Tongjin, Gimpo, Yangcheon, Ansan, Siheung, Gwacheon, and so on.</p>
<ul>
<li>January 1, 1968 Establishment of Buk-gu, Incheon, Gyeonggi-do.</li>
<li>July 1, 1981 Buk-gu, Incheon Direct Administration City</li>
<li>January 1, 1988 Transfer of Geomam-dong and Yeonhui-dong to Seo-gu.</li>
<li>May 1, 1988 Elevated to autonomous district.</li>
<li>January 1, 1989 Gyeyang-myeon, Gimpo-gun, Gyeonggi-do included in Buk-gu.</li>
<li>January 1, 1995 Buk-gu, Incheon Metropolitan City</li>
<li>March 1, 1995 Name change from Buk-gu to Bupyeong-gu, and creation of Gyeyang-gu by dividing part of Bupyeong-gu.</li>
<li>April 20, 2006 Sub-division of Samsan-dong to Samsan-1-dong and Samsan-2-dong.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bupyeong_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the core of the district lies <a href="/wiki/Bupyeong_Station" title="Bupyeong Station">Bupyeong Station</a>. The station lies at the intersection of the Incheon Subway Line 1 and Seoul Subway Line 1, instantly making it one of the busiest subway stations in all of Korea.</p>
<p>The area around Bupyeong Station includes the Bupyeong Underground Market, which boasts hundreds of small shops selling mostly clothing and make-up, and the Bupyeong Cultural Street, a walking street which holds several performances and cultural events throughout the year. The area also contains many restaurants and bars, making it a popular weekend destination for residents of Incheon. Nearby is Bupyeong Market, a large traditional market where vendors sell fresh meat, produce, and traditional medicine.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/GM_Korea" title="GM Korea">GM Korea</a>, formerly <a href="/wiki/Daewoo_Motors" title="Daewoo Motors">Daewoo Motors</a>, has its company headquarters and largest automobile manufacturing plant in Bupyeong. The GM Korea Design Center, which is said to "play an important role in GM's Global Design organization," is located there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Incheon's professional basketball team, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Et_land_black_slamer" title="Et land black slamer">ET Land Elephants</a>, plays at Samsan Gymnasium in Bupyeong.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_Article:Camp_Market">Main Article:<a href="/wiki/Camp_Market" title="Camp Market">Camp Market</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bupyeong_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main Article:Camp Market">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bupyeong District is also home to Camp Market, a small United States Army depot.</p>
