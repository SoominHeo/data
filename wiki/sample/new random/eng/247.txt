<p><b>Michael</b> "<b>M.</b>" <b>Emmet Walsh</b> (born March 22, 1935) is an American actor who has appeared in over 200 films and television series. He is arguably most known for his work in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coen_Brothers" title="Coen Brothers">Coen Brothers</a> first film, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_Simple" title="Blood Simple">Blood Simple</a></i>. Walsh has also appeared in a number of other films including <a href="/wiki/Carl_Reiner" title="Carl Reiner">Carl Reiner</a>'s comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jerk" title="The Jerk">The Jerk</a></i> (1979), <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_Runner" title="Blade Runner">Blade Runner</a></i> (1982), and <a href="/wiki/Brad_Bird" title="Brad Bird">Brad Bird</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated" title="Animated">animated</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Iron_Giant" title="The Iron Giant">The Iron Giant</a></i> (1999).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._Emmet_Walsh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walsh was born in <a href="/wiki/Ogdensburg,_New_York" title="Ogdensburg, New York">Ogdensburg, New York</a>, the son of Agnes Katharine (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%A9e" title="Née">née</a></i> Sullivan) and Harry Maurice Walsh, Sr., a <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a> agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He is of <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was raised in rural <a href="/wiki/Swanton_(town),_Vermont" title="Swanton (town), Vermont">Swanton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref2-3">[3]</a></sup> and attended college at <a href="/wiki/Clarkson_University" title="Clarkson University">Clarkson University</a>. He graduated in 1958 (B.A., Business Administration). In 1998, the Clarkson Alumni Association presented him with the Golden Knight Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._Emmet_Walsh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walsh came to prominence in the 1978 crime film <i><a href="/wiki/Straight_Time" title="Straight Time">Straight Time</a></i>, in which he played a parole officer. He also had a small but memorable role as a crazed sniper in the <a href="/wiki/Steve_Martin" title="Steve Martin">Steve Martin</a> comedy <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Jerk_(film)" title="The Jerk (film)">The Jerk</a></i>. One of his best-known roles was Bryant in <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_Runner" title="Blade Runner">Blade Runner</a></i>. His most acclaimed performance was arguably the double-crossing private detective in <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_Simple" title="Blood Simple">Blood Simple</a></i> (1984), for which he won the 1986 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit_Award" title="Independent Spirit Award">Independent Spirit Award</a> for Best Male Lead.</p>
<p>Walsh made occasional guest appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/Home_Improvement_(TV_series)" title="Home Improvement (TV series)">Home Improvement</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Home_Improvement_characters#Tim_Taylor" title="List of Home Improvement characters">List of Home Improvement characters#Tim Taylor</a>'s father-in-law. In 1992, he appeared as a powerful U.S. Senator in <a href="/wiki/David_Winning" title="David Winning">David Winning</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Killer_Image_(1992_film)" title="Killer Image (1992 film)">Killer Image</a></i>. In <i><a href="/wiki/Christmas_with_the_Kranks" title="Christmas with the Kranks">Christmas with the Kranks</a></i>, he played one of the Kranks' neighbors. He also appeared as Alex Lembeck, a motorcycle cop who appointed himself as Sandy Stockton's chaperone and protector on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sandy_Duncan_Show" title="The Sandy Duncan Show">The Sandy Duncan Show</a></i> in 1972. He appeared in an episode of the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Gibbsville_(TV_series)" title="Gibbsville (TV series)">Gibbsville</a></i> in 1976 and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Little_House_on_the_Prairie_episodes#Season_8_.281981-1982.29" title="List of Little House on the Prairie episodes">Little House on the Prairie</a> in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In comedy, Walsh played the cynical small town sportswriter Dickie Dunn in the iconic 1977 hockey film <a href="/wiki/Slap_Shot_(film)" title="Slap Shot (film)"><i>Slap Shot</i></a>, and a college diving coach in the <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Dangerfield" title="Rodney Dangerfield">Rodney Dangerfield</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Back_to_School" title="Back to School">Back to School</a></i>. According to <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a>'s Stanton-Walsh Rule, "no movie featuring either <a href="/wiki/Harry_Dean_Stanton" title="Harry Dean Stanton">Harry Dean Stanton</a> or M. Emmet Walsh in a supporting role can be altogether bad". Ebert later conceded that this rule was broken by 1989's <i><a href="/wiki/Dream_a_Little_Dream_(film)" title="Dream a Little Dream (film)">Dream a Little Dream</a></i> (Stanton) and 1999's <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Wild_West" title="Wild Wild West">Wild Wild West</a></i> (Walsh). Walsh appeared on an episode of "Frasier".</p>
