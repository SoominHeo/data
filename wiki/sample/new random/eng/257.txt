<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Brothers Home</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Brothers Home</b> (Korean: 형제복지원) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internment_camp" title="Internment camp">internment camp</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> during the 1970s and 80s. During its operation, it held thousands of people who were illegally rounded up off of the street, including children, the homeless, and even a college student who was protesting the regime. The camp was home to some of the worst human rights abuses in South Korea during the period, which were exposed in an <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> article in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THK_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-THK-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
