<p><i><b>Minecraft: The Story of Mojang</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/2012_in_film" title="2012 in film">2012</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Documentary" title="Documentary">documentary</a> about the history of the company <a href="/wiki/Mojang" title="Mojang">Mojang</a> and its creation, <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft" title="Minecraft">Minecraft</a></i>. <i>Minecraft</i> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open-world" title="Open-world">open-world</a> game where players can craft, build and mine. The film features interviews with company personnel such as <a href="/wiki/Markus_Persson" title="Markus Persson">Markus Persson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jens_Bergensten" title="Jens Bergensten">Jens Bergensten</a> and insights from people involved in the gaming industry, as well as players profoundly impacted by the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2pp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-2pp-2">[2]</a></sup> The film was produced by <a href="/wiki/2_Player_Productions" title="2 Player Productions">2 Player Productions</a> and production was funded through a <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a> campaign and the production spanned nearly two years in locations across North America and Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2pp_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-2pp-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pcgamer_-_trailer_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pcgamer_-_trailer-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kickstarter_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-kickstarter-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film premiered first on <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live" title="Xbox Live">Xbox Live</a> on December 22, 2012, and was made available for download and streaming the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pcgamer_-_trailer_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-pcgamer_-_trailer-3">[3]</a></sup> 2 Player Productions also uploaded the documentary to torrent index <a href="/wiki/The_Pirate_Bay" title="The Pirate Bay">The Pirate Bay</a>, but urged people to consider purchasing the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Minecraft is the second feature from game culture archivists 2 Player Productions (Reformat the Planet, Penny Arcade: The Series - Season 1, Double Fine Adventure!).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minecraft:_The_Story_of_Mojang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The documentary follows <i>Minecraft</i> developer Mojang through the <a href="/wiki/Video_game_development" title="Video game development">development</a> process of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2pp_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-2pp-2">[2]</a></sup> The film also features insights from many people in the video gaming industry to discuss <i>Minecraft</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s popularity and influence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2pp_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-2pp-2">[2]</a></sup> Interviews from Persson and other Mojang employees give an insight on the creation and expansion of the studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2pp_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-2pp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minecraft:_The_Story_of_Mojang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film's soundtrack was created by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Rosenfeld" title="Daniel Rosenfeld">Daniel Rosenfeld</a>, who has also created most of the music and sounds for the game itself. The music featured is from Rosenfeld's album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/One_(C418_album)" title="One (C418 album)">One</a></i> which contains 31 tracks. The CD features remixes by crashfaster, <a href="/wiki/Danimal_Cannon" title="Danimal Cannon">Danimal Cannon</a>, Bud Melvin and minusbaby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
