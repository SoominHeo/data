<p><b>Theoderic the Great</b> (Thē-ŏd'ə-rik; <a href="/wiki/Gothic_language" title="Gothic language">Gothic</a>: <span lang="got" xml:lang="got"><i><span style="font-style:normal;">𐌸𐌹𐌿𐌳𐌰𐍂𐌴𐌹𐌺𐍃</span></i></span>; <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Gothic transliteration">Þiudareiks</span></i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Flāvius Theodericus</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el"><span style="font-style:normal;">Θευδέριχος</span></span>, <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Greek transliteration">Theuderikhos</span></i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_English_language" title="Old English language">Old English</a>: <span lang="ang" xml:lang="ang"><i>Þēodrīc</i></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" title="Old Norse language">Old Norse</a>: <span lang="non" xml:lang="non"><i>Þjōðrēkr</i>, <i>Þīðrēkr</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Theoderich</i></span>; 454 – August 30, 526 AD), often referred to as <b>Theodoric</b>, was king of the <a href="/wiki/Ostrogoths" title="Ostrogoths">Ostrogoths</a> (475–526),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> ruler of <a href="/wiki/Ostrogothic_Kingdom" title="Ostrogothic Kingdom">Italy</a> (493–526), <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a> (511–526), and a <a href="/wiki/Patrician_(ancient_Rome)#Late_Roman_and_Byzantine_periods" title="Patrician (ancient Rome)">patricius</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Roman_Empire" title="Eastern Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>. His Gothic name <i>Þiudareiks</i> translates into "people-king" or "ruler of the people".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Theodoric was born in <a href="/wiki/Pannonia" title="Pannonia">Pannonia</a>, now northern <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> in 454, after his people had defeated the <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nedao" title="Battle of Nedao">Battle of Nedao</a>. His father was King <a href="/wiki/Theodemir" title="Theodemir">Theodemir</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">Germanic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amali" title="Amali">Amali</a> nobleman, and his mother was <a href="/wiki/Ereleuva" title="Ereleuva">Ereleuva</a>. Theodoric grew up as a hostage in <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, received a privileged education, and succeeded his father as leader of the Pannonian Ostrogoths in 473.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Settling his people in lower <a href="/wiki/Moesia" title="Moesia">Moesia</a>, Theoderic came into conflict with <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thracian</a> Ostrogoths led by <a href="/wiki/Theodoric_Strabo" title="Theodoric Strabo">Theodoric Strabo</a>, whom he eventually supplanted, uniting the peoples in 484.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine_Emperor" title="Byzantine Emperor">Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Zeno_(emperor)" title="Zeno (emperor)">Zeno</a> subsequently gave him the title of <a href="/wiki/Patrician_(ancient_Rome)#Late_Roman_and_Byzantine_periods" title="Patrician (ancient Rome)">Patrician</a> and the office of <a href="/wiki/Magister_militum" title="Magister militum">Magister militum</a> (master of the soldiers), and even appointed him as <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">Roman Consul</a>. Seeking further gains, Theoderic frequently ravaged the provinces of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Roman_Empire" title="Eastern Roman Empire">Eastern Roman Empire</a>, eventually threatening Constantinople itself. In 488, Emperor Zeno ordered Theoderic to overthrow the German Foederatus <a href="/wiki/Odoacer" title="Odoacer">Odoacer</a>, who had likewise been made patrician and even <a href="/wiki/King_of_Italy" title="King of Italy">King of Italy</a>, but who had since betrayed Zeno, supporting the rebellious <a href="/wiki/Leontius_(usurper)" title="Leontius (usurper)">Leontius</a>. After a victorious three-year war, Theoderic killed Odoacer with his own hands, settled his 200,000 to 250,000 people in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and founded an <a href="/wiki/Ostrogothic_Kingdom" title="Ostrogothic Kingdom">Ostrogothic Kingdom</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> While he promoted separation between the <a href="/wiki/Arianism" title="Arianism">Arian</a> <a href="/wiki/Ostrogoths" title="Ostrogoths">Ostrogoths</a> and the Roman population, Theoderic stressed the importance of racial harmony, though <a href="/wiki/Interracial_marriage" title="Interracial marriage">intermarriage</a> was outlawed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Seeking to restore the glory of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Rome</a>, he ruled Italy in its most peaceful and prosperous period since <a href="/wiki/Valentinian_I" title="Valentinian I">Valentinian</a>, until his death in 526. Memories of his reign made him a <a href="/wiki/Legends_about_Theoderic_the_Great" title="Legends about Theoderic the Great">hero of German legend</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dietrich_von_Bern" title="Dietrich von Bern">Dietrich von Bern</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theoderic_the_Great&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The man who would later rule under the name of Theoderic was born in 454 AD, on the banks of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neusiedler_See" title="Neusiedler See">Neusiedler See</a> near <a href="/wiki/Carnuntum" title="Carnuntum">Carnuntum</a>. This was just a year after the Ostrogoths had thrown off nearly a century of domination by the <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a>. The son of the King <a href="/wiki/Theodemir" title="Theodemir">Theodemir</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ereleuva" title="Ereleuva">Ereleuva</a>, Theoderic went to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> as a young boy, as a <a href="/wiki/Hostage" title="Hostage">hostage</a> to secure the Ostrogoths' compliance with a treaty Theodemir had concluded with the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Leo_I_the_Thracian" title="Leo I the Thracian">Leo the Thracian</a> (ruled 457–474).</p>
<p>He lived as a hostage at the court of Constantinople for many years and learned a great deal about Roman government and military tactics, which served him well when he became the Gothic ruler of a mixed but largely Romanized "barbarian people". Treated with favor by the Emperors <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leo_I_(emperor)" title="Leo I (emperor)">Leo I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zeno_(emperor)" title="Zeno (emperor)">Zeno</a> (ruled 474–475 and 476–491), he became <i><a href="/wiki/Magister_militum" title="Magister militum">magister militum</a></i> (Master of Soldiers) in 483, and one year later he became <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">consul</a>. Afterwards, he returned to live among the Ostrogoths when he was 31 years old and became their king in 488.</p>

<p>At the time, the Ostrogoths were settled in Byzantine territory as <i><a href="/wiki/Foederati" title="Foederati">foederati</a></i> (allies) of the Romans, but were becoming restless and increasingly difficult for <a href="/wiki/Zeno_(emperor)" title="Zeno (emperor)">Zeno</a> to manage. Not long after Theoderic became king, the two men worked out an arrangement beneficial to both sides. The Ostrogoths needed a place to live, and Zeno was having serious problems with <a href="/wiki/Odoacer" title="Odoacer">Odoacer</a>, the King of Italy who had come to power in 476. Ostensibly a viceroy for Zeno, Odoacer was menacing Byzantine territory and not respecting the rights of Roman citizens in Italy. At Zeno's encouragement, Theoderic invaded Odoacer's kingdom.</p>
<p>Theoderic came with his army to Italy in 488, where he won the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Isonzo_(489)" title="Battle of Isonzo (489)">Isonzo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Verona_(489)" title="Battle of Verona (489)">Verona</a> in 489 and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adda_River" title="Adda River">Adda</a> in 490. In 493 he took <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a>. On February 2, 493, Theoderic and Odoacer signed a treaty that assured both parties would rule over Italy. A banquet was organised in order to celebrate this treaty. It was at this banquet that Theoderic, after making a toast, killed Odoacer; Theoderic drew his sword and struck him on the collarbone.</p>

<p>Like Odoacer, Theoderic was ostensibly only a <a href="/wiki/Viceroy" title="Viceroy">viceroy</a> for the emperor in Constantinople. In reality, he was able to avoid imperial supervision, and dealings between the emperor and Theoderic were as equals. Unlike Odoacer, however, Theoderic respected the agreement he had made and allowed Roman citizens within his kingdom to be subject to Roman law and the Roman judicial system. The Goths, meanwhile, lived under their own laws and customs. In 519, when a mob had burned down the <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogues</a> of Ravenna, Theoderic ordered the town to rebuild them at its own expense.</p>
<p>Theoderic the Great sought alliances with, or hegemony over, the other Germanic kingdoms in the west. He allied with the <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Franks</a> by his marriage to <a href="/wiki/Audofleda" title="Audofleda">Audofleda</a>, sister of <a href="/wiki/Clovis_I" title="Clovis I">Clovis I</a>, and married his own female relatives to princes or kings of the <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burgundians" title="Burgundians">Burgundians</a>. He stopped the <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a> from raiding his territories by threatening the weak Vandal king <a href="/wiki/Thrasamund" title="Thrasamund">Thrasamund</a> with invasion, and sent a guard of 5,000 troops with his sister <a href="/wiki/Amalafrida" title="Amalafrida">Amalafrida</a> when she married Thrasamund in 500. For much of his reign, Theoderic was the <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> king of the Visigoths as well, becoming regent for the infant Visigothic king, his grandson <a href="/wiki/Amalaric" title="Amalaric">Amalaric</a>, following the defeat of <a href="/wiki/Alaric_II" title="Alaric II">Alaric II</a> by the Franks under Clovis in 507. The Franks were able to wrest control of <a href="/wiki/Aquitaine" title="Aquitaine">Aquitaine</a> from the Visigoths, but otherwise Theoderic was able to defeat their incursions.</p>
<p>Theoderic's achievements began to unravel even before his death. He had married off his daughter <a href="/wiki/Amalasuntha" title="Amalasuntha">Amalasuntha</a> to the Visigoth <a href="/wiki/Eutharic" title="Eutharic">Eutharic</a>, but Eutharic died in August 522 or 523, so no lasting dynastic connection of Ostrogoths and Visigoths was established. In 522, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> Burgundian king <a href="/wiki/Sigismund_of_Burgundy" title="Sigismund of Burgundy">Sigismund</a> killed his own son, Theoderic's grandson, Sergeric. Theoderic retaliated by invading the Burgundian kingdom and then annexing its southern part, probably in 523. The rest was ruled by Sigismund's <a href="/wiki/Arianism" title="Arianism">Arian</a> brother <a href="/wiki/Godomar" title="Godomar">Godomar</a>, under Gothic protection against the Franks who had captured Sigismund. This brought the territory ruled by Theoderic to its height (see map), but in 523 or 524 the new Catholic Vandal king <a href="/wiki/Hilderic" title="Hilderic">Hilderic</a> imprisoned Amalafrida and killed her Gothic guard. Theoderic was planning an expedition to restore his power over the Vandal kingdom when he died in 526.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_progeny">Family and progeny</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theoderic_the_Great&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family and progeny">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Theoderic was married once.</p>
<p>He had a concubine in <a href="/wiki/Moesia" title="Moesia">Moesia</a>, name unknown, with whom he had two daughters:</p>
<ul>
<li>Theodegotha (ca. 473 – ?). In 494, she was married to <a href="/wiki/Alaric_II" title="Alaric II">Alaric II</a> as a part of her father's alliance with the Visigoths.</li>
<li>Ostrogotha or Arevagni (ca. 475 – ?).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 494 or 496, she was married to the king <a href="/wiki/Sigismund_of_Burgundy" title="Sigismund of Burgundy">Sigismund of Burgundy</a> as a part of her father's alliance with the Burgundians.</li>
</ul>
<p>By his marriage to <a href="/wiki/Audofleda" title="Audofleda">Audofleda</a> in 493 he had one daughter:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Amalasuntha" title="Amalasuntha">Amalasuntha</a>, Queen of the Goths. She was married to Eutharic and had two children: <a href="/wiki/Athalaric" title="Athalaric">Athalaric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matasuntha" title="Matasuntha">Matasuntha</a> (the latter being married to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Witiges" title="Witiges">Witiges</a> first, then, after Witiges' death, married to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Germanus_Justinus" title="Germanus Justinus">Germanus Justinus</a>, neither had children). Any hope for a reconciliation between the Goths and the Romans in the person of a Gotho-Roman Emperor from this family lineage was shattered.</li>
</ul>
<p>After his death in Ravenna in 526, Theoderic was succeeded by his grandson <a href="/wiki/Athalaric" title="Athalaric">Athalaric</a>. Athalaric was at first represented by his mother Amalasuntha, who was a regent queen from 526 until 534. The kingdom of the Ostrogoths, however, began to wane and was conquered by <a href="/wiki/Justinian_I" title="Justinian I">Justinian I</a> starting after the rebellion of 535 and finally ending in 553 with the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mons_Lactarius" title="Battle of Mons Lactarius">Battle of Mons Lactarius</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theoderic_the_Great&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In about 520 the philosopher <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anicius_Manlius_Severinus_Boethius" title="Anicius Manlius Severinus Boethius">Boethius</a> became his <i>magister officiorum</i> (head of all the government and court services). Boethius was a man of science, a dedicated Hellenist bent on translating all the works of <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a> into Latin and harmonizing them with the works of <a href="/wiki/Plato" title="Plato">Plato</a>. Eventually Boethius fell out of favor with Theoderic, perhaps out of a suspicion that he was in sympathy with Justin, emperor of the East, for Arian Theoderic was always somewhat of an outsider among <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicaean_Christianity" title="Nicaean Christianity">Nicaean Christians</a>. Theoderic ordered Boethius executed in 525.</p>

<p>In the meantime <a href="/wiki/Cassiodorus" title="Cassiodorus">Cassiodorus</a> had succeeded Boethius as <i>magister</i> in 523. The pliant historian and courtier could be counted on to provide refined touches to official correspondence. "To the monarch you [Cassiodorus] were a friendly judge and an honored intimate. For when he got free of his official cares he looked to your conversation for the precepts of the sages, that he might make himself a worthy equal to the great men of old. Ever curious, he wanted to hear about the courses of the stars, the tides of the sea, and legendary fountains, that his earnest study of natural science might make him seem to be a veritable philosopher in the purple" (Cassiodorus' letterbook, <i>Variae</i> 9.24.8). The gulf was widening between the ancient senatorial aristocracy whose center was Rome and the adherents of Gothic rule at Ravenna: other distinguished public figures followed Boethius to the block.</p>
<p>Theoderic in his final years was no longer the disengaged Arian patron of religious toleration that he had seemed earlier in his reign. "Indeed, his death cut short what could well have developed into a major persecution of Catholic churches in retaliation for measures taken by Justinian in Constantinople against Arians there"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Theoderic was of <a href="/wiki/Arianism" title="Arianism">Arian</a> faith. At the end of his reign quarrels arose with his Roman subjects and the Byzantine emperor <a href="/wiki/Justin_I" title="Justin I">Justin I</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Arianism" title="Arianism">Arianism</a> issue. Relations between the two nations deteriorated, although Theoderic's ability dissuaded the Byzantines from waging war against him. After his death, that reluctance faded quickly.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mausoleum">Mausoleum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theoderic_the_Great&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mausoleum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Theoderic the Great was interred in Ravenna, but his bones were scattered and his mausoleum was converted to a church after <a href="/wiki/Belisarius" title="Belisarius">Belisarius</a> conquered the city in 540.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RingSalkin1996_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-RingSalkin1996-8">[8]</a></sup> His <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mausoleum_of_Theodoric" title="Mausoleum of Theodoric">mausoleum</a> is one of the finest monuments in <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a>, but an equestrian statue originally there, the <i><a href="/wiki/Regisole" title="Regisole">Regisole</a></i>, which may have been of him, was moved to <a href="/wiki/Pavia" title="Pavia">Pavia</a>, then destroyed during the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> by the local <a href="/wiki/Jacobin" title="Jacobin">Jacobin</a> Club.</p>

<li id="cite_note-5"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-5">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Theodoric led the Ostrogothic invasion of Italy (supported by elements of the Rugii). During the course of four years of fighting, the invasion swept away Odoacer's Post-Imperial Romano-Gothic kingdom. In its place Theodoric created an Ostrogothic kingdom which held much of Italy until Byzantium began a re-conquest of the western empire in southern Italy. Despite the fact that the invasion had been devised by Emperor Zeno, the Ostrogoths ruled independently, and Theodoric and Zeno addressed each other as equals. Overtures to Byzantium were only made by some Ostrogoth leaders after Theodoric's death. A Roman consul was given nominal authority, and the two peoples lived together amicably, with Roman culture greatly influencing the barbarians. The Goths took one-third of the land while the Romans retained the rest. Each side observed their own laws, and intermarriage between Roman and Goth was forbidden. One area in which they didn't agree was in Christianity. The Ostrogoths were confirmed Arians, which the Catholics of the Roman Church found hard to stomach. Not all the Ostrogoths pursued this path into Italy and eventual Italianisation. A branch known as the Tauric Ostrogoths ventured further eastwards, ending up in Crimea by the end of the 5th century. They settled in the region and established an Eastern Germanic Gothic principality, later known as <a href="/wiki/Mangup" title="Mangup">Doros</a>. Additionally, some elements of the Gothic peoples in southern Germany formed part of the Bavarii confederation at the start of the 6th century. <a class="external text" href="http://www.historyfiles.co.uk/KingListsEurope/BarbarianOstrogoths.htm" rel="nofollow">Germanic Tribes: Goths</a></span></li>
