<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Myeongho-myeon</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Myeongho-myeon</b> (Hangeul: 명호면, Hanja: 明湖面) is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_South_Korea#Myeon_.28Township.29" title="Administrative divisions of South Korea">myeon</a> or a township in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonghwa" title="Bonghwa">Bonghwa</a> county of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Gyeongsang" title="North Gyeongsang">North Gyeongsang</a> province in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. The total area of Myeongho-myeon is 114.38 square kilometers, and, as of 2006, the population was 2,428 people. Myeongho-myeon is further divided into eight "ri", or small villages.</p>
<p>Cheongnyang Mountain Provincial Park is within Myeongho-myeon and is home to the famous Cheongnyang Temple and Mountain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
