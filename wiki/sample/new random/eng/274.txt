<p><b>Rocket Raccoon</b> is a fictional <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> appearing in <a href="/wiki/American_comic_book" title="American comic book">American comic books</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>. Created by writer <a href="/wiki/Bill_Mantlo" title="Bill Mantlo">Bill Mantlo</a> and artist <a href="/wiki/Keith_Giffen" title="Keith Giffen">Keith Giffen</a>, the character first appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Preview" title="Marvel Preview">Marvel Preview</a></i> #7 (Summer 1976). He is an intelligent, <a href="/wiki/Anthropomorphism" title="Anthropomorphism">anthropomorphic</a> <a href="/wiki/Raccoon" title="Raccoon">raccoon</a>, who is an expert <a href="/wiki/Marksman" title="Marksman">marksman</a> and master <a href="/wiki/Military_tactics" title="Military tactics">tactician</a>. His name and aspects of his character are a nod to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beatles" title="Beatles">Beatles</a>' 1968 song "<a href="/wiki/Rocky_Raccoon" title="Rocky Raccoon">Rocky Raccoon</a>".</p>
<p>Rocket Raccoon appeared as a prominent member in the 2008 relaunch of the superhero team <a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy_(2008_team)" title="Guardians of the Galaxy (2008 team)">Guardians of the Galaxy</a>. The character has appeared in several media adaptations as a member of that team, including <a href="/wiki/Animation" title="Animation">animated</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toy" title="Toy">toys</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a>. He appears in the 2014 live-action film <i><a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy_(film)" title="Guardians of the Galaxy (film)">Guardians of the Galaxy</a></i>, albeit only as "Rocket", with his voice provided by <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Cooper" title="Bradley Cooper">Bradley Cooper</a> and motion capture provided by <a href="/wiki/Sean_Gunn" title="Sean Gunn">Sean Gunn</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_history">Publication history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rocket_Raccoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The character was created by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Mantlo" title="Bill Mantlo">Bill Mantlo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keith_Giffen" title="Keith Giffen">Keith Giffen</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and inspired by <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Rocky_Raccoon" title="Rocky Raccoon">Rocky Raccoon</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBRArticle_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBRArticle-2">[2]</a></sup> Other references to the song were featured in Rocket's appearance in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(comic_book)" title="The Incredible Hulk (comic book)">The Incredible Hulk</a></i> #271 (May 1982), which was titled "Now Somewhere In the Black Holes of Sirius Major There Lived a Young Boy Named Rocket Raccoon" and saw the Hulk help Rocket stop a villain trying to steal "Gideon's Bible", which in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Universe" title="Marvel Universe">Marvel Universe</a> was a book that contained the sum of all knowledge on the Loonies colony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBRFeb2014_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBRFeb2014-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rocket Raccoon first appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Preview" title="Marvel Preview">Marvel Preview</a></i> #7 (Summer 1976), in the back-up feature "The Sword in the Star", under the name "Rocky".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He would next appear in <i>The Incredible Hulk</i> #271 (May 1982), where it is learned that "Rocky" is short for "Rocket". In 1985, he received his own four-issue <a href="/wiki/Rocket_Raccoon_(limited_series)" title="Rocket Raccoon (limited series)">limited series</a> and in an afterword to the first issue, Mantlo himself asserted that this was the same character seen in <i>Preview</i>, <a href="/wiki/Penciller" title="Penciller">penciled</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Mignola" title="Mike Mignola">Mike Mignola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inked" title="Inked">inked</a> by <a href="/wiki/Al_Gordon" title="Al Gordon">Al Gordon</a> with <a href="/wiki/Al_Milgrom" title="Al Milgrom">Al Milgrom</a>. Rocket appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Quasar_(Wendell_Vaughn)" title="Quasar (Wendell Vaughn)">Quasar</a></i> #15 in 1990 and later appeared in three issues of <i><a href="/wiki/She-Hulk" title="She-Hulk">Sensational She-Hulk</a></i> in 1992 (#44-46).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBRFeb2014_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBRFeb2014-3">[3]</a></sup> The character only appeared in a total of ten comic books in his first thirty years of existence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collins_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Besides a brief appearance in a 2006 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Exiles_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Exiles (Marvel Comics)">Exiles</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBRFeb2014_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-CBRFeb2014-3">[3]</a></sup> Rocket Raccoon was next seen in 2007's <i><a href="/wiki/Annihilation:_Conquest" title="Annihilation: Conquest">Annihilation: Conquest</a></i> and <i>Annihilation: Conquest - <a href="/wiki/Star-Lord" title="Star-Lord">Star-Lord</a></i> limited series,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBRFeb2014_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-CBRFeb2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and their spin-off series, a new volume of <i><a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy_(2008_team)" title="Guardians of the Galaxy (2008 team)">Guardians of the Galaxy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBRFeb2014_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-CBRFeb2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He remained a regular member of the series cast until it was canceled with issue #25 in 2010, also appearing in the follow-up limited series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thanos_Imperative" title="The Thanos Imperative">The Thanos Imperative</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-4">[4]</a></sup> Along with fellow Guardian <a href="/wiki/Groot" title="Groot">Groot</a>, Rocket starred in backup features in <i><a href="/wiki/Annihilators" title="Annihilators">Annihilators</a></i> #1-4 (March–June 2011) and <i>Annihilators: Earthfall</i> #1-4 (Sept.–Dec. 2011).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCD_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-GCD-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rocket Raccoon, along with the other members of the Guardians, appeared in issues #4-8 of <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers_Assemble_(comics)" title="Avengers Assemble (comics)">Avengers Assemble</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AvengersAssemble_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-AvengersAssemble-9">[9]</a></sup> a series intended as a jumping-on point for fans of the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Avengers_(2012_film)" title="The Avengers (2012 film)">The Avengers</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AvengersAssemble2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-AvengersAssemble2-10">[10]</a></sup> He appears prominently in <i>Guardians of the Galaxy</i> vol. 3, a part of the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Marvel_NOW!" title="Marvel NOW!">Marvel NOW!</a> relaunch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MarvelNOW_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-MarvelNOW-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2014, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Skottie_Young" title="Skottie Young">Skottie Young</a> will be the writer and artist for a <i>Rocket Raccoon</i> ongoing series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The series began in July 2014, with the first issue selling over 300,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jake_Parker" title="Jake Parker">Jake Parker</a> replaced Young as the artist beginning with issue #5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The series ended in May 2015 as one of many titles to be cancelled for Marvel's <i><a href="/wiki/Secret_Wars_(2015_comic_book)" title="Secret Wars (2015 comic book)">Secret Wars</a></i> event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> A new volume, titled <i>Rocket Raccoon and Groot</i>, began in January 2016 as part of the <a href="/wiki/All-New,_All-Different_Marvel" title="All-New, All-Different Marvel">All-New, All-Different Marvel</a> relaunch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>Rocket Raccoon acts as the "Guardian of the Keystone Quadrant", an area of outer space sealed off from the rest of the cosmos by the so-called Galacian Wall. Rocket is captain of the starship <i>Rack 'n' Ruin</i>, and he and his first mate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wal_Rus" title="Wal Rus">Wal Rus</a> (a talking <a href="/wiki/Walrus" title="Walrus">walrus</a>) come from the planet Halfworld in the Keystone Quadrant, an abandoned colony for the <a href="/wiki/Mental_disorder" title="Mental disorder">mentally ill</a> where the animal companions were genetically manipulated to grant them human level intelligence and bipedal body construction for many to become caretakers of the inmates. Rocket was Halfworld's chief law officer ("ranger") who protected the colony against various threats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hulk271_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hulk271-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>At one point, Judson Jakes tried to steal the Halfworld Bible, but was thwarted by Rocket and various animal associates. Later, Lord Dyvyne abducted Rocket's friend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lylla" title="Lylla">Lylla</a>, and Jakes began the Toy War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RRminiNo1_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-RRminiNo1-18">[18]</a></sup> As the Toy War continued, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blackjack_O%27Hare" title="Blackjack O'Hare">Blackjack O'Hare</a> teamed up with Rocket, and Rocket was reunited with Lylla.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RRminiNo2_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-RRminiNo2-19">[19]</a></sup> The <i>Rack 'n' Ruin</i> was soon destroyed, as Judson Jakes and Lord Dyvyne teamed up to kill Rocket Raccoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RRminiNo3_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-RRminiNo3-20">[20]</a></sup> Rocket Raccoon and his friends cured the Loonies of their mental illnesses, as Judson Jakes and Lord Dyvyne were apparently killed. Rocket and the animals as well as the robots left Halfworld and took off into space for their own adventures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RRminiNo4_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-RRminiNo4-21">[21]</a></sup> Some time later, Rocket was revealed to have been a laboratory subject on the <a href="/wiki/Stranger_(comics)" title="Stranger (comics)">Stranger</a>'s planet, and escaped his captivity there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Quasar15_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Quasar15-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guardians_of_the_Galaxy">Guardians of the Galaxy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rocket_Raccoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Guardians of the Galaxy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rocket Raccoon resurfaced as a member of the team chosen to accompany <a href="/wiki/Star-Lord" title="Star-Lord">Star-Lord</a> on his mission to stop a <a href="/wiki/Phalanx_(comics)" title="Phalanx (comics)">Phalanx</a> infiltration of the <a href="/wiki/Kree" title="Kree">Kree</a> homeworld.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnnCSL1_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-AnnCSL1-23">[23]</a></sup> Rocket is depicted as a gifted military tactician who is fearless, loyal, and insightful. It is heavily implied that he has a case of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Obsessive_compulsive_disorder" title="Obsessive compulsive disorder">obsessive compulsive disorder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnnCSL2_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-AnnCSL2-24">[24]</a></sup> Rocket's trademark rocket skates are absent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnnCSL1_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-AnnCSL1-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rocket joins the new <a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy_(2008_team)" title="Guardians of the Galaxy (2008 team)">Guardians of the Galaxy</a> at the behest of his friend Star-Lord.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GotGv2no1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-GotGv2no1-25">[25]</a></sup> It is he who suggests that the team adopt that moniker after hearing it mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Vance_Astro" title="Vance Astro">Major Victory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GotGv2no2_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-GotGv2no2-26">[26]</a></sup> Later on, when the team almost disbands and Peter Quill (Star-Lord) disappears (sent into the Negative Zone by <a href="/wiki/Ronan_the_Accuser" title="Ronan the Accuser">Ronan</a>), Rocket keeps the team alive, and brings in <a href="/wiki/Groot" title="Groot">Groot</a> as member, as the two had become firm friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GotGv2no8_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-GotGv2no8-27">[27]</a></sup> Rocket takes over as leader until they rescue Peter, and save the Earth from an alien invasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GotGv2no10_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-GotGv2no10-28">[28]</a></sup> When the team tries to halt the growing <a href="/wiki/War_of_Kings" title="War of Kings">War of Kings</a>, Rocket leads the portion of the team assigned to make contact with the <a href="/wiki/Shi%27ar" title="Shi'ar">Shi'ar</a>. They are unable to teleport into the Emperor <a href="/wiki/Vulcan_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Vulcan (Marvel Comics)">Vulcan</a>'s flagship and have to be rescued by the <a href="/wiki/Starjammers" title="Starjammers">Starjammers</a> and Rocket's old friend <a href="/wiki/Ch%27od" title="Ch'od">Ch'od</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GotGv2no13_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-GotGv2no13-29">[29]</a></sup> They are able to bring back the rightful queen of the Shi'ar, but she is killed after Rocket departs, much to his horror.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WarofK3_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-WarofK3-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the Guardians disband,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThanosImpDev_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ThanosImpDev-31">[31]</a></sup> Rocket Raccoon takes up a normal job with Timely Inc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Annihilators1_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Annihilators1-32">[32]</a></sup> He and Groot were reunited and tricked into returning to Halfworld: here, he found out his memories of the place were mostly half-truths and deliberately crafted fake memories. In reality, Jakes and Blackjack O'Hare had worked with Rocket in providing security at Halfworld Asylum for the Criminally Insane; <i>Doctor</i> Dyvyne had been Head of Psychology there; and both the anthropomorphic animals and the automaton clowns were deliberately created to work at the asylum, as their appearance would calm the inmates. The crises that Rocket half-remembered had been caused by the psychic supervillain the <a href="/wiki/Star_Thief" title="Star Thief">Star Thief</a>, who had been admitted as an inmate and used his psychic powers to turn the inhabitants against each other. Rocket had turned the warders into a biological "key" to keep Star-Thief locked up, and deliberately altered his mind and left Halfworld so it could never be opened - but he was tricked into returning by the Thief, whose mind had escaped into the asylum after his host body died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Annihilators3_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Annihilators3-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>When the <i>Guardians of the Galaxy</i> title was relaunched in 2013, Rocket was again a member of the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Secret_Wars_(2015_comic_book)" title="Secret Wars (2015 comic book)">Secret Wars</a></i>, the Guardians of the Galaxy take part in the incursion between Earth-616 and Earth-1610, in which Rocket and Groot are killed by the Children of Tomorrow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All-New.2C_All-Different_Marvel">All-New, All-Different Marvel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rocket_Raccoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: All-New, All-Different Marvel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As part of the <i><a href="/wiki/All-New,_All-Different_Marvel" title="All-New, All-Different Marvel">All-New, All-Different Marvel</a></i>, Rocket Raccoon takes leadership of the Guardians of the Galaxy while Star-Lord was busy handling things on Spartax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_abilities">Powers and abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rocket_Raccoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Powers and abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rocket Raccoon possesses the normal attributes of an Earth <a href="/wiki/Raccoon" title="Raccoon">raccoon</a>, including speed (which has been additionally amplified by his training), and an acute sense of smell, sight, hearing and touch. Sharp claws allow him to scale walls, buildings, and trees with ease. He is an accomplished starship pilot, an expert marksman with the two laser pistols he carries as well as having an affinity for heavy weapons. He is a master military tactician and leader, attributes that help him take charge of the Guardians of the Galaxy when <a href="/wiki/Star-Lord" title="Star-Lord">Star-Lord</a> is unavailable.</p>

In addition to being genetically altered, Rocket is shown to have cybernetic implants within his body. He is only referred to as "Rocket" in the film, even asking "what's a raccoon?" when told of his resemblance to the Earthly animal. He serves as the team's pilot and technical expert, providing the weapon that <a href="/wiki/Star-Lord" title="Star-Lord">Peter Quill</a> uses against <a href="/wiki/Ronan_the_Accuser" title="Ronan the Accuser">Ronan the Accuser</a> in the final confrontation and piloting a Ravager ship in the final assault against Ronan's fleet. Rocket also displays a talent for engineering jailbreaks. Having previously escaped from 22 prisons, he devises and executes a scheme to free his eventual teammates from a Nova Corps prison. Screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Perlman" title="Nicole Perlman">Nicole Perlman</a> has stated that early drafts of the film did not include the character due to "a little bit of a fear that he would come across as cartoonish because he's a raccoon." Marvel Studios president <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Feige" title="Kevin Feige">Kevin Feige</a> was persuaded to approve Rocket's inclusion in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> A live raccoon named "Oreo" was used on set to help animators accurately capture the motion and behaviors of it for their CGI model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Director <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Gunn_(filmmaker)" title="James Gunn (filmmaker)">James Gunn</a> took Oreo to the film's European premiere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></li>
