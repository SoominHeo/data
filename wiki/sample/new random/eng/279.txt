<p><b>Tomioka Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">富岡駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tomioka-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tomioka,_Fukushima" title="Tomioka, Fukushima">Tomioka, Fukushima</a>, Japan.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomioka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tomioka Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dban_Line" title="Jōban Line">Jōban Line</a>, and is located 247.0 kilometers from the official starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Nippori_Station" title="Nippori Station">Nippori Station</a>. However, operations on the Jōban Line between <a href="/wiki/Tatsuta_Station" title="Tatsuta Station">Tatsuta Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nittaki_Station" title="Nittaki Station">Nittaki Station</a> are suspended indefinitely due <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Daiichi_nuclear_disaster" title="Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster">Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomioka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tomioka Station formerly had one <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a>, connected by a footbridge. The station building had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midori_no_Madoguchi" title="Midori no Madoguchi">Midori no Madoguchi</a> staffed ticket office.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomioka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yonomori Station was opened on 23 August 1898. The station was absorbed into the JR East network upon the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987. The station was destroyed by the <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> from the 11 March <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake</a>. In January 2015, work started on dismantling the remains of the station building and footbridge. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surrounding_area">Surrounding area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomioka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Surrounding area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tomioka is within the evacuation zone surrounding the <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Daiichi_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant">Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant</a>. Since August 2012 it has been possible to enter the area, but remaining in the area overnight is prohibited due to high radiation levels.</p>
