<p>The <b>State of Hanover</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Land Hannover</i></span>) was a short-lived state within the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> zone of <a href="/wiki/Allied-occupied_Germany" title="Allied-occupied Germany">Allied-occupied Germany</a>. It existed for 92 days in the course of the dissolution of the <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Prussia" title="Free State of Prussia">Free State of Prussia</a> after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> until the foundation of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> in 1946. The state saw itself in the tradition of the former <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hanover" title="Kingdom of Hanover">Kingdom of Hanover</a>, annexed by Prussia in 1866, reflected in the <a href="/wiki/Saxon_Steed" title="Saxon Steed">Saxon Steed</a> state emblem. After Lower Saxony was founded by merging Hanover with several smaller states, it would continue to use the Hanover emblems.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_of_Hanover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The State of Hanover covered the territory of the former Prussian <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Hanover" title="Province of Hanover">Province of Hanover</a> without those eastern parts that had become part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_occupation_zone" title="Soviet occupation zone">Soviet occupation zone</a> after World War II (<a href="/wiki/Amt_Neuhaus" title="Amt Neuhaus">Amt Neuhaus</a> and the eastern part of <a href="/wiki/Bleckede" title="Bleckede">Bleckede</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elbingerode" title="Elbingerode">Elbingerode</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilfeld" title="Ilfeld">Ilfeld</a>). It therefore included 85 percent of the present-day state of Lower Saxony.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_of_Hanover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the Second World War the State of Hanover was founded under <a href="/wiki/Ordinance_No._46" title="Ordinance No. 46">Ordinance No. 46</a> of the British <a href="/wiki/Military_occupation" title="Military occupation">military government</a> dated 23 August 1946 "concerning the dissolution of the provinces of the former State of Prussia in the British zone and their recreation as independent states". Its first <a href="/wiki/Minister-president" title="Minister-president">minister-president</a> was the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">Social Democratic</a> politician <a href="/wiki/Hinrich_Wilhelm_Kopf" title="Hinrich Wilhelm Kopf">Hinrich Wilhelm Kopf</a>.</p>
<p>Nevertheless, on 23 November 1946 the <a href="/wiki/Allied_Control_Council" title="Allied Control Council">British Military Government</a> had approved the formation of the new state of Lower Saxony from the unification of the German states of <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Brunswick" title="Free State of Brunswick">Brunswick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Oldenburg" title="Free State of Oldenburg">Oldenburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Schaumburg-Lippe" title="Free State of Schaumburg-Lippe">Schaumburg-Lippe</a> with Hanover at the instigation of their German leaders. Kopf also discussed other territorial options for a Lower Saxony state which would have also included <a href="/wiki/Bremen_(state)" title="Bremen (state)">Bremen</a> and the region of <a href="/wiki/Ostwestfalen-Lippe" title="Ostwestfalen-Lippe">Ostwestfalen-Lippe</a>.</p>
<p>Like the eastern parts of Hanover, the eastern areas of Brunswick which had fallen to the Soviet zone, including the former <a href="/wiki/County_of_Blankenburg" title="County of Blankenburg">County of Blankenburg</a> and the exclave of <a href="/wiki/Calv%C3%B6rde" title="Calvörde">Calvörde</a> (part of the <a href="/wiki/Helmstedt_(district)" title="Helmstedt (district)">Helmstedt</a> district) were excluded and later integrated into the <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East German</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Saxony-Anhalt" title="Saxony-Anhalt">Saxony-Anhalt</a>. Only the Hanoverian Amt Neuhaus and those parts of Bleckede, which had also laid on Soviet-occupied territory, were again united with Lower Saxony after the <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a> in 1993.</p>
