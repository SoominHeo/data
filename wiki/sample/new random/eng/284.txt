<p>The <b>Neotominae</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Subfamily" title="Subfamily">subfamily</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_classification" title="Scientific classification">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Cricetidae" title="Cricetidae">Cricetidae</a>. They consist of four tribes, 16 <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a>, and many <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_World_rats_and_mice" title="New World rats and mice">New World rats and mice</a>, predominantly found in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. Among them are the well-known deer mice, white-footed mice, packrats, and grasshopper mice.</p>
<p>Neotomines are related to the other two subfamilies of mice in the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sigmodontinae" title="Sigmodontinae">Sigmodontinae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tylomyinae" title="Tylomyinae">Tylomyinae</a>. Many authorities place them all in a single subfamily, <b>Sigmodontinae</b>.</p>
