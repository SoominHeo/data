<p><b>Buyeo County</b> (<i>Buyeo-gun</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_South_Korea" title="Administrative divisions of South Korea">county</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Chungcheong_Province" title="South Chungcheong Province">South Chungcheong Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. Buyeo-eup, the county's capital, was the site of the capital of <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a> from 538-660 AD, during which it was called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sabi_Fortress" title="Sabi Fortress">Sabi Fortress</a>.</p>
<p>Famous people associated with Buyeo County in more recent times include noted stem-cell researcher <a href="/wiki/Hwang_Woo-suk" title="Hwang Woo-suk">Hwang Woo-suk</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buyeo_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Buyeo is located at the southern area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chungcheongnam-do" title="Chungcheongnam-do">Chungcheongnam-do</a>, the heart of the Korean peninsula, and has the same longitude as <a href="/wiki/Wyndham,_Western_Australia" title="Wyndham, Western Australia">Wyndham, Australia</a> and the same latitude as <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Three Kingdoms Era</a>, the capital of Baekje was moved to present-day Buyeo-eup (then called Sabi) on account of crowding in the former capital, which was near present-day <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. A fortress called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Garimseong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Garimseong (page does not exist)">Garimseong</a> was constructed for defending the new capital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Buyeo County has numerous historical sites from this era, such as the mountain fortresses (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_castles_in_Korea" title="List of castles in Korea">sanseong</a></i>) at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seongheung_mountain_fortress&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seongheung mountain fortress (page does not exist)">Seongheung</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Buso_mountain_fortress&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Buso mountain fortress (page does not exist)">Buso</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cheong_mountain_fortress&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cheong mountain fortress (page does not exist)">Cheong</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Tumulus" title="Tumulus">tumuli</a> at Neungsan-ri, and the temple site at Gunsu-ri, all designated <a href="/wiki/Historic_Sites_of_South_Korea" title="Historic Sites of South Korea">Historic Sites of South Korea</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Baekje_Cultural_Festival">Baekje Cultural Festival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buyeo_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Baekje Cultural Festival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Buyeo County held its first Baekje cultural festival in 1955 celebrating the history of ancient Baekje and the culture that blossomed in the Buyeo area, sponsored by the residents of Buyeo County themselves. The province began</p>
<p>From 1965, province officially announced to take place Baekje cultural memorial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The festival showcases traditional costumes and marching from the Baekje era, focusing on the works of the six kings of the Sabi era and the story of the loyalty of three subjects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This festival was ranked among the best in the country in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
