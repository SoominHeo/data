<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">1976 Tangshan earthquake</h1>

<p>The <b>Tangshan earthquake</b>, also known as the <b>Great Tangshan earthquake</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zschau_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zschau-1">[1]</a></sup> was a natural disaster that occurred on July 28, 1976. It is believed to be the largest <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> of the 20th century by death toll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spignesi_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spignesi-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Epicenter" title="Epicenter">epicenter</a> of the earthquake was near <a href="/wiki/Tangshan" title="Tangshan">Tangshan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, an industrial city with approximately one million inhabitants. The number of deaths initially reported by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Government of the People's Republic of China">Chinese government</a> was 655,000, but this number has since been stated to be around 240,000 to 255,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spignesi_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spignesi-2">[2]</a></sup> Another report indicates that the actual death toll was much higher, at approximately 650,000, and explains that the lower estimates are limited to Tangshan and exclude fatalities in the densely populated surrounding areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>A further 164,000 people were recorded as being severely injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stoltman_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stoltman-4">[4]</a></sup> The earthquake occurred between a series of political events involving the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a>, ultimately leading to the expulsion of the ruling <a href="/wiki/Gang_of_Four" title="Gang of Four">Gang of Four</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>'s chosen successor, <a href="/wiki/Hua_Guofeng" title="Hua Guofeng">Hua Guofeng</a>. In traditional Chinese thought, natural disasters are seen as a precursor of dynastic change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The earthquake hit in the early morning and lasted 14 to 16 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roza_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Roza-6">[6]</a></sup> Chinese government official sources state a magnitude of 7.8 on the <a href="/wiki/Richter_magnitude_scale" title="Richter magnitude scale">Richter magnitude scale</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stoltman_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stoltman-4">[4]</a></sup> though some sources listed it as high as 8.2. It was followed by a major 7.1 magnitude <a href="/wiki/Aftershock" title="Aftershock">aftershock</a> some 16 hours later, increasing the death toll to over 255,000. The earthquake was generated by the 25-mile-long Tangshan Fault, which runs near the city and ruptured due to tectonic forces caused by the <a href="/wiki/Amurian_Plate" title="Amurian Plate">Amurian Plate</a> sliding past the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Plate" title="Eurasian Plate">Eurasian Plate</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_warnings_and_predictions">Early warnings and predictions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early warnings and predictions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Water_well" title="Water well">Well</a> water in a village outside of Tangshan reportedly rose and fell three times the day before the earthquake. Gas began to spout out of a well in another village on July 12 and then increased on July 25 and July 26. City dwellers from the "downtown" area who had fish discovered that the fish were restless, jumping out of the aquarium as if wanting to escape; therefore, some animals may have anticipated the earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>More than half a month before the earthquake struck, Wang Chengmin (汪成民) of the State Seismological Bureau (SSB) Analysis and Prediction Department had already concluded that the <a href="/wiki/Tangshan" title="Tangshan">Tangshan</a> region would be struck by a significant earthquake between July 22, 1976, and August 5, 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zschau_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Zschau-1">[1]</a></sup> Abnormal signals were mentioned for <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tangshan" title="Tangshan">Tangshan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bohai_Economic_Rim" title="Bohai Economic Rim">Bohai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zhangjiakou" title="Zhangjiakou">Zhangjiakou</a> regions. Wang made an effort to publicize the information to 60 people. One of the people listening in Qinglong was official <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wang_Chunqing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wang Chunqing (page does not exist)">Wang Chunqing</a> (王春青).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zschau_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Zschau-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_prepared:_Qinglong_County">The prepared: Qinglong County</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The prepared: Qinglong County">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After voicing the concerns to Wang Chunqing (王春青), his county took the report very seriously. Already some sources showed that the county had been preparing as much as two years earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Up to 800 members of his county tried to respond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zschau_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Zschau-1">[1]</a></sup> On July 25 and 26, 1976 each community of Qinglong county had emergency meetings to prepare and instruct villagers. Buildings were examined and water reservoirs were given special attention. The county secretary in charge, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ran_Guangqi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ran Guangqi (page does not exist)">Ran Guangqi</a> (冉广岐) decided to risk his political career and certain jail term to prepare the 470,000 residents of the county for the upcoming earthquake by ordering officials to educate the people as well as evacuate the local population to safer areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zschau_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Zschau-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Damage">Damage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Damage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tangshan_earthquake.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="499" data-file-width="756" height="145" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Tangshan_earthquake.jpg/220px-Tangshan_earthquake.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Tangshan_earthquake.jpg/330px-Tangshan_earthquake.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Tangshan_earthquake.jpg/440px-Tangshan_earthquake.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tangshan_earthquake.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Buildings were flattened into rubble when the earthquake hit.</div>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Earthquake_engineering" title="Earthquake engineering">Earthquake engineering</a></div>
<p>The high loss of life caused by the earthquake can be attributed to the time it struck and how suddenly it struck, as well as to the quality and nature of building construction in China. The earthquake lacked the <a href="/wiki/Foreshock" title="Foreshock">foreshocks</a> that sometimes come with earthquakes of this magnitude. It also struck at just before 4 AM, when most people were asleep and unprepared. Tangshan itself was thought to be in a region with a relatively low risk of earthquakes. Very few buildings had been built to withstand an earthquake, and the city lies on unstable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alluvial_soil" title="Alluvial soil">alluvial soil</a>. Therefore, hundreds of thousands of buildings were destroyed.</p>
<p>The earthquake devastated the city over an area of roughly 6.5 kilometres (4.0 mi) by 8 kilometres (5.0 mi). Many of the people who survived the initial earthquake were trapped under collapsed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buildings" title="Buildings">buildings</a>. Tremors were felt as far away as <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a> approximately 760 km (470 mi) away. Eighty-five percent of the buildings in the city were collapsed into ruins or became uninhabitable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roza_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Roza-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Seismic_wave" title="Seismic wave">seismic waves</a> spread far, with damage in cities such as <a href="/wiki/Qinhuangdao" title="Qinhuangdao">Qinhuangdao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a>, and a few buildings as far away as <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, 140 km from the epicenter, were damaged. The economic loss totaled to 10 billion <a href="/wiki/Chinese_yuan" title="Chinese yuan">yuan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stoltman_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stoltman-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_toll">Death toll</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death toll">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversial_statistics">Controversial statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversial statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Until fairly recently, China's political environment has made it difficult to properly gauge the extent of natural disasters. Successive governments have placed more importance on the <i>appearance of harmony</i> rather than accurate information on damage. The Tangshan Earthquake came at a rather politically sensitive time during the late stages of the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>, making accurate statistics especially difficult to find. The Tangshan earthquake killed 242,000 people according to official figures, though some sources estimate a death toll up to three times higher. This would make it the deadliest earthquake in modern times, and the second or third deadliest in recorded history. It is worth noting that the population of Tangshan at the time the quake struck was estimated to be around 1.6 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and that most of Tangshan's city proper was flattened.</p>
<p>Many experts believe the Chinese government has never released an accurate death toll for the disaster. The death toll figure of 242,419 came from the Chinese Seismological Service in 1988,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spignesi_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Spignesi-2">[2]</a></sup> while some sources have estimated the death toll to be at 650,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Others range as high as 700,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The initial estimates of 655,000 dead and 779,000 injured were released by Hebei Revolutionary Committee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spence_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spence-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg/250px-Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg/375px-Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg/500px-Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tangshan_Earthquake_Memorial_III.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A Tangshan earthquake memorial in Tianjin</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Government of the People's Republic of China">Chinese government</a> refused to accept international aid from the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, and insisted on self-reliance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spence_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spence-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> sent 56 medical teams to Tangshan, in addition to the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> who were assisting while also trying to fix their tarnished image of <a href="/wiki/Red_Guards_(China)" title="Red Guards (China)">Red Guard</a> suppressors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spence_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Spence-12">[12]</a></sup> Rebuilding infrastructure started immediately in Tangshan, and the city was completely rebuilt. Today Tangshan city is home to nearly three million people and is known as "Brave City of China."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_aftermath">Political aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Political aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The earthquake came in one of the most dramatic years in the history of the People's Republic. The earthquake was preceded by the death of <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Enlai" title="Zhou Enlai">Zhou Enlai</a> in earlier months and followed by the death of <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a> in September. The political repercussions of the disaster and its aftermath contributed to the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>. Mao's chosen successor <a href="/wiki/Hua_Guofeng" title="Hua Guofeng">Hua Guofeng</a> showed concern, thereby solidifying his status as China's leader. He, along with Vice-Premier <a href="/wiki/Chen_Yonggui" title="Chen Yonggui">Chen Yonggui</a>, made a personal visit to Tangshan on August 4 to survey the damage and was photographed in the tasks of cleaning up and comforting the survivors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Leaders who opposed the return of <a href="/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping" title="Deng Xiaoping">Deng Xiaoping</a>, especially the group which became known as <a href="/wiki/Gang_of_Four" title="Gang of Four">Gang of Four</a>, filled the press with concern for the victims, but explicitly said that the nation should not be diverted by the earthquake, and that the priority was to denounce Deng instead. <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Qing" title="Jiang Qing">Jiang Qing</a> was widely quoted as saying "There were merely several hundred thousand deaths. So what? Denouncing Deng Xiaoping concerns 800 million people."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Other Gang of Four slogans said: "Be alert to Deng Xiaoping's criminal attempt to exploit earthquake phobia to suppress revolution!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison">Comparison</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Comparison">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within China's geography, the deadliest known earthquake in <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a> occurred in 1556 in <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a>, China. The <a href="/wiki/1556_Shaanxi_earthquake" title="1556 Shaanxi earthquake">1556 Shaanxi earthquake</a> is estimated to have killed 830,000 people in China, although reliable figures from this period are hard to verify.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Another earthquake is the <a href="/wiki/1920_Haiyuan_earthquake" title="1920 Haiyuan earthquake">1920 Haiyuan earthquake</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gansu_Province" title="Gansu Province">Gansu Province</a>, which killed an estimated 235,000. In 1927 another earthquake struck in the same area, this time at <a href="/wiki/Xining" title="Xining">Xining</a>; measuring 8.6 on the Richter scale, it also resulted in 200,000 deaths. Other earthquakes that have caused an extreme loss of life in the same decade include the <a href="/wiki/1923_Great_Kant%C5%8D_earthquake" title="1923 Great Kantō earthquake">1923 Great Kantō earthquake</a>, which killed 143,000 in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> in 1923.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2008_Sichuan_earthquake" title="2008 Sichuan earthquake">2008 Sichuan earthquake</a> had the same measurement on the Richter scale at 8.0 in magnitude. It, however, occurred in a mountainous region where relief efforts were noticeably hampered by the geographical makeup of the land nearby. The Sichuan earthquake also had a much quicker and more organized response system than Tangshan, as the political, social and technological environment was different. The Chinese government allowed international aid and open media access to the disaster area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Tangshan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cultural references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Personal accounts of the earthquake are related by two characters in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xinran" title="Xinran">Xinran</a>'s debut novel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Women_of_China" title="The Good Women of China">The Good Women of China</a></i> (2002), and addressed in Liu Zongren's book about the Cultural Revolution from a Beijing resident's perspective called <i>6 Tanyin Alley</i>.</p>
<p>Chinese director <a href="/wiki/Feng_Xiaogang" title="Feng Xiaogang">Feng Xiaogang</a>'s 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/Aftershock_(2010_film)" title="Aftershock (2010 film)">Aftershock</a></i> gives a dramatic account about this tragic earthquake.</p>
<p>The 2011 Malaysian television series <i><a href="/wiki/Tribulations_of_Life" title="Tribulations of Life">Tribulations of Life</a></i> wrote it into the plot for one of its episodes, to reinforce the setting of it during the 70's, and used it as a storyline where villagers showed their charitable side.</p>
<p>In Johnathan Maberry's novel, <i>The Extinction Machine,</i> released in March 2013, the 1976 Tangshan earthquake is mentioned in part of the plot.</p>
