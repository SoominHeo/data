<p><b>Sveta Trojica v Slovenskih Goricah</b> (<small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Slovene" title="Help:IPA for Slovene">[ˈsʋeːta tɾɔˈjiːtsa ʍ slɔˈʋeːnskiɣ ɡɔˈɾiːtsax]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Slovene_language" title="Slovene language">Slovene</a>: <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>Sveta Trojica v Slovenskih goricah</i></span>) is a settlement and a <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>. It became an independent municipality in 2006. Before that it was part of the Municipality of <a href="/wiki/Municipality_of_Lenart" title="Municipality of Lenart">Lenart</a>. It lies in the <a href="/wiki/Slovene_Hills" title="Slovene Hills">Slovene Hills</a> (<span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>Slovenske gorice</i></span>). Traditionally the area was part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Styria" title="Lower Styria">Lower Styria</a>. It is now included in the <a href="/wiki/Drava_Statistical_Region" title="Drava Statistical Region">Drava Statistical Region</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sveta_Trojica_v_Slovenskih_Goricah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name of the settlement was changed from <i>Sveta Trojica v Slovenskih Goricah</i> (literally, 'Holy Trinity in the Slovene Hills') to <i>Gradišče</i> (literally, 'fortified hillfort') in 1952. The name was changed on the basis of the 1948 Law on Names of Settlements and Designations of Squares, Streets, and Buildings as part of efforts by Slovenia's postwar communist government to remove religious elements from toponyms. The name was then changed from <i>Gradišče</i> to <i>Gradišče v Slovenskih Goricah</i> in 1953. The name <i>Sveta Trojica v Slovenskih Goricah</i> was restored in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Church">Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sveta_Trojica_v_Slovenskih_Goricah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The local landmark is the <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> in the settlement from which the village gets its name. It is dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Trinity" title="Trinity">Holy Trinity</a> (<a href="/wiki/Slovene_language" title="Slovene language">Slovene</a>: <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>sveta Trojica</i></span>) and belongs to the Slovenian province of the Franciscan order (OFM). It is a large <a href="/wiki/Baroque_architecture" title="Baroque architecture">Baroque</a> church with three <a href="/wiki/Bell_tower" title="Bell tower">belfries</a> that also appear in the municipality's <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>. It was built between 1636 and 1643 and expanded between 1735 and 1740.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> There is also a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franciscan" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> in the village.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
