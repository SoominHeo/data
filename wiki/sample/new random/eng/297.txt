<p><b>98 Aquarii</b> (abbreviated <b>98 Aqr</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Celestial_equator" title="Celestial equator">equatorial</a> <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aquarius_(constellation)" title="Aquarius (constellation)">Aquarius</a>. <i>98 Aquarii</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Flamsteed_designation" title="Flamsteed designation">Flamsteed designation</a> although it also bears the <a href="/wiki/Bayer_designation" title="Bayer designation">Bayer designation</a> <b>b<sup>1</sup> Aquarii</b>. It is visible to the naked eye with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_visual_magnitude" title="Apparent visual magnitude">apparent visual magnitude</a> of +3.97.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras172_667_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-mnras172_667-2">[2]</a></sup> The distance to this star, 163 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> (50 <a href="/wiki/Parsec" title="Parsec">parsecs</a>), is known from <a href="/wiki/Parallax" title="Parallax">parallax</a> measurements made with the <a href="/wiki/Hipparcos" title="Hipparcos">Hipparcos</a> spacecraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa474_2_653_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa474_2_653-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>With over double the mass of the Sun,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa439_1_227_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-aaa439_1_227-5">[5]</a></sup> this is an <a href="/wiki/Stellar_evolution" title="Stellar evolution">evolved</a> <a href="/wiki/Giant_star" title="Giant star">giant star</a> that has a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> of K0 III.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-houk1978_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-houk1978-3">[3]</a></sup> The measured <a href="/wiki/Angular_diameter" title="Angular diameter">angular diameter</a> of this star is <span class="nowrap">2.54 ± 0.13 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milliarcsecond" title="Milliarcsecond">mas</a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa431_773_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-aaa431_773-8">[8]</a></sup> At the estimated distance of 98 Aquarii,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa474_2_653_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-aaa474_2_653-1">[1]</a></sup> this yields a physical size of about 14 times the <a href="/wiki/Solar_radius" title="Solar radius">radius of the Sun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lang2006_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-lang2006-6">[6]</a></sup> The expanded outer envelope has an <a href="/wiki/Effective_temperature" title="Effective temperature">effective temperature</a> of 4,630 K,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa439_1_227_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa439_1_227-5">[5]</a></sup> giving it the orange glow of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-type_star" title="K-type star">K-type star</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csiro_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-csiro-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
