<p>On 22 August 2015, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vintage_aircraft" title="Vintage aircraft">vintage</a> <a href="/wiki/Jet_aircraft" title="Jet aircraft">jet aircraft</a> crashed during a display at the <a href="/wiki/Shoreham_Airshow" title="Shoreham Airshow">Shoreham Airshow</a> at <a href="/wiki/Shoreham_Airport" title="Shoreham Airport">Shoreham Airport</a>, England, killing 11 people and injuring 16 others. It was the deadliest air show accident in the United Kingdom since the <a href="/wiki/1952_Farnborough_Airshow_DH.110_crash" title="1952 Farnborough Airshow DH.110 crash">1952 Farnborough air show crash</a>, which killed 31 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvnewsroom_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvnewsroom-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The aircraft, a <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Hunter" title="Hawker Hunter">Hawker Hunter</a> T7, failed to complete a <a href="/wiki/Aerobatic_maneuver#loop" title="Aerobatic maneuver">loop manoeuvre</a> and crashed onto vehicles on the <a href="/wiki/A27_road" title="A27 road">A27 trunk road</a>. The pilot, Andy Hill, survived the crash, and was placed in a medically-induced coma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34035158_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34035158-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upto20_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-upto20-3">[3]</a></sup> As a result of the accident, all civilian-registered Hawker Hunter aircraft in the United Kingdom were grounded, and restrictions were put in place on civilian vintage jet aircraft displays over land, limiting them to flypasts and banning high-energy <a href="/wiki/Aerobatic_maneuver" title="Aerobatic maneuver">aerobatic manoeuvres</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft_and_crew">Aircraft and crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Aircraft and crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4000" data-file-width="6000" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg/220px-Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg/330px-Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg/440px-Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hawker_Hunter_T7_Shoreham_Airshow_2014.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The aircraft performing at <a href="/wiki/Shoreham-by-Sea" title="Shoreham-by-Sea">Shoreham</a> in 2014</div>

<p>The aircraft was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vintage_aircraft" title="Vintage aircraft">vintage</a> two-seat <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Hunter" title="Hawker Hunter">Hawker Hunter T7</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_registration" title="Aircraft registration">registration</a> G-BXFI,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manufacturer%27s_serial_number" title="Manufacturer's serial number">serial</a> 41H-670815,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup> displaying its former military <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_military_aircraft_serials" title="United Kingdom military aircraft serials">serial number</a> WV372 as part of its <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_livery" title="Aircraft livery">livery</a>. Having first flown for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> (RAF) in July 1955, it was rebuilt following a fire, returning to service in 1959 after conversion to <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Hunter_variants#Production_versions" title="Hawker Hunter variants">T7 specification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-T.2BL_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-T.2BL-7">[7]</a></sup> It had been making civilian display flights as a <a href="/wiki/Warbird" title="Warbird">warbird</a> since 1998, under a variety of owners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-T.2BL_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-T.2BL-7">[7]</a></sup> At the time of the accident, it was owned by Graham Peacock,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph-Boyle_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-Boyle-8">[8]</a></sup> and based at <a href="/wiki/North_Weald_Airfield" title="North Weald Airfield">North Weald Airfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34038989_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34038989-9">[9]</a></sup> The aircraft had flown to Shoreham from North Weald and was scheduled to return there after the display.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Andy Hill, the 51-year-old pilot, was described by colleagues as experienced, with more than 12,000 flight hours. He had worked as a captain at <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a>. He had flown <a href="/wiki/Harrier_Jump_Jet" title="Harrier Jump Jet">Harrier Jump Jets</a> and worked as an instructor for the RAF before joining the airline. As well the Hawker Hunter, he flew a <a href="/wiki/Van%27s_Aircraft_RV-8" title="Van's Aircraft RV-8">Van's Aircraft RV-8</a> and a <a href="/wiki/BAC_Jet_Provost" title="BAC Jet Provost">BAC Jet Provost</a> at airshows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bognorobs_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-bognorobs-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airshow">Airshow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Airshow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The aircraft was taking part in the first day of the two-day Shoreham Airshow, held in aid of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Forces_Association" title="Royal Air Forces Association">Royal Air Forces Association</a>. The conditions were hot and sunny, but with a crosswind up to 15 knots, described as not unusual for Shoreham by the local media. The Hunter had been opening the afternoon session of displays; the morning programme up to 12:30 <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">BST</a> (11:30 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>) had already featured <a href="/wiki/The_Blades_(aerobatic_team)" title="The Blades (aerobatic team)">The Blades</a> aerobatic team (opening), Justyn Gorman Aerobatics, an <a href="/wiki/AutoGyro_Calidus" title="AutoGyro Calidus">AutoGyro Calidus</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiger_Moth" title="Tiger Moth">Tiger 9 Aeronautical Display Team</a> (six aircraft only), a <a href="/wiki/Pitts_Special" title="Pitts Special">Pitts Special</a>, The Twister aerobatic team (one aircraft only), an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grob_Tutor" title="Grob Tutor">RAF Tutor</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/RAF_Falcons" title="RAF Falcons">RAF Falcons</a> parachute display team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FlypastTribute_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FlypastTribute-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">Accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crash">Crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Hunter commenced its display with a low pass along the runway from south to north, turning for a second pass in the opposite direction. As it neared the airport, it pulled up into an <a href="/wiki/Aerobatic_maneuver#loop" title="Aerobatic maneuver">inside loop</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Aerobatic_maneuver" title="Aerobatic maneuver">manoeuvre</a> started from a height of 200 feet (61 m),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph-Boyle_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-Boyle-8">[8]</a></sup> which David Learmount later said "left no room for misjudgement".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph-Barrett_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-Barrett-13">[13]</a></sup> Before it could complete the loop, the aircraft crashed in a nose-high attitude<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup> onto the west-bound carriageway of the <a href="/wiki/A27_road" title="A27 road">A27 road</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34027260_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34027260-14">[14]</a></sup> The aircraft broke into four parts on impact: cockpit, tail, left wing and main body, and right wing,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FlypastTribute_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-FlypastTribute-12">[12]</a></sup> destroying several cars in the process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_2015-08-22_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_2015-08-22-15">[15]</a></sup> Fuel escaping from the fuel tanks ignited in a large fireball and plume of smoke immediately following the impact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34029344_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34029344-16">[16]</a></sup> The crash occurred at 13:22 <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">BST</a> (12:22 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casualties">Casualties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Casualties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eleven people on the ground were killed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34034430_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34034430-17">[17]</a></sup> and 16 others were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_2015-08-22_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_2015-08-22-15">[15]</a></sup> Those confirmed dead included two players from <a href="/wiki/Worthing_United_F.C." title="Worthing United F.C.">Worthing United F.C.</a>, a <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system#The_system" title="English football league system">level 9</a> team in <a href="/wiki/Football_in_England" title="Football in England">English football</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTLEC_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-RTLEC-18">[18]</a></sup> Eight vehicles were destroyed in the crash, including a <a href="/wiki/Daimler_DS420" title="Daimler DS420">Daimler DS420</a> limousine which was en route to collect a bride to transport her to church for her wedding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metro240815_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Metro240815-19">[19]</a></sup> The driver of the Daimler was subsequently confirmed as one of the victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34047001_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34047001-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hill, the pilot, was thrown clear of the aircraft in his <a href="/wiki/Ejection_seat" title="Ejection seat">ejection seat</a>, which was live when the aircraft departed from North Weald.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-7"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup> He survived the crash with serious injuries. He was flown to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Sussex_County_Hospital" title="Royal Sussex County Hospital">Royal Sussex County Hospital</a> in nearby <a href="/wiki/Brighton" title="Brighton">Brighton</a>; his condition was described as critical and he was said to be fighting for his life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34029764_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34029764-21">[21]</a></sup> He was subsequently placed in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medically-induced_coma" title="Medically-induced coma">medically-induced coma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34044383_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34044383-22">[22]</a></sup> He was released from hospital in September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph11855280_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph11855280-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was feared that more bodies might be recovered from the scene,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34029764_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34029764-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-24">[24]</a></sup> but when the wreckage of the aircraft was removed on 24 August these fears proved to be unfounded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34038479_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34038479-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34117125_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34117125-26">[26]</a></sup> All the recovered components of the aircraft were taken to the <a href="/wiki/Air_Accidents_Investigation_Branch" title="Air Accidents Investigation Branch">Air Accidents Investigation Branch</a> (AAIB) facility at <a href="/wiki/Farnborough,_Hampshire" title="Farnborough, Hampshire">Farnborough, Hampshire</a>, for examination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34038479_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34038479-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Response">Response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Sea_Vixen" title="De Havilland Sea Vixen">de Havilland Sea Vixen</a> was already airborne ready to perform the next display after the Hunter; instead it flew overhead at altitude and departed to the north. With an <a href="/wiki/Avro_Vulcan" title="Avro Vulcan">Avro Vulcan</a> due half an hour later at 14:05, it was decided to let it perform a tribute flypast, after which the show was closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FlypastTribute_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-FlypastTribute-12">[12]</a></sup> All the aircraft at the airport were already grounded by the lack of fire cover and the creation of an exclusion zone around the accident site.</p>
<p>Following the crash, the A27 was closed in both directions, stranding those attending the airshow. People were initially able to leave the site only on foot, as the main access from the car parks to the A27 was closed. A <a href="/wiki/West_Sussex_Fire_and_Rescue_Service" title="West Sussex Fire and Rescue Service">West Sussex Fire and Rescue Service</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_appliance" title="Fire appliance">appliance</a> was first on scene, closely followed by the airport emergency vehicles,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bognorobs_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-bognorobs-10">[10]</a></sup> and medical personnel from the <a href="/wiki/British_Red_Cross" title="British Red Cross">British Red Cross</a>, who were providing medical cover at the airshow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The second day of the air show on 23 August was cancelled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cancellation_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cancellation-28">[28]</a></sup> The A27 reopened on 30 August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34101625_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34101625-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Adur_and_Worthing_Councils" title="Adur and Worthing Councils">Adur and Worthing Councils</a> (Shoreham Airport is within <a href="/wiki/Adur_District" title="Adur District">Adur District</a>) set up online and physical books of condolence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoC_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-BoC-30">[30]</a></sup> Together with <a href="/wiki/West_Sussex_County_Council" title="West Sussex County Council">West Sussex County Council</a> they also opened a charitable fund to support victims of the accident, to be administered by the Sussex Community Foundation, a registered charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WH-1-6921953_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-WH-1-6921953-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg/220px-People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg/330px-People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg/440px-People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:People_pay_tribute_to_those_who_lost_their_lives_in_the_Shoreham_Airshow_Crash.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Floral tributes to those who died, on <a href="/wiki/Shoreham_Tollbridge" title="Shoreham Tollbridge">Shoreham Tollbridge</a> near the site of the crash.</div>

<p>After a review, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tendring_District_Council" title="Tendring District Council">Tendring District Council</a> stated that the airshow scheduled to be held at <a href="/wiki/Clacton-on-Sea" title="Clacton-on-Sea">Clacton-on-Sea</a>, Essex, on 27–28 August would go ahead. Any advice issued by the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">Civil Aviation Authority</a> (CAA) following the accident would be acted upon. The Clacton airshow takes place over the sea, similar to the Bournemouth Airshow which took place the same weekend as the accident and ran for both days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECS_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ECS-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 August 2015, the CAA announced that certain restrictions on "vintage jet aircraft" performing over land at airshows were to be introduced until further notice, reducing displays to flypasts only. In addition all Hawker Hunters on the United Kingdom civil aircraft register were grounded until further notice,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theguardian2_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian2-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grounded_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grounded-34">[34]</a></sup> although military-registered Hunters were not affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HHAMilNotRestricted_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-HHAMilNotRestricted-35">[35]</a></sup> The restrictions are scheduled to remain in place until the investigation into the accident is completed. The CAA is to undertake a wider review of safety at airshows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metro250815_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Metro250815-36">[36]</a></sup> In January 2016, the CAA announced that it was bringing in stricter requirements for airshows in the United Kingdom. Measures put in place in the immediate aftermath of the accident would remain until the AAIB publishes its Final Report, at which point a review would be made, based on the recommendations in the Final Report.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newregs_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Newregs-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 August, it was announced that the airshow scheduled to be held at <a href="/wiki/Durham_Tees_Valley_Airport" title="Durham Tees Valley Airport">Durham Tees Valley Airport</a> on 29 August had been postponed. Organisers of the airshow stated that the new regulations in place would have "severely limited" some of the displays of the jet aircraft. The airshow would have been the first in the area since 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34070073_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34070073-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other air shows at the following weekend were not postponed but some displays were altered to match the Civil Aviation Authority restrictions, including the <a href="/wiki/Wings_and_Wheels_(Dunsfold)" title="Wings and Wheels (Dunsfold)">Wings and Wheels</a> display at <a href="/wiki/Dunsfold_Aerodrome" title="Dunsfold Aerodrome">Dunsfold Aerodrome</a> where a one-minute silent tribute to the victims was held before the start of the air display.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34096742_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34096742-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2016 Shoreham Airshow was cancelled. The organisers added that the possibility of a 2017 airshow would be explored when and if it was appropriate to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC35364481_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC35364481-40">[40]</a></sup> The 2016 <a href="/wiki/Llandudno" title="Llandudno">Llandudno</a> Airshow was cancelled, due to a lack of time to make changes in light of new regulations introduced by the CAA. The organisers intend that the 2017 airshow will go ahead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DP10844729_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-DP10844729-41">[41]</a></sup> For the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Farnborough_Airshow" title="Farnborough Airshow">Farnborough Airshow</a>, the Red Arrows performed a flypast rather than an aerobatic display, stating that the latter would not be appropriate in the aftermath of the Shoreham accident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC36546206_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC36546206-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2016, it was announced that the aircraft's owners had admitted liability for the accident in late 2015. Two claims for compensation had been settled with a third in the final stages of settlement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37121498_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37121498-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inquest">Inquest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Inquest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coroner%27s_inquest" title="Coroner's inquest">coroner's inquest</a> was opened on 2 September in <a href="/wiki/Horsham" title="Horsham">Horsham</a>, West Sussex. After the naming of all the victims and a minute's silence, the inquest was adjourned until 22 March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34117125_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34117125-26">[26]</a></sup> On 22 March, a date was set for a pre-inquest review on 19 September. It was revealed that the police had applied to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">High Court</a> for permission to obtain evidence relevant to the investigation from the AAIB. A full hearing is proposed to take place in March 2017. Any prosecution of the pilot would mean that the inquest would be further delayed, depending on the severity of offence being prosecuted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Argus14375348_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Argus14375348-45">[45]</a></sup> In September 2016, it was reported that a second pre-inquest review had been set for 21 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EGC2972451_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-EGC2972451-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>A further pre-inquest review hearing is set for 17 March 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IHD_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-IHD-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Investigations">Investigations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Investigations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AAIB">AAIB</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: AAIB">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Air_Accidents_Investigation_Branch" title="Air Accidents Investigation Branch">Air Accidents Investigation Branch</a> (AAIB), responsible for investigating civil aviation accidents and incidents in the United Kingdom, sent a team to Shoreham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deployed_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deployed-48">[48]</a></sup> As a part of the investigation, the AAIB appealed for members of the public to contact them if they had photographs or video of the accident,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34027260_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34027260-14">[14]</a></sup> and received a large number of such recordings from a variety of locations around the airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-8"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup> The aircraft was not equipped with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cockpit_voice_recorder" title="Cockpit voice recorder">cockpit voice recorder</a> or a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flight_data_recorder" title="Flight data recorder">flight data recorder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC34048989_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC34048989-49">[49]</a></sup> The AAIB published an interim report on 4 September. The report stated that "To date, no abnormal indications have been identified. Throughout the flight, the aircraft appeared to be responding to the pilot’s control inputs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IR_6-9"><a href="#cite_note-IR-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>A second special bulletin was published by the AAIB on 21 December 2015. It revealed that the aircraft was not compliant with its Permit to Fly insofar as the ejection seat cartridges installed in the aircraft had passed their expiry date. The maintenance organisation had ordered new cartridges in January 2014, but the new cartridges did not arrive until June 2015, a year and a half after ordering them, and two months before the accident. The maintainer of the aircraft stated it was acting under the privileges of its maintenance approvals. The <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">Civil Aviation Authority</a> was reported to be of the view that the maintainer did not have such privilege.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special2_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-Special2-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>An issue with compliance with a Mandatory Permit Directive (MPD) in relation to time between engine overhauls raised questions whether the aircraft's Permit to Fly was valid on the day that it was issued. The CAA stated that it was unclear whether an Alternative Means of Compliance (AMOC) was in effect at the time for the accident aircraft, which would mean the Permit to Fly was valid. According to the AAIB's second interim report, "On this basis [the CAA] could not determine if the aircraft met the requirements of its Permit to Fly from December 2014 onwards."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special2_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-Special2-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">(p10)</sup> The second special bulletin made seven safety recommendations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special2_50-2"><a href="#cite_note-Special2-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>A third update was published on 10 March 2016. The Special Bulletin covered the organisation of the airshow at Shoreham, and also the United Kingdom in general, with particular attention to risk management. Comparison was made with how airshows are organised in Australia, Canada, the United Kingdom and United States. British civil and military rules were examined separately. It was reported that at the 2014 Shoreham Airshow, G-BFXI had overflown <a href="/wiki/Lancing,_West_Sussex" title="Lancing, West Sussex">Lancing</a> with a bank angle in excess of 90°. The pilot had not been instructed to stop his display. The AAIB did not name the pilot of G-BFXI who flew the 2014 display. It was also reported that the Flying Display Director at both displays did not have prior knowledge of the display intended to be flown. The issue of low flying during air displays was investigated. A previous accident involving glider BGA 4665 at an air race in <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a> in August 2005 had resulted in a recommendation to the CAA to change a rule. This had not been done and new rules subsequently introduced had the effect of leaving that rule unchanged, despite the CAA agreeing with the recommendation to change it. It was noted that the police had no powers to prevent people from watching an airshow from outside the boundaries of the venue where it was taking place. In the case of Shoreham, neither Sussex Constabulary nor the organisers had asked for such powers. Signs had been put out stating that viewing was prohibited and that offenders may be prosecuted. Another issue looked at was how often CAA Flight Standards Officers attended airshows. Only 1.4% of airshows in 2014 and 7.1% of airshows in 2015 had been attended. Fourteen recommendations were made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special3_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-Special3-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sussex_Police">Sussex Police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Shoreham_Airshow_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sussex Police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sussex_Police" title="Sussex Police">Sussex Police</a> opened a separate investigation into the accident. The pilot was released from hospital in early September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph11855280_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph11855280-23">[23]</a></sup> He was interviewed by police in December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC35136046_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC35136046-52">[52]</a></sup> In February 2016, it was announced that the police investigation was being extended to cover an incident at an airshow in <a href="/wiki/Southport" title="Southport">Southport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merseyside" title="Merseyside">Merseyside</a> in August 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC35473857_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC35473857-53">[53]</a></sup> That incident involved a <a href="/wiki/BAC_Jet_Provost" title="BAC Jet Provost">BAC Jet Provost</a> aircraft which had descended too low and got too close to the crowd line whilst being displayed by Hill. He was ordered to cease flying the display by the airshow's Flying Display Director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITV020216_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITV020216-54">[54]</a></sup> A CAA Flight Standards Officer did not attend that airshow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special3_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-Special3-51">[51]</a></sup> In July 2016, it was revealed that Hill could face charges of endangerment under Article 138 of the <a href="/wiki/Air_Navigation_and_Transport_Act" title="Air Navigation and Transport Act">Air Navigation Order 2009</a> and also of <a href="/wiki/Manslaughter" title="Manslaughter">manslaughter by gross negligence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC36743282_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC36743282-55">[55]</a></sup> Sussex Constabulary applied to the <a href="/wiki/High_Court_of_Justice" title="High Court of Justice">High Court</a> for a disclosure of records order in respect of certain evidence gathered by the AAIB, which they wanted to be released to them. They wanted copies of interviews between the AAIB and Hill, the results of tests carried out by the AAIB and video evidence filmed from inside the aircraft during its display. At the High Court on 28 September 2016, an order was made that the onboard video evidence would be released, but not copies of the interview or the results of tests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37495884_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37495884-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
