<p><b>James Gregory</b> "<b>Greg</b>" <b>Vaughan, Jr.</b> (born June 15, 1973) is an American actor and former fashion model, known for his roles in the soap operas <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i> (2002–03), <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i> (2003–09), and <i><a href="/wiki/Days_of_Our_Lives" title="Days of Our Lives">Days of Our Lives</a></i> (2012–2016). Vaughan also starred as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dan_Gordon_(Charmed)" title="Dan Gordon (Charmed)">Dan Gordon</a> on the second season of the supernatural series <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i> (1999-2000). In 2016, Vaughan began starring in the <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey_Network_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="Oprah Winfrey Network (U.S. TV channel)">Oprah Winfrey Network</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Sugar" title="Queen Sugar">Queen Sugar</a></i> as Detective Calvin.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Vaughan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The actor and former male fashion model has starred in soap operas such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i>. To date, his best-known role is that of <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Spencer" title="Lucky Spencer">Lucky Spencer</a> in the latter, which he played from 2003 to 2009.</p>
<p>At age 16, while getting a haircut, he learned of a "Back to School" modeling competition, and sent in some snapshots. He was selected as a finalist but did not win. However, the winner encouraged Vaughan to pursue modeling, and Vaughan signed up with a modeling agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soap_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-soap-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shortly after graduating from Mesquite High School, he was invited to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milan,_Italy" title="Milan, Italy">Milan, Italy</a>, where he modeled for designer <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Armani" title="Giorgio Armani">Giorgio Armani</a> for two and a half months. More campaigns soon followed, with designers such as <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Versace" title="Gianni Versace">Gianni Versace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hilfiger" title="Tommy Hilfiger">Tommy Hilfiger</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banana_Republic_(clothing_retailer)" title="Banana Republic (clothing retailer)">Banana Republic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Lauren" title="Ralph Lauren">Ralph Lauren</a>.</p>
<p>While modeling in Miami, Vaughan started to become dissatisfied with the modeling life, and decided to relocate to pursue his dream of becoming an actor, he then moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. His first role was on <i><a href="/wiki/Baywatch" title="Baywatch">Baywatch</a></i>, as well as on the pilot for a 1996 <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Spelling" title="Aaron Spelling">Aaron Spelling</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Malibu_Shores" title="Malibu Shores">Malibu Shores</a></i>, that lead to his future appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_90210" title="Beverly Hills, 90210">Beverly Hills, 90210</a></i> and was a cast regular for the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soap_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-soap-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>His next major role was from 2002 to 2003 as <a href="/wiki/Diego_Guittierez" title="Diego Guittierez">Diego Guittierez</a> in the daytime drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i>. When the character was written off the show, Vaughan got a call from the producers of <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i>, who were seeking a new actor for the popular character of <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Spencer" title="Lucky Spencer">Lucky Spencer</a>, son of the famous soap opera couple <a href="/wiki/Luke_and_Laura" title="Luke and Laura">Luke and Laura</a>. Vaughan was the first actor to portray Lucky as a full-fledged adult, with the part having been played by <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Jackson_(actor)" title="Jonathan Jackson (actor)">Jonathan Jackson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Young" title="Jacob Young">Jacob Young</a>.</p>
<p>On June 7, 2006, Vaughan won the role of the new spokesperson for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Can%27t_Believe_It%27s_Not_Butter" title="I Can't Believe It's Not Butter">I Can't Believe It's Not Butter</a> in a ceremony in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. The contest was created to replace <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Lanzoni" title="Fabio Lanzoni">Fabio</a>.</p>
<p>In 2007, Vaughan appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tyra_Banks_Show" title="The Tyra Banks Show">The Tyra Banks Show</a></i> to help <a href="/wiki/Tyra_Banks" title="Tyra Banks">Tyra Banks</a> through "soap opera school."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2007sow_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-2007sow-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 28, 2009, Vaughan announced that he would exit the role of <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Spencer" title="Lucky Spencer">Lucky Spencer</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i>; he last aired on October 20, with original portrayer <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Jackson_(actor)" title="Jonathan Jackson (actor)">Jonathan Jackson</a> re-assuming the role on October 27.</p>
<p>In the July 16, 2012 edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Soap_Opera_Digest" title="Soap Opera Digest">Soap Opera Digest</a></i>, it was announced that Vaughan would join the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Days_of_Our_Lives" title="Days of Our Lives">Days of Our Lives</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Eric_Brady" title="Eric Brady">Eric Brady</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> who was ordained as a priest before returning to Salem. He first aired on November 13.</p>
<p>In July 2013, Vaughan appeared in "Second Chances", a Hallmark Original Movie, alongside <i>Days</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Alison_Sweeney" title="Alison Sweeney">Alison Sweeney</a>. The movie was filmed before Sweeney and Vaughn became siblings on <i>Days of Our Lives</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Vaughan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vaughan was born in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> to James Gregory Vaughan, Sr. and Barbara Ann Alt, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Mesquite,_Texas" title="Mesquite, Texas">Mesquite, Texas</a>. His grandparents were also a big part of his life, and lived in the small town of <a href="/wiki/Fredericksburg,_Texas" title="Fredericksburg, Texas">Fredericksburg, Texas</a>, just outside <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio</a>. He spent summers in the country and worked at his grandparents' restaurant <a href="/wiki/Busboy" title="Busboy">bussing</a> tables.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Vaughan married Dutch model and actress <a href="/wiki/Touriya_Haoud" title="Touriya Haoud">Touriya Haoud</a> on June 4, 2006. The couple have three sons: Jathan James (born May 4, 2007), Cavan Thomas (born January 19, 2010), and Landan Reid (born March 6, 2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 14, 2014, Vaughan and Haoud announced that they will be splitting up after being married for 8 years, and later divorced.</p>
