<p><b>Zhili</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wade-Giles" title="Wade-Giles">formerly</a> <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Chinese" title="Romanization of Chinese">romanized</a> as <b>Chihli</b>, was a northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_China" title="Province of China">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">China</a> from the 14th-century <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> until the province was dissolved in 1928 during the <a href="/wiki/Warlord_Era" title="Warlord Era">Warlord Era</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zhili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Zhili</i> means "directly ruled" and indicates regions directly ruled by the imperial government of China. Zhili province was first constituted during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> when the capital of China was located at <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a> along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Yangtze River</a>. In 1403, the Ming <a href="/wiki/Yongle_Emperor" title="Yongle Emperor">Yongle Emperor</a> relocated the capital to Beiping, which was subsequently renamed <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peking_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-peking-1">[1]</a></sup> The region known as <span class="nowrap"><b>North Zhili</b></span> was composed of parts of the modern provinces of <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a>, including the provincial-level municipalities of Beijing and <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a>. There was another region located around the "reserve capital" Nanjing known as <span class="nowrap"><b>South Zhili</b></span> that included parts of what are today the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anhui" title="Anhui">Anhui</a>, including the provincial-level municipality of <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a>, Nanjing lost its status of the "second capital" and Southern Zhili was reconstituted as a regular province, <a href="/wiki/Jiangnan" title="Jiangnan">Jiangnan</a>, while Northern Zhili was renamed Zhili Province. In the 18th century the borders of Zhili province were redrawn and spread over what is today <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a> and the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a>, Western <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>, Northern <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia_Autonomous_Region" title="Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region">Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1928 the government of the Republic of China assigned portions of northern Zhili province to its neighbors in the north and renamed the remainder Hebei Province.</p>
