<p>The <b>1932 Winter Olympics</b>, officially known as the <b>III Olympic Winter Games</b>, were a winter <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Placid,_New_York" title="Lake Placid, New York">Lake Placid</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. The games opened on February 4 and closed on February 15. It was the first of four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympics</a> held in the United States; Lake Placid hosted again in <a href="/wiki/1980_Winter_Olympics" title="1980 Winter Olympics">1980</a>.</p>
<p>The games were originally to take place in <a href="/wiki/Wrightwood,_California" title="Wrightwood, California">Wrightwood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Pines,_California" title="Big Pines, California">Big Pines</a>, California. The largest ski jump in the world was constructed in Big Pines for the event. But poor snow conditions caused Olympic officials to move the event to Lake Placid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OCR1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-OCR1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Highlights">Highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The Games were opened by <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_New_York" title="Governor of New York">Governor of New York</a>. He was elected <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> nine months later.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Irving_Jaffee" title="Irving Jaffee">Irving Jaffee</a> won the 5,000 m (3.1 mi) and the 10,000 m (6.2 mi) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Speedskating" title="Speedskating">speedskating</a> gold medals, beating previous champion and world record holder <a href="/wiki/Ivar_Ballangrud" title="Ivar Ballangrud">Ivar Ballangrud</a> in the 10,000 m by 4.5 m (15 ft), mainly because the favourites were unaccustomed to the common start.</li>
<li>The USA won the medal tally with a total of 12 medals (6 gold, 4 silver, and 2 bronze).</li>
<li>Seventeen countries participated.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demonstration_sports">Demonstration sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demonstration sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Games also included events in three <a href="/wiki/Demonstration_sport" title="Demonstration sport">demonstration sports</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Curling_at_the_1932_Winter_Olympics" title="Curling at the 1932 Winter Olympics">Curling</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sled_dog_race_at_the_1932_Winter_Olympics" title="Sled dog race at the 1932 Winter Olympics">Sled dog race</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Speed_skating_at_the_1932_Winter_Olympics" title="Speed skating at the 1932 Winter Olympics">Speed skating</a> (women)</li>
</ul>
<p>These were the last Winter Olympics without <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Alpine skiing at the Winter Olympics">alpine skiing</a>, which was added in <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing_at_the_1936_Winter_Olympics" title="Alpine skiing at the 1936 Winter Olympics">1936</a>. Alpine skiing held its <a href="/wiki/FIS_Alpine_World_Ski_Championships_1932" title="FIS Alpine World Ski Championships 1932">1932 World Championships</a> during the Olympics, February 4–6 in <a href="/wiki/Cortina_d%27Ampezzo" title="Cortina d'Ampezzo">Cortina d'Ampezzo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</p>
