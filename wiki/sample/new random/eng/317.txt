<p><b>The Principal York</b> is an historic Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> adjacent to <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York railway station</a>, England. It is a five-storey building of yellow Scarborough brick and was completed in 1878, a year after the present station opened.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1853_Hotel">The 1853 Hotel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Principal_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The 1853 Hotel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first York station hotel (The York Royal Station Hotel) was opened on 22 February 1853, the architect was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G._T._Andrews" title="G. T. Andrews">G. T. Andrews</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fawcett_1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fawcett_1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Denby_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Denby-3">[3]</a></sup> as an addition to <a href="/wiki/York_old_railway_station" title="York old railway station">York old railway station</a>. The hotel became redundant after the opening of the new station in June 1877 and was converted into offices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fawcett_1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fawcett_1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="center">
<div class="floatnone"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:National_Railway_Museum_item_1998-10584_High_Sheriff%27s_Dinner_Menu_Royal_Station_Hotel_York_8_March_1858.jpg" title="A menu from 1858 held by the National Railway Museum, York."><img alt="A menu from 1858 held by the National Railway Museum, York." data-file-height="1794" data-file-width="1280" height="561" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/National_Railway_Museum_item_1998-10584_High_Sheriff%27s_Dinner_Menu_Royal_Station_Hotel_York_8_March_1858.jpg/400px-National_Railway_Museum_item_1998-10584_High_Sheriff%27s_Dinner_Menu_Royal_Station_Hotel_York_8_March_1858.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/National_Railway_Museum_item_1998-10584_High_Sheriff%27s_Dinner_Menu_Royal_Station_Hotel_York_8_March_1858.jpg/600px-National_Railway_Museum_item_1998-10584_High_Sheriff%27s_Dinner_Menu_Royal_Station_Hotel_York_8_March_1858.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/National_Railway_Museum_item_1998-10584_High_Sheriff%27s_Dinner_Menu_Royal_Station_Hotel_York_8_March_1858.jpg/800px-National_Railway_Museum_item_1998-10584_High_Sheriff%27s_Dinner_Menu_Royal_Station_Hotel_York_8_March_1858.jpg 2x" width="400"/></a></div>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1878_Hotel">The 1878 Hotel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Principal_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The 1878 Hotel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The new hotel opened on 20 May 1878 as the Royal Station Hotel, York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fawcett_1_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Fawcett_1-2">[2]</a></sup> The architect was William Peachey of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Railway_(UK)" title="North Eastern Railway (UK)">North Eastern Railway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Denby_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Denby-3">[3]</a></sup> The hotel was designed as an integral part of the new station and the North Eastern Railway Company's flagship hotel, and as such was managed directly by the railway company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fawcett_2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fawcett_2-4">[4]</a></sup> It featured elegant, high-ceilinged banqueting rooms and 100 large bedrooms costing 14 shillings a night.</p>
<p>A 27-room west wing was added in 1896, named Klondyke for the <a href="/wiki/Klondike_Gold_Rush" title="Klondike Gold Rush">Klondike Gold Rush</a> of the time.</p>
<p>In 1923, ownership and management of the hotel transferred to the <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London and North Eastern Railway">London and North Eastern Railway</a> (LNER).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Transport_Hotels">British Transport Hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Principal_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: British Transport Hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="704" data-file-width="1024" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg/220px-York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg/330px-York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg/440px-York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:York_-_Station_Road_and_Royal_Station_Hotel_geograph-2183699-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The station and hotel in 1955</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1948, Britain's railways were nationalised and with them York's railway hotel. Initially this was as part of the 'Hotels Executive' of the British Transport Commission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/British_Transport_Hotels" title="British Transport Hotels">British Transport Hotels</a> brand came about in 1953.</p>
<p>In 1981, an annexe was opened. It uses the building of the erstwhile North Eastern Railway catering department. it is called the <i>Friars Garden Hotel</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This was the last investment under nationalised ownership.</p>
<p>A typical turnover at this time was 1981's £1,225,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This source stated that there were then 135 bedrooms and 23 in Friar's Garden. At this time 24 rooms were not en-suite. The Ebor Restaurant had 52 covers. In 2013, this was The Tempus Restaurant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Privatisation">Privatisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Principal_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Privatisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under Mrs Thatcher's government ancillary activities of the railways were privatised and this included <a href="/wiki/British_Transport_Hotels" title="British Transport Hotels">British Transport Hotels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The sale was held by public tender. The sale inventory dated October 1982 is held by the <a href="/wiki/National_Railway_Museum" title="National Railway Museum">National Railway Museum</a> at <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first purchaser of the hotel in 1983 was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Batchshire_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Batchshire Limited (page does not exist)">Batchshire Limited</a> (later <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Distinguished_Hotels_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Distinguished Hotels Limited (page does not exist)">Distinguished Hotels Limited</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seaco_Hotels_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seaco Hotels Limited (page does not exist)">Seaco Hotels Limited</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Venice_Simplon_Orient_Express_Hotels_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Venice Simplon Orient Express Hotels Limited (page does not exist)">Venice Simplon Orient Express Hotels Limited</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Orient_Hotels_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Orient Hotels Limited (page does not exist)">Orient Hotels Limited</a>/<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Orient_Hotels_(U.K.)_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Orient Hotels (U.K.) Limited (page does not exist)">Orient Hotels (U.K.) Limited</a> before becoming <a href="/wiki/Belmond_(company)" title="Belmond (company)">Belmond Limited</a>), a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Sea_Containers" title="Sea Containers">Sea Containers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The owners in 2013 are <a href="/wiki/Principal_Hotel_Company" title="Principal Hotel Company">Principal Hotel Company</a>.</p>
<p>Completed in 2016, The Principal York hotel underwent a refurbishment of the public areas, 164 rooms and suites and corridors. Award-winning luxury design studio Goddard Littlefair were asked to reinstate the hotel’s essential character to help position it as a great destination to spend time in, as well as being a great place for visitors to stay in.</p>
<p>The new scheme is designed to breathe life back into every corner of the building, giving a nod to tradition, whilst creating a light, welcoming and contemporary environment in the form of a modern and softer country house interior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
