<p><b>The Old Man of the South Pole</b> (in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">南极老人, 南极仙翁</span> or <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南極老人</span>) is the <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoist</a> <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/deify" title="wikt:deify">deification</a> of <a href="/wiki/Canopus" title="Canopus">Canopus</a>, the brightest star of the constellation <a href="/wiki/Carina_(constellation)" title="Carina (constellation)">Carina</a>.</p>
<p>It is the symbol of happiness and <a href="/wiki/Longevity" title="Longevity">longevity</a> in <a href="/wiki/Far_East" title="Far East">Far Eastern</a> culture.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Man_of_the_South_Pole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Old Man of the South Pole is often depicted in the Chinese pictures as an old man with a long white beard, with a deer by his side. This style of picture is related to the story of an emperor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty#Northern_Song.2C_960.E2.80.931127" title="Song Dynasty">Northern Song</a> Dynasty who had invited such an old man from the street and later considered the old man as the sign of his longevity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Canopus is usually called in Chinese the Star of the Old Man (in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">老人星</span>) or the Star of the Old Man of the South Pole (in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">南极老人星</span>). Since Carina is a Southern constellation, Canopus is rarely seen in <a href="/wiki/North_China" title="North China">Northern China</a> and, if seen in good weather, looks reddish lying near the southern horizon. Because the <a href="/wiki/Red#In_different_cultures_and_traditions" title="Red">color red</a> is the symbol of happiness and longevity in China, Canopus is also known in China and its neighboring countries of Korea, Japan and Vietnam as the Star of Old Age (in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">寿星</span>) or the Star of the Man of the Old Age (in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">寿老人</span>). In Japan, it became <a href="/wiki/Jur%C5%8Djin" title="Jurōjin">Jurōjin</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">寿老人</span>), one of the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Lucky_Gods" title="Seven Lucky Gods">Seven Gods of Fortune</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">七福神</span>).</p>
