<p><b>Longneck eels</b> or <b>neck eels</b> are a family, <b>Derichthyidae</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Eel" title="Eel">eels</a>. They are <a href="/wiki/Pelagic_fish" title="Pelagic fish">pelagic fishes</a>, found in the middle and depths of most oceans. The name comes from <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>deres</i> meaning "neck" and <i>ichthys</i> meaning "fish".</p>
<p>They are distinguished by the presence of a series stripes on the head that form part of the sensory system, but otherwise the two genera of the family are quite different in appearance. <i>Derichthys</i> has a narrow neck and large eyes, while <i>Nessorhamphus'</i> has a long, flattened, snout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EoF_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EoF-1">[1]</a></sup> They grow to about 60 cm (24 in) in length.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Species">Species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longneck_eel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The three species in two genera are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FB_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FB-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Genus <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Derichthys" title="Derichthys">Derichthys</a></i> (one species)</li>
<li>Genus <i><a href="/wiki/Nessorhamphus" title="Nessorhamphus">Nessorhamphus</a></i> (two species)</li>
</ul>
<p>In some classifications (for example, <i>Systema Naturae</i> 2000), this family is split in two, with Derichthyidae containing only the genus <i>Derichthys</i>, and <i>Nessorhamphus</i> being assigned its own family, Nessorhamphidae.</p>
