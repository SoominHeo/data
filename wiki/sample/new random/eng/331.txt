<p><br/>
<b>The Vegan Society</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Registered_charity" title="Registered charity">registered charity</a> and the oldest vegan society in the world, founded in November 1944, in the UK by <a href="/wiki/Donald_Watson" title="Donald Watson">Donald Watson</a>, Elsie "Sally" Shrigley, and 23 others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Watson_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Watson-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Watson coined the word "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vegan" title="Vegan">vegan</a>" to stand for "non-dairy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vegetarian" title="Vegetarian">vegetarians</a>" who also ate no eggs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Watson_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Watson-3">[3]</a></sup> The founding of the Society is celebrated annually on <a href="/wiki/World_Vegan_Day" title="World Vegan Day">World Vegan Day</a>, which was established in 1994 by <a href="/wiki/Louise_Wallis" title="Louise Wallis">Louise Wallis</a> the President and Chair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Society now defines veganism as "...a way of living that seeks to exclude, as far as possible and practicable, all forms of exploitation of, and cruelty to, animals for food, clothing and any other purpose."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
