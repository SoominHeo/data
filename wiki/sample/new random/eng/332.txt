<p><b>Acerra</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[aˈtʃɛrra]</a></span> is a town and <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>, southern Italy, in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_City_of_Naples" title="Metropolitan City of Naples">Metropolitan City of Naples</a>, about 15 kilometres (9 miles) northeast of the capital in <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>. It is part of the Agro Acerrano plain.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acerra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Acerra is one of the most ancient cities of the region, probably founded by the <a href="/wiki/Osci" title="Osci">Osci</a> with the name of <i><b>Akeru</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>: <i><b>Acerrae</b></i>).</p>
<p>Acerra was destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Hannibal" title="Hannibal">Hannibal</a> in 216 BC, but was restored in 210 BC.</p>
<p>Acerra served as a Roman base during the <a href="/wiki/Social_War_(91%E2%80%9388_BC)" title="Social War (91–88 BC)">Social War</a> in 90 BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 826 the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a> built here a castle, later destroyed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bono_of_Naples&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bono of Naples (page does not exist)">Bono of Naples</a>. In 881 it was sacked by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saracens" title="Saracens">Saracens</a>. Later it was a <a href="/wiki/Italo-Norman" title="Italo-Norman">Norman</a> possession, the seat of a county. As part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a>, it was a fief of the Aquino, the Origlia, the Orsini del Balzo and, from 1496 until 1812, the Cardenas. From 1927 it was part of the province of <a href="/wiki/Terra_di_Lavoro" title="Terra di Lavoro">Terra di Lavoro</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acerra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Acerra_Cathedral" title="Acerra Cathedral">Acerra Cathedral</a>, originally built over an ancient temple of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hercules_(mythology)" title="Hercules (mythology)">Hercules</a> and remade in the 19th century. It houses some Baroque canvasses from the 17th century. Annexed is the Bishop's Palace.</li>
<li>Church of <i>Corpus Domini</i> (16th century).</li>
<li>Church of <i>Annunziata</i> (15th century), with a 12th-century crucifix and a 15th-century <i>Annunciation</i> attributed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dello_Delli" title="Dello Delli">Dello Delli</a>.</li>
<li>Church of <i>San Pietro</i> (16th-17th centuries)</li>
<li>Baronal Castle.</li>
<li>Archaeological area of <a href="/wiki/Suessula" title="Suessula">Suessula</a>. Location 40°59'23.47"N 14°23'53.41"E</li>
</ul>
