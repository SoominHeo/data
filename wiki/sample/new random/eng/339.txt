<p><b>Yojiro Takahagi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">高萩 洋次郎</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takahagi Yōjiro</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 2 August 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Iwaki,_Fukushima" title="Iwaki, Fukushima">Iwaki, Fukushima</a>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Seoul" title="FC Seoul">FC Seoul</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yojiro_Takahagi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sanfrecce_Hiroshima">Sanfrecce Hiroshima</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yojiro_Takahagi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sanfrecce Hiroshima">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Takahagi started his professional career at <a href="/wiki/Sanfrecce_Hiroshima" title="Sanfrecce Hiroshima">Sanfrecce Hiroshima</a>, a club he previously played with at youth levels. In 2006, Takahagi played a full season on loan with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ehime_F.C." title="Ehime F.C.">Ehime F.C.</a>, before returning to Sanfrecce Hiroshima in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_Sydney_Wanderers">Western Sydney Wanderers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yojiro_Takahagi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Western Sydney Wanderers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 January 2015, Takahagi signed with <a href="/wiki/Western_Sydney_Wanderers_FC" title="Western Sydney Wanderers FC">Western Sydney Wanderers</a> for their <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Champions_League" title="2015 AFC Champions League">2015 AFC Champions League</a> campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was released by the Wanderers on 6 June 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FC_Seoul">FC Seoul</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yojiro_Takahagi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: FC Seoul">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 June 2015, Takahagi joined South Korean side <a href="/wiki/FC_Seoul" title="FC Seoul">FC Seoul</a> in <a href="/wiki/K_League_Classic" title="K League Classic">K League Classic</a>.</p>
