<p><b>Durrës</b> (<small>Albanian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Albanian" title="Help:IPA for Albanian">[ˈdurəs]</a></span>), historically also known as <b>Durazzo</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[duˈrattso]</a></span>) and <b>Dyrrachium</b>, is the second largest city and a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Albania" title="Municipalities of Albania">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, and one of the world's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_cities_by_time_of_continuous_habitation" title="List of cities by time of continuous habitation">oldest continuously inhabited cities</a>. Geographically, it is central on the coast, about 33 km (21 mi) west of the capital <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a>.</p>
<p>It is one of the most ancient and economically significant cities of Albania. Durrës is home to Albania's main port, the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Port of Durrës">Port of Durrës</a>. The city is at one of the narrower points of the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a>, opposite the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> ports of <a href="/wiki/Bari" title="Bari">Bari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brindisi" title="Brindisi">Brindisi</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Port of Durrës">port</a> is also the 10th largest cargo port on the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a> that handle more than 3.4 million tonnes of cargo per year.</p>
<p>Many roads and railways pass through Durrës and connect the northern part of the country to the south and the west with the east. Moreover, it is the starting point of <a href="/wiki/Pan-European_Corridor_VIII" title="Pan-European Corridor VIII">Pan-European Corridor VIII</a>, national roads <a href="/wiki/National_Road_2_(Albania)" title="National Road 2 (Albania)">SH2</a> and SH4, and serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Railway_Station" title="Durrës Railway Station">main railway station</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albanian_Railways" title="Albanian Railways">HSH</a>.</p>
<p>The city of Durrës is home of one of the largest amphitheatres in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Roman_amphitheatres" title="List of Roman amphitheatres">Balkan peninsula</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Amphitheatre" title="Durrës Amphitheatre">Durrës Amphitheatre</a> once having a capacity of 20,000 people.</p>
<p>Durrës is one of largest cities on the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a> and ranks 5th with a population of 175,110. The total population is 175,110 (2011 census), in a total area of 338.30 km<sup>2</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The population of the former municipality at the 2011 census was 113,249.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census11_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-census11-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_County" title="Durrës County">metropolitan area</a> has a population of 265,330.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Albanian_2011_census_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Albanian_2011_census-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient">Ancient</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Illyrians" title="Illyrians">Illyrians</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_Albania" title="History of Albania">History of Albania</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mosaic_of_The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="508" data-file-width="726" height="168" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a7/Mosaic_of_The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs.jpg/240px-Mosaic_of_The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a7/Mosaic_of_The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs.jpg/360px-Mosaic_of_The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a7/Mosaic_of_The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs.jpg/480px-Mosaic_of_The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>

<p>Though surviving remains are minimal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> as one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_cities_by_time_of_continuous_habitation" title="List of cities by time of continuous habitation">oldest cities</a> in Albania, the city was founded as <i><a href="/wiki/Epidamnos" title="Epidamnos">Epidamnos</a></i> in the ancient region of <a href="/wiki/Illyria" title="Illyria">Illyria</a> in 627 BC by <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">ancient Greek</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> colonists from <a href="/wiki/Corinth" title="Corinth">Corinth</a> and Corcyra, modern-day <a href="/wiki/Corfu" title="Corfu">Corfu</a>. The Romans replaced the rule of Teuta with that of <a href="/wiki/Demetrius_of_Pharos" title="Demetrius of Pharos">Demetrius of Pharos</a>, one of her generals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He lost his kingdom, including Epidamnus, to the Romans in 219 BC at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Illyrian_War" title="Second Illyrian War">Second Illyrian War</a>. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Illyrian_War" title="Third Illyrian War">Third Illyrian War</a> Epidamnus was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Gentius" title="Gentius">Gentius</a> but he was defeated by the Romans<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> at the same year.</p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Catullus" title="Catullus">Catullus</a>, the city was <i>Durrachium Hadriae tabernam</i>, "the <i><a href="/wiki/Taberna" title="Taberna">taberna</a></i> of the Adriatic", one of the stopping places for a Roman traveling up the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adriatic" title="Adriatic">Adriatic</a>, as Catullus had done himself in the sailing season of 56.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Amphitheatre_of_durres_albania_2016.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3744" data-file-width="5616" height="160" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Amphitheatre_of_durres_albania_2016.jpg/240px-Amphitheatre_of_durres_albania_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Amphitheatre_of_durres_albania_2016.jpg/360px-Amphitheatre_of_durres_albania_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Amphitheatre_of_durres_albania_2016.jpg/480px-Amphitheatre_of_durres_albania_2016.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>

<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Illyrian_Wars" title="Illyrian Wars">Illyrian Wars</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman Republic</a> in 229 BC ended in a decisive defeat for the Illyrians, the city passed to Roman rule, under which it was developed as a major military and naval base. The Romans renamed it <i>Dyrrachium</i> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: Δυρράχιον / <i>Dyrrhachion</i>). They considered the name <i>Epidamnos</i> to be inauspicious because of its wholly coincidental similarities with the Latin word <i>damnum</i>, meaning "loss" or "harm". The meaning of <i>Dyrrachium</i> ("bad spine" or "difficult ridge" in Greek) is unclear, but it has been suggested that it refers to the imposing cliffs near the city.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:07Durres01.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/07Durres01.jpg/240px-07Durres01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/07Durres01.jpg/360px-07Durres01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/07Durres01.jpg/480px-07Durres01.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>

<a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Castle" title="Durrës Castle">Durrës Ancient City Wall Entrance</a></div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>'s rival <a href="/wiki/Pompey" title="Pompey">Pompey</a> made a stand there in 48 BC before fleeing south to <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. Under Roman rule, Dyrrachium prospered; it became the western end of the <i><a href="/wiki/Via_Egnatia" title="Via Egnatia">Via Egnatia</a></i>, the great <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_road" title="Roman road">Roman road</a> that led to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thessalonica" title="Thessalonica">Thessalonica</a> and on to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>. Another lesser road led south to the city of <i>Buthrotum</i>, the modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Butrint" title="Butrint">Butrint</a>. The Roman emperor <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Caesar Augustus</a> made the city a colony for veterans of his <a href="/wiki/Roman_legion" title="Roman legion">legions</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Actium" title="Battle of Actium">Battle of Actium</a>, proclaiming it a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civitas_libera" title="Civitas libera">civitas libera</a></i> (free town).</p>
<p>In the 4th century, Dyrrachium was made the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_province" title="Roman province">Roman province</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epirus_nova" title="Epirus nova">Epirus nova</a>. It was the birthplace of the emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anastasius_I_(emperor)" title="Anastasius I (emperor)">Anastasius I</a> in c. 430. Some time later that century, Dyrrachium was struck by a powerful earthquake which destroyed the city's defences. Anastasius I rebuilt and strengthened the city walls, thus creating the strongest fortifications in the western Balkans. The 12-metre-high (39-foot) walls were so thick that, according to the Byzantine historian <a href="/wiki/Anna_Komnene" title="Anna Komnene">Anna Komnene</a>, four horsemen could ride abreast on them. Significant portions of the ancient city defences still remain, although they have been much reduced over the centuries.</p>
<p>Like much of the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>, Dyrrachium and the surrounding <i>Dyrraciensis provinciae</i> suffered considerably from <a href="/wiki/Barbarian" title="Barbarian">barbarian</a> incursions during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Migrations_Period" title="Migrations Period">Migrations Period</a>. It was besieged in 481 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theodoric_the_Great" title="Theodoric the Great">Theodoric the Great</a>, king of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ostrogoth" title="Ostrogoth">Ostrogoths</a>, and in subsequent centuries had to fend off frequent attacks by the <a href="/wiki/First_Bulgarian_Empire" title="First Bulgarian Empire">Bulgarians</a>. Unaffected by the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Western_Roman_Empire" title="Fall of the Western Roman Empire">fall of the Western Roman Empire</a>, the city continued under the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> as an important port and a major link between the Empire and western Europe.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages">Middle Ages</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Durr%C3%ABs,_Pinargenti.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="442" data-file-width="599" height="184" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Durr%C3%ABs%2C_Pinargenti.jpg/250px-Durr%C3%ABs%2C_Pinargenti.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Durr%C3%ABs%2C_Pinargenti.jpg/375px-Durr%C3%ABs%2C_Pinargenti.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Durr%C3%ABs%2C_Pinargenti.jpg/500px-Durr%C3%ABs%2C_Pinargenti.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Durr%C3%ABs,_Pinargenti.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Durrës in 1573</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city and the surrounding coast became a Byzantine province, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theme_of_Dyrrhachium" title="Theme of Dyrrhachium">Theme of Dyrrhachium</a>, probably in the first decade of the 9th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668-10">[10]</a></sup> The city remained in Byzantine hands until the late 10th century, when <a href="/wiki/Samuel_of_Bulgaria" title="Samuel of Bulgaria">Samuel of Bulgaria</a> gained control of the city, possibly through his marriage with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agatha,_wife_of_Samuel_of_Bulgaria" title="Agatha, wife of Samuel of Bulgaria">Agatha</a>, daughter of the local magnate <a href="/wiki/John_Chryselios" title="John Chryselios">John Chryselios</a>. Samuel made his son-in-law <a href="/wiki/Ashot_Taronites" title="Ashot Taronites">Ashot Taronites</a>, a Byzantine captive who had married his daughter <a href="/wiki/Miroslava_of_Bulgaria" title="Miroslava of Bulgaria">Miroslava</a>, governor of the city. In circa 1005, however, Ashot and Miroslava, with the connivance of Chryselios, fled to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, where they notified Emperor <a href="/wiki/Basil_II" title="Basil II">Basil II</a> of their intention to surrender the city to him. Soon, a Byzantine squadron appeared off the city under <a href="/wiki/Eustathios_Daphnomeles" title="Eustathios Daphnomeles">Eustathios Daphnomeles</a>, and the city returned to Byzantine rule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStephenson200317.E2.80.9318.2C_34.E2.80.9335_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStephenson200317.E2.80.9318.2C_34.E2.80.9335-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHolmes2005103.E2.80.93104.2C_497.E2.80.93498_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHolmes2005103.E2.80.93104.2C_497.E2.80.93498-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 11th–12th centuries, the city was important as a military stronghold and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_see" title="Metropolitan see">metropolitan see</a> rather than as a major economic center, and never recovered its late antique prosperity; <a href="/wiki/Anna_Komnene" title="Anna Komnene">Anna Komnene</a> makes clear that medieval Dyrrhachium occupied only a portion of the ancient city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668-10">[10]</a></sup> In the 1070s, two of its governors, <a href="/wiki/Nikephoros_Bryennios_the_Elder" title="Nikephoros Bryennios the Elder">Nikephoros Bryennios the Elder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nikephoros_Basilakes" title="Nikephoros Basilakes">Nikephoros Basilakes</a>, led unsuccessful rebellions trying to seize the Byzantine throne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668-10">[10]</a></sup> Dyrrachium was lost in February 1082 when <a href="/wiki/Alexios_I_Komnenos" title="Alexios I Komnenos">Alexios I Komnenos</a> was defeated by the <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Normans</a> under <a href="/wiki/Robert_Guiscard" title="Robert Guiscard">Robert Guiscard</a> and his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bohemund_I_of_Antioch" title="Bohemund I of Antioch">Bohemund</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dyrrhachium_(1081)" title="Battle of Dyrrhachium (1081)">Battle of Dyrrhachium</a>. Byzantine control was restored a few years later, but the Normans under Bohemund returned to besiege it in 1107–08, and sacked it again in 1185 under King <a href="/wiki/William_II_of_Sicily" title="William II of Sicily">William II of Sicily</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEODB.22Dyrrachion.22_.28T._E._Gregory.29.2C_p._668-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1205, after the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Crusade" title="Fourth Crusade">Fourth Crusade</a>, the city was transferred to the rule of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a>, which formed the "Duchy of Durazzo". This Duchy was conquered in 1213 and the city taken by the <a href="/wiki/Despotate_of_Epirus" title="Despotate of Epirus">Despotate of Epirus</a> under <a href="/wiki/Michael_I_Komnenos_Doukas" title="Michael I Komnenos Doukas">Michael I Komnenos Doukas</a>.</p>

<p>In 1257, Durrës was briefly occupied by the King of Sicily, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manfred_of_Sicily" title="Manfred of Sicily">Manfred of Hohenstaufen</a>. It was re-occupied by the Despot of Epirus <a href="/wiki/Michael_II_Komnenos_Doukas" title="Michael II Komnenos Doukas">Michael II Komnenos Doukas</a> until 1259, when the Despotate was defeated by the Byzantine <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Nicaea" title="Empire of Nicaea">Empire of Nicaea</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Pelagonia" title="Battle of Pelagonia">Battle of Pelagonia</a>. In the 1270s, Durrës was again controlled by Epirus under <a href="/wiki/Nikephoros_I_Komnenos_Doukas" title="Nikephoros I Komnenos Doukas">Nikephoros I Komnenos Doukas</a>, the son of Michael II, who in 1278 was forced to yield the city to Charles d' Anjou (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_Sicily" title="Charles I of Sicily">Charles I of Sicily</a>).</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3008" data-file-width="2000" height="301" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg/200px-Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg/300px-Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg/400px-Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Palazzo_Reale_di_Napoli_-_Carlo_I_d%27Angi%C3%B2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Albania_(medieval)" title="Kingdom of Albania (medieval)">Kingdom of Albania</a> was established in 1272 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_of_Anjou" title="Charles of Anjou">Charles of Anjou</a>. The kingdom extended from the region of Durrës south along the coast to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Butrinto" title="Butrinto">Butrint</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In c. 1273, it was wrecked by a devastating <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> (according to <a href="/wiki/George_Pachymeres" title="George Pachymeres">George Pachymeres</a>; R. Elsie, <i>Early Albania</i> (2003), p. 12) but soon recovered. It was briefly occupied by King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_Uro%C5%A1_II_Milutin_of_Serbia" title="Stephen Uroš II Milutin of Serbia">Milutin</a> of Serbia in 1296.</p>
<p>In the early 14th century, the city was ruled by a coalition of Anjous, Hungarians, and Albanians of the Thopia family. In 1317 or 1318, the area was taken by the Serbs and remained under their rule until the 1350s. At that time the Popes, supported by the Anjous, increased their diplomatic and political activity in the area, by using the Latin bishops, including the archbishop of Durrës. The city had been a religious center of Catholicism after the Anjou were installed in Durrës. In 1272, a Catholic archbishop was installed, and until the mid-14th century there were both Catholic and Orthodox archbishops of Durrës.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two Irish pilgrims who visited Albania on their way to Jerusalem in 1322, reported that Durrës was "inhabited by Latins, Greeks, perfidious Jews and barbaric Albanians".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>When the Serbian Tsar <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_Uro%C5%A1_IV_Du%C5%A1an_of_Serbia" title="Stephen Uroš IV Dušan of Serbia">Dušan</a> died in 1355, the city passed into the hands of the Albanian family of <a href="/wiki/Thopia_family" title="Thopia family">Thopias</a>. In 1376 the <a href="/wiki/Navarrese_Company" title="Navarrese Company">Navarrese Company</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_of_%C3%89vreux,_Duke_of_Durazzo" title="Louis of Évreux, Duke of Durazzo">Louis of Évreux, Duke of Durazzo</a>, who had gained the rights on the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Albania_(medieval)" title="Kingdom of Albania (medieval)">Kingdom of Albania</a> from his second wife, attacked and conquered the city, but in 1383 <a href="/wiki/Karl_Topia" title="Karl Topia">Karl Topia</a> regained control of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a> regained control in 1392 and retained the city, known as <i>Durazzo</i> in those years, as part of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albania_Veneta" title="Albania Veneta">Albania Veneta</a></i>. It fended off a siege by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> Sultan <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mehmed_II" title="Mehmed II">Mehmed II</a> in 1466 but fell to Ottoman forces in 1501.</p>
<p>Durrës became a <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> city quite early on; its <a href="/wiki/Diocese" title="Diocese">bishopric</a> was created around 58 and was raised to the status of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishopric" title="Archbishopric">archbishopric</a> in 449. It was also the seat of a <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Greek Orthodox</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_bishop" title="Metropolitan bishop">metropolitan bishop</a>. Under Turkish rule, many of its inhabitants converted to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> and many <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosques</a> were erected. This city was renamed as Dıraç but did not prosper under the Ottomans and its importance declined greatly. By the mid-19th century, its population was said to have been only about 1,000 people living in some 200 households. Its decrepitude was noted by foreign observers in the early 20th century: "The walls are dilapidated; plane-trees grow on the gigantic ruins of its old Byzantine citadel; and its harbour, once equally commodious and safe, is gradually becoming silted up."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It was a main centre in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scutari_Province,_Ottoman_Empire" title="Scutari Province, Ottoman Empire">İşkodra Vilayet</a> before 1912.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span></h3>
<p>Durrës was an active city in the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_National_Awakening" title="Albanian National Awakening">Albanian national liberation movement</a> in the periods 1878–1881 and 1910–1912. <a href="/wiki/Ismail_Qemali" title="Ismail Qemali">Ismail Qemali</a> raised the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Albania" title="Flag of Albania">Albanian flag</a> on 26 November 1912 but the city was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbia" title="Kingdom of Serbia">Kingdom of Serbia</a> three days later during the <a href="/wiki/First_Balkan_War" title="First Balkan War">First Balkan War</a>. On 29 November 1912 Durrës became the county town of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_County_(Kingdom_of_Serbia)" title="Durrës County (Kingdom of Serbia)">Durrës County</a> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>: <span lang="sr" xml:lang="sr">Драчки округ</span>) one of the counties of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbia" title="Kingdom of Serbia">Kingdom of Serbia</a> established on the part of the territory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albania_(toponym)" title="Albania (toponym)">Albania</a> occupied from <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>.</p>

<p>The Durrës County had four districts (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>: <span lang="sr" xml:lang="sr">срез</span>): Durrës, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lezha" title="Lezha">Lezha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elbasan" title="Elbasan">Elbasan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The army of the Kingdom of Serbia retreated from Durrës in April 1913.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The city became <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Albania" title="Principality of Albania">Albania</a>'s second national capital (after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vlora" title="Vlora">Vlora</a>) on 7 March 1914 under the brief rule of Prince <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_of_Wied" title="William of Wied">William of Wied</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-licodu.cois.it_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-licodu.cois.it-19">[19]</a></sup> It remained Albania's capital until 11 February 1920, when the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Lushnj%C3%AB" title="Congress of Lushnjë">Congress of Lushnjë</a> made <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a> the new capital.</p>

<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>, the city was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Italy</a> in 1915 and by <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a> in 1916–1918. It was captured by the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I" title="Allies of World War I">Allies</a> in October 1918. Restored to Albanian sovereignty, Durrës became the country's temporary capital between 1918 and March 1920. It experienced an economic boom due to Italian investments and developed into a major seaport under the rule of King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zog_of_Albania" title="Zog of Albania">Zog</a>, with a modern harbour being constructed in 1927.</p>
<p>An earthquake in 1926 damaged some of the city and the rebuilding that followed gave the city its more modern appearance. During the 1930s, the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Athens" title="Bank of Athens">Bank of Athens</a> had a branch in the city. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> saw Durrës (called <i>Durazzo</i> again in Italian) and the rest of Albania being annexed to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_(1861%E2%80%931946)" title="Kingdom of Italy (1861–1946)">Kingdom of Italy</a> between 1939–1943, then occupied by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> until 1944. Durrës's strategic value as a seaport made it a high-profile military target for both sides. It was the site of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_invasion_of_Albania" title="Italian invasion of Albania">initial Italian landings</a> on 7 April 1939 (and was fiercely defended by <a href="/wiki/Mujo_Ulqinaku" title="Mujo Ulqinaku">Mujo Ulqinaku</a>) as well as the launch point for the ill-fated <a href="/wiki/Greco-Italian_War" title="Greco-Italian War">Italian invasion of Greece</a>. The city was heavily damaged by <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> bombing during the war and the port installations were blown up by the retreating Germans in 1944.</p>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist" title="Communist">Communist</a> regime of <a href="/wiki/Enver_Hoxha" title="Enver Hoxha">Enver Hoxha</a> rapidly rebuilt the city following the war, establishing a variety of heavy industries in the area and expanding the port. It became the terminus of Albania's first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway" title="Railway">railway</a>, begun in 1947. In the late 1980s, the city was briefly renamed Durrës-Enver Hoxha. The city was and continues to remain the center of Albanian mass beach tourism. Following the collapse of communist rule in 1990, Durrës became the focus of mass emigrations from Albania with ships being hijacked in the harbour and sailed at gunpoint to Italy. In one month alone, August 1991, over 20,000 people migrated to Italy in this fashion. Italy intervened militarily, putting the port area under its control, and the city became the center of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Community" title="European Community">European Community</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operation_Pelican" title="Operation Pelican">Operation Pelican</a>", a food-aid program. In 1997, Albania slid into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1997_rebellion_in_Albania" title="1997 rebellion in Albania">anarchy</a> following the collapse of a massive <a href="/wiki/Pyramid_scheme" title="Pyramid scheme">pyramid scheme</a> which devastated the national economy. An Italian-led peacekeeping force was controversially deployed to Durrës and other Albanian cities to restore order, although there were widespread suggestions that the real purpose of "<a href="/wiki/Operation_Alba" title="Operation Alba">Operation Alba</a>" was to prevent economic refugees continuing to use Albania's ports as a route to migrate to Italy.</p>
<p>Following the start of the 21st century, Durrës has been revitalized as many streets were repaved, while parks and façades experienced a face lift.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Topography">Topography</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Albania" title="Geography of Albania">Geography of Albania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Rodon" title="Cape of Rodon">Cape of Rodon</a></div>
<p>Durrës is the second largest city and a municipality of Albania. It is central on the coast, about 33 kilometres (21 miles) west of the capital <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a>. The city is at one of the narrower points of the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a>, opposite the Italian ports of <a href="/wiki/Bari" title="Bari">Bari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brindisi" title="Brindisi">Brindisi</a>. Durrës is located 41.32 latitude and 19.44 longitude and it is situated at an elevation of 13 metres (43 feet) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_sea_level" title="Above sea level">above sea level</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span></h3>
<p>Durrës has a typical <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a> with hot, dry summers and cool winters. The average of water temperature in Durrës is from 14 °C (57 °F) in February to 26 °C (79 °F) in August. The summer in Durrës starts from May to middle of October .</p>
<p>The southern part of the coastal plain is characterized by a relatively dry Mediterranean climate, hot summers with an average temperature of 26 °C (79 °F). Winter is mild and wet with an average temperature of 9.8 °C (49.6 °F). The average annual rainfall amounts to 800 to 1,300 mm (31.5 to 51.2 inches), but only 12 percent of the total falls in the period June–September. In this area many crops are grown (cereals, industrial crops, vegetables, forages etc.), also citrus and olive trees.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Religion_in_Albania" title="Religion in Albania">Religion in Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_religion_in_Albania" title="Freedom of religion in Albania">Freedom of religion in Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Albania" title="Islam in Albania">Islam in Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_Albania" title="Christianity in Albania">Christianity in Albania</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judaism_in_Albania" title="Judaism in Albania">Judaism in Albania</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a> is one of the most ancient countries of <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_Albania" title="Christianity in Albania">Christianity</a>. There are thought to have been about seventy Christian families in the port town of Durrës as early as the time of the Apostles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-21">[21]</a></sup> The archbishopric of Durrës having been founded by the apostle Paul while preaching in Illyria and Epirus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthodox_Church_of_Albania" title="Orthodox Church of Albania">Orthodox Church of Albania</a>, which has been autocephalous since 1923 was divided into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Tirana" title="Archbishop of Tirana">archbishopric of Tirana – Durrës</a>, headed by the Metropolitan and sub-divided into the local church districts of <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a>, Durrës, <a href="/wiki/Shkod%C3%ABr" title="Shkodër">Shkodër</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elbasan" title="Elbasan">Elbasan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-21">[21]</a></sup> When it comes to religion, Muslims (including the large Bektashi minority in Albania) and Christians make up the majority of Durrë's population.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Tourism_in_Albania" title="Tourism in Albania">Tourism in Albania</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:382px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2627" data-file-width="7051" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg/380px-Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg/570px-Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg/760px-Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg 2x" width="380"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Golem_Albania_2016_Durres.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Golem Beach south of Durrës in Tirana county.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Durrës is an important link to <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a> due to its port and its proximity to the Italian port cities, notably Bari, to which daily ferries run. As well as the dockyard, it also possesses an important shipyard and manufacturing industries, notably producing leather, plastic and tobacco products. The southern coastal stretch of Golem is renowned for its traditional mass beach tourism having experienced uncontrolled urban development. The city's beaches are also a popular destination for many foreign and local tourists, with an estimated 800,000 tourists visiting annually. Many Albanians from Tirana and elsewhere spend their summer vacations on the beaches of Durrës. In 2012, new water sanitation systems are being installed to completely eliminate sea water pollution. In contrast, the northern coastal stretch of Lalzit Bay is mostly unspoiled and set to become an elite tourism destination as a number of beach resorts are being built since 2009. Neighboring districts are known for the production of good wine and a variety of foodstuffs.</p>
<p>According to the World Bank, Durrës has made significant steps of starting a business in 2016. Durrës ranks 9<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> among 22 cities in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southeastern_Europe" title="Southeastern Europe">Southeastern Europe</a> before the capital <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>, Serbia and <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>, Bosnia and Herzegovina.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Pan-European_Corridor_VIII" title="Pan-European Corridor VIII">Pan-European Corridor VIII</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A1_motorway_(Albania)" title="A1 motorway (Albania)">A1 motorway (Albania)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/National_Road_2_(Albania)" title="National Road 2 (Albania)">National Road 2 (Albania)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Autostrada_Durr%C3%ABs-Morina-04.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="540" data-file-width="720" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Autostrada_Durr%C3%ABs-Morina-04.jpg/240px-Autostrada_Durr%C3%ABs-Morina-04.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Autostrada_Durr%C3%ABs-Morina-04.jpg/360px-Autostrada_Durr%C3%ABs-Morina-04.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Autostrada_Durr%C3%ABs-Morina-04.jpg/480px-Autostrada_Durr%C3%ABs-Morina-04.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<a href="/wiki/Albania%E2%80%93Kosovo_Highway" title="Albania–Kosovo Highway">A1 motorway</a> through Durrës to Morina</div>

<p>Durrës is the starting point of <a href="/wiki/Pan-European_Corridor_VIII" title="Pan-European Corridor VIII">Pan-European Corridor VIII</a>, national roads SH2 and SH4, and serves as the main railway station of the Albanian Railways (HSH).</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pan-European_Corridor_VIII" title="Pan-European Corridor VIII">Pan-European Corridor VIII</a> is one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan-European_corridor" title="Pan-European corridor">Pan-European corridors</a>. It runs between Durrës, at the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic</a> coast, and <a href="/wiki/Varna" title="Varna">Varna</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Black_Sea_Coast" title="Bulgarian Black Sea Coast">Bulgarian Black Sea coast</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Road_2_(Albania)" title="National Road 2 (Albania)">National Road 2</a> (SH2) begins at the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Port of Durrës">Port of Durrës</a> at the Dajlani Overpass, bypasses the road to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tirana_International_Airport" title="Tirana International Airport">Tirana International Airport</a>, and ends at the Kamza Overpass in the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a> where it meets <a href="/wiki/National_Road_1_(Albania)" title="National Road 1 (Albania)">National Road 1</a> (SH1) State Road heading to northern Albania.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Albania%E2%80%93Kosovo_Highway" title="Albania–Kosovo Highway">Albania–Kosovo Highway</a> is a four-lane highway constructed from 2006 to 2013 between Albania and Kosovo. As part of the South-East European Route 7,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> the highway will connect the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a> ports of Durrës via Pristina, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan-European_corridor_X" title="Pan-European corridor X">E75/Corridor X</a> near <a href="/wiki/Ni%C5%A1" title="Niš">Niš</a>, Serbia. As most tourists come through Kosovo, the laying of the highway make it easier to travel to Durrës.</p>

The very advantageous geographical location of Durrës makes the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Port of Durrës">port</a> to the biggest port of Albania and among the largest in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adriatic_sea" title="Adriatic sea">Adriatic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ionian_sea" title="Ionian sea">Ionian seas</a>.</div>

<p>The very advantageous geographical location makes the port of Durrës to the greatest port of Albania, and among the largest in the Adriatic and <a href="/wiki/Ionian_Sea" title="Ionian Sea">Ionian Sea</a>.</p>
<p>The port is located in the south-western part of Durrës it is an artificial basin that is formed between two moles, with a west-northwesterly oriented entrance approximately wide as it passes between the ends of the moles. Port of Durrës is also a key location for transit networks and passenger ferry, giving Durrës a strategic position with respect to the <a href="/wiki/Pan-European_Corridor_VIII" title="Pan-European Corridor VIII">Pan-European Corridor VIII</a>.</p>
<p>The port has experienced major upgrades in recent years culminating with the opening of the new terminal in July 2012. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/The_Globe_and_Mail" title="The Globe and Mail">The Globe and Mail</a> ranked Durrës at no. 1 among 8 exciting new cruise ports to explore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Port of Durrës">Port of Durrës</a> is one of the largest passenger port on the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a> that handle more than 1.5 million passengers per year. The rail station of Durrës is connected to other cities in Albania including Vlorë and the capital of Tirana.</p>
<p>The Durrës–Tiranë railway was a 38-kilometre (24-mile) <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">railway</a> line which joined the two biggest cities in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>: Durrës and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiran%C3%AB" title="Tiranë">Tiranë</a>. The line connects to the <a href="/wiki/Shkod%C3%ABr%E2%80%93Vor%C3%AB_railway" title="Shkodër–Vorë railway">Shkodër–Vorë railway</a> halfway in <a href="/wiki/Vor%C3%AB" title="Vorë">Vorë</a>, and to the Durrës-Vlorë railway in Durrës. In 2015, some rail stations and rolling stock along the Durrës-Tiranë line are being upgraded and latter colored red and white. In October 2016 they will start building a new Railway with the cost of 81,5 million Euros. And it is expected that it will be used around 1,4 million times a year.<a class="external autonumber" href="http://invest-in-albania.org/bid-tender-on-tirana-durres-rinas-railroad-to-be-launched-in-october/" rel="nofollow">[2]</a></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Durres_film_Fest.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="163" data-file-width="520" height="75" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Durres_film_Fest.jpg/240px-Durres_film_Fest.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Durres_film_Fest.jpg/360px-Durres_film_Fest.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Durres_film_Fest.jpg/480px-Durres_film_Fest.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Durres_film_Fest.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/International_Film_Summerfest_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="International Film Summerfest of Durrës">Durrës International Film Festival</a> has since taken place every year in late August or early September on <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Amphitheatre" title="Durrës Amphitheatre">Durrës Amphitheatre</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Durrës is home to the largest archaeological museum in the country. There are also several museums such as <a href="/wiki/Royal_Villa_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Royal Villa of Durrës">Royal Villa of Durrës</a> and the Museum of History (the house of Aleksandër Moisiu). Durrës host also the <a href="/wiki/Aleksand%C3%ABr_Moisiu_Theatre" title="Aleksandër Moisiu Theatre">Aleksandër Moisiu Theatre</a>, the Estrada Theater, the puppet theater, and the philharmonic orchestra.</p>
<p>The city hosts the Durrës Ancient City Wall called also <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Castle" title="Durrës Castle">Durrës Castle</a> while the largest <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Amphitheatre" title="Durrës Amphitheatre">amphitheatre</a> in the Balkans is in the city close to the harbour. This first-century construction is currently under consideration for inscription as a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Heritage" title="World Heritage">World Heritage</a> site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Durrës host the <a href="/wiki/International_Film_Summerfest_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="International Film Summerfest of Durrës">International Film Summerfest of Durrës</a>, it has since taken place every year in late August or early September on Durrës Amphitheatre in Durrës, Albania. In 2004 and 2009 the <a href="/wiki/Miss_Globe_International" title="Miss Globe International">Miss Globe International</a> were be held in Durrës.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span></h3>
<p>Durrës is home to different architectural styles that represent influential periods in its history. The architecture is influenced by Illyrian, Greek, Roman and Italian architecture. In the 21th century, Durrës has turned into a proper modernist city, with large blocks of flats, modern new buildings, new shopping centres and many green spaces.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museums">Museums</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2322" data-file-width="4128" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg/250px-Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg/375px-Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg/500px-Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum_02.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum" title="Durrës Archaeological Museum">Durrës Archaeological Museum</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Durrës host the largest archaeological museum in the country, the <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Archaeological_Museum" title="Durrës Archaeological Museum">Durrës Archaeological Museum</a>. The museum is located near the beach and north of the museum are the 6th-century Byzantine walls, constructed after the Visigoth invasion of 481. The bulk of the museum consists of artifacts found in the nearby ancient site of Dyrrhachium and includes an extensive collection from the Illyrian, Ancient Greek, Hellenistic and Roman periods. Items of major note include Roman funeral steles and stone sarcophagi, an elliptical colourful mosaic measuring 17 by 10 feet, referred to as <a href="/wiki/The_Beauty_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="The Beauty of Durrës">The Beauty of Durrës</a>, and a collection of miniature busts of Venus, testament to the time when Durrës was a centre of worship of the goddess.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Monuments">Monuments</span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Amphitheatre" title="Durrës Amphitheatre">Durrës Amphitheatre</a> is a large Roman amphitheatre, built in the 2nd century AD. The Amphitheatre is one of the largest amphitheatres in the Balkan peninsula.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_Castle" title="Durrës Castle">Durrës Castle</a> was built by the Emperor of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> <a href="/wiki/Anastasius_I_Dicorus" title="Anastasius I Dicorus">Anastasius I</a> who originated from Durrës. At the time, Anastasius made the city one of the most fortified cities on the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Rodon" title="Cape of Rodon">Cape of Rodon</a> is a rocky cape on the Adriatic Sea north of Durrës. On the Cape is the Rodoni Castle, built by <a href="/wiki/Skanderbeg" title="Skanderbeg">Skanderbeg</a> in 1463 and a Saint Anthony Church.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Great_Mosque_of_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Great Mosque of Durrës">Great Mosque of Durrës</a> is a historic Albanian Mosque in the port area. The mosque was built in 1931 under the Kingdomship of <a href="/wiki/Zog_I_of_Albania" title="Zog I of Albania">Zog I of Albania</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fatih_Mosque,_Durr%C3%ABs" title="Fatih Mosque, Durrës">Fatih Mosque</a> was built in 1502 and named after the Ottoman Turkish Sultan <a href="/wiki/Mehmed_the_Conqueror" title="Mehmed the Conqueror">Mehmed the Conqueror</a>.</li>
<li>The Church of Apostle Paul and St. Asti in Durrës is Orthodox church located in the center of the City and was named after the bishop of Dyrrhachium <a href="/wiki/Saint_Astius" title="Saint Astius">Saint Astius</a>.</li>
</ul>
