<p>The <b>Lecythidaceae</b> comprise a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of about 20 genera and 250-300 species of <a href="/wiki/Woody_plant" title="Woody plant">woody plants</a> native to tropical <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>.</p>
<p>According to the most recent molecular analysis of Lecythidaceae by Mori <i>et al.</i> (2007),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the three subfamilies are:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Foetidioideae</b> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Foetidiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Foetidiaceae (page does not exist)">Foetidiaceae</a>) from Madagascar include only <i><a href="/wiki/Foetidia" title="Foetidia">Foetidia</a></i>.</li>
<li><b>Planchonioideae</b> (including <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Barringtoniaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Barringtoniaceae (page does not exist)">Barringtoniaceae</a>) are restricted to the Old World tropics.</li>
<li><b>Lecythidoideae</b> (Lecythidaceae) are restricted to the New World tropics.</li>
</ul>
<p>Two other families are sometimes included in Lecythidaceae; the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scytopetalaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scytopetalaceae (page does not exist)">Scytopetalaceae</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Napoleonaeaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Napoleonaeaceae (page does not exist)">Napoleonaeaceae</a> are hypothesized as most closely related to Lecythidaceae.</p>
<p>More detailed information about Lecythidaceae, especially the New World taxa, can be found at the <a class="external text" href="http://sweetgum.nybg.org/lp/index.html" rel="nofollow">Lecythidaceae Pages</a>.</p>
<p>The most important member of the family in world trade is the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_nut" title="Brazil nut">Brazil nut</a> (<i>Bertholletia excelsa</i>), valued for its edible <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nuts</a>; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paradise_nut" title="Paradise nut">paradise nut</a> (<i>Lecythis</i> species) is also eaten.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/APG_II_system" title="APG II system">APG II system</a> of 2003 includes genera from the family <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scytopetalaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scytopetalaceae (page does not exist)">Scytopetalaceae</a> in the Lecythidaceae, including <i><a href="/wiki/Rhaptopetalum" title="Rhaptopetalum">Rhaptopetalum</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Brazzeia" title="Brazzeia">Brazzeia</a></i>. <i>Careya</i> is called <i>pezham</i> in Malayalam.</p>
