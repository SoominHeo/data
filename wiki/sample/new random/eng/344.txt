<p><b>Klay Alexander Thompson</b> (born February 8, 1990)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). The son of former NBA player <a href="/wiki/Mychal_Thompson" title="Mychal Thompson">Mychal Thompson</a>, he played <a href="/wiki/College_basketball" title="College basketball">college basketball</a> for three seasons at <a href="/wiki/Washington_State_Cougars_men%27s_basketball" title="Washington State Cougars men's basketball">Washington State University</a>, where he was a two-time first-team <a href="/wiki/List_of_All-Pac-12_Conference_men%27s_basketball_teams" title="List of All-Pac-12 Conference men's basketball teams">all-conference</a> selection in the <a href="/wiki/Pac-12_Conference_men%27s_basketball" title="Pac-12 Conference men's basketball">Pac-10</a>. Thompson was selected in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_NBA_draft" title="2011 NBA draft">2011 NBA draft</a> by Golden State with the 11th overall pick. In 2014, he and teammate <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Curry" title="Stephen Curry">Stephen Curry</a> set a then NBA record with 484 combined <a href="/wiki/Three-point_field_goal" title="Three-point field goal">three-pointers</a> in a season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bleacherreport.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bleacherreport.com-2">[2]</a></sup> as the pair were given the nickname the "<a href="/wiki/Splash_Brothers" title="Splash Brothers">Splash Brothers</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Thompson is a two-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_All-Star" title="NBA All-Star">NBA All-Star</a> and a two-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-NBA_Third_Team" title="All-NBA Third Team">All-NBA Third Team</a> honoree. In 2015, he helped lead the Warriors to their first <a href="/wiki/2015_NBA_Finals" title="2015 NBA Finals">NBA championship</a> since 1975.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school_career">High school career</span></h2>
<p>Thompson was born in Los Angeles to Julie and <a href="/wiki/Mychal_Thompson" title="Mychal Thompson">Mychal Thompson</a>. His mother was a <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> player in college, and his dad was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_first_overall_NBA_draft_picks" title="List of first overall NBA draft picks">first overall pick</a> of the <a href="/wiki/1978_NBA_draft" title="1978 NBA draft">1978 NBA draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At age 2, Thompson's family moved to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Oswego,_Oregon" title="Lake Oswego, Oregon">Lake Oswego, Oregon</a>, where he was childhood friends and <a href="/wiki/Little_League_Baseball" title="Little League Baseball">Little League</a> teammates with fellow future NBA star <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Love" title="Kevin Love">Kevin Love</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> At age 14, Thompson moved with his family to <a href="/wiki/Ladera_Ranch,_California" title="Ladera Ranch, California">Ladera Ranch, California</a>, and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Santa_Margarita_Catholic_High_School" title="Santa Margarita Catholic High School">Santa Margarita Catholic High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Santa_Margarita,_California" title="Rancho Santa Margarita, California">Rancho Santa Margarita</a> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup> In his junior season he was named to the All-Area second team and to the Orange County third team. As a senior Thompson averaged 21 points per game and led SMCHS to a 30–5 record and a Division III State Championship appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay2-7">[7]</a></sup> During the state championship, Thompson set a state finals record with seven <a href="/wiki/Three-point_field_goal" title="Three-point field goal">3-pointers</a> in a game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay2_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Klay2-7">[7]</a></sup> He was named Division III State player of the year, League MVP, first-team Best in the West, and an EA Sports Second Team All American.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Considered as a four-star recruit by <a href="/wiki/Rivals.com" title="Rivals.com">Rivals.com</a>, Thompson was listed as the No. 6 shooting guard and the No. 51 player in the nation in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Freshman_season">Freshman season</span></h3>
<p>As a freshman, Thompson started all 33 games at <a href="/wiki/Washington_State_Cougars_men%27s_basketball" title="Washington State Cougars men's basketball">Washington State University</a>. He was named to the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Pacific-10_Conference_men%27s_basketball_season" title="2008–09 Pacific-10 Conference men's basketball season">Pac-10 All-Freshman Team</a> and Collegehoops.net All-Freshman Honorable Mention Team after leading his team in 3-point field goal percentage and free throw percentage, and averaging 12.5 points per game.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sophomore_season">Sophomore season</span></h3>
<p>Thompson began his sophomore season by leading the Cougars to the <a href="/wiki/Great_Alaska_Shootout" title="Great Alaska Shootout">Great Alaska Shootout</a> Championship, of which he was named Most Outstanding Player after scoring a tournament single game record of 43 points in the championship game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klayx_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klayx-9">[9]</a></sup> This was also the third highest single game point total in WSU history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup> After becoming the third fastest Cougar to reach 1,000 points, Thompson was named to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-Pac-10_men%27s_basketball_team" title="All-Pac-10 men's basketball team">All-Pac-10 First Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay4_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay4-10">[10]</a></sup> He earned Pac-10 Player of the Week honors twice during the season and was chosen as a midseason candidate for the <a href="/wiki/John_R._Wooden_Award" title="John R. Wooden Award">John R. Wooden Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay5_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay5-11">[11]</a></sup> Thompson finished the season averaging 19.6 points, which was second in the conference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay1_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-Klay1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>As a junior, Thompson again earned All-Pac-10 first team honors after leading the Pac-10 in scoring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay8_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay8-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay14_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay14-13">[13]</a></sup> He became just the third Cougar to win first-team all-district honors from the National Association of Basketball Coaches twice in his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay8_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Klay8-12">[12]</a></sup> In addition, he became the first Cougar to win Pac-10 Player of the Week three times when he won the award for the week of Nov. 22–28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay9_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay9-14">[14]</a></sup> He also won the award for the week of December 6–12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay10_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay10-15">[15]</a></sup> Soon after, Thompson was named one of the 30 midseason candidates for the John R. Wooden Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay11_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay11-16">[16]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2011_Pacific-10_Conference_Men%27s_Basketball_Tournament" title="2011 Pacific-10 Conference Men's Basketball Tournament">2011 Pac-10 tournament</a>, he set tournament records with 43 points and 8 three pointers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay15_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay15-17">[17]</a></sup> Thompson finished the season by setting WSU's single season scoring record with 733 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay16_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klay16-18">[18]</a></sup> He is WSU's 3rd all-time leading scorer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay8_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Klay8-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Golden_State_Warriors_.282011.E2.80.93present.29">Golden State Warriors (2011–present)</span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312_season">2011–12 season</span></h4>
<p>Thompson declared for, and opted to remain in, the <a href="/wiki/2011_NBA_draft" title="2011 NBA draft">2011 NBA draft</a> after his junior season. He was selected 11th overall by the Golden State Warriors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klay16_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Klay16-18">[18]</a></sup> This pick of a guard prompted speculation that the Warriors would trade starting guard <a href="/wiki/Monta_Ellis" title="Monta Ellis">Monta Ellis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Warriors_pick_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Warriors_pick-19">[19]</a></sup> Warriors general manager <a href="/wiki/Larry_Riley_(basketball)" title="Larry Riley (basketball)">Larry Riley</a> praised Thompson for his shooting ability and expressed confidence that Thompson would improve his defensive skills with new coach <a href="/wiki/Mark_Jackson_(basketball)" title="Mark Jackson (basketball)">Mark Jackson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Warriors_pick_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Warriors_pick-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The NBA did not select Thompson for the 2012 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_All-Star_Weekend_Rising_Stars_Challenge" title="NBA All-Star Weekend Rising Stars Challenge">NBA All-Star Weekend Rising Stars Challenge</a>. However, in the four games after that decision, Thompson improved in all areas of basketball over his current season averages: points per game (12.5 over 7.6), shooting percentage (54.3% overall including 55.6% for three-pointers, up from 46.7 and 48.1), rebounds (2.8 from 1.6), assists (1.5 from 1.3), steals, and turnovers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top_shooter_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-top_shooter-20">[20]</a></sup> The Warriors traded Ellis to the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a> on March 13, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-delivers_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-delivers-21">[21]</a></sup> The following game, Thompson scored a season-high 26 points in a loss to the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> A week later, he exceeded his previous high with 27 points in a win over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans_Hornets" title="New Orleans Hornets">New Orleans Hornets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> As of mid-February 2012, Thompson played around 17 minutes per game,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top_shooter_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-top_shooter-20">[20]</a></sup> but he played an average 30 minutes per game during the next month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESPN_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-24">[24]</a></sup> At the end of the season, Thompson was voted to the NBA <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_All-Rookie_First_Team" title="NBA All-Rookie First Team">NBA All-Rookie First Team</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313_season">2012–13 season</span></h4>

<p>On January 29, 2013, Thompson scored a season-high 32 points against the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-go_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-go-25">[25]</a></sup> Warriors coach <a href="/wiki/Mark_Jackson_(basketball)" title="Mark Jackson (basketball)">Mark Jackson</a> said that Thompson and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_Curry_(basketball)" title="Stephen Curry (basketball)">Stephen Curry</a> formed the best shooting duo in NBA history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> That season, the two combined made 483 three-pointers, the most ever by an NBA duo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[a]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[28]</a></sup> The Warriors defeated the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Nuggets" title="Denver Nuggets">Denver Nuggets</a> in the first round of the playoffs and would be matched up against the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_San_Antonio_Spurs_season" title="2012–13 San Antonio Spurs season">San Antonio Spurs</a>. On May 8, 2013, Thompson recorded a playoff career-high 34 points against San Antonio, hitting 8 out of 9 three point attempts, along with a career-high 14 rebounds. Thompson and the Warriors would go on to lose to the Spurs in six games.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span></h4>
<p>In the opening game for the Warriors, Thompson scored a season-high 38 points, including 5-of-7 three-pointers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[29]</a></sup> He and Curry set an NBA record for 484 combined threes on the season, besting by one the record they set the previous year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bleacherreport.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bleacherreport.com-2">[2]</a></sup> Thompson averaged 18.4 points, 3.1 rebounds and 2.2 assists on the year. Thompson and the Warriors entered the 2014 NBA playoffs as the sixth seed in the Western Conference and were matched up with the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Los_Angeles_Clippers_season" title="2013–14 Los Angeles Clippers season">Los Angeles Clippers</a> in the first round; they lost the series in seven games.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season:_First_All-Star_selection_and_championship">2014–15 season: First All-Star selection and championship</span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Klay_Thompson_(16614748116).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3368" data-file-width="4065" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Klay_Thompson_%2816614748116%29.jpg/220px-Klay_Thompson_%2816614748116%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Klay_Thompson_%2816614748116%29.jpg/330px-Klay_Thompson_%2816614748116%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Klay_Thompson_%2816614748116%29.jpg/440px-Klay_Thompson_%2816614748116%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>On October 31, 2014, Thompson signed a four-year contract extension with the Warriors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[31]</a></sup> The next day, he scored a then career-high 41 points in the Warriors' 127-104 win over the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[32]</a></sup> On January 23, 2015, Thompson scored a career-high 52 points, with 11 three-pointers, in a 126–101 win over the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_Kings" title="Sacramento Kings">Sacramento Kings</a>. In the third quarter of that game, he scored an <a href="/wiki/NBA_regular_season_records" title="NBA regular season records">NBA-record</a> 37 points for a single quarter, going 13-for-13 from the field, including nine three-pointers (also a league record for a single quarter). The 13 field goals tied <a href="/wiki/David_Thompson_(basketball)" title="David Thompson (basketball)">David Thompson</a>'s (no relation) record for a quarter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[b]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_nba_record_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-new_nba_record-34">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[35]</a></sup> On January 29, 2015, Thompson was named a reserve for the 2015 Western Conference All-Star team for the first time in his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 8, 2015, Thompson hit 3 three-pointers against the Los Angeles Clippers to pass head coach <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kerr" title="Steve Kerr">Steve Kerr</a> (726) on the NBA's all-time list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[37]</a></sup> On March 17, he was ruled out for 7–10 days with a sprained ankle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[38]</a></sup> That season, Stephen Curry broke his own record for three-pointers (286), and Thompson again finished second in the league (239) as the two combined to make 525 threes, surpassing their previous record by 41. On June 7, in Game 2 of the NBA Finals, Thompson scored a playoff career-high 34 points in a losing effort to the Cleveland Cavaliers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[40]</a></sup> The Warriors went on to defeat the Cavaliers in six games to win the NBA championship and end the franchise's 40-year championship drought.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jared_Dudley_vs_Klay_Thompson.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3039" data-file-width="3456" height="193" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Jared_Dudley_vs_Klay_Thompson.jpg/220px-Jared_Dudley_vs_Klay_Thompson.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Jared_Dudley_vs_Klay_Thompson.jpg/330px-Jared_Dudley_vs_Klay_Thompson.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Jared_Dudley_vs_Klay_Thompson.jpg/440px-Jared_Dudley_vs_Klay_Thompson.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Thompson began the season averaging 17.2 points per game over his first 21 games, but failed to record a 30-point game over that stretch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[41]</a></sup> That streak ended on December 8, as he scored a then season-high 39 points on 13-of-21 shooting in a 131–123 win over the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Pacers" title="Indiana Pacers">Indiana Pacers</a>, helping the Warriors extend their unbeaten record to start the season to 23–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[42]</a></sup> The Warriors' NBA-record start ended after 24 wins when they lost to the Milwaukee Bucks 108–95 on December 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[43]</a></sup> In the Warriors' next game, on December 16, Thompson scored 27 of his then season-high 43 points in the third quarter of their 128–103 win over the Phoenix Suns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[44]</a></sup> On January 8, he recorded his third consecutive game with 30 or more points, finishing with 36 points in a 128–108 win over the Portland Trail Blazers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[45]</a></sup> On January 27, he scored a season-high 45 points on 14-of-20 shooting in a 127–107 win over the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[46]</a></sup> The following night, he was named a Western Conference All-Star reserve for the <a href="/wiki/2016_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2016 NBA All-Star Game">2016 NBA All-Star Game</a>, earning his second straight All-Star nod.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[47]</a></sup> On February 13, he competed in the All-Star Weekend's <a href="/wiki/Three-Point_Contest" title="Three-Point Contest">Three-Point Contest</a> and won the event after defeating Stephen Curry and <a href="/wiki/Devin_Booker" title="Devin Booker">Devin Booker</a> in the final round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[48]</a></sup> On March 25, he scored 40 points against the Dallas Mavericks. Two days later, he had another 40-point game against the Philadelphia 76ers, scoring 40 points in consecutive games for the first time in his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[49]</a></sup> On April 7, Thompson scored 14 points against the San Antonio Spurs, helping the Warriors become the second team in NBA history to win 70 games in a season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the No. 1 seed in the Western Conference, the Warriors faced the eighth-seeded Houston Rockets in the first round of the playoffs. In Game 5 of the series, Thompson became the first player in NBA history to make at least seven three-pointers in consecutive playoff games, as he helped the Warriors advance through to the second round with a 4–1 victory, stepping up with Stephen Curry out injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[51]</a></sup> The Warriors went on to defeat the Portland Trail Blazers 4–1 in the second round, moving on to the Western Conference Finals where they faced the Oklahoma City Thunder. After going down 3 games to 1 following a Game 4 loss, Thompson helped the Warriors rally in Games 5 and 6 to even the series at 3–3. In Game 6, Thompson made a playoff-record 11 three-pointers and scored 41 points, as the Warriors forced a Game 7 with a 108–101 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[52]</a></sup> With a Game 7 victory, the Warriors became the 10th team to rally from a 3–1 deficit and win a postseason series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[53]</a></sup> The Warriors went on to lose to the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2016_NBA_Finals" title="2016 NBA Finals">2016 NBA Finals</a> in seven games, despite being up 3–1 in the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span></h4>
<p>On November 13, 2016, Thompson scored a then season-high 30 points in a 133–120 win over the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a>, helping the Warriors start the season 8–2. He shot 11-of-18 with five three-pointers and scored 14 of his points in the fourth quarter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[55]</a></sup> On December 5, 2016, he scored 60 points (shooting 21-of-33 and 8-of-14 on three-pointers) in 29 minutes over just three quarters in a 142–106 win over the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Pacers" title="Indiana Pacers">Indiana Pacers</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[56]</a></sup> becoming the first player in NBA history to score 60 points in less than 30 minutes of action.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[57]</a></sup> Thompson had an NBA season-high and career-best performance for the highest-scoring output by a Golden State player in more than 42 years. His 40 first-half points tied him for second-most scored in a half over the past decade. In addition, Thompson joined Hall of Famers <a href="/wiki/Wilt_Chamberlain" title="Wilt Chamberlain">Wilt Chamberlain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Barry" title="Rick Barry">Rick Barry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Fulks" title="Joe Fulks">Joe Fulks</a> as the only Warriors to <a href="/wiki/List_of_National_Basketball_Association_single-game_scoring_leaders" title="List of National Basketball Association single-game scoring leaders">score 60 points</a>—Barry was the last to do so, scoring 64 on March 26, 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[58]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team_career">National team career</span></h2>
<p>Thompson was a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_basketball_team" title="United States men's national basketball team">United States national team</a> that won the gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/2014_United_States_FIBA_Basketball_World_Cup_team" title="2014 United States FIBA Basketball World Cup team">2014 FIBA Basketball World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Rio Olympics</a>. He also won gold as a member of the Under-19 national team at the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIBA_Under-19_World_Championship" title="2009 FIBA Under-19 World Championship">2009 FIBA Under-19 World Championship</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Thompson, born in Los Angeles, is the son of <a href="/wiki/Mychal_Thompson" title="Mychal Thompson">Mychal Thompson</a>, born in Nassau, Bahamas and who came to the United States in his younger years. Mychal Thompson was a basketball player and first overall pick of the 1978 NBA draft. He played for <a href="/wiki/Portland_Trail_Blazers" title="Portland Trail Blazers">Portland Trail Blazers</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a>. Klay's mother is Julie Leslie a college volleyball player with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Portland" title="University of Portland">University of Portland</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_San_Francisco" title="University of San Francisco">University of San Francisco</a>.</p>
<p>His older brother, <a href="/wiki/Mychel_Thompson" title="Mychel Thompson">Mychel</a>, played basketball for <a href="/wiki/Pepperdine_University" title="Pepperdine University">Pepperdine University</a> and has played in the NBA Development League, while his younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Trayce_Thompson" title="Trayce Thompson">Trayce</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> player with the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>.</p>
<p>Thompson faced controversy when he was suspended for his final regular season game at WSU after being issued a misdemeanor criminal citation for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marijuana" title="Marijuana">marijuana</a> possession.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>After winning the NBA championship in 2015, Thompson and his father became the fourth <a href="/wiki/List_of_second-generation_National_Basketball_Association_players" title="List of second-generation National Basketball Association players">father-son duo</a> to each win an NBA title as players, joining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_Guokas,_Sr." title="Matt Guokas, Sr.">Matt Guokas, Sr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matt_Guokas" title="Matt Guokas">Jr.</a>; <a href="/wiki/Rick_Barry" title="Rick Barry">Rick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brent_Barry" title="Brent Barry">Brent Barry</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Walton" title="Bill Walton">Bill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Walton" title="Luke Walton">Luke Walton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[63]</a></sup></p>
