<p><b>Lupita Amondi Nyong'o</b> (<small><a href="/wiki/Kenyan_English" title="Kenyan English">Kenyan English</a>: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[luˈpiːtɑː ˈɲɔːŋɔ]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Lupita_Nyong%27o_pronunciation.ogg" title="File:Lupita Nyong'o pronunciation.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6a/Lupita_Nyong%27o_pronunciation.ogg" title="Lupita Nyong'o pronunciation.ogg">listen</a>)</small>, Spanish: <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[luˈpita ˈɲoŋo (ˈɲoŋgo)]</a></span>; born March 1, 1983)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-biography.com-2">[2]</a></sup> is a Kenyan-Mexican actress. She was born in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> to Kenyan parents and raised in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-presume_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-presume-3">[3]</a></sup> She attended college in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, earning a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> in film and theater studies from <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_College" title="Hampshire College">Hampshire College</a>.</p>
<p>Nyong'o began her career in Hollywood as a production assistant. In 2008, she made her acting debut with the short film <i><a href="/wiki/East_River_(film)" title="East River (film)">East River</a></i> and subsequently returned to Kenya to star in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Shuga_(TV_series)" title="Shuga (TV series)">Shuga</a></i> (2009). Also in 2009, she wrote, produced and directed the documentary <i><a href="/wiki/In_My_Genes" title="In My Genes">In My Genes</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YaleSchoolDrama-2012-2013_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-YaleSchoolDrama-2012-2013-1">[1]</a></sup> She then pursued a master's degree in acting from the <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Drama" title="Yale School of Drama">Yale School of Drama</a>. Soon after her graduation, she had her first <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a> role as <a href="/wiki/Patsey" title="Patsey">Patsey</a> in <a href="/wiki/Steve_McQueen_(director)" title="Steve McQueen (director)">Steve McQueen</a>'s historical drama <i><a href="/wiki/12_Years_a_Slave_(film)" title="12 Years a Slave (film)">12 Years a Slave</a></i> (2013), for which she won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a>. She became the first Kenyan and first Mexican actress to win an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nyong'o made her <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut as a teenage orphan in the critically acclaimed play <i><a href="/wiki/Eclipsed_(play)" title="Eclipsed (play)">Eclipsed</a></i> (2015), where she received a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Play#2010s" title="Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play">Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play</a> nomination for her performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_background">Early life and background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lupita_Nyong%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nyong'o was born in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CraigyFerg_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CraigyFerg-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lupitadaily_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-lupitadaily-10">[10]</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenyan</a> parents, Dorothy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dorothy_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dorothy-11">[11]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Anyang%27_Nyong%27o" title="Peter Anyang' Nyong'o">Peter Anyang' Nyong'o</a>, a college professor turned politician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torture_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-torture-12">[12]</a></sup> Nyong'o identifies as Kenyan-Mexican and has dual Kenyan and Mexican citizenship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kmr_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-kmr-13">[13]</a></sup> She is of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luo_people" title="Luo people">Luo</a> descent on both sides of her family, and she is the second of six children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-williams_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-williams-14">[14]</a></sup> It is a tradition of the Luo people to name a child after the events of the day, so her parents gave her a Spanish name, Lupita (a <a href="/wiki/Diminutive" title="Diminutive">diminutive</a> of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Guadalupe" title="Our Lady of Guadalupe">Guadalupe</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kimmel_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-kimmel-15">[15]</a></sup> Her father is a former Minister for Medical Services in the Kenyan government. At the time of her birth, he was a visiting lecturer in political science at <a href="/wiki/El_Colegio_de_M%C3%A9xico" title="El Colegio de México">El Colegio de México</a> in Mexico City,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-williams_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-williams-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nymag.com_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-nymag.com-16">[16]</a></sup> and her family had been living in Mexico for three years.</p>
<p>Nyong'o and her family moved back to their native Kenya when she was less than one year old,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kimmel_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-kimmel-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> as her father was appointed a professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nairobi" title="University of Nairobi">University of Nairobi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-williams_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-williams-14">[14]</a></sup> She grew up primarily in Kenya, and describes her upbringing as "middle class, suburban".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nymag.com_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-nymag.com-16">[16]</a></sup> When she was sixteen, her parents sent her to Mexico for seven months to learn Spanish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kimmel_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-kimmel-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost.com_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost.com-18">[18]</a></sup> During those seven months, Nyong'o lived in <a href="/wiki/Taxco" title="Taxco">Taxco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a>, and took classes at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_Nacional_Aut%C3%B3noma_de_M%C3%A9xico" title="Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México">Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México</a>'s Learning Center for Foreigners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost.com_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost.com-18">[18]</a></sup> Her family was later forced to leave Kenya because of political unrest. Her uncle, Charles Nyong'o, disappeared after he was thrown off a ferry in 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torture_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-torture-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nyong'o grew up in an artistic family, where family get-togethers often included performances by the children in the family, and trips to see plays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday.com_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday.com-19">[19]</a></sup> She attended Rusinga International school in Kenya and acted in school plays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dorothy_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dorothy-11">[11]</a></sup> At age 14, Nyong'o made her professional acting debut as <a href="/wiki/Juliet" title="Juliet">Juliet</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dorothy_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dorothy-11">[11]</a></sup> in a production by the <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>-based repertory company <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Players" title="Phoenix Players">Phoenix Players</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nymag.com_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-nymag.com-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday.com_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday.com-19">[19]</a></sup> While a member of the Phoenix Players, Nyong'o also performed in the plays <i>On The Razzle</i> and <i>There Goes The Bride</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nation.co.ke_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-nation.co.ke-20">[20]</a></sup> Nyong'o cites the performances of American actresses <a href="/wiki/Whoopi_Goldberg" title="Whoopi Goldberg">Whoopi Goldberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Color_Purple_(1985_film)" title="The Color Purple (1985 film)">The Color Purple</a></i> with inspiring her to pursue a professional acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nyong'o later attended <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s_School,_Nairobi" title="St. Mary's School, Nairobi">St. Mary's School</a> in Nairobi, where she received an <a href="/wiki/IB_Diploma_Programme" title="IB Diploma Programme">IB Diploma</a> in 2001 before attending college in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saints_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saints-23">[23]</a></sup> She graduated from <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_College" title="Hampshire College">Hampshire College</a> with a degree in film and theatre studies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lupita_Nyong%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_work">Early work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lupita_Nyong%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nyong'o started her career working as part of the production crew for several films, including <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Meirelles" title="Fernando Meirelles">Fernando Meirelles</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Constant_Gardener_(film)" title="The Constant Gardener (film)">The Constant Gardener</a></i> (2005), <a href="/wiki/Mira_Nair" title="Mira Nair">Mira Nair</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Namesake_(film)" title="The Namesake (film)">The Namesake</a></i> (2006) and Salvatore Stabile's <i><a href="/wiki/Where_God_Left_His_Shoes" title="Where God Left His Shoes">Where God Left His Shoes</a></i> (2007).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hampshire.edu_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-hampshire.edu-25">[25]</a></sup> She cites <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" title="Ralph Fiennes">Ralph Fiennes</a>, the star of <i>The Constant Gardener</i>, as someone who inspired her to pursue a professional acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nymag.com_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-nymag.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Nyong'o starred in the short film "<a href="/wiki/East_River_(film)" title="East River (film)">East River</a>", directed by Marc Grey and shot in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> She returned to Kenya that same year and appeared in the Kenyan television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shuga_(Kenyan_TV_series)" title="Shuga (Kenyan TV series)">Shuga</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/MTV_Base" title="MTV Base">MTV Base</a> Africa/<a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a> drama about <a href="/wiki/HIV/AIDS" title="HIV/AIDS">HIV/AIDS</a> prevention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hampshire.edu_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-hampshire.edu-25">[25]</a></sup> In 2009, she wrote, directed, and produced the documentary <i><a href="/wiki/In_My_Genes" title="In My Genes">In My Genes</a></i>, about the discriminatory treatment of Kenya's <a href="/wiki/Albinism" title="Albinism">albino</a> population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-williams_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-williams-14">[14]</a></sup> It played at several film festivals and won first prize at the 2008 Five College Film Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hampshire.edu_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-hampshire.edu-25">[25]</a></sup> Nyong'o also directed the music video <i>The Little Things You Do</i> by <a href="/wiki/Wahu" title="Wahu">Wahu</a>, featuring <a href="/wiki/Bobi_Wine" title="Bobi Wine">Bobi Wine</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hampshire.edu_25-3"><a href="#cite_note-hampshire.edu-25">[25]</a></sup> which was nominated for the Best Video Award at the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Africa_Music_Awards_2009" title="MTV Africa Music Awards 2009">MTV Africa Music Awards 2009</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hampshire.edu_25-4"><a href="#cite_note-hampshire.edu-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nyong'o went on to enroll herself in a master's degree program in acting at the <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Drama" title="Yale School of Drama">Yale School of Drama</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale</a>, she appeared in many stage productions, including <a href="/wiki/Gertrude_Stein" title="Gertrude Stein">Gertrude Stein</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Faustus_Lights_the_Lights" title="Doctor Faustus Lights the Lights">Doctor Faustus Lights the Lights</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anton_Chekov" title="Anton Chekov">Chekhov</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Uncle_Vanya" title="Uncle Vanya">Uncle Vanya</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Taming_of_the_Shrew" title="The Taming of the Shrew">The Taming of the Shrew</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Winter%27s_Tale" title="The Winter's Tale">The Winter's Tale</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lupitadaily2_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-lupitadaily2-27">[27]</a></sup> While at Yale, she won the Herschel Williams Prize for "acting students with outstanding ability" during the 2011–12 academic year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YaleSchoolDrama-2012-2013_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-YaleSchoolDrama-2012-2013-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_breakthrough">Acting breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lupita_Nyong%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acting breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Immediately after graduating from Yale, Nyong'o landed her <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_role" title="Breakthrough role">breakthrough role</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> when she was cast for <a href="/wiki/Steve_McQueen_(director)" title="Steve McQueen (director)">Steve McQueen</a>'s historical drama <i><a href="/wiki/12_Years_a_Slave_(film)" title="12 Years a Slave (film)">12 Years a Slave</a></i> (2013).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nymag.com_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-nymag.com-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday.com_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday.com-19">[19]</a></sup> The film, which met with wide critical acclaim, tells the historical account of <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Northup" title="Solomon Northup">Solomon Northup</a> (played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiwitel_Ejiofor" title="Chiwitel Ejiofor">Chiwitel Ejiofor</a>), a free-born <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African American</a> man of upstate New York who is kidnapped and sold into slavery in Washington, DC in 1841.</p>
<p>Nyong'o played the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patsey_(12_Years_a_Slave)" title="Patsey (12 Years a Slave)">Patsey</a>, a slave who works alongside Northup at a Louisiana cotton plantation; her performance met with rave reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ian_Freer" title="Ian Freer">Ian Freer</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_(film_magazine)" title="Empire (film magazine)">Empire</a></i> wrote that she "gives one of the most committed big-screen debuts imaginable," and critic <a href="/wiki/Peter_Travers" title="Peter Travers">Peter Travers</a> added that she "is a spectacular young actress who imbues Patsey with grit and radiant grace".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Nyong'o was nominated for several awards including a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture">Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress</a>, a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role">BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role</a> and two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild Awards</a> including <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_a_Female_Actor_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Supporting Role">Best Supporting Actress</a>, which she won.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> She was also awarded the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a>, becoming the sixth black actress to win the award, the first African actress to win the award, the first Kenyan actress to win an Oscar, and the first Mexican to win the award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WP_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-WP-34">[34]</a></sup> She also became the fifteenth actress to win an Oscar for a debut performance in a feature film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following a supporting role in the action-thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Non-Stop_(film)" title="Non-Stop (film)">Non-Stop</a></i> (2014), Nyong'o co-starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Awakens" title="Star Wars: The Force Awakens">Star Wars: The Force Awakens</a></i> (2015) as <a href="/wiki/The_Force_(Star_Wars)" title="The Force (Star Wars)">force sensitive</a>-<a href="/wiki/Space_pirate" title="Space pirate">space pirate</a> <a href="/wiki/Maz_Kanata" title="Maz Kanata">Maz Kanata</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">CGI</a> character created using <a href="/wiki/Motion_capture" title="Motion capture">motion capture</a> technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mazkanatareveal_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-mazkanatareveal-37">[37]</a></sup> Nyong'o said that she had wanted to play a role where her appearance was not relevant, and that the acting provided a different challenge than her role as Patsey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buzzfdmaz_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-buzzfdmaz-38">[38]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scott_Mendelson" title="Scott Mendelson">Scott Mendelson</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>, found Nyong'o's role as "the center of the film's best sequence," and <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_Zacharek" title="Stephanie Zacharek">Stephanie Zacharek</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> called her a "delightful minor character".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sf_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-sf-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steptimereview_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-steptimereview-40">[40]</a></sup> Nyong'o was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress">Best Supporting Actress</a> at the <a href="/wiki/42nd_Saturn_Awards" title="42nd Saturn Awards">42nd Saturn Awards</a> and Best Virtual Performance at the <a href="/wiki/2016_MTV_Movie_Awards" title="2016 MTV Movie Awards">2016 MTV Movie Awards</a> for her role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-satawards_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-satawards-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvmvp_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvmvp-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eclipsed_and_beyond"><i>Eclipsed</i> and beyond</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lupita_Nyong%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Eclipsed and beyond">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The year 2015 saw Nyong’o make a return to stage with a starring role as an unnamed girl in the play <i><a href="/wiki/Eclipsed_(play)" title="Eclipsed (play)">Eclipsed</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Danai_Gurira" title="Danai Gurira">Danai Gurira</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People-Eclipsed-SoWhite-2015_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-People-Eclipsed-SoWhite-2015-43">[43]</a></sup> The play takes place during the chaos of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Liberian_Civil_War" title="Second Liberian Civil War">Second Liberian Civil War</a>, where the captive wives of a rebel officer band together to form a community, until the balance of their lives are upset by the arrival of a new girl (played by Nyong'o). <i>Eclipsed</i> became <a href="/wiki/The_Public_Theater" title="The Public Theater">The Public Theater</a>'s fastest-selling new production in recent history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bsthepub_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-bsthepub-44">[44]</a></sup> The play premiered on Broadway at the <a href="/wiki/John_Golden_Theatre" title="John Golden Theatre">John Golden Theatre</a> the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beclipsed_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-beclipsed-45">[45]</a></sup> The production became the first play with an all-black and female creative cast and crew to premiere at Broadway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bwb_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-bwb-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bw2_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-bw2-47">[47]</a></sup> Nyong'o said that she understudied the play at Yale in 2009 and was terrified to play the character on stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eclipsedvariety_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-eclipsedvariety-48">[48]</a></sup> Her performance met with critical acclaim. <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span> critic <a href="/wiki/Charles_Isherwood" title="Charles Isherwood">Charles Isherwood</a> called Nyong'o "one of the most radiant young actors to be seen on Broadway in recent seasons, shines with a compassion that makes us see beyond the suffering to the indomitable humanity of its characters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Nyong'o's performance in <i>Eclipsed</i> earned her a <a href="/wiki/Theatre_World_Award" title="Theatre World Award">Theatre World Award</a> for Outstanding Broadway or Off-Broadway Debut Performance, an <a href="/wiki/Obie_Award" title="Obie Award">Obie Award</a> for a Distinguished Performance by an Ensemble, and a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Play" title="Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play">Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play</a>. In addition, she was nominated for Outstanding Actress in a Play at the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Critics_Circle_Award" title="Outer Critics Circle Award">Outer Critics Circle Award</a> and a Distinguished Performance Award at the <a href="/wiki/Drama_League_Award" title="Drama League Award">Drama League Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ta16_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-ta16-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twa16_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-twa16-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tm_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-tm-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dla_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-dla-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oa16_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-oa16-54">[54]</a></sup> Nyong'o revealed in her <a href="/wiki/Lenny_Letter" title="Lenny Letter">Lenny Letter</a> that she turned down Hollywood films in order to bring <i>Eclipsed</i> to life on Broadway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyongoessay_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyongoessay-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nyong'o co-starred in <a href="/wiki/Jon_Favreau" title="Jon Favreau">Jon Favreau</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jungle_Book_(2016_film)" title="The Jungle Book (2016 film)">The Jungle Book</a></i> (2016), a live-action/animated movie, voicing <a href="/wiki/Raksha_(The_Jungle_Book)" title="Raksha (The Jungle Book)">Raksha</a>, a mother wolf who adopts <a href="/wiki/Mowgli" title="Mowgli">Mowgli</a> (played by Neel Sethi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jungleb_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-jungleb-56">[56]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Collin" title="Robbie Collin">Robbie Collin</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> wrote in his review that Nyong'o brought a "gentle dignity" to her role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jbr1_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-jbr1-57">[57]</a></sup> Nyong'o then starred in <a href="/wiki/Mira_Nair" title="Mira Nair">Mira Nair</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_of_Katwe" title="Queen of Katwe">Queen of Katwe</a></i> (2016), a biopic based on the true story about the rise of a young Ugandan chess prodigy,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qok_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-qok-58">[58]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Phiona_Mutesi" title="Phiona Mutesi">Phiona Mutesi</a> (played by Madina Nalwanga), who becomes a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woman_Candidate_Master" title="Woman Candidate Master">Woman Candidate Master</a> after her performances at World <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chess_Olympiads" title="Chess Olympiads">Chess Olympiads</a>. Nyong’o played Phiona's protective mother, Nakku Harriet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qok2_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-qok2-59">[59]</a></sup> Her performance was praised by critics.</p>
<p>Nyong'o will star opposite <a href="/wiki/Chadwick_Boseman" title="Chadwick Boseman">Chadwick Boseman</a> as Dora Milaje <a href="/wiki/Malice_(comics)#Malice_.28Black_Panther_villain.29" title="Malice (comics)">Nakia</a> in the upcoming <a href="/wiki/Black_Panther_(comics)" title="Black Panther (comics)"><i>Black Panther</i></a> film. Her character was revealed at the 2016 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">San Diego Comic-Con</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Nyong'o resides in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> She is fluent in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luo_languages" title="Luo languages">Luo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_(language)" title="English (language)">English</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swahili_(language)" title="Swahili (language)">Swahili</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost.com_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost.com-18">[18]</a></sup> On February 27, 2014, at the <i><a href="/wiki/Essence_(magazine)" title="Essence (magazine)">Essence</a></i> Black Women In Hollywood luncheon in Beverly Hills, she gave a speech on the beauty of black women and talked about the insecurities she had as a teenager. She said her views changed when she saw South Sudanese supermodel <a href="/wiki/Alek_Wek" title="Alek Wek">Alek Wek</a> become successful.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> She mentioned receiving the following letter from a girl she had inspired in turn:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>"I was just about to buy Dencia's Whitenicious cream to lighten my skin when you appeared on the world map and saved me."</p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— Lupita Nyong'o quoting a letter written to her by an unidentified little girl from Africa<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>In 2013, her father was elected to represent <a href="/wiki/Kisumu_County" title="Kisumu County">Kisumu County</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Kenya" title="Senate of Kenya">Kenyan Senate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kimmel_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-kimmel-15">[15]</a></sup> Nyong'o's mother is the managing director of the Africa Cancer Foundation and her own <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communications" title="Communications">communications</a> company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday.com_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday.com-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nation.co.ke_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-nation.co.ke-20">[20]</a></sup> Other family members include: <a href="/wiki/Tavia_Nyong%27o" title="Tavia Nyong'o">Tavia Nyong'o</a>, a scholar and professor at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>; Dr. Omondi Nyong'o, a pediatric ophthalmologist in Palo Alto, CA; Kwame Nyong'o, one of Kenya's leading animators and leading technology expert; <a href="/wiki/Isis_Nyong%27o" title="Isis Nyong'o">Isis Nyong'o</a>, a media and technology leader who was named one of Africa's most powerful young women by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Historic_Preservation" title="National Trust for Historic Preservation">National Trust for Historic Preservation</a> recruited Nyong'o in an effort to oppose development, including a new minor league baseball stadium, in the <a href="/wiki/Shockoe_Bottom" title="Shockoe Bottom">Shockoe Bottom</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> The historic neighborhood, one of Richmond's oldest, was the site of major slave-trading before the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. On October 19, 2014, Nyong'o sent a letter to Richmond Mayor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dwight_C._Jones" title="Dwight C. Jones">Dwight C. Jones</a>, which she posted on social media sites, asking him to withdraw support for the development proposal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015, Nyong'o returned to Kenya and announced that she will advocate globally for elephants with the international conservation organization <a href="/wiki/WildAid" title="WildAid">WildAid</a>, as well as promote women's issues, acting and the arts in Kenya. WildAid announced Nyong'o as their Global Elephant Ambassador.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wildaid_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-wildaid-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nyong'o is involved in the organization Mother Health International, which is dedicated to providing relief to women and children in <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> or other impoverished, war-torn regions by creating locally engaged birthing centers. She said she’d never thought much about birthing practices until her sister introduced her to MHI executive director Rachel Zaslow. Nyong'o felt bringing attention to such important but overlooked issues is a mandate for her as an artist. She was honored for her work at 2016 <a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a>'s Power of Women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-varietypower16_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-varietypower16-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2016, Nyong'o launched an anti-poaching "hearts and minds" campaign with her organization Wildaid in advance of <a href="/wiki/Kenya_Wildlife_Service" title="Kenya Wildlife Service">Kenya Wildlife Service</a>'s history-making <a href="/wiki/Destruction_of_ivory" title="Destruction of ivory">ivory burn</a> that occurred April 30. The Kenyan government burned 105 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tonnes" title="Tonnes">tonnes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ivory" title="Ivory">Ivory</a> and 1.35 tonnes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhino_horn" title="Rhino horn">rhino horn</a> in a demonstration of their zero tolerance approach to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poachers" title="Poachers">poachers</a> and smugglers who were threatening the survival of elephants and <a href="/wiki/Rhinoceros" title="Rhinoceros">rhinoceros</a> in the wild.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bik16_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-bik16-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wildaid1_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-wildaid1-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_media">In the media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lupita_Nyong%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In the media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2213" data-file-width="1663" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg/220px-Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg/330px-Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg/440px-Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ms._magazine_Cover_-_Spring_2016.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>Nyong'o was mentioned in Christian rapper <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a>'s song "Nuthin'" from his 2014 album <i><a href="/wiki/Anomaly_(Lecrae_album)" title="Anomaly (Lecrae album)">Anomaly</a></i> and was referenced by <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a> in his verse from <a href="/wiki/Jay_Electronica" title="Jay Electronica">Jay Electronica</a>'s song "We Made It". She was also mentioned in the parody song "American Apparel Ad Girls" by the drag queens <a href="/wiki/Willam_Belli" title="Willam Belli">Willam Belli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Act" title="Courtney Act">Courtney Act</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alaska_Thunderfuck" title="Alaska Thunderfuck">Alaska Thunderfuck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Nyong'o was mentioned in the 2015 African song Nerea by Kenyan afro-pop band <a href="/wiki/Sauti_Sol" title="Sauti Sol">Sauti Sol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kari2015_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kari2015-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2014, she was chosen as one of the faces for <a href="/wiki/Miu_Miu" title="Miu Miu">Miu Miu</a>'s spring campaign, with <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Olsen" title="Elizabeth Olsen">Elizabeth Olsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elle_Fanning" title="Elle Fanning">Elle Fanning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bella_Heathcote" title="Bella Heathcote">Bella Heathcote</a>. She has also appeared on the covers of several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magazines" title="Magazines">magazines</a>, including <i>New York'</i>s spring fashion issue<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> and the UK magazine <i>Dazed &amp; Confused</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> She has also been a regular on <i><a href="/wiki/Harper%27s_Bazaar" title="Harper's Bazaar">Harper's Bazaar</a>'s</i> <a href="/wiki/Derek_Blasberg" title="Derek Blasberg">Derek Blasberg</a>'s best dressed listing since the autumn of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday.com_19-4"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday.com-19">[19]</a></sup> The same year, Nyong'o was named "<a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)#People_Magazine.27s_100_Most_Beautiful_People" title="People (magazine)">The Most Beautiful Woman</a>" by <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People</a></i> and "<a href="/wiki/Glamour_(magazine)#Glamour_Woman_of_the_Year_Awards" title="Glamour (magazine)">Woman of the Year</a>" by <i><a href="/wiki/Glamour_(magazine)" title="Glamour (magazine)">Glamour</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GlamMag_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-GlamMag-77">[77]</a></sup> Nyong'o was announced as the new face of <a href="/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me" title="Lancôme">Lancôme</a> in 2014, making her the first black woman to appear on the brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nyong'o was on the July 2014 cover of <i>Vogue</i>, making her the second African woman<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> and ninth black woman<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> to cover the magazine. Nyong'o also appeared on the cover of July's issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Elle_(magazine)" title="Elle (magazine)">Elle</a></i> (France). She appeared on other covers of magazines such as, March's issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Lucky_(magazine)" title="Lucky (magazine)">Lucky Magazine</a></i>, <i>Harper's Baazar's</i> (United Kingdom) May issue, Spanish Magazine <i>MujerHoy</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Paris_Match" title="Paris Match">Paris Match</a></i>, <i>Elle</i> (Indonesia), and <i>Glamour</i> (South Africa). She appeared on the American October '15 issue of <i>Vogue</i>, making it her second cover in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vogue2_81-0"><a href="#cite_note-vogue2-81">[81]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 20, 2015, Congressman <a href="/wiki/Charles_Rangel" title="Charles Rangel">Charles Rangel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voza_Rivers" title="Voza Rivers">Voza Rivers</a>, the head of the <a href="/wiki/New_Heritage_Theatre_Group" title="New Heritage Theatre Group">New Heritage Theatre Group</a>, announced the day is officially "Lupita Nyong’o Day" in Harlem, New York. The honor was announced as a surprise during an open discussion between Nyong'o and image activist <a href="/wiki/Michaela_Angela_Davis" title="Michaela Angela Davis">Michaela Angela Davis</a> at Mist Harlem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lnday_82-0"><a href="#cite_note-lnday-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2016, Nyong'o was honored with a <a href="/wiki/Caricature" title="Caricature">caricature</a> portrait at <a href="/wiki/Sardi%27s" title="Sardi's">Sardi's</a> restaurant in New York City for her debut on Broadway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr16_83-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr16-83">[83]</a></sup> Later, she was chosen as one of the first celebrities, alongside with Elle Fanning, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christy_Turlington_Burns" title="Christy Turlington Burns">Christy Turlington Burns</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Westling" title="Natalie Westling">Natalie Westling</a> to star in <a href="/wiki/Tiffany_%26_Co." title="Tiffany &amp; Co.">Tiffany &amp; Co.</a>'s Fall '16 campaign styled by <a href="/wiki/Grace_Coddington" title="Grace Coddington">Grace Coddington</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tiffany_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tiffany-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
