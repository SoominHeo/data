<p>The <b>7th century BC</b> began the first day of <a href="/wiki/700_BC" title="700 BC">700 BC</a> and ended the last day of <a href="/wiki/601_BC" title="601 BC">601 BC</a>.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assyrian_Empire" title="Assyrian Empire">Assyrian Empire</a> continued to dominate the <a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a> during this century, exercising formidable power over neighbors like <a href="/wiki/Babylon" title="Babylon">Babylon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. In the last two decades of the century, however, the empire began to unravel as numerous enemies made alliances and waged war from all sides. The Assyrians finally left the world stage permanently when their capital <a href="/wiki/Nineveh" title="Nineveh">Nineveh</a> was destroyed in 612 BC. These events gave rise to the <a href="/wiki/Neo-Babylonian_Empire" title="Neo-Babylonian Empire">Neo-Babylonian Empire</a>, which would dominate the region for much of the following century.</p>
