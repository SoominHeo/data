<p><b>Stade de la Mosson</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[stad də la mɔˈsɔ̃]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> stadium in <a href="/wiki/Montpellier" title="Montpellier">Montpellier</a>, France. It is the home of <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_HSC" title="Montpellier HSC">Montpellier HSC</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a>) and has a capacity of 32,900. Formerly a 16,000-seater stadium, it was entirely rebuilt in 1998 to host 6 games of the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998 FIFA World Cup</a>. It was also used as a venue for group stage matches in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Rugby_World_Cup" title="2007 Rugby World Cup">2007 Rugby World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>On Saturday 9 June 2012, the stadium will host <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> for the first time. The <a href="/wiki/Catalans_Dragons" title="Catalans Dragons">Catalans Dragons</a> will continue their policy of playing some home games away from the <a href="/wiki/Stade_Gilbert_Brutus" title="Stade Gilbert Brutus">Stade Gilbert Brutus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perpignan" title="Perpignan">Perpignan</a> by playing their <a href="/wiki/Stobart" title="Stobart">Stobart</a> <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> fixture against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Warriors" title="Wigan Warriors">Wigan Warriors</a> at the stadium. This will be the second time the fixture has been played in Montpellier with the previous year's game being held in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stade_Yves-du-Manoir_(Montpellier)" title="Stade Yves-du-Manoir (Montpellier)">Stade Yves-du-Manoir</a>.</p>
<p>The stadium also hosted a semi-final game in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Heineken_Cup" title="2012–13 Heineken Cup">2012–13 Heineken Cup</a> between <a href="/wiki/ASM_Clermont_Auvergne" title="ASM Clermont Auvergne">Clermont</a> and <a href="/wiki/Munster_Rugby" title="Munster Rugby">Munster</a> on 27 April 2013.</p>
<p>In October 2014 the stadium flooded twice as over 12 inches of rain fell in <a href="/wiki/Montpellier" title="Montpellier">Montpellier</a>. The stadium and pitch were damaged and this resulted in Montpellier relocating for 5 fixtures during the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Montpellier_HSC_season" title="2014–15 Montpellier HSC season">2014–15 season</a> (4 <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> matches and 1 <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_la_Ligue" title="Coupe de la Ligue">Coupe de la Ligue</a> match) to the <a href="/wiki/Altrad_Stadium" title="Altrad Stadium">Altrad Stadium</a>, until repairs had been made to the stadium. The relocation lasted for the remainder of 2014, the first fixture on 28 October, and the last being 13 December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The flooding caused Montpellier to cancel their 40-year anniversary celebration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
