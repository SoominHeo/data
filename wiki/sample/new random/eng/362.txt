<p><b>Adina Elizabeth Porter</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-2">[2]</a></sup> (born March 13, 1971) is an American actress. She began her career appearing on Off-Broadway stage, winning <a href="/wiki/Obie_Award" title="Obie Award">Obie Award</a> in 1996 for <i><a href="/wiki/Venus_(play)" title="Venus (play)">Venus</a></i>.</p>
<p>Porter is best known for her performances on television. From 2008 to 2014, she starred as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lettie_Mae_Thornton" title="Lettie Mae Thornton">Lettie Mae Thornton</a> in the HBO drama series <i><a href="/wiki/True_Blood" title="True Blood">True Blood</a></i>. From 2012 to 2014, Porter also co-starred as Kendra James in the HBO drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Newsroom_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Newsroom (U.S. TV series)">The Newsroom</a></i>. In 2014, she began appearing in a recurring role as Indra in the <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> post-apocalyptic drama, <i><a href="/wiki/The_100_(TV_series)" title="The 100 (TV series)">The 100</a></i>, and in 2016 was cast as Lee Harris in the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> anthology series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Roanoke" title="American Horror Story: Roanoke">American Horror Story: Roanoke</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adina_Porter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Porter was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-2">[2]</a></sup> She graduated from the <a href="/wiki/State_University_of_New_York_at_Purchase" title="State University of New York at Purchase">State University of New York at Purchase</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> Her first acting teacher was <a href="/wiki/Butterfly_McQueen" title="Butterfly McQueen">Butterfly McQueen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> She was married two times and has a son with her second husband, Larry Earl Madison Jr.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theater">Theater</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adina_Porter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Theater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Porter began her acting career in the theatre, appearing in off Broadway plays and in regional theatre. Her off-Broadway credits include <i>The Debutante Ball</i>, <i>Jersey City</i>, <i>Aven' U Boys</i>, <i>Girl Gone</i>, <i>Silence, Cunning, Exile</i>, <i>Dancing on Moonlight</i> and <i>Hurricane</i>. In 1996, she received <a href="/wiki/Obie_Award_for_Distinguished_Performance_by_an_Actress" title="Obie Award for Distinguished Performance by an Actress">Obie Award for Distinguished Performance by an Actress</a> for her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Venus_(play)" title="Venus (play)">Venus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her Broadway debut came in 2001 with the Roundabout Theater's revival of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Women_(play)" title="The Women (play)">The Women</a></i>, directed by Scott Elliott, broadcast as part of PBS's Stage on Screen series, in addition, she performed in multiple productions at the New York Shakespeare Festival.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_film">Television and film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adina_Porter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television and film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Porter began her screen career playing guest starring roles on television dramas include <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Undercover" title="New York Undercover">New York Undercover</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_South" title="Brooklyn South">Brooklyn South</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>. She had a recurring role as housekeeper Gwen Walker in the NBC period drama <i><a href="/wiki/American_Dreams" title="American Dreams">American Dreams</a></i> from 2002 to 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In film, she made her debut in 1992 <a href="/wiki/Leopold_and_Loeb" title="Leopold and Loeb">Leopold/Loeb</a> <a href="/wiki/New_Queer_Cinema" title="New Queer Cinema">New Queer Cinema</a> feature, <i><a href="/wiki/Swoon_(film)" title="Swoon (film)">Swoon</a></i>. She went to appear in small roles in films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Peacemaker_(1997_film)" title="The Peacemaker (1997 film)">The Peacemaker</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/Gia" title="Gia">Gia</a></i> (1998), <i><a href="/wiki/Body_Shots_(film)" title="Body Shots (film)">Body Shots</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fluffer" title="The Fluffer">The Fluffer</a></i> (2001), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Salon_(film)" title="The Salon (film)">The Salon</a></i> (2005). In 2005, Porter played Ricky in the film adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Ruben_Santiago-Hudson" title="Ruben Santiago-Hudson">Ruben Santiago-Hudson</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Lackawanna_Blues" title="Lackawanna Blues">Lackawanna Blues</a></i> for HBO. She received <a href="/wiki/Black_Reel_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress:_Television_Movie/Cable" title="Black Reel Award for Best Supporting Actress: Television Movie/Cable">Black Reel Award for Best Supporting Actress: Television Movie/Cable</a> for her role. She went to appear <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Prison_Break" title="Prison Break">Prison Break</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Without_a_Trace" title="Without a Trace">Without a Trace</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2008, Porter was cast as Lettie Mae Thornton, mother to <a href="/wiki/Rutina_Wesley" title="Rutina Wesley">Rutina Wesley</a>'s Tara Thorton, in the HBO drama series, <i><a href="/wiki/True_Blood" title="True Blood">True Blood</a></i>. She was promoted to series regular in seventh and final season of show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2012, she guest starred in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> From 2012 to 2014, she also had a recurring role on the HBO series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Newsroom_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Newsroom (U.S. TV series)">The Newsroom</a></i> playing Kendra James. In 2014, she began appearing in a recurring role as Indra in the <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> post-apocalyptic drama, <i><a href="/wiki/The_100_(TV_series)" title="The 100 (TV series)">The 100</a></i>. Porter also starred in two unsold drama pilots for ABC, <i>Doubt</i> in 2013, and <i>The Jury</i> in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2016, she played Pearly Mae during first season of <a href="/wiki/WGN_America" title="WGN America">WGN America</a> period drama <i><a href="/wiki/Underground_(TV_series)" title="Underground (TV series)">Underground</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Later that year, Porter was cast as Lee Harris in the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> anthology series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Roanoke" title="American Horror Story: Roanoke">American Horror Story: Roanoke</a></i>, after previously appearing as a minor character in <a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Murder_House" title="American Horror Story: Murder House">American Horror Story: Murder House</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
