<p>A <b>hotel</b> is an establishment that provides <a href="/wiki/Lodging" title="Lodging">lodging</a> paid on a short-term basis. Facilities provided may range from a modest-quality mattress in a small room to large suites with bigger, higher-quality beds, a dresser, a fridge and other kitchen facilities, upholstered chairs, a flatscreen television and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/En-suite" title="En-suite">en-suite bathrooms</a>. Small, lower-priced hotels may offer only the most basic guest services and facilities. Larger, higher-priced hotels may provide additional guest facilities such as a swimming pool, business centre (with computers, printers and other office equipment), childcare, conference and event facilities, tennis or basketball courts, gymnasium, restaurants, day spa and social function services. Hotel rooms are usually <a href="/wiki/Room_number" title="Room number">numbered</a> (or named in some smaller hotels and <a href="/wiki/Bed_and_breakfast" title="Bed and breakfast">B&amp;Bs</a>) to allow guests to identify their room. Some boutique, high-end hotels have custom decorated rooms. Some hotels offer meals as part of a room and board arrangement. In the United Kingdom, a hotel is required by law to serve food and drinks to all guests within certain stated hours. In Japan, <a href="/wiki/Capsule_hotel" title="Capsule hotel">capsule hotels</a> provide a tiny room suitable only for sleeping and shared bathroom facilities.</p>
<p>The precursor to the modern hotel was the <a href="/wiki/Inn" title="Inn">inn</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval_Europe" title="Medieval Europe">medieval Europe</a>. For a period of about 200 years from the mid-17th century, <a href="/wiki/Coaching_inn" title="Coaching inn">coaching inns</a> served as a place for lodging for <a href="/wiki/Coach_(carriage)" title="Coach (carriage)">coach</a> travelers. Inns began to cater to richer clients in the mid-18th century. One of the first hotels in a modern sense was opened in <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a> in 1768. Hotels proliferated throughout <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> in the early 19th century, and luxury hotels began to spring up in the later part of the 19th century.</p>
<p>Hotel operations vary in size, function, and cost. Most hotels and major hospitality companies have set industry standards to classify hotel types. An upscale full-service hotel facility offers <a href="/wiki/Luxury_goods" title="Luxury goods">luxury</a> amenities, full service accommodations, an on-site <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurant</a>, and the highest level of personalized service, such as a <a href="/wiki/Concierge" title="Concierge">concierge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Room_service" title="Room service">room service</a> and clothes pressing staff. <a href="/wiki/Conference_and_resort_hotels" title="Conference and resort hotels">Full service hotels</a> often contain upscale full-service facilities with a large number of full service accommodations, an on-site full service <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurant</a>, and a variety of on-site <a href="/wiki/Amenity" title="Amenity">amenities</a>. <a href="/wiki/Boutique_hotel" title="Boutique hotel">Boutique hotels</a> are smaller independent, non-branded hotels that often contain upscale facilities. Small to medium-sized hotel establishments offer a limited amount of on-site <a href="/wiki/Amenity" title="Amenity">amenities</a>. Economy hotels are small to medium-sized hotel establishments that offer basic accommodations with little to no services. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extended_stay_hotel" title="Extended stay hotel">Extended stay hotels</a> are small to medium-sized hotels that offer longer-term full service accommodations compared to a traditional hotel.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Timeshare" title="Timeshare">Timeshare</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Destination_clubs" title="Destination clubs">destination clubs</a> are a form of property ownership involving ownership of an individual unit of accommodation for seasonal usage. A <a href="/wiki/Motel" title="Motel">motel</a> is a small-sized low-rise lodging with direct access to individual rooms from the car park. <a href="/wiki/Boutique_hotel" title="Boutique hotel">Boutique hotels</a> are typically hotels with a unique environment or intimate setting. A number of hotels have entered the public consciousness through popular culture, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ritz_London_Hotel" title="The Ritz London Hotel">Ritz Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Some hotels are built specifically as a destination in itself, for example at <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a> and holiday <a href="/wiki/Resort" title="Resort">resorts</a>.</p>
<p>Most hotel establishments are run by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Manager" title="General Manager">General Manager</a> who serves as the head executive (often referred to as the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotel_Manager" title="Hotel Manager">Hotel Manager</a>"), department heads who oversee various departments within a hotel (e.g., food service), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_manager" title="Middle manager">middle managers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administration_of_business" title="Administration of business">administrative</a> staff, and line-level supervisors. The organizational chart and volume of job positions and hierarchy varies by hotel size, function and class, and is often determined by hotel ownership and managing companies.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The word <i>hotel</i> is derived from the French <i>hôtel</i> (coming from the same origin as <i><a href="/wiki/Hospital" title="Hospital">hospital</a></i>), which referred to a French version of a building seeing frequent visitors, and providing care, rather than a place offering accommodation. In contemporary French usage, <i>hôtel</i> now has the same meaning as the English term, and <i><a href="/wiki/H%C3%B4tel_particulier" title="Hôtel particulier">hôtel particulier</a></i> is used for the old meaning, as well as "hôtel" in some place names such as <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B4tel-Dieu_de_Paris" title="Hôtel-Dieu de Paris">Hôtel-Dieu</a> (in Paris), which has been a hospital since the Middle Ages. The French spelling, with the <a href="/wiki/Circumflex" title="Circumflex">circumflex</a>, was also used in English, but is now rare. The circumflex replaces the 's' found in the earlier <i><a href="/wiki/Hostel" title="Hostel">hostel</a></i> spelling, which over time took on a new, but closely related meaning. Grammatically, hotels usually take the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Definite_article" title="Definite article">definite article</a> – hence "The Astoria Hotel" or simply "The Astoria."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="379" height="224" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg/170px-Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg/255px-Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg/340px-Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tabard_inn_mid19th.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Tabard Inn, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Southwark" title="London Borough of Southwark">Southwark, London</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Facilities offering hospitality to travellers have been a feature of the earliest civilizations. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greco-Roman_culture" title="Greco-Roman culture">Greco-Roman culture</a> hospitals for recuperation and rest were built at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thermal_bath" title="Thermal bath">thermal baths</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> various religious orders at <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monasteries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abbey" title="Abbey">abbeys</a> would offer accommodation for travellers on the road.</p>
<p>The precursor to the modern hotel was the <a href="/wiki/Inn" title="Inn">inn</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval_Europe" title="Medieval Europe">medieval Europe</a>, possibly dating back to the rule of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Rome</a>. These would provide for the needs of travelers, including food and lodging, <a href="/wiki/Stable" title="Stable">stabling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fodder" title="Fodder">fodder</a> for the traveler's horse(s) and fresh horses for the <a href="/wiki/Mail_coach" title="Mail coach">mail coach</a>. Famous London examples of inns include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_George,_Southwark" title="The George, Southwark">George</a> and the <a href="/wiki/The_Tabard" title="The Tabard">Tabard</a>. A typical layout of an inn had an inner court with bedrooms on the two sides, with the <a href="/wiki/Kitchen" title="Kitchen">kitchen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parlour" title="Parlour">parlour</a> at the front and the stables at the back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>For a period of about 200 years from the mid-17th century, <a href="/wiki/Coaching_inn" title="Coaching inn">coaching inns</a> served as a place for lodging for <a href="/wiki/Coach_(carriage)" title="Coach (carriage)">coach</a> travelers (in other words, a <a href="/wiki/Roadhouse_(facility)" title="Roadhouse (facility)">roadhouse</a>). Coaching <a href="/wiki/Inn" title="Inn">inns</a> stabled teams of <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a> for <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach" title="Stagecoach">stagecoaches</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mail_coach" title="Mail coach">mail coaches</a> and replaced tired teams with fresh teams. Traditionally they were seven miles apart but this depended very much on the terrain.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TremontHouse_ca1830s_byJamesBennett_Boston_SimonsUPNE.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="429" data-file-width="531" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/TremontHouse_ca1830s_byJamesBennett_Boston_SimonsUPNE.png/220px-TremontHouse_ca1830s_byJamesBennett_Boston_SimonsUPNE.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/TremontHouse_ca1830s_byJamesBennett_Boston_SimonsUPNE.png/330px-TremontHouse_ca1830s_byJamesBennett_Boston_SimonsUPNE.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/TremontHouse_ca1830s_byJamesBennett_Boston_SimonsUPNE.png/440px-TremontHouse_ca1830s_byJamesBennett_Boston_SimonsUPNE.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Some <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> towns had as many as ten such <a href="/wiki/Inn" title="Inn">inns</a> and rivalry between them was intense, not only for the income from the stagecoach operators but for the revenue for food and drink supplied to the wealthy passengers. By the end of the century, coaching inns were being run more professionally, with a regular timetable being followed and fixed menus for food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Inns began to cater for richer clients in the mid-18th century, and consequently grew in grandeur and the level of service provided. One of the first hotels in a modern sense was <a href="/wiki/Royal_Clarence_Hotel" title="Royal Clarence Hotel">opened</a> in <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a> in 1768, although the idea only really caught on in the early 19th century. In 1812 <a href="/wiki/Claridge%27s" title="Claridge's">Mivart's Hotel</a> opened its doors in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, later changing its name to Claridge's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hotels proliferated throughout <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> in the 19th century, and luxury hotels, including the <a href="/wiki/Savoy_Hotel" title="Savoy Hotel">Savoy Hotel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and the <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Ritz" title="César Ritz">Ritz chain of hotels</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tremont_House_(Boston)" title="Tremont House (Boston)">Tremont House</a> and <a href="/wiki/Astor_House" title="Astor House">Astor House</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> began to spring up in the later part of the century, catering to an extremely wealthy clientele.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Hotel operations vary in size, function, and cost. Most hotels and major hospitality companies that operate hotels have set widely accepted industry standards to classify hotel types. General categories include the following:</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Upscale_luxury"><span id="Upscale"></span>Upscale luxury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Upscale luxury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An upscale full service hotel facility that offers <a href="/wiki/Luxury_goods" title="Luxury goods">luxury</a> amenities, full service accommodations, on-site full service <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurant</a>(s), and the highest level of personalized and professional service. Luxury hotels are normally classified with at least a Four Diamond or <a href="/wiki/AAA_Five_Diamond_Award" title="AAA Five Diamond Award">Five Diamond</a> status or a Four or <a href="/wiki/Star_(classification)" title="Star (classification)">Five Star</a> rating depending on the country and local classification standards. <i>Examples may include: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/InterContinental_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="InterContinental Hotels &amp; Resorts">InterContinental</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waldorf_Astoria_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Waldorf Astoria Hotels and Resorts">Waldorf Astoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Four_Seasons_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Four Seasons Hotels and Resorts">Four Seasons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Hotels" title="Conrad Hotels">Conrad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fairmont_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Fairmont Hotels and Resorts">Fairmont</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ritz-Carlton" title="The Ritz-Carlton">The Ritz-Carlton</a>.</i></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Full_service"><span id="Full"></span>Full service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Full service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Conference_and_resort_hotels" title="Conference and resort hotels">Full service hotels</a> often contain upscale full-service facilities with a large volume of full service accommodations, on-site full service <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurant</a>(s), and a variety of on-site <a href="/wiki/Amenity" title="Amenity">amenities</a> such as swimming pools, a health club, children's activities, ballrooms, on-site conference facilities, and other amenities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Examples include: <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Inn" title="Holiday Inn">Holiday Inn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheraton_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Sheraton Hotels and Resorts">Sheraton</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westin_Hotels" title="Westin Hotels">Westin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Hilton Hotels &amp; Resorts">Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marriott_International" title="Marriott International">Marriott</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hyatt" title="Hyatt">Hyatt</a> hotels.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_inns_and_boutique_hotels"><span id="Boutique"></span>Historic inns and boutique hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Historic inns and boutique hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Boutique_hotel" title="Boutique hotel">Boutique hotels</a> are smaller independent non-branded hotels that often contain upscale facilities of varying size in unique or intimate settings with full service accommodations. Boutique hotels are generally 100 rooms or less.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Some historic inns and boutique hotels may be classified as luxury hotels. Examples include <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Indigo" title="Hotel Indigo">Hotel Indigo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimpton_Hotels_%26_Restaurants" title="Kimpton Hotels &amp; Restaurants">Kimpton Hotels</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Focused_or_select_service"><span id="Focused"></span>Focused or select service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Focused or select service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Small to medium-sized hotel establishments that offer a limited amount of on-site <a href="/wiki/Amenity" title="Amenity">amenities</a> that only cater and market to a specific demographic of travelers, such as the single business traveler. Most focused or select service hotels may still offer full service accommodations but may lack leisure <a href="/wiki/Amenity" title="Amenity">amenities</a> such as an on-site restaurant or a swimming pool. Examples include <a href="/wiki/Crowne_Plaza" title="Crowne Plaza">Crowne Plaza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Courtyard_by_Marriott" title="Courtyard by Marriott">Courtyard by Marriott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Garden_Inn" title="Hilton Garden Inn">Hilton Garden Inn</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_and_limited_service"><span id="Economy"></span>Economy and limited service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy and limited service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Small to medium-sized hotel establishments that offer a very limited amount of on-site <a href="/wiki/Amenity" title="Amenity">amenities</a> and often only offer basic accommodations with little to no services, these facilities normally only cater and market to a specific demographic of travelers, such as the budget-minded traveler seeking a "no frills" accommodation. Limited service hotels often lack an on-site restaurant but in return may offer a limited complimentary food and beverage amenity such as on-site continental breakfast service. Examples include <a href="/wiki/Ibis_Budget" title="Ibis Budget">Ibis Budget</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hampton_Inn" title="Hampton Inn">Hampton Inn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aloft_Hotels" title="Aloft Hotels">Aloft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Inn_Express" title="Holiday Inn Express">Holiday Inn Express</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fairfield_Inn_by_Marriott" title="Fairfield Inn by Marriott">Fairfield Inn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Four_Points_by_Sheraton" title="Four Points by Sheraton">Four Points by Sheraton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Days_Inn" title="Days Inn">Days Inn</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extended_stay"><span id="Extended"></span>Extended stay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Extended stay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extended_stay_hotel" title="Extended stay hotel">Extended stay hotels</a> are small to medium-sized hotels that offer longer term full service accommodations compared to a traditional hotel. Extended stay hotels may offer non-traditional pricing methods such as a weekly rate that cater towards travelers in need of short-term accommodations for an extended period of time. Similar to limited and select service hotels, on-site amenities are normally limited and most extended stay hotels lack an on-site restaurant. Examples include <a href="/wiki/Staybridge_Suites" title="Staybridge Suites">Staybridge Suites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Candlewood_Suites" title="Candlewood Suites">Candlewood Suites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Homewood_Suites_by_Hilton" title="Homewood Suites by Hilton">Homewood Suites by Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home2_Suites_by_Hilton" title="Home2 Suites by Hilton">Home2 Suites by Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Residence_Inn_by_Marriott" title="Residence Inn by Marriott">Residence Inn by Marriott</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Element_by_Westin" title="Element by Westin">Element</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extended_Stay_Hotels" title="Extended Stay Hotels">Extended Stay Hotels</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeshare_and_destination_clubs"><span id="Timeshare"></span>Timeshare and destination clubs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Timeshare and destination clubs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Timeshare" title="Timeshare">Timeshare</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Destination_clubs" title="Destination clubs">Destination clubs</a> are a form of property ownership also referred to as a vacation ownership involving the purchase and ownership of an individual unit of accommodation for seasonal usage during a specified period of time. Timeshare resorts often offer amenities similar that of a Full service hotel with on-site restaurant(s), swimming pools, recreation grounds, and other leisure-oriented amenities. Destination clubs on the other hand may offer more exclusive private accommodations such as private houses in a neighborhood-style setting. Examples of timeshare brands include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hilton_Grand_Vacations" title="Hilton Grand Vacations">Hilton Grand Vacations</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marriott_Vacation_Club_International" title="Marriott Vacation Club International">Marriott Vacation Club International</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westgate_Resorts" title="Westgate Resorts">Westgate Resorts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disney_Vacation_Club" title="Disney Vacation Club">Disney Vacation Club</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Inn" title="Holiday Inn">Holiday Inn Club Vacations</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motel"><span id="Motel"></span>Motel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Motel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Motel" title="Motel">motel</a>, an abbreviation for "motor hotel", is a small-sized low-rise lodging establishment similar to a limited service, lower-cost hotel, but typically with direct access to individual rooms from the car park. Motels were built to serve road travellers, including travellers on <a href="/wiki/Road_trip" title="Road trip">road trip</a> vacations and workers who drive for their job (travelling salespeople, truck drivers, etc.). Common during the 1950s and 1960s, motels were often located adjacent to a major highway, where they were built on inexpensive land at the edge of towns or along stretches of freeway.</p>
<p>New motel construction is rare in the 2000s as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotel_chain" title="Hotel chain">hotel chains</a> have been building economy-priced, limited service franchised properties at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freeway" title="Freeway">freeway</a> exits which compete for largely the same clientele, largely saturating the market by the 1990s. Motels are still useful in less populated areas for driving travelers, but the more populated an area becomes, the more hotels move in to meet the demand for accommodation. Many of the motels which remain in operation have joined national franchise chains, often rebranding themselves as hotels, inns or lodges.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotel_management" title="Hotel management">Hotel management</a></div>
<p>Hotel management is a globally accepted professional career field and academic field of study. Degree programs such as <a href="/wiki/Hospitality_management_studies" title="Hospitality management studies">hospitality management studies</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_degree" title="Business degree">business degree</a>, and/or certification programs formally prepare hotel managers for industry practice.</p>
<p>Most hotel establishments consist of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Manager" title="General Manager">General Manager</a> who serves as the head executive (often referred to as the "Hotel Manager"), department heads who oversee various departments within a hotel, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_manager" title="Middle manager">middle managers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administration_of_business" title="Administration of business">administrative</a> staff, and line-level supervisors. The organizational chart and volume of job positions and hierarchy varies by hotel size, function, and is often determined by hotel ownership and managing companies.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Boutique_hotel" title="Boutique hotel">Boutique hotels</a> are typically hotels with a unique environment or intimate setting. Some hotels have gained their renown through tradition, by hosting significant events or persons, such as Schloss <a href="/wiki/Cecilienhof" title="Cecilienhof">Cecilienhof</a> in <a href="/wiki/Potsdam" title="Potsdam">Potsdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, which derives its fame from the <a href="/wiki/Potsdam_Conference" title="Potsdam Conference">Potsdam Conference</a> of the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> allies <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harry_Truman" title="Harry Truman">Harry Truman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a> in 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taj_Mahal_Palace_%26_Tower" title="Taj Mahal Palace &amp; Tower">Taj Mahal Palace &amp; Tower</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> is one of India's most famous and historic hotels because of its association with the <a href="/wiki/Indian_independence_movement" title="Indian independence movement">Indian independence movement</a>. Some establishments have given name to a particular meal or beverage, as is the case with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waldorf-Astoria_Hotel" title="Waldorf-Astoria Hotel">Waldorf Astoria</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, United States where the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waldorf_Salad" title="Waldorf Salad">Waldorf Salad</a> was first created or the <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Sacher" title="Hotel Sacher">Hotel Sacher</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, Austria, home of the <a href="/wiki/Sachertorte" title="Sachertorte">Sachertorte</a>. Others have achieved fame by association with dishes or cocktails created on their premises, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotel_de_Paris" title="Hotel de Paris">Hotel de Paris</a> where the <a href="/wiki/Cr%C3%AApe_Suzette" title="Crêpe Suzette">crêpe Suzette</a> was invented or the <a href="/wiki/Raffles_Hotel" title="Raffles Hotel">Raffles Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Sling" title="Singapore Sling">Singapore Sling</a> cocktail was devised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>A number of hotels have entered the public consciousness through popular culture, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ritz_London_Hotel" title="The Ritz London Hotel">Ritz Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, through its association with <a href="/wiki/Irving_Berlin" title="Irving Berlin">Irving Berlin's</a> song, '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puttin%27_on_the_Ritz" title="Puttin' on the Ritz">Puttin' on the Ritz</a>'. The <a href="/wiki/Algonquin_Hotel" title="Algonquin Hotel">Algonquin Hotel</a> in New York City is famed as the meeting place of the literary group, the <a href="/wiki/Algonquin_Round_Table" title="Algonquin Round Table">Algonquin Round Table</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Chelsea" title="Hotel Chelsea">Hotel Chelsea</a>, also in New York City, has been the subject of a number of songs and the scene of the stabbing of <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Spungen" title="Nancy Spungen">Nancy Spungen</a> (allegedly by her boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Sid_Vicious" title="Sid Vicious">Sid Vicious</a>).</p>

<p>Some hotels are built specifically as a destination in itself to create a captive trade, example at <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amusement_park" title="Amusement park">amusement parks</a> and holiday <a href="/wiki/Resort" title="Resort">resorts</a>. Though of course hotels have always been built in popular destinations, the defining characteristic of a resort hotel is that it exists purely to serve another attraction, the two having the same owners.</p>
<p>On the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip" title="Las Vegas Strip">Las Vegas Strip</a> there is a tradition of <a href="/wiki/One-upmanship" title="One-upmanship">one-upmanship</a> with luxurious and extravagant hotels in a concentrated area. This trend now has extended to other resorts worldwide, but the concentration in Las Vegas is still the world's highest: nineteen of the world's twenty-five largest hotels by room count are on the Strip, with a total of over 67,000 rooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Europe <a href="/wiki/Center_Parcs" title="Center Parcs">Center Parcs</a> might be considered a chain of resort hotels, since the sites are largely man-made (though set in natural surroundings such as <a href="/wiki/Country_park" title="Country park">country parks</a>) with captive trade, whereas <a href="/wiki/Holiday_camp" title="Holiday camp">holiday camps</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Butlins" title="Butlins">Butlins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontin%27s" title="Pontin's">Pontin's</a> are probably not considered as resort hotels, since they are set at traditional holiday destinations which existed before the camps.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cave_hotels">Cave hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Cave hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Cuevas Pedro Antonio de Alarcón (named after the <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Antonio_de_Alarc%C3%B3n" title="Pedro Antonio de Alarcón">author</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Guadix" title="Guadix">Guadix</a>, Spain, as well as several hotels in <a href="/wiki/Cappadocia" title="Cappadocia">Cappadocia</a>, Turkey, are notable for being built into natural <a href="/wiki/Cave" title="Cave">cave</a> formations, some with rooms underground. The Desert Cave Hotel in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coober_Pedy,_South_Australia" title="Coober Pedy, South Australia">Coober Pedy, South Australia</a> is built into the remains of an <a href="/wiki/Opal" title="Opal">opal</a> mine.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Day_room_hotels">Day room hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Day room hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some hotels fill daytime occupancy with <a href="/wiki/Day_room_(hotel)" title="Day room (hotel)">day rooms</a>, for example, <a href="/wiki/Rodeway_Inn_and_Suites" title="Rodeway Inn and Suites">Rodeway Inn and Suites</a> near <a href="/wiki/Port_Everglades" title="Port Everglades">Port Everglades</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale" title="Fort Lauderdale">Fort Lauderdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chris_Cruises_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chris_Cruises-15">[15]</a></sup> Day rooms are booked in a block of hours typically between 8 am and 5 pm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNBC_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNBC-16">[16]</a></sup> before the typical night shift. These are similar to transit hotels in that they appeal to travelers, however, unlike transit hotels, they do not eliminate the need to go through Customs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Garden_hotels">Garden hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Garden hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Garden_hotels" title="Garden hotels">Garden hotels</a>, famous for their gardens before they became hotels, include Gravetye Manor, the home of garden designer <a href="/wiki/William_Robinson_(gardener)" title="William Robinson (gardener)">William Robinson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cliveden" title="Cliveden">Cliveden</a>, designed by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Barry" title="Charles Barry">Charles Barry</a> with a rose garden by <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Jellicoe" title="Geoffrey Jellicoe">Geoffrey Jellicoe</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ice.2C_snow_and_igloo_hotels">Ice, snow and igloo hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Ice, snow and igloo hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ice_hotel" title="Ice hotel">Ice hotel</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Icehotel_entre_ms.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="750" data-file-width="500" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Icehotel_entre_ms.jpg/220px-Icehotel_entre_ms.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Icehotel_entre_ms.jpg/330px-Icehotel_entre_ms.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Icehotel_entre_ms.jpg/440px-Icehotel_entre_ms.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

Ice Hotel in Jukkasjärvi, Sweden</div>

<p>The Ice Hotel in <a href="/wiki/Jukkasj%C3%A4rvi" title="Jukkasjärvi">Jukkasjärvi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, was the first ice hotel in the world; first built in 1990, it is built each winter and melts every spring. Other ice hotels include the Igloo Village in Kakslauttanen, Finland, and the Hotel de Glace in Duschenay, Canada. They can also be included within larger ice complexes; for example, the Mammut Snow Hotel in Finland is located within the walls of the <a href="/wiki/Kemi" title="Kemi">Kemi</a> snow castle; and the Lainio Snow Hotel is part of a snow village near <a href="/wiki/Yll%C3%A4s" title="Ylläs">Ylläs</a>, Finland.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Referral_hotel">Referral hotel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Referral hotel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Referral_chain" title="Referral chain">Referral chain</a></div>
<p>A referral hotel is a hotel chain that offers branding to independently-operated hotels; the chain itself is founded by or owned by the member hotels as a group. Many former referral chains have been converted to franchises; the largest surviving member-owned chain is <a href="/wiki/Best_Western" title="Best Western">Best Western</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Railway_hotels">Railway hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Railway hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Frequently, expanding railway companies built grand hotels at their termini, such as the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Hotel,_Manchester" title="Midland Hotel, Manchester">Midland Hotel, Manchester</a> next to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Central_railway_station" title="Manchester Central railway station">the former Manchester Central Station</a>, and in London the ones above <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">St Pancras railway station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charing_Cross_railway_station" title="Charing Cross railway station">Charing Cross railway station</a>. London also has the Chiltern Court Hotel above <a href="/wiki/Baker_Street_tube_station" title="Baker Street tube station">Baker Street tube station</a>, there are also <a href="/wiki/Canada%27s_grand_railway_hotels" title="Canada's grand railway hotels">Canada's grand railway hotels</a>. They are or were mostly, but not exclusively, used by those traveling by rail.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Straw_bale_hotels">Straw bale hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Straw bale hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Maya Guesthouse in Nax Mont-Noble in the Swiss Alps, is the first hotel in Europe built entirely with straw bales. Due to the insulation values of the walls it needs no conventional heating or air conditioning system, although the Maya Guesthouse is built at an altitude of 1,300 metres (4,300 ft) in the Alps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transit_hotels">Transit hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Transit hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transit_Hotel" title="Transit Hotel">Transit Hotel</a></div>
<p>Transit hotels are short stay hotels typically used at international airports where passengers can stay while waiting to change airplanes. The hotels are typically on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airside_(airport)" title="Airside (airport)">airside</a> and do not require a visa for a stay or re-admission through security checkpoints.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Treehouse_hotels">Treehouse hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Treehouse hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some hotels are built with living trees as structural elements, for example the Treehotel near <a href="/wiki/Pite%C3%A5" title="Piteå">Piteå</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, the Costa Rica Tree House in the Gandoca-Manzanillo Wildlife Refuge, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Treetops_Hotel" title="Treetops Hotel">Treetops Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Aberdare_National_Park" title="Aberdare National Park">Aberdare National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Ariau_Towers" title="Ariau Towers">Ariau Towers</a> near <a href="/wiki/Manaus" title="Manaus">Manaus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Negro_(Amazon)" title="Rio Negro (Amazon)">Rio Negro</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Basin" title="Amazon Basin">Amazon</a>; and Bayram's Tree Houses in <a href="/wiki/Olympos_(Lycia)" title="Olympos (Lycia)">Olympos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Largest">Largest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Largest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_largest_hotels_in_the_world" title="List of largest hotels in the world">List of largest hotels in the world</a></div>
<p>In 2006, <i><a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a></i> listed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_Hotel" title="First World Hotel">First World Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Genting_Highlands" title="Genting Highlands">Genting Highlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, as the world's largest hotel with a total of 6,118 rooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Izmailovo_Hotel" title="Izmailovo Hotel">Izmailovo Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> has the most rooms, with 7,500, followed by <a href="/wiki/The_Venetian_Las_Vegas" title="The Venetian Las Vegas">The Venetian</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Palazzo" title="The Palazzo">The Palazzo</a> complex in Las Vegas (7,117 rooms) and <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand Las Vegas</a> complex (6,852 rooms).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oldest">Oldest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Oldest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_Book_of_World_Records" title="Guinness Book of World Records">Guinness Book of World Records</a>, the oldest hotel in operation is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nisiyama_Onsen_Keiunkan_in_Yamanashi" title="Nisiyama Onsen Keiunkan in Yamanashi">Nisiyama Onsen Keiunkan in Yamanashi</a>, Japan. The hotel, first opened in 707 A.D. has been operated by the same family for forty-six generations. The title was held until 2011 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hoshi_Ryokan" title="Hoshi Ryokan">Hoshi Ryokan</a>, in the Awazu Onsen area of <a href="/wiki/Komatsu,_Ishikawa" title="Komatsu, Ishikawa">Komatsu</a>, Japan, which opened in the year 718, as the history of the Nisiyama Onsen Keiunkan was virtually unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Highest">Highest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Highest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Ritz-Carlton,_Hong_Kong" title="The Ritz-Carlton, Hong Kong">The Ritz-Carlton, Hong Kong</a> claims to be the world's highest hotel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> It is located on the top floors of the <a href="/wiki/International_Commerce_Centre" title="International Commerce Centre">International Commerce Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, at 484 metres (1,588 ft) above ground level.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Living_in_hotels">Living in hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Living in hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A number of public figures have notably chosen to take up semi-permanent or permanent residence in hotels.</p>
<ul>
<li>Fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Coco_Chanel" title="Coco Chanel">Coco Chanel</a> lived in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H%C3%B4tel_Ritz" title="Hôtel Ritz">Hôtel Ritz</a>, Paris, on and off for more than 30 years.</li>
<li>Inventor <a href="/wiki/Nikola_Tesla" title="Nikola Tesla">Nikola Tesla</a> lived the last ten years of his life at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Yorker_Hotel" title="New Yorker Hotel">New Yorker Hotel</a> until he died in his room in 1943.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Larry_Fine" title="Larry Fine">Larry Fine</a> (of <a href="/wiki/The_Three_Stooges" title="The Three Stooges">The Three Stooges</a>) and his family lived in hotels, due to his extravagant spending habits and his wife's dislike for housekeeping. They first lived in the President Hotel in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">Atlantic City, New Jersey</a>, where his daughter Phyllis was raised, then the Knickerbocker Hotel in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>. Not until the late 1940s did Larry buy a home in the <a href="/wiki/Los_Feliz,_Los_Angeles" title="Los Feliz, Los Angeles">Los Feliz area of Los Angeles</a>.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waldorf-Astoria_Hotel" title="Waldorf-Astoria Hotel">Waldorf-Astoria Hotel</a> and its affiliated Waldorf Towers has been the home of many famous persons over the years including former President <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Hoover</a> who lived there from the end of his presidency in 1933 until his death in 1964. General <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a> lived his last 14 years in the penthouse of the Waldorf Towers. And the composer <a href="/wiki/Cole_Porter" title="Cole Porter">Cole Porter</a> also spent the last 25 years of his life in an apartment at the Waldorf Towers.</li>
<li>Millionaire <a href="/wiki/Howard_Hughes" title="Howard Hughes">Howard Hughes</a> lived in hotels during the last ten years of his life (1966–76), primarily in Las Vegas, as well as <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills" title="Beverly Hills">Beverly Hills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Bahamas" title="Freeport, Bahamas">Freeport</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Managua" title="Managua">Managua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, and others.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Nabokov" title="Vladimir Nabokov">Vladimir Nabokov</a> and his wife Vera lived in the <a href="/wiki/Montreux_Palace" title="Montreux Palace">Montreux Palace</a> Hotel in <a href="/wiki/Montreux" title="Montreux">Montreux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> (1961-his death in 1977).</li>
<li>Actor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Harris" title="Richard Harris">Richard Harris</a> lived at the Savoy Hotel while in London. Hotel archivist Susan Scott recounts an anecdote that, when he was being taken out of the building on a stretcher shortly before his death in 2002, he raised his hand and told the diners "it was the food."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
