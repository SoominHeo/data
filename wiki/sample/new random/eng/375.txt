<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Shan State Cultural Museum</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Shan State Cultural Museum</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museum</a> located at Bogyoke Aung San Road and Eintawshay Road, in <a href="/wiki/Taunggyi" title="Taunggyi">Taunggyi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a>.</p>
<p>It displays divans, swords, fans, chairs used by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sawbwa" title="Sawbwa">Sawbwa</a> (Shan Chief of former times), old <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">paintings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coin" title="Coin">coins</a> and traditional costumes of the <a href="/wiki/Shan_people" title="Shan people">Shan</a> races.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was established in March 1989. Admission fee is $2 US and opening hours are from 10:00 am to 3:30 pm (from Tuesday to Sunday).</p>
