<p><b>Prignitz</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ËpÊiËÉ¡nÉªts]</a></span>) is a <i>Kreis</i> (district) in the northwestern part of <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Neighboring are (from the north clockwise) the district <a href="/wiki/Ludwigslust-Parchim" title="Ludwigslust-Parchim">Ludwigslust-Parchim</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mecklenburg-Western_Pomerania" title="Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania">Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania</a>, the district <a href="/wiki/Ostprignitz-Ruppin" title="Ostprignitz-Ruppin">Ostprignitz-Ruppin</a> in Brandenburg, the district <a href="/wiki/Stendal_(district)" title="Stendal (district)">Stendal</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saxony-Anhalt" title="Saxony-Anhalt">Saxony-Anhalt</a> and the district <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCchow-Dannenberg" title="LÃ¼chow-Dannenberg">LÃ¼chow-Dannenberg</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prignitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term Prignitz originally meant the region north of the confluence of the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Havel" title="Havel">Havel</a> rivers. This region is larger than the district. It also includes the town of <a href="/wiki/Havelberg" title="Havelberg">Havelberg</a> in Saxony-Anhalt and large portions of the neighbouring district of <a href="/wiki/Ostprignitz-Ruppin" title="Ostprignitz-Ruppin">Ostprignitz-Ruppin</a>.</p>
<p>The Elbe river forms the southwestern border of the district.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prignitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The historical region Prignitz consisted of the following eleven districts, established in the 13th century: <a href="/wiki/Wittenberge" title="Wittenberge">Wittenberge</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lenzen" title="Lenzen">Lenzen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perleberg" title="Perleberg">Perleberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Putlitz" title="Putlitz">Putlitz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyritz" title="Kyritz">Kyritz</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nitzow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nitzow (page does not exist)">Nitzow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wittstock" title="Wittstock">Wittstock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pritzwalk" title="Pritzwalk">Pritzwalk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Havelberg" title="Havelberg">Havelberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wusterhausen" title="Wusterhausen">Wusterhausen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grabow" title="Grabow">Grabow</a>.</p>
<p>The present district of Prignitz was created in 1993 by merging the previous districts of Pritzwalk and Perleberg and a few municipalities from the district Kyritz. The westernmost part of the district was previously part of Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania and became part of Brandenburg on August 1, 1992.</p>
<p>The earlier district of Prignitz was the buffer between Brandenburg and Mecklenburg. It was resettled by Germans, especially from the Bremen area, following the First <a href="/wiki/Wendish_Crusade" title="Wendish Crusade">Wendish Crusade</a> of 1147. The rate of German settlement increased over the following decades. The eastern half was dominated by the pro-German counts <a href="/wiki/Von_Plotho" title="Von Plotho">von Plotho</a> who brought their own vassals such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Von_Blumenthal" title="Von Blumenthal">von Blumenthal</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Von_Grabow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Von Grabow (page does not exist)">von Grabow</a> families with them. The western half was dominated by robber barons, especially the <a href="/wiki/Gans_zu_Putlitz" title="Gans zu Putlitz">Gans zu Putlitz</a> family and their vassals, the <a href="/wiki/Von_Quitzow" title="Von Quitzow">von Quitzows</a>. When Frederick, Count of Zollern was appointed Margrave in 1411, he faced an uprising of the <a href="/wiki/Wends" title="Wends">Wendish</a> nobility, supported by the Wendish Duke of Mecklenburg. However, he was able to put down the revolt at the battle of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cremmer_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cremmer Dam (page does not exist)">Cremmer Dam</a>, with the support of the German nobility. Families who had stayed loyal were rewarded. Otto <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Von_Blumenthal" title="Von Blumenthal">von Blumenthal</a>, for example, was made Captain of the Prignitz from 1415 to 1422 and of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lenzen" title="Lenzen">Lenzen</a> from 1420.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prignitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table>
<tr valign="top">
<td>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:132px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="583" data-file-width="512" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg/130px-Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg/195px-Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg/260px-Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg.png 2x" width="130"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wappen_des_Landkreises_Prignitz.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Coat of arms</div>
</div>
</div>
</td>
<td>The coat of arms shows a goose preparing for flight, the symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Gans_zu_Putlitz" title="Gans zu Putlitz">Gans zu Putlitz</a> family (<i>Gans</i> is <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> for goose), who in the 12th century were one of a number of families (such as the <a href="/wiki/Von_Plotho" title="Von Plotho">von Plotho</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Von_Blumenthal" title="Von Blumenthal">von Blumenthal</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Von_Arnim_family&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Von Arnim family (page does not exist)">von Arnim</a> families) who introduced Christianity and German culture to Prignitz. The pearls around the goose symbolize Perleberg (which translates to <i>Pearl Mountain</i>). The wolf at bottom stands for the former administrative seat, Pritzwalk, as <i>walk</i> derives from the <a href="/wiki/Slavic_languages" title="Slavic languages">Slavonic</a> <i>wolk</i> meaning wolf. The wavy separation between the top and bottom symbolizes the river Elbe; the colors red and white are those of Brandenburg.</td>
</tr>
</table>
