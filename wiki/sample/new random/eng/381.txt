<p>The <b>Archdiocese of Daegu</b> (previously known as Taiku or Taegu) is a <a href="/wiki/Particular_church" title="Particular church">particular church</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Rite" title="Latin Rite">Latin Rite</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>. The <b>Archbishop of Daegu</b>, whose <a href="/wiki/Cathedra" title="Cathedra">seat</a> is at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kyesan_Cathedral&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kyesan Cathedral (page does not exist)">Kyesan Cathedral</a> in <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_bishop" title="Metropolitan bishop">Metropolitan bishop</a> for the Dioceses of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Andong" title="Diocese of Andong">Andong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Cheongju" title="Diocese of Cheongju">Cheongju</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Masan" title="Diocese of Masan">Masan</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Busan" title="Diocese of Busan">Busan</a>.</p>
<p>It is the second oldest <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_see" title="Episcopal see">episcopal see</a> in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, erected as an <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_vicariate" title="Apostolic vicariate">apostolic vicariate</a> on April 8, 1911 from the Apostolic Vicariate of Korea. It was elevated to archdiocesan status on March 10, 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gcat_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gcat-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
