<p><b>Elvedin Džinić</b> (born 25 August 1985) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slovenian_people" title="Slovenian people">Slovenian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/NK_Celje" title="NK Celje">Celje</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elvedin_D%C5%BEini%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005, he signed his first professional contract with <a href="/wiki/NK_Maribor" title="NK Maribor">Maribor</a>. He played for the club, between 2005 and 2010, and made a total of 140 appearances and scored 16 goals in Slovenian first league, <a href="/wiki/Slovenian_PrvaLiga" title="Slovenian PrvaLiga">1.SNL</a>. He was voted, by the fans, with the title of <i>Purple Warrior trophy</i>, as the best player in the 2009–10 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 January, Elvedin Džinić signed a contract with Belgian club RSC Charleroi. Maribor had accepted the bid in excess of 132,000 euros for the player, whose contract with Maribor was due to expire in 6 months.</p>
<p>On 25 June 2013, Džinić signed a two-year contract with <a href="/wiki/PFC_Botev_Plovdiv" title="PFC Botev Plovdiv">Botev Plovdiv</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elvedin_D%C5%BEini%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Džinić was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia national football team</a> and was part of the squad at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
