<p>The <b>King of Saudi Arabia</b> is <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Absolute_monarch" title="Absolute monarch">absolute monarch</a> (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a>). He serves as the head of the Saudi monarchy — <a href="/wiki/House_of_Saud" title="House of Saud">House of Saud</a>. The King is called the <b><a href="/wiki/Custodian_of_the_Two_Holy_Mosques" title="Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques">Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques</a></b> (<span lang="ar">خادم الحرمين الشريفين</span>). The title, which signifies Saudi Arabia's jurisdiction over the mosques of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masjid_al_Haram" title="Masjid al Haram">Masjid al Haram</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masjid_al-Nabawi" title="Masjid al-Nabawi">Masjid al-Nabawi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Medina" title="Medina">Medina</a>, replaced <a href="/wiki/Majesty" title="Majesty">His Majesty</a> (<span lang="ar">صاحب الجلالة</span>) in 1986.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King_of_Saudi_Arabia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="History of Saudi Arabia">History of Saudi Arabia</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ibn_Saud" title="Ibn Saud">King Abdulaziz</a> (also known as Ibn Saud) began conquering today's Saudi Arabia in 1902, by restoring his family as emirs of <a href="/wiki/Riyadh" title="Riyadh">Riyadh</a>. He then proceeded to conquer first the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejd" title="Nejd">Nejd</a> (1922) and then the <a href="/wiki/Hejaz" title="Hejaz">Hejaz</a> (1925). He progressed from Sultan of Nejd, to King of Hejaz and Nejd, and finally to King of Saudi Arabia in 1932.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Succession">Succession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King_of_Saudi_Arabia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Succession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Succession_to_the_Saudi_Arabian_throne" title="Succession to the Saudi Arabian throne">Succession to the Saudi Arabian throne</a></div>
<p>The kings since Ibn Saud's death have all been his sons, and all likely immediate successors to the reigning King Salman will be from his progeny. Sons of Ibn Saud are considered to have primary claim on the throne of Saudi Arabia. This makes the Saudi monarchy quite distinct from Western monarchies, which usually feature large, clearly defined <a href="/wiki/Royal_family" title="Royal family">royal families</a> and orders of succession. The current Crown Prince is the first grandson of Ibn Saud to be in the line of succession.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_position">Legal position</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King_of_Saudi_Arabia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal position">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saudi Arabia is ruled by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_law" title="Islamic law">Islamic law</a> (Sharia) and purports to be an <a href="/wiki/Islamic_state" title="Islamic state">Islamic state</a>, but many Muslims see a <a href="/wiki/Hereditary_monarchy" title="Hereditary monarchy">hereditary monarchy</a> as being a discouraged system of government in <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_functions">Other functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King_of_Saudi_Arabia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The King of Saudi Arabia is also considered the Head of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Saud" title="House of Saud">House of Saud</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="Prime Minister of Saudi Arabia">Prime Minister</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Crown_Prince_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia">Crown Prince</a> is also the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister" title="Deputy Prime Minister">Deputy Prime Minister</a>". The kings after Faisal have named a "second Deputy Prime Minister" as the subsequent heir after the Crown Prince.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_Standard">Royal Standard</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King_of_Saudi_Arabia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Royal Standard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="Flag of Saudi Arabia">Flag of Saudi Arabia</a></div>
<p>The Royal Standard consists of a green flag, with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> inscription and a sword featured in white, and with the <a href="/wiki/Emblem_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="Emblem of Saudi Arabia">national emblem</a> embroidered in gold in the lower right canton.</p>
<div class="center">
<div class="thumb tnone">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="750" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg/220px-Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg/330px-Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg/440px-Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Royal_Standard_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Royal Standard of the King</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The script on the flag is written in the <a href="/wiki/Thuluth" title="Thuluth">Thuluth script</a>. It is the <i><a href="/wiki/Shahada" title="Shahada">shahada</a></i> or Islamic declaration of faith:</p>
<dl>
<dd><span lang="ar">لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا الله مُحَمَّدٌ رَسُولُ الله</span></dd>
<dd><i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="DIN 31635 Arabic">lā ʾilāha ʾillā-llāh, muhammadun rasūlu-llāh</span></i></dd>
<dd><i>There is no other god but <a href="/wiki/God_in_Islam" title="God in Islam">God</a>, Muhammad is the messenger of God.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
