<p><b>…And You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead</b> (often abbreviated as <b>Trail of Dead</b>) are an American <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. The chief members of the band are <a href="/wiki/Jason_Reece" title="Jason Reece">Jason Reece</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Keely" title="Conrad Keely">Conrad Keely</a> (formerly Conrad Sobsamai). The two switch between drumming, guitar and lead vocals, both on recordings and live shows. The band is known for their wild, energetic concerts. Their ninth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/IX_(...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead_album)" title="IX (...And You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead album)">IX</a></i>, was released in November 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Keely and Reece have been friends since their youth; they met originally in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>. They each formed their first band in 1993, soon after moving to <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Washington" title="Olympia, Washington">Olympia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_(U.S._state)" title="Washington (U.S. state)">Washington</a>, where Keely studied at <a href="/wiki/The_Evergreen_State_College" title="The Evergreen State College">The Evergreen State College</a>. Keely started a band called Benedict Gehlen that lasted only a year. Nancyville was Keely's next project, though it also was short-lived. Reece played drums with Honeybucket and then <a href="/wiki/Queercore" title="Queercore">queercore</a> band <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mukilteo_Fairies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mukilteo Fairies (page does not exist)">Mukilteo Fairies</a> (the name is a play on words, referencing the ferry boat that travels between Mukilteo and Clinton, Washington) while living in Olympia. The two moved to <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a> and started playing as a duo under the moniker You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead. The band expanded to include guitarist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_Allen_(guitarist)" title="Kevin Allen (guitarist)">Kevin Allen</a> and bassist Neil Busch. They then officially lengthened their name to ...And You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead. Conrad's girlfriend from Olympia, Deanne Rowley (later Deanne McAdams), briefly played with them<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> as a live back-up guitarist.</p>
<p>According to the band's website, their name is taken from an ancient <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Mayan</a> ritual chant, which shows a striking similarity to an <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">ancient Egyptian</a> chant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This explanation, however, is said to be merely an ongoing joke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_and_success_.281995.E2.80.932006.29">Rise and success (1995–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rise and success (1995–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>By 1995, they had issued their first release (one song on cassette-only compilation called <i>Austin Live Houses</i>), followed by another self-titled four-song cassette on Golden Hour, and in January 1998 a <a href="/wiki/...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead_(album)" title="...And You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead (album)">self-titled</a> full-length on <a href="/wiki/Trance_Syndicate" title="Trance Syndicate">Trance Syndicate</a>.</p>
<p>Trail of Dead soon moved on to <a href="/wiki/Merge_Records" title="Merge Records">Merge Records</a> after the Trance label folded. The band released <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead_album)" title="Madonna (...And You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead album)"><i>Madonna</i></a> on Merge in late 1999, and toured the U.S. opening for labelmates <a href="/wiki/Superchunk" title="Superchunk">Superchunk</a> to promote it. They signed to <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and released the <i><a href="/wiki/Relative_Ways" title="Relative Ways">Relative Ways</a></i> EP in 2001.</p>
<p>In early 2002, the Trail of Dead released <i><a href="/wiki/Source_Tags_%26_Codes" title="Source Tags &amp; Codes">Source Tags &amp; Codes</a></i>, which received universal acclaim, including a 10/10 rating from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> later saying it is "one of indie rock's truly epic albums".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Their following tour was chronicled by <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, with Andrew Dansby commenting on their group dynamic as a "<a href="/wiki/Post-punk" title="Post-punk">post-punk</a> <a href="/wiki/Voltron" title="Voltron">Voltron</a>, that just might be the most exciting unit working today."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>A follow-up EP to <i>Source Tags &amp; Codes</i> was released in April 2003, titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Secret_of_Elena%27s_Tomb" title="The Secret of Elena's Tomb">The Secret of Elena's Tomb</a></i>. The EP featured contained the electronic track "Intelligence" which featured, and was co-written by, Tyler Jacobson from <a href="/wiki/A_Roman_Scandal_(band)" title="A Roman Scandal (band)">A Roman Scandal</a>, Reece's other band.</p>
<p>In July 2004, it was announced that Neil Busch had been removed from the band and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danny_Wood_(bass_player)" title="Danny Wood (bass player)">Danny Wood</a> would be taking his place on bass. Second drummer/percussionist Doni Schroader also joined the band around this time. Trail of Dead released <i><a href="/wiki/Worlds_Apart_(...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead_album)" title="Worlds Apart (...And You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead album)">Worlds Apart</a></i> on January 25, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Music videos were filmed for singles "And the Rest Will Follow" and "Caterwaul". The band also added live keyboardist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=David_Longoria_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="David Longoria (musician) (page does not exist)">David Longoria</a> to their touring lineup. Now a six-piece, the band toured heavily on the album, with its trademark explosive live performances and a two-drummer attack. Opening for part of this tour was Longoria's band <a href="/wiki/The_Black" title="The Black">The Black</a>, which featured Longoria on vocals and guitarist Alan Schaefer, with the rest of TOD filling out the group. The band's fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/So_Divided" title="So Divided">So Divided</a></i>, was released on November 14, 2006. Clay Morris replaced Longoria as live keyboardist, and eventually became a full-time member.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Interscope_era_.282007.E2.80.93present.29">Post-Interscope era (2007–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...And_You_Will_Know_Us_by_the_Trail_of_Dead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-Interscope era (2007–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late summer of 2007, the Trail of Dead once again toured Europe, this time with drummer Aaron Ford replacing Doni Schroader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Danny Wood was absent from these performances, having quietly left the band. He would eventually be replaced by <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Up!" title="Apollo Up!">Jay Leo Phillips</a>. The band provided the soundtrack to the 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_on_Wheels_(2007_film)" title="Hell on Wheels (2007 film)">Hell on Wheels</a></i>, a documentary about the formation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roller_Derby" title="Roller Derby">Roller Derby</a> in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>. After their overseas tour ended on March 15, 2007, the Trail of Dead returned to the studio to begin recording their sixth record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In October they supported the virtual band <a href="/wiki/Dethklok" title="Dethklok">Dethklok</a> on an <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> college tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In that same month, the band became independent, having left <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> after lack of support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2008, Trail of Dead went back into the studio, without a record contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Using their own capital, they filmed their recording experiences and released them on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On October 21, they released an EP, resembling an "album teaser", titled <i><a href="/wiki/Festival_Thyme" title="Festival Thyme">Festival Thyme</a></i> on Richter Scale Records. On February 17, 2009, a 13-track album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Century_of_Self" title="The Century of Self">The Century of Self</a></i> was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In September 2010, Conrad Keely posted news of an upcoming album 2011 release, produced by <a href="/wiki/Chris_%22Frenchie%22_Smith" title='Chris "Frenchie" Smith'>Chris "Frenchie" Smith</a>, with an accompanying comic book.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Frenchie Smith is also set to re-mix their debut eponymous album. On November 10, 2010, it was announced that their seventh album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tao_of_the_Dead" title="Tao of the Dead">Tao of the Dead</a></i>, would be released in North America on February 8, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> It was also revealed that the band had stripped down to a core four-piece, and that Jay Phillips, Clay Morris and longtime guitarist Kevin Allen had been removed from the lineup.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Circumstances of these departures are currently unknown. Aaron Ford recorded all of the drums for <a href="/wiki/Tao_of_the_Dead" title="Tao of the Dead">Tao of the Dead</a>, but has left the band. The band performed live on <a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Jimmy_Fallon" title="Late Night with Jimmy Fallon">Late Night with Jimmy Fallon</a> on February 7, 2011, debuting bassist Autry Fulbright II (from Reece side project Midnight Masses) and drummer/guitarist Jamie Miller, of the band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TheSTART" title="TheSTART">theSTART</a>. On February 17, 2011, it was announced that Jason Reece would <a href="/wiki/Guest_host" title="Guest host">guest host</a> Toby Ryan's radio show on Austin's <a href="/wiki/KROX-FM" title="KROX-FM">KROX-FM</a> (101X).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, the band recorded material for a new album in <a href="/wiki/Hanover" title="Hanover">Hanover</a>, Germany with producer, Chris "Frenchie" Smith.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On August 22 the band announced their eighth studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lost_Songs_(...And_You_Will_Know_Us_By_the_Trail_of_Dead_album)" title="Lost Songs (...And You Will Know Us By the Trail of Dead album)">Lost Songs</a></i>, and released song entitled "Up To Infinity", which is dedicated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pussy_riot" title="Pussy riot">Pussy Riot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lost_Songs_Announce_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lost_Songs_Announce-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cover_art" title="Cover art">Cover art</a> was revealed on September 6, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On September 25, they released "Catatonic" through <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2013, the band announced a forthcoming EP <i>Tao of the Dead Part III</i> via Twitter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Using <a href="/wiki/PledgeMusic" title="PledgeMusic">PledgeMusic</a>, they are taking pre-orders for special editions of the EP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In February 2014, the band announced a limited U.S. tour focused on playing music from <i>Source Tags and Codes</i> and plans to record their next album, a sequel to <i>Tao of the Dead</i>, in the spring and summer of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In April 2014, the album <i>Live at Rockpalast 2009</i> was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In August 2014, the band announced their ninth album, <i>IX</i>, using an online <a href="/wiki/Jigsaw_puzzle" title="Jigsaw puzzle">jigsaw puzzle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
