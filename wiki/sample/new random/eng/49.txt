<p><b>Shimonoseki Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">下関駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shimonoseki-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanyo_Main_Line" title="Sanyo Main Line">Sanyo Main Line</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR West) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shimonoseki,_Yamaguchi" title="Shimonoseki, Yamaguchi">Shimonoseki, Yamaguchi</a>, Japan. <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_Railway_Company" title="Kyushu Railway Company">Kyushu Railway Company</a> (JR Kyushu) and <a href="/wiki/Japan_Freight_Railway_Company" title="Japan Freight Railway Company">Japan Freight Railway Company</a> (JR Freight) services also use this station. The company boundary between JR West and JR Kyushu is at the west end of this station where there is an entrance signal from <a href="/wiki/Moji_Station" title="Moji Station">Moji</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>27 May 1901: Opened as Bakan (馬関) Station of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sany%C5%8D_Railway" title="Sanyō Railway">San'yō Railway</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">山陽鉄道</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>San'yō Tetsudō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. Kanmon Renrakusen (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%96%A2%E9%96%80%E9%80%A3%E7%B5%A1%E8%88%B9" title="ja:関門連絡船">関門連絡船</a>, Shimonoseki Moji Railway Ferry) was started. The station was located about 700m east from today's location.</li>
<li>1 June 1902: Changed to Shimonoseki Station according to the change of the city name.</li>
<li>11 September 1905: San'yō Railway started Kanfu Renrakusen (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%96%A2%E9%87%9C%E9%80%A3%E7%B5%A1%E8%88%B9" title="ja:関釜連絡船">関釜連絡船</a>, Shimonoseki <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pusan" title="Pusan">Pusan</a> Railway Ferry).</li>
<li>1 December 1906: San'yō Railway was nationarized.</li>
<li>20 December 1930: Kawasaki Ship (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%B7%9D%E5%B4%8E%E6%B1%BD%E8%88%B9" title="ja:川崎汽船">川崎汽船</a>) started Kanrei Renrakusen (関麗連絡船, Shimonseki <a href="/wiki/Yeosu" title="Yeosu">Yeosu</a> Railway Ferry).</li>
<li>27 November 1938: Sanyō Electric Tram (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%B1%B1%E9%99%BD%E9%9B%BB%E6%B0%97%E8%BB%8C%E9%81%93" title="ja:山陽電気軌道">山陽電気軌道</a>) Station was open in front of the station.</li>
<li>1 July 1942: <a href="/wiki/Kanmon_Tunnel" title="Kanmon Tunnel">Kanmon Tunnel</a> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%96%A2%E9%96%80%E3%83%88%E3%83%B3%E3%83%8D%E3%83%AB" title="ja:関門トンネル">関門トンネル</a>, Shimonoseki Moji tunnel) was opened.</li>
<li>15 November 1942: Station was moved to today's location.</li>
<li>May 1945: Kanrei Renrakusen stopped.</li>
<li>Jun 1945: Kanfu Renrakusen stopped.</li>
<li>25 December 1946: Tram station was moved in front of the new station.</li>
<li>31 October 1964: Kanmon Renrakusen stopped.</li>
<li>7 February 1971: Tram stopped.</li>
<li>1 April 1987: Privatization of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JNR" title="JNR">JNR</a>, the station becomes part of the JR West network.</li>
<li>29 September 1999: <a href="/wiki/Shimonoseki_Station_massacre" title="Shimonoseki Station massacre">Shimonoseki Station massacre</a>, at least 5 deaths with 10 injuries.</li>
<li>7 January 2006: A main station building fire with collapsed, and a 74-year-old man arrested of suspiction of arson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
