<p><b>Lamiaceae</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-howjsay_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-howjsay-3">[3]</a></sup> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/iː/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iː</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-howjsay_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-howjsay-3">[3]</a></sup>) or <b>Labiatae</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plants</a> commonly known as the <b>mint</b> or <b>deadnettle</b> family. Many of the plants are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aromatic" title="Aromatic">aromatic</a> in all parts and include widely used culinary <a href="/wiki/Herb" title="Herb">herbs</a>, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basil_(herb)" title="Basil (herb)">basil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mentha" title="Mentha">mint</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosemary" title="Rosemary">rosemary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvia_officinalis" title="Salvia officinalis">sage</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Savory_(herb)" title="Savory (herb)">savory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marjoram" title="Marjoram">marjoram</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregano" title="Oregano">oregano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyssopus_officinalis" title="Hyssopus officinalis">hyssop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thyme" title="Thyme">thyme</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lavender" title="Lavender">lavender</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Perilla" title="Perilla">perilla</a>. Some species are <a href="/wiki/Shrub" title="Shrub">shrubs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">trees</a> (such as <a href="/wiki/Teak" title="Teak">teak</a>), or, rarely, <a href="/wiki/Vine" title="Vine">vines</a>. Many members of the family are widely cultivated, not only for their aromatic qualities but also their ease of cultivation, since they are readily propagated by stem cuttings.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Besides those grown for their edible leaves, some are grown for decorative foliage, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Coleus" title="Coleus">Coleus</a></i>. Others are grown for seed, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Salvia_hispanica" title="Salvia hispanica">Salvia hispanica</a></i> (chia), or for their edible tubers, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Plectranthus_edulis" title="Plectranthus edulis">Plectranthus edulis</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Plectranthus_esculentus" title="Plectranthus esculentus">Plectranthus esculentus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Plectranthus_rotundifolius" title="Plectranthus rotundifolius">Plectranthus rotundifolius</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Stachys_affinis" title="Stachys affinis">Stachys affinis</a></i> (Chinese artichoke).</p>
<p>The family has a <a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_distribution" title="Cosmopolitan distribution">cosmopolitan distribution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heywood2007_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-heywood2007-4">[4]</a></sup> The enlarged Lamiaceae contains about 236 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genera" title="Genera">genera</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup> and has been stated to contain 6,900<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heywood2007_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-heywood2007-4">[4]</a></sup> to 7,200<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, but the World Checklist lists 7,534.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The largest genera are <i>Salvia</i> (900), <i>Scutellaria</i> (360), <i>Stachys</i> (300), <i>Plectranthus</i> (300), <i>Hyptis</i> (280), <i>Teucrium</i> (250), <i>Vitex</i> (250), <i>Thymus</i> (220), and <i>Nepeta</i> (200).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Clerodendrum</i> was once a genus of over 400 species,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup> but by 2010, it had been narrowed to about 150.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yuan2010_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-yuan2010-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The family has traditionally been considered closely related to <a href="/wiki/Verbenaceae" title="Verbenaceae">Verbenaceae</a>,;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup> in the 1990s, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phylogenetic" title="Phylogenetic">phylogenetic</a> <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">studies</a> suggested that many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genera" title="Genera">genera</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_classification" title="Biological classification">classified</a> in <a href="/wiki/Verbenaceae" title="Verbenaceae">Verbenaceae</a> should be classified in the Lamiaceae <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cantino_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cantino-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wagstaff1998_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-wagstaff1998-9">[9]</a></sup> or to other families in the <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">order</a> <a href="/wiki/Lamiales" title="Lamiales">Lamiales</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MOBOT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MOBOT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The original family name Labiatae refers to the fact that the flowers typically have petals fused into an upper lip and a lower lip (<i><span lang="la" xml:lang="la">labia</span></i> in Latin). The <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> are bilaterally symmetrical with 5 united <a href="/wiki/Petal" title="Petal">petals</a>, 5 united <a href="/wiki/Sepal" title="Sepal">sepals</a>. They are usually <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plant_sexuality" title="Plant sexuality">bisexual</a> and verticillastrate (a flower cluster that looks like a whorl of flowers but actually consists of two crowded clusters). Although this is still considered an acceptable alternative name, most botanists now use the name "Lamiaceae" in referring to this family. The <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> emerge oppositely, each pair at right angles to the previous one (called <i>decussate</i>) or whorled. The stems are frequently square in cross section,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> but this is not found in all members of the family, and is sometimes found in other plant families.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Genera">Genera</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lamiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Genera">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The last revision of the entire family was published in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup> It described and provided <a href="/wiki/Identification_key" title="Identification key">keys</a> to 236 genera. These are marked with an asterisk in the list below. A few genera have been established or resurrected since 2004. These are marked with a plus sign (+). The remaining genera in the list are mostly of historical interest only and are from a source that includes such genera without explanation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grin_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-grin-11">[11]</a></sup> Few of these are recognized in modern treatments of the family.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_changes">Recent changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lamiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Circumscription_(taxonomy)" title="Circumscription (taxonomy)">circumscription</a> of several genera has changed since 2004. <i>Tsoongia</i>, <i>Paravitex</i>, and <i>Viticipremna</i> have been sunk into <a href="/wiki/Synonym_(taxonomy)" title="Synonym (taxonomy)">synonymy</a> with <i>Vitex</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bramley2009_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-bramley2009-14">[14]</a></sup> <i>Huxleya</i> has been sunk into <i>Volkameria</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yuan2010_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-yuan2010-7">[7]</a></sup> <i>Kalaharia</i>, <i>Volkameria</i>, <i>Ovieda</i>, and <i>Tetraclea</i> have been <a href="/wiki/Segregate_(taxonomy)" title="Segregate (taxonomy)">segregated</a> from a formerly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polyphyletic" title="Polyphyletic">polyphyletic</a> <i>Clerodendrum</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yuan2010_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-yuan2010-7">[7]</a></sup> <i>Rydingia</i> has been separated from <i>Leucas</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scheen2007_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-scheen2007-15">[15]</a></sup> The remaining <i>Leucas</i> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paraphyletic" title="Paraphyletic">paraphyletic</a> over four other genera.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scheen2009_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-scheen2009-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subfamilies_and_tribes">Subfamilies and tribes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lamiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subfamilies and tribes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, Lamiaceae were divided into seven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subfamilies" title="Subfamilies">subfamilies</a> with ten genera not placed in any of the subfamilies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Incertae_sedis" title="Incertae sedis">unplaced</a> genera are: <i>Tectona, Callicarpa, Hymenopyramis, Petraeovitex, Peronema, Garrettia, Cymaria, Acrymia, Holocheila</i>, and <i>Ombrocharis</i>. The subfamilies are <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Symphorematoideae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Symphorematoideae (page does not exist)">Symphorematoideae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viticoideae" title="Viticoideae">Viticoideae</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ajugoideae" title="Ajugoideae">Ajugoideae</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Prostantheroideae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Prostantheroideae (page does not exist)">Prostantheroideae</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nepetoideae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nepetoideae (page does not exist)">Nepetoideae</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scutellarioideae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scutellarioideae (page does not exist)">Scutellarioideae</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lamioideae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lamioideae (page does not exist)">Lamioideae</a>. The subfamily Viticoideae is probably not <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monophyletic" title="Monophyletic">monophyletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bramley2009_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-bramley2009-14">[14]</a></sup> Prostantheroideae and Nepetoideae are divided into <a href="/wiki/Tribe_(biology)" title="Tribe (biology)">tribes</a>. These are shown in the <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetic_tree" title="Phylogenetic tree">phylogenetic tree</a> below.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phylogeny">Phylogeny</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lamiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Phylogeny">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most of the genera of Lamiaceae have never been <a href="/wiki/Sample_(material)" title="Sample (material)">sampled</a> for <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetic" title="Molecular phylogenetic">molecular phylogenetic</a> studies. Most of those that have been are included in the following <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetic_tree" title="Phylogenetic tree">phylogenetic tree</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phylogeny" title="Phylogeny">phylogeny</a> depicted below is based on seven different sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harley2004_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-harley2004-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wagstaff1998_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-wagstaff1998-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yuan2010_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-yuan2010-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bramley2009_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-bramley2009-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zhong2010_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-zhong2010-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-walker2007_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-walker2007-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ryding2010_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ryding2010-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
