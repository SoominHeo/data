<p>Year <b>470</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CDLXX</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">common year starting on Thursday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Severus and Iordanes</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1223 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 470 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=470&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=470&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=470&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Anthemius" title="Anthemius">Anthemius</a> appeals to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britons_(historical)" title="Britons (historical)">Britons</a> for military help against the <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a>. A Breton force (12,000 men) under the <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Riothamus" title="Riothamus">Riothamus</a> lands in <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>, but is defeated by King <a href="/wiki/Euric" title="Euric">Euric</a>. He expands the <a href="/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom" title="Visigothic Kingdom">Visigothic Kingdom</a> further north, possibly as far as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Somme" title="River Somme">Somme River</a>.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santo_Stefano_Rotondo" title="Santo Stefano Rotondo">Santo Stefano Rotondo</a> at <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> is consecrated (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=470&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Odoacer" title="Odoacer">Odoacer</a> becomes the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">Germanic</a> tribes (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heruli" title="Heruli">Herulic</a> – <a href="/wiki/Scirii" title="Scirii">Scirian</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Foederati" title="Foederati">foederati</a></i>) in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Italy" title="Northern Italy">Northern Italy</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=470&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=470&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mamertus" title="Mamertus">Mamertus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Diocese_of_Vienne" title="Ancient Diocese of Vienne">bishop of Vienne</a>, introduces the <a href="/wiki/Rogation_days" title="Rogation days">Rogation days</a> (a three days' <a href="/wiki/Procession" title="Procession">procession</a> involving prayer to invoke <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a>'s mercy).</li>
