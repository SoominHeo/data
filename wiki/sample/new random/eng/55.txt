<p>The <b>Region of Magallanes</b>, officially the <b>XII Region of Magallanes and Chilean Antarctica</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>XII Región de Magallanes y de la Antártica Chilena</i></span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is one of <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>'s 15 first order <a href="/wiki/Administrative_division" title="Administrative division">administrative divisions</a>. It is the southernmost, largest, and second least populated <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Chile" title="Regions of Chile">region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>. It comprises four <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Chile" title="Provinces of Chile">provinces</a>: <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Altima_Esperanza_Province" title="Última Esperanza Province">Última Esperanza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magallanes_Province" title="Magallanes Province">Magallanes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego_Province,_Chile" title="Tierra del Fuego Province, Chile">Tierra del Fuego</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ant%C3%A1rtica_Chilena" title="Antártica Chilena">Antártica Chilena</a>.</p>
<p>Magallanes has many globally known places and geographical features including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torres_del_Paine" title="Torres del Paine">Torres del Paine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Horn" title="Cape Horn">Cape Horn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a> island, and the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>. It also includes the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Antarctic_Territory" title="Chilean Antarctic Territory">Antarctic territory claimed by Chile</a>. Despite its large area, much of the land in the region is rugged or closed off for <a href="/wiki/Sheep_farming" title="Sheep farming">sheep farming</a>, and is thus not suitable for settlement. 80% of the population lives in the capital <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a>, a major market city and one of the main hubs for Antarctic exploration.</p>
<p>The main economic activities are sheep farming, oil extraction, and tourism. It is also the region with the least amount of poverty in Chile (5,8%); households in Magallanes have the highest income of any region in Chile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magallanes_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This region contains mountainous peaks and <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glaciers</a> in the far north, including elements of the <a href="/wiki/Patagonian_Ice_Sheet" title="Patagonian Ice Sheet">Patagonian Ice Sheet</a>. Further south there are other mountain ranges such as the <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Toro" title="Cerro Toro">Cerro Toro</a> and numerous surface waters including the <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Altima_Esperanza_Sound" title="Última Esperanza Sound">Seno Última Esperanza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eberhard_Fjord" title="Eberhard Fjord">Eberhard Fjord</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lago_Grey" title="Lago Grey">Lago Grey</a>. Protected areas include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torres_del_Paine" title="Torres del Paine">Torres del Paine</a> National Park and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cueva_del_Milodon_Natural_Monument" title="Cueva del Milodon Natural Monument">Cueva del Milodon Natural Monument</a>. At the latter, remains have been discovered of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extinct" title="Extinct">extinct</a> <a href="/wiki/Ground_sloth" title="Ground sloth">ground sloth</a> as well as of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prehistoric" title="Prehistoric">prehistoric</a> man dating to circa <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/10,000_BC" title="10,000 BC">10,000 BC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magallanes_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magallanes_koeppen.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="6838" data-file-width="5100" height="295" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Magallanes_koeppen.png/220px-Magallanes_koeppen.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Magallanes_koeppen.png/330px-Magallanes_koeppen.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Magallanes_koeppen.png/440px-Magallanes_koeppen.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The region is characterized by low temperatures and strong winds throughout the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCNclima_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BCNclima-5">[5]</a></sup> The region has 5 different climatic types, owing to the diversity in altitudes and the width of the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> Owing to its proximity to 60<sup>o</sup>S (a zone characterized by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure" title="Low pressure">low pressure</a> systems and <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">subpolar cyclones</a>), frontal system regularly pass through the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The westernmost islands have a cold, humid and rainy climate, being exposed to strong winds and low temperatures throughout the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCNclima_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-BCNclima-5">[5]</a></sup> Precipitation averages 3,500 mm (140 in) per year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCNclima_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-BCNclima-5">[5]</a></sup> while the mean annual temperature on these islands is 9 °C (48.2 °F) with a low thermal amplitude due to the maritime influences and the strong winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> Precipitation decreases eastwards while temperatures increases eastwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCNclima_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-BCNclima-5">[5]</a></sup> These islands are the wettest places in Chile, particularly on <a href="/wiki/Guarello_Island" title="Guarello Island">Guarello Island</a> where it can record 9,000 mm (350 in) during the wettest years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> Precipitation is high throughout the year though fall tends to be the wettest season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Immediately to the east of the westernmost islands, east of the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> and bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Paine" title="Cordillera Paine">Cordillera Paine</a> to the north and the <a href="/wiki/Brunswick_Peninsula" title="Brunswick Peninsula">Brunswick Peninsula</a> to the south, the climate is much drier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCNclima_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-BCNclima-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> to the west block most of the precipitation, causing most of the air coming in to be dry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup> Mean annual precipitation ranges between 250 to 400 mm (9.8 to 15.7 in) which is fairly evenly distributed through the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-7"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup> During winters, precipitation mainly falls as <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">snow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-8"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup> Mean annual temperatures are between 6 to 7 °C (42.8 to 44.6 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-9"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup> Due to the close proximity to the ocean, winters are not cold and snow cover does not last long.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-10"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the easternmost parts of the region, including much of <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego_Province,_Chile" title="Tierra del Fuego Province, Chile">Tierra del Fuego province</a>, a cold <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steppe_climate" title="Steppe climate">steppe climate</a> prevails.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-11"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> Temperatures are cold while precipitation is lower than areas to the west.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCNclima_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-BCNclima-5">[5]</a></sup> Mean precipitation is between 250 to 500 mm (9.8 to 19.7 in), decreasing eastwards and is fairly evenly distributed throughout the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-12"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> Mean annual temperatures range between 8 to 9 °C (46.4 to 48.2 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-13"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> From December to March, mean temperatures exceed 10 °C (50.0 °F) while during winter, they reach down to 2 °C (35.6 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-14"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Corresponding to the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Patagonian_Ice_Field" title="Southern Patagonian Ice Field">Southern Patagonian Ice Field</a>, located at higher altitudes, temperatures are cold enough to maintain permanent ice fields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-15"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup> Mean temperatures are below 0 °C (32.0 °F) in all months while precipitation (mostly as snow) is abundant year round, receiving 2,000 mm (79 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCNclima_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-BCNclima-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-16"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> These areas have a <a href="/wiki/Polar_climate" title="Polar climate">polar climate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the southernmost islands, south of <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tundra_climate" title="Tundra climate">tundra climate</a> prevails.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-17"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-11"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> These islands are strongly influenced by the surrounding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_ocean" title="Pacific ocean">Pacific ocean</a> to the west and southwest and the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a> to the south, resulting in constant temperatures throughout the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-18"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-12"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> Mean annual temperatures are between 5 to 7 °C (41.0 to 44.6 °F) while precipitation is high, with most places receiving 1,000 mm (39 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-19"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-13"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> In sheltered areas, precipitation is below 600 mm (24 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-20"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_7-14"><a href="#cite_note-clima-7">[7]</a></sup> Summers are the warmest and the wettest season in these islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMC_6-21"><a href="#cite_note-DMC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magallanes_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The population of Magellan is one of the lowest in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, as one of the last regions colonized and populated.</p>
<p>In the 1854 census counted 158 settlers, who belonged to the people of <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a>, the native population, according to King, amounted in 1830, approximately 2,200 (about 400 in western Patagonia and south approximately 1,600 Strait).</p>
<p>The census of 1875 was aware of 1,144 inhabitants and in 1895 the population had risen to 5,170 inhabitants, mostly concentrated in the city of <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a> and its surroundings.</p>
<p>The installation of the cattle ranches attracted people from <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (mostly <a href="/wiki/Croats" title="Croats">Croats</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italians</a>) and southern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> (mostly from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chilo%C3%A9" title="Chiloé">Chiloé</a>), which greatly increased the population of the region.</p>
<p>Punta Arenas is said to have the largest percentage of Croatians in the world outside Croatia and the former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. There is a higher proportion of non-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_people" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> Europeans there as well (esp. <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scots</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a>), and descendants of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">Germans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danes" title="Danes">Danes</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavians</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_people" title="Russian people">Russians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> peoples.</p>
<p>From the late 18th century to the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a> in 1914, thousands of trans-oceanic voyages stopped by Punta Arenas as the most convenient strait between the Atlantic and Pacific oceans. The settlement of Punta Arenas and the Magellan and Chilean Antarctica Region is a result of its past being a hub to international travel.</p>
<p>Already in the 1907 census, the Territory of Magellan had 17,330 inhabitants, distributed as follows: - City of Punta Arenas: 12,785 hab. - Patagonia Chilena: 1,094 hab. - Pen. of Brunswick: 1,062 hab. - Tierra del Fuego: 1,626 hab. - Beagle Channel: 184 inhabitants. - Last Hope: 392 inhabitants. - Baker River: 187 inhabitants.</p>
<p>This proportion has persisted, the bulk of the regional population in the city of <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a>, and in the provincial capital <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Natales" title="Puerto Natales">Puerto Natales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porvenir,_Chile" title="Porvenir, Chile">Porvenir</a> (Spanish for "future") and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Williams" title="Puerto Williams">Puerto Williams</a>, one of the world's southernmost cities.</p>
<p>In the past 50 years the population has maintained a moderate increase, as shown below, keeping one of the lowest population densities in the country and since this is mostly urban, mostly concentrated in <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a>.</p>

<p>The most populated cities (2002 census) are: Punta Arenas (116,005 inhabitants), Puerto Natales (16,978) Porvenir (4,734), Puerto Williams (1,952), and <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Sombrero" title="Cerro Sombrero">Cerro Sombrero</a> (687).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magallanes_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/University_of_Magallanes" title="University of Magallanes">University of Magellan</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UMAG" title="UMAG">UMAG</a>) is a university in the southern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> city of Punta Arenas. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Traditional_Universities" title="Chilean Traditional Universities">Chilean Traditional Universities</a>. The University of Magellan was established in 1981 during the economic reforms of the Chile's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_regime" title="Military regime">military regime</a> as the successor of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Universidad_T%C3%A9cnica_del_Estado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Universidad Técnica del Estado (page does not exist)">Universidad Técnica del Estado</a>'s Punta Arenas section. Universidad Técnica del Estado had established the Punta Arenas section in 1961. The University of Magellan have campuses in <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Natales" title="Puerto Natales">Puerto Natales</a> as well as a university centre in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Williams" title="Puerto Williams">Puerto Williams</a>. University of Magellan publishes the humanities and social sciences journal <i><a href="/wiki/Magallania" title="Magallania">Magallania</a></i> twice a year.</p>
