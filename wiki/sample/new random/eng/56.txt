<p>The <b>National Ballet of China</b> (NBC), known in China as the <b>Central Ballet Troupe</b> was founded on 31 December 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">mainland Chinese national</a> <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a> troupe.</p>
<p>The ballet company works from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tianqiao_Theater&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tianqiao Theater (page does not exist)">Tianqiao Theater</a>, this was specifically built for NBC in 1959, and it was renovated in 2001. This theater is one of a handful of such theaters in China that specialize in <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> performances.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Ballet_of_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1954, the first ballet school in China, Beijing Dance School, was established, and in the following years ballets such as <i><a href="/wiki/Swan_Lake" title="Swan Lake">Swan Lake</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet_(Prokofiev)" title="Romeo and Juliet (Prokofiev)">Romeo and Juliet</a></i> were performed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gunde_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gunde-2">[2]</a></sup> Later in 1959 the National Ballet of China was founded as the Experimental Ballet Company of the Beijing Dance School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-3">[3]</a></sup> Two persons made their mark on both the school and the company - <a href="/wiki/Dai_Ailian" title="Dai Ailian">Dai Ailian</a> who was the principal of the school and one of the ballet company directors, and <a href="/wiki/Pyotr_Gusev" title="Pyotr Gusev">Pyotr Gusev</a> who instituted the Russian training system that formed the technical foundation for the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>, the company came under the control of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madame_Mao" title="Madame Mao">Madame Mao</a> and Dai Ailian was sidelined. In this period, <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_opera" title="Revolutionary opera">Revolutionary Model dramas</a> came to the fore, and the repertory of the company was eventually reduced to two sanctioned ballets - <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Detachment_of_Women_(ballet)" title="Red Detachment of Women (ballet)">The Red Detachment of Women</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_White_Haired_Girl" title="The White Haired Girl">The White Haired Girl</a></i>. After the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Gang_of_Four" title="Gang of Four">Gang of Four</a> in 1976, Ailian returned as director until 1980 and then continued as artistic adviser. The ballet company began to reform and change direction with the classical Western ballets resurrected, and it also broadened its range to include more modern ballets from around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1994, Zhao Ruheng (赵汝蘅), who had been a company dancer since 1959, took over as its artistic director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2009, Feng Ying was appointed artistic director of the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-balletco_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-balletco-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Company">The Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Ballet_of_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current director is Feng Ying (冯英), with Wang Quanxing (王全兴) as deputy, and Wang Caijun (王才军) the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9p%C3%A9titeur" title="Répétiteur">répétiteur</a>. The company has its own orchestra, which is directed and conducted by Zhang Yi (张艺).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company employs over 70 dancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Many NBC dancers have won gold, silver and bronze medals at various international ballet competitions.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Repertoire">Repertoire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Ballet_of_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Repertoire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>NBC has toured internationally and produced and performed many Chinese and Western ballets. According to its current director Feng Ying, there are three components in the repertoire of the company: Chinese ballets, 19th century Western classic, and 20th century ballet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-balletco_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-balletco-5">[5]</a></sup> They have also collaborated on contemporary dance pieces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-4">[4]</a></sup> Their repertoire includes:</p>
