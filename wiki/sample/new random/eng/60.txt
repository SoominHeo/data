<p>The South Korean ferry <i>Seohae</i> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">서해 페리호</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko">西海페리號</span>) sank on October 10, 1993 in the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a> near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wido,_Buan_County&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wido, Buan County (page does not exist)">Wido</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buan_County" title="Buan County">Buan County</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Jeolla_Province" title="North Jeolla Province">North Jeolla Province</a>. 292 of the 362 passengers and crew on board were killed. 70 people were rescued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news10416_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-news10416-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the time, this accident was the largest ferry disaster in South Korea since December 15, 1970, when 323 people on board were killed on the ferry <i>Namyoung</i> (남영호).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news10416_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-news10416-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">Accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinking_of_the_MV_Seohae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Seohae</i> was a ferry of 110 <a href="/wiki/Gross_tonnage" title="Gross tonnage">gross tons</a>, with a maximum capacity of 221 passengers. At the time of the accident, <i>Seohae</i> was carrying 362 people (355 passengers and 7 crew), an excess of 141, and the weather condition was harsh with winds of 10 to 14 metres per second (22 to 31 mph) and wave height of 2 to 3 metres (6.6 to 9.8 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news10416_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-news10416-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Overload was a factor in the sinking. Another was a 1-cm thick rope found wrapped around both propeller shafts. The rope, left behind by fishing operations, may have made the ferry heel over onto her starboard side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
