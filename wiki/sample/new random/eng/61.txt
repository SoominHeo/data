<p><b>Savannakhet</b> or <b>Kaysone Phomvihane</b> (<a href="/wiki/Lao_language" title="Lao language">Lao</a>: <span lang="lo" xml:lang="lo">ໄກສອນ ພົມວິຫານ</span>; <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">ไกสอน พมวิหาน</span>) (population 120,000) is a city in western <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> and the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Savannakhet_Province" title="Savannakhet Province">Savannakhet Province</a>, previously known as <b>Khanthabouli</b> (ຄັນທະບູລີ). This is the second-largest city in Laos, after <a href="/wiki/Vientiane" title="Vientiane">Vientiane</a>. The city is birthtown of <a href="/wiki/Kaysone_Phomvihane" title="Kaysone Phomvihane">Kaysone Phomvihane</a>, former president of Laos, and was named after him in 2005. Although the old French colonial quarter of the town, along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mekong_River" title="Mekong River">Mekong River</a>-front, is depressed and crumbling, the town's proximity to Thailand's booming economy has brought about new commercial development in the northern part of the town, near both the river crossing and the bus terminal.</p>
<p>Like all Lao cities, Kaysone Phomvihane has a mixed population of Lao, Thai, Vietnamese and Chinese, as well as minority peoples from the Lao interior. It has a large 15th-century Buddhist temple, Wat Sainyaphum, a Chinese temple, a Catholic church and a mosque. The <a href="/wiki/Second_Thai%E2%80%93Lao_Friendship_Bridge" title="Second Thai–Lao Friendship Bridge">Second Thai–Lao Friendship Bridge</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Mekong" title="Mekong">Mekong</a> connects to <a href="/wiki/Mukdahan_Province" title="Mukdahan Province">Mukdahan Province</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. The two-lane, 12-meter (39 ft) wide, 1,600-meter (5,250 ft) long bridge, opened to the general public on January 9, 2007.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savannakhet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is served by <a href="/wiki/Savannakhet_Airport" title="Savannakhet Airport">Savannakhet Airport</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savannakhet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Savannakhet features a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_savanna_climate" title="Tropical savanna climate">tropical savanna climate</a> (<i>Aw</i>) according to <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> with a little <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical_climate" title="Subtropical climate">subtropical climate</a> characteristics as the city located 16,5° away from the equator. The hottest month is April (mean = 29.5 °C (85.1 °F)) with temperature ranging from 23.9 °C (75.0 °F) to 35.2 °C (95.4 °F), while the coldest month is December (mean = 21.7 °C (71.1 °F)) with temperature ranging from 15.2 °C (59.4 °F) to 28.7 °C (83.7 °F). The <a href="/wiki/Diurnal_temperature_variation" title="Diurnal temperature variation">diurnal temperature variation</a> seems to be greater during winter due to dry conditions. The city experience <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Dry" title="Dry">dry</a> season during winter months and <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Wet" title="Wet">wet</a> season during summer months due to activation of <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a>. The driest month is December with precipitation total 2.0 millimetres (0.079 in), while the wettest month is August with precipitation total 323.1 millimetres (12.72 in).</p>
