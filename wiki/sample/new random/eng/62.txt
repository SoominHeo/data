<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jessica_Barden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barden was born in <a href="/wiki/Northallerton" title="Northallerton">Northallerton</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, and moved to <a href="/wiki/Wetherby" title="Wetherby">Wetherby</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, in 1995. She attended <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_High_School" title="Wetherby High School">Wetherby High School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, it was announced that she would play the part of <a href="/wiki/Kayleigh_Morton" title="Kayleigh Morton">Kayleigh Morton</a> on the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i>Coronation Street</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wn_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-wn-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She made her first appearance on Sunday, 18 March 2007. She then appeared in the UK hit drama <i>Death on Angel's Tide</i> in late July 2007.</p>
<p>In April 2008, it was announced that Kayleigh and the rest of the Morton family would leave <i>Coronation Street</i> at the end of 2008, after just a year and a half. Jessica made her final appearance on <i>Coronation Street</i> on 29 September 2008.</p>
<p>She has also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chase_(2006_TV_series)" title="The Chase (2006 TV series)">The Chase</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/No_Angels" title="No Angels">No Angels</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Parents_Are_Aliens" title="My Parents Are Aliens">My Parents Are Aliens</a></i> and plays Mary Ratcliffe in the 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Mrs_Ratcliffe%27s_Revolution" title="Mrs Ratcliffe's Revolution">Mrs Ratcliffe's Revolution</a></i>. She has played the part of 'Pea' in the highly acclaimed production <i>Jerusalem</i> at the 'Royal Court Theatre' in Sloane Square before being transferred to 'The Apollo' in the West End.</p>
<p>Barden appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Tamara_Drewe_(film)" title="Tamara Drewe (film)">Tamara Drewe</a></i>, a dramatisation of the comic strip of the same name, directed by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Frears" title="Stephen Frears">Stephen Frears</a>. She played the role of 'Sophie' in Joe Wright's film <i><a href="/wiki/Hanna_(film)" title="Hanna (film)">Hanna</a></i>. Her most recent film projects are <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Dark_Half" title="In the Dark Half">In the Dark Half</a></i> and the 2012 urban horror flick <i><a href="/wiki/Comedown_(film)" title="Comedown (film)">Comedown</a></i>. She appeared in an American independent movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lullaby_(2013_film)" title="Lullaby (2013 film)">Lullaby</a></i> that was filmed in New York.</p>
<p>In July 2015, Barden played the role of Kit Carmichael in the BBC’s <a href="/wiki/The_Outcast_(TV_adaptation)" title="The Outcast (TV adaptation)">two-part television adaptation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sadie_Jones" title="Sadie Jones">Sadie Jones</a>’ debut novel <i>The Outcast</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_One:_The_Outcast_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_One:_The_Outcast-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Barden played Ellen in Channel 4's TV movie: Ellen. Her character was a fourteen year old girl from an impoverished background who struggled to find her place and eventually got lured into a sex gang. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
