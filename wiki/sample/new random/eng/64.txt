<p><b>Daniel</b> "<b>Danny</b>" <b>Fox</b> (born 29 May 1986) is a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full_back" title="Defender (association football)">left back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a>. He represented <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England at under-21 international level</a>, has since been selected for <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a>, making his debut in the November 2009 loss against <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Everton">Everton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Everton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fox was born in <a href="/wiki/Winsford" title="Winsford">Winsford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a> and learnt his trade at his boyhood club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Everton_F.C" title="Everton F.C">Everton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">youth system</a> at thirteen from <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy">Liverpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As Fox progressed through the academy, Fox was offered a full time contract with the club,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in which he signed on 19 May 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After signing a full time contract with the club, Fox continued to develop in the reserve team and soon came to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_team" title="First team">first team</a> appearance was as an 18-year-old being named on the bench for Everton's home match against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> in October 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stranraer">Stranraer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stranraer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 February 2005, Fox was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loan_(association_football)" title="Loan (association football)">loaned</a> out by Everton to <a href="/wiki/Stranraer_F.C." title="Stranraer F.C.">Stranraer</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> where he played 11 games (scoring once against <a href="/wiki/Berwick_Rangers_F.C." title="Berwick Rangers F.C.">Berwick Rangers</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and helped them secure promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Football_League_First_Division" title="Scottish Football League First Division">Scottish First Division</a> before his eventual release from Everton in the summer of 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Seven years on, Fox revealed that his time at Stranraer considerably helped with his career development: “It was a great grounding for me and made me realise I had to take every opportunity to make sure I played at the highest level possible."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Walsall">Walsall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Walsall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After leaving Everton, <a href="/wiki/Walsall_F.C." title="Walsall F.C.">Walsall</a> moved quickly to sign Fox, alongside Everton youth teammate <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Gerrard" title="Anthony Gerrard">Anthony Gerrard</a> on 3 June 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fox made his debut for the club against <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a> in the opening game of the season, making first start.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In the next game against <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a>, Fox provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Matty_Fryatt" title="Matty Fryatt">Matty Fryatt</a> to score the club’s first goal in the match, as they drew 2–2 and was named Man of the Match by <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Mail" title="Birmingham Mail">Birmingham Mail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Fox’s performance throughout August earned him Evening Mail Saddlers Player of the Month<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FoxBM_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FoxBM-14">[14]</a></sup> and since making his debut, Fox quickly becoming a regular in the first team. Despite the injuries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FoxBM_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-FoxBM-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and suspension,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Fox finished his first season at Walsall, making 33 appearances. Fox later reflected the 2005–06 season, in which he stated the poor season made him "more mature as a player and a person".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2006–07 season, Fox started the season well when he scored his first goal for the club in a 5–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a> on 12 September 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and then scored his second goal of the season, two weeks later on 23 September 2006, in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers_F.C." title="Bristol Rovers F.C.">Bristol Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Fox then scored his third goal against Peterborough United on 1 January 2007, for the second time this season, in a 2–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Despite missing two matches in the 2006–07 season, due to injury<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and suspension.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Fox ended of the season with 44 appearances and three goals, as he helped the club promoted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a>.</p>
<p>In the 2007–08 season saw Fox continued to make an impression at Walsall when he scored his first goal of the season against <a href="/wiki/Gillingham_F.C." title="Gillingham F.C.">Gillingham</a> on 1 September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Two weeks later, on 15 September 2007, Fox scored again, in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Millwall_F.C." title="Millwall F.C.">Millwall</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Fox scored again three weeks later on 2 October 2007, in a 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Rovers_F.C." title="Doncaster Rovers F.C.">Doncaster Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> However, on 26 December 2007, Fox received the first red card of his career in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Port_Vale_F.C." title="Port Vale F.C.">Port Vale</a> after a second bookable offence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In the January transfer window, Fox’s future was in doubt after attracted interests from clubs, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_United_A.F.C." title="Leeds United A.F.C.">Leeds United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his time at Walsall, Fox made over 100 appearances and scoring six times in all competitions, as well as, having a positive attitude.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coventry_City">Coventry City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Coventry City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 January 2008, Fox joined <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> in a double transfer deal (with his defensive partner <a href="/wiki/Scott_Dann" title="Scott Dann">Scott Dann</a> also moving to the <a href="/wiki/Ricoh_Arena" title="Ricoh Arena">Ricoh Arena</a> only 72 hours later), after agreeing personal terms and a passing a medical. Before this, <a href="/wiki/Colchester_United_F.C." title="Colchester United F.C.">Colchester United</a> had agreed a fee with Walsall for Fox.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walsall_accept_U.27s_offer_for_Fox_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Walsall_accept_U.27s_offer_for_Fox-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Just days after signing for Coventry City, Fox made his debut for the club in a 1–0 defeat against <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Two months after making his debut, Fox scored his first goal in a 5–1 win over Colchester United.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In the next game against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> on 26 April 2008, Fox provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Ward" title="Elliott Ward">Elliott Ward</a>, in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> As the 2007–08 season progressed, in which Fox made eighteen appearance and scoring once, Fox immediately made an impact in the first team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ahead of the 2008–09 season, Fox attracted interests from <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> clubs, though <a href="/wiki/Ray_Ranson" title="Ray Ranson">Ray Ranson</a> made it clear that Fox and Dann were not for sale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Despite this, Fox remained in the first team in his first full season at the club. Fox scored his first goal of the season, in a 1–1 draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swansea_City_F.C." title="Swansea City F.C.">Swansea City</a> on 25 November 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and scored in the next game on 1 December 2008, in a 3–1 loss against <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> After serving a suspension for one match,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FoxBM2_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-FoxBM2-38">[38]</a></sup> Fox scored on his return in the next game, in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a> on 9 December 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> After an injury struck him in late–December,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Fox scored on his return on 10 January 2009, in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Fox scored his sixth goal of the season on 28 February 2009, in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>, in which he stated afterwards that it was his best goal ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Despite suffering from an injury once again in March<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> and being sent–off, in a 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on 25 April 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Fox, alongside his team–mate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kieren_Westwood" title="Kieren Westwood">Kieren Westwood</a>, were named in the Championship PFA Team of the Year for 2009 in his debut season at the club,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> as well as, winning the Players’ Player of the Year award for the 2008–09 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> In the 2008–09 season, Fox finished the season, scoring six times in forty–five appearance in all competitions. The 2008–09 season was also a turning for Fox, as he began to taking free–kicks, in which went in four of the five times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FoxBM2_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-FoxBM2-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the club keen on extending his contract,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Fox, himself, stated he wanted to stay at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> However, Fox continued to attract interests from <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> clubs, such as, <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Fox later credited Coventry City for helping him turn his life around.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Celtic">Celtic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Celtic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 24 July 2009, Fox signed for Celtic for an undisclosed fee (believed to be £1.5 million)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Celtic_sign_Coventry_defender_Fox_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-Celtic_sign_Coventry_defender_Fox-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Celtic_snare_Fox_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-Celtic_snare_Fox-53">[53]</a></sup> Coventry's manager, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Coleman_(footballer)" title="Chris Coleman (footballer)">Chris Coleman</a> expressed his disappointment at losing Fox: "I was determined not to lose . . . Danny Fox and so were the club, but it is hard once players are offered a lot of money to go to big, big clubs." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fox made his competitive debut for Celtic in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> qualifiers against <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Moscow" title="FC Dynamo Moscow">Dynamo Moscow</a>. He became a regular free kick taker for Celtic, but did not score a goal, although he did provide assists in games against <a href="/wiki/St_Johnstone_F.C." title="St Johnstone F.C.">St Johnstone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dundee_United_F.C." title="Dundee United F.C.">Dundee United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Fox made his last appearance for Celtic in an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Firm_derby" title="Old Firm derby">Old Firm derby</a> against <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> in a 1–1 draw, where he played 90 minutes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Burnley">Burnley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Burnley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 January 2010, after just six months in Glasgow, Fox joined <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> on a three and a half-year contract for an undisclosed fee, believed to be £1.8 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>On his debut for Burnley, Fox claimed an assist and the winning goal against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on 6 February in a 2–1 home win. Fox afterwards claimed that his debut with Burnley was "his best day in football".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> His efforts lifted Burnley above West Ham and out of the relegation zone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Despite this, Burnley went on to be relegated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a>. Fox went on to make fourteen appearance for the club.</p>
<p>Following their relegation, Fox continued to remain as a first team regular at Burnley and went on to provide seven assists during the 2010–11 season. However, his 35 appearance for the club was restricted, due to suspension<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> and injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Fox made his only appearance of the 2011–12 season, playing in the opening game of the season, in a 2–2 draw against Watford.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southampton">Southampton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Southampton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 August 2011, Fox signed for <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> on a four-year deal for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> It was later revealed Fox’s move to Southampton was Burnley’s board of directors decision to sell him, citing an offer “could not refuse”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 August 2011, he made his debut coming off from the bench replacing <a href="/wiki/David_Connolly" title="David Connolly">David Connolly</a> in a 5–2 away win over <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Fox then provided a hat–trick assists, in a 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> on 18 February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> At Southampton, Fox began to established himself in the first team, playing in the left back position and helped the team gain promotion to the Premier League.</p>
<p>In the 2012–13 season, Fox continued to be in the first team regular and scored his first goal for the club in a 6–1 defeat at <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> on 15 September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> However, Fox’s own injury concern<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> and his sending off against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Brom</a> on 27 April 2013, which saw him served them a three–match ban,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> restricted him to twenty appearance in the 2012–13 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fox made his first appearance of the 2013–14 season, playing in the left–back position, in a 5–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> in the second round of League Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> However, Fox found himself competing over a left–back position with <a href="/wiki/Luke_Shaw" title="Luke Shaw">Luke Shaw</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Fox found himself in the sidelined when he suffered a hamstring injury that kept him out for weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> Fox made his return to the first team on 22 December 2013, where he set up one of the goals, in a 3–2 loss against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nottingham_Forest">Nottingham Forest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Nottingham Forest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 January 2014, Fox signed on loan for Championship side <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a> until the end of the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fox made his Nottingham Forest debut on 2 February 2014, where he played 62 minutes before being substituted, in a 3-1 win over <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> After making his debut, Fox expressed delight to make his Nottingham Forest debut, though he stated the game was tough.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> A week later on 11 February 2014 against <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>, Fox provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Paterson_(footballer,_born_1991)" title="Jamie Paterson (footballer, born 1991)">Jamie Paterson</a>, who scored twice, in a 3-0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> However, personal reason<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> and injury, which he sustained in a match against <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> on 19 April 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> restricted him to 14 appearance.</p>
<p>On 9 May, Nottingham Forest signed Danny Fox on a three-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> Ahead of the 2014-15 season, Fox continued to wear number 13 shirt, as he previously did whilst on loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> Fox captained Nottingham Forest for the first time in the first round of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">Football League Cup</a> and played 90 minutes, in a 1-0 win over <a href="/wiki/Tranmere_Rovers_F.C." title="Tranmere Rovers F.C.">Tranmere Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> However, Fox's performance later came criticism from Nottingham Forest supporters in a 1-0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_A.F.C." title="Rochdale A.F.C.">Rochdale</a> that saw Nottingham Forest were eliminated in the FA Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> After missing three matches because of this, Fox's return saw an improvement when he set up a goal, in a 3-0 win over <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> and in the next game, in a 4-4 against <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> Fox then provided three assists in two matches, which he provided twice against Reading<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> and once against Charlton Athletic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> Despite his own injury concern<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> and losing his first team place to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Mancienne" title="Michael Mancienne">Michael Mancienne</a> in the left-back position,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> Fox made 27 appearance in his first full season at Nottingham Forest.</p>
<p>In the 2015-16 season saw Fox continued to compete with Mancienne over the left-back position, as newly Manager <a href="/wiki/Dougie_Freedman" title="Dougie Freedman">Dougie Freedman</a> remained in his first team plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> Fox made his first start of the 2015-16 season, playing as a left-back, in the opening game of the season, in a 1-0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup> However, Fox's first team place was soon dispatched and was demoted to the substitute bench as a result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> After almost two months since being in the first team, Fox made his return, where he played in a right-back position and 90 minutes, in a 2-0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> Fox was told by Freedman that he would no longer be part of his first team plan and he was allowed to leave the club, but this never happened and stayed at Nottingham Forest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> While on the sidelined rom the first team, Fox was sent to the training in the club's reserve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> After five months absence from the first team, Fox was recalled to the first team by newly caretaker Manager <a href="/wiki/Paul_Williams_(footballer,_born_1971)" title="Paul Williams (footballer, born 1971)">Paul Williams</a> in March<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> and made his first appearance in five months, in a 2-1 loss against Reading on 5 April 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup> Fox remained in the first team for last seven games,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> as the season progressed until he received a straight red card, in the last game of the season, in a 2-1 win over already relegated side <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">MK Dons</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup> Fox later finished the 2015-16 season, making ten appearance.</p>
<p>Ahead of the 2016-17 season, Fox signed a one-year contract extension with the club on 25 July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup> Because of his red card against MK Dons on the last game of the season, Fox had to serve a three match ban. Fox made his return from suspension from the first team, where he made his first start and played 90 minutes, in a 1-0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a> on 16 August 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fox, alongside Dann, was called up to the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21 squad</a> for the match against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Poland national under-21 football team">Poland</a> on 25 March 2008 and was selected in the starting 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox_and_Dann_make_Under-21_debut_107-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_and_Dann_make_Under-21_debut-107">[107]</a></sup> Fox and Dann were once again called–up for the England U21 squad in May 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup> but didn’t play, due to injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> In early 2009, Fox was called–up by England U21 for the third running,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-110"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup> but didn’t play, due to the similar circumstances he had before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-111"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup></p>
<p>He declared himself available to play for <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> through his grandfather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup> Fox was selected for Scotland's friendly against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> in October 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-113"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup> but had to withdraw due to an injury sustained while playing for Celtic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> Fox finally made his debut for Scotland in a 3–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a> in November 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-115"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup> He did not feature for Scotland again until August 2012, when he was recalled by <a href="/wiki/Craig_Levein" title="Craig Levein">Craig Levein</a> for a friendly against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_association_football_team" title="Australia national association football team">Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-116"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup> He played 69 minutes and was involved in two goals scored by Scotland in a 3–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-117"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup> He also played in Scotland's loss against Wales on the 12 October 2012 at left back.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Outside of football, Fox likes to relax by watching films, with comedies and thrillers are his film genre preference. His parents resided in Cheshire and has a younger brother, who is in the RAF, and a sister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-122"><a href="#cite_note-122">[120]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2014, Fox dyed his beard yellow in effort to raise money for charity called Cystic Fibrosis Foundation following his friend's death, who suffered from Cystic Fibrosis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-123"><a href="#cite_note-123">[121]</a></sup> Because of this, Fox was appointed as the ambassadorial role for the Cystic Fibrosis charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-124"><a href="#cite_note-124">[122]</a></sup></p>
