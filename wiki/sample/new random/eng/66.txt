<p><b>Joshua "Josh" Smith</b> (born December 5, 1985) is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Sichuan_Blue_Whales" title="Sichuan Blue Whales">Sichuan Blue Whales</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Basketball_Association" title="Chinese Basketball Association">Chinese Basketball Association</a> (CBA). Entering the NBA straight out of high school, Smith played nine seasons with the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> before playing for the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Pistons" title="Detroit Pistons">Detroit Pistons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a> between 2013 and 2016. He is sometimes referred to by his nickname "J-Smoove".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school_career">High school career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: High school career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith attended <a href="/wiki/McEachern_High_School" title="McEachern High School">John McEachern High</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Powder_Springs" title="Powder Springs">Powder Springs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>. During the summer before his senior year, he played alongside future NBA players <a href="/wiki/Randolph_Morris" title="Randolph Morris">Randolph Morris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Howard" title="Dwight Howard">Dwight Howard</a> on the Atlanta Celtics <a href="/wiki/Amateur_Athletic_Union" title="Amateur Athletic Union">AAU</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Smith transferred to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oak_Hill_Academy_(Virginia)" title="Oak Hill Academy (Virginia)">Oak Hill Academy</a> for his senior year where he was teammates with <a href="/wiki/Rajon_Rondo" title="Rajon Rondo">Rajon Rondo</a>. He averaged 22 points, 8 rebounds, 4 assists, 6 blocks, and 3 steals per game on the season, helping lead Oak Hill to 38-0 record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jilts_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jilts-4">[4]</a></sup> Smith was highly recruited as <a href="/wiki/Rivals.com" title="Rivals.com">Rivals.com</a> rated him the third best overall player in the nation and the number one small forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Smith committed to play for <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Hoosiers" title="Indiana Hoosiers">Indiana University</a> but he decided to forgo college and enter the NBA draft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jilts_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jilts-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlanta_Hawks_.282004.E2.80.932013.29">Atlanta Hawks (2004–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Atlanta Hawks (2004–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Smith was selected by the Atlanta Hawks with the 17th overall pick in the <a href="/wiki/2004_NBA_draft" title="2004 NBA draft">2004 NBA draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_Slam_Dunk_Contest" title="NBA Slam Dunk Contest">NBA Slam Dunk Contest</a> his rookie year during the 2005 NBA All-Star Weekend. He became only the fifth player to record three perfect scores in the same contest along with <a href="/wiki/Jason_Richardson" title="Jason Richardson">Jason Richardson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Carter" title="Vince Carter">Vince Carter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spud_Webb" title="Spud Webb">Spud Webb</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jordan" title="Michael Jordan">Michael Jordan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Smith averaged 9.7 points, 6.2 rebounds and 1.95 blocks per game for the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_NBA_season" title="2004–05 NBA season">2004–05 season</a> and was selected to the NBA All-Rookie Second Team.</p>
<p>On March 3, 2007, Smith broke the 500 career block mark, making him the youngest player to do so in NBA history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He ended the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_NBA_season" title="2006–07 NBA season">2006–07 season</a> with averages of 16.4 points, 8.6 rebounds, 3.3 assists, 1.4 steals and 2.9 blocks per game. On November 17, 2007, Smith scored a career-high 38 points on the road against the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 8, 2008, Smith signed an offer sheet with the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Grizzlies" title="Memphis Grizzlies">Memphis Grizzlies</a>, but the Hawks quickly matched the offer sheet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith_price_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-smith_price-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 2, 2010, in a 99–106 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_Thunder" title="Oklahoma City Thunder">Oklahoma City Thunder</a>, Smith became the youngest player (at 24 years old) to record 1,000 career blocked shots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> At season's end, he was named to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_All-Defensive_Second_Team" title="NBA All-Defensive Second Team">NBA All-Defensive Second Team</a> for the first time in his career.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Detroit_Pistons_.282013.E2.80.932014.29">Detroit Pistons (2013–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Detroit Pistons (2013–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 10, 2013, Smith signed with the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Pistons" title="Detroit Pistons">Detroit Pistons</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> on a reported four-year, $54 million deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 22, 2014, Smith recorded a first half career high of 24 points as he went on to finish with a season high of 32 in a 102–113 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He finished his first season in Detroit with averages of 16.4 points, 6.8 rebounds, 3.3 assists, 1.4 steals, and 1.4 blocks per game</p>
<p>On December 22, 2014, Smith was waived by the Pistons. In 28 games during the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season" title="2014–15 NBA season">2014–15 season</a>, Smith averaged 13.1 points, 7.2 rebounds and 4.7 assists per game, while shooting 39.1% from the field, 24.3% from three point range and 46.8% from the free throw line. In 105 career games with the Pistons, Smith averaged 15.5 points, 6.9 rebounds and 3.7 assists per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Houston_Rockets_.282014.E2.80.932015.29">Houston Rockets (2014–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Houston Rockets (2014–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 26, 2014, Smith signed with the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and went on to make his debut that night. In just under 32 minutes of action off the bench, he recorded 21 points, 8 rebounds and 3 assists in the Rockets' 117-111 overtime win over the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Grizzlies" title="Memphis Grizzlies">Memphis Grizzlies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Following that game, Smith was moved into the starting lineup but struggled as the Rockets won only one of their next four games. After a blowout loss to the <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans_Pelicans" title="New Orleans Pelicans">New Orleans Pelicans</a>, Smith was moved back to the bench in order to help him get more comfortable with the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2015_NBA_Playoffs" title="2015 NBA Playoffs">2015 NBA Playoffs</a>, Smith had multiple impressive games. In Game 2 of the first round against the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>, Smith tied his playoff career-high of 9 assists, 6 of which were alley-oops to childhood friend and former AAU teammate <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Howard" title="Dwight Howard">Dwight Howard</a>, as he helped lead the Rockets to victory. He later scored 23 and 20 points in Games 4 and 5, respectively, as the Rockets won the series in five games. In Game 6 of the conference semi-finals against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a>, he scored 19 points, 14 coming in the fourth quarter, as he led a comeback from 19 points down in an elimination game on the road to force, and eventually win, a decisive Game 7. During the playoffs, Smith made a career-high 38% of his three-point attempts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Los_Angeles_Clippers_.282015.E2.80.932016.29">Los Angeles Clippers (2015–2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Los Angeles Clippers (2015–2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 16, 2015, Smith signed a one-year deal with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He made his debut for the Clippers in the team's season opener against the Sacramento Kings on October 28, recording 1 point, 6 rebounds and 4 blocks off the bench in a 111–104 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Houston_.282016.29">Return to Houston (2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Houston (2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 22, 2016, Smith was traded back to the Houston Rockets, along with the draft rights to <a href="/wiki/Serhiy_Lishchuk" title="Serhiy Lishchuk">Serhiy Lishchuk</a> and cash considerations, in exchange for the draft rights to <a href="/wiki/Maarty_Leunen" title="Maarty Leunen">Maarty Leunen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He made his return game for the Rockets later that night, recording 2 points (on 1-of-10 shooting), 5 rebounds, 6 assists, 2 steals and 3 blocks in a 102–98 win over the Milwaukee Bucks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Two days later, in his second game back for the Rockets, Smith scored a then season-high 16 points in a 115–104 win over the Dallas Mavericks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He topped that mark on February 2, scoring 19 points as a starter in a 115–102 win over the Miami Heat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sichuan_Blue_Whales_.282016.E2.80.93present.29">Sichuan Blue Whales (2016–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josh_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sichuan Blue Whales (2016–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 8, 2016, Smith signed a three-month, $1.5+ million contract with the <a href="/wiki/Sichuan_Blue_Whales" title="Sichuan Blue Whales">Sichuan Blue Whales</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Basketball_Association" title="Chinese Basketball Association">Chinese Basketball Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
