<p><b>Sienna Tiggy Guillory</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/Ë/ primary stress follows">Ë</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">É¡</span><span title="/Éª/ short 'i' in 'bid'">Éª</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/Ér/ 'er' in 'finger'">Ér</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born 16 March 1975) is an English actress, and former model. She is known for playing the title role in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_miniseries" title="TV miniseries">TV miniseries</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helen_of_Troy_(TV_miniseries)" title="Helen of Troy (TV miniseries)">Helen of Troy</a></i>, as elf princess <a href="/wiki/List_of_Inheritance_Cycle_characters#Arya" title="List of Inheritance Cycle characters">Arya DrÃ¶ttningu</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_film" title="Fantasy film">fantasy</a>-<a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure film</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Eragon_(film)" title="Eragon (film)">Eragon</a></i>, and for her portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Jill_Valentine" title="Jill Valentine">Jill Valentine</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_film" title="Science fiction film">science fiction</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Apocalypse" title="Resident Evil: Apocalypse">Resident Evil: Apocalypse</a></i>. She resumed her role as Jill Valentine for a cameo appearance in the 2010 action-horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Afterlife" title="Resident Evil: Afterlife">Resident Evil: Afterlife</a></i>, and in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Retribution" title="Resident Evil: Retribution">Resident Evil: Retribution</a></i>, released on 14 September 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sienna_Guillory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Kettering" title="Kettering">Kettering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a>, England, Guillory is the daughter of the American folk guitarist <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Guillory" title="Isaac Guillory">Isaac Guillory</a> and his first wife, English model Tina Thompson, whom he married in 1973.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG-obit_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IG-obit-2">[2]</a></sup> Isaac Guillory was of American Jewish and Turkish heritage<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG-obit_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-IG-obit-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-3">[3]</a></sup> and born at the <a href="/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay_Naval_Base" title="Guantanamo Bay Naval Base">Guantanamo</a> naval base in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. He was the son of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_people" title="American people">American</a> sailor and his <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turkish</a> wife.</p>
<p>Her parents moved to <a href="/wiki/Fulham" title="Fulham">Fulham</a>, London when Guillory was two, then later to <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> when she was 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sunday_Times_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sunday_Times-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At the age of ten, she went to stay with cousins in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> to learn <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-divorce_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-divorce-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has a half-brother named Jace, who has the same mother. Her father adopted him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG-obit_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-IG-obit-2">[2]</a></sup> Her parents divorced in 1990 when Sienna was 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG-obit_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-IG-obit-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1993, her father married Vickie McMillan, and had two children with her, Ellie and Jacob.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG-obit_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-IG-obit-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Guillory attended <a href="/wiki/Gresham%27s_School" title="Gresham's School">Gresham's School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Holt,_Norfolk" title="Holt, Norfolk">Holt, Norfolk</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ogcab_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ogcab-1">[1]</a></sup> where she took part in numerous school productions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV-7">[7]</a></sup> She started horseback riding at the age of two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At 14, she was given a horse, which she named The Night Porter, or "Porty", after the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_Porter" title="The Night Porter">The Night Porter</a></i>; Guillory was a fan of its leading actress <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Rampling" title="Charlotte Rampling">Charlotte Rampling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-porter_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-porter-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2000, Guillory's father died of <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> at the age of 53.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG-obit_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-IG-obit-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sienna_Guillory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modelling">Modelling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sienna_Guillory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Modelling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1997, Guillory accompanied a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballerina" title="Ballerina">ballerina</a> friend to the London modelling agency <a href="/wiki/Select_Model_Management" title="Select Model Management">Select</a> and was soon signed. She modelled solely to support her acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scholastic_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-scholastic-10">[10]</a></sup> As a model, she worked in campaigns for <a href="/wiki/Armani" title="Armani">Armani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolce_%26_Gabbana" title="Dolce &amp; Gabbana">Dolce &amp; Gabbana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burberry" title="Burberry">Burberry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Smith_(fashion_designer)" title="Paul Smith (fashion designer)">Paul Smith</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-TV-7">[7]</a></sup> and appeared on the covers of several fashion magazines. In 1999, she became the face of the <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Boss" title="Hugo Boss">Hugo Boss</a> fragrance campaign, succeeding model Karen Ferrari and continuing the campaign for three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sunday_Times_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sunday_Times-4">[4]</a></sup> After modelling for a few years, Guillory returned her focus to acting in 2000. She is signed to Independent Models in London.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sienna_Guillory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Guillory explained that she "became an actor because I wanted to know what it was like to be other people. Because possibly I don't like myself", and later said "I hate acting, really hate it. I kind of fell into it sideways ... I started acting because I got offered a job when I was 16, and they wanted to pay me Â£8,000, and we'd always lived on Family Support..." She went on to say that her frustration with later film roles left her bitter, but after seeing <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a> onstage she was inspired again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-B_and_B_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-B_and_B-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1993, Guillory made her screen debut in a TV adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Jilly_Cooper" title="Jilly Cooper">Jilly Cooper</a>'s novel, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riders_(novel)" title="Riders (novel)">Riders</a></i>, for which the casting directors needed a young actress who was able to ride a horse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scholastic_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-scholastic-10">[10]</a></sup> Her performance landed her in another small role in the miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Buccaneers" title="The Buccaneers">The Buccaneers</a></i>, opposite <a href="/wiki/Mira_Sorvino" title="Mira Sorvino">Mira Sorvino</a>.</p>
<p>Before becoming a model to support her acting career, Guillory waited tables at a club. In an interview in 2000, she said about the experience "Supporting my acting habit with waitressing was probably the most useful thing I've done."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-London_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-London-12">[12]</a></sup> During her time as a model, she studied acting at the <a href="/wiki/New_World_School_of_the_Arts" title="New World School of the Arts">New World School of the Arts</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Conservatoire" title="Paris Conservatoire">Paris Conservatoire</a>, also taking small television and film roles in <i>The Future Lasts a Long Time</i> (1996), <i>The Rules of Engagement</i> (1999), <i>Star! Star!</i> (1999), <i>Kiss Kiss (Bang Bang)</i> (2000), <i>The 3 Kings</i> (2000), and <i>Two Days, Nine Lives</i> (2000). During this time, Guillory decided to go on with her acting career because "I was so scared of going to university and being with people my own age again, having been with that tiny little sect of girls in Norfolk whose daddies were farmers and politicians, who didn't trust me and didn't know where I was coming from".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-B_and_B_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-B_and_B-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>She returned to film with a more substantial role in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Sorted_(film)" title="Sorted (film)">Sorted</a></i> (2000). Guillory went on to appear in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> production <i><a href="/wiki/Take_a_Girl_Like_You_(TV_series)" title="Take a Girl Like You (TV series)">Take a Girl Like You</a></i>, a television adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Amis" title="Kingsley Amis">Kingsley Amis</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Take_a_Girl_Like_You" title="Take a Girl Like You">novel of the same name</a>. She portrayed Jenny Bunn, the story's virginal heroine, and gained critical attention for her part in the period piece. Guillory was pleased to have played a more innocent character, saying "I've played floozies, psychopaths, assassins, crackheads... It's nice to do something with a lighter touch."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The production did not fare well in the ratings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, Guillory continued with other roles in predominantly British films, including <i>Oblivious</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_Shopping" title="Late Night Shopping">Late Night Shopping</a></i> (with husband Enzo Cilenti), <i>The Last Minute</i>, and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Superstition_(2001_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Superstition (2001 film) (page does not exist)">Superstition</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">Â (<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superstition_%E2%80%93_Spiel_mit_dem_Feuer" title="de:Superstition â Spiel mit dem Feuer">de</a>)</span></i>. 2002 saw Guillory appear in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Time_Machine_(2002_film)" title="The Time Machine (2002 film)">The Time Machine</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Guy_Pearce" title="Guy Pearce">Guy Pearce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Irons" title="Jeremy Irons">Jeremy Irons</a>. She was pleased to take on a larger scale film, after having worked on low-budget independent films, and compared the experience with "finding an unicorn in your sock drawer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unicorn_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-unicorn-15">[15]</a></sup> She played Emma, the fiancÃ©e of Pearce's character, who was killed early in the film and whose death set off its main events.</p>
<p>In 2003, after a friend had received a script for <i>The Principles of Lust</i>, Guillory claims she "read it and couldn't put it down, and begged to read for the part". She was impressed by director <a href="/wiki/Penny_Woolcock" title="Penny Woolcock">Penny Woolcock</a>'s unconventional style of shooting, without rehearsing the actors, using raw takes and improvisational performances. For the role of Juliette, Guillory performed nude in the film's sex scenes which were filmed during the first week of filming, making her somewhat nervous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lust_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-lust-16">[16]</a></sup> She later went on to say that it was one of her favorite acting experiences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RT_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-RT-17">[17]</a></sup> Afterwards, Guillory had a small role in the romantic comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Actually" title="Love Actually">Love Actually</a></i> before taking the starring role in the television miniseries <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helen_of_Troy_(TV_miniseries)" title="Helen of Troy (TV miniseries)">Helen of Troy</a></i>. The series was mostly based on <a href="/wiki/Homer" title="Homer">Homer</a>'s epic poem <i><a href="/wiki/Iliad" title="Iliad">Iliad</a></i> and focuses on the mythological life of <a href="/wiki/Helen_of_Troy" title="Helen of Troy">Helen</a>. <i>Helen of Troy</i> was nominated for best miniseries at the <a href="/wiki/Satellite_Awards" title="Satellite Awards">Satellite Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>In May 2004, Guillory starred in a new stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shape_of_Things" title="The Shape of Things">The Shape of Things</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Ambassadors_Theatre_(London)" title="Ambassadors Theatre (London)">New Ambassadors Theatre</a> in London, with husband Cilenti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-3">[3]</a></sup> That year she also played her first big <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a> role. She was cast as <a href="/wiki/Jill_Valentine" title="Jill Valentine">Jill Valentine</a>, one of the lead characters in the video game adaptation sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Apocalypse" title="Resident Evil: Apocalypse">Resident Evil: Apocalypse</a></i>. The film makers had searched widely for an actress to portray the video game character and found her in Guillory, who had to "please not only the movie-going audience but also the gamers." She was "a dead ringer for Jill Valentine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HWjesus_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-HWjesus-20">[20]</a></sup> Guillory was reported to have studied the original character's movements by watching playthroughs of the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_3:_Nemesis" title="Resident Evil 3: Nemesis">Resident Evil 3: Nemesis</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>After the success of <i>Resident Evil: Apocalypse</i>, Guillory appeared in other film and television roles, including <i><a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Beauty_(film)" title="Beauty (film)">Beauty</a></i> (2004), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marple:_A_Murder_Is_Announced" title="Marple: A Murder Is Announced">Marple: A Murder Is Announced</a></i> (2005), <i>In the Bathroom</i> (2005), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Virgin_Queen_(TV_show)" title="The Virgin Queen (TV show)">The Virgin Queen</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Silence_Becomes_You" title="Silence Becomes You">Silence Becomes You</a></i> (2005), and <i>Rabbit Fever</i> (2006).</p>
<p>In 2006, Guillory was a lead in the ensemble cast of the fantasy <i><a href="/wiki/Eragon_(film)" title="Eragon (film)">Eragon</a>,</i> the film adaptation of the novel by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Paolini" title="Christopher Paolini">Christopher Paolini</a>; the role reunited her with <i>The Time Machine</i> co-star Jeremy Irons. She portrayed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arya_Dr%C3%B6ttningu" title="Arya DrÃ¶ttningu">Arya DrÃ¶ttningu</a>, an elf princess. She had not read the novels before filming, but after accepting the role she became a fan of Paolini's work. Although Arya is a key character in the film, Guillory had little screen time and went on to say jokingly: "I've been trying to explain to friends who've seen the trailer [...] I'm like, "[...] but that's my whole part!""<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RT_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-RT-17">[17]</a></sup> <i>Eragon</i> was not well received by the critics, but was nominated as Best Fantasy Film at the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saturn_Awards" title="Saturn Awards">Saturn Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The film grossed approximately <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>75Â million in the US and $173.9Â million elsewhere, totaling $249Â million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Guillory was invited to reprise her role as Jill Valentine in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Extinction" title="Resident Evil: Extinction">Resident Evil: Extinction</a></i>, the sequel to <i>Resident Evil: Apocalypse,</i> but could not accept due to scheduling conflicts with <i>Eragon.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, she starred in the Spanish production <i>El CorazÃ³n de la tierra,</i> or <i>The Heart of the Earth</i>, and later in the year finished filming the horror/thriller <i>Victims</i> and the fantasy novel adaptation <i><a href="/wiki/Inkheart_(film)" title="Inkheart (film)">Inkheart</a>.</i> In <i>Inkheart</i>, she plays Resa, the wife of <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Fraser" title="Brendan Fraser">Brendan Fraser</a>'s character and the mother of <a href="/wiki/Eliza_Bennett" title="Eliza Bennett">Eliza Bennett</a>'s. Guillory acted alongside <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a>, a friend of the Guillory family who was a role model when she first had aspirations towards acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-London_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-London-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Guillory was to be a lead in the television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Oaks_(TV_series)" title="The Oaks (TV series)">The Oaks</a>,</i> expected to begin airing in 2008. She joined <a href="/wiki/Bahar_Soomekh" title="Bahar Soomekh">Bahar Soomekh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lanter" title="Matt Lanter">Matt Lanter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romy_Rosemont" title="Romy Rosemont">Romy Rosemont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Renner" title="Jeremy Renner">Jeremy Renner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Rispoli" title="Michael Rispoli">Michael Rispoli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Lucio" title="Shannon Lucio">Shannon Lucio</a> in the series, which follows the intertwining stories of three familiesâa young couple in 1968 who have lost a child, a family of four in 1998, and a pregnant couple in 2008âwho are haunted by the same spirit in their house. The television series began filming on 5 November 2007 on location in <a href="/wiki/Pasadena,_California" title="Pasadena, California">Pasadena, California</a>. Guillory will play Jessica, the 2008 couple's neighbour, a woman who has <a href="/wiki/Asperger_syndrome" title="Asperger syndrome">Asperger syndrome</a> who went to school with the husband (and father-to-be) and shares a secret with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-names_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-names-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2010, <i>Shock Till You Drop.com</i> confirmed the return of Jill Valentine in the <i>Resident Evil</i> film franchise's fourth installment, <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Afterlife" title="Resident Evil: Afterlife">Resident Evil: Afterlife</a>,</i> with Guillory reprising the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 31 May, Jovovich also confirmed that Guillory would return as Jill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Guillory reappears in the film in a mid-end credit scene. She returned to a starring role in the following film, <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Retribution" title="Resident Evil: Retribution">Resident Evil: Retribution</a>,</i> as the main antagonist of the film.</p>
<p>In 2015 she joined the main cast as scientist Natalie Yelburton in the new thriller series <i><a href="/wiki/Fortitude_(TV_series)" title="Fortitude (TV series)">Fortitude</a>,</i> commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Sky_Atlantic" title="Sky Atlantic">Sky Atlantic</a> and set in the fictional Arctic Norwegian oil settlement of the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> She will return to the role for the show's second series in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2016 she also joined the cast of Sky One's new series, <i>Stan Lee's Lucky Man,</i> playing the character Eve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sienna_Guillory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2000, Guillory began dating English actor <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Cilenti" title="Enzo Cilenti">Enzo Cilenti</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-London_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-London-12">[12]</a></sup> and they married in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-horses_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-horses-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SomethingDeeplyUnfeminist_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-SomethingDeeplyUnfeminist-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Company_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Company-38">[38]</a></sup> The couple appeared in the 2001 film <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_Shopping" title="Late Night Shopping">Late Night Shopping</a></i>, and have continued to appear together in various works. In August 2004, Guillory and Cilenti cycled five stages of the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> to raise money for charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2011, Guillory gave birth to their twin daughters, Valentina and Lucia, named after Guillory's grandmother and great aunt, who were also twin sisters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
