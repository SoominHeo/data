<p><b>James Addison Baker III</b> (born April 28, 1930)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Historian_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Historian-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American attorney and statesman. He served as <a href="/wiki/White_House_Chief_of_Staff" title="White House Chief of Staff">White House Chief of Staff</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Secretary of the Treasury">United States Secretary of the Treasury</a> under President <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a>, and as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State" title="United States Secretary of State">U.S. Secretary of State</a> and White House Chief of Staff under President <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a>. As Secretary of State, he helped oversee <a href="/wiki/Foreign_policy_of_the_United_States" title="Foreign policy of the United States">U.S. foreign policy</a> during the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">dissolution of the Soviet Union</a>, as well as during the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">Gulf War</a>.</p>
<p>He is the namesake of the <a href="/wiki/James_A._Baker_III_Institute_for_Public_Policy" title="James A. Baker III Institute for Public Policy">James A. Baker III Institute for Public Policy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Rice_University" title="Rice University">Rice University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jbipp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jbipp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>James Addison Baker was born in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> at 1216 Bissonnet,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CofH-P4HDD_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CofH-P4HDD-3">[3]</a></sup> the son of <a href="/wiki/James_A._Baker,_Jr." title="James A. Baker, Jr.">James A. Baker, Jr.</a> (1892–1973) and Ethel Bonner (née Means) Baker (August 6, 1894 – April 26, 1991). His father was a partner of Houston law firm <a href="/wiki/Baker_Botts" title="Baker Botts">Baker Botts</a>. Baker has a sister, Bonner Baker Moffitt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His grandfather was attorney and banker <a href="/wiki/James_A._Baker,_Sr." title="James A. Baker, Sr.">James A. Baker, Sr.</a>, and his great-grandfather was jurist and politician <a href="/wiki/James_Addison_Baker_the_elder" title="James Addison Baker the elder">James Addison Baker the elder</a>.</p>
<p>Baker attended <a href="/wiki/The_Hill_School" title="The Hill School">The Hill School</a>, a boarding school in <a href="/wiki/Pottstown,_Pennsylvania" title="Pottstown, Pennsylvania">Pottstown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>. He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton University</a> in 1952 with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.B." title="A.B.">A.B.</a> in history <i>cum laude</i>. He was a member of <a href="/wiki/Phi_Delta_Theta" title="Phi Delta Theta">Phi Delta Theta</a>. Baker served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> (1952-1954), attaining the rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_Lieutenant#United_States" title="First Lieutenant">First Lieutenant</a> and later rising to <a href="/wiki/Captain_(United_States_O-3)" title="Captain (United States O-3)">Captain</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps_Reserve" title="United States Marine Corps Reserve">United States Marine Corps Reserve</a>. Afterwards, he earned a <a href="/wiki/Juris_Doctor" title="Juris Doctor">J.D.</a> (1957) from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_School_of_Law" title="University of Texas School of Law">The University of Texas at Austin</a> and began to practice law in Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1957 to 1969, and then from 1973 to 1975 he practiced law at the law firm of <a href="/wiki/Andrews_Kurth" title="Andrews Kurth">Andrews &amp; Kurth</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_political_career">Early political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baker's first wife, the former Mary Stuart McHenry, was active in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Republican_Party" title="United States Republican Party">Republican Party</a>, working on the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congressional</a> campaigns of <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a>. Originally, Baker had been a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Democratic_Party" title="United States Democratic Party">Democrat</a> but too busy trying to succeed in a competitive law firm to worry about politics, and considered himself <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apolitical" title="Apolitical">apolitical</a>. His wife's influence led Baker to politics and the Republican Party. He was a regular <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> partner of <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Country_Club" title="Houston Country Club">Houston Country Club</a> in the late 1950s. When Bush Sr. decided to vacate his <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">Congressional</a> seat and run for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Senate" title="U.S. Senate">U.S. Senate</a> in 1969, he supported Baker's decision to run for the Congressional seat he was vacating. However, Baker changed his mind about running for Congress when his wife was diagnosed with breast cancer; she died in February 1970.</p>
<p>Bush Sr. then encouraged Baker to become active in politics to help deal with the grief of his wife's death, something that Bush Sr. himself had done when his daughter, Pauline Robinson (1949–1953), died of <a href="/wiki/Leukemia" title="Leukemia">leukemia</a>. Baker became chairman of Bush's Senate campaign in <a href="/wiki/Harris_County,_Texas" title="Harris County, Texas">Harris County</a>, Texas. Though Bush lost to <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Bentsen" title="Lloyd Bentsen">Lloyd Bentsen</a> in the election, Baker continued in politics, becoming the Finance Chairman of the Republican Party in 1971. The following year, he was selected as Gulf Coast Regional Chairman for the <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon_presidential_campaign,_1968" title="Richard Nixon presidential campaign, 1968">presidential campaign</a>. In 1973 and 1974, in the wake of the Nixon Administration's <a href="/wiki/Watergate_scandal" title="Watergate scandal">implosion</a>, Baker returned to full-time law practice at Andrews &amp; Kurth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewhouseJ-TNY-1990-05-07_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NewhouseJ-TNY-1990-05-07-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PUL-BofJB_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-PUL-BofJB-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Baker's time away from politics was very brief, however. He served as Undersecretary of Commerce for President <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Ford" title="Gerald Ford">Gerald Ford</a> and ran Ford's unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1976" title="United States presidential election, 1976">1976 election campaign</a>. In 1978, with <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a> as his campaign manager, Baker ran unsuccessfully for <a href="/wiki/Texas_Attorney_General" title="Texas Attorney General">Attorney General of Texas</a>, losing to future <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texas_governor" title="Texas governor">Texas governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Mark_White" title="Mark White">Mark White</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reagan_Administration">Reagan Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reagan Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In 1981, Baker was named <a href="/wiki/White_House_Chief_of_Staff" title="White House Chief of Staff">White House Chief of Staff</a> by President <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a>, in spite of the fact that Baker managed the presidential campaigns of Gerald Ford in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)_presidential_primaries,_1976" title="Republican Party (United States) presidential primaries, 1976">1976</a> and of George Bush in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)_presidential_primaries,_1980" title="Republican Party (United States) presidential primaries, 1980">1980</a> opposing Reagan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He served in that capacity until 1985. Baker is considered to have had a high degree of influence over the first Reagan Administration, particularly in domestic policy.</p>
<p>In 1982, conservative activists <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Howard_Phillips_(politics)" title="Howard Phillips (politics)">Howard Phillips</a>, founder of <a href="/wiki/The_Conservative_Caucus" title="The Conservative Caucus">The Conservative Caucus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Clymer_Wright" title="Clymer Wright">Clymer Wright</a> of Houston joined in an unsuccessful effort to convince Reagan to dismiss Baker as Chief of Staff. They claimed that Baker, a former Democrat and a Bush political intimate, was undermining conservative initiatives in the administration. Reagan rejected the Phillips-Wright request, but in 1985, he named Baker as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Secretary of the Treasury">United States Secretary of the Treasury</a>, in a job-swap with then Secretary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donald_T._Regan" title="Donald T. Regan">Donald T. Regan</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/Merrill_Lynch" title="Merrill Lynch">Merrill Lynch</a> officer who became Chief of Staff. Reagan rebuked Phillips and Wright for having waged a "campaign of sabotage" against Baker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Baker managed Reagan's 1984 re-election campaign in which Reagan polled a record 525 electoral votes total (of 538 possible), and received 58.8 percent of the popular vote to <a href="/wiki/Walter_Mondale" title="Walter Mondale">Walter Mondale</a>'s 40.6 percent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>While serving as Treasury Secretary, Baker organized the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Accord" title="Plaza Accord">Plaza Accord</a> of September 1985 and the <a href="/wiki/Baker_Plan_(debt_relief)" title="Baker Plan (debt relief)">Baker Plan</a> to target international debt. He had <a href="/wiki/Richard_Darman" title="Richard Darman">Richard Darman</a> of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> as his Deputy Secretary of the Treasury. Darman would continue in the next administration as the Director of the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Management_and_Budget" title="Office of Management and Budget">Office of Management and Budget</a>.</p>
<p>In 1985, Baker received the U.S. Senator John Heinz Award for Greatest Public Service by an Elected or Appointed Official, an award given out annually by <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Awards_for_Public_Service" title="Jefferson Awards for Public Service">Jefferson Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jeffersonawards_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-jeffersonawards-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the Reagan Administration, Baker also served on the Economic Policy Council, where he played an instrumental role in achieving the passage of the administration's tax and budget reform package in 1981. He also played a role in the development of the Silver Eagle and the Gold Eagle, which both were released in 1986.</p>
<p>Baker also served on Reagan's <a href="/wiki/United_States_National_Security_Council" title="United States National Security Council">National Security Council</a>, and remained Treasury Secretary until 1988, during which time he also served as campaign chairman for George H. W. Bush's successful presidential bid.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bush_Administration">Bush Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bush Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_H.W._Bush#Foreign_policy" title="George H.W. Bush">George H.W. Bush § Foreign policy</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_H.W._Bush" title="George H.W. Bush">George H.W. Bush</a> appointed Baker <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State" title="United States Secretary of State">Secretary of State</a> in 1989. Baker served in this role through 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> From 1992 to 1993, he served as Bush's <a href="/wiki/White_House_Chief_of_Staff" title="White House Chief of Staff">White House Chief of Staff</a>, the same position that he had held from 1981 to 1985 during the first Reagan Administration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 9, 1991, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneva_Peace_Conference" title="Geneva Peace Conference">Geneva Peace Conference</a> with <a href="/wiki/Tariq_Aziz" title="Tariq Aziz">Tariq Aziz</a> in Geneva, Baker declared that "If there is any user of (chemical or biological weapons), our objectives won't just be the liberation of Kuwait, but the elimination of the current Iraqi regime...."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Baker later acknowledged that the intent of this statement was to threaten a retaliatory <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_warfare" title="Nuclear warfare">nuclear strike</a> on Iraq,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and the Iraqis received his message.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Baker helped to construct the 34-nation alliance that fought alongside the United States in the Gulf War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Medal_of_Freedom" title="Presidential Medal of Freedom">Presidential Medal of Freedom</a> in 1991.</p>

<p>Baker blocked the creation of Palestine by threatening to cut funding to agencies in the United Nations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> As far back as 1988, the <a href="/wiki/Palestine_Liberation_Organization" title="Palestine Liberation Organization">Palestine Liberation Organization</a> (PLO) issued a "declaration of statehood” and changed the name of its observer delegation to the United Nations from the PLO to Palestine.</p>
<p>Baker warned publicly, "I will recommend to the President that the United States make no further contributions, voluntary or assessed, to any international organization which makes any changes in the PLO's status as an observer organization."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Cabinet_career">Post-Cabinet career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-Cabinet career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1993.E2.80.932000">1993–2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1993–2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1993 Baker became the honorary chair of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Baker_Institute" title="James Baker Institute">James A. Baker III Institute of Public Policy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Rice_University" title="Rice University">Rice University</a> in Houston, Texas.</p>
<p>Also in 1993, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enron_Corporation" title="Enron Corporation">Enron Corporation</a> hired Baker as a consultant within a month of his departure from the White House, and Enron said that Baker would have an opportunity to invest in any projects he developed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_Times_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-NY_Times-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1995, Baker published his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Memoirs" title="Memoirs">memoirs</a> of service as Secretary of State in a book entitled <i>The Politics of Diplomacy: Revolution, War and Peace, 1989–1992</i> (<a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0399140875">ISBN 0-399-14087-5</a>).</p>
<p>In March 1997, Baker became the Personal Envoy of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN_Secretary-General" title="UN Secretary-General">UN Secretary-General</a> for <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In June 2004 he resigned from this position, frustrated over the lack of progress in reaching a complete settlement acceptable to both the government of <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> and the pro-independence <a href="/wiki/Polisario_Front" title="Polisario Front">Polisario Front</a>. He left behind the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baker_plan" title="Baker plan">Baker II plan</a>, accepted as a suitable basis of negotiations by the Polisario and unanimously endorsed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Security_Council" title="Security Council">Security Council</a>, but rejected by Morocco.</p>
<p>In addition to the numerous recognitions received by Baker, he was presented with the prestigious <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson_Awards" title="Woodrow Wilson Awards">Woodrow Wilson Award</a> for public service on September 13, 2000 in Washington, D.C..</p>
<p>In 2000, Baker served as chief legal adviser for <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_U.S._presidential_election" title="2000 U.S. presidential election">2000 election</a> campaign and oversaw the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_recount" title="Florida recount">Florida recount</a>. The 2008 film <i><a href="/wiki/Recount_(film)" title="Recount (film)">Recount</a></i> covers the days following the controversial election. During the making of the film Baker was interviewed. Baker was portrayed in the film by British actor <a href="/wiki/Tom_Wilkinson" title="Tom Wilkinson">Tom Wilkinson</a>.</p>
<p>On September 11, 2001, Baker watched television coverage of the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">attacks on the World Trade Center and the Pentagon</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ritz-Carlton_Hotel" title="Ritz-Carlton Hotel">Ritz-Carlton Hotel</a> in Washington DC, where Baker and representatives of <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden" title="Osama bin Laden">Osama bin Laden</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bin_Laden_family" title="Bin Laden family">family</a> were among those attending the annual conference for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carlyle_Group" title="Carlyle Group">Carlyle Group</a>. Baker is Senior Counselor for the Carlyle Group, and the bin Ladens are among its major investors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nndb_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-nndb-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Disputed statement"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (June 2015)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:James_Baker#Dubious" title="Talk:James Baker">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/State_of_Denial" title="State of Denial">State of Denial</a></i>, a book by investigative reporter <a href="/wiki/Bob_Woodward" title="Bob Woodward">Bob Woodward</a>, says that <a href="/wiki/White_House_Chief_of_Staff" title="White House Chief of Staff">White House Chief of Staff</a> <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Card" title="Andrew Card">Andrew Card</a> urged President Bush to replace <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_Defense" title="Secretary of Defense">Secretary of Defense</a> <a href="/wiki/Donald_Rumsfeld" title="Donald Rumsfeld">Donald Rumsfeld</a> with Baker following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_United_States_Presidential_election" title="2004 United States Presidential election">2004 election</a>. However, another G. H. W. Bush Administration veteran, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Gates" title="Robert Gates">Robert Gates</a>, was appointed instead, and only after the 2006 elections. Baker was elected a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="American Academy of Arts and Sciences">American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAAS_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAAS-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Iraq">Iraq</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Iraq">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In December 2003, President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> appointed Baker as his special envoy to ask various foreign creditor nations to forgive or restructure $100 billion in international debts owed by the Iraq government which had been incurred during the tenure of Saddam Hussein.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnnking_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnnking-23">[23]</a></sup> On March 15, 2006, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> announced the formation of the <a href="/wiki/Iraq_Study_Group" title="Iraq Study Group">Iraq Study Group</a>, a high-level panel of prominent former officials charged by members of Congress with taking a fresh look at <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a>'s policy on Iraq. Baker was the Republican Co-Chairman along with Democratic Congressman <a href="/wiki/Lee_H._Hamilton" title="Lee H. Hamilton">Lee H. Hamilton</a>, to advise Congress on <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PaleyA-WP-2006-10-9_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-PaleyA-WP-2006-10-9-24">[24]</a></sup> Baker also advised George W. Bush on <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Iraq Study Group examined a number of ideas, including one that would create a new power-sharing arrangement in Iraq that would give more autonomy to regional factions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SangerD-NYT-2006-10-09_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-SangerD-NYT-2006-10-09-26">[26]</a></sup> On October 9, 2006, the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_Post" title="Washington Post">Washington Post</a></i> quoted co-chairman Baker as saying "our commission believes that there are alternatives between the stated alternatives, the ones that are out there in the political debate, of 'stay the course' and 'cut and run'".</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_advisory_positions">Other advisory positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other advisory positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Baker serves on the Honorary Council of Advisers for the <a href="/wiki/U.S.-Azerbaijan_Chamber_of_Commerce" title="U.S.-Azerbaijan Chamber of Commerce">U.S.-Azerbaijan Chamber of Commerce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Justice_Project">World Justice Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: World Justice Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>James Baker serves as an Honorary Co-Chair for the <a href="/wiki/World_Justice_Project" title="World Justice Project">World Justice Project</a>. The World Justice Project works to lead a global, multidisciplinary effort to strengthen the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rule_of_Law" title="Rule of Law">Rule of Law</a> for the development of communities of opportunity and equity.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baker met his first wife, the former Mary Stuart McHenry, of <a href="/wiki/Dayton,_Ohio" title="Dayton, Ohio">Dayton, Ohio</a> while on <a href="/wiki/Spring_break" title="Spring break">spring break</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Princeton_Rugby" title="Princeton Rugby">Princeton University rugby team</a>. They married in 1953. Together they had four sons. Mary Stuart Baker (Mary Stuart was her full first name) died of breast cancer in February 1970.</p>
<p>In 1973, Baker and Susan Garrett Winston, a widow and a close friend of Mary Stuart, were married.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PUL-BofJB_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-PUL-BofJB-7">[7]</a></sup> She had two sons and a daughter with her late husband. She and Baker welcomed their daughter Mary Bonner Baker born in 1977.</p>
<p>On June 15, 2002, Virginia Graeme Baker, the seven-year-old granddaughter of Baker, daughter of Nancy and James Baker IV, was the victim of lethal suction-pump entrapment in an in-ground spa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> To promote greater safety in pools and spas, Nancy Baker gave testimony to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consumer_Product_Safety_Commission" title="Consumer Product Safety Commission">Consumer Product Safety Commission</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and James Baker helped form an advocacy group,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> which led to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virginia_Graeme_Baker_Pool_And_Spa_Safety_Act" title="Virginia Graeme Baker Pool And Spa Safety Act">Virginia Graeme Baker Pool And Spa Safety Act</a> (15 USC 8001).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
