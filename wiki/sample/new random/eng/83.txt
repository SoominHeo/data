<p>The <b>narcissus flycatcher</b> (<i>Ficedula narcissina</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a> family. It is native to east Asia, from <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin" title="Sakhalin">Sakhalin</a> to the north, through <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> across through <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland China</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, wintering in southeast Asia, including the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>. It is highly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, and has been found as a vagrant from Australia in the south to Alaska in the north <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.birdskorea.org/narcissustypes.asp" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.</p>
<p>Narcissus flycatcher males are very distinctive in full breeding plumage, having a black crown and mantle, a bright orange throat with paler chest and underparts, an orange-yellow eyebrow, black wings with a white wing patch, an orange-yellow rump, and a black tail. Non-breeding males have varying levels of yellow. Females are completely dissimilar, with generally buff-brown coloration, with rusty-colored wings, and a two-toned eyering.</p>
<p>This species primarily feeds on insects, and lives in deciduous woodlands. Breeding males sing in repeated melodious whistles. The <a href="/wiki/Green-backed_flycatcher" title="Green-backed flycatcher">green-backed flycatcher</a> was formerly considered a subspecies.</p>
<p>There are several subspecies, largely determined by plumage and range variations, at least of which has been split off as separate species.</p>
<ul>
<li><i>F. n. narcissina</i>, the nominate race, found from Sakhalin south to the Philippines</li>
<li><i>F. n. owstoni</i>, a short-range migrant based in the Ryukyu Islands, breeding males have an olive-green crown and mantle instead of black</li>
</ul>
<p>The name of the bird is a reference to the yellow color of many varieties of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narcissus_(genus)" title="Narcissus (genus)">narcissus</a> flower.</p>
