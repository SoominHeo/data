<p>An <b>art school</b> is an educational institution with a primary focus on the <a href="/wiki/Visual_arts" title="Visual arts">visual arts</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Illustration" title="Illustration">illustration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Graphic_design" title="Graphic design">graphic design</a>. Art schools are institutions with elementary, secondary, post-secondary or undergraduate, or graduate or postgraduate programs in these areas. They are distinguished from larger institutions which also may offer majors or degrees in the visual arts, but only as one part of a broad-based range of programs (such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liberal_arts" title="Liberal arts">liberal arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">sciences</a>). France's <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_des_Beaux-Arts" title="École des Beaux-Arts">École des Beaux-Arts</a> is, perhaps, the first model for such organized instruction, breaking with a tradition of <i>master and apprentice</i> instruction when it was formed.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Canada, there are four standalone Art and Design Universities. They are <a href="/wiki/Emily_Carr_University_of_Art_and_Design" title="Emily Carr University of Art and Design">Emily Carr University of Art and Design</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in Vancouver, <a href="/wiki/NSCAD_University" title="NSCAD University">NSCAD University</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in Halifax, <a href="/wiki/OCAD_University" title="OCAD University">OCAD University</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in Toronto, and <a href="/wiki/Alberta_College_of_Art_and_Design" title="Alberta College of Art and Design">Alberta College of Art and Design</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> in Calgary. Emily Carr university has the most active research program<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> among the four with over 15 million dollars in research funding over the last five years. OCAD University's research intensity has reached 3.2 million dollars in 2011/12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> All four schools teach in the major disciplines from painting through to new media and design. All four are public institutions with OCAD the largest followed by Emily Carr. Over the last five years, Emily Carr has garnered the most of the major awards for students and alums across the country. The most recent RBC Painting Competition was won by Vanessa Maltese, a graduate of OCAD University. OCAD has been very successful in the development of its graduate area with Emily Carr a close second. NSCAD has a long tradition of graduate teaching and learning.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Toronto-Area_Public_Art_Schools">Toronto-Area Public Art Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Toronto-Area Public Art Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claude_Watson_School_for_the_Arts" title="Claude Watson School for the Arts">Claude Watson School for the Arts</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karen_Kain_School_of_the_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karen Kain School of the Arts (page does not exist)">Karen Kain School of the Arts</a> are intermediate-age public art schools in Toronto, Ontario. They are continued by the Claude Watson program at <a href="/wiki/Earl_Haig_Secondary_School" title="Earl Haig Secondary School">Earl Haig Secondary School</a> and by the <a href="/wiki/Etobicoke_School_of_the_Arts" title="Etobicoke School of the Arts">Etobicoke School of the Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosedale_Heights_School_of_the_Arts" title="Rosedale Heights School of the Arts">Rosedale Heights School of the Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wexford_Collegiate_School_for_the_Arts" title="Wexford Collegiate School for the Arts">Wexford Collegiate School for the Arts</a>, and the Catholic board <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Carter_Academy_for_the_Arts" title="Cardinal Carter Academy for the Arts">Cardinal Carter Academy for the Arts</a>.</p>
<p>In Brampton, <a href="/wiki/Mayfield_Secondary_School" title="Mayfield Secondary School">Mayfield Secondary School</a>'s Regional Arts Program offers a public high school-level art school.</p>
<p>Innovators like Voice of Purpose out of Toronto, Ontario are currently working on promoting the Purpose Driven Education pedagogy through the use of arts-based programming. Currently through the development and delivery of workshops and keynotes to specialized arts high schools, as well as digital content on platforms online which aims to help students find and pursue their life's purpose.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In France, art schools have an quite old history. The oldest is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_des_Beaux-Arts_de_Paris" title="École des Beaux-Arts de Paris">Paris fine art school</a>, established in 1682, but most present public art schools are over two centuries old too : Nancy (1708), Toulouse (1726), Rouen (1741), etc. Some of those schools were called academies and were prestigious institutions, devoted to the education of great painters or sculptors. Others were called "école gratuite de dessin" (free school for drawing), and were devoted to the education of arts and craft artists.<br/>
As today, there is in France 45 national or territorial public high schools of art, that deliver bachelor (DNA) and Master (DNSEP) degrees. They don't belong to universities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Art schools have a history in Sweden since the first half of the 18th Century. Students may attend the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_Art" title="Royal Institute of Art">Royal Institute of Art</a>, which got its start in 1735. Established in 1844 originally as a part-time art school for Sunday artisans, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_College_of_Arts,_Crafts_and_Design" title="University College of Arts, Crafts and Design">University College of Arts, Crafts and Design</a> known as "Konstfack" is an arts college offering bachelor's and master's degrees in ceramics, glass, textiles, metalworking, and more.</p>
<p>There are also tertiary art schools attached to universities in Gothenburg, Malmö and Umeå.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Perhaps those generally felt most applicable to the definition of 'art school' are the autonomous colleges or schools of art offering courses across both <a href="/wiki/Further_education" title="Further education">further</a> and <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a> boundaries, of which there are approximately eighteen, under the banner of <a class="external text" href="http://www.ukadia.ac.uk" rel="nofollow">United Kingdom Art &amp; Design Institutions Association</a>. Others, whose existence ties in indelibly with that of larger, non-discipline-specific universities (such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slade_School_of_Art" title="Slade School of Art">Slade School of Art</a>) exist. Most art schools of either orientation are equipped to offer opportunities spanning from post-16 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postgraduate" title="Postgraduate">postgraduate</a> level.</p>
<p>The range of colleges span from predominantly further education establishments to research-led specialist institutes. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_London" title="University of the Arts London">University of the Arts London</a>, for example, is a federally structured institution that comprises six previously independent schools situated in London. These include <a href="/wiki/Camberwell_College_of_Arts" title="Camberwell College of Arts">Camberwell College of Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_Saint_Martins" title="Central Saint Martins">Central Saint Martins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_College_of_Arts" title="Chelsea College of Arts">Chelsea College of Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_College_of_Communication" title="London College of Communication">London College of Communication</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_College_of_Fashion" title="London College of Fashion">London College of Fashion</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wimbledon_College_of_Arts" title="Wimbledon College of Arts">Wimbledon College of Arts</a>; others include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Slade_School_of_Fine_Art" title="The Slade School of Fine Art">The Slade School of Fine Art</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Art" title="Royal College of Art">Royal College of Art</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goldsmiths_College,_University_of_London" title="Goldsmiths College, University of London">Goldsmiths College, University of London</a>, which each grant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undergraduate" title="Undergraduate">undergraduate</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postgraduate" title="Postgraduate">postgraduate</a> awards under one collegiate arm. The <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Art" title="Royal College of Art">Royal College of Art</a> with its <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">degree</a>-awarding arm and singular focus on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postgraduate" title="Postgraduate">postgraduate</a> awards being a most singular exception.</p>
<p>Outside of London Art Schools in the UK include <a class="external text" href="http://www.academyofrealistart.co.uk/" rel="nofollow">Academy of Realist Art UK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_College_of_Art" title="Leeds College of Art">Leeds College of Art</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_for_the_Creative_Arts" title="University for the Creative Arts">University for the Creative Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_College_of_Art" title="Edinburgh College of Art">Edinburgh College of Art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hereford_College_of_Arts" title="Hereford College of Arts">Hereford College of Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duncan_of_Jordanstone_College_of_Art_and_Design" title="Duncan of Jordanstone College of Art and Design">Duncan of Jordanstone College of Art &amp; Design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_School_of_Art" title="Glasgow School of Art">Glasgow School of Art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gray%27s_School_of_Art" title="Gray's School of Art">Gray's School of Art</a>, Moray School of Art (University of the Highlands and Islands), <a href="/wiki/Manchester_School_of_Art" title="Manchester School of Art">Manchester School of Art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Institute_of_Art_and_Design" title="Birmingham Institute of Art and Design">Birmingham Institute of Art and Design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coventry_School_of_Art_and_Design" title="Coventry School of Art and Design">Coventry School of Art and Design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwich_University_of_the_Arts" title="Norwich University of the Arts">Norwich University of the Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Falmouth_University" title="Falmouth University">Falmouth University</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plymouth_College_of_Art_and_Design" title="Plymouth College of Art and Design">Plymouth College of Art and Design</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University_School_of_Art_and_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Loughborough University School of Art and Design (page does not exist)">Loughborough University School of Art and Design</a> among others.</p>
<p>Since the 1970s, degrees have replaced diplomas as the top-tier qualification in the field.</p>
<p>In the case of wholly freestanding institutions, degree <a href="/wiki/Validation_of_foreign_studies_and_degrees" title="Validation of foreign studies and degrees">validation</a> agreements in liaison with a university have long been the custom for <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> (Hons) level upward. There has been a general trend for all-encompassing universities to offer programs in the visual arts, and formerly independent art schools have merged with <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_technology" title="Institute of technology">polytechnics</a> and universities to offer such degrees. A notable exception to this is the <a href="/wiki/City_and_Guilds_of_London_Art_School" title="City and Guilds of London Art School">City and Guilds of London Art School</a>, an independent art school solely focused on fine art and related disciplines such as carving and conservation. A few art schools have taken on university status themselves, namely the aforementioned <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Art" title="Royal College of Art">Royal College of Art</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_London" title="University of the Arts London">University of the Arts London</a>.</p>
<p>Most specialist institutions in the United Kingdom can trace their histories back to the nineteenth century or beyond, originating usually from government initiatives.</p>
<p>In April 2011 <a href="/wiki/Modern_Painters_(magazine)" title="Modern Painters (magazine)">Modern Painters (magazine)</a> surveyed art world professionals to create a list of the top 10 UK art schools which ranked them: 1. <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Art" title="Royal College of Art">Royal College of Art</a>, 2. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_Schools" title="Royal Academy Schools">Royal Academy Schools</a>, 3. <a href="/wiki/City_and_Guilds_of_London_Art_School" title="City and Guilds of London Art School">City and Guilds of London Art School</a>, 4. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slade_School_of_Art" title="Slade School of Art">Slade School of Art</a>, 5. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goldsmiths_College,_University_of_London" title="Goldsmiths College, University of London">Goldsmiths College, University of London</a>, 6. Glasgow School of Fine Art, 7. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Saint_Martins_College_of_Art_and_Design" title="Central Saint Martins College of Art and Design">Central Saint Martins College of Art and Design</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_London" title="University of the Arts London">University of the Arts London</a>, 8. <a href="/wiki/Camberwell_College_of_Arts" title="Camberwell College of Arts">Camberwell College of Arts</a>, University of the Arts London, 9. <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_College_of_Art" title="Edinburgh College of Art">Edinburgh College of Art</a> and 10. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design" title="Chelsea College of Art and Design">Chelsea College of Art and Design</a>, University of the Arts London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the U.S., art and design schools that offer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_fine_arts" title="Bachelor of fine arts">bachelor of fine arts</a> or <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Fine_Arts" title="Master of Fine Arts">Master of Fine Arts</a> degrees break down into basic types with some overlap and variations.</p>
<p>The most highly rated schools belong to a consortium formed in 1991 and called the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Independent_Colleges_of_Art_and_Design" title="Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design">Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design (AICAD)</a>. These schools differ from for-profit career schools in that they require a strong component of liberal arts courses in addition to art and design courses, providing a well-rounded college degree.</p>
<p>There also are partnerships between art schools and universities such as <a href="/wiki/School_of_the_Art_Institute_of_Chicago" title="School of the Art Institute of Chicago">School of the Art Institute of Chicago</a> with <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_University" title="Roosevelt University">Roosevelt University</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> the New England School of Art and Design at <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_University" title="Suffolk University">Suffolk University</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_Institute_of_Boston" title="Art Institute of Boston">Art Institute of Boston</a> at <a href="/wiki/Lesley_University" title="Lesley University">Lesley University</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_School_of_Design" title="Rhode Island School of Design">Rhode Island School of Design</a> with <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland_Institute_College_of_Art" title="Maryland Institute College of Art">Maryland Institute College of Art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johns_Hopkins_University" title="Johns Hopkins University">Johns Hopkins University</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corcoran_College_of_Art_and_Design" title="Corcoran College of Art and Design">Corcoran College of Art and Design</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_George_Washington_University" title="The George Washington University">The George Washington University</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/School_of_the_Museum_of_Fine_Arts,_Boston" title="School of the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston">School of the Museum of Fine Arts</a> in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Tufts_University" title="Tufts University">Tufts University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyler_School_of_Art" title="Tyler School of Art">Tyler School of Art</a> at <a href="/wiki/Temple_University" title="Temple University">Temple University</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parsons_The_New_School_for_Design" title="Parsons The New School for Design">Parsons The New School for Design</a> at <a href="/wiki/The_New_School" title="The New School">The New School</a>, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herron_School_of_Art" title="Herron School of Art">Herron School of Art at Indiana University</a>.</p>
<p>There is one state-supported independent art school in the U.S., <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_College_of_Art_and_Design" title="Massachusetts College of Art and Design">Massachusetts College of Art and Design</a>. The first Art School in the U.S. is the <a class="external text" href="http://www.pafa.org" rel="nofollow">Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts (PAFA)</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cooper_Union" title="Cooper Union">Cooper Union</a> in New York City is among the most selective of art schools, admitting 4%, with every student on half scholarship. The <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Art" title="Yale School of Art">Yale School of Art</a> at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> offers only graduate instruction in its two-year MFA programs. The <a href="/wiki/Yale_Daily_News" title="Yale Daily News">Yale Daily News</a> reported on Thursday, February 1, 2007 that the School had 1215 applications for its class of 2009 and would offer admission to fifty-five students.</p>
<p>Next up the scale in size for an art school would be a large art or design department, school, or college at a university. If it is a college, such as the College of Design, <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.design.iastate.edu/" rel="nofollow">[3]</a> at <a href="/wiki/Iowa_State_University" title="Iowa State University">Iowa State University</a> typically, it would contain programs that teach studio art, graphic design, photography, architecture, landscape architecture, interior design, or interior architecture, as well as art, design, and architectural history areas. Sometimes these are simply the schools of art, architecture, and design such as those at the <a href="/wiki/UIUC_College_of_Fine_and_Applied_Arts" title="UIUC College of Fine and Applied Arts">College of Fine and Applied Arts at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Art" title="Yale School of Art">Yale School of Art</a>. With over 3,000 students, <a href="/wiki/VCU_School_of_the_Arts" title="VCU School of the Arts">VCU School of the Arts</a> at <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Commonwealth_University" title="Virginia Commonwealth University">Virginia Commonwealth University</a> is one of the largest art schools in the nation and is also has achieved the highest ranking ever for a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public university</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Variation exists among art schools that are larger institutions, however, the essential element is that programs at universities tend to include more liberal arts courses and slightly less studio work, when compared to dedicated, but independent, schools of art.</p>
<p>The final and most common type of art school, a state supported or private program, would be at a university or college. It typically is a BA program, but also might be a BFA, MA, or MFA. These programs tend to emphasize a more general degree in art and do not require a major in a specific field, but might offer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_major" title="Academic major">concentrations</a>. A concentration is not accepted by some accrediting or professional organizations as being adequate preparation in some fields that would lead to success as a professional. This is the case for graphic design, where typically, the minimal degree is a BFA major in graphic design.</p>
<p>Many of the degree-offering institutions do not offer intense training in classical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Realism_(visual_arts)" title="Realism (visual arts)">realism</a> and academic <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drawing" title="Drawing">drawing</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Lyme_Academy_College_of_Fine_Arts" title="Lyme Academy College of Fine Arts">Lyme Academy College of Fine Arts</a> is considered a collegiate version of this educational model. This gap is filled by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atelier_Method" title="Atelier Method">Atelier</a> art schools (schools located inside an artist's studio) or in separate locations, such as the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Academy_of_Art" title="New York Academy of Art">New York Academy of Art</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Design" title="National Academy of Design">National Academy of Design</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Studio_School" title="New York Studio School">New York Studio School</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Academy_of_the_Fine_Arts" title="Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts">Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts (PAFA)</a>, established 1805, the <a href="/wiki/Art_Students_League_of_New_York" title="Art Students League of New York">Art Students League of New York</a>, established in 1875.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_art_schools">Online art schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Online art schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In recent years a number of art schools have begun to offer some or all of their curricula online, which by nature, transcends national boundaries. Among these are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Art_Institute_of_Pittsburgh" title="The Art Institute of Pittsburgh">The Art Institute of Pittsburgh</a> Online and <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Art_University" title="Academy of Art University">Academy of Art University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> As with on-ground schools, many of the majors involve computer-based work, such as compositions created in Photoshop, Illustrator, or 3D-Studio Max. Submission and review of these materials proceeds virtually identically for on-ground and online classes. When online courses require production of traditional drawings or other such materials, they usually are photographed or scanned for submission and review by instructors.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Art_school_culture">Art school culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Art school culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In contrast to the contemporary stereotype professional art and design education accredited by the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Schools_of_Art_and_Design" title="National Association of Schools of Art and Design">National Association of Schools of Art and Design</a> or offered by the members of the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Independent_Colleges_of_Art_and_Design" title="Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design">Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design</a> incorporates rigorous liberal arts and general education requirements so that students receive an authentic college or university degree.</p>
