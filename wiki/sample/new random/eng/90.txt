<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Isotopes of scandium</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>Naturally occurring <b><a href="/wiki/Scandium" title="Scandium">scandium</a></b> (<b>Sc</b>) is composed of one stable <a href="/wiki/Isotope" title="Isotope">isotope</a> <sup>45</sup>Sc. Twenty-four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radioisotope" title="Radioisotope">radioisotopes</a> have been characterized with the most stable being <sup>46</sup>Sc with a <a href="/wiki/Half-life" title="Half-life">half-life</a> of 83.8 days, <sup>47</sup>Sc with a half-life of 3.35 days, and <sup>48</sup>Sc with a half-life of 43.7 hours and <a href="/wiki/Scandium-44" title="Scandium-44">Sc-44</a> with a half-life of 3.97 hours. All of the remaining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radioactive" title="Radioactive">radioactive</a> isotopes have half-lives that are less than four hours, and the majority of these have half-lives that are less than two minutes, the least stable being <sup>39</sup>Sc with a half-life shorter than 300 nanoseconds. The half-lives for isotopes with mass numbers less than 39 is unknown. This element also has 10 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meta_state" title="Meta state">meta states</a> with the most stable being <sup>44m</sup>Sc (t<sub>1/2</sub> 58.6 h).</p>
<p>The isotopes of scandium range in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atomic_weight" title="Atomic weight">atomic weight</a> from 36 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atomic_mass_unit" title="Atomic mass unit">u</a> (<sup>36</sup>Sc) to 60 u (<sup>60</sup>Sc). The primary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decay_mode" title="Decay mode">decay mode</a> at masses lower than the only stable isotope, <sup>45</sup>Sc, is <a href="/wiki/Beta_decay" title="Beta decay">Beta-plus</a> or <a href="/wiki/Electron_capture" title="Electron capture">electron capture</a>, and the primary mode at masses above it is beta-minus. The primary <a href="/wiki/Decay_product" title="Decay product">decay products</a> at atomic weights below <sup>45</sup>Sc are <a href="/wiki/Isotopes_of_calcium" title="Isotopes of calcium">calcium isotopes</a> and the primary products from higher atomic weights are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titanium_isotopes" title="Titanium isotopes">titanium isotopes</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Relative_atomic_mass" title="Relative atomic mass">Relative atomic mass</a>: 44.955912(6).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isotopes_of_scandium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Values marked # are not purely derived from experimental data, but at least partly from systematic trends. Spins with weak assignment arguments are enclosed in parentheses.</li>
<li>Uncertainties are given in concise form in parentheses after the corresponding last digits. Uncertainty values denote one standard deviation, except isotopic composition and standard atomic mass from IUPAC, which use expanded uncertainties.</li>
<li>Nuclide masses are given by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUPAP" title="IUPAP">IUPAP</a> Commission on Symbols, Units, Nomenclature, Atomic Masses and Fundamental Constants (SUNAMCO)</li>
<li>Isotope abundances are given by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUPAC" title="IUPAC">IUPAC</a> <a href="/wiki/Commission_on_Isotopic_Abundances_and_Atomic_Weights" title="Commission on Isotopic Abundances and Atomic Weights">Commission on Isotopic Abundances and Atomic Weights</a></li>
</ul>
