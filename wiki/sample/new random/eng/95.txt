<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Liu Wu, Prince of Chu</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Liu_Wu,_Prince_of_Liang" title="Liu Wu, Prince of Liang">Liu Wu, Prince of Liang</a>.</div>
<p><b>Liu Wu</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">刘戊</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">劉戊</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Liǘ Wù</span></i>, died 154 BC) was the son of <a href="/wiki/Liu_Yingke" title="Liu Yingke">Liu Yingke</a>, Prince Yi of Chu. After the short reign of his father, he inherited the title Prince of Chu in 174 BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Han14_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Han14-1">[1]</a></sup> In 155 BC, <a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Bo" title="Empress Dowager Bo">Empress Dowager Bo</a> died: Liu Wu was caught drinking during the grieving period, so <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Jing_of_Han" title="Emperor Jing of Han">Emperor Jing of Han</a> reduced the size of his land. Wu was later convinced to join the <a href="/wiki/Rebellion_of_the_Seven_States" title="Rebellion of the Seven States">Rebellion of the Seven States</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liu_Pi_(prince)" title="Liu Pi (prince)">Li Pi</a> despite objections from his prime minister and tutor. Wu put both of them to death.</p>
<p>In 154, he launched his campaign against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Principality_of_Liang" title="Principality of Liang">principality of Liang</a> but was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Yafu" title="Zhou Yafu">Zhou Yafu</a>. When remaining troops surrendered and his supply cutoff, Wu committed suicide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Han35_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Han35-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Han36_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Han36-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grand50_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grand50-4">[4]</a></sup> His son Liu Li was allowed to succeed the Prince of Chu title despite the rebellion.</p>
