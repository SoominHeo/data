<p><i><b>Fantasy! Jūichi</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">Fantasy! 拾壱</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Fantasy! Eleven</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the eleventh studio album by all-girl Japanese idol group <a href="/wiki/Morning_Musume" title="Morning Musume">Morning Musume</a>. The album was released on December 1, 2010, in a normal and limited edition, the first press of the normal edition coming with a photocard, and the limited edition coming with a bonus DVD<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (containing solo shots of each member for the "<a href="/wiki/Onna_to_Otoko_no_Lullaby_Game" title="Onna to Otoko no Lullaby Game">Onna to Otoko no Lullaby Game</a>" <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">PV</a>, and the graduation announcement)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CDJ_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CDJ-2">[2]</a></sup> and a different cover. The album, their second album release in less than a year, was the last to feature sixth-generation member <a href="/wiki/Eri_Kamei" title="Eri Kamei">Eri Kamei</a> and eighth-generation members <a href="/wiki/Junjun_(singer)" title="Junjun (singer)">Junjun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Linlin" title="Linlin">Linlin</a> prior to their December 15 graduation.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_development">Writing and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fantasy!_J%C5%ABichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writing and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album's concept, as stated by writer and producer <a href="/wiki/Tsunku" title="Tsunku">Tsunku</a>, is "a mixture of fantasy and reality".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liner_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Liner-3">[3]</a></sup> The album's songs represent various genres, from rock and pop to Motown. The opening song, "Onna to Otoko no Lullaby Game", is described as the "prologue" to the album.</p>
<p>The album features two solos: "Ai no Honō", performed by <a href="/wiki/Reina_Tanaka" title="Reina Tanaka">Reina Tanaka</a>, and "Denwa de ne", performed by <a href="/wiki/Ai_Takahashi" title="Ai Takahashi">Ai Takahashi</a>. While these are the only solos, other members are allocated main vocals on some songs: <a href="/wiki/Sayumi_Michishige" title="Sayumi Michishige">Sayumi Michishige</a> on "Fantasy ga Hajimaru", <a href="/wiki/Risa_Niigaki" title="Risa Niigaki">Risa Niigaki</a> on "Onna Gokoro to Nan to Yara", and <a href="/wiki/Eri_Kamei" title="Eri Kamei">Eri Kamei</a> on "Itoshiku Kurushii Kono Yoru ni".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liner_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Liner-3">[3]</a></sup> Similarly to the group's previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/10_My_Me" title="10 My Me">10 My Me</a></i>, the eighth generation, <a href="/wiki/Aika_Mitsui" title="Aika Mitsui">Aika Mitsui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Junjun_(singer)" title="Junjun (singer)">Junjun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Linlin" title="Linlin">Linlin</a> are heavily featured on the same track: track 7, "Sungoi My Birthday".</p>
<p>Ai Takahashi and Eri Kamei provide almost all of the chorus vocals on the album; Kamei is featured in the chorus of all but two songs, Tanaka and Takahashi's solos, and Takahashi is featured on all songs except tracks 5 and 9. Producer Tsunku also appears in the chorus for track 2, "Bravo!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fantasy!_J%C5%ABichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album only features two singles, a rarity for the group. It also does not include "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Appare_Kaiten_Zushi!&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Appare Kaiten Zushi! (page does not exist)">Appare Kaiten Zushi!</a>", a promotional song released under the name "Muten Musume".</p>
<p>The first single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Onna_to_Otoko_no_Lullaby_Game" title="Onna to Otoko no Lullaby Game">Onna to Otoko no Lullaby Game</a>", was released on November 27, 2010 as the group's 44th single. Although it was a graduation single it charted relatively poorly, peaking at #6 on the Oricon singles chart, despite a reported total of 42,405 copies in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second and final single to be featured on the album, "<a href="/wiki/Seishun_Collection" title="Seishun Collection">Seishun Collection</a>", was released on June 6, 2010. The single, their 43rd, reached a weekly peak of #3 on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> singles charts, making it one of the group's more successful singles of recent years.</p>
