<p><b>Fly High</b> is the debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> by <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> boy group <a href="/wiki/Infinite_(band)" title="Infinite (band)">Infinite</a>'s sub-unit <a href="/wiki/Infinite_H" title="Infinite H">Infinite H</a>. The EP was released on January 11 and immediately topped the charts upon release.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fly_High_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After a series of image and video teasers, the music video for promotional track "Special Girl" was released on January 10, 2013 on Woollim Entertainment's official <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel and following the music video's release, the duo also performed the song and their follow up track "Without You" on M! Countdown on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allkpop.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allkpop.com-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">[2]</a></sup> Following the release of the music video, the EP was officially released on January 11, 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fly_High_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Focusing mainly on the genre hip hop, Infinite H (Dongwoo and Hoya) collaborated with many different artists, including various well-known hip hop composers and rappers such as Primary, <a href="/wiki/Zion.T" title="Zion.T">Zion.T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bumkey" title="Bumkey">Bumkey</a>, Paloalto, Vida Loca, and more. The album's title track, "Fly High," features labelmate Baby Soul, who was featured on the sub-unit's first ever track "Crying" as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fly_High_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video for "Special Girl" was uploaded onto Woollim Entertainment's official YouTube channel on January 10, 2013 and on January 28, 2013, the music video for the follow up track, "Without You" was also uploaded onto YouTube.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotions">Promotions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fly_High_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Promotions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Infinite H began their promotions by performing their title track and their follow up song, "Without You" on January 10, 2013 episode of <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a> on the same day when the music video for "Special Girl" was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allkpop.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-allkpop.com-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">[2]</a></sup> The duo also performed on <a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Show!_Music_Core" title="Show! Music Core">Show! Music Core</a> on January 11 and 12 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They continued to perform on various music programs to promote the EP.</p>
