<p>"<b>Focus</b>" is a song recorded by American singer <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a>, released on October 30, 2015. Originally intended to be the lead single from her third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Woman_(album)" title="Dangerous Woman (album)">Dangerous Woman</a></i>, the song was scrapped from the standard edition track listing, with the track only being included on the Japanese edition of the album. Later, "<a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Woman_(song)" title="Dangerous Woman (song)">Dangerous Woman</a>" replaced it as the lead single. The song was written by <a href="/wiki/Savan_Kotecha" title="Savan Kotecha">Savan Kotecha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Svensson" title="Peter Svensson">Peter Svensson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ilya_Salmanzadeh" title="Ilya Salmanzadeh">Ilya</a> and Grande.</p>
<p>The song debuted and peaked at number seven on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, becoming Grande's sixth top-ten single and selling 113,000 downloads in its first week. As of March 2016, "Focus" has sold 425,000 copies in the United States and has been certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ussales_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ussales-1">[1]</a></sup> The single won Best Song To Dance To at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Radio_Disney_Music_Awards" title="2016 Radio Disney Music Awards">2016 Radio Disney Music Awards</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_release">Background and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grande posted an unfocused picture of herself on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> in July 2015 with the caption "focus", stating it was a hint. Grande officially announced the single during her September 15, 2015 appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i> and revealed that it would be released on October 30, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goodman_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Goodman-3">[3]</a></sup> Grande started revealing parts of the song's lyrics, video and audio previews of the song, as well as pixelated versions of the single's cover in early October through Instagram and <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>. She revealed the official "Focus" single art cover on October 14, 2015. A week before its release date, a 15-second section of the track was used in a commercial for her debut fragrance, Ari by Ariana Grande.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Removal_from_Dangerous_Woman">Removal from <i>Dangerous Woman</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Removal from Dangerous Woman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The song was originally intended to be the lead single from <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Woman_(album)" title="Dangerous Woman (album)">Dangerous Woman</a></i>, Grande's 2016 album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> which she first titled <i>Moonlight</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i> in January 2016, however, Grande revealed that she might re-title the album after another song on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She announced the new title of the album on February 22, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Grande released the single "<a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Woman_(song)" title="Dangerous Woman (song)">Dangerous Woman</a>", on March 11, 2016, along with the pre-order of the album, with a partial track listing that did not include "Focus".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Her record company later confirmed to <i>Billboard</i> that "Focus" was removed from the standard edition of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> However, it is included as a bonus track on the Japanese edition of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JPA_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-JPA-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to Ryan Reed of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, "Focus" is "built on stabbing big-band brass, slick synths and clanging percussion".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The song's structure has been heavily compared to Grande's previous lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Problem_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="Problem (Ariana Grande song)">Problem</a>", from her album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Everything_(Ariana_Grande_album)" title="My Everything (Ariana Grande album)">My Everything</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Tonight" title="Entertainment Tonight">Entertainment Tonight</a></i>, Grande acknowledged its similarity with "Problem", stating that she thought this song was "the perfect transition record from the last album to the new album, because it sounds like 'Problem 2.0'".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> "Focus" features uncredited vocals from <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song is written in the key of <a href="/wiki/G-sharp_minor" title="G-sharp minor">G<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♯</span> minor</a> and follows a <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">tempo</a> of 100 beats per minute. Grande's vocals span from C<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♯</span><sub>4</sub> to F<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♯</span><sub>6</sub> in the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaitlyn Tiffany from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Verge" title="The Verge">The Verge</a></i> said, "As Ariana Grande is a massively talented vocalist, the song 'Focus' itself is very pleasant to listen to".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Brennan Carley of <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> commented that the song was a "catchy slow-burn", describing it as a "horn heavy piece of retro-modern fusion". He also said that Grande's vocals "[lacked] any real excitement from a star who’s got the restraint and skill to take her instrument places most other pop singers can't anymore". He criticized the "male howling the faux-chorus" for "[getting] grating quickly" and for making "Focus" too similar to Grande's previous lead single "<a href="/wiki/Problem_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="Problem (Ariana Grande song)">Problem</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Nolan Feeney also said it was similar to "Problem", but said this might not be a bad thing: "If it ain't broke, don't fix it—no need for Grande to check her vision here".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Steven J. Horowitz and Alex Gale from <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> rated the song two-and-a-half out of five stars, commenting "Dating back to her 2011 debut "Put Your Hearts Up," Grande has never released a less-than-great single, until now, with 'Focus', a rehash of 2014's 'Problem'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> named "Focus" the sixth-worst song of 2015: "Grande needed a tightly constructed, elegant hit, and this isn't it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, "Focus" debuted at number seven on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, becoming Grande's sixth top ten single (and fourth top ten debut), selling 113,000 copies in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top10_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-top10-24">[24]</a></sup> It became her first top 10 song in the US unaccompanied by a featured artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top10_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-top10-24">[24]</a></sup> The song fell to number 13 in its second week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> As of March 2016, "Focus" had sold 425,000 copies in the United States and had been certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ussales_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ussales-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Focus" debuted at number 10 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Official_Singles_Chart" title="Official Singles Chart">Official Singles Chart</a> in the UK, marking it as Grande's third top 10 single in the country, following number one singles "<a href="/wiki/Problem_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="Problem (Ariana Grande song)">Problem</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Bang_Bang_(Jessie_J,_Ariana_Grande_and_Nicki_Minaj_song)" title="Bang Bang (Jessie J, Ariana Grande and Nicki Minaj song)">Bang Bang</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grande performed the song live for the first time on the Honda Stage at the iHeartRadio Theater LA on October 30, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> She also performed "Focus" at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2015" title="American Music Awards of 2015">2015 American Music Awards</a> held on November 22, 2015, which was ranked by <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> as the best performance of the night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Grande performed "Focus" along with a cover of "Zero to Hero" on <a href="/wiki/Disney_Parks_Christmas_Day_Parade" title="Disney Parks Christmas Day Parade">Disney's Christmas Day Special</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Lux_Davis" title="Hannah Lux Davis">Hannah Lux Davis</a> directed the music video, which was released on October 29, 2015. She had previously directed the music videos for Grande's "<a href="/wiki/Love_Me_Harder" title="Love Me Harder">Love Me Harder</a>" and her joint single "<a href="/wiki/Bang_Bang_(Jessie_J,_Ariana_Grande_and_Nicki_Minaj_song)" title="Bang Bang (Jessie J, Ariana Grande and Nicki Minaj song)">Bang Bang</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> It <a href="/wiki/Product_placement" title="Product placement">features</a> the <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_Note_5" title="Samsung Galaxy Note 5">Samsung Galaxy Note 5</a> throughout the music video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The video surpassed 100 million views on November 19, 2015, making it Grande's eighth Vevo-certified music video after "<a href="/wiki/Baby_I" title="Baby I">Baby I</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> By December 2016, the video had been viewed more than 630 million times on YouTube.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The video begins with video flashes of Grande in a two-piece outfit and platinum hair as shown in the single art cover. She makes several different poses while standing/dancing inside of a circular shape. The circle is meant to look like a camera lens and is one way of using the song title, "Focus". As the video progresses, Grande and numerous female background dancers perform multiple choreographed numbers, including the big bridge choreographed number halfway into the track. For this number, they wear matching outfits that are mostly black with white stars. The dancers have a different style of outfit, wearing starry pants. While still matching, Ariana's outfit is a starry bodysuit. The song progresses on and ends with Grande striking a final pose as the track ends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Focus_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nick Maslow of <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People</a></i> says, ""Focus" finds Grande in a world of purple – even the diva's locks are a shade of lavender, a look she teased on Instagram earlier this month." He goes on to say, "Grande embraces her sultry side for the clip, performing intense choreography and delivering flirty lines like, 'Just come and get it – let them say what they say.'"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Michelle Geslani of <i><a href="/wiki/Consequence_of_Sound" title="Consequence of Sound">Consequence of Sound</a></i> praised the video as, "A groovy number from the get-go, it's highlighted by a massive chorus which couples the stylish attitude and busy percussion of tracks like "<a href="/wiki/Bang_Bang_(Jessie_J,_Ariana_Grande_and_Nicki_Minaj_song)" title="Bang Bang (Jessie J, Ariana Grande and Nicki Minaj song)">Bang Bang</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Problem_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="Problem (Ariana Grande song)">Problem</a>" with some urgent pleading that strangely enough reminds me of <a href="/wiki/Mystikal" title="Mystikal">Mystikal</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Shake_Ya_Ass" title="Shake Ya Ass">Shake Ya Ass</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
