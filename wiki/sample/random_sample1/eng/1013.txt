<p><i><b>Forever, Michael</b></i> is the fourth studio album by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, released by <a href="/wiki/Motown" title="Motown">Motown Records</a> on January 16, 1975. <i>Forever, Michael</i> was generally well received by contemporary music critics. Unlike Jackson's previous studio albums, the album was not commercially successful worldwide. Except for the peak position of number 101 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and number 10 on a <i>Billboard</i> component chart in the United States, the album did not chart on any music charts. The album has reportedly sold over one million copies worldwide since its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album, with the length of over 30 minutes, is credited as having songs with R&amp;B, pop, soul and pop rock material. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Holland,_Jr." title="Edward Holland, Jr.">Edward Holland, Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Holland" title="Brian Holland">Brian Holland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hal_Davis" title="Hal Davis">Hal Davis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Perren" title="Freddie Perren">Freddie Perren</a>, Sam Brown III served as producers to <i>Forever, Michael</i>. As part of promotion for the album, three singles were released from <i>Forever, Michael</i>, all of which were moderate commercial successes on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and other music charts worldwide. Songs from the album were reissued in 2009 after <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Michael_Jackson" title="Death of Michael Jackson">Jackson's death in June of the same year</a> as part of the 3-disc compilation album entitled, <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_World:_The_Motown_Solo_Collection" title="Hello World: The Motown Solo Collection">Hello World: The Motown Solo Collection</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Album_information">Album information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forever,_Michael&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Album information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was Jackson's fourth as a solo artist and would end up being his final album released with Motown before he and his brothers (<a href="/wiki/The_Jackson_5" title="The Jackson 5">The Jackson 5</a>, save for Jermaine, who would remain with Motown) left for <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">CBS Records</a> a year later. This album displayed a change in musical style for the 16-year-old, who adopted a smoother <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a> sound that he would continue to develop on his later solo records for <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>.</p>
<p>Most of the tracks were recorded in 1974, and the album was originally set to be released that year. However, because of demand from the Jackson 5's huge hit "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Machine" title="Dancing Machine">Dancing Machine</a>", production on Jackson's album was delayed until the hype from that song died down.</p>
<p>The album helped return Jackson to the top 40, aided by the singles "<a href="/wiki/We%27re_Almost_There" title="We're Almost There">We're Almost There</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Little_Bit_of_You" title="Just a Little Bit of You">Just a Little Bit of You</a>", both written by the Holland Brothers (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Holland,_Jr." title="Edward Holland, Jr.">Eddie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Holland" title="Brian Holland">Brian</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Holland%E2%80%93Dozier%E2%80%93Holland" title="Holland–Dozier–Holland">Holland–Dozier–Holland</a>.</p>
<p>In 1981, Motown released "<a href="/wiki/One_Day_in_Your_Life_(Michael_Jackson_song)" title="One Day in Your Life (Michael Jackson song)">One Day in Your Life</a>" as a single, coupled with the <i><a href="/wiki/One_Day_in_Your_Life_(album)" title="One Day in Your Life (album)">One Day in Your Life</a></i> compilation album release, to capitalize off Jackson's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Off_the_Wall_(album)" title="Off the Wall (album)">Off the Wall</a> success on Epic. The single went to number one in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>, becoming the 6th best-selling single of 1981 in the UK. This is the only Jackson studio album that doesn't share a name with one of the songs on the album.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">compact disc</a> version of the album removes the white border around the photograph of Jackson from the album cover, and instead makes the image larger so that the background can not be seen. Furthermore, the "FOREVER, MICHAEL" text is changed to more simple text which lists both "MICHAEL JACKSON" and "Forever, Michael" in a different font.</p>
