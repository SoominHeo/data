<p><i><b>Fragmen</b></i> (Fragment) is Malaysian recording artist <a href="/wiki/Siti_Nurhaliza" title="Siti Nurhaliza">Siti Nurhaliza</a>'s sixteenth studio album and her fifteenth <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a>-language album. It was released physically on 30 June 2014 by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group (Malaysia)</a> in a collaboration with her own record company, Siti Nurhaliza Productions. Lyrically, most of the songs included in the album is inspired by her life and experiences. The production of the album saw a collaboration between Malaysian and Indonesian composers, lyricists and producers.</p>
<p><i>Fragmen</i> has so far spawned four singles for the Malaysian market, "<a href="/wiki/Lebih_Indah" title="Lebih Indah">Lebih Indah</a>" ("More Meaningful"), "<a href="/wiki/Jaga_Dia_Untukku" title="Jaga Dia Untukku">Jaga Dia Untukku</a>" ("Take Care of Him for Me"), "<a href="/wiki/Terbaik_Bagimu" title="Terbaik Bagimu">Terbaik Bagimu</a>" ("The Best for You") and "<a href="/wiki/Seluruh_Cinta" title="Seluruh Cinta">Seluruh Cinta</a>" ("All of Love"). In 2014, "Lebih Indah" received two nominations from <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Music_Awards" title="2014 World Music Awards">2014 World Music Awards</a> in the category of World's Best Song and World's Best Music Video. In the same year, on 17 October, "Lebih Indah" also enabled Siti to win Best Artist (Female) and Best Song (Malaysia) awards from 2014 <a href="/wiki/Anugerah_Planet_Muzik" title="Anugerah Planet Muzik">Anugerah Planet Muzik</a>. Four days later, on 21 October, <i>Fragmen</i> and its singles were nominated in six different categories for 2014 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anugerah_Industri_Muzik" title="Anugerah Industri Muzik">Anugerah Industri Muzik</a>. On 6 December, "Lebih Indah" and <i>Fragmen</i> also enabled Siti to win the Best Vocal Performance in a Song (Female) and Best Album from the 2014 Anugerah Industri Muzik. Her winning for "Lebih Indah" also marked the 12th time Siti winning the Best Vocal Performance in Song (Female) category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 October 2014, <i>Fragmen</i> was officially launched in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. Additional track, a duet with Cakra Khan, "<a href="/wiki/Seluruh_Cinta" title="Seluruh Cinta">Seluruh Cinta</a>" was released as Fragmen's first single for the Indonesian market on the same day.</p>
<p>As of 27 August 2014, the album has been certified Platinum after it has been shipped for more than 10 000 copies. Less than three months later, <i>Fragmen</i> is estimated to be sold around 50 000 copies.</p>

<p><i>Fragmen</i> is Siti's first debut album through the collaboration of her own record company, Siti Nurhaliza Productions with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group (Malaysia)</a> since July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_Kini_Bersama_Universal_Music_Malaysia_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza_Kini_Bersama_Universal_Music_Malaysia-5">[5]</a></sup> The production of the album took place in October 2012 with the release of "<a href="/wiki/Galau" title="Galau">Galau</a>" ("Perplexed") on 11 January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Though it was released digitally in January 2013, "Galau" was first performed during Dato' Siti Nurhaliza – Live in Kuantan 2012 concert in November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_Persembah_Single_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza_Persembah_Single-7">[7]</a></sup> However, when <i>Fragmen</i> was officially slated for release on 30 June 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Terbaru_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Terbaru-1">[1]</a></sup> "Galau" was dropped from the final track list. According to her management, the release of "Galau" was only intended as a promotional single to introduce the collaboration effort between Siti Nurhaliza Productions and Universal Music Group (Malaysia) to the public and not suitable for inclusion due to the differences of musical direction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fragmen_Laris_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fragmen_Laris-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 May,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RKSLSN_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-RKSLSN-9">[9]</a></sup> the first official single of the album, "Lebih Indah" was performed during the taping of her 90-minute special television program for <a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr" title="Eid al-Fitr">Eid al-Fitr</a> with <a href="/wiki/TV2_(Malaysia)" title="TV2 (Malaysia)">TV2</a>, <i>Konsert Sanggar Lebaran Dato' Siti Nurhaliza</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KSL_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-KSL-10">[10]</a></sup> The show was aired much later, on 8 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_Syawal_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza_Syawal-11">[11]</a></sup> Recorded in 2013, the song is said to reflect the state of her emotions when she has to take care of her husband who was involved in a motorcycle accident in New Zealand in December 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pengaruh_Siti_Nurhaliza_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pengaruh_Siti_Nurhaliza-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>In July, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group (Malaysia)</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Artists_and_repertoire" title="Artists and repertoire">A&amp;R Director</a>, Mujahid Abd Wahab revealed that the overall cost production that Universal has spent for this album reached almost RM 200 000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berkos_RM200.2C000_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Berkos_RM200.2C000-15">[14]</a></sup> According to Mujahid, "The collaboration between Universal Music Malaysia and Siti is not only viewed from the sole perspective of profit only, in contrast, it is more than that. Universal Music Malaysia is willing to spend a large amount of expenditure for the creation of Siti's newest album since she is the country's asset. As the best singer, it is fitting that the album also matches her status."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[b]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berbelanja_besar_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Berbelanja_besar-17">[15]</a></sup> The RM 200 000 is said to only cover the production cost of the album alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cecah_RM200.2C000_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cecah_RM200.2C000-18">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the process of the creation of the album, the album saw a collaboration of musicians, composers and technicians from multiple countries. The mastering of the album was done by <a href="/wiki/Ted_Jensen#Sterling_Sound" title="Ted Jensen">Sterling Sound Studio</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cecah_RM200.2C000_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cecah_RM200.2C000-18">[16]</a></sup> Ade Govinda, music composer from Indonesia who contributed "Terbaik Bagimu" is also one of the producers for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kisah_Datuk_K_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kisah_Datuk_K-19">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Out of nine songs that are included in the album, three of them ("Mula dan Akhir", "Sanubari" and "Membunuh Benci")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kisah_Datuk_K_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kisah_Datuk_K-19">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="ref_fn_2"><a href="#endnote_fn_2">[note 2]</a></sup> are chosen from her collaboration with "<i>Bengkel Cipta Ekspres Badan Pelindung Hakcipta Karyawan Malaysia</i>" (Music Author's Copyright Protection (MACP) Xpress Creation Workshop) on 25 April 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza-13">[13]</a></sup> During the workshop, 10 music composers and 10 lyricists that were chosen were given three days to create 10 songs, before they were presented to Siti in order for her to evaluate their suitability to be included in her album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza-13">[13]</a></sup> This marked her second time collaborating with MACP to find new materials for her album since her last traditional album, <i><a href="/wiki/Lentera_Timur" title="Lentera Timur">Lentera Timur</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Originally intended to be the tenth track for the album, "<a href="/wiki/Seluruh_Cinta" title="Seluruh Cinta">Seluruh Cinta</a>", a duet with an Indonesia singer, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cakra_Khan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cakra Khan (page does not exist)">Cakra Khan</a> did not make the final cut of the album. The song which was recorded in three hours on 29 May 2014 at My Music, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jakarta_Selatan" title="Jakarta Selatan">Jakarta Selatan</a>, Indonesia <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[19]</a></sup> was later revealed by Siti's management to be of a different project, though the possibility of its inclusion was actually considered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fragmen_Laris_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fragmen_Laris-8">[8]</a></sup> The song was finally included for the Indonesian version of <i>Fragmen</i> when it was officially launched and released on 21 October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rilis_Indonesia_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rilis_Indonesia-22">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p>Shot by Bustamam Mokhtar,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-liner-25">[23]</a></sup> the album cover portrays Siti in a black ensemble. Siti explained on her choice of the black ensemble as, "Apart from the result of our discussion with the involved party in the creation of the album cover, I chose black because it is my favourite colour and it is perfect with the concept of elegance that I wanted."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[c]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fragmen_Muatkan_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fragmen_Muatkan-4">[4]</a></sup> The album also marked her first cover album with her wearing a <a href="/wiki/Hijab" title="Hijab">hijab</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kisah_Hidup_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kisah_Hidup-24">[22]</a></sup> She explained, "This is my first album that portrays me in a hijab. Even with hijab, I still want uniqueness and in the same time the image that is being shown is suitable with the element of pop that is present in the album. Therefore, I intentionally chose black for the dress because black looks elegant."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[d]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kisah_Hidup_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kisah_Hidup-24">[22]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fragmen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to its official launch on 21 July, the album was released both physically and digitally on two separate dates. The physical copies of the album was released on 30 June,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Terbaru_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Terbaru-1">[1]</a></sup> while the digital version of the album was released on 14 July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-First_Promo_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-First_Promo-2">[2]</a></sup> The album was officially launched at <a href="/wiki/Le_M%C3%A9ridien" title="Le Méridien">Le Méridien</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a> on 21 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dedikasi_Lagu_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dedikasi_Lagu-29">[24]</a></sup> Apart from various performances of her songs for various occasions, she also did several meet and greet sessions to promote <i>Fragmen</i> to both Malaysian and Singaporean fans on 24 August (<a href="/wiki/Pavilion_Kuala_Lumpur" title="Pavilion Kuala Lumpur">Pavilion</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Malaysia_M.26G_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Malaysia_M.26G-30">[25]</a></sup> 25 September (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universiti_Teknologi_Malaysia" title="Universiti Teknologi Malaysia">Universiti Teknologi Malaysia</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[26]</a></sup> Malaysia and 17 October 2014 (<a href="/wiki/Tampines_Mall" title="Tampines Mall">Tampines Mall</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Singapore_M.26G_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Singapore_M.26G-32">[27]</a></sup> Singapore.</p>
<p>On 21 October 2014, the Indonesian version of <i>Fragmen</i> was officially launched and released at Artotel, <a href="/wiki/Central_Jakarta" title="Central Jakarta">Central Jakarta</a> with an additional track, a duet with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cakra_Khan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cakra Khan (page does not exist)">Cakra Khan</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Seluruh_Cinta" title="Seluruh Cinta">Seluruh Cinta</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rilis_Indonesia_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rilis_Indonesia-22">[20]</a></sup> During her visit to promote her album, she appeared on several days in a row on multiple Indonesian entertainment-based programs like radio programs and television channels including "Persembahan Cinta 23" on (MNCTV),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cinta_23_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cinta_23-33">[28]</a></sup> "dahSyat"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dahSyat_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-dahSyat-34">[29]</a></sup> and "Superstars"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Superstars_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Superstars-35">[30]</a></sup> on RCTI, "Inbox" on SCTV,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inbox_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inbox-36">[31]</a></sup> "Ada Ada Aja" on Global TV<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ada_Ada_Aja_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ada_Ada_Aja-37">[32]</a></sup> and "Sarah Sechan" on NET..<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarah_Sechan_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sarah_Sechan-38">[33]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fragmen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The first single, "<a href="/wiki/Lebih_Indah" title="Lebih Indah">Lebih Indah</a>" was released on 31 August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lebih_Indah_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lebih_Indah-39">[34]</a></sup> The song is said by Siti "to immortalize the spirit and share the trying moments"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a cosmetic range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Demi_Zain_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-Demi_Zain-58">[52]</a></sup> The music video was released to <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> on 30 May, two weeks after its first sneak peek during the first promotional tour of the eau de toilettes on 11 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Demi_Zain_58-1"><a href="#cite_note-Demi_Zain-58">[52]</a></sup> "Lebih Indah" has allowed Siti to win several local and international awards — Best Artist (Female) and Best Song (Malaysia) from 2014 <a href="/wiki/Anugerah_Planet_Muzik" title="Anugerah Planet Muzik">Anugerah Planet Muzik</a> and Best Vocal Performance in a Song (Female) from 2014 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anugerah_Industri_Muzik" title="Anugerah Industri Muzik">Anugerah Industri Muzik</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-APM_2014_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-APM_2014-59">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kuasai_AIM_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kuasai_AIM-60">[54]</a></sup> The same song also received nominations from <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Music_Awards" title="2014 World Music Awards">2014 World Music Awards</a> for World's Best Song and World's Best Music Video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tempa_nama_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tempa_nama-61">[55]</a></sup></li>
<li>The second single, "<a href="/wiki/Jaga_Dia_Untukku" title="Jaga Dia Untukku">Jaga Dia Untukku</a>" was released on 4 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-First_Promo_Jaga_Dia_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-First_Promo_Jaga_Dia-47">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Out_now_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-Out_now-48">[43]</a></sup> The song is said to be inspired by her experiences in dealing with the news of her husband's motorcycle incident and her worries when taking care of him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_rakam_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_rakam-62">[56]</a></sup> Its music video was recorded at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puncak_Pass" title="Puncak Pass">Puncak Pass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cisarua" title="Cisarua">Cisarua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bogor_Regency" title="Bogor Regency">Kabupaten Bogor</a>, Indonesia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diam-diam_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-Diam-diam-63">[57]</a></sup> The music video was first previewed to the media during the launch of her album on 21 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dedikasi_Lagu_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dedikasi_Lagu-29">[24]</a></sup> It was performed live a number of times including on "Apa Saja FBI" on TV9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Apa_Saja_FB_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-Apa_Saja_FB-64">[58]</a></sup></li>
<li>The third single, "<a href="/wiki/Terbaik_Bagimu" title="Terbaik Bagimu">Terbaik Bagimu</a>" was released on 15 October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siaran_Media_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-Siaran_Media-65">[59]</a></sup> It was first performed live during Siti's 2014 "Dato' Siti Nurhaliza – Live in Singapore" concert on 12 April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Concert_Review_53-1"><a href="#cite_note-Concert_Review-53">[48]</a></sup> During her visit to Indonesia to promote her album, she also performed "Terbaik Bagimu" for several times including – "Persembahan Cinta 23" (<a href="/wiki/MNCTV" title="MNCTV">MNCTV</a>) on 20 October 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cinta_23_33-3"><a href="#cite_note-Cinta_23-33">[28]</a></sup> "dahSyat"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dahSyat_34-3"><a href="#cite_note-dahSyat-34">[29]</a></sup> and "Superstars"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Superstars_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-Superstars-35">[30]</a></sup> on <a href="/wiki/RCTI" title="RCTI">RCTI</a> (21 October), "Inbox" on <a href="/wiki/SCTV_(Indonesia)" title="SCTV (Indonesia)">SCTV</a> (22 October)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inbox_36-2"><a href="#cite_note-Inbox-36">[31]</a></sup> and "Sarah Sechan" on <a href="/wiki/NET." title="NET.">NET.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarah_Sechan_38-3"><a href="#cite_note-Sarah_Sechan-38">[33]</a></sup></li>
<li>Its first Indonesian single, "<a href="/wiki/Seluruh_Cinta" title="Seluruh Cinta">Seluruh Cinta</a>" was first performed live by Siti and Cakra during MNCTV's special program in conjunction with its 23rd anniversary, "Persembahan Cinta 23" on 20 October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cinta_23_33-4"><a href="#cite_note-Cinta_23-33">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_banyak_54-3"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza_banyak-54">[49]</a></sup> It was officially released on 21 October 2014 in with the launch of Indonesia version of <i>Fragmen</i> on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rilis_Indonesia_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rilis_Indonesia-22">[20]</a></sup> The same song was performed on several days in a row on multiple Indonesian entertainment-based programs and television channels, including "dahSyat" on RCTI (21 October)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dahSyat_34-4"><a href="#cite_note-dahSyat-34">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_banyak_54-4"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza_banyak-54">[49]</a></sup> and "Ada Ada Aja" on Global TV (22 October).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siti_Nurhaliza_banyak_54-5"><a href="#cite_note-Siti_Nurhaliza_banyak-54">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ada_Ada_Aja_37-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ada_Ada_Aja-37">[32]</a></sup> "Seluruh Cinta" was also performed live during the taping of a one-hour talk show by Sarah Sechan on NET. which was broadcast on 1 November 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarah_Sechan_38-4"><a href="#cite_note-Sarah_Sechan-38">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hilang_suara_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hilang_suara-55">[50]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fragmen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the first week of its release, <i>Fragmen</i> has been sold more than 5000 copies, thus making it eligible to receive Gold certification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kisah_Datuk_K_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kisah_Datuk_K-19">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dedikasi_Lagu_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dedikasi_Lagu-29">[24]</a></sup> As of 27 August, the album has been certified Platinum after it has been shipped for more than 10 000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hikmah_sebalik_musibah_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hikmah_sebalik_musibah-66">[60]</a></sup> Less than three months later, on 20 November, it is revealed that the album has been sold to an estimated of 50 000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fragmen_50k_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fragmen_50k-67">[61]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fragmen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014, Siti received five nominations from <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Awards</a>, three for herself (World's Best Female Artist, World's Best Live Act, World's Best Entertainer of the Year) and two for "<a href="/wiki/Lebih_Indah" title="Lebih Indah">Lebih Indah</a>" (World's Best Song and World's Best Video).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[64]</a></sup> On 17 October 2014, Siti received two awards from 2014 <a href="/wiki/Anugerah_Planet_Muzik" title="Anugerah Planet Muzik">Anugerah Planet Muzik</a> in the category of Best Female Artiste and Best Song (Malaysia) for "Lebih Indah" in an award ceremony that involves musicians and artists from Indonesia, Malaysia and Singapore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-APM_2014_59-1"><a href="#cite_note-APM_2014-59">[53]</a></sup></p>
