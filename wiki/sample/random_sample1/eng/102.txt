<p><br/>
<b>1679 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCLXXIX</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Common year starting on Sunday">starting on Sunday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> A) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Wednesday" title="Common year starting on Wednesday">common year starting on Wednesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> E) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1679th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 679th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 79th year of the <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th century</a>, and the 10th and last year of the <a href="/wiki/1670s" title="1670s">1670s</a> decade. As of the start of 1679, the Gregorian calendar was 10 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1679&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1679&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_24" title="January 24">January 24</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II of England</a> dissolves the "<a href="/wiki/Cavalier_Parliament" title="Cavalier Parliament">Cavalier Parliament</a>" after nearly 18 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology278279_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology278279-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_6" title="March 6">March 6</a>–<a href="/wiki/May_27" title="May 27">May 27</a> – In England, the "<a href="/wiki/Habeas_Corpus_Parliament" title="Habeas Corpus Parliament">Habeas Corpus Parliament</a>" (or "First Exclusion Parliament") meets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology278279_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology278279-1">[1]</a></sup> It is dissolved on <a href="/wiki/July_12" title="July 12">July 12</a> while in recess by <a href="/wiki/Royal_prerogative" title="Royal prerogative">Royal prerogative</a> to prevent it passing a Bill excluding the king's brother, the Catholic <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_England" title="James II of England">James, Duke of York</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Succession_to_the_British_throne" title="Succession to the British throne">succession to the English throne</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Exclusion_Crisis" title="Exclusion Crisis">Exclusion Crisis</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_27" title="May 27">May 27</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_England" title="Parliament of England">Parliament of England</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Habeas_Corpus_Act_1679" title="Habeas Corpus Act 1679">Habeas Corpus Act</a> "for the better securing the liberty of the subject".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology278279_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology278279-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_1" title="June 1">June 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Drumclog" title="Battle of Drumclog">Battle of Drumclog</a>: Scottish <a href="/wiki/Covenanter" title="Covenanter">Covenanters</a> defeat a small government force.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/1679_Armenia_earthquake" title="1679 Armenia earthquake">Armenia earthquake</a>: A tremor with an estimated <a href="/wiki/Surface_wave_magnitude" title="Surface wave magnitude">surface wave magnitude</a> of 6.4 takes place in the <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> region of the <a href="/wiki/Safavid_dynasty" title="Safavid dynasty">Persian Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_22" title="June 22">June 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bothwell_Bridge" title="Battle of Bothwell Bridge">Battle of Bothwell Bridge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>: Royal forces led by <a href="/wiki/James_Scott,_1st_Duke_of_Monmouth" title="James Scott, 1st Duke of Monmouth">James Scott, 1st Duke of Monmouth</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Graham_of_Claverhouse" title="John Graham of Claverhouse">John Graham of Claverhouse</a> subdue the Scottish Covenanters.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1679&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: July–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_7" title="August 7">August 7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Brigantine" title="Brigantine">brigantine</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Griffon" title="Le Griffon">Le Griffon</a></i>, commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9-Robert_Cavelier,_Sieur_de_La_Salle" title="René-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle">René-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle</a>, is towed to the southern end of the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_River" title="Niagara River">Niagara River</a>, to become the first ship to sail the upper <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_27" title="November 27">November 27</a> – A fire in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, burns all of the warehouses, 80 houses, and all of the ships in the dockyards.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1679&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Tibet%E2%80%93Ladakh%E2%80%93Mughal_War" title="Tibet–Ladakh–Mughal War">Tibet–Ladakh–Mughal War</a> (1679–84) starts with the Tibetan invasion of Ladakh.</li>
<li>European explorers discover <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls" title="Niagara Falls">Niagara Falls</a>.</li>
<li>The city of <a href="/wiki/Duluth,_Minnesota" title="Duluth, Minnesota">Duluth</a>, is founded. (citation needed)</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mughal_Emperor" title="Mughal Emperor">Mughal Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Aurangzeb" title="Aurangzeb">Aurangzeb</a> reimposes <a href="/wiki/Jizya" title="Jizya">jizya</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malpas_Tunnel" title="Malpas Tunnel">Malpas Tunnel</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Canal_du_Midi" title="Canal du Midi">Canal du Midi</a> in <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9rault" title="Hérault">Hérault</a>, France, Europe's first navigable canal tunnel, is excavated by <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Paul_Riquet" title="Pierre-Paul Riquet">Pierre-Paul Riquet</a> (165 m, concrete lined).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
