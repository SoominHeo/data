<p><b>GAM</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ギャム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gyamu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> unit under the <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a> umbrella consisting of <a href="/wiki/Aya_Matsuura" title="Aya Matsuura">Aya Matsuura</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Morning_Musume" title="Morning Musume">Morning Musume</a> member <a href="/wiki/Miki_Fujimoto" title="Miki Fujimoto">Miki Fujimoto</a>. This pairing was announced mid June 2006. Previously, Matsuura and Fujimoto worked together in the one-off group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gomattou" title="Gomattou">Gomattou</a> with <a href="/wiki/Maki_Goto" title="Maki Goto">Maki Goto</a>. Because "gam" is slang for "attractive legs" in English (French <i>gambe</i>), the girls are known for wearing very short skirts in their performances and PVs.</p>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a> producer <a href="/wiki/Tsunku" title="Tsunku">Tsunku</a>, the term GAM stands for "Great Aya and Miki". After GAM's debut, Tsunku has decided to keep the group permanent instead of a one-time deal. However, Fujimoto and Matsuura graduating from Hello! Project in March 2009, the group subsequently disbanded.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GAM_(group)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 13, 2006 their first single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thanks!" title="Thanks!">Thanks!</a>" was released in Japan and made its debut at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> Daily Ranking chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The single made its debut—and peak—the following week at number five in the Oricon Weekly Ranking chart. The song has so far stayed in the top 200 for five weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Their second single, "Melodies" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">メロディーズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Merodīzu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was released October 18, 2006 making its debut at number five on the Oricon Daily Ranking chart the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The Ninki weekly singles chart ranked "Melodies" as the fourth most anticipated new release of the week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> Japan, the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">PV</a> for this song was ranked number one on the Top Music Videos list, presumably ranked by sales, as of November 3, 2006. "Melodies" features a more mature theme and contains several scenes implying an almost sexual relationship between the two singers, such as the near kiss sequence featured in the televised form of the PV; just as the two of them are about to kiss the video comes to an abrupt end. The complete version of the PV released on the DVD showed that the two singers had actually kissed at the end of the video. Due to this, they have been compared to the Russian pop duo <a href="/wiki/T.A.T.u." title="T.A.T.u.">t.A.T.u.</a>. and dubbed among some listeners as the "Japanese t.A.T.u.".</p>
<p>Additionally, on the evening of the 28th of an uncertain month, Matsuura officially revealed her desire to go on a tour as part of GAM. Though her partner agreed, Fujimoto commented on how the group would need to record more original songs in order to do such a thing. Eventually, both members requested an album from producer <a href="/wiki/Tsunku" title="Tsunku">Tsunku</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>A third single, entitled <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lu_Lu_Lu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lu Lu Lu (page does not exist)">Lu Lu Lu</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> was released on March 21, 2007. Three days later, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Daisuki_Rakuten_Eagles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Daisuki Rakuten Eagles (page does not exist)">Daisuki Rakuten Eagles</a>" was released as a theme song of the baseball team <a href="/wiki/Tohoku_Rakuten_Golden_Eagles" title="Tohoku Rakuten Golden Eagles">Tohoku Rakuten Golden Eagles</a>—the third <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a> song to do so.</p>
