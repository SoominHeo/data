<p><b>Grupo Desportivo de Chaves</b>, commonly known as <b>Chaves</b>, is a Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Football_team" title="Football team">club</a> from <a href="/wiki/Chaves,_Portugal" title="Chaves, Portugal">Chaves</a> currently playing in the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a>. They were founded in 1949 and currently play at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Municipal_de_Chaves" title="Estádio Municipal de Chaves">Estádio Municipal de Chaves</a> with a capacity of 12,000, one of the biggest in the division. Their home kit is red-and-blue striped shirt with blue shorts and socks and the away kit is all white. Their current president is Bruno Carvalho and their manager is <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Sim%C3%A3o" title="Jorge Simão">Jorge Simão</a>. Chaves have been in the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Portuguese First Division</a> 13 times and had their best finish of fifth-place in the 1989–90 season. They went to Europe in the 1987–88 season where they played in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>, beating Romanian side <a href="/wiki/FC_Universitatea_Craiova" title="FC Universitatea Craiova">Universitatea Craiova</a> in the first round but lost in the next round to Hungarian team <a href="/wiki/Budapest_Honv%C3%A9d_FC" title="Budapest Honvéd FC">Budapest Honvéd</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G.D._Chaves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grupo Desportivo de Chaves was founded on 27 September 1949 and is one of the most experienced teams in the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Second_Division" title="Portuguese Second Division">Portuguese Second Division</a>, not just because of its old culture, but also many years in the top-flight <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a>, doing quite well and playing in European tournaments, such as the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>. Their best ever finish was in the Primeira Liga when they finished fifth in both 1986–87 and 1989–90. In the 1986–87 season, meanwhile, they had fantastic results, beating <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Clube de Portugal</a> 2–1 at home in a memorable night at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Municipal_de_Chaves" title="Estádio Municipal de Chaves">Estádio Municipal de Chaves</a>. After those fantastic seasons, they finished sixth, seventh and ninth before getting relegated in the 1992–93 season to the <a href="/wiki/LigaPro" title="LigaPro">Liga de Honra</a>. They returned for another few seasons but then again suffered relegation to the Liga de Honra until 2007, where they eventually got relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Second_Division" title="Portuguese Second Division">Portuguese Second Division: Série A</a>. In the previous season of 2007–08, they finished in fourth place, just missing out on promotion.</p>
<p>After a series of great results in 2008–09 that granted the team the lead of Série A, Chaves finally achieved promotion to the second-flight Liga de Honra with an aggregate 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/F.C._Penafiel" title="F.C. Penafiel">Penafiel</a>, the winner of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Second_Division" title="Portuguese Second Division">Portuguese Second Division: Série B</a> in a semi-final playoff. In the playoff final, it was between Chaves and <a href="/wiki/C.D._F%C3%A1tima" title="C.D. Fátima">Fátima</a>, in which both were guaranteed promotion to the Liga de Honra, with the final set to determine the champions. Fátima won the match 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Chaves spent the following three years in the third division before being crowned <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Segunda_Divis%C3%A3o" title="2012–13 Segunda Divisão">Segunda Divisão champions in 2012–13</a>, thus gaining promotion back to the Segunda Liga. After missing out on promotion to the first division during a thrilling final day of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Segunda_Liga" title="2014–15 Segunda Liga">2014–15 season</a>, Chaves were promoted the following season back to the top-flight Primeira Liga for the first time in 17 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G.D._Chaves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Municipal_de_Chaves" title="Estádio Municipal de Chaves">Estádio Municipal de Chaves</a> is a multi-use stadium in Chaves. It is currently used mostly for football matches and is the home stadium of G.D. Chaves. The stadium is able to hold 12,000 people. The stadium normally holds the Portugal national team youth games and also some <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_under-21_football_team" title="Portugal national under-21 football team">under-21</a> games, and also very rarely the senior team. This stadium is famous because it is where <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Ronaldo" title="Cristiano Ronaldo">Cristiano Ronaldo</a> made his senior <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">international</a> debut with <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
