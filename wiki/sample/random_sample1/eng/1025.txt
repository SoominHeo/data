<p><i><b>Girls' Generation</b></i> is the debut <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> album by South Korean <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>, released by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nayutawave_Records" title="Nayutawave Records">Nayutawave Records</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Japan" title="Universal Music Japan">Universal Music Japan</a> on June 1, 2011. A repackaged edition of the album, <i>The Boys</i>, was released on December 28, 2011.</p>
<p>To promote the album, Girls' Generation embarked on their first Japan concert tour, <a href="/wiki/The_First_Japan_Arena_Tour_(Girls%27_Generation)" title="The First Japan Arena Tour (Girls' Generation)">The First Japan Arena Tour</a>. Furthermore, the album is also ranked 18th on <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> magazine's Top 20 Pop Albums of 2011, and makes <i>Girls' Generation</i> the highest selling album and the highest annually ranked album achieved so far by a Korean group in Oricon history.</p>
<p>With the aid of their repackaged album, the album has officially shipped over 1 million copies as of January 11, 2012. As of June 2012, the album has been "Million"-certified by the Recording Industry Association of Japan (RIAJ), making the group the second Korean artist to earn this achievement in Japan since Korean labelmate <a href="/wiki/BoA" title="BoA">BoA</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Best_of_Soul" title="Best of Soul">Best of Soul</a></i> in 2005.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_release">Background and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girls%27_Generation_(2011_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After releasing three singles in the Japanese market, including "<a href="/wiki/Tell_Me_Your_Wish_(Genie)_(song)#Japanese_version" title="Tell Me Your Wish (Genie) (song)">Genie</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Gee_(Girls%27_Generation_song)#Japanese_version" title="Gee (Girls' Generation song)">Gee</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Mr._Taxi" title="Mr. Taxi">Mr. Taxi</a>/<a href="/wiki/Run_Devil_Run_(Girls%27_Generation_song)" title="Run Devil Run (Girls' Generation song)">Run Devil Run</a>", Girls' Generation released their first Japanese studio album <i>Girls' Generation</i> on June 1, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A music video for the track "Bad Girl" was released on August 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Other tracks in the album, "Mr. Taxi" and "Let It Rain", were used as the background music for various versions of Girls' Generation's <a href="/wiki/Television_advertisement" title="Television advertisement">CF</a> with <a href="/wiki/Lipton" title="Lipton">Lipton Tea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Girls' Generation became the highest earning foreign artist in Japan for the first half of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 28, the group released a repackaged version of the album, <i>Re:package Album "Girls' Generation" ~The Boys~</i>. It includes a Japanese version of their international hit "The Boys", and remixed versions of "The Great Escape", "Bad Girl", "Mr.Taxi", and a new song "Time Machine". A music video for "Time Machine" was released on March 13, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girls%27_Generation_(2011_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was announced on March 7, 2011 that Girls' Generation would embark on their first nationwide Japan tour starting May 18, 2011 at Yoyogi National Stadium in Tokyo, with a total of 7 stops in Tokyo, Nagoya, Osaka and Fukuoka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The start of the tour was postponed to May 31, 2011 because of the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>. Due to overwhelming demand, with up to 300,000 applicants applying for tickets,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> additional stops were added to the tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The tour was named as <a href="/wiki/The_First_Japan_Arena_Tour_(Girls%27_Generation)" title="The First Japan Arena Tour (Girls' Generation)">The First Japan Arena Tour</a>.</p>

<p><i>Girls' Generation</i> received mixed to positive reviews from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_critics" title="Music critics">music critics</a>. James Hadfield writing for the Japan edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Out_(magazine)" title="Time Out (magazine)">Time Out</a></i> were not impressed with the Japanese versions of earlier Korean singles "Genie" and "Run Devil Run", commenting that the <a href="/wiki/Audio_mastering" title="Audio mastering">mastering</a> lacks the "heavily compressed punch" of the originals. Nevertheless, he lauded it as a "cohesive" album and regarded the record as an "improvement" on the group's previous Korean releases. Hadfield also labelled <i>Girls' Generation</i> one of the best J-pop albums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimeOut_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-TimeOut-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s editor Chuck Eddy picked "Bad Girl" as a highlight on the album, calling it "over-the-top" and "<a href="/wiki/Latin_music_(genre)" title="Latin music (genre)">Latin</a>-freestyle-emoted" track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spin_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spin-11">[11]</a></sup> Writing for the same magazine, Charles Aaron ranked <i>Girls' Generation</i> number 18 on <i>Spin</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s list of 20 Best Pop Albums of 2011; he praised the record as a collection of "over terrifyingly sophisticated dance tracks" and chose "Run Devil Run" as a standout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girls%27_Generation_(2011_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pre-orders started on different e-stores. On Amazon Japan, the deluxe edition sold very well and rose quickly to No.1 on the pre-order chart while the limited edition was ranked 7th. On various Japanese album stores, <i>Girls' Generation</i> was the No.1 pre-ordered album with over 300,000 units expected to be shipped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album topped the Oricon Daily Album Chart at first place on June 1 with a sales count of 43,583 copies, which made Girls' Generation the first foreign girl group ever to top the chart on the release date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> According to Oricon, the album sold 131,553 copies in the first week and placed first on the Oricon Weekly Album Chart, surpassing the record for first week debut album sales for a foreign artist, a record previously held by SM Entertainment labelmate <a href="/wiki/BoA" title="BoA">BoA</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Listen_to_My_Heart_(BoA_album)" title="Listen to My Heart (BoA album)">Listen to My Heart</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> One month after the album release, Oricon reported a total sales of 211,972 copies. The album placed second on the Oricon Monthly Album Chart for the month of June and became the 5th best selling album of 2011 in Japan according to the Oricon Album Rankings.</p>
<p>On December 19, 2011, Oricon reported that <i>Girls' Generation</i> sold over 542,054 copies in the 2011 calendar year to rank 5th place on the Oricon Yearly Album Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-20">[20]</a></sup> This surpassed the previous record of 7th place and 369,530 copies sold by SM Entertainment labelmate <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">TVXQ</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Best_Selection_2010" title="Best Selection 2010">Best Selection 2010</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and makes <i>Girls' Generation</i> the highest selling album and the highest annually ranked album achieved so far by a Korean group in Oricon history. On June 8, 2012, the RIAJ certified the album "Million" for shipping over a million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album placed 5th on the Oricon Weekly CD Albums chart for the second week of January 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and Oricon announced that it was the first time in history that an album by a Korean artist placed in the Top 10 for 17 weeks. The previous record holder was the original soundtrack for the Korean drama <i><a href="/wiki/Winter_Sonata" title="Winter Sonata">Winter Sonata</a></i>, which remained in the Top 10 for 16 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Editions">Editions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girls%27_Generation_(2011_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Editions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was originally released in three different editions: Deluxe Limited First Press Edition, Limited Pressing Edition, and the Regular Edition. A repackaged Edition was released later.</p>
<ul>
<li>Deluxe Limited First Press Edition comes with a bonus DVD featuring "Mr. Taxi"'s original and dance versions of the music video, plus "Genie" and "Gee" Japanese music videos, a 40-page photobook, a mini bag that comes in a special box.</li>
<li>Limited Pressing Edition comes in a slipcase with a 32-page photobook and a bonus DVD featuring the same contents at the Deluxe Limited First Press Edition except "Mr. Taxi"'s dance version.</li>
<li>Regular Edition comes in a standard jewel case with a lyrics-only booklet.</li>
</ul>
<p>The album was later re-released as <i>Re:package Album "Girls' Generation" ~The Boys~</i> was released in three editions: a Regular Edition with a lyrics-only booklet, a Limited Edition with a 36-page photobook, a bonus DVD featuring music videos of "Bad Girl" and "The Boys" (English Version) plus two rubber coasters and a Limited Pressing with the same photobook, bonus DVD and a case from the limited edition, but with two B2-size posters.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girls%27_Generation_(2011_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>"<a href="/wiki/Tell_Me_Your_Wish_(Genie)_(song)#Japanese_version" title="Tell Me Your Wish (Genie) (song)">Genie</a>"</b> is the official title for the debut in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>. The single was the first ever Japanese single released by Girls' Generation on September 8, 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. A new version of the music video was filmed to accompany the Japanese version, and the teaser was released on August 19, 2010. On August 26, 2010, the full music video was released. The song reached #5 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oricon_Charts" title="Oricon Charts">Oricon Charts</a> on the releasing day. The song reached #2 on September 11, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The song was released in three editions, two CD+DVD (first press and regular), and a CD Only edition. First press CD+DVD edition includes a special photobook and a random (1 of 9) photocard. The single includes also includes the Korean version, "Tell Me Your Wish (Genie)".</p>
<p>Girls' Generation also had success in Japan with their second Japanese single <b>"<a href="/wiki/Gee_(Girls%27_Generation_song)#Japanese_version" title="Gee (Girls' Generation song)">Gee</a>"</b>. They took second place and were crowned the first ever overseas girl group to reach number 1 on the chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The song was ranked fifty-seventh on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Hot_100" title="Japan Hot 100">Japan Hot 100</a> 2010 year-end chart. On April 20, 2011, the girls added another accomplishment to their belt as the RIAJ (Recording Industry Association of Japan) website updated their charts showing that SNSD's Chaku Uta Full (ringtones) downloads for "Gee" went Double Platinum. The RIAJ certifies Platinum as moving 250,000 units, so Double Platinum would mean that the girls had over 500,000 downloads for "Gee".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>"<a href="/wiki/Mr._Taxi" title="Mr. Taxi">Mr. Taxi</a> / <a href="/wiki/Run_Devil_Run_(Girls%27_Generation_song)" title="Run Devil Run (Girls' Generation song)">Run Devil Run</a>"</b> set a new record for Girls' Generation, being their first Japanese single to shift 100,000 units in its first week of release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Before they released the physical single, they released the Japanese version of "<a href="/wiki/Run_Devil_Run_(Girls%27_Generation_song)" title="Run Devil Run (Girls' Generation song)">Run Devil Run</a>" as a digital single on January 25, 2011. The single also managed to clinch the number 1 spot on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Hot_100" title="Japan Hot 100">Japan Hot 100</a> chart for two consecutive weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.E5.B0.91.E5.A5.B3.E6.99.82.E4.BB.A3_.E8.A9.B1.E9.A1.8C.E3.81.AE.E6.96.B0.E6.9B.B2.E3.81.8C.E9.A6.96.E4.BD.8D.E7.8D.B2.E5.BE.97.EF.BC.81_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-.E5.B0.91.E5.A5.B3.E6.99.82.E4.BB.A3_.E8.A9.B1.E9.A1.8C.E3.81.AE.E6.96.B0.E6.9B.B2.E3.81.8C.E9.A6.96.E4.BD.8D.E7.8D.B2.E5.BE.97.EF.BC.81-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> "Mr. Taxi" was first performed live on May 13, 2011 on <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">TV Asahi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Music_Station" title="Music Station">Music Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On May 25, "Mr. Taxi" topped the Taiwanese music chart Gmusic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
