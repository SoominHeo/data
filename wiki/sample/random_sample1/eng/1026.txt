<p><b>PGE GKS Bełchatów</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Polish" title="Help:IPA for Polish">[ˌpɛɡʲɛˈʔɛ ˌɡʲɛkaˈɛs bɛwˈxatuf]</a></span> is a professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club that competes in the <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Poland" title="Football in Poland">Polish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Liga_(Poland)" title="I Liga (Poland)">First League</a>. GKS is an abbreviation for the Miners' Sport Club (Górniczy Klub Sportowy).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GKS_Be%C5%82chat%C3%B3w&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GKS Bełchatów entered Poland's Division 4 in 1977 and gained promotion to Division 3 after four years (in the 1981/1982 season). It took the club another seven years to rise to Division 2 (1986/1987 season), before winning a place in the Polish Premier League in 1992. After another spell in Division 2, the club again won promotion to the First league at the end of the 2004/2005 season. The club finished the 2005/2006 season in 10th place, with 37 points. The following season they challenged for the league title. After spending much of the latter part of the 2006/07 season in first place, they were ultimately overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Zag%C5%82%C4%99bie_Lubin" title="Zagłębie Lubin">Zagłębie Lubin</a> and had to settle for second place.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fans">Fans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GKS_Be%C5%82chat%C3%B3w&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fans are called <i>Torfiorze</i> (the "<a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">Turfers</a>" in translation). They have friendly relations with fans of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wis%C5%82a_Sandomierz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wisła Sandomierz (page does not exist)">Wisła Sandomierz</a>. Their biggest rivals are neighbours <a href="/wiki/RKS_Radomsko" title="RKS Radomsko">RKS Radomsko</a>, and as Bełchatów is located in the <a href="/wiki/%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA_Voivodeship" title="Łódź Voivodeship">Łódź Province</a>, they also have rivalries with the two traditional well-established <a href="/wiki/%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA" title="Łódź">Łódź</a> teams, <a href="/wiki/%C5%81KS_%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA" title="ŁKS Łódź">ŁKS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Widzew_%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA" title="Widzew Łódź">Widzew</a>.</p>
