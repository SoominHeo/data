<p><b>Glay</b> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band, formed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hakodate,_Hokkaid%C5%8D" title="Hakodate, Hokkaidō">Hakodate</a> in 1988. Glay primarily composes songs in the rock and pop genres, but they have also arranged songs using elements from a wide variety of genres, including <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ska" title="Ska">ska</a>. Originally a <a href="/wiki/Visual_kei" title="Visual kei">visual kei</a> band,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> the group slowly shifted to less dramatic attire through the years. As of 2008, Glay had sold an estimated 51 million records; 28 million singles and 23 million albums,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mainichi_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mainichi-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barks_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Barks-3">[3]</a></sup> making them one of the top ten <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists_in_Japan" title="List of best-selling music artists in Japan">best-selling artists of all time in Japan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1988.E2.80.931994:_Indies_era">1988–1994: Indies era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1988–1994: Indies era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Glay formed in 1988 as a high school band when <a href="/wiki/Takuro_(musician)" title="Takuro (musician)">Takuro</a> asked <a href="/wiki/Teru_(singer)" title="Teru (singer)">Teru</a>, a schoolmate, to play the drums. They found a bassist but had difficulty finding a vocalist. When Teru made a tape of his singing and gave it to Takuro he was immediately recruited for the part, leaving the drums part to be filled by another person. On the search for a second guitarist, <a href="/wiki/Hisashi_(musician)" title="Hisashi (musician)">Hisashi</a> was asked to join but declined the offer, as he was already part of a locally well-known heavy punk/rock band called Ari, which better suited his taste in music. Hisashi eventually accepted Takuro's offer and became Glay's lead guitarist after Ari disbanded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glad_to_be_Glay_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Glad_to_be_Glay-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the time of Takuro and Teru's graduation, Glay were enjoying some popularity in their hometown of Hakodate and were playing full live houses. Following Hisashi's high school graduation in 1990, the three moved to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> to try to further expand their musical career. The bassist and the drummer chose to stay in Hakodate. In Tokyo, they found a completely different situation: although they had been relatively popular in their hometown, it was not easy to begin a career in Tokyo. Their concerts attracted few people, and sometimes none at all,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_Magazine_Article_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time_Magazine_Article-5">[5]</a></sup> and many live houses would not accept them because they did not fit well into either rock or pop categories. The band had to conciliate their music career to part-time jobs, and faced financial problems during their first years in Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glad_to_be_Glay_2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Glad_to_be_Glay_2-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>During this time, members (particularly drummers) were constantly joining and leaving the band. When their bassist quit, Takuro knew that <a href="/wiki/Jiro_(musician)" title="Jiro (musician)">Jiro</a>, who was also from Hakodate and had played with the indie band Pierrot (different from the now disbanded <a href="/wiki/Pierrot_(band)" title="Pierrot (band)">Pierrot</a>, that enjoyed mainstream popularity), had moved to Tokyo and invited him to join Glay. He too, declined the offer, insisting that he was already heading in the right direction for himself. It wasn't until Takuro asked him to play at just one show to fill in for their missing bassist that he decided to go. Following that show, Jiro continued to receive invitations to play with Glay, and eventually he became Glay's official bass player, finalizing the official four member lineup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glad_to_be_Glay_2_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Glad_to_be_Glay_2-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>They promoted the band by handing out flyers on the street and giving out demo tapes. Eventually they became better known on the Tokyo live house circuit, and began drawing larger crowds. Eventually, <a href="/wiki/Hide_(musician)" title="Hide (musician)">hide</a> of <a href="/wiki/X_Japan" title="X Japan">X Japan</a> gave one of Glay's demos to his band mate <a href="/wiki/Yoshiki_(musician)" title="Yoshiki (musician)">Yoshiki</a>. During an October 1993 show, Yoshiki and his entourage came to watch and offered the band a contract to his record label <a href="/wiki/Extasy_Records" title="Extasy Records">Extasy Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Their debut single, "Rain", and their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hai_to_Diamond" title="Hai to Diamond">Hai to Diamond</a></i>, were both released on May 25, 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1995.E2.80.932000">1995–2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1995–2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the time of their debut, Glay steadily became more popular. Their 4th single "Freeze My Love" made it onto the Oricon Top 20, debuting at No. 19. Two months later their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Speed_Pop" title="Speed Pop">Speed Pop</a></i>, peaked at No. 8. Their first No. 1 hit was in 1996 with their third album <i><a href="/wiki/Beat_Out!" title="Beat Out!">Beat Out!</a></i>, which was marked as a major turning point in Glay's career. Released later that same year, the fourth album <i><a href="/wiki/Beloved_(Glay_album)" title="Beloved (Glay album)">Beloved</a></i> was their first album to sell over 1 million copies. In September 1996, Glay played the final concert of the "Beat Out! Reprise Tour" at Budokan, their first concert at the venue, which is stated by them to have been their biggest dream coming true. In August 1997, their 12th single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/However_(song)" title="However (song)">However</a>" remained at the top of the charts for two weeks in a row and, after it was displaced by <a href="/wiki/Zard" title="Zard">Zard</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Eien_(Zard_song)" title="Eien (Zard song)">Eien</a>" for one week, it re-appeared at the top position for another three weeks.</p>
<p>Since then, Glay has produced several hit singles, most of them reaching No. 1 on the charts. In the period between 1997 and 2000, Glay produced six million-selling singles, being the third artist with most million-selling singles of all time. Their 13th single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuuwaku_(song)" title="Yuuwaku (song)">Yuuwaku</a>" topped Oricon yearly single ranking in 1998. In 1999, their 16th single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter,_Again_(song)" title="Winter, Again (song)">Winter, Again</a>" was number two of the year and won the <a href="/wiki/41st_Japan_Record_Awards" title="41st Japan Record Awards">41st Japan Record Awards</a> "Grand Prix" award, which is considered the most important Japanese music award. Their albums were also massively successful; both <i><a href="/wiki/Pure_Soul" title="Pure Soul">Pure Soul</a></i> (1998) and <i><a href="/wiki/Heavy_Gauge" title="Heavy Gauge">Heavy Gauge</a></i> (1999), along with the compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Drive:_Glay_Complete_Best" title="Drive: Glay Complete Best">Drive - Glay Complete Best</a></i> (2000), sold more than two million copies each.</p>
<p>The impact of their popularity was such that, in 1998, Japan's telephone service was temporarily put out of order due to the mass number of fans trying to reserve tickets to their upcoming tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_Magazine_Article_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Time_Magazine_Article-5">[5]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/1998_Winter_Olympics" title="1998 Winter Olympics">1998 Winter Olympics</a> were being held, and press coverage of the event became chaotic because of the incident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Day_in_Music_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Day_in_Music-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In this period, the band won several prizes and established some records. In 1997, the album <i><a href="/wiki/Review_(Glay_album)" title="Review (Glay album)">Review - Best of Glay</a></i> stayed for five weeks in a row at the top position of the Oricon charts and became the all-time best-selling album in Japan at the time, with almost 5 million copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_Magazine_Article_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Time_Magazine_Article-5">[5]</a></sup> (at present, it is ranked at No. 3 spot, according to Oricon). Their 1999 video-single "Survival" sold approximately 900 thousand copies, and is up to the present the all-time best-selling video/DVD. In the same year, their first "Expo" concert would lure a record audience of 200,000 people (see "Glay Expo").</p>
<p>1999 was the year Glay played their first dome tour, "Glay Dome Tour "pure soul" 1999", which made the band the first to hold a consecutive 5 day "dome" concert. This tour had a total of 15 performances in four venues and attracted a total audience of 750,000 people. Glay closed the year of 1999, playing the concert "The Millennium Eve - A Christmas Present for the People Who Love Live a Lot", a joint concert with <a href="/wiki/Luna_Sea" title="Luna Sea">Luna Sea</a>, on December 23, and the "Glay Countdown Live in Messe "Come Together"" on December 31.</p>
<p>Takuro described the "Expo'99" as a dream come true. When the album <i><a href="/wiki/Heavy_Gauge" title="Heavy Gauge">Heavy Gauge</a></i> was released, in October 1999, Takuro wrote a diary entry in Glay's official home page, describing the album as a representation of his feelings at that moment. His feelings were not of happiness, but of "emptiness" and not knowing what to do after "doing everything" they wanted to. The album is seen as a first step to a new direction in the band's career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiroi_Heiya_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shiroi_Heiya-12">[12]</a></sup> The band considered disbanding in 2000, after releasing their <a href="/wiki/Drive:_Glay_Complete_Best" title="Drive: Glay Complete Best">second compilation album</a>, which was a suggestion made by fellow singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuki_Isoya" title="Yuki Isoya">Yuki Isoya</a>, whose group <a href="/wiki/Judy_and_Mary" title="Judy and Mary">Judy and Mary</a> had disbanded.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.932003">2001–2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2001–2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After this period of massive success and a considerably steady style in their music, Glay's work took a turn in 2001. Their 2001 album <i><a href="/wiki/One_Love_(Glay_album)" title="One Love (Glay album)">One Love</a></i> got mixed feelings among the fans, due to its disparities to their earlier works, with a different, generally poppier and experimental sound, and songs that had reggae and R&amp;B influences. Their 2002 album <i><a href="/wiki/Unity_Roots_and_Family,_Away" title="Unity Roots and Family, Away">Unity Roots and Family, Away</a></i>, which was almost completely made of slow paced songs and also had elements from other types of music (gospel, rap and traditional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawan</a> music), was not welcomed by most fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiroi_Heiya_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shiroi_Heiya-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In spite of the "cold" reception these works had, the band reached No. 1 spot in the charts with both albums. Glay continued being successful with their tours in Japan. They proved to enjoy popularity outside Japan as well, in 2001, when members visited <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> to promote the Expo 2001 "Global Communication" and officially invited artists Dome, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Tse" title="Nicholas Tse">Nicholas Tse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayday_(Taiwanese_band)" title="Mayday (Taiwanese band)">Mayday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaurim" title="Jaurim">Jaurim</a> to participate in one of the Expo concerts. The next year, they played outside Japan for the first time (not counting one concert in Hawaii for their fanclub members), when they played a sold-out live performance for a 35,000 audience in Beijing. The concert is the most expensive in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>'s history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Glay also met then-Chinese President Jiang Zemin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2003, they made a contract transfer from Pony Canyon to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toshiba_EMI" title="Toshiba EMI">Toshiba EMI</a>, the most expensive transfer in Japan's history, at 4 billion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yen" title="Yen">yen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Japan_Zone_Newsletter_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Japan_Zone_Newsletter-15">[15]</a></sup> Under the new company, Glay released their first DVD—single "Itsuka" and their first B-Side compilation albums, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glay_Rare_Collectives_vol._1_and_2" title="Glay Rare Collectives vol. 1 and 2">Rare Collectives Vol.1</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glay_Rare_Collectives_vol._1_and_2" title="Glay Rare Collectives vol. 1 and 2">Rare Collectives Vol. 2</a></i>.</p>
<p>Also in 2003, an art gallery dedicated to Glay was opened in their hometown, Hakodate. "Art Style of Glay" was located in the Winning Hall, a famous building in the city and remained open until 2007.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004:_10th_Anniversary">2004: 10th Anniversary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2004: 10th Anniversary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Glay had several activities to celebrate their 10th anniversary since their major debut. As a start to this celebration, Glay presented the concert "Rock Shock vol. 3" in 2003. The name was taken from an indie festival organized by Glay in 1989, which had had two editions. In March of that year, they released their 9th studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Frustrated" title="The Frustrated">The Frustrated</a></i>, and in order to promote the album, the band held the "Glay Concert Tour 2004 X-Rated" and later, in October, the "Glay Arena Tour 2004 "The Frustrated" -Extreme-".</p>
<p>On July 27 they played a "pre-Expo" concert at Universal Studios Japan and on July 31, they played the third edition of the "Glay Expo", which was the main event of the year by the band. At the end of the year they held the fan-club exclusive "Countdown Live in Messe "Come Together"" on December 31. They finished the 10th anniversary celebration with the release of the compilation <i><a href="/wiki/Ballad_Best_Singles:_White_Road" title="Ballad Best Singles: White Road">Ballad Best Singles: White Road</a></i> in January 2005 and with the "10th Anniversary Year Final Glay Dome Tour 2005 "White Road"" which finished with the "Glay 10th Anniversary Year Final Glay Osaka 4 Days Special 2005 "White Road"".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.932006:_Rebirth">2005–2006: Rebirth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2005–2006: Rebirth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, after years of touring, promoting, and recording, the four took a hiatus. Their only new release as a group was the single "Scream", their first collaboration work, with the vocal–dance group <a href="/wiki/Exile_(Japanese_band)" title="Exile (Japanese band)">Exile</a>. "Scream", which was released under Exile's record company, was the fifth best selling single of the year.</p>
<p>During that time, Jiro created a side band with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_pillows" title="The pillows">the pillows</a> guitarist Sawao Yamanaka and <a href="/wiki/Straightener_(band)" title="Straightener (band)">Straightener</a>'s drummer Nakayama Shinpei called <a href="/wiki/The_Predators_(Japanese_band)" title="The Predators (Japanese band)">The Predators</a>, in the summer of 2005. The Predators played in summer festivals and released the mini-album <i>Hunting!</i>. Teru and Hisashi formed a band called Rally with <a href="/wiki/The_Mad_Capsule_Markets" title="The Mad Capsule Markets">The Mad Capsule Markets</a> drummer Motokatsu and bassist Koji Ueno from <a href="/wiki/Thee_Michelle_Gun_Elephant" title="Thee Michelle Gun Elephant">Thee Michelle Gun Elephant</a>. Rally contributed a cover of <a href="/wiki/Buck-Tick" title="Buck-Tick">Buck-Tick</a>'s "Aku no Hana" for the tribute CD <i><a href="/wiki/Parade_-Respective_Tracks_of_Buck-Tick-" title="Parade -Respective Tracks of Buck-Tick-">Parade -Respective Tracks of Buck-Tick-</a></i>.</p>
<p>Although it was believed that Glay took a break as a group to take a rest, it was later reported in the news that the band had split from their former agency, Unlimited Group. The reasons were obscure, but the event managed to delay the band's work. Information about the episode has always been unclear, as the band just refers to it as a time when they "couldn't move forward" and "had thoughts of quitting".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nikkansports_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nikkansports-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dailysports_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dailysports-17">[17]</a></sup> <i>Cyzo</i> Magazine's October 2005 issue had reported Glay's management dispute and referred to it as a media taboo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cyzo_Magazine_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cyzo_Magazine-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2006, Glay ended their silence by having a two-day live performance in Zepp Tokyo and a three-day live performance at <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Budokan" title="Nippon Budokan">Nippon Budokan</a>, titled "Rock 'n' Roll Swindle", which was produced as an independent event. Here, they played many of their old songs and introduced two new written songs as their new sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-What.27s_In.3F_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-What.27s_In.3F-19">[19]</a></sup> It was the start of a campaign called "Re-Birth" to promote the band's return to the music scene. Posters of their "dead" faces were displayed around the city of Tokyo and a movie of the "old" Glay burial by the "new" Glay was shown on television during the commercial breaks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glay_Re-Birth_CM_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Glay_Re-Birth_CM-20">[20]</a></sup> Takuro founded his own office, called <i>Lover Soul</i>, but the band signed up with <i>Amuse</i>, the office of <a href="/wiki/Southern_All_Stars" title="Southern All Stars">Southern All Stars</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Keisuke_Kuwata" title="Keisuke Kuwata">Keisuke Kuwata</a>, in 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nikkansports_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nikkansports-16">[16]</a></sup> until the new office was established.</p>
<p>On July 12, 2006, they released a new single titled "G4" after a year and seven months away from the limelight, returning to their rock and roll roots. In the following month, they collaborated with former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boowy" title="Boowy">Boowy</a> vocalist <a href="/wiki/Kyosuke_Himuro" title="Kyosuke Himuro">Kyosuke Himuro</a> in their single "Answer". Together, they performed in "Kyosuke Himuro + Glay Swing Addiction 2006", with "Answer" being one of the songs. In August, Glay performed at <a href="/wiki/Southern_All_Stars" title="Southern All Stars">Southern All Stars</a>'s summer concert <i>Mujintou</i> alongside various artists. In September, "Natsuoto/Henna Yume -Thousand Dreams-" was released, with Natsuoto being the theme song to TV show <i>Koi suru Hanikami</i>.</p>
<p>Following the release of these singles was their tour, "Rock 'n' Roll Swindle -Re-Birth-" from November 2006 to early 2007.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932008">2007–2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2007–2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 31, 2007, Glay released their tenth full-length studio album, <a href="/wiki/Love_Is_Beautiful_(album)" title="Love Is Beautiful (album)">Love is Beautiful</a>, containing 14 tracks, which reached No. 1 spot and was their 10th album to do so, Until then, Glay had been the all-male band with most No. 1 albums in Japan, but later that year <a href="/wiki/Mr._Children" title="Mr. Children">Mr. Children</a> would replace them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Following the release of this album was their arena tour, titled after the album.</p>
<p>Although there was not much to promote, Glay was very active as a live band. Over the summer of 2007, they performed at the "15th Anniversary Cue Music Jam-Boree" in Yubari on July 1 and produced the "Rock Shock. Vol. 4" on August 23, 2007. They revived the side projects: Jiro's band, The Predators, performed at the "<a href="/wiki/Rising_Sun_Rock_Festival" title="Rising Sun Rock Festival">Rising Sun Rock Festival</a>" in Ezo on August 17 and 18 while Teru and Hisashi's side band, Rally, performed at the "<a href="/wiki/Buck-Tick" title="Buck-Tick">Buck-Tick</a> Fest 2007" in September. Glay also performed the "Highcommunications Tour 2007-2008" from November 2007 to March 2008.</p>
<p>As of 2008, the band continued the series of shows with the concerts "Rock Shock vol. 5", in Zepp Fukuoka, on April 19 and the "AP Bank Fes '08", on July 19 and 20. In August, they performed for the first time in the United States. The "Verb tour 2008 in U.S." was held at <a href="/wiki/The_Fillmore" title="The Fillmore">The Fillmore</a>, in San Francisco, on August 12,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFWeekly.com_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-SFWeekly.com-22">[22]</a></sup> and at the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Blues" title="House of Blues">House of Blues</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.21-ENT_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-J.21-ENT-23">[23]</a></sup> in Los Angeles, on August 15 and 16, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Musical_Express_website_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Musical_Express_website-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 21, the band appeared in a concert by Kyosuke Himuro held in Hokkaido. In December, the band continued their "Glay Verb Tour" in Japan, with four concerts in Zepp Sendai, Zepp Osaka, Zepp Nagoya and Zepp Tokyo for their fan-club members. On December 30 and 31 they played the "Glay Verb Tour Final "Come Together" 2008-2009", the third edition of their year-end countdown concert.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_15th_Anniversary">2009: 15th Anniversary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2009: 15th Anniversary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Glay celebrates 15 years since the release of their debut single "Rain". In January 2009, the band announced several activities as concert tours during the year and two special concerts at the Nissan Stadium in August.</p>
<p>The first commemorative release was the 6 track single "Say Your Dream" on March 4, 2009. The title song is 13 minutes long and is reported to be a song about Glay's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mainichi_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mainichi-2">[2]</a></sup> Takuro reported that he approached this song as the message he would like to leave if he were to die, and that it took seven years to be completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glay_Official_Myspace_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Glay_Official_Myspace-25">[25]</a></sup> The promotional video was divided into two parts, the first of them started broadcasting on TV channels five days before the release of the single. The second part remains accessible on internet only through a pass code obtained with the limited edition of the single. On the same premium site, there is a PV of the coupling song "Haru made wa". The single finishes Glay's series of western song covers with "The Meaning of Life" by the band <a href="/wiki/The_Offspring" title="The Offspring">The Offspring</a>.</p>
<p>During April, the band held five concerts produced by the members, the "Glay Member Produced Live 2009 - The Great Vacation- Extra-". On April 8 and 9, the band played two concerts for official fanclub "Happy Swing" members who also have been affiliated with the fanclub for over 10 years at NHK Hall. The concerts were produced by Takuro. Teru produced another edition of the "men only night" concert (as he had done in 1999), which was held on April 13 at Studio Coast. Jiro produced a "pair only" concert, which was played on April 15 at the Osaka Hall, and, on April 17, Hisashi produced "Resonance Vol. 2", which was broadcast by Yahoo!douga.</p>
<p>On May 25, 2009, the day Glay completed 15 years since their debut, the band released the single "I am XXX". The song was used as the main theme for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Blood:_The_Last_Vampire" title="Blood: The Last Vampire">Blood: The Last Vampire</a></i> around Asia, chosen by producer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bill_Kong" title="Bill Kong">Bill Kong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On June 10, 2009, Glay released the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_hits" title="Greatest hits">greatest hits</a> collection <i>The Great Vacation Vol. 1 - Super Best of Glay</i>, a triple CD containing most of their singles released after 2000, and a re-recorded version of 1998's "Yuuwaku" in the 2 first CD's, while the third CD is mostly composed of unreleased songs.</p>
<p>During the period between June 15 and July 6 the band held the "Glay Hall Tour 2009 - The Great Vacation", prior to their two date "Glay 15th Anniversary Special Live 2009 "The Great Vacation in Nissan Stadium"", held on August 15 and 16, which gathered a total of 150 thousand people and is said to have cost 1.5 billion yen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September, Glay returned to the U.S. and held "The Great Vacation U.S. Tour" in California. The band played at <a href="/wiki/The_Fillmore" title="The Fillmore">The Fillmore</a>, in San Francisco, on September 9 and at the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Blues" title="House of Blues">House of Blues</a> on September 11 and 12.</p>
<p>On October 21, 2009, Glay released the second part of their greatest hits collection <i>The Great Vacation Vol. 2 - Super Best of Glay</i>, another triple CD containing hit singles from 1994 to 2000 and re-recorded versions of "Acid Head", "Shutter Speeds no Teema" and "Burst", as well as a remastering of the older tracks. The third disc is another collection of unreleased songs.</p>
<p>On October 22, 2009, a Tokyo court has ruled in favor of Glay over the ownership of 147 songs and ordered Unlimited Records to pay them 670 million yen for back royalties and unpaid contract fees. This was the issue that started in 2005 wherein royalties to Glay have ceased to be provided, leading for the four-man band to seek termination of their contract with the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.93present:_Loversoul_Music_.26_Associates">2010–present: Loversoul Music &amp; Associates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2010–present: Loversoul Music &amp; Associates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 1, 2010, Glay released "Apologize", a free digital single that, according to Takuro, was a signal of gratitude to the fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In June, the band announced the establishment of their own label, Loversoul Music &amp; Associates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Their first work under their new label was the single "Precious", released on September 8, followed by their tenth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Glay_(album)" title="Glay (album)">Glay</a></i>, released on October 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> From June 2010 to February 2011 the band performed their "Live Tour 2010-2011 Rock Around the World" in several halls and arenas all over Japan.</p>
<p>Glay's song "Winter, Again" was covered by <a href="/wiki/12012" title="12012">12012</a> on the compilation <i>Crush! -90's V-Rock Best Hit Cover Songs-</i>, which was released on January 26 and features current <a href="/wiki/Visual_kei" title="Visual kei">visual kei</a> bands covering songs from bands that were important to the '90s visual kei movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crush.21_-90.27s_V-Rock_Best_Hit_Cover_Songs-_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crush.21_-90.27s_V-Rock_Best_Hit_Cover_Songs--33">[33]</a></sup> Their song "Yuuwaku" was covered by Hero on its sequel, <i>Crush! 2 -90's V-Rock Best Hit Cover Songs-</i>, released on November 23, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crush.21_2_-90.27s_V-Rock_Best_Hit_Cover_Songs-_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crush.21_2_-90.27s_V-Rock_Best_Hit_Cover_Songs--34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 9, 2011, Glay released the albums; <i>Rare Collectives Vol. 3</i> and <i>Rare Collectives Vol. 4</i>. Each volume is a double CD set containing the b-sides of all their singles released since 2003, as well as unreleased tracks. On May 25, the band released the video <i>Rock Around the World 2010–2011</i> of the final concert of the tour performed at <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Super_Arena" title="Saitama Super Arena">Saitama Super Arena</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Officially, the name "Glay" is a deliberate misspelling of the word "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gray" title="Gray">gray</a>" and represents the style of music they wanted to play: a mixture between rock (black) and pop (white).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_Magazine_Article_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Time_Magazine_Article-5">[5]</a></sup> Friday magazine ran an article quoting a former classmate of a band member as saying the "glay" spelling was a simple spelling mistake rather than a deliberate decision.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Confusing the R and L is a very common mistake amongst Japanese speakers of English, because the Japanese language does not distinguish between the retroflex ("r") and lateral ("l")</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Glay_Expo">Glay Expo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Glay Expo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:JAL_GLAY_JUMBO_B747-100SUD_TYO.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1706" data-file-width="2520" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/JAL_GLAY_JUMBO_B747-100SUD_TYO.jpg/220px-JAL_GLAY_JUMBO_B747-100SUD_TYO.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/JAL_GLAY_JUMBO_B747-100SUD_TYO.jpg/330px-JAL_GLAY_JUMBO_B747-100SUD_TYO.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/JAL_GLAY_JUMBO_B747-100SUD_TYO.jpg/440px-JAL_GLAY_JUMBO_B747-100SUD_TYO.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

A <a href="/wiki/Japan_Airlines" title="Japan Airlines">Japan Airlines</a> <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> featuring Glay Expo '99 Survival livery</div>
</div>

<p>From time to time, not in a regular basis, Glay holds an exposition and a concert called Glay Expo, which is always expensively produced and lures greater audiences than their normal stadium concerts. The first edition of the event was held on July 31, 1999. The Glay Expo '99 Survival at <a href="/wiki/Makuhari_Messe" title="Makuhari Messe">Makuhari Messe</a> gathered a total audience of 200,000 people, making it the largest concert with a monetary fee ever held by a single act, certified by <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expos_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-expos-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second edition was held in 2001. Glay Expo 2001 "Global Communication" was divided into four performances: two in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, one in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ishikari" title="Ishikari">Ishikari</a>, and an all-night long performance in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fukuoka,_Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka, Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>, which featured artists; Dome from <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Tse" title="Nicholas Tse">Nicholas Tse</a> from Hong Kong, <a href="/wiki/Mayday_(Taiwanese_band)" title="Mayday (Taiwanese band)">Mayday</a> from <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaurim" title="Jaurim">Jaurim</a> from <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, and The D.e.p., a group formed by Glay's producer Masahide Sakuma, Taiwanese singer <a href="/wiki/Vivian_Hsu" title="Vivian Hsu">Vivian Hsu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gota_Yashiki" title="Gota Yashiki">Gota Yashiki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masami_Tsuchiya" title="Masami Tsuchiya">Masami Tsuchiya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mick_Karn" title="Mick Karn">Mick Karn</a>. These concerts accumulated a total audience of over 280,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expos_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-expos-35">[35]</a></sup> The concert in Ishikari had part of its fuel generated from solar and aeolic energy generating devices displayed in a booth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Japan_Times_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Japan_Times-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>July 2004 had three Glay Expo 2004 in "The Frustrated" shows. The latter two celebrating their 10th anniversary were held in the parking lot of <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios_Japan" title="Universal Studios Japan">Universal Studios Japan</a> and sold out the 100,000 tickets in under 15 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> 2014 had two Glay Expos in September, the second celebrated the band's 20th anniversary and brought 55,000 fans to <a href="/wiki/Miyagi_Stadium" title="Miyagi Stadium">Miyagi Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_exposure_and_works_outside_music">Media exposure and works outside music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Media exposure and works outside music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Glay has appeared on several TV programs and front covers of many famous band/music magazines in Japan, such as <i>Gigs</i>, <i>What's In?</i>, <i>BPass</i>, <i>Ongaku to Hito</i>, <i>PatiPati</i>, <i>Newsmaker</i>, etc. <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a> has also produced two "Glay only" special edition books.</p>
<p>They have starred several campaigns for different products, as clothes, chocolate and <a href="/wiki/MiniDisc" title="MiniDisc">Mini Discs</a>. To promote the "Expo '99", at the peak of their mainstream popularity, they had their faces printed on the sides of <a href="/wiki/Japan_Airlines" title="Japan Airlines">Japan Airlines</a> jumbo jets,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> the sponsor of the event. The campaign was also a celebration of the 10th anniversary of the Tokyo-Hakodate route.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In 2002, they starred another campaign for Japan Airlines, promoting the interchange between Japan and China.</p>
<p>Each member of Glay, except Teru, has written a book (Jiro has made two and Takuro, three). In 2001, as a part of their "Expo 2001" preparation, they presented the TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Glay_Global_Communication" title="Glay Global Communication">Glay Global Communication</a></i>. Besides appearing in magazines and on TV, all of them have or used to have their own weekly radio shows. As of 2008, only Teru and Jiro keep their radio-shows (aired on BayFM and FM802, respectively). In January 2009, Hisashi started his first TV regular segment "RX-72 -Hisashi (Glay) VS Junichi Mogi-", every last Monday of each month on the Music-On TV channel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Takuro has also started a web radio-show, <i>Takuro Mobile Meeting</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Humanitarian_and_environmental_works">Humanitarian and environmental works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Humanitarian and environmental works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Besides performing in charity festivals as the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Ribbon" title="Red Ribbon">Red Ribbon</a> Live" (in which Teru has participated since 2005), the "White Band Fes" in 2005, the "Re-style Live" (organized by the DJ Hisashi Yamada) and the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ap_bank_fes#AP_Bank_Fes" title="Ap bank fes">AP Bank Fes</a>" in 2008, Glay, specially Teru and Takuro, have been actively participating in different causes.</p>
<p>In 2001, Takuro along with <a href="/wiki/Ryuichi_Sakamoto" title="Ryuichi Sakamoto">Ryuichi Sakamoto</a> started "Artists Power", an organization of artists who want to be active in the research on renewable energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Artists_Power_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Artists_Power-42">[42]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/AP_Bank" title="AP Bank">AP Bank</a>", which was founded by <a href="/wiki/Mr._Children" title="Mr. Children">Mr. Children</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kazutoshi_Sakurai" title="Kazutoshi Sakurai">Kazutoshi Sakurai</a>, composer Takeshi Kobayashi and also Ryuichi Sakamoto, is linked to this organization - "AP" stands for both "Artists Power" and "Alternative Power".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The two started talking about this project when they worked in Sakamoto's charity project <a href="/wiki/Zero_Landmine" title="Zero Landmine">Zero Landmine</a> and the first event linked to it was the Ishikari leg of the "Glay Expo 2001" (see "Glay Expo" above).</p>
<p>Teru has been engaged to the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Ribbon" title="Red Ribbon">Red Ribbon</a> Link Project" since 2005 and has become an active spokesperson for the project. In 2008, he starred a HIV awareness campaign with posters and a CM in which he has his own blood tested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, after Eastern Japan was affected by the 9.0 magnitude <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>, Glay joined other artists in making donations for the victim's relief. The band donated 20 million yen to the "Asahi Shimbun Social Welfare Organization".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Takuro also made a donation of 10 million yen to "Mudef" (Music Design Foundation), an organization that aims at the accomplishment of all the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Organization" title="United Nations Organization">United Nations Organization</a> 8 MDGs (Millennium Development Goals) and seeks to help out people all over the world through music and arts. Takuro is also a board member along with singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Misia_(Japanese_singer)" title="Misia (Japanese singer)">Misia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Teru wrote and recorded a song called "Thank You for Your Love" to send a message of support to the victims and released it via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>. The song was re-recorded and will be released as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">download-only</a> track with its profits being donated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Red_Cross" title="Japanese Red Cross">Japanese Red Cross</a>.</p>
