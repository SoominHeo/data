<p><br/>
<b>1697 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCXCVII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">starting on Tuesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> F) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Friday" title="Common year starting on Friday">common year starting on Friday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> C) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1697th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 697th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 97th year of the <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th century</a>, and the 8th year of the <a href="/wiki/1690s" title="1690s">1690s</a> decade. As of the start of 1697, the Gregorian calendar was 10 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1697&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1697&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>January – French writer <a href="/wiki/Charles_Perrault" title="Charles Perrault">Charles Perrault</a> publishes <i><a href="/wiki/Histoires_ou_contes_du_temps_pass%C3%A9" title="Histoires ou contes du temps passé">Histoires ou contes du temps passé</a></i> ("Mother Goose tales") in Paris, a collection of popular <a href="/wiki/Fairy_tale" title="Fairy tale">fairy tales</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella" title="Cinderella">Cinderella</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Puss_in_Boots" title="Puss in Boots">Puss in Boots</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Riding_Hood" title="Red Riding Hood">Red Riding Hood</a></i>, <i>The <a href="/wiki/Sleeping_Beauty" title="Sleeping Beauty">Sleeping Beauty</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Bluebeard" title="Bluebeard">Bluebeard</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_8" title="January 8">January 8</a> – Scottish student <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Aikenhead" title="Thomas Aikenhead">Thomas Aikenhead</a> became the last person in Great Britain to be executed for <a href="/wiki/Blasphemy_law_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Blasphemy law in the United Kingdom">blasphemy</a> when he is hanged outside <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_9" title="March 9">March 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great" title="Peter the Great">Peter the Great</a> of Russia <a href="/wiki/Grand_Embassy_of_Peter_the_Great" title="Grand Embassy of Peter the Great">sets out to travel in Europe officially</a> <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/incognito" title="wiktionary:incognito">incognito</a> as "Artilleryman Pjotr Mikhailov".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_13" title="March 13">March 13</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Pet%C3%A9n" title="Spanish conquest of Petén">Spanish conquest of Petén</a>, and of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Spanish conquest of Yucatán">Yucatán</a>, is completed with the fall of <a href="/wiki/Nojpet%C3%A9n" title="Nojpetén">Nojpetén</a>, capital of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Itza" title="Itza">Itza</a> <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya</a> Kingdom, the last independent <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">indigenous peoples of the Americas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_22" title="March 22">March 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_Spain" title="Charles II of Spain">Charles II</a> of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> issued a Royal Cedula extending to the indigenous nobles of the Spanish Crown colonies, as well as to their descendants, the preeminence and honors customarily attributed to the <a href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(nobility)" title="Hidalgo (nobility)">Hidalgos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_5" title="April 5">April 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_XII_of_Sweden" title="Charles XII of Sweden">Charles XII</a>, the "Swedish Meteor", becomes king of Sweden upon the death of his father, <a href="/wiki/Charles_XI_of_Sweden" title="Charles XI of Sweden">Charles XI</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_7" title="May 7">May 7</a> – The 13th century royal <a href="/wiki/Tre_Kronor_(castle)" title="Tre Kronor (castle)">Tre Kronor</a> ("Three Crowns") castle in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> burns to the ground. A large portion of the royal library is destroyed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_1" title="June 1">June 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Augustus_II_the_Strong" title="Augustus II the Strong">Augustus II the Strong</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Polish_monarchs" title="List of Polish monarchs">king of Poland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_30" title="June 30">June 30</a> – The earliest known <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class cricket</a> match takes place in <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a> (England).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1697&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: July–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zentaicsata_(Battle_of_Senta).JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1007" data-file-width="1274" height="119" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Zentaicsata_%28Battle_of_Senta%29.JPG/150px-Zentaicsata_%28Battle_of_Senta%29.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Zentaicsata_%28Battle_of_Senta%29.JPG/225px-Zentaicsata_%28Battle_of_Senta%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Zentaicsata_%28Battle_of_Senta%29.JPG/300px-Zentaicsata_%28Battle_of_Senta%29.JPG 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Zentaicsata_(Battle_of_Senta).JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Battle of Senta</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_5" title="September 5">September 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hudson%27s_Bay" title="Battle of Hudson's Bay">Battle of Hudson's Bay</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nine_Years%27_War" title="Nine Years' War">Nine Years' War</a>): <a href="/wiki/French_ship_P%C3%A9lican_(1693)" title="French ship Pélican (1693)">French warsship <i>Pélican</i></a> captures <a href="/wiki/York_Factory" title="York Factory">York Factory</a>, a trading post of the English <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson's Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a> in modern-day <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> (Canada).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_11" title="September 11">September 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Zenta" title="Battle of Zenta">Battle of Zenta</a> – <a href="/wiki/Prince_Eugene_of_Savoy" title="Prince Eugene of Savoy">Prince Eugene of Savoy</a> crushes the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> army of <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_II" title="Mustafa II">Mustafa II</a> and effectively ends Turkish hopes of recovering lost ground in Hungary.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_20" title="September 20">September 20</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Ryswick" title="Treaty of Ryswick">Treaty of Ryswick</a> signed by France and the Grand Alliance to end both the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Years%27_War" title="Nine Years' War">Nine Years' War</a> and <a href="/wiki/King_William%27s_War" title="King William's War">King William's War</a>. The conflict having been inconclusive, the treaty is proposed because the combatants have exhausted their national treasuries. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_XIV" title="Louis XIV">Louis XIV</a> recognises <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_England" title="William III of England">William III</a> as King of England &amp; Scotland and both sides return territories they have taken in battle. In North America, the treaty returns Port Royal (<a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>) to France. In practice, the treaty is little more than a truce; it does not resolve any of the fundamental colonial problems and the peace lasts only five years.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_2" title="December 2">December 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a> is opened in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_14" title="December 14">December 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_XII_of_Sweden" title="Charles XII of Sweden">Charles XII of Sweden</a> is crowned king at the age of 15.</li>
</ul>
