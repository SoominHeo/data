<p><i><b>Garage, Inc.</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover songs</a> by American <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>. It was released on November 23, 1998 through <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra Records</a>. Over 2.5 million copies have been sold in the U.S. as certified by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>. It includes cover songs, B-side covers, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_$5.98_E.P.:_Garage_Days_Re-Revisited" title="The $5.98 E.P.: Garage Days Re-Revisited">The $5.98 E.P.: Garage Days Re-Revisited</a></i>, which had gone out of print since its original release in 1987. The title is a combination of <i>Garage Days Revisited</i> and their song "Damage, Inc.", from <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_Puppets" title="Master of Puppets">Master of Puppets</a></i>, and the album's artwork draws heavily from the 1987 EP. The album features songs by artists that have influenced Metallica, including many bands from the <a href="/wiki/New_wave_of_British_heavy_metal" title="New wave of British heavy metal">new wave of British heavy metal</a> movement, hardcore punk bands and popular songs. As of August 2013 the album has sold more than 6 million copies worldwide.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garage_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The day after Metallica finished the North American leg of the <a href="/wiki/Poor_Re-Touring_Me_Tour" title="Poor Re-Touring Me Tour">Poor Re-Touring Me Tour</a> in San Diego's <a href="/wiki/Sleep_Train_Amphitheatre_(Chula_Vista,_California)" title="Sleep Train Amphitheatre (Chula Vista, California)">Coors Amphitheatre</a>, they hit the studio to start recording a new album of cover versions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As <a href="/wiki/Lars_Ulrich" title="Lars Ulrich">Lars Ulrich</a> explained, the band wanted to do something different after "three pretty serious albums in a row, starting with <a href="/wiki/Metallica_(album)" title="Metallica (album)">the Black album</a> and then <i><a href="/wiki/Load_(album)" title="Load (album)">Load</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Reload_(Metallica_album)" title="Reload (Metallica album)">ReLoad</a></i>", and the process would be easier by working with covers, specially as the band had a tradition of taking other people's songs and "turn them into something very Metallica, different from what the original artist did".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bill_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-bill-5">[5]</a></sup> Given the band had recorded many covers that were spread across various releases, such as B-sides of their singles and the 1987 EP <i><a href="/wiki/The_$5.98_E.P.:_Garage_Days_Re-Revisited" title="The $5.98 E.P.: Garage Days Re-Revisited">The $5.98 E.P.: Garage Days Re-Revisited</a></i>, the band would "put them all in a nice little packaging for easy listening" along with newly recorded version chosen through a group decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bill_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-bill-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Only one of the eleven songs in the "New Recordings '98" disk was not done in the three week sessions, a version of <a href="/wiki/Lynyrd_Skynyrd" title="Lynyrd Skynyrd">Lynyrd Skynyrd</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Tuesday%27s_Gone" title="Tuesday's Gone">Tuesday's Gone</a>" the band recorded for a radio broadcast along with friends such as <a href="/wiki/Les_Claypool" title="Les Claypool">Les Claypool</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Popper" title="John Popper">John Popper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Rossington" title="Gary Rossington">Gary Rossington</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Packaging">Packaging</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garage_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Packaging">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cover for <i>Garage Inc.</i> had a <a href="/wiki/Ross_Halfin" title="Ross Halfin">Ross Halfin</a> photograph of Metallica dressed as mechanics. The band wanted the booklet to hold a detailed account of the contents of the project, and designer Andy Airfix was allowed to search through Ulrich's catalogue of Metallica memorabilia in San Francisco to create a 32-page booklet. Airfix also did the back cover, where the front of <i>Garage Days Re-Revisited</i> was modified with headshots of Metallica in 1998 and the track list written on <a href="/wiki/Tracing_paper" title="Tracing paper">tracing paper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales_and_certification">Sales and certification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garage_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sales and certification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the U.S., <i>Garage Inc.</i> sold 426,500 units in the first week of release, making Metallica's fourth straight debut surpassing 400,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Still, the million-plus record breaking debut of <a href="/wiki/Garth_Brooks" title="Garth Brooks">Garth Brooks</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Live_(Garth_Brooks_album)" title="Double Live (Garth Brooks album)">Double Live</a></i> made <i>Garage Inc.</i> land only at second on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> As of 2003 the album has sold over 2.5 million copies and has been certified 5× Platinum.</p>
