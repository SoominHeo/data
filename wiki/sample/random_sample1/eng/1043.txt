<p><b>HAT-P-13c</b> is a substellar object orbiting the star <a href="/wiki/HAT-P-13" title="HAT-P-13">HAT-P-13</a> located 698 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_year" title="Light year">light years</a> away from Earth in the constellation of <a href="/wiki/Ursa_Major" title="Ursa Major">Ursa Major</a>. A search for transits was negative, however only 72% of the possible transit configurations could be ruled out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Szab.C3.B32010_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Szab.C3.B32010-4">[4]</a></sup> With a mass at least 15.2 times that of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>, it may be a massive planet or a small <a href="/wiki/Brown_dwarf" title="Brown dwarf">brown dwarf</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakos2009_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bakos2009-3">[3]</a></sup> The gravitational effect of this object on the inner transiting planet <a href="/wiki/HAT-P-13b" title="HAT-P-13b">HAT-P-13b</a> may allow a precise determination of the inner planet's internal structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
