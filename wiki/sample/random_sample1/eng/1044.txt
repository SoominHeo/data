<p><b>HB Køge</b> (<small>Danish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Danish" title="Help:IPA for Danish">[ˈkøːə]</a></span> or <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈkøːjə]</a></span>) is a professional <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based primarily in the town of <a href="/wiki/Herf%C3%B8lge" title="Herfølge">Herfølge</a>, and secondly in the town of <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B8ge" title="Køge">Køge</a>, both in the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B8ge_Municipality" title="Køge Municipality">Køge Municipality</a>, part of 'Region of Zealand', in the eastern part of Zealand, south of <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>. It was created through the merger of <a href="/wiki/Herf%C3%B8lge_Boldklub" title="Herfølge Boldklub">Herfølge Boldklub</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B8ge_Boldklub" title="Køge Boldklub">Køge Boldklub</a> in 2009.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HB_K%C3%B8ge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Danish_1st_Division" title="Danish 1st Division">Danish 1st Division</a> club <a href="/wiki/Herf%C3%B8lge_Boldklub" title="Herfølge Boldklub">Herfølge Boldklub</a> and bankrupt club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B8ge_Boldklub" title="Køge Boldklub">Køge Boldklub</a> decided in March 2009 to merge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The club played their first season as a merged club in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009-10_Danish_Superliga" title="2009-10 Danish Superliga">2009–10 season</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Superliga" title="Danish Superliga">Danish Superliga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The season ended in relegatation.</p>
<p>In the following <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Danish_1st_Division" title="2010–11 Danish 1st Division">1st Division</a> season, they finished second and returned to the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Superliga" title="Danish Superliga">Superliga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Following the promotion manager <a href="/wiki/Aurelijus_Skarbalius" title="Aurelijus Skarbalius">Aurelijus Skarbalius</a> left the club for a job as assistant manager of <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8ndby_IF" title="Brøndby IF">Brøndby IF</a> and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Tommy_M%C3%B8ller_Nielsen" title="Tommy Møller Nielsen">Tommy Møller Nielsen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The club's second appearance in the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Superliga" title="Danish Superliga">Danish Superliga</a> ended in another relegation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Danish_1st_Division" title="2012–13 Danish 1st Division">2012–13 season</a> started with a series of bad results, and this led to the sacking of Møller Nielsen in September 2012 after a 5–0 defeat against <a href="/wiki/Lyngby_Boldklub" title="Lyngby Boldklub">Lyngby Boldklub</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He was replaced by his assistant <a href="/wiki/Per_Frandsen" title="Per Frandsen">Per Frandsen</a>, who led the club to two consecutive sixth-place finishes before leaving for a youth job at <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8ndby_IF" title="Brøndby IF">Brøndby IF</a> in June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
