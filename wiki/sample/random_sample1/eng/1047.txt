<p><b>HD 111232 b</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">extrasolar planet</a> that orbits almost 2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronomical_Unit" title="Astronomical Unit">AU</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Mass" title="Mass">mass</a> of at least 6.8 times that of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>. This planet was discovered in the <a href="/wiki/La_Silla_Observatory" title="La Silla Observatory">La Silla Observatory</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michel_Mayor" title="Michel Mayor">Michel Mayor</a> using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CORALIE_spectrograph" title="CORALIE spectrograph">CORALIE spectrograph</a> on 30 June 2003, along with six other planets, including <a href="/wiki/HD_41004_Ab" title="HD 41004 Ab">HD 41004 Ab</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_65216_b" title="HD 65216 b">HD 65216 b</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_169830_c" title="HD 169830 c">HD 169830 c</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_216770_b" title="HD 216770 b">HD 216770 b</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_10647_b" title="HD 10647 b">HD 10647 b</a>, and <a href="/wiki/HD_142415_b" title="HD 142415 b">HD 142415 b</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mayor2004_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mayor2004-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
