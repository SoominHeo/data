<p><b>HD 1461 b</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">extrasolar planet</a>, orbiting the 6th <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G-type_star" title="G-type star">G-type star</a> <a href="/wiki/HD_1461" title="HD 1461">HD 1461</a>, 76 light years away in the constellation <a href="/wiki/Cetus" title="Cetus">Cetus</a>. This planet has a <a href="/wiki/Minimum_mass" title="Minimum mass">minimum mass</a> 8.1 times that of Earth and orbits at a precise distance of 0.063434 AU with an eccentricity of 0.04. It is currently unknown whether the planet is a <a href="/wiki/Gas_giant" title="Gas giant">gas giant</a> like <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">Uranus</a> or <a href="/wiki/Neptune" title="Neptune">Neptune</a>, or has terrestrial composition like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CoRoT-7_b" title="CoRoT-7 b">CoRoT-7 b</a>. This planet was discovered on 14 December 2009 from using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radial_velocity_method" title="Radial velocity method">radial velocity method</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keck_Observatory" title="Keck Observatory">Keck</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Australian_Observatory" title="Anglo-Australian Observatory">Anglo-Australian Observatories</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disco-rivera_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-disco-rivera-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ucsc-news_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ucsc-news-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
