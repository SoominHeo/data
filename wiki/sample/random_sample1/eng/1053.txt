<p><b>HD 172051</b> (<b>86 G. Sagittarii</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Main_sequence" title="Main sequence">main sequence</a> star in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Sagittarius_(constellation)" title="Sagittarius (constellation)">Sagittarius</a>. It is located about 42 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_year" title="Light year">light years</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> and is considered a <a href="/wiki/Solar_analog" title="Solar analog">solar analog</a>. The mass is similar to the Sun, although it is cooler and has a lower luminosity. Due to this similarity, HD 172051 has been selected as an early target star for both the <a href="/wiki/Terrestrial_Planet_Finder" title="Terrestrial Planet Finder">Terrestrial Planet Finder</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darwin_(ESA)" title="Darwin (ESA)">Darwin</a> missions, which seek to find an Earth-like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">extrasolar planet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC031107_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC031107-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>During a search for <a href="/wiki/Brown_dwarf" title="Brown dwarf">brown dwarf</a> companions using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hale_telescope" title="Hale telescope">Hale telescope</a> in 2004, two candidate companions were identified at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angular_separation" title="Angular separation">angular separations</a> of 5″ and 6″. However, these were determined to be background stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pasp122_896_1195_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-pasp122_896_1195-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
