<p><b>HD 23079 b</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Exoplanet" title="Exoplanet">exoplanet</a> approximately 114 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_year" title="Light year">light years</a> away in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reticulum_(constellation)" title="Reticulum (constellation)">Reticulum</a>. The planet has <a href="/wiki/Mass" title="Mass">mass</a> of at least 2.45 Jupiters, although only the <a href="/wiki/Minimum_mass" title="Minimum mass">minimum mass</a> is known since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">inclination</a> is unknown. The planet takes almost exactly two years to orbit the star at the average distance of 1.6 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronomical_Unit" title="Astronomical Unit">AU</a>. The planet's <a href="/wiki/Orbital_eccentricity" title="Orbital eccentricity">orbital eccentricity</a> is about the same as <a href="/wiki/109_Piscium_b" title="109 Piscium b">109 Piscium b</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_75898_b" title="HD 75898 b">HD 75898 b</a>, and <a href="/wiki/HD_69830_b" title="HD 69830 b">HD 69830 b</a> at 0.102. The distance range from as close as 1.44 AU to as far away as 1.76 AU from the parent star.</p>
<p>The planet was discovered in 2001 by Tinney et al., who used <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doppler_spectrometer" title="Doppler spectrometer">doppler spectrometer</a> to look for shifts of star’s spectrum caused by a <a href="/wiki/Gravity" title="Gravity">gravity</a> of the planet tug the star around as the planet orbits.</p>
