<p><br/>
<b>1708 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCVIII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap year</a> <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Leap year starting on Sunday">starting on Sunday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> AG) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Leap year starting on Thursday">leap year starting on Thursday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> DC) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1708th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 708th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 8th year of the <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>, and the 9th year of the <a href="/wiki/1700s_(decade)" title="1700s (decade)">1700s</a> decade. As of the start of 1708, the Gregorian calendar was 11 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923. In the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_calendar" title="Swedish calendar">Swedish calendar</a> it was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Wednesday" title="Leap year starting on Wednesday">leap year starting on Wednesday</a>, one day ahead of the Julian and ten days behind the Gregorian calendar.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1708&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_11" title="March 11">March 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Queen_of_Great_Britain" title="Anne, Queen of Great Britain">Queen Anne</a> withholds <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Assent" title="Royal Assent">Royal Assent</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Militia_Bill" title="Scottish Militia Bill">Scottish Militia Bill</a>, the last time a British monarch vetoes legislation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_23" title="March 23">March 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/James_Francis_Edward_Stuart" title="James Francis Edward Stuart">James Francis Edward Stuart</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jacobite_pretender" title="Jacobite pretender">Jacobite pretender</a> to the throne of Great Britain, unsuccessfully tries to land from a French fleet in the <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Forth" title="Firth of Forth">Firth of Forth</a> in Scotland.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1708&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: July–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Tewoflos" title="Tewoflos">Tewoflos</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Ethiopia" title="Emperor of Ethiopia">Emperor of Ethiopia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a>: Allied victory under the command of <a href="/wiki/John_Churchill,_1st_Duke_of_Marlborough" title="John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough">John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough</a>, against the French at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Oudenarde" title="Battle of Oudenarde">Battle of Oudenarde</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology292_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology292-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>August – The future <a href="/wiki/Charles_VI,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor</a> weds <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_Christine_of_Brunswick-Wolfenb%C3%BCttel" title="Elisabeth Christine of Brunswick-Wolfenbüttel">Elisabeth Christine of Brunswick-Wolfenbüttel</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_18" title="August 18">August 18</a> – War of the Spanish Succession: Capture of <a href="/wiki/Minorca" title="Minorca">Minorca</a> by British forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology292_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology292-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_23" title="August 23">August 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pamheiba" title="Pamheiba">Meidingu Pamheiba</a> is crowned King of <a href="/wiki/Manipur_(princely_state)" title="Manipur (princely state)">Manipur</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_29" title="August 29">August 29</a> – A native American attack in <a href="/wiki/Haverhill,_Massachusetts" title="Haverhill, Massachusetts">Haverhill, Massachusetts</a>, kills 16 settlers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_28" title="September 28">September 28</a> (<a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a>); September 29 (<a href="/wiki/Swedish_calendar" title="Swedish calendar">Swedish calendar</a>); October 9 (N.S.) – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lesnaya" title="Battle of Lesnaya">Battle of Lesnaya</a> (<a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_War" title="Great Northern War">Great Northern War</a>): <a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great" title="Peter the Great">Peter the Great</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tsardom_of_Russia" title="Tsardom of Russia">Russia</a> defeats the forces of the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Empire" title="Swedish Empire">Swedish Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_12" title="October 12">October 12</a> – War of the Spanish Succession: British forces capture <a href="/wiki/Lille" title="Lille">Lille</a> after a two-month siege, although the <a href="/wiki/Citadel" title="Citadel">citadel</a> continues to hold out for another six weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH205206_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH205206-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_26" title="October 26">October 26</a> – Completion of the construction of <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1708&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kandahar" title="Kandahar">Kandahar</a> is conquered by Mir Wais.</li>
<li>Fearful of a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> attack, the Russians blow up the city of <a href="/wiki/Tartu" title="Tartu">Tartu</a> in <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Estonia_(1561%E2%80%931721)" title="Duchy of Estonia (1561–1721)">Estonia</a>.</li>
<li>One third of the population of <a href="/wiki/Masuria" title="Masuria">Masuria</a> dies of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Death" title="Black Death">plague</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Johann Sebastian Bach</a> is appointed as chamber musician and organist at the court in <a href="/wiki/Weimar" title="Weimar">Weimar</a>.</li>
<li>Italian philosopher <a href="/wiki/Giambattista_Vico" title="Giambattista Vico">Giambattista Vico</a> delivers his inaugural lecture to the University of Naples, published as his first book, <i>De Nostri Temporis Studiorum Ratione</i> ("On the Order of the Scholarly Disciplines of Our Times"), in <a href="/wiki/1709" title="1709">1709</a>.</li>
<li>Calcareous <a href="/wiki/Hard-paste_porcelain" title="Hard-paste porcelain">hard-paste porcelain</a> is produced for the first time in Europe at <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ehrenfried_Walther_von_Tschirnhaus" title="Ehrenfried Walther von Tschirnhaus">Ehrenfried Walther von Tschirnhaus</a> and developed after his death (October) by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_B%C3%B6ttger" title="Johann Friedrich Böttger">Johann Friedrich Böttger</a>.</li>
<li>Merger (with consent of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a>) of the Company of Merchants of London Trading into the East Indies and the more recently established English Company Trading to the East Indies to form the United Company of Merchants of England Trading to the East Indies, known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honourable_East_India_Company" title="Honourable East India Company">Honourable East India Company</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
