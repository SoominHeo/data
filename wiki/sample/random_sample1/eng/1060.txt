<p><b>HD 73526 c</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">extrasolar planet</a> orbiting about 97 million miles (1.05 AU) away from its parent star. Based on its <a href="/wiki/Mass" title="Mass">mass</a>, this planet is likely to be a <a href="/wiki/Gas_giant" title="Gas giant">gas giant</a>. At the distance this planet orbits from its star, which is more luminous than our Sun, HD 73526 c would receive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insolation" title="Insolation">insolation</a> 84% that of <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">Venus</a>.</p>
<p>HD 73526 c is in a 2:1 <a href="/wiki/Orbital_resonance" title="Orbital resonance">orbital resonance</a> with <a href="/wiki/HD_73526_b" title="HD 73526 b">HD 73526 b</a>. Every time planet b goes around its sun twice, planet c goes around once, a similar configuration to planets <a href="/wiki/Gliese_876_b" title="Gliese 876 b">b</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gliese_876_c" title="Gliese 876 c">c</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Gliese_876" title="Gliese 876">Gliese 876</a> system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tinney2006_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tinney2006-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
