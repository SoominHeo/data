<p><b>High and Mighty Color</b> (stylized as <b>HIGH and MIGHTY COLOR</b>) was a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band active from 2003 to 2010. They had two vocalists; a male and a female.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_and_Anti-Nobunaga">Formation and Anti-Nobunaga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation and Anti-Nobunaga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band started in Okinawa when Sassy and Meg left a <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> <a href="/wiki/Cover_band" title="Cover band">cover band</a> after they decided to make their own music. Sassy offered Mackaz the opportunity to join their new band, he accepted and invited long time friend Kazuto to join as well. Meg sat in on a school performance one day, and Yuusuke's voice stood out to him, so he offered him a chance to join the band. Initially Yuusuke declined due to wanting to be a solo singer, but joined after Meg reportedly asked him to join every day for nearly four months. The band, known as <b>Anti-Nobunaga</b> at this time, played mainly in small coffee shops and art theaters for about a year. Sassy, the bandleader, sent demo tapes to every major Japanese label, all of whom rejected the band. It was not until a small label signed the band that they found their first big break. Anti-Nobunaga were now playing areas further from their local hometown and eventually landed a spot at the yearly music festival of Okinawa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="M.C4.81kii_joins_and_name_change">Mākii joins and name change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mākii joins and name change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the summer of 2003, at the yearly music festival in Okinawa, the band's label promoted their spot and eventually they ended up playing for a sold out crowd. Due to this festival being a "per show" ticket selling one, it meant that Anti-Nobunaga was the only band to play for a sold out crowd. A scout for a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment_Japan" title="Sony Music Entertainment Japan">Sony Music Entertainment Japan</a> was in the crowd and offered the band the chance to produce music for an upcoming various artists album entitled <i>Okinawa 2003</i>. The band then composed their two and only completely original songs under the name of Anti-Nobunaga entitled "Meaning" and "Hate You!". At another festival known as the "Music Picnic Festival", vocalist <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81kii" title="Mākii">Mākii</a> also grabbed the attention of the same scout that found Anti-Nobunaga. The scout felt that both projects on their own were not remarkable, but combined, the band could be something never before seen on the Japanese music scene. Mākii first rejected the offer due to her wanting to study English abroad, but was later convinced by her future band mates to watch them perform. After she was impressed by the band's energy, Mākii agreed to join them. With the addition of Mākii the band took on a new name, <b>High and Mighty Color</b>. After six months of perfecting their craft, they moved up from SMJ's minor label to their major label, in league with bands such as <a href="/wiki/Orange_Range" title="Orange Range">Orange Range</a>. High and Mighty Color is some times referred to as the sister band of Orange Range, because they are both from the Okinawa area, an area known for musical fusion thanks in part to various military bases stationed there and were both discovered at the same music festival. They spent much of 2004 writing and recording, in late 2004 they released their first single, "<a href="/wiki/Over_(High_and_Mighty_Color_song)" title="Over (High and Mighty Color song)">Over</a>" which was only sold through <a href="/wiki/Tower_Records" title="Tower Records">Tower Records</a> stores. It sold out in a month and became immensely scarce, thus it was re-released the next year. The album sat at number one spot of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indie_(music)" title="Indie (music)">indie</a> <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> charts for an entire month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005">2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2005">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During 2005, the band released their first major single, "<a href="/wiki/Pride_(High_and_Mighty_Color_song)" title="Pride (High and Mighty Color song)">Pride</a>". It was originally going to be attached to a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> event being held at the same time, but was picked up for something else. A Sunrise music partner was looking for a song to use for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam_Seed_Destiny" title="Mobile Suit Gundam Seed Destiny">Mobile Suit Gundam Seed Destiny</a></i> and "Pride" was chosen to be that song. At the time, the show was the number one rated <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a>, and being attached to this helped the single's sales immensely. With a final sales count of 223,208 copies, "Pride" remains the band's best selling single of their career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over the next year, the band went on a media blitz, performing for shows such as <i>Music Fighter</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Station" title="Music Station">Music Station</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hey!_Hey!_Hey!" title="Hey! Hey! Hey!">Hey! Hey! Hey!</a></i> at various times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> During August, the band released "<a href="/wiki/Days_(High_and_Mighty_Color_song)" title="Days (High and Mighty Color song)">Days</a>", their fourth single, selling 7,679 copies and not breaking the top 20 mark on the Oricon charts. Despite this setback, the band was still honored with the Japanese Record Grand Prix's "Rookie of the Year" award, and Mākii's grandmother who had just died was given a tribute just before their performance.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006:_Gou_on_Progressive">2006: <i>Gou on Progressive</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2006: Gou on Progressive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2006, the band released their second album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gou_on_Progressive" title="Gou on Progressive">Gou on Progressive</a></i> after only releasing two singles for the album. The album sold less copies than their first album, but it stayed on the Oricon charts longer and managed to gain a higher peak position than their first album. The band's first single of the year was "<a href="/wiki/Ichirin_no_Hana" title="Ichirin no Hana">Ichirin no Hana</a>" and was used as the third opening to the anime adaption of <i><a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)" title="Bleach (manga)">Bleach</a></i>. This was their first and only single to break the top five mark on the Oricon charts since their debut with "Pride." They also played their first American concert at an <a href="/wiki/Anime_convention" title="Anime convention">anime convention</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> on April 28.</p>
<p>The band then went on a large-scale tour of Japan. They played 65 venues over the course of the next five months. They released their seventh single, "<a href="/wiki/Dive_into_Yourself" title="Dive into Yourself">Dive into Yourself</a>", in August. The song was used as the opening theme of the video game <i>Sengoku Basara II</i>. For a time this was the band's second lowest selling single and was also their lowest ranking single among the Oricon charts, only managing to reach the 24th spot on the weekly charts. Their 8th single "<a href="/wiki/Enrai_(Tooku_ni_Aru_Akari)" title="Enrai (Tooku ni Aru Akari)">Enrai ~Tooku ni Aru Akari~</a>" was released on October 25 and used as the ending theme for the third <i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam_SEED_Destiny:_Special_Edition" title="Mobile Suit Gundam SEED Destiny: Special Edition">Mobile Suit Gundam Seed Destiny: Special Edition</a></i> compilation movie. The single entered the charts 12 spots higher than "Dive into Yourself" at number 12. "Energy", a B-side song from the "<a href="/wiki/Style_(Get_Glory_in_This_Hand)" title="Style (Get Glory in This Hand)">Style ~Get Glory in this Hand~</a>" single, was used in a tribute compilation album composed for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Note" title="Death Note">Death Note: The Last Name</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007:_San">2007: <i>San</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2007: San">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mākii made her acting debut with the December 2006 movie, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anata_wo_Wasurenai" title="Anata wo Wasurenai">Anata wo Wasurenai</a> (I Won't Forget You)</i>. The band performed two songs for the film, "Tadoritsuku Basho", the film's theme song and "Oxalis", an insert song. The two songs were released as a double <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" title="A-side and B-side">A-side</a> single, "<a href="/wiki/Tadoritsuku_Basho/Oxalis" title="Tadoritsuku Basho/Oxalis">Tadoritsuku Basho/Oxalis</a>". When <i>Anata wo Wasurenai</i> was released, it entered the Japanese box offices in the 10th position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Also, "Resistance" was used as the theme song for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a> game, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bleach_DS_2nd:_Kokui_Hirameku_Requiem" title="Bleach DS 2nd: Kokui Hirameku Requiem">Kokui Hirameku Requiem</a></i>.</p>
<p>On February 4, the official High and Mighty Color website released various 30 second previews of songs from the band's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/San_(album)" title="San (album)">San</a></i>, which was released on February 21. The album contained 15 tracks with 10 being new releases. The album was ranked number 10 on the Oricon daily rankings during its first day and 16th on the weekly charts.</p>
<p>In March, it was announced that Yūsuke would work with former <a href="/wiki/Porno_Graffitti" title="Porno Graffitti">Porno Graffitti</a> bassist, <a href="/wiki/Masami_Shiratama" title="Masami Shiratama">Masami Shiratama</a>. Yūsuke provided vocals for the joint single entitled "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Honno_(single)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Honno (single) (page does not exist)">Honnō</a>", the single was released on May 23 featured Yūsuke using what the band describes as his "machine gun vocals".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band also made their second American performance on May 11 and May 12 during 2007's <a href="/wiki/Anime_Central" title="Anime Central">Anime Central</a> anime convention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The band performed 12 songs and were opened by the Spiral Spiders.</p>
<p>The 10th single from the band was released on August 1, 2007. The title track, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dreams_(high_and_mighty_color_single)" title="Dreams (high and mighty color single)">Dreams</a>", was used as the second ending theme to the anime <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darker_Than_Black" title="Darker Than Black">Darker Than Black</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> During its first week, the single entered the Oricon charts at the 24th spot.</p>
<p>On November 4, 2007, it was announced that the band would be releasing a single collection album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/10_Color_Singles" title="10 Color Singles">10 Color Singles</a></i> featuring their first 10 singles and a bonus track, "Ichirin no Hana (Live Studio Version)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It also includes a DVD with music videos that never made it to DVD like; "Style", "Dreams", "Here I Am", and the new music video for "Mushroom". In December, they contributed to <i><a href="/wiki/Luna_Sea_Memorial_Cover_Album_-Re:birth-" title="Luna Sea Memorial Cover Album -Re:birth-">Luna Sea Memorial Cover Album -Re:birth-</a></i>, covering <a href="/wiki/Luna_Sea" title="Luna Sea">Luna Sea</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Rosier_(song)" title="Rosier (song)">Rosier</a>".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008:_Rock_Pit_and_M.C4.81kii_leaves">2008: <i>Rock Pit</i> and Mākii leaves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2008: Rock Pit and Mākii leaves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>High and Mighty Color's 12th single, "<a href="/wiki/Flashback/Komorebi_no_Uta" title="Flashback/Komorebi no Uta">Flashback/Komorebi no Uta</a>", was released on February 27, 2008. It was their second double a-side single and was used in promotion with the anime series <i><a href="/wiki/Hero_Tales" title="Hero Tales">Hero Tales</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Their 4th album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Pit" title="Rock Pit">Rock Pit</a></i>, was released on March 19 and became their lowest selling album.</p>
<p>The band began the fourth volume of their "Live Bee Loud" tour on March 17, 2008 at the famous Club Quattro in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> They then went on to perform at 13 other venues across the country, with the final venue being in their hometown of Okinawa. The 13th single of the band, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Limit_(High_and_Mighty_Color_song)" title="Hot Limit (High and Mighty Color song)">Hot Limit</a>", is a cover of one of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T.M._Revolution" title="T.M. Revolution">T.M. Revolution</a>'s early singles. The visual style of both the music video and the promotional images is meant to mimic that of the original. This was also the first time since "Dive into Yourself" that Sony Music Entertainment had produced a commercial for advertisement of a single from the band. The single went on to garner the best sales for the band in nearly two years and ranked at number 20 on the Oricon charts in the weekly rankings for its first week.</p>
<p>On July 1, 2008, it was announced that Mākii had married <a href="/wiki/Dreams_Come_True_(band)" title="Dreams Come True (band)">Dreams Come True</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Masato_Nakamura" title="Masato Nakamura">Masato Nakamura</a> on June 22, 2008 and would be leaving the band by the end of the year. In her parting message, Mākii thanked her fellow band members and all of their fans for supporting her and the group for such a long time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> A special event was planned for Mākii's graduation from the band as well as at least one more single. It was also announced that the band will begin searching for a new vocalist in early 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>High and Mighty Color appeared at the <a href="/wiki/Zepp" title="Zepp">Zepp</a> in Nagoya, alongside <a href="/wiki/175R" title="175R">175R</a>, <a href="/wiki/One_Ok_Rock" title="One Ok Rock">One Ok Rock</a> and Gollbetty, on August 15 as part of the "Steal The Show Friday Night" lineup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The 14th single from the band, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Remember_(High_and_Mighty_Color)" title="Remember (High and Mighty Color)">Remember</a>", was released on October 15, 2008 and is their final single featuring Mākii.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Halca_joins_and_Swamp_Man">2009: Halca joins and <i>Swamp Man</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2009: Halca joins and Swamp Man">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band was transferred to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spice_Records" title="Spice Records">Spice Records</a> on January 1, 2009. Spice Records is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a> based studio that maintained minor rights to the band while under SME. The first live <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> from the band was released on January 25 and reached the 11th spot on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> weekly DVD charts, reaching a peak of 9th on the daily charts. After the departure of Mākii, the band began auditions for a replacement vocalist; the auditions were open to women for a month before call backs and performances were done. On February 9, 2009, the band announced that a replacement vocalist was found, whose information would soon be added to their website after an extensive overhaul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The band planned to begin working on a summer single and live tour to promote the new vocalist in May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The first single, titled "XYZ", featuring the band's new vocalist <a href="/wiki/Halca" title="Halca">Halca</a>, was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> in both Japan and Australia on July 8, 2009.</p>
<p>The band released their fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Swamp_Man" title="Swamp Man">Swamp Man</a></i>, on September 2, 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and took the 25th spot on the Oricon charts on its release date.</p>
<p>Following the release of the album, High and Mighty Color released a tribute song for two popular manga named "Red". The song was released digitally in Japan on December 9, 2009 and re-released December 23, 2009 on iTunes Japan and Japan Files.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010:_Break_up">2010: Break up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=High_and_Mighty_Color&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2010: Break up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 13, 2010, the band was scheduled to give a live concert with Halca, titled "Night Light Parade Vol. 1.1: One of the New Beginning" at Shibuya Club Quattro in Tokyo.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>It was announced on May 19 that on August 11 High and Mighty Color would release a single, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Re:ache&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Re:ache (page does not exist)">Re:ache</a>", which will be the band's last. Speaking of the reason for the breakup, band members cited "musical differences" and "future opportunities". "Re:ache" contained three new songs, with four songs in total, the fourth being a live rendition of "Red". The single was bundled with a DVD of the music videos for "Re:ache" and "Good Bye", as well as live recordings of their American concert from <a href="/wiki/Sakura-Con" title="Sakura-Con">Sakura-Con</a> 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
