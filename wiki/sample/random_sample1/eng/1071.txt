<p><b>HR 4523</b> (<b>66 G. Centauri</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary star</a> system that is located in the northeastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Centaurus" title="Centaurus">Centaurus</a> <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a>, at a distance of about 30.07 ly (9.22 pc) from the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Solar System</a>. The larger member of the system is a G-type star that is smaller than the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> but of similar mass. It has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Common_proper_motion" title="Common proper motion">common proper motion</a> companion that was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Willem_Jacob_Luyten" title="Willem Jacob Luyten">W. J. Luyten</a> in 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aphs190_1_1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-aphs190_1_1-5">[5]</a></sup> This M-type star appears to be in a wide orbit around the primary at a current separation of about 211 <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">astronomical units</a> (AU),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aphs190_1_1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-aphs190_1_1-5">[5]</a></sup> (or 211 times the separation of the Earth from the Sun). By comparison, <a href="/wiki/Neptune" title="Neptune">Neptune</a> orbits at an average distance of 30 AU.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> for the primary star in this system is G2V;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apj132_1_161_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-apj132_1_161-4">[4]</a></sup> the same as the Sun. That of the <a href="/wiki/Red_dwarf" title="Red dwarf">red dwarf</a> companion is M4V.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aphs190_1_1_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-aphs190_1_1-5">[5]</a></sup> The primary star has an estimated 86%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apj701_2_1732_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-apj701_2_1732-12">[12]</a></sup> to 89% the <a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass">mass of the Sun</a>, 96% of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_radius" title="Solar radius">Sun's radius</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apjss168_2_297_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-apjss168_2_297-7">[7]</a></sup> and 85% of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_luminosity" title="Solar luminosity">Sun's luminosity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ab6_2_308_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-ab6_2_308-8">[8]</a></sup> It is a slow rotator, with a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_rotation" title="Stellar rotation">projected rotational velocity</a> of 0.5 km/s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa493_3_1099_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-aaa493_3_1099-9">[9]</a></sup> Age estimates range from 4.5–5.7 billion years (Gyr)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apj687_2_1264_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-apj687_2_1264-10">[10]</a></sup> up to 7.1 Gyr<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa501_3_941_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-aaa501_3_941-13">[13]</a></sup> or 9.48 Gyr.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apjss168_2_297_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-apjss168_2_297-7">[7]</a></sup> Compared to the Sun, it only has about 52%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metal_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-metal-14">[14]</a></sup> of the abundance of elements other than hydrogen and helium; what astronomers term the <a href="/wiki/Metallicity" title="Metallicity">metallicity</a> of a star.</p>
<p>This star system has a relatively large proper motion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIMBAD_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-SIMBAD-11">[11]</a></sup> The HR 4523 system is presently located within the <a href="/wiki/Epsilon_Indi" title="Epsilon Indi">Epsilon Indi</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stellar_association" title="Stellar association">Moving Group</a>, although it gives itself away as an interloper, since the star is older and has a different composition than the group members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa68_1_27_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-aaa68_1_27-15">[15]</a></sup> It has <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_velocity_(astronomy)" title="Space velocity (astronomy)">space velocity</a> components <span class="nowrap">[<i>U</i>, <i>V</i>, <i>W</i>]</span> = <span class="nowrap">[−67, −40, +4] km/s</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gliese1969_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-gliese1969-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Planetary_system">Planetary system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HD_102365&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Planetary system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The primary star is believed to be orbited by a Neptune-like planet with a mass 16 times that of the Earth. The orbital period of this planet is 122.1 days. No other planets have been discovered orbiting this star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apj727_103_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-apj727_103-17">[17]</a></sup> An examination of this system in the <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> did not reveal an <a href="/wiki/Infrared_excess" title="Infrared excess">excess emission</a> that would otherwise suggest the presence of a circumstellar <a href="/wiki/Debris_disk" title="Debris disk">debris disk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apj652_2_1674_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-apj652_2_1674-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
