<p><i><b>Hail to the King</b></i> is the sixth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by American <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Avenged_Sevenfold" title="Avenged Sevenfold">Avenged Sevenfold</a>. It was released on August 23, 2013 in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, and was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on August 27, 2013 and in North America on the same day. The album was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Elizondo" title="Mike Elizondo">Mike Elizondo</a>. <i>Hail to the King</i> is the first and only Avenged Sevenfold album to feature Arin Ilejay on drums, prior to his departure in July 2015. It is also the first Avenged Sevenfold album without musical contributions from Ilejay's late predecessor, <a href="/wiki/The_Rev" title="The Rev">Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan</a>; the bonus track, "St. James", was written in his memory.</p>
<p>The album was a commercial and critical success, reaching number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. This marks Avenged Sevenfold's second album to top the <i>Billboard</i> chart (the first being <i><a href="/wiki/Nightmare_(Avenged_Sevenfold_album)" title="Nightmare (Avenged Sevenfold album)">Nightmare</a></i>), as well as their first to reach number 1 in the UK, topping the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>. It also topped the Canadian, Brazilian, Finnish and Irish album charts. Also, the album spawned two number 1 singles on Billboard's Mainstream Rock, Hail to the King, and Shepherd of Fire. The former spent 11 weeks on the top of the chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On July 16, 2014 the album was certified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_record" title="Gold record">Gold</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> for sales of over 500,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hail_to_the_King_(Avenged_Sevenfold_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 15, 2012, vocalist <a href="/wiki/M._Shadows" title="M. Shadows">M. Shadows</a> said that the band has been working on a new album since the recording of "<a href="/wiki/Carry_On_(Avenged_Sevenfold_song)" title="Carry On (Avenged Sevenfold song)">Carry On</a>" in August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In December, the band said that it plans to begin recording material for their next album in January 2013, with release planned for later in the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In January 2013 the band started recording their new album. The band started streaming snippets of their new album in May 2013 on their new radio app.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was on the app that Arin Ilejay was confirmed as an official band member and replacement of deceased drummer and founding member <a href="/wiki/The_Rev" title="The Rev">The Rev</a>. In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Metal_Hammer" title="Metal Hammer">Metal Hammer</a></i> about the new album, M. Shadows said that the album would sound more <a href="/wiki/Blues_rock" title="Blues rock">blues rock</a>-influenced and more like classic rock and classic metal in the vein of <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath</a> and <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band released four teasers on YouTube showing the band going through the recording process as well as providing snippets of songs off the album. On June 26, 2013, the band revealed the title, cover art and release date of album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In July, the band revealed the complete track list of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 8, 2013, "Shepherd of Fire" was revealed to be the theme song of the <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Black_Ops_II" title="Call of Duty: Black Ops II">Call of Duty: Black Ops II</a></i> Zombies map "Origins", included in its final <a href="/wiki/Downloadable_content" title="Downloadable content">downloadable content</a> pack titled <i>Apocalypse</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hail_to_the_King_(Avenged_Sevenfold_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Synyster_Gates" title="Synyster Gates">Synyster Gates</a> stated of the album: "I think our songwriting has improved by leaps and bounds. Sonically, this record is our biggest record by fucking far. When you turn this fucking thing on, it blasts your fucking head off."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/M._Shadows" title="M. Shadows">M. Shadows</a> stated about the album's music: "On this record, we want a very bare bones, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riff" title="Riff">riff</a>-oriented approach. Because it's really easy for us to say 'That melody would sound great here, throw this background vocal here, here's this harmony.' We had to restrain ourselves from doing that just to keep it more badass and just more straightforward <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Zacky_Vengeance" title="Zacky Vengeance">Zacky Vengeance</a> stated: "We styled everything back and went to the core of <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> instead of trying to overcomplicate it. We wrote and wrote, and felt we really achieved what we were going for ... we're really proud of what we've come up with." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Christ" title="Johnny Christ">Johnny Christ</a> stated about the album's musical style in an interview with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bravewords" title="Bravewords">bravewords</a>: "It's definitely us. It's just the next progression. It's probably the heaviest record that we've put out - in its own right. It's been a great response for the single (<a href="/wiki/Hail_to_the_King_(song)" title="Hail to the King (song)">Hail to the King</a>) so far, which I feel is a good way to see what the rest of the record is going to sound like. There will be some surprises in there for fans, but overall, I'm just excited." He also said the album is more <a href="/wiki/Groove_metal" title="Groove metal">groove metal</a>-oriented.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The album also shows a thrash metal sound, with fast to mid-paced tempos, heavily distorted guitars and war, religious intolerance or corruption themed lyrics.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hail_to_the_King_(Avenged_Sevenfold_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 15, 2013 the album was made available for pre-order and the title track, "<a href="/wiki/Hail_to_the_King_(song)" title="Hail to the King (song)">Hail to the King</a>", was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The same day, the band revealed a Limited Edition of <i>Hail to the King</i> for <a href="/wiki/Pre-order" title="Pre-order">pre-order</a> available on their website, much as they did with their previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/Nightmare_(Avenged_Sevenfold_album)" title="Nightmare (Avenged Sevenfold album)">Nightmare</a></i>. The limited edition set included:</p>
<ul>
<li>Limited Edition “Treasure Box”: Antique gold colored embossed foil paper wrapped box featuring exclusive <i>Hail To The King</i> artwork</li>
<li>Deluxe <i>Hail To The King</i> album on CD: Housed in a digi-pak with a card including a download to an extra bonus track</li>
<li>Exclusive Canvas Print: 11.75″ × 11.75″ Canvas print of the Album Cover artwork, printed and rolled up to fit inside the treasure box</li>
<li>Photo Book: 11.5″ × 7.125″ 20-page + cover perfect-bound soft-cover book featuring a sampling of photos of the band throughout the years</li>
<li>Death Bat Challenge Coin: 2″ silver colored metal coin with a design stamped on front &amp; back</li>

<p><i>Hail to the King</i> received positive reviews upon release. At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalized</a> rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream music critics, the album received an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">average</a> score of 70, based on 9 reviews, which indicates "generally favorable" reviews. In a summary of his review, Chris Epting of <a href="/wiki/Loudwire" title="Loudwire">Loudwire</a> called the album "a modern metal classic that reflects the past while very much embracing the present".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LW_-_review_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-LW_-_review-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Artistdirect" title="Artistdirect">Artistdirect</a>'s Rick Florino stated, "Hail to the King doesn't just raise the bar for the band, but it raises the bar for everyone else to follow and compete with." In a very positive review, Dom Lawson of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> judged, "Stripped down to a core of thudding Sabbath-like grooves and brash, spiky vocal refrains, Avenged now sound every bit as vital and imperious as the bands they aspire to emulate."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_-_review_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian_-_review-27">[27]</a></sup> "<i>Hail to the King</i> represents a clean sweep, a divergence into classic metal and their best chance of hitting the heights they've always longed for", according to <i><a href="/wiki/Q_(magazine)" title="Q (magazine)">Q magazine</a></i>. "The next giant crossover metal band has arrived", reviewer Tom Bryant concluded, giving the album a 4 stars out of 5 rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-q_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-q-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Not all critics were as enthusiastic. In a mixed review, Jason Lymangrover of <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> remarked: "Unfortunately, once they tried to take inspiration from other bands, they mimicked them so well that they lost their sense of identity in the process". He also accused "This Means War" of ripping off <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Sad_but_True" title="Sad but True">Sad but True</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-24">[24]</a></sup> In a more negative review, Bradley Zorgdrager of <i><a href="/wiki/Exclaim!" title="Exclaim!">Exclaim!</a></i> said: "After it concludes, <i>Hail to the King</i> makes it challenging for listeners to recall any specific moment, which defeats the purpose of a pop (metal) album". The album also has been compared to bands like <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AEaston_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-AEaston-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a blog post, <a href="/wiki/Machine_Head_(band)" title="Machine Head (band)">Machine Head</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/Robb_Flynn" title="Robb Flynn">Robb Flynn</a> was also very critical of the album, denouncing it as a mere "cover album" and making several jokes about the similarities a selection of songs share with those by <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> M. Shadows responded to the criticism saying that he "read it as a joke", but noted that "if it is a joke, it was kind of overboard".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hail_to_the_King_(Avenged_Sevenfold_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Hail to the King</i> debuted at number one on the UK Albums Chart on September 1, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_chart_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-UK_chart-38">[38]</a></sup> It also debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/The_Official_Finnish_Charts" title="The Official Finnish Charts">Official Finnish Albums Chart</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIN_chart_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIN_chart-39">[39]</a></sup> and at number five in Germany.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The album sold 159,000 copies in the United States in its first week of release to debut at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
