<p><i><b>Hannah Montana Forever</b></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack_album" title="Soundtrack album">soundtrack album</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana_(season_4)" title="Hannah Montana (season 4)">fourth and final season</a> of the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i>, released on October 15, 2010 by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records" title="Walt Disney Records">Walt Disney Records</a>. All eleven tracks are performed by its primary actress <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>, and are credited to her character <a href="/wiki/Miley_Stewart#Hannah_Montana" title="Miley Stewart">Hannah Montana</a>. Recording artists <a href="/wiki/Billy_Ray_Cyrus" title="Billy Ray Cyrus">Billy Ray Cyrus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iyaz" title="Iyaz">Iyaz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a> appear as featured vocalists. The soundtrack is primarily a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> record, which sees additional influences from <a href="/wiki/Teen_pop" title="Teen pop">teen pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Power_pop" title="Power pop">power pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Country_pop" title="Country pop">country pop</a> musical styles.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> released from the album is "<a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Girl_(Hannah_Montana_song)" title="Ordinary Girl (Hannah Montana song)">Ordinary Girl</a>", which charted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at number ninety-one. "Are You Ready" and "<a href="/wiki/Gonna_Get_This" title="Gonna Get This">Gonna Get This</a>" were both released as <a href="/wiki/Promotional_recording" title="Promotional recording">promotional singles</a> from <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i>. The latter also managed to peak at number 66 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. The album was also promoted via exclusive releases to <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_composition">Background and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hannah_Montana_Forever&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Miley_Cyrus_-_Gypsy_Heart_Tour_-_Pasay.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="878" height="233" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Miley_Cyrus_-_Gypsy_Heart_Tour_-_Pasay.jpg/200px-Miley_Cyrus_-_Gypsy_Heart_Tour_-_Pasay.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Miley_Cyrus_-_Gypsy_Heart_Tour_-_Pasay.jpg/300px-Miley_Cyrus_-_Gypsy_Heart_Tour_-_Pasay.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Miley_Cyrus_-_Gypsy_Heart_Tour_-_Pasay.jpg/400px-Miley_Cyrus_-_Gypsy_Heart_Tour_-_Pasay.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

<p>The opening track "Gonna Get This", a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duet_(music)" title="Duet (music)">duet</a> with British Virgin Islander <a href="/wiki/Iyaz" title="Iyaz">Iyaz</a>, was written by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Niclas_Molinder" title="Niclas Molinder">Niclas Molinder</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joacim_Persson" title="Joacim Persson">Joacim Persson</a>, Johan Alkenas, and Drew Ryan Scott. Iyaz claimed he listened to Cyrus' music very often and that <i>Hannah Montana</i> was among his favorite television series with others from <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> "I've been watching the show for a very long time, and finally got the opportunity to do a song with Miley", he said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboardiyaz_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboardiyaz-2">[2]</a></sup> "Gonna Get This" was recorded in April 2010 rapidly, according to Iyaz,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-metro-3">[3]</a></sup> because of his appearance on an episode of <i>Hannah Montana</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At first, he arrived to the studio not knowing what to expect and became hesitant but in the end could not believe they "pulled it off".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-metro-3">[3]</a></sup> Iyaz described "Gonna Get This" as a track with an edge, claiming Cyrus added her usual signature style while he put an "island twist" on it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboardiyaz_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-billboardiyaz-2">[2]</a></sup> The track leaked into the Internet in July 2010, entitled "This Boy, That Girl".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-metro-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Toby_Gad" title="Toby Gad">Toby Gad</a> contributed to the soundtrack by co-writing "<a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Girl_(Hannah_Montana_song)" title="Ordinary Girl (Hannah Montana song)">Ordinary Girl</a>", "Que Será", and "Are You Ready".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> "Are You Ready" was written by Gad, <a href="/wiki/BC_Jean" title="BC Jean">BC Jean</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lyrica_Anderson" title="Lyrica Anderson">Lyrica Anderson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The song was recorded for <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana_3" title="Hannah Montana 3">Hannah Montana 3</a></i> and leaked on the Internet in November 2008, along with tracks from <i>Hannah Montana 3</i>, yet, was not included in the soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A duet with <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a>, guest star on the episode "It's the End of Jake as We Know It", titled "Need a Little Love" was solely penned by Jaime Houston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> "Love That Lets Go" is a duet with <a href="/wiki/Billy_Ray_Cyrus" title="Billy Ray Cyrus">Billy Ray Cyrus</a> (who portrays Miley's father, Robbie Ray Stewart, on <i>Hannah Montana</i> and is Cyrus' real father) was composed by Adam Anders and Nikki Hassman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> "I'm Still Good" and "Been Here All Along" were written by Jennie Lurie, Aris Archontis, and Chen Neeman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Walt Disney Records, <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> covers much musical ground, from intimate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballad_(music)" title="Ballad (music)">ballads</a> to direct high-voltage pop. "Gonna Get This" is heavy on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synthesizers" title="Synthesizers">synthesizers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caribbean_music" title="Caribbean music">Caribbean-based melodies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pressrelease_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-pressrelease-11">[11]</a></sup> Musically, the ballad "Ordinary Girl" is a pop song characterized by its mature vocals and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percussion" title="Percussion">percussion</a>-driven <a href="/wiki/Instrumentation" title="Instrumentation">instrumentation</a>. Lyrically, "Ordinary Girl" attempts to convince audiences that despite the fame, she is just an everyday person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> "Kiss It Goodbye" is an upbeat <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> number and "I'll Always Remember You" lyrically speaks about the sentiments that occur after a wistful farewell. "Need a Little Love" is rich in pop <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harmony_(music)" title="Harmony (music)">harmonies</a>. "Are You Ready"'s upbeat sound was inspired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_music" title="Club music">club music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pressrelease_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-pressrelease-11">[11]</a></sup> The ballad "Love That Lets Go" is a tribute to Cyrus' and her father's, who duets in the track, close relationship, both on screen and off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pressrelease_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-pressrelease-11">[11]</a></sup> "I'm Still Good", an uptempo track with pop rock characteristics in its musical composition, and "Been Here All Along", a ballad about loving someone separated by distance, follow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pressrelease_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-pressrelease-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The closing track on <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i>, "Barefoot Cinderella" was described as an explosion of pop music by Walt Disney Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pressrelease_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-pressrelease-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hannah_Montana_Forever&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Girl_(Hannah_Montana_song)" title="Ordinary Girl (Hannah Montana song)">Ordinary Girl</a>" was released as the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i>. It premiered on Radio Disney on July 2, 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aolradio_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-aolradio-14">[14]</a></sup> and was released on July 6, 2010, to all digital outlets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On October 15, 2010, the song was released as a <a href="/wiki/CD_single" title="CD single">CD single</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The song peaked at number ninety-one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> An accompanying <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "Ordinary Girl" does not feature Cyrus and follows an adolescent female after she is gifted a digital camera by Hannah Montana (portrayed by a <a href="/wiki/Body_double" title="Body double">body double</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>"I'm Still Good" was released as the official second single from <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i>. It premiered on August 13, 2010, to <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a>, and was later released as a CD single on December 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It has failed to chart in any country. A music video of Miley Cyrus as Hannah Montana singing the song live onstage was released on August 15, 2010. The video is featured on the <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana_(season_4)" title="Hannah Montana (season 4)">Hannah Montana season 4</a> episode "Been Here All Along".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hannah_Montana_Forever&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Are You Ready" was the first song to premiere on <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a> on May 14, 2010. The song was later released as the first <a href="/wiki/Promotional_recording" title="Promotional recording">promotional single</a> from <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> on July 1, 2010, exclusively through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wal-Mart" title="Wal-Mart">Wal-Mart</a>'s digital retailer. On September 3, 2010, "Are You Ready" was released to digital retailers in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Subsequently, "I'm Still Good" and "Que Será" received premieres on Radio Disney on August 13, 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aolradio_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-aolradio-14">[14]</a></sup> and on September 3, 2010, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The former was given a promotional music video, taken from a performance from the episode "Been Here All Along". "Que Sera" received a music video that features a body double of Cyrus at a recording studio while images rapidly show to portray the sequence of a teenage male attempting to redeem a relationship with a teenage female.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The song "Gonna Get This" premiered on Radio Disney and the video on Disney Channel on September 24, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> It was released as the second promotional single from <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> on October 5, 2010, exclusively by the United States <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> had its broadcast debut on October 16 with a rebroadcast on the two following days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pressrelease_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-pressrelease-11">[11]</a></sup> The song "Wherever I Go" was added to the station after the series finale of <i><a href="/wiki/Wherever_I_Go_(Hannah_Montana)" title="Wherever I Go (Hannah Montana)">Hannah Montana</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Wherever I Go was added as the Bonus Track on the US iTunes store, however this version of the song is not the duet heard on the show with Emily Osment, it is just Hannah Montana. The version of Wherever I Go featuring Emily Osment can be found online, yet it is unavailable for purchase.</p>

<p><i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> has received generally mixed reviews. Heather Phares of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> gave the album 2.5 out of 5 stars and stated, "<i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> doesn’t shy away from the fact that this is the end, or that Cyrus and her fans are growing up. Instead, these songs attempt to showcase the growing range of music Cyrus and her audience are listening to, but the results are all over the place... the album sounds scattered instead of versatile." She went on to criticize "Que Sera" and "Kiss It Goodbye"'s annoying vocals, though she praised "Ordinary Girl", "I'll Always Remember You", "Love That Lets Go", and "Been Here All Along", commenting, "Not surprisingly, the album’s best songs are about farewells and embracing whatever may come next." She finished off the review by saying, "By the time the album closes with 'Barefoot Cinderella,' an easygoing pop track that sounds remarkably like Cyrus' hit '<a href="/wiki/Party_in_the_U.S.A." title="Party in the U.S.A.">Party in the U.S.A.</a>,' <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> proves that everyone involved with Hannah was ready to move on."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusicreview_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicreview-27">[27]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> was more gracious, awarding the album a B+ and saying, "Miley Cyrus struck out with this summer's whiny <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Be_Tamed" title="Can't Be Tamed">Can't Be Tamed</a></i>, but she's in finer form on <i>Hannah Montana Forever</i>, the soundtrack to her TV show's final season. Credit the would-be film star's relief at finally hanging up Hannah's blond wig; there's no mistaking the glee with which she sings <i>I'm free as a bird</i> on <i>Que Sera.</i> Even <i>Love That Lets Go,</i> an acoustic duet with her dad Billy Ray, feels more sweet than sappy." They also stated that "I'm Still Good" and "Need a Little Love" were the best songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-ew-28">[28]</a></sup> Common Sense Media gave the album 4 out of 5 stars, commenting "The music from the <i>Hannah Montana Forever Soundtrack</i> is a nice mix of pop-rock, synth beats, and sweet ballads that Hannah Montana is known for, as well as a satisfying wrap to four seasons of fun, friends, family, and music."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csmreview_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-csmreview-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hannah_Montana_Forever&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Hannah Montana Forever</i> debuted at no. 11 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB-HMF_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-BB-HMF-30">[30]</a></sup> This is an all-time low for the artist, as no previous album of new material released by Cyrus has failed to reach the top ten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new.music.yahoo.com_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-new.music.yahoo.com-31">[31]</a></sup> It also the first album in the <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a> franchise to miss the top ten since 2008's <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a> exclusive <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hits_Remixed" title="Hits Remixed">Hits Remixed</a></i>. First week sales for the album were projected to be around 28,000, which is over 109,000 less than the previous year's <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana_3" title="Hannah Montana 3">Hannah Montana 3</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
