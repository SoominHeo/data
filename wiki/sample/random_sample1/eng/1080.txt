<p><i><b>Heart Attack</b></i> is the third <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">mini-album</a> by South Korean <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AOA_(band)" title="AOA (band)">AOA</a>, released on June 22, 2015 by <a href="/wiki/FNC_Entertainment" title="FNC Entertainment">FNC Entertainment</a>. The song of the same name was used as the lead single for the album.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_Attack_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song "Heart Attack" was written and composed by <a href="/wiki/Brave_Brothers" title="Brave Brothers">Brave Brothers</a>.</p>
<p>The first music video teaser was released on June 14, 2015 with the second teaser released on June 16, 2015. AOA are dressed as <a href="/wiki/Lacrosse" title="Lacrosse">lacrosse</a> players in their teasers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>AOA started their Korean promotions with a showcase on June 22 at AX Korea. The promotion of "Heart Attack" began June 25 on the show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i>. The song was also promoted on the shows, <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Show!_Music_Core" title="Show! Music Core">Music Core</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">Inkigayo</a></i>.</p>
<p>They won their first music show for 'Heart Attack' on July 1, 2015 on <a href="/wiki/Show_Champion" title="Show Champion">Show Champion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_and_Chinese_version">Japanese and Chinese version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_Attack_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Japanese and Chinese version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 17, 2015 was revealed that AOA will release a Japanese and Chinese version of "Heart Attack". "Heart Attack" was released in Chinese on July 31, 2015 through <a href="/wiki/Baidu" title="Baidu">Baidu</a> Music, <a href="/wiki/Sina_Corp" title="Sina Corp">Sina</a> Music, QQ Music and Taiwan's KKBOX.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Japanese version was released in Japan as a single on July 29, 2015 and AOA filmed a separate music video for it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The single was released in eleven different versions: two limited CD+DVD editions, two limited CD+Photobook editions and seven limited CD only editions, one edition per member.</p>
