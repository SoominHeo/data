<p><i><b>Heartbreaker</b></i> is the solo debut album of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Artist" title="Artist">artist</a> <a href="/wiki/G-Dragon" title="G-Dragon">G-Dragon</a>, member and leader of <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(South_Korean_band)" title="Big Bang (South Korean band)">Big Bang</a>. The lead single "<a href="/wiki/Heartbreaker_(G-Dragon_song)" title="Heartbreaker (G-Dragon song)">Heartbreaker</a>", was a hit, topping various charts upon release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heartbreaker_top_chart_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heartbreaker_top_chart-2">[2]</a></sup> The album is the best-selling by a South Korean solo artist since its release.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Information">Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heartbreaker_(G-Dragon_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although initially scheduled to be released in April, the released date was pushed back to August<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> to coincide with his 21st birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> G-Dragon collaborated with several artists, including <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Park" title="Teddy Park">Teddy Park</a> of <a href="/wiki/1TYM" title="1TYM">1TYM</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tae_Yang" title="Tae Yang">Tae Yang</a> of G-Dragon's own <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(South_Korean_band)" title="Big Bang (South Korean band)">group</a>, and CL and Dara of <a href="/wiki/2NE1" title="2NE1">2NE1</a>. His song "The Leaders" (which featured Teddy and CL) also referenced <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Gee_(Girls%27_Generation_song)" title="Gee (Girls' Generation song)">Gee</a>", <a href="/wiki/SS501" title="SS501">SS501</a>'s Kim Kyu Jong's "Wuss Up", as well as 2NE1's "I Don't Care." Additionally, the song "Sonyeoniya" featured the thoughts and struggles he had to go through as a young boy who started as a YG trainee at the age of 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The lead single "<a href="/wiki/Heartbreaker_(G-Dragon_song)" title="Heartbreaker (G-Dragon song)">Heartbreaker</a>" has reached number-one on various charts, including the Mnet and Melon Music chart, while "Breathe" was ranked in the Top 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heartbreaker_top_chart_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Heartbreaker_top_chart-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heartbreaker_(G-Dragon_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>G-Dragon was accused of plagiarism by Sony Music, as his tracks "Heartbreaker" and "Butterfly" are similar to <a href="/wiki/Flo_Rida" title="Flo Rida">Flo Rida's</a> "<a href="/wiki/Right_Round" title="Right Round">Right Round</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Oasis_(band)" title="Oasis (band)">Oasis's</a> "She's Electric", respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plagiarism_overview_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-plagiarism_overview-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>1, who also has rights to "Right Round" said that they saw no similarities between these two songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Sony Korea has denied G-Dragon the ability to promote the track any further without paying reparations. On September 21, 2009, <i>Sisa Magazine</i>, a news program on MBC, stated that officials from Sony reportedly sent YG Entertainment warning letters about plagiarism. Sony's legal representative stated, "It was a hard decision for the music critics." However, they decided that there were similarities and accordingly issued a warning letter to the production company and composers. They have also denied YG Entertainment the permission to promote these songs. However, <a href="/wiki/YG_Entertainment" title="YG Entertainment">YG Entertainment</a> claims that nothing has been decided or legally determined and that the "letter" which YG received from Sony is not considered legal action; the company has also stated that they have not received a direct response from the American publishers regarding this matter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On March 6, 2010, YG Entertainment announced that they personally contacted Flo Rida's representatives and Flo Rida is going to appear as a featuring artist in a new version of Heartbreaker, to be released as a bonus track on G-Dragon's Live Concert Album. Then on May 22 G-Dragon appeared as a special guest at Flo Rida's Korea concert and performed the new version of Heartbreaker featuring Flo Rida.</p>
