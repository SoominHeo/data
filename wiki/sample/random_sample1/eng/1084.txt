<p>"<b>Hillbilly Shoes</b>" is a song recorded by American country music duo <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_Gentry" title="Montgomery Gentry">Montgomery Gentry</a>. It was released in February 1999 as their debut single and the lead-off single to their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Tattoos_%26_Scars" title="Tattoos &amp; Scars">Tattoos &amp; Scars</a></i>. It peaked at number 13 in the United States, and number 7 in Canada. It was written by Bobby Taylor, Mike Geiger and Woody Mullis.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hillbilly_Shoes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Deborah Evans Price, of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine reviewed the song favorably, calling it a "high-energy brand of traditional country blended with loads of outlaw attitude." She goes on to say that the song has an "absolutely infectious guitar riff, sassy sawing fiddles, and Gentry's high-octane lead vocal."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hillbilly_Shoes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video was directed by Chris Rogers and premiered in early 1999.</p>
