<p>"<b>Honey, This Mirror Isn't Big Enough for the Two of Us</b>" is the second single released by the band <a href="/wiki/My_Chemical_Romance" title="My Chemical Romance">My Chemical Romance</a> from their debut <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Brought_You_My_Bullets,_You_Brought_Me_Your_Love" title="I Brought You My Bullets, You Brought Me Your Love">I Brought You My Bullets, You Brought Me Your Love</a></i> (and second overall single). It is the second <a href="/wiki/Track_(CD)" title="Track (CD)">track</a> on the album. The song peaked at #182 in the UK.</p>
<p>The song deals with drug use, reflecting on vocalist <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Way" title="Gerard Way">Gerard Way</a>'s past problems with alcohol.</p>

<p>The music video was first shown at the record release party of <i><a href="/wiki/I_Brought_You_My_Bullets,_You_Brought_Me_Your_Love" title="I Brought You My Bullets, You Brought Me Your Love">I Brought You My Bullets, You Brought Me Your Love</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/2002_in_music" title="2002 in music">2002</a>, and was available online. It was not widely released until September 23, 2005 on the 2005 re-release of <i>I Brought You My Bullets, You Brought Me Your Love</i>. Scenes featuring the band performing were directed by Marc Debiak for the production company Grey Sky Films. The interspersed scenes containing a storyline similar to the <a href="/wiki/Takashi_Miike" title="Takashi Miike">Takashi Miike</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Audition_(1999_film)" title="Audition (1999 film)">Audition</a></i> were directed by John Armson. It is about a man looking for a spouse and after a series of interviews, he finds a woman. After their first date he tries to find where she lives and apparently cannot find her address. When he returns to his home, he pours himself a drink and after drinking he collapses on the floor. The woman comes out of a doorway. She then proceeds to torture the paralyzed man, putting needles in his chest and below his eye and chopping off his leg.</p>
