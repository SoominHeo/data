<p>The <b>IAR 330</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a>-built version of <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale" title="Aérospatiale">Aérospatiale</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale_Puma" title="Aérospatiale Puma">SA 330 Puma</a> <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a>, manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Industria_Aeronautic%C4%83_Rom%C3%A2n%C4%83" title="Industria Aeronautică Română">IAR Brașov</a>. Twenty-four helicopters were upgraded to IAR 330 SOCAT in cooperation with <a href="/wiki/Elbit_Systems" title="Elbit Systems">Elbit Systems</a> (<a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_development">Design and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAR_330&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAR_330&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite being a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/COMECON" title="COMECON">COMECON</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Pact" title="Warsaw Pact">Warsaw Pact</a> member, Romania strove for some measure of independence from the USSR. Instead of buying popular Soviet designs, Romania bought a licence to manufacture the French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale_Puma" title="Aérospatiale Puma">Aérospatiale SA 330 Puma</a> on 30 July 1974. The first licensed helicopter was flown on 22 October 1975 under the designation IAR-330L.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marnix_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-marnix-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>At least 163 of these helicopters have been built, out of which 104 were assigned to Romania's military, 2 were retained by the manufacturer and 57 were produced for export<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marnix_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-marnix-1">[1]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>). There is also a search and rescue (SAR) version, fitted with inflatable floats for emergency landing at sea, which has been built in a small batch. Production is still ongoing at the <a href="/wiki/Industria_Aeronautic%C4%83_Rom%C3%A2n%C4%83" title="Industria Aeronautică Română">Industria Aeronautică Română</a> plant at <a href="/wiki/Ghimbav" title="Ghimbav">Ghimbav</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bra%C5%9Fov" title="Braşov">Brașov</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SOCAT_version">SOCAT version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAR_330&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: SOCAT version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 1990s, the Romanian Air Force decided to enhance the combat capabilities of the IAR 330L, making it a <a href="/wiki/Gunship" title="Gunship">universal anti-tank and support helicopter</a>. At that time, Romania started to cooperate with Israel on some military programs. The Israeli company <a href="/wiki/Elbit_Systems" title="Elbit Systems">Elbit Systems</a> was chosen, and in September 1995 the Romanian Air Force signed a contract to upgrade 24 helicopters with the SOCAT system (<i>Sistem Optronic de Cercetare și Anti-Tanc</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marnix_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-marnix-1">[1]</a></sup> The first IAR 330L SOCAT was flown on 26 May 1998, from IAR's airfield in <a href="/wiki/Ghimbav" title="Ghimbav">Ghimbav</a>, near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bra%C5%9Fov" title="Braşov">Brașov</a>. On 23 October 1999, the second prototype flew. The first IAR 330L SOCAT was delivered to a combat unit in 2001. In all, 25 SOCATs were made, including the prototype, rebuilt to production standard in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marnix_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-marnix-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The IAR 330M NATO is a modernized transport version with the SOCAT version's avionics, but without weapons and <a href="/wiki/Optoelectronics" title="Optoelectronics">optronic</a> systems. Among others, it has a weather radar. Twelve IAR 330Ls were modernized to 330M standard between 2005 and 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marnix_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-marnix-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Naval_version">Naval version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAR_330&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Naval version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first IAR 330 NAVAL helicopter was officially unveiled at Ghimbav on 30 January 2007. The <a href="/wiki/Romanian_Naval_Forces" title="Romanian Naval Forces">Romanian Naval Forces</a> ordered three of this variant. The helicopter is in a similar configuration to the Romanian Air Force variant, including the SOCAT upgrade package; the Navy aircraft also have flotation gear fitted under the nose and main undercarriage fairings. They are operated from Navy frigates for <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medevac" title="Medevac">medevac</a> and maritime surveillance missions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_incidents">Notable incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAR_330&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notable incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>An IAR 330 was presumably shot at and crash-landed on December 23, 1989 during a transport flight near <a href="/wiki/Alba_Iulia" title="Alba Iulia">Alba Iulia</a>, killing the crew of three and two passengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>An IAR 330 SOCAT crashed on August 16, 2001 during a training flight shortly after take-off from <a href="/wiki/List_of_Romanian_Air_Force_bases#Closed" title="List of Romanian Air Force bases">Titu Air Base</a>. The crash occurred at an altitude of 50 meters (165 ft.), wounding the crew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marnix_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-marnix-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>An IAR 330 SOCAT helicopter, belonging to the <a href="/wiki/RoAF_90th_Airlift_Base" title="RoAF 90th Airlift Base">90th Airlift Base</a>, crashed on November 7, 2007, in <a href="/wiki/Ungheni,_Arge%C8%99" title="Ungheni, Argeș">Ungheni</a>, 30 km south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pite%C5%9Fti" title="Piteşti">Pitești</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arge%C5%9F_County" title="Argeş County">Argeș County</a>, during a night training mission, killing all three crew members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>An IAR 330 SOCAT crashed on March 7, 2013 in Berești-Bistrița, near <a href="/wiki/Bac%C4%83u" title="Bacău">Bacău</a>, during a training flight, killing two crew members and wounding three other crew members. Romanian Air Force and Navy grounded the entire IAR 330 SOCAT fleet pending an investigation into the crash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>An IAR-330 MEDEVAC crashed on November 21, 2014 in Mălâncrav, near <a href="/wiki/Sibiu" title="Sibiu">Sibiu</a>, during a training flight, killing eight military personnel and injuring two others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size: 90%; margin:0;"><i>Data from</i> Jane's All The World's Aircraft 1992–1993<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jawa92_p193_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-jawa92_p193-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Crew:</b> 1–3</li>
<li><b>Capacity:</b> 16 fully equipped troops</li>
<li><b>Length:</b> 18.15 m (59 ft 7 in) (overall), 14.06 m (46 ft 2 in)</li>
<li><b>Height:</b> 5.14 m (16 ft 10 in)</li>
<li><b>Empty weight:</b> 3,615 kg (7,970 lb)</li>
<li><b>Max takeoff weight:</b> 7,400 kg (16,314 lb)</li>
<li><b>Powerplant:</b> 2 × <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turbom%C3%A9ca_Turmo" title="Turboméca Turmo">Turboméca Turmo</a> IVC <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turboshaft_engine" title="Turboshaft engine">turboshaft engines</a>, 1,175 kW (1,576 hp) each</li>
<li><b>Main rotor diameter:</b> 15.00 m (49 ft 3 in)</li>
<li><b>Main rotor area:</b> 176.7 m<sup>2</sup> (1,902 sq ft)</li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Performance</b></span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Cruising speed:</b> 271 km/h (168 mph; 146 kn) (max cruise)</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/V_speeds" title="V speeds">Never exceed speed</a>:</b> 294 km/h (183 mph; 159 kn)</li>
<li><b>Range:</b> 572 km (355 mi; 309 nmi)</li>
<li><b>Service ceiling:</b> 6,000 m (19,685 ft)</li>
<li><b>Rate of climb:</b> 9.2 m/s (1,810 ft/min)</li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Armament</b></span><br/></p>
<ul>
<li>2 x 23mm single barrel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NR-23" title="NR-23">NR-23</a> <a href="/wiki/Gun_pod" title="Gun pod">gun pods</a> (on the sides of the nose) with 400 rounds each (optional)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roaf_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-roaf-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/S-5_rocket#Typical_launchers_specifications" title="S-5 rocket">LPR 57</a> unguided <a href="/wiki/Rocket_launcher" title="Rocket launcher">rocket launcher</a> (optional, on 4 hardpoints)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roaf_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-roaf-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>2 x 7.62 mm caliber door mounted <a href="/wiki/Machine_gun" title="Machine gun">machine guns</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roaf_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-roaf-15">[15]</a></sup> (usually only one mounted on the starboard side)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AT-3_Sagger" title="AT-3 Sagger">Malyutka</a> wire guided <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ATGM" title="ATGM">ATGM</a> (4 rails above the hardpoints, used only on trials)</li>
<li>50 or 100 kg bombs (4 hardpoints, used only on trials)</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Puma SOCAT</b><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nexter" title="Nexter">Nexter</a> <a href="/wiki/M621_cannon" title="M621 cannon">THL-20 20mm</a> <a href="/wiki/Chain_gun" title="Chain gun">chain gun</a> (chin mounted) with 750 rounds</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/S-5_rocket#Typical_launchers_specifications" title="S-5 rocket">LPR 57</a> unguided <a href="/wiki/Rocket_launcher" title="Rocket launcher">rocket launcher</a> (inner 2 hardpoints)</li>
<li>8 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RAFAEL_Armament_Development_Authority" title="RAFAEL Armament Development Authority">RAFAEL</a> <a href="/wiki/Spike_(missile)#Versions" title="Spike (missile)">Spike-ER</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-tank_guided_missile" title="Anti-tank guided missile">anti-tank guided missiles</a> (outer 2 hardpoints)</li>
<li>Nexter NC-621 20mm gun pod with 180 rounds (outer 2 hardpoints, used only on trials)</li>
