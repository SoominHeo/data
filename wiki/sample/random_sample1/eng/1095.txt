<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">International League of non-religious and atheists</h1>

<p>The <b>International League of Non-religious and Atheists</b> (German: <i>Internationaler Bund der Konfessionslosen und Atheisten</i>, <b>IBKA</b>) is an international association founded in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> in 1976 as "International League of Non-religious" (IBDK). It originated from the <i>MIZ magazine</i> and the Bund der Konfessionslosen Berlin (Union of the non-religious in Berlin) founded in 1972, and adopted the name IBKA in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It has approximately 1000 members<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and 13 coorporative Members,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as well as a Scientific Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It is registered as an <a href="/wiki/Eingetragener_Verein" title="Eingetragener Verein">eingetragener Verein</a> (e.V.) in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>
<p>Among its objectives, IBKA promotes <a href="/wiki/Rationalism" title="Rationalism">rationalism</a> and pursues the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_freedom" title="Scientific freedom">scientific freedom</a>, as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Separation_of_Church_and_state" title="Separation of Church and state">separation of Church and State</a>. In 2014, it was amongst several secularist organisations to campaign for the right to <a href="/wiki/Euthanasia" title="Euthanasia">euthanasia</a> in Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
