<p><b>ICCV</b>, the <b>International Conference on Computer Vision</b>, is a research conference sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_and_Electronics_Engineers" title="Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers">Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers</a> (IEEE) held every other year. It is considered, together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CVPR" title="CVPR">CVPR</a>, the top level conference in computer vision,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> with a 'A*' rating from the Australian Ranking of ICT Conferences obtained in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The acceptance rate for ICCV has been around 25%-30%, with about 4% being accepted for oral presentations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Like many IEEE conferences, ICCV has tutorial talks, technical sessions, and poster sessions. The conference is usually spread over four to five days. Leading experts in the focus areas would give tutorial talks on the first day. Then the technical sessions (and poster sessions in parallel) would follow.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Computer_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Azriel_Rosenfeld_Lifetime_Achievement_Award">Azriel Rosenfeld Lifetime Achievement Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Computer_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Azriel Rosenfeld Lifetime Achievement Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Azriel_Rosenfeld_Award" title="Azriel Rosenfeld Award">Azriel Rosenfeld Award</a>, or Azriel Rosenfeld Lifetime Achievement Award, recognizes researchers who have made significant contributions to the field of computer vision over their careers. It is named in memory of computer scientist and mathematician <a href="/wiki/Azriel_Rosenfeld" title="Azriel Rosenfeld">Azriel Rosenfeld</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Recipients:</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Jan_Koenderink" title="Jan Koenderink">Jan Koenderink</a> (2013)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Huang" title="Thomas Huang">Thomas Huang</a> (2011)</li>
<li>Berthold K.P. Horn (2009)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Takeo_Kanade" title="Takeo Kanade">Takeo Kanade</a> (2007)</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Helmholtz_Prize">Helmholtz Prize</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Computer_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Helmholtz Prize">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The ICCV Helmholtz Prize, known as the Test of Time Award before 2013, is awarded every other year at the ICCV, recognizing ICCV papers from ten or more years earlier that had a significant impact on computer vision research.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-helmholtz_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-helmholtz-5">[5]</a></sup> Winners are selected by the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a>'s Technical Committee on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TCPAMI).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-helmholtz_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-helmholtz-5">[5]</a></sup> The award is named after the 19th century physician and physicist <a href="/wiki/Hermann_von_Helmholtz" title="Hermann von Helmholtz">Hermann von Helmholtz</a>, and the ICCV's award is not related to the various Helmholtz Prizes in physics, or the Hermann von Helmholtz Prize in neuroscience.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marr_Prize">Marr Prize</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Computer_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Marr Prize">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The best paper of the conference is awarded the <b><a href="/wiki/Marr_Prize" title="Marr Prize">Marr Prize</a></b>, which is one of the top honors for a researcher in computer vision.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mark_Everingham_Prize">Mark Everingham Prize</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Computer_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mark Everingham Prize">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Mark Everingham Prize</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> is an award given yearly by the Technical Committee on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TCPAMI) of the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a> at the IEEE International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV) or the <a href="/wiki/European_Conference_on_Computer_Vision" title="European Conference on Computer Vision">European Conference on Computer Vision</a> (ECCV) to commemorate the late Mark Everingham, “one of the rising stars of computer vision”,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and to encourage others to follow in his footsteps by acting to further progress in the computer vision community as a whole. The prize is given to a researcher, or a team of researchers, who have made a selfless contribution of significant benefit to other members of the computer vision community. The Mark Everingham Prize for Rigorous Evaluation was an award given in 2012 at the British Machine Vision Conference (BMVC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PAMI_Distinguished_Researcher_Award">PAMI Distinguished Researcher Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Computer_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: PAMI Distinguished Researcher Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This award<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> (until 2013 called Significant Researcher Award) is awarded to candidates whose research contributions have significantly contributed to the progress of Computer Vision. Awards are made based on major research contributions, as well as the role of those contributions in influencing and inspiring other research. Candidates are nominated by the community.</p>
