<p><br/>
<b>1729 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCXXIX</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">starting on Saturday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> B) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Wednesday" title="Common year starting on Wednesday">common year starting on Wednesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> E) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1729th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 729th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 29th year of the <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>, and the 10th and last year of the <a href="/wiki/1720s" title="1720s">1720s</a> decade. As of the start of 1729, the Gregorian calendar was 11 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1729&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1729&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_23" title="March 23">March 23</a> (evening) – Première performance of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Johann Sebastian Bach</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_K%C3%B6then_Funeral_music" title="First Köthen Funeral music">First Köthen Funeral music</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/St._Jakob,_K%C3%B6then" title="St. Jakob, Köthen">St. Jakob, Köthen</a> in honor of the funeral of his former employer <a href="/wiki/Leopold,_Prince_of_Anhalt-K%C3%B6then" title="Leopold, Prince of Anhalt-Köthen">Leopold, Prince of Anhalt-Köthen</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_24" title="March 24">March 24</a> (morning) – Première performance of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Johann Sebastian Bach</a>'s funeral cantata <a href="/wiki/Klagt,_Kinder,_klagt_es_aller_Welt,_BWV_244a" title="Klagt, Kinder, klagt es aller Welt, BWV 244a"><i>Klagt, Kinder, klagt es aller Welt</i>, BWV 244a</a> at St. Jakob, for the same event as the previous day.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_15" title="April 15">April 15</a> – Repeat performance of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Johann Sebastian Bach</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/St_Matthew_Passion" title="St Matthew Passion">St Matthew Passion</a></i>, BWV 244b at <a href="/wiki/St._Thomas_Church,_Leipzig" title="St. Thomas Church, Leipzig">St. Thomas Church, Leipzig</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1729&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: July–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_25" title="July 25">July 25</a> – Seven of the original eight <a href="/wiki/Lords_Proprietor" title="Lords Proprietor">Lords Proprietor</a> sell their tracts within the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Carolina" title="Province of Carolina">Province of Carolina</a> back to the British crown. The Province is permanently divided and reorganized into the Royal Colonies of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_30" title="July 30">July 30</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_1" title="August 1">August 1</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Comet_of_1729" title="Comet of 1729">Comet of 1729</a>, possibly the largest <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comet</a>, with the highest <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">apparent magnitude</a>, on record, is discovered by Fr. <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarrabat" title="Nicolas Sarrabat">Nicolas Sarrabat</a>, a professor of mathematics at <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_29" title="September 29">September 29</a>–<a href="/wiki/October_5" title="October 5">October 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Damghan_(1729)" title="Battle of Damghan (1729)">Battle of Damghan</a>: The Persians under <a href="/wiki/Nader_Shah" title="Nader Shah">Nader Shah</a> defeat the Afghans and their allies.</li>
<li>November – Completion of the first (wooden) <a href="/wiki/Putney_Bridge" title="Putney Bridge">Putney Bridge</a> as the only fixed crossing of the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> between <a href="/wiki/London_Bridge" title="London Bridge">London Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingston_Bridge,_London" title="Kingston Bridge, London">Kingston</a> in England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_9" title="November 9">November 9</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Seville_(1729)" title="Treaty of Seville (1729)">Treaty of Seville</a> is signed between <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pocket_Date_Book_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_Date_Book-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_29" title="November 29">November 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Natchez_revolt" title="Natchez revolt">Natchez revolt</a>: The worst Indian massacre to take place on <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a> soil occurs when <a href="/wiki/Natchez_people" title="Natchez people">Natchez people</a> kill 138 Frenchmen, 35 French women, and 56 children at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Rosalie" title="Fort Rosalie">Fort Rosalie</a> (near modern-day <a href="/wiki/Natchez,_Mississippi" title="Natchez, Mississippi">Natchez, Mississippi</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1729&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The third oldest settlement in <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Port_Gibson,_Mississippi" title="Port Gibson, Mississippi">Port Gibson</a>, is founded.</li>
<li>A fire in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> destroys 12,000 houses and kills 7,000 inhabitants.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Swift" title="Jonathan Swift">Jonathan Swift</a> (anonymously) publishes his satire <i><a href="/wiki/A_Modest_Proposal" title="A Modest Proposal">A Modest Proposal</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
