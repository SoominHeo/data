<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_Resource_Management">Radio Resource Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IEEE_802.11k-2008&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Radio Resource Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>IEEE 802.11k and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IEEE_802.11r" title="IEEE 802.11r">802.11r</a> are industry standards that enable seamless Basic Service Set (BSS) transitions in the WLAN environment. The 802.11k standard provides information to discover the best available access point.</p>
<p>802.11k is intended to improve the way traffic is distributed within a network. In a wireless LAN, each device normally connects to the access point (AP) that provides the strongest signal. Depending on the number and geographic locations of the subscribers, this arrangement can sometimes lead to excessive demand on one AP and underutilization of others, resulting in degradation of overall network performance. In a network conforming to 802.11k, if the AP having the strongest signal is loaded to its full capacity, a wireless device is connected to one of the underutilized APs. Even though the signal may be weaker, the overall throughput is greater because more efficient use is made of the network resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NetworkWorldArticle_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NetworkWorldArticle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protocol_operation">Protocol operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IEEE_802.11k-2008&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Protocol operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following steps are performed before switching to a new access point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NetworkWorldArticle_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NetworkWorldArticle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wireless_access_point" title="Wireless access point">Access point</a> determines that client is moving away from it.</li>
<li>Informs client to prepare to switch to a new access point.</li>
<li>Client requests list of nearby access points</li>
<li>Access point gives site report</li>
<li>Client moves to best access point based on report</li>
</ol>
