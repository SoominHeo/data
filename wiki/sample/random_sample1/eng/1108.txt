<p><b>Ítróttarfelag Fuglafjarðar</b>, abbreviated to <b>ÍF</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroese</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Fuglafj%C3%B8r%C3%B0ur" title="Fuglafjørður">Fuglafjørður</a>. They play their home games at <a href="/wiki/Fuglafj%C3%B8r%C3%B0ur_Stadium" title="Fuglafjørður Stadium">Í Fløtugerði</a>. ÍF's <a href="/wiki/1979_1._deild" title="1979 1. deild">1979 Faroese championship</a> win is the club's sole to date. It has also won the second tier championship three times.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.2C_2007_and_2008">2006, 2007 and 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%8DF_Fuglafj%C3%B8r%C3%B0ur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2006, 2007 and 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the end of the 2006 season, the club was relegated from the top league, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formuladeildin" title="Formuladeildin">Formuladeildin</a>. In October 2007 the club was again promoted to the top flight, Formuladeildin. The team had some difficulties this season, not by adjusting to Formuladeildin, but because the first team coach, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=J%C3%B3n_Simonsen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jón Simonsen (page does not exist)">Jón Simonsen</a>, had to quit his job during the pre-season. Then ÍF hired old legend and Champion of the 1979 team, <a href="/wiki/Abraham_L%C3%B8kin" title="Abraham Løkin">Abraham Løkin</a>, to take care of the team until a new coach was hired. Late in April the third coach this season arrived. As the new headcoach of ÍF was another old legend from the late seventees, David R. Jones was hired. Even though the team have had 3 coaches during this season and are newly promoted, they ended at a comfortable 6th place after the first half of the season. After two lost matches in the second half of the 2008 season, ÍF hired coach number 4 that season. The new coach was called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Ellefsen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Albert Ellefsen (page does not exist)">Albert Ellefsen</a> and is the former coach at <a href="/wiki/Havnar_B%C3%B3ltfelag" title="Havnar Bóltfelag">HB Tórshavn</a>. Albert Ellefsen has won the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">faroese</a> championship 2 times with <a href="/wiki/Havnar_B%C3%B3ltfelag" title="Havnar Bóltfelag">HB Tórshavn</a> and is also coaching the U19 and <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Faroe Islands national under-21 football team">U21 national team</a> with <a href="/wiki/Abraham_L%C3%B8kin" title="Abraham Løkin">Abraham Løkin</a>. ÍF also used the transfer market during its opening in June and July. First <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Helgi_L._Petersen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Helgi L. Petersen (page does not exist)">Helgi L. Petersen</a> was brought in from <a href="/wiki/FC_Hoyv%C3%ADk" title="FC Hoyvík">Fram Tórshavn</a> and close to the end of the transfer opening ÍF bought <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Olsen_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andy Olsen (footballer) (page does not exist)">Andy Olsen</a> for an undisclosed fee from <a href="/wiki/NS%C3%8D_Runav%C3%ADk" title="NSÍ Runavík">NSÍ</a>. ÍF also lost one player during the transfer window opening. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%A1njal_Pauli_H%C3%B8jgaard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dánjal Pauli Højgaard (page does not exist)">Dánjal Pauli Højgaard</a> left to his hometown club in <a href="/wiki/Toftir" title="Toftir">Toftir</a>, <a href="/wiki/B68_Toftir" title="B68 Toftir">B68 Toftir</a>. ÍF finished 7th in their first season back in the top flight.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%8DF_Fuglafj%C3%B8r%C3%B0ur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jón Simonsen was back as first team coach for the start of the 2009 season and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_R%C3%B3in&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roy Róin (page does not exist)">Roy Róin</a> was his assistant, but the board decided to bring in <a href="/wiki/Abraham_L%C3%B8kin" title="Abraham Løkin">Abraham Løkin</a> to help Jón Simonsen as first team coach, and decided to let assistant coach, Roy Róin, go for the 2nd half of the season because of bad resaults during the 1st half of the season. ÍF tried to bring in new players during the season and got Faroe Islands all-time top goalscorer <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B3gvi_Jacobsen" title="Rógvi Jacobsen">Rógvi Jacobsen</a>, former ÍF player <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Viggo_Johannesen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Viggo Johannesen (page does not exist)">Viggo Johannesen</a> and one of fierce rivals, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADkingur_G%C3%B8ta" title="Víkingur Gøta">Víkingur</a>'s most popular player <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Paule_Petersen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hans Paule Petersen (page does not exist)">Hans Paule Petersen</a>. During the summer transfer window in 2009, Viggo Johannesen retired as footballer, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hanus_Eliasen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hanus Eliasen (page does not exist)">Hanus Eliasen</a> went to school in Copenhagen while Hans Paule Petersen went back to <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADkingur_G%C3%B8ta" title="Víkingur Gøta">Víkingur</a> but ÍF got <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kaj_Ennigar%C3%B0&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kaj Ennigarð (page does not exist)">Kaj Ennigarð</a>, younger brother of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Poul_Ennigar%C3%B0&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Poul Ennigarð (page does not exist)">Poul Ennigarð</a>, from Víkingur and made a loan deal with <a href="/wiki/NS%C3%8D_Runav%C3%ADk" title="NSÍ Runavík">NSÍ</a> for their young attacker, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%98ssur_Dalb%C3%BA%C3%B0&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Øssur Dalbúð (page does not exist)">Øssur Dalbúð</a>. ÍF ended up as number 7 again in 2009.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%8DF_Fuglafj%C3%B8r%C3%B0ur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the end of 2009 <a href="/wiki/Abraham_L%C3%B8kin" title="Abraham Løkin">Abraham Løkin</a> was announced as new first team coach for the season 2010 and ÍF decided to hire <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Torkil_Hansen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Torkil Hansen (page does not exist)">Torkil Hansen</a> as his assistant. ÍF sold their Hungarian goalkeeper, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A1s_G%C3%A1ng%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="András Gángó (page does not exist)">András Gángó</a> to <a href="/wiki/NS%C3%8D_Runav%C3%ADk" title="NSÍ Runavík">NSÍ</a> and didn't resign Hungarian midfielder, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bal%C3%A1zs_Sink%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Balázs Sinkó (page does not exist)">Balázs Sinkó</a> and Faroese national team defender, <a href="/wiki/Bartal_Eliasen" title="Bartal Eliasen">Bartal Eliasen</a>. ÍF made a permanent deal with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%98ssur_Dalb%C3%BA%C3%B0&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Øssur Dalbúð (page does not exist)">Øssur Dalbúð</a> and bought young starlet, <a href="/wiki/Karl_L%C3%B8kin" title="Karl Løkin">Karl Løkin</a>, from NSÍ in February 2010. ÍF also signed former national team goalkeeper and Goalkeeper of the Year 2008 and 2009, <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A1kup_Mikkelsen" title="Jákup Mikkelsen">Jákup Mikkelsen</a>, from <a href="/wiki/K%C3%8D_Klaksv%C3%ADk" title="KÍ Klaksvík">KÍ</a> and defender <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%BAni_E._Eltt%C3%B8r&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rúni E. Elttør (page does not exist)">Rúni E. Elttør</a> from HB Tórshavn. ÍF won the newly started pre-season competition Eysturoyar Kappingin 2010. They won the final 2–0 against fierce rivals, Víkingur. During the summer transfer window ÍF got back defender <a href="/wiki/Bartal_Eliasen" title="Bartal Eliasen">Bartal Eliasen</a> and lost <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kaj_Ennigar%C3%B0&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kaj Ennigarð (page does not exist)">Kaj Ennigarð</a>. The team reached the cup final but lost it 1–0 against <a href="/wiki/EB/Streymur" title="EB/Streymur">EB/Streymur</a> and ended as number 4 in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vodafonedivision&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vodafonedivision (page does not exist)">Vodafonedivision</a>. The 4th position was enough to secure a qualification place in the 1st round of Euro League 2011–12 competition.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%8DF_Fuglafj%C3%B8r%C3%B0ur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the 2011 season the club's top goal scorer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%98ssur_Dalb%C3%BA%C3%B0&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Øssur Dalbúð (page does not exist)">Øssur Dalbúð</a> went to Danish 1st Division club <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hvidovre_BK&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hvidovre BK (page does not exist)">Hvidovre BK</a> and national team star attacker <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B3gvi_Jacobsen" title="Rógvi Jacobsen">Rógvi Jacobsen</a> decided to stop his career after struggling with injuries, so the club signed a new exciting striker <a href="/wiki/Christian_Muomaife" title="Christian Muomaife">Christian Muomaife</a> from the Danish 1st division club <a href="/wiki/Viborg_FF" title="Viborg FF">Viborg FF</a> and Faroese national team midfielder <a href="/wiki/Bogi_L%C3%B8kin" title="Bogi Løkin">Bogi Løkin</a> from <a href="/wiki/NS%C3%8D_Runav%C3%ADk" title="NSÍ Runavík">NSÍ</a>. The team also got some young starlets to their team, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Margeir_Toftegaard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Margeir Toftegaard (page does not exist)">Margeir Toftegaard</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hallgr%C3%ADm_Gregersen_Hansen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hallgrím Gregersen Hansen (page does not exist)">Hallgrím G. Hansen</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=P%C3%A6tur_%C3%A1_L%C3%ADknarg%C3%B8tu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pætur á Líknargøtu (page does not exist)">Pætur á Líknargøtu</a> and 32-year-old attacker <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A1r%C3%B0ur_%C3%A1_Lakjuni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bárður á Lakjuni (page does not exist)">Bárður á Lakjuni</a> decided to give it a last try because of missing strikers at the club.</p>

They lost the cup final 1–0 against <a href="/wiki/EB/Streymur" title="EB/Streymur">EB/Streymur</a> in 2010 and 4–1 against former fierce rivals, GÍ, in 2005.
