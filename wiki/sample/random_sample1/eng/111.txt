<p><br/>
<b>1743 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCXLIII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">starting on Tuesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> F) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> B) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1743rd year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 743rd year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 43rd year of the <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>, and the 4th year of the <a href="/wiki/1740s" title="1740s">1740s</a> decade. As of the start of 1743, the Gregorian calendar was 11 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1743&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_14" title="February 14">February 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Henry_Pelham" title="Henry Pelham">Henry Pelham</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/March_2" title="March 2">March 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_La_Guaira" title="Battle of La Guaira">Battle of La Guaira</a>: a British expeditionary fleet under Sir <a href="/wiki/Sir_Charles_Knowles,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Charles Knowles, 1st Baronet">Charles Knowles</a> is defeated by the Spanish.</li>
