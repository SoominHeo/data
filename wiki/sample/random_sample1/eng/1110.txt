<p><b>IMG Academy</b> is a boarding school and sport training destination in <a href="/wiki/Bradenton,_Florida" title="Bradenton, Florida">Bradenton, Florida</a>, United States. The boarding school offers an integrated academic and athletic college preparatory experience across eight sports – baseball, basketball, football, golf, lacrosse, soccer, tennis, and track &amp; field and cross country. IMG Academy offers camp programs on a year-round basis, serves as a training and competition venue for amateur, collegiate, and professional teams, adults and families, and a host site for a variety of events.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nick_Bollettieri" title="Nick Bollettieri">Nick Bollettieri</a> founded the <b>Nick Bollettieri Tennis Academy</b> in 1978. Sports and entertainment company <a href="/wiki/IMG_(company)" title="IMG (company)">IMG</a> purchased the academy in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-a-1">[1]</a></sup> IMG acquired the youth division of the <a href="/wiki/David_Leadbetter_(golf_instructor)" title="David Leadbetter (golf instructor)">David Leadbetter</a> Golf Academy in 1993 and added programs for soccer and baseball in 1994. Hockey and basketball programs were added in 2000 and 2001, respectively, and by 2002 the IMG campus had expanded to 190 acres (0.77 km<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> IMG Academy suspended its hockey program in 2003.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Football was added in 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as well as lacrosse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Track &amp; field and cross country was added in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>IMG Academy currently sits on 450 acres (1.8 km<sup>2</sup>) of land, and in 2011, IMG paid $7.5 million for an additional 110 acres (0.45 km<sup>2</sup>) adjacent to the current campus for future expansion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-a-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2014, IMG purchased an additional 24 acres (0.097 km<sup>2</sup>). The land borders IMG's west campus, where the sports performance academy is undergoing a $198 million expansion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The IMG Pendleton School was founded in 1999 as a co-educational, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/College_preparatory" title="College preparatory">college preparatory</a> school for athletic students. In 2012, the school changed its name to "IMG Academy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It delivers both academics and athletics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programs">Programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bollettieri_Tennis">Bollettieri Tennis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bollettieri Tennis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Bollettieri Tennis program offers year-round tennis camps ranging from one to five weeks in length and is led by director Rohan Goetzke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The campus has 35 outdoor hard courts, 5 indoor hard courts, and 16 green <a href="/wiki/Clay_court" title="Clay court">clay courts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1987, 32 academy students or former students were in the <a href="/wiki/The_Championships,_Wimbledon" title="The Championships, Wimbledon">Wimbledon</a> draw and 27 were in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Open_(tennis)" title="U.S. Open (tennis)">U.S. Open</a>. The famous students included <a href="/wiki/Andre_Agassi" title="Andre Agassi">Andre Agassi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monica_Seles" title="Monica Seles">Monica Seles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Courier" title="Jim Courier">Jim Courier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kei_Nishikori" title="Kei Nishikori">Kei Nishikori</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kournikova" title="Anna Kournikova">Anna Kournikova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serena_Williams" title="Serena Williams">Serena Williams</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maria_Sharapova" title="Maria Sharapova">Maria Sharapova</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_Football">American Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: American Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IMG announced the <a href="/wiki/John_Madden" title="John Madden">John Madden</a> Football Academy in March 2010 and held its first camp from June 4–6, 2010. The football program offers a residency program and year-round camps ranging from three days to five weeks in length and was formerly led by former <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> <a href="/wiki/Quarterback" title="Quarterback">quarterback</a> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Weinke" title="Chris Weinke">Chris Weinke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The academy fielded a high school football team for the first time in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2013, MaxPreps called IMG Academy the "nation's top high school football facility."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The varsity team is now led by head coach Kevin Wright, who was named head coach in spring 2015 after Weinke accepted a position as quarterbacks coach with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams" title="Los Angeles Rams">Los Angeles Rams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Wright guided the program to its first undefeated regular season (9–0) in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soccer">Soccer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Soccer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IMG Academy offers an Academy soccer program and year-round soccer camps. IMG Academy is home to <a href="/wiki/United_States_Soccer_Federation" title="United States Soccer Federation">U.S. Soccer's</a> full-time residency program for the United States' U-16 and U-17 men's national teams. Started in 1999, it has become integral in developing the United States' top youth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a> prospects. The original idea of the residency program was to give elite players the opportunity to train in a professional environment as most <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">MLS</a> clubs did not have a substantial youth academy system in place prior to 2009. The Bradenton Academy has grown from an initial 20 players to 30 in 2002 and then to 40 in 2003. The program is now split into a U-16 squad and a U-17 squad.</p>
<p>A typical day for the student-athlete at Bradenton consists of academic classes in the morning followed by sports training in the afternoon. Students at the academy take accelerated courses and graduate high school a year early, making the players who do not turn pro immediately some of the most heavily recruited prospects in <a href="/wiki/College_soccer" title="College soccer">college soccer</a>. The academy can trace its roots back to <a href="/wiki/Project_2010" title="Project 2010">Project 2010</a> which highlighted ways U.S. Soccer could make the senior men's national team a legitimate threat to win the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> by the end of the decade. Two programs that were born from Project 2010 were <a href="/wiki/Generation_Adidas" title="Generation Adidas">Generation Adidas</a> (previously called <i>Project-40</i>) and the Bradenton academy. The academy was started in January 1999 with backing of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Management_Group" title="International Management Group">IMG</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baseball">Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IMG Academy has an Academy baseball program and year-round camps, in addition to summer Wood Bat Leagues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basketball">Basketball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Basketball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IMG Academy's post graduate program played in the 2016 National Prep Championship in New Haven, Connecticut. Among the teams were Brewster Academy, <a href="/wiki/DME_Academy" title="DME Academy">DME Academy</a>, Elev-8 Sports Institute, Fork Union Military Academy, Hargrave Military Academy, IMG Academy, Northfield Mount Hermon School, and more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Golf">Golf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Golf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IMG Academy has an Academy golf program and offers summer camps. The students and camp participants play on two very typical Florida golf courses in Sara Bay Country Club and IMG academy Golf Club. Both are fairly well kept and offer a test of the students game. IMG's practice facilities include a big range which is open to golf students most of the day and a little range which is open to everyone 24/7. The coaches at IMG are well trained and help in any way they can.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lacrosse">Lacrosse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Lacrosse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IMG Academy has an Academy lacrosse program and year-round camps. IMG Academy launched a high school team in 2012. Prior to that, IMG Academy hosted their first full-time lacrosse student-athlete, Matthew Xirinachs, starting in September 2010. Xirinachs went on to join the Drexel Men's Lacrosse Team in the fall of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>IMG Academy has an Academy track &amp; field and cross country program and year-round camps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_alumni">Notable alumni</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMG_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Notable alumni">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_IMG_Academy_alumni" title="List of IMG Academy alumni">List of IMG Academy alumni</a></div>
<p>Since opening in 1978, IMG Academy has trained hundreds of <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> and professional athletes, including those in the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">NBA</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a>, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">MLB</a>, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">MLS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Tennis_Professionals" title="Association of Tennis Professionals">ATP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Tennis_Association" title="Women's Tennis Association">WTA</a>, <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">PGA Tour</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LPGA_Tour" title="LPGA Tour">LPGA Tour</a>.</p>
