<p>"<b>I Love Rock 'n' Roll</b>" is a <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> song written in 1975 by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Merrill" title="Alan Merrill">Alan Merrill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake_Hooker_(musician)" title="Jake Hooker (musician)">Jake Hooker</a> of <a href="/wiki/Arrows_(British_band)" title="Arrows (British band)">The Arrows</a>, who recorded the first released version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The song was later made famous by <a href="/wiki/Joan_Jett" title="Joan Jett">Joan Jett &amp; the Blackhearts</a> in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Outsight_Radio_Hours_interview_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Outsight_Radio_Hours_interview-2">[2]</a></sup> The only Arrows band member still living, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Merrill" title="Alan Merrill">Alan Merrill</a> has been playing the song recently live in Europe, Japan and most often in his home town New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Arrows_original_version">The Arrows original version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Arrows original version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was originally recorded and <a href="/wiki/Release_(music)" title="Release (music)">released</a> by <a href="/wiki/Arrows_(British_band)" title="Arrows (British band)">The Arrows</a> in 1975 on <a href="/wiki/Rak_Records" title="Rak Records">Rak Records</a>, with lead vocals, guitar, music &amp; lyrics written by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Merrill" title="Alan Merrill">Alan Merrill</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Mickie_Most" title="Mickie Most">Mickie Most</a>. In an interview with Songfacts, Merrill said he wrote it as "a knee-jerk response to <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">the Rolling Stones</a>' '<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Only_Rock_%27n_Roll_(But_I_Like_It)" title="It's Only Rock 'n Roll (But I Like It)">It's Only Rock 'n Roll (But I Like It)</a>'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Outsight_Radio_Hours_interview_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Outsight_Radio_Hours_interview-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-songfacts_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-songfacts-4">[4]</a></sup> This version was first released as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-side" title="B-side">B-side</a>, but was soon re-recorded and flipped to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-side" title="A-side">A-side</a> status on a subsequent pressing of the record. The Arrows performed the song in 1975 on the <a href="/wiki/Muriel_Young" title="Muriel Young">Muriel Young</a>-produced show <i>45</i>, after which Young offered the Arrows a weekly UK television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Arrows_(TV_series)" title="Arrows (TV series)">Arrows</a></i>, which was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> starting in March 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cover_song_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cover_song-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Joan_Jett" title="Joan Jett">Joan Jett</a> saw the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_(British_band)" title="Arrows (British band)">Arrows</a> perform "I Love Rock 'n' Roll" on their weekly UK television series <i><a href="/wiki/Arrows_(TV_series)" title="Arrows (TV series)">Arrows</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Outsight_Radio_Hours_interview_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Outsight_Radio_Hours_interview-2">[2]</a></sup> when she was touring England with <a href="/wiki/The_Runaways" title="The Runaways">the Runaways</a> in 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cover_song_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cover_song-5">[5]</a></sup> She first recorded the song in 1979 with two of the <a href="/wiki/Sex_Pistols" title="Sex Pistols">Sex Pistols</a>: <a href="/wiki/Steve_Jones_(musician)" title="Steve Jones (musician)">Steve Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Cook" title="Paul Cook">Paul Cook</a>. This first version was released on vinyl in 1979 on Vertigo records as a B-side to "You Don't Own Me". In 1981, Jett re-recorded the song, this time with her band, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Blackhearts" title="The Blackhearts">the Blackhearts</a>. This recording became a U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> number-one single for seven weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>Billboard</i> ranked it at the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Year-End_Hot_100_singles_of_1982" title="Billboard Year-End Hot 100 singles of 1982">No. 3 song for 1982</a>. The single was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a>, representing two million units shipped to stores with Jett's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll_(album)" title="I Love Rock 'n' Roll (album)">I Love Rock 'n' Roll</a></i> album reaching number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. Joan Jett's version was ranked No. 89 in the list 100 Greatest Guitar Songs of <i>Rolling Stone</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and has also been inducted into the Grammys Hall of Fame in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"I Love Rock 'n' Roll"'s black-and-white <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> received heavy play from the just-launched <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> network. In it, Jett and the Blackhearts travel to a small, dingy bar and proceed to excite the drunken crowd by performing the song and yelling out its <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">chorus</a>. A snippet of Jett's 1981 "<a href="/wiki/Bad_Reputation_(Joan_Jett_song)" title="Bad Reputation (Joan Jett song)">Bad Reputation</a>" is featured at the beginning of the video. The video was originally in color, but it was converted to black and white because Jett was ultimately displeased with the look of her red leather jumpsuit in color.</p>
<p>In 1993 Joan Jett &amp; the Blackhearts made another music video for the song as part of the <i><a href="/wiki/Wayne%27s_World_2" title="Wayne's World 2">Wayne's World 2</a></i> soundtrack. In the video appear several scenes from the movie with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Myers_(actor)" title="Mike Myers (actor)">Mike Myers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dana_Carvey" title="Dana Carvey">Dana Carvey</a> mixed with footage of Jett and her band in concert. It was again released as a single by Warner/Reprise with "Activity Grrrl" as the B-side.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jett's version has received many accolades, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>Ranking No. 85 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Q_magazine" title="Q magazine"><i>Q</i> magazine</a> March 2005 list of the 100 Greatest Guitar Tracks</li>
<li>Ranking No. 491 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> magazine's list of "<a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone%27s_500_Greatest_Songs_of_All_Time" title="Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Songs of All Time">the 500 Greatest Songs of All Time</a>"</li>
<li>Ranking No. 56 in <i>Billboard's Greatest Songs of all time.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background.2C_release_and_composition">Background, release and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Background, release and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<b>I Love Rock 'n' Roll</b>" is the fourth European single released by <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Britney_(album)" title="Britney (album)">Britney</a></i> on 27 May 2002. The song was used in her 2002 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Crossroads_(2002_film)" title="Crossroads (2002 film)">Crossroads</a></i>. Spears's character, Lucy, performs it in a karaoke bar. Spears said of the song, "They asked me to sing karaoke in the movie <i>Crossroads</i> and I've actually sung I Love Rock 'n' Roll in a lot of clubs that I've been to."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Spears has publicly admitted the original song to be one of her favorites. Spears listened to the original Arrows Mickie Most-produced version just before she recorded the song, according to <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive</a> A&amp;R representative Steve Lunt. The scratches performed on this song were performed by Corey Chase, at Hit Factory Miami. When promoting the single's release she mistakenly attributed the hit version of the song to <a href="/wiki/Pat_Benatar" title="Pat Benatar">Pat Benatar</a> instead of Jett.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical">Critical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Critical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Spears' cover was met with mostly favorable reviews. <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Ted Kessler wrote that Britney "still works best when making a good pop cheese and dance sandwich: there's the ace <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Jerkins" title="Rodney Jerkins">Rodney Jerkins</a>-produced version of <a href="/wiki/Joan_Jett" title="Joan Jett">Joan Jett</a>'s (sic) 'I Love Rock'n'Roll', which does exactly what is says on the tin".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Barry Walters wrote that "producer Rodney Jerkins' hip-hop blaspheming of Joan Jett's "I Love Rock 'n' Roll" doesn't go as far as it should (is a <a href="/wiki/Limp_Bizkit" title="Limp Bizkit">Limp Bizkit</a> remix in its future?), but it certainly beats what her earlier studio architects did to those <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonny_and_Cher" title="Sonny and Cher">Sonny and Cher</a> ("<a href="/wiki/The_Beat_Goes_On_(Sonny_%26_Cher_song)" title="The Beat Goes On (Sonny &amp; Cher song)">The Beat Goes On</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(album)" title="...Baby One More Time (album)">...Baby One More Time</a></i>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" title="Rolling Stones">Stones</a> ("<a href="/wiki/(I_Can%27t_Get_No)_Satisfaction" title="(I Can't Get No) Satisfaction">(I Can't Get No) Satisfaction</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_(album)" title="Oops!... I Did It Again (album)">Oops!... I Did It Again</a></i>) songs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSreview_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-RSreview-32">[32]</a></sup> Another positive reception came from <a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a>'s editor Nikki Tranter, who enjoyed that the song is "different from the average run-of-the-mill pop offering, and praised that "she does strange justice to the tune vamping up her vocals and turning out something, that while silly and camp, is actually a fun listen."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In contrast, David Browne wrote for <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> that "her remake is neither imaginative (it simply xeroxes Joan Jett's arrangement) nor all that believable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial">Commercial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Commercial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>"I Love Rock 'n' Roll" charted moderately upon release, reaching the top twenty in most regions. The song was moderately successful in the UK, where it peaked at number thirteen (which, at the time, was her lowest peak for a single released there, until "<a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">Radar</a>" only managed to reach number forty-six in 2009, and then "<a href="/wiki/I_Wanna_Go" title="I Wanna Go">I Wanna Go</a>, which peaked at one-hundred and eleven). The single sold a total of 65,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> It was also certified Gold in Australia.</p>

<p>Directed by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Applebaum" title="Chris Applebaum">Chris Applebaum</a>, the music video for "I Love Rock 'n' Roll" features Spears with her own band, a stack of speakers, and flashing lights. It was shot at The Inn, a bar in Long Beach, New York. The video was ranked at No. 2 on the 100 Best Videos of 2002 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Latin_America" title="MTV Latin America">MTV Latin America</a>'s countdown. The Director's Cut version of the video was later leaked; it included previously unseen scenes from the video.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Love_Rock_%27n%27_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The song was performed live during Spears' <a href="/wiki/Dream_Within_a_Dream_Tour" title="Dream Within a Dream Tour">Dream Within a Dream Tour</a> (2001–02). In 2016, it was added to the revamped set list of her <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> residency show, <a href="/wiki/Britney:_Piece_of_Me" title="Britney: Piece of Me">Britney: Piece of Me</a> (2016–17), marking the first time Spears performed the song in 14 years. The performance features Spears riding a mechanical electric guitar, which simulates a mechanical bull, as it rotates on stage. The same prop electric guitar was previously used by Spears during her <a href="/wiki/Femme_Fatale_Tour" title="Femme Fatale Tour">Femme Fatale Tour</a> (2011) for a segment in which she covered the song "<a href="/wiki/Burning_Up_(Madonna_song)" title="Burning Up (Madonna song)">Burning Up</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2016_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2016 Billboard Music Awards">2016 Billboard Music Awards</a>, the song was performed as part of a medley, using the same guitar prop.</p>

<p><br/>
"I Love Rock 'n' Roll" is an often-covered song, and has been notably recorded by such artists as:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%27Arc~En~Ciel" title="L'Arc~En~Ciel">L'Arc~En~Ciel</a> released the song as a single internationally in 2011 and it was featured in a Pepsi commercial in Japan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lee_Da-hae" title="Lee Da-hae">Lee Da-hae</a> released the song as a single in 2007, with versions in both English and Korean.</li>
<li>Boy band <a href="/wiki/Five_(band)" title="Five (band)">Five</a> sampled the song for "<a href="/wiki/Everybody_Get_Up" title="Everybody Get Up">Everybody Get Up</a>".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Joseph_Simmons" title="Joseph Simmons">Reverend Run</a> sampled the song for his song "Mind on the Road" on his solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Distortion_(Joseph_Simmons_album)" title="Distortion (Joseph Simmons album)">Distortion</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic" title='"Weird Al" Yankovic'>"Weird Al" Yankovic</a> (<a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">parody</a>, as "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_Rocky_Road" title="I Love Rocky Road">I Love Rocky Road</a>")</li>
<li>The group Forever Young covered the song in 2003 and made the French top 30 peaking at #27 in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></li>
<li>Fast Forward Highway covered Joan Jett's version for the video game <a href="/wiki/Just_Dance_2017" title="Just Dance 2017">Just Dance 2017</a>.</li>
</ul>
