<p>"<b>I Remember You</b>" is the twenty-fifth and penultimate episode of the <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time_(season_4)" title="Adventure Time (season 4)">fourth season</a> of the American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_television_series" title="Animated television series">animated television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i>. The episode was written and storyboarded by Cole Sanchez and <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Sugar" title="Rebecca Sugar">Rebecca Sugar</a>, from a story by Patrick McHale, <a href="/wiki/Kent_Osborne" title="Kent Osborne">Kent Osborne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_Ward" title="Pendleton Ward">Pendleton Ward</a>. It originally aired on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> on October 15, 2012. The episode later re-aired on March 25, 2013, together with the <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time_(season_5)" title="Adventure Time (season 5)">fifth season</a> episode "<a href="/wiki/Simon_%26_Marcy" title="Simon &amp; Marcy">Simon &amp; Marcy</a>," and as such was advertised as a half-hour special.</p>
<p>The series follows the adventures of <a href="/wiki/Finn_the_Human" title="Finn the Human">Finn</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Shada" title="Jeremy Shada">Jeremy Shada</a>), a human boy, and his best friend and adoptive brother <a href="/wiki/Jake_the_Dog" title="Jake the Dog">Jake</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/John_DiMaggio" title="John DiMaggio">John DiMaggio</a>), a dog with magical powers to change shape and grow and shrink at will. In this episode, <a href="/wiki/Marceline_the_Vampire_Queen" title="Marceline the Vampire Queen">Marceline the Vampire Queen</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Olivia_Olson" title="Olivia Olson">Olivia Olson</a>) collaborates with the <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kenny" title="Tom Kenny">Tom Kenny</a>) to write a song, and Marceline tries to get the Ice King to remember who he really is. This episode reveals that Marceline and the Ice King knew each other during aftermath of the Mushroom War, a cataclysmic event that occurred a thousand years previous.</p>
<p>Formerly titled "Help," "I Remember You" helped expand upon and explore the Ice King's previous life. The episode features four songs—"The Fry Song," "Oh Bubblegum," "Nuts," and "Remember You"—all of which were written by Sugar, although Sanchez co-wrote the lyrics on "Oh Bubblegum." Sugar used an <a href="/wiki/Omnichord" title="Omnichord">omnichord</a> for the demo of the eponymous song, and her playing ended up as part of the episode. "I Remember You" was watched by 2.535 million people and received universal critical acclaim, with many critics praising the story's depth and its exploration of mental disorders, memory, and loss.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Remember_You_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Remember_You_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the context of the series, <a href="/wiki/Marceline_the_Vampire_Queen" title="Marceline the Vampire Queen">Marceline the Vampire Queen</a> is a thousand-year-old vampire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evicted_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-evicted-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cotw_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-cotw-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a> is a recurring antagonist of the series, and frequently steals princesses throughout Ooo; although he is often at odds with Finn and Jake, he is generally not a serious threat. He was formerly a human archaeologist named Simon Petrikov who discovered a magic crown that gives him magical ice powers but eroded his memory and made him go insane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-characters_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-characters-4">[4]</a></sup> In addition, throughout the series, various character and events hint at the mysterious Mushroom War, a cataclysmic war that destroyed modern human society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avclub_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-avclub-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Remember_You_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Ice King decides that he wants to enlist Marceline's help in writing a song to woo princesses, taking pages randomly torn from an old scrapbook with him for later inspiration. He flies to her cave, and after a minor scuffle that involves <a href="/wiki/Finn_the_Human" title="Finn the Human">Finn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake_the_Dog" title="Jake the Dog">Jake</a>, Marceline finally relents and helps him write a song. At first, the Ice King simply sings a song he wrote about Princess Bubblegum, before breaking down, crying about his loneliness and damaging Marceline's living room with his ice magic. Marceline tells him to stop acting crazy, and he pushes her, before timidly retreating to the top of her refrigerator. Marceline begins to lament, via the song "Nuts," and notes that, despite his instability, she is glad to see him. The Ice King is surprised to find out that she "likes" him and asks for a hug. However, when he misinterprets her feelings and tries to kiss her, Marceline angrily confronts him and asks if he remembers his past by calling him by his actual name: Simon.</p>
<p>The Ice King is oblivious to his former self, and Marceline begins looking through the Ice King's scrapbook pages to show him evidence of his former life. Among the pages is a picture of a young Marceline, with a message to her from a thousand years ago scrawled on the back. The message, written by Simon before his mind was completely consumed by the magic of the crown, asks Marceline to forgive him for whatever he does under the crown's influence. The Ice King fails to understand the context, and believes that he has written not a note of apology but lyrics, and attempts to add music to his "song". Overcome by emotion, Marceline cries and begins singing along while the Ice King plays drums, oblivious as to what is actually going on.</p>
<p>While the eponymous song "Remember You" is being sung, the episode flashes back almost a thousand years before the events of the episode. A young Marceline is seen crying in the wreckage of a city, presumably after the cataclysmic Mushroom War. Simon Petrikov sees her from a distance and runs over. In an attempt to comfort her, he gives her Hambo, a teddy bear, that he finds in the ruins of a toy store. Marceline takes the stuffed animal, stops crying, and looks up in happiness.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Remember_You_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Omnichord.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="142" data-file-width="220" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/41/Omnichord.jpg" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Omnichord.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Storyboard artist Rebecca Sugar used an <a href="/wiki/Omnichord" title="Omnichord">omnichord</a> for the eponymous song "Remember You".</div>

<p>"I Remember You" was storyboarded by Cole Sanchez and <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Sugar" title="Rebecca Sugar">Rebecca Sugar</a>, from a story by Patrick McHale, <a href="/wiki/Kent_Osborne" title="Kent Osborne">Kent Osborne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_Ward" title="Pendleton Ward">Pendleton Ward</a>. The episode was directed by <a href="/wiki/Larry_Leichliter" title="Larry Leichliter">Larry Leichliter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The idea to create an episode based on a shared history between Marceline and the Ice King was Ward's idea. Sugar had also been pushing for a Marceline and Ice King musical episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-music_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-music-8">[8]</a></sup> Sugar was responsible for designing Marceline's look in the episode. According to her, the shirt that Marceline wears was "loosely based off a stretched out old <a href="/wiki/Drug_Abuse_Resistance_Education" title="Drug Abuse Resistance Education">D.A.R.E.</a> shirt."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The original working title for the episode was "Help". Former storyboard artist and creative director for the series Adam Muto joked that the change was made due to the famous <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">Beatles</a> <a href="/wiki/Help!_(song)" title="Help! (song)">song of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The episode features three new songs: "Oh Bubblegum", "Nuts", and the titular "Remember You".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bubblenuts_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-bubblenuts-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rememberyousong_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-rememberyousong-12">[12]</a></sup> Sugar composed the music for all four, and wrote the lyrics (Sanchez co-wrote the lyrics for "Oh Bubblegum").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bubblenuts_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-bubblenuts-11">[11]</a></sup> Sugar recorded demos for the songs and Rob Sugar, Rebecca's father, released the three new demos on his official YouTube account. For the demo for "I Remember You", Sugar recorded vocals over a separate track of her playing an <a href="/wiki/Omnichord" title="Omnichord">omnichord</a>; Sugar's ominchord playing was eventually worked directly into the episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> According to Rob, the "off-key notes were accidents of running out of power—it took a lot of tries to make it happen on cue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> While Sugar was pitching the song, she played her songs but her omnichord started to lose power. Sugar and the crew realized that the sound of the omnichord losing power fit with the mood of the episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebeccaremember_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-rebeccaremember-15">[15]</a></sup> For the eponymous song, Sugar noted that it "was so amazing to work with Olivia Olson and Tom Kenny on the songs for this episode, they had so much chemistry in the booth together!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allnew_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-allnew-16">[16]</a></sup> She wrote that she constructed the episode to "do them justice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allnew_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-allnew-16">[16]</a></sup> Olson noted that, despite the sad themes featured in the installment, recording the dialogue with Kenny and music for the episode was a fun experience. While the two were recording, Olson had to sing Kenny his melodies; Kenny would then parrot the lines back in the voice of the Ice King.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soundcloudoliviamartin_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-soundcloudoliviamartin-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to the three new songs, the episode also features a modified version of "The Fry Song", which had originally appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time_(season_2)" title="Adventure Time (season 2)">second season</a> episode "It Came from the Nightosphere". During the recording of the aforementioned episode, Tom Kenny recorded his own version of the "Fry Song" for fun, which was later included in the <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time_(season_3)" title="Adventure Time (season 3)">third season</a> episode "<a href="/wiki/Holly_Jolly_Secrets" title="Holly Jolly Secrets">Holly Jolly Secrets</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wardholly2_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-wardholly2-18">[18]</a></sup> This version later inspired Ice King's alternate "Gunter" version in "I Remember You".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-penremember_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-penremember-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Remember_You_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"I Remember You" aired on October 15, 2012. It was viewed by 2.535 million people, and received a 0.5 rating in the 18–49 demographic. This means that it was seen by 0.5 percent of all 18- to 49-year-olds at the time of the broadcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The episode later re-aired on March 25, 2013, together with the <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time_(season_5)" title="Adventure Time (season 5)">fifth season</a> episode "Simon &amp; Marcy," and was advertised by <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> as a "half hour special."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> This special was watched by 2.6 million viewers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The episode received glowing critical reception. <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Jane_Anders" title="Charlie Jane Anders">Charlie Jane Anders</a> of <a href="/wiki/Io9" title="Io9">io9</a> called the episode "one of the most intense things I've seen in ages."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-io9-23">[23]</a></sup> She specifically cited the final flashback as noteworthy, noting that "The final scene, where Marceline and Ice King have this weird duet, made me lose my shit and keeps popping into my head. It's rare that any television show gets its hooks into your brain like that, but especially a kid's cartoon."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-io9-23">[23]</a></sup> She concluded that the main reason that the ending was so powerful was that it showed the viewers the "immediate aftermath of the Mushroom War" and allowed the viewers to see "the way Marceline's personal loss mirrors the wider loss of the end of the entire world" following the war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-io9-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Oliver Sava of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> wrote that "never has an <i>Adventure Time</i> episode triggered waterworks like 'I Remember You'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avclub_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-avclub-5">[5]</a></sup> He called it "one of the most touching stories this series has ever told," and praised the way it took Marceline and the Ice King—whom he called two of the show's most tragic characters—and put "them together in a way that hits like a sledgehammer."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avclub_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-avclub-5">[5]</a></sup> He felt that the episode also served as a way to tell "a heartbreaking story about how mental illness can tear people apart," noting that the Ice King's condition is similar to how people with <a href="/wiki/Bipolar_disorder" title="Bipolar disorder">bipolar disorder</a> behave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avclub_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-avclub-5">[5]</a></sup> Eric Kohn of <a href="/wiki/IndieWire" title="IndieWire">IndieWire</a>, in an article explaining why "<i>Adventure Time</i> is the best sci-fi show on TV right now" following the airing of "Simon &amp; Marcy," wrote that "I Remember You" was "the finest entry in the series' history."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indie_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-indie-24">[24]</a></sup> He further complimented the titular song, describing it as "heartbreaking" and "show-stopping."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indie_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-indie-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Steven Aoun of <a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a> applauded the way the episode "focuses on two popular supporting characters, and foregrounds the relation between memory and identity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-pop-25">[25]</a></sup> Furthermore, the site noted that "the sound that takes you by surprise may be your own crying."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-pop-25">[25]</a></sup> Novelist <a href="/wiki/Lev_Grossman" title="Lev Grossman">Lev Grossman</a>, in an interview with <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a>, praised the backstory of the Ice King and the exploration of his condition, noting that his origin is "psychologically plausible."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npr_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-npr-26">[26]</a></sup> Grossman praised the way the series was able to tackle the issues of mental illness, saying: "It's very affecting. My dad has been going through having Alzheimer's, and he's forgotten so much about who he used to be. And I look at him and think this cartoon is about my father dying."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npr_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-npr-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_release">Media release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Remember_You_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Media release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The episode first saw physical release as part of the 2013 <i>Jake the Dad</i> DVD, which included 16 episodes from the series' fourth and fifth seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> It was later re-released as part of the complete fourth season DVD in October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seasonfour_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-seasonfour-28">[28]</a></sup> In addition, the 2014 limited edition 12" <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vinyl_record" title="Vinyl record">vinyl record</a> release <i>Marceline the Vampire Queen – Rock the Nightosphere</i> included both "Nuts" and "I Remember You" alongside other songs sung by Marceline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
