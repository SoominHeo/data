<p>"<b>If I Never See Your Face Again</b>" is a song by American <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a> from the June 2008 re-release of the group's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/It_Won%27t_Be_Soon_Before_Long" title="It Won't Be Soon Before Long">It Won't Be Soon Before Long</a></i> (2007). It was also included on the June 2008 <a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_Gone_Bad:_Reloaded" title="Good Girl Gone Bad: Reloaded">re-release of Rihanna's album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_Gone_Bad" title="Good Girl Gone Bad">Good Girl Gone Bad</a></i> (2007). The song was originally included on the standard version of the album without the inclusion of Rihanna. It was written by band members <a href="/wiki/Adam_Levine" title="Adam Levine">Adam Levine</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Valentine_(musician)" title="James Valentine (musician)">James Valentine</a>, with production of the song helmed by <a href="/wiki/Tricky_Stewart" title="Tricky Stewart">Christopher "Tricky" Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Elizondo" title="Mike Elizondo">Mike Elizondo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Endert" title="Mark Endert">Mark Endert</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_%22Spike%22_Stent" title='Mark "Spike" Stent'>Mark "Spike" Stent</a> and Maroon 5. It was released as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extended_Play" title="Extended Play">Extended Play</a> (EP) in Australia on May 22, 2007, and as an official single on May 2, 2008, in the United States.</p>
<p>"If I Never See Your Face Again" was originally intended to be a duet with <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a> and appear on her <i><a href="/wiki/Discipline_(Janet_Jackson_album)" title="Discipline (Janet Jackson album)">Discipline</a></i> album, although label problems interfered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janet_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Janet-1">[1]</a></sup> The collaboration with Rihanna later came to fruition when Levine asked her to add her vocal to the remix version, which she accepted. "If I Never See Your Face Again" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> song featuring <a href="/wiki/Instrumentation" title="Instrumentation">instrumentation</a> provided by <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizers</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>. The song received a mixed response from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>, who praised the song's composition but criticized Levine and Rihanna's vocal performance. The song's accompanying <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was directed by <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Mandler" title="Anthony Mandler">Anthony Mandler</a> and shot on an indoor soundstage in <a href="/wiki/Castaic,_California" title="Castaic, California">Castaic, California</a>. The concept for the video was "high-end erotica".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=If_I_Never_See_Your_Face_Again&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"If I Never See Your Face Again" was written by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Levine" title="Adam Levine">Adam Levine</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Valentine_(musician)" title="James Valentine (musician)">James Valentine</a>, two of the five band members from <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GGGBReloadedNotes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GGGBReloadedNotes-2">[2]</a></sup> Production of the song was helmed by Mike Elizondo, Mike Endert, Mike "Spike" Stent, <a href="/wiki/Tricky_Stewart" title="Tricky Stewart">Christopher "Tricky" Stewart</a> and Maroon 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GGGBReloadedNotes_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GGGBReloadedNotes-2">[2]</a></sup> It was recorded by "Spike" Stent at Conway Studios, <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>, CA; Glenwood Place Studios, <a href="/wiki/Burbank,_California" title="Burbank, California">Burbank</a>, CA and Phantom Studios, <a href="/wiki/Westlake_Village,_California" title="Westlake Village, California">Westlake Village</a>, CA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GGGBReloadedNotes_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-GGGBReloadedNotes-2">[2]</a></sup> The song was originally intended to be a duet with <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a> and appear on her <i><a href="/wiki/Discipline_(Janet_Jackson_album)" title="Discipline (Janet Jackson album)">Discipline</a></i> album, but due to label problems Rihanna later appeared on the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janet_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Janet-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song was included on the standard version of <i><a href="/wiki/It_Won%27t_Be_Soon_Before_Long" title="It Won't Be Soon Before Long">It Won't Be Soon Before Long</a></i> without the inclusion of Rihanna, however, Levine stated in an interview with James Montgomery for <i><a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a></i> whilst on set of the accompanying music video that he wanted to try something different for the album's re-release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTVInterview_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTVInterview-3">[3]</a></sup> The singer continued, saying that he asked Rihanna to do some "bits and pieces" in the recording studio and that it came together very quickly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTVInterview_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MTVInterview-3">[3]</a></sup> Levine stated that if there is "magic" between two artists, then "you don't even have to think about it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTVInterview_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-MTVInterview-3">[3]</a></sup> During the interview, Rihanna confirmed that the song would be the only one to feature a collaboration on her re-release of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Good_Girl_Gone_Bad_(Rihanna_album)" title="Good Girl Gone Bad (Rihanna album)">Good Girl Gone Bad</a></i>, revealing that she had always wanted to work with the group and was honored when they contacted her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTVInterview_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-MTVInterview-3">[3]</a></sup> "If I Never See Your Face Again" was released as a single in the United States on May 2, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iTunesUS_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-iTunesUS-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=If_I_Never_See_Your_Face_Again&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"If I Never See Your Face Again" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Contactmusic_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Contactmusic-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> song,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> which lasts for 3:18 (3 minutes, 18 seconds).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iTunesUS_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-iTunesUS-4">[4]</a></sup> The song was composed using <a href="/wiki/Time_signature" title="Time signature">common time</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Key_(music)" title="Key (music)">key</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ab_minor" title="Ab minor">Ab minor</a>, with a moderate <a href="/wiki/Groove_(music)" title="Groove (music)">groove</a> of 106 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_Sheet_Music.2C_If_I_Never_See_Your_Face_Again_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Digital_Sheet_Music.2C_If_I_Never_See_Your_Face_Again-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Instrumentation" title="Instrumentation">Instrumentation</a> is provided by <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synths</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-8">[8]</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-9">[9]</a></sup> A reviewer for <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> noted that "If I Never See Your Face Again" is complete with "slinky synth insinuations,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-8">[8]</a></sup> whilst Jerome Blakeney for <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> wrote that it is a "guitar drenched" and "synth-crunching" song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-9">[9]</a></sup> Alex Fletcher for <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> commented that the song incorporated a "jagged" use of synthesizers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-DS-10">[10]</a></sup> For the most part of the song, Levine sings in his <a href="/wiki/Falsetto" title="Falsetto">falsetto register</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-DS-10">[10]</a></sup> whilst Rihanna adopts "silky tone".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-DS-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=If_I_Never_See_Your_Face_Again&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon the release of <i>It Won't Be Soon Before Long</i>, <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a> commented on the original version of the song, which does not include guest vocals by Rihanna. Jerome Blakeney for BBC wrote that "If I Never See Your Face Again", along with another <i>It Won't Be Soon Before Long</i> track "<a href="/wiki/Makes_Me_Wonder" title="Makes Me Wonder">Makes Me Wonder</a>", were "monster weapons of mass-marketing aimed at the feet and riding on swathes of string-drenched, synth-crunching disco."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-9">[9]</a></sup> A reviewer for IGN was complimentary of 'If I Never See Your Face Again', writing that it is hard to resist listening to the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-8">[8]</a></sup> The reviewer also noted that Levine appeared to adopt a vocal style reminiscent of techniques employed by <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>, writing "Like Justin Timberlake it's all about the high pitched tenor falsetto and some sinewy grooves."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bill Lamb for <a href="/wiki/About.com" title="About.com">About.com</a> noted that "If I Never See Your Face Again" and "Makes Me Wonder" appeared to be inspired by British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jazz_funk" title="Jazz funk">jazz funk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acid_jazz" title="Acid jazz">acid jazz</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_ensemble" title="Musical ensemble">band</a> <a href="/wiki/Jamiroquai" title="Jamiroquai">Jamiroquai</a>, writing "Any Jamiroquai fan may call foul if they listen to 'If I Never See Your Face Again' or 'Makes Me Wonder'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lamb_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lamb-11">[11]</a></sup> Sal Cinquemani was critical of the song, labeling it as "nasty."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Slant_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Slant-12">[12]</a></sup> Alex Fletcher for Digital Spy disapproved of the collaboration between the band and Rihanna, with specific thoughts on Levine and Rihanna's pairing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-DS-10">[10]</a></sup> Fletcher was critical of their vocal performance, writing that their voices clashed with one another and that "the sexual chemistry radar for the pairing registers at zero."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-DS-10">[10]</a></sup> Fletcher commented about the song further, writing that it should never have left the recording studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-DS-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=If_I_Never_See_Your_Face_Again&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"If I Never See Your Face Again" achieved moderate chart success on singles charts around the world. It debuted on the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">Australian Singles Chart</a> at number 28 June 1, 2008, and peaked at number 11 in its sixth week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Australia_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Australia-14">[14]</a></sup> The song debuted on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_New_Zealand" title="Recording Industry Association of New Zealand">New Zealand Singles Chart</a> at number 37 on June 9, 2008, and peaked at number 21 the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ-15">[15]</a></sup> In Europe, the song debuted and peaked at number 15 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federation_of_the_Italian_Music_Industry" title="Federation of the Italian Music Industry">Italian Singles Chart</a> on July 17, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Italy_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Italy-16">[16]</a></sup> The song remained on the chart for one week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Italy_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Italy-16">[16]</a></sup> It debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Top_40" title="Dutch Top 40">Dutch Singles Chart</a> at number 35 on June 14, 2008, and peaked at number 20 the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Netherlands_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Netherlands-17">[17]</a></sup> The song fluctuated between positions in the twenties and seventies for 13 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Netherlands_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Netherlands-17">[17]</a></sup> It debuted on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tracklisten" title="Tracklisten">Danish Singles Chart</a> at number 36 on July 27, 2008, and peaked at number 31 in its sixth week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Denmark_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Denmark-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>"If I Never See Your Face Again" debuted on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swiss_Music_Charts" title="Swiss Music Charts">Swiss Singles Chart</a> at number 61 on June 29, 2008, and peaked at number 52 the following week, where it remained in its third week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Switzerland_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Switzerland-19">[19]</a></sup> It debuted on the <a href="/wiki/%C3%963_Austria_Top_40" title="Ö3 Austria Top 40">Austrian Singles Chart</a> at number 67 on September 5, 2008, and peaked at number 54 the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Austria_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Austria-20">[20]</a></sup> "If I Never See Your Face Again" debuted and peaked on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> at number 28 on June 14, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UKDebut_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-UKDebut-21">[21]</a></sup> Over the following two weeks, the song descend down the top-forty, before falling out in its third week. The song made a re-entry on the chart at number 36 on July 7, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UKReentry_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-UKReentry-22">[22]</a></sup> In the United States, the song peaked at number 51 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> chart on July 26, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USChartHistory_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-USChartHistory-23">[23]</a></sup> It peaked at number 10 on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Top_40" title="Adult Top 40">Adult Pop Songs</a> chart; number 30 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40_(Pop_Songs)" title="Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)">Pop Songs</a> chart and 21 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Digital_Songs" title="Hot Digital Songs">Hot Digital Songs</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USChartHistory_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-USChartHistory-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>The song's accompanying <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was directed by <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Mandler" title="Anthony Mandler">Anthony Mandler</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianVideo_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianVideo-24">[24]</a></sup> and shot on an indoor soundstage in <a href="/wiki/Castaic,_California" title="Castaic, California">Castaic, California</a> on April 23, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianVideo_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianVideo-24">[24]</a></sup> The concept for the video was "high-end erotica".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianVideo_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianVideo-24">[24]</a></sup> In an interview with James Montgomery for MTV News whilst on set of the music video, Levine provided a summary of what the video would entail, saying: "It's this kind of ultra-glamorous, photography-based, late-'70s/ early-'80s situation. It's really stylish and really beautiful ... It's the most choreographed thing I've ever done, because usually I just get up there and screw around. But with Rihanna, it's completely different and so cool." In response, Rihanna continued to say that "I don't do a lot of videos where I have so much chemistry with the other artist, and this is only my second duet in a video ... It's really intense, because you have to work with each other so much. It's new for me, but I'm enjoying it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianVideo_24-3"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianVideo-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=If_I_Never_See_Your_Face_Again&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The video begins with the sound of a ticking clock and Levine and Rihanna sitting opposite each other at a table, tapping their hands and staring at one and other. As the songs audio begins to play, band members of Maroon 5 start to play their instruments as Rihanna sits and watches them. The table and band scenes are intercut with each other for the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verse_(popular_music)#Verse" title="Verse (popular music)">verse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">chorus</a>. For the second verse, Levine and Rihanna appear to argue and try to ignore the other's advances. Rihanna lays suggestively on a bed while Levine sits on a chair with his head turned in the opposite direction for the <a href="/wiki/Bridge_(music)" title="Bridge (music)">bridge</a>. For the final chorus, the pair appear to reconcile, ending with Levine finally giving in to his temptations and walk to the end of the table where Rihanna sits and he caresses her neck causing Rihanna to lean towards him seductively and the two nearly kiss as the video ends. The video was made available to <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">download digitally</a> via <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on May 12, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iTunesVideoUS_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-iTunesVideoUS-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
