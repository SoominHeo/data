<p><i><b>Britney Spears: In the Zone</b></i> is the fifth video release by American recording artist <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>. Released by <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> on April 6, 2004, it accompanied the album <a href="/wiki/In_the_Zone" title="In the Zone">of the same name</a>. The DVD contains her <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> concert special <i>Britney Spears: In the Zone</i>, as well as footage from live performances to promote the album. It also includes the music videos for "<a href="/wiki/Me_Against_the_Music" title="Me Against the Music">Me Against the Music</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Toxic_(song)" title="Toxic (song)">Toxic</a>" and their respective episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Making_the_Video" title="Making the Video">Making the Video</a></i>, as well as unreleased material, such as several new tracks.</p>
<p>The DVD received positive reviews from critics, with one describing it as "recommended for fans". <i>Britney Spears: In the Zone</i> was certified two-times <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA_certifications" title="RIAA certifications">platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) for shipment of 200,000 copies across United States. It was also certified platinum in Argentina and France, and gold in Australia and Brazil.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britney_Spears:_In_the_Zone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2003, it was announced through <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> that Spears planned to release a DVD in mid-March 2004, with previously unreleased footage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-promo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-promo-1">[1]</a></sup> On February 26, 2004, it was reported by <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> that the release would concentrate on the live performances to promote <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Zone" title="In the Zone">In the Zone</a></i>, such as her performances of "<a href="/wiki/Me_Against_the_Music" title="Me Against the Music">Me Against the Music</a>" and "(I Got That) Boom Boom" from <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">TRL</a></i> and the half-hour surprise appearance at <a href="/wiki/Rain_Nightclub" title="Rain Nightclub">Rain Nightclub</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Palms_Casino_Resort" title="Palms Casino Resort">Palms Casino Resort</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dvdtalk_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dvdtalk-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-3">[3]</a></sup> The DVD also includes <i>Britney Spears: In the Zone</i>, her concert special in <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> that aired on November 17, 2003. Shot in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gotham_Hall" title="Gotham Hall">Gotham Hall</a> in October, the show featured stage settings evoking <i><a href="/wiki/Cabaret_(musical)" title="Cabaret (musical)">Cabaret</a></i>. The release also contains the music videos of "Me Against the Music" and "<a href="/wiki/Toxic_(song)" title="Toxic (song)">Toxic</a>" with their respective episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Making_the_Video" title="Making the Video">Making the Video</a></i>. The North American edition includes two bonus tracks, "Don't Hang Up" and "The Answer", whereas other editions contain the songs "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I%27ve_Just_Begun_(Having_My_Fun)" title="I've Just Begun (Having My Fun)">I've Just Begun (Having My Fun)</a>" and "Girls and Boys".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-3">[3]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also included are a photo gallery and an additional interview. The total running time is 88 minutes. The presentation is offered in the <a href="/wiki/Aspect_ratio_(image)" title="Aspect ratio (image)">aspect ratio</a> of 1.33:1, aside from the "Me Against the Music" video, which is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letterboxed" title="Letterboxed">letterboxed</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dvdtalk_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-dvdtalk-2">[2]</a></sup> The DVD was set to be released on March 23, 2004, but was later moved to April 6, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Sippin' On", under its original title "Tell Me What You're Sippin' On" was initially to be included on select versions of the "In the Zone" DVD but was omitted. Spears later considered including a new version of the song featuring vocals from rapper AC on her fifth studio album "<a href="/wiki/Blackout_(Britney_Spears_album)" title="Blackout (Britney Spears album)">Blackout</a>", with a review of the song from <i>DJ Booth</i> saying "On a possible inclusion to her yet-to-be-titled comeback album (dropping this November), Spears has hooked up with producer Tricky Stewart for the song Sippin' On. The mid-tempo track has the same familiar drum pattern as <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' <a href="/wiki/I_Can_(Nas_song)" title="I Can (Nas song)">I Can</a>; except here lyricism has been replaced by sexiness. After all, come the end of the song Britney does a 'soul clap'." Other reviews noted an influence from <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a> on "Sippin' On".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This version <a href="/wiki/Internet_leak" title="Internet leak">leaked</a> from a "Blackout" demo CD, while the original intended for "In the Zone" <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_unreleased_Britney_Spears_songs" title="List of unreleased Britney Spears songs">surfaced</a> in September 2011. The song, a mid-tempo <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> track, was produced by <a href="/wiki/Tricky_Stewart" title="Tricky Stewart">Tricky Stewart</a> and co-written by Spears.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdon.fi_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdon.fi-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britney_Spears:_In_the_Zone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aaron Beierle of <a href="/wiki/DVD_Talk" title="DVD Talk">DVD Talk</a> highlighted the concert special, but said that the interviews and behind-the-scenes footage "are geared clearly towards promoting the album and, while they achieve that goal, they break up the momentum of the concert terribly." He summarized his review by saying that, "<i>In the Zone</i> does disappoint somewhat with the audio, but the video quality is good. Recommended for fans."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dvdtalk_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-dvdtalk-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> gave the compilation three stars out of five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Britney Spears: In the Zone</i> deduted at the number one on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Music_Videos" title="Top Music Videos">Top Music Videos</a> on April 24, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On May 11, the DVD was certified two-times <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA_certifications" title="RIAA certifications">platinum</a> in the United States by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) for shipments of 200,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaa-11">[11]</a></sup> It was also certified <a href="/wiki/List_of_music_recording_certifications" title="List of music recording certifications">gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a> (ARIA) for shipments of 7,500 units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ariacerti_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ariacerti-12">[12]</a></sup> <i>In the Zone</i> was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Chamber_of_Phonograms_and_Videograms_Producers" title="Argentine Chamber of Phonograms and Videograms Producers">Argentine Chamber of Phonograms and Videograms Producers</a> (CAPIF) for shipments of 30,000 copies in total, as well as gold in Brazil by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Brasileira_dos_Produtores_de_Discos" title="Associação Brasileira dos Produtores de Discos">Associação Brasileira dos Produtores de Discos</a> (ABPD) for shipment of 25,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capif_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-capif-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On June 1, 2005, it was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Asociaci%C3%B3n_Mexicana_de_Productores_de_Fonogramas_y_Videogramas" title="Asociación Mexicana de Productores de Fonogramas y Videogramas">Asociación Mexicana de Productores de Fonogramas y Videogramas</a> (AMPROFON) for shipments of 10,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mex_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-mex-15">[15]</a></sup> <i>In the Zone</i> also received a platinum certification in France, for shipments of 15,000 copies of the video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-france_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-france-16">[16]</a></sup> In Japan, <i>In the Zone</i> peaked at number seven on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> DVD chart, staying on the chart for 29 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
