<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1770">1770</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/1770" title="1770">1770</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1770&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1770&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1770&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1770">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a> – Foundation of <a href="/wiki/Fort_George,_Bombay" title="Fort George, Bombay">Fort George, Bombay</a> laid by Colonel Keating, principal engineer, on the site of the former <a href="/wiki/Dongri_Fort" title="Dongri Fort">Dongri Fort</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_5" title="March 5">March 5</a> – Eleven American men are <a href="/wiki/Boston_Massacre" title="Boston Massacre">shot, five fatally</a>, by British troops in an event that helps starting the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> five years later.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_26" title="March 26">March 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/First_voyage_of_James_Cook" title="First voyage of James Cook">First voyage of James Cook</a>: English explorer Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> and his crew aboard <a href="/wiki/HMS_Endeavour" title="HMS Endeavour">HMS <i>Endeavour</i></a> complete the circumnavigation of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_18" title="April 18">April 18</a> (<a href="/wiki/April_19" title="April 19">April 19</a> by Cook's log)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> 18:00 – First voyage of James Cook: English explorer Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> and his crew become the first recorded Europeans to encounter the eastern coastline of the <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> continent.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_20" title="April 20">April 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgian</a> king <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erekle_II" title="Erekle II">Erekle II</a> defeats the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> forces in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aspindza" title="Battle of Aspindza">battle of Aspindza</a>, despite being abandoned by an ally, Russian General <a href="/wiki/Gottlieb_Heinrich_Totleben" title="Gottlieb Heinrich Totleben">Totleben</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_29" title="April 29">April 29</a> – First voyage of James Cook: Captain Cook drops anchor on <a href="/wiki/HMS_Endeavour" title="HMS Endeavour">HMS <i>Endeavour</i></a> in a wide bay about 16 km (10 mi) south of the present city of <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Australia. Because the young <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Botanist" title="Botanist">botanist</a> on board the ship, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Banks" title="Joseph Banks">Joseph Banks</a>, discovers 30,000 specimens of plant life in the area, 1,600 of them unknown to European science, Cook names the place <a href="/wiki/Botany_Bay" title="Botany Bay">Botany Bay</a> on <a href="/wiki/May_7" title="May 7">May 7</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_7" title="May 7">May 7</a> – Fourteen-year-old <a href="/wiki/Marie_Antoinette" title="Marie Antoinette">Marie Antoinette</a> arrives at the French court.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_16" title="May 16">May 16</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marie_Antoinette" title="Marie Antoinette">Marie Antoinette</a> marries Louis-Auguste (who later becomes King <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">Fireworks</a> lit by Eric Engelbrecht at the wedding of the <a href="/wiki/Crown_prince" title="Crown prince">crown prince</a> of France in Paris cause a fire, killing 132 people.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
</li>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_2">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1771&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1771">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_5" title="January 5">January 5</a> – Great <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kalmyk_people" title="Kalmyk people">Kalmyk</a> (<a href="/wiki/Torghut" title="Torghut">Torghut</a>) Migration under <a href="/wiki/Ubashi_Khan" title="Ubashi Khan">Ubashi Khan</a> from the east bank of the Lower <a href="/wiki/Volga_River" title="Volga River">Volga River</a> back to the homeland of <a href="/wiki/Dzungaria" title="Dzungaria">Dzungaria</a>, at this time under <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a> rule.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_9" title="January 9">January 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Momozono" title="Emperor Go-Momozono">Emperor Go-Momozono</a> accedes to the throne of <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Japan</a>, following his aunt's abdication.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_12" title="February 12">February 12</a> – Upon the death of <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Frederick,_King_of_Sweden" title="Adolf Frederick, King of Sweden">Adolf Frederick</a>, he is succeeded as King of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> by his son <a href="/wiki/Gustav_III_of_Sweden" title="Gustav III of Sweden">Gustav III</a>. At the time, however, Gustav is unaware of this, since he is abroad in Paris. The news of his father's death reaches him about a month later.</li>
<li>March – <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Regulation" title="War of the Regulation">War of the Regulation</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Colony_of_North_Carolina" title="Royal Colony of North Carolina">North Carolina</a> Governor <a href="/wiki/William_Tryon" title="William Tryon">William Tryon</a> raises a militia to put down the long running uprising of backcountry militias against North Carolina's colonial government.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_12" title="March 12">March 12</a> – The <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_General_Assembly" title="North Carolina General Assembly">North Carolina General Assembly</a> establishes <a href="/wiki/Wake_County,_North_Carolina" title="Wake County, North Carolina">Wake County</a> (named for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Margaret_Wake_(North_Carolina)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Margaret Wake (North Carolina) (page does not exist)">Margaret Wake</a>, the wife of North Carolina Royal Governor <a href="/wiki/William_Tryon" title="William Tryon">William Tryon</a>) from portions of <a href="/wiki/Cumberland_County,_North_Carolina" title="Cumberland County, North Carolina">Cumberland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnston_County,_North_Carolina" title="Johnston County, North Carolina">Johnston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_North_Carolina" title="Orange County, North Carolina">Orange</a> counties. Bloomsbury (later known as Wake Courthouse) is made the informal county seat.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_15" title="March 15">March 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Smeatonian_Society_of_Civil_Engineers" title="Smeatonian Society of Civil Engineers">Society of Civil Engineers</a> first meets (in London), the world's oldest engineering society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_11" title="May 11">May 11</a> – War of the Regulation: North Carolina Governor William Tryon marches his militia out of <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough,_North_Carolina" title="Hillsborough, North Carolina">Hillsborough</a> to come to the aid of General Hugh Waddell's beleaguered forces. Tryon's army stops at Alamance Creek, 5 miles (8.0 km) away from the Regulator army.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_16" title="May 16">May 16</a> – War of the Regulation: The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Alamance" title="Battle of Alamance">Battle of Alamance</a> commences after Regulators reject an appeal by Governor Tryon to peacefully disperse. Governor Tryon's forces crush the rebellion, causing many Regulators to move to frontier areas outside of North Carolina.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_23" title="May 23">May 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lanckorona" title="Battle of Lanckorona">Battle of Lanckorona</a>: A force of 4,000 <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russians</a> under <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Suvorov" title="Alexander Suvorov">Alexander Suvorov</a> defeat a <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish</a> formation of 1,300 men.</li>
<li>May – The <a href="/wiki/Three_battles_of_Sarbakusa" title="Three battles of Sarbakusa">Three battles of Sarbakusa</a>: an alliance of three of the most powerful aristocrats of <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> – <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Goshu_of_Amhara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Goshu of Amhara (page does not exist)">Goshu of Amhara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wand_Bewossen" title="Wand Bewossen">Wand Bewossen</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fasil_of_Damot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fasil of Damot (page does not exist)">Fasil of Damot</a> – defeats <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ras_(title)" title="Ras (title)">Ras</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Mikael_Sehul" title="Mikael Sehul">Mikael Sehul</a> and Emperor <a href="/wiki/Tekle_Haymanot_I" title="Tekle Haymanot I">Tekle Haymanot I</a>, taking control of Ethiopia.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/January_17" title="January 17">January 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_Struensee" title="Johann Friedrich Struensee">Johann Friedrich Struensee</a> and Queen <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Matilda_of_Great_Britain" title="Caroline Matilda of Great Britain">Caroline Matilda</a> are arrested, leading to his execution and her banishment from <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.</li>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bretons" title="Bretons">Breton</a>-French explorer <a href="/wiki/Yves-Joseph_de_Kerguelen-Tr%C3%A9marec" title="Yves-Joseph de Kerguelen-Trémarec">Yves-Joseph de Kerguelen-Trémarec</a> discovers the uninhabited <a href="/wiki/Kerguelen_Islands" title="Kerguelen Islands">Kerguelen Islands</a> in the Southern Ocean.</li>

</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_17" title="February 17">February 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/First_Partition_of_Poland" title="First Partition of Poland">first partition of Poland</a> is agreed to by <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a>, later including <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Austria</a>.</li>
<li>May – The <a href="/wiki/Watauga_Association" title="Watauga Association">Watauga Association</a> is formed in East <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_9" title="June 9">June 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Gaspee_Affair" title="Gaspee Affair"><i>Gaspee</i> Affair</a>: In an act of defiance against the British <a href="/wiki/Navigation_Acts" title="Navigation Acts">Navigation Acts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patriot_(American_Revolution)" title="Patriot (American Revolution)">American patriots</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Whipple" title="Abraham Whipple">Abraham Whipple</a> attack and burn the British customs <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">schooner</a> HMS <i>Gaspee</i> off of <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_10" title="June 10">June 10</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Credit_crisis_of_1772" title="Credit crisis of 1772">Credit crisis of 1772</a> is triggered when, following the flight of their partner <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Fordyce" title="Alexander Fordyce">Alexander Fordyce</a> to France, the London banking house of <a href="/wiki/Neal,_James,_Fordyce_and_Down" title="Neal, James, Fordyce and Down">Neal, James, Fordyce and Down</a> (which has been speculating in <a href="/wiki/East_India_Company" title="East India Company">East India Company</a> stock) suspends payment. The resultant panic causes other banks, particularly in Scotland, to fail, extends to Amsterdam and the <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a> of British North America, and threatens the East India Company with bankruptcy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_22" title="June 22">June 22</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Somersett%27s_Case" title="Somersett's Case">Somersett's Case</a>: <a href="/wiki/William_Murray,_1st_Earl_of_Mansfield" title="William Murray, 1st Earl of Mansfield">Lord Mansfield</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Chief_Justice_of_England_and_Wales" title="Lord Chief Justice of England and Wales">Lord Chief Justice of England and Wales</a>, delivers the decision that leads to the end of <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology327_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology327-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_3">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1772&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1772">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_5" title="August 5">August 5</a> – The <a href="/wiki/First_Partition_of_Poland" title="First Partition of Poland">first Partition of the Polish–Lithuanian commonwealth</a> begins.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_12" title="August 12">August 12</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> <a href="/wiki/Mount_Papandayan" title="Mount Papandayan">Mount Papandayan</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Java" title="West Java">West Java</a> erupts and partially collapses, the debris avalanche killing several thousands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_21" title="August 21">August 21</a> – A <a href="/wiki/Revolution_of_1772" title="Revolution of 1772">coup d'état</a> by King <a href="/wiki/Gustav_III_of_Sweden" title="Gustav III of Sweden">Gustav III</a>, is completed by adopting a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Instrument_of_Government_(1772)" title="Instrument of Government (1772)">Constitution</a>, ending <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Liberty" title="Age of Liberty">half a century of parliamentary rule</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and making him an <a href="/wiki/Enlightened_absolutism" title="Enlightened absolutism">enlightened despot</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_1" title="September 1">September 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mission_San_Luis_Obispo_de_Tolosa" title="Mission San Luis Obispo de Tolosa">Mission San Luis Obispo de Tolosa</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/San_Luis_Obispo,_California" title="San Luis Obispo, California">San Luis Obispo, California</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_28" title="October 28">October 28</a> – <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basque</a>–Spanish explorer <a href="/wiki/Domingo_de_Bonechea" title="Domingo de Bonechea">Domingo de Bonechea</a> in the <i>Aguila</i> sights <a href="/wiki/Tauere" title="Tauere">Tauere</a> <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atoll</a> which he names <i>San Simon y Judas</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_2" title="November 2">November 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Adams" title="Samuel Adams">Samuel Adams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Warren" title="Joseph Warren">Joseph Warren</a> form the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Committee_of_correspondence" title="Committee of correspondence">Committee of Correspondence</a></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1773">1773</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/1773" title="1773">1773</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1773&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1773&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_4">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1773&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1773">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_17" title="January 17">January 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Second_voyage_of_James_Cook" title="Second voyage of James Cook">Second voyage of James Cook</a>: <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">Captain Cook</a> in <a href="/wiki/HMS_Resolution_(1771)" title="HMS Resolution (1771)">HMS <i>Resolution</i> (1771)</a> becomes the first European explorer to cross the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circle" title="Antarctic Circle">Antarctic Circle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_18" title="January 18">January 18</a> – The first opera performance in the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish language</a>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thetis_and_Phel%C3%A9e&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thetis and Phelée (page does not exist)">Thetis and Phelée</a></i>, performed by <a href="/wiki/Carl_Stenborg" title="Carl Stenborg">Carl Stenborg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_Olin" title="Elisabeth Olin">Elisabeth Olin</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bollhuset" title="Bollhuset">Bollhuset</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, marks the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Opera" title="Royal Swedish Opera">Royal Swedish Opera</a>.</li>
<li>Spring – Second voyage of James Cook: <a href="/wiki/Tobias_Furneaux" title="Tobias Furneaux">Tobias Furneaux</a> in <a href="/wiki/HMS_Adventure_(1771)" title="HMS Adventure (1771)">HMS <i>Adventure</i> (1771)</a> explores the coast of <a href="/wiki/Van_Diemen%27s_Land" title="Van Diemen's Land">Van Diemen's Land</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_27" title="April 27">April 27</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Tea_Act" title="Tea Act">Tea Act</a>, designed to save the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" title="British East India Company">British East India Company</a> by granting it a monopoly on the <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a> trade, coming into force on <a href="/wiki/May_10" title="May 10">May 10</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology327328_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology327328-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>May – The Parliament of Great Britain passes the <a href="/wiki/Regulating_Act_of_1773" title="Regulating Act of 1773">Regulating Act</a> creating the office of governor general, with an advising council, to exercise political authority over the territory under <a href="/wiki/Company_rule_in_India" title="Company rule in India">British East India Company rule in India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology327328_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology327328-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_8" title="May 8">May 8</a> – In <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> rebels revolt, killing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ali_Bey" title="Ali Bey">Ali Bey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mamluk" title="Mamluk">Mamluk</a> Sultan of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_4">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1773&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1773">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_21" title="July 21">July 21</a> – Under pressure from the Bourbon courts, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_XIV" title="Pope Clement XIV">Pope Clement XIV</a> suppresses the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Jesus" title="Society of Jesus">Society of Jesus</a> (brief <i><a href="/wiki/Dominus_ac_Redemptor" title="Dominus ac Redemptor">Dominus ac Redemptor</a></i>). <a href="/wiki/Joseph_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Joseph II, Holy Roman Emperor">Joseph II, Holy Roman Emperor</a>, expels the order from his territories.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_29" title="July 29">July 29</a> (Feast of St Martha) – <a href="/wiki/1773_Guatemala_earthquake" title="1773 Guatemala earthquake">Guatemala earthquake</a>: Santa Marta <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a>, with an estimated epicentral magnitude of 7.5 M<sub>i</sub>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> strikes <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>; numerous <a href="/wiki/Aftershock" title="Aftershock">aftershocks</a> last until December. The city of <a href="/wiki/Antigua_Guatemala" title="Antigua Guatemala">Antigua Guatemala</a> is virtually destroyed, leading to the decision to move the country's capital to <a href="/wiki/Guatemala_City" title="Guatemala City">La Nueva Guatemala de la Asunción</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_11" title="August 11">August 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Second_voyage_of_James_Cook" title="Second voyage of James Cook">Second voyage of James Cook</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tuamotus" title="Tuamotus">Tuamotus</a>: <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">Captain Cook</a> discovers <a href="/wiki/Tekokota" title="Tekokota">Tekokota</a> which he names as "Doubtful Island".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_12" title="August 12">August 12</a> – Second voyage of James Cook in the Tuamotus: Captain Cook discovers <a href="/wiki/Marutea_Nord" title="Marutea Nord">Marutea Nord</a> which he names as "Furneaux Island".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_11" title="September 11">September 11</a> – <i>The Public Advertiser</i> publishes a satirical essay titled <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Rules_By_Which_A_Great_Empire_May_Be_Reduced_To_A_Small_One" title="s:Rules By Which A Great Empire May Be Reduced To A Small One">Rules By Which A Great Empire May Be Reduced To A Small One</a>,</i> written by <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin Franklin</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_10" title="October 10">October 10</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Boone" title="Daniel Boone">Daniel Boone</a> leads the first attempt by British colonists to establish a settlement in <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a>, but is turned back in an attack by <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a> in which his son is killed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paul_Revere" title="Paul Revere">Paul Revere</a> marries Rachel Walker, his second wife.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_12" title="October 12">October 12</a> – America's first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insane_asylum" title="Insane asylum">insane asylum</a> opens for <i>Persons of Insane and Disordered Minds</i> in <a href="/wiki/Williamsburg,_Virginia" title="Williamsburg, Virginia">Williamsburg, Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_13" title="October 13">October 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomer</a> <a href="/wiki/Charles_Messier" title="Charles Messier">Charles Messier</a> discovers the <i><a href="/wiki/Whirlpool_Galaxy" title="Whirlpool Galaxy">Whirlpool Galaxy</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/Interacting_galaxy" title="Interacting galaxy">interacting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_design_spiral_galaxy" title="Grand design spiral galaxy">grand design spiral galaxy</a> located at a distance of approximately 23 million <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Canes_Venatici" title="Canes Venatici">Canes Venatici</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_14" title="October 14">October 14</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Komisja_Edukacji_Narodowej" title="Komisja Edukacji Narodowej">Komisja Edukacji Narodowej</a> (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a> for <i>Commission for the Education of the People</i>), formed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish-Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth</a>, is considered to be the world's first ministry of education.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_16" title="December 16">December 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Boston_Tea_Party" title="Boston Tea Party">Boston Tea Party</a>: A group of American <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">colonists</a>, dressed as Mohawk Indians, steal aboard ships of the <a href="/wiki/East_India_Company" title="East India Company">East India Company</a> and dump their cargo of tea into <a href="/wiki/Boston_Harbor" title="Boston Harbor">Boston Harbor</a> in protest against British tax policies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology327328_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology327328-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown_3">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1773&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1773">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War,_1768-1774" title="Russo-Turkish War, 1768-1774">Russo-Turkish War, 1768-1774</a>: Russian forces fail to take <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silistria" title="Silistria">Silistria</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emelian_Pugachev" title="Emelian Pugachev">Emelian Pugachev</a> starts <a href="/wiki/Pugachev%27s_Rebellion" title="Pugachev's Rebellion">Pugachev's Rebellion</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russia</a>, attacking and occupying <a href="/wiki/Samara,_Russia" title="Samara, Russia">Samara</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_Harrison" title="John Harrison">John Harrison</a>'s wins the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Longitude_prize" title="Longitude prize">Longitude prize</a> for his invention of the <a href="/wiki/Marine_chronometer" title="Marine chronometer">marine chronometer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hilaire_Rouelle" title="Hilaire Rouelle">Hilaire Rouelle</a> discovers <a href="/wiki/Urea" title="Urea">urea</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Technical_University" title="Istanbul Technical University">Istanbul Technical University</a> is established (under the name of Royal School of Naval Engineering) as the world's first comprehensive institution of higher learning dedicated to engineering education.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, written work begins on the <i><a href="/wiki/Siku_Quanshu" title="Siku Quanshu">Siku Quanshu</a></i>, the largest literary compilation of books in China's history (surpassing the <a href="/wiki/Yongle_Encyclopedia" title="Yongle Encyclopedia">Yongle Encyclopedia</a> of the 15th Century). Upon completion in <a href="/wiki/1782" title="1782">1782</a>, the books are bound in 36,381 volumes (册) with more than 79,000 chapters (卷), comprising about 2.3 million pages, and approximately 800 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_character" title="Chinese character">Chinese characters</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> judge <a href="/wiki/James_Burnett,_Lord_Monboddo" title="James Burnett, Lord Monboddo">James Burnett, Lord Monboddo</a>, begins publication of <i>Of the Origin and Progress of Language</i>, a contribution to <a href="/wiki/Evolutionary_ideas_of_the_Renaissance_and_Enlightenment" title="Evolutionary ideas of the Renaissance and Enlightenment">evolutionary ideas of the Enlightenment</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Gottlieb_Klopstock" title="Friedrich Gottlieb Klopstock">Friedrich Gottlieb Klopstock</a> publishes the last five <a href="/wiki/Canto" title="Canto">cantos</a> of his <a href="/wiki/Epic_poetry" title="Epic poetry">epic poem</a> <i>Der Messias</i> in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_5">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1774&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1774">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_21" title="January 21">January 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_III" title="Mustafa III">Mustafa III</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Ottoman_Sultans" title="List of Ottoman Sultans">Sultan</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> dies and is succeeded by his brother <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Hamid_I" title="Abdul Hamid I">Abdul Hamid I</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_27" title="January 27">January 27</a> – An angry crowd in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> seizes, tars and feathers British customs collector and <a href="/wiki/John_Malcolm_(Loyalist)" title="John Malcolm (Loyalist)">Loyalist John Malcolm</a> for striking a boy and a shoemaker, <a href="/wiki/George_Robert_Twelves_Hewes" title="George Robert Twelves Hewes">George Hewes</a>, with his cane.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_31" title="March 31">March 31</a> – <a href="/wiki/Intolerable_Acts" title="Intolerable Acts">Intolerable Acts</a>: The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Parliament" title="British Parliament">British Parliament</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Port_Act" title="Boston Port Act">Boston Port Act</a>, closing the port of Boston, Massachusetts as punishment for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Tea_Party" title="Boston Tea Party">Boston Tea Party</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_17" title="April 17">April 17</a> – The first avowedly <a href="/wiki/Unitarianism" title="Unitarianism">Unitarian</a> congregation, Essex Street Chapel, is founded in London by <a href="/wiki/Theophilus_Lindsey" title="Theophilus Lindsey">Theophilus Lindsey</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_10" title="May 10">May 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI</a> becomes King of France following the death of his grandfather, <a href="/wiki/Louis_XV_of_France" title="Louis XV of France">Louis XV</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_19" title="May 19">May 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Shakers" title="Shakers">Shakers</a> <a href="/wiki/Ann_Lee" title="Ann Lee">Ann Lee</a> and eight followers sail from Liverpool in England for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colonial_America" title="Colonial America">colonial America</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_2" title="June 2">June 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Intolerable_Acts" title="Intolerable Acts">Intolerable Acts</a>: A new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quartering_Act" title="Quartering Act">Quartering Act</a>, requiring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colonial_America" title="Colonial America">American colonists</a> to provide better housing for British soldiers upon demand, is passed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_16" title="June 16">June 16</a>/<a href="/wiki/June_17" title="June 17">17</a> – English explorer <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> becomes the first European to sight (and name) <a href="/wiki/Palmerston_Island" title="Palmerston Island">Palmerston Island</a> in the Pacific Ocean.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_20" title="June 20">June 20</a> (June 9 <a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a>) – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1768%E2%80%9374)" title="Russo-Turkish War (1768–74)">Russo-Turkish War (1768–74)</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kozludzha" title="Battle of Kozludzha">Battle of Kozludzha</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army" title="Imperial Russian Army">Imperial Russian Army</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Suvorov" title="Alexander Suvorov">Alexander Suvorov</a> routs numerically superior <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_22" title="June 22">June 22</a> – The Parliament of Great Britain passes the <a href="/wiki/Quebec_Act" title="Quebec Act">Quebec Act</a>, setting out rules of governance for the colony of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> in British North America, enlarging its territory and granting freedom of religion for Roman Catholics.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_5">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1774&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1774">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_21" title="July 21">July 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> sign the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_K%C3%BC%C3%A7%C3%BCk_Kaynarca" title="Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca">Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca</a> with Russian victory, ending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1768%E2%80%9374)" title="Russo-Turkish War (1768–74)">six years of war</a>. The treaty gives Russia the right to intervene in Ottoman politics to protect its Christian subjects.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_1" title="August 1">August 1</a> – The element <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> is discovered for the third (and last) time – the second quantitatively following the somewhat earlier work of <a href="/wiki/Carl_Wilhelm_Scheele" title="Carl Wilhelm Scheele">Carl Wilhelm Scheele</a> (1771–1772) – by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Priestley" title="Joseph Priestley">Joseph Priestley</a>, who publishes the fact in <a href="/wiki/1775" title="1775">1775</a> and so names the element and usually gets all the credit because his work was published first.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_4" title="September 4">September 4</a> – English explorer <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> becomes the first European to sight (and name) the island of <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melanesia" title="Melanesia">Melanesia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_5" title="September 5">September 5</a> – The <a href="/wiki/First_Continental_Congress" title="First Continental Congress">First Continental Congress</a> assembles in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_21" title="September 21">September 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/George_Mason" title="George Mason">George Mason</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> found the <a href="/wiki/Fairfax_County,_Virginia" title="Fairfax County, Virginia">Fairfax County</a> <a href="/wiki/Militia" title="Militia">Militia</a> Association, a military unit independent of British control.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_29" title="September 29">September 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Johann_Wolfgang_von_Goethe" title="Johann Wolfgang von Goethe">Johann Wolfgang von Goethe</a>'s semi-autobiographical <a href="/wiki/Epistolary_novel" title="Epistolary novel">epistolary novel</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sorrows_of_Young_Werther" title="The Sorrows of Young Werther">The Sorrows of Young Werther</a></i> (<i>Die Leiden des jungen Werthers</i>) (written January–March) is published anonymously in Leipzig, Germany; it is influential in the <i><a href="/wiki/Sturm_und_Drang" title="Sturm und Drang">Sturm und Drang</a></i> movement and <a href="/wiki/Romanticism" title="Romanticism">Romanticism</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_10" title="October 10">October 10</a>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dunmore%27s_War" title="Dunmore's War">Dunmore's War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Point_Pleasant" title="Battle of Point Pleasant">Battle of Point Pleasant</a>: <a href="/wiki/Cornstalk" title="Cornstalk">Cornstalk</a> is forced to make peace with Dunmore at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Camp_Charlotte" title="Treaty of Camp Charlotte">Treaty of Camp Charlotte</a>, ceding <a href="/wiki/Shawnee" title="Shawnee">Shawnee</a> land claims south of the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_River" title="Ohio River">Ohio</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a>) to <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.</li>
<li>English explorer James Cook becomes the first European to sight (and name) <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Island" title="Norfolk Island">Norfolk Island</a> in the Pacific Ocean, uninhabited at this date.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_21" title="October 21">October 21</a> – The word "Liberty" is first displayed on a flag raised by colonists in <a href="/wiki/Taunton,_Massachusetts" title="Taunton, Massachusetts">Taunton, Massachusetts</a>, in defiance of British rule in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colonial_America" title="Colonial America">Colonial America</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_25" title="October 25">October 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Edenton_Tea_Party" title="Edenton Tea Party">Edenton Tea Party</a> takes place in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, marking the first major gathering of women in support of the American cause.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1775">1775</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/1775" title="1775">1775</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1775&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1775&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<p><br/></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1775&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1775">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a> begins this year, with the first military engagement being the April 19 <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Lexington_and_Concord" title="Battles of Lexington and Concord">Battles of Lexington and Concord</a> on the day after <a href="/wiki/Paul_Revere" title="Paul Revere">Paul Revere</a>'s now-legendary ride. The <a href="/wiki/Second_Continental_Congress" title="Second Continental Congress">Second Continental Congress</a> takes various steps toward organizing an American government, appointing <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> commander-in-chief (June 14), <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin Franklin</a> postmaster general (July 26) and creating a <a href="/wiki/Continental_Navy" title="Continental Navy">Continental Navy</a> (October 13) and a Marine force (November 10) as landing troops for it, but as yet the 13 colonies have not declared independence, and both the British (June 12) and American (July 15) governments make laws. On July 6, Congress issues the <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_the_Causes_and_Necessity_of_Taking_Up_Arms" title="Declaration of the Causes and Necessity of Taking Up Arms">Declaration of the Causes and Necessity of Taking Up Arms</a> and on August 23, King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_III_of_England" title="George III of England">George III of England</a> declares the American colonies in rebellion, announcing it to parliament on November 10. On June 17, two months into the colonial <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Boston" title="Siege of Boston">siege of Boston</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bunker_Hill" title="Battle of Bunker Hill">Battle of Bunker Hill</a>, just north of Boston, British forces are victorious, but only after suffering severe casualties and after Colonial forces run out of ammunition, <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Fort_Ticonderoga" title="Capture of Fort Ticonderoga">Fort Ticonderoga is taken</a> by American forces in New York Colony's northern frontier, and American forces unsuccessfully <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Canada_(1775)" title="Invasion of Canada (1775)">invade Canada</a>, with an attack on <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> defeated by British forces on November 13 and an attack on <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> repulsed December 31.</p>
<p>Human knowledge and mastery over nature advances when <a href="/wiki/James_Watt" title="James Watt">James Watt</a> builds a successful prototype of a steam engine, and a scientific expedition continues as Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> claims the South Georgia and South Sandwich Islands in the south Atlantic Ocean for Britain. Nature's power over humanity is dramatically demonstrated when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independence_Hurricane" title="Independence Hurricane">Independence Hurricane</a> (August 29 – September 13) devastates the east coast of North America, killing 4,173, and when a <a href="/wiki/1775%E2%80%9382_North_American_smallpox_epidemic" title="1775–82 North American smallpox epidemic">smallpox epidemic</a> begins in New England.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_6">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1775&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1775">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>January – The <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy</a> forces the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> to cede <a href="/wiki/Bukovina" title="Bukovina">Bukovina</a> to its rule.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_5" title="January 5">January 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</a> finishes a Sonata for Keyboard in C.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_16" title="January 16">January 16</a>–<a href="/wiki/January_20" title="January 20">20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Second_voyage_of_James_Cook" title="Second voyage of James Cook">Second voyage of James Cook</a>: Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> circumnavigates, makes the first landing on and (on <a href="/wiki/January_17" title="January 17">January 17</a>) takes possession of <a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_and_the_South_Sandwich_Islands#History" title="South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands">South Georgia</a> in the southern Atlantic Ocean for the Kingdom of Great Britain. The following month he discovers the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Sandwich_Islands" title="South Sandwich Islands">South Sandwich Islands</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_9" title="February 9">February 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a> declares the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Massachusetts_Bay" title="Province of Massachusetts Bay">Province of Massachusetts Bay</a> to be in rebellion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_15" title="February 15">February 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_VI" title="Pope Pius VI">Pope Pius VI</a> succeeds <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_XIV" title="Pope Clement XIV">Pope Clement XIV</a> as the 250th pope.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_6" title="March 6">March 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Raghunathrao" title="Raghunathrao">Raghunathrao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peshwa" title="Peshwa">Peshwa</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Maratha_Empire" title="Maratha Empire">Maratha Empire</a> in India, signs the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Surat" title="Treaty of Surat">Treaty of Surat</a> with the British Governor-General <a href="/wiki/Warren_Hastings" title="Warren Hastings">Warren Hastings</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombay" title="Bombay">Bombay</a> ceding the territories of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salsette" title="Salsette">Salsette</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vasai-Virar" title="Vasai-Virar">Bassein</a> to the British <a href="/wiki/East_India_Company" title="East India Company">East India Company</a> along with part of the revenues from <a href="/wiki/Surat" title="Surat">Surat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bharuch" title="Bharuch">Bharuch</a> districts in return for military assistance. This leads to the <a href="/wiki/First_Anglo-Maratha_War" title="First Anglo-Maratha War">First Anglo-Maratha War</a> fought between the British and the Marathas, ending with the Treaty of Salbai in <a href="/wiki/1782" title="1782">1782</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_17" title="March 17">March 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Catherine_the_Great" title="Catherine the Great">Catherine the Great</a> of Russia issues a manifesto prohibiting freed serfs from being returned to <a href="/wiki/Serfdom_in_Russia" title="Serfdom in Russia">serfdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madriaga_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Madriaga-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_23" title="March 23">March 23</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Henry" title="Patrick Henry">Patrick Henry</a>, a delegate to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Virginia_Convention" title="Second Virginia Convention">Second Virginia Convention</a> after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Virginia" title="Commonwealth of Virginia">Virginia</a> <a href="/wiki/House_of_Burgesses" title="House of Burgesses">House of Burgesses</a> was disbanded by the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Virginia" title="Governor of Virginia">Royal Governor</a>, delivers his "<a href="/wiki/Give_me_liberty,_or_give_me_death!" title="Give me liberty, or give me death!">Give me liberty, or give me death!</a>" speech at <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_Episcopal_Church_(Richmond,_Virginia)" title="St. John's Episcopal Church (Richmond, Virginia)">St. John's Church</a> in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_18" title="April 18">April 18</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Paul_Revere" title="Paul Revere">Paul Revere</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Dawes" title="William Dawes">William Dawes</a>, instructed by Dr. <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Warren" title="Joseph Warren">Joseph Warren</a>, ride from Boston to Lexington to warn <a href="/wiki/John_Hancock" title="John Hancock">John Hancock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Adams" title="Samuel Adams">Sam Adams</a> that British forces are coming to take them prisoner and to seize colonial weapons and ammunition in Concord.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_19" title="April 19">April 19</a> – American Revolution: Hostility between Britain and its American colonies explodes into bloodshed at the <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Lexington_and_Concord" title="Battles of Lexington and Concord">Battles of Lexington and Concord</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> igniting the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_9" title="May 9">May 9</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Brunswick,_Maine" title="Brunswick, Maine">Brunswick</a> militiamen commanded by <a href="/wiki/Thompson%27s_War" title="Thompson's War">Samuel Thompson</a> capture <a href="/wiki/Henry_Mowat" title="Henry Mowat">Henry Mowat</a>, captain of <a href="/wiki/HMS_Canceaux_(1764)" title="HMS Canceaux (1764)">HMS <i>Canceaux</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_10" title="May 10">May 10</a>
<ul>
<li>American Revolution: The <a href="/wiki/Second_Continental_Congress" title="Second Continental Congress">Second Continental Congress</a> meets, elects <a href="/wiki/John_Hancock" title="John Hancock">John Hancock</a> president, raises the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a> under <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> as commander and authorizes the colonies to adopt their own <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitutions</a>.</li>
<li>American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Allen" title="Ethan Allen">Ethan Allen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold" title="Benedict Arnold">Benedict Arnold</a>, leading the <a href="/wiki/Green_Mountain_Boys" title="Green Mountain Boys">Green Mountain Boys</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Fort_Ticonderoga" title="Capture of Fort Ticonderoga">capture Fort Ticonderoga</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_14" title="May 14">May 14</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_off_Fairhaven" title="Battle off Fairhaven">Battle off Fairhaven</a>, the first naval engagement of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_17" title="May 17">May 17</a> – American Revolution: The Continental Congress bans trade with Canada.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_11" title="June 11">June 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Machias" title="Battle of Machias">Battle of Machias</a>, the second naval engagement of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_12" title="June 12">June 12</a> – American Revolution:
<ul>
<li>The British forces offer a pardon to all colonists who lay down their arms.</li>
<li>Action by citizens of <a href="/wiki/Machias,_Maine" title="Machias, Maine">Machias, Maine</a>, in capturing British ships recognises the existence of a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Merchant_Marine" title="United States Merchant Marine">United States Merchant Marine</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_14" title="June 14">June 14</a> – American Revolution: The Continental Congress names <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> as commander of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_16" title="June 16">June 16</a> – Post of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Engineer" title="Chief Engineer">Chief Engineer</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a> created.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_17" title="June 17">June 17</a> – American Revolution: Two months into the colonial <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Boston" title="Siege of Boston">siege of Boston</a>, British open fire on <a href="/wiki/Breed%27s_Hill" title="Breed's Hill">Breed's Hill</a> on Charles Town Peninsula. After 3 charges, the British take the hill in the misnamed <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bunker_Hill" title="Battle of Bunker Hill">Battle of Bunker Hill</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_19" title="June 19">June 19</a> – Post of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commanding_General" title="Commanding General">Commanding General</a> was created by the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Congress" title="Continental Congress">Continental Congress</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_25" title="June 25">June 25</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Great_Fire_of_Tartu" title="Great Fire of Tartu">Great Fire of Tartu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Governorate_of_Livonia" title="Governorate of Livonia">Governorate of Livonia</a>, destroys most of the town.</li>
</ul>

</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_3" title="July 3">July 3</a> – American Revolution: George Washington takes command of the 17,000-man Continental Army at Cambridge.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_5" title="July 5">July 5</a> – American Revolution: The Continental Congress sends the <a href="/wiki/Olive_Branch_Petition" title="Olive Branch Petition">Olive Branch Petition</a>, hoping for a reconciliation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_6" title="July 6">July 6</a> – American Revolution: The Continental Congress issues <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_the_Causes_and_Necessity_of_Taking_Up_Arms" title="Declaration of the Causes and Necessity of Taking Up Arms">Declaration of the Causes and Necessity of Taking Up Arms</a>, which contains the words: "Our cause is just. Our union is perfect... being with one mind resolved to die freemen rather than to live slaves...".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_26" title="July 26">July 26</a> – The Second Continental Congress appoints <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin Franklin</a> to be the first <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postmaster_General" title="United States Postmaster General">Postmaster General</a> of what later becomes the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Post_Office_Department" title="United States Post Office Department">United States Post Office Department</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_30" title="July 30">July 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Second_voyage_of_James_Cook" title="Second voyage of James Cook">Second voyage of James Cook</a>: <a href="/wiki/HMS_Resolution_(1771)" title="HMS Resolution (1771)">HMS <i>Resolution</i></a> anchors off the south coast of England, <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">Captain Cook</a> having completed the first eastabout global <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">circumnavigation</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_18" title="August 18">August 18</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tucson" title="Tucson">Tucson</a> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_21" title="August 21">August 21</a> – American Revolution – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Fort_St._Jean" title="Siege of Fort St. Jean">Siege of Fort St. Jean</a>: American rebels launch an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Canada_(1775)" title="Invasion of Canada (1775)">invasion of Canada</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_23" title="August 23">August 23</a> – American Revolution: Refusing to even look at the Olive Branch Petition, King George issues a <a href="/wiki/Proclamation_of_Rebellion" title="Proclamation of Rebellion">Proclamation of Rebellion</a> against the American colonies.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_29" title="August 29">August 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/September_12" title="September 12">September 12</a> – The <i>Independence Hurricane</i> from <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> to <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> kills 4,170, mostly fishermen and sailors.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_8" title="September 8">September 8</a> – The unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/Rising_of_the_Priests" title="Rising of the Priests">Rising of the Priests</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_25" title="September 25">September 25</a> – American Revolution: Siege of Fort St. Jean – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Longue-Pointe" title="Battle of Longue-Pointe">Battle of Longue-Pointe</a>: <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a> revolutionary forces under Maj. <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Allen" title="Ethan Allen">Ethan Allen</a> attack <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> in Quebec, commanded by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> General <a href="/wiki/Guy_Carleton,_1st_Baron_Dorchester" title="Guy Carleton, 1st Baron Dorchester">Guy Carleton</a>. Allen's forces are defeated, and Allen himself is captured and held on British ships until he is later released.</li>
<li>October – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sayre_Plot" title="Sayre Plot">Sayre Plotters</a> attempt to kidnap <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_III_of_Great_Britain" title="George III of Great Britain">George III of Great Britain</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_13" title="October 13">October 13</a> – American Revolution: The Continental Congress orders the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Navy" title="Continental Navy">Continental Navy</a> (later the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_18" title="October 18">October 18</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Burning_of_Falmouth" title="Burning of Falmouth">Burning of Falmouth</a> by ships of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> commanded by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Mowat" title="Henry Mowat">Henry Mowat</a>, who had been humiliated by local militiamen on May 9th.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_26" title="October 26">October 26</a> – American Revolution: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_III" title="George III">George III</a> announces to <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament</a> that the American colonies are in an uprising and must be dealt with accordingly.</li>
<li>November – American Revolution: Colonel <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Richardson_(general)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Richard Richardson (general) (page does not exist)">Richard Richardson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> revolutionaries march through <a href="/wiki/Ninety-Six_District,_South_Carolina" title="Ninety-Six District, South Carolina">Ninety-Six District</a> in what becomes known as the <i>Snow Campaign</i>, effectively ending all major support for the <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Loyalist</a> cause in the <a href="/wiki/Backcountry" title="Backcountry">backcountry</a> of South Carolina.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_10" title="November 10">November 10</a> – American Revolution: The Continental Congress passes a resolution creating the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Marines" title="Continental Marines">Continental Marines</a> to serve as landing troops for the recently created Continental Navy (the Marines are disbanded at end of the war in April <a href="/wiki/1783" title="1783">1783</a> but reformed on <a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a>, <a href="/wiki/1798" title="1798">1798</a> as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_13" title="November 13">November 13</a> – American Revolution – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Fort_St._Jean" title="Siege of Fort St. Jean">Battle of Montreal</a>: American forces under <a href="/wiki/Brigadier_general_(United_States)" title="Brigadier general (United States)">Brigadier General</a> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Montgomery" title="Richard Montgomery">Richard Montgomery</a> capture <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>. British General <a href="/wiki/Guy_Carleton,_1st_Baron_Dorchester" title="Guy Carleton, 1st Baron Dorchester">Guy Carleton</a> escapes to <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_17" title="November 17">November 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Murray,_4th_Earl_of_Dunmore" title="John Murray, 4th Earl of Dunmore">John Murray, 4th Earl of Dunmore</a> offers freedom to slaves who join the loyalist army, thus losing the support of most planters, who see slaves as their vital livelihood.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_5" title="December 5">December 5</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Henry_Knox" title="Henry Knox">Henry Knox</a> begins his journey to <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a> with the artillery that has been captured from <a href="/wiki/Fort_Ticonderoga" title="Fort Ticonderoga">Fort Ticonderoga</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_9" title="December 9">December 9</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Great_Bridge" title="Battle of Great Bridge">Battle of Great Bridge</a>: Victory by the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a> and militia leads to withdrawal of the British from the <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Virginia" title="Colony of Virginia">Colony of Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_31" title="December 31">December 31</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Quebec_(1775)" title="Battle of Quebec (1775)">Battle of Quebec</a>: British forces repulse an attack by Continental Army generals <a href="/wiki/Richard_Montgomery" title="Richard Montgomery">Richard Montgomery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold" title="Benedict Arnold">Benedict Arnold</a> at <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93February">January–February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1776&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1776">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Burning_of_Norfolk" title="Burning of Norfolk">Burning of Norfolk</a>: The town of <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk, Virginia</a>, is destroyed by the combined actions of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> and occupying <a href="/wiki/Patriot_(American_Revolution)" title="Patriot (American Revolution)">Patriot</a> forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_10" title="January 10">January 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>: <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Paine" title="Thomas Paine">Thomas Paine</a> publishes his pamphlet <i><a href="/wiki/Common_Sense_(pamphlet)" title="Common Sense (pamphlet)">Common Sense</a></i> "written by an Englishman" in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> arguing for independence from British rule in the <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_20" title="January 20">January 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>: <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Loyalists</a> led by Robert Cunningham sign a petition from prison agreeing to all demands for peace by the formed state government of South Carolina.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_24" title="January 24">January 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>: <a href="/wiki/Henry_Knox" title="Henry Knox">Henry Knox</a> arrives at <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>, with the artillery that he has transported from <a href="/wiki/Fort_Ticonderoga" title="Fort Ticonderoga">Fort Ticonderoga</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_17" title="February 17">February 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Edward_Gibbon" title="Edward Gibbon">Edward Gibbon</a> publishes the first volume of <i><a href="/wiki/The_History_of_the_Decline_and_Fall_of_the_Roman_Empire" title="The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire">The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_27" title="February 27">February 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Moore%27s_Creek_Bridge" title="Battle of Moore's Creek Bridge">Battle of Moore's Creek Bridge</a>: Scottish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Colony_of_North_Carolina" title="Royal Colony of North Carolina">North Carolina</a> Loyalists charge across Moore's Creek bridge near <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington</a> to attack what they mistakenly believe to be a small force of rebels. Several loyalist leaders are killed in the ensuing battle. The patriot victory<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331-21">[21]</a></sup> virtually ends all British authority in the province.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="March.E2.80.93April">March–April</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1776&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1776">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>March – Restrictions on the <a href="/wiki/Cereal" title="Cereal">cereal</a> trade in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> are lifted.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_2" title="March 2">March 2</a>–<a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">3</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nassau" title="Battle of Nassau">Battle of Nassau</a>: The American <a href="/wiki/Continental_Navy" title="Continental Navy">Continental Navy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Continental_Marines" title="Continental Marines">Marines</a> make a successful assault on <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau, Bahamas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Rice_Boats" title="Battle of the Rice Boats">Battle of the Rice Boats</a>: American <a href="/wiki/Patriot_(American_Revolution)" title="Patriot (American Revolution)">Patriots</a> resist the Royal Navy on the <a href="/wiki/Savannah_River" title="Savannah River">Savannah River</a>. British control over the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Georgia" title="Province of Georgia">Province of Georgia</a> is lost.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_4" title="March 4">March 4</a> – American Revolutionary War: <a href="/wiki/Patriot_(American_Revolution)" title="Patriot (American Revolution)">American Patriots</a> capture <a href="/wiki/Dorchester_Heights" title="Dorchester Heights">Dorchester Heights</a> dominating the port of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_9" title="March 9">March 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> economist <a href="/wiki/Adam_Smith" title="Adam Smith">Adam Smith</a> publishes <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wealth_of_Nations" title="The Wealth of Nations">The Wealth of Nations</a></i> in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_17" title="March 17">March 17</a> – American Revolutionary War: Threatened by <a href="/wiki/Patriot_(American_Revolution)" title="Patriot (American Revolution)">Patriot</a> cannons on <a href="/wiki/Dorchester_Heights" title="Dorchester Heights">Dorchester Heights</a>, the British evacuate <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, ending the 11‑month <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Boston" title="Siege of Boston">Siege of Boston</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_28" title="March 28">March 28</a> – <a href="/wiki/Juan_Bautista_de_Anza" title="Juan Bautista de Anza">Juan Bautista de Anza</a> finds the site for the <a href="/wiki/Presidio_of_San_Francisco" title="Presidio of San Francisco">Presidio of San Francisco</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_12" title="April 12">April 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>: The Royal Colony of North Carolina produces the <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Resolves" title="Halifax Resolves">Halifax Resolves</a> making it the first British colony officially to authorize its <a href="/wiki/Continental_Congress" title="Continental Congress">Continental Congress</a> delegates to vote for independence from the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="May.E2.80.93June">May–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1776&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1776">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_1" title="May 1">May 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Adam_Weishaupt" title="Adam Weishaupt">Adam Weishaupt</a> founds the <a href="/wiki/Illuminati" title="Illuminati">Illuminati</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ingolstadt" title="Ingolstadt">Ingolstadt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_4" title="May 4">May 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> becomes the first American colony to renounce allegiance to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_George_III_of_Great_Britain" title="King George III of Great Britain">King George III of Great Britain</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_15" title="May 15">May 15</a>–<a href="/wiki/May_26" title="May 26">26</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_The_Cedars" title="Battle of The Cedars">Battle of The Cedars</a>: British forces skirmish with the American <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a> around <a href="/wiki/Les_C%C3%A8dres,_Quebec" title="Les Cèdres, Quebec">Les Cèdres, Quebec</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_6" title="June 6">June 6</a> – A fire destroys major parts of the town of <a href="/wiki/Askersund" title="Askersund">Askersund</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_7" title="June 7">June 7</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Richard_Henry_Lee" title="Richard Henry Lee">Richard Henry Lee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> proposes to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Continental_Congress" title="Second Continental Congress">Second Continental Congress</a> (meeting in Philadelphia) that <a href="/wiki/Lee_Resolution" title="Lee Resolution">"these united colonies are, and of right ought to be, free and independent states."</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_8" title="June 8">June 8</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Trois-Rivi%C3%A8res" title="Battle of Trois-Rivières">Battle of Trois-Rivières</a>: The invading American Continental Army is driven back at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trois-Rivi%C3%A8res,_Quebec" title="Trois-Rivières, Quebec">Trois-Rivières, Quebec</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_11" title="June 11">June 11</a> – American Revolution: The <a href="/wiki/Continental_Congress" title="Continental Congress">Continental Congress</a> appoints a <a href="/wiki/Committee_of_Five" title="Committee of Five">Committee of Five</a> to draft a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence" title="United States Declaration of Independence">Declaration of Independence</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_12" title="June 12">June 12</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Declaration_of_Rights" title="Virginia Declaration of Rights">Virginia Declaration of Rights</a> by <a href="/wiki/George_Mason" title="George Mason">George Mason</a> adopted by the Virginia Convention of Delegates.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_15" title="June 15">June 15</a> – American Revolution: Delaware Separation Day: The <a href="/wiki/Delaware_General_Assembly" title="Delaware General Assembly">Delaware General Assembly</a> votes to suspend government under the British Crown.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_17" title="June 17">June 17</a> – Lt. <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Joaqu%C3%ADn_Moraga" title="José Joaquín Moraga">José Joaquín Moraga</a> leads a band of colonists from <a href="/wiki/Monterey,_California" title="Monterey, California">Monterey</a> Presidio, landing on <a href="/wiki/June_29" title="June 29">June 29</a> and, with Father <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pal%C3%B3u" title="Francisco Palóu">Francisco Palóu</a>, constructing the <a href="/wiki/Mission_San_Francisco_de_As%C3%ADs" title="Mission San Francisco de Asís">Mission San Francisco de Asís</a> ("Mission Dolores") of the new Presidio of <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, the oldest surviving building in the modern-day city.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_28" title="June 28">June 28</a> – American Revolutionary War: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sullivan%27s_Island" title="Battle of Sullivan's Island">Battle of Sullivan's Island</a> – <a href="/wiki/Province_of_South_Carolina" title="Province of South Carolina">South Carolina</a> militia repel a British attack on <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_29" title="June 29">June 29</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Turtle_Gut_Inlet" title="Battle of Turtle Gut Inlet">Battle of Turtle Gut Inlet</a> – The American <a href="/wiki/Continental_Navy" title="Continental Navy">Continental Navy</a> successfully challenges the British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> blockade off <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_2" title="July 2">July 2</a> – American Revolution: The final (despite minor revisions) U.S. Declaration of Independence is written. The Continental Congress passes the <a href="/wiki/Lee_Resolution" title="Lee Resolution">Lee Resolution</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_4" title="July 4">July 4</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence" title="United States Declaration of Independence">United States Declaration of Independence</a>: The Continental Congress ratifies the declaration by the United States of its independence from the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pocket_On_This_Day_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_On_This_Day-23">[23]</a></sup></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/July_9" title="July 9">July 9</a> – American Revolution: An angry mob in New York City topples the equestrian statue of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_III_of_Great_Britain" title="George III of Great Britain">George III of Great Britain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bowling_Green_(New_York_City)" title="Bowling Green (New York City)">Bowling Green</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/July_21" title="July 21">July 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Mozart</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Serenade_No._7_(Mozart)" title="Serenade No. 7 (Mozart)">Serenade No. 7</a> (the "Haffner") is first performed in <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a>, Austria.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_29" title="July 29">July 29</a> – Francisco Silvestre Vélez de Escalante, Francisco Atanasio Domínguez, and eight other Spaniards set out from Santa Fe on an eighteen-hundred mile trek through the American Southwest. They are the first Europeans to explore the vast region between the Rockies and the Sierras.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saunt_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saunt-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_2" title="August 2">August 2</a> – Most of the American colonies ratify the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence" title="United States Declaration of Independence">Declaration of Independence</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_15" title="August 15">August 15</a> – American Revolution: First <a href="/wiki/Hessian_(soldier)" title="Hessian (soldier)">Hessian</a> troops land on <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a> to join British forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_27" title="August 27">August 27</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Long_Island" title="Battle of Long Island">Battle of Long Island</a>: <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">Washington</a>'s troops routed in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> by British under <a href="/wiki/William_Howe,_5th_Viscount_Howe" title="William Howe, 5th Viscount Howe">William Howe</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="September.E2.80.93October">September–October</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1776&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="1776">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_1" title="September 1">September 1</a> – Invasion of Cherokee Nation by 6,000 patriot troops from Virginia, North Carolina, and South Carolina begins. The troops destroy thirty-six Cherokee towns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saunt_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Saunt-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_6" title="September 6">September 6</a> – Hurricane hits <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>, killing more than 6,000 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_7" title="September 7">September 7</a> – American Revolution: World's first <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a> attack. American submersible craft <i><a href="/wiki/Turtle_(submersible)" title="Turtle (submersible)">Turtle</a></i> attempts to attach a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_bomb_(explosive)" title="Time bomb (explosive)">time bomb</a> to the hull of British Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Howe" title="Richard Howe">Richard Howe</a>'s flagship <a href="/wiki/HMS_Eagle_(1774)" title="HMS Eagle (1774)">HMS <i>Eagle</i></a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_Harbor" title="New York Harbor">New York Harbor</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_11" title="September 11">September 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Long_Island#Western_Long_Island" title="Battle of Long Island">American Revolution</a>: abortive peace conference between British and Americans on <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_15" title="September 15">September 15</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Landing_at_Kip%27s_Bay" title="Landing at Kip's Bay">Landing at Kip's Bay</a>: British troops land on <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kips_Bay" title="Kips Bay">Kips Bay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_16" title="September 16">September 16</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Harlem_Heights" title="Battle of Harlem Heights">Battle of Harlem Heights</a>: The Continental Army under Washington are victorious against the British on Manhattan.</li>

<ul>

The winner is a filly (later named <a href="/wiki/Allabaculia" title="Allabaculia">Allabaculia</a>) owned by the organiser, the <a href="/wiki/Charles_Watson-Wentworth,_2nd_Marquess_of_Rockingham" title="Charles Watson-Wentworth, 2nd Marquess of Rockingham">2nd Marquess of Rockingham</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Bolshoi_Theatre,_Saint_Petersburg" title="Bolshoi Theatre, Saint Petersburg">Bolshoi Theatre</a> company hosts its first annual <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> season, with the opening of the Bolshoi Kamenny Theatre in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>, Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/October_9" title="October 9">October 9</a> – Father <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pal%C3%B3u" title="Francisco Palóu">Francisco Palóu</a> founds the <a href="/wiki/Mission_San_Francisco_de_As%C3%ADs" title="Mission San Francisco de Asís">Mission San Francisco de Asís</a> in what is now <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_11" title="October 11">October 11</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Valcour_Island" title="Battle of Valcour Island">Battle of Valcour Island</a>: On <a href="/wiki/Lake_Champlain" title="Lake Champlain">Lake Champlain</a> near <a href="/wiki/Valcour_Island" title="Valcour Island">Valcour Island</a>, a British fleet led by Sir <a href="/wiki/Guy_Carleton,_1st_Baron_Dorchester" title="Guy Carleton, 1st Baron Dorchester">Guy Carleton</a> defeats 15 American gunboats commanded by Brigadier General <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold" title="Benedict Arnold">Benedict Arnold</a>. Although nearly all of Arnold's ships are destroyed, the two-day-long battle will give Patriot forces enough time to prepare defenses of New York City.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_18" title="October 18">October 18</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Pell%27s_Point" title="Battle of Pell's Point">Battle of Pell's Point</a>: Troops of the American <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a> resist a British and <a href="/wiki/Hessian_(soldier)" title="Hessian (soldier)">Hessian</a> force in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_28" title="October 28">October 28</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_White_Plains" title="Battle of White Plains">Battle of White Plains</a>: British forces arrive at White Plains, attack and capture Chatterton Hill from the Americans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology330331-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_31" title="October 31">October 31</a> – In his first speech before British Parliament since the Declaration of Independence that summer, King <a href="/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George III of the United Kingdom">George III</a> acknowledges that all is not going well for Britain in the war with the United States.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_16" title="November 16">November 16</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fort_Washington" title="Battle of Fort Washington">Battle of Fort Washington</a> – <a href="/wiki/Hessian_(soldier)" title="Hessian (soldier)">Hessian forces</a> under Lieutenant General <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_von_Knyphausen" title="Wilhelm von Knyphausen">Wilhelm von Knyphausen</a> capture <a href="/wiki/Fort_Washington_(Manhattan)" title="Fort Washington (Manhattan)">Fort Washington (Manhattan)</a> from the American <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a>. The captain of the American navy ship <i><a href="/wiki/Andrew_Doria_(1775_brig)" title="Andrew Doria (1775 brig)">Andrew Doria</a></i> fires a salute to the Dutch flag on Fort Oranje and <a href="/wiki/Johannes_de_Graaff" title="Johannes de Graaff">Johannes de Graaff</a> answers with eleven gun shots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_20" title="November 20">November 20</a> – American Revolution: <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lee_Historic_Park#Battle_of_Fort_Lee" title="Fort Lee Historic Park">Battle of Fort Lee</a> – Invasion of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> by British and Hessian forces and subsequent general retreat of the American Continental Army.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_5" title="December 5">December 5</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Phi_Beta_Kappa_Society" title="Phi Beta Kappa Society">Phi Beta Kappa Society</a> is founded at the <a href="/wiki/College_of_William_%26_Mary" title="College of William &amp; Mary">College of William &amp; Mary</a> in <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_7" title="December 7">December 7</a> – American Revolution: The <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_du_Motier,_Marquis_de_Lafayette" title="Gilbert du Motier, Marquis de Lafayette">Marquis de Lafayette</a> attempts to enter the American military as a major general.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_21" title="December 21">December 21</a> – American Revolution: The Royal Colony of North Carolina reorganizes into the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">State of North Carolina</a> after adopting its own constitution. <a href="/wiki/Richard_Caswell" title="Richard Caswell">Richard Caswell</a> becomes the first governor of the newly formed state.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/January_2" title="January 2">January 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Assunpink_Creek" title="Battle of the Assunpink Creek">Battle of the Assunpink Creek</a>: American general <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a>'s army defeats the British under Lieutenant General <a href="/wiki/Charles_Cornwallis,_1st_Marquess_Cornwallis" title="Charles Cornwallis, 1st Marquess Cornwallis">Charles Cornwallis</a> in a second battle at <a href="/wiki/Trenton,_New_Jersey" title="Trenton, New Jersey">Trenton, New Jersey</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/September_3" title="September 3">September 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cooch%27s_Bridge" title="Battle of Cooch's Bridge">Battle of Cooch's Bridge</a>: British and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hessian_(soldiers)" title="Hessian (soldiers)">Hessian</a> forces defeat American <a href="/wiki/Militia_(United_States)" title="Militia (United States)">militia</a> in a minor skirmish in <a href="/wiki/New_Castle_County,_Delaware" title="New Castle County, Delaware">New Castle County, Delaware</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/October_4" title="October 4">October 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Germantown" title="Battle of Germantown">Battle of Germantown</a>: Troops under <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> are repelled by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> troops under <a href="/wiki/Sir" title="Sir">Sir</a> <a href="/wiki/William_Howe,_5th_Viscount_Howe" title="William Howe, 5th Viscount Howe">William Howe</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/November_15" title="November 15">November 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>: After 16 months of debate, the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Congress" title="Continental Congress">Continental Congress</a> approves the <a href="/wiki/Articles_of_Confederation" title="Articles of Confederation">Articles of Confederation</a> in the temporary American capital at <a href="/wiki/York,_Pennsylvania" title="York, Pennsylvania">York, Pennsylvania</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/December_30" title="December 30">December 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_III_Joseph,_Elector_of_Bavaria" title="Maximilian III Joseph, Elector of Bavaria">Maximilian III Joseph, Elector of Bavaria</a> dies &amp; is succeeded by his distant cousin <a href="/wiki/Charles_Theodore,_Elector_of_Bavaria" title="Charles Theodore, Elector of Bavaria">Charles Theodore, Elector of Bavaria</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/July_10" title="July 10">July 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a> declares war on the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_27" title="July 27">July 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ushant_(1778)" title="Battle of Ushant (1778)">First Battle of Ushant</a>: <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> and French fleets fight to a standoff.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_3" title="August 3">August 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/La_Scala" title="La Scala">La Scala</a> opera house in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> opens with the première of <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Salieri" title="Antonio Salieri">Antonio Salieri</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Europa_riconosciuta" title="Europa riconosciuta">Europa riconosciuta</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_26" title="August 26">August 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/Triglav" title="Triglav">Triglav</a>, at 2,864 meters above sea level the highest peak of <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, is ascended for the first time by four men: Luka Korošec, Matevž Kos, Štefan Rožič, and Lovrenc Willomitzer on <a href="/wiki/Sigmund_Zois" title="Sigmund Zois">Sigmund Zois</a>' initiative.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_29" title="August 29">August 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: The tactically inconclusive <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rhode_Island" title="Battle of Rhode Island">Battle of Rhode Island</a> takes place, after which the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a> abandons its position on <a href="/wiki/Aquidneck_Island" title="Aquidneck Island">Aquidneck Island</a>.</li>
<li>September – The <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Banishment_Act" title="Massachusetts Banishment Act">Massachusetts Banishment Act</a>, providing punishment for <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Loyalists</a>, is passed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_7" title="September 7">September 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: French <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Dominica_(1778)" title="Invasion of Dominica (1778)">invasion of Dominica</a> captures the British fort there before the latter is aware that France has entered the war in the <a href="/wiki/Franco-American_alliance" title="Franco-American alliance">Franco-American alliance</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_17" title="September 17">September 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fort_Pitt" title="Treaty of Fort Pitt">Treaty of Fort Pitt</a> is signed, the first formal treaty between the United States and a <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> tribe (the <a href="/wiki/Lenape" title="Lenape">Lenape</a> or Delaware).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_19" title="September 19">September 19</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Continental_Congress" title="Continental Congress">Continental Congress</a> passes the first <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">budget</a> of the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_26" title="November 26">November 26</a> – In the <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Islands" title="Hawaiian Islands">Hawaiian Islands</a>, Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> becomes the first European to land on <a href="/wiki/Maui" title="Maui">Maui</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_9">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1779&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1779">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_11" title="January 11">January 11</a> – English troops surrender to the <a href="/wiki/Maratha" title="Maratha">Marathas</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wadgaon" title="Wadgaon">Wadgaon</a>, India, and are forced to return all territories acquired since <a href="/wiki/1773" title="1773">1773</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_19" title="January 19">January 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ching-Thang_Khomba" title="Ching-Thang Khomba">Ching-Thang Khomba</a> is crowned <a href="/wiki/List_of_Meitei_kings" title="List of Meitei kings">King</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manipur" title="Manipur">Manipur</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_22" title="January 22">January 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Claudius_Smith" title="Claudius Smith">Claudius Smith</a> is hanged at <a href="/wiki/Goshen_(village),_New_York" title="Goshen (village), New York">Goshen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_New_York" title="Orange County, New York">Orange County, New York</a> for supposed acts of terrorism upon the people of the surrounding communities.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_29" title="January 29">January 29</a> – After a second petition for partition from its residents, the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_General_Assembly" title="North Carolina General Assembly">North Carolina General Assembly</a> abolishes <a href="/wiki/Bute_County,_North_Carolina" title="Bute County, North Carolina">Bute County</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> (established <a href="/wiki/1764" title="1764">1764</a>) by dividing it and naming the northern portion <a href="/wiki/Warren_County,_North_Carolina" title="Warren County, North Carolina">Warren County</a> (for Revolutionary War hero <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Warren" title="Joseph Warren">Joseph Warren</a>) and the southern portion <a href="/wiki/Franklin_County,_North_Carolina" title="Franklin County, North Carolina">Franklin County</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin Franklin</a>). The General Assembly also establishes <a href="/wiki/Warrenton,_North_Carolina" title="Warrenton, North Carolina">Warrenton</a> (also named for Joseph Warren) to be the county seat of Warren County and <a href="/wiki/Louisburg,_North_Carolina" title="Louisburg, North Carolina">Louisburg</a> (named for <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a>) to be the county seat of Franklin County.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_12" title="February 12">February 12</a> – Lieutenant Colonel <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Bouligny" title="Francisco Bouligny">Francisco Bouligny</a> arrives with <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga" title="Málaga">Malagueño</a> colonists at <a href="/wiki/Bayou_Teche" title="Bayou Teche">Bayou Teche</a> to establish the city of <a href="/wiki/New_Iberia,_Louisiana" title="New Iberia, Louisiana">New Iberia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_14" title="February 14">February 14</a> – Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> is killed on the <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Islands" title="Hawaiian Islands">Sandwich Islands</a> on his third and last voyage.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_10" title="March 10">March 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Aynal%C4%B1kavak" title="Treaty of Aynalıkavak">Treaty of Aynalıkavak</a> between <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Turkey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> about <a href="/wiki/Crimean_Khanate" title="Crimean Khanate">Crimean Khanate</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_13" title="May 13">May 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Bavarian_Succession" title="War of the Bavarian Succession">War of the Bavarian Succession</a>: Russian and French mediators at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congress_of_Teschen" title="Congress of Teschen">Congress of Teschen</a> negotiate an end to the war. In the agreement Austria receives a part of the Bavarian territory (the <a href="/wiki/Innviertel" title="Innviertel">Innviertel</a>) and relinquishes the rest.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_1" title="June 1">June 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold" title="Benedict Arnold">Benedict Arnold</a> is <a href="/wiki/Court-martial" title="Court-martial">court-martialed</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malfeasance" title="Malfeasance">malfeasance</a> in his treatment of government property.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_16" title="June 16">June 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: In support of the U.S., Spain declares war on Britain.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_9">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1779&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1779">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_16" title="July 16">July 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: United States forces led by General <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Wayne" title="Anthony Wayne">Anthony Wayne</a> capture <a href="/wiki/Stony_Point,_New_York" title="Stony Point, New York">Stony Point, New York</a> from British troops.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_20" title="July 20">July 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Tekle_Giyorgis_I" title="Tekle Giyorgis I">Tekle Giyorgis I</a> begins the first of his five reigns as <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Ethiopia" title="Emperor of Ethiopia">Emperor of Ethiopia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_22" title="July 22">July 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Minisink" title="Battle of Minisink">Battle of Minisink</a>: The Goshen Militia is destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Brant" title="Joseph Brant">Joseph Brant</a>'s forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_24" title="July 24">July 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: American forces led by Commodore <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Saltonstall" title="Dudley Saltonstall">Dudley Saltonstall</a> launch the <a href="/wiki/Penobscot_Expedition" title="Penobscot Expedition">Penobscot Expedition</a> in what is now <a href="/wiki/Castine,_Maine" title="Castine, Maine">Castine</a>, Maine, resulting in the worst naval defeat in U.S. history until <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor" title="Pearl Harbor">Pearl Harbor</a>.</li>
<li>July – The <a href="/wiki/Great_Siege_of_Gibraltar" title="Great Siege of Gibraltar">Great Siege of Gibraltar</a> (fourteenth and last military siege) starts. This is an action by French and Spanish forces to wrest control of <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> from the established British garrison. The garrison, led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Augustus_Eliott" title="George Augustus Eliott">George Augustus Eliott</a>, later 1st Baron Heathfield of Gibraltar, survives all attacks and a blockade of supplies.</li>
</ul>

<li>September – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Baton_Rouge_(1779)" title="Battle of Baton Rouge (1779)">Battle of Baton Rouge</a>: Spanish troops under <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_de_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Bernardo de Gálvez">Bernardo de Gálvez</a> capture the city from the British.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_14" title="September 14">September 14</a> - <a href="/wiki/September_15" title="September 15">September 15</a> - <a href="/wiki/Little_Beard%27s_Town" title="Little Beard's Town">Little Beard's Town</a> is burnt by the <a href="/wiki/Sullivan_Expedition" title="Sullivan Expedition">Sullivan Expedition</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_23" title="September 23">September 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Flamborough_Head" title="Battle of Flamborough Head">Battle of Flamborough Head</a>: The American ship <i><a href="/wiki/USS_Bonhomme_Richard_(1765)" title="USS Bonhomme Richard (1765)">Bonhomme Richard</a></i>, commanded by <a href="/wiki/John_Paul_Jones" title="John Paul Jones">John Paul Jones</a>, engages the British ship <i><a href="/wiki/HMS_Serapis_(1779)" title="HMS Serapis (1779)">HMS Serapis</a></i>. The <i>Bonhomme Richard</i> sinks, but the Americans board the <i>Serapis</i> and other vessels, and are victorious.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_4" title="October 4">October 4</a> – The Fort Wilson Riot against <a href="/wiki/James_Wilson" title="James Wilson">James Wilson</a> and others in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> takes place.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_2" title="November 2">November 2</a> – The North Carolina General Assembly carves a new county from <a href="/wiki/Dobbs_County,_North_Carolina" title="Dobbs County, North Carolina">Dobbs County</a>, North Carolina and names it <a href="/wiki/Wayne_County,_North_Carolina" title="Wayne County, North Carolina">Wayne County</a> in honor of United States General <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Wayne" title="Anthony Wayne">Anthony Wayne</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_13" title="December 13">December 13</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandre,_Vicomte_de_Beauharnais" title="Alexandre, Vicomte de Beauharnais">Alexandre, Vicomte de Beauharnais</a> marries <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9phine_de_Beauharnais" title="Joséphine de Beauharnais">Joséphine Tascher</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_22" title="December 22">December 22</a> – American Revolutionary War: <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Savannah" title="Capture of Savannah">Capture of Savannah</a> – British forces under <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Campbell_(British_Army_officer,_born_1739)" title="Archibald Campbell (British Army officer, born 1739)">Archibald Campbell</a> take the city of <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_25" title="December 25">December 25</a> – Fort Nashborough, later to become <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, is founded by <a href="/wiki/James_Robertson_(explorer)" title="James Robertson (explorer)">James Robertson</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_31" title="December 31">December 31</a> – <a href="/wiki/Affair_of_Fielding_and_Bylandt" title="Affair of Fielding and Bylandt">Affair of Fielding and Bylandt</a>, a brief naval engagement between the British and Dutch off the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight</a>.</li>
</ul>
