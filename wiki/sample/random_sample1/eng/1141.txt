<p><i><b>Intermission</b></i> is a compilation album by <a href="/wiki/Power_metal" title="Power metal">power metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Stratovarius" title="Stratovarius">Stratovarius</a>, released on 26 June 2001 through <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Blast" title="Nuclear Blast">Nuclear Blast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finnishcharts_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-finnishcharts-1">[1]</a></sup> The album consists of <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">covers</a>, live and bonus tracks, as well as four new tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">[3]</a></sup> It charted in the top 100 in four countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finnishcharts_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-finnishcharts-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Antti J. Ravelin at <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> gave <i>Intermission</i> three stars out of five, calling it "just an attempt to please fans and to postpone the making of their next studio album", but that "oddly enough, <i>Intermission</i> succeeds to offer a very album-like (a strong and steady whole) compilation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
