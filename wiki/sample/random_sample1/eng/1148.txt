<p><a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police#Ranks" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">Superintendent</a> <b>J. Greg Peters</b> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Royal Victorian Order">MVO</a></span> is a retired member of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">Royal Canadian Mounted Police</a> and the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usher_of_the_Black_Rod_of_the_Senate_of_Canada" title="Usher of the Black Rod of the Senate of Canada">Usher of the Black Rod</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Canada" title="Senate of Canada">Senate of Canada</a>. He was appointed to the position by order of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor-in-Council" title="Governor-in-Council">Governor-in-Council</a> effective 1 October 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J._Greg_Peters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A native of <a href="/wiki/List_of_municipalities_in_Prince_Edward_Island" title="List of municipalities in Prince Edward Island">Souris West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, John Gregory Peters was heavily involved in volunteering for <a href="/wiki/St._John_Ambulance" title="St. John Ambulance">St. John Ambulance</a> as a youth, teaching first aid and CPR in the local community. He was later certified in advanced first aid<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Peters went on to study political science and history at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Prince_Edward_Island" title="University of Prince Edward Island">University of Prince Edward Island</a>, later completing his undergraduate degree in political science at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Ottawa" title="University of Ottawa">University of Ottawa</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. He has also completed a graduate certificate in public sector leadership and governance at the University of Ottawa<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J._Greg_Peters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peters joined the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">Royal Canadian Mounted Police</a> in 1981 and trained at the <a href="/wiki/RCMP_Academy,_Depot_Division" title="RCMP Academy, Depot Division">RCMP Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Regina,_Saskatchewan" title="Regina, Saskatchewan">Regina</a>. Early years saw him go from municipal policing in <a href="/wiki/Flin_Flon" title="Flin Flon">Flin Flon, Manitoba</a> to small town and Aboriginal policing in southern Manitoba. In 1984, he was assigned to protective duties for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_tours_of_Canada" title="Royal tours of Canada">royal tour</a> of Her Majesty <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a> to Manitoba.</p>
<p>In 1987, Peters was accepted to the RCMP equestrian drill team, the <a href="/wiki/Musical_Ride" title="Musical Ride">Musical Ride</a>. His first tour was Europe for a period of three months in 1988, staying as a guest at <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Castle" title="Windsor Castle">Windsor Castle</a> and stabling in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Mews" title="Royal Mews">Royal Mews</a>. This was his first intimate encounter with Her Majesty The Queen and members of the Royal Family.</p>
<p>In 1993, Peters served with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Police" title="United Nations Police">United Nations Civilian Police</a> contingent in <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>. He was one of the first 25 RCMP members deployed, and served as spokesperson for the contingent. From 1997 to 1998, Peters was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Law_Enforcement_Training_Centers" title="Federal Law Enforcement Training Centers">Federal Law Enforcement Training Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Glynco,_Georgia" title="Glynco, Georgia">Glynco, Georgia</a>, where he facilitated senior police leadership sessions for local, state, and federal law enforcement agencies throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</p>
<p>In 2002, Peters led the design and development of the RCMP Musical Ride Centre at the RCMP Stables at Rockcliffe, Ottawa. He organized the official opening of the Centre by Her Majesty <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a> on 14 October 2002 to celebrate The Queen's <a href="/wiki/Golden_Jubilee_of_Elizabeth_II#Canada" title="Golden Jubilee of Elizabeth II">Golden Jubilee</a>.</p>
<p>In 2007, as the RCMP contingent commander, Peters led the ceremonial and protocol functions for the official arrival of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a> and Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Harper" title="Stephen Harper">Stephen Harper</a> for the ceremony commemorating the 90th anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vimy_Ridge" title="Battle of Vimy Ridge">Battle of Vimy Ridge</a>.</p>
<p>As the Director of Strategic Partnerships and Heritage for the RCMP, Peters was a key player in the repatriation of the <a href="/wiki/Sam_Steele" title="Sam Steele">Sir Sam Steele</a> collection from the United Kingdom to Canada in 2008. The collection, which was at risk of being broken up and auctioned off, now rests with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alberta" title="University of Alberta">University of Alberta</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Glenbow_Museum" title="Glenbow Museum">Glenbow Museum</a>.</p>
<p>Peters led the RCMP guard of honour at the Canada Reception Centre for official visits to Canada by foreign heads of state and heads of government. He also led the ceremonial salute at the <a href="/wiki/Embassy_of_Canada,_Washington,_D.C." title="Embassy of Canada, Washington, D.C.">Embassy of Canada, Washington, D.C.</a> for the inauguration of <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> as <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> in <a href="/wiki/First_inauguration_of_Barack_Obama" title="First inauguration of Barack Obama">2009</a> and <a href="/wiki/Second_inauguration_of_Barack_Obama" title="Second inauguration of Barack Obama">2013</a>.</p>
<p>Peters organized the presentation of RCMP horses to The Queen in 2009 and 2012, and played a significant role in royal tours to Canada by Her Majesty The Queen and His Royal Highness <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">The Duke of Edinburgh</a> in 2010, Their Royal Highnesses <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">The Prince of Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Camilla,_Duchess_of_Cornwall" title="Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall">The Duchess of Cornwall</a> in 2009 and 2012, as well as Their Royal Highnesses <a href="/wiki/Prince_William,_Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Prince William, Duke of Cambridge">The Duke of Cambridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catherine,_Duchess_of_Cambridge" title="Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge">The Duchess of Cambridge</a> in 2011.</p>
<p>Peters served as the RCMP Diamond Jubilee contingent commander at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Windsor_Horse_Show" title="Royal Windsor Horse Show">Royal Windsor Horse Show</a> tribute to The Queen in 2012, and the historic mounting of the <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Guard" title="Queen's Guard">Queenâs Life Guard</a> by the RCMP in London on 23 May 2012. This marked the first time that a non-British semi-military unit formed the Queenâs Life Guard since the 17th century.</p>
<p>Peters was the only Canadian named to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Royal Victorian Order">Royal Victorian Order</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2013_New_Year_Honours" title="2013 New Year Honours">2013 New Year Honours</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Peters retired from the RCMP on 30 September 2013 at the rank of superintendent, after 32 years of service. His appointment as Usher of the Black Rod took effect on 1 October 2013.</p>
