<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Template:Events_by_year_for_decade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="Template:Events by year for decade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1790">1790</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/1790" title="1790">1790</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1790&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1790&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<p><br/></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1790&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1790">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_8" title="January 8">January 8</a> – United States President <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> gives the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_of_the_Union_address" title="State of the Union address">State of the Union address</a>, in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_11" title="January 11">January 11</a> – 11 minor states of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Netherlands" title="Austrian Netherlands">Austrian Netherlands</a> which took part in the <a href="/wiki/Brabant_Revolution" title="Brabant Revolution">Brabant Revolution</a> at the end of 1789 sign a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Union_(United_States_of_Belgium)" title="Treaty of Union (United States of Belgium)">Treaty of Union</a> creating the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_Belgium" title="United States of Belgium">United States of Belgium</a>. Prime Minister of Great Britain <a href="/wiki/William_Pitt_the_Younger" title="William Pitt the Younger">William Pitt</a> refuses to recognize the new confederation's independence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_26" title="January 26">January 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Mozart</a>'s opera <i><a href="/wiki/Cos%C3%AC_fan_tutte" title="Così fan tutte">Così fan tutte</a></i> premiered in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_30" title="January 30">January 30</a> – The first boat specialized as a <a href="/wiki/Lifeboat_(rescue)" title="Lifeboat (rescue)">rescue lifeboat</a> is tested on the <a href="/wiki/River_Tyne" title="River Tyne">River Tyne</a> in England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_1" title="February 1">February 1</a> – In New York City the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">Supreme Court of the United States</a> convenes for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-overview_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-overview-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_4" title="February 4">February 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a> declares to the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(French_Revolution)" title="National Assembly (French Revolution)">National Assembly</a> that he will maintain the <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitutional</a> laws.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_11" title="February 11">February 11</a> – Two <a href="/wiki/Quakers" title="Quakers">Quaker</a> delegates petition the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_States" title="Abolitionism in the United States">abolition</a> of <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a> – The <a href="/wiki/1790_United_States_Census" title="1790 United States Census">first United States Census</a> is authorized; it is held later in the year.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_4" title="March 4">March 4</a> – France is divided into 83 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%C3%A9partement" title="Département">départements</a>, which cut across the former <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a>, in an attempt to dislodge regional loyalties based on noble ownership of land.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_6" title="March 6">March 6</a> – The legislature of New York consents to the <a href="/wiki/Admission_to_the_Union" title="Admission to the Union">admission to the Union</a> of a new state, <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>, formed within the boundaries of New York, contingent upon the successful conclusion of negotiations concerning disputed real-estate claims and the boundary between the two states.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_21" title="March 21">March 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a> reports to President George Washington in New York as the new <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State" title="United States Secretary of State">United States Secretary of State</a>.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/April_10" title="April 10">April 10</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_patent_law" title="United States patent law">United States patent system</a> is established.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_13" title="May 13">May 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Reval" title="Battle of Reval">Battle of Reval</a>: <a href="/wiki/Gustav_III_of_Sweden" title="Gustav III of Sweden">Gustav III of Sweden</a> sends the battlefleet to eliminate the Russian squadron wintering at Reval (<a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>), but is defeated: 8 Russians, 51 Swedes are killed, 250 captured, and 2 ships are sunk.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_17" title="May 17">May 17</a>–18 – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Andros_(1790)" title="Battle of Andros (1790)">Battle of Andros</a>: an <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman_Algiers" title="Ottoman Algiers">Algerian</a> fleet destroys the fleet of the Greek privateer <a href="/wiki/Lambros_Katsonis" title="Lambros Katsonis">Lambros Katsonis</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_29" title="May 29">May 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> ratifies the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" title="United States Constitution">United States Constitution</a> and becomes the last of the <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">13 original states</a> to do so.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_9" title="June 9">June 9</a> – Royal assent is given to establishment of the port of <a href="/wiki/Milford_Haven" title="Milford Haven">Milford Haven</a> in Wales.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_20" title="June 20">June 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Compromise_of_1790" title="Compromise of 1790">Compromise of 1790</a>: Thomas Jefferson, <a href="/wiki/James_Madison" title="James Madison">James Madison</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton" title="Alexander Hamilton">Alexander Hamilton</a> come to an agreement: Madison agrees to not be "strenuous" in opposition for the assumption of state debts by the federal government; Hamilton agrees to support the capital site being above the Potomac.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_23" title="June 23">June 23</a> – The alleged <i><a href="/wiki/London_Monster" title="London Monster">London Monster</a></i> is arrested in London: he later receives 40 years for 10 assaults.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1790&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1790">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>July – <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a> accepts a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_9" title="July 9">July 9</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Swedish_War_(1788%E2%80%9390)" title="Russo-Swedish War (1788–90)">Russo-Swedish War</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Svensksund" title="Second Battle of Svensksund">Second Battle of Svensksund</a>: In a massive <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> battle of 300 ships, the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Navy" title="Swedish Navy">Swedish Navy</a> captures one third of the Russian galley fleet: 304 Swedes are killed, 3,500 Russians killed and 6,000 captured, 51 Russian galleys and other rowing craft are sunk and 22 are taken.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_12" title="July 12">July 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Civil_Constitution_of_the_Clergy" title="Civil Constitution of the Clergy">Civil Constitution of the Clergy</a> is passed. This completes the destruction of the monastic orders, legislating out of existence all regular and secular chapters for either sex, abbacies and priorships.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_14" title="July 14">July 14</a> – French Revolution: Citizens of Paris celebrate the unity of the French people and the national reconciliation in the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%AAte_de_la_F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration" title="Fête de la Fédération">Fête de la Fédération</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_16" title="July 16">July 16</a> – The signing of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Residence_Bill" title="Residence Bill">Residence Bill</a> establishes a site along the <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">Potomac River</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">District of Columbia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Capital_districts_and_territories" title="Capital districts and territories">capital district</a> of the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_26" title="July 26">July 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton" title="Alexander Hamilton">Alexander Hamilton</a>'s Assumption Bill, giving effect to his <a href="/wiki/First_Report_on_the_Public_Credit" title="First Report on the Public Credit">First Report on the Public Credit</a>, is passed in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a>, allowing the federal government to assume the consolidated debts of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_27" title="July 27">July 27</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Reichenbach_(1790)" title="Treaty of Reichenbach (1790)">Convention of Reichenbach</a> is signed between <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a> and Austria.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_31" title="July 31">July 31</a> – Inventor <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Hopkins_(inventor)" title="Samuel Hopkins (inventor)">Samuel Hopkins</a> becomes the first to be issued a U.S. patent (for an improved method of making <a href="/wiki/Potash" title="Potash">potash</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_4" title="August 4">August 4</a> – A newly passed U.S. <a href="/wiki/Tariff" title="Tariff">tariff</a> act creates the system of <a href="/wiki/Cutter_(boat)" title="Cutter (boat)">cutters</a> for revenue enforcement (later named the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Revenue_Cutter_Service" title="United States Revenue Cutter Service">United States Revenue Cutter Service</a>), the forerunner of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">Coast Guard</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_14" title="August 14">August 14</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_V%C3%A4r%C3%A4l%C3%A4" title="Treaty of Värälä">Treaty of Värälä</a> ends the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Swedish_War_(1788%E2%80%9390)" title="Russo-Swedish War (1788–90)">Russo-Swedish War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_25" title="September 25">September 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Peking_opera" title="Peking opera">Peking opera</a> is born when the Four Great Anhui Troupes introduce <a href="/wiki/Huiju" title="Huiju">Anhui opera</a> to Beijing in honor of the <a href="/wiki/Qianlong_Emperor" title="Qianlong Emperor">Qianlong Emperor</a>'s eightieth birthday</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_30" title="September 30">September 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Leopold_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Leopold II, Holy Roman Emperor">Leopold II, Holy Roman Emperor</a> starts to rule.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_7" title="October 7">October 7</a> – Commissioners appointed by the legislature of New York announce the successful conclusion of negotiations between New York and <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> concerning disputed real-estate claims and the consent of the legislature to the <a href="/wiki/Admission_to_the_Union" title="Admission to the Union">admission to the Union</a> of the state of Vermont, which was formed within what New York claimed as its territory under an <a href="/wiki/Order_in_Council" title="Order in Council">Order in Council</a> that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_George_III" title="King George III">King George III</a> issued on July 20, 1764.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November" title="November">November</a> – <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman</a> forces recapture the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Netherlands" title="Austrian Netherlands">Austrian Netherlands</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_10" title="December 10">December 10</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Hawkesbury_and_Nepean_Wars" title="Hawkesbury and Nepean Wars">Hawkesbury and Nepean Wars</a> begin in New South Wales, Australia as a result of deterioration in relations and increasing colonization.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_11" title="December 11">December 11</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1787%E2%80%9392)" title="Russo-Turkish War (1787–92)">Russo-Turkish War (1787–92)</a>: 26,000 Turkish soldiers lose their lives during <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Suvorov" title="Alexander Suvorov">Alexander Suvorov</a>'s storm of <a href="/wiki/Izmail" title="Izmail">Izmail</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_2">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1791&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1791">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_12" title="January 12">January 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman</a> troops reenter <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège">Liège</a>, heralding the end of the <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge_Revolution" title="Liège Revolution">Liège Revolution</a> and the restoration of its <a href="/wiki/Prince-Bishopric_of_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Prince-Bishopric of Liège">Prince-Bishops</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_25" title="January 25">January 25</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">British Parliament</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Act_1791" title="Constitutional Act 1791">Constitutional Act 1791</a>, splitting the old <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_Quebec_(1763%E2%80%9391)" title="Province of Quebec (1763–91)">province of Quebec</a> into <a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lower_Canada" title="Lower Canada">Lower Canada</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_21" title="February 21">February 21</a> – The United States opens <a href="/wiki/Portugal%E2%80%93United_States_relations" title="Portugal–United States relations">diplomatic relations</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Portugal" title="Kingdom of Portugal">Portugal</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_2" title="March 2">March 2</a> – In France:
<ul>
<li>Abolition of <a href="/wiki/Guild" title="Guild">guilds</a> is enacted.</li>
<li>A mechanical <a href="/wiki/Semaphore_line" title="Semaphore line">semaphore line</a> for rapid long-distance communication is demonstrated by <a href="/wiki/Claude_Chappe" title="Claude Chappe">Claude Chappe</a> in Paris.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_4" title="March 4">March 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> is <a href="/wiki/Admission_to_the_Union" title="Admission to the Union">admitted</a> as the 14th <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_13" title="March 13">March 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Paine" title="Thomas Paine">Thomas Paine</a>'s chief work <i><a href="/wiki/Rights_of_Man" title="Rights of Man">Rights of Man</a></i> (first part) is published in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HF_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HF-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>March – In France, the <a href="/wiki/National_Constituent_Assembly" title="National Constituent Assembly">National Constituent Assembly</a> accepts the recommendation of its Commission of Weights and Measures that the nation should adopt the <a href="/wiki/Metric_system" title="Metric system">metric system</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_21" title="April 21">April 21</a> – The first of forty <a href="/wiki/Boundary_Markers_of_the_Original_District_of_Columbia" title="Boundary Markers of the Original District of Columbia">boundary stones</a> delineating the borders of the new <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">District of Columbia</a> in the United States is laid at <a href="/wiki/Jones_Point_Light" title="Jones Point Light">Jones Point Light</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Virginia" title="Alexandria, Virginia">Alexandria, Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_3" title="May 3">May 3</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Sejm_of_the_Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Sejm of the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Sejm of the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a> proclaims the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_May_3,_1791" title="Constitution of May 3, 1791">Constitution of May 3, 1791</a>, the first modern codified <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> in Europe.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_20" title="June 20">June 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Flight_to_Varennes" title="Flight to Varennes">Flight to Varennes</a>: The French Royal Family is captured when they try to flee in disguise.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_21" title="June 21">June 21</a> – Foundation date of the <a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey" title="Ordnance Survey">Ordnance Survey</a> in Great Britain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_2">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1791&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1791">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_8" title="July 8">July 8</a> – Composer <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Haydn" title="Joseph Haydn">Joseph Haydn</a> is awarded an honorary doctorate of music at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_14" title="July 14">July 14</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Priestley_Riots" title="Priestley Riots">Priestley Riots</a> against <a href="/wiki/Dissenter" title="Dissenter">Dissenters</a> break out in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_17" title="July 17">July 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Champ_de_Mars" title="Champ de Mars">Champ de Mars</a> Massacre occurs during the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_4" title="August 4">August 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Sistova" title="Treaty of Sistova">Treaty of Sistova</a> is signed, ending the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Habsburg_wars" title="Ottoman–Habsburg wars">Ottoman–Habsburg wars</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_6" title="August 6">August 6</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg_Gate" title="Brandenburg Gate">Brandenburg Gate</a> in Berlin is finished.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_21" title="August 21">August 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Revolution" title="Haitian Revolution">Haitian Revolution</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Slave_rebellion" title="Slave rebellion">slave rebellion</a> breaks out in the French colony of <a href="/wiki/Saint-Domingue" title="Saint-Domingue">Saint-Domingue</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_26" title="August 26">August 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Fitch_(inventor)" title="John Fitch (inventor)">John Fitch</a> is granted a patent for the <a href="/wiki/Steamboat" title="Steamboat">steamboat</a> in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_27" title="August 27">August 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tellicherry" title="Battle of Tellicherry">Battle of Tellicherry</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Anglo-Mysore_War" title="Third Anglo-Mysore War">Third Anglo-Mysore War</a>) off the south-west coast of India: a British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> patrol forces a French convoy bound for <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Mysore" title="Kingdom of Mysore">Mysore</a> to surrender.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_6" title="September 6">September 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Opera_seria" title="Opera seria">opera seria</a></i> <i><a href="/wiki/La_clemenza_di_Tito" title="La clemenza di Tito">La clemenza di Tito</a></i> premières at the <a href="/wiki/Estates_Theatre" title="Estates Theatre">Estates Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> to mark the coronation of <a href="/wiki/Leopold_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Leopold II, Holy Roman Emperor">Leopold II</a> as King of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bohemia" title="Kingdom of Bohemia">Bohemia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_13" title="September 13">September 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a> accepts the final version of the completed constitution.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_25" title="September 25">September 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mission_Santa_Cruz" title="Mission Santa Cruz">Mission Santa Cruz</a> is founded by Father <a href="/wiki/Ferm%C3%ADn_Lasu%C3%A9n" title="Fermín Lasuén">Fermín Lasuén</a>, becoming the 12th mission in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/California_mission" title="California mission">California mission</a> chain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_28" title="September 28">September 28</a> – Promulgation of the law on <a href="/wiki/Jewish_emancipation" title="Jewish emancipation">Jewish emancipation</a> in France, the first such legislation in modern Europe.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_30" title="September 30">September 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Singspiel" title="Singspiel">singspiel</a> opera <i><a href="/wiki/The_Magic_Flute" title="The Magic Flute">The Magic Flute</a></i> (<i>Die Zauberflöte</i>) premières at the <a href="/wiki/Theater_auf_der_Wieden" title="Theater auf der Wieden">Freihaus-Theater auf der Wieden</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>.</li>
<li>October – The <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_(France)" title="Legislative Assembly (France)">Legislative Assembly (France)</a> convenes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_9" title="October 9">October 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mission_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Soledad" title="Mission Nuestra Señora de la Soledad">Mission Nuestra Señora de la Soledad</a> is founded by Father <a href="/wiki/Ferm%C3%ADn_Lasu%C3%A9n" title="Fermín Lasuén">Fermín Lasuén</a>, becoming the 13th mission in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/California_mission" title="California mission">California mission</a> chain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_28" title="October 28">October 28</a> – Publication of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Declaration_of_the_Rights_of_Woman_and_the_Female_Citizen" title="Declaration of the Rights of Woman and the Female Citizen">Declaration of the Rights of Woman and the Female Citizen</a> in France.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_4" title="December 4">December 4</a> – The first issue of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Observer" title="The Observer">The Observer</a></i>, the world's first Sunday newspaper, is published in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_5" title="December 5">December 5</a> – Austrian composer <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</a> dies aged 35 at his home in Vienna, perhaps of acute <a href="/wiki/Rheumatic_fever" title="Rheumatic fever">rheumatic fever</a>, and is buried two days later.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_15" title="December 15">December 15</a> – Ratification by the states of the first ten amendments to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" title="United States Constitution">United States Constitution</a> is completed, creating the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Bill_of_Rights" title="United States Bill of Rights">United States Bill of Rights</a>. Two additional amendments remain pending, and one of these is finally ratified in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, becoming the <a href="/wiki/Twenty-seventh_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Twenty-seventh Amendment to the United States Constitution">Twenty-seventh Amendment</a>.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>The first American ship reaches Japan.</li>
<li>An ordinance is written barring the game of baseball within 80 yards of the Meeting House in <a href="/wiki/Pittsfield,_Massachusetts" title="Pittsfield, Massachusetts">Pittsfield, Massachusetts</a> (first known reference to the game of baseball in North America).</li>
<li>The first serious secondary education school open to girls in Denmark, the <i><a href="/wiki/D%C3%B8treskolen_af_1791" title="Døtreskolen af 1791">Døtreskolen af 1791</a></i>, is founded in Copenhagen.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Royal_School_for_the_Blind,_Liverpool" title="Royal School for the Blind, Liverpool">School for the Indigent Blind</a>, the oldest continuously operating specialist school of its kind in the world, is founded in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, England, by blind ex-merchant seaman, writer and <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Abolitionism in the United Kingdom">abolitionist</a> <a href="/wiki/Edward_Rushton" title="Edward Rushton">Edward Rushton</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Casbah_of_Algiers" title="Casbah of Algiers">Casbah of Algiers</a> Palace is completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WDL_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WDL-5">[5]</a></sup></li>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93March">January–March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1792&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1792">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_9" title="January 9">January 9</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Jassy" title="Treaty of Jassy">Treaty of Jassy</a> ends the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>'s war with the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> over <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_20" title="February 20">February 20</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Postal_Service_Act" title="Postal Service Act">Postal Service Act</a>, establishing the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">United States Post Office Department</a>, is signed by President <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Francis_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor">Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor</a>, the last emperor, takes office.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_16" title="March 16">March 16</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Gustav_III_of_Sweden" title="Gustav III of Sweden">Gustav III</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> is shot in the back by <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Johan_Anckarstr%C3%B6m" title="Jacob Johan Anckarström">Jacob Johan Anckarström</a> at a midnight <a href="/wiki/Masquerade_ball" title="Masquerade ball">masquerade</a> at the Royal Opera in Stockholm; he lives until March 29, and is then succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Gustav_IV_Adolf_of_Sweden" title="Gustav IV Adolf of Sweden">Gustav IV Adolf</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_20" title="March 20">March 20</a> – A new capital of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> and county seat of the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Wake_County,_North_Carolina" title="Wake County, North Carolina">Wake County</a> is established after North Carolina <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Senator" title="State Senator">State Senator</a> and surveyor William Christmas submits his design for the city. A few months later the capital is officially named <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">Raleigh</a> in honor of Sir <a href="/wiki/Walter_Raleigh" title="Walter Raleigh">Walter Raleigh</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93June">April–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1792&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1792">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_2" title="April 2">April 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Coinage_Act_of_1792" title="Coinage Act of 1792">Coinage Act</a> is passed, establishing the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Mint" title="United States Mint">United States Mint</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_5" title="April 5">April 5</a> – United States President <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> <a href="/wiki/Veto" title="Veto">vetoes</a> a bill designed to apportion representatives among <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a>. This is the first time the presidential veto is used in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_20" title="April 20">April 20</a> – France declares war against Austria, beginning the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_21" title="April 21">April 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Tiradentes" title="Tiradentes">Tiradentes</a>, prime figure in the <a href="/wiki/Inconfid%C3%AAncia_Mineira" title="Inconfidência Mineira">Inconfidência Mineira</a> plot, is executed in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_25" title="April 25">April 25</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Highwayman" title="Highwayman">Highwayman</a> <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Jacques_Pelletier" title="Nicolas Jacques Pelletier">Nicolas Pelletier</a> becomes the first person executed by <a href="/wiki/Guillotine" title="Guillotine">guillotine</a> in France.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/La_Marseillaise" title="La Marseillaise">La Marseillaise</a></i>, the French <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a>, is composed by <a href="/wiki/Claude_Joseph_Rouget_de_Lisle" title="Claude Joseph Rouget de Lisle">Claude Joseph Rouget de Lisle</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_11" title="May 11">May 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Robert_Gray%27s_Columbia_River_expedition" title="Robert Gray's Columbia River expedition">Robert Gray's Columbia River expedition</a>: Captain <a href="/wiki/Robert_Gray_(sea_captain)" title="Robert Gray (sea captain)">Robert Gray</a> on the <i><a href="/wiki/Columbia_Rediviva" title="Columbia Rediviva">Columbia Rediviva</a></i> becomes the first white man to enter the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_17" title="May 17">May 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Buttonwood_Agreement" title="Buttonwood Agreement">Buttonwood Agreement</a> is signed, beginning the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_18" title="May 18">May 18</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_in_Defence_of_the_Constitution" title="War in Defence of the Constitution">War in Defence of the Constitution</a>: Russia invades Poland.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_21" title="May 21">May 21</a> – An old lava dome collapses in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a>, Japan when <a href="/wiki/Mount_Unzen" title="Mount Unzen">Mount Unzen</a> <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> erupts; the resulting <a href="/wiki/Avalanche" title="Avalanche">avalanche</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> kills about 14,300 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_1" title="June 1">June 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a> becomes the 15th state of the United States of America.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – Captain <a href="/wiki/George_Vancouver" title="George Vancouver">George Vancouver</a> claims <a href="/wiki/Puget_Sound" title="Puget Sound">Puget Sound</a> for Great Britain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_13" title="June 13">June 13</a>
<ul>
<li>Vancouver becomes the first European to enter <a href="/wiki/Burrard_Inlet" title="Burrard Inlet">Burrard Inlet</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a> declares war against France.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93September">July–September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1792&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1792">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_18" title="July 18">July 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Russian_War_of_1792" title="Polish–Russian War of 1792">Polish–Russian War</a>: At the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dubienka" title="Battle of Dubienka">Battle of Dubienka</a>, soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Tadeusz_Ko%C5%9Bciuszko" title="Tadeusz Kościuszko">Tadeusz Kościuszko</a> resist an attack from <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army" title="Imperial Russian Army">Imperial Russian Army</a> forces five times their size.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_10" title="August 10">August 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Tuileries_Palace" title="Tuileries Palace">Tuileries Palace</a> is stormed, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a> is arrested and taken into custody.</li>
<li>September – <a href="/wiki/Macartney_Embassy" title="Macartney Embassy">Macartney Embassy</a>: <a href="/wiki/George_Macartney,_1st_Earl_Macartney" title="George Macartney, 1st Earl Macartney">George Macartney, 1st Earl Macartney</a>, sails from <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth" title="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</a> in <a href="/wiki/HMS_Lion_(1777)" title="HMS Lion (1777)">HMS <i>Lion</i></a> as the first official envoy from the Great Britain to China.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_2" title="September 2">September 2</a>–<a href="/wiki/September_7" title="September 7">7</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>: During what become known as the <a href="/wiki/September_Massacres" title="September Massacres">September Massacres</a>, rampaging mobs slaughter three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishops</a> and more than 200 <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priests</a>, together with at least a thousand criminals.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_11" title="September 11">September 11</a> – Six men steal some of the former <a href="/wiki/French_Crown_Jewels" title="French Crown Jewels">French Crown Jewels</a> from a warehouse where the revolutionary government had stored them.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_14" title="September 14">September 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Paine" title="Thomas Paine">Thomas Paine</a> flees from England to France after being indicted for treason. He is <a href="/wiki/Trial_in_absentia" title="Trial in absentia">tried <i>in absentia</i></a> during December and <a href="/wiki/Outlaw" title="Outlaw">outlawed</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH232233_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH232233-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_20" title="September 20">September 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Valmy" title="Battle of Valmy">Battle of Valmy</a>: The French revolutionary army defeats Prussians under the <a href="/wiki/Charles_William_Ferdinand,_Duke_of_Brunswick-Wolfenb%C3%BCttel" title="Charles William Ferdinand, Duke of Brunswick-Wolfenbüttel">Duke of Brunswick</a> after a 7-hour artillery duel.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_21" title="September 21">September 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_abolition_of_the_monarchy" title="Proclamation of the abolition of the monarchy">Proclamation of the abolition of the monarchy</a> by the French Convention and establishment of the <a href="/wiki/French_First_Republic" title="French First Republic">French First Republic</a> with effect from the following day.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_22" title="September 22">September 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">Era</a> of the historical <a href="/wiki/French_Republican_Calendar" title="French Republican Calendar">French Republican Calendar</a> begins.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="October.E2.80.93December">October–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1792&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="1792">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_12" title="October 12">October 12</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Day" title="Columbus Day">Columbus Day</a> celebration in the United States is held in New York City, 300 years after his arrival in the New World.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_13" title="October 13">October 13</a> – Foundation of <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>: The cornerstone of the United States Executive Mansion, known as the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> after <a href="/wiki/1818" title="1818">1818</a>, is laid.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_29" title="October 29">October 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mount_Hood" title="Mount Hood">Mount Hood</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>) is named after the British Admiral <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Hood,_1st_Viscount_Hood" title="Samuel Hood, 1st Viscount Hood">Lord Hood</a> by <a href="/wiki/William_Robert_Broughton" title="William Robert Broughton">Lt. William Broughton</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Expedition" title="Vancouver Expedition">Vancouver Expedition</a>, who spots the mountain near the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Willamette_River" title="Willamette River">Willamette River</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_3" title="December 3">December 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> is re-elected President of the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_26" title="December 26">December 26</a> – The trial of <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a> begins.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown_2">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1792&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="1792">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baptist_Missionary_Society" title="Baptist Missionary Society">Baptist Missionary Society</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/Kettering" title="Kettering">Kettering</a>, England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tipu_Sultan" title="Tipu Sultan">Tipu Sultan</a> invades <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a> in India, but is repulsed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Franz_Xaver_von_Zach" title="Franz Xaver von Zach">Franz Xaver von Zach</a>, an astronomer, publishes <i>The Tables of the Sun</i>, an essential early work for navigation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Claude_Chappe" title="Claude Chappe">Claude Chappe</a> successfully demonstrates the first <a href="/wiki/Semaphore_line" title="Semaphore line">semaphore line</a>, between Paris and Lille.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Murdoch" title="William Murdoch">William Murdoch</a> begins experimenting with <a href="/wiki/Gas_lighting" title="Gas lighting">gas lighting</a>.</li>
<li>George Anschutz constructs the first <a href="/wiki/Blast_furnace" title="Blast furnace">blast furnace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Holcroft" title="Thomas Holcroft">Thomas Holcroft</a> produces the play <i>Road to Ruin</i> in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Barth%C3%A9lemy_Catherine_Joubert" title="Barthélemy Catherine Joubert">Barthélemy Catherine Joubert</a>, later general, becomes sub-lieutenant.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Johann_Georg_Albrechtsberger" title="Johann Georg Albrechtsberger">Johann Georg Albrechtsberger</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/Kapellmeister" title="Kapellmeister">Kapellmeister</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/State_Street_Corporation" title="State Street Corporation">State Street Corporation</a> is founded.</li>
<li>Shiloh Meeting House, predecessor of Shiloh United Methodist Church in Lynchburg, Virginia, is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mary_Wollstonecraft" title="Mary Wollstonecraft">Mary Wollstonecraft</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Vindication_of_the_Rights_of_Woman" title="A Vindication of the Rights of Woman">A Vindication of the Rights of Woman</a></i> is published.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_3">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1793&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1793">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_7" title="January 7">January 7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Ebel_riot" title="Ebel riot">Ebel riot</a> in Sweden.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_9" title="January 9">January 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Blanchard" title="Jean-Pierre Blanchard">Jean-Pierre Blanchard</a> becomes the first to fly in a <a href="/wiki/Gas_balloon" title="Gas balloon">gas balloon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_21" title="January 21">January 21</a> – After being found guilty of treason by the French <a href="/wiki/National_Convention" title="National Convention">National Convention</a>, <i>Citizen Capet</i>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Guillotine" title="Guillotine">guillotined</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_23" title="January 23">January 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Second_Partition_of_Poland" title="Second Partition of Poland">Second Partition of Poland</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a> <a href="/wiki/Partition_(politics)" title="Partition (politics)">partition</a> the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_1" title="February 1">February 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a>: France declares war on <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Netherlands</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_25" title="February 25">February 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> holds the first <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_(government)" title="Cabinet (government)">Cabinet</a> meeting as President of the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_27" title="February 27">February 27</a> – The Giles resolutions are introduced to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a>, asking the House to condemn <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton" title="Alexander Hamilton">Alexander Hamilton</a>'s handling of loans.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a>–<a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">3</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Langdon_(politician)" title="John Langdon (politician)">John Langdon</a> serves as <a href="/wiki/President_pro_tempore_of_the_United_States_Senate" title="President pro tempore of the United States Senate">President pro tempore of the United States Senate</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_4" title="March 4">March 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> is <a href="/wiki/Second_inauguration_of_George_Washington" title="Second inauguration of George Washington">sworn in</a> as President of the United States in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> for his second term.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_5" title="March 5">March 5</a> – French troops are defeated by Austrian forces and <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège">Liège</a> is recaptured.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_7" title="March 7">March 7</a> – France declares war on Spain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_18" title="March 18">March 18</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republican</a> state in Germany, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Mainz" title="Republic of Mainz">Republic of Mainz</a>, is declared by <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Joseph_Hofmann" title="Andreas Joseph Hofmann">Andreas Joseph Hofmann</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_6" title="April 6">April 6</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Committee_of_Public_Safety" title="Committee of Public Safety">Committee of Public Safety</a> is established in France with <a href="/wiki/Georges_Danton" title="Georges Danton">Georges Danton</a> as its head.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_25" title="April 25">April 25</a> – The pioneer parishes of <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> are erected as well as incorporated into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Louisiana_and_the_Two_Floridas" title="Diocese of Louisiana and the Two Floridas">Diocese of Louisiana and the Two Floridas</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_22" title="April 22">April 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> signs the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neutrality_Proclamation" title="Neutrality Proclamation">Neutrality Proclamation</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_31" title="May 31">May 31</a> – Regular troops under <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hanriot" title="François Hanriot">François Hanriot</a> demand that the <a href="/wiki/Girondins" title="Girondins">Girondins</a> be expelled from the national convention.</li>
<li>June – The <a href="/wiki/Macartney_Embassy" title="Macartney Embassy">Macartney Embassy</a>, a British diplomatic mission to China led by <a href="/wiki/George_Macartney,_1st_Earl_Macartney" title="George Macartney, 1st Earl Macartney">George Macartney, 1st Earl Macartney</a>, reaches <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Canton</a>, but will be rebuffed by the <a href="/wiki/Qianlong_Emperor" title="Qianlong Emperor">Qianlong Emperor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_People.27s_Chronology_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_People.27s_Chronology-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_2" title="June 2">June 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Girondins" title="Girondins">Girondins</a> are overthrown in France.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_10" title="June 10">June 10</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Jardin_des_Plantes" title="Jardin des Plantes">Jardin des Plantes</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9um_national_d%27histoire_naturelle" title="Muséum national d'histoire naturelle">Muséum national d'histoire naturelle</a> are created by the National Convention. The museum opens in Paris the following year and the garden houses one of the first public <a href="/wiki/Zoo" title="Zoo">zoos</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_3">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1793&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1793">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_9" title="July 9">July 9</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Act_Against_Slavery" title="Act Against Slavery">Act Against Slavery</a> is passed in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper Canada</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_13" title="July 13">July 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Corday" title="Charlotte Corday">Charlotte Corday</a> kills <a href="/wiki/Jean-Paul_Marat" title="Jean-Paul Marat">Jean-Paul Marat</a> in his bath.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_17" title="July 17">July 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Corday" title="Charlotte Corday">Charlotte Corday</a> is executed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_20" title="July 20">July 20</a> – Scottish explorer <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Mackenzie_(explorer)" title="Alexander Mackenzie (explorer)">Alexander Mackenzie</a>'s 1792–1793 Peace River expedition to the Pacific Ocean reaches its goal at <a href="/wiki/Bella_Coola,_British_Columbia" title="Bella Coola, British Columbia">Bella Coola, British Columbia</a>, making him the first known person to complete a transcontinental crossing of northern North America.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_29" title="July 29">July 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Graves_Simcoe" title="John Graves Simcoe">John Graves Simcoe</a> decides to build a fort and settlement at Toronto, having sailed into the bay there.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_1" title="August 1">August 1</a>–November 9 – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yellow_fever_epidemic_of_1793" title="Yellow fever epidemic of 1793">yellow fever epidemic of 1793</a> hits <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, Pennsylvania; 5,000 die.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_10" title="August 10">August 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> – Feast of Unity: Crowds in Paris burn <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monarchist" title="Monarchist">monarchist</a> emblems.
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Louvre" title="Louvre">Louvre</a> is officially opened in Paris, France.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_23" title="August 23">August 23</a> – The following universal <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">conscription</a> decree is enacted in France: "The young men shall go to battle and the married men shall forge arms. The women shall make tents and clothes and shall serve in the hospitals; children shall tear rags into lint. The old men will be guided to the public places of the cities to kindle the courage of the young warriors and to preach the unity of the Republic and the hatred of kings."</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_5" title="September 5">September 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> – The <a href="/wiki/National_Convention" title="National Convention">National Convention</a> begins the 10-month <a href="/wiki/Reign_of_Terror" title="Reign of Terror">Reign of Terror</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_5" title="October 5">October 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a>: <a href="/wiki/Raid_on_Genoa" title="Raid on Genoa">Raid on Genoa</a> – The British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Naval_boarding" title="Naval boarding">boards</a> and captures French warships sheltering in the neutral port of <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_15" title="October 15">October 15</a>–<a href="/wiki/October_16" title="October 16">16</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Wattignies" title="Battle of Wattignies">Battle of Wattignies</a> – A French Republican force commanded by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Jourdan" title="Jean-Baptiste Jourdan">Jean-Baptiste Jourdan</a> compels a <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg</a> Austrian Coalition army to retire.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_16" title="October 16">October 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>: <a href="/wiki/Marie_Antoinette" title="Marie Antoinette">Marie Antoinette</a>, the widowed <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">queen consort</a> of <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI of France</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Guillotine" title="Guillotine">guillotined</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Place_de_la_R%C3%A9volution" title="Place de la Révolution">Place de la Révolution</a> in Paris at the conclusion of a 2-day trial before the <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_Tribunal" title="Revolutionary Tribunal">Revolutionary Tribunal</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_24" title="October 24">October 24</a> – The <a href="/wiki/French_Republican_Calendar" title="French Republican Calendar">French Republican Calendar</a> is adopted by the <a href="/wiki/National_Convention" title="National Convention">National Convention</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_10" title="November 10">November 10</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dechristianisation_of_France_during_the_French_Revolution" title="Dechristianisation of France during the French Revolution">dechristianisation of France during the French Revolution</a> reaches a climax with the celebration of the Goddess "Reason" in the cathedral of <a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_de_Paris" title="Notre Dame de Paris">Notre Dame de Paris</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_8" title="December 8">December 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/Madame_du_Barry" title="Madame du Barry">Madame du Barry</a> is executed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_9" title="December 9">December 9</a> – New York City's first daily newspaper, the <i>American Minerva</i>, is established by <a href="/wiki/Noah_Webster" title="Noah Webster">Noah Webster</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_18" title="December 18">December 18</a> – French forces under <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Fran%C3%A7ois_Dugommier" title="Jacques François Dugommier">Dugommier</a> <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Toulon" title="Siege of Toulon">capture Toulon</a> from royalists and British forces under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice_Admiral" title="Vice Admiral">Vice Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Hood,_1st_Viscount_Hood" title="Samuel Hood, 1st Viscount Hood">Lord Hood</a>. The British fire the dockyards and take 16 ships, one of which, the <i><a href="/wiki/HMS_Lutine_(1779)" title="HMS Lutine (1779)">Lutine</a></i>, becomes a famous treasure ship.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_23" title="December 23">December 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/War_in_the_Vend%C3%A9e" title="War in the Vendée">War in the Vendée</a> (<a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>): <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Savenay" title="Battle of Savenay">Battle of Savenay</a> – A Republican force decisively defeats the counterrevolutionary <a href="/wiki/Catholic_and_Royal_Army" title="Catholic and Royal Army">Catholic and Royal Army</a>, ending the <a href="/wiki/Vir%C3%A9e_de_Galerne" title="Virée de Galerne">Virée de Galerne</a>.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/March_14" title="March 14">March 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Eli_Whitney" title="Eli Whitney">Eli Whitney</a> is granted a <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Cotton_gin" title="Cotton gin">cotton gin</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/April_4" title="April 4">April 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rac%C5%82awice" title="Battle of Racławice">Battle of Racławice</a>: Polish supporters of the <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%9Bciuszko_Uprising" title="Kościuszko Uprising">Kościuszko Uprising</a> defeat forces of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_5" title="April 5">April 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>: <a href="/wiki/Georges_Danton" title="Georges Danton">Georges Danton</a> is executed.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/April_29" title="April 29">April 29</a>–<a href="/wiki/May_1" title="May 1">May 1</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Boulou" title="Battle of Boulou">Battle of Boulou</a>: The French defeat the Spanish and Portuguese forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_7" title="May 7">May 7</a> – French Revolution: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robespierre" title="Robespierre">Robespierre</a> establishes the <a href="/wiki/Cult_of_the_Supreme_Being" title="Cult of the Supreme Being">Cult of the Supreme Being</a> as the new <a href="/wiki/State_religion" title="State religion">state religion</a> of the <a href="/wiki/French_First_Republic" title="French First Republic">French First Republic</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_8" title="May 8">May 8</a> – French Revolution: Chemist <a href="/wiki/Antoine_Lavoisier" title="Antoine Lavoisier">Antoine Lavoisier</a> is executed by <a href="/wiki/Guillotine" title="Guillotine">guillotine</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_18" title="May 18">May 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tourcoing" title="Battle of Tourcoing">Battle of Tourcoing</a>: French troops defeat British forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_28" title="May 28">May 28</a>–<a href="/wiki/June_1" title="June 1">June 1</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Glorious_First_of_June" title="Glorious First of June">Glorious First of June</a> (Battle of Ushant): British and French ships battle to a draw.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> troops capture <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a> in <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_17" title="June 17">June 17</a>

<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mykonos" title="Battle of Mykonos">Battle of Mykonos</a>: The British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> captures <a href="/wiki/French_frigate_Sibylle_(1792)" title="French frigate Sibylle (1792)">French frigate <i>Sibylle</i></a>.</li>

</li>

French use of an <a href="/wiki/Observation_balloon" title="Observation balloon">observation balloon</a> marks the first participation of an aircraft in battle.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_4">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1794&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1794">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_12" title="July 12">July 12</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horatio_Nelson" title="Horatio Nelson">Horatio Nelson</a> loses the sight in his right eye in a British military operation at <a href="/wiki/Calvi,_Haute-Corse" title="Calvi, Haute-Corse">Calvi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_13" title="July 13">July 13</a>–<a href="/wiki/September_6" title="September 6">September 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%9Bciuszko_Uprising" title="Kościuszko Uprising">Kościuszko Uprising</a> – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Warsaw_(1794)" title="Siege of Warsaw (1794)">Siege of Warsaw</a>: The Polish people resist a siege by armies of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_27" title="July 27">July 27</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/9_Thermidor" title="9 Thermidor">9 Thermidor</a>) – <a href="/wiki/Thermidorian_Reaction" title="Thermidorian Reaction">Thermidorian Reaction</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robespierre" title="Robespierre">Robespierre</a> is arrested on the orders of the French <a href="/wiki/National_Convention" title="National Convention">National Convention</a>; he is executed the next day, ending the French Revolution's <a href="/wiki/Reign_of_Terror" title="Reign of Terror">Reign of Terror</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_20" title="August 20">August 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fallen_Timbers" title="Battle of Fallen Timbers">Battle of Fallen Timbers</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northwestern_Ohio" title="Northwestern Ohio">Northwestern Ohio</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_29" title="August 29">August 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Stonyhurst_College" title="Stonyhurst College">Stonyhurst College</a> is finally established as a Roman Catholic school in Lancashire, England, having had several European locations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_10" title="September 10">September 10</a> – The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tennessee" title="University of Tennessee">University of Tennessee</a> is established at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knoxville" title="Knoxville">Knoxville</a>.</li>
<li>October – A <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">Federal</a> army quells the <a href="/wiki/Whiskey_Rebellion" title="Whiskey Rebellion">Whiskey Rebellion</a> in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_10" title="October 10">October 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Maciejowice" title="Battle of Maciejowice">Battle of Maciejowice</a>: Forces of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> defeat Polish supporters of the <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%9Bciuszko_Uprising" title="Kościuszko Uprising">Kościuszko Uprising</a>; <a href="/wiki/Tadeusz_Ko%C5%9Bciuszko" title="Tadeusz Kościuszko">Tadeusz Kościuszko</a> is wounded and captured.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_4" title="November 4">November 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Praga" title="Battle of Praga">Battle of Praga</a>: Russian General <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Suvorov" title="Alexander Suvorov">Alexander Suvorov</a> storms <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a> in the war against the Polish <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%9Bciuszko_Uprising" title="Kościuszko Uprising">Kościuszko Uprising</a> and captures Praga, one of its suburbs, killing many civilians.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_14" title="November 14">November 14</a> – The first recorded meeting of the <a href="/wiki/Literary_societies_at_Washington_%26_Jefferson_College" title="Literary societies at Washington &amp; Jefferson College">Franklin Literary Society</a> is held at Canonsburg Academy (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Washington_%26_Jefferson_College" title="Washington &amp; Jefferson College">Washington &amp; Jefferson College</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_19" title="November 19">November 19</a> – The United States and Great Britain conclude the <a href="/wiki/Jay_Treaty" title="Jay Treaty">Jay Treaty</a>, the basis for ten years of peaceful trade between the two nations.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown_3">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1794&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1794">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">Coffee</a> is forbidden by royal decree in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WeinbergBealer2001_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-WeinbergBealer2001-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li>France occupies <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Ayrshire_(Earl_of_Carrick%27s_Own)_Yeomanry" title="Ayrshire (Earl of Carrick's Own) Yeomanry">Ayrshire (Earl of Carrick's Own) Yeomanry</a>, a <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Yeomanry" title="Yeomanry">Yeomanry</a> <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">Cavalry</a> <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">Regiment</a>, is formed by the <a href="/wiki/Marquess_of_Ailsa" title="Marquess of Ailsa">Earl of Cassillis</a> at <a href="/wiki/Culzean_Castle" title="Culzean Castle">Culzean Castle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ayrshire" title="Ayrshire">Ayrshire</a>.</li>
<li>Colombian <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Nari%C3%B1o" title="Antonio Nariño">Antonio Nariño</a> translates and publishes the French <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_the_Rights_of_Man_and_of_the_Citizen" title="Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen">Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen</a>.</li>
<li>Britain agrees to evacuate border forts in the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Territory" title="Northwest Territory">Northwest Territory</a> (roughly the area north of the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_River" title="Ohio River">Ohio River</a> and east of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>) and thereby end British support for the Indians.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bowdoin_College" title="Bowdoin College">Bowdoin College</a> is founded.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Oban_distillery" title="Oban distillery">Oban distillery</a> is built.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_5">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1795&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1795">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_14" title="January 14">January 14</a> – The <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_at_Chapel_Hill" title="University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill">University of North Carolina</a> opens to students at <a href="/wiki/Chapel_Hill,_North_Carolina" title="Chapel Hill, North Carolina">Chapel Hill</a>, becoming the first state university in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_16" title="January 16">January 16</a> – The French occupy <a href="/wiki/Utrecht" title="Utrecht">Utrecht</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Netherlands</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_18" title="January 18">January 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Revolution_in_Amsterdam" title="Batavian Revolution in Amsterdam">Batavian Revolution in Amsterdam</a>. <a href="/wiki/William_V,_Prince_of_Orange" title="William V, Prince of Orange">William V, Prince of Orange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stadtholder" title="Stadtholder">Stadtholder</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a>, flees the country.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_19" title="January 19">January 19</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> is proclaimed in Amsterdam.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_20" title="January 20">January 20</a> – French troops enter <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_21" title="January 21">January 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_the_Dutch_fleet_at_Den_Helder" title="Capture of the Dutch fleet at Den Helder">Capture of the Dutch fleet at Den Helder</a>: The Dutch fleet, frozen in Zuiderzee, is captured by the French 8th <a href="/wiki/Hussar" title="Hussar">Hussars</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_7" title="February 7">February 7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Eleventh Amendment to the United States Constitution">Eleventh Amendment to the United States Constitution</a> is passed.</li>
<li>March – English <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Benedict" title="Order of Saint Benedict">Benedictine</a> monks expelled from <a href="/wiki/Douai" title="Douai">Douai</a> are permitted to proceed to England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_5" title="April 5">April 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Basel" title="Peace of Basel">Peace of Basel</a> signed between France and <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_7" title="April 7">April 7</a> – Adoption of the <a href="/wiki/Metric_system" title="Metric system">metric system</a> in France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_8" title="April 8">April 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/George_IV_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George IV of the United Kingdom">George, Prince of Wales</a>, marries <a href="/wiki/Caroline_of_Brunswick" title="Caroline of Brunswick">Caroline of Brunswick</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_23" title="April 23">April 23</a> – Former <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_India" title="Governor-General of India">Governor-General of India</a> <a href="/wiki/Warren_Hastings" title="Warren Hastings">Warren Hastings</a> is acquitted by the British <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a> of misconduct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology345346_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology345346-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_1" title="May 1">May 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nu%CA%BBuanu" title="Battle of Nuʻuanu">Battle of Nuʻuanu</a>: <a href="/wiki/Kamehameha_I" title="Kamehameha I">Kamehameha I</a> of the Island of Hawaii defeats the <a href="/wiki/Oahu" title="Oahu">Oahuans</a>, solidifying his control of the major islands of the archipelago and officially founding the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hawaii" title="Kingdom of Hawaii">Kingdom of Hawaii</a>.</li>
<li>May–June – The <i>Battle of Richmond Hill</i> is fought in the colony of <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, between the <a href="/wiki/Darug" title="Darug">Darug</a> people and British colonial forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_5" title="June 5">June 5</a>–<a href="/wiki/June_7" title="June 7">7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Fire_of_1795" title="Copenhagen Fire of 1795">Copenhagen Fire of 1795</a>, starting in a naval warehouse, destroys 941 houses.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_8" title="June 8">June 8</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Dauphin_of_France" title="Dauphin of France">Dauphin of France</a>, would-be-<a href="/wiki/Louis_XVII_of_France" title="Louis XVII of France">Louis XVII</a>, dies. <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVIII_of_France" title="Louis XVIII of France">Louis XVIII</a> becomes titular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_France" title="King of France">King of France</a> (he becomes the actual king on <a href="/wiki/April_6" title="April 6">April 6</a>, <a href="/wiki/1814" title="1814">1814</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_16" title="June 16">June 16</a>–<a href="/wiki/June_17" title="June 17">17</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a>: <a href="/wiki/Cornwallis%27s_Retreat" title="Cornwallis's Retreat">Cornwallis's Retreat</a> – A British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> battle squadron commanded by <a href="/wiki/William_Cornwallis" title="William Cornwallis">William Cornwallis</a> fends off a numerically superior <a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">French Navy</a> fleet off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_28" title="June 28">June 28</a> – The French government announces that the <a href="/wiki/List_of_heirs_to_the_French_throne" title="List of heirs to the French throne">heir to the French throne</a> has died of illness (many doubt the statement).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_27" title="June 27">June 27</a>
<ul>
<li>British forces land off <a href="/wiki/Quiberon" title="Quiberon">Quiberon</a> to aid the revolt in <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>.</li>
<li>French troops recapture <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lucia" title="St. Lucia">St. Lucia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mary_Robinson_(poet)" title="Mary Robinson (poet)">Mary Robinson</a> writes the poem <i>January, 1795</i>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_5">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1795&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1795">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_22" title="July 22">July 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Basel" title="Peace of Basel">Second Treaty of Basel</a> between the <a href="/wiki/French_First_Republic" title="French First Republic">French First Republic</a> and Spain, ending the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Pyrenees" title="War of the Pyrenees">War of the Pyrenees</a>. Spain cedes its half of the Caribbean island of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> to France.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_25" title="July 25">July 25</a> – Construction of the <a href="/wiki/Pontcysyllte_Aqueduct" title="Pontcysyllte Aqueduct">Pontcysyllte Aqueduct</a> in Wales begins.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_3" title="August 3">August 3</a> – Signing of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Greenville" title="Treaty of Greenville">Treaty of Greenville</a> puts an end to the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Indian_War" title="Northwest Indian War">Northwest Indian War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_22" title="August 22">August 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Year_III" title="Constitution of the Year III">Constitution of the Year III</a> is ratified by the <a href="/wiki/National_Convention" title="National Convention">National Convention</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_25" title="August 25">August 25</a> – British forces capture <a href="/wiki/Trincomalee" title="Trincomalee">Trincomalee</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Ceylon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH234235_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH234235-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_28" title="August 28">August 28</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Treaty_of_Basel" title="Third Treaty of Basel">Third Treaty of Basel</a> between the <a href="/wiki/French_First_Republic" title="French First Republic">French First Republic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Landgraviate_of_Hesse-Kassel" title="Landgraviate of Hesse-Kassel">Landgraviate of Hesse-Kassel</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_11" title="September 11">September 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Krtsanisi" title="Battle of Krtsanisi">Battle of Krtsanisi</a>: The Persian emperor <a href="/wiki/Agha_Mohammad_Khan_Qajar" title="Agha Mohammad Khan Qajar">Agha Mohammad Khan Qajar</a> defeats the forces of <a href="/wiki/Heraclius_II_of_Georgia" title="Heraclius II of Georgia">Heraclius II of Georgia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_16" title="September 16">September 16</a> – British forces capture <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a> from the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH234235_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBH234235-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_21" title="September 21">September 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Diamond" title="Battle of the Diamond">Battle of the Diamond</a>: Protestant forces defeat Catholic troops in <a href="/wiki/Loughgall" title="Loughgall">Loughgall</a>, Ireland, leading to the foundation of the <a href="/wiki/Orange_Order" title="Orange Order">Orange Order</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_28" title="September 28">September 28</a> – The Alliance of St Petersburg is formed between Britain, Russia and Austria against France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology345346_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology345346-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_1" title="October 1">October 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Netherlands" title="Austrian Netherlands">Austrian Netherlands</a> is annexed to the <a href="/wiki/French_First_Republic" title="French First Republic">French Republic</a> as the <i>Belgian departments</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_2" title="October 2">October 2</a> – British forces capture the <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_d%27Yeu" title="Île d'Yeu">Île d'Yeu</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH234235_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-CBH234235-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_5" title="October 5">October 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/13_Vend%C3%A9miaire" title="13 Vendémiaire">13 Vendémiaire</a>: Royalist riots in Paris are crushed by troops under <a href="/wiki/Paul_Barras" title="Paul Barras">Paul Barras</a> and newly reinstalled artillery officer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_24" title="October 24">October 24</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Third_Partition_of_Poland" title="Third Partition of Poland">Third Partition of Poland</a> is made, dividing the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Commonwealth of Poland</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>. On <a href="/wiki/November_25" title="November 25">November 25</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_August_Poniatowski" title="Stanisław August Poniatowski">Stanisław August Poniatowski</a> formally <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdicate" title="Abdicate">abdicates</a> as last <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Poland" title="King of Poland">King of Poland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_27" title="October 27">October 27</a> – The United States and Spain sign the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Madrid_(1795)" title="Treaty of Madrid (1795)">Treaty of Madrid</a>, which establishes the boundaries between <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish colonies</a> and the U.S.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_2" title="November 2">November 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/French_Directory" title="French Directory">French Directory</a> takes power. Influence of the <a href="/wiki/Sans-culottes" title="Sans-culottes">Sans-culottes</a> declines.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_13" title="December 13">December 13</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wold_Newton_meteorite" title="Wold Newton meteorite">Wold Newton meteorite</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Meteorite" title="Meteorite">meteorite</a> falls at <a href="/wiki/Wold_Newton,_East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="Wold Newton, East Riding of Yorkshire">Wold Newton</a>, a hamlet in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> in England. This meteorite fall is subsequently used as a literary premise by <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> writer <a href="/wiki/Philip_Jos%C3%A9_Farmer" title="Philip José Farmer">Philip José Farmer</a> as the basis for the <a href="/wiki/Wold_Newton_family" title="Wold Newton family">Wold Newton family</a>.</li>
</ul>

<li>The <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson's Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a> trading post <a href="/wiki/Fort_Edmonton" title="Fort Edmonton">Fort Edmonton</a> is constructed; the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edmonton,_Alberta" title="Edmonton, Alberta">Edmonton, Alberta</a>, eventually grows from it.</li>
<li>A large <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slave</a> rebellion occurs in <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a>.</li>
<li>The British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> makes the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lemon_juice" title="Lemon juice">lemon juice</a> mandatory to prevent <a href="/wiki/Scurvy" title="Scurvy">scurvy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li>The harvest fails in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>.</li>
<li>Daniel McGinnis discovers the supposed Money Pit on <a href="/wiki/Oak_Island" title="Oak Island">Oak Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_6">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1796&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1796">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_16" title="January 16">January 16</a> – The first Dutch (and general) elections are held for the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_the_Batavian_Republic" title="National Assembly of the Batavian Republic">National Assembly of the Batavian Republic</a> (the next Dutch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_elections" title="General elections">general elections</a> are held in <a href="/wiki/1888" title="1888">1888</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_1" title="February 1">February 1</a> – The capital of <a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper Canada</a> is moved from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newark,_Upper_Canada" title="Newark, Upper Canada">Newark</a> to <a href="/wiki/York,_Upper_Canada" title="York, Upper Canada">York</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_9" title="February 9">February 9</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Qianlong_Emperor" title="Qianlong Emperor">Qianlong Emperor</a> of China abdicates at age 84 to make way for his son, the <a href="/wiki/Jiaqing_Emperor" title="Jiaqing Emperor">Jiaqing Emperor</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_16" title="February 16">February 16</a> – The Kingdom of Great Britain is granted control of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceylon" title="Ceylon">Ceylon</a> by the Dutch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology346_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology346-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_9" title="March 9">March 9</a> – Widow <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9phine_de_Beauharnais" title="Joséphine de Beauharnais">Joséphine de Beauharnais</a> marries <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General" title="General">General</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napol%C3%A9on_Bonaparte" title="Napoléon Bonaparte">Napoléon Bonaparte</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_26" title="March 26">March 26</a> – Napoleon Bonaparte arrives at <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a> to take command of the <a href="/wiki/Army_of_Italy_(France)" title="Army of Italy (France)">Army of Italy</a> (37,000 men and 60 guns), which is scattered in detachments as far as <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_30" title="March 30">March 30</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carl_Gauss" title="Carl Gauss">Carl Gauss</a> obtains conditions for the constructibility by ruler and compass of regular polygons, and is able to announce that the regular 17-gon is constructible by ruler and compasses.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_2" title="April 2">April 2</a> – The only night of the supposed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shakespeare" title="Shakespeare">Shakespearean</a> play <i><a href="/wiki/Vortigern_and_Rowena" title="Vortigern and Rowena">Vortigern and Rowena</a></i> (actually written by <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Ireland" title="William Henry Ireland">William Henry Ireland</a>) ends in the audience's laughter.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_12" title="April 12">April 12</a> – War of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_Coalition" title="First Coalition">First Coalition</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Montenotte" title="Battle of Montenotte">Battle of Montenotte</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a> gains his first victory as an army commander.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_26" title="April 26">April 26</a> – The French proclaim the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Alba" title="Republic of Alba">Republic of Alba</a> on the occupied territories. Two days later, King <a href="/wiki/Victor_Amadeus_III_of_Sardinia" title="Victor Amadeus III of Sardinia">Victor Amadeus III of Sardinia</a> signs the <a href="/wiki/Armistice_of_Cherasco" title="Armistice of Cherasco">Armistice of Cherasco</a>, in the headquarters of Napoleon. The fortresses of <a href="/wiki/Cuneo" title="Cuneo">Coni</a>, <a href="/wiki/G%C3%AAnes" title="Gênes">Tortoni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alessandria" title="Alessandria">Alessandria</a> with all their guns are given up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_27" title="April 27">April 27</a> – Case of the Lyons Mail: During the night, five highwaymen attack the mail between Paris and <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>, kill the postmen and steal the funds sent to the armies in Italy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_6" title="May 6">May 6</a> – Napoleon Bonaparte forms an advanced guard (3,500 infantry and 1,500 cavalry) under General <a href="/wiki/Claude_Dallemagne" title="Claude Dallemagne">Claude Dallemagne</a>. He sends this force along the south bank of the <a href="/wiki/Po_(river)" title="Po (river)">Po River</a> to cross it with boats at <a href="/wiki/Piacenza" title="Piacenza">Piacenza</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_10" title="May 10">May 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_First_Coalition" title="War of the First Coalition">War of the First Coalition</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lodi" title="Battle of Lodi">Battle of Lodi</a>: General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a> defeats the Austrian rearguard in forcing a crossing of the bridge over the <a href="/wiki/Adda_(river)" title="Adda (river)">Adda River</a> in Italy. The Austrians lose some 2,000 men, 14 guns, and 30 ammunition wagons.
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Persian_Expedition_of_1796" title="Persian Expedition of 1796">Persian Expedition of 1796</a>: Russian troops <a href="/wiki/Storming_of_Derbent" title="Storming of Derbent">storm Derbent</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_14" title="May 14">May 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Edward_Jenner" title="Edward Jenner">Edward Jenner</a> administers the first <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a> <a href="/wiki/Vaccination" title="Vaccination">vaccination</a>, in England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_15" title="May 15">May 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>'s troops take <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_20" title="May 20">May 20</a> – The last mock <a href="/wiki/Garrat_Elections" title="Garrat Elections">Garrat Elections</a> are held in <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a>, England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_1" title="June 1">June 1</a> – The French-Republican army divisions of the <a href="/wiki/Army_of_Italy_(France)" title="Army of Italy (France)">Army of Italy</a> invade the territories of the <i><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_San_Marco" title="Republic of San Marco">Serenissima Repubblica di San Marco</a></i>.
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a> is admitted as the 16th <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_21" title="June 21">June 21</a> – British explorer <a href="/wiki/Mungo_Park_(explorer)" title="Mungo Park (explorer)">Mungo Park</a> becomes the first European to reach the <a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology346_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology346-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_23" title="June 23">June 23</a> – Napoleon Bonaparte seizes the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States#French_Revolution_and_Napoleonic_era" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> which become part of the revolutionary <a href="/wiki/Cisalpine_Republic" title="Cisalpine Republic">Cisalpine Republic</a>. Pope <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_VI" title="Pope Pius VI">Pius VI</a> signs the <a href="/wiki/Armistice_of_Bologna" title="Armistice of Bologna">Armistice of Bologna</a> and is forced to pay contribution (34 million francs).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_6">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1796&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1796">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_10" title="July 10">July 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Carl_Friedrich_Gauss" title="Carl Friedrich Gauss">Carl Friedrich Gauss</a> discovers that every positive integer is representable as a sum of at most 3 <a href="/wiki/Triangular_number" title="Triangular number">triangular numbers</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – The United States takes possession of <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a> from Great Britain under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Jay_Treaty" title="Jay Treaty">Jay Treaty</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_21" title="July 21">July 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mungo_Park_(explorer)" title="Mungo Park (explorer)">Mungo Park</a> reaches <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9gou" title="Ségou">Ségou</a>, the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Bamana_Empire" title="Bamana Empire">Bamana Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_22" title="July 22">July 22</a> – Surveyors of the <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Land_Company" title="Connecticut Land Company">Connecticut Land Company</a> name an area in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> "<a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>" after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gen." title="Gen.">Gen.</a> <a href="/wiki/Moses_Cleaveland" title="Moses Cleaveland">Moses Cleaveland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Superintendent_(construction)" title="Superintendent (construction)">superintendent</a> of the surveying party.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_29" title="July 29">July 29</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_and_Royal_Army_during_the_Napoleonic_Wars" title="Imperial and Royal Army during the Napoleonic Wars">Austrian army</a> under Marshal <a href="/wiki/Dagobert_Sigmund_von_Wurmser" title="Dagobert Sigmund von Wurmser">Wurmser</a> advances from the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a> and captures <a href="/wiki/Rivoli_Veronese" title="Rivoli Veronese">Rivoli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Verona" title="Verona">Verona</a>. The French abandoned the east bank of the <a href="/wiki/Mincio" title="Mincio">Mincio River</a>, the outnumbered division (15,000 men) of <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Mass%C3%A9na" title="André Masséna">Masséna</a> retreats towards <a href="/wiki/Lake_Garda" title="Lake Garda">Lake Garda</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_4" title="August 4">August 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lonato" title="Battle of Lonato">Battle of Lonato</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Army_of_Italy_(France)" title="Army of Italy (France)">French Army of Italy</a> under Napoleon crushes an Austrian brigade.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_5" title="August 5">August 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castiglione" title="Battle of Castiglione">Battle of Castiglione</a>: Napoleon Bonaparte defeats the Austrian army (25,000 men) under <a href="/wiki/Dagobert_Sigmund_von_Wurmser" title="Dagobert Sigmund von Wurmser">Wurmser</a>. He is forced to retreat north up the <a href="/wiki/Adige" title="Adige">Adige Valley</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_9" title="August 9">August 9</a> – Opening to traffic of the <a href="/wiki/Wearmouth_Bridge" title="Wearmouth Bridge">Wearmouth Bridge</a> in England, designed by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Paine" title="Thomas Paine">Thomas Paine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">cast iron</a>. Its span of 72 m (237 feet) makes it the world's longest single-span vehicular bridge extant at this date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_19" title="August 19">August 19</a> – By the <a href="/wiki/Second_Treaty_of_San_Ildefonso" title="Second Treaty of San Ildefonso">Second Treaty of San Ildefonso</a>, Spain and France form an alliance against Great Britain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_2" title="September 2">September 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Jewish_emancipation" title="Jewish emancipation">Jewish emancipation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> (Netherlands).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_8" title="September 8">September 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bassano" title="Battle of Bassano">Battle of Bassano</a>: French forces (20,000 men) under <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Mass%C3%A9na" title="André Masséna">André Masséna</a> defeat the Austrians in <a href="/wiki/Veneto" title="Veneto">Veneto</a>. Wurmser retreats towards <a href="/wiki/Vicenza" title="Vicenza">Vicenza</a> with just 3,500 men of his originally 11,000 left to him.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_15" title="September 15">September 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Mantua_(1796%E2%80%9397)" title="Siege of Mantua (1796–97)">Siege of Mantua</a>: Napoleon Bonaparte fights a pitched battle at <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Mantua_(1796%E2%80%9397)#Second_relief" title="Siege of Mantua (1796–97)">La Favorita</a> on the east side of the Mincio River. The Austrians withdraw into the fortress of <a href="/wiki/Mantua" title="Mantua">Mantua</a>, which is crowded nearly with 30,000 men. Within six weeks, 4,000 die from wounds or sickness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_17" title="September 17">September 17</a> – U.S. President <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> issues his <i><a href="/wiki/George_Washington%27s_Farewell_Address" title="George Washington's Farewell Address">Farewell Address</a></i>, which warns against partisan politics and foreign entanglements.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_28" title="September 28">September 28</a> – Empress <a href="/wiki/Catherine_the_Great" title="Catherine the Great">Catherine the Great</a> signs a agreement with Great Britain, formally joining Russia to the coalition.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_3" title="November 3">November 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Adams" title="John Adams">John Adams</a> defeats <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._presidential_election,_1796" title="U.S. presidential election, 1796">U.S. presidential election</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_4" title="November 4">November 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tripoli" title="Treaty of Tripoli">Treaty of Tripoli</a> (between the United States and <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Tripolitania" title="Ottoman Tripolitania">Tripoli</a>) is signed at <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a> (see also <a href="/wiki/1797" title="1797">1797</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_6" title="November 6">November 6</a> – Catherine the Great dies and is succeeded by her son <a href="/wiki/Paul_I_of_Russia" title="Paul I of Russia">Paul I of Russia</a>. His wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sophie_Marie_Dorothea_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Sophie Marie Dorothea of Württemberg">Sophie Marie Dorothea of Württemberg</a> becomes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empress_consort" title="Empress consort">Empress consort</a>.
<ul>
<li>French forces (9,500 men) under Masséna attack the Austrian army at <a href="/wiki/Fontaniva" title="Fontaniva">Fontaniva</a>. After a desperate assault he is outnumbered and forced to retreat to Verona.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_12" title="November 12">November 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Caldiero_(1796)" title="Battle of Caldiero (1796)">Battle of Caldiero</a>: French forces are defeated by the Austrians at <a href="/wiki/Caldiero" title="Caldiero">Caldiero</a> and pushed back to Verona. This marked Napoleon's first defeat, losing nearly 2,000 men and 2 guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Groton,_New_Hampshire" title="Groton, New Hampshire">Groton, New Hampshire</a> is incorporated as a town.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_17" title="November 17">November 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Arcole" title="Battle of Arcole">Battle of Arcole</a>: French forces under General Napoleon Bonaparte defeat the Austrians at <a href="/wiki/Arcole" title="Arcole">Arcole</a>. After a bold <a href="/wiki/Maneuver_warfare#Napoleon.27s_use_of_maneuver" title="Maneuver warfare">maneuver</a> he outflank the Austrian army (24,000 men) under Freiherr <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B3zsef_Alvinczi" title="József Alvinczi">József Alvinczi</a> and cut off its line of retreat. Alvinczi is forced to take up a defensive position behind the <a href="/wiki/Brenta_(river)" title="Brenta (river)">Brenta River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li>December – The British government begins work on a 40-acre (162,000 m²) site at <a href="/wiki/Norman_Cross" title="Norman Cross">Norman Cross</a> for the world's first purpose-built <a href="/wiki/Prisoner-of-war_camp" title="Prisoner-of-war camp">prisoner-of-war camp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_7" title="December 7">December 7</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Electoral_College" title="U.S. Electoral College">U.S. Electoral College</a> meets to elect <a href="/wiki/John_Adams" title="John Adams">John Adams</a> president of the United States.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown_4">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1796&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1796">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<li>Spanish government lifts the restrictions against neutrals trading with the colonies, thus acknowledging Spain's inability to supply the colonies with needed goods and markets.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jane_Austen" title="Jane Austen">Jane Austen</a> writes her first draft of <i><a href="/wiki/Pride_and_Prejudice" title="Pride and Prejudice">Pride and Prejudice</a></i>, under the title <i>First Impressions</i>. The book will not be published until 1813.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Robert_Burns" title="Robert Burns">Robert Burns</a>' version of the <a href="/wiki/Scots_language" title="Scots language">Scots</a> poem "<a href="/wiki/Auld_Lang_Syne" title="Auld Lang Syne">Auld Lang Syne</a>" is first published, in this year's volume of <i>The <a href="/wiki/Scots_Musical_Museum" title="Scots Musical Museum">Scots Musical Museum</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_7">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1797&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1797">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_3" title="January 3">January 3</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tripoli" title="Treaty of Tripoli">Treaty of Tripoli</a> (a peace treaty between the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Tripolitania" title="Ottoman Tripolitania">Tripoli</a>) is signed at <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a> (<i>see also</i> <a href="/wiki/1796" title="1796">1796</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_7" title="January 7">January 7</a> – The parliament of the <a href="/wiki/Cisalpine_Republic" title="Cisalpine Republic">Cisalpine Republic</a> adopts the Italian green-white-red tricolour as the official flag (this is considered the birth of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Italy" title="Flag of Italy">flag of Italy</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_13" title="January 13">January 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Action_of_13_January_1797" title="Action of 13 January 1797">Action of 13 January 1797</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a>: Two British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigates</a>, <a href="/wiki/HMS_Indefatigable_(1784)" title="HMS Indefatigable (1784)">HMS <i>Indefatigable</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/HMS_Amazon_(1795)" title="HMS Amazon (1795)">HMS <i>Amazon</i></a>, drive the French <a href="/wiki/Seventy-four_(ship)" title="Seventy-four (ship)">74-gun</a> <a href="/wiki/Ship_of_the_line" title="Ship of the line">ship of the line</a> <i><a href="/wiki/French_ship_Droits_de_l%27Homme_(1794)" title="French ship Droits de l'Homme (1794)">Droits de l'Homme</a></i> aground on the coast of <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a> with over 900 deaths.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_14" title="January 14">January 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rivoli" title="Battle of Rivoli">Battle of Rivoli</a>: French forces under General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a> defeat an Austrian army (28,000 men) under <i><a href="/wiki/General_of_the_branch" title="General of the branch">Feldzeugmeister</a></i> <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B3zsef_Alvinczi" title="József Alvinczi">József Alvinczi</a> near <a href="/wiki/Rivoli_Veronese" title="Rivoli Veronese">Rivoli</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>), ending <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>'s fourth and final attempt to relieve the fortress city of <a href="/wiki/Mantua" title="Mantua">Mantua</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_26" title="January 26">January 26</a> – The Treaty of the <a href="/wiki/Third_Partition_of_Poland" title="Third Partition of Poland">Third Partition of Poland</a> is signed in St. Petersburg by the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hapsburg_Monarchy" title="Hapsburg Monarchy">Austria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2" title="February 2">February 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Mantua_(1796%E2%80%9397)" title="Siege of Mantua (1796–97)">Siege of Mantua</a>: Field marshal <a href="/wiki/Dagobert_Sigmund_von_Wurmser" title="Dagobert Sigmund von Wurmser">Dagobert von Wurmser</a> capitulates the fortress city to the French. Only 16,000 men of the <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> are capable of marching out as prisoners of war.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_3" title="February 3">February 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Faenza" title="Battle of Faenza">Battle of Castel Bolognese</a>: A French corps (9,000 men) under General <a href="/wiki/Claude_Victor-Perrin,_Duc_de_Belluno" title="Claude Victor-Perrin, Duc de Belluno">Claude Victor-Perrin</a> defeats the forces from the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> at <a href="/wiki/Castel_Bolognese" title="Castel Bolognese">Castel Bolognese</a> near <a href="/wiki/Faenza" title="Faenza">Faenza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_12" title="February 12">February 12</a> – First performance of <i><a href="/wiki/Gott_erhalte_Franz_den_Kaiser" title="Gott erhalte Franz den Kaiser">Gott erhalte Franz den Kaiser</a></i>, with the music composed in January by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Haydn" title="Joseph Haydn">Joseph Haydn</a> which also becomes the tune to the German national anthem, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deutschland,_Deutschland_%C3%BCber_alles" title="Deutschland, Deutschland über alles">Deutschland, Deutschland über alles</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_14" title="February 14">February 14</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cape_St_Vincent_(1797)" title="Battle of Cape St Vincent (1797)">Battle of Cape St Vincent</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a>: The British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> under Admiral Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Jervis,_1st_Earl_of_St_Vincent" title="John Jervis, 1st Earl of St Vincent">John Jervis</a> defeats a larger Spanish fleet off <a href="/wiki/Cape_St._Vincent" title="Cape St. Vincent">Cape St. Vincent</a>, Portugal.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_18" title="February 18">February 18</a> – Spanish Governor <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Chac%C3%B3n" title="José María Chacón">José María Chacón</a> peacefully surrenders the colony of <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad</a> to a British naval force commanded by Sir <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Abercromby" title="Ralph Abercromby">Ralph Abercromby</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_19" title="February 19">February 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tolentino" title="Treaty of Tolentino">Treaty of Tolentino</a>: <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_VI" title="Pope Pius VI">Pope Pius VI</a> signs a peace treaty with <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">Revolutionary France</a>. He is forced to deliver works of art, treasures, territory, the <a href="/wiki/Comtat_Venaissin" title="Comtat Venaissin">Comtat Venaissin</a> and 30 million francs.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_22" title="February 22">February 22</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_invasion_of_Britain" title="Last invasion of Britain">last invasion of Britain</a> begins: French forces under the command of American Colonel <a href="/wiki/William_Tate_(soldier)" title="William Tate (soldier)">William Tate</a> land near <a href="/wiki/Fishguard" title="Fishguard">Fishguard</a> in Wales.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_25" title="February 25">February 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/William_Tate_(soldier)" title="William Tate (soldier)">William Tate</a> surrenders to the British at Fishguard.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_26" title="February 26">February 26</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_England" title="Bank of England">Bank of England</a> (national bank of Britain) issues the first one-pound and two-pound notes (discontinued <a href="/wiki/March_11" title="March 11">March 11</a>, <a href="/wiki/1988" title="1988">1988</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_4" title="March 4">March 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Adams" title="John Adams">John Adams</a> is <a href="/wiki/Inauguration_of_John_Adams" title="Inauguration of John Adams">sworn in</a> as the second President of the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_16" title="March 16">March 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Valvasone_(1797)" title="Battle of Valvasone (1797)">Battle of Valvasone</a>: The Austrian army led by <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Charles,_Duke_of_Teschen" title="Archduke Charles, Duke of Teschen">Archduke Charles</a> fights a <a href="/wiki/Rearguard" title="Rearguard">rear guard</a> action at the crossing of the <a href="/wiki/Tagliamento" title="Tagliamento">Tagliamento River</a> but is defeated by Napoleon Bonaparte at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valvasone" title="Valvasone">Valvasone</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_16" title="April 16">April 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Spithead_and_Nore_mutinies" title="Spithead and Nore mutinies">Spithead and Nore mutinies</a> in the British Royal Navy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_17" title="April 17">April 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Juan_(1797)" title="Battle of San Juan (1797)">Battle of San Juan</a>: Sir <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Abercromby" title="Ralph Abercromby">Ralph Abercromby</a> unsuccessfully invades <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a> in what will be one of the largest British attacks on Spanish territories in the western hemisphere, and one of the worst defeats of the British Royal Navy for years to come.</li>
<li>April 17 – <a href="/wiki/Veronese_Easter" title="Veronese Easter">Veronese Easter</a>: Citizens of <a href="/wiki/Verona" title="Verona">Verona</a>, Italy, began an unsuccessful eight-day rebellion against the French occupying forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_18" title="April 18">April 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Leoben" title="Treaty of Leoben">Armistice of Leoben</a>: On behalf of the <a href="/wiki/French_First_Republic" title="French First Republic">French Republic</a> a delegation under Napoleon Bonaparte signs a <a href="/wiki/Peace_treaty" title="Peace treaty">peace treaty</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> at <a href="/wiki/Leoben" title="Leoben">Leoben</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_10" title="May 10">May 10</a> – The first ship of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, the frigate <a href="/wiki/USS_United_States_(1797)" title="USS United States (1797)">USS <i>United States</i></a>, is commissioned.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_12" title="May 12">May 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_First_Coalition" title="War of the First Coalition">War of the First Coalition</a>: Napoleon Bonaparte conquers <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a>, ending the city and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a>'s 1,100 years of independence. The last <a href="/wiki/Doge_of_Venice" title="Doge of Venice">doge of Venice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Manin" title="Ludovico Manin">Ludovico Manin</a>, steps down. The <a href="/wiki/Venetian_Ghetto" title="Venetian Ghetto">Venetian Ghetto</a> is thrown open.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_30" title="May 30">May 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/William_Wilberforce" title="William Wilberforce">William Wilberforce</a> marries <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Spooner_Wilberforce" title="Barbara Spooner Wilberforce">Barbara Ann Spooner</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_29" title="June 29">June 29</a> – Napoleon Bonaparte creates in a decree the birth of the <a href="/wiki/Cisalpine_Republic" title="Cisalpine Republic">Cisalpine Republic</a>. He appoints ministers and establishes the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Cisalpine_Republic_(1797)" title="Constitution of the Cisalpine Republic (1797)">first constitution</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_7">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1797&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1797">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_24" title="July 24">July 24</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horatio_Nelson" title="Horatio Nelson">Horatio Nelson</a> is wounded at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Santa_Cruz_de_Tenerife_(1797)" title="Battle of Santa Cruz de Tenerife (1797)">Battle of Santa Cruz</a>, losing an arm.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_29" title="August 29">August 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Massacre_of_Tranent" title="Massacre of Tranent">Massacre of Tranent</a>: British troops attack protestors against enforced recruitment into the <a href="/wiki/Militia_(Great_Britain)" title="Militia (Great Britain)">militia</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tranent" title="Tranent">Tranent</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, killing 12.</li>
<li>From c. October – The <a href="/wiki/XYZ_Affair" title="XYZ Affair">XYZ Affair</a> inflames tensions between France and the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_11" title="October 11">October 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Camperdown" title="Battle of Camperdown">Battle of Camperdown</a>: the British Royal Navy defeats the fleet of the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">Holland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH236237_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH236237-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_17" title="October 17">October 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Campo_Formio" title="Treaty of Campo Formio">Treaty of Campo Formio</a> ends the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_First_Coalition" title="War of the First Coalition">War of the First Coalition</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_21" title="October 21">October 21</a> – In <a href="/wiki/Boston_Harbor" title="Boston Harbor">Boston Harbor</a>, the 44-gun United States Navy frigate <a href="/wiki/USS_Constitution" title="USS Constitution">USS <i>Constitution</i></a> is launched to fight <a href="/wiki/Barbary_pirates" title="Barbary pirates">Barbary pirates</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>; the ship will remain in commission in the 21st century.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_16" title="November 16">November 16</a> – The Prussian heir apparent, Frederick William, becomes King of Prussia as <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_III_of_Prussia" title="Frederick William III of Prussia">Fredrick William III</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_17" title="December 17">December 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a> leads a successful French charge against Fort l'Aiguilette to secure <a href="/wiki/Toulon" title="Toulon">Toulon</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_8">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1798&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1798">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_4" title="January 4">January 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Constantine_Hangerli" title="Constantine Hangerli">Constantine Hangerli</a> enters <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Wallachia" title="List of rulers of Wallachia">Prince of Wallachia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_22" title="January 22">January 22</a> – A <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> is staged in the Netherlands (<a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a>). Unitarian Democrat <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Vreede" title="Pieter Vreede">Pieter Vreede</a> makes an end to the power of the parliament (with a conservative-moderate majority).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_10" title="February 10">February 10</a> – The papacy is removed from power by French General <a href="/wiki/Louis-Alexandre_Berthier" title="Louis-Alexandre Berthier">Louis-Alexandre Berthier</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_5" title="March 5">March 5</a> – French troops enter <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_7" title="March 7">March 7</a> – French forces invade the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> and establish the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic_(18th_century)" title="Roman Republic (18th century)">Roman Republic</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_7" title="April 7">April 7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Territory" title="Mississippi Territory">Mississippi Territory</a> is organized by the United States from territory ceded by <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>; later it is twice expanded to include disputed territory claimed by both the U.S. and Spain (which acquired territory in trade with Great Britain).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_12" title="April 12">April 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Helvetic_Republic" title="Helvetic Republic">Helvetic Republic</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_client_republic" title="French client republic">French client republic</a>, proclaimed following collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Swiss_Confederacy" title="Old Swiss Confederacy">Old Swiss Confederacy</a> on French invasion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_26" title="April 26">April 26</a> – France annexes <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_9" title="May 9">May 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a> sets off for <a href="/wiki/Toulon" title="Toulon">Toulon</a>, and sails aboard Vice-Admiral <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois-Paul_Brueys_d%27Aigalliers" title="François-Paul Brueys d'Aigalliers">Brueys</a>'s flagship <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_ship_L%27Orient_(1791)" title="French ship L'Orient (1791)"><i>L'Orient</i></a>, his <a href="/wiki/Squadron_(naval)" title="Squadron (naval)">squadron</a> part of a larger fleet of over 300 vessels carrying almost 37,000 troops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_23" title="May 23">May 23</a> – Irish republicans and <a href="/wiki/Irish_nationalism" title="Irish nationalism">nationalists</a>, known as the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_United_Irishmen" title="Society of United Irishmen">Society of United Irishmen</a>, launch a <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rebellion_of_1798" title="Irish Rebellion of 1798">rebellion</a> against British rule in expectation of greater support from France which only sent 1100 men. The United Irishmen are unique amongst Irish nationalist movements in that they unify Catholics and Protestants around republican ideals. The rebellion rages sporadically but is defeated by the British by October.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_12" title="June 12">June 12</a>
<ul>
<li>The French take <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>.</li>
<li>A moderate <i>coup d'état</i> in the Netherlands (<a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a>) deposes <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Vreede" title="Pieter Vreede">Pieter Vreede</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_13" title="June 13">June 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mission_San_Luis_Rey_de_Francia" title="Mission San Luis Rey de Francia">Mission San Luis Rey de Francia</a> is founded.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_8">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1798&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1798">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_campaign_in_Egypt_and_Syria" title="French campaign in Egypt and Syria">Egyptian Campaign</a>: Napoleon disembarks his French army in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marabout_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marabout Bay (page does not exist)">Marabout Bay</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_7" title="July 7">July 7</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Quasi-War" title="Quasi-War">Quasi-War</a>: The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> rescinds treaties with France, sparking the war.</li>
<li>In the action of <a href="/wiki/USS_Delaware_vs_La_Croyable" title="USS Delaware vs La Croyable">USS <i>Delaware</i> vs <i>La Croyable</i></a>, the newly-formed <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> makes its first capture.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> is re-established.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_12" title="July 12">July 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shubra_Khit" title="Battle of Shubra Khit">Battle of Shubra Khit</a> between French and Mamelukes, during Napoleon's march from Alexandria to take Cairo.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_14" title="July 14">July 14</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Alien_and_Sedition_Acts" title="Alien and Sedition Acts">Alien and Sedition Acts</a> become United States law, making it a federal crime to write, publish, or utter false or malicious statements about the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">United States government</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_16" title="July 16">July 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/An_Act_for_the_relief_of_sick_and_disabled_seamen" title="An Act for the relief of sick and disabled seamen">The Relief of Sick and Disabled Seamen Act</a> is signed into law, creating the <a href="/wiki/Marine_Hospital_Service" title="Marine Hospital Service">Marine Hospital Service</a>, the forerunner to the current <a href="/wiki/United_States_Public_Health_Service_Commissioned_Corps" title="United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps">United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_21" title="July 21">July 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Pyramids" title="Battle of the Pyramids">Battle of the Pyramids</a>: Napoleon defeats <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> forces near the Pyramids.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_24" title="July 24">July 24</a> – Napoleon occupies <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_31" title="July 31">July 31</a> – A second round of elections are held in the Netherlands (<a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a>); no <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_elections" title="General elections">general elections</a> this time.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">March 3</a> – The Russo-Ottoman <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Corfu_(1798%E2%80%9399)" title="Siege of Corfu (1798–99)">siege of Corfu</a> ends with the surrender of the French garrison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_7" title="March 7">March 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a> captures <a href="/wiki/Jaffa" title="Jaffa">Jaffa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Palestine_(region)" title="Palestine (region)">Palestine</a> and his troops proceed to kill more than 2,000 <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albanian</a> captives.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_29" title="March 29">March 29</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> passes a law aimed at gradually abolishing <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> in the state.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_4" title="May 4">May 4</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Seringapatam" title="Battle of Seringapatam">Battle of Seringapatam</a>: <a href="/wiki/Tipu_Sultan" title="Tipu Sultan">Tipu Sultan</a> is defeated and killed by the British; <a href="/wiki/Captivity_of_Mangalorean_Catholics_at_Seringapatam" title="Captivity of Mangalorean Catholics at Seringapatam">captivity of Mangalorean Catholics at Seringapatam</a> ends.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_21" title="May 21">May 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Acre_(1799)" title="Siege of Acre (1799)">Siege of Acre</a> ends after two months: <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>'s attempt to widen his Middle Eastern campaign into Syria is frustrated by Ottoman forces, and he withdraws to Egypt.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/June_18" title="June 18">June 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Action_of_18_June_1799" title="Action of 18 June 1799">Action of 18 June 1799</a> (French Revolutionary Wars): a French frigate squadron under Rear-admiral <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Perr%C3%A9e" title="Jean-Baptiste Perrée">Perrée</a> is captured by the British fleet under <a href="/wiki/George_Elphinstone,_1st_Viscount_Keith" title="George Elphinstone, 1st Viscount Keith">Lord Keith</a> off <a href="/wiki/Toulon" title="Toulon">Toulon</a>.</li>

<ul>
<li>The Place Royale in Paris is renamed <i><a href="/wiki/Place_des_Vosges" title="Place des Vosges">Place des Vosges</a></i> when the Department of Vosges becomes the first to pay new Revolutionary taxes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eli_Whitney" title="Eli Whitney">Eli Whitney</a>, holding a 1798 United States government contract for the manufacture of <a href="/wiki/Musket" title="Musket">muskets</a>, is introduced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oliver_Wolcott,_Jr." title="Oliver Wolcott, Jr.">Oliver Wolcott, Jr.</a> to the concept of <a href="/wiki/Interchangeable_parts" title="Interchangeable parts">interchangeable parts</a>, an origin of the <a href="/wiki/American_system_of_manufacturing" title="American system of manufacturing">American system of manufacturing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></li>

Conrad's father John Reed learns that the rock is actually gold in <a href="/wiki/1802" title="1802">1802</a>, initiating the first gold rush in the United States.</li>
<li>The assassination of the 14th <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tu%27i_Kanokupolu" title="Tu'i Kanokupolu">Tu'i Kanokupolu</a>, Tukuʻaho, plunges <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a> into half a century of civil war.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Nawab" title="Nawab">Nawab</a> (provincial governor) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oudh" title="Oudh">Oudh</a> in northern India sends to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_III_of_England" title="George III of England">George III of England</a> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Padshah_Nama" title="Padshah Nama">Padshah Nama</a>, an official history of the reign of <a href="/wiki/Shah_Jahan" title="Shah Jahan">Shah Jahan</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Cockerill" title="William Cockerill">William Cockerill</a> begins building <a href="/wiki/Cotton-spinning_machinery" title="Cotton-spinning machinery">cotton-spinning</a> equipment in Belgium.</li>
<li>The small town of <a href="/wiki/Tignish,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Tignish, Prince Edward Island">Tignish, Prince Edward Island</a>, Canada is founded.</li>
</ul>
