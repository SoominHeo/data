<p><b>.bv</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code_top-level_domain" title="Country code top-level domain">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (ccTLD) reserved for the uninhabited <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> <a href="/wiki/Dependent_territory" title="Dependent territory">dependent territory</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bouvet_Island" title="Bouvet Island">Bouvet Island</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_registry" title="Domain name registry">domain name registry</a> and sponsor is <a href="/wiki/Norid" title="Norid">Norid</a>, but <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> is not open for registration. <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> was designated on 21 August 1997 and was placed under the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.no" title=".no">.no</a></span> registry Norid. Norwegian policy states that <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> is sufficient for those institutions connected to Bouvet Island, and therefore the domain is not open to registration. It is Norwegian policy not to commercialize domain resources, so there are no plans to sell <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span>. Should the domain later come into use, it will be under the regulation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Post_and_Telecommunications_Authority" title="Norwegian Post and Telecommunications Authority">Norwegian Post and Telecommunications Authority</a> and follow the same policy as <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.bv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bouvet_Island" title="Bouvet Island">Bouvet Island</a> is an uninhabited <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volcanic_island" title="Volcanic island">volcanic island</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_Ocean" title="South Atlantic Ocean">South Atlantic Ocean</a>. It was claimed by Norway in 1927.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wfbsv_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-wfbsv-1">[1]</a></sup> The domain was allocated on 21 August 1997,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> at the same time <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.sj" title=".sj">.sj</a></span> was allocated for <a href="/wiki/Svalbard_and_Jan_Mayen" title="Svalbard and Jan Mayen">Svalbard and Jan Mayen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The allocation occurred because the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Assigned_Numbers_Authority" title="Internet Assigned Numbers Authority">Internet Assigned Numbers Authority</a> (IANA) assigns ccTLDs to all entities with an <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166" title="ISO 3166">ISO 3166</a> code,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> for which Bouvet Island is designated <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">BV</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015, Norwegian computer scientist <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A5kon_Wium_Lie" title="Håkon Wium Lie">Håkon Wium Lie</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Left_Party_(Norway)" title="Socialist Left Party (Norway)">Socialist Left Party</a> proposed using the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> domain, along with <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.sj" title=".sj">.sj</a></span>, as online free havens. The proposal aims at protecting both the Norwegian authorities and foreign dissidents from surveillance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2012, <a href="/wiki/Norid" title="Norid">Norid</a> began an initial collaboration with the Dutch domain registry SIDN, with the purpose of examining the possibility of utilizing the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> domain on the Dutch market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Besloten_vennootschap" title="Besloten vennootschap">BV</a> is the most common form of limited company in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, which could have made <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> a popular domain. The collaboration ended in June 2016, when The Ministry of Transport and Communications advised that dispensation from certain parts of the Norwegian Domain Regulations, which would have opened for the sale of the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> domain, should not be granted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy">Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.bv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Management of <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> lies with <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a>-based Norid, which is also the domain name registry for <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> and the unused <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.sj</span>. Norid is a limited company owned by <a href="/wiki/Uninett" title="Uninett">Uninett</a>, which is owned by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Ministry_of_Education_and_Research" title="Norwegian Ministry of Education and Research">Norwegian Ministry of Education and Research</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The legal right to manage the domains is twofold, based both on an agreement with the IANA and regulations via the <i>Telecommunication Act</i> which is supervised by the <a href="/wiki/Lillesand" title="Lillesand">Lillesand</a>-based Norwegian Post and Telecommunications Authority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The policy for the use of <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> is regulated by the <i>Regulation Concerning Domain Names Under Norwegian Country Code Top-level Domains</i>, also known as the <i>Domain Regulation</i>. This regulation also regulates Norway's other two ccTLDs, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.sj</span>. Should <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> eventually come into use, the same rules and procedures as those currently regarding <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> would be used for <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The domain remains reserved for potential future use.</p>
