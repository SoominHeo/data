<p><b>JG Summit Holdings, Inc</b> is one of the largest conglomerates in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> with business interests in air transportation, banking, food manufacturing, hotels, petrochemicals, power generation, publishing, real estate and property development, and telecommunications. Companies that JG Summit Holdings has major holdings in include <a href="/wiki/Digital_Telecommunications_Philippines" title="Digital Telecommunications Philippines">Digital Telecommunications Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cebu_Pacific" title="Cebu Pacific">Cebu Pacific</a>. Its chairman is <a href="/wiki/John_Gokongwei" title="John Gokongwei">John Gokongwei</a>, one of the wealthiest individuals in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes2-3">[3]</a></sup> It was one of the ten most profitable companies on the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Stock_Exchange" title="Philippine Stock Exchange">Philippine Stock Exchange</a> for 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ManilaTimes_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ManilaTimes-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_Businesses">Former Businesses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JG_Summit_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Former Businesses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Philippine Commercial International Bank - A joint venture between Benpres and the Lopez Group of Companies. Both sold their shares in 1999 to the SSS and GSIS which gave way to the Equitable Banking Corporation - Philippine Commercial International Bank merger and eventually became Banco de Oro Universal Bank.</li>
<li>Far East Bank and Trust Company - Sold the shares to BPI during the time PCIBank was acquired by Equitable, the GSIS and the SSS.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Digital_Telecommunications_Philippines" title="Digital Telecommunications Philippines">Digital Telecommunications Philippines</a> (Digitel and <a href="/wiki/Sun_Cellular" title="Sun Cellular">Sun Cellular</a>) - Acquired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PLDT_Incorporated" title="PLDT Incorporated">PLDT</a> last March 2011. As part of the deal, JG Summit will have a 12% share in PLDT. It was finalized by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Telecommunications_Commission" title="National Telecommunications Commission">National Telecommunications Commission</a> last October 26, 2011.</li>
</ul>
