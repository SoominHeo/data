<p><b>JR Namba Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">JR難波駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Jeiāru-Nanba-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in <a href="/wiki/Namba" title="Namba">Namba</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naniwa_Ward" title="Naniwa Ward">Naniwa Ward</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, Japan, adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Namba_Station" title="Namba Station">Namba Station</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nankai_Railway" title="Nankai Railway">Nankai Railway</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osaka_Subway" title="Osaka Subway">Osaka Subway</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osaka_Namba_Station" title="Osaka Namba Station">Osaka Namba Station</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Railway" title="Kintetsu Railway">Kintetsu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hanshin_Railway" title="Hanshin Railway">Hanshin Railway</a>) operated by the <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR West).</p>
<p>JR Namba is the western terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Kansai_Main_Line" title="Kansai Main Line">Kansai Main Line</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yamatoji_Line" title="Yamatoji Line">Yamatoji Line</a>).</p>

<p>The station opened as Minatomachi Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">湊町駅</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on March 1, 1899. The station was renamed JR Namba on September 4, 1994, the day <a href="/wiki/Kansai_International_Airport" title="Kansai International Airport">Kansai International Airport</a> opened. New underground facilities opened on March 22, 1996, and replaced the former above-ground station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
