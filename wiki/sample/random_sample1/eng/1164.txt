<p><b>JTBC</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">제이티비씨</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> nationwide general <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">cable TV</a> network and broadcasting company, in which the largest shareholder is <a href="/wiki/JoongAng_Ilbo" title="JoongAng Ilbo">JoongAng Ilbo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>/JoongAng Media Network with 25% of shares.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was launched on December 1, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>JTBC is one of four new <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> nationwide general <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">cable TV</a> networks alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dong-A_Ilbo" title="Dong-A Ilbo">Dong-A Ilbo</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Channel_A_(TV_channel)" title="Channel A (TV channel)">Channel A</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chosun_Ilbo" title="Chosun Ilbo">Chosun Ilbo</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Chosun" title="TV Chosun">TV Chosun</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maeil_Kyungje" title="Maeil Kyungje">Maeil Kyungje</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Maeil_Broadcasting_Network" title="Maeil Broadcasting Network">MBN</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The four new networks supplement existing conventional free-to-air TV networks like <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HLKV-TV" title="HLKV-TV">MBC</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HLSQ-TV" title="HLSQ-TV">SBS</a> and other smaller channels launched following deregulation in 1990.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JTBC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The JoongAng Ilbo, which used to be a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samsung_Group" title="Samsung Group">Samsung Group</a>, had owned a TV station before. In 1964 it founded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tongyang_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Tongyang Broadcasting Corporation">Tongyang Broadcasting Corporation</a> (TBC) and ran the network for 16 years. In 1980 however TBC was forcibly merged with the state-run KBS by the military regime of <a href="/wiki/Chun_Doo-hwan" title="Chun Doo-hwan">Chun Doo-hwan</a>. Market watchers see the return of JoongAng Ilbo to television in JTBC as the reincarnation of TBC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Timeline:</p>
<ul>
<li>26 June 1965: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tongyang_Broadcasting" title="Tongyang Broadcasting">Tongyang Broadcasting</a> Corporation was launched.</li>
<li>7 December 1965: TBC-TV Started broadcasting on channel number 7.</li>
<li>30 November 1980: TBC-TV merged into <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS Television</a> by the special law of <a href="/wiki/Chun_Doo-hwan" title="Chun Doo-hwan">Chun Doo-hwan</a>, president of military authorities, resulting in the launching of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KBS_2TV" title="KBS 2TV">KBS 2TV</a>.</li>
<li>22 July 2009: Amendment of the Media law passed the national assembly to deregulate the media market of South Korea.</li>
<li>31 December 2010: JTBC, TV Chosun, MBN, Channel A selected as <i>General Cable Television Channel Broadcasters</i>.</li>
<li>11 March 2011: <a href="/wiki/JoongAng_Ilbo" title="JoongAng Ilbo">JoongAng Ilbo</a> established JTBC corporation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>1 December 2011: JTBC (Channel number 15) started broadcasting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>January 2015: JTBC constructs a new building in <a href="/wiki/Digital_Media_City" title="Digital Media City">Digital Media City</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sangam-dong" title="Sangam-dong">Sangam-dong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.</li>
<li>May 2015: Former <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> news anchor <a href="/wiki/Sohn_Suk-hee" title="Sohn Suk-hee">Sohn Suk-hee</a> was designated as JTBC's new president for its news division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
