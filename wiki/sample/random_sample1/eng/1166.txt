<p>The <b>JW Marriott Marquis Dubai Hotel</b> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_hotels_in_the_world" title="List of tallest hotels in the world">world's tallest hotel</a>, a 76-storey, 355 m (1,165 ft) twin-tower skyscraper complex in <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_dirham" title="United Arab Emirates dirham">AED</a>1.8 billion complex features a 1,608-room hotel run by <a href="/wiki/Marriott_International" title="Marriott International">Marriott International</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JW_Marriott_Marquis_Dubai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This project, owned by <a href="/wiki/The_Emirates_Group" title="The Emirates Group">The Emirates Group</a>, was originally conceived as a single, 350 m (1,150 ft), 77-storey tower intended to be completed in 2008 and built alongside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheikh_Zayed_Road" title="Sheikh Zayed Road">Sheikh Zayed Road</a>. However, its design and location had to be changed because of the construction of a creek extension belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Business_Bay" title="Business Bay">Business Bay</a> <a href="/wiki/Megaproject" title="Megaproject">megaproject</a>. The new twin-tower design was launched at the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Travel_Market" title="Arabian Travel Market">Arabian Travel Market</a> in Dubai in 2006 with 395 m (1,296 ft) towers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> However, the shape of the towers was modified and the height decreased to 355 m (1,165 ft) in a later redesign. The concrete structural frames of both towers topped out in April 2010. As of April 2011 the spires on both towers have been added while the outer structure of the both towers has almost been completed. The buildings were completed in 2012, surpassing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rose_Tower" title="Rose Tower">Rose Rayhaan by Rotana</a> as the world's tallest hotel.</p>
<p>With the first tower open, the hotel comprises 14 food and beverage outlets, rooftop bars, a business center, conference halls and meeting rooms, an extensive banquet hall, a 3,700 m<sup>2</sup> (40,000 sq ft) spa and health club, as well as retail outlets, a swimming pool, and a gymnasium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gulf_News_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gulf_News-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bncnetwork.net_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bncnetwork.net-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2B+G+82 storey twin towers were completed in November 2012.</p>
<p>The hotel has 1364 standard guest rooms, 240 suites, 4 "presidential suites", a banquet hall, an auditorium, 18 Shops, 19 restaurants and a spa.</p>
<p>The form is inspired by the date palm . The towers are symmetrically placed on the 7-storey high podium to get views of the Burj Khalifa, Business Bay and the sea. The podium houses all the public areas, restaurants, banquet hall and back of house areas. The entrance lobby is 4 floors high and overlooks the Business Bay. The 1000-capacity banquet hall has an independent access. The podium terrace has one of the largest spas in Dubai and is landscaped with gardens and swimming pool.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Architect Ashok Korgaonkar, founder and principal architect of Archgroup International Consultants designed the J W Marriott Marquis.</p>
