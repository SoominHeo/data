<p><b>JYP Entertainment</b> (Korean: JYP 엔터테인먼트) is a South Korean entertainment company established by <a href="/wiki/Park_Jin-young" title="Park Jin-young">Park Jin-young</a> in 1997. The company operates as a record label, talent agency, music production company, event management and concert production company, and music publishing house. It is currently one of the largest entertainment companies in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KJA_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-KJA-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>JYP Entertainment is currently the home of artists including the <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girls" title="Wonder Girls">Wonder Girls</a>, <a href="/wiki/2AM_(band)" title="2AM (band)">2AM</a>, <a href="/wiki/2PM" title="2PM">2PM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miss_A" title="Miss A">Miss A</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baek_A-yeon" title="Baek A-yeon">Baek A-yeon</a>, <a href="/wiki/15%26" title="15&amp;">15&amp;</a>, <a href="/wiki/JJ_Project" title="JJ Project">JJ Project</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sunmi" title="Sunmi">Sunmi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GOT7" title="GOT7">GOT7</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yenny_(singer)" title="Yenny (singer)">HA:TFELT</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Park" title="Bernard Park">Bernard Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/G.Soul" title="G.Soul">G.Soul</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DAY6" title="DAY6">DAY6</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Twice_(band)" title="Twice (band)">Twice</a>, and formerly to artists like <a href="/wiki/Park_Ji-yoon" title="Park Ji-yoon">Park Ji-yoon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G.o.d_(South_Korean_band)" title="G.o.d (South Korean band)">god</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rain_(entertainer)" title="Rain (entertainer)">Rain</a>.</p>
<p>It was reported that for the year 2014, JYP Entertainment took revenues of KR₩48.5 billion (approximately US$43 million) with net profits of KR₩7.85 billion (US$7.07 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JYP_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>JYP Entertainment was founded in 1997 by South Korean singer and record producer <a href="/wiki/Park_Jin-young" title="Park Jin-young">Park Jin-young</a>. Although its production and trainee system largely followed that of <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a>, it initially focused on breaking into the American market, opening the studio and headquarters of its US branch, JYP USA, in New York City on June 20, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2009, JYP Entertainment and the Jonas Group signed a management deal that allowed the <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girls" title="Wonder Girls">Wonder Girls</a> to join the <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Brothers_World_Tour_2009" title="Jonas Brothers World Tour 2009">Jonas Brothers' World Tour</a> in selected cities as the opening act.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jobro_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-jobro-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011, the US-based subsidiary JYP Creative was established and it was reported that Park invested approximately US$1.2 million in the branch. However, after a year of operation, the company reportedly saw a net loss of around US$1.5 million by the end of 2012, forcing the CEO to liquidate the branch and close all US operations, including the New York branch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="KMP_Holdings_and_KT_Music">KMP Holdings and KT Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JYP_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: KMP Holdings and KT Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/KT_Music" title="KT Music">KT Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/KMP_Holdings" title="KMP Holdings">KMP Holdings</a></div>
<p>In March 2010, <a href="/wiki/KMP_Holdings" title="KMP Holdings">KMP Holdings</a> was established via a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> between JYP, S.M. Entertainment, YG Entertainment, Star Empire, Media Line, CAN Entertainment, and Music Factory Entertainment. KMP Holdings was acquired by KT Music in November 2012, and in June 2013, KT Music absorbed KMP's distribution network. In January 2014, the seven talent agencies behind <a href="/wiki/KMP_Holdings" title="KMP Holdings">KMP Holdings</a> formed a collective bond partnership and bought 13.48% of <a href="/wiki/KT_Music" title="KT Music">KT Music</a>'s stocks, leaving <a href="/wiki/KT_Corporation" title="KT Corporation">KT Corporation</a> with 49.99%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AQ_Entertainment">AQ Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JYP_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: AQ Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 9, 2013, it was reported that JYP Entertainment had liquidated its sub-label AQ Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Asia_Management">United Asia Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JYP_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United Asia Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, JYP Entertainment joined with S.M. Entertainment, YG Entertainment, KeyEast, AMENT, and Star J Entertainment to form United Asia Management in an effort to promote Korean pop music internationally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UAM_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-UAM-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smile_Gate">Smile Gate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JYP_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Smile Gate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 17, 2013, JYP Entertainment partnered with Smile Gate to promote the game 'CrossFire' with <a href="/wiki/2PM" title="2PM">2PM</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miss_A" title="Miss A">miss A</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
