<p>"<b>Jingle Bell Rock</b>" is an American popular <a href="/wiki/Christmas_music" title="Christmas music">Christmas song</a> first released by <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Helms" title="Bobby Helms">Bobby Helms</a> in 1957. It has received frequent airplay in the United States during every <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> season since then. "Jingle Bell Rock" was composed by Joseph Carleton Beal (1900–1967) and James Ross Boothe (1917–1976). Beal was a <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Public_relations" title="Public relations">public relations</a> professional and longtime resident of South Ocean Avenue in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">Atlantic City, New Jersey</a>, and Boothe was an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> writer in the advertising business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Helms_recordings">Helms recordings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jingle_Bell_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Helms recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Jingle Bell Rock" has been performed by many, but Helms' version is the best known. The song's title and some of its lyrics are an extension of the old Christmas standard, "<a href="/wiki/Jingle_Bells" title="Jingle Bells">Jingle Bells</a>". It makes brief references to other popular songs of the 1950s, such as "<a href="/wiki/Rock_Around_the_Clock" title="Rock Around the Clock">Rock Around the Clock</a>", and mentions going to a "Jingle <a href="/wiki/Sock_hop" title="Sock hop">hop</a>". An electric guitar played by <a href="/wiki/Hank_Garland" title="Hank Garland">Hank Garland</a> can be heard playing the first notes of the chorus of "Jingle Bells". Backup singers were the <a href="/wiki/Anita_Kerr" title="Anita Kerr">Anita Kerr Quartet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Helms' original version, on <a href="/wiki/Decca_Records" title="Decca Records">Decca</a> 9-30513 from 1957, was re-recorded by him on <a href="/wiki/Kapp_Records" title="Kapp Records">Kapp</a> K-719 in 1965, and yet again in 1967 on Little Darlin' LD-0038. In 1970, Helms recorded an entire album titled <i>Jingle Bell Rock</i> on Certron C-7013, releasing the title track on Certron C-10021, with a picture sleeve. In yet another re-recording, Helms released a version on Ashley AS-4200 (year unknown). In 1983, Helms released his last recording of "Jingle Bell Rock" on Black Rose 82713.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_and_sales_performance">Chart and sales performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jingle_Bell_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chart and sales performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Helms' original version charted at No. 13 on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'</i>s Most Played C&amp;W by Jockeys chart, a predecessor to the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart. It also crossed to the pop charts, peaking at No. 6 on the <i>Billboard</i> Best Sellers in Stores chart, and at No. 11 on <i><a href="/wiki/Cashbox_(magazine)" title="Cashbox (magazine)">Cashbox</a></i> magazine's Top 60 on the week ending January 11, 1958.</p>
<p>After the song was featured on the soundtrack album to the 1996 film <i><a href="/wiki/Jingle_All_the_Way" title="Jingle All the Way">Jingle All the Way</a></i>, the original Bobby Helms version returned to the <i>Billboard</i> country singles charts in late 1996 and early 1997, reaching a peak of No. 60.</p>
<p>The Helms version entered the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart on the week ending December 24, 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> eventually reaching a peak of No. 29 two weeks later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of November 25, 2016, total sales of the digital track of the original 1957 Helms recording stand at 780,000 downloads according to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a>, placing it ninth on the list of all-time <a href="/wiki/Best-selling_Christmas/holiday_singles_in_the_United_States" title="Best-selling Christmas/holiday singles in the United States">best-selling Christmas/holiday digital singles</a> in SoundScan history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_notable_recordings">Other notable recordings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jingle_Bell_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other notable recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A notable cover version of the song was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Haley_%26_His_Comets" title="Bill Haley &amp; His Comets">Bill Haley &amp; His Comets</a> in 1968 for <a href="/wiki/United_Artists" title="United Artists">United Artists</a> records. Intended for a seasonal single release, it was not released and it was considered lost for nearly 30 years until it was finally issued in the mid-1990s.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Brenda_Lee" title="Brenda Lee">Brenda Lee</a> released a version of "Jingle Bell Rock" which made the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts"><i>Billboard</i> Christmas singles chart</a> in both 1964 and 1967.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2013, American comedian KopShtick did a parody version entitled "<b>Chanuka Rock</b>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
