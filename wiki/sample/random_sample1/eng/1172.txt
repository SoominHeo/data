<p><b>Just for Laughs</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Juste pour rire</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Comedy_festival" title="Comedy festival">comedy festival</a> held each July in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. Founded in 1983, it is the largest international <a href="/wiki/Comedy_festival" title="Comedy festival">comedy festival</a> in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<p>Just for Laughs was founded in 1983 by <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Rozon" title="Gilbert Rozon">Gilbert Rozon</a> as a two-day French language event. In 1985, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Nulman" title="Andy Nulman">Andy Nulman</a> joined the festival's staff and introduced English language events as well; under Nulman's stewardship, the festival increased to a full month, with French-speaking performers during the first half, and English speakers in the second half. International and non-verbal acts (acrobats, pantomimes, etc.) are scattered throughout the program.</p>
<p>In 1999, Nulman left the festival's full-time employ; however, he directed its major Gala Shows at the <a href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_Saint-Denis" title="ThÃ©Ã¢tre Saint-Denis">Saint-Denis Theatre</a> every July, and remained on the board of directors of the festival's parent company. After an 11-year absence, Nulman returned to Just for Laughs in July 2010 as president of Festivals and Television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Each day, performers, "New <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vaudevillians" title="Vaudevillians">Vaudevillians</a>" and other acts both vocal and visual perform throughout the city, particularly in the "<a href="/wiki/Quartier_Latin,_Montreal" title="Quartier Latin, Montreal">Latin Quarter</a>"âan area known for its theatres, cafÃ©s and boutique shopping. In the evenings, the nightclubs and live venue theatres offer special programs supporting the performers.</p>
<p>Although Just for Laughs attracts spectators from around the world, many of those in the audience are talent scouts, booking agents, producers and managers from the entertainment industry. Performing at the festival is one of the biggest opportunities for undiscovered talent to showcase their act in front of industry professionals.</p>
<p>The Just for Laughs festival also has the <a href="/wiki/Comedia_(festival)" title="Comedia (festival)">Comedia</a> comedy <a href="/wiki/Film_festival" title="Film festival">film festival</a> component, which started in 1996. It gives awards for feature and short films. 2005, Comedia screened 125 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Short_films" title="Short films">short films</a> from around the world as well as several feature-length films.</p>
<p>In February 1994, the festival sponsored a splinter project in Hollywood, Florida. That coastal area is a favourite winter destination for <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebecers</a> who head south to vacation in the warmer weather. The event, Juste Pour RireâEn Vacances (Just For LaughsâOn Vacation), was held in the Young Circle Park, an outdoor venue with an urban park setting.</p>
<p>In July 2007, Just For Laughs celebrated its 25th edition, launching a festival in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, Ontario. In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> festival was launched and aired on <a href="/wiki/TBS_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="TBS (U.S. TV channel)">TBS</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_shows">TV shows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Just_for_Laughs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: TV shows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Just for Laughs has also been a launchpad for many US network television and <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> careers, and is frequently featured as a <i>foreign on-location</i> highlight of late-night US network talk shows.</p>
<p>Just For Laughs films many performances at the annual festival in Montreal and the TV productions from these tapings air regularly on <a href="/wiki/CBC_Television" title="CBC Television">CBC Television</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Comedy_Network" title="The Comedy Network">The Comedy Network</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TVA_(TV_network)" title="TVA (TV network)">TVA</a> and <a href="/wiki/YTV_(TV_channel)" title="YTV (TV channel)">YTV</a>. The shows feature stand-up performances from the festival and sometimes include "canned bits" shot on location around <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, Canada.</p>
<p>During the summer of 2007, <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> aired episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Just_for_Laughs:_Gags" title="Just for Laughs: Gags">Just for Laughs: Gags</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It consisted of two half-hour episodes, and aired mostly on Tuesdays from 8:00 pm to 9:00 pm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone_(North_America)" title="Eastern Time Zone (North America)">ET</a>.</p>
<p>The content and format was exactly the same as the Canadian counterpart, but was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Rick_Miller_(comedian)" title="Rick Miller (comedian)">Rick Miller</a>, who introduced each segment with a quirky pun. The show returned to the ABC lineup as a <a href="/wiki/Mid-season_replacement" title="Mid-season replacement">mid-season replacement</a> on January 1, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The series was initially not renewed after the half-season, but ABC reversed their decision.</p>
<p>The third season of the show, titled simply <i>Just for Laughs</i>, began airing on June 21, 2009 at 7:00 pm ET.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The program returned in the 2014-15 season under the <i>Just for Laughs: Gags</i> title as a syndicated offering to the United States through PPI Releasing, which mainly specializes in syndicating Canadian programming to American stations.</p>
<p><i>Just for Laughs: All Access</i>, which airs on The Comedy Network, airs recordings of live stand-up comedy performances from the festival.</p>
