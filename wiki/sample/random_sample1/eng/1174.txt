<p><b>K–12</b> (spoken as "k twelve", "k through twelve", or "k to twelve") comprises the sum of <a href="/wiki/Primary_education" title="Primary education">primary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Secondary_education" title="Secondary education">secondary education</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_United_States" title="Education in the United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Canada" title="Education in Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_South_Korea" title="Education in South Korea">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Turkey" title="Education in Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_Philippines" title="Education in the Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Egypt" title="Education in Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Australia" title="Education in Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_India" title="Education in India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Iran" title="Education in Iran">Iran</a>. The expression is a shortening of <a href="/wiki/Kindergarten" title="Kindergarten">kindergarten</a> (K) for 4- to 6-year-olds through <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_grade" title="Twelfth grade">twelfth grade</a> (12) for 17- to 19-year-olds, the first and last <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grade_level" title="Grade level">grades</a> of <a href="/wiki/Free_education" title="Free education">free education</a> in these countries, respectively. The related term <b>P–12</b> is also occasionally used in Australia and the United States, to refer to the sum of K–12 plus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preschool_education" title="Preschool education">preschool education</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K%E2%80%9312&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term is often used in school website <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/URL" title="URL">URLs</a>, generally appearing before the <a href="/wiki/Country_code_top-level_domain" title="Country code top-level domain">country code top-level domain</a> (or in the United States, the state top-level domain). The term "PK–12" is sometimes used to add <a href="/wiki/Pre-kindergarten" title="Pre-kindergarten">pre-kindergarten</a>.</p>
<p>It is also used by American multinationals selling into the educational sector, such as <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a> where UK customers are presented with this as a market segment choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="P.E2.80.9312">P–12</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K%E2%80%9312&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: P–12">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Australia, P–12 is sometimes used in place of K–12, particularly in <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, where it is used as an official term in the curriculum framework.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> P–12 schools serve children for the thirteen years from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preschool_education" title="Preschool education">prep</a> until <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Year_12" title="Year 12">Year 12</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> without including the separate kindergarten component. In Canada (Nova Scotia) P–12 is used commonly in place of K–12 and serves students from grade Primary through 12.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="K.E2.80.9314.2C_K.E2.80.9316.2C_K.E2.80.9318_and_K.E2.80.9320">K–14, K–16, K–18 and K–20</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K%E2%80%9312&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: K–14, K–16, K–18 and K–20">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>K–14 education also includes <a href="/wiki/Community_college" title="Community college">community colleges</a> (the first two years of university). <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=K%E2%80%9316K%E2%80%9316_education&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="K–16K–16 education (page does not exist)">K–16K–16 education</a> adds a four-year undergraduate university degree. For simplicity purposes education shorthand was created to denote specific education levels of achievement. This shorthand is commonly used in articles, publications, and educational legislations. The following list contains the most commonly found shorthand descriptors:</p>
<ul>
<li>P–14: Pre-School to Two-Year Degree</li>
<li>P–16: Pre-School to Four-Year Degree</li>
<li>P–18: Pre-School to master's degree</li>
<li>P–20: Pre-School to Graduate Degree</li>
<li>K–14: Kindergarten to Two-Year Degree</li>
<li>K–16: Kindergarten to Four-Year Degree</li>
<li>K–18: Kindergarten to master's degree</li>
<li>K–20: Kindergarten to Graduate Degree</li>
</ul>
<p>The Career Technical Education (CTE) Unit of the California Community College Economic Development and Workforce Preparation Division focuses on program coordination and advocacy, policy development and coordination with K–18 workforce preparation and career and technical education systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ASCCC Chancellor's Office Career Technical Education (CTE) Unit of the Economic Development and Workforce Preparation Division focuses on program coordination and advocacy, policy development and coordination with K–18 workforce preparation and career and technical education systems. Responsible for the implementation of the Vocational and Technical Education Act (VTEA), managing and coordinating activities that impact other interagency and intra-agency objectives. In addition, the CTE Unit is also responsible for the development, dissemination, and implementation of the California State Plan and the annual performance reports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following link is a table that defines the education system in the United States. The table shows the progression of the education system starting with the basic K–12 system then progressing through post-secondary education. K–14 refers to K–12 plus two years of post-secondary where training was received from vocational technical institutions or community or junior colleges. The K numbers refer to the years of educational attainment and continues to progress upward accordingly depending on the degree being sought.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Further reference to K–18 education can be found in this publication by Ann Diver-Stamnes and Linda Catelli<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> in chapter 4 "College/University Partnership Projects for Instituting Change and Improvement in K–18 Education".</p>
