<p>"<b>(You Drive Me) Crazy</b>" is a song recorded by American singer <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> for her debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(album)" title="...Baby One More Time (album)">...Baby One More Time</a></i> (1999). Written and produced by <a href="/wiki/Max_Martin" title="Max Martin">Max Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Per_Magnusson" title="Per Magnusson">Per Magnusson</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Kreuger" title="David Kreuger">David Kreuger</a>, with additional writing by <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B6rgen_Elofsson" title="Jörgen Elofsson">Jörgen Elofsson</a> and remix by Martin and <a href="/wiki/Rami_Yacoub" title="Rami Yacoub">Rami Yacoub</a>, it was released as the album's third single on August 23, 1999, by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JIVE_Records" title="JIVE Records">JIVE Records</a>. It was remixed for the soundtrack of <i><a href="/wiki/Drive_Me_Crazy" title="Drive Me Crazy">Drive Me Crazy</a></i>. "(You Drive Me) Crazy" is a pop song. The song garnered positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>, some of whom praised its simple formula and noted similarities to Spears' debut single, "<a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(song)" title="...Baby One More Time (song)">...Baby One More Time</a>".</p>
<p>"(You Drive Me) Crazy" was a commercial success, and peaked inside the top ten on the singles charts of seventeen countries. In the United Kingdom, it became Spears' third consecutive single to peak inside the top five, while it reached number 10 in the United States' <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, and peaked at number one in Belgium (Wallonia). An accompanying music video, directed by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Dick" title="Nigel Dick">Nigel Dick</a>, and portrayed Spears as a waitress of a dance club, and performed a highly choreographed dance routine with the other waitresses. The video premiered on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Making_the_Video" title="Making the Video">Making the Video</a></i> special, and featured <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearances</a> of actors <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Joan_Hart" title="Melissa Joan Hart">Melissa Joan Hart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Grenier" title="Adrian Grenier">Adrian Grenier</a>. As part of promotion for the song, Spears performed the song at the <a href="/wiki/1999_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="1999 MTV Europe Music Awards">1999 MTV Europe Music Awards</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards" title="Billboard Music Awards">1999 Billboard Music Awards</a>. It has also been included on five of her concert tours.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=(You_Drive_Me)_Crazy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before recording her debut album, Spears had originally envisioned it in style of "<a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a> music, but younger [and] more adult contemporary".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rs1-1">[1]</a></sup> However, the singer agreed with her label's appointment of producers, who had the objective to target a teenage audience at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rs1-1">[1]</a></sup> She flew to <a href="/wiki/Cheiron_Studios" title="Cheiron Studios">Cheiron Studios</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, where half of the album was recorded from March to April 1998, with producers <a href="/wiki/Max_Martin" title="Max Martin">Max Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denniz_Pop" title="Denniz Pop">Denniz Pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rami_Yacoub" title="Rami Yacoub">Rami Yacoub</a>, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-rs1-1">[1]</a></sup> "(You Drive Me) Crazy" was written by <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B6rgen_Elofsson" title="Jörgen Elofsson">Jörgen Elofsson</a>, while song production and additional songwriting was done by Martin, <a href="/wiki/Per_Magnusson" title="Per Magnusson">Per Magnusson</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Kreuger" title="David Kreuger">David Kreuger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-liner-2">[2]</a></sup> Spears recorded the vocals for the song in March 1998, at Cheiron Studios in Stockholm, Sweden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-liner-2">[2]</a></sup> It was also mixed at Cheiron Studios by Martin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-liner-2">[2]</a></sup> Esbjörn Öhrwall and Johan Carlberg played the <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a> was done by Thomas Lindberg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-liner-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keyboards" title="Keyboards">Keyboards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Programming_(music)" title="Programming (music)">programming</a> was done by Kreuger, and additional keyboards by Magnusson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-liner-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Background_vocals" title="Background vocals">Background vocals</a> were provided by Jeanette Söderholm, Martin, Yacoub and THE FANCHOIR, formed by Chatrin Nyström, Jeanette Stenhammar, Johanna Stenhammar, Charlotte Björkman and Therese Ancker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-liner-2">[2]</a></sup> In May 1999, Martin and Spears went to the Battery Studios in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_City,_New_York" title="New York City, New York">New York City, New York</a>, to re-record the vocals of the track,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> due to the fact that a remixed version called "The Stop! Remix" was going to be included on the original motion picture soundtrack of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Drive_Me_Crazy" title="Drive Me Crazy">Drive Me Crazy</a></i> (1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> "(You Drive Me) Crazy" was released as a remix package as the third single from <i>...Baby One More Time</i> on August 23, 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britstory_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-britstory-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>"(You Drive Me) Crazy" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bp92_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-bp92-6">[6]</a></sup> The song's composition follows a simple formula and infuses edgy synthesized instruments, including a recurring <a href="/wiki/Cowbell_(instrument)" title="Cowbell (instrument)">cowbell</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bp92_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-bp92-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abcom_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-abcom-7">[7]</a></sup> and having a roughly similar sound to Spears' debut single "...Baby One More Time" (1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sputnik_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-sputnik-8">[8]</a></sup> According to the sheet music published at Musicnotes.com by Universal Music Publishing Group, "(You Drive Me) Crazy" is composed in the key of <a href="/wiki/C_minor" title="C minor">C minor</a> and runs through a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Down-tempo" title="Down-tempo">moderately slow</a> <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> <a href="/wiki/Beat_(music)" title="Beat (music)">beat</a> infused metronome of 92 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sheet_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-sheet-9">[9]</a></sup> Spears' vocals were deemed as heavily processed when compared to the ones of her previous single, "<a href="/wiki/Sometimes_(Britney_Spears_song)" title="Sometimes (Britney Spears song)">Sometimes</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Her vocal range spans over an octave, from the low-key of G<small>3</small> to the high-note of D<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span><small>5</small>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sheet_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-sheet-9">[9]</a></sup> The song's primary <a href="/wiki/Chord_progression" title="Chord progression">chord progression</a> is Cm–A<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span>-G (vi-IV-III), with a few deviations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sheet_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-sheet-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=(You_Drive_Me)_Crazy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song garnered positive reviews from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_critics" title="Music critics">music critics</a>. Kyle Anderson for <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> considered "(You Drive Me) Crazy" as "a similar-sounding anthem [to '...Baby One More Time'] with some streamlined rock guitar taking center stage (there's even a solo). It's catchy enough."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Spence D. of <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> considered "(You Drive Me) Crazy" a "[Max] Martin's glossy grown-up pop" song,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ign_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ign-12">[12]</a></sup> while Caryn Ganz of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> called "(You Drive Me) Crazy" a "further hit" from <i>...Baby One More Time</i>, along with "<a href="/wiki/From_the_Bottom_of_My_Broken_Heart" title="From the Bottom of My Broken Heart">From the Bottom of My Broken Heart</a>" and "Sometimes".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Music critic Walt Mueller wrote "When Spears starts to sing on this one, she sounds a lot like <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Christy Lemire of the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> noted that the song and "Stronger" are "so lamely feel-good" tracks that they "could have been the theme song to a 'Karate Kid' sequel."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbc_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-msnbc-15">[15]</a></sup> Evan Sawdey of <a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a> called it a "lightly dorky" song,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popmatters_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-popmatters-16">[16]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> deemed it as a "fluffy dance-pop at its best."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In a list compiled by Sara Anderson of <a href="/wiki/AOL_Radio" title="AOL Radio">AOL Radio</a>, "(You Drive Me) Crazy" was ranked ninth in a list of Spears' best songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aolradio_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-aolradio-18">[18]</a></sup> During the 2001 BMI Pop Awards, "(You Drive Me) Crazy" was honored with the award of Most Performed BMI Song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>"(You Drive Me) Crazy" was a commercial success. The song peaked at number two on the <a href="/wiki/European_Hot_100_Singles" title="European Hot 100 Singles">European Hot 100 Singles</a>, being held off the top spot by <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/If_I_Could_Turn_Back_the_Hands_of_Time" title="If I Could Turn Back the Hands of Time">If I Could Turn Back the Hands of Time</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-europe_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-europe-20">[20]</a></sup> In the United Kingdom, it was Spears' third consecutive single to reach a top five position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_charts_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-UK_charts-21">[21]</a></sup> The track debuted and peaked at number five on the chart issue dated October 2, 1999, and stayed on the chart for a total of eleven weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ukcs_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ukcs-22">[22]</a></sup> It was eventually certified Silver by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">British Phonographic Industry</a> (BPI), for shipments over 200,000 units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ukcert_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ukcert-23">[23]</a></sup> According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Official_Charts_Company" title="The Official Charts Company">The Official Charts Company</a>, "(You Drive Me) Crazy" is Spears' seventh best-selling single in the United Kingdom, with sales over 275,000 physical units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The song peaked at number two in France and number four in Germany, being certified Gold in both countries for shipping over 250,000 units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultratop_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-ultratop-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ger_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ger-26">[26]</a></sup> It also peaked at number one in Belgium (Wallonia), finishing the year of 1999 as the 17th best-selling single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultratop_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-ultratop-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vl_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-vl-27">[27]</a></sup> "(You Drive Me) Crazy" was also able to peak inside the top five in Belgium (Flanders), Finland, Ireland, Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, and Switzerland, while reaching top ten positions in Denmark and Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultratop_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-ultratop-25">[25]</a></sup> Later in 2012, the song managed to peak at number 65 in Czech Republic due to high airplay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cr_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-cr-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United States, "(You Drive Me) Crazy" peaked at number ten on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> on the chart issue dated November 13, 1999, and became Spears' second single to peak inside the top ten in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charthistoryus_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-charthistoryus-29">[29]</a></sup> On the same week, it peaked at number four on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40_(Pop_Songs)" title="Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)">Pop Songs</a> component chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charthistoryus_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-charthistoryus-29">[29]</a></sup> On the chart compiled by <i><a href="/wiki/RPM_(magazine)" title="RPM (magazine)">RPM</a></i> magazine, the song peaked at number three in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rpm_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-rpm-30">[30]</a></sup> However, on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> compiled by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nielsen_Soundscan" title="Nielsen Soundscan">Nielsen Soundscan</a>, it peaked at number 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charthistoryus_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-charthistoryus-29">[29]</a></sup> The latter revealed that "(You Drive Me) Crazy" was the 44th best-selling single of 1999 in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caye_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-caye-31">[31]</a></sup> The track peaked at number five in New Zealand, but failed to reach the top ten in Australia, where it peaked at number 12 on the chart issue dated November 12, 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultratop_25-3"><a href="#cite_note-ultratop-25">[25]</a></sup> However, the single was later certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a> (ARIA),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auscert_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-auscert-32">[32]</a></sup> and was one of the best-selling singles of 2000 in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endaus_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-endaus-33">[33]</a></sup> "(You Drive Me) Crazy" performed poorly in Japan, where it peaked at number 80, and stayed on the chart for two weeks only. Despite the low sales, it is Spears' 12th best-selling CD release in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jp_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-jp-34">[34]</a></sup></p>

<p>The song's accompanying <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was directed by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Dick" title="Nigel Dick">Nigel Dick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> Spears conceptualized the video's treatment, and explained during an interview with MTV in 1999 that "it would be cool to be in a club, and we're dorky waitresses, and we break out and start dancing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> At the time, Spears expected that the video would take her "to the next level".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> To promote the film <i><a href="/wiki/Drive_Me_Crazy" title="Drive Me Crazy">Drive Me Crazy</a></i>, actors <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Grenier" title="Adrian Grenier">Adrian Grenier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Joan_Hart" title="Melissa Joan Hart">Melissa Joan Hart</a> were invited to make <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearances</a> in the video, since the song had been included on the film's soundtrack, however, Grenier did not want to participate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-3"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> Dick commented on the issue, saying, "I was given instructions to ring him up and make sure he appeared in the video. I said, 'You know what, Adrian, I just think it would be great for your career, and Britney's a great girl and she's fun to work with.' Eventually he came around."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-4"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> Dick also revealed that he was impressed by the singer's work ethic, adding that she "came to the set completely rehearsed."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-5"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> The music video premiered on MTV's <i><a href="/wiki/Making_the_Video" title="Making the Video">Making the Video</a></i> special that aired on July 18, 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>The video opens with Spears as a waitress of a dance club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-6"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> She then goes with other waitresses to their dressing room, where they finish their make-up and change costumes. Spears, now wearing a green sparkly outfit, goes through the corridor to the dance floor with her friends, and starts to perform a high-profile choreography, including a chair dance sequence referencing <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Miss_You_Much" title="Miss You Much">Miss You Much</a>" video, which Spears also referenced in live performances of the song on the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crazy_2K_Tour" title="Crazy 2K Tour">Crazy 2K Tour</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-7"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> Scenes of Spears singing in front of a shining orange sign with the word "CRAZY" are also seen throughout the video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvarticle_35-8"><a href="#cite_note-mtvarticle-35">[35]</a></sup> On August 24, 1999, the music video debuted at number four on <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">Total Request Live</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trl_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-trl-37">[37]</a></sup> It is the longest running by a female artist on <i>TRL</i>, staying on the top ten for seventy-three days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aolradio_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-aolradio-18">[18]</a></sup> The video was nominated on the category of Best Dance Video on the <a href="/wiki/2000_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2000 MTV Video Music Awards">2000 MTV Video Music Awards</a>; it lost, however, to <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Waiting_for_Tonight" title="Waiting for Tonight">Waiting for Tonight</a>" (1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> An alternate footage of the video can be found on the DVD of Spears first compilation album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_My_Prerogative_(DVD)" title="Greatest Hits: My Prerogative (DVD)">Greatest Hits: My Prerogative</a></i> (2004).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alternatefootage_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-alternatefootage-39">[39]</a></sup> Jennifer Vineyard of MTV commented, "the alternate audio gives the feel of Spears singing the song as a round, where the beat is in sync but one layer of her vocals is just slightly ahead of the other."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alternatefootage_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-alternatefootage-39">[39]</a></sup></p>

<p>As part of promotion for "(You Drive Me) Crazy"<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s release as a single, Spears performed it at the <a href="/wiki/1999_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="1999 MTV Europe Music Awards">1999 MTV Europe Music Awards</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards" title="Billboard Music Awards">1999 Billboard Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> It was also performed on five concert tours, the first being the <a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour" title="...Baby One More Time Tour">...Baby One More Time Tour</a> (1999). The show began with a dance introduction by Spears' dancers among smoke effects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-42">[42]</a></sup> She appeared shortly after at the top of the staircase wearing a hot pink vinyl <a href="/wiki/Tube_top" title="Tube top">tube top</a> and white vinyl pants with pink knee patches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-42">[42]</a></sup> During the 2000 leg of the tour, entitled <a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour" title="...Baby One More Time Tour">Crazy 2k Tour</a>, Spears changed the opening sequence of the show; the show started with a skit in which the dancers came out of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Locker_(cabinet)" title="Locker (cabinet)">lockers</a> and stayed in the stage until a bell rang. They all sat until a female teacher voice started calling their names. After the teacher called Spears, she emerged at the top of the staircase in a cloud of smoke, wearing a top and white stretch pants, to perform a short dance mix of "...Baby One More Time".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-rolling-43">[43]</a></sup> She then entered one of the lockers and appeared in another one on the opposite side of the stage to perform "(You Drive Me) Crazy", which included a chair dance sequence referencing <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Miss_You_Much" title="Miss You Much">Miss You Much</a>" music video that ended with Spears saying "Is that the end?", quoting Jackson's phrase from the video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stpeter_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-stpeter-44">[44]</a></sup> The song was once again performed in a dance-oriented form on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_World_Tour" title="Oops!... I Did It Again World Tour">Oops!... I Did It Again World Tour</a> (2000),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dnrw_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-dnrw-45">[45]</a></sup> while in 2001's <a href="/wiki/Dream_Within_a_Dream_Tour" title="Dream Within a Dream Tour">Dream Within a Dream Tour</a>, the performance featured Spears being captured by her dancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvreview_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvreview-46">[46]</a></sup> "(You Drive Me) Crazy" was also performed on <a href="/wiki/The_Onyx_Hotel_Tour" title="The Onyx Hotel Tour">The Onyx Hotel Tour</a> (2004). For the tour, the song was remixed with elements of <a href="/wiki/Latin_percussion" title="Latin percussion">latin percussion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbcp_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-msnbcp-47">[47]</a></sup> "Crazy" would not be performed by Spears for another nine years until it was included on the setlist of her Las Vegas residency show <a href="/wiki/Britney:_Piece_of_Me" title="Britney: Piece of Me">Britney: Piece of Me</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2003, American musician <a href="/wiki/Richard_Cheese" title="Richard Cheese">Richard Cheese</a> covered "(You Drive Me) Crazy" and included it on his album <i><a href="/wiki/Tuxicity" title="Tuxicity">Tuxicity</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> American pop band <a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez_%26_the_Scene" title="Selena Gomez &amp; the Scene">Selena Gomez &amp; the Scene</a> performed a homage to Spears during their 2011 <a href="/wiki/We_Own_the_Night_Tour" title="We Own the Night Tour">We Own the Night Tour</a>. They performed "(You Drive Me) Crazy" along with a medley of hits that included "<a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(song)" title="...Baby One More Time (song)">...Baby One More Time</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_(song)" title="Oops!... I Did It Again (song)">Oops!... I Did It Again</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Slave_4_U" title="I'm a Slave 4 U">I'm a Slave 4 U</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Toxic_(song)" title="Toxic (song)">Toxic</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Hold_It_Against_Me" title="Hold It Against Me">Hold It Against Me</a>", mixed similar to the Chris Cox Megamix included in <i>Greatest Hits: My Prerogative</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In the 2012 <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Britney_2.0" title="Britney 2.0">Britney 2.0</a>", the characters of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marley_Rose" title="Marley Rose">Marley Rose</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jake_Puckerman" title="Jake Puckerman">Jake Puckerman</a> performed a medley of the track with <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_(Aerosmith_song)" title="Crazy (Aerosmith song)">Crazy</a>" (1993).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
