<p>The <b>KAI KUH-1 Surion</b> is a twin-engine, transport <a href="/wiki/Utility_helicopter" title="Utility helicopter">utility helicopter</a> developed primarily by <a href="/wiki/Korea_Aerospace_Industries" title="Korea Aerospace Industries">Korea Aerospace Industries</a> (KAI), <a href="/wiki/Agency_for_Defense_Development" title="Agency for Defense Development">Agency for Defense Development</a> (ADD) and <a href="/wiki/Korea_Aerospace_Research_Institute" title="Korea Aerospace Research Institute">Korea Aerospace Research Institute</a> (KARI) jointly with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eurocopter" title="Eurocopter">Eurocopter</a>. In 2006, the research and development phase of the Korea Helicopter Project - Korea Utility Helicopter (KHP-KUH), costing around 1.3 trillion won ($1.2 billion), was launched by the Agency for Defense Development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DN_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-DN-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-News-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2012, full-scale production of the Surion commenced; KAI has been designated as the principal manufacturer of the type.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-News-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>An initial force of around 245 Surions have been ordered by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Army" title="Republic of Korea Army">Republic of Korea Army</a> to replace their aging fleets of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UH-1_Iroquois" title="UH-1 Iroquois">UH-1H</a> utility helicopters and <a href="/wiki/MD_Helicopters_MD_500" title="MD Helicopters MD 500">500MD</a> light utility helicopters,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-News-8">[8]</a></sup> which have been in service for decades. KAI shall also construct civilian and law enforcement variants of the helicopter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-News-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KAI_KUH-1_Surion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KAI_KUH-1_Surion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2005, the South Korean government appointed Eurocopter as the primary partner to KAI for the then-formative Korea Helicopter Project - Korea Utility Helicopter (KHP-KUH).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_commit_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-2011_commit-9">[9]</a></sup> In June 2006, KAI and Eurocopter won the KHP-KUH 1.3 trillion-won ($1.2 billion) research and development contract from the <a href="/wiki/Defense_Acquisition_Program_Administration" title="Defense Acquisition Program Administration">Defense Acquisition Program Administration</a> (DAPA) to start the project. It remains Seoul's biggest arms deal ever with a non-US company. The development of the aircraft was funded 84% by the South Korean government and 16% by <a href="/wiki/Korea_Aerospace_Industries" title="Korea Aerospace Industries">Korea Aerospace Industries</a> (KAI) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eurocopter" title="Eurocopter">Eurocopter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_flight_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-first_flight-11">[11]</a></sup> At the time, it was the biggest South Korean defense contract to be issued to a non-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> defense company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-audit_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-audit-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>A production contract worth about 4.4 trillion won ($4.3 billion) is expected to be signed around 2011 before the start of mass production. Eurocopter took a stake of 30 percent in the 2006-2012 development phase and 20 percent in the following ten-year production phase, KAI and the South Korean government held the remaining stakes in the program. As the prime sub-contractor, Eurocopter has provided technical assistance in part for developing the power transmission, main gearbox, boom and tail gearboxes, automatic flight control system and rotor mast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-News-8">[8]</a></sup> Eurocopter experts were dispatched to KAI premises (Sacheon, South Korea) to provide support and teach KAI engineers state of the art processes and technologies for the design and manufacture of helicopters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-News-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>In June 2008, KAI announced that the first prototype KUH was to be rolled in the following month and that ground tests would begin later that year; the firm also stated that it aimed to conduct the type's first flight in early 2010 and for the first production aircraft to be delivered in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In August 2009, the first prototype was unveiled by President Lee Myung-bak at an unveiling ceremony in Sacheon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Defense_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Defense-14">[14]</a></sup> In October 2009, it was announced that the program was to be delayed as the result of several ministries having received reduced budgets as greater priority had been placed on social welfare programs; the Ministry of National Defense announced that stability of existing defense programs had been assured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 March 2010, KAI announced that a prototype had performed the <a href="/wiki/Maiden_flight" title="Maiden flight">maiden flight</a> of the Surion; two test pilots and an engineer had performed a series of taxiing and hovering maneuvers, as well as a stationary hover at 30 ft (9.1m), during this initial flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_flight_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-first_flight-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In May 2010, following three months of flight testing, the prototype performed its first public flight demonstration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In January 2011, Eurocopter and KAI established a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a>, KAI-EC, for the purposes of marketing the Surion and handling export sales; at the time, it was envisioned that 250-300 units would be sold worldwide by 2021.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_commit_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-2011_commit-9">[9]</a></sup> In December 2012, deliveries of the first Surion model formally commenced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kai_history_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-kai_history-18">[18]</a></sup> In February 2013, low temperature testing in <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>, United States, was completed, leading to development of the KUH-1 Surion being formally recognized as completed in the following month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kai_history_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-kai_history-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_development">Further development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KAI_KUH-1_Surion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Further development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The KUH-1 Surion served as the basis for a navalised derivative, the Korean Naval Helicopter (KNH). By 2011, the KNH had entered into the development stage; work was being performed on the project by a partnership between KAI, Eurocopter, and <a href="/wiki/Elbit_Systems" title="Elbit Systems">Elbit Systems</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_commit_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-2011_commit-9">[9]</a></sup> In January 2016, following completion of development work on the amphibious variant of the Surion, it was announced that this variant had been cleared to enter production later that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Various specialised models and derivatives of the KUH-1 have been proposed. In October 2009, KAI revealed that it was studying the potential for developing an indigenous <a href="/wiki/Attack_helicopter" title="Attack helicopter">attack helicopter</a> based upon the KUH platform as one available option for meeting an established Korean Army requirement for a new attack helicopter (AH-X). This variant was reportedly to possibly share up to 70 per cent component commonality with the base KUH version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In December 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Forest_Service" title="Korea Forest Service">Korea Forest Service</a>, the largest civil government helicopter operator, ordered a firefighting version of the KUH-1 Surion; this requires a special <a href="/wiki/Airworthiness_certificate" title="Airworthiness certificate">airworthiness certificate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, DAPA's Board of Audit and Inspection begun an investigation into the level of indigenous technology used on the Surion; the investigation revealed that the technology transfer arrangement with Airbus Helicopters only covered 134 out of the 450 components used in the rotorcraft's power delivery system amid allegations of a breach of contract, Airbus Helicopters responded that it had honoured the contractual terms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-audit_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-audit-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2015, KAI announced plans to increase production of the Surion from one helicopter per month to three per month in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flightglobal23oct15_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-flightglobal23oct15-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 2011 and 2016, the <a href="/wiki/National_Aerospace_Laboratory" title="National Aerospace Laboratory">National Aerospace Laboratory</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> conducted a technology demonstration program under contract from KAI of an experimental <a href="/wiki/Fly-by-wire" title="Fly-by-wire">fly-by-wire</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flight_control_system" title="Flight control system">flight control system</a> for the Surion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>KAI is offering the Surion to international markets for military and civilian purposes. In late 2013, it was reported that KAI had received requests for proposals regarding the Surion from two South American nations and another Asian nation; at the time, KAI stated that the company hoped to sell 60-120 over the following 15–20 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2013_export_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-2013_export-1">[1]</a></sup> International marketing efforts are expected to escalate in 2017, as prior to this point the overwhelming priority had been to fully develop the Surion to conform with existing domestic requirements and roles. KAI has deliberately focused on marketing the type to countries in which previous export success had been found for the <a href="/wiki/KAI_T-50_Golden_Eagle" title="KAI T-50 Golden Eagle">KAI T-50 Golden Eagle</a> and <a href="/wiki/KAI_KT-1_Woongbi" title="KAI KT-1 Woongbi">KAI KT-1 Woongbi</a> trainer aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flightglobal23oct15_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-flightglobal23oct15-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KAI_KUH-1_Surion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1667" data-file-width="2500" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg/220px-KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg/330px-KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg/440px-KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:KUH-1_Surion_in_Alaska.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
KUH-1 Surion, 2012</div>

<p>The KAI KUH-1 Surion is a twin-engine medium-sized multipurpose rotorcraft, carrying upon to nine troops along with a crew of four (two pilots and two gunners in the main cabin area) in a utility transport capacity. It has been designed to be rapidly reconfigured to serve different roles, some models are also <a href="/wiki/Navalised_aircraft" title="Navalised aircraft">navalised</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_commit_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-2011_commit-9">[9]</a></sup> The Surion can perform various duties and roles, such as <a href="/wiki/Military_transport_aircraft" title="Military transport aircraft">military transport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Police_aviation" title="Police aviation">law enforcement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aerial_reconnaissance" title="Aerial reconnaissance">aerial observation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Military_helicopter#Maritime_helicopters" title="Military helicopter">maritime support</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aerial_firefighting" title="Aerial firefighting">aerial firefighting</a>, and for civilian purposes along with other missions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kai_prod_page_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-kai_prod_page-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Power is provided by a pair of 1,600 shp <a href="/wiki/Hanwha_Techwin" title="Hanwha Techwin">Hanwha Techwin</a> T700-ST-701K <a href="/wiki/Turboshaft" title="Turboshaft">turboshaft</a> engines, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Licence-built" title="Licence-built">licence-built</a> localized development of the <a href="/wiki/General_Electric_T700" title="General Electric T700">General Electric T700</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The T700/701K, co-developed by <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> and Hanwha Techwin, is the first rear-drive variant of the T700 engine and features a -701D common core, high-efficiency counter-rotating power turbine, and a new <a href="/wiki/FADEC" title="FADEC">FADEC</a> system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The exhausts of the engines are equipped with large <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> suppressors; these have been adapted from those used on the <a href="/wiki/Eurocopter_EC725" title="Eurocopter EC725">Eurocopter EC725</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Defense_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Defense-14">[14]</a></sup> Airbus Helicopters manufactures various elements of the Surion's <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">transmission</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> the lower-than-intended level of indigenous production of these components has been a point of controversy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-audit_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-audit-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Surion is equipped with various survivability and damage reduction features. Both the airframe and the cockpit have been made <a href="/wiki/Bulletproofing" title="Bulletproofing">bulletproof</a> against <a href="/wiki/Small_arms" title="Small arms">small arms</a> fire. The airframe, tail rotor, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rotor_blade" title="Rotor blade">rotor blades</a> used on both the main and tail rotors are of a <a href="/wiki/Crashworthiness" title="Crashworthiness">crashworthy</a> construction; anti-explosion sealed <a href="/wiki/Fuel_tank" title="Fuel tank">fuel tanks</a> are also used. The main gearbox is capable of flying for a limited period after suffering the loss of lubricating oil. Various electronic defensive measures are incorporated onto the Surion, such as a <a href="/wiki/Infrared_countermeasure" title="Infrared countermeasure">countermeasures</a> dispenser system (CMDS), <a href="/wiki/Radar_warning_receiver" title="Radar warning receiver">radar warning receiver</a> (RWR), <a href="/wiki/Laser_warning_receiver" title="Laser warning receiver">laser warning receiver</a> (LWR).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kai_prod_page_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-kai_prod_page-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>British firm <a href="/wiki/Cobham_plc" title="Cobham plc">Cobham plc</a> provides navigation and communication equipment for the Surion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Sandle Avionic's HeliTAWS multi-hazard avoidance system is installed on Surions in police service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Elbit_Systems" title="Elbit Systems">Elbit Systems</a> of Israel produces the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helmet_mounted_display" title="Helmet mounted display">helmet mounted display</a> (HMD) used on the Surion, the HMD allows the crew to conduct full day-and-night operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Defense_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-Defense-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 22 May 2013, a handover ceremony of ten Surions was conducted to mark the helicopter's deployment at the Army Aviation School in <a href="/wiki/Nonsan" title="Nonsan">Nonsan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Chungcheong_Province" title="South Chungcheong Province">South Chungcheong Province</a>, attended by President <a href="/wiki/Park_Geun-hye" title="Park Geun-hye">Park Geun-hye</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>The KUH-1 performed its first operational mission in August 2015, conducting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MEDEVAC" title="MEDEVAC">MEDEVAC</a> in the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> artillery shells that struck South Korean territory near the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Demilitarized_Zone" title="Korean Demilitarized Zone">Korean Demilitarized Zone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flightglobal23oct15_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-flightglobal23oct15-23">[23]</a></sup> Since entering service, the Surion has reportedly held an availability rate in excess of 80 percent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flightglobal23oct15_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-flightglobal23oct15-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Surion was grounded in July 2016 in response to the <a href="/wiki/CHC_Helikopter_Service_Flight_241" title="CHC Helikopter Service Flight 241">CHC Helikopter Service Flight 241</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In late 2016, deliveries of the Surion were halted while a resolution for an icing issue that was discovered during winter testing was worked upon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>

<p>The following variants were planned by KAI aside from the manufacture of the Surion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DN_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-DN-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>KUH-1 Surion</dt>
<dd>The basic military utility helicopter used by the Republic of Korea Army.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>KUH-Medevac</dt>
<dd>A medical evacuation variant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> It is equipped with a hoist for lifting patients, a weather radar, a detachable auxiliary fuel tank, a traffic collision avoidance system, medical equipment to treat six patients, and additional communications. First flight occurred on 25 January 2016 and will be delivered in 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-medevac_maiden_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-medevac_maiden-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>KUH-Amphibious</dt>
<dd>A planned land and sea-based amphibious variant for the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Marine_Corps" title="Republic of Korea Marine Corps">Republic of Korea Marine Corps</a>, it will have a 96 percent part commonality with the Surion. Modifications will include an integrated flotation system, an auxiliary fuel tank, and specialised radio equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The Marine Surion first flew on 19 January 2015. It will have a folding main rotor to serve aboard <a href="/wiki/Dokdo-class_amphibious_assault_ship" title="Dokdo-class amphibious assault ship">Dokdo-class amphibious assault ships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In December 2016, KAI secured a contract to deliver 30 Surions to the Marine Corps, to be delivered from 2017 to 2023.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flightglobal30dec16_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-flightglobal30dec16-5">[5]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>

<dt>KUH-ASW (Anti-Submarine Warfare)</dt>
<dd>A naval variant<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> equipped with torpedoes and <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship missiles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flightglobal23oct15_23-4"><a href="#cite_note-flightglobal23oct15-23">[23]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>

<dt>KUH-1P Chamsuri</dt>
<dd>Korean Police version (KUH-1P/ Chamsuri) - Surion utility helicopter chosen by the Korean National Police Agency (KNPA) for its Police Helicopter Project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Equipped with a weather radar, traffic collision avoidance system, <a href="/wiki/Inertial_navigation_system" title="Inertial navigation system">inertial navigation system</a>, and an external hoist and cameras for rescue duties (extra electronics are needed compared to ordinary police helicopters for operations near North Korea, which frequently conducts jamming operations).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airreco21oct15_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-airreco21oct15-43">[43]</a></sup></dd>
