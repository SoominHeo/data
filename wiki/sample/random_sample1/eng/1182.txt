<p><i><b>KARA Best 2007-2010</b></i> is the first Japanese <a href="/wiki/Greatest_hits_album" title="Greatest hits album">greatest hits album</a> by the <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/Kara_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kara (South Korean band)">Kara</a>. It was released on September 29, 2010 in Japan in 2 editions: CD+DVD and CD only.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tracklist_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tracklist-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kara_Best_2007%E2%80%932010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album contain all singles from 2007 to 2010 and some songs from their albums <i>Rock U</i>, <i>Pretty Girl</i>, <i>Revolution</i> and <i>Lupin</i>. On October 1, 2010, It was announced on their official Japanese website on that the album will include a bonus. The group gave photographs to the applicants to show their gratitude. Among those applicants, 10 were chosen via lottery to attend a meet &amp; greet session on October 15, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kara_Best_2007%E2%80%932010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at number two on the Oricon Daily Chart selling 18,223 copies. This marked the best achievement by a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> girl group for a full album in Japan at that time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On October 5, 2010, the album ranked number-two on the Oricon Weekly Chart selling over 51,000 copies, making it the first <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> girl group album to enter Top 10 on Oricon.</p>
<p>The album was certified Gold by <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_Japan" title="Recording Industry Association of Japan">RIAJ</a>, making it the first all-<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a> album from a South Korean group since the 90s to break the 100,000 copies barrier in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The album was also the most downloaded album in Japan's iTunes Store Pop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The album exceeded the ranking of <a href="/wiki/Iconiq" title="Iconiq">Iconiq</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Change_Myself" title="Change Myself">Change Myself</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/My_World_(EP)" title="My World (EP)">My World</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Kesha" title="Kesha">Kesha</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_(Kesha_album)" title="Animal (Kesha album)">Animal</a></i> to become the highest ranked album among newcomers in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
