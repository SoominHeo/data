<p><i><b>Kara Best Clips</b></i> is the first <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">Music video</a> compilation and fourth DVD by the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/Kara_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kara (South Korean band)">Kara</a>. It was released in February 23, 2011 in two editions: Regular<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and Limited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It ranked #1 in Oricon Weekly DVD Chart for two consecutive weeks with 132,132 copies sold in first week.</p>
<p>A Blu-ray edition of the DVD was released in February 29, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kara_Best_Clips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It debuted #1 in the Oricon daily chart and eventually became #1 in the Oricon weekly chart making the group the first foreign "female" artist to top the composite DVD ranking chart since its inception in 1999. The previous record was held by Chinese group <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Girls_Band" title="Twelve Girls Band">Twelve Girls Band</a>'s, "<i>Twelve Girls Band: First Premium Concert in Japan</i>" (January 26, 2004) and American artist <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_My_Prerogative" title="Greatest Hits: My Prerogative">Greatest Hits: My Prerogative</a></i> (December 20, 2004) have come in at #2 on Oricon's Weekly Composite DVD Ranking. Furthermore, Kara sold 132,000 copies of their music video collection "Kara Best Clips" in their first week and this is the greatest amount sold for a "music video collection" for a female artist; not including live performance DVDs. The previous top seller was <a href="/wiki/Mai_Kuraki" title="Mai Kuraki">Mai Kuraki</a>'s "<i>First Cut</i>" (November 20, 2000), which sold 99,000 copies. Kuraki has held the record for 10 years and 3 months, but Kara has just taken her record away. In total, there have been only 6 foreign artists who have made it to number one on the Weekly DVD Composite Ranking: Kara, <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>, <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">Tohoshinki (TVXQ)</a>, <a href="/wiki/JYJ" title="JYJ">Junsu/Jejung/Yuchun (JYJ)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-no1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-no1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p><i><b>Kara Best Clips II &amp; Shows</b></i> is a second part of the music video compilation "Kara Best Clips" and also first live DVD of the group. It was released in February 29, 2012 in 2 formats: <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> and 2 different editions: Limited (with 3 discs)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-limiteddvd_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-limiteddvd-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-limitedbd_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-limitedbd-8">[8]</a></sup> and Regular edition (with 2 discs).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-regularbd_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-regularbd-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-regulardvd_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-regulardvd-10">[10]</a></sup> Disc 2 includes the show "Now, the Word You Want to Give... KARA JAPAN COME BACK 2011", held in <a href="/wiki/Yokohama" title="Yokohama">Yokohama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It was the first showcase of the group in Japan.</p>
