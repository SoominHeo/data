<p><b>KBS1</b> is the premier channel of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">Korean Broadcasting System</a>, previously known as KBS Television/KBS Channel 9 until the launch of <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a> in 1980, is the oldest TV channel in South Korea and was the successor to HLKZ-TV (or Daehan Bangsong), Korea's first TV channel. It is also the only commercial-free free-to-air television channel in the country. KBS1 is similar to <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</p>
<p>The channel is well known for its <a href="/wiki/News" title="News">news</a> programmes, <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a>, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sports" title="Sports">sports</a>, <a href="/wiki/Entertainment" title="Entertainment">entertainment</a> and some children's programmes and a minimal amount of education programming.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBS1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>KBS TV began broadcasting on December 31, 1961, as South Korea's first full-scale television station. Regular broadcasts commenced on January 15 the following year.</p>
<p>KBS1 was once known for being one of the first commercial TV stations in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. But in 1963, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_South_Korea" title="Government of South Korea">South Korean government</a> through its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_South_Korea" title="National Assembly of South Korea">parliament</a>, introduced the Television License Fee system. Advertisements on KBS1 were abolished in 1994.</p>
<p>The monopoly was broken in 1965 when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tongyang_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Tongyang Broadcasting Corporation">TBC</a> began broadcasting. In 1980, following KBS's acquisition of various private broadcasters, it became known as KBS1.</p>
<p>KBS 1TV began 24-hour broadcasting on October 8, 2012, the first channel in South Korea to do so, in line with the digital switchover and following the lifting the ban of broadcasting into the early hours of the morning on terrestrial television. Even though KBS1 is 24 hours, they continue closing down on Sunday and Monday nights, due to technical maintenance.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming">Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBS1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>KBS 1TV is the home of predominantly the news, current affairs, talkshow and documentary programmes being produced by KBS, as well as primetime daily dramas and weekend historical and literature series. It also airs musical shows. All <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS 2TV</a> dramas are also shown on this channel as reruns, notably the Monday/Tuesday dramas &amp; Wednesday/Thursday dramas.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>KBS 1TV has musical variety shows, but unlike its sister channel, <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS 2TV</a> and most commercial stations which air it on a live basis, most KBS 1TV musical shows are either recorded or broadcast live on special occasions as specials. The only live music program shown on this channel is <b>Korea Sings</b>, a talent contest currently shown since 1980.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding">Funding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBS1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Funding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 1994, KBS1 is solely funded by license fees.</p>
<p>Although commercial advertisements are not allowed on the channel, KBS1 accepts spot advertisements similar to the ones being used by <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> to generate private funding. This is usually implemented for programs such as public affairs and musical variety shows wherein, the show's host/s will only thank the sponsor's brand name but will not promote it directly.</p>
<p>Spot advertisements for KBS1 shows appear before the show's end credits.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising">Advertising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBS1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Advertising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>KBS1 had stopped accepting ads since 1994, and is prohibited to air commercial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Advertisements" title="Advertisements">advertisements</a> due to the revised Broadcasting Act, but is allowed to show public service ads of government agencies, most of which are from <a href="/wiki/KOBACO_(Korea_Broadcast_Advertising_Corporation)" title="KOBACO (Korea Broadcast Advertising Corporation)">KOBACO</a>, which lost its monopoly on TV advertising in May 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Besides showing public ads, they also show program previews for this channel and/or from their sister channels, public messages from the Government and the safety messages produced either by KBS or the Government agencies.</p>
<p>These ads or messages are often shown before the station ident is shown, or if the ident isn't shown, before the program starts, the national anthem is played when they restart their transmission at 4:50am (KST).</p>
