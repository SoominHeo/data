<p><b>KFC Uerdingen 05</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Germany" title="Football in Germany">German football club</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Uerdingen" title="Uerdingen">Uerdingen</a> district of the city of <a href="/wiki/Krefeld" title="Krefeld">Krefeld</a>, North Rhine-Westphalia. The one time Bundesliga side enjoyed its greatest successes in the 1980s but now currently plays in the 5th division, <a href="/wiki/Oberliga_Niederrhein" title="Oberliga Niederrhein">Oberliga Niederrhein</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KFC_Uerdingen_05&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded on 17 November 1905 as <i>Fußball-Club Uerdingen 05</i>. On 1 August 1919, following World War I, <i>FC</i> was joined by <i>Sportvereinigung des Realgymnasiums Urdingen</i>. During World War II from 1941–45 the club played as part of the combined wartime side <i>Kriegspielgemeinschaft Uerdingen</i> alongside <i>VfB 1910 Uerdingen</i> (which was known from 1910–19 as <i>Sport-Club Preussen Uerdingen</i>). That partnership continued after the war with the two clubs playing as <i>Spielvereinigung Uerdingen 05</i>. On 20 February 1948, <i>VfB</i> became independent again and in 1950 <i>SpVgg</i> resumed their original identity as <i>FC Uerdingen 05</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1953, the club merged with <i>Werkssportgruppe Bayer AG Uerdingen</i>, the local worker's sports club of the chemical giant <a href="/wiki/Bayer" title="Bayer">Bayer AG</a>, becoming <i>FC Bayer 05 Uerdingen</i>. Bayer withdrew its sponsorship of the football team in 1995 at which time the club took on the name <i>Krefelder Fußball-Club Uerdingen 05</i>. Bayer continues to support the non-footballing departments of the club as <i>Sport-Club Bayer 05 Uerdingen</i>.</p>

<p><i>Uerdingen</i> played in the amateur local leagues throughout their early history. By the early 1960s they had advanced as far as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amateurliga_Niederrhein" title="Amateurliga Niederrhein">Amateurliga Niederrhein</a> (III) where they would play until 1971 when they stepped up into the <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_West" title="Regionalliga West">Regionalliga West</a> (II). The club then enjoyed a succession of strong finishes: a second-place result in 1974–75 earned them promotion to the top flight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="Fußball-Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>, where they finished dead last. After three seasons in the second tier <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2._Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga_Nord_(1974%E2%80%931981)" title="2. Fußball-Bundesliga Nord (1974–1981)">2. Bundesliga Nord</a>, another second-place finish returned <i>Uerdingen</i> to the Bundesliga in 1979, this time for a two-year stay. The club would go on to enjoy its most successful years through the 1980s. They returned to the Bundesliga in 1983 and earned a best-ever third-place result there in 1986. <i>Uerdingen</i> also captured the <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal" title="DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a> (German Cup) in 1985 with a 2:1 victory over Bundesliga champions <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich" title="Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin's</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympiastadion" title="Olympiastadion">Olympiastadion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Legendary in the club's history from this time is their victory over <i><a href="/wiki/Dynamo_Dresden" title="Dynamo Dresden">Dynamo Dresden</a></i> in the quarter-finals of the 1986 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Cup_Winners_Cup" title="European Cup Winners Cup">European Cup Winners Cup</a>. Down 0–2 after the first leg away and behind 1–3 by half-time at home in the return leg, <i>Uerdingen</i> came storming back with six unanswered goals to win 7–3.</p>
<p>In 1987, Uerdingen also became the first club to win both the German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Under_19_Bundesliga_(football)" title="Under 19 Bundesliga (football)">under 19's</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Under_17_Bundesliga_(football)" title="Under 17 Bundesliga (football)">under 17's</a> championship in the same season.</p>
<p>The team spent the first half of the 1990s as an "elevator crew" bouncing up and down between the Bundesliga and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2._Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="2. Fußball-Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a>. After the 1995 season Bayer withdrew its sponsorship of the football team which has suffered chronic financial difficulties ever since. Uerdingen took up their final year of play in the Bundesliga in the 1995–96 season as <i>Krefelder Fußball-Club Uerdingen 05</i>. By the turn of the millennium they had slipped through the second division and into third division play. The club's persistent financial problems led the <a href="/wiki/German_Football_Association" title="German Football Association">DFB</a> to deny them a license for play in the Regionalliga Nord (III) in 2003 despite a mid-table finish and they were relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Oberliga_Nordrhein" title="Oberliga Nordrhein">Oberliga Nordrhein</a> (IV).</p>
<p>Veteran manager and <a href="/wiki/Fortuna_D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Fortuna Düsseldorf">Fortuna Düsseldorf</a> legend <a href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Risti%C4%87" title="Aleksandar Ristić">Aleksandar Ristić</a> was put in charge of the team as German football was reorganised with the introduction the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3._Fu%C3%9Fball-Liga" title="3. Fußball-Liga">3. Liga</a> in 2008–09. KFC attempted to qualify for the restructured Regionalliga (IV), but failed in its attempt and was instead relegated to the Verbandsliga (VI) after finishing 13th.</p>
<p>In 2010–11, the club won the Verbandsliga and thus gained promotion to the <a href="/wiki/NRW-Liga" title="NRW-Liga">NRW-Liga</a> (V). It was the first promotion in 17 years. KFC finished 8th NRW-Liga and missed second consecutive promotion to <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_West" title="Regionalliga West">Regionalliga West</a> due to finishing behind <a href="/wiki/VfB_H%C3%BCls" title="VfB Hüls">VfB Hüls</a>. After the disbanding of the NRW-Liga, KFC qualified for the <a href="/wiki/Oberliga_Niederrhein" title="Oberliga Niederrhein">Oberliga Niederrhein</a>. It won a league championship at this level in 2013 and was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_West" title="Regionalliga West">Regionalliga West</a> but relegated back to the Oberliga again in 2015.</p>
<p>KFC has struggled with financial difficulties in recent years, and its efforts to raise money included auctioning on eBay the right to coach the squad for one match and inviting childhood fan <a href="/wiki/Pete_Doherty" title="Pete Doherty">Pete Doherty</a> to a league match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
