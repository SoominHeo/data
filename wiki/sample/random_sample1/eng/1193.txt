Following is a list of destinations the airline and its subsidiaries <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KLM_Cargo" title="KLM Cargo">KLM Cargo</a> and <a href="/wiki/KLM_Cityhopper" title="KLM Cityhopper">KLM Cityhopper</a> fly to according to their scheduled services, as of April 2013<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=KLM_destinations&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-klm_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-klm-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KLM_Boeing_777-300ER_PH-BVF_SIN_2012-7-29.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="738" data-file-width="1024" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/KLM_Boeing_777-300ER_PH-BVF_SIN_2012-7-29.png/220px-KLM_Boeing_777-300ER_PH-BVF_SIN_2012-7-29.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/KLM_Boeing_777-300ER_PH-BVF_SIN_2012-7-29.png/330px-KLM_Boeing_777-300ER_PH-BVF_SIN_2012-7-29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/KLM_Boeing_777-300ER_PH-BVF_SIN_2012-7-29.png/440px-KLM_Boeing_777-300ER_PH-BVF_SIN_2012-7-29.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

A KLM <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boeing_777-300ER" title="Boeing 777-300ER">Boeing 777-300ER</a> on approach to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Changi_Airport" title="Changi Airport">Changi Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a></div>

<p><a href="/wiki/KLM" title="KLM">KLM</a> was set up by <a href="/wiki/Albert_Plesman" title="Albert Plesman">Albert Plesman</a> on 7 October 1919 and started operations on 19 May 1920.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI2001-92_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FI2001-92-2">[2]</a></sup> The first route served was the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>–<a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> one, flown with <a href="/wiki/Airco_DH.9" title="Airco DH.9">DH.9As</a> that carried just two passengers on a charter basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flying_Dutchmen_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flying_Dutchmen-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:13</sup> Two <a href="/wiki/Fokker_F.II" title="Fokker F.II">Fokker F.IIs</a> that were delivered in September 1920<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1920-09</span>)</span> were later deployed on this very first route.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forty_years_of_Fokker_airliners_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forty_years_of_Fokker_airliners-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:1014</sup> Intercontinental flights were started with <a href="/wiki/Fokker_F.VII" title="Fokker F.VII">Fokker F.VII</a> equipment in November 1924<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1924-11</span>)</span>, serving the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Towards_Australia_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Towards_Australia-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:36</sup> although it was not until 1931 that regular services on this line were implemented.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight1932-196_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flight1932-196-6">[6]</a></sup> It normally took 11 or 12 days to complete the 9,000-mile (14,000 km) long flight between Amsterdam and <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Batavia</a>, calling at <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bushehr" title="Bushehr">Bushire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jask" title="Jask">Jask</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jodhpur" title="Jodhpur">Jodhpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allahabad" title="Allahabad">Allahabad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Calcutta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sittwe" title="Sittwe">Akyab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yangon" title="Yangon">Rangoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medan" title="Medan">Medan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palembang" title="Palembang">Palembang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight1932-196_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Flight1932-196-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1926, the <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>–Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> run was extended to <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malmö">Malmö</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight1927-415_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flight1927-415-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:416</sup></p>
<p>Singapore was first served in May 1933<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1933-05</span>)</span>, when it was taken over from <a href="/wiki/KNILM" title="KNILM">KNILM</a> and added as an intermediate stop for the Amsterdam–Batavia line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight1934-403_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flight1934-403-8">[8]</a></sup> By April 1934<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1934-04</span>)</span>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> were already part of the European route network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight1934-354_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flight1934-354-9">[9]</a></sup> In May that year, KLM became the first airline that linked <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">Continental Europe</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_of_England" title="North of England">North of England</a>, when the Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a> route was inaugurated;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight1934-563_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flight1934-563-10">[10]</a></sup> the Amsterdam–Liverpool service was re-routed via <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a> in mid-1936.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> By April 1939<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1939-04</span>)</span>, the following twenty-one lines were operative: Amsterdam–Berlin, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">Bremen</a>–Hamburg, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Kristiansand" title="Kristiansand">Christiansand</a>–<a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>, Amsterdam–Copenhagen–Malmo, Amsterdam–Copenhagen–<a href="/wiki/Norrk%C3%B6ping" title="Norrköping">Norrkoping</a>–<a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, Amsterdam–Copenhagen–Stockholm, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a>, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Groningen" title="Groningen">Groningen</a>–<a href="/wiki/Leeuwarden" title="Leeuwarden">Leeuwarden</a>–Amsterdam, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Leipzig" title="Leipzig">Leipzig</a>–Budapest–Athens–<a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a>–<a href="/wiki/Lod_Air_Force_Base" title="Lod Air Force Base">Lydda</a>–Baghdad–<a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a>–Jask–Karachi–Jodhpur–Allahabad–Calcutta–Rangoon–Bangkok–Penang–<a href="/wiki/Medan" title="Medan">Medan</a>–Singapore–<a href="/wiki/Palembang" title="Palembang">Palembang</a>–Batavia–<a href="/wiki/Bandung" title="Bandung">Bandoeng</a> that connected with KNILM services, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>–Liverpool, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Twente" title="Twente">Twente</a>, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Texel" title="Texel">Texel</a>, Amsterdam–London, Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, Amsterdam–Rotterdam–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basle" title="Basle">Basle</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zurich" title="Zurich">Zurich</a>, Amsterdam–Rotterdam–<a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, Amsterdam–Rotterdam–<a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfort</a>–<a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>–<a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, Amsterdam–Rotterdam–<a href="/wiki/Haamstede" title="Haamstede">Haamstede</a>–<a href="/wiki/Vlissingen" title="Vlissingen">Flushing</a>–<a href="/wiki/Knokke" title="Knokke">Knocke</a>/<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zoute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zoute (page does not exist)">Zoute</a>, Amsterdam–Rotterdam–London, Amsterdam–Rotterdam–<a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>–<a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>–Budapest, and Paris–Copenhagen–Stockholm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>With an intermediate stop at <a href="/wiki/Espinho,_Portugal" title="Espinho, Portugal">Espinho</a>, the Amsterdam–<a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> route was opened in April 1940<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1940-04</span>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Initially, these flights carried freight and mail, connected at Lisbon with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan_American_Airways" title="Pan American Airways">Pan American Airways</a> Clipper services to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_city" title="New York city">New York</a> and had a subsidy from the <a href="/wiki/Koninklijke_TNT_Post" title="Koninklijke TNT Post">Dutch Post Office</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight1940-340_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flight1940-340-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
