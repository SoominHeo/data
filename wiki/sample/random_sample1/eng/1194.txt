<p><b>Kuala Lumpur Sentral (KL Sentral)</b> or <b>Sentral Kuala Lumpur</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Transit-oriented_development" title="Transit-oriented development">transit-oriented development</a> that houses the main <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. Opened on 16 April 2001, KL Sentral replaced the old <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_railway_station" title="Kuala Lumpur railway station">Kuala Lumpur railway station</a> as the city's main <a href="/wiki/Inter-city_rail" title="Inter-city rail">intercity</a> railway station. KL Sentral is the largest railway station in Malaysia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>KL Sentral is designed as an <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/intermodal" title="wiktionary:intermodal">intermodal</a> transport hub. All of Kuala Lumpur's passenger rail lines except the <a href="/wiki/Ampang_Line" title="Ampang Line">Ampang Line</a> serve KL Sentral, while many intercity trains serving <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Peninsular Malaysia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> start there. All the railway components of the scheme have been completed, but some other parts such as shopping malls, offices and apartments are still under construction and are expected to be complete by 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sentral_dev_timeline_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sentral_dev_timeline-2">[2]</a></sup> It was also designed to be a new business and financial hub for Kuala Lumpur.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>KL Sentral refers to the entire 290,000 square metres of development built on the former <a href="/wiki/Keretapi_Tanah_Melayu" title="Keretapi Tanah Melayu">Keretapi Tanah Melayu</a> marshalling yard in <a href="/wiki/Brickfields" title="Brickfields">Brickfields</a>. The development includes the transport hub, hotels, office towers, condominiums and shopping malls and is expected to be completed in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sentral_dev_timeline_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-sentral_dev_timeline-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>KL Sentral has been divided into 14 land parcels, each representing a different function. Some of these lots have been fully constructed and are already in use, while others are either in the process of being built, or are still awaiting development, according to the phased programme.</p>
<p>KL Sentral is being developed by a consortium made up of Malaysian Resources Corporation Berhad (<a href="/wiki/MRCB" title="MRCB">MRCB</a>), Keretapi Tanah Melayu Berhad (KTMB) and Pembinaan Redzai Sdn Bhd.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1994, with the objective to enhance <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a> city’s public transportation, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Malaysia" title="Government of Malaysia">Government of Malaysia</a> awarded a contract to a consortium to transform 72 acres (290,000 m<sup>2</sup>) at Kuala Lumpur’s old railway marshalling yard into a modern transit hub within a self-contained urban development.</p>
<p>The consortium, led by Malaysian Resources Corp Bhd (MRCB) appointed architect <a href="/wiki/Kisho_Kurokawa" title="Kisho Kurokawa">Kisho Kurokawa</a> and Associates, who also designed the <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_International_Airport" title="Kuala Lumpur International Airport">Kuala Lumpur International Airport</a> (KLIA), to design the master plan for the entire development.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_A:_CIMB_Headquarters_.28Menara_CIMB.29">Lot A: CIMB Headquarters (Menara CIMB)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lot A: CIMB Headquarters (Menara CIMB)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Lot A</b> will be the new headquarters for <a href="/wiki/CIMB" title="CIMB">CIMB</a> Group which is located facing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_Malaysia" title="National Museum of Malaysia">National Museum</a>. As of June 2011, the new headquarters' name which appears on the construction site is <i>Menara CIMB</i> (CIMB Tower). The company acquired the land in July 2008, and construction is expected to begin in Q4 2007 and complete in 2011. It is unknown how many floors there will be but it should have around 600,000 sq ft (56,000 m<sup>2</sup>) of office space available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_M:_Plaza_Sentral">Lot M: Plaza Sentral</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Lot M: Plaza Sentral">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Lot M</b> or <b>Plaza Sentral</b> is a completed office complex in the west of KL Sentral. The complex was completed in two stages.</p>
<ul>
<li>Phase I was completed in June 2001, comprising three blocks and total gross floor area of 785,000 sq ft (72,900 m<sup>2</sup>). Offering prestigious office suites, Plaza Sentral was sold and quickly taken up by multinationals and Malaysian companies alike, eager to take advantage of the global connectivity and convenience of working in KL Sentral. Its take-up rate is reaching 100%.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Phase II was launched in September 2003 due to the escalating demand for premium office space. It has since been fully taken up. Comprising two towers - 18-storey and 26-storey - made up of four blocks and a total gross floor area of 879,381 sq ft (81,697.2 m<sup>2</sup>), the building was completed in 2006. Tenants include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Telecom" title="British Telecom">British Telecom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maxis_Communications" title="Maxis Communications">Maxis Communications</a>.</li>
<li>Sentral Tower, a purpose-built 30-storey office tower, was sold to <a href="/wiki/Tabung_Haji" title="Tabung Haji">Lembaga Tabung Haji</a> for RM161.46mil in March 2004.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_J:_Sooka_Sentral_.26_4_Office_Towers">Lot J: Sooka Sentral &amp; 4 Office Towers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Lot J: Sooka Sentral &amp; 4 Office Towers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Lot J</b> is a development lot for four 35-storey office towers of identical architecture.</p>
<p>Lot J was sold to United Engineers (M) Bhd (UEM) in May 2005 with plans for four towers. Tower A was built by UEM for its corporate headquarters called <i><a href="/wiki/UEM_Group#Mercu_UEM" title="UEM Group">Mercu UEM</a></i> on the prime piece of land with a gross floor area of 1,300,000 sq ft (120,000 m<sup>2</sup>). Tower B will be an office tower for the Malaysian Industrial Development Authority. Tower C was built by Quill Realty Sdn Bhd and sold to Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM) for its corporate headquarters. Tower D is an office building built by Quill, named Quill 7. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A development on Lot J is the 180,000 sq ft (17,000 m<sup>2</sup>) six-storey lifestyle centre which houses a fitness and spa centre, business centre, food court, restaurants and alfresco dining facilities. This development, called Sooka Sentral, opened its doors in December 2007.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_N:_1_Sentral_Tower">Lot N: 1 Sentral Tower</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Lot N: 1 Sentral Tower">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>1 Sentral Tower is an office tower, offering a gross floor area of 453,000 sq ft (42,100 m<sup>2</sup>). Construction was completed in 2007. It houses companies such as PricewaterhouseCoopers, MRCB and General Electric.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_H:_Stesen_Sentral">Lot H: Stesen Sentral</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Lot H: Stesen Sentral">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Klsentralst.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1244" data-file-width="1266" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Klsentralst.gif/150px-Klsentralst.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Klsentralst.gif/225px-Klsentralst.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Klsentralst.gif/300px-Klsentralst.gif 2x" width="150"/></a>

<p><b>Stesen Sentral</b> (English: <b>Central Station</b>) is the name of Lot H, which is designated the transport hub within its KL Sentral development project, although both the public and connected transit lines generally refer to the station itself as "KL Sentral".</p>
<p>Stesen Sentral, designed to house six rail networks, was completed in December 2000 and the rail, retail and food and beverage operations began in April 2001. Standing on 9.3 acres (38,000 m<sup>2</sup>), the main building has a gross floor area of half a million sq ft and the specifications were based on passenger projections up to the year 2020, which is when Malaysia aspires to be a fully developed nation.</p>
<p>In 2008, Stesen Sentral registered over 100,000 passengers daily, which translates into 36.5 million people a year.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/KL_Monorail" title="KL Monorail">KL Monorail</a>'s KL Sentral station is across the busy Jalan Tun Sambanthan, and it is connected with the overhead bridge that integrates the station with the new retails shopping complex, Nu Sentral, and other lines in KL Sentral main hub.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Klang_Valley_Mass_Rail_Transit" title="Klang Valley Mass Rail Transit">Klang Valley Mass Rapid Transit (MRT)</a>'s station will be built nearly across Jalan Travers in proximity to the <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_(Malaysia)" title="National Museum (Malaysia)">National Museum</a>. The station's working name was KL Sentral, although now, the official name is designated as Muzium Negara. A walkway will be constructed to provide connectivity with KL Sentral.</p>
<p>Stesen Sentral is divided into separate sections for different rail services:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Level One of Transit Concourse</b> for urban public transit services: KTM Komuter, KLIA Transit and the <a href="/wiki/Kelana_Jaya_Line" title="Kelana Jaya Line">Kelana Jaya Line</a> light rail line.</li>
<li><b>Level Two of Transit Concourse</b> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rantau_Panjang_Line" title="Rantau Panjang Line">KTM intercity trains</a>.</li>
<li><b>KL City Air Terminal (KL CAT)</b> on Level One for the <a href="/wiki/KLIA_Ekspres" title="KLIA Ekspres">KLIA Ekspres</a>, the dedicated fast rail service to <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_International_Airport" title="Kuala Lumpur International Airport">Kuala Lumpur International Airport</a> (KLIA) (see section <a href="#KL_City_Air_Terminal">below</a>).</li>
</ul>
<p>Stesen Sentral also has retail and food outlets and a food court. Hidden from public view is KTM's maintenance depot beneath the complex. There is a facility for purchasing and reloading the <a href="/wiki/Touch_%27n_Go" title="Touch 'n Go">Touch 'n Go</a> card in this station.</p>
<p>Stesen Sentral will be able to accommodate 50 million passengers a year and up to 100 million by 2020 (KL Sentral website).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="KL_City_Air_Terminal">KL City Air Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: KL City Air Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Located within Stesen Sentral, the <b>KL City Air Terminal (KL CAT)</b> is virtually an extension of the Kuala Lumpur International Airport. It is recognised by the <a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association" title="International Air Transport Association">International Air Transport Association</a> (IATA) as a city destination, with the code <b><a href="/wiki/List_of_IATA-indexed_railway_stations" title="List of IATA-indexed railway stations">XKL</a></b>. It was officially opened together with the KLIA Ekspres rail line on 14 April 2002.</p>

<p>Besides being the departure and arrival point for the <a href="/wiki/KLIA_Ekspres" title="KLIA Ekspres">KLIA Ekspres</a>, KL CAT also has luggage check-in services. Passengers flying <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines" title="Malaysia Airlines">Malaysia Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cathay_Pacific" title="Cathay Pacific">Cathay Pacific</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emirates_Airline" title="Emirates Airline">Emirates Airline</a>, <a href="/wiki/Etihad_Airways" title="Etihad Airways">Etihad Airways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Brunei_Airlines" title="Royal Brunei Airlines">Royal Brunei Airlines</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> may check in luggage at KL CAT. The minimum check-in time is two hours before flight departure time.</p>
<p>There were plans to offer check-out services but these have not materialised.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hotels_and_residential_zones">Hotels and residential zones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Hotels and residential zones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_C:_St_Regis_Hotel">Lot C: St Regis Hotel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Lot C: St Regis Hotel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Lot C</b> houses the <a href="/wiki/Starwood" title="Starwood">The St. Regis Hotel Kuala Lumpur</a>. The luxurious hotel consists of 208 rooms and suites started its operation in May 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stregisKL_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-stregisKL-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_D:_57-storey_Residential_Tower">Lot D: 57-storey Residential Tower</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Lot D: 57-storey Residential Tower">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Lot D</b> will consist a single 60-storey residential tower overlooking the Lake Gardens and the city. It will be jointly developed by MRCB, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capitaland" title="Capitaland">Capitaland</a> and Quill Group. It is currently pending approval from the city hall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_I_:_Hilton_KL_.26_Le_M.C3.A9ridien_Hotels">Lot I : Hilton KL &amp; Le Méridien Hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Lot I : Hilton KL &amp; Le Méridien Hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Lot I</b> consists of two skyscraper hotels occupied by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hilton_Hotels" title="Hilton Hotels">Hilton Kuala Lumpur</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starwood_Hotels_%26_Resorts_Worldwide#Le_M.C3.A9ridien" title="Starwood Hotels &amp; Resorts Worldwide">Le Méridien</a>, owned by Daito Asia Development Sdn. Bhd. and Daisho Asia Development Sdn. Bhd. respectively. The hotels opened for business in September and October 2004 respectively.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lot_K_and_L:_Suasana_Sentral_and_Suasana_Sentral_Loft">Lot K and L: Suasana Sentral and Suasana Sentral Loft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Lot K and L: Suasana Sentral and Suasana Sentral Loft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Lot K</b> is the location of a serviced apartment, Suasana Sentral. Suasana Sentral consists of two blocks, or 400 units of apartments. It was completed in 2002 and is fully sold and occupied, with about 40% of the buyers coming from the international community.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The second phase of condominiums on <b>Lot L</b>, Suasana Sentral Loft was launched in early 2005 and began occupancy in early 2008. The 37 and 38-storey apartment towers have a total of 600 units. All have been sold.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Others_2">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2006, a joint venture company acquired <b>Lot D</b> and will develop residential and serviced apartments with supporting retail amenities. This luxury development will face the National Museum, Lake Gardens as well as KL City Centre. Construction is expected to commence by early 2008 and to be completed in 2010.</p>
<p>In June 2007, a joint venture company purchased Lot C for the development of a 6-star hotel, 6-star residences and prime offices. Work on the 1,400,000 sq ft (130,000 m<sup>2</sup>) development is expected to start in 2008.</p>

<p>The KL Sentral Monorail station stands on Jalan Tun Sambanthan in Brickfields. The station is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminating stop</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_Monorail" title="Kuala Lumpur Monorail">KL Monorail</a> line. Previously, the station is not directly connected to the Kuala Lumpur Sentral transport hub. Despite its proximity to the building, the monorail users needed to exit the station and crossed the busy main thoroughfare in <a href="/wiki/Brickfields" title="Brickfields">Brickfields</a> to reach the main hub of KL Sentral. With the upgrade of the Monorail station, the station is now integrated with the new shopping centre, Nu Sentral (Lot G, above), in which the Nu Sentral overhead bridge becomes the new entrance to the station.</p>
<p>The monorail station is also equipped with new ticket vending machines, ticketing gates as well as a new Customer Service Office. <a href="/wiki/Tactile_paving" title="Tactile paving">Tactile paving</a> for the visually impaired is also provided beginning from Nu Sentral. The completion of the overhead bridge now enables customers to travel between KL Sentral and the monorail station safely and conveniently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The station uses the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_solution" title="Spanish solution">Spanish solution</a>, having one rail line with a platform on each side, for passengers embarking and disembarking the train separately.</p>
<p>These connecting stations between <a href="/wiki/KL_Monorail" title="KL Monorail">KL Monorail</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kelana_Jaya_Line" title="Kelana Jaya Line">Kelana Jaya Line</a> are not the interchange station or out of station interchange station. Commuter will be charged 2 single way journey fare instead of integrated fare whenever using these connecting stations.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="KL_Sentral_as_a_bus_hub">KL Sentral as a bus hub</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuala_Lumpur_Sentral_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: KL Sentral as a bus hub">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>KL Sentral has also been made a city bus hub by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RapidKL" title="RapidKL">rapidKL</a> as part of its bus network revamp. It is the bus hub for city shuttle (BANDAR) bus routes and trunk (UTAMA) bus routes.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Buses to Singapore, <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_International_Airport" title="Kuala Lumpur International Airport">Kuala Lumpur International Airport</a> and to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Klia2" title="Klia2">klia2</a> are also operate from KL Sentral, while most other bus operators go near KL Sentral along Jalan Tun Sambanthan. Also a hop-on/hop-off tourist bus stop is located between Hilton and the station. Travel by bus to Genting Highlands also available here.</p>
