<p>The <b>United States Senate elections of 1808 and 1809</b> were elections that had the <a href="/wiki/Federalist_Party" title="Federalist Party">Federalist Party</a> gain one seat in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>, and which coincided with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1808" title="United States presidential election, 1808">1808 presidential election</a>. The Federalists had gone into the elections with such a small share of Senate seats (6 out of 34, or 18%) that even if they had won every election, they would have still remained a minority caucus.</p>
<p>As these elections were prior to ratification of the <a href="/wiki/Seventeenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Seventeenth Amendment to the United States Constitution">seventeenth amendment</a>, Senators were chosen by <a href="/wiki/State_legislature_(United_States)" title="State legislature (United States)">State legislatures</a>.</p>
