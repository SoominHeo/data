<p><b>L</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_alphabet#Letter_names" title="English alphabet">named</a> <i>el</i><span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/Àà/ primary stress follows">Àà</span><span title="/…õ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">…õ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span></span>/</a></span></span>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is the twelfth <a href="/wiki/Letter_(alphabet)" title="Letter (alphabet)">letter</a> of the <a href="/wiki/English_alphabet" title="English alphabet">modern English alphabet</a> and the <a href="/wiki/ISO_basic_Latin_alphabet" title="ISO basic Latin alphabet">ISO basic Latin alphabet</a>, used in words such as <i>lagoon</i>, <i>lantern</i>, and <i>less</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_in_writing_systems">Use in writing systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Use in writing systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English">English</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: English">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/English_orthography" title="English orthography">English orthography</a>, ‚ü®l‚ü© usually represents the phoneme <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span></span>/</a></span></span>, which can have several sound values, depending on whether it occurs before or after a vowel. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alveolar_lateral_approximant" title="Alveolar lateral approximant">alveolar lateral approximant</a> (the sound represented in <a href="/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet" title="International Phonetic Alphabet">IPA</a> by lowercase <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[l]</span>) occurs before a vowel, as in <i>lip</i> or <i>blend</i>, while the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Velarized_alveolar_lateral_approximant" title="Velarized alveolar lateral approximant">velarized alveolar lateral approximant</a> (IPA <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[…´]</span>) occurs in <i>bell</i> and <i>milk</i>. This velarization does not occur in many European languages that use ‚ü®l‚ü©; it is also a factor making the pronunciation of ‚ü®l‚ü© difficult for users of languages that lack ‚ü®l‚ü© or have different values for it, such as <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> or some southern dialects of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>. A medical condition or speech impediment restricting the pronunciation of ‚ü®l‚ü© is known as <a href="/wiki/Lambdacism" title="Lambdacism">lambdacism</a>.</p>
<p>In English orthography, ‚ü®l‚ü© is often silent in such words as <i>walk</i> or <i>could</i> (though its presence can modify the preceding vowel letter's sound), and it is usually silent in such words as <i>palm</i> and <i>psalm</i>; however, there is some regional variation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_languages">Other languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>‚ü®l‚ü© usually represents the sound <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[l]</span> or some other <a href="/wiki/Lateral_consonant" title="Lateral consonant">lateral consonant</a>.</p>
<p>Common digraphs include ‚ü®ll‚ü©, which has a value identical to ‚ü®l‚ü© in English, but has the separate value <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voiceless_alveolar_lateral_fricative" title="Voiceless alveolar lateral fricative">voiceless alveolar lateral fricative</a> (IPA <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[…¨]</span>) in <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>, where it can appear in an initial position. In Spanish, ‚ü®ll‚ü© represents [ é], [j], [ ù], […ü ù], or [ É], depending on dialect.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Palatal_lateral_approximant" title="Palatal lateral approximant">palatal lateral approximant</a> or palatal ‚ü®l‚ü© (IPA <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ é]</span>) occurs in many languages, and is represented by ‚ü®gli‚ü© in <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, ‚ü®ll‚ü© in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>, ‚ü®lh‚ü© in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, and ‚ü®ƒº‚ü© in <a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_systems">Other systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In phonetic and phonemic transcription, the <a href="/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet" title="International Phonetic Alphabet">International Phonetic Alphabet</a> uses ‚ü®<span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">l</span>‚ü© to represent the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lateral_alveolar_approximant" title="Lateral alveolar approximant">lateral alveolar approximant</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_uses">Other uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The capital letter L is used as the currency sign for the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_lek" title="Albanian lek">Albanian lek</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Honduran_lempira" title="Honduran lempira">Honduran lempira</a>. It was often used, especially in handwriting, as the currency sign for the <a href="/wiki/Italian_lira" title="Italian lira">Italian lira</a>. It is also infrequently used as a substitute for the <a href="/wiki/Pound_sign" title="Pound sign">pound sign</a> (¬£), which is based on it.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_numeral" title="Roman numeral">Roman numeral</a> ‚Ö¨ represents the number <a href="/wiki/50_(number)" title="50 (number)">50</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gordon_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gordon-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Forms_and_variants">Forms and variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Forms and variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">"‚Ñì" redirects here. For the azimuthal quantum number, see <a href="/wiki/Azimuthal_quantum_number" title="Azimuthal quantum number">azimuthal quantum number</a>.</div>
<p>In some fonts, the lowercase letter ‚ü®l‚ü© may be difficult to distinguish from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1_(number)" title="1 (number)">digit one</a>, ‚ü®1‚ü©, or an uppercase letter ‚ü®<a href="/wiki/I" title="I">I</a>‚ü©. In recent times, many new fonts have curved the lowercase form to the right, and it is increasingly common, especially on European road signs and advertisements. A more modern version based on the handwritten <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letterlike_Symbols_(Unicode_block)" title="Letterlike Symbols (Unicode block)">letter-like</a> ‚ü®‚Ñì‚ü© is sometimes used in mathematics and elsewhere. In Japan, for example, this is the symbol for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liter" title="Liter">liter</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/LaTeX" title="LaTeX">LaTeX</a> command is <tt>\ell</tt>, its codepoint is U+2113, and its numeric character reference is "<tt>&amp;#8467;</tt>".</p>
