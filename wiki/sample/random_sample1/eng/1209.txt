<p><b>L'Arc-en-Ciel</b> ("The Rainbow" in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-1">[1]</a></sup> stylized as <b>L'Arc~en~Ciel</b>) is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band, founded in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> in 1991. With a reported 40 million records sold, they were the first Japanese act to headline at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2003, they were ranked 58 on a list of the top 100 Japanese pop musicians by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HMV_Group" title="HMV Group">HMV Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hmv_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-hmv-3">[3]</a></sup> While they first got their start as a <a href="/wiki/Visual_kei" title="Visual kei">visual kei</a> band, they have not had any association with the movement since their major label debut in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991.E2.80.931996:_Early_career">1991–1996: Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1991–1996: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 1991, bassist <a href="/wiki/Tetsuya_(musician)" title="Tetsuya (musician)">tetsuya</a> (then known as TETSU) recruited vocalist <a href="/wiki/Hyde_(musician)" title="Hyde (musician)">hyde</a> (then known as HIDE),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HIDE_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-HIDE-6">[6]</a></sup> guitarist hiro and drummer pero. Together, they formed a band and named themselves L'Arc-en-Ciel. A year later, after gaining popularity in their hometown of <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, hiro left the band on June 12, 1992. Tetsuya then convinced his friend <a href="/wiki/Ken_(musician)" title="Ken (musician)">ken</a> to quit his university studies and join the band as guitarist. However, pero then left the group on December 30, 1992. The following year, <a href="/wiki/Sakura_(musician)" title="Sakura (musician)">sakura</a> joined as L'Arc-en-Ciel's new drummer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hmv_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-hmv-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 1, 1993, the band released their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Dune_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Dune (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Dune</a></i> on the independent, but well-known, record label <a href="/wiki/Danger_Crue_Records" title="Danger Crue Records">Danger Crue</a>. The album was a success and ranked number one on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> indies charts. This caught the attention of some major labels. In 1994, L'Arc-en-Ciel signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment_Japan" title="Sony Music Entertainment Japan">Sony Music Entertainment Japan</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ki/oon_Records" title="Ki/oon Records">Ki/oon</a> division. They released their major debut album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tierra_(album)" title="Tierra (album)">Tierra</a></i> that same year. Their third album <i><a href="/wiki/Heavenly_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Heavenly (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Heavenly</a></i> followed in 1995. In 1996, their fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/True_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="True (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">True</a></i>, became their first to sell over a million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> However, the success would be short-lived.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997:_sakura.27s_departure">1997: sakura's departure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1997: sakura's departure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 1997, drummer sakura was arrested for heroin possession.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He officially quit the band on October 4, 1997. This proved to be the lowest point in the band's history; when news of sakura's arrest became public, the group's CDs were removed from the shelves in record shops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Their song "<a href="/wiki/The_Fourth_Avenue_Cafe" title="The Fourth Avenue Cafe">The Fourth Avenue Cafe</a>", was replaced as the fourth ending theme of the <i><a href="/wiki/Rurouni_Kenshin" title="Rurouni Kenshin">Rurouni Kenshin</a></i> anime after only four episodes. In addition, the release of the "The Fourth Avenue Cafe" single was postponed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It was eventually released in 2006.</p>
<p>Over the next few months, the group continued to be featured in magazines as a trio. The members formed "The Zombies", which was advertised as a "copy band", and sang their songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrock_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrock-11">[11]</a></sup> They also covered <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Manson_(band)" title="Marilyn Manson (band)">Marilyn Manson</a>'s "Irresponsible Hate Anthem".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrock_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-jrock-11">[11]</a></sup> The return from the break was marked by a concert entitled Reincarnation 97 Live in Tokyo Dome. During the concert, they also revealed their new drummer <a href="/wiki/Yukihiro_(musician)" title="Yukihiro (musician)">yukihiro</a>, former drummer of the groups <a href="/wiki/Zi:Kill" title="Zi:Kill">Zi:Kill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Die_in_Cries" title="Die in Cries">Die in Cries</a>. This concert was also the group's first performance at the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a> and had an attendance of 56,000 people, with the tickets sold out in a record-breaking four minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1997, "<a href="/wiki/Niji_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="Niji (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">Niji</a>", the group's first single after sakura's departure, was released with yukihiro being listed as a support member. Not long after its release, yukihiro was signed on as the official drummer for L'Arc-en-Ciel.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1998.E2.80.932000:_Blockbuster_success">1998–2000: Blockbuster success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1998–2000: Blockbuster success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>L'Arc-en-Ciel's single "<a href="/wiki/Winter_Fall" title="Winter Fall">Winter Fall</a>", which was released in 1998, became the group's first number one single on the Oricon charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> They subsequently released their album <i><a href="/wiki/Heart_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Heart (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Heart</a></i> on February 25, 1998. The band was also included in the Japan and The Philippines release of the official movie soundtrack of <a href="/wiki/Godzilla_(1998_film)" title="Godzilla (1998 film)">Godzilla</a>. It was released on May 19, 1998 through Epic Records and mainly consists of alternative rock songs. They provided the track titled Lose Control.</p>
<p>On July 1, 1999, the group released two albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Ark_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Ark (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Ark</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ray_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Ray (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Ray</a></i>, simultaneously. These two albums became the first Japanese albums to be released simultaneously in other Asian countries outside Japan. Both albums topped the Oricon music charts, with <i>Ark</i> occupying number one and <i>Ray</i> at number two, with each selling over two million copies. This release, along with the "1999 Grand Cross Tour" who reunited over 650.000 people in 12 concerts, marked the commercial high point of the group's career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2000, two of the band's songs were included in the soundtrack for <i><a href="/wiki/Ring_0:_Birthday" title="Ring 0: Birthday">Ring 0: Birthday</a></i>.</p>
<p>Their next album <i><a href="/wiki/Real_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Real (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Real</a></i>, released on August 30, 2000, was their last studio album for some time. The song "<a href="/wiki/Stay_Away_(song)" title="Stay Away (song)">Stay Away</a>", was featured in the arcade game <i><a href="/wiki/Drum_Mania" title="Drum Mania">DrumMania 4th Mix</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.932003:_Hiatus_and_solo_projects">2001–2003: Hiatus and solo projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2001–2003: Hiatus and solo projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Clicked_Singles_Best_13" title="Clicked Singles Best 13">Clicked Singles Best 13</a></i> was released on March 14, 2001. It includes twelve songs chosen online by the group's fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It also includes an additional thirteenth song, "Anemone".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Their album <i>Real</i> was re-released as a <a href="/wiki/Super_Audio_CD" title="Super Audio CD">Super Audio CD</a> on July 4, 2001. "<a href="/wiki/Spirit_Dreams_Inside_(Another_Dream)" title="Spirit Dreams Inside (Another Dream)">Spirit Dreams Inside (Another Dream)</a>", L'Arc-en-Ciel's last new single before their hiatus, was released on September 5, 2001. The English-language version of the title track was adopted as the <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy:_The_Spirits_Within" title="Final Fantasy: The Spirits Within">Final Fantasy: The Spirits Within</a>'</i>s ending theme song.</p>
<p>After "Spirit Dreams Inside (Another Dream)" was released, the band went on a hiatus. During this period of time, the members began working on solo projects. hyde released two solo albums (<i><a href="/wiki/Roentgen_(album)" title="Roentgen (album)">Roentgen</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/666_(Hyde_album)" title="666 (Hyde album)">666</a></i>) and co-starred in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Moon_Child_(2003_film)" title="Moon Child (2003 film)">Moon Child</a></i>. He also played the part of Adam in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Kagen_no_Tsuki" title="Kagen no Tsuki">Kagen no Tsuki</a></i> ("Last Quarter of the Moon"). ken founded <a href="/wiki/Sons_of_All_Pussys" title="Sons of All Pussys">Sons of All Pussys</a> with L'Arc-en-Ciel's former drummer sakura. tetsuya started his solo career as Tetsu69 and yukihiro formed <a href="/wiki/Acid_Android" title="Acid Android">Acid Android</a>.</p>
<p>Three compilation albums; <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_L%27Arc-en-Ciel_1994%E2%80%931998" title="The Best of L'Arc-en-Ciel 1994–1998">The Best of L'Arc-en-Ciel 1994–1998</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_L%27Arc-en-Ciel_1998%E2%80%932000" title="The Best of L'Arc-en-Ciel 1998–2000">The Best of L'Arc-en-Ciel 1998–2000</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_L%27Arc-en-Ciel_C/W" title="The Best of L'Arc-en-Ciel C/W">The Best of L'Arc-en-Ciel C/W</a></i>, were released on March 19, 2003. The album series included all of their previous singles, with the exception of "Spirit Dreams Inside (Another Dream)".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004:_Comeback_and_U.S._concert_debut">2004: Comeback and U.S. concert debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2004: Comeback and U.S. concert debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2003, L'Arc-en-Ciel performed a series of seven concerts in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> entitled Shibuya Seven Days. During this event, the group announced that a new album will be released the following year. "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ready_Steady_Go_(song)" title="Ready Steady Go (song)">Ready Steady Go</a>", the group's first single in more than three years, was released in February 2004. This song was used as an opening theme song for the popular <a href="/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist_(anime)" title="Fullmetal Alchemist (anime)">anime adaption</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist" title="Fullmetal Alchemist">Fullmetal Alchemist</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The single topped the Oricon weekly singles charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Additionally, a sample of this song was also used in a <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a> game called <i><a href="/wiki/Osu!_Tatakae!_Ouendan" title="Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan">Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan</a></i>. The group subsequently released another single, "<a href="/wiki/Hitomi_no_Juunin" title="Hitomi no Juunin">Hitomi no Juunin</a>", in early March 2004. This was followed by the group's album, <i><a href="/wiki/Smile_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Smile (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Smile</a></i>, on March 31. Shortly after, on June 2, the single "<a href="/wiki/Jiyuu_e_no_Shoutai" title="Jiyuu e no Shoutai">Jiyuu e no Shoutai</a>" was the first to feature a song by <a href="#P.27unk-en-Ciel">P'unk-en-Ciel</a>.</p>
<p>On July 31, 2004, L'Arc-en-Ciel made their North American debut at the anime convention <a href="/wiki/Otakon" title="Otakon">Otakon</a>. Approximately 12,000 people attended the concert, which was held at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1st_Mariner_Arena" title="1st Mariner Arena">1st Mariner Arena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> They were the first Japanese band to perform at the arena. On May 31, 2005, <a href="/wiki/Tofu_Records" title="Tofu Records">Tofu Records</a>, Sony Music Japan's U.S. label, released a DVD of their 2004 Otakon concert. On June 25, 2007, L'Arc-en-Ciel signed a record deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HMV_Group" title="HMV Group">HMV America</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.932006:_Asian_tour_and_solo_projects">2005–2006: Asian tour and solo projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2005–2006: Asian tour and solo projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, the band released various singles, including "<a href="/wiki/Killing_Me" title="Killing Me">Killing Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/New_World_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="New World (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">New World</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Jojoushi" title="Jojoushi">Jojoushi</a>". These were later complied onto the album <i><a href="/wiki/Awake_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Awake (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Awake</a></i>, which was released on June 22, 2005. It features twelve tracks, including the "Lost Heaven", the ending theme song of <i><a href="/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist_the_Movie:_Conqueror_of_Shamballa" title="Fullmetal Alchemist the Movie: Conqueror of Shamballa">Fullmetal Alchemist the Movie: Conqueror of Shamballa</a></i>. Another of the band's single, "<a href="/wiki/Link_(song)" title="Link (song)">Link</a>", was released on July 20, 2005. This song was also featured in the movie as the opening theme song, and was included on their next album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Kiss (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Kiss</a></i>.</p>
<p>In August 2005, the band went on a nationwide tour for their album <i>Awake</i>. It was followed by another tour titled Asia Live 2005, which had the band performing in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> and ended at the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a>.</p>
<p>Following these two tours, L'Arc-en-Ciel's members turned their attention back to their own solo projects. tetsuya began recording with, veteran metal vocalist of <a href="/wiki/Dead_End_(band)" title="Dead End (band)">Dead End</a>, Morrie for his solo project Creature Creature. yukihiro regrouped his band Acid Android and released the single "Let's Dance" on April 5, 2006. Acid Android joined <a href="/wiki/Mucc" title="Mucc">Mucc</a> to perform two concerts in Shanghai in April 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>hyde composed "<a href="/wiki/Glamorous_Sky" title="Glamorous Sky">Glamorous Sky</a>", the theme song of the film adaption of the <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Nana_(manga)" title="Nana (manga)">Nana</a></i>. The song was sung by pop singer <a href="/wiki/Mika_Nakashima" title="Mika Nakashima">Mika Nakashima</a>, the first time hyde composed music for another vocalist. In addition, his own solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Faith_(Hyde_album)" title="Faith (Hyde album)">Faith</a></i>, was released on April 26, 2006. hyde also went on a five-month tour throughout Japan to publicize his album. After signing with <a href="/wiki/Tofu_Records" title="Tofu Records">Tofu Records</a>, he held four small concerts in the United States in July 2006, making his US debut. This was the first of L'Arc-en-Ciel's solo projects to be held outside Japan.</p>
<p>On August 23, 2006, ken released a solo single entitled "Speed".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.932008:_15th_Anniversary_and_Europe_concert_debut">2006–2008: 15th Anniversary and Europe concert debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2006–2008: 15th Anniversary and Europe concert debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 25 and 26, 2006, L'Arc-en-Ciel played two shows at the Tokyo Dome to celebrate their 15th anniversary. The tickets for these concerts were sold out in two minutes, breaking the previous record, which was also set by them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> A poll was created on their official website, during the weeks before the concert, which allowed fans to pick the songs they would like to hear at the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The concert was later broadcast on the <a href="/wiki/WOWOW" title="WOWOW">WOWOW</a> channel on December 23, 2006, and was subsequently broadcast on Korean <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> on February 8, 2007.</p>
<p>L'Arc-en-Ciel recorded the song "Shine", which was to be used as the opening song for the NHK anime <i><a href="/wiki/Moribito:_Guardian_of_the_Spirit" title="Moribito: Guardian of the Spirit">Moribito: Guardian of the Spirit</a></i>. In the spring of 2007, the group kicked-off their Mata Heart ni hi wo Tsukero 2007 tour in Japan. They also released the single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seventh_Heaven_(song)" title="Seventh Heaven (song)">Seventh Heaven</a>" on May 30, 2007, which topped the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Their song "<a href="/wiki/My_Heart_Draws_a_Dream" title="My Heart Draws a Dream">My Heart Draws a Dream</a>", which was used in a <a href="/wiki/Subaru" title="Subaru">Subaru</a> commercial, was also released as a single on August 29, 2007. This single also topped the Oricon charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>L'Arc-en-Ciel subsequently recorded the song "<a href="/wiki/Daybreak%27s_Bell" title="Daybreak's Bell">Daybreak's Bell</a>", which was used as the first opening theme for the anime <i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam_00" title="Mobile Suit Gundam 00">Mobile Suit Gundam 00</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> This song was released as a single on October 10, 2007, later topping the Oricon weekly charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> From November 14 to December 25, 2007, they released a limited edition single titled "<a href="/wiki/Hurry_Xmas" title="Hurry Xmas">Hurry Xmas</a>", along with two new DVDs, titled <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=15th_L%27Anniversary_Live&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="15th L'Anniversary Live (page does not exist)">15th L'Anniversary Live</a></i> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chronicle_3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chronicle 3 (page does not exist)">Chronicle 3</a></i>. Their studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Kiss (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Kiss</a></i> was released on November 21, 2007, and debuted at number one on the Oricon charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>L'Arc-en-Ciel then performed the Theater of Kiss tour, which was held from December 22, 2007 to February 17, 2008. The song "<a href="/wiki/Drink_It_Down_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="Drink It Down (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">Drink It Down</a>" was used as the Japanese opening song for the PS3/Xbox360 game <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_4" title="Devil May Cry 4">Devil May Cry 4</a></i>. It was released as a single on April 2, 2008, and topped the Oricon weekly charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The band went on a tour, entitled Tour 2008 L'7: Trans Asia via Paris, which covered several major Asian cities, as well as <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On this tour, a live show hiatus until 2011 was announced, although new material would still continue to be released. Their double A-side single "<a href="/wiki/Nexus_4/Shine" title="Nexus 4/Shine">Nexus 4/Shine</a>" and the DVD <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tour_2007-2008_Theater_of_Kiss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tour 2007-2008 Theater of Kiss (page does not exist)">Tour 2007-2008 Theater of Kiss</a></i> were released together on August 27, 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.932010:_Live_show_hiatus">2009–2010: Live show hiatus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2009–2010: Live show hiatus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 20, 2009, L'Arc-en-Ciel released the DVD of their live concert in Paris, titled <i>Live in Paris</i>. hyde and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K.A.Z" title="K.A.Z">K.A.Z</a> formed the hard rock group <a href="/wiki/Vamps_(band)" title="Vamps (band)">Vamps</a> and released their self-titled first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Vamps_(album)" title="Vamps (album)">Vamps</a></i>, on June 10, 2009.</p>
<p>On December 1, 2009, L'Arc-en-Ciel announced the release of a new single named "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bless_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="Bless (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">Bless</a>" on January 27, 2010. This was used as the theme song for <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a>'s broadcast of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_Vancouver_Olympics" title="2010 Vancouver Olympics">2010 Vancouver Olympics</a>. Also, tetsu announced that he had changed his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> to "tetsuya" and released his first artist book. The book, which ranked number six amongst the Talent Book Charts, featured early live pictures and a 30,000 character interview with the bassist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 10, 2010, L'Arc-en-Ciel released their fifth "best of" album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Quadrinity:_Member%27s_Best_Selections" title="Quadrinity: Member's Best Selections">Quadrinity: Member's Best Selections</a></i>. It contains 4 CDs with 7 tracks per band member, chosen and remastered by each of them. The first press edition of this compilation also came with a DVD that includes a variety quiz show with the band titled <i>The L'Arquiz</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.932013:_20th_Anniversary_and_world_tour">2011–2013: 20th Anniversary and world tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2011–2013: 20th Anniversary and world tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 1, 2011, L'Arc-en-Ciel celebrated both their 20th anniversary and the New Year with the midnight concert L'A Happy New Year! at <a href="/wiki/Makuhari_Messe" title="Makuhari Messe">Makuhari Messe</a>. On February 16, 2011, they released the compilation album <i>Twenity</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> This album consists of three parts: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenity_1991-1996" title="Twenity 1991-1996">Twenity 1991-1996</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenity_1997-1999" title="Twenity 1997-1999">Twenity 1997-1999</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenity_2000-2010" title="Twenity 2000-2010">Twenity 2000-2010</a></i>. A box set entitled <i>Twenity Box</i> was later released on March 9, 2011. The albums are an all-around "best of" collection of the band, and it covers everything from the formation of the group, including their hit singles till to their newest works. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenity_1991-1996" title="Twenity 1991-1996">Twenity 1991-1996</a></i> covers their work from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Dune_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Dune (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Dune</a></i> until their album <i><a href="/wiki/True_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="True (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">True</a></i>. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenity_1997-1999" title="Twenity 1997-1999">Twenity 1997-1999</a></i> covers the releases from their single "<a href="/wiki/Niji_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="Niji (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">Niji</a>" to the single "<a href="/wiki/Love_Flies" title="Love Flies">Love Flies</a>". <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenity_2000-2010" title="Twenity 2000-2010">Twenity 2000-2010</a></i> covers their releases from the double A-side "<a href="/wiki/Neo_Universe/Finale" title="Neo Universe/Finale">Neo Universe/Finale</a>" up to their commercial-only song from 2010, a cover of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Love_Rock_%27n_Roll" title="I Love Rock 'n Roll">I Love Rock 'n Roll</a>". The <i>Twenity Box</i> released on March 9 contains all three of the albums in one release. It also comes with a special digest DVD that covers the 20-year history of the band, a deluxe booklet, and a special <a href="/wiki/Music_box" title="Music box">music box</a> that contains either "<a href="/wiki/Flower_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="Flower (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">Flower</a>", "Anata", "<a href="/wiki/New_World_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="New World (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">New World</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/My_Heart_Draws_a_Dream" title="My Heart Draws a Dream">My Heart Draws a Dream</a>" at random.</p>
<p>Later, it was announced that L'Arc-en-Ciel would provide the theme song for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist_the_Movie:_The_Sacred_Star_of_Milos" title="Fullmetal Alchemist the Movie: The Sacred Star of Milos">Fullmetal Alchemist the Movie: The Sacred Star of Milos</a></i>, entitled "<a href="/wiki/Good_Luck_My_Way" title="Good Luck My Way">Good Luck My Way</a>". This is their fourth contribution to the <i>Fullmetal Alchemist</i> franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The song was released as their first 20th L'Anniversary single on June 29, 2011. To further celebrate their 20 years together, L'Arc-en-Ciel put on a "20th L'Anniversary Concert" at <a href="/wiki/Ajinomoto_Stadium" title="Ajinomoto Stadium">Ajinomoto Stadium</a> in Tokyo on May 28–29, 2011, with each day dedicated to one half of their career. All of the proceeds from the concerts were donated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Tohoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tohoku earthquake and tsunami">Great East Japan earthquake</a> relief efforts. Additionally, at the end of the concert, it was announced that there will be a L'Anniversary Japan Tour in 2011, as well as a world tour in 2012.</p>
<p>On October 12, the second 20th L'Anniversary single was released, entitled "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/XXX_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="XXX (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">XXX</a>" (Kiss Kiss Kiss). L'Arc-en-Ciel was subsequently approached by the producers of the <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_7" title="Wild 7">Wild 7</a></i> live-action film to perform the movie's theme song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Entitled "<a href="/wiki/Chase_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_song)" title="Chase (L'Arc-en-Ciel song)">Chase</a>", the song was released as the group's third L'Anniversary single on December 21, 2011.</p>
<p>L'Arc-en-Ciel then announced that they will be releasing their twelfth studio album, and gave fans who bought "Chase" a sneak peek with a live internet broadcast of the band mastering the album on December 26 on <a href="/wiki/Ustream" title="Ustream">Ustream</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12th_album_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-12th_album-38">[38]</a></sup> They also announced that their alter ego band P'unk-en-Ciel will be releasing their first album, titled <i>P'unk is Not Dead</i>, the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12th_album_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-12th_album-38">[38]</a></sup> The band's twelfth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Butterfly_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Butterfly (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Butterfly</a></i>, was released on February 8, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Butterfly_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Butterfly-39">[39]</a></sup> It includes all their singles from the single "Drink it Down" onwards and four new songs.</p>
<p>The band's 2012 world tour took them to Hong Kong on March 3, Bangkok on March 7, Shanghai on March 10, Taipei on March 17, New York on March 25, London on April 11, Paris on April 14, Singapore on April 28, Jakarta on May 2, Seoul on May 5, Yokohama on May 12–13, Osaka on May 19–20, Tokyo on May 26–27, and Honolulu on May 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-world_tour_2012_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-world_tour_2012-40">[40]</a></sup> Their show in New York was originally scheduled to be at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden#The_Theater_at_Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">The Theater at Madison Square Garden</a> on March 23, but was later moved to <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">the main arena</a>. Making L'Arc-en-Ciel the first Japanese artist to be the lead act at the legendary venue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msg_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-msg-41">[41]</a></sup> They ended the tour by returning to the US for two nights in Honolulu, Hawaii on May 31 and June 1, the latter being for the group's fan club members only.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Butterfly2_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Butterfly2-42">[42]</a></sup> At the first day, the mayor of Honolulu <a href="/wiki/Peter_Carlisle" title="Peter Carlisle">Peter Carlisle</a> made a surprise appearance and declared May 31 "L’Arc~en~Ciel Day", saying he felt the band "made a great contribution to cultural activities, and built a bridge of friendship between Hawaii and Japan".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.93current:_Return_to_the_National_Stadium_and_L.27ArCasino">2014–current: Return to the National Stadium and L'ArCasino</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2014–current: Return to the National Stadium and L'ArCasino">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 21 and 22, the band played a two-day concert at the iconic National Stadium in Tokyo, where they also played and announced the future release of their new single "Everlasting".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015, the band announced a two-day concert under the name of L'ArCasino, held on Umeshima Outdoor Special Stage, Osaka, on September 21 and 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> As they did with the previous live performances in Tokyo, they played an upcoming single for a Christmas release, "Wing's Flap".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="P.27unk-en-Ciel">P'unk-en-Ciel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Arc-en-Ciel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: P'unk-en-Ciel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>P'unk-en-Ciel</b> (stylized as <b>P'UNK~EN~CIEL</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">alter ego</a> of L'Arc-en-Ciel. Introduced in 2004, hyde takes over guitar, ken the drums, yukihiro plays the bass, while tetsuya performs the vocals. P'unk-en-Ciel's songs have a more punk sound and their music is usually heavier and faster with a different tone due to tetsuya's vocals. hyde wears a pirate patch over his left eye in P'unk-en-Ciel performances. yukihiro is known to wear a gas mask; however, this has only been seen once at a live concert during their <i><a href="/wiki/Smile_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Smile (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">Smile</a></i> tour in 2004.</p>
<p>Each member is called in capital letters; T.E.Z P'UNK (formerly TETSU P'UNK), KEN P'UNK, HYDE P'UNK and YUKI P'UNK. Each member produces one song of their choice, and they alternate their productions.</p>
<p>P'unk-en-Ciel is the re-visitation of <b>D'ark-en-Ciel</b> (stylized as <b>D'ARK~EN~CIEL</b>), a special event that took place during concerts in the years when sakura was their drummer. The only material released from D'ark-en-Ciel is the 18-minute self-titled B-side found on the (then) unreleased "<a href="/wiki/The_Fourth_Avenue_Cafe" title="The Fourth Avenue Cafe">The Fourth Avenue Cafe</a>" single. Each member was called; DARK TETSU, HYDE DARK, Suck・D'ark・La and Kën D'Ark.</p>
<p>P'unk-en-Ciel songs have been recorded and used as B-sides on singles since 2004 and their performances can be found on live DVDs. They also featured television personality <a href="/wiki/Sayaka_Aoki_(comedian)" title="Sayaka Aoki (comedian)">Sayaka Aoki</a> (called P'UNK <span class="t_nihongo_kanji"><span lang="ja">青木</span></span>) in the song "Round and Round 2005".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On February 8, 2012 P'unk-en-Ciel released their first album <i><a href="/wiki/Butterfly_(L%27Arc-en-Ciel_album)" title="Butterfly (L'Arc-en-Ciel album)">P'unk is Not Dead</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12th_album_38-2"><a href="#cite_note-12th_album-38">[38]</a></sup> which contains all twelve songs they released up to that point.</p>
