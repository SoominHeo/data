<p><b>Ahn Hyo-jin</b> (born December 10, 1991), better known by her stage name <b>LE</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>), is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>. She is the main rapper of the <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/EXID" title="EXID">EXID</a>.</p>
<p>LE made her debut as a rapper with the underground rap group Jiggy Fellaz, performing under the stage name "Elly". In 2011, after signing a recording contract with Gamgak Entertainment, LE released "Whenever You Play That Song", a duet with <a href="/wiki/Huh_Gak" title="Huh Gak">Huh Gak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-hg-3">[3]</a></sup> LE made her official debut with EXID in February 2012.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EXID">EXID</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LE_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: EXID">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/EXID" title="EXID">EXID</a></div>
<p>EXID officially debuted on February 15, 2012 with the release of their debut single, "Whoz That Girl".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_activities">Solo activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LE_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2012, LE collaborated with Gavy NJ on their song, "Don't Call Me"; she sang the rap part.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2013, producer <a href="/wiki/Brave_Brothers" title="Brave Brothers">Brave Brothers</a> revealed that LE had collaborated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B2ST" title="B2ST">B2ST</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Yong_Jun-hyung" title="Yong Jun-hyung">Junhyung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Star_(South_Korean_band)" title="Big Star (South Korean band)">Big Star</a>'s Feeldog in a project group and would be releasing a single on February 21, 2013. A teaser was released on February with a "19+" rating, which was previously acknowledged in the single cover, "19+ Profanity Included – Absurd", and on February 20, Brave Brothers released LE's version of the "You Got Some Nerve" MV teaser.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>A "15+" version of the song was released on February 21, with the curse words in the song bleeped out. The original song was rated "19+", but representatives of Brave Brothers said "You Got Some Nerve was scheduled to be released as is with a 19+ rating, but due to the interest and requests of the fans, we decided to beep out the profanity that was the reason for the 19+ rating." The explicit version of the song and its music video was released on February 25, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwave_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwave-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Some fans were angered by this, saying "Then why did they attract attention by writing 19+ on the poster? You've got some nerve" while a media outlet revealed that the song had never been sent to the TV networks to be reviewed and expressed disappointment on Brave Brothers' attitude on the situation due to their "media play" and mocking their fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwave_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwave-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014, LE was featured in <a href="/wiki/Hyuna" title="Hyuna">Hyuna</a>'s mini-album "<a href="/wiki/A_Talk" title="A Talk">A Talk</a>" and helped write the lyrics for the songs "French Kiss" and "Blacklist".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
