<p><b>LFO</b> were a British <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a> act on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warp_Records" title="Warp Records">Warp Records</a> label. LFO were pioneers of the bass-heavy techno, IDM, and acid house music of the late 1980s to mid-1990s. Originally, the group was composed of <a href="/wiki/Gez_Varley" title="Gez Varley">Gez Varley</a> (born 1971) and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bell_(British_musician)" title="Mark Bell (British musician)">Mark Bell</a>. (1971–2014)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After Varley left in 1996, LFO was Bell alone. Bell died in October 2014.</p>
<p>The group's name is derived from the abbreviation for the term <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low-frequency_oscillator" title="Low-frequency oscillator">low-frequency oscillator</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizer</a> function widely used in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LFO_(British_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Varley and Bell met while studying at <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and gave their first track, the eponymous "LFO", to <a href="/wiki/Nightmares_on_Wax" title="Nightmares on Wax">Nightmares on Wax</a>. The popularity of the demo in clubs led to the track being released by the Sheffield-based Warp Records in <a href="/wiki/1990_in_music" title="1990 in music">1990</a>, and it was a Top 20 hit in the U.K., reaching number 12 in the singles charts in July.</p>
<p>Their follow-up single, "We Are Back", was released in the summer of <a href="/wiki/1991_in_music" title="1991 in music">1991</a>.</p>
<p>DJ Martin (Martin Williams) is credited as a cowriter and coproducer of the track "LFO" but was not a member of the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Mark Bell explains:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"We gave a tape of our recordings to DJ Martin who helped loads with arranging our tracks so it'd work on the dancefloor. We'd just been messing around with drum machines since we were like thirteen, tapping away at them like they were arcade games, making tapes to play our mates at school. Anyway, DJ Martin would play our cassettes in his sets and people would go mental - in a good way - cos they were totally raw."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Later signed to <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Boy_Records" title="Tommy Boy Records">Tommy Boy Records</a> in the U.S., the duo remixed <a href="/wiki/Afrika_Bambaataa" title="Afrika Bambaataa">Afrika Bambaataa</a>'s "Planet Rock", as well as songs from <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Depeche_Mode" title="Depeche Mode">Depeche Mode</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Garnier" title="Laurent Garnier">Laurent Garnier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Sabres_of_Paradise" title="The Sabres of Paradise">The Sabres of Paradise</a>.</p>
<p>Varley left in 1996 and formed Feedback with Simon Hartley (a.k.a. Wild Planet). Mark Bell produced <i><a href="/wiki/Homogenic" title="Homogenic">Homogenic</a></i> with Björk and <i><a href="/wiki/Exciter_(album)" title="Exciter (album)">Exciter</a></i> with Depeche Mode. Bell performed with Björk on her 1997 <i><a href="/wiki/Homogenic" title="Homogenic">Homogenic</a></i> tour and 2007/2008 <i><a href="/wiki/Volta_(album)" title="Volta (album)">Volta</a></i> tour.</p>
<p>The track "Flu shot" was used in the 2005 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Wipeout_Pure" title="Wipeout Pure">Wipeout Pure</a></i>.</p>
<p>The song "Freak" was used in the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_Candy_(film)" title="Hard Candy (film)">Hard Candy</a></i> and in the 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Void" title="Enter the Void">Enter the Void</a></i>. And also it was performed live by Björk as a mashup with her 1996 single "<a href="/wiki/Hyperballad" title="Hyperballad">Hyperballad</a>".</p>
<p>In 2009 the <i><a href="/wiki/Warp20_(Recreated)" title="Warp20 (Recreated)">Warp20 (Recreated)</a></i> compilation featured covers of two early LFO songs, "LFO" by <a href="/wiki/Luke_Vibert" title="Luke Vibert">Luke Vibert</a> and "What is House? (LFO Remix)" by <a href="/wiki/Autechre" title="Autechre">Autechre</a>. The original version of "LFO", albeit in the Leeds Warehouse Mix, featured on Warp's 10th anniversary album <i><a href="/wiki/Warp_10:_Influences,_Classics,_Remixes" title="Warp 10: Influences, Classics, Remixes">Warp 10+2: Classics 89–92</a></i>.</p>
