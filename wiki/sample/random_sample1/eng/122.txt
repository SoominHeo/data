<p><b>.al</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CcTLD" title="CcTLD">ccTLD</a>) for <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>. It is administered by the Electronic and Postal Communications Authority of Albania (AKEP). As of May 1, 2013, anyone can register an .al domain.</p>
<p>Albanian domains can be registered through AKEP,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> AKEP accredited registrars and many international domain registrars. Domain names should be between 2 and 63 characters. Although the Albanian language has a number of special characters, the registry has not enabled the use of <a href="/wiki/Internationalized_domain_name" title="Internationalized domain name">IDN characters</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2nd_level_registrations">2nd level registrations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.al&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2nd level registrations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the past, registrations were not permitted directly at the second level, but a few existing names were "grandfathered"; they were <b>uniti.al</b>, <b>tirana.al</b>, <b>soros.al</b>, <b>upt.al</b> and <b>inima.al</b>.</p>
<p>In 2012 registrars started to resell .al domain names widely making them popular outside <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a> as <a href="/wiki/Domain_hack" title="Domain hack">domain hacks</a>.</p>
<p>In November 2014 the registry released two character domain names allowing for free landrush.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="3rd_level_registrations">3rd level registrations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.al&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 3rd level registrations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Registrations used to be only beneath the second-levels, i.e., <b>.com.al</b>, <b>.net.al</b>, <b>.org.al</b>, and <b>.edu.al</b> labeled appropriately for the type of organisation, but many now exist directly below .al.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Whois_Servers">Whois Servers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.al&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Whois Servers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The .al zone has no official whois server.</p>
