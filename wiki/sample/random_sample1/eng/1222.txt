<p><b>LG Corporation</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: 주식회사 LG), formerly <b>Lucky-Goldstar</b> (Korean: <i>Leogki <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geumseong" title="Geumseong">Geumseong</a></i> (럭키금성/樂喜金星)), is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(company)" title="Conglomerate (company)">conglomerate</a> corporation. It is the fourth-largest <a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">chaebol</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. It is headquartered in the LG Twin Towers building in <a href="/wiki/Yeouido-dong" title="Yeouido-dong">Yeouido-dong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeongdeungpo-gu" title="Yeongdeungpo-gu">Yeongdeungpo-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> LG makes <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">chemicals</a>, and telecom products and operates subsidiaries such as <a href="/wiki/LG_Electronics" title="LG Electronics">LG Electronics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zenith_Electronics" title="Zenith Electronics">Zenith</a>, <a href="/wiki/LG_Display" title="LG Display">LG Display</a>, <a href="/wiki/LG_Uplus" title="LG Uplus">LG Uplus</a> and <a href="/wiki/LG_Chem" title="LG Chem">LG Chem</a> in over 80 countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LG Corp. established as Lak-Hui Chemical Industrial Corp. in 1947.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1952, Lak-Hui (pronounced "Lucky", currently LG Chem) became the first Korean company to enter the <a href="/wiki/Plastics_industry" title="Plastics industry">plastics industry</a>. As the company expanded its plastic business, it established <a href="/wiki/GoldStar" title="GoldStar">GoldStar</a> Co. Ltd. (currently LG Electronics Inc.) In 1958, both companies Lucky and GoldStar merged and formed Lucky Goldstar.</p>
<p>GoldStar produced South Korea's first <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a>. Many <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">consumer electronics</a> were sold under the brand name GoldStar, while some other household products (not available outside South Korea) were sold under the brand name of Lucky. The Lucky brand was famous for hygiene products such as soaps and HiTi laundry detergents, but the brand was mostly associated with its Lucky and Perioe <a href="/wiki/Toothpaste" title="Toothpaste">toothpaste</a>. Even today, LG continues to manufacture some of these products for the South Korean market, such as laundry detergent.</p>
<p>In 1995, to compete better in the Western market, the Lucky-Goldstar Corporation was renamed "LG". The company also associates the letters LG with the company's tagline "Life's Good". Since 2009, LG has owned the <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain name</a> LG.com.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_ventures">Joint ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Joint ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2001, LG had two joint ventures with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Philips_Electronics" title="Royal Philips Electronics">Royal Philips Electronics</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG_Philips_Display" title="LG Philips Display">LG Philips Display</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG_Philips_LCD" title="LG Philips LCD">LG Philips LCD</a>, but Philips sold off its shares in late 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2005, LG entered into a joint venture with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nortel_Networks" title="Nortel Networks">Nortel Networks</a>, creating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG-Ericsson" title="LG-Ericsson">LG-Nortel Co. Ltd.</a></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_markets">International markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International markets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 30 November 2012, <a href="/wiki/ComScore" title="ComScore">comScore</a> released a report of the October 2012 U.S. Mobile Subscriber Market Share that found LG lost its place as second in the U.S. mobile market share to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apple_Inc" title="Apple Inc">Apple Inc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 20 January 2013, Counterpoint Research announced that LG has overtaken Apple to become second largest in U.S. market share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 August 2013, <a href="/wiki/ComScore" title="ComScore">comScore</a> released a report of the June 2013 U.S. Smartphone Subscriber Market Share that found LG fell to fifth place in the U.S mobile market share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure_and_financial_position">Structure and financial position</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Structure and financial position">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LG Corporation is a <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> that operates worldwide through more than 30 companies in the electronics, chemical, and telecom fields. Its electronics subsidiaries manufacture and sell products ranging from electronic and digital home appliances to televisions and mobile telephones, from <a href="/wiki/Thin-film-transistor_liquid-crystal_display" title="Thin-film-transistor liquid-crystal display">thin-film transistor liquid-crystal displays</a> to security devices and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semiconductors" title="Semiconductors">semiconductors</a>. In the chemical industry, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subsidiaries" title="Subsidiaries">subsidiaries</a> manufacture and sell products including cosmetics, industrial textiles, rechargeable batteries and toner products, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polycarbonates" title="Polycarbonates">polycarbonates</a>, medicines, and surface decorative materials. Its telecom products include long-distance and international phone services, mobile and broadband telecommunications services, as well as consulting and telemarketing services. LG also operates the <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> Korea Bottling Company, manages real estate, offers <a href="/wiki/Management_consulting" title="Management consulting">management consulting</a>, and operates professional sports clubs.</p>
