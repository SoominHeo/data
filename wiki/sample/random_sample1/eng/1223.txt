<p><b>LG Uplus Corp.</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">LG유플러스</span>; stylized as <b>LG U<sup>+</sup></b>, <a href="/wiki/Korea_Exchange" title="Korea Exchange">KRX</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://eng.krx.co.kr/por_eng/m2/m2_1/m2_1_1/JHPENG02001_01.jsp?isu_cd=A032640" rel="nofollow">032640</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">cellular</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_network_operator" title="Mobile network operator">carrier</a> owned by <a href="/wiki/LG_Corporation" title="LG Corporation">LG Corporation</a>, Korea's fourth largest <a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">conglomerate</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and parent company of the <a href="/wiki/LG_Electronics" title="LG Electronics">LG Electronics</a>. It was formerly known as LG Telecom.</p>
<p>The carrier adopted its current name after the July 2010 merger with another two LG telecommunication subsidiaries, Dacom and Powercom. LG U<sup>+</sup> offers a variety of mobile services. BankOn is one of the most popular <a href="/wiki/Mobile_banking" title="Mobile banking">Mobile banking</a> services in South Korea and MusicOn is a music e-store.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Uplus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After decision of <a href="/wiki/KT_Corporation" title="KT Corporation">Korea Telecom</a>, the state-owned phone company to sell its cellular business to private investors in 1994, the South Korean government opened the telecommunications sector up to competition. LG entered the wireless communications market in 1996 by acquiring a <a href="/wiki/Code_division_multiple_access" title="Code division multiple access">CDMA</a> license in June and founded a new carrier named LG Telecom, which built a nationwide digital cellular network. In October 1997, <a href="/wiki/Personal_Communications_Service" title="Personal Communications Service">PCS</a> cellular service was launched.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 1998, in an effort to make itself stand out from the bigger, more established players in the market, LG Telecom launched the world's first commercial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IS-95" title="IS-95">cdmaOne</a> data service using PCS technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>To better position itself to compete in the bundled services market LG Telecom acquired LG Dacom, a fixed-line communications networks and Internet-related service provider and LG Powercom, one of Korea's largest <a href="/wiki/Internet_service_provider" title="Internet service provider">ISPs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On July 1, 2010, LG Telecom switched to its current name, "LG U<sup>+</sup>."</p>

<p><span style="font-size:60%;"><i>Source: Slowtime</i></span></p>
<p>In July 2006, the South Korean government canceled LG Telecom's license for 2.1 GHz <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/W-CDMA" title="W-CDMA">W-CDMA</a> bandwidth after the company opted not to develop the technology. LG Telecom will instead continue investing and upgrading in its <a href="/wiki/CDMA2000" title="CDMA2000">CDMA2000</a> <a href="/wiki/Evolution-Data_Optimized" title="Evolution-Data Optimized">EV-DO</a> Rev. A network.</p>
<p>In July 2011, LG U+ launched its <a href="/wiki/LTE_(telecommunication)" title="LTE (telecommunication)">LTE</a> network, nationwide coverage is expected to be complete by March 2012.</p>
<p>On July 17, 2013 LG Uplus launched LTE-A service with the introduction of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galaxy_S4" title="Galaxy S4">Galaxy S4</a> LTE-A, the world’s first "100% LTE" smartphone that can utilize data, voice and text with LTE and not fall back to CDMA. Starting from 2014 LG Uplus plans to release only "100% LTE" phones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Landline">Landline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Uplus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Landline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010 LG Telecom acquired Dacom Corp., a network services firm that operated Hanaro Telecom's fixed line networks. The new affiliate helped LG enter the landline communications market.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadband">Broadband</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Uplus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Broadband">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Launched in September 2005, U+Home is an optic LAN service that provides fast speeds of up to 100Mbit/s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IPTV">IPTV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Uplus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: IPTV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>U+TV was launched in December 2007, providing various two-way services, terrestrial and HD broadcasting.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business-to-business_sales">Business-to-business sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Uplus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Business-to-business sales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After merging LG DACOM in 2010, It was succeeded to LG Uplus one of the largest B2B service area. It is account for about 20% of gross sales by the role of cash-cow. It has not only traditional cable industry, also non-traditional one like electronic payment and so on.</p>
