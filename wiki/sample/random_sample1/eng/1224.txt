<p><b>LG Chem Ltd.</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: LGíí), often referred to as LG Chemical, is the largest Korean chemical company<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stock_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-stock-1">[1]</a></sup> and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical_and_Engineering_News" title="Chemical and Engineering News">Chemical and Engineering News</a>, it was the 13th largest chemical company in the world by sales in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was first established as the Lucky Chemical Industrial Corporation, which manufactured <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetics</a>. It is now solely a <a href="/wiki/Business-to-business" title="Business-to-business">business-to-business</a> company.</p>
<p>In order to create a holding company, the "old" LG Chem (the legal predecessor of LG Corp) was split off, creating the "new" LG Chem and LG Care. The "old" LG Chem changed its name to LG CI l. After its merger with LG EI (the legal successor of Goldstar) in 2003, it changed its name to LG Corp.</p>
<p>The company has eight factories in South Korea and a network of 29 business locations in 15 countries. This network includes a holding company in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, 14 overseas manufacturing subsidiaries, five marketing subsidiaries, seven representative offices, and two <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">R&amp;D</a> centers. LG Chem has three main business areas:</p>
<ul>
<li>Basic materials and chemicals</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">Information technology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronics</a> materials</li>
<li>Energy solutions</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Basic_materials_and_chemicals">Basic materials and chemicals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Chem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Basic materials and chemicals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LG Chem is a supplier of petrochemicals ranging from basic distillates to specialty <a href="/wiki/Polymer" title="Polymer">polymers</a>. For example, it is a large producer of common <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plastics" title="Plastics">plastics</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Acrylonitrile_butadiene_styrene" title="Acrylonitrile butadiene styrene">acrylonitrile butadiene styrene</a> (ABS),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Styrene-acrylonitrile_resin" title="Styrene-acrylonitrile resin">styrene-acrylonitrile resin</a> (SAN),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lg-abs-san_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-lg-abs-san-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Polyvinyl_chloride" title="Polyvinyl chloride">polyvinyl chloride</a> (PVC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It also produces raw materials and liquids, including <a href="/wiki/Plasticizer" title="Plasticizer">plasticizers</a>, specialty additives, <a href="/wiki/Alcohol" title="Alcohol">alcohols</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polyolefin" title="Polyolefin">polyolefins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acrylic_acid" title="Acrylic acid">acrylic acid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_rubber" title="Synthetic rubber">synthetic rubber</a>, styrenics, performance polymers, <a href="/wiki/Engineering_plastic" title="Engineering plastic">engineering plastics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elastomer" title="Elastomer">elastomers</a>, conductive resins, and other chemicals.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Information_technology_and_electronics_materials">Information technology and electronics materials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Chem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Information technology and electronics materials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LG Chem supplies display and optical films, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Printed-circuit_board" title="Printed-circuit board">printed circuit</a> materials, and <a href="/wiki/Toner" title="Toner">toners</a>. It also supplies LCD polarizers, which are multi-layer sheets of film applied to the top and bottom surfaces of <a href="/wiki/Thin-film-transistor_liquid-crystal_display" title="Thin-film-transistor liquid-crystal display">TFT-LCD</a> panels to transmit the light from the backlight unit through the panel, and <a href="/wiki/Stereoscopy" title="Stereoscopy">3D</a> FPR (<a href="/wiki/Film-type_patterned_retarder" title="Film-type patterned retarder">film-type patterned retarder</a>) film, which enables three-dimensional viewing.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_solutions">Energy solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG_Chem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Energy solutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LG Chem completed development and began mass production of Koreaâs first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lithium-ion_batteries" title="Lithium-ion batteries">lithium-ion batteries</a> back in 1999. At the end of 2011, LG Chem was the worldâs third-largest maker with an annual production capacity of 1,000 million cells.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is also a supplier of automotive battery for electric vehicles.</p>
<p>LG Chem Michigan is a wholly owned subsidiary of LG Chem based in <a href="/wiki/Holland,_Michigan" title="Holland, Michigan">Holland, Michigan</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> which operates a plant to manufacture advanced battery cells for electric vehicles in <a href="/wiki/Holland,_Michigan" title="Holland, Michigan">Holland, Michigan</a>. The <span style="white-space: nowrap"><a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>303</span> million Holland plant received 50% of its funding from <a class="mw-redirect" and Reinvestment Act of 2009">stimulus funds</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and started manufacturing battery systems in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The plant can produce enough cells per year to build between 50,000 and 200,000 battery packs for <a href="/wiki/Electric_car" title="Electric car">electric cars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_electric_vehicle" title="Hybrid electric vehicle">hybrids</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Volt" title="Chevrolet Volt">Chevrolet Volt</a> by <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">General Motors</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cpivolt_12-0"><a
