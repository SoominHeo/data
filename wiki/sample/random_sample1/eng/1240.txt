<p>"<b>Love Sosa</b>" is the second single by rapper <a href="/wiki/Chief_Keef" title="Chief Keef">Chief Keef</a> from his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Finally_Rich" title="Finally Rich">Finally Rich</a></i>. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Young_Chop" title="Young Chop">Young Chop</a> and Dougie. It was written by Keith Cozart and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyree_Pittman" title="Tyree Pittman">Tyree Pittman</a> for Chief Keef's first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Finally_Rich" title="Finally Rich">Finally Rich</a></i> (2012). The song was released as the album's second single on October 18, 2012.</p>
<p>The accompanying <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was directed by Duan Gaines, more commonly known as DGainz. The film features Chief Keef and his collective performing in front of the camera while inside a house. The music video was released on October 18, 2012, the same day the single was released.</p>

<p>The song leaked to the internet unfinished in 2012, and about a week later, it was leaked in full version. The song was released through iTunes a few weeks later.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Sosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video, directed by DGainz, was shot and released on October 18, 2012. It was shot in the same spot as the video for Keef's first single <a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Like" title="I Don't Like">I Don't Like</a>. The video was shot on the South Side of Chicago. The video has over 60 million views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remix">Remix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Sosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Remix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although an official remix is not released yet, in December 2012 the song's producer Young Chop stated that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> may appear on another version of the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On January 16, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Maybach_Music_Group" title="Maybach Music Group">MMG</a> artists <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and Stalley released a freestyle over the instrumental of "Love Sosa."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Sosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a> named the song #20 of the 50 best songs of 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Rapper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a> praised the song on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, saying he played it 130 times in 3 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-versed_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-versed-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drake_Tweet_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Drake_Tweet-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chief_Keef" title="Chief Keef">Chief Keef</a> announced "Love Sosa" will be on <a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7">[7]</a></sup> however this would turn out to be untrue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Jayson Greene of Pitchfork found the song to be very catchy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
