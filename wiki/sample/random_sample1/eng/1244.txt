<p><i><b>Lügenmarsch</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> for <i>Lie march</i>) is a picture LP of the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">Hard rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/B%C3%B6hse_Onkelz" title="Böhse Onkelz">Böhse Onkelz</a>. It was released in 1989. Lügenmarsch contains 7 tracks, 5 of them are also on the album <a href="/wiki/Kneipenterroristen" title="Kneipenterroristen">Kneipenterroristen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Track_notes">Track notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BCgenmarsch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Track notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ein_guter_Freund">Ein guter Freund</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BCgenmarsch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ein guter Freund">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The songs contains the message that a good friend is the best that you can find on the earth. It is sung in the style of a typical <a href="/wiki/Drinking_song" title="Drinking song">drinking song</a>, including an easy text and a rhythmic melody.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="K.C3.B6nige_f.C3.BCr_einen_Tag">Könige für einen Tag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BCgenmarsch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Könige für einen Tag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>They dreamed about to be kings for one day. "Vermin, carrying me on their hands / and goes to soccer with me / <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> is yard chapel / so should it be/ "Chorus:"We are kings for one day, kings for one day"</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="L.C3.BCgenmarsch">Lügenmarsch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BCgenmarsch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lügenmarsch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This song is written for the press and for the Bundesprüfstelle, which embargoed the <a href="/wiki/Der_nette_Mann" title="Der nette Mann">first album</a>.<br/>
The song parodies common cliches about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metaller" title="Metaller">metallers</a>, the press used to discredit the band.<br/>
For example, it contains a passage, that addresses the song to people who don't like the band due to their long hair. (<i>Für die Leute die uns immer wieder schreiben, mit langen Haaren können wir euch nicht leiden</i>)<br/>
In the refrain they are singing "we are total metallers" (<i>Wir sind die totalen Metaller</i>). The term metaller is pronounced in a very German way, that could be a site blow to their conservative opposers, who like to use traditional expressions.<br/>
The ironic use of "total" expresses that they don't want to be labeled as typical metallers, but as individual band.</p>
