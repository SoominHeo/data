<p>The domain name <b>net</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Generic_top-level_domain" title="Generic top-level domain">generic top-level domain</a> (gTLD) used in the <a href="/wiki/Domain_Name_System" title="Domain Name System">Domain Name System</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>. The name is derived from the word <i>network</i>, indicating it was originally intended for organizations involved in networking technologies, such as <a href="/wiki/Internet_service_provider" title="Internet service provider">Internet service providers</a> and other infrastructure companies. However, restrictions were never enforced and the domain is now a general purpose namespace. It is still popular with network operators, the advertising sector,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and is often treated as an alternative to <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.com" title=".com">com</a></span>.</p>
<p><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">net</span> is one of the original top-level domains<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (the other six being <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.com" title=".com">com</a></span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.org" title=".org">org</a></span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.edu" title=".edu">edu</a></span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.gov" title=".gov">gov</a></span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.mil" title=".mil">mil</a></span>, and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.arpa" title=".arpa">arpa</a></span>) despite not being mentioned in <a class="external mw-magiclink-rfc" href="//tools.ietf.org/html/rfc920" rel="nofollow">RFC 920</a>, having been created in January 1985. As of 2015, it is the fourth most popular top-level domain, after <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.com" title=".com">.com</a></span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.tk" title=".tk">.tk</a></span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.de" title=".de">.de</a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Verisign" title="Verisign">Verisign</a>, the operator of <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">net</span> after acquiring <a href="/wiki/Network_Solutions" title="Network Solutions">Network Solutions</a>, held an operations contract that expired on 30 June 2005. <a href="/wiki/ICANN" title="ICANN">ICANN</a>, the organization responsible for domain management, sought proposals from organizations to operate the domain upon expiration of the contract. Verisign regained the contract bid, and secured its control over the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">net</span> registry for another six years. On 30 June 2011, the contract with Verisign was automatically renewed for another six years. This is because of a resolution approved by the ICANN board, which states that renewal will be automatic as long as Verisign meets certain ICANN requirements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Registrations are processed via accredited registrars and <a href="/wiki/Internationalized_domain_name" title="Internationalized domain name">internationalized domain names</a> are also accepted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
