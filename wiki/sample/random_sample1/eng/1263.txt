<p><b>MBK Entertainment</b> is a entertainment company established in 2006 by Kim Kwang-soo. The label was formerly known as <b>Core Contents Media</b>, the entertainment division of <a href="/wiki/CJ_E%26M_Music_Performance_Division" title="CJ E&amp;M Music Performance Division">CJ E&amp;M Music Performance Division</a> created after the acquisition of MAXMP3, Poibos, and Mediopia. On October 1, 2014, it was announced that Core Contents Media had been acquired by MBK Co., Ltd. (formerly CS ELSOLAR Co., Ltd.) and renamed as MBK Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> MBK is an abbreviation for "Music Beyond Korea".</p>
<p>Formerly home to ballad group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seeya" title="Seeya">Seeya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Davichi" title="Davichi">Davichi</a>, it's current home to <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">k-pop</a> artists such as <a href="/wiki/T-ara" title="T-ara">T-ara</a>, HighBrow, <a href="/wiki/Dia_(band)" title="Dia (band)">DIA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shannon_(British_singer)" title="Shannon (British singer)">Shannon</a> and Janey. It also manages veteran actor such as, <a href="/wiki/Moon_Hee-kyung" title="Moon Hee-kyung">Moon Hee-kyung</a>.</p>

Ryu Hyoyoung filing for contract termination with MBK Entertainment in 2015 and accepted in 2016. <li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Taewoon"><sup>13</sup>Taewoon debuted as a member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coed_School" title="Coed School">Coed School</a> in 2010 and joined the male unit <a href="/wiki/Speed" title="Speed">Speed</a> in 2012. He left from the group in 2015 to pursue his solo career.</span></li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Speed"><sup>14</sup>Speed originally debuted as member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coed_School" title="Coed School">Coed School</a>. The group's member individual confirmed parted ways with MBK Entertainment in 2016.
