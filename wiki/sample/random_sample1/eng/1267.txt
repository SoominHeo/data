<p><b>Aaron Tyler</b> (born May 22, 1967), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>MC Eiht</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist born in <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Georgia" title="Augusta, Georgia">Augusta, Georgia</a> but moved as a child to <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. Many of his songs are based on his life in Compton. His stage name was partly inspired by the numeral in <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>'s name. He chose Eiht for its links to "hood culture", including <a href="/wiki/Olde_English_800" title="Olde English 800">Olde English 800</a> (8 Ball) and .3<b>8</b> caliber firearms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> leader of <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Compton%27s_Most_Wanted" title="Compton's Most Wanted">Compton's Most Wanted</a>, which also included fellow Compton-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rappers</a> <a href="/wiki/Boom_Bam" title="Boom Bam">Boom Bam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tha_Chill" title="Tha Chill">Tha Chill</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ_Mike_T" title="DJ Mike T">DJ Mike T</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Slip" title="DJ Slip">DJ Slip</a> and Ant Capone. He is also known for his role in the 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Menace_II_Society" title="Menace II Society">Menace II Society</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Eiht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tyler grew to fame on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Coast_Hip_Hop" title="West Coast Hip Hop">West Coast</a> as one of two MCs in the four-man crew, <a href="/wiki/Compton%27s_Most_Wanted" title="Compton's Most Wanted">Compton's Most Wanted</a>. After enjoying increasing success with the group, which peaked with 1992's <i><a href="/wiki/Music_to_Driveby" title="Music to Driveby">Music to Driveby</a></i>, Eiht took a role in the hit movie <a href="/wiki/Menace_II_Society" title="Menace II Society">Menace II Society</a> as well as a high-profile spot on the soundtrack. The song, "<a href="/wiki/Streiht_Up_Menace" title="Streiht Up Menace">Streiht Up Menace</a>," which chronicles the unfortunate downturn of the film's protagonist within the pathological conditions of American inner city life, is considered by many critics to be Eiht's <i>magnum opus</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The movie spurred him into a solo career, although his next two albums <i><a href="/wiki/We_Come_Strapped" title="We Come Strapped">We Come Strapped</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Threatz" title="Death Threatz">Death Threatz</a></i> would be accredited as MC Eiht featuring Compton's Most Wanted. These albums were considered his best and took place during the most tense years of the 'beef' between Eiht and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>, Eiht's childhood friends Boom Bam and Tha Chill of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N.O.T.R" title="N.O.T.R">N.O.T.R</a> were heavily involved with both albums and the <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">Quik</a> beef, there are several explicit remarks towards Quik made on these albums by E, Bam and Chill. E was also associated with W 159th St Tragniew Park Compton Crips.</p>
<p>He states that he did not wish to star in comedy films in order to maintain his public image. He later only appeared in the low-budget movies "Reasons," "Thicker Than Water" and "Who Made the Potato Salad?" as well as providing the voice for Lance 'Ryder' Wilson in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_San_Andreas" title="Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas">Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His music has also been included in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto" title="Grand Theft Auto">Grand Theft Auto</a> series, both alone and with Compton's Most Wanted.</p>
<p>He put out <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Man_Standing_(MC_Eiht_album)" title="Last Man Standing (MC Eiht album)">Last Man Standing</a></i>, the first album accredited only to MC Eiht, then broke from the label for Big Beat/<a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic</a>. Eiht never ended up putting out an album on the label, instead eventually signing to and releasing the album <i><a href="/wiki/Section_8_(album)" title="Section 8 (album)">Section 8</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority</a> in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Eiht would hop between record labels and one-off releases in the next years, moving between independent labels Half-Ounce, D3 Entertainment, and Lookin' Up Entertainment from 2001 to 2003. He would put out records with <a href="/wiki/Spice_1" title="Spice 1">Spice 1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brotha_Lynch_Hung" title="Brotha Lynch Hung">Brotha Lynch Hung</a> in 2004 and 2006 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Talk_Ent." title="Real Talk Ent.">Real Talk Ent.</a>, releasing another solo album between them on West Inc./Native Records. His inability to settle at a label contributed to his joining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Warzone_(group)" title="The Warzone (group)">The Warzone</a>, a trio put together by <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and consisting of <a href="/wiki/Goldie_Loc" title="Goldie Loc">Goldie Loc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kam_(rapper)" title="Kam (rapper)">Kam</a> and MC Eiht himself. In 2006, Eiht and the group appeared on the single "<a href="/wiki/Candy_(Drippin%27_Like_Water)" title="Candy (Drippin' Like Water)">Candy (Drippin' Like Water)</a>" on <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Blue_Carpet_Treatment" title="Tha Blue Carpet Treatment">Tha Blue Carpet Treatment</a></i>. In 2010, Eiht appears on the remix to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dam_Funk" title="Dam Funk">Dam Funk</a>'s "hood Pass Intact".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> MC Eiht also appeared with <a href="/wiki/Young_Maylay" title="Young Maylay">Young Maylay</a> in Blaq Poet's new street single, <i>Ain't Nuttin' Changed (Remix)</i>.</p>
<p>In June 2011, MC Eiht released his single "Fine By Me" accompanied by a music video directed by Director Hugo V. under his new label Year Round/Blue Stamp Records. Year Round Records is owned by New York-based hip hop artist <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>.</p>
<p>In October 2012, MC Eiht was featured on the song "<a href="/wiki/M.A.A.D_City" title="M.A.A.D City">m.A.A.d city</a>", by <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, from his major label debut <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kid,_M.A.A.D_City" title="Good Kid, M.A.A.D City">Good Kid, m.A.A.d City</a></i>. Eiht released the <i>Keep It Hood</i> EP, through BLUESTAMP Music Group on January 4, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The album features cuts from <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a> and production from Brenk Sinatra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2013, he plans to release a collaboration album with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer of 2014 MC Eiht released a single with The Outlawz, Tupac Shakur's old group, titled "Shut Em Down" which makes references to old Outlawz members Tupac and Kadafi.The song is a part of Eiht's upcoming "Which Way Iz West LP" and is MC Eiht's first LP in eight years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feud_with_DJ_Quik">Feud with DJ Quik</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Eiht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Feud with DJ Quik">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MC Eiht and his group CMW got involved in a long tumultuous rivalry with fellow Compton DJ/rapper <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a> that lasted for several years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The feud traces back to a track on DJ Quik's debut mixtape <i>The Red Tape</i>, where Quik indirectly dissed both Compton's Most Wanted and <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a>. During that time, Quik was a member to the Tree Top <a href="/wiki/Pirus" title="Pirus">Piru Bloods</a> and Eiht was a member of the 159th St. Tragniew Park Compton <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crips</a>. On the track "Duck Sick" from CMW's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Compton_Thang" title="It's a Compton Thang">It's a Compton Thang</a></i>, they criticized and questioned Quik's street credibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> They hit Quik again in 1991 on their sophomore effort <i><a href="/wiki/Straight_Checkn_%27Em" title="Straight Checkn 'Em">Straight Checkn 'Em</a></i>. Quik didn't respond to CMW's disses on his debut album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quik_is_the_Name" title="Quik is the Name">Quik is the Name</a></i>, but he responded to Eiht on the title track to his sophomore effort <i><a href="/wiki/Way_2_Fonky" title="Way 2 Fonky">Way 2 Fonky</a></i>. CMW responded months later with a music video that featured a DJ Quik lookalike that was being chased by CMW. Quik didn't respond to it as he was facing label problems and other music projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> However, on the soundtrack to the 1994 short film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murder_Was_The_Case" title="Murder Was The Case">Murder Was The Case</a></i>, on the track "Dollaz + Sense", Quik ruthlessly verbally attacks Eiht, calling him a movie script killer (in reference to Eiht's appearance in the critically acclaimed 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Menace_II_Society" title="Menace II Society">Menace II Society</a></i>), a coward, and more. Quik furthered the flames by performing the song at the 1995 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Source_Awards" title="Source Awards">Source Awards</a> in Atlanta. The feud stayed quiet until April 1996, where Eiht responded to Quik on his sophomore solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Threatz" title="Death Threatz">Death Threatz</a></i>. The feud between Quik and Eiht slowly died down in 1997 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> following the deaths of <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G" title="The Notorious B.I.G">The Notorious B.I.G</a>, but the feud didn't actually end until the summer of 2002, where <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, and other West Coast rappers helped Eiht and Quik squash the beef. Since then, Quik and Eiht collaborated on various songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Even though the rivalry was not on the level of the <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>-<a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a> rivalry or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Coast-West_Coast_hip_hop_rivalry" title="East Coast-West Coast hip hop rivalry">East Coast-West Coast hip hop rivalry</a> since it was not covered by the media, it has been hailed by most hip hop fans and critics that it stands out as one of the longest active hip hop rivalries of all time (from 1987 to 2002).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
