<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">MIT Gangnam Style</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>MIT Gangnam Style</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (also known as <b>Chomsky Style</b>), is a <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">parody</a> of the <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> viral video, "<a href="/wiki/Gangnam_Style" title="Gangnam Style">Gangnam Style</a>". The lighthearted satirical video was directed by Eddie Ha '13 and produced by Ingwon Chae '14 of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> (MIT).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The video, which has been called the "Best Gangnam Style Parody Yet" by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a></i>, stars MIT student Richard Yoon '13 and features <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearances</a> by MIT professors <a href="/wiki/Noam_Chomsky" title="Noam Chomsky">Noam Chomsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Lander" title="Eric Lander">Eric Lander</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Donald_Sadoway" title="Donald Sadoway">Donald Sadoway</a>, as well as hundreds of MIT students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_reaction">Release and reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MIT_Gangnam_Style&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Release and reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The video was released on YouTube on the morning of October 27, 2012, and had nearly 1.5 million views by October 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The day after its release, the video was listed on YouTube's "Most Popular in Entertainment" playlist. That week, it was listed as the number 1 most popular video in Entertainment, and number 6 overall. One week after its release, on Saturday, November 3, the video had nearly 4 million views. As of April 29, 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MIT_Gangnam_Style&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the video has over 5.2 million views.</p>
<p>Soon after the video's release, <a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a> published an article with the headline "MIT 'Chomsky Style' Best Gangnam Parody Yet? Noted Intellectual Steals The Wacky Show".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 30, author <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Coelho" title="Paulo Coelho">Paulo Coelho</a>, tweeted the video, along with the line "Et tu, Noam Chomsky?".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 3, Psy re-tweeted a post by his manager, <a href="/wiki/Scooter_Braun" title="Scooter Braun">Scooter Braun</a>, that read "sorry Harvard Business School but MIT GANGNAM STYLE was killer! acapella part was awesome".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The MIT Admissions Office student blog commented on the video, showing both the original and the parody versions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MITGangnam_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-MITGangnam-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_details">Production details</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MIT_Gangnam_Style&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production details">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The parody video was a major student-run project that involved coordinating hundreds of people and over 25 different student organizations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TechGangnam_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TechGangnam-8">[8]</a></sup> The music video closely follows the original version, and includes cameo appearances by MIT professors <a href="/wiki/Donald_Sadoway" title="Donald Sadoway">Donald Sadoway</a>, recognized by <i>Time Magazine</i> in 2012 as one of the "Top 100 Most Influential People in the World", <a href="/wiki/Eric_Lander" title="Eric Lander">Eric Lander</a>, who is co-chairman of President Barack Obama's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology, and <a href="/wiki/Noam_Chomsky" title="Noam Chomsky">Noam Chomsky</a>, a pioneer of modern linguistics.</p>
<p>The entire production took only 7 weeks from conception to filming to editing and release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TechGangnam_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-TechGangnam-8">[8]</a></sup> The project was organized by MIT student Eddie Ha, with help from fellow members of the MIT Korean Student Association, and many others recruited from other student groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TechGangnam_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-TechGangnam-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>A highlight of the video occurs when Professor Eric Lander is interrupted while teaching (in MIT's largest lecture hall, 26-100), and he joins the dancer by leaping up onto a lab bench and doing some exuberant dance moves of his own.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TechGangnam_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-TechGangnam-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Landergan_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Landergan-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Throughout the video, the main dancer is often accompanied by two other dancers who wear <a href="/wiki/Horse_head_mask" title="Horse head mask">horse head masks</a>. The MIT <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">mascot</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tim_the_Beaver" title="Tim the Beaver">Tim the Beaver</a>, makes several cameo appearances, as does a dancer dressed as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panda_bear" title="Panda bear">panda bear</a>. Two <a href="/wiki/Dragon_dance" title="Dragon dance">dancing dragons</a> also are glimpsed briefly.</p>
<p>At the end of the video, a sleepy student in a chemical <a href="/wiki/Laboratory" title="Laboratory">laboratory</a> rubs his face with his gloved hand as he tries to wake up. This scene has been criticized as showing <a href="/wiki/Laboratory_safety" title="Laboratory safety">unsafe laboratory procedure</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TechGangnam_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-TechGangnam-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
