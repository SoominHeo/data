<p><i><b>Mona Lisa</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">모나리자</span>) is an EP by South Korean boy band <a href="/wiki/MBLAQ" title="MBLAQ">MBLAQ</a>. The album was released online on July 12 and physically on July 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mona_Lisa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mona_Lisa-1">[1]</a></sup> The online pre-orders started in July 8, 2011. The album consists of six new tracks, and the title track, also called <i>Mona Lisa</i> is a Spanish electronic dance track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mona_Lisa_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mona_Lisa-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">Concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mona_Lisa_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The concept features the members parodying famous historical figures; Seungho chose <a href="/wiki/Papillon_(book)" title="Papillon (book)">Papillon</a>, G.O chose <a href="/wiki/James_Dean" title="James Dean">James Dean</a>, Joon chose <a href="/wiki/Zorro" title="Zorro">Zorro</a>, Thunder chose <a href="/wiki/Boy_George" title="Boy George">Boy George</a>, and Mir chose <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mona_Lisa_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mona_Lisa-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotions">Promotions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mona_Lisa_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The group started promoting the title track "Mona Lisa" starting July 14 on TV music shows such as <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i>. The song "I Think You Know" was used for the comeback. They finished promoting the song on August 28 and followed with the song "I Don't Know" on September 1. The promotions of the song and the EP ended on September 11.</p>
