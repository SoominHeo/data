<p><i><b>Mystic Ballad</b></i> is the second studio album by <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Davichi" title="Davichi">Davichi</a>. The album was digitally released on March 18, 2013, with the physical release following on April 2, 2013. "Just the Two of Us" (둘이서 한잔해) served as the promotional single. The track "Turtle" was released as a digital single two weeks prior to the initial album release.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mystic_Ballad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The release of album was officially announced on January 21, 2013. Davichi expressed, "We’re going to be showing a lot of music that we’ve never attempted before through our second studio album in March. Please look forward to it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On February 27, 2013, the members of Davichi informed fans that the album would be released on March 18, 2013, and that they would be working with famous artists <a href="/wiki/Duble_Sidekick" title="Duble Sidekick">Duble Sidekick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Verbal_Jint" title="Verbal Jint">Verbal Jint</a>, Jung Suk Won and Ryu Jae Hyun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-producers_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-producers-2">[2]</a></sup> On March 3, 2013, Davichi pre-released the music video for the track, "Turtle", which featured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/5dolls" title="5dolls">5dolls</a> member Hyoyoung.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The song quickly climbed real-time charts and placed #1 on South Korea's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Single_Chart" title="Gaon Single Chart">Gaon Single Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On March 18, 2013, the album, and music video for their title track, "Just the Two of Us" were digitally released to the public. Despite being left off the initial digital release, Davichi released the single "Be Warmed (feat. Verbal Jint)", as well as the accompanying music video, on April 1, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The song was met with instant success in South Korea, and even managed to place number one on every single digital real time charting, obtaining the coveted 'all-kill'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The album was physically released to the public the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mystic_Ballad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album is composed of twelve tracks; ten new songs and two instrumentals. The pre-released track list showed a tenth new song featuring Verbal Jint, which was not initially included on the digital release of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Davichi's own member, Minkyung, contributed to the album by writing the lyrics to "Things That Still Come Up in My Memory". Ryu Jae Hyun of Korean R&amp;B group <a href="/wiki/Vibe_(band)" title="Vibe (band)">Vibe</a> contributed to the album through the production of the promotional single "Just the Two of Us", as well as "Nagging".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-producers_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-producers-2">[2]</a></sup> Famous producers Duble Sidekick, who are known for their work with <a href="/wiki/Leessang" title="Leessang">Leessang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baek_Ji-young" title="Baek Ji-young">Baek Ji-young</a> and ZIA, lent their help to the duo and produced the pre-release track "Turtle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-producers_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-producers-2">[2]</a></sup> Legendary producer Cho Young Soo contributed to the album by producing the first track "One Person's Story". Davichi has worked with Cho Young Soo in the past to produce their promotional single "Love and War" from the album <i>Vivid Summer Edition</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-producers_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-producers-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotions">Promotions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mystic_Ballad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Promotions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davichi announced that they would be focusing solely on radio promotions for the first part of their comeback. The members expressed that they want to be able to introduce and explain all the songs on their album to the general public, not just their promotional single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Promotions for "Just the Two of Us" on South Korea's televised music programs began on the March 21, 2013, broadcast of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On March 28, 2013, "Just the Two of Us" achieved the #1 spot on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a>, securing Davichi's fifteenth televised music show win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
