<p><b>Megpoid</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">メグッポイド</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Vocaloid" title="Vocaloid">Vocaloid</a> by <a href="/wiki/Internet_Co.,_Ltd." title="Internet Co., Ltd.">Internet Co., Ltd.</a>. Her voice is sampled by <a href="/wiki/Megumi_Nakajima" title="Megumi Nakajima">Megumi Nakajima</a>. The mascot of the software is called "GUMI".</p>
<p>The name GUMI is the voice provider Megumi Nakajima's nickname from her childhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The software name, "Megpoid", was taken from the provider's name, "Megumi." The second half, the "poid", is short for "like Vocaloid," the full implied name of this product is "Megumi-like Vocaloid."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GUMI was developed by Internet Co., Ltd. using <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Corporation" title="Yamaha Corporation">Yamaha Corporation's</a> <a href="/wiki/Vocaloid_2" title="Vocaloid 2">Vocaloid 2</a> synthesizer software. Her voice was created by taking vocal samples from <a href="/wiki/List_of_Japanese_singers" title="List of Japanese singers">singer</a> <a href="/wiki/Megumi_Nakajima" title="Megumi Nakajima">Megumi Nakajima</a> at a controlled pitch and tone.</p>
<p>On Dec 11th, 2010, the president of INTERNET Co. announced that <a href="/wiki/Gackpoid" title="Gackpoid">Gackpoid</a>, Megpoid, and <a href="/wiki/Lily_(music_software)" title="Lily (music software)">Lily</a> would be distributed in Taiwan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_software">Additional software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Additional software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 21, 2011, a new version of the software, called <i>V3 Megpoid</i>, was released containing a package of four different tones of Gumi's voice for <a href="/wiki/Vocaloid_3" title="Vocaloid 3">VOCALOID3</a>: Adult, Power, Sweet, and Whisper. This was one of the first 4 packages released on the same day for the new engine, the others being <a href="/wiki/Mew_(software)" title="Mew (software)">Mew</a>, <a href="/wiki/SeeU" title="SeeU">SeeU</a> and <a href="/wiki/VY1" title="VY1">VY1v</a>3. This was originally going to be an expansion pack for the Vocaloid 2 version of Gumi under the name of "Megpoid Extend" and was based on the "Append" vocals of Crypton Future Media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On March 16, 2012, a update of the Vocaloid 2 Megpoid was released, called <i>V3 Megpoid - Native</i>. On February 28, 2013, another new version of Gumi, called '"Megpoid English" was released also for the Vocaloid 3 engine.</p>
<p>On November 5, 2015, an update to the V3 Megpoid package, called <i>Megpoid V4</i>, was released for the <a href="/wiki/Vocaloid_4" title="Vocaloid 4">VOCALOID4</a> engine updating the vocals from <i>V3 Megpoid</i> and <i>V3 Megpoid - Native</i> along with adding alternative versions to the vocals including: MellowAdult, PowerFat, NaturalSweet, SoftWhisper, and NativeFat. These vocals made use of the new Vocaloid 4 feature "Cross-Synthesis". Vocaloid 4 Cross Synthesis was also allowed for the Vocaloid 3 version of the Megpoid software, though was restricted only to working with the 5 Vocaloid 3 Japanese vocals. Noboru responded that the English bank for Vocaloid 4 would be updated at a later time but there is currently no schedule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> When asked about what this meant, Noboru replied "this year or next" (2016/2017). In regards to making more than 1 vocal, he noted this may be too difficult.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The voice was also featured in use on the Vocaloid Keyboard prototype.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition, an unrelated Vocaloid product called "Megpoid Talk" was developed which allowed Megpoid speech.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Native" and "English" were released for the <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Vocaloid_Editor" title="Mobile Vocaloid Editor">Mobile Vocaloid Editor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was also to have a "<a href="/wiki/Falsetto" title="Falsetto">Falsetto</a>" vocal. Due issues with balancing the vocal with the normal vocals, it was dropped and a Vocaloid called "<a href="/wiki/Kokone" title="Kokone">Kokone</a>" was developed instead with Falsetto capabilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vol.3 of the <i>VOCALOID-P data series</i> was also dedicated to the Megpoid V3 software.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GUMI initially struggled to build popularity against the Crypton Future Media's Character Vocal series and was considered to be "nothing special" when first released. She was, however, quicker to gain popularity in comparison to Camui Gackpo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 2010, GUMI's popularity was on par with Crypton Future Media's VOCALOIDs and had out sold her predecessor Camui Gackpo, becoming the most popular and well known non-Crypton VOCALOID. In 2011, her usage grew and in a number of weeks she would have even more songs in the top 100 rankings than some of the Crypton Future Media vocalists. In January 2015, a popularity poll was released, confirming GUMI was the second most popular VOCALOID of 2015 on Niconico.</p>
<p>GUMI has so far over 300 known songs and one of her songs titled "モザイクロール <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">Mozaik Role</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>", by DECO*27, is considered one of her most popular songs with almost 7,000,000 views on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nico_Nico_Douga" title="Nico Nico Douga">Nico Nico Douga</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Concerning GUMI's age, during the Extend development, Noboru once tweeted that there was a rough age preferred for GUMI, set around the teenage years. However, no official age was ever given to GUMI in her various designs. When writing about the Extend, it was noted the voice could be considered 'extensions' of the age conceptualized for GUMI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga.2FAnime">Manga/Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Manga/Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GUMI has appeared in several manga based series. The bi-monthly Vocaloid magazine, Comic@loid, has released some titles, and others have not been released yet. The released titles include Mozaik Role, Kiritorisen, Shiryoku Kensa, Setsuna Trip, Ringo Uri no Utakata Shoujo, and Yowamushi Montblanc.</p>
<p>Another manga is "GUMI from Vocaloid" which ran from 2013 to 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This particular manga is not based on a specific song, but rather on GUMI's adventures as a VOCALOID.</p>
<p>Another manga which appeared in 2014, which stars Gumi. This features the fictional birth story of "Gumi", who was a girl who becomes an Android and debuts as an idol. The story focuses on her and a mysterious team called <i>Interface sound Orchestra</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">インタネ団</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. Fellow Vocaloids <a href="/wiki/Gackpoid" title="Gackpoid">Camui Gackpo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lily_(music_software)" title="Lily (music software)">Lily</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cul_(software)" title="Cul (software)">Cul</a>, also feature in the manga. The name of the manga is <i>MeguMegu☆Singer Song Fighter</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">メグメグ☆シンガーソングファイター</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>GUMI stars in an anime television short series called <i>Koi Suru Dessan Ningyō</i>, it is named after a song by sasakure.UK of which the short is based on. Masanori Okamoto is responsible for many aspects of this project including the script and filming of the short. If the short is popular a series will follow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Game">Video Game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Video Game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GUMI starred in her game <i>Megpoid the Music♯</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">グッポイド ザ ミュージック シャープ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. This was a rhythm based game created by <i>ParaPhray</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">パラフレ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a>. Both a standard and limited edition were released on March 28, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>She also made a guest appearance in <a href="/wiki/Hatsune_Miku_and_Future_Stars:_Project_Mirai" title="Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai">Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Competition">Competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megpoid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Competition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2012, a song contest was announced for GUMI, which was hosted by CreoFUGA. Producers entering the contest could use any <i>VOCALOID2</i> or <i>VOCALOID3</i> GUMI voicebank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>A contest was held by VocaloTracks in 2013 to create an original song using any of Gumi's voicebank. They were able to sell their songs under a "professional label".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another song contest was run, linking with the release of her English vocal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
