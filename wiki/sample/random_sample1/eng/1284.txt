<p>"<b>Misery</b>" is a song performed by English rock band <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a> on their album <i><a href="/wiki/Please_Please_Me" title="Please Please Me">Please Please Me</a></i>. It was co-written by <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a>. According to Lennon, "It was kind of a John song more than a Paul song, but it was written together."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESheff2000169_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESheff2000169-1">[1]</a></sup> McCartney was to say: "I don't think either one of us dominated on that one, it was just a hacking job."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMiles199794_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMiles199794-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A 1963 single by <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Lynch" title="Kenny Lynch">Kenny Lynch</a> made "Misery" the first Beatles' song to be <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">covered</a> by another artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEUnterberger2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEUnterberger2009-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Misery_(Beatles_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 1963, <a href="/wiki/Helen_Shapiro" title="Helen Shapiro">Helen Shapiro</a> was Britain's most successful female singer (having first achieved chart success two years earlier at the age of 14)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHarry1992598_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarry1992598-4">[4]</a></sup> and The Beatles were fifth on the bill as part of her nationwide tour of the United Kingdom. Her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artist_and_repertoire" title="Artist and repertoire">artist and repertoire</a> manager, <a href="/wiki/Norrie_Paramor" title="Norrie Paramor">Norrie Paramor</a>, was looking for new material for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_and_western" title="Country and western">country and western</a> album she planned to record in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a> and suggested that the Beatles compose a song especially for her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHarry1992599_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarry1992599-5">[5]</a></sup> "Misery" was started backstage before The Beatles' performance at the King's Hall, Stoke-on-Trent, on 26 January 1963, and later completed at Paul McCartney's <a href="/wiki/20_Forthlin_Road" title="20 Forthlin Road">Forthlin Road</a> home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMiles199794_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMiles199794-2">[2]</a></sup> At the time, McCartney commented: "We've called it 'Misery', but it isn't as slow as it sounds, it moves along at quite a pace, and we think Helen will make a pretty good job of it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadman200051_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadman200051-6">[6]</a></sup> But Paramor considered it unsuitable,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEUnterberger2009_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEUnterberger2009-3">[3]</a></sup> and so British singer and entertainer <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Lynch" title="Kenny Lynch">Kenny Lynch</a>, who was on the same tour, recorded it instead (<a href="/wiki/HMV" title="HMV">HMV</a> Pop 1136<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGlobal_Dog_Productions2009_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGlobal_Dog_Productions2009-7">[7]</a></sup>), thus becoming the first artist to cover a <a href="/wiki/Lennon%E2%80%93McCartney" title="Lennon–McCartney">Lennon–McCartney</a> composition<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEUnterberger2009_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEUnterberger2009-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMiles199794_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMiles199794-2">[2]</a></sup> although he failed to enter the charts with it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHarry1992468_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarry1992468-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1973, Lynch appeared in the cover photograph for McCartney's album, <i><a href="/wiki/Band_on_the_Run" title="Band on the Run">Band on the Run</a></i>.</p>
<p>When the Beatles needed original material for their <i><a href="/wiki/Please_Please_Me" title="Please Please Me">Please Please Me</a></i> LP they recorded it themselves, giving its treatment, according to writer <a href="/wiki/Ian_MacDonald" title="Ian MacDonald">Ian MacDonald</a>, "a droll portrait of adolescent self-pity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMacDonald200570.E2.80.9371_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMacDonald200570.E2.80.9371-9">[9]</a></sup> It was credited to McCartney and Lennon in that order, as were all other Lennon &amp; McCartney originals on the <i>Please Please Me</i> album. The songwriting credit was changed to what would become the more familiar "<a href="/wiki/Lennon%E2%80%93McCartney" title="Lennon–McCartney">Lennon–McCartney</a>" for their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/With_the_Beatles" title="With the Beatles">With the Beatles</a></i>. McCartney: "It was our first stab at a ballad and had a little spoken preface. It was co-written. I don't think either of us dominated on that one, it was just a job, you could have called us hacks, hacking out a song for someone." (Barry Miles. Paul McCartney: Many Years From Now).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-BB-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Beatles recorded "Misery" on 11 February 1963 (marathon session) in 11 takes. <a href="/wiki/Norman_Smith_(record_producer)" title="Norman Smith (record producer)">Norman Smith</a> was the engineer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-BB-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Normal studio <a href="/wiki/Multitrack_recording" title="Multitrack recording">multi-track</a> tape speed at the time was 15 ips (inches per second), but “Misery” was recorded at 30 ips, as George Martin intended to personally add piano at a later date, and preferred playing this at half-tempo an octave below.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELewisohn198824_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELewisohn198824-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Helen Shapiro had starred in her own film called <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Trad,_Dad!" title="It's Trad, Dad!">It's Trad, Dad!</a></i> (released UK 16 April 1962) directed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Lester" title="Richard Lester">Richard Lester</a>, who later directed The Beatles' films <i><a href="/wiki/A_Hard_Day%27s_Night_(film)" title="A Hard Day's Night (film)">A Hard Day's Night</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Help!_(film)" title="Help! (film)">Help!</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHarry1992598_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarry1992598-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
