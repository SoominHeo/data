<p><i><b>Mr. A–Z</b></i> is the second studio album by the American singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jason_Mraz" title="Jason Mraz">Jason Mraz</a>, released on July 26, 2005. It is the studio follow-up to his first studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Waiting_for_My_Rocket_to_Come" title="Waiting for My Rocket to Come">Waiting for My Rocket to Come</a></i>, released in 2002. Even with mixed reviews, it was a moderate commercial success and debuted at #5 on <i>Billboard</i>'s Top 200 albums chart. It was recognized for mixing many different genres of music together.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>It was nominated by the Recording Academy for best engineered album, and the producer <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lillywhite" title="Steve Lillywhite">Steve Lillywhite</a>, who previously worked with <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Dave_Matthews_Band" title="Dave Matthews Band">Dave Matthews Band</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, won for Producer of the Year, but not for his work on <i>Mr. A–Z</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The name of the album is a play on the artist's surname, "Mraz".</p>
<p>An opera solo performed by Mraz himself can be found on the track "Mr. Curiosity". In March 2010, this song charted in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> after it had been performed by <a href="/wiki/Lena_Meyer-Landrut" title="Lena Meyer-Landrut">Lena Meyer-Landrut</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Talent_show" title="Talent show">talent show</a> contest <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unser_Star_f%C3%BCr_Oslo" title="Unser Star für Oslo">Unser Star für Oslo</a></i> (<i>Our Star for Oslo</i>), reaching a peak position of #44.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
