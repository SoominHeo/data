<p>The <b>N-I</b> or <b>N-1</b> was a derivative of the American <a href="/wiki/Delta_(rocket_family)" title="Delta (rocket family)">Delta</a> rocket, produced under licence in Japan. It used a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thor_(rocket)" title="Thor (rocket)">Thor-ELT</a> first stage, a <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Heavy_Industries" title="Mitsubishi Heavy Industries">Mitsubishi Heavy Industries</a>-designed <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/LE-3" title="ja:LE-3">LE-3</a> engine was used as a second stage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MHInagoya_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MHInagoya-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castor_(rocket)" title="Castor (rocket)">Castor</a> SRMs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAXA-archive_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-JAXA-archive-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSP_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GSP-6">[6]</a></sup> Seven were launched between 1975 and 1982, before it was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/N-II_(rocket)" title="N-II (rocket)">N-II</a>. Six of the seven launches were successful, however on the fifth flight, there was recontact between the satellite and the third stage, which caused the satellite to fail.</p>
<p>On 29 February 1976, the second N-I conducted the only orbital launch to occur on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leap_day" title="Leap day">leap day</a> (as of year 2008).</p>
