<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of NGC objects (1001–2000)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>This is a <b>list of NGC objects 1001–2000</b> from the <a href="/wiki/New_General_Catalogue" title="New General Catalogue">New General Catalogue</a> (NGC). The <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_catalog" title="Astronomical catalog">astronomical catalogue</a> is composed mainly of <a href="/wiki/Star_cluster" title="Star cluster">star clusters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebula" title="Nebula">nebulae</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galaxies" title="Galaxies">galaxies</a>. Other objects in the catalogue can be found in the other subpages of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_NGC_objects" title="List of NGC objects">list of NGC objects</a>.</p>
<p>The constellation information in these tables is taken from <i>The Complete New General Catalogue and Index Catalogue of Nebulae and Star Clusters by J. L. E. Dreyer</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sinnott1988_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sinnott1988-1">[1]</a></sup> which was accessed using the "VizieR Service".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Galaxy types are identified using the <i>NASA/IPAC Extragalactic Database</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The other data of these tables are from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SIMBAD_Astronomical_Database" title="SIMBAD Astronomical Database">SIMBAD Astronomical Database</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> unless otherwise stated.</p>
