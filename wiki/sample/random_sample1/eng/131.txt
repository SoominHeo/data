<p><b>Elections to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a></b> for the <a href="/wiki/29th_United_States_Congress" title="29th United States Congress">29th Congress</a> were held at different dates in the various states from July 1, 1844 (<a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>) to November 4, 1845 (<a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>). All 227 members of the House of Representatives elected<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NH_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NH-3">[Note 3]</a></sup> took their seats when Congress convened December 1, 1845. The elections mostly coincided with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1844" title="United States presidential election, 1844">1844 presidential election</a>, won by <a href="/wiki/Dark_horse" title="Dark horse">dark horse</a> <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> candidate <a href="/wiki/James_K._Polk" title="James K. Polk">James K. Polk</a>, who won on a campaign advocating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Territorial_changes_of_the_United_States" title="Territorial changes of the United States">territorial expansion</a>. The new states of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a> were added during this Congress. <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> had actually been admitted on the last day of the 28th Congress, but was not represented until the 29th. Iowa and Texas both elected their first representatives in 1846.</p>
<p>Despite Polk's victory, the Democrats had a net loss of six Representatives. They still retained a large majority of 142-79 over their major rivals, the <a href="/wiki/Whig_Party_(United_States)" title="Whig Party (United States)">Whigs</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Know_Nothing" title="Know Nothing">American Party</a>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Nativism_(politics)" title="Nativism (politics)">nativist</a> "Know Nothing" movement characterized by <a href="/wiki/Opposition_to_immigration" title="Opposition to immigration">opposition to immigration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anti-Catholicism" title="Anti-Catholicism">anti-Catholicism</a>, gained six seats, its first in Congress. The fragmentation of votes by this new party, combined with a generally negative political environment cause by dislike of outgoing President <a href="/wiki/John_Tyler" title="John Tyler">John Tyler</a>, contributed to the slight Democratic loses.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Election_summaries">Election summaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections,_1844&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Election summaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Five seats were added for the new States of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[1]</a></sup> (1), <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[2]</a></sup> (2), and <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[3]</a></sup> (2). Florida was represented for the entirety of the 29th Congress, while Texas was unrepresented for part of the 1st session and Iowa for all of the 1st session.</p>

A special election was held which was, initially, won by <a href="/wiki/Edward_Carrington_Cabell" title="Edward Carrington Cabell">Edward C. Cabell</a> (W).
