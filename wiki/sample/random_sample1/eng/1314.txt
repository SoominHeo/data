<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">NGC 85</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>NGC 85</b> is an interacting <a href="/wiki/Spiral_galaxy" title="Spiral galaxy">spiral</a> or <a href="/wiki/Lenticular_galaxy" title="Lenticular galaxy">lenticular galaxy</a> estimated to be from 245 to 275 million light-years away in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_(constellation)" title="Andromeda (constellation)">Andromeda</a>. It was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Copeland" title="Ralph Copeland">Ralph Copeland</a> in 1873 and its apparent magnitude is 15.7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The galaxy appears to be interacting with the companion spiral IC 1546.</p>
