<p><b>NGC 925</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Barred_spiral_galaxy" title="Barred spiral galaxy">barred spiral galaxy</a> located about 30<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Silbermann1996_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Silbermann1996-4">[4]</a></sup> million <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> away in the constellation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triangulum_(constellation)" title="Triangulum (constellation)">Triangulum</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_morphological_classification" title="Galaxy morphological classification">morphological classification</a> of this galaxy is SB(s)d,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin1994_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Martin1994-3">[3]</a></sup> indicating that it has a <a href="/wiki/Barred_spiral_galaxy#The_bars" title="Barred spiral galaxy">bar structure</a> and loosely wound spiral arms with no ring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Buta2007_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Buta2007-6">[6]</a></sup> The spiral arm to the south is stronger than the northern arm, with the latter appearing <a href="/wiki/Flocculent_spiral_galaxy" title="Flocculent spiral galaxy">flocculent</a> and less coherent. The bar is offset from the center of the galaxy and is the site of star formation all along its length. Both of these morphological traits—a dominant spiral arm and the offset bar—are typically characteristics of a <a href="/wiki/Magellanic_spiral" title="Magellanic spiral">Magellanic spiral</a> galaxy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pisano2000_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pisano2000-7">[7]</a></sup> The galaxy is inclined at an angle of 55° to the line of sight along a <a href="/wiki/Position_angle" title="Position angle">position angle</a> of 102°.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin1994_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Martin1994-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The galaxy is a member of the <a href="/wiki/NGC_1023_Group" title="NGC 1023 Group">NGC 1023 Group</a>, a nearby, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gravitationally_bound" title="Gravitationally bound">gravitationally-bound</a> <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_groups_and_clusters" title="Galaxy groups and clusters">group of galaxies</a> associated with <a href="/wiki/NGC_1023" title="NGC 1023">NGC 1023</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tully1980_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tully1980-5">[5]</a></sup> However, the nearest member lies at least 650,000 ly (200,000 pc) distant from NGC 925. There is a 10 million <a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass">solar mass</a> (<a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass"><i>M</i><sub>☉</sub></a>) cloud of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neutral_hydrogen" title="Neutral hydrogen">neutral hydrogen</a> attached to NGC 925 by a streamer. It is uncertain whether this is a satellite <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_galaxy" title="Dwarf galaxy">dwarf galaxy</a>, the remnant of a past <a href="/wiki/Interacting_galaxy" title="Interacting galaxy">tidal interaction</a>, or a cloud of primordial gas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pisano2007_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pisano2007-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
