<p><b>NGC 93</b> is an interacting <a href="/wiki/Spiral_galaxy" title="Spiral galaxy">spiral galaxy</a> estimated to be about 260 million light-years away in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_(constellation)" title="Andromeda (constellation)">Andromeda</a>. It was discovered by R. J. Mitchell in 1854.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cseligman_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-cseligman-3">[3]</a></sup> The galaxy is currently interacting with <a href="/wiki/NGC_90" title="NGC 90">NGC 90</a> and has some signs of interacting with it.</p>
<p>NGC 93 and NGC 90 form the interacting galaxy pair <a href="/wiki/Atlas_of_Peculiar_Galaxies#Spiral_galaxies_with_small_high_surface_brightness_companions" title="Atlas of Peculiar Galaxies">Arp 65</a>.</p>
