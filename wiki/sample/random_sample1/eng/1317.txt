<p><b>NHK Radio 1</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">NHKラジオ第1放送</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>NHK Rajio Dai-ichi Hōsō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese radio station operated by the <a href="/wiki/Public_broadcasting" title="Public broadcasting">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Broadcasting" title="Broadcasting">broadcaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a>. Its programming output, which consists of news, current affairs, and information programming is broadly similar to <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBC_Radio_One" title="CBC Radio One">CBC Radio One</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Deutschlandfunk" title="Deutschlandfunk">Deutschlandfunk</a>. NHK Radio 1 is available mainly on AM. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Callsign" title="Callsign">callsign</a> is JOAK in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. During World War 2 it often broadcast official announcements.</p>
