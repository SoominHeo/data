<p><b>NKOTBSB</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> consisting of the members of American <a href="/wiki/Boy_band" title="Boy band">boy bands</a> <a href="/wiki/New_Kids_on_the_Block" title="New Kids on the Block">New Kids on the Block</a> and <a href="/wiki/Backstreet_Boys" title="Backstreet Boys">Backstreet Boys</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Howie_Dorough" title="Howie Dorough">Howie Dorough</a> of the Backstreet Boys came up with the name,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which is a combination of established <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Initialism" title="Initialism">initialisms</a> of each groups' names, <b>NKOTB</b> and <b>BSB</b>. Together they have released one compilation album, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eponymous" title="Eponymous">eponymous</a> <i><a href="/wiki/NKOTBSB_(album)" title="NKOTBSB (album)">NKOTBSB</a></i> (2011), one single, and toured in North America, Europe, Australia, and Asia in 2011 and 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NKOTBSB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In summer 2010, the Backstreet Boys joined New Kids on the Block on stage at the <a href="/wiki/Radio_City_Music_Hall" title="Radio City Music Hall">Radio City Music Hall</a> in New York at their invitation<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and performed "<a href="/wiki/I_Want_It_That_Way" title="I Want It That Way">I Want It That Way</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ryan_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ryan-3">[3]</a></sup> The show was a part of NKOTB's <a href="/wiki/New_Kids_on_the_Block:_Live" title="New Kids on the Block: Live">Casi-NO Tour</a>. Since the performance, the media began to circulate rumors of the two uniting for a tour in the summer of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 8, 2010, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Littrell" title="Brian Littrell">Brian Littrell</a>, a member of the Backstreet Boys, gave the tour a 78% chance of happening. He also stated that he and <a href="/wiki/Donnie_Wahlberg" title="Donnie Wahlberg">Donnie Wahlberg</a> had been working on some songs together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tour was officially announced to begin in the summer of 2011 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/On_Air_with_Ryan_Seacrest_(radio)" title="On Air with Ryan Seacrest (radio)">On Air with Ryan Seacrest</a> on November 8, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ryan_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ryan-3">[3]</a></sup> During the interview, it was mentioned that the groups were recording a single to be released later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ryan_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-ryan-3">[3]</a></sup> To promote the tour further, the groups conducted a live Q&amp;A session on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ustream.tv" title="Ustream.tv">Ustream</a> on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The newly formed supergroup first performed together at the closing of <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2010" title="American Music Awards of 2010">38th Annual American Music Awards</a> on November 21, 2010, to give the public a taste of what they would see on the tour,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and again on 2011 New Year's on ABC's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dick_Clark%27s_New_Year%27s_Rockin%27_Eve_with_Ryan_Seacrest" title="Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve with Ryan Seacrest">Dick Clark/Ryan Seacrest show</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 14, 2011, the group announced that they would be releasing a <a href="/wiki/NKOTBSB_(album)" title="NKOTBSB (album)">joint compilation album</a> on May 24, 2011. The album featured five tracks from each group and some new recordings by the combined supergroup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The single, titled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don%27t_Turn_Out_The_Lights" title="Don't Turn Out The Lights">Don't Turn Out The Lights</a> was premiered on April 5, 2011 on Ryan Seacrest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/NKOTBSB_Tour" title="NKOTBSB Tour">NKOTBSB Tour</a> started at the <a href="/wiki/Allstate_Arena" title="Allstate Arena">Allstate Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rosemont,_Illinois" title="Rosemont, Illinois">Rosemont, Illinois</a> on May 25, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On June 3, 2011, while in the middle of the tour, they went to <a href="/wiki/Rockefeller_Center" title="Rockefeller Center">Rockefeller Center</a> to perform together at the <i><a href="/wiki/Today_(U.S._TV_program)" title="Today (U.S. TV program)">The Today Show</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tour ended on June 3, 2012 in <a href="/wiki/Pasay" title="Pasay">Pasay</a>, Philippines, and they performed one last time on August 18, 2012 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hershey,_PA" title="Hershey, PA">Hershey, PA</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Mixtape_Festival" title="Summer Mixtape Festival">Summer Mixtape Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The performance was also their only North American appearance in 2012.</p>
<p>During a show in London on April 29, 2012, while announcing original member <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Richardson_(musician)" title="Kevin Richardson (musician)">Kevin Richardson</a>'s return to the Backstreet Boys, Littrell hinted that they might play some shows with all ten members in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In August 2012, members of New Kids on the Block also stated during Mixtape Festival that while it was their last performance together for quite some time, they might get back together down the line, and they hope there will be many more shows with the Backstreet Boys in the future. They also said that NKOTBSB is like another group altogether.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
