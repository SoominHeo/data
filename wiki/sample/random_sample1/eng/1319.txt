<p><b>Nogometni Klub Istra 1961</b> (English: Istra 1961 Football Club), commonly referred to as <b>NK Istra 1961</b> or simply <b>Istra 1961</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Pula" title="Pula">Pula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, currently playing in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prva_HNL" title="Prva HNL">Croatian First Division</a>.</p>
<p>The football club <i>NK Uljanik</i> from which the club originates was founded in 1948. In 1961, it joined with another local club, <i>NK Pula</i>, to form <i><a href="/wiki/NK_Istra" title="NK Istra">NK Istra</a></i>. For this reason, 1961 is occasionally considered as the club's founding year. The greatest club success was in the 2002–03 season when they finished runners-up in the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Football_Cup" title="Croatian Football Cup">Croatian Cup</a>, losing to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hajduk_Split" title="Hajduk Split">Hajduk Split</a> 4–1 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aggregate_score" title="Aggregate score">aggregate</a>. In the 2004–05 season, the club changed its name into <i>NK Pula 1856</i>, because the year 1856 was the year that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Empire" title="Austro-Hungarian Empire">Austro-Hungarian Empire</a> made Pula the port of its arsenal and the shipyard <a href="/wiki/Uljanik" title="Uljanik">Uljanik</a> was opened. Also in the same year, in competed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prva_HNL" title="Prva HNL">Prva Liga</a> for the first time. In 2005, it was renamed again to <i>NK Pula Staro Češko</i> due to a sponsorship contract with the brewery Daruvarska pivovara (Staro Češko is a beer brand). Just one year later, the name was once again changed, this time to <i>NK Pula</i>. Again, just a year later in summer 2007, the name was changed for the fifth time in as many years, to <i>NK Istra 1961</i>. The change of name came after an ultimatum from the local fans <i>Demons</i> who said the club would have their support only if the club changed its name and club colours to the traditional city colours (yellow and green).</p>
<p>Its crest features the city's Roman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arena_(colosseum)" title="Arena (colosseum)">Arena</a> in Pula, as well as the old team crest.</p>
<p>Pula play their home matches at the recently renovated <a href="/wiki/Stadion_Aldo_Drosina" title="Stadion Aldo Drosina">Stadion Aldo Drosina</a>, which has a capacity of approximately 10,000 spectators.</p>
