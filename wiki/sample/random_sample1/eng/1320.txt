<p><b>NMB48</b> (read "N.M.B. Forty-eight") is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_idol" title="Japanese idol">Japanese idol</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> that debuted in 2011 as the second sister group to <a href="/wiki/AKB48" title="AKB48">AKB48</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Yasushi_Akimoto" title="Yasushi Akimoto">Yasushi Akimoto</a>. The group performs at the NMB48 Theater which is located in the basement of the Yes-Namba Building in <a href="/wiki/Namba" title="Namba">Namba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>. The name "NMB" comes from "<a href="/wiki/Namba" title="Namba">Namba</a>" in the Minami district in Osaka.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NMB48&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>2010</dt>
</dl>
<p>On 10 July 2010, <a href="/wiki/AKB48" title="AKB48">AKB48</a> first announced that they would be forming a second sister national group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20101204_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20101204-1">[1]</a></sup> based in Namba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie2010710_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-natalie2010710-2">[2]</a></sup> NMB48 officially became active on 9 October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20101204_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20101204-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Yasushi_Akimoto" title="Yasushi Akimoto">Yasushi Akimoto</a> announced that the group would have 26 trainees for the first generation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie2010109_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-natalie2010109-3">[3]</a></sup> These members made their first appearance at the <i>AKB48 Tokyo Autumn Festival</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie2010109_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-natalie2010109-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>2011</dt>
</dl>
<p>NMB48 made their debut performance at their theater on New Year's Day 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20101204_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20101204-1">[1]</a></sup> They released their debut single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zetsumetsu_Kurokami_Sh%C5%8Djo" title="Zetsumetsu Kurokami Shōjo">Zetsumetsu Kurokami Shōjo</a>", on 20 July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110727_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110727-4">[4]</a></sup> In its debut week, the single sold a total of about 218,000 copies, making it the bestselling single on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> weekly charts for the week of 20–27 July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110727_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110727-4">[4]</a></sup> This makes NMB48 the second girl group after <a href="/wiki/Passpo" title="Passpo">Passpo</a> to reach number 1 with a debut single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110727_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110727-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>2012</dt>
</dl>
<p>On the first day of AKB48's Tokyo Dome Concert held on August 24, 2012, it was announced that AKB48 Team A member Yui Yokoyama would hold a concurrent Position in NMB48. <a href="/wiki/Miyuki_Watanabe" title="Miyuki Watanabe">Miyuki Watanabe</a> of Team N would hold a concurrent position in AKB48 Team B. Also, <a href="/wiki/Riho_Kotani" title="Riho Kotani">Riho Kotani</a> of Team N will hold a concurrent position in AKB48, Team A.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AKB48_Official_Blog_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AKB48_Official_Blog-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 3, Eriko Jo, a member of Team M, announced that she would be leaving the group during the performance at the theater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Her "graduation" performance was on September 28 at the NMB48 Theater. Following Jo's graduation, two members from Team M announced their resignation in early October. On October 9, three trainees were promoted to Team M to replace the members who had left. Fuuko Yagura became the new "center" member of Team M.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 10, sixteen third-generation trainees formed Team BII.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On December 19, <a href="/wiki/Yui_Yokoyama" title="Yui Yokoyama">Yui Yokoyama</a> was promoted to Team N.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>2013</dt>
</dl>
<p>On April 18, 2013, first-generation member Nana Yamada was transferred from Team N to Team M, and trainee members Naruma Koga and Aika Nishimura were promoted to Team N.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At the AKB48 Group Concert in the Nippon Budokan on April 28, 2013, it was announced that Yui Yokoyama's concurrent position in the group would end, and that AKB48 Member <a href="/wiki/Miori_Ichikawa" title="Miori Ichikawa">Miori Ichikawa</a> would have a concurrent position in Team N.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>2014</dt>
</dl>
<p>In the AKB48 Group <i>Daisokaku Matsuri</i> held on February 24, 2014, major changes occurred to NMB48. Sayaka Yamamoto became a concurrent member of Team K. Miyuki Watanabe became a concurrent member of Team S, and was transferred from Team N to Team BII. Mayu Ogasawara was transferred completely to Team B, Nana Yamada became a concurrent member of Team KII.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
