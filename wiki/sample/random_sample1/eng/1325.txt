<p><b>NSB El 18</b> is a class of 22 <a href="/wiki/Electric_locomotive" title="Electric locomotive">electric</a> <a href="/wiki/Locomotive" title="Locomotive">locomotives</a> built by <a href="/wiki/Adtranz" title="Adtranz">Adtranz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Locomotive_and_Machine_Works" title="Swiss Locomotive and Machine Works">Swiss Locomotive and Machine Works</a> (SLM) for the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_State_Railways" title="Norwegian State Railways">Norwegian State Railways</a> (NSB). The class is a modification of the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Federal_Railways" title="Swiss Federal Railways">Swiss Federal Railways</a> <a href="/wiki/SBB-CFF-FFS_Re_460" title="SBB-CFF-FFS Re 460">Re 460</a> locomotive and built at <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%B8mmens_V%C3%A6rksted" title="Strømmens Værksted">Adtranz Strømmen</a> in 1996 and 1997. The class remains the only mainline electric locomotive used by NSB, and is predominantly used on some intercity services and all night trains on the <a href="/wiki/Bergen_Line" title="Bergen Line">Bergen Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dovre_Line" title="Dovre Line">Dovre Line</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C3%B8rland_Line" title="Sørland Line">Sørland Line</a>, as well as some regional trains.</p>
<p>The locomotives are 18.5 metres (61 ft) long and weigh 83 tonnes (82 long tons; 91 short tons). They have <a href="/wiki/Three-phase" title="Three-phase">three-phase</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asynchronous_motor" title="Asynchronous motor">asynchronous motors</a> with a maximum power output of 5,880 kilowatts (7,890 hp), giving a <a href="/wiki/Tractive_effort" title="Tractive effort">tractive effort</a> of 275 kilonewtons (62,000 lb<sub>f</sub>) and a maximum speed of 200 km/h (120 mph). They have a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bo%27Bo%27" title="Bo'Bo'">Bo'Bo'</a> wheel arrangement and <a href="/wiki/Regenerative_brake" title="Regenerative brake">regenerative brakes</a>. The exterior was designed by <a href="/wiki/Pininfarina" title="Pininfarina">Pininfarina</a> and the cabs have <a href="/wiki/Pressurization" title="Pressurization">pressurization</a>. The units are numbered 2241 through 2262.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NSB_El_18&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:El_18.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="806" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/El_18.jpg/220px-El_18.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/El_18.jpg/330px-El_18.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/El_18.jpg/440px-El_18.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:El_18.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
El 18 hauling a freight train on the <a href="/wiki/%C3%98stfold_Line" title="Østfold Line">Østfold Line</a> for <a href="/wiki/CargoNet" title="CargoNet">CargoNet</a></div>

<p>During the early 1990s, NSB was in need of new electric haulage for their passenger trains, as both classes <a href="/wiki/NSB_El_11" title="NSB El 11">El 11</a> and <a href="/wiki/NSB_El_13" title="NSB El 13">El 13</a> were in need of replacement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/NSB_El_17" title="NSB El 17">El 17</a>, the latest purchase, had proved unreliable, and NSB wanted to remove them from mainline service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1993, Re 460 and <a href="/wiki/EuroSprinter" title="EuroSprinter">EuroSprinter</a> locomotives were tested in Norway, with the Re 460 being tested from 28 August through 8 October. NSB was satisfied with both units, and stated that it would be possible to increase the train weight on the intercity services from 700 to 800 t (690 to 790 long tons; 770 to 880 short tons). During the first half of 1994, NSB leased two Re 460s to have sufficient locomotives for operation during the <a href="/wiki/1994_Winter_Olympics" title="1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Winter Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps80_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ps80-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a124_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-a124-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>When the deadline for bids for the units was reached on 8 May 1994, five bids had been received. <a href="/wiki/Alstom" title="Alstom">GEC Alsthom</a> offered a modification of the French <a href="/wiki/SNCF_Class_BB_36000" title="SNCF Class BB 36000">SNCF Class BB 36000</a> and <a href="/wiki/AEG" title="AEG">AEG</a> offered a variation of the German prototype 12X. <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a> offered two models, the EuroSprinter and an adaptation of the Austrian <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%96BB_Class_1014&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ÖBB Class 1014 (page does not exist)">ÖBB Class 1014</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps80_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ps80-4">[4]</a></sup> Siemens' proposal for the former was branded Dovresprinter and was a cooperation between <a href="/wiki/Kv%C3%A6rner" title="Kværner">Kværner</a> and NSB's workshop at Sundland in <a href="/wiki/Drammen" title="Drammen">Drammen</a>. Siemens would deliver the overall design and electrical components, Kværner would build the mechanical components and the assembly would occur in Drammen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alternativ_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-alternativ-6">[6]</a></sup> The final offer was from <a href="/wiki/ABB_Group" title="ABB Group">Asea Brown Boveri</a> (ABB, which by delivery would merge to become Adtranz) and SLM for "Lok 2000", a modification of the Swiss Re 460.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps80_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-ps80-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NSB_El_18_and_B7_train_near_Domb%C3%A5s_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2743" data-file-width="4443" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/NSB_El_18_and_B7_train_near_Domb%C3%A5s_2.jpg/220px-NSB_El_18_and_B7_train_near_Domb%C3%A5s_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/NSB_El_18_and_B7_train_near_Domb%C3%A5s_2.jpg/330px-NSB_El_18_and_B7_train_near_Domb%C3%A5s_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/NSB_El_18_and_B7_train_near_Domb%C3%A5s_2.jpg/440px-NSB_El_18_and_B7_train_near_Domb%C3%A5s_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

El 18-hauled B7 train near <a href="/wiki/Domb%C3%A5s_Station" title="Dombås Station">Dombås</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Dovre_Line" title="Dovre Line">Dovre Line</a></div>

</div>
<p>Prior to the final negotiations, union representatives for the train drivers stated that Lok 2000 was their preference, and that NSB could expect a dispute if they chose a different model. The representatives stated that they were "tired of experimenting with Norwegian solutions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leven_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-leven-7">[7]</a></sup> Another important aspect for NSB was that as much of the production as possible take place in Norway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alternativ_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-alternativ-6">[6]</a></sup> The final negotiations were made with ABB/SLM and AEG and on 2 September, and NSB approved the agreement with ABB/SLM for a purchase of 22 units. The contract was signed on 27 September,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps80_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-ps80-4">[4]</a></sup> and the 22 units cost approximately 700 million <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_krone" title="Norwegian krone">Norwegian krone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leven_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-leven-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>NSB considered ordering the units with support for both the Norwegian and Swedish <a href="/wiki/15_kV_AC_railway_electrification" title="15 kV AC railway electrification">15 kV <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">16<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> Hz AC system</a>, and the Danish <a href="/wiki/25_kV_AC_railway_electrification" title="25 kV AC railway electrification">25 kV 50 Hz AC system</a>. This would have allowed the trains to operate directly to Denmark via the <a href="/wiki/%C3%98resund_Bridge" title="Øresund Bridge">Øresund Bridge</a>, which was then under construction. The <a href="/wiki/Multi-system_(rail)" title="Multi-system (rail)">dual-voltage system</a> was dropped during the procurement process, but NSB stated that if they needed such units, compatibility could be provided in future orders of the class. The units were built by Adtranz Strømmen at <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%B8mmen" title="Strømmen">Strømmen</a> outside <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>, and delivered between 3 September 1996 and 12 June 1997. The units are numbered 2241 through 2262.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a124_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-a124-5">[5]</a></sup> When entering service, the locomotives replaced NSB's oldest units, El 13, which were then retired. This reduced NSB's average locomotive age from 31 to <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">18<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> years at the time of the end of the delivery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>During 1997, there were five incidents where NSB's <a href="/wiki/Nordic_Mobile_Telephone" title="Nordic Mobile Telephone">Nordic Mobile Telephone</a> equipment interfered with the locomotive's electronics, causing the <a href="/wiki/Emergency_brake_(train)" title="Emergency brake (train)">emergency brakes</a> to activate. This caused a temporary halt until the motorman had unlocked the brakes. The problem was fixed by moving NSB's mobile senders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The units were taken into use on the Bergen Line from 5 January 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Later they entered into use on the Dovre and Sørland Lines,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and then on regional trains around Oslo, such as the Vestfold Line. In August 1998, NSB stated that El 18 used more power than some of the <a href="/wiki/Electrical_substation" title="Electrical substation">substation</a> transformers along the line could handle, particularly along the Vestfold Line. Part of the problem was caused by a mechanism in the locomotives whereby the motor was turned off if the <a href="/wiki/Locomotive_wheelslip" title="Locomotive wheelslip">wheels spin</a>. The result was that the full power output of the El 18 along parts of the railway network could not be utilized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trobbel_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-trobbel-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">Specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NSB_El_18&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The locomotives have a maximum power output of 5,880 kW (7,890 hp), and are capable of a continual power output of 5,400 kW (7,200 hp). This gives a maximum speed of 200 km/h (120 mph) and a tractive effort of 275 kN (62,000 lb<sub>f</sub>). The locomotive is fed <a href="/wiki/15_kV_AC_railway_electrification" title="15 kV AC railway electrification">15 kV <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">16<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> Hz AC</a> power from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pantograph_(rail)" title="Pantograph (rail)">pantograph</a>. This is then converted to <a href="/wiki/Direct_current" title="Direct current">direct current</a> before being converted to <a href="/wiki/Three-phase" title="Three-phase">three-phase</a> electricity through one of three <a href="/wiki/Gate_turn-off_thyristor" title="Gate turn-off thyristor">gate turn-off thyristors</a>. Each bogie has three <a href="/wiki/Rectifier" title="Rectifier">rectifiers</a>, each connected to a <a href="/wiki/Transformer" title="Transformer">transformer</a> that is again connected to two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inverter_(electrical)" title="Inverter (electrical)">inverters</a>. The motors are three-phase <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asynchronous_motor" title="Asynchronous motor">asynchronous motors</a> located in the bogie frame and equipped with <a href="/wiki/Regenerative_brake" title="Regenerative brake">regenerative brakes</a>. There is also an auxiliary three-phase power supply which powers the compressor, pumps, ventilators and other auxiliary equipment, operated by four separate inverters. The controller is a 16-bit microprocessor that communicates using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optical_fibre_cable" title="Optical fibre cable">optical fibre cables</a>. The rectifier, auxiliary rectifiers, controllers and the error and diagnostic system is of the same type as used on the <a href="/wiki/NSB_Class_70" title="NSB Class 70">NSB Class 70</a> multiple units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps80_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-ps80-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>Each unit weighs 83 t (82 long tons; 91 short tons). The body is 18,500 millimeters (60 ft 8 in) long, 3,000 mm (9 ft 10 in) wide and 4,322 mm (14 ft 2.2 in) tall. The center distance between the bogies is 11,000 mm (36 ft 1 in) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wheel_base" title="Wheel base">center wheel distance</a> in the bogies is 2,800 mm (9 ft 2 in). The wheel diameter is 1,125 mm (3 ft 8.3 in)—this is 25 mm (0.98 in) larger than the Re 460.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The El 18 has a Knorr HSM mechanical braking system, but unlike the Swiss versions does not have a <a href="/wiki/Railway_brake" title="Railway brake">rail brake</a>. The design of the locomotive was by the Italian company <a href="/wiki/Pininfarina" title="Pininfarina">Pininfarina</a>. The machine room is designed with a center aisle, the driver's cabs have pressurization applied to avoid air pressure dropping when running through tunnels, and the cabs are equipped with <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioning</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps80_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-ps80-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>El 18 is a modification of the Swiss Re 460. The class was originally built in 119 units from 1992 to 1995 for the Swiss State Railways, where it was given the brand Lok 2000. It was part of a project to create a series of new intercity locomotives and cars. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bern-L%C3%B6tschberg-Simplon_railway" title="Bern-Lötschberg-Simplon railway">Bern–Lötschberg–Simplon-Bahn</a> received eight units in 1994 (as Re 465), the <a href="/wiki/VR_Group" title="VR Group">Finnish State Railways</a> received 46 units between the years 1995-2003 (as <a href="/wiki/VR_Class_Sr2" title="VR Class Sr2">Sr2</a>) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kowloon%E2%80%93Canton_Railway_Corporation" title="Kowloon–Canton Railway Corporation">Kowloon–Canton Railway Corporation</a> received 2 units in 1997. The units are designed to haul heavy passenger trains along existing curved railways at high speeds. It is designed as a universal locomotive, so it is also suitable for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freight_rail_transport" title="Freight rail transport">freight trains</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ps80_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-ps80-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
