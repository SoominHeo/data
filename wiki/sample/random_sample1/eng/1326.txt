<p><b>NS Yoon-G</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_name" title="English name">English name</a>: <b>Christine Kim</b>; <a href="/wiki/Korean_name" title="Korean name">Korean name</a>: <b>Kim Yoonji</b>; <a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 김윤지; born September 6, 1988), sometimes stylized as <b>NS Yoonji</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> singer who debuted in 2009 with the digital single "Head Hurts" under JTM Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official-website_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-website-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88-line_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-88-line-2">[2]</a></sup> The NS in her name stands for "New Spirit". Yoon-G uses her Korean name as her stage name as she found "Christine" was difficult for Korean people to pronounce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soompi-interview_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-soompi-interview-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NS_Yoon-G&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yoon-G moved to <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando_Valley" title="San Fernando Valley">San Fernando Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> in 4th grade, and studied at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UCLA" title="UCLA">UCLA</a>, graduating with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's</a> in Communication. Yoon-G is cousins with former <a href="/wiki/Kara_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kara (South Korean band)">Kara</a> member, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kang_Jiyoung" title="Kang Jiyoung">Kang Jiyoung</a>, and has a younger brother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brother_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-brother-4">[4]</a></sup> She moved back to <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> to pursue her dream of becoming a singer, joining <a href="/wiki/DSP_Media" title="DSP Media">DSP Media</a> as a trainee,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> before debuting with JTM Entertainment with single "Head Hurts".</p>
<p>She recorded the English version of "You Make Me Happy" for <i><a href="/wiki/Fresh_Pretty_Cure!" title="Fresh Pretty Cure!">Fresh Pretty Cure!</a></i> in about 2009.</p>
<p>On November 1, 2012, Yoon-G released a digital single, "If You Love Me", featuring <a href="/wiki/Jay_Park" title="Jay Park">Jay Park</a>, and released an official music video as well as a behind-the-scenes version, showing Yoon-G and Jay Park in the studio together. Yoon-G performed her single on Korean music shows such as KBS' <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i>, Mnet's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i>, MBC's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_Core" title="Music Core">Music Core</a></i> and SBS' <i><a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a></i>, alongside Jay Park, who is occasionally replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dalmatian_(band)" title="Dalmatian (band)">Dalmatian</a>'s Simon.</p>
<p>Also in November 2012, Yoon-G became the host of the new show, <i>K-pop Tasty Road</i> with <a href="/wiki/U-KISS" title="U-KISS">U-KISS</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Eli_Kim" title="Eli Kim">Eli Kim</a>, combining the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Wave" title="Korean Wave">Korean Wave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korean_cuisine" title="Korean cuisine">food</a> into one show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2013, Yoon-G performed in <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aurea_(singer)" title="Aurea (singer)">Aurea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
