<p><b>NTT DATA Corporation</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社エヌ・ティ・ティ・データ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kabushiki-kaisha Enu-tī-tī Dēta</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese system integration company and a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Telegraph_and_Telephone" title="Nippon Telegraph and Telephone">Nippon Telegraph and Telephone</a> (NTT).</p>
<p>Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Public Corporation, a predecessor of NTT, started Data Communications business in 1967. NTT, following its privatization in 1985, spun off the Data Communications division as NTT DATA in 1988, which has now become the largest of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IT_Services" title="IT Services">IT Services</a> companies headquartered in Japan.</p>
<p>NTT DATA is a publicly traded company, but is about 54 percent owned by NTT. Its business areas are in national and local governments, financial, and telecommunication sectors. According to reports in 2012, <a href="/wiki/Forbes_Global_2000" title="Forbes Global 2000">Forbes Global 2000</a> recognizes NTT DATA as the 5th largest IT Services company.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/List_of_the_largest_software_companies" title="List of the largest software companies"><span title="List of Largest Software Companies 2012">Forbes Global 2000</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NTT_DATA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within Japan, NTT DATA has established many <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint ventures</a>, such as NTT Data-Sanyo Electric to take care of the IT services of <a href="/wiki/Sanyo" title="Sanyo">Sanyo</a> electric group. Outside Japan, NTT DATA has its wholly owned subsidiaries or offices in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, Singapore, Vietnam and other countries or regions.</p>
<p>Within the NTT group, while <a href="/wiki/NTT_Comware" title="NTT Comware">NTT Comware</a> focuses on the IT services to the Group companies, NTT Data mainly services non-NTT Group companies.</p>
<p>In 2010, NTT DATA acquired Intelligroup Inc., an US-based IT consulting and service providing company. After taking over Intelligroup, NTT became the ninth largest software company in the world, worth over $11 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>NTT DATA and Boston-based IT Service company <a href="/wiki/Keane_(company)" title="Keane (company)">Keane</a> agreed to a merger on 29 October 2010. The acquisition is worth over US$1.23 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> After the acquisition of Keane Inc., NTT DATA became the 8th largest software company in the world, with the annual revenue of $14 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
