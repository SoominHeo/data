<p><b>NYC</b> was a Japanese band formed by <a href="/wiki/Ryosuke_Yamada" title="Ryosuke Yamada">Ryosuke Yamada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Chinen" title="Yuri Chinen">Yuri Chinen</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hey!_Say!_JUMP" title="Hey! Say! JUMP">Hey! Say! JUMP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny%27s_Jr." title="Johnny's Jr.">Johnny's Jr.</a> <a href="/wiki/Yuma_Nakayama" title="Yuma Nakayama">Yuma Nakayama</a> in 2010. Before that, seven-members unit <b>NYC Boys</b> (stylized <b>NYC boys</b>) was formed on June 7, 2009, to promote the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIVB_World_Grand_Prix_2009" title="FIVB World Grand Prix 2009">FIVB World Grand Prix 2009</a>, an event that began on July 31 and ended on August 23, 2009. They performed the theme song "NYC" in Tokyo and Osaka before each game.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NYC_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 7, 2009, at the concert "Forum Shinkiroku!! <a href="/wiki/Johnny%27s_Jr." title="Johnny's Jr.">Johnny's Jr.</a> 1 Day 4 Performances Yaruzo!", it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Ryosuke_Yamada" title="Ryosuke Yamada">Ryosuke Yamada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Chinen" title="Yuri Chinen">Yuri Chinen</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hey!_Say!_JUMP" title="Hey! Say! JUMP">Hey! Say! JUMP</a> would join <a href="/wiki/Yuma_Nakayama_w/B.I.Shadow" title="Yuma Nakayama w/B.I.Shadow">Yuma Nakayama w/B.I.Shadow</a> to form NYC Boys. On July 15, 2009, their first single "NYC/Akuma na Koi", "NYC" being the theme song for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIVB_World_Grand_Prix_2009" title="FIVB World Grand Prix 2009">FIVB World Grand Prix 2009</a>, was released. During the announcement for forming NYC Boys, it was said that the group will be a temporary one, only active for the time of the Volleyball Tournament which was from July 31 to August 23, but even after the end of the tournament, the members of the group meet periodically to perform their songs.</p>
<p>In November, the media announced that they would be guest appearing on Japan's highest rated annual music television show, <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dhaku_Uta_Gassen" title="Kōhaku Uta Gassen">Kōhaku Uta Gassen</a>, on December 31, 2009. Other guest appearances this year include FNS Kayosai on December 2, 2009. On January 1, 2010, media announced that the group would not be a temporary one anymore and that <a href="/wiki/Ryosuke_Yamada" title="Ryosuke Yamada">Ryosuke Yamada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Chinen" title="Yuri Chinen">Yuri Chinen</a> would be working as members of both <a href="/wiki/Hey!_Say!_JUMP" title="Hey! Say! JUMP">Hey! Say! JUMP</a> and NYC Boys in the future.</p>
<p>On March 3, 2010, it was announced on the Johnny's website that NYC without the boys (B.I.Shadow) will release a new single entitled "<a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABki_100%25" title="Yūki 100%">Yūki 100%</a>" which is opening theme song for an anime entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Nintama_Rantar%C5%8D" title="Nintama Rantarō">Nintama Rantarō</a></i>. The coupling song entitled "Yume no Tane" will be the ending theme. Their single was released last April 7, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The said song "Yūki 100%" topped Japan Billboards' Weekly Hot 100 and Oricon's Weekly Chart.</p>
<p>On October 20, 2010, NYC released a single titled Yoku Asobi Yoku Manabe. The single became #1 on the Oricon weekly charts in its first week of sales.</p>
<p>On March 9, 2011, the group released a new single with the name of '<a href="/wiki/Yume_Tamago" title="Yume Tamago">Yume Tamago</a>', which peaked first on the Oricon Daily Singles chart with 31,393 sales and ended up in fourth position on the Oricon Monthly singles chart with 154,140 sales, being certified gold by the RIAJ.</p>
<p>On February 2012, NYC released their 4th single which is titled Wonderful Cupid and contains <a href="/wiki/Yuma_Nakayama" title="Yuma Nakayama">Yuma Nakayama</a>'s solo song Glass no Mahou.</p>
<p>On May 2012, NYC released their 5th single which is titled HAINA!.</p>
