<p><b>1859 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCLIX</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">starting on Saturday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> B) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">common year starting on Thursday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> D) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1859th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 859th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 59th year of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, and the 10th and last year of the <a href="/wiki/1850s" title="1850s">1850s</a> decade. As of the start of 1859, the Gregorian calendar was 12 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1859&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93March">January–March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1859&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_24" title="January 24">January 24</a> (<a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O. S.</a>) – <a href="/wiki/Wallachia" title="Wallachia">Wallachia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moldavia" title="Moldavia">Moldavia</a> are <a href="/wiki/United_Principalities" title="United Principalities">united</a> under <a href="/wiki/Alexandru_Ioan_Cuza" title="Alexandru Ioan Cuza">Alexandru Ioan Cuza</a> (<a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> since 1866, final unification takes place on December 1, <a href="/wiki/1918" title="1918">1918</a>; Transylvania and other regions are still missing at that time).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_28" title="January 28">January 28</a> – The city of <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Washington" title="Olympia, Washington">Olympia</a> is incorporated in the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Territory" title="Washington Territory">Washington Territory</a> in the United States of America.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_4" title="February 4">February 4</a> – German scholar <a href="/wiki/Constantin_von_Tischendorf" title="Constantin von Tischendorf">Constantin von Tischendorf</a> rediscovers the <a href="/wiki/Codex_Sinaiticus" title="Codex Sinaiticus">Codex Sinaiticus</a>, a 4th-century <a href="/wiki/Uncial_script" title="Uncial script">uncial</a> manuscript of the <a href="/wiki/Koine_Greek" title="Koine Greek">Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Catherine%27s_Monastery" title="Saint Catherine's Monastery">Saint Catherine's Monastery</a> on the foot of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Sinai" title="Mount Sinai">Mount Sinai</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Khedivate_of_Egypt" title="Khedivate of Egypt">Khedivate of Egypt</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_14" title="February 14">February 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a> is admitted as the 33rd <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_17" title="February 17">February 17</a> – French naval forces under <a href="/wiki/Charles_Rigault_de_Genouilly" title="Charles Rigault de Genouilly">Charles Rigault de Genouilly</a> capture the city and <a href="/wiki/Citadel_of_Saigon" title="Citadel of Saigon">Citadel of Saigon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, beginning the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Saigon" title="Siege of Saigon">Siege of Saigon</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_27" title="February 27">February 27</a> – United States Congressman <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Sickles" title="Daniel Sickles">Daniel Sickles</a> shoots <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philip_Barton_Key_(U.S._District_Attorney)" title="Philip Barton Key (U.S. District Attorney)">Philip Barton Key (U.S. District Attorney)</a> for having an affair with his wife.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_9" title="March 9">March 9</a> – The army of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Kingdom of Sardinia</a> mobilizes against Austria, beginning the crisis which will lead to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Sardinian_War" title="Austro-Sardinian War">Austro-Sardinian War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_21" title="March 21">March 21</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Pennsylvania" title="Commonwealth of Pennsylvania">Commonwealth of Pennsylvania</a> issues the charter establishing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zoological_Society_of_Philadelphia" title="Zoological Society of Philadelphia">Zoological Society of Philadelphia</a>, the first organization of its kind in the United States and founder of the nation's first zoo.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_26" title="March 26">March 26</a> – A French amateur astronomer, <a href="/wiki/Edmond_Modeste_Lescarbault" title="Edmond Modeste Lescarbault">Edmond Modeste Lescarbault</a>, claims to have noticed a planet closer to the Sun than Mercury – later named <a href="/wiki/Vulcan_(hypothetical_planet)" title="Vulcan (hypothetical planet)">Vulcan</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93June">April–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1859&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_13" title="April 13">April 13</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Cooper_Union_for_the_Advancement_of_Science_and_Art" title="The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art">The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art</a> is founded by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Cooper" title="Peter Cooper">Peter Cooper</a>, industrialist, inventor and philanthropist of New York.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_25" title="April 25">April 25</a> – Ground is broken for the <a href="/wiki/Suez_Canal" title="Suez Canal">Suez Canal</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_28" title="April 28">April 28</a> – The <i>Pomona</i> is wrecked off the English coast, with 424 dead.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_29" title="April 29">April 29</a> – Austrian troops begin to cross the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ticino_River" title="Ticino River">Ticino River</a> to <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_30" title="April 30">April 30</a> – <i><a href="/wiki/A_Tale_of_Two_Cities" title="A Tale of Two Cities">A Tale of Two Cities</a></i> by Charles Dickens is published.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_4" title="May 4">May 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Railway" title="Cornwall Railway">Cornwall Railway</a> opens across the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Bridge" title="Royal Albert Bridge">Royal Albert Bridge</a>, linking the counties of <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> in England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_5" title="May 5">May 5</a> – Border Treaty between <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>: The two countries agree their borders should be traced at the water divide between the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Orinoco" title="Orinoco">Orinoco</a> basins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_22" title="May 22">May 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Ferdinand II of the Two Sicilies">Ferdinand II of the Two Sicilies</a> is succeeded by his 23-year-old son <a href="/wiki/Francis_II_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Francis II of the Two Sicilies">Francis II of the Two Sicilies</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_26" title="May 26">May 26</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Sardinian_War" title="Austro-Sardinian War">Austro-Sardinian War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hunters_of_the_Alps" title="Hunters of the Alps">Hunters of the Alps</a> confront Austrian forces led by Field Marshal-Lieutenant Carl Baron Urban <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Varese" title="Battle of Varese">at Varese</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_26" title="May 26">May 26</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/June_2" title="June 2">June 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">Geologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Prestwich" title="Joseph Prestwich">Joseph Prestwich</a> and amateur <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeologist" title="Archaeologist">archaeologist</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Evans_(archaeologist)" title="John Evans (archaeologist)">John Evans</a> report (to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> and <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Antiquaries_of_London" title="Society of Antiquaries of London">Society of Antiquaries of London</a>, respectively) the results of their investigations of gravel-pits in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Somme_valley" title="Somme valley">Somme valley</a> and elsewhere, extending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_history" title="Human history">human history</a> back to what will become known as the <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">Paleolithic</a> Era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_30" title="May 30">May 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Palestro" title="Battle of Palestro">Battle of Palestro</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Sardinians</a> defeat the Austrian army.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_31" title="May 31">May 31</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Big_Ben" title="Big Ben">Great Clock</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Westminster" title="Palace of Westminster">Palace of Westminster</a>, London was started, and its bells ring for the very first time.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Sardinian_War" title="Austro-Sardinian War">Austro-Sardinian War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Magenta" title="Battle of Magenta">Battle of Magenta</a>: The French and Sardinians defeat the Austrians.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_6" title="June 6">June 6</a> – The British <a href="/wiki/Crown_colony" title="Crown colony">Crown colony</a> of Queensland in Australia is created by devolving part of the territory of <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> (<a href="/wiki/Queensland_Day" title="Queensland Day">Queensland Day</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_8" title="June 8">June 8</a> – The discovery of the <a href="/wiki/Comstock_Lode" title="Comstock Lode">Comstock Lode</a> in the western Utah Territory setting off the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_to_Washoe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rush to Washoe (page does not exist)">Rush to Washoe</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_15" title="June 15">June 15</a> – The so-called <a href="/wiki/Pig_War_(1859)" title="Pig War (1859)">Pig War</a> border dispute between the Americans and the British on the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Islands" title="San Juan Islands">San Juan Islands</a> begins by the death of the namesake pig.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_17" title="June 17">June 17</a> – The only recorded <a href="/wiki/Simoom" title="Simoom">simoom</a> ever in North America hit <a href="/wiki/Goleta,_California" title="Goleta, California">Goleta, California</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara,_California" title="Santa Barbara, California">Santa Barbara, California</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_18" title="June 18">June 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Aletschhorn" title="Aletschhorn">Aletschhorn</a>, second summit of the <a href="/wiki/Bernese_Alps" title="Bernese Alps">Bernese Alps</a>, is first ascended.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_24" title="June 24">June 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Solferino" title="Battle of Solferino">Battle of Solferino</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Kingdom of Sardinia</a> and the armies of <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_III" title="Napoleon III">Napoleon III</a> of France defeat <a href="/wiki/Franz_Joseph_I_of_Austria" title="Franz Joseph I of Austria">Franz Joseph I of Austria</a> in northern Italy; the battle inspires <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henri_Dunant" title="Henri Dunant">Henri Dunant</a> to found the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_30" title="June 30">June 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_Blondin" title="Charles Blondin">Charles Blondin</a> crosses <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls" title="Niagara Falls">Niagara Falls</a> on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tightrope" title="Tightrope">tightrope</a> for the first time.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93September">July–September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1859&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: July–September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>July
<ul>
<li>Count <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camillo_Benso_di_Cavour" title="Camillo Benso di Cavour">Camillo Benso di Cavour</a> resigns.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pike%27s_Peak_Gold_Rush" title="Pike's Peak Gold Rush">Pike's Peak Gold Rush</a> begins in the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Territory" title="Colorado Territory">Colorado Territory</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a> – The first intercollegiate <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> game is played, between <a href="/wiki/Amherst_College" title="Amherst College">Amherst</a> and <a href="/wiki/Williams_College" title="Williams College">Williams</a> Colleges.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_8" title="July 8">July 8</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_XV_of_Sweden" title="Charles XV of Sweden">Charles XV</a> succeeds his father <a href="/wiki/Oscar_I_of_Sweden" title="Oscar I of Sweden">Oscar I</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> (as Charles IV).</li>
<li>An <a href="/wiki/Armistice" title="Armistice">armistice</a> is declared between Austria and others.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – The chimes of <a href="/wiki/Big_Ben" title="Big Ben">Big Ben</a> ring for the first time in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – By the preliminary treaty signed at Villafranca, Italy, <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a> is ceded to the French (who immediately cede it to Sardinia), while the Austrians keep <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venetia_(region)" title="Venetia (region)">Venetia</a> and the French promise to restore the Central Italian rulers expelled in the course of the war. This brings the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Sardinian_War" title="Austro-Sardinian War">Austro-Sardinian War</a> effectively to a close.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_30" title="July 30">July 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Grand_Combin" title="Grand Combin">Grand Combin</a>, one of the highest summits in the Alps, is first ascended.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_16" title="August 16">August 16</a> – The Tuscan National Assembly formally deposes the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">House of Habsburg-Lorraine</a>; ending an ascendancy of 109 years.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_27" title="August 27">August 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Drake" title="Edwin Drake">Edwin Drake</a> drills the first <a href="/wiki/Oil_well" title="Oil well">oil well</a> in the United States, near <a href="/wiki/Titusville,_Pennsylvania" title="Titusville, Pennsylvania">Titusville, Pennsylvania</a>, starting the <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_oil_rush" title="Pennsylvania oil rush">Pennsylvania oil rush</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_28" title="August 28">August 28</a>–<a href="/wiki/September_2" title="September 2">September 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Solar_storm_of_1859" title="Solar storm of 1859">solar storm of 1859</a>, the largest geomagnetic solar storm on record, causes the <a href="/wiki/Aurora" title="Aurora">Northern lights</a> to be visible as far south as Cuba and knocks out <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telegraph" title="Telegraph">telegraph</a> communication. (This is also called the <a href="/wiki/Richard_Christopher_Carrington" title="Richard Christopher Carrington">Carrington</a> event).</li>
<li>September – British merchant <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Blake_Glover" title="Thomas Blake Glover">Thomas Blake Glover</a> sets up in business in <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>, Japan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_17" title="September 17">September 17</a> – In <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Norton" title="Emperor Norton">Joshua Norton</a> proclaims himself to be His Imperial Majesty Emperor Norton I, "Emperor of the United States" and "Protector of Mexico".</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October.E2.80.93December">October–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1859&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: October–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_16" title="October 16">October 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_(abolitionist)" title="John Brown (abolitionist)">John Brown</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Brown%27s_raid_on_Harpers_Ferry" title="John Brown's raid on Harpers Ferry">raids</a> the <a href="/wiki/Harpers_Ferry_Armory" title="Harpers Ferry Armory">Harpers Ferry Armory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harpers_Ferry,_West_Virginia" title="Harpers Ferry, West Virginia">Harper's Ferry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, in an unsuccessful bid to spark a general slave rebellion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_18" title="October 18">October 18</a> – Troops under Colonel <a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Lee" title="Robert E. Lee">Robert E. Lee</a> overpower <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_(abolitionist)" title="John Brown (abolitionist)">Brown</a> at the Federal arsenal.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_26" title="October 26">October 26</a> – The steamship <i><a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter_(ship)" title="Royal Charter (ship)">Royal Charter</a></i> is wrecked on the coast of <a href="/wiki/Anglesey" title="Anglesey">Anglesey</a>, Wales with 454 dead.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_1" title="November 1">November 1</a> – The current <a href="/wiki/Cape_Lookout_(North_Carolina)" title="Cape Lookout (North Carolina)">Cape Lookout</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Lookout_Lighthouse" title="Cape Lookout Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> is lighted for the first time (its first-order <a href="/wiki/Fresnel_lens" title="Fresnel lens">Fresnel lens</a> can be seen for 19 miles).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_10" title="November 10">November 10</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Z%C3%BCrich" title="Treaty of Zürich">Treaty of Zürich</a>, reaffirming the terms of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Villafranca" title="Treaty of Villafranca">Treaty of Villafranca</a>, brings the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Sardinian_War" title="Austro-Sardinian War">Austro-Sardinian War</a> to an official close.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_15" title="November 15">November 15</a> – First <a href="/wiki/Zappas_Olympics" title="Zappas Olympics">Zappas Olympics</a> open in Greece.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_24" title="November 24">November 24</a>
<ul>
<li>English naturalist <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> publishes <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Origin_of_Species" title="On the Origin of Species">On the Origin of Species</a></i>, a book which argues for the gradual <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolution</a> of <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> through <a href="/wiki/Natural_selection" title="Natural selection">natural selection</a> (it immediately sells out its initial print run).</li>
<li>The French Navy's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Gloire" title="La Gloire">La Gloire</a></i>, the first ocean-going ironclad warship in history, is launched.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_2" title="December 2">December 2</a> – Militant <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_States" title="Abolitionism in the United States">abolitionist</a> leader <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_(abolitionist)" title="John Brown (abolitionist)">John Brown</a> is hanged for his <a href="/wiki/October_16" title="October 16">October 16</a> raid on <a href="/wiki/Harpers_Ferry,_West_Virginia" title="Harpers Ferry, West Virginia">Harpers Ferry, West Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_4" title="December 4">December 4</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mekteb-i_M%C3%BClkiye" title="Mekteb-i Mülkiye">Mekteb-i Mülkiye</a> School is founded in the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_10" title="December 10">December 10</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Separation_of_Queensland" title="Separation of Queensland">Separation of Queensland</a> from <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Ateneo_de_Manila_University" title="Ateneo de Manila University">Ateneo de Manila University</a> is founded as the <i>Escuela Municipal de Manila</i>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1859&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/District_nurse" title="District nurse">District nursing</a> begins in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, England, when philanthropist <a href="/wiki/William_Rathbone_VI" title="William Rathbone VI">William Rathbone</a> employs Mary Robinson to nurse the sick poor in their own homes.</li>
<li>The island of <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a> is divided between Portugal and the Netherlands.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Rincon_de_Los_Esteros" title="Rancho Rincon de Los Esteros">Rancho Rincon de Los Esteros</a> Land Grant is confirmed to Rafael Alvisa, (part of the present <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara_County,_California" title="Santa Clara County, California">Santa Clara County, California</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bernhard_Riemann" title="Bernhard Riemann">Bernhard Riemann</a> formulates the <a href="/wiki/Riemann_hypothesis" title="Riemann hypothesis">Riemann hypothesis</a>, one of the most important open problems of contemporary mathematics.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a> is declared the capital of newly separated colony Queensland, Australia.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan_Law_School" title="University of Michigan Law School">University of Michigan Law School</a> is founded.</li>
<li>Marx publishes <i><a href="/wiki/A_Contribution_to_the_Critique_of_Political_Economy" title="A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy">A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy</a></i>.</li>
<li>Mary Institute is founded.</li>
<li>The beginning of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidskrift_f%C3%B6r_hemmet" title="Tidskrift för hemmet">Tidskrift för hemmet</a></i>, the first women's magazine in the Nordic countries.</li>
</ul>
