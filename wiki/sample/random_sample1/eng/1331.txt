<p><b>Ne Obliviscaris</b> (Latin for "forget not") are a six-piece <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Australia" title="Melbourne, Australia">Melbourne, Australia</a>. Formed initially as a four-piece in April 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the group was founded by Xenoyr and Corey Baker .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The current lineup comprises vocalist Xenoyr, violinist and vocalist Tim Charles, guitarist Matt Klavins, guitarist Benjamin Baret, bassist Brendan "Cygnus" Brown and drummer Daniel Presland. To date, the band has released a demo, two extended plays and two studio albums. The most recent album, <i><a href="/wiki/Citadel_(Ne_Obliviscaris_album)" title="Citadel (Ne Obliviscaris album)">Citadel</a></i>, was released on 7 November 2014 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ne_Obliviscaris_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_and_early_years">Formation and early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ne_Obliviscaris_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation and early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ne Obliviscaris were formed in April 2003 by vocalist Marc "Xenoyr" Campbell and drummer Corey Baker,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> later adding a second guitarist and a female soprano. Current vocalist/violinist Tim Charles followed in September 2003. The band went through a number of lineup changes before releasing an independent demo, <i>The Aurora Veil</i>, in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The demo featured three songs which would later appear on their debut album. Their music, whilst being firmly rooted in extreme metal, shows influences from a range of musical styles, including death, black, thrash and melodic metal, as well as classical, jazz, avant-garde and flamenco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Drummer Dan Presland left the band towards the end of 2011<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and was replaced by Nelson Barnes. However, due to the distance between Queensland and Melbourne, Ne Obliviscaris parted ways with Barnes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and Presland's return to the band was announced on 24 August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portal_of_I_.282012.E2.80.932014.29"><i>Portal of I</i> (2012–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ne_Obliviscaris_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Portal of I (2012–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Released 7 May 2012 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Portal_of_I" title="Portal of I">Portal of I</a></i> was the first full-length album by the band. The release of the album was delayed due to the departure of their guitarist, Corey King, in 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and the search for a replacement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Benjamin Baret later joined as the lead guitarist, but was subject to lengthy delays in his visa application process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album received generally positive reviews<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> with Metal Injection praising the composition and the musicianship of the album <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band toured in support of the album, which took them overseas for the first time,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> with the band playing in Japan, China, Taiwan, Hong Kong and Thailand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> They were also the headlining act for Progfest 2012 around Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>And Plague Flowers The Kaleidoscope</i> from Portal of I was included in a teaching curriculum at Sydney Conservatorium of Music, the song being analysed for its composition<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Citadel_.282014.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Citadel</i> (2014–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ne_Obliviscaris_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Citadel (2014–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Recorded during 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> <i>Citadel</i> was released through Seasons of Mist on November 7, 2014. The album reached number 42 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARIA_Albums_Chart" title="ARIA Albums Chart">ARIA Albums Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album was generally well received,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> with Metal Hammer giving it a four out of five rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Metal Injection again praising the complexity of the arrangements<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2014, the band announced a <a href="/wiki/Crowdfunding" title="Crowdfunding">crowdfunding</a> campaign<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> to support a world tour with an initial target of $40,000 AUD. This target was reached in just two days,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> reaching a final figure of $86,132 AUD and breaking the Australian record for crowd-funding in the process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>The subsequent world tour saw Ne Obliviscaris return to Asia supporting <a href="/wiki/Fleshgod_Apocalypse" title="Fleshgod Apocalypse">Fleshgod Apocalypse</a> as well as their first tours of Europe, the UK and North America where they supported British band, <a href="/wiki/Cradle_of_Filth" title="Cradle of Filth">Cradle of Filth</a>. They also played in India for the first time, where they headlined the B-School of Rock at the Unmaad Festival in Bangalore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2016, the band began a headlining tour of North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> with headlining shows in the U.K and Ireland following in October 2016 with Atlanta-based band Oceans of Slumber as support. Also announced were further European tour supporting <a href="/wiki/Enslaved_(band)" title="Enslaved (band)">Enslaved</a> between September and November 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and headline shows in Australia at the end of November and beginning of December 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_Crowdfunding">Use of Crowdfunding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ne_Obliviscaris_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Use of Crowdfunding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to the campaign to fund their world tour, the band announced in March 2016 that they were starting a subscription based service through the <a href="/wiki/Patreon" title="Patreon">Patreon</a> micro-payments platform. This was in part inspired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thy_Art_is_Murder" title="Thy Art is Murder">Thy Art is Murder</a> frontman, CJ McMahon, quitting the band for financial reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The decision to use Patreon generated some controversy on social media platforms to which vocalist and violinist Tim Charles responded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
