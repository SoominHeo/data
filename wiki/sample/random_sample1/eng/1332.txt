<p>"<b>Never Gonna Give You Up</b>" is a single by <a href="/wiki/Rick_Astley" title="Rick Astley">Rick Astley</a>, released in 1987, written and produced by <a href="/wiki/Stock_Aitken_Waterman" title="Stock Aitken Waterman">Stock Aitken Waterman</a>. The song was released as the first single from his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Whenever_You_Need_Somebody" title="Whenever You Need Somebody">Whenever You Need Somebody</a></i> (1987). The song was a worldwide number-one hit, initially in the singer's native United Kingdom in 1987, where it stayed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_UK_Singles_Chart_number_ones" title="List of UK Singles Chart number ones">at the top of the chart</a> for five weeks and was the best-selling single of that year. It eventually topped the charts in 25 countries, including the United States and West Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> "Never Gonna Give You Up" hit number one in the US in March 1988 after it was played by resident DJ, <a href="/wiki/Larry_Levan" title="Larry Levan">Larry Levan</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Paradise_Garage" title="Paradise Garage">Paradise Garage</a> in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song won Best British Single at the 1988 <a href="/wiki/Brit_Awards" title="Brit Awards">Brit Awards</a>. In 2004, it was voted number 28 in <i>50 Most Awesomely Bad Songs... Ever</i> by <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>.</p>
<p>The music video for the song has become the basis for the "<a href="/wiki/Rickrolling" title="Rickrolling">Rickrolling</a>" Internet meme. In 2008, Rick Astley won the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Europe_Music_Award_for_Best_Act" title="MTV Europe Music Award for Best Act">MTV Europe Music Award for Best Act Ever</a> with the song, as a result of collective voting from thousands of people on the internet, due to the popular phenomenon of Rickrolling. It was also featured as a downloadable track for the <i>Rock Band</i> series.</p>
<p>The song is considered Astley's signature song and it is often played at the end of Astley's live concerts. During a live performance, usually on the last verse, Rick sometimes shouts out to the audience that he is feeling "bloody marvellous" or in other countries that he is feeling "fantastic" after the line "And if you ask me how I'm feeling." to have some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banter" title="Banter">banter</a> with the audience.</p>

<ul>
<li>In 1997, French band <a href="/wiki/2Be3" title="2Be3">2Be3</a> covered the song under the name "Toujours là pour toi", which had a great success in France (No. 4) and Belgium (Wallonia) (No. 12).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1999, an Italian cover entitled "Non ti lascerò" was made by <a href="/wiki/Rosario_Fiorello" title="Rosario Fiorello">Fiorello</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eurobeat" title="Eurobeat">Eurobeat</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Johnson_(singer)" title="Kevin Johnson (singer)">Kevin Johnson</a> covered the song in 2004 for the album <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Eurobeat" title="Super Eurobeat">Super Eurobeat 149</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>A group of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> dance producers called The Rickrollerz made a <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house music</a> cover version of "Never Gonna Give You Up"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In May 2008, the track entered the UK Club Charts at no. 22.</li>
<li>In August 2007, <a href="/wiki/Internet_celebrity" title="Internet celebrity">internet celebrity</a> and singer <a href="/wiki/Tay_Zonday" title="Tay Zonday">Tay Zonday</a> covered the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>On 25 November 2008, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Manilow" title="Barry Manilow">Barry Manilow</a> released a version of the song on his <i><a href="/wiki/The_Greatest_Songs_of_the_Eighties" title="The Greatest Songs of the Eighties">The Greatest Songs of the Eighties</a></i> album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Tisdale" title="Ashley Tisdale">Ashley Tisdale</a> released a <a href="/wiki/List_of_songs_recorded_by_Ashley_Tisdale" title="List of songs recorded by Ashley Tisdale">cover</a> of the song, along with three other songs of the 80s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rickrolling">Rickrolling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Never_Gonna_Give_You_Up&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rickrolling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rickrolling" title="Rickrolling">Rickrolling</a></div>
<p>"Never Gonna Give You Up" is the subject of a popular Internet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prank" title="Prank">prank</a> known as "rickrolling" involving misleading links (commonly shortened URLs) redirecting to the song's music video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Originally started by <a href="/wiki/4chan" title="4chan">4chan</a>, by May 2007, the practice had achieved notoriety on the Internet, and it increased in popularity after its use as a 2008 <a href="/wiki/April_Fools%27_Day" title="April Fools' Day">April Fools' Day</a> joke by various media companies and websites, including <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> rickrolling all of its featured videos on that day and a website allowing people to rickroll their friends' phones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In "a couple of weeks," about 13 million people had been tricked into watching Astley's video, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> reported on 1 April 2008. "I think it's just one of those odd things where something gets picked up and people run with it," Astley told the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> in late March 2008, adding: "That's what's brilliant about the Internet."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Astley also appeared in the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s_Thanksgiving_Day_Parade" title="Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade">Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade</a> interrupting a song performed by those on a float promoting the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> program <i><a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Home_for_Imaginary_Friends" title="Foster's Home for Imaginary Friends">Foster's Home for Imaginary Friends</a></i> with a lipsynched performance of "Never Gonna Give You Up."</p>
<p>There were reports that despite the video garnering millions of hits on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, Astley earned almost no money from the <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">online phenomenon</a>, receiving only $12 USD in royalties from YouTube for his performance share, as of August 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> but Astley denied those reports in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
