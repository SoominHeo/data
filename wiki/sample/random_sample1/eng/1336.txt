<p><b>O2 Arena</b> (formerly <b>Sazka Arena</b>, stylised as <b>O<sub>2</sub> arena</b>) is a multi-purpose <a href="/wiki/Arena" title="Arena">arena</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>. It is home to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HC_Sparta_Prague" title="HC Sparta Prague">HC Sparta Prague</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Extraliga" title="Czech Extraliga">Czech Extraliga</a> and is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_European_ice_hockey_arenas" title="List of European ice hockey arenas">second-largest ice hockey arena in Europe</a>.</p>
<p>It has hosted important sporting events such as the <a href="/wiki/2015_European_Athletics_Indoor_Championships" title="2015 European Athletics Indoor Championships">European Athletics Indoor Championships</a>, two Ice Hockey World Championships (2004, 2015), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euroleague_Final_Four" title="Euroleague Final Four">Euroleague Final Four 2006</a>, the World <a href="/wiki/Floorball" title="Floorball">Floorball</a> Championship, the <a href="/wiki/2012_Davis_Cup_World_Group" title="2012 Davis Cup World Group">Davis Cup finals</a>, as well as a handful of NHL and KHL games, including the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_KHL_season" title="2013–14 KHL season">2014 Gagarin Cup final</a>. It can also host stage shows, such as concerts, and other large-scale events.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_events">Notable events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_Arena_(Prague)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Notable events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Visitor record held by <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> concert in 2006, which was attended by 18,628 spectators. On September 6 and 7, 2006 <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> performed there during her <a href="/wiki/Confessions_Tour" title="Confessions Tour">Confessions Tour</a>. She performed again at the arena on November 7 and 8, 2015 as part of her <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Heart_Tour" title="Rebel Heart Tour">Rebel Heart Tour</a> selling out crowds of over 16,000 patrons.</p>
<p>On May 12 2008, <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a> performed there as part of her <a href="/wiki/KylieX2008" title="KylieX2008">KylieX2008</a>. She performed again at the arena on March 2, 2011 as part of her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aphrodite_World_Tour" title="Aphrodite World Tour">Aphrodite World Tour</a>. On October 21, 2014 Minogue performed again as part of her <a href="/wiki/Kiss_Me_Once_Tour" title="Kiss Me Once Tour">Kiss Me Once Tour</a>.</p>
<p>In October 2008, the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Rangers" title="New York Rangers">New York Rangers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Lightning" title="Tampa Bay Lightning">Tampa Bay Lightning</a> opened the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_NHL_season" title="2008–09 NHL season">2008–09 NHL season</a> at O2 Arena with two games. Two years later, the NHL returned, with the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Bruins" title="Boston Bruins">Boston Bruins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phoenix_Coyotes" title="Phoenix Coyotes">Phoenix Coyotes</a> playing twice.</p>
<p>In November 2008, the French electronica pioneer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Michel_Jarre" title="Jean Michel Jarre">Jean Michel Jarre</a> performed his <i><a href="/wiki/Oxyg%C3%A8ne" title="Oxygène">Oxygène</a></i> album live at the arena, as part of the second leg of the Oxygène 30th anniversary tour.</p>
<p>In December 2008, the arena played host to the playoff matches of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Men%27s_World_Floorball_Championships" title="2008 Men's World Floorball Championships">2008 Men's World Floorball Championships</a>, including Finland's 7-6 victory over Sweden in the final.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sting_(musician)" title="Sting (musician)">Sting</a> performed during his <a href="/wiki/Symphonicities" title="Symphonicities">Symphonicities</a> Tour on September 22, 2010, along with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Philharmonic Orchestra">Royal Philharmonic Orchestra</a>.</p>
<p>In November 2010 <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> performed there during her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monster_Ball_Tour" title="Monster Ball Tour">Monster Ball Tour</a>.</p>
<p>On December 7, 2011 <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a> performed there during her <a href="/wiki/Loud_Tour" title="Loud Tour">Loud Tour</a></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_Davis_Cup_Team" title="Czech Republic Davis Cup Team">Czech Republic Davis Cup Team</a> defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spain_Davis_Cup_Team" title="Spain Davis Cup Team">Spain</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Davis_Cup_World_Group" title="2012 Davis Cup World Group">2012 Davis Cup Final</a>.</p>
<p>On October 26, 2012 <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a> performed a sold-out show during her <a href="/wiki/Dance_Again_World_Tour" title="Dance Again World Tour">Dance Again World Tour</a>.</p>
<p>On November 22, 2012 <a href="/wiki/Muse_(band)" title="Muse (band)">Muse</a> performed there during their <a href="/wiki/The_2nd_Law_World_Tour" title="The 2nd Law World Tour">The 2nd Law World Tour</a>.</p>
<p>On October 5, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> performed there as part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artrave:_The_Artpop_Ball" title="Artrave: The Artpop Ball">Artrave: The Artpop Ball</a> tour.</p>
<p>On November 9, 2014 the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_Fed_Cup_team" title="Czech Republic Fed Cup team">Czech Fed Cup team</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/Germany_Fed_Cup_team" title="Germany Fed Cup team">German Fed Cup team</a> in the final of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Fed_Cup" title="2014 Fed Cup">2014 Fed Cup</a>.</p>
<p>On February 17, 2015 <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert" title="Queen + Adam Lambert">Queen + Adam Lambert</a> performed there as part of their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert_Tour_2014-2015" title="Queen + Adam Lambert Tour 2014-2015">Queen + Adam Lambert Tour 2014-2015</a>.</p>
<p>On February 23, 2015 <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a> performed there as part of <a href="/wiki/The_Prismatic_World_Tour" title="The Prismatic World Tour">Prismatic World Tour</a>.</p>
<p>On January 30, 2016 <a href="/wiki/Ellie_Goulding" title="Ellie Goulding">Ellie Goulding</a> performed there as part of <a href="/wiki/Delirium_World_Tour" title="Delirium World Tour">Delirium World Tour</a>.</p>
<p>On November 12,2016 <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a> performed a sold-out show during him's <a href="/wiki/Purpose_World_Tour" title="Purpose World Tour">Purpose World Tour</a></p>
<p>The arena is also home of <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(festival)" title="Transmission (festival)">Transmission (festival)</a> since 2007.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_Arena_(Prague)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The idea of building a new arena in Prague came on the heels of the "golden era" of Czech <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a>: winning the gold medal at the 1998 Winter Olympics and three gold medals in a row at the <a href="/wiki/Ice_Hockey_World_Championships" title="Ice Hockey World Championships">Ice Hockey World Championships</a>. The arena was proposed to be built in time to host the <a href="/wiki/2003_Men%27s_World_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="2003 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships">2003 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships</a>, but due to unforeseen complications with the investors,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> the ice hockey governing body had to switch that tournament to <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>. The arena's main backer then became Sazka a.s., a Czech betting company.</p>
<p>The construction of the arena (which began in September 2002) was not without problems, but it was finally finished in time to host the 2004 tournament, the <a href="/wiki/2004_Men%27s_World_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="2004 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships">2004 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2008, the building was renamed O2 Arena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2011, Sazka filed for insolvency due to debts from building the arena. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>From its opening until 2015, it was home to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HC_Slavia_Prague" title="HC Slavia Prague">HC Slavia Prague</a> of the Czech Extraliga. Slavia won the national championship on home ice in Game 7 of the 2008 Extraliga finals against <a href="/wiki/HC_Karlovy_Vary" title="HC Karlovy Vary">HC Karlovy Vary</a> 4-0 in front of a then-league-record crowd of 17,117. In 2015, Slavia was relegated to the 1.liga, and the club chose to move back to the smaller Zimní stadion Eden, the team's former home and current training centre. In its place, O2 Arena reached an agreement with cross-town rivals Sparta Prague on 24 June 2015. Sparta ownership cited the need for significant renovations at <a href="/wiki/Tipsport_Arena_(Prague)" title="Tipsport Arena (Prague)">Tipsport Arena</a> as the main reason for the move.</p>
<p>For two seasons, 2012-13 and 2013-14, O2 Arena also hosted occasional home games of <a href="/wiki/HC_Lev_Praha" title="HC Lev Praha">HC Lev Prague</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kontinental_Hockey_League" title="Kontinental Hockey League">Kontinental Hockey League</a>. The club played its home games of the 2014 Gagarin Cup Finals at O2 Arena, attracting the three largest crowds in league history.</p>
<p>In 2015, O2 Arena co-hosted the IIHF World Championship with ČEZ Aréna in Ostrava for the second time. This time, the tournament re-established the record for World Championship attendance.</p>
