<p><b>1860 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCLX</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap year</a> <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Leap year starting on Sunday">starting on Sunday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> AG) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Friday" title="Leap year starting on Friday">leap year starting on Friday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> CB) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1860th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 860th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 60th year of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, and the 1st year of the <a href="/wiki/1860s" title="1860s">1860s</a> decade. As of the start of 1860, the Gregorian calendar was 12 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1860&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93March">January–March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1860&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_10" title="January 10">January 10</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Pemberton_Mill" title="Pemberton Mill">Pemberton Mill</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Massachusetts" title="Lawrence, Massachusetts">Lawrence, Massachusetts</a>, USA, collapses, killing 146 workers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_13" title="January 13">January 13</a> – Spanish victory (under General Leopoldo O'Donnell, 1st Duke of Tetuanat) at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_T%C3%A9touan" title="Battle of Tétouan">Battle of Tétouan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_20" title="January 20">January 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Camillo_Benso,_Count_of_Cavour" title="Camillo Benso, Count of Cavour">Camillo Benso, Count of Cavour</a> is recalled as Prime Minister of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piedmont-Sardinia" title="Piedmont-Sardinia">Piedmont-Sardinia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_22" title="February 22">February 22</a> – Shoe-making workers of <a href="/wiki/Lynn,_Massachusetts" title="Lynn, Massachusetts">Lynn, Massachusetts</a>, strike successfully for higher wages. The strike spreads throughout <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> and eventually involves 20,000 workers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_26" title="February 26">February 26</a> – White settlers <a href="/wiki/1860_Wiyot_massacre" title="1860 Wiyot massacre">massacre</a> a band of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wiyot_people" title="Wiyot people">Wiyot Indians</a> on <a href="/wiki/Indian_Island_(Humboldt_Bay)" title="Indian Island (Humboldt Bay)">Indian Island</a> near <a href="/wiki/Eureka,_California" title="Eureka, California">Eureka, California</a>. At least 60 women, children and elders are killed. <a href="/wiki/Bret_Harte" title="Bret Harte">Bret Harte</a>, newspaper reporter in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arcata" title="Arcata">Arcata</a>, reports the news to newspapers in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_28" title="February 28">February 28</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Artists_Rifles" title="Artists Rifles">Artists Rifles</a> is established, as the 38th Middlesex (Artists) Rifle Volunteer Corps, with headquarters at <a href="/wiki/Burlington_House" title="Burlington House">Burlington House</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Website_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Website-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_6" title="March 6">March 6</a> – While campaigning for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1860" title="United States presidential election, 1860">presidency</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> makes a speech defending the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_to_strike" title="Right to strike">right to strike</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_9" title="March 9">March 9</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Embassy_to_the_United_States_(1860)" title="Japanese Embassy to the United States (1860)">Japanese embassy to the United States</a> arrive in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_17" title="March 17">March 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/First_Taranaki_War" title="First Taranaki War">First Taranaki War</a> begins at <a href="/wiki/Waitara,_New_Zealand" title="Waitara, New Zealand">Waitara</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> when <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori</a> refuse to sell land to British settlers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_22" title="March 22">March 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Grand Duchy of Tuscany</a> is annexed to the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Kingdom of Italy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_24" title="March 24">March 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sakuradamon_Incident_(1860)" title="Sakuradamon Incident (1860)">Sakuradamon Incident</a>: <a href="/wiki/R%C5%8Dnin" title="Rōnin">Rōnin</a> <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Mito_Domain" title="Mito Domain">Mito Domain</a> in Japan assassinate <a href="/wiki/Tair%C5%8D" title="Tairō">tairō</a> (Chief Minister) <a href="/wiki/Ii_Naosuke" title="Ii Naosuke">Ii Naosuke</a> outside the Sakurada Gate of <a href="/wiki/Edo_Castle" title="Edo Castle">Edo Castle</a>, dissafected with his role in the opening of Japan to foreign powers.</li>
<li>March–August – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Jiangnan_(1860)" title="Battle of Jiangnan (1860)">The second rout</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Jiangnan_Daying" title="Jiangnan Daying">Jiangnan Daying</a> destroys the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a>'s army of 180,000.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93June">April–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1860&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_2" title="April 2">April 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Italian_Parliament" title="Italian Parliament">first Italian Parliament</a> meet at <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_3" title="April 3">April 3</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Pony_Express" title="Pony Express">Pony Express</a> begins its first run from <a href="/wiki/St._Joseph,_Missouri" title="St. Joseph, Missouri">St. Joseph, Missouri</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a> with riders carrying <a href="/wiki/Pony_Express_Bible" title="Pony Express Bible">a small bible</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_4" title="April 4">April 4</a> – A new uprising erupts in <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_9" title="April 9">April 9</a> – French typesetter <a href="/wiki/%C3%89douard-L%C3%A9on_Scott_de_Martinville" title="Édouard-Léon Scott de Martinville">Édouard-Léon Scott de Martinville</a> sings the French folk song "<a href="/wiki/Au_clair_de_la_lune" title="Au clair de la lune">Au clair de la lune</a>" to his <a href="/wiki/Phonautograph" title="Phonautograph">phonautograph</a>; producing the world's earliest known <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sound_recording" title="Sound recording">sound recording</a> (however, it is not rediscovered until <a href="/wiki/2008" title="2008">2008</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_1" title="May 1">May 1</a> – A Chondrite-type <a href="/wiki/Meteorite" title="Meteorite">meteorite</a> falls to earth in <a href="/wiki/Muskingum_County,_Ohio" title="Muskingum County, Ohio">Muskingum County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> near the town of <a href="/wiki/New_Concord,_Ohio" title="New Concord, Ohio">New Concord</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_6" title="May 6">May 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a> and his troops depart from <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Quarto</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Expedition_of_the_Thousand" title="Expedition of the Thousand">Expedition of the Thousand</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_8" title="May 8">May 8</a> – In <a href="/wiki/Granadine_Confederation" title="Granadine Confederation">New Granada</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>) the southern state of <a href="/wiki/Cauca_Department" title="Cauca Department">Cauca</a> secedes from the central government in protest at the suggestion of increase of presidential powers; <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_Department" title="Magdalena Department">Magdalena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bol%C3%ADvar_Department" title="Bolívar Department">Bolívar</a> join it, <a href="/wiki/Colombian_Civil_War_(1860%E2%80%9362)" title="Colombian Civil War (1860–62)">civil war</a> erupts.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_9" title="May 9">May 9</a> – The U.S. <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Union_Party_(United_States)" title="Constitutional Union Party (United States)">Constitutional Union Party</a> holds its convention and nominates <a href="/wiki/John_Bell_(Tennessee_politician)" title="John Bell (Tennessee politician)">John Bell</a> for <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_15" title="May 15">May 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Calatafimi" title="Battle of Calatafimi">Battle of Calatafimi</a>: Troops under <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a> defeat the army of <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Expedition_of_the_Thousand" title="Expedition of the Thousand">Expedition of the Thousand</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_17" title="May 17">May 17</a> – The German association football club <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TSV_1860_M%C3%BCnchen" title="TSV 1860 München">TSV 1860 München</a></i> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_18" title="May 18">May 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> is selected as the U.S. presidential candidate for the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_27" title="May 27">May 27</a> – Garibaldi's forces take <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a>, the capital of Sicily.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_28" title="May 28">May 28</a> – One of the worst storms ever experienced in the region hits the east coast of England, sinking more than 100 ships and killing at least 40 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>12 June [<a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a> 31 May] 1860 – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Bank_of_the_Russian_Empire" title="State Bank of the Russian Empire">State Bank of the Russian Empire</a> is established.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_30" title="June 30">June 30</a> – The historic <a href="/wiki/1860_Oxford_evolution_debate" title="1860 Oxford evolution debate">debate about evolution</a> is held at the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="Oxford University Museum of Natural History">Oxford University Museum</a>.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_2" title="July 2">July 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a> is founded in Russia.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_9" title="July 9">July 9</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florence_Nightingale_School_of_Nursing_and_Midwifery" title="Florence Nightingale School of Nursing and Midwifery">Nightingale Training School and Home for Nurses</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Nursing_school" title="Nursing school">nursing school</a> based on the ideas of <a href="/wiki/Florence_Nightingale" title="Florence Nightingale">Florence Nightingale</a>, is opened at <a href="/wiki/St_Thomas%27_Hospital" title="St Thomas' Hospital">St Thomas' Hospital</a> in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – Mutsuhito (the future <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a>) becomes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_Prince" title="Crown Prince">Crown Prince</a> of Japan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_20" title="July 20">July 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Milazzo_(1860)" title="Battle of Milazzo (1860)">Battle of Milazzo</a>: The forces of <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a> defeat royal Neapolitan forces near Messina, bringing nearly all of Sicily under Garibaldi's control.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_22" title="August 22">August 22</a> – Assisted by the British navy, the troops of <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a> cross from Sicily to the Italian mainland.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_3" title="September 3">September 3</a>–<a href="/wiki/September_5" title="September 5">September 5</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe_Congress" title="Karlsruhe Congress">First International Chemistry Congress</a> is held in <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe" title="Karlsruhe">Karlsruhe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Baden" title="Grand Duchy of Baden">Baden</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_7" title="September 7">September 7</a>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/PS_Lady_Elgin" title="PS Lady Elgin">PS <i>Lady Elgin</i></a> is accidentally rammed and sunk in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Michigan" title="Lake Michigan">Lake Michigan</a>; hundreds drown.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a>'s forces capture Naples.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_10" title="September 10">September 10</a> – Piedmontese forces invade the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a>, hoping to link up with Garibaldi in Naples.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_18" title="September 18">September 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castelfidardo" title="Battle of Castelfidardo">Battle of Castelfidardo</a>: The Piedmontese decisively defeat the Papal forces, allowing them to continue their march into Neapolitan territory, and effectively reducing the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> to the territory around Rome.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_24" title="September 24">September 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Guayaquil" title="Battle of Guayaquil">Battle of Guayaquil</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuadorian</a> forces led by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Jos%C3%A9_Flores" title="Juan José Flores">Juan José Flores</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Garc%C3%ADa_Moreno" title="Gabriel García Moreno">Gabriel García Moreno</a> take the port of Guayaquil from Supreme Chief Guillermo Franco, who is backed by <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peruvian</a> forces.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October.E2.80.93December">October–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1860&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: October–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>October – <a href="/wiki/John_Hanning_Speke" title="John Hanning Speke">John Hanning Speke</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Augustus_Grant" title="James Augustus Grant">James Augustus Grant</a> leave <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a> to search for the source of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nile_River" title="Nile River">Nile River</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_1" title="October 1">October 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Volturnus_(1860)" title="Battle of Volturnus (1860)">Battle of Volturnus</a>: Garibaldi defeats the last organized army of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Kingdom of the Two Sicilies">Kingdom of the Two Sicilies</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_5" title="October 5">October 5</a> – Austria, Britain, France, <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> form a commission to investigate the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1860_Druze%E2%80%93Maronite_conflict" title="1860 Druze–Maronite conflict">causes of the massacres</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maronite_Christians" title="Maronite Christians">Maronite Christians</a>, committed by <a href="/wiki/Druze" title="Druze">Druzes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> earlier in the year.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_17" title="October 17">October 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/The_Open_Championship" title="The Open Championship">The Open Championship</a>, also known as the British Open, is played for the first time at <a href="/wiki/Prestwick_Golf_Club" title="Prestwick Golf Club">Prestwick Golf Club</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ayrshire" title="Ayrshire">Ayrshire</a>, Scotland. The event is won by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Willie_Park_Sr" title="Willie Park Sr">Willie Park Sr</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_18" title="October 18">October 18</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/Convention_of_Peking" title="Convention of Peking">Convention of Peking</a> formally ends the <a href="/wiki/Second_Opium_War" title="Second Opium War">Second Opium War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_18" title="October 18">October 18</a>–<a href="/wiki/October_21" title="October 21">21</a> – Beijing's <a href="/wiki/Old_Summer_Palace" title="Old Summer Palace">Old Summer Palace</a> is burned to the ground by orders of British general <a href="/wiki/James_Bruce,_8th_Earl_of_Elgin" title="James Bruce, 8th Earl of Elgin">Lord Elgin</a> in retaliation for mistreatment of several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prisoners_of_war" title="Prisoners of war">prisoners of war</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Opium_War" title="Second Opium War">Second Opium War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_19" title="October 19">October 19</a> – A new <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori</a> revolt begins in New Zealand.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_26" title="October 26">October 26</a>
<ul>
<li>Garibaldi again defeats the Neapolitan forces, advancing on <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Gaeta_(1860)" title="Siege of Gaeta (1860)">Gaeta</a>, the last remaining Neapolitan strong-point.</li>
<li>Meeting at <a href="/wiki/Teano" title="Teano">Teano</a>: <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a> gives <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> to the king <a href="/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_II_of_Italy" title="Victor Emmanuel II of Italy">Victor Emmanuel II</a>, recognizing him as <a href="/wiki/King_of_Italy" title="King of Italy">King of Italy</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_3" title="November 3">November 3</a> – The combined forces of <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a> and King <a href="/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_II_of_Italy" title="Victor Emmanuel II of Italy">Victor Emmanuel II</a> besiege King <a href="/wiki/Francis_II_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Francis II of the Two Sicilies">Francis II of the Two Sicilies</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gaeta" title="Gaeta">Gaeta</a>, his last remaining stronghold.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_6" title="November 6">November 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1860" title="United States presidential election, 1860">U.S. presidential election</a>: <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> beats <a href="/wiki/John_C._Breckinridge" title="John C.
