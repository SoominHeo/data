<p><b>OGLE-2006-BLG-109L</b> (where 'L' stands for lens) is a dim <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a> 17 M0V <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galactic_bulge" title="Galactic bulge">galactic bulge</a> <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> approximately 4,920 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> away in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scorpius_(constellation)" title="Scorpius (constellation)">Scorpius</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIMBAD_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-SIMBAD-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Planetary_system">Planetary system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=OGLE-2006-BLG-109L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Planetary system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">extrasolar planets</a> were discovered around the star using <a href="/wiki/Gravitational_microlensing" title="Gravitational microlensing">gravitational microlensing</a>. The two planets are at a distance from their star that make them suspected analogs of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaudi2008_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gaudi2008-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:OGLE-2006-BLG-109_scheme2.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="166" data-file-width="266" height="137" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/OGLE-2006-BLG-109_scheme2.png/220px-OGLE-2006-BLG-109_scheme2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/59/OGLE-2006-BLG-109_scheme2.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:OGLE-2006-BLG-109_scheme2.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A scheme of the planetary system.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The star is surrounded by a <a href="/wiki/Planetary_system" title="Planetary system">planetary system</a> consisting of at least two planets: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OGLE-2006-BLG-109L_b" title="OGLE-2006-BLG-109L b">b</a> with a mass of 0.727 of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OGLE-2006-BLG-109L_c" title="OGLE-2006-BLG-109L c">c</a> with the mass of approximately 0.271 of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>. Their mass ratios, distance ratios, and equilibrium temperatures are similar to those of Jupiter and <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">solar system</a> as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/47_UMa" title="47 UMa">47 UMa</a> system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaudi2008_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gaudi2008-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Both planets were discovered simultaneously by <a href="/wiki/Gravitational_microlensing" title="Gravitational microlensing">gravitational microlensing</a> in a common effort by the <a href="/wiki/Optical_Gravitational_Lensing_Experiment" title="Optical Gravitational Lensing Experiment">Optical Gravitational Lensing Experiment</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.astronomy.ohio-state.edu/~microfun/" rel="nofollow">microFUN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microlensing_Observations_in_Astrophysics" title="Microlensing Observations in Astrophysics">MOA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Probing_Lensing_Anomalies_Network" title="Probing Lensing Anomalies Network">PLANET</a> and <a href="/wiki/RoboNet" title="RoboNet">RoboNet</a> collaborations, as announced on 14 February 2008. This is the first planetary system where more than one planet was detected using gravitational microlensing.</p>
