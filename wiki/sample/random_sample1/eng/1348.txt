<p>"<b>Oh!</b>" is the lead single from <i><a href="/wiki/Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_album)" title="Oh! (Girls' Generation album)">Oh!</a></i>, the second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>. The single was digitally released on January 25, 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digital_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-digital-2">[2]</a></sup> and came to be a huge hit song in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. The group used a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheerleader" title="Cheerleader">cheerleader</a> concept for the song's promotional activities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The song hit #1 on various charts, and won several times on the Korean music chart shows <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">The Music Trend</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-top-4">[4]</a></sup> The song ranked #4 on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Chart" title="Gaon Chart">Gaon Chart</a> Top 10 Digital Songs of the Year</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and it was also the second best selling single in that year, selling over 3.3 million copies. Music site <i>Monkey3</i> named "Oh!" the best song in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Oh!" was remade as a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> song for the group's second single titled, "Oh!", from their second Japanese album <i><a href="/wiki/Girls_%26_Peace" title="Girls &amp; Peace">Girls &amp; Peace</a></i>.. It was their fifth <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> single. The music video for "Oh!" was released on September 14, 2012. Promotion for the single began on <a href="/wiki/Hey!_Hey!_Hey!_Music_Champ" title="Hey! Hey! Hey! Music Champ">Hey! Hey! Hey! Music Champ</a>. The single ranked highly among Japanese music charts, clinching the top spot on the Oricon Daily and Weekly single chart as well as the top spot on the Japanese Hot 100. The single was released with the song, "All My Love Is for You", on September 26, 2012, with an accompanying music video premiering on September 14, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9.E6.9C.8826.E6.97.A5.E6.B0.B4.E7.99.BA.E5.A3.B2.E3.81.AE.E8.A1.A8.E9.A1.8C.E6.9B.B2.E3.81.8C.E3.80.8COh.21.E3.80.8D.E3.81.AB.E6.B1.BA.E5.AE.9A.EF.BC.81_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-9.E6.9C.8826.E6.97.A5.E6.B0.B4.E7.99.BA.E5.A3.B2.E3.81.AE.E8.A1.A8.E9.A1.8C.E6.9B.B2.E3.81.8C.E3.80.8COh.21.E3.80.8D.E3.81.AB.E6.B1.BA.E5.AE.9A.EF.BC.81-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Akino_Makoto_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Akino_Makoto-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Kenzie, a songwriter who previously composed the group's debut single "<a href="/wiki/Into_the_New_World_(song)" title="Into the New World (song)">Into the New World</a>", also composed "Oh!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Oh!" was released to digital music sites on January 25, 2010. The song quickly topped various digital music charts within 10 minutes of release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digital_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-digital-2">[2]</a></sup> Moreover, the song reached #1 on Gaon Music Chart<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_version">Japanese version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Japanese version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 10, 2012, it was announced the group was to release their next Japanese single after the success of their previous single "<a href="/wiki/Paparazzi_(Girls%27_Generation_song)" title="Paparazzi (Girls' Generation song)">Paparazzi</a>", in the following month on September 26, 2012. The single was announced with no confirmation of title tracks, however fans were allowed to pre-order the regular, and limited editions of the single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> After several weeks of speculation, the single's title track, a Japanese remake of the group's popular 2010 Korean hit, "Oh!" was finally announced on September 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The accompanying music video to "Oh!" was released on September 14, 2012, along with an exclusive ringtone which was released at 5AM JST.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Korean_version_2">Korean version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Korean version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The group worked with long-time collaborator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rino_Nakasone_Razalan" title="Rino Nakasone Razalan">Rino Nakasone Razalan</a>, whom they had previously worked on for the group's previous single, "<a href="/wiki/Tell_Me_Your_Wish_(Genie)_(song)" title="Tell Me Your Wish (Genie) (song)">Tell Me Your Wish (Genie)</a>" on the choreography for "Oh!", and director Cheo Soo-hyun. A teaser video was released to various websites on January 23, 2010, causing at least one of them – <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver</a> – to be overwhelmed by high visitor traffic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The full video was released on January 27, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Eventually, the dance version of "Oh!" was released on <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a>'s <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel. As of December 2016, the song's official music video has attracted over 100 million views on YouTube, becoming the group's fifth music video to do so following "Gee", "I Got A Boy", "The Boys", and "Mr. Taxi".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The girls wear specific numbers in the music video; each number was personally chosen by the members. In the story version, it is shown that they were cheerleaders for an <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> team. In the middle of the video, Sooyoung mishandles an American football helmet (which if checked, shows the logo of the University of Iowa Hawkeyes) causing the computer monitor to be damaged (it was the main highlight of their "<a href="/wiki/Run_Devil_Run_(Girls%27_Generation_song)" title="Run Devil Run (Girls' Generation song)">Run Devil Run</a>" story video because when the helmet hits the monitor, it causes the Black SoShi to emerge from the monitor). At the end of the video, the girls meet the Black SoShi, their malevolent selves from a parallel universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_version_2">Japanese version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Japanese version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Japanese version of "Oh!" was released on September 14, 2012. A dance version for "Oh!" was released on September 18, 2012.</p>
<p>The "Oh!" video shares similarities with its Korean counterpart, however, instead of being a High School oriented video, the video is shot as if the girls have now entered College, implying their growth as artists and women. The video begins with the camera panning through their College dorm, all of the members are seen reading, except for <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Jung" title="Jessica Jung">Jessica</a> who is playing the Ukele, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Hyo-yeon" title="Kim Hyo-yeon">Hyoyeon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seohyun" title="Seohyun">Seohyun</a> who are playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock-paper-scissors" title="Rock-paper-scissors">Rock-paper-scissors</a>. The camera then focuses on a cheerleading jacket with "Girls' Generation" written on it, as well as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Football" title="American Football">football</a> helmet with "GG" written on the side and a decorated cake with "Oh!" written in decorative writing. It then cuts to Tiffany, who says "Go!" and the girls quickly file out into a red-colored changing room where they begin to sing and dance. The video cuts between this and a stadium setting where the group perform as cheerleaders, as well as solo shots of each of the individual members dressed in their cheerleading uniforms. The video ends with the girls entering back into their dorm, taking off their blue cheer jackets. Taeyeon walks towards the camera with her jacket and puts the jacket over it. The final scene is the girls' empty dorm with the camera focusing on a picture on the wall of the group.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Oh!" continued the success of its predecessor and ranked highly on Japanese music charts, after ranking first on the Top Songs and Top Singles on the Japanese iTunes store, the single managed to clench the top spot on <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>'s Daily Single chart, where it sold close to 30,000 in its first day, with 29,666 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> After a strong first day of sales, the single debuted atop Oricon's Weekly Single chart, selling 66,000 copies in its first week. Despite only having a week of sales, "Oh!" managed to peak inside the top 10 at #8 on Oricon's Monthly Single chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The single managed to reach the top spot on Billboard Japan's Hot 100 and Hot Single Sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard-japan.com_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-japan.com-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-22">[22]</a></sup> However, fairing less well on radio, with the single only peaking at #27 on Top Airplay chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-23">[23]</a></sup> and #38 on the Adult Contemporary Airplay chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceC_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceC-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Korean_version_3">Korean version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Korean version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The song was first performed live on January 30, 2010 on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>'s <i>Music Core</i>, as part of their <i>Comeback Stage</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> However, there was a technical error in MBC's broadcast, with a few seconds of <a href="/wiki/Dead_air" title="Dead air">dead air</a> near the end of the performance; the network was subsequently flooded with complaints.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The incident was then parodied on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, mixing in clips of the Korean drama <i><a href="/wiki/Iris_(TV_series)" title="Iris (TV series)">IRIS</a></i>, receiving attention from Korean <a href="/wiki/Netizen" title="Netizen">netizens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group followed up with their second performance on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">The Music Trend</a></i> the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>On their first <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i> comeback stage, they won the "K-Chart!", beating <a href="/wiki/CNBLUE" title="CNBLUE">CNBLUE</a> and <a href="/wiki/2AM_(band)" title="2AM (band)">2AM</a> while at the same time breaking the record for the most points on the chart with 23,077 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_version_3">Japanese version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oh!_(Girls%27_Generation_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Japanese version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The group was set to perform the single for the first time on September 24, 2012, on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuji_TV" title="Fuji TV">Fuji TV</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Hey!_Hey!_Hey!_Music_Champ" title="Hey! Hey! Hey! Music Champ">Hey! Hey! Hey! Music Champ</a>, which marked their sixth appearance on the show. The group donned pink cheerleading outfits, with knee high boots for the performance of "Oh!" as well as a special performance of "<a href="/wiki/Mr._Taxi" title="Mr. Taxi">Mr. Taxi</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The group also participated in a "Special Program" in anticipation of the single's release, with three Japanese music websites airing performances of the single across three consecutive nights; September 25, 26 and 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On September 29, the group performed "Oh!" on <a href="/wiki/Count_Down_TV" title="Count Down TV">Count Down TV</a>, dressed in white cheerleading uniforms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The group pre-recorded a performance for NHK-TV's <i>Music Japan</i> prior to the single's release and was scheduled to be broadcast on September 30, however due to a typhoon, the broadcast was delayed. The performance was shown on October 6, with the group performing in blue cheerleading outfits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Promotion for the single continued on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">Nippon Television</a>’s “Music Lovers", where the group recorded a performance on September 25 for the show that was broadcast on October 8, 2012. The group performed "Oh!", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_My_Love_Is_for_You" title="All My Love Is for You">All My Love Is for You</a>" and "Mr. Taxi".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
