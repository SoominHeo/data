<p><i><b>Only Human</b></i> is the fourth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by English recording artist <a href="/wiki/Cheryl_(entertainer)" title="Cheryl (entertainer)">Cheryl</a>. It was released on 7 November 2014, through <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a>. Following a slew of media invasion, Cheryl took a one-year hiatus from music. Following her hiatus, Cheryl confirmed that Girls Aloud would reunite and release a greatest hits compilation. After its release, the group embarked on their last tour and announced their permanent split. In July 2013 Cheryl began recording <i>Only Human</i>; the recording was later completed in August 2014.</p>
<p><i>Only Human</i> explores themes of being at peace with oneself, banishing bad influences and living life to the full. Musically, the album follows in the same vein as Cheryl's previous work, taking influence from pop and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, however the album contains a diverse musical style ranging from the 1980s, disco-pop to <a href="/wiki/Electro_(music)" title="Electro (music)">electro</a>. The album's production is characterized as being sparsely-produced. Cheryl worked with a variety of writers and producers including frequent collaborators and new ones to create her desired sound, she also contributed a large amount of writing herself.</p>
<p>The album was preceded by lead single "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_Stupid_Love_(song)" title="Crazy Stupid Love (song)">Crazy Stupid Love</a>", which featured British rapper <a href="/wiki/Tinie_Tempah" title="Tinie Tempah">Tinie Tempah</a>, released on 18 July 2014. The song debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, selling 118,000 copies in its first week, becoming Cole's fourth number one single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cole_Lands_Fourth_Number_One_.28BBC_News.29_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cole_Lands_Fourth_Number_One_.28BBC_News.29-1">[1]</a></sup> The album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(Cheryl_song)" title="I Don't Care (Cheryl song)">I Don't Care</a>", was released before the album on 31 October 2014 and became Cheryl's fifth number one making chart history as she is the first ever British female solo artist to have five number ones in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Talks_New_Single_.28DS_August_2014.29_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Talks_New_Single_.28DS_August_2014.29-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>Only Human</i> entered the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> at number seven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Debut_Position_.28OOC_November_2014.29_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Debut_Position_.28OOC_November_2014.29-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Human_(Cheryl_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cheryl released her third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Million_Lights" title="A Million Lights">A Million Lights</a></i>, on 18 June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Album_.28DSpy_April_2012.29_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Album_.28DSpy_April_2012.29-4">[4]</a></sup> The album was supported with the release of the lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Call_My_Name_(Cheryl_song)" title="Call My Name (Cheryl song)">Call My Name</a>" (2012), which became Cheryl's third number one single on the UK Singles Chart, with the sales of 152,001 digital copies. The song also became 2012's fastest selling number one single on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> until December of the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Best_Selling_Number_One_.28OCC_June_2012.29_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Best_Selling_Number_One_.28OCC_June_2012.29-5">[5]</a></sup> During this time, Cheryl also embarked on her first solo concert tour, <a href="/wiki/A_Million_Lights_Tour" title="A Million Lights Tour">A Million Lights Tour</a>, which started on 3 October 2012 and concluded on 17 October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Announces_First_Solo_Tour_.28DMail_June_2012.29_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Announces_First_Solo_Tour_.28DMail_June_2012.29-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following months of speculation, Cheryl confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Girls_Aloud" title="Girls Aloud">Girls Aloud</a>'s reunion would occur in November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GA_Announce_Reunion_.28HPost_October_2012.29_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GA_Announce_Reunion_.28HPost_October_2012.29-7">[7]</a></sup> To celebrate their tenth anniversary, Girls Aloud reunited for the release of their second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_hits" title="Greatest hits">greatest hits</a> compilation, <i><a href="/wiki/Ten_(Girls_Aloud_album)" title="Ten (Girls Aloud album)">Ten</a></i> (2012). In March 2013, following the completion of the <a href="/wiki/Ten:_The_Hits_Tour" title="Ten: The Hits Tour">Ten: The Hits Tour</a>, the group released a statement, confirming their permanent split.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Girls_Aloud_Announce_Split_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Girls_Aloud_Announce_Split-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>In July 2013, Cheryl uploaded a picture of a music studio to her official <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> with the caption: "Back with my first true love", hinting that new material could potentially be on its way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Back_to_Music_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Back_to_Music-9">[9]</a></sup> In September 2013, it was reported in that Cheryl might collaborate with former Girls Aloud member <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Roberts" title="Nicola Roberts">Nicola Roberts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_and_Nicola_.28Mirror.29_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_and_Nicola_.28Mirror.29-10">[10]</a></sup> Cheryl revealed she had recorded a hip-hop song, co-written by Bellion, who had recently worked with <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>; producer <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Shatkin" title="Jesse Shatkin">Jesse Shatkin</a> confirmed the collaboration during an interview with MuuMuse, and also revealed that Cheryl had worked on a new ballad, saying, "It's strong, it's powerful and really from the heart."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eminem_Songwriter_.28DS_March_2014.29_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eminem_Songwriter_.28DS_March_2014.29-11">[11]</a></sup> In June 2014, Cheryl revealed that Australian <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sia_Furler" title="Sia Furler">Sia Furler</a>, had written a couple of songs for <i>Only Human</i>. Cheryl commented, "The song, when I first heard it, was just full of personality. The girl is a genius. I'm just glad I got the chance to work with her."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sia_Two_Songs_.28Clickmusic_June_2014.29_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sia_Two_Songs_.28Clickmusic_June_2014.29-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the recording of the album Cheryl contributed to the album's writing, including: "Bombs", "One Love", "We Could All Be Stars", "Throwback" and "Coming Up for Air"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Penning_New_Album_Tracks_.28Belfast_Telegraph_July_2014.29_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Penning_New_Album_Tracks_.28Belfast_Telegraph_July_2014.29-13">[13]</a></sup> <i>Only Human</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s lead single was originally recorded by <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a> for her twelfth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_Me_Once" title="Kiss Me Once">Kiss Me Once</a></i> (2014). She worked with Wilkins on the track and wanted it to be used on the album, but Minogue's management <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a> did not like it because it was "too pop". Wilkins later gave the track to Cheryl to record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kylie_Crazy_Stupid_Love_.28Metro_August_2014.29_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kylie_Crazy_Stupid_Love_.28Metro_August_2014.29-14">[14]</a></sup> Cheryl also worked with producers, such as <a href="/wiki/Greg_Kurstin" title="Greg Kurstin">Greg Kurstin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Invisible_Men" title="Invisible Men">Invisible Men</a> on songs for the album, as well as new producers, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Triangle_(Red_Triangle_Productions)" title="Red Triangle (Red Triangle Productions)">Red Triangle</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric" title="Electric">Electric</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Feisty_New_Single_.28DMail_September_2014.29_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Feisty_New_Single_.28DMail_September_2014.29-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Human_(Cheryl_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album's lyrics explore themes of being at peace with oneself, banishing bad influences and living life to the full.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28Entertainmentwise_November_2014.29_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28Entertainmentwise_November_2014.29-16">[16]</a></sup> Musically, the album is a mid-tempo pop and R&amp;B album with electro leanings that explores a variety of genres ranging from 80s style disco-pop to sparsely-produced contemporary songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28So_So_Gay_November_2014.29_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28So_So_Gay_November_2014.29-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29-18">[18]</a></sup> <i>Only Human</i> opens was an introduction of a speech from the philosopher <a href="/wiki/Alan_Watts" title="Alan Watts">Alan Watts</a>, the speech sees Watts discussing a profound dialogue that money doesn't equal happiness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29-19">[19]</a></sup> "Live Life Now" is an electronic dance song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29-18">[18]</a></sup> Like the introduction, "Live Life Now" continues to sample Watts speech, while Cheryl sings lyrics about money and desires.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29-19">[19]</a></sup> "It's About Time" lyrical revolves around Cheryl throwing "barbs" and a former lover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29-18">[18]</a></sup> "Crazy Stupid Love" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and dancey <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_pop" title="Urban pop">urban pop</a> song built on a toe-tapping structure with plenty of <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophones</a> and a “la la la la la” refrain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Announces_New_Single_.28Idolator_May_2014.29_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Announces_New_Single_.28Idolator_May_2014.29-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSL_Review_.28RFS_July_2014.29_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-CSL_Review_.28RFS_July_2014.29-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSL_Lyric_Video_.28Idolator_May_2014.29_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-CSL_Lyric_Video_.28Idolator_May_2014.29-22">[22]</a></sup> Lyrically, the song has Cheryl gushing about her uncontrollable feelings for her boyfriend, with lyrics like.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>"I Don't Care" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> song which lyrically finds Cole singing "It feels so fucking good to say, I swear that I don't care".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Is_Unbothered_.28Idolator_October_2014.29_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Is_Unbothered_.28Idolator_October_2014.29-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sweary_New_Single_.28HPost_October_2014.29_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sweary_New_Single_.28HPost_October_2014.29-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Idolator_(website)" title="Idolator (website)">Idolator</a> website editor Bianca Gracie noted that the song carries "an 80s carefree vibe to it, thanks to the breezy synths and pumping bass line", and incontrast to her other songs like "<a href="/wiki/Ghetto_Baby" title="Ghetto Baby">Ghetto Baby</a>", there are no "massive bass drops or an in-your-face dance theme".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Is_Unbothered_.28Idolator_October_2014.29_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Is_Unbothered_.28Idolator_October_2014.29-23">[23]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Only_Human_(Cheryl_song)" title="Only Human (Cheryl song)">Only Human</a>" is an electro-ballad, with vocals layered in the style of <a href="/wiki/Imogen_Heap" title="Imogen Heap">Imogen Heap</a> according to Bradley Stern of <a href="/wiki/Idolator_(website)" title="Idolator (website)">Idolator</a>, the song contains encouraging lyrics built over a glittering production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Unveils_Title_Track_.28Idolator_October_2014.29_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Unveils_Title_Track_.28Idolator_October_2014.29-25">[25]</a></sup> “Stars” is a song Cheryl wrote for her fans, the songs lyrics are "inspiring" described by Stern of Idolator as "bright and self-empowering", "Stars" is an electronic song backed by shimmering and stomping production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stars_.28Idolator_November_2014.29_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stars_.28Idolator_November_2014.29-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Bradley Stern of <a href="/wiki/MuuMuse" title="MuuMuse">MuuMuse</a>, “All In One Night” is a song described as is "a slow-burning R&amp;B song" that is compared to Cheryl's song "<a href="/wiki/Ghetto_Baby" title="Ghetto Baby">Ghetto Baby</a>" from her third studio album, <i>A Million Lights</i> (2012). “Coming Up For Air" featuring <a href="/wiki/Joel_Compass" title="Joel Compass">Joel Compass</a>, is described as the album's most "adventurous" song and "carries just the vaguest hint of the creeping, skeletal production of BANKS and <a href="/wiki/FKA_Twigs" title="FKA Twigs">FKA Twigs</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28Muumuse_November_2014.29_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28Muumuse_November_2014.29-27">[27]</a></sup> “Fight On” is backed by a choir and pounding percussion, with ethereal, tribal beats and lyrically sees Cheryl telling the story of a girl seeking sanctuary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Debut_Song_Fight_On_.28Idolator_November_2014.29_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Debut_Song_Fight_On_.28Idolator_November_2014.29-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Human_(Cheryl_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During an appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Graham_Norton_Show" title="The Graham Norton Show">The Graham Norton Show</a></i>, Cheryl confirmed the album's title to be <i>Only Human</i>, stating: "I was exhausted, I’d had a solo tour and then toured with Girls Aloud so I took a year off to be with my family. It was the first time off I’d had in 10 years – it was a normal time. I did party a lot. I needed to let loose and it was the best thing I could do. The title sums up that time."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Title_.28MTV_News_June_2014.29_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Title_.28MTV_News_June_2014.29-29">[29]</a></sup> On 3 October 2014, the official album artwork for <i>Only Human</i> was unveiled in both a standard and deluxe edition. While both covers featured the same headshot of Cheryl, the editions differed in their design aspects; the standard cover shows the singer with <a href="/wiki/Leopard" title="Leopard">leopard</a> print makeup around her left eye, while the deluxe shows a stripe across her left eye that is torn away, to reveal a face of a leopard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Cover_.28DS_October_2014.29_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Cover_.28DS_October_2014.29-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cheryl and Tempah performed "Crazy Stupid Love" for the first time on the final of the <a href="/wiki/Britain%27s_Got_Talent_(series_8)" title="Britain's Got Talent (series 8)">eighth series</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Britain%27s_Got_Talent" title="Britain's Got Talent">Britain's Got Talent</a></i> on 7 June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BGT_Comeback_Performance_.28DStar_May_2014.29_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-BGT_Comeback_Performance_.28DStar_May_2014.29-31">[31]</a></sup> Cole appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Graham_Norton_Show" title="The Graham Norton Show">The Graham Norton Show</a></i> on 20 June, and also performed the song as part of her set at <a href="/wiki/Summertime_Ball" title="Summertime Ball">Capital FM's Summertime Ball</a> the following day, 21 June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cole_on_Graham_Norton_.28DSpy_June_2014.29_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cole_on_Graham_Norton_.28DSpy_June_2014.29-32">[32]</a></sup> As part of the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> pre-order, Cheryl allowed a download of the title track, "Only Human", for those who pre-ordered the album. Bradley Stern of <a href="/wiki/Idolator_(website)" title="Idolator (website)">Idolator</a> called the track an "impressive ballad" and also called the track "one of her best offerings from her back catalog".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Unveils_Title_Track_.28Idolator_October_2014.29_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Unveils_Title_Track_.28Idolator_October_2014.29-25">[25]</a></sup> Cheryl also performed on the live results show during the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_11)" title="The X Factor (UK series 11)">eleventh series</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-She.27s_Only_Human_.28DMail_October_2014.29_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-She.27s_Only_Human_.28DMail_October_2014.29-33">[33]</a></sup> On 4 November 2014, the track "Stars" was put on iTunes as part of a pre-order of the album. Stern of Idolator called the song a "bright and self-empowering number".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stars_.28Idolator_November_2014.29_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stars_.28Idolator_November_2014.29-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Human_(Cheryl_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2014, Cheryl announced the album's lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_Stupid_Love_(song)" title="Crazy Stupid Love (song)">Crazy Stupid Love</a>", for release on 18 July 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Announces_New_Single_.28Idolator_May_2014.29_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Announces_New_Single_.28Idolator_May_2014.29-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSL_Lyric_Video_.28Idolator_May_2014.29_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-CSL_Lyric_Video_.28Idolator_May_2014.29-22">[22]</a></sup> The single features rapper <a href="/wiki/Tinie_Tempah" title="Tinie Tempah">Tinie Tempah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Premieres_New_Single_.28DS_June_2014.29_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Premieres_New_Single_.28DS_June_2014.29-34">[34]</a></sup> The single debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, securing the singer her fourth consecutive number one lead single from an album. On 30 September 2014, Cheryl announced that "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(Cheryl_song)" title="I Don't Care (Cheryl song)">I Don't Care</a>" would receive its radio debut on the following day, 1 October on <a href="/wiki/Capital_(radio_network)" title="Capital (radio network)">Capital FM Radio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Announces_New_Single_.28DS_September_2014.29_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Announces_New_Single_.28DS_September_2014.29-35">[35]</a></sup> The song was co-written by <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_McKee" title="Bonnie McKee">Bonnie McKee</a>, Jocke Ahlund, John Newman and Cheryl, was released on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> on 31 October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Listen_to_New_Single_.28DS_October_2014.29_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Listen_to_New_Single_.28DS_October_2014.29-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Listen_to_New_Single_.28MTV_October_2014.29_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Listen_to_New_Single_.28MTV_October_2014.29-37">[37]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Idolator_(website)" title="Idolator (website)">Idolator.com</a> editor Bianca Gracie described the song as, "a breezy, upbeat pop song built over a 80s production, breezy synths and a pumping bass".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheryl_Is_Unbothered_.28Idolator_October_2014.29_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cheryl_Is_Unbothered_.28Idolator_October_2014.29-23">[23]</a></sup> The song debuted at the top of the U.K. charts, setting a new record for most number-one singles by a British female solo artist for Cheryl, with five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fifth_Number_One_.28OCC_November_2014.29_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fifth_Number_One_.28OCC_November_2014.29-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fifth_Number_One_.28DSpy_November_2014.29_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fifth_Number_One_.28DSpy_November_2014.29-39">[39]</a></sup> On 1 February 2015, Cheryl announced on Twitter that the third single to be lifted from the album would be the title track, "Only Human".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Single_.28Capital_February_2015.29_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Single_.28Capital_February_2015.29-40">[40]</a></sup> The official music video made its premiere on <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> on 4 February, with an official digital release, featuring a new radio edit of the track by <a href="/wiki/Wilkinson_(musician)" title="Wilkinson (musician)">Wilkinson</a>, released on 22 March 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-She.27s_Only_Human_.28DM_February_2015.29_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-She.27s_Only_Human_.28DM_February_2015.29-41">[41]</a></sup> The song, which originally peaked at number 70 as an album cut in October 2014, failed to reach the top 100 on the UK Singles Chart upon the single's release, therefore becoming the lowest-charting single of her career, surpassing 2011's "<a href="/wiki/The_Flood_(Cheryl_song)" title="The Flood (Cheryl song)">The Flood</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chronicle_Live_1_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chronicle_Live_1-42">[42]</a></sup></p>

<p>Upon its release, <i>Only Human</i> received mixed reviews. Cheryl gave <a href="/wiki/Dan_Wootton" title="Dan Wootton">Dan Wootton</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i> an exclusive first listen of her album, who described the new collection as "brilliant" and "her best work".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28The_Sun_October_2014.29_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28The_Sun_October_2014.29-47">[47]</a></sup> Adrian Thrills of <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i> gave the album a mixed review, awarding it three out of five stars. Thrills stated that Cheryl had shown artistic growth during the course of her career and that the album is "much more expressive" than her previous work. He noted songs such as "It's About Time" and "Throwback" as examples, with lyrical connotations linking closely to Cole's personal life and divorce from footballer <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Cole" title="Ashley Cole">Ashley Cole</a>. However, Thrills also noted how "her vocal limitations are exposed on ill-advised detours into R&amp;B. All In One Night demands a swagger that Cheryl doesn't possess". They also said the album is "proof Cheryl isn't a mime act". The article praised songs "Coming Up For Air" which features guest vocals from Joel Compass, "Goodbye Means Hello" and "Fight On".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29_18-4"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DMail_November_2014.29-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> editor Lewis Corner gave <i>Only Human</i> a predominately positive review, calling the album "one of her most focused and cohesive records to date". Corner criticized, however, that some songs should not have made it onto the album ("Beats N Bass") and that they "never really peaks the way it should"( "Coming Up for Air"); he even called one track "thin and limp" ("Throwback").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29_19-5"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28DSpy_November_2014.29-19">[19]</a></sup> The website <a href="/wiki/Gigwise" title="Gigwise">Entertainmentwise</a> praised the singer's latest release, calling it "most cohesive collection' to date"; they even praised such songs as "I Don't Care", "Waiting For Lightning" and the title track, explaining that the latter had "affecting vulnerability that nicely counterbalances the brash confidence found elsewhere".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28Entertainmentwise_November_2014.29_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28Entertainmentwise_November_2014.29-16">[16]</a></sup> Brendon Veevers of Renownedforsound.com described the album as a "strong collection", citing that the album had "promise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28RFS_November_2014.29_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28RFS_November_2014.29-50">[50]</a></sup> Ed Potton of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> gives a largely positive review, citing Alan Watts and "the importance of doing what you love" as the driving force behind songs like "Live Life Now". Potton goes on to say "she's good at it too", especially on "Stars", which he calls "a soaring anthem to be".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Review_.28The_Times_November_2014.29_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Review_.28The_Times_November_2014.29-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Human_(Cheryl_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Only Human</i> debuted at number seven in the United Kingdom, becoming her lowest charting album to date, with a reported 20,000 units sold in the first week of release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_Debut_Position_.28OOC_November_2014.29_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Album_Debut_Position_.28OOC_November_2014.29-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Disappointing_Sales_.28TGJ_November_2014.29_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-Disappointing_Sales_.28TGJ_November_2014.29-52">[52]</a></sup> The album also debuted at number nine in Ireland, also becoming her lowest charting album to date in that territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Irishchartpeak_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-Irishchartpeak-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
