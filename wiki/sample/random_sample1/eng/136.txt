<p><b>1868 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCLXVIII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap year</a> <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Wednesday" title="Leap year starting on Wednesday">starting on Wednesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> ED) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Leap year starting on Monday">leap year starting on Monday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> GF) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1868th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 868th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 68th year of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, and the 9th year of the <a href="/wiki/1860s" title="1860s">1860s</a> decade. As of the start of 1868, the Gregorian calendar was 12 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1868&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93March">January–March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1868&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Meiji_Emperor.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="436" height="252" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Meiji_Emperor.jpg/220px-Meiji_Emperor.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Meiji_Emperor.jpg/330px-Meiji_Emperor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/73/Meiji_Emperor.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Meiji_Emperor.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/January_3" title="January 3">January 3</a>: <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_2" title="January 2">January 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/British_Expedition_to_Abyssinia" title="British Expedition to Abyssinia">British Expedition to Abyssinia</a>: <a href="/wiki/Robert_Napier,_1st_Baron_Napier_of_Magdala" title="Robert Napier, 1st Baron Napier of Magdala">Robert Napier</a> leads an expedition to free captive British officials and missionaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pocket_On_This_Day_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_On_This_Day-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_3" title="January 3">January 3</a> – The 15-year-old Mutsuhito, <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a> of Japan, declares the "<a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>", his own restoration to full power, under the influence of supporters from the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Domain" title="Chōshū Domain">Chōshū</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Domain" title="Satsuma Domain">Satsuma Domains</a> and against the supporters of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>, triggering the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_5" title="January 5">January 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_War" title="Paraguayan War">Paraguayan War</a>: Brazilian Army commander <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Alves_de_Lima_e_Silva,_Duke_of_Caxias" title="Luís Alves de Lima e Silva, Duke of Caxias">Luís Alves de Lima e Silva, Duke of Caxias</a> enters <a href="/wiki/Asunci%C3%B3n" title="Asunción">Asunción</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>'s capital. Some days later he declares the war is over. Nevertheless, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Solano_L%C3%B3pez" title="Francisco Solano López">Francisco Solano López</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>'s president, prepares guerrillas to fight in the countryside.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_7" title="January 7">January 7</a> – Arkansas constitutional convention meets in <a href="/wiki/Little_Rock,_Arkansas" title="Little Rock, Arkansas">Little Rock</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_9" title="January 9">January 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Penal_transportation" title="Penal transportation">Penal transportation</a> from <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a> to <a href="/wiki/Convicts_in_Australia" title="Convicts in Australia">Australia</a> ends with arrival of the <a href="/wiki/Convict_ship" title="Convict ship">convict ship</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hougoumont_(ship)" title="Hougoumont (ship)">Hougoumont</a></i> in Western Australia after an 89-day voyage from England. There are 62 <a href="/wiki/Fenian" title="Fenian">Fenians</a> among the transportees.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_10" title="January 10">January 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">Shogun</a> <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Yoshinobu" title="Tokugawa Yoshinobu">Tokugawa Yoshinobu</a> declares the emperor's declaration "illegal" and prepares to attack <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_27" title="January 27">January 27</a>–<a href="/wiki/January_31" title="January 31">31</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toba%E2%80%93Fushimi" title="Battle of Toba–Fushimi">Battle of Toba–Fushimi</a>: forces of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> and the allied pro-Imperial forces of the Chōshū, Satsuma and <a href="/wiki/Tosa_Domain" title="Tosa Domain">Tosa Domains</a> clash near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fushimi,_Kyoto" title="Fushimi, Kyoto">Fushimi, Kyoto</a>, ending in a decisive victory for the Imperial forces (although in the <a href="/wiki/January_28" title="January 28">January 28</a> naval <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Awa" title="Battle of Awa">Battle of Awa</a> the Shogunate is victorious against Satsuma).</li>
<li>February – Foreign ministers meeting in <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Hyōgo</a> are persuaded to recognise the restored <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a> of Japan with promises that harbours will be open in accordance with international treaties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_13" title="February 13">February 13</a> – The British <a href="/wiki/War_Office" title="War Office">War Office</a> sanctions the formation of what becomes the <a href="/wiki/Post_Office_Rifles" title="Post Office Rifles">Army Post Office Corps</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_16" title="February 16">February 16</a> – In New York City the Jolly Corks organization is renamed the <i><a href="/wiki/Benevolent_and_Protective_Order_of_Elks" title="Benevolent and Protective Order of Elks">Benevolent and Protective Order of Elks</a></i> (BPOE).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_24" title="February 24">February 24</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Impeachment_of_Andrew_Johnson" title="Impeachment of Andrew Johnson">Impeachment of Andrew Johnson</a>: Three days after his action to dismiss <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_War" title="United States Secretary of War">United States Secretary of War</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edwin_M._Stanton" title="Edwin M. Stanton">Edwin M. Stanton</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> votes 126 to 47 in favor of a resolution to <a href="/wiki/Impeachment_in_the_United_States" title="Impeachment in the United States">impeach</a> <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson" title="Andrew Johnson">Andrew Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, the first of two Presidents to be impeached by the full House. Johnson is later acquitted by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>.</li>
<li>The first parade to have floats takes place at <a href="/wiki/Mardi_Gras" title="Mardi Gras">Mardi Gras</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>March – French <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">geologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Louis_Lartet" title="Louis Lartet">Louis Lartet</a> discovers the first identified skeletons of <a href="/wiki/Cro-Magnon" title="Cro-Magnon">Cro-Magnon</a>, the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_modern_humans" title="Early modern humans">early modern humans</a> (early <i>Homo sapiens sapiens</i>), at Abri de Crô-Magnon, a rock shelter at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_Eyzies" title="Les Eyzies">Les Eyzies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dordogne" title="Dordogne">Dordogne</a>, France.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_12" title="March 12">March 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Alfred,_Duke_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Alfred, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Alfred, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Duke of Edinburgh">Duke of Edinburgh</a>, is shot in the back in Sydney, Australia, at a fundraising event for the Sydney Sailors Home by Irishman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_James_O%27Farrell" title="Henry James O'Farrell">Henry James O'Farrell</a>. The prince survives and quickly recovers; O'Farrell is executed on April 21 despite attempts by the prince to gain clemency for him.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_23" title="March 23">March 23</a> – The <a href="/wiki/University_of_California" title="University of California">University of California</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>, when the Organic Act is signed into <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> law.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_24" title="March 24">March 24</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Life_Insurance_Company" title="Metropolitan Life Insurance Company">Metropolitan Life Insurance Company</a> is formed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_27" title="March 27">March 27</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario_Shore_Railroad" title="Lake Ontario Shore Railroad">Lake Ontario Shore Railroad</a> Company is organized in <a href="/wiki/Oswego,_New_York" title="Oswego, New York">Oswego, New York</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March" title="March">March</a> – The first transnational women's organization, <i><a href="/wiki/Association_internationale_des_femmes" title="Association internationale des femmes">Association internationale des femmes</a></i>, is founded.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93June">April–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1868&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_1" title="April 1">April 1</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Hampton_University" title="Hampton University">Hampton Normal and Agricultural Institute</a> is established in <a href="/wiki/Hampton,_Virginia" title="Hampton, Virginia">Hampton, Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_7" title="April 7">April 7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Charter_Oath" title="Charter Oath">Charter Oath</a>, drawn up by his councilors, is promulgated at the enthronement of the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a> of Japan, promising deliberative assemblies and an end to <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudalism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_9" title="April 9">April 9</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Tewodros_II" title="Tewodros II">Tewodros II</a> of Ethiopia massacres at least 197 of his own people at <a href="/wiki/Amba_Mariam" title="Amba Mariam">Magdala</a>. These are prisoners incarcerated, for the most part, for very trivial offenses, and are killed for requesting bread and water.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_9" title="April 9">April 9</a>–<a href="/wiki/April_13" title="April 13">April 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Magdala" title="Battle of Magdala">Battle of Magdala</a>: A British-Indian task force under <a href="/wiki/Robert_Napier,_1st_Baron_Napier_of_Magdala" title="Robert Napier, 1st Baron Napier of Magdala">Robert Napier</a> inflicts 700 deaths and a crushing defeat on the army of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Tewodros_II" title="Tewodros II">Tewodros II</a>; the British and Indians suffer 30 wounded, 2 of whom die subsequently. Tewodros commits suicide and Magdala is captured, ending the <a href="/wiki/British_Expedition_to_Abyssinia" title="British Expedition to Abyssinia">British Expedition to Abyssinia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_11" title="April 11">April 11</a>–July – <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Edo" title="Fall of Edo">Fall of Edo</a>: the Japanese city is surrendered to the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">Shogun</a> <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Yoshinobu" title="Tokugawa Yoshinobu">Tokugawa Yoshinobu</a> submits to the Emperor.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_29" title="April 29">April 29</a> – General <a href="/wiki/William_Tecumseh_Sherman" title="William Tecumseh Sherman">William Tecumseh Sherman</a> brokers the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fort_Laramie_(1868)" title="Treaty of Fort Laramie (1868)">Treaty of Fort Laramie</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">federal government of the United States</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Plains_Indians" title="Plains Indians">Plains Indians</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_10" title="May 10">May 10</a>–<a href="/wiki/May_14" title="May 14">14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Utsunomiya_Castle" title="Battle of Utsunomiya Castle">Battle of Utsunomiya Castle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>) in Japan: forces of the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a> resist the retreating troops of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_16" title="May 16">May 16</a>, <a href="/wiki/May_26" title="May 26">May 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson" title="Andrew Johnson">Andrew Johnson</a> is twice acquitted during his <a href="/wiki/Impeachment" title="Impeachment">impeachment</a> trial, by one vote in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_26" title="May 26">May 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/Fenian" title="Fenian">Fenian</a> bomber <a href="/wiki/Michael_Barrett_(Fenian)" title="Michael Barrett (Fenian)">Michael Barrett</a> becomes the last person publicly <a href="/wiki/Hanging" title="Hanging">hanged</a> in the United Kingdom.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_29" title="May 29">May 29</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Capital_Punishment_Amendment_Act_1868" title="Capital Punishment Amendment Act 1868">Capital Punishment Amendment Act</a>, thus ending public hanging.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_30" title="May 30">May 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a> is observed in the United States for the first time (it was proclaimed on <a href="/wiki/May_5" title="May 5">May 5</a> by General <a href="/wiki/John_A._Logan" title="John A. Logan">John A. Logan</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_31" title="May 31">May 31</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Spence" title="Thomas Spence">Thomas Spence</a> declares himself president of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Manitobah" title="Republic of Manitobah">Republic of Manitobah</a>; he soon alienates the locals.</li>
<li>The first popular bicycle race is held at <a href="/wiki/Parc_de_Saint-Cloud" title="Parc de Saint-Cloud">Parc de Saint-Cloud</a>, Paris.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_2" title="June 2">June 2</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/Trades_Union_Congress" title="Trades Union Congress">Trades Union Congress</a> is held in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, England.</li>
<li>June – <a href="/wiki/T%C4%ABtokowaru%27s_War" title="Tītokowaru's War">Tītokowaru's War</a> breaks out in the <a href="/wiki/South_Taranaki_District" title="South Taranaki District">South Taranaki District</a> of New Zealand's <a href="/wiki/North_Island" title="North Island">North Island</a> between the Ngāti Ruanui <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori</a> tribe and the New Zealand Government.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_20" title="June 20">June 20</a> – Fort Fred Steele is established to protect what is at this time the western terminus of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railway" title="Union Pacific Railway">Union Pacific Railway</a>, near the modern-day <a href="/wiki/Sinclair,_Wyoming" title="Sinclair, Wyoming">Sinclair, Wyoming</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93September">July–September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1868&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: July–September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Grand_Tetons11.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="768" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Grand_Tetons11.jpg/220px-Grand_Tetons11.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Grand_Tetons11.jpg/330px-Grand_Tetons11.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Grand_Tetons11.jpg/440px-Grand_Tetons11.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Grand_Tetons11.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/July_25" title="July 25">July 25</a>: <a href="/wiki/Wyoming_Territory" title="Wyoming Territory">Wyoming Territory</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a> – The cable-operated <a href="/wiki/IRT_Ninth_Avenue_Line#History" title="IRT Ninth Avenue Line">West Side and Yonkers Patent Railway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> becomes the first <a href="/wiki/Elevated_railway" title="Elevated railway">elevated railway</a> in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_4" title="July 4">July 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ueno" title="Battle of Ueno">Battle of Ueno</a>: Imperial Japanese troops defeat the <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dgitai" title="Shōgitai">Shōgitai</a> (forces remaining loyal to the <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">Shogun</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_5" title="July 5">July 5</a> – Preacher <a href="/wiki/William_Booth" title="William Booth">William Booth</a> establishes the Christian Mission, predecessor of <a href="/wiki/The_Salvation_Army" title="The Salvation Army">The Salvation Army</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_9" title="July 9">July 9</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Fourteenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution">Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution</a> is ratified.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_18" title="July 18">July 18</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Navajo" title="Navajo">Navajo</a> people begin their long march home.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_25" title="July 25">July 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a> becomes a <a href="/wiki/United_States_territory" title="United States territory">United States territory</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_25" title="July 25">July 25</a> – the Allies in an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphibious_operation" title="Amphibious operation">amphibious operation</a> capture the fortress of <a href="/wiki/Humait%C3%A1" title="Humaitá">Humaitá</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_War" title="Paraguayan War">Paraguayan War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_27" title="July 27">July 27</a> – The United States <a href="/wiki/Expatriation_Act_of_1868" title="Expatriation Act of 1868">Expatriation Act</a> ("An Act concerning the Rights of American Citizens in foreign States") is adopted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_28" title="July 28">July 28</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Fourteenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution">Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution</a> is adopted, including the <a href="/wiki/Citizenship_Clause" title="Citizenship Clause">Citizenship Clause</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Equal_Protection_Clause" title="Equal Protection Clause">Equal Protection Clause</a>, legally, if not actually, guaranteeing <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African Americans</a> full citizenship and equal protection and all persons in the United States <a href="/wiki/Due_process" title="Due process">due process</a> of law.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_13" title="August 13">August 13</a> – The 8.5–9.0 <a href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude_scale" title="Moment magnitude scale">Mw</a> <a href="/wiki/1868_Arica_earthquake" title="1868 Arica earthquake">Arica earthquake</a> struck southern <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> with a maximum <a href="/wiki/Mercalli_intensity_scale" title="Mercalli intensity scale">Mercalli intensity</a> of XI (<i>Extreme</i>), causing 25,000+ deaths and a destructive basin wide <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> that affected Hawaii and New Zealand.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_18" title="August 18">August 18</a> – The element later named as <a href="/wiki/Helium" title="Helium">helium</a> is first detected in the <a href="/wiki/Emission_spectrum" title="Emission spectrum">spectrum</a> of the Sun's <a href="/wiki/Chromosphere" title="Chromosphere">chromosphere</a> by <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">astronomer</a> <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Janssen" title="Pierre Janssen">Jules Janssen</a> during a total <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_August_18,_1868" title="Solar eclipse of August 18, 1868">eclipse</a> in <a href="/wiki/Guntur" title="Guntur">Guntur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Presidencies_and_provinces_of_British_India" title="Presidencies and provinces of British India">British India</a>, but is assumed to be <a href="/wiki/Sodium" title="Sodium">sodium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_20" title="August 20">August 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Abergele_rail_disaster" title="Abergele rail disaster">Abergele rail disaster</a> in Wales: An <i>Irish Mail</i> passenger train collides with 4 cargo trucks loaded with paraffin oil (more akin to modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerosine" title="Kerosine">kerosine</a>): 33 are killed (the first major train disaster in Britain).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_22" title="August 22">August 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Yangzhou_riot" title="Yangzhou riot">Yangzhou riot</a> in China targets a station of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_Inland_Mission" title="China Inland Mission">China Inland Mission</a>, and nearly leads to war between Britain and China.</li>
<li>September – <a href="/wiki/Glorious_Revolution_(Spain)" title="Glorious Revolution (Spain)">Glorious Revolution</a>: Queen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isabella_II_of_Spain" title="Isabella II of Spain">Isabella II of Spain</a> is effectively deposed and sent into exile; she formally <a href="/wiki/Abdication" title="Abdication">abdicates</a> on <a href="/wiki/June_25" title="June 25">June 25</a>, <a href="/wiki/1870" title="1870">1870</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_3" title="September 3">September 3</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a> of Japan announces that the name of the city of <a href="/wiki/Edo" title="Edo">Edo</a> is to be changed to Tokyo.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_7" title="September 7">September 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/T%C4%ABtokowaru%27s_War" title="Tītokowaru's War">Tītokowaru's War</a>: <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori</a> leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titokowaru" title="Titokowaru">Titokowaru</a> defeats a New Zealand military force at Te Ngutu o Te Manu, North Island.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_18" title="September 18">September 18</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_the_South" title="University of the South">University of the South</a> holds its first convocation in <a href="/wiki/Sewanee,_Tennessee" title="Sewanee, Tennessee">Sewanee, Tennessee</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_23" title="September 23">September 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Grito_de_Lares" title="Grito de Lares">Grito de Lares</a>: Rebels (some 400–600 led by <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Emeterio_Betances" title="Ramón Emeterio Betances">Ramón Emeterio Betances</a>) in the town of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Lares</a> declare <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> independent; the local militia easily defeats them a week later.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_24" title="September 24">September 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Croatian%E2%80%93Hungarian_Settlement" title="Croatian–Hungarian Settlement">Croatian–Hungarian Settlement</a> (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <span lang="hr" xml:lang="hr"><i>Hrvatsko-ugarska nagodba</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <span lang="hu" xml:lang="hu"><i>Horvát–magyar kiegyezés</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Kroatisch-Ungarischer Ausgleich</i></span>) is concluded, governing Croatia's political status in the Hungarian-ruled part of <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a> until <a href="/wiki/1918" title="1918">1918</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October.E2.80.93December">October–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1868&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: October–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_1" title="October 1">October 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Chulalongkorn" title="Chulalongkorn">Chulalongkorn</a> starts to rule in <a href="/wiki/Rattanakosin_Kingdom" title="Rattanakosin Kingdom">Siam</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/October_10" title="October 10">October 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Manuel_de_C%C3%A9spedes" title="Carlos Manuel de Céspedes">Carlos Manuel de Céspedes</a> declares a revolt against Spanish rule in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> in an event known as <i>El Grito de Yara</i>, initiating a war that lasts ten years (Cuba ultimately loses the war at a cost of 400,000 lives and widespread destruction).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_20" title="October 20">October 20</a>
<ul>
<li>English astronomer <a href="/wiki/Norman_Lockyer" title="Norman Lockyer">Norman Lockyer</a> observes and names the D<sub>3</sub> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fraunhofer_line" title="Fraunhofer line">Fraunhofer line</a> in the solar spectrum and concludes that it is caused by a hitherto unidentified <a href="/wiki/Chemical_element" title="Chemical element">element</a> which he later names <a href="/wiki/Helium" title="Helium">helium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedro_Figueredo" title="Pedro Figueredo">Pedro Figueredo</a> creates the <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuban</a> national anthem, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Himno_de_Bayamo" title="El Himno de Bayamo">El Himno de Bayamo</a></i>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_23" title="October 23">October 23</a> – The current <a href="/wiki/Japanese_era_name" title="Japanese era name">Japanese era name</a> is changed to the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>. The 265-year-long <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> is at an end.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_28" title="October 28">October 28</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Edison" title="Thomas Edison">Thomas Edison</a> applies for his first patent, the electric vote recorder.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_2" title="November 2">November 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Time_zone" title="Time zone">Time zone</a>: New Zealand officially adopts a standard time to be observed nationally.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_3" title="November 3">November 3</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._presidential_election,_1868" title="U.S. presidential election, 1868">U.S. presidential election, 1868</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ulysses_S._Grant" title="Ulysses S. Grant">Ulysses S. Grant</a> defeats <a href="/wiki/Horatio_Seymour" title="Horatio Seymour">Horatio Seymour</a> in the election.</li>
