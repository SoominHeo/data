<p>The <b>PACMAD clade</b> (previously PACCMAD, PACCAD, or PACC<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SorengPeterson2015_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SorengPeterson2015-1">[1]</a></sup>) is one of two major <a href="/wiki/Lineage_(evolution)" title="Lineage (evolution)">lineages</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clades</a>) of the true grasses (<a href="/wiki/Poaceae" title="Poaceae">Poaceae</a>), regrouping six subfamilies and about 5700 species, more than half of all true grasses. Its <a href="/wiki/Sister_group" title="Sister group">sister group</a> is the <a href="/wiki/BOP_clade" title="BOP clade">BOP clade</a>. The PACMAD lineage is the only group within the grasses within which the <a href="/wiki/C4_carbon_fixation" title="C4 carbon fixation">C<sub>4</sub> photosynthesis pathway</a> has evolved; studies have shown that this happened independently multiple times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GWPGII_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GWPGII-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The name of the clade comes from the first initials of the included subfamilies <a href="/wiki/Panicoideae" title="Panicoideae">Panicoideae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arundinoideae" title="Arundinoideae">Arundinoideae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chloridoideae" title="Chloridoideae">Chloridoideae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Micrairoideae" title="Micrairoideae">Micrairoideae</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aristidoideae" title="Aristidoideae">Aristidoideae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Danthonioideae" title="Danthonioideae">Danthonioideae</a>. It has no defined taxonomic rank but is used frequently because it refers to a well-defined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monophyletic_group" title="Monophyletic group">monophyletic group</a> with a distinct ecology.</p>
<p>Phylogeny of the PACMAD clade, with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Fixation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carbon Fixation (page does not exist)">photosynthetic pathways</a> for each subfamily:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GWPGII_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GWPGII-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
