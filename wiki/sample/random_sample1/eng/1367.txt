<p>The <b>PL-9</b> is a short-range, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infrared-homing" title="Infrared-homing">infrared-homing</a> <a href="/wiki/Air-to-air_missile" title="Air-to-air missile">air-to-air missile</a> (AAM) developed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. It was originally designed by Dong Bingyin (董秉印) at the Luoyang Electro Optical Center, which is also known as Institute 612 and renamed in 2002 as the China Air-to-Air Guided Missile Research Institute (中国空空导弹研究院). After Dong Bingyin died, his position was succeeded by Mr. Jin Xianzhong (金先仲). The deputy general designer of PL-9 is the same deputy general designer of <a href="/wiki/PL-12" title="PL-12">PL-12</a>, Dr. Liang Xiaogeng (梁晓庚).</p>
<p>The PL-9C is one of the latest versions of the PL-9, which also has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surface-to-air" title="Surface-to-air">surface-to-air</a> variant (DK-9). The PL-9C tactical low-altitude <a href="/wiki/Surface-to-air_missile" title="Surface-to-air missile">surface-to-air missile</a> (SAM) was first revealed during the 1989 <a href="/wiki/Paris_Air_Show" title="Paris Air Show">Paris Air Show</a>. The launch unit is available in both towed and self-propelled arrangement. The self-propelled version has a launch complex consisting four-rail launcher and the associated target acquisition and radar and electro-optical director mounted on a 6X6 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WZ551" title="WZ551">WZ551</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armored_personnel_carrier" title="Armored personnel carrier">armored personnel carrier</a> (APC). The towed version has a four-rail launcher mounted on a four-wheel carriage. The launch unit is supported by a range of truck-mounted ground equipment for maintenance and missile testing.</p>
<p>The missile is fitted with a cryogenic <a href="/wiki/Liquid_nitrogen" title="Liquid nitrogen">liquid nitrogen</a> gas-cooled <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">IR</a> seeker capable of +/-40 degree off boresight angles. Flight control is by long span pointed delta fins at the front of the missile with Sidewinder-type slipstream driven <a href="/wiki/Rolleron" title="Rolleron">rollerons</a> on the aft tail fin surfaces to prevent roll and so enhance the operation of the guidance system. The missile has a maximum effective range of 35 km and an altitude limit of 6.5 km. The single-shot hit probability for a single missile launch at an approaching target is 90%. The missile can be used at a stand-alone system, or as a part of the Type 390 (DK-9) brigade (regiment)-level combined AAA/SAM air defence system. The missile entered production in 1991 and saw limited service with the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">PLA</a> ground forces.</p>
