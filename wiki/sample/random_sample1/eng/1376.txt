<p><b>PSM Makassar</b>, or <b>PSM</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Makassar" title="Makassar">Makassar</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Sulawesi" title="South Sulawesi">South Sulawesi</a>. The club was founded in 1915 (as <i>Makassar Voetbal Bond</i>) and currently competes in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indonesia_Soccer_Championship_A" title="Indonesia Soccer Championship A">Indonesia Soccer Championship A</a>. PSM plays their home matches in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andi_Mattalatta_Stadium" title="Andi Mattalatta Stadium">Andi Mattalatta Stadium</a>.</p>
<p>PSM is the oldest team in the history of <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_football_league_system" title="Indonesian football league system">Indonesian football</a>. They are also considered as one of the most successful ones in eastern Indonesia.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PSM_Makassar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation_and_early_years_.281915.E2.80.931942.29">Foundation and early years (1915–1942)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PSM_Makassar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Foundation and early years (1915–1942)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>PSM was founded on November 2, 1915 as <i>Makassar Voetbal Bond (MVB)</i>. They are considered as the oldest football club in Indonesia and as one of the oldest in South East Asia. During the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_the_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Japanese occupation of the Dutch East Indies">Japanese occupation of the Dutch East Indies</a>, all type of organization with Dutch spellings are removed and MVB changed to PSM Makassar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sejarah_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sejarah-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Perserikatan_era_.281942.E2.80.931994.29">Perserikatan era (1942–1994)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PSM_Makassar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Perserikatan era (1942–1994)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some of the early players of PSM Makassar was regularly called to the <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_national_football_team" title="Indonesia national football team">Indonesia national football team</a>, the most famous was the club legend <a href="/wiki/Ramang" title="Ramang">Ramang</a>. They won their first <a href="/wiki/Perserikatan" title="Perserikatan">Perserikatan</a> title in 1957 by beating <a href="/wiki/PSMS_Medan" title="PSMS Medan">PSMS Medan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> They managed to also won the championship in 1959, 1965, 1966 and 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sejarah_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sejarah-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_era_and_recent_history_.281994.E2.80.93present.29">Modern era and recent history (1994–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PSM_Makassar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Modern era and recent history (1994–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the merger of <a href="/wiki/Perserikatan" title="Perserikatan">Perserikatan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galatama" title="Galatama">Galatama</a> in 1994, they won their first Indonesia Premier Division title in 2000. The squad consists of the combination of national team players, such as <a href="/wiki/Miro_Baldo_Bento" title="Miro Baldo Bento">Miro Baldo Bento</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aji_Santoso" title="Aji Santoso">Aji Santoso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bima_Sakti" title="Bima Sakti">Bima Sakti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurniawan_Dwi_Yulianto" title="Kurniawan Dwi Yulianto">Kurniawan Dwi Yulianto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hendro_Kartiko" title="Hendro Kartiko">Hendro Kartiko</a>, and local talents, such as Ronny Ririn, Syamsudin Batola, Yusrifar Djafar, dan Rachman Usman. They won the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_Liga_Indonesia_Premier_Division" title="1999–2000 Liga Indonesia Premier Division">1999–2000 Liga Indonesia Premier Division</a> by only losing 2 from 31 matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011 they joined the breakaway league <a href="/wiki/Liga_Primer_Indonesia" title="Liga Primer Indonesia">Liga Primer Indonesia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lpi_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-lpi-4">[4]</a></sup> They continued to play in the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_Premier_League" title="Indonesian Premier League">Indonesian Premier League</a> organized by PT Liga Prima Indonesia Sportindo until 2013, before joining the <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_Super_League" title="Indonesia Super League">Indonesia Super League</a> again in the 2014 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
