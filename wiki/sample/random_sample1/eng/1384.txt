<p><b>PeR</b> that stands for "Please explain the Rhythm"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvian</a> <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beatboxing" title="Beatboxing">beatboxing</a> band formed in 2007. The original line-up was Ralfs Eilands, Emīls Vegners, and Pēteris Upenieks. Vegners left the band in 2007 and was replaced by Edmunds Rasmanis. But when Upelnieks left the trio in 2011, he was not replaced, rendering the band a duo consisting of just Eilands and Rasmanis. After three failed attempts in earlier years to represent <a href="/wiki/Latvia_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Latvia in the Eurovision Song Contest">Latvia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>, PeR won the 2013 Dziesma contest and represented the country in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="2013 Eurovision Song Contest">2013 Contest</a> with the song "<a href="/wiki/Here_We_Go_(PeR_song)" title="Here We Go (PeR song)">Here We Go</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PeR&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.282007.E2.80.932008.29">Early years (2007–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PeR&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years (2007–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band was founded in 2007, with original members; Ralfs Eilands, Emīls Vegners, and Pēteris Upelnieks. On 21 July 2007, the band first appeared and performed on <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dziesma_manai_paaudzei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dziesma manai paaudzei (page does not exist)">Dziesma manai paaudzei</a>, the Latvian music festival. After the festival, the band travelled to <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, to compete with other artists on the talent show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minuta_Slavy" title="Minuta Slavy">Minuta Slavy</a></i> (Minute of Fame), with the band coming in fourth place during voting. The band returned to Latvia where they received an invitation to participate in the annual music event <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bildes_2007&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bildes 2007 (page does not exist)">Bildes 2007</a>. This was the last event with the original members.</p>
<p>In 2008, Emīls Vegners left the band and was replaced by Edmunds Rasmanis. The band gained popularity appearing on the first series of the Latvian television station <a href="/wiki/Latvijas_Neatkar%C4%ABg%C4%81_Telev%C4%ABzija" title="Latvijas Neatkarīgā Televīzija">LNT</a> contest <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Latvijas_zelta_talanti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Latvijas zelta talanti (page does not exist)">Latvijas zelta talanti</a></i>, the group managed to reach the final, but failed to win. After the contest, the band performed at many events, including the 2009 edition of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Muzik%C4%81l%C4%81_banka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Muzikālā banka (page does not exist)">Muzikālā banka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eirodziesma">Eirodziesma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PeR&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Eirodziesma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>2009</dt>
</dl>
<p>In 2009, the band with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sab%C4%ABne_Berezina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sabīne Berezina (page does not exist)">Sabīne Berezina</a> entered the <a href="/wiki/Latvia_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2009" title="Latvia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2009">2009 edition</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eirodziesma" title="Eirodziesma">Eirodziesma</a>, the Latvian national selection for the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2009" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2009">Eurovision Song Contest 2009</a>, with the song "Bye, Bye". The song qualified from the semi-final in sixth place, but finished ninth in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>2010</dt>
</dl>
<p>The band returned to Eirodziesma in <a href="/wiki/Latvia_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2010" title="Latvia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2010">2010</a>, with the song "Like a Mouse", finishing in tenth and last place in the final.</p>
<dl>
<dt>2011–2012</dt>
</dl>
<p>In 2011, the band was reduced to two members when Pēteris Upenieks left the band. They again participated in Eirodziesma in <a href="/wiki/Latvia_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2012" title="Latvia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2012">2012</a>, with "Disco Superfly", qualifying from the semi-final and finishing fifth in the final.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dziesma_and_Eurovision_Song_Contest_.282013.29">Dziesma and Eurovision Song Contest (2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PeR&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dziesma and Eurovision Song Contest (2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band entered two songs for <a href="/wiki/Latvia_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013" title="Latvia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2013">Dziesma 2013</a>, "Sad Trumpet" was entered into the first semi-final, and "<a href="/wiki/Here_We_Go_(PeR_song)" title="Here We Go (PeR song)">Here We Go</a>" into the second semi-final. Both songs qualified from their semi-finals to the final. "Sad Trumpet" and "Here We Go" were drawn fourth and twelfth respectively in the final running order, during voting "Here We Go" qualified to the super-final with two other songs. At the close of voting "Here We Go" had received the most votes and won the contest, and thus represented<a href="/wiki/Latvia_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013" title="Latvia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2013">Latvia</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2013">Eurovision Song Contest 2013</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malmö">Malmö</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Latvia_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Latvia-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The song was the first performed during the second semi-final, and it failed to qualify for the final. Ralfs Eilands will be one of the five jurors for <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2015" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2015">Eurovision Song Contest 2015</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>.</p>
