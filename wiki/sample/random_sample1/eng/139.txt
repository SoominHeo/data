<p><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><b>.no</b></span> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code_top-level_domain" title="Country code top-level domain">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (ccTLD) for <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. <a href="/wiki/Norid" title="Norid">Norid</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_registry" title="Domain name registry">domain name registry</a>, is based in <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a>, is owned by the state-owned <a href="/wiki/Uninett" title="Uninett">Uninett</a> and operates under supervision of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Post_and_Telecommunications_Authority" title="Norwegian Post and Telecommunications Authority">Norwegian Post and Telecommunications Authority</a>. As of May 10, 2013 there were 583,962 registered <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span>-domains. Organizations with a presence in Norway and registration at the <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8nn%C3%B8ysund_Register_Centre" title="Brønnøysund Register Centre">Brønnøysund Register Centre</a> are limited to 100 domains each. Individuals residing in Norway may register in the <a href="/wiki/Second-level_domain" title="Second-level domain">second-level domain</a> <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">priv.no</span> and, as of June 17, 2014, directly under <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Other second-level domains exist for organizations of certain types, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Norway" title="Municipalities of Norway">municipalities</a> and schools. The strict regulations have resulted in near-absence of <a href="/wiki/Cybersquatting" title="Cybersquatting">cybersquatting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_warehousing" title="Domain name warehousing">warehousing</a>.</p>
<p>Management of the ccTLD was awarded to <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A5l_Spilling" title="Pål Spilling">Pål Spilling</a> in 1983, but was taken over by Uninett four years later. The 1000th domain was registered in 1995. Norid is the result of several re-organizations within Uninett, in 2003 becoming a separate limited company. Norway has also been allocated two other ccTLDs, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.sj" title=".sj">.sj</a></span> for <a href="/wiki/Svalbard_and_Jan_Mayen" title="Svalbard and Jan Mayen">Svalbard and Jan Mayen</a> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.bv" title=".bv">.bv</a></span> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bouvet%C3%B8ya" title="Bouvetøya">Bouvetøya</a>; neither are open to registration. Originally only a single domain was permitted per organization, and this was manually checked by Norid to ensure compliance with <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademark</a> ownership. The regulations were liberalized in 2001, when the process was automated and a retrospective dispute resolution scheme was introduced. This resulted in a boom of registrations, with the accumulated registrations exceeding 100,000 in the course of the year. Domain names may consist of the twenty-six basic Latin letters, digits and the <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">hyphen</a>, and beginning in 2004 three <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian language</a> letters and twenty <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sami_language" title="Sami language">Sami language</a> letters have been permitted. All-numeric domains were introduced in 2007 and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">priv.no</span> in 2011.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.no&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The domain name registry responsibility for <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> was in 1983 awarded to <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A5l_Spilling" title="Pål Spilling">Pål Spilling</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Telecommunications_Administration" title="Norwegian Telecommunications Administration">Norwegian Telecommunications Administration</a>'s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Norwegian_Telecommunications_Administration_Research_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Norwegian Telecommunications Administration Research Institute (page does not exist)">Research Institute</a>. The actual registration work was carried out by Jens Thomassen. The first registered domains were <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">tor.nta.no</span> (the Norwegian Telecommunications Administration) and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">ifi.uio.no</span> (the Department of Informatics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oslo" title="University of Oslo">University of Oslo</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Initially the workload of domain registration was light, but after a few years the workload had become unmanageable as a side project for an individual. Policy-makers also indicated a need for the domain registration to be managed by a non-commercial organization. The responsibility was therefore transferred to the publicly owned Uninett, a supplier of <a href="/wiki/Information_and_communications_technology" title="Information and communications technology">information and communications technology</a> to Norwegian public universities, colleges and research institutions<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> on 17 March 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The oldest archived <a href="/wiki/Zone_file" title="Zone file">zone file</a> dates from 1989 and includes 19 domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1991 and 1992, all state university colleges were connected to the Internet and issued domain names, causing a boom of registrations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-growth_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-growth-6">[6]</a></sup> The 1000th domain was registered in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Uninett was at first administrated as a division of <a href="/wiki/SINTEF" title="SINTEF">SINTEF</a>, but was in 1993 transformed to a limited company owned by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Education_and_Research_(Norway)" title="Ministry of Education and Research (Norway)">Ministry of Education and Research</a>. Norid was established as a division within Uninett in 1996, and issued the responsibility of managing the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> domain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> On 21 August 1997, Norid was given the responsibility for the newly created <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.sj</span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Uninett FAS was established the following year as a subsidiary of Uninett to manage the technical network and service infrastructure, including operation of the network systems for the universities and colleges. Thus Norid also became part of the Uninett FAS portfolio. Two organizations were established in 1998: the Domain Resolution Body, to resolve domain disputes, and Norpol, a political advisory board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_registrar" title="Domain name registrar">Domain name registrars</a> were introduced in 1999 to handle aspects that could be provided by a third party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Uninett Norid was in 2003 registered as a limited company owned by Uninett, to secure the management of the domains within an independent organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until 2001, each organization could only register a single domain and had to document its right to the name, either directly connected to the company's name or a trademark; this information was verified by Norid. The rules were liberalized on 19 February 2001. A new cap was set at fifteen domains per organization and Norid no longer made an evaluation of the right to the name; if the domain was not registered it would be awarded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This resulted in a large increase in the number of domains, and the 100,000th domain was registered that same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> Originally only the basic <a href="/wiki/Latin_script" title="Latin script">Latin letters</a> were permitted, but from 9 February 2004 an additional twenty-three characters from the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sami_languages" title="Sami languages">Sami</a> languages were permitted, along with the number of permitted domains per organization increasing to twenty. From 13 June 2007, all-numerical domains names were allowed. The 500,000th domain was registered in January 2011. The <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">priv.no</span> second-level domain was permitted from June 2011, which gave individuals the possibility to register. On 30 November 2011, organizations were permitted to register 100 domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 June 2014 <a href="/wiki/Norid" title="Norid">Norid</a> opened up for private citizen registrations of .no domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 9 December 2014 <a href="/wiki/Norid" title="Norid">Norid</a> enabled support for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DNSSEC" title="DNSSEC">DNSSEC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.no&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Management of <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> lies with the <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a>-based Norid, which is also the domain name registry for the unused <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.sj</span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span>. Norid is a limited company owned by <a href="/wiki/Uninett" title="Uninett">Uninett</a>, which is again owned by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Education_and_Research_(Norway)" title="Ministry of Education and Research (Norway)">Ministry of Education and Research</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The legal right to manage the domains is two-fold, based both on an agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Assigned_Numbers_Authority" title="Internet Assigned Numbers Authority">Internet Assigned Numbers Authority</a> (IANA) and regulations under the <i>Electronic Communications Act</i> which is supervised by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Post_and_Telecommunications_Authority" title="Norwegian Post and Telecommunications Authority">Norwegian Post and Telecommunications Authority</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The policy for use of <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> is regulated by the <i>Regulation Concerning Domain Names Under Norwegian Country Code Top-level Domains</i>, also known as the <i>Domain Regulation</i>. This regulation also regulates Norway's other two ccTLDs, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.bv</span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.sj</span>, for Bouvetøya and Svalbard and Jan Mayen, respectively; neither are open to registration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Registration takes place through a third party, a <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_registrar" title="Domain name registrar">domain name registrar</a>. The relationship between Norid, the registrar and the holder is regulated through civil legal agreements. The registrar registers the domain on behalf of the holder, and the holder then holds the right to the domain name until it is terminated, unless a dispute resolution dictates otherwise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-conflicts_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-conflicts-18">[18]</a></sup> Norid charges registrars 60 <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_krone" title="Norwegian krone">Norwegian krone</a> (NOK) per registration and change of holder, as well as NOK 60 in an annual fee per domain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Registrars must meet criteria regarding technical and administrative resources, must pay an annual fee of NOK 5,000 and a deposit of minimum NOK 10,000, depending on activity level. Registrars must also undertake a minimum activity of either administrating or registering forty domains per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon registering a domain, the user signs a declaration that they are not infringing on the rights of other parties and that the user will assume full responsibility for any consequences of use of the domain name. The registration process at Norid is fully automated and does not include any steps to ensure that the user has the rights to the name. Disputes regarding the right to a domain name are handled retrospectively, either through the Alternative Dispute Resolution Committee or <a href="/wiki/Judiciary_of_Norway" title="Judiciary of Norway">court proceedings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-conflicts_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-conflicts-18">[18]</a></sup> Issues which could give the right to take over a domain name include infringements of the <i>Trademarks Act</i> and the <i>Marketing Control Act</i>. Trademark holders will also be awarded the domain if the registration was solely made to sell the domain to the trademark owner. The dispute bodies will also consider the risk of confusion, based on the domain name itself rather than the content of the site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-langree_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-langree-22">[22]</a></sup> For one domain, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">volvoimport.no</span>, the issue of ownership was subject to a <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Norway" title="Supreme Court of Norway">Supreme Court</a> case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Supreme Court has ruled that the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Prosecuting_Authority" title="Norwegian Prosecuting Authority">Norwegian Prosecuting Authority</a> may seize domain registrations under specifications of the General Civil Penal Code, as domains are legally regarded as assets with financial value.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> As of 27 September 2012 there were 552,255 registered domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stats_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-stats-1">[1]</a></sup> <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span>-domains had a 90.6-percent renewal rate in 2009, which is significantly higher than more liberal domains, such as 71 percent of <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;"><a href="/wiki/.com" title=".com">.com</a></span> domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-growth_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-growth-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cybersquatting" title="Cybersquatting">Cybersquatting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_warehousing" title="Domain name warehousing">warehousing</a> has not been a problem with <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span>-domains because of the strict registration requirements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-langree_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-langree-22">[22]</a></sup> Norpol is an advisory body with thirteen members appointed to discuss and comment on the domain policy. It consists of members from several government authorities, the Internet industry and other stakeholders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy">Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.no&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Only organizations with a local presence in Norway and with registration in the <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8nn%C3%B8ysund_Register_Centre" title="Brønnøysund Register Centre">Brønnøysund Register Centre</a> are allowed to register domains under <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span>. Specifically, they must be registered in the Central Coordinating Register for Legal Entities—and thus have an organization number, have a postal address in Norway and must be able to actually document activity in Norway upon request from Norid. All qualified users may register up to 100 domains directly under <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.no</span> and five additional domains under each second-level domain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-policy_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-policy-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">priv.no</span> domain is available for registration by individuals. To register, an individual must be registered in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_Registry_(Norway)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National Registry (Norway) (page does not exist)">National Registry</a> and have been issued a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_identity_number_(Norway)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National identity number (Norway) (page does not exist)">national identity number</a>, must be resident in Norway and be at least 18 years old. Moving abroad will not result in the domain being deleted, but the person cannot register any more domains while living abroad. There is a limit of five registered domains per person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-policy_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-policy-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Domain names must consist of 2 to 63 characters. Permitted characters are the <a href="/wiki/ISO_basic_Latin_alphabet" title="ISO basic Latin alphabet">ISO basic Latin alphabet</a> (a through z), digits (0 through 9), the <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">hyphen</a> (-), the three <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian language</a> letters <a href="/wiki/%C3%86" title="Æ">æ</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%98" title="Ø">ø</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%85" title="Å">å</a>, and twenty special <a href="/wiki/Sami_languages" title="Sami languages">Sami languages</a> letters (<a href="/wiki/%C3%81" title="Á">á</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%80" title="À">à</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%84" title="Ä">ä</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C4%8C" title="Č">č</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%87" title="Ç">ç</a>·<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%90" title="Đ">đ</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%89" title="É">é</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%88" title="È">è</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%8A" title="Ê">ê</a>·<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%8A" title="Ŋ">ŋ</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C5%83" title="Ń">ń</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%91" title="Ñ">ñ</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%93" title="Ó">ó</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%92" title="Ò">ò</a>·<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%94" title="Ô">ô</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%96" title="Ö">ö</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C5%A0" title="Š">š</a>·<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%A6" title="Ŧ">ŧ</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C3%9C" title="Ü">ü</a>·<a href="/wiki/%C5%BD" title="Ž">ž</a>). The domain name must start and end in a digit or a letter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-policy_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-policy-26">[26]</a></sup> A large number of domains cannot be registered, including all that are the basis for second-level domains, and a specified series of geographical names, including names of settlements and islands, which may become second-level domains in the future. Eight specific Internet terms may not be registered (<span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">ftp</span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">localhost</span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">whois</span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">www</span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">no</span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">nic</span>, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">internet</span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">internett</span>). Additional general-category potential future second-level domains have also been blocked, such as <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">com.no</span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">as.no</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Second-level_domains">Second-level domains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.no&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Second-level domains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are three types of <a href="/wiki/Second-level_domain" title="Second-level domain">second-level domains</a>: geographaphy-oriented, category-oriented and those managed by other agencies than Norid. There are reserved geographic second-level domains for all counties (such as <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">oslo.no</span> for <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">nt.no</span> for <a href="/wiki/Nord-Tr%C3%B8ndelag" title="Nord-Trøndelag">Nord-Trøndelag</a>), all <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_and_cities_in_Norway" title="List of towns and cities in Norway">urban areas with at least 5,000 inhabitants</a> (such as <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">orkanger.no</span> for <a href="/wiki/Orkanger" title="Orkanger">Orkanger</a>) and all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Norway" title="Municipalities of Norway">municipalities</a> (such as <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">bergen.no</span> for <a href="/wiki/Bergen" title="Bergen">Bergen</a> and both <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">inderoy.no</span> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">inderøy.no</span> for <a href="/wiki/Inder%C3%B8y" title="Inderøy">Inderøy</a>). In addition, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">svalbard.no</span> has been reserved for <a href="/wiki/Svalbard" title="Svalbard">Svalbard</a> and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">jan-mayen.no</span> for <a href="/wiki/Jan_Mayen" title="Jan Mayen">Jan Mayen</a>. Users are asked to not register with a geographic second-level domain unless they have a local presence in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-policy_26-3"><a href="#cite_note-policy-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-appendixb_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-appendixb-28">[28]</a></sup> The <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">www</span> name under a geographic second-level domain (such as <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">www.lillehammer.no</span>) may only be carried out by the local government of the area, such as the municipality, the <a href="/wiki/County_municipality_(Norway)" title="County municipality (Norway)">county municipality</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Svalbard" title="Governor of Svalbard">Governor of Svalbard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-appendixb_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-appendixb-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The category second-level domains require the user to meet specific characteristics; for instance, only upper secondary schools may register under <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">vgs.no</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-policy_26-4"><a href="#cite_note-policy-26">[26]</a></sup> Most of the second-level domains are managed by Norid, although five domains are managed by three other government agencies, the <a href="/wiki/Government_Administration_Services" title="Government Administration Services">Government Administration Services</a> for central parts of the government, the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Armed_Forces" title="Norwegian Armed Forces">Norwegian Armed Forces</a> for their own sites and the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Association_of_Local_and_Regional_Authorities" title="Norwegian Association of Local and Regional Authorities">Norwegian Association of Local and Regional Authorities</a> for municipalities and county municipalities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-appendixd_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-appendixd-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
