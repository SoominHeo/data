<p><i><b>Polaris</b></i> is the twelfth studio album by <a href="/wiki/Power_metal" title="Power metal">power metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Stratovarius" title="Stratovarius">Stratovarius</a>, released on 15 May 2009 (see <a href="/wiki/Polaris_(Stratovarius_album)#Release_history" title="Polaris (Stratovarius album)">release history</a>). It is the first Stratovarius album to feature bassist <a href="/wiki/Lauri_Porra" title="Lauri Porra">Lauri Porra</a> and guitarist <a href="/wiki/Matias_Kupiainen" title="Matias Kupiainen">Matias Kupiainen</a>, following former bassist <a href="/wiki/Jari_Kainulainen" title="Jari Kainulainen">Jari Kainulainen</a>'s departure from the band in 2005 and former guitarist <a href="/wiki/Timo_Tolkki" title="Timo Tolkki">Timo Tolkki</a>'s departure in 2008.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Timo_Kotipelto" title="Timo Kotipelto">Timo Kotipelto</a>, who at the time of <i>Polaris'</i> release had been a member of Statovarius in the earliest year (1994) among the current members, considered <i>Polaris</i> to be "the album that saved the band" and said that "without it there would not be any Stratovarius."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> More time passed between the release of Stratovarius' eponymous album <a href="/wiki/Stratovarius_(album)" title="Stratovarius (album)"><i>Stratovarius</i></a> in 2005 and the release of <i>Polaris</i> than between any other two consecutive albums released by the band. Stratovarius in 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blabbermouth2006ref_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-blabbermouth2006ref-6">[6]</a></sup> had recorded a demo album called Revolution Renaissance, performed one Revolution Renaissance song live in August 2007 ("Last Night on Earth") and according to Kotipelto had intended to release a completed version of Revolution Renaissance in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lastnightonearth2007_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-lastnightonearth2007-7">[7]</a></sup> However, the songs of that album were re-recorded and released in 2008 as <a href="/wiki/New_Era_(album)" title="New Era (album)"><i>New Era</i></a>, the first album of Tolkki's new band, <a href="/wiki/Revolution_Renaissance" title="Revolution Renaissance">Revolution Renaissance</a>, which acquired the album's prior name.</p>
<p><i>Polaris</i> reached No. 2 on the <a href="/wiki/The_Official_Finnish_Charts" title="The Official Finnish Charts">Finnish albums chart</a>, as well as reaching the top 70 in five other countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finnishcharts_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-finnishcharts-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-italiancharts_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-italiancharts-8">[8]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Deep_Unknown" title="Deep Unknown">Deep Unknown</a>" was released as a single, reaching No. 20 on the <a href="/wiki/The_Official_Finnish_Charts" title="The Official Finnish Charts">Finnish singles chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finnishchartssingle_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-finnishchartssingle-9">[9]</a></sup> In its first week of release, the album sold around 800 copies in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blabbermouthsales_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-blabbermouthsales-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
