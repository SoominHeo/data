<p>"<b>Price Tag</b>" is a song by English singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jessie_J" title="Jessie J">Jessie J</a>, featuring American rapper <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>. It was released on 25 January 2011, in the United Kingdom as the second single from Jessie J's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Who_You_Are_(Jessie_J_album)" title="Who You Are (Jessie J album)">Who You Are</a></i> (2011).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Releasedate_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Releasedate-1">[1]</a></sup> "Price Tag" was written by Jessie J, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Luke" title="Dr. Luke">Dr. Luke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Claude_Kelly" title="Claude Kelly">Claude Kelly</a> and <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>, and it was produced by Dr. Luke. It serves as the album's lead single in the United States. An official remix features British rapper <a href="/wiki/Devlin_(rapper)" title="Devlin (rapper)">Devlin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The song debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. Elsewhere, the single also topped the charts in Ireland and New Zealand, becoming Jessie J's first number-one single in all these countries and her native United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NumberOne_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NumberOne-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sc_Ireland_Jessie_J_feat._B.o.B_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sc_Ireland_Jessie_J_feat._B.o.B-4">[4]</a></sup> In the United States, "Price Tag" peaked at number 23 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sc_Billboardhot100_Jessie_J_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-sc_Billboardhot100_Jessie_J-5">[5]</a></sup> Additionally, the song charted at number one in nineteen countries, becoming Jessie J's biggest hit to date. The song was also the biggest-selling collaboration of the year in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2012_Brit_Awards" title="2012 Brit Awards">2012 Brit Awards</a>, "Price Tag" was nominated for <a href="/wiki/2012_Brit_Awards#Nominees_and_winners" title="2012 Brit Awards">Best British Single</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jessie J has performed the song at a number of live performances, with other singers filling in for B.o.B or his verse being completely removed. The video features an oversized teddy bear, a money tree, and Jessie J as a ballerina in a large jewellery box. The single has sold 1,240,000 copies in the UK as of October 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_sales_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-UK_sales-8">[8]</a></sup> The song was covered by <a href="/wiki/The_Wombats" title="The Wombats">The Wombats</a> on BBC Radio 1's Live Lounge on 31 May 2011. In December 2011, Jessie J recorded a Christmas-themed version of the song, with an interpolation of "<a href="/wiki/Jingle_Bells" title="Jingle Bells">Jingle Bells</a>", featuring vocals from British singer-songwriter Jan.</p>

<p>"Price Tag" is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Up-tempo" title="Up-tempo">up-tempo</a>, "feel good" track, inflected with <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> bounce and a sing-along chorus with powerful vocals from Jessie J, slinky guitars, and a vintage backbeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rap-Up_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rap-Up-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOL_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-AOL-10">[10]</a></sup> The drum beat for the song bears a striking resemblance to that of "Zimba Ku" by <a href="/wiki/Black_Heat" title="Black Heat">Black Heat</a>, whose record label have filed a lawsuit alleging copyright infringement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the sheet music published at Musicnotes.com by Sony/ATV Music Publishing, "Price Tag" is written in the key of <a href="/wiki/F_major" title="F major">F major</a> with a tempo of 84 beats per minute. The song is in <a href="/wiki/Time_signature" title="Time signature">common time</a>, and it follows a chord progression of F–Am–Dm–B<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span> (<a href="/wiki/I%E2%80%93V%E2%80%93vi%E2%80%93IV_progression" title="I–V–vi–IV progression">I–iii–vi–IV</a>). Jessie's vocals span from A<sub>3</sub> to D<sub>5</sub>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Jessie J has been criticized by people who find the lyrics of the song hypocritical: a "rich rock star" telling the rest of us that money doesn't matter, or questioning why she then charges for her music. She has repeatedly explained in interviews that this is a misreading of her meaning, which skewers the superficiality of <a href="/wiki/Conspicuous_consumption" title="Conspicuous consumption">conspicuous consumption</a> -- or more simply, that "show-offs suck." "For me, so much I hear about how much money people have and how people just take stuff too seriously. It’s not me saying that you can live for free, because obviously that isn’t the case; but I’m saying that you don’t always have to let it be about that. It isn’t always about how much your shoes cost. It’s about the fact that you even have a pair of shoes to walk in."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Price_Tag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Popjustice" title="Popjustice">Popjustice</a> said it was a "ridiculously addictive introduction to an effortlessly great, new(ish) talent."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a>'</i>s Caspar Smith referred to both the song and J's previous single "Do It Like A Dude" as "planet-saving R&amp;B-based hits-in-waiting."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/AOL_Radio" title="AOL Radio">AOL Radio</a>'s James Wells said that it "shows off the tirelessly upbeat, Motown-tinged singing and songwriting for which Jessie J has become known." He went on to contrast the song from other tracks from J's debut album <i>Who You Are</i>, calling it less aggressive and more "about the positive vibes."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOL_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-AOL-10">[10]</a></sup> Nick Levine of <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> compared the song to <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Party_in_the_U.S.A." title="Party in the U.S.A.">Party in the U.S.A.</a>" (2009), which was co-written by Jessie J. He said, "Truth be told, 'Price Tag' does come off a little bit corny, but thanks in no small part to Jessie's spunky likability" and described it as a "sun-dappled, hip-hop-inflected midtempo head-nodder".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Price_Tag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song debuted at number twelve in Ireland on 4 February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In the United Kingdom, the single entered at number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> on 6 February 2011―for the week ending dated 12 February 2011―with first week sales of 84,000 copies. It became Jessie J's first number-one single on the chart. The song's charting also gave <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a> his third UK number-one single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NumberOne_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NumberOne-3">[3]</a></sup> "Price Tag" became the first single to top the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> after being released to radio and retail on the same day, following a new "On Air, On Sale" initiative by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On its second week on the chart, the song retained its number-one position by a margin of less than 5%, selling over 90,000 copies, preventing <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>'s new single "<a href="/wiki/Born_This_Way_(song)" title="Born This Way (song)">Born This Way</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Chip_(rapper)" title="Chip (rapper)">Chipmunk</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Champion_(Chipmunk_song)" title="Champion (Chipmunk song)">Champion</a>" from debuting at number one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> As of October 2015, the song had sold 1,240,000 copies in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_sales_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-UK_sales-8">[8]</a></sup> In the United States, the song debuted at number 88 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> on the week of 16 March 2011 and has reached number 23.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sc_Billboardhot100_Jessie_J_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-sc_Billboardhot100_Jessie_J-5">[5]</a></sup> It went straight to number one in New Zealand and it debuted at number 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bubbling_Under_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Singles" title="Bubbling Under R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Singles">Bubbling Under R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Singles</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Debut_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Debut-21">[21]</a></sup> and peaked at number 9 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Play" title="Hot Dance Club Play">Hot Dance Club Play</a>. It also received <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a> airplay, peaking at number 2 on the Top 30 Countdown for 2 weeks, behind "<a href="/wiki/On_My_Mind_(Cody_Simpson_song)" title="On My Mind (Cody Simpson song)">On My Mind</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Cody_Simpson" title="Cody Simpson">Cody Simpson</a>.</p>

Jessie J as a marionette in the music video.</div>

<p>The music video was directed by Emil Nava and was premiered on 30 January 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The video begins closing up on a giant teddy bear that is missing an eye and an arm, accompanied by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musical_box" title="Musical box">musical box</a>-rendering of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Strauss_II" title="Johann Strauss II">Johann Strauss II</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blue_Danube" title="The Blue Danube">The Blue Danube</a></i>, before zooming out to show a child version of Jessie J sitting next to it, who then gets up and walks around the bear, turning her into her full-sized self. Jessie J is then shown standing and holding out her hands, and as the lyrics suggest, a <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> man and a moonhead appear on her outstretched hands (this is a reference to the two individuals who co-wrote the song with her: <a href="/wiki/Dr._Luke" title="Dr. Luke">Dr. Luke</a> is <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> man and Kelly is moonhead in reference to his large head) and pointing to herself (referring to herself as pea because of her small head). The video then goes back to Jessie J in the lap of the large teddy bear and shows various scenes of Jessie J holding up items that relate to the lyrics, including a huge price tag and pointing to left and the right. As the chorus begins, Jessie J is shown dancing under a money tree with dollar bills covering its branches. A scene of Jessie J is then shown of her as an oversized ballerina in a jewelry box. As Jessie J says the words "video hoes", a scene of her wearing the same outfit as in her "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Do_It_Like_a_Dude" title="Do It Like a Dude">Do It Like a Dude</a>" video and dancing holding her crotch, suggesting that she does not approve of video hoes. Jessie J is next shown riding around on a small tricycle. As the song progresses into the second chorus, Jessie J and two background dancers are shown as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marionettes" title="Marionettes">marionettes</a>, with Jessie J wearing a <a href="/wiki/Pinocchio" title="Pinocchio">Pinocchio</a> hat, dancing to the song. The video then moves to B.o.B as his verse begins, showing him dancing in front of a beat up car, that starts jumping and moving to the beat. He is then shown standing in the middle of four large, toy green army men. As his verse ends, Jessie J is shown squeezed into a doll house and holding a glass doll that is missing part of its head. As the video goes into its final chorus, scenes of Jessie J are shown her dancing with B.o.B, laying on the floor missing a leg, sitting in an oversized chair, dressed up as a doll in a blue and white dress, and dancing with her younger self. The video ends with Jessie J closing the door to her doll house; with her still inside it, the camera carries on zooming out.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Price_Tag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jessie J performed an acoustic version of the song on the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Later..._with_Jools_Holland" title="Later... with Jools Holland">Later... with Jools Holland</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> "Price Tag" was also performed at the club <a href="/wiki/Scala_(club)" title="Scala (club)">Scala</a> in London on 18 January 2011, with UK rapper <a href="/wiki/Devlin_(rapper)" title="Devlin (rapper)">Devlin</a> performing B.o.B's verse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The song was performed in the US for the first time in a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> She performed the song, along with a track titled "Nobody's Perfect" from her debut album, in a studio session at <a href="/wiki/Maida_Vale_Studios" title="Maida Vale Studios">Maida Vale studios</a> for <a href="/wiki/MistaJam" title="MistaJam">MistaJam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Jessie J also performed Price Tag on <i>The Graham Norton Show</i> on 25 February and has also performed it on <i>Lets Dance for Comic Relief</i>. Jessie also performed the song (along with "Mamma Knows Best") alongside <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> on 12 March 2011, making it her first performance on American television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> She has also performed the song on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/X_Factor_(France)" title="X Factor (France)">the French <i>X Factor</i></a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In 2011, she performed the song in the <a href="/wiki/Times_Square%E2%80%9342nd_Street/Port_Authority_Bus_Terminal_(New_York_City_Subway)" title="Times Square–42nd Street/Port Authority Bus Terminal (New York City Subway)">Times Square</a> subway station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Also, Jessie presented the song in the <a href="/wiki/2011_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2011 MTV Video Music Awards">2011 MTV VMAs</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2011 MTV Europe Music Awards">2011 MTV EMAs</a>. She performed the song at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_closing_ceremony" title="2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony">2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony</a>.</p>
