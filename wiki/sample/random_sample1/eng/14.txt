<p>The <b>oscar</b> (<i>Astronotus ocellatus</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Cichlid" title="Cichlid">cichlid</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> known under a variety of common names, including <b>tiger oscar</b>, <b>velvet cichlid</b>, and <b>marble cichlid</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fishbase_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-fishbase-1">[1]</a></sup> In tropical South America, where the species naturally resides, <i>A. ocellatus</i> specimens are often found for sale as a food fish in the local markets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kullander_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-kullander-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The fish has been introduced to other areas, including China, Australia, and the United States. It is considered a popular <a href="/wiki/Aquarium" title="Aquarium">aquarium</a> fish in Europe and the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amcichlidsII_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-amcichlidsII-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loiselle_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Loiselle-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The species was originally described by <a href="/wiki/Louis_Agassiz" title="Louis Agassiz">Louis Agassiz</a> in 1831 as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lobotes" title="Lobotes">Lobotes</a> ocellatus</i>, as he mistakenly believed the species was marine; later work assigned the species to the genus <i>Astronotus</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-florida_museum_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-florida_museum-7">[7]</a></sup> The species also has a number of junior synonyms: <i>Acara compressus</i>, <i>Acara hyposticta</i>, <i>Astronotus ocellatus zebra</i>, and <i>Astronotus orbiculatus</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1897" data-file-width="3035" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg/220px-Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg/330px-Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg/440px-Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Astronotus_ocellatus05.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Ocelli on dorsal fin and caudal peduncle</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>A. ocellatus</i> examples have been reported to grow to about 45 cm (18 in) in length and 1.6 kilograms (3.5 lb) in weight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fishbase_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-fishbase-1">[1]</a></sup> The wild-caught forms of the species are typically darkly coloured with yellow-ringed spots or <a href="/wiki/Eyespot_(mimicry)" title="Eyespot (mimicry)">ocelli</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caudal_peduncle" title="Caudal peduncle">caudal peduncle</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/Dorsal_fin" title="Dorsal fin">dorsal fin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amcichlidsII_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-amcichlidsII-5">[5]</a></sup> These ocelli have been suggested to function to limit fin-nipping by <a href="/wiki/Piranha" title="Piranha">piranha</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Serrasalmus" title="Serrasalmus">Serrasalmus</a></i> spp.), which co-occur with <i>A. ocellatus</i> in its natural environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-florida_museum_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-florida_museum-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-winemiller_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-winemiller-9">[9]</a></sup> The species is also able to rapidly alter its colouration, a trait which facilitates ritualised territorial and combat behaviours amongst conspecifics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Juvenile oscars have a different colouration from adults, and are striped with white and orange wavy bands and have spotted heads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-florida_museum_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-florida_museum-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg/220px-Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg/330px-Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg/440px-Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Two_Tiger_Oscar.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Two tiger oscars</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>A. ocellatus</i> is native to Peru, Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, and <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, and occurs in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_River_basin" title="Amazon River basin">Amazon River basin</a>, along the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I%C3%A7%C3%A1" title="Içá">Içá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_Negro_(Amazon)" title="Rio Negro (Amazon)">Negro</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solim%C3%B5es" title="Solimões">Solimões</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ucayali" title="Ucayali">Ucayali</a> River systems, and also in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Approuague_river" title="Approuague river">Approuague</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oyapock_River" title="Oyapock River">Oyapock River</a> drainages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fishbase_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-fishbase-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kullander_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-kullander-2">[2]</a></sup> In its natural environment, the species typically occurs in slow-moving <a href="/wiki/Blackwater_river#Comparison_between_white_and_black_waters" title="Blackwater river">white-water</a> habitats, and has been observed sheltering under submerged branches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amcichlidsII_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-amcichlidsII-5">[5]</a></sup> Feral populations also occur in China,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> northern Australia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida, USA</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> as a byproduct of the ornamental fish trade. The species is limited in its distribution by its intolerance of cooler water temperatures, the lower lethal limit for the species is 12.9 °C (55.22 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the species is widely regarded as <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">sexually monomorphic</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amcichlidsII_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-amcichlidsII-5">[5]</a></sup> males have been suggested to grow more quickly,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and in some naturally occurring strains, males are noted to possess dark blotches on the base of their dorsal fins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loiselle_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Loiselle-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-florida_museum_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-florida_museum-7">[7]</a></sup> The species reaches sexual maturity around one year of age,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and continues to reproduce for 9–10 years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Frequency and timing of spawning may be related to the occurrence of rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <i>A. ocellatus</i> fish are biparental substrate spawners, though detailed information regarding their reproduction in the wild is scarce.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Oscaroscar.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Oscaroscar.JPG/200px-Oscaroscar.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Oscaroscar.JPG/300px-Oscaroscar.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Oscaroscar.JPG/400px-Oscaroscar.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Oscaroscar.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Young oscar, about 2 in</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In captivity, pairs are known to select and clean generally flattened horizontal or vertical surfaces on which to lay their 1,000 to 3,000 eggs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Like most cichlids, <i>A. ocellatus</i> practices brood care, although the duration of brood care in the wild remains unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loiselle_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Loiselle-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_aquarium">In the aquarium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In the aquarium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The oscar is one of the most popular cichlids in the aquarium hobby.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style" role="presentation">
<tr>
<td class="mbox-image">
<div style="width:52px"><img alt="" data-file-height="48" data-file-width="48" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/40px-Edit-clear.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/60px-Edit-clear.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/80px-Edit-clear.svg.png 2x" width="40"/></div>
</td>

</table>
<p>Captive oscars may be fed prepared fish food designed for large carnivorous fish: <a href="/wiki/Crayfish" title="Crayfish">crayfish</a>, worms, and insects (such as flies, crickets and grasshoppers). Feeding live foods may increase the rate of growth but also may cause endoparasites. Poultry and/or mammalian flesh, including beefheart, should not be fed long term as these fatty foods will contribute to fatty liver disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Since these fish eat fruit in the wild, items such as melons, oranges, and other fruits can also be used as a type of food. Just about anything that falls into the water would be eaten by oscars. Live feeder fish can be given, but fish such as goldfish and rosy red feeder minnows should not be fed. These contain an enzyme (thiaminase) within their flesh which binds vitamin B<sub>1</sub>, leading to deficiency. Most fish eaten by <i>A. ocellatus</i> in the wild are relatively sedentary <a href="/wiki/Catfish" title="Catfish">catfish</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/Bunocephalus" title="Bunocephalus">Bunocephalus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rineloricaria" title="Rineloricaria">Rineloricaria</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ochmacanthus" title="Ochmacanthus">Ochmacanthus</a></i> species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-winemiller_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-winemiller-9">[9]</a></sup> The species uses a suction mechanism to capture prey,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and has been reported to exhibit "laying-on-side" death mimicry in a similar fashion to <i>Parachromis friedrichsthalii</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Nimbochromis_livingstonii" title="Nimbochromis livingstonii">Nimbochromis livingstonii</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The species also has an absolute requirement for <a href="/wiki/Vitamin_C" title="Vitamin C">vitamin C</a>, and develops health problems in its absence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Territorial_behavior">Territorial behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Territorial behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="131" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/68/Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg/175px-Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/68/Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg/263px-Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/68/Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg/350px-Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg 2x" width="175"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Felix_the_partially_albino_Tiger_Oscar.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
An albino oscar</div>

</div>
<p>Oscars will often lay claim to an area of the aquarium and will be very aggressive towards other fish encroaching on their newly established <a href="/wiki/Territory_(animal)" title="Territory (animal)">territory</a> inside the aquarium or lake. The size of the territory varies depending on the size and aggressiveness of the fish based on its surroundings. Once the oscar establishes a territory, it will vigorously defend it by chasing away other fish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Varieties">Varieties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_(fish)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Varieties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Long_finned_oscar.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="602" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Long_finned_oscar.jpg/220px-Long_finned_oscar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Long_finned_oscar.jpg/330px-Long_finned_oscar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Long_finned_oscar.jpg/440px-Long_finned_oscar.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Long_finned_oscar.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A <a href="/wiki/Leucism" title="Leucism">leucistic</a> long-finned oscar</div>
</div>

<p>A number of ornamental varieties of <i>A. ocellatus</i> have been developed for the <a href="/wiki/Aquarium" title="Aquarium">aquarium</a> industry. These include forms with greater intensity and quantities of red marbling across the body, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albino" title="Albino">albino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leucism" title="Leucism">leucistic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Xanthochromism" title="Xanthochromism">xanthistic</a> forms. <i>A. ocellatus</i> with marbled patches of red pigmentation are sold as red tiger oscars, while those strains with mainly red colouration of the flanks are frequently sold under the trade name of red oscars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tankbusters_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-tankbusters-22">[22]</a></sup> The patterning of red pigment differs between individuals; in the United Kingdom, one <i>A. ocellatus</i> reportedly had markings that resembled the Arabic word for "Allah".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In recent years long-finned varieties have also been developed. The species is also occasionally artificially coloured by a process known as <a href="/wiki/Painted_fish" title="Painted fish">painting</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
