<p>The <b>RD-170</b> (<b>РД-170, Ракетный Двигатель-170, Rocket Engine-170</b>) is the world's most powerful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquid-fuel_rocket" title="Liquid-fuel rocket">liquid-fuel</a> <a href="/wiki/Rocket_engine" title="Rocket engine">rocket engine</a>, designed and produced in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> by <a href="/wiki/NPO_Energomash" title="NPO Energomash">NPO Energomash</a> for use with the <a href="/wiki/Energia" title="Energia">Energia</a> launch vehicle. The engine burns the Russian equivalent of <a href="/wiki/RP-1" title="RP-1">RP-1</a> fuel and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LOX" title="LOX">LOX</a> oxidizer in four <a href="/wiki/Combustion_chamber" title="Combustion chamber">combustion chambers</a>, all supplied by one single-shaft, single-turbine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turbo_pump" title="Turbo pump">turbo pump</a> rated at 170 MW in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Staged_combustion_cycle_(rocket)" title="Staged combustion cycle (rocket)">staged combustion cycle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ea-rd170_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ea-rd170-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yna-20090825_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-yna-20090825-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shared_turbopump">Shared turbopump</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Shared turbopump">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> and Russian rocket engines use the approach of clustering small combustion chambers around a single turbine and pump. During the early 1950s, many Soviet engine designers, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valentin_Petrovich_Glushko" title="Valentin Petrovich Glushko">Valentin P. Glushko</a>, faced problems of combustion instability while designing bigger thrust chambers. At that time they solved the problem by using a cluster of smaller thrust chambers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="RD-170">RD-170</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: RD-170">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The RD-170 engine featured four combustion chambers and was developed for use on the <a href="/wiki/Energia" title="Energia">Energia</a> launch vehicle – both the engine and the launch vehicle were in production only for a short time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="RD-171">RD-171</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: RD-171">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:RD171_ILA2006.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1020" height="345" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/RD171_ILA2006.jpg/220px-RD171_ILA2006.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/RD171_ILA2006.jpg/330px-RD171_ILA2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/RD171_ILA2006.jpg/440px-RD171_ILA2006.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:RD171_ILA2006.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
RD-171 model</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Building on the technology from the Energia launch vehicle the <a href="/wiki/Zenit_(rocket_family)" title="Zenit (rocket family)">Zenit (rocket family)</a> was developed, which uses a RD-170 variant, the RD-171. While the RD-170 had nozzles which swiveled on only one axis, the RD-171 swivels on two axes. Models called the RD-172 and RD-173 were proposed upgrades that would provide additional thrust, but they were never built.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dual-chamber_derivative">Dual-chamber derivative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dual-chamber derivative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/RD-180" title="RD-180">RD-180</a></div>
<p>The <b>RD-180</b> uses only two combustion chambers instead of the four of the RD-170. The <a href="/wiki/RD-180" title="RD-180">RD-180</a> used on the <a href="/wiki/Atlas_V" title="Atlas V">Atlas V</a>, replaced the three engines used on early Atlas rockets with a single engine and achieved significant payload and performance gains. This engine had also been chosen to be the main propulsion system for the first stage of the now cancelled Russian <a href="/wiki/Rus-M" title="Rus-M">Rus-M</a> rocket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coppinger_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-coppinger-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Single-chamber_derivative">Single-chamber derivative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Single-chamber derivative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/RD-191" title="RD-191">RD-191</a></div>
<p>The <b>RD-191</b> is a single-chamber version used in the Russian <a href="/wiki/Angara_(rocket_family)" title="Angara (rocket family)">Angara</a> rocket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Variants of RD-191 include <b>RD-151</b> in South Korean <a href="/wiki/Naro-1" title="Naro-1">Naro-1</a> rocket,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npoenergomash-pr94_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-npoenergomash-pr94-8">[8]</a></sup> <b>RD-181</b> in American <a href="/wiki/Orbital_ATK" title="Orbital ATK">Orbital ATK</a> <a href="/wiki/Antares_(rocket)" title="Antares (rocket)">Antares</a> rocket, and the proposed <b>RD-193</b> for the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz-2-1v" title="Soyuz-2-1v">Soyuz-2-1v</a> project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsw-rd193_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsw-rd193-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_Variants">Proposed Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Proposed Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 July 2011 NPO Energomash summarised the results of the work on Rus-M rocket engine and considered the possibility of construction several new variants of RD-170 family engines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> According to the information, new and proposed variants will be marked as:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>RD-180M</b> for manned <a href="/wiki/Atlas_V" title="Atlas V">Atlas V</a> rocket (<i>Not required, current RD-180 meets manned Atlas V requirements.</i>)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (October 2015)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:RD-170#RD-180M_.22variant.22_misleading_or_incorrect" title="Talk:RD-170">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup></li>
<li><b>RD-180V</b> for <a href="/wiki/Rus-M" title="Rus-M">Rus-M</a> rocket.</li>
<li><b>RD-175</b> with <b>9800</b> kN thrust for proposed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energia-K" title="Energia-K">Energia-K</a> rocket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vor-20120222_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-vor-20120222-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">Specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RD-170&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>4 combustion chambers, 4 nozzles</li>
<li>1 set of turbines and pumps - Turbine produces approximately 257,000 hp (192 MW); equivalent to the power output of 3 <a href="/wiki/Nuclear-powered_icebreaker" title="Nuclear-powered icebreaker">nuclear-powered icebreakers</a></li>
<li>Ignition: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hypergolic" title="Hypergolic">Hypergolic</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Vacuum thrust of 7,887 kN (1,773,000 lb<sub>f</sub>)</li>
<li>Vacuum I<sub>sp</sub> of 338 s (3.31 km/s)</li>
<li>Sea Level I<sub>sp</sub> of 309 s (3.03 km/s)</li>
<li>Weight: 9,750 kg (21,500 lb)</li>
<li>Thrust to weight ratio: 82</li>
</ul>
