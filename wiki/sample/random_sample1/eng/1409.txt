<p><b>R.E. Mouscron</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Belgium" title="Football in Belgium">Belgian association football</a> club from the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Mouscron" title="Mouscron">Mouscron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hainaut_(province)" title="Hainaut (province)">Hainaut</a>. In December 2009 they were declared bankrupt and soon ceased to exist. A new club known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Mouscron-P%C3%A9ruwelz" title="Royal Mouscron-Péruwelz">Royal Mouscron-Péruwelz</a> was formed in March 2010 and placed in the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Third_Division" title="Belgian Third Division">Belgian Third Division</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R.E._Mouscron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was the result of the merger between Stade Mouscron and A.R.A. Mouscron in 1964.</p>
<p>R.E. Mouscron had financial problems during the 2004–05 season and so the president and mayor of Mouscron Jean-Pierre Detremmerie left the club and was replaced by Edward Van Daele. The players with the higher wages were asked to leave the club, as did <a href="/wiki/Marcin_%C5%BBew%C5%82akow" title="Marcin Żewłakow">Marcin Żewłakow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franky_Vandendriessche" title="Franky Vandendriessche">Franky Vandendriessche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Claeys" title="Geoffrey Claeys">Geoffrey Claeys</a>, Koen De Vleeschauwer and <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Teklak" title="Alexandre Teklak">Alexandre Teklak</a>.</p>
<p>In late 2009 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_City" title="Manchester City">Manchester City</a> was ready to spend £3million to save R.E. Mouscron from <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a> and use them as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feeder_club" title="Feeder club">feeder club</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 28 December 2009, Mouscron announced its third forfeit in a row because of enduring financial problems, and was thus, according to Belgian league rules, excluded from competition, with all its previous results in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Belgian_First_Division" title="2009–10 Belgian First Division">ongoing competition</a> being scrapped. The club in its current form ceased to exist, with all its players (and staff) becoming <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agents</a>.</p>
<p>In March 2010 a successor club was formed with the merging of the bankrupt R.E. Mouscron and <a href="/wiki/R.R.C._Peruwelz" title="R.R.C. Peruwelz">R.R.C. Peruwelz</a>. The new club is known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Mouscron-P%C3%A9ruwelz" title="Royal Mouscron-Péruwelz">Royal Mouscron-Péruwelz</a>.</p>
