<p><b>1894 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCXCIV</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">starting on Monday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> G) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> B) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1894th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 894th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 94th year of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, and the 5th year of the <a href="/wiki/1890s" title="1890s">1890s</a> decade. As of the start of 1894, the Gregorian calendar was 12 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>February – In Korea, peasant unrest erupts in the <a href="/wiki/Donghak_Peasant_Revolution" title="Donghak Peasant Revolution">Donghak Peasant Revolution</a>, a massive revolt of followers of the <a href="/wiki/Donghak" title="Donghak">Donghak</a> movement. Both China and Japan send military forces, claiming to come to the ruling <a href="/wiki/Joseon" title="Joseon">Joseon</a> dynasty government's aid.</li>
</ul>
<p><br/></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="America.28North_America_and_Latin_America.29">America(North America and Latin America)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: America(North America and Latin America)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_7" title="January 7">January 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/William_Kennedy_Dickson" title="William Kennedy Dickson">William Kennedy Dickson</a> receives a patent for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motion_picture" title="Motion picture">motion picture</a> film.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_9" title="January 9">January 9</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_England_Telephone_and_Telegraph" title="New England Telephone and Telegraph">New England Telephone and Telegraph</a> installs the first <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" title="Battery (electricity)">battery</a>-operated <a href="/wiki/Telephone_switchboard" title="Telephone switchboard">telephone switchboard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Massachusetts" title="Lexington, Massachusetts">Lexington, Massachusetts</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_17" title="February 17">February 17</a> – American outlaw <a href="/wiki/John_Wesley_Hardin" title="John Wesley Hardin">John Wesley Hardin</a> is released from prison.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_12" title="March 12">March 12</a> – For the first time, <i><a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a></i> is sold in bottles.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_21" title="March 21">March 21</a> – A <a href="/wiki/Syzygy_(astronomy)" title="Syzygy (astronomy)">syzygy</a> of planets occurs as Mercury transits the Sun as seen from Venus, and Mercury and Venus both transit the Sun as seen from Saturn, but no two of the transits are simultaneous.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_25" title="March 25">March 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Coxey%27s_Army" title="Coxey's Army">Coxey's Army</a> (of the unemployed), the first significant protest march in the United States, departs from <a href="/wiki/Massillon,_Ohio" title="Massillon, Ohio">Massillon, Ohio</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_4" title="January 4">January 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Franco-Russian_Alliance" title="Franco-Russian Alliance">A military alliance</a> is established between the <a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">French Third Republic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_12" title="February 12">February 12</a>
<ul>
<li>French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist" title="Anarchist">anarchist</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%89mile_Henry_(anarchist)" title="Émile Henry (anarchist)">Émile Henry</a> sets off a bomb in a Paris café, killing one person and wounding twenty.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Barque" title="Barque">barque</a> <i>Elisabeth Rickmers</i> of Bremerhaven is wrecked at Haurvig, Denmark, but all crew and passengers are saved.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_15" title="February 15">February 15</a> – At 04:51 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GMT" title="GMT">GMT</a>, French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist" title="Anarchist">anarchist</a> <a href="/wiki/Martial_Bourdin" title="Martial Bourdin">Martial Bourdin</a> attempts to destroy the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Observatory,_Greenwich" title="Royal Observatory, Greenwich">Royal Observatory, Greenwich</a>, London, England, with a bomb, killing himself instead.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1894" title="Local Government Act 1894">Local Government Act</a> (coming into effect December 1894–January 1895) reforms <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local government</a> in Britain, creating a system of <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rural_district" title="Rural district">rural districts</a> with elected councils, with elected <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">parish councils</a> in rural areas, and gives women, irrespective of marital status, the right to vote and stand in local (but not national) elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_11" title="April 11">April 11</a> – Britain establishes a Protectorate over <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_16" title="April 16">April 16</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_City_Football_Club" title="Manchester City Football Club">Manchester City Football Club</a> is formed in England.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="others">others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_21" title="April 21">April 21</a> A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bituminous_Coal_Miners%27_Strike_of_1894" title="Bituminous Coal Miners' Strike of 1894">bituminous coal miners' strike</a> closes mines across the central United States.</li>
<li>April 23 – St. George's Day – Howard Ruff founded the Royal Society of St George to foster the love of England and to strengthen England and the Commonwealth by spreading the knowledge of English History, Traditions and Ideals.</li>
<li>April 27 – <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>'s largest known landslide occurred in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Alban,_Quebec" title="Saint-Alban, Quebec">Saint-Alban</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. Displacing 185 million cubic metres (6.5<span style="margin-left:0.2em">×<span style="margin-left:0.1em">10</span></span><s style="display:none">^</s><sup>9</sup> cu ft) of rock and dirt, leaving a 40 metres (130 ft) scar, that covered 4.6 million square metres (50<span style="margin-left:0.2em">×<span style="margin-left:0.1em">10</span></span><s style="display:none">^</s><sup>6</sup> sq ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1894landslide_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-1894landslide-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-landslide27april_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-landslide27april-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>May – <a href="/wiki/Bubonic_plague" title="Bubonic plague">Bubonic plague</a> breaks out in the <a href="/wiki/Tai_Ping_Shan_Street" title="Tai Ping Shan Street">Tai Ping Shan</a> area of Hong Kong (by the end of the year, the death toll is 2,552 people).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_1" title="May 1">May 1</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Coxey%27s_Army" title="Coxey's Army">Coxey's Army</a> arrives in Washington; Coxey is arrested.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/May_Day_riots_of_1894" title="May Day riots of 1894">May Day Riots</a> (against unemployment) break out in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Ohio" title="Cleveland, Ohio">Cleveland, Ohio</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_11" title="May 11">May 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pullman_Strike" title="Pullman Strike">Pullman Strike</a>: Three thousand <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pullman_Palace_Car_Company" title="Pullman Palace Car Company">Pullman Palace Car Company</a> factory workers go on a "wildcat" (without union approval) strike in <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_14" title="May 14">May 14</a>
<ul>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Meteor_shower" title="Meteor shower">meteor shower</a> is seen in southern France.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Blackpool_Tower" title="Blackpool Tower">Blackpool Tower</a> is opened in <a href="/wiki/Blackpool" title="Blackpool">Blackpool</a>, England.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_21" title="May 21">May 21</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Ship_Canal" title="Manchester Ship Canal">Manchester Ship Canal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salford_Quays" title="Salford Quays">Docks</a> are opened by <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>, linking the previously landlocked English industrial city of <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Sea" title="Irish Sea">Irish Sea</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_22" title="June 22">June 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Dahomey" title="Dahomey">Dahomey</a> becomes a French colony.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_23" title="June 23">June 23</a> – The <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> is founded at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paris" title="University of Paris">Sorbonne</a>, Paris, at the initiative of Baron <a href="/wiki/Pierre_de_Coubertin" title="Pierre de Coubertin">Pierre de Coubertin</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_24" title="June 24">June 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Marie_Fran%C3%A7ois_Sadi_Carnot" title="Marie François Sadi Carnot">Sadi Carnot</a>, <a href="/wiki/President_of_France" title="President of France">president of France</a>, is assassinated.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_30" title="June 30">June 30</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Tower_Bridge" title="Tower Bridge">Tower Bridge</a> in London opens for traffic.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93September">July–September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: July–September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>July – A fire at the site of the 1893 <a href="/wiki/World%27s_Columbian_Exposition" title="World's Columbian Exposition">World's Columbian Exposition</a> in Chicago destroys most of the remaining buildings.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_4" title="July 4">July 4</a>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Hawaii" title="Republic of Hawaii">Republic of Hawaii</a> is proclaimed by <a href="/wiki/Sanford_B._Dole" title="Sanford B. Dole">Sanford B. Dole</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_La_Chaux-de-Fonds" title="FC La Chaux-de-Fonds">FC La Chaux-de-Fonds</a> is founded in Switzerland.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/July_22" title="July 22">July 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Paris%E2%80%93Rouen_(motor_race)" title="Paris–Rouen (motor race)">Paris–Rouen</a> Competition for Horseless Carriages, the first automobile competition.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_1" title="August 1">August 1</a> – War is declared between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Empire" title="Qing Empire">Qing Empire</a> of China and the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a>, over their rival claims of influence on their common ally, the <a href="/wiki/Joseon" title="Joseon">Joseon</a> dynasty of Korea. <li><a href="/wiki/September_1" title="September 1">September 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Great_Hinckley_Fire" title="Great Hinckley Fire">Great Hinckley Fire</a>: A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forest_fire" title="Forest fire">forest fire</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hinckley,_Minnesota" title="Hinckley, Minnesota">Hinckley, Minnesota</a>, kills more than 450 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_4" title="September 4">September 4</a> – In New York City, 12,000 tailors strike against <a href="/wiki/Sweatshop" title="Sweatshop">sweatshop</a> working conditions.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October.E2.80.93December">October–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: October–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_1" title="October 1">October 1</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Owl_Club" title="Owl Club">Owl Club</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>, South Africa, a dining club, has its first formal meeting.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_15" title="October 15">October 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Dreyfus_affair" title="Dreyfus affair">Dreyfus affair</a>: French Army officer <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Dreyfus" title="Alfred Dreyfus">Alfred Dreyfus</a> is arrested for spying.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_30" title="October 30">October 30</a> – Domenico Menegatti obtains a patent for a procedure to be applied in producing <a href="/wiki/Pandoro" title="Pandoro">pandoro</a> industrially.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_1" title="November 1">November 1</a> – Russian emperor <a href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Russia" title="Alexander III of Russia">Alexander III</a> is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_II_of_Russia" title="Nicholas II of Russia">Nicholas II</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_6" title="November 6">November 6</a> – Major Republican landslide in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections,_1894" title="United States House of Representatives elections, 1894">United States House of Representatives elections</a>, which sets the stage for the decisive <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1896" title="United States presidential election, 1896">presidential election of 1896</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_7" title="November 7">November 7</a> – The Masonic Grand Lodge de France is founded, splitting from the larger and older <a href="/wiki/Grand_Orient_de_France" title="Grand Orient de France">Grand Orient de France</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_21" title="November 21">November 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lushunkou" title="Battle of Lushunkou">Battle of Lushunkou</a> (<a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a>) – Japanese troops secure a decisive victory over the Chinese, capture the port city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BCshunkou" title="Lüshunkou">Lüshunkou</a> and begin the "<a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur_massacre_(China)" title="Port Arthur massacre (China)">Port Arthur massacre</a>" in which more than 1,000 Chinese servicemen and civilians.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_18" title="December 18">December 18</a> – Women in <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">South Australia</a> are legislated to become the first in Australia to gain the right to vote and to be elected to Parliament, taking effect from 1895.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_21" title="December 21">December 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mackenzie_Bowell" title="Mackenzie Bowell">Mackenzie Bowell</a> becomes Canada's fifth <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Canada" title="Prime Minister of Canada">prime minister</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_22" title="December 22">December 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Dreyfus_affair" title="Dreyfus affair">Dreyfus affair</a>: French Army officer <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Dreyfus" title="Alfred Dreyfus">Alfred Dreyfus</a> is convicted of treason.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1894&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Petr%C3%B3polis" title="Petrópolis">Petrópolis</a> becomes the capital of the state of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro_(state)" title="Rio de Janeiro (state)">Rio de Janeiro</a> until 1902.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WDL_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-WDL-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Western countries give up their <a href="/wiki/Extraterritoriality" title="Extraterritoriality">extraterritorial rights</a> in Japan.</li>
<li>New Zealand enacts the world's first <a href="/wiki/Minimum_wage_law" title="Minimum wage law">minimum wage law</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Grace_Kimmins" title="Grace Kimmins">Grace Kimmins</a> founds the <i><a href="/wiki/Guild_of_the_Poor_Brave_Things" title="Guild of the Poor Brave Things">Guild of the Poor Brave Things</a></i> in England for the education of <a href="/wiki/Cripple" title="Cripple">crippled</a> boys.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/National_College_of_Music" title="National College of Music">National College of Music</a>, London, is founded by the Moss family.</li>
<li>In the U.S., the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Society_of_Beaux-Arts_Architects" title="Society of Beaux-Arts Architects">Society of Beaux-Arts Architects</a> is founded.</li>
<li>Chatham Episcopal Institute (now known as <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Hall" title="Chatham Hall">Chatham Hall</a> ) is founded in <a href="/wiki/Chatham,_Virginia" title="Chatham, Virginia">Chatham, Virginia</a>, U.S.</li>
<li>Sir <a href="/wiki/William_Ramsay" title="William Ramsay">William Ramsay</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_William_Strutt,_3rd_Baron_Rayleigh" title="John William Strutt, 3rd Baron Rayleigh">Lord Rayleigh</a> discover the first noble gas, <a href="/wiki/Argon" title="Argon">argon</a>.</li>
<li>Oil is discovered on the Osage Indian reservation, making the <a href="/wiki/Osage_Nation" title="Osage Nation">Osage</a> the "richest group of people in the world".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kate_Chopin" title="Kate Chopin">Kate Chopin</a> writes <i>The Story of An Hour</i> (fiction).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pomfret_School" title="Pomfret School">Pomfret School</a> is founded.</li>
<li>Edward B. Marks and Joe Stern publish <i><a href="/wiki/The_Little_Lost_Child" title="The Little Lost Child">The Little Lost Child</a></i>, promoting its release with the earliest version of <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> known as the <a href="/wiki/Illustrated_song" title="Illustrated song">illustrated song</a>.</li>
<li>Frederick W. Tamblyn founded the Tamblyn School of Penmanship which later became Ziller of Kansas City, the oldest Calligraphy studio still operating in United States.</li>
</ul>
