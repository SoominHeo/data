<p><i><b>Rock Musical Bleach</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ロックミュージカル ブリーチ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rokkumyūjikaru Burīchi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, often referred as <i>BuriMyu</i>, are a series of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_musicals" title="Rock musicals">rock musicals</a> produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_Pierrot" title="Studio Pierrot">Studio Pierrot</a> and Nelke Planning. Musicals are based on <a href="/wiki/Tite_Kubo" title="Tite Kubo">Tite Kubo</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)" title="Bleach (manga)">Bleach</a></i>. The <i>Rock Musical Bleach</i> consist of live performances that follow the manga plotline from <a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)#Plot" title="Bleach (manga)">Soul Society arc</a> and <i>The Live Bankai</i> shows with the original script. The first musical was directed by Takuya Hiramitsu, with a script adaptation by Naoshi Okumura. The music is completely original and it is written by Shoichi Tama. The premiere was held in Space Zero Hall in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinjuku,_Tokyo" title="Shinjuku, Tokyo">Shinjuku, Tokyo</a> from August 17 - 28, 2005.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story follows a life of high school student <a href="/wiki/Ichigo_Kurosaki" title="Ichigo Kurosaki">Ichigo Kurosaki</a> after he obtains a Soul Reaper powers from another Soul Reaper, <a href="/wiki/Rukia_Kuchiki" title="Rukia Kuchiki">Rukia Kuchiki</a>. By transferring her Soul Reaper powers to a human, Rukia Kuchiki was sent back to the Soul Society and sentenced to death. In order to save Rukia from execution, Ichigo along with his friends goes to the enemy territory where he engages the members of Gotei 13, the main military force in the Soul Society.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Productions">Productions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Productions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rock_Musical_Bleach">Rock Musical Bleach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rock Musical Bleach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The musical was first produced under the direction of Takuya Hiramitsu at Space Zero Hall in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinjuku,_Tokyo" title="Shinjuku, Tokyo">Shinjuku, Tokyo</a> and opened officially on August 17, 2005. The script was adapted by Naoshi Okumura and music was originally produced by Shoichi Tama. The main role of Ichigo Kurosaki went to <a href="/wiki/Tatsuya_Isaka" title="Tatsuya Isaka">Tatsuya Isaka</a>. The role of Rukia Kuchiki was played by <a href="/wiki/Miki_Sat%C5%8D" title="Miki Satō">Miki Satō</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rei_Yoshii" title="Rei Yoshii">Rei Yoshii</a> portrayed Orihime Inoue and Naofumi Yoshida played the role of Yasutora Sado. The show was performed until August 28, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saien">Saien</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Saien">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second musical was performed under name "Saien" and it was officially premiered at Meil Park Hall in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> on January 5, 2006. The Tokyo premiere of "Saien" was held at <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Seinenkan" title="Nippon Seinenkan">Nippon Seinenkan Great Hall</a> on January 14, 2006. The production added the new cast member for the show, <a href="/wiki/Eiki_Kitamura" title="Eiki Kitamura">Eiki Kitamura</a> to play a role of Izuru Kira.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Dark_of_the_Bleeding_Moon">The Dark of the Bleeding Moon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Dark of the Bleeding Moon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The third <i>Rock Musical Bleach</i> was opened at Miel Park Hall in Osaka under name "The Dark of the Bleeding Moon" on August 10, 2006. In Tokyo, the musical was held at Nippon Seinenkan Great Hall on August 16, 2006. The show added Kumiko Saito to portray Yoruichi Shihōin beside her role of Momo Hinamori. The production also added <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kohei_Murakami" title="Kohei Murakami">Kohei Murakami</a> to portray Hanatarō Yamada, <a href="/wiki/Harumi_Inoue" title="Harumi Inoue">Harumi Inoue</a> to play Rangiku Matsumoto, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shogo_Suzuki" title="Shogo Suzuki">Shogo Suzuki</a> to play Kenpachi Zaraki, and Takuya Usui to play Ikkaku Madarame. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yousuke_Ito" title="Yousuke Ito">Yousuke Ito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rei_Yoshii" title="Rei Yoshii">Rei Yoshii</a> and Naofumi Yoshida were removed from the original cast member list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="No_Clouds_in_the_Blue_Heavens">No Clouds in the Blue Heavens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: No Clouds in the Blue Heavens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"No Clouds in the Blue Heavens" is a sequel of previous musical and it focuses on Rukia's execution. The performance was held at Nippon Seinenkan Great Hall in Tokyo on March 21, 2007, following the next premiere at <a href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo_Prefecture" title="Hyōgo Prefecture">Hyōgo Theater</a> on April 3, 2007. The show features new cast members and new songs. Asuka Sekine, <a href="/wiki/Hiroko_Kasahara" title="Hiroko Kasahara">Hiroko Kasahara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masahiro_Kuranuki" title="Masahiro Kuranuki">Masahiro Kuranuki</a> were added to the cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_All">The All</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The All">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The closing musical "The All" was held at <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Koma_Theater" title="Shinjuku Koma Theater">Shinjuku Koma Theater</a> on March 24, 2008. The show is the part of "Rock Musical Bleach DX" performance and it features complete <a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)#Plot" title="Bleach (manga)">Soul Society arc</a> storyline from <i>Bleach</i> manga along with the main actors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RMBleachDX_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-RMBleachDX-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Live_Bankai_Show">The Live Bankai Show</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Live Bankai Show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Live Bankai Shows are musicals with original script and they don't follow the manga plotline. The first "Live Bankai Show Code 001" was held at Aoyama Theater in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> on January 10, 2007. The next one, "Code 002" was opened at <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Koma_Theater" title="Shinjuku Koma Theater">Shinjuku Koma Theater</a> on March 24, 2008 as a part of "Rock Musical Bleach DX" show. In 2010, the third show "Code 003", was performed across the country starting at Momochi Palace in <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>, following the performance at Theater Brava in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> and closing show at Nippon Seinenkan Great Hall in Tokyo. The shows features new songs and new cast members. <a href="/wiki/Naoya_Gomoto" title="Naoya Gomoto">Naoya Gomoto</a> was added to portray Shūhei Hisagi while <a href="/wiki/Kengo_Ohkuchi" title="Kengo Ohkuchi">Kengo Ohkuchi</a> didn't perform his role of Sōsuke Aizen in the third show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RMBleachDX_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-RMBleachDX-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shinsei_2011_and_2012">Shinsei 2011 and 2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Musical_Bleach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Shinsei 2011 and 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Celebrating the <i>Bleach</i> manga 10th anniversary a new production under name "Shinsei" was opened in July 2011. The tour ran nationwide in six different cities. The show featured a new set of cast members along with the new script and music. The leading role of Ichigo Kurosaki was portrayed by Norizuki Kouhei. <a href="/wiki/Miki_Sat%C5%8D" title="Miki Satō">Miki Satō</a> reprised her role of Rukia Kuchiki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shinsei2011_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shinsei2011-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The musical was reprised at Shinagawa Stellar Ball in Tokyo on August 9, 2012. The "Shinsei 2012" performance featured the original set of cast members with addition of Oyama Masashi who portrayed Urahara Kisuke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
