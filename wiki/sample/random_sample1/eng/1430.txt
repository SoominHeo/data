<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Remove before flight</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Remove before flight</b> is a safety warning commonly seen on removable <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">spacecraft</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> components, typically in the form of a red ribbon, to indicate that a device, such as a protective cover or a pin to prevent the movement of mechanical parts, is only used when the aircraft is on the ground (parked or taxiing). On small general aviation aircraft, this may include a <a href="/wiki/Pitot_tube" title="Pitot tube">pitot tube</a> cover or a control lock. In some cases, non-removal of a labeled part have caused airplane crashes. The warning appears in English only. Similar ribbons labelled <b>pull to arm</b> or similar are found on missiles and other weapon systems that are not mounted on aircraft.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remove_before_flight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is common to see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Key_ring" title="Key ring">key rings</a>, T-shirts, <a href="/wiki/Bag_tag" title="Bag tag">bag tags</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belt_(clothing)" title="Belt (clothing)">belts</a> and other such products with this ribbon, especially for people who work in aviation or aviation enthusiasts.</p>
