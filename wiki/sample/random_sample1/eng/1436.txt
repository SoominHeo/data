<p><b>S.H.E</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> whose members are <a href="/wiki/Selina_Jen" title="Selina Jen">Selina Jen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebe_Tien" title="Hebe Tien">Hebe Tien</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ella_Chen" title="Ella Chen">Ella Chen</a>, managed by the <a href="/wiki/HIM_International_Music" title="HIM International Music">HIM International Music</a> label. <a href="/wiki/Avex_Trax" title="Avex Trax">Avex Asia</a> distributed S.H.E's music in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, but transferred this role to <a href="/wiki/WOW_Music" title="WOW Music">WOW Music</a> in 2007. <a href="/wiki/Gold_Typhoon" title="Gold Typhoon">Gold Typhoon</a> is the distributor in the <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">People's Republic of China</a>.</p>
<p>Since releasing their first album <i><a href="/wiki/Girl%27s_Dorm" title="Girl's Dorm">Girls Dorm</a></i> (2001), S.H.E has recorded 12 albums with sales totaling well over 10 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-she-albums_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-she-albums-4">[4]</a></sup> and set ticketing records in each of their two concert tours. The group has acted in seven <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_drama" title="Taiwanese drama">drama series</a>, hosted two variety shows, and contributed ten songs to six drama soundtracks. S.H.E has endorsed more than 30 companies and products, including <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft" title="World of Warcraft">World of Warcraft</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cc_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-cc-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wow_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-wow-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001-2003:_Pre_debut.2C_Girls_Dorm.2C_Youth_Co..2CLtd..2C_Genesis.2C_Together_and_Super_Star">2001-2003: Pre debut, <i>Girls Dorm</i>, <i>Youth Co.,Ltd.</i>, <i>Genesis</i>, <i>Together</i> and <i>Super Star</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2001-2003: Pre debut, Girls Dorm, Youth Co.,Ltd., Genesis, Together and Super Star">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before releasing S.H.E's first album, HIM International focused on the group's chemistry, and assigned the three girls to live together in a dormitory room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 11 September 2001, hours before <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/911_attacks" title="911 attacks">911 attacks</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crisis_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-crisis-8">[8]</a></sup> S.H.E released their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Girl%27s_Dorm" title="Girl's Dorm">Girls Dorm</a></i>, in memory of their prior living quarters, selling an impressive 150,000 copies for a debut group, it also quashed the speculation of an unremarkable debut being On 29 January 2002, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Youth_Society" title="Youth Society">Youth Society</a></i> was released and went on to sell more than 250,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-b-9">[9]</a></sup> With the release of their second album, "S.H.E Girl Friends" underwent a name change and became simply "S.H.E". The trio's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Genesis_(S.H.E_album)" title="Genesis (S.H.E album)">Genesis</a></i>, was released on 5 August 2002, approximately six months after <i>Youth Society</i>. Continuing the successes of the group's previous albums, 20,000 copies of <i>Genesis</i> were ordered in advance of the release date. The album would go on to sell 180,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crisis_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-crisis-8">[8]</a></sup> S.H.E later signed an endorsement deal with N-age, a South Korean on-line game company. Not only did N-age appear in a couple of music videos, they also sponsored S.H.E's first major concert in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tainan_City" title="Tainan City">Tainan City</a>, the N-age Genesis Concert, which attracted over 20,000 fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 23 January 2003, S.H.E released <i><a href="/wiki/Together_(S.H.E_album)" title="Together (S.H.E album)">Together</a></i>, their first compilation album.</p>
<p>S.H.E's fourth album was intended for release on 6 August, but due to an injury to Ella,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellainjury_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ellainjury-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="ref_Anone"><a href="#endnote_Anone">[a]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Star_(S.H.E_album)" title="Super Star (S.H.E album)">Super Star</a></i> arrived in stores on 22 August 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The album featured the group's first pop/rock song, "Super Star", which, for ten consecutive weeks, placed no lower than fourth on Singapore's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Y.E.S._93.3_FM" title="Y.E.S. 93.3 FM">YES 93.3</a> music charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> To date, <i>Together</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Star_(S.H.E_album)" title="Super Star (S.H.E album)">Super Star</a></i> have combined to sell 580,000 copies;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> 250,000 copies of <i>Super Star</i> were sold in Taiwan alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004-2005:_Magical_Journey.2C_Fantasy_Land_World_Tour_and_Once_Upon_a_Time">2004-2005: <i>Magical Journey</i>, Fantasy Land World Tour and <i>Once Upon a Time</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004-2005: Magical Journey, Fantasy Land World Tour and Once Upon a Time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 6 February 2004, S.H.E released their fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Magical_Journey" title="Magical Journey">Magical Journey</a></i>, in two different versions: a Magical Version and a Journey Version. The two versions combined to sell 1.5 million copies in Asia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> including 160,000 copies in Taiwan alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crisis_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-crisis-8">[8]</a></sup> On 2 June, S.H.E and HIM International agreed to extend the trio's contract,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> which would have expired in October 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Later that year, on 4 September 2004, S.H.E began their first major tour, the Fantasy Land Tour. The tour started in <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>, where more than 25,000 attendees were present,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wifey_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-wifey-20">[20]</a></sup> and ended in <a href="/wiki/Genting_Highlands" title="Genting Highlands">Genting Highlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. During their concert at the <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Stadium" title="Shanghai Stadium">Shanghai Stadium</a>, the trio set an attendance record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-att_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-att-21">[21]</a></sup> The tour raked in <a href="/wiki/New_Taiwan_dollar" title="New Taiwan dollar">NT$</a>22.4 million in gate receipts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The release of S.H.E's sixth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Encore_(S.H.E_album)" title="Encore (S.H.E album)">Encore</a></i>, was pushed to 12 November 2004 to accommodate Selina's graduation from university in late November. <i>Encore</i> sold one million units in Asia within one week of its release;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> since then, that number has surpassed 2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> By the time <i>Encore</i> was due for release, media outlets had already begun labelling S.H.E as "The Number One Girl Group" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">女子第一天團</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="ref_Bnone"><a href="#endnote_Bnone">[b]</a></sup></p>
<p>S.H.E had released albums every few months until the end of 2004. But due to their busy concert schedule, it was over a year until their next album appeared. After releasing <i>Encore</i>, they contributed to the <a href="/wiki/Reaching_for_the_Stars_(soundtrack)" title="Reaching for the Stars (soundtrack)"><i>Reaching for the Stars</i> soundtrack</a>, which sold only 50,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crisis_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-crisis-8">[8]</a></sup> That same year, the girl group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twins_(band)" title="Twins (band)">Twins</a> released their first Mandarin album, <i><a href="/wiki/Trainee_Cupid" title="Trainee Cupid">Trainee Cupid</a></i>, in the Taiwanese market. That album would sell 800,000 copies in two months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The poor sales of the soundtrack and the success of the Twins album led to this period being described as one of the lowest times of S.H.E's career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 25 November 2005, the trio finally released their seventh album, <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_(S.H.E_album)" title="Once Upon a Time (S.H.E album)">Once Upon a Time</a></i>. The album not only set a record with 50,000 pre-orders, but it sold more than one million copies, overshadowing <i>Trainee Cupid</i>. <i>Once Upon a Time</i> debuted on the <a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/G-music" title="zh:G-music">G-music</a> Chinese language album charts at #1, retained that position for four consecutive weeks, and stayed on the charts for a total of 13 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The album's title track, "Don't Wanna Grow Up" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">不想長大</span>),<sup class="reference" id="ref_Cnone"><a href="#endnote_Cnone">[c]</a></sup> won Song of the Year at Hong Kong’s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">TVB8</a> Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006-2007:_Forever.2C_The_Perfect_3_World_Tour_and_Play">2006-2007: <i>Forever</i>, The Perfect 3 World Tour and <i>Play</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006-2007: Forever, The Perfect 3 World Tour and Play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 July 2006, S.H.E released a second compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_(S.H.E_album)" title="Forever (S.H.E album)">Forever</a></i>, which sold over 150,000 copies within a week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The album featured five new songs, a compilation of older works, and three songs from older drama soundtracks. <i>Forever</i> included many collaborative works with other artists in the Taiwanese pop music industry. <a href="/wiki/C-pop" title="C-pop">C-pop</a> megastar <a href="/wiki/Jay_Chou" title="Jay Chou">Jay Chou</a> composed "Electric Shock" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">觸電</span>) for the album. The song "Solo Madrigal" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">獨唱情歌</span>) was a duet between Selina and <a href="/wiki/Tank_(Taiwanese_singer)" title="Tank (Taiwanese singer)">Tank</a>, the latter of whom had originally recorded the song for his debut album. For the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_Juliet_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tokyo Juliet (soundtrack) (page does not exist)"><i>Tokyo Juliet</i> soundtrack</a>, Hebe and boyband <a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit_(Taiwanese_band)" title="Fahrenheit (Taiwanese band)">Fahrenheit</a> sang "Only Have Feelings for You" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">只對你有感覺</span>), which won several awards including Taiwan's Best Duet Song at the 2006 Sprite China Music Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>S.H.E's second major concert tour, The Perfect 3 World Tour, commenced on 8 July 2006 in Shanghai, where they set yet another attendance record by filling more than 60% of the stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shanghairecord_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-shanghairecord-32">[32]</a></sup> After their Perfect 3 concert in Hong Kong, which was recorded and released as the group's second live album, S.H.E was commended for their perseverance, amount of dance preparation, and ability to hit their notes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ydcbreview_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-ydcbreview-33">[33]</a></sup> Within the tour's first five months, the group attracted over 200,000 fans, and collected NT$200 million in gate receipts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> On 18 April, S.H.E signed on with <a href="/wiki/WOW_Music" title="WOW Music">WOW Music</a> to improve their presence in Hong Kong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Play_(S.H.E_album)" title="Play (S.H.E album)">Play</a></i>, the trio's eighth album, was released on 11 May 2007, and was the first S.H.E album released under the new distribution label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> <i>Play</i> went double gold within the first four days of the album's pre-order period;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> by 5 June, more than 150,000 copies were sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> A month after <i>Play'</i>s release, S.H.E was nominated for the Most Popular Female Artist award at the <a href="/wiki/18th_Golden_Melody_Awards" title="18th Golden Melody Awards">18th Golden Melody Awards</a> but lost to pop diva <a href="/wiki/Jolin_Tsai" title="Jolin Tsai">Jolin Tsai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008-2009:_FM_S.H.E.2C_Map_of_Love.2C_S.H.E_is_the_One_concert_and_SHERO">2008-2009: <i>FM S.H.E</i>, <i>Map of Love</i>, S.H.E is the One concert and <i>SHERO</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2008-2009: FM S.H.E, Map of Love, S.H.E is the One concert and SHERO">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 September 2008, S.H.E released their ninth album, <i><a href="/wiki/FM_S.H.E" title="FM S.H.E">FM S.H.E</a></i>. On this album, members of the band take on the role of radio hosts, and the album itself is interspersed with radio station-esque snippets. The album had a pre-order sale of 42,000 copies in Taiwan. An online radio station, FM S.H.E, was also set up for a month in line with the album where there will be different programs being broadcast everyday. The viewer-ship of the online radio hit a record high of 100,000 views.</p>
<p>In June 2009, S.H.E released their first digital album, <i><a href="/wiki/Map_of_Love" title="Map of Love">Map of Love</a></i> in China, where this album will only be digitally release on internet music stores. The album consist of 10 songs of which 8 songs are from their previous albums and 2 new songs, 鎖住時間 "(Lock Up the Time)" and 可愛萬歲 "(Long Live Adorableness)". On 16 October 2009, S.H.E commenced their third world tour, S.H.E is the One concert, with Hong Kong being their first stop. The tickets to the Hong Kong concert were sold out within a few days and this concert had received positive reviews. As of 2010, the concert is still ongoing with more stops to be made. On 26 March 2010, S.H.E released their tenth album, <i><a href="/wiki/SHERO" title="SHERO">SHERO</a></i>. The album received positive reviews, with 50,000 copies being pre-ordered in Taiwan before it was released.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-present:_Selina.27s_injury_and_Blossomy">2010-present: Selina's injury and <i>Blossomy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010-present: Selina's injury and Blossomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 October 2010, Selina suffered third degree burns on 54% of her body, mostly her back and legs, during an indoor film shooting in Shanghai for musical movie "I have a date with Spring (我和春天有个约会)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 January 2011, after Selina was hospitalized for two months and discharged, the trio held a press conference to formally address their fans about Selina's injury and her recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In May 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/SHERO" title="SHERO">SHERO</a></i>, nominated the 22nd <a href="/wiki/Golden_Melody_Awards" title="Golden Melody Awards">Golden Melody Awards</a>'s "Top vocal group award".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2012, S.H.E performed on the 23rd <a href="/wiki/Golden_Melody_Awards" title="Golden Melody Awards">Golden Melody Awards</a> and declared regression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On October 11, 2012, S.H.E declared renewal and became one of the main <a href="/wiki/Shareholder" title="Shareholder">shareholders</a> of <a href="/wiki/HIM_International_Music" title="HIM International Music">HIM International Music</a>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Blossomy" title="Blossomy">Blossomy</a></i>, their eleventh album, was released on 16 November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>

<p>S.H.E has released eleven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio albums</a> to date, including three <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation albums</a> and one which is only digitally released. All of S.H.E's album covers display an <a href="/wiki/Oxalis" title="Oxalis">oxalis</a>, the symbol for the group. On some covers, the clover is easy to spot on the front (<i>Girls Dorm</i>, <i>Together</i>, <i>Encore</i>, <i>Forever</i>, <i>Play</i>) or on the back (<i>FM S.H.E</i>); on other covers, its appearance is more subtle (<i>Super Star</i>, <i>Once Upon a Time</i>). S.H.E's other contributions include songs in the soundtracks of <i><a href="/wiki/Magical_Love" title="Magical Love">Magical Love</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rose_(TV_series)" title="The Rose (TV series)">The Rose</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Reaching_for_the_Stars_(soundtrack)" title="Reaching for the Stars (soundtrack)">Reaching for the Stars</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Juliet_(TV_series)" title="Tokyo Juliet (TV series)">Tokyo Juliet</a></i>, <i>The Little Fairy</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hanazakarino_Kimitachihe" title="Hanazakarino Kimitachihe">Hanazakarino Kimitachihe</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bull_Fighting_(TV_series)" title="Bull Fighting (TV series)">Bull Fighting</a></i>. In 2008, they sang the ending theme for the film <i><a href="/wiki/CJ7" title="CJ7">CJ7</a></i>.</p>
<p>All three members have different vocal ranges to complement each other. Ella sings within the alto to mezzo-soprano range, while Hebe and Selina sing within the mezzo-soprano to soprano range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="ref_Dnone"><a href="#endnote_Dnone">[d]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most S.H.E songs fall into the category of <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a>. Songs from this genre consist of light melodies,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> simple drum beats, and the occasional piano or synthesizer accompaniment. In <i>Girls Dorm</i> and <i>Youth Society</i>, acoustic guitar melody lines were used as well. While some slower songs, particularly those in <i>Genesis</i>, fall into the genre of <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-b-9">[9]</a></sup> high-tempo songs, such as "Beauty Up My Life", focus more on <a href="/wiki/Techno" title="Techno">techno</a>. S.H.E has also sung a few <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop/rock</a> songs, including "Piquancy" (Chinese: <span lang="zh">痛快</span>), "Super Star", and "Star Light" (Chinese: <span lang="zh">星光</span>). As their career progressed, S.H.E began mixing light-hearted pop ballads with elements of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a>. Attempts to fuse these genres started with songs such as "If You're Happy, Then I'll Be Pleased" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">你快樂我隨意</span>), which contained a short rap segment, and evolved into songs such as "Listen to Yüan Wei-Jen Play Guitar" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">聽袁惟仁彈吉他</span>), which is composed entirely of rap with the exception of the chorus.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyrics">Lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shi Rencheng (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">施人誠</span>) serves as the group's main lyricist since the group's debut. Between the releases of <i>Magical Journey</i> and <i>Forever</i>, Daryl Yao (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">姚若龍</span>; pinyin: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Yáo Ruòlóng</span></i>) was a regular contributor as well.<sup class="reference" id="ref_Enone"><a href="#endnote_Enone">[e]</a></sup> The lyrics of Jay Chou's main lyricist, <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Fang_(lyricist)" title="Vincent Fang (lyricist)">Vincent Fang</a>, have also made numerous appearances.<sup class="reference" id="ref_Fnone"><a href="#endnote_Fnone">[f]</a></sup> On some occasions, S.H.E members even contributed their own lyrics. Hebe wrote the words for "Say You Love Me" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">說你愛我</span>) and "Too Late" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">來不及</span>), and penned the rap portion of "If You're Happy, Then I'll Be Pleased". Selina was responsible for the lyrics of "Wifey" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">老婆</span>) and "Silenced" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">安靜了</span>).</p>

<p>S.H.E's albums contain quite a number of <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> songs. Among the group's 90 songs, 28 are covers. Earlier albums often included cover songs; however, since 2004, the group has recorded no more than two covers per album. In most covers, the songs' original titles were kept. Other songs, such as <a href="/wiki/Westlife" title="Westlife">Westlife</a>'s "Soledad", were simply given new Chinese titles. The lyrics are usually rewritten, but some songs, such as <a href="/wiki/Charlene_(singer)" title="Charlene (singer)">Charlene</a>'s "I've Never Been To Me", are sung in their original English. While the musical style is usually maintained, some covers, such as "Only Lonely" and "I.O.I.O", have a lighter, <a href="/wiki/Bubblegum_pop" title="Bubblegum pop">bubblegum pop</a> feel.</p>
<p>Older pieces and nursery rhymes are occasionally parodied. "Don't Wanna Grow Up" is a parody of <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._40_(Mozart)" title="Symphony No. 40 (Mozart)">Mozart's 40th Symphony</a>, and "London Bridge is Falling Down" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">倫敦大橋垮下來</span>) is based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Bridge_is_Falling_Down" title="London Bridge is Falling Down">children's song of the same name</a>. The verses of "Thanks for Your Gentleness" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">謝謝你的溫柔</span>) are original compositions, but the chorus is borrowed from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wu_Yue_Tian" title="Wu Yue Tian">Mayday</a>'s "Gentle and Soft".</p>
<p>S.H.E's covers have received less critical acclaim than their original works. During the group's career, original compositions have combined for nearly 20 awards, whereas covers have been lauded only twice.<sup class="reference" id="ref_Gnone"><a href="#endnote_Gnone">[g]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_film_career">Television and film career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Television and film career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/S.H.E_filmography" title="S.H.E filmography">S.H.E filmography</a></div>
<p>From late 2001 to early 2002, Hebe and Ella acted in the drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Magical_Love" title="Magical Love">Magical Love</a></i>. Ella starred as Juliet, a recent high school graduate who is constantly looking for her Romeo. Hebe portrayed the secondary role of Sha Sha, Juliet's self-conscious and assertive friend. Selina was absent because she was studying at the National Taiwan Normal University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> The songs "Remember" and "Belief" were used as the drama's opening and ending themes, respectively. In February, S.H.E signed on to be co-hosts on Jacky Wu's show, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guess_Guess_Guess" title="Guess Guess Guess">Guess Guess Guess</a></i>. During S.H.E's tenure on the show, ratings averaged over 3.5, regularly putting the show in the top 10 for weekend shows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> S.H.E stopped hosting <i>Guess Guess Guess</i> in July. In April 2003, the trio guest-hosted two episodes of the Taiwanese variety show, <i>Happy Sunday</i>, before signing on as co-hosts in May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> However, during S.H.E's <i>Happy Sunday</i> tenure, at the height of the <a href="/wiki/Severe_acute_respiratory_syndrome" title="Severe acute respiratory syndrome">SARS epidemic</a>, Hebe exhibited fever-like symptoms while coming home from Singapore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Since Ella was living with Hebe at the time, both were forced into quarantine for 10 days, leaving Selina as the only one in the group who could host the television show <i>Happy Sunday</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>

<p>All three S.H.E members appeared in the drama, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rose_(TV_series)" title="The Rose (TV series)">The Rose</a></i>, where Ella starred as Zheng Bai He, a girl suffering from low self-esteem. Selina played two secondary roles: her first character, Zhuang Zhe Qin, was a beautiful yet frail girl with a heartwarming personality. Her second character, Di Ya Man, is Qin's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doppelganger" title="Doppelganger">doppelganger</a> with an arrogant and domineering personality. Hebe portrayed Xiao Feng, a girl who quietly pursues Han Kui but Kui is infatuated with Bai He. Each episode of <i>The Rose</i> was originally estimated to cost NT$1 million;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therose_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-therose-53">[53]</a></sup> that number quickly rose to 1.3 million by the time filming had finished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therose_53-1"><a href="#cite_note-therose-53">[53]</a></sup> In spite of the high costs, <i>The Rose</i> was the highest rated television drama in its time slot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roseratings_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-roseratings-54">[54]</a></sup> At the 2004 Golden Bell Awards, which are presented by the <a href="/wiki/Government_Information_Office" title="Government Information Office">GIO</a> to honour the year's best television programs, <i>The Rose</i> was crowned Most Popular Drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> S.H.E sang "Flowers Have Blossomed" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">花都開好了</span>) for the drama's soundtrack. The song not only debuted at #1 on the UFO Music Charts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> but it also stayed on the YES 93.3 charts for ten weeks, holding the #1 position for two consecutive weeks.</p>
<p>On July 29, 2003, Ella performed a stunt as part of S.H.E's <i>Happy Sunday</i> segment. The stunt, which involved jumping off different floors of a building, was part of a public service video that explained proper fire escape procedures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellareturn_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-ellareturn-57">[57]</a></sup> On her first attempt, Ella followed protocol, jumped off the second floor of a building, and landed safely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellainjury_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ellainjury-11">[11]</a></sup> On her second attempt, she was asked to jump off the third floor. However, as she jumped off, Ella became nervous and failed to follow the safety procedures correctly, leading to her falling on a fire escape from twenty feet and shattering her hip.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Paramedics transported her to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neihu" title="Neihu">Neihu</a> Tri-Service General Hospital before being redirected to the National Taiwan University Hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellainjury_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-ellainjury-11">[11]</a></sup> Ella was released from the hospital after a 23-day stay,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> but she went home to <a href="/wiki/Pingtung_City" title="Pingtung City">Pingtung</a> for five months in order to recuperate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellareturn_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-ellareturn-57">[57]</a></sup> In spite of this incident, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_National_Tourist_Organization" title="Japan National Tourist Organization">Japan National Tourist Organization</a>, who had admired S.H.E's hosting abilities, asked <i>Happy Sunday</i> to promote Japanese tourism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> As a result, Selina and Hebe hosted "Yokoso! Japan", a special <i>Happy Sunday</i> segment that explored numerous facets of Japanese culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> In January 2004, Ella returned to hosting after her recuperation period,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> and appeared in the final two episodes of "Yokoso! Japan". However, during the segment's four-episode run, producers were already searching for S.H.E's replacements, and ultimately chose Taiwanese singer <a href="/wiki/Phil_Chang" title="Phil Chang">Phil Chang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> S.H.E discontinued hosting <i>Happy Sunday</i> once they began promoting the album <i>Magical Journey</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>Selina and Hebe, along with 28 other celebrities, were cast for the three-episode drama series <i>Happy New Year 2004</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> In February, both S.H.E members co-starred in the movie, <i>A Disguised Superstar</i> (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">冒牌天皇</span>), with <a href="/wiki/Miriam_Yeung" title="Miriam Yeung">Miriam Yeung</a>; in April, Hebe acted as the female love interest in the first episode of <i>Say Yes Enterprise</i> (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">求婚事務所</span>). The episode would earn a rating of 1.93, placing third behind <i>My Secret Garden II</i> (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">我的秘密花園II</span>) and <i>Snow Angel</i> (Chinese: <span lang="zh">雪天使</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> In October 2005, all three members of S.H.E starred in the drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Reaching_for_the_Stars_(TV_series)" title="Reaching for the Stars (TV series)">Reaching for the Stars</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="ref_Hnone"><a href="#endnote_Hnone">[h]</a></sup> and sang its opening theme, "Star Light". The drama focused on the fate of an electronics company whose chairman had just died. Selina played the role of Zhou Xinlei, the chairman's spoiled but good-natured daughter. Ella starred as Ren Jie, a clever girl who assumed the identity of the company's heiress to save her brother. Hebe portrayed Shen Xiaorou, a strong-willed policewoman who lives with her ailing grandmother. <i>Reaching for the Stars</i> had the misfortune of airing its pilot episode during the final episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Prince_Who_Turns_into_a_Frog" title="The Prince Who Turns into a Frog">The Prince Who Turns into a Frog</a></i>. While the average rating for the pilot episode of <i>Reaching for the Stars</i> was 1.17, the final episode of <i>The Prince Who Turns into a Frog</i> set a 2005 record for highest average episode rating (6.93) and highest episode rating (11.35).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> In the end, despite heavy investment in the series' production,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> <i>Reaching for the Stars</i> had only achieved mediocre television ratings. Nevertheless, Ella was nominated for Best Actress at the 2006 Golden Bell Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the spring of 2006, Ella pursued her own on-screen interests, and was cast for the lead female role of a Taiwanese idol drama, which was scheduled to air later that year. In January 2007, S.H.E and <a href="/wiki/Wu_Bai" title="Wu Bai">Wu Bai</a> were invited to voice over several characters for the Taiwanese rendition of <a href="/wiki/Luc_Besson" title="Luc Besson">Luc Besson</a>'s movie, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_and_the_Minimoys" title="Arthur and the Minimoys">Arthur and the Minimoys</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> During the same month, Selina and Hebe signed half-year contracts to be the co-hosts of <i>Guess Guess Guess</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> the show that they had hosted five years earlier. The drama starring Ella, <i><a href="/wiki/Hanazakarino_Kimitachihe" title="Hanazakarino Kimitachihe">Hanazakarino Kimitachihe</a></i>, finally began airing in November, and continued until its finale on March 4, 2007. Ella was featured as Lu Ruixi, an American tomboy who transfers schools so she can see her high jump idol every day. During its 15-episode run, the drama never relinquished its ratings crown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> S.H.E sang the song, "What to Do?" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">怎麽辦</span>) as the drama's opening theme. In June 2007, <a href="/wiki/Sanlih_E-Television" title="Sanlih E-Television">SET TV</a> selected Hebe to star in <i><a href="/wiki/Bull_Fighting_(TV_series)" title="Bull Fighting (TV series)">Bull Fighting</a></i> as the daughter of the man who owns 13th Street. S.H.E sang "How Have You Been Lately?" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">你最近還好嗎</span>) as the drama's ending theme.</p>
<p>In the movie CJ7, S.H.E sang one of its OST entitled "Qi Zai".</p>
<p>In May 2009, Ella was cast for the lead female role for the Taiwanese idol drama, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Down_With_Love_(TV_Series)" title="Down With Love (TV Series)">Down With Love</a> (就想賴著妳). The drama was aired on 31 January 2010. In the drama, Ella was featured as Yang Guo, kind-natured and innocent. When met with any kind of unlucky circumstances, she would always find a way to encourage and spur herself on, adopting a bright and optimistic outlook. S.H.E sang the song, "Loving You" (traditional Chinese: 愛上你) as the drama's ending theme.</p>
<p>In May 2010, Selina was invited as a guest host for the 700th episode of 'Guess Guess Guess' special edition, along with Jacky Wu. From 24 July 2010 onwards, Selina would be co-hosting 'Guess Guess Guess' with Halem Yu, with changes made to the television programme.</p>
<p>On 22 Oct 2010. Selina was seriously injured during an accident on the set of her new drama, I have a date with spring (我和春天有個約會) in Shanghai. She suffered from third degree burns in 54% of her body. Her co-star <a href="/wiki/Yu_Haoming" title="Yu Haoming">Yu Haoming (俞灏明)</a> was also seriously injured. On 19 Jan, 2011, Selina had finally made her first appearance in the press conference at the hospital. Selina was thankful for the concern, care, and blessings from the public she was also grateful to the nurses who took care of her saying that they were like angels, and was happy that she finally got to leave the hospital and go back home.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Endorsements">Endorsements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Endorsements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, S.H.E began promoting the first of many products, including those of Digimaster and beverage chain Fuzion Smoothie. Since then, the group has endorsed numerous companies, including Bobson Jeans (2003),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bausch_%26_Lomb" title="Bausch &amp; Lomb">Bausch &amp; Lomb</a> (2002–present),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> (2004–present),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cc_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-cc-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/China_Mobile" title="China Mobile">China Mobile</a> (2006–present).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> One of S.H.E's more notable commercials was for <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft" title="World of Warcraft">World of Warcraft</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wow_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-wow-6">[6]</a></sup> where the girls were put into the context of a fantasy gaming world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>S.H.E regularly contributes songs for endorsement campaigns. The songs "Too Much" and "Beauty Up My Life", from their debut album <i>Girls Dorm</i>, were used in commercials for <a href="/wiki/Sogo" title="Sogo">Sogo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wacoal" title="Wacoal">Wacoal</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> "Genesis" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">美麗新世界</span>) and "Watch Me Shine", from the 2002 album <i>Genesis</i>, were used for N-age, a South Korean online game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> "Piquancy", from 2004 album <i>Encore</i>, was used to promote the online game <i>New Legendary Twins</i> (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">新絕代雙嬌</span>). In collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit_(Taiwanese_band)" title="Fahrenheit (Taiwanese band)">Fahrenheit</a>, S.H.E sang "Always Open" for <a href="/wiki/7-Eleven" title="7-Eleven">7-Eleven</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<p>Companies frequently use S.H.E's music videos as advertising tools. The videos for "Genesis" and "Watch Me Shine" featured gameplay in N-age; "Excuse" (Chinese: <span lang="zh">藉口</span>) showed flashes of <a href="/wiki/Inventec" title="Inventec">Inventec</a>'s OKWAP phones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> Daphne (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">達芙妮女鞋</span>), a Chinese shoe company, used the songs "Super Model", "Laurel Tree Goddess" (Chinese: <span lang="zh">月桂女神</span>), "Good Mood Just Be Yourself" (Chinese: <span lang="zh">好心情Just Be Yourself</span>), and "Miss Universe" (Chinese: <span lang="zh">宇宙小姐</span>) for their promotional campaigns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> Top Girl, a Taiwan clothing company, had also used the song, "Girls' Be Strong" (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">女孩當自強</span>) from FM S.H.E for their promotional campaigns.</p>
<p>Ella was recently launched as the endorser of My Scheming Facial Mask, a top-selling facial mask which originated in Taiwan.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lack_of_originality">Lack of originality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Lack of originality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A review of S.H.E's 2005 album <i>Once Upon a Time</i> described the majority of the songs as "mundane", "predictable" and no better than "mushy pop standards", indicating that little has changed over their career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> Chris Taylor of the <i><a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Review" title="Taiwan Review">Taiwan Review</a></i> stated that their songs are hardly artistic,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ydcbreview_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-ydcbreview-33">[33]</a></sup> and that the girls are merely "industry-created" idols.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> According to <i><a href="/wiki/Taipei_Times" title="Taipei Times">Taipei Times</a></i> columnist Johnny Neihu, S.H.E's music simply "sucks. [It] really, really, really sucks. Really."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-neihu_86-0"><a href="#cite_note-neihu-86">[86]</a></sup> An article in <i>Style Weekly</i>, a Chinese fashion magazine, asserted that the trio's songs, compared to those of "talented musicians",<sup class="reference" id="ref_Inone"><a href="#endnote_Inone">[i]</a></sup> are lyrically weaker because they only reflect the female view of love.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group's regular covering of songs has also earned some ire over the years. After the release of the <i>Reaching for the Stars</i> soundtrack in 2005, there were accusations on Internet forums stating that the song "Star Light" sounded suspiciously similar to <a href="/wiki/Ayumi_Hamasaki" title="Ayumi Hamasaki">Ayumi Hamasaki</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/My_Story_(Ayumi_Hamasaki_album)#Tracklisting" title="My Story (Ayumi Hamasaki album)">About You</a>". In response, S.H.E declared that "Star Light" and "About You" were completely different, and did not even sound similar to each other.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> The same year, a <a href="/wiki/Netizen" title="Netizen">netizen</a> lashed out at the group for singing too many covers over the course of their career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-netizen_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-netizen-89">[89]</a></sup> However, S.H.E fans felt that the netizen was being slightly uptight about the issue, and stated that "as long as the song is nice, [there's no] point worrying about whether the song is a cover."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-netizen_89-1"><a href="#cite_note-netizen-89">[89]</a></sup> Despite the trio's claims that their covers were merely products of company decisions, some critics feel that the trio is still oblivious to the concept of artistic integrity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-neihu_86-1"><a href="#cite_note-neihu-86">[86]</a></sup> The use of covers is controversial for other artists as well as for S.H.E; an article in the Beijing times in 2007 asserted that the frequent use of cover songs to stay successful indicates the declining originality and quality of Chinese popular music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison_with_Twins">Comparison with Twins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Comparison with Twins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a 2015 review of female artists in <a href="/wiki/Greater_China" title="Greater China">Greater China</a>, S.H.E was given an 80% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Approval_rating" title="Approval rating">approval rating</a>, while archrivals <a href="/wiki/Twins_(group)" title="Twins (group)">Twins</a> received a mere 50%. The difference in quality was apparently so great that there was no reason for S.H.E to be concerned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> According to another review in early 2005, S.H.E still seemed to hold an advantage, beating out their <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> counterparts in yearly earnings, and amount of progress made since their debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> However, <i>Reaching for the Stars</i> received mediocre ratings, and the group was rumored to have made a controversial comment about <a href="/wiki/Political_status_of_Taiwan" title="Political status of Taiwan">Taiwan's political relationship with mainland China</a> by saying they were Taiwanese, not Chinese during an interview in <a href="/wiki/Lanzhou" title="Lanzhou">Lanzhou</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> The rumor was denied by them and the representatives from their company but it spread so quickly that it resulted in temporary falling out of favour with the Chinese media. By 2006, S.H.E had lost several product endorsements, and an entertainment review in that year gave Twins the advantage over the trio in almost every category, from song quality to concert presence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twinsman_94-0"><a href="#cite_note-twinsman-94">[94]</a></sup> Despite starring in several dramas, including the award-winning <i>The Rose</i>, S.H.E scored 2.5 out of 5 stars in the 2006 review.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twinsman_94-1"><a href="#cite_note-twinsman-94">[94]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_and_impact">Influence and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Influence and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical">Musical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Musical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By 2001, the vast majority of notable singing groups in Taiwan<sup class="reference" id="ref_Jnone"><a href="#endnote_Jnone">[j]</a></sup> were male. When S.H.E was formed, other girl groups were only beginning to earn their fame in the Taiwanese music industry, but few would last more than two years. Formed in 2000, <a href="/wiki/4_in_Love_(group)" title="4 in Love (group)">4 in Love</a> had similar success to S.H.E during their first year in the entertainment business, but broke up less than two years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> Among the former members of 4 in Love, <a href="/wiki/Rainie_Yang" title="Rainie Yang">Rainie Yang</a> is the only member to have continued her singing career. Other groups, such as Walkie Talkie (Chinese: <span lang="zh-Hant">錦繡二重唱</span>), were formed as a result of talent competitions, but were less notable during the course of their careers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ggrouphistory_96-0"><a href="#cite_note-ggrouphistory-96">[96]</a></sup> Over a year after S.H.E's formation, the state of girl groups in Taiwan had changed little. In January 2003, radio station UFM1003 released a list of its Top 10 Taiwanese Pop Groups for the year 2002. Although S.H.E was ranked first, they were the only all-female group on the list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the course of S.H.E's career, a significant number of musical groups were introduced by their companies as "the next S.H.E" with hopes of emulating their success. When Warner Music Taiwan wanted to form a three-person boy band, they planned on marketing them as "the male version of S.H.E" before coming up with the name "G-Boys".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gboys_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-gboys-2">[2]</a></sup> Some groups were formed to topple S.H.E's standing as Taiwan's premier pop group. Jungiery Star manager Sun Derong stated that <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=7_Flowers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="7 Flowers (page does not exist)">7 Flowers</a> was formed just to provide competition for S.H.E.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> Despite the increase in competition, S.H.E maintains a definitive stranglehold on the <a href="/wiki/Mandopop" title="Mandopop">Mandopop</a> group awards, suggesting that the formation of these new groups has done little to ruffle the trio's feathers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Baidu" title="Baidu">Baidu</a>'s year-end Top Search Terms survey, the most-searched movie of 2004 was <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_Flying_Daggers" title="House of Flying Daggers">House of Flying Daggers</a></i> (Chinese: <span lang="zh">十面埋伏</span>). The phrase logged more than 1.2 million searches, which was nearly twice as many as second place (<i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Fu_Hustle" title="Kung Fu Hustle">Kung Fu Hustle</a></i>). Baidu attributed this difference to the fact that S.H.E had released a song with the same name earlier that year. As a result, searches for the song were lumped together with the movie, thereby inflating the latter's search totals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup></p>
<p>S.H.E has also influenced television dramas, education systems, and even business operations. In the <i><a href="/wiki/I_Not_Stupid_Too" title="I Not Stupid Too">I Not Stupid Too</a></i> television series, Chengcai, the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_martial_arts" title="Chinese martial arts">wushu</a>-practicing protagonist, was courted by three members of the school's chess society: Shirley, Helen, and Elaine, who were collectively known as S.H.E.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> On a 2005 secondary school English examination, S.H.E was the subject of a reading comprehension passage that mentioned their involvement with <a href="/wiki/China_Central_Television" title="China Central Television">CCTV</a>'s annual Spring Festival Evening show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> Cliff Wu, the president of <a href="/wiki/Teradata" title="Teradata">Teradata</a>'s Greater China operations, was so amazed at how S.H.E affected his teenage daughters that he borrowed the group's name and transformed it into an acronym, summarizing the three main elements of Teradata's plan for entering the Chinese market. S represents "Strategy", while H and E represent "Human" and "Execution".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S.H.E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="refbegin columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 35em; -webkit-column-width: 35em; column-width: 35em;">
<ul>
<li><b>a.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Anone"><a href="#ref_Anone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> See <a href="/wiki/S.H.E#Television_and_film_career" title="S.H.E">Television and film career</a>.</li>
<li><b>b.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Bnone"><a href="#ref_Bnone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> Searching "女子第一天團" with "S.H.E" on Chinese search engines yields only results written after the release of <i>Encore</i>.</li>
<li><b>c.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Cnone"><a href="#ref_Cnone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> Although the English translations of the song and album are different, the Chinese names are the same (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">不想长大</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">不想長大</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Bù Xiǎng Zhǎng Dà</span></i>).</li>
<li><b>d.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Dnone"><a href="#ref_Dnone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> The article states that Ella is an alto, but there have been instances when Ella sings slightly above the alto range (<i>i.e.</i> into the mezzo soprano range) in songs such as "What to Do?" and "Don't Wanna Grow Up". Similarly Hebe and Selina are listed in the article as sopranos, but there have been songs, such as "Persian Cat", where they sang notes more fitting for a mezzo-soprano.</li>
<li><b>e.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Enone"><a href="#ref_Enone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> Yao wrote the lyrics for "Wisteria" (紫藤花), "A Vision Of Eternity" (一眼萬年), "I Love You" (我愛你) and "The Story of Romeo and Juliet" (茱羅記).</li>
<li><b>f.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Fnone"><a href="#ref_Fnone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> Fang's lyrics are found in five songs: "Riverside Park" (河濱公園), "Norse Mythology" (北歐神話), "Laurel Goddess" (月桂女神), "Tropical Rainforest" (熱帶雨林), and "Migratory Bird" (侯鳥). The last two songs, which Fang co-produced with Jay Chou, are both Annual Best Singles (2002 and 2004, respectively).</li>
<li><b>g.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Gnone"><a href="#ref_Gnone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> See <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_S.H.E" title="List of awards and nominations received by S.H.E">List of awards and nominations received by S.H.E</a>.</li>
<li><b>h.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Hnone"><a href="#ref_Hnone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> The Chinese name for <i>Reaching for the Stars</i>, Zhen Ming Tian Nü (真命天女), is derived from the Chinese name for the group <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a>, Tian Ming Zhen Nü (天命真女). Coincidentally, the plot of the drama revolves around three girls who seemed destined to meet each other.</li>
<li><b>i.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Inone"><a href="#ref_Inone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> The magazine article refers to artists like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richie_Ren" title="Richie Ren">Richie Ren</a> (also mentioned in the article) as "talented types", while S.H.E qualified as an "idol type".</li>
<li><b>j.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Jnone"><a href="#ref_Jnone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> The usage of "Taiwan" in this article, unless otherwise specified, refers to the Taiwanese music industry (as opposed to the music industries of China and Hong Kong).</li>
<li><b>k.</b><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Knone"><a href="#ref_Knone"><b><sup>^</sup></b></a></span> HIM announced the Xuite blog on April 20, 2007 initially as a countdown for the album <i>Play</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
