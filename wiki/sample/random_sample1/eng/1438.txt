<p><b>S7 Airlines</b>, legally <i>PJSC Siberia Airlines</i> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">ПАО «Авиакомпания "Сибирь"»</span> "PАО Aviakompania Sibir"), is an <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airline</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk_Oblast" title="Novosibirsk Oblast">Novosibirsk Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> with offices in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As of 2008, it was Russia's biggest domestic airline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Its main bases are <a href="/wiki/Domodedovo_International_Airport" title="Domodedovo International Airport">Domodedovo International Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tolmachevo_Airport" title="Tolmachevo Airport">Tolmachevo Airport</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S7_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S7_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>What is now S7 Airlines started in 1957 as "the <a href="/wiki/Tolmachevo_Airport" title="Tolmachevo Airport">Tolmachevo</a> united squadron" of the General Directorate of Civil Aviation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a>. After the Soviet Union disintegration and during 1990's Russian economic reforms a state-run "Siberia Airlines" was created based on the squadron in 1992 and later privatized in 1994. The same year Siberia was assigned an IATA airline code. Siberia Airlines was rebranded S7 Airlines in 2006.</p>

<p>In 1997, Siberia Airlines tried to buy <a href="/wiki/Vnukovo_Airlines" title="Vnukovo Airlines">Vnukovo Airlines</a>, to make Moscow its next main hub, but this didn't eventuate.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/1998_Russian_financial_crisis" title="1998 Russian financial crisis">1998 Russian financial crisis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vnukovo_Airlines" title="Vnukovo Airlines">Vnukovo Airlines</a> was heading towards <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a>, and Siberia Airlines advised it to merge, but Vnukovo refused. In 1999, Siberia Airlines signed a document for the Vnukovo Airlines take-over, in the event the airline ceased operations due to insolvency.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_since_the_2000s">Development since the 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S7_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development since the 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>S7 Airlines began merging with <a href="/wiki/Vnukovo_Airlines" title="Vnukovo Airlines">Vnukovo Airlines</a> in 2001. S7 Airlines had acquired all the aircraft from Vnukovo Airlines including <a href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-154" title="Tupolev Tu-154">Tupolev Tu-154</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-204-100" title="Tupolev Tu-204-100">Tupolev Tu-204-100</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Il-86" title="Ilyushin Il-86">Ilyushin Il-86</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2001, the airline absorbed <a href="/wiki/Baikal_Airlines" title="Baikal Airlines">Baikal Airlines</a> and then in 2004, the airline absorbed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chelyabinsk_Airlines" title="Chelyabinsk Airlines">Chelyabinsk Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enkor" title="Enkor">Enkor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2002, S7 Airlines (then known as Siberia Airlines) painted all Vnukovo Airlines fleet with Siberia Airlines livery and the airline began its service from <a href="/wiki/Vnukovo_International_Airport" title="Vnukovo International Airport">Moscow-Vnukovo</a>, but after some time it shifted all the flights, including the charters from <a href="/wiki/Sheremetyevo_International_Airport" title="Sheremetyevo International Airport">Moscow-Sheremetyevo</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Domodedovo_International_Airport" title="Domodedovo International Airport">Moscow-Domodedovo</a>.</p>
<p>The first western aircraft, <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A310" title="Airbus A310">Airbus A310s</a>, were acquired in 2004. In summer 2004, during the <a href="/wiki/Farnborough_Airshow" title="Farnborough Airshow">Farnborough Airshow</a>, the company signed a <a href="/wiki/Memorandum_of_understanding" title="Memorandum of understanding">memorandum of understanding</a> to purchase 50 of the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sukhoi_Russian_Regional_Jet" title="Sukhoi Russian Regional Jet">Sukhoi Russian Regional Jet</a>, with the first plane to be delivered in 2007. However, the airline subsequently dropped its plans to order this aircraft, citing that the aircraft's changed specifications no longer met its requirements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In line with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IATA" title="IATA">IATA</a> resolution, from December 2006 the airline began to publish its fares for international destinations originating in Russia in <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euros</a>, rather than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_dollar" title="US dollar">US dollars</a>. This resulted in a fare increase, as the conversion rate used was 1 euro = 1 US dollar. Fuel surcharges were also published in euros. Its domestic fares were still to be shown in the local currency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Also in December 2006, the airline became the second Russian air carrier to complete, and pass, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IATA" title="IATA">IATA</a> International Safety Audit (<a href="/wiki/IATA_Operational_Safety_Audit" title="IATA Operational Safety Audit">IOSA</a>), which is the first global air safety standard. On 27 September 2007, OAO Sibir Airlines /S7 Airlines/ received an official notice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IATA" title="IATA">IATA</a> when the carrier was entered in the register of operators <a href="/wiki/IATA_Operational_Safety_Audit" title="IATA Operational Safety Audit">IOSA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced in April 2007 that a new division had been set up within the airline, called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Globus_(airline)" title="Globus (airline)">Globus</a>. This division was to focus on charter flights for tourists to foreign holiday destinations. Initially, the aircraft for this division would be drawn from the mainline fleet, but during 2010–2014, 10 <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_Next_Generation" title="Boeing 737 Next Generation">Boeing 737–800</a> aircraft were leased with an all-economy layout, with the option for a further 10 aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 May 2007, the airline announced a proposed order for 15 <a href="/wiki/Boeing_787_Dreamliner" title="Boeing 787 Dreamliner">Boeing 787 Dreamliners</a> scheduled for delivery in 2014, with an option for 10 additional aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> However, the order was officially cancelled on 29 January 2009, with S7 stating that it was considering the possibility of taking the aircraft under a leasing scheme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> As of November 2008, all Soviet-made aircraft had left the fleet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>S7 joined the <a href="/wiki/Oneworld" title="Oneworld">Oneworld</a> <a href="/wiki/Airline_alliance" title="Airline alliance">alliance</a> in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CAPA_S7_profile_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CAPA_S7_profile-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2015, S7 Airlines made an offer to acquire a majority stake in bankrupt <a href="/wiki/Transaero" title="Transaero">Transaero</a>. However, the proposal was rejected by shareholders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, American band <a href="/wiki/OK_Go" title="OK Go">OK Go</a> partnered with S7 to film a "zero-g" music video, <a href="/wiki/Upside_Down_%26_Inside_Out" title="Upside Down &amp; Inside Out">Upside Down &amp; Inside Out</a>, aboard a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reduced_gravity_aircraft" title="Reduced gravity aircraft">reduced gravity aircraft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety2016021_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety2016021-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S7_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>On 4 October 2001, <a href="/wiki/Siberia_Airlines_Flight_1812" title="Siberia Airlines Flight 1812">Siberia Airlines Flight 1812</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-154M" title="Tupolev Tu-154M">Tupolev Tu-154M</a>, registration RA-85693, en route from <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a> to <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk" title="Novosibirsk">Novosibirsk</a> crashed into the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> off <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a>, after being hit with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S-200_Angara/Vega/Dubna" title="S-200 Angara/Vega/Dubna">S-200V</a> surface-to-air missile fired as part of a Ukrainian Air Defense exercise staged off Cape Opuk (or Chuluk) in <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a>. All 78 people on board were killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li>On 24 August 2004, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siberia_Airlines_Flight_1047" title="Siberia Airlines Flight 1047">Siberia Airlines Flight 1047</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-154" title="Tupolev Tu-154">Tupolev Tu-154</a>B2, registration RA-85556, en route from Moscow to Sochi <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_aircraft_bombings_of_August_2004" title="Russian aircraft bombings of August 2004">exploded and crashed due to a terrorist bombing</a> near Rostov-on-Don, Russia, killing all 46 people on board.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>On 9 July 2006, <a href="/wiki/S7_Airlines_Flight_778" title="S7 Airlines Flight 778">S7 Airlines Flight 778</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A310" title="Airbus A310">Airbus A310</a> carrying 193 passengers and 10 crew members, suffered a landing accident at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irkutsk_International_Airport" title="Irkutsk International Airport">Irkutsk International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. The jet failed to decelerate on landing, overran the runway and crashed into a concrete barricade. 124 people died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
