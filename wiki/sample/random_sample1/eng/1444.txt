<p>"<b>Sakura</b>" is the debut single of Japanese trio <a href="/wiki/Ikimono-gakari" title="Ikimono-gakari">Ikimono-gakari</a>, released in Japan on March 15, 2006. The single peaked at number 17 on the weekly <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> chart, charting for 31 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon-1">[1]</a></sup> It also reached number 182 on the yearly chart for 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> "Sakura" was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_Japan" title="Recording Industry Association of Japan">Recording Industry Association of Japan</a> (digital download single).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The A-side was used in a commercial promoting <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Telegraph_and_Telephone" title="Nippon Telegraph and Telephone">NTT</a>'s Denpo 115 service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon-1">[1]</a></sup> Track 3, "Sotsugyō Shashin", was included on the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Yumi_Matsutoya" title="Yumi Matsutoya">Yumi Matsutoya</a> tribute album "Shout At Yuming Rocks".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The single was certified gold for physical copies shipped in May 2011, and double platinum for ringtone downloads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
