<p><b>Scandinavian Airlines System Aktiebolag</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Oslo_Stock_Exchange" title="Oslo Stock Exchange">OSE</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www.oslobors.no/ob_eng/markedsaktivitet/stockOverview?newt__ticker=SAS+NOK" rel="nofollow">SAS+NOK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nasdaq_Nordic" title="Nasdaq Nordic">Nasdaq Stockholm</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www.nasdaqomxnordic.com/shares/shareinformation?Instrument=SSE13557" rel="nofollow">SAS</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.nasdaqomxnordic.com/shares/shareinformation?Instrument=CSE13584" rel="nofollow">SAS DKK</a>), trading as <b>SAS Group</b> and <b>SAS AB</b>, is an airline <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> headquartered in the <a href="/wiki/SAS_Fr%C3%B6sundavik_Office_Building" title="SAS Frösundavik Office Building">SAS Frösundavik Office Building</a> in <a href="/wiki/Solna_Municipality" title="Solna Municipality">Solna Municipality</a>, Sweden. It is the owner of the airlines <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_Airlines" title="Scandinavian Airlines">Scandinavian Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blue1" title="Blue1">Blue1</a> and 20% of <a href="/wiki/Wider%C3%B8e" title="Widerøe">Widerøe</a>. SAS used to own 19.9% of the now defunct Spanish airline <a href="/wiki/Spanair" title="Spanair">Spanair</a>. It also owns the aviation services companies <a href="/wiki/SAS_Business_Opportunities" title="SAS Business Opportunities">SAS Business Opportunities</a>, <a href="/wiki/SAS_Cargo_Group" title="SAS Cargo Group">SAS Cargo Group</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SAS_Ground_Services" title="SAS Ground Services">SAS Ground Services</a>, and <a href="/wiki/SAS_Technical_Services" title="SAS Technical Services">SAS Technical Services</a>. It holds minority ownership of <a href="/wiki/Air_Greenland" title="Air Greenland">Air Greenland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Air" title="Estonian Air">Estonian Air</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skyways_Express" title="Skyways Express">Skyways Express</a>. SAS Group is partially owned by the governments of Sweden, Denmark, and Norway, with a 21.4%, 14.3%, and 14.3% ownership, respectively. The remaining 50% is held by private owners, of which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foundation_Asset_Management" title="Foundation Asset Management">Foundation Asset Management</a> at 7.6% is the only significant one. The company is listed on the <a href="/wiki/Oslo_Stock_Exchange" title="Oslo Stock Exchange">Oslo Stock Exchange</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Stock_Exchange" title="Stockholm Stock Exchange">Stockholm Stock Exchange</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Stock_Exchange" title="Copenhagen Stock Exchange">Copenhagen Stock Exchange</a>.</p>
<p>The conglomerate was founded in 1951 as a merger between the three Scandinavian <a href="/wiki/Flag_carrier" title="Flag carrier">flag carriers</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerotransport" title="Aerotransport">Aerotransport</a> (ABA), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Det_Danske_Luftfartselskab" title="Det Danske Luftfartselskab">Det Danske Luftfartselskab</a> (DDL), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Det_Norske_Luftfartselskap" title="Det Norske Luftfartselskap">Det Norske Luftfartselskap</a> (DNL), after the three had been cooperating on international routes since 1946. Until 2001, the three national companies owned a fixed share of the SAS Group, after which the shares of the three companies were merged. The SAS Group previously has owned the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rezidor_Hotel_Group" title="Rezidor Hotel Group">Rezidor Hotel Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Braathens" title="Braathens">Braathens</a> and has had minority ownership of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bmi_(airline)" title="Bmi (airline)">bmi</a>, <a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">airBaltic</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Airways_International" title="Thai Airways International">Thai Airways International</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lan_Airlines" title="Lan Airlines">Lan Airlines</a>. SAS was a founder of the <a href="/wiki/Amadeus_CRS" title="Amadeus CRS">Amadeus Computerised Reservation System</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Star_Alliance" title="Star Alliance">Star Alliance</a>, where several of the group's airlines are members. SAS runs the frequent flyer program <a href="/wiki/EuroBonus" title="EuroBonus">EuroBonus</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SAS_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The airline was founded on 1 August 1946 when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Det_Danske_Luftfartsselskab" title="Det Danske Luftfartsselskab">Det Danske Luftfartselskab A/S</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerotransport" title="Aerotransport">AB Aerotransport</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Det_Norske_Luftfartsselskap" title="Det Norske Luftfartsselskap">Det Norske Luftfartselskap AS</a> (the flag carriers of Denmark, Sweden, and Norway) formed a <a href="/wiki/Business_alliance" title="Business alliance">partnership</a> to handle intercontinental traffic to <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>. Operations started on 17 September 1946. The companies then started coordination of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> operations in 1948 and finally merged to form the current SAS Consortium in 1951. When established the airline was divided between SAS Danmark (28.6%), SAS Norge (28.6%), and SAS Sweden (42.8%), all owned 50% by private investors and 50% by their respective governments. SAS gradually acquired control of the domestic markets in all three countries by acquiring full or partial control of several local airlines. In May 1997 SAS formed the global <a href="/wiki/Star_Alliance" title="Star Alliance">Star Alliance</a> network with <a href="/wiki/Air_Canada" title="Air Canada">Air Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Airways_International" title="Thai Airways International">Thai Airways International</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a>.</p>

<dl>
<dt>SAS Group Milestones</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>1918 Det Danske Luftfartselskab A/S (DDL), SAS' Danish parent company, is founded</li>
<li>1920 DDL is listed on the <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Stock_Exchange" title="Copenhagen Stock Exchange">Copenhagen Stock Exchange</a></li>
<li>1924 AB Aerotransport (ABA), SAS' <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> parent company, is founded</li>
<li>1927 Det Norske Luftfartselskap A/S (DNL), SAS' <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> parent company, is founded</li>
<li>1946 SAS is formed from Det Danske Luftfartselskab A/S (DDL), Det Norske Luftfartselskap A/S (DNL), and Svensk Interkontinental Lufttrafik AB (SILA). The first intercontinental flight is from <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>.</li>
<li>1951 DDL, DNL, and ABA form the present SAS Consortium.</li>
<li>1954 SAS is the world's first airline to fly the <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> - <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> polar route in regular scheduled service.</li>
<li>1955 SILA (which owns 50% of ABA) is quoted on the "Stockbrokers' List" in Sweden.</li>
<li>1957 SAS is the first airline to offer "round the world service over the <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">North Pole</a>" from <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> via <a href="/wiki/Anchorage,_Alaska" title="Anchorage, Alaska">Anchorage</a>.</li>
<li>1959 SAS enters the jet age. The first jet aircraft, the <a href="/wiki/Sud_Aviation_Caravelle" title="Sud Aviation Caravelle">Caravelle</a>, is introduced in service.</li>
<li>1960 SAS opens its first hotel, the SAS Royal Hotel Copenhagen. SAS helps set up <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Airways_International" title="Thai Airways International">Thai Airways International</a>, taking a 30% share in the joint venture.</li>
<li>1965 SAS is the first airline to introduce an electronic reservation system.</li>
<li>1967 DNL is listed on the <a href="/wiki/Oslo_Stock_Exchange" title="Oslo Stock Exchange">Oslo Stock Exchange</a>.</li>
<li>1971 SAS puts its first <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> jumbo jet into service.</li>
<li>1977 SAS sells its remaining stakes in Thai Airways.</li>
<li>1980 SAS opens its first hotel outside of <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>, the SAS <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a> Hotel. SILA is listed on the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Stock_Exchange" title="Stockholm Stock Exchange">Stockholm Stock Exchange</a>.</li>
<li>1981 SAS EuroClass is introduced on all <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> routes.</li>
<li>1982 SAS is named the most punctual <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airline</a> in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> for the first time.</li>
<li>1984 SAS receives the Air Transport World's distinction "Airline of the Year" for 1983.</li>
<li>1986 <a href="/wiki/Spanair" title="Spanair">Spanair</a> is founded.</li>
<li>1987 SAS cofounds the <a href="/wiki/Amadeus_CRS" title="Amadeus CRS">Amadeus</a> Computerised Reservation System (also known as <a href="/wiki/Global_Distribution_System" title="Global Distribution System">GDS</a>).</li>
<li>1989 SAS International Hotels owns 40% of Intercontinental Hotels Group. This stake is sold in 1992.</li>
<li>1994 SAS begins to refocus on airline operations in the SAS Group - selling a number of subsidiaries, along with the <a href="/wiki/Franchising" title="Franchising">franchise</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diners_Club" title="Diners Club">Diners Club</a> Nordic.</li>
<li>1996 SAS celebrates its 50th anniversary on August 1. SAS parent company changes its name to SAS Danmark A/S, SAS Norge ASA, and SAS Sverige AB.</li>
<li>1997 SAS is one of the founding members of <a href="/wiki/Star_Alliance" title="Star Alliance">Star Alliance</a>.</li>
<li>1998 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Botnia" title="Air Botnia">Air Botnia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Blue1" title="Blue1">Blue1</a>) becomes a wholly owned subsidiary of the SAS Group.</li>
<li>1999 The SAS Group becomes a majority owner of <a href="/wiki/Wider%C3%B8e" title="Widerøe">Widerøe</a>.</li>
<li>2001 A single SAS share is established. On July 6, SAS is listed on the stock exchanges in Stockholm, Copenhagen, and Oslo. Braathens is acquired by the SAS Group in December.</li>
<li>2002 Rezidor SAS Hospitality signs a master franchise agreement with Carlson Hotels Worldwide; the agreement comes to an end in 2009.</li>
<li>2003 SAS acquires 49% of the shares in <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Air" title="Estonian Air">Estonian Air</a>.</li>
<li>2004 Scandinavian Airlines Sverige, SAS Braathens, and Scandinavian Airlines Danmark are incorporated.</li>
<li>2006 SAS sells its remaining shares in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rezidor_Hotel_Group" title="Rezidor Hotel Group">Rezidor Hotel Group</a> chain.</li>
<li>2007 CEO and President Mats Jansson is inaugurated; SAS sells the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SAS_Flight_Academy" title="SAS Flight Academy">SAS Flight Academy</a>.</li>
<li>2010 CEO Jansson departs his position and is replaced by John S. Dueholm on an interim basis.</li>
<li>2011 Rickard Gustafson becomes the new permanent CEO.</li>
<li>2012 In January Spanair collapses and lead to write-downs of 1.7bn kronor by SAS.</li>
<li>2013 SAS sells 80% of the shares in Widerøe.</li>
<li>2014 SAS sells the cleaning part of SAS Ground Handling to Sodexo.</li>
<li>2015 SAS sells SAS Ground Handling in 14 Airports in Norway to Widerøe Ground Handling. SAS sells Blue1 to CityJet. In November Estonian Air collapses and SAS loss 2.5% of the shares.</li>
<li>2016 SAS sells its remaining 20% stake in Widerøe.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="SAS_Museum">SAS Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SAS_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: SAS Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The exhibits at the SAS Museum at <a href="/wiki/Oslo_Airport,_Gardermoen" title="Oslo Airport, Gardermoen">Oslo Airport, Gardermoen</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, represent an important part of Scandinavian civil aviation history. The museum collections cover Scandinavian Airlines System (SAS) as well as its parent companies: AB Aerotransport (ABA), Det Danske Luftfartselskab (DDL), and Det Norske Luftfartselskap (DNL). A museum was originally established in 1989 in the hangar area at <a href="/wiki/Oslo_Airport,_Fornebu" title="Oslo Airport, Fornebu">Oslo Airport, Fornebu</a> at the same time as the formation of the DNL/SAS Historic Society. It was built up through the efforts of a group of enthusiasts among retired and active SAS employees. The establishment of the new museum in 2003-2004 is a result of SAS feeling a responsibility to document the history of Scandinavian civil aviation. For this purpose, the airline has entered a partnership with its three national historic societies and the latter undertake the day-to-day work on a volunteer basis. SAS absorbs the rental cost of the museum building and has also provided depots for museum exhibits in Denmark and Sweden. The museum at Oslo's Gardermoen is therefore more than just a continuation of the facility at Fornebu – it is a completely new and considerably expanded Scandinavian museum. It is run by a board that includes representatives of the SAS consortium and the Swedish, Danish, and Norwegian historic societies.</p>
