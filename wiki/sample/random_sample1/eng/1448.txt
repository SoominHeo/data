<p>The <b>SCP Foundation</b> is a fictional organization that is the subject of a web-based <a href="/wiki/Collaborative_writing" title="Collaborative writing">collaborative writing</a> project. (SCP stands for "Special Containment Procedures", though it also refers to the organization's slogan of "Secure, Contain, Protect".) The stories generated by the project describe the exploits of the fictional Foundation, supposedly responsible for containing entities, locations, and objects that violate natural law (referred to as SCPs). The majority of the written works on the SCP Foundation website consist of "special containment procedures", each of which purports to be the containment process for a specific SCP. The website also contains several hundred "Foundation Tales", which are short stories set within the universe of the SCP Foundation.</p>
<p>The SCP Foundation series has received praise for its ability to convey horror through its scientific and academic writing style. The SCP Foundation has also inspired numerous spin-off works, including the video game <i><a href="/wiki/SCP_%E2%80%93_Containment_Breach" title="SCP â Containment Breach">SCP â Containment Breach</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview_of_series">Overview of series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SCP_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview of series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fictional_universe" title="Fictional universe">In-universe</a>, the SCP Foundation is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secret_organization" title="Secret organization">secret organization</a> entrusted by global governments to contain and study anomalous entities, locations, objects, and phenomena that defy natural law (or SCPs, as each is referred to by their Special Containment Procedures file number).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCiP_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-SCiP-6">[4]</a></sup> SCP objects, if left uncontained, pose a threat to humans â or, at the very least, to humanity's sense of reality and normalcy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCiP_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-SCiP-6">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The existence of SCPs is kept secret by the SCP Foundation to prevent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mass_panic" title="Mass panic">mass panic</a> and related chaos, and to allow human civilization to function normally. When an SCP is discovered, the SCP Foundation deploys agents to either collect and transport the SCP to a Foundation facility, or to contain it at its location of discovery if transport is not possible. Once SCPs are contained, they are studied by Foundation scientists. Prison inmates acquired by the Foundation (referred to as D-class) are used to interact with certain SCPs due to the danger posed by the SCPs and the expendability of the D-class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCiP_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-SCiP-6">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The SCP Foundation maintains documentation of the special containment procedures for all of the SCPs in its custody. These documents describe the SCPs and include instructions for keeping them safely contained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCiP_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-SCiP-6">[4]</a></sup> As of 2016, the SCP Foundation maintains containment procedures for over two thousand SCPs; new procedures are frequently added. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup></p>

<ul>
<li><b>SCP-055</b>: SCP-055 is something that causes anyone who examines it to forget its various characteristics, thus making it indescribable except in terms of what it is <i>not</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SCP-087</b>: SCP-087 is a staircase that appears to descend downwards forever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-net_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-net-8">[6]</a></sup> The staircase is inhabited by SCP-087-1, which is described as a face without a mouth, pupils or nostrils.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SCP-108</b>: SCP-108 is a Nazi bunker system that is only accessible through a portal found in a woman's nose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-io9-10">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SCP-173</b>: SCP-173 is a humanoid statue composed of <a href="/wiki/Rebar" title="Rebar">rebar</a>, concrete and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krylon" title="Krylon">Krylon</a> spray paint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> It is stationary when directly observed, but attacks people when line of sight with it is broken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-net_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-net-8">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SCP-294</b>: SCP-294 is a coffee machine that can dispense almost anything that does or can exist in liquid form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-net_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-net-8">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SCP-426</b>: SCP-426 is a toaster which can only be referred to in the first person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-net_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-net-8">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SCP-1171</b>: SCP-1171 is a home whose windows are always covered in <a href="/wiki/Condensation" title="Condensation">condensation</a>; by writing in the condensation on the glass, it is possible to communicate with an extra-dimensional entity whose windows are likewise covered in condensation. This entity bears significant hostility towards humans, but does not know that the Foundation members are humans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SCP-1609</b>: SCP-1609 is a <a href="/wiki/Mulch" title="Mulch">mulch</a> that teleports into the lungs of anyone who approaches it in an aggressive fashion or while wearing a uniform. It was previously a peaceful chair that teleported to whichever nearby person felt the need to sit down, but it entered its current aggressive state after being inserted into a <a href="/wiki/Woodchipper" title="Woodchipper">woodchipper</a> by a rival organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_style">Writing style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SCP_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Writing style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The majority of the works of fiction on the SCP Foundation website consist of individual pieces, each of which purports to be the "special containment procedures" documentation of a particular SCP object.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> In special containment procedures, the SCP is assigned a number.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newsom_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-newsom-11">[9]</a></sup> The SCPs are also sorted into classes, with the majority being classed as: "Safe", for objects understood well enough that they can be contained easily and will not manifest abilities without deliberate activation, "Euclid" for objects that are unpredictable or not well-understood but are not a high-level threat, and "Keter" for objects that require extensive containment procedures and which pose a large-scale threat to human life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> The documentation then gives an overview of the procedures needed to safely contain the SCP, followed by a description of the SCP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> Containment procedures may also contain images, the history of the entities, and addenda showing the results of tests on the SCPs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newsom_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-newsom-11">[9]</a></sup> The reports are written in a pseudo-scientific tone and often "<a href="/wiki/Sanitization_(classified_information)" title="Sanitization (classified information)">redact</a>" information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The SCP Foundation contains several hundred short stories referred to as "Foundation Tales".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> The stories often focus on members of the fictional organization interacting with various SCPs. Gregory Burkhart, writing for <a href="/wiki/Blumhouse_Productions" title="Blumhouse Productions">Blumhouse Productions</a>, noted that some of the Foundation Tales had a dark and bleak tone, while others were "surprisingly light-hearted".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is no central canon. Rather, each procedure or report forms its own continuity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> The genre has been described as <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_fantasy" title="Urban fantasy">urban fantasy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horror_fiction" title="Horror fiction">horror</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCiP_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-SCiP-6">[4]</a></sup> The content and the stories on the website are released under a <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons_license" title="Creative Commons license">Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0</a> license.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SCP_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The SCP Foundation series originated in the "paranormal" /x/ forum of <a href="/wiki/4chan" title="4chan">4chan</a>, where the first special containment procedure, SCP-173, was posted as a <a href="/wiki/Creepypasta" title="Creepypasta">creepypasta</a> thread during 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-11"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> Many other special containment procedures were followed shortly after, inspired by SCP-173. In 2008, the SCP Foundation series was transferred to its current <a href="/wiki/Wikidot" title="Wikidot">Wikidot</a> website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-12"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wikidot users are required to submit an application before they are allowed to contribute content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-13"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> Writers from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daily_Dot" title="Daily Dot">Daily Dot</a> and Bustle have noted that the website maintains strict quality control standards, and that sub-par content tends to be quickly deleted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-14"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bust_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-bust-16">[14]</a></sup> Internal forums are used by members of the website to evaluate and provide constructive criticism on posted stories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Wikidot website routinely holds creative writing contests. For example, in November 2014, the SCP Foundation held a "<a href="/wiki/Dystopia" title="Dystopia">Dystopia</a> Contest" in which its members were encouraged to submit writings about the Foundation set in a bleak or degraded world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cross_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-cross-18">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The SCP Foundation maintains a forum on <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Role-playing" title="Role-playing">role-playing</a> community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-15"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[17]</a></sup> Notable contributors to the SCP Foundation include screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Max_Landis" title="Max Landis">Max Landis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCPTweet_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-SCPTweet-20">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SCP_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The SCP Foundation has received largely positive reviews. Michelle Starr of <a href="/wiki/CNET" title="CNET">CNET</a> praised the creepy nature of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-net_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-net-8">[6]</a></sup> Gavia Baker-Whitelaw, writing for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daily_Dot" title="Daily Dot">Daily Dot</a>, praised the originality of the SCP Foundation and described it as the "most uniquely compelling horror writing on the Internet".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-16"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> She noted that special containment procedures rarely contained gratuitous gore. Rather, the horror of the series was often established through the reports' "pragmatic" and "deadpan" style, as well as through the inclusion of detail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-17"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> Lisay Suhay, writing for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Science_Monitor" title="Christian Science Monitor">Christian Science Monitor</a>, also praised the SCP Foundation's "tongue-in-cheek style".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cross_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-cross-18">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alex Eichler, writing for <a href="/wiki/Io9" title="Io9">io9</a>, noted that the series had varying levels of quality and that some of the reports were dull or repetitive. However, he praised the SCP Foundation for not becoming overly dark, and for containing more light-hearted reports. Additionally, he praised the wide variety of concepts covered in the report, and noted that the SCP Foundation contained writings that would appeal to all readers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-io9-10">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winston Cook-Wilson, writing for Inverse, compared the SCP Foundation to the writings of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H.P._Lovecraft" title="H.P. Lovecraft">H.P. Lovecraft</a>. Like Lovecraft, SCP Foundation case files generally lack action sequences and are written in a pseudo-academic tone. Cook-Wilson argued that both Lovecraftâs works and those of the SCP Foundation were strengthened by the tensions between their scientific tone and the horrific nature of the stories being told.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-off_works">Spin-off works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SCP_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spin-off works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The SCP Foundation has inspired multiple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indie_games" title="Indie games">independent video games</a>. One of these games is <i><a href="/wiki/SCP_%E2%80%93_Containment_Breach" title="SCP â Containment Breach">SCP â Containment Breach</a></i>. In the game, the protagonist is a member of D-class personnel who attempts to escape the facility he is stationed at during a containment breach. During the escape attempt, the protagonist is forced to avoid numerous SCPs, including SCP-173, a concrete statue that moves towards and attacks the player when not observed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_7-18"><a href="#cite_note-day-7">[5]</a></sup> Other SCP Foundation video games include <i>SCP-087</i> and <i>SCP-087-B</i>, both based on <a class="external text" href="http://www.scp-wiki.net/scp-087" rel="nofollow">SCP-087</a>, with the latter being loosely inspired by it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to video games, a stage play entitled "Welcome to the Ethics Committee" was performed in Dublin during 2014. The play focused on the SCP Foundation's Ethics Committee and its task of limiting unethical tactics utilized by the SCP Foundation to contain the paranormal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Belfield_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Belfield-23">[21]</a></sup> A live-action web-series also entered production in 2013 which was based on the SCP Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-net_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-net-8">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other spin-offs include The Wanderer's Library, a similar writing project focusing on one of the groups of interest, the Serpent's Hand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Links_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Links-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
