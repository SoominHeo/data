<p><b>Sociedad Deportiva Huesca, S.A.D.</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_football_clubs_in_Spain" title="List of football clubs in Spain">Spanish football club</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Huesca" title="Huesca">Huesca</a>, in the autonomous community of <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a>. Founded in 1960 it currently plays in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>, holding home games at <i><a href="/wiki/Estadio_El_Alcoraz" title="Estadio El Alcoraz">Estadio El Alcoraz</a></i>, which seats 5,500 spectators.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SD_Huesca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1910 <i>Huesca CF</i> was born, with Santos Solana as president. 16 years later it folded – after it had joined the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Spanish_Football_Federation" title="Royal Spanish Football Federation">Royal Spanish Football Federation</a> in 1922 – but in 1929 <i>CD Huesca</i>, being renamed <i>Unión Deportiva</i> in 1940, took its place, but the club again disappeared in 1956 due to financial problems. Lorenzo Lera was the first associate of the club, which was enrolled in the Federation with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blaugrana" title="Blaugrana">blaugrana</a> colours as its founding members were <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> supporters.</p>
<p>One of the first games of written reference was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_derby" title="Local derby">local derby</a> against <i>Bosco FC</i>, a 3–5 loss. In the mid-1920s the club turned professional and, in 1926, a match against <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> was played at the <i>Villa Isabel</i>, in a 2–2 draw; In 1951, Huesca first reached <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>.</p>
<p>On 29 March 1960 Sociedad Deportiva Huesca was born, first playing in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a> in 1977. In 2006 the club finished second in the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Federaci%C3%B3n_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Copa Federación de España">Copa Federación de España</a>, losing to <a href="/wiki/CD_Puertollano" title="CD Puertollano">Puertollano</a>; in that same season it narrowly avoided relegation to <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">Tercera División</a>, after a dramatic play-off against <a href="/wiki/Castillo_CF" title="Castillo CF">Castillo</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2006–07 Segunda División B">2006–07 campaign</a> the club reached the play-offs for promotion to the second level, having lost a two-legged final against <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_CF" title="Córdoba CF">Córdoba CF</a>. In <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2007–08 Segunda División B">the following season</a>, it returned to the "silver category".</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2008–09 Segunda División">2008–09's second division</a> was a regular one for Huesca, with the new league status being maintained with many rounds left. <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Castro" title="Rubén Castro">Rubén Castro</a>, loaned by <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_de_La_Coru%C3%B1a" title="Deportivo de La Coruña">Deportivo La Coruña</a>, was one of the most important players during the campaign, scoring 14 times, ninth-best in the league.</p>
<p>Relegation followed at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2012–13 Segunda División">2012–13</a> season, but the club returned to the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a> in 2015 after a first-place finish and eventually a two-leg play-off victory over <a href="/wiki/Hurac%C3%A1n_Valencia_CF" title="Huracán Valencia CF">Huracán Valencia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SD_Huesca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the 1971–72 season Huesca decided to build a new football stadium, the team's third in its history, located in the hills of San Jorge. In <i>El Alcoraz</i> (5,500-seats capacity), the 1974 Amateur Cup of Spain final took place.</p>

<ul>
<li><i>Name</i>: Ciudad Deportiva San Jorge</li>
<li><i>Size</i>: 100 x 64 m.</li>
<li><i>Grass</i>: Artificial (since 2005)</li>
