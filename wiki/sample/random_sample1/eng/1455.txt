<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_effects">Special effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SFX&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Special effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Special_effect" title="Special effect">Special effects</a>, illusions used in film, television, and entertainment</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sound_effect" title="Sound effect">Sound effects</a>, artificially created or enhanced sounds</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeia" title="Onomatopoeia">Onomatopoeia</a> is referred to in comics as "SFX" or "sound effects"</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_fiction">Science fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SFX&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Science fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/SFX_(magazine)" title="SFX (magazine)"><i>SFX</i> (magazine)</a>, a science fiction and fantasy magazine</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SFX_(Science_Fiction_Expo)" title="SFX (Science Fiction Expo)">SFX (Science Fiction Expo)</a>, a science fiction convention in Toronto, Canada</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_and_entertainment">Music and entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SFX&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music and entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/SFX_Entertainment" title="SFX Entertainment">SFX Entertainment</a>, an American <a href="/wiki/Tour_promoter" title="Tour promoter">concert and live events promoter</a> focused on the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">electronic dance music</a> (EDM) scene</li>
<li>SFX, a psychedelic trance musical band that changed their name to <a href="/wiki/Astral_Projection_(band)" title="Astral Projection (band)">Astral Projection</a></li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C2%B7F%C2%B7X" title="S·F·X">S·F·X</a></i>, a 1984 album by Japanese electronic artist <a href="/wiki/Haruomi_Hosono" title="Haruomi Hosono">Haruomi Hosono</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SFX&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/SFX_(software)" title="SFX (software)">SFX (software)</a>, an OpenURL link server</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Self-extracting_archive" title="Self-extracting archive">Self-extracting archive</a>, a compressed file with an embedded executable to decompress itself</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spread_Firefox" title="Spread Firefox">Spread Firefox</a>, a campaign to encourage the use of Mozilla Firefox</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/WebKit#JavaScriptCore" title="WebKit">SquirrelFish Extreme</a>, a JavaScript engine for WebKit</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ilford_SFX" title="Ilford SFX">Ilford SFX</a>, a photographic film</li>
<li>SFX, the prototype name for the <a href="/wiki/Super_Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Super Nintendo Entertainment System">Super NES</a> video game console</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Super_FX" title="Super FX">Super FX</a>, a coprocessor chip used in some Super NES video game cartridges</li>
<li>SFX (PSU), a form factor for a power supply unit for PCs (see <a href="/wiki/ATX#SFX" title="ATX">ATX#SFX</a>)</li>
<li>SFX, an abbreviation of Serial Femtosecond Crystallography, a technique to collect X-ray diffraction data on very small protein crystals with a <a href="/wiki/Free-electron_laser" title="Free-electron laser">Free-electron laser</a></li>
</ul>
