<p>The <b>men's military pistol</b> was one of the five <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooting_sports" title="Shooting sports">sport shooting</a> events on the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_1896_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 1896 Summer Olympics">Shooting at the 1896 Summer Olympics</a> programme.</p>
<p>16 competitors from four nations entered the military pistol match, held on 10 April. Each shot thirty rounds in five strings of six at a target 25 <a href="/wiki/Metre" title="Metre">metres</a> away. The winner, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Paine_(sharpshooter)" title="John Paine (sharpshooter)">John Paine</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, hit the target 25 times. His brother, <a href="/wiki/Sumner_Paine" title="Sumner Paine">Sumner Paine</a>, hit the target 23 times. Their American-made military revolvers (<a href="/wiki/Colt%27s_Manufacturing_Company" title="Colt's Manufacturing Company">Colt revolvers</a>) were far superior to the arms used by the other contestants.</p>
