<p><i><b>Mr. Simple</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">미스터 심플</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Miseuteo Shimpeul</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Reissue" title="Reissue">reissue</a> name <i><b>A-CHa</b></i>) is the award-winning fifth studio album of <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Boy_band" title="Boy band">boy band</a> <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior" title="Super Junior">Super Junior</a>. It was released on 3 August 2011 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM_Entertainment" title="SM Entertainment">SM Entertainment</a>, distributed by KMP Holdings and digitally release on 2 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 19 September 2011, a repackaged edition, re-titled <i>A-CHa</i> was released. It features four new tracks including title track "A-CHa" and "Superman" which was previously released in version B only.</p>
<p>This is the group's second album that features ten of the original 13 members, the first being their previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bonamana" title="Bonamana">Bonamana</a></i>, and the last one with member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heechul" title="Heechul">Heechul</a>, who enlisted for his <a href="/wiki/Conscription_in_South_Korea" title="Conscription in South Korea">mandatory military service</a> in September 2011.</p>
<p>At the year end award shows of 2011, the album won the <i>Disk Daesang Award</i> at the 26th <a href="/wiki/Golden_Disk_Awards" title="Golden Disk Awards">Golden Disk Awards</a> and 21st <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Music_Awards" title="Seoul Music Awards">Seoul Music Awards</a>, as well as the <i>Album of the Year</i> at the 13th <a href="/wiki/Mnet_Asian_Music_Awards" title="Mnet Asian Music Awards">Mnet Asian Music Awards</a>. The album, including the repackaged version, is listed as the 2nd best-selling album of the year for 2011. According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Chart" title="Gaon Chart">Gaon Chart</a>, as of the end of December 2012, the album has sold a cumulative total of 543,623 copies domestically.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_development">Background and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Simple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 31, 2011, leader Leeteuk announced during the interview, "At the earliest, we’ll be coming out with our fifth album this summer."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Leeteuk and Eunhyuk confirmed on their radio show "Super Junior Kiss the Radio" on June 1, 2011 that they had begun recording their upcoming album that day. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Heechul" title="Kim Heechul">Heechul</a> <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweeted</a> on June 2, 2011 saying that the group "may be going crazy with preparations for our fifth album, but when it’s finally released, you’re all going to go mad."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> American dancer <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Wormald" title="Kenny Wormald">Kenny Wormald</a> posted on his official Twitter on July 11, 2011 that he had started the choreography rehearsal for the group's upcoming new single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On the same day, Leeteuk and Eunhyuk stated on their radio show "Super Junior Kiss the Radio" that songwriter Jinu (hitchhiker) had contributed something for the new album. Shim Jae Won, current choreographer and former member of the disbanded SM group <a href="/wiki/Black_Beat" title="Black Beat">Black Beat</a> tweeted two pictures of the choreography practice of the group showing the lower half of their bodies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first of the teaser photos was released on July 20, 2011 featuring Eunhyuk. Other photos followed, in order of: Donghae, Leeteuk, Shindong, Kyuhyun, Sungmin, Ryeowook, Yesung, Siwon and Heechul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nate_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nate-6">[6]</a></sup> On the same day, it was announced that the album is set for an August 3 release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nate_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nate-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_and_MV">Music and MV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Simple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music and MV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album is mostly <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a>, with the title track, "Mr. Simple", being the last installation of the "SJ Funky" genre in which the group has been consistently pursuing since "<a href="/wiki/Sorry,_Sorry" title="Sorry, Sorry">Sorry, Sorry</a>" (2009) and "<a href="/wiki/Bonamana" title="Bonamana">Bonamana</a>" (2010). Songs encompassed with synthpop elements include "Opera", "Be My Girl", "Walkin'" and "My Love My Kiss My Heart". Other tracks included in the album are the country-flavored tracks "Good Friends" and "Sunflower", an electropop number "Feels Good", a cover of a 1996 song by Jinu (hitchhiker) entitled "White Christmas", ballads "Storm" and "Memories", a <a href="/wiki/Lee_Donghae" title="Lee Donghae">Donghae</a>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/One_Way_(Korean_band)" title="One Way (Korean band)">Chance</a>-written track "Y", as well as the inclusion of subgroup <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-M" title="Super Junior-M">Super Junior-M</a>'s Korean version of their 2011 title track "<a href="/wiki/Perfection_(EP)" title="Perfection (EP)">Perfection</a>", in which it serves a purpose "to give a chance to Korean fans to enjoy the music of Super Junior-M which is appearing in various stages in Asia." Meanwhile, for the B-version of the album, a further-included track "Superman" is described to be "a minimalist hip-hop track which feature the members singing in a low vocal range."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 22 to 24, 2011, the group filmed the music video for their new lead single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Two video teasers were released on August 1, 2011, in which the first teaser gained a million views on YouTube a day after its release and 2,500,000 views by the second day. Meanwhile, the second teaser passing a million views by the second day. The music video reached more than half a million views within the first few hours of release. It passed 1.5 million views by the end of the first day, and 3 million by the end of the second, views. Currently, the music video has over 100 million views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On August 26, 2011, a music video for the album's supporting single, "Superman", was released on their YouTube channel, featuring clips from their <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_Show_3_Tour" title="Super Show 3 Tour">Super Show 3 Tour</a></i> as well as the photoshoots and music video making for "Mr. Simple".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 1, 2015, the music video officially reached 100 million views on YouTube.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion_and_release">Promotion and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Simple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Promotion and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The group held a press conference at the Imperial Palace Hotel in Seoul on August 4, which attracted over 200 reporters, including 70 foreign journalists from Japan, China, Taiwan, Thailand, Hong Kong and Singapore<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The group expressed that they will promote the album for as long as possible till Heechul's enlistment date and also to continue with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_Show_4_Tour" title="Super Show 4 Tour">Super Show 4 World Tour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></i></p>
<p>Super Junior had their debut performance of "Mr. Simple" and a non-album track "Superman" at Music Bank on August 5, 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> followed by Music Core the following day,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">Inkigayo</a> after that<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and on August 11 on M! Countdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> However, promotions for the song ended on August 28 due to the enlistment of member Heechul to the army on September 1, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album was released in 11 countries across Asia.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-21">[21]</a></sup> including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Taiwan</a> on September 6, 2011 by <a href="/wiki/Avex_Taiwan" title="Avex Taiwan">Avex Taiwan</a>. The album was supposed to be pre-released in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Records_(Philippines)" title="Universal Records (Philippines)">Universal Records</a> on October 1, 2011 but was moved to October 8, citing manufacturing problems caused by <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Nesat_(2011)" title="Typhoon Nesat (2011)">typhoon Nesat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The Type A album was officially released in the country on November 24.</p>
<p>After the released of "Mr Simple" in Japanese, the group followed-up with the release of "Opera" in Japanese. The single album also include the travel-themed mid-tempo ballad "Way", Japanese original music video and making-of DVD. It has been available for pre-order from 11 March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Simple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at number 3 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> World Albums Chart for the week of 20 August 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-24">[24]</a></sup> It peaked at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a> for the month of August, with 287,427 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Meanwhile, the repackaged album, <i>A-CHa</i>, which was released on 19 September, debuted at number three with 97,210 copies sold.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>At the year-end list of album sales for 2011, complied by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Chart" title="Gaon Chart">Gaon Chart</a>, the album is listed as the second best-selling album of the year with 343,348 copies sold and the repackaged version at number seven with 129,894 copies sold. Thus making a cumulative total sale of 473,242 copies in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On 12 April 2012, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Chart" title="Gaon Chart">Gaon Chart</a> reported that up till the end of March 2012, the album has sold a cumulative total of 502,830 copies, making it the first album in four years to exceed half a million in sales since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Gun_Mo" title="Kim Gun Mo">Kim Gun Mo</a>'s eighth album <i>He-story</i> in 2003 and <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">TVXQ</a>'s fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Mirotic" title="Mirotic">Mirotic</a></i> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-halfmillion_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-halfmillion-27">[27]</a></sup> The released in the Philippines on October 8, peaked at number one in <i>Odyssey Weekly Album Chart</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> After a week of selling the album reached gold status with standout sales of 7,500. This was the group's 5th number one album and 4th album to certify gold status in the country making them the only Korean act to do so.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
