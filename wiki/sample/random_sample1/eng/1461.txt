<p>The <b>SIG Pro</b> is a series of <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_pistol" title="Semi-automatic pistol">semi-automatic pistols</a> developed and manufactured jointly by <a href="/wiki/Schweizerische_Industrie_Gesellschaft" title="Schweizerische Industrie Gesellschaft">Schweizerische Industrie Gesellschaft</a> (SIG) and <a href="/wiki/Sauer_%26_Sohn" title="Sauer &amp; Sohn">J. P. Sauer &amp; Sohn</a>. It was the first <a href="/wiki/Polymer" title="Polymer">polymer</a>-frame handgun from either company and one of the first pistols to feature a built-in universal accessory rail and interchangeable grips.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ayoob_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ayoob-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The SIG Pro was marketed as a lightweight and compact alternative to the "legacy" SIG Sauer handguns in an increasingly competitive and budget-oriented law enforcement market. The pistol is the result of continued collaboration between two famous <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> arms makers: SIG (now <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Arms" title="Swiss Arms">Swiss Arms AG</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Neuhausen_am_Rheinfall" title="Neuhausen am Rheinfall">Neuhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sauer_%26_Sohn" title="Sauer &amp; Sohn">Sauer &amp; Sohn</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. As with other joint ventures between the two companies, these pistols are sometimes referred to as "SIG-Sauer". It was originally developed as a <a href="/wiki/.40_S%26W" title=".40 S&amp;W">.40 S&amp;W</a>–caliber <a href="/wiki/Service_pistol" title="Service pistol">service pistol</a> and introduced in June 1998 with the <b>SP 2340</b> model, followed shortly by a version in <a href="/wiki/.357_SIG" title=".357 SIG">.357 SIG</a> (which retained the same model designation). About one year later, a <a href="/wiki/9%C3%9719mm_Parabellum" title="9×19mm Parabellum">9×19mm Parabellum</a> variant, the <b>SP 2009</b>, was introduced and entered production in response to demand for the type.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ayoob_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ayoob-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_details">Design details</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_Sauer_Pro_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design details">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The SIG Pro is a <a href="/wiki/Recoil_operation#Short_recoil_operation" title="Recoil operation">short recoil operated</a>, <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_pistol" title="Semi-automatic pistol">locked breech semi-automatic pistol</a>. It uses a traditional Browning cam-operated locking system. The barrel locks into the slide via enlarged ejection port.</p>
<p>The frame is made from <a href="/wiki/Polymer" title="Polymer">polymer</a>, with detachable grip panels. Grip panels can be easily replaced and are available in several different sizes and shapes.</p>
<p>The overall design is slightly different from earlier SIG Sauer pistols such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SIG_P220" title="SIG P220">P220</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SIG_P226" title="SIG P226">P226</a>, as there is no separate disassembly lever on the frame and the <a href="/wiki/Slide_stop" title="Slide stop">slide stop</a> lever looks quite different.</p>
<p>The standard SIG Pro does not have a manual safety but it does incorporate a decocking lever (lowers the hammer without striking the firing pin), trigger-bar disconnect (disconnects the trigger when the slide is out of battery), automatic firing pin lock (does not free the firing pin until the trigger is depressed) and a hammer safety intercept notch (prevents the hammer from striking the rear of the firing pin until the trigger is depressed).</p>

<p>The Sig Pro comes standard as a double-action/single action pistol with 10 lb and 4.5 lb trigger pulls, respectively. Double-action only conversion kits are also available from SIG.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>SP 2340</b>: The original pistol introduced in 1998. It has a rounded trigger guard and is chambered for <a href="/wiki/.40_S%26W" title=".40 S&amp;W">.40 S&amp;W</a> and <a href="/wiki/.357_SIG" title=".357 SIG">.357 SIG</a>.</li>
<li><b>SP 2009</b>: <a href="/wiki/9%C3%9719mm_Parabellum" title="9×19mm Parabellum">9×19mm Parabellum</a> version announced in 1999.</li>
<li><b>SPC 2009</b>: A shorter version of the SP 2009 with a 3.58" barrel length and only available in 9 mm. It is equipped with the SIG accessory rail system and the trigger guard is rounded. This is the basis for the SPC 2022</li>
<li><b>SP2009-9-BMS</b>: A variant of the standard SP 2009 with a slide-mounted manual safety and short trigger. These were offered for a short time to the U.S. civilian market.</li>
<li><b>SP 2022</b>: A modified version of the SP 2009/2340. It entered service with the French Police, Customs, Penitentiary administration and <i><a href="/wiki/National_Gendarmerie" title="National Gendarmerie">Gendarmerie</a></i> in 2002 for a planned service life of 20 years, hence the name. 460,000 rounds have been used for testing it before adoption by a sampling of future users and modifications of the SIG Pro followed to produce the 2022. It is available in 9×19mm, .40 S&amp;W and .357 SIG. The pistol is fitted with an integral <a href="/wiki/Picatinny_rail" title="Picatinny rail">Picatinny rail</a> instead of the proprietary rail of the other models and the front trigger guard is shaped differently so as to allow a finger hold. It can also be equipped with a threaded barrel and detachable <a href="/wiki/Suppressor" title="Suppressor">suppressor</a> and the fixed <a href="/wiki/Iron_sights" title="Iron sights">open sights</a> are dovetailed into the slide.</li>
<li><b>SPC 2022</b>: A more compact version of the SP 2022 that is only available in 9×19mm. The SPC 2022 has a slightly shorter barrel and slide, as well as flat magazine base plates which make its grip shorter by 7 mm. It is equipped with a Picatinny rail and a finger groove trigger guard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
