<p>The <b>SG 550</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Assault_rifle" title="Assault rifle">assault rifle</a> manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Arms" title="Swiss Arms">Swiss Arms AG</a> (formerly <a href="/wiki/Schweizerische_Industrie_Gesellschaft" title="Schweizerische Industrie Gesellschaft">Schweizerische Industrie Gesellschaft</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Neuhausen_am_Rheinfall" title="Neuhausen am Rheinfall">Neuhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. "SG" is an abbreviation for <i>Sturmgewehr</i>, or "assault rifle". The rifle is based on the earlier <a href="/wiki/5.56%C3%9745mm_NATO" title="5.56×45mm NATO">5.56mm</a> <a href="/wiki/SIG_SG_540" title="SIG SG 540">SG 540</a> and is also known as the <b>Fass 90</b> (<i>Fusil d'assaut</i> 90/<i>Fucile d'assalto</i> 90) in French/Italian or <b>Stgw 90</b> in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> (<i>Sturmgewehr</i> 90).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1978, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_Switzerland" title="Military of Switzerland">Swiss Army</a> formulated requirements for a successor to the <a href="/wiki/SIG_SG_510" title="SIG SG 510">Stgw 57</a> <a href="/wiki/Battle_rifle" title="Battle rifle">battle rifle</a> (known commercially as the SG 510) using the <a href="/wiki/7.5%C3%9755mm_Swiss" title="7.5×55mm Swiss">7.5×55mm Schmidt–Rubin</a> <a href="/wiki/Cartridge_(firearms)" title="Cartridge (firearms)">cartridge</a>. Emphasis was placed on <a href="/wiki/Modular_weapon" title="Modular weapon">modularity</a>; the weapon family was to include several variants of the base design, including a compact <a href="/wiki/Carbine" title="Carbine">carbine</a> that would be issued to rear-echelon and support troops, commanding staff, vehicle crews, <a href="/wiki/Special_operations" title="Special operations">special operations</a> personnel and <a href="/wiki/Paratrooper" title="Paratrooper">paratroopers</a>. Another aim was to reduce the overall weight of the rifle while retaining comparable or improved accuracy out to 300 m. The solicitation was narrowed down to two designs: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/W%2BF_C42" title="W+F C42">W+F C42</a> (developed by the state-owned <a href="/wiki/Waffenfabrik_Bern" title="Waffenfabrik Bern">Waffenfabrik Bern</a>, using both 6.45×48mm and 5.6×45mm cartridges) and the <b>SG 541</b> (developed by SIG). In 1981, the experimental <a href="/wiki/6.45%C3%9748mm_XPL_Swiss" title="6.45×48mm XPL Swiss">6.45mm GP 80</a> cartridge was rejected in favor of the more conventional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/5.6mm_Gw_Pat_90" title="5.6mm Gw Pat 90">5.6×45mm GP 90</a> round (with a 4.1 g, <a href="/wiki/Tombac" title="Tombac">tombac</a>-jacketed, <a href="/wiki/Lead" title="Lead">lead</a> core projectile) that is the Swiss equivalent to <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>'s standard 5.56×45mm cartridge.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 1983, the decision to adopt the SG 541 was publicly announced (the designation of the rifle was changed in October of the following year to SG 550, while the carbine version became known as the <b>SG 551</b>). Production began in 1986 and four years later the rifle was officially accepted into service in 1990, hence the military designation Stgw 90. Over 600,000 rifles have been delivered since then and production for the military has now ceased.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operating_mechanism">Operating mechanism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Operating mechanism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Caroline-demontee-p1000529.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1233" data-file-width="2472" height="110" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Caroline-demontee-p1000529.jpg/220px-Caroline-demontee-p1000529.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Caroline-demontee-p1000529.jpg/330px-Caroline-demontee-p1000529.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Caroline-demontee-p1000529.jpg/440px-Caroline-demontee-p1000529.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The SG 550 is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Selective-fire" title="Selective-fire">selective-fire</a> 5.56×45mm NATO <a href="/wiki/Assault_rifle" title="Assault rifle">assault rifle</a> firing from a <a href="/wiki/Closed_bolt" title="Closed bolt">closed bolt</a>. It has a <a href="/wiki/Gas-operated_reloading" title="Gas-operated reloading">gas-actuated</a> piston-driven <a href="/wiki/Gas-operated_reloading#Long-stroke_piston" title="Gas-operated reloading">long-stroke operating system</a> derived from the <a href="/wiki/SIG_SG_540" title="SIG SG 540">SIG SG 540</a> series of rifles, which uses burnt powder gases vented through a port in the <a href="/wiki/Gun_barrel" title="Gun barrel">barrel</a> to power the weapon's moving parts. Once inside the gas cylinder, propellant gases pass through an L-shaped channel machined in the piston head and are directed forward towards the gas valve. The pressure build-up in front of the piston head pushes the piston and bolt carrier rearward. As the piston is driven back, the gas port and the L-shaped channel move out of alignment, cutting off the supply of gas to the cylinder. Surplus gas and powder residues are evacuated through an exhaust port in the gas cylinder. This system ensures that only the precise volume of gas required to overcome the mass and resistance of the rifle's moving assembly is admitted from the barrel. The manually adjustable gas valve has two settings, one for normal operation, and the second setting for use in the presence of heavy fouling or icing.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rotating_bolt" title="Rotating bolt">rotary bolt</a> locking mechanism consists of two <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> locking lugs that engage locking recesses in the breech, and is identical to that used in the SG 540. A spring-loaded <a href="/wiki/Extractor_(firearms)" title="Extractor (firearms)">extractor</a> is incorporated into the bolt while a fixed protrusion on one of the receiver's internal guide rails ejects the spent cartridge casings.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="2560" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg/220px-Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg/330px-Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg/440px-Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Caroline-pontet-p1000527.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The ergonomics of the rifle provide the shooter with easy access to various controls. The ambidextrous fire selector/safety is manipulated by the thumb of the shooting hand.</div>

<p>The rifle is hammer-fired and has a trigger mechanism with an <a href="/wiki/Ambidexterity#Firearms" title="Ambidexterity">ambidextrous</a> <a href="/wiki/Safety_(firearms)" title="Safety (firearms)">safety</a> and <a href="/wiki/Selective_fire" title="Selective fire">fire selector</a> switch that has 4 settings: "S"—safe, "1"—single fire, "3"—3-round <a href="/wiki/Burst_mode_(weapon)" title="Burst mode (weapon)">burst</a> and "20"—fully <a href="/wiki/Automatic_firearm" title="Automatic firearm">automatic fire</a>. The 3-round burst mode "3" and the fully automatic "20" position can be disabled by a rotating safety guard to avert accidentally activating the continuous fire mode. The <a href="/wiki/Trigger_(firearms)" title="Trigger (firearms)">trigger</a> is enclosed in a pivoting trigger guard which can be folded down to the left or right side allowing for unhindered operation with winter gloves. The trigger pull is approximately 35 <a href="/wiki/Newton_(unit)" title="Newton (unit)">N</a> (7.9 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pound-force" title="Pound-force">lb<sub>f</sub></a>)</p>
<p>The firearm is fed by lightweight 20-round box magazines, 30-, 10- and 5-round magazines are also available. The magazines are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Injection_molding" title="Injection molding">molded</a> from a translucent polymer and can be locked together using studs in order to facilitate quicker reloading; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jungle_style" title="Jungle style">Jungle style</a>. The empty weight of a 20-round magazine is 95 g (3.4 oz) and 110 g (3.9 oz) for a 30-round magazine.</p>
<p>A bolt hold-open device locks the bolt carrier assembly open after expending the last cartridge from the <a href="/wiki/Magazine_(firearms)" title="Magazine (firearms)">magazine</a> and is released by lifting the bolt catch lever located on the left side of the <a href="/wiki/Receiver_(firearms)" title="Receiver (firearms)">receiver</a>. Alternatively, a left-handed shooter may release the bolt by pulling the rubber-coated charging handle to rear a short distance.</p>
<p>The SG 550 has a side-folding skeletonized <a href="/wiki/Stock_(firearms)" title="Stock (firearms)">buttstock</a> (folds to the right side of the receiver) and a <a href="/wiki/Aluminium_alloy" title="Aluminium alloy">lightweight aluminium</a> <a href="/wiki/Bipod" title="Bipod">bipod</a> that folds into grooves in the lower handguard. The hinged stock is firmly locked in the folded position by a socket in the butt which clips into a plastic stud on the handguard; a firm pull will release the stock which is then swung into the closed position and locked by a button catch. A collapsible side-folding stock is also available. The stock, <a href="/wiki/Pistol_grip" title="Pistol grip">pistol grip</a>, and handguards are made of a high-strength <a href="/wiki/Polymer" title="Polymer">polymer</a>, and are produced in either green or black colour options. The <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> receiver housing and several other components are manufactured using stamping and welding; external steel surfaces are finished with a ceramic-reinforced enamel coat known as Ilaflon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The gas system's components are made of <a href="/wiki/Stainless_steel" title="Stainless steel">stainless steel</a>. The barrel, bolt, bolt carrier, and firing pin are all made with steel that has been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nitridization" title="Nitridization">gas nitrided</a>, hardened and tempered. The bolt and carrier, along with most other components internal to the receiver undergo a <a href="/wiki/Parkerizing" title="Parkerizing">phosphating</a> process.</p>
<p>The heavy, cold hammer-forged barrel is screwed into the receiver and is equipped with a slotted "bird cage" type <a href="/wiki/Flash_suppressor" title="Flash suppressor">flash suppressor</a> that is also used to launch <a href="/wiki/Rifle_grenade" title="Rifle grenade">rifle grenades</a> (using standard, live <a href="/wiki/Ammunition" title="Ammunition">ammunition</a>) or attach a knife <a href="/wiki/Bayonet" title="Bayonet">bayonet</a> (the bayonet is supported by a lug located at the base of the gas block). The rifled barrel has 6 right-hand grooves and the Swiss Army specification 254 mm (1:10 in) rifling twist rate is optimized for Swiss military GP 90 ammunition. An export-oriented barrel with a 178 mm (1:7 in) twist rate is also available, designed to stabilize 5.56×45mm NATO rounds with the heavier SS109 projectile.</p>
<p>All rifles are <a href="/wiki/Proof_test#Firearms" title="Proof test">test fired</a> for accuracy and function prior to leaving the factory at the manufacturer's underground 300 m test range. Random new rifles out of production were tested on a machine rest. In a 24 single shot string starting with a cold weapon and using GP 90 ammunition, the 50% <a href="/wiki/Windage" title="Windage">windage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elevation_(ballistics)" title="Elevation (ballistics)">elevation</a> dispersion of any individual weapon must have been within an 11 cm (4.3 in) group at 300 m, the 50% windage and elevation dispersion must have averaged 7 cm (2.8 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The SG 550 series rifles have a 540 mm (21.3 in) long sight radius and are equipped with <a href="/wiki/Iron_sights" title="Iron sights">iron sights</a> adjustable for both windage and elevation. The sights are similar to those on some <a href="/wiki/Heckler_%26_Koch" title="Heckler &amp; Koch">Heckler &amp; Koch</a> weapons, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HK_G3" title="HK G3">HK G3</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HK_MP5" title="HK MP5">HK MP5</a>. The sights consist of a rear, rotating <a href="/wiki/Diopter_sight" title="Diopter sight">diopter</a> drum soldered to the receiver and a hooded front post installed in the gas block. The rear sight has an open notch setting marked "1" designed for immediate firing up to 100 m but also contains apertures with settings "2", "3" and "4" corresponding to 200, 300 and 400 m firing ranges. The 400 m setting has a removable iris for sportive shooting. The sights are adjustable via micrometer screws with windage and elevation increments of 1.5 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mrad" title="Mrad">mrad</a> or 4.5 cm (1.8 in) at 300 m. For night use, the dedicated "1" notch setting in the rear sight drum is provided with two self-luminous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tritium_illumination" title="Tritium illumination">tritium-powered</a> inserts fitted laterally on each side of the notch and additionally in a flip-up post attached to the foresight. When firing rifle grenades the front sight hood is aligned with the uppermost edge of the grenade's warhead, this provides an estimated point of impact up to 75 m. The rifle grenades intended for this purpose were FN/Luchaire Type 58-N bullet-though <a href="/wiki/Anti-tank_grenade" title="Anti-tank grenade">anti-tank grenades</a>.</p>
<p>The rifle's <a href="/wiki/Sight_(device)" title="Sight (device)">sights</a> were placed relatively low over the bore axis to ensure a minimal silhouette when firing from the <a href="/wiki/Prone_position" title="Prone position">prone position</a> (the shooter presents a 20% smaller target than with the Stgw 57), but high enough to provide comfort and speed when shouldering the rifle in the <a href="/wiki/Kneeling_position" title="Kneeling position">kneeling</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standing_position" title="Standing position">offhand</a> shooting positions. The design of the shoulder stock also facilitates the rapid alignment of the shooting eye with the rifle's line of sight.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:5130_055_Sturmgewehr_90_mit_Kern_4x24_Zielfernrohr.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="202" data-file-width="719" height="62" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/5130_055_Sturmgewehr_90_mit_Kern_4x24_Zielfernrohr.jpg/220px-5130_055_Sturmgewehr_90_mit_Kern_4x24_Zielfernrohr.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/5130_055_Sturmgewehr_90_mit_Kern_4x24_Zielfernrohr.jpg/330px-5130_055_Sturmgewehr_90_mit_Kern_4x24_Zielfernrohr.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/5130_055_Sturmgewehr_90_mit_Kern_4x24_Zielfernrohr.jpg/440px-5130_055_Sturmgewehr_90_mit_Kern_4x24_Zielfernrohr.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>For designated marksman use, the SG 550 is equipped with a Kern 4x24 <a href="/wiki/Telescopic_sight" title="Telescopic sight">telescopic sight</a> on a quick-detachable mount.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The sight weighs 730 g (26 oz) and includes a variety of features, such as <a href="/wiki/Picatinny_rail" title="Picatinny rail">STANAG 2324/MIL-STD-1913</a> compliant mounting components, a <a href="/wiki/Telescopic_sight#Bullet_Drop_Compensation" title="Telescopic sight">Bullet Drop Compensation</a> (BDC) elevation adjustment knob for ranges from 100 to 600 m, a tritium-illuminated <a href="/wiki/Reticle" title="Reticle">reticle</a> that enables target acquisition in low-light conditions and a diopter eyesight correction adjustment. Included with the sight is a lens hood for mounting on the ocular that reduces image quality-impairing stray light and a gray filter for glare reduction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The basic model of this optical sight was already used on the Stgw 57.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The upper receiver can accept quick-detachable <a href="/wiki/Rail_system_(firearms)" title="Rail system (firearms)">rails</a> and adapters used to mount optics (STANAG 2324 compliant). The scope mounting system consists of a centering hole located on the front face of the rear sight assembly and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dovetail" title="Dovetail">dovetail</a>-like mounting point at the front end of the receiver. Swiss Arms (respectively <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%BCgger_%26_Thomet" title="Brügger &amp; Thomet">Brügger &amp; Thomet</a>) offer several types of quick-release scope mounts and <a href="/wiki/Picatinny_rail" title="Picatinny rail">Picatinny rails</a>. A version of the rifle with an integral receiver-mounted Picatinny rail is also offered; in this configuration the weapon is fitted with flip-up emergency battle sights—a rear aperture sight which folds down into a recess in the rail and a folding front blade.</p>

<p>Both the rifle and carbine come standard with a spare magazine, <a href="/wiki/Sling_(firearms)" title="Sling (firearms)">sling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firearm_maintenance#Cleaning" title="Firearm maintenance">cleaning</a> kit and a loading aid for rapid magazine filling.</p>
<p>The full-sized SG 550/551 will accept SIG's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/40_mm" title="40 mm">40 mm</a> <a href="/wiki/SIG_GL_5040" title="SIG GL 5040">GL 5040/5140</a> <a href="/wiki/Grenade_launcher" title="Grenade launcher">grenade launcher</a> (Swiss military designation: 40 mm <i>Gewehraufsatz</i> 97), which is mounted under the barrel via an eccentric latch and replaces the lower <a href="/wiki/Handguard" title="Handguard">handguard</a>. The grenade launcher is a <a href="/wiki/Single-shot" title="Single-shot">single-shot</a> <a href="/wiki/Breech-loading_weapon" title="Breech-loading weapon">breech-loaded weapon</a> that is supplied with a leaf sight that attaches to the rifle's rear sight base and enables accurate firing out to 200 m. The lightweight <a href="/wiki/Aluminium" title="Aluminium">aluminium</a> launcher weighs 1.7 kg (3.7 lb) unloaded, and is operated independently of the rifle. It can use a wide array of 40×46mm grenades, including extended range high-pressure types and non-lethal baton or anti-riot projectiles. The compact SG 552/553 can be fitted with smaller GL 5340 underbarrel grenade launcher.</p>
<p>An Stgw 90 <a href="/wiki/Bayonet" title="Bayonet">bayonet</a> can also be mounted to the rifle. The bayonet has an overall length of 310 mm and a muzzle ring diameter of 22 mm. The 177 mm long blade is single-edged and it has no <a href="/wiki/Fuller_(weapon)" title="Fuller (weapon)">fuller</a>. The bayonets are manufactured exclusively for the Swiss Army by <a href="/wiki/Victorinox" title="Victorinox">Victorinox</a> and in the past by <a href="/wiki/Wenger" title="Wenger">Wenger</a> until Victorinox acquired Wenger in 2005. With a proper lug adaptor, the rifle will also accept a NATO-pattern KCB-77 (made originally by Carl Eickhorn of <a href="/wiki/Solingen" title="Solingen">Solingen</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>) or the American <a href="/wiki/M9_bayonet" title="M9 bayonet">M9</a>.</p>

SG 551 carbine</div>

<p>The SG 551 carbine has a short pattern 363 mm (14.3 in) barrel, gas tube and piston compared to the SG 550. The SG 551 series rifles have a 466 mm (18.3 in) long sight radius. The handguards were also changed and the bipod removed. The SG 551 cannot be used with a bayonet or fire rifle grenades. The SG 551 comes in several specialized variants designed for use with security and special forces. Among those variants are the <b>SG 551-1P</b> police carbine (designed to engage point targets out to 300 m; equipped with a Hensoldt 6x42 BL <a href="/wiki/Telescopic_sight" title="Telescopic sight">telescopic sight</a> and detachable cheek riser), the <b>SG 551 SWAT</b> carbine (coated with a corrosion-resistant finish and equipped with an optical sight mount used with a wide array of sights, and can also accept mission-critical accessories such as a bipod, laser pointer or tactical light) and the <b>SG 551 LB</b> carbine with an extended 454 mm (17.9 in) barrel that enables the use of rifle grenades and a bayonet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SG_552_Commando">SG 552 Commando</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: SG 552 Commando">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stgw_90_kurz.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="286" data-file-width="719" height="88" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Stgw_90_kurz.jpg/220px-Stgw_90_kurz.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Stgw_90_kurz.jpg/330px-Stgw_90_kurz.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Stgw_90_kurz.jpg/440px-Stgw_90_kurz.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stgw_90_kurz.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The compact SG 552 Commando <a href="/wiki/Carbine" title="Carbine">carbine</a> with 30-round magazine</div>

<p>The compact <b>SG 552 Commando</b> (full designation as the 552-2P<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-SIG_SG_550_.28Sturmgewehr_Model_550.29_.2F_Stgw_90_Assault_Rifle_.2F_Carbine_June_22.2C_2014-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>) carbine was released in July 1998. It has a shorter 226 mm (8.9 in) barrel (with an open, 3-prong flash suppressor) and gas tube, ventilated handguards and a redesigned bolt carrier group that was integrated with the piston rod to form a single moving assembly. The SG 552 series rifles have a 360 mm (14.2 in) long sight radius. The return mechanism has been moved to the rear of the receiver housing and its recoil spring is guided in a way analogous to that of the <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">AK-47</a>: on a steel guide rod (later models feature a polymer guide rod) resting against the lower receiver's rear surface under tension of the compressed recoil spring. Like the SG 550/551, this model can accept rails and accessories enabling the use of optics. A long barrel version of the SG 552 known as the <b>SG 552 LB</b> incorporates a 346 mm (13.6 in) barrel with provision to fire rifle grenades and support a bayonet.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SG_553">SG 553</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: SG 553">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1312" data-file-width="1384" height="209" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg/220px-SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg/330px-SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg/440px-SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SIG_SG_553_LB.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
SG 553 in the hands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysian_Air_Force" title="Malaysian Air Force">Malaysian Air Force</a> <a href="/wiki/PASKAU" title="PASKAU">PASKAU</a> commando during LIMA 2009</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <b>SG 553</b> is an improved version of the SG 552 and was released in 2008. Even though it mostly resembles the SG 552, the SG 553 has one key advantage, the recoil spring is now wrapped around the piston rod like in the SG 550/551 models, which address several reliability issues encountered in the SG 552 and also allows the usage of the standard SG 550/551 charging handle. The SG 553 series rifles have a 339 mm (13.3 in) long sight radius. A long barrel version of the SG 553 known as the <b>SG 553 LB</b> incorporates a 347 mm (13.7 in) barrel with provision to fire rifle grenades and support a bayonet. Further factory options for the SG 553 rifle series are an integrated receiver <a href="/wiki/Picatinny_rail" title="Picatinny rail">Picatinny rail</a> and an adjustable butt stock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <b>SG 553 R</b> is a variant chambered for the <a href="/wiki/7.62%C3%9739mm" title="7.62×39mm">7.62×39mm</a> cartridge fed from <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">AK family</a> box magazines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SG_550_Sniper_.28SIG_550-1.29">SG 550 Sniper (SIG 550-1)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: SG 550 Sniper (SIG 550-1)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Another member of the SG 550 family is the <b>SG 550 Sniper</b> variant designed specifically for Swiss security forces. This accurized rifle has a refined two-stage trigger (the pull force was reduced from 35 N (7.9 lb<sub>f</sub>) to 15 N (3.4 lb<sub>f</sub>), a heavy, hammer-forged 650 mm (25.6 in) long barrel with a 254 mm (1:10 in) rifling twist rate (it has no flash hider) and is used exclusively with telescopic sights. The new folding stock has an adjustable cheek piece and a spacer system on the butt, the ergonomic pistol grip's angle of inclination can be regulated, the forend was shortened, and the bipod features a height and cant adjustment mechanism. The rifle is no longer in production.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civilian_variants">Civilian variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Civilian variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The SG 550/551/552/553 are also available in <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_firearm" title="Semi-automatic firearm">semi-automatic</a> only configurations, intended for the civilian shooting market. Among these variants are the <b>SG 550/551/552 SP</b>, <b>PE 90</b> and <b>SIG Sport</b> rifles. The SG 550 series is available with either 178 mm or 254 mm (1:7 and 1:10 in) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twist_rate" title="Twist rate">twist rate</a> barrels. Rifles designated SG 55x-1 have a 254 mm (1:10 in) twist rate, while models marked SG 55x-2 have a 178 mm (1:7 in) twist rate. The ordnance GP 90 ammunition is optimized for use with the original Swiss 254 mm (1:10 in) rifling twist rate.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sig_556_Classic.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1840" data-file-width="3264" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Sig_556_Classic.jpg/220px-Sig_556_Classic.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Sig_556_Classic.jpg/330px-Sig_556_Classic.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Sig_556_Classic.jpg/440px-Sig_556_Classic.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

A SIG556 Classic equipped with an <a href="/wiki/EOTech" title="EOTech">EOTech</a> 512 holographic weapon sight and <a href="/wiki/STANAG_magazine" title="STANAG magazine">STANAG magazine</a>.</div>

<p>Due to import restrictions, the American civilian market required a partially American-made version assembled by <a href="/wiki/SIG_Sauer" title="SIG Sauer">SIG SAUER, Inc.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Exeter,_New_Hampshire" title="Exeter, New Hampshire">Exeter, New Hampshire</a>. The <b>SIG556</b> is designed to meet these requirements. The 556 lacks full-auto capability and the overall length is 940 mm (37.0 in). One difference is a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aluminum" title="Aluminum">aluminum</a> lower receiver that accepts <a href="/wiki/M16_rifle" title="M16 rifle">M16</a> <a href="/wiki/STANAG_magazine" title="STANAG magazine">STANAG magazines</a> and an <a href="/wiki/M4_carbine" title="M4 carbine">M4</a> <a href="/wiki/Telescoping_stock" title="Telescoping stock">telescoping buttstock</a>. The barrel's twist rate is 178 mm (1:7 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are many variants of this rifle offered for sale. The first variant was sold with an aluminum Picatinny rail on the upper receiver and a series of plastic rails on the handguard. The market pushed SIG to produce the rifle with the slimmer profile 551-type handguards and a hooded front sight; this version is marketed as the <b>SIG556 Classic</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Several folding stock models have been released as well as variants with railed forend combinations. Another major variant is a <b>SIG556 DMR</b>. This features a 21 in (533.4 mm) long barrel without flash suppressor, upgraded, a match type trigger, <a href="/wiki/Magpul_Industries" title="Magpul Industries">Magpul</a> PRS stock and older style plastic handguards. <b>SIGP556</b> pistol variants with 10 in barrels are also available. In 2012 the <b>SIG556R</b> or SIG556 Russian chambered for the <a href="/wiki/7.62%C3%9739mm" title="7.62×39mm">7.62×39mm</a> cartridge and using <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">AK</a>-pattern box magazines was introduced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/SIG_522LR" title="SIG 522LR">SIG 522LR</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/.22_Long_Rifle" title=".22 Long Rifle">.22</a>-caliber sporting rifle styled after the SG 551. It uses a simple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blowback_(arms)#Simple_blowback" title="Blowback (arms)">blowback</a> semi-automatic operating system and its barrel has a 406 mm (1:16 in) twist rate. Due to its operating principle, the rifle has no mechanical commonality with other SG 550 variants. The SIG522 accepts commonly available AR-style .22-caliber conversion magazines.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation_in_Canada">Regulation in Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SIG_SG_550&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Regulation in Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 February 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> semi-automatic "Classic Green" sporting rifle, also known as the <i>Swiss Arms PE 90</i>, was re-classified as a "prohibited weapon".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The rifle had been popular with hunters and gun enthusiasts, who until February 2014, only required a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Possession_and_acquisition_licence" title="Possession and acquisition licence">possession and acquisition licence</a> to obtain the rifle. However, late in 2013, a gun dealer had imported some PE 90 rifles from Switzerland and brought them to Canada for sale. After some were sold, it was alleged by another retailer that they had previously been full-auto variants, converted to semi-auto. Upon investigation and examination by the RCMP, this was proven not to be the case as the rifles were purpose-built semi-automatic versions. This caused outrage among gun owners, firearms lobbyists and some civil right advocates, who felt the RCMP over-stepped their authority, and that such policy changes should be enacted by legislation. The <a href="/wiki/National_Firearms_Association" title="National Firearms Association">National Firearms Association</a> of Canada considered pursuit of the matter through the legal system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 31 July 2015, the Canadian government overturned the reclassification and returned the Swiss rifles to the original non-restricted and restricted classifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
