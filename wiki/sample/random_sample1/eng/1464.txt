<p><b>X40</b> is a series of <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple units</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/SJ_AB" title="SJ AB">SJ</a> of Sweden. They are in service from <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> to <a href="/wiki/Link%C3%B6ping" title="Linköping">Linköping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eskilstuna" title="Eskilstuna">Eskilstuna</a>/<a href="/wiki/Arboga" title="Arboga">Arboga</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4ster%C3%A5s" title="Västerås">Västerås</a>/<a href="/wiki/%C3%96rebro" title="Örebro">Örebro</a> and <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4vle" title="Gävle">Gävle</a>/<a href="/wiki/Sandviken" title="Sandviken">Sandviken</a>, and since 2010 to <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Bilevel_rail_car" title="Bilevel rail car">double decker trains</a> are built by <a href="/wiki/Alstom" title="Alstom">Alstom</a> since 2004, with 43 units being delivered, either in a two-car or three-car configuration. It is based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coradia_(train)" title="Coradia (train)">Coradia</a> series, very similar to the French <a href="/wiki/SNCF_Class_Z_26500" title="SNCF Class Z 26500">SNCF Class Z 26500</a> double decker trains, and similar to the <a href="/wiki/SL_X60" title="SL X60">X60-series</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">Specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SJ_X40&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The double deckers have a little higher passenger capacity than conventional cars, with 85 compared to 78 seats. Wider doors allow the trains to make only 30 second stops in smaller stations and 60 second stops in larger stations. Top speed is 200 km/h. Often the trains are paired, with one two-car and one three-car unit. The X40 has both first and second class, but with the same level of comfort. There is <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioning</a>, signal boosting for mobile telephones, electrical plugs at each chair, that is also equipped with radio. <a href="/wiki/Wireless_LAN" title="Wireless LAN">WLAN</a> is available for free to all passengers.</p>
<p>There were many technical problems during the first year of operation in 2006, including problems with doors and the air conditioning. Complaints from passengers included that there was too little room for baggage and that the seat pitch was insufficient. These problems have been addressed through rebuilding the interior. The trains are specified for regional commuter traffic, and some complaints come from passengers on longer journeys. For example, a common route for the train is Gävle-Stockholm-Linköping, with stop at Arlanda Airport, which means that tourists with heavy luggage use the train. During 2011 they have been used for even longer distances, like Stockholm-Örebro-Göteborg and Stockholm-Sundsvall, which has added to these complaints. In 2012 the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SJ_X55" title="SJ X55">SJ 3000</a> will replace X40 on some of these longer trips.</p>
