<p><b>X10</b> is a series of two-car <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple units</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/Storstockholms_Lokaltrafik" title="Storstockholms Lokaltrafik">Greater Stockholm Transport</a> (SL) on the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_commuter_rail" title="Stockholm commuter rail">Stockholm commuter rail</a>. The X10 was operated in sets of up to five units, making ten-car trains, each unit consisting of one motor car and one unpowered car. They are completely compatible with the <a href="/wiki/SL_X1" title="SL X1">X1</a> introduced in the 1960s. 101 units were built by <a href="/wiki/ASEA" title="ASEA">ASEA</a> between 1983 and 1993.</p>
<p>Between 1994 and 1998, 49 similar <b>X11</b> units were delivered for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sk%C3%A5ne_Commuter_Rail" title="Skåne Commuter Rail">Skåne Commuter Rail</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg_commuter_rail" title="Gothenburg commuter rail">Gothenburg Commuter Rail</a>. They have a different interior and are slightly heavier.</p>
<p>During the early 1990s, the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_State_Railways_(1883%E2%80%931996)" title="Norwegian State Railways (1883–1996)">Norwegian State Railways</a> borrowed X10 units during the summer to operate on the <a href="/wiki/Fl%C3%A5m_Line" title="Flåm Line">Flåm Line</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThue200274_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThue200274-1">[1]</a></sup> This was in part because they wanted to use the X10's larger windows for the tourist route.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
