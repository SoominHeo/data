<p>A <b>SO-DIMM</b>, <b>SODIMM</b>, or <b>small outline dual in-line memory module</b>, is a type of <a href="/wiki/Computer_memory" title="Computer memory">computer memory</a> built using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integrated_circuits" title="Integrated circuits">integrated circuits</a>. SO-DIMMs are a smaller alternative to a <a href="/wiki/DIMM" title="DIMM">DIMM</a>, being roughly half the size of regular DIMMs.</p>
<p>SO-DIMMs are often used in systems that have limited space, which include <a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">notebooks</a>, small-footprint <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computers</a> such as those based on <a href="/wiki/Mini-ITX" title="Mini-ITX">Mini-ITX</a> <a href="/wiki/Motherboard" title="Motherboard">motherboards</a>, high-end upgradable office <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_printer" title="Computer printer">printers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Networking_hardware" title="Networking hardware">networking hardware</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Router_(computing)" title="Router (computing)">routers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Network-attached_storage" title="Network-attached storage">NAS</a> devices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visual_identification">Visual identification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SO-DIMM&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Visual identification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most types of SO-DIMMs can be recognized at a glance by the distinctive notches that “key” them for different applications:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-custhelp_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-custhelp-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>100-pin SO-DIMMs have two notches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>144-pin SO-DIMMs have a single notch near the center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>200-pin SO-DIMMs have a single notch nearer to one side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The exact location of this notch varies (read below).</li>
<li>204-pin SO-DIMMs (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DDR3" title="DDR3">DDR3</a>) have a single notch closer to the center than on 200-pin SO-DIMMs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>260-pin SO-DIMMs (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DDR4" title="DDR4">DDR4</a>), 69.6 mm (2.74 inches) wide and 30 mm (1.2 inches) high, making them 2 mm (0.079 inches) wider than DDR3 SO-DIMMs, with a single notch after pin 144.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>260-pin SO-DIMMs (<a href="/wiki/UniDIMM" title="UniDIMM">UniDIMM</a>), 69.6 mm (2.74 inches) wide and 30 mm (1.2 inches) or 20 mm (0.79 inches) high, with a single notch placed differently than on DDR3 SO-DIMMs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants_of_200-pin_SO-DIMMs">Variants of 200-pin SO-DIMMs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SO-DIMM&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variants of 200-pin SO-DIMMs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 200-pin SO-DIMM can be of the types DDR and DDR2. In both cases the notch is located at one fifth of the board length (20 pins + notch + 80 pins), but in DDR2 the notch is located slightly closer to the center of the board. These two types of memory are not interchangeable. The different notch locations are designed to prevent cross-installation, aiding at the same time in visually identifying 200-pin SO-DIMM modules.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-custhelp_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-custhelp-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This variance is designed to prevent coupling of memory and controllers of different DDR generations due to electrical incompatibility. DDR SO-DIMMs operate at 2.5 V while DDR2 SO-DIMMs operate at 1.8 V.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_characteristics"><span id="SO-CDIMM"></span>General characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SO-DIMM&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: General characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>200- and 204-pin SO-DIMMs are 67.6 mm (2.66 inches) long and 31.75 mm (1.250 inches) wide, with a maximum total depth of 3.8 mm (0.15 inches).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>SO-DIMMs are more or less equal in power and voltage rating to DIMMs, and despite the smaller size of memory modules, SO-DIMM technology does not mandate lower performance compared to larger DIMMs. For example, DDR3 SO-DIMMs provide clock speeds such as 533 MHz (1066 MT/s, <a href="/wiki/DDR3_SDRAM#JEDEC_standard_modules" title="DDR3 SDRAM">PC3-8500</a>), <a href="/wiki/CAS_latency" title="CAS latency">CAS latencies</a> such as 7, and higher capacities such as 4 GB per module.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>DDR2 SO-DIMM memory modules commonly have clock speeds from 200 MHz up to 400 MHz (<a href="/wiki/DDR2_SDRAM#Specification_standards" title="DDR2 SDRAM">PC2-6400</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> 204-pin SO-DIMMs can also contain DDR3 SDRAM, with specifications such as PC3-6400, PC3-8500, PC3-10600, PC3-14900 and PC3-17000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> 260-pin <a href="/wiki/UniDIMM" title="UniDIMM">UniDIMMs</a> may contain either DDR3 or DDR4 SDRAM, depending on their configuration. Some SO-DIMMs also provide <a href="/wiki/ECC_memory" title="ECC memory">ECC</a> functionality; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unbuffered_memory" title="Unbuffered memory">unbuffered</a> ECC SO-DIMMs are called <i>SO-CDIMMs</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>LowHal SODIMMs are made using a low-halogen, environmentally-friendly process,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> which does not affect chip performance.</p>
