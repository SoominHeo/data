<p><b>SOHO China Limited</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Stock_Exchange" title="Hong Kong Stock Exchange">SEHK</a>: <a class="external text" href="//www.hkex.com.hk/eng/invest/company/quote_page_e.asp?WidCoID=0410&amp;WidCoAbbName=&amp;Month=&amp;langcode=e" rel="nofollow">0410</a>), founded in 1995, is the largest prime office <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real-estate" title="Real-estate">real-estate</a> developer in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, with corporate headquarters in Chaowai SOHO in <a href="/wiki/Chaoyang_District,_Beijing" title="Chaoyang District, Beijing">Chaoyang District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The company is incorporated in <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>. The founders are <a href="/wiki/Pan_Shiyi" title="Pan Shiyi">Pan Shiyi</a> (潘石屹), a former Oil Ministry employee, and his wife <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Xin_(businesswoman)" title="Zhang Xin (businesswoman)">Zhang Xin</a> (张欣), a former Goldman Sachs employee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ihtprofile_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ihtprofile-3">[3]</a></sup> The couple ranked the 21st richest 'person' in China a <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> ranking in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>SOHO China focuses on developing properties in the central business districts of <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>. SOHO China developments are known for modern iconic architecture, the company collaborates with internationally recognized architects such as <a href="/wiki/Pritzker_Architecture_Prize" title="Pritzker Architecture Prize">Pritzker Architecture Prize</a> winning architect <a href="/wiki/Zaha_Hadid" title="Zaha Hadid">Zaha Hadid</a> and Japanese architect <a href="/wiki/Kengo_Kuma" title="Kengo Kuma">Kengo Kuma</a>. As of 2012 has a development portfolio of 5.2 million square meters.</p>
<p>SOHO China has a total of 18 properties in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. Since 2009 the Company has successfully expanded into and established its presence in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, through the acquisition and development of a total of 11 properties.</p>
<p>SOHO China's completed projects and projects currently under construction in Beijing include: SOHO New Town, Jianwai SOHO, SOHO Shangdu - designed by <a href="/wiki/Lab_Architecture_Studio" title="Lab Architecture Studio">Lab Architecture Studio</a>, Chaowai SOHO, Guanghualu SOHO, Guanghualu SOHO II, Beijing SOHO Residences, ZhongGuanCun SOHO, <a href="/wiki/Sanlitun" title="Sanlitun">Sanlitun</a> SOHO, Qianmen Avenue, Chaoyangmen SOHO I, Chaoyangmen SOHO II, and Galaxy SOHO (formerly, Chaoyangmen SOHO III). SOHO China also has built internationally recognized avant garde projects, <a href="/wiki/Commune_by_the_Great_Wall" title="Commune by the Great Wall">Commune by the Great Wall</a> in Beijing and Boao Canal Village in Boao, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hainan_Island" title="Hainan Island">Hainan Island</a>.</p>
<p>SOHO China developments have attained tremendous commercial success, in large part due to the company’s ability to roll out high-quality, innovative products in prime locations, specifically catering to China’s high-net-worth individuals and urban professionals. SOHO China is China’s only pure prime office developer.</p>
<p>In August 2012, the Company announced a shift in business model from “build to sell” to “build to hold” to seize the growth in value of SOHO China properties. SOHO China has a current investment portfolio of 1.7 million square meters in China’s leading cities, and will be the largest landlord on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bund_(Shanghai)" title="The Bund (Shanghai)">the Bund</a> in Shanghai.</p>
<p>On 8 October 2007, SOHO China was listed in the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Stock_Exchange" title="Hong Kong Stock Exchange">Hong Kong Stock Exchange</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Financial_capital" title="Financial capital">capital</a> raising of HK$12.8 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1,000,000,000_(number)" title="1,000,000,000 (number)">billion</a>, approximately US$1.9 billion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>The company was also named as one of the "Most Admired Companies" in China by FORTUNE (China edition) Magazine for six times running from 2006 to 2012.</p>
