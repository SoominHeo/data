<p><b>SR-71</b> is an American rock band that was formed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a>. They are best known for their 2000 single "<a href="/wiki/Right_Now_(SR-71_song)" title="Right Now (SR-71 song)">Right Now</a>", their 2002 single "<a href="/wiki/Tomorrow_(SR-71_song)" title="Tomorrow (SR-71 song)">Tomorrow</a>", and as the co-authors of <a href="/wiki/Bowling_for_Soup" title="Bowling for Soup">Bowling for Soup</a>'s 2004 hit "<a href="/wiki/1985_(song)" title="1985 (song)">1985</a>" (which was released first on their album <i><a href="/wiki/Here_We_Go_Again_(SR-71_album)" title="Here We Go Again (SR-71 album)">Here We Go Again</a></i>). The name of the band came from <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_SR-71_Blackbird" title="Lockheed SR-71 Blackbird">SR-71 Blackbird</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supersonic" title="Supersonic">supersonic</a> <a href="/wiki/Surveillance_aircraft" title="Surveillance aircraft">surveillance aircraft</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a>. The band was originally known as Honor Among Thieves, and as would be the case with SR-71, lead singer <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Allan" title="Mitch Allan">Mitch Allan</a> was the only constant member.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SR-71_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_and_Now_You_See_Inside_.281998.E2.80.932001.29">Early years and <i>Now You See Inside</i> (1998–2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SR-71_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years and Now You See Inside (1998–2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band was formed in 1998, as a continuation project for frontman <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Allan" title="Mitch Allan">Mitch Allan</a> after the demise of his previous band Honor Among Thieves. The band was quickly signed to <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>, where they recorded their debut studio album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Now_You_See_Inside" title="Now You See Inside">Now You See Inside</a></i> which was released on June 20, 2000, and produced by <a href="/wiki/David_Bendeth" title="David Bendeth">David Bendeth</a> (<a href="/wiki/Underoath" title="Underoath">Underoath</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killswitch_Engage" title="Killswitch Engage">Killswitch Engage</a>), <a href="/wiki/Gil_Norton" title="Gil Norton">Gil Norton</a> (<a href="/wiki/Foo_Fighters" title="Foo Fighters">Foo Fighters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pixies" title="Pixies">Pixies</a>) and <a href="/wiki/John_Shanks" title="John Shanks">John Shanks</a> (<a href="/wiki/Unwritten_Law" title="Unwritten Law">Unwritten Law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jane%27s_Addiction" title="Jane's Addiction">Jane's Addiction</a>). The album proved successful for the unknown band, and peaked at number 81 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" title="Top Heatseekers"><i>Billboard</i> Heatseekers Chart</a>. The album spawned the band's debut hit single, which remains the band's only hit single to date, "<a href="/wiki/Right_Now_(SR-71_song)" title="Right Now (SR-71 song)">Right Now</a>". Written by frontman <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Allan" title="Mitch Allan">Mitch Allan</a> and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Butch_Walker" title="Butch Walker">Butch Walker</a>, it reached number 2 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_Rock_Tracks" title="Modern Rock Tracks">Modern Rock Tracks</a> chart, number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and number 3 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mainstream_Rock_Tracks" title="Mainstream Rock Tracks">Mainstream Rock Tracks</a>.</p>
<p>The band's original drummer, Dan Garvin, left the band soon after the supporting tour of the album, to join <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> outfit <a href="/wiki/Nine_Days" title="Nine Days">Nine Days</a> while their drummer was unable to perform on the supporting tour for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Madding_Crowd" title="The Madding Crowd">The Madding Crowd</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tomorrow_and_drop_from_RCA_Records_.282002.E2.80.9303.29"><i>Tomorrow</i> and drop from RCA Records (2002–03)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SR-71_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tomorrow and drop from RCA Records (2002–03)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All of 2002, the band recorded their follow-up to <i><a href="/wiki/Now_You_See_Inside" title="Now You See Inside">Now You See Inside</a></i>. In the recording sessions for the album, they recorded a cover of the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>-winning <a href="/wiki/Funk_rock" title="Funk rock">funk rock</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Let_It_Whip" title="Let It Whip">Let It Whip</a>", originally by the <a href="/wiki/Dazz_Band" title="Dazz Band">Dazz Band</a>, for the soundtrack of the movie "<a href="/wiki/The_New_Guy" title="The New Guy">The New Guy</a>".</p>
<p>Their second studio album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Tomorrow_(SR-71_album)" title="Tomorrow (SR-71 album)">Tomorrow</a></i> was released on October 22, 2002 and was produced by Neal Avron (<a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yellowcard" title="Yellowcard">Yellowcard</a>), <a href="/wiki/Butch_Walker" title="Butch Walker">Butch Walker</a> (<a href="/wiki/American_Hi-Fi" title="American Hi-Fi">American Hi-Fi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a>), <a href="/wiki/David_Bendeth" title="David Bendeth">David Bendeth</a> (<a href="/wiki/Underoath" title="Underoath">Underoath</a>, <a href="/wiki/Breaking_Benjamin" title="Breaking Benjamin">Breaking Benjamin</a>) and frontman Mitch Allan himself. On this album, Mitch Allan contributed songwriting to all the songs in the album, with most of them written only by him, except a few, written along with <a href="/wiki/Butch_Walker" title="Butch Walker">Butch Walker</a>, Kevin Kadish, and the other members of the band. It was the band's first album with new drummer John Allen.</p>
<p>The album was much darker, and proved to be less successful than its predecessor, and peaked only at number 138 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. The first single "Tomorrow", reached only number 18 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_Rock_Tracks" title="Modern Rock Tracks">Modern Rock Tracks</a> chart, which was far less successful than "Right Now's" number 2 peak. The song "My World" was later recorded for <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> runner-up <a href="/wiki/Bo_Bice" title="Bo Bice">Bo Bice</a>'s debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_Thing_(Bo_Bice_album)" title="The Real Thing (Bo Bice album)">The Real Thing</a></i>, where frontman Mitch Allan played guitar and bass on. The song "Goodbye" was used by <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> as the theme song for the 2004 edition of <a href="/wiki/WWE_No_Way_Out" title="WWE No Way Out">WWE No Way Out</a>.</p>
<p>Despite the commercial failure, <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA</a> still heavily promoted a supporting tour for the album from late 2002 until 2003. The tour also proved unsuccessful. Soon tensions began to arise in the band, resulting in the departure of guitarist Mark Beauchemin and bassist Jeff Reid after the end of the tour in 2003.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Reid left for medical reasons, believing he had lung cancer. Reid died from the cancer on June 11, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Mark Beauchemin went on playing guitar for <a href="/wiki/Soft_rock" title="Soft rock">soft rock</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Carlton" title="Vanessa Carlton">Vanessa Carlton</a>.</p>
<p>The band was then joined by new members: guitarist Pat DeMent and bassist <a href="/wiki/Mike_Ruocco" title="Mike Ruocco">Mike Ruocco</a>, only to be dropped by their label, <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>, not long afterward.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Here_We_Go_Again_and_decline_in_popularity_.282003.E2.80.9305.29"><i>Here We Go Again</i> and decline in popularity (2003–05)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SR-71_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Here We Go Again and decline in popularity (2003–05)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though without a label at the time, the band went on to record an album of new material by themselves, during mid to late 2003. All songwriting on the album was done by Mitch Allan, with the exception of two tracks co-written by drummer John Allen, and one old-penned hidden track co-written a year earlier by Kevin Kadish. All production, mixing and mastering was also done by Mitch Allan.</p>
<p>In 2004, the band finally found a new label, a Japanese label under the name Crown Japan, who released their third album <i><a href="/wiki/Here_We_Go_Again_(SR-71_album)" title="Here We Go Again (SR-71 album)">Here We Go Again</a></i> on May 21, 2004, in Japan only. The album was available outside Japan only through imports on the band's official website. The album gained little attention and entered no charts, proving to be, again, a commercial flop. Despite that, one song on the album, "<a href="/wiki/1985_(song)" title="1985 (song)">1985</a>", was then re-recorded by the band's close friends <a href="/wiki/Bowling_for_Soup" title="Bowling for Soup">Bowling for Soup</a> for their album <i><a href="/wiki/A_Hangover_You_Don%27t_Deserve" title="A Hangover You Don't Deserve">A Hangover You Don't Deserve</a></i>, released later in 2004. "1985" was released as a single and reached number 23 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. Frontman Mitch Allan and drummer John Allen were still credited for the song on the Bowling for Soup album and single, along with Bowling for Soup frontman Jaret Reddick. It was former SR-71 producer and collaborator <a href="/wiki/Butch_Walker" title="Butch Walker">Butch Walker</a> who produced the album and single. Mitch Allan also had a cameo in <a href="/wiki/Bowling_for_Soup" title="Bowling for Soup">BFS</a>'s music video for "1985".</p>
<p>The album's title track "Here We Go Again" was also re-recorded in 2006, by <a href="/wiki/Pop_punk" title="Pop punk">pop punk</a> band JParis for their album <i>Call It What You Want</i>. Mitch Allan has produced the band's album and was credited as the writer of this song.</p>
<p>The first song on the album, "Axl Rose", was released as a single later, with "Here We Go Again" and "All American" as B-sides. The <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gabriel" title="Peter Gabriel">Peter Gabriel</a> cover "<a href="/wiki/In_Your_Eyes_(Peter_Gabriel_song)" title="In Your Eyes (Peter Gabriel song)">In Your Eyes</a>" was also released a single, and played frequently during the 2004–05 tour by the band.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hiatus_and_Mitch_Allan_debut_studio_album_.282006.E2.80.9309.29">Hiatus and Mitch Allan debut studio album (2006–09)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SR-71_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hiatus and Mitch Allan debut studio album (2006–09)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though the band still played some dates in 2005, it stayed inactive since, with Mitch Allan starting to play small solo club shows around 2006. At his solo shows, he played SR-71 songs, as well as some previously unreleased solo material, including a new fan favorite song called "Superman". Allan then announced that he started on working on his debut studio album. He said the album will be called <i>Clawing My Way to the Middle</i> and released in late 2007, with sessions commencing in Los Angeles and <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a> in the spring of 2006 with record producer <a href="/wiki/Ben_Moody" title="Ben Moody">Ben Moody</a>. Since it was announced the album would be released in late 2007, it was pushed back a few times in 2008 and 2009, and now it is unknown when or if the album will be released.</p>
<p>Allan kept playing solo club shows around the Hollywood area through 2007, when the band announced that they will be officially going into an indefinite hiatus after a one last show in <a href="/wiki/Henderson,_Nevada" title="Henderson, Nevada">Henderson, Nevada</a>, on August 18, 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Sunset_Station" title="Sunset Station">Sunset Station</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Vertical_Horizon" title="Vertical Horizon">Vertical Horizon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nine_Days" title="Nine Days">Nine Days</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marcy_Playground" title="Marcy Playground">Marcy Playground</a>.</p>
<p>While keeping a low profile on performances, Mitch Allan has worked as a songwriter and record producer with many artists including <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Lohan" title="Lindsay Lohan">Lindsay Lohan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lovehammers" title="Lovehammers">Lovehammers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daughtry_(band)" title="Daughtry (band)">Daughtry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Duff" title="Hilary Duff">Hilary Duff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Backstreet_Boys" title="Backstreet Boys">Backstreet Boys</a> and more. In 2007, Allan received a <a href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy_Award" title="Latin Grammy Award">Latin Grammy Award</a> nomination for co-writing the song "<a href="/wiki/Bella_Traici%C3%B3n" title="Bella Traición">Bella Traición</a>" for Mexican teen sensation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belinda_(entertainer)" title="Belinda (entertainer)">Belinda</a>.</p>
<p>In January 2008, Mitch Allan has released his debut single with a music video on his Myspace page. The song, entitled "Make Me High" was a soft remake of an old SR-71 song called "My World", from their 2002 album <i><a href="/wiki/Tomorrow_(SR-71_album)" title="Tomorrow (SR-71 album)">Tomorrow</a></i>. Allan has also said that the SR-71 song "Mosquito" from their 2004 album <i><a href="/wiki/Here_We_Go_Again_(SR-71_album)" title="Here We Go Again (SR-71 album)">Here We Go Again</a></i> will also be re-recorded for his debut studio album. Allan then revealed that he is working with his former SR-71 bandmates on his debut studio album, and they will probably appear as his solo band on the album.</p>
<p>In 2009, while working on his debut studio album, Allan launched a weekly event in Hollywood where he and other notable songwriters meet up and play covers and original tunes acoustically to small crowds. Allan was then involved, along with former <a href="/wiki/Evanescence" title="Evanescence">Evanescence</a> guitarist <a href="/wiki/Ben_Moody" title="Ben Moody">Ben Moody</a> in a new contest for finding a teen singer sensation.</p>
<p>Also in 2009, drummer John Allen was involved as the lead singer of the new <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Charm_City_Devils" title="Charm City Devils">Charm City Devils</a>. The band toured as part of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe" title="Mötley Crüe">Mötley Crüe</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cr%C3%BCe_Fest_2" title="Crüe Fest 2">Crüe Fest 2</a> tour in summer 2009. Their debut studio album <i>Let's Rock and Roll</i>, included one song co-written and produced by Mitch Allan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reunion_.282009.E2.80.93present.29">Reunion (2009–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SR-71_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reunion (2009–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 25, 2009, the band reunited for a one off performance at the Wicker Park Bucktown Arts and Music Festival in Chicago, playing a full set on the Center Stage.</p>
<p>On October 13, 2009, Mitch Allan announced on his Myspace page that SR-71 will be playing another one-off reunion show at the Bourbon Street in hometown <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a> on November 25, 2009. <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">Alternative rock</a> act <a href="/wiki/Cinder_Road" title="Cinder Road">Cinder Road</a> supported them on this date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> During the band's show, drummer John Allen stepped to the front and traded duties with frontman Mitch Allan, to sing "Best of the Worst", a song he co-wrote with Mitch Allan for his other band <a href="/wiki/Charm_City_Devils" title="Charm City Devils">Charm City Devils</a>, which members also got on stage to play the song.</p>
<p>On October 22, 2010, Allan, through his Twitter account, revealed that SR-71's third album, <i>Here We Go Again</i> would finally be released in the United States. On Thanksgiving Day, the album will be available as a digital download through iTunes, Amazon MP3, and Rhapsody. The original album tracks have been remastered and 3 live bonus tracks will be made available, as well.</p>
