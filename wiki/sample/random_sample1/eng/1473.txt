<p><b>STX Europe AS</b>, formerly <b>Aker Yards ASA</b>, was until 2012 a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> industrial <a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">chaebol</a> <a href="/wiki/STX_Corporation" title="STX Corporation">STX Corporation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>With headquarters in Oslo, Norway, STX Europe operated 15 <a href="/wiki/Shipyard" title="Shipyard">shipyards</a> in Brazil, Finland, France, Norway, Romania and Vietnam. The company had three business areas: Cruise &amp; Ferries, Offshore &amp; Specialized Vessels and Other Operations.</p>
<p>In 2012, with rising outstanding debts, STX retained the Finnish cruise shipbuilding yard and sold the remainder as <i>STX OSV Holdings</i>, (Offshore &amp; Specialist Vessels), including all the yards, to <a href="/wiki/Fincantieri" title="Fincantieri">Fincantieri</a>, which renamed the group <a class="external text" href="http://www.vard.com" rel="nofollow">Vard</a>.</p>
<p>The Turku shipyard was sold, 70% to Meyer Werft and 30% to the Finnish government, in September 2014. Meyer Werft acquired the Finnish government's 30% in April 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=STX_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background:_before_2006">Background: before 2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=STX_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background: before 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The evolution of STX Europe originated with the founding of two prominent shipbuilding groups in Europe. One was the Norway based <b>Aker Yards</b>, created in 2004 by combining the shipbuilding activities of <a href="/wiki/Aker_ASA" title="Aker ASA">Aker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kv%C3%A6rner" title="Kværner">Kværner</a> with the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Alstom" title="Alstom">Alstom</a> shipbuilding group, which has shipyards (formerly those of <a href="/wiki/Chantiers_de_l%27Atlantique" title="Chantiers de l'Atlantique">Chantiers de l'Atlantique</a> in Saint-Nazaire) and in Lorient.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition_to_STX_Europe.2C_and_expansion:_2006_-_2012">Transition to STX Europe, and expansion: 2006 - 2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=STX_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transition to STX Europe, and expansion: 2006 - 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 January 2006, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aker_Yards" title="Aker Yards">Aker Yards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alstom" title="Alstom">Alstom</a> announced their intention to join forces in shipbuilding and create together one of the world leaders in this industry, focused on high-value-added ships, including world-class cruise ships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AY-AS-H-01_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-AY-AS-H-01-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AY-AS-H-02_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AY-AS-H-02-4">[4]</a></sup> The merger gave Aker Yards a majority shareholding over Alstom's shipbuilding activities; which included <a href="/wiki/Chantiers_de_l%27Atlantique" title="Chantiers de l'Atlantique">Chantiers de l'Atlantique</a> shipyard - the builder of the iconic cruise liner - the <a href="/wiki/RMS_Queen_Mary_2" title="RMS Queen Mary 2">RMS Queen Mary 2</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Aker_ASA" title="Aker ASA">Aker ASA</a>, the majority shareholder of Aker Yards; reduced its ownership share from 50.4% to 40.1% in January 2007. Aker divested its total shareholding in March 2007, and in October 2007, STX Business Group secured a 39.2% stake of Aker yards. Later, STX took a controlling stake, and renamed the group to <b>STX Europe</b> on 3 November 2008 to reflect the new ownership structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AK-STX-H-01_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AK-STX-H-01-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2009, STX business group acquired the remaining shares in STX Europe and became the company's sole shareholder. In February 2009, it was decided to delist STX Europe from Oslo Stock Exchange where it was previously listed under the ticker STXEUR.</p>

<p>STX Europe has two shipyards in Romania, Braila and Tulcea. The steel hulls for most of STX Europe's new building projects (Offshore &amp; Specialized Vessels) are being built by Braila and Tulcea in Romania. STX Europe's yard in <i>Braila</i> is situated 170 km up the River Danube. Portfolios consist of hull production, delivery of complete merchant vessels together with repair and conversion work. The yard has a 1300m long outfitting quay, facilities for simultaneous mounting or repairing of 12 vessels and equipped with multiple 50 tonne portal cranes. The mounting platform and launching berth have capacity for vessels up to 135 x 23 m and a maximum launching and lifting weight of 2500 tonne and 2200 tonne respectively.</p>
<p>STX Europe's yard in <b>Tulcea</b> is located at nautical mile 39.5 on the right bank of the Danube, upstream of Tulcea, and it is one of the youngest Romanian firms in the field. In Tulcea STX Europe offers services in shipbuilding, conversions and repairs. It has a large covered hall where four ships can be built simultaneously. Over 6000 people working here, it is the greater capacity of offshore and specialised division.</p>
