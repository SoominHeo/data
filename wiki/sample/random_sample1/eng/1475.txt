<p>The <b>SU-100</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Samokhodnaya_ustanovka" title="Samokhodnaya ustanovka">Samokhodnaya ustanovka</a></i> 100) was a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Tank_destroyer" title="Tank destroyer">tank destroyer</a> armed with a powerful 100 mm <a href="/wiki/Anti-tank_gun" title="Anti-tank gun">anti-tank gun</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Casemate#Armoured_vehicles" title="Casemate">casemate</a> superstructure. It was used extensively during the last year of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and saw service for many years afterwards with the armies of Soviet allies around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osprey_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Osprey-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SU-100&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/SU-85" title="SU-85">SU-85</a> was developed from the chassis of the <a href="/wiki/T-34" title="T-34">T-34</a> tank replacing the turret with a larger, fixed superstructure that allowed a larger gun to be fitted: the <a href="/wiki/85_mm_air_defense_gun_M1939_(52-K)" title="85 mm air defense gun M1939 (52-K)">85 mm D-5 gun</a>, providing dramatically upgraded firepower compared to the T-34's 76.2 mm models. Introduced to service in 1943, the SU-85 was quickly rendered obsolete as a new design featured the same gun on the <a href="/wiki/T-34#Model_designations" title="T-34">T-34-85</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osprey_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Osprey-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This prompted the design of a more advanced turretless tank destroyer with an even more powerful cannon. Development was conducted under supervision of <a href="/wiki/Lev_Gorlitsky" title="Lev Gorlitsky">L. I. Gorlitskiy</a>, chief designer of all medium Soviet self-propelled guns. The work started in February 1944 and the first prototype of the SU-100, "Object 138", was delivered in March. After intensive testing with different models of 100 mm gun Soviet engineers approved the <a href="/wiki/D-10_tank_gun" title="D-10 tank gun">D-10S</a> gun for mass production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osprey_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Osprey-1">[1]</a></sup> This gun was developed in Constructors Bureau of Artillery Factory No. 9 under guidance of <a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Petrov" title="Fyodor Petrov">F. F. Petrov</a>. After the Second World War this gun was installed on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T-54" title="T-54">T-54</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T-55" title="T-55">T-55</a> tanks and its derivatives were in service forty years after initial development.</p>
<p>It was built at the UZTM (Russian abbreviature УЗТМ for Уральский Завод Тяжелого Машиностроения – <a href="/wiki/Uralmash" title="Uralmash">Ural Heavy Machinery Factory, also called <i>Uralmash</i></a>) in <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">Yekaterinburg</a>. The SU-100 quickly proved itself to be able to penetrate around 125 mm (4.9 in) of vertical armor from a range of 2,000 m (1.2 mi) and the sloped 80 mm (3.1 in) front armor of the German Panther from 1,500 m (0.93 mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osprey_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Osprey-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hull of the SU-100 had major improvements over the <a href="/wiki/SU-85" title="SU-85">SU-85</a>; the thickness of the front armour was increased from 45 to 75 mm (1.8 to 3.0 in), and the commander's workplace was made in a small sponson on the right side of the hull; combined with the commander's cupola this improved the commander's effectiveness. Its German <i><a href="/wiki/Jagdpanzer" title="Jagdpanzer">Jagdpanzer</a></i>-family counterparts — the <a href="/wiki/Jagdpanzer_IV" title="Jagdpanzer IV">Jagdpanzer IV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jagdpanther" title="Jagdpanther">Jagdpanther</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jagdtiger" title="Jagdtiger">Jagdtiger</a>, by comparison, lacked this key piece of observational equipment. For better ventilation two ventilator units were installed, instead of only one as in the SU-85. Mass production began in September 1944.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_history">Service history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SU-100&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Service history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="924" data-file-width="1222" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg/220px-SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg/330px-SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg/440px-SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SU-100-batey-haosef-1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Captured SU-100, Batey Ha-Osef Museum, Israel.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The SU-100 saw extensive service during the last year of the war. It was used <i>en masse</i> in <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> in March 1945, when Soviet forces defeated the German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operation_Fr%C3%BChlingserwachen" title="Operation Frühlingserwachen">Operation Frühlingserwachen</a> offensive at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Balaton" title="Lake Balaton">Lake Balaton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osprey_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Osprey-1">[1]</a></sup> By July 1945, 2,335 SU-100s had been built.</p>
<p>The vehicle remained in service with the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> well after the war; production continued in the Soviet Union until 1947 and into the 1950s in <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>. It was withdrawn from Soviet service in 1957 but many vehicles were transferred to reserve stocks. Some exist to this day in the Russian Army holding facilities.</p>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Pact" title="Warsaw Pact">Warsaw Pact</a> countries also used the SU-100, as did Soviet allies such as <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. A few SU-100 were delivered to Yugoslavia after the war, under the designation M-44.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The SU-100 saw service in the fighting that accompanied the 1956 <a href="/wiki/Suez_Crisis" title="Suez Crisis">Suez Crisis</a>, in which the Egyptians used SU-100s against Israel's <a href="/wiki/M4_Sherman" title="M4 Sherman">M4 Sherman</a> tanks. The vehicle was also utilized in the 1967 <a href="/wiki/Six-Day_War" title="Six-Day War">Six-Day War</a> and the 1973 <a href="/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War" title="Yom Kippur War">Yom Kippur War</a>. It was modified slightly to adapt it to the sandy conditions of the Middle East, thus creating the SU-100M variant<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Exported SU-100s continued in service until the 1970s, and in some countries, even later. Yugoslavs used them during the civil war; however, due to lack of spare parts they were quickly retired, despite their satisfactory performance. The SU-100 remains in use by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vietnam_People%27s_Army" title="Vietnam People's Army">Vietnam People's Army</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Army_Ground_Force" title="Korean People's Army Ground Force">Korean People's Army Ground Force</a>.</p>
<p>SU-100s entered service with the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> (PLA) of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> after 1 December 1950 when Soviet forces left <a href="/wiki/Dalian" title="Dalian">Dalian</a>. The armaments in Dalian were sold to China, including 99 SU-100s, 18 IS-2 heavy tanks, and 224 T-34s, with which PLA formed its 1st Mechanised Division.</p>
<p>In April 2015, a SU-100 self-propelled gun was photographed being used in <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a> as part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yemeni_Civil_War_(2015)" title="Yemeni Civil War (2015)">the ongoing conflict</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Video evidence uploaded to YouTube in November 2016 showed an apparent Su-100 being knocked out by an anti-tank guided missile in Yemen. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SU-100&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The crew of a World War II SU-100 and their vehicle are the heroes of the old Soviet film <i>Na vojne kak na vojne</i> ("At War as at War", literally: "In wartime it's like wartime"), one of several Soviet films made about self-propelled artillery men. Veterans of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Patriotic_War" title="Great Patriotic War">Great Patriotic War</a> found this picture quite realistic. The movie includes a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Tankmen%27s_Song" title="Soviet Tankmen's Song">Soviet tankmen song</a>, which was popular with both Soviet <a href="/wiki/Armoured_warfare" title="Armoured warfare">armoured</a> soldiers and civilians.</p>

<p>A SU-100 is used by the protagonists in the movie <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Misfit_Brigade&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Misfit Brigade (page does not exist)">The Misfit Brigade</a></i>, where it is portrayed as a German tank, because it was readily available in <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a> (where the movie was shot) and for its passable resemblance to German turretless <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sturmgeschutz" title="Sturmgeschutz">assault guns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jagdpanzer" title="Jagdpanzer">tank destroyers</a>.</p>
