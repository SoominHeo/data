<p><b>Solána Rowe</b> (born November 8, 1990), better known by her <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>SZA</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'z' in 'Zion'">z</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">SIZ</span></span></b>-ə</i></a></span>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>. She was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, later relocating to <a href="/wiki/Maplewood,_New_Jersey" title="Maplewood, New Jersey">Maplewood</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup> In October 2012, Rowe self-released an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) titled <i><a href="/wiki/See.SZA.Run" title="See.SZA.Run">See.SZA.Run</a></i>, which she then followed up with her second EP, titled "<i><a href="/wiki/S_(EP)" title="S (EP)">S</a></i>," in October 2013. In July 2013, it was revealed that she had signed to the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> record label <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a>, through which she released "<i><a href="/wiki/Z_(EP)" title="Z (EP)">Z</a></i>," her third EP and first retail release, in April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard-Z_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-Z-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>SZA is a <a href="/wiki/Neo_soul" title="Neo soul">Neo Soul</a> singer whose music is described as <a href="/wiki/Alternative_R%26B" title="Alternative R&amp;B">Alternative R&amp;B</a>, with elements of <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a>, minimalist R&amp;B, cloud rap, ethereal R&amp;B, <a href="/wiki/Witch_house_(music_genre)" title="Witch house (music genre)">witch house</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chillwave" title="Chillwave">chillwave</a>. Rowe's lyrics are described as "unravelling", that revolve around themes of sexuality, nostalgia, and abandonment. Rowe cites a variety of artists as influences including <a href="/wiki/Billie_Holiday" title="Billie Holiday">Billie Holiday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamiroquai" title="Jamiroquai">Jamiroquai</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>. SZA also takes influence from non-musical artists including gymnasts, ice-skaters, painters and movie directors, including <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Solána Rowe was born in November 8, 1990, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>, United States before later relocating to <a href="/wiki/Maplewood,_New_Jersey" title="Maplewood, New Jersey">Maplewood, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup> Her father was an executive producer at <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>, while her mother was an executive at <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T">AT&amp;T</a>. Rowe was born to a <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> mother and a <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> father. She was raised as an Orthodox <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> and has an older half-sister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup> She attended a "Muslim prep school" and secondary school everyday after her "regular school." After the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>, Rowe was subject to bullying, leading her to stop wearing her <a href="/wiki/Hijab" title="Hijab">hijab</a>. After secondary school, Rowe attended three separate colleges. In her freshman year she "got high and failed out."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rowe went to college to study <a href="/wiki/Marine_biology" title="Marine biology">marine biology</a>, with the intention to go to graduate school to be a scientist and travel. The plan never materialized, and she then took random jobs in order to make money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BillboardMay2013_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardMay2013-3">[3]</a></sup> Rowe formed her stage name from the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Alphabet" title="Supreme Alphabet">Supreme Alphabet</a>, taking influence from rapper <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>, the last two letters in her name stand for Zig-zag and Allah, while the first letter Rowe developed herself switching it from savior to "sovereign.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9314:_See.SZA.Run.2C_S_and_Z">2011–14: <i>See.SZA.Run</i>, <i>S</i> and <i>Z</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011–14: See.SZA.Run, S and Z">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>SZA first met members of Top Dawg Entertainment during the CMJ 2011, when her boyfriend's clothing company sponsored a show in which <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> was performing. A friend attending the show with her foisted early SZA songs onto TDE president Terrence "Punch" Henderson, who liked the material and stayed in touch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard-Z_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-Z-2">[2]</a></sup> SZA first began recording music by accident; after recording one song, she continued the process until she decided she should "make something out of it." SZA recorded the songs with her friend and neighbor in which they "stole a bunch of beats off the Internet."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BillboardMay2013_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardMay2013-3">[3]</a></sup> On October 29, 2012, SZA released her debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-released" title="Self-released">self-released</a> <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> entitled <i><a href="/wiki/See.SZA.Run" title="See.SZA.Run">See.SZA.Run</a></i>, which featured production from brandUn DeShay, APSuperProducer, amongst others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex-S-EP_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-S-EP-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>On April 10, 2013 SZA released her second EP <i><a href="/wiki/S_(EP)" title="S (EP)">S</a></i>, which was met with positive reviews from music critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-COS-2013_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-COS-2013-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> SZA promoted the extended play with the release of music video for the song "Ice Moon", the accompanying video was directed by Lemar &amp; Dauley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_Media_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Complex_Media-7">[7]</a></sup> On July 14, 2013 it was announced that Top Dawg had signed an upcoming female singer named SZA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thesource.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-thesource.com-8">[8]</a></sup> In October 2013, SZA went on a four show tour with Swedish band <a href="/wiki/Little_Dragon" title="Little Dragon">Little Dragon</a>; starting on October 17 at the El Rey Theater Los Angeles and ending on October 24 at the Music Hall of Williamsbug, Brooklyn NY.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In December 2013, SZA released the song "Teen Spirit" which was followed by the release of a remix featuring American rapper <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, along with a music video directed by <a class="external text" href="http://www.aplusfilmz.com/" rel="nofollow">APLUSFILMZ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-complex.com_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-complex.com-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2014 SZA featured on a variety of songs from her label-mates albums including two songs for <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Rashad" title="Isaiah Rashad">Isaiah Rashad</a>'s debut EP <i><a href="/wiki/Cilvia_Demo" title="Cilvia Demo">Cilvia Demo</a></i> as well as featuring on <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Oxymoron_(album)" title="Oxymoron (album)">Oxymoron</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cilvia_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-cilvia-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 26, 2014, R&amp;B artist SZA released the single "Child's Play" featuring <a href="/wiki/Chance_the_Rapper" title="Chance the Rapper">Chance the Rapper</a> and produced by Dae One &amp; <a href="/wiki/XXYYXX" title="XXYYXX">XXYYXX</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> SZA announced the title and release date of her next EP, and released a new video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boombox_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Boombox-14">[14]</a></sup> Her debut studio album, titled <i>"Z</i>," was released on April 8, 2014 and was preceded by the release of the lead single, "Babylon," which was accompanied with a music video directed by <a class="external text" href="http://www.aplusfilmz.com/" rel="nofollow">APLUSFILMZ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boombox_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Boombox-14">[14]</a></sup> To promote <i>"Z"</i> SZA performed at several performance showcases at the SXSW Music Festival in Austin, Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boombox_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-Boombox-14">[14]</a></sup> SZA made her chart debut in the UK, where <i>Z</i> charted at thirty two, on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">R&amp;B Charts</a>, the week ending April 19, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <i>Z</i> debuted on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number thirty nine, selling 6,980 copies in its opening week, the album also peaked at number nine on the US Hip-Hop/R&amp;B chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2014, SZA announced she was planning to embark on an international tour and has begun recording her third EP entitled <i>A</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_News_2014_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_News_2014-17">[17]</a></sup> In July 2014 SZA featured on Kitty Cash's single "Moodring",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up.com_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up.com-18">[18]</a></sup> the same month SZA released a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Jill_Scott" title="Jill Scott">Jill Scott</a> called "Divinity" the song was produced by Om’Mas Keith and lasts for seven-and-a-half-minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 11 July 2014, SZA released a video for <i>"Julia"</i> from <i>Z</i>, during the songs accompanying video featured a snippet of a new song entitled “Tender”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-20">[20]</a></sup> In early October Jhene Aiko tweeted SZA, which led to speculation that the two would be working together; however on October 23, 2014, Aiko announced that she would be headlining a tour entitled "Enter The Void tour" which would be supported by SZA and The Internet. The tour begins on December 12 at Club Nokia in Los Angeles, California and more dates will be announced at a later time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On November 18, 2014 SZA released the song “Sobriety,” the song was produced by Thundercat and lyrically talks about candid confessions and love over a "moody" beat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present:_CTRL">2015–present: <i>CTRL</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015–present: CTRL">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2014, SZA revealed that "A" would be released as her debut studio album, continuing to say that "A" would be similar to "S" and would include trap influences with more aggressive lyrics, she also announced that she has begun working with James Fauntleroy, Hit-Boy and long time collaborator Felix Snow again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-23">[23]</a></sup> Along with Rihanna and Tyran Donaldson, SZA co-wrote a song entitled "<a href="/wiki/Consideration_(song)" title="Consideration (song)">Consideration</a>" which featured on Rihanna's album <i><a href="/wiki/Anti_(album)" title="Anti (album)">Anti</a></i> (2016); besides writing, SZA also featured on the track. SZA later performed "Consideration" with Rihanna live for the first time at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Brit_Awards" title="2016 Brit Awards">2016 Brit Awards</a> on February 24, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>After teasing new music, SZA premiered the lead single from her debut album titled “Drew Barrymore,” live on “Jimmy Kimmel Live!”. SZA didn’t just perform the new single on, she also announced the title of her debut studio album, initially titled A, the project will be called CTRL.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>SZA described her vocals as having a "rasp" which she initially tried to "tenderise"; SZA also listens to <a href="/wiki/Ella_Fitzgerald" title="Ella Fitzgerald">Ella Fitzgerald</a> for vocal influence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> SZA vocal style has been described as taking on the "lilt" of a <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> According to Marissa G. Muller of <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a> magazine, Rowe's vocals alternate between a "vapory husk and a sky-high falsetto."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Jordan Sargent of Pitchfork magazine labelled Rowe's vocals as being "chillwave" and "ethereal."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>SZA musical style is described as "alt R&amp;B".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-30">[30]</a></sup> SZA songs are built over "layers of sliced, delayed, and reversed vocals" and contains "twists and mutates".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex-S-EP_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-S-EP-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-neontommy.com_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-neontommy.com-31">[31]</a></sup> Reggie Ugwu from <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> finds her musical style to feature an "agnostic utopia dripping with mood", that straddles the "line between minimalist R&amp;B, '80s synth pop and soul".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard-Z_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-Z-2">[2]</a></sup> Rowe's music is predominately <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PBR%26B" title="PBR&amp;B">PBR&amp;B</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neo-soul" title="Neo-soul">neo-soul</a>, but has been noted for taking influences from a broad variety of genres including <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a>, minimalist R&amp;B, cloud rap, ethereal R&amp;B, <a href="/wiki/Witch_house_(music_genre)" title="Witch house (music genre)">witch house</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chillwave" title="Chillwave">chillwave</a> elements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-30">[30]</a></sup> Michael Madden described SZA's musical genre as being "agnosticism corresponds", noting that her work is not just one style of music and is versatile, noting the musical style is not just "R&amp;B, pop, soul, or one thing at all."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-consequenceofsound1_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-consequenceofsound1-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>SZA began writing songs due to being "passionate" about writing and enjoyed poetry, when writing lyrics SZA "freestyles" them in order to express whatever comes to her "mind", noting that it does not always make sense to herself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BillboardMay2013_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardMay2013-3">[3]</a></sup> Thematically, SZA work contains "unravelling lyrics", that touch upon themes of sexuality, nostalgia, and abandonment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-neontommy.com_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-neontommy.com-31">[31]</a></sup> According to Michael Madden from <a href="/wiki/Consequence_of_Sound" title="Consequence of Sound">Consequence of Sound</a>, SZA lyrical is sometimes "purposefully general" and sometimes "an ambitious but quick reference", which Madden compared to the rapper <a href="/wiki/Angel_Haze" title="Angel Haze">Angel Haze</a> and her debut album <i>Dirty Gold</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-consequenceofsound1_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-consequenceofsound1-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>SZA cites a range of musical artists as influences, as a child she grew up listening to <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> music,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BillboardMay2013_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardMay2013-3">[3]</a></sup> growing up SZA "couldn't do anything other than what" her dad did. So she listened to a lot of his music collection which included <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles Davis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billie_Holiday" title="Billie Holiday">Billie Holiday</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Armstrong" title="Louis Armstrong">Louis Armstrong</a>. Later musical influences happened by "accident" according to SZA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup> During one of her classmates bar mitzvah SZA was given a mixtape which introduced her to artists including the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>, LFO and <a href="/wiki/Macy_Gray" title="Macy Gray">Macy Gray</a>, she "loved" the CD and "played it until it was finished".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup> Via her sister she continued to broaden her influences when she was first introduced to rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a>, her sister continued to introduce SZA to rappers including Wu-Tang Clan and members of Cash Money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>SZA later began listening to rapper Common, <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a>, and "a lot of Wu, Nas, Mos Def, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" title="Jay Z">Hov</a>", she described the artists music she discovered as sticking "hard".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complex_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-1">[1]</a></sup> SZA cites Björk as being a major musical influences, speaking on Björk, SZA said her "personal influences came from dancing with American Ballet Theatre and doing pieces to Björk. That's the only time I had really any outside influence to music. So, the people that I fell in love with on a musical level were always much older. <a href="/wiki/Jamiroquai" title="Jamiroquai">Jamiroquai</a> is just, like, the shit for me," whom she also grew up listening too.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-refinery29.com_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-refinery29.com-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>During an interview, SZA said she was never really inspired by musicians or to become a musician, but was interested in creating a form of art; she looked up to people who were not "typical artists" including her "favorite gymnast, ice-skater, saxophonist, painter, or movie director" continuing to say she was inspired by film director <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-refinery29.com_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-refinery29.com-33">[33]</a></sup> SZA commented that singer <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a> was one of her personal and style influences saying she looked up to Hill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vogue_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vogue-34">[34]</a></sup> During an interview, SZA spoke again on her style influences, saying a large amount of her style inspiration comes from movies, expanding on this SZA commended <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a> films and his use of "Pantone color palette" continuing to say the "wardrobe in all of his films and I would love to dress like a character from Moonrise Kingdom. Or perhaps Bill Murray in The Life Aquatic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMag_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-WMag-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_image">Public image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SZA_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Public image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>SZA defined her style as being "Girl next door chic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMag_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-WMag-35">[35]</a></sup> Solana's hair became a point of interest during the early stages of her career and she discussed it in interviews with <a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue magazine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harper%27s_Bazaar" title="Harper's Bazaar">Harper's Bazaar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vogue_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vogue-34">[34]</a></sup> During performance SZA tends to wear "free-flowing" clothes that are easy to move and has been known to wear pajamas onstage in order to execute her vocals and enhance her performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>SZA is known as TDE's first female signee and first singer, which drew attention during the early stages of her career, which Rowe described as uncomfortable and did not like the attention, Rowe also stated she felt uncomfortable with being seen as a "public figure" and did not like the idea of people forming opinions on her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost.com_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost.com-37">[37]</a></sup> During her career Rowe, her music and her image were compared to neo-soul artists <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a> which Rowe has disagreed with and states that she has no "desire to be that" and just wants to be able to make her own style of music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost.com_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost.com-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
