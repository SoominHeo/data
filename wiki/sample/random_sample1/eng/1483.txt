<p>"<b>Sabotage</b>" is a 1994 song by American hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>, released as the first single from their fourth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Ill_Communication" title="Ill Communication">Ill Communication</a></i>.</p>
<p>The song features traditional rock instrumentation (<a href="/wiki/Ad-Rock" title="Ad-Rock">Ad-Rock</a> on <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MCA_(musician)" title="MCA (musician)">MCA</a> on <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mike_D" title="Mike D">Mike D</a> on <a href="/wiki/Drum_kit" title="Drum kit">drums</a>), <a href="/wiki/Turntablism" title="Turntablism">turntable</a> <a href="/wiki/Scratching" title="Scratching">scratches</a> and heavily distorted <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riff" title="Riff">riffs</a>. A moderate commercial success, the song was notable as well for its video, directed by <a href="/wiki/Spike_Jonze" title="Spike Jonze">Spike Jonze</a> and nominated in five categories at the 1994 MTV Music Video Awards.</p>
<p>In 2004, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> magazine ranked "Sabotage" #480 on their list of the <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone%27s_500_Greatest_Songs_of_All_Time" title="Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Songs of All Time">500 Greatest Songs of All Time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In March 2005, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Q_magazine" title="Q magazine"><i>Q</i> magazine</a> placed it at #46 in its list of the 100 Greatest Guitar Tracks, and was ranked #19 on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s 100 Greatest Songs of the 90s list. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a></i> included the song at #39 on their Top 200 Tracks of the 90s list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sabotage_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song's <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Spike_Jonze" title="Spike Jonze">Spike Jonze</a> and played extensively on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Homage_(arts)" title="Homage (arts)">homage</a> to, and <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">parody</a> of, 1970s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crime_drama" title="Crime drama">crime drama</a> shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-O" title="Hawaii Five-O">Hawaii Five-O</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Streets_of_San_Francisco" title="The Streets of San Francisco">The Streets of San Francisco</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/S.W.A.T._(TV_series)" title="S.W.A.T. (TV series)">S.W.A.T.</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Baretta" title="Baretta">Baretta</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starsky_and_Hutch" title="Starsky and Hutch">Starsky and Hutch</a></i>. The video is presented as the <a href="/wiki/Opening_credits" title="Opening credits">opening credits</a> of a fictional 1970s-style police show called <i>Sabotage</i>, with the band members appearing as the show's <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonists</a>. Each band member is introduced as a fictional <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, and the names of the characters are also given.</p>
<p>The characters appearing on the show are (in order of credits):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Sir Stewart Wallace guest-starring as himself (played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MCA_(musician)" title="MCA (musician)">MCA</a>)</li>
<li>Nathan Wind as Cochese (also played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MCA_(musician)" title="MCA (musician)">MCA</a>)</li>
<li>Vic Colfari as Bobby, "The Rookie" (played by <a href="/wiki/Ad-Rock" title="Ad-Rock">Ad-Rock</a>)</li>
<li>Alasondro AlegrÃ© as "The Chief" (played by <a href="/wiki/Mike_D" title="Mike D">Mike D</a>)</li>
<li>Fred Kelly as Bunny (played by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Hurricane" title="DJ Hurricane">DJ Hurricane</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>Some scenes had to be removed when the video was shown on MTV, including a <a href="/wiki/Knife_fight" title="Knife fight">knife fight</a> sequence, a falling-off-a-bridge scene, as well as a scene in which a man is thrown out of a car into a street.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In addition, the <i><a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys_Video_Anthology" title="Beastie Boys Video Anthology">Beastie Boys Video Anthology</a></i> featured a mock interview of the "cast" of <i>Sabotage</i> conducted by Jonze's then-wife <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Coppola" title="Sofia Coppola">Sofia Coppola</a>. A more recent version, the uncut version, can be found on Vevo.</p>
<p>In the DVD commentary for the 1996 film <i><a href="/wiki/Trainspotting_(film)" title="Trainspotting (film)">Trainspotting</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Boyle" title="Danny Boyle">Danny Boyle</a> credits the film's opening credits to those used in "Sabotage."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1994_MTV_Video_Music_Awards">1994 MTV Video Music Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sabotage_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1994 MTV Video Music Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The video for "Sabotage" was nominated for <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Video_of_the_Year" title="MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year">Video of the Year</a>, <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_Group_Video" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best Group Video">Best Group Video</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breakthrough_Video" title="Breakthrough Video">Breakthrough Video</a>, <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_Direction" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best Direction">Best Direction in a Video</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viewer%27s_Choice_(MTV)" title="Viewer's Choice (MTV)">Viewer's Choice</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="1994 MTV Video Music Awards">1994 MTV Video Music Awards</a>. However, it lost all five categories it was nominated in, losing Video of the Year, Best Group Video and Viewer's Choice to <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Cryin%27" title="Cryin'">Cryin'</a>", and Breakthrough Video and Best Direction in a Video to <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Everybody_Hurts" title="Everybody Hurts">Everybody Hurts</a>".</p>
<p>During R.E.M. lead singer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Stipe" title="Michael Stipe">Michael Stipe</a>'s acceptance speech for the Best Direction award, Beastie Boys member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MCA_(musician)" title="MCA (musician)">MCA</a> bum-rushed the stage in his "Nathaniel Hornblower" disguise, interrupting Stipe to protest the shutout of "Sabotage" from every category it was nominated in.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2009_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2009 MTV Video Music Awards">2009 MTV Video Music Awards</a>, the "Sabotage" video won best video in the new category of "Best Video (That Should Have Won a Moonman)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sabotage_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>During <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s 25th Anniversary Special in 1999, the band played the first fifteen seconds of the song before their performance was "sabotaged" by <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Costello" title="Elvis Costello">Elvis Costello</a>, who in 1977 had done the same to one of his own songs on the show; the Beastie Boys then accompanied him on "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio,_Radio" title="Radio, Radio">Radio, Radio</a>", the song performed during the original incident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Phish" title="Phish">Phish</a> covered the song in their 1998 concerts at <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Coliseum" title="Hampton Coliseum">Hampton Coliseum</a>, and it appears on the live album of those concerts, <i><a href="/wiki/Hampton_Comes_Alive" title="Hampton Comes Alive">Hampton Comes Alive</a></i>.</li>
<li>The song is heard early in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.J._Abrams" title="J.J. Abrams">J.J. Abrams</a>' 2009 <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_(film)" title="Star Trek (film)">Star Trek</a></i> film, played on a car stereo by an adolescent <a href="/wiki/James_T._Kirk" title="James T. Kirk">James T. Kirk</a>. This is one of the few uses of licensed music in a <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> production. The song also appears in the 2016 sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Beyond" title="Star Trek Beyond">Star Trek Beyond</a></i>, used by Kirk and his officers to disrupt an alien attack on a Federation starbase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>The song is included in the track list for the video games <i><a href="/wiki/Guitar_Hero_III:_Legends_of_Rock" title="Guitar Hero III: Legends of Rock">Guitar Hero III: Legends of Rock</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_(video_game)" title="Rock Band (video game)">Rock Band</a></i>.</li>
<li>The song was covered by hardcore punk band <a href="/wiki/Cancer_Bats" title="Cancer Bats">Cancer Bats</a> for their 2010 album <i><a href="/wiki/Bears,_Mayors,_Scraps_%26_Bones" title="Bears, Mayors, Scraps &amp; Bones">Bears, Mayors, Scraps &amp; Bones</a></i>.</li>
<li>In the 1999 <i><a href="/wiki/Futurama" title="Futurama">Futurama</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Hell_Is_Other_Robots" title="Hell Is Other Robots">Hell Is Other Robots</a>", the Beastie Boys perform several songs, including an <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> version of "Sabotage."</li>
<li>Finnish band <a href="/wiki/Steve_%27N%27_Seagulls" title="Steve 'N' Seagulls">Steve 'N' Seagulls</a> performed a version of the song in November 2015 for <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> A.V. Undercover series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>The song played during the infamous Bronco chase scene in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_People_v._O.J._Simpson:_American_Crime_Story" title="The People v. O.J. Simpson: American Crime Story">The People v. O.J. Simpson: American Crime Story</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
