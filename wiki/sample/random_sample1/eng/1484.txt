<p><b>Sean Cody</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Gay_pornography" title="Gay pornography">gay pornography</a> studio founded in Fall 2001, named after its founder Sean Cody. The website predominantly features young, muscular, white men in solo and hardcore scenes. Sean Cody has a strict model selection, with contracts requiring no prior pornographic experience ('exclusive' men).</p>
<p>Sean Cody was run by under a company named Cody Media <a href="/wiki/Incorporation_(business)" title="Incorporation (business)">Inc</a> from 2003 until 2015 where it was sold to porn conglomerate <a href="/wiki/MindGeek" title="MindGeek">MindGeek</a> (who owns other competitor studios such as <a href="/wiki/Men.com" title="Men.com">Men.com</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-queermenowscsale_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-queermenowscsale-2">[2]</a></sup> This raised some considerable questions around the quality and exclusivity of the content, as the same parent company owned the content-sharing websites that have negatively affected many other studios. A 2014 article by internet magazine Slate explained how "MindGeek has become the porn monopoly, putting industry members in the paradoxical position of working for the very company that profits from the piracy of their work."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_models">Notable models</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Cody&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable models">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sean Cody alumni include underwear model Simon Dexter;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Aaron Savy, a fitness model and <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighter" title="Ultimate Fighter">Ultimate Fighter</a> who has appeared on <a href="/wiki/Hole_in_the_Wall_(U.S._game_show)" title="Hole in the Wall (U.S. game show)"><i>Hole in the Wall</i></a>, a reality TV show;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and Dayton O'Connor, <a href="/wiki/Colby_Keller" title="Colby Keller">Colby Keller</a>, Paul Wagner and Brady Jensen ("Dayton", "Colby", "Barry" and "Jonah" respectively), now professional porn actors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dakota_Cochrane" title="Dakota Cochrane">Dakota Cochrane</a>, who appeared on the 15th season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter" title="The Ultimate Fighter">The Ultimate Fighter</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a>, modeled as "Danny" on Sean Cody during the latter half of 2007—a decision he now regrets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Sean" (real first name Ben) appeared on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/True_Life" title="True Life">True Life</a></i> in December 2015, in an episode entitled "I Am a Gay-for-Pay Porn Star," where he talks about his family and his career in gay porn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gawker_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-gawker-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvTL_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvTL-12">[12]</a></sup> Fellow Sean Cody performer "Forrest" (real name Anthony) also appears in the episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gawker_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-gawker-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvTL_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtvTL-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Cody&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jason Andrews, who appeared on the site as "Addison" in 2008, was arrested for first-degree murder in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Addison was very popular among fans and he appeared in seven videos from April to July 2008. Andrews later pleaded guilty to first-degree murder and was sentenced to life in prison without the possibility of parole.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2010, Devon Hunter, an escort and model who had performed in SeanCody videos under the name "Ryan", wrote a detailed account of his experience while filming for the company,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> claiming that he experienced homophobia on set and went on to criticize "gay for pay" scenes. An intense, international debate across many blogs began over the "gay for pay" debate in porn. It intensified when SeanCody released Hunter's personal information in retaliation to Queerty.com,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> which came under intense criticism for the outing and deleted the post.</p>
<p>In January 2012, SeanCody.com's parent company filed suit in a federal court against 122 unidentified file sharers for allegedly trading unauthorized copies of the site's first condom-free video, "Brandon &amp; Pierce Unwrapped", in December 2011. This was the first time Sean Cody sued over online file sharing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2015, Teofil Brank, who had modeled for Sean Cody under the name Jarec Wentworth, was arrested for allegedly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extorting" title="Extorting">extorting</a> a <a href="/wiki/MagicJack" title="MagicJack">Magicjack Vocaltec Ltd.</a> executive, Donald Burns, who is also a prominent <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> political activist. Brank was found guilty in July 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In October 2015, Brank was sentenced to six years in prison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
