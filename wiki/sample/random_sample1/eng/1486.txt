<p>"<b>See Emily Play</b>" is a song by English <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Pink_Floyd" title="Pink Floyd">Pink Floyd</a>, released as their second single in June 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Written by original frontman <a href="/wiki/Syd_Barrett" title="Syd Barrett">Syd Barrett</a> and recorded on 23 May 1967, it featured "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Scarecrow_(Pink_Floyd_song)" title="The Scarecrow (Pink Floyd song)">The Scarecrow</a>" as its B-side. Though it was initially released as a non-album single, the song appeared on the American edition of their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Piper_at_the_Gates_of_Dawn" title="The Piper at the Gates of Dawn">The Piper at the Gates of Dawn</a></i> (1967).</p>
<p>"See Emily Play" is included in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame%27s_500_Songs_that_Shaped_Rock_and_Roll" title="The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame's 500 Songs that Shaped Rock and Roll">The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame's 500 Songs that Shaped Rock and Roll</a> list and reached No. 6 in the United Kingdom singles chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officialcharts_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-officialcharts-4">[4]</a></sup> As of 2015, the song has never been mixed to stereo, so the US album version was rechannelled and all subsequent reissues have been in mono.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_Emily_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"See Emily Play" is also known as "Games for May", after a free concert in which Syd Barrett's Pink Floyd performed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Irgh160-161_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Irgh160-161-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toby38_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-toby38-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song was reportedly about a girl named Emily, who Barrett claimed to have seen while sleeping in the woods after taking a <a href="/wiki/Psychedelic_drug" title="Psychedelic drug">psychedelic drug</a>. According to <i>A Saucerful of Secrets: The Pink Floyd Odyssey</i>, by <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Schaffner" title="Nicholas Schaffner">Nicholas Schaffner</a>, Emily is the Honourable <a href="/wiki/Emily_Young" title="Emily Young">Emily Young</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Irgh161_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Irgh161-8">[8]</a></sup> daughter of <a href="/wiki/Wayland_Young,_2nd_Baron_Kennet" title="Wayland Young, 2nd Baron Kennet">Wayland Young, 2nd Baron Kennet</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Irgh93-94_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Irgh93-94-9">[9]</a></sup> and the sister of author Louisa Young, and nicknamed "the psychedelic schoolgirl" at the <a href="/wiki/UFO_Club" title="UFO Club">UFO Club</a>. An article in <i><a href="/wiki/Mojo_(magazine)" title="Mojo (magazine)">Mojo</a></i> magazine called "See the Real Emily" supposedly shows a picture of Barrett's Emily. It has been suggested by some that the <a href="/wiki/Slide_guitar" title="Slide guitar">slide guitar</a> effect was produced by Barrett using a <a href="/wiki/Zippo" title="Zippo">Zippo</a> lighter,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> but elsewhere that he used a plastic ruler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Irgh171_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Irgh171-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Train" title="Train">train</a> depicted on the single's sleeve was drawn by Barrett.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recording">Recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_Emily_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The details as to the recording remain shrouded in mystery due to the lack of paperwork in the EMI archive. Engineer Jeff Jarrett recalls that "See Emily Play" was recorded in a much longer form which was then edited down<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toby38_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-toby38-6">[6]</a></sup> for the single release. It was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Sound_Techniques" title="Sound Techniques">Sound Techniques</a> studios on 21 May 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toby38_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-toby38-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCLM29_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-MCLM29-12">[12]</a></sup> There was much trickery involved in the recording with backward tapes, much use of echo and reverb, and the first piano bridge between the first chorus and second verse was recorded at a slow pace then sped up for the final master.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The four-track master tape was wiped or misplaced.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It no longer exists and has never been mixed into true stereo; it was reprocessed for fake stereo on the 1971 <i>Relics</i> compilation.</p>
<p>Barrett, reportedly, was not happy with the final studio cut, and he protested against its release, which producer <a href="/wiki/Norman_Smith_(record_producer)" title="Norman Smith (record producer)">Norman Smith</a> speculated was based on his fear of commercialism. It was during sessions for the song that <a href="/wiki/David_Gilmour" title="David Gilmour">David Gilmour</a> visited the studio,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toby38_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-toby38-6">[6]</a></sup> and although being invited by Barrett, was shocked by the perceived changes in Barrett's personality when he did not appear to recognise him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toby38_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-toby38-6">[6]</a></sup> For many years Gilmour would recall this, saying, "I'll go on record as saying, that was when he changed".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toby38_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-toby38-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_Emily_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The US single (Tower 356) was released by <a href="/wiki/Tower_Records_(record_label)" title="Tower Records (record label)">Tower Records</a> three times between July 1967 and late 1968. Each time it failed to duplicate its UK success. The song only stayed in the band's setlist for a few months, and was last played on 25 November 1967 in <a href="/wiki/Blackpool" title="Blackpool">Blackpool</a>.</p>
<p>"See Emily Play" later appeared on a number of compilations: <i><a href="/wiki/Relics_(album)" title="Relics (album)">Relics</a></i> (1971), <i><a href="/wiki/Works_(Pink_Floyd_album)" title="Works (Pink Floyd album)">Works</a></i> (1983), <i><a href="/wiki/Shine_On_(Pink_Floyd_album)" title="Shine On (Pink Floyd album)">Shine On</a></i> (1992), <i><a href="/wiki/Echoes:_The_Best_of_Pink_Floyd" title="Echoes: The Best of Pink Floyd">Echoes: The Best of Pink Floyd</a></i> (2001),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Echoes_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Echoes-13">[13]</a></sup> <i>The Piper at the Gates of Dawn</i> 40th Anniversary Edition (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_Pink_Floyd:_A_Foot_in_the_Door" title="The Best of Pink Floyd: A Foot in the Door">The Best of Pink Floyd: A Foot in the Door</a></i> (2011) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Early_Years_1965%E2%80%931972" title="The Early Years 1965â1972">The Early Years</a></i> (2016). The song was also included on the Barrett retrospective <i><a href="/wiki/An_Introduction_to_Syd_Barrett" title="An Introduction to Syd Barrett">An Introduction to Syd Barrett</a></i> (2010).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_performances">Television performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_Emily_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Top_of_the_Pops.2C_BBCTV.2C_July_1967"><i>Top of the Pops</i>, BBCTV, July 1967</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_Emily_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Top of the Pops, BBCTV, July 1967">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pink Floyd performed the song three times on BBC TV's <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schaffner13_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schaffner13-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toby38-36_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-toby38-36-15">[15]</a></sup> On each occasion, they mimed to the single, over which Barrett sang a live vocal. The BBC wiped the shows all of which were "live" transmissions. In late 2009 a badly damaged home video recording was recovered by the <a href="/wiki/British_Film_Institute" title="British Film Institute">British Film Institute</a> containing the first and third show the song was performed on, though only the first appearance was recoverable in part. The first performance was on the 6 July 1967 edition hosted by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Freeman" title="Alan Freeman">Alan Freeman</a>. Parts of this performance have been recovered from the damaged video recording.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>They returned for the following week's edition, 13 July, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Pete_Murray_(DJ)" title="Pete Murray (DJ)">Pete Murray</a>. Barrett complained that the band shouldn't appear, because "<a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> doesn't do <i>Top of the Pops</i>". He did perform, but without the enthusiasm of the previous week. The last appearance was on 27 July 1967, once more hosted by Freeman. Barrett failed to turn up for rehearsals, however, at BBC Television Centre, prompting managers <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jenner" title="Peter Jenner">Peter Jenner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrew_King_(music_manager)" title="Andrew King (music manager)">Andrew King</a> to perform a frantic search. He was found at a friend's house, with badly bruised feet after arriving barefoot. He was said to be in a poor state of mind. Taken to the BBC, he performed seated on a large cushion due to his physical and mental condition.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The recoverable parts of the 6 July performance were given a public screening in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> on 9 January 2010 at an event called "<a href="/wiki/Missing_Believed_Wiped" title="Missing Believed Wiped">Missing Believed Wiped</a>" devoted to recovered TV shows. It was the first time any footage of the performance had been seen since its original broadcast.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Pink Floyd management now have a copy of the footage, and have promised to use it on a future project.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beat-Club.2C_Radio_Bremen.2C_August_1967_.28cancelled.29"><i>Beat-Club</i>, Radio Bremen, August 1967 (cancelled)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_Emily_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Beat-Club, Radio Bremen, August 1967 (cancelled)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band were booked to appear on this edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Beat-Club" title="Beat-Club">Beat-Club</a></i>. Barrett had suffered "nervous exhaustion" and the band managers decided to give the band a month-long break in the hope his health would recover. The appearance thus had to be cancelled.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Belgian_TV.2C_February_1968">Belgian TV, February 1968</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_Emily_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Belgian TV, February 1968">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1968, Pink Floyd travelled to Belgium where they filmed a TV special entitled "Pink Floid" (this misspelling is on the title credits) which featured lip-synched promotional films for "See Emily Play", as well as for "<a href="/wiki/Astronomy_Domine" title="Astronomy Domine">Astronomy Domine</a>", "The Scarecrow", "<a href="/wiki/Apples_and_Oranges_(song)" title="Apples and Oranges (song)">Apples and Oranges</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Paint_Box_(song)" title="Paint Box (song)">Paint Box</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Set_the_Controls_for_the_Heart_of_the_Sun" title="Set the Controls for the Heart of the Sun">Set the Controls for the Heart of the Sun</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Corporal_Clegg" title="Corporal Clegg">Corporal Clegg</a>". This was Gilmour's first TV work with the band. Barrett was still technically a member of the band but it had recently been decided to no longer include him in gigs and shows. Gilmour, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Waters" title="Roger Waters">Roger Waters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Wright_(musician)" title="Richard Wright (musician)">Rick Wright</a> thus had to mime to Barrett's vocals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

and by <a href="/wiki/3_(American_band)" title="3 (American band)">3</a>, on the 2008 re-release of their album <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_Is_Begun" title="The End Is Begun">The End Is Begun</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/John_Frusciante" title="John Frusciante">John Frusciante</a> has played it live.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
