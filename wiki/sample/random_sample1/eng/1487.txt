<p>"<b>Shake That</b>" (also known as "<b>Shake That Ass</b>") is the second and final single taken from American rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s first compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/Curtain_Call:_The_Hits" title="Curtain Call: The Hits">Curtain Call: The Hits</a></i> (2005), following "<a href="/wiki/When_I%27m_Gone_(Eminem_song)" title="When I'm Gone (Eminem song)">When I'm Gone</a>." It is one of three new songs featured on the album.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Song_information">Song information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shake_That&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Song information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song features <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> in a prominent vocal role, marking his sixth and final <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> collaboration, following "Bitch Please II" from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP" title="The Marshall Mathers LP">The Marshall Mathers LP</a></i>, "Till I Collapse" from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eminem_Show" title="The Eminem Show">The Eminem Show</a></i>, and "Never Enough" from <i><a href="/wiki/Encore_(Eminem_album)" title="Encore (Eminem album)">Encore</a></i>, and the two collaborated on <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>'s "My Name", from the album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Man_vs._Machine_(album)" title="Man vs. Machine (album)">Man vs. Machine</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>' "Warrior Part 2" on the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More" title="The Hunger for More">The Hunger for More</a></i>. "Shake That" is the first of these songs to include Nate Dogg during <a href="/wiki/Verse_(poetry)" title="Verse (poetry)">verse</a>, whereas his contributions in the other works are limited to the <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">refrain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Conclusion_(music)" title="Conclusion (music)">outro</a>. Contrary to many of the second singles from each of Eminem's albums, such as "<a href="/wiki/The_Way_I_Am_(Eminem_song)" title="The Way I Am (Eminem song)">The Way I Am</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Cleanin%27_Out_My_Closet" title="Cleanin' Out My Closet">Cleanin' Out My Closet</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Like_Toy_Soldiers" title="Like Toy Soldiers">Like Toy Soldiers</a>", which are usually much darker and more stern than their predecessors, "Shake That" is a departure from the serious tone of "When I'm Gone," representing a far more playful atmosphere. It focuses on Eminem and Nate Dogg rapping about seducing women into sex in cars, and strip clubs. The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> is animated, and follows this context from the song. The single was released in early 2006 in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It became a big hit, peaking at #6. In the UK, the song was released as a download only single in April 2006, managing to peak at #28 on the UK Download Chart.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remix">Remix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shake_That&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Remix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A remixed version of the song, with Nate Dogg, <a href="/wiki/Obie_Trice" title="Obie Trice">Obie Trice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Creekwater" title="Bobby Creekwater">Bobby Creekwater</a>, appears on <i><a href="/wiki/Eminem_Presents:_The_Re-Up" title="Eminem Presents: The Re-Up">Eminem Presents: The Re-Up</a></i> (2006). Eminem does not have a verse on the remix, he does, however, still rap the intro to the song.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shake_That&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Allmusic wrote a mixed opinion: "sex song that finds Shady sounding as if he's drifting along in his own orbit. "Shake That" has an incongruous Nate Dogg crooning the chorus."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Pitchfork thinks that this song is just another "lesser version of Eminem songs that already piss me (critic) off."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> IGN called it a "rump mover" and wrote a positive review: "a booty shaker, again showing promise in Em's production. It's still a pretty simple bump-n-shuffle number, but it swings with a sense of upbeat minimalism and takes his trademark dark sounds into a slightly lighter venue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Sputnik Music wasn't satisfied: "Shake That features Nate Dogg, and is the quintessential club banger". Rolling Stone called this song a "Nate Dogg throwaway."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Shake That" was nominated for "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap/Sung_Collaboration" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration">Best Rap/Sung Collaboration</a>" at the <a href="/wiki/49th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="49th Annual Grammy Awards">49th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, but it lost to <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a> and <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/My_Love_(Justin_Timberlake_song)" title="My Love (Justin Timberlake song)">My Love</a>". "Shake That" was certified Gold in Sweden,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and Platinum in Denmark,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> both due to strong <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital downloads</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reuse_in_memes">Reuse in memes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shake_That&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reuse in memes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Shake That" and Casin by glue70 were remixed by "ＳＨＯＫＫ青" into the song "You Reposted in the Wrong Neighborhood" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> This song has spawned the "x in the wrong neighborhood" video parodies, as well as being used by several YouTubers as a background song to comic scenes.</p>
