<p><b>Sinsy</b> (<b>Sin</b>ging Voice <b>Sy</b>nthesis System) (しぃんしぃ) is an online <a href="/wiki/Hidden_Markov_model" title="Hidden Markov model">Hidden Markov model</a> (HMM)-based singing voice synthesis system by the <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Institute_of_Technology" title="Nagoya Institute of Technology">Nagoya Institute of Technology</a> that was created under the <a href="/wiki/BSD_licenses" title="BSD licenses">Modified BSD license</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The online demonstrator is free to use, but will only generate tracks up to 5 minutes. The user uploads data in the <a href="/wiki/MusicXML" title="MusicXML">MusicXML format</a>, which the Sinsy website reads to output a WAV file of the generated voice. Gender factor, vibrato intensity, and pitch shift can be adjusted prior to output.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of December 25, 2015 the official creators of the Sinsy were Keiichi Tokuda (Producer and designer), Keiichiro Oura (Design and Development), Kazuhiro Nakamura (Development and Main Maintainer), and Yoshihiko Nankaku.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Currently it only supports Japanese and English, though Mandarin is in development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Yoko (謡子), Japanese female vocal.</li>
<li>Xiang-Ling (香鈴), Japanese female vocal,an English vocal was added Christmas, 2015. Mandarin was added also to her language capabilities.</li>
<li>Matsuo-P (松尾P), English masculine vocal.</li>
<li>Namine Ritsu S (波音リツＳ), a Japanese masculine vocal. Originally produced for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTAU" title="UTAU">UTAU</a>, it released on December 25, 2013. Despite having a male avatar, it has a feminine voice.</li>
</ul>
