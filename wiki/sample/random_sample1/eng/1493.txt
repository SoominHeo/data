<p><b>Sky 2</b> was originally a <a href="/wiki/Television_channel" title="Television channel">television channel</a> from <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">British Sky Broadcasting</a>, which launched on 1 September 1996 as a sister channel to <a href="/wiki/Sky_1" title="Sky 1">Sky One</a>, as part of a new bouquet of Sky channels including <a href="/wiki/.tv_(TV_channel)" title=".tv (TV channel)">The Computer Channel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sky_Scottish" title="Sky Scottish">Sky Scottish</a>. For an opening night it held an <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">X-Files</a></i> theme night. While the channel was still being broadcast, Sky One was branded as "Sky 1". Unlike the later Sky One spin-off, Sky One Mix, the channel showed exclusive first-run television shows rather than the same programming as its parent. Prominent shows on its schedule included <i><a href="/wiki/Xena:_Warrior_Princess" title="Xena: Warrior Princess">Xena: Warrior Princess</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Profit_(TV_series)" title="Profit (TV series)">Profit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/TekWar" title="TekWar">TekWar</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Melrose_Place" title="Melrose Place">Melrose Place</a></i>.</p>
<p>Sky used the launch of the channel and the rebrand of Sky 1 to introduce <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_On-screen_Graphic" title="Digital On-screen Graphic">DOGs</a>, on both channels.</p>
<p>While at some points the channel reached viewing figures nearly equal to that of its sister channel (such as for the initial episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Xena:_Warrior_Princess" title="Xena: Warrior Princess">Xena: Warrior Princess</a></i>), it was never very successful.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was eventually closed the following year along with <a href="/wiki/Granada_Talk_TV" title="Granada Talk TV">Granada Talk TV</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Diana,_Princess_of_Wales" title="Death of Diana, Princess of Wales">death of Diana, Princess of Wales</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resurrection">Resurrection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Resurrection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sky One Mix originally launched on 9 December 2002 as Sky One's sister channel. <i>Sky One Mix</i> was rebranded as <i>Sky Mix</i> in 2004. This branded-name was short lived, because in 2005, it was rebranded as <i>Sky Two</i>, effectively reviving the Sky 2 brand. It was rebranded as <i>Sky 2</i> on 31 August 2008, when Sky's entertainment channels rebranded into the solids (used by Sky 1), liquids (used by Sky 2) and particles (used by the fallen <a href="/wiki/Pick_(TV_channel)" title="Pick (TV channel)">Sky 3</a>) theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebrand-guardian_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rebrand-guardian-1">[1]</a></sup> On 1 February 2011, Sky refreshed the presentation on many of its channels. Sky 2's idents follow the same themes as Sky 1's new idents, but feature a smaller metal '2' icon, and the action is in close-up.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming">Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sky 2 carries some programming from <a href="/wiki/Sky_Travel" title="Sky Travel">Sky Travel</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_Vegas" title="Sky Vegas">Sky Vegas</a>. Sky 2's programming mostly features sci-fi programming such as <i><a href="/wiki/Futurama" title="Futurama">Futurama</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Stargate" title="Stargate">Stargate</a></i> franchise and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">Star Trek: Enterprise</a></i>. However, other <a href="/wiki/Sky_1" title="Sky 1">Sky 1</a> drama shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Bones_(TV_series)" title="Bones (TV series)">Bones</a></i>, documentary strands such as <i><a href="/wiki/Road_Wars_(TV_series)" title="Road Wars (TV series)">Road Wars</a></i>, <i>Street Wars</i>, <i>Hello Goodbye</i> also form the Sky 2 schedule.</p>
<p>Like <a href="/wiki/Pick_(TV_channel)" title="Pick (TV channel)">Sky 3</a> (now Pick), Sky 2 did not feature repeat episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>, until recently <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> where it has started to air at 8pm daily.</p>
<p>Sky 2 is essentially a "catch-up" service for Sky's main entertainment channel <a href="/wiki/Sky_1" title="Sky 1">Sky 1</a>, broadcasting popular <a href="/wiki/Sky_1" title="Sky 1">Sky 1</a> shows a few days after their original airing. Doing so, Sky 2 is described as a <i>time shuffle</i> channel, so called to distinguish it from <a href="/wiki/Timeshift_channel" title="Timeshift channel">timeshift channels</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/E4_(TV_channel)" title="E4 (TV channel)">E4 +1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sky_Living" title="Sky Living">Sky Living +1</a> which simply broadcasts <a href="/wiki/E4_(TV_channel)" title="E4 (TV channel)">E4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sky_Living" title="Sky Living">Sky Living</a> respectively, delayed by one hour. Sky currently broadcast a <a href="/wiki/Timeshift_channel" title="Timeshift channel">timeshifted</a> version of one of their entertainment channels, <a href="/wiki/Pick_(TV_channel)" title="Pick (TV channel)">Sky 3</a>, (now Pick) which took over the <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a> EPG slot which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_News" title="Sky Sports News">Sky Sports News</a> occupied. On 12 November 2012, Sky launched a 1-hour timeshift of Sky 1, however do not currently offer a <a href="/wiki/Timeshift_channel" title="Timeshift channel">timeshifted</a> version of Sky 2.</p>
<p>Sky 2's programming showcases the best of Sky 1's content and a mix of sci-fi, action and factual programmes targeting young men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shows Sky 2 used to air include <a href="/wiki/Melrose_Place" title="Melrose Place">Melrose Place</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="The Late Show with David Letterman">The Late Show with David Letterman</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Virgin_Media_dispute">Virgin Media dispute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Virgin Media dispute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On Thursday 1 March 2007, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Media" title="Virgin Media">Virgin Media</a> removed Sky's basic channels, including <a href="/wiki/Sky_1" title="Sky 1">Sky 1</a>, Sky 2, <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_News" title="Sky Sports News">Sky Sports News</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Travel" title="Sky Travel">Sky Travel</a> and Sky Travel Extra, from their television services after a dispute between Virgin Media and <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">BSkyB</a> caused by the expiry of their carriage agreement and their inability to reach a new deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At midnight, Sky Two was removed, and the name of the station in the electronic programming guide was changed to "Old Sky Two Try <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Living_(channel)" title="Living (channel)">Living</a>" on the analogue cable service. On 13 November 2008, Sky 2 along with <a href="/wiki/Sky_1" title="Sky 1">Sky 1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_News" title="Sky Sports News">Sky Sports News</a> were returned to Virgin Media's television service along with four more Sky channels: <a href="/wiki/Sky_Arts" title="Sky Arts">Sky Arts 1</a>, Sky Arts 2, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Real_Lives" title="Sky Real Lives">Sky Real Lives</a> and Sky Real Lives 2.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky_1_timeshift_experiment">Sky 1 timeshift experiment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sky 1 timeshift experiment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 10 May 2010, Sky 2 carried an hour <a href="/wiki/Timeshift_channel" title="Timeshift channel">timeshift</a> of programming on Sky 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Sky 2 will, however, remain a standalone offering. A Sky spokesperson said: "We are experimenting with different channel schedules to bring maximum value to our customers."</p>
<p>Particular programmes which weren't shown an hour later included the premier of Sky 1's <i><a href="/wiki/Terry_Pratchett%27s_Going_Postal" title="Terry Pratchett's Going Postal">Terry Pratchett's Going Postal</a></i>, the station's latest adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Terry_Pratchett" title="Terry Pratchett">Terry Pratchett</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Discworld" title="Discworld">Discworld</a></i> novels. Also not seen on a timeshift basis was the final ever episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i> which was aired at 5am, as it was simultaneously being broadcast in the US. The last ever episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/24_(TV_series)" title="24 (TV series)">24</a></i> (Sky showed it as a two-part finale) also weren't shown an hour later on Sky 2.</p>
<p><i>Terry Pratchett's Going Postal</i> would be shown later in the week with both <i>Lost</i> and <i>24</i> being shown on Sky 2 after the final credits rolled on Sky 1. Again, alternative programmes filled the gaps.</p>
<p>The trial concluded on 9 August 2010, with the channel reverting to its previous scheduling pattern.</p>
