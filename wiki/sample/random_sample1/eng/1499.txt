<p><i><b>Spiders</b></i> is a song by Armenian-American <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/System_of_a_Down" title="System of a Down">System of a Down</a> from their <a href="/wiki/System_of_a_Down_(album)" title="System of a Down (album)">self-titled debut album</a>. The song was featured on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scream_3:_The_Album" title="Scream 3: The Album"><i>Scream 3</i> soundtrack</a> and in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_4" title="Rock Band 4">Rock Band 4</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spiders_(System_of_a_Down_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>â<b>Spiders</b>â, like many of <a href="/wiki/System_of_a_Down" title="System of a Down">System of a Down</a>'s songs, is written in the key of C minor. The song relies heavily on the Cm, Bâ­, and Eâ­ chords, as well as Fm, Gm, Aâ­, B, and Dâ­. âSpidersâ uses 4/4 time at a slow tempo, and employs drum-rolls and <a href="/wiki/Syncopation" title="Syncopation">syncopation</a> in the verses. The music can be described as haunting, ominous, dark, frightening, and depressing with its dark melody and echoing vocal overdubs. <a href="/wiki/Serj_Tankian" title="Serj Tankian">Serj Tankian</a>'s vocals, while low and melodic in the verses, become more energetic and dissonant in the refrains. The song's minor-key intro serves as a musical motif for the rest of the song; it repeats throughout the song in its normal and raised third forms. After the second refrain, <a href="/wiki/Daron_Malakian" title="Daron Malakian">Daron Malakian</a> delivers a solo bridge that creates harmonic tension with its B and Dâ­ (non-key) chords. The song ends with a soft vocal version of the intro motif.</p>
