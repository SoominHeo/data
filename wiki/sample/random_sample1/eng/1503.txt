<p><i><b>Stratovarius</b></i> is the self-titled eleventh studio album by <a href="/wiki/Power_metal" title="Power metal">power metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Stratovarius" title="Stratovarius">Stratovarius</a>, released on 5 September 2005 through <a href="/wiki/Sanctuary_Records" title="Sanctuary Records">Sanctuary Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finnishcharts_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-finnishcharts-1">[1]</a></sup> The album reached No. 4 on the <a href="/wiki/The_Official_Finnish_Charts" title="The Official Finnish Charts">Finnish albums chart</a> as well as the top 100 in six other countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finnishcharts_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-finnishcharts-1">[1]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Maniac_Dance" title="Maniac Dance">Maniac Dance</a>" was released as a single, reaching No. 4 on the <a href="/wiki/The_Official_Finnish_Charts" title="The Official Finnish Charts">Finnish singles chart</a> and the top 100 in three other countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finnishchartssingle_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-finnishchartssingle-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is the last album to feature the band's longest-standing line-up which had been consistent since <i><a href="/wiki/Episode_(Stratovarius_album)" title="Episode (Stratovarius album)">Episode</a></i> (1996), as bassist <a href="/wiki/Jari_Kainulainen" title="Jari Kainulainen">Jari Kainulainen</a> would leave Stratovarius later in the year, followed by guitarist and bandleader <a href="/wiki/Timo_Tolkki" title="Timo Tolkki">Timo Tolkki</a> in 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stratovarius_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The release of <i>Stratovarius</i> came after a highly troubled period for the band in 2004, during which they temporarily split up following a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nervous_breakdown" title="Nervous breakdown">nervous breakdown</a> suffered by Tolkki. After a year-long hiatus, they reunited in 2005 and embarked upon a worldwide tour for the album, playing for the first time in the United States and Canada.</p>
<p>Stylistically the album displays a dramatically noticeable shift from the <a href="/wiki/Symphonic_metal" title="Symphonic metal">symphonic</a> power metal sound of previous albums: the songs are more hard-edged and slower, with Tolkki's signature <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neo-classical_metal" title="Neo-classical metal">neo-classical</a> <a href="/wiki/Shred_guitar" title="Shred guitar">shred</a> <a href="/wiki/Solo_(music)" title="Solo (music)">soloing</a> almost completely absent. Keyboardist <a href="/wiki/Jens_Johansson" title="Jens Johansson">Jens Johansson</a>'s role is particularly restricted, as well as <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B6rg_Michael" title="Jörg Michael">Jörg Michael</a>'s drumming, which features hardly any of his signature <a href="/wiki/Bass_drum#Double_bass_drum" title="Bass drum">double bass</a> playing. Singer <a href="/wiki/Timo_Kotipelto" title="Timo Kotipelto">Timo Kotipelto</a> also attempts a different vocal approach, mostly avoiding his usual high <a href="/wiki/Vocal_range" title="Vocal range">vocal range</a>. Tolkki has since stated in interviews that he was unsatisfied with the end result of the album due to it being too far removed from the band's true style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
