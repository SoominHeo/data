<p>"<b>Super Bass</b>" is a song by Trinidadian-American recording artist <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> from the deluxe version of her debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pink_Friday" title="Pink Friday">Pink Friday</a></i> (2010). It was sent to US rhythmic radio stations as the album's fifth single on April 5, 2011, while "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Girls_Fall_like_Dominoes" title="Girls Fall like Dominoes">Girls Fall like Dominoes</a>" was released in the United Kingdom, Ireland, Australia, and New Zealand. The song was co-written by Minaj, along with <a href="/wiki/Ester_Dean" title="Ester Dean">Ester Dean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roahn_Hylton" title="Roahn Hylton">Roahn Hylton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kane_Beatz" title="Kane Beatz">Kane Beatz</a>, who also produced the song along with JMIKE. According to Minaj, the song's lyrics detail the story of a playful romance between a male and a female. The <a href="/wiki/Pop-rap" title="Pop-rap">pop-rap</a> song contains <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a> influences, while Minaj raps over a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> beat.</p>
<p>The song peaked at number three on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, as well as number six in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It also reached number eight on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. An accompanying music video for the single was shot in March 2011 by director <a href="/wiki/Sanaa_Hamri" title="Sanaa Hamri">Sanaa Hamri</a>. The video shows Minaj playfully taunting a group of men and makes use of multiple props, all of which are colored bubblegum pink. As of 2017, "Super Bass" has sold 8.2 million copies worldwide.</p>

<p>"Super Bass" was co-written by Minaj, <a href="/wiki/Ester_Dean" title="Ester Dean">Ester Dean</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roahn_%E2%80%9CFirst_Born%E2%80%9D_Hylton" title="Roahn “First Born” Hylton">Roahn Hylton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Kane_Beatz" title="Kane Beatz">Daniel "Kane Beatz" Johnson</a>, who produced the song alongside JMIKE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cre_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cre-4">[4]</a></sup> While being interviewed by <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a> during the video shoot for the single, Minaj explained the song's concept, stating: "'Super Bass' is about the boy that you are crushing over, [...] And you kind of want to get your mack on, but you're taking the playful approach."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Superbassvideo_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Superbassvideo-5">[5]</a></sup> The song utilizes <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> in its composition, while Minaj raps over a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> beat,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spy_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-spy-6">[6]</a></sup> while also infusing elements of <a href="/wiki/J-pop" title="J-pop">J-pop</a> combined with an upbeat hook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-guard-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-metro1-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longislandpress1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-longislandpress1-9">[9]</a></sup> It uses a busy beat that is interspersed with digital rain drop sound effects and strong bass. Minaj's vocals in the song have been described as "breathy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baltimoresun1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-baltimoresun1-10">[10]</a></sup> "Super Bass" is written in the key of <a href="/wiki/B_major" title="B major">B major</a> with a moderate tempo of 128 <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">beats per minute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Despite writing in the key of B major, the song has no B major chords.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Minaj's lyrics in the song are often provocative and suggestive, with lines like "He just gotta give me that look, when he give me that look / Then the panties comin' off, off, uh" and a catchy onomatopoetic refrain: "Boom, badoom, boom, boom, badoom, boom, bass, he got dat super bass".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-metro1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brad Wete of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> stated that the song describes Minaj's type of man as "one who hits the gym often and has first-class... money" as she states: "He pops bottles and he’s got the right kind of build.... He’s always in the air / But he never flies coach".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-music-mixmix_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-music-mixmix-12">[12]</a></sup> Charley Rogulewski of <a href="/wiki/AOL" title="AOL">AOL</a>'s The Boombox stated that "the uber-catchy 'boom, badoom, boom, boom' chorus makes 'Super Bass' the most pop-friendly Minaj song released to radio [sic] yet".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Described as a lighter side to Minaj by Jessica Sinclair of <i>Long Island Press</i>, the song gets more in-depth with a little more pop and an upbeat hook that "really sticks"; however, Minaj still assists the song's pop edge with "crazy rapping skills".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longislandpress1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-longislandpress1-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wesley Case of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Baltimore_Sun" title="The Baltimore Sun">The Baltimore Sun</a></i> gave the song a positive review, stating that it was an "obvious hit". He compared the song to the rest of <i>Pink Friday</i>, stating that it is much more potent as compared to the rest of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baltimoresun1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-baltimoresun1-10">[10]</a></sup> Case later added: "<i>Pink Friday</i> is short on memorable hooks, but 'Super Bass' is an exception".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baltimoresun1_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-baltimoresun1-10">[10]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rap-Up" title="Rap-Up">Rap-Up</a></i> described the tune as "infectious".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Jessica Sinclair of <i><a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Press" title="Long Island Press">Long Island Press</a></i> complimented Nicki Minaj for taking "center stage" on the song, due to her solo performance, and added that in doing so Minaj created a success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longislandpress2_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-longislandpress2-15">[15]</a></sup> Sinclair additionally commented on the song as a lighter side to Minaj, and complimented the hook as upbeat and added that it "really sticks".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longislandpress2_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-longislandpress2-15">[15]</a></sup> Rosie Gray of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BlackBook_(magazine)" title="BlackBook (magazine)">BlackBook</a></i> complimented the song as a "killer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackbookmag1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-blackbookmag1-16">[16]</a></sup> Alex Pielak of <i><a href="/wiki/Metro_(British_newspaper)" title="Metro (British newspaper)">Metro</a></i> compared Minaj's "Super Bass" to <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>'s "Move Your Body", stating that: "Nicki wins for the sheer number of words she's managed to cram in – and therefore takes the face off".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro1_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-metro1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lewis Corner from <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> gave the single four out of a possible five stars. Corner wrote that the song's chorus was "sharper than a Sherbet Dib Dab." Minaj's "eccentric rapping style" was praised in the review with Corner writing that the song was likely to become her first solo top ten hit in the United States or the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Claire Suddath of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> magazine named "Super Bass" one of the Top 10 Songs of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> As of January 2015, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> listed the song as one of "The 20 Best Songs of 2010s (So Far)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Pazz_%26_Jop" title="Pazz &amp; Jop">Pazz &amp; Jop</a> annual critics' poll ranked "Super Bass" at number three to find the best music of 2011.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the week of May 14, 2011, "Super Bass" debuted on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at position 98.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The following week it rose 50 spots to number 48.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On the week titled May 21, 2011, "Super Bass" debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Songs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Digital_Songs" title="Hot Digital Songs">Hot Digital Songs</a> charts at number 25 and 31 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In its 14th week, the song reached a new peak at number three on the Hot 100, while also having longevity on the charts for 39 weeks; 38 of which were in the top 50.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcurrent_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcurrent-24">[24]</a></sup> In Canada, "Super Bass" debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> at number 92.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> "Super Bass" entered the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> at number 46 on May 21, 2011, and after nine non-consecutive weeks of moving up the chart, reached a peak of number eight on the chart for the week ending August 13, 2011. In December 2013, "Super Bass" was certified 8× platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> As of December 2014, the song has sold 5 million copies in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-us_sales_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-us_sales-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:30%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"I just wanted to do something real colorful and cutesy. This is an icy world, it's a sexy world, it's a playful world. Of course I have lots of eye candy for my girls and my boys [...] We have a lot of little surprises in store for this particular video, [...] I would like to put out 50 videos. The videos really tell the story a lot more elaborately than just the song does."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">Minaj explaining the video's inspiration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Superbassvideo_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Superbassvideo-5">[5]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>On March 10, 2011, Minaj revealed during an interview with <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a> that she was shooting a video for "Super Bass" with director <a href="/wiki/Sanaa_Hamri" title="Sanaa Hamri">Sanaa Hamri</a> and producers: Kimberly S. Stuckwisch, Michelle Larkin &amp; Keith "KB" Brown. She explained the video's concept, stating that she wanted the video to be full of eye candy and that she wanted it to be colorful.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Superbassvideo_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Superbassvideo-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up1_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up1-28">[28]</a></sup> On April 26, 2011, Minaj premiered a sneak peek of the music video on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>'s <a href="/wiki/106_%26_Park" title="106 &amp; Park">106 &amp; Park</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up2_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up2-29">[29]</a></sup> The preview showed Minaj in a pink wig with similarly-clad backup dancers gyrating to the song, wearing a uniform of white tank tops, denim shorts, and different colored work boots and the same laces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up2_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up2-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Originally the video was set to premiere on the same show as where the preview was premiered on April 27, 2011, but instead was postponed. Minaj took to <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> to explain that the music video has been pulled from the show and would not be premiering that date for unknown reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up2_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up2-29">[29]</a></sup> The music video instead premiered on Minaj's official <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> account on May 5, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up3_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up3-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The music video begins with a close-up of Minaj as she opens her eyes and begins to blink as the song begins. As Minaj raps verses of the song, <a href="/wiki/Barbie" title="Barbie">Barbie</a>- approved props; a pink Ferrari, pink plane and a pink pool, are shown contemplating what Minaj is saying in the verse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longislandpress1_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-longislandpress1-9">[9]</a></sup> Minaj then begins to playfully tease men as clips of ice-speakers and a motorcycle made of ice are interlaced into the scene. As the chorus begins, Minaj is seen performing choreography with five <a href="/wiki/Cloning" title="Cloning">clones</a> of herself. As the video continues, Minaj is seen in a green wig at a pool side with several well-built men, and then splashing with a man in the pool of pink water. Minaj then continues to ride the motorcycle made of ice while wearing a skintight pink bodysuit emblazoned with giraffe prints, a half-blond and pink wig, gold eye shadow and bright pink lipstick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Superbassvideo_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Superbassvideo-5">[5]</a></sup> Minaj then begins to reprise her lap dances in the dark, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_lights" title="Black lights">black lights</a> setting the glowing tone of the dancers, as Minaj glows in the dark with glowing lips, hair and makeup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longislandpress1_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-longislandpress1-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up3_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up3-30">[30]</a></sup> She and her dancers play in feathers that are also glow in the dark towards the end of the video. The video closes with Safaree S.B. Samuels dancing beside her, and Minaj lit in the black lighting, biting her finger while laying across the lap of a male she gave a lapdance to in the video.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception_and_accolades">Critical reception and accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical reception and accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>D.L. Chandler from Rapfix complimented the video's visuals writing, "it is a dazzling visual affair if nothing else".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> AOL music's Contessa Gayles reviewed the video, writing, "The highlight of the Sanaa Hamri-directed video? The glow-in-the-dark lap-dance scene."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On the day of its premiere, <i><a href="/wiki/Rap-Up" title="Rap-Up">Rap-Up</a></i> complimented the video as "vibrant".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up3_30-3"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up3-30">[30]</a></sup> Becky Bain of <i>Idolator</i> gave the song a positive review, comparing it to "<a href="/wiki/Judas_(Lady_Gaga_song)" title="Judas (Lady Gaga song)">Judas</a>" stating, "Super Bass had the better choreography in an anxiously-awaited music video featuring a pop star riding a motorcycle released today. (Sorry, 'Judas')".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>'s <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> found Minaj's signature lap dance "saucy" and complimented the clip's use of "hunky men" and "outrageous flirting".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Brad Wete of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> stated that there is plenty of "eye candy" for both men and women, stating that Minaj "ratchets up the sex appeal" in the clip.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-music-mixmix_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-music-mixmix-12">[12]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i> favored the fluorescent-lighting scene of the music video stating that "She'd be a good person to have with you at <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glasto</a> when you're trying to find your tent".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Wesley Case of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Baltimore_Sun" title="The Baltimore Sun">The Baltimore Sun</a></i> complimented the video as wildly stylized that "looks like a comic book come to life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baltimoresun1_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-baltimoresun1-10">[10]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2011_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2011 MTV Video Music Awards">2011 MTV Video Music Awards</a>, Minaj's video for "Super Bass" was nominated under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Female_Video" title="Best Female Video">Best Female Video</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Hip-Hop_Video" title="Best Hip-Hop Video">Best Hip-Hop Video</a> categories, winning the latter and gaining Minaj her first moonman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moonman_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-moonman-36">[36]</a></sup> As of January 2017, the video has been viewed over 640 million times on YouTube.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Super Bass" was performed live for the first time during the <a href="/wiki/2011_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2011 Billboard Music Awards">2011 Billboard Music Awards</a> on May 22, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Minaj performed the song alongside <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a> at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2011" title="American Music Awards of 2011">American Music Awards of 2011</a> on November 20, 2011, and also performed Guetta's song "<a href="/wiki/Turn_Me_On_(David_Guetta_song)" title="Turn Me On (David Guetta song)">Turn Me On</a>", which Minaj was featured on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Minaj also performed the song on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dick_Clark%27s_New_Year%27s_Rockin%27_Eve_with_Ryan_Seacrest" title="Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve with Ryan Seacrest">Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve with Ryan Seacrest</a></i> and on the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Victoria%27s_Secret_Fashion_Show" title="Victoria's Secret Fashion Show">Victoria's Secret Fashion Show</a> which aired on November 29, 2011 on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_year_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-new_year-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-victoria_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-victoria-40">[40]</a></sup> The song was the encore song on Minaj's debut concert tour, the <a href="/wiki/Pink_Friday_Tour" title="Pink Friday Tour">Pink Friday Tour</a>. She also has performed the song on her <a href="/wiki/Pink_Friday:_Reloaded_Tour" title="Pink Friday: Reloaded Tour">Pink Friday: Reloaded Tour</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Pinkprint_Tour" title="The Pinkprint Tour">The Pinkprint Tour</a>.</p>
<p>Minaj performed "Super Bass" at the <a href="/wiki/2014_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2014 MTV Europe Music Awards">2014 MTV Europe Music Awards</a> followed by "Bed of Lies" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Skylar_Grey" title="Skylar Grey">Skylar Grey</a>) and "<a href="/wiki/Anaconda_(Nicki_Minaj_song)" title="Anaconda (Nicki Minaj song)">Anaconda</a>". On May 30, 2015, Minaj also performed the song on the iHeartRadio Summer Pool Party 2015 in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_versions">Cover versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cover versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was covered by <a href="/wiki/Karmin" title="Karmin">Karmin</a> and it was released as a single on iTunes on June 6, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Also, British girl group <a href="/wiki/Little_Mix" title="Little Mix">Little Mix</a> (named Rhythmix at that time) covered it on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK)" title="The X Factor (UK)">The X Factor</a></i> in the eighth series which was also featured on their debut single "Cannonball", it was covered in the TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i> as a mashup with <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I_Still_Believe_(Brenda_K._Starr_song)" title="I Still Believe (Brenda K. Starr song)">I Still Believe</a>". American <a href="/wiki/Power_pop" title="Power pop">power pop</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Downtown_Fiction" title="The Downtown Fiction">The Downtown Fiction</a> covered "Super Bass" for 2011's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punk_Goes_Pop_4" title="Punk Goes Pop 4">Punk Goes Pop 4</a></i> album, giving it more of a rock n roll sound while changing some (but not all) of the gender references. <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Grace_%26_Rosie" title="Sophia Grace &amp; Rosie">Sophia Grace Brownlee</a> also covered the song in a YouTube video that went viral and launched her career in the film and music industry.</p>
