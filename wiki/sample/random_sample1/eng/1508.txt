<p><b>Superfly</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> rock act that debuted on April 4, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mpaj.or.jp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mpaj.or.jp-2">[2]</a></sup> Formerly a duo, the act now consists solely of lyricist and vocalist Shiho Ochi with former guitarist Kōichi Tabo still credited as the group's composer and part-time lyricist. Superfly's first two studio albums have been certified double platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_Japan" title="Recording Industry Association of Japan">Recording Industry Association of Japan</a>, and their four albums (the third being classified as a "single" by the group) all debuted at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>'s Weekly Album Charts, a first for a female recording artist in Japan in over seven years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon.co.jp_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon.co.jp-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003.E2.80.932006:_Formation">2003–2006: Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2003–2006: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shiho Ochi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">越智 志帆</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ochi Shiho</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> met Kōichi Tabo <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">多保 孝一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tabo Kōichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in 2003 while they were students at <a href="/wiki/Matsuyama_University" title="Matsuyama University">Matsuyama University</a>. They were both members of a music circle that covered songs by <a href="/wiki/Finger_5" title="Finger 5">Finger 5</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">the Rolling Stones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2004, the group formed the blues band "Superfly",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nippon_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nippon-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superfly-web_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-superfly-web-5">[5]</a></sup> naming themselves after <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Mayfield" title="Curtis Mayfield">Curtis Mayfield</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Superfly_(song)" title="Superfly (song)">Superfly</a>". The group disbanded in 2005, with only Ochi and Tabo remaining when they went to Tokyo to seek out a label.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932008:_Superfly">2007–2008: <i>Superfly</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2008: Superfly">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a battle between recording labels, the duo debuted on <a href="/wiki/Warner_Music_Group" title="Warner Music Group">Warner Music Group</a> with their 2007 single "<a href="/wiki/Hello_Hello_(Superfly_song)" title="Hello Hello (Superfly song)">Hello Hello</a>". This was followed by their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shibuya,_Tokyo" title="Shibuya, Tokyo">Shibuya, Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Store" title="Apple Store">Apple Store</a> performance and the subsequent EP <i><a href="/wiki/Live_from_Tokyo_(EP)" title="Live from Tokyo (EP)">Live from Tokyo</a></i>. On November 8, 2007, a few months after their second single "<a href="/wiki/Manifesto_(Superfly_song)" title="Manifesto (Superfly song)">Manifesto</a>" was released, Kōichi Tabo announced on their website that he stopped appearing on the stage to concentrate on composing the songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Besides being active behind the scenes as Superfly’s composer &amp; arranger, Tabo has also written music for artists such as <a href="/wiki/Charice_Pempengco" title="Charice Pempengco">Charice Pempengco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mai_Fukui" title="Mai Fukui">Mai Fukui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yuna_Ito" title="Yuna Ito">Yuna Ito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maaya_Sakamoto" title="Maaya Sakamoto">Maaya Sakamoto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Becca_(musician)" title="Becca (musician)">BECCA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saki_Fukuda" title="Saki Fukuda">Saki Fukuda</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Asia_Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Asia Engineer (page does not exist)">Asia Engineer</a>. Later that month on November 28, 2007, Superfly released a single in collaboration with the Australian band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jet_(band)" title="Jet (band)">Jet</a>, titled "<a href="/wiki/I_Spy_I_Spy" title="I Spy I Spy">I Spy I Spy</a>".</p>
<p>Superfly's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Superfly_(Superfly_album)" title="Superfly (Superfly album)">Superfly</a></i> was released on May 14, 2008, topping the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> weekly album charts for two weeks. The release of the album was also celebrated with a free concert at <a href="/wiki/Yoyogi_Park" title="Yoyogi Park">Yoyogi Park</a> in Tokyo. Following its release, Ochi, who has a great admiration for <a href="/wiki/Janis_Joplin" title="Janis Joplin">Janis Joplin</a>, was given the chance to travel to <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> in 2008 where she travelled through <a href="/wiki/Haight-Ashbury" title="Haight-Ashbury">Haight-Ashbury</a> and met up with <a href="/wiki/Sam_Andrew" title="Sam Andrew">Sam Andrew</a> of <a href="/wiki/Big_Brother_and_the_Holding_Company" title="Big Brother and the Holding Company">Big Brother and the Holding Company</a> as part of a documentary titled <i>Following the Steps of Janis</i> on <a href="/wiki/Music_On!_TV" title="Music On! TV">Music On! TV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Ochi also counts <a href="/wiki/Carole_King" title="Carole King">Carole King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Muldaur" title="Maria Muldaur">Maria Muldaur</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">the Rolling Stones</a> as some of her other favorite artists. In June and July, Superfly did a national tour titled the Rock'N'Roll Show 2008 Tour</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.932009:_Woodstock_and_Box_Emotions">2008–2009: Woodstock and <i>Box Emotions</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2008–2009: Woodstock and Box Emotions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The asteroid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/91907_Shiho" title="91907 Shiho">91907 Shiho</a> was named after Ochi in 2009, as the asteroid was discovered at the <a href="/wiki/Kuma_Kogen_Astronomical_Observatory" title="Kuma Kogen Astronomical Observatory">Kuma Kogen Astronomical Observatory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ehime_Prefecture" title="Ehime Prefecture">Ehime Prefecture</a>, her home prefecture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> April also saw the release of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock%27N%27Roll_Show_2008" title="Rock'N'Roll Show 2008">Rock'N'Roll Show 2008</a></i> DVD set from the band's 2008 tour stop at the <a href="/wiki/NHK_Hall" title="NHK Hall">NHK Hall</a>.</p>
<p>During the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Heroes_of_Woodstock_Tour" title="Heroes of Woodstock Tour">Heroes of Woodstock Tour</a> for the tour's stop in <a href="/wiki/Bethel,_New_York" title="Bethel, New York">Bethel, New York</a>, the site of the original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woodstock_Festival" title="Woodstock Festival">Woodstock Festival</a>, Ochi joined Big Brother and the Holding Company on stage and performed "<a href="/wiki/Down_on_Me_(traditional_song)" title="Down on Me (traditional song)">Down on Me</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Piece_of_My_Heart" title="Piece of My Heart">Piece of My Heart</a>" as a follow up to the previous documentary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The third single from <i>Box Emotions</i>, "<a href="/wiki/Alright!!_(Superfly_song)" title="Alright!! (Superfly song)">Alright!!</a>", was used as the opening theme to the Japanese television drama, BOSS, which began airing on April 16, 2009, as well as its second season which aired in 2011.</p>
<p>Superfly's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Box_Emotions" title="Box Emotions">Box Emotions</a></i>, was released on September 2, 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jame-22.07.2009_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-jame-22.07.2009-14">[14]</a></sup> which she celebrated by holding a free live show at the <a href="/wiki/Roppongi_Hills" title="Roppongi Hills">Roppongi Hills</a> arena,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> which hosted 4000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <i>Box Emotions</i> debuted at No. 1 on the Oricon weekly charts, with the first week sales of around 214,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> For the Japanese <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> Rewind of 2009, <i>Box Emotions</i> was the album of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Superfly went on tour from October through December to support the album in the "Superly Box Emotions 2009 Tour".</p>
<p>Ochi has also teamed up with fashion designer Manami Kobayashi for the A.I.C (Another Important Culture) brand to create a line of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woodstock_Festival" title="Woodstock Festival">Woodstock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hippie" title="Hippie">hippie</a> subculture themed clothing called "Cosmic Market".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010:_.22Wildflower_.26_Cover_Songs:_Complete_Best.22">2010: "Wildflower &amp; <i>Cover Songs: Complete Best</i>"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title='Edit section: 2010: "Wildflower &amp; Cover Songs: Complete Best"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Superfly's first single since <i>Box Emotions</i> titled "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_on_the_Fire" title="Dancing on the Fire">Dancing on the Fire</a>" won Best Rock Video at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards_Japan" title="MTV Video Music Awards Japan">MTV Video Music Awards Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> April saw the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_at_Budokan!!" title="Dancing at Budokan!!">Dancing at Budokan!!</a></i>, Superfly's second video album featuring the band's performance at the <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Budokan" title="Nippon Budokan">Nippon Budokan</a> in 2009.</p>
<p>Her first released song of 2010, "Tamashii Revolution", was used as <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> theme song,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and was released digitally on June 18, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> "Tamashii Revolution" was later included as a B-side on the single and cover album compilation "<a href="/wiki/Wildflower_(Superfly_song)" title="Wildflower (Superfly song)">Wildflower</a>" &amp; <i><a href="/wiki/Cover_Songs:_Complete_Best_%27Track_3%27" title="Cover Songs: Complete Best 'Track 3'">Cover Songs: Complete Best 'Track 3'</a></i>, which became Superfly's third album to debut at number 1 on the Oricon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 30, 2010, Superfly performed at the <a href="/wiki/Zepp" title="Zepp">Zepp</a> Tokyo arena alongside a session band called "The Lemon Bats" (consisting of <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/MO%27SOME_TONEBENDER" title="ja:MO'SOME TONEBENDER">Mo'Some Tonebender</a>'s Kazuhiro Momo and Superfly's touring &amp; recording guitarist Yoshiyuki Yatsuhashi on guitar, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Koichi_Tsutaya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Koichi Tsutaya (page does not exist)">Koichi Tsutaya</a> on keyboard, <a href="/wiki/Straightener_(band)" title="Straightener (band)">Straightener</a>'s Hidekazu Hinata on bass, and <a href="/wiki/Tatsuya_Nakamura" title="Tatsuya Nakamura">Tatsuya Nakamura</a> on drums) in a show called the <i>Switch 25th Anniversary presents Superfly &amp; The Lemon Bats Special Live "Rock'N' Roll Muncher"</i>. Performing as "Superfly &amp; The Lemon Bats", Ochi performed covers of "<a href="/wiki/Dr._Feelgood_(song)" title="Dr. Feelgood (song)">Dr. Feelgood</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Beat_It" title="Beat It">Beat It</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Barracuda_(song)" title="Barracuda (song)">Barracuda</a>", "<a href="/wiki/White_Room" title="White Room">White Room</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I_Saw_Her_Standing_There" title="I Saw Her Standing There">I Saw Her Standing There</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Land_of_a_Thousand_Dances" title="Land of a Thousand Dances">Land of a Thousand Dances</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/L.A.M.F." title="L.A.M.F.">Born to Lose</a>", in addition to covers of her own songs "Tanjō", "Manifesto", "Free Planet", "Alright!!", "<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll,_Hoochie_Koo" title="Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo">Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Bitch_(The_Rolling_Stones_song)" title="Bitch (The Rolling Stones song)">Bitch</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_Mind_Travel">2011: <i>Mind Travel</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011: Mind Travel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Superfly's song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beep_(Superfly_song)" title="Beep (Superfly song)">Beep!!</a>" was used as the theme for the film <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Manzai_Gang&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Manzai Gang (page does not exist)">Manzai Gang</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and was on a double A-side single with "<a href="/wiki/Sunshine_Sunshine" title="Sunshine Sunshine">Sunshine Sunshine</a>", a song used for the <a href="/wiki/KDDI" title="KDDI">KDDI</a> "Meet the Music 2011" campaign. It was intended that she was to perform "Sunshine Sunshine" live at the Okinawa Namura Hall in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naha,_Okinawa" title="Naha, Okinawa">Naha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Prefecture" title="Okinawa Prefecture">Okinawa Prefecture</a>, on March 20, 2011, with the performance broadcast live over FM52 radio stations <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_FM" title="Tokyo FM">Tokyo FM</a>, <a href="/wiki/J-Wave" title="J-Wave">J-Wave</a>, and <a href="/wiki/FM_Okinawa" title="FM Okinawa">FM Okinawa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> This performance, however, was postponed due to the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>. In response, Ochi recorded an <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> version of "Sunshine Sunshine" which she posted on her blog on March 18, 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and wrote the new song "<a href="/wiki/You_%26_Me_(Superfly_song)" title="You &amp; Me (Superfly song)">You &amp; Me</a>", which she posted on her official blog four days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> "You &amp; Me" was subsequently made available for purchase on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recochoku" title="Recochoku">Recochoku</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wamo!&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wamo! (page does not exist)">wamo!</a> digital music stores and all proceeds went to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Red_Cross" title="Japanese Red Cross">Japanese Red Cross</a>'s relief efforts in Tōhoku.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> By May, the song had raised ¥7,831,780 (approx. US$96.8k, €68k, or £59.4k) for the Red Cross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Superfly also had a national tour in 2011, visiting 32 cities and 35 venues. The tour, dubbed "Mind Traveler", supported the group's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Mind_Travel" title="Mind Travel">Mind Travel</a></i>, which was released on June 15, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Superfly also held a free concert at the <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Red_Brick_Warehouse" title="Yokohama Red Brick Warehouse">Yokohama Red Brick Warehouse</a> to celebrate the release, and did a free stream on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ustream.tv" title="Ustream.tv">Ustream.tv</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> On June 21, 2011, <i>Mind Travel</i> topped the Oricon's weekly album charts having sold over 166,000 copies, making it Superfly's fourth consecutive album to debut at number 1, a feat not duplicated by a female artist since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hikaru_Utada" title="Hikaru Utada">Hikaru Utada</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon.co.jp_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon.co.jp-3">[3]</a></sup> In August 2011, Superfly announced that the band would go on an arena tour titled "Shout in the Rainbow!!", with shows at the <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Super_Arena" title="Saitama Super Arena">Saitama Super Arena</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a> <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Gaishi_Hall" title="Nippon Gaishi Hall">Nippon Gaishi Hall</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Osaka-j%C5%8D_Hall" title="Osaka-jō Hall">Osaka-jō Hall</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> This news was followed by the release of the band's 14th single "<a href="/wiki/Ai_o_Kurae" title="Ai o Kurae">Ai o Kurae</a>", a song for their upcoming 4th album.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_Force">2012: <i>Force</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2012: Force">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, Superfly participated in the JAPAN UNITED with MUSIC 30-member supergroup in recording a cover of <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a>' "<a href="/wiki/All_You_Need_Is_Love" title="All You Need Is Love">All You Need Is Love</a>" to raise money for reconstruction efforts regarding the <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_of_the_2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="Aftermath of the 2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">aftermath of the 2011 earthquake and tsunami</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> For the band's 5th anniversary, Superfly is releasing the <i><a href="/wiki/Shout_in_the_Rainbow!!" title="Shout in the Rainbow!!">Shout in the Rainbow!!</a></i> video album featuring performances from the band's December 2011 arena tour stop at the Osaka-jō Hall. Limited editions of the video release will include a CD featuring the previously unreleased track "Sasurai no Tabibito" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">さすらいの旅人</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Wandering Traveller")</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>While doing a special live broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Ustream" title="Ustream">Ustream</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nico_Nico_Douga" title="Nico Nico Douga">Nico Nico Douga</a> to celebrate the <i>Shout in the Rainbow!!</i> release, Superfly announced that they would be releasing a fourth studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Force_(Superfly_album)" title="Force (Superfly album)">Force</a></i> on September 19, 2012. To commemorate the band's five-year anniversary, a special edition of the album including a bonus CD, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vinyl_record" title="Vinyl record">vinyl</a> copy of the album, and a commemorative poster are to be released along with a standard single-CD edition, a limited two CD edition, and an edition exclusively released through <a href="/wiki/Lawson_(store)" title="Lawson (store)">Lawson</a> convenience stores.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-natalie-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barks_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-barks-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-40">[40]</a></sup> One week later, <i>Shout in the Rainbow!!</i> would top the Oricon's Blu-ray weekly rankings, making Superfly the fourth ever solo artist to top those charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> After a free live concert at Tokyo's Yoyogi Park with livecasts on various internet services on the release date,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> <i>Force</i> will be supported by a 35-stop concert hall tour called "Superfly Tour 2012 'Live Force'", starting on October 26, 2012.</p>
<p>Superfly has also recorded a duet with <a href="/wiki/Tortoise_Matsumoto" title="Tortoise Matsumoto">Tortoise Matsumoto</a> titled "<a href="/wiki/Stars_(Superfly_%26_Tortoise_Matsumoto_song)" title="Stars (Superfly &amp; Tortoise Matsumoto song)">STARS</a>" which will be used by <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Television" title="Fuji Television">Fuji Television</a> as its theme song for its broadcasts of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>. It was released to <a href="/wiki/Truetone" title="Truetone">truetone</a> distributing stores on May 11, 2012, and was released as a single on July 25, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The release of "STARS" was followed by the double A-side single "<a href="/wiki/Kagayaku_Tsuki_no_Y%C5%8D_ni" title="Kagayaku Tsuki no Yō ni">Kagayaku Tsuki no Yō ni</a>" / "<a href="/wiki/The_Bird_Without_Wings" title="The Bird Without Wings">The Bird Without Wings</a>", with its title tracks used as the theme songs for the television drama <i>Summer Rescue</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and the film <i><a href="/wiki/Ushijima_the_Loan_Shark" title="Ushijima the Loan Shark">Ushijima the Loan Shark</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> respectively. <i>Force</i> sold 119,000 units in its first week, placing it at number 1 on the Oricon's Weekly charts, making Superfly one of only 4 female solo artists to have five consecutive number 1 debuts on the charts; she is included amongst <a href="/wiki/Namie_Amuro" title="Namie Amuro">Namie Amuro</a> (fifth #1 in February 2000), <a href="/wiki/Mai_Kuraki" title="Mai Kuraki">Mai Kuraki</a> (January 2004), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hikaru_Utada" title="Hikaru Utada">Hikaru Utada</a> (June 2006).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013-2014:_Fifth_anniversary_and_Superfly_Best">2013-2014: Fifth anniversary and <i>Superfly Best</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2013-2014: Fifth anniversary and Superfly Best">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The group announced it would release their first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_of" title="Best of">best of</a> album on September 25, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> During this time, Superfly recorded "Starting Over" which is used by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J-WAVE" title="J-WAVE">J-WAVE</a> as the theme song for their 25th anniversary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> The album was then announced to be simply titled <i><a href="/wiki/Superfly_Best" title="Superfly Best">Superfly Best</a></i>, and includes the 26 singles Superfly has released since their major debut and three new songs: "Starting Over", "Always", and "Bi-Li-Li Emotion".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> The album sold 157 thousand copies in its first week, earning the number 1 spot on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>. <i>Superfly Best</i> became Superfly's 6th consecutive number 1 album, making Shiho Ochi only the second solo female artist to achieve such a goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-present:_White">2014-present: <i>White</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfly_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2014-present: White">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Superfly was chosen again to perform the theme song to the sequel of the film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Ushijima_the_Loan_Shark" title="Ushijima the Loan Shark">Ushijima the Loan Shark</a></i>, as the song "<a href="/wiki/Live_(Superfly_song)" title="Live (Superfly song)">Live</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Superfly will also perform the theme song for the <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Zestiria" title="Tales of Zestiria">Tales of Zestiria</a></i> video game, a song titled "<a href="/wiki/White_Light_(Superfly_song)" title="White Light (Superfly song)">White Light</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVspot_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVspot-61">[61]</a></sup> Superfly also released their 18th single "<a href="/wiki/Ai_o_Karada_ni_Fukikonde" title="Ai o Karada ni Fukikonde">Ai o Karada ni Fukikonde</a>" in 2014. After the digital release of "White Light" in January 2015, it was announced that Superfly's 5th studio album was titled <i><a href="/wiki/White_(Superfly_album)" title="White (Superfly album)">White</a></i>, and would be released on May 27, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
