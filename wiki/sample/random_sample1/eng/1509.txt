<p><b>Superfruit</b> (often stylized as <b>SUP3RFRUIT</b>) is a comedy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Web_show" title="Web show">web show</a> on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, hosted by Mitchell Coby Michael Grassi (known professionally as Mitch Grassi)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and Scott Richard Hoying (known professionally as Scott Hoying).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Grassi and Hoying are both members of the <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> group <a href="/wiki/Pentatonix" title="Pentatonix">Pentatonix</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and have been friends since they were 10 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>While the show mainly focuses on themselves and comedy, the show occasionally features vocal performances by Grassi and Hoying. They also tend to have a number of collaborations with many YouTube celebrities, such as <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Oakley" title="Tyler Oakley">Tyler Oakley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Hart" title="Hannah Hart">Hannah Hart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mamrie_Hart" title="Mamrie Hart">Mamrie Hart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Sings" title="Miranda Sings">Miranda Sings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todrick_Hall" title="Todrick Hall">Todrick Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Dillon" title="Ricky Dillon">Ricky Dillon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Connor_Franta" title="Connor Franta">Connor Franta</a>. On October 18, 2016, the pair released an original song Bad 4 Us on their channel. Later, on November 15, 2016, they released a second original song Sweet Life, with more official music planned for 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The channel currently has over two million subscribers and has accumulated over 280 million views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfruit_(web_show)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to Grassi, "[Scott and I] were at <a href="/wiki/IHOP" title="IHOP">IHOP</a> one day and both discussed how we wanted to individually start [YouTube] channels. [Then] we were [like, 'Why don't we just do it together?']"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> According to Scott, the name Superfruit "came from Mitch's random mind".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The first Superfruit video was released on August 13, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>Superfruit consists mainly of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vlogs" title="Vlogs">vlogs</a> about the duo's daily lives and various casual games and competitions, but the channel also features a number of musical projects. Both Grassi and Hoying are members of the LGBTQ community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Although they are not dating, the pair gave themselves the portmanteau Sc√∂m√¨che to represent their relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Together they have a cat named Wyatt Blue Grassi-Hoying, who appears in a number of their videos and logo. Most videos feature just Hoying and Grassi, however several include other artists or guests.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="End_segments">End segments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfruit_(web_show)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: End segments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Superfruit has had many different end segments to their videos, which have varied over time but (as of September 2015) consist of Song Suggestions and Weekly Obsessions. The 'Weekly Obsessions' segment began from the first Superfruit episode and has continued ever since. They usually consist of items that Grassi or Hoying have recently purchased, and are often items of clothing. In the 'Comment on Comments' segment, inspired by YouTuber <a href="/wiki/Grace_Helbig" title="Grace Helbig">Grace Helbig</a>'s previous channel, DailyGrace, featured a number of comments on the last week's video, which were commented on by Grassi and Hoying. It featured one positive and one negative comment. This segment was discontinued after the episode "HEADS UP!". In the eighth episode of their web show, Superfruit began making weekly song suggestions. They entitled this segment 'Hey everyone! Here's a good song! *Don't listen to anything else today', which has adapted through each episode.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_notable_works">Other notable works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superfruit_(web_show)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other notable works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>As Superfruit, Grassi and Hoying appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grace_Helbig_Show" title="The Grace Helbig Show">The Grace Helbig Show</a></i> on an episode airing June 7, 2015, on which the duo perform various songs written for the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
