<p><i><b>Sweet Girl</b></i> is the sixth mini-album released by <a href="/wiki/B1A4" title="B1A4">B1A4</a> under WM Entertainment. The album was released on August 10, 2015 by WM Entertainment and distributed by <a href="/wiki/LOEN_Entertainment" title="LOEN Entertainment">LOEN Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LOEN_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-LOEN-1">[a]</a></sup> The title song of the album, "Sweet Girl", along with the other songs, was produced by leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jinyoung_(singer)" title="Jinyoung (singer)">Jinyoung</a>. CNU also produced one song in the album called "Love is Magic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[1]</a></sup> B1A4 won a trophy with "Sweet Girl" on the August 19 episode of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MBC_Every1" title="MBC Every1">MBC Every1</a> <a href="/wiki/Show_Champion" title="Show Champion">Show Champion</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup> and earned the title of the tenth most watched music video in America for August by <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/B1A4" title="B1A4">B1A4</a> posted five red-tinted images on Facebook and Twitter of the individual members showing off a new mature B1A4. On July 31, 2015, B1A4 posted a teaser video of all five songs on the album and a music video teaser on August 4, 2015. On August 6, they held a Guerilla Concert which was broadcast live via <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver</a> V app where they debuted three songs from their new album including "Sweet Girl".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup> The official music video for "Sweet Girl" was released August 10, 2015 alongside their album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup></p>
