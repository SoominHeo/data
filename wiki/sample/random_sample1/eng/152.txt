<p>Thirteen athletes from <b><a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a></b> competed in six sports at the <b><a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Summer Olympics</a></b>. France won the fourth-most <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medals</a> with 5 and the fourth-most total medals with 11. Cycling was the sport in which the French competitors had the most success, as they completely dominated the field. The French team had 27 entries in 18 events, winning 11 medals.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cycling">Cycling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=France_at_the_1896_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cycling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cycling_at_the_1896_Summer_Olympics" title="Cycling at the 1896 Summer Olympics">Cycling at the 1896 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Masson_flameng.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="290" data-file-width="430" height="270" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Masson_flameng.jpg/400px-Masson_flameng.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/12/Masson_flameng.jpg 1.5x" width="400"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Masson_flameng.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Leon Flameng and Paul Masson</div>

<p>France dominated the <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a> events, taking 4 of the 6 <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medals</a>. Three were won by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Masson_(cyclist)" title="Paul Masson (cyclist)">Paul Masson</a> who won every event he entered, with <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Flameng" title="Léon Flameng">Léon Flameng</a> adding the fourth as well as a silver and a bronze. One of the two won each event that a Frenchman contested; the two gold medals won by other countries were in competitions that Masson and Flameng did not enter.</p>

<p>The French fencers were held in high regard prior to the Games; Gravelotte and Callot validated those expectations in the amateur foil competition. Each went undefeated in their pool, advancing to face each other in the final. Gravelotte won the first-to-3 bout. Surprisingly, however, Perronet lost to the <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Leonidas_Pyrgos" title="Leonidas Pyrgos">Leonidas Pyrgos</a> in the only match of the masters competition.</p>
