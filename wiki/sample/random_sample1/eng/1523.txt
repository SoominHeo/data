<p><b>The Boom</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_band" title="Rock band">rock band</a>. Its members are <a href="/wiki/Kazufumi_Miyazawa" title="Kazufumi Miyazawa">Kazufumi Miyazawa</a> (vocals), Kobayashi Takashi (<a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>), Yamakawa Hiromasa (<a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a>), and Tochigi Takao (<a href="/wiki/Drum_kit" title="Drum kit">drums</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Boom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The four musicians, friends from <a href="/wiki/Yamanashi_Prefecture" title="Yamanashi Prefecture">Yamanashi Prefecture</a>, formed the band in 1986 as a <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hokoten&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hokoten (page does not exist)">hokoten</a></i> street band in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. They soon began getting club gigs and a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>, with whom they released their first album, <i>A Peacetime Boom</i>, in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vocalist <a href="/wiki/Kazufumi_Miyazawa" title="Kazufumi Miyazawa">Kazufumi Miyazawa</a> who is the <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a> and lyricist for The Boom was inspired by the music of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>. A single <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD" title="CD">CD</a> "<a href="/wiki/Shima_Uta_(The_Boom_song)" title="Shima Uta (The Boom song)">Shima Uta</a>" was released in 1993 with high sales, which reached approximately 1.5 million and brought fame to the band, around the world as well as at home.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On December 31, 1993, The Boom participated in <i><a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dhaku_Uta_Gassen" title="Kōhaku Uta Gassen">Kōhaku Uta Gassen</a></i>, an exclusive show usually reserved for elite musicians.</p>
<p>After they released "Shima-uta," the group went on to perform "Kaze-ni Naritai," "Kaero-kana," and "Tsukisaemo-nemuruyoru," but Miyazawa wanted to write more Okinawa songs, so an album comprised purely of Okinawa songs was released.</p>
<p>Miyazawa released several solo albums and composed songs for other musicians, including <a href="/wiki/Akiko_Yano" title="Akiko Yano">Akiko Yano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ky%C5%8Dko_Koizumi" title="Kyōko Koizumi">Kyōko Koizumi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rimi_Natsukawa" title="Rimi Natsukawa">Rimi Natsukawa</a>. He also started a new band named <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ganga_Zumba_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ganga Zumba (band) (page does not exist)">Ganga Zumba</a> who released their first album <i>Um</i> in April 2007.</p>
