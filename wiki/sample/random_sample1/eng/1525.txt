<p><i><b>The Story Begins</b></i> is the debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> by <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/Twice_(band)" title="Twice (band)">Twice</a>. The album was released digitally and physically on October 20, 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melon1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Melon1-1">[1]</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/JYP_Entertainment" title="JYP Entertainment">JYP Entertainment</a> and distributed by <a href="/wiki/KT_Music" title="KT Music">KT Music</a>. The album contains six tracks including the lead single, "Like Ooh Ahh", which was composed by <a href="/wiki/Black_Eyed_Pilseung" title="Black Eyed Pilseung">Black Eyed Pilseung</a> and is a blend of several different genres.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_release">Background and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Story_Begins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On October 7, 2015, JYP Entertainment launched the band's official website and announced via <a href="/wiki/Social_networking_service" title="Social networking service">SNS</a> that the group would debut with the mini-album <i>The Story Begins</i> and title-track "Like OOH-AHH". The track is described as a 'color pop' dance track with elements of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip-hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">R&amp;B</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The composition team included <a href="/wiki/Black_Eyed_Pilseung" title="Black Eyed Pilseung">Black Eyed Pilseung</a>, known for composing successful releases such as <a href="/wiki/Miss_A" title="Miss A">Miss A</a>'s "Only You."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Teasers featuring each of the members for their music video were released from October 12 to 14, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On October 20, the song's music video were released online and through the Naver V App.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within five months after its release, the music video for "Like OOH-AHH" hit 50 million views and replaced <a href="/wiki/2NE1" title="2NE1">2NE1</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Fire_(2NE1_song)" title="Fire (2NE1 song)">Fire</a>" as the most-viewed debut music video for any K-pop group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It went on to hit 100 million views on November 11, 2016, making Twice the fourth K-pop girl group to reach this milestone, as well as the first debut music video to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Story_Begins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Twice held a live showcase on October 20, where they performed "Like OOH-AHH" along with dance tracks "Must Be Crazy" and "Do It Again."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group started promoting their title track "Like Ooh Ahh" with the b-side "Do It Again" on music shows on October 22. They first performed the songs on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> followed by performances on <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Show!_Music_Core" title="Show! Music Core">Show! Music Core</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Story_Begins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Story Begins</i> debuted at number four on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a> for the week ending October 24, 2015 and peaked at number three two weeks later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaon46_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaon46-17">[17]</a></sup> As of April 2016, the album had sold 70,647 copies in South Korea<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and 12,141+ copies in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_Impacts_and_Effects">Future Impacts and Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Story_Begins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Future Impacts and Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 23, 2016, Twice released their two official colours, namely Apricot (<a href="/wiki/Pantone" title="Pantone">Pantone</a> 712 C) and Neon Magenta (Pantone 812 C), which signify the group and their <a href="/wiki/Fan_club" title="Fan club">fan club</a> called "Once", which was also based on the costumes used on Like Ooh Ahh MV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On October 19, Twice revealed their official light stick adorned with their official colours dubbed "Candy Bong", with <i>bong</i> meaning stick in Korean, which is inspired by the song "Candy Boy" from their first mini album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
