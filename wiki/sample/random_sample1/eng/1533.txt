<p><b>TOP 09</b> (name derived from <i>Tradice Odpovědnost Prosperita</i>, meaning "Tradition Responsibility Prosperity"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lansford2015_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lansford2015-5">[5]</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_the_Czech_Republic" title="List of political parties in the Czech Republic">political party</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, led by the former Czech Finance Minister <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_Kalousek" title="Miroslav Kalousek">Miroslav Kalousek</a>. TOP 09 holds 26 seats in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_the_Czech_Republic" title="Chamber of Deputies of the Czech Republic">Chamber of Deputies</a> and has four <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEPs</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TOP_09&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The party was founded on 11 June 2009 by <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_Kalousek" title="Miroslav Kalousek">Miroslav Kalousek</a> who left the <a href="/wiki/Christian_and_Democratic_Union_%E2%80%93_Czechoslovak_People%27s_Party" title="Christian and Democratic Union – Czechoslovak People's Party">Christian and Democratic Union – Czechoslovak People's Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Karel_Schwarzenberg" title="Karel Schwarzenberg">Karel Schwarzenberg</a>, who had previously served as Minister of Foreign Affairs in the <a href="/wiki/Mirek_Topol%C3%A1nek%27s_Second_Cabinet" title="Mirek Topolánek's Second Cabinet">second Topolánek cabinet</a> from January 2007 to March 2009, having been nominated by the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_(Czech_Republic)" title="Green Party (Czech Republic)">Green Party</a> for the post, and who had been elected to the Senate in 2004 as nominee of the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Union_%E2%80%93_Democratic_Union" title="Freedom Union – Democratic Union">Freedom Union – Democratic Union</a> (US-DEU) and <a href="/wiki/Civic_Democratic_Alliance" title="Civic Democratic Alliance">Civic Democratic Alliance</a> (ODA) parties, became the party's first leader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Czech_legislative_election,_2010" title="Czech legislative election, 2010">2010 parliament elections</a> on 28–29 May 2010, TOP 09 received 16.7% of the vote and 41 seats, becoming the third largest party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The party joined the new coalition government, the <a href="/wiki/Petr_Ne%C4%8Das%27_Cabinet" title="Petr Nečas' Cabinet">Nečas cabinet</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Civic_Democratic_Party_(Czech_Republic)" title="Civic Democratic Party (Czech Republic)">Civic Democratic Party</a> (ODS) and <a href="/wiki/Public_Affairs_(political_party)" title="Public Affairs (political party)">Public Affairs</a> (VV).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berglund2013_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Berglund2013-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2010 TOP09 applied to join the <a href="/wiki/European_People%27s_Party" title="European People's Party">European People's Party</a>. <a href="/wiki/Karel_Schwarzenberg" title="Karel Schwarzenberg">Karel Schwarzenberg</a> has already officially participated in two EPP summits (15 September<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and 16 December 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>). On 10 February 2011 TOP 09 has officially been granted permission to join the EPP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Czech_legislative_election,_2013" title="Czech legislative election, 2013">2013 legislative election</a> on 25–26 October 2013, TOP 09 won 12% of the vote and 26 seats. The party entered opposition to the <a href="/wiki/Bohuslav_Sobotka%27s_Cabinet" title="Bohuslav Sobotka's Cabinet">Sobotka cabinet</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2014_(Czech_Republic)" title="European Parliament election, 2014 (Czech Republic)">2014 European elections</a> on 24 and 25 May 2014, TOP 09 receached second place nationally with 15.95% of the vote, electing 4 <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEPs</a>.</p>
<p>In March 2016, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karel_Ture%C4%8Dek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karel Tureček (page does not exist)">Karel Tureček</a> left the party and joined ANO 2011 which left TOP 09 with 25 MPs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In May 2016, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pavol_Luk%C5%A1a&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pavol Lukša (page does not exist)">Pavol Lukša</a>, one of founders of TOP 09, left the party and established the new party <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Good_Choice&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Good Choice (page does not exist)">Good Choice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>TOP 09 was heavily defeated in <a href="/wiki/Czech_regional_elections,_2016" title="Czech regional elections, 2016">2016 regional elections</a>. The party has gained only 19 seat and 3.4% of votes. Miroslav Kalousek then considered resignation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology">Ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TOP_09&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>TOP 09 has been noted for its support of <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_conservatism" title="Fiscal conservatism">fiscal conservatism</a> and is considered pro-<a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MarekBaun2010_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-MarekBaun2010-17">[17]</a></sup> being strongly in favour of <a href="/wiki/European_integration" title="European integration">European integration</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Viola2015_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Viola2015-18">[18]</a></sup> However it has been noted that some policy stances are <a href="/wiki/National_conservatism" title="National conservatism">national-conservative</a> rather than strictly <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BakkePeters2011_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-BakkePeters2011-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
