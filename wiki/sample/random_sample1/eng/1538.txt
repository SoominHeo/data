<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsunami_(Southern_All_Stars_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was the first number-one single for Southern All Stars since the 1996 single <i>Ai no Kotodama</i> on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> weekly charts. The band used the style of <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a> on the previous single "Yellow Man," which was released in March 1999. However, it only managed to reach the number-ten position on Oricon charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Therefore, they returned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_pop" title="Japanese pop">Japanese pop</a>. The song sold over 654,000 copies in the first week and debuted at number-one, beating out <a href="/wiki/Morning_Musume" title="Morning Musume">Morning Musume</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Koi_no_Dance_Site" title="Koi no Dance Site">Koi no Dance Site</a>" on the Oricon weekly charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The song once spent two weeks at the number-one position, lost one week to <a href="/wiki/B%27z" title="B'z">B'z</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Kon%27ya_Tsuki_no_Mieru_Oka_ni" title="Kon'ya Tsuki no Mieru Oka ni">Kon'ya Tsuki no Mieru Oka ni</a>", and reached the number-one position again for three weeks. It sold over 2.9 million copies and became the best selling single for the band. In June 2005, it became the third best-selling single on the Oricon chart, surpassing the sales of "<a href="/wiki/Dango_3_Kyodai" title="Dango 3 Kyodai">Dango 3 Kyodai</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song was included in their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_hits" title="Greatest hits">greatest hits</a> album <i>Ballad 3: The Album Of Love</i>, released on November 22, 2000. The album debuted at the number-one position on the Oricon weekly charts, selling over 1.36 million copies within only one week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song resurrected the band. The song won the "Song of The Year" award at the 14th <a href="/wiki/Japan_Gold_Disc_Award" title="Japan Gold Disc Award">Japan Gold Disc Award</a>. It also won the grand prix award at the <a href="/wiki/42nd_Japan_Record_Awards" title="42nd Japan Record Awards">42nd Japan Record Awards</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_versions">Cover versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsunami_(Southern_All_Stars_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cover versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A cover version was recorded and released by the American band <a href="/wiki/Allister" title="Allister">Allister</a> in their 2006 album <i><a href="/wiki/Guilty_Pleasures_(EP)" title="Guilty Pleasures (EP)">Guilty Pleasures</a></i>. An instrumental cover version was made by <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a>'s former guitarist <a href="/wiki/Marty_Friedman" title="Marty Friedman">Marty Friedman</a>, and featured on his 2009 solo album <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Jukebox" title="Tokyo Jukebox">Tokyo Jukebox</a>. <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Gibson" title="Debbie Gibson">Debbie Gibson</a> recorded an English-language cover of the song in her 2010 Japan-only release <i><a href="/wiki/Ms._Vocalist" title="Ms. Vocalist">Ms. Vocalist</a></i>.</p>
