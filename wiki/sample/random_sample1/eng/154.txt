<p>The <b>1900 Summer Olympics</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Les Jeux olympiques d'été de 1900</i></span>), today officially known as the <b>Games of the II Olympiad</b>, was an <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">international</a> <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> which was celebrated in 1900 in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, France, and the last Summer Olympics to be held in the 19th century. No opening or closing ceremonies were held; competitions began on May 14 and ended on October 28. The Games were held as part of the <a href="/wiki/Exposition_Universelle_(1900)" title="Exposition Universelle (1900)">1900 World's Fair</a>. In total, 997 competitors took part in 19 different sports. Women took part in the games for the first time and sailor <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9l%C3%A8ne_de_Pourtal%C3%A8s" title="Hélène de Pourtalès">Hélène de Pourtalès</a> became the first female Olympic champion. The decision to hold competitions on a Sunday brought protests from many American athletes, who travelled as representatives of their colleges and were expected to withdraw rather than compete on their religious day of rest.</p>
<p>At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sorbonne_(building)" title="Sorbonne (building)">Sorbonne</a> conference of 1894, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_de_Coubertin" title="Pierre de Coubertin">Pierre de Coubertin</a> proposed that the Olympic Games should take place in 1900 in Paris. The delegates to the conference were unwilling to wait six years and lobbied to hold the first games in 1896. A decision was made to hold the <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">first Olympic Games</a> in 1896 in Athens and that Paris would host the second celebration.</p>
<p>Most of the winners in 1900 did not receive medals, but were given cups or trophies. Professionals competed in fencing and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albert_Robert_Ayat" title="Albert Robert Ayat">Albert Robert Ayat</a> (France), who won the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89p%C3%A9e" title="Épée">épée</a> for amateurs and masters, was awarded a prize of 3000 <a href="/wiki/French_franc" title="French franc">francs</a>.</p>
<p>Some unusual events were contested for the only time in the history of the Games, including <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">automobile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_racing" title="Motorcycle racing">motorcycle racing</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joh_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-joh-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hot_air_ballooning" title="Hot air ballooning">ballooning</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joh13_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-joh13-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joh32_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-joh32-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Croquet" title="Croquet">croquet</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joh33_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-joh33-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Basque_pelota" title="Basque pelota">Basque pelota</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joh52_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-joh52-6">[6]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_200_metre_obstacle_event" title="Swimming at the 1900 Summer Olympics – Men's 200 metre obstacle event">200m swimming obstacle race and underwater swimming</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joh77_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-joh77-7">[7]</a></sup> This was also the only Olympic Games in history to use live animals (pigeons) as targets during the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 1900 Summer Olympics">shooting event</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1900 Games were held as part of the <a href="/wiki/Exposition_Universelle_(1900)" title="Exposition Universelle (1900)">1900 Exposition Universelle</a>. The Baron de Coubertin believed that this would help public awareness of the Olympics and submitted elaborate plans to rebuild the ancient site of <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Greece" title="Olympia, Greece">Olympia</a>, complete with statues, temples, stadia and gymnasia. The director of the Exposition Universelle, Alfred Picard, thought holding an ancient sport event at the Exposition Universelle was an "absurd anachronism".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Googlebooks_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Googlebooks-9">[9]</a></sup> After thanking de Coubertin for his plans, Picard filed them away and nothing more came of it.</p>
<p>A committee was formed for the organization of the Games, consisting of some of the more able sports administrators of the day and a provisional program was drawn up. Sports to be included at the games were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Track_and_field_athletics" title="Track and field athletics">track and field athletics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" title="Swimming (sport)">swimming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wrestling" title="Wrestling">wrestling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artistic_gymnastics" title="Artistic gymnastics">gymnastics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savate" title="Savate">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">British boxing</a>, river and ocean <a href="/wiki/Yacht_racing" title="Yacht racing">yacht racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lifesaving" title="Lifesaving">lifesaving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archery_at_the_Summer_Olympics" title="Archery at the Summer Olympics">archery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olympic_weightlifting" title="Olympic weightlifting">weightlifting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rowing_(sport)" title="Rowing (sport)">rowing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diving" title="Diving">diving</a> and <a href="/wiki/Water_polo" title="Water polo">water polo</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> sports associations announced a desire to compete, as did a number of powerful American universities and sports clubs. Competitors from <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and Australia also confirmed their intentions to travel to Paris.</p>

<p>On November 9, 1898, the <a href="/wiki/Union_des_Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9s_Fran%C3%A7aises_de_Sports_Athl%C3%A9tiques" title="Union des Sociétés Françaises de Sports Athlétiques">Union des Sociétés Françaises de Sports Athlétiques</a> ("Union of the French Societies for Athletic Sports" or USFSA) put out an announcement that it would have sole right to any organised sport held during the World's Fair. It was an empty threat but <a href="/wiki/Viscount" title="Viscount">Viscount</a> Charles de La Rochefoucauld, the nominated head of the organizing committee, stepped down rather than be embroiled in the political battle. The Baron de Coubertin, who was also secretary-general of the USFSA, was urged to withdraw from active involvement in the running of the Games and did so, only to comment later, "I surrendered - and was incorrect in doing so."</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOC" title="IOC">IOC</a> ceded control of the Games to a new committee which was to oversee every sporting activity connected to the 1900 Exposition Universelle. Alfred Picard appointed Daniel Mérillon, the head of the French Shooting Association, as president of this organization in February 1899. Mérillon proceeded to publish an entirely different schedule of events, with the result that many of those that had made plans to compete in concordance with the original program withdrew, and refused to deal with the new committee.</p>
<p>Between May and October 1900, the new organizing committee held an enormous number of sporting activities alongside the Paris Exposition. The sporting events rarely used the term of "Olympic". Indeed, the term "Olympic Games" was replaced by "<i>Concours internationaux d'exercices physiques et de sport</i>" ("International physical exercises and sports" in English) in the official report of the sporting events of the 1900 Exposition Universelle. The press reported competitions variously as "International Championships", "International Games", "Paris Championships", "World Championships" and "Grand Prix of the Paris Exposition".</p>
<p>De Coubertin commented later to friends: "It's a miracle that the Olympic Movement survived that celebration".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Highlights">Highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>These Olympic Games were the first organised under the IOC Presidency of <a href="/wiki/Pierre_de_Coubertin" title="Pierre de Coubertin">Pierre de Coubertin</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alvin_Kraenzlein" title="Alvin Kraenzlein">Alvin Kraenzlein</a> (United States) won the <a href="/wiki/60_metres" title="60 metres">60 metres</a> (he was one of two people to ever win this event at the Olympic Games as it was withdrawn from Olympic competition after the <a href="/wiki/1904_Summer_Olympics" title="1904 Summer Olympics">1904 Olympics</a>), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/110_metre_hurdles" title="110 metre hurdles">110 metre hurdles</a>, the 200 metre <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurdles" title="Hurdles">hurdles</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Long_jump" title="Long jump">long jump</a> events; as of 2005, these four individual gold medals are still a record for a track and field athlete. For his victory in the long jump, he was allegedly punched in the face by his rival <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meyer_Prinstein" title="Meyer Prinstein">Meyer Prinstein</a>, who was prevented from competing in the final by officials of <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a> because it was scheduled for a Sunday.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9l%C3%A8ne_de_Pourtal%C3%A8s" title="Hélène de Pourtalès">Hélène de Pourtalès</a> became the first female Olympic champion as part of the winning team in the <a href="/wiki/Sailing_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_1_to_2_ton" title="Sailing at the 1900 Summer Olympics – 1 to 2 ton">1-2 ton sailing</a> event. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlotte_Cooper_(tennis_player)" title="Charlotte Cooper (tennis player)">Charlotte Cooper</a> was the first woman to win an individual Olympic event after winning the women's singles <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> competition. She later went on to win the <a href="/wiki/Types_of_tennis_match" title="Types of tennis match">mixed doubles</a> tournament.</li>
<li>Three <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a> runners from the United States contested the result saying the French runners who got first and second places took a short cut, and the proof was they were the only contestants not spattered with mud.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Coxed_pair" title="Coxed pair">coxed pairs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eight_(rowing)" title="Eight (rowing)">eights</a> events in <a href="/wiki/Rowing_(sport)" title="Rowing (sport)">rowing</a>, crews replaced adult <a href="/wiki/Coxswain_(rowing)" title="Coxswain (rowing)">coxswain</a> with children. The identities and ages of these boys were not recorded but they are believed to have been amongst the youngest of all Olympic competitors.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>85 events<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> in 20 disciplines, comprising 19 sports, were part of the Olympic program in Paris. Weightlifting and wrestling had been dropped since the <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Summer Olympics</a>, while 13 new sports were added. Swimming and water polo were considered to be two disciplines within a single sport of <i>aquatics</i> in the Olympic context. The number of events in each discipline is noted in parentheses.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport-by-sport_overview">Sport-by-sport overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sport-by-sport overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The standard of competition at the Games was variable. Despite a poor quality track, a strong contingent of top-class American collegiate athletes ensured the track and field competitions were of the highest quality. The tennis gold medalists were all former <a href="/wiki/The_Championships,_Wimbledon" title="The Championships, Wimbledon">Wimbledon</a> champions, swimming and fencing events were of a good standard and even polo, a minority sport for the social elite, was well represented by some of the best players in the game. Other sports were noticeably weak in both quality and depth. Only athletics, swimming and fencing had competitors from more than ten nations.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Archery">Archery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Archery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Archery_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Archery at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Archery at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The history of the archery competition at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1900_Olympics" title="1900 Olympics">1900 Olympics</a> is extremely confusing. The International Olympic Committee currently list six events as having full Olympic status but a case could be made that as many as eight other events as equally as deserving to be considered as part of official Olympic history. About 150 archers competed in these six official events but as many as 5,000 were involved in archery competition in conjunction with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1900_World%27s_Fair" title="1900 World's Fair">1900 World's Fair</a>. <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Van_Innis" title="Hubert Van Innis">Hubert Van Innis</a> took two gold medals and one silver and would add to his tally twenty years later in <a href="/wiki/1920_Summer_Olympics" title="1920 Summer Olympics">Antwerp</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> events were held at the home of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RCF_Paris" title="RCF Paris">Racing Club de France</a> at the Croix-Catelan stadium in <a href="/wiki/Bois_de_Boulogne" title="Bois de Boulogne">Bois de Boulogne</a>. No track was laid and races took place on an uneven field of grass littered with trees. Additional events were held for professionals and a series of handicap races also took place. These are not considered official Olympic events.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_sprints">The sprints</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The sprints">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the seven events contested over 400 metres or less, the United States took 13 out of a possible 21 medals. Athletes from <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton University</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a> all won gold medals. Indeed, two would-be dentists from the University of Pennsylvania were amongst the stars of the Games. <a href="/wiki/Alvin_Kraenzlein" title="Alvin Kraenzlein">Alvin Kraenzlein</a> won 4 individual gold medals, a feat that has never repeated, while <a href="/wiki/Walter_Tewksbury" title="Walter Tewksbury">Walter Tewksbury</a> took five medals including two golds. The hurdles in the 400 m hurdle race were 30-foot (9.1 m)-long telegraph poles arranged on the track and the race, uniquely in Olympic competition, had a water jump on the final straight.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_and_long_distance_races">Middle and long distance races</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Middle and long distance races">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>United States dominance in sprinting was matched in the longer track races by United Kingdom. Only <a href="/wiki/George_Orton" title="George Orton">George Orton</a>, who won Canada's first Olympic title in the shorter of the two <a href="/wiki/Steeplechase_(athletics)" title="Steeplechase (athletics)">steeplechases</a>, ruined a perfect record for the British. Orton won his title less than an hour after placing third in the 400 metre hurdles.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Marathon">The Marathon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The Marathon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The most contentious of all the events in these Games began and ended in the Bois de Boulogne. Intended to follow the track of the <a href="/wiki/Thiers_wall" title="Thiers wall">old city wall</a>, the course was poorly marked out and runners often got lost and had to double back on themselves before continuing. On some parts of the course, runners had to contend with distractions from cars, bicycles, pedestrians and animals. <a href="/wiki/Arthur_L._Newton" title="Arthur L. Newton">Arthur Newton</a> of the United States finished fifth but stated he had not been passed by any other runner during the race. Another American, <a href="/wiki/Dick_Grant" title="Dick Grant">Richard Grant</a>, claimed he was run down by a cyclist as he made ground on the leaders. French honour seemed to have been satisfied when <a href="/wiki/Michel_Th%C3%A9ato" title="Michel Théato">Michel Théato</a> crossed the finish line and a military band struck up <a href="/wiki/La_Marseillaise" title="La Marseillaise">La Marseillaise</a>. However, modern research has revealed that Théato was born in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> and maintained <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxembourgian" title="Luxembourgian">Luxembourgian</a> citizenship throughout his life.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Field_events">Field events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Field events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Discus_throw" title="Discus throw">discus thrower</a> <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Bauer_(athlete)" title="Rudolf Bauer (athlete)">Rudolf Bauer</a> was only non-American crowned as Olympic Champion. American domination was even greater in the field events than the track events with outstanding performances coming from <a href="/wiki/Ray_Ewry" title="Ray Ewry">Ray Ewry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irving_Baxter" title="Irving Baxter">Irving Baxter</a>. Ewry started his Olympic career with a sweep of the three standing jumps whilst Baxter finished second to Ewry three times and won both the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_high_jump" title="Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics – Men's high jump">regular high jump</a> and <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_pole_vault" title="Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics – Men's pole vault">pole vault</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meyer_Prinstein" title="Meyer Prinstein">Meyer Prinstein</a> became the first Jewish Olympic gold medalist in the <a href="/wiki/Triple_jump" title="Triple jump">triple jump</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basque_pelota">Basque pelota</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Basque pelota">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Basque_pelota_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Basque pelota at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Basque pelota at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Basque_pelota#Chistera_.28basket.29" title="Basque pelota">chistera</a> form of the game was played at this, the sport's only appearance at full Olympic level. Two pairs entered and the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> partnerships of Amezola and Villota became their nations' first Olympic champions. The <a href="/wiki/Basque_pelota#Hand-pelota" title="Basque pelota">mano</a> form of the game and a chistera tournament for professional players were contested unofficially.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cricket_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Cricket at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Cricket at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>After the withdrawal of teams from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and Belgium, only two teams played in the cricket tournament. A team made up of players from the Albion Cricket Club and the Standard Athletic Club, two Paris clubs consisting almost exclusively of British expatriates, played a touring team from the southwest of England. The Devon and Somerset Wanderers were no more than a team of competent club cricketers (made up from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blundells_School" title="Blundells School">Blundells School</a> old boys and members of Castle Cary Cricket Club), and only <a href="/wiki/Montagu_Toller" title="Montagu Toller">Montagu Toller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Bowerman" title="Alfred Bowerman">Alfred Bowerman</a> were deemed good enough to play at county level for Somerset. The game was played before a small crowd at the <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A9lodrome_de_Vincennes" title="Vélodrome de Vincennes">Vélodrome de Vincennes</a>. An emphatic second innings bowling performance from Toller captured victory for the visitors as time appeared to be running out for them. If the French had held out for five more minutes the game would have declared a draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Knowledge of the game would have been lost but for the forethought of <a href="/wiki/John_Symes" title="John Symes">John Symes</a>, a member of the victorious team, who kept a scorecard in his own writing.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Croquet">Croquet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Croquet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Croquet_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Croquet at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Croquet at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The croquet tournament was notable as it marked the first appearance of women at Olympic level. Madame <a href="/wiki/Despr%C3%A8s" title="Desprès">Desprès</a>, Madame <a href="/wiki/Jeanne_Filleul-Brohy" title="Jeanne Filleul-Brohy">Filleul-Brohy</a> and Mademoiselle <a href="/wiki/Marie_Ohier" title="Marie Ohier">Ohier</a> were eliminated in the first round of competition. All players were French. A single paying spectator attended the tournament, an elderly English gentleman who travelled from <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a> for the early stages. An unofficial two-ball handicap competition was also held.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cycling">Cycling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Cycling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cycling_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Cycling at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Cycling at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The home nation won five out of the six medals available. A number of unofficial events were held for both amateurs and professionals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Equestrian">Equestrian</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Equestrian">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Equestrian_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Equestrian at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Equestrian at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Equestrian sport made its debut at the Olympic Games with three events being held. The Italian rider <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giovanni_Giorgio_Trissino" title="Giovanni Giorgio Trissino">Giovanni Giorgio Trissino</a> won a gold and a silver. He narrowly missed making Olympic history by winning two medals in the same event. Competing with two different horses in the high jump, he jointly won the gold medal and finished in 4th place on his second horse.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fencing">Fencing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Fencing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Fencing at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Fencing at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Nineteen nations were represented in the fencing competition, which was held in a field near the cutlery exhibit at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1900_World%27s_Fair" title="1900 World's Fair">1900 World's Fair</a>. French fencers dominated the proceedings but both Cuba and Italy also took titles. The early rounds of the foil competitions were judged on style rather than the actual result of the contest. This meant that some fencers were eliminated without losing a contest whilst others were defeated and still progressed to the next rounds.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Football at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The first football (soccer) champions at the Olympics were the London amateurs of <a href="/wiki/Upton_Park_F.C." title="Upton Park F.C.">Upton Park F.C.</a> A crowd of around 500 spectators saw them defeat their French rivals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Golf">Golf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Golf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Golf_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Golf at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Golf at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Margaret_Ives_Abbott" title="Margaret Ives Abbott">Margaret Ives Abbott</a>, a student of art from Chicago, played in and won a nine-hole golf tournament on an October Tuesday in Paris. She died in 1955 without being aware that the tournament was part of the Olympic Games and she had become America's first ever female Olympic champion.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gymnastics">Gymnastics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Gymnastics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Gymnastics at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Gymnastics at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>135 gymnasts took part in a competition that involved elements from <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> and <a href="/wiki/Powerlifting" title="Powerlifting">weightlifting</a> as well as gymnastic disciplines</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Polo">Polo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Polo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Polo_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Polo at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Polo at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Eight separate tournaments were held in 1900 as part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1900_World%27s_Fair" title="1900 World's Fair">1900 World's Fair</a>. Only the Grand Prix Internationale de l'Exposition is counted as an official medal event. Entries were from clubs rather than countries and the winning Foxhunters club comprised English, Irish and American players.</p>
<p>Mexico won its first medal in this sport. Bronze by Guillermo Hayden Wright, and three brothers from Mexico; Eustaquio de Escandón y Barron, Pablo de Escandón y Barron and Manuel de Escandón y Barron, Marquez de Villavieja</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rowing">Rowing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Rowing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rowing_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Rowing at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Rowing at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Competitions were held on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Seine" title="River Seine">River Seine</a>. The coxed fours descended into farce when officials changed the qualifying criteria for the final several times. The first final was held without any of the original qualifiers, who had withdrawn as a protest against the decision to run six boats on a course laid out for only four. The officials then decided to run another "final" for the boycotting crews. Both events are considered official Olympic competitions. In a number of events crews saw the advantage of having ultra-lightweight coxswain and recruited local boys for the period of the Games. Most of these remain a mystery but some could have been under ten years old</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Rugby union at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Rugby union at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Three teams competed in the Rugby tournament. A French representative team defeated a team from the German city of <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moseley_Wanderers" title="Moseley Wanderers">Moseley Wanderers</a> from England. The Moseley team had played a full game of rugby in England the day before they made the journey to Paris. They arrived in the morning, played the match in the afternoon and were back in their home country by the next morning. The proposed game between the British and German sides was cancelled and both are credited as silver medallists. The Franco-Haitian centre <a href="/wiki/Constantin_Henriquez" title="Constantin Henriquez">Constantin Henriquez</a> become the first black gold medallist.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sailing">Sailing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Sailing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sailing_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Sailing at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Sailing at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The 1900 sailing regatta differs from every other Olympic regatta in a number of ways. In most classes there were two distinct "finals", boats were assigned time handicaps according to their weight within each class and cash prizes were handed out to the winner of each race. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOC" title="IOC">IOC</a> initially recognized the winner of the first race in each class as Olympic champion except in the case of the 10-20 ton class, which was decided on aggregate time over three races. However currently the participants of both first and second races in 3 classes (0-0.5t, 1-2t and 2-3t) are present in the <a class="external text" href="http://www.olympic.org/content/results-and-medalists/gamesandsportsummary/?sport=31594&amp;games=1900%2f1" rel="nofollow">IOC database</a> as medalists, so the second races in these 3 classes had been recognized by the IOC, and so according to IOC for each of these 3 events two gold, two silver and two bronze medals were retrospectively awarded. Races were held at both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meulan" title="Meulan">Meulan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Le_Havre" title="Le Havre">Le Havre</a> and medals shared amongst five nations. France and Great Britain were the most successful of the countries involved. A number of people named as members of medal-winning crews by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOC" title="IOC">IOC</a> have been proved not to have competed; others have their participation seriously questioned by historical research.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shooting">Shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Shooting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Shooting at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Switzerland's <a href="/wiki/Konrad_St%C3%A4heli" title="Konrad Stäheli">Konrad Stäheli</a> was the outstanding marksman of the Games, taking a trio of titles and leading his country to the top of the shooting medal table. The medals were shared between six different nations. There is a debate as to whether the live pigeon shooting event was a full Olympic event, Belgian Leon Lunden shot twenty-one birds on his way to the championship. Up to thirty unofficial shooting events were also held.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Swimming">Swimming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Swimming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Swimming at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Swimming at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>The muddied waters of the <a href="/wiki/Seine" title="Seine">Seine</a> hosted the swimming events in 1900. Run with the current, the races produced very fast times by the standards of the day. <a href="/wiki/John_Arthur_Jarvis" title="John Arthur Jarvis">John Arthur Jarvis</a> of Great Britain, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Lane" title="Frederick Lane">Frederick Lane</a> of Australia and the German <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Hoppenberg" title="Ernst Hoppenberg">Ernst Hoppenberg</a> each won two titles. Lane received a 50-pound bronze statue of a horse as a prize. A couple of unusual events were held. The obstacle race required both swimming underneath and climbing over rows of boats whilst Charles de Venville stayed submerged for over a minute to win the underwater swimming event.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tennis">Tennis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Tennis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tennis_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Tennis at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Tennis at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>A high quality men's tournament saw three past or future <a href="/wiki/The_Championships,_Wimbledon" title="The Championships, Wimbledon">Wimbledon</a> champions reach the semi-finals. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lawrence_Doherty" title="Lawrence Doherty">Lawrence Doherty</a> reached the final when older brother <a href="/wiki/Reginald_Doherty" title="Reginald Doherty">Reggie</a> stepped aside and let his sibling advance to the final. The two refused to play each other in what they considered a minor tournament. On the 11th of July a landmark was reached in the history of the Olympic Games. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlotte_Cooper_(tennis_player)" title="Charlotte Cooper (tennis player)">Charlotte Cooper</a>, already three times Wimbledon champion, took the singles championship to become the first individual female Olympic champion, also winning the mixed doubles event.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_polo">Water polo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Water polo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympic_status_of_sports_and_events">Olympic status of sports and events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Olympic status of sports and events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1900 games were not governed by a specific Olympic organizing committee, but instead held as an appendage to the <a href="/wiki/Exposition_Universelle_(1900)" title="Exposition Universelle (1900)">1900 World's Fair</a>. An enormous number of events were held, though many fall short of the standards later required for Olympic championship status. Decisions as to which Olympic events are termed "official" and which have "unofficial" or "demonstration" status are usually left to the Olympic organizing committees and/or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOC" title="IOC">IOC</a>. In the absence of any overall authority capable of making an official distinction of this kind, no decision as to the official status of any event was made at the time of the Games. A document from 1912 exists, listing results from the 1900 Games, but the reliability of this paper is questioned by Olympic historians. This document forms the basis of the results of the Paris games in the IOC database.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-status_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-status-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unofficial_sports">Unofficial sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Unofficial sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p><a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">Gold medals</a> were not given at the 1900 Games. A <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medal</a> was given for a first place and a <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze medal</a> was given for second. The <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> has retroactively assigned gold, silver, and bronze medals to competitors who earned 1st, 2nd, and 3rd-place finishes respectively in order to bring early Olympics in line with current awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
