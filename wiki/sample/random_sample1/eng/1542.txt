<p><b>TV Tokyo Corporation</b> (or <b>TX</b>) <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社テレビ東京</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kabushiki-gaisha Terebi Tōkyō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">TYO</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www2.tse.or.jp/tseHpFront/StockSearch.do?callJorEFlg=1&amp;method=&amp;topSearchStr=9413" rel="nofollow">9413</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Television_station" title="Television station">television station</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Roppongi" title="Roppongi">Roppongi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">Minato, Tokyo</a>, Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Also known as "<b>Teleto</b>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">テレ東</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Teretō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a blend of "<a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">terebi</a>" and "Tokyo", it is the key station of <a href="/wiki/TX_Network" title="TX Network">TX Network</a>. It is one of the major Tokyo television stations, particularly <a href="/wiki/List_of_anime_distributed_by_TV_Tokyo" title="List of anime distributed by TV Tokyo">specializing in anime</a>. The station is partly owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nihon_Keizai_Shimbun" title="Nihon Keizai Shimbun">Nihon Keizai Shimbun</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TV_Tokyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>TV Tokyo was established by the Japan Science Promotion Foundation in 1951 and started broadcasting on April 12, 1964.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Original phrasing was unclear. (July 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A <a href="/wiki/Limited_liability_company" title="Limited liability company">limited liability company</a>, Tokyo Channel 12 Production, was formed for the channel on July 1, 1968.</p>
<p>In October 1973, the name was shortened to Tokyo Channel 12 and it took over the broadcasting from the science foundation. A month later, it became a general purpose TV station along with Japan Educational Television (now <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">TV Asahi</a>).</p>
<p>In 1981, it was again renamed, this time to Television Tokyo Channel 12.</p>
<p>In 1983, TV Tokyo formed the Mega TON Network (now TXN) with <a href="/wiki/TV_Osaka" title="TV Osaka">TV Osaka</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aichi_Television_Broadcasting" title="Aichi Television Broadcasting">Aichi Television Broadcasting</a>. The company shifted its head offices from <a href="/wiki/Shiba_Park" title="Shiba Park">Shiba Park</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toranomon" title="Toranomon">Toranomon</a> in December 1985. On June 25, 2004, the company assumed its current English name of TV Tokyo Corporation. On November 7, 2016, TV Tokyo moved its headquarters to new building at <a href="/wiki/Sumitomo_Fudosan_Roppongi_Grand_Tower" title="Sumitomo Fudosan Roppongi Grand Tower">Sumitomo Fudosan Roppongi Grand Tower</a> from its old studios in Toranomon.</p>
