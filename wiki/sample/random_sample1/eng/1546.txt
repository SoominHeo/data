<p>"<b>That's What You Get</b>" is a song by American <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Paramore" title="Paramore">Paramore</a>. It is the second Australian single, third U.S. single and the fourth UK single from their second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Riot!" title="Riot!">Riot!</a></i>. The song was released on radio on March 22 in Australia<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and on March 25 in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United States, the song was released on May 5 as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> and physically on May 12. The song is featured in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_2" title="Rock Band 2">Rock Band 2</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rock_Band_2_Complete_Track_List_Revealed_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rock_Band_2_Complete_Track_List_Revealed-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song was certified Platinum in the United States on March 24, 2016, selling over 1,000,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-riaa-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=That%27s_What_You_Get&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stylistically, "That's What You Get" has been labeled as <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pop_punk" title="Pop punk">pop punk</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Power_pop" title="Power pop">power pop</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> as well as having influences from <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disco_music" title="Disco music">disco music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-7">[7]</a></sup> Jonathan Bradley from <i><a href="/wiki/Stylus_Magazine" title="Stylus Magazine">Stylus Magazine</a></i> described the song as containing a "relentless assault of sugar-sweet riffs and soaring choruses".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=That%27s_What_You_Get&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fraser McAlpine at <a href="/wiki/BBC_Online" title="BBC Online">BBC Online</a> gave the song a rating of 4 out of 5 stars, and stated "Paramore's sense of dynamics has always been strong, and the introduction to this song is a great example of that." McAlpine also praises the funk and disco influences during the verses, as well as the drumming style of the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=That%27s_What_You_Get&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video, directed by <a href="/wiki/Marcos_Siega" title="Marcos Siega">Marcos Siega</a>, was shot in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, on March 2 and March 3, 2008. <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a> released the official music video on March 24, 2008. The music video shows the band playing in a living room with clips of a relationship of two lovers (Aaron Holmes of Death in the Park and Jenna Galing, both from Richmond, VA) and a small gathering of the band's family and friends. The couple's relationship is shown to be on the rocks as the girl calls the boy to meet up but then pushes him away. They go throughout their day before the party spending time with the band members and try to be together. Cut scenes of Hayley Williams singing the song outside in front of the camera with her back to the friends and family are shown. At the party, the boyfriend is approached by another girl who flirts with him and holds him hand. The girlfriend becomes distraught but reunites in an embrace with her boyfriend as the party-goers all sit around a fire pit. The videos ends with the lovers kissing and taking a picture of themselves on a cellphone and all the people at the party are rushing out the living room, knocking over a couch, and leaving a record spinning.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=That%27s_What_You_Get&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The music video was shot just over a week after Paramore cancelled their European <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">tour</a> to work on "personal issues",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paramore_Cancel_European_Tour_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Paramore_Cancel_European_Tour-9">[9]</a></sup> amidst media <a href="/wiki/Speculation" title="Speculation">speculation</a> of the band breaking up. Hayley Williams explained that, given the fragile state of the band, they all thought it best if they kept the shoot low-key, surrounding themselves with their friends and family, keeping it simple.</p>
<p>Williams added "We had tons of friends there, and it really just felt like a hangout session. And Marcos was so cool about it. He said, 'Bring your friends.' We shot it in some of our friends' houses, and it just felt so real ... and I think it's the first time in a video you're gonna get to see who we really are."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paramore_Explain_Why_.27That.27s_What_You_Get.27_Video_Shoot_Was_Top_Secret_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Paramore_Explain_Why_.27That.27s_What_You_Get.27_Video_Shoot_Was_Top_Secret-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
