<p><i><b>The Black Parade</b></i> is the third studio album by American <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/My_Chemical_Romance" title="My Chemical Romance">My Chemical Romance</a>. Released on October 23, 2006 through <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise Records</a>, it was produced by <a href="/wiki/Rob_Cavallo" title="Rob Cavallo">Rob Cavallo</a>, known for having produced multiple albums for <a href="/wiki/Goo_Goo_Dolls" title="Goo Goo Dolls">Goo Goo Dolls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Rock_opera" title="Rock opera">rock opera</a> centering on a dying character with cancer known as "The Patient". The album tells the story of his apparent death, experiences in the afterlife, and subsequent reflections on his life.</p>
<p>Four <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a> were released from the album: "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_the_Black_Parade" title="Welcome to the Black Parade">Welcome to the Black Parade</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Famous_Last_Words_(My_Chemical_Romance_song)" title="Famous Last Words (My Chemical Romance song)">Famous Last Words</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Love_You" title="I Don't Love You">I Don't Love You</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Teenagers_(song)" title="Teenagers (song)">Teenagers</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard.com_2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard.com_2-11">[11]</a></sup> <i>The Black Parade</i> has received generally favorable reviews, and the band achieved its first number one single in the United Kingdom with "Welcome to the Black Parade". The album debuted at number two on both the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> and is also certified as <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-12">[12]</a></sup> as well as a Platinum certification in the United Kingdom and Gold certifications in both <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> (by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CAPIF" title="CAPIF">CAPIF</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> (by the IFPI Chile).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Mercurio_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-El_Mercurio-14">[14]</a></sup> <i>The Black Parade</i> was given the Platinum Europe Award by the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_the_Phonographic_Industry" title="International Federation of the Phonographic Industry">International Federation of the Phonographic Industry</a> for 1 million sales in Europe. The limited edition boxed set also earned My Chemical Romance a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-NME-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-16">[16]</a></sup> In the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Guitar_Hero_II" title="Guitar Hero II">Guitar Hero II</a></i> (Xbox 360 version), the song "Dead!" was added to the game's track list prior to the earlier PlayStation 2 version, and the three songs "Teenagers," "Famous Last Words" and "This Is How I Disappear" are available for download. <i>The Black Parade</i> has sold two million copies in the United States as of 2016, and three million worldwide. The record was reissued as a <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Parade/Living_with_Ghosts" title="The Black Parade/Living with Ghosts">10th Anniversary Edition</a> on September 23, 2016 in celebration of the album's release.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_and_lyrical_themes">Musical and lyrical themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Black_Parade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical and lyrical themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><i>The Black Parade</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Rock_opera" title="Rock opera">rock opera</a> centering around the character of "The Patient". It is about his passage out of life and the memories he has of his life. "The Patient" dies and <a href="/wiki/Death_(personification)" title="Death (personification)">death</a> comes for him in the form of a parade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stylus_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stylus-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-NME-15">[15]</a></sup> This is based upon Gerard Way's notion of death appearing to a person in the form of their fondest memory, in this case seeing a parade as a child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-NME-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album also saw the creation of the alter-ego band, The Black Parade. My Chemical Romance would perform the album live in costume as the Black Parade up until October 7, 2007 <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dead_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-dead-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alterego_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-alterego-18">[18]</a></sup> On stage, the band would don black marching uniforms similar to those worn by <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a> for the album <i><a href="/wiki/Sgt._Pepper%27s_Lonely_Hearts_Club_Band" title="Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band">Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band</a></i> (1967). The live performance was theatrical, with Gerard Way assuming the character of a member of The Black Parade. Way's mannerisms were compared to <a href="/wiki/Bob_Geldof" title="Bob Geldof">Bob Geldof</a> in his performance of the lead character in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pink_Floyd_The_Wall_(film)" title="Pink Floyd The Wall (film)">movie adaption</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pink_Floyd" title="Pink Floyd">Pink Floyd</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">The Wall</a></i> (1979). His performance was also compared to <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s performance of Ziggy Stardust and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>'s stage presence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alterego_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-alterego-18">[18]</a></sup> There are also similarities to <a href="/wiki/Alice_Cooper" title="Alice Cooper">Alice Cooper</a> in his "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_My_Nightmare" title="Welcome to My Nightmare">Welcome to My Nightmare</a>" period. The video for "Welcome to the Black Parade", directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sam_Bayer" title="Sam Bayer">Sam Bayer</a>, portrays the events of the entire story, starring all its characters, including <i>Mother War</i>, who is primarily involved in the song "Mama". Gerard Way has described this video as "The definitive video for the record" and one that "summed up the album."</p>
<p>The album also has strong influences from 1970s classic rock,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altpress_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-altpress-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-patrick_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-patrick-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Glam_rock" title="Glam rock">glam rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altpress_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-altpress-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spinreview_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-spinreview-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Gothic_rock" title="Gothic rock">gothic rock</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spinreview_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-spinreview-22">[22]</a></sup> The album's style is considered <a href="/wiki/Pop_punk" title="Pop punk">pop punk</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-patrick_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-patrick-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spinreview_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-spinreview-22">[22]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-about-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lead singer <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Way" title="Gerard Way">Gerard Way</a> has cited the bands <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a> and Pink Floyd as major influences on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alternative_press_magazine_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-alternative_press_magazine-24">[24]</a></sup> Similarities have been noted between the <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a> orchestration in "Welcome to the Black Parade", and the arrangements of Queen. Additionally, Pink Floyd's <i>The Wall</i> and David Bowie's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rise_and_Fall_of_Ziggy_Stardust_and_the_Spiders_from_Mars" title="The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars">The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars</a></i> (1972) are noted as major influences for the album, which is especially evident when comparing the album's opening track, "The End.", to the first tracks on <i>The Wall</i>, "<a href="/wiki/In_the_Flesh%3F" title="In the Flesh?">In the Flesh?</a>", and on <i>Ziggy Stardust</i>, "<a href="/wiki/Five_Years" title="Five Years">Five Years</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW.com_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW.com-25">[25]</a></sup> Gerard Way has also stated that one of their biggest influences were <a href="/wiki/The_Smashing_Pumpkins" title="The Smashing Pumpkins">The Smashing Pumpkins</a>, often giving them credit for their thematic videos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pumpkins_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-pumpkins-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>“The intention was to make something that was classic, something timeless,” explained guitarist Ray Toro. “Something that 20 or 30 years from now, parents could play for their kids and say, “This is what I was listening to when I was your age. Check it out, it’s still fucking cool.’ We wanted to make a record you could pass down. There’s a lot of music out now that doesn’t feel like that.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altpress1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-altpress1-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Looking back at the creation of the album, guitarist Frank Iero was blasé about <span class="clarify-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">criticism</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Clarify" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="This passage needs to be better explained. (May 2016)">clarify</span></a></i>]</sup> the band received for this decidedly “non-scene” record. "When we did <i>Three Cheers For Sweet Revenge</i>, we didn't fit in," he said. “There was a little less screaming and a little more melody, but it was still us. Never was it a case of, 'Don't put that melody there because Hardcore Chuck, who took me to my first show at Fairfield American Legion Hall, is really going to be bummed at me.' I don’t give a shit: If I had to work at McDonald's for the rest of my life to play shows and ride in a shitty van on tour? I've done it. I'll do it again."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altpress1_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-altpress1-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Black_Parade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 31, 2006, <i>The Black Parade</i> was announced for release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AltPannounce_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-AltPannounce-28">[28]</a></sup> In August, the band was filming a music video in Los Angeles<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alternative_Press_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alternative_Press-29">[29]</a></sup> for "Famous Last Words". During filming, Way torn his ankle ligaments and Bryar suffered burns on his leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Bryar was hospitalized for a period of time, which resulted in the band cancelling two shows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On August 25, the band released a video of a pre-recorded press conference, during which they revealed a number of details about the album, such as song titles and touring information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On August 31, the band performed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_Video_Music_Awards" title="2006 Video Music Awards">2006 Video Music Awards</a> preshow in New York, debuting new song "Welcome to the Black Parade" during the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Two days later, the song was made available for streaming via the band's <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> account.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alternative_Press_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-Alternative_Press-29">[29]</a></sup> On September 12, the album's artwork and track listing was revealed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The music video for "Welcome to the Black Parade" was released on September 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Black Parade</i> was made available for streaming on October 19,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and was released through <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise</a> on October 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AltPannounce_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-AltPannounce-28">[28]</a></sup> In March 2007, the band filmed another music video in Los Angeles,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> this time for "Teenagers".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Versions">Versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Black_Parade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Several special editions of <i>The Black Parade</i> were released. One bears white text on a black background, and the second has black text on a white background. A third version has the normal booklet reversed, showing the picture of the parade drawn and painted by comic artist <a href="/wiki/James_Jean" title="James Jean">James Jean</a>. Inside the booklet there are also lyrics, a photo of the band, and characters from the album.</p>
<p>A limited edition of the album was released the same time as the original release. It contains the same track listing as the original release but it is sold in a box wrapped in black velveteen material. It also contains a 64-page book which includes concept art by Gerard Way and making of the album notes by the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amazon_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-amazon-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>The version of <i>The Black Parade</i> that was released in Japan contains different content than the other regular editions. It contains 14 tracks, but the 14th track is the song "Heaven Help Us" (which was released with the single version of "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_the_Black_Parade" title="Welcome to the Black Parade">Welcome to the Black Parade</a>"), instead of the song "Blood". The Japanese version is also an <a href="/wiki/Enhanced_CD" title="Enhanced CD">Enhanced CD</a> and includes the music video of "Welcome to the Black Parade".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eil.com_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-eil.com-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 11, 2007, <i>The Black Parade</i> was released as a <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_record" title="Gramophone record">vinyl LP</a>, a first for the band. Two versions were released, the regular edition and the special edition. Both editions contain two records. The first record of both editions has tracks one to four on the A-side and five to seven the B-side. The second record has tracks eight to ten on the A-side and 11 to 13 on the B-side. The special edition includes the hidden track, "Blood," while the regular edition does not. The special edition comes in a slip-case box with two 15-page books. 2,500 copies were made of the special edition, while 3,000 copies of the regular vinyl edition were made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Store_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Store-40">[40]</a></sup> On February 10, 2015, the album was repressed on vinyl as a 2-LP set. The D side of the album includes an engraved picture of the cover of artwork.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="10th_anniversary_reissue">10th anniversary reissue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Black_Parade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 10th anniversary reissue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Parade/Living_with_Ghosts" title="The Black Parade/Living with Ghosts">The Black Parade/Living with Ghosts</a></div>
<p>On July 20, 2016, the band posted on their official <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> pages a video with the piano intro from "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_the_Black_Parade" title="Welcome to the Black Parade">Welcome to the Black Parade</a>", ending with a cryptic date, "9/23/16".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The video was also published on the band's <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel with the video titled "MCRX".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> This led to numerous rumors and reports on the band's possible reunion until it was revealed to be a reissue of <i>The Black Parade</i> with unreleased demos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> The reissue, titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Parade/Living_with_Ghosts" title="The Black Parade/Living with Ghosts">The Black Parade/Living with Ghosts</a></i>, includes 11 demos and live tracks. Two months before its release, an early version of "Welcome to the Black Parade", titled "The Five of Us Are Dying", was made available for streaming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<p>At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalized</a> rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean">average</a> score of 79, based on 24 professional critic reviews, which indicates "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metacritic_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-Metacritic-49">[49]</a></sup> Dan Martin from <i>NME</i> compared the album to <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Idiot_(album)" title="American Idiot (album)">American Idiot</a></i>, positing that "it's a piece of work that will challenge every preconception you ever had about the people who made it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-NME-15">[15]</a></sup> Tim Karan of Alternative Press called <i>The Black Parade</i> "MCR's whole raison d'etre rolled up into one mega-decibel calling card". Ed Thompson of <i>IGN</i> stated, "<i>The Black Parade</i> is a rock and roll gem that celebrates everything that was over the top about the 1970s rock scene."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-21">[21]</a></sup> David Fricke of <i>Rolling Stone</i> praised the classic rock feel of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-16">[16]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> stated that "On their third studio album, a musical H-bomb of an effort, the Jersey quintet combine the rock-opera pomp of Queen with the darker, dirtier tones of their screamo past: Call it a Bro-hemian Rhapsody. Even without its broad concept — a dying cancer patient seeks revenge and redemption — Parade stands as one of the most cohesive, engaging rock records of 2006."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-EW-51">[51]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> gave the album a two-star honorable mention (<span style="white-space:nowrap" title="(2-star Honorable Mention)"><img alt="(2-star Honorable Mention)" data-file-height="110" data-file-width="110" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Black_star.svg/11px-Black_star.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Black_star.svg/17px-Black_star.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Black_star.svg/22px-Black_star.svg.png 2x" title="(2-star Honorable Mention)" width="11"/><img alt="" data-file-height="110" data-file-width="110" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Black_star.svg/11px-Black_star.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Black_star.svg/17px-Black_star.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Black_star.svg/22px-Black_star.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></span>) and said, "In prog, a good sense of humor means so much."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album was not without its share of criticism, and was panned by such publications as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Observer" title="The Observer">The Observer</a></i> and <i>Aversion</i>. Stated Jamie Hodgson of <i>The Observer</i>, "...it reeks of a band with ideas above its station."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Observer_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Observer-60">[60]</a></sup> Matt Schild of <i>Aversion</i> originally refused to review the album, simply writing the word "lame" in lieu of an actual review. Bowing to editorial pressure, he later posted a review listing specific aspects of the album before denouncing them as "lame".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aversion_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aversion-61">[61]</a></sup> Theon Weber of <i>Stylus</i> praised the album's use of Queen influences, but went on to summarize the album as "...a goofy record of bubblegum punk, with Queen lapping at its edges and enough good tracks to justify the smattering of empty screamfests."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stylus_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stylus-7">[7]</a></sup> The album was included in <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Sound" title="Rock Sound">Rock Sound</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s 101 Modern Classics list at number 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Black_Parade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Black Parade</i> debuted at number two in the United States on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> behind <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana_(soundtrack)" title="Hannah Montana (soundtrack)">Hannah Montana</a></i> (2006).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_chart_74-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_chart-74">[74]</a></sup> It also debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>, behind <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Williams" title="Robbie Williams">Robbie Williams</a>' <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rudebox_(album)" title="Rudebox (album)">Rudebox</a></i> (2006).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everyhit_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-everyhit-75">[75]</a></sup> In its first week, the album sold 240,000 copies, far surpassing the 38,000 best-week sales of the band's previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/Three_Cheers_for_Sweet_Revenge" title="Three Cheers for Sweet Revenge">Three Cheers for Sweet Revenge</a></i> (2004).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard.com_3_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard.com_3-76">[76]</a></sup> The album debuted at number three on the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">Australian ARIA Albums Chart</a> and was certified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARIA_certifications" title="ARIA certifications">platinum</a> after shipping more than 70,000 copies. It debuted atop the charts in New Zealand and was certified platinum there, with shipments of more than 15,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rianz_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-rianz-77">[77]</a></sup> In 2012, <i>The Black Parade</i> was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_the_Phonographic_Industry" title="International Federation of the Phonographic Industry">International Federation of the Phonographic Industry</a> (IFPI) for 1,000,000 sales in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> <i>The Black Parade</i> has also been certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> for selling over 1,100,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard.com_79-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard.com-79">[79]</a></sup> It has been certified 2x Platinum in the UK, and has sold just over 600,000 units there to date. As of August 25, 2007, the album has sold 1,169,697 copies in the U.S. "Welcome to the Black Parade", a single from "The Black Parade" became My Chemical Romance's first and only top 10 single in the United States.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tour">Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Black_Parade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Parade_World_Tour" title="The Black Parade World Tour">The Black Parade World Tour</a></div>
<p>My Chemical Romance began <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Parade_World_Tour" title="The Black Parade World Tour">The Black Parade World Tour</a></i> on February 22, 2007 in <a href="/wiki/Manchester,_New_Hampshire" title="Manchester, New Hampshire">Manchester, New Hampshire</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verizon_Wireless_Arena" title="Verizon Wireless Arena">Verizon Wireless Arena</a>. The tour featured 138 performances worldwide, as well as several festival and condensed shows. The tour is the longest and most internationally comprehensive headlining tour that the band has played to date, featuring three legs in North America, two legs in Europe, and one in Asia, Australia, and Latin America. The shows at the <a href="/wiki/Palacio_de_los_Deportes" title="Palacio de los Deportes">Palacio de los Deportes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> on October 7, 2007 and <a href="/wiki/Maxwell%27s" title="Maxwell's">Maxwell's</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hoboken,_New_Jersey" title="Hoboken, New Jersey">Hoboken</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> on October 24, 2007 were filmed for the <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Parade_Is_Dead!" title="The Black Parade Is Dead!">The Black Parade Is Dead!</a></i>, which was released on July 1, 2008.</p>
<p>Over the course of this tour, My Chemical Romance dubbed themselves The Black Parade for the first part of their performance, convincing many concert goers that The Black Parade was initially a separate, opening band. During the tour there were several cancellations and some members left the tour for personal or medical reasons. Leading up to their tour date at The DCU Arena, many of the staff members of the tour fell ill with food poisoning. The band Circa Survive had to replace Muse, members of which also suffered from the same food poisoning. On January 11, 2007 <a href="/wiki/Frank_Iero" title="Frank Iero">Frank Iero</a> left the tour because of an unspecified illness. He was replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drive_By" title="Drive By">Drive By</a> guitarist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Todd_Price" title="Todd Price">Todd Price</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-franksick_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-franksick-80">[80]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mikey_Way" title="Mikey Way">Mikey Way</a> took time off to get married and spend time with his new wife (Alicia Simmons) and was replaced by guitar tech, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_Cortez" title="Matt Cortez">Matt Cortez</a>, from April 18, 2007 until October 4, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCR_News_81-0"><a href="#cite_note-MCR_News-81">[81]</a></sup> Six shows were cancelled from April 29, 2007 to May 4, 2007 after the band and crew contracted food poisoning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foodpoisoning_82-0"><a href="#cite_note-foodpoisoning-82">[82]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bob_Bryar" title="Bob Bryar">Bob Bryar</a> suffered injuries relating to his wrists during the tour which led to the cancellation of the show at the University of Maine on October 27, 2007. Bryar left the tour on November 9, 2007 and was replaced by a friend of the band, Wagner, who wished to remain anonymous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wristproblems_83-0"><a href="#cite_note-wristproblems-83">[83]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wristNME_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-wristNME-84">[84]</a></sup> Following the November 11, 2007 show in <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle</a>, Frank Iero left the tour to return home after learning about a family member's illness. He was replaced by Matt Cortez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frankabsence_85-0"><a href="#cite_note-frankabsence-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
