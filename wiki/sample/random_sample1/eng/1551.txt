<p><i><b>The Sing-Off</b></i> is an American television singing competition featuring <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> groups. It debuted on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> on December 14, 2009, and is produced by <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures_Television" title="Sony Pictures Television">Sony Pictures Television</a> and Outlaw Productions, with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Burnett" title="Mark Burnett">Mark Burnett</a>'s One Three Media (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Artists_Media_Group" title="United Artists Media Group">United Artists Media Group</a>) being added for the fourth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Season_4_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Season_4-1">[1]</a></sup> A fifth season aired as a holiday special in December 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Sing-Off&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is hosted by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Lachey" title="Nick Lachey">Nick Lachey</a> (from the boy band <a href="/wiki/98_Degrees" title="98 Degrees">98 Degrees</a>), with judges <a href="/wiki/Ben_Folds" title="Ben Folds">Ben Folds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Stockman" title="Shawn Stockman">Shawn Stockman</a> (of the rhythm-and-blues vocal group <a href="/wiki/Boyz_II_Men" title="Boyz II Men">Boyz II Men</a>), and originally <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Scherzinger" title="Nicole Scherzinger">Nicole Scherzinger</a>, later replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sara_Bareilles" title="Sara Bareilles">Sara Bareilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Like_The_Sing_Off_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Like_The_Sing_Off-2">[2]</a></sup> Bareilles left the show after the third season and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Jewel_(singer)" title="Jewel (singer)">Jewel</a> for the fourth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jewel_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jewel-3">[3]</a></sup> Several members of the show's music staff have come from the <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> community, including <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Deke_Sharon" title="Deke Sharon">Deke Sharon</a>, Bill Hare, Ed Boyer, Ben Bram, Robert Dietz, Christopher Diaz, and Nick Girard.</p>
<p>A cappella groups from the United States, including <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, were chosen by audition and advanced in the competition based on judges' eliminations. Before the fourth season, the winners of the competition were determined by viewer votes during the finale.</p>
<p>The program has featured guest appearances by various popular artists, including <a href="/wiki/Bobby_McFerrin" title="Bobby McFerrin">Bobby McFerrin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Bedingfield" title="Natasha Bedingfield">Natasha Bedingfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Sean" title="Jay Sean">Jay Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smokey_Robinson" title="Smokey Robinson">Smokey Robinson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Diamond" title="Neil Diamond">Neil Diamond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boyz_II_Men" title="Boyz II Men">Boyz II Men</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flo_Rida" title="Flo Rida">Flo Rida</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sara_Bareilles" title="Sara Bareilles">Sara Bareilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DigitalJournal_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-DigitalJournal-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Sing-Off</i> was renewed for a third season and started to air much earlier on September 19, 2011, though the first two series had been broadcast in December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidetv_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-insidetv-7">[7]</a></sup> Unlike previous seasons, the early start in September allowed for the production of more episodes and the number of participant groups increased.</p>
<p>The Puerto Rican group <a href="/wiki/Nota_(group)" title="Nota (group)">Nota</a> won the title for the inaugural season in 2009. For season 2, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Committed_(group)" title="Committed (group)">Committed</a> from <a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Alabama" title="Huntsville, Alabama">Huntsville, Alabama</a> took the 2010 title, and for season 3, <a href="/wiki/Pentatonix" title="Pentatonix">Pentatonix</a> from <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington, Texas</a> won the 2011 title. The country group <a href="/wiki/Home_Free_(group)" title="Home Free (group)">Home Free</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minneapolis,_Minnesota" title="Minneapolis, Minnesota">Minneapolis, Minnesota</a> won the 2013 title for season 4.</p>
<p>On May 13, 2012, <i>The Sing-Off</i> was not renewed for another season, presumably cancelled after three seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, on March 13, 2013, almost a year after the show was not renewed, NBC announced that <i>The Sing-Off</i> would return for a fourth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Season_4_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Season_4-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_1_.282009.29">Season 1 (2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Sing-Off&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Season 1 (2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Sing-Off_(season_1)" title="The Sing-Off (season 1)">The Sing-Off (season 1)</a></div>
<p>The show featured eight a cappella groups performing popular songs live. The winner's prize was $100,000 and a <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a> recording contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Like_The_Sing_Off_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Like_The_Sing_Off-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first episode saw all groups perform their signature song. The second episode saw the remaining six groups perform two songs: in the first half of the show, each group performed one big hit from recent years; and in the second half, each group sang one "guilty pleasure" song. The third episode had the five remaining groups perform a medley from a classic artist. In the second part of the show, the four remaining groups performed songs that the judges picked specifically to showcase each group's strengths.</p>
<p>At the end of the third show, viewers were asked to call or text to vote for one of the remaining groups, <a href="/wiki/Lee_University" title="Lee University">Voices of Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beelzebubs" title="Beelzebubs">The Beelzebubs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nota_(group)" title="Nota (group)">Nota</a>. The group with the most votes was revealed in the finale, and in the end Voices of Lee was revealed to come in third place halfway through the finale, with <a href="/wiki/Nota_(group)" title="Nota (group)">Nota</a> being declared winners.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_2_.282010.29">Season 2 (2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Sing-Off&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Season 2 (2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Sing-Off_(season_2)" title="The Sing-Off (season 2)">The Sing-Off (season 2)</a></div>
<p>In the second season, the show returned on December 6, 2010, for five episodes, with the number of groups increased from eight to ten, with all acts coming from the United States. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Lachey" title="Nick Lachey">Nick Lachey</a> returned as host for the second season, as well as the three judges <a href="/wiki/Ben_Folds" title="Ben Folds">Ben Folds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Stockman" title="Shawn Stockman">Shawn Stockman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Scherzinger" title="Nicole Scherzinger">Nicole Scherzinger</a>.</p>
<p>The first episode had the groups sing their signature songs. In the second episode, the remaining eight groups sang a modern song that was released in the past five years. For the third episode, the groups covered a rock song and a "guilty pleasure" piece. The fourth episode was to sing a medley of songs by a noteworthy artist and perform a song chosen by the judges. One more group was supposed to be eliminated, but the judges chose to put Jerry Lawson and The Talk of the Town, The Backbeats, Street Corner Symphony, and Committed all through to the finale. This is the only season to have four groups in the finale.</p>
<p>At the finale, Jerry Lawson and The Talk of the Town was the first to be eliminated. The Backbeats came in third place, Street Corner Symphony came in second, and Committed won the title.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Sing-Off_(season_3)" title="The Sing-Off (season 3)">The Sing-Off (season 3)</a></div>
<p><i>The Sing-Off</i> was renewed for a third season of eleven episodes and premiered on September 19, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidetv_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-insidetv-7">[7]</a></sup> This was the first <i>Sing-Off</i> season to include sixteen groups of a cappella singers. On May 16, 2011, it was announced that judge <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Scherzinger" title="Nicole Scherzinger">Nicole Scherzinger</a> would not return for this season due to her duties as one of the judges of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(U.S.)" title="The X Factor (U.S.)">The X Factor</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and that <a href="/wiki/Sara_Bareilles" title="Sara Bareilles">Sara Bareilles</a> would replace Scherzinger on the judging panel. Nick Lachey, Ben Folds, and Shawn Stockman all returned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since there were sixteen vocal groups, the groups were divided into two brackets of eight for the first four episodes to get down to the top ten. The first and second episodes had the groups sing their signature songs, while the third and fourth episodes had the groups perform a current hit and a classic from the 1960s. The fifth episode saw the two brackets merge, with the remaining ten groups performing their guilty pleasure songs. In the sixth episode, the eight remaining groups performed a hip-hop song and at the end of the episode, the show had its first-ever <i>Sing-Off</i> showdown, in which bottom two groups sang different versions of a song and the judges voted which to send home. The remaining groups in the seventh episode sang a superstar medley and in the eighth episode, the remaining six performed a country song and a rock-and-roll song. The top five groups performed rhythm-and-blues hits from today, and classics from the past, in the ninth episode. In the tenth episode, the top four performed master mix songs, a song picked by the judges. In the elimination, Urban Method and Pentatonix advanced to the finale. The judges could not come to a decision on the remaining two groups, Afro-Blue and the Dartmouth Aires, so they performed their favorite songs from past performances. In a two-to-one decision by the judges, Afro-Blue was voted off and the Dartmouth Aires advanced to the finale. The live finale crowned <a href="/wiki/Pentatonix" title="Pentatonix">Pentatonix</a> as winners, with the Dartmouth Aires coming in second, and Urban Method coming in third place.</p>
<p>One week after the third season ended, a Christmas special (titled "The Sing-Off Christmas") was aired on December 5, 2011. It featured groups from all three seasons singing various Christmas songs.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Sing-Off_(season_4)" title="The Sing-Off (season 4)">The Sing-Off (season 4)</a></div>
<p>On March 13, 2013, NBC announced that <i>The Sing-Off</i> would return for a fourth season, with additional production by Mark Burnett's One Three Media, which also produces NBC's other singing competition show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> After the fourth season was announced, Sara Bareilles announced that she would not be returning to the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On July 2, 2013, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Jewel_(singer)" title="Jewel (singer)">Jewel</a> would replace Bareilles as a judge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jewel_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jewel-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a new addition to the season, at the end of every show, the bottom two groups decided by the judges faced off in an "Ultimate Sing-Off", with each group singing the same song trying to eliminate their opponent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The show returned on December 9, 2013, and aired over several nights, concluding on December 23, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Season_4_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Season_4-1">[1]</a></sup> In a first for <i>The Sing-Off</i>, groups from the show will go on tour, with thirty-two shows scheduled in thirty-two cities during February and March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Home_Free_(group)" title="Home Free (group)">Home Free</a> was crowned the winner, with Ten coming in second and Vocal Rush coming in third.</p>

<p>On October 1, 2014, NBC renewed the series for a fifth season to air at the end of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Although a "season" of shows did not air (to the confusion of some fans), NBC aired a two-hour special on December 17, 2014. Lachey, Stockman and Jewel returned with <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Stump" title="Patrick Stump">Patrick Stump</a> replacing Folds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S5Airdate_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-S5Airdate-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of the night, the <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_University" title="Vanderbilt University">Vanderbilt Melodores</a> were crowned as the new champions, <a href="/wiki/The_Exchange_(band)" title="The Exchange (band)">The Exchange</a> were the runners-up, and Traces placed third. The other competitors on Season 5 were a.squared, San Fran 6, and Timothy's Gift.</p>
<p>The special received 5.10 million viewers, a 1.3 rating, and a 4 share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
