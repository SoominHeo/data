<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a> competed as <b>Great Britain</b> at the <b><a href="/wiki/1900_Summer_Olympics" title="1900 Summer Olympics">1900 Summer Olympics</a></b> in Paris, France. It was the second appearance of Britain after having participated in the inaugural <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Games</a>. In Olympic competition, the nation has always shortened its official name to <i>Great Britain</i> rather than the <i>United Kingdom</i> seen elsewhere.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Great_Britain_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Great Britain took 4 gold medals in athletics, including one as part of a mixed team (with 4 British athletes and one Australian). This put them second on the leaderboard for that sport, behind the dominant <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="United States at the 1900 Summer Olympics">United States</a> as the two nations to win multiple gold medals in the sport. The British team won a total of 9 athletics medals including a sweep of the 4000 metre steeplechase event. 9 athletes competed in 10 events.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sailing">Sailing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Great_Britain_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sailing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sailing_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Sailing at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Sailing at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Great Britain matched France in gold medals at the 1900 sailing events with 5, but took only 1 other medal, a bronze. France took 8 silvers and 8 bronzes for a total of 21 medals to Britain's 6. However, even a perfect performance by the British athletes would have earned only a total of 8 medals—Great Britain failed to medal in only 2 of its attempts. The crew members listed are those listed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOC" title="IOC">IOC</a> in their database. The Olympic historian Ian Buchanan in his book "British Olympians" (1991) states that "reports on many aspects of the 1900 regatta are inconclusive and the crew members of the British entry "Scotia" has never been positively settled. The records of the British Olympic Association give the crew as Lorne Currie, John Gretton and Linton Hope, but it has been established that Linton Hope was in England at the time of the races and his name only appears in the Olympic records as he was the designer of the "Scotia". Similarly the names of Currie and Gretton are probably only listed as the owners of the boat but as it is possible that they sailed their boat, they are listed as Olympic champions, although the participation of Lorne Currie, in particular, is in doubt. The one crew member whose participation has been established is Algernon Maudslay, whose name does not appear in any Olympic records, but from contemporary press reports it is clear that he was the helmsman of the "Scotia"".</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_Polo_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics" title="Water Polo at the 1900 Summer Olympics">Water Polo at the 1900 Summer Olympics</a></div>
