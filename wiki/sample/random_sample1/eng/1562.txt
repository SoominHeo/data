<p><b>US Bank Tower</b>, formerly <b>Library Tower</b> and <b>First Interstate Bank World Center</b>, is a 1,018-foot (310 m) <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">skyscraper</a> at 633 West Fifth Street in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Los_Angeles" title="Downtown Los Angeles">downtown Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. Until recently, it was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tallest_building_in_California" title="Tallest building in California">tallest building in California</a>, the tallest building in LA, the fourteenth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tallest_in_the_United_States" title="Tallest in the United States">tallest in the United States</a>, the second tallest west of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_the_world" title="List of tallest buildings in the world">92nd tallest building in the world</a>. Because local <a href="/wiki/Building_code" title="Building code">building codes</a> required all high-rise buildings to have a helipad, it was known as the tallest building in the world with a roof-top <a href="/wiki/Heliport" title="Heliport">heliport</a> from its completion in 1989 to 2004 when <a href="/wiki/Taipei_101" title="Taipei 101">Taipei 101</a> opened. It is also the third tallest building in a major active seismic region; its structure was designed to resist an <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> of 8.3 on the <a href="/wiki/Richter_magnitude_scale" title="Richter magnitude scale">Richter scale</a>. It consists of 73 stories above ground and two parking levels below ground. Construction began in 1987 with completion in 1989. The building was designed by <a href="/wiki/Henry_N._Cobb" title="Henry N. Cobb">Henry N. Cobb</a> of the architectural firm <a href="/wiki/Pei_Cobb_Freed_%26_Partners" title="Pei Cobb Freed &amp; Partners">Pei Cobb Freed &amp; Partners</a> and cost $350 million to build. It is one of the most recognizable buildings in Los Angeles, often used in <a href="/wiki/Establishing_shot" title="Establishing shot">establishing shots</a> for the city in films and television programs.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership">Ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Bank_Tower_(Los_Angeles)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ownership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>US Bank Tower in Los Angeles was sold to Overseas Union Enterprise Ltd (OUE), a hotel and property group controlled by Indonesia's <a href="/wiki/Lippo_Group" title="Lippo Group">Lippo Group</a> in 2013. OUE, a Singapore-based hotel and property company run by Indonesian billionaire Stephen Riady, acquired the tower and other related assets for $367.5 million. OUE acquired the 72-floor office building, the adjacent Maguire Gardens park, and a car park from a unit of Los Angeles-based real-estate investment trust MPG Office Trust Inc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Bank_Tower_(Los_Angeles)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The building is also known as Library Tower because it was built as part of the $1 billion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Central_Library" title="Los Angeles Central Library">Los Angeles Central Library</a> redevelopment area following two disastrous fires in 1986, and its location across the street.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The City of Los Angeles sold <a href="/wiki/Air_rights" title="Air rights">air rights</a> to the developers of the tower to help pay for the reconstruction of the library. The building was also known for a time as First Interstate Bank World Center but the name Library Tower was restored after <a href="/wiki/First_Interstate_Bancorp" title="First Interstate Bancorp">First Interstate Bancorp</a> merged with <a href="/wiki/Wells_Fargo" title="Wells Fargo">Wells Fargo Bank</a>. In March 2003, the property was leased by <a href="/wiki/U.S._Bancorp" title="U.S. Bancorp">U.S. Bancorp</a> and the building was renamed US Bank Tower. Residents, however, generally continue to refer to it as Library Tower.</p>

<li>white (every day)</li>
<li>red and green during the <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> holiday season</li>
<li>pink and red for <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine's Day">Valentine's Day</a></li>
<li>red and gold for <a href="/wiki/Chinese_New_Year" title="Chinese New Year">Chinese New Year</a></li>
<li>red, white, and blue for <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(United_States)" title="Independence Day (United States)">Independence Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Veterans_Day" title="Veterans Day">Veterans Day</a></li>
<li>orange for <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a></li>
<li>red, green, gold, blue, and purple leading up to the <a href="/wiki/2015_Special_Olympics_World_Summer_Games" title="2015 Special Olympics World Summer Games">2015 Special Olympics World Summer Games</a></li>
<li>blue and white when the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> are playing in the postseason</li>

<li>black and white when the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Kings" title="Los Angeles Kings">Los Angeles Kings</a> are playing in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanley_Cup_Final" title="Stanley Cup Final">Stanley Cup Final</a></li>

<li>red for Blood Cancer Awareness Month</li>
<li>pink for <a href="/wiki/National_Breast_Cancer_Awareness_Month" title="National Breast Cancer Awareness Month">National Breast Cancer Awareness Month</a></li>
</ul>
<p>On February 28, 2004, two 23 m (75 ft) “U.S. Bank” logo signs were installed on the crown, amid controversy for their effect on the aesthetic appearance of the building, much like the previous First Interstate Bank logos were placed on the crown between 1990 and 1998. First Interstate Bank's “I” logo on the crown was in the 1993 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_Book_of_World_Records" title="Guinness Book of World Records">Guinness Book of World Records</a> for highest placed logo.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="OUE_Skyspace">OUE Skyspace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Bank_Tower_(Los_Angeles)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: OUE Skyspace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2014, Overseas Union Enterprise Ltd. (OUE), the new owners of the skyscraper, announced construction of an observation deck named OUE Skyspace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> on the 69th and 70th floors and a restaurant named 71Above<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>on the 71st floor. The facilities opened on June 24, 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> following remodeling and construction costing $31 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> that included a makeover of the ground floor lobby as well as a separate second floor entrance for tourists, and a skylobby and exhibit hall on the 54th floor. Access to the observation deck costs $25 per person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> For an additional $8 visitors can take a trip down a transparent glass slide affixed to the outside of the building between the 70th and 69th floors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terrorist_target">Terrorist target</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Bank_Tower_(Los_Angeles)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Terrorist target">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 16, 2004, the <a href="/wiki/9/11_Commission" title="9/11 Commission">9/11 Commission</a> reported that the <a href="/wiki/Planning_of_the_September_11_attacks#Origins_of_the_9.2F11_attacks" title="Planning of the September 11 attacks">original plan</a> for the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> called for the hijacking of ten planes, one of which was to be crashed into the building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 6, 2005, House officials stated that the government had foiled a previously undisclosed second plot to crash a plane into the building in mid-2002. In his televised <a href="/wiki/2006_State_of_the_Union_Address" title="2006 State of the Union Address">2006 State of the Union Address</a>, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> asserted that American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counterterrorism" title="Counterterrorism">counterterrorism</a> officials foiled a plot to fly planes into the tower, which he erroneously identified as “Liberty Tower”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> According to President Bush, <a href="/wiki/Al-Qaeda" title="Al-Qaeda">Al-Qaeda</a> leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khaled_Sheikh_Mohammed" title="Khaled Sheikh Mohammed">Khaled Sheikh Mohammed</a>'s plan was to use Asian confederates from <a href="/wiki/Jemaah_Islamiyah" title="Jemaah Islamiyah">Jemaah Islamiyah</a> recruited by Islamic militant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hambali" title="Hambali">Hambali</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_hijacking" title="Aircraft hijacking">hijacking</a>. President Bush asserted the hijackers were going to use shoe bombs to breach the plane's cockpit door. Some counter-terrorism experts have expressed doubt that the plot was ever fully developed or likely to occur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Bank_Tower_(Los_Angeles)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The building and the Los Angeles skyline is featured many times in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminator_2" title="Terminator 2">Terminator 2</a></i></li>
<li>In the 1990 film <i><a href="/wiki/Predator_2" title="Predator 2">Predator 2</a></i> , Danny Glover is seen chasing the Predator around different parts of Los Angeles</li>
<li>The building made a notable appearance in the 1996 film <i><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(1996_film)" title="Independence Day (1996 film)">Independence Day</a></i>, in which the tower was the first structure to be destroyed during the beginning of a massive <a href="/wiki/Alien_invasion" title="Alien invasion">alien invasion</a>.</li>
<li>In the 2000 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_The_Kid" title="Disney's The Kid">Disney's The Kid</a></i> (2000), <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a>' character, an image consultant, works in the building.</li>
<li>In the 2001 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Swordfish_(film)" title="Swordfish (film)">Swordfish</a></i>, the escape bus is deposited on top of the building by the Skyhook helicopter. Note that while the helipad is rated for 12 tons, a loaded bus can weigh upwards of 16.)</li>
<li>In the 2004 movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Day_After_Tomorrow" title="The Day After Tomorrow">The Day After Tomorrow</a></i>, the US Bank Tower is severely damaged by the super tornado, but ultimately still standing.</li>
<li>The 2007 film, <i><a href="/wiki/D-War" title="D-War">Dragon Wars</a></i> features a giant monster dragon that first destroys and rampages through the city and then climbs the tower.</li>
<li>In the 2007 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Southland_Tales" title="Southland Tales">Southland Tales</a></i>, The building stands for US-IDENT, a Big Brother surveillance agency that is under the guise of a national security think-tank.</li>
<li>In the 2009 disaster film <i><a href="/wiki/2012_(film)" title="2012 (film)">2012</a></i>, it is one of the many buildings in Los Angeles destroyed by a major earthquake.</li>
<li>In the 2015 disaster film, <i><a href="/wiki/San_Andreas_(film)" title="San Andreas (film)">San Andreas</a></i>, the tower is among many high-rise buildings in Downtown Los Angeles that collapse due to a 9.1 earthquake striking the city. In one scene, protagonist Ray Gaines (played by Dwayne Johnson) barely flew his helicopter past the collapsing tower.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/History_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="History (U.S. TV channel)">History Channel</a> series <a href="/wiki/Life_After_People" title="Life After People">Life After People</a>, the US Bank Tower is shown withstanding a giant fire that consumed nearly all <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, and "The Big One", an earthquake measuring 8.0 in the Richter scale. Finally, it collapses 600 years after the disappearance of people.</li>
<li>The 2002 music video for "Thug Lovin'" by <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Brown" title="Bobby Brown">Bobby Brown</a> was filmed on the roof of tower.</li>
<li>The building was featured as an unnamed tower in the 2004 game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_San_Andreas" title="Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas">Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas</a></i> and then also features in the 2013 game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i> as the Maze Bank Tower.</li>
</ul>
