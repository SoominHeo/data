<p>The Arabian Gulf League is the top professional football league in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> (UAE). The first team to win the title was <a href="/wiki/Al-Sharjah_SCC" title="Al-Sharjah SCC">Al-Sharjah</a>, whilst <a href="/wiki/Al_Ain_FC" title="Al Ain FC">Al-Ain</a> has the record with 12 league titles to their name. Fourteen clubs compete in the League that operates on a system of <a href="/wiki/Promotion_and_relegation" title="Promotion and relegation">promotion and relegation</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE_Division_1_Group_A" title="UAE Division 1 Group A">UAE First Division</a>.</p>
<p>The League was founded in 1973 as the <b>UAE Football League</b>. The first 1973–74 season was a "trial" championship but was declared official by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE_Football_Association" title="UAE Football Association">UAE FA</a> in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In February 2007, the <a href="/wiki/UAE_Pro_League_Committee" title="UAE Pro League Committee">Pro League Committee</a> was formed, and became the organizing body of the League.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_change">Name change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UAE_Arabian_Gulf_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Starting from the 2013–14 season the name was changed to <b>UAE Arabian Gulf League</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> after a 70 million AED one-year renewable partnership deal with Arabian Gulf Development to be named Official Title Partner,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> however, the name change has been viewed as a revival of the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf_naming_dispute" title="Persian Gulf naming dispute">Persian Gulf naming dispute</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with Iran accusing the United Arab Emirates of racism,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran" title="Football Federation Islamic Republic of Iran">Iranian Football Federation</a> barring the transfer of <a href="/wiki/Javad_Nekounam" title="Javad Nekounam">Javad Nekounam</a> to a UAE club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
