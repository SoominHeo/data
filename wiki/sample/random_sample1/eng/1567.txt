<p><b>UCC Ueshima Coffee Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">UCC上島珈琲株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yū Shī Shī Ueshima Kōhī Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a manufacturer of <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a> products in <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo_Prefecture" title="Hyōgo Prefecture">Hyōgo</a>. "UCC" stands for "Ueshima Coffee Company".</p>
<p>It owns a <a href="/wiki/Kona_coffee" title="Kona coffee">Kona coffee</a> farm on the island of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_(island)" title="Hawaii (island)">Hawaii</a>. The company is a part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=UCC_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="UCC Group (page does not exist)">UCC Group</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UCC_Ueshima_Coffee_Co.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company started as a store under the name of "Ueshima Tadao Shoten" in 1933. It became a limited partnership in 1940 and in 1951, became "Ueshima Coffee Co., Ltd.". The company introduced the world's first canned coffee, "UCC Coffee with Milk" in April, 1969, which started the trend for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Canned_Coffee" title="Japanese Canned Coffee">canned coffee</a> (缶コーヒー) in Japan.</p>
<p>It formally became "UCC Ueshima Coffee Co., Ltd." in 1991.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing_Tie-Ins">Marketing Tie-Ins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UCC_Ueshima_Coffee_Co.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marketing Tie-Ins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>UCC Coffee became famous with <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">otaku</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> fans) around the world after their canned coffee was featured in the 1997 feature film <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_of_Evangelion" title="The End of Evangelion">The End of Evangelion</a></i>. As a tie-in with the makers of the film, UCC created six coffee can designs featuring the <a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion">Evangelion</a> characters holding the company's products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCC_Official_Evangelion_History_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-UCC_Official_Evangelion_History-3">[3]</a></sup> These cans subsequently became prized collector's items. UCC continued this partnership when, a decade later, a new set of four Evangelion films began their release with 2007's <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_1.0_You_Are_(Not)_Alone" title="Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone">Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</a></i>. The cans were again featured in the film, and UCC again featured illustrations of the film's characters on a limited release of their cans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCC_Official_Evangelion_History_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-UCC_Official_Evangelion_History-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2009, UCC created another line of limited edition cans featuring the characters from the second film in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance" title="Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance">Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eva_2.0_UCC_Official_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eva_2.0_UCC_Official-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EvaGeeks_Eva_2.0_UCC_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-EvaGeeks_Eva_2.0_UCC-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
