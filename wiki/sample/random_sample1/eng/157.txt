<p>The <b>men's 100 metres</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" title="Sprint (running)">sprinting</a> event on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">athletics programme</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1900_Summer_Olympics" title="1900 Summer Olympics">1900 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. It was held on July 14, 1900. 20 athletes from nine nations competed.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Arthur_Duffey" title="Arthur Duffey">Arthur Duffey</a> in the first heat of the first round and <a href="/wiki/Walter_Tewksbury" title="Walter Tewksbury">Walter Tewksbury</a> in the second heat of the first round set new Olympic records with 11.4 seconds. In the third heat of the first round <a href="/wiki/Frank_Jarvis" title="Frank Jarvis">Frank Jarvis</a> equalled the unofficial world record with 10.8 seconds. In the second semifinal Tewksbury also equalled the world record with 10.8 seconds.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Semifinals">Semifinals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Semifinals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There were three semifinals, each with four runners. The top runner in each of the semifinals advanced to the final, while the second and third place runners competed in the repechage. The winner of the six-runner repechage advanced to the final as well.</p>
