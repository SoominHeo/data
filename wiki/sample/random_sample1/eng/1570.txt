<p><b>Unión Deportiva Salamanca, S.A.D.</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[uˈnjon deporˈtiβa salaˈmaŋka]</a></span>) was a <a href="/wiki/List_of_football_clubs_in_Spain" title="List of football clubs in Spain">Spanish football team</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Salamanca" title="Salamanca">Salamanca</a>, in the autonomous community of <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a>. Founded on 16 March 1923, it played its last season in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a>.</p>
<p>Nicknamed <i>Los Charros</i>, the club played in white shirts and black shorts, holding home games at <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estadio_El_Helm%C3%A1ntico" title="Estadio El Helmántico">Estadio El Helmántico</a></i>, which seated 17,341 spectators.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UD_Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initially formed by <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> students, Salamanca first played in early Spanish championships in 1907, before an official league was founded later on. On 16 March 1923, at the tables of <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Novelty" title="Café Novelty">Café Novelty</a>, situated in the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mayor,_Salamanca" title="Plaza Mayor, Salamanca">Plaza Mayor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dionisio_Ridruejo" title="Dionisio Ridruejo">Dionisio Ridruejo</a> set the club's early official foundations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and, from 1939 and during the following three decades, it fluctuated between the <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">third</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">second levels</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_football_league_system" title="Spanish football league system">Spanish football league</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1974%E2%80%9375_La_Liga" title="1974–75 La Liga">1974–75 season</a>, Salamanca made its <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> debuts, overachieving for a final 7th position (out of 18 teams), which eventually would be its best classification ever. The team lasted in the topflight until 1981, mainly coached by José Luis García Traid, then returned the following year for a further two seasons, being further relegated to <i><a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a></i> – the new third division created in 1977 – in <a href="/wiki/1984%E2%80%9385_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1984–85 Segunda División">1984–85</a>, and spending three years in that category before promoting back.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1994–95 Segunda División">1994–95's second division</a>, after finishing fourth in the league, Salamanca lost the first leg of the <a href="/wiki/Promotion_and_relegation" title="Promotion and relegation">promotion/relegation</a> playoffs against <a href="/wiki/Albacete_Balompi%C3%A9" title="Albacete Balompié">Albacete Balompié</a>, 0–2 at home, but won 5–0 away, returning to the main level after eleven years. The club was managed by 29-year-old <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Lillo" title="Juan Manuel Lillo">Juan Manuel Lillo</a>, also in charge for <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_La_Liga" title="1995–96 La Liga">the following season</a>, as the <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a> club ranked 22nd and last in the top level.</p>
<p>From 1999 onwards (with two more visits to the first division, with 15th and 20th-place finishes respectively), Salamanca stabilized in the second level, save for the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2005–06 Segunda División B">2005–06 season</a> spent in the third division, with the club winning the regular season and promoting in the playoffs. Veteran <a href="/wiki/Quique_Mart%C3%ADn" title="Quique Martín">Quique Martín</a> was arguably the most important player of the club in the decade, whilst Argentine <a href="/wiki/Jorge_D%27Alessandro" title="Jorge D'Alessandro">Jorge D'Alessandro</a>, who held the record for most games in the top division in the club's history, worked with the team as a manager in a further two spells (three in total).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2010–11 Segunda División">2010–11</a> brought a club record ten consecutive defeats between December 2010/February 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and two coaching changes, as Salamanca returned to the third division after five years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On 18 June 2013, 90 years after its foundation, the club was liquidated due to the accumulation of unpaid debts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
