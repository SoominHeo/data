<p>The <b>1997 UEFA Super Cup</b> was a two-legged <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> match played on 8 January 1998 and 11 March 1998 between <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, winner of the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_UEFA_Champions_League" title="1996–97 UEFA Champions League">1996–97 UEFA Champions League</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, winner of the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="1996–97 UEFA Cup Winners' Cup">1996–97 UEFA Cup Winners' Cup</a>. Barcelona won the match 3–1 on aggregate, beating Borussia Dortmund 2–0 at <a href="/wiki/Camp_Nou" title="Camp Nou">Camp Nou</a> in the first leg and drawing the second leg 1–1 in <a href="/wiki/Westfalenstadion" title="Westfalenstadion">Westfalenstadion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund">Dortmund</a>.</p>
<p>This was the last Super Cup played over two legs. Since 1998, it has been played as a single match at a neutral venue.</p>
