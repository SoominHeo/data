<p>The <b>final of UEFA Euro 1984</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> match played on 27 June 1984 at <a href="/wiki/Parc_des_Princes" title="Parc des Princes">Parc des Princes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> to determine the winner of <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1984" title="UEFA Euro 1984">UEFA Euro 1984</a>. The match featured tournament hosts, <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, who went into the match as favourites and arguably the best team in Europe,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>, who qualified for the final tournament with a highly unlikely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spain_12-1_Malta" title="Spain 12-1 Malta">12-1 win</a> over <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a>.</p>
<p>France midfielder <a href="/wiki/Michel_Platini" title="Michel Platini">Michel Platini</a> opened the scoring in the 57th minute with his ninth goal of the tournament, a low free-kick which spilled through the hands of the diving Spanish keeper <a href="/wiki/Luis_Arconada" title="Luis Arconada">Luis Arconada</a> and over the goal line. <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Bellone" title="Bruno Bellone">Bruno Bellone</a> doubled France's lead in the final minute with a left footed clip over the advancing goalkeeper to give them a 2–0 victory. Bellone's goal was the only one of France's fourteen goals to be scored by a striker.</p>
