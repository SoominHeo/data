<p>The <b>UGM-96 Trident I</b>, or <b>Trident C4</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Submarine-launched_ballistic_missile" title="Submarine-launched ballistic missile">submarine-launched ballistic missile</a>, built by <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin_Space_Systems" title="Lockheed Martin Space Systems">Lockheed Martin Space Systems</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sunnyvale,_California" title="Sunnyvale, California">Sunnyvale, California</a>. First deployed in 1979, the Trident I replaced the <a href="/wiki/UGM-73_Poseidon" title="UGM-73 Poseidon">Poseidon</a> missile. It was retired in 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> having been replaced by the <a href="/wiki/UGM-133_Trident_II" title="UGM-133 Trident II">Trident II</a>. In 1980, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> requested Trident I missiles under the <a href="/wiki/Polaris_Sales_Agreement" title="Polaris Sales Agreement">Polaris Sales Agreement</a>; in 1982, this was changed to Trident IIs. It was the first <a href="/wiki/Trident_(missile)" title="Trident (missile)">Trident</a> missile to enter service.</p>
<p>The Trident I is a three-stage, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solid-fuel_rocket" title="Solid-fuel rocket">solid-fuelled</a> missile.</p>
<p>The first eight <a href="/wiki/Ohio-class_submarine" title="Ohio-class submarine"><i>Ohio</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarines</a> were armed with Trident I missiles. Twelve <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Madison_class_submarine" title="James Madison class submarine"><i>James Madison</i></a>- and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin_class_submarine" title="Benjamin Franklin class submarine"><i>Benjamin Franklin</i>-class</a> submarines were also retrofitted with Trident I missiles, which replaced older <a href="/wiki/UGM-73_Poseidon" title="UGM-73 Poseidon">Poseidon</a> missiles.</p>
