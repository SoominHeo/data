<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">UNOVIS</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Black_square.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="360" data-file-width="361" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Black_square.jpg/220px-Black_square.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Black_square.jpg/330px-Black_square.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/99/Black_square.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Black_square.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The seal of UNOVIS; Kazimir Malevich's <i>Black square</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p><b>UNOVIS</b> (also known as <b>MOLPOSNOVIS</b> and <b>POSNOVIS</b>) was a short-lived but influential group of artists, founded and led by <a href="/wiki/Kazimir_Malevich" title="Kazimir Malevich">Kazimir Malevich</a> at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vitebsk_Art_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vitebsk Art School (page does not exist)">Vitebsk Art School</a> in 1919.</p>
<p>Initially formed by students and known as MOLPOSNOVIS, the group formed to explore and develop new theories and concepts in art. Under the leadership of Malevich they renamed to UNOVIS, chiefly focusing on his ideas on <a href="/wiki/Suprematism" title="Suprematism">Suprematism</a> and producing a number of projects and publications whose influence on the <a href="/wiki/Russian_avant-garde" title="Russian avant-garde">avant-garde in Russia</a> and abroad was immediate and far-reaching.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schickerart_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-schickerart-1">[1]</a></sup> The group disbanded in 1922.</p>
<p>The name UNOVIS is an <a href="/wiki/Abbreviation" title="Abbreviation">abbreviation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> of "Utverditeli Novovo Iskusstva" or "The Champions of the New Art", while POSNOVIS was an abbreviation of "Posledovateli Novovo Iskusstva" or "Followers of the New Art", and MOLPOSNOVIS meant "Young Followers of the New Art."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation_and_growth">Foundation and growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNOVIS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Foundation and growth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="687" data-file-width="490" height="280" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg/200px-UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg/300px-UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg/400px-UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:UNOVIS_group_photo_1920.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A photo of UNOVIS members, with Malevich in the center</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In its short history, the group underwent many changes. First founded as MOLPOSNOVIS, the group's membership started to include some of the school's professors and quickly evolved into POSNOVIS. The group was very active, working on numerous projects and experiments, in most if not all <a href="/wiki/Multimedia" title="Multimedia">media</a> available at the time. In January 1920, Malevich was invited to teach at the school in 1919 by <a href="/wiki/Marc_Chagall" title="Marc Chagall">Marc Chagall</a> and immediately appointed by the director of the school at the time, <a href="/wiki/Vera_Ermolaeva" title="Vera Ermolaeva">Vera Ermolaeva</a>, to head a teaching studio. In February of the same year, under the leadership of Malevich, the group worked on a "Suprematist ballet", choreographed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nina_Kogan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nina Kogan (page does not exist)">Nina Kogan</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9A%D0%BE%D0%B3%D0%B0%D0%BD,_%D0%9D%D0%B8%D0%BD%D0%B0_%D0%98%D0%BE%D1%81%D0%B8%D1%84%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%BD%D0%B0" title="ru:Коган, Нина Иосифовна">ru</a>)</span>, and the precursor to <a href="/wiki/Aleksei_Kruchenykh" title="Aleksei Kruchenykh">Aleksei Kruchenykh</a>'s influential <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Futurism_(art)" title="Futurism (art)">futurist</a> opera, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victory_Over_the_Sun" title="Victory Over the Sun">Victory Over the Sun</a>.</i> Following the production, POSNOVIS underwent more changes and was renamed UNOVIS on February 14, 1920.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_and_influence">Expansion and influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNOVIS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Expansion and influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In early 1920, Marc Chagall selected Malevich to succeed him as director. Malevich accepted and radically reorganized not only UNOVIS but the entire school's curriculum. He transformed UNOVIS into a highly structured organization, forming the UNOVIS Council. Meanwhile, the group's theories and styles were rapidly evolving at the hands of Malevich and his star students and colleagues, including notable Russian artists <a href="/wiki/El_Lissitzky" title="El Lissitzky">El Lissitzky</a>, Lazar Khidekel, <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Suetin" title="Nikolai Suetin">Nikolai Suetin</a>, [Ilia Chashnik], [Vera Ermolaeva], <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anna_Kagan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anna Kagan (page does not exist)">Anna Kagan</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lev_Yudin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lev Yudin (page does not exist)">Lev Yudin</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%AE%D0%B4%D0%B8%D0%BD,_%D0%9B%D0%B5%D0%B2_%D0%90%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%80%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87" title="ru:Юдин, Лев Александрович">ru</a>)</span>, amongst others. The group's objective was now to introduce <a href="/wiki/Suprematism" title="Suprematism">Suprematist</a> designs and ideals to Russian society, working with and for the Soviet government:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>...organization of design work for new types of useful structures and requirements, and implementation; the formulation of tasks of new architecture; elaboration of new ornamentation (textiles, printed textiles, castings and other products); designs of monumental decorations for use in the embellishment of towns on national holidays; designs for internal and external decoration and painting of accommodation, and implementation; creation of furniture and all objects of practical use; creation of a contemporary type of book and other achievements in the field of printing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schickerart_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-schickerart-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p><br/>
The group took this plan to the streets, furnishing much of Vitebsk in Suprematist art and propaganda. Still, Malevich had more ambitious plans and he urged his students to do bigger, more permanent works—namely architecture. El Lissitzky, who was director of the architectural faculty, worked with Lazar Khidekel and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ilia_Chashnik" title="Ilia Chashnik">Ilia Chashnik</a>, a young students of his, drafting unorthodox plans for free-floating buildings and enormous steel and glass structures along with more practical designs for housing complexes and even a speaker's podium for the town square. Ilia would go on to succeed Lissitzky as head of the architectural facility along with his fellow student, <a href="/wiki/Lazar_Khidekel" title="Lazar Khidekel">Lazar Khidekel</a>.</p>
<p>Embracing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist" title="Communist">Communist</a> ideal, the group chose to share credit and responsibility for all works produced. They signed all works with a solitary black square, a <a href="/wiki/Homage_(arts)" title="Homage (arts)">homage</a> to a previous artwork by Malevich. This would become the de facto <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seal_(device)" title="Seal (device)">seal</a> of UNOVIS and took the place of individual names or initials.</p>
<p>In June 1920 UNOVIS's ambitions accelerated, culminating in a print collection of UNOVIS philosophies and theories such as Lazar Khidekel and Ilya Chashnik, АERO: Articles and Designs (Vitebsk, UNOVIS. 1920), and participation in the "First All-Russian Conference of Teachers and Students of Arts," which took place in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>. UNOVIS students who made the trip from Vitebsk to Moscow rapidly distributed artworks, newsletters, manifestos, flyers, and copies of Malevich's "On New Systems in Art" and copies of the "UNOVIS Almanac." UNOVIS succeeded in achieving recognition and became respected as an established and influential movement.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dissolution_and_legacy">Dissolution and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNOVIS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dissolution and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="606" data-file-width="963" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg/220px-%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg/330px-%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg/440px-%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:%D0%A3%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%81_1922.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

</div>
</div>
<p>While their influence on art lasted for generations, their popularity immediately following the conference was short-lived. By 1922, the core group splintered and two contrasting, adverse factions formed. Malevich and his followers championed practical, productive methods of changing society while the rest favored a more philosophical Suprematism, working on furthering Suprematist theory and ideology. By this time most of the native artists associated with UNOVIS had moved on to other schools, cities, and movements. Even after the group's dissolution, publications bearing the UNOVIS black square appeared for years.</p>
