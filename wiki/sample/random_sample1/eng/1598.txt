<p>The <b>United Soccer Leagues Second Division</b> (commonly referred to as <b>USL-2</b>) was a professional men's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a> league in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Soccer_Leagues" title="United Soccer Leagues">United Soccer Leagues (USL)</a> league <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Soccer_Pyramid" title="American Soccer Pyramid">pyramid</a>. It was at the third tier of soccer in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, behind <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> (top division) and the <a href="/wiki/USL_First_Division" title="USL First Division">USL First Division</a> (second division), and one step up from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Premier_Development_League" title="USL Premier Development League">USL Premier Development League</a> and the other leagues at Level 4.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USL_Second_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1995 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USISL" title="USISL">United States Interregional Soccer League</a> (USISL), the de facto second tier of American soccer at the time, changed its name to the <b>United States International Soccer League</b>, and split into two leagues, one professional and one amateur. The professional league, initially called the <b>USISL Pro League</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>-sanctioned Division 3 league, while the amateur league, (the 'Premier League'), was given Division 4 status and would later go on to become the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Premier_Development_League" title="USL Premier Development League">USL Premier Development League</a>. The first champions of the new USISL Pro League were the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Rough_Riders" title="Long Island Rough Riders">Long Island Rough Riders</a>, who beat <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Thunder" title="Minnesota Thunder">Minnesota Thunder</a> 2-1 in the championship game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1996, the USISL established a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USISL_Select_League" title="USISL Select League">USISL Select League</a>. The strongest USISL Pro League teams joined this new league, which was given Division 2 status alongside the existing <a href="/wiki/A-League_(1995%E2%80%932004)" title="A-League (1995–2004)">A-League</a>, while the remainder of the teams (plus expansion teams) remained at Division 3 level. <a href="/wiki/Charleston_Battery" title="Charleston Battery">Charleston Battery</a> became the league's second champions in this year, beating the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Eagles" title="Charlotte Eagles">Charlotte Eagles</a> in a penalty shootout in the 1996 USISL Pro League championship game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1997 the league changed its name to the <b>USISL D-3 Pro League</b> to further distinguish itself from the A-League, and then in 1999 the umbrella USISL changed its name to the United Soccer Leagues (USL), and as such the Pro League officially became known as the <b>USL D3 Pro League</b>. In 2003 the name was changed again to the <b>USL Pro Select League</b>, but during the season had to be changed to the <b>USL Pro Soccer League</b> for to legal reasons. In 2005 the league took its final name as the <b>USL Second Division</b>.</p>
<p>The USL Second Division dissolved following the 2010 season to make way for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Pro" title="USL Pro">USL Pro</a>, which combined the existing USL First and Second divisions and began play in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
