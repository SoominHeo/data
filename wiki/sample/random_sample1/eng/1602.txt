<p><b>USS <i>Marlin</i> (SST-2)</b>, originally <b>USS <i>T-2</i> (SST-2)</b>, was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T-1_class_submarine" title="T-1 class submarine"><i>T-1</i>-class</a> training <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a> in commission from 1953 to 1973. She was the second submarine of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> to be named for the <a href="/wiki/Marlin" title="Marlin">marlin</a>, a large game fish. She was one of the smallest operational submarines ever built for the U.S. Navy.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_and_commissioning">Construction and commissioning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Marlin_(SST-2)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Construction and commissioning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>T-2</i> was laid down on 1 May 1952 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_Boat" title="Electric Boat">Electric Boat</a> Division of <a href="/wiki/General_Dynamics" title="General Dynamics">General Dynamics</a> Corporation in <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>. She was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_naming_and_launching" title="Ship naming and launching">launched</a> on 14 October 1953, sponsored by Mrs. William R. DeLoach, and <a href="/wiki/Ship_commissioning" title="Ship commissioning">commissioned</a> as USS <i>T-2</i> on 20 November 1953 with <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">Lieutenant</a> Edward Holt in command.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_history">Service history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Marlin_(SST-2)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Service history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Shakedown_(testing)" title="Shakedown (testing)">shakedown</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay" title="Massachusetts Bay">Massachusetts Bay</a> area, <i>T-2</i>, got underway from <a href="/wiki/New_London,_Connecticut" title="New London, Connecticut">New London</a>, Connecticut, in late January 1954 for her <a href="/wiki/Home_port" title="Home port">home port</a> at <b>Naval Station Key West</b> at <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">Key West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. Assigned to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Submarine_Squadron_12&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Submarine Squadron 12 (page does not exist)">Submarine Squadron 12</a>, Submarine Force, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Atlantic_Fleet" title="United States Atlantic Fleet">United States Atlantic Fleet</a>, she immediately began operations in the sector from southern <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Bay,_Cuba" title="Guantánamo Bay, Cuba">Guantánamo Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. For a decade and a half she performed valuable service as a target and training ship and helped to evaluate submarine and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antisubmarine_warfare" title="Antisubmarine warfare">antisubmarine warfare</a> equipment and tactics.</p>
<p>Renamed <i>Marlin</i> on 15 May 1956, the submarine deployed to Guantanamo Bay for services to the Fleet Training Group in January 1956, July and August 1958, March 1960, and December 1961. In March 1963, <i>Marlin</i> participated in the <a href="/wiki/National_Defense_Industrial_Association" title="National Defense Industrial Association">National Defense Industrial Association</a> (NDIA) demonstration.</p>
<p>From 1963, <i>Marlin</i> performed mainly target duty for both surface and air antisubmarine units at the <a href="/wiki/Fleet_Sonar_School" title="Fleet Sonar School">Fleet Sonar School</a> at Key West.</p>
<p>In addition to target and training duty, <i>Marlin</i> participated in various fleet operations. From 7 March 1965 to 4 April 1965, for example, <i>Marlin</i> joined her <a href="/wiki/Sister_ship" title="Sister ship">sister ship</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Mackerel_(SST-1)" title="USS Mackerel (SST-1)">USS <i>Mackerel</i> (SST-1)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Attack_submarine" title="Attack submarine">attack submarines</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Amberjack_(SS-522)" title="USS Amberjack (SS-522)">USS <i>Amberjack</i> (SS-522)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Batfish_(SS-310)" title="USS Batfish (SS-310)">USS <i>Batfish</i> (SS-310)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/USS_Chivo_(SS-341)" title="USS Chivo (SS-341)">USS <i>Chivo</i> (SS-341)</a>, along with a task force under Commander, Mine Force, in participating in <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">mine warfare</a> maneuvers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decommissioning">Decommissioning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Marlin_(SST-2)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Decommissioning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Marlin</i> and her sister ship <i>Mackerel</i> were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_decommissioning" title="Ship decommissioning">decommissioned</a> on 31 January 1973 in a dual ceremony at Naval Station Key West. Both also were stricken from the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Vessel_Register" title="Naval Vessel Register">Naval Vessel Register</a> that day.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Museum_ship">Museum ship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Marlin_(SST-2)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Museum ship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 15 August 1973, the U.S. Navy donated <i>Marlin</i> for use as a <a href="/wiki/Museum_ship" title="Museum ship">museum ship</a>. On 20 August 1974, she was assigned for display as a memorial submarine in <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Park_(Omaha,_Nebraska)" title="Freedom Park (Omaha, Nebraska)">Freedom Park</a> at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Omaha_Marina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Greater Omaha Marina (page does not exist)">Greater Omaha Marina</a> at 2000 North 25th Street, in <a href="/wiki/Omaha,_Nebraska" title="Omaha, Nebraska">Omaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>.</p>
