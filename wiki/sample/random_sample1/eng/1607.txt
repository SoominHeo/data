<p><b>USS <i>Hornet</i> (CV-8)</b>, the seventh ship to carry the name <i>Hornet</i>, was a <a href="/wiki/Yorktown-class_aircraft_carrier" title="Yorktown-class aircraft carrier"><i>Yorktown</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>. During World War II in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific Theater</a>, she launched the <a href="/wiki/Doolittle_Raid" title="Doolittle Raid">Doolittle Raid</a> on Tokyo and participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a> and the Buin-Faisi-Tonolai Raid. In the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands_campaign" title="Solomon Islands campaign">Solomon Islands campaign</a> she was involved in the capture and defense of <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Santa_Cruz_Islands" title="Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands">Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands</a> where she was irreparably damaged by enemy torpedo and dive bombers. Faced with an approaching Japanese surface force, "Hornet" was abandoned and later torpedoed and sunk by approaching Japanese destroyers. <i>Hornet</i> was in service for a year and six days and was the last US <a href="/wiki/Fleet_carrier" title="Fleet carrier">fleet carrier</a> ever sunk by enemy fire. For these actions, she was awarded four service stars, a citation for the <a href="/wiki/Doolittle_Raid" title="Doolittle Raid">Doolittle Raid</a> in 1995, and her Torpedo Squadron 8 received a Presidential Unit Citation for extraordinary heroism for the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a>.</p>

</div>
<p><i>Hornet</i> had a length of 770 feet (235 m) at the <a href="/wiki/Waterline_length" title="Waterline length">waterline</a> and 824 feet 9 inches (251.38 m) <a href="/wiki/Length_overall" title="Length overall">overall</a>. She had a <a href="/wiki/Beam_(nautical)" title="Beam (nautical)">beam</a> of 83 feet 3 inches (25.37 m) at the waterline, 114 feet (35 m) overall, with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Draft_(ship)" title="Draft (ship)">draft</a> of 24 feet 4 inches (7.42 m) as designed and 28 feet (8.5 m) at full load. She <a href="/wiki/Displacement_(ship)" title="Displacement (ship)">displaced</a> 20,000 <a href="/wiki/Long_ton" title="Long ton">long tons</a> (20,000 <a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">t</a>) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_displacement#Standard_displacement" title="Standard displacement">standard load</a> and 25,500 long tons (25,900 t) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Full-load_displacement" title="Full-load displacement">full load</a>. She was designed for a ship's crew consisting of 86 officers and 1280 men and an <a href="/wiki/Carrier_air_wing" title="Carrier air wing">air complement</a> consisting of 141 officers and 710 men.</p>
<p>She was powered by nine <a href="/wiki/Babcock_%26_Wilcox" title="Babcock &amp; Wilcox">Babcock &amp; Wilcox</a> <a href="/wiki/Boiler" title="Boiler">boilers</a> providing steam at 400 <a href="/wiki/Pounds_per_square_inch" title="Pounds per square inch">psi</a> (2,800 <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">kPa</a>) and 648 °F (342 °C) to four <a href="/wiki/Parsons_Marine_Steam_Turbine_Company" title="Parsons Marine Steam Turbine Company">Parsons Marine</a> geared <a href="/wiki/Steam_turbine" title="Steam turbine">steam turbines</a> each driving its own <a href="/wiki/Propeller" title="Propeller">propeller</a>. The turbines were designed to produce a total of 120,000 <a href="/wiki/Horsepower#Shaft_horsepower" title="Horsepower">shaft horsepower</a> [shp] (89,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kilowatt" title="Kilowatt">kW</a>) giving her a range of 12,000 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (14,000 mi; 22,000 km) at a speed of 15 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (17 mph; 28 km/h). She was designed to carry 4,280 long tons (4,350 t) of fuel oil and 178,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_gallon" title="US gallon">US gallons</a> (670,000 <a href="/wiki/Litre" title="Litre">l</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Avgas" title="Avgas">Avgas</a>. Her designed speed was 32.5 knots (60.2 km/h; 37.4 mph). During <a href="/wiki/Sea_trial" title="Sea trial">sea trials</a>, she produced 120,500 shp (89,900 kW) and reached 33.85 knots (62.69 km/h; 38.95 mph).</p>
<p><i>Hornet</i> was equipped with eight <a href="/wiki/5%22/38_caliber_gun" title='5"/38 caliber gun'>5-inch (127 mm)/38 caliber</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dual_purpose_gun" title="Dual purpose gun">dual purpose guns</a> and 16 <a href="/wiki/1.1%22/75_caliber_gun" title='1.1"/75 caliber gun'>1.1-inch (28 mm)/75 caliber</a> <a href="/wiki/Anti-aircraft_warfare" title="Anti-aircraft warfare">anti-aircraft guns</a> in quad mounts (four guns operating together). Originally, she had 24 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M2_Browning_machine_gun" title="M2 Browning machine gun">M2 Browning .50-inch (12.7 mm)</a> machine guns but these were replaced in January 1942 with 30 <a href="/wiki/Oerlikon_20_mm_cannon" title="Oerlikon 20 mm cannon">20-mm Oerlikon</a> anti-aircraft cannon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983392_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983392-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup> An additional 1.1-inch (28 mm) quad mount was later added at her bow and two more 20 mm anti-aircraft guns were added for a total of 32 mounts. In addition, her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athwartships" title="Athwartships">athwartships</a> hangar-deck <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_catapult" title="Aircraft catapult">aircraft catapult</a> was removed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92-3">[3]</a></sup> In June 1942, following the battle of Midway, <i>Hornet</i> had a new CXAM <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a> installed atop her tripod mast, and her SC radar was relocated to her mainmast. Unlike her sisters, Hornet's tripod mast and its signal bridge were not enclosed when the CXAM was installed, making her unique among the three ships.</p>
<p>For armor, she had an <a href="/wiki/Belt_armor" title="Belt armor">armor belt</a> of 30-pound (14 kg) <a href="/wiki/Special_treatment_steel" title="Special treatment steel">special treatment steel</a> (STS) that was 2.5 to 4 inches (64–102 mm) thick. The flight and hangar <a href="/wiki/Deck_(ship)" title="Deck (ship)">decks</a> had no armor but the protective deck had 4 inches (100 mm) of 60-pound (27 kg) STS. Bulkheads had 4-inch (100 mm) armor while the <a href="/wiki/Conning_tower" title="Conning tower">conning tower</a> had 30–16 mm splinter pro armor 4 inches (100 mm) on the sides with 2 inches (51 mm) on top. The steering gear had 4-inch (100 mm) protection on the sides with 60–16 mm on the deck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983392_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983392-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her <a href="/wiki/Flight_deck" title="Flight deck">flight deck</a> was 814 by 86 feet (248 m × 26 m) and her hangar deck was 546 by 63 feet (166 m × 19 m) and 17 feet 3 inches (5.26 m) high. She had three aircraft <a href="/wiki/Elevator#Elevators_for_aircraft" title="Elevator">elevators</a> each 48 by 44 feet (15 by 13 m) with a lifting capacity of 17,000 pounds (7,700 kg). She had two flight-deck and one hangar-deck hydraulic catapults and equipped with Mark IV Mod 3A <a href="/wiki/Arresting_gear" title="Arresting gear">arresting gear</a> with a capability of 16,000 pounds (7,300 kg) and 85 miles per hour (137 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983381_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983381-4">[4]</a></sup> She was designed to host a <a href="/wiki/Carrier_air_wing#World_War_II" title="Carrier air wing">Carrier Air Group</a> of 18 <a href="/wiki/Fighter_aircraft" title="Fighter aircraft">fighters</a>, 18 <a href="/wiki/Bomber" title="Bomber">bombers</a>, 37 <a href="/wiki/Scout_plane" title="Scout plane">scout planes</a>, 18 <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_bomber" title="Torpedo bomber">torpedo bombers</a>, and 6 <a href="/wiki/Utility_aircraft" title="Utility aircraft">utility aircraft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983392_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFriedman1983392-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENavsource.org_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENavsource.org-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Hornet</i> was laid down on 25 September 1939 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northrop_Grumman_Newport_News" title="Northrop Grumman Newport News">Newport News Shipbuilding</a> of <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">Newport News, Virginia</a> and was launched on 14 December 1940, sponsored by Annie Reid Knox, wife of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_the_Navy" title="Secretary of the Navy">Secretary of the Navy</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frank_M._Knox" title="Frank M. Knox">Frank M. Knox</a>. She was commissioned at <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Norfolk" title="Naval Station Norfolk">Naval Station Norfolk</a> on 20 October 1941, with Captain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marc_A._Mitscher" title="Marc A. Mitscher">Marc A. Mitscher</a> in command.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENavsource.org_Commanding_Officers_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENavsource.org_Commanding_Officers-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose19955.2C6.2C10_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose19955.2C6.2C10-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_history">Service history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Hornet_(CV-8)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Service history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the period before the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a>, <i>Hornet</i> trained out of Norfolk. A hint of a future mission occurred on 2 February 1942, when <i>Hornet</i> departed Norfolk with two <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">Army Air Forces</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-25_Mitchell" title="B-25 Mitchell">B-25 Mitchell</a> <a href="/wiki/Medium_bomber" title="Medium bomber">medium bombers</a> on deck. Once at sea, the planes were launched to the surprise and amazement of <i>Hornet</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s crew. Her men were unaware of the meaning of this experiment, as <i>Hornet</i> returned to Norfolk, prepared to leave for combat, and on 4 March sailed for the West Coast via the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199538-39.2C41_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199538-39.2C41-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doolittle_Raid.2C_April_1942">Doolittle Raid, April 1942</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Hornet_(CV-8)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Doolittle Raid, April 1942">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Doolittle_Raid" title="Doolittle Raid">Doolittle Raid</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Army_B-25_(Doolittle_Raid).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2156" data-file-width="2945" height="161" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Army_B-25_%28Doolittle_Raid%29.jpg/220px-Army_B-25_%28Doolittle_Raid%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Army_B-25_%28Doolittle_Raid%29.jpg/330px-Army_B-25_%28Doolittle_Raid%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Army_B-25_%28Doolittle_Raid%29.jpg/440px-Army_B-25_%28Doolittle_Raid%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Army_B-25_(Doolittle_Raid).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A B-25 takes off from <i>Hornet</i>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Hornet</i> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Alameda" title="Naval Air Station Alameda">Naval Air Station Alameda</a>, California on 20 March 1942.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199542_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199542-9">[9]</a></sup> With her own planes on the hangar deck, by midafternoon on 1 April she loaded 16 B-25s on the flight deck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199552_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199552-10">[10]</a></sup> Under the command of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Colonel" title="Lieutenant Colonel">Lieutenant Colonel</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_H._Doolittle" title="James H. Doolittle">James H. Doolittle</a>, 70 <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Corps" title="United States Army Air Corps">United States Army Air Corps</a> officers and 64 enlisted men reported aboard. In company of her escort, <i>Hornet</i> departed Alameda on 2 April<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199552_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199552-10">[10]</a></sup> under sealed orders. That afternoon, Captain Mitscher informed his men of their mission: <a href="/wiki/Doolittle_Raid" title="Doolittle Raid">a bombing raid on Japan</a>.</p>
<p>Eleven days later, <i>Hornet</i> joined the aircraft carrier <a href="/wiki/USS_Enterprise_(CV-6)" title="USS Enterprise (CV-6)"><i>Enterprise</i></a> off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midway_Island" title="Midway Island">Midway</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Task_Force_16" title="Task Force 16">Task Force 16</a> turned toward Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199562_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199562-11">[11]</a></sup> With <i>Enterprise</i> providing combat air patrol cover, <i>Hornet</i> was to steam deep into enemy waters. Originally, the task force intended to proceed to within 400 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nmi</a> (460 mi; 740 km) of the Japanese coast; however, on the morning of 18 April, a Japanese patrol boat, <i>No. 23 Nitto Maru</i>, sighted the American task force. <a href="/wiki/USS_Nashville_(CL-43)" title="USS Nashville (CL-43)"><i>Nashville</i></a> sank the patrol boat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199565-71_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199565-71-12">[12]</a></sup> Amid concerns that the Japanese had been made aware of their presence, Doolittle and his raiders were forced to launch prematurely from 600 nmi (690 mi; 1,100 km) out instead of the planned 400 nmi (460 mi; 740 km). Because of this decision, none of the 16 planes made it to their designated landing strip in China. After the war, it was found that Tokyo received the <i>Nitto Maru'</i>s message in a garbled form and that the Japanese ship was sunk before it could get a clear message through to the Japanese mainland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199571_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199571-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>As <i>Hornet</i> came about and prepared to launch the bombers which had been readied for take-off the previous day, a gale of more than 40 kn (46 mph; 74 km/h) churned the sea with 30 ft (9.1 m) crests; heavy swells, which caused the ship to pitch violently, shipped sea and spray over the bow, wet the flight deck and drenched the deck crews. The lead plane, commanded by Colonel Doolittle, had only 467 ft (142 m) of flight deck, while the last B-25 hung its twin rudders far out over the fantail. Doolittle, timing himself against the rise and fall of the ship's bow, lumbered down the flight deck, circled <i>Hornet</i> after take-off, and set course for Japan. By 09:20, all 16 were airborne, heading for the first American air strike against the Japanese home islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199565-71_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199565-71-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Hornet</i> brought her own planes on deck as Task Force 16 steamed at full speed for Pearl Harbor. Intercepted broadcasts, both in Japanese and English, confirmed at 14:46 the success of the raids. Exactly one week to the hour after launching the B-25s, <i>Hornet</i> sailed into Pearl Harbor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199577_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199577-14">[14]</a></sup> That the Tokyo raid was the <i>Hornet</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s mission was kept an official secret for a year; until then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt">President Roosevelt</a> referred to the ship from which the bombers were launched only as "<a href="/wiki/Shangri-La" title="Shangri-La">Shangri-La</a>". Two years later, the Navy would give this name to <a href="/wiki/USS_Shangri-La_(CV-38)" title="USS Shangri-La (CV-38)">an aircraft carrier</a>.</p>
<p><i>Hornet</i> steamed from Pearl Harbor on 30 April to aid <a href="/wiki/USS_Yorktown_(CV-5)" title="USS Yorktown (CV-5)"><i>Yorktown</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Lexington_(CV-2)" title="USS Lexington (CV-2)"><i>Lexington</i></a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199581-82_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199581-82-15">[15]</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Coral_Sea" title="Battle of the Coral Sea">Battle of the Coral Sea</a>, but the battle ended before she reached the scene. On 4 May Task Force 16 crossed the equator, the first time ever for <i>Hornet</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199590_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199590-16">[16]</a></sup> After executing, with <i>Enterprise</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Feint" title="Feint">feint</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Nauru" title="Nauru">Nauru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banaba_Island" title="Banaba Island">Banaba</a> (Ocean) islands which caused the Japanese to cancel their <a href="/wiki/Operation_RY" title="Operation RY">operation to seize the two islands</a>, she returned to Hawaii on 26 May,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199597_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199597-17">[17]</a></sup> and sailed two days later to help repulse an expected Japanese assault on <a href="/wiki/Midway_Atoll" title="Midway Atoll">Midway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Midway.2C_June_1942">Battle of Midway, June 1942</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Hornet_(CV-8)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Battle of Midway, June 1942">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a></div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_United_States_Naval_Operations_in_World_War_II" title="History of United States Naval Operations in World War II">History of United States Naval Operations in World War II</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="615" data-file-width="800" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg/220px-SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg/330px-SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg/440px-SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SBDs_and_Mikuma.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
SBDs from <i>Hornet</i> at Midway</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On 28 May 1942, <i>Hornet</i> and Task Force 16 steamed out of Pearl Harbor heading for Point "Luck", an arbitrary spot in the ocean roughly 325 miles (523 km) northeast of Midway, where they would be in a flank position to ambush Japan's mobile strike force of four frontline aircraft carriers, the <a href="/wiki/1st_Air_Fleet_(Imperial_Japanese_Navy)#Kid.C5.8D_Butai" title="1st Air Fleet (Imperial Japanese Navy)"><i>Kidō Butai</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199549.2C_110.E2.80.93111_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199549.2C_110.E2.80.93111-18">[18]</a></sup> Japanese carrier-based planes were reported headed for Midway in the early morning of 4 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose1995120-122_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose1995120-122-19">[19]</a></sup> <i>Hornet</i>, <i>Yorktown</i>, and <i>Enterprise</i> launched aircraft, just as the Japanese carriers struck their planes below to prepare for a second attack on Midway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose1995125_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose1995125-20">[20]</a></sup> <i>Hornet</i> <a href="/wiki/Dive_bomber" title="Dive bomber">dive bombers</a> followed an incorrect heading and did not find the enemy fleet. Several bombers and all of the escorting fighters were forced to ditch when they ran out of fuel attempting to return to the ship. Fifteen torpedo bombers of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torpedo_Squadron_8" title="Torpedo Squadron 8">Torpedo Squadron 8</a> (VT-8) found the Japanese ships and attacked. They were met by overwhelming fighter opposition about eight nautical miles (9 mi; 15 km) out, and with no escorts to protect them, they were shot down one by one. <a href="/wiki/Ensign_(rank)" title="Ensign (rank)">Ensign</a> <a href="/wiki/George_H._Gay,_Jr." title="George H. Gay, Jr.">George H. Gay</a>, USNR, was the only survivor of 30 men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose1995128-132.2C_146.E2.80.93149_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose1995128-132.2C_146.E2.80.93149-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMitscher13_June_1942_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitscher13_June_1942-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Further attacks by <i>Enterprise</i> and <i>Yorktown</i> torpedo planes proved equally disastrous, but succeeded in forcing the Japanese carriers to keep their decks clear for combat air patrol operations, rather than spotting a counter-attack against the Americans. Japanese fighters were shooting down the last of the torpedo planes over <a href="/wiki/Japanese_aircraft_carrier_Hiry%C5%AB" title="Japanese aircraft carrier Hiryū"><i>Hiryū</i></a> when dive bombers of <i>Enterprise</i> and <i>Yorktown</i> attacked, starting enormous fires aboard the three other Japanese carriers that led to their loss. <i>Hiryu</i> was hit late in the afternoon of 4 June by a strike from <i>Enterprise</i> and sank early the next morning. <i>Hornet</i> aircraft, launching late due to the necessity of recovering <i>Yorktown</i> scout planes and faulty communications, attacked a battleship and other escorts, but failed to score hits. <i>Yorktown</i> was lost to combined aerial and submarine attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199597-155_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199597-155-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Hornet</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s warplanes attacked the fleeing Japanese fleet on 6 June and they assisted in sinking the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_cruiser" title="Heavy cruiser">heavy cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Mikuma" title="Japanese cruiser Mikuma"><i>Mikuma</i></a>, damaging a <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a>, and leaving the heavy cruiser <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Mogami_(1934)" title="Japanese cruiser Mogami (1934)"><i>Mogami</i></a>, heavily damaged and on fire, to limp away from the battle zone. The attack by the Hornet's on the <i>Mogami</i> ended one of the great decisive battles of naval history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERose199597-155_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERose199597-155-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Midway_Atoll" title="Midway Atoll">Midway Atoll</a> was saved as an important base for American operations into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Pacific_Ocean" title="Western Pacific Ocean">Western Pacific Ocean</a>. Of greatest importance was the crippling of the Japanese carrier strength, a severe blow from which the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> never fully recovered. The four large carriers took with them to the bottom about 250 naval aircraft and a high percentage of the most highly trained and experienced Japanese aircraft maintenance personnel. The victory at Midway was a decisive turning point in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_in_the_Pacific" title="War in the Pacific">War in the Pacific</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 June 1942, Captain Charles Perry Mason became commanding officer of <i>Hornet</i> upon her return to <i>Pearl Harbor</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENavsource.org_Commanding_Officers_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENavsource.org_Commanding_Officers-6">[6]</a></sup> <i>Hornet</i> spent the next six weeks replenishing her stores, having minor repairs performed, and most importantly having additional light anti-aircraft guns and the new RCA CXAM air-search radar fitted. She did not sail in late July with the forces sent to re-capture <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a>, but instead remained at Pearl Harbor in case she was needed elsewhere.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solomons_campaign.2C_August.E2.80.93October.2C_1942">Solomons campaign, August–October, 1942</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Hornet_(CV-8)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Solomons campaign, August–October, 1942">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Hornet</i> steamed out of <a href="/wiki/Harbor" title="Harbor">harbor</a> on 17 August 1942 to guard the sea approaches to bitterly contested <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>. Bomb damage to <i>Enterprise</i> on 24 August, torpedo damage to <a href="/wiki/USS_Saratoga_(CV-3)" title="USS Saratoga (CV-3)"><i>Saratoga</i></a> on 31 August, and the sinking of <a href="/wiki/USS_Wasp_(CV-7)" title="USS Wasp (CV-7)"><i>Wasp</i></a> on 15 September left <i>Hornet</i> as the only operational U.S. carrier in the South Pacific. She was responsible for providing air cover over the Solomon Islands until 24 October 1942, when she was joined by <i>Enterprise</i> just northwest of the <a href="/wiki/New_Hebrides" title="New Hebrides">New Hebrides</a> Islands. These two carriers and their escorts then steamed out to intercept a Japanese aircraft carrier/<a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleship</a>/<a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> force closing in on Guadalcanal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_the_Santa_Cruz_Islands">Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Hornet_(CV-8)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Santa_Cruz_Islands" title="Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands">Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Santa_Cruz_Islands" title="Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands">Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands</a> took place on 26 October 1942 without contact between surface ships of the opposing forces. That morning, <i>Enterprise</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s planes bombed the carrier <a href="/wiki/Japanese_aircraft_carrier_Zuih%C5%8D" title="Japanese aircraft carrier Zuihō"><i>Zuihō</i></a>, while planes from <i>Hornet</i> severely damaged the carrier <a href="/wiki/Japanese_aircraft_carrier_Sh%C5%8Dkaku" title="Japanese aircraft carrier Shōkaku"><i>Shōkaku</i></a> and the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_cruiser" title="Heavy cruiser">heavy cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Chikuma_(1938)" title="Japanese cruiser Chikuma (1938)"><i>Chikuma</i></a>. Two other cruisers were also attacked by <i>Hornet</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s warplanes. Meanwhile, <i>Hornet</i> was attacked by a coordinated dive bomber and torpedo plane attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup> In a 15-minute period, <i>Hornet</i> was hit by three bombs from <a href="/wiki/Aichi_D3A" title="Aichi D3A">Aichi D3A</a> "Val" dive bombers. One "Val", after being heavily damaged by anti-aircraft fire while approaching <i>Hornet</i>, crashed into the carrier's island, killing seven men and spreading burning Avgas over the deck. Meanwhile, a flight of <a href="/wiki/Nakajima_B5N" title="Nakajima B5N">Nakajima B5N</a> "Kate" torpedo planes attacked <i>Hornet</i> and scored two hits, which seriously damaged the electrical systems and engines. As the carrier came to a halt, another damaged "Val" deliberately crashed into <i>Hornet'</i>s port side near the bow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>With power knocked out to her engines, <i>Hornet</i> was unable to launch or land aircraft, forcing its aviators to either land on <i>Enterprise</i> or ditch in the ocean. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rear_Admiral" title="Rear Admiral">Rear Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/George_D._Murray" title="George D. Murray">George D. Murray</a> ordered the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_cruiser" title="Heavy cruiser">heavy cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Northampton_(CA-26)" title="USS Northampton (CA-26)"><i>Northampton</i></a> to tow <i>Hornet</i> clear of the action. Since the Japanese planes were attacking <i>Enterprise</i>, this allowed <i>Northampton</i> to tow <i>Hornet</i> at a speed of about five knots (9 km/h; 6 mph). Repair crews were on the verge of restoring power when another flight of nine "Kate" torpedo planes attacked. Eight of these aircraft were either shot down or failed to score hits, but the ninth scored a fatal hit on the starboard side. The torpedo hit destroyed the repairs to the electrical system and caused a 14-degree list. After being informed that Japanese surface forces were approaching and that further towing efforts were futile, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Halsey,_Jr." title="William Halsey, Jr.">Vice Admiral William Halsey</a> ordered <i>Hornet</i> sunk, and an order of "abandon ship" was issued. Captain <a href="/wiki/Charles_P._Mason" title="Charles P. Mason">Charles P. Mason</a>, the last man on board, climbed over the side, and the survivors were soon picked up by the escorting <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECampbell201191-92-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>American warships next attempted to <a href="/wiki/Scuttling" title="Scuttling">scuttle</a> the stricken carrier, which absorbed nine torpedoes, many of which <a href="/wiki/Mark_14_torpedo#Problems" title="Mark 14 torpedo">failed to explode</a>, and more than 400 5-inch (130 mm) rounds from the destroyers <a href="/wiki/USS_Mustin_(DD-413)" title="USS Mustin (DD-413)"><i>Mustin</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Anderson_(DD-411)" title="USS Anderson (DD-411)"><i>Anderson</i></a>. The destroyers steamed away when a Japanese surface force entered the area. The Japanese destroyers <a href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Makigumo_(1942)" title="Japanese destroyer Makigumo (1942)"><i>Makigumo</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Akigumo" title="Japanese destroyer Akigumo"><i>Akigumo</i></a> finally finished off <i>Hornet</i> with four 24-inch (610 mm) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Lance_torpedo" title="Long Lance torpedo">Long Lance torpedoes</a>. At 01:35 on 27 October, <i>Hornet</i> was finally sunk with the loss of 140 of her sailors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHammel2005380_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHammel2005380-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Hornet</i> was struck from the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Vessel_Register" title="Naval Vessel Register">Naval Vessel Register</a> on 13 January 1943.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-8.29_vii-2">[2]</a></sup> However, her name was revived less than a year later when one of the newly constructed <a href="/wiki/Essex-class_aircraft_carrier" title="Essex-class aircraft carrier"><i>Essex</i>-class aircraft carrier</a> was commissioned as <a href="/wiki/USS_Hornet_(CV-12)" title="USS Hornet (CV-12)">USS <i>Hornet</i> (CV-12)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-12.29_viii_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHornet_.28CV-12.29_viii-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other than the <a href="/wiki/Independence-class_aircraft_carrier" title="Independence-class aircraft carrier">light carrier</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Princeton_(CVL-23)" title="USS Princeton (CVL-23)"><i>Princeton</i></a> and a number of smaller <a href="/wiki/Escort_carrier" title="Escort carrier">escort carriers</a>, <i>Hornet</i> was the last American carrier ever sunk by enemy fire.</p>

<p>In addition, Torpedo Squadron 8 flying from <i>Hornet</i> was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Unit_Citation_(United_States)" title="Presidential Unit Citation (United States)">Presidential Unit Citation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENavy_and_Marine_Corps_Awards_Manual_-_Part_II1953_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENavy_and_Marine_Corps_Awards_Manual_-_Part_II1953-28">[28]</a></sup> "for extraordinary heroism and distinguished service beyond the call of duty" during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a>.</p>
