<p><b>US Airways Group Inc.</b> was an <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airline</a> <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Tempe,_Arizona" title="Tempe, Arizona">Tempe, Arizona</a>. US Airways Group operated <a href="/wiki/US_Airways" title="US Airways">US Airways</a>, along with its subsidiaries <a href="/wiki/PSA_Airlines" title="PSA Airlines">PSA Airlines, Inc.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piedmont_Airlines" title="Piedmont Airlines">Piedmont Airlines, Inc.</a>, which are wholly owned but <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketed</a> under the branding of <a href="/wiki/US_Airways_Express" title="US Airways Express">US Airways Express</a>. It merged with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America_West_Holdings_Corporation" title="America West Holdings Corporation">America West Holdings Corporation</a>, parent of <a href="/wiki/America_West_Airlines" title="America West Airlines">America West Airlines</a>, in 2005, and the combined company adopted the better-known US Airways name; the two airlines' operating certificates merged in 2007. It also operates additional companies that provide associated services. <a href="/wiki/ACE_Aviation_Holdings" title="ACE Aviation Holdings">ACE Aviation Holdings</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> parent of <a href="/wiki/Air_Canada" title="Air Canada">Air Canada</a> retained a roughly 6.1% investment stake in US Airways Group. The route network covered destinations in 47 states, as well as international destinations.</p>
<p>The company merged with <a href="/wiki/AMR_Corporation" title="AMR Corporation">AMR Corporation</a>; the combined business began trading under the new name of <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines_Group" title="American Airlines Group">American Airlines Group</a> on December 9, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The combined airlines formed the largest airline in the world. US Airways' CEO, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Parker" title="Doug Parker">Doug Parker</a>, became CEO of the new company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Airways_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to merging with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America_West_Holdings_Corporation" title="America West Holdings Corporation">America West Holdings Corporation</a>, US Airways Group was based in <a href="/wiki/Arlington_County,_Virginia" title="Arlington County, Virginia">Arlington County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secf_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-secf-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2002, US Airways Group Inc. filed for bankruptcy protection in an effort to correct its financial problems brought on by rising costs and the post-Sept. 11 drop-off in air travel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 12, 2004, US Airways Group and most of its subsidiaries filed for <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a> protection for the second time in three years. US Airways Group and the aforementioned subsidiaries, including US Airways, emerged from Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection with the help $125 million equity investment from <a href="/wiki/Air_Wisconsin" title="Air Wisconsin">Air Wisconsin</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Parent_company" title="Parent company">parent company</a> Air Wisconsin Airlines Corporation <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.jsonline.com/story/index.aspx?id=359041" rel="nofollow">[1]</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot"><span title=" Dead link since May 2016">dead link</span></a></i>]</span></sup> on September 27, 2005, in connection with the completion of its merger with America West Holdings Corporation.</p>
<p>On May 19, 2005, US Airways Group announced that it would merge with <a href="/wiki/Tempe,_Arizona" title="Tempe, Arizona">Tempe, Arizona</a>-based America West Holdings Corporation, whose strength in the West would complement US Airways' routes in the Northeast, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>. At the time of announcement, plans called for the new entity to retain the US Airways brand, its Dividend Miles <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frequent_flyer_program" title="Frequent flyer program">frequent flyer program</a>, and participation in the <a href="/wiki/Star_Alliance" title="Star Alliance">Star Alliance</a> (subject to the approval of partner United Airlines); however, it would be headquartered at America West's corporate offices and America West executives and board members would be in control of the merged company. The merger was completed on September 27, 2005.</p>
<p>In October 2006, US Airways leased new office space in a building across the street from its corporate headquarters in Tempe, Arizona to expand and centralize its Corporate operations.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="US_Airways.2FDelta_proposed_merger">US Airways/Delta proposed merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Airways_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: US Airways/Delta proposed merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 15, 2006, US Airways Group announced a proposal to purchase <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> for $8.0 billion, half of this amount to be paid in <a href="/wiki/Cash" title="Cash">cash</a> with the remainder to be paid in <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a>. US Airways Group had obtained a firm commitment from <a href="/wiki/Citibank" title="Citibank">Citibank</a> to provide financing for both the cash portion of the merger offer, as well as the required secured loan refinancing at both airlines. The combined airlines would have flown under the Delta Air Lines name. Delta Air Lines repeatedly stated its intentions of exiting bankruptcy as a stand-alone carrier. On December 19, 2006, Delta's Board of Directors officially rejected US Airways offer. The final decision, however, was up to Delta Air Lines creditors as well as a bankruptcy judge as to what would happen.</p>
<p>Delta employees, represented by the <a href="/wiki/Delta_Board_Council" title="Delta Board Council">Delta Board Council</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Line_Pilots_Association" title="Air Line Pilots Association">Air Line Pilots Association</a>, organized against what they termed the "hostile takeover" effort by US Airways under the title "Keep Delta My Delta". A rally against the US Airways merger at the <a href="/wiki/Georgia_International_Convention_Center" title="Georgia International Convention Center">Georgia International Convention Center</a> was held December 13, 2006, and attended by nearly 3000 pilots, family members, and other supporters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 10, 2007, US Airways upped their bid for Delta from $8.5 billion to $10.2 billion, and imposed a deadline of February 1 for Delta's creditors committee to move forward with the deal. The move was made, according to US Airways president Scott Kirby, to remove any doubt that the US Airways offer was the best one on the table.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 31, 2007, following a decision by the creditors committee to support Delta's standalone reorganization plan, US Airways withdrew its merger offer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="US_Airways.2FAmerican_Airlines_merger">US Airways/American Airlines merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Airways_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: US Airways/American Airlines merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines_Group" title="American Airlines Group">American Airlines Group</a></div>
<p>In January 2012, US Airways expressed interest<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> to take over American Airlines and the AMR CEO said in March, that American is open to merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> As of September 2012, American Airlines, under <a href="/wiki/Chapter_11,_Title_11,_United_States_Code" title="Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code">chapter 11 bankruptcy protection</a>, was looking to merge with another airline. In July of that year, in a Bankruptcy Court filing US Airways disclosed that it was an American Airlines creditor and "prospective merger partner. Then on August 31, 2012, US Airways CEO Doug Parker announced that American Airlines and US Airways had signed a nondisclosure agreement, in which the airlines would discuss their financials and a possible merger."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 14, 2013, US Airways Group and <a href="/wiki/AMR_Corporation" title="AMR Corporation">AMR Corporation</a> officially announced that the two companies would merge to form the largest airline in the world. The combined airline will carry the American Airlines name and branding, while US Airways' management team, including CEO Doug Parker, will retain most operational management positions. The headquarters for the new airline will also be consolidated at American's current headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
