<p><b>Uverworld</b> (stylized as <b>UVERworld</b>) is a Japanese rock band consisting of six members and originating from <a href="/wiki/Kusatsu,_Shiga" title="Kusatsu, Shiga">Kusatsu, Shiga</a>. They have released eight studio albums and over twenty singles, most of them being successful. They have sold two million records worldwide.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.932005:_Beginnings_and_debut">2000–2005: Beginnings and debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2000–2005: Beginnings and debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Uverworld.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="90" data-file-width="559" height="35" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5f/Uverworld.png/220px-Uverworld.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5f/Uverworld.png/330px-Uverworld.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5f/Uverworld.png/440px-Uverworld.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The five-member band known as Uverworld first formed in 2003, and were known as <b>Sound Goku Road</b>, (more popularly known as "Sangoku Road" in the independent music scene). The band originally consisted of seven members, and released Prime'03, a demo CD which ended up selling 3,000 copies. Unfortunately, two of the band members, the saxophonist Seika, and Ryohei the second-man vocalist left. After Seika's departure Ace∞Trigger renamed himself Takuya∞ and the band renamed itself Uverworld.</p>
<p>According to an interview with the band there was another vocalist who left the band, however this vocalist hasn't yet been identified. It seems that this vocalist may have left the band quite some time before Seika did, as Seika is the only one of the two credited on the demo CD and pictured with very early live photos of the band pre-Uverworld.</p>
<p>The year 2005 was the year that the band signed with the label Gr8! Records, which is under Sony Music Records. The band made their debut with the single "D-tecnolife", which was the second opening theme for the anime <i><a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)" title="Bleach (manga)">Bleach</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_release_info_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_release_info-1">[1]</a></sup> The single made its debut in the <i><a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a></i> charts at the fourth spot with 31,569 copies together with <a href="/wiki/Mr._Children" title="Mr. Children">Mr. Children</a>'s twenty-seventh single, "Yonjigen Four Dimensions" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">四次元 Four Dimensions</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on the top spot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_-_First_week_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_-_First_week-2">[2]</a></sup> The single then later dropped to seventh place in the second week with 16,306 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_-_Second_week_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_-_Second_week-3">[3]</a></sup> The third week in the <i>Oricon</i> charts saw the single dropped 2 places to the ninth spot with 13,761 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_-_Third_week_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_-_Third_week-4">[4]</a></sup> The single lasted five weeks in the Top 30 weekly singles ranking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_-_Fourth_week_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_-_Fourth_week-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_-_Fifth_week_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_-_Fifth_week-6">[6]</a></sup> The single's sales and <i>Chaku-Uta Full</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">着うたフル</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> downloads were certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_-_Single_Certification_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_-_Single_Certification-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-technoLife_-_Chaku-Uta_Full_Certification_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-technoLife_-_Chaku-Uta_Full_Certification-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Three months later, Uverworld's second single, "Chance!" was used as the commercial and opening theme for second PSP game of <i><a href="/wiki/Bleach:_Heat_the_Soul_(series)" title="Bleach: Heat the Soul (series)">Bleach: Heat the Soul</a></i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The band's second single first entered the <i>Oricon</i> charts on the fifth spot with 14,150 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chance.21_-_First_week_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chance.21_-_First_week-10">[10]</a></sup> The single later dropped to the 23rd spot selling 6,360 copies on the second week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chance.21_-_Second_week_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chance.21_-_Second_week-11">[11]</a></sup> The single's ranking remained the same, however the amount of copies sold on the third week was lesser than the previous week with 5,495 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chance.21_-_Third_week_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chance.21_-_Third_week-12">[12]</a></sup> The single only managed to stay on the Top 30 weekly singles ranking for three weeks.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.932008:_Timeless.2C_BUGRIGHT_and_PROGLUTION">2006–2008: <i>Timeless</i>, <i>BUGRIGHT</i> and <i>PROGLUTION</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–2008: Timeless, BUGRIGHT and PROGLUTION">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 25, 2006, the band's third single "Just Melody" ended up at number-seventeen on the <i>Oricon</i> charts. Three weeks later Uverworld's first album <i>Timeless</i> was released, breaking the Top 10 at number-five and selling 60,000 copies. Seika made a brief return during this album, playing <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophone</a> for track 5, <i>Yasashisa no Shizuku</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">優しさの雫</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. After a three-month break, Uverworld released "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colors_of_the_Heart&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colors of the Heart (page does not exist)">Colors of the Heart</a>" as the third opening theme for <i><a href="/wiki/Blood%2B" title="Blood+">Blood+</a></i>, an anime series based on the animated movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood:_The_Last_Vampire" title="Blood: The Last Vampire">Blood: The Last Vampire</a></i>. The single debuted at number-three and was the highest position any of Uverworld's singles had reached up to that time.</p>
<p>Their fifth single, entitled "<a href="/wiki/Shamrock_(song)" title="Shamrock (song)">Shamrock</a>", was released on August 2, 2006, and was used as the ending theme for the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">drama</a> <i>Dance Drill</i>, a show about a group of girls who aspire to become cheerleaders. Uverworld's sixth Single was released on November 15, 2006, entitled "<a href="/wiki/Kimi_no_Suki_na_Uta" title="Kimi no Suki na Uta">Kimi no Suki na Uta</a>", the song was used as the theme song for the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS</a> TV show <i>Koi Suru Hanikami!</i> or <i>Honey Coming!</i> from the broadcast of October until December. It reached number two on the <i>Oricon</i> charts, which was the second highest place for an Uverworld single so far until it fell off of the charts three and a half weeks later. It sold around 70,000 copies according to the <i>Oricon</i> sales charts.</p>
<p>On February 21, 2007, Uverworld released their second album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Bugright" title="Bugright">Bugright</a></i>. It featured the singles Uverworld had released after <i>Timeless</i>. "Nagare, Kūkyo, This Word" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">～流れ・空虚・This Word～</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was included in the tribute soundtrack titled as <i>The Songs for Death Note The Movie: The Last Name Tribute</i> for <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Note" title="Death Note">Death Note</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nagare.E3.83.BBK.C5.ABkyo.E3.83.BBThis_Word_in_Death_Note_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nagare.E3.83.BBK.C5.ABkyo.E3.83.BBThis_Word_in_Death_Note-13">[13]</a></sup> On March 6, 2007, the band was also part of an event called 'We Love Music Vol.2' held in the <a href="/wiki/Shibuya-AX" title="Shibuya-AX">Shibuya-AX</a> where Uverworld performed against <a href="/wiki/Sid_(band)" title="Sid (band)">Sid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-We_Love_Music_Vol.2_Report_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-We_Love_Music_Vol.2_Report-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band's third album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Proglution" title="Proglution">Proglution</a></i>, was released on January 16, 2008 and contain full eighteen tracks. The album also contain their singles "<a href="/wiki/Ukiyo_Crossing" title="Ukiyo Crossing">Ukiyo Crossing</a>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">浮世Crossing</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, "<a href="/wiki/Endscape" title="Endscape">Endscape</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Shaka_Beach:_Laka_Laka_La" title="Shaka Beach: Laka Laka La">Shaka Beach: Laka Laka La</a>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">シャカビーチ~Laka Laka La~</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. The album was released in a CD-only version and a limited edition that includes an extra DVD. <i>Proglution</i> sold over 150,000 copies. June 11, 2008 saw the release of the single "<a href="/wiki/Gekid%C5%8D/Just_Break_the_Limit!" title="Gekidō/Just Break the Limit!">Gekidō/Just Break the Limit!</a>". "Gekidō" was used as the fourth opening theme of the anime series <i><a href="/wiki/D.Gray-man" title="D.Gray-man">D.Gray-man</a></i> while the song "Just Break the Limit!" was used as a support song for <a href="/wiki/Pocari_Sweat" title="Pocari Sweat">Pocari Sweat</a>'s CM. Two more singles were released in 2008, "<a href="/wiki/Koishikute" title="Koishikute">Koishikute</a>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">恋いしくて</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and "<a href="/wiki/Hakanaku_mo_Towa_no_Kanashi" title="Hakanaku mo Towa no Kanashi">Hakanaku mo Towa no Kanashi</a>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">儚くも永久のカナシ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. "Hakanaku mo Towa no Kanashi" was used as the opening for the second season of the <i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam_00" title="Mobile Suit Gundam 00">Mobile Suit Gundam 00</a></i> anime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12th_single_as_OP_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-12th_single_as_OP-15">[15]</a></sup> The single became the band's first number-one single on <i>Oricon</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> with approximately 181,784 copies sold. The single has since gone on to become the highest selling single from the band.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_AwakEVE">2009: <i>AwakEVE</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009: AwakEVE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 18, 2009 the band released their fourth album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Awakeve" title="Awakeve">Awakeve</a></i>. The album sold over 115,000 copies on its first week and ranked number-two on the <i>Oricon</i> album chart. A DVD of their live performance in <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Budokan" title="Nippon Budokan">Nippon Budokan</a> was released on April 29, 2009. The band's thirteenth single was released on August 5, 2009 titled as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go-On" title="Go-On">Go-On</a>." The single's title track will be featured as the second ending theme on the Japanese broadcast of the <a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a>'s first season. The band performed at 2009's <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Inazuma_Rock_Fes.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Inazuma Rock Fes. (page does not exist)">Inazuma Rock Fes.</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Shiga_Prefecture" title="Shiga Prefecture">Shiga Prefecture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inazuma_Rock_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inazuma_Rock-17">[17]</a></sup> They are also releasing their fourth DVD of the <a href="/wiki/Awakeve" title="Awakeve">Awakeve</a> Tour 09 on September 30, 2009.</p>
<p>The band's fourteenth single was released on October 28, 2009 entitled "<a href="/wiki/Kanashimi_wa_Kitto" title="Kanashimi wa Kitto">Kanashimi wa Kitto</a>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">哀しみはきっと</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> for the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">Tokyo Broadcasting System</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dk%C5%8Djo_Seira" title="Shōkōjo Seira">Shōkōjo Seira</a></i>, based on the children's novel, <a href="/wiki/A_Little_Princess" title="A Little Princess">A Little Princess</a> written by <a href="/wiki/Frances_Hodgson_Burnett" title="Frances Hodgson Burnett">Frances Hodgson Burnett</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Little_Princess_theme_song_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-A_Little_Princess_theme_song-18">[18]</a></sup> The song's lyrics was written by Takuya and was co-composed by both the lyricist and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Satoru_Hiraide&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Satoru Hiraide (page does not exist)">Satoru Hiraide</a> while the arrangement was done by the band and Hiraide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kanashimi_wa_Kitto_release_info_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kanashimi_wa_Kitto_release_info-19">[19]</a></sup> On October 9, 2009, an English official website was announced and it went online at 12pm <a href="/wiki/Japan_Standard_Time" title="Japan Standard Time">Japan Standard Time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News.2FAnnouncements.2FEvents_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-News.2FAnnouncements.2FEvents-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_hits" title="Greatest hits">best of</a> album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Neo_Sound_Best" title="Neo Sound Best">Neo Sound Best</a></i> was announced on November 9, 2009 and released on December 9, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Best_of_Album_announcement_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Best_of_Album_announcement-21">[21]</a></sup> They were also scheduled to perform at the Ontama Carnival 2010 together with <a href="/wiki/Funky_Monkey_Babys" title="Funky Monkey Babys">Funky Monkey Babys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flumpool" title="Flumpool">Flumpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Best_of_Album_announcement_2_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Best_of_Album_announcement_2-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-2012:_LAST_and_LIFE_6_SENSE">2010-2012: <i>LAST</i> and <i>LIFE 6 SENSE</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010-2012: LAST and LIFE 6 SENSE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Their fifteenth single "<a href="/wiki/Gold_(Uverworld_song)" title="Gold (Uverworld song)">GOLD</a>" was released on March 31, 2010, making it their first release of the year. The music video for "Gold" was made into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3-D_film" title="3-D film">3-D</a>, making it the first 3-D music video in Japan. It was premiered on April 17, 2010. Additionally, their fifth album <i><a href="/wiki/Last_(Uverworld_album)" title="Last (Uverworld album)">Last</a></i> was released two weeks later on April 14, 2010.</p>
<p>Their sixteenth single "Qualia" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">クオリア</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was released on September 15, 2010, and contains the ending theme song for the <i>Mobile Suit Gundam 00</i> movie and the title song for the PSP game <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Ranker" title="Last Ranker">Last Ranker</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Their seventeenth single "NO.1" was released on November 24, 2010. NO.1 was featured as their starting song for their concert LAST TOUR FINAL at TOKYO DOME which was in November 27, 2010.</p>
<p>Their eighteenth single "MONDO PIECE" was to be released on March 23, 2011. However, due to the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>, the release date has been pushed to April 6. It was also revealed earlier last year concert at TOKYO DOME as the last song. Their nineteenth single "CORE PRIDE" was selected to be the 1st opening theme for the anime <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Exorcist" title="Blue Exorcist">Blue Exorcist</a></i>.</p>
<p>The band released their sixth album <i><a href="/wiki/Life_6_Sense" title="Life 6 Sense">LIFE 6 SENSE</a></i> on June 1, 2011. The album's title was leaked out in bands page on Japanese <i>Wikipedia</i> and also the following month after, The band released their 6th DVD which is "LAST TOUR FINAL at TOKYO DOME 2010/11/27".</p>
<p>On December 14, 2011, the band released their twentieth single (second A-side single) entitled "BABY BORN &amp; GO/KINJITO".</p>
<p>Blu-ray versions of <i>UVERworld 2008 Premium LIVE at Nippon Budokan</i> and <i>LAST TOUR FINAL at TOKYO DOME</i> were released on December 17, 2011.</p>
<p>On March 28, 2012, UVERworld has released their 21st single which is entitled to be <i>7th Trigger</i> and 6 days after they have released another Live DVD &amp; Blu-ray <i>UVERworld 2011 Premium LIVE on Xmas at Nippon Budokan</i> which was at April 4, 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012-2014:_THE_ONE">2012-2014: <i>THE ONE</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012-2014: THE ONE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 8, 2012, The band has released their 22nd single <i>THE OVER</i> which is also the ending theme of <i>Kuro no Onna Kyoushi</i> (黒の女教師) and Three months after until 28 November 2012, The band has finally released their 7th album which is to be entitled "THE ONE".</p>
<p>After the release of 7th album, UVERworld released a re-cut single from their Latest album entitled <i>REVERSI</i> and it was also the movie theme song for <i>Blue Exorcist -The Movie-</i>.</p>
<p>On August 14, 2013, the band will release their 24th Single <i>Fight For Liberty / Wizard CLUB</i>. This will be their first single for 2013. Fight For Liberty will be used as the opening theme song for the anime <a href="/wiki/Space_Battleship_Yamato_2199" title="Space Battleship Yamato 2199">Space Battleship Yamato 2199</a> while Wizard CLUB will be used as the ending theme song for the TV series Kaikin! Bakuro Night.</p>
<p>On November 6, 2013, UVERworld release their 10th Video <i>KING'S PARADE Zepp DiverCity (2013.02.08)</i> on DVD and Blu-ray after their long period of THE ONE Tour 2012-2013. UVERworld have finally decided to have a full LIVE HOUSE Footage from Zepp DiverCity as their main concept instead of Arenas, Stadiums or Halls.</p>
<p>On December 18, 2013, the band has released their 25th Single <i>Nano-Second (ナノ・セカンド)</i>. Which is their second single and the last for 2013.</p>
<p>On March 19, 2014, they released their 2nd video album <i>UVERworld Video Complete -act.2-</i> from LIFE 6 SENSE to THE ONE promotional videos which also includes a LIVE bonus disc from 2013.12.25 ARENA LIVE 2013 winter QUEEN'S PARTY at Nippon Budokan.</p>
<p>On March 26, 2014, It was announced that UVERworld would have their former member from Sangoku Road and live support member, Seika as their new 6th member to the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seika_officially_joins_band_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Seika_officially_joins_band-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 27, 2014, The band has officially announced the next big stage on July 5 which will be hosted at Kyocera Dome Osaka.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-present:_.C3.98_CHOIR">2014-present: <i>Ø CHOIR</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014-present: Ø CHOIR">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 18, 2014, UVERworld released their 26th Single entitled to be called <i>Nanokame no ketsui (7日目の決意, Decision of the Seventh Day)</i> which will be their 1st single for 2014.</p>
<p>On July 2, 2014, UVERworld released their 8th studio album, entitled "Ø CHOIR", which subsequently reached second place in the Oricon Style Charts.</p>
<p>On August 19, UVERworld officially announced their new 12th DVD "KING'S PARADE Nippon Budokan 2013.12.26" and it was released on September 24, 2014.</p>
<p>On April 15, 2015, UVERworld released their 13th video content "LIVE at KYOCERA DOME OSAKA 2014.07.05".</p>
<p>On May 27, UVERworld released their 27th single "Boku no Kotoba Dewanai Kore wa Bokutachi no Kotoba". The single is used for the anime series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Heroic_Legend_of_Arslan" title="The Heroic Legend of Arslan">The Heroic Legend of Arslan</a></i></p>
<p>On August 26, UVERworld released their 28th single "I LOVE THE WORLD" which is used as a theme song Dragon Nest R Game in Japan.</p>
<p>On September 30, 2015, the band released their video content on DVD/Blu-ray "KING'S PARADE at Yokohama Arena 2015.01.10".</p>
<p>On June 8, 2016, UVERworld released their recent video on DVD/Blu-ray "UVERworld 15&amp;10 Anniversary Live".</p>
<p>On December 17, 2016, the Blue Exorcist website revealed several new promo trailers for its upcoming second season where it was revealed that UVERworld would again be providing the opening song for the series. "Itteki no Eikyō (一滴の影響, Drop's Influence)" is set to be released February 1, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blue_Exorcist:_Kyoto_Saga_Anime.27s_TV_Ads_Preview_Theme_Songs_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blue_Exorcist:_Kyoto_Saga_Anime.27s_TV_Ads_Preview_Theme_Songs-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uverworld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Shimizu Takuya (stylized as <b>TAKUYA∞</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">vocalist</a> for the group as well as in charge of writing the band's music, <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> and <a href="/wiki/Programming_(music)" title="Programming (music)">programming</a>. He was born on December 21, 1979 in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> while he later moved to Kusatsu, Shiga Prefecture.</li>
<li><b>Katsuya</b> (克哉), who is in charge of <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_guitar" title="Rhythm guitar">rhythm guitar</a>, is the band's <a href="/wiki/Bandleader" title="Bandleader">leader</a>. He was born on February 22, 1981.</li>
<li><b>Akira</b> (彰) is in charge of <a href="/wiki/Lead_guitar" title="Lead guitar">lead guitar</a> for the band. He was born on March 8, 1984.</li>
<li><b>Nobuto</b> (信人) is in charge of the <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a>. He was born on February 14, 1980.</li>
<li><b>Shintarō</b> (真太郎) is in charge of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drums" title="Drums">drums</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percussion" title="Percussion">percussion</a>. He was born on November 5, 1983 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%8Ctsu,_Shiga" title="Ōtsu, Shiga">Ōtsu</a>, Shiga Prefecture.</li>
<li><b>SEIKA</b> (誠果), who was originally part of the band but left before their debut as UVERworld, rejoined on March 26, 2014. He is the manipulator and saxophonist.</li>
</ul>
