<p><b>GRW +70 8247</b> is a <a href="/wiki/White_dwarf" title="White dwarf">white dwarf</a> located about 43 light years from <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Draco_(constellation)" title="Draco (constellation)">Draco</a>. With a <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a> of about 13 it is visible only through a large <a href="/wiki/Telescope" title="Telescope">telescope</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GRW_%2B70_8247&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although photographed in the 19th century as part of the <a href="/wiki/Carte_du_Ciel" title="Carte du Ciel">Carte du Ciel</a> project, it was not determined to be a white dwarf until observed in 1934 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G._P._Kuiper" title="G. P. Kuiper">G. P. Kuiper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kuiper_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-kuiper-3">[3]</a></sup> This makes it the fifth or sixth white dwarf discovered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At first, its <a href="/wiki/Spectrum" title="Spectrum">spectrum</a> was thought to be almost featureless,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kuiper_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-kuiper-3">[3]</a></sup> but later observation showed it to have unusual broad, shallow <a href="/wiki/Spectral_line" title="Spectral line">absorption bands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup>, p. 28;</sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1970, when light it emitted was observed to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circularly_polarized" title="Circularly polarized">circularly polarized</a>, it became the first white dwarf known to have a <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_field" title="Magnetic field">magnetic field</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the 1980s it was realized that the unusual absorption bands could be explained as <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> absorption lines shifted by the <a href="/wiki/Zeeman_effect" title="Zeeman effect">Zeeman effect</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-g_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-g-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
