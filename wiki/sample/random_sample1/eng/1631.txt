<p><i><b>Vroom Vroom</b></i> is the second <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) by English singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Charli_XCX" title="Charli XCX">Charli XCX</a>, released on 26 February 2016 by Vroom Vroom Recordings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rolling_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It features production work from English producer <a href="/wiki/Sophie_(musician)" title="Sophie (musician)">Sophie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_release">Background and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vroom_Vroom_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2015, in an interview with British fashion magazine <i><a href="/wiki/I-D" title="I-D">i-D</a></i>, Charli XCX said that she was working on her third album and described it as "the most pop thing, and the most electronic thing" she had done.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> British producer <a href="/wiki/Sophie_(musician)" title="Sophie (musician)">Sophie</a> has been confirmed to be involved in the album's production. In October 2015, XCX released "Vroom Vroom" on the Beats 1 Radio Show, which is the first song released from the EP, then claiming it would be the first song released from her third studio album.</p>
<p>On 23 February 2016, it was announced that XCX had set up a new record label, Vroom Vroom Recordings, and that she would release an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> titled <i>Vroom Vroom</i> on 27 February 2016. The second song released from the EP, titled "Trophy", received its first play on <a href="/wiki/Zane_Lowe" title="Zane Lowe">Zane Lowe</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Beats 1</a> show on that night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rolling_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling-2">[2]</a></sup> XCX will also host her own Beats 1 show fortnightly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>XCX released a music video for the song "Vroom Vroom" on 22 April 2016 via <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 27 May 2016, an autographed <a href="/wiki/LP_record" title="LP record">vinyl</a> version of the EP was released in a limited run of 1,000 copies by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Music_Group" title="Warner Music Group">Warner Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalised</a> rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">average score</a> of 64, which indicates "generally favorable reviews", based on 9 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MC_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-MC-17">[17]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> gave the album a favorable review, stating that, "Vroom Vroom" is scintillating new ground for Charli, totally unlike anything she's ever done before, and still quintessentially her in its streamlined, indomitable turbo-pop."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spin_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spin-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Mix_Tapes" title="Tiny Mix Tapes">Tiny Mix Tapes</a> wrote "this is pop music reinventing itself, reasserting its autonomy," describing Sophie's production as "like liquid metal or maybe the noise equivalent of <a href="/wiki/Non-Euclidean_geometry" title="Non-Euclidean geometry">non-Euclidean geometry</a>, often precluding ready perception and identification and escaping non-hyperbolic description."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tmt_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-tmt-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a negative review for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a></i>, Laura Snapes wrote, "At times, <i>Vroom Vroom</i> recalls <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>' <a href="/wiki/Blackout_(Britney_Spears_album)" title="Blackout (Britney Spears album)"><i>Blackout</i></a><i>,</i> made in the wake of Spears’ public breakdown. Spears was always assumed to have little agency in her own work, and on that record she toyed brilliantly with ideas of autonomy and unwholesomeness, using sharp lyrics and sharper production to play with the idea of disintegrating identity. These same ideas underpin the PC Music project, and you can hear them at work on <i>Vroom Vroom</i>. But no one is being done any favors reducing Charli XCX to a vapid cypher, particularly as it drains her vivid personality from the work". <i>Pretty Much Amazing</i> wrote that "Vroom Vroom" bro might have worked had Charli written some better hooks, or actually put some effort into her raps, or just not rapped at all."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PMA_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-PMA-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
