<p><b>WABC-TV</b>, channel 7, is the <a href="/wiki/Flagship_(broadcasting)" title="Flagship (broadcasting)">flagship station</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> television network, located in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. WABC-TV is owned by the <a href="/wiki/ABC_Owned_Television_Stations" title="ABC Owned Television Stations">ABC Owned Television Stations</a> subsidiary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney-ABC_Television_Group" title="Disney-ABC Television Group">Disney-ABC Television Group</a> division of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Media_Networks" title="Disney Media Networks">Disney Media Networks</a> subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>. The station's studios and offices are located near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_Square_(Manhattan)" title="Lincoln Square (Manhattan)">Lincoln Square</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Upper_West_Side" title="Upper West Side">Upper West Side</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, adjacent to ABC's corporate headquarters; its transmitter is atop the <a href="/wiki/Empire_State_Building" title="Empire State Building">Empire State Building</a>.</p>
<p>WABC-TV is best known in broadcasting circles for its version of the <i><a href="/wiki/Eyewitness_News" title="Eyewitness News">Eyewitness News</a></i> format and for its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly_and_Michael" title="Live! with Kelly and Michael">morning show</a>, syndicated nationally by corporate cousin <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney-ABC_Domestic_Television" title="Disney-ABC Domestic Television">Disney-ABC Domestic Television</a>.</p>
<p>In the few areas of the eastern United States where an ABC station isn't receivable over-the-air, WABC is available on satellite via <a href="/wiki/DirecTV" title="DirecTV">DirecTV</a> (which also provides coverage of the station to Latin American and Caribbean countries and through major U.S. air carriers on <a href="/wiki/LiveTV" title="LiveTV">LiveTV</a> inflight entertainment system) and <a href="/wiki/Dish_Network" title="Dish Network">Dish Network</a> (which carries the station as part of All American Direct's distant network package).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station signed on August 10, 1948, as <b>WJZ-TV</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the first of three television stations signed on by ABC during that same year, with WENR-TV (now <a href="/wiki/WLS-TV" title="WLS-TV">WLS-TV</a>) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago</a> and <a href="/wiki/WXYZ-TV" title="WXYZ-TV">WXYZ-TV</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit,_Michigan" title="Detroit, Michigan">Detroit</a> being the other two. Channel 7's call letters came from its then-sister radio station, WJZ (770 AM, now <a href="/wiki/WABC_(AM)" title="WABC (AM)">WABC</a>). In its early years, WJZ-TV was programmed much like an <a href="/wiki/Independent_station_(North_America)" title="Independent station (North America)">independent station</a>, as the ABC television network was still, for the most part, in its very early stages of development; the ABC-owned stations did air some common programming during this period, especially after the 1949 fall season when the network's prime time schedule began to expand. The station's original transmitter site was atop <a href="/wiki/The_Pierre" title="The Pierre">The Pierre</a> Hotel at 2 East 61st Street, before moving to the Empire State Building a few years later. The station's original studios were located at 77 West 66th Street, with studios at 7 West 66th Street. An underground tunnel linked ABC studios at 7 West 66th Street to the lobby of the <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_des_Artistes" title="Café des Artistes">Hotel des Artistes</a>, a block north on West 67th Street. Another studio inside the Hotel des Artistes was used for <i>Eyewitness News Conference</i>.</p>
<p>The station's call letters were changed to <b>WABC-TV</b> on March 1, 1953<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> after ABC merged its operations with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Paramount_Theaters" title="United Paramount Theaters">United Paramount Theaters</a>, a firm which was broken off from former parent company <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> by decree of the U.S. government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The WJZ-TV callsign was later reassigned to <a href="/wiki/Westinghouse_Broadcasting" title="Westinghouse Broadcasting">Westinghouse Broadcasting</a> (the original owners of WJZ radio in New York) in 1957 for their newly acquired <a href="/wiki/WJZ-TV" title="WJZ-TV">television station</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore</a> – a station that was, by coincidence, an ABC affiliate until 1995.</p>
<p>As part of ABC's expansion program, initiated in 1977, ABC built 7 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_Square,_New_York" title="Lincoln Square, New York">Lincoln Square</a> on the southeast corner of West 67th Street and <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Ninth Avenue (Manhattan)">Columbus Avenue</a>, on a site of an abandoned moving and storage warehouse. At about the same time, construction was started at 30 West 67th Street on the site of a former parking lot. Both buildings were completed in June 1979 and WABC-TV moved its offices from 77 West 66th Street to 7 Lincoln Square.</p>
<p>On September 11, 2001, the transmitter facilities of WABC-TV, as well as eight other local television stations and several radio stations, were destroyed when two <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">hijacked airplanes crashed</a> into and destroyed the north and south towers of the <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Center_(1973%E2%80%932001)" title="World Trade Center (1973–2001)">World Trade Center</a>. Transmitter maintenance engineer Donald DiFranco died in the attack. In the immediate aftermath, the station fed its signal to then-<a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> member station (now educational independent station) <a href="/wiki/WNYE-TV" title="WNYE-TV">WNYE-TV</a> and then-<a href="/wiki/American_Independent_Network" title="American Independent Network">AIN</a> (later <a href="/wiki/Urban_America_Television" title="Urban America Television">UATV</a>) affiliate WHSE-TV (now <a href="/wiki/UniM%C3%A1s" title="UniMás">UniMás</a> O&amp;O <a href="/wiki/WFUT-DT" title="WFUT-DT">WFUT-DT</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> before establishing temporary facilities at the <a href="/wiki/Armstrong_Tower" title="Armstrong Tower">Armstrong Tower</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alpine,_New_Jersey" title="Alpine, New Jersey">Alpine, New Jersey</a>. The station eventually established transmission facilities at the Empire State Building.</p>
<p>On May 27, 2007, WABC-TV's studios suffered major damage as the result of a fire that knocked the station off the air shortly before the start of the 11:00 PM Newscast. According to preliminary reports, the fire may have been ignited by a spotlight coming into contact with a curtain inside the news studio; the station's website later reported the cause as an "electrical malfunction". The station's building was evacuated and the fire was brought under control, though the studio was said to be "badly damaged",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> having suffered smoke and water damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> WABC-TV resumed broadcasting at around 1:00 AM on May 28, 2007 (initially carrying the network's 10:00 PM West Coast feed of <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_%26_Sisters_(2006_TV_series)" title="Brothers &amp; Sisters (2006 TV series)">Brothers &amp; Sisters</a></i>, followed by the full broadcast of <i><a href="/wiki/World_News_Now" title="World News Now">World News Now</a></i>). Due to the fire, the station broadcast <i>Eyewitness News</i> from a temporary set in the newsroom, while <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly_and_Michael" title="Live! with Kelly and Michael">Live with Regis and Kelly</a></i>, whose set was also affected, moved to the set of <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire_(U.S._game_show)" title="Who Wants to Be a Millionaire (U.S. game show)">Who Wants to Be a Millionaire</a></i>. Starting with the 5:00 PM Newscast on June 20, 2007, the station resumed the <i>Eyewitness News</i> and <i>Live...</i> broadcasts from its main studios at Columbus Avenue and 66th Street.</p>
<p>In May 2013, WABC-TV and <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> <a href="/wiki/Sister_station" title="Sister station">sister station</a> <a href="/wiki/WPVI-TV" title="WPVI-TV">WPVI-TV</a> became the first two ABC-owned stations to offer live, web-based streaming of programming to authenticated subscribers of participating cable and satellite television providers as provided through the relaunched <i>Watch ABC</i> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_app" title="Mobile app">mobile apps</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-watchabc_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety-watchabc-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engadget-watchabc_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-engadget-watchabc-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cablevision">Cablevision</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cablevision">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 7, 2010 at 12:02 AM, WABC-TV's signal was removed from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cablevision" title="Cablevision">Cablevision</a>'s New York area systems (including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IO_Digital_Cable_Service" title="IO Digital Cable Service">iO Digital Cable</a>) after failing to reach terms on a new <a href="/wiki/Retransmission_consent" title="Retransmission consent">retransmission consent</a> agreement; the station was replaced by either a blank screen or a looping video containing a message from Cablevision about the removal. To avoid interruption of programming, the station urged Cablevision subscribers in the station's viewing area (totaling up to three million subscribers) to switch to other services, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verizon_FiOS" title="Verizon FiOS">Verizon FiOS</a> and <a href="/wiki/DirecTV" title="DirecTV">DirecTV</a> or simply view the station over the air through an over-the-air digital antenna and if necessary, a digital converter box, for older television sets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> WABC's sister station, <a href="/wiki/WPVI-TV" title="WPVI-TV">WPVI-TV</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> was also pulled from Cablevision's New Jersey systems in <a href="/wiki/Mercer_County,_New_Jersey" title="Mercer County, New Jersey">Mercer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ocean_County,_New_Jersey" title="Ocean County, New Jersey">Ocean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monmouth_County,_New_Jersey" title="Monmouth County, New Jersey">Monmouth Counties</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later that same day at approximately 8:50 PM, 20 Minutes into ABC's broadcast of the <a href="/wiki/82nd_Academy_Awards" title="82nd Academy Awards">82nd Annual Academy Awards</a>, Cablevision and ABC reached a deal, restoring WABC and WPVI's signals for Cablevision subscribers after a nearly 21-hour blackout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Time_Warner_Cable">Time Warner Cable</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Time Warner Cable">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In July 2010, ABC's parent company Disney announced that it was involved in a carriage dispute with <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner_Cable" title="Time Warner Cable">Time Warner Cable</a>, its first with that provider in 10 Years. This dispute involved 4 ABC owned-and-operated stations (WABC-TV and sister stations KABC-TV in Los Angeles, <a href="/wiki/WTVD" title="WTVD">WTVD</a> in <a href="/wiki/Durham,_North_Carolina" title="Durham, North Carolina">Durham, North Carolina</a> and <a href="/wiki/WTVG" title="WTVG">WTVG</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Ohio" title="Toledo, Ohio">Toledo, Ohio</a>, which at that time, WTVG was once an ABC O&amp;O), <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> networks. If a deal wasn't in place, the affected stations and cable channels would've been removed from Time Warner and <a href="/wiki/Bright_House_Networks" title="Bright House Networks">Bright House Networks</a> systems across the country. On September 2, 2010, Disney and Time Warner Cable reached a long-term agreement to keep the channels on Time Warner Cable systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Analog-to-digital_conversion">Analog-to-digital conversion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Analog-to-digital conversion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>WABC-TV discontinued regular programming on its analog signal, over <a href="/wiki/Very_high_frequency" title="Very high frequency">VHF</a> channel 7, at 12:30 PM on June 12, 2009, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Digital_television_transition_in_the_United_States" title="Digital television transition in the United States">federally mandated transition from analog to digital television</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The station's digital signal relocated from its pre-transition UHF channel 45 to VHF channel 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCCForm387_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FCCForm387-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>WABC's digital signal was initially difficult to receive over-the-air in New York City. The station was requested by <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">Federal Communications Commission</a> (FCC) to broadcast at a lower power; WABC was among many stations which have found it necessary to increase power to restore coverage to the same level as its former analog signal. On June 29, 2009, WABC filed an application with the FCC to increase power from 11.69 kW to 27 kW.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On January 31, 2010, the FCC granted a <a href="/wiki/Special_temporary_authority" title="Special temporary authority">special temporary authority</a> (STA) for the station to increase power to 26.9 kW.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming">Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>WABC has long presented events such as The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Puerto_Rican_Day_Parade" title="National Puerto Rican Day Parade">National Puerto Rican Day Parade</a> and the Columbus Day Parade. In addition, The station is also producing local programs such as <i>Viewpoint</i>, a weekly program that highlights the cultural and community efforts in New York, Long Island and New Jersey (each of these regions rotate weekly), <i>Here and Now</i>, a program covering the latest issues, trends and news stories impacting the local black community, <i>Up Close with Diana Williams</i>, a public affairs program on the latest issues with the newsmakers, and <i>Tiempo</i>, a weekly program that focus on the issues affecting local Hispanic citizens.</p>
<p>WABC serves as the flagship station for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly_(U.S._talk_show)" title="Live! with Kelly (U.S. talk show)">Live! with Kelly</a></i> (see below), <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire_(U.S._game_show)" title="Who Wants to Be a Millionaire (U.S. game show)">Who Wants to Be a Millionaire</a></i> (which is distributed by corporate sister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney-ABC_Domestic_Television" title="Disney-ABC Domestic Television">Disney-ABC Domestic Television</a>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Rachael_Ray_(TV_series)" title="Rachael Ray (TV series)">Rachael Ray</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_Programming">Sports Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sports Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>WABC-TV serves as the local over-the-air broadcaster of <a href="/wiki/Monday_Night_Football" title="Monday Night Football">Monday Night Football</a> games involving the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Giants" title="New York Giants">New York Giants</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Jets" title="New York Jets">New York Jets</a>, airing simulcasts of the team's <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>-televised games (WABC-TV's corporate parent, the The Walt Disney Company, holds 80% majority ownership stake in ESPN, and the ABC Owned Television Stations have right of first refusal for simulcasts of ESPN's <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> telecasts within a team's home market). Because of this, atypical for a network-owned station outside of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breaking_News" title="Breaking News">breaking news</a> and severe weather coverage necessitating such situations, the station has had to reschedule ABC network programs pre-empted by the telecasts. The preseason and MNF telecasts mark the only NFL games to have aired on WABC-TV since ABC <a href="/wiki/Monday_Night_Football" title="Monday Night Football">lost the rights to NFL games</a> in 2006 on wards. During the regular season, Giants and Jets are rotated between <a href="/wiki/WCBS-TV" title="WCBS-TV">WCBS-TV</a> (through the <a href="/wiki/NFL_on_CBS" title="NFL on CBS">NFL on CBS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thursday_Night_Football" title="Thursday Night Football">Thursday Night Football</a>), <a href="/wiki/WNYW" title="WNYW">WNYW</a> (through the <a href="/wiki/Fox_NFL" title="Fox NFL">Fox NFL</a>), <a href="/wiki/WPIX" title="WPIX">WPIX</a> (through select telecasts via the <a href="/wiki/NFL_Network" title="NFL Network">NFL Network</a>'s Thursday Night Football and/or via ESPN's MNF if WABC-TV decides not to air the event on WABC-TV), WABC-TV (with MNF), <a href="/wiki/WNBC" title="WNBC">WNBC</a> (through <a href="/wiki/NBC_Sunday_Night_Football" title="NBC Sunday Night Football">NBC Sunday Night Football</a> and TNF), and at some cases, <a href="/wiki/WWOR-TV" title="WWOR-TV">WWOR-TV</a> (through MNF).</p>
<p>Since 2013, WABC-TV serves the exclusive local English-language carrier of the annual <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Marathon" title="New York City Marathon">New York City Marathon</a>, in which WABC-TV will televised the marathon live and will televised the highlights through <a href="/wiki/ESPN_on_ABC" title="ESPN on ABC">ESPN on ABC</a>. The Station pre-empts a weekend edition of <i>Good Morning America</i> and delays <i>This Week</i> to schedule time for the live broadcast of the Marathon. The Marathon is also simulcasted on <a href="/wiki/ESPN2" title="ESPN2">ESPN2</a> nationally (although viewers in the WABC-TV viewing area cannot see it via ESPN2 because the ESPN2 simulcast is blacked out locally) The telecasts of the marathon on station which former broadcaster <a href="/wiki/WNBC" title="WNBC">WNBC</a> backed out after the 2011 event and the cancelation of 2012 event due to the effects of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy" title="Hurricane Sandy">Hurricane Sandy</a> in the area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="News_operation">News operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: News operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>WABC-TV presently broadcasts 43½ hours of locally produced newscasts each week (with 6½ hours on weekdays and 5½ hours each on Saturdays and Sundays). The station cooperates with Philadelphia sister station WPVI-TV – which popularized the <i><a href="/wiki/Action_News" title="Action News">Action News</a></i> format – in the production and broadcast of statewide <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> political debates. When the two stations broadcast a statewide office debate, such as for <a href="/wiki/Governor_(United_States)" title="Governor (United States)">governor</a> or <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">U.S. Senate</a>, they'll pool resources and have anchors or reporters from both stations participate in the debate. Additionally, the two stations cooperate in coverage of news from New Jersey where their markets overlap, sharing reporters, live trucks and helicopters.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginning_to_1999">Beginning to 1999</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Beginning to 1999">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:WABC_Eyewitness_News_2013.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="350" data-file-width="640" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/WABC_Eyewitness_News_2013.png/220px-WABC_Eyewitness_News_2013.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/WABC_Eyewitness_News_2013.png/330px-WABC_Eyewitness_News_2013.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/WABC_Eyewitness_News_2013.png/440px-WABC_Eyewitness_News_2013.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>WABC-TV launched <i>Report to New York</i>, its first regular news program, on October 26, 1959, featuring <a href="/wiki/Scott_Vincent" title="Scott Vincent">Scott Vincent</a> with news, <a href="/wiki/Howard_Cosell" title="Howard Cosell">Howard Cosell</a> with sports, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lynn_Dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lynn Dollar (page does not exist)">Lynn Dollar</a> with weather. <i>Report to New York</i> aired Monday through Friday at 11 pm. By January 1961, channel 7 expanded “Report to New York” with a 15-minute early edition at 6:15 pm on weeknights, and on Saturday and Sunday evenings.</p>
<p>On October 22, 1962, WABC-TV expanded its weeknight news to 45-minutes, and retitled it as "The Big News<i>. Newcomers <a href="/wiki/Bill_Beutel" title="Bill Beutel">Bill Beutel</a> and Jim Barnes were the anchors, with Cosell continuing on sports and Rosemary Haley as "weather girl". However, this effort failed to draw viewers from ratings leader <a href="/wiki/WCBS-TV" title="WCBS-TV">WCBS-TV</a> and second-place <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WNBC-TV" title="WNBC-TV">WNBC-TV</a>.</i></p>
<p>In early 1968, Beutel left the station to become the London bureau chief for <a href="/wiki/ABC_News" title="ABC News">ABC News</a> and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Roger_Grimsby" title="Roger Grimsby">Roger Grimsby</a>, who was transferred by ABC from <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> sister station <a href="/wiki/KGO-TV" title="KGO-TV">KGO-TV</a>. In a complete revamp, Grimsby was joined by <a href="/wiki/Tex_Antoine" title="Tex Antoine">Tex Antoine</a> doing weather, celebrity gossip columnist <a href="/wiki/Rona_Barrett" title="Rona Barrett">Rona Barrett</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Daily_News" title="New York Daily News">New York Daily News</a></i> columnist <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Breslin" title="Jimmy Breslin">Jimmy Breslin</a> with political commentary and reviews by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Bookspan" title="Martin Bookspan">Martin Bookspan</a> and Allan Jeffries, while Cosell continued doing sports. Known as <i>Roger Grimsby and the Noisemakers</i>, this format didn't help the ratings, which plunged to an all-time low.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Later that year, newly hired news director <a href="/wiki/Al_Primo" title="Al Primo">Al Primo</a> brought to WABC-TV the <a href="/wiki/Eyewitness_News" title="Eyewitness News">Eyewitness News</a> format, in which reporters present their stories directly to the viewers. Having experienced great success introducing the format during his time at <a href="/wiki/KYW-TV" title="KYW-TV">KYW-TV</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, Primo this time added a twist – a degree of conversational chatter among the anchors, known as "happy talk". The "Tar Sequence" cue from the musical score of the <a href="/wiki/1967_in_film" title="1967 in film">1967 film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Cool_Hand_Luke" title="Cool Hand Luke">Cool Hand Luke</a></i>, composed by <a href="/wiki/Lalo_Schifrin" title="Lalo Schifrin">Lalo Schifrin</a>, was introduced as the theme music. The score included a telegraphic-style melody appropriate for a newscast. The <i>Eyewitness News</i> format and theme music were quickly adopted by ABC's other four owned-and-operated stations at the time: KGO-TV, WLS-TV in Chicago, WXYZ-TV in Detroit and KABC-TV in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> (though KGO-TV and WXYZ-TV didn't use the <i>Eyewitness News</i> title for their programs). The format quickly rejuvenated a station that had long been an also-ran to WCBS-TV and WNBC-TV. Within a year, Channel 7 had shot to first place in the ratings for the first time in its history, displacing longtime leader WCBS-TV. It spent most of the decade going back and forth with WCBS-TV for first place. For a time in the 1980s, it fell into last place, but still fought with WNBC-TV for second place.</p>
<p>Retaining only Grimsby, Cosell and Antoine from the earlier <i>Noisemakers</i> format, Primo also hired <a href="/wiki/Tom_Dunn" title="Tom Dunn">Tom Dunn</a> away from WCBS-TV to serve as Grimsby's co-anchor. After Dunn departed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WOR-TV" title="WOR-TV">WOR-TV</a> in 1970, Bill Beutel returned to the station as his replacement and for the next 16 years, Grimsby and Beutel were the faces of <i>Eyewitness News</i>.</p>
<p>The Grimsby-Beutel team were split up for several months in 1975 after ABC had reassigned Beutel to its new morning show <i><a href="/wiki/AM_America" title="AM America">AM America</a></i>. The station brought in <a href="/wiki/WXYZ-TV" title="WXYZ-TV">WXYZ-TV</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bill_Bonds" title="Bill Bonds">Bill Bonds</a> and veteran <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> anchor <a href="/wiki/Tom_Ellis_(journalist)" title="Tom Ellis (journalist)">Tom Ellis</a> to help replace Beutel, with Grimsby teaming with Ellis at 6:00 and Bonds at 11:00. When <i>AM America</i> was cancelled and replaced with <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> in September 1975, Beutel was re-teamed with Grimsby at 6:00, with Ellis joining Bonds at 11:00. Bonds returned to Detroit in 1976, while Ellis remained until 1978 and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Larry_Kane" title="Larry Kane">Larry Kane</a>, who lasted only one year as the sole 11:00 PM anchor before returning to his home market of Philadelphia. Kane's successor, <a href="/wiki/Ernie_Anastos" title="Ernie Anastos">Ernie Anastos</a>, began his New York career at the station; he co-anchored at 11:00 PM for his entire 12-year tenure there.</p>
<p>On December 7, 1981, the station became the second in the city to expand its late afternoon/evening newscasts by adding of a 5:00 PM edition, first presented by <a href="/wiki/Rose_Ann_Scamardella" title="Rose Ann Scamardella">Rose Ann Scamardella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Storm_Field" title="Storm Field">Storm Field</a>, later succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Kaity_Tong" title="Kaity Tong">Kaity Tong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Snyder" title="Tom Snyder">Tom Snyder</a>, the latter only lasting several months before being replaced by 11:00 PM co-anchor Ernie Anastos.</p>
<p>In the wake of declining ratings, Grimsby was fired on April 16, 1986, a move for which Applegate drew considerable ire, and Grimsby was quickly hired by rival WNBC-TV. Beutel stepped down from the anchor desk in 2001, which concluded the longest tenure for a main anchor in New York City television history, at that time. His record has since been surpassed by <a href="/wiki/WNBC" title="WNBC">WNBC</a>'s Chuck Scarborough and <a href="/wiki/WXTV-DT" title="WXTV-DT">WXTV</a>'s Rafael Pineda. Scarborough's uninterrupted run behind the desk is second in New York television to Pineda, who started with WXTV in 1972.</p>
<p>In 1985, the station lured <a href="/wiki/WLS-TV" title="WLS-TV">WLS-TV</a>'s news director, Bill Applegate, from <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> to New York City. Applegate claimed credit for taking WLS-TV from last to first in only two years and ABC hoped he could work the same magic at the flagship station. Their hopes were rewarded in 1987 when Channel 7 surged back into first place. It has been the ratings leader in New York City since then, and has grown to become the most-watched broadcast television station in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.93present">2000–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2000–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>WABC-TV's news department is respected for its straightforward presentation (especially during breaking news). For the last decade, it has waged a spirited battle for first place, but for most of the time has held onto the lead, helped in part by lead-ins from highly rated talk and entertainment shows. For over 24 years (December 1986 to May 2011), the lead-in for the 5:00 PM <i>Eyewitness News</i> broadcast had been <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show" title="The Oprah Winfrey Show">The Oprah Winfrey Show</a></i> at 4:00 pm and its strong ratings brought viewers along to the 5:00 PM Newscast.</p>
<p>The newscasts were replayed on one of channel 7's digital subchannels, another which also carried a local weather and news channel. WABC-TV's website had a link for live streaming video of "Eyewitness News Now", which offered live local and national weather updated from <a href="/wiki/AccuWeather" title="AccuWeather">AccuWeather</a>. Local news headlines and updates were also provided. The format of "Eyewitness News Now" is similar to the defunct <a href="/wiki/NBC_Weather_Plus" title="NBC Weather Plus">NBC Weather Plus</a>. In February 2011, ABC pulled ENN as well as similar news channels on its Los Angeles and Chicago sister stations, replacing them in all three cities with a <a href="/wiki/Standard-definition_television" title="Standard-definition television">standard definition</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letterbox" title="Letterbox">letterboxed</a> simulcast of the <a href="/wiki/Live_Well_Network" title="Live Well Network">Live Well Network</a>.</p>
<p>On December 2, 2006, WABC-TV became the second station in the New York City market to begin broadcasting its local newscasts in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high definition</a>. On September 7, 2010, WABC-TV expanded its weekday morning newscast, moving its start time to 4:30 AM. 3 Days earlier on September 4, 2010, WABC added an hour-long extension of its Saturday morning newscast from 9:00 to 10:00 AM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On May 26, 2011, WABC-TV added another hour of local news at 4:00 PM to replace <i>Oprah</i>, which aired its last original episode the previous day.</p>
<p>On September 24, 2011, the station began broadcasting its newscasts and public affairs programs from a new street-level window studio at a former <a href="/wiki/Disney_Store" title="Disney Store">Disney Store</a> location in the ABC building on 66th Street and Columbus Avenue. The space previously used for news broadcasts was used to expand the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly_and_Michael" title="Live! with Kelly and Michael">Live with Kelly</a></i> studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In January 2012, the station also expanded its weekend 11:00 pm newscasts to an hour. On September 8, 2014, the station expanded its Noon newscast to one full hour from the previous half hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_with_Kelly"><i>Live with Kelly</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WABC-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Live with Kelly">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Live_with_Kelly" title="Live with Kelly">Live with Kelly</a></div>

<p>WABC-TV also produces the nationally-<a href="/wiki/Broadcast_syndication" title="Broadcast syndication">syndicated</a> talk show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly_and_Michael" title="Live! with Kelly and Michael">Live! with Kelly and Michael</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Until the station's newscasts were moved to a separate studio in 2011, the program originated in the same ground-floor studio at 7 Lincoln Square as <i>Eyewitness News</i>, thus creating a situation which forced local news updates broadcast during <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> and <i>Live</i> to be produced from the WABC-TV newsroom and the morning show's presence also limited the size of the <i>Eyewitness News</i> set.</p>
<p>The program's roots originated with <i>A.M. New York</i>, which debuted in 1970 as a local version of <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Today_(U.S._TV_program)" title="Today (U.S. TV program)">Today</a></i> show;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> its first host was <a href="/wiki/John_Bartholomew_Tucker" title="John Bartholomew Tucker">John Bartholomew Tucker</a>, who remained with the program until 1972. After Tucker's departure, a succession of hosts came and went, the most successful of whom was Stanley Siegel, who hosted from 1975 to 1978 (for a year beginning in 1977, the series was called <i>The Stanley Siegel Show</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> After 1980, the show was retitled <i>Good Morning New York</i>, whose co-hosts in the last years of its run in that form included <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Christian" title="Spencer Christian">Spencer Christian</a>, Andrea Kirby, <a href="/wiki/Judy_Licht" title="Judy Licht">Judy Licht</a>, Dick Wolfsie and longtime <i>Eyewitness News</i> reporter and anchor Doug Johnson. After years of a losing ratings battle against <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donahue_(TV_series)" title="Donahue (TV series)">Donahue</a></i> on WNBC-TV, WABC-TV cancelled <i>Good Morning New York</i> in early 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>The current show began as the station's second attempt at a local morning show a month later, aptly titled <i>The Morning Show</i> (using the "Circle 7" logo in the actual text for one of the "o"s) and was originally hosted by <a href="/wiki/Regis_Philbin" title="Regis Philbin">Regis Philbin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyndy_Garvey" title="Cyndy Garvey">Cyndy Garvey</a>. After Garvey's departure a year later, she was replaced by Ann Abernathy, who in turn, left in 1985 to return to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. That year, <a href="/wiki/Kathie_Lee_Gifford" title="Kathie Lee Gifford">Kathie Lee Johnson</a> (who would marry <a href="/wiki/Frank_Gifford" title="Frank Gifford">Frank Gifford</a> a year later) became Philbin's new co-host.</p>
<p>In 1988, Buena Vista Television (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney-ABC_Domestic_Television" title="Disney-ABC Domestic Television">Disney-ABC Domestic Television</a>) began syndicating the show nationally as <i>Live with Regis and Kathie Lee</i>. Gifford left the show in 2000 and was eventually replaced by <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Ripa" title="Kelly Ripa">Kelly Ripa</a>. Philbin left the show in November 2011 and the show aired for nearly a year as <i>Live! with Kelly</i> until former <a href="/wiki/New_York_Giants" title="New York Giants">New York Giants</a> defensive end <a href="/wiki/Michael_Strahan" title="Michael Strahan">Michael Strahan</a> became Ripa's permanent co-host in September 2012. Until May 2016, Strahan left the show to become a full-time anchor at <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i>, thus leaving Ripa as the solo host again.</p>
