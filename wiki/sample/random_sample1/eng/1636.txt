<p>The <b>WCW World Television Championship</b> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_championship#Gimmick.2Fstyle_championships" title="Professional wrestling championship">world television championship</a> owned by the now-defunct <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Championship Wrestling">World Championship Wrestling</a> (WCW) <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_promotion" title="Professional wrestling promotion">promotion</a>.</p>
<p>The title was created in 1974 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_Championship_Wrestling" title="Mid-Atlantic Championship Wrestling">Mid-Atlantic Championship Wrestling</a> (MACW) as a secondary title. It was known as the Mid-Atlantic Television Championship and then simply the NWA Television Championship a few years later. As Mid-Atlantic (later known as <a href="/wiki/Jim_Crockett_Promotions" title="Jim Crockett Promotions">Jim Crockett Promotions</a>) grew, the title became known as the NWA World Television Championship and in 1991 as the WCW World TV Championship.</p>
<p>The title was often defended in matches with a time limit of ten or fifteen minutes. More often than with other championships, title matches resulted in <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Draw" title="Professional wrestling">time limit draws</a> and the champion retaining the title. This was often used as a <a href="/wiki/Heat_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heat (professional wrestling)">heat</a>-building device to allow a <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">villain</a> champion to retain his title.The NWA version of the TV Title Belt had major Television Networks (NBC,CBS,ABC,and PDQ) on either side of the belt, henceforth, said titleholder is the champion of the television wrestling networks.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tully_Blanchard" title="Tully Blanchard">Tully Blanchard</a> had the longest World Television Championship reign, holding the title for 353 days. <a href="/wiki/Booker_T_(wrestler)" title="Booker T (wrestler)">Booker T</a> had the most reigns as World Television Champion, with six. <a href="/wiki/Arn_Anderson" title="Arn Anderson">Arn Anderson</a> holds the record for most days as champion, with 870 over four title reigns. The last champion was <a href="/wiki/Jim_Duggan" title="Jim Duggan">Jim Duggan</a>, who claimed the title while working as the WCW janitor after <a href="/wiki/Scott_Hall" title="Scott Hall">Scott Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Nash" title="Kevin Nash">Kevin Nash</a> threw it in the garbage and he found it in a <a href="/wiki/Dumpster" title="Dumpster">dumpster</a>. The title was retired on April 10, 2000 after the <a href="/wiki/Vince_Russo" title="Vince Russo">Vince Russo</a>-<a href="/wiki/Eric_Bischoff" title="Eric Bischoff">Eric Bischoff</a> WCW <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reboot_(continuity)" title="Reboot (continuity)">reboot</a>.</p>
