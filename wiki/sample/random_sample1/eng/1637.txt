<p><b>WHOI</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Television_station" title="Television station">television station</a> <a href="/wiki/City_of_license" title="City of license">licensed</a> for <a href="/wiki/Peoria,_Illinois" title="Peoria, Illinois">Peoria, Illinois</a>. It broadcasts a <a href="/wiki/Digital_television" title="Digital television">digital</a> signal on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UHF" title="UHF">UHF</a> channel 19 from a transmitter on North Stewart Street in <a href="/wiki/Creve_Coeur,_Illinois" title="Creve Coeur, Illinois">Creve Coeur</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Village_(United_States)" title="Village (United States)">village</a> in <a href="/wiki/Groveland_Township,_Tazewell_County,_Illinois" title="Groveland Township, Tazewell County, Illinois">Groveland Township, Tazewell County</a>. The station was the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>-<a href="/wiki/Network_affiliate" title="Network affiliate">affiliated</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_station" title="Television station">television station</a> for North-<a href="/wiki/Central_Illinois" title="Central Illinois">Central Illinois</a> until 2016. As of October 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=WHOI_(TV)&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, WHOI only carries the <a href="/wiki/Comet_(TV_network)" title="Comet (TV network)">Comet</a> network owned by their parent company, <a href="/wiki/Sinclair_Broadcast_Group" title="Sinclair Broadcast Group">Sinclair Broadcast Group</a>.</p>

<p>WHOI was Peoria's second television station, signing-on as <b>WTVH</b> on October 20, 1953. The station was founded by Hugh Norman and Edward Schoede. Hilltop Broadcasting, which co-owned the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_Star_(Peoria)" title="Journal Star (Peoria)">Peoria Journal Star</a></i> bought the station in 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pjs-whoiweek_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pjs-whoiweek-3">[3]</a></sup> Its first studios were on Main Street in Peoria. Originally broadcasting an <a href="/wiki/Analog_television" title="Analog television">analog</a> signal on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VHF" title="VHF">VHF</a> channel 8,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> it was a primary <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> affiliate but also carried shows from ABC and <a href="/wiki/DuMont_Television_Network" title="DuMont Television Network">DuMont</a>. WTVH lost DuMont when the network ceased operations in 1955, and lost CBS when WMBD-TV began broadcasting.</p>
<p>The Metropolitan Broadcasting Corporation, later known as <a href="/wiki/Metromedia" title="Metromedia">Metromedia</a>, purchased the station in 1959. In 1963, WTVH was bumped down to UHF channel 19 so that a third <a href="/wiki/Commercial_broadcasting" title="Commercial broadcasting">commercial</a> VHF station could sign-on in the <a href="/wiki/Quad_Cities" title="Quad Cities">Quad Cities</a> using that channel (the new station, <a href="/wiki/WQAD-TV" title="WQAD-TV">WQAD-TV</a>, is also an ABC affiliate). In 1965, <a href="/wiki/Metromedia" title="Metromedia">Metromedia</a> sold the station to Mid-America Media, owners of <a href="/wiki/WIRL" title="WIRL">WIRL radio</a> (1290 AM) who, on September 12 of that year, changed the call sign to <b>WIRL-TV</b>. It became <b>WRAU-TV</b> in 1971 and adopted its present <a href="/wiki/Call_signs_in_North_America" title="Call signs in North America">calls</a> of <b>WHOI</b> in 1985. The <a href="/wiki/WTVH" title="WTVH">WTVH</a> call sign was picked up by a station in <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse, New York</a> in 1976.</p>
<p>In 1987, WHOI came under the ownership of Adams Communications following a merger with its previous owner, Forward Communications. The station was sold to Brissette Broadcasting in 1991, then to <a href="/wiki/Benedek_Broadcasting" title="Benedek Broadcasting">Benedek Broadcasting</a> in 1996. When Benedek declared <a href="/wiki/Chapter_7,_Title_11,_United_States_Code" title="Chapter 7, Title 11, United States Code">Chapter 7</a> bankruptcy in 2002, WHOI was sold to Chelsey Broadcasting instead of <a href="/wiki/Gray_Television" title="Gray Television">Gray Television</a>. In April 2004, WHOI and <a href="/wiki/KHQA-TV" title="KHQA-TV">KHQA-TV</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hannibal,_Missouri" title="Hannibal, Missouri">Hannibal, Missouri</a>/<a href="/wiki/Quincy,_Illinois" title="Quincy, Illinois">Quincy, Illinois</a> <a href="/wiki/Media_market" title="Media market">media market</a> became two of the founding stations of <a href="/wiki/Barrington_Broadcasting" title="Barrington Broadcasting">Barrington Broadcasting</a>.</p>
<p>WHOI carried some programming from <a href="/wiki/UPN" title="UPN">UPN</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Voyager" title="Star Trek: Voyager">Star Trek: Voyager</a></i>, from the network's launch in January 1995<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pjs-whoivoyager_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pjs-whoivoyager-5">[5]</a></sup> until <a href="/wiki/WAOE" title="WAOE">WAOE</a> (channel 59) went on the air in 1999. Starting in 1998, WHOI began to run a <a href="/wiki/The_WB_100%2B_Station_Group" title="The WB 100+ Station Group">cable-only</a> <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">WB</a> affiliate. Known by the fictional call sign "WBPE", it was on channel 4 on most <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">cable</a> systems in the area. On September 18, 2006, when The WB and UPN <a href="/wiki/2006_United_States_broadcast_TV_realignment" title="2006 United States broadcast TV realignment">merged to create The CW</a>, "WBPE" became part of <a href="/wiki/The_CW_Plus" title="The CW Plus">The CW Plus</a> which is a similar operation to The WB 100+. WHOI added a new second <a href="/wiki/Digital_subchannel" title="Digital subchannel">digital subchannel</a> to simulcast this programming to offer non-cable subscribers access to The CW. The channel then began to use WHOI-DT2 as its official calls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>WHOI has been digital-only since February 17, 2009 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Analog_to_Digital_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Analog_to_Digital-7">[7]</a></sup> with the "WHOI" calls being transferred from the now-defunct analog channel 19 to the new digital channel 19 and the "WHOI-DT" call sign from the pre-transition digital channel 40 being permanently discontinued. However, the <a href="/wiki/Program_and_System_Information_Protocol" title="Program and System Information Protocol">PSIP</a> identifier still identifies the station's main channel on 19.1 as "WHOI-DT".</p>
<p>On March 2, 2009, it was made public that rival <a href="/wiki/WEEK-TV" title="WEEK-TV">WEEK-TV</a> (channel 25) would take over the operations of WHOI through joint sales and shared services agreements. It resulted in WHOI closing its longtime studios near its transmitter in Creve Coeur and moving into WEEK-TV's East Peoria facility on Springfield Road, along <a href="/wiki/Interstate_474" title="Interstate 474">I-474</a>, in <a href="/wiki/East_Peoria,_Illinois" title="East Peoria, Illinois">East Peoria</a>. Sixteen employees were transferred to WEEK-TV but as many as thirty were laid-off immediately.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pjs-whoiweek_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-pjs-whoiweek-3">[3]</a></sup> This left the five full-power commercial stations in the market operated by two entities; WEEK-TV already controlled the market's <a href="/wiki/MyNetworkTV" title="MyNetworkTV">MyNetworkTV</a> outlet, WAOE (owned by <a href="/wiki/Venture_Technologies_Group#Stations_owned_through_Four_Seasons_Broadcasting" title="Venture Technologies Group">Four Seasons Broadcasting</a>), under a separate joint sales agreement (JSA). The WHOI and CW subchannel websites were immediately changed to redirects to WEEK-TV's web address. As part of the agreement, Granite-owned CBS affiliate WTVH merged its operations with Barrington's NBC affiliate <a href="/wiki/WSTM-TV" title="WSTM-TV">WSTM-TV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Low-power_broadcasting" title="Low-power broadcasting">low-powered</a> CW affiliate <a href="/wiki/WSTQ-LP" title="WSTQ-LP">WSTQ-LP</a> in a similar arrangement on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 28, 2013, Barrington Broadcasting announced the sale of its entire group, including WHOI, to the Sinclair Broadcast Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b.26c-saletosinclair_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-b.26c-saletosinclair-9">[9]</a></sup> Sinclair already owned the license of <a href="/wiki/WYZZ-TV" title="WYZZ-TV">WYZZ-TV</a> (channel 43), which was sold to <a href="/wiki/Cunningham_Broadcasting" title="Cunningham Broadcasting">Cunningham Broadcasting</a> to satisfy <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">Federal Communications Commission</a> (FCC) regulations on <a href="/wiki/Duopoly_(broadcasting)" title="Duopoly (broadcasting)">duopoly</a> ownership. While most of Cunningham's stations are operated by Sinclair though <a href="/wiki/Local_marketing_agreement" title="Local marketing agreement">local marketing agreements</a>, WYZZ is operated separately by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nexstar_Broadcasting_Group" title="Nexstar Broadcasting Group">Nexstar Broadcasting Group</a> at the facility of <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> outlet <a href="/wiki/WMBD-TV" title="WMBD-TV">WMBD-TV</a>. The sale was completed on November 25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On February 11, 2014, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Quincy,_Illinois" title="Quincy, Illinois">Quincy</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Quincy_Media" title="Quincy Media">Quincy Newspapers</a> would acquire WEEK-TV from <a href="/wiki/Granite_Broadcasting" title="Granite Broadcasting">Granite Broadcasting</a>. Originally, Quincy intended to continue providing services to WHOI but Sinclair gave notice that the JSA/SSA between WHOI and WEEK-TV (which was originally set to expire in March 2017) would be terminated within nine months of Quincy closing on its purchase of WEEK-TV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvnc-saletoquincy_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvnc-saletoquincy-11">[11]</a></sup> The Quincy/Granite sale was completed on November 2, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 26, 2016, Quincy Media announced that it had acquired WHOI's ABC and <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> affiliations, and would consolidate them onto subchannels of WEEK beginning August 1, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cinewsnow-hoiweek_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-cinewsnow-hoiweek-13">[13]</a></sup> As an aspect of this deal, Quincy-owned <a href="/wiki/WSJV" title="WSJV">WSJV</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Bend,_Indiana" title="South Bend, Indiana">South Bend</a> similarly relinquished its Fox affiliation to Sinclair-owned <a href="/wiki/WSBT-TV" title="WSBT-TV">WSBT-TV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The ABC and CW subchannels were simulcast on WHOI for 60 days following the consolidation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pjr-weekabccw_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-pjr-weekabccw-15">[15]</a></sup> After the end of the transition period, the <a href="/wiki/Comet_(TV_network)" title="Comet (TV network)">Comet TV</a> affiliation moved to WHOI's main 19.1 channel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pjstar-20160802_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-pjstar-20160802-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
