<p><b>WOWOW</b> (<b>株式会社WOWOW(ワウワウ、WOWOW Inc.)</b> ), listed as <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">TYO</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www2.tse.or.jp/tseHpFront/StockSearch.do?callJorEFlg=1&amp;method=&amp;topSearchStr=4839" rel="nofollow">4839</a>, is a private <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">satellite broadcasting</a> and pay-per-view <a href="/wiki/Television_station" title="Television station">television station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Its headquarters are located on the 21st floor of the Akasaka Park Building in <a href="/wiki/Akasaka,_Minato,_Tokyo" title="Akasaka, Minato, Tokyo">Akasaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">Minato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Addresses_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Addresses-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its broadcasting center is in <a href="/wiki/Koto,_Tokyo" title="Koto, Tokyo">Koto</a>, Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Addresses_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Addresses-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 1, 2011, WOWOW expanded their single channel broadcast satellite service to provide three high-definition TV channels:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><b>WOWOW Prime</b> (WOWOWプライム), a general entertainment channel;</li>
<li><b>WOWOW Live</b> (WOWOWライブ), covering sports, documentaries and live performances; and</li>
<li><b>WOWOW Cinema</b> (WOWOWシネマ), a 24-hour movie channel.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WOWOW&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>WOWOW was the first 24/7, 3 channel, full high-definition broadcaster in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>WOWOW began broadcasting in analog on April 1, 1991. Digital broadcasting began on December 1, 2000. The network began with 207,753 subscribers (31.5 billion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yen" title="Yen">yen</a> in sales), growing to 2,667,414 two years later (64.5 billion yen in sales). As of December 2011, WOWOW claimed approximately 2.56 million subscribers to its digital service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On July 24, 2011, WOWOW shut down its analog broadcast.</p>
<p>WOWOW mostly rebroadcasts movies, but is also well known for showing (and even co-producing and/or assisting in the production of) original <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_O_(anime)" title="Big O (anime)">Big O</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Brain_Powerd" title="Brain Powerd">Brain Powerd</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Carried_by_the_Wind:_Tsukikage_Ran" title="Carried by the Wind: Tsukikage Ran">Carried by the Wind: Tsukikage Ran</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Trinity_Blood" title="Trinity Blood">Trinity Blood</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cowboy_Bebop" title="Cowboy Bebop">Cowboy Bebop</a></i> (the complete uncut version), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinreigari/Ghost_Hound" title="Shinreigari/Ghost Hound">Shinreigari/Ghost Hound</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Crest_of_the_Stars" title="Crest of the Stars">Crest of the Stars</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ergo_Proxy" title="Ergo Proxy">Ergo Proxy</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/X/1999" title="X/1999">X/1999</a>,</i> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SHUFFLE!" title="SHUFFLE!">SHUFFLE!</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Paranoia_Agent" title="Paranoia Agent">Paranoia Agent</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Now_and_Then,_Here_and_There" title="Now and Then, Here and There">Now and Then, Here and There</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_Chevalier_d%27Eon" title="Le Chevalier d'Eon">Le Chevalier d'Eon</a>,</i> the <i><a href="/wiki/Berserk_(2016_TV_series)" title="Berserk (2016 TV series)">2016 Berserk series</a></i> as well as the <i><a href="/wiki/Anime_Complex" title="Anime Complex">Anime Complex</a></i> block. Due to the looser broadcast standards for satellite television in Japan, WOWOW has become a primary means of widespread distribution for anime with themes or subject matter that the broadcast networks cannot show. Several anime studios have partnership deals for distributing their more mature series, with the famed <a href="/wiki/Madhouse_(company)" title="Madhouse (company)">Studio Madhouse</a> among them.</p>
<p>The channel's name is a double "Wow", and the three W's also stand for "World-Wide-Watching".</p>
<p>WOWOW also broadcasts <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>-dubbed <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_and_the_City" title="Sex and the City">Sex and the City</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sopranos" title="The Sopranos">The Sopranos</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_Case_(TV_series)" title="Cold Case (TV series)">Cold Case</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Medium_(TV_series)" title="Medium (TV series)">Medium</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_4400" title="The 4400">The 4400</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Project_Runway" title="Project Runway">Project Runway</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/South_Park" title="South Park">South Park</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>, among others. WOWOW has also screened <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">Ultimate Fighting Championship</a> events for Japanese audiences, the <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">Korean drama</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Name_Is_Kim_Sam_Soon" title="My Name Is Kim Sam Soon">My Name Is Kim Sam Soon</a></i> and the UK comedy sketch show <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Britain" title="Little Britain">Little Britain</a></i>, as well as the British drama/action show <i><a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Force" title="Ultimate Force">Ultimate Force</a></i> under the name <i>SAS: British Special Forces</i>.</p>
<p>Previously WOWOW's headquarters were in another facility in Akasaka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WOWOW&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>WOWOW has broadcast all four tennis Grand Slam Championships since 2008. They are the Australian Open, the French Open, Wimbledon and the US Open. They broadcast UFC Japan on February 26, 2012 and have since continued to broadcast UFC events.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="President">President</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WOWOW&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: President">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The president of WOWOW as of October 2011 was Nobuya Wazaki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
