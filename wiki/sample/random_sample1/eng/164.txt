<p><b>1901 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCMI</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">starting on Tuesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> F) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> G) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1901st year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 901st year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 1st year of the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>, and the 2nd year of the <a href="/wiki/1900s_(decade)" title="1900s (decade)">1900s</a> decade. As of the start of 1901, the Gregorian calendar was 13 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Crown_colony" title="Crown colony">British colonies</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">South Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Federation" title="Australian Federation">federate</a> as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Australia" title="Commonwealth of Australia">Commonwealth of Australia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Barton" title="Edmund Barton">Edmund Barton</a> becomes first <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Australia" title="Prime Minister of Australia">Prime Minister of Australia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> becomes a British protectorate.</li>
<li>The birth of <a href="/wiki/Pentecostalism" title="Pentecostalism">Pentecostalism</a> at a prayer meeting at Bethel Bible College in <a href="/wiki/Topeka,_Kansas" title="Topeka, Kansas">Topeka, Kansas</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_5" title="January 5">January 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">Typhoid fever</a> breaks out in a <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> jail, the first of two typhoid outbreaks in the United States during the year.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_7" title="January 7">January 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Alferd_Packer" title="Alferd Packer">Alferd Packer</a> is released from prison after serving 18 years for <a href="/wiki/Cannibalism" title="Cannibalism">cannibalism</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_9" title="January 9">January 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Kitchener,_1st_Earl_Kitchener" title="Herbert Kitchener, 1st Earl Kitchener">Lord Kitchener</a> reports that <a href="/wiki/Christiaan_de_Wet" title="Christiaan de Wet">Christiaan de Wet</a> has shot one of the "peace" envoys, and flogged two more, who had gone to his commando to ask the Burgher citizens of South Africa to halt fighting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_10" title="January 10">January 10</a> – In the first great <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> gusher, <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">oil</a> is discovered at <a href="/wiki/Spindletop" title="Spindletop">Spindletop</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beaumont,_Texas" title="Beaumont, Texas">Beaumont, Texas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_22" title="January 22">January 22</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> of the United Kingdom dies at age 81, after more than 63 years on the throne, and her son the Prince of Wales formally succeeds her as King <a href="/wiki/Edward_VII" title="Edward VII">Edward VII</a>.</li>
<li>The Grand Opera House in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a> is destroyed in a fire.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_28" title="January 28">January 28</a> – <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">Baseball</a>'s <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> declares itself a <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="February">February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1901&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/February_1901" title="February 1901">February 1901</a></div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2" title="February 2">February 2</a> – Funeral of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> at <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Chapel,_Windsor_Castle" title="St George's Chapel, Windsor Castle">St George's Chapel, Windsor Castle</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_5" title="February 5">February 5</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hay%E2%80%93Pauncefote_Treaty" title="Hay–Pauncefote Treaty">Hay–Pauncefote Treaty</a> signed by United Kingdom and United States, ceding control of the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a> to the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J._P._Morgan" title="J. P. Morgan">J. P. Morgan</a> buys mines and steel mills in the United States, marking the first billion dollar business deal.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Evansville,_Indiana" title="Evansville, Indiana">Evansville, Indiana</a>, a fire burns through the business district, causing $175,000 of damage.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_11" title="February 11">February 11</a> – Anti-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuits" title="Jesuits">Jesuit</a> riots sweep across Spain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_12" title="February 12">February 12</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viceroy_of_India" title="Viceroy of India">Viceroy of India</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Curzon" title="Lord Curzon">Lord Curzon</a> creates the new <a href="/wiki/North-West_Frontier_Province_(1901%E2%80%9355)" title="North-West Frontier Province (1901–55)">North-West Frontier Province</a> in the north of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punjab_region" title="Punjab region">Punjab region</a>, bordering <a href="/wiki/Emirate_of_Afghanistan" title="Emirate of Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_14" title="February 14">February 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Edward_VII" title="Edward VII">Edward VII</a> opens his first <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">parliament of the United Kingdom</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_15" title="February 15">February 15</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Alianza_Lima" title="Alianza Lima">Alianza Lima</a> Foundation is created in <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_20" title="February 20">February 20</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii Territory</a> Legislature convenes for the first time.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_22" title="February 22">February 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Mail_Steamship_Company" title="Pacific Mail Steamship Company">Pacific Mail Steamship Company</a>'s <a href="/wiki/SS_City_of_Rio_de_Janeiro" title="SS City of Rio de Janeiro">SS <i>City of Rio de Janeiro</i></a> sinks entering <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a>, killing 128.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_23" title="February 23">February 23</a> – The United Kingdom and <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a> agree the frontier between <a href="/wiki/German_East_Africa" title="German East Africa">German East Africa</a> and the British colony of <a href="/wiki/Nyasaland" title="Nyasaland">Nyasaland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_25" title="February 25">February 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/U.S._Steel" title="U.S. Steel">U.S. Steel</a> is incorporated by industrialist <a href="/wiki/J._P._Morgan" title="J. P. Morgan">J. P. Morgan</a> as the first billion-dollar corporation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_26" title="February 26">February 26</a>
<ul>
<li>Chi-hsui and Hsu-cheng-yu, <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a> leaders, executed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peking" title="Peking">Peking</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Middelburg,_Mpumalanga" title="Middelburg, Mpumalanga">Middelburg</a> peace conference fails in South Africa as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boers" title="Boers">Boers</a> continue to demand autonomy.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_27" title="February 27">February 27</a> – The Sultan of <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Turkey</a> orders 50,000 troops to the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Bulgaria" title="Principality of Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> frontier because of unrest in <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="March">March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1901&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/March_1901" title="March 1901">March 1901</a></div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a>
<ul>
<li>The United Kingdom, <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japan</a> protest at the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Sino</a>-<a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian</a> agreement on <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a>.</li>
<li>1901 <a href="/wiki/Census_of_India" title="Census of India">Census of India</a> taken, the fourth, and first reliable, <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_2" title="March 2">March 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Platt_Amendment" title="Platt Amendment">Platt Amendment</a>, limiting the autonomy of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> as a condition for the withdrawal of American troops.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_4" title="March 4">March 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/William_McKinley" title="William McKinley">William McKinley</a> is <a href="/wiki/Second_inauguration_of_William_McKinley" title="Second inauguration of William McKinley">sworn in</a> for a second term as President of the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_5" title="March 5">March 5</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_nationalist" title="Irish nationalist">Irish nationalist</a> demonstrators are ejected by police from <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons of the United Kingdom</a> in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_6" title="March 6">March 6</a> – In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bremen_(city)" title="Bremen (city)">Bremen</a>, an assassination attempt is made on <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_II,_German_Emperor" title="Wilhelm II, German Emperor">Wilhelm II, German Emperor</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_11" title="March 11">March 11</a> – The United Kingdom rejects the amended <a href="/wiki/Hay%E2%80%93Pauncefote_Treaty" title="Hay–Pauncefote Treaty">Hay–Pauncefote Treaty</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_13" title="March 13">March 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Harrison" title="Benjamin Harrison">Benjamin Harrison</a>, 23rd president of the United States, dies of pneumonia at age 67.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_17" title="March 17">March 17</a>
<ul>
<li>A showing of 71 <a href="/wiki/Vincent_van_Gogh" title="Vincent van Gogh">Vincent van Gogh</a> paintings in Paris, 11 years after his death, creates a sensation.</li>
<li>Student riots in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a> and Moscow.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_18" title="March 18">March 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Donahoe" title="Patrick Donahoe">Patrick Donahoe</a>, businessman and publisher of the <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pilot_(newspaper)" title="The Pilot (newspaper)">The Pilot</a></i>, dies aged 90.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_31" title="March 31">March 31</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1901_Black_Sea_earthquake" title="1901 Black Sea earthquake">1901 Black Sea earthquake</a> of 7.2 on the <a href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude_scale" title="Moment magnitude scale">moment magnitude scale</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_1901" title="United Kingdom Census 1901">United Kingdom Census 1901</a> is taken. The number of people employed in manufacturing is at its highest-ever level.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/December_10" title="December 10">December 10</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> ceremony is held in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> on the fifth anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Nobel" title="Alfred Nobel">Alfred Nobel</a>'s death.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_12" title="December 12">December 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Guglielmo_Marconi" title="Guglielmo Marconi">Guglielmo Marconi</a> receives the first trans-Atlantic radio signal, sent from <a href="/wiki/Poldhu" title="Poldhu">Poldhu</a> in England, UK to <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland</a>; it is the letter "S" in <a href="/wiki/Morse_code" title="Morse code">Morse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_20" title="December 20">December 20</a> – The final spike is driven into the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_Railway" title="Uganda Railway">Mombasa–Victoria–Uganda Railway</a> in what is now <a href="/wiki/Kisumu" title="Kisumu">Kisumu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_22" title="December 22">December 22</a> – Peace Sunday and Charles Aked, a Baptist minister in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, says about the war in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>: "Great Britain cannot win the battles without resorting to the last despicable cowardice of the most loathsome cur on earth — the act of striking a brave man's heart through his wife's honour and his child's life. The cowardly war has been conducted by methods of barbarism... the concentration camps have been Murder Camps." A crowd follows him home and breaks the windows of his house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAHO_WhiteCamps_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-SAHO_WhiteCamps-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Significance_of_1901_for_modern_computers">Significance of 1901 for modern computers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1901&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Significance of 1901 for modern computers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The date of Friday December 13 20:45:52 1901 is significant for modern computers because it is the earliest date representable with a signed <a href="/wiki/32-bit" title="32-bit">32-bit</a> integer on systems that reference time in seconds since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unix_epoch" title="Unix epoch">Unix epoch</a>. This corresponds to -2147483648 seconds from Thursday January 1 00:00:00 1970. For the same reason, many computers are also unable to represent an earlier date. For related reasons, many computer systems suffer from the <a href="/wiki/Year_2038_problem" title="Year 2038 problem">Year 2038 problem</a>. This is when the positive number of seconds since 1970 exceeds 2147483647 (01111111 11111111 11111111 11111111 in binary) and wraps to -2147483648. Hence the computer system erroneously displays or operates on the time Friday December 13 20:45:52 1901. In this way, the year 1900 is to the <a href="/wiki/Year_2000_problem" title="Year 2000 problem">Year 2000 problem</a> as the year 1901 is to the <a href="/wiki/Year_2038_problem" title="Year 2038 problem">Year 2038 problem</a>.</p>
