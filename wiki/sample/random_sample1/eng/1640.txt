<p><b>WATN-TV</b>, <a href="/wiki/Virtual_channel" title="Virtual channel">virtual channel</a> 24 (<a href="/wiki/Ultra_high_frequency" title="Ultra high frequency">UHF</a> <a href="/wiki/Digital_television" title="Digital television">digital</a> channel 25), is an <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>-<a href="/wiki/Network_affiliate" title="Network affiliate">affiliated</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_station" title="Television station">television station</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The station is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group" title="Nexstar Media Group">Nexstar Media Group</a>, as part of a <a href="/wiki/Duopoly_(broadcasting)" title="Duopoly (broadcasting)">duopoly</a> with <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/WLMT" title="WLMT">WLMT</a> (channel 30). The two stations share studios at the Shelby Oaks Corporate Park on Shelby Oaks Drive in the northeast section of Memphis,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> WATN's transmitter is located in the <a href="/wiki/Brunswick,_Tennessee" title="Brunswick, Tennessee">Brunswick</a> section of <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Tennessee" title="Arlington, Tennessee">Arlington</a>. The station also handles master control and some internal operations for <a href="/wiki/Jackson,_Tennessee" title="Jackson, Tennessee">Jackson</a>-<a href="/wiki/City_of_license" title="City of license">licensed</a> <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/WJKT" title="WJKT">WJKT</a> (channel 16). Syndicated programming on WATN includes <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Doctors_(2008_TV_series)" title="The Doctors (2008 TV series)">The Doctors</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Phil_(TV_series)" title="Dr. Phil (TV series)">Dr. Phil</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Maury_(TV_series)" title="Maury (TV series)">Maury</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WATN-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_an_independent_station">As an independent station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WATN-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: As an independent station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The station first signed on the air on September 10, 1978 as <b>WPTY-TV</b>, and was the first station on the UHF band and first <a href="/wiki/Independent_station_(North_America)" title="Independent station (North America)">independent station</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Media_market" title="Media market">market</a>, as well as the first new commercial station to sign on in Memphis since <a href="/wiki/WREG-TV" title="WREG-TV">WREG-TV</a> (channel 3) debuted 23 years earlier. Memphis had a longer wait for an independent station than other cities its size. However, the Memphis market has always been a medium-sized market because the surrounding suburban and rural areas aren't much larger than Memphis itself. The station originally operated from studios located at 2225 Union Avenue in Memphis.</p>
<p>It was owned by Petry Television (the source of its call letters), and ran a general entertainment format featuring cartoons, movies, sitcoms, westerns and drama series. WPTY also carried <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> programs whenever WREG-TV, <a href="/wiki/WMC-TV" title="WMC-TV">WMC-TV</a> (channel 5) and <a href="/wiki/WHBQ-TV" title="WHBQ-TV">WHBQ-TV</a> (channel 13) preempted network shows in favor of local programs. By 1983, WPTY gained competition when the <a href="/wiki/TVX_Broadcast_Group" title="TVX Broadcast Group">TVX Broadcast Group</a> signed on WMKW (channel 30, now <a href="/wiki/WLMT" title="WLMT">WLMT</a>), sparking a rivalry between the two independents. Petry sold the station to Precht Communications in 1984.</p>
<p>TVX signed a deal to affiliate all of its stations with <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> in 1987, which resulted in WMKW becoming Memphis' Fox affiliate. Precht Communications sold the station to Chase Broadcasting in 1988. In February 1990, Fox pulled its affiliation from channel 30 (by then known as WLMT) and gave it to WPTY. This occurred because WLMT had been sold a few months earlier, and TVX's affiliation agreement with Fox included a clause stating that if an under-performing TVX station was sold, it ran the risk of losing its affiliation.</p>
<p>Chase Broadcasting merged with <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_Broadcasting" title="Renaissance Broadcasting">Renaissance Broadcasting</a> in 1992. Due to <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">Federal Communications Commission</a> rules which limited the number of stations a company could own, Renaissance was forced to put WPTY and several other stations up for sale. WPTY was purchased by <a href="/wiki/IHeartMedia" title="IHeartMedia">Clear Channel Communications</a> (now <a href="/wiki/IHeartMedia" title="IHeartMedia">iHeartMedia</a>) later that year. In 1993, Clear Channel entered into a <a href="/wiki/Local_marketing_agreement" title="Local marketing agreement">local marketing agreement</a> (LMA) with WLMT's then-owner MT Communications, allowing the once rivals to pool resources and programming.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_an_ABC_affiliate">As an ABC affiliate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WATN-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As an ABC affiliate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, WPTY was set to be displaced as Memphis' Fox outlet after Fox unexpectedly bought longtime ABC affiliate WHBQ-TV. However, News Corporation had to run WHBQ as an ABC affiliate for over five months after the sale was completed. ABC ultimately chose to affiliate with WPTY; the two stations switched affiliations on December 1, 1995.</p>
<p>The station gradually took on the look of a traditional network affiliate, running mostly first run syndicated shows and a few sitcoms while WLMT ran cartoons, movies, sitcoms and some reality/talk shows. Shortly after joining ABC, WPTY picked up a secondary affiliation with <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">The WB</a>, airing the network's schedule in late nights. This was because Memphis, despite its relatively large size, didn't have enough stations for a separate WB affiliate. In 2001, Clear Channel bought WLMT outright from <a href="/wiki/Max_Media" title="Max Media">Max Media</a>, creating the first television <a href="/wiki/Duopoly_(broadcasting)" title="Duopoly (broadcasting)">duopoly</a> in the Memphis market. Clear Channel moved WB programming to WLMT in 2003.</p>

<p>On April 20, 2007, Clear Channel entered into an agreement to sell its entire television station group to <a href="/wiki/Newport_Television" title="Newport Television">Newport Television</a>, a company controlled by <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">private equity firm</a> <a href="/wiki/Providence_Equity_Partners" title="Providence Equity Partners">Providence Equity Partners</a>. Newport announced on July 19, 2012, that it would sell 12 of its stations, including WPTY and WLMT, to <a href="/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group" title="Nexstar Media Group">Nexstar Broadcasting Group</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvnc-saletonexstar_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvnc-saletonexstar-2">[2]</a></sup> The transaction was finalized on December 3.</p>
<p>On June 1, 2013, WPTY, WLMT and WJKT's operations were moved to a converted former <a href="/wiki/MCI_Inc." title="MCI Inc.">MCI</a> call center on the city's northeast side. Nexstar invested $5 million in constructing the new facilities, which included high definition cameras and other new studio equipment, a new set repurposed from sister station <a href="/wiki/KLRT-TV" title="KLRT-TV">KLRT-TV</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Little_Rock" title="Little Rock">Little Rock</a> (which consolidated its news department with <a href="/wiki/KARK-TV" title="KARK-TV">KARK-TV</a> earlier that year after Nexstar purchased the station through <a href="/wiki/Mission_Broadcasting" title="Mission Broadcasting">Mission Broadcasting</a> as a result of the Newport deal), equipment to allow reporters from its new sister station KARK to appear on-air, and a shift away from <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_automation" title="Broadcast automation">automation</a> for the production of its newscasts (the station formerly used <a href="/wiki/Ross_Video" title="Ross Video">Ross</a> Overdrive for newscast automation, but continues to use other Ross products in the new facilities). As a result of these changes, WPTY became the final station in the Memphis market to begin broadcasting its local newscasts in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high definition</a>. Alongside the new studio and HD transition, WPTY re-launched as "Local 24" and changed its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Call_letters" title="Call letters">call letters</a> to <b>WATN-TV</b> as part of a campaign coinciding with the move.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvnc-newstudio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvnc-newstudio-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Analog-to-digital_conversion">Analog-to-digital conversion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WATN-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Analog-to-digital conversion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>WATN-TV (as WPTY-TV) discontinued regular programming on its analog signal, over <a href="/wiki/Ultra_high_frequency" title="Ultra high frequency">UHF</a> channel 24, on June 12, 2009, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Digital_television_transition_in_the_United_States" title="Digital television transition in the United States">federally mandated transition from analog to digital television</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Analog_to_Digital_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Analog_to_Digital-7">[7]</a></sup> The station's digital signal remained on its pre-transition UHF channel 25, using <a href="/wiki/Program_and_System_Information_Protocol" title="Program and System Information Protocol">PSIP</a> to display WATN-TV's <a href="/wiki/Virtual_channel" title="Virtual channel">virtual channel</a> as 24 on digital television receivers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="News_operation">News operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WATN-TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: News operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Local24open.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="233" data-file-width="427" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a7/Local24open.png/220px-Local24open.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a7/Local24open.png/330px-Local24open.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/a/a7/Local24open.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Local24open.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>WATN-TV presently broadcasts 24½ hours of locally produced newscasts each week (with 4½ hours on weekdays and one hour each on Saturdays and Sundays). In addition, WATN produces an hour-long extension of its weekday morning newscast at 7 a.m. and a nightly at 9 p.m. newscast (in addition to a sports highlight program padding out the final 15 minutes of the latter program) for WLMT. Fox affiliate WJKT simulcasts WLMT's 9 p.m. newscast and sports program. That station has microwave facilities at its studios in Jackson which allows the transmitting of live shots from the area. During weather segments, WATN and WLMT use live radar data from the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> Weather Forecast Office on Farm Road (officially Walnut Grove Road) in Memphis.</p>
<p>After becoming an ABC affiliate, Clear Channel decided to invest in a news department for WPTY. When it launched on November 1, 1995, newscasts were initially branded as <i><a href="/wiki/NewsWatch_(branding)" title="NewsWatch (branding)">NewsWatch</a> 24</i> and featured an energetic, youthful and almost "grunge" look. From the start, in addition to the newscasts on channel 24, the station has also produced a prime time newscast on WLMT, initially titled <i>NewsWatch 30 at 9</i>, in competition with the in-house 9 p.m. newscast that WHBQ debuted when it joined Fox. Several years later, WPTY rebranded as <i>ABC 24 News</i> (with the WLMT newscast being renamed <i>UPN 30 News at 9</i>) and began to modify its style to reach a broader audience.</p>
<p>In 2002, WPTY adopted the <i><a href="/wiki/Eyewitness_News" title="Eyewitness News">Eyewitness News</a></i> format (which was previously used by WHBQ until 1997), and adopted a harder-edged, more aggressive and often "confrontational" approach to its reporting style. The change resulted in most of its original news anchors and reporters leaving or being laid off, with a complete overhaul in the station's image and presentation. During this time, WPTY's news operation had its share of recognition and awards. It was honored in 2005 with <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> broadcasting awards for "Best Breaking News", "Best Newscast", "Best Reporter" and "Best Sportscast". The station was also honored in 2005 with an <a href="/wiki/Edward_R._Murrow" title="Edward R. Murrow">Edward R. Murrow</a> and regional <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> as the "Best Weekend Newscast".</p>
<p>In 2006, the Associated Press honored WPTY with nine awards including "Best Overall Newscast". However, since its news department began, WPTY's newscasts have lagged in last place in the ratings, placing far behind long-dominating rivals WREG, WMC and WHBQ. In 2009, with continued low ratings, and under its new Newport Television ownership, the station brought in new management, leading to several staff layoffs. Gradually, WPTY's newscasts dropped most of the confrontational and aggressive style. In November 2010, after eight years of operating under the <i>Eyewitness News</i> name, WPTY reverted to the previous <i>ABC 24 News</i> brand. On April 29, 2012, WPTY began broadcasting its newscasts in <a href="/wiki/16:9" title="16:9">16:9</a> <a href="/wiki/Widescreen" title="Widescreen">widescreen</a> <a href="/wiki/Standard-definition_television" title="Standard-definition television">standard definition</a>.</p>
<p>When the station became WATN on June 1, 2013, it ushered in a complete overhaul of its news programming. With the move to its new Shelby Oaks studio, WATN began broadcasting newscasts in high definition on that date;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvnc-newstudio_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvnc-newstudio-3">[3]</a></sup> the newscasts were also rebranded as <i>Local 24 News</i>. On October 7, 2013, WATN debuted a local talk program, <i>Local Memphis Live</i> (replacing <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly_and_Michael" title="Live! with Kelly and Michael">Live! with Kelly and Michael</a></i>, which was dropped in early September) that competes with WHBQ's newscast and WREG's own news/talk program in the 9 a.m. timeslot. WMC-TV now airs <i>Live! with Kelly and Michael</i>.</p>
