<p>The <b>WWE Raw Women's Championship</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_professional_wrestling" title="Women's professional wrestling">women's professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_championship" title="Professional wrestling championship">championship</a> created and promoted by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_promotion" title="Professional wrestling promotion">professional wrestling promotion</a> <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> on the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a> brand. It is one of two women's championships for WWE's main roster, along with the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown_Women%27s_Championship" title="WWE SmackDown Women's Championship">SmackDown Women's Championship</a> on the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a> brand. The current champion is <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Flair" title="Charlotte Flair">Charlotte Flair</a>, who is in her fourth reign.</p>
<p>Introduced as the WWE Women's Championship on April 3, 2016 at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_32" title="WrestleMania 32">WrestleMania 32</a>, it replaced the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Divas_Championship" title="WWE Divas Championship">Divas Championship</a> and has a unique title history, separate from <a href="/wiki/WWE_Women%27s_Championship_(1956%E2%80%932010)" title="WWE Women's Championship (1956–2010)">WWE's original Women's Championship</a> and the Divas Championship. <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Flair" title="Charlotte Flair">Charlotte Flair</a>, then known simply as Charlotte, was the inaugural champion. As a result of the <a href="/wiki/2016_WWE_draft" title="2016 WWE draft">2016 draft</a>, the championship became exclusive to Raw with a subsequent rename, and SmackDown created the SmackDown Women's Championship as a counterpart title.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Raw_Women%27s_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On April 3, 2016, <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hall_of_Fame" title="WWE Hall of Fame">WWE Hall of Famer</a> <a href="/wiki/Lita_(wrestler)" title="Lita (wrestler)">Lita</a> appeared during the <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_32" title="WrestleMania 32">WrestleMania 32</a> pre-show and, after recapping the history of women's professional wrestling in WWE, unveiled the brand-new championship and declared that WWE's women would no longer be referred to as <a href="/wiki/WWE_Diva" title="WWE Diva">WWE Divas</a>, but as "WWE Superstars" just as their male counterparts are.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titlehistory_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-titlehistory-2">[1]</a></sup> This came after the term "Diva" was scrutinized by some commentators, fans, and several past and present WWE female performers, including then-Divas Champion <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Flair" title="Charlotte Flair">Charlotte</a>, who were in favor of changing the championship to the Women's Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup> It was also changed because some WWE female wrestlers felt it diminished their athletic abilities and relegated them to "eye candy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup> Lita then announced that the winner of the Divas Championship <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Basic_non-elimination_matches" title="Professional wrestling match types">triple threat match</a> between Charlotte, <a href="/wiki/Becky_Lynch" title="Becky Lynch">Becky Lynch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Banks" title="Sasha Banks">Sasha Banks</a> later that night would become the first-ever WWE Women's Champion, subsequently retiring the Divas Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2016_Champ_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-2016_Champ-6">[5]</a></sup> Charlotte, the final Divas Champion, became the inaugural WWE Women's Champion when she defeated Sasha Banks and Becky Lynch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-preWrestleMania32_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-preWrestleMania32-7">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the reintroduction of the <a href="/wiki/WWE_brand_extension" title="WWE brand extension">brand extension</a>, then champion Charlotte was <a href="/wiki/2016_WWE_draft" title="2016 WWE draft">drafted</a> to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a> brand on the July 19, 2016 premiere episode of <i>SmackDown Live</i>, making the championship exclusive to Raw. In response, <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a> created the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown_Women%27s_Championship" title="WWE SmackDown Women's Championship">SmackDown Women's Championship</a> on August 23, 2016. The WWE Women's Championship was subsequently renamed to reflect its exclusivity to Raw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titlehistory_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-titlehistory-2">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>When the title was introduced, it shared its name with the original Women's Championship. However, the new title does not share the same title history as the original, which was unified with the Divas Championship in 2010, with the combined title inheriting the latter's lineage and history. WWE acknowledges the original championship as its predecessor,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titlehistory_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-titlehistory-2">[1]</a></sup> and notes that the lineage of female champions dates back to <a href="/wiki/The_Fabulous_Moolah" title="The Fabulous Moolah">The Fabulous Moolah</a>'s reign in 1956.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2016_Champ_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-2016_Champ-6">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brand_designation_history">Brand designation history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Raw_Women%27s_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brand designation history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the championship was unveiled, there was no <a href="/wiki/WWE_brand_extension" title="WWE brand extension">brand division</a> as that had ended in August 2011. From its inception until the reintroduction of the brand extension in July 2016, then-champion Charlotte defended the title on both <i>Raw</i> and <i>SmackDown</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_belt_design">Championship belt design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Raw_Women%27s_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Championship belt design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Raw Women's Championship belt is similar in appearance to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Championship#Current_design" title="WWE Championship">WWE Championship</a> belt, with a few notable differences. The strap is smaller to fit the champion, and white, as opposed to black. The die-cut WWE logo in the center plate sits on a red background, as opposed to a black one. The small print below the logo reads "Women's Champion". Like the WWE World Championship belt, the Raw Women's Championship belt features two side plates, both separated by gold divider bars, with the WWE logo on the globe as default plates, which are customized with the current champion's logos as a similarity of the name plate feature of other championship belts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2016_Champ_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-2016_Champ-6">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reigns">Reigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Raw_Women%27s_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of January 25, 2017, overall there have been 7 reigns between 2 champions. Charlotte Flair, then known simply as Charlotte, was the inaugural champion, who defeated Sasha Banks and Becky Lynch in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triple_threat_match" title="Triple threat match">triple threat match</a> on April 3, 2016 at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_32" title="WrestleMania 32">WrestleMania 32</a>; her first reign is also the longest at 113 days (though WWE says 114), and she has the most reigns at four. Sasha Banks is the youngest champion at 24 years old and has the shortest reign at 20 days.</p>
<p>The current champion is Charlotte Flair, who is in her fourth reign. She defeated Sasha Banks in a <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_match" title="Iron Man match">30-minute Iron Man match</a> by a score of 3–2 in sudden death overtime at <a href="/wiki/Roadblock:_End_of_the_Line" title="Roadblock: End of the Line">Roadblock: End of the Line</a> on December 18, 2016.</p>
