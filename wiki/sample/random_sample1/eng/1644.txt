<p><b>Judgment Day</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> event, produced every May by <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_promotion" title="Professional wrestling promotion">professional wrestling promotion</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>. The event was created in 1998, with its inaugural event produced as an <a href="/wiki/In_Your_House" title="In Your House">In Your House</a> event in October of that year. The event was then replaced in 1999 by the PPV <a href="/wiki/WWF_Over_the_Edge" title="WWF Over the Edge">Over the Edge</a>, which saw the death of <a href="/wiki/Owen_Hart" title="Owen Hart">Owen Hart</a>. The event was then brought back in 2000 and was rebranded as an annual PPV event for WWE. To coincide with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Brand_Extension" title="WWE Brand Extension">brand extension</a>, the event was made exclusive to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown!</a> brand in 2004. In 2007, following <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_23" title="WrestleMania 23">WrestleMania 23</a>, brand-exclusive pay-per-view events were discontinued. The final event was held in 2009, with <a href="/wiki/WWE_Over_the_Limit" title="WWE Over the Limit">Over the Limit</a> replacing Judgment Day in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Judgment Day was a <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> (PPV) event consisting of a <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)#Main_event" title="Card (sports)">main event</a> and <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)#Undercard" title="Card (sports)">undercard</a> that featured championship matches and other various matches. The first Judgment Day was originally produced as an <a href="/wiki/In_Your_House" title="In Your House">In Your House</a> event for World Wrestling Federation (WWF), the former name of WWE. The In Your House event was titled <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day:_In_Your_House" title="Judgment Day: In Your House">Judgment Day: In Your House</a>. It took place on October 18, 1998 and aired <a href="/wiki/Live_television" title="Live television">live</a> on PPV. In 1999 Judgment Day was replaced by <a href="/wiki/WWF_Over_the_Edge" title="WWF Over the Edge">Over the Edge</a>, which saw the death of <a href="/wiki/Owen_Hart" title="Owen Hart">Owen Hart</a>. In 2000 Judgment Day was brought back and rebranded as an annual PPV event – production of In Your House events ceased.</p>
<p>In 2002 a <a href="/wiki/Court_order" title="Court order">court order</a> led to WWF <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment#World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">changing its name</a> to WWE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Later that year, WWE held a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Brand_Extension" title="WWE Brand Extension">draft</a> that split its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_World_Wrestling_Entertainment_employees" title="List of World Wrestling Entertainment employees">roster</a> into two distinctive brands of wrestling, <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a> and <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a>.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/ECW_(WWE)" title="ECW (WWE)">ECW</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Before the draft, matches featured wrestlers from the roster without any limitations; after the draft, matches only consisted of wrestlers from their distinctive brands. The first Judgment Day event to be produced under the WWE banner and with roster limitations was <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2003)" title="Judgment Day (2003)">Judgment Day (2003)</a>, which took place on May 18, 2003. The following year, WWE announced that PPV events, excluding <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania" title="WrestleMania">WrestleMania</a>, <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam" title="SummerSlam">SummerSlam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series" title="Survivor Series">Survivor Series</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble" title="Royal Rumble">Royal Rumble</a>, would be made exclusive to each brand; Judgment Day was made exclusive to the Smackdown! brand. After three years of being produced as a brand exclusive event, <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2006)" title="Judgment Day (2006)">Judgment Day (2006)</a> was the final Judgment Day event that was brand exclusive, as WWE announced that PPV events from then on would feature all three brands of WWE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Each Judgment Day event has been held in an <a href="/wiki/Arena" title="Arena">indoor arena</a>, with all eleven events taking place in the United States.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1998">1998</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1998">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day:_In_Your_House" title="Judgment Day: In Your House">Judgment Day: In Your House</a></div>
<p>The inaugural event was held on October 18, 1998 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosemont_Horizon" title="Rosemont Horizon">Rosemont Horizon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rosemont,_Illinois" title="Rosemont, Illinois">Rosemont, Illinois</a>. Nine professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)" title="Card (sports)">card</a> as well as four matches on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Heat" title="WWE Heat">Sunday Night Heat</a></i> before the show aired live, also known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Dark_match" title="List of professional wrestling terms">dark matches</a>. The event had an attendance of 18,153. The <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)#Main_event" title="Card (sports)">main event</a> scheduled on the card was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">Singles match</a> for the vacant <a href="/wiki/WWE_Championship" title="WWE Championship">WWF Championship</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">special guest referee</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/The_Undertaker" title="The Undertaker">The Undertaker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kane_(wrestler)" title="Kane (wrestler)">Kane</a> with the guest referee, <a href="/wiki/Stone_Cold_Steve_Austin" title="Stone Cold Steve Austin">Steve Austin</a>. The match ended in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#No_contest" title="Professional wrestling">no contest</a> after Austin pinned both wrestlers and ignored his orders to "raise either the Undertaker or Kane's hand." Austin was fired for the violation, but was quickly reinstated the following day on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monday_Night_Raw" title="Monday Night Raw">Monday Night Raw</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to the main event, the <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)" title="Card (sports)">undercard</a> featured a singles match for the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">WWF Intercontinental Championship</a> between reigning champion <a href="/wiki/Ken_Shamrock" title="Ken Shamrock">Ken Shamrock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mick_Foley" title="Mick Foley">Mankind</a>. Shamrock defeated Mankind and retained the championship. The other primary match on the undercard was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">Tag team match</a> for the <a href="/wiki/World_Tag_Team_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Tag Team Championship (WWE)">WWF Tag Team Championship</a>. The match featured <a href="/wiki/The_Headbangers" title="The Headbangers">The Headbangers</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mosh_(wrestler)" title="Mosh (wrestler)">Mosh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thrasher_(wrestler)" title="Thrasher (wrestler)">Thrasher</a>) defeating <a href="/wiki/The_New_Age_Outlaws" title="The New Age Outlaws">The New Age Outlaws</a> (<a href="/wiki/Road_Dogg" title="Road Dogg">Road Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Gunn" title="Billy Gunn">Billy Gunn</a>) by <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Disqualification" title="Professional wrestling">disqualification</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000">2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2000)" title="Judgment Day (2000)">Judgment Day (2000)</a></div>
<p>The second Judgment Day event was held on May 21, 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Hall" title="Freedom Hall">Freedom Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>. Six professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the card. The event had an attendance of 16,827 and grossed US$596,050 through ticket sales and pay-per-view buys. The main event scheduled on the card was an <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_match" title="Iron Man match">Iron Man match</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a> defeating <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">The Rock</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Michaels" title="Shawn Michaels">Shawn Michaels</a> as special guest referee, for the WWF Championship. Triple H won the match and the championship.</p>
<p>Other matches on the event card included a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Tables_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Double Tables match</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/D-Generation_X" title="D-Generation X">D-Generation X</a> (<a href="/wiki/Road_Dogg" title="Road Dogg">Road Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Waltman" title="Sean Waltman">X-Pac</a>) defeating <a href="/wiki/The_Dudley_Boyz" title="The Dudley Boyz">The Dudley Boyz</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_LoMonaco" title="Mark LoMonaco">Bubba Ray</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Devon_Hughes" title="Devon Hughes">D-Von</a>). The other featured match on the card was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">Submission match</a> for the WWF Intercontinental Championship between reigning champion <a href="/wiki/Chris_Benoit" title="Chris Benoit">Chris Benoit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a>. Benoit won the match and retained the championship.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001">2001</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2001">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>2001's Judgment Day was the third event under the Judgment Day chronology and was held on May 20, 2001 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARCO_Arena" title="ARCO Arena">ARCO Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>. Seven professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the card with two dark matches taking place before the event began airing live. The event had an attendance of 13,623 and grossed $674,855 through ticket sales and pay-per-views, topping the previous years grossing. The main event scheduled on the card was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">No Hold Barred match</a> for the WWF Championship and featured Steve Austin defeating The Undertaker to retain the championship.</p>
<p>Featured matches on the <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)" title="Card (sports)">undercard</a> included a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">Tag Team Turmoil match</a> featuring Chris Benoit and Chris Jericho defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acolytes_Protection_Agency" title="Acolytes Protection Agency">The APA</a> (<a href="/wiki/John_Layfield" title="John Layfield">Bradshaw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ron_Simmons" title="Ron Simmons">Faarooq</a>), <a href="/wiki/Dean_Malenko" title="Dean Malenko">Dean Malenko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Perry_Saturn" title="Perry Saturn">Perry Saturn</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Terri_Runnels" title="Terri Runnels">Terri</a>), The Dudley Boyz (Bubba Ray and D-Von) (with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_Hyson" title="Matt Hyson">Spike Dudley</a>), <a href="/wiki/Edge_and_Christian" title="Edge and Christian">Edge and Christian</a>, The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hardy_Boyz" title="Hardy Boyz">Hardy Boyz</a> (<a href="/wiki/Matt_Hardy" title="Matt Hardy">Matt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Hardy" title="Jeff Hardy">Jeff</a>) and <a href="/wiki/X-Factor_(professional_wrestling)" title="X-Factor (professional wrestling)">X-Factor</a> (X-Pac and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Polaco" title="Peter Polaco">Justin Credible</a>) (with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Bloom" title="Matt Bloom">Albert</a>). The other primary match on the undercard was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#.28Weapon.29_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Chain match</a> between Kane and Triple H for the WWF Intercontinental Championship, which Kane won to win the championship.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002">2002</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2002">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2002)" title="Judgment Day (2002)">Judgment Day (2002)</a></div>
<p>The fourth Judgment Day event was held on May 19, 2002 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaylord_Entertainment_Center" title="Gaylord Entertainment Center">Gaylord Entertainment Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a> and was the first Judgment Day event produced under the WWE name. The theme song was Broken by 12 stones. Eight professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the event card with one dark match between <a href="/wiki/William_Regal" title="William Regal">William Regal</a> and <a href="/wiki/D%27Lo_Brown" title="D'Lo Brown">D'Lo Brown</a> for the <a href="/wiki/WWE_European_Championship" title="WWE European Championship">WWE European Championship</a> taking place before the event went live. The event had an attendance of 14,521. The main event on the card was a Singles match for the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Championship" title="WWE Championship">WWE Undisputed Championship</a> featuring The Undertaker defeating <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hollywood Hulk Hogan</a> to win the championship.</p>
<p>Other matches that were scheduled on the card included a <a href="/wiki/Hell_in_a_Cell" title="Hell in a Cell">Hell in a Cell</a> match between Triple H and Chris Jericho which Triple H won by pinfall following a <a href="/wiki/Facebuster#Double_underhook_facebuster" title="Facebuster">Pedigree</a> on the top of the cell. Plus, <a href="/wiki/Edge_(wrestler)" title="Edge (wrestler)">Edge</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Angle" title="Kurt Angle">Kurt Angle</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Luchas_de_Apuestas" title="Professional wrestling match types">Hair vs. Hair match</a> and Steve Austin defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_Show_(wrestler)" title="Big Show (wrestler)">The Big Show</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ric_Flair" title="Ric Flair">Ric Flair</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Handicap_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Handicap match</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003">2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2003)" title="Judgment Day (2003)">Judgment Day (2003)</a></div>
<p>The 2003 event was held on May 18, 2003 at the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Coliseum" title="Charlotte Coliseum">Charlotte Coliseum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a> and had an attendance of 13,000. The event grossed $650,000 through ticket sales and pay-per-views despite receiving mixed-negative reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J-Day_just_pure_vomit_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-J-Day_just_pure_vomit-6">[6]</a></sup> Nine professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the event card with one dark match taking place on <a href="/wiki/WWE_Heat" title="WWE Heat">Sunday Night Heat</a> before the event went live. The main event on the card was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Stretcher_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Stretcher match</a> between The Big Show and <a href="/wiki/Brock_Lesnar" title="Brock Lesnar">Brock Lesnar</a> for the WWE Championship. The match was won by Lesnar to retain the championship.</p>
<p>Other matches that were scheduled on the card included a Singles match for the <a href="/wiki/World_Heavyweight_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)">World Heavyweight Championship</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Nash" title="Kevin Nash">Kevin Nash</a> defeating Triple H by disqualification, although Triple H retained the Championship. Other primary matches on the card were a <a href="/wiki/Battle_royal_(professional_wrestling)" title="Battle royal (professional wrestling)">Battle royal</a> for the vacant Intercontinental Championship and a <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">Tag team</a> <a href="/wiki/Ladder_match" title="Ladder match">ladder match</a> for the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Raw Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Championship</a> between <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Guerrero" title="Eddie Guerrero">Eddie Guerrero</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoshihiro_Tajiri" title="Yoshihiro Tajiri">Tajiri</a> facing <a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Greatest_Tag_Team" title="The World's Greatest Tag Team">Team Angle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shelton_Benjamin" title="Shelton Benjamin">Shelton Benjamin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Haas" title="Charlie Haas">Charlie Haas</a>). Guerrero and Tajiri won the match to win the championship.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004">2004</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2004">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2004)" title="Judgment Day (2004)">Judgment Day (2004)</a></div>
<p>The 2004 event was held on May 16, 2004 at the <a href="/wiki/Staples_Center" title="Staples Center">Staples Center</a> in Los Angeles and was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Friday_Night_Smackdown" title="WWE Friday Night Smackdown">SmackDown!</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Brand_Extension" title="WWE Brand Extension">brand</a>-exclusive event. Eight professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the event card with one dark match taking place before the event went live. The event had an attendance of 18,722 and grossed $813,000 through ticket sales and received 235,000 pay-per-view buys. The main event was a Singles match between <a href="/wiki/John_Layfield" title="John Layfield">John "Bradshaw" Layfield</a> and Eddie Guerrero for the WWE Championship, which Layfield won via disqualification when Guerrero was caught hitting Bradshaw with the WWE Championship belt. However, since the title could not switch hands due to a disqualification, Eddie Guerrero retained the title.</p>
<p>Featured matches on the undercard included a Singles match between The Undertaker and <a href="/wiki/Booker_T_(wrestler)" title="Booker T (wrestler)">Booker T</a>, which Taker won. Also on the undercard was a Singles match for the <a href="/wiki/WWE_United_States_Championship" title="WWE United States Championship">WWE United States Championship</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a> defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Goguen" title="René Goguen">René Duprée</a>, to retain the championship.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005">2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2005">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2005)" title="Judgment Day (2005)">Judgment Day (2005)</a></div>
<p>The seventh event under the Judgment Day chronology was held on May 22, 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/Target_Center" title="Target Center">Target Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>. Seven professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the event card with one dark match between <a href="/wiki/James_Maritato" title="James Maritato">Nunzio</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Yun" title="James Yun">Akio</a> taking place before the event went live. The event had an attendance of 9,500 and grossed $500,000 through ticket sales and pay-per-view buys which was much lower than <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2004)" title="Judgment Day (2004)">Judgment Day (2004)</a> (the previous year's event).The main event on the card was an <a href="/wiki/%22I_Quit%22_match" title='"I Quit" match'>"I Quit" match</a> for the WWE Championship between John Cena and John "Bradshaw" Layfield. The match was won by Cena after Layfield spoke the words "I Quit", to retain the championship.</p>
<p>Other matches that were scheduled on the event card included the encounter between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rey_Mysterio,_Jr." title="Rey Mysterio, Jr.">Rey Mysterio</a> and Eddie Guerrero, which Mysterio won. The other primary match on the undercard included a Singles match for the WWE United States Championship, in which <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Jordan" title="Orlando Jordan">Orlando Jordan</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Jon_Heidenreich" title="Jon Heidenreich">Heidenreich</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006">2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2006)" title="Judgment Day (2006)">Judgment Day (2006)</a></div>
<p>The eighth Judgment Day event was held on May 21, 2006 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Airways_Center" title="US Airways Center">US Airways Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>. This was the last Judgment Day that featured talent from only the SmackDown! brand. Eight professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the card and one dark match took place before the event began airing live. The event had an attendance of 14,000 and grossed $560,000 through ticket sales and pay-per-view buys. The theme song for the event was "This Fire Burns" (performed by <a href="/wiki/Killswitch_Engage" title="Killswitch Engage">Killswitch Engage</a>). The main event on the card was a Singles match for the World Heavyweight Championship and featured Rey Mysterio defeating John "Bradshaw" Layfield, to retain the championship.</p>
<p>In addition to the main event the <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Ring_(2006)" title="King of the Ring (2006)">2006 King of the Ring Final</a> was scheduled on the card between Booker T and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Lashley" title="Bobby Lashley">Bobby Lashley</a>, which Booker T won to then become known as <a href="/wiki/Booker_T_(wrestler)" title="Booker T (wrestler)">King Booker</a> as well as a Singles match between <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Daivari" title="Shawn Daivari">Daivari</a>) and The Undertaker, which Khali won.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007">2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2007)" title="Judgment Day (2007)">Judgment Day (2007)</a></div>
<p>The 2007 event was held on May 20, 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Scottrade_Center" title="Scottrade Center">Scottrade Center</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>. This was the first ever Judgment Day event that became tri-branded with talent from Raw, SmackDown!, and ECW. Eight professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the event card with one dark match taking place before the event went live. The event had an attendance of 10,500 and grossed $575,000 through ticket sales and pay-per-view buys. The theme song for the event was "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Wanna_Stop" title="I Don't Wanna Stop">I Don't Wanna Stop</a>" (performed by <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy Osbourne</a>). The main event and featured match on the Raw brand was the encounter between John Cena and The Great Khali for the WWE Championship. Cena won the match and retained the championship after making Khali submit to the <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#STF" title="Professional wrestling holds">STF</a>. The featured match on the SmackDown! brand was a Singles match between Edge and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dave_Batista" title="Dave Batista">Batista</a> for the World Heavyweight Championship, which Edge won to retain the championship.</p>
<p>Other matches that were scheduled on the card included the primary match on the ECW brand; a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Handicap_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Handicap match</a> for the <a href="/wiki/ECW_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="ECW World Heavyweight Championship">ECW World Championship</a> and featured Bobby Lashley defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_McMahon" title="Team McMahon">Team McMahon</a> (champion <a href="/wiki/Vince_McMahon" title="Vince McMahon">Vince McMahon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shane_McMahon" title="Shane McMahon">Shane McMahon</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Fatu" title="Eddie Fatu">Umaga</a>). Lashley, however did not win the championship due to pinning Shane and not the champion, Vince. The other featured match on the card was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Series_variations" title="Professional wrestling match types">two out of three falls match</a> for the WWE United States Championship, featuring Montel Vontavious Porter defeating Chris Benoit to win the championship.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2008)" title="Judgment Day (2008)">Judgment Day (2008)</a></div>
<p>The tenth event produced under the Judgment Day chronology was held on May 18, 2008 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qwest_Center_Omaha" title="Qwest Center Omaha">Qwest Center Omaha</a> in <a href="/wiki/Omaha,_Nebraska" title="Omaha, Nebraska">Omaha, Nebraska</a>. Seven professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the event card with one dark match between the teams of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bob_Holly" title="Bob Holly">Hardcore Holly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cody_Rhodes" title="Cody Rhodes">Cody Rhodes</a> facing <a href="/wiki/Santino_Marella" title="Santino Marella">Santino Marella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carly_Col%C3%B3n" title="Carly Colón">Carlito</a> for the World Tag Team Championship taking place before the event went live. The event had an attendance of 10,663 and grossed $653,740 through ticket sales and pay-per-view buys. The theme song for the event was "Take It All" (performed by Zididada). The main event on the card was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Cages" title="Professional wrestling match types">Steel Cage match</a> featuring Triple H defeating <a href="/wiki/Randy_Orton" title="Randy Orton">Randy Orton</a> for the WWE Championship.</p>
<p>Other matches that were scheduled on the card included a Singles match for the vacant World Heavyweight Championship featuring The Undertaker defeating Edge via <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Countout" title="Professional wrestling">countout</a>, which meant that neither wrestler won the championship. Plus, Jeff Hardy faced Montel Vontavious Porter in a Singles match, which Hardy won.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Judgment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2009)" title="Judgment Day (2009)">Judgment Day (2009)</a></div>
<p>The eleventh and final Judgment Day event took place on May 17, 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Allstate_Arena" title="Allstate Arena">Allstate Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rosemont,_Illinois" title="Rosemont, Illinois">Rosemont, Illinois</a>. Seven professional wrestling matches were scheduled on the event card with one dark match taking place before the event went live. The event had an attendance of 14,822. The theme song for the event was "<a href="/wiki/Black_Butterfly_(album)" title="Black Butterfly (album)">Rescue Me</a>" (performed by <a href="/wiki/Buckcherry" title="Buckcherry">Buckcherry</a>). The main event for the show was for the World Heavyweight Championship and featured Edge successfully retaining his championship to Jeff Hardy. The featured match from the Raw brand was Randy Orton facing Batista for the WWE Championship, which Orton got <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Disqualification" title="Professional wrestling">disqualified</a> in, however he retained the championship.</p>
<p>In addition to the main event several Singles matches were scheduled on the event card which included <a href="/wiki/CM_Punk" title="CM Punk">CM Punk</a> facing Umaga, ECW Champion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_(wrestler)" title="Christian (wrestler)">Christian</a> defending his championship against <a href="/wiki/Jack_Swagger" title="Jack Swagger">Jack Swagger</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Morrison_(wrestler)" title="John Morrison (wrestler)">John Morrison</a> versus Shelton Benjamin, Rey Mysterio defended his Intercontinental Championship against Chris Jericho and The Big Show facing John Cena.</p>
