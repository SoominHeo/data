<p>"<b>Waking Up in Vegas</b>" is a song by American singer <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>, taken as the fourth and final single from her second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/One_of_the_Boys_(Katy_Perry_album)" title="One of the Boys (Katy Perry album)">One of the Boys</a></i> (2008). Perry wrote the song with <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Child" title="Desmond Child">Desmond Child</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Carlsson" title="Andreas Carlsson">Andreas Carlsson</a>, and co-produced it with <a href="/wiki/Greg_Wells" title="Greg Wells">Greg Wells</a>, who is also credited for playing all the instruments on the song. It was officially released to US radio stations on April 21, 2009<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2009-04-21</span>)</span>. "Waking Up in Vegas" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> track about an underage couple getting drunk and blowing their money in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, paying homage to the phrase "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/What_happens_in_Vegas,_stays_in_Vegas" title="What happens in Vegas, stays in Vegas">What happens in Vegas, stays in Vegas</a>".</p>
<p>The song reached the top 10 in over eight countries, including the United States, where it peaked at number nine on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and became Perry's third top ten single, Canada, New Zealand, Ireland, Slovakia and others. The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was directed by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Kahn" title="Joseph Kahn">Joseph Kahn</a> and it features Perry and actor <a href="/wiki/Joel_David_Moore" title="Joel David Moore">Joel David Moore</a> in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Vegas</a> partying and gambling. Perry performed the track during her <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_Katy_Tour" title="Hello Katy Tour">Hello Katy Tour</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/California_Dreams_Tour" title="California Dreams Tour">California Dreams Tour</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_inspiration">Background and inspiration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waking_Up_in_Vegas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and inspiration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to A&amp;R <a href="/wiki/Chris_Anokute" title="Chris Anokute">Chris Anokute</a>, "Waking Up in Vegas" played a significant role in initiating Perry's pivotal move to <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>. Having been recorded while still under contract with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> for an album that was then shelved, Anokute later heard the track on a three-song demo and tried to convince his employers at Capitol to sign Perry, claiming it was a number-one single and its singer a star talent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-1">[1]</a></sup> In an interview with <a href="/wiki/HitQuarters" title="HitQuarters">HitQuarters</a>, one of the songwriters of the song, <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Carlsson" title="Andreas Carlsson">Andreas Carlsson</a>, claimed that he worked together with Perry and <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Child" title="Desmond Child">Desmond Child</a> to write "Waking Up In Vegas". Carlsson remarked that they "really wanted to tell the story that described that moment when everybody's checking out on Vegas after they’ve had their fun. And Katy is the perfect artist to tell such a story - she has humour, and she knows how to deliver it." Carlsson also said that two of the first aspects of the song to be composed were the main guitar riff - which he already had pre-prepared - and the phrase "put your money where your mouth is," which they were very keen to use and which for a time was almost used as the song's title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters2-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an interview for <i>Pop Eater</i>, Perry revealed the inspiration behind the track, claiming:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"What inspired it [was] this boy I was dating at the time. I was 21. We went to Vegas on a whim and we decided to get fake married. We took all the pictures with the minister, with the fake cake, in the fake chapel and got a fake marriage certificate. We went and bought a wedding dress and a suit at a thrift store, and scanned the pictures and the certificate to my family members, my manager at the time [and] totally freaked the s--- out of them. It was the most hilarious, stupid prank I've ever pulled. I still have the wedding dress and the certificate."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popeater_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-popeater-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>"Waking Up in Vegas" was eventually picked as the album's fourth and final single, with a radio edit version of the song being released on April 7, 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> as well as being sent to US radio stations on April 21, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On the single's artwork, Perry is seen with two <a href="/wiki/Dice" title="Dice">dices</a> behind her, giving more emphasis to the song's lyrical content of being in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vegas" title="Vegas">Vegas</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition_and_lyrics">Composition and lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waking_Up_in_Vegas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition and lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Waking Up in Vegas" was produced by <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greg_Wells" title="Greg Wells">Greg Wells</a>, who is also responsible for all instruments of the track, as well as for recording the track at the Rocket Carousel Studio, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. According to the sheet music published at Musicnotes.com, by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfred_Music_Publishing" title="Alfred Music Publishing">Alfred Music Publishing</a>, the song is written in the <a href="/wiki/Key_(music)" title="Key (music)">key</a> of <a href="/wiki/F_(musical_note)" title="F (musical note)">F major</a>, and moves at a tempo of 126 <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">beats per minute</a>. Perry's vocals span from the low note of <a href="/wiki/A_(musical_note)" title="A (musical note)">A3</a> to the high note of <a href="/wiki/C_(musical_note)" title="C (musical note)">C5</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Musically, the song has been described as a <a href="/wiki/Power_pop" title="Power pop">power pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Power_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Power-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digital_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-digital-8">[8]</a></sup> Lyrically, according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plugged_In" title="Plugged In">Plugged In</a>, "Waking Up in Vegas" talks about an underage couple get[ting] drunk and blow[ing] their money before [literally] 'waking Up in Vegas'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pluggedin_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-pluggedin-9">[9]</a></sup> Perry added that the song is not about apologizing, "[It's] basically a song about getting into trouble with your best friend or your boyfriend or your girlfriend - or whoever you're with - and not having any 'I'm sorry for what I did'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popeater_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-popeater-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>The song debuted on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> at number 107 following her performance on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Takeaway" title="Saturday Night Takeaway">Ant &amp; Dec's Saturday Night Takeaway</a>, and peaked at number 19 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. In the US, it debuted at number 65 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> peaking at number nine, becoming her third top-ten single.</p>
<p>"Waking Up in Vegas" also went on to become Perry's second number one hit on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40_(Pop_Songs)" title="Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)">Mainstream Top 40</a>, matching the airplay success of "Hot n Cold". In Canada, the song was driven all the way to number five and then number two thanks in part to her performance on <i>Idol</i>, but also due to increasing sales and airplay. On the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Singles_Chart" title="Irish Singles Chart">Irish Singles Chart</a> it peaked at number eight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Ultimately, the song's success was considered a rebound for Perry after "Thinking of You" failed to ignite the charts. The single also helped <i>One of the Boys</i> return to the top 50 of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> albums chart. "Waking Up In Vegas" was the 33rd most purchased song on iTunes in the US during 2009. As of January 2015, the song has sold 2.3 million copies in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The song also peaked at number nine in New Zealand, being certificated Gold.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waking_Up_in_Vegas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Billboard</i> gave the song a positive review: "most radio-friendly song yet."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Digital Spy gave "Waking Up in Vegas" 3/5 stars, calling the song "a giddy pop-rock romp featuring an ace sing-along chorus."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digital_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-digital-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

The music video for "Waking Up in Vegas" showcasing Perry in a <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini_Murci%C3%A9lago" title="Lamborghini Murciélago">Lamborghini Murciélago</a> as she is about to race down the streets of Las Vegas.</div>

<p>The music video was directed by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Kahn" title="Joseph Kahn">Joseph Kahn</a>, and was shot in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a> on March 26, 2009. It made its premiere when released on April 28, 2009 on the Australian, UK, and U.S. <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>.</p>
<p>It begins with Perry holding hands with her love interest played by <a href="/wiki/Joel_David_Moore" title="Joel David Moore">Joel David Moore</a>. They are in a laundromat, standing in front of a slot machine. It is the morning after the night before, setting the theme of the song's lyric "That's what you get for waking up in Vegas". Moore drops one coin into the slot machine and pulls the handle. Three "blazing 7" symbols land on the payline, and the machine delivers a jackpot in quarters. Moore and Perry look at each other in astonishment.</p>
<p>The next scene shows them winning at roulette the night before. They then head to the hotel room where they kick out <a href="/wiki/Penn_%26_Teller" title="Penn &amp; Teller">Penn and Teller</a>. Subsequent scenes show the couple playing various casino games and parlaying their winnings into millions, defeating poker legend <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Negreanu" title="Daniel Negreanu">Daniel Negreanu</a>, being welcomed into to the <a href="/wiki/Palms_Casino_Resort" title="Palms Casino Resort">Palms Hotel</a>, by the owners Gavin and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_J._Maloof,_Jr." title="George J. Maloof, Jr.">George J. Maloof, Jr.</a>. As the winning streak continues Perry and Moore enjoy a lavish lifestyle, racing through the streets of Las Vegas in his-and-hers <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini_Murci%C3%A9lago" title="Lamborghini Murciélago">Lamborghini Murciélagos</a>. At the height of the streak they are dressed in show costumes riding in chariots down Fremont Street, accompanied by a fire breather and an elephant. Then they are feasting at a Roman-style banquet, in a manner reminiscent of the <a href="/wiki/Last_Supper" title="Last Supper">Last Supper</a>.</p>
<p>Perry and Moore kiss in a <a href="/wiki/Money_booth" title="Money booth">money booth</a>. After the kiss, the couple's luck changes. They begin to argue. They lose all their winnings, but save a single coin and are ejected from their hotel suite, which Penn and Teller reclaim (even performing a card trick before slamming the door on the couple). Perry steals food from a room service tray. The video ends in the laundromat where it began. The couple is broke. Moore places a single coin in the slot machine and pulls the handle. Three "blazing 7" symbols land on the payline, and the machine delivers a jackpot in quarters. Moore and Perry look at each other in astonishment as the screen goes to black.</p>

<p>Perry performed "Waking Up in Vegas" on <i><a href="/wiki/Later..._with_Jools_Holland" title="Later... with Jools Holland">Later... with Jools Holland</a></i> in March 2008. She was also a guest on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Takeaway" title="Saturday Night Takeaway">Ant and Dec's Saturday Night Takeaway</a></i> in March 2009 where she performed "Waking Up in Vegas" instead of her then-current single, "<a href="/wiki/Thinking_of_You_(Katy_Perry_song)" title="Thinking of You (Katy Perry song)">Thinking of You</a>". which had been released that month. She performed on a Las Vegas-themed stage with her backing band dressed as <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>. The single was released to Australian radio on March 23, where it became the fourth most added song in its release week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Perry performed the song on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> on May 13, 2009. During the performance, she was dressed in an Elvis-themed outfit and a cape emblazoned with Adam Lambert's name on it. The video stars <i><a href="/wiki/Grandma%27s_Boy_(2006_film)" title="Grandma's Boy (2006 film)">Grandma's Boy</a></i> actor, <a href="/wiki/Joel_David_Moore" title="Joel David Moore">Joel David Moore</a>. Perry performed the song on July 24, 2009 on NBC's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today_(NBC_program)" title="Today (NBC program)">Today</a></i> show in New York's Rockefeller Plaza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The song was a part of the former's set list on <i>Warped Tour 2008</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_Katy_Tour" title="Hello Katy Tour">Hello Katy Tour</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Tour_2009_(No_Doubt)" title="Summer Tour 2009 (No Doubt)">Summer Tour 2009</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/California_Dreams_Tour" title="California Dreams Tour">California Dreams Tour</a></i>.</p>
