<p>"<b>We Are One (Ole Ola)</b>" is a song recorded by Cuban American rapper <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/One_Love,_One_Rhythm_%E2%80%93_The_2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Official_Album" title="One Love, One Rhythm – The 2014 FIFA World Cup Official Album">One Love, One Rhythm – The 2014 FIFA World Cup Official Album</a></i> (2014). It is the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_official_songs" title="FIFA World Cup official songs">official song</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and features guest vocals from American entertainer <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a> and Brazilian singer <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Leitte" title="Claudia Leitte">Claudia Leitte</a>. The artists co-wrote the song with <a href="/wiki/Sia_Furler" title="Sia Furler">Sia</a>, <a href="/wiki/RedOne" title="RedOne">RedOne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Mercer" title="Danny Mercer">Danny Mercer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Luke" title="Dr. Luke">Dr. Luke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cirkut_(record_producer)" title="Cirkut (record producer)">Cirkut</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Troelsen" title="Thomas Troelsen">Thomas Troelsen</a>; the latter three are also the producers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Whilst receiving positive reviews from critics, initially the song received some negative reactions from Brazilians over its lack of Brazilian feeling. To change that, another version was also released and the music changed slightly to fit Afro Brazilian group <a href="/wiki/Olodum" title="Olodum">Olodum</a>'s style of drumming - that version being the one that was used in the music video for the song. "We Are One" has experienced commercial success, reaching the top 20 in countries including Austria, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, and World Cup's host country, Brazil. The song went to its peak at no.1 in 3 countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_release">Background and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=We_Are_One_(Ole_Ola)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 22, 2014, <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music Entertainment</a> announced that "We Are One (Ole Ola)" would serve as the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_official_songs" title="FIFA World Cup official songs">official song</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> At the occasion, Pitbull said, "I'm honored to join Jennifer Lopez and Claudia Leitte at the FIFA World Cup to bring the world together. I truly believe that this great game and the power of music will help unify us because we are best when we are one."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Lopez said Pitbull asked her to join the song: "I can't take credit for this. This was one of Pitbull's call-ins. He had this record, and he's like, 'I think this record could be great for the World Cup. He's like, 'Will you do it with me?' And I go, 'Yeah, of course.'"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A solo version of the song featuring only Pitbull leaked online on February 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On April 8, Pitbull tweeted an <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> link to the track, debuting the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffpost_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-huffpost-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=We_Are_One_(Ole_Ola)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song received generally lukewarm reviews. Carl Williott of <a href="/wiki/Idolator_(website)" title="Idolator (website)">Idolator</a> opined that there is too much of Pitbull in the song, and not enough Lopez or Leitte. He, however, stated that the song is an upgrade from <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Waka_Waka_(This_Time_for_Africa)" title="Waka Waka (This Time for Africa)">Waka Waka (This Time for Africa)</a>", the official song of the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuse.tv" title="Fuse.tv">Fuse.tv</a> wrote that, similarly to "Waka Waka", the song "blends the feel of the tournament's hosting country with an accessible, exciting pop sound that has instant repeat appeal".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Judy Cantor-Navas of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> described the song as "surprisingly breezy",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> with <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> noting it to contain the "rah-rah international unity you'd expect for a Cup song".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Luís Antônio Giron of <i><a href="/wiki/%C3%89poca_(Brazilian_magazine)" title="Época (Brazilian magazine)">Época</a></i> criticized the album saying "maybe I'm wrong and Brazil only crave to be the same as what FIFA wants it to be. Brazil is <i>fifalized</i>". And praised music produced in Miami, "bomb on any track."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song has received some criticism from Brazilian and football fans over its tone and lyrics, particularly with the lyrics and video being labeled as a "big pile of clichés" and its failure to pay homage to Brazil's own rich musical heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Such criticism has pointed out the small amount of actual Portuguese lyrics involved,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and critics stating their confusion to why "were Cuban-American rapper Pitbull and Bronx-born Puerto Rican singer Jennifer Lopez were chosen for the song, when there are so many other great musicians in the land of bossa nova", as well as its "reinforcement of stereotypes with smiling, barefoot children and semi-naked, samba-dancing women".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theweek.co.uk_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-theweek.co.uk-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=We_Are_One_(Ole_Ola)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2014, Pitbull, Leitte and Lopez shot the music video for "We Are One (Ole Ola)" with <a href="/wiki/Olodum" title="Olodum">Olodum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale,_Florida" title="Fort Lauderdale, Florida">Fort Lauderdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, United States. Its concept was to simulate <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a> in its home country Brazil with the Olodum mix of the song being used as the soundtrack. It was released on May 16, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffpost_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-huffpost-6">[6]</a></sup> As of January 2017, the video has received over 540 million views.</p>
