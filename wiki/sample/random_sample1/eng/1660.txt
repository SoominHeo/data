<p>The <b>Wii MotionPlus</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">Wiiモーションプラス</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an expansion device for the <a href="/wiki/Wii_Remote" title="Wii Remote">Wii Remote</a> video game controller for the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> that allows it to capture complex motion more accurately. According to <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>, the sensor in the device supplements the <a href="/wiki/Accelerometer" title="Accelerometer">accelerometer</a> and Sensor Bar capabilities of the Wii Remote to enable actions to be rendered identically on the screen in real time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Introducing_MotionPlus_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Introducing_MotionPlus-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Wii's successor console, the <a href="/wiki/Wii_U" title="Wii U">Wii U</a>, supports the Wii MotionPlus and its peripherals in games where use of <a href="/wiki/Wii_U_GamePad" title="Wii U GamePad">its touchscreen-built-in primary controller</a> is not necessary.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wii_MotionPlus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Wii MotionPlus was announced by Nintendo in a press release on July 14, 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Introducing_MotionPlus_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Introducing_MotionPlus-4">[4]</a></sup> and revealed the next day at a press conference at the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Entertainment_Expo" title="Electronic Entertainment Expo">E3</a> Media &amp; Business Summit. It was released in June 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MotionPlus_NA_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MotionPlus_NA-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MotionPlus_EU_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MotionPlus_EU-1">[1]</a></sup> On May 3, 2010, Nintendo announced that starting May 9, 2010, the company will include its Wii Sports Resort game and MotionPlus controller with new consoles with no price increase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wii_Bundle_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wii_Bundle-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wii_MotionPlus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Wii MotionPlus was developed by Nintendo in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Game_development_tool" title="Game development tool">game development tool</a> company <a href="/wiki/AiLive" title="AiLive">AiLive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sutra2008_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sutra2008-6">[6]</a></sup> The sensor used is an <a href="/wiki/InvenSense" title="InvenSense">InvenSense</a> <a class="external text" href="http://invensense.com/mems/gaming.html" rel="nofollow">IDG-600</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InvenSense_press_release_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-InvenSense_press_release-7">[7]</a></sup> or IDG-650 in later units, designed in accordance to Nintendo's specification;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Virginia_Ars_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Virginia_Ars-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Virginia_IGN_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Virginia_IGN-9">[9]</a></sup> with a high <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_range" title="Dynamic range">dynamic range</a>, high <a href="/wiki/Shock_(mechanics)" title="Shock (mechanics)">mechanical shock</a> tolerance, high temperature and <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a> resistance, and small physical size.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IDG-600_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-IDG-600-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iwata_Asks_2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Iwata_Asks_2-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Wii MotionPlus was conceived soon after the completion of the initial Wii Remote design, to address developer demand for more capable motion sensing, but was only announced after the sensors could be purchased in sufficient volume at a reasonable price.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iwata_Asks_1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Iwata_Asks_1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> During a developer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Round_table" title="Round table">roundtable</a> discussion at E3 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/Wii_Sports_Resort" title="Wii Sports Resort">Wii Sports Resort</a></i> producer <a href="/wiki/Katsuya_Eguchi" title="Katsuya Eguchi">Katsuya Eguchi</a> discussed the potential influence of the Wii MotionPlus on the Wii's existing market, stating that Nintendo was looking at whether the MotionPlus capability will be built into the Wii Remote in the future, or kept "as an attachment we only use for certain software."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2009, a black Wii MotionPlus was released to coincide with the release of the black Wii console. Bundles containing a black Wii Remote and MotionPlus attachment were also released. In North America, all black, blue, and pink Wii remotes are bundled with a MotionPlus. The Wii Remote Jacket accompanying each black Wii Remote is itself black, while the jacket in blue and pink bundles is the standard translucent color. In a 2010 Nintendo Conference, the Wii Remote Plus was announced, which places the function of the MotionPlus into a regular sized Wii remote.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technology_and_features">Technology and features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wii_MotionPlus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Technology and features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The device incorporates a dual-axis<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IDG-600_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-IDG-600-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vibrating_structure_gyroscope#Tuning_fork_gyroscope" title="Vibrating structure gyroscope">tuning fork gyroscope</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and a single-axis gyroscope which can determine rotational motion. The information captured by the angular rate sensor can then be used to distinguish true linear motion from the accelerometer readings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> This allows for the capture of more complex movements than possible with the Wii Remote alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iwata_Asks_1_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Iwata_Asks_1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Wii MotionPlus features a pass-through External Extension Connector, allowing other expansions such as the Nunchuk or Classic Controller to be used simultaneously with the device. The attachment has a color-coded mechanical slide switch for locking the clip release. When attached to the (original, non-MotionPlus) Wii Remote, the add-on unit extends the length of the controller body<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iwata_Asks_3_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Iwata_Asks_3-18">[18]</a></sup> by approximately 1.5 in (4 cm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pass-through_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-pass-through-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated.">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup> Each Wii MotionPlus includes a longer version of the Wii Remote Jacket to accommodate the added length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iwata_Asks_1_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Iwata_Asks_1-12">[12]</a></sup> However, the newer Wii Remote Plus includes the MotionPlus functionality without any extra length.</p>
<p>The device is only used by games that have been specifically developed to use its functionality. It can remain attached to the Wii Remote when playing games that do not support it without causing any problems, but it will not enhance gameplay. The add-on version of the device must be detached when using the Wii Remote with certain accessories such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wii_Wheel" title="Wii Wheel">Wii Wheel</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wii_Zapper" title="Wii Zapper">Wii Zapper</a> and when using most third-party peripherals, such as the <a href="/wiki/Guitar_Hero" title="Guitar Hero">Guitar Hero</a> controllers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wii_Remote_Plus">Wii Remote Plus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wii_MotionPlus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wii Remote Plus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Wii_Remote#Wii_Remote_Plus" title="Wii Remote">Wii Remote § Wii Remote Plus</a></div>

<p>In September 2010, rumors were circulating of a Wii Remote with Wii MotionPlus already built in after the box art for the upcoming <i><a href="/wiki/FlingSmash" title="FlingSmash">FlingSmash</a></i> revealed it to be bundled with "Wii Remote Plus". Nintendo initially declined to comment, but later announced the device on September 29, 2010, confirming it to be a Wii Remote with MotionPlus built in, allowing players to use peripherals like the <a href="/wiki/Wii_Zapper" title="Wii Zapper">Wii Zapper</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wii_Wheel" title="Wii Wheel">Wii Wheel</a> without having to remove Wii MotionPlus from the Wii Remote. It competes with the <a href="/wiki/Kinect" title="Kinect">Kinect</a> and the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Move" title="PlayStation Move">PlayStation Move</a> with <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Eye" title="PlayStation Eye">PlayStation Eye</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motion_controllers" title="Motion controllers">motion controllers</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> and the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> home consoles, respectively. Nintendo later announced that the remote would be available in white, black, blue and pink. It was released in Australia on October 28, 2010, in Europe on November 5, 2010, in North America on November 7, 2010 and in Japan on November 11, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It was also released as part of a bundle containing Wii Sports, New Super Mario Bros. Wii, a red Wii, red Wii Remote Plus and red Nunchuk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> It has been announced that the European version of <i><a href="/wiki/Wii_Play:_Motion" title="Wii Play: Motion">Wii Play: Motion</a></i> will be bundled with the red Wii Remote Plus,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wii_Play:_Motion_announced_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wii_Play:_Motion_announced-22">[22]</a></sup> while the Black Wii Remote Plus is also included with other versions of the game.</p>
<p>At E3 2011, it was revealed that a gold Wii Remote Plus stylized with a <a href="/wiki/Triforce" title="Triforce">Triforce</a> logo will be released alongside <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_Skyward_Sword" title="The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword">The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> It was available as part of a bundle with <i>Skyward Sword</i> for a limited time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Third-party_alternatives">Third-party alternatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wii_MotionPlus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Third-party alternatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nyko" title="Nyko">Nyko</a> revealed the Nyko Wand+ at <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Electronics_Show" title="Consumer Electronics Show">CES</a> 2009, a Wii Remote alternative with the MotionPlus functionality built into the device, saving players from having to change plugins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Europe, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Snakebyte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Snakebyte (page does not exist)">Snakebyte</a> launched its Premium Remote XL +, with built-in Wii MotionPlus,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and its Minimote, a smaller version of the Premium Remote, for children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Memorex" title="Memorex">Memorex</a> launched a series of controllers in 2011 under the "Game Controller Plus" moniker. Available in black, pink and blue, the Game Controller Plus line has Wii MotionPlus functionality built in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wii_MotionPlus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Developers at <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Arts" title="Electronic Arts">Electronic Arts</a> have noted that the fidelity of the device can be too sensitive<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and at times inaccurate<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, although technology reviewers have likened the improved accuracy to the leap from the fidelity of VHS to that of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Game reviewers at Official Nintendo Magazine have called the device "100% accurate" and stated that movements are "captured extremely accurately".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In North America, approximately 374,000 Wii MotionPlus units were sold in June 2009 (including nearly 205,000 units sold as part of a bundle with <i>Tiger Woods PGA Tour 10</i>), according to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPD_Group" title="NPD Group">NPD Group</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Japan, the Wii MotionPlus sold approximately 650,000 units in its opening week (including units sold as part of a bundle with <i>Wii Sports Resort</i>), with about 296,000 sold as standalone units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
