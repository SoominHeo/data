<p><b>Wink</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ウィンク</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was a popular <a href="/wiki/J-pop" title="J-pop">Japanese pop</a> female duo in the late 1980s and early-to-mid-1990s composed of <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sachiko_Suzuki" title="Sachiko Suzuki">Sachiko Suzuki</a></b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鈴木早智子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Suzuki Sachiko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, b. February 22, 1969)</span> and <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dko_Aida" title="Shōko Aida">Shōko Aida</a></b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">相田翔子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aida Shōko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, b. February 23, 1970)</span>. They released their first single on April 27, 1988, and their final release was on March 31, 1996. Many of their singles topped the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> charts in Japan, including their biggest hit, "<a href="/wiki/Samishii_Nettaigyo" title="Samishii Nettaigyo">Samishii Nettaigyo</a>", which has since been covered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/W_(Double_You)" title="W (Double You)">W (Double You)</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Wink">History of Wink</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wink_(Japanese_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of Wink">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1987, Suzuki and Aida both entered a beauty contest given by the magazine "Up to boy". Suzuki won the grand prize, while Aida was one of the runners-up. As a result, Wink was formed the next year, and in April they debuted with the single "<a href="/wiki/Sugar_Baby_Love" title="Sugar Baby Love">Sugar Baby Love</a>", a cover of an English song by <a href="/wiki/The_Rubettes" title="The Rubettes">The Rubettes</a>. Indeed, many Wink songs were covers of Western songs, but with different lyrics in Japanese.</p>
<p>"Sugar Baby Love" and their next single, "Amaryllis", did decently, but it wasn't until the release of their third single "Ai ga Tomaranai (Turn It Into Love)" (a cover of <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turn_It_Into_Love" title="Turn It Into Love">Turn It Into Love</a>") the next year that they became popular. It quickly became No. 1 on the Oricon charts, along with many of the singles that followed it.</p>
<p>Wink had a different style and image than many other J-pop <a href="/wiki/Japanese_idol" title="Japanese idol">idols</a> of the era who focused on a 'cute' style with sugar coated lyrics. Wink had emotionless facial expressions while they sang, and almost never smiled. They also dressed in extravagant <a href="/wiki/Lolita_fashion" title="Lolita fashion">lolita fashions</a>, and almost looked doll-like. This uniqueness helped make them popular.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_break-up_of_Wink">The break-up of Wink</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wink_(Japanese_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The break-up of Wink">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By the early 1990s, however, Wink's sales were declining. Their singles rarely hit the No. 1 spot after early 1990, although they sold respectably until 1994. In the 1990s, many people were looking for fresher-sounding music, and <a href="/wiki/Eurodance" title="Eurodance">Eurodance</a> music became popular thanks to <a href="/wiki/Tetsuya_Komuro" title="Tetsuya Komuro">Tetsuya Komuro</a> and the many artists he produced, many of them under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avex_trax" title="Avex trax">avex trax</a> label. In 1995, Wink tried to generate more sales by putting out a Euro-style single, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jive_Into_The_Night_~Yaban_na_Yoru_ni~&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jive Into The Night ~Yaban na Yoru ni~ (page does not exist)">Jive Into The Night ~Yaban na Yoru ni~</a>", but it was a <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/flop" title="wikt:flop">flop</a>, only reaching No. 92 on the Oricon charts. Their next single, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Angel_Love_Story_~Aki-iro_no_Tenshi~&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Angel Love Story ~Aki-iro no Tenshi~ (page does not exist)">Angel Love Story ~Aki-iro no Tenshi~</a>", did somewhat better, but sales were still poor. Faced with this, Wink officially disbanded in 1996.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_careers">Solo careers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wink_(Japanese_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo careers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Wink, Suzuki and Aida became <a href="/wiki/Tarento" title="Tarento">TV personalities</a>, although not often appearing together. They also continued recording music as solo singers; they released their first solo albums in 1992, and after the break-up, they released their first solo singles. Their solo music is quite different from the music they did as Wink; for example, Aida's music features a more <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> style.</p>
<p>In 2004, Aida released a DVD and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photobook" title="Photobook">photobook</a> called <i>The Aegean Sea</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エーゲ海</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Eegekai</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> with then-<a href="/wiki/Morning_Musume" title="Morning Musume">Morning Musume</a> member <a href="/wiki/Kaori_Iida" title="Kaori Iida">Kaori Iida</a>.</p>
<p>Aida tied the knot in 2008 and bore a daughter in 2012.</p>
