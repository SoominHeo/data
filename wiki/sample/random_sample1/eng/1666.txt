<p>The <b>XASM-3</b> is an supersonic <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship missile</a> being developed by <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Heavy_Industries" title="Mitsubishi Heavy Industries">Mitsubishi Heavy Industries</a> to replace the <a href="/wiki/Type_80_Air-to-Ship_Missile" title="Type 80 Air-to-Ship Missile">ASM-1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Type_93_Air-to-Ship_Missile" title="Type 93 Air-to-Ship Missile">ASM-2</a> missiles. The major launch platform is the <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_F-2" title="Mitsubishi F-2">Mitsubishi F-2</a>. Planned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Initial_Operational_Capability" title="Initial Operational Capability">Initial Operational Capability</a> is 2016. The missile will be used by the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Air_Self-Defense_Force" title="Japan Air Self-Defense Force">Japan Air Self-Defense Force</a>.</p>
<p>In November 2015, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Ministry_of_Defense" title="Japanese Ministry of Defense">Japanese Ministry of Defense</a> announced it would conduct a live-fire experiment of the XASM-3 in 2016, targeting the decommissioned ship <a href="/wiki/JDS_Shirane" title="JDS Shirane">JDS Shirane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-navyreco16nov15_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-navyreco16nov15-1">[1]</a></sup> A ship based version is possible with a booster stage.</p>
