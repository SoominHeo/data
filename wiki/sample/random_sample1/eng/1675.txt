<p><i><b>X</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_combat_simulator" title="Space combat simulator">space trading and combat simulator</a> series created by <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> developer <a href="/wiki/Egosoft" title="Egosoft">Egosoft</a>. The series is set in the X-Universe where several races populate a number of worlds connected by jumpgates. The games feature free roaming gameplay with trading, combat, empire building, and missions; leading to the series' phrase: <i>"Trade, Fight, Build, Think"</i>. The series, which was launched in 1999 on the <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> platform, consists of four base games: <i><a href="/wiki/X:_Beyond_the_Frontier" title="X: Beyond the Frontier">X: Beyond the Frontier</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/X2:_The_Threat" title="X2: The Threat">X2: The Threat</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/X3:_Reunion" title="X3: Reunion">X3: Reunion</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/X_Rebirth" title="X Rebirth">X Rebirth</a></i> with a new rendering engine and plot.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Story">Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=X_(video_game_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fiction behind the <i>X</i> series is provided in the games themselves and in four novels by series fictional writer Helge Kautz: <i>Farnham's Legend</i>, <i>Nopileos</i>, <i>Yoshiko</i> and <i>Hüter der Tore (The Keeper of the Gates)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>Farnham's Legend</i> is available in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>.</p>
<p>Over the course of the 21st century, mankind experimented with <a href="/wiki/Wormhole" title="Wormhole">wormhole</a> technology and successfully built <a href="/wiki/Jumpgate" title="Jumpgate">jumpgates</a> in space between <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a> paving the way for the colonization of the solar system and interstellar transport. A jumpgate was shuttled to <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Centauri" title="Alpha Centauri">Alpha Centauri</a> in an attempt to claim a foothold in interstellar space. Even before the gate left the solar system scientists discovered that many objects classified as black holes showed the characteristics of artificial wormholes. There were alien jumpgates which the human constructed gates could connect to. The <a href="/wiki/Earthling" title="Earthling">Terrans</a> had discovered the X Universe, a seemingly uninhabited collection of star systems connected by a vast network of large, bi-directional jump gates.</p>
<p>Puzzled by the lack of intelligent life within this network but driven by their curiosity and the urge to colonise the new worlds the Terrans ventured into the X Universe intent on expanding their territory. Terraforming machines were created and dispatched to <a href="/wiki/Terraforming" title="Terraforming">terraform</a> planets into habitable worlds. The Terraformers were <a href="/wiki/Self-replicating_spacecraft" title="Self-replicating spacecraft">self-replicating spacecraft</a>, governed by a simple <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>. During a software update several flaws were introduced into the Terraformer fleet and distributed between them causing a radical change in behaviour. The Terraformers replicated and attempted to re-terraform colonised planets, causing the planets to lose their ability to support human life. In an attempt to halt the Terraformers the Terrans became embroiled in a war with their creations. No colony was able to defend against the attacks and mankind was pushed back to the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Sol System</a> where the Terraformers launched an attempt to destroy all life forms and terraform all of the inner planets.</p>
<p>In a daring attempt to save the solar system, a group of ships, led by Nathan R. Gunne, lured the Terraformers through the gate into the X Universe. The Earth gate was then destroyed, trapping the Terraformers in the X-Universe and cutting Earth off from the rest of the network.</p>
<p>The X-series is set more than 750 years after these events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">[2]</a></sup> A single Terraformer craft appears in the solar system using an apparently experimental gateless jumpdrive. The Terrans reverse engineer the device. In its maiden flight, the X-Shuttle, piloted by test pilot Kyle Brennan, is accidentally transported into the X Universe. Unlike before, this part of the network is inhabited by several space faring alien races and the descendants of Nathan R. Gunne's fleet. The Terraformers are attempting to get to Earth and Kyle must stop them.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_X-Universe">The X-Universe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=X_(video_game_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The X-Universe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fictional X-Universe is a collection of sectors connected by two-way jumpgates. The total number of sectors is unknown, but the number of "discovered" sectors has increased in each game; from 54, in <i>X: Beyond the Frontier</i>, to over 200 in <i>X³: Terran Conflict</i>, including the re-discovered <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Sol</a> system.</p>
<p>Sectors typically orbit <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">planets</a>, within a <a href="/wiki/Star_system" title="Star system">star system</a>. Most contain game resources such as <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">asteroids</a> of <a href="/wiki/Silicon" title="Silicon">silicon</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ore" title="Ore">ore</a>, and many contain inhabitable planets. Each sector contains up to four jumpgates, in a North, East, South, West pattern. Each sector is named by the organisation that controls it.</p>
<p>Notable sectors include: Argon Federation home-sector, "Argon Prime"; Boron Kingdom home-sector "Kingdom End", Teladi Company headquarters "Seizewell"; Split Dynasty home-sector "Family Pride"; and Paranid Empire home world "Paranid Prime". The Xenon home-sector has never been discovered.</p>
<p>Most sectors are controlled by one X-Universe race, and will contain only stations economically <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_(ecology)" title="Indigenous (ecology)">indigenous</a> to that race. For example, <i>Atreus<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span> Clouds</i> is a Boron sector, containing only Boron stations. Here the player can buy special Boron weaponry and equipment that is unavailable from other races. Boron stations sell Boron trade products, and they often want to buy the trade products of other races. Such products can be <a href="/wiki/Import" title="Import">imported</a>, <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exported</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">traded</a> by the player for profit. This compels the player to explore the sectors of the different races to discover the most useful upgrades, and most profitable trade routes.</p>
<p>For convenience, most stations and facilities are located in the space between the jumpgates, known as the "<a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">ecliptic plane</a>". However, each sector is unbounded, and the player may travel in any direction almost indefinitely. Beyond the main area of a sector is mostly empty void, though official X material, as well as in-game messages and missions hint at lost ships, secret stations, and other treasures and dangers lurking in remote areas.</p>
<p>In games to <i>X²: The Threat</i>, X-Universe sectors are of uniform size, and gates are usually centre-North, South, East and West of the ecliptic plane. In <i>X³: Reunion</i> sectors became much more varied. The distances between gates varies between 50 km in some sectors to over 200 km in others. The exact position of the gates is unpredictable and gates are generally harder to find.</p>
<p>It is not possible to fly to planets in game. Planet based activity is only possible in plot-related events of <i>X²: The Threat</i> and <i>X³: Reunion</i>. It is only possible to fly into planet atmospheres, though this will result in the ship's destruction.</p>
<p>It is possible that all sectors in the X-Universe are in the <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">Milky Way</a>. With gate technology, some sectors which are distant in the gate system could be cosmically close together. Conversely, sectors near to each other in the jumpgate grid could be physically far apart. Due to the ease of use of the jumpgate system, most races have not progressed far enough in more conventional interstellar science to be sure.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_races">Featured races</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=X_(video_game_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Featured races">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The X-Universe features several sentient species, some of which are yet to be seen in the games themselves.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Boron</b>: A squidlike alien race that reveres their queen and love peace.</li>
<li><b>Human</b>: Divided into three factions:
<dl>
<dd><i>Argon</i>: Humans who are descended from the remains of the fleet that lured the Terraformers through the gateway that saved Earth in the terraformer wars. The Argon government decided centuries ago that the pain of separation with Earth was best dealt with by changing the official version of history so that humans in the X-Universe believed they evolved in their current location.</dd>
<dd><i>Goner</i>: Argons who believe the legend of Earth and Nathan R. Gunne (who led the fleet that saved Earth), despite both being officially just a myth.</dd>
<dd><i>Terran</i>: Humans who were born in the Sol system.</dd>
</dl>
</li>
<li><b>Khaak</b>: A buglike alien race that started war with the Argon in <i>X²: The Threat</i>. They decimated the sector now known as "President's End"</li>
<li><b>Paranid</b>: A three-eyed alien race that hail to their Xaar emperor and have a strict religion.</li>
<li><b>Split</b>: A tribal alien race that fight within themselves as much as they fight their enemies.</li>
<li><b>Teladi</b>: A reptilian alien race that worship trade and profits.</li>
<li><b>Xenon</b>: A self-replicating artificial intelligence evolved from the terraformers created by the Terrans in an effort to colonise worlds. Now hostile to all life, they destroy anything organic.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_X_Series">The <i>X</i> Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=X_(video_game_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The X Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Individual_games">Individual games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=X_(video_game_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Individual games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series, which was launched in 1999 on the <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> platform, consists of four base games: <i><a href="/wiki/X:_Beyond_the_Frontier" title="X: Beyond the Frontier">X: Beyond the Frontier</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/X2:_The_Threat" title="X2: The Threat">X2: The Threat</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/X3:_Reunion" title="X3: Reunion">X3: Reunion</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/X_Rebirth" title="X Rebirth">X Rebirth</a></i> with a new rendering engine and plot.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The series games are expanded by sequels that both add features and extend the plot. A plot can be undertaken in all games except <i>X-Tension</i> where the player takes part in missions to unfold events.</p>
<p>A free downloadable demo version of <i>X³: Reunion</i> and <i>X³: Terran Conflict</i> can be obtained from the Egosoft website.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_editions">Special editions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=X_(video_game_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Special editions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>X-Gold</b> is a double pack of <i>X: Beyond the Frontier</i> and <i>X-Tension</i>. It was released in September 2000 in English, followed by the German version in 2001. The pack included the two games on a single CD, along with X-Tensions manual. It has since been re-released as a budget game in 2004.</li>
<li><b>X³-Gold</b> is a double pack of <i>X³: Reunion</i>, <i>X³: Terran Conflict</i> and an <i>X³: Terran Conflict</i> soundtrack CD. It was released on April 9, 2009 in Germany and several other EU states, with <i>X³: Reunion 2.5</i> and <i>X³: Terran Conflict 2.0</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>X³-Gold</i> was released on November 12, 2009 for UK and American markets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>X³-GoldBox</b> (available on Steam) combines <i>X³: Reunion</i>, <i>X³: Terran Conflict</i> and <i>X³: Albion Prelude</i>.</li>
<li><b>X-Superbox</b> is a collectors edition of the X-Series, containing the first 5 games, bonus content and an encyclopedia of the X-Universe by author Helge Kautz. Egosoft, using their forum community, gathered a range of fan-made content for inclusion in the X-Superbox.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Updated versions of <i>X: Beyond the Frontier</i> and <i>X-Tension</i> were made especially for the X-Superbox and were made available for testing on the Egosoft forums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>X³: Terran Conflict</i> also receives an update with new content in the X-Superbox. The X-Superbox was officially announced on August 16, 2010 and was released on October 8, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>X³-Terran War Pack</b> combines the latest version of <i>X³: Terran Conflict</i> and the new release of <i>X³: Albion Prelude</i> into a single package. This edition is available on Steam while the retail edition is anticipated to also include the X³:AP sound track CD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Novel">Novel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=X_(video_game_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Novel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Farnham's Legend</i> is a science fiction novel set in the imaginary universe created for the <i>X</i> series. It was written in German by Helge T. Kautz, and has been translated into English by Steve Miller and Andreas Fuchs. Published by Egosoft in 2005, it contains 349 pages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Each chapter tells the story from one character's viewpoint. The main characters in this book are:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Capt. Kyle William Brennan</b> - the human test pilot of the X-perimental space shuttle and the playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/X:_Beyond_the_Frontier" title="X: Beyond the Frontier">X: Beyond the Frontier</a></i>.</li>
<li><b>Major Elena Kho</b> - another human, longtime friend of Brennan from Earth, sent to rescue Brennan by the USC.</li>
<li><b>Cho</b> - a humanoid Split, he is the special envoy of the patriarch of Chin.</li>
<li><b>Nopileos</b> - a young reptilian Teladi, she encounters Elena on her mission to find Kyle.</li>
</ul>
