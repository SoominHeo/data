<p><b>YMCK</b> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Chiptune" title="Chiptune">chiptune</a> band, composed of Midori Kurihara (vocals), Takeshi Yokemura (music, lyrics, arrangement), and Tomoyuki Nakamura (composition, music video).</p>
<p>Their work has not been confined to Japan, having released albums in Korea, Thailand and the United States as well as appearing at various events around East Asia and Europe.</p>
<p>YMCK's name is derived from the <a href="/wiki/CMYK_color_model" title="CMYK color model">CMYK</a> <a href="/wiki/Subtractive_color" title="Subtractive color">subtractive color</a> model.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YMCK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>YMCK was formed in May 2003 when Kurihara joined Yokemura and Nakamura. They soon released their first work; a CD-R containing 6 tracks.</p>
<p>In 2004, YMCK appeared at <a class="external text" href="http://microdisko.gg8.se/disko.php?disko=2004-08-07" rel="nofollow">Microdisko</a> in Stockholm, Sweden. In the same year they released their first full album, <i>Family Music</i>.</p>
<p>In 2005, YMCK again appeared outside Japan, this time at the <a class="external text" href="http://www.formoz.com/" rel="nofollow">Formoz Festival</a> in Taipei, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and at the Fat Festival in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, Thailand. This year also marks the first release of the <i>Magical 8bit Plug</i>, a synthesizer developed by Yokemura himself, motivated by frustration with the quality of game sounds available in the synthesizers used for Family Music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Now making use of the Magical 8bit Plug, they released their second album: <i>Family Racing</i>.</p>
<p>In 2006, they again appeared at the Formoz Festival in Taiwan.</p>
<p>In 2008, YMCK released their third album <i>Family Genesis</i>, now under pop label <a href="/wiki/Avex_Trax" title="Avex Trax">Avex Trax</a>, made available for the first time on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> in the United States. Later that year and under the same label, they would release their first cover album, the <i>YMCK Songbook</i>, a collection of covers of japanese music from the era before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NES" title="NES">NES</a>.</p>
<p>In January 2009, they appeared in Sweden once again, this time at <a class="external text" href="http://09.uppcon.se" rel="nofollow">UppCon</a> in Uppsala, and also played in the opening ceremonies for Katsucon 15 in Arlington, Virginia (DC). In that same month they also released <i>Family Cooking</i>. In 2009, YMCK also remixed the No. 1 hit song <a href="/wiki/Days/Green" title="Days/Green">Days</a> by Japanese mega-star <a href="/wiki/Ayumi_Hamasaki" title="Ayumi Hamasaki">Ayumi Hamasaki</a>.</p>
<p>During 2013, YMCK released <i>Family Days</i>, this time through <a class="external text" href="http://not-records.com/" rel="nofollow">not records</a>. The theme of this album was said to be YMCK describing their own ordinary lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, they released <i>Family Dancing</i>.</p>
<p>In 2017, <i>Family Swing</i> was released, and for the first time they also released through <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a>, under U.K. chiptune label Hyperwave.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PiCTOBiTS">PiCTOBiTS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YMCK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: PiCTOBiTS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>YMCK composed the music for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DSiWare" title="DSiWare">DSiWare</a> downloadable puzzle game <i><a href="/wiki/Picopict" title="Picopict">PiCTOBiTS</a></i>, which features 8-bit music from <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> games.</p>
