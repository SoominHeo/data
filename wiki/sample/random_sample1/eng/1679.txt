<p><b>Yui</b> (born March 26, 1987), stylized as <b>YUI</b>, is a Japanese singer-songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, and actress. Born and raised in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fukuoka_prefecture" title="Fukuoka prefecture">Fukuoka prefecture</a>, she played live at various locations in her hometown before being noticed by <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment_Japan" title="Sony Music Entertainment Japan">Sony Music Japan</a> when she was 17 years old, and released her debut single months later. However her singles were only met with moderate success until her breakout "<a href="/wiki/Good-bye_Days" title="Good-bye Days">Good-bye Days</a>", which charted for 44 weeks on <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> and marked her as one of the Japanese music industry's rising stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since her debut album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/From_Me_to_You_(Yui_album)" title="From Me to You (Yui album)">From Me to You</a></i>, each of her albums has topped the charts, and has had at least one single reach number one yearly since 2007, including five straight from mid-2008 to late 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon_bio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon_bio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is popular in Japan and in surrounding countries, ranking number one in 2011 <a href="/wiki/Count_Down_TV" title="Count Down TV">Count Down TV</a> "Dearest Female Artist"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Music_Station" title="Music Station">Music Station</a> "Artist You Most Want to Marry"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> polls, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_Television_Hong_Kong" title="Radio Television Hong Kong">Radio Television Hong Kong</a>'s "Most Popular Japanese Artist".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After retiring from music as a soloist in 2012, she formed the band <a href="/wiki/Flower_Flower" title="Flower Flower">Flower Flower</a> in 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yui was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fukuoka_prefecture" title="Fukuoka prefecture">Fukuoka, Japan</a>. The <i><a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a></i> has reported that her full name is Yui Yoshioka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>She grew up in a single-parent family as her father left her mother when she was three. She stated that she had always been close to music, as she would remember the beats from music she heard on the radio, and be able to sing it. By the time she was in elementary school she thought she would like to become a singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ld5_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ld5-8">[8]</a></sup> Self-described as shy and horrible at talking to others as a child, she would play outside by herself in mountains, rivers, by the sea and in rice paddies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_Oricon_interview_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-first_Oricon_interview-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>During her third year in primary school, she was influenced by her mother to begin writing a journal of her feelings and tried to compose them into poems. By the time Yui attended high school, she began to write songs, thinking of that as being able to express herself little by little.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_Oricon_interview_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-first_Oricon_interview-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ld3_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ld3-10">[10]</a></sup> While in high school, she worked part-time at a Chinese restaurant to help pay for tuition. Yet, between music, school and work, she only had one or two hours of sleep, and believed she no longer had the time to realize her dreams of a music career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ld1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ld1-11">[11]</a></sup> She subsequently became ill; however, it was at the hospital that she was overwhelmed with the desire to make music and finally decided that school and music could not coexist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ld1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ld1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Soon after leaving the hospital, she got a chance to see her first live <a href="/wiki/Street_performance" title="Street performance">street performance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ld1_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-ld1-11">[11]</a></sup> She expressed a desire to pursue a musical career to the band, Bianco Nero, at the end of the concert. The band advised Yui to join a private music school, <i><a href="/wiki/Juku" title="Juku">juku</a></i>. Despite the social norm of finishing school in Japan and discouragement from her teachers, Yui did not hesitate to drop out of high school and began to study guitar and songwriting at the music cram school "Voice" in her hometown of Fukuoka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ld1_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-ld1-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News_Maker_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-News_Maker-12">[12]</a></sup> Aspiring to become a professional, she took to street performing at Fukuoka's <a href="/wiki/Tenjin_Station" title="Tenjin Station">Tenjin Station</a>. These street performances helped Yui to overcome her shyness.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Initial_career">Initial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Initial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her professional career began in March 2004 when, at the recommendation of her cram school instructors, she applied for an audition hosted by <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment_Japan" title="Sony Music Entertainment Japan">Sony Music Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Although instructed that a participant could only perform two songs, she first sang "Why Me" (a song later included in her debut Sony Records <a href="/wiki/Feel_My_Soul_(song)" title="Feel My Soul (song)">single</a>), followed by "It's Happy Line," and then half of "I Know", performing a third song because she wanted the judges to hear a song full of hope.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ld1_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-ld1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The judges gave her the maximum score. Because "I Know" was incomplete at the time, the judges were able to get a glimpse of what would later be dubbed "Yui-go" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">YUI<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%AA%9E" title="wikt:語">語</a></span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>", or Yui-speak, nonsensical English hummed to a tune during her songwriting process – an example of Yui-go can be found in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_Sun_(2006_film)" title="Midnight Sun (2006 film)">Song of the Sun</a></i> when Yui is working on the song "Goodbye Days".</p>
<p>On December 24, 2004, she released her debut single, "It's Happy Line", under the indie label Leaflet Records, coupled with the track "I Know," although the pressing was limited to only 2,000 copies in her home area.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_Me_to_You_.282005.E2.80.932006.29"><i>From Me to You</i> (2005–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: From Me to You (2005–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon leaving her hometown in Fukuoka for Tokyo, Yui wrote the song "<a href="/wiki/Feel_My_Soul_(song)" title="Feel My Soul (song)">Feel My Soul</a>" as a tribute to her hometown. Although she initially had planned its release on an indie label, Fuji Television producer Yamaguchi was so inspired by Yui's voice that he insisted on having her work on the music for his primetime drama <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fukigen_na_Gene&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fukigen na Gene (page does not exist)">Fukigen na Gene</a></i> even before she released her first major single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The music in <i>Fukigen na Gene</i> was eventually based on her songs "Feel My Soul" and "It's Happy Line".</p>
<p>On February 23, Yui released her first major debut single "Feel My Soul". With the publicity the drama tie-in drew in, "Feel My Soul" managed to sell over 100,000 copies and managed to chart at number 8 on <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> Weekly Charts in its first week. Her next three singles, "<a href="/wiki/Tomorrow%27s_Way" title="Tomorrow's Way">Tomorrow's Way</a>" (theme song for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Hinokio" title="Hinokio">Hinokio</a></i>), "<a href="/wiki/Life_(Yui_song)" title="Life (Yui song)">Life</a>" (5th ending theme for the <i><a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)" title="Bleach (manga)">Bleach</a></i> anime), and "<a href="/wiki/Tokyo_(Yui_song)" title="Tokyo (Yui song)">Tokyo</a>" did not chart as high as "Feel My Soul", and were only moderately successful in comparison. After the release of four singles, Yui released her debut album, titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/From_Me_to_You_(Yui_album)" title="From Me to You (Yui album)">From Me to You</a></i>, a moderate success with sales of more than 200,000 copies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Can.27t_Buy_My_Love_.282006.E2.80.932007.29"><i>Can't Buy My Love</i> (2006–2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Can't Buy My Love (2006–2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yui made her acting debut in the full-length feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_Sun_(2006_film)" title="Midnight Sun (2006 film)">Song of the Sun</a></i> (Taiyou no uta, タイヨウのうた),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> which opened on June 17, 2006. The film was screened at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2006 Cannes Film Festival">2006 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> She had a unique way of approaching her role, such as imagining unscripted conversations between other characters and hers as well as staying in her character's room and fitting the aberrant sleeping patterns of her character to give herself a sense of her role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> She would eventually attend the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Academy_Prize_(film)" title="Japan Academy Prize (film)">Japan Academy Prize</a> and win the Best Newcomer Award for <i>Midnight Sun</i>. Prior to the release of the movie, Yui released her fifth single "<a href="/wiki/Good-bye_Days" title="Good-bye Days">Good-bye Days</a>" written specifically for the film. The single is thus so far her highest selling at more than 200,000 copies sold. All three tracks on the single are featured in <i>Midnight Sun</i>. The single also re-released the track "It's Happy Line", originally released on her debut single.</p>
<p>Her next single, "<a href="/wiki/I_Remember_You_(Yui_song)" title="I Remember You (Yui song)">I Remember You</a>", did well in terms of sales, riding on the wave of the popularity of "Good-bye Days". Yui's seventh single "<a href="/wiki/Rolling_Star" title="Rolling Star">Rolling Star</a>" was chosen as the 5th opening theme for the <i><a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)" title="Bleach (manga)">Bleach</a></i> anime. Her eighth single, "<a href="/wiki/Cherry_(Yui_song)" title="Cherry (Yui song)">Cherry</a>" was featured in commercial promoting <a href="/wiki/KDDI" title="KDDI">KDDI</a>, a Listen Mobile Service.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>She released her second album <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Buy_My_Love" title="Can't Buy My Love">Can't Buy My Love</a></i> on April 4, 2007. The album spent two weeks at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> charts, shattering her entire previous album's record sales in one week. <i>Can't Buy My Love</i> managed to sell over 680,000 copies. Due to the success of <i>Can't Buy My Love</i>, Yui's previous album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/From_Me_to_You_(Yui_album)" title="From Me to You (Yui album)">From Me to You</a></i> charted once again, adding another 9,000 copies to the Oricon counting.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="I_Loved_Yesterday_.282007.E2.80.932008.29"><i>I Loved Yesterday</i> (2007–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: I Loved Yesterday (2007–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yui released her 9th single "<a href="/wiki/My_Generation/Understand" title="My Generation/Understand">My Generation/Understand</a>" on June 13, 2007. It was her first double A-side single. "My Generation" was selected as the ending theme for the TV drama <i>Seito Shokun!</i>, and "Understand" was chosen as the theme song for the movie <i>Dog in a Sidecar</i> (サイドカーに犬),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> (starring her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senpai" title="Senpai">senpai</a> at Stardust Promotion, <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABko_Takeuchi" title="Yūko Takeuchi">Yūko Takeuchi</a>). The single charted at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> Weekly Charts the first week of its release.</p>
<p>Her tenth single, "<a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Truth" title="Love &amp; Truth">Love &amp; Truth</a>" was released on September 26, 2007. The title track is the theme song to the film <i>Closed Note</i> (クローズド・ノート) (starring <a href="/wiki/Erika_Sawajiri" title="Erika Sawajiri">Erika Sawajiri</a>, also of Stardust). During this time, her previous two albums were re-released as Winter Sleeve Editions in alternative covers taken from her "Love &amp; Truth" photoshoot. Her first live concert DVD <i><a href="/wiki/Thank_You_My_Teens" title="Thank You My Teens">Thank You My Teens</a></i> was released on November 14, 2007, which contained footage of her second live concert tour.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On November 19, 2007, Yui opened her first live show at <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Budokan" title="Nippon Budokan">Nippon Budokan</a>, which promptly sold out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b-passbukodan_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-b-passbukodan-20">[20]</a></sup> The Budokan concert was structured similar to a live performance, which a writer from B-Pass called it fitting considering her beginnings as a street performer on the streets of Fukuoka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b-passbukodan_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-b-passbukodan-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yui started 2008 with her eleventh single, "<a href="/wiki/Namidairo" title="Namidairo">Namidairo</a>", released on February 27, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Yui composed the song as a "mysterious and sad sounding" insert for the television drama <i>4 Shimai Tantei Dan</i>. While writing the song, she attempted to convey a feeling of saying "I'm fine" in order to not worry one's lover, and how during such occasions, we realize little things we don't normally notice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lt_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-lt-22">[22]</a></sup> The chorus of the song was incidentally written by Yui long ago, during her singing debut.</p>
<p>A week following the release of her 11th single, the promotional video of a new song "Laugh Away", was released. The song was used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glico" title="Glico">Glico</a>'s "Watering KissMint" commercial. "Laugh Away" was released as a digital single on March 10, 2008.</p>
<p>Her third studio album was released on April 9, 2008 titled <i><a href="/wiki/I_Loved_Yesterday" title="I Loved Yesterday">I Loved Yesterday</a></i>. It quickly charted at number 1 on the Oricon Weekly Charts and sold more than 400,000 copies, only a little behind her second album. The 10th track on the album, titled "Oh Yeah", was used as the opening theme to <i><a href="/wiki/Mezamashi_TV" title="Mezamashi TV">Mezamashi TV</a></i>, a morning television show. The limited edition version of the album included a DVD which contained music videos of her previous singles and live footage of her <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Budokan" title="Nippon Budokan">Nippon Budokan</a> show. The album was composed mainly of semi-ballads with much of the same themes as her previous releases such as youth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yui's third tour, named "Oui: I Loved Yesterday'" started in May 2008 and ran until July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="My_Short_Stories.2FHolidays_in_the_Sun_.282008.E2.80.932010.29"><i>My Short Stories/Holidays in the Sun</i> (2008–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: My Short Stories/Holidays in the Sun (2008–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 2, 2008, Yui released "<a href="/wiki/Summer_Song_(Yui_song)" title="Summer Song (Yui song)">Summer Song</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> again charting at number 1 on the Oricon Weekly Charts. "Summer Song" sold 83,440 copies in a week, making "Summer Song" second highest in first week sales after "Love &amp; Truth."</p>
<p>Yui released her B-side compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Short_Stories" title="My Short Stories">My Short Stories</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> on November 12, 2008, which charted at number 1 on <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>. The album included all the B-side tracks of all her singles to date, along with a new song "I'll Be".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Yui is the second female artist whose B-side compilation album topped the charts, after <a href="/wiki/Seiko_Matsuda" title="Seiko Matsuda">Seiko Matsuda</a>'s <i>Touch Me</i> in 1984. The last compilation album that reached No. 1 was <a href="/wiki/Mr._Children" title="Mr. Children">Mr. Children</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/B-Side_(album)" title="B-Side (album)">B-Side</a></i> in May 2007. On the success of "<a href="/wiki/My_Short_Stories" title="My Short Stories">My Short Stories</a>", sales of her first album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/From_Me_to_You_(Yui_album)" title="From Me to You (Yui album)">From Me to You</a></i> rose again.</p>
<p>In her "Yui Diary" blog entry on August 29, 2008, Yui mentioned that she would put her career on hold, not making any public appearances after the release of the album. With this break, she would be able to fully concentrate on her plans for the next year. During this break, she co-wrote the song "I Do It" with <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Prefecture" title="Okinawa Prefecture">Okinawa</a> girl band <a href="/wiki/Stereopony" title="Stereopony">Stereopony</a>. It was released as Stereopony's third single on April 22, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yui announced her return from her five-month hiatus on March 25, 2009 on her official website. The single celebrating her return is an up-tempo tune titled "<a href="/wiki/Again_(Yui_song)" title="Again (Yui song)">Again</a>." The track was chosen as the first opening theme of the new season of the <i><a href="/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood" title="Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood">Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood</a></i> anime, premiered on April 5, 2009. The single was released on June 3, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> "Again" debuted atop the Oricon charts, selling over 110,000 copies in its first week. It had the highest opening week sales for a female act in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her website Yui-net.com announced the release of her second double-A side single named "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_All_Too_Much/Never_Say_Die" title="It's All Too Much/Never Say Die">It's All Too Much/Never Say Die</a>" on July 27, 2009. The two songs were featured in the movie adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Kaiji_(manga)" title="Kaiji (manga)">Kaiji</a></i> as both a theme and an insert song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The single was released on October 7, 2009 and <i>Kaiji</i> released on October 10, 2009. The single debuted at number 1, selling 75,000 copies in its first week of sales. This is her fifth overall number 1 single. Yui released "<a href="/wiki/Gloria_(Yui_song)" title="Gloria (Yui song)">Gloria</a>" on January 20, 2010. It was another chart-topper, selling 80,000 copies in the first week. Her next single, "<a href="/wiki/To_Mother_(song)" title="To Mother (song)">To Mother</a>," was released on June 2, 2010.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On July 14, 2010, Yui released her fourth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Holidays_in_the_Sun_(album)" title="Holidays in the Sun (album)">Holidays in the Sun</a></i>, which included her five singles from "Summer Song" to "To Mother". It became her fourth straight album to top the Oricon charts, eventually selling over 300,000 copies, and attained the certification of Platinum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="How_Crazy_Your_Love_.282010.E2.80.932013.29"><i>How Crazy Your Love</i> (2010–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: How Crazy Your Love (2010–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her single "<a href="/wiki/Rain_(Yui_song)" title="Rain (Yui song)">Rain</a>" was released on the November 24, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Yui's released a double-A side of "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_My_Life/Your_Heaven" title="It's My Life/Your Heaven">It's My Life/Your Heaven</a>," on January 26, 2011. Yui traveled to Sweden for the shooting of the "Your Heaven" music video and a short movie showing Yui's journey to various places and shops in Sweden.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Yui's tour DVD, <i>Holidays in the Sun</i> was released on March 9. Her newest single, "<a href="/wiki/Hello_(Paradise_Kiss)" title="Hello (Paradise Kiss)">Hello (Paradise Kiss)</a>", was released on June 1, 2011, the A-side and B-side being theme song and ending song, respectively, of the live-action movie "<a href="/wiki/Paradise_Kiss_(film)" title="Paradise Kiss (film)">Paradise Kiss</a>". On June 16, 2011, Yui traveled to Hong Kong for her first overseas concert at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AsiaWorld-Arena" title="AsiaWorld-Arena">AsiaWorld-Expo Arena</a>, performing to a capacity audience of 14,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yui visited schools in stricken areas from the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a> on July 7, 2011, and performed a charity concert. Her single "<a href="/wiki/Green_a.Live" title="Green a.Live">Green a.Live</a>" uses the emotions and thoughts she felt from the visit, and was released on October 5, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> It was previewed on the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_FM" title="Tokyo FM">Tokyo FM</a> radio show <i>School of Lock</i> on August 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> "Green a.Live" debuted on top of the charts, and became her first number one single since July 2010.</p>
<p>On November 2, 2011, her album, <i><a href="/wiki/How_Crazy_Your_Love" title="How Crazy Your Love">How Crazy Your Love</a></i>, was released. The album debuted at number one on the daily charts, selling just under 50,000 copies on the first day of release, and would go on to reach number one on the weekly charts – making <i>How Crazy Your Love</i> her fifth straight album to debut at the weekly number one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> She started her fifth major tour on November 11, 2011 to promote her recent album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YUI5tour_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-YUI5tour-40">[40]</a></sup> It has cruising as the major theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yui's upcoming single was announced through her participation in Ncon (Nコン), a national school music competition where schools compete to provide their best rendition of songs composed by select artists for a specific theme for that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Yui was selected to compose for the middle school category. The song was eventually revealed as "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fight_(Yui_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fight (Yui song) (page does not exist)">fight</a>" on <i>R no Housoku</i> (Rの法則), and was released on September 5, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a video uploaded to Yui's official Vevo channel on December 18, 2012, she announced that she would be taking a break from music. She explained that "...Yui is so precious and special for me, so I want to leave it as is for a while." To her fans, she said, "Actually, part of the reason I'm going into hiatus again is for the sake of my fans. The biggest reason is that I don't want them to see me with a broken-off heart, if that happens."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flower_Flower_.282013.E2.80.93present.29">Flower Flower (2013–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Flower Flower (2013–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yui returned from hiatus shortly thereafter with a new band called <a href="/wiki/Flower_Flower" title="Flower Flower">Flower Flower</a>, which performed incognito throughout Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The band has since released four digital and Japan-only singles; "月(Tsuki)", "神様(Kamisama)", "夏/秋(Natsu/Aki)" and "素晴らしい世界(Subarashii Sekai)". On March 27, 2014, Yui revealed in her band blog that she had been diagnosed with a <a href="/wiki/Panic_disorder" title="Panic disorder">panic disorder</a>, which resulted in the cancellation of some presentations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 17, 2015, the singer announced via the official blog of her band Flower Flower that she has married a man outside of the industry and is currently pregnant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In August 2015, it was announced that, in the first week of August, Yui gave birth to healthy twin boys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
