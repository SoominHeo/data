<p><b>"Adventure Time"</b> is an animated short created by <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_Ward" title="Pendleton Ward">Pendleton Ward</a>, as well as the pilot to the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">series of the same name</a>. The short follows the adventures of <a href="/wiki/Finn_the_Human" title="Finn the Human">Pen</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Zack_Shada" title="Zack Shada">Zack Shada</a>), a human boy, and his best friend <a href="/wiki/Jake_the_Dog" title="Jake the Dog">Jake</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/John_DiMaggio" title="John DiMaggio">John DiMaggio</a>), a dog with magical powers to change shape and grow and shrink at will. In this episode, Pen and Jake have to rescue <a href="/wiki/Princess_Bubblegum" title="Princess Bubblegum">Princess Bubblegum</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Paige_Moss" title="Paige Moss">Paige Moss</a>) from the antagonistic <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/John_Kassir" title="John Kassir">John Kassir</a>).</p>
<p>"Adventure Time" first aired as a stand-alone short on <a href="/wiki/Nicktoons_(TV_channel)" title="Nicktoons (TV channel)">Nicktoons Network</a> in January 2007, after which it subsequently went viral on the internet; as of April 2008, the pilot has had over 3,000,000 views. It was then re-aired as part of <a href="/wiki/Nicktoons_(TV_channel)" title="Nicktoons (TV channel)">Nicktoons Network</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Random!_Cartoons" title="Random! Cartoons">Random! Cartoons</a></i> series showcase on December 7, 2008, subsequently leading to the creation of the animated series. It was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Annie_Award" title="Annie Award">Annie Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Annie_Award_for_Best_Animated_Short_Subject" title="Annie Award for Best Animated Short Subject">Best Animated Short Subject</a>. The short and the later-produced television series share elements, but the two differ slightly in setting, conception and continuity, especially in regard to the post-apocalyptic setting, which is only featured in the television series.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adventure_Time_(pilot)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The short focused on a boy named <a href="/wiki/Finn_the_Human" title="Finn the Human">Pen</a> and a dog named <a href="/wiki/Jake_the_Dog" title="Jake the Dog">Jake</a> as they learn from the Rainicorn that the Ice King has kidnapped <a href="/wiki/Princess_Bubblegum" title="Princess Bubblegum">Princess Bubblegum</a>, in the hope of marrying her. Declaring that it's "Adventure Time", Pen and Jake set off to the Ice King's mountain lair. Pen and the Ice King fight while Jake remains outside flirting with Lady Rainicorn, ignoring the battle. Just when Pen seems to be gaining the upper hand, the Ice King uses his "frozen lightning bolts" to freeze Pen in a block of ice. For unexplained reasons, this transports Pen's mind back in time, and to <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a>, where he has a short motivational conversation with <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>. After being told to believe in himself, Pen's mind is returned to the present, where he breaks out of the ice, just in time to see the Ice King fly away with Princess Bubblegum. Chasing after him using Jake's extendable legs, Pen rescues the princess from the Ice King's grasp. Jake pushes the magical crown off the Ice King's head, thereby removing the King's source of power. The Ice King then plummets off screen, yelling a long list of complex threats of things he will do when he returns. The story closes with Princess Bubblegum giving Pen a kiss; he enjoys it, but is also greatly embarrassed by the act. He attempts to leave, but Jake claims that they have nowhere else to go and that there are no adventures that need them. However, some nearby ninjas are stealing an old man's diamonds, and they both run off in pursuit.</p>

<p>"Adventure Time" was created by <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_Ward" title="Pendleton Ward">Pendleton Ward</a>. The short's style was influenced by his time at <a href="/wiki/California_Institute_of_the_Arts" title="California Institute of the Arts">California Institute of the Arts</a> (CalArts).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeforsome_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-timeforsome-7">[7]</a></sup> "Adventure Time" was Ward's first job in animation after he graduated from CalArts. Ward had been contacted by Eric Homan, the vice president of Development at <a href="/wiki/Frederator_Studios" title="Frederator Studios">Frederator Studios</a> after Homan saw one of Wards films at a CalArts animation screening called "The Producer's Show". Homan told Ward that he should consider pitching an idea to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederator" title="Frederator">Frederator</a> <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/incubation" title="wikt:incubation">incubator</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Random!_Cartoons" title="Random! Cartoons">Random! Cartoons</a></i>. Ward spent approximately one to two weeks storyboarding the outline for "Adventure Time". He described the process as "exciting" because he was "jumping into it not knowing whether [he] would sink or swim."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awnrandom_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-awnrandom-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the initial storyboard pitch to Frederator Studios, Ward brought a guitar and played the episode's theme song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awnrandom_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-awnrandom-1">[1]</a></sup> Frederator's CEO <a href="/wiki/Fred_Seibert" title="Fred Seibert">Fred Seibert</a> was initially disinclined to make the short, feeling it was too much of a "student film" and without much commercial appeal. Longtime colleagues, development executive Homan and production executive Kevin Kolde, convinced him otherwise, arguing that Seibert had actually laughed in the presentation, something that he did not often do.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bleeding_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bleeding-8">[8]</a></sup> Frederator approved the pitch, and "Adventure Time" soon went into production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bleeding_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-bleeding-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ward hired several of his recently graduated CalArts friends to work on the short with him. Neil Graf was tasked with coloring, Julian Narino was the background designer, and Adam Muto drew the props. Graf and Narino later got jobs with other series and studios—<i><a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Hill" title="King of the Hill">King of the Hill</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Laika_(company)" title="Laika (company)">Laika</a> studio, respectively—but Muto continued working with Ward and currently serves as <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s co-executive producer and <a href="/wiki/Showrunner" title="Showrunner">showrunner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awnrandom_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-awnrandom-1">[1]</a></sup> The finished short ran for seven minutes, and production wrapped up in the spring of 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anotherera_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-anotherera-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_reception">Release and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adventure_Time_(pilot)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Adventure Time" first aired as a stand-alone short on Nicktoons network on January 11, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anotherera_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-anotherera-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and it was re-aired as part of Frederator Studios' <i>Random! Cartoons</i> on December 7, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeenter_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-timeenter-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In between airings, it leaked onto the internet and went <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeenter_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-timeenter-11">[11]</a></sup> According to Frederator Studios producer and founder <a href="/wiki/Fred_Seibert" title="Fred Seibert">Fred Seibert</a> the short, "between all of its distribution points," had been viewed almost 3,000,000 times by April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The feature was later nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Annie_Award" title="Annie Award">Annie Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Annie_Award_for_Best_Animated_Short_Subject" title="Annie Award for Best Animated Short Subject">Best Animated Short Subject</a>, although it did not win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>After its release and success, Frederator Studios then pitched an <i>Adventure Time</i> series to <a href="/wiki/Nicktoons_(TV_channel)" title="Nicktoons (TV channel)">Nicktoons</a>, but the network passed on it twice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeforsome_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-timeforsome-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enchiridion_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-enchiridion-15">[15]</a></sup> The studio then approached <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>. The network said they would be willing to produce the series if Ward could prove that "the seven-minute short made for Nick wasn’t a one-hit wonder".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enchiridion_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-enchiridion-15">[15]</a></sup> Ward quickly retooled the concept of the pilot; he wanted a potential series to be "fully realized", rather than possess the "pre-school vibe" that the original pilot had.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeforsome_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-timeforsome-7">[7]</a></sup> Initially, Ward submitted a rough storyboard that featured Finn and an "oblivious" Princess Bubblegum going on a spaghetti-supper date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enchiridion_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-enchiridion-15">[15]</a></sup> However, the network was not happy with this story, and specifically asked for an episode that contained the same things that had "made the short so special, like the crazy opening dance, the 'Abe Lincoln moment,' funny catchwords, and the awkward princess/kiss moment at the end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enchiridion_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-enchiridion-15">[15]</a></sup> Ward then created an early storyboard for the episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Enchiridion!" title="The Enchiridion!">The Enchiridion!</a>", which was his attempt to emulate the style of the original short. Eventually, Cartoon Network approved the first season in September 2008, and "<a href="/wiki/The_Enchiridion!" title="The Enchiridion!">The Enchiridion!</a>" became the first episode to enter into production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enchiridion_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-enchiridion-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-begin_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-begin-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
