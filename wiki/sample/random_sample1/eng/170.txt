<p><b>.br</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CcTLD" title="CcTLD">ccTLD</a>) for <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It was administered by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brazilian_Internet_Steering_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brazilian Internet Steering Committee (page does not exist)">Brazilian Internet Steering Committee</a> (ComitÃª Gestor da Internet no Brasil) until 2005 when it started being administered by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brazilian_Network_Information_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brazilian Network Information Center (page does not exist)">Brazilian Network Information Center</a> (NÃºcleo de InformaÃ§Ã£o e CoordenaÃ§Ã£o do Ponto br). A local contact is required for any registration. Registrations of domain names with Portuguese characters are also accepted.</p>
<p>With the exception of universities, the <a href="/wiki/Second-level_domain" title="Second-level domain">second-level domain</a> is fixed and selected from a list that defines the category. For example, <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">site.art.br</span> is in the <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a> (music, folklore <i>etc.</i>) category, and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">site.org.br</span> is in the <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">non-governmental organization</a> category. Institutions of <a href="/wiki/Tertiary_education" title="Tertiary education">tertiary education</a> were allowed to use the <a href="/wiki/Country_code_second-level_domain" title="Country code second-level domain">ccSLD</a> <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.edu.br</span>, although some use <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.com.br</span> and others (mainly public universities) use <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.br</span>. There are also some other few exceptions that were allowed to use the second level domain until the end of 2000. As of April 2010, most domain registrations ignore categories and register in the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.com.br</span> domain, which has over 90% of all registered domains. The <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.jus.br</span> (Judiciary), and <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.b.br</span> (banks) domains have mandatory <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DNSSEC" title="DNSSEC">DNSSEC</a> use.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.br&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Created and delegated to Brazil in 1989<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/Jon_Postel" title="Jon Postel">Jon Postel</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> initially the domain was operated manually by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Registro.br&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Registro.br (page does not exist)">Registro.br</a> and administered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funda%C3%A7%C3%A3o_de_Amparo_%C3%A0_Pesquisa_do_Estado_de_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="FundaÃ§Ã£o de Amparo Ã  Pesquisa do Estado de SÃ£o Paulo">FundaÃ§Ã£o de Amparo Ã  Pesquisa do Estado de SÃ£o Paulo</a> (FAPESP). Originally, only researchers and institutions to which they belonged had the interest and ability to adopt the new system and register domains under .br.</p>
<p>At the time, networks prevalent in the Brazilian academic setting were the <a href="/wiki/BITNET" title="BITNET">BITNET</a> ("Because It's Time NETwork"), the <a href="/wiki/HEPnet" title="HEPnet">HEPnet</a> ("High Energy Physics Network") and the <a href="/wiki/UUCP" title="UUCP">UUCP</a> ("Unix-to-Unix Copy Program"). As such, even before Brazil officially connected to the Internet in 1991, the .br domain was used to identify the machines participating in networks already in use by academics.</p>
<p>In 1995 the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Comit%C3%AA_Gestor_da_Internet_no_Brasil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ComitÃª Gestor da Internet no Brasil (page does not exist)">ComitÃª Gestor da Internet no Brasil</a> "Internet Management Committee of Brazil" (CGI.br) was created with an objective to coordinate the allocation of Internet addresses (IPs) and the registration of .br domain names. There were 851 domains registered with the Brazilian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DNS" title="DNS">DNS</a> (<i>Domain Name System") by the beginning of 1996, thereafter experiencing rapid growth with the mass arrival of companies, Internet providers and media onto the Internet. The registration system was automated in 1997 and was developed using open source software.</i></p>
<p>In 2005, CGI.br created his own executive arm, the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%BAcleo_de_Informa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_e_Coordena%C3%A7%C3%A3o_do_Ponto_BR&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NÃºcleo de InformaÃ§Ã£o e CoordenaÃ§Ã£o do Ponto BR (page does not exist)">NÃºcleo de InformaÃ§Ã£o e CoordenaÃ§Ã£o do Ponto BR</a>'</i> "The Center for Information and Coordination of Dot BR" (NIC.br),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> which currently serves in both administrative and operational capacity for the registry.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_registry">Domain registry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.br&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Domain registry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To register any domains under .br, it is necessary to enter into contact with Registro.br. Entities legally established in Brazil as a company ("pessoa jurÃ­dica") or a physical person ("profissional liberal" and "pessoas fÃ­sicas") that has a contact within Brazil can register domains. Foreign companies that have a power-of-attorney legally established in Brazil can also do it by following <a class="external text" href="http://registro.br/dominio/reg-estrangeiros.html" rel="nofollow">specific rules</a>.</p>
<p>The registration of domains with special Portuguese characters (Ã , Ã¡, Ã¢, Ã£, Ã©, Ãª, Ã­, Ã³, Ã´, Ãµ, Ãº, Ã¼ and Ã§) is accepted since 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Registro.br_-_Tips_and_Rules_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Registro.br_-_Tips_and_Rules-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Syntactic_rules_for_.br_domains">Syntactic rules for .br domains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.br&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Syntactic rules for .br domains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Minimum of 2 and maximum of 26 characters, not including the category. For example, in the field XXXX.COM.BR, this limitation relates to the XXXX.</li>
<li>Valid characters are [A-Z, 0-9], the hyphen, and the following accented characters: Ã , Ã¡, Ã¢, Ã£, Ã©, Ãª, Ã­, Ã³, Ã´, Ãµ, Ãº, Ã¼, Ã§.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Registro.br_-_Tips_and_Rules_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Registro.br_-_Tips_and_Rules-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Domains cannot contain only numbers.</li>
<li>To maintain the integrity of the registry, Registro.br sets up an equivalence mapping to compare domain names with and without accented characters. The mapping is done by converting accented characters and the cedilla for their non-accented versions and "c", respectively, and discards hyphens. A new domain will only be allowed to be registered when there is no equivalent to a pre-existing domain, or when the applicant is the same entity that owns the domain equivalent.</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd><b>Note:</b> Specifically for the domain .NOM.BR, it is necessary to choose two names, i.e.: NAME1.NAME2.NOM.BR.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_Statistics">Usage Statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.br&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Usage Statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
