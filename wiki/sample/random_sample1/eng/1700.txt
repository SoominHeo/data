<p><b>ゆ</b>, in <a href="/wiki/Hiragana" title="Hiragana">hiragana</a>, or <b>ユ</b> in <a href="/wiki/Katakana" title="Katakana">katakana</a>, is one of the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Kana" title="Kana">kana</a>, which each represent one <a href="/wiki/Mora_(linguistics)" title="Mora (linguistics)">mora</a>. Both are written in two strokes and represent <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ju͍]</span>.</p>
<p>When small and preceded by an -i kana, this kana represents not a separate sound but a modification of that of another (see <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Don" title="Yōon">yōon</a>).</p>
