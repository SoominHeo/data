<p>"<b>Bonamana</b>" (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean title</a>: 미인아 <i>Miina</i>; literal meaning <i>Beauty</i>) is a song performed by <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Boy_band" title="Boy band">boy band</a> <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior" title="Super Junior">Super Junior</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from the group's fourth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bonamana" title="Bonamana">Bonamana</a></i>. The song was released as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_single" title="Digital single">digital single</a> on May 10, 2010, three days before the South Korean release of <i>Bonamana</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4release_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-4release-1">[1]</a></sup> The song was written by <a href="/wiki/Yoo_Young-jin" title="Yoo Young-jin">Yoo Young-jin</a>, who also penned the lyrics.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonamana_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Followed by the establishment of "SJ Funky," a style that Super Junior had been exploring since the release of their third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Sorry,_Sorry" title="Sorry, Sorry">Sorry, Sorry</a></i>, "Bonamana" draws influences from American <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a> <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, incorporating African rhythms and club house beats into the melody, similar to the song's writer <a href="/wiki/Yoo_Young-jin" title="Yoo Young-jin">Yoo Young-jin</a> which later showed the same style on Yoo's later works <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-M" title="Super Junior-M">Super Junior-M</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Super_Girl_(Super_Junior_song)" title="Super Girl (Super Junior song)">Super Girl</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Shinee" title="Shinee">Shinee</a>'s "Ring Ding Dong".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genre_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-genre-2">[2]</a></sup> The song incorporates the Afro tom, an African percussive instrument, and the powerful riff of a Jupiter synth, a sound synthesizer. Vocals are portrayed through usage of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autotune" title="Autotune">autotune</a>. The song is about the sincerity of a man who is trying to win a girl's love, and the lyrics are described as witty and humorous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-song_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-song-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion_and_reception">Promotion and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonamana_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 6, 2010, "Bonamana" was announced to be the lead single from the group's fourth album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4release_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-4release-1">[1]</a></sup> The full single was released via <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> on South Korean MP3 download sites on May 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4release_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-4release-1">[1]</a></sup> The song was first performed on <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS2TV</a>'s music show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_show)" title="Music Bank (TV show)">Music Bank</a></i> on May 14,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-musicbank_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-musicbank-4">[4]</a></sup> and their performance peaked at number 3 on Nate's top trending search. The group performed "Bonamana" on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>'s <i>Show! Music Core</i> on May 15<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-musiccore_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-musiccore-5">[5]</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">The Music Trend</a></i> on May 16.</p>
<p>After only one week in the charts, "Bonamana" surged to number 1 on <i>Music Bank'</i>s K-Chart, making it Super Junior's fastest rise to the top of the chart for a lead single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-firstplace_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-firstplace-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Directed by Jang Jae-hyuk, the music video was filmed in a filming set in <a href="/wiki/Namyangju" title="Namyangju">Namyangju</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeonggi" title="Gyeonggi">Gyeonggi</a> in late April 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mv_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-mv-7">[7]</a></sup> The video is fairly low budget and was entirely shot in a disoriented <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sepia_tone" title="Sepia tone">sepia</a> color, with shadows and alternating brighter lights shining on the members, consistently changing the lighting of the video. The video first begins with different angle shots of each member standing and slowly spinning around in a dark background with alternating <a href="/wiki/Backlighting_(lighting_design)" title="Backlighting (lighting design)">backlighting</a>, and then proceeds to a lighter tone when the song begins. A majority of the music video focuses on showing the group's various <a href="/wiki/Hip-hop_dance" title="Hip-hop dance">hip-hop dance</a> formations and techniques. The choreography of the dance is inspired by <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating" title="Speed skating">speed skating</a>, and the dance steps during the first <a href="/wiki/Hook_(music)" title="Hook (music)">hook</a> of the song is inspired by <a href="/wiki/Kim_Yuna" title="Kim Yuna">Kim Yuna</a>'s "Danse Macabre" performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dance_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-dance-8">[8]</a></sup> Also, in the music video, there is a dance break performed by all members.</p>
<p>A minute teaser of the music video was revealed on May 7 through <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-announce_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-announce-9">[9]</a></sup> and the full music video premiered on May 12 on Korea's GOMTV video site and through SM Entertainment's official YouTube account,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> attracting over 600,000 views on the first day of release. It currently has over 55 million views.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_release">Japanese release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonamana_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Japanese release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On April 25, 2011, it was announced that the single is going to be re-released into a Japanese version and that it will be a tie-up for Japanese convenient store chain <a href="/wiki/Circle_K_Sunkus" title="Circle K Sunkus">Circle K Sunkus</a>. The song will be released as a digital download on May 18, 2011 to be followed by a physical release on June 8, 2011, therefore officially their first Japanese single. There will be an upcoming CM (commercial movie) for the tie-up that was filmed way back February 2011 and that the Japanese lyrics were recorded a month after, in March 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The single takes 2nd on Oricon chart on the first day of release. According to the Oricon Chart, <i>Bonamana (美人)</i> sold 27,168 units on its release day. In addition, <i>Bonamana (美人)</i> music video which is made of videos from ‘Super Junior 3rd Asia Tour Super Show 3 in Japan’ held at Yokohama Arena in February, received over 300,000 views on the Japanese UCC website ‘Nikoniko’ in a day.</p>
