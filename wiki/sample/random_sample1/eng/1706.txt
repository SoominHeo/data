<p><b>Gaga'emauga</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Samoa" title="Districts of Samoa">district</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Savai%27i" title="Savai'i">Savai'i</a> in <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>. The district is situated on the central north side of Savai'i. The name Gaga'emauga literally means "near side of the mountain", meaning the eastern side of the mountain chain running through the centre of Savai'i Island.</p>
<p>The traditional centre of the district is <a href="/wiki/Saleaula" title="Saleaula">Saleaula</a>, where the district chiefs (<a href="/wiki/Fa%27amatai" title="Fa'amatai"><i>matai</i></a>) and orators meet at Vaitu’utu’u malae.</p>
<p>Like most villages in Samoa, the villages in Gaga'emauga are situated by the sea although there are some settlements inland, including <a href="/wiki/Patamea" title="Patamea">Patamea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samalae%27ulu" title="Samalae'ulu">Samalae'ulu</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/George_Pratt_(missionary)" title="George Pratt (missionary)">Reverend George Pratt</a> (1817–1894), a missionary with the <a href="/wiki/London_Missionary_Society" title="London Missionary Society">London Missionary Society</a>, lived in <a href="/wiki/Matautu" title="Matautu">Matautu</a> (1839–1879)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and authored the first grammar and dictionary of the <a href="/wiki/Samoan_language" title="Samoan language">Samoan language</a>, "A Grammar and Dictionary of the Samoan Language, with English and Samoan Vocabulary", which was first printed in 1862 at the Samoa Mission Press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The village of Saleaula maintains strong traditional connections with <a href="/wiki/Safotulafai" title="Safotulafai">Safotulafai</a> to the east, through the paramount chiefly title of Letufuga. Safotulafai is the capital of the political district <a href="/wiki/Fa%27asaleleaga" title="Fa'asaleleaga">Fa'asaleleaga</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exclaves_on_Upolu">Exclaves on Upolu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gaga%27emauga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Exclaves on Upolu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Two small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclaves</a> of Gaga'emauga district are situated on the island of <a href="/wiki/Upolu" title="Upolu">Upolu</a>, following the resettlement of villages destroyed during volcanic eruptions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mt_Matavanu" title="Mt Matavanu">Mt Matavanu</a> in the early 1900s. These exclaves are <a href="/wiki/Le%27auva%27a" title="Le'auva'a">Le'auva'a</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salamumu" title="Salamumu">Salamumu</a> villages which remain politically part of Savai'i despite their relocation.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Volcanic_eruptions_1900s">Volcanic eruptions 1900s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gaga%27emauga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Volcanic eruptions 1900s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2077" data-file-width="2656" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg/190px-Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg/285px-Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg/380px-Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg 2x" width="190"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lava_Fields_-_Savai%27i_island_-_Samoa.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lava fields on Savai'i.</div>

<p>The relocated villages of Gaga'emauga district on the main island Upolu was a result of 1905 - 1911 volcanic eruptions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mt_Matavanu" title="Mt Matavanu">Mt Matavanu</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> situated about 11 km inland from <a href="/wiki/Matautu" title="Matautu">Matautu</a> on the central north coast. Lava flows from eruptions destroyed villages in its path, including Saleaula and Salago villages, transforming the northern half of this district into lava fields which are still visible today, especially at Saleaula. The people of the affected areas were evacuated to Upolu, where they established the villages of <a href="/wiki/Le%27auva%27a" title="Le'auva'a">Le'auva'a</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salamumu" title="Salamumu">Salamumu</a>. Despite the move, they still retain their traditional links and <i>fa'alupega</i> (genealogy) affiliations to Gagaemauga. The names Leava'a and Salamumu denote the events of the 1905 Mt Matavanu eruption. In recent years, a few families have re-built houses on the lava fields where vegetation is still sparse.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Itu_o_Tane">Itu o Tane</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gaga%27emauga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Itu o Tane">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gaga'emauga and the neighbouring political district <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gagaifomauga" title="Gagaifomauga">Gagaifomauga</a> are together referred to as Itu-o-Tane, 'The Side of Men.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-test-4">[4]</a></sup> The term Itu-o-Tane originates from the bravery of this north coast side in war, and in particular the 1830 war with A'ana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-test-4">[4]</a></sup> In contrast, part of the island's south coast is known as Itu-o-Fafine, 'The Side of Women.'</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_administration">Government administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gaga%27emauga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Government administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the village of <a href="/wiki/Fagamalo" title="Fagamalo">Fagamalo</a>, a sub-village <i>pito nu'u</i> of the larger <a href="/wiki/Matautu" title="Matautu">Matautu</a> village, had a wharf and anchorage and was the main government administrative centre on Savai'i. Fagamalo was also the home of the colonial resident commissioner of Savai'i when Allied forces were defending the South Pacific against Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-test-4">[4]</a></sup> In modern times, the government administration on Savai'i has moved to <a href="/wiki/Tuasivi" title="Tuasivi">Tuasivi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Fa%27asaleleaga" title="Fa'asaleleaga">Fa'asaleleaga</a> district, where there's a hospital, police station and judicial court offices.</p>
