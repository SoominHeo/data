<p><b>Masaru Kageura</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">景浦 將</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kageura Masaru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, July 20, 1915 – May 20, 1945)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> player from <a href="/wiki/Matsuyama,_Ehime" title="Matsuyama, Ehime">Matsuyama, Ehime</a>. His team reached the final in the 1932 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_High_School_Baseball_Championship" title="National High School Baseball Championship">National High School Baseball Championship</a>, but his team was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Masao_Yoshida_(baseball)" title="Masao Yoshida (baseball)">Masao Yoshida</a> in the final. He joined <a href="/wiki/Hanshin_Tigers" title="Hanshin Tigers">Tigers</a> and he was a good rival for <a href="/wiki/Eiji_Sawamura" title="Eiji Sawamura">Eiji Sawamura</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Giants" title="Yomiuri Giants">Giants</a>. He was killed in the battle of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. He was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" title="Japanese Baseball Hall of Fame">Japanese Baseball Hall of Fame</a> in 1965. He is one of two baseball players (<a href="/wiki/Eiji_Sawamura" title="Eiji Sawamura">Eiji Sawamura</a> is the other) who was inducted into the Hall of Fame after being killed in World War II.</p>
