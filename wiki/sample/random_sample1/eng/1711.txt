<p><b>Kagoshima-Chūō Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鹿児島中央駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kagoshima-Chūō-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit: Kagoshima Central Station)</span> is a major railway station in <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima" title="Kagoshima">Kagoshima</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_Railway_Company" title="Kyushu Railway Company">Kyushu Railway Company</a> (JR Kyushu). It is the southern terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_Shinkansen" title="Kyushu Shinkansen">Kyushu Shinkansen</a> and is located on the <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Main_Line" title="Kagoshima Main Line">Kagoshima Main Line</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ibusuki-Makurazaki_Line" title="Ibusuki-Makurazaki Line">Ibusuki-Makurazaki Line</a>. Before the Kyushu Shinkansen opened in 2004, the station was called Nishi-Kagoshima Station (lit: West Kagoshima Station).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kagoshima-Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1288" data-file-width="1844" height="154" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg/220px-Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg/330px-Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg/440px-Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nishi-Kagoshima_Station_1959.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Nishi-Kagoshima station at 1959.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>October 11, 1913 - Opened as Take Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">武駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Take-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on the Sendai Line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railwaymap51_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-railwaymap51-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nishinihon20070316_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nishinihon20070316-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>December 17, 1915 - Opened as Take Ekimae tram stop <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">武駅前停留所</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by Kagoshima Tram.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railwaymap52_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-railwaymap52-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>October 17, 1927 - Take Station changed its name to Nishi-Kagoshima Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">西鹿児島駅</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railwaymap51_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-railwaymap51-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>August 7, 1928 - Take Ekimae tram stop was renamed Nishi-Kagoshima Ekimae tram stop <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">西鹿児島駅前停留所</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railwaymap52_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-railwaymap52-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>On March 13, 2004, Nishi-Kagoshima Station was renamed Kagoshima-Chūō Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鹿児島中央駅</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railwaymap51_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-railwaymap51-1">[1]</a></sup> and Kyushu Shinkansen services began between Shin-Yatsushiro and Kagoshima-Chūō. Nishi-Kagoshima Ekimae tram stop was renamed Kagoshima-Chuo Ekimae tram stop <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鹿児島中央駅前停留所</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railwaymap52_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-railwaymap52-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
