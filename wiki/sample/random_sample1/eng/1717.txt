<p>The <a href="/wiki/Shinto" title="Shinto">Shinto</a> <i><b>Kanamara Matsuri</b></i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">かなまら祭り</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, "Festival of the Steel Phallus")</span> is held each spring at the <b>Kanayama Shrine</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">金山神社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kanayama-jinja</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki,_Kanagawa" title="Kawasaki, Kanagawa">Kawasaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The exact dates vary: the main festivities fall on the first Sunday in April. The <a href="/wiki/Phallus" title="Phallus">phallus</a>, as the central theme of the event, is reflected in illustrations, candy, carved vegetables, decorations, and a <a href="/wiki/Mikoshi" title="Mikoshi">mikoshi</a> parade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cosmopolitan_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cosmopolitan-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeslive_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-timeslive-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-abc-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Kanamara Matsuri is centered on a local <a href="/wiki/Penis" title="Penis">penis</a>-venerating shrine. The legend being that a jealous sharp-toothed demon hid inside the <a href="/wiki/Vagina" title="Vagina">vagina</a> of a young woman the demon fell in love with and bit off penises of two young men on their wedding nights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost3_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost3-6">[6]</a></sup> After that the woman sought help from a blacksmith, who fashioned an iron <a href="/wiki/Phallus" title="Phallus">phallus</a> to break the demon's teeth, which led to the enshrinement of the item.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metropolis_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-metropolis-7">[7]</a></sup> This legend in <a href="/wiki/Ainu_language" title="Ainu language">Ainu language</a> was published as "The Island of Women" by <a href="/wiki/Basil_Hall_Chamberlain" title="Basil Hall Chamberlain">Basil Hall Chamberlain</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Kanayama Shrine was popular among <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prostitute" title="Prostitute">prostitutes</a> who wished to pray for protection from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sexually_transmitted_infections" title="Sexually transmitted infections">sexually transmitted infections</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is also said the shrine offers divine protections for business prosperity, and for the clan's prosperity; and for easy delivery, marriage, and married-couple harmony.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The festival started in 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-php_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-php-10">[10]</a></sup> Today, the festival has become something of a tourist attraction and is used to raise money for HIV research.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heraldsun_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-heraldsun-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
