<p><b>Kaneko Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">金子駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kaneko-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Hachik%C5%8D_Line" title="Hachikō Line">Hachikō Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iruma,_Saitama" title="Iruma, Saitama">Iruma, Saitama</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaneko_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaneko Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Hachik%C5%8D_Line" title="Hachikō Line">Hachikō Line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Hachi%C5%8Dji_Station" title="Hachiōji Station">Hachiōji</a> and <a href="/wiki/Komagawa_Station" title="Komagawa Station">Komagawa</a>, with many services continuing to and from <a href="/wiki/Kawagoe_Station" title="Kawagoe Station">Kawagoe</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Kawagoe_Line" title="Kawagoe Line">Kawagoe Line</a>. The station is 20.5 kilometers from the official starting point of the line at Hachiōji.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaneko_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The station consists of two opposed <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a> serving two tracks, which form a passing loop on the single-track line. The platforms are connected to the station building by a footbridge.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaneko_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kaneko_sta.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Kaneko_sta.jpg/220px-Kaneko_sta.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Kaneko_sta.jpg/330px-Kaneko_sta.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Kaneko_sta.jpg/440px-Kaneko_sta.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

</div>

<p>The station opened on 10 December 1931 in the former village of Kaneko.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-2">[2]</a></sup> With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR East.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The southern section of the Hachikō Line between Hachiōji and Komagawa was electrified on 16 March 1996, with through services commencing between Hachiōji and Kawagoe.</p>
<p>From September 2014, the wooden station building was replaced by a temporary structure while a new station building was built.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20140918_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20140918-3">[3]</a></sup> The new station structure was brought into use from 8 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmnews20150210_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rmnews20150210-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
