<p><b>Godman's rock-wallaby</b> (<i>Petrogale godmani</i>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diprotodont" title="Diprotodont">diprotodont</a> <a href="/wiki/Marsupial" title="Marsupial">marsupial</a>, and a typical <a href="/wiki/Rock-wallaby" title="Rock-wallaby">rock-wallaby</a>. It is found in northern and north-eastern <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. This rock-wallaby is found in low open forest, open scrub, or montane regions, often near the coast. It shelters in rocky terrain adjacent to feeding areas. Godman's rock-wallaby is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nocturnal" title="Nocturnal">nocturnal</a> gregarious <a href="/wiki/Territory_(animal)" title="Territory (animal)">territorial</a> <a href="/wiki/Folivore" title="Folivore">folivore</a>.</p>
