<p>The <b>Garena Premier League</b> (<b>GPL</b>) is a professional <i><a href="/wiki/League_of_Legends" title="League of Legends">League of Legends</a></i> league with teams in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. It was founded in 2012. The opening event took place on May 5 with Riot's CEO Brandon Beck, VP of Gaming Dustin Beck, Taiwanese game designer Jeff Lin, and Nikasaur attending personally. The league currently consists of twelve teams playing in two groups of six. Most games are played online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The top four teams of both groups advance to the playoffs, which are held in a best-of-5 single elimination system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2014 Garena split teams from <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> into their own league, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/League_of_Legends_Masters_Series" title="League of Legends Masters Series">League of Legends Masters Series</a>, which began play in Spring 2015. Following the changes, the Garena Premier League no longer sends any teams directly to the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Legends_World_Championship" title="League of Legends World Championship">League of Legends World Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
