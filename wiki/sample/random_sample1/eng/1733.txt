<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garissa_University_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Garissa University College was founded in 2011 as a constituent college of <a href="/wiki/Moi_University" title="Moi University">Moi University</a>, in the facilities of the former <b>Garissa Teachers Training College</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-facts_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-facts-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The school's library was established in 1996, as a resource for the former <a href="/wiki/Teacher_training_college" title="Teacher training college">teacher training college</a>. It was later staffed in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was the first and only public post-secondary school to offer approved university degree courses in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Province,_Kenya" title="North Eastern Province, Kenya">North Eastern Province</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-facts_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-facts-1">[1]</a></sup> The institution provides courses in the schools of education, information science, and arts and social sciences.</p>
<p>The school principal is Prof. Ahmed Warfa, the Deputy Principal (Finance) is Prof. Kirimi Kiriamiti, and the Deputy Principal (Academics) is Prof. Genevieve Mwayuli. The university college has 75 staff members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_attack">2015 attack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garissa_University_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2015 attack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Garissa_University_College_attack" title="Garissa University College attack">Garissa University College attack</a></div>
<p>On April 2, 2015, gunmen stormed Garissa University College, killing 148 people and wounding 79 or more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The attackers claimed to be from the <a href="/wiki/Al-Shabaab_(militant_group)" title="Al-Shabaab (militant group)">Al-Shabaab</a> militant group and indicated that they were retaliating over non-Muslims occupying Muslim territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mtsdiaa_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mtsdiaa-5">[5]</a></sup> The militants took several Christian students hostage, but freed most Muslims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup> The following day, national and county leaders agreed to suspend courses at the college indefinitely, to allow security services to investigate the attack. Buses were also hired to return students to their homes, and several students insisted they would not return to the university.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mutambo_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mutambo-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Approximately 650 surviving students of the University College were absorbed into courses at the main <a href="/wiki/Moi_University" title="Moi University">Moi University</a> campus at <a href="/wiki/Eldoret" title="Eldoret">Eldoret</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Kaimenyi" title="Jacob Kaimenyi">Jacob Kaimenyi</a>, Kenya's Education cabinet secretary, urged students from the teacher training college to return to the Garissa campus which was re-opened on 5 May 2015 with improved security. Many students, however, have refused to return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The governor of <a href="/wiki/Homa_Bay_County" title="Homa Bay County">Homa Bay County</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cyprian_Awiti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cyprian Awiti (page does not exist)">Cyprian Awiti</a>, decided to award 30,000 <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_shilling" title="Kenyan shilling">Kenyan shillings</a> to each of the 16 surviving students from that county, in order to help students overcome their trauma and to make arrangements to attend school in Eldoret.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
