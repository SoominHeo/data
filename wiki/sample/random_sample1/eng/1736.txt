<p><b>Kamagaya</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">鎌ヶ谷市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamagaya-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Prefecture" title="Chiba Prefecture">Chiba Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of December 1, 2015, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 108,392 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 5,140 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area was 21.08 km<sup>2</sup> (8.14 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamagaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area around Kamagaya has been inhabited since prehistory, and archaeologists have found <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dmon_period" title="Jōmon period">Jōmon period</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shell_midden" title="Shell midden">shell middens</a> in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nipponika_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nipponika-2">[2]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Kamakura_period" title="Kamakura period">Kamakura period</a>, the area was controlled by the <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dma_clan" title="Sōma clan">Sōma clan</a>. Kamagaya flourished in the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> 1603–1868 when the area was largely <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%8D" title="Tenryō">tenryō</a></i> territory within <a href="/wiki/Shim%C5%8Dsa_Province" title="Shimōsa Province">Shimōsa Province</a> controlled directly by the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>. One part of present-day Kamagaya was part of the Kogane Ranch, which raised war horses for the army of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>. In the Edo period Kamagaya was also a thriving <i><a href="/wiki/Shukuba" title="Shukuba">shukuba</a></i> post on the Kioroshi Road, which connected present-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inzai,_Chiba" title="Inzai, Chiba">Inzai</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Tone_River" title="Tone River">Tone River</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edogawa-ku" title="Edogawa-ku">Edogawa-ku</a> in Tokyo. The road was utilized to bring fresh fish and other marine products from the Tone River region to the capital Edo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kokugo_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-kokugo-3">[3]</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, it became part of Chiba Prefecture. Kamagaya Village was one of several villages created on April 1, 1889 under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inba_District,_Chiba" title="Inba District, Chiba">Inba District</a>. In 1945, the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a> appropriated the former Musashino Country Club, turning the <a href="/wiki/Golf_course" title="Golf course">golf course</a> into an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_field" title="Air field">air field</a>. The base was turned over to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> after World War II, becoming Shiroi Air Base, and was transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Maritime_Self-Defense_Force" title="Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force">Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force</a> in 1959, becoming <a href="/wiki/Shimofusa_Air_Base" title="Shimofusa Air Base">JMSDF Shimofusa Air Base</a>. Kamagaya became a town on August 1, 1968, and was elevated to city status on September 1, 1971.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamagaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamagaya, formerly a farming area, is now a regional commercial center. Due to its numerous train connections it serves as a transportation hub and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedroom_community" title="Bedroom community">bedroom community</a> for nearby <a href="/wiki/Chiba,_Chiba" title="Chiba, Chiba">Chiba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. Kamagaya's trademark produce is the round, <a href="/wiki/Pyrus_pyrifolia" title="Pyrus pyrifolia">Nashi Pear</a>, and there are many pear <a href="/wiki/Orchard" title="Orchard">orchards</a> dotted about the city, whose white blossoms open toward the end of the cherry blossom season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-enc-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamagaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city operates five junior high schools and nine elementary schools. Kamagaya has two public high schools, operated by the Chiba Prefectural government.</p>
