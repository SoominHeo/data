<p><b>Kamata Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">蒲田駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamata-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cta,_Tokyo" title="Ōta, Tokyo">Ōta, Tokyo</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East) and the private railway operator <a href="/wiki/Tokyu_Corporation" title="Tokyu Corporation">Tokyu Corporation</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamata_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamata Station is served by the following lines:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keihin-Tohoku_Line" title="Keihin-Tohoku Line">Keihin-Tohoku Line</a> (JR East)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyu_Tamagawa_Line" title="Tokyu Tamagawa Line">Tokyu Tamagawa Line</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyu_Ikegami_Line" title="Tokyu Ikegami Line">Tokyu Ikegami Line</a></li>
</ul>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keikyu_Kamata_Station" title="Keikyu Kamata Station">Keikyu Kamata Station</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keikyu_Main_Line" title="Keikyu Main Line">Keikyu Main Line</a> is located about 700 m to the east of Kamata Station.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamata_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The JR East station opened on 11 April 1904.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnr1985_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jnr1985-1">[1]</a></sup> The Tokyu station opened on 6 October 1922 on the Ikegami Line, and services on Tamagawa Line began on 1 November 1923.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_plans">Future plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamata_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Future plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Plans exist to extend the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyu_Tamagawa_Line" title="Tokyu Tamagawa Line">Tokyu Tamagawa Line</a> from Kamata eastward by approximately 800 m to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keikyu_Kamata_Station" title="Keikyu Kamata Station">Keikyu Kamata Station</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keikyu_Main_Line" title="Keikyu Main Line">Keikyu Main Line</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keikyu_Airport_Line" title="Keikyu Airport Line">Keikyu Airport Line</a>. This would provide an interchange between the lines, improving accessibility to Tokyo's <a href="/wiki/Haneda_Airport" title="Haneda Airport">Haneda Airport</a> ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Summer_Olympics" title="2020 Summer Olympics">2020 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jt20140729_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jt20140729-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamata_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2013, the JR East station was used by an average of 139,728 passengers daily (boarding passengers only), making it the nineteenth-busiest station operated by JR East.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast2013stats_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast2013stats-4">[4]</a></sup> Over the same fiscal year the Tōkyū Ikegami and Tamagawa Line stations were used by an average of 69,464 and 88,102 passengers daily respectively (entering and exiting passengers).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tokyu2013stats_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tokyu2013stats-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
