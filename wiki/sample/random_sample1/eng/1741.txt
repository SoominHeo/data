<p><i><b>Kamen Rider OOO</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーオーズ／ＯＯＯ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Ōzu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a 2010-2011 Japanese <a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a> drama in <a href="/wiki/Toei_Company" title="Toei Company">Toei Company</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Series" title="Kamen Rider Series">Kamen Rider Series</a>, being the twelfth series in the <a href="/wiki/Heisei_period" title="Heisei period">Heisei period</a> run and the twenty-first overall. It began airing on September 5, 2010, the week following the conclusion of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_W" title="Kamen Rider W">Kamen Rider W</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uchu129_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-uchu129-1">[1]</a></sup> joining <i><a href="/wiki/Tensou_Sentai_Goseiger" title="Tensou Sentai Goseiger">Tensou Sentai Goseiger</a></i> and then <i><a href="/wiki/Kaizoku_Sentai_Gokaiger" title="Kaizoku Sentai Gokaiger">Kaizoku Sentai Gokaiger</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Super_Hero_Time" title="Super Hero Time">Super Hero Time</a> lineup, until its conclusion on August 28, 2011. The series' titular Kamen Rider made a cameo appearance in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_W_Forever:_A_to_Z/The_Gaia_Memories_of_Fate" title="Kamen Rider W Forever: A to Z/The Gaia Memories of Fate">Kamen Rider W Forever: A to Z/The Gaia Memories of Fate</a></i>.</p>

<p><i>OOO</i> is written by <a href="/wiki/Yasuko_Kobayashi" title="Yasuko Kobayashi">Yasuko Kobayashi</a>, known for the screenplays of <i><a href="/wiki/Seijuu_Sentai_Gingaman" title="Seijuu Sentai Gingaman">Seijuu Sentai Gingaman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mirai_Sentai_Timeranger" title="Mirai Sentai Timeranger">Mirai Sentai Timeranger</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Ryuki" title="Kamen Rider Ryuki">Kamen Rider Ryuki</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Den-O" title="Kamen Rider Den-O">Den-O</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Sentai_Shinkenger" title="Samurai Sentai Shinkenger">Samurai Sentai Shinkenger</a></i>. The creature designers are <a href="/wiki/Yutaka_Izubuchi" title="Yutaka Izubuchi">Yutaka Izubuchi</a>, known for his work on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_(Kamen_Rider)" title="Lord (Kamen Rider)">Lords</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Agito" title="Kamen Rider Agito">Kamen Rider Agito</a></i>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tamotsu_Shinohara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tamotsu Shinohara (page does not exist)">Tamotsu Shinohara</a>, known for his work on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mirror_Monster" title="Mirror Monster">Mirror Monsters</a> of <i>Ryuki</i>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orphnoch" title="Orphnoch">Orphnoch</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_555" title="Kamen Rider 555">Kamen Rider 555</a></i>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fangire" title="Fangire">Fangires</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Kiva" title="Kamen Rider Kiva">Kamen Rider Kiva</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uchu129_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-uchu129-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike several previous Heisei Rider Series, no alternate name has been given yet for the three O's in the title (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">Ｗ（ダブル）</span> read as "Double" and <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">555（ファイズ）</span> read as "Φ's/Phi's/Faiz") aside from an alternate spelling of the name of the main Kamen Rider: Ooz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Both "OOO" and "Ooz" are intended to be read as "O's", referring to the multiple uses of the letter <a href="/wiki/O" title="O">O</a> in the title as well as "multiple kings" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">王ｓ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōzu</i>)</span>. The title <i>OOO</i> signifies the three medals that the main Kamen Rider uses to transform as well as being representative of the <a href="/wiki/Infinity" title="Infinity">infinity symbol</a> (<span><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">

<p>The 28th episode of <i>Kamen Rider OOO</i> serves as the 1000th episode of the <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Series" title="Kamen Rider Series">Kamen Rider Series</a> since <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider" title="Kamen Rider">Kamen Rider</a></i> premiered on April 3, 1971. The episode was originally set to premiere on March 27, 2011. However, due to the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>, the airing of this episode was postponed by one week to April 3, 2011. For the 999th and 1000th episodes, various guest stars with the kanji for thousand <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">千</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>chi</i>)</span> in their name were featured: <a href="/wiki/Chiaki_(tarento)" title="Chiaki (tarento)">Chiaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chisato_Morishita" title="Chisato Morishita">Chisato Morishita</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chinatsu_Wakatsuki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chinatsu Wakatsuki (page does not exist)">Chinatsu Wakatsuki</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Owarai" title="Owarai">owarai</a> duo <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Harisenbon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Harisenbon (page does not exist)">Harisenbon</a> (<i>sen</i> also being Japanese for 1000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Story">Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_OOO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_OOO_characters" title="List of Kamen Rider OOO characters">List of Kamen Rider OOO characters</a></div>
<p>Eiji Hino is a traveling man who has no place to call home and a tragic past. When metallic creatures known as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_OOO_characters#Greeed" title="List of Kamen Rider OOO characters">Greeed</a> awaken after their 800-year slumber to attack humans and feed off of their desires, the disembodied arm of the Greed named <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_OOO_characters#Ankh" title="List of Kamen Rider OOO characters">Ankh</a> gives Eiji a belt and three Medals to fight the other Greed as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_OOO_(character)" title="Kamen Rider OOO (character)">Kamen Rider OOO</a>. The mysterious <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_OOO_characters#Kougami_Foundation" title="List of Kamen Rider OOO characters">Kougami Foundation</a> approaches Eiji and begins assisting him in his fight against the Greed, though their true motives are not clear. As Eiji fights the Greed and their Yummy monsters, learning more of the Greed and Ankh, he starts to find a purpose beyond his journey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uchu129_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-uchu129-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_%C3%97_Kamen_Rider_Fourze_%26_OOO:_Movie_War_Mega_Max" title="Kamen Rider × Kamen Rider Fourze &amp; OOO: Movie War Mega Max">Kamen Rider × Kamen Rider Fourze &amp; OOO: Movie War Mega Max</a></div>
<p>A crossover film between <i>Kamen Rider OOO</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Fourze" title="Kamen Rider Fourze">Kamen Rider Fourze</a></i> in the same vein of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Movie_War_2010" title="Movie War 2010">Movie War 2010</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Movie_War_Core" title="Movie War Core">Movie War Core</a></i> films, titled <i>Kamen Rider × Kamen Rider Fourze &amp; OOO: Movie War Mega Max</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダー×仮面ライダー フォーゼ&amp;オーズ MOVIE大戦MEGA MAX</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā × Kamen Raidā Fōze Ando Ōzu Mūbī Taisen Mega Makkusu</i>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[9]</a></sup> is planned for a December 10, 2011, theatrical release in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Super_Hero_Taisen"><i>Super Hero Taisen</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_OOO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Super Hero Taisen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_%C3%97_Super_Sentai:_Super_Hero_Taisen" title="Kamen Rider × Super Sentai: Super Hero Taisen">Kamen Rider × Super Sentai: Super Hero Taisen</a></div>
<p><i>Kamen Rider × Super Sentai: Super Hero Taisen</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダー×スーパー戦隊 スーパーヒーロー大戦</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā × Sūpā Sentai Sūpā Hīrō Taisen</i>)</span> is a film which features a crossover between the characters of the <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Series" title="Kamen Rider Series">Kamen Rider</a> and <a href="/wiki/Super_Sentai" title="Super Sentai">Super Sentai</a> Series. The protagonists of <i>Kamen Rider Decade</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kaizoku_Sentai_Gokaiger" title="Kaizoku Sentai Gokaiger">Kaizoku Sentai Gokaiger</a></i> will be featured, but the casts of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Fourze" title="Kamen Rider Fourze">Kamen Rider Fourze</a></i>, <i>Kamen Rider OOO</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Tokumei_Sentai_Go-Busters" title="Tokumei Sentai Go-Busters">Tokumei Sentai Go-Busters</a></i> will also participate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Shu_Watanabe_(actor)" title="Shu Watanabe (actor)">Shu Watanabe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Riho_Takada" title="Riho Takada">Riho Takada</a> had reprise their roles as <i>Eiji Hino</i> and <i>Hina Izumi</i>, while <a href="/wiki/Asaya_Kimijima" title="Asaya Kimijima">Asaya Kimijima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hiroaki_Iwanaga" title="Hiroaki Iwanaga">Hiroaki Iwanaga</a> reprise their roles voicing <i>Kamen Rider Birth</i> and <i>Kamen Rider Birth Prototype</i> respectively.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_%C3%97_Kamen_Rider_Wizard_%26_Fourze:_Movie_War_Ultimatum" title="Kamen Rider × Kamen Rider Wizard &amp; Fourze: Movie War Ultimatum">Kamen Rider × Kamen Rider Wizard &amp; Fourze: Movie War Ultimatum</a></div>
<p><i>Kamen Rider × Kamen Rider Wizard &amp; Fourze: Movie War Ultimatum</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダー×仮面ライダー ウィザード&amp;フォーゼ MOVIE大戦アルティメイタム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā × Kamen Raidā Wizādo Ando Fōze Mūbī Taisen Arutimeitamu</i>)</span> was released on December 8, 2012, as the annual winter "Movie War" film. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game">Video game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_OOO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Video game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A port of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider:_Climax_Heroes" title="Kamen Rider: Climax Heroes">Kamen Rider: Climax Heroes</a></i> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider:_Climax_Heroes#Climax_Heroes_OOO" title="Kamen Rider: Climax Heroes">Kamen Rider: Climax Heroes OOO</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダー クライマックスヒーローズ オーズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Kuraimakkusu Hīrōzu Ōzu</i>)</span> was released on both the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> and Nintendo <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> on December 2, 2010. In addition to adding Kamen Rider OOO to the game, the previous Heisei Riders' most powerful forms (e.g., Kuuga Ultimate Form &amp; Double CycloneJokerXtreme) are added as playable characters as well as other Heisei Kamen Riders not included in either of the two previous versions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hyper_Battle_DVD">Hyper Battle DVD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_OOO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Hyper Battle DVD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Kamen Rider OOO Hyper Battle DVD: Quiz, Dance, and Takagarooba!?</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーオーズ ハイパーバトルDVD クイズとダンスとタカガルバ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Ōzu Haipā Batoru Dī Bui Dī Kuizu to Dansu to Takagaruba</i>)</span> was released alongside an issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Televi-Kun" title="Televi-Kun">Televi-Kun</a></i> magazine. The DVD featured the Kangaroo Medal, which was used by Kamen Rider OOO to transform either his arms or legs to have <a href="/wiki/Kangaroo" title="Kangaroo">kangaroo</a>-themed powers.</p>
<p>"Kangaroo Dance" is a song used in the Hyper Battle DVD featuring Akira Kushida chanting "Kangaroo" in the background with composition &amp; arrangement from <a href="/wiki/Shuhei_Naruse" title="Shuhei Naruse">Shuhei Naruse</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_Episode"><i>Final Episode</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_OOO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Final Episode">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Kamen Rider OOO: Final Episode</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーオーズ FINAL EPISODE</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Ōzu Fainaru Episōdo</i>)</span> is the director's cut version of the last two episodes of <i>Kamen Rider OOO</i> put together. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Novel">Novel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_OOO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Novel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Kamen Rider OOO</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーオーズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Ōzu</i>)</span>, written by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nobuhiro_Mori&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nobuhiro Mori (page does not exist)">Nobuhiro Mori</a>, is part of a series of spin-off novel adaptions of the Heisei Era Kamen Riders. The novel is split in three parts. The first part, Ankh's part, focuses the origin of the Core Medals, the King, and the Greeeds from Ankh's point of view and reveal secrets unanswered from the series. The second part, Birth's part, takes on the Birth Driver's point of view, deciding between Akira Date and Shintaro Goto to see who is worthy to use it. The final part, Eiji's part, focuses on Eiji's past and reveals how he met Alfreed. The novel was released on November 30, 2012.</p>

<dt>Opening theme</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Anything_Goes!_(Maki_Ohguro_song)" title="Anything Goes! (Maki Ohguro song)">Anything Goes!</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oops_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-oops-21">[19]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li>Lyrics: <a href="/wiki/Shoko_Fujibayashi" title="Shoko Fujibayashi">Shoko Fujibayashi</a></li>
<li>Composition: Tatsuo (of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Everset&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Everset (page does not exist)">everset</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_W#Songs" title="Kamen Rider W">Galveston 19</a>)</li>

<li>Artist: <a href="/wiki/Maki_Ohguro" title="Maki Ohguro">Maki Ohguro</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">大黒 摩季</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōguro Maki</i>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon-22">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[22]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>"Anything Goes!" was released on November 17, 2010, as a standard CD and a CD+DVD combo pack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oops_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-oops-21">[19]</a></sup> The rapping on the song was performed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rah-D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rah-D (page does not exist)">Rah-D</a>, who provides his vocal talent in some of the show's background music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[23]</a></sup></dd>
<dd>A balladic version of the song titled "Anything Goes! Ballad" was during the finale of <i>Kamen Rider OOO</i>. Ohguro stated that she thought that the song would also work as a slow ballad, especially after she has been watching how the series has progressed, and recorded the song during her hiatus from releases. The balladic version was released as a single on December 7, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[24]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dt>Ending themes</dt>
<dd>Each of the series' various ending themes are titled in such a way to resemble the Kamen Rider OOO Combo that they serve as the theme for. The songs also feature <a href="/wiki/Akira_Kushida" title="Akira Kushida">Akira Kushida</a>, the voice for the OOO Driver, chanting the name of the combo throughout the song. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsuhiro_Ayano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mitsuhiro Ayano (page does not exist)">Ayano</a>, a session guitarist for many of the recent <i>Kamen Rider</i> Series songs and member of the "Tsuyoshi &amp; Ayano" group from the <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_W" title="Kamen Rider W">Kamen Rider W</a></i> radio show, performs guitars on all of the tracks.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>

<ul>
<li>Lyrics: Shoko Fujibayashi</li>
<li>Composition &amp; Arrangement: <a href="/wiki/Shuhei_Naruse" title="Shuhei Naruse">Shuhei Naruse</a></li>
<li>Artist: Eiji Hino (<a href="/wiki/Shu_Watanabe_(actor)" title="Shu Watanabe (actor)">Shu Watanabe</a>)</li>
<li>Episodes: 5, 7, 13</li>

When played during scenes with other Combos, the "Tatoba" chant is removed from the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ending_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ending-29">[27]</a></sup> The single for "Regret nothing ~Tighten Up~" features a <a href="/wiki/Ska" title="Ska">ska</a> arrangement by <a href="/wiki/Trombone" title="Trombone">trombone</a> player <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eijiro_Nakagawa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eijiro Nakagawa (page does not exist)">Eijiro Nakagawa</a>.</dd>
