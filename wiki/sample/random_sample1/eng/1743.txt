<p><i><b>Kamen Rider Blade</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">仮面ライダー剣（ブレイド）</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Bureido</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <i>Masked Rider ♠ (Blade)</i>)</span>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_media" title="Japanese media">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a> <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> series. It is the fourteenth installment in the <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Series" title="Kamen Rider Series">Kamen Rider Series</a>. It aired on <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">TV Asahi</a> from January 25, 2004 to January 23, 2005. It is a joint collaboration between <a href="/wiki/Ishimori_Productions" title="Ishimori Productions">Ishimori Productions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toei_Company" title="Toei Company">Toei</a>. Along with the standard insect motif of the Kamen Rider series, <i>Kamen Rider Blade</i> also uses a <a href="/wiki/Playing_card" title="Playing card">playing card</a> motif. Each Rider is assigned one of the <a href="/wiki/Suit_(cards)" title="Suit (cards)">suits</a> from a deck of cards.</p>
<p><i>Blade</i> was later released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> by Toei. There were twelve volumes released with the first eleven having four episodes per DVD and the twelfth volume with five episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Toei-DVD_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Toei-DVD-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Story">Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Blade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Blade_characters" title="List of Kamen Rider Blade characters">List of Kamen Rider Blade characters</a></div>
<p>Ten thousand years ago, a massive battle known as the Battle Royal was fought by fifty-two Undead, each representing a species fighting for dominance over all others. The winner was the Human Undead, known as Category Two of Hearts, giving dominion of the Earth to humanity. In the present day, archaeologists discover the sealed Undead, and accidentally set them free. Thus, a new Battle Royal begins.</p>
<p>Having developed the Rider System, based on the Joker's ability to copy sealed Undead, the organization BOARD (Board Of Archaeological Research Department) equips two young men, who become Kamen Riders: Kazuma Kenzaki and Sakuya Tachibana fight together as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Blade_characters#Kazuma_Kenzaki.2FKamen_Rider_Blade" title="List of Kamen Rider Blade characters">Kamen Rider Blade</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Blade_characters#Sakuya_Tachibana.2FKamen_Rider_Garren" title="List of Kamen Rider Blade characters">Kamen Rider Garren</a> to protect humans from Undead and seal them. Also fighting Undead is the mysterious <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Blade_characters#Hajime_Aikawa.2FKamen_Rider_Chalice" title="List of Kamen Rider Blade characters">Chalice</a>, a Kamen Rider whose purpose is unknown. In addition, a young man struggles to free himself from the control of the sealed Undead powering his own Undead-made Rider System, fighting as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Blade_characters#Mutsuki_Kamij.C5.8D.2FKamen_Rider_Leangle" title="List of Kamen Rider Blade characters">Kamen Rider Leangle</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Undead">The Undead</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Blade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Undead">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undead_(Kamen_Rider)" title="Undead (Kamen Rider)">Undead (Kamen Rider)</a></div>
<p>The Undead <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">アンデッド</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Andeddo</i>)</span> are the series monsters in <i>Kamen Rider Blade</i>. There are 52 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undead_(Kamen_Rider)" title="Undead (Kamen Rider)">Undead</a> organized into four suits with the <a href="/wiki/Joker_(playing_card)" title="Joker (playing card)">Joker</a>, making a common <a href="/wiki/Playing_card" title="Playing card">card deck</a>.</p>
<p>The Undead are also divided by Category, with the "Royal" Undead being of a much higher power level than the rest. The Categories Jack, Queen and King are all capable of disguising themselves as humans, and are capable of much higher forms of thought than other Undead, forming plans of depth rather than just blindly attacking everything nearby. The Category Ace Undead, whose cards power the Rider Systems, are also very powerful, but lack the form-shifting abilities of the high-class Undead, but are among the greatest warriors of the Undead. Finally, the Joker can assume the form of any sealed Undead it possesses. More Jokers can be created if a human abuses the Rider System. In the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Blade:_Missing_Ace" title="Kamen Rider Blade: Missing Ace">Missing Ace</a></i>, an additional Albino Joker is also present.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kamen_Rider_Blade:_Missing_Ace"><i>Kamen Rider Blade: Missing Ace</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Blade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Kamen Rider Blade: Missing Ace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Blade:_Missing_Ace" title="Kamen Rider Blade: Missing Ace">Kamen Rider Blade: Missing Ace</a></div>
<p><i>Kamen Rider Blade the Movie: Missing Ace</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">劇場版 仮面ライダー剣 MISSING ACE</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gekijōban Kamen Raidā Bureido Misshingu Ēsu</i>)</span> is the theatrical release, first in theaters on September 11, 2004.</p>
<p>Four years after event of the series, however, Blade forcing seals the Joker, the characters have moved on with their lives: Kenzaki is a garbage man, Mutsuki has graduated high school, and Kotarō has published a book about the Kamen Riders to great success, but on Amane's upcoming birthday, it was revealed that she has become a delinquent without the emotional support of Hajime. The Undead have been re-released, and a new trio of Kamen Riders—<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Glaive" title="Kamen Rider Glaive">Glaive</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Larc" title="Kamen Rider Larc">Larc</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Lance" title="Kamen Rider Lance">Lance</a>—have emerged from BOARD, now led by Tachibana. After recapturing two of the Category Aces, Kenzaki and Mutsuki join with the new Riders. When all the Undead are sealed once more, Glaive reveals himself to be the Albino Joker, a white version of the original Joker, and captures Kotarō's niece to obtain the ultimate power, sealing her in the Vanity Card. In order to free Amane from the card, Hajime is unsealed from the Joker Card and the four Riders are again united, intent on saving Amane and stopping the Albino Joker.</p>

General Black</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">3ごうライダーたい ブラックしょうぐんのまき</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sangō Raidā Tai Burakku Shōgun no Maki</i>)</span>. <a href="/wiki/Kousei_Amano" title="Kousei Amano">Kousei Amano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Takayuki_Tsubaki" title="Takayuki Tsubaki">Takayuki Tsubaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryoji_Morimoto" title="Ryoji Morimoto">Ryoji Morimoto</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takahiro_Hojo" title="Takahiro Hojo">Takahiro Hojo</a> reprise their roles in this film, which is scheduled to open in theaters on March 21, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specials">Specials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Blade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Specials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Kamen Rider Blade: Blade vs. Blade</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">仮面ライダー剣（ブレイド） ブレイドVSブレイド</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Bureido: Bureido Buiesu Bureido</i>)</span> is a Hyper Battle Video, wherein a Trial-series cyborg pretends to be Kenzaki and copies the Blay Rouzer, starting a fight with the real Kenzaki. During their battle, Tachibana and Mutsuki are unable to tell the two apart, which results in their humorously attacking the real Kenzaki (shooting him in the rear end and bashing him over the head), before Hajime steps in and reveals the impostor. As with all Hyper Battle Videos, this serves as a device to exposit the Rider's various powers and abilities.</p>
<p><i>Kamen Rider Blade: New Generation</i> is a television special made of a collection of <a href="/wiki/Vignette_(literature)" title="Vignette (literature)">vignettes</a> which aired following certain episodes of <i>Kamen Rider Blade</i>, following the history of Kamen Riders Glaive, L'arc, and Lance. This is a direct tie-in to the movie <i>Missing Ace</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Novel">Novel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Blade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Novel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><b>Kamen Rider Blade</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">仮面ライダー剣（ブレイド）</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Bureido</i>, <i>Masked Rider ♠ (Blade)</i>)</span>, written by Toshiki Inoue, is part of a series of spin-off novel adaptations of the Heisei Era Kamen Rider Series. This sequel to the TV series takes place 300 years following the television series finale. It chronicles Kenzaki's quest to end his life as the Navy Joker after three centuries. In one instance, Kenzaki is captured by a criminal organization and is tortured, but still does not die.</p>
<p>Later in the novel, he becomes a war photographer, all he sees is death. He then enlists in the army for a foreign country, steps on landmines and explodes. Also named are the new Garren and Leangle, Tachihara and Satsuki.</p>
<p>The only returning characters are Kenzaki and Hajime. Kenzaki has a weaker personality compared to the TV Series due to living for a long time and seeing a lot of horrible things. Satsuki, the new Leangle's treated the same like the original Leangle, Mutsuki.</p>
<p>The novel was released on February 28, 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game">Video game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Blade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Video game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A video game based on the series was produced by <a href="/wiki/Bandai" title="Bandai">Bandai</a> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a>. It is an action game that featured many of the characters from the TV series. It was released only in Japan near the end of the TV series on December 9, 2004.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcasts">International broadcasts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Blade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: International broadcasts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Continuing the Heisei Kamen Rider Series in order, after the success of <i>Ryuki</i> and <i>555</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <i>Blade</i> was shown on local TV station in GMA 7 and also on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network_Philippines" title="Cartoon Network Philippines">Cartoon Network Philippines</a>, as well as reruns on TV5. It also aired in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> as <i>Masked Rider Blade</i> (<span lang="ko">가면라이더 블레이드</span> <i>Gamyeon Raideo Bureideu</i>) in 2006 and run at NTV 7 in Malaysia in 2007 after the last episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_555" title="Kamen Rider 555">Kamen Rider 555</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
