<p><i><b>Kamen Rider Kiva</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーキバ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Kiba</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <i>Masked Rider Kiva</i>)</span> is the title of the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_series" title="Kamen Rider series">Kamen Rider</a> Japanese <a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a> television series produced by <a href="/wiki/Toei_Company" title="Toei Company">Toei Company</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ishimori_Productions" title="Ishimori Productions">Ishimori Productions</a>. It premiered on January 27, 2008, following the finale of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Den-O" title="Kamen Rider Den-O">Kamen Rider Den-O</a></i>. It aired as a part of <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">TV Asahi</a>'s 2008 <a href="/wiki/Super_Hero_Time" title="Super Hero Time">Super Hero Time</a> block with <i><a href="/wiki/Engine_Sentai_Go-onger" title="Engine Sentai Go-onger">Engine Sentai Go-onger</a></i>. Advertisements showed a <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> theme to the series, with the motif for Kamen Rider Kiva as a <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a>. The first episode began with a commemoration of the series in honor of the seventieth anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Shotaro_Ishinomori" title="Shotaro Ishinomori">Shotaro Ishinomori</a>'s birthday.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Kiva_characters" title="List of Kamen Rider Kiva characters">List of Kamen Rider Kiva characters</a></div>
<p>Twenty-two years after the disappearance of his father, Wataru Kurenai lives in an infamous "haunted house" where he is destined as Kamen Rider Kiva to fight life-draining monsters called Fangires, the very race his father fought years ago before his disappearance. Wataru must also deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Ixa" title="Kamen Rider Ixa">Kamen Rider Ixa</a> who is part of an organization seeking to destroy the Fangire menace, as well as the Fangires' own Rider, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Saga" title="Kamen Rider Saga">Kamen Rider Saga</a>. The story is split between the actions of Wataru in the present (2008–09) and his father Otoya in the past (1986–87), slowly revealing the link between the Fangire Race and Kiva.</p>
<p>The characters of <i>Kamen Rider Kiva</i> are spread throughout two time periods, each related to one another. There are the actions of Otoya Kurenai and the Fangire Hunter Yuri Aso with the Wolfen Jiro in the year 1986 with the first Ixa Ver.Ⅰ that result in the various Fangires that persist to the current day, in 2008, with Otoya's son Wataru (as Kamen Rider Kiva) along and Keisuke Nago (as Kamen Rider Ixa Ver.Ⅹ) and Yuri's daughter Megumi (also a Fangire Hunter), dealing with the Fangires to prevent deaths.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fangire">Fangire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fangire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fangire" title="Fangire">Fangire</a></div>
<p>The Fangires <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ファンガイア</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Fangaia</i>)</span> are <a href="/wiki/Stained_glass" title="Stained glass">stained glass</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampires</a> who feed off of the Life Energy <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ライフエナジー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Raifu Enajī</i>)</span> of humans to survive, with which they can disguise themselves as humans. Because of this, aware humans have formed a group to hunt the Fangires. Though Kiva usually captures a Fangire's soul to feed to Castle Doran, Fangires can be completely destroyed when shattered as seen by Ixa and stronger Fangires.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes">Episodes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Episodes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Kiva_episodes" title="List of Kamen Rider Kiva episodes">List of Kamen Rider Kiva episodes</a></div>
<p>Each episode's title is a word or phrase relating to music and a phrase describing the episode separated by a symbol from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_musical_symbols" title="Modern musical symbols">musical notation</a>. For example, the second episode's title is written in Japanese as "<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">組曲♪親子のバイオリン</span>", using the <a href="/wiki/Eighth_note" title="Eighth note">eighth note</a>. An exception is the finale, which uses the <a href="/wiki/Bar_(music)" title="Bar (music)">music end barline</a> at the end of the title. From episode 2 onward, Kivat begins each episode by stating a piece of trivia about music, art, chess, and other subjects. From episode 30 onward, Tatsulot joins the opening statement as he and Kivat provide recaps of the previous episode.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climax_Deka"><i>Climax Deka</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climax Deka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Den-O_%26_Kiva:_Climax_Deka" title="Kamen Rider Den-O &amp; Kiva: Climax Deka">Kamen Rider Den-O &amp; Kiva: Climax Deka</a></div>
<p>A movie titled <i>Kamen Rider Den-O &amp; Kiva the Movie: Climax Deka</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">劇場版 仮面ライダー電王&amp;キバ クライマックス刑事（デカ）</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gekijōban Kamen Raidā Den'ō Ando Kiba Kuraimakkusu Deka</i>)</span> opened in theaters on April 12, 2008. It features a meeting between the characters of <i>Kiva</i> and the characters of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Den-O" title="Kamen Rider Den-O">Kamen Rider Den-O</a></i>. They team up to fight a new evil Imagin who has teamed up with the Fangire Clan. Alongside <i>Climax Deka</i>, a short film titled <i>Momotaros's Let's Go Kiva!</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">モモタロスのキバっていくぜ！</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Momotarosu no Kibatte Ikuze!</i>)</span> will be shown as double feature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="King_of_the_Castle_in_the_Demon_World"><i>King of the Castle in the Demon World</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: King of the Castle in the Demon World">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Kiva:_King_of_the_Castle_in_the_Demon_World" title="Kamen Rider Kiva: King of the Castle in the Demon World">Kamen Rider Kiva: King of the Castle in the Demon World</a></div>
<p><i>Kamen Rider Kiva the Movie: King of the Castle in the Demon World</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">劇場版 仮面ライダーキバ 魔界城の王</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gekijōban Kamen Raidā Kiba Makaijō no Ō</i>)</span> opened in Japanese theaters on August 9, 2008. It featured two new Riders who have been shown in silhouettes in Japanese children's magazines, Kamen Rider Rey <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーレイ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Rei</i>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> who is a monster hunter named Takato Shiramine <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">白峰 天斗</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shiramine Takato</i>)</span> portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Shouma_Yamamoto" title="Shouma Yamamoto">Shouma Yamamoto</a>, partnered to Rey Kivat <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">レイキバット</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rei Kibatto</i>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Norio_Wakamoto" title="Norio Wakamoto">Norio Wakamoto</a>)</span> and the villain of the movie Takashi Sugimura <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">杉村 隆</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sugimura Takashi</i>)</span>, a death-row inmate who transforms into Kamen Rider Arc <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーアーク</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Āku</i>)</span> with Arc Kivat <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アークキバット</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Āku Kibatto</i>, voiced by Norio Wakamoto)</span>, portrayed by Ken Horiuchi of the comedy troupe <a href="/wiki/Neptune_(owarai)" title="Neptune (owarai)">Neptune</a>. The movie takes place in an alternate universe, as trying to place the story of the movie anywhere within the story of the series always leaves events out of place and would cancel out other events. Furthermore, Shouma Yamamoto portrays Takato Shiramine in the Kamen Rider Kiva movie, but he also portrays Taiga Nobori in the actual series.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Den-Liner.2C_Into_Space.21.22">"Den-Liner, Into Space!"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title='Edit section: "Den-Liner, Into Space!"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Kamen Rider Kiva &amp; Den-O: Den-Liner, Into Space!" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーキバ&amp;電王 デンライナー、宇宙へ！</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Kiba Ando Den'ō Denrainā, Uchū e!</i>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Planetarium" title="Planetarium">planetarium</a> show using the cast of <i>Kiva</i> and <i>Den-O</i> to teach children about the universe. It was being shown at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kagoshima,_Kagoshima" title="Kagoshima, Kagoshima">Kagoshima</a> Municipal Science Hall's planetarium between January 2 and March 30, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adventure_Battle_DVD">Adventure Battle DVD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Adventure Battle DVD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Hyper Battle DVD <i>Kamen Rider Kiva: You Can Also Be Kiva</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮面ライダーキバ キミもキバになろう</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamen Raidā Kiba Kimi mo Kiba ni Narō</i>)</span> Wataru Kurenai, Keisuke Nago, and Otoya Kurenai introduce themselves to a boy (the viewer) who has wandered into the Café mald'amour and offer to teach him how he can be like each of them. This DVD is referred to as an Adventure Battle DVD <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アドベンチャーバトルDVD</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Adobenchā Batoru Dī Bui Dī</i>)</span> and it takes on the form of a <a href="/wiki/Choose_Your_Own_Adventure" title="Choose Your Own Adventure">Choose Your Own Adventure</a> story. After having an "Ixa-cise" with Nago and a special lesson from Otoya, a Fangire attacks and the viewer can choose to transform Kiva into Garulu Form, Basshaa Form, Dogga Form, or the secret DoGaBaKi Emperor Form.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="King_of_Vampire"><i>King of Vampire</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamen_Rider_Kiva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: King of Vampire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For <i>Kiva</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Super_Imaginative_Chogokin" title="Super Imaginative Chogokin">S.I.C.</a> Hero Saga side story <i>Masked Rider Kiva: King of Vampire</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">MASKED RIDER KIVA -KING OF VAMPIRE-</span>)</span>, the story follows the life of the characters following the finale while expanding on other instances in the history of the 1986 storyline. The story is set to begin running in the January 2010 issue of <i>Monthly Hobby Japan</i> magazine. Like the series' episode titles, the titles of the first three chapters of the S.I.C. Hero Saga follow a similar format, but features two musically themed titles separated by an item from musical notation (the former is an opera while the latter is a song from said opera, the third names the composer and one of his songs). The first chapter uses the <a href="/wiki/Dal_Segno" title="Dal Segno">Segno</a>. The last chapter is a retelling of the final scene of the TV series, except instead of Masao and the Neo-Fangires, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kamen_Rider_Decade_characters#Kivala" title="List of Kamen Rider Decade characters">Kiva-la</a> comes to warn them about the Lion Fangire having turned into a giant Sabbat.</p>

<dl>
<dd>This arrangement of "Individual-System" is the first ending theme to be performed by someone other than TETRA-FANG. The single was released on July 30, 2008, with Keisuke Kato providing the vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shinseido_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shinseido-24">[24]</a></sup> This version was initially titled "Individual-System～Fight for justice～,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Toei_20_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Toei_20-23">[23]</a></sup> however the single has since been renamed "Fight for Justice" on the single "Fight for Justice ~Individual-System NAGO ver.~" The song was sampled for a preview in the "Individual-System NAGO advance fist" track on the "Individual-System" single. <li>Episodes: 34–36, 43</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>"Roots of the King" is the theme song for Kamen Rider Dark Kiva and his son Kamen Rider Saga. On September 13, 2008, Naruse announced on the Avex Movie blog for <i>Kiva</i> that the next single to be released by TETRA-FANG is to be titled "Roots of the KING."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He also refers to the song as "Roots of the King" on his personal blog,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> which has since become the title of the song.</dd>
</dl>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Avex's blog for <i>Kiva</i> mentions a song entitled "Bite There Soul" that was written by Naruse and YUJI as a heavy metal song played by street musicians in episode 9 which was planned to be released at a future date,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and was subsequently put on the final CD box set as "Bite Their Soul"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> set for release on January 21, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> A mini-album released on August 6, 2008, titled <i>SUPERNOVA</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> features the theme songs for Kiva's four additional forms performed by TETRA-FANG. The tracks include "Innocent Trap,",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Toei_20_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-Toei_20-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shinseido_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shinseido-24">[24]</a></sup> "Shout in the Moonlight," and "Supernova," which has a music video included on a special DVD edition of <i>SUPERNOVA</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> An unfeatured song from <i>SUPERNOVA</i> titled "Silent Shout" is Dogga Form's theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Another track from the album "Message" is a tribute song for the character Otoya Kurenai.</p>
<p>Kouhei Takeda has recorded a song titled "This love never ends" as the theme song for Otoya as Ixa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kiva_39_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kiva_39-33">[33]</a></sup> Nana Yanagisawa and Yu Takahashi have also provided their voices for tracks to be included on an album for all characters who have become Kamen Rider Ixa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> This song has since been revealed to be titled "Feel the same" on an album titled <i>Inherited-System</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kiva_40_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kiva_40-35">[35]</a></sup> Other songs on this album include "Don't Lose Yourself" performed by Keisuke Kato as Keisuke Nago and "Inherited-System" performed by the Wonderful Blue Sky Organization <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">素晴らしき青空の会</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Subarashiki Aozora no Kai</i>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Kenji Matsuda as Jiro performs the song "Keep alive" for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> For a TETRA-FANG album titled <i>DESTINY</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kiva_40_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kiva_40-35">[35]</a></sup> Koji Seto has recorded the vocals for a song titled "Mind garden."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kiva_39_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kiva_39-33">[33]</a></sup> Several songs from this album are theme songs for the members of the Checkmate Four group: "Lightning to Heaven" for the Rook, "Exterminate Time" for the King (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Dark_Kiva" title="Kamen Rider Dark Kiva">Kamen Rider Dark Kiva</a>), "Eternity Blood" for the Bishop, and "Rainy Rose" for the Queen (Maya). Also on the album is "Prayer~Message 2", another tribute to Otoya.</p>
<p>An album titled <i>Masked Rider Kiva Re-Union</i> was released on June 24, 2009, with some original <i>Kiva</i> songs to be rearranged. "Destiny's Play" will be rearranged, Koji Seto &amp; Shouma Yamamoto will sing "Roots of the King" together, Koji Seto shall give his own rendition of "This love never ends", and in turn Kouhei Takeda will give his own rendition of "Supernova".</p>
