<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gamō Ujisato</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="hatnote" role="note">In this <a href="/wiki/Japanese_name" title="Japanese name">Japanese name</a>, the family name is Gamō.</div>
<p><b>Gamō Ujisato</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">蒲生 氏郷</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 1556 – March 17, 1595)</span> was a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">daimyo</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azuchi-Momoyama_period" title="Azuchi-Momoyama period">Azuchi-Momoyama periods</a>. The heir and son of <a href="/wiki/Gam%C5%8D_Katahide" title="Gamō Katahide">Gamō Katahide</a>, lord of Hino Castle in <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmi_Province" title="Ōmi Province">Ōmi Province</a>, he later held Matsusaka (<a href="/wiki/Ise_Province" title="Ise Province">Ise Province</a>) and finally <a href="/wiki/Aizuwakamatsu_Castle" title="Aizuwakamatsu Castle">Aizuwakamatsu Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a>. He also controlled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Obama_Castle_(Mutsu)" title="Obama Castle (Mutsu)">Obama Castle</a> through one of his retainers, Gamō Chūzaemon.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gam%C5%8D_Ujisato&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ujisato, known in his childhood as <b>Tsuruchiyo</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鶴千代</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was born in Hino, in the Gamō district of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmi_Province" title="Ōmi Province">Ōmi Province</a> in 1556. In 1568, <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a>, who was en route to Kyoto, defeated the <a href="/wiki/Rokkaku_clan" title="Rokkaku clan">Rokkaku clan</a>, who were the masters of Tsuruchiyo's father, Katahide <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">賢秀</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. Upon the Rokkaku clan's defeat, Katahide as a former influential vassal, pledged loyalty to Nobunaga, and became an Oda retainer. However, the price of Katahide's pledge was giving up his son as a hostage, and so Tsuruchiyo was taken to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gifu,_Gifu" title="Gifu, Gifu">Gifu</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/Oda_clan" title="Oda clan">Oda clan</a>'s headquarters.</p>
<p>Tsuruchiyo's sagacity impressed Nobunaga, and soon, the young man had his manhood rite in Gifu, taking the name Utahide <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">賦秀</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yoropara_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-yoropara-1">[1]</a></sup> In the summer of 1569, Utahide took part in his first campaign, during Nobunaga's subjucation of Kitabatake Tomomasa of Kizukuri Castle. For his distinction in battle, Nobunaga gave his daughter <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fuyuhime&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fuyuhime (page does not exist)">Fuyuhime</a> in marriage to Utahide, affectionately referring to Utahide as "my little son-in-law."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yoropara_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-yoropara-1">[1]</a></sup> At the same time, he was allowed to return to his father's castle at Hino. Though Fuyuhime was still young at this point, she is said to have later matured into a stunningly beautiful woman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yoropara_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-yoropara-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_campaigns_under_Nobunaga">Major campaigns under Nobunaga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gam%C5%8D_Ujisato&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Major campaigns under Nobunaga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1570, Utahide fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Anegawa" title="Battle of Anegawa">Battle of Anegawa</a>, and later that same year, joined his father in the Oda assault on the <a href="/wiki/Asakura_clan" title="Asakura clan">Asakura clan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Echizen_Province" title="Echizen Province">Echizen Province</a>. The two led a force of 1,000 men as the vanguard of <a href="/wiki/Shibata_Katsuie" title="Shibata Katsuie">Shibata Katsuie</a>'s army. The total number of men under Katsuie's overall command at that battle totaled 5,000. Of those 5,000, the number under Katsuie's command totaled 600, so this may give some impression of the importance of the Gamō family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yoropara_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-yoropara-1">[1]</a></sup> Following the betrayal of <a href="/wiki/Azai_Nagamasa" title="Azai Nagamasa">Azai Nagamasa</a>, Utahide assisted in Nobunaga's withdrawal by taking him into his own Hino Castle, and facilitating his escape to Gifu from there. In recognition of this feat, Nobunaga gave Utahide and his father a stipend increase, and posted them to southern Omi, under the command of <a href="/wiki/Shibata_Katsuie" title="Shibata Katsuie">Shibata Katsuie</a>. The Gamō would see action against the Asakura once more, in 1573. In 1575, upon Katsuie's posting to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kitanosh%C5%8D_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kitanoshō Castle (page does not exist)">Kitanoshō Castle</a>, the Gamō, ruling from their castle at Hino, came under Nobunaga's direct command, serving as <a href="/wiki/Hatamoto" title="Hatamoto">hatamoto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yoropara_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-yoropara-1">[1]</a></sup> That year, Utahide fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nagashino" title="Battle of Nagashino">Battle of Nagashino</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nobunaga.27s_assassination_and_Hideyoshi.27s_rise">Nobunaga's assassination and Hideyoshi's rise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gam%C5%8D_Ujisato&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nobunaga's assassination and Hideyoshi's rise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Nobunaga was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incident_at_Honn%C5%8D-ji" title="Incident at Honnō-ji">assassinated</a> in 1582, Utahide was with his father, who had been posted as warden of <a href="/wiki/Azuchi_Castle" title="Azuchi Castle">Azuchi Castle</a>. Together, the two sheltered Nobunaga's wife and children in Hino Castle, saving their lives. In the same year, Utahide submitted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hashiba_Hideyoshi" title="Hashiba Hideyoshi">Hashiba Hideyoshi</a>. The following year, he joined Hideyoshi's attack on <a href="/wiki/Takigawa_Kazumasu" title="Takigawa Kazumasu">Takigawa Kazumasu</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shizugatake" title="Battle of Shizugatake">Battle of Shizugatake</a>, and received the title of <i>Hida no Kami</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">飛騨守</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. In this year, his son Tsuruchiyo (<a href="/wiki/Gam%C5%8D_Hideyuki" title="Gamō Hideyuki">Gamō Hideyuki</a>) was born.</p>
<p>Following his siege of <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobukatsu" title="Oda Nobukatsu">Oda Nobukatsu</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Komaki_and_Nagakute" title="Battle of Komaki and Nagakute">Battle of Komaki and Nagakute</a> in 1585, he received Matsusaka, in the southern section of <a href="/wiki/Ise_Province" title="Ise Province">Ise Province</a>, as his fief (rated at 120,000 <i><a href="/wiki/Koku" title="Koku">koku</a></i>). (The main castle of this fief was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Matsugashima_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Matsugashima Castle (page does not exist)">Matsugashima Castle</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">松ヶ島城</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yoropara_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-yoropara-1">[1]</a></sup>). After taking part in the subjugation of <a href="/wiki/Kii_Province" title="Kii Province">Kii Province</a> in 1585, Utahide took the name of "Ujisato." Soon after, due to the influence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takayama_Ukon" title="Takayama Ukon">Takayama Ukon</a>, he received a Christian baptism in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, and took the baptismal name of Leo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_under_Hideyoshi.2C_and_later_life">Career under Hideyoshi, and later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gam%C5%8D_Ujisato&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Career under Hideyoshi, and later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1588, construction was completed on <a href="/wiki/Matsusaka_Castle" title="Matsusaka Castle">Matsusaka Castle</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">松坂城</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, where he immediately moved. Ujisato took part in all of Hideyoshi's subsequent campaigns: the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Odawara_(1590)" title="Siege of Odawara (1590)">Siege of Odawara (1590)</a>, the pacification of Ōshū (<a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewa_Province" title="Dewa Province">Dewa Provinces</a>) (1590), and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_invasions_of_Korea_(1592%E2%80%931598)" title="Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–1598)">Japanese invasions of Korea</a>. For his role in the pacification of Ōshū, he received a 420,000 <i><a href="/wiki/Koku" title="Koku">koku</a></i> fief with its headquarters at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurokawa_Castle" title="Kurokawa Castle">Kurokawa Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Aizu" title="Aizu">Aizu</a>. He renamed the castle Wakamatsu, the name which even the town retains to this day.</p>
<p>In preparation for the Japanese invasions of Korea, Ujisato proceeded in 1592 to Hideyoshi's base in <a href="/wiki/Nagoya,_Saga" title="Nagoya, Saga">Nagoya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hizen_Province" title="Hizen Province">Hizen Province</a>. He fell ill there, coughing up blood in early 1593. From Nagoya, he headed first to Aizu, and then to Fushimi, where the Gamō family's mansion was almost complete. Hideyoshi himself would visit the mansion twice after its completion<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yoropara_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-yoropara-1">[1]</a></sup> Ujisato died at age 40, at <a href="/wiki/Fushimi_Castle" title="Fushimi Castle">Fushimi Castle</a>. Though his family would lose Aizu soon after with <a href="/wiki/Gam%C5%8D_Hideyuki" title="Gamō Hideyuki">Hideyuki</a>'s transfer to Utsunomiya, the Gamō would later be returned to Aizu by <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gam%C5%8D_Ujisato&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
