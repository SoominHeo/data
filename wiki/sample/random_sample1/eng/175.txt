<p>The <b>1908 World Series</b> matched the defending champion <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a> in a rematch of the <a href="/wiki/1907_World_Series" title="1907 World Series">1907 Series</a>. In this first-ever rematch of this young event, the Cubs won in five games for their second consecutive World Series title.</p>
<p>The 1908 World Series was significant for being the last World Series championship the Cubs would win until the <a href="/wiki/2016_World_Series" title="2016 World Series">2016</a> World Series, 108 years later. Before the 2016 series, the team would go on to appear in seven World Series; in <a href="/wiki/1910_World_Series" title="1910 World Series">1910</a>, <a href="/wiki/1918_World_Series" title="1918 World Series">1918</a>, <a href="/wiki/1929_World_Series" title="1929 World Series">1929</a>, <a href="/wiki/1932_World_Series" title="1932 World Series">1932</a>, <a href="/wiki/1935_World_Series" title="1935 World Series">1935</a>, <a href="/wiki/1938_World_Series" title="1938 World Series">1938</a>, and <a href="/wiki/1945_World_Series" title="1945 World Series">1945</a>, losing each time. The Cubs had been one of baseball's most dominant teams in the early 1900s. This was the year of the infamous "<a href="/wiki/Merkle%27s_Boner" title="Merkle's Boner">Merkle's Boner</a>" play that allowed the Chicago Cubs to reach the <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> after beating the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Giants_(NL)" title="New York Giants (NL)">New York Giants</a> (now the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>) in a one-game "playoff", actually the makeup game for the tie that the Merkle play had caused.</p>
<p>The Series was anti-climactic after tight pennant races in both leagues. <a href="/wiki/Ty_Cobb" title="Ty Cobb">Ty Cobb</a> had a much better World Series than in the previous year, as did the rest of his team. The final two games, held in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, were shutouts. This was also the most poorly attended World Series in history, with the final game drawing a record-low 6,210 fans. Attendance in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> was harmed by a ticket-scalping scheme that fans accused the club's owner of participating in, and the World Series was boycotted to some degree.</p>
<p>For the first time, four umpires were used in the series, in alternating two-man teams.</p>

<p>It was in this game that Ty Cobb enjoyed the finest World Series outing he ever had. The 21-year-old Georgian rapped three singles and a double in five at-bats, and stole two bases. In the top of the ninth, he singled and promptly stole second and third, but then the hyped-up boy wonder pressed his luck and was thrown out trying to steal home. This was the only Tiger win in their back-to-back first two World Series losses to the Cubs.</p>

<p>The attendance during this last game of the 1908 World Series (6,210) was the smallest crowd in Series history.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Boss_Schmidt" title="Boss Schmidt">Boss Schmidt</a>, who made the last out of the 1907 Series with a popup to short, also made the last out of this Series with a feeble catcher-to-first groundout.</p>
<p>This was also the first World Series game in which neither team committed an error.</p>
<p>The Cubs would not win another World Series title until finally reclaiming the crown in 2016.</p>
