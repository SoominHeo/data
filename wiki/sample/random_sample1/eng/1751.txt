<p>The <b>Jezo spruce</b> or <b>Yezo spruce</b> (<i><b>Picea jezoensis</b></i> or <i><b>Picea yezoensis</b></i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Evergreen" title="Evergreen">evergreen</a> <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">tree</a> growing to 30–50 m tall and with a trunk diameter of up to 2 m. It is native to northeast <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, from the mountains of central <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Changbai_Mountains" title="Changbai Mountains">Changbai Mountains</a> on the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>-<a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> border, north to eastern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Sikhote-Alin" title="Sikhote-Alin">Sikhote-Alin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuril_Islands" title="Kuril Islands">Kuril Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin" title="Sakhalin">Sakhalin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Peninsula" title="Kamchatka Peninsula">Kamchatka</a>. It is found in cold but humid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate_rain_forests" title="Temperate rain forests">temperate rain forests</a>, and nowhere does its range extend more than 400 km from the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bark" title="Bark">bark</a> is thin and scaly, becoming fissured in old trees. The crown is broad conic. The shoots are pale buff-brown, glabrous (hairless) but with prominent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pulvini" title="Pulvini">pulvini</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> are needle-like, 15–20 mm long, 2 mm broad, flattened in cross-section, dark green above with no <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stomata" title="Stomata">stomata</a>, and blue-white to white below with two dense bands of stomata.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Conifer_cone" title="Conifer cone">cones</a> are pendulous, slender cylindrical, 4–7 cm long and 2 cm broad when closed, opening to 3 cm broad. They have thin, flexible scales 12–18 mm long. They are green or reddish, maturing pale brown 5–6 months after pollination. The <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a> are black, 3 mm long, with a slender, 6–8 mm long pale brown wing.</p>
<p>There are two geographical <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>, treated as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Variety_(biology)" title="Variety (biology)">varieties</a> by some authors, and as distinct species by others:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Picea jezoensis</i> subsp. <i>jezoensis</i> (<b>Jezo spruce</b>). All of the range except as below, south to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hokkaid%C5%8D" title="Hokkaidō">Hokkaidō</a>, Japan. Shoots very pale buff-brown, almost white; stomatal bands blue-white; cones pale brown with flexible scales.</li>
<li><i>Picea jezoensis</i> subsp. <i>hondoensis</i> (Mayr) P. A. Schmidt (<b>Hondo spruce</b>). An isolated southern population on high mountains in central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honsh%C5%AB" title="Honshū">Honshū</a>, Japan. Shoots buff-brown to orange-brown, less often very pale; stomatal bands bright white; cones orange-brown with stiffer scales.</li>
</ul>
<p>Jezo spruce is very closely related to Sitka spruce (<i><a href="/wiki/Picea_sitchensis" title="Picea sitchensis">Picea sitchensis</a></i>), which replaces it on the opposite side of the north Pacific. They, particularly subsp. <i>jezoensis</i>, can be difficult to distinguish, with the absence of stomata on the upper surface of the leaves of <i>P. jezoensis</i> being the best feature. Its leaves are also somewhat blunter, less sharply spine-tipped, than Sitka Spruce.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Picea_jezoensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jezo spruce is important in the Russian Far East and northern Japan, for timber and paper production. Much of what is cut is harvested unsustainably (and often <a href="/wiki/Illegal_logging" title="Illegal logging">illegally</a>) from pristine natural <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>.</p>
<p>It is also occasionally planted as an ornamental tree in large gardens.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ainu_people" title="Ainu people">Ainu</a> string instrument called <i><a href="/wiki/Tonkori" title="Tonkori">tonkori</a></i> has a body made from Jezo Spruce.</p>
