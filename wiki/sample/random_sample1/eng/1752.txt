<p><b>Kami</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">加美郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kami-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Miyagi_Prefecture" title="Miyagi Prefecture">Miyagi Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2010, the district has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 33,240 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 58,3 persons per km². The total area is 570.05 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kami_District,_Miyagi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kami District is first recorded in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nara_Period" title="Nara Period">Nara Period</a> as 賀美郡. During the Edo era it was respelled 加美郡</p>
<ul>
<li>1871 Kami District incorporated into Sendai Prefecture (now <a href="/wiki/Miyagi_Prefecture" title="Miyagi Prefecture">Miyagi Prefecture</a>)</li>
<li>1889 With the creation of the modern municipal system, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakaniida,_Miyagi" title="Nakaniida, Miyagi">Nakaniida</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">Town</a> (中新田町), Hirohara <a href="/wiki/List_of_villages_in_Japan" title="List of villages in Japan">Village</a> (広原村), Naruse Village (鳴瀬村), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Onoda,_Miyagi" title="Onoda, Miyagi">Onoda</a> Village (小野田村), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyagi" title="Miyazaki, Miyagi">Miyazaki</a> Village (宮崎村), Kamiishi Village (賀美石村), and Shikama Village (色麻村) are established</li>
<li>1894 District offices established in Nakaniida Town</li>
<li>1926 District offices abolished</li>
<li>1943 Onoda Village becomes a town</li>
<li>1954 Nakaniida Town, Hirohara Village, Naruse Village merge to form new Nakaniida Town</li>
<li>1954 Kamiishi Village, Miyazaki Village merge to form Miyazaki Town</li>
<li>1977 Shikama Village becomes a town</li>
<li>2003 Nakaniida Town, Onoda Town and Miyazaki Town merge to form Kami Town (加美町)</li>
</ul>
