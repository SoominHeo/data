<p><b>Kamisato</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">上里町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamisato-machi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kodama_District,_Saitama" title="Kodama District, Saitama">Kodama District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, in the central <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 February 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kamisato,_Saitama&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 30,434 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 1040 persons per km². Its total area was 29.18 square kilometres (11.27 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamisato,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamisato is located on the extreme northwestern border of Saitama Prefecture, separated from Gunma Prefecture by the Kanna River, and isolated from the remainder of Saitama by the Chichibu Mountains.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamisato,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamisato village was created on May 3, 1954 by the merger of the villages of Jimbohara, Kami, Shichihongi and Nagahata, all from within <a href="/wiki/Kodama_District,_Saitama" title="Kodama District, Saitama">Kodama District</a>. Kamisato was elevated to town status on November 3, 1971.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamisato,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamisato has a large industrial park in the southeastern corner; however, many people commute to neighboring Takasaki or Fujioka.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamisato,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamisato has five elementary schools and two middle schools.
