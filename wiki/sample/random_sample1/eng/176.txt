<p>The <b>Class A (open class)</b> was one of three <a href="/wiki/Motorboat" title="Motorboat">motorboating</a> classes contested on the <a href="/wiki/Water_motorsports_at_the_1908_Summer_Olympics" title="Water motorsports at the 1908 Summer Olympics">Water motorsports at the 1908 Summer Olympics</a> programme.</p>
<p>The open class was scheduled to take place on the first day of competition, 28 August. The race was a 40 nautical miles long. Two boats, <i>Wolseley-Siddely</i> and <i>Dylan</i>, began the race. <i>Dylan</i> abandoned the race partway through the first lap, with <i>Wolseley-Siddely</i> finishing the first before the weather became too severe to continue the race.</p>
<p>A second attempt to run the event took place the next day, after the other two races had been completed. <i>Wolseley-Siddely</i> again started, this time against <i>Camille</i> (the only French boat to take part in competition). <i>Wolseley-Siddely</i> ran aground on a mud spit, leaving <i>Camille</i> to finish alone for the gold medal.</p>
