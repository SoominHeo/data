<p><b>Gabriel Imuetinyan Agbonlahor</b> (born 13 October 1986) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.</p>
<p>He is a product of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Aston Villa F.C. Reserves and Academy">Aston Villa's Academy</a>, and made his first-team debut in 2006 following loans to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">Football League</a> clubs <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a>. Since then, he has made over 300 <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> appearances for the club. With 73 goals, he is Aston Villa's all-time leading Premier League goalscorer, having scored in 11 consecutive league seasons. Formerly a regular with the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21s</a>, Agbonlahor made his senior international debut for <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> in 2008 and has earned three caps.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9306_season">2005–06 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2005–06 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agbonlahor came through the youth ranks at local club <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>. He signed a two-year professional contract at the club in June 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watford_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-watford-3">[3]</a></sup> On 22 September 2005, he was loaned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watford_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-watford-3">[3]</a></sup> where he made his senior debut six days later, replacing <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Benjamin" title="Trevor Benjamin">Trevor Benjamin</a> at half time in a 1–3 defeat away to <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He made his first start in his only other match for Watford, a goalless draw against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> at <a href="/wiki/Vicarage_Road" title="Vicarage Road">Vicarage Road</a> on 1 October, in which he made way for <a href="/wiki/Francino_Francis" title="Francino Francis">Francino Francis</a> after 65 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Agbonlahor was loaned to another team from the division on 28 October, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> where he made eight appearances without scoring. Agbonlahor's <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut for Villa came on 18 March 2006 at the age of 19, under manager <a href="/wiki/David_O%27Leary" title="David O'Leary">David O'Leary</a>, against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> at <a href="/wiki/Goodison_Park" title="Goodison Park">Goodison Park</a>. O'Leary was forced to delve into the youth academy because of a lack of senior strikers due to injury, thus earning Agbonlahor a rare start. He scored after 63 minutes, although Villa went on to lose the match 4–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9307_season">2006–07 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–07 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In five pre-season games of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Aston_Villa_F.C._season" title="2006–07 Aston Villa F.C. season">2006–07 season</a>, Agbonlahor netted three goals for Aston Villa; a quick double salvo against <a href="/wiki/Walsall_F.C." title="Walsall F.C.">Walsall</a> (the first a near-post header, the latter a close range finish) in a 5–0 victory,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and a consolation strike in a 2–1 defeat at <a href="/wiki/N.E.C._(football_club)" title="N.E.C. (football club)">NEC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor started the season on the right wing in <a href="/wiki/Martin_O%27Neill" title="Martin O'Neill">Martin O'Neill</a>'s new-look Villa side, and would be part of the team that managed to get a point from the first Premiership game at <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>'s new <a href="/wiki/Emirates_Stadium" title="Emirates Stadium">Emirates Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Agbonlahor's first goal of the season came at home against <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a> in a 2–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 30 September, Agbonlahor scored a crucial equaliser against Premiership champions <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> just before half-time, heading in <a href="/wiki/Liam_Ridgewell" title="Liam Ridgewell">Liam Ridgewell</a>'s cross with a glancing header to earn Villa a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor also provided cup heroics in 2006, scoring an injury-time winner to beat <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> 3–2 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walkers_Stadium" title="Walkers Stadium">Walkers Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The following game, he scored a consolation effort in Villa's first loss of the season, on 28 October, ten games in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor completed his run of scoring against the so-called "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Premier_league#.22Big_Four.22_dominance" title="Premier league">Big Four</a>", when he scored against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>. Aston Villa were beaten 3–1 in the game at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Agbonlahor then scored deep into injury time in the following game, against Watford on 20 January 2007, to end a Villa winless run that stretched back to 11 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor had played every minute of Aston Villa's campaign in 2006–07, until he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Patrik_Berger" title="Patrik Berger">Patrik Berger</a> in the 2–0 away fixture against <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> on 10 February 2007. The following match was the first of the season that Agbonlahor did not start—he instead appeared from the bench, replacing new Villa signing <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Maloney" title="Shaun Maloney">Shaun Maloney</a>. Manager Martin O'Neill suggested that Agbonlahor, who had been playing out of position on the right wing for much of the season, had done superbly, and being dropped from the first 11 was in order to rest him, and alleviate growing crowd pressure. Following this break, Agbonlahor went on a scoring spree. He scored from the right-wing against Everton on 2 April to gain Villa a valuable point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Following this match, Agbonlahor signed a new-four-year contract at Villa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The following Saturday, in the absence of <a href="/wiki/John_Carew" title="John Carew">John Carew</a>, he was moved back to his natural position at centre forward and again found his name on the scoresheet. Agbonlahor made it three in a row on <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a>, again scoring an equaliser, adjudged to have crossed the line by assistant referee Dave Richardson.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9308_season">2007–08 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2007–08 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agbonlahor also scored the vital second goal in the match between Aston Villa and Chelsea on 2 September, in which Villa won 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Agbonlahor scored the winning goal in the 86th minute of the <a href="/wiki/Second_City_derby" title="Second City derby">Second City derby</a>, 60 seconds after saving the ball on the line from a Liam Ridgewell shot. After 13 matches, Agbonlahor was the club's top goal scorer, with six Premier League goals. Agbonlahor won the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_Player_of_the_Month" title="Premier League Player of the Month">Premier League Player of the Month</a> award for November and manager Martin O'Neill won <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_Manager_of_the_Month" title="Premier League Manager of the Month">the equivalent award</a> for the same month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 April 2008, Agbonlahor scored the fourth goal against <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> in a 6–0 victory at <a href="/wiki/Pride_Park_Stadium" title="Pride Park Stadium">Pride Park Stadium</a> in the Premier League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Second_City_derby" title="Second City derby">Second City derby</a> against <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> on 20 April 2008, he scored the fifth and final goal in the 5–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.9309_season">2008–09 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2008–09 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 15 August 2008, Agbonlahor signed a new four-year contract with Villa, tying him to the club until 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following his contract extension, on 17 August 2008, Agbonlahor scored a "perfect" <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick#Association_football" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> (scoring goals with his head, right foot and left foot) against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> in Villa's opening game of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Premier_League" title="2008–09 Premier League">2008–09 season</a> at Villa Park. The three goals were netted in the space of seven minutes, making it the third-fastest hat-trick in the history of the Premier League after <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Fowler" title="Robbie Fowler">Robbie Fowler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sadio_Man%C3%A9" title="Sadio Mané">Sadio Mané</a>. He was later named <a href="/wiki/Man_of_the_match" title="Man of the match">man of the match</a> for this performance, which led to a 4–2 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Early in the season, he began to form a successful strike partnership with teammate John Carew, this demonstrated in the Premier League away games against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> where both players scored in both games and provided assists. On 3 November 2008, Agbonlahor was at the centre of a controversy with <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Joey_Barton" title="Joey Barton">Joey Barton</a> when, during the match, Barton appeared to brush his fingers on Agbonlahor's face. It later emerged that Barton's attack might have been race-orientated but, after careful consideration by both Aston Villa and <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a>, no further action was taken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On 8 November, it emerged in some national newspapers that the reason Agbonlahor decided not to pursue legal action against Barton was because he "felt sorry" for him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On 15 November, he scored once and played a part in the other goal, when Aston Villa beat Arsenal 2–0 at Emirates Stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He scored a brace against <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> on 13 December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Agbonlahor notched his tenth league goal of the season in the 90th minute in a 2–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> on 7 February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, this was a high point in a dismal run of form in which he scored just one goal in 12 league games. Some fans began to get frustrated with Agbonlahor, and in Villa's home defeat against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>, there were some sarcastic cheers as he was substituted off by Martin O'Neill. He was subsequently dropped to the bench for Villa's next game away to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, which Villa lost 5–0. After the heavy defeat to Liverpool and despite his lack of form, Agbonlahor was recalled to the starting 11 and went on to score a goal against Manchester United on 5 April in a 3–2 defeat for Villa. Agbonlahor was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a>, with teammate ultimately <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Young" title="Ashley Young">Ashley Young</a> winning the honour.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310_season">2009–10 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2009–10 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agbonlahor's first goal of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009-10_Premier_League" title="2009-10 Premier League">2009–10 season</a> came in a 2–0 home victory over <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> on 30 August 2009. The striker brought the ball from the centre of the field to strike, left-footed, past goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Mark_Schwarzer" title="Mark Schwarzer">Mark Schwarzer</a> from around 20 yards out.</p>
<p>The striker scored the only goal in the season's opening Birmingham derby against Birmingham City, netting an open header past <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hart" title="Joe Hart">Joe Hart</a> as a result of a free kick taken by Ashley Young. Agbonlahor scored his third goal of the season scoring in a 2–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He scored his fourth and fifth consecutive goals of the season against <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Football_League_Cup" title="2009–10 Football League Cup">League Cup</a> and Blackburn in the Premier League. His next goal came on 24 October 2009 against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Molineux_Stadium" title="Molineux Stadium">Molineux</a> in the Premier League. Fellow Villa forward <a href="/wiki/Emile_Heskey" title="Emile Heskey">Emile Heskey</a> played the ball into the penalty area where Agbonlahor turned a defender and placed a low shot past Wolves goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Hennessey" title="Wayne Hennessey">Wayne Hennessey</a>. The game finished 1–1.</p>
<p>He scored the only goal in the 1–0 win against Manchester United at Old Trafford, heading in a cross from Ashley Young after 21 minutes, providing Villa's first league win at Old Trafford since 1983. Agbonlahor also scored in the second leg of the League Cup semi-final against Blackburn, helping Villa to get to the <a href="/wiki/2010_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2010 Football League Cup Final">final</a> where they lost to Manchester United, 2–1. He scored twice in 2–0 win at Fulham on 30 January 2010. On 21 February 2010, Agbonlahor provided an assist for Emile Heskey before adding Villa's final goal in a 5–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> for his 11th league goal of the season.</p>
<p>Agbonlahor finished the season with 13 goals in the Premier League, and 16 in all competitions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311_season">2010–11 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010–11 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite Villa suffering some issues on and off the field at the beginning of the season, such as the resignation of manager Martin O'Neill and a humiliating 6–0 defeat at the hands of Newcastle United, Agbonlahor defended the club and stated his intent to remain there for the long term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skysports.com_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-skysports.com-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor signed a new four-and-a-half-year contract on 12 November 2010, keeping him at Villa Park until at least 2015. On 16 January 2011, he made his 200th appearance in an Aston Villa shirt in a 1–1 draw with rivals Birmingham, playing the full 90 minutes at <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_(stadium)" title="St Andrew's (stadium)">St Andrew's</a>. Agbonlahor scored his first goal in the Premier League since April 2010 in the 2–1 victory over Wigan Athletic on 26 January 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/DW_Stadium" title="DW Stadium">DW Stadium</a>. On 16 April 2011, he scored an injury-time winner away at <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor was less prolific in terms of goalscoring during 2010–11 due to Aston Villa manager <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Houllier" title="Gérard Houllier">Gérard Houllier</a> preferring to use him as a winger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312_season">2011–12 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2011–12 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agbonlahor scored his first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Premier_League" title="2011–12 Premier League">2011–12 season</a> in Aston Villa's 3–1 home win over Blackburn Rovers on 20 August 2011, and followed this up with the leveller in a 2–2 away draw against Everton ten days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> For his positive performances during the opening month of the season, Agbonlahor was named as Aston Villa's Player of the Month for August 2011, beating off competition from <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Delph" title="Fabian Delph">Fabian Delph</a> to the top spot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> He then made it three goals in three games when he scored in a 1–1 home draw against Newcastle on 17 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> This meant that Agbonlahor had equalled his Premier League goals tally from 2010–11 after just five games into the new season.</p>
<p>After rediscovering his goalscoring form under new boss <a href="/wiki/Alex_McLeish" title="Alex McLeish">Alex McLeish</a>, the striker publicly spoke of his discontent at the managerial tenure of Gérard Houllier and his assistant <a href="/wiki/Gary_McAllister" title="Gary McAllister">Gary McAllister</a> and said that he even considered quitting the club had the previous regime remained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Meanwhile, on the pitch Agbonlahor continued to impress, scoring the first goal and setting up <a href="/wiki/Darren_Bent" title="Darren Bent">Darren Bent</a> for the second in a man of the match performance against Wigan on 1 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> He then put in another man of the match performance against <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> in a 3–2 win on 5 November, setting up both of Darren Bent's goals and scoring the second himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> On 7 January 2012, he scored the second goal in a 3–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers_F.C." title="Bristol Rovers F.C.">Bristol Rovers</a> in the third round of the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> after <a href="/wiki/Marc_Albrighton" title="Marc Albrighton">Marc Albrighton</a> had given Villa the lead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> On 3 April 2012, after Aston Villa captain <a href="/wiki/Stiliyan_Petrov" title="Stiliyan Petrov">Stiliyan Petrov</a>'s diagnosis with acute leukaemia, Agbonlahor was named team captain upon Petrov's diagnosis.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313_season">2012–13 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2012–13 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the sacking of Alex McLeish in the summer of 2012, Agbonlahor was not chosen as interim captain by McLeish's successor, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Lambert" title="Paul Lambert">Paul Lambert</a>. The Scotsman opted firstly for Darren Bent as skipper, before ultimately choosing new signing <a href="/wiki/Ron_Vlaar" title="Ron Vlaar">Ron Vlaar</a> to wear the captain's armband in the absence of Stiliyan Petrov.</p>
<p>Agbonlahor began the season injured, and could not make an appearance until the 1–1 away draw with Newcastle United on 2 September 2012. He later returned to full fitness and started the away game at Manchester City on 25 September. Agbonlahor managed to score two goals in the match as Villa secured a memorable 4–2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> win (after extra time) over the Premier League champions at the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Manchester_Stadium" title="City of Manchester Stadium">City of Manchester Stadium</a>. On 3 November 2012, he scored his first Premier League goal in just under a year against <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> in a run stretching 29 games. On 6 April 2013, Agbonlahor scored his 60th Premier League goal for Aston Villa, making him joint top Premier League goalscorer for the club alongside <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Yorke" title="Dwight Yorke">Dwight Yorke</a>. On 29 April, in a 6–1 win over Sunderland, Agbonlahor scored his 61st Premier League goal to overtake Yorke as Villa's highest all-time Premier League goal scorer. He scored two goals in a 2–1 win over Norwich on 4 May, moving Aston Villa up to 13th place in the Premier League.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agbonlahor began the season brightly, winning two penalties in a 3–1 away win over Arsenal on 17 August 2013 and assisting <a href="/wiki/Christian_Benteke" title="Christian Benteke">Christian Benteke</a>'s goal against Chelsea four days later in a controversial 2–1 defeat at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a>. This resulted in strong praise from manager Paul Lambert, and a huge demand from the public for an England call up, which was ignored by <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a>.</p>
<p>In September, Agbonlahor participated in a charity match organised by Stiliyan Petrov, which featured many celebrities and footballers, both former and current. During the match, Agbonlahor tackled and injured <a href="/wiki/One_Direction" title="One Direction">One Direction</a> member <a href="/wiki/Louis_Tomlinson" title="Louis Tomlinson">Louis Tomlinson</a>, who then became ill after being substituted. Despite apologising to Tomlinson, Agbonlahor received many threats after the match from One Direction fans via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 10 September 2014, Agbonlahor signed a new four-year contract, saying, "I can still remember making my debut, so to still be able to contribute is a great feeling."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Three days later, he scored the only goal as Villa won away at Liverpool, a club he had only scored once against before in the Premier League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor was given a straight red card on 20 December for a challenge on former teammate Ashley Young as Villa drew 1–1 with Manchester United,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> however his three-match suspension was rescinded on appeal three days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbonlahor made his 300th Premier League appearance for Villa on 14 March 2015 away to Sunderland, scoring twice in the first half in a 4–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 May in the <a href="/wiki/2015_FA_Cup_Final" title="2015 FA Cup Final">FA Cup Final</a> against Arsenal at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, manager <a href="/wiki/Tim_Sherwood" title="Tim Sherwood">Tim Sherwood</a> chose to start <a href="/wiki/Charles_N%27Zogbia" title="Charles N'Zogbia">Charles N'Zogbia</a> instead of Agbonlahor, and then brought him on in place of the Frenchman after 53 minutes, with Villa already 0–2 down; they eventually lost 0–4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agbonlahor was named <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)#Club" title="Captain (association football)">club captain</a> at the start of the 2015–16 season, with new signing <a href="/wiki/Micah_Richards" title="Micah Richards">Micah Richards</a> named team captain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>Soon after manager <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9mi_Garde" title="Rémi Garde">Rémi Garde</a> joined, Agbonlahor was dropped from the starting line-up. During the January transfer window, there was speculation surrounding Agbonlahor and his future at the club; however, though Turkish club <a href="/wiki/Kayserispor" title="Kayserispor">Kayserispor</a> were linked, Garde later revealed that no offers had been made for him. Agbonlahor returned to the squad in an FA Cup tie against Manchester City on 30 January 2015, and scored his first goal of the season a week later in a Premier League match in a 2–0 home victory against Norwich. This was his first goal in 11 months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 April 2016, Aston Villa suspended Agbonlahor, pending an investigation into reports that he was pictured appearing to hold a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shisha_pipe" title="Shisha pipe">shisha pipe</a> while on holiday in Dubai.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> On 19 April 2016, he was again suspended after he was allegedly pictured with laughing gas canisters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> He was punished with a substantial fine and resigned the captaincy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agbonlahor failed to impress new manager <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Di_Matteo" title="Roberto Di Matteo">Roberto Di Matteo</a> in pre-season, and his lack of involvement lead to media speculation of a move away from the club. Despite interest from <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading F.C.</a>, he turned them down<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> and a move failed to materialise. His first "appearance" for the club this season came for the U23's squad, a 3-0 loss vs. <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> on 12 September 2016. Agbonlahor played 59 minutes before being substituted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>When manager <a href="/wiki/Steve_Bruce" title="Steve Bruce">Steve Bruce</a> arrived, he immediately reinstated Agbonlahor into first-team training, saying that he's "not interested in what has happened in the past" and that it's a "clean slate" for him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Bruce subsequently put him on a six-week fitness regime in a bid to get him fit for the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agbonlahor's ancestry qualifies him to play for <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> or <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> at international level, but he chose to play for his homeland of <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>.</p>
<p>On 20 September 2006, Agbonlahor was pencilled in as a player in the <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_under-20_football_team" title="Nigeria national under-20 football team">Nigeria under-20</a> team for the match against <a href="/wiki/Rwanda_national_under-20_football_team" title="Rwanda national under-20 football team">Rwanda</a>. He turned down the call, whilst not ruling out playing for the country in the future,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> but subsequently pledged his future to England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 28 September 2006, Agbonlahor received his first call-up to the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21 team</a>, and won his first cap as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_under-21_football_team" title="Germany national under-21 football team">Germany</a> on 6 October. Agbonlahor was then omitted from the England's <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a> squad, by manager <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Pearce" title="Stuart Pearce">Stuart Pearce</a>, on 30 May 2007, as a result of missing the England U21 training camp in Spain. <a href="/wiki/David_Bentley" title="David Bentley">David Bentley</a>, also included in the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England first team</a> line-up, took his place.</p>
<p>Agbonlahor was later recalled to the side by Pearce, in September 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> netting his first goal for the under-21s, by scoring the second in a 3–0 victory, over <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_under-21_football_team" title="Montenegro national under-21 football team">Montenegro</a>.</p>
<p>On 1 February, Agbonlahor was named in <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Capello" title="Fabio Capello">Fabio Capello</a>'s 23-man England squad to play <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> in February 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> although a hamstring injury forced him to withdraw from the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Agbonlahor was also an unused substitute in England's respective 2–0 and 3–0 friendly victories against the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team" title="Trinidad and Tobago national football team">Trinidad &amp; Tobago</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> in May–June 2008.</p>
<p>On 15 November 2008, he was called up to Capello's squad for the upcoming match <a href="/wiki/Olympiastadion_(Berlin)" title="Olympiastadion (Berlin)">away</a> to <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>, joining three other Aston Villa players in the squad: Ashley Young, <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Davies" title="Curtis Davies">Curtis Davies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Barry" title="Gareth Barry">Gareth Barry</a>. On 19 November 2008, he was named to start against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>. He played for 76 minutes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> had a goal disallowed and received high praise from <a href="/wiki/John_Terry" title="John Terry">John Terry</a>, the latter describing him as "a nightmare to play against".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 February 2009, he was in the starting line up against <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>, where he played for 75 minutes before being substituted for debutant <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cole" title="Carlton Cole">Carlton Cole</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> He made his first appearance in a competitive international on 14 October 2009, playing 66 minutes in the 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Belarus_national_football_team" title="Belarus national football team">Belarus</a> in the last game of <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification">qualification</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> He was last called up to the squad in November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Agbonlahor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agbonlahor was one of four children born to a Nigerian father and a Scottish mother in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">West Midlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman2010-11_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman2010-11-2">[2]</a></sup> His parents, however, separated while he was still young and he lived with his father without any contact with his mother for around 20 years. In 2009, ahead of the striker's 23rd birthday, his mother made a public appeal to him via the city's <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Mercury" title="Sunday Mercury">Sunday Mercury</a></i> newspaper in order to become part of his life once more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> Agbonlahor later contacted his mother and the pair reconciled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
