<p><b>Gasa District</b> or <b>Gasa Dzongkhag</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dzongkha" title="Dzongkha">Dzongkha</a>: མགར་ས་རྫོང་ཁག་; <a href="/wiki/Wylie_transliteration" title="Wylie transliteration">Wylie</a>: <i>Mgar-sa rdzong-khag</i>) is one of the 20 <a href="/wiki/Dzongkhag" title="Dzongkhag">dzongkhags</a> (districts) comprising Bhutan. The northern part of Gasa District is a disputed zone under the control of the People`s Republic of China. The capital of Gasa District is <a href="/wiki/Gasa_Dzong" title="Gasa Dzong">Gasa Dzong</a> near <a href="/wiki/Gasa,_Bhutan" title="Gasa, Bhutan">Gasa</a>. It is located in the far north of the county and spans the Middle and High regions of the Tibetan Himalayas. The dominant language of the district is <a href="/wiki/Dzongkha" title="Dzongkha">Dzongkha</a>, which is the national language. Related languages, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Layakha" title="Layakha">Layakha</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunanakha" title="Lunanakha">Lunanakha</a>, are spoken by semi-nomadic communities in the north of the district.</p>
<p>Gasa has an area of 3,117.74 km<sup>2</sup> (1,203.77 sq mi) as of 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup> formerly 4,409.30 km<sup>2</sup> (1,702.44 sq mi) as of 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It had a population of 3,116 as of the 2005 census,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup> making it the largest, least populated, and thus least densely populated of all the dzongkhags; it is also the least developed.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gasa_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gasa was formerly a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drungkhag" title="Drungkhag">drungkhag</a></i> (sub-district) of the <a href="/wiki/Punakha_District" title="Punakha District">Punakha</a> <i>dzongkhag</i> (district). It became a separate <i>dzongkhag</i> in 1992, the start of 7th <a href="/wiki/Five-Year_Plans_of_Bhutan" title="Five-Year Plans of Bhutan">Five Year Plan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gasa_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gasa District is divided into four village blocks (or <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gewog" title="Gewog">gewogs</a></i>):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-election_2011_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-election_2011-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Khamaed_Gewog" title="Khamaed Gewog">Khamaed Gewog</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-election_2011_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-election_2011-3">[3]</a></sup> formerly known as ⋅Goenkhamae<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Khatoed_Gewog" title="Khatoed Gewog">Khatoed Gewog</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-election_2011_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-election_2011-3">[3]</a></sup> formerly known as Goenkaatoe<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Laya_Gewog" title="Laya Gewog">Laya Gewog</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-election_2011_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-election_2011-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lunana_Gewog" title="Lunana Gewog">Lunana Gewog</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsb.gov.bt_2010_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-nsb.gov.bt_2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-election_2011_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-election_2011-3">[3]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gasa_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gasa is bordered to the north by <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> and by <a href="/wiki/Thimphu_District" title="Thimphu District">Thimphu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punakha_District" title="Punakha District">Punakha</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wangdue_Phodrang_District" title="Wangdue Phodrang District">Wangdue Phodrang</a> dzongkhags to the south.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gasa_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gasa has become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tourist_destination" title="Tourist destination">tourist destination</a> because of its pristine forests and the exceptionally scenic location of its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dzong" title="Dzong">Dzong</a>. In 2008 a massive flood on the <a href="/wiki/Mo_Chhu" title="Mo Chhu">Mo Chhu</a> (Female River) destroyed a popular hot spring complex, which is under restoration and was to re-open in late 2011. The high altitude makes farming difficult, although government programs seek to establish <a href="/wiki/Mustard_plant" title="Mustard plant">mustard</a> and summer vegetable planting programs. Residents herd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yak" title="Yak">yaks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dzo" title="Dzo">dzos</a>, and a small number benefit from the nascent tourism industry. A narrow road from Punakha, which is mostly unpaved, reaches up to the Dzong and is now being extended up to Laya. The majority of the known herds of wild <a href="/wiki/Takin" title="Takin">Takin</a> occur in Gasa. Electricity is also being supplied to some of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gewog" title="Gewog">gewogs</a> and all electrification programmes are expected to be complete by 2012.</p>
<p>Gasa is most famous for its <a href="/wiki/Layap" title="Layap">Layap</a> people, and for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Snowman_Trek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Snowman Trek (page does not exist)">Snowman Trek</a>, one of the most challenging treks in the Himalayas.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gasa_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All of Gasa is an environmentally <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protected_areas_of_Bhutan" title="Protected areas of Bhutan">protected area of Bhutan</a>. Most of the dzongkhag lies within <a href="/wiki/Jigme_Dorji_National_Park" title="Jigme Dorji National Park">Jigme Dorji National Park</a> (<a href="/wiki/Khamaed_Gewog" title="Khamaed Gewog">Khamaed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khatoed_Gewog" title="Khatoed Gewog">Khatoed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laya_Gewog" title="Laya Gewog">Laya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lunana_Gewog" title="Lunana Gewog">Lunana Gewogs</a>), although the northeast reaches of Gasa are part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wangchuck_Centennial_Park" title="Wangchuck Centennial Park">Wangchuck Centennial Park</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lunana_Gewog" title="Lunana Gewog">Lunana Gewog</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BTF1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BTF1-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Several of <a href="/wiki/Glaciers_of_Bhutan" title="Glaciers of Bhutan">Bhutan's glaciers</a> are located in Gasa, namely in <a href="/wiki/Lunana_Gewog" title="Lunana Gewog">Lunana Gewog</a>, which borders Tibet.</p>
