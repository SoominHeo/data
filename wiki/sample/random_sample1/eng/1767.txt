<p><b>Yukio Kasahara</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">笠原 幸雄</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kasahara Yukio</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 6 November 1889 – 2 January 1988)</span> was a leading general in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">Second Sino-Japanese War</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yukio_Kasahara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kasahara was born into a military family in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sendai,_Miyagi" title="Sendai, Miyagi">Sendai</a>, but attended the <a href="/wiki/Hibiya_High_School" title="Hibiya High School">First Tokyo Middle School</a> as a youth. He graduated from the 22nd class of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army_Academy" title="Imperial Japanese Army Academy">Imperial Japanese Army Academy</a> in 1913, and from the 22nd class of the <a href="/wiki/Army_War_College_(Japan)" title="Army War College (Japan)">Army Staff College</a> in November 1918.</p>
<p>Kasahara was sent as a <a href="/wiki/Military_attach%C3%A9" title="Military attaché">military attaché</a> to <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> from 1929–1932, and became fluent in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian language</a>. On his return to Japan, he was assigned to the Soviet Branch of the 4th Section (European &amp; American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_Intelligence" title="Military Intelligence">Military Intelligence</a>), 2nd Bureau, of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army_General_Staff" title="Imperial Japanese Army General Staff">Imperial Japanese Army General Staff</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1933-1934, he became an instructor at the Cavalry School, and was subsequently appointed commander of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Guard_of_Japan" title="Imperial Guard of Japan">Imperial Guards Cavalry Regiment</a> from 1934-1936. Kasahara returned to the 2nd Bureau of the General Staff in 1936 as Chief of the 4th Section, 5th Section and 6th Section, covering all of the European, American and Russian Intelligence. He was a major proponent of the Strike North Group (<i><a href="/wiki/Hokushin-ron" title="Hokushin-ron">hokushin-ron</a></i>) philosophy, feeling strongly that the Soviet Union posed a major threat and a major opportunity for Japan.</p>
<p>From 1937-1938, Kasahara was sent to <a href="/wiki/Manchukuo" title="Manchukuo">Manchukuo</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_of_staff_(military)" title="Chief of staff (military)">Vice Chief of Staff</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kwantung_Army" title="Kwantung Army">Kwantung Army</a>. He visited <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> as part of a military mission in 1938 after the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Tripartite_Pact" title="Tripartite Pact">Tripartite Pact</a>, and returned to the General Staff on his return to Japan. However, Kasahara was soon dispatched back to China. From 1939-1941, he served as Chief of Staff of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_China_Area_Army" title="Northern China Area Army">Northern China Area Army</a>. He was then promoted to commander in chief of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IJA_12th_Division" title="IJA 12th Division">IJA 12th Division</a> from 1941-1942. For most the remainder of the war (1942–1945), Kasahara served as Chief of Staff of the Kwantung Army. However, in April 1945, he was appointed to replace General <a href="/wiki/Yoshio_Kozuki" title="Yoshio Kozuki">Yoshio Kozuki</a> as commander of the <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Army_(Japan)" title="Eleventh Army (Japan)">IJA 11th Army</a>, and thus participated in the <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ichi-Go" title="Operation Ichi-Go">Operation Ichi-Go</a> offensive, notably at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Guilin-Liuzhou" title="Battle of Guilin-Liuzhou">Battle of Guilin-Liuzhou</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
