<p><b>Ghassan Massoud</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">غسّان مسعود</span>‎‎; born September 20, 1958) is a <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrian</a> actor and <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">filmmaker</a>. He is known in the West for playing the role of <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> ruler <a href="/wiki/Saladin" title="Saladin">Saladin</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2005_in_film" title="2005 in film">2005 film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Heaven_(film)" title="Kingdom of Heaven (film)">Kingdom of Heaven</a></i>.</p>
<p>Massoud also played the "<a href="/wiki/Sheikh" title="Sheikh">Sheikh</a>" in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_film" title="Turkish film">Turkish film</a> <i>Kurtlar Vadisi: Irak</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Valley_of_the_Wolves:_Iraq" title="Valley of the Wolves: Iraq">Valley of the Wolves: Iraq</a></i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brethren_Court#Ammand_the_Corsair" title="Brethren Court">Ammand the Corsair</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_At_World%27s_End" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: At World's End">Pirates of the Caribbean: At World's End</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Massoud1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Massoud1-1">[1]</a></sup> and Ramesses II's Grand Vizier in <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a>'s 2014 Bible epic, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exodus_(2014_film)" title="Exodus (2014 film)">Exodus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Massoud turned down a role in the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Syriana" title="Syriana">Syriana</a></i>, claiming he feared the movie would be anti-Arab.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Massoud1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Massoud1-1">[1]</a></sup> Massoud also cited concerns regarding the political situation in his country and the wider region in declining the role. He later said he regretted his decision after seeing the completed film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Massoud2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Massoud2-3">[3]</a></sup> Massoud is known in Syria for his appearance in many Syrian-made films, and writing and directing the theatre play <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Diplomasiyyoun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Diplomasiyyoun (page does not exist)">Diplomasiyyoun</a></i>, and was part of the Syrian Ministry of Culture’s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_National_Theater&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Syrian National Theater (page does not exist)">National Theater</a>’s 2002 season. He has appeared in the Syrian television miniseries <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Unshudet_al-Matar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Unshudet al-Matar (page does not exist)">The Chant of Rain</a></i>, and in Haytham Hakky's well known work <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Memories_of_the_Forthcoming_Age&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Memories of the Forthcoming Age (page does not exist)">Memories of the Forthcoming Age</a></i>, and on the Syrian stage was an actor in <a href="/wiki/August_Strindberg" title="August Strindberg">August Strindberg</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Julie" title="Miss Julie">Miss Julie</a></i>. Most recently, he starred as the companion of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abu_Bakr" title="Abu Bakr">Abu Bakr</a> in <a href="/wiki/Middle_East_Broadcasting_Center" title="Middle East Broadcasting Center">MBC</a>'s series <i><a href="/wiki/Omar_(TV_series)" title="Omar (TV series)">Omar</a></i>.</p>
<p>Massoud was born in a small village called Fejlit in <a href="/wiki/Duraykish_District" title="Duraykish District">Durakish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tartus" title="Tartus">Tartus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>. He is married with a son and a daughter. Massoud teaches <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">Drama</a> at both the Damascus Music and Drama School and <a href="/wiki/Higher_Institute_for_Dramatic_Arts" title="Higher Institute for Dramatic Arts">Higher Institute for Dramatic Arts</a>.</p>
