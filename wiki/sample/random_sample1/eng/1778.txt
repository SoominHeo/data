<p><b>Gaston Louis Alfred Leroux</b> (6 May 1868<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup>Â â 15 April 1927) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">author</a> of <a href="/wiki/Detective_fiction" title="Detective fiction">detective fiction</a>.</p>
<p>In the English-speaking world, he is best known for writing the novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phantom_of_the_Opera" title="The Phantom of the Opera">The Phantom of the Opera</a></i> (<i>Le FantÃ´me de l'OpÃ©ra</i>, 1910), which has been made into several film and stage productions of the same name, notably the <a href="/wiki/The_Phantom_of_the_Opera_(1925_film)" title="The Phantom of the Opera (1925 film)">1925 film</a> starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lon_Chaney,_Sr." title="Lon Chaney, Sr.">Lon Chaney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Lloyd_Webber" title="Andrew Lloyd Webber">Andrew Lloyd Webber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Phantom_of_the_Opera_(1986_musical)" title="The Phantom of the Opera (1986 musical)">1986 musical</a>. His novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mystery_of_the_Yellow_Room" title="The Mystery of the Yellow Room">The Mystery of the Yellow Room</a></i> is also one of the most famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Locked_room_mystery" title="Locked room mystery">locked-room mysteries</a> ever.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gaston_Leroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leroux was born in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> in 1868 and died in 1927 in France. He went to school in <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a> and studied law in Paris, graduating in 1889. He inherited millions of francs and lived wildly until he nearly reached bankruptcy. In 1890, he began working as a <a href="/wiki/Court_reporter" title="Court reporter">court reporter</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theater" title="Theater">theater</a> critic for <i><a href="/wiki/L%27%C3%89cho_de_Paris" title="L'Ãcho de Paris">L'Ãcho de Paris</a></i>. His most important journalism came when he began working as an international correspondent for the <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Matin_(France)" title="Le Matin (France)">Le Matin</a></i>. He was present at, and covered, the <a href="/wiki/1905_Russian_Revolution" title="1905 Russian Revolution">1905 Russian Revolution</a>.</p>
<p>Another case at which he was present involved the investigation and in-depth coverage of the former <a href="/wiki/Palais_Garnier" title="Palais Garnier">Paris Opera</a> (presently housing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballet_de_l%27Op%C3%A9ra_National_de_Paris" title="Ballet de l'OpÃ©ra National de Paris">Paris Ballet</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> The basement contained a cell that held prisoners of the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Commune" title="Paris Commune">Paris Commune</a>.</p>
<p>He suddenly left journalism in 1907, and began writing fiction. In 1919, he and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Bern%C3%A8de" title="Arthur BernÃ¨de">Arthur BernÃ¨de</a> formed their own film company, <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_des_Cin%C3%A9romans" title="SociÃ©tÃ© des CinÃ©romans">SociÃ©tÃ© des CinÃ©romans</a>, to publish novels simultaneously and turn them into films. He first wrote a mystery novel entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mystery_of_the_Yellow_Room" title="The Mystery of the Yellow Room">Le mystÃ¨re de la chambre jaune</a></i> (1908; <a href="/wiki/The_Mystery_of_the_Yellow_Room" title="The Mystery of the Yellow Room">The Mystery of the Yellow Room</a>), starring the amateur detective <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Rouletabille" title="Joseph Rouletabille">Joseph Rouletabille</a>. Leroux's contribution to French detective fiction is considered a parallel to <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Conan_Doyle" title="Arthur Conan Doyle">Sir Arthur Conan Doyle</a>'s in the United Kingdom and <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Allan_Poe" title="Edgar Allan Poe">Edgar Allan Poe</a>'s in the United States.</p>
<p>Leroux published his most famous work, <i>The Phantom of the Opera</i>, as a serial in 1909 and 1910, and as a book in 1910 (with an English translation appearing in 1911).</p>
<p>Leroux was made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chevalier_de_la_Legion_d%27honneur" title="Chevalier de la Legion d'honneur">Chevalier de la Legion d'honneur</a> in 1909.</p>
