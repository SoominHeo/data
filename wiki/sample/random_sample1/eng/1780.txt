<p>The <b>Amur stickleback</b> (<i>Pungitius sinensis</i>) is a species of fish in the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gasterosteidae" title="Gasterosteidae">Gasterosteidae</a>. This <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brackish_water" title="Brackish water">brackish water</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Marine_(ocean)" title="Marine (ocean)">marine</a> benthopelagic fish is usually 6.5 cm (up to 9  cm) in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is widespread in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>: off the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_peninsula" title="Korean peninsula">Korean peninsula</a>, northeast and north <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kuril_Islands" title="Kuril Islands">Kuril Islands</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamchatka_peninsula" title="Kamchatka peninsula">Kamchatka peninsula</a>, and the basin of the <a href="/wiki/Amur_River" title="Amur River">Amur River</a>.</p>
