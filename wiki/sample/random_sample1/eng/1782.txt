<p><b>Kashima Soccer Stadium Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">鹿島サッカースタジアム駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kashima Sakkā Sutajiamu-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kashima,_Ibaraki" title="Kashima, Ibaraki">Kashima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ibaraki_Prefecture" title="Ibaraki Prefecture">Ibaraki Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>The station operates for passengers only when there is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">football</a> match at the nearby <a href="/wiki/Kashima_Soccer_Stadium" title="Kashima Soccer Stadium">Kashima Soccer Stadium</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kashima_Soccer_Stadium_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on November 12, 1970, as a freight terminal named Kita-Kashima Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">北鹿島駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kita-Kashima eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. It formed the boundary between <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (Kashima Line) and <a href="/wiki/Kashima_Rinkai_Railway" title="Kashima Rinkai Railway">Kashima Rinkai Railway</a> (Kashima Rinkō Line).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the Kashima Rinkō Line provided passenger services between July 25, 1978, and December 1, 1983, and the Ōarai Kashima Line opened on March 14, 1985, for both freight and passenger traffic, Kita-Kashima Station was not used by passenger services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo-1">[1]</a></sup> The station was absorbed into the JR East network upon the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987.</p>
<p>On March 12, 1994, the station was renamed to its present name and passenger services began on a limited basis (only on days when there is a game at <a href="/wiki/Kashima_Soccer_Stadium" title="Kashima Soccer Stadium">Kashima Soccer Stadium</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
