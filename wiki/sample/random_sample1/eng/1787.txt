<p><a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">Count</a> <b>Katsu Kaishū</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">勝 海舟</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, March 12, 1823 – January 21, 1899)</span> was a Japanese statesman and naval engineer during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Late_Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Late Tokugawa shogunate">late Tokugawa shogunate</a> and early <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Kaishū was a nickname which he took from a piece of calligraphy (Kaishū Shooku <span class="t_nihongo_kanji"><span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">海舟書屋</span></span>) by <a href="/wiki/Sakuma_Sh%C5%8Dzan" title="Sakuma Shōzan">Sakuma Shōzan</a>. He went through a series of <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given names</a> throughout his life; his childhood name was <b>Rintarō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">麟太郎</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and his real name was <b>Yoshikuni</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">義邦</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. He was often called <b>Awa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">安房</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> from his title <b>Awano-kami</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">安房守</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> during the late Tokugawa shogunate and changed his name to <b>Yasuyoshi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">安芳</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> after the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>.</p>
<p>Katsu Kaishū eventually rose to occupy the position of commissioner <i>(Gunkan-bugyō)</i> in the Tokugawa navy. He is particularly known for his role in the surrender of <a href="/wiki/Edo" title="Edo">Edo</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Katsu was born in <a href="/wiki/Edo" title="Edo">Edo</a> (present day <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>) to a low-ranking retainer of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa</a> <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">Shogun</a>. His father, <a href="/wiki/Katsu_Kokichi" title="Katsu Kokichi">Katsu Kokichi</a>, the subject of the autobiography, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Musui%27s_Story&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Musui's Story (page does not exist)">Musui's Story</a></i>, was the ill-behaved head of a minor <i>samurai</i> family. As a youth Katsu Kaishu, whose given name was Katsu Rintaro (Kaishu was pseudonym), studied <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> and European military science, and was eventually appointed translator by the government when European powers attempted to open contact with Japan. Katsu developed the reputation as an expert in western military technology.</p>
<p>Under the advice of Dutch naval officers, Katsu served as head naval cadet at the <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki_Naval_Training_Center" title="Nagasaki Naval Training Center">Nagasaki Naval Academy</a> between 1855 and 1859.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_service">Military service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="567" data-file-width="471" height="265" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg/220px-Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg/330px-Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg/440px-Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Katsu at San Francisco, 1860</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1860, Katsu served as captain of the warship <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kanrin-maru" title="Kanrin-maru">Kanrin-maru</a></i>, (with assistance from US naval officer Lt. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_M._Brooke" title="John M. Brooke">John M. Brooke</a>), to escort the first Japanese delegation to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, en route to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_DC" title="Washington, DC">Washington, DC</a>, for the formal ratification of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Amity_and_Commerce_(United_States%E2%80%93Japan)" title="Treaty of Amity and Commerce (United States–Japan)">Harris Treaty</a>. The <i>Kanrin Maru</i>, built by the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a>, was the first Japanese vessel to sail to the Western world. Kaishū remained in San Francisco for nearly two months, observing American society, culture and technology. Following his return to Japan, Katsu held a series of high-ranking posts in the Tokugawa navy, arguing before government councils in favor of a unified Japanese naval force led by professionally trained officers in disregard of promotion and assignment due to hereditary status. During his command as director of the <a href="/wiki/Kobe_Naval_Training_Center" title="Kobe Naval Training Center">Kobe Naval School</a>, the institute would become a major source of activity for progressive thinking and reformists between 1863 and 1864.</p>
<p>In 1866, Katsu was appointed negotiator between the <i>bakufu</i> forces and the anti-shogunal domain of <a href="/wiki/Nagato_Province" title="Nagato Province">Chōshū</a>, and later served as chief negotiator for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokugawa_bakufu" title="Tokugawa bakufu">Tokugawa bakufu</a>, ensuring a relatively peaceful and orderly transition of power in the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>.</p>
<p>Although sympathetic to the anti-Tokugawa cause, Katsu remained loyal to the Tokugawa <i>bakufu</i> during the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>. After the collapse of the Tokugawa forces in late 1867, Katsu negotiated the surrender of Edo castle to <a href="/wiki/Saig%C5%8D_Takamori" title="Saigō Takamori">Saigō Takamori</a> on 11 April 1868.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years">Later years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kaishu_Katsu_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="602" data-file-width="334" height="306" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Kaishu_Katsu_2.jpg/170px-Kaishu_Katsu_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Kaishu_Katsu_2.jpg/255px-Kaishu_Katsu_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d1/Kaishu_Katsu_2.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kaishu_Katsu_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Kaishū Katsu seated in traditional clothing</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Katsu relocated to Shizuoka after the new Imperial government took control of the shogun's former capital, which was renamed Tokyo ("Eastern Capital"). He returned briefly to government service as Vice Minister of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> in 1872, followed by first Minister of the Navy from 1873 until 1878. He was the most prominent of the former Tokugawa retainers who found employment within the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_government" title="Meiji government">Meiji government</a>, and was <i><a href="/wiki/Dainagon#A_revealing_framework" title="Dainagon">sangi</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">参議</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> between 1869 and 1885 who did not come from one of the four paramount domains. Although his influence within the navy was minimal, as the Navy was largely dominated by a core of Satsuma officers, Katsu served in a senior advisory capacity on national policy. During the next two decades, Katsu served on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privy_Council_(Japan)" title="Privy Council (Japan)">Privy Council</a> and wrote extensively on naval issues before his death in 1899.</p>
<p>In 1887, he was elevated to the title of <i>hakushaku</i> (count) in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i> peerage system.</p>
<p>Katsu recorded his memoirs in the book <i>Hikawa Seiwa</i>.</p>
