<p><b>Kawarada Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">河原田駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kawarada-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yokkaichi,_Mie" title="Yokkaichi, Mie">Yokkaichi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, jointly operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JR_Central" title="JR Central">JR Central</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ise_Railway_Ise_Line" title="Ise Railway Ise Line">Ise Railway</a>. The station is 44.1 rail kilometers from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kansai_Main_Line" title="Kansai Main Line">Kansai Main Line</a> at <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Station" title="Nagoya Station">Nagoya Station</a>, and is officially the terminus of the Ise Railway, although most trains continue through to <a href="/wiki/Yokkaichi_Station" title="Yokkaichi Station">Yokkaichi Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawarada_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kawarada Station opened on December 20, 1895 as a station on the Kansai Railway. The line was nationalized on October 1, 1907, becoming part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Government_Railway" title="Japanese Government Railway">Japanese Government Railways</a> (JGR) system. The station building was rebuilt in December 1915. The JGR became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_National_Railways" title="Japan National Railways">Japan National Railways</a> (JNR) after World War II. The JNR Ise Line began operations on September 1, 1973. The Ise Line was <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatized</a> on March 27, 1987, four days before the dissolution of the JNR on April 1, 1987. The present station building was completed in March 2003.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawarada_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kawarada Station has two opposed <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a>, serving four tracks for use by the Kansai Main Line, and a single <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> for use by the Ise Railway.</p>
