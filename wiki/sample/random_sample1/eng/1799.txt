<p>Count <b>Kawamura Sumiyoshi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">川村 純義</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 18 December 1836 – 12 August 1904)</span>, was an admiral in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>. Kawamura's wife Haru was the aunt of <a href="/wiki/Saig%C5%8D_Takamori" title="Saigō Takamori">Saigō Takamori</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawamura_Sumiyoshi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A native of <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Province" title="Satsuma Province">Satsuma</a>, Kawamura studied navigation at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokugawa_bakufu" title="Tokugawa bakufu">Tokugawa bakufu</a> naval school at <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki_Naval_Training_Center" title="Nagasaki Naval Training Center">Nagasaki Naval Training Center</a>. In 1868, he joined his Satsuma clansmen, and fought on the imperial side in the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a> as an army general. He was especially noted for his role in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aizu" title="Battle of Aizu">Battle of Aizu-Wakamatsu</a>.</p>
<p>Under the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_government" title="Meiji government">Meiji government</a>, he became an officer in the fledgling Imperial Japanese Navy, and steadily rose through the ranks. He became first Director of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Naval_Academy" title="Imperial Japanese Naval Academy">Imperial Japanese Naval Academy</a> in 1870 and <i>taifu</i> (senior vice minister) of Navy in 1872. He was in command of Japanese naval forces during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_Expedition_of_1874" title="Taiwan Expedition of 1874">Taiwan Expedition of 1874</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Rebellion" title="Satsuma Rebellion">Satsuma Rebellion</a>, he was placed in command of all Imperial troops in September 1877 at the final <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shiroyama" title="Battle of Shiroyama">Battle of Shiroyama</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kumamoto,_Kumamoto" title="Kumamoto, Kumamoto">Kumamoto</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Saig%C5%8D_Takamori" title="Saigō Takamori">Saigō Takamori</a> was killed (or committed <i><a href="/wiki/Seppuku" title="Seppuku">seppuku</a></i>). This battle, Saigō's last stand against the Meiji government, was the historical basis for the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Samurai" title="The Last Samurai">The Last Samurai</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1878, Kawamura became <i>sangi</i> (councillor) and the second Navy Minister. He remained in that position until 1885 except when he was temporarily replaced by <a href="/wiki/Enomoto_Takeaki" title="Enomoto Takeaki">Enomoto Takeaki</a>, and during that period he expanded the influence of people from Satsuma within the navy.</p>
<p>In 1884, he was ennobled with the title of <i>hakushaku</i> (count) under the <i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i> peerage system. Later serving as court councillor and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privy_Councillor" title="Privy Councillor">Privy Councillor</a>, in 1901 he was given responsibility for the upbringing of the newborn <a href="/wiki/Hirohito" title="Hirohito">Prince Michi</a> (the future Emperor Hirohito) and his younger brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Chichibu" title="Prince Chichibu">Prince Chichibu (Yasuhito)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1904, Kawamura was posthumously appointed to the rank of <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a>, setting a precedent for such honors. His cause of death remains unknown, setting a mystery.</p>
