<p><b>Kawakami Gensai</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">河上 彦斎</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 25 December 1834 – 13 January 1872)</span> was a Japanese samurai of the late <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. A highly skilled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swordsman" title="Swordsman">swordsman</a>, he was one of the <a href="/wiki/Four_Hitokiri_of_the_Bakumatsu" title="Four Hitokiri of the Bakumatsu">four most notable assassins</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bakumatsu_period" title="Bakumatsu period">Bakumatsu period</a>. Gensai's high-speed sword discipline allowed him to assassinate targets in broad daylight.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Birth_and_early_life">Birth and early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawakami_Gensai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Birth and early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kawakami Gensai was born in <a href="/wiki/Kumamoto" title="Kumamoto">Kumamoto</a> in 1834, to Komori Sadasuke, a retainer of the daimyo of the <a href="/wiki/Kumamoto_Domain" title="Kumamoto Domain">Kumamoto Domain</a>. Because Gensai's older brother Hanzaemon was chosen as the family's heir, at age 11 he was given in adoption to Kawakami Genbei (河上彦兵衛), another Kumamoto retainer. He then entered the domain's school, the <b>Jishūkan</b> (時習館), and followed its academic and martial courses of study. Given his later prowess, it is rather curious to note that during his martial training he apparently did not win many bouts. With regards to this he is said to have commented "<i>Kenjutsu</i> (swordsmanship) with bamboo <i><a href="/wiki/Shinai" title="Shinai">shinai</a></i> is nothing more than play." At age 16 he was called to serve in the Kumamoto castle town as a menial in charge of cleaning (<i>Osōji-bōzu</i> お掃除坊主). Although this was a low-level position, Gensai devoted himself wholeheartedly to it, using his free time to polish his martial and literary skills, as well as learn <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sad%C5%8D" title="Sadō">sadō</a> (tea ceremony) and <a href="/wiki/Ikebana" title="Ikebana">ikebana</a> (flower arrangement). It was at this time that he met two men who would later be important in the activities of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ishin_shishi" title="Ishin shishi">ishin shishi</a></i>: Todoroki Buhei and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Miyabe_Teiz%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Miyabe Teizō (page does not exist)">Miyabe Teizō</a>. Thanks to his discussions with them, he took a serious interest in the concept of <i>kinnō</i> (勤王), or imperial loyalty.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activity_in_the_1850s">Activity in the 1850s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawakami_Gensai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activity in the 1850s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1851, he joined the Kumamoto lord <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hosokawa_Narimori&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hosokawa Narimori (page does not exist)">Hosokawa Narimori</a> and went to Edo for his lord's <i><a href="/wiki/Sankin-k%C5%8Dtai" title="Sankin-kōtai">sankin-kōtai</a></i> rotation. It was during his service to the lord in Edo that <a href="/wiki/Matthew_C._Perry" title="Matthew C. Perry">Commodore Perry</a> arrived in 1853. As the shogunate subsequently entered into a series of increasingly unfair <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unequal_treaties" title="Unequal treaties">unequal treaties</a>, Gensai left Edo in anger and returned to Kumamoto, where he entered the <b>Gendōkan</b> academy of the <i>kinnō</i> scholar <a href="/wiki/Hayashi_%C5%8Cen" title="Hayashi Ōen">Hayashi Ōen</a>. After a thorough schooling in Ōen's <i>kinnō</i> philosophy, Gensai returned to Edo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activity_in_the_1860s">Activity in the 1860s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawakami_Gensai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Activity in the 1860s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gensai was present at the Kumamoto residence in Edo during the <a href="/wiki/Ansei_Purge" title="Ansei Purge">Ansei Purge</a>. In the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/Ii_Naosuke" title="Ii Naosuke">Ii Naosuke</a>'s assassination, when a group of the escaping assassins suddenly entered the residence, it was Gensai who calmed down the subsequent uproar, calling for a doctor and having a private tea ceremony for the men. It was during this ceremony that he told the men of his admiration for them.</p>
<p>In 1861, Gensai married <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kawakami_Teiko&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kawakami Teiko (page does not exist)">Misawa Teiko</a>, the daughter of another Kumamoto retainer. A martial artist herself, she was highly skilled in the use of the <a href="/wiki/Naginata" title="Naginata">naginata</a>. The couple would have a son, Gentarō, who survived even after Gensai's execution, thanks to Teiko's efforts.</p>
<p>In 1862, he joined Kumamoto forces who were posted to security duty in Kyoto. After the political event of Higo-han, he left there and went to Chōshū-han, where he became a personal body guard of <a href="/wiki/Sanj%C5%8D_Sanetomi" title="Sanjō Sanetomi">Sanjō Sanetomi</a>. It was at this point, he quit his job as <i>bōzu</i>, and soon after, left Kumamoto service altogether. In 1864, he lost his mentor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Miyabe_Teiz%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Miyabe Teizō (page does not exist)">Miyabe Teizō</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Shinsengumi" title="Shinsengumi">Shinsengumi</a> raid at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ikedaya_Jiken" title="Ikedaya Jiken">Ikedaya</a>. Soon after, Gensai carried out his most famous and only confirmed assassination: that of <a href="/wiki/Sakuma_Sh%C5%8Dzan" title="Sakuma Shōzan">Sakuma Shōzan</a>. He killed Shōzan in one stroke, in broad daylight. While other assassinations have been attributed to him, only his murder of Shōzan can be proven.</p>
<p>After this, he withdrew to <a href="/wiki/Nagato_Province" title="Nagato Province">Chōshū</a> and took part in the military actions of Takasugi Shinsaku's <a href="/wiki/Kiheitai" title="Kiheitai">Kiheitai</a> against the shogunate's <a href="/wiki/First_Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_expedition" title="First Chōshū expedition">Chōshū Expeditions</a>. During the second <a href="/wiki/Second_Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_expedition" title="Second Chōshū expedition">Chōshū campaign</a> by the Tokugawa regime, he participated for Chōshū and eventually won the battle. However, during action in Kokura, he decisively surrendered to Kumamoto forces, and was imprisoned until just after the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Restoration_and_Gensai.27s_death">The Restoration and Gensai's death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawakami_Gensai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Restoration and Gensai's death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, Gensai was released from prison. He changed his name to <b>Kouda Genbei</b> (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">高田 源兵衛</span>), and served as a military official and teacher for the Kumamoto domain. However, because of his harboring of some <a href="/wiki/Kiheitai" title="Kiheitai">Kiheitai</a> stragglers under his old comrade Oraku Gentarō, he was again put in prison, and was executed in 1872.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gensai_in_fiction">Gensai in fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kawakami_Gensai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gensai in fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The character <a href="/wiki/Himura_Kenshin" title="Himura Kenshin">Himura Kenshin</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Rurouni_Kenshin" title="Rurouni Kenshin">Rurouni Kenshin</a></i> is based on Gensai.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-design_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-design-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The character <a href="/wiki/List_of_Gin_Tama_characters#Bansai_Kawakami" title="List of Gin Tama characters">Kawakami Bansai</a> from the manga <i><a href="/wiki/Gin_Tama" title="Gin Tama">Gin Tama</a></i> takes his name from Gensai.</p>
