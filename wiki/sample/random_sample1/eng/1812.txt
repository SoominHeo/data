<p><i><b>Dear Land of Guyana, of Rivers and Plains</b></i> is the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> of <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>. Selected a month before <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> in 1966, the lyrics were written by Archibald Leonard Luker and the music was composed by Robert Cyril Gladstone Potter.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_lyrics">Official lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dear_Land_of_Guyana,_of_Rivers_and_Plains&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Official lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dd>Dear land of Guyana, of rivers and plains</dd>
<dd>Made rich by the sunshine, and lush by the rains,</dd>
<dd>Set gem-like and fair, between mountains and seas,</dd>
<dd>Your children salute you, dear land of the free.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Green land of Guyana, our heroes of yore,</dd>
<dd>Both bondsmen and free, laid their bones on your shore.</dd>
<dd>This soil so they hallowed, and from them are we,</dd>
<dd>All sons of one Mother, Guyana the free.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Great land of Guyana, diverse though our strains,</dd>
<dd>We're born of their sacrifice, heirs of their pains,</dd>
<dd>And ours is the glory their eyes did not see,</dd>
<dd>One land of six peoples, united and free.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Dear land of Guyana, to you will we give,</dd>
<dd>Our homage, our service, each day that we live;</dd>
<dd>God guard you, great Mother, and make us to be</dd>
<dd>More worthy our heritage, land of the free.</dd>
</dl>
