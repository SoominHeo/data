<p><b>Astacidea</b> is a group of <a href="/wiki/Decapoda" title="Decapoda">decapod</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crustacea" title="Crustacea">crustaceans</a> including <a href="/wiki/Lobster" title="Lobster">lobsters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crayfish" title="Crayfish">crayfish</a> and their close relatives.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astacidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The infraorder Astacidea comprises five extant superfamilies, two of <a href="/wiki/Crayfish" title="Crayfish">crayfish</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astacoidea" title="Astacoidea">Astacoidea</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parastacoidea" title="Parastacoidea">Parastacoidea</a>), one of true <a href="/wiki/Lobster" title="Lobster">lobsters</a> (Nephropoidea), one of <a href="/wiki/Reef_lobster" title="Reef lobster">reef lobsters</a> (the genus <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enoplometopus" title="Enoplometopus">Enoplometopus</a></i>), and a number of fossil taxa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grave_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grave-1">[1]</a></sup> As of 2009<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Astacidea&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the group contains 782 recognised species, over 400 of which are in the crayfish family <a href="/wiki/Cambaridae" title="Cambaridae">Cambaridae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grave_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Grave-1">[1]</a></sup> The members of the infra-order <a href="/wiki/Glypheidea" title="Glypheidea">Glypheidea</a> (containing numerous fossils and the two extant species <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neoglyphea_inopinata" title="Neoglyphea inopinata">Neoglyphea inopinata</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurentaeglyphea_neocaledonica" title="Laurentaeglyphea neocaledonica">Laurentaeglyphea neocaledonica</a></i>) was formerly included here.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Poore_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Poore-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astacidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Members of the infraorder Astacidea are distinguished from most other decapods by the presence of <a href="/wiki/Chela_(organ)" title="Chela (organ)">chelae</a> (claws) on each of the first three pairs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pereiopod" title="Pereiopod">pereiopods</a> (walking legs), the first of which is much larger than the remaining two pairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Poore_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Poore-2">[2]</a></sup> The last two pairs of pereiopods are simple (without claws), except in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thaumastocheles" title="Thaumastocheles">Thaumastocheles</a></i>, where the fifth pereiopod may have "a minute pincer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astacidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Members of the infraorder Astacidea are found throughout the world – both in the <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Fresh_water" title="Fresh water">fresh water</a> – except for mainland Africa and parts of Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
