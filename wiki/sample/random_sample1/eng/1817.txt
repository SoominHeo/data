<p><i><b>Kaseifu no Mita</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">家政婦のミタ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, I am Mita, Your Housekeeper<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ntv2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ntv2-1">[1]</a></sup>)</span> is a 2011 <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">Japanese television drama</a> series. The story revolves around a family who is grieving over their mother's recent suicide. They hired Mita as a housekeeper to upkeep the house, which has been thrown into disarray. Mita, who is played by actress <a href="/wiki/Nanako_Matsushima" title="Nanako Matsushima">Nanako Matsushima</a>, will do anything that her employer orders her to do, except smiling or revealing her past.</p>
<p>This television series was broadcast from October 12 to December 21, 2011, as part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">Nippon Television</a>'s <i>Tears Wednesday</i> time slot, which airs every Wednesday from 10pm to 10:54pm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On average, <i>Kaseifu no Mita</i> garnered a viewership rating of 25.2%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ntv_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ntv-3">[3]</a></sup> Its last episode garnered a viewership rating of over 40% when it was aired,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ntv_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ntv-3">[3]</a></sup> making it the highest watched show of 2011 in Japan (The last TBS drama breaking 40% viewing was "Beautiful Life" in 2000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mantan20120102_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mantan20120102-4">[4]</a></sup> <i>Kaseifu no Mita</i> won several awards, including the "Best Drama" award, at the 71st Television Drama Academy Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dramaaward_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-dramaaward-5">[5]</a></sup> Despite the popularity of <i>Kaseifu no Mita,</i> the scriptwriter revealed that there will not be any sequels to this series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nikkan20111207_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-nikkan20111207-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaseifu_no_Mita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Asuda family, who are still grieving over their mother's death, hires a new housekeeper to take charge of the housework. They get Mita, a cold-faced housekeeper who does everything that she is ordered to do, even killing people, except tasks that requires her to smile or to reveal her past. During this period of time, the family becomes fragmented, as they each blame themselves for their mother's death. Mita helps guide the family through these problems, though she did not explicitly give them advice.</p>
<p>Eventually, the family manages to come to terms with their mother's death. They in turn help Mita to overcome her extremely traumatic past, the reason for which she cannot smile or speak her mind. Thanks to their constant care for her, Mita gradually starts to discover love again. However, Mita knows that she cannot become the stepmother of these children, and thus takes the drastic step of becoming a cruel and ultimately short-lived stepmother. In the end, Urara, the children's aunt, becomes their stepmother. Mita then revealed that she cannot work for them anymore, since she has found employment elsewhere. On Christmas Eve, the family finally managed to make Mita smile during their last dinner together. With the family having found their priorities in life, Mita leaves the family on Christmas Day to work for yet another household.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harumi_Housekeeping_Agency">Harumi Housekeeping Agency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaseifu_no_Mita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Harumi Housekeeping Agency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nanako_Matsushima" title="Nanako Matsushima">Nanako Matsushima</a> as Akari Mita <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">三田 灯</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 36 years old)</span></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The housekeeper of the Asuda household. She does not smile, and is very efficient in carrying out her duties. Beneath her facade however, she have had the traumatic experience of losing all of the people that she had loved the most in her life. These people includes her father, husband and son. All of them had died under tragic circumstances. Because of this, she dare not start to love another person, for the fear that she might bring them ill-luck. Eventually, she starts to feel emotions again after the Asuda family's constant pestering for her to show her own feelings.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yumi_Shirakawa" title="Yumi Shirakawa">Yumi Shirakawa</a> as Akemi Harumi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">晴海 明美</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>She is the head of the housekeeper agency that Akari is from. In addition to that, Harumi also used to be the housekeeper for Mita's family, and therefore knows her the best. She hopes that Mita would eventually smile on her own free will. Harumi later shifted the housekeeping agency to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>, bringing Mita along with her.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asuda_Household">Asuda Household</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaseifu_no_Mita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asuda Household">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hiroki_Hasegawa" title="Hiroki Hasegawa">Hiroki Hasegawa</a> as Keiichi Asuda <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">阿須田 恵一</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>He is the head of the Asuda household. However, he wanted to divorce his wife to marry Kazama, with whom he was having an affair. This led to his wife's suicide. After the children found out, he and his children fell apart, and were only reunited with Mita's help. This also led to him being sacked from his prestigious job as a section chief in a reputable company. On the other hand, after this incident, he realized that his children were very important part of him.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shiori_Kutsuna" title="Shiori Kutsuna">Shiori Kutsuna</a> as Yui Asuda <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">阿須田 結</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 17 years old)</span></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The eldest child in the family. She is a second-year high school student, and is a member of her school's photography club. She blamed for being the cause of her mother's misery, because her parents were forced to marry due to the fact that Nagiko was pregnant with her. Yui nearly committed suicide after discovering that her boyfriend was actually toying with her. She even asked Mita to kill herself, though Mita was stopped in time. Yui later acted as a mother to the rest of the family, helping out with housework and making important decisions for them. Yui's name implies her role in the family - the role of being the one who bonds the family.</dd>

She later confessed her love for Keiichi, though knowing that the love was ill-fated, she married someone whom she had met at a marriage meeting. However, thanks to Mita, the Asuda family realized that she was an important member of the family, and she actually wards off the family's bad luck. In the end, the Asuda family choose Urara over Mita to be their mother (which was Mita's plan) and removed Urara from her wedding ceremony. However, he soften his attitude towards the family after Mita pretended to be Nagiko's ghost at the Asuda family's orders. </dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaseifu_no_Mita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Kaseifu no Mita</i> was first announced on 12 August 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110813_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110813-7">[7]</a></sup> It was announced that <a href="/wiki/Nanako_Matsushima" title="Nanako Matsushima">Nanako Matsushima</a> will be the lead actress in the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110813_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110813-7">[7]</a></sup> This is her first role in a television drama series after a two-year hiatus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110813_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110813-7">[7]</a></sup> Nanako said that she was familiar with the scriptwriter <i>Kaseifu no Mita</i>, since they had collaborated on drama series like <i><a href="/wiki/Great_Teacher_Onizuka" title="Great Teacher Onizuka">Great Teacher Onizuka</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Majo_no_J%C5%8Dken" title="Majo no Jōken">Majo no Jōken</a></i> (which are Nanako's signature works), though this was the first time they had collaborated on a family drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110813_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110813-7">[7]</a></sup> She added that she was "happy to challenge a new genre."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110813_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110813-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast">Broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaseifu_no_Mita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Kaseifu no Mita</i> was aired in the Nippon Television's <i>Tears Wednesday</i> drama time slot, which is aired every Wednesday, 10 to 10:54pm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110813_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110813-7">[7]</a></sup> Due to the popularity of this series, episodes 9, 10 and 11 all had an extended broadcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nikkan201112072_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nikkan201112072-8">[8]</a></sup> Before the final episode of the series was shown, an hour-long special program featuring special behind-the-scenes footage of <i>Kaseifu no Mita</i> was broadcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nikkan201112072_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-nikkan201112072-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scriptwriter Kazuhiko Yukawa announced that there will be no more sequels to this series, despite its popularity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nikkan20111207_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-nikkan20111207-6">[6]</a></sup> He said that he wanted "to leave [the remainder] to the viewers' imaginations".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nikkan20111207_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-nikkan20111207-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaseifu_no_Mita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaseifu no Mita was extremely well received by the Japanese television viewers. It garnered an average viewership rating of 25.17%, the highest of any 2011 Japanese television dramas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artv2011_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-artv2011-9">[9]</a></sup> In addition, the "Mita" effect is attributed as one of the main factors that allowed broadcaster <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">Nippon Television</a> to become the top broadcaster in Japan in 2011, beating the previous record-holder <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Television" title="Fuji Television">Fuji Television</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20120102_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20120102-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In particular, its last episode achieved the highest viewership rating of 40.0%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20111222_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20111222-12">[12]</a></sup> At one point in the broadcast, the viewership ratings hit 42.8% in the <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō region</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20111222_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20111222-12">[12]</a></sup> This makes this episode the second-most watched Japanese television program of 2011, after <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a>'s 62nd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kouhaku_Uta_Gassen" title="Kouhaku Uta Gassen">Kouhaku Uta Gassen</a> program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mantan20120102_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mantan20120102-4">[4]</a></sup> The episode also ranks as the third-most viewed in the history of Japanese television dramas, excluding NHK's "Asadora" and "Taiga" dramas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20111222_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20111222-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> noted that previously, viewership ratings of 30% were considered as the norm, but in modern days, viewership ratings of 40% can be considered "divine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20111222_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20111222-12">[12]</a></sup> Additionally, this episode achieved viewership ratings of 36.4% and 34.6% in the <a href="/wiki/Kansai_region" title="Kansai region">Kansai region</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a> respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sankei20111222_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-sankei20111222-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theme_song">Theme song</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaseifu_no_Mita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Theme song">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The theme song for the television drama <i>Kaseifu no Mita</i> is <i>Yasashiku Naritai</i> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kazuyoshi_Saito&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kazuyoshi Saito (page does not exist)">Kazuyoshi Saito</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110929_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110929-16">[16]</a></sup> This was revealed in an announcement made on September 29, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110929_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110929-16">[16]</a></sup> <i>Yasashiku Naritai</i> is the first song Kazuyoshi has provided for a Japanese television drama since the 2009 NHK drama <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Keiji_no_Genba_2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Keiji no Genba 2 (page does not exist)">Keiji no Genba 2</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110929_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110929-16">[16]</a></sup> Main leads from <i>Kaseifu no Mita</i> were also featured in the song's <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20110929_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20110929-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song later became Kazuyoshi's 39th <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, and this single album was released in Japan by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victor_Entertainment" title="Victor Entertainment">Victor Entertainment</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> on November 2, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20111227_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20111227-18">[18]</a></sup> The single debuted at the 6th position on the Oricon TOP10 weekly charts, with its sales boosted by the good reception of <i>Kaseifu no Mita</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20111227_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20111227-18">[18]</a></sup> It sold 70 thousand copies as of December 27, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon20111227_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-oricon20111227-18">[18]</a></sup> <i>Yasashiku Naritai</i> is also the winner of the "Best Theme Song" category at the 71st Television Drama Academy Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dramaaward_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-dramaaward-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
