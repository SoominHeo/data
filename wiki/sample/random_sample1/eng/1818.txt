<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Working title</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>A <b>working title</b>, sometimes called a <b>production title</b>, is the temporary <a href="/wiki/Title_(publishing)" title="Title (publishing)">title</a> of a product or project used during its development, usually used in <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">filmmaking</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_production" title="Television production">television production</a>, <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">novel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Video_game_development" title="Video game development">video game development</a>, or music <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">Purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_title&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Working titles are used primarily for two reasons – the first being that an official <a href="/wiki/Title_(publishing)" title="Title (publishing)">title</a> has not yet been decided upon, with the working title being used purely for identification purposes, and the second being a ruse to intentionally disguise the real nature of a project.</p>
<p>Examples of the former include the film <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Hard_with_a_Vengeance" title="Die Hard with a Vengeance">Die Hard with a Vengeance</a></i>, which was produced under the title <i>Die Hard: New York</i> and the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> films, which are commonly produced under titles such as <i>Bond 22</i> until an official title is decided upon.</p>
<p>Examples of the latter include <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_World" title="Jurassic World">Jurassic World</a></i>, produced under <i>Ebb Tide</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Jedi" title="Return of the Jedi">Return of the Jedi</a></i>, which was produced under the title <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Harvest" title="Blue Harvest">Blue Harvest</a></i>; 2009's <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_(film)" title="Star Trek (film)">Star Trek</a></i> which was produced under the title <i>Corporate Headquarters</i>; and the <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a> films <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Returns" title="Batman Returns">Batman Returns</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Begins" title="Batman Begins">Batman Begins</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film)" title="The Dark Knight (film)">The Dark Knight</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="The Dark Knight Rises">The Dark Knight Rises</a></i>, which were produced under the titles <i>Dictel</i>, <i>The Intimidation Game</i>, <i>Rory's First Kiss</i> and <i>Magnus Rex</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> respectively.</p>
<p>In some cases a working title may ultimately be used as the official title, as in the case of the films <i><a href="/wiki/Cloverfield" title="Cloverfield">Cloverfield</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Project_X_(2012_film)" title="Project X (2012 film)">Project X</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/High_School_Musical" title="High School Musical">High School Musical</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Snakes_on_a_Plane" title="Snakes on a Plane">Snakes on a Plane</a></i> (at the insistence of leading man, <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a>, who joked that he took the role for the working title alone, after he learned the title was going to be changed to <i>Pacific Air Flight 121</i> upon release), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_show" title="Television show">television shows</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mindy_Project" title="The Mindy Project">The Mindy Project</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cleveland_Show" title="The Cleveland Show">The Cleveland Show</a></i>, and video games <i><a href="/wiki/Quake_II" title="Quake II">Quake II</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Spore_(2008_video_game)" title="Spore (2008 video game)">Spore</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Hill:_Origins" title="Silent Hill: Origins">Silent Hill: Origins</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Epic_Mickey" title="Epic Mickey">Epic Mickey</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Title_ruse">Title ruse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_title&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Title ruse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A title ruse is a practice by which a high-profile film or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> is given a fake working title to keep its production a secret, and to prevent <a href="/wiki/Price_gouging" title="Price gouging">price gouging</a> by suppliers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> casual theft and undesirable attention.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Purchase_order" title="Purchase order">Purchase orders</a> from vendors, outdoor signs, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Videocassette" title="Videocassette">videocassettes</a> and <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> labels will use the cover title of a film.</p>
