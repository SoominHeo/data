<p><b>Family law</b> (also called <b>matrimonial law</b>) is an area of the <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">law</a> that deals with <a href="/wiki/Family" title="Family">family</a> matters and <a href="/wiki/Domestic_relations" title="Domestic relations">domestic relations</a>, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civil_union" title="Civil union">civil unions</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Domestic_partnership" title="Domestic partnership">domestic partnerships</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adoption" title="Adoption">adoption</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surrogacy" title="Surrogacy">surrogacy</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Child_abuse" title="Child abuse">child abuse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Child_abduction" title="Child abduction">child abduction</a>;</li>
<li>the termination of relationships and ancillary matters, including <a href="/wiki/Divorce" title="Divorce">divorce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annulment" title="Annulment">annulment</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Property_settlement" title="Property settlement">property settlements</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alimony" title="Alimony">alimony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Child_custody" title="Child custody">child custody</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contact_(law)" title="Contact (law)">visitation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Child_support" title="Child support">child support</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alimony" title="Alimony">alimony</a> awards;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adjudication#Referring_to_a_minor" title="Adjudication">juvenile adjudication</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/DNA_paternity_testing" title="DNA paternity testing">paternity testing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paternity_fraud" title="Paternity fraud">paternity fraud</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>This list is not exhaustive and varies depending on <a href="/wiki/Jurisdiction" title="Jurisdiction">jurisdiction</a>. In many jurisdictions in the United States, the family <a href="/wiki/Court" title="Court">courts</a> see the most crowded <a href="/wiki/Docket_(court)" title="Docket (court)">dockets</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Litigant" title="Litigant">Litigants</a> representative of all <a href="/wiki/Social_class" title="Social class">social</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic_class" title="Economic class">economic classes</a> are parties within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judicial_system" title="Judicial system">system</a>.</p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/Conflict_of_laws" title="Conflict of laws">conflict of laws</a> elements dealing with transnational and interstate issues, see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marriage_(conflict)" title="Marriage (conflict)">marriage (conflict)</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Divorce_(conflict)" title="Divorce (conflict)">divorce (conflict)</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nullity_(conflict)" title="Nullity (conflict)">nullity (conflict)</a>.</p>
