<p><b>Ghaziabad</b> (<small>Hindustani pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Hindi_and_Urdu" title="Help:IPA for Hindi and Urdu">[ɣaːziːaːbaːd̪]</a></span>) is a city in the <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>. It is sometimes referred to as the "Gateway of UP" because it is close to <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a>, on the main route into <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Capital_Region" title="National Capital Region">National Capital Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is a large and planned industrial city, well connected by roads and railways, and is the administrative headquarters of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghaziabad_district,_India" title="Ghaziabad district, India">Ghaziabad District</a> as well as being the primary commercial, industrial and educational centre of <a href="/wiki/Western_Uttar_Pradesh" title="Western Uttar Pradesh">western Uttar Pradesh</a> and a major rail junction for <a href="/wiki/North_India" title="North India">North India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Recent construction works have led to the city being described by a City Mayors Foundation survey as the second fastest-growing in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Situated in the Upper Gangetic Plains, the city has two major divisions separated by the <a href="/wiki/Hindon_River" title="Hindon River">Hindon River</a>, namely: Trans-Hindon on the west and Cis-Hindon on the east side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GNN_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GNN-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Excavations carried out at the mound of Kaseri, at the banks of river <a href="/wiki/Hindon_River" title="Hindon River">Hindon</a>, some 2 km north of Mohan Nagar, have shown that civilisation existed there as early as 2500 BC. Mythologically, some neighbouring towns and villages of the city including <a href="/wiki/Garhmukteshwar" title="Garhmukteshwar">Garhmukteshwar</a>, Pooth Village and Ahar region have been associated with the <i><a href="/wiki/Mahabharata" title="Mahabharata">Mahabharata</a></i> and the fort at <a href="/wiki/Loni,_Ghaziabad" title="Loni, Ghaziabad">Loni</a>, is associated with the legend of Lavanasura of the <a href="/wiki/Ramayana" title="Ramayana">Ramayana</a> period. According to the Gazetteer, the fort, "Loni" is named after Lavanasura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ghaziabad_Nagar_Nigam_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ghaziabad_Nagar_Nigam-8">[8]</a></sup> The city and its surrounding region have historically witnessed major wars and battles over the last many centuries. In AD 1313, the entire region including present day Ghaziabad became a huge battlefield, when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taimur" title="Taimur">Taimur</a> laid siege on the area during <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_bin_Tughluq" title="Muhammad bin Tughluq">Muhammad bin Tughluq</a>'s reign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ghaziabad_Nagar_Nigam_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ghaziabad_Nagar_Nigam-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> During the Anglo- Maratha War, Sir General Lake and the Royal Maratha army fought here circa. Altama Religion was started from Ghaziabad in 1803.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ghaziabad_Nagar_Nigam_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ghaziabad_Nagar_Nigam-8">[8]</a></sup> The name "Ghaziuddinnagar" was shortened to its present form, i.e. "Ghaziabad" with the opening of the Railways in 1864.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nagarnigamghaziabad.com_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-nagarnigamghaziabad.com-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Establishment of the Scientific Society here, during the same period is considered as a milestone of the educational movement launched by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Syed_Ahmad_Khan" title="Sir Syed Ahmad Khan">Sir Syed Ahmad Khan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The Ghaziabad Municipality came into existence in 1868.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Sind, Punjab and Delhi Railway, connecting <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a>, up till <a href="/wiki/Ambala" title="Ambala">Ambala</a> through Ghaziabad was opened in the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> With the completion of the <a href="/wiki/Amritsar" title="Amritsar">Amritsar</a>-<a href="/wiki/Saharanpur" title="Saharanpur">Saharanpur</a>-Ghaziabad line of the Sind, Punjab and Delhi Railway in 1870, <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> was connected to <a href="/wiki/Multan" title="Multan">Multan</a> through Ghaziabad, and Ghaziabad became the junction of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Indian_Railway" title="East Indian Railway">East Indian Railway</a> and Sind, Punjab and Delhi Railway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city of Ghaziabad was founded in AD 1740 by Wazir Ghazi-ud-din, who named it Ghaziuddinnagar after himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nagarnigamghaziabad.com_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-nagarnigamghaziabad.com-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-History-16">[16]</a></sup> During the Mughal period, Ghaziabad and especially the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Hindon_River" title="Hindon River">Hindon</a> in Ghaziabad, remained a picnic spot for the Mughal royal family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ghaziabad, along with <a href="/wiki/Meerut" title="Meerut">Meerut</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bulandshahr" title="Bulandshahr">Bulandshahr</a>, remained one of the three Munsifis of the District, under the Meerut Civil Judgeship during most periods of the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ghaziabad was associated with the <a href="/wiki/Indian_independence_movement" title="Indian independence movement">Indian independence movement</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857" title="Indian Rebellion of 1857">Indian Rebellion of 1857</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nagarnigamghaziabad.com_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-nagarnigamghaziabad.com-10">[10]</a></sup> During that rebellion, there were fierce clashes between the British forces and Indian rebel <a href="/wiki/Sepoy" title="Sepoy">sepoys</a> on the banks of the Hindon, and the rebels checked the advancing British forces coming from Meerut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_growth">Industrial growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Industrial growth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although connected by railway since 1865, it was not until 1940 that the first modern industry appeared in Ghaziabad. However, it was in the post-independence period that industry really expanded, with a further 22 factories opening in the four years after 1947. This development can be attributed to the influx of people from the newly formed Pakistan and the relocation of businesses from what was now the Pakistani province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punjab_(Pakistan)" title="Punjab (Pakistan)">Punjab</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> John Oakey and Mohan Ltd., one of India's largest concerns manufacturing coated and bonded abrasives, and originally functioning under the name of 'National Abrasives' at <a href="/wiki/Rawalpindi" title="Rawalpindi">Rawalpindi</a> was shifted here under the proprietorship of 'Dyer Meakins' in 1947.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Subsequently, the <a href="/wiki/Mohan_Meakin" title="Mohan Meakin">Mohan Meakin</a> breweries were also set up in the year 1949.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> This period also saw the development of Ghaziabad as one of India's most famous centres of the Oil Engines industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1967, the municipal limits were extended up to the Delhi-UP border. Starting early 1970s, a large number of steel manufacturing units also came up in the city making it one of the primary industries of the city. This period also saw the emergence of the Electronics industry, with the setting up of <a href="/wiki/Bharat_Electronics_Limited" title="Bharat Electronics Limited">Bharat Electronics Limited</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Electronics_Limited" title="Central Electronics Limited">Central Electronics Limited</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Over the years, planned Industrial development saw participation from major industrial houses of the country including Mohans (Mohan Nagar Industrial Estate, 1949), Tatas (Tata Oil Mills), Modis (Modinagar, 1933; International Tobacco Co. 1967), Shri Rams (Shri Ram Pistons, 1964), Jaipurias etc. and also significant participation through foreign capital in concerns such as Danfoss India Ltd. (estd. 1968); Indo- Bulgar Food Ltd. and International Tobacco Company (estd. 1967).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ghaziabad, the headquarters of the district of the same name that was established in 1976, lies on the Grand Trunk road about a mile east of the Hindon river in Lat. 280 40' North and Long. 770 25' East, 19 km. east of Delhi and 46 km. south-west of Meerut with which it is connected by a metalled road. Other roads lead west to Loni and Baghpat and east to Hapur and Garhmukteshwar. Buses run at frequent intervals from here to Delhi, Meerut, Aligarh, Bulandshahr, Moradabad, Lucknow and to other districts also. It is an important station on the Northern Railway where railway lines, from Delhi to Calcutta, Moradabad and Saharanpur meet, connecting it with many important cities of India.</p>
<p>Bulandshahr and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gautambudh_Nagar_district" title="Gautambudh Nagar district">Gautambudh Nagar</a>, on the south-west by <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> and on the east by the newly-formed district of <a href="/wiki/Hapur" title="Hapur">Hapur</a>. As its boundary is adjacent to Delhi, it acts as the main entrance to Uttar Pradesh and hence is also called the Gateway of Uttar Pradesh.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The provisional data derived from the 2011 census shows that Ghaziabad urban agglomeration had a population of 4,681,452, of which males were 2,479,710 and females were 2,201,742. The literacy rate was 93.81%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Ghaziabad is a subcategory B1 district of category B i.e. having socioeconomic parameters below the national average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It is the second largest industrial city in <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a> after <a href="/wiki/Kanpur" title="Kanpur">Kanpur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghaziabad1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-ghaziabad1-27">[27]</a></sup> Brahmins, Jats and <a href="/wiki/Tyagi" title="Tyagi">Tyagis</a> have 1.2 lakh, 60,000 and 70,000 votes respectively. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gujjar" title="Gujjar">Gujjars</a> have close to 10000 votes in the constituency. <a href="/wiki/Yadav" title="Yadav">Yadavs</a> have over 70000 votes. Thakurs has over 360,000 votes. Goel's Vaishya community has close to 2 lakh votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Among the significant areas within the city are Ghanta Ghar, Bazaria, Chopla Mandir, Maliwara, Model Town, Gandhi Nagar, Nehru Nagar, Kavi Nagar, Raj Nagar, Shastri Nagar, Sanjay Nagar, Ambedkar Road, Navyug Market, Delhi Gate, Goel Market, Mukand Nagar, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sarai_Nazar_Ali&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sarai Nazar Ali (page does not exist)">Sarai Nazar Ali</a>, Rajendra Nagar, <a href="/wiki/Shalimar_Garden,_Ghaziabad" title="Shalimar Garden, Ghaziabad">Shalimar Garden</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Turab_Nagar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Turab Nagar (page does not exist)">Turab Nagar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indirapuram" title="Indirapuram">Indirapuram</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raj_Nagar_Extension" title="Raj Nagar Extension">Raj Nagar Extension</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loni,_India" title="Loni, India">Loni, India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crossings_Republik" title="Crossings Republik">Crossings Republik</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As it is connected to the national capital, its temperature and rainfall are similar to Delhi. <a href="/wiki/Rajasthan" title="Rajasthan">Rajasthan</a>'s dust storms and snowfall in the <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kumaon_division" title="Kumaon division">Kumaon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Garhwal_Himalaya" title="Garhwal Himalaya">Garhwal</a> hills name their impact in the weather regularly. The monsoon arrives in the district during the end of the June or the first week of July and normally it rains until October. As in other districts of northern India mainly three seasons - summer, winter and rainy - prevail here, but sometimes due to severe snowfall in the Himalayas and Kumaon Hills, adverse weather can also be seen.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_estate_hub">Real estate hub</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Real estate hub">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A proposal has been made to widen <a href="/wiki/National_Highway_24_(India)" title="National Highway 24 (India)">National Highway 24</a> (NH-24) from four to sixteen lanes on the stretch between the Ghaziabad-Delhi border and Dasna. As a large number of residential and commercial projects are coming up along the highway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Some major developers which have invested heavily on NH-24 are SARE, Mahagun, Antriksh, <a href="/wiki/Crossings_Republik" title="Crossings Republik">Crossings Republik</a>, Ansal, Wave etc. Places on NH-24 and <a href="/wiki/National_Highway_58_(India)" title="National Highway 58 (India)">National Highway 58</a> are flourishing as residential options to the capital because of their proximity to Delhi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Delhi_Metro">Delhi Metro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Delhi Metro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Metro" title="Delhi Metro">Delhi Metro</a> extends to Dilshad Garden station which is situated at the Apsara Border. At present, it serves the areas of <a href="/wiki/Shalimar_Garden,_Ghaziabad" title="Shalimar Garden, Ghaziabad">Shalimar Garden</a>, Rajendra Nagar and other neighbouring colonies. This line will be extended to New Bus Stand, Ghaziabad by 2016-17. Work for the same already started in December 2014. Another station exists at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vaishali_(Delhi_Metro)" title="Vaishali (Delhi Metro)">Vaishali</a>, which serves that area as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasundhara" title="Vasundhara">Vasundhara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indirapuram" title="Indirapuram">Indirapuram</a>, and there is also a station at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaushambi_(Ghaziabad)" title="Kaushambi (Ghaziabad)">Kaushambi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
