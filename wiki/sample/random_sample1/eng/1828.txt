<p><b>Katashina</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">片品村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Katashina-mura</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_villages_in_Japan" title="List of villages in Japan">village</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Tone_District,_Gunma" title="Tone District, Gunma">Tone District</a> of northern <a href="/wiki/Gunma_Prefecture" title="Gunma Prefecture">Gunma Prefecture</a> in the northern <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of February 2015, the village had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 4,458 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 11.4 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. Its total area was 391.76 km<sup>2</sup>. Much of the village is within the borders of <a href="/wiki/Oze_National_Park" title="Oze National Park">Oze National Park</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katashina,_Gunma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located in northern Gunma, Katashina is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Tochigi_Prefecture" title="Tochigi Prefecture">Tochigi Prefecture</a> to the east and <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Prefecture" title="Fukushima Prefecture">Fukushima Prefecture</a> to the northeast. The village is very mountainous, with the highest elevation being 2578m and the lowest 640m. The temperature can reach up to 36°C in the summer, but in the winter, it can drop to -18°C. The average temperature is 11°C, the yearly precipitation is 1042 mm, and amount of snowfall is 95 cm, with the snowy period lasting for 120 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Mountains: <a href="/wiki/Mount_Nikk%C5%8D-Shirane" title="Mount Nikkō-Shirane">Mount Nikkō-Shirane</a> (2578m), Mount Shibutsu (2228m), Mount Hotaka(2158m)</li>
<li>Rivers: Katashina River, Ōtaki River</li>
<li>Lakes: Marunuma (Maru Swamp), Suganuma</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katashina,_Gunma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Kawabe was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%8D" title="Tenryō">tenryō</a> holdings within <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dzuke_Province" title="Kōzuke Province">Kōzuke Province</a> administered directly by the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. On April 1, 1889 with the creation of the municipalities system after the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, Katashina village was established within <a href="/wiki/Tone_District,_Gunma" title="Tone District, Gunma">Tone District, Gunma</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katashina,_Gunma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Katashina is heavily dependent on seasonal tourism to ski resorts and to <a href="/wiki/Onsen" title="Onsen">onsen</a> hot springs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katashina,_Gunma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Katashina has four elementary schools (Minami-sho, Kata-sho, Kita-sho, Hotaka-sho) and one middle school.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katashina,_Gunma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Katashina is not served by any railway lines.</p>
