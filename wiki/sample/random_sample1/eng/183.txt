<p>A <b>referendum on the return of King <a href="/wiki/Constantine_I_of_Greece" title="Constantine I of Greece">Constantine I</a></b> was held in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> on 22 November 1920.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NS-1">[1]</a></sup> It followed the death of his son, King <a href="/wiki/Alexander_of_Greece" title="Alexander of Greece">Alexander</a>. The proposal was approved by 99.0% of voters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The result ensured and affirmed the dominance of the anti-<a href="/wiki/Venizelism" title="Venizelism">Venizelist</a> camp in the country. Constantine returned, albeit questioned by the supporters of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(Greece)" title="Liberal Party (Greece)">Liberal Party</a>, while Liberal leader <a href="/wiki/Eleftherios_Venizelos" title="Eleftherios Venizelos">Eleftherios Venizelos</a> maintained his silence, being in voluntary exile abroad.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Constantine_I_of_Greece" title="Constantine I of Greece">Constantine I</a> was opposed by the Entente powers (<a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>) because of his pro-German stance during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> (see <a href="/wiki/National_Schism" title="National Schism">National Schism</a>) and his enthusiastic return was short-lived as a result of the disastrous military events that followed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor_Campaign" title="Asia Minor Campaign">Asia Minor Campaign</a> of 1922.</p>
