<p>The <b>Roman Catholic Diocese of Oslo</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Osloënsis</i></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Exemption_(church)" title="Exemption (church)">exempt</a> <a href="/wiki/Diocese" title="Diocese">diocese</a> located in the city of <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parishes">Parishes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Oslo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Parishes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The territory is divided into 25 parishes, located in the following sites: <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> (3), <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">Moss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Askim" title="Askim">Askim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fredrikstad" title="Fredrikstad">Fredrikstad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halden" title="Halden">Halden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lillestr%C3%B8m" title="Lillestrøm">Lillestrøm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamar" title="Hamar">Hamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kongsvinger" title="Kongsvinger">Kongsvinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer" title="Lillehammer">Lillehammer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jessheim" title="Jessheim">Jessheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B8nefoss" title="Hønefoss">Hønefoss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stabekk" title="Stabekk">Stabekk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drammen" title="Drammen">Drammen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fagernes" title="Fagernes">Fagernes</a>, <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B8nsberg" title="Tønsberg">Tønsberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Larvik" title="Larvik">Larvik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandefjord" title="Sandefjord">Sandefjord</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porsgrunn" title="Porsgrunn">Porsgrunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arendal" title="Arendal">Arendal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kristiansand" title="Kristiansand">Kristiansand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stavanger" title="Stavanger">Stavanger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haugesund" title="Haugesund">Haugesund</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bergen" title="Bergen">Bergen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Oslo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By 1070? the see was established as the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Oslo" title="Diocese of Oslo">Diocese of Oslo</a>, and the bishop was seated at <a href="/wiki/St._Hallvard%27s_Cathedral" title="St. Hallvard's Cathedral">St. Hallvard's Cathedral</a>. In 1537 - in the course of the Lutheran <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reformation_in_Denmark-Norway_and_Holstein" title="Reformation in Denmark-Norway and Holstein">Reformation in Denmark-Norway and Holstein</a> - <a href="/wiki/Christian_III_of_Denmark" title="Christian III of Denmark">Christian III of Denmark</a> suppressed the Catholic episcopates at the Norwegian sees. Thereafter <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheranism</a> prevailed in Scandinavia.</p>
<p>In 1582 the stray Catholics in Norway and elsewhere in Northern Europe were placed under the jurisdiction of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papal_nuncio" title="Papal nuncio">papal nuncio</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congregation_de_propaganda_fide" title="Congregation de propaganda fide">Congregation de propaganda fide</a>, on its establishment in 1622, took charge of the vast missionary field, which - at its third session - it divided among the nuncio of <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> (for the Catholics in Denmark and Norway), the <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_Nuncio_to_Cologne" title="Apostolic Nuncio to Cologne">nuncio at Cologne</a> (much of Northern Germany) and the <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_Nuncio_to_Poland" title="Apostolic Nuncio to Poland">nuncio to Poland</a> (Finland, Mecklenburg, and Sweden).</p>
<p>In 1688 Norway became part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vicariate_Apostolic_of_the_Northern_Missions" title="Vicariate Apostolic of the Northern Missions">Apostolic Vicariate of the Nordic Missions</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archdiocese_of_Paderborn" title="Archdiocese of Paderborn">Paderborn bishops</a> functioned as administrators of the apostolic vicariate. In 1834 the Catholic missions in Norway became part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apostolic_Vicariate_of_Sweden" title="Apostolic Vicariate of Sweden">Apostolic Vicariate of Sweden</a>, seated in the Swedish capital Stockholm. Whereas Norway north of the polar circle became the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Apostolic_Prefecture_of_the_North_Pole&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Apostolic Prefecture of the North Pole (page does not exist)">Apostolic Prefecture of the North Pole</a> in 1855, the rest of Norway stayed with the Swedish vicariate. When a new Catholic missionary jurisdiction was established, it was not at any of the ancient <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_see" title="Episcopal see">episcopal sees</a> but a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mission_%E2%80%9Csui_iuris%E2%80%9D" title="Mission “sui iuris”">mission “sui iuris”</a> on 7 August 1868, created out of parts of North Pole prefecture and the Norwegian part of the Swedish vicariate. On 17 August 1869 the mission became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apostolic_prefecture" title="Apostolic prefecture">apostolic prefecture</a> of Norway. On 11 March 1892 the Apostolic Prefecture of Norway was promoted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apostolic_Vicariate_of_Norway" title="Apostolic Vicariate of Norway">Apostolic Vicariate of Norway</a>, with an altered name as the <i>Apostolic Vicariate of Norway and <a href="/wiki/Spitsbergen" title="Spitsbergen">Spitsbergen</a></i> between 1 June 1913 and 15 December 1925.</p>
<p>On 10 April 1931 the apostolic vicariate was divided into the Apostolic Vicariate of Oslo (extant 1931–1953; comprising southern Norway), a Catholic jurisdiction for central Norway (called <i>Missionary District of Central Norway</i>, 1931–1935; <i>Apostolic Prefecture of Central Norway</i>, 1935–1953; <i>Apostolic Vicariate of Central Norway</i>, 1953–1979; <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Territorial_Prelature_of_Trondheim" title="Roman Catholic Territorial Prelature of Trondheim">Prelature of Trondheim</a> since), and a jurisdiction for Norway north of the polar circle (called <i>Missionary District of Northern Norway</i>, 1931–1944; <i>Apostolic Prefecture of Northern Norway</i>, 1944–1955; <i>Apostolic Vicariate of Northern Norway</i>, 1955–1979; <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Territorial_Prelature_of_Troms%C3%B8" title="Roman Catholic Territorial Prelature of Tromsø">Prelature of Tromsø</a> since). On 29 June 1953 the Apostolic Vicariate of Oslo became a separate Roman Catholic diocese, when the same status was given in Sweden to the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Stockholm" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Stockholm">Stockholm diocese</a>.</p>
<p>On 26 February 2015, Norwegian authorities levelled charges of fraud against the Roman Catholic Diocese of Oslo and Bishop <a href="/wiki/Bernt_Ivar_Eidsvig" title="Bernt Ivar Eidsvig">Bernt Ivar Eidsvig</a>. According to the charges, the diocese is under suspicion of registering people as members of the Roman Catholic Church in Norway without their knowing or consent and over the course of several years fraudulently claiming membership grants from the Norwegian government to the amount of 50 million kr.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In connection with the case, Norwegian police raided the offices of the diocese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
