<p><b>Gaafu Alifu</b> (also known as <b>Northern Huvadhu Atoll</b> or <b>Huvadhu Atholhu Uthuruburi</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_the_Maldives" title="Administrative divisions of the Maldives">administrative division</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a> created on February 8, 1962 when <a href="/wiki/Huvadhu_Atoll" title="Huvadhu Atoll">Huvadhu Atoll</a> was divided into two districts. <b>Gaafu Alifu</b> corresponds to the northeastern section of this large natural <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atoll</a> north of the line extending between the channels of Footukandu and Vaarulu Kandu. The capital of this district is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vilingili_(Gaafu_Alif_Atoll)" title="Vilingili (Gaafu Alif Atoll)">Vilingili</a>.</p>
<p><b>NOTE:</b> <i>Haa Alifu, Haa Dhaalu, Shaviyani, Noonu, Raa, Baa, Kaafu, etc. (including Gaafu Alif) are code letters assigned to the present administrative divisions of the Maldives. They are not the proper names of the <a href="/wiki/Atolls_of_the_Maldives" title="Atolls of the Maldives">natural atolls</a> that make up these divisions. Some atolls are divided into two administrative divisions while other divisions are made up of two or more natural atolls. The order followed by the code letters is from North to South, beginning with the first letters of the <a href="/wiki/Thaana" title="Thaana">Thaana</a> alphabet used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dhivehi_language" title="Dhivehi language">Dhivehi</a>. These code letters are not accurate from the geographical and cultural point of view. However, they have become popular among tourists and foreigners in the Maldives who find them easier to pronounce than the true atoll names in Dhivehi, (save a few exceptions, like Ari Atoll).</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gaafu_Alif_Atoll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some of the ancient kings of Maldives traced their ancestry to certain families of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dhevvadhoo_(Gaafu_Alif_Atoll)" title="Dhevvadhoo (Gaafu Alif Atoll)">Devvadu Island</a>, located in the centre of the large lagoon. There are many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhist" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a> archaeological remains in Devvadū, mainly low hills left by ruined <a href="/wiki/Stupa" title="Stupa">stupas</a>. None of these ancient remains have been properly investigated yet.</p>
<p>In January 1959, the three southern atolls: Huvadhu, <a href="/wiki/Fuvahmulah" title="Fuvahmulah">Fuvahmulah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Addu_Atoll" title="Addu Atoll">Addu</a>, were involved in setting up the breakaway <a href="/wiki/United_Suvadive_Republic" title="United Suvadive Republic">United Suvadive Republic</a> which survived until September 1963. The name of the secessionist state was taken from the ancient name of Huvadhu Atoll, "Suvadiva".</p>
