<p><i><b>Garfield: The Movie</b></i> is a 2004 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_film" title="Family film">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Hewitt_(film_director)" title="Peter Hewitt (film director)">Peter Hewitt</a> inspired by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Davis_(cartoonist)" title="Jim Davis (cartoonist)">Jim Davis</a>' <a href="/wiki/Garfield" title="Garfield">comic strip of the same name</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Breckin_Meyer" title="Breckin Meyer">Breckin Meyer</a> as <a href="/wiki/Jon_Arbuckle" title="Jon Arbuckle">Jon Arbuckle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Love_Hewitt" title="Jennifer Love Hewitt">Jennifer Love Hewitt</a> as Dr. Liz Wilson, and features <a href="/wiki/Bill_Murray" title="Bill Murray">Bill Murray</a> as the voice of Garfield. Garfield the cat was created with <a href="/wiki/Computer_animation" title="Computer animation">computer animation</a>, though all other animals were real. The film was produced by <a href="/wiki/Davis_Entertainment" title="Davis Entertainment">Davis Entertainment Company</a> and distributed by <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>. The film shares several similarities to the 1982 animated special <i><a href="/wiki/Here_Comes_Garfield" title="Here Comes Garfield">Here Comes Garfield</a></i>. <i>Garfield: The Movie</i> earned $200.8 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-1">[1]</a></sup> on a $50 million budget. The film was released in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on June 11, 2004. A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Garfield:_A_Tail_of_Two_Kitties" title="Garfield: A Tail of Two Kitties">Garfield: A Tail of Two Kitties</a></i>, was released in June 2006.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garfield:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Garfield_(character)" title="Garfield (character)">Garfield</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Obesity" title="Obesity">fat</a> and free-spirited orange cat who lives with his owner, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Arbuckle" title="Jon Arbuckle">Jon Arbuckle</a>. Garfield passes his time by antagonizing Jon and teasing an aggressive neighbor <a href="/wiki/Doberman_Pinscher" title="Doberman Pinscher">Doberman Pinscher</a>, Luca. Aside from Jon, Garfield maintains an unlikely friendship with a helpful <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mouse</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_(Garfield)" title="Louis (Garfield)">Louis</a>. He also socializes with his fellow neighborhood cats, including Garfield's stooge <a href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Garfield_franchise#Nermal" title="List of characters in the Garfield franchise">Nermal</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Garfield_franchise#Arlene" title="List of characters in the Garfield franchise">Arlene</a>.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, a local television host, Happy Chapman, known for his cat "Persnikitty" is introduced as supposedly a happy man. In reality he is allergic to cats, jealous of his brother Walter J. Chapman, a news reporter, and to be more successful by performing on TV show <a href="/wiki/Good_Day_New_York" title="Good Day New York">Good Day New York</a>. Jon has made a habit of bringing Garfield to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veterinarian" title="Veterinarian">veterinarian</a>, in-order to see vet Dr. Liz Wilson. Jon tries to ask her out, but due to a misunderstanding, he is given custody of a stray dog, <a href="/wiki/Odie" title="Odie">Odie</a>. Regardless, Jon and Liz begin dating. Garfield is angry at having to share the house with a dog, of whom Jon grows fond. Odie is brought to a canine talent show, where Liz is a judge. Garfield gets involved in an altercation there with other animals, which moves Odie to the center of the ring, where he begins dancing to "<a href="/wiki/Hey_Mama_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Hey Mama (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Hey Mama</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">the Black Eyed Peas</a>.</p>
<p>His <a href="/wiki/Improvisation" title="Improvisation">improvised</a> performance is a hit. Happy Chapman, who also is a judge of the dog show is impressed with Odie, and offers Jon a television deal for Odie, but Jon declines. After Garfield causes a mess inside Jon's house out of jealousy, Jon has no other choice but to punish Garfield by finally forcing him to sleep outside for one night. Odie comes out to comfort Garfield but Garfield locks Odie out. Nermal and Arlene witness this as Odie runs away; he is then picked up by an elderly woman named Mrs. Baker. Jon searches with Liz for Odie while the neighborhood animals treat Garfield as an outcast. Meanwhile, Chapman and his assistant Wendell find a notice Mrs. Baker created of Odie and, recognizing the lucrative possibilities, claim Odie as Happy's own.</p>
<p>When Garfield sees Odie on television and hears Chapman announce he and Odie are going to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, Garfield sets out to rescue Odie. Jon discovers Garfield missing so Jon and Liz start searching. Garfield gets into the broadcast tower via the air vents but he is blown around violently. Garfield finds Odie locked in a room; Chapman enters and secures a shock collar to Odie, which, when activated, releases an electric discharge that forces him to perform tricks.</p>
<p>Chapman heads for the train station with Garfield in close pursuit. However, an animal control officer catches Garfield mistaking him as a runaway. Mrs. Baker tells Jon that Chapman took Odie, but Jon believes Garfield was taken too and he and Liz race to Telegraph Tower and then to the train station, after learning Chapman has left. Garfield is released from the pound by Chapman's abandoned feline star, Persnikitty, who is really named Sir Roland. Chapman boards a Texas-bound train, with Odie in the luggage car. Garfield arrives only to see the train depart. Garfield sneaks into the train system control room and rearranges some tracks, leading to an impending train wreck. Garfield hits an emergency control and causes Chapman/Odie's train to return to the station. Garfield frees Odie and they exit the train. However, Chapman chases them. Chapman threatens Odie with the shock collar, but is stopped by Garfield's friends and animals from the pound, led by Sir Roland. They swarm and attack Chapman, allowing Odie and Garfield to escape.</p>
<p>The shock collar is now on Chapman who gets shocked. Jon and Liz arrive to reclaim the animals and find Chapman disoriented. Jon punches Chapman in the face for stealing his pets, and leaves with Liz and the two animals. Chapman is arrested for his supposed involvement with the trains, as well as for abducting Odie. Garfield regains the respect of his animal friends as a hero. Back at home, Liz and Jon form a relationship, while Garfield learns a lesson about friendship.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garfield:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">directed</a> by Peter Hewitt, <a href="/wiki/Production_company" title="Production company">produced</a> by <a href="/wiki/Davis_Entertainment" title="Davis Entertainment">Davis Entertainment</a> for <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>, and stars <a href="/wiki/Breckin_Meyer" title="Breckin Meyer">Breckin Meyer</a> as <a href="/wiki/Jon_Arbuckle" title="Jon Arbuckle">Jon Arbuckle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Love_Hewitt" title="Jennifer Love Hewitt">Jennifer Love Hewitt</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Liz_Wilson" title="Dr. Liz Wilson">Dr. Liz Wilson</a>, and features <a href="/wiki/Bill_Murray" title="Bill Murray">Bill Murray</a> as the voice of <a href="/wiki/Garfield_(character)" title="Garfield (character)">Garfield</a>.</p>
<p>Filming was at several locations including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Union_Station" title="Los Angeles Union Station">Los Angeles Union Station</a> in downtown where the <a href="/wiki/Gold_Line_(Los_Angeles_Metro)" title="Gold Line (Los Angeles Metro)">Metro Gold Line</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Red_Line_(Los_Angeles_Metro)" title="Red Line (Los Angeles Metro)">Metro Red Line</a> as part of the metro's hub. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chuck_E_Cheese%27s" title="Chuck E Cheese's">Chuck E Cheese's</a> is mentioned in the film when Garfield leaves to go to the <a href="/wiki/Veterinary_physician" title="Veterinary physician">vet</a> while <a href="/wiki/Wendy%27s" title="Wendy's">Wendy's</a> was mentioned and shown numerous times throughout the film.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jim_Carrey" title="Jim Carrey">Jim Carrey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Stiller" title="Ben Stiller">Ben Stiller</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Adam_Sandler" title="Adam Sandler">Adam Sandler</a> were considered for the role of Jon. All three of them were considered too expensive. <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Garner" title="Jennifer Garner">Jennifer Garner</a> was considered for the role of Liz, and <a href="/wiki/Brad_Dourif" title="Brad Dourif">Brad Dourif</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ironside" title="Michael Ironside">Michael Ironside</a> were considered to play Happy Chapman. Ironside was cast, but he dropped out after one day of filming for unknown reasons.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garfield:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Garfield: The Movie</i> was released by <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox_Home_Entertainment" title="20th Century Fox Home Entertainment">20th Century Fox Home Entertainment</a> on <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">VHS</a> and <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> on October 19, 2004.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The special features includes a behind-the-scenes footage, deleted scenes, and the <a href="/wiki/Baha_Men" title="Baha Men">Baha Men</a> music video "Holla!". The film was released on a 3-disc <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> on October 11, 2011.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It included an <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age_(franchise)" title="Ice Age (franchise)">Ice Age</a></i> short film, <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_Nutty" title="Gone Nutty">Gone Nutty</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garfield:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Baha_Men" title="Baha Men">Baha Men</a> performed the song "Holla!" for the film and its soundtrack. The music video premiered in early summer 2004 and featured clips from the film and gags showing obvious references to the <i>Garfield</i> franchise (such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lasagna" title="Lasagna">lasagna</a> jokes).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garfield:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garfield:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Garfield: The Movie</i> received generally negative reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> the film has an approval rating of 15% based on 135 reviews with an average rating of 3.5/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "When the novelty of the CGI <i>Garfield</i> wears off, what's left is a simplistic kiddie movie."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 27 out of 100 based on 31 critics, indicating "generally unfavorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Audiences polled by <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> gave the film an average grade of "B+" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CinemaScore_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CinemaScore-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> gave the film a "thumbs up", saying the movie was "charming".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Murray said in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/GQ" title="GQ">GQ</a></i> that he was fooled into playing the voice of Garfield for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gq_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-gq-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I thought it would be kind of fun, because doing a voice is challenging, and I'd never done that. Plus, I looked at the script, and it said, "So-and-so and Joel Coen." And I thought: Christ, well, I love those Coens! They're funny. So I sorta read a few pages of it and thought, Yeah, I'd like to do that...So they went off and shot the movie, and I forgot all about it. Finally, I went out to L.A. to record my lines. And usually when you're looping a movie, if it takes two days, that's a lot. I don't know if I should even tell this story, because it's kind of mean. [beat] What the hell? It's interesting. So I worked all day and kept going, "That's the line? Well, I can't say that." And you sit there and go, What can I say that will make this funny? And make it make sense? And I worked. I was exhausted, soaked with sweat, and the lines got worse and worse. And I said, "Okay, you better show me the whole rest of the movie, so we can see what we're dealing with." So I sat down and watched the whole thing, and I kept saying, "Who the hell cut this thing? Who did this? What the *bleep* was Coen thinking?" And then they explained it to me: It wasn't written by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joel_Coen" title="Joel Coen">that Joel Coen</a>.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Zombieland" title="Zombieland">Zombieland</a></i>, when Bill Murray (playing himself) is shot he is asked if he had any regrets before dying. He responds by saying "Garfield, maybe."</p>
