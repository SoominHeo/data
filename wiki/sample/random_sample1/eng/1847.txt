<p><b>Kanda Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">神田駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kanda-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in <a href="/wiki/Chiyoda,_Tokyo" title="Chiyoda, Tokyo">Chiyoda, Tokyo</a>, Japan. <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East) and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro" title="Tokyo Metro">Tokyo Metro</a> operate individual portions of the station.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanda_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kanda consists of two separate stations that form an interchange. The elevated station is operated by JR East and the underground station is operated by the Tokyo Metro. Although they are an interchange, passengers must pass through <a href="/wiki/Turnstile" title="Turnstile">ticket barriers</a> and pay separate fares to switch between services.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="JR_East_station">JR East station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanda_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: JR East station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The JR East station is the older of the two stations and opened in 1919. It is situated on an elevated viaduct and has three island platforms serving six tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-station_map_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-station_map-1">[1]</a></sup> The platforms are numbered sequentially from east to west starting with platform 1. <a href="/wiki/Yamanote_Line" title="Yamanote Line">Yamanote Line</a> trains use the inner platforms 2 and 3, <a href="/wiki/Keihin-T%C5%8Dhoku_Line" title="Keihin-Tōhoku Line">Keihin-Tōhoku Line</a> trains use platforms 1 and 4, and <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Line_(Rapid)" title="Chūō Line (Rapid)">Chūō Line</a> trains use platforms 5 and 6 as they split off from the main line north of Kanda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-station_map_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-station_map-1">[1]</a></sup> There are an additional two tracks east of the station; these are used for <a href="/wiki/Shinkansen" title="Shinkansen">Shinkansen</a> trains running between <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ueno_Station" title="Ueno Station">Ueno</a>.</p>
<p>There are two sets of entrances and exits (a total of four) that allow passengers to access the JR East station. The northern set, the north and east exits, offers a connection to the Ginza Line on the Tokyo Metro. The southern set, the south and west exits, has a View Plaza travel service centre. Both exits have rows of ticket machines, ticket gates, and a JR reservation office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-station_map_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-station_map-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tokyo_Metro">Tokyo Metro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanda_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tokyo Metro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Tokyo Metro station is the newer of the two station and opened in 1931 as part of an extension of first subway line in Asia, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ginza_Line" title="Ginza Line">Ginza Line</a>. There is a simple island platform setup with two tracks. Platform 1 is for southbound trains to <a href="/wiki/Ginza_Station" title="Ginza Station">Ginza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shibuya_Station" title="Shibuya Station">Shibuya</a> whilst platform 2 is used for northbound trains to <a href="/wiki/Ueno_Station" title="Ueno Station">Ueno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asakusa_Station_(Tokyo_Metro,_Toei,_Tobu)" title="Asakusa Station (Tokyo Metro, Toei, Tobu)">Asakusa</a>.</p>
<p>Access to the station is provided by a total of six entrances and exits. Exits and 1 and 2 are used as the connection to the JR East station and are on Chūō-dōri (中央通り). Exits 3 and 4 are on the same street but in the centre of the station near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kajich%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Kajichō, Tokyo">Kanda-Kajichō</a>. Exits 5 and 6 are at the northernmost part of the station.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanda_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station first opened on March 1, 1919 when the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Main_Line" title="Chūō Main Line">Chūō Main Line</a> was extended from <a href="/wiki/Manseibashi_Station" title="Manseibashi Station">Manseibashi Station</a>, which existed between Kanda and <a href="/wiki/Ochanomizu_Station" title="Ochanomizu Station">Ochanomizu</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tracks of <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Main_Line" title="Tōhoku Main Line">Tōhoku Main Line</a>, now used by trains on the <a href="/wiki/Keihin-T%C5%8Dhoku_Line" title="Keihin-Tōhoku Line">Keihin-Tōhoku Line</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Yamanote_Line" title="Yamanote Line">Yamanote Line</a>, extended from <a href="/wiki/Akihabara_Station" title="Akihabara Station">Akihabara Station</a> to Kanda and further to Tokyo on November 1, 1925.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This extension completed the loop of the <a href="/wiki/Yamanote_Line" title="Yamanote Line">Yamanote Line</a>.</p>
<p>The subway station opened on November 21, 1931. On this day, the subway closed the temporary terminal at <a href="/wiki/Manseibashi_Station" title="Manseibashi Station">Manseibashi Station</a> and made Kanda Station the new terminus. The station became an intermediate station on April 29, 1932 when the line was extended to <a href="/wiki/Mitsukoshimae_Station" title="Mitsukoshimae Station">Mitsukoshimae Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The extension through Kanda Station of the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Shinkansen" title="Tōhoku Shinkansen">Tōhoku Shinkansen</a> from its previous terminus at <a href="/wiki/Ueno_Station" title="Ueno Station">Ueno</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo</a> breached a pair of express tracks of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tohoku_Main_Line" title="Tohoku Main Line">Tohoku Main Line</a> through the station.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> These are being reinstated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_J%C5%ABkan_Line" title="Tōhoku Jūkan Line">Tōhoku Jūkan Line</a> project. The project was due to be completed in 2013 but was subsequently delayed until 2015 as a result of the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-interview-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annual07_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-annual07-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mainichi20131209_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-mainichi20131209-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>From March 14, 2015 onwards, Keihin-Tōhoku rapid services began serving the station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanda_Station_(Tokyo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
