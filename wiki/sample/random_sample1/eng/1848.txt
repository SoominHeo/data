<p><b>Gandalf</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Character_(arts)" title="Character (arts)">fictional character</a> and one of the protagonists in <a href="/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien" title="J. R. R. Tolkien">J. R. R. Tolkien</a>'s novels <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit" title="The Hobbit">The Hobbit</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="The Lord of the Rings">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>. He is a <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(Middle-earth)" title="Wizard (Middle-earth)">wizard</a>, member of the <i><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(Middle-earth)" title="Wizard (Middle-earth)">Istari</a></i> order, as well as leader of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellowship_of_the_Ring_(characters)" title="Fellowship of the Ring (characters)">Fellowship of the Ring</a> and the army of the West. In <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>, he is initially known as <b>Gandalf the Grey</b>, but returns from death as <b>Gandalf the White</b>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Emblema_Gandalf.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="220" data-file-width="200" height="187" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Emblema_Gandalf.svg/170px-Emblema_Gandalf.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Emblema_Gandalf.svg/255px-Emblema_Gandalf.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Emblema_Gandalf.svg/340px-Emblema_Gandalf.svg.png 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Emblema_Gandalf.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Cirth" title="Cirth">Certh</a> no. 19 "G", used by Gandalf as a personal <a href="/wiki/Sign" title="Sign">sign</a> or <a href="/wiki/Seal_(emblem)" title="Seal (emblem)">seal</a>.</div>

<p>Tolkien discusses Gandalf in his essay on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Istari" title="Istari">Istari</a>, which appears in the work <i><a href="/wiki/Unfinished_Tales" title="Unfinished Tales">Unfinished Tales</a></i>. He describes Gandalf as the last of the wizards to appear in <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a>, one who: "seemed the least, less tall than the others, and in looks more aged, grey-haired and grey-clad, and leaning on a <a href="/wiki/Magic_(Middle-earth)#Magic_items" title="Magic (Middle-earth)">staff</a>". Yet the <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">Elf</a> <a href="/wiki/C%C3%ADrdan" title="Círdan">Círdan</a> who met him on arrival nevertheless considered him "the greatest spirit and the wisest" and gave him the elven <a href="/wiki/Three_Rings" title="Three Rings">Ring of power</a> called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narya" title="Narya">Narya</a>, the Ring of Fire, containing a "red" stone for his aid and comfort. Tolkien explicitly links Gandalf to the element <a href="/wiki/Fire_(classical_element)" title="Fire (classical element)">Fire</a> later in the same essay:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Warm and eager was his spirit (and it was enhanced by the ring Narya), for he was the Enemy of <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a>, opposing the fire that devours and wastes with the fire that kindles, and succours in wanhope and distress; but his joy, and his swift wrath, were veiled in garments grey as ash, so that only those that knew him well glimpsed the flame that was within. Merry he could be, and kindly to the young and simple, yet quick at times to sharp speech and the rebuking of folly; but he was not proud, and sought neither power nor praise... Mostly he journeyed tirelessly on foot, leaning on a staff, and so he was called among <a href="/wiki/Man_(Middle-earth)" title="Man (Middle-earth)">Men</a> of the North Gandalf 'the <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">Elf</a> of the Wand'. For they deemed him (though in error) to be of Elven-kind, since he would at times work wonders among them, loving especially the beauty of fire; and yet such marvels he wrought mostly for mirth and delight, and desired not that any should hold him in awe or take his counsels out of fear. ... Yet it is said that in the ending of the task for which he came he suffered greatly, and was slain, and being sent back from death for a brief while was clothed then in white, and became a radiant flame (yet veiled still save in great need).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olorin_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-olorin-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Internal_biography">Internal biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Internal biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maiar" title="Maiar">Maiar</a>, Gandalf would have participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_of_the_Ainur" title="Music of the Ainur">Music of the Ainur</a> at the creation of the world. However he does not attain any prominence until the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valar" title="Valar">Valar</a> settle in Valinor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valinor">Valinor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Valinor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Valinor" title="Valinor">Valinor</a>, Gandalf was known as <b>Olórin</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olorin_Two_Towers_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Olorin_Two_Towers-1">[1]</a></sup> As recounted in the "<a href="/wiki/Valaquenta" title="Valaquenta">Valaquenta</a>" in <i>The Silmarillion</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> he was one of the <a href="/wiki/Maia_(Middle-earth)" title="Maia (Middle-earth)">Maiar</a> of Valinor, specifically, of the people of the Vala <a href="/wiki/Manw%C3%AB" title="Manwë">Manwë</a>; and was said to be the wisest of the Maiar. He was also closely associated with two other Valar: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irmo_(Middle-earth)" title="Irmo (Middle-earth)">Irmo</a>, in whose gardens he lived, and <a href="/wiki/Nienna" title="Nienna">Nienna</a>, the patron of mercy, who gave him tutelage. When the <a href="/wiki/Vala_(Middle-earth)" title="Vala (Middle-earth)">Valar</a> decided to send the order of the <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(Middle-earth)" title="Wizard (Middle-earth)">Wizards (Istari)</a> to <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a> in order to counsel and assist all those who opposed <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a>, Olórin was proposed by Manwë. Olórin initially begged to be excused as he feared Sauron and lacked the strength to face him, but Manwë replied that that was all the more reason for him to go.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>As one of the Maiar, Gandalf was not a mortal Man but an angelic being who had taken human form. As one of those spirits, Olórin was in service to the Creator (<a href="/wiki/Eru_Il%C3%BAvatar" title="Eru Ilúvatar">Eru Ilúvatar</a>) and the Creator's '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secret_Fire" title="Secret Fire">Secret Fire</a>'. Along with the other Maiar who entered into the world as the five Wizards, he took on the specific form of an aged old man as a sign of his humility. The role of the wizards was to advise and counsel but never to attempt to match Sauron's strength with his own, and hopefully the kings and lords of Middle-earth would be more receptive to the advice of a humble old man than a more glorious form giving them direct commands.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Middle-earth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gandalf the Grey was the last of the Istari landing in the Havens of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mithlond" title="Mithlond">Mithlond</a>. He seemed the oldest and least in stature of them, but <a href="/wiki/C%C3%ADrdan" title="Círdan">Círdan</a> the Shipwright felt that he had the highest inner greatness on their first meeting in the Havens, and gave him <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narya" title="Narya">Narya</a>, the Ring of Fire. <a href="/wiki/Saruman" title="Saruman">Saruman</a>, the chief Wizard, later learned of the gift and resented it. Gandalf hid the ring well, and it was not widely known until he left with the other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ring-bearers" title="Ring-bearers">ring-bearers</a> at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">Third Age</a> that he, and not Círdan, was the holder of the third of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Rings" title="Three Rings">Elven-rings</a>.</p>
<p>Gandalf's relationship with Saruman, the head of their Order, was strained. The Wizards were commanded to aid <a href="/wiki/Man_(Middle-earth)" title="Man (Middle-earth)">Men</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">Elves</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_(Middle-earth)" title="Dwarf (Middle-earth)">Dwarves</a>, but only through counsel; it was forbidden to use force to dominate them — an injunction that Saruman increasingly disregarded.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_White_Council">The White Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The White Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In "<a href="/wiki/Of_the_Rings_of_Power_and_the_Third_Age" title="Of the Rings of Power and the Third Age">Of the Rings of Power and the Third Age</a>" (in <i>The Silmarillion</i>) and "The Istari" (in <i><a href="/wiki/Unfinished_Tales" title="Unfinished Tales">Unfinished Tales</a></i>), Tolkien fleshes out the background and the history briefly tabulated by date in Appendix B of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>. Gandalf suspected early on that the <a href="/wiki/Sauron#The_Necromancer_of_Dol_Guldur" title="Sauron">Necromancer of Dol Guldur</a> was not a <a href="/wiki/Nazg%C3%BBl" title="Nazgûl">Nazgûl</a> but Sauron himself. He went to <a href="/wiki/Dol_Guldur" title="Dol Guldur">Dol Guldur</a> (in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 2063<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup>) to discover the truth, but the Necromancer withdrew before him. The Necromancer returned to Dol Guldur with greater force in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 2460,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup> and the White Council was formed in response (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 2463<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup>). <a href="/wiki/Galadriel" title="Galadriel">Galadriel</a> had hoped Gandalf would lead the Council, but Gandalf refused, declining to be bound by any but the Valar who sent him. Saruman was chosen instead, as being most knowledgeable about Sauron's work in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Age" title="Second Age">Second Age</a>.</p>
<p>Gandalf returned to Dol Guldur in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 2850<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup> "at great peril" and learned that the Necromancer was indeed Sauron returned. (This is when Gandalf found <a href="/wiki/Thr%C3%A1in_II" title="Thráin II">Thráin II</a> imprisoned in Dol Guldur and recovered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_objects#Thr.C3.B3r.27s_map" title="List of Middle-earth objects">Thrór's map</a> and the key to <a href="/wiki/Lonely_Mountain" title="Lonely Mountain">Erebor</a> before Thráin died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup>) The following year<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/White_Council" title="White Council">White Council</a> was summoned, and Gandalf urged that Sauron be driven out. Saruman, however, reassured the Council that Sauron's evident effort to find the <a href="/wiki/One_Ring" title="One Ring">One Ring</a> (a necessary component of his resurgence) would fail, as the Ring would long since have been carried by the river <a href="/wiki/Anduin" title="Anduin">Anduin</a> to the Sea; and the matter was allowed to rest. But at this time Saruman himself began actively seeking the Ring near the <a href="/wiki/Gladden_Fields" title="Gladden Fields">Gladden Fields</a> where <a href="/wiki/Isildur" title="Isildur">Isildur</a> had been killed, not far from Dol Guldur.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Quest_of_Erebor">The Quest of Erebor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Quest of Erebor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/The_Quest_of_Erebor" title="The Quest of Erebor">The Quest of Erebor</a>" in <i><a href="/wiki/Unfinished_Tales" title="Unfinished Tales">Unfinished Tales</a></i> elaborates upon the story behind <i>The Hobbit</i>. It tells of a chance meeting between Gandalf and <a href="/wiki/Thorin_Oakenshield" title="Thorin Oakenshield">Thorin Oakenshield</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_(Middle-earth)" title="Dwarf (Middle-earth)">Dwarf</a>-king in exile, in the inn of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prancing_Pony" title="Prancing Pony">Prancing Pony</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a>. Gandalf had for some time foreseen the coming war with <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a>, and knew that the North was especially vulnerable. If <a href="/wiki/Rivendell" title="Rivendell">Rivendell</a> were to be attacked, the <a href="/wiki/Dragon_(Middle-earth)" title="Dragon (Middle-earth)">dragon</a> <a href="/wiki/Smaug" title="Smaug">Smaug</a> could cause great devastation. He persuaded Thorin that he could help him regain his lost territory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erebor" title="Erebor">Erebor</a> from Smaug, and so the quest was born.</p>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 2941,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup> Gandalf arranged (and frequently accompanied) a <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_Hobbit_characters#Thorin.27s_company" title="List of The Hobbit characters">band of 13 dwarves</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hobbit" title="Hobbit">hobbit</a> <a href="/wiki/Bilbo_Baggins" title="Bilbo Baggins">Bilbo Baggins</a> to reclaim from Smaug the Dwarves' lost treasure in Erebor. To the quest, Gandalf contributed a <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth_objects#Thr.C3.B3r.27s_map" title="Middle-earth objects">map</a> and key to Erebor. It was on this Quest of Erebor that Gandalf found his sword, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_weapons_and_armour#Glamdring" title="List of Middle-earth weapons and armour">Glamdring</a>,</i> in a <a href="/wiki/Troll_(Middle-earth)" title="Troll (Middle-earth)">troll's</a> treasure hoard.</p>
<p>After escaping from the <a href="/wiki/Misty_Mountains" title="Misty Mountains">Misty Mountains</a> pursued by <a href="/wiki/Orc_(Middle-earth)" title="Orc (Middle-earth)">Goblins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warg" title="Warg">Wargs</a>, the party was carried to safety by the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_(Middle-earth)" title="Eagle (Middle-earth)">Great Eagles</a>. Gandalf then persuaded <a href="/wiki/Beorn" title="Beorn">Beorn</a>—who did not like uninvited guests or dwarves—to house and provision the company for the trip through <a href="/wiki/Mirkwood" title="Mirkwood">Mirkwood</a>.</p>
<p>Gandalf left the company before they entered Mirkwood, saying that he had pressing business to attend to. He turned up again, however, before the walls of Erebor disguised as an old man, revealing himself when it seemed the Men of <a href="/wiki/Esgaroth" title="Esgaroth">Esgaroth</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mirkwood_Elves" title="Mirkwood Elves">Mirkwood Elves</a> would fight Thorin and the Dwarves over Smaug's treasure. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Five_Armies" title="Battle of the Five Armies">Battle of the Five Armies</a> ensued when hosts of Goblins and Wargs attacked all three parties. After the battle, Gandalf accompanied Bilbo back to <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a>, revealing at Rivendell what his pressing business had been: Gandalf had once again urged the Council to evict Sauron, since quite evidently Sauron did not require the <a href="/wiki/One_Ring" title="One Ring">One Ring</a> to continue to attract evil to Mirkwood. Then, in an event only briefly described (in <i>The Silmarillion</i>, <i>The Hobbit</i>, and <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>), the Council "put forth its power" and drove Sauron from <a href="/wiki/Dol_Guldur" title="Dol Guldur">Dol Guldur</a>. Sauron, however, had anticipated this and withdrew as a feint, only to reappear in <a href="/wiki/Mordor" title="Mordor">Mordor</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prelude_to_the_War_of_the_Ring">Prelude to the War of the Ring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Prelude to the War of the Ring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>As explained in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Fellowship of the Ring">The Fellowship of the Ring</a></i>, Gandalf spent the years between <i>The Hobbit</i> and <i>The Lord of the Rings</i> travelling Middle-earth in search of information on <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a>'s resurgence and <a href="/wiki/Bilbo_Baggins" title="Bilbo Baggins">Bilbo</a>'s mysterious <a href="/wiki/One_Ring" title="One Ring">ring</a>, spurred particularly by Bilbo's initial misleading story of how he had obtained it as a "present" from <a href="/wiki/Gollum" title="Gollum">Gollum</a>. During this period he befriended <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn</a> and first became suspicious of <a href="/wiki/Saruman" title="Saruman">Saruman</a>. He spent as much time as he could in <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a>, strengthening his friendship with Bilbo and <a href="/wiki/Frodo_Baggins" title="Frodo Baggins">Frodo</a>, Bilbo's heir.</p>
<p>He returned to the Shire for Bilbo's "eleventy-first" (111st) birthday party in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 3001, bringing many fireworks for the occasion. After Bilbo, as a prank on his guests, put on the Ring and disappeared, Gandalf strongly encouraged his old friend to leave the Ring to Frodo, as they had planned. Bilbo became hostile and accused Gandalf of trying to steal the Ring—which he called "my precious", much as Gollum had done. (<a href="/wiki/Isildur" title="Isildur">Isildur</a>, who earlier possessed the ring and was destroyed by it, had written that "it is precious to me").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Alarmed, Gandalf impressed on Bilbo the foolishness of this accusation. Coming to his senses, Bilbo admitted that the Ring had been troubling him, and leaving it behind for Frodo, he departed for <a href="/wiki/Rivendell" title="Rivendell">Rivendell</a>. Though Bilbo would never be entirely free of the desire for the Ring, he was the first of its bearers to give it up willingly.</p>
<p>Over the next 17 years, Gandalf travelled extensively, searching for answers on the Ring. He found some answers in <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth_objects#Scroll_of_Isildur" title="Middle-earth objects">Isildur's scroll</a>, in the archives of <a href="/wiki/Minas_Tirith" title="Minas Tirith">Minas Tirith</a>. But he also wanted to interview Gollum, who had borne the Ring for many years. Gandalf searched long and widely for Gollum, and often had the assistance of Aragorn. Aragorn eventually succeeded, and Gandalf questioned Gollum, threatening him with fire when he proved unwilling to speak. Gandalf learned finally that Sauron had forced Gollum under torture in <a href="/wiki/Barad-d%C3%BBr" title="Barad-dûr">Barad-dûr</a> to tell what he knew of the Ring. This reinforced Gandalf's growing suspicion that Bilbo's ring was the One Ring.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring">The Fellowship of the Ring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Fellowship of the Ring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Returning to <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 3018, Gandalf confirmed his suspicions by throwing <a href="/wiki/One_Ring" title="One Ring">the Ring</a> into <a href="/wiki/Frodo_Baggins" title="Frodo Baggins">Frodo</a>'s hearth-fire and reading the writing that appeared on <a href="/wiki/One_Ring#Appearance" title="One Ring">the Ring's surface</a>. He told Frodo the full history of the Ring, and urged him to take the Ring to <a href="/wiki/Rivendell" title="Rivendell">Rivendell</a>; for he would be in grave danger if he stayed in the Shire. Gandalf said he would attempt to return for Frodo's 50th birthday party, in order to accompany him on the road thereafter; and that meanwhile Frodo should arrange to leave quietly, as the servants of <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a> would be searching for him.</p>
<p>Outside the Shire, Gandalf encountered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radagast_the_Brown" title="Radagast the Brown">Radagast the Brown</a>, another of the Istari, who brought the news that the <a href="/wiki/Nazg%C3%BBl" title="Nazgûl">Nazgûl</a> had ridden forth and crossed the River <a href="/wiki/Anduin" title="Anduin">Anduin</a>—and a request from <a href="/wiki/Saruman" title="Saruman">Saruman</a> that Gandalf come to <a href="/wiki/Isengard" title="Isengard">Isengard</a>. Gandalf left a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_objects#Gandalf.27s_letter" title="List of Middle-earth objects">letter</a> to Frodo (urging his immediate departure) with <a href="/wiki/Barliman_Butterbur" title="Barliman Butterbur">Barliman Butterbur</a> at the inn in <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a>, and headed towards Isengard. There Saruman revealed his true colours, urging Gandalf to help him obtain the Ring for his own use. Gandalf refused, and Saruman imprisoned him at the top of the tower of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthanc" title="Orthanc">Orthanc</a>. Eventually Gandalf was rescued by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwaihir" title="Gwaihir">Gwaihir</a> the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_(Middle-earth)" title="Eagle (Middle-earth)">Eagle</a>.</p>
<p>Gwaihir set Gandalf down in <a href="/wiki/Rohan_(Middle-earth)" title="Rohan (Middle-earth)">Rohan</a>, where Gandalf appealed to King <a href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9oden" title="Théoden">Théoden</a> for a horse. Théoden, under the malign influence of <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%ADma_Wormtongue" title="Gríma Wormtongue">Gríma Wormtongue</a>, Saruman's spy, told Gandalf to take any horse he pleased, but to leave quickly. It was then that Gandalf met the great horse <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shadowfax_(Middle-earth)" title="Shadowfax (Middle-earth)">Shadowfax</a>, one of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mearas" title="Mearas">mearas</a></i>, who would be his mount and companion for most of the rest of the war. Gandalf pursued the horse for two days<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-7"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup> before Shadowfax permitted Gandalf to ride him. Gandalf then rode hard for the Shire, but did not reach it until Frodo had already set out. Knowing that Frodo and his companions would be heading for Rivendell, Gandalf began to make his own way there. He learned at Bree that the Hobbits had fallen in with <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn</a>. He faced the Nazgûl at <a href="/wiki/Weathertop" title="Weathertop">Weathertop</a> but escaped after an all-night battle, drawing four of them northward. Frodo, Aragorn and company faced the remaining five on Weathertop a few nights later. Gandalf reached Rivendell just before Frodo's arrival.</p>
<p>In Rivendell, Gandalf helped <a href="/wiki/Elrond" title="Elrond">Elrond</a> drive off the Nazgûl pursuing Frodo and played a great part in the following <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_of_Elrond" title="Council of Elrond">council</a> as the only person who knew the full history of the Ring. He also revealed that Saruman had betrayed them and was in league with Sauron. When it was decided that the Ring had to be destroyed, Gandalf volunteered to accompany Frodo—now the Ring-bearer—in his quest. He also persuaded Elrond to let Frodo's cousins <a href="/wiki/Meriadoc_Brandybuck" title="Meriadoc Brandybuck">Merry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peregrin_Took" title="Peregrin Took">Pippin</a> join the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellowship_of_the_Ring_(characters)" title="Fellowship of the Ring (characters)">Fellowship</a>.</p>
<p>Taking charge of the Fellowship (comprising nine representatives of the free peoples of <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a>, "set against the Nine Riders"), Gandalf and Aragorn led the Hobbits and their companions south. After an unsuccessful attempt to cross Mount <a href="/wiki/Caradhras" title="Caradhras">Caradhras</a> in winter, they crossed <i>under</i> the mountains through the Mines of <a href="/wiki/Moria_(Middle-earth)" title="Moria (Middle-earth)">Moria</a>, though only <a href="/wiki/Gimli_(Middle-earth)" title="Gimli (Middle-earth)">Gimli</a> the <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_(Middle-earth)" title="Dwarf (Middle-earth)">Dwarf</a> was enthusiastic about that route. In Moria, they discovered that the Dwarf colony established there earlier had been overrun by <a href="/wiki/Orc_(Middle-earth)" title="Orc (Middle-earth)">Orcs</a>. The Fellowship fought with the Orcs and Trolls of Moria and escaped them.</p>
<p>At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bridge_of_Khazad-d%C3%BBm" title="Bridge of Khazad-dûm">Bridge of Khazad-dûm</a>, they encountered "<a href="/wiki/Balrog#Durin.27s_Bane" title="Balrog">Durin's Bane</a>," a fearsome <a href="/wiki/Balrog" title="Balrog">Balrog</a> from ancient times. Gandalf faced the Balrog to enable the others to escape. After a brief exchange of blows, Gandalf broke the bridge beneath the Balrog with his <a href="/wiki/Magic_(Middle-earth)#Magic_items" title="Magic (Middle-earth)">staff</a>. As the Balrog fell, it wrapped its whip around Gandalf's legs, dragging him over the edge. As his friends looked on in horror, Gandalf fell into the abyss, crying "Fly, you fools!" as he went.</p>
<p>After a long fall, Gandalf and the Balrog crashed into a deep subterranean lake far under Moria. Gandalf pursued the Balrog through the tunnels for eight days until they climbed to the peak of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor_places_in_Middle-earth#Celebdil" title="Minor places in Middle-earth">Zirakzigil</a>. Here they fought for two days and nights. In the end, the Balrog was defeated and cast down onto the mountainside. Gandalf himself died shortly afterwards, and his body lay on the peak while his spirit travelled "out of thought and time".</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Gandalf_the_White">Gandalf the White</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Gandalf the White">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Gandalf was eventually "sent back"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> as Gandalf the White, and returned to life on the mountain top. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwaihir" title="Gwaihir">Gwaihir</a>, lord of eagles, carried him to <a href="/wiki/Lothl%C3%B3rien" title="Lothlórien">Lórien</a>, where he was healed of his injuries and re-clothed in white robes by <a href="/wiki/Galadriel" title="Galadriel">Galadriel</a>. He travelled to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fangorn_Forest" title="Fangorn Forest">Fangorn Forest</a>, where he encountered <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gimli_(Middle-earth)" title="Gimli (Middle-earth)">Gimli</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Legolas" title="Legolas">Legolas</a> (who were tracking <a href="/wiki/Meriadoc_Brandybuck" title="Meriadoc Brandybuck">Merry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peregrin_Took" title="Peregrin Took">Pippin</a>). They mistook him for <a href="/wiki/Saruman" title="Saruman">Saruman</a>, but he stopped their attacks and revealed himself.</p>
<p>They travelled to <a href="/wiki/Rohan_(Middle-earth)" title="Rohan (Middle-earth)">Rohan</a>, where Gandalf found that <a href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9oden" title="Théoden">Théoden</a> had been further weakened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wormtongue" title="Wormtongue">Wormtongue</a>'s influence. He broke Wormtongue's hold over Théoden, and convinced the king to join in the fight against <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a>. Gandalf then set off to gather <a href="/wiki/Erkenbrand" title="Erkenbrand">Erkenbrand</a> of the Westfold and his warriors to assist Théoden in the coming battle with Saruman. Gandalf arrived just in time to shatter Saruman's attack on <a href="/wiki/Helm%27s_Deep" title="Helm's Deep">Helm's Deep</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Hornburg" title="Battle of the Hornburg">ensuing battle</a>, Gandalf and the king rode to <a href="/wiki/Isengard" title="Isengard">Isengard</a>, which in the interim had itself been attacked and conquered by <a href="/wiki/Treebeard" title="Treebeard">Treebeard</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ent" title="Ent">Ents</a>, along with Merry and Pippin. Gandalf broke Saruman's <a href="/wiki/Magic_(Middle-earth)#Magic_items" title="Magic (Middle-earth)">staff</a> and expelled him from the White Council and the Order of Wizards, and assumed Saruman's place as head of both. Wormtongue made an indecisive attempt to kill Gandalf or Saruman with the <i><a href="/wiki/Palant%C3%ADr" title="Palantír">palantír</a></i> of Orthanc, but missed both. Pippin retrieved the <i>palantír</i>, but Gandalf quickly appropriated it. After the group left Isengard, Pippin took the <i>palantír</i> from a sleeping Gandalf, looked into it, and came face to face with Sauron himself. Gandalf then took the chastened Pippin with him to <a href="/wiki/Minas_Tirith" title="Minas Tirith">Minas Tirith</a> to keep the young hobbit out of further trouble.</p>
<p>Gandalf arrived in time to help order the defences of Minas Tirith. His presence was resented by <a href="/wiki/Denethor" title="Denethor">Denethor</a>, the Steward of <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a>; but after Denethor's son <a href="/wiki/Faramir" title="Faramir">Faramir</a> was gravely wounded in battle, Denethor sank into despair and madness. Together with Prince <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imrahil" title="Imrahil">Imrahil</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dol_Amroth" title="Dol Amroth">Dol Amroth</a>, Gandalf led the defenders during the siege of the city. When the forces of <a href="/wiki/Mordor" title="Mordor">Mordor</a> finally broke the main gate, Gandalf alone on Shadowfax confronted the <a href="/wiki/Witch-king_of_Angmar" title="Witch-king of Angmar">Witch-king of Angmar</a>, Lord of the Nazgûl. But at that moment the Rohirrim arrived, compelling the Witch-king to withdraw and engage them. Gandalf would have ridden to their aid, but he too was suddenly required elsewhere—to save Faramir from Denethor, who sought in desperation to burn himself and his son on a funeral pyre in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_roads#Rath_D.C3.ADnen" title="List of Middle-earth roads">Rath Dínen</a>.</p>
<p>Aragorn and Gandalf then led the final campaign against Sauron's forces at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Gate_(Middle-earth)" title="Black Gate (Middle-earth)">Black Gate</a>, in an effort to distract the Dark Lord's attention from <a href="/wiki/Frodo_Baggins" title="Frodo Baggins">Frodo Baggins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sam_Gamgee" title="Sam Gamgee">Sam Gamgee</a>, who were at the same moment scaling <a href="/wiki/Mount_Doom" title="Mount Doom">Mount Doom</a> to destroy the <a href="/wiki/One_Ring" title="One Ring">One Ring</a>. In a parley before the battle, Gandalf and the other leaders of the West met the <a href="/wiki/Mouth_of_Sauron" title="Mouth of Sauron">Mouth of Sauron</a>, who showed them Frodo's <i><a href="/wiki/Mithril" title="Mithril">mithril</a></i> shirt and other items from the Hobbits' equipment. But Gandalf rejected Mordor's terms of surrender, and the forces of the West faced the full might of Sauron's armies, until the Ring was destroyed in Mount Doom. Gandalf led the Eagles to rescue Frodo and Sam from the erupting mountain.</p>
<p>After the war, Gandalf crowned Aragorn as King Elessar, and helped him find a sapling of the <a href="/wiki/White_Tree_of_Gondor" title="White Tree of Gondor">White Tree of Gondor</a>. He accompanied the Hobbits back to the borders of <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a>, before leaving to visit <a href="/wiki/Tom_Bombadil" title="Tom Bombadil">Tom Bombadil</a>.</p>
<p>Two years later, Gandalf—who by now had spent about 2,000 years in <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AppB_6-8"><a href="#cite_note-AppB-6">[6]</a></sup>—departed with Frodo, <a href="/wiki/Bilbo_Baggins" title="Bilbo Baggins">Bilbo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galadriel" title="Galadriel">Galadriel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elrond" title="Elrond">Elrond</a> across <a href="/wiki/Belegaer" title="Belegaer">the sea</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undying_Lands" title="Undying Lands">Undying Lands</a>. It was only then that his possession of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narya" title="Narya">Narya</a>, one of the Three Elven Rings, became generally known.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept_and_creation">Concept and creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Concept and creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Der_Berggeist.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
"Der Berggeist" by <a href="/wiki/Josef_Madlener" title="Josef Madlener">Josef Madlener</a>.</div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Carpenter" title="Humphrey Carpenter">Humphrey Carpenter</a> in his 1977 biography relates that <a href="/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien" title="J. R. R. Tolkien">Tolkien</a> owned a postcard entitled <i>Der Berggeist</i> ("the mountain spirit"), and on the paper cover in which he kept it, he wrote "the origin of Gandalf". The postcard reproduces a painting of a bearded figure, sitting on a rock under a pine tree in a mountainous setting. He wears a wide-brimmed round hat and a long red cloak, and a white fawn is nuzzling his upturned hands.</p>
<p>Carpenter said that Tolkien recalled buying the postcard during his holiday in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> in 1911. Manfred Zimmerman,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zimmerman_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-zimmerman-9">[9]</a></sup> however, discovered that the painting was by <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Josef_Madlener" title="Josef Madlener">Josef Madlener</a> and dates to the mid-1920s. Carpenter acknowledged that Tolkien was probably mistaken about the origin of the postcard.</p>
<p>The original painting was auctioned at <a href="/wiki/Sotheby%27s" title="Sotheby's">Sotheby's</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> on 12 July 2005 for £84,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The previous owner had been given the painting by Madlener in the 1940s and recalled that Madlener said the mountains in the background were the <a href="/wiki/Vajolet_Towers" title="Vajolet Towers">Torri del Vaiolet</a> peaks in the <a href="/wiki/Dolomites" title="Dolomites">Dolomites</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zimmerman_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-zimmerman-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>When writing <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit" title="The Hobbit">The Hobbit</a></i> in the early 1930s Tolkien gave the name <i>Gandalf</i> to the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_(Middle-earth)" title="Dwarf (Middle-earth)">Dwarves</a>, the character later called <a href="/wiki/Thorin_Oakenshield" title="Thorin Oakenshield">Thorin Oakenshield</a>. The name is taken from the same source as all the other Dwarf names (save <a href="/wiki/Balin_(Middle-earth)" title="Balin (Middle-earth)">Balin</a>) in <i>The Hobbit</i>: the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norse_dwarves#Dvergatal:_the_list_of_the_Dvergar" title="Norse dwarves">Catalogue of Dwarves</a>" in the <i><a href="/wiki/V%C3%B6lusp%C3%A1" title="Völuspá">Völuspá</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> name <i><span lang="non" xml:lang="non">Gandalfr</span></i> incorporates the words <i><span lang="non" xml:lang="non">gandr</span></i> meaning "wand", "staff" or (especially in compounds) "<a href="/wiki/Magic_(paranormal)" title="Magic (paranormal)">magic</a>" and <i><span lang="non" xml:lang="non">álfr</span></i> "<a href="/wiki/Elf" title="Elf">elf</a>". The name <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gandalf_(Norse_mythology)" title="Gandalf (Norse mythology)">Gandalf</a></i> is found in at least one more place in Norse myth, in the semi-historical <a href="/wiki/Heimskringla" title="Heimskringla">Heimskringla</a>, which briefly describes <a href="/wiki/Gandalf_Alfgeirsson" title="Gandalf Alfgeirsson">Gandalf Alfgeirsson</a>, a legendary Norse king from Eastern Norway and rival of <a href="/wiki/Halfdan_the_Black" title="Halfdan the Black">Halfdan the Black</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Gandalf is also the name of a Norse sea-king in <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Ibsen" title="Henrik Ibsen">Ibsen's</a> second play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Burial_Mound" title="The Burial Mound">The Burial Mound</a></i>.</p>

<p>The name "Gandolf" occurs as a character in <a href="/wiki/William_Morris" title="William Morris">William Morris</a>' 1896 fantasy novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Well_at_the_World%27s_End" title="The Well at the World's End">The Well at the World's End</a></i>. Morris' book is a multi-part 'magical journey' involving <a href="/wiki/Elf" title="Elf">elves</a>, dwarves and kings in a pseudo-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">medieval</a> landscape which is known to have deeply influenced Tolkien.</p>
<p>The wizard that became Gandalf was originally named <i>Bladorthin</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Tolkien later assigned this name to an ancient king who had ordered some spears from the dwarves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tolkien came to regret his <i>ad hoc</i> use of <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> name, referring to a "rabble of eddaic-named dwarves, ... invented in an idle hour" in 1937.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> But the decision to use Old Norse names came to have far-reaching consequences in the composition of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="The Lord of the Rings">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>; in 1942, Tolkien decided that the work was to be a purported translation from the fictional language of <a href="/wiki/Westron" title="Westron">Westron</a>, and in the English translation Old Norse names were taken to represent names in the language of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dale_(Middle-earth)" title="Dale (Middle-earth)">Dale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <i>Gandalf</i>, in this setting, is thus a representation in English (anglicised from Old Norse) of the name the dwarves of Dale had given to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ol%C3%B3rin" title="Olórin">Olórin</a> in the language they used "externally" in their daily affairs, while <i>Tharkûn</i> is the (untranslated) name, presumably of the same meaning, that the dwarves gave him in their native <a href="/wiki/Khuzdul" title="Khuzdul">Khuzdul</a> language. Tolkien explains this in his <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guide_to_the_Names_in_The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="Guide to the Names in The Lord of the Rings">Guide to the Names in The Lord of the Rings</a></i> (1967) to prospective translators.</p>
<p>Throughout the early drafts, and through to the first edition of <i>The Hobbit</i> Bladorthin/Gandalf is described as being a "little old man", distinct from a dwarf, but not of the full human stature that would later be described in <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>. Even in <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>, Gandalf was not tall; shorter, for example, than <a href="/wiki/Elrond" title="Elrond">Elrond</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> or the other wizards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gandalf's role and importance was substantially increased in the conception of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="The Lord of the Rings">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>, and in a letter of 1954, Tolkien refers to Gandalf as an "<a href="/wiki/Angel" title="Angel">angel</a> incarnate".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-letters_156_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-letters_156-20">[20]</a></sup> In the same letter Tolkien states he was given the form of an old man in order to limit his powers on Earth. Both in 1965 and 1971 Tolkien again refers to Gandalf as an angelic being.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a letter of 1946 Tolkien stated that he thought of Gandalf as an <i>"Odinic wanderer"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-letters_107_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-letters_107-23">[23]</a></sup> Other commentators have also compared Gandalf to the <a href="/wiki/Norse_mythology" title="Norse mythology">Norse god</a> <a href="/wiki/Odin" title="Odin">Odin</a> in his "Wanderer" guise—an old man with one eye, a long white beard, a wide brimmed hat, and a staff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

Gandalf was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Vesa_Vierikko" title="Vesa Vierikko">Vesa Vierikko</a> in the Finnish television <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hobitit" title="Hobitit">Hobitit</a></i> (1993).</p>
<p>Sir <a href="/wiki/Ian_McKellen" title="Ian McKellen">Ian McKellen</a> portrayed Gandalf in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i> film series (2001–2003) directed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Sean_Connery" title="Sean Connery">Sean Connery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Stewart" title="Patrick Stewart">Patrick Stewart</a> both turned down the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The make-up and costumes were based on designs by <a href="/wiki/John_Howe_(illustrator)" title="John Howe (illustrator)">John Howe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Lee_(illustrator)" title="Alan Lee (illustrator)">Alan Lee</a>. According to Jackson, McKellen based his performance as Gandalf on Tolkien himself:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>We listened to audio recordings of Tolkien reading excerpts from <i>Lord of the Rings</i>. We watched some BBC interviews with him — there's a few interviews with Tolkien — and Ian based his performance on an impersonation of Tolkien. He's literally basing Gandalf on Tolkien. He sounds the same, he uses the speech patterns and his mannerisms are born out of the same roughness from the footage of Tolkien. So, Tolkien would recognize himself in Ian's performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-impersonate_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-impersonate-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>

(March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Brent_Carver" title="Brent Carver">Brent Carver</a> portrayed Gandalf in the 2006 musical production <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Rings_(musical)" title="Lord of the Rings (musical)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>, which opened in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_legacy">Reception and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gandalf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reception and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gandalf was chosen by <a href="/wiki/Mary_Hoffman" title="Mary Hoffman">Mary Hoffman</a> as one of her favourite fictional characters, saying he was "the best white wizard in fiction".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_100_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Independent_100-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ian_McKellen" title="Ian McKellen">Ian McKellen</a> received widespread acclaim for his performance as Gandalf in <i>The Lord of the Rings</i> films; particularly in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring">The Fellowship of the Ring</a></i>, for which he received both a <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award" title="Saturn Award">Saturn Award</a> for Best Supporting Actor, and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nomination under the same category (making him the only individual cast member of the films to be nominated for an Oscar). In addition, <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_(film_magazine)" title="Empire (film magazine)">Empire</a></i> named Gandalf, as portrayed by McKellen, the 28th greatest film character of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
