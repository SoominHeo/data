<p><i><b>Kanrin Maru</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">咸臨丸</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>'s first sail and screw-driven steam <a href="/wiki/Corvette" title="Corvette">corvette</a> (the first steam-driven Japanese warship, <i><a href="/wiki/Japanese_barque_Kank%C5%8D_Maru" title="Japanese barque Kankō Maru">Kankō Maru</a></i>, was a <a href="/wiki/Paddle_steamer" title="Paddle steamer">side-wheeler</a>). She was ordered in 1853 from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the only Western country with which Japan had diplomatic relations throughout its period of <i><a href="/wiki/Sakoku" title="Sakoku">sakoku</a></i> (seclusion), by the <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">Shogun</a>'s government, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bakufu" title="Bakufu">Bakufu</a>. She was delivered on September 21, 1857 (with the name <i>Japan</i>) by Lt. <a href="/wiki/Willem_Huyssen_van_Kattendijke" title="Willem Huyssen van Kattendijke">Willem Huyssen van Kattendijke</a> of the Dutch navy. The ship was used at the newly established Naval School of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nagasaki,_Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki, Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a> in order to build up knowledge of Western warship technology.</p>
<p><i>Kanrin Maru</i>, as a screw-driven steam warship, represented a new technological advance in warship design which had been introduced in the West only ten years earlier with <a href="/wiki/HMS_Rattler_(1843)" title="HMS Rattler (1843)">HMS <i>Rattler</i> (1843)</a>. The ship was built by the shipyard of <a href="/wiki/Fop_Smit" title="Fop Smit">Fop Smit</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kinderdijk" title="Kinderdijk">Kinderdijk</a> in the Netherlands, where the virtually identical screw-steamship with schooner-rig <i>Bali</i> of the Dutch navy was also built in 1856. She allowed Japan to get its first experience with some of the newest advances in ship design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_embassy_to_the_US">Japanese embassy to the US</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_warship_Kanrin_Maru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Japanese embassy to the US">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Embassy_to_the_United_States_(1860)" title="Japanese Embassy to the United States (1860)">Japanese Embassy to the United States (1860)</a></div>
<p>Three years later, the Bakufu sent <i>Kanrin Maru</i> on a mission to the United States, clearly wanting to make a point to the world that Japan had now mastered western navigation techniques and western ship technologies. On 9 February 1860 (18 January in the Japanese calendar), the Kanrin Maru, captained by <a href="/wiki/Katsu_Kaish%C5%AB" title="Katsu Kaishū">Katsu Kaishū</a> together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakahama_Manjiro" title="Nakahama Manjiro">John Manjiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukuzawa_Yukichi" title="Fukuzawa Yukichi">Fukuzawa Yukichi</a>, and a total of 96 Japanese sailors, and the American officer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_M._Brooke" title="John M. Brooke">John M. Brooke</a>, left <a href="/wiki/Uraga_Channel" title="Uraga Channel">Uraga</a> for <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.</p>
<p>This became the second official Japanese embassy to cross the Pacific Ocean, around 250 years after the embassy of <a href="/wiki/Hasekura_Tsunenaga" title="Hasekura Tsunenaga">Hasekura Tsunenaga</a> to Mexico and then Europe in 1614, aboard the Japanese-built galleon <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_warship_San_Juan_Bautista" title="Japanese warship San Juan Bautista"><i>San Juan Bautista</i></a>.</p>
<p><i>Kanrin Maru</i> was accompanied by a United States Navy ship, the <a href="/wiki/USS_Powhatan_(1850)" title="USS Powhatan (1850)"><i>Powhatan</i></a>.</p>
<p>The official objective of the mission was to send the first ever Japanese embassy to the US, and to ratify the new treaty of Friendship, Commerce, and Navigation between the United States and Japan.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boshin_war">Boshin war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_warship_Kanrin_Maru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Boshin war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kanrin_Maru_members.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1035" data-file-width="1450" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Kanrin_Maru_members.jpg/300px-Kanrin_Maru_members.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Kanrin_Maru_members.jpg/450px-Kanrin_Maru_members.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Kanrin_Maru_members.jpg/600px-Kanrin_Maru_members.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>

Members of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Embassy_to_the_United_States_(1860)" title="Japanese Embassy to the United States (1860)">Japanese Embassy to the United States (1860)</a>, who sailed on the <i>Kanrin Maru</i> and the USS <i>Powhatan</i>. <a href="/wiki/Fukuzawa_Yukichi" title="Fukuzawa Yukichi">Fukuzawa Yukichi</a> sits on the right.</div>

<p>By the end of 1867, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bakufu" title="Bakufu">Bakufu</a> was attacked by pro-imperial forces, initiating the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a> which led to the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>. Towards the end of the conflict, in September 1868, after several defeats by the Bakufu, <i>Kanrin Maru</i> was one of the eight modern ships led by <a href="/wiki/Enomoto_Takeaki" title="Enomoto Takeaki">Enomoto Takeaki</a> towards the northern part of Japan, in his final attempt to wage a counter-attack against pro-imperial forces.</p>
<p>The fleet encountered a typhoon on its way northward, and <i>Kanrin Maru</i>, having suffered damage, was forced to take refuge in Shimizu harbour, where she was captured by Imperial forces, who bombarded and boarded the ship notwithstanding a white flag of surrender, and put the skeleton crew aboard to the sword.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Enomoto Takeaki finally made a rendition in May 1869, and after the end of the conflict, <i>Kanrin Maru</i> was used by the new Imperial government for the development of the northern island of Hokkaido.</p>
<p>She was lost there in a typhoon in 1871, at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esashi,_Hokkaid%C5%8D_(Hiyama)" title="Esashi, Hokkaidō (Hiyama)">Esashi</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kanrin_Maru_today">Kanrin Maru today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_warship_Kanrin_Maru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kanrin Maru today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1990, a <a href="/wiki/Ship_replica" title="Ship replica">ship replica</a> twice the size of the original was ordered for manufacture in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, according to the original plans. The ship was visible in the theme park of <a href="/wiki/Huis_Ten_Bosch_(theme_park)" title="Huis Ten Bosch (theme park)">Huis Ten Bosch</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a>, in southern Japan. It is now used as a sightseeing ship to the <a href="/wiki/Naruto_whirlpools" title="Naruto whirlpools">Naruto whirlpools</a> from <a href="/wiki/Minamiawaji" title="Minamiawaji">Minami Awaji</a> harbour.</p>
