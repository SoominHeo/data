<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jiandi</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Jiandi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">簡狄</span>; <a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">简狄</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i>Jiǎndí</i>), also with variants 簡易/简易 and 簡逷/简逷, is an important figure in <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">Chinese history</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_mythology" title="Chinese mythology">Chinese mythology</a>. She was the second wife of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ku" title="Emperor Ku">Emperor Ku</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> who was a descendent of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Emperor" title="Yellow Emperor">Yellow Emperor</a>. Jiandi was the mother of Qi, the predynastic founder of the <a href="/wiki/Shang_dynasty" title="Shang dynasty">Shang dynasty</a> (note that this is a different Qi than the Qi Ji born to Di Ku's first wife <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Yuan" title="Jiang Yuan">Jiang Yuan</a>). Her pregnancy miraculously occurred following her swallowing or holding in her bosom the egg of a bird.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Jiandi was said to be of the Yousong clan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Jiandi is mentioned in various sources, including the poems "Eulogy of Shang", in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shijing" title="Shijing">Shijing</a></i>, and the "<a href="/wiki/Heavenly_Questions" title="Heavenly Questions">Heavenly Questions</a>" in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chuci" title="Chuci">Chuci</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
