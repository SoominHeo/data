<p>The <b>You Drive Me Crazy Tour</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (also known as <b>Crazy 2k Tour</b>) was the second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a> by American recording artist <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>. It was launched in support of her studio albums <i><a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(album)" title="...Baby One More Time (album)">...Baby One More Time</a></i> (1999) and <i><a href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_(album)" title="Oops!... I Did It Again (album)">Oops!... I Did It Again</a></i> (2000). The tour was designed as a continuation of the <a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour" title="...Baby One More Time Tour">...Baby One More Time Tour</a> and a prelude to the <a href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_Tour" title="Oops!... I Did It Again Tour">Oops!... I Did It Again Tour</a>. It was sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Got_Milk%3F" title="Got Milk?">Got Milk?</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polaroid_Corporation" title="Polaroid Corporation">Polaroid</a>.</p>
<p>The tour was divided into various segments, with each segment being followed by an interlude to the next segment, and it ended with an encore. The set list consisted of nine songs, seven from <i>...Baby One More Time</i> and two unreleased songs from <i>Oops!... I Did It Again</i>, her then-upcoming album. During the tour, Spears was accused of lip synching, although she denied these claims. The show was recorded and broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>. A DVD entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_and_More!" title="Live and More!">Live and More!</a></i> was also released.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_development">Background and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=(You_Drive_Me)_Crazy_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 17, 1999, during the premiere of the music video for "<a href="/wiki/From_the_Bottom_of_My_Broken_Heart" title="From the Bottom of My Broken Heart">From the Bottom of My Broken Heart</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">Total Request Live</a></i>, Spears called the show to announce March US tour dates. The tour was designed as a continuation of the <a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour" title="...Baby One More Time Tour">...Baby One More Time Tour</a> and a prelude to her <a href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_Tour" title="Oops!... I Did It Again Tour">future world tour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newtour_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-newtour-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rolling-4">[4]</a></sup> The leg's main sponsor was <a href="/wiki/Got_Milk%3F" title="Got Milk?">Got Milk?</a>. Media director Peter Gardiner explained, "Britney is magic with teen-age girls, and that's an absolutely crucial target for milk". Spears shot an advertising campaign to be shown before her performances began.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milk_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-milk-5">[5]</a></sup> The secondary sponsor was <a href="/wiki/Polaroid_Corporation" title="Polaroid Corporation">Polaroid</a> and the corporation released the <a href="/wiki/I-Zone" title="I-Zone">Polaroid I-Zone</a> as the official camera of the tour. Spears also used the I-Zone onstage to take pictures of the audience and further promote the product.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-polaroid_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-polaroid-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The stage of the (You Drive Me) Crazy Tour was similar to that of the ...Baby One More Time Tour, although much bigger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-7">[7]</a></sup> There were many special effects, including smoke machines and fireworks that erupted during the show. There was a giant projection screen that resembled the magical mirror from <i><a href="/wiki/Snow_White_and_the_Seven_Dwarfs_(1937_film)" title="Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (1937 film)">Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs</a></i> (1937). Also present was a mechanical <a href="/wiki/Magic_carpet" title="Magic carpet">magic carpet</a> in which Spears sat and flew over the first 100 feet above the crowd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-journal_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-journal-8">[8]</a></sup> Spears, who had five costumes changes during the show, was joined on stage by eight dancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-rolling-4">[4]</a></sup> The setlist consisted of nine songs, seven from her debut album and two unreleased songs from her then upcoming album, <i><a href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_(album)" title="Oops!... I Did It Again (album)">Oops!... I Did It Again</a></i> (2000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-rolling-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concert_synopsis">Concert synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=(You_Drive_Me)_Crazy_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Concert synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The show opened with a skit in which the dancers came out of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Locker_(cabinet)" title="Locker (cabinet)">lockers</a> and stayed in the stage until a bell rang. They all sat until a female teacher voice started calling their names. After the teacher called Spears, she emerged at the top of the staircase in a cloud of smoke, wearing a top and white stretch pants, to perform a short dance mix of "...Baby One More Time".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-rolling-4">[4]</a></sup> She then entered one of the lockers and appeared in another one on the opposite side of the stage to perform "(You Drive Me) Crazy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stpeter_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-stpeter-9">[9]</a></sup> Spears briefly talked to the audience, the segment continued with performances of "Born to Make You Happy" and "I Will Be There". After a dance interlude, Spears appeared onstage sitting on the magic carpet and flew over the audience while singing "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Let_Me_Be_the_Last_to_Know" title="Don't Let Me Be the Last to Know">Don't Let Me Be the Last to Know</a>". When she returned to the stage, she performed another song from her upcoming album, "<a href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_(song)" title="Oops!... I Did It Again (song)">Oops!... I Did It Again</a>". Spears addressed the audience again before the "Who is the Ultimate Heartbreaker?" interlude, in which her dancers picked a boy from the audience and invited him onstage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmlife_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-cmlife-10">[10]</a></sup> Spears took to the stage again wearing a jacket and dedicated the performance of "From the Bottom of My Broken Heart" to the boy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stpeter_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-stpeter-9">[9]</a></sup> She took off her jacket to reveal a pair of black pants that featured a sequined red heart in the back and performed "The Beat Goes On". After two interludes that presented her dancers and band, Spears appeared onstage to perform "Sometimes". The encore consisted of a dance-oriented performance of "...Baby One More Time".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmlife_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-cmlife-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=(You_Drive_Me)_Crazy_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jae-Ha Kim of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> said that Spears "showed why she has got a leg up on blonder competitors such as <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Simpson" title="Jessica Simpson">Jessica Simpson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Moore" title="Mandy Moore">Mandy Moore</a>. Aguilera may have a better voice (and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> to validate it), but Spears has that 'it' factor that worked for pinup queens of the past, such as <a href="/wiki/Farrah_Fawcett" title="Farrah Fawcett">Farrah Fawcett</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chicago_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-chicago-11">[11]</a></sup> Adam Graham of <a href="/wiki/Central_Michigan_Life" title="Central Michigan Life">Central Michigan Life</a> commented that "although the show was only about 10 songs long and the authenticity of her voice was in question throughout, it was really truly hard to walk away feeling anything but completely gratified".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmlife_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-cmlife-10">[10]</a></sup> Dave Tianen of the <i><a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Journal_Sentinel" title="Milwaukee Journal Sentinel">Milwaukee Journal Sentinel</a></i> believed that the show "was energetic, good-humored, fast-paced and bright".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-journal_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-journal-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the tour, accusations of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lip_synch" title="Lip synch">lip synching</a> arose. Spears talked to <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> about the accusations, saying,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"There's a delay in the screen above me, so if you listen to the music and watch the screen, they don't sync up. I think that confuses people. But I'm singing every song. I'm singing my ass off. [...] There are times during the show, when I'm dancing so much, where I get out of breath, and we have a signal where I'm dying and they'll help me out. Believe me, I'd give anything to do a show where I just sit there and sing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rolling_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-rolling-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
