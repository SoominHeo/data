<p><b>Gala Inc.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社ガーラ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gala <a href="/wiki/Kabushiki_gaisha" title="Kabushiki gaisha">Kabushiki gaisha</a></i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo,_Japan" title="Tokyo, Japan">Tokyo, Japan</a>, that administers <b>GALA Group</b>, which is made up of subsidiary companies of Gala Inc. The group of companies embraces three types of business: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MMORPG" title="MMORPG">MMORPG</a> games, web design and <a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">data mining</a>. Within the group companies, Gala Lab Corp., established after the merger of Aeonsoft and nFlavor,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> located in South Korea, develops games for the group's online gaming portal <i>gPotato</i>. GALA Group focuses on developing massively multiplayer online role-playing games. To cover local areas, there are Group companies located in the USA, Japan and South Korea.</p>
<p>GALA Group's games are <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> so no annual/monthly payment is required; revenue is generated from a <a href="/wiki/Micropayment" title="Micropayment">micropayment</a> system. Games and their premium item shops can be accessed by the Group's regional <a href="/wiki/GPotato" title="GPotato">gPotato</a> portal sites.</p>
<p>The majority of GALA Group's business revolves around MMORPG and smartphone games. Within GALA Group, Gala Web Inc.'s main business was in web design, but this company was sold to Tribeck Strategies, Inc. in 2015.</p>
<p>In February 2013, Gala-Net, Gala Networks Europe and Gala-Net Brazil were acquired by <a href="/wiki/Webzen" title="Webzen">Webzen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gpotato_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gpotato-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Years">Early Years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early Years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gala Inc. was originally founded in September 1993 by <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Kikugawa" title="Satoru Kikugawa">Satoru Kikugawa</a> to create an <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> company with the goal of bringing people together from around the world. While at <a href="/wiki/Keio_University" title="Keio University">Keio University</a>, Kikugawa was an avid event planner where he would rent out clubs in <a href="/wiki/Roppongi" title="Roppongi">Roppongi</a> and students could gather. He called these events "Gala". With the expansion of the internet in the early nineties, Kikugawa, inspired by his university events, founded Gala Inc. with the goal of bringing people together online.</p>
<p>The early business model of Gala Inc. was in homepage creation. One of Gala Inc.'s first website creations was for the <a href="/wiki/All_Japan_Pro_Wrestling" title="All Japan Pro Wrestling">All Japan Pro Wrestling</a> site in 1996. The initial business approach was to create home pages free of charge and then share the advertising revenue. Gala Inc. also set up bulletin boards on sites for visitors to share their thoughts. In February 2000, Gala Inc. acquired the company RIS Inc., which was then renamed to Gala Web Inc. While Gala Inc. acts as the headquarters for GALA Group, Gala Web continues to develop websites and systems for clients in Japan. Gala Inc.'s early business model eventually led to Gala's gaming business model, where its games are free and sales are generated from <a href="/wiki/Micropayment" title="Micropayment">micropayments</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Globalization">Globalization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Globalization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2005, Gala-Net Inc. opened as the North American subsidiary and online games publisher with its office in <a href="/wiki/Sunnyvale,_California" title="Sunnyvale, California">Sunnyvale, California</a>. In October 2006, Gala-Net established a wholly owned subsidiary, Gala Networks Europe based out of Dublin, Ireland.</p>
<p>Also in 2006, South Korean game developers Aeonsoft Inc. and nFlavor Corp. joined GALA Group. Aeonsoft's flagship game was <i><a href="/wiki/Flyff" title="Flyff">Flyff</a></i>, while nFlavor brought the <i><a href="/wiki/Rappelz" title="Rappelz">Rappelz</a></i> MMO into the Group. The two game developing companies would later merge to become Gala Lab Corp. in July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In December 2007, another subsidiary was created after Gala Inc. split off its buzz research section to create a new Group company named Gala Buzz Inc. The company's role was to offer data mining and information analysis services for corporate clients through its e-mining and buzz reports. The company was sold to Hottolink Inc. in May 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2011, Gala Inc. announced it will enter the smartphone market. The company will launch a smartphone social game platform and the development of games for said platform. Gala Pocket Inc. was established to operate the gPumpkin platform and Gala Lab Corp. and 3rd party developers will develop the games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gala_Inc_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gala_Inc-5">[5]</a></sup> Gala Inc. also expanded in 2011 by opening a branch office in Seoul, South Korea that has been tasked with handling the technical issues across all of GALA Group. In May 2012, that branch was transferred to Gala Lab also located in Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiaries">Subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Subsidiaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_Publishing">Game Publishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Game Publishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The companies publish games by the Gala Lab development teams in Seoul and other third party developers. The publishing companies in GALA Group are:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Gala Innovative Inc.</b> (California, U.S.A.) - was in the mobile games industry doing both publishing and developing, but is currently dormant. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.gala.jp/english/group.html" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></li>
<li><b>Gala Japan Inc.</b> <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%AC%E3%83%BC%E3%83%A9%E3%82%B8%E3%83%A3%E3%83%91%E3%83%B3" title="jp:ガーラジャパン">ガーラジャパン</a> (Tokyo, Japan) - Gala Japan is the publisher for games in the Japanese market and was established in April 2007. Games localized into Japanese offered through their <a class="external text" href="http://www.gpotato.jp/" rel="nofollow">gPotato.jp</a> portal site are <i>Flyff</i> and <i>Rappelz</i>. In 2015 Gala Pocket Inc., which was involved in smartphone and tablet games, was merged into Gala Japan.</li>
<li><b>Gala Lab Corp.</b> (Seoul, South Korea) - Gala Lab was created by the 2010 merger of former GALA Group companies Aeonsoft Inc. and nFlavor Corp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The merged company now publishes games for the Korean market through their <a class="external text" href="http://www.gpotato.kr/" rel="nofollow">gPotato.kr</a> portal site and also develops games for GALA Group as well as third party publishers.</li>
<li><b>Gala Connect Inc.</b> (Seoul, South Korea) - Gala Connect was established in October 2014 and is involved in developing smartphone games.</li>
<li><b>Gala MIX Inc.</b> (Seoul, South Korea) - Gala MIX was established in September 2015 and is involved in developing smartphone applications.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_Development">Game Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Game Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>Gala Lab Corp.</b> (Seoul, South Korea) - Gala Lab Corp. was created by the 2010 merger of former GALA Group companies Aeonsoft Inc. and nFlavor Corp. Aeonsoft Inc., founded in 2002 in Seoul, developed the game <i><a href="/wiki/Flyff" title="Flyff">Flyff</a></i>, meaning "Fly for Fun". nFlavor Corp., founded in 2003 in Seoul, developed the games <i><a href="/wiki/Rappelz" title="Rappelz">Rappelz</a></i>, <i>Street Gears</i> and <i>IL: Soulbringer</i>.</li>
</ul>
<ol>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Flyff" title="Flyff">Flyff</a></i>, or "Fly for Fun", is the first MMORPGs to introduce free roaming flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>Flyff</i> is a party-oriented MMO game where no character can do everything and efficient play requires working together in groups to level up by defeating monsters. One distinctive feature is its flying system; flying is the normal method of transportation for characters above level 20. The game offers a vast amount of content that is constantly being updated, and it caters to audiences both young and old. In June 2004, the game was awarded the "Best Game of the Year" from South Korea's Ministry of Culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Rappelz" title="Rappelz">Rappelz</a></i> is set in a medieval fantasy world, dominated by three races: the Deva, who represent light, the Asura, who represent darkness, and the Gaia, humans that possess an affinity with nature. Each major installment of the game is referred to as an "Epic". The current installment, which is the second part of Epic VII, is called "Epic VII: Dimensions". The game was also published in Arabic for <a href="/wiki/MENA" title="MENA">MENA</a> gamers by the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">Emirati</a> company Game Power 7. The game was released under a different title known as <i><b>Hope of Nations</b></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar"><big>أمل الشعوب</big></span>‎‎), and was launched on March 16, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Street Gears</i> is based on a persistent manga-style universe. It is a massively multiplayer roller game where players can challenge each other's in races or trick contests. As for how to use the tricks, it's related to the context and environment of the game. This means that some tricks can only be used while skating on the ground and/or during a jump, while others can be used while in the air, on walls or when there's a barrier that requires bypassing.</li>
<li><i>Eternal Blade</i> is a fantasy/anime MMORPG with a new concept of “Postmodern RPG” and Pioh System where Piohs (pet-type creatures) evolve at three stages that aid the player during the course of the game. The game also has a randomized map system to add to gameplay variety.</li>
</ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_Portal">Game Portal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Game Portal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To promote and publish games, GALA Group uses its <a href="/wiki/GPotato" title="GPotato">gPotato</a> <a href="/wiki/Game_portal" title="Game portal">game portal</a>. People can play or download games from the gPotato portal for free. gPotato services downloadable client-based games. The gPotato portal launched in November 2005 in the USA and 2007 in Europe, and was sold to Webzen in 2013. The gPotato portal and brand is one of the main assets of GALA Group. Currently, gPotato portals are serviced by two subsidiary companies of Gala Inc.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of September 2011, the global gPotato community has over 20 million registered members<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gala_Inc_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gala_Inc-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> playing on 18 games globally. As with all <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> games or gaming communities, the number of registered users has to be differentiated from:</p>
<ul>
<li>Unique users; i.e. the number of <i>persons</i> actually registered as differentiated from the number of accounts (a same user can make several accounts, all counted as a "registered user").</li>
<li>Active users (users returning regularly).</li>
<li>Paying users (since all of gPotato's games are F2P, it's possible to play without paying anything).</li>
</ul>
<p>There are no public numbers for unique, active, or paying users.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portal_Features">Portal Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Portal Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>gPotato portals have a variety of features. Each gPotato portal is designed and maintained based on the cultural trends where that gPotato portal is serviced so the features vary on each gPotato portal site. In general, the portals offer:</p>
<ul>
<li>Profile page</li>
<li>Achievements</li>
<li>gPotato balance and fill-up</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Currency">Currency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Currency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Virtual_economy" title="Virtual economy">virtual currency</a> is called gPotatoes. The gPotatoes can be used to obtain virtual game items via a premium item shop. Other users normally would not have access to these items or would have to spend more time in-game to obtain them. gPotatoes can be purchased using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Credit_cards" title="Credit cards">credit cards</a>, <a href="/wiki/PayPal" title="PayPal">PayPal</a>, charging through the mobile phone, through gPotato pre-paid cards or through earning free gPotatoes by taking online surveys. Payment methods differ from each gPotato portal based on the regional payment options available.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Insider_Trading_Incident">Insider Trading Incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Insider Trading Incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, three Gala Inc. employees were directed to pay fines for <a href="/wiki/Insider_trading" title="Insider trading">insider trading</a> when they purchased stock with insider knowledge that Gala had secured a deal with <a href="/wiki/Dentsu" title="Dentsu">Dentsu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On January 13, 2006, it was announced that the three were the first employees in Japan ordered to pay fines under a new law created by the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Services_Agency" title="Financial Services Agency">Financial Services Agency</a> of Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Group CEO Mr. Kikugawa gave a public apology regarding the insider trading. Gala also released a statement outlining the changes to their compliance management system and procedures put in place to prevent insider trading from happening again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
