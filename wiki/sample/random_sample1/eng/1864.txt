<p><b>Gala Gordon</b> (born 26 March 1991) is an English <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gala Gordon was born in <a href="/wiki/Bryanston" title="Bryanston">Bryanston</a>. Her mother, Patricia MacTaggart, is a London-based <a href="/wiki/Interior_design" title="Interior design">interior designer</a> and her father, Xavier Botana, is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine_people" title="Argentine people">Argentinian</a> artist who lives in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>. Gala, named after <a href="/wiki/Salvador_Dal%C3%AD" title="Salvador Dalí">Salvador Dalí</a>’s wife, chose her mother’s maiden name as her surname. She attended <a href="/wiki/Bryanston_School" title="Bryanston School">Bryanston School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, and graduated in 2012 from London's <a href="/wiki/Guildhall_School_of_Music_and_Drama" title="Guildhall School of Music and Drama">Guildhall School of Music and Drama</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-standard_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-standard-1">[1]</a></sup> where she played lead roles in several stage productions such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seagull" title="The Seagull">The Seagull</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Agamemnon" title="Agamemnon">Agamemnon</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Adventures_of_Nicholas_Nickleby" title="The Life and Adventures of Nicholas Nickleby">The Life and Adventures of Nicholas Nickleby</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduation, Gordon made her professional stage debut in 2012, playing the lead role of Irina in <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Andrews" title="Benedict Andrews">Benedict Andrews</a>’ production of <a href="/wiki/Anton_Chekhov" title="Anton Chekhov">Chekhov</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/Three_Sisters_(play)" title="Three Sisters (play)">Three Sisters</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Young_Vic" title="Young Vic">Young Vic</a> starring opposite <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Kirby" title="Vanessa Kirby">Vanessa Kirby</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-standard_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-standard-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013 she filmed her first feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Kids_in_Love_(film)" title="Kids in Love (film)">Kids in Love</a></i> about a group of modern <a href="/wiki/Bohemianism" title="Bohemianism">bohemian</a> friends. The main cast also includes <a href="/wiki/Cara_Delevingne" title="Cara Delevingne">Cara Delevingne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alma_Jodorowsky" title="Alma Jodorowsky">Alma Jodorowsky</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modelling">Modelling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Modelling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gordon is represented by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NEXT_Model_Management" title="NEXT Model Management">NEXT Model Management</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She appeared in fashion magazines such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Vogue" title="British Vogue">British Vogue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/LOVE_(magazine)" title="LOVE (magazine)">LOVE</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lula_Magazine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lula Magazine (page does not exist)">Lula</a></i>, and on the covers of <i><a href="/wiki/Tatler" title="Tatler">Tatler</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Madame_Figaro" title="Madame Figaro">Madame Figaro</a></i> shot by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Smith_(fashion_designer)" title="Paul Smith (fashion designer)">Paul Smith</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014 Gordon featured in <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Cavalli" title="Roberto Cavalli">Roberto Cavalli</a> Class line campaign fall Winter 2014/15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She's the face of <a href="/wiki/Bella_Freud" title="Bella Freud">Bella Freud</a>'s fragrance <i>1970</i>, launched in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gordon lives in <a href="/wiki/Notting_Hill" title="Notting Hill">Notting Hill</a>. She has known <a href="/wiki/Cara_Delevingne" title="Cara Delevingne">Cara Delevingne</a> since they were 13 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
