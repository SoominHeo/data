<p><b>Galați</b> (<small>Romanian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Romanian" title="Help:IPA for Romanian">[ɡaˈlat͡sʲ]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Ro-Gala%C8%9Bi.ogg" title="File:Ro-Galați.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f2/Ro-Gala%C8%9Bi.ogg" title="Ro-Galați.ogg">listen</a>)</small>; also known by other <a href="#Etymology_and_names">alternative names</a>) is the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_County" title="Galați County">Galați County</a>, in the historical region of <a href="/wiki/Moldavia" title="Moldavia">Moldavia</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>. Galați is a port town on the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2011, the Romanian census recorded 249,432 residents,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INSSER_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-INSSER-1">[1]</a></sup> making it the 8th <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cities_in_Romania" title="Cities in Romania">most populous city</a> in Romania. Galați is a major economic centre based around the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Gala%C8%9Bi" title="Port of Galați">port of Galați</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gala%C5%A3i_shipyard" title="Galaţi shipyard">naval shipyard</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_steel_works" title="Galați steel works">ArcelorMittal Galați</a> steel plant and mineral exports.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_and_names">Etymology and names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology and names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Galați" is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Cuman_language" title="Cuman language">Cuman</a> word <i>galat</i>. This word is borrowed from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> word قَلْعَةٌ <i>qalʿat</i>, "fortress". Other <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">etymologies</a> have been suggested, such as the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> <i>galac</i>. However, the <i>galat</i> root appears in nearby toponyms, some of which show clearly a Cuman origin, for example <b>Gălățui Lake</b>, which has the typical Cuman -<i>ui</i> suffix for "water". Another toponym in the region is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galicia_(Central_Europe)" title="Galicia (Central Europe)">Galicia</a>, with its town of <a href="/wiki/Halych" title="Halych">Halych</a>, locally associated with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jackdaw" title="Jackdaw">jackdaw</a> (Kawka, Halka). Before the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasion_of_Rus%27" title="Mongol invasion of Rus'">Mongol invasion of Rus</a>, Galați was known as Malyi Halych (Little Halych) as part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Galicia%E2%80%93Volhynia" title="Kingdom of Galicia–Volhynia">Kingdom of Galicia–Volhynia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Other similar place names are <a href="/wiki/Galich,_Russia" title="Galich, Russia">Galich, Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galatia" title="Galatia">Galatia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a> might be a derivation from <i>Galatia</i>, thus suggesting a <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> origin. A similar pattern occurs with the Celtic-sounding word, <a href="/wiki/Ligures" title="Ligures">Ligures</a>, referring to "Caladaa" *=(a city of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">the Ligurian or Genoan territories</a>). Galați has several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exonyms" title="Exonyms">exonyms</a>: <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el">Γαλάτσι</span> (Galatsi); <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de"><i>Galatz</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <span lang="hu"><i>Galac</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl"><i>Gałacz</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr"><i>Kalas</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg">Галац</span> (Galats); <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Галац</span> (Halats) and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Галац</span> (Galats).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_origins">Ancient origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ancient origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Archeological evidence points to occupation of the region in the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">neolithic</a> period. For example, north west of the town of Galați, on the eastern shores of the Malina marshes, fragments of ceramic-type Stoicani Aldeni, stilex and tools made of bone have been found. A stone sceptre, from the late Bronze Age, belonging to the Coslogeni culture was found on the marshes' southern bank. Galați town itself developed from an ancient <a href="/wiki/Dacia" title="Dacia">Dacian</a> settlement of the sixth and fifth centuries BCE where there was a ford across the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> river. In 101 to 102 and 105 to 106, the <a href="/wiki/Dacians" title="Dacians">Dacians</a> fought wars against the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a> and the area became part of the Roman empire. A <a href="/wiki/Castra_of_Tirighina-B%C4%83rbo%C8%99i" title="Castra of Tirighina-Bărboși">strong Roman fortress</a> was built at Barboși to defend the ford across Danube. From the 300s a Daco-Roman settlement developed at a ford south of the site of the Church of the Virgin.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medieval_period">Medieval period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Medieval period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There is evidence of continuous inhabitation of Galați since the 600s. A treasure hoard consisting of 12 silver coins issued between 613 and 685 was found in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine" title="Byzantine">Byzantine</a> tomb near the Church of the Virgin. Western and Byzantine coins from the time of Emperor Michael IV (1034–1041) were also found. At one time<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (November 2013)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup>, the city became part of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">Republic of Genoa Territories</a> and was called "Caladda".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1445, a document signed by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_II_of_Moldavia" title="Stephen II of Moldavia">Stephen II of Moldavia</a> mentions Galați. In 1484, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiliya,_Ukraine" title="Kiliya, Ukraine">Chilia</a> was conquered by <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottomans</a>. Galați township remained <a href="/wiki/Moldavia" title="Moldavia">Moldova's</a> only port, not only for domestic trade but also for trade with Turkey and Poland. In 1590, the Galați Jewish cemetery was opened.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Burial_of_Hetman_Mazeppa">Burial of Hetman Mazeppa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Burial of Hetman Mazeppa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Ukrainian folk hero, Ivan Stepanovich <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Mazepa" title="Ivan Mazepa">Mazeppa</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Hetmans_of_Ukrainian_Cossacks" title="Hetmans of Ukrainian Cossacks">Hetman</a> who died on 1709 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Varni%C5%A3a,_Anenii_Noi" title="Varniţa, Anenii Noi">Varniţa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bessarabia" title="Bessarabia">Bessarabia</a>, and was buried in Galați. Hetman Mazeppa was buried in a brick <a href="/wiki/Burial_vault_(tomb)" title="Burial vault (tomb)">tomb</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_St.C4.83nile.C8.99ti">Battle of Stănilești</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Battle of Stănilești">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1710, Tatars plundered Galați after the <a href="/wiki/Pruth_River_Campaign" title="Pruth River Campaign">Battle of Stănilești</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Russo-Turkish_war">Russo-Turkish war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Russo-Turkish war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1775, Russia established a consulate in Galați. However, in 1789, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1787%E2%80%9392)" title="Russo-Turkish War (1787–92)">Russo-Turkish war</a> of 1787–92, Galați was burned by the armies of the Russian general <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Kamensky" title="Mikhail Kamensky">Mikhail Kamensky</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to unrest in this part of Europe, Galați port became a site for the construction of large warships. Abbot Boskov, a Romanian traveller, stated:</p>
<dl>
<dd>"I saw a large boat, the way those who say Turks caravels, which is on site, ready to be launched into the water. He was commissioned by Isaac-aga, big tax collector of Constantinople. The ship was huge, seventeen seventy steps, and loaded with eighty-four bronze cannons."</dd>
</dl>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Massacre_of_1821">Massacre of 1821</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Massacre of 1821">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the Greek–Turkish war of 1821, Ottoman subjects were killed in Galați (and in other towns). This was the result of a series of rebellions by members of the port workers' association and city clerks. <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (April 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth_through_trade">Growth through trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Growth through trade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Despite the wars and unrest, Galați developed based on trade (especially grain exports). In 1805, France and England established vice-consulates. In 1832, the School of the Holy Archangels Michael and Gabriel is founded. Two years later, in 1834, Austrian ships were having scheduled arrivals and in 1837, Galați was declared a free port (this was revoked in 1882). In 1850, <a href="/wiki/James_Buchanan" title="James Buchanan">James Buchanan</a>, the U.S. president, sent a vice-consul to Galați and the U.S. opened a consulate in 1858. Galați was also a trading port for German lands. When the <a href="/wiki/Crimean_War" title="Crimean War">Crimean War</a> (1854–1856) finished, Galați became a seat of the <a href="/wiki/Commissions_of_the_Danube_River" title="Commissions of the Danube River">Danube European Commission</a>. In 1869, the Mihai Eminescu municipal park opened and by 1870, factories were opening. By, 1908 they numbered 41. On 13 September 1872, the King Charles I railway station and northern city rail tunnel opened. The River station opened shortly after on 24 September 1880. In 1889, the V. A. Urechia library opened.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth_through_unity">Growth through unity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Growth through unity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/United_Principalities" title="United Principalities">union of the Romanian principalities</a> in 1859, with <a href="/wiki/Alexandru_Ioan_Cuza" title="Alexandru Ioan Cuza">Alexandru Ioan Cuza</a> as <a href="/wiki/Domnitor" title="Domnitor">leader</a>, development in Galați increased. Zeletin wrote,</p>
<dl>
<dd>"The birth of the modern Romanian state must distinguish two main currents – one exuberant but superficial, based on liberal ideas which depart from Paris to Bucharest and Iași. The other is quieter but deep, leaving from London to Galați and Brăila: English is the current capitalist economy." (<i>Romanian Bourgeoisie</i>, Zeletin, Stephen.)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1900.E2.80.931947">1900–1947</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 1900–1947">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Between 1900 and the beginning of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, Galați continued its trade in grain and timber with sixteen consulates. Galați was part of <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Romania" title="Counties of Romania">Covurlui County</a>.</p>
<p>In 1907, social unrest among the peasant classes precipitated intervention by the Romanian army. In 1911, a statue of the poet <a href="/wiki/Mihai_Eminescu" title="Mihai Eminescu">Mihai Eminescu</a> was erected.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gala%C8%9Bi" title="Battle of Galați">Battle of Galați</a></div>
<p>Galați remained in the Romanian control during <a href="/wiki/Romania_during_World_War_I" title="Romania during World War I">World War I</a>. Romanian soldiers fought with those of Russia against the army of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Galați was bombed by the Russians in 1919.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stoica1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stoica1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Interwar_period">Interwar period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Interwar period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1919, a high school for Jewish students opened. A first air race between Galați and Bucharest was held in 1926. The 1930 Romanian census recorded 100,000 residents in Galați. After <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="Chișinău">Chișinău</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ia%C8%99i" title="Iași">Iași</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chernivtsi" title="Chernivtsi">Cernăuți</a>, Galați was Romania's fifth city. In 1938, the <a href="/wiki/%C8%9Ainutul_Dun%C4%83rii" title="Ținutul Dunării">Ținutul Dunării de Jos</a> (Lower Danube Land) was established.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_war_II">World war II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: World war II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/Romania_in_World_War_II" title="Romania in World War II">World War II</a>, Galați was bombed by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Air_Forces" title="Soviet Air Forces">Soviet Air Forces</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a>. The railway station, inaugurated on 13 September 1872, was destroyed as were many other historic buildings and most of the old town. On 27 August 1944, Galați was captured by <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Soviet</a> troops of the <a href="/wiki/3rd_Ukrainian_Front" title="3rd Ukrainian Front">3rd Ukrainian Front</a> in the course of the <a href="/wiki/Jassy%E2%80%93Kishinev_Offensive" title="Jassy–Kishinev Offensive">Jassy–Kishinev Offensive</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Jewish_community">Jewish community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Jewish community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Before World War II Galați hosted 22 synagogues and in 1926, it was the base for the Zionist Revisionist Organization of Romania. Although Galați's Jewish community suffered persecution by the pro-Nazi authorities during World War II, the community was not destroyed in the Holocaust. Since the 1940s the community has gradually diminished through emigration.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communist_period_.281947.E2.80.931989.29">Communist period (1947–1989)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Communist period (1947–1989)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_war_rebuilding">Post war rebuilding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Post war rebuilding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After world war II, Galați was rebuilt along communist lines. The town's population was about 80,000. In 1956, a proposal to join Brăila and Galați was made and the building of a speed tram pier was proposed with a Swiss company. The plans never materialized. Construction of the <a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_steel_works" title="Galați steel works">Galați steel works</a> began in 1960 and the plant opened in 1966. The population at this time was 151,000. In 1971, the <a href="/wiki/F%C4%83urei" title="Făurei">Făurei</a> – Galați railway capacity was increased, and, in 1982, it was electrified.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mogo.C8.99oaia_boating_tragedy">Mogoșoaia boating tragedy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Mogoșoaia boating tragedy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On September 11, 1989, a Bulgarian ship collided with a cruise ship near Galați. 151 passengers and crew died (all but sixteen).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Contemporary_period">Contemporary period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Contemporary period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Romania's first information technology park opened in Galați.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_flood">2005 flood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: 2005 flood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 14 July 2005, Galați was affected by widespread flooding. The Siret river reached record levels and across Romania, the death toll reached 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2010, broken flood barriers caused flooding of the Valley City area of Galați.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Topography">Topography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Topography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Galați is located in southeastern Romania. Its coordinates are latitude 45 ° 27 'north and longitude 28 ° 02' east. Its area is 246.4 square kilometers (95.1 sq mi). Galați lies in the southern part of the <a href="/wiki/Moldavian_Plateau" title="Moldavian Plateau">Moldavian Plateau</a> on the left (west) bank of the Danube river at the junction of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siret_River" title="Siret River">Siret River</a> (west) and the <a href="/wiki/Prut" title="Prut">Prut River</a> (east), near Lake Brates. It is 80 kilometers (50 mi) from the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>. The nearest town is <a href="/wiki/Br%C4%83ila" title="Brăila">Brăila</a>, 15 kilometers (9.3 mi) to the south. Galați is built on three geological terraces. One lies beneath "Valley City", with elevations between 5 and 7 metres (16 and 23 ft). The other two, which make a fan shape, have elevations of 20–25 meters (66–82 ft) (the site of the old town, now the city centre) and 40 meters (130 ft) (the site of the modern city) respectively. The Danube river is considered the "life-blood" of Galați. The Danube is the second longest river in Europe 2,850 km [ 1,770 mi ]), with an average flow of 6,199 cubic meters (218,900 cu ft) per second in the Galați section. After the Siret River, the largest tributary in Romania, joins the Danube, the flow is 210 cubic meters per second (7,400 cu ft/s). After the Prut River joins the Danube, the flow becomes 86 cubic meters per second (3,000 cu ft/s). The Danube river flows have significant seasonal variation. The maximum flows occur in May; 18,000–19,000 m<sup>3</sup>/s or 640,000–670,000 cu ft/s. The minimum flows occur during the summer; 2,000–2,450 m<sup>3</sup>/s or 71,000–87,000 cu ft/s.</p>

<p>Galați has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">continental</a> climate (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> "Dfa" – summer wetter than winter, European subtype) with four distinct seasons. Summers are very warm with temperatures sometimes exceeding 35 °C (95 °F) while winters are cold and dry with temperatures at night sometimes dropping below −10 °C (14 °F). Average monthly precipitation ranges from about 28 to 68.6 mm (1.1 to 2.7 in) in June.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_interest">Places of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Places of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_camp_at_Tirighina-Barbosi">Roman camp at Tirighina-Barbosi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Roman camp at Tirighina-Barbosi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Castra_of_Tirighina-B%C4%83rbo%C8%99i" title="Castra of Tirighina-Bărboși">Castra of Tirighina-Bărboși</a></div>
<p>From 1959 to 1962, archeological excavations were carried out at the Roman castellum in Barbosi. The site lies north of the Danube, near the mouth of the Siret. A comparison can be made to the fortress at <a href="/wiki/Dinogetia" title="Dinogetia">Dinogetia</a>. The archeologists found clear stratigraphy in the excavation. They located the Tirighina Dacian fortress, reinforced by an earthen bank. Finds included Dacian pottery from an import business. Also found was a silver coin dating the Dacian fortress to the first century BCE. A burning layer suggested damage to the fortress by fire. Also found were coins dating from the rule of <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a>(63 BCE – 14 AD) through to <a href="/wiki/Nero" title="Nero">Nero</a> (37AD – 68AD).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lambrinidi_House">Lambrinidi House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Lambrinidi House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GL-II-m-B-03014_Casa_Lambrinidi.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1892" data-file-width="2488" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/GL-II-m-B-03014_Casa_Lambrinidi.JPG/220px-GL-II-m-B-03014_Casa_Lambrinidi.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/GL-II-m-B-03014_Casa_Lambrinidi.JPG/330px-GL-II-m-B-03014_Casa_Lambrinidi.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/GL-II-m-B-03014_Casa_Lambrinidi.JPG/440px-GL-II-m-B-03014_Casa_Lambrinidi.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>This house is located near the university, at 51 Royal Street. It was built by Lambrinidi Epaminonda, a Greek mill owner. He owned a machine shop to repair ships. The Prince Charles stayed at the house on a visit to Galați, in April 1879. It has served as the seat of the Court of Appeals; the Mihail Kogalniceanu High School and, after the earthquake of November 9, 1940, the city hall.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Robescu_House">Robescu House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Robescu House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Robescu House was built about 1896. The architect was <a href="/wiki/Ion_Mincu" title="Ion Mincu">Ion Mincu</a>. A Romanian tourist website describes the building: :"Robescu House has an elevated basement, two levels with two turret-balcony on the first floor and another balcony at the ground floor, to the street. The exterior decorations are made in Brancoveanu style, glossy ceramic plates. The glossy ceramic is used to decorate the floral upper register. Today it serves as the "Children's Palace".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Palace_of_Navigation_.28River_Station.29">Palace of Navigation (River Station)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Palace of Navigation (River Station)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This building was designed by <a href="/wiki/Petre_Antonescu" title="Petre Antonescu">Petre Antonescu</a>. It dates to the late 19th century. It is the office of "Maritime Danube Ports Administration" and Navrom, which controls Romania's navigable waterways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="University_Palace">University Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: University Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This former "Palace of Justice" is a prominent architectural monument in the Galați old town. It was built between 1911 and 1913. Now, it is the university headquarters.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lupa_Capitolina">Lupa Capitolina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Lupa Capitolina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Meaning "Capitoline Wolf", this statue was erected in 1995 and reflects Roman influence in Galați.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_Palace">Administrative Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Administrative Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This building was erected in 1904 and 1905 and opened on 27 April 1906. Like Robescu House, it was designed by Ion Mincu. Frederick Storck contributed white marble sculptures of "industry" and "agriculture" at the top of the main façade. Other features are two bronze flags and a large clock which plays the waltz, "Danube Waves" by Joseph Ivanovich (1845–1902).</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Archdiocesan_Cathedral_of_the_Lower_Danube_.E2.80.93_St._Andrew">Archdiocesan Cathedral of the Lower Danube – St. Andrew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Archdiocesan Cathedral of the Lower Danube – St. Andrew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The cornerstone of the cathedral of the archbishop of Galați was placed on 27 April 1906 by Prince Ferdinand and Queen Mary. Also present was the Bishop of the Lower Danube, Pimen Georgescu. Construction continued from 1906 to 1917. The architects were Petre Antonescu and Stephen Burcuș. The church has one dome and no side isles. From 1989, restoration work has been underway at the church. This includes cleaning of the 1957 paint work, paving around the cathedral and the construction of the St John Cassian centre, a building for cultural, pastoral and missionary work. At the church's centennial in 2006, the relics of <a href="/wiki/Nectarios_of_Aegina" title="Nectarios of Aegina">St. Nectarios of Aegina</a> were brought to Galați.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fortified_Monastery_of_the_Holy_Virgin">Fortified Monastery of the Holy Virgin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: Fortified Monastery of the Holy Virgin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg/220px-Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg/330px-Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg/440px-Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Biserica_Fortificata_Precista_Galati.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Precista fortified church</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This is the oldest building in Galați. It was consecrated as a place of worship in September 1647 during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Vasile_Lupu" title="Vasile Lupu">Vasile Lupu</a>. The church was dedicated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vatopedi_Monastery" title="Vatopedi Monastery">Vatopedi Monastery</a> on <a href="/wiki/Mount_Athos" title="Mount Athos">Mount Athos</a>. It was built from local materials including stone, forest wood, brick and lime, sand from the beaches of the Danube and so on. As well as its typical Romanian church architecture, the monastery has some specific elements of interest such as a bell tower with battlements for observation of the Danube valley and for defense. The tower has two levels and a room to hide assets. There are two ramparts and a door to a balcony. The second floor is fitted with windows and battlements. Another of the church's defences is a reinforced bridge consisting of two parts, one above the nave and one above the altar. The bridge has 28 battlements. The church survived the turbulent periods of Galați's history and was rebuilt and restored in 1829 and again in 1859. From 1953 to 1957, the church was used as a museum. It was restored in the period 1991–1994. There may be a tunnel from the church beneath the Danube.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bulgarian_Church">Bulgarian Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=39" title="Edit section: Bulgarian Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="918" data-file-width="625" height="323" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg/220px-Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg/330px-Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg/440px-Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bulgarian_church_Galati.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Bulgarian Church in Galați</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Construction of the Bulgarian Orthodox Church of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Pantaleon" title="Saint Pantaleon">Saint Pantaleon</a> began in 1861 under the guidance of <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_National_Revival" title="Bulgarian National Revival">Bulgarian National Revival</a> figure Archimandrite <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maxim_Raykovich&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maxim Raykovich (page does not exist)">Maxim Raykovich</a> (1801–1874) of <a href="/wiki/Dryanovo" title="Dryanovo">Dryanovo</a>. However, the lack of funds prolonged the construction works and the church was only consecrated in 1887. The Bulgarian Church of Saint Pantaleon features a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neo-Byzantine" title="Neo-Byzantine">Neo-Byzantine</a> design with Slavic influences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cilinca_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-cilinca-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Along with the church, Raykovich also founded a Bulgarian school which occupied an adjacent house. The same house would become a hub for Bulgarian revolutionaries persecuted by the Ottoman authorities. In 1871–72, Bulgaria's national poet <a href="/wiki/Hristo_Botev" title="Hristo Botev">Hristo Botev</a> lived in the house during a part of his exile, as a commemorative plaque still reminds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cilinca_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-cilinca-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Raykovich died in Galați in 1874 and was buried in the courtyard of the Bulgarian Church, where his tombstone still stands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cilinca_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-cilinca-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greek_Church">Greek Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=40" title="Edit section: Greek Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The cornerstone of the Greek Church was laid on 6 August 1866. The church was dedicated to the "Transfiguration" on 17 September 1872 by the Bishop and the <a href="/wiki/Archimandrite" title="Archimandrite">Archimandrite</a> Melchizedek Eughenie Xiropotamo. In the nave is a marble plaque with the names of the founders and two marble plates with the names of the founders and major benefactors. The church is a cruciform tower with two bell towers on the west side. Papadopoulos of Adrianople was the artist. There are large icons representing the holy virgin Mary with child, the resurrection, St. Gerasimos, St. Sophia, St. Gregory and St. Basil the great. There are eight stained glass church windows containing the holy apostles Peter, Andrew, Mark, Thomas, Bartholomew and Luke on the northern side and Paul Simon, John, James, Philip, on the southern side.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="St._Paraskeva_Cathedral">St. Paraskeva Cathedral</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: St. Paraskeva Cathedral">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Parascheva_of_the_Balkans" title="Parascheva of the Balkans">This church</a> is located together with the Cătușa cemetery in the southern part of Galați. Its general construction was completed but the interior layout is not. The basement of the Cathedral is a very small space, which will serve as a mortuary chapel. The official opening of the interior was planned for 2012.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Church_Mavromol">Church Mavromol</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: Church Mavromol">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Mavromol is a former monastery. Its name means "black rock" in Greek. It was built in 1669 and dedicated to the Assumption by <a href="/wiki/George_Ducas" title="George Ducas">George Ducas</a> (died 1685) and his son. There was rebuilding between 1700 and 1703. During the revolution of 1821 the church was burned by the Turks. The current building dates from 1858 to 1861 and respects the original plan. Interior murals and other valuable elements were restored between 1973 and 1975. The monks of the church taught at Galați's first school in 1765. They taught in Greek. (In 1803, teaching was conducted in Romanian under the orders of Constantin Moruz). During the Russo-Turkish War (1768–1774), and the Russo-Austrian-Turkish War (1787–1792), Mavromol church was damaged and teaching ceased until 1803. The church is constructed in the style of a <a href="/wiki/Basilica" title="Basilica">basilica</a> with <a href="/wiki/Apse" title="Apse">apses</a> beyond the main walls. The church has a carved wooden icon of the "Virgin Mary" from the church of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Sava_National_College" title="Saint Sava National College">St. Sava Monastery</a> in Bucharest. The ceiling is composed of three parts separated by double arches. The entrance is on the south side through a door marked by an icon and heraldic symbols: a bull's head and two lions. Also on the south side is the coat of arms of Moldova, carved in a stone medallion. During communist rule, the church, as a sacred place, was improperly appropriated.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Church_of_St._Spyridon">The Church of St. Spyridon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: The Church of St. Spyridon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Built in 1817, this church has four buttresses, three towers and a dome supported by crossed arches. Although its murals are not maintained, it contains valuable icons.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Synagogue_of_Gala.C8.9Bi">Synagogue of Galați</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: Synagogue of Galați">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>There is only one remaining <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogue</a> in Galați (near the Museum of History on Domnească street).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Masonic_Temple">Masonic Temple</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=45" title="Edit section: Masonic Temple">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The temple was built in 1896 on the site of a synagogue dating from 1806.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Schitul_Vovidenia">Schitul Vovidenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=46" title="Edit section: Schitul Vovidenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This church was built in 1790. It is the site of the burial tomb of Smaranda Cuza, the mother of Alexandru Ioan Cuza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The original church was burned in the unrest of 1821 then renovated in 1851. Above the nave, a dome is supported by arches crossed in the traditional Moldovan style. There is a bell tower above the main dome, a smaller one above the altar and another above the porch added in 1901.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Catholic_Church">Roman Catholic Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=47" title="Edit section: Roman Catholic Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Galați's Roman Catholic church was built in 1844 and expanded in 1873. It contains objects of artistic as well as sacred value including a high altar made of marble; a painting of "St. John the Baptist preaching"; a "Most Holy Heart" statue of Jesus Christ; <a href="/wiki/Piet%C3%A0" title="Pietà">Pietà</a> statues and a statue of Saint <a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assisi" title="Francis of Assisi">Francis of Assisi</a>. The church was strengthened and renovated to its current state between 1985 and 1988.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mihai_Eminescu_Municipal_Park">Mihai Eminescu Municipal Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=49" title="Edit section: Mihai Eminescu Municipal Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This park was designed in 1869 in honour of <a href="/wiki/Mihai_Eminescu" title="Mihai Eminescu">Mihai Eminescu</a>, who visited the area, particularly in his later life. some of the trees are from the time of Eminescu. There is a small lake and a place for recitals once used, for example, by military bands. People visit the park to see a statue of Eminescu.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Gala.C8.9Bi_Public_Gardens">Galați Public Gardens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=50" title="Edit section: Galați Public Gardens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This park is located near the student complex. It looks over Lake Brates, the railway station and train depot.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="C.F.R._.28Romanian_State_Railways.29_Park">C.F.R. (Romanian State Railways) Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=51" title="Edit section: C.F.R. (Romanian State Railways) Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The railway park is located north of the railway, near the railway tunnel, the terminus of railway lines (102 and 104) and a nursing home.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Botanical_Garden">Botanical Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=52" title="Edit section: Botanical Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG/220px-Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG/330px-Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG/440px-Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gradina_Botanica_Galati.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Botanical Garden</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Botanical Garden dominates the left bank of the Danube at Galați. Its southern slope is the site of the Museum of Natural History in Galați. The museum has collections of tropical and subtropical plants (cactuses, euphorbias, palms, crotons, geraniums, begonias, etc.) rosary chic, over 200 varieties of roses, flora and vegetation. There are over 3000 specimens of tree species and shrubs, an oak area with more than 50 examples of birch and conifers, and a replica Japanese garden.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Garboavele_Zoo_and_Reserve">Garboavele Zoo and Reserve</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=53" title="Edit section: Garboavele Zoo and Reserve">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Both are located at the northern exit of town. Garboavele Forest Zoo is included in the Natural Sciences Museum Complex. The zoo keeps various exotic species. Riding enthusiasts can practice their sport at the zoo. The zoo theme was developed together with experts from European Association of Zoos and Aquaria, and aims to preserve rare animals and provide conservation education.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hunters_Lake">Hunters Lake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=54" title="Edit section: Hunters Lake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hunters Lake is located in Galați's far north-east. It covers 28 hectares of land including an 11 hectare natural lake; a 10 hectare forest of acacia; and 10 hectares of developed parkland for recreation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Danube_leisure_complex">Danube leisure complex</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=55" title="Edit section: Danube leisure complex">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This park is located outside Galați proper on the road to Brăila. The complex has three swimming pools, houses, camping sites and a restaurant. There is also a <a href="/wiki/Western_(genre)" title="Western (genre)">western</a> club that is equipped with a pool, restaurant, bar and cafe.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Waterfront">Waterfront</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=56" title="Edit section: Waterfront">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The waterfront is a green recreational area for walking, sports competitions and recitals. Boats moored on the shores of the Danube are converted into restaurants. Also, there are many original iron relics and a statue of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicolae_Balcescu" title="Nicolae Balcescu">Nicolae Balcescu</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Danube_Waves_Beach">Danube Waves Beach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=57" title="Edit section: Danube Waves Beach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The beach is located in south-west Galați. Popular in the summer, it has two large pools and three smaller pools for children, each with a slide. There is a beach, some restaurants and shower facilities.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Brate.C8.99_beach">Brateș beach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=58" title="Edit section: Brateș beach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This is a private beach with clean sand situated on the left bank of Lake Brates. Nearby is the Galați–Bereștiul Barlad railway line.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fani_Tardini_drama_theatre">Fani Tardini drama theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=62" title="Edit section: Fani Tardini drama theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This theatre was opened in 1955. It is named after the actress <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fani_Tardini&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fani Tardini (page does not exist)">Fani Tardini</a> (1823–1908). The theatre supports a yearly comedy festival, "Festingal".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Gulliver_Theatre">Gulliver Theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=63" title="Edit section: Gulliver Theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Gulliver Theatre was opened on 1 October 1952. It is devoted to children's theatre such as <a href="/wiki/Puppetry" title="Puppetry">puppetry</a>, for example.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nae_Leonard_theatre">Nae Leonard theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=64" title="Edit section: Nae Leonard theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nae_Leonard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nae Leonard (page does not exist)">Nae Leonard</a> (1886), was known locally as "the prince of the operetta". The theatre named after him was opened in 1956. The theatre was originally called "The theatre of operetta and variety art of Galatz county".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museums">Museums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=65" title="Edit section: Museums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_Sciences_Museum_Complex">Natural Sciences Museum Complex</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=66" title="Edit section: Natural Sciences Museum Complex">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Botanical Gardens on the south bank, hosts a 7-meter (23 ft) planetarium. There is also an aquarium displaying rare species of the Danube basin and the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> as well as exotic fish.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Gala.C8.9Bi_History_Museum">Galați History Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=67" title="Edit section: Galați History Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The museum was inaugurated on 24 January 1939. This was the 80th anniversary of the union of the Romanian principalities. It is housed in the residence of Cuza, Galați's chief magistrate. In 1951, after world war II, scientific activity resumed at the museum. In 1956, some items were donated to the Natural History museum and the museum of art. The finds from Tirighina-Barbosi, are housed in this museum.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alexandru_Ioan_Cuza_Memorial_House">Alexandru Ioan Cuza Memorial House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=68" title="Edit section: Alexandru Ioan Cuza Memorial House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Cuza's former residence houses a retrospective display of his life.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Visual_Arts_Museum">Visual Arts Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=69" title="Edit section: Visual Arts Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>When the Galați visual arts museum opened in 1967, it was the first contemporary arts museum in Romania. It has halls and outdoor exhibit spaces. There are also spaces for artists' workshops. The heritage section exhibits Romanian art in the second half of the nineteenth and avant-garde art from the twentieth century.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Gala.C8.9Bi_Village_Museum">Galați Village Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=70" title="Edit section: Galați Village Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This museum at the entrance to the Garboavele forest has small village buildings to display the traditional Romanian life in the Lower Danube.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="House_of_Collections">House of Collections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=71" title="Edit section: House of Collections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This museum has philatelic and numismatic collections. It is situated near the Memorial House of Alexandru Ioan Cuza.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=72" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1712" data-file-width="2288" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg/220px-MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg/330px-MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg/440px-MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MittalSteelGalati200607.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_steel_works" title="Galați steel works">Arcelor-Mittal Steel Works</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Galați has the largest iron and steel plant in Romania, the <a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_steel_works" title="Galați steel works">ArcelorMittal Galați</a>, which was state-owned until 2001. It is still commonly referred to as "Sidex", its old name.</p>
<p>Romania's <a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_shipyard" title="Galați shipyard">largest shipyard</a> is located in Galați. It benefits from easy access to the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> and from its co-location with the Mittal Plant.</p>
<p>Galați is also a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Moldova%E2%80%93Romania_border_crossings" title="List of Moldova–Romania border crossings">border checkpoint to Moldova</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_proper">City proper</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=74" title="Edit section: City proper">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of <a href="/wiki/Demographic_history_of_Romania" title="Demographic history of Romania">2011 census</a> data, 249,432 inhabitants live within the city limits,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INSSER_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-INSSER-1">[1]</a></sup> a decrease from the figure recorded at the 2002 census.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnography">Ethnography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=75" title="Edit section: Ethnography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The largest ethnic group in Galați are <a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanians</a> (98.54%). The second largest but less than 1% of the total are the <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Roma</a> (0.78%). In smaller numbers are <a href="/wiki/Lipovans" title="Lipovans">Lipovans</a> (0.07%), <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a> (0.07%) and <a href="/wiki/Hungarians" title="Hungarians">Hungarians</a> (0.09%). Other groups number 0.27% in all.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metropolitan_area">Metropolitan area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=76" title="Edit section: Metropolitan area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lower_Danube_metropolitan_area" title="Lower Danube metropolitan area">Lower Danube metropolitan area</a></div>
<p>The metropolitan area is proposed to be formed from the cities of Galați and <a href="/wiki/Br%C4%83ila" title="Brăila">Brăila</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=77" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gala%C5%A3i_University_-_Dun%C4%83rea_de_Jos,_founded_1974..jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1158" data-file-width="1800" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Gala%C5%A3i_University_-_Dun%C4%83rea_de_Jos%2C_founded_1974..jpg/220px-Gala%C5%A3i_University_-_Dun%C4%83rea_de_Jos%2C_founded_1974..jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Gala%C5%A3i_University_-_Dun%C4%83rea_de_Jos%2C_founded_1974..jpg/330px-Gala%C5%A3i_University_-_Dun%C4%83rea_de_Jos%2C_founded_1974..jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Gala%C5%A3i_University_-_Dun%C4%83rea_de_Jos%2C_founded_1974..jpg/440px-Gala%C5%A3i_University_-_Dun%C4%83rea_de_Jos%2C_founded_1974..jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Galați has educational facilities catering to all ages and scholastic levels. School Nr. 24 (Holy Archangels Michael and Gabriel) is the oldest school in the county. It was founded in 1832. In 1896 it was located inside the Mitoc Monastery. On 26 October 1896 the current school building was inaugurated.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Gala%C8%9Bi" title="University of Galați">University of Galați</a>, founded in 1974, includes 11 faculties (with two unique faculties in Romania: <i>The Faculty of Naval Architecture</i> and <i>Department of Food Science and Engineering</i>) and 3 colleges.</p>
<p>The "Danubius University" is a private institution with departments in legal, economic and social studies fields.</p>
<p>Galați also hosts several specialized research institutes. Four are unique within Romania: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Icepronav&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Icepronav (page does not exist)">Icepronav</a> (Research and Design Institute for Shipbuilding), Uzinsider Engineering (Institute for Research and Design for flat metal products), the Research Institute of Fish and the Wine Research Station Targu Bujor.</p>
<p>In February 2013, Galați was the host city of the general meeting of the <a class="extiw" href="https://ro.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consiliul_Jude%C8%9Bean_al_Elevilor" title="ro:Consiliul Județean al Elevilor">National Student Association</a>, a forum of the Romanian Ministry of Education.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries">Libraries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=78" title="Edit section: Libraries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="V._A._Urechia_Library">V. A. Urechia Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=79" title="Edit section: V. A. Urechia Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1889, the V. A. Urechia Library was founded through the benevolence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasile_Alexandrescu_Urechia" title="Vasile Alexandrescu Urechia">Vasile Alexandrescu Urechia</a>. In 1991, the library moved to a new purpose built site. The library maintains its own local history collection as well as a rare book collection.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="LCMHF">LCMHF</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=80" title="Edit section: LCMHF">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This library was established in 1939. It is associated with teacher training and hosts an active literary circle.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=82" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_railway_station" title="Galați railway station">Galați railway station</a> has train services to cities such as <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Constan%C8%9Ba" title="Constanța">Constanța</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bra%C8%99ov" title="Brașov">Brașov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ia%C8%99i" title="Iași">Iași</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=83" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transport">Public transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=84" title="Edit section: Public transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Public transport in Galați is provided by a combination of <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">trams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trolleybus" title="Trolleybus">trolleybuses</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autobus" title="Autobus">autobuses</a> (all of which are operated by TRANSURB, an agency of the municipality), and <a href="/wiki/Minibus" title="Minibus">minibuses</a> (operated by a number of private companies).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gala.C8.9Bi_in_popular_culture">Galați in popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=85" title="Edit section: Galați in popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Explosion"><i>Explosion</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=86" title="Edit section: Explosion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Explosion</i> is a communist film made entirely in Galați. It is based on the true story of an incident where a Panamanian vessel, loaded with ammonium nitrate, threatened to explode and destroy Galați and Brăila. <a class="external text" href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0123829/?ref_=fn_al_tt_1" rel="nofollow">"Explozia"</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dracula"><i>Dracula</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=87" title="Edit section: Dracula">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Bram_Stoker" title="Bram Stoker">Bram Stoker</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Dracula" title="Dracula">Dracula</a></i>, Galați (Galatz) is the port at which <a href="/wiki/Count_Dracula" title="Count Dracula">Count Dracula</a> disembarked on his way back to <a href="/wiki/Transylvania" title="Transylvania">Transylvania</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Meta_Tempesta"><i>Meta Tempesta</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=88" title="Edit section: Meta Tempesta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is a film about tunnels built in the ruins of Tirighina being used to protect Germans in World War II. It was based on a play called <i>The road to Constanta</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Danube_Waves"><i>Danube Waves</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gala%C8%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=89" title="Edit section: Danube Waves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is a film made in <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>, in the "Film Production Center", Buftea. It was directed by Liviu Ciulei, from a screenplay by Dumitru Munteanu Caraba and Francis. It stars Irina Petrescu, who makes her debut as the wife of a barge driver. Lazarus Vrabie plays an undercover officer. Ciulei is the barge driver. Lucian Pintilie played the part of a German soldier.</p>

<p>Video of Galați is seen in tourist documentaries, documentaries about World War II and clips of Lautars singers.</p>
