<p><b><a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a></b> competed at the <b><a href="/wiki/1924_Summer_Olympics" title="1924 Summer Olympics">1924 Summer Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>; this was the third <a href="/wiki/India_at_the_Olympics" title="India at the Olympics">Olympics where India participated</a>. Some months ahead of the Olympics, in Feb 1924, the All India Olympic Games (that later became the <a href="/wiki/National_Games_of_India" title="National Games of India">National Games of India</a>) were held at Delhi. A few sportsmen from these games were selected to represent India at the 1924 Paris Olympics: Dalip Singh, Lakshmanan, Hinge, Hall, Pala Singh, Pitt, Heathcote, and Venkataraman. The Indian team eventually comprised seven athletes and seven-eight tennis players (<a class="external text" href="http://www.thehindu.com/sport/tennis/when-polley-made-history-in-paris/article3635901.ece" rel="nofollow">including two women</a>), with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Buck" title="Harry Buck">Harry Crowe Buck</a> as manager. The team <a class="external text" href="http://www.newindianexpress.com/education/edex/article578298.ece?service=print" rel="nofollow">trained</a> at the Madras (YMCA) College of Physical Education where Buck was founding principal.</p>
