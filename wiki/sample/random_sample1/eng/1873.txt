<p><b>Gali</b> (<a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>: <span lang="ka">გალი</span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Georgian" title="Help:IPA for Georgian">[gɑli]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Gali.ogg" title="File:Gali.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/44/Gali.ogg" title="Gali.ogg">listen</a>)</small>; <a href="/wiki/Abkhaz_language" title="Abkhaz language">Abkhaz</a>: <span lang="ab">Гал</span>, <i>Gal</i>) is a town in <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, 77 km southeast to <a href="/wiki/Sukhumi" title="Sukhumi">Sukhumi</a> in region <a href="/wiki/Abkhazia" title="Abkhazia">Abkhazia</a>. It is the centre of <a href="/wiki/Gali_District,_Abkhazia" title="Gali District, Abkhazia">Gali District</a> and was in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> security zone prior to the Russian veto of the UNOMIG Mission in 2009. As of 2011 the town has a population of 7 605 inhabitants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gali_(town)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gali was granted town status in 1932.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 April 1953, the newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Izvestia" title="Izvestia">Izvestia</a></i> reported that the residents of Gali were celebrating the 132nd birthday of its oldest resident, Tlabgan Ketsba, and that he had founded the town in the 19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aworld895_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-aworld895-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
