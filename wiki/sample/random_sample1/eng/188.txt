<p>The <b>Daegu Line</b> is a railway line in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. The line connects <a href="/wiki/Gacheon_Station" title="Gacheon Station">Gacheon Station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Gyeongbu_Line" title="Gyeongbu Line">Gyeongbu Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a> to <a href="/wiki/Yeongcheon_Station" title="Yeongcheon Station">Yeongcheon</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Jungang_Line" title="Jungang Line">Jungang Line</a>. The line is served by frequent passenger trains between <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> (via the Gyeongbu Line), Dongdaegu, and <a href="/wiki/Gyeongju" title="Gyeongju">Gyeongju</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pohang" title="Pohang">Pohang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ulsan" title="Ulsan">Ulsan</a> (via the Jungang and <a href="/wiki/Donghae_Nambu_Line" title="Donghae Nambu Line">Donghae Nambu Lines</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daegu_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first section of the Daegu Line was opened in 1917, between <a href="/wiki/Daegu_Station" title="Daegu Station">Daegu</a> and Hayang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relocate_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-relocate-1">[1]</a></sup> The line was extended to the Haksan station in <a href="/wiki/Pohang" title="Pohang">Pohang</a> until 1919 as follows:</p>

<p>A branch was opened from <a href="/wiki/Gyeongju" title="Gyeongju">Gyeongju</a> to <a href="/wiki/Taehwagang_Station" title="Taehwagang Station">Ulsan</a> on October 25, 1921.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relocate_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-relocate-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The sections from Gyeongju to Pohang and Ulsan were integrated into the <a href="/wiki/Donghae_Nambu_Line" title="Donghae Nambu Line">Donghae Nambu Line</a> on December 16, 1935. On 1 July 1938 the reconstruction of the section <a href="/wiki/Daegu_Station" title="Daegu Station">Daegu</a>–Yeongcheon was complete with the standard gauge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 1 December 1938 the section Yeongcheon–Gyeongju became a part of the Gyeonggyeong Nambu Line (the southern part of the <a href="/wiki/Jungang_Line" title="Jungang Line">Jungang Line</a>), which was established on April 1, 1942. At the same time the present section was renamed the Daegu Line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Upgrade">Upgrade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daegu_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Upgrade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 14 km (8.7 mi) section from Dongdaegu to Cheongcheon was replaced by a new alignment that takes the Daegu Line to meet the Gyeongbu Line at <a href="/wiki/Gacheon_Station" title="Gacheon Station">Gacheon</a>, lengthening the Dongdaegu–Cheongcheon line distance to 16.5 km (10.3 mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relocate_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-relocate-1">[1]</a></sup> The Dongdaegu to Cheongcheon rail distance changed to 38.4 km (23.9 mi),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relocate_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-relocate-1">[1]</a></sup> line proper from Gacheon to Yeongcheon is 29.0 km (18.0 mi) long.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lineop_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-lineop-4">[4]</a></sup> The project was launched in August 1997 with a planned completion in 2000, but economic difficulties and planning changes delayed completion, the line relocation was finally inaugurated on November 3, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relocate_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-relocate-1">[1]</a></sup> Part of the project was the replacement of a 1.3 km (0.81 mi) branch to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daegu_Airport" title="Daegu Airport">Daegu Airport</a> from the old alignment with a 9.04 km (5.62 mi) spur line paralleling the highway west from Cheongcheon to the airport, finished a year later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relocate_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-relocate-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Daegu Line was considered for an upgrade to a double-tracked, electrified railway in a straighter, 34.9 km (21.7 mi) long alignment from 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KRNA_2010_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-KRNA_2010-5">[5]</a></sup> Detailed plans were prepared by 2009, the foreseen budget was 988.042 billion won, and the completion of the project was set for 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KRNA_2010_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-KRNA_2010-5">[5]</a></sup> On September 1, 2010, the South Korean government announced a strategic plan to reduce travel times from Seoul to 95% of the country to under 2 hours by 2020. As part of the plan, the Daegu Line is to be set out for 230 km/h and may see <a href="/wiki/Korea_Train_Express" title="Korea Train Express">KTX</a> service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plans2020_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-plans2020-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
