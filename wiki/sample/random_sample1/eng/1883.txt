<p>The <b>Gan River</b> flows through the western part of <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> province, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, before flowing into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Poyang" title="Lake Poyang">Lake Poyang</a> and thence into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Yangtze River</a>.</p>
<p>The Xiang-Gan uplands separate it from the <a href="/wiki/Xiang_River" title="Xiang River">Xiang River</a> of neighbouring eastern <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is the major geographical backbone of Jiangxi, and gives its name to the <a href="/wiki/Gan_Chinese" title="Gan Chinese">Gan language</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The river feeds into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Poyang" title="Lake Poyang">Lake Poyang</a>, which in turns connects with the <a href="/wiki/Yangtze" title="Yangtze">Yangtze</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
