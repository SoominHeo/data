<p><b>Gamma Ray</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Power_metal" title="Power metal">power metal</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, founded and fronted by <a href="/wiki/Kai_Hansen" title="Kai Hansen">Kai Hansen</a> after his departure from the German power metal band <a href="/wiki/Helloween" title="Helloween">Helloween</a>. Hansen is the current lead vocalist, <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" title="Guitarist">guitarist</a> as well as the chief songwriter of Gamma Ray. The band is known as one of the most prominent bands of the <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Germany#Metal" title="Music of Germany">German heavy metal scene</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma_Ray_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma_Ray_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1988, after four years with the German <a href="/wiki/Power_metal" title="Power metal">power metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Helloween" title="Helloween">Helloween</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" title="Guitarist">guitarist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a> <a href="/wiki/Kai_Hansen" title="Kai Hansen">Kai Hansen</a> decided, for reasons that are still the subject of much debate, to leave the group. Hansen claimed that Helloween had become too big for him to handle, although the group's troubles with financial issues and their record company, <a href="/wiki/Noise_Records" title="Noise Records">Noise Records</a>, most likely played a part as well. He proceeded to do some studio work with German <a href="/wiki/Power_metal" title="Power metal">power metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Blind_Guardian" title="Blind Guardian">Blind Guardian</a> and in 1989 decided to form his own project with long-time friend <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Scheepers" title="Ralf Scheepers">Ralf Scheepers</a>, former vocalist of the band Tyran Pace. This two-man project grew into a four-man band with the addition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uwe_Wessel" title="Uwe Wessel">Uwe Wessel</a> on bass and Mathias Burchardt on drums. This was the first line-up of Gamma Ray, bearing a sound understandably close to that of Helloween of that period.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Scheepers_era_.281989.E2.80.931995.29">The Scheepers era (1989–1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma_Ray_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Scheepers era (1989–1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The original line-up released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Heading_for_Tomorrow" title="Heading for Tomorrow">Heading for Tomorrow</a></i> in February 1990 and later that year the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heaven_Can_Wait_(EP)" title="Heaven Can Wait (EP)">Heaven Can Wait EP</a></i>, with new guitarist <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Schl%C3%A4chter" title="Dirk Schlächter">Dirk Schlächter</a> and new drummer <a href="/wiki/Uli_Kusch" title="Uli Kusch">Uli Kusch</a>.</p>
<p>In February 1991, the band began rehearsing for the recording of their second album in a small, remote house in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>. With some brand new songs written, Gamma Ray entered the studio under the supervision of producer Tommy Newton and recorded their second album <i><a href="/wiki/Sigh_No_More_(Gamma_Ray_album)" title="Sigh No More (Gamma Ray album)">Sigh No More</a></i>, which was released in September 1991. The style differed vastly from that of <i>Heading for Tomorrow</i>, featuring darker lyrics inspired by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf_War" title="Persian Gulf War">Persian Gulf War</a> that was raging at the time. A 50-date worldwide tour followed.</p>
<p>After the Japanese tour at the beginning of 1991, Gamma Ray underwent another personnel change: the rhythm section (Wessel and Kusch) left due to a personal disagreement and were replaced by Jan Rubach (bass) and Thomas Nack (drums), both from the <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> band Anesthesia. The band also began to build their own studio, so work on their new album didn't start until 1993. The album <i><a href="/wiki/Insanity_and_Genius" title="Insanity and Genius">Insanity and Genius</a></i> was released in June 1993, with a style which was closer to that of <i>Heading for Tomorrow</i> than <i>Sigh No More</i>. In September 1993 Gamma Ray, along with <a href="/wiki/Rage_(German_band)" title="Rage (German band)">Rage</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Helicon_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Helicon (band) (page does not exist)">Helicon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conception_(band)" title="Conception (band)">Conception</a>, embarked on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Melodic_Metal_Strikes_Back&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Melodic Metal Strikes Back (page does not exist)">Melodic Metal Strikes Back</a> tour. The tour contributed to the release of the double CD <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Power_of_Metal_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Power of Metal (album) (page does not exist)">The Power of Metal</a></i>, and the videos <i>Power of Metal</i> and <i>Lust for Live</i>, in December.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Land_of_the_Free_to_Majestic_.281995.E2.80.932006.29"><i>Land of the Free</i> to <i>Majestic</i> (1995–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma_Ray_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Land of the Free to Majestic (1995–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>More changes in the lineup were to follow for Gamma Ray. Vocalist Ralf Scheepers, who lived far away from the other band members hometown of <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>, was attempting to become the new <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">Judas Priest</a> singer after <a href="/wiki/Rob_Halford" title="Rob Halford">Rob Halford</a> left. He felt that his position in the band had been strained due to the distance between him and the other members. Hansen and Scheepers agreed to an amicable departure. After failing to be recruited for Judas Priest, Scheepers started his own band, <a href="/wiki/Primal_Fear_(band)" title="Primal Fear (band)">Primal Fear</a>. Hansen then began to search for a new vocalist but, due to demand from friends and fans, took on the guitar-vocal duties himself as he had done for Helloween's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helloween_(album)" title="Helloween (album)">first EP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walls_of_Jericho_(album)" title="Walls of Jericho (album)">album</a>.</p>
<p>In a 2008 interview, Hansen spoke about the importance of the Land of the Free album and what it represented: "We made it exactly at a time point when this kind of metal was proclaimed to be dead as can be. Where it was almost like if a drummer came up with a double bass drum people would say ‘ ya dooga daga yourself out of here man.’ Everything was ruled by Kurt Cobain and the alternative to the alternative and all that kind of stuff. So at that point we made an album like this and it went down very successful. That was cool, that was something special. I think it was the album that gave Gamma Ray the acceptance as being a band not only a Kai Hansen project."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1995 the fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Land_of_the_Free_(Gamma_Ray_album)" title="Land of the Free (Gamma Ray album)">Land of the Free</a></i>, the first to feature Hansen on vocals, was released. The album was universally praised by critics and fans alike. The tour following the album, <i>Men on a Tour</i>, brought the recording and release of the live album <i><a href="/wiki/Alive_%2795" title="Alive '95">Alive '95</a></i> in 1996.</p>

<p>Soon after, there was yet another major lineup change. Jan Rubach and Thomas Nack both left in order to return to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anesthesia_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anesthesia (band) (page does not exist)">Anesthesia</a> and in came new <a href="/wiki/Drummer" title="Drummer">drummer</a> <a href="/wiki/Dan_Zimmermann" title="Dan Zimmermann">Dan Zimmermann</a>. Zimmerman is also known for being a founding member of the German power metal band <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Call" title="Freedom Call">Freedom Call</a>. Schlächter, who was originally a bass player, as can be seen on the 1990 video <i><a href="/wiki/Heading_for_the_East" title="Heading for the East">Heading for the East</a></i>, left the second guitar spot and took back his original instrument, being replaced on guitar by <a href="/wiki/Henjo_Richter" title="Henjo Richter">Henjo Richter</a>.</p>
<p>Work started on the next full-length album, and 1997 saw the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Somewhere_Out_in_Space" title="Somewhere Out in Space">Somewhere Out in Space</a></i>, which marked the beginning of the band's thematic concentration on space. The album featured the hit "Valley of the Kings" and entered in the charts of many European countries. After two years of touring came the album <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Plant_(Gamma_Ray_album)" title="Power Plant (Gamma Ray album)">Power Plant</a></i>, which was a continuation of <i>Somewhere Out in Space'</i>s lyrical approach, but a new direction musically. The album was highly acclaimed throughout the world and gained even more chart success than its predecessor.</p>
<p>For Gamma Ray's next work, Hansen decided to do things differently from the usual compilation of songs. He let the fans decide by voting on their website for their three favorite songs on each album, then the band went back to the studio to re-record the old tracks from the first three albums and made remixed versions for the songs on the later ones. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blast_from_the_Past_(Gamma_Ray_album)" title="Blast from the Past (Gamma Ray album)">Blast from the Past</a></i> was the name chosen for this double album.</p>
<p>After a break of one year, when Hansen concentrated on his side project <a href="/wiki/Iron_Savior" title="Iron Savior">Iron Savior</a>, the band was ready for the recording and release of the album <i><a href="/wiki/No_World_Order_(Gamma_Ray_album)" title="No World Order (Gamma Ray album)">No World Order</a></i>, which was stylistically similar to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NWOBHM" title="NWOBHM">NWOBHM</a> bands such as <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">Judas Priest</a>. Again, the album was highly praised and the <i>No Order World Tour</i> saw the band visiting dozens of European countries and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. After resting from the tour, the band went on with the <i>Skeletons in the Closet</i> tour, which saw the band performing songs that they never or rarely played live before. Once again, the setlist was voted by the fans on the band's website. Only a few shows were played on this tour but two of those were recorded for the live album <i><a href="/wiki/Skeletons_in_the_Closet_(Gamma_Ray_album)" title="Skeletons in the Closet (Gamma Ray album)">Skeletons in the Closet</a></i>.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Kai_Hansen" title="Kai Hansen">Kai Hansen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henjo_Richter" title="Henjo Richter">Henjo Richter</a> also participated in <a href="/wiki/Tobias_Sammet" title="Tobias Sammet">Tobias Sammet</a>'s project <a href="/wiki/Avantasia" title="Avantasia">Avantasia</a>, on both <a href="/wiki/The_Metal_Opera" title="The Metal Opera">The Metal Opera</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Metal_Opera_Part_II" title="The Metal Opera Part II">The Metal Opera Part II</a>, along with various other musicians including Hansen's former bandmates from <a href="/wiki/Helloween" title="Helloween">Helloween</a>, <a href="/wiki/Markus_Grosskopf" title="Markus Grosskopf">Markus Grosskopf</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kiske" title="Michael Kiske">Michael Kiske</a>.</p>
<p>A live DVD, <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_Yeah!_The_Awesome_Foursome" title="Hell Yeah! The Awesome Foursome">Hell Yeah - The Awesome Foursome (And The Finnish Keyboarder Who Didn't Want To Wear His Donald Duck Costume) Live in Montreal</a></i> was recorded on May 6, 2006 at Le Medley in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. The setlist contained songs drawn from all of their albums up to <i><a href="/wiki/Majestic_(album)" title="Majestic (album)">Majestic</a></i> (excluding <i>Insanity and Genius</i>) and also a cover of the <a href="/wiki/Helloween" title="Helloween">Helloween</a> hit "<a href="/wiki/I_Want_Out" title="I Want Out">I Want Out</a>". The DVD finally saw release on November 4, 2008. It entered the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> media control charts at No. 9 and the <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> charts at No. 1, according to the band's official website.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Land_of_the_Free_II_to_Empire_of_the_Undead.2C_and_additional_vocalist_.282007-present.29"><i>Land of the Free II</i> to <i>Empire of the Undead</i>, and additional vocalist (2007-present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma_Ray_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Land of the Free II to Empire of the Undead, and additional vocalist (2007-present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In recent years, Gamma Ray have made use of touring keyboard players to fully augment their sound in a live environment. <a href="/wiki/Axel_Mackenrott" title="Axel Mackenrott">Axel Mackenrott</a> of <a href="/wiki/Masterplan_(band)" title="Masterplan (band)">Masterplan</a> fulfilled these duties in the past and was followed by Eero Kaukomies, a <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finn</a> who also plays in a Gamma Ray <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tribute_band" title="Tribute band">tribute band</a> named <i>Guardians of Mankind</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cover_band_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cover_band-2">[2]</a></sup> His bandmate <a href="/wiki/Kasperi_Heikkinen" title="Kasperi Heikkinen">Kasperi Heikkinen</a> also played on part of the <i>Majestic</i> tour in 2006 following an injury to <a href="/wiki/Henjo_Richter" title="Henjo Richter">Henjo Richter</a>. On their most recent "To The Metal" tour,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gamma_Ray_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gamma_Ray-3">[3]</a></sup> Kasperi Heikkinen replaced Henjo Richter once again for shows scheduled in Germany and Czech Republic in March 2010. Richter was hospitalized on March 16, 2010 due to <a href="/wiki/Retinal_detachment" title="Retinal detachment">retinal detachment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Heikkinen also shared stage with fellow axemen Hansen and Richter making "a three guitar special" for the encore numbers at the Nosturi club in Helsinki, Finland on March 29, 2010.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Land_of_the_Free_II" title="Land of the Free II">Land of the Free II</a></i> was released in late 2007 as a sequel to the hugely successful <i>Land of the Free</i> album. To promote the album, Gamma Ray were the "very special guest" on <a href="/wiki/Helloween" title="Helloween">Helloween</a>'s Hellish Rock 2007/2008 World Tour, on some shows along with the band <a href="/wiki/Axxis" title="Axxis">Axxis</a>. For the final encores of the evening, Kai and members of Gamma Ray joined Helloween to play a couple of songs from when Kai was in the latter band. Kai would also regularly join Helloween co-founder <a href="/wiki/Michael_Weikath" title="Michael Weikath">Michael Weikath</a> at center stage to the delight of fans of both bands.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/To_the_Metal!" title="To the Metal!">To the Metal!</a></i> was released as the tenth full-length studio album by the band. It was released on January 29, 2010 to modest critical praise, but disappointed some fans, who felt that it was uninspired and a weaker effort than Land of the Free II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 31, 2011, Gamma Ray released an EP entitled <i>Skeletons and Majesties</i>. It contains newly recorded, rarely played material (Skeletons) and acoustic versions of other older songs (Majesties).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hansen stated in an interview in February 2012 that he expected the next Gamma Ray album to be released in January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On September 1, 2012, the band announced Michael Ehré as their new drummer, replacing Daniel Zimmermann after 15 years of band activity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kai Hansen revealed, in an interview with <i>Metal Blast</i> in April 2013 that their eleventh album, <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_the_Undead" title="Empire of the Undead">Empire of the Undead</a> would have a "more thrashy" sound. In the same interview, <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Schl%C3%A4chter" title="Dirk Schlächter">Dirk Schlächter</a> announced that the band would do a headlining tour following its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Empire of the Undead was released in March 2014, despite Gamma Ray's studio being completely destroyed by a fire.</p>
<p>In October 2015, it was announced that Frank Beck would be a new lead vocalist of Gamma Ray, in addition to Kai Hansen. This was due to Hansen's degrading vocals due to lengthy tour schedules, as well as Hansen's desire to have more freedom onstage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_songs">Cover songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma_Ray_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Cover songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gamma Ray have covered numerous bands and musicians during their career, including, but not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Helloween" title="Helloween">Helloween</a> - the former band of Kai Hansen. Some of their songs, written by Kai, are often performed by Gamma Ray live, such as "Ride the Sky," "<a href="/wiki/I_Want_Out" title="I Want Out">I Want Out</a>," "Save Us," "Judas," "Victim of Fate" or "<a href="/wiki/Future_World_(Helloween_song)" title="Future World (Helloween song)">Future World</a>."</li>
<li>The song "Heavy Metal Mania" by the band Holocaust was covered one time in studio and many times live.</li>
<li>Gamma Ray have covered <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">Judas Priest</a>'s "Victim of Changes" with Kai on vocals and "Exciter" with Ralf. It has been stated in various interviews, that Judas Priest is one of Kai Hansen's favourite bands. They are also a favourite of Scheepers, as he left Gamma Ray during the tryouts for the new Judas Priest vocalist. He ultimately lost to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tim_Owens" title="Tim Owens">Tim "Ripper" Owens</a>. Additionally, Gamma Ray's song "Wings of Destiny" (where it is being sung "..on sad wings of destiny..") could be linked to Judas Priest's <a href="/wiki/Sad_Wings_of_Destiny" title="Sad Wings of Destiny">Sad Wings of Destiny</a>, the album from which "Victim of Changes" originates.</li>
<li>They also covered two <a href="/wiki/Uriah_Heep_(band)" title="Uriah Heep (band)">Uriah Heep</a> songs: "<a href="/wiki/Look_at_Yourself" title="Look at Yourself">Look at Yourself</a>" on the <i><a href="/wiki/Heading_for_Tomorrow" title="Heading for Tomorrow">Heading for Tomorrow</a></i> CD with Scheepers on vocals, and "Return to Fantasy," sung by Hansen, featured on the 2003 release of <i><a href="/wiki/Somewhere_Out_in_Space" title="Somewhere Out in Space">Somewhere Out in Space</a></i>.</li>
<li>Gamma Ray covered the <a href="/wiki/Pet_Shop_Boys" title="Pet Shop Boys">Pet Shop Boys</a> song "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Sin" title="It's a Sin">It's a Sin</a>" on their 1999 album <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Plant_(Gamma_Ray_album)" title="Power Plant (Gamma Ray album)">Power Plant</a>.</i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbow_(English_band)" title="Rainbow (English band)">Rainbow</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Live_Rock_%27N%27_Roll" title="Long Live Rock 'N' Roll">Long Live Rock 'N' Roll</a>, recorded by Gamma Ray was featured on the 2003 release of the <i>Power Plant</i> album.</li>
<li>Gamma Ray covered the <a href="/wiki/Thin_Lizzy" title="Thin Lizzy">Thin Lizzy</a> song "Angel of Death" on their 2001 single <i>Heaven or Hell</i>, which was released in Japan only.</li>
<li>Gamma Ray have covered "Gamma ray," off the album <i>Hoodoo Man</i>, by <a href="/wiki/Birth_Control_(band)" title="Birth Control (band)">Birth Control</a>.</li>
<li>Gamma Ray covered "Death Or Glory", by Holocaust and "Lost Angels", by The Sweet. Both songs were featured on the EP Master of Confusion released in March 2013</li>
</ul>
