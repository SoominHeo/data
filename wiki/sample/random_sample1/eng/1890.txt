<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physics_and_engineering">Physics and engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Susceptibility&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Physics and engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In physics the <b>susceptibility</b> is a quantification for the change of an <a href="/wiki/Intensive_and_extensive_properties#Extensive_properties" title="Intensive and extensive properties">extensive property</a> under variation of an <a href="/wiki/Intensive_and_extensive_properties#Intensive_properties" title="Intensive and extensive properties">intensive property</a>. The word may refer to:</p>
<ul>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, the susceptibility of a material or substance describes its response to an applied field
<ul>
<li>Examples
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Magnetic_susceptibility" title="Magnetic susceptibility">Magnetic susceptibility</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electric_susceptibility" title="Electric susceptibility">Electric susceptibility</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>The two types of susceptibility above are examples of a <a href="/wiki/Linear_response_function" title="Linear response function">linear response function</a>; sometimes the terms "susceptibility" and "linear response function" are used interchangeably.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_compatibility" title="Electromagnetic compatibility">electromagnetic compatibility</a> (EMC), susceptibility is the sensitivity of a device's function to incoming electromagnetic interference.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_and_medicine">Health and medicine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Susceptibility&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Health and medicine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In epidemiology, a <a href="/wiki/Susceptible_individual" title="Susceptible individual">susceptible individual</a> is a member of a population who is at risk of becoming infected by a disease</li>
<li>In microbiology and pharmacology, antibiotic susceptibility (<a href="/wiki/Antibiotic_sensitivity" title="Antibiotic sensitivity">antibiotic sensitivity</a>) is the susceptibility of bacteria to antibiotics</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Botany_and_environmental_science">Botany and environmental science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Susceptibility&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Botany and environmental science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Susceptibility to <a href="/wiki/Pathogen" title="Pathogen">pathogens</a> is the extent to which a plant, vegetation complex, or ecological community would suffer from a pathogen if exposed, without regard to the likelihood of exposure
<ul>
<li>It should not be confused with vulnerability, which by convention in this field takes into account both the effect of exposure <i>and</i> the likelihood of exposure</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
