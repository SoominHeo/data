<p><b>Potato waffles</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potato</a>-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Savoury_(small_dish)" title="Savoury (small dish)">savoury</a> food in a <a href="/wiki/Waffle" title="Waffle">waffle</a>-like lattice shape. They are common in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a> and are also available in some other countries, including Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>They are made of potato, oil, and <a href="/wiki/Seasoning" title="Seasoning">seasonings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> They are sold <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frozen_Food" title="Frozen Food">frozen</a>. They may be baked, grilled, or fried, and are used as a side dish, often together with sausages or bacon, or as a snack food. The leading brand is <a href="/wiki/Birds_Eye" title="Birds Eye">Birds Eye</a>, who introduced the item in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
