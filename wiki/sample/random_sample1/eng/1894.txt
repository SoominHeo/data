<p><i><b>Gap-dong</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">갑동이</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Gapdongi</span></i>) is a 2014 <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> television series starring <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Sang-hyun" title="Yoon Sang-hyun">Yoon Sang-hyun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sung_Dong-il" title="Sung Dong-il">Sung Dong-il</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Min-jung_(actress)" title="Kim Min-jung (actress)">Kim Min-jung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Ji-won_(actress)" title="Kim Ji-won (actress)">Kim Ji-won</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Joon" title="Lee Joon">Lee Joon</a>. It aired on cable channel <a href="/wiki/TVN_(South_Korea)" title="TVN (South Korea)">tvN</a> from April 11 to June 14, 2014 on Fridays and Saturdays at 20:40 for 20 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series is based on the real-life unsolved <a href="/wiki/Hwaseong_serial_murders" title="Hwaseong serial murders">Hwaseong serial murders</a> case, which involved the <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">rape</a> and <a href="/wiki/Murder" title="Murder">murder</a> of ten women from 1986 to 1991 in the Taean neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Hwaseong,_Gyeonggi" title="Hwaseong, Gyeonggi">Hwaseong City</a>. The case also inspired the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/Memories_of_Murder" title="Memories of Murder">Memories of Murder</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gap-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>1996, the (fictional) city of Iltan in <a href="/wiki/Gyeonggi_Province" title="Gyeonggi Province">Gyeonggi Province</a>: A young girl gets brutally murdered by an unknown person on a dark night. As the city is gripped by a succession of <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">serial killings</a>, a total of nine murders within a twelve-kilometer radius since 1993, the police conclude that a man they've nicknamed "Gap-dong" is behind the crimes. But detective Yang Cheol-gon is convinced that the actual killer is Ha Il-sik, a resident of the town who is <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_disability" title="Intellectual disability">intellectually challenged</a>. His attempt to arrest the suspect fails as Ha commits suicide to prove his innocence. His death inspires his son Moo-yeom to become a cop to clear his father's name and restore his honor.</p>
<p>Seventeen years later, Moo-yeom is a police detective who spends his career chasing dead ends and helping <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juvenile_delinquent" title="Juvenile delinquent">juvenile delinquents</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_limitations" title="Statute of limitations">statute of limitations</a> on the case expires, Moo-yeom becomes resigned to the belief that Gap-dong is dead. But then a series of incidents occur in the town that bear an eerie resemblance to Gap-dong's crimes. Cheol-gon, now a well-decorated officer, has recently transferred back to Iltan, and to his dismay, Moo-yeom joins his investigation team to catch Gap-dong once and for all.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gap-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yoon_Sang-hyun" title="Yoon Sang-hyun">Yoon Sang-hyun</a> as Ha Moo-yeom</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>A <a href="/wiki/Detective" title="Detective">detective</a> in Iltan Police Department's violent crimes unit. He vows to avenge his wrongly accused father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sung_Dong-il" title="Sung Dong-il">Sung Dong-il</a> as Yang Cheol-gon</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>He was a police <a href="/wiki/Inspector" title="Inspector">inspector</a> at the time of the original murders, and is now a well-decorated officer. Upon receiving his latest promotion, he surprises his superiors by requesting to be assigned back to Iltan, because he wants to end his career by capturing Gap-dong. Still convinced that Moo-yeom's father was the killer, he is extremely prejudiced against Moo-yeom, which makes for a combative working relationship. He also hiding grief of his daughter's death.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kim_Min-jung_(actress)" title="Kim Min-jung (actress)">Kim Min-jung</a> as Maria Oh</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>A <a href="/wiki/Psychiatrist" title="Psychiatrist">psychiatrist</a> with a mysterious double life and a suspicious curiosity for all things Gap-dong.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kim_Ji-won_(actress)" title="Kim Ji-won (actress)">Kim Ji-won</a> as Ma Ji-wool</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>A 17-year-old high school student and <a href="/wiki/Webtoon" title="Webtoon">webtoon</a> artist with the <a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">pen name</a> "Matilda." Ji-wool becomes pivotal to the case when her webtoon titled <i>The Beast's Path</i> ends up foretelling the crimes. When she becomes the killer's next target, it also serves as a set of clues to find him.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lee_Joon" title="Lee Joon">Lee Joon</a> as Ryu Tae-oh</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>An inmate of a prison <a href="/wiki/Psychiatric_hospital" title="Psychiatric hospital">psychiatric hospital</a>, who becomes a <a href="/wiki/Barista" title="Barista">barista</a> after his release. With a genius <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IQ" title="IQ">IQ</a> of 150, his excellent and detailed memory may prove valuable to cracking the case, but he also happens to have the disposition to be a dangerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychopath" title="Psychopath">psychopath</a>, and considers Gap-dong his god and hero.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
