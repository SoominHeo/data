<p><i><b>A Jitney Elopement</b></i> was <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Chaplin" title="Charlie Chaplin">Charlie Chaplin</a>'s fifth film for <a href="/wiki/Essanay_Studios" title="Essanay Studios">Essanay Films</a>. It starred Chaplin and <a href="/wiki/Edna_Purviance" title="Edna Purviance">Edna Purviance</a> as lovers, with Purviance wanting Chaplin to take her away from an arranged marriage her father (played by Fred Goodwins) had planned for her. Chaplin does take her away in a jitney, a type of <a href="/wiki/Share_taxi" title="Share taxi">share taxi</a> popular in the US between 1914 and 1916. Most of the film was made in San Francisco and includes scenes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Golden_Gate_Park" title="Golden Gate Park">Golden Gate Park</a> and the large <a href="/wiki/Windmill" title="Windmill">windmills</a> still on the park's west side.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Jitney_Elopement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Purviance's father wants her to marry wealthy Count Chloride de Lime. Chaplin, Purviance's true love, impersonates the Count at dinner.</p>
