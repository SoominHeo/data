<p><i><b>God's Pocket</b></i> is a 2014 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/John_Slattery" title="John Slattery">John Slattery</a> and co-written with Alex Metcalf, based on a <a href="/wiki/1983_in_literature" title="1983 in literature">1983 novel</a> of the same name by <a href="/wiki/Pete_Dexter" title="Pete Dexter">Pete Dexter</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Philip_Seymour_Hoffman" title="Philip Seymour Hoffman">Philip Seymour Hoffman</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Turturro" title="John Turturro">John Turturro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christina_Hendricks" title="Christina Hendricks">Christina Hendricks</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Jenkins" title="Richard Jenkins">Richard Jenkins</a>. The film premiered at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2014 Sundance Film Festival">2014 Sundance Film Festival</a> to mixed critical reviews, and was picked up for domestic distribution by <a href="/wiki/IFC_Films" title="IFC Films">IFC Films</a>. The film is set in a poor <a href="/wiki/Working_class_in_the_United_States" title="Working class in the United States">working class</a> <a href="/wiki/South_Philadelphia" title="South Philadelphia">South Philadelphia</a> neighborhood modeled on <a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Pocket,_Philadelphia" title="Devil's Pocket, Philadelphia">Devil's Pocket</a>, but filmed in Yonkers and New Jersey.</p>
<p>Hoffman died within two weeks of the film's premiere at <a href="/wiki/List_of_films_at_the_2014_Sundance_Film_Festival#GodsPocket" title="List of films at the 2014 Sundance Film Festival">Sundance's 2014 U.S. Dramatic Competition</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=God%27s_Pocket_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>God's Pocket</i> received mixed reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film has a rating of 36% based on 89 reviews, with the critical consensus reading, "Well-cast but frustratingly clichéd, <i>God's Pocket</i> fails to strike a sensible balance between comedy and drama."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film received a score of 51 out of 100, based on 27 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> called it a "half-good effort" that lacked the "snap, precision and stylistic smarts a mixed-tone project like this requires."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Screen International called it "too shaggy and tonally inconsistent to hold together." Stephanie Merry from the <i>Washington Post</i> said, "What began as an intriguing snapshot begins to feel grotesque and inscrutable."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film received some positive reviews from notable critics. <a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> said, "John Slattery's direction is skilled and steady.... Great actors at the top of their game working with rich material."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/David_Edelstein" title="David Edelstein">David Edelstein</a> of <i>New York Magazine</i>/Vulture said, ″Slattery adapted the book with Alex Metcalf and gets the tone just right. The film is damnably amusing.″<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In a Sundance first-look review <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> gave the film 4 out of 5 stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> said the film was ″crafted with great skill.″<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=God%27s_Pocket_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/IFC_Films" title="IFC Films">IFC Films</a> gave the film a limited release on May 9, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Domestically the film widest release was in 80 theaters, generating only $170,000 in box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boxoffmo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-boxoffmo-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Arrow_Films" title="Arrow Films">Arrow Films</a> acquired the UK rights and <a href="/wiki/Electric_Entertainment" title="Electric Entertainment">Electric Entertainment</a> handled the international rights.</p>
