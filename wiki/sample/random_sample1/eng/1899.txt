<p><i><b>Summer Days with Coo</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">河童のクゥと夏休み</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kappa no Kū to Natsuyasumi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "<i>Summer Vacation with Coo the Kappa</i>")</span> is a 2007 <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">Japanese animated</a> film about a <a href="/wiki/Kappa_(folklore)" title="Kappa (folklore)">kappa</a> and its impact on an ordinary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suburban" title="Suburban">suburban</a> family, directed by <a href="/wiki/Keiichi_Hara" title="Keiichi Hara">Keiichi Hara</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Summer_Days_with_Coo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film won the Best Animation Film award at the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mainichi_Film_Award" title="Mainichi Film Award">Mainichi Film Awards</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Japan_Academy_Prize_for_Animation_of_the_Year" title="Japan Academy Prize for Animation of the Year">Best Animation Film</a> at the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Academy_Prize_(film)" title="Japan Academy Prize (film)">Japanese Academy Awards</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and for Best Animated Feature Film at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Asia_Pacific_Screen_Awards" title="Asia Pacific Screen Awards">Asia Pacific Screen Awards</a>. It also won the Grand Prize for Animation at the 11th <a href="/wiki/Japan_Media_Arts_Festival" title="Japan Media Arts Festival">Japan Media Arts Festival</a> and the Feature Film category at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Anime_Award" title="Tokyo Anime Award">Tokyo Anime Award</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Summer_Days_with_Coo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A small, lone Kappa miraculously survives over 200 years into modern day Tokyo, Japan when he is found by young Koichi. "Coo" who is secretly adopted by the whole family, faces a tragic past and the present's challenge of living in a dramatically changed environment, searches with his new human friend for places not invaded by people hoping to find any remaining of his kind. When this intelligent "water spirit" draws the popular attention of the whole city upon being discovered and then confronted with his past, it forces him to take action and make an ultimate decision, in order to preserve his kind, inner peace and well being in today's harsh world.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Summer_Days_with_Coo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Coo (クゥ <i>Kuu</i>) - Coo is the <a href="/wiki/Kappa_(folklore)" title="Kappa (folklore)">Kappa</a> the whole story revolves around. He is found by Koichi Uehara, after he had survived over 200 years as a fossil. He then lives with Uehara family.</li>
<li>Kouichi Uehara (上原康一 <i>Uehara Kōichi</i>0 - A fourth grade student,one day he finds a fossil of a baby Coo who survived for 200 years.</li>
<li>Ossan (オッサン) - The Uehara family dog.</li>
</ul>
