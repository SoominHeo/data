<p><b>Stolidobranchia</b> is an order of <a href="/wiki/Tunicate" title="Tunicate">tunicates</a> in the class <a href="/wiki/Ascidiacea" title="Ascidiacea">Ascidiacea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The group includes both colonial and solitary animals. They are distinguished from other tunicates by the presence of folded pharyngeal baskets. This provides the etymology of their name: in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_greek" title="Ancient greek">ancient greek</a>, <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">στολίς, ίδος</span> means the "fold" of a cloth. Stolidobranchian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_squirts" title="Sea squirts">sea squirts</a> are also characterized by the complete absence of an abdomen. The abdominal organs of other tunicates are instead located to one side of the pharyngeal basket in this group.</p>
