<p><b>Kang Seok-woo</b> (born Kang Man-heung on October 1, 1957) is a South Korean actor. He made his acting debut in 1978 in the <a href="/wiki/Kim_Soo-yong" title="Kim Soo-yong">Kim Soo-yong</a> film <i>Yeosu (The Loneliness of the Journey)</i>, then starred in his first <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television drama</a> <i>Ordinary People</i> in 1982. Kang was most active on the big screen in the 1980s, but has worked exclusively in television since 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has also displayed his artwork in several exhibitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
