<p><b>Kang Song-san</b> (3 March 1931 – 2007)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> politician who served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Premier_of_North_Korea" title="Premier of North Korea">Premier of North Korea</a> from 1984 to 1986 and again from 1992 to 1997. He succeeded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Jong-ok" title="Li Jong-ok">Li Jong-ok</a> in his first term and <a href="/wiki/Yon_Hyong-muk" title="Yon Hyong-muk">Yon Hyong-muk</a> in his second term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Hamgyong" title="North Hamgyong">North Hamgyong</a>. He graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mangyongdae_Revolutionary_School" title="Mangyongdae Revolutionary School">Mangyongdae Revolutionary School</a> and from <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung_University" title="Kim Il-sung University">Kim Il-sung University</a> and went to study in the Soviet Union in <a href="/wiki/Moscow_State_University" title="Moscow State University">Moscow State University</a>. He became an instructor in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea">Central Committee</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Workers' Party of Korea">Workers' Party of Korea</a> in 1955. Candidate member of the political politburo in 1973, Deputy Prime Minister in 1977, the Sixth Party Congress in 1980 he was elected a member of the Politburo. In 1984, he became Prime Minister.</p>
<p>1991 Chairman of the People's president and secretary hambuk faction, the People's Committee of the Party Central Committee, member of the Political Bureau of the Korean Workers' Party, the Supreme People's Assembly to the delegates in charge of such positions. He was awarded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orders_and_Medals_of_the_Democratic_People%27s_Republic_of_Korea" title="Orders and Medals of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea">Kim Il-sung Order</a> in 1982.</p>
