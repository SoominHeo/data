<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kang_Je-gyu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After graduating from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ChungAng_University" title="ChungAng University">ChungAng University</a>, Kang received his first prize at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_Youth_Film_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Korea Youth Film Festival (page does not exist)">Korea Youth Film Festival</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_Scenario_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Korea Scenario Awards (page does not exist)">Korea Scenario Awards</a> in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kang's most notable contributions to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_cinema" title="Korean cinema">Korean cinema</a> have been <i><a href="/wiki/Shiri_(film)" title="Shiri (film)">Shiri</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Taegukgi_(film)" title="Taegukgi (film)">Taegukgi</a></i>. <i>Shiri</i> was the first big budget <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>-style action film made in Korea, which broke box office records and was partially responsible for the popularization of domestic films in the country. <i>Taegukgi</i>, directed five years later, again rewrote box office records, having been seen by over ten million people in South Korea alone.</p>
<p>After establishing his own production film company under his name, he merged it with Myung Film in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an interview for the BBC special Asian Invasion, Kang revealed that he wanted his next project to be a science fiction film. He said, "I have produced two movies about Korea. So now I'm preparing a new movie that is related to something more global--a problem that the whole world is facing right now."</p>
<p>After a 7-year hiatus, in 2011 Kang unveiled his film <i><a href="/wiki/My_Way_(2011_film)" title="My Way (2011 film)">My Way</a></i>, set during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> with a star-studded pan-Asian cast and the highest budget to date for a Korean film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
