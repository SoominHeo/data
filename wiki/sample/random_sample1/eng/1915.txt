<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Korean_name" title="Korean name">Korean name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Korean_family_names" title="List of Korean family names">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Kang_(Korean_surname)" title="Kang (Korean surname)"><i>Kang</i></a>.</div>
<p><b>Kang Phyo-yong</b> (강표영) is a <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> military officer and politician. He holds the <a href="/wiki/Comparative_military_ranks_of_Korea" title="Comparative military ranks of Korea">rank</a> of Lieutenant General in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Army_Ground_Force" title="Korean People's Army Ground Force">Korean People's Army Ground Force</a>. As of 2012, he is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea">Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kang_Phyo-yong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 1995, Kang was promoted from Senior Colonel to Major General in the Korean People's Army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was subsequently promoted to Lieutenant General, and in September 2010 he was elected to the politburo of the <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Workers' Party of Korea">Workers' Party of Korea</a> at the Congress of September 2010.</p>
<p>Kang served as a member of the national mourning committee upon the deaths of <a href="/wiki/Jo_Myong-rok" title="Jo Myong-rok">Jo Myong-rok</a> in 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-il" title="Kim Jong-il">Kim Jong-il</a> in December 2011.</p>
