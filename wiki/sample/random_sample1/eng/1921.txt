<p><b>GarageBand</b> is a line of <a href="/wiki/Digital_audio_workstation" title="Digital audio workstation">digital audio workstations</a> for <a href="/wiki/MacOS" title="MacOS">macOS</a> and <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> that allows users to create <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a> or <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcasts</a>. GarageBand is developed and sold by <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc.</a> for macOS, and is part of the <a href="/wiki/ILife" title="ILife">iLife</a> software suite. Its music and podcast creation system enables users to create multiple tracks with pre-made <a href="/wiki/MIDI_keyboard" title="MIDI keyboard">MIDI keyboards</a>, pre-made <a href="/wiki/Loop_(music)" title="Loop (music)">loops</a>, an array of various instrumental effects, and voice recordings.</p>
<p>GarageBand supports many sound formats, though notably not <a href="/wiki/MP3" title="MP3">MP3</a> and those that are at <a href="/wiki/Audio_bit_depth" title="Audio bit depth">8-bits</a> or lower.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GarageBand was developed by Apple under the direction of Dr. Gerhard Lengeling. Dr. Gerhard Lengeling was formerly from the German company <a href="/wiki/Emagic" title="Emagic">Emagic</a>, makers of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logic_Audio" title="Logic Audio">Logic Audio</a>. (Apple acquired Emagic in July 2002.)</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Steve_Jobs" title="Steve Jobs">Steve Jobs</a> announced the application in his keynote speech at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macworld_Conference_%26_Expo" title="Macworld Conference &amp; Expo">Macworld Conference &amp; Expo</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> on January 6, 2004. The musician <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Mayer_(musician)" title="John Mayer (musician)">John Mayer</a> assisted with its demonstration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Apple announced GarageBand 2 at the 2005 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macworld_Conference_%26_Expo" title="Macworld Conference &amp; Expo">Macworld Conference &amp; Expo</a> on January 11, 2005. It shipped, as announced, around January 22, 2005. Notable new features included the abilities to view and edit music in <a href="/wiki/Musical_notation" title="Musical notation">musical notation</a>. It was also possible to record up to 8 tracks at once and to fix timing and <a href="/wiki/Pitch_(music)" title="Pitch (music)">pitch</a> of recordings. Apple added automation of track pan position, master volume, and the master pitch. Transposition of both audio and MIDI has been added by Apple along with the ability to import MIDI files.</p>
<p>GarageBand 3, announced at 2006’s Macworld Conference &amp; Expo, includes a '<a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcast</a> studio', including the ability to use more than 200 effects and jingles, and integration with <a href="/wiki/IChat" title="IChat">iChat</a> for remote interviews.</p>
<p>GarageBand 4, also known as GarageBand '08, is part of <a href="/wiki/ILife" title="ILife">iLife</a> '08. It incorporates the ability to record sections of a song separately, such as <a href="/wiki/Bridge_(music)" title="Bridge (music)">bridges</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">chorus lines</a>. Additionally, it provides support for the automation of <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">tempos</a> and instruments, the creation, and exportation of iPhone ringtones, and a "Magic GarageBand" feature which includes a virtual <a href="/wiki/Jam_session" title="Jam session">jam session</a> with a complete 3D view of the Electric instruments.</p>
<p>GarageBand 5 is part of the <a href="/wiki/ILife" title="ILife">iLife</a> '09 package. It includes music instruction and allows the user to buy instructional videos by contemporary artists. It also contains new features for electric guitar players, including a dedicated 3D Electric Guitar Track containing a virtual stompbox pedalboard, and <a href="/wiki/Amplifier_modeling" title="Amplifier modeling">virtual amplifiers</a> with spring reverb and tremolo. GarageBand 5 also includes a cleaner, redesigned user interface as well as Project Templates.</p>
<p>GarageBand 6, also known as GarageBand '11, is part of the <a href="/wiki/ILife" title="ILife">iLife</a> '11 package, which Apple released on October 20, 2010. This version brings new features such as Flex Time, a tool to adjust the rhythm of a recording. It also includes the ability to match the tempo of one track with another instantly, additional guitar amps and stompboxes, 22 new lessons for guitar and piano, and "How Did I Play?", a tool to measure the accuracy and progress of a piano or guitar performance in a lesson.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOT#SOAPBOX" title="Wikipedia:NOT"><span title="This text contains promotional language. (March 2014)">promotional language</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Apple released GarageBand 10 along with OS X 10.9 Mavericks in October 2013. This version has lost Magic GarageBand and the podcast functionality.</p>
<p>Apple updated GarageBand 10 for Mac on March 20, 2014. Version 10.0.2 adds the ability to export tracks in MP3 format as well as a new drummer module, but removed support for podcasting; users with podcast files created in GarageBand 6 can continue to edit them using the older version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garageband updated to version 10.0.3 on 16 October 2014. This version included myriad bug fixes and several new features including a dedicated Bass Amp Designer, the introduction of global track effects and dynamic track resizing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Audio_recording">Audio recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Audio recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GarageBand is a <a href="/wiki/Digital_audio_workstation" title="Digital audio workstation">digital audio workstation</a> (DAW) and <a href="/wiki/Music_sequencer" title="Music sequencer">music sequencer</a> that can record and play back multiple tracks of audio. Built-in audio filters that use the <a href="/wiki/Audio_Units" title="Audio Units">AU</a> (audio unit) standard allow the user to enhance the audio track with various effects, including reverb, echo, and distortion amongst others. GarageBand also offers the ability to record at both <a href="/wiki/Audio_bit_depth" title="Audio bit depth">16-bit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Audio_bit_depth" title="Audio bit depth">24-bit</a> Audio Resolution,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> but at a fixed sample rate of 44.1 kHz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> An included tuning system helps with pitch correction and can effectively imitate the <a href="/wiki/Auto-Tune" title="Auto-Tune">Auto-Tune</a> effect when tuned to the maximum level. It also has a large array of preset effects to choose from, with an option to create your own effects.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Virtual_software_instruments">Virtual software instruments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Virtual software instruments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GarageBand includes a large selection of realistic, sampled instruments and software modeled <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizers</a>. These can be used to create original compositions or play music live through the use of a USB <a href="/wiki/MIDI_keyboard" title="MIDI keyboard">MIDI keyboard</a> connected to the computer. An on-screen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virtuality" title="Virtuality">virtual</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_keyboard" title="Musical keyboard">keyboard</a> is also available as well as using a standard <a href="/wiki/QWERTY" title="QWERTY">QWERTY</a> keyboard with the "musical typing" feature. The synthesizers were broken into two groups: [virtual] analog and digital. Each synthesizer has a wide variety of adjustable parameters, including richness, glide, cut off, standard attack, decay, sustain, and release; these allow for a wide array of sound creation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guitar_features">Guitar features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Guitar features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In addition to the standard tracks, Garageband allows for guitar-specific tracks that can use a variety of simulated amplifiers, stompboxes, and effects processors. These imitate popular hardware from companies including <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Amplification" title="Marshall Amplification">Marshall Amplification</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_Music_Electronic_Company" title="Orange Music Electronic Company">Orange Music Electronic Company</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fender_Musical_Instruments_Corporation" title="Fender Musical Instruments Corporation">Fender Musical Instruments Corporation</a>. Up to five simulated effects can be layered on top of the virtual amplifiers, which feature adjustable parameters including tone, reverb, and volume. Guitars can be connected to Macs using the built-in input (requires hardware that can produce a standard stereo signal using a 3.5mm output) or a USB interface.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MIDI_editing">MIDI editing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: MIDI editing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GarageBand can import <a href="/wiki/MIDI" title="MIDI">MIDI</a> files and offers piano roll or notation-style editing and playback. By complying with the MIDI Standard, a user can edit many different aspects of a recorded note, including pitch, velocity, and duration. Pitch was settable to 1/128 of a semitone, on a scale of 0–127 (sometimes described on a scale of 1–128 for clarity). Velocity, which determines amplitude (volume), can be set and adjusted on a scale of 0–127. Note duration can be adjusted manually via the piano roll or in the score view. Note rhythms can be played via the software instruments, or created in the piano roll environment; rhythm correction is also included to lock notes to any time signature subdivision. GarageBand also offers global editing capabilities to MIDI information with Enhanced Timing, also known as <a href="/wiki/Quantization_(music)" title="Quantization (music)">Quantizing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> While offering comprehensive control over MIDI files, GarageBand does not include several features of professional-level DAWs, such as a sequencer for drum tracks separate from the normal piano roll. However, many of these shortcomings have been addressed with each successive release of GarageBand. Also of note, MIDI sequences edited or created in GarageBand cannot be exported to other DAWs or programs without first being converted to audio.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_lessons">Music lessons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Music lessons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

There are two types of lesson available in the Lesson Store: <i>Basic Lessons</i>, which are a free download, and <i>Artist Lessons</i>, which a user must purchase. The first Basic Lessons for both guitar and piano are included with GarageBand. In GarageBand 10, many sounds (aka patches, which Apple refers to as 'audio units') which are listed within the sound library are dimmed and unusable until the user pays an additional fee which allows the utilization of those sounds, bundled with the guitar and piano lessons. Attempting to click on and select the dimmed audio units to apply to the track causes promotional prompts to appear, requiring the user to log on with their Apple ID and furnish credit card information before knowing the price of the bundle.</p>
<p>In both types of the lesson, a music teacher presents the lesson, which is in a special format offering high-quality video and audio instructions. The lessons include a virtual guitar or piano, which demonstrates finger position and a musical notation area to show the correct musical notations. The music examples used in these lessons features popular music.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_audio_loops">Additional audio loops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Additional audio loops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Garageband includes an extensive array of pre-made audio loops to choose from with an option to import custom sound loops and an additional loop pack that is purchasable via the App Store. All loops have an edit and effects option.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Jam_Packs">Jam Packs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Jam Packs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Jam Packs are Apple’s official add-ons for GarageBand. Each Jam Pack contains loops and software instruments grouped into certain genres and styles.</p>
<p>The current Jam Packs are as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>GarageBand Jam Pack: Remix Tools</li>
<li>GarageBand Jam Pack: Rhythm Section</li>
<li>GarageBand Jam Pack: Symphony Orchestra</li>
<li>GarageBand Jam Pack: World Music</li>

Beginning with the release of the Remix Tools and Rhythm Section Jam Packs, each Jam Pack has been designated with a number. The release of GarageBand Jam Pack: World Music also saw a redesign in packaging.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="MainStage_2">MainStage 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: MainStage 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>MainStage 2 by Apple also includes 40 built-in instruments – including synths, vintage keyboards, and a drum machine – to use in GarageBand. It also features an interface for live performances and includes a large collection of plug-ins and sounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Third-party_instrument_and_Apple_Loop_packages">Third-party instrument and Apple Loop packages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Third-party instrument and Apple Loop packages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In addition to Apple, many other companies today offer commercial or shareware virtual software instruments designed especially for GarageBand, and collections of Apple Loops intended for GarageBand users.</p>
<p>GarageBand can also use any third-party <a href="/wiki/Software_synthesizer" title="Software synthesizer">software synthesizer</a> that adheres to the Core Audio (<a href="/wiki/Audio_Units" title="Audio Units">Audio Units</a>) standard. However, there are limitations, including that Audio Unit instruments which can respond to multiple MIDI channels or ports can be triggered only on the first channel of the first port. This means that multi-timbral instruments which contain multiple channels and respond to many MIDI channels, such as <a href="/wiki/Native_Instruments" title="Native Instruments">Native Instruments</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kontakt_(software)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kontakt (software) (page does not exist)">Kontakt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_of_the_Unicorn" title="Mark of the Unicorn">MOTU</a> MachFive, are not ideally suited for use in GarageBand.</p>
<p>Third-party vendors also offer extra loops for use in GarageBand. Users can also record custom loops through a <a href="/wiki/Microphone" title="Microphone">microphone</a>, via a software instrument, or by using an audio interface to connect physically a <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a> or other hardware instruments to a Mac or iOS device.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sample_multitrack_source_files">Sample multitrack source files</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sample multitrack source files">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Trent_Reznor" title="Trent Reznor">Trent Reznor</a> from the band <a href="/wiki/Nine_Inch_Nails" title="Nine Inch Nails">Nine Inch Nails</a> released the source multitrack GarageBand files for the song "The Hand That Feeds" to allow the public to experiment with his music, and permitted prospective GarageBand users to remix the song. He also gave permission for anyone to share their personalized remix with the world. Since then, Nine Inch Nails has released several more GarageBand source files, and several other artists have also released their GarageBand files that the public could use to experiment. (For more information, and to download the source files for that song and others, visit <a class="external autonumber" href="http://remix.nin.com/" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>).</p>
<p>New Zealand band <a href="/wiki/Evermore_(band)" title="Evermore (band)">Evermore</a> also released the source multi-track files for GarageBand for their song "<a href="/wiki/Never_Let_You_Go_(Evermore_song)" title="Never Let You Go (Evermore song)">Never Let You Go</a>".</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ben_Folds" title="Ben Folds">Ben Folds</a> released <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stems_%26_Seeds" title="Stems &amp; Seeds">Stems &amp; Seeds</a></i>, special version of his 2008 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Way_to_Normal" title="Way to Normal">Way to Normal</a></i>. <i>Stems and Seeds</i> contained a remastered version of <i>Way to Normal</i>, and a separate disc containing GarageBand files for each track from the album to allow fans to remix the songs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Limitations">Limitations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Limitations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A lack of MIDI-out capability limits the use of external MIDI instruments. There is also only limited support for messages sent from knobs on MIDI keyboards, as only real-time pitch bend, modulation, sustain, and foot control is recognizable. However, since GarageBand '08, other parameters affected by <a href="/wiki/MIDI" title="MIDI">MIDI</a> knobs can be automated later, per-track. GarageBand has no functions for changing time signature mid-song though the software does now allow a tempo track to automate tempo changes. The ability to reverse tracks is also not available.</p>
<p>Other than pitch bend, GarageBand is limited to the pitches and intervals of standard 12-tone <a href="/wiki/Equal_temperament" title="Equal temperament">equal temperament</a>, so it does not natively support <a href="/wiki/Xenharmonic_music" title="Xenharmonic music">xenharmonic music</a>. <a href="/wiki/Logic_Pro" title="Logic Pro">Logic Pro</a> supports many different tunings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> GarageBand does not support different tunings however, audio units which support micro tuning (using .scl or .tun files, or some other method) can be employed in GarageBand to produce alternative pitches.</p>
<p>Before GarageBand 10, there was no MIDI export option, and the only option was to save files as .band or export to iTunes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="GarageBand_for_iOS">GarageBand for iOS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: GarageBand for iOS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On March 2, 2011, Apple announced a version of GarageBand for the <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">iPad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It has many features similar to the <a href="/wiki/MacOS" title="MacOS">Mac OS X</a> version. Music can be created using the on-screen instruments, which include keyboards, <a href="/wiki/Drum_kit" title="Drum kit">drums</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Sampler_(musical_instrument)" title="Sampler (musical instrument)">sampler</a>, and various "smart instruments". It also acts as a multitrack recording studio with <a href="/wiki/Stomp_box" title="Stomp box">Stompbox</a> effects and guitar amps. Songs can be emailed or sent to an iTunes Library. Additionally, projects can be imported to GarageBand for macOS, where they are further editable. This feature also allows instruments from the iOS platform to be savable to software instrument library on the Mac. The app is compatible with <a href="/wiki/IPhone_3GS" title="IPhone 3GS">iPhone 3GS</a> or higher, the third generation <a href="/wiki/IPod_Touch" title="IPod Touch">iPod Touch</a> or higher, and all versions of the iPad, including the <a href="/wiki/IPad_Mini" title="IPad Mini">iPad Mini</a>. The app, with all instruments included, is available for free from the Apple App Store.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Instruments">Instruments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Instruments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>GarageBand includes a standard keyboard instrument and "Smart Keyboard" instrument. The keyboard is set up like a standard keyboard, and features several keyboard instruments (some of which are customizable), including grand <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_piano" title="Electric piano">electric piano</a>, various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organ_(instrument)" title="Organ (instrument)">organs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clavinet" title="Clavinet">clavinet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synth leads</a>, synth pads, and bass synths. It also has many different sound effects. The keyboard is capable of playing arpeggio and includes a pitch bend. The smart keyboard is arranged like the other smart instruments, allowing the user to play chords on the piano, electric piano, organ, clavinet, and four adjustable synthesizers. Also included is an autoplay feature, which plays one of 4 rhythms for each instrument.</li>
<li>There are two different kinds of drum instruments in GarageBand. The touch drums instrument includes three acoustic drum kits (Studio Kit, Live Kit, and Vintage Kit) with a realistic set up, and three electronic drum kits (<a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip Hop drums</a>, <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">House drums</a>, and a drum kit with <a href="/wiki/Roland_TR-808" title="Roland TR-808">Roland TR-808</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roland_TR-909" title="Roland TR-909">909</a> samples). These were set up like drum machines with customizable sounds that can be saved as separate drum kits. The other drum instrument is the "Smart Drums" instrument, in which the drum sounds are arrangable on a grid by complexity and volume. It contains all six drum kits in the touch drums.</li>
<li>A "Smart <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">Guitar</a>" is included in GarageBand, which includes 4 guitars: an <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_guitar" title="Acoustic guitar">acoustic guitar</a>, two <a href="/wiki/Electric_guitar" title="Electric guitar">electric guitars</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Distortion_(guitar)" title="Distortion (guitar)">distortion guitar</a>. Each guitar (except for the acoustic one) has two optional sound boxes. The instrument is set up like the smart keyboard, where multiple chords are playable. Also, each note in a chord can be played separately, or muted by holding the left side of the string.</li>
<li>The "Smart <a href="/wiki/Bass_(instrument)" title="Bass (instrument)">Bass</a>" instrument is set up like the guitar, where four strings can play various notes. However, the bass cannot play chords. Included are three <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">electric basses</a>, an acoustic orchestral <a href="/wiki/Double_bass" title="Double bass">bass</a>, and four customizable synth basses. Like the smart keyboard and smart guitars, there is an "autoplay" feature.</li>
<li>Added in update 1.2 are "Smart Strings", a string section made of <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">1st and 2nd violins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viola" title="Viola">violas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cellos</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Double_bass" title="Double bass">bass</a>, capable of playing notes <a href="/wiki/Legato" title="Legato">legato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staccato" title="Staccato">staccato</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pizzicato" title="Pizzicato">pizzicato</a>. The orchestra is customizable, including four different string styles (all with a different "autoplay" feature). The instruments are also selectable. For example, one can play a chord made up of all the available instruments, or simply play a violin note.</li>
<li>In the sampler instrument, the user can record a sound and play it on the keyboard (it has the same interface as the keyboard instrument). After the sound has been recorded, it can be modified with a various amount of tools within the sampler. It can also play notes in a loop or reversed. In Version 2.0, the user can import audio files to create samples.</li>
<li>The audio recorder is a standard recorder that works through the internal microphone or a headphone mic. After the sound has been recorded, many audio effects that can be applied.</li>
<li>The guitar amp is meant to be played by plugging a guitar into the device and recording, but can also work with sounds from the audio recorder, included Apple Loops, and imported music files. Within it are several customizable amplifiers and stompboxes, allowing for a broad range of different sounds.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Updates">Updates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Updates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 1, 2011, Apple introduced GarageBand for iOS 1.1, adding support for the iPhone and iPod touch, among other features. These included the ability to create custom chords for Smart Instruments, support for 3/4 and 6/8 time signatures, and exporting in <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Audio_Coding" title="Advanced Audio Coding">AAC</a> or <a href="/wiki/Audio_Interchange_File_Format" title="Audio Interchange File Format">AIFF</a> format.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 7, 2012, Apple updated GarageBand to 1.2, adding support for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPad_(third_generation)" title="IPad (third generation)">third-generation iPad</a>. It introduced the new Smart Strings instrument, a string orchestra of <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">1st and 2nd violins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viola" title="Viola">violas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">celli</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Double_bass" title="Double bass">bass</a>, capable of playing notes <a href="/wiki/Legato" title="Legato">legato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staccato" title="Staccato">staccato</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pizzicato" title="Pizzicato">pizzicato</a>. Additionally, it added <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizers</a> to the Smart Keyboard and Smart Bass instruments. It also added a note editor which allows users to fine-tune note placement and length and the ability to upload songs to <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> and <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>, as well as the ability to upload projects to iCloud. It also included Jam Session, a feature that enables up to 4 iPhones, iPod touches, and/or iPads with GarageBand installed to play simultaneously.</p>
<p>On May 1, 2012, GarageBand was updated to 1.2.1, providing minor bug fixes and stability improvements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alongside the new <a href="/wiki/IOS_6" title="IOS 6">iOS 6</a>, Apple updated GarageBand to 1.3 on September 19, 2012. The update added the ability to import music from one's music library, <a href="/wiki/Ringtone" title="Ringtone">ringtone</a> creation, the ability to use the app in the background, and minor bug fixes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>GarageBand was updated to 1.4 on March 20, 2013. The update added support for Audiobus, the ability to remove grid snapping, and minor bug fixes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-macrumors1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-macrumors1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>GarageBand received an overhaul of design coinciding with the reveal of the <a href="/wiki/IPad_Air" title="IPad Air">iPad Air</a> on October 22, 2013. GarageBand 2.0 features a new design to match <a href="/wiki/IOS_7" title="IOS 7">iOS 7</a>, an extended number of tracks per song, and new functions in the Sampler instrument.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Garageband received a new design.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Availability">Availability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Availability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the launch of Apple's <a href="/wiki/Mac_App_Store" title="Mac App Store">Mac App Store</a>, GarageBand was only available as a part of <a href="/wiki/ILife" title="ILife">iLife</a>, a suite of applications (also including <a href="/wiki/IPhoto" title="IPhoto">iPhoto</a>, <a href="/wiki/IMovie" title="IMovie">iMovie</a>, <a href="/wiki/IDVD" title="IDVD">iDVD</a>, and <a href="/wiki/IWeb" title="IWeb">iWeb</a>) intended to simplify the creation and organization of digital content, or available on a new Mac. On January 6, 2011, GarageBand was made available independently on the Mac App Store in addition to iPhoto and iMovie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supported_music_file_formats">Supported music file formats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GarageBand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Supported music file formats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This app can support any music format, including AIFF, WAV, and MIDI. The app can export songs to MP3, MP4 or AIFF format.</p>
<p>In the new update, GarageBand 10.0 cannot support 8-bit sounds, but can support 16-bit sounds and greater.</p>
