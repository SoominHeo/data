<p><i><b>Gigli</b></i> is a 2003 American <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy_film" title="Romantic comedy film">romantic comedy film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brest" title="Martin Brest">Martin Brest</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bartha" title="Justin Bartha">Justin Bartha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Pacino" title="Al Pacino">Al Pacino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Walken" title="Christopher Walken">Christopher Walken</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lainie_Kazan" title="Lainie Kazan">Lainie Kazan</a>.</p>
<p>Popular media gave attention and interest to the film during production, primarily because Affleck and Lopez, the film's stars, were romantically involved at the time.</p>
<p>After release, critical reaction was universally negative, and in the years since its release <i>Gigli</i> has been considered one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_films_considered_the_worst" title="List of films considered the worst">worst films of all time</a>. The film was also one of the most expensive <a href="/wiki/Box_office_bomb" title="Box office bomb">box office bombs</a> in history, grossing $7.2 million against a $75.6 million budget. As of 2016, it is the last film Brest has directed, making it his longest hiatus between projects.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Larry Gigli (<a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a>) is a low-ranking <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> mobster who isn't nearly as tough as he likes to act. Louis (<a href="/wiki/Lenny_Venito" title="Lenny Venito">Lenny Venito</a>), a higher-ranking member of Gigli's organization, commands Gigli to <a href="/wiki/Kidnapping" title="Kidnapping">kidnap</a> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mental_retardation" title="Mental retardation">mentally challenged</a> younger brother of a powerful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_prosecutor" title="Federal prosecutor">federal prosecutor</a> to use as a bargaining chip to save New York-based mob boss Starkman (<a href="/wiki/Al_Pacino" title="Al Pacino">Al Pacino</a>) from prison. Gigli successfully convinces the young man, Brian (<a href="/wiki/Justin_Bartha" title="Justin Bartha">Justin Bartha</a>), to go off with him by promising to take him "to the Baywatch", apparently a reference to the <a href="/wiki/Baywatch" title="Baywatch">television show of that name</a>, which seems to be Brian's singular obsession. Louis does not trust Gigli to get the job done right, so he hires a woman calling herself Ricki (<a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>) to take charge.</p>
<p>Gigli is attracted to Ricki, but he resents both Louis' lack of faith in him and having to take orders from a woman. He is also frustrated by Brian's insistence on going to "the Baywatch" and by Ricki's being <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a>. A suspicious detective (<a href="/wiki/Christopher_Walken" title="Christopher Walken">Christopher Walken</a>) comes to the apartment to question Gigli in reference to Brian's disappearance. Gigli is further annoyed when his mother (<a href="/wiki/Lainie_Kazan" title="Lainie Kazan">Lainie Kazan</a>) takes an immediate liking to Ricki and when the two women team up to needle him.</p>
<p>The events take a darker turn when Gigli and Ricki receive orders to cut off Brian's thumb, something that neither wants to do. Worse, Ricki's ex-girlfriend, Robin (Missy Crider), shows up at Gigli's apartment, accusing Ricki of changing sexual orientation. Robin <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-mutilation" title="Self-mutilation">slits her wrists</a> and has to be rushed to the hospital. While at the hospital, Gigli goes to the morgue and cuts off a corpse's thumb, which he sends to the prosecutor as Brian's thumb. Gigli and Ricki go back to Gigli's apartment, where Gigli confesses his love and the two sleep together.</p>
<p>They are summoned to meet with the mob's boss. Starkman reveals that he did not approve of the plan to kidnap a federal prosecutor's brother or the order to cut off Brian's thumb. He nevertheless rages at them because the thumb they sent didn't match Brian's <a href="/wiki/Fingerprint" title="Fingerprint">fingerprint</a> and therefore not only failed to increase pressure on the prosecutor but even undermined the organization's credibility. Starkman then kills Louis, presumably in retaliation for the kidnapping and associated scrutiny by law enforcement. Starkman is about to kill Ricki and Gigli as well, but Ricki talks him out of it by pointing out that only they know where Brian is and only they can silence Brian and prevent him from revealing the involvement of Starkman's organization in the kidnapping or even accusing Starkman of having been personally involved. They leave Starkman's, decide to leave the mob, and discuss taking Brian back to where they found him. On the way, they discover <i>Baywatch</i> (or a similarly themed show or film) shooting an episode on the beach. Brian begs to be let off there and finally they consent.</p>
<p>Gigli convinces Ricki to take his car, but at the last minute, Ricki returns and picks up Gigli, and they leave town together.</p>
<p>All of the characters are transformed by the episode. Louis's mob is disrupted, Gigli has dropped his hyper-masculine facade, Ricki has a heterosexual fling with Gigli, and Brian gradually emerges from his shell, noticing other people and conversing.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> the film has a rating of 6%, based on 183 reviews, with an average rating of 2.7/10. The critical consensus states: "Bizarre and clumsily plotted, <i>Gigli</i> is a mess. As for its stars, Affleck and Lopez lack chemistry."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> the film has a score of 18 out of 100, based on 37 critics, indicating "overwhelming dislike".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebert_and_Roeper" title="Ebert and Roeper">Ebert and Roeper</a></i>, critics <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> both gave the film thumbs down, although Ebert showed some sympathy towards the film, stating it had "clever dialogue", but was "...too disorganized for me to recommend it". Roeper called the film "a disaster" and "one of the worst movies I've ever seen". He then included <i>Gigli</i> on his 100 worst films of the decade at #7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ebert and <a href="/wiki/James_Berardinelli" title="James Berardinelli">James Berardinelli</a> were two of the very few major critics to not write it off completely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Ebert gave the film two and a half stars out of four, saying, "They didn't quite get to where they wanted to be, but the film is worth seeing for some very good scenes." Berardinelli gave it two stars, saying, "This isn't a good film, but, when set alongside the likes of <i><a href="/wiki/Dumb_and_Dumberer:_When_Harry_Met_Lloyd" title="Dumb and Dumberer: When Harry Met Lloyd">Dumb and Dumberer</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde_2:_Red,_White_%26_Blonde" title="Legally Blonde 2: Red, White &amp; Blonde">Legally Blonde 2</a></i>, Jen &amp; Ben offer less pain."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Owen_Gleiberman" title="Owen Gleiberman">Owen Gleiberman</a> gave the film a "C+", stating "A watchable bad movie, but it's far from your typical cookie-cutter blockbuster. There are no shoot-outs or car chases, and there isn't much romantic suspense, either."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of the few positive reviews came from Amy Dawes of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i>, who wrote that the story was ludicrous and that the film would tank, but that on balance she found it a fun film with several good performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Gigli</i> grossed $3,753,518 in its opening weekend from 2,215 theaters averaging $1,694 per theater and ranking #8 at the box office. The film set a record to date for the biggest <a href="/wiki/Second_weekend_in_box_office_performance" title="Second weekend in box office performance">second-weekend</a> drop in box office gross of any film in wide release since that statistic was kept; it dropped by 81.9% in its second weekend compared to its first, grossing $678,640.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> By its third weekend in release, only 73 US theaters were showing it, a 97% drop from its first weekend. The film ultimately earned $6,087,542 domestically and $1,178,667 internationally for a total of $7,266,209 on a $75.6 million production budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_wrap.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_wrap.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was withdrawn from US theaters after only three weeks (one of the shortest circulation times for a big-budget film), earning a total of only $6 million domestically and $1 million abroad. In the United Kingdom, the film was dropped by virtually every cinema after critics panned it.</p>
<p>In 2014, <i>The Los Angeles Times</i> listed the film as one of the most expensive box office flops of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was nominated for nine and received six <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Awards" title="Golden Raspberry Awards">Razzies</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003_Golden_Raspberry_Awards" title="2003 Golden Raspberry Awards">2003 Golden Raspberry Awards</a> – <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Picture" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Picture">Worst Picture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Actor" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Actor">Worst Actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Actress" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Actress">Worst Actress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Director" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Director">Worst Director</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Screenplay" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Screenplay">Worst Screenplay</a> and Worst Screen Couple. A year later, the film won a seventh Razzie for "Worst Comedy of Our First 25 Years". The film was also nominated for eleven and received five <a href="/wiki/Stinkers_Bad_Movie_Awards" title="Stinkers Bad Movie Awards">Stinkers Bad Movie Awards</a> in 2003; Worst Actor and Worst Fake Accent - Male, Worst Actress and Worst Fake Accent - Female and Worst On-Screen Couple.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its title was named by the <a href="/wiki/Global_Language_Monitor" title="Global Language Monitor">Global Language Monitor</a> as one of the top words from Hollywood having an impact on the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Late night talk show hosts in particular lampooned the film in their monologues; <a href="/wiki/Conan_O%27Brien" title="Conan O'Brien">Conan O'Brien</a> said "The <a href="/wiki/2003_New_York_Mets_season" title="2003 New York Mets season">Mets</a> are doing so badly that they will be renamed 'The New York Gigli.'"</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Movies" title="Yahoo! Movies">Yahoo! Movies</a> rates <i>Gigli</i> number one on their <i>Bottom Rated Movies of All Time</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> with a critics' rating of D−.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Onion" title="The Onion">The Onion</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Satire" title="Satire">satirical</a> newspaper, ran an article about the film, titled "<i>Gigli</i> focus groups demand new ending in which Affleck and Lopez die."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2015, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> named <i>Gigli</i> #25 on its list of "50 Worst Movie Titles of All Time".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
