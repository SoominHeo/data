<p>A <b>national unity government</b>, <b>government of national unity</b>, or <b>national union government</b> is a broad <a href="/wiki/Coalition_government" title="Coalition government">coalition government</a> consisting of all parties (or all major parties) in the legislature, usually formed during a time of war or other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_emergency" title="National emergency">national emergency</a>.</p>

<p>During World War I the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_of_Canada_(historical)" title="Conservative Party of Canada (historical)">Conservative</a> government of Sir <a href="/wiki/Robert_Borden" title="Robert Borden">Robert Borden</a> invited the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Canada" title="Liberal Party of Canada">Liberal</a> opposition to join the government as a means of dealing with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conscription_crisis_of_1917" title="Conscription crisis of 1917">Conscription crisis of 1917</a>. The Liberals, led by Sir <a href="/wiki/Wilfrid_Laurier" title="Wilfrid Laurier">Wilfrid Laurier</a> refused; however, Borden was able to convince many individual Liberals to join what was called a <i><a href="/wiki/Unionist_Party_(Canada)" title="Unionist Party (Canada)">Union Government</a></i>, which defeated the Laurier Liberals in the fall <a href="/wiki/Canadian_federal_election,_1917" title="Canadian federal election, 1917">1917 election</a>.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_opposition" title="Parliamentary opposition">opposition</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_of_Canada_(historical)" title="Conservative Party of Canada (historical)">Conservative Party</a> ran under the name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_of_Canada_(historical)#National_Government_label" title="Conservative Party of Canada (historical)">National Government</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_federal_election,_1940" title="Canadian federal election, 1940">1940 election</a> as a means of promoting their platform of creating a wartime national government coalition (evocative of the previous war's <i><a href="/wiki/Unionist_Party_(Canada)" title="Unionist Party (Canada)">Union government</a></i>). The party was not successful in the election, which re-elected the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Canada" title="Liberal Party of Canada">Liberal</a> government of <a href="/wiki/William_Lyon_Mackenzie_King" title="William Lyon Mackenzie King">William Lyon Mackenzie King</a>, whose party continued to rule alone for the duration of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newfoundland">Newfoundland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Newfoundland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Newfoundland" title="Dominion of Newfoundland">Dominion of Newfoundland</a> (not to be part of Canada for another three decades) had a National Government during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Patrick_Morris,_1st_Baron_Morris" title="Edward Patrick Morris, 1st Baron Morris">Edward Patrick Morris</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Croatia">Croatia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Croatia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> formed a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Franjo_Greguri%C4%87" title="Cabinet of Franjo Gregurić">national unity government in 1991</a> under prime minister <a href="/wiki/Franjo_Greguri%C4%87" title="Franjo Gregurić">Franjo Gregurić</a> in response to the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a>. Even though the cabinet included ministers from minority parties, all heads of <a href="/wiki/Ministry_(government_department)" title="Ministry (government department)">ministries</a> were either from the majority <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Democratic_Union" title="Croatian Democratic Union">Croatian Democratic Union</a> or soon defected to it.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hungary">Hungary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hungary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are four periods in Hungary when national unity governments emerged:</p>
<ul>
<li>1917–1918, during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> (<a href="/wiki/M%C3%B3ric_Esterh%C3%A1zy" title="Móric Esterházy">Móric Esterházy</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A1ndor_Wekerle" title="Sándor Wekerle">Sándor Wekerle</a> cabinets)</li>
<li>1919–1920, cabinet of <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A1roly_Husz%C3%A1r" title="Károly Huszár">Károly Huszár</a>, restoration of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary_(1920%E2%80%9346)" title="Kingdom of Hungary (1920–46)">Kingdom of Hungary</a></li>
<li>1944–1947, after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> (<a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9la_Mikl%C3%B3s" title="Béla Miklós">Béla Miklós</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zolt%C3%A1n_Tildy" title="Zoltán Tildy">Zoltán Tildy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferenc_Nagy" title="Ferenc Nagy">Ferenc Nagy</a> cabinets)</li>
<li>1956, during the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1956" title="Hungarian Revolution of 1956">Hungarian Revolution of 1956</a> (third cabinet of <a href="/wiki/Imre_Nagy" title="Imre Nagy">Imre Nagy</a>)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Israel">Israel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Israel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> has had several <i>national unity governments</i>, in which major rival parties formed a ruling coalition. Such a coalition was notably formed in the days leading up to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Six_Day_War" title="Six Day War">Six Day War</a>, and after the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_legislative_election,_1984" title="Israeli legislative election, 1984">1984 elections</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/De_Gasperi_II_Cabinet" title="De Gasperi II Cabinet">De Gasperi II Cabinet</a>, <a href="/wiki/De_Gasperi_III_Cabinet" title="De Gasperi III Cabinet">De Gasperi III Cabinet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Historic_Compromise" title="Historic Compromise">Historic Compromise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciampi_Cabinet" title="Ciampi Cabinet">Ciampi Cabinet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monti_Cabinet" title="Monti Cabinet">Monti Cabinet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Letta_Cabinet" title="Letta Cabinet">Letta Cabinet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grand_coalition_(Italy)" title="Grand coalition (Italy)">Grand coalition (Italy)</a></div>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Republic" title="Italian Republic">republican era</a>, the first two cabinets, led by <a href="/wiki/Alcide_De_Gasperi" title="Alcide De Gasperi">Alcide De Gasperi</a>, were supported by both the pro-American <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democracy_(Italy)" title="Christian Democracy (Italy)">Christian Democrats</a> and the pro-Soviet <a href="/wiki/Italian_Communist_Party" title="Italian Communist Party">Italian Communist Party</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italian_Socialist_Party" title="Italian Socialist Party">Italian Socialist Party</a>.</p>
<p>Afterwards, the first government generally recognised as a national unity government was the <a href="/wiki/Andreotti_III_Cabinet" title="Andreotti III Cabinet">third Andreotti Cabinet</a>, also known as <i>non-no confidence vote</i> government, as the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Communist_Party" title="Italian Communist Party">Italian Communist Party</a> decided to not take part at the confidence vote. The communists voted in favour of the motion of confidence for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andreotti_IV_Cabinet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andreotti IV Cabinet (page does not exist)">following cabinet</a>, still led by <a href="/wiki/Giulio_Andreotti" title="Giulio Andreotti">Giulio Andreotti</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/European_debt_crisis" title="European debt crisis">Eurozone crisis</a>, the two main parties, <a href="/wiki/The_People_of_Freedom" title="The People of Freedom">The People of Freedom</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Italy)" title="Democratic Party (Italy)">Democratic Party</a>, along with other minor political forces, supported the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monti_cabinet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monti cabinet (page does not exist)">Monti cabinet</a>, and eventually, after the <a href="/wiki/Italian_general_election,_2013" title="Italian general election, 2013">2013 general election</a>, formed a <a href="/wiki/Grand_coalition" title="Grand coalition">grand coalition</a> in support of the <a href="/wiki/Letta_Cabinet" title="Letta Cabinet">Letta Cabinet</a>, which, however, was opposed by a new major political force in parliament, the anti-establishment <a href="/wiki/Five_Star_Movement" title="Five Star Movement">Five Star Movement</a>.</p>
<p>Here is a list of national unity or <a href="/wiki/Grand_coalition" title="Grand coalition">grand coalition</a> governments:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/De_Gasperi_II_Cabinet" title="De Gasperi II Cabinet">De Gasperi II Cabinet</a> (14 July 1946 – 2 February 1947)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/De_Gasperi_III_Cabinet" title="De Gasperi III Cabinet">De Gasperi III Cabinet</a> (2 February 1947 – 1 June 1947)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Andreotti_III_Cabinet" title="Andreotti III Cabinet">Andreotti III Cabinet</a> (29 July 1976 – 11 March 1978)</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andreotti_IV_Cabinet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andreotti IV Cabinet (page does not exist)">Andreotti IV Cabinet</a> (11 March 1978 – 20 March 1979)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ciampi_Cabinet" title="Ciampi Cabinet">Ciampi Cabinet</a> (28 April 1993 – 10 May 1994) – <small>Note: grand coalition support lasted only until 4 May 1993</small></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Monti_Cabinet" title="Monti Cabinet">Monti Cabinet</a> (16 November 2011 – 28 April 2013)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Letta_Cabinet" title="Letta Cabinet">Letta Cabinet</a> (28 April 2013 – 22 February 2014) – <small>Note: grand coalition support lasted only until 15 November 2013</small></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kenya">Kenya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Kenya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 2008 to 2013, Kenya was governed by <a href="/wiki/Government_of_National_Unity_(Kenya)" title="Government of National Unity (Kenya)">Government of National Unity</a> between the rival <a href="/wiki/Party_of_National_Unity_(Kenya)" title="Party of National Unity (Kenya)">Party of National Unity</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mwai_Kibaki" title="Mwai Kibaki">Mwai Kibaki</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Orange_Democratic_Movement" title="Orange Democratic Movement">Orange Democratic Movement</a> of <a href="/wiki/Raila_Odinga" title="Raila Odinga">Raila Odinga</a> following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kenyan_presidential_election,_2007" title="Kenyan presidential election, 2007">2007 presidential election</a> and subsequent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%932008_Kenyan_crisis" title="2007–2008 Kenyan crisis">violence</a>. This was due to the ODM winning the majority of seats in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Kenya" title="National Assembly of Kenya">National Assembly</a>, but controversially losing the presidential election by a margin that has since been called into question for its validity.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lebanon">Lebanon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Lebanon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> is a multireligious state and <a href="/wiki/Consensus_democracy" title="Consensus democracy">consensus democracy</a>, having a national unity government is more favorable in this country. Unlike other democracies, no group in Lebanon can govern alone.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Luxembourg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> has had two <i><a href="/wiki/National_Union_Government_(Luxembourg)" title="National Union Government (Luxembourg)">National Union Governments</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/National_Union_Government_(1916)" title="National Union Government (1916)">The first</a> was formed in 1916, during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> (in which Luxembourg was <a href="/wiki/Neutral_country" title="Neutral country">neutral</a>, but occupied by <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a> nonetheless). It was led by <a href="/wiki/Victor_Thorn" title="Victor Thorn">Victor Thorn</a> and included all of the major factions in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Luxembourg" title="Chamber of Deputies of Luxembourg">Chamber of Deputies</a>, but lasted for only sixteen months.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/National_Union_Government_(1945)" title="National Union Government (1945)">The second National Union Government</a> was formed in November 1945, in the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, which had devastated Luxembourg. It was led by <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Dupong" title="Pierre Dupong">Pierre Dupong</a>, who had been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Luxembourg" title="Prime Minister of Luxembourg">Prime Minister</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Government_in_exile" title="Government in exile">government in exile</a> in the war, and included all four parties represented in the Chamber of Deputies. The government lasted until 1947, by which time, a normal coalition between two of the three largest parties had been arranged, thus maintaining the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Confidence_(political_science)" title="Confidence (political science)">confidence</a> of the legislature.</p>
<p>In addition, Luxembourg had a <i><a href="/wiki/Liberation_Government_(Luxembourg)" title="Liberation Government (Luxembourg)">Liberation Government</a></i> between November 1944 and November 1945, also under Dupong. It served a similar emergency role to a national government, but included only the two largest parties, the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Social_People%27s_Party" title="Christian Social People's Party">CSV</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Luxembourg Socialist Workers' Party">LSAP</a>.</p>

The major political parties and unified political fronts have agreed to settle the disputed issues of the constitution drafting process by 3 June and to form a national unity government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/National_Government_(United_Kingdom)" title="National Government (United Kingdom)">National Government (United Kingdom)</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/First-past-the-post_voting" title="First-past-the-post voting">First-past-the-post voting</a>, the British electoral system, in <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_Kingdom_Parliament_constituencies" title="List of United Kingdom Parliament constituencies">constituencies</a> that are far from homogenous, has long increased the likelihood of a single party gaining a majority of members of Parliament, who have run most departments and the government legislation of the country since the early 20th century.</p>
<p>After the formation of clear political parties in the Lords and Commons, the first national unity government in the country was the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_All_the_Talents" title="Ministry of All the Talents">Ministry of All the Talents</a> that led the United Kingdom for about a year after the death of <a href="/wiki/William_Pitt_the_Younger" title="William Pitt the Younger">William Pitt the Younger</a> in 1806, during the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>. This ministry had cross-party support, ranging from very <a href="/wiki/Social_conservatism" title="Social conservatism">socially conservative</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tory_(British_political_party)" title="Tory (British political party)">Tories</a>, and the broad range of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whig_(British_political_party)" title="Whig (British political party)">Whigs</a> (among them <a href="/wiki/Charles_James_Fox" title="Charles James Fox">Charles James Fox</a> and his 'Foxites'), selected for their combined broad political support in both Houses of Parliament and known capabilities in a time of crisis. However, the ministry was frustrated in its attempts to make peace with France, and despite one major legislative success (banning the slave trade in Britain), it fell apart in 1807 over the question of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_Emancipation" title="Catholic Emancipation">Catholic Emancipation</a> and was replaced following a general election by a Tory ministry led by the <a href="/wiki/William_Cavendish-Bentinck,_3rd_Duke_of_Portland" title="William Cavendish-Bentinck, 3rd Duke of Portland">Duke of Portland</a>.</p>
<p>It proved that major wars and the long recovery to <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> would be the only further instances of National Governments.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Asquith_Ministry" title="Second Asquith Ministry">Second Asquith Ministry</a> presented a brief national coalition (1915–1916) during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quasi-national_governments">Quasi-national governments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Quasi-national governments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thereafter <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_George_ministry" title="Lloyd George ministry">a coalition that faced few opposition MPs</a> under <a href="/wiki/David_Lloyd_George" title="David Lloyd George">David Lloyd George</a> lasted until 1922 when his <a href="/wiki/National_Liberal_Party_(UK,_1922)" title="National Liberal Party (UK, 1922)">National Liberal Party</a> formally split with the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal Party</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> the first of four consecutive <i>National Governments</i> was formed in 1931 by <a href="/wiki/Ramsay_MacDonald" title="Ramsay MacDonald">Ramsay MacDonald</a> (<a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a>/<a href="/wiki/National_Labour_Organisation" title="National Labour Organisation">National Labour</a>) succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Baldwin" title="Stanley Baldwin">Stanley Baldwin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a>) with their largest opponent and the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberals</a>. Most members of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a> Parties rejected the government, however, and moved to the opposition benches leaving MacDonald's supporters to rival mainstream party candidates in many cases as <a href="/wiki/National_Labour_Organisation" title="National Labour Organisation">National Labour/National Labour Organisation</a> or in Lloyd George's revived <a href="/wiki/National_Liberal_Party_(UK,_1931)" title="National Liberal Party (UK, 1931)">National Liberal Party</a>. Notably candidates styled in this way contested the 1935 election; this long period of quasi-national government took in broader support and widened its selections of ministers in the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">war years</a>, and its fourth transmutation persisted until the general election of 1945.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In hopes of bridging partisan politics during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, Republican <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> ran his second term as a National Union government with Democrat <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson" title="Andrew Johnson">Andrew Johnson</a> as his vice president. The new <a href="/wiki/National_Union_Party_(United_States)" title="National Union Party (United States)">National Union Party</a> allowed members to retain affiliations with other political parties.</p>
<p>Since the Civil War, there has never been a "national unity" government in the United States in the traditional sense. There have been several instances, however, during national disasters or wars, that the two parties have briefly "rallied around the President." Such instances include the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_John_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of John F. Kennedy">assassination of John F. Kennedy</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>, all of which preceded a massive spike in the approval rating of the sitting President.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Zimbabwe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Zimbabwean_political_negotiations" title="2008 Zimbabwean political negotiations">2008 Zimbabwean political negotiations</a></div>
<p>The 2008–2009 Zimbabwean political negotiations between the opposition Movement for Democratic Change (led by Morgan Tsvangirai), its small splinter group, the Movement for Democratic Change – Mutambara (led by Arthur Mutambara), and the ruling Zimbabwe African National Union – Patriotic Front (led by Robert Mugabe) created a framework for a power-sharing executive government between the two parties. These negotiations followed the 2008 presidential election, in which Mugabe was controversially re-elected, as well as the 2008 parliamentary election, in which the MDC won a majority in the House of Assembly. The new national unity government, including Tsvangirai, was sworn in on 11 February 2009.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_parties">National parties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_unity_government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: National parties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some countries such as <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> have or have had a <i>National Party</i>, which can lead to the use of the phrase "National government" when it is in power. Such governments are not national governments in the sense of this article.</p>
