<p><b>NGC 7635</b>, also called the <b>Bubble Nebula</b>, <b>Sharpless 162</b>, or <b>Caldwell 11</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/H_II_region" title="H II region">H II region</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESIMBAD2007_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESIMBAD2007-1">[1]</a></sup> emission nebula in the constellation <a href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_(constellation)" title="Cassiopeia (constellation)">Cassiopeia</a>. It lies close to the direction of the <a href="/wiki/Open_cluster" title="Open cluster">open cluster</a> <a href="/wiki/Messier_52" title="Messier 52">Messier 52</a>. The "bubble" is created by the <a href="/wiki/Stellar_wind" title="Stellar wind">stellar wind</a> from a massive hot, 8.7<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESIMBAD2007_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESIMBAD2007-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a> young central <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a>, SAO 20575 (<a href="/wiki/BD%2B60%C2%B02522" title="BD+60°2522">BD+60°2522</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAPOD2005_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAPOD2005-7">[7]</a></sup> The nebula is near a giant <a href="/wiki/Molecular_cloud" title="Molecular cloud">molecular cloud</a> which contains the expansion of the bubble nebula while itself being excited by the hot central star, causing it to glow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAPOD2005_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAPOD2005-7">[7]</a></sup> It was discovered in 1787 by <a href="/wiki/William_Herschel" title="William Herschel">William Herschel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ngc_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ngc-5">[5]</a></sup> The star BD+60°2522 is thought to have a mass of about 44 <i>M</i><sub>☉</sub>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_observation">Amateur observation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NGC_7635&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur observation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With an 8 or 10-inch (250 mm) telescope, the nebula is visible as an extremely faint and large shell around the star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKeppleSanner1998_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKeppleSanner1998-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESIMBAD2007_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESIMBAD2007-1">[1]</a></sup> The nearby 7th magnitude star on the west hinders observation, but one can view the nebula using <a href="/wiki/Averted_vision" title="Averted vision">averted vision</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKeppleSanner1998_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKeppleSanner1998-6">[6]</a></sup> Using a 16 to 18-inch (460 mm) scope, one can see that the faint nebula is irregular, being elongated in the north south direction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKeppleSanner1998_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKeppleSanner1998-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
