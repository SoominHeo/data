<p><i><b>Gundam Build Fighters</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ガンダムビルドファイターズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Gandamu Birudo Faitāzu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a 2013 Japanese science fiction <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series based on <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_(company)" title="Sunrise (company)">Sunrise's</a> long-running <i><a href="/wiki/Gundam" title="Gundam">Gundam</a></i> franchise. The series is directed by Kenji Nagasaki of <i><a href="/wiki/No._6" title="No. 6">No. 6</a></i> and written by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yousuke_Kuroda" title="Yousuke Kuroda">Yousuke Kuroda</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam_00" title="Mobile Suit Gundam 00">Mobile Suit Gundam 00</a></i>. Character designs were done by both Kenichi Ohnuki and Suzuhito Yasuda. The series was first unveiled under the name "1/144 Gundam Mobile" project by Sunrise,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> before its official announcement. In contrast to other <i>Gundam</i> series, <i>Gundam Build Fighters</i> focuses on the <a href="/wiki/Gundam_model" title="Gundam model">Gundam model</a> (Gunpla) aspect of the franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Asahi_Shimbun_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Asahi_Shimbun-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series was officially unveiled by <a href="/wiki/Bandai" title="Bandai">Bandai</a> on July 2, 2013, during the series's live press conference as part of Gundam's 35th Anniversary in 2014. It premiered on <a href="/wiki/TX_Network" title="TX Network">TXN</a> stations in Japan<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> in limited international markets on October 7, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouTube_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube-4">[4]</a></sup> One <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">Manga</a> adaptation and two photonovels were also announced by Sunrise, and currently running on <i><a href="/wiki/Gundam_Ace" title="Gundam Ace">Gundam Ace</a></i> (<i>Gundam Build Fighters Amazing</i>), <i><a href="/wiki/Hobby_Japan" title="Hobby Japan">Hobby Japan</a></i> (<i>Gundam Build Fighters Honno</i>), and <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Hobby_Magazine" title="Dengeki Hobby Magazine">Dengeki Hobby Magazine</a></i> (<i>Gundam Build Fighter Document</i>) magazines.</p>
<p><i>Gundam Build Fighters</i> is followed by the 2014 sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Gundam_Build_Fighters_Try" title="Gundam Build Fighters Try">Gundam Build Fighters Try</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Story">Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gundam_Build_Fighters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Gundam_Build_Fighters_characters" title="List of Gundam Build Fighters characters">List of Gundam Build Fighters characters</a></div>
<p>Back in the 1980s, the success of the series <i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam" title="Mobile Suit Gundam">Mobile Suit Gundam</a></i> resulted in an economic boom due to sales of the <a href="/wiki/Gundam_model" title="Gundam model">Gundam model</a> kits, or "Gunpla" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ガンプラ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ganpura</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, dubbed the Gunpla Boom. Years later, with the success of the second Gunpla Boom, special tournaments called Gunpla Battles are established throughout the world to see which customized Gunpla and its builder are the best. These incredibly popular Gunpla Battles culminate in an annual global tournament.</p>
<p>The story of the first series revolves around Sei Iori, a young Gunpla Builder and student who has a dream of becoming the best Gunpla Fighter in the tournament and someday become as good as his father. As the only child, his family owns a small Gunpla shop and his talent is well-honed, however his weak piloting abilities have led him to a series of first-round losses. But one day, he meets a strange boy named Reiji, who helps him out. Reiji gives him a jewel, promising that he will come to Sei's aid if wished enough. Together, both of them will tackle the world of Gunpla Battle and compete in the tournament using Sei's customized Gunpla, the GAT-X105B Build Strike Gundam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gunpla_Battle">Gunpla Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gundam_Build_Fighters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Gunpla Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The central theme of this series is the Gunpla Battle <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ガンプラバトル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ganpura Batoru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a virtual game that involves the use of Gunpla. Each Gunpla Fighter has a GP Base <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">GPベース</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Jī Pī Bēsu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a device that stores their Gunpla information. Once the GP Bases are set, the Battle System <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">バトルシステム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Batoru Shisutemu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> disperses "Plavsky particles" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">プラフスキー粒子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Purafusukī Ryūshi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of "plastic" and "<a href="/wiki/Universal_Century_technology#The_Minovsky_particle" title="Universal Century technology">Minovsky particles</a>")</span>, which react only to the plastic material of Gunpla and animate them into the game. The Battle System creates a holographic landscape that simulates a battlefield from different <i>Gundam</i> series, and the battle begins once the Gunpla Fighters set their Gunpla to be scanned by the Plavsky particles. A Gunpla's combat statistics are determined by its build quality; modifications such as adding scratch-built parts and paint weathering will increase its stats. Battle effects such as explosions and beam attacks are faithfully simulated in the Battle System. Consequently, damage sustained during battle is reflected directly on the Gunpla itself, requiring Gunpla Fighters to have spare parts ready for necessary repairs. Scale also plays a major factor in a Gunpla Battle; just like in the real world, a 1/48 scale Mega Size Model Gunpla will dwarf a 1/144 scale opponent in the battlefield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Gunpla Battle World Championships tournament begins with eight battles, including a four-person knockout battle, a three-on-three team battle, and the closing one-on-one battle. Five of the battles are revealed only on their scheduled dates. Each contestant receives four points for every battle won. The 16 Gunpla Fighters with the most points will advance to the final tournament, a knockout stage that decides the World Champion.</p>
<p>It is later on revealed that Plavsky particles are processed from Arista <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アリスタ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Arisuta</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a crystal originating from Reiji's home country of Arian <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アリアン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gundam_Build_Fighters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gundam_Build_Fighters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series was first announced in June 2013 under the name "1/144 Gundam Mobile" project by Sunrise and was later revealed to have a storyline more akin to the OVA <i><a href="/wiki/Model_Suit_Gunpla_Builders_Beginning_G" title="Model Suit Gunpla Builders Beginning G">Model Suit Gunpla Builders Beginning G</a></i>. Along with the announcement, several Mobile Suits exclusively from the show will receive their own High Grade kits and will be tied in in the long running Gunpla plastic model line, the new version of the <i>Gundam Try Age</i> Arcade Game and the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a>/<a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Vita" title="PlayStation Vita">PlayStation Vita</a> Game <i>Gundam Breaker</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 31, 2013, Sunrise aired the second trailer of the series, which details more about the story, characters and the Gunpla used in the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In September 30, they announced they will stream the series on YouTube in limited areas of the globe<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouTube_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube-4">[4]</a></sup> with a United States release on the same day as the Japanese release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Sunrise will release the anime on home video via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_Stuf" title="Right Stuf">Right Stuf</a> in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gundam_Build_Fighters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series' music is composed by Yuuki Hayashi, who composed several music scores for <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_Lad" title="Blood Lad">Blood Lad</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Robotics;Notes" title="Robotics;Notes">Robotics;Notes</a></i>.</p>
<p>The first opening theme "Nibun no Ichi" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ニブンノイチ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "One Half")</span> by <a href="/wiki/Back-On" title="Back-On">Back-On</a>, and the ending theme "Imagination &gt; Reality" by <a href="/wiki/AiRI" title="AiRI">AiRI</a> were used from episodes 2 to 13. From episodes 14 to 24, "wimp" by Back-On featuring Lil' Fang (from FAKY)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> was the opening theme while "Han Pan Spirit" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">半パン魂（スピリット）</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Han Pan Supiritto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "Short-Pants Spirit")</span> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyadain" title="Hyadain">Hyadain</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> became the ending theme from episodes 14 to 25.</p>
