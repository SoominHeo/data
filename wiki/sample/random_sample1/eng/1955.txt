<p><b>Heavy equipment</b> refers to heavy-duty <a href="/wiki/Vehicle" title="Vehicle">vehicles</a>, specially designed for executing <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> tasks, most frequently ones involving <a href="/wiki/Earthworks_(engineering)" title="Earthworks (engineering)">earthwork operations</a>. They are also known as <b>heavy machines</b>, <b>heavy trucks</b>, <b>construction equipment</b>, <b>engineering equipment</b>, <b>heavy vehicles</b>, or <b>heavy hydraulics</b>. They usually comprise five equipment systems: implement, <a href="/wiki/Traction_(engineering)" title="Traction (engineering)">traction</a>, structure, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_train" title="Power train">power train</a>, control and information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bare_url_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bare_url-1">[1]</a></sup> Heavy equipment functions through the <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_advantage" title="Mechanical advantage">mechanical advantage</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Simple_machine" title="Simple machine">simple machine</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ratio" title="Ratio">ratio</a> between input force applied and force exerted is multiplied. Some equipment uses <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_drive_system" title="Hydraulic drive system">hydraulic drives</a> as a primary source of motion.</p>

<p>The use of heavy equipment has a long history; the <a href="/wiki/Roman_engineering" title="Roman engineering">ancient Roman engineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Vitruvius" title="Vitruvius">Vitruvius</a> (1st century BCE) gave descriptions of <a href="/wiki/Roman_technology#Engineering_and_construction" title="Roman technology">heavy equipment</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_crane" title="Roman crane">cranes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">ancient Rome</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Treatise" title="Treatise">treatise</a> <i><a href="/wiki/De_architectura" title="De architectura">De architectura</a></i>. The <a href="/wiki/Pile_driver" title="Pile driver">pile driver</a> was invented around 1500. The first <a href="/wiki/Tunnelling_shield" title="Tunnelling shield">tunnelling shield</a> was patented by <a href="/wiki/Isambard_Kingdom_Brunel" title="Isambard Kingdom Brunel">Isambard Kingdom Brunel</a> in 1818.</p>

<p>Until the 19th century and into the early 20th century heavy machines were drawn under human or animal power. With the advent of <a href="/wiki/Portable_engine" title="Portable engine">portable</a> steam-powered engines the drawn machine precursors were reconfigured with the new engines, such as the <a href="/wiki/Combine_harvester" title="Combine harvester">combine harvester</a>. The design of a core tractor evolved around the new steam power source into a new machine core <a href="/wiki/Traction_engine" title="Traction engine">traction engine</a>, that can be configured as the <a href="/wiki/Steam_tractor" title="Steam tractor">steam tractor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Steamroller" title="Steamroller">steamroller</a>. During the 20th century, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internal-combustion_engine" title="Internal-combustion engine">internal-combustion engines</a> became the major power source of heavy equipment. <a href="/wiki/Kerosene" title="Kerosene">Kerosene</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethanol_fuel" title="Ethanol fuel">ethanol</a> engines were used, but today <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel engines</a> are dominant. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mechanical_transmission" title="Mechanical transmission">Mechanical transmission</a> was in many cases replaced by hydraulic machinery. The early 20th century also saw new electric-powered machines such as the <a href="/wiki/Forklift" title="Forklift">forklift</a>. <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar_Inc." title="Caterpillar Inc.">Caterpillar Inc.</a> is a present-day brand from these days, starting out as the <a href="/wiki/Holt_Manufacturing_Company" title="Holt Manufacturing Company">Holt Manufacturing Company</a>. The first mass-produced heavy machine was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fordson_tractor" title="Fordson tractor">Fordson tractor</a> in 1917.</p>
<p>The first commercial <a href="/wiki/Continuous_track" title="Continuous track">continuous track</a> vehicle was the <a href="/wiki/Lombard_Steam_Log_Hauler" title="Lombard Steam Log Hauler">Lombard Steam Log Hauler</a> from 1901. Tracks became extensively used for <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">tanks</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, and after the war they became commonplace for civilian machinery such as the <a href="/wiki/Bulldozer" title="Bulldozer">bulldozer</a>. The largest engineering vehicles, and the largest mobile land machines altogether, are <a href="/wiki/Bucket-wheel_excavator" title="Bucket-wheel excavator">bucket-wheel excavators</a>, built from the 1920s.</p>
<p>"Until almost the twentieth century, one simple tool constituted the primary earthmoving machine: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hand_shovel" title="Hand shovel">hand shovel</a> - moved with animal and human powered, sleds, barges, and wagons. This tool was the principal method by which material was either sidecast or elevated to load a conveyance, usually a <a href="/wiki/Wheelbarrow" title="Wheelbarrow">wheelbarrow</a>, or a cart or wagon drawn by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Draft_animal" title="Draft animal">draft animal</a>. In antiquity, an equivalent of the hand shovel or hoe and head basket—and masses of men—were used to move earth to build civil works. Builders have long used the <a href="/wiki/Inclined_plane" title="Inclined plane">inclined plane</a>, levers, and <a href="/wiki/Pulley" title="Pulley">pulleys</a> to place solid building materials, but these labor-saving devices did not lend themselves to earthmoving, which required digging, raising, moving, and placing loose materials. The two elements required for mechanized earthmoving, then as now, were an independent power source and off-road mobility, neither of which could be provided by the technology of that time."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Container_crane" title="Container crane">Container cranes</a> were used from the 1950s and onwards, and made <a href="/wiki/Containerization" title="Containerization">containerization</a> possible.</p>
<p>Nowadays such is the importance of this machinery, some transport companies have developed specific equipment to transport heavy construction equipment to and from sites.</p>

<p>Heavy equipment requires specialized <a href="/wiki/Tire" title="Tire">tires</a> for various construction applications. While many types of equipment have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caterpillar_track" title="Caterpillar track">continuous tracks</a> applicable to more severe service requirements, tires are used where greater speed or mobility is required. An understanding of what equipment will be used for during the life of the tires is required for proper selection. Tire selection can have a significant impact on production and unit cost. There are three types of off-the-road tires, <i>transport</i> for earthmoving machines, <i>work</i> for slow moving earth moving machines, and <i>load and carry</i> for transporting as well as digging. Off-highway tires have six categories of service <b>C</b> compactor, <b>E</b> earthmover, <b>G</b> grader, <b>L</b> loader, <b>LS</b> log-skidder and <b>ML</b> mining and logging. Within these service categories are various tread types designed for use on hard-packed surface, soft surface and rock. Tires are a large expense on any construction project, careful consideration should be given to prevent excessive wear or damage.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heavy_equipment_operator">Heavy equipment operator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heavy_equipment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Heavy equipment operator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Heavy_equipment_operator" title="Heavy equipment operator">Heavy equipment operator</a></div>
<p>A heavy equipment operator drives and operates <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heavy_equipment_(construction)" title="Heavy equipment (construction)">heavy equipment</a> used in <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Typically only <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skilled_workers" title="Skilled workers">skilled workers</a> may operate heavy equipment, and there is specialized training for learning to use heavy equipment.</p>
<p>Much publication about heavy equipment operators focuses on improving safety for such workers. The field of <a href="/wiki/Occupational_medicine" title="Occupational medicine">occupational medicine</a> researches and makes recommendations about safety for these and other workers in safety-sensitive positions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment_cost">Equipment cost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heavy_equipment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Equipment cost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the small profit margins on construction projects it is important to maintain accurate records concerning equipment utilization, repairs and maintenance. The two main categories of equipment costs are ownership cost and <a href="/wiki/Operating_cost" title="Operating cost">operating cost</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership_Cost">Ownership Cost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heavy_equipment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Ownership Cost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To classify as an ownership cost an expense must have been incurred regardless of if the equipment is used or not. These costs are as follows:</p>
<div class="div-col columns column-count column-count-4" style="-moz-column-count: 4; -webkit-column-count: 4; column-count: 4;">
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Price" title="Price">purchase expense</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salvage_value" title="Salvage value">salvage value</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/MACRS" title="MACRS">tax savings</a> from depreciation</li>
<li>major repairs and <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/overhaul" title="wikt:overhaul">overhauls</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Property_taxes" title="Property taxes">property taxes</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a></li>
<li>storage</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Depreciation" title="Depreciation">Depreciation</a> can be calculated several ways, the simplest is the straight-line method. The annual depreciation is constant, reducing the equipment value annually. The following are simple equations paraphrased from the Peurifoy &amp; Schexnayder text:</p>
<table>
<tr>
<td width="50%">
<p><i>m</i> = some year in the future</p>
<p>N = equipment useful life (years)</p>
<p>and D<sub>n</sub> = Annual depreciation amount</p>
<dl>
<dd><b>D<sub>n</sub> = <i>purchase price</i> / N</b></dd>
</dl>
<p><a href="/wiki/Book_value" title="Book value">Book value</a> (BV) in year <i>m</i></p>
<dl>
<dd><b>BV<sub>m</sub> = <i>purchase price</i> - (<i>m</i> x D<sub>n</sub>)</b></dd>
</dl>
</td>
<td width="50%">
<p><b>example:</b></p>
<p>N = 5</p>
<p><i>purchase price</i> = $350,000</p>
<p><i>m</i> = 3 years from now</p>
<dl>
<dd>BV<sub>3</sub> = $350,000 - ( 3 x $350,000/5) = $140,000</dd>
</dl>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operating_cost">Operating cost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heavy_equipment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Operating cost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For an expense to be classified as an operating cost, it must be incurred through use of the equipment. These costs are as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<table>
<tr>
<td width="33%">
<ul>
<li>F.O.G.
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuel</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lubricants" title="Lubricants">lubricants</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lube_oil" title="Lube oil">lube oils</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oil_filter" title="Oil filter">filters (oil, air, fuel, hydraulic)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grease_(lubricant)" title="Grease (lubricant)">grease</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</td>
<td width="33%">
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Repairs" title="Repairs">repairs</a>
<ul>
<li>repair parts</li>
<li>repair labor</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</td>
<td width="33%">
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tires" title="Tires">tires</a></li>
<li>3rd party service contract</li>
<li>replacement of high-wear items</li>
</ul>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>The biggest distinction from a cost standpoint is if a repair is classified as a <i>major repair</i> or a <i>minor repair</i>. A major repair can change the depreciable equipment value due to an extension in <a href="/wiki/Service_life" title="Service life">service life</a>, while a minor repair is normal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maintenance,_repair_and_operations" title="Maintenance, repair and operations">maintenance</a>. How a firm chooses to cost major and minor repairs vary from firm to firm depending on the costing strategies being used. Some firms will charge only major repairs to the equipment while minor repairs are costed to a project. Another common costing strategy is to cost all repairs to the equipment and only frequently replaced wear items are excluded from the equipment cost. Many firms keep their costing structure closely guarded<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> as it can impact the bidding strategies of their competition. In a company with multiple semi-independent divisions, the equipment department often wants to classify all repairs as "minor" and charge the work to a job - therefore improving their 'profit' from the equipment.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Die-cast_toy" title="Die-cast toy">Die-cast</a> metal promotional <a href="/wiki/Scale_model" title="Scale model">scale models</a> of <b>heavy equipment</b> are often produced for each vehicle to give to prospective customers. These are typically in <a href="/wiki/1:50_scale" title="1:50 scale">1:50 scale</a>. The popular manufacturers of these models are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conrad_(company)" title="Conrad (company)">Conrad</a> and <a href="/wiki/NZG_Models" title="NZG Models">NZG</a> in Germany, even for US vehicles.</p>
