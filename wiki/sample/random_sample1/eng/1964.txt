<p>The <b>sooty dunnart</b> (<i>Sminthopsis fuliginosus</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Dunnart" title="Dunnart">dunnart</a> found in <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>. It is one of the least-known of the dunnarts, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN">IUCN</a> classifying it as <a href="/wiki/Data_deficient" title="Data deficient">data deficient</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup> It was formerly believed to be a subspecies of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Common_dunnart" title="Common dunnart">common dunnart</a> (<i>S. murina</i>).</p>
