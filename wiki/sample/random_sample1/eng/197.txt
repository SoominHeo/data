<p><i><b>[e]</b></i> is Korean hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/Epik_High" title="Epik High">Epik High</a>'s sixth official album released in September 2009 and is also their third major album release of the same year. The two other albums released in 2009 are <i><a href="/wiki/Map_the_Soul" title="Map the Soul">Map the Soul</a></i> and <i>Remixing the Human Soul</i>, an electronica-inspired remix compilation album with labelmates Planet Shiver. Like <a href="/wiki/Map_the_Soul" title="Map the Soul">Map the Soul</a> before it, <i>[e]</i> includes a complimentary mini-book along with the actual audio CDs. The 74 page mini-book contains song lyrics, interviews, photographs, and various production notes from the artists themselves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>[e]</i> contains two discs of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop musical</a> experimentation in various genres. Genres experimented include electro-hop, <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical music</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acoustic_rock" title="Acoustic rock">acoustic rock</a>, and traditional Korean <a href="/wiki/Trot_(music)" title="Trot (music)">trot music</a>. The album's themes are divided separately into two discs. Disc 1, entitled [e]motion, contains tracks dealing with personal themes and is relatively less mainstream friendly compared to the second disc. Disc 2, conversely entitled [e]nergy, contains radio-friendly tracks, such as the lead single "따라해(Wannabe)(ft. Mellow)."</p>
<p>Notable guest artists on this release include frequent collaborator and Map the Soul labelmate MYK, Korean American emcee <a href="/wiki/Kero_One" title="Kero One">Kero One</a>, battle rapper <a href="/wiki/Dumbfoundead" title="Dumbfoundead">Dumbfoundead</a>, Rakka (from <a href="/wiki/Dilated_Peoples" title="Dilated Peoples">Dilated Peoples</a>), underground rap prodigy <a href="/wiki/Dok2" title="Dok2">Dok2</a>, and electronica group Planet Shiver.</p>
