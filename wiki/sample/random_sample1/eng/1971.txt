<p>The <b>Nilgiri langur</b> (<i>Trachypithecus johnii</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Lutung" title="Lutung">lutung</a> (a type of <a href="/wiki/Old_World_monkey" title="Old World monkey">Old World monkey</a>) found in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nilgiri_Hills" title="Nilgiri Hills">Nilgiri Hills</a> of the Western Ghats in <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South India</a>. Its range also includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kodagu" title="Kodagu">Kodagu</a> in <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, Kodayar Hills in <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a>, and many other hilly areas in <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a> and Tamil Nadu. This <a href="/wiki/Primate" title="Primate">primate</a> has glossy black fur on its body and golden brown fur on its head. It is similar in size and long-tailed like the <a href="/wiki/Gray_langur" title="Gray langur">gray langurs</a>. Females have a white patch of fur on the inner thigh. It typically lives in troops of nine to ten monkeys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup> Its diet consists of fruits, shoots and leaves. The species is endangered due to deforestation and poaching for its fur and flesh, the latter believed to have <a href="/wiki/Aphrodisiac" title="Aphrodisiac">aphrodisiac</a> properties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
