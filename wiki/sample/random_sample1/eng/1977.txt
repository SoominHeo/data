<p>The <b>black titi</b> (<i>Callicebus lugens</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Titi" title="Titi">titi</a>, a type of <a href="/wiki/New_World_monkey" title="New World monkey">New World monkey</a>, from <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. It is found in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communication">Communication</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_titi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Communication">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some vocalizations of black titi are listed here: (1). Morning duet â the most commonly heard vocalization of the pair, singing in duet, complex and utilized to defend territory; it is interchanged with neighboring groups as counter-singing; (2) danger peep â various soft, high-pitched peeps but sometimes low intensity, advising of danger; very difficult to localize; (3) purr â sounds very much like a cat's purr; used by all members of the group to show contentment, affection or request for food, grooming or contact; (4) rough growl â given by young animals when complaining of rain or when greeting adults; (5) sharp scream â when fighting to express extra disgust; (6) play growl â low, gargling growl used in play and changing in tone, terminating in interrogative tone; (7) soft whine â especially young animals but also adults when requesting something of another such as food or while grooming another; (8) bark â loud, sharp and sudden bark when molested by the unwelcome close presence of other larger primates such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lagothrix" title="Lagothrix">Lagothrix</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cebus" title="Cebus">Cebus</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ateles" title="Ateles">Ateles</a></i> or raptors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
