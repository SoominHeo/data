<p><b>.asia</b> is the official designated regional domain extension for <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">Pacific</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Sponsored_top-level_domain" title="Sponsored top-level domain">sponsored generic top-level-domain (gTLD)</a> operated by the DotAsia Organisation Ltd. .asia is open to companies, individuals and organisations who have connection to the region. .Asia domains can be seen and used by international and Asian businesses; regional conferences and symposiums; as well as Asian artists and celebrities.</p>
<p>The .Asia web address was introduced to the public through a comprehensive launch involving a multiphased <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_period" title="Sunrise period">Sunrise</a> and <a href="/wiki/Landrush_period" title="Landrush period">Landrush</a> process from October 9, 2007 to March 12, 2008. It became available on a first-come-first-served registration basis on March 26, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2013, there are more than 455,000 .asia domains registered<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> across 155 countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roll-out.2Fresponse">Roll-out/response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Roll-out/response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>.asia founded the first Pioneer Domains Program on July 20, 2007, more than two months prior to the opening of its Sunrise launch. It offered businesses and individuals an opportunity to own and build on any .Asia domain before the TLD opened its doors to mass public registration. Applicants were asked to submit a brief business plan for the domain of choice and make a marketing deposit of US$10,000. The full deposit was returned to successful applicants against proof of marketing attributed to the promotion of the built out .Asia website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Pioneer Domains Program marks the first time ever a domain registry is opening its doors for domains based on the quality of proposals received for any domain name of choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-4">[4]</a></sup> Similar programs (often referred to as “Founders Program” or “RFP process”) were implemented by other domain launches since.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pioneer_Domains_Program">Pioneer Domains Program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pioneer Domains Program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>.Asia founded the first Pioneer Domains Program on July 20, 2007, more than two months prior to the opening of its Sunrise launch. It offered businesses and individuals an opportunity to own and build on any .Asia domain before the TLD opened its doors to mass public registration. Applicants were asked to submit a brief business plan for the domain of choice and make a marketing deposit of US$10,000. The full deposit was returned to successful applicants against proof of marketing attributed to the promotion of the built out .Asia website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Pioneer Domains Program marks the first time ever a domain registry is opening its doors for domains based on the quality of proposals received for any domain name of choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-4">[4]</a></sup> Similar programs (often referred to as “Founders Program” or “RFP process”) were implemented by other domain launches since.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".Asia_Community_.2F_Eligibility">.Asia Community / Eligibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: .Asia Community / Eligibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The DotAsia Organisation, operator of the .Asia TLD, is governed by a community of country-code-Top \-Level-Domains within the region including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/.CN" title=".CN">.CN</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>), <a href="/wiki/.ir" title=".ir">.IR</a> (<a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/.JP" title=".JP">.JP</a> (<a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/.KR" title=".KR">.KR</a>, (<a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/.PH" title=".PH">.PH</a> (<a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/.SG" title=".SG">.SG</a> (<a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>) and pan-Asia Internet / Information Technology related groups such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=APTLD&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="APTLD (page does not exist)">APTLD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APNIC" title="APNIC">APNIC</a>.</p>
<p>While the .Asia TLD aims to serve Asian communities worldwide as avowed on their website:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>".Asia is open to any individual, business and organization around the world, and is fast becoming the web address of choice by Asian personalities, international brands and local initiatives across the Asia-Pacific markets. Beyond the region itself, .Asia appeals to Asian communities globally, including Asian Americans, Chinatowns, Korean Towns, Little Indias, etc. around the world."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The registry has adopted the boundaries as defined by <a href="/wiki/ICANN" title="ICANN">ICANN</a> for the Asia / Australia / Pacific (AP) region as a basis for its scope of eligibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Every .Asia domain must be associated with a Charter Eligibility Contact to be eligible and to help demonstrate that the .ASIA domain is associated with the Asia Pacific region. Since registration criteria is not based on address, this means that one does not necessary have to reside within Asia to own a .Asia domain (as long as the registrant satisfies the Charter Eligibility requirement), and indeed many individuals / entities outside the region have been able to register .Asia domains by selecting a proxy agent (sometimes provided by a registrar or agent).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Allegations of <a href="/wiki/Insider_trading" title="Insider trading">insider trading</a> and conflict of interest have been levelled at Pool.com, exclusive auction service provider for the .Asia Sunrise and Landrush. The ‘DotAsia.com Plot’ describes that the CEO of Pool.com, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Schreier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Richard Schreier (page does not exist)">Richard Schreier</a>, is accused of securing with unfair advantage several premium .Asia domain names via a number of corporations he apparently has links with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mr Schreier specifies that he does <i>"not have any ownership interest"</i> in the corporations involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> As of 15 May 2008, it is unclear whether Mr Schreier has control over the domains in question, nor whether this would breach the DotAsia contract or any laws for countries that the TLD applies to.</p>
<p>The 'DotAsia.com Plot' website that makes the allegations, claims to have received correspondence from CEO of DotAsia, Mr Chung, stating that <i>"Being a party related to a bidding party, in my understanding, does not automatically create any issue."</i> If there is such an affiliation, it would almost certainly be illegal under Australian competition law (where one of the companies involved, Throne Ventures Pty Ltd, is registered.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>.Asia responded by releasing a Special Advisory clarifying the rules already in place for addressing conflicts of interest and bid rigging issues and that there is no evidence suggesting that bidders have been advantaged or disadvantaged due to their relationship or non-relationship with Pool.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some of the domains in question: porn.asia; dating.asia; insurance.asia; lotto.asia; stocks.asia; auction.asia; beer.asia; fitness.asia; girls.asia; wine.asia</p>
<p>However, all of these domains according to the .ASIA whois service, were awarded during the Sunrise period which means their applications were made with a claim of prior right (registered trademark identical to the domain name). These claims would have been validated by the .ASIA validation partner (there were two, including Deloitte Touche) and where multiple applications were received, the domains would go to auction. The actual auction schedule shows these domains did not go to auction from which it can be concluded there was only a single verified application for the domains in question.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Internationalized_Domain_Names">Internationalized Domain Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Internationalized Domain Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>.Asia launched registrations for Chinese, Japanese and Korean <a href="/wiki/Internationalized_domain_name" title="Internationalized domain name">Internationalized Domain Name</a> (IDN) registrations (e.g. "交易.asia", "ビデオ.asia", "게임.asia") from May 11 – October 11, 2011. Over 10,000 domains were applied during the Sunrise and Landrush launch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Chinese, Japanese and Korean .Asia IDN domains started selling on a first-come-first-served basis on June 21, 2012. .Asia plans to launch other Asian language IDN .Asia domains at a later date.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="DotAsia_Organisation">DotAsia Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: DotAsia Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The DotAsia Organisation, registry operator of the .Asia domain, is a not-for-profit, membership-based organization with a mandate to promote Internet development and adoption in Asia. DotAsia is headquartered in Hong Kong and formed as an open membership consortium of national / official top-level domain authorities around the region (e.g. “.cn” in China, “.jp” in Japan, “.in” in India, “.sg” in Singapore”, etc.) and regional Internet organizations (e.g. APNIC, APTLD, APIA, etc.).</p>
<p>Since the launch of the “.Asia” DotAsia has contributed funds and support to different socio-technological advancement community projects around Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> These projects include but are not limited to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OLPC" title="OLPC">OLPC</a> <a class="external text" href="http://www.olpc.asia" rel="nofollow">(One-Laptop-Per-Child Asia Pacific)</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.isif.asia" rel="nofollow">Information Society Innovations Fund</a>, youth volunteer program &amp; Network: the <a class="external text" href="http://www.netmission.asia" rel="nofollow">NetMission Ambassador Program</a>, the <a class="external text" href="http://www.aprigf.asia" rel="nofollow">Asia Pacific Regional Internet Governance Forum</a> (APrIGF), <a class="external text" href="http://digitalreview.asia" rel="nofollow">Digital Review of Asia Pacific</a>.</p>
<p>Together with HN Group in Macau, DotAsia Organisation is also providing its expertise and knowledge to support the Macau government in the operation of the .MO country-code-top-level-domain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
