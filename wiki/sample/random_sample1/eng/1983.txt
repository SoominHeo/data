<p><b>Gary Faye Locke</b> (born January 21, 1950) is an <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_the_United_States" title="Politics of the United States">American politician</a>. Locke was the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_China" title="United States Ambassador to China">United States ambassador to China</a> (2011-14). He was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_Washington" title="List of Governors of Washington">21st Governor of Washington</a> (1997-2005) and served in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Obama_administration" title="Obama administration">Obama administration</a> as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_Commerce" title="United States Secretary of Commerce">United States Secretary of Commerce</a> (2009-11).</p>
<p>Locke is the first <a href="/wiki/Governor_(United_States)" title="Governor (United States)">governor</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Continental_United_States" title="Continental United States">continental United States</a> of Asian descent, and is the only <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_American" title="Chinese American">Chinese American</a> ever to serve as a governor of any state. He is also the first Chinese American to serve as the U.S. ambassador to China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimeBio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TimeBio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_education">Family and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gary Locke was born on January 21, 1950, in Seattle, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, and spent his early years living in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yesler_Terrace" title="Yesler Terrace">Yesler Terrace</a> public <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Housing_project" title="Housing project">housing project</a>. A third-generation Chinese American with paternal ancestry from <a href="/wiki/Taishan" title="Taishan">Taishan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">China</a>, Locke is the second of five children of James <a href="/wiki/Locke_(surname)" title="Locke (surname)">Locke</a>, who served as a <a href="/wiki/Staff_sergeant#United_States" title="Staff sergeant">staff sergeant</a> in the <a href="/wiki/5th_Armored_Division_(United_States)" title="5th Armored Division (United States)">U.S. Fifth Armored Division</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. His wife, Julie, is from <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His paternal grandfather left China in the 1890s and moved to the United States, where he worked as a <a href="/wiki/Houseboy" title="Houseboy">houseboy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Washington" title="Olympia, Washington">Olympia</a>, Washington, in exchange for <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> lessons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-englishlessons_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-englishlessons-3">[3]</a></sup> Gary Locke did not learn how to speak English until he was five years old and entered kindergarten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-english_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-english-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Locke graduated with honors from Seattle’s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franklin_High_School_(Seattle,_Washington)" title="Franklin High School (Seattle, Washington)">Franklin High School</a> in 1968. He achieved <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagle_Scout_rank_(Boy_Scouts_of_America)" title="Eagle Scout rank (Boy Scouts of America)">Eagle Scout</a> rank and received the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Eagle_Scout_Award" title="Distinguished Eagle Scout Award">Distinguished Eagle Scout Award</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scouting_in_Washington" title="Scouting in Washington">Boy Scouts of America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-english_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-english-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HistoryLinkBio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-HistoryLinkBio-5">[5]</a></sup> Through a combination of part-time jobs, financial aid, and scholarships, Locke attended <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>, graduating with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">political science</a> in 1972.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AsianBio_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AsianBio-6">[6]</a></sup> He later received his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juris_doctor" title="Juris doctor">juris doctor</a> from <a href="/wiki/Boston_University_School_of_Law" title="Boston University School of Law">Boston University School of Law</a> in 1975.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Washington_state">Washington state</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Washington state">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1982, Locke was elected to Washington state's South Seattle district in the <a href="/wiki/Washington_House_of_Representatives" title="Washington House of Representatives">Washington House of Representatives</a>, where he served as the chair of the Appropriations Committee. Eleven years later, in 1993, Locke was elected as <a href="/wiki/King_County,_Washington" title="King County, Washington">King County</a>'s executive, defeating incumbent <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hill_(politician)" title="Tim Hill (politician)">Tim Hill</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governor">Governor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gary_Locke.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="725" data-file-width="510" height="313" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Gary_Locke.jpg/220px-Gary_Locke.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Gary_Locke.jpg/330px-Gary_Locke.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Gary_Locke.jpg/440px-Gary_Locke.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gary_Locke.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Locke as Governor of Washington</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1996, Locke won the <a href="/wiki/Washington_gubernatorial_election,_1996" title="Washington gubernatorial election, 1996">Democratic primary and general election for governor of Washington</a>, becoming the first Chinese American governor in United States history. His political committee was fined $2,500 by regulators in 1997 after admitting to state campaign finance law violations during his successful campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sidoti_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sidoti-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Locke faced criticism from fellow Democrats for embracing the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a>'s "no-new-taxes" approach to dealing with Washington's budget woes during and after the 2001 economic turmoil. Among his spending-reduction proposals were laying off thousands of state employees; reducing health coverage; freezing most state employees' pay; and cutting funding for nursing homes and programs for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developmentally_disabled" title="Developmentally disabled">developmentally disabled</a>. In his final budget, Locke suspended two voter-passed school initiatives and cut state education funding. Supported by the state's political left, former <a href="/wiki/Washington_Supreme_Court" title="Washington Supreme Court">Washington Supreme Court</a> Justice Phil Talmadge announced his plans to challenge Locke in the 2004 Democratic primary, but Talmadge ended his campaign early for health reasons; Locke <a href="/wiki/Washington_gubernatorial_election,_2000" title="Washington gubernatorial election, 2000">won re-election in 2000</a>.</p>
<p>On the national stage, Democrats saw Gary Locke as a possible vice-presidential choice. In 1997, Locke was a guest at that year's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_of_the_Union_address" title="State of the Union address">State of the Union address</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Locke was chosen to give his party's response to <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2003_State_of_the_Union_Address" title="2003 State of the Union Address">2003 State of the Union Address</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-speaker_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-speaker-9">[9]</a></sup> In a surprise move, Locke announced in July 2003 that he would not seek a third term,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-retire-10">[10]</a></sup> saying, "Despite my deep love of our state, I want to devote more time to my family."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-retire-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Susan_Paynter" title="Susan Paynter">Susan Paynter</a>, a columnist at the <i><a href="/wiki/Seattle_Post-Intelligencer" title="Seattle Post-Intelligencer">Seattle Post-Intelligencer</a></i>, suggested that racist slurs, insults, and threats that Locke and his family received, especially a large number which came after his rebuttal to George W. Bush's 2003 State of the Union address, may have played a role in Locke's decision to leave office after two terms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PI_Paynter_20030726_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PI_Paynter_20030726-11">[11]</a></sup> The governor's office received hundreds of threatening letters and e-mails; others threatened to kill his children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PI_Paynter_20030726_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-PI_Paynter_20030726-11">[11]</a></sup> His official portrait, painted by <a href="/wiki/Michele_Rushworth" title="Michele Rushworth">Michele Rushworth</a>, was unveiled in the state capitol by Governor <a href="/wiki/Christine_Gregoire" title="Christine Gregoire">Christine Gregoire</a> on January 4, 2006.</p>
<p>After leaving office, Locke joined the Seattle office of international law firm <a href="/wiki/Davis_Wright_Tremaine" title="Davis Wright Tremaine">Davis Wright Tremaine</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LLP" title="LLP">LLP</a>, in their China and governmental-relations practice groups. During the leadup to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)_presidential_primaries,_2008" title="Democratic Party (United States) presidential primaries, 2008">2008 Democratic presidential primary</a>, Governor Locke signed on as Washington co-chairman of Democratic candidate <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton_presidential_campaign,_2008" title="Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2008">bid for President</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PI_Ammons_20071007_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-PI_Ammons_20071007-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Secretary_of_Commerce">Secretary of Commerce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Secretary of Commerce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GaryLockeFamily.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="640" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/GaryLockeFamily.jpg/250px-GaryLockeFamily.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/GaryLockeFamily.jpg/375px-GaryLockeFamily.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/GaryLockeFamily.jpg/500px-GaryLockeFamily.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>

<p>On December 4, 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> reported that Locke was a potential candidate for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Secretary_of_the_Interior" title="U.S. Secretary of the Interior">Secretary of the Interior</a> in then-President-elect <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s cabinet. Eventually, then-<a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a> Senator, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Salazar" title="Ken Salazar">Ken Salazar</a>, was nominated for that position instead.</p>
<p>On February 25, 2009, Locke was announced as President Barack Obama's choice for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_Commerce" title="Secretary of Commerce">Secretary of Commerce</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sidoti_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sidoti-7">[7]</a></sup> and his nomination was confirmed by the United States Senate by unanimous consent on March 24, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-confirmation_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-confirmation-13">[13]</a></sup> Locke was sworn in March 26, 2009, by District Judge <a href="/wiki/Richard_A._Jones" title="Richard A. Jones">Richard A. Jones</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and he was sworn in by President Obama on May 1, 2009. Locke was the first Chinese American appointed as Secretary of Commerce, and one of three Asian Americans in Obama's cabinet, joining <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_Energy" title="United States Secretary of Energy">Secretary of Energy</a> <a href="/wiki/Steven_Chu" title="Steven Chu">Steven Chu</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_Veterans_Affairs" title="United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs">Secretary of Veterans Affairs</a> <a href="/wiki/Eric_Shinseki" title="Eric Shinseki">Eric Shinseki</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Politico" title="Politico">Politico</a></i> reported Locke had been a popular cabinet member among both businesses and the executive branch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> A declaration of assets made in March 2011 showed Locke to be the sixth-richest official in the U.S. executive branch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scmp20120518jiao_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-scmp20120518jiao-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ambassador_to_China">Ambassador to China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ambassador to China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1,_2012.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3057" data-file-width="4585" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1%2C_2012.jpg/220px-Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1%2C_2012.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1%2C_2012.jpg/330px-Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1%2C_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1%2C_2012.jpg/440px-Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1%2C_2012.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kurt_Campbell_with_Chen_Guangcheng_at_US_Embassy_May_1,_2012.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Chen_Guangcheng" title="Chen Guangcheng">Chen Guangcheng</a> (left) with Locke (center) and <a href="/wiki/Kurt_M._Campbell" title="Kurt M. Campbell">Kurt M. Campbell</a> (right) at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Embassy_in_Beijing" title="U.S. Embassy in Beijing">U.S. Embassy in Beijing</a> on May 1, 2012</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Following the resignation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jon_Huntsman,_Jr." title="Jon Huntsman, Jr.">Jon Huntsman, Jr.</a>, Locke was nominated by President Obama to serve as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_China" title="United States Ambassador to China">U.S. ambassador to China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The Senate confirmed Locke unanimously on July 27, 2011;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Locke resigned as Secretary of Commerce on August 1, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>A photo of Locke carrying his own backpack and ordering his own coffee at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seattle-Tacoma_International_Airport" title="Seattle-Tacoma International Airport">Seattle-Tacoma International Airport</a> went viral in China on the <a href="/wiki/Sina_Weibo" title="Sina Weibo">Sina Weibo</a> <a href="/wiki/Social_network" title="Social network">social network</a>, with many commentators approving of his humble low-key style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> At his first news conference after arrival in Beijing, Locke pledged to promote bilateral cooperation and understanding between the two countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Early in Locke's ambassadorship, Chinese dissident <a href="/wiki/Chen_Guangcheng" title="Chen Guangcheng">Chen Guangcheng</a> escaped from house arrest and sought refuge in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Embassy_in_Beijing" title="US Embassy in Beijing">U.S. Embassy in Beijing</a> in April 2012. On May 2, <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China">China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs</a> demanded an apology from the U.S. for its role in the incident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In an editorial on May 4, <i><a class="external text" href="http://www.chnsourcing.com/" rel="nofollow">Beijing Daily</a></i> questioned Locke's motives by taking in Chen<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scmp20120518jiao_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-scmp20120518jiao-16">[16]</a></sup> and described Chen as "a tool and a pawn for American politicians to blacken China".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late November 2013, Locke announced that he would step down as ambassador to spend more time with his family in Seattle. In an analysis of his ambassadorship, Sun Zhe, a professor at <a href="/wiki/Tsinghua_University" title="Tsinghua University">Tsinghua University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, said, "It is not an easy job to be the American ambassador to China. Gary Locke is not a shining star, but a simple and unadorned ambassador." Shen Dingli, dean of the international studies department at <a href="/wiki/Fudan_University" title="Fudan University">Fudan University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, largely concurred, commenting that Locke "showed us how a U.S. minister-level official behaves by taking economy-class flights" but "caused a twist" when he allowed Chen refuge in the U.S. embassy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Max_Baucus" title="Max Baucus">Max Baucus</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senator</a> from <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, was President Obama's nominee to replace Locke and was confirmed by the Senate 96–0; Vice President <a href="/wiki/Joe_Biden" title="Joe Biden">Joe Biden</a> administered the <a href="/wiki/Oath_of_office" title="Oath of office">oath of office</a> on February 21, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Ambassador">Post-Ambassador</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-Ambassador">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On Feb 17, 2016, Locke joined the board of the now Chinese-owned <a href="/wiki/AMC_Theatres" title="AMC Theatres">AMC Theatres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 31, 2016, Locke joined the board of the Seattle-based global health nonprofit <a class="external text" href="http://www.PATH.org" rel="nofollow">PATH</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Locke married his wife, Mona Lee, a television reporter for the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/KING-TV" title="KING-TV">KING 5 television</a> in Seattle and former <a href="/wiki/Miss_Asian_America" title="Miss Asian America">Miss Asian America</a>, on October 15, 1994. Her parents immigrated to the United States from <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, while they were originally from <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">Mainland China</a>, her paternal side from <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> and maternal side from <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a>. Mona Lee's father Larry Lee has a half-sister <a href="/wiki/Nora_Sun" title="Nora Sun">Nora Sun</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Sun_Fo" title="Sun Fo">Sun Fo</a> and granddaughter of <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen" title="Sun Yat-sen">Sun Yat-sen</a>, father of modern China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The Lockes have three children: Emily Nicole (b. March 1997), Dylan James (b. March 1999), and Madeline Lee (b. November 2004).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Regarding his ethnicity and being the only person of Chinese descent to have served as an ambassador to China, he said, "I’m proud of my Chinese heritage. I’m proud of the great contributions that China has made to world civilization over thousands of years. But I’m thoroughly American. I’m proud of the great values that America has brought to the entire world and all that America stands for."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
