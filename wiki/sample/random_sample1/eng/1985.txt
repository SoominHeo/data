<p><b>Gary Ronald Honey</b> (born 26 July 1959 in <a href="/wiki/Thomastown,_Victoria" title="Thomastown, Victoria">Thomastown, Victoria</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Long_jump" title="Long jump">long jumper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. He won a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1984_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1984 Summer Olympics">1984 Olympics</a> with a jump of 8.24 metres.</p>
<p>In addition, Honey won gold medals at the <a href="/wiki/1982_Commonwealth_Games" title="1982 Commonwealth Games">1982</a> and <a href="/wiki/1986_Commonwealth_Games" title="1986 Commonwealth Games">1986 Commonwealth Games</a>. He participated in three Summer Olympics, starting in 1980. He was ranked number 2 in the World from 1984 to 1986. He was also ranked in the top 6 in the world from 1981 to 1988. He was 10 times Australian Champion, and 11 times Victorian Champion. In 1988, Gary was the Team Captain for the Seoul Olympic Games.</p>
<p>Honey was inducted into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sport_Australia_Hall_of_Fame" title="Sport Australia Hall of Fame">Sport Australia Hall of Fame</a> in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
