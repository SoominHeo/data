<p><b>Crab sticks</b> (<b>imitation crab meat</b>, <b>seafood sticks</b>, <b>krab</b>) are a form of <a href="/wiki/Kamaboko" title="Kamaboko">kamaboko</a>, a processed seafood made of starch and finely pulverized <a href="/wiki/Whitefish_(fisheries_term)" title="Whitefish (fisheries term)">white fish</a> (<a href="/wiki/Surimi" title="Surimi">surimi</a>), shaped and cured to resemble the leg meat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snow_crab" title="Snow crab">snow crab</a> or <a href="/wiki/Japanese_spider_crab" title="Japanese spider crab">Japanese spider crab</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Food_Hydrocolloids_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Food_Hydrocolloids-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crab_stick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sugiyo Co., Ltd.</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja"><a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%B9%E3%82%AE%E3%83%A8" title="ja:ã¹ã®ã¨">ã¹ã®ã¨</a></span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sugiyo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> of Japan first produced and patented imitation crab meat in 1973, as Kanikama. This was a flake type. In 1974, Osaki Suisan Co., Ltd., of Japan first produced and patented imitation crab sticks.</p>
<p>In 1976, The Berelson Company of San Francisco, California, US, working with Sugiyo, introduced them internationally. Kanikama is still their common name in Japan, but internationally they are marketed under names including Krab Sticks, Ocean Sticks, Sea Legs and Imitation Crab Sticks. Legal restrictions now prevent them from being marketed as "Crab Sticks" in many places, as they usually do not have crab meat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alaska_pollock" title="Alaska pollock">Alaska pollock</a> (<i>Theragra chalcogramma</i>) from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Pacific" title="North Pacific">North Pacific</a> is commonly the main ingredient, often mixed with fillers such as wheat, and egg white (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albumen" title="Albumen">albumen</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Food_Hydrocolloids_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Food_Hydrocolloids-1">[1]</a></sup> or other binding ingredient, such as the enzyme <a href="/wiki/Transglutaminase" title="Transglutaminase">transglutaminase</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Crab flavoring is added (either artificial or crab-derived), and a layer of red food coloring is applied to the outside.</p>
