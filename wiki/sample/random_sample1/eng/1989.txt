<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gebhard, Count of the Lahngau</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Gebhard</b> (died 879) was a mid-9th-century count in the <a href="/wiki/Lahngau" title="Lahngau">Lahngau</a> and the first documented ancestor of the dynasty later known as the <a href="/wiki/Conradines" title="Conradines">Conradines</a>. He was a "leading man of the <a href="/wiki/East_Francia" title="East Francia">[East] Franks</a>" and a brother-in-law of Ernest, margrave of the <a href="/wiki/March_of_the_Nordgau" title="March of the Nordgau">Bavarian Nordgau</a>. Gebhard may be a son of <a href="/wiki/Odo_I,_Count_of_Orl%C3%A9ans" title="Odo I, Count of Orléans">Odo I, Count of Orléans</a>, if identical with Udo the Elder, count in the Lahngau from 821 to 826.</p>
<p>In 838, he allied with <a href="/wiki/Poppo_of_Grapfeld" title="Poppo of Grapfeld">Poppo of Grapfeld</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otgar,_Archbishop_of_Mainz" title="Otgar, Archbishop of Mainz">Otgar, Archbishop of Mainz</a>, against the rebellious <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_German" title="Louis the German">Louis the German</a> and in favour of the emperor <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_Pious" title="Louis the Pious">Louis the Pious</a>.</p>
<p>He was the father of</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Udo_of_Neustria" title="Udo of Neustria">Udo</a>, count in the Lahngau</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Waldo_the_Abbot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Waldo the Abbot (page does not exist)">Waldo</a>, abbot of <a href="/wiki/St._Maximin%27s_Abbey,_Trier" title="St. Maximin's Abbey, Trier">St. Maximin's at Trier</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bertulf_(Archbishop_of_Trier)" title="Bertulf (Archbishop of Trier)">Bertulf</a>, Bishop of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Trier" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Trier">Trier</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Berengar_I_of_Neustria" title="Berengar I of Neustria">Berengar</a>, count in the <a href="/wiki/Hessengau" title="Hessengau">Hessengau</a></li>
</ul>
<p>who all rose to prominent positions in <a href="/wiki/West_Francia" title="West Francia">West Francia</a>.</p>
