<p><b>George Terter II</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Георги Тертер II</span>) reigned as emperor (<a href="/wiki/Tsar" title="Tsar">tsar</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> between 1322 and 1323. The exact date of his birth is unknown, but he was born not long before 1307.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_II_of_Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>George Terter II was the son of <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Svetoslav_of_Bulgaria" title="Theodore Svetoslav of Bulgaria">Theodore Svetoslav</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euphrosyne_of_Crimea" title="Euphrosyne of Crimea">Euphrosyne</a>, and was named after his paternal grandfather <a href="/wiki/George_I_of_Bulgaria" title="George I of Bulgaria">George Terter I</a>. It is possible that he was associated as co-emperor by his father in 1321, but the sources are unclear. After his father's death in 1322, he became actively involved in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine_civil_war_of_1321-1328" title="Byzantine civil war of 1321-1328">civil war</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>, in which the throne was being contested between <a href="/wiki/Andronikos_II_Palaiologos" title="Andronikos II Palaiologos">Andronikos II Palaiologos</a> and his grandson <a href="/wiki/Andronikos_III_Palaiologos" title="Andronikos III Palaiologos">Andronikos III Palaiologos</a>. Taking advantage of the situation, George Terter II invaded Byzantine <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a> and, encountering little, if any, resistance, conquered the major city of Philippopolis (<a href="/wiki/Plovdiv" title="Plovdiv">Plovdiv</a>) and part of the surrounding area in 1322. A Bulgarian garrison was installed under the command of a general named <a href="/wiki/Ivan_the_Russian" title="Ivan the Russian">Ivan the Russian</a>, and a court scribe praised George Terter II as a "possessor of the Bulgarian and the Greek sceptre". A new campaign later the same year brought the subjugation of several fortresses around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adrianople" title="Adrianople">Adrianople</a>, but the Bulgarians were now turned back and defeated by Andronikos III. The Byzantine emperor was preparing for an invasion of Bulgaria, when he heard the news that George Terter II had died, apparently of natural causes.</p>
<p>George Terter II died as a young man and did not have any known offspring. He was succeeded by his distant cousin <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Shishman" title="Michael Shishman">Michael Asen III</a>, now commonly called Michael Shishman.</p>
