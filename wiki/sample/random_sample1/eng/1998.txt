<p><b>Gabriel Isaac Luna</b> (born December 5, 1982) is an American actor. He is known for his roles as Tony Bravo on the <a href="/wiki/El_Rey_Network" title="El Rey Network">El Rey Network</a> action drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Matador_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Matador (U.S. TV series)">Matador</a></i> and Paco Contreras on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> crime drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Wicked_City_(TV_series)" title="Wicked City (TV series)">Wicked City</a></i>. He has starred in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Bernie_(2011_film)" title="Bernie (2011 film)">Bernie</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Balls_Out_(2014_film)" title="Balls Out (2014 film)">Balls Out</a></i> (2014), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freeheld_(2015_film)" title="Freeheld (2015 film)">Freeheld</a></i> (2015), <i><a href="/wiki/Gravy_(film)" title="Gravy (film)">Gravy</a></i> (2015), and <i><a href="/wiki/Transpecos_(film)" title="Transpecos (film)">Transpecos</a></i> (2016). In 2016, he joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Agents_of_S.H.I.E.L.D." title="Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.">Marvel's Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Ghost_Rider_(Robbie_Reyes)" title="Ghost Rider (Robbie Reyes)">Robbie Reyes / Ghost Rider</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Luna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gabriel Isaac Luna was born in <a href="/wiki/Travis,_Texas" title="Travis, Texas">Travis, Texas</a> to Deborah Ann (née Perez) and Gabriel Lopez Luna (1962–1982), both of whom are of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_American" title="Mexican American">Mexican</a> parentage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Birth_Record_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Birth_Record-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davis_Luna_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Davis_Luna-3">[3]</a></sup> His father died aged 20, three months prior to Luna's birth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was raised by his mother in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>. Luna attended <a href="/wiki/St._Edward%27s_University" title="St. Edward's University">St. Edward's University</a>, where he made his stage debut as <a href="/wiki/Romeo" title="Romeo">Romeo Montague</a> in a production of <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i>. Luna graduated from St. Edward's in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Luna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Luna made his screen acting debut as Kristofer Rostropovich in the drama film <i>Fall to Grace</i>, which premiered at <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> on March 12, 2005. He then provided voice-over work for the science fiction video game <i><a href="/wiki/BlackSite:_Area_51" title="BlackSite: Area 51">BlackSite: Area 51</a></i>, which was released on November 12, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-5">[5]</a></sup> Luna is a founding member of the Austin-based Paper Chairs Theatre Company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On stage in Austin, Luna has portrayed Sergei Maxudov in <i><a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Bulgakov#Novels_and_short_story_collections" title="Mikhail Bulgakov">Black Snow</a></i> (2009),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> the title character in <i><a href="/wiki/Orestes_(play)" title="Orestes (play)">Orestes</a></i> (2009),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and Clov in <i><a href="/wiki/Endgame_(play)" title="Endgame (play)">Endgame</a></i> (2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> For these three performances, he received the Austin Critics Table Best Lead Actor Award in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Luna then starred as the lead character, Nate Hitchins, in the drama film <i>Dance with the One</i>, which was selected for South by Southwest and premiered on March 20, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-5">[5]</a></sup> The following year, he had a supporting role in the black comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Bernie_(2011_film)" title="Bernie (2011 film)">Bernie</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Linklater" title="Richard Linklater">Richard Linklater</a>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Film_Festival" title="Los Angeles Film Festival">Los Angeles Film Festival</a> on June 16, 2011. Luna's television credits include minor roles on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>'s serial drama <i><a href="/wiki/Prison_Break" title="Prison Break">Prison Break</a></i> (2008), <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s television film <i><a href="/wiki/Temple_Grandin_(film)" title="Temple Grandin (film)">Temple Grandin</a></i> (2010), Fox's drama-thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Touch_(TV_series)" title="Touch (TV series)">Touch</a></i> (2013), and <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>' action procedural drama <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS:_Los_Angeles" title="NCIS: Los Angeles">NCIS: Los Angeles</a></i> (2013).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, he was cast in the leading role of Tony Bravo on the <a href="/wiki/El_Rey_Network" title="El Rey Network">El Rey Network</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Matador_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Matador (U.S. TV series)">Matador</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The series premiered on July 15, 2014 and ran for one season of 13 episodes, ending on October 7, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> That same year, he co-starred as Vinnie in the sports comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Balls_Out_(2014_film)" title="Balls Out (2014 film)">Balls Out</a></i>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a> on April 19, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Luna next recurred as Miguel Gilb, a former lover of <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Kitsch" title="Taylor Kitsch">Taylor Kitsch</a>'s character, on the <a href="/wiki/True_Detective_(season_2)" title="True Detective (season 2)">second season</a> of the HBO anthology drama series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_Detective_(TV_series)" title="True Detective (TV series)">True Detective</a></i>, appearing in 3 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He then co-starred in the <a href="/wiki/Julianne_Moore" title="Julianne Moore">Julianne Moore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Page" title="Ellen Page">Ellen Page</a>-led drama film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freeheld_(2015_film)" title="Freeheld (2015 film)">Freeheld</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sollett" title="Peter Sollett">Peter Sollett</a>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a> on September 13, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Luna subsequently had a supporting role in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Gravy_(film)" title="Gravy (film)">Gravy</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/James_Roday" title="James Roday">James Roday</a>, which was released in the United States on October 2, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Luna was cast as Paco Contreras, a detective searching for a serial killer on the Sunset Strip, on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> crime drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Wicked_City_(TV_series)" title="Wicked City (TV series)">Wicked City</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The series premiered on October 27, 2015, but was cancelled following its third episode; the remaining episodes were later released through <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following year, he starred as <a href="/wiki/Border_guard" title="Border guard">border patrol agent</a> Lance Flores in the thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/Transpecos_(film)" title="Transpecos (film)">Transpecos</a></i>, opposite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clifton_Collins,_Jr." title="Clifton Collins, Jr.">Clifton Collins, Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Simmons" title="Johnny Simmons">Johnny Simmons</a>. The film had its world premiere at <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> on March 13, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Luna next appeared as motorcycle racer <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Hasha" title="Eddie Hasha">Eddie Hasha</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Harley_and_the_Davidsons" title="Harley and the Davidsons">Harley and the Davidsons</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In July of the same year, it was announced that he would join the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Agents_of_S.H.I.E.L.D." title="Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.">Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Agents_of_S.H.I.E.L.D._(season_4)" title="Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. (season 4)">season 4</a> as <a href="/wiki/Ghost_Rider_(comics)" title="Ghost Rider (comics)">Robbie Reyes / Ghost Rider</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Luna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Luna has been married to <a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanian</a> actress Smaranda Luna (née Ciceu) since February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSL_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-PSL-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The couple resides in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSL_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-PSL-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
