<p><b>Louis Gabriel Basso III</b> (born December 11, 1994) is an American actor, known for his roles in the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_C_(TV_series)" title="The Big C (TV series)">The Big C</a></i>, the 2011 science fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/Super_8_(film)" title="Super 8 (film)">Super 8</a></i>, and the 2013 comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kings_of_Summer" title="The Kings of Summer">The Kings of Summer</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Basso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Louis Gabriel Basso III was born in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, the son of Marcia and Louis J. Basso.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVGuide_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TVGuide-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was homeschooled as a child along with his two sisters (one is actress <a href="/wiki/Annalise_Basso" title="Annalise Basso">Annalise Basso</a>). His family attended Grace Doctrine Church in St. Charles, Missouri. Basso initially wanted to become a professional <a href="/wiki/Football_player" title="Football player">football player</a> before he found acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gabriel_Basso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Basso booked minor roles in two feature films in early 2007, while living in St. Louis: <i><a href="/wiki/Meet_Bill" title="Meet Bill">Meet Bill</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Eckhart" title="Aaron Eckhart">Aaron Eckhart</a>; and <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_Upside_Down" title="Alice Upside Down">Alice Upside Down</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Alyson_Stoner" title="Alyson Stoner">Alyson Stoner</a>. Basso's older sister Alexandria also had a role in the latter. He later moved with his mother and sisters to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He found representation his first week there, securing a lead role in the <a href="/wiki/Dailymotion" title="Dailymotion">Dailymotion Kids</a> web series <i>Ghost Town</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tribune_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tribune-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Basso has had several television guest roles in popular series such as <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/ICarly" title="ICarly">iCarly</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Middle_(TV_series)" title="The Middle (TV series)">The Middle</a></i>. From 2010 until 2013, he maintained a regular role in the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_C_(TV_series)" title="The Big C (TV series)">The Big C</a></i>, portraying Adam Jamison, the son of the lead character played by <a href="/wiki/Laura_Linney" title="Laura Linney">Laura Linney</a>. He then starred as Hal Mitchell in the independent film <i><a href="/wiki/Alabama_Moon" title="Alabama Moon">Alabama Moon</a></i> (2009), starring <a href="/wiki/John_Goodman" title="John Goodman">John Goodman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clint_Howard" title="Clint Howard">Clint Howard</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Tim_McCanlies" title="Tim McCanlies">Tim McCanlies</a>, and based on the best-selling novel by <a href="/wiki/Watt_Key" title="Watt Key">Watt Key</a>.</p>
<p>Basso appeared in the <a href="/wiki/J._J._Abrams" title="J. J. Abrams">J. J. Abrams</a> science fiction adventure film <i><a href="/wiki/Super_8_(film)" title="Super 8 (film)">Super 8</a></i> (2011), for which he was submitted for consideration for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="BAFTA Award for Best Supporting Actor">BAFTA Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Vogt-Roberts" title="Jordan Vogt-Roberts">Jordan Vogt-Roberts</a>' comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kings_of_Summer" title="The Kings of Summer">The Kings of Summer</a></i> (2013), which had its premiere at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2013 Sundance Film Festival">2013 Sundance Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He then starred in <a href="/wiki/Kyle_Newman" title="Kyle Newman">Kyle Newman</a>'s action comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Barely_Lethal" title="Barely Lethal">Barely Lethal</a></i> (2015), with <a href="/wiki/Hailee_Steinfeld" title="Hailee Steinfeld">Hailee Steinfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dove_Cameron" title="Dove Cameron">Dove Cameron</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On April 1, 2014, it was reported that Basso had been cast in the courtroom drama film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Whole_Truth_(2015_film)" title="The Whole Truth (2015 film)">The Whole Truth</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Hunt" title="Courtney Hunt">Courtney Hunt</a> and stars opposite <a href="/wiki/Keanu_Reeves" title="Keanu Reeves">Keanu Reeves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9e_Zellweger" title="Renée Zellweger">Renée Zellweger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In September 2014, he joined the cast of <a href="/wiki/Meg_Ryan" title="Meg Ryan">Meg Ryan</a>'s directorial debut film <i><a href="/wiki/Ithaca_(film)" title="Ithaca (film)">Ithaca</a></i>, starring alongside <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
