<p><b>Epsilon Cancri</b> (ε Cnc, ε Cancri) is a <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Cancer_(constellation)" title="Cancer (constellation)">Cancer</a>. ε Cancri is one of the brighter members of the <a href="/wiki/Beehive_Cluster" title="Beehive Cluster">Beehive Cluster</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Bayer_designation" title="Bayer designation">Bayer designation</a> ε Cancri originally referred to the entire cluster.</p>
<p>ε Cancri is a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">class</a> A5III<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Abt1995_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Abt1995-3">[3]</a></sup> white <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">A-type</a> <a href="/wiki/Giant_star" title="Giant star">giant</a> with an <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">apparent magnitude</a> of +6.29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XHIP_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-XHIP-2">[2]</a></sup> It is approximately 590 light years away from Earth based on parallax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vanLeeuwen2007_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-vanLeeuwen2007-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is a spectroscopic binary, with an orbit of 35 days and eccentricity of 0.32.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pourbaix_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pourbaix-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
