<p>The <b>Ilyushin Il-103</b> is a single-engine, low-wing training aircraft developed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Design_Bureau" title="Ilyushin Design Bureau">Ilyushin Design Bureau</a> starting in 1990 in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. The aircraft is now produced in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was the first Russian aircraft to achieve <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> certification for sales in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</p>
<p>Produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RSK-MiG" title="RSK-MiG">RSK-MiG</a>, it has been adopted by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Air_Force" title="Republic of Korea Air Force">Republic of Korea Air Force</a> as a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Primary_trainer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Primary trainer (page does not exist)">primary trainer</a>, where it is known as the T-103.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications_.28Il-103.29">Specifications (Il-103)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilyushin_Il-103&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specifications (Il-103)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p style="font-size: 90%; margin:0;"><i>Data from</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Taylor96_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Taylor96-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janes2004-2005_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Janes2004-2005-6">[6]</a></sup> Type Certificate no A45SE FAA received in December 1998, Type certificate No. 89-103 THE AR IAC received in February 1996. Type certificate No. A45SE FAA received in December 1998</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Crew:</b> two, stick controls</li>
<li><b>Capacity:</b> three places in back, total seating=five</li>
<li><b>Length:</b> 8 m (26 ft 3 in)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan:</b> 10.56 m (34 ft 7.75 in)</li>
<li><b>Height:</b> 3.135 m (10 ft 3.5 in)</li>
<li><b>Wing area:</b> 14.71 m<sup>2</sup> (158.34 ft<sup>2</sup>)</li>
<li><b>Empty weight:</b> 900 kg (1984 lb)</li>
<li><b>Gross weight:</b> 1310 kg (2888 lb)</li>
<li><b>Powerplant:</b> 1 × <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Continental_IO-360ES&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Continental IO-360ES (page does not exist)">Continental IO-360ES</a>- Fuel Injected - piston engine with one <a href="/wiki/Hartzell_Propeller" title="Hartzell Propeller">Hartzell</a> BHC-C2YF-1BF/F8459A-8R two-blade variable pitch (hydraulic) metal propeller, 156 kW (210 hp)</li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Performance</b></span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Range:</b> <i>(at cruising speed, pilot and 270 kg (595 lb) payload and 30 min reserves)</i>: 800 km (497 miles)</li>
<li><b>Service ceiling:</b> <i>(max certified altitude):</i> 3000 m (9840 ft)</li>
<li><b>Rate of climb:</b> <i>(at ISA mean sea level)</i>: 3.167 m/s ( ft/min)</li>
</ul>
