<p><b>Kenryaku</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">建暦</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_era_name" title="Japanese era name">Japanese era name</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">年号,</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>nengō,</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "year name")</span> after <i><a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dgen_(Kamakura_period)" title="Jōgen (Kamakura period)">Jōgen</a></i> and before <i><a href="/wiki/Kempo_(era)" title="Kempo (era)">Kempo</a>.</i> This period spanned the years from March 1211 through December 1213.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The reigning emperor was <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Juntoku" title="Emperor Juntoku">Juntoku</a><i>-tennō</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">順徳天皇</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Change_of_era">Change of era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenryaku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Change of era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>1211</b> <i>Kenryaku gannen</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">建暦元年</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>: The new era name was created because the previous era ended and a new one commenced in <i>Jōgen</i> 2, on the 9th day of the 3rd month of 1211.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_of_the_Kenryaku_era">Events of the <i>Kenryaku</i> era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenryaku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events of the Kenryaku era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</ul>

</div>
<ul>
<li><b>1211</b> (<i>Kenryaku 1, 1st month</i>): The Buddhist priest <a href="/wiki/H%C5%8Dnen" title="Hōnen">Hōnen</a> returned to Kyoto from a period of exile. He was the founder and guiding force behind the early development of the <a href="/wiki/Senny%C5%AB-ji" title="Sennyū-ji">Sennyū-ji</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">泉涌寺,</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sennyū-ji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> temple-complex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-t230_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-t230-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>January 12, 1212</b> (<i>Kenryaku 2, 20th day of the 12th month</i>): The Buddhist priest <a href="/wiki/H%C5%8Dnen" title="Hōnen">Hōnen</a> died at age 80, mere days after drafted a brief, written summary of his life teachings. This last written document is known as the <a href="/wiki/One-Sheet_Document" title="One-Sheet Document">One-Sheet Document</a> (<i>ichimai-kishomon</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1212</b> (<i>Kenryaku 2, 16th day of the 1st month</i>): The former-Senior High Priest Jien (1155–1225) was appointed Tendai Abbot by Imperial Mandate. He would administer Mt. Hiei for one year before yielding the position on the 11th day of the 1st month of 1213.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
