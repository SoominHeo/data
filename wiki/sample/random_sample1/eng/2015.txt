<p><b>Genchū</b> (元中) was a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_era_name" title="Japanese era name">Japanese era</a> of the Southern Court during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanboku-cho" title="Nanboku-cho">Era of Northern and Southern Courts</a> lasting from April 1384 to October 1392.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Reigning Emperors were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Kameyama_of_Japan" title="Emperor Go-Kameyama of Japan">Go-Kameyama</a> in the south and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Komatsu_of_Japan" title="Emperor Go-Komatsu of Japan">Go-Komatsu</a> in the north.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nanboku-ch.C5.8D_overview">Nanboku-chō overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gench%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nanboku-chō overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nanbokucho-capitals.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="804" data-file-width="950" height="118" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Nanbokucho-capitals.svg/140px-Nanbokucho-capitals.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Nanbokucho-capitals.svg/210px-Nanbokucho-capitals.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Nanbokucho-capitals.svg/280px-Nanbokucho-capitals.svg.png 2x" width="140"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nanbokucho-capitals.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Imperial seats during the <i>Nanboku-chō</i> period were in relatively close proximity, but geographically distinct. They were conventionally identified as:
<div>
<ul>
<li>Northern capital : <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a></li>
<li>Southern capital : <a href="/wiki/Yoshino,_Nara" title="Yoshino, Nara">Yoshino</a>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>, an Imperial decree dated March 3, 1911 established that the legitimate reigning monarchs of this period were the direct descendants of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Daigo" title="Emperor Go-Daigo">Emperor Go-Daigo</a> through <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Murakami" title="Emperor Go-Murakami">Emperor Go-Murakami</a>, whose Southern Court <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南朝</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>nanchō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> had been established in exile in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoshino_District" title="Yoshino District">Yoshino</a>, near Nara.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concise_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-concise-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until the end of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, the militarily superior pretender-Emperors supported by the <a href="/wiki/Ashikaga_shogunate" title="Ashikaga shogunate">Ashikaga shogunate</a> had been mistakenly incorporated in Imperial chronologies despite the undisputed fact that the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Regalia_of_Japan" title="Imperial Regalia of Japan">Imperial Regalia</a> were not in their possession.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concise_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-concise-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>This illegitimate <a href="/wiki/Northern_Court" title="Northern Court">Northern Court</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">北朝</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>hokuchō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> had been established in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ashikaga_Takauji" title="Ashikaga Takauji">Ashikaga Takauji</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concise_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-concise-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
