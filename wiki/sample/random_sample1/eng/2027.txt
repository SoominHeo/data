<p><b>Gyeomji of Geumgwan Gaya</b> (r. 492–521)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was the ninth ruler of <a href="/wiki/Geumgwan_Gaya" title="Geumgwan Gaya">Geumgwan Gaya</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Gaya_confederacy" title="Gaya confederacy">Gaya</a> state of ancient <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Jilji_of_Geumgwan_Gaya" title="Jilji of Geumgwan Gaya">King Jilji</a> and Queen Bangwon. He married Queen Suk, who was the daughter of the general (<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gakgan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gakgan (page does not exist)">gakgan</a></i>) Chulchung. She gave birth to <a href="/wiki/Guhyeong_of_Geumgwan_Gaya" title="Guhyeong of Geumgwan Gaya">Guhyeong</a>, who would be the kingdom's final ruler.</p>
