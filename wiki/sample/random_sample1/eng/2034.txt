<p>A <b>patrol boat</b> is a relatively small <a href="/wiki/Naval_ship" title="Naval ship">naval vessel</a> generally designed for coastal defence duties. There have been many designs for patrol boats. They may be operated by a nation's <a href="/wiki/Navy" title="Navy">navy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coast_guard" title="Coast guard">coast guard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a> force or <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a> and may be intended for marine (<a href="/wiki/Maritime_geography" title="Maritime geography">blue water</a>) and/or <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuarine</a> or river ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brown_water" title="Brown water">brown water</a>") environments. They are commonly found engaged in various border protection roles, including anti-<a href="/wiki/Smuggling" title="Smuggling">smuggling</a>, anti-<a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fisheries" title="Fisheries">fisheries</a> patrols, and <a href="/wiki/Immigration_law" title="Immigration law">immigration law</a> <a href="/wiki/Enforcement" title="Enforcement">enforcement</a>. They are also often called upon to participate in <a href="/wiki/Rescue" title="Rescue">rescue</a> operations. Vessels of this type include the original <a href="/wiki/Yacht" title="Yacht">yacht</a> (from Dutch/Low German <i>jacht</i> meaning hunting or hunt), a light, fast-sailing vessel used by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Navy" title="Royal Netherlands Navy">Dutch navy</a> to pursue pirates and other transgressors around and into shallow waters.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patrol_boat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>They may be broadly classified as inshore patrol vessels (IPVs) and offshore patrol vessels (OPVs). They are warships typically smaller in size than a <a href="/wiki/Corvette" title="Corvette">corvette</a> and can include <a href="/wiki/Fast_attack_craft" title="Fast attack craft">fast attack craft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_boat" title="Torpedo boat">torpedo boats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Missile_boat" title="Missile boat">missile boats</a>, although some are as large as a <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigate</a>. The offshore patrol vessels are usually the smallest ship in a navy's fleet that are large and seaworthy enough to patrol off-shore in the open <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a>. In larger militaries, such as in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_armed_forces" title="United States armed forces">United States military</a>, offshore patrol vessels usually serve in the coast guard, but many smaller nations navies operate these type of ships.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patrol_boat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During both World Wars in order to rapidly build up numbers, all sides created auxiliary patrol boats by arming <a href="/wiki/Motorboat" title="Motorboat">motorboats</a> and seagoing <a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">fishing trawlers</a> with machine guns and <a href="/wiki/Obsolescence" title="Obsolescence">obsolescent</a> naval weapons. Some modern patrol vessels are still based on fishing and leisure boats. Seagoing patrol boats are typically around 30 m (100 ft) in length and usually carry a single medium caliber <a href="/wiki/Naval_artillery" title="Naval artillery">artillery gun</a> as main armament, and a variety of lighter secondary armament such as machine guns or a <a href="/wiki/Close-in_weapon_system" title="Close-in weapon system">close-in weapon system</a>. Depending on role, vessels in this class may also have more sophisticated sensors and <a href="/wiki/Fire-control_system" title="Fire-control system">fire control</a> systems that would enable them to carry <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surface-to-air_missile" title="Surface-to-air missile">surface-to-air missiles</a>.</p>
<p>Most modern designs are powered by <a href="/wiki/Gas_turbine" title="Gas turbine">gas turbine</a> arrangements such as <a href="/wiki/Combined_diesel_and_gas" title="Combined diesel and gas">CODAG</a>, and speeds are generally in the 25–30 knots (46–56 km/h; 29–35 mph) range. They are primarily used for patrol in a country's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zone</a>. Common tasks are <a href="/wiki/Fisheries_management" title="Fisheries management">fisheries inspection</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smuggling" title="Smuggling">anti-smuggling</a> (usually <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prohibition_(drugs)" title="Prohibition (drugs)">anti-narcotics</a>) duties, <a href="/wiki/Illegal_immigration" title="Illegal immigration">illegal immigration</a> patrols, anti-<a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a> patrols and <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a> (law enforcement-type of work). The largest OPVs might also have a <a href="/wiki/Flight_deck" title="Flight deck">flight deck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> embarked. In times of crisis or war, these vessels are expected to support the larger vessels in the navy.</p>

<p>Their small size and relatively low cost make them one of the most common type of warship in the world. Almost all navies operate at least a few offshore patrol vessels, especially those with only "<a href="/wiki/Green-water_navy" title="Green-water navy">green water</a>" capabilities. They are useful in smaller seas such as the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> as well as in open oceans. Similar vessels for exclusively military duties include <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_boat" title="Torpedo boat">torpedo boats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Missile_boat" title="Missile boat">missile boats</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> operated the <a href="/wiki/Pegasus-class_hydrofoil" title="Pegasus-class hydrofoil"><i>Pegasus</i> class</a> of armed hydrofoils for years, in a patrol boat role. The <a href="/wiki/Patrol_Boat,_River" title="Patrol Boat, River">River Patrol Boat</a> (PBR, sometimes called "Riverine" and "Pibber") is a U.S. design of small patrol boat type designed to patrol waters of large rivers.</p>
