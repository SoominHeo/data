<p><i><b>Qing Shi Huangfei</b></i>, also known as <i><b>The Glamorous Imperial Concubine</b></i> or <i><b>Introduction of the Princess</b></i>, is a 2011 Chinese television series starring and produced by <a href="/wiki/Ruby_Lin" title="Ruby Lin">Ruby Lin</a>. Based on a novel of the same title by Murong Yinr (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">慕容湮儿</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">慕容湮兒</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Mùróng Yīnr / Mùróng Yīner</span></i>), it was Lin's producing debut. Multimedia released with English subtitles.</p>
<p>Filming for the series started on 19 January 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Hengdian_World_Studios" title="Hengdian World Studios">Hengdian</a> and wrapped up on 6 April 2011. It was first aired in China on <a href="/wiki/Hunan_Broadcasting_System" title="Hunan Broadcasting System">HBS</a> on 30 September 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Storyline">Storyline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Glamorous_Imperial_Concubine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Storyline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ma Fuya (<a href="/wiki/Ruby_Lin" title="Ruby Lin">Ruby Lin</a>) is a princess of the <a href="/wiki/Chu_(Ten_Kingdoms)" title="Chu (Ten Kingdoms)">Chu</a> state in 10th-century <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">imperial China</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Five_Dynasties_and_Ten_Kingdoms_period" title="Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period">Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period</a>. In this time of warring kingdoms and political upheaval, Fuya's uncle usurps the throne, and brutally kills her father. The fate of her mother and younger brother is revealed later in the drama. Fuya bravely takes on the quest of avenging her father and restoring her kingdom, no matter the cost. Loved by both the Prince of <a href="/wiki/Later_Shu" title="Later Shu">Later Shu</a>, Meng Qiyou (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_Yan" title="Kevin Yan">Kevin Yan</a>) and the Prince of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Han" title="Northern Han">Northern Han</a>, Liu Liancheng (<a href="/wiki/Wallace_Huo" title="Wallace Huo">Wallace Huo</a>), the exiled princess becomes entrenched in the Shu court succession struggle, and the battle between their contending states. Rising to power amid war, romance, and betrayal, what fate awaits Fuya, her people, and her love?</p>

<tr>
<td><a href="/wiki/Ruby_Lin" title="Ruby Lin">Ruby Lin</a></td>
<td>Ma Fu Ya<br/>
(马馥雅)</td>
<td>Beautiful, smart, resourceful, is compassionate to friends, but does not concede to hostility. She is the exiled Princess of the Kingdom of Chu, as her father's throne was usurped by her uncle, and she was forced to flee as he sent assassins after her. Firstly marrying to Meng Qiyou, the King of Shu, who gives her the title, Yi Fei. She was later forced to marry Lian Cheng, the King of Northern Han, who bestows her the title Di Huang Fei. She never consummates marriage to Lian Cheng and stays faithful to Qiyou,King of Shu.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_Yan" title="Kevin Yan">Kevin Yan</a></td>
<td>Meng Qi You<br/>
(孟祈佑)</td>
<td>The Prince of Shu, entangled in a power struggle between his father the King and mother the Queen. He was raised believing that his mother never loved him, preferring his youngest brother the Crown Prince, and that his father had wanted him to ascend to his throne. He later turned the throne over to his younger half-brother, Qiyun.</td>
