<p>A <b>race track</b> (or 'racetrack', 'racing track' or 'racing circuit') is a facility built for <a href="/wiki/Racing" title="Racing">racing</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vehicles" title="Vehicles">vehicles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a>, or animals (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Horse_racing" title="Horse racing">horse racing</a> or <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_racing" title="Greyhound racing">greyhound racing</a>). A race track also may feature <a href="/wiki/Grandstand" title="Grandstand">grandstands</a> or concourses. Racetracks are also used in the study of <a href="/wiki/Animal_locomotion" title="Animal locomotion">animal locomotion</a>. Some motorsport tracks are called <b>speedways</b>.</p>
<p>A <b>racetrack</b> is a permanent facility or building. <b>Racecourse</b> is an alternate term for a <a href="/wiki/Horse_racing" title="Horse racing">horse racing</a> track, found in countries such as the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. Race tracks built for bicycles are known as <b><a href="/wiki/Velodrome" title="Velodrome">velodromes</a></b>. <b>Circuit</b> is a common alternate term for racetrack, given the circuit configuration of most race tracks, allowing races to occur over several laps.</p>
<p>A <b>race course</b>, as opposed to a <i>racecourse</i>, is a term for non-permanent tracks for sports, particularly <a href="/wiki/Road_running" title="Road running">road running</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_sports" title="Water sports">water sports</a>, <a href="/wiki/Road_racing" title="Road racing">road racing</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Rallying" title="Rallying">rallying</a>. Many sports usually held on racetracks also can occur on temporary tracks, such as the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Race_track&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is some evidence of racetracks being developed in several ancient civilizations. The most developed ancient racetracks were the <a href="/wiki/Hippodrome" title="Hippodrome">hippodromes</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Ancient Greeks</a> and the circuses of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>. Both of these structures were designed for horse and <a href="/wiki/Chariot" title="Chariot">chariot</a> racing. The stadium of the <a href="/wiki/Circus_Maximus" title="Circus Maximus">Circus Maximus</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Rome</a> could hold 200,000 spectators.</p>
<p>Racing facilities existed during the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, and there are records of a public racecourse being opened at <a href="/wiki/Newmarket_Racecourse" title="Newmarket Racecourse">Newmarket</a> in London in 1174. In 1780 the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Derby" title="Earl of Derby">Earl of Derby</a> created a horse-racing course on his estate at Epsom; the <a href="/wiki/Epsom_Derby" title="Epsom Derby">English Derby</a> continues to be held there today. Racecourses in the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> are based on grass, known as turf tracks. In the United States, the race tracks are dirt.</p>

<p>With the advent of the automobile in the late nineteenth/early twentieth century, racetracks were designed to suit the nature of powered machines. The earliest tracks were modified horse racing courses. Racing automobiles in such facilities began in September 1896, at Narragansett Park in <a href="/wiki/Cranston,_Rhode_Island" title="Cranston, Rhode Island">Cranston, Rhode Island</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway</a> was opened in August 1909.</p>
<p>Beginning in the early 1900s, motorcycle races were run on high, banked, wooden race tracks called <a href="/wiki/Board_track_racing" title="Board track racing">board tracks</a>. During the 1920s, many of the races on the <a href="/wiki/American_Automobile_Association" title="American Automobile Association">AAA</a> Champttrhghionship circuit were run on such board tracks. Modern racetracks are designed with spectator safety being paramount, following incidents of spectator and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Track_marshal" title="Track marshal">track marshals</a> fatalities. These often involve run off areas, barriers, and high fencing.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Configurations">Configurations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Race_track&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Configurations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1084" data-file-width="1784" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg/220px-Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg/330px-Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg/440px-Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hongkongjockeyclub.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Horseracing track, <a href="/wiki/Happy_Valley_Racecourse" title="Happy Valley Racecourse">Happy Valley Racecourse</a> in Hong Kong, showing grandstands</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Some racetracks offer little in the way of permanent infrastructure other than the track; others incorporate spectator facilities such as grandstands, <a href="/wiki/Hospitality" title="Hospitality">hospitality</a> or facilities for competitors, such as pit lanes and garages, paddocks and stables. Several racetracks are incorporated into larger venues or complexes, incorporating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golf_courses" title="Golf courses">golf courses</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Museums" title="Museums">museums</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotels" title="Hotels">hotels</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_centre" title="Conference centre">conference centres</a>. Some racetracks are small enough to be contained indoors, for sports such as <a href="/wiki/Motocross" title="Motocross">motocross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">athletics</a>.</p>
<p>Many racetracks are multi-use, allowing different types of sport on the same track, or incorporating many tracks in one venue. Commonly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Running_track" title="Running track">running tracks</a> are incorporated within general use or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a> stadiums, either permanently visible or covered by stands or pitches.</p>
<p>Many horse and motorsport tracks are configurable, allowing different routes or sections. Some venues contain smaller tracks inside larger ones, with access tunnels and bridges for spectators. Some racetracks incorporate a short course and a longer course which uses part of the shorter one, usually the main straight, such as <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Le_Mans" title="Le Mans">Le Mans</a> road race venue is centred on a smaller permanent circuit within its complex.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Motorsport">Motorsport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Race_track&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Motorsport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Racetracks are primarily designed for competition through speed, featuring defined start-finish lines or posts, and sometimes even a series of defined timing points that divide the track into time sectors. Some sports merely measure endurance, or how long a competitor can race. Racetracks can host individual or team sports. Racetracks can feature <a href="/wiki/Rolling_start" title="Rolling start">rolling starts</a>, or fixed starts, with associated equipment (starting blocks, cages, wheel traps etc.) They invariably feature a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pit_lane" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a>, and usually timing equipment.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Track_layout">Track layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Race_track&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Track layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some racetracks are of an <a href="/wiki/Oval" title="Oval">oval</a> shape, often banked<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, which allows almost universal spectator views or high speed racing (cycling, stock cars), but are often criticised for lack of excitement; these predominate in North America. A famous one is <a href="/wiki/Nard%C3%B2_Ring" title="Nardò Ring">Nardò</a> where high-speed manufacturer testing often takes place, and the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway</a>. Some oval tracks are variations on an oval shape, for practical reasons or to introduce varying difficulties such as <a href="/wiki/Talladega_Superspeedway" title="Talladega Superspeedway">Talladega</a> (a tri-oval). Most racetracks have meandering circuits with many curves, <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicanes</a> and changes in height, to allow for a challenge in skill to the competitors, notably <a href="/wiki/Motocross" title="Motocross">motocross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">touring car racing</a> - these tend to predominate throughout most of the world, but especially in Europe.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nard%C3%B2_ring.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1000" data-file-width="1000" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Nard%C3%B2_ring.jpg/220px-Nard%C3%B2_ring.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Nard%C3%B2_ring.jpg/330px-Nard%C3%B2_ring.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Nard%C3%B2_ring.jpg/440px-Nard%C3%B2_ring.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road_circuits">Road circuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Race_track&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Road circuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">Street circuit</a></div>
<p>Flatter meandering motorsport courses are sometimes called 'road circuits', originating in the fact that the earliest road racing circuits were simply closed-off public roads. True road circuits are still in use, e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian GP</a> has been run in <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide</a> and continues to be in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne</a> on regular city streets. The most famous of these are the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco GP</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> circuit in Belgium. Some racetracks are specifically configured in a long straight, namely <a href="/wiki/Drag_racing" title="Drag racing">drag racing</a>. Some races will be held only over the straight portion of a track (some horse racing and sprint athletics).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Converted_airfields">Converted airfields</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Race_track&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Converted airfields">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The boom in motorsport flared after WW2, when many airfields particularly in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> were left without further use. The most famous British track - <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> - is one after being a WW2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_a_airfield" title="Class a airfield">Class A Airfield</a>, as are <a href="/wiki/Castle_Combe_Circuit" title="Castle Combe Circuit">Castle Combe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_Circuit" title="Goodwood Circuit">Goodwood Circuit</a>'s. The long runways were perfect for drag strips such as at <a href="/wiki/Santa_Pod_Raceway" title="Santa Pod Raceway">Santa Pod Raceway</a>, and circuit layout usually creatively combined parts of the runways and the surrounding perimeter taxiway. This type of track also appears on the popular motoring show <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(2002_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (2002 TV series)">Top Gear</a></i>, which is filmed at <a href="/wiki/Dunsfold_Aerodrome" title="Dunsfold Aerodrome">Dunsfold Aerodrome</a>, in Surrey, England.</p>
