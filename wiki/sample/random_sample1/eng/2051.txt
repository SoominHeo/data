<p><b>Gye of Baekje</b> (died 346, r. 344–346) was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_monarchs_of_Korea" title="List of monarchs of Korea">twelfth king</a> of <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Three Kingdoms of Korea</a>.</p>
<p>He was the eldest son of the 10th king <a href="/wiki/Bunseo_of_Baekje" title="Bunseo of Baekje">Bunseo</a>, who was assassinated. The <a href="/wiki/Samguk_Sagi" title="Samguk Sagi">Samguk Sagi</a> records that <a href="/wiki/Biryu_of_Baekje" title="Biryu of Baekje">Biryu</a> (younger brother of the 7th king <a href="/wiki/Saban_of_Baekje" title="Saban of Baekje">Saban</a>) became king because of Gye's young age. Gye became king upon Biryu's death, and is said to have been courageous and skilled in archery and horseriding.</p>
<p>His rule indicated the continued the rivalry between two royal lines, that of the 5th king <a href="/wiki/Chogo_of_Baekje" title="Chogo of Baekje">Chogo</a> and that of the 8th king <a href="/wiki/Goi_of_Baekje" title="Goi of Baekje">Goi</a>, from whom Gye descended. The Goi line ended with Gye's 2-year reign, as he was succeeded by Biryu's son <a href="/wiki/Geunchogo_of_Baekje" title="Geunchogo of Baekje">Geunchogo</a>.</p>
