<p>The <b>Go Ahead Eagles</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈɡoː əˈɦɛt ˈiɡəls]</a></span>) are a Dutch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Deventer" title="Deventer">Deventer</a>, as of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Go_Ahead_Eagles&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> playing in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>. The club's home stadium is <a href="/wiki/De_Adelaarshorst" title="De Adelaarshorst">De Adelaarshorst</a>. The club won the national championship in 1917, 1922, 1930 and 1933.</p>
<p>They have produced a number of notable players, such as <a href="/wiki/Raimond_van_der_Gouw" title="Raimond van der Gouw">Raimond van der Gouw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Eijkelkamp" title="René Eijkelkamp">René Eijkelkamp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Overmars" title="Marc Overmars">Marc Overmars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Bosvelt" title="Paul Bosvelt">Paul Bosvelt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Kromkamp" title="Jan Kromkamp">Jan Kromkamp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Sikora" title="Victor Sikora">Victor Sikora</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bert_van_Marwijk" title="Bert van Marwijk">Bert van Marwijk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demy_de_Zeeuw" title="Demy de Zeeuw">Demy de Zeeuw</a>, whilst providing <a href="/wiki/Henk_ten_Cate" title="Henk ten Cate">Henk ten Cate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leo_Beenhakker" title="Leo Beenhakker">Leo Beenhakker</a> with their first tastes of management.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Ahead_Eagles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in 1902 as <i>Be Quick</i>, by the brothers 'Hollander'. The name was soon changed to Go Ahead at the request of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Football_Association" title="Royal Dutch Football Association">Dutch Football Association</a>. The suffix Eagles was added in 1971, following a suggestion from the then coach, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hughes" title="Barry Hughes">Barry Hughes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The eagle is the charge in the coat of arms of the city of Deventer.</p>
<p>The Go Ahead Eagles were famously the only side to defeat <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> in any competition during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1971-72_Eredivisie" title="1971-72 Eredivisie">1971–72</a> season, when they beat Ajax 3–2 at <a href="/wiki/De_Adelaarshorst" title="De Adelaarshorst">De Adelaarshorst</a> in the Eredivisie. They have played in the Europacup II once, in the 1965–66 season. They lost in the first round to <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_seasons">Recent seasons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Ahead_Eagles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent seasons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After almost two decades in the second division, Go Ahead won promotion to the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Eredivisie" title="2013–14 Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> at the end of the 2012–13 season, winning the promotion play-offs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The club remained in the top division for the 2014–15 season, having finished thirteenth.</p>
<p>Go Ahead Eagles would compete in Europe in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Europa League">2015–16 UEFA Europa League</a> due to the Netherlands' first place in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Respect_Fair_Play_ranking" title="UEFA Respect Fair Play ranking">UEFA Respect Fair Play ranking</a>. National Fair Play winner <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> withdrew due to financial difficulties, making place for Go Ahead Eagles. However, they also suffered relegation after losing 2–0 on aggregate to <a href="/wiki/De_Graafschap" title="De Graafschap">De Graafschap</a> in the May 2015 promotion/relegation play-offs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In July 2015 they were beaten 2–5 on aggregate by Hungarian side <a href="/wiki/Ferencv%C3%A1rosi_TC" title="Ferencvárosi TC">Ferencváros</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Europa_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round" title="2015–16 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round">first Europa League qualifying round</a> with the home leg played in Emmen due to the Adelaarshorst being renovated and the away leg without spectators because the Hungarians were serving a ban by UEFA.</p>
<p>The club bounced back to the Eredivisie on the first attempt in May 2016, after beating De Graafschap this time around (5–2 on aggregate) in the promotion/relegation play-offs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Ahead_Eagles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/IJsselderby" title="IJsselderby">IJsselderby</a></div>
<p><i>Go Ahead Eagles</i> biggest rivals are <a href="/wiki/PEC_Zwolle" title="PEC Zwolle">PEC Zwolle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Both clubs are located at the river <a href="/wiki/IJssel" title="IJssel">IJssel</a>, hence the name <b>IJssel-derby</b>. In the early years Go Ahead was the best club in the region but since the relegation to the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Dutch First Division</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eredivisie_1986%E2%80%9387" title="Eredivisie 1986–87">1987</a>, Zwolle has won most derbies.</p>
