<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Steve Blank</h1>

<p><b>Steve Blank</b> (born 1953) is a <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> serial-entrepreneur and <a href="/wiki/Academician" title="Academician">academician</a> who is based in <a href="/wiki/Pescadero,_California" title="Pescadero, California">Pescadero, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FC_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FC-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank is recognized for developing the Customer Development methodology, which launched the Lean Startup movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> Blank is also the co-founder of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E.piphany" title="E.piphany">E.piphany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IN_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-IN-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank has spent over thirty years in the high technology industry. He has founded or worked within eight <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startup companies</a>, four of which have gone public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HP_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-HP-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Google_TechTalks" title="Google TechTalks">Google Tech talk</a>, "The Secret History of Silicon Valley", offers a widely regarded insider's perspective on the emerging Silicon Valley's start-up innovation. Blank has published three books: <i>The Four Steps to the Epiphany</i>, <i>Not All Those Who Wander Are Lost</i> and <i>The Startup Owner's Manual</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WA_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-WA-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASGB_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASGB-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank teaches and writes about Customer Development and is a consulting associate professor of <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneurship" title="Entrepreneurship">entrepreneurship</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanford" title="Stanford">Stanford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCB_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-UCB-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SUEC_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-SUEC-9">[9]</a></sup> He currently lectures at the <a href="/wiki/Haas_School_of_Business" title="Haas School of Business">Haas School of Business</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_California_Berkeley" title="University of California Berkeley">University of California Berkeley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> and the <a href="/wiki/California_Institute_of_Technology" title="California Institute of Technology">California Institute of Technology</a> (Caltech). Together with the Entrepreneurship Center at the University of California San Francisco (UCSF), he created a version of Lean Launchpad for Life Sciences and Healthcare which he taught there in the fall 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blank was born to immigrant parents who ran a grocery in the Chelsea neighborhood in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup> He grew up with a sister who was 12 years older than him and both siblings were raised by his mother after his father left home when he was aged 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup> His mother and father had never been to college and wished for their son to graduate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBP_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-SBP-11">[11]</a></sup> He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a>, but disliked being in school and dropped out after one semester.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBP_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-SBP-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOUN_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOUN-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank hitchhiked to <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, where he found work at the <a href="/wiki/Miami_International_Airport" title="Miami International Airport">Miami International Airport</a>, loading <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racehorses" title="Racehorses">racehorses</a> onto <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBP_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-SBP-11">[11]</a></sup> At the airport, Blank developed an interest in <a href="/wiki/Avionics" title="Avionics">avionics</a>, which he nurtured until the early 1970s when he joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Force" title="Air Force">Air Force</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBP_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-SBP-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>His military career took him to <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, where he was managing a team of 15 <a href="/wiki/Electronics_technician" title="Electronics technician">electronics technicians</a> within a year and a half.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBP_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-SBP-11">[11]</a></sup> He was 20 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBP_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-SBP-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank left the military and moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palo_Alto" title="Palo Alto">Palo Alto</a>, a town in an area which would ultimately become known as <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBP_11-6"><a href="#cite_note-SBP-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Steve Blank arrived in Silicon Valley at the start of the business boom in 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASGB_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ASGB-7">[7]</a></sup> His first job within the region was with ESL, a startup that was a primary US intelligence company for <a href="/wiki/National_technical_means_of_verification" title="National technical means of verification">National Means of Technical Verification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOUN_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOUN-12">[12]</a></sup> The company helped the government understand the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviets" title="Soviets">Soviets</a>' technological and arms developments during the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOUN_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOUN-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his 34-year career, Blank founded or worked with a wide range of high-tech companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SE_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-SE-13">[13]</a></sup> Four of his companies went public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup> Some of his ventures include <a href="/wiki/Zilog" title="Zilog">Zilog</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MIPS_Computer" title="MIPS Computer">MIPS Computers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Convergent_Technologies" title="Convergent Technologies">Convergent Technologies</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ardent_Computer" title="Ardent Computer">Ardent</a>, <a href="/wiki/UMAX_Technologies" title="UMAX Technologies">SuperMac Technologies</a>, ESL and <a href="/wiki/Rocket_Science_Games" title="Rocket Science Games">Rocket Science Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCB_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-UCB-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SE_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-SE-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank has since served on the boards of the public entities <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macrovision" title="Macrovision">Macrovision</a>/Rovi and <a href="/wiki/Immersion_Corporation" title="Immersion Corporation">Immersion</a> as well as several private companies. He continues to invest in and advise Silicon Valley startups including <a href="/wiki/Udacity" title="Udacity">Udacity</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Votizen" title="Votizen">Votizen</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FC_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FC-1">[1]</a></sup> and venture capital firm <a href="/wiki/Engineering_Capital" title="Engineering Capital">Engineering Capital</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2015, Steve Blank began hosting a weekly radio show on Sirius XM radio called "Entrepreneurs are Everywhere." The show is aimed at helping startups and features interviews with successful entrepreneurs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="E.piphany">E.piphany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: E.piphany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E.piphany" title="E.piphany">E.piphany</a></div>
<p>Blank co-founded his 8th and last startup, the Customer Relationship Management provider <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E.piphany" title="E.piphany">E.piphany</a>, in 1996 and retired the day before its IPO in September 1999. In 2005, E.piphany was acquired by <a href="/wiki/SSA_Global_Technologies" title="SSA Global Technologies">SSA Global Technologies</a> for $329 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SL_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-SL-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VB_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-VB-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MA_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-MA-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The software package was designed to scrape customer information from databases and deposit the data into a web browser.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFG_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-SFG-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Notable members of the company include Roger Siboni and <a href="/wiki/Karen_Richardson" title="Karen Richardson">Karen Richardson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFG_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-SFG-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SF_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-SF-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Customer_Development_Methodology">Customer Development Methodology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Customer Development Methodology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blank created the <a href="/wiki/Customer_Development" title="Customer Development">Customer Development</a> methodology in the mid 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOUN_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOUN-12">[12]</a></sup> The concept details a scientific approach that can be applied by <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startups</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entrepreneurs" title="Entrepreneurs">entrepreneurs</a> to improve their products success by developing a better understanding of their consumers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOUN_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOUN-12">[12]</a></sup> Primary to the concept is a balanced relationship between developing a <a href="/wiki/Product_(business)" title="Product (business)">product</a> and understanding the <a href="/wiki/Customer" title="Customer">customer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CND_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-CND-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lean_Startup_Movement">Lean Startup Movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lean Startup Movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blank's Customer Development methodology is a cornerstone of the Lean Startup Movement, popularized by <a href="/wiki/Eric_Ries" title="Eric Ries">Eric Ries</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SLL_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-SLL-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WSLL_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-WSLL-23">[23]</a></sup> whom Blank states was “the best student I ever had.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup> The Lean Startup approach relies on <a href="/wiki/Validated_learning" title="Validated learning">validated learning</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_experimentation" title="Scientific experimentation">scientific experimentation</a>, and iterative product releases to shorten <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Product_development" title="Product development">product development cycles</a>, measure progress, and gain valuable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Customer_feedback" title="Customer feedback">customer feedback</a>. Blank and Ries developed the ideas beginning around 2004 when Blank was an investor and advisor to the company, <a href="/wiki/IMVU" title="IMVU">IMVU</a>, that Ries co-founded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ER_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ER-24">[24]</a></sup> It has attracted attention among <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entrepreneurs" title="Entrepreneurs">entrepreneurs</a> world-wide as a constructive way to try out ideas and gain customers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SL_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-SL-16">[16]</a></sup> Ries has integrated the Customer Development methodology into the Lean Startup practices and considers it to be one of the Lean Startup Movement's pillars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CND_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-CND-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STA_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-STA-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VLA_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-VLA-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blank's academic teaching largely focuses on the Customer Development methodology and the application of scientific methodologies to the startup process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IN_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-IN-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SL_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-SL-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SLL_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-SLL-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Four_Steps_to_the_Epiphany"><i>The Four Steps to the Epiphany</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Four Steps to the Epiphany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Blank published <i>The Four Steps to the Epiphany: Successful Strategies for Products that Win</i>, (K&amp;S Ranch Press) which details his approach to the Customer Development process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASGB_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-ASGB-7">[7]</a></sup> In the book, Blank outlines his views about how <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneurship" title="Entrepreneurship">entrepreneurship</a> is a practice that can be actively managed rather than an art which must be passively experienced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOUN_12-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOUN-12">[12]</a></sup> The book is viewed in entrepreneurship circles as the definitive source on the Customer Development methodology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOUN_12-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOUN-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Not_All_Who_Wander_Are_Lost"><i>Not All Who Wander Are Lost</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Not All Who Wander Are Lost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blank released a second book in 2010, <i>Not All Who Wander Are Lost</i> which relates stories from his life as an entrepreneur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASGB_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-ASGB-7">[7]</a></sup> The collection of material develops a narrative about how to live life amid the fast-paced world of Silicon Valley startups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMZ_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMZ-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Startup_Owner.27s_Manual"><i>The Startup Owner's Manual</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The Startup Owner's Manual">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Steve Blank and Bob Dorf co-authored <i>The Startup Owner's Manual</i>, which was released in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FS_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-FS-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FS2_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-FS2-29">[29]</a></sup> The 608-page reference manual details a scientific approach to entrepreneurship and emphasizes the importance of "rigorous and repeated testing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WA_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-WA-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-7"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup> The work draws on ideas from The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_Model" title="Business Model">Business Model</a> Generation and The Four Steps to the Epiphany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WA_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-WA-6">[6]</a></sup> According to Blank, the book was designed to be used as an "encyclopedia and a blueprint" for <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startups</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-RE-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blog">Blog</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Blog">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blank authors a personal blog that addresses issues of entrepreneurship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IN_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-IN-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2012, his blog was ranked one of the "10 Must-Read Blogs for Any Lean Startup" by Welovelean.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WN_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-WN-31">[31]</a></sup> The blog is considered a must-read for entrepreneurs and is often <a href="/wiki/Web_syndication" title="Web syndication">syndicated</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UC_Berkeley" title="UC Berkeley">UC Berkeley</a>, <a href="/wiki/VentureBeat" title="VentureBeat">VentureBeat</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huffington_Post" title="Huffington Post">Huffington Post</a></i>, is available as an audio podcast (produced by Clearshore and featuring the voice of HP Lewis) on the blog itself or through iTunes, and translated into Spanish (by Alberto Peralta). He also writes weekly for the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i> Accelerators blog and occasionally for <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>, the <i>Huffington Post</i> and in Japan for NikkeiBP."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HP_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-HP-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-RE-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UB_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-UB-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_Teaching_Career">Academic Teaching Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Academic Teaching Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blank has taught entrepreneurship to both undergraduate and graduate students. His courses are available at a variety of institutions, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanford" title="Stanford">Stanford</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Haas_School_of_Business" title="Haas School of Business">Haas School of Business</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_California_Berkeley" title="University of California Berkeley">University of California Berkeley</a>, the <a href="/wiki/California_Institute_of_Technology" title="California Institute of Technology">California Institute of Technology</a>, and Columbia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCB_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-UCB-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SUEC_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-SUEC-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VH_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-VH-33">[33]</a></sup> His curricula commonly focus on the Customer Development methodology that he developed throughout his career as a serial-entrepreneur and conservationist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-8"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup> Blank actively lectures at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanford" title="Stanford">Stanford</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UC_Berkeley" title="UC Berkeley">UC Berkeley</a>, and the joint Berkeley/Columbia MBA program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNBC_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNBC-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009 Blank won the Stanford University Undergraduate Teaching Award in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Management_Science" title="Management Science">Management Science</a> and Engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HP_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-HP-5">[5]</a></sup> That same year he was also named one of the Top 10 Influencers in Silicon Valley by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley_Mercury_News" title="Silicon Valley Mercury News">Silicon Valley Mercury News</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-COB_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-COB-35">[35]</a></sup> In 2010, Blank was a recipient of the Earl F. Cheit Outstanding Teaching Award at U.C. Berkeley <a href="/wiki/Haas_School_of_Business" title="Haas School of Business">Haas School of Business</a>.</p>
<p>Blank was the commencement speaker at <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_University" title="Philadelphia University">Philadelphia University</a> in 2011, and in 2012 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Harvard_Business_Review" title="The Harvard Business Review">The Harvard Business Review</a> named him one of 12 Masters of Innovation and <a href="/wiki/CNBC" title="CNBC">CNBC</a> recognized him as one of the "11 Notable Entrepreneurs Teaching the Next Generation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNBC_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-CNBC-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HBR_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-HBR-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2013, Blank was the commencement speaker at the University of Minnesota College of Science and Engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2013, Blank wrote the cover story in the Harvard Business Review.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Lean_LaunchPad">The Lean LaunchPad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: The Lean LaunchPad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of 2011, Blank launched the Lean LaunchPad class at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> and UC Berkeley. The class teaches founders how to reduce their failure rate through the combination of business model design, customer development and agile development. In July 2011 he was asked by the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a> to adapt the class to be the curriculum for its Innovation Corps which develops and nurtures a <a href="/wiki/Innovation_system" title="Innovation system">national innovation ecosystem</a> by helping discoveries from fundamental research to become new companies. The NSF teachers will teach 150 science and engineering teams in 2012 and will expand to 350 teams in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FC_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SS_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-SS-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSF_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-NSF-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the fall of 2012, an online version of the Lean LaunchPad was developed with help from <a href="/wiki/Udacity" title="Udacity">Udacity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> It incorporates learning aides such as videos, quizzes, and homework assignments to teach Blank's principles of <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneurship" title="Entrepreneurship">entrepreneurship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CP_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-CP-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HP2_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-HP2-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_service">Public service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Public service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blank was a past <a href="/wiki/Chairman" title="Chairman">Chairman</a> of Audubon California and<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SE_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-SE-13">[13]</a></sup> has also served on the board of the Peninsula Open Space Trust<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POST_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-POST-44">[44]</a></sup> and was a trustee of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.C._Santa_Cruz" title="U.C. Santa Cruz">U.C. Santa Cruz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCB_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-UCB-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SE_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-SE-13">[13]</a></sup> Blank currently sits on the board of the <a href="/wiki/California_League_of_Conservation_Voters" title="California League of Conservation Voters">California League of Conservation Voters</a>, or CLCV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CL_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-CL-45">[45]</a></sup> In 2007 he was appointed to the <a href="/wiki/California_Coastal_Commission" title="California Coastal Commission">California Coastal Commission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCC_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCC-46">[46]</a></sup> He has made <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropic gifts</a> to preserve the California Coast and has contributed funds to support the visitors' center at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%C3%B1o_Nuevo_State_Reserve" title="Año Nuevo State Reserve">Año Nuevo State Reserve</a> and to the Peninsula Open Space Trust.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-SB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POST_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-POST-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blank is known amongst entrepreneurs and students as one of the "The Godfathers of Silicon Valley".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-RE-30">[30]</a></sup> His books, blog, and interviews are often referred to or featured in world news publications such as <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a>, the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i>, <i>Forbes</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inc._Magazine" title="Inc. Magazine">Inc</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/TechCrunch" title="TechCrunch">TechCrunch</a> and <i>The Wall Street Journal</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FC_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-FC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IN_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-IN-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_10-9"><a href="#cite_note-TC-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE_30-3"><a href="#cite_note-RE-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WSJ_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-47">[47]</a></sup> Blank has hosted numerous sold-out speaking events and conferences about <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneurship" title="Entrepreneurship">entrepreneurship</a> and the Customer Development methodology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-COM_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-COM-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XCO_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-XCO-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XCO_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-XCO-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TEC_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-TEC-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EVB_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-EVB-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
