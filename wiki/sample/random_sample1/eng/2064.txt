<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kodaira embedding theorem</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, the <b>Kodaira embedding theorem</b> characterises <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-singular" title="Non-singular">non-singular</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Projective_varieties" title="Projective varieties">projective varieties</a>, over the <a href="/wiki/Complex_number" title="Complex number">complex numbers</a>, amongst <a href="/wiki/Compact_space" title="Compact space">compact</a> <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A4hler_manifold" title="Kähler manifold">Kähler manifolds</a>. In effect it says precisely which <a href="/wiki/Complex_manifold" title="Complex manifold">complex manifolds</a> are defined by <a href="/wiki/Homogeneous_polynomial" title="Homogeneous polynomial">homogeneous polynomials</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kunihiko_Kodaira" title="Kunihiko Kodaira">Kunihiko Kodaira</a>'s result is that for a compact Kähler manifold <i>M</i>, with a <b>Hodge metric</b>, meaning that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cohomology_class" title="Cohomology class">cohomology class</a> in degree 2 defined by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%A4hler_form" title="Kähler form">Kähler form</a> ω is an <i>integral</i> cohomology class, there is a complex-analytic embedding of <i>M</i> into <a href="/wiki/Complex_projective_space" title="Complex projective space">complex projective space</a> of some high enough dimension <i>N</i>. The fact that <i>M</i> embeds as an <a href="/wiki/Algebraic_variety" title="Algebraic variety">algebraic variety</a> follows from its compactness by <a href="/wiki/Algebraic_geometry_and_analytic_geometry#Chow.27s_theorem" title="Algebraic geometry and analytic geometry">Chow's theorem</a>. A Kähler manifold with a Hodge metric is occasionally called a <b>Hodge manifold</b> (named after <a href="/wiki/W._V._D._Hodge" title="W. V. D. Hodge">W. V. D. Hodge</a>), so Kodaira's results states that Hodge manifolds are projective. The converse that projective manifolds are Hodge manifolds is more elementary and was already known.</p>
<p>Kodaira also proved (Kodaira 1963), by recourse to the classification of compact complex surfaces, that every compact Kähler manifold is a <i>deformation</i> of a projective Kähler manifold. This was later simplified by Buchdahl to remove reliance on the classification (Buchdahl 2008).</p>
