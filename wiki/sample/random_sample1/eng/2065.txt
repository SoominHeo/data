<p><b>Old Great Bulgaria</b> or <b>Great Bulgaria</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine_Greek" title="Byzantine Greek">Byzantine Greek</a>: Παλαιά Μεγάλη Βουλγαρία, <i>Palaiá Megálē Voulgaría</i>), also often known by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a> names <i>Magna Bulgaria</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiedler2008152_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiedler2008152-6">[6]</a></sup>) and <b>Patria Onoguria</b> ("<a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Onogur" title="Onogur">Onogur</a> land"),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7th_Century" title="7th Century">7th Century</a> state formed by the <a href="/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Onogurs" title="Onogurs">Onogurs</a> on the western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontic_Steppe" title="Pontic Steppe">Pontic Steppe</a> (modern southern Ukraine and south-west Russia).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Great Bulgaria was originally centred on <a href="/wiki/Phanagoria" title="Phanagoria">Phanagoria</a>, between the <a href="/wiki/Dniester" title="Dniester">Dniester</a> and lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volga" title="Volga">Volga</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> (in modern <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>, Russia).</p>
<p>In the mid-7th century, Great Bulgaria expanded west to include <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Avars" title="Pannonian Avars">Avar territory</a> and was centered in <a href="/wiki/Poltava" title="Poltava">Poltava</a>. During the late 7th century, however, an <a href="/wiki/Avar_Khaganate#Middle_.28670-720.29_and_Late_.28720-804.29_Avar_periods" title="Avar Khaganate">Avar-Slavic alliance</a> in the west, and <a href="/wiki/Khazars" title="Khazars">Khazars</a> in the east, defeated the Bulgars and the Great Bulgaria disintegrated. Successor states included the <a href="/wiki/First_Bulgarian_Empire" title="First Bulgarian Empire">First Bulgarian Empire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volga_Bulgaria" title="Volga Bulgaria">Volga Bulgaria</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Great_Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Origin_of_the_Bulgars" title="Origin of the Bulgars">Origin of the Bulgars</a></div>
<p>The etymology of the ethnonym <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Bulgar" title="wikt:Bulgar">Bulgar</a></i> is not completely understood and difficult to trace back earlier than the 4th century AD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gurov_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gurov-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGolden1992103.E2.80.93104_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGolden1992103.E2.80.93104-11">[11]</a></sup> It is generally believed to derive from the <a href="/wiki/Turkic_languages" title="Turkic languages">Turkic</a> verb <i>bulğha</i> (to "stir", "mix", "disturb", "confuse"),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBowersock.2C_Brown.2C_Grabar1999354_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBowersock.2C_Brown.2C_Grabar1999354-12">[12]</a></sup> possibly suggesting that other Turkic peoples regarded the Bulgars as a "mixed" people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMaenchen-Helfen1973384_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMaenchen-Helfen1973384-13">[13]</a></sup> and/or "rebellious".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChen201297_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChen201297-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGolden1992104_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGolden1992104-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGolden2011143_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGolden2011143-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGolden1992104_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGolden1992104-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGolden2011143_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGolden2011143-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later Byzantine scholars implied that the Bulgars had previously been known as the Onogurs (<i>Onoğur</i>). <a href="/wiki/Agathon" title="Agathon">Agathon</a> wrote about the "nation of Onogur Bulğars", <a href="/wiki/Nikephoros_I_of_Constantinople" title="Nikephoros I of Constantinople">Nikephoros I</a> stated that Kubrat was lord of the <i>Onogundurs</i>, <a href="/wiki/Theophanes_the_Confessor" title="Theophanes the Confessor">Theophanes</a> referred to them as <i>Onogundur–Bulgars</i> and <a href="/wiki/Constantine_VII" title="Constantine VII">Constantine VII</a> remarked that the Bulgars formerly called themselves <i>Onogundurs</i>. Variations of the name include: <i>Onoguri, Onoghuri, Onghur, Ongur, Onghuri, Onguri, Onogundur, Unogundur</i> and <i>Unokundur</i>. There are several theories about the origin of the name Onogur. In some <a href="/wiki/Turkic_languages" title="Turkic languages">Turkic languages</a> <i>on</i> means "10" and <i>ğur</i> "arrow"; and "ten arrows" a federation of ten tribes, i.e. the <a href="/wiki/Western_Turkic_Khaganate" title="Western Turkic Khaganate">Western Turkic Khaganate</a>. Within the Turkic languages, "z" sounds in the easternmost languages tend to have become "r" in the westernmost Turkic languages; therefore, the ethnonym <a href="/wiki/Oghuz_Turks" title="Oghuz Turks">Oghuz</a> may be the source of Oghur; that is, <i>on Oğur</i> would mean "ten clans of Oghuz".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment">Establishment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Great_Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Establishment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Between 630 and 635, <a href="/wiki/Khan_(title)" title="Khan (title)">Khan</a> <a href="/wiki/Kubrat" title="Kubrat">Kubrat</a> managed to unite the Bulgars, <a href="/wiki/Onogurs" title="Onogurs">Onogurs</a> with the tribes of <a href="/wiki/Kutrigurs" title="Kutrigurs">Kutrigurs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utigurs" title="Utigurs">Utigurs</a> under a single rule, creating a powerful confederation which was referred to by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">medieval</a> authors in Western Europe as Old Great Bulgaria,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-18">[18]</a></sup> or <i>Patria Onoguria</i>. According to some scholars, it is more correctly called the <a href="/wiki/Onogurs" title="Onogurs">Onogundur</a>-Bulgar Empire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some scholars<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (September 2016)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> assume that it stretched as far west as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pannonian_plain" title="Pannonian plain">Pannonian plain</a> and included among its subjects some of the <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Avars" title="Pannonian Avars">Pannonian Avars</a>. It is presumed that his capital was the ancient city of <a href="/wiki/Phanagoria" title="Phanagoria">Phanagoria</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taman_peninsula" title="Taman peninsula">Taman peninsula</a>. Kubrat's grave was discovered in 1912 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pereshchepina" title="Pereshchepina">Pereshchepina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Khan_Kubrat">Khan Kubrat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Great_Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Khan Kubrat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kubrat" title="Kubrat">Kubrat</a></div>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Nominalia_of_the_Bulgarian_khans" title="Nominalia of the Bulgarian khans">Nominalia of the Bulgarian khans</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Kubrat" title="Kubrat">Kubrat</a> was from the royal <a href="/wiki/Dulo_clan" title="Dulo clan">clan Dulo</a> and a rightful heir to the Bulgar throne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-21">[21]</a></sup> H. Zotenberg (1883), while translating John Nikiu <i>Chronicles</i> from old-Ethiopian, intentionally replaced the name Qetrades to Kubrat. Since then, the historiography erroneously holds a misconception that Kubrat was raised and baptized by the Byzantine court, while the John’s character Qetrades has no real-life connection to the ruler of the Great Bulgaria Kubrat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chronicle_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Chronicle-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kubrat quickly managed to overthrow Avar domination, extending Onogur influence among the Bulgarians in Pannonia in what subsequently became known as Hungary. Ultimately however, although there is no evidence that the Utigurs were independent of the Onogurs until after Kubrat's empire disintegrated, it is believed he seceded from the Onogurs when they became entangled in dynastic wars. After Kubrat's burial in <a href="/wiki/Pereshchepina_Treasure" title="Pereshchepina Treasure">Mala Pereshchepina</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Khazars" title="Khazars">Khazars</a>, who had triumphed in the collapse of Onoguria, subjugated Kubrat's eldest son and heir <a href="/wiki/Batbayan" title="Batbayan">Batbayan</a>, forcing his other sons to flee north up the Volga (2nd son Kotrag) and west into the Balkans (4th son Kuber &amp; 3rd son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asparukh" title="Asparukh">Asparukh</a>) and Italy (5th son Alcek, Alzek) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Disintegration">Disintegration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Great_Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Disintegration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The events that unfolded following Kubrat's death are described by the Byzantine Patriarch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicephorus_I" title="Nicephorus I">Nicephorus I</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-18">[18]</a></sup> In the times of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Constantine_IV" title="Constantine IV">Constantine IV</a>, he narrates, Kubrat died and <a href="/wiki/Batbayan" title="Batbayan">Batbayan</a>, the eldest of his five sons, was left in charge of the state. Under strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khazar" title="Khazar">Khazar</a> pressure, Kubrat's other sons disregarded their father's advice to stay together in order to resist the enemies and soon departed, taking their own tribes.</p>
<p>Old Great Bulgaria disintegrated under Khazars pressure in 668.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Great_Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Old_Great_Bulgaria_and_migration_of_Bulgarians.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1430" data-file-width="2180" height="184" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Old_Great_Bulgaria_and_migration_of_Bulgarians.png/280px-Old_Great_Bulgaria_and_migration_of_Bulgarians.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Old_Great_Bulgaria_and_migration_of_Bulgarians.png/420px-Old_Great_Bulgaria_and_migration_of_Bulgarians.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Old_Great_Bulgaria_and_migration_of_Bulgarians.png/560px-Old_Great_Bulgaria_and_migration_of_Bulgarians.png 2x" width="280"/></a>

</div>
</div>
<p>Some Bulgars remained in the former Onoguria, under the domination of the <a href="/wiki/Khazars" title="Khazars">Khazars</a>.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Balkars</dt>
</dl>
<p>Some<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (March 2014)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> also believe that the present-day <a href="/wiki/Balkars" title="Balkars">Balkars</a> of the Caucasus are the descendants of the Batbayan horde even though they . and speak a Turkic language of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kipchak_people" title="Kipchak people">Kipchak</a> type. But in most Turkic languages the sound "b" became "m".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Volga Bulgars</dt>
</dl>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Volga_Bulgaria" title="Volga Bulgaria">Volga Bulgaria</a></div>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Kotrag" title="Kotrag">Kotrag</a>, the leader of the Kutrigurs, took control on the western Steppe, <a href="/wiki/Batbayan" title="Batbayan">Batbayan</a> led the ... into the upper Volga-Ural region. There they established <a href="/wiki/Volga_Bulgaria" title="Volga Bulgaria">Volga Bulgaria</a>, at the confluence of the Volga and <a href="/wiki/Kama_River" title="Kama River">Kama</a>. As the Volga or Silver Bulgars (<i>Bessermens</i>), they converted voluntarily to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> in the 9th century. They managed to preserve their national identity well into the 13th century, by repelling the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mongol" title="Mongol">Mongol</a> attacks in 1223. However, they were eventually subdued, their capital <a href="/wiki/Bolghar" title="Bolghar">Bulgar</a> city became one of major cities of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Horde" title="Golden Horde">Golden Horde</a> of the Mongols and the Bulgars mixed with the <a href="/wiki/Tatars" title="Tatars">Tatars</a>. The citizens of the modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" title="Russian Federation">Russian</a> republics of <a href="/wiki/Tatarstan" title="Tatarstan">Tatarstan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chuvashia_Republic" title="Chuvashia Republic">Chuvashia</a> are considered to be descendants of those Bulgars.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Bulgars in Vojvodina and Macedonia</dt>
</dl>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kuber" title="Kuber">Kuber</a> ruled in <a href="/wiki/Sirmium" title="Sirmium">Sirmium</a> over a mixed group of peoples – Bulgars, Byzantine subjects, Slavs, Germanic tribes – as a vassal of the <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Avars" title="Pannonian Avars">Avar</a> khagan. After a revolt he led his people to <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(region)" title="Macedonia (region)">Macedonia</a>. There he had settled in the region of Keremisia and made an unsuccessful attempt to capture the city of <a href="/wiki/Thessaloniki" title="Thessaloniki">Thessaloniki</a>. After this, he disappears from history and his people were later consolidated into the Bulgarian Empire by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khan_Krum" title="Khan Krum">Khan Krum</a>.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Bulgars in Italy</dt>
</dl>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bulgarians_in_Italy" title="Bulgarians in Italy">Bulgarians in Italy</a></div>
<p>Other Bulgars, circa 662, led by their "Duke Alzeco" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Altsek" title="Altsek">Altsek</a>) sought refuge from the Avars with the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a> and requested land from the Lombard King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grimoald_I_of_Benevento" title="Grimoald I of Benevento">Grimoald I of Benevento</a> in exchange for military service "for an uncertain reason", initially staying near <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a> and later moving further south. Grimoald sent Altzek and his followers to his son <a href="/wiki/Romuald_I_of_Benevento" title="Romuald I of Benevento">Romuald</a> in Benevento and they were then granted by Romuald land northeast of <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> in the "spacious but up till that time deserted" towns of <a href="/wiki/Sepino" title="Sepino">Sepino</a>, Bovianum (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boiano" title="Boiano">Boiano</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Isernia" title="Isernia">Isernia</a>, in the present-day region of <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">Molise</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apennines" title="Apennines">Apennines</a>. Instead of the title "Duke" Altzek was granted the Lombard title of "<a href="/wiki/Gastald" title="Gastald">Gastald</a>". <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Deacon" title="Paul the Deacon">Paul the Deacon</a> in his <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historia_Langobardorum" title="Historia Langobardorum">Historia Langobardorum</a></i> writing after the year 787 says that in his time Bulgars still inhabited the area, and that even though they speak "Latin", "they have not forsaken the use of their own tongue".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Excavations in the necropolis of Vicenne-<a href="/wiki/Campochiaro" title="Campochiaro">Campochiaro</a> near Boiano which dates from the 7th century, found among 130 burials that there were 13 human burials alongside horses along with artifacts of Germanic and <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Avars" title="Pannonian Avars">Avar</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Horse burials are characteristic of Central Asian horse-nomads, and therefore these burials are clearly those of the Bulgar settlers of Molise and <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>First Bulgarian Empire</dt>
</dl>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/First_Bulgarian_Empire" title="First Bulgarian Empire">First Bulgarian Empire</a></div>
<p>After the state disintegrated under Khazar attack in 668, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asparukh" title="Asparukh">Asparukh</a> parted ways with his brothers and led some of the <a href="/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars</a> to seek a secure home. He was followed by 30,000 to 50,000 Bulgars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ongal" title="Battle of Ongal">Battle of Ongal</a> Asparukh founded the First Bulgarian Empire, which was officially recognized as an independent state by the Byzantine Empire in 681.</p>
