<p><b>Polypodiaceae</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of polypod <a href="/wiki/Fern" title="Fern">ferns</a>, which includes more than 60 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genera" title="Genera">genera</a> divided into several <a href="/wiki/Tribe_(biology)" title="Tribe (biology)">tribes</a> and containing around 1,000 <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>. Nearly all are <a href="/wiki/Epiphyte" title="Epiphyte">epiphytes</a>, but some are terrestrial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pani_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pani-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Polypodiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stems of Polypodiaceae range from erect to long-creeping. The <a href="/wiki/Frond" title="Frond">fronds</a> are entire, pinnatifid, or variously forked or pinnate. The <a href="/wiki/Petiole_(botany)" title="Petiole (botany)">petioles</a> lack <a href="/wiki/Stipule" title="Stipule">stipules</a>. The scaly <a href="/wiki/Rhizome" title="Rhizome">rhizomes</a> are generally creeping in nature. Polypodiaceae species are found in wet climates, most commonly in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rain_forest" title="Rain forest">rain forests</a>. In temperate zones, most species tend to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epiphytic" title="Epiphytic">epiphytic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epipetric" title="Epipetric">epipetric</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pani_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-pani-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Notable examples of ferns in this family include the resurrection fern (<i><a href="/wiki/Pleopeltis_polypodioides" title="Pleopeltis polypodioides">Pleopeltis polypodioides</a></i>) and the golden serpent fern (<i><a href="/wiki/Phlebodium_aureum" title="Phlebodium aureum">Phlebodium aureum</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pani_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-pani-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Polypodiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Recent <a href="/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetics" title="Molecular phylogenetics">molecular phylogenic</a> analysis has led to the division of the Polypodiaceae into five subfamiles, and to the inclusion of genera that have at various times been placed in other families, including the Drynariaceae, Grammitidaceae, Gymnogrammitidaceae, Loxogrammaceae, Platyceriaceae, and Pleurisoriopsidaceae.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-chr-2">[2]</a></sup> In the list that follows, the taxa shown with the "<small>(=)</small>" prefix are considered to be synonymyms for the accepted subfamily name that they follow.
