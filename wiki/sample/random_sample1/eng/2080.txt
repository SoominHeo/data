<p>The <b>Yonsei–Korea, Korea-Yonsei rivalry</b> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_rivalry" title="Team rivalry">college rivalry</a> between two <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">universities</a> located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seoul,_South_Korea" title="Seoul, South Korea">Seoul, South Korea</a>: <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> (<a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a>). Both schools are regarded as the most prestigious private universities in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. Located within the same city, the campuses are only 30 minutes apart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Each year, various festivals are held before and after the friendship games between the two colleges. Since 1956, this annual Yonsei-Korea (Korea-Yonsei) games has served as the most hotly contested collegiate rivalries in the country. Five sports teams: <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> teams compete against each other.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A large number of current students and alumni from both universities attend this event to support their respective sides. The enthusiasm and cheers from the crowd led by the cheerleaders fill the stadium throughout the entire duration of the game. It is truly a unique experience.</p>
<p>A long running gag between the two universities is the order of the two universities; <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a> refers to it as the "Yonsei-Korea" rivalry, whereas <a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> refers to it as the "Korea-Yonsei" rivalry.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_University%E2%80%93Yonsei_University_rivalry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1927_Korean_FA_Cup_Semi-final">1927 Korean FA Cup Semi-final</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_University%E2%80%93Yonsei_University_rivalry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1927 Korean FA Cup Semi-final">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>First meeting of the two schools in football.</p>
<p>The match was held in Gyeongseong Stadium. Data From 1927 Through 1942: Yonhi College (now <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a>) had 14 wins, and Bosung College (now <a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a>) also had 14 wins. In 1927, the <a href="/wiki/Korean_FA_Cup" title="Korean FA Cup">FA Cup</a> was called the <a href="/wiki/Korean_National_Football_Championship" title="Korean National Football Championship">National Football Championship</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1945_Yonhi-BC_OB_Football_Championship">1945 Yonhi-BC OB Football Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_University%E2%80%93Yonsei_University_rivalry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1945 Yonhi-BC OB Football Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Beginning of the regular scheduled football match between Yonsei and Korea (Korea and Yonsei).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a> was formally established in 1885 through the union of Yonhi College and Severance Union Medical College. In 1946, Bosung College was renamed <a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> and the match was renamed Yon-Ko (Ko-Yon) games since then.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Train_Game">Train Game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_University%E2%80%93Yonsei_University_rivalry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Train Game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After annual Yonsei-Korea (Korea-Yonsei) games, students from both schools engage in Train Game (Korean <i>기차놀이</i>). The game is performed in a manner similar to that of making a conga line, forming a line by holding a person in front of another by his/her shoulder. The Trains head to bars, stores, and restaurants to demand food and drinks for free. It is told to both Yonsei and Korea (Korea and Yonsei) students that the tradition originates from the 1980s when pro-democratic student demonstrations were prevalent. The shopkeepers nearby the campuses witnessed the events and sympathized with the cause, thus served food and drinks free of charge to the students.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympic_athletes">Olympic athletes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_University%E2%80%93Yonsei_University_rivalry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Olympic athletes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> (<a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a>) send many athletes to the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>. 2010 Winter Olympic four-time gold medalist short track speed skater <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chun_Lee-Kyung" title="Chun Lee-Kyung">Chun Lee-Kyung</a> is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a>, and gold medalist figure skater <a href="/wiki/Kim_Yuna" title="Kim Yuna">Kim Yuna</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> graduate.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_location">Geographic location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_University%E2%80%93Yonsei_University_rivalry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Geographic location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Korea_University" title="Korea University">Korea University</a> is located on the northeastern-side of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a> is located on the west-side of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.</p>
