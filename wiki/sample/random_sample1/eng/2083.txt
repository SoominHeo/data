<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gordan_Bunoza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 2 July 2010, <a href="/wiki/Wis%C5%82a_Krak%C3%B3w" title="Wisła Kraków">Wisła Kraków</a> signed him from <a href="/wiki/NK_Karlovac" title="NK Karlovac">Karlovac</a> for an undisclosed fee. On 19 March 2011, Wisła Kraków announced that Bunoza will be out for the rest of the 2010-11 season due to an injury he suffered during the training.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gordan_Bunoza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After several good performances for the <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_under-21_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national under-21 football team">U-21 side</a>, the coach of the Bosnia-Herzegovina senior team, <a href="/wiki/Safet_Su%C5%A1i%C4%87" title="Safet Sušić">Safet Sušić</a>, included Bunoza in the squad to face <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_national_football_team" title="Luxembourg national football team">Luxembourg</a> and <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> in early September 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
