<p>The <b><i>Admiral Gorshkov</i>-class frigates</b>, also known as <b>Project 22350</b>, are a <a href="/wiki/Ship_class" title="Ship class">class</a> of frigate of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Navy" title="Russian Navy">Russian Navy</a>. They have been designed by the <a href="/wiki/Severnoye_Design_Bureau" title="Severnoye Design Bureau">Severnoye Design Bureau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lead_ship" title="Lead ship">lead ship</a> being named after <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Gorshkov" title="Sergey Gorshkov">Sergey Gorshkov</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Admiral_Gorshkov-class_frigate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Project 22350</i> is the effective successor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krivak_class_frigate" title="Krivak class frigate">Krivak class</a>. Unlike their Soviet-era predecessors the new ships are designed to perform multiple roles. The ships are to be able to execute long-range strikes, conduct <a href="/wiki/Anti-submarine_warfare" title="Anti-submarine warfare">anti-submarine warfare</a> and to carry out escort missions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Admiral_Gorshkov-class_frigate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Russian Navy has set a requirement for 20-30 such ships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rusnavy1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-rusnavy1-8">[8]</a></sup> The design of the ship, developed by <i>Severnoye PKB</i> (Northern Design Bureau) FSUE in St. Petersburg, was approved by Naval Command in July 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The plan is to fully replace the older <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sovremenny_class_destroyer" title="Sovremenny class destroyer">Project 956 <i>Sovremenny</i> class destroyers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burevestnik_class_frigate" title="Burevestnik class frigate">Project 1135 <i>Burevestnik</i> class frigates</a> in four Russian fleets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The lead ship, <a href="/wiki/Russian_frigate_Admiral_Gorshkov" title="Russian frigate Admiral Gorshkov"><i>Admiral Gorshkov</i></a> (actual name <i>Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union Gorshkov</i>), was laid down on 1 February 2006 in <a href="/wiki/Severnaya_Verf" title="Severnaya Verf">Severnaya Verf</a> Shipyard at St. Petersburg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIA_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIA-11">[11]</a></sup> In late October 2008 the Russian <a href="/wiki/Deputy_prime_minister" title="Deputy prime minister">deputy prime minister</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Ivanov" title="Sergei Ivanov">Sergei Ivanov</a>, announced that the first ship in the class would be ready by 2011. He said the timely construction of combat ships is a priority task of the Russian shipbuilding industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIA_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-RIA-11">[11]</a></sup> The lead ship completion date was announced first as 2009, but later as 2010, and then as 2011. Latest report states that the frigate will join the Russian Navy in November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rusnavy.com_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-rusnavy.com-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 June 2009, during the "ÐÐÐÐ¡-IMDS 2009" International Naval Expo, the Commander in Chief of the Russian Navy, Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vladimir_Vysotsky_(Admiral)" title="Vladimir Vysotsky (Admiral)">Vladimir Vysotsky</a>, announced that the production of a second <i>Admiral Gorshkov</i> class frigate would begin at Severnaya Verf shipyard in St. Petersburg by the end of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In November 2009 the Severnaya Verf shipyard in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Petersburg" title="St Petersburg">St Petersburg</a> announced that it would start the construction of the second ship of the class <a href="/wiki/Russian_frigate_Admiral_Kasatonov" title="Russian frigate Admiral Kasatonov"><i>Admiral Kasatonov</i></a> on 26 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The ship was laid down in a ceremony attended by representatives of the Russian Navy, the St. Petersburg administration, and Admiral Igor Kasatonov - the son of the ship's namesake, <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kasatonov" title="Vladimir Kasatonov">Vladimir Kasatonov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In December 2014, one engine from <i>Admiral Kasatonov</i> was transferred to <i>Admiral Gorshkov</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first frigate was floated out of the launch dock on 29 October 2010. The ship was only 40 percent complete and then began to be fitted out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-17">[17]</a></sup> At present, main mechanical equipment and systems providing safe launching has been installed, including a combined diesel and gas turbine (CODAG) powerplant, gear assemblies, drive shafts and screws, and electric power supply facilities. Further works will be performed at the shipyard's fitting quay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rusnavy2_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-rusnavy2-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 21, 2012, the Severnaya Verf shipyard announced that it had taken out two four-year loans worth RUB 16.23bn (approx. USD 509.88m) from state-owned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sberbank" title="Sberbank">Sberbank</a>. The money will be used on the construction of the <i>Admiral Gorshkov</i> class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between eight and fifteen ships are to be constructed for the Russian Navy. The Northern Wharf Shipyard has thus far announced having received orders for six units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first two ships of the class have gas turbines from <a href="/wiki/Zorya-Mashproekt" title="Zorya-Mashproekt">Zorya-Mashproekt</a> in Ukraine. Following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_Crimean_crisis" title="2014 Crimean crisis">2014 Crimean crisis</a>, the Ukrainians refused to supply Russia with military technology and so <a href="/wiki/NPO_Saturn" title="NPO Saturn">NPO Saturn</a> has been commissioned to design new engines. USC forecast these new engines will be available in 2017-18, allowing ships to be commissioned from 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> However, after the intervention by the Russian government the engines will be constructed in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
