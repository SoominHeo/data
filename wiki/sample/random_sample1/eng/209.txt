<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Military patrol at the 1936 Winter Olympics</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>At the <b><a href="/wiki/1936_Winter_Olympics" title="1936 Winter Olympics">1936 Winter Olympics</a></b>, in <a href="/wiki/Garmisch-Partenkirchen" title="Garmisch-Partenkirchen">Garmisch-Partenkirchen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, in 1936, a <b><a href="/wiki/Military_patrol" title="Military patrol">military patrol</a></b> competition was held. The <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> refused admission of this sport into the Olympic Program, but the expressed desires of <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> forced the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">IOC</a> to make this program a <a href="/wiki/Demonstration_sport" title="Demonstration sport">demonstration sport</a>. Military patrol is considered the precursor to <a href="/wiki/Biathlon" title="Biathlon">biathlon</a>.</p>
<p>The success of the Italian team was honored by <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a> with 30,000 <a href="/wiki/Italian_lira" title="Italian lira">lira</a> per participant, because it was the first time, that the Italians placed first in <a href="/wiki/Nordic_skiing" title="Nordic skiing">Nordic skiing</a> at the Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scilligo_Sisto_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scilligo_Sisto-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
