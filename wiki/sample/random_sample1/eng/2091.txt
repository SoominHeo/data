<p><b>Alonzo Mathis</b>, better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Gorilla Zoe</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'z' in 'Zion'">z</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is an American rapper. He is a former artist with Trustnobody Ent. Under Tavares "Buck" Thomas. He subsequently became a member of rap group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boyz_n_da_Hood_(rap_group)" title="Boyz n da Hood (rap group)">Boyz N Da Hood</a>. His solo debut album <i>Welcome to the Zoo</i> ghost written by Ernest Gibbs. came out in 2007. His next two albums, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don%27t_Feed_Da_Animals" title="Don't Feed Da Animals">Don't Feed Da Animals</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/King_Kong_(Gorilla_Zoe_album)" title="King Kong (Gorilla Zoe album)">King Kong</a></i> were released in 2009 and 2011. In 2015, he released two mix tapes: Recovery and Raised in the Jungle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorilla_Zoe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_beginnings">Music beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorilla_Zoe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006 He replaced <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a> as a member of <a href="/wiki/Boyz_n_da_Hood" title="Boyz n da Hood">Boyz n da Hood</a>. He first saw success in collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Yung_Joc" title="Yung Joc">Yung Joc</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Coffee_Shop_(Yung_Joc_song)" title="Coffee Shop (Yung Joc song)">Coffee Shop</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bottle_Poppin%27" title="Bottle Poppin'">Bottle Poppin'</a>," which charted under several <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">[2]</a></sup> He would then be signed as a solo artist for <a href="/wiki/Block_Entertainment" title="Block Entertainment">Block Entertainment</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_South" title="Bad Boy South">Bad Boy South</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007:_Welcome_to_the_Zoo">2007: <i>Welcome to the Zoo</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorilla_Zoe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007: Welcome to the Zoo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 19, 2007 Gorilla Zoe released his first single "<a href="/wiki/Hood_Figga" title="Hood Figga">Hood Figga</a>" it was a success peaking on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_100" title="Billboard 100">Billboard 100</a> at #38. His first solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_the_Zoo" title="Welcome to the Zoo">Welcome to the Zoo</a></i>, was released October 2007, peaking at #18 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, #8 on Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums, and #3 on Top Rap Albums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_Albums_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_Albums-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Top_Rap_Albums_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Top_Rap_Albums-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hip_Hop_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hip_Hop-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2007 Gorilla Zoe was chosen for the 2008 <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL Freshmen</a> cover along with rappers <a href="/wiki/Saigon_(rapper)" title="Saigon (rapper)">Saigon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plies_(rapper)" title="Plies (rapper)">Plies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rich_Boy" title="Rich Boy">Rich Boy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lil_Boosie" title="Lil Boosie">Lil Boosie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papoose_(rapper)" title="Papoose (rapper)">Papoose</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Young_Dro" title="Young Dro">Young Dro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Don.27t_Feed_da_Animals">2009: <i>Don't Feed da Animals</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorilla_Zoe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009: Don't Feed da Animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 28, 2008 Gorilla Zoe released His first single from his second album entitled "<a href="/wiki/Lost_(Gorilla_Zoe_song)" title="Lost (Gorilla Zoe song)">Lost</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lost_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lost-7">[7]</a></sup> <i>Don't Feed da Animals</i> topped the <i>Billboard</i> Top Rap Albums chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Top_Rap_Albums_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Top_Rap_Albums-4">[4]</a></sup> On March 17, 2009 Gorilla Zoe's second solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Feed_da_Animals" title="Don't Feed da Animals">Don't Feed da Animals</a></i>, it sold 29,000 copies its first week peaking on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 at #8. The second single from the album was "What It Is", featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>, and Kollosus, and the third single "Echo" followed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-11:_Mixtapes.2C_I_Am_Atlanta_3_EP_.26_King_Kong">2010-11: Mixtapes, <i>I Am Atlanta 3 EP</i> &amp; <i>King Kong</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorilla_Zoe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010-11: Mixtapes, I Am Atlanta 3 EP &amp; King Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the month of February 2010, Gorilla Zoe released a mixtape every day on the mixtape website DatPiff.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28_Mixtapes_28_Days_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-28_Mixtapes_28_Days-8">[8]</a></sup> Gorilla Zoe is currently working on a mixtape with Die-Verse City's member "Qu1k". He also released an EP named <i>I Am Atlanta 3</i>, it is currently available on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>.</p>
<p>On June 14, 2011 Gorilla Zoe released his third solo album <i><a href="/wiki/King_Kong_(Gorilla_Zoe_album)" title="King Kong (Gorilla Zoe album)">King Kong</a></i> the album was not as successful as his previous albums only charting on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at no. 56 with 10,300 copies in its first week out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> rated the album three-and-a-half stars out of five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusic_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-10">[10]</a></sup> The song from the album, "What's Goin' On", was released on December 14, 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and peaked no. 99 at the Billboard <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-present:_Recovery">2014-present: <i>Recovery</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorilla_Zoe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014-present: Recovery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 6, 2014 after a two-year hiatus Gorilla Zoe released a new mixtape entitled <i>Recovery</i>, it was also revealed that Gorilla Zoe had signed to fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Flo_Rida" title="Flo Rida">Flo Rida</a>'s label International Music Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
