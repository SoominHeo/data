<p><b>Goma</b> or <b>GOMA</b> may also refer to:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Homa_(ritual)" title="Homa (ritual)">Homa</a>, an ancient Buddhist fire ritual originating in India practiced mainly in the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Shingon_Buddhism" title="Shingon Buddhism">Shingon sect</a>; <i>goma</i> is the Japanese transliteration</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gallery_of_Modern_Art" title="Gallery of Modern Art">Gallery of Modern Art</a>, an art gallery in the centre of Glasgow</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Queensland_Gallery_of_Modern_Art" title="Queensland Gallery of Modern Art">Queensland Gallery of Modern Art</a> in Brisbane</li>
<li>Goma, "lake" in Algonquian used in North American place names such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Algoma_(word)" title="Algoma (word)">Algoma (word)</a></li>
<li>Goma, "sesame" in Japanese, as in <a href="/wiki/Gomashio" title="Gomashio">gomashio</a>, sesame seeds and salt used as a seasoning</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Goma_(software)" title="Goma (software)">Goma</a>, A Full-Newton Finite Element Program for Free and Moving Boundary Problems with Coupled Fluid/Solid Momentum, Energy, Mass, and Chemical Species Transport</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Goma-2" title="Goma-2">Goma-2</a>, a high explosive</li>
<li>The word for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubber" title="Rubber">rubber</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, colloquially may also refer to a <a href="/wiki/Hangover" title="Hangover">hangover</a></li>
</ul>
