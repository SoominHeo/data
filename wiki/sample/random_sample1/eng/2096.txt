<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kazutaka Komori</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Kazutaka Komori</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">小森 一孝</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Komori Kazutaka</i></span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 1943 – November 1971) was a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> political <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultranationalist" title="Ultranationalist">ultranationalist</a> activist and a member of a <a href="/wiki/Right-wing_politics" title="Right-wing politics">right-wing</a> <a href="/wiki/Uyoku_dantai" title="Uyoku dantai">Uyoku dantai</a> group close to <a href="/wiki/Satoshi_Akao" title="Satoshi Akao">Satoshi Akao</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Axelbank_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Axelbank-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1960, at the age of 17, Komori went to the home of prominent Tokyo publisher <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hoji_Shimanaka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hoji Shimanaka (page does not exist)">Hoji Shimanaka</a> with the intent to kill him in retribution for a fiction story by <a href="/wiki/Shichir%C5%8D_Fukazawa" title="Shichirō Fukazawa">Shichirō Fukazawa</a> printed in one of Shimanaka's magazines that described the overthrow and murder of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_House_of_Japan" title="Imperial House of Japan">Japanese Imperial family</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Shimanaka was not at home but Komori murdered a maid and stabbed the publisher's wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Axelbank_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Axelbank-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Komori was later tried as an adult and sentenced to 15 years in prison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-RE-4">[4]</a></sup> This murder and the assassination of <a href="/wiki/Inejiro_Asanuma" title="Inejiro Asanuma">Inejiro Asanuma</a> by <a href="/wiki/Otoya_Yamaguchi" title="Otoya Yamaguchi">Otoya Yamaguchi</a> led to the arrest of <a href="/wiki/Satoshi_Akao" title="Satoshi Akao">Satoshi Akao</a> in 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SaS_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SaS-5">[5]</a></sup> Komori died in prison in 1971.</p>
