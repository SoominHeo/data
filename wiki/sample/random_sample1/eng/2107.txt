<p><b>Emperor Go-Saga</b> (後嵯峨天皇 <i>Go-Saga-tennō</i>) (April 1, 1220 – March 17, 1272) was the 88th <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">emperor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, according to the traditional order of succession. This reign spanned the years 1242 through 1246.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This 13th-century <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sovereign" title="Sovereign">sovereign</a> was named after the 8th-century <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Saga" title="Emperor Saga">Emperor Saga</a> and <i>go-</i> (後), translates literally as "later"; and thus, he is sometimes called the "Later Emperor Saga". The Japanese word <i>go</i> has also been translated to mean the "second one;" and in some older sources, this emperor may be identified as "Saga, the second," or as "Saga II."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Genealogy">Genealogy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Saga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Genealogy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before his ascension to the <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum_Throne" title="Chrysanthemum Throne">Chrysanthemum Throne</a>, his personal name (his <i>imina</i>) was Kunihito<i>-shinnō</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">邦仁親王</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was the second son of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Tsuchimikado" title="Emperor Tsuchimikado">Emperor Tsuchimikado</a>, and second cousin of his predecessor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Shij%C5%8D" title="Emperor Shijō">Emperor Shijō</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_of_Go-Saga.27s_life">Events of Go-Saga's life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Saga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events of Go-Saga's life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He ruled from February 21, 1242 to February 16, 1246.</p>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Tsuchimikado" title="Emperor Tsuchimikado">Emperor Tsuchimikado</a> moved to <a href="/wiki/Tosa_Province" title="Tosa Province">Tosa Province</a> (on <a href="/wiki/Shikoku" title="Shikoku">Shikoku</a>), he was raised by his mother's side of the family.</p>
<p>Because of the sudden death of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Shij%C5%8D" title="Emperor Shijō">Emperor Shijō</a> at the age of 10, the question of succession arose. Because the expectations of the court nobility and the <a href="/wiki/Kamakura_shogunate" title="Kamakura shogunate">Bakufu</a> conflicted, the issue was bitterly contested. <a href="/wiki/Kuj%C5%8D_Michiie" title="Kujō Michiie">Kujō Michiie</a> and the court nobility supported Prince Tadanari (忠成王), a son of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Juntoku" title="Emperor Juntoku">Retired Emperor Juntoku</a>, but the <i><a href="/wiki/Shikken" title="Shikken">shikken</a></i> <a href="/wiki/H%C5%8Dj%C5%8D_Yasutoki" title="Hōjō Yasutoki">Hōjō Yasutoki</a> was opposed to the sons of Juntoku because of his involvement in the <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dky%C5%AB_War" title="Jōkyū War">Jōkyū War</a>. Michiie instead supported Tsuchimikado's son Prince Kunihito as a neutral figure for Emperor. During these negotiations, there was a vacancy on the throne of 11 days.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>1242</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Ninji" title="Ninji">Ninji</a> 3, 10th day of the 1st month</i>): In the 10th year of Shijō<i>-tennō</i> 's reign (四条天皇10年), the emperor died suddenly; and despite a dispute over who should follow him as sovereign, contemporary scholars then construed that the succession (<i>senso</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> was received by the second son of former Emperor Tsuchimikado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1242</b> (<i>Ninji 3, 5th month</i>): Emperor Go-Saga is said to have acceded to the throne (<i>sokui</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>In 1242, Prince Kunihito became emperor. In 1246 he abdicated to his son, <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Fukakusa" title="Emperor Go-Fukakusa">Emperor Go-Fukakusa</a>, beginning his reign as <a href="/wiki/Cloistered_rule" title="Cloistered rule">cloistered emperor</a>. In 1259, he compelled <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Fukakusa" title="Emperor Go-Fukakusa">Emperor Go-Fukakusa</a> to abdicate to his younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Kameyama" title="Emperor Kameyama">Emperor Kameyama</a>. <a href="/wiki/Prince_Munetaka" title="Prince Munetaka">Imperial Prince Munetaka</a> became <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">shōgun</a> instead of the Hōjō regents. Henceforth, the shōguns of the Kamakura Bakufu came from the imperial house. Still, the Hōjō regents increased their control of the shogunate, setting up the system of rule by regents.</p>
<p>The descendants of his two sons contested the throne between them, forming into two lines, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jimyoin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jimyoin (page does not exist)">Jimyōin-tō</a> (Go-Fukakusa's descendants) and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Daikaku&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Daikaku (page does not exist)">Daikakuji-tō</a> (Kameyama's descendants).</p>

<p>In 1272, Go-Saga died.</p>
<p>Go-Saga's final resting place is designated as an Imperial mausoleum (<i>misasagi</i>) at Saa no minami no <i>Misasagi</i> in Kyoto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kugy.C5.8D">Kugyō</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Saga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kugyō">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Kugy%C5%8D" title="Kugyō">Kugyō</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">公卿</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a collective term for the very few most powerful men attached to the court of the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Emperor of Japan</a> in pre-<a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji</a> eras. Even during those years in which the court's actual influence outside the palace walls was minimal, the hierarchic organization persisted.</p>
<p>In general, this elite group included only three to four men at a time. These were hereditary courtiers whose experience and background would have brought them to the pinnacle of a life's career. During Go-Saga's reign, this apex of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daij%C5%8D-kan" title="Daijō-kan">Daijō-kan</a> included:</i></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kampaku" title="Kampaku">Kampaku</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Konoe_Kanetsune" title="Konoe Kanetsune">Konoe Kanetsune</a>, 1242</li>
<li><i>Kampaku</i>, <a href="/wiki/Nij%C5%8D_Yoshizane" title="Nijō Yoshizane">Nijō Yoshizane</a>, 1242–1246</li>
<li><i>Kampaku</i>, <a href="/wiki/Ichij%C5%8D_Sanetsune" title="Ichijō Sanetsune">Ichijō Sanetsune</a>, 1246</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Sadaijin" title="Sadaijin">Sadaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Udaijin" title="Udaijin">Udaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nadaijin" title="Nadaijin">Nadaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Dainagon" title="Dainagon">Dainagon</a></i></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eras_of_Go-Saga.27s_reign">Eras of Go-Saga's reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Saga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Eras of Go-Saga's reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The years of Go-saga's reign are more specifically identified by more than one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_era_names" title="Japanese era names">era name</a> or <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neng%C5%8D" title="Nengō">nengō</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
