<p>The order <b>Polypodiales</b> encompasses the major lineages of polypod <a href="/wiki/Fern" title="Fern">ferns</a>, which comprise more than 80% of today's fern species. They are found in many parts of the world including tropical, semitropical and temperate areas. The characteristics of this group include: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sporangia" title="Sporangia">sporangia</a> with a vertical <a href="/wiki/Annulus_(botany)" title="Annulus (botany)">annulus</a> interrupted by the stalk and stomium; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indusium" title="Indusium">indusia</a> laterally or centrally attached (or lost); <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gametophytes" title="Gametophytes">gametophytes</a> green, chordate, and surficial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smith-2006_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-2006-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Polypodiales may be regarded as one of the most <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolutionarily</a> advanced orders of monilophytes (ferns), based on recent genetic analysis. They arose and diversified about 100 million years ago, probably subsequent to the diversification of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angiosperm" title="Angiosperm">angiosperms</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Polypodiales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the molecular phylogenetic classification of Smith et al. in 2006, the Polypodiales were placed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leptosporangiate_ferns" title="Leptosporangiate ferns">leptosporangiate ferns</a>, class <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polypodiopsida" title="Polypodiopsida">Polypodiopsida</a>. Fifteen families, including members of the eupolypods, comprising two unranked clades, were recognized: <a href="/wiki/Lindsaeaceae" title="Lindsaeaceae">Lindsaeaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saccolomataceae" title="Saccolomataceae">Saccolomataceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dennstaedtiaceae" title="Dennstaedtiaceae">Dennstaedtiaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pteridaceae" title="Pteridaceae">Pteridaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eupolypods_I" title="Eupolypods I">eupolypods I</a> (<a href="/wiki/Aspleniaceae" title="Aspleniaceae">Aspleniaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thelypteridaceae" title="Thelypteridaceae">Thelypteridaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woodsiaceae" title="Woodsiaceae">Woodsiaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blechnaceae" title="Blechnaceae">Blechnaceae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Onocleaceae" title="Onocleaceae">Onocleaceae</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Eupolypods_II" title="Eupolypods II">eupolypods II</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dryopteridaceae" title="Dryopteridaceae">Dryopteridaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lomariopsidaceae" title="Lomariopsidaceae">Lomariopsidaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tectariaceae" title="Tectariaceae">Tectariaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oleandraceae" title="Oleandraceae">Oleandraceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Davalliaceae" title="Davalliaceae">Davalliaceae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Polypodiaceae" title="Polypodiaceae">Polypodiaceae</a>). While many of these families had previously been recognized with similar superscriptions, the authors noted that Dryopteridaceae was more narrowly bounded than in historical circumscriptions, which had included their Tectariaceae, Onocleaceae, and Woodsiaceae. The circumscription of Lomariopsidaceae changed dramatically, most historical genera of that family (except <i><a href="/wiki/Lomariopsis" title="Lomariopsis">Lomariopsis</a></i> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thysanosoria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thysanosoria (page does not exist)">Thysanosoria</a></i>) being moved to Dryopteridaceae, while <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclopeltis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cyclopeltis (page does not exist)">Cyclopeltis</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Nephrolepis" title="Nephrolepis">Nephrolepis</a></i> were added. Saccolomataceae were removed from the dennstaedtioids. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cystodium_(plant)" title="Cystodium (plant)">Cystodium</a></i> was tentatively placed in <a href="/wiki/Lindsaeaceae" title="Lindsaeaceae">Lindsaeaceae</a>, away from its historical position with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tree_fern" title="Tree fern">tree ferns</a>. Woodsiaceae was acknowledged to be of uncertain circumscription and perhaps paraphyletic; the inclusion of <i><a href="/wiki/Hypodematium" title="Hypodematium">Hypodematium</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Didymochlaena" title="Didymochlaena">Didymochlaena</a></i>, and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Leucostegia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Leucostegia (page does not exist)">Leucostegia</a></i> perhaps also rendering Dryopteridaceae paraphyletic. The <a href="/wiki/Grammitid" title="Grammitid">grammitids</a> were included in <a href="/wiki/Polypodiaceae" title="Polypodiaceae">Polypodiaceae</a> to render that family monophyletic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith-etal-2006_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-smith-etal-2006-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The linear sequence of Christenhusz et al. (2011), intended for compatibility with the classification of Chase and Reveal (2009)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-christenhusz-etal-2011_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-christenhusz-etal-2011-4">[4]</a></sup> which placed all land plants in Equisetopsida,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> reclassified Smith's Polypodiopsida as subclass <a href="/wiki/Polypodiidae_(plant)" title="Polypodiidae (plant)">Polypodiidae</a> and placed the Polypodiales there. This classification incorporated new phylogenetic evidence to make several changes at the familial level. <i><a href="/wiki/Lonchitis" title="Lonchitis">Lonchitis</a></i> and <i>Cystodium</i> were removed from the Lindsaeaceae and incorporated into new families, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lonchitidaceae" title="Lonchitidaceae">Lonchitidaceae</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cystodiaceae" title="Cystodiaceae">Cystodiaceae</a> respectively. Within eupolypods I, Woodsiaceae proved to be paraphyletic and was reduced to the genera <i>Cheilanthopsis</i>, <i>Hymenocystis</i>, and <i>Woodsia</i>, while the remainder of its genera were removed to <a href="/wiki/Cystopteridaceae" title="Cystopteridaceae">Cystopteridaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diplaziopsidaceae" title="Diplaziopsidaceae">Diplaziopsidaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhachidosoraceae" title="Rhachidosoraceae">Rhachidosoraceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Athyriaceae" title="Athyriaceae">Athyriaceae</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hemidictyaceae" title="Hemidictyaceae">Hemidictyaceae</a>. Within eupolypods II, <i><a href="/wiki/Nephrolepis" title="Nephrolepis">Nephrolepis</a></i> was placed in a new family, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nephrolepidaceae" title="Nephrolepidaceae">Nephrolepidaceae</a>, due to uncertainty in its phylogenetic placement, while <a href="/wiki/Hypodematiaceae" title="Hypodematiaceae">Hypodematiaceae</a> was split from Dryopteridaceae to contain the three problematic genera mentioned by Smith et al.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-christenhusz-etal-2011_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-christenhusz-etal-2011-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The classification of Christenhusz and Chase (2014) dramatically reduced the number of families recognized in the group by expanding the circumscription of Aspleniaceae and Dryopteridaceae to encompass all of eupolypods I and II, respectively. In the new Aspleniaceae, the former Cystopteridaceae, Rhachidosoraceae, Diplaziopsidaceae, Aspleniaceae, Thelypteridaceae, Woodsiaceae, Athyriaceae, and Blechnaceae became subfamilies Cystopteridoideae, Rhachidosoroideae, Diplaziopsidoideae, Asplenioideae, Thelypteridoideae, Woodsioideae, Athyrioideae, and Blechnoideae, respectively. The former Hemidictyaceae were included in the Asplenioideae, and the Onocleaceae in the Blechnoideae. In the new Polypodiaceae, the former Hypodematiaceae, Dryopteridaceae, Lomariopsidaceae, Tectariaceae, Oleandraceae, Davalliaceae, and Polypodiaceae became subfamilies Hypodematioideae, Dryopteridoideae, Lomariopsidoideae, Tectarioideae, Oleandroideae, Davallioideae, and Polypodioideae respectively. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Didymochlaena" title="Didymochlaena">Didymochlaena</a></i> was placed in its own subfamily, the Didymochlaenoideae.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-christenhusz-chase-2014_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-christenhusz-chase-2014-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The PPG I classification (2016) largely returned to the families set out by Christenhusz et al. in 2011. In lieu of the expansion of Aspleniaceae and Polypodiaceae, it divided Polypodiales into six suborders to allow taxonomic recognition of the eupolypod clades:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PPGI_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-PPGI-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Saccolomatineae includes the single family Saccolomataceae.</li>
<li>Lindsaeinae corresponds to the Lindseaceae of Smith et al., and includes the Cystodiaceae, Lindsaeaceae, and Lonchitidaceae. It is probably, but not certainly, monophyletic.</li>
<li>Pteridineae includes the single family Pteridaceae.</li>
<li>Dennstaedtiineae includes the single family Dennstaedtiaceae.</li>
<li>Aspleniinae (eupolypods I) includes the families Cystopteridaceae, Rhachidosoraceae, Diplaziopsidaceae, Aspleniaceae, Hemidictyaceae, Woodsiaceae, Onocleaceae, Blechnaceae, Athyriaceae, Thelypteridaceae, and the family <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Desmophlebiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Desmophlebiaceae (page does not exist)">Desmophlebiaceae</a>, containing the single genus <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Desmophlebium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Desmophlebium (page does not exist)">Desmophlebium</a></i>, separated from <i><a href="/wiki/Diplazium" title="Diplazium">Diplazium</a></i> in 2016.</li>
<li>Polypodiineae (eupolypods II) includes the families <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Didymochlaenaceae" title="Didymochlaenaceae">Didymochlaenaceae</a> (containing only <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Didymochlaena" title="Didymochlaena">Didymochlaena</a></i>), Hypodematiaceae, Dryopteridaceae, Nephrolepidaceae, Lomariopsidaceae, Tectariaceae, Oleandraceae, Davalliaceae, and Polypodiaceae.</li>
</ul>

<p>Now-obsolete families of Polypodiales include:</p>

<li>Drynariaceae - now in Polypodiaceae</li>
