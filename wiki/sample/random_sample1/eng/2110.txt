<p><b>Kosai</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">湖西市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kosai-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in far western <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka_Prefecture" title="Shizuoka Prefecture">Shizuoka Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of September 2015, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 58,608 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 677 persons per km². The total area was 85.65 square kilometres (33.07 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosai,_Shizuoka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kosai is located in the far southwest corner of Shizuoka Prefecture, bordered by <a href="/wiki/Aichi_Prefecture" title="Aichi Prefecture">Aichi Prefecture</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Hamana" title="Lake Hamana">Lake Hamana</a> to the east. The name of the city means "west of the lake", in reference to its location near Lake Hamana. The city is bordered to the south by the Enshū Gulf of <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. Due to its location, the warm <a href="/wiki/Kuroshio_Current" title="Kuroshio Current">Kuroshio Current</a> offshore provides for a temperate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_climate" title="Maritime climate">maritime climate</a> with hot, humid summers and mild, cool winters.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosai,_Shizuoka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, Kosai was <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%8D" title="Tenryō">tenryō</a></i> territory administered directly by the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>, and the location of a barrier gate regulating traffic on the old <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_(road)" title="Tōkaidō (road)">Tōkaidō</a> highway connecting <a href="/wiki/Edo" title="Edo">Edo</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Shukuba" title="Shukuba">post station</a> named <a href="/wiki/Shirasuka-juku" title="Shirasuka-juku">Shirasuka-juku</a> developed near the barrier, and this was the forerunner of modern Kosai. During the cadastral reform of the early <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a> in 1889, the area was reorganized into five villages within <a href="/wiki/Hamana_District,_Shizuoka" title="Hamana District, Shizuoka">Hamana District</a>, Shizuoka. Four of these villages (Washizu, Iride, Shirasuka, Chibata) merged to form the city of Kosai on January 1, 1972.</p>
<p>On March 23, 2010, the town of <a href="/wiki/Arai,_Shizuoka" title="Arai, Shizuoka">Arai</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Hamana_District,_Shizuoka" title="Hamana District, Shizuoka">Hamana District</a>) was merged into Kosai. Hamana District was dissolved as a result of this merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Plannedmerge_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Plannedmerge-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosai,_Shizuoka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Kosai is dominated by light manufacturing of automobile components and electronics, as well as <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">commercial fishing</a>. Kosai is home to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suzuki_Motor_Corporation" title="Suzuki Motor Corporation">Suzuki Motor Corporation</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Suzuki#Production_Facilities" title="Suzuki">Kosai Assembly Plant</a>, Suzuki's main domestic production plant as well as to plants owned by <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Panasonic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>. <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">Commercial fishing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aquaculture" title="Aquaculture">aquaculture</a> on <a href="/wiki/Lake_Hamana" title="Lake Hamana">Lake Hamana</a> is also a component of the city's economy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosai,_Shizuoka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kosai has six elementary schools, five middle schools and two high schools in addition to a special education school. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ENE_-_Escola_Nova_Era&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ENE - Escola Nova Era (page does not exist)">ENE - Escola Nova Era</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_schools_in_Japan" title="Brazilian schools in Japan">Brazilian international school</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brazilschools_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brazilschools-2">[2]</a></sup> is also located in Kosai.</p>
