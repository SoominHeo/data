<p>The <b>Gotham Independent Film Awards</b> are American film awards, presented annually to the makers of <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent films</a> at a ceremony in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, the city first <a href="/wiki/Nicknames_of_New_York_City" title="Nicknames of New York City">nicknamed</a> "Gotham" by native son <a href="/wiki/Washington_Irving" title="Washington Irving">Washington Irving</a>, in an issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Salmagundi_(periodical)" title="Salmagundi (periodical)">Salmagundi</a></i>, published on November 11, 1807.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sederstrom_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sederstrom-1">[1]</a></sup> Part of the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Filmmaker_Project" title="Independent Filmmaker Project">Independent Filmmaker Project</a> (IFP), "the largest membership organization in the United States dedicated to independent film" (founded in 1979), the awards were inaugurated in 1991 as a means of showcasing and honoring films made primarily in the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">northeastern region of the United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFPpr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IFPpr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope">Scope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gotham_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, the scope of the awards broadened to include the international film scene, when the number of awards presented increased from six awards –  given to films and those involved in making them primarily from the northeastern U.S. film community –  to nine awards, including in its broader scope films originating in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, and international locations as well.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Venue">Venue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gotham_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Venue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Having outgrown its previous locations in the city's <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> <a href="/wiki/Borough_(New_York_City)" title="Borough (New York City)">borough</a>, for the first time in its history, the 17th Annual Gotham Awards gala occurred outside of that borough, in the city's <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> borough at <a href="/wiki/Steiner_Studios" title="Steiner Studios">Steiner Studios</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Navy_Yard" title="Brooklyn Navy Yard">Brooklyn Navy Yard</a>, on November 27, 2007, and it was "promoted nationally via a partnership with <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> and locally via broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Nyctv" title="Nyctv">nyctv</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sederstrom_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sederstrom-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFPpr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-IFPpr-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDb17_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMDb17-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
