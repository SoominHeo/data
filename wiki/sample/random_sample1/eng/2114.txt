<p><b>Kosŏng Station</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> in Kosŏng-ŭp, <a href="/wiki/Kosong_County" title="Kosong County">Kosŏng</a> county, <a href="/wiki/Kangwon_Province_(North_Korea)" title="Kangwon Province (North Korea)">Kangwŏn</a> province, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> on the <a href="/wiki/Kumgangsan_Chongnyon_Line" title="Kumgangsan Chongnyon Line">Kŭmgangsan Ch'ŏngnyŏn Line</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_State_Railway" title="Korean State Railway">Korean State Railway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hayato_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hayato-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosong_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station, originally called <b>Changjŏn Station</b>, was opened on 1 August 1932 by the <a href="/wiki/Chosen_Government_Railway" title="Chosen Government Railway">Chosen Government Railway</a>, along with the rest of the fourth section of the original <a href="/wiki/Donghae_Bukbu_Line" title="Donghae Bukbu Line">Tonghae Pukpu Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/Tupo_Station" title="Tupo Station">Tup'o</a> to here.</p>
