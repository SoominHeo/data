<p>The <b>Amur hedgehog</b> (<i>Erinaceus amurensis</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Hedgehog" title="Hedgehog">hedgehog</a> similar to the <a href="/wiki/European_hedgehog" title="European hedgehog">European hedgehog</a> in appearance and behaviour, although it is more lightly coloured.It is native to <a href="/wiki/Amur_Oblast" title="Amur Oblast">Amur Oblast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primorsky_Krai" title="Primorsky Krai">Primorye</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Peninsula" title="Korean Peninsula">Korean Peninsula</a>. Like other hedgehogs, it use scent and hearing while looking for prey, and the name "hedgehog" refers to the pig-like grunts it makes as it forages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McMillan_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-McMillan-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amur_hedgehog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Amur hedgehog is closely related to and slightly larger than the <a href="/wiki/European_hedgehog" title="European hedgehog">European hedgehog</a>, with a head-and-body length of between 160 and 290 mm (6 and 11 in) and a very short tail. It weighs between 600 and 1,000 g (21 and 35 oz). The head, back and sides are covered with long, sharp spines. These are of two different colours; some are plain white; others have a white or yellowish-brown base and tip, and a central portion that is mid to dark brown, giving the animal an altogether pale, brownish-grey colour. The spines on the head are separated from each other by narrow strips of bare skin. The face and underparts are covered in pale hair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smith_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amur_hedgehog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Amur hedgehog is found in the <a href="/wiki/Amur_Oblast" title="Amur Oblast">Amur Oblast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primorsky_Krai" title="Primorsky Krai">Primorye</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Peninsula" title="Korean Peninsula">Korean Peninsula</a>. Its range extends from about 29°N, which is just south of the <a href="/wiki/Yangtze" title="Yangtze">Yangtze River</a> northwards to the <a href="/wiki/Amur_River" title="Amur River">Amur Basin</a> and the Korean Peninsula. It occupies a wide range of habitats including grassland, forest edges, and areas with mixed coniferous and broadleaf woodland. The <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a> states that it is found in valleys and lowlands but not in highland regions or cultivated fields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-2">[2]</a></sup> The <i>Mammals of China</i> disagrees, stating that suitable habitats include montane and sub-alpine terrain, steppe, shrublands, cultivated land, villages and city parks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smith_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amur_hedgehog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Amur hedgehog is nocturnal, emerging at night to forage for small arthropods, especially fly larvae, and earthworms, centipedes, snails, mice, frogs and occasionally fruit. Chinese sources state that this hedgehog is <a href="/wiki/Predation" title="Predation">preyed</a> on by <a href="/wiki/Sable" title="Sable">sable</a> (<i>Martes zibellina</i>), but Russian sources dispute this. There are one or two litters in the summer, each consisting of four to six young, and in about October, the hedgehog enters a state of <a href="/wiki/Torpor" title="Torpor">torpor</a> and hibernates until spring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McMillan_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-McMillan-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smith_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amur_hedgehog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Amur hedgehog is a common species and no specific threats have been identified, so the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has assessed its conservation status as being of "<a href="/wiki/Least-concern_species" title="Least-concern species">least concern</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
