<p><b>Koshigaya Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">越谷駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Koshigaya-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Skytree_Line" title="Tobu Skytree Line">Tobu Skytree Line</a> (Tobu Isesaki Line) in <a href="/wiki/Koshigaya,_Saitama" title="Koshigaya, Saitama">Koshigaya, Saitama</a>, Japan, operated by the <a href="/wiki/Private_railway" title="Private railway">private railway</a> operator <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Railway" title="Tobu Railway">Tobu Railway</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koshigaya_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Skytree_Line" title="Tobu Skytree Line">Tobu Skytree Line</a>, and is 24.4 kilometers from the terminus of the line at <a href="/wiki/Asakusa_Station_(Tokyo_Metro,_Toei,_Tobu)" title="Asakusa Station (Tokyo Metro, Toei, Tobu)">Asakusa Station</a> in Tokyo. Through services also operate to and from <a href="/wiki/Naka-Meguro_Station" title="Naka-Meguro Station">Naka-Meguro</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Hibiya_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Hibiya Line">Tokyo Metro Hibiya Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shibuya_Station" title="Shibuya Station">Shibuya</a> via the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Hanzomon_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Hanzomon Line">Tokyo Metro Hanzomon Line</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koshigaya_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has two elevated <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> with two tracks each, one for all-stations "Local" services and one for express/rapid services. The station building is located underneath the platforms. There are two additional tracks for non-stopping trains to pass this station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kawashima2010_Vol.12_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-kawashima2010_Vol.12-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Koshigaya Station opened on 17 April 1920, with the name originally written as "<span class="t_nihongo_kanji"><span lang="ja">越ヶ谷駅</span></span>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-2">[2]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a></i> characters for the station name were changed to "<span class="t_nihongo_kanji"><span lang="ja">越谷駅</span></span>" (with the pronunciation unchanged) on 1 December 1956.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-2">[2]</a></sup> The station was rebuilt with elevated platforms in 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 17 March 2012, station numbering was introduced on all Tobu lines, with Koshigaya Station becoming "TS-21".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobu20120209_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tobu20120209-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koshigaya_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2014, the station was used by an average of 48,128 passengers daily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobustats2014_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tobustats2014-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
