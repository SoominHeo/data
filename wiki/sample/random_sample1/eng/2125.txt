<p>The <b>tympanic cavity</b> is a small cavity surrounding the <a href="/wiki/Ossicles" title="Ossicles">bones</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_ear" title="Middle ear">middle ear</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tympanic_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On its lateral surface, it abuts the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/External_auditory_meatus" title="External auditory meatus">external auditory meatus</a> from which it is separated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_membrane" title="Tympanic membrane">tympanic membrane</a> (eardrum).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Walls">Walls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tympanic_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Walls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The tympanic cavity is bounded by:</p>
<ul>
<li>Facing the <a href="/wiki/Inner_ear" title="Inner ear">inner ear</a>, the <b>medial wall</b> (or <i>labyrinthic wall</i>, <i>labyrinthine wall</i>) is vertical, and has the <a href="/wiki/Oval_window" title="Oval window">oval window</a> and <a href="/wiki/Round_window" title="Round window">round window</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Promontory_of_tympanic_cavity" title="Promontory of tympanic cavity">promontory</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prominence_of_the_facial_canal" title="Prominence of the facial canal">prominence of the facial canal</a>.</li>
<li>Facing the <a href="/wiki/Outer_ear" title="Outer ear">outer ear</a>, the <b>lateral wall</b> (or <i>membranous wall</i>), is formed mainly by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_membrane" title="Tympanic membrane">tympanic membrane</a>, partly by the ring of bone into which this membrane is inserted. This ring of bone is incomplete at its upper part, forming a notch (notch of Rivinus), close to which are three small apertures: the "iter chordæ posterius", the <a href="/wiki/Petrotympanic_fissure" title="Petrotympanic fissure">petrotympanic fissure</a>, and the "iter chordæ anterius". The iter chordæ posterius (apertura tympanica canaliculi chordæ) is situated in the angle of junction between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mastoid_wall_of_tympanic_cavity" title="Mastoid wall of tympanic cavity">mastoid</a> and <i>membranous wall of tympanic cavity</i> immediately behind the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_membrane" title="Tympanic membrane">tympanic membrane</a> and on a level with the upper end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manubrium" title="Manubrium">manubrium</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Malleus" title="Malleus">malleus</a>; it leads into a minute canal, which descends in front of the canal for the <a href="/wiki/Facial_nerve" title="Facial nerve">facial nerve</a>, and ends in that canal near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stylo-mastoid_foramen" title="Stylo-mastoid foramen">stylo-mastoid foramen</a>. Through it the <a href="/wiki/Chorda_tympani" title="Chorda tympani">chorda tympani</a> nerve enters the tympanic cavity. The <a href="/wiki/Petrotympanic_fissure" title="Petrotympanic fissure">petrotympanic fissure</a> opens just above and in front of the ring of bone into which the tympanic membrane is inserted; in this situation it is a mere slit about 2 mm. in length. It lodges the anterior process and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anterior_ligament_of_the_malleus" title="Anterior ligament of the malleus">anterior ligament of the malleus</a>, and gives passage to the anterior <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_branch" title="Tympanic branch">tympanic branch</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internal_maxillary_artery" title="Internal maxillary artery">internal maxillary artery</a>. The iter chordæ anterius (canal of Huguier) is placed at the medial end of the petrotympanic fissure; through it the chorda tympani nerve leaves the tympanic cavity.</li>
<li>The <b>roof</b> of the cavity (also called the <i>tegmental wall</i>, <i>tegmental roof</i> or <i>tegmentum tympani</i>) is formed by a thin plate of bone, the tegmen tympani, which separates the <a href="/wiki/Cranial_cavity" title="Cranial cavity">cranial</a> and tympanic cavities. It is situated on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anterior" title="Anterior">anterior</a> (frontal) surface of the <a href="/wiki/Petrous_part_of_the_temporal_bone" title="Petrous part of the temporal bone">petrous portion</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Temporal_bone" title="Temporal bone">temporal bone</a> close to its angle of junction with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squama_temporalis" title="Squama temporalis">squama temporalis</a>; it is prolonged backward so as to roof in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_antrum" title="Tympanic antrum">tympanic antrum</a>, and forward to cover in the <a href="/wiki/Eustachian_tube" title="Eustachian tube">semicanal</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Tensor_tympani_muscle" title="Tensor tympani muscle">tensor tympani muscle</a>. Its lateral edge corresponds with the remains of the <a href="/wiki/Petrosquamous_suture" title="Petrosquamous suture">petrosquamous suture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <i>Atticus</i> is the part of the tegmentum tympani where the <a href="/wiki/Stapes" title="Stapes">stapes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Incus" title="Incus">incus</a> are attached.</li>
<li>The <b>floor</b> of the cavity (also called the <i>jugular wall</i>) is narrow, and consists of a thin plate of bone (fundus tympani) which separates the tympanic cavity from the jugular fossa. It presents, near the labyrinthic wall, a small aperture for the passage of the tympanic branch of the glossopharyngeal nerve.</li>
<li>The <b>posterior wall</b> (or <i>mastoid wall</i>) is wider above than below, and presents for examination the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entrance_to_the_tympanic_antrum" title="Entrance to the tympanic antrum">entrance to the tympanic antrum</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pyramidal_eminence" title="Pyramidal eminence">pyramidal eminence</a>, and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fossa_incudis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fossa incudis (page does not exist)">fossa incudis</a>.</li>
<li>The <b>anterior wall</b> (or <i>carotid wall</i>) is wider above than below; it corresponds with the <a href="/wiki/Carotid_canal" title="Carotid canal">carotid canal</a>, from which it is separated by a thin plate of bone perforated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_branch" title="Tympanic branch">tympanic branch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Internal_carotid_artery" title="Internal carotid artery">internal carotid artery</a>, and by the <a href="/wiki/Deep_petrosal_nerve" title="Deep petrosal nerve">deep petrosal nerve</a> which connects the sympathetic plexus on the internal carotid artery with the tympanic plexus on the promontory. At the upper part of the anterior wall are the orifice of the semicanal for the <a href="/wiki/Tensor_tympani_muscle" title="Tensor tympani muscle">Tensor tympani muscle</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tympanic_orifice_of_the_auditory_tube&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tympanic orifice of the auditory tube (page does not exist)">tympanic orifice of the auditory tube</a>, separated from each other by a thin horizontal plate of bone, the septum canalis musculotubarii. These canals run from the tympanic cavity forward and downward to the retiring angle between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squama_temporalis" title="Squama temporalis">squama</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrous_portion_of_the_temporal_bone" title="Petrous portion of the temporal bone">petrous portion of the temporal bone</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tympanic_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It is formed from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tubotympanic_recess&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tubotympanic recess (page does not exist)">tubotympanic recess</a>, an expansion of the first <a href="/wiki/Pharyngeal_pouch_(embryology)" title="Pharyngeal pouch (embryology)">pharyngeal pouch</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clinical_significance">Clinical significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tympanic_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Clinical significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>If damaged, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_membrane" title="Tympanic membrane">tympanic membrane</a> can be repaired in a procedure called <a href="/wiki/Tympanoplasty" title="Tympanoplasty">tympanoplasty</a>.</p>
<p>Should fluid accumulate within the middle ear as the result of infection or for some other reason, it can be drained by puncturing the tympanic membrane with a large bore needle (<a href="/wiki/Tympanocentesis" title="Tympanocentesis">tympanocentesis</a>).</p>
