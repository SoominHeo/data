<p><b>Emperor Go-En'yū</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">後円融天皇</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Go-En'yū-tennō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (11 January 1359 – 6 June 1393) was the "5th" of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Court_(Japan)" title="Northern Court (Japan)">Ashikaga Pretenders</a> during the period of two courts in Japan. According to pre-Meiji scholars, his reign spanned the years from 1371 through 1382.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanboku-ch%C5%8D" title="Nanboku-chō">Nanboku-chō</a> "sovereign" was named after the 10th century <a href="/wiki/Emperor_En%27y%C5%AB" title="Emperor En'yū">Emperor En'yū</a> and <i>go-</i> (後), translates literally as "later;" and thus, he may be called the "Later Emperor En'yū", or, in some older sources, may be identified as "Emperor En'yū, the second", or as "Emperor En'yū II."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Genealogy">Genealogy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-En%27y%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Genealogy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His personal name was Ohito (緒仁).</p>
<p>He was the second son of the fourth Northern Pretender <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-K%C5%8Dgon" title="Emperor Go-Kōgon">Emperor Go-Kōgon</a>. His mother was Fujiwara no Nakako (藤原仲子), daughter of Hirohashi Kanetsuna (広橋兼綱).</p>
<ul>
<li>Consort: Itsuko (厳子), daughter of Sanjō Kintada (三条公忠)
<ul>
<li>First son: Imperial Prince Motohito (幹仁親王) (<a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Komatsu" title="Emperor Go-Komatsu">Emperor Go-Komatsu</a>)</li>
<li>First daughter: Imperial Princess Keiko (珪子内親王)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Lady-in-waiting Fujiwara no Imako (藤原今子)
<ul>
<li>Second son: Imperial Prince Dōchō (道朝法親王)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_of_Go-En.27y.C5.AB.27s_life">Events of Go-En'yū's life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-En%27y%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events of Go-En'yū's life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his own lifetime, Go-En'yū and those around him believed that he occupied the <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum_Throne" title="Chrysanthemum Throne">Chrysanthemum Throne</a> from 9 April 1371 to 24 May 1382.</p>
<p>In 1371, by Imperial Proclamation, he received the rank of <i>shinnō</i> (親王), or Imperial Prince (and potential heir). Immediately afterwards, he became emperor upon the abdication of his father, <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-K%C5%8Dgon" title="Emperor Go-Kōgon">Emperor Go-Kōgon</a>. There was said to be a disagreement between Go-Kōgon and the retired Northern Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Suk%C5%8D" title="Emperor Sukō">Emperor Sukō</a> over the Crown Prince. With the support of Hosokawa Yoriyuki, who controlled the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shogunate" title="Shogunate">Bakufu</a>, Go-Kōgon's son became the Northern Emperor.</p>
<p>Until 1374, Go-Kōgon ruled as <a href="/wiki/Cloistered_rule" title="Cloistered rule">cloistered emperor</a>. In 1368, <a href="/wiki/Ashikaga_Yoshimitsu" title="Ashikaga Yoshimitsu">Ashikaga Yoshimitsu</a> was named <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">Shōgun</a>, and with his guardianship, the Imperial Court was stabilized. In 1382, upon abdicating to <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Komatsu" title="Emperor Go-Komatsu">Emperor Go-Komatsu</a>, his <a href="/wiki/Cloistered_rule" title="Cloistered rule">cloistered rule</a> began. Having no actual power, he rebelled, attempting suicide and accusing <a href="/wiki/Ashikaga_Yoshimitsu" title="Ashikaga Yoshimitsu">Ashikaga Yoshimitsu</a> and his consort Itsuko of adultery.</p>
<p>In 1392, peace with the Southern Court being concluded, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanboku-cho" title="Nanboku-cho">Period of the Northern and Southern Courts</a> came to an end. On 6 June 1393, Go-En'yū died. He is enshrined with other emperors at the imperial tomb called <i>Fukakusa no kita no misasagi</i> (深草北陵) in <a href="/wiki/Fushimi-ku,_Kyoto" title="Fushimi-ku, Kyoto">Fushimi-ku, Kyoto</a>.</p>
