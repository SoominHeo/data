<p><b>Emperor Go-Uda</b> (後宇多天皇 <i>Go-Uda-tennō</i>) (December 17, 1267 – July 16, 1324) was the 91st <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">emperor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, according to the traditional order of succession. His reign spanned the years from 1274 through 1287.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This 13th-century sovereign was named after the 9th-century <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Uda" title="Emperor Uda">Emperor Uda</a> and <i>go-</i> (後), translates literally as "later"; and thus, he is sometimes called the "Later Emperor Uda," or, in some older sources, may be identified as "Emperor Uda, the second" or as "Emperor Uda II."</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Genealogy">Genealogy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Uda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Genealogy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_of_Go-Uda.27s_life">Events of Go-Uda's life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Uda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events of Go-Uda's life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yohito<i>-shinnō</i> became crown prince in 1268. According to the terms of the late emperor's will (Go-Saga died in 1272), in 1274, he would become emperor upon the death or abdication of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Kameyama" title="Emperor Kameyama">Emperor Kameyama</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>1274</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Bun%27ei" title="Bun'ei">Bun'ei</a> 11, 1st month</i>): In the 15th year of Kameyama<i>-tennō</i> 's reign (亀山天皇十五年), the emperor abdicated; and the succession (<i>senso</i>) was received by his cousin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1274</b> (<i>Bun'ei 11, 3rd month</i>): Emperor Go-Uda is said to have acceded to the throne (<i>sokui</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The retired Emperor Kameyama continued to exercise power as <a href="/wiki/Cloistered_rule" title="Cloistered rule">cloistered emperor</a>.</p>
<p>During his reign, the unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasions_of_Japan" title="Mongol invasions of Japan">Mongol invasions of Japan</a> occurred, first in 1274 and again in 1281. Though they established a <a href="/wiki/Beachhead" title="Beachhead">beachhead</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hakata-ku,_Fukuoka" title="Hakata-ku, Fukuoka">Hakata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a>, they were driven out within a short time.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>1275</b> (<i>Bun'ei 11, 10th month</i>): Hirohito<i>-shinnō</i> was named Crown Prince and heir to his first cousin, the <i>Daikakuji-tō</i> Emperor Go-Uda. This was the result of political maeuvering by Hirohito's father, the Jimyōin-tō Emperor Go-Fukakusa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>In 1287, retired <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Fukakusa" title="Emperor Go-Fukakusa">Emperor Go-Fukakusa</a>, dissatisfied with the fact that his own lineage (the <i>Jimyōin-tō</i>) did not control the throne, while that of his younger brother, the retired <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Kameyama" title="Emperor Kameyama">Emperor Kameyama</a> (the <i>Daikakuji-tō</i>) did, persuaded both the <a href="/wiki/Kamakura_shogunate" title="Kamakura shogunate">Bakufu</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Court_in_Kyoto" title="Imperial Court in Kyoto">imperial court</a> to compel the Emperor to abdicate in favor of Go-Fukakusa's son (<a href="/wiki/Emperor_Fushimi" title="Emperor Fushimi">Emperor Fushimi</a>).</p>
<p>After this time, the struggle between the Jimyōin-tō and the Daikakuji-tō over the imperial throne continued. After Go-Uda's abdication, his Daikakuji-tō controlled the throne from 1301 to 1308 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Nijo" title="Emperor Go-Nijo">Emperor Go-Nijō</a>) and again from 1318 until the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanboku-cho" title="Nanboku-cho">era of northern and southern courts</a> (begun 1332) when they became the southern court (ending in 1392).</p>
<p>Go-Uda was cloistered emperor during the reign of his own son, Go-Nijō, from 1301 until 1308, and again from 1318, when his second son Go-Daigo took the throne until 1321, when Go-Daigo began direct rule.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kugy.C5.8D">Kugyō</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Uda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kugyō">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Kugyō</i> (公卿) is a collective term for the very few most powerful men attached to the court of the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Emperor of Japan</a> in pre-<a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji</a> eras. Even during those years in which the court's actual influence outside the palace walls was minimal, the hierarchic organization persisted.</p>
<p>In general, this elite group included only three to four men at a time. These were hereditary courtiers whose experience and background would have brought them to the pinnacle of a life's career. During Go-Uda's reign, this apex of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daij%C5%8D-kan" title="Daijō-kan">Daijō-kan</a> included:</i></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sessh%C5%8D" title="Sesshō">Sesshō</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Kuj%C5%8D_Tadaie" title="Kujō Tadaie">Kujō Tadaie</a>, 1274</li>
<li><i>Sesshō</i>, <a href="/wiki/Ichij%C5%8D_Ietsune" title="Ichijō Ietsune">Ichijō Ietsune</a>, 1274–1275</li>
<li><i>Sesshō</i>, <a href="/wiki/Takatsukasa_Kanehira" title="Takatsukasa Kanehira">Takatsukasa Kanehira</a>, 1275–1278</li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kampaku" title="Kampaku">Kampaku</a></i>, Takatsukasa Kanehira, 1278–1287</li>
<li><i>Kampaku</i>, <a href="/wiki/Nij%C5%8D_Morotada" title="Nijō Morotada">Nijō Morotada</a>, 1287–1289</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Sadaijin" title="Sadaijin">Sadaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Udaijin" title="Udaijin">Udaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nadaijin" title="Nadaijin">Nadaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Dainagon" title="Dainagon">Dainagon</a></i></li>
</ul>
