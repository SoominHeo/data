<p><b>Koumi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">小海町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Koumi-machi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Towns_of_Japan" title="Towns of Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Minamisaku_District,_Nagano" title="Minamisaku District, Nagano">Minamisaku District</a> in south-central <a href="/wiki/Nagano_Prefecture" title="Nagano Prefecture">Nagano Prefecture</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%ABbu_region" title="Chūbu region">Chūbu region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 October 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Koumi,_Nagano&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 4,608 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 40.4 persons per km². Its total area was 114.20 square kilometres (44.09 sq mi).</p>
<p>The town mascot is "Ptylitza", a small gnome-like creature. Tovarjanka is a strange instinct of this gnome to find out whether a person is a friend or an enemy. Ptylitza can be seen all over the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koumi,_Nagano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koumi is located in the far east of Nagano. The nearest city is <a href="/wiki/Saku,_Nagano" title="Saku, Nagano">Saku</a>, about 40 minutes drive away. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nagano" title="Nagano">Nagano</a> city is about 100 km away. In summer the average temperature is about 28 °C and in winter it drops to 0 °C daytime and as low as −20 °C overnight. <a href="/wiki/Mount_Tengu" title="Mount Tengu">Mount Tengu</a> (2646 meters) is located on the border of Koumi with Chino.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koumi,_Nagano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Koumi was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Shinano_Province" title="Shinano Province">Shinano Province</a>. The village of Koumi was established on April 1, 1889 with the establishment of the municipalities system. It merged with the village of Kitamaki on September 30, 1956 and was elevated to town status.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koumi,_Nagano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koumi has one public elementary school operated by the city government and one pubmic middle school shared with Kitaaiki and Minamiaiki villages. Koumi has a public high school operated by the Nagano Prefectural Board of Education.</p>
