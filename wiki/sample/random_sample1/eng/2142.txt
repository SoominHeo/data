<p><b>Emperor Go-Ichijō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">後一条天皇</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Go-Ichijō-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenn%C5%8D" title="Tennō">tennō</a></i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, October 12, 1008 – May 15, 1036)</span> was the 68th <a href="/wiki/Emperor" title="Emperor">emperor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kunaicho_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-kunaicho-1">[1]</a></sup> according to the traditional <a href="/wiki/List_of_Emperors_of_Japan" title="List of Emperors of Japan">order of succession</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Go-Ichijō's reign spanned the years from 1016 through 1036.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>This 11th century sovereign was named after <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ichij%C5%8D" title="Emperor Ichijō">Emperor Ichijō</a> and <i>go-</i> (後), translates literally as "later;" and thus, he is sometimes called the "Later Emperor Ichijō", or, in some older sources, may be identified as " Emperor Ichijō, the second."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditional_narrative">Traditional narrative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Ichij%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Traditional narrative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before his ascension to the <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum_Throne" title="Chrysanthemum Throne">Chrysanthemum Throne</a>, his personal name (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imina" title="Imina">imina</a></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> was <b>Atsuhira</b> <i>-shinnō</i> (敦成親王).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was also known as Atsunari<i>-shinnō</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Atsuhira was the second son of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ichij%C5%8D" title="Emperor Ichijō">Emperor Ichijō</a>. His mother, Fujiwara no Akiko/<a href="/wiki/Empress_Sh%C5%8Dshi" title="Empress Shōshi">Shōshi</a> (藤原彰子) (988–1074), was a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Fujiwara_no_Michinaga" title="Fujiwara no Michinaga">Fujiwara no Michinaga</a>. In her later years, Ichijō's <i>chūgo</i> consort was known as Jōtō-mon In (上東門院).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_of_Go-Ichij.C5.8D.27s_life">Events of Go-Ichijō's life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Ichij%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events of Go-Ichijō's life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Atsuhira<i>-shinnō</i> was used as a pawn in Imperial court politics when he was only a child.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>1012</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dwa" title="Chōwa">Chōwa</a> 1, 8th month</i>): Prince Atsuhira marries a daughter of <i>sesshō</i> and later <i>kampaku</i> <a href="/wiki/Fujiwara_no_Michinaga" title="Fujiwara no Michinaga">Fujiwara no Michinaga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Atsuhira became emperor at the age of 8, upon the abdication of his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_cousin_once_removed" title="First cousin once removed">first cousin once removed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Sanj%C5%8D" title="Emperor Sanjō">Emperor Sanjō</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>March 10, 1016</b> (<i>Chōwa 5, 29th day of the 1st month</i>): In the 5th year of Emperor Sanjō's reign (三条天皇五年), he abdicated; and the succession (‘‘senso’’) was received by a cousin. Shortly thereafter, Emperor Go-Ichijō is said to have acceded to the throne (‘‘sokui’’).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>During the initial years of Go-Ichijō's reign, <a href="/wiki/Fujiwara_no_Michinaga" title="Fujiwara no Michinaga">Fujiwara no Michinaga</a> actually ruled from his position as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sessh%C5%8D" title="Sesshō">sesshō</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><b>June 5, 1017</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Kannin" title="Kannin">Kannin</a> 1, 9th day of the 5th month</i>): The former-Emperor Sanjō died at the age of 41.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b310_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-b310-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1017</b> (<i>Kannin 1, 8th month</i>): Prince Atsuakira, the eldest son of Emperor Sanjo, had been named Crown Prince. But after he is struck by a skin disease and intense pressure from Michinaga; he withdrew from this role and his younger brother, Prince Atsunaga, was named Crown Prince in his place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1017</b> (<i>Kannin 1, 9th month</i>): Michinaga made a pilgrimage to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iwashimizu_Shrine" title="Iwashimizu Shrine">Iwashimizu Shrine</a> accompanied by many courtiers. The travelers divided themselves amongst 15 boats for a floating trip down the Yotogawa River. One of the vessels overturned, and more than 30 people lost their lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-t157_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-t157-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1017</b> (<i>Kannin 1, 12th month</i>): Michinaga was elevated to the office of <i>Daijō-Diajin</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-t157_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-t157-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>May 15, 1036</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dgen" title="Chōgen">Chōgen</a> 9, 17th day of the 4th month</i>): Emperor Go-Ichijō died at the age of 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b310_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-b310-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The actual site of Go-Ichijō's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grave_(burial)" title="Grave (burial)">grave</a> is known.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kunaicho_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-kunaicho-1">[1]</a></sup> This emperor is traditionally venerated at a <a href="/wiki/Memorial" title="Memorial">memorial</a> <a href="/wiki/Shinto" title="Shinto">Shinto</a> <a href="/wiki/Shrine" title="Shrine">shrine</a> (<i>misasagi</i>) at Kyoto.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Agency" title="Imperial Household Agency">Imperial Household Agency</a> designates this location as Go-Ichijō's <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleum</a>. It is formally named <i>Bodaijuin no misasagi</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kugy.C5.8D">Kugyō</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Ichij%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kugyō">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Kugy%C5%8D" title="Kugyō">Kugyō</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">公卿</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a collective term for the very few most powerful men attached to the court of the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Emperor of Japan</a> in pre-<a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji</a> eras. Even during those years in which the court's actual influence outside the palace walls was minimal, the hierarchic organization persisted.</p>
<p>In general, this elite group included only three to four men at a time. These were hereditary courtiers whose experience and background would have brought them to the pinnacle of a life's career. During Go-Ichijō's reign, this apex of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daij%C5%8D-kan" title="Daijō-kan">Daijō-kan</a> included:</i></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sessh%C5%8D" title="Sesshō">Sesshō</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujiwara_Michinaga" title="Fujiwara Michinaga">Fujiwara Michinaga</a>, 966–1027.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b308-309_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-b308-309-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sessh%C5%8D" title="Sesshō">Sesshō</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujiwara_Yorimichi" title="Fujiwara Yorimichi">Fujiwara Yorimichi</a>, 992–1074.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b309_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-b309-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kampaku" title="Kampaku">Kampaku</a></i>, Fujiwara Yorimichi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b309_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-b309-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Daij%C5%8D-daijin" title="Daijō-daijin">Daijō-daijin</a></i>, Fujiwara Michinaga.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b308-309_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-b308-309-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Daij%C5%8D-daijin" title="Daijō-daijin">Daijō-daijin</a></i>, Kan'in Kinsue, 956–1029.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b309_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-b309-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Sadaijin" title="Sadaijin">Sadaijin</a></i>, Fujiwara Michinaga.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b308-309_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-b308-309-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Sadaijin" title="Sadaijin">Sadaijin</a></i>, Fujiwara Akimitsu, 944–1021.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b309_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-b309-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Sadaijin" title="Sadaijin">Sadaijin</a></i>, Fujiwara Yorimichi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b309_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-b309-16">[16]</a></sup></li>

<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nadaijin" title="Nadaijin">Nadaijin</a></i>, Fujiwara Norimichi, 997–1075.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b309_16-6"><a href="#cite_note-b309-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Dainagon" title="Dainagon">Dainagon</a></i></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eras_of_Go-Ichij.C5.8D.27s_reign">Eras of Go-Ichijō's reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Ichij%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Eras of Go-Ichijō's reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The years of Go-Ichijō's reign are more specifically identified by more than one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_era_names" title="Japanese era names">era name</a> or <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neng%C5%8D" title="Nengō">nengō</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<ul>

<p>Go-Ichijō had one Empress and two Imperial daughters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b310_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-b310-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Empress (<i>chūgū</i>): <a href="/wiki/Fujiwara_clan" title="Fujiwara clan">Fujiwara</a> no <i><a href="/wiki/Fujiwara_no_Ishi" title="Fujiwara no Ishi">Ishi</a></i> (藤原威子) (999–1036), third daughter of Fujiwara no Michinaga</p>
<ul>
<li>Imperial Princess Akiko/<i>Shōshi</i> (章子内親王) (Nijō-In, 二条院) (1026–1105), Empress (<i>chūgū</i>) to <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Reizei" title="Emperor Go-Reizei">Emperor Go-Reizei</a></li>
<li>Imperial Princess Kaoruko/<i>Keishi</i> (馨子内親王) (1029–1093), Empress (<i>chūgū</i>) to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Sanjo" title="Emperor Go-Sanjo">Emperor Go-Sanjō</a></li>
</ul>
