<p><b>Ko Jin-young</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">고진영</span>, born 7 July 1995), also known as <b>Jin-Young Ko</b> and <b>J.Y. Ko</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golfer</a> who plays on the <a href="/wiki/LPGA_of_Korea_Tour" title="LPGA of Korea Tour">LPGA of Korea Tour</a>. She has won four times on the LPGA of Korea and has featured inside the top 30 in the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_World_Golf_Rankings" title="Women's World Golf Rankings">Women's World Golf Rankings</a>. Ko is best known for finishing runner up to fellow South Korean golfer <a href="/wiki/Inbee_Park" title="Inbee Park">Inbee Park</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2015_Women%27s_British_Open" title="2015 Women's British Open">2015 Women's British Open</a>, after losing a three stroke lead on the back 9 at the Ailsa Course of <a href="/wiki/Turnberry_(golf_course)" title="Turnberry (golf course)">Trump Turnberry</a>. Ko famously only met her caddie, a young local man, the day before the Open and had never played links golf before.</p>
