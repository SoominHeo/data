<p>The <b>Soviet occupation of the Baltic states</b> covers the period from the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltic_States" title="Baltic States">Baltic</a> mutual assistance pacts in 1939, to their invasion and <a href="/wiki/Annexation" title="Annexation">annexation</a> in 1940, to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mass_deportation" title="Mass deportation">mass deportations</a> of 1941.</p>
<p>In September and October 1939 the Soviet government compelled the much smaller Baltic states to conclude mutual assistance pacts which gave the Soviets the right to establish <a href="/wiki/Military_base" title="Military base">military bases</a> there. Following invasion by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> in the summer of 1940, Soviet authorities compelled the Baltic governments to resign. The presidents of <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> were imprisoned and later died in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. Under Soviet supervision, new puppet communist governments and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellow_traveler" title="Fellow traveler">fellow travelers</a> arranged <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rigged_election" title="Rigged election">rigged elections</a> with falsified results. Shortly thereafter, the newly elected "people's assemblies" passed resolutions requesting admission into the Soviet Union. In June 1941 the new Soviet governments carried out mass deportations of "<a href="/wiki/Enemy_of_the_people" title="Enemy of the people">enemies of the people</a>". Consequently, at first many Balts greeted the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Germans</a> as liberators when they <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occupation_of_the_Baltic_states_by_Nazi_Germany" title="Occupation of the Baltic states by Nazi Germany">occupied the area</a> a week later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GernerHedlund-b1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-GernerHedlund-b1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_occupation_of_the_Baltic_states_(1940)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Background_of_the_occupation_of_the_Baltic_states" title="Background of the occupation of the Baltic states">Background of the occupation of the Baltic states</a></div>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_invasion_of_Poland" title="Soviet invasion of Poland">Soviet invasion of Poland</a> on 17 September 1939, in accordance with the <a href="/wiki/Molotov%E2%80%93Ribbentrop_Pact" title="Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact">Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact</a> the Soviet forces were given freedom over Latvia, Lithuania and Estonia, an important aspect of the agreement to the Soviet government as they were afraid of Germany using the three states as a corridor to get close to Leningrad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PB_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-PB-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:31</sup> the Soviets pressured Finland and the Baltic states to conclude mutual assistance treaties. The Soviets questioned the neutrality of Estonia following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orzel_incident" title="Orzel incident">escape of a Polish submarine</a> from <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a> on 18 September. A week after on 24 September, the Estonian foreign minister was given an <a href="/wiki/Ultimatum" title="Ultimatum">ultimatum</a> in Moscow. The Soviets demanded the conclusion of a treaty of mutual assistance to establish military bases in Estonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Salmon30_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Salmon30-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Baltic_States_Page_24_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Baltic_States_Page_24-4">[4]</a></sup> The Estonians had no choice but to allow the establishment of Soviet naval, air and army bases on two Estonian islands and at the port of <a href="/wiki/Paldiski" title="Paldiski">Paldiski</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Salmon30_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Salmon30-3">[3]</a></sup> The corresponding agreement was signed on 28 September 1939. Latvia followed on 5 October 1939 and Lithuania shortly thereafter, on 10 October 1939. The agreements permitted the Soviet Union to establish military bases on the Baltic states' territory for the duration of the European war<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Baltic_States_Page_24_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Baltic_States_Page_24-4">[4]</a></sup> and station 25,000 Soviet soldiers in <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, 30,000 in <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> and 20,000 in <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> from October 1939.</p>
<p>In 1939 <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> had <a href="/wiki/Background_of_the_Winter_War" title="Background of the Winter War">rejected similar Soviet demands</a> for Finland ceding or leasing parts of its territory. Consequently, the Soviet Union attacked Finland, starting the <a href="/wiki/Winter_War" title="Winter War">Winter War</a> in November. The war ended in March 1940 with Finnish territorial losses exceeding the pre-war Soviet demands, but Finland kept its <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a>. The Baltic states were neutral in the Winter War and the Soviets praised their relations with the USSR as exemplary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Malksoo-1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Malksoo-1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_occupation">Soviet occupation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_occupation_of_the_Baltic_states_(1940)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Soviet occupation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_military_plans">Soviet military plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_occupation_of_the_Baltic_states_(1940)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Soviet military plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SovietBlockade1940.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1052" data-file-width="1452" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/SovietBlockade1940.jpg/220px-SovietBlockade1940.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/SovietBlockade1940.jpg/330px-SovietBlockade1940.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/SovietBlockade1940.jpg/440px-SovietBlockade1940.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SovietBlockade1940.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Schematics of the Soviet military blockade and invasion of Estonia and Latvia in 1940 (Russian State Naval Archives)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Soviet troops allocated for possible military actions against the Baltic states numbered 435,000 troops, around 8,000 guns and mortars, over 3,000 tanks, and over 500 armoured cars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 3 June 1940 all Soviet military forces based in Baltic states were concentrated under the command of <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Loktionov" title="Aleksandr Loktionov">Aleksandr Loktionov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 9 June the directive 02622ss/ov was given to the Red Army's <a href="/wiki/Leningrad_Military_District" title="Leningrad Military District">Leningrad Military District</a> by <a href="/wiki/Semyon_Timoshenko" title="Semyon Timoshenko">Semyon Timoshenko</a> to be ready by 12 June to a) capture the vessels of the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Navy" title="Estonian Navy">Estonian</a>, Latvian and Lithuanian navies in their bases or at sea; b) capture the Estonian and Latvian commercial fleets and all other vessels; c) prepare for an invasion and landing in Tallinn and <a href="/wiki/Paldiski" title="Paldiski">Paldiski</a>; d) close the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Riga" title="Gulf of Riga">Gulf of Riga</a> and blockade the coasts of Estonia and Latvia in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Finland" title="Gulf of Finland">Gulf of Finland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a>; e) prevent an evacuation of the Estonian and Latvian governments, military forces and assets; f) provide naval support for an invasion towards <a href="/wiki/Rakvere" title="Rakvere">Rakvere</a>; and g) prevent Estonian and Latvian airplanes from flying either to Finland or Sweden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 June 1940, according to the director of the Russian State Archive of the Naval Department Pavel Petrov (C.Phil.) referring to the records in the archive,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> the Soviet <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Fleet" title="Baltic Fleet">Baltic Fleet</a> was ordered to implement a total military blockade of Estonia. On 13 June at 10:40 AM Soviet forces started to move to their positions and were ready by 14 June at 10 PM: Four submarines and a number of light navy units were positioned in the Baltic Sea, in the Gulfs of Riga and Finland to isolate the Baltic states by the sea; a navy squadron including three destroyer divisions was positioned to the west of <a href="/wiki/Naissaar" title="Naissaar">Naissaar</a> in order to support the invasion; the 1st marine brigade's four battalions were positioned on the transport ships <i>Sibir</i>, <i>2nd Pjatiletka</i> and <i>Elton</i> for landings on the islands Naissaare and <a href="/wiki/Aegna" title="Aegna">Aegna</a>; the transport ship <i>Dnester</i> and destroyers <i>Storozevoi</i> and <i>Silnoi</i> were positioned with troops for the invasion of the capital Tallinn; the 50th battalion was positioned on ships for an invasion near <a href="/wiki/Kunda,_Estonia" title="Kunda, Estonia">Kunda</a>. 120 Soviet vessels participated in the naval blockade, including one cruiser, seven destroyers, and seventeen submarines, along with 219 airplanes including the 8th air-brigade with 84 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DB-3" title="DB-3">DB-3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tupolev_SB" title="Tupolev SB">Tupolev SB</a> <a href="/wiki/Bomber" title="Bomber">bombers</a> and the 10th brigade with 62 airplanes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 June 1940 the Soviets <a href="/wiki/1940_Soviet_ultimatum_to_Lithuania" title="1940 Soviet ultimatum to Lithuania">issued an ultimatum to Lithuania</a>. The Soviet military blockade of Estonia went into effect while the world's attention was focused on the fall of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> to <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>. Two Soviet bombers downed the Finnish passenger airplane "<a href="/wiki/Kaleva_(airplane)" title="Kaleva (airplane)">Kaleva</a>" flying from Tallinn to Helsinki carrying three diplomatic pouches from the U.S. legations in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>. The US Foreign Service employee <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_W._Antheil,_Jr." title="Henry W. Antheil, Jr.">Henry W. Antheil, Jr.</a> was killed in the crash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Red_Army_invades">Red Army invades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_occupation_of_the_Baltic_states_(1940)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Red Army invades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vyacheslav_Molotov" title="Vyacheslav Molotov">Molotov</a> had accused the Baltic states of conspiracy against the Soviet Union and delivered an ultimatum to all Baltic countries for the establishment of Soviet-approved governments. Threatening invasion and accusing the three states of violating the original pacts as well as forming a conspiracy against the Soviet Union, Moscow presented ultimatums, demanding new concessions, which included the replacement of their governments and allowing an unlimited number of troops to enter the three countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Baltic governments had decided that, given their international isolation and the overwhelming Soviet forces on their borders and already on their territories, it was futile to actively resist and better to avoid bloodshed in an unwinnable war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The occupation of the Baltic states coincided with a communist <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> in each country, supported by the Soviet troops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 June the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a> invaded <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TM006241940_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-TM006241940-19">[19]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> troops attacked the <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> border guards at <a href="/wiki/Maslenki" title="Maslenki">Masļenki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OPL_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-OPL-20">[20]</a></sup> On 16 June 1940 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a> invaded <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TM006241940_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-TM006241940-19">[19]</a></sup> According to a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Magazine" title="Time Magazine">Time</a></i> magazine article published at the time of the invasions, in a matter of days around 500,000 Soviet <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> troops occupied the three <a href="/wiki/Baltic_states" title="Baltic states">Baltic states</a>—just one week before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fall_of_France" title="Fall of France">Fall of France</a> to <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hundreds of thousands Soviet troops entered Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> These additional Soviet military forces far outnumbered the armies of each country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Black_book_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Black_book-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estonian_Defence_Forces" title="Estonian Defence Forces">Estonian Defence Forces</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Defence_League" title="Estonian Defence League">Estonian Defence League</a> <a href="/wiki/Surrender_(military)" title="Surrender (military)">surrendered</a> according to the orders of the Estonian Government and were disarmed by the Red Army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Only the Estonian Independent Signal Battalion stationed in Tallinn at Raua Street showed resistance to the Red Army and "People's Self-Defence" Communist militia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> fighting the invading troops on 21 June 1940.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> As the Red Army brought in additional reinforcements supported by six <a href="/wiki/Armoured_fighting_vehicle" title="Armoured fighting vehicle">armoured fighting vehicles</a>, the battle lasted several hours until sundown. Finally the military resistance was ended with <a href="/wiki/Negotiation" title="Negotiation">negotiations</a> and the Independent Signal Battalion surrendered and was disarmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> There were two dead Estonian servicemen, Aleksei Männikus and Johannes Mandre, and several wounded on the Estonian side and about ten killed and more wounded on the Soviet side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The Soviet militia that participated in the battle was led by <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Stepulov" title="Nikolai Stepulov">Nikolai Stepulov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_Reaction">Western Reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_occupation_of_the_Baltic_states_(1940)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Western Reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winston Churchill, First Lord of the Admiralty at the time, said in his 1939 radio broadcast:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> That the Russian armies should stand on this line was clearly necessary for the safety of Russia against the Nazi menace. At any rate, the line is there, and an Eastern front has been created which Nazi Germany does not dare assail. When Herr von Ribbentrop was summoned to Moscow last week it was to learn the fact, and to accept the fact, that the Nazi designs upon the Baltic States and upon the Ukraine must come to a dead stop.</p>
<p>Governments in exile - with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legations" title="Legations">legations</a> in London - were recognised by a number of Western governments throughout the Cold War. With the reestablishment of independence by the Soviet Republics leaving the USSR, these governments in exile were integrated into the new governing establishments.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sovietization_of_the_Baltic_states">Sovietization of the Baltic states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_occupation_of_the_Baltic_states_(1940)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sovietization of the Baltic states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sovietization_of_the_Baltic_states" title="Sovietization of the Baltic states">Sovietization of the Baltic states</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg/250px-PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg/375px-PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg/500px-PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PlaqueMemorizingEstonianGovernment.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Plaque on the building of <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Estonia" title="Government of Estonia">Government of Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toompea" title="Toompea">Toompea</a>, commemorating government members killed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_terror" title="Communist terror">communist terror</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Political repressions followed with <a href="/wiki/June_deportation" title="June deportation">mass deportations</a> of around 130,000 citizens carried out by the Soviets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PB_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-PB-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:48</sup> The <a href="/wiki/Serov_Instructions" title="Serov Instructions">Serov Instructions</a>, <i>"On the Procedure for carrying out the Deportation of Anti-Soviet Elements from Lithuania, Latvia, and Estonia"</i>, contained detailed procedures and protocols to observe in the deportation of Baltic nationals.</p>
<p>The Soviets began a constitutional metamorphosis of the Baltic states by first forming transitional "People's Governments."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-misiunas20_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-misiunas20-34">[34]</a></sup> Led by Stalin’s close associates,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and local communist supporters as well as officials brought in from the Soviet Union, they forced the presidents and governments of all three countries to resign, replacing them with the provisional People's Governments.</p>
<p>On 14–15 July, following illegal amendments to the electoral laws of the respective states, rigged parliamentary elections for the "<a href="/wiki/People%27s_Parliament" title="People's Parliament">People's Parliaments</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-misiunas26_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-misiunas26-36">[36]</a></sup> were conducted by local Communists loyal to the Soviet Union. The laws were worded in such a way that the Communists and their allies were the only ones allowed to run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-misiunas26_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-misiunas26-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOTMSN_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-AOTMSN-37">[37]</a></sup> The election results were completely fabricated: the Soviet press service released them early, with the results having already appeared in print in a <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> newspaper a full 24 hours before the polls closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TSOL_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-TSOL-39">[39]</a></sup> The "People's Parliaments" met on 21 July, each with only one piece of business—a request to join the Soviet Union. These requests carried unanimously. In early August, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_the_USSR" title="Supreme Soviet of the USSR">Supreme Soviet of the USSR</a> "accepted" all three requests. The official Soviet line was that all three Baltic states carried out socialist revolutions and voluntarily requested to join the Soviet Union.</p>
<p>The new Soviet-installed governments in the Baltic states began to align their policies with current Soviet practices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oconnor117_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-oconnor117-40">[40]</a></sup> According to the prevailing doctrine in the process, the old "bourgeois" societies were destroyed so that new socialist societies, run by loyal Soviet citizens, could be constructed in their place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oconnor117_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-oconnor117-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
