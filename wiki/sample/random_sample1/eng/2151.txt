<p><b>Louis Koo</b> (<b>Koo Tin-lok</b> 古天樂; born 21 October 1970) is a Hong Kong film actor. He began his professional career as an actor in local television series, winning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">TVB</a>'s Best Actor award in 1999 and 2001. For the past decade, he has focused primarily on his film career. Koo has become one of the stalwarts of the <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Hong_Kong" title="Cinema of Hong Kong">Hong Kong film industry</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and is a popular spokesman for various brands including <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osim_International" title="Osim International">Osim</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tag_Heuer" title="Tag Heuer">Tag Heuer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lotte_(conglomerate)" title="Lotte (conglomerate)">Lotte</a>, Zero Eyewear, <a href="/wiki/Lay%27s" title="Lay's">Lay's</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy" title="Samsung Galaxy">Samsung Galaxy</a>.</p>
<p>Koo is the highest earning actor in Hong Kong with 236 million HKD (30.4 million USD) in 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 2014 with 300 million HKD (38.6 million USD) and 2015 with 400 million HKD (51.8 million USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Koo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koo left school after completing his secondary education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He worked in a modelling agency before he was approached to model for advertisements and karaoke videos. He then signed as a contract artist with TVB in 1993. During the period when he was under contract with TVB, he starred in many television dramas, movies and commercials. Koo started gaining recognition in 1995 with his leading role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Condor_Heroes_95" title="The Condor Heroes 95">The Condor Heroes 95</a></i>, a popular series based on a classic <a href="/wiki/Wuxia" title="Wuxia">wuxia</a> novel. In 1997, Koo began sporting an extreme tan (for which he is well known for), which apparently enabled him to take on more contemporary roles. He also began taking on roles in films such as the paranormal <i><a href="/wiki/Troublesome_Night" title="Troublesome Night">Troublesome Night</a></i> series, romantic film <i>Sealed with a Kiss</i> as well as action films such as <i>The Suspect</i>. In 1999, Koo received TVB's Most Popular Male Artist Award for <i><a href="/wiki/Detective_Investigation_Files_IV" title="Detective Investigation Files IV">Detective Investigation Files IV</a></i>. In 2001, Koo was given the award once again for <i><a href="/wiki/A_Step_into_the_Past" title="A Step into the Past">A Step into the Past</a></i> and also the Most Stylish Personality Award and Men of Power 2001. He then went on an indefinite hiatus from television and has since focused on his film career.</p>
<p>Koo is known for many of his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">TVB</a> television dramas, most notably <i><a href="/wiki/The_Condor_Heroes_95" title="The Condor Heroes 95">The Condor Heroes 95</a></i> (1995); <i><a href="/wiki/Detective_Investigation_Files_IV" title="Detective Investigation Files IV">Detective Investigation Files IV</a></i> (1999); <i><a href="/wiki/At_the_Threshold_of_an_Era" title="At the Threshold of an Era">At the Threshold of an Era</a> ll</i> (2000) and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Step_into_the_Past" title="A Step into the Past">A Step into the Past</a></i> (2001). Due to the popularity of TVB dramas in the late 1990s, Koo amassed a sizeable fan base in Hong Kong, Taiwan, China, South-east Asia and North America. While he had often been cast in cool, aloof characters, he took a different approach to his role in <i>A Step Into the Past</i>. This deviation from his usual aloof on-screen persona to a funnier, carefree and down-to-earth one resonated with the audience and widened his acting range, leading to many offers to star in romantic comedies. He began acting in a number of romantic comedies immediately after his departure from TVB, opposite actresses such as <a href="/wiki/Gigi_Leung" title="Gigi Leung">Gigi Leung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cecilia_Cheung" title="Cecilia Cheung">Cecilia Cheung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sammi_Cheng" title="Sammi Cheng">Sammi Cheng</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miriam_Yeung" title="Miriam Yeung">Miriam Yeung</a>.</p>
<p>In 2004, Koo took on the role of Sze-to Bo, a perpetually drunk and despondent former judo champion, in <a href="/wiki/Johnnie_To" title="Johnnie To">Johnnie To</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/Throw_Down_(film)" title="Throw Down (film)">Throw Down</a></i>; the movie was a marked departure from his usual commercial genres. He also began his long-term collaboration with To and <a href="/wiki/Milkyway_Image" title="Milkyway Image">Milkyway Image</a>. Koo later received international acclaim<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> for his chilling performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Election_2" title="Election 2">Election 2</a></i> (a.k.a. <i><a href="/wiki/Election_2" title="Election 2">Triad Election</a></i>), which was screened during the Out of Competition section at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2006 Cannes Film Festival">2006 Cannes Film Festival</a>.</p>
<p>By the mid-2000s, Louis Koo has become a widely known film actor in Greater China, often playing supporting roles to the biggest film stars in China. Koo played Octopus in <a href="/wiki/Benny_Chan_(film_director)" title="Benny Chan (film director)">Benny Chan</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/Rob-B-Hood" title="Rob-B-Hood">Robin B Hood</a></i> (2006) along with <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">Jackie Chan</a>. He had also worked with <a href="/wiki/Donnie_Yen" title="Donnie Yen">Donnie Yen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wilson_Yip" title="Wilson Yip">Wilson Yip</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Flash_Point_(film)" title="Flash Point (film)">Flash Point</a></i> (2007).</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_films_of_2007" title="Hong Kong films of 2007">2007 film</a>, <i>Happy Birthday</i>, differed from many of his past romantic movies due to its earnest tone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Jingle_Ma" title="Jingle Ma">Jingle Ma</a> film, which was based on co-star <a href="/wiki/Rene_Liu" title="Rene Liu">Rene Liu</a>'s short story,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Koo portrayed a sensitive man sans the comical gestures and expressions he has adopted in past <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy_film" title="Romantic comedy film">romantic comedies</a>.</p>
<p>His character as an unscrupulous drug addict in <i><a href="/wiki/Prot%C3%A9g%C3%A9_(film)" title="Protégé (film)">Protégé</a></i> differed from his previous performances, as he forwent his suave image for an emaciated look. The supporting role earned him positive critical feedback and several major film award nominations.</p>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Run_Papa_Run" title="Run Papa Run">Run Papa Run</a></i> (2008), Koo played a reckless triad leader who tried to shield his daughter from his past misdemeanours. Koo earned a Best Actor nomination in the Hong Kong Film Awards for this role, which required him to portray a character from his late teens to his fifties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the same year, he starred in the remake of <i><a href="/wiki/Cellular_(film)" title="Cellular (film)">Cellular</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Connected_(film)" title="Connected (film)">Connected</a></i>, in which he played an average Joe<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> cast in a race against time to save a stranger.</p>
<p>In recent years, Koo had often been cast in action films and psychological thrillers. In <i><a href="/wiki/Accident_(2009_film)" title="Accident (2009 film)">Accident</a></i>, Koo played a high-strung killer. In the <i><a href="/wiki/Overheard_(film)" title="Overheard (film)">Overheard</a></i> series, he put on weight to play middle-aged roles more convincingly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Koo made a return to his romantic comedy roots in the 2011 film <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Go_Breaking_My_Heart_(film)" title="Don't Go Breaking My Heart (film)">Don't Go Breaking My Heart</a></i>, which was directed by Johnnie To and Wai Ka-Fai. Koo played a debonair CEO of a broker film along with co-stars Daniel Wu and Gao Yuanyuan.</p>
<p>Koo received a Hong Kong Film Awards Best Actor nomination for his role in Benny Chan's 2013 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_White_Storm" title="The White Storm">The White Storm</a></i>. His upcoming films include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pang_Ho-Cheung" title="Pang Ho-Cheung">Pang Ho-Cheung</a>'s movie <i><a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_(2014_film)" title="Aberdeen (2014 film)">Aberdeen</a></i>, <i>Overheard 3</i> by directors Alan Mak and Felix Chong,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Johnnie_To" title="Johnnie To">Johnnie To</a>'s <i>Don't Go Breaking My Heart 2</i> and <i>Z Storm</i>. Koo is also attached to several upcoming projects including <i>Triumph in the Skies</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Monkey_King_(film)" title="The Monkey King (film)">The Monkey King</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>He won the award for Most Beloved Actor in the Hong Kong UA Film Awards 2006 through public voting for his role in <i>Election 2</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_works">Other works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Koo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Other works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koo has published several books over the years including his photo albums <i>Present</i> (1999) and <i>Cool</i> (2001), as well as his autobiography <i>Find Happiness</i> (2002).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He also released <i>Toys Battle</i> (2008), a book chronicling his love for comic book characters and movie superheroes, and donated all proceeds to UNICEF.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Koo recorded several theme songs for the TVB dramas he had starred in. He had also cut two albums <i>Boyfriend</i> and <i>Popular Louis</i> in 2000 and released another album <i>Mr.Cool</i> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>With a career spanning nearly two decades, Koo has endorsed a large number of brands in Hong Kong and Mainland China. Some of the major brand endorsements include, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gillette_(brand)" title="Gillette (brand)">Gillette</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epson" title="Epson">Epson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Boss" title="Hugo Boss">Hugo Boss</a> Perfume, <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Lotte" title="Lotte">Lotte</a><sup class="plainlinks noprint Inline-Template" style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="external text" href="http://dispenser.homenet.org/~dispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py?page=Louis_Koo&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn" rel="nofollow"><span title="Link needs disambiguation. (July 2016)">disambiguation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, Tag Heuer, Lipton, Clinique, OSIM,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Yokohama Tire,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Pepsi,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Lay's, Tropicana,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Samsung Galaxy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and Xbox 360 Kinect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity">Charity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Koo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Charity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 3 July 2012, Chinese microblogs caught on to the news of Koo's low profile philanthropy in China, which became a trending topic. Major Chinese dailies reported that he had helped build 49 schools, over 20 clinics and completed 50 water supply projects to help the underprivileged in remote areas in rural China for the past three years<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> without any publicity. He was reluctant to comment on the issue but it was learnt that Koo set up his charity foundation after witnessing the devastation from the Sichuan earthquake in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Koo is also a UNICEF ambassador<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> since 2007.</p>
