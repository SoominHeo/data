<p><b>Staphyleaceae</b> is a small family of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plants</a> in the order <a href="/wiki/Crossosomatales" title="Crossosomatales">Crossosomatales</a>, native to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> and also in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. The largest genus <i>Staphylea</i>, which gives the family its name, contains the "bladdernut" trees. The family includes two genera with ca 45 known species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christenhusz-Byng2016_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christenhusz-Byng2016-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Excluded genera</dt>
</dl>
<p>These two genera, formerly placed here, are now included in the <a href="/wiki/Tapisciaceae" title="Tapisciaceae">Tapisciaceae</a> (<a href="/wiki/Huerteales" title="Huerteales">Huerteales</a>) as of the <a href="/wiki/APG_III_system" title="APG III system">APG III system</a> (2009).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apgiii_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-apgiii-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
