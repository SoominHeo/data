<p><b>Kokura Domain</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">小倉藩</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kokura-han</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>', also known as "Kawara-<i>han</i>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">香春藩</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> or then "Toyotsu-<i>han</i>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">豊津藩</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> domain of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. It was associated with <a href="/wiki/Buzen_Province" title="Buzen Province">Buzen Province</a> in modern-day <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Prefecture" title="Fukuoka Prefecture">Fukuoka Prefecture</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Han_system" title="Han system">han system</a>, Kokura was a <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">political</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economic</a> abstraction based on periodic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cadastral" title="Cadastral">cadastral</a> surveys and projected agricultural yields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In other words, the domain was defined in terms of <i><a href="/wiki/Kokudaka" title="Kokudaka">kokudaka</a></i>, not land area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> This was different from the <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudalism</a> of the West.</p>
