<p><b>Kokurakita-ku</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">小倉北区</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_Japan" title="Wards of Japan">ward</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kitakyūshū">Kitakyūshū</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Prefecture" title="Fukuoka Prefecture">Fukuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is the north part of what used to be <a href="/wiki/Kokura" title="Kokura">Kokura</a> City before the merger of five cities to create the new city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kitakyūshū">Kitakyūshū</a> in 1963. JR Kyūshū's <a href="/wiki/Kokura_Station" title="Kokura Station">Kokura Station</a> is the main rail hub of Kitakyūshū, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sany%C5%8D_Shinkansen" title="Sanyō Shinkansen">Sanyō Shinkansen</a> stops there.</p>
<p>Population: 184,545 persons (2000 national census)<br/>
Area: 39.27 km²</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_.26_Commerce">Industry &amp; Commerce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kokurakita-ku,_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Industry &amp; Commerce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The largest commercial and shopping areas of Kitakyūshū, mainly in Uomachi (魚町　"fish town"), are clustered around <a href="/wiki/Kokura_Station" title="Kokura Station">Kokura Station</a>.</p>
<p>The two main department stores are Colet (next to Kokura Station) and Izutsuya (next to the Murasaki River and opposite <a href="/wiki/Riverwalk_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Riverwalk Kitakyūshū">Riverwalk Kitakyūshū</a>). The Tanga Market is a traditional open-air market where fresh fish, meat and vegetables are the main produce for sale.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kokurakita-ku,_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kokura_Castle" title="Kokura Castle">Kokura Castle</a> (built by the Hosokawa family in 1602) and the <a href="/wiki/Riverwalk_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Riverwalk Kitakyūshū">Riverwalk Kitakyūshū shopping complex</a> (completed in 2003) are about a ten-minute walk from JR Kokura Station. So also is the house once inhabited by <a href="/wiki/Mori_%C5%8Cgai" title="Mori Ōgai">Mori Ōgai</a> when he was a doctor to the Kokura garrison and wrote <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kokura_Nikki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kokura Nikki (page does not exist)">Kokura Nikki</a></i> ("Kokura Diary").</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB_Monorail" title="Kitakyūshū Monorail">Kitakyūshū Monorail</a> terminus is in Kokura Station and serves the commuters and residents living in Kokurakita and Kokuraminami Wards.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crime_and_safety">Crime and safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kokurakita-ku,_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Crime and safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kudo-kai" title="Kudo-kai">Kudo-kai</a> <a href="/wiki/Yakuza" title="Yakuza">yakuza</a> syndicate is headquartered in Kokurakita.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Yakuza#Designated_boryokudan" title="Yakuza">designated yakuza group</a>, the Kudo-kai is the largest yakuza syndicate in the Kitakyushu area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
