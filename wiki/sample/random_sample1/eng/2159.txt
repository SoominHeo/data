<p>The <b>Gotōji Line</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">後藤寺線</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gotōji-sen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese railway line in <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Prefecture" title="Fukuoka Prefecture">Fukuoka Prefecture</a> connecting <a href="/wiki/Tagawa-Got%C5%8Dji_Station" title="Tagawa-Gotōji Station">Tagawa-Gotōji Station</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Tagawa,_Fukuoka" title="Tagawa, Fukuoka">Tagawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shin-Iizuka_Station" title="Shin-Iizuka Station">Shin-Iizuka Station</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Iizuka,_Fukuoka" title="Iizuka, Fukuoka">Iizuka</a>. It is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JR_Kyushu" title="JR Kyushu">JR Kyushu</a> network.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Got%C5%8Dji_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The Hōshū Railway Co. opened the central section of the line in 1897 to haul freight, the company merging with the <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_Railway" title="Kyushu Railway">Kyushu Railway</a> Co. in 1901, which extended the line to Kami-Mio in 1902. That company was nationalised in 1909, and the line extended to Shin-Iizuka in 1920 with passenger services to Funao introduced at that time.</p>
<p>The Kyushu Industrial Railway Co. opened the Funao - Tagawa-Gotoji section in 1922 to service a cement plant. That company was nationalised in 1943, creating the current line. Passenger services were extended to Tagawa-Gotoji in 1945, and freight services ceased in 1987.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_connecting_lines">Former connecting lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Got%C5%8Dji_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Former connecting lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shimo-Kamoo station -</p>
<ul>
<li>A 1 km line to the Asakasa coal mine operated from 1926 until 1945.</li>
<li>The 8 km Urushio line to Shimo-Yamada (on the Kami-Yamada line, closed in 1986) opened between 1908 and 1913 as a freight line, with passenger services introduced in 1920. Freight services ceased in 1974, and the line closed in 1986.</li>
</ul>
