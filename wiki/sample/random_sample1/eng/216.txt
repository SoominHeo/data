<p><i><b>1943: The Battle of Midway</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">1943 ミッドウェイ海戦</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ichi Kyū Yon San: Middouei Kaisen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/1987_in_video_gaming" title="1987 in video gaming">1987</a> <a href="/wiki/Shoot_%27em_up" title="Shoot 'em up">shoot 'em up</a> <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> developed and published by <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was the first followup to Capcom's earlier <i><a href="/wiki/1942_(video_game)" title="1942 (video game)">1942</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1943:_The_Battle_of_Midway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:226px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1943_arcade.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="256" data-file-width="224" height="256" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/6f/1943_arcade.png" width="224"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1943_arcade.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Gameplay screenshot of <i>1943</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The game is set in the <a href="/wiki/Asiatic-Pacific_Theater" title="Asiatic-Pacific Theater">Pacific theater</a> of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, off the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Midway_Atoll" title="Midway Atoll">Midway Atoll</a>. The goal is to attack the Japanese air fleet that bombed the American aircraft carrier, pursue all Japanese air and sea forces, fly through the 16 <a href="/wiki/Level_(video_gaming)" title="Level (video gaming)">stages</a> of play, and make their way to the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Yamato" title="Japanese battleship Yamato">Japanese battleship Yamato</a> and destroy her. 11 of these stages consist of an air-to-sea battle (with a huge battleship or an aircraft carrier as the stage boss), while 5 stages consist of an all-aerial battle against a squadron of Japanese bombers with a mother bomber at the end.</p>
<p>As in <i><a href="/wiki/1942_(video_game)" title="1942 (video game)">1942</a></i>, players pilot a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P-38_Lightning" title="P-38 Lightning">P-38 Lightning</a>. Controls are also similar: button 1 fires main weapons, and button 2 performs two special actions: a loop maneuver like in <i><a href="/wiki/1942_(video_game)" title="1942 (video game)">1942</a></i>, or one of three special lightning attacks in exchange for some of the player's fuel. Indeed, players now have only one life, in the form of a large "fuel" meter; constantly depleting, but refillable by collecting various powerups (chiefly "Pow" icons). In 2-player mode, when both players overlap their planes on screen, the energy bar can be transferred from the player with more fuel to the player with less. Destroying a complete formation of red enemy planes will result in a <a href="/wiki/Power-up" title="Power-up">power-up</a>, such as a health boost or a new main weapon.</p>
<p>There are cheat codes, different for every stage, ranging from holding down a fire button or pointing the joystick in a certain direction; player(s) are rewarded with fully upgraded weapons.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ports">Ports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1943:_The_Battle_of_Midway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Capcom released their own port for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">NES</a>, but the game has also been ported to the <a href="/wiki/Atari_ST" title="Atari ST">Atari ST</a>, the <a href="/wiki/ZX_Spectrum" title="ZX Spectrum">ZX Spectrum</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Amstrad_CPC" title="Amstrad CPC">Amstrad CPC</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">Commodore 64</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Amiga" title="Amiga">Amiga</a>. In 1998 it was rereleased as <i><a href="/wiki/Capcom_Generations" title="Capcom Generations">Capcom Generation 1</a></i> for the Sega Saturn and the PlayStation. In <a href="/wiki/2005_in_video_gaming" title="2005 in video gaming">2005</a> it was re–released for <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a> and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> as part of <i><a href="/wiki/Capcom_Classics_Collection" title="Capcom Classics Collection">Capcom Classics Collection</a></i>, and again in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capcom_Classics_Collection:_Reloaded" title="Capcom Classics Collection: Reloaded">Capcom Classics Collection: Reloaded</a></i> on the PlayStation Portable. It also included as the initial game in <i>Capcom Arcade Cabinet</i>, a compilation of games released digitally for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> on February 19, 2013 in which the games are sold individually or in packs. The overall faithfulness and quality of execution of these third party versions varies greatly.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NES">NES</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1943:_The_Battle_of_Midway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: NES">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Released exactly one year after the arcade version, the NES version of <i>1943</i> introduced the ability to improve the player's plane by permanently upgrading certain aspects of its abilities. These include the plane's offensive and defensive powers, the maximum fuel level, and its special weapons and their durations. This somewhat alters the game balance and a different tactic is required to survive the game. For example, initially very few weapons are made available; more can be attained from power-ups by putting statistic points into "special weapons ability".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1943_Kai"><i>1943 Kai</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1943:_The_Battle_of_Midway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1943 Kai">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Developed and released alongside the Family Computer version, <i>1943 Kai: Midway Kaisen</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">1943改 ミッドウェイ海戦</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an arcade game, an "alternate" version of the original <i>1943</i>, released in 1987<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> only in Japan. Most of the graphics and sounds have been reworked, and the game has been made more "extreme" (laser-firing World War II planes and ships that run on ground). Further, the trademark <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P-38_Lightning" title="P-38 Lightning">P-38</a> has been replaced with a <a href="/wiki/Boeing-Stearman_Model_75" title="Boeing-Stearman Model 75">Boeing Stearman E75 N68828</a>.</p>
<p>In 1991, this version was converted to the <a href="/wiki/TurboGrafx-16" title="TurboGrafx-16">PC Engine</a> as simply <i>1943 Kai</i>, again exclusively in Japan; this version contains many additional levels and original music.</p>
<p>Although the arcade release was exclusive to Japan, the arcade version itself was included in the 1998 <i><a href="/wiki/Capcom_Generations" title="Capcom Generations">Capcom Generations</a></i> for the Sega Saturn and the PlayStation and in the 2005 <i><a href="/wiki/Capcom_Classics_Collection" title="Capcom Classics Collection">Capcom Classics Collection</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a>.</p>
