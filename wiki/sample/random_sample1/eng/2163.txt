<p><b>Goffredo Petrassi</b> (16 July 1904 – 3 March 2003) was an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_classical_music" title="Modern classical music">modern classical music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conducting" title="Conducting">conductor</a>, and teacher. He is considered one of the most influential Italian composers of the twentieth century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-britannica-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goffredo_Petrassi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Petrassi was born at <a href="/wiki/Zagarolo" title="Zagarolo">Zagarolo</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. At the age of 15 he began to work at a music shop to supply his family's financial needs, and became fascinated by music. In 1928, he entered the <a href="/wiki/Accademia_Nazionale_di_Santa_Cecilia" title="Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia">Santa Cecilia Conservatory</a> in Rome to study <a href="/wiki/Organ_(music)" title="Organ (music)">organ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musical_composition" title="Musical composition">composition</a>. In 1934, composer <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Casella" title="Alfredo Casella">Alfredo Casella</a> conducted Petrassi's <i>Partita</i> for orchestra at the ISCM festival in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.</p>
<p>Later, Petrassi became musical director of the opera house <a href="/wiki/La_Fenice" title="La Fenice">La Fenice</a>, and from 1959 taught composition at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Cecilia_Conservatory" title="Santa Cecilia Conservatory">Santa Cecilia Conservatory</a> and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salzburg_Mozarteum" title="Salzburg Mozarteum">Salzburg Mozarteum</a>. Petrassi had many famous students, including <a href="/wiki/Franco_Donatoni" title="Franco Donatoni">Franco Donatoni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aldo_Clementi" title="Aldo Clementi">Aldo Clementi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Cardew" title="Cornelius Cardew">Cornelius Cardew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ennio_Morricone" title="Ennio Morricone">Ennio Morricone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karl_Korte" title="Karl Korte">Karl Korte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boris_Porena" title="Boris Porena">Boris Porena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norma_Beecroft" title="Norma Beecroft">Norma Beecroft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mario_Bertoncini" title="Mario Bertoncini">Mario Bertoncini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Rubin_de_Cervin" title="Ernesto Rubin de Cervin">Ernesto Rubin de Cervin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Salzman" title="Eric Salzman">Eric Salzman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Leighton" title="Kenneth Leighton">Kenneth Leighton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Maxwell_Davies" title="Peter Maxwell Davies">Peter Maxwell Davies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dellaira" title="Michael Dellaira">Michael Dellaira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armando_Santiago" title="Armando Santiago">Armando Santiago</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Teitelbaum" title="Richard Teitelbaum">Richard Teitelbaum</a>. See: <a href="/wiki/List_of_music_students_by_teacher:_N_to_Q#Goffredo_Petrassi" title="List of music students by teacher: N to Q">List of music students by teacher: N to Q#Goffredo Petrassi</a>. Petrassi died in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> at the age of 98.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goffredo_Petrassi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Petrassi's early work was part of an attempt by several <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> composers to create a national "Italian" revival in classical music, corresponding to the romantic work of Germans such as <a href="/wiki/Richard_Wagner" title="Richard Wagner">Richard Wagner</a>. During this time, his work was characteristically <a href="/wiki/Neoclassicism_(music)" title="Neoclassicism (music)">neoclassical</a> in style, influenced by <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9la_Bart%C3%B3k" title="Béla Bartók">Bartók</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Hindemith" title="Paul Hindemith">Hindemith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Igor_Stravinsky" title="Igor Stravinsky">Stravinsky</a>.</p>
<p>In later years, Petrassi's open musical mind and acute personality led him to experiment with different post-<a href="/wiki/Anton_Webern" title="Anton Webern">Webernian</a> influences and a wide range of <a href="/wiki/Poetry" title="Poetry">poetic</a> materials, from Latin hymns to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ariosto" title="Ariosto">Ariosto</a>'s <i>La follia d'Orlando</i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cervantes" title="Cervantes">Cervantes</a>' <i>Ritratto di Don Chisciotte</i>. All these influences are present in a remarkable series of eight <a href="/wiki/Concerto_for_Orchestra" title="Concerto for Orchestra">Concerti for Orchestra</a> which he composed between the late 1930s and the late 1970s.</p>
<p>Petrassi stopped composing in 1986 due to increasing loss of eyesight.</p>
