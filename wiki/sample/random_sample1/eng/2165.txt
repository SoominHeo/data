<p><b>Go Hyun-jung</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">고현정</span>; born March 2, 1971) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> actress. She debuted in the entertainment scene as a <a href="/wiki/Miss_Korea" title="Miss Korea">Miss Korea</a> runner-up in 1989 and went on to star in <i><a href="/wiki/Sandglass_(TV_series)" title="Sandglass (TV series)">Sandglass</a></i>, one of the highest-rated and critically acclaimed <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">dramas</a> in Korean television history. She retired after marrying <a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">chaebol</a> <a href="/wiki/Chung_Yong-jin" title="Chung Yong-jin">Chung Yong-jin</a> in 1995, then returned to acting after their divorce in 2003. Go has since regained her top star status in Korea, becoming the highest paid actress on TV after the success of her series <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Seondeok_(TV_series)" title="Queen Seondeok (TV series)">Queen Seondeok</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daemul" title="Daemul">Daemul</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10line_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-10line-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Go Hyun-jung graduated from <a href="/wiki/Dongguk_University" title="Dongguk University">Dongguk University</a> with a degree in Performing Arts. She was a runner-up in the <a href="/wiki/Miss_Korea" title="Miss Korea">Miss Korea</a> pageant in 1989, which launched her acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Her big break came in the 1995 <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Sandglass_(TV_series)" title="Sandglass (TV series)">Sandglass</a></i>. The drama dealt with modern Korean history from 1970 to the 1990s and was one of the highest rated dramas in Korean television history, with average ratings of 50.8% and a peak of 64.5%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> People would rush home just to see a new episode, saying “it’s time to go home,” which meant they had to go home to watch <i>Sandglass</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daemul_interview_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-daemul_interview-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_divorce">Marriage and divorce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage and divorce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 1995, at the peak of her career, Go married <a href="/wiki/Chung_Yong-jin" title="Chung Yong-jin">Chung Yong-jin</a>, vice chairman and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shinsegae" title="Shinsegae">Shinsegae Group</a> and grandson of <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a> founder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Byung-chull" title="Lee Byung-chull">Lee Byung-chull</a>. The two first met in New York purely by coincidence when Chung had offered to help Go find her seat at the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Garden_Theatre" title="Winter Garden Theatre">Winter Garden Theatre</a> because her English was so bad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After the most talked-about wedding in Korea at the time, Go announced her retirement from acting to focus on her new role as a <a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">chaebol</a>'s wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They had two children together, a boy (born in 1998) and a girl (born in 2000).</p>
<p>But after eight years of marriage, a messy divorce followed in November 2003, during which she lost complete custody of her children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daemul_interview_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-daemul_interview-4">[4]</a></sup> Two years after the divorce, <a href="/wiki/Shinsegae" title="Shinsegae">Shinsegae</a> passed down a ruling stating that none of its department stores are allowed to display any Go-related material, including all her product endorsements. It is rumored that the ruling is still in effect.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_comeback">Television comeback</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television comeback">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She staged a comeback in the 2005 melodrama <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Day_(TV_series)" title="Spring Day (TV series)">Spring Day</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which she followed up with <a href="/wiki/Age_disparity_in_sexual_relationships" title="Age disparity in sexual relationships">May–December</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic comedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/What%27s_Up_Fox" title="What's Up Fox">What's Up Fox</a>?</i> and <a href="/wiki/Police_procedural" title="Police procedural">police procedural</a> <i><a href="/wiki/H.I.T_(TV_series)" title="H.I.T (TV series)">H.I.T</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Go regained her reputation as Korea's top actress in 2009 after playing <a href="/wiki/Femme_fatale" title="Femme fatale">femme fatale</a> royal concubine Lady Mishil in the hit historical drama <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Seondeok_(TV_series)" title="Queen Seondeok (TV series)">Queen Seondeok</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-changes_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-changes-12">[12]</a></sup> She was not the titular lead character, but her interpretation of the power-hungry main rival to the queen was impressive enough to make viewers remember the show as Mishil’s story.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daemul_interview_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-daemul_interview-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The drama reached ratings of over 40 percent and won her the highest award at the <a href="/wiki/MBC_Drama_Awards" title="MBC Drama Awards">MBC Drama Awards</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baeksang_Arts_Awards" title="Baeksang Arts Awards">Baeksang Arts Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>That same year, Go surprised fans not only by appearing on a TV entertainment show (popular talk show <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Fishery" title="Golden Fishery">Golden Fishery</a></i> hosted by comedian <a href="/wiki/Kang_Ho-dong" title="Kang Ho-dong">Kang Ho-dong</a>) for the first time in 15 years, but also by very frankly addressing rumors surrounding her shrouded personal life. Once notorious for declining to appear on entertainment programs other than <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">dramas</a> or films, she maintained a mysterious image for a long time. Her new easy-going, and down-to-earth attitude seemingly reflected a philosophical view of the many ups and downs in life. In interviews with print and online magazines, Go has even expressed her feelings towards her ex-husband and children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-changes_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-changes-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Twenty-one years after making her acting debut, Go held her very first fanmeeting on June 13, 2010—she held a press conference beforehand, sang songs for the 500 fans present, answered questions, and prepared video clips.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Though plagued with production issues prior to airing,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Go returned to television ten months later in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daemul" title="Daemul">Daemul</a></i>, which means "big shot" or "big thing" in Korean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In the drama, Go plays Seo Hye-rim, an anchorwoman who enters politics after the death of her war correspondent husband and becomes the nation's first female president. The 24-episode series revolves around an ordinary woman in extraordinary circumstances: how and why she became head of state and also the complicated schemes and plots surrounding her, her presidency, allies and enemies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Besides its star-studded cast (Go starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Korean_Wave" title="Korean Wave">Korean Wave</a> star <a href="/wiki/Kwon_Sang-woo" title="Kwon Sang-woo">Kwon Sang-woo</a>), the show's ratings was helped by its controversial plot, which included events from Korea's recent past such as a presidential <a href="/wiki/Impeachment" title="Impeachment">impeachment</a> and the sinking of a Navy warship, and it topped its time slot for 11 consecutive weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daemul_interview_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-daemul_interview-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Go repeated her feat by winning the top prize at the <a href="/wiki/2010_SBS_Drama_Awards" title="2010 SBS Drama Awards">2010 SBS Drama Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbs2010_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-sbs2010-26">[26]</a></sup> According to industry sources, Go was reportedly paid ₩55 million (<span style="white-space: nowrap">US$51,000</span>) per episode, setting a new salary record for a Korean actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Talent_agency">Talent agency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Talent agency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, after her contract ended with De Chocolate E&amp;TF, she set up her own talent agency with her brother Go Byung-cheol as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a>, called IOK Company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In 2012, <i>Spring Day</i> costar and close friend <a href="/wiki/Jo_In-sung" title="Jo In-sung">Jo In-sung</a> also signed on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Skincare_author">Skincare author</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Skincare author">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She published a book on skin care titled <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_books_about_beauty" title="List of books about beauty">Go Hyun-jung’s Texture</a></i> in 2011. The actress has always been admired for her youthful appearance, largely attributable to her young and healthy skin which had made fans wonder about her beauty secrets. The book, arranged in the format of a documentary, contains Go's own philosophy on beauty classified under six themes—texture, color, light, line, formality and scent—as well as her ideas on leading a sound and healthy lifestyle in general. The book is co-written by another writer who observed the actress's everyday life for six months, describing it in detail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The book became a bestseller, with all 30,000 copies of the first edition selling out in just two days of release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Voice_work">Voice work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Voice work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Go also narrated the <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Documentary" title="Documentary">documentary</a> <i>The Last Tundra - Movie Edition</i> (also known as <i>The Final Tundra - Cinema Edition</i>) which offered a rare glimpse into the life of the <a href="/wiki/Nenets_people" title="Nenets people">Nenets</a>, the last reindeer herding <a href="/wiki/Nomad" title="Nomad">nomads</a> living in the <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberian</a> <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Not content with her small screen success, Go began her late-blooming movie career by going against her image and taking pay cuts to star in non-mainstream films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> She was part of the ensemble cast in <a href="/wiki/Art_film" title="Art film">arthouse films</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Woman_on_the_Beach" title="Woman on the Beach">Woman on the Beach</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Like_You_Know_It_All" title="Like You Know It All">Like You Know It All</a></i> by auteur <a href="/wiki/Hong_Sang-soo" title="Hong Sang-soo">Hong Sang-soo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> She then starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Actresses_(film)" title="Actresses (film)">Actresses</a></i>, a semi-<a href="/wiki/Improvisation" title="Improvisation">improvisational</a> movie featuring six actresses each playing themselves. Director <a href="/wiki/E_J-yong" title="E J-yong">E J-yong</a>, who was first inspired to make the film after going out for a drink with actresses <a href="/wiki/Youn_Yuh-jung" title="Youn Yuh-jung">Youn Yuh-jung</a> and Go in 2007, said he focused on conveying the "reality" of the actresses' lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>2012's <i>Miss Go</i> (international title: <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Conspirator" title="Miss Conspirator">Miss Conspirator</a></i>) was her first commercial film, and the first film she headlined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> The action comedy is about a nerdy, reclusive cartoonist with a severe case of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sociophobia" title="Sociophobia">sociophobia</a> who somehow gets mixed up in a drug deal involving one of the biggest organized crime groups in Korea, and is forced to deal with her phobia and interact with others as she runs from the police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Talk_show_host">Talk show host</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Talk show host">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the press event for her eponymously named talk show <i><a href="/wiki/Go_Show" title="Go Show">GO Show</a></i> (which premiered on April 6, 2012), Go quipped, "I became an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MC" title="MC">MC</a> because I wanted to. I want to meet a lot of people and hear their stories. I have always wanted to do that and <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> gave me that opportunity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cakewalk_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-cakewalk-52">[52]</a></sup> Given her reputation for saying what is on her mind (one that has won the actress many fans), concerns arose as to how her work as show host would affect the impressive image of flawless elegance and sophistication she has gained over the years. But Go harbored little anxiety over how her public image might change, saying, "I think I can afford to put a small dent in my image and have some fun."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cakewalk_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-cakewalk-52">[52]</a></sup> Co-hosted by singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Jong-shin" title="Yoon Jong-shin">Yoon Jong-shin</a>, and comedians <a href="/wiki/Jeong_Hyeong-don" title="Jeong Hyeong-don">Jung Hyung-don</a> and Kim Young-chul, the talk show was cancelled after eight months on the air.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013-current">2013-current</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Hyun-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2013-current">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Go returned to television in 2013 with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Queen%27s_Classroom_(2013_TV_series)" title="The Queen's Classroom (2013 TV series)">The Queen's Classroom</a></i>, a remake of the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">Japanese drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Queen%27s_Classroom" title="The Queen's Classroom">Jyoou no Kyoushitsu</a></i>. The story depicts the struggle between a ferocious and ruthless elementary school teacher and her class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, she began teaching acting at her <a href="/wiki/Alma_mater" title="Alma mater">alma mater</a> <a href="/wiki/Dongguk_University" title="Dongguk University">Dongguk University</a>, as an <a href="/wiki/Professor#Non-tenure-track_positions" title="Professor">adjunct professor</a> in the theater department.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Back in 2006, she donated a ₩100 million scholarship fund to the school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Go also launched her own fashion brand, atti.k, a label that sells clothes, shoes, bags, furniture, food, and household goods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
