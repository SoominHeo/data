<p><b>Emperor Go-Fukakusa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">後深草天皇</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Go-Fukakusa-tennō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (June 28, 1243 – August 17, 1304) was the 89th <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">emperor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, according to the traditional order of succession. This reign spanned the years 1246 through 1260.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This 13th-century sovereign was named after the 9th-century <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Nimmy%C5%8D" title="Emperor Nimmyō">Emperor Nimmyō</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i>go-</i> (後), translates literally as "later;" and thus, he could be called the "Later Emperor Fukakusa". The Japanese word <i>go</i> has also been translated to mean the "second one;" and in some older sources, this emperor may be identified as "Fukakusa, the second," or as "Fukakusa II."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Fukakusa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before his ascension to the <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum_Throne" title="Chrysanthemum Throne">Chrysanthemum Throne</a>, his personal name (his <i>imina</i>) was Hisahito <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">久仁</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Japanese" title="Romanization of Japanese">Roman-alphabet</a> spelling of the name of this 13th-century emperor is the same as that of the personal name of a current member of the Imperial family, the <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a> are different:</p>
<ul>
<li>Emperor Go-Fukakusa, formerly Prince Hisahito (久仁)</li>
<li>Prince Hisahito of Akishino (悠仁)</li>
</ul>
<p>He was the second son of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Saga" title="Emperor Go-Saga">Emperor Go-Saga</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_of_Go-Fukakusa.27s_life">Events of Go-Fukakusa's life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Fukakusa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Events of Go-Fukakusa's life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hisahito<i>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinn%C5%8D" title="Shinnō">shinnō</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">久仁親王</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> formally became Go-Fukakusa<i>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenn%C5%8D" title="Tennō">tennō</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">後深草天皇</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> at the age of 2; and Go-Saga began to exercise power as <a href="/wiki/Cloistered_rule" title="Cloistered rule">cloistered Emperor</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>1247</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Kangen" title="Kangen">Kangen</a> 4, 1st month</i>): In the 4th year of Go-Saga<i>-tennō</i> 's reign (後嵯峨天皇四年), he abdicated; and the succession (‘‘senso’’) was received by his 4-year-old son. Shortly thereafter, Emperor Go-Fukakusa is said to have acceded to the throne (‘‘sokui’’).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>In 1259, at the insistence of Retired Emperor Go-Saga, he abdicated at the age of 15 to his younger brother, who would become <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Kameyama" title="Emperor Kameyama">Emperor Kameyama</a>.</p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Uda" title="Emperor Go-Uda">Emperor Go-Uda</a>'s ascension in 1260, Saionji Sanekane negotiated with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shogunate" title="Shogunate">Bakufu</a>, and succeeded in getting Emperor Go-Fukakusa's son Hirohito named as Crown Prince. In 1287, with his ascension as <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Fushimi" title="Emperor Fushimi">Emperor Fushimi</a>, Go-Fukakusa's <a href="/wiki/Cloistered_rule" title="Cloistered rule">cloistered rule</a> began.</p>
<p>In 1290, he entered the priesthood, retiring from the position of cloistered Emperor. But, with his seventh son, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Hisaaki" title="Prince Hisaaki">Imperial Prince Hisaaki</a> becoming the 8th <a href="/wiki/Kamakura_shogunate" title="Kamakura shogunate">Kamakura shōgun</a> among other things, the position of his Jimyōin-tō became strengthened.</p>
<p>In 1304, he died. He is enshrined with other emperors at the imperial tomb called <i>Fukakusa no kita no misasagi</i> (深草北陵) in <a href="/wiki/Fushimi-ku,_Kyoto" title="Fushimi-ku, Kyoto">Fushimi-ku, Kyoto</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kugy.C5.8D">Kugyō</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Fukakusa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kugyō">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Kugy%C5%8D" title="Kugyō">Kugyō</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">公卿</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a collective term for the very few most powerful men attached to the court of the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Emperor of Japan</a> in pre-<a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji</a> eras. Even during those years in which the court's actual influence outside the palace walls was minimal, the hierarchic organization persisted.</p>
<p>In general, this elite group included only three to four men at a time. These were hereditary courtiers whose experience and background would have brought them to the pinnacle of a life's career. During Go-Fukakusa's reign, this apex of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daij%C5%8D-kan" title="Daijō-kan">Daijō-kan</a> included:</i></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sessh%C5%8D" title="Sesshō">Sesshō</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Ichij%C5%8D_Sanetsune" title="Ichijō Sanetsune">Ichijō Sanetsune</a>, 1246–1247</li>
<li><i>Sesshō</i>, <a href="/wiki/Konoe_Kanetsune" title="Konoe Kanetsune">Konoe Kanetsune</a>, 1247–1252</li>
<li><i>Sesshō</i>, <a href="/wiki/Takatsukasa_Kanehira" title="Takatsukasa Kanehira">Takatsukasa Kanehira</a>, 1252–1254</li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kampaku" title="Kampaku">Kampaku</a></i>, Takatsukasa Kanehira, 1254–1261</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Sadaijin" title="Sadaijin">Sadaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Udaijin" title="Udaijin">Udaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nadaijin" title="Nadaijin">Nadaijin</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Dainagon" title="Dainagon">Dainagon</a></i></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eras_of_Go-Fukakusa.27s_reign">Eras of Go-Fukakusa's reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emperor_Go-Fukakusa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Eras of Go-Fukakusa's reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The years of Go-Fukakusa's reign are more specifically identified by more than one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_era_names" title="Japanese era names">era name</a> or <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neng%C5%8D" title="Nengō">nengō</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Kangen" title="Kangen">Kangen</a></i> (1243–1247)</li>
