<p><b>Sigmurethra</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxonomic" title="Taxonomic">taxonomic</a> category of air-breathing <a href="/wiki/Land_snail" title="Land snail">land snails</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slug" title="Slug">slugs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terrestrial_animal" title="Terrestrial animal">terrestrial</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pulmonate" title="Pulmonate">pulmonate</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gastropod" title="Gastropod">gastropod</a> <a href="/wiki/Mollusca" title="Mollusca">molluscs</a>. This is an informal group which includes the majority of land snails and <a href="/wiki/Slug" title="Slug">slugs</a>.</p>
<p>The two strong synapomorphies of Sigmurethra are a long pedal gland placed beneath a membrane and retractile tentacles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Several families in this group contain species of snails and slugs that create <a href="/wiki/Love_dart" title="Love dart">love darts</a>.</p>
<p>Sigmurethra are known from the <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Holocene" title="Holocene">Recent</a> periods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Bouchet_%26_Rocroi,_2005)" title="Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Bouchet &amp; Rocroi, 2005)">taxonomy of the Gastropoda by Bouchet &amp; Rocroi, 2005</a>, Sigmurethra is an "Informal Group", a subsection of the <a href="/wiki/Stylommatophora" title="Stylommatophora">Stylommatophora</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
