<p><b>Elections to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union">Supreme Soviet</a></b> were held in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> on 10 February 1946.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NS-1">[1]</a></sup> According to Soviet law, 325,000 out of an eligible adult population of 101,718,000 were disenfranchised for various reasons. This election was the first in which a 1945 decree allowed members of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> stationed outside the Soviet Union to vote for both chambers of the Supreme Soviet in special 100,000-member districts, a practice which would continue for decades with the Red Army presence in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_bloc" title="Eastern bloc">Eastern bloc</a>.</p>
