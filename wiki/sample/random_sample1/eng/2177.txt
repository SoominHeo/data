<p><b>Golden Valley</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Hennepin_County,_Minnesota" title="Hennepin County, Minnesota">Hennepin County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It is a western <a href="/wiki/Suburb" title="Suburb">suburb</a> of <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> and is the main corporate headquarters of <a href="/wiki/General_Mills" title="General Mills">General Mills</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pentair" title="Pentair">Pentair</a>. Golden Valley is also the home of <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/KARE" title="KARE">KARE</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Perpich_Center_for_Arts_Education" title="Perpich Center for Arts Education">Perpich Center for Arts Education</a> and <a href="/wiki/Breck_School" title="Breck School">Breck School</a>. The population was 20,371 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010_Census_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Census-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Valley,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tribes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chippewa" title="Chippewa">Chippewa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sioux" title="Sioux">Sioux</a> had encampments on nearby Medicine Lake. The first white settlers arrived in the early 1850s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Golden Valley was incorporated December 17, 1886. In the early twentieth century, it was mostly a farming community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Upham_1920_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Upham_1920-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Valley,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 10.55 square miles (27.32 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 10.20 square miles (26.42 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.35 square miles (0.91 km<sup>2</sup>) is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/45th_parallel_north" title="45th parallel north">45th parallel north</a> runs through Golden Valley, coinciding approximately with Duluth Street.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Interstate_394" title="Interstate 394">Interstate 394</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Highway_169" title="U.S. Highway 169">U.S. Highway 169</a>, and Minnesota State Highways <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_State_Highway_55" title="Minnesota State Highway 55">55</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_State_Highway_100" title="Minnesota State Highway 100">100</a> are four of the main routes in the area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Valley,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg/250px-2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg/375px-2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg/500px-2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:2009-0611-006-BreckSchool.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Breck School, a private <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Church_(United_States)" title="Episcopal Church (United States)">Episcopal</a> school.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Most children who live in Golden Valley attend the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robbinsdale_School_District" title="Robbinsdale School District">Robbinsdale School District</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Hopkins_Public_Schools_(Minnesota)" title="Hopkins Public Schools (Minnesota)">Hopkins School District</a>, as all of the territory of the city belongs to one or the other school district. Some students attend public schools in other school districts chosen by their families under Minnesota's open enrollment statute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Golden Valley High School was founded in 1957, and the adjacent Golden Valley Middle School was opened in 1964,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and were closed in the early 1980s after the Golden Valley School District merged with the Hopkins School District. Carl Sandburg Junior High School opened in 1959. In 1988, it became Sandburg Middle School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1981, the <a href="/wiki/Breck_School" title="Breck School">Breck School</a>, a private Episcopal school, purchased the former Golden Valley High School and Middle School property and moved from <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> to the campus of the former Golden Valley schools.</p>
<p>There is also a private elementary Catholic School named Good Shepherd Catholic School. Its name was changed in 2006 from the former Parkvalley Catholic.</p>
<p>What is currently the site of the <a href="/wiki/Perpich_Center_for_Arts_Education" title="Perpich Center for Arts Education">Perpich Center for Arts Education</a> was originally the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Valley_Lutheran_College" title="Golden Valley Lutheran College">Golden Valley Lutheran College</a>, which closed in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Valley,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FactFinder_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-2">[2]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 20,371 people, 8,816 households, and 5,417 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,997.2 inhabitants per square mile (771.1/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 9,349 housing units at an average density of 916.6 per square mile (353.9/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 85.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 7.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 3.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.9% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 2.7% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2.6% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 8,816 households of which 25.6% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 50.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.1% had a female householder with no husband present, 3.1% had a male householder with no wife present, and 38.6% were non-families. 30.4% of all households were made up of individuals and 14.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.26 and the average family size was 2.84.</p>
<p>The median age in the city was 45.7 years. 19.9% of residents were under the age of 18; 5.1% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 23.7% were from 25 to 44; 30.9% were from 45 to 64; and 20.3% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 48.6% male and 51.4% female.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Valley,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of 2000, there were 20,281 people, 8,449 households, and 5,508 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,982.3 people per square mile (765.4/km²). There were 8,589 housing units at an average density of 839.5 per square mile (324.2/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 91.07% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 3.59% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.29% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 2.87% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.03% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.55% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.61% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.76% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 8,449 households out of which 26.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 55.5% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 7.5% had a female householder with no husband present, and 34.8% were non-families. 27.6% of all households were made up of individuals and 12.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.31 and the average family size was 2.84.</p>
<p>In the city the population was spread out with 20.6% under the age of 18, 5.0% from 18 to 24, 28.2% from 25 to 44, 26.5% from 45 to 64, and 19.6% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 43 years. For every 100 females there were 93.7 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 89.9 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the city was $62,063, and the median income for a family was $75,899 (these figures had risen to $77,976 and $87,828 respectively as of a 2007 estimate). Males had a median income of $49,890 versus $35,967 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $34,094. About 0.8% of families and 3.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 2.2% of those under age 18 and 3.6% of those age 65 or over.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Valley,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Golden Valley is a statutory city, where the Mayor votes with the City Council. Golden Valley operates under the council–manager form of government. The City Council sets the policy and overall direction for the city, and appoints a city manager to serve as administrator. The city manager directs city staff in carrying out council decisions and providing services.</p>
<p>The Mayor serves a four-year term. There are four council members serving staggered four-year terms. Two council seats are up for election every two years, in odd-numbered years. The council members run citywide; there are no wards. The current Mayor is Shep Harris. The current City Council includes Joanie Clausen, Larry Fonnest, Steve Schmidgall and Andy Snope.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Valley,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Golden Valley is located in <a href="/wiki/Minnesota%27s_5th_congressional_district" title="Minnesota's 5th congressional district">Minnesota's 5th congressional district</a>, represented in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._House_of_Representatives" title="U.S. House of Representatives">U.S. House of Representatives</a> by <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> lawyer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keith_Ellison_(politician)" title="Keith Ellison (politician)">Keith Ellison</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Farmer_Labor_Party" title="Democratic Farmer Labor Party">Democrat.</a> The city is split between two state legislative districts: 44B, represented by Representative Ryan Winkler and Senator Ron Latz, and 45B, represented by Representative Lyndon Carlson and Senator Ann Rest. All four are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Farmer_Labor_Party" title="Democratic Farmer Labor Party">Democrats</a>.</p>
