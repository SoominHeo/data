<p><i><b>Golden Time</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">골든타임</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Goldeuntaim</span></i>) is a 2012 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Medical_drama" title="Medical drama">medical drama</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Lee_Sun-kyun" title="Lee Sun-kyun">Lee Sun-kyun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hwang_Jung-eum" title="Hwang Jung-eum">Hwang Jung-eum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Sung-min_(actor)" title="Lee Sung-min (actor)">Lee Sung-min</a> and <a href="/wiki/Song_Seon-mi" title="Song Seon-mi">Song Seon-mi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cheery_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cheery-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It aired on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> from July 9 to September 25, 2012 on Mondays and Tuesdays at 21:55 for 23 episodes.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Emergency_medicine" title="Emergency medicine">emergency medicine</a>, "golden time" or "<a href="/wiki/Golden_hour_(medicine)" title="Golden hour (medicine)">golden hour</a>" refers to the crucial time period right after a patient suffers a traumatic injury, during which timely medical treatment could determine life or death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Time_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After spending two years teaching in the country, Lee Min-woo (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Sun-kyun" title="Lee Sun-kyun">Lee Sun-kyun</a>) returns to a city hospital to complete his <a href="/wiki/Residency_(medicine)" title="Residency (medicine)">residency</a> and face his own uncertainties about being a doctor. Free-spirited and goofy, he is jaded about his job and just wants it easy. He is then jolted out of apathy when a traumatic incident forces him to rethink why he wanted to be a doctor in the first place. First-year resident Kang Jae-in (<a href="/wiki/Hwang_Jung-eum" title="Hwang Jung-eum">Hwang Jung-eum</a>) comes from a rich family that owns hospitals, but she just simply wants to be a doctor who can support herself and help others. Both Min-woo and Jae-in work in <a href="/wiki/Emergency_department" title="Emergency department">ER</a> under the guidance of Choi In-hyuk (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Sung-min_(actor)" title="Lee Sung-min (actor)">Lee Sung-min</a>), a famously astute workaholic surgeon who puts his patients before himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Time_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series was praised for its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verisimilitude_(literature)" title="Verisimilitude (literature)">verisimilitude</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_commentary" title="Social commentary">social commentary</a> on the country's lack of <a href="/wiki/Trauma_center" title="Trauma center">trauma centers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The character of trauma specialist Choi In-hyuk was based on real-life doctor Lee Guk-jong,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> who became famous in 2011 when he saved the life of Captain Seok Hae-kyun after Seok received multiple gunshot wounds during a rescue mission from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Somali_pirate" title="Somali pirate">Somali pirates</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Time_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With a rise of 5.1 percent in the third week of July 2012, <i>Golden Time</i> landed its first win on the Monday and Tuesday primetime ratings chart after drawing in 12.6 percent of the total viewers, according to data compiled by the TNmS (Total National Multimedia Statistics). The series, which had been struggling at the bottom for its first two weeks with single-digit ratings, made this leap as the former chart-topper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chaser_(TV_series)" title="The Chaser (TV series)">The Chaser</a></i> and runner-up <i><a href="/wiki/Big_(TV_series)" title="Big (TV series)">Big</a></i> wrapped up their runs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> With average ratings of 13.3-15.5 percent, it thereafter topped its timeslot for ten consecutive weeks, despite the rival channels' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Olympics_2012" title="London Olympics 2012">London Olympics 2012</a> coverage and the premieres of <i><a href="/wiki/Faith_(2012_TV_series)" title="Faith (2012 TV series)">Faith</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Haeundae_Lovers" title="Haeundae Lovers">Haeundae Lovers</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Originally slated at 20 episodes, it was given a 3-episode extension, and discussions are underway for a possible second season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Time_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
