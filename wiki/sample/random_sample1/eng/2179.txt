<p><b>Clifford Joseph Price</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">MBE</a> (born 19 September 1965), better known by his stage name <b>Goldie</b>, is an English musician, DJ, visual artist and actor from <a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a>. Initially gaining exposure for his work as a <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a> artist, Goldie became well known for his pioneering role in the 1990s UK <a href="/wiki/Rave" title="Rave">rave</a> scene as a producer, including his work in musical styles such as <a href="/wiki/Oldschool_jungle" title="Oldschool jungle">jungle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Breakbeat_hardcore" title="Breakbeat hardcore">breakbeat hardcore</a>. He released a variety of singles under the psuedonym <b>Rufige Kru</b>, and would later release several albums, including the 1995 album <i><a href="/wiki/Timeless_(Goldie_album)" title="Timeless (Goldie album)">Timeless</a></i>, which entered the UK charts at number 7. His acting credits include the 1999 <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_World_Is_Not_Enough" title="The World Is Not Enough">The World Is Not Enough</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Snatch_(film)" title="Snatch (film)">Snatch</a></i> (2000) and the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i>. He has also appeared in a number of celebrity reality television shows, including <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Big_Brother_2" title="Celebrity Big Brother 2">Celebrity Big Brother 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_Come_Dancing" title="Strictly Come Dancing">Strictly Come Dancing</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Come_Dine_with_Me" title="Come Dine with Me">Come Dine with Me</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Maestro" title="Maestro">Maestro</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goldie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Price is of <a href="/wiki/Jamaicans" title="Jamaicans">Jamaican</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoAndroidsDance_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DoAndroidsDance-4">[4]</a></sup> He was put up for <a href="/wiki/Adoption" title="Adoption">adoption</a> and raised in child-care homes and by several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foster_parents" title="Foster parents">foster parents</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoAndroidsDance_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-DoAndroidsDance-4">[4]</a></sup> Price was a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breakdance" title="Breakdance">breakdance</a> crew Westside, based in the <a href="/wiki/Whitmore_Reans" title="Whitmore Reans">Whitmore Reans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heath_Town" title="Heath Town">Heath Town</a> areas of <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton" title="Wolverhampton">Wolverhampton</a>, in the 1980s. He later joined a breakdance crew called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bboy" title="Bboy">Bboys</a>, and made his name as a <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a> artist in the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">West Midlands</a>.</p>
<p>His artwork around <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton" title="Wolverhampton">Wolverhampton</a> was featured heavily in <a href="/wiki/Dick_Fontaine" title="Dick Fontaine">Dick Fontaine</a>'s documentary <i>Bombin'</i>. He took part in the largest ever British graffiti art battle alongside <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Robert_Del_Naja" title="Robert Del Naja">Robert "3D" Del Naja</a>, who later formed <a href="/wiki/Massive_Attack" title="Massive Attack">Massive Attack</a>. He is mentioned for his graffiti in the book <i>Spraycan Art</i> by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Chalfant" title="Henry Chalfant">Henry Chalfant</a> and James Prigoff, which contains several examples of his art.</p>
<p>He moved to the US owing to graffiti projects, and also started selling <a href="/wiki/Grill_(jewelry)" title="Grill (jewelry)">grills</a> (gold teeth jewellery) in New York and Miami; he continued this business after his return to the UK in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoAndroidsDance_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-DoAndroidsDance-4">[4]</a></sup> Contrary to popular belief, this is not where his nickname comes from; it stems from "Goldielocks", an earlier nickname given to him during his B-boys days and subsequently shortened when he no longer wore <a href="/wiki/Dreadlocks" title="Dreadlocks">dreadlocks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoAndroidsDance_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-DoAndroidsDance-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goldie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By 1991, Price had become fascinated by the British <a href="/wiki/Breakbeat" title="Breakbeat">breakbeat</a> music scene when his girlfriend, DJ <a href="/wiki/Kemistry" title="Kemistry">Kemistry</a>, introduced him to the pioneering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oldskool_jungle" title="Oldskool jungle">jungle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drum_and_bass" title="Drum and bass">drum and bass</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producers</a> Dennis "Dego" McFarlane and Mark "Marc Mac" Clair, known as <a href="/wiki/4hero" title="4hero">4hero</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoAndroidsDance_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-DoAndroidsDance-4">[4]</a></sup> He went on to execute some design and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26R" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> work for 4hero's Reinforced <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>.</p>
<p>In 1992, Price made his first album appearance, by contributing a short vocal piece to the track "Rufige" by Icelandic group, Ajax Project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Since then the track has been repeatedly misattributed to Price himself, perhaps because of his subsequent use of "Rufige" as a moniker for his own ambitions.</p>
<p>His releases <i>Killa Muffin</i> b/w <i>Krisp Biscuit</i> and the <i>Dark Rider</i> <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> were released under the alias "Rufige Cru".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-6">[6]</a></sup> Recently, he has used the alias "Rufige Kru" to release collaborations with other producers such as Heist. His track "Terminator", released under the name "Metalheadz" in 1992,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> was a hit in the jungle scene and is noted for pioneering the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_timescale-pitch_modification" title="Audio timescale-pitch modification">time stretching</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reynolds_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reynolds-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1993, he released <i>Angel</i>, another <a href="/wiki/12-inch_single" title="12-inch single">12"</a> on the Synthetic Hardcore Phonography label. 1994 saw him setting up his own record label, <a href="/wiki/Metalheadz" title="Metalheadz">Metalheadz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>His first studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Timeless_(Goldie_album)" title="Timeless (Goldie album)">Timeless</a></i>, followed in 1995. <i>Timeless</i> entered the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> at number seven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-9">[9]</a></sup> The album fused the <a href="/wiki/Breakbeat" title="Breakbeat">breakbeats</a> and basslines common in jungle with orchestral textures and <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a> vocals by <a href="/wiki/Diane_Charlemagne" title="Diane Charlemagne">Diane Charlemagne</a>. The album's title track was a 21-minute <a href="/wiki/Symphony" title="Symphony">symphonic</a> piece. "Inner City Life", a track from the album, reached number 39 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-9">[9]</a></sup> <i>Timeless</i> helped to popularise drum and bass as a form of <a href="/wiki/Musical_expression" title="Musical expression">musical expression</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The music critic <a href="/wiki/Simon_Reynolds" title="Simon Reynolds">Simon Reynolds</a> noted that Price's credentials as a musical innovator – and particularly as one of the key driving forces of innovation in the jungle/breakbeat scene – were exceptional. "Goldie revolutionised jungle not once but thrice", he noted in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire_(magazine)" title="The Wire (magazine)">The Wire</a></i> magazine, continuing, "First there was 'Terminator' (pioneering the use of time stretching), then 'Angel' (fusing Diane Charlemagne's live vocal with <a href="/wiki/David_Byrne" title="David Byrne">David Byrne</a>/<a href="/wiki/Brian_Eno" title="Brian Eno">Brian Eno</a> samples to prove that hardcore could be more 'conventionally' musical), now there's 'Timeless', a 22-minute hardcore symphony."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reynolds_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Reynolds-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>1996 saw the release of his <i>Toasted Both Sides Please</i> remix of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bush_(band)" title="Bush (band)">Bush</a> song <i><a href="/wiki/Swallowed_(song)" title="Swallowed (song)">Swallowed</a></i>, which topped charts in the USA and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Price released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Saturnz_Return" title="Saturnz Return">Saturnz Return</a></i>, in 1998. The album's opening track, "Mother", is an hour-long orchestral drum and bass piece. The album featured appearances by <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Noel_Gallagher" title="Noel Gallagher">Noel Gallagher</a> and <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>.</p>
<p>In 2002, Price said that he had been working for three years on a film called <i>Sine Tempus</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> described as a coming-of-age story of a young paintbrush artist. In 2006, he announced the soundtrack as his new album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-independent06_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-independent06-15">[15]</a></sup> The album was released via the Metalheadz website in 2008, but the film has not been released.</p>
<p>Price is known for his work as the leader of Rufige Kru. The group has no fixed members and has included drum and bass producers such as Technical Itch, Heist, Cujo, Agzilla Da Ice, Danny J, <a href="/wiki/Doc_Scott" title="Doc Scott">Doc Scott</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rob_Playford" title="Rob Playford">Rob Playford</a>.</p>
<p>March 2013 saw the release of <i>The Alchemist: The Best of Goldie 1992–2012</i>, featuring prominent tracks from throughout Price's musical career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> A subsequent compilation, the three-CD Masterpiece set released by Ministry of Sound in 2014, brought together tracks that influenced him (Soul II Soul's Back To Life, Roy Ayers' Everybody Loves The Sunshine) with cuts that soundtracked his entry into the rave scene and key moments from the drum'n'bass scene. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goldie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Price has appeared in <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Snatch_(film)" title="Snatch (film)">Snatch</a></i> and several other films, most notably the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_World_Is_Not_Enough" title="The World Is Not Enough">The World Is Not Enough</a></i>. He also played gangster <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angel_Hudson" title="Angel Hudson">Angel Hudson</a> in the British soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i> (2001–2002). Price starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_Loves_Sunshine" title="Everybody Loves Sunshine">Everybody Loves Sunshine</a></i> (1999) (aka <i>B.UST.E.D</i> – United States title) with <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_appearances">Television appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goldie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1980s, Price appeared on <i>Central Weekend</i>, a Friday evening topical debate show on <a href="/wiki/ITV_Central" title="ITV Central">Central TV</a>, promoting <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a> as an art form. He had a small documentary made about his own art on Central TV's <i>Here and Now</i> programme featuring <a href="/wiki/Pogus_Caesar" title="Pogus Caesar">Pogus Caesar</a>'s photographs of New York. He has appeared on various young people's TV shows as part of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breakdance" title="Breakdance">breakdance</a> crew, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bboy" title="Bboy">Bboys</a> from Wolverhampton. In 1995, he appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Passengers_(TV_series)" title="Passengers (TV series)">Passengers</a></i>, and in a <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> documentary about himself in 1998.</p>
<p>His next TV appearance was hosting <i>Crime Business</i> on the digital TV channels <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bravo_(UK_TV_network)" title="Bravo (UK TV network)">Bravo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ftn_(TV_channel)" title="Ftn (TV channel)">Ftn</a>. He presented the documentary series <i>The World's Deadliest Gangs</i> on Bravo in 2002.</p>
<p>Price appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Big_Brother_2" title="Celebrity Big Brother 2">second series</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Big_Brother" title="Celebrity Big Brother">Celebrity Big Brother</a></i> in 2002. He was the first celebrity to be "evicted". In 2006, he was scheduled to appear in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Games_(UK_TV_series)" title="The Games (UK TV series)">The Games</a></i>, a UK reality TV show on Channel 4, but during training for the water-ski jump event he <a href="/wiki/Bone_fracture" title="Bone fracture">fractured</a> his <a href="/wiki/Femur" title="Femur">femur</a> and was unable to take part in the show. He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Rickitt" title="Adam Rickitt">Adam Rickitt</a>. In 2009, he was reported to be suing the producers of the show for damages as a result of the injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>During August and September 2008, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> broadcast <i><a href="/wiki/Maestro_(TV_series)" title="Maestro (TV series)">Maestro</a></i>, a reality television show in which eight celebrities, including Price, learned to conduct a concert orchestra. Price placed second, behind <a href="/wiki/Sue_Perkins" title="Sue Perkins">Sue Perkins</a>.</p>
<p>On 31 July 2009, the first of a two-part television programme <i><a href="/wiki/Classic_Goldie" title="Classic Goldie">Classic Goldie</a></i> was broadcast, showing how in the wake of his success in the <i>Maestro</i> programme he learns to write a score for a large orchestra and choir. The resulting composition, commissioned by the BBC and entitled <i>Sine Tempore</i> (<i>Timeless</i>), was performed at two children's <a href="/wiki/The_Proms" title="The Proms">Promenade concerts</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> on 1 and 2 August 2009, which featured music connected with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> and the creation and evolution of the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>He appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Mastermind" title="Celebrity Mastermind">Celebrity Mastermind</a></i> on 27 December 2009 and came fourth behind <a href="/wiki/Paul_O%27Grady" title="Paul O'Grady">Paul O'Grady</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gail_Emms" title="Gail Emms">Gail Emms</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loyd_Grossman" title="Loyd Grossman">Loyd Grossman</a>. On 11 September 2010, he was announced as part of the line-up in <i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_Come_Dancing_(series_8)" title="Strictly Come Dancing (series 8)">Strictly Come Dancing</a></i> and on 10 October, he became the first celebrity to leave the show.</p>
<p>On 22 December 2010, he appeared in a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celebrity_Come_Dine_with_Me" title="Celebrity Come Dine with Me">Celebrity Come Dine with Me</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christmas_special" title="Christmas special">Christmas special</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 March 2011, he appeared in a three-part reality television series, <i>Goldie's Band – By Royal Appointment</i>, in which he led a group of music experts as they conducted a nationwide search for young talented musicians and then selected and intensively coached 12 of them, who collaborated to create some musical pieces for a performance at <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 August 2012, he appeared in the Channel 4 documentary <i>Idris Elba's How Clubbing Changed the World</i> (hosted by <a href="/wiki/Idris_Elba" title="Idris Elba">Idris Elba</a>) to explain how he invented the revolutionary technique of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_timescale-pitch_modification" title="Audio timescale-pitch modification">time stretching</a> by misusing an <a href="/wiki/High_frequency" title="High frequency">HF</a> ultra-harmonizer, which is usually used for guitars. He then went on to say that when he crossed this with digital <a href="/wiki/Breakbeat" title="Breakbeat">breakbeat</a>, the sound evolved from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oldskool_jungle" title="Oldskool jungle">jungle</a> into <a href="/wiki/Drum_and_bass" title="Drum and bass">drum and bass</a>.</p>
<p>In 2014, he appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Telethon" title="Telethon">telethon</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_Children_in_Need" title="BBC Children in Need">BBC Children in Need</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Art">Art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goldie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, Price returned to the art world with an art exhibition, "Love Over Gold", which was held at the Leonard Street Gallery, London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2008, he teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Pete_Tong" title="Pete Tong">Pete Tong</a> to provide much of the artwork for Tong's new <a href="/wiki/Wonderland_(event)" title="Wonderland (event)">Wonderland</a> club night at Eden nightclub in <a href="/wiki/Sant_Antoni_de_Portmany" title="Sant Antoni de Portmany">San Antonio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibiza</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>There was an exhibition of Price's art in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> on 13–26 June 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In April 2009, his "Kids Are All Riot" exhibition was mounted in <a href="/wiki/Shoreditch" title="Shoreditch">Shoreditch</a>, London, and his screenprint "Apocalypse Angel" was released to coincide with this restrospective of his prolific works and originals.</p>
<p>Price has had his art work displayed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a> by the arts company <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Below&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Art Below (page does not exist)">Art Below</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goldie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the early 1990s, Price had a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Drum_and_bass" title="Drum and bass">drum and bass</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Kemistry" title="Kemistry">Kemistry</a>, who died in a car accident in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He was romantically involved with singer <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a> for several years until their break-up in September 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1998, he bought a country house in <a href="/wiki/Bovingdon" title="Bovingdon">Bovingdon</a>, Hertfordshire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-independent06_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-independent06-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian03_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian03-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2002, Hodder &amp; Stoughton published his autobiography, <i>Nine Lives</i>, which he wrote with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gorman" title="Paul Gorman">Paul Gorman</a>.</p>
<p>Price married model Sonjia Ashby in 2002,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> but they divorced in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-independent06_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-independent06-15">[15]</a></sup> As of 2003, he had five children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian03_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian03-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, Price began a relationship with Mika Wassenaar, a Canadian. The couple appeared in the 20 June 2009 episode of ITV's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Star_Mr_and_Mrs" title="All Star Mr and Mrs">All Star Mr and Mrs</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Schofield" title="Phillip Schofield">Phillip Schofield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fern_Britton" title="Fern Britton">Fern Britton</a>. Mika and Price married in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-observer_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-observer-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2010, Price received an honorary doctorate in Social Sciences from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brunel_University" title="Brunel University">Brunel University</a>, Uxbridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brunel_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-brunel-30">[30]</a></sup> On 3 September 2010, he received an honorary degree of Doctor of Design from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wolverhampton" title="University of Wolverhampton">University of Wolverhampton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 September 2010, Price's 23-year-old son Jamie Price was sentenced to <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment" title="Life imprisonment">life imprisonment</a>, with a minimum sentence of 21 years, for murder. Jamie was convicted after stabbing a man to death on 24 August 2008. The victim was a member of a rival gang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Price was appointed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Member of the Order of the British Empire">Member of the Order of the British Empire</a> (MBE) in the <a href="/wiki/2016_New_Year_Honours" title="2016 New Year Honours">2016 New Year Honours</a> awards, for services to music and young people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
