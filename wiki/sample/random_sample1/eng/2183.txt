<p>The <b>goliath frog</b> or <b>giant slippery frog</b> (<i>Conraua goliath</i>) is the largest living <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frog</a> on <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amnh2007_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-amnh2007-2">[2]</a></sup> Specimens can grow up to 32 cm (12.6 in) in length from <a href="/wiki/Snout" title="Snout">snout</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cloaca" title="Cloaca">vent</a>, and weigh up to 3.25 kg (7.17 lb). This species has a relatively small habitat range in <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>. Its numbers are dwindling due to habitat destruction and its collection for food and the pet trade.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goliath_frog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a sample of 15 individuals,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sabater1985_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sabater1985-3">[3]</a></sup> weights ranged between 600 and 3,250 g (1.32 and 7.17 lb), and snout-vent lengths were between 17 and 32 cm (6.7 and 12.6 in). Their eyes can be nearly 2.5 cm (0.98 in) in diameter. The conspicuous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanum_(zoology)" title="Tympanum (zoology)">tympanum</a> has a diameter of about 0.5 cm (0.20 in) and is separated from the eye by about 5 cm (2.0 in) in adults. The next largest extant anurans are the <a href="/wiki/Cane_toad" title="Cane toad">cane toad</a>, about a third smaller than the goliath in the largest specimens, and the <a href="/wiki/African_bullfrog" title="African bullfrog">African bullfrog</a>, which is just over half the size of the goliath. Goliath frog eggs and tadpoles are about the same size as other frogs despite its very large adult form.</p>
<p>A lateral fold extends from the eye to the posterior portion of the tympanum. Toes are fully webbed, with large interdigital membranes extending down to the toe tips. The second toe is the longest. The skin on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dorsum_(anatomy)" title="Dorsum (anatomy)">dorsum</a> and on top of the limbs is granular. Dorsal coloration is green sienna, while the abdomen and ventral part of the limbs are yellow/orange. They have acute hearing but no <a href="/wiki/Vocal_sac" title="Vocal sac">vocal sac</a>, and also lack <a href="/wiki/Nuptial_pad" title="Nuptial pad">nuptial pads</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goliath_frog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The goliath frog is normally found in and near fast-flowing <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sand" title="Sand">sandy</a> bottoms in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_Africa" title="Middle Africa">Middle African</a> countries of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sabater1985_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-sabater1985-3">[3]</a></sup> These rivers are usually clear and highly oxygenated. Their actual range spans from the last 200 km (120 mi) of the <a href="/wiki/Sanaga_River" title="Sanaga River">Sanaga</a> basin in Cameroon to the north to the last 50 km (31 mi) of the <a href="/wiki/Benito_River" title="Benito River">Benito River</a> basin in Equatorial Guinea to the south. The river systems in which these frogs live are often found in dense, extremely humid areas with relatively high temperatures.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology_and_behavior">Ecology and behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goliath_frog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology and behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goliath_frog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like most amphibians, water is vital for their <a href="/wiki/Amphibian#Reproduction" title="Amphibian">reproduction</a>. Because the goliath frog lacks a vocal sac, it does not produce mating calls, a behavior generally present in frogs and toads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sabater1985_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-sabater1985-3">[3]</a></sup> Males construct spawning and breeding areas alongside and within rivers by pushing rocks into semicircular patterns. The egg masses consist of several hundred eggs, approximately 3.5 mm each, attached to vegetation at the bottom of rivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-netindustries2008_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-netindustries2008-4">[4]</a></sup> Larval development takes between 85 and 95 days.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goliath_frog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Goliath tadpoles are herbivorous and feed on a single aquatic plant, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dicraeia_warmingii&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dicraeia warmingii (page does not exist)">Dicraeia warmingii</a></i>, found only near waterfalls and rapids, which may help explain their restricted range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amnh2007_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-amnh2007-2">[2]</a></sup> Adult goliath frogs feed on worms, and insects, such as dragonflies and locusts. They also eat smaller frogs, <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crabs</a>, baby <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtles</a>, and young <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a>. There is a report of a <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bat</a> found in a goliath frog's stomach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Longevity">Longevity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goliath_frog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Longevity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The goliath frog can live up to 15 years in the wild. In captivity, they can live up to 21 years. It is preyed upon by snakes, <a href="/wiki/Nile_crocodile" title="Nile crocodile">Nile crocodiles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nile_monitor" title="Nile monitor">Nile monitors</a>, among other predators.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goliath_frog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Goliath frogs are considered to be a source of food in some parts of west Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup> They were also highly exported to zoos and animal dealers to be sold as pets. Unfortunately, these frogs fail to thrive in captivity and almost never reproduce there. Due to their classification as an endangered species, the Equatorial Guinean government has declared that no more than 300 goliaths may be exported out of the country per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amnh2007_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-amnh2007-2">[2]</a></sup> Some are captured and kept as pets.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
