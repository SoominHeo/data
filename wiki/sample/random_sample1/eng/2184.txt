<p><b>Periostitis</b>, also known as <b>periostalgia</b>, is a medical condition caused by <a href="/wiki/Inflammation" title="Inflammation">inflammation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Periosteum" title="Periosteum">periosteum</a>, a layer of <a href="/wiki/Connective_tissue" title="Connective tissue">connective tissue</a> that surrounds bone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CEHP1998_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CEHP1998-1">[1]</a></sup> The condition is generally chronic, and is marked by tenderness and swelling of the bone and an aching pain.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">Causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Periostitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Acute periostitis is due to <a href="/wiki/Infection" title="Infection">infection</a>, is characterized by diffuse formation of pus, severe pain, constitutional symptoms, and usually results in <a href="/wiki/Necrosis" title="Necrosis">necrosis</a>. It can be caused by excessive physical activity as well, as in the case of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medial_tibial_stress_syndrome" title="Medial tibial stress syndrome">medial tibial stress syndrome</a> (also referred to as tibial periostalgia, soleus periostalgia, or shin splints). Congenital infection with syphilis can also cause periostitis in newborn infants.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fossil_record">Fossil record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Periostitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fossil record">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evidence for periostitis found in the fossil record is studied by <a href="/wiki/Paleopathology" title="Paleopathology">paleopathologists</a>, specialists in ancient disease and injury. Periostitis has been seen in the late Cretaceous-Eocene crocodile <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borealosuchus_formidabilis" title="Borealosuchus formidabilis">Borealosuchus formidabilis</a></i>, once known as <i><a href="/wiki/Leidyosuchus" title="Leidyosuchus">Leidyosuchus</a></i>. In one study, periostitis was the most common pathology in this species, with 134 instances of the condition out of 7,154 bones the scientists examined showing evidence for the condition. Periostitis has also been documented in dinosaurs, including a forelimb referred to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauropod" title="Sauropod">long-necked</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camarasaurus_grandis" title="Camarasaurus grandis">Camarasaurus grandis</a></i>, as well as the shoulder blade of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceratopsian" title="Ceratopsian">horned dinosaur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dino-peri_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dino-peri-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
