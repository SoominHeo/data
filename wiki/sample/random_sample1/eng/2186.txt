<p><b>Gold's Gym International, Inc.</b> is an American chain of international co-ed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fitness_center" title="Fitness center">fitness centers</a> (commonly referred to as "<a href="/wiki/Gym" title="Gym">gyms</a>") originally started in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> by <a href="/wiki/Joe_Gold" title="Joe Gold">Joe Gold</a>. Each gym features a wide array of <a href="/wiki/Exercise_equipment" title="Exercise equipment">exercise equipment</a>, group exercise classes and <a href="/wiki/Personal_trainer" title="Personal trainer">personal trainers</a> to assist clients. Its headquarters are in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gold%27s_Gym&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first Gold's Gym opened in August 1965 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venice_Beach,_California" title="Venice Beach, California">Venice Beach, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whyabout_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-whyabout-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Dubbed "the <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bodybuilding" title="Bodybuilding">bodybuilding</a>", it was frequented by <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schwarzenegger" title="Arnold Schwarzenegger">Arnold Schwarzenegger</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Dave_Draper" title="Dave Draper">Dave Draper</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and featured in the 1977 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Pumping_Iron" title="Pumping Iron">Pumping Iron</a></i>. The movie brought attention not only to the gym itself, but also to the world of bodybuilding and physique in general. To this day, it is considered a <a href="/wiki/Landmark" title="Landmark">landmark</a> in the bodybuilding subculture,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and has achieved cult status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1970, Joe Gold sold the chain.</p>

<p>Today, Gold's Gym claims to be the largest chain of mixed-gender gyms in the world, with more than 700 locations in 37 U.S. states, the District of Columbia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and 20 other countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Gold's Gym has a membership of more than three and a half million, split almost equally between male and female patrons.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Gold's Gym locations are equipped with cardiovascular and strength training equipment and offer group exercise classes such as group cycling, <a href="/wiki/Pilates" title="Pilates">pilates</a>, Latin dance, yoga<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and stretching through the company GGX (Gold's Group Exercise) program. Many gyms also offer basketball, swimming pools and boxing studios.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The company also licenses its name to products such as fitness equipment and clothing. The original Gold's Gym <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logo</a>, a bald-headed weightlifter holding a barbell, was designed in 1973 by <a href="/wiki/Ric_Drasin" title="Ric Drasin">Ric Drasin</a>, who was Schwarzenegger's training partner for four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Gold's Gym members have included such celebrities as <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">Dwayne Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Swank" title="Hilary Swank">Hilary Swank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Alba" title="Jessica Alba">Jessica Alba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Morrison" title="Jim Morrison">Jim Morrison</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, <a href="/wiki/Jodie_Foster" title="Jodie Foster">Jodie Foster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keanu_Reeves" title="Keanu Reeves">Keanu Reeves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Freeman" title="Morgan Freeman">Morgan Freeman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods" title="Tiger Woods">Tiger Woods</a> among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AGB_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-AGB-14">[14]</a></sup> To this day, the original Gold's Gym in Venice is considered a sports landmark by ESPN.com on its list of the 100 most important sports venues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caple_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Caple-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>Gold's Gym is privately owned. It was acquired from its previous owner, private equity firm <a href="/wiki/Brockway_Moran_%26_Partners" title="Brockway Moran &amp; Partners">Brockway Moran &amp; Partners</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rowling" title="Robert Rowling">Robert Rowling's TRT Holdings</a> in 2004 for approximately $158 million. Brockway Moran had acquired the company in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Corporate headquarters are in the <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> metropolitan area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since Gold's Gym opened its first international location in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> in 1985, the company has expanded its global franchising program to include nearly 180 international gyms including operations in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AGB_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-AGB-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2009, the company opened its first locations in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.</p>
<p>In 2004, the company introduced the Gold's Gym Fitness Institute to serve as a think tank on critical health and fitness issues. The institute's members includes Ramona Braganza, Robert Reames, Mike Ryan and Eric the Trainer among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gold's Gym operates a national corporate wellness program. Clients include Whole Foods, the U.S. House of Representatives, American Airlines and Exxon Mobil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Gold's Gym became a national fitness sponsor of the American Diabetes Association's Tour de Cure. In 2006 the company became a National Strategic Partner of the American Diabetes Association, the ADA's highest designation for a corporate partner. Since their relationship began, Gold's Gym has raised more than $2.5 million to benefit diabetes research, education and awareness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diabetes.org_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-diabetes.org-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Gold's Gym partnered with video game manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Ubisoft" title="Ubisoft">Ubisoft</a> to create the "Gold's Gym Cardio Workout" video game for the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>. The game received positive reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gold's is one of two official health clubs of the <a href="/wiki/AARP" title="AARP">AARP</a>, where it offers month-to-month memberships, and is the official health club of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Cross_and_Blue_Shield_Association" title="Blue Cross and Blue Shield Association">Blue Cross and Blue Shield Association</a>. Gold's has begun offering online enrollment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under a license agreement with Gold's Gym, <a href="/wiki/Icon_Health_%26_Fitness" title="Icon Health &amp; Fitness">Icon Health &amp; Fitness</a> designs, manufactures, markets and sells a full line of fitness equipment under the Gold's Gym brand name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gold%27s_Gym&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Numerous customers have reported Gold's Gym franchises acting in dishonest and unscrupulous ways. They have reported advertised deals not being honored, billing irregularities, contract terms being fraudulently altered by sales staff after signing and problems canceling accounts or relocating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> A Gold's Gym in <a href="/wiki/Provo,_Utah" title="Provo, Utah">Provo, Utah</a>, was successfully sued for fraud in 2006 for changing a contract after it was signed in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In November 2014, the same Provo gym left the Gold's Gym brand and was converted to Vasa Fitness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Like many gyms, some Gold's Gym franchisees lure customers with free sign-up or gift certificates with fabricated nominal values, and then require long-term contracts that are very difficult to exit without paying cancellation fees and following long and inconvenient procedures (such as having to send a certified letter to their central billing office a month in advance).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In the past, gym websites did not give membership rates and the gyms would usually not tell customers the rate over the phone, asking instead that customers come to the gym to have a tour and discussion, which often included a heavy sales pitch.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardio_Cinema">Cardio Cinema</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gold%27s_Gym&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cardio Cinema">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gold's Gym has provided the "Cardio Cinema" concept since at least 2008. It features a cinema projector screen and feature-length films but has treadmills installed instead of seats. Gold's Gym has more than 50 cardio cinemas across the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
