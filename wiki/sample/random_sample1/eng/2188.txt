<p><b>Gombe</b> is the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Gombe_State" title="Gombe State">Gombe State</a>, north-eastern Nigeria, with an estimated population of 261,536.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-world_gazetteer_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-world_gazetteer-1">[1]</a></sup> The city is the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Gombe_Emirate" title="Gombe Emirate">Gombe Emirate</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nigerian_traditional_states" title="Nigerian traditional states">traditional state</a> that covers most of Gombe State.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Recent<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (July 2010)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> developments include an electrical generation operation forming the basis for the <a href="/wiki/Dadin_Kowa_Dam" title="Dadin Kowa Dam">Dadin Kowa</a> water project and the opening of the new <a href="/wiki/Gombe_Lawanti_International_Airport" title="Gombe Lawanti International Airport">Gombe Lawanti International Airport</a> serving <a href="/wiki/Gombe_State" title="Gombe State">Gombe State</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gombe,_Gombe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gombe State is mainly populated by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fulani_People&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fulani People (page does not exist)">Fulani People</a> constituting more than half of the state population (more than 50%) Other minor ethnic groups include the Hausa, Bolewa, Tera, Tangale, Waja and Kanuri.</p>
<p>The LGA has an area of 52 km² and a population of 268,000 at the 2006 census.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Postal_code" title="Postal code">postal code</a> of the area is 760.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gombe,_Gombe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current Governor is <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Hassan_Dankwambo" title="Ibrahim Hassan Dankwambo">Ibrahim Hassan Dankwambo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gombe,_Gombe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gombe is served by <a href="/wiki/Gombe_Lawanti_International_Airport" title="Gombe Lawanti International Airport">Gombe Lawanti International Airport</a>, and by a station on the eastern mainline of the <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Nigeria" title="Rail transport in Nigeria">national railway network</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gombe_Line" title="Gombe Line">Gombe Line</a> Terminal in mid-city. On Feb. 1, President Goodluck Jonathan announced that the Gombe -Kafancha-Kaduna intercity train services had been "rehabilitated", saying "the inauguration of the train services was crucial to the growth and development of the Gombe and other adjoining states."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> "Minister of Transport, Idris Umar ... flagged off the Gombe-Kafanchan- Port Harcourt and Gombe- Kafanchan-Kaduna intercity train services."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_education">Culture and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gombe,_Gombe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gombe is home to <a href="/wiki/Gombe_State_University" title="Gombe State University">Gombe State University</a>, Federal University Kashere, College of Education Billiri and State Polytechnic Bajoga . The modern <a href="/wiki/Pantami_Stadium" title="Pantami Stadium">Pantami Stadium</a> has replaced the older <a href="/wiki/Abubakar_Umar_Memorial_Stadium" title="Abubakar Umar Memorial Stadium">Abubakar Umar Memorial Stadium</a>, and is home to the <a href="/wiki/Gombe_United_F.C." title="Gombe United F.C.">Gombe United F.C.</a></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gombe,_Gombe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008 an agreement was reached "to set up anti-venom manufacturing plant in Kaltungo General Hospital in Gombe," and provide medical training for snakebite patients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The Gombe State government ordered an additional N4 million in anti-venom for the hospital in 2009, due to a local increase in snakebites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Violence_in_2014_and_2015">Violence in 2014 and 2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gombe,_Gombe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Violence in 2014 and 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gombe has been impacted by the <a href="/wiki/Boko_Haram" title="Boko Haram">Boko Haram</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamist_insurgency_in_Nigeria" title="Islamist insurgency in Nigeria">Islamist insurgency in Nigeria</a>.</p>
<p>On 22 December 2014, a <a href="/wiki/2014_Gombe_bus_station_bombing" title="2014 Gombe bus station bombing">suicide bombing at a bus station in Gombe</a> killed over 20 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 January 2015, a suicide bomber attacked a New Year's Eve service at a Gombe church, injuring several people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 14 January 2015, a suicide bomber killed two people and left 14 wounded at a Gombe mosque.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 February 2015, just minutes after a campaign rally by Nigerian President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goodluck_Johnathan" title="Goodluck Johnathan">Goodluck Johnathan</a>, an attack by a female suicide bomber left one person dead and eighteen injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc20150202_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc20150202-13">[13]</a></sup> Two blasts had occurred in the city the previous day, leaving five people dead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Local residents "have set up checkpoints to defend Gombe" according to the BBC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc20150202_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc20150202-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
