<p><b>Gob</b> is a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_ensemble" title="Musical ensemble">band</a> from <a href="/wiki/Langley,_British_Columbia_(city)" title="Langley, British Columbia (city)">Langley, British Columbia</a>, formed in 1993. The band consists of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Thacker_(musician)" title="Tom Thacker (musician)">Tom Thacker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theo_Goutzinakis" title="Theo Goutzinakis">Theo Goutzinakis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabe_Mantle" title="Gabe Mantle">Gabe Mantle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Fairweather" title="Steven Fairweather">Steven Fairweather</a>. Juno nominated for best new group in 2000, and another Juno nomination for best video in 2002. Gob's most successful album is <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_According_to_Gob" title="World According to Gob">World According to Gob</a></i>. Their most successful songs to date are "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Hear_You_Calling_(Gob_song)" title="I Hear You Calling (Gob song)">I Hear You Calling</a>", "Soda", and "Banshee Song". They have been featured in movies, TV shows and many sporting video games such as <i><a href="/wiki/NHL_2002" title="NHL 2002">NHL 2002</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NHL_2003" title="NHL 2003">NHL 2003</a>, NHL 2004</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Madden_NFL_2004" title="Madden NFL 2004">Madden NFL 2004</a></i> with songs such as "I've Been Up These Steps", "Sick With You" (both re-mixed for the game), "I Hear You Calling" and "Give Up The Grudge". The band has appeared at several music festivals, the most well-known being the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vans_Warped_Tour" title="Vans Warped Tour">Vans Warped Tour</a>. Most recently, the band has been noticed due to singer/guitarist Thacker's involvement as a lead guitarist in <a href="/wiki/Sum_41" title="Sum 41">Sum 41</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Gob">History of Gob</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gob_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of Gob">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gob.2C_Too_Late..._No_Friends_and_How_Far_Shallow_Takes_You_.281994.E2.80.932000.29"><i>Gob</i>, <i>Too Late... No Friends</i> and <i>How Far Shallow Takes You</i> (1994–2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gob_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Gob, Too Late... No Friends and How Far Shallow Takes You (1994–2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gob formed in 1993 consisting of Tom Thacker as lead guitarist and vocalist, <a href="/wiki/Theo_Goutzinakis" title="Theo Goutzinakis">Theo Goutzinakis</a> as rhythm/co-lead guitarist and vocalist, Patrick "Wolfman Pat" Paszana on drums, and Kelly Macauley on bass guitar. Thacker and Goutzinakis often switched between lead vocals with Goutzinakis playing the occasional lead guitar line. During the early days of the band, Tom usually kept his place as lead guitar while Theo had more vocal duties and focused on rhythm guitar. They recorded their self-titled <a href="/wiki/Gob_(Gob_album)" title="Gob (Gob album)"><i>Gob</i></a> in 1993 and released it in 1994 on Landspeed Records with the odd numbered tracks sung by Theo and the even numbered tracks sung by Tom. The tracks 1, 2 and 8 were re-recorded and released on their next album <i><a href="/wiki/Too_Late..._No_Friends" title="Too Late... No Friends">Too Late... No Friends</a></i>. Kelly Macauley was replaced by Jamie Fawkes and in 1995 Gob released <i><a href="/wiki/Too_Late..._No_Friends" title="Too Late... No Friends">Too Late... No Friends</a></i> on Mint Records and Landspeed Records. It was later reissued by Nettwerk in 2000. After the release of <i>Too Late... No Friends</i>, Gob had replaced many bassists until they found Craig Wood. "Wolfman" Pat left the band due to his daughter, Rhyleah, being born before the recording of "How Far Shallow Takes You", so the band replaced him with Gabe Mantle, former member of a Vancouver Punk band <a href="/wiki/Brand_New_Unit" title="Brand New Unit">Brand New Unit</a>. With a newly formed band, Gob released <i><a href="/wiki/How_Far_Shallow_Takes_You" title="How Far Shallow Takes You">How Far Shallow Takes You</a></i>. It was home to a heavier sound with a better production value, mature lyrics focusing on personal and political issues and a growth in musicianship in contrast to "Too Late... No Friends" snotty, immature attitude. It was released on Fearless records in 1998 but re-released in 1999 on Landspeed because of conflicts with Fearless. It was also re-released on <a href="/wiki/Nettwerk" title="Nettwerk">Nettwerk records</a> that same year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_World_According_to_Gob_and_Foot_in_Mouth_Disease_.282000.E2.80.9304.29"><i>The World According to Gob</i> and <i>Foot in Mouth Disease</i> (2000–04)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gob_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The World According to Gob and Foot in Mouth Disease (2000–04)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gob released <i><a href="/wiki/The_World_According_to_Gob" title="The World According to Gob">World According to Gob</a></i>, in 2001. This album was Gob's most successful album to date; featuring one of their biggest hits "I Hear You Calling." Musically, the sound was a continuation of their previous 1990s-era punk rock sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Other singles released from <i>World According To</i> include "For The Moment", "That's The Way" and "No Regrets". The album was certified Gold by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Canadian Recording Industry Association">CRIA</a> in May 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, Gob recorded the <i><a href="/wiki/F.U._EP" title="F.U. EP">F.U. EP</a></i> as a promotional record for their new label <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a>. "Ming Tran" was released as a single for the EP which also featured a re-recorded version of a classic Gob song "Soda", two exclusive tracks, "L.A Song" and "Sick With You," as well as new tracks from the forthcoming full-length album <i><a href="/wiki/Foot_in_Mouth_Disease" title="Foot in Mouth Disease">Foot in Mouth Disease</a></i>, which was released on <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> in April 2003. Singles from <i>Foot in Mouth Disease</i> include "Give Up the Grudge", "Oh! Ellin", "Ming Tran" and "This Evil World". Unlike the band's other albums, Theo only provides lead vocals on the album's twelfth (eleventh on Canadian version) track "Bully".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Craig_Wood.27s_departure.2C_Steven_Fairweather.27s_arrival_and_Muertos_Vivos_.282004.E2.80.9308.29">Craig Wood's departure, Steven Fairweather's arrival and <i>Muertos Vivos</i> (2004–08)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gob_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Craig Wood's departure, Steven Fairweather's arrival and Muertos Vivos (2004–08)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, longtime bassist Wood left the band to play guitar for Canadian superstar <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a>. The band have since recruited a bassist known only to fans as "Peter Pan" or, more commonly, Tyson, as Gob's members are on a "first-name basis" with their fans. (All albums credit the musicians simply by their first names, as well.)</p>
<p>Gob's friendship with several Canadian bands has come up often in the past few years. During Gob's downtime, Thacker has played lead guitar for <a href="/wiki/Sum_41" title="Sum 41">Sum 41</a> live since 2007 because of the departure of their lead guitarist, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Baksh" title="Dave Baksh">Dave Baksh</a>. Since then, Thacker has become Baksh's permanent replacement in Sum 41.</p>
<p>Their fifth full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/Muertos_Vivos" title="Muertos Vivos">Muertos Vivos</a></i>, was released on Cobraside November 27, 2007, in the U.S. and was released in Canada on Aquarius Records October 23. They recruited Tyson Maiko as their bassist.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Muertos_Vivos" title="Muertos Vivos">Muertos Vivos</a></i> was heavier than any of the band's previous efforts, and edged into <a href="/wiki/Alternative_metal" title="Alternative metal">alternative metal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a>, with some earlier <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_punk" title="Hardcore punk">hardcore punk</a> influences.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The album's first single was "We're All Dying". They shot the video for "We're All Dying", as well as for their second single "Underground", on September 4 and 5, 2007, in downtown Vancouver. The album's final single was "Banshee Song".</p>
<p>In mid-2008, bassist Tyson Maiko left the band and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Fairweather" title="Steven Fairweather">Steven Fairweather</a> who was a long time friend of the band. Even having them record most of their fifth full-length album <i><a href="/wiki/Muertos_Vivos" title="Muertos Vivos">Muertos Vivos</a></i> in his childhood home. <a href="/wiki/Steven_Fairweather" title="Steven Fairweather">Steven Fairweather</a> had played in past bands including <a href="/wiki/Revelation_Records" title="Revelation Records">Revelation Records</a>' <a href="/wiki/Post-hardcore" title="Post-hardcore">post-hardcore</a> band By a Thread,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and appeared on demo versions of songs by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sub_pop" title="Sub pop">sub pops</a>' <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Halos" title="The Black Halos">the Black Halos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="APT_13_and_upcoming_documentary_film_.282009.E2.80.93present.29">APT 13 and upcoming documentary film (2009–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gob_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: APT 13 and upcoming documentary film (2009–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 2009, the band toured Canada, after which, in April, Thacker joined up with <a href="/wiki/Sum_41" title="Sum 41">Sum 41</a> for their Japanese and US tour.</p>
<p>In an interview with AMP Magazine, guitarist <a href="/wiki/Theo_Goutzinakis" title="Theo Goutzinakis">Theo Goutzinakis</a> revealed that the band had already started writing for their sixth studio album, which would probably be released in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Thacker also revealed that he and Theo were currently in the studio, producing the first record by new <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band Floodlight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2009, Gob revealed that their close friends are working on a <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary film</a> about the band, to be released on DVD, or air on TV, sometime in the future. The film will follow the band since formation and until today.</p>
<p>On September 11, 2009, the band said on their MySpace account that they would soon record the new album. Frontman Thacker confirmed on his <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> that recording of the new Gob album would start in March 2010.</p>
<p>On March 13, 2010, in a chat with a fansite, Thacker revealed that the drums for the album are already recorded and there are still vocals, guitars and bass left to record. Thacker also revealed the musical direction of the album will be more <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_n%27_roll" title="Rock n' roll">rock n' roll</a> influenced, rather than the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> influences on <i><a href="/wiki/Muertos_Vivos" title="Muertos Vivos">Muertos Vivos</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Thacker also said that the upcoming documentary about the band will be made into a documentary about the recording of the new album, rather than about the band's whole history.</p>
<p>In summer 2010, Thacker confirmed that the band will continue working on their upcoming album, upon his return from the <a href="/wiki/Sum_41" title="Sum 41">Sum 41</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Screaming_Bloody_Murder_Tour" title="Screaming Bloody Murder Tour">Screaming Bloody Murder Tour</a> in the fall of 2010. While touring with Sum 41, he has confirmed that Sum 41 has extended and touring with the band will take more time than he expected, delaying the recording of his parts in Gob's upcoming album. Meanwhile, other members of Gob are busy with their day jobs.</p>
<p>On January 11, 2011, Thacker said via a <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> message that he is home recording vocals for Gob's upcoming album. He hoped to finish it within two weeks, though it was yet unknown when the album is going to be mixed and mastered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 28, 2011, in an interview that took place with a Sum 41 fansite, Thacker announced that although the band had been on-and-off recording the album for the past two years already, there are still some vocals to record, as well as background vocals, which Thacker plans to record at home. He has commented that recording for the new album took more than expected because of his busy schedule with Sum 41, and that the band members are recording and producing the album by themselves.</p>
<p>On January 12, 2012, Thacker confirmed that he will be finishing vocals for the new album soon, which will be followed by Goutzinakis mixing it. The album is expected for a later 2012 release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 20, 2012, it was announced that guitarist <a href="/wiki/Theo_Goutzinakis" title="Theo Goutzinakis">Theo Goutzinakis</a> is mixing the band's new album in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, while lead singer/guitarist <a href="/wiki/Tom_Thacker_(musician)" title="Tom Thacker (musician)">Tom Thacker</a> was getting ready to go on tour again with <a href="/wiki/Sum_41" title="Sum 41">Sum 41</a> in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2013, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Thacker_(musician)" title="Tom Thacker (musician)">Tom Thacker</a>'s wedding was featured in <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 8, 2013, Tom Thacker tweeted on his <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account that Gob's sixth studio album will be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Apt._13" title="Apt. 13">Apt. 13</a></i> and posted a teaser video for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 27, 2014, Gob announced that <i><a href="/wiki/Apt._13" title="Apt. 13">Apt. 13</a></i> will be released on August 26 through New Damage Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 6, 2014, the band announced their upcoming Canadian tour, with Canadian punk band Seaway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
