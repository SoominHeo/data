<p><i><b>Gopchang jeongol</b></i> is a spicy <a href="/wiki/Korean_cuisine" title="Korean cuisine">Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Stew" title="Stew">stew</a> or <a href="/wiki/Casserole" title="Casserole">casserole</a> made by boiling beef <a href="/wiki/Tripe" title="Tripe">tripe</a>, vegetables, and seasonings in beef <a href="/wiki/Broth" title="Broth">broth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lifeinkorea_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-lifeinkorea-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>Gopchang</i> refers to <a href="/wiki/Small_intestine" title="Small intestine">small intestines</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a> or <a href="/wiki/Chitterlings" title="Chitterlings">chitterlings</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MK-4">[4]</a></sup> while <i><a href="/wiki/Jeongol" title="Jeongol">jeongol</a></i> refers to a category of <a href="/wiki/Stew" title="Stew">stew</a> or <a href="/wiki/Casserole" title="Casserole">casserole</a> in <a href="/wiki/Korean_cuisine" title="Korean cuisine">Korean cuisine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nate_English_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nate_English-5">[5]</a></sup> Although the dish is mainly based on beef <i>gopchang</i>, other parts of beef tripe are also used to give the dish a richer flavor and chewy texture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nate_Korean_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nate_Korean-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>To remove any odor and excessive fat in the dish, any white <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rendering_(food_processing)" title="Rendering (food processing)">fat</a> adhering to the intestines should be meticulously cleaned out by rubbing them with <a href="/wiki/Wheat_flour" title="Wheat flour">wheat flour</a> and salt and washing them several times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyber_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-encyber-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daegu_Ilbo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daegu_Ilbo-8">[8]</a></sup> Since cooking the dish requires not only specialized cooking techniques and a great deal of labor to prepare, but also very fresh ingredients, <i>gopchang jeongol</i> is generally eaten at restaurants specializing in tripe dishes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-MK-4">[4]</a></sup> The dish, which is seasoned with a hot and spicy <a href="/wiki/Chili_pepper" title="Chili pepper">red chili</a>-based sauce, is served as a main <a href="/wiki/Entr%C3%A9e" title="Entrée">entrée</a>, accompanied with a bowl of <a href="/wiki/Steamed_rice" title="Steamed rice">steamed rice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lifeinkorea_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-lifeinkorea-2">[2]</a></sup> It is often shared between several diners on the center of the table, and is also a popular <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anju_(dish)" title="Anju (dish)">anju</a></i> when drinking alcoholic beverages such as <i><a href="/wiki/Soju" title="Soju">soju</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lifeinkorea_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-lifeinkorea-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daegu_Ilbo_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Daegu_Ilbo-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ingredients">Ingredients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gopchangjeongol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ingredients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koreans classify beef <a href="/wiki/Tripe" title="Tripe">tripe</a> into subcategories, which include <i>yang</i> (양), <i>beoljipui</i> (벌집위), <i>cheonyeop</i> (천엽), <i>makchang</i> (막창), <i>gopchang</i> (곱창, also called <i>sochang</i> 소창), and <i>daechang</i> (대창).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first chamber of a beef stomach is called <i>yang</i> and is high in <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">protein</a>. It has been recommended for a person in weak health to drink the broth of <i>yang</i> since ancient times. The outer portion of the intestines is hard and lumpy, with a lot of black <a href="/wiki/Biological_membrane" title="Biological membrane">membranes</a> and unclean materials attached between the projections, so those should be thoroughly cleaned out before cooking. <i>Gopchang</i> (small intestines of cattle) is commonly referred to as such because of its curvy shape<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daegu_Ilbo_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Daegu_Ilbo-8">[8]</a></sup> rather than as <i>sochang</i>, which literally means "small intestines". It is the counterpart of <i>daechang</i>, meaning "large intestines". Compared to other cuts of meat, <i>gopchang</i> is high in <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitamin" title="Vitamin">vitamins</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="compared to liver, heart? (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is relatively inexpensive and has a characteristic flavor and a chewy yet palatable texture, so it is used in many Korean dishes such as <i><a href="/wiki/Gui_(food)" title="Gui (food)">gui</a></i> (grilled dishes) or <i><a href="/wiki/Bokkeum" title="Bokkeum">bokkeum</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Stir_frying" title="Stir frying">stir-fried</a> dishes).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyber_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-encyber-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daegu_Ilbo_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-Daegu_Ilbo-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">Preparation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gopchangjeongol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The broth for the dish can be either a mixture of two broths, made from beef tripe and <a href="/wiki/Brisket" title="Brisket">brisket</a>, respectively, or <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gomtang" title="Gomtang">gomtang</a></i>, a thin soup with a rich flavor made by simmering beef bones for a long time. If choosing the first type, the beef tripe and brisket have to be boiled separately because beef tripe has strong flavor that can affect the brisket broth and the meat. To make the beef tripe broth, cleaned beef tripe, onions, <a href="/wiki/Scallion" title="Scallion">scallions</a>, garlic, and ginger are placed in a large pot with a large amount of water and boiled until thoroughly cooked. Likewise, the brisket broth is prepared using the same method, using the same secondary ingredients to remove any bad odors that might persist. After that, both broths are chilled, and any fat floating on the surface is skimmed off. The cooked brisket is cut into bite-sized pieces, while the <i>yang</i> and <i>gopchang</i> are cut into long, diagonal strips. The boiled beef tripe and brisket are marinated with seasonings and kneaded for a long time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyber_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-encyber-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oyster_mushroom" title="Oyster mushroom">Oyster mushrooms</a> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parboil" title="Parboil">parboiled</a> in salted boiling water, slightly pressed to squeeze water, torn by their <a href="/wiki/Grain" title="Grain">grain</a>, and seasoned. Dried <a href="/wiki/Shiitake" title="Shiitake">shiitake</a> mushrooms are soaked in lukewarm water, and if a small amount of sugar is added to the water, that helps the mushrooms to rehydrate more quickly. The <a href="/wiki/Stipe_(mycology)" title="Stipe (mycology)">stipes</a> of the mushroom are taken out, and the caps are shredded and seasoned. Green chili peppers are seeded and chopped and onions and carrots are chopped into pieces the same size as the peppers. Scallions are cut into long diagonal strips.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyber_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-encyber-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a pan, the remaining vegetables are placed on the bottom, and the marinated beef tripe and brisket are placed over them in a circular fashion. The shredded mushrooms and sliced vegetables are arranged between the tripe and meat. The prepared broths are mixed together, seasoned with salt, and poured little by little into the pan. When the dish is cooked, any floating foam is skimmed off and the <i>gopchang jeongol</i> is served.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyber_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-encyber-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
