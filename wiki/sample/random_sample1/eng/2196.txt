<p><i><b>Heteropneustes</b></i>, the <b>airsac catfishes</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Catfish" title="Catfish">catfishes</a> native to <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. This genus is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotypic" title="Monotypic">only one</a> in its family.</p>
<p>Their bodies are elongated and compressed with greatly depressed heads. They have long air sac that serve as lungs that extends from the <a href="/wiki/Gill" title="Gill">gill</a> chamber. The dorsal fin is short and has no spine. The pectoral fins have an associated venom gland and is considered dangerous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nelson_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nelson-1">[1]</a></sup> They are <a href="/wiki/Oviparity" title="Oviparity">oviparous</a>; distinct pairing is possible.</p>
