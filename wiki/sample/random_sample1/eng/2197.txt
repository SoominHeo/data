<p>The <b>Labour Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">工黨</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Centre-left_politics" title="Centre-left politics">centre-left</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_democratic" title="Social democratic">social democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> established in 2011. Chaired by <a href="/wiki/Suzanne_Wu" title="Suzanne Wu">Suzanne Wu</a>, it currently holds one seat in the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Council_of_Hong_Kong" title="Legislative Council of Hong Kong">Legislative Council of Hong Kong</a> and three seats in the <a href="/wiki/District_Councils_of_Hong_Kong" title="District Councils of Hong Kong">District Councils</a>.</p>
<p>The party was founded in 2011 by three veteran <a href="/wiki/Pro-democracy_camp" title="Pro-democracy camp">pro-democracy</a> legislators to consolidate the centre-left pro-labour pro-democracy voices in the legislature. Led by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Cheuk-yan" title="Lee Cheuk-yan">Lee Cheuk-yan</a>, the long-time general secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Confederation_of_Trade_Unions" title="Hong Kong Confederation of Trade Unions">Hong Kong Confederation of Trade Unions</a> (HKCTU), the party won four seats in the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_legislative_election,_2012" title="Hong Kong legislative election, 2012">2012 Legislative Council election</a> and received about six per cent of the popular votes, making it the third largest political party in the pro-democracy camp and sixth largest in the legislature.</p>
<p>It received a big loss in the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_legislative_election,_2016" title="Hong Kong legislative election, 2016">2016 election</a> in which two veterans Lee Cheuk-yan and <a href="/wiki/Cyd_Ho" title="Cyd Ho">Cyd Ho</a> lost in the direct election, which left the party only one seat in the legislature, occupied by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Cheung" title="Fernando Cheung">Fernando Cheung</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Territories_East_(constituency)" title="New Territories East (constituency)">New Territories East</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beliefs">Beliefs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labour_Party_(Hong_Kong)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Beliefs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Labour Party positions itself as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_democratic" title="Social democratic">social democratic</a> party with the principles of "Democracy, Justice, Sustainability and Solidarity". It also demands <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_suffrage_in_Hong_Kong" title="Universal suffrage in Hong Kong">universal suffrage</a>, legislation of <a href="/wiki/Competition_law" title="Competition law">competition law</a> and maximum weekly working hours, rehabilitate the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiananmen_protests_of_1989" title="Tiananmen protests of 1989">Tiananmen protests of 1989</a> and against the legislation of <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Basic_Law_Article_23" title="Hong Kong Basic Law Article 23">Article 23</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Labour Party is the first main party that has declared to put their policy statement on support laws that prohibit discrimination against the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labour_Party_(Hong_Kong)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The idea of establishing a pro-labour party first emerged in the 1990s, when four pro-labour <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro-democracy" title="Pro-democracy">pro-democracy</a> legislators, <a href="/wiki/Lau_Chin-shek" title="Lau Chin-shek">Lau Chin-shek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Cheuk-yan" title="Lee Cheuk-yan">Lee Cheuk-yan</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Confederation_of_Trade_Unions" title="Hong Kong Confederation of Trade Unions">Hong Kong Confederation of Trade Unions</a> (CTU), <a href="/wiki/Leung_Yiu-chung" title="Leung Yiu-chung">Leung Yiu-chung</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Neighbourhood_and_Worker%27s_Service_Centre" title="Neighbourhood and Worker's Service Centre">Neighbourhood and Worker's Service Centre</a> (NWSC) and <a href="/wiki/Tsang_Kin-shing" title="Tsang Kin-shing">Tsang Kin-shing</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Hong_Kong)" title="Democratic Party (Hong Kong)">Democratic Party</a> set up a joint office in preparation of the labour party, but the idea did not realise at last.</p>
<p>In early 2011, Lee Cheuk-yan expressed interest in forming a new labour party, and discussed the details with legislators Leung Yiu-chung, <a href="/wiki/Cheung_Kwok-che" title="Cheung Kwok-che">Cheung Kwok-che</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Social_Workers%27_General_Union" title="Hong Kong Social Workers' General Union">Hong Kong Social Workers' General Union</a> (SWGU), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyd_Ho_Sau-lan" title="Cyd Ho Sau-lan">Cyd Ho Sau-lan</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Civic_Act-up" title="Civic Act-up">Civic Act-up</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fernando_Cheung_Chiu-hung" title="Fernando Cheung Chiu-hung">Fernando Cheung Chiu-hung</a>, an ex-<a href="/wiki/Civic_Party" title="Civic Party">Civic Party</a> politician. Cheung was the first to advocate the formation of a labour party for the labour rights, <a href="/wiki/New_immigrants_in_Hong_Kong" title="New immigrants in Hong Kong">new immigrants</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Hong_Kong" title="Ethnic groups in Hong Kong">ethnic minorities</a> and environmental issues in the coming <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_legislative_election,_2012" title="Hong Kong legislative election, 2012">2012 Legislative Council election</a>.</p>
<p>The Labour Party was officially founded on 18 December 2011. The New World First Bus Company Staff Union, the K.M.B. Staff Union and the Hong Kong Buildings Management And Security Workers General Union under the CTU and Civic Act-up joined the party as affiliated groups. The NWSC vetoed the motion of joining the Labour Party. 20-strong Executive Committee was elected with CTU General Secretary Lee Cheuk-yan was elected uncontestedly by 131 founding members, while Cyd Ho, Fernando Cheung and Yeung Ho-yan became the Vice-Chairmen, Cheung Kwok-che the Senate Chairman and Tam Chun-yin the General Secretary.</p>
<p>The party filled three lists in the <a href="/wiki/Geographical_constituency" title="Geographical constituency">geographical constituency</a> election in the 2012 Legislative Council elections, the incumbent Lee Cheuk-yan and Cyd Ho ran in <a href="/wiki/New_Territories_West_(constituency)" title="New Territories West (constituency)">New Territories West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Island_(constituency)" title="Hong Kong Island (constituency)">Hong Kong Island</a> and former legislator Fernando Cheung ran in <a href="/wiki/New_Territories_East_(constituency)" title="New Territories East (constituency)">New Territories East</a>. The party secured four seats with all the lists were elected and Cheung Kwok-che returned through the <a href="/wiki/Social_Welfare_(constituency)" title="Social Welfare (constituency)">Social Welfare</a> <a href="/wiki/Functional_constituency_(Hong_Kong)" title="Functional constituency (Hong Kong)">functional constituency</a>.</p>
<p>The party gained its first seat in the district level in the <a href="/wiki/San_Fu_(constituency)" title="San Fu (constituency)">San Fu</a> by-election in 2015. In the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_local_elections,_2015" title="Hong Kong local elections, 2015">2015 District Council election</a>, the party filled in 12 candidates and 3 of them were elected. On 13 December 2015, the Labour chairman Lee Cheuk-yan stepped down and three candidates, Kwok Wing-kin, Cheng Sze-lut and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suzanne_Wu_Shui-shan" title="Suzanne Wu Shui-shan">Suzanne Wu Shui-shan</a> ran for the chairmanship. Suzanne Wu of the Association for the Advancement of Feminism won the election in the end and became the new chairwoman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Labour suffered a major defeat In the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_legislative_election,_2016" title="Hong Kong legislative election, 2016">2016 Legislative Council election</a> by losing two veteran legislators <a href="/wiki/Lee_Cheuk-yan" title="Lee Cheuk-yan">Lee Cheuk-yan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyd_Ho" title="Cyd Ho">Cyd Ho</a>. With the retiring Social Welfare legislator Cheung Kwok-che, the party dropped their seats in the legislature from four to only one. Chairwoman Suzanne Wu, who was running in <a href="/wiki/Kowloon_East_(constituency)" title="Kowloon East (constituency)">Kowloon East</a> and pulled out from the race in the last days resigned for the election results.</p>
