<p><b>Gongsun Gong</b> was a minor warlord during the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_Dynasty" title="Han Dynasty">Han Dynasty</a> and <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms" title="Three Kingdoms">Three Kingdoms</a> period of <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">Chinese history</a>. He was the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Gongsun_Kang" title="Gongsun Kang">Gongsun Kang</a> and son of <a href="/wiki/Gongsun_Du" title="Gongsun Du">Gongsun Du</a>. Gongsun Gong advised his brother to kill <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Xi" title="Yuan Xi">Yuan Xi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Shang" title="Yuan Shang">Yuan Shang</a> and deliver their heads to <a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a>. After the death of Gongsun Kang, Gongsun Gong inherited the lordship of Xiangping. <a href="/wiki/Cao_Pi" title="Cao Pi">Cao Pi</a> confirmed the lordship and gave Gongsun Gong the rank of General of the Flying Cavalry. In 228, <a href="/wiki/Gongsun_Yuan" title="Gongsun Yuan">Gongsun Yuan</a>, the son of Gongsun Kang, took away his uncle's power and imprisoned him. He was released after <a href="/wiki/Sima_Yi" title="Sima Yi">Sima Yi</a> totally defeated Gongsun Yuan in 238.</p>
