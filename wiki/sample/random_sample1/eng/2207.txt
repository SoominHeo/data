<p>A <b>craft</b> is a pastime or a <a href="/wiki/Profession" title="Profession">profession</a> that requires particular skills and knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Skilled_worker" title="Skilled worker">skilled work</a>. In a historical sense, particularly as pertinent to the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> and earlier, the term is usually applied to people occupied in small-scale production of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Good_(economics)" title="Good (economics)">goods</a>, or their <a href="/wiki/Maintenance,_repair,_and_operations" title="Maintenance, repair, and operations">maintenance</a>, for example by <a href="/wiki/Tinker" title="Tinker">tinkers</a>. The traditional terms <i><a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Craftsman_(disambiguation)" title="Craftsman (disambiguation)">craftsman</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Craftswoman" title="Craftswoman">craftswoman</a></i> are nowadays often replaced by <i><a href="/wiki/Artisan" title="Artisan">artisan</a></i> and rarely by <i>craftsperson</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Craftspeople" title="Craftspeople">craftspeople</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_from_the_past_until_today">Development from the past until today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development from the past until today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historically, <a href="/wiki/Artisan" title="Artisan">craftsmen</a> tended to concentrate in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_centers" title="Urban centers">urban centers</a> and formed <a href="/wiki/Guild" title="Guild">guilds</a>. The skill required by their professions is and the need to be permanently involved in the <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">exchange of goods</a> also demanded a generally higher level of <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, and craftsmen were usually in a more privileged position than the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peasantry" title="Peasantry">peasantry</a> in <a href="/wiki/Complex_society" title="Complex society">societal hierarchy</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Households" title="Households">households</a> of craftsmen were not as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-sufficient" title="Self-sufficient">self-sufficient</a> as those of people engaged in <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural work</a> and therefore had to rely on the exchange of goods.</p>
<p>Once an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apprentice" title="Apprentice">apprentice</a> of a craft had finished his <a href="/wiki/Apprenticeship" title="Apprenticeship">apprenticeship</a>, he would become a <a href="/wiki/Journeyman" title="Journeyman">journeyman</a> searching for a place to set up his own <a href="/wiki/Workshop" title="Workshop">shop</a> and make a living. After he set up his own shop, he could then call himself a <a href="/wiki/Master_craftsman" title="Master craftsman">master of his craft</a>.</p>
<p>This system of a stepwise approach to mastery of a craft, which includes the obtainment of a certain amount of education and the learning of skills, has survived in some countries of the world until today. But crafts have undergone deep structural changes during and since the era of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">mass production</a> of goods by large-scale <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a> has limited crafts to market segments in which industry's modes of functioning or its mass-produced goods would not or cannot satisfy the preferences of potential buyers. Moreover, as an outcome of these changes, craftspeople today increasingly make use of semi-finished components or materials and adapt these to their customers' requirements or demands and, if necessary, to the <a href="/wiki/Built_environment" title="Built environment">environments</a> of their customers. Thus, they participate in a certain <a href="/wiki/Division_of_labour" title="Division of labour">division of labour</a> between industry and craft.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Outline_of_crafts" title="Outline of crafts">Outline of crafts</a></div>
<p>There are three aspects to human creativity - Art, Crafts, and Science. Roughly determinated, art relies upon intuitive sensing, vision and expression, crafts upon sophisticated technique and science upon knowledge.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Handicraft">Handicraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Handicraft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Handicraft" title="Handicraft">Handicraft</a></div>
<p><b>Handicraft</b> is the "<a href="/wiki/Tradition" title="Tradition">traditional</a>" main sector of the crafts, it is a type of work where useful and decorative devices are made completely by hand or by using only simple tools. Usually the term is applied to traditional means of making goods. The individual <a href="/wiki/Artisan" title="Artisan">artisanship</a> of the items is a paramount criterion, such items often have cultural and/or religious significance. Items made by <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">mass production</a> or machines are not handicraft goods. Handicraft goods are made with <a href="/wiki/Craft_production" title="Craft production">craft production</a> processes.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Arts_and_Crafts_Movement">The Arts and Crafts Movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Arts and Crafts Movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arts_and_Crafts_Movement" title="Arts and Crafts Movement">Arts and Crafts Movement</a></div>

<p>The term <i>crafts</i> is often used to describe the family of artistic practices within the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decorative_art" title="Decorative art">decorative arts</a> that traditionally are defined by their relationship to functional or utilitarian products (such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sculptural" title="Sculptural">sculptural</a> forms in the vessel tradition) or by their use of such natural media as <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ceramic" title="Ceramic">ceramics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glass" title="Glass">glass</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Textiles" title="Textiles">textiles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metal</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Studio_crafts">Studio crafts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Studio crafts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Studio_craft" title="Studio craft">Studio craft</a></div>
<p>Crafts practiced by independent artists working alone or in small groups are often referred to as <b>studio craft</b>. Studio craft includes <a href="/wiki/Studio_pottery" title="Studio pottery">studio pottery</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metal_work" title="Metal work">metal work</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weaving" title="Weaving">weaving</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wood_turning" title="Wood turning">wood turning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a> and other forms of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wood_working" title="Wood working">wood working</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glass_blowing" title="Glass blowing">glass blowing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Glass_art" title="Glass art">glass art</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Craft_fairs">Craft fairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Craft fairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A craft <a href="/wiki/Fair" title="Fair">fair</a> is an organized event to display and sell crafts. There are craft shops where such goods are sold and craft communities, such as <a href="/wiki/Craftster" title="Craftster">Craftster</a>, where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expertise" title="Expertise">expertise</a> is shared.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tradesperson">Tradesperson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tradesperson">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tradesperson" title="Tradesperson">Tradesperson</a></div>
<p>A <b>tradesperson</b> is a skilled manual worker in a particular trade or craft. Economically and socially, a tradesperson's status is considered between a <a href="/wiki/Laborer" title="Laborer">laborer</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Professional" title="Professional">professional</a>, with a high degree of both practical and theoretical knowledge of their trade. In cultures where professional careers are highly prized there can be a shortage of skilled manual workers, leading to lucrative niche markets in the trades.</p>
