<p><i><b>Callistephus</b></i> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/Ã¦/ short 'a' in 'bad'">Ã¦</span><span title="/Ë/ primary stress follows">Ë</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/Éª/ short 'i' in 'bid'">Éª</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/Éª/ or /É/ 'e' in 'roses'">áµ»</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="/É/ 'a' in 'about'">É</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Monotypic_taxon" title="Monotypic taxon">monotypic genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plants</a> in the aster family, <a href="/wiki/Asteraceae" title="Asteraceae">Asteraceae</a>, containing the single species <i><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Callistephus_chinensis" title="Callistephus chinensis">Callistephus chinensis</a></b></i>. Its common names include <b>China aster</b> and <b>annual aster</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grin_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-grin-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fl_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-fl-5">[5]</a></sup> It is native to <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grin_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-grin-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mobot_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mobot-6">[6]</a></sup> and it is cultivated worldwide as an <a href="/wiki/Ornamental_plant" title="Ornamental plant">ornamental plant</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-w-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-china_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-china-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>This is an annual or biennial herb with one erect, mostly unbranched stem growing 20 to 100 centimeters tall. The alternately arranged leaves vary in shape or size. The basal leaves usually fall away be flowering. Leaves around the middle of the stem are a few centimeters long and are borne on winged <a href="/wiki/Petiole_(botany)" title="Petiole (botany)">petioles</a>. The blades have serrated edges. Leaves toward the top may have smooth edges. The large solitary <a href="/wiki/Pseudanthium" title="Pseudanthium">flower head</a> grows at the top of the stem and sometimes on branches. The head is lined with layers of <a href="/wiki/Bract" title="Bract">phyllaries</a>, those in the outer layer large and leaflike, measuring up to 3 centimeters long. The inner layer can be purple-tinged. The head contains one or two rings of ray florets, most often in shades of reddish purple. Cultivated varieties can have ray florets in most any color, including red, pink, blue, purple, and white. They are up to 3.5 centimeters long. There are many yellow disc florets in the center. The fruit is a rough-textured, glandular, purple-mottled cypsela that turns gray with age. It has a <a href="/wiki/Pappus_(flower_structure)" title="Pappus (flower structure)">pappus</a> composed of one outer layer of reddish scales and two inner layers of white bristles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-china_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-china-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>This plant is a <a href="/wiki/Naturalisation_(biology)" title="Naturalisation (biology)">naturalized</a> species in some areas outside of its native range (Indochina, Europe, North America, Australia, New Zealand, etc.).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-w-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grin_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-grin-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>This species has been grown and bred extensively in cultivation. <a href="/wiki/Cultivar" title="Cultivar">Cultivars</a> are available with flowers of many colors, varying heights, and single and double heads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mobot_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-mobot-6">[6]</a></sup> The plants are susceptible to <a href="/wiki/Fusarium_wilt" title="Fusarium wilt">fusarium wilt</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">fungal</a> disease. Some cultivars are more resistant to this than others. <a href="/wiki/Pest_(organism)" title="Pest (organism)">Pest insects</a> can include <a href="/wiki/Leafhopper" title="Leafhopper">leafhoppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aphid" title="Aphid">aphids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blister_beetle" title="Blister beetle">blister beetles</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Tarnished_plant_bug" title="Tarnished plant bug">tarnished plant bug</a>. The plant is also susceptible to <a href="/wiki/Mite" title="Mite">mites</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fl_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-fl-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
