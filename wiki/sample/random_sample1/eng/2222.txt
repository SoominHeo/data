<p>The <b>Guadeloupe big-eyed bat</b> (<i><b>Chiroderma improvisum</b></i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bat</a> in the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phyllostomidae" title="Phyllostomidae">Phyllostomidae</a>. It is found in <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">Montserrat</a>. It is threatened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a> mostly because of Hurricane Hugo, which destroyed 90% of the population of this bat in 1989. The species may be locally extinct in some areas of Guadeloupe.</p>
