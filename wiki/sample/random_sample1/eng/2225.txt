<p><b>Guainía</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ɡwaiˈni.a]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Yuri_language_(Amazon)" title="Yuri language (Amazon)">Yuri language</a>: <i>Land of many waters</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Colombia" title="Departments of Colombia">department</a> of <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>. It is in the east of the country, bordering <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/In%C3%ADrida,_Guain%C3%ADa" title="Inírida, Guainía">Inírida</a>. In 1963 Guainía was split off from <a href="/wiki/Vaup%C3%A9s_Department" title="Vaupés Department">Vaupés department</a>. The northern part and the <a href="/wiki/In%C3%ADrida_River" title="Inírida River">Inírida River</a> are included in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orinoco_river" title="Orinoco river">Orinoco river</a> basin; the rest is part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazonia" title="Amazonia">Amazonia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Guaviare_River" title="Guaviare River">Guaviare River</a> is the main area of colonization, many <i>colonos</i> come from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andean_Natural_Region,_Colombia" title="Andean Natural Region, Colombia">Colombian Andean zone</a>, most of them from <a href="/wiki/Boyac%C3%A1_Department" title="Boyacá Department">Boyacá</a>. They are followed by the <i><a href="/wiki/Llanero" title="Llanero">llaneros</a></i>, people from the Eastern plains (<a href="/wiki/Los_Llanos_(South_America)" title="Los Llanos (South America)">Los Llanos</a>). The main population is composed by Amerindians, the largest ethnic groups are the <i><a href="/wiki/Puinave_language" title="Puinave language">Puinaves</a></i> (from the <i>makú-puinave</i> family) and the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baniwa_people" title="Baniwa people">curripacos</a></i> (from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arawak_people" title="Arawak people"><i>Arawak</i> family</a>). There are a total of 24 ethnic groups in the department, many of them speak four Indian languages besides <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipalities">Municipalities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guain%C3%ADa_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Municipalities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There's only one municipality in Guainía: Inírida, its capital. The rest of the territory is subdivided in <i><a href="/wiki/Corregimientos_of_Colombia" title="Corregimientos of Colombia">corregimientos departamentales</a></i>, a pending figure due to public disorder<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urlSentencia_C-141.2F01_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-urlSentencia_C-141.2F01-2">[2]</a></sup> This case happens only in <a href="/wiki/Amazonas_Department" title="Amazonas Department">Amazonas</a>, Vaupés and <a href="/wiki/Vichada_Department" title="Vichada Department">Vichada</a>. <a href="/wiki/Barranco_Minas" title="Barranco Minas">Barranco Minas</a> is the second biggest population and its main corregimiento; it is located in the Guaviare River.</p>
