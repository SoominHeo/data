<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Tourist attraction</h1>

<p>A <b>tourist attraction</b> is a place of interest where <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourists</a> visit, typically for its inherent or exhibited natural or cultural value, historical significance, natural or built beauty, offering leisure, adventure and amusement.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourist_attraction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="128" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg/170px-Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg/255px-Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg/340px-Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bali_religi%C3%B6s_ceremoni_p%C3%A5_strand_med_turister.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tropical beaches and <a href="/wiki/Bali#Culture" title="Bali">Balinese culture</a> are attractions that draw tourists to this popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Island_resort" title="Island resort">island resort</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Nyepi#Melasti" title="Nyepi">Melasti</a> rituals performed on the beach.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Natural beauty such as <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beaches</a>, tropical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Island_resort" title="Island resort">island resorts</a> with coral reefs, <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hiking</a> and <a href="/wiki/Camping" title="Camping">camping</a> in national <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">parks</a>, mountains and <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>, are examples of traditional tourist attractions to spend <a href="/wiki/Summer_vacation" title="Summer vacation">summer vacations</a>. Other examples of cultural tourist attractions include historical places, <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monuments</a>, ancient <a href="/wiki/Temple" title="Temple">temples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoo" title="Zoo">zoos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_aquarium" title="Public aquarium">aquaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museums</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_galleries" title="Art galleries">art galleries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botanical_garden" title="Botanical garden">botanical gardens</a>, buildings and structures (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">castles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">libraries</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">prisons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">skyscrapers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bridge" title="Bridge">bridges</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theme_park" title="Theme park">theme parks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">carnivals</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Living_history_museum" title="Living history museum">living history museums</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_enclave" title="Ethnic enclave">ethnic enclave</a> communities, <a href="/wiki/Heritage_railway" title="Heritage railway">historic trains</a> and cultural events. Factory tours, industrial heritage, creative art and crafts workshops are the object of cultural niches like <a href="/wiki/Industrial_tourism" title="Industrial tourism">industrial tourism</a> and creative tourism. Many tourist attractions are also <a href="/wiki/Landmark" title="Landmark">landmarks</a>.</p>
<p>Tourist attractions are also created to capitalise on legends such as a supposed <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_flying_object" title="Unidentified flying object">UFO</a> crash site near <a href="/wiki/Roswell,_New_Mexico" title="Roswell, New Mexico">Roswell, New Mexico</a> and the alleged <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loch_Ness_monster" title="Loch Ness monster">Loch Ness monster</a> sightings in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. Ghost sightings also make tourist attractions.</p>
<p>Ethnic communities may become tourist attractions, such as <a href="/wiki/Chinatown" title="Chinatown">Chinatowns</a> in the United States and the black British neighbourhood of <a href="/wiki/Brixton" title="Brixton">Brixton</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, England.</p>
<p>In the US, owners and marketers of attractions advertise tourist attractions on billboards along the side of highways and roadways, especially in remote areas. Tourist attractions often provide free promotional brochures and flyers in information centres, fast food restaurants, <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motel" title="Motel">motel</a> rooms or lobbies, and <a href="/wiki/Rest_area" title="Rest area">rest area</a>.</p>
<p>While some tourist attractions provide visitors a memorable experience for a reasonable admission charge or even for free, others can have a tendency to be of low quality and to overprice their goods and services (such as admission, food, and souvenirs) in order to profit from tourists excessively. Such places are commonly known as <a href="/wiki/Tourist_trap" title="Tourist trap">tourist traps</a>.</p>
<p>Within cities such transport tourist attractions as rides by boats and buses (<a href="/wiki/City_Sightseeing" title="City Sightseeing">City Sightseeing</a>, etc.) are very popular .</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Novelty_attraction">Novelty attraction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourist_attraction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Novelty attraction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Novelty attractions are oddities such as the "<a href="/wiki/Biggest_ball_of_twine" title="Biggest ball of twine">biggest ball of twine</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Cawker_City,_Kansas" title="Cawker City, Kansas">Cawker City, Kansas</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Corn_Palace" title="Corn Palace">Corn Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mitchell,_South_Dakota" title="Mitchell, South Dakota">Mitchell, South Dakota</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Carhenge" title="Carhenge">Carhenge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alliance,_Nebraska" title="Alliance, Nebraska">Alliance, Nebraska</a>, where old cars serve in the place of stones in a replica of <a href="/wiki/Stonehenge" title="Stonehenge">Stonehenge</a>. Novelty attractions are not limited to the American Midwest, but are part of <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States#Culture" title="Midwestern United States">Midwestern culture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourist_destination">Tourist destination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourist_attraction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tourist destination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Moss-landing-otter.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="900" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Moss-landing-otter.jpg/220px-Moss-landing-otter.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Moss-landing-otter.jpg/330px-Moss-landing-otter.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Moss-landing-otter.jpg/440px-Moss-landing-otter.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Moss-landing-otter.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Sea otters, like this one near <a href="/wiki/Moss_Landing,_California" title="Moss Landing, California">Moss Landing</a>, are a popular tourist attraction in the <a href="/wiki/Monterey_Bay" title="Monterey Bay">Monterey Bay, California</a> area.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A <b>tourist destination</b> is a <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a>, <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a>, or other area that is dependent to a significant extent on the revenues accruing from tourism, or "a country, state, region, city, or town which is marketed or markets itself as a place for tourists to visit".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It may contain one or more tourist attractions and possibly some "tourist traps." <a href="/wiki/Siem_Reap" title="Siem Reap">Siem Reap</a> town for example is a popular tourist destination in <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, mainly owed to its proximity to <a href="/wiki/Angkor" title="Angkor">Angkor</a> temples.</p>
<p>A tropical island resort is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> or <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> that also depends on tourism as its source of revenue. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> archipelago, <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">Bali</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phuket_Province" title="Phuket Province">Phuket</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palawan" title="Palawan">Palawan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a> in the Pacific, <a href="/wiki/Vamizi_Island" title="Vamizi Island">Vamizi Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibiza</a> in Mediterranean are examples of popular island resorts.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_Impact">Economic Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourist_attraction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economic Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tourism industry generates substantial economic benefits to both host countries and tourists home countries.• Especially in developing countries, one of the primary motivations for a region to promote itself as a tourism destination is the expected economic improvement. According to the World Tourism Organization:• 698 million people travelled to a foreign country in 2000, spending more US$ 478 billion. International tourism receipts combined with passenger transport currently total more than US$ 575 billion - making tourism the worlds number one export earner, ahead of automotive products, chemicals, petroleum and food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tourist attractions can:</p>
<ul>
<li>Contribute to government revenues, direct contributions are generated by taxes on incomes from tourism employment and tourism businesses, and by direct levies on tourists such as departure taxes.</li>
<li>Provide employment.</li>
<li>Stimulate infrastructure investment.</li>
<li>Contribute to local economies.</li>
<li>Provide foreign exchange earnings.</li>
</ul>
