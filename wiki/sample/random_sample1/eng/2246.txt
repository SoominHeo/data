<p><b>Kwan Chi-lam</b> (born <b>Kwan Kar-wai</b> on 24 September 1962), better known as <b>Rosamund Kwan</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> actress, best known for starring as the female lead "<a href="/wiki/Thirteenth_Aunt" title="Thirteenth Aunt">Thirteenth Aunt</a>" in the 1990s <a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_China_(film_series)" title="Once Upon a Time in China (film series)"><i>Once Upon a Time in China</i> film series</a>.</p>
<p>Although her last film role was in 2005, she officially announced her retirement from acting in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosamund_Kwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kwan was born in <a href="/wiki/British_Hong_Kong" title="British Hong Kong">British Hong Kong</a>. Her father, <a href="/wiki/Shaw_Brothers_Studio" title="Shaw Brothers Studio">Shaw Brothers</a> star <a href="/wiki/Kwan_Shan" title="Kwan Shan">Kwan Shan</a>, was from <a href="/wiki/Shenyang" title="Shenyang">Shenyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>, China and was of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchu" title="Manchu">Manchu</a> ethnicity. Her mother, Cheung Bing-sai (張冰茜), was from <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> and was also an actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Growing up, Kwan attended <a href="/wiki/Maryknoll_Convent_School" title="Maryknoll Convent School">Maryknoll Convent School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kowloon_Tong" title="Kowloon Tong">Kowloon Tong</a>.</p>
<p>Kwan's first acting role was in the <a href="/wiki/Asia_Television" title="Asia Television">ATV</a> <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i>Agency 24</i> (<span lang="zh-han">甜甜廿四味</span>). Her film debut was alongside <a href="/wiki/Chow_Yun-fat" title="Chow Yun-fat">Chow Yun-fat</a> in the 1982 film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Head_Hunter" title="The Head Hunter">The Head Hunter</a></i>. She appeared with <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">Jackie Chan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sammo_Hung" title="Sammo Hung">Sammo Hung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuen_Biao" title="Yuen Biao">Yuen Biao</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Twinkle,_Twinkle_Lucky_Stars" title="Twinkle, Twinkle Lucky Stars">Twinkle, Twinkle Lucky Stars</a></i> and again with Chan in <i><a href="/wiki/Project_A_Part_II" title="Project A Part II">Project A Part II</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Armour_of_God_(film)" title="Armour of God (film)">Armour of God</a></i>. She also appeared with <a href="/wiki/Jet_Li" title="Jet Li">Jet Li</a> in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Swordsman_II" title="Swordsman II">Swordsman II</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Wai_in_%22The_Scripture_with_No_Words%22" title='Dr. Wai in "The Scripture with No Words"'>Dr. Wai in "The Scripture with No Words"</a></i> and as Shao Yun a.k.a. "Sup Sum Yee" ("Shi San Yi" in Mandarin, "13th Aunt" in English) throughout the <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_China" title="Once Upon a Time in China">Once Upon a Time in China</a></i> film series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the majority of her acting roles were in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">dramatic films</a>, she is best known internationally for her roles in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_action_cinema" title="Hong Kong action cinema">Hong Kong action films</a>, alongside a variety of major stars of the genre.</p>
<p>Amongst her more dramatic roles, she notably appeared with <a href="/wiki/Andy_Lau" title="Andy Lau">Andy Lau</a> in several films including <i><a href="/wiki/Casino_Raiders" title="Casino Raiders">Casino Raiders</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wesley%27s_Mysterious_File" title="The Wesley's Mysterious File">The Wesley's Mysterious File</a></i>. Kwan also released a record in 1994, a duet with Lau called "Love Forever".</p>
<p>In 2001 she appeared in <a href="/wiki/Feng_Xiaogang" title="Feng Xiaogang">Feng Xiaogang</a>'s Chinese comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Shot%27s_Funeral" title="Big Shot's Funeral">Big Shot's Funeral</a></i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/Donald_Sutherland" title="Donald Sutherland">Donald Sutherland</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a> and Kwan as his assistant.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal">Personal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosamund_Kwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
