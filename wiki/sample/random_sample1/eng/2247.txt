<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of <i>EastEnders</i> characters (1985)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p>The following is a list of characters that first appeared in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i> in 1985, by order of first appearance. They were all introduced by executive producer <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. The first episode of <i>EastEnders</i> was broadcast on 19 February 1985, and twenty-three main characters were already created for their first appearance. The first character to be seen was <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur Fowler</a>, all of whom find <a href="#Reg_Cox">Reg Cox</a> dead. <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Skinner" title="Ethel Skinner">Ethel Skinner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harold_Legg" title="Harold Legg">Harold Legg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pauline_Fowler" title="Pauline Fowler">Pauline Fowler</a> appear, after Den alerts them of Reg's death. With Ethel is her pug <a href="/wiki/Willy_(EastEnders)" title="Willy (EastEnders)">Willy</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Lou_Beale" title="Lou Beale">Lou Beale</a>. <a href="/wiki/Saeed_Jeffery" title="Saeed Jeffery">Saeed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naima_Jeffery" title="Naima Jeffery">Naima Jeffery</a> are seen working in the local shop whilst <a href="/wiki/Angie_Watts" title="Angie Watts">Angie Watts</a> is seen in <a href="/wiki/The_Queen_Victoria" title="The Queen Victoria">The Queen Victoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>'s local pub. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cotton" title="Nick Cotton">Nick Cotton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue Osman</a> are next seen, whilst <a href="/wiki/Pete_Beale" title="Pete Beale">Pete</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kathy_Beale" title="Kathy Beale">Kathy Beale</a> work at the fruit and veg stall and <a href="#Hassan_Osman">Hassan Osman</a> is seen with his parents in the café. <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Watts" title="Sharon Watts">Sharon Watts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian Beale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Fowler" title="Michelle Fowler">Michelle Fowler</a> are next seen and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Fowler" title="Mark Fowler">Mark Fowler</a> is seen going into the bookies. Lastly, <a href="/wiki/Roly" title="Roly">Roly</a> the dog is seen in the pub when a fight breaks out.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lofty_Holloway" title="Lofty Holloway">Lofty Holloway</a> is introduced on 26 February along with <a href="#Terry_Rich">Terry Rich</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Carpenter" title="Tony Carpenter">Tony Carpenter</a> is introduced on 28 February. <a href="/wiki/Mary_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Mary Smith (EastEnders)">Mary Smith</a> and her daughter <a href="#Annie_Smith">Annie</a> are introduced on 5 March. Tony's son <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_Carpenter" title="Kelvin Carpenter">Kelvin</a> is introduced on 12 March. <a href="/wiki/Andy_O%27Brien_(EastEnders)" title="Andy O'Brien (EastEnders)">Andy O'Brien</a> is introduced on 21 March along with <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Wilkins" title="Debbie Wilkins">Debbie Wilkins</a>. <a href="#Chris_Smith">Chris Smith</a> was introduced on 7 May and <a href="/wiki/Mehmet_Osman" title="Mehmet Osman">Mehmet Osman</a> was introduced on 13 June as a recurring character. <a href="#Hannah_Carpenter">Hannah Carpenter</a> arrived on 25 June as Tony's wife and <a href="/wiki/Dot_Cotton" title="Dot Cotton">Dot Cotton</a> was introduced on 4 July followed by <a href="#Ernie_Mears">Ernie Mears</a> on 16 July. <a href="#Roy_Quick">Detective Sergeant Roy Quick</a> was introduced on 20 August as Walford's <a href="/wiki/Detective" title="Detective">detective</a>. <a href="/wiki/Martin_Fowler_(EastEnders)" title="Martin Fowler (EastEnders)">Martin Fowler</a> was born on 30 July and <a href="/wiki/Simon_Wicks" title="Simon Wicks">Simon Wicks</a> arrived on 5 October. Lastly, the wife of Mehmet, <a href="#Guizin_Osman">Guizin Osman</a> arrived on 22 October, and <a href="#Cassie_Carpenter">Cassie Carpenter</a> arrived as the daughter of Hannah and Tony.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Den_Watts">Den Watts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Den Watts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a></div>
<p><b>Dennis Alan "Den" Watts</b>, played by actor <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>, is the original landlord of <a href="/wiki/The_Queen_Victoria" title="The Queen Victoria">The Queen Victoria</a>. He became well known for his tabloid nickname, "Dirty Den". He perhaps is best remembered for his stormy love-hate relationship with his alcoholic first wife <a href="/wiki/Angie_Watts" title="Angie Watts">Angie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anita_Dobson" title="Anita Dobson">Anita Dobson</a>). After nearly 20 years of marriage, he hands divorce papers to her in the 1986 Christmas Day episode that was watched by a record-breaking 30.15 million viewers, more than half of the UK population at the time. He is also well known for his later involvement with the criminal gang known as <a href="/wiki/The_Firm_(EastEnders)" title="The Firm (EastEnders)">The Firm</a> that eventually leads to an attempt on his life in 1989. For 14 years, it was believed that he had been killed, but he returns to Walford in September 2003. Seventeen months later, his character is killed off again, this time for good, at the hands of his manipulative second wife <a href="/wiki/Chrissie_Watts" title="Chrissie Watts">Chrissie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tracy-Ann_Oberman" title="Tracy-Ann Oberman">Tracy-Ann Oberman</a>). Den was described by <i>EastEnders</i> executive producer <a href="/wiki/Louise_Berridge" title="Louise Berridge">Louise Berridge</a> as being arguably one of the most iconic soap characters ever.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ali_Osman">Ali Osman</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ali Osman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a></div>
<p><b>Ali Osman</b>, played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>, is a happy-go-lucky, easy going chap, which is in stark contrast to his highly strung wife, <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sandy_Ratcliff" title="Sandy Ratcliff">Sandy Ratcliff</a>). He has a compulsive addiction to gambling, which gets him into trouble on more than one occasion and his marriage ends in disaster after he has his wife sectioned. He is a member of the original <i>EastEnders</i> cast, appearing in the first episode on 19 February 1985. He remains with the show for nearly five years afterwards, making his final appearance on 10 October 1989. Ali Osman was one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Ali, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Cypriot" title="Turkish Cypriot">Turkish Cypriot</a>, was originally intended to be named Chris. His name was changed to Ali when it dawned on Holland that he had given a Christian name to a Muslim. Ali was a well-intentioned attempt to represent the proportion of Turkish Cypriots who had immigrated to England and settled in the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a>. Holland and Smith knew that for the soap to succeed there needed to be a varied group of characters, so that several different sections of the audience had someone to identify with.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arthur_Fowler">Arthur Fowler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arthur Fowler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur Fowler</a></div>
<p><b>Arthur George Fowler</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Treacher" title="Bill Treacher">Bill Treacher</a>, is the father of the <a href="/wiki/Beale_family" title="Beale family">Fowler family</a>. Arthur is essentially a good man, but makes some foolish choices and always ends up paying dearly for them. Bossed to the brink of insanity by his wife <a href="/wiki/Pauline_Fowler" title="Pauline Fowler">Pauline</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wendy_Richard" title="Wendy Richard">Wendy Richard</a>) and mother-in-law <a href="/wiki/Lou_Beale" title="Lou Beale">Lou Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anna_Wing" title="Anna Wing">Anna Wing</a>), Arthur falls into the arms of another woman. During his time in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> he suffers with a <a href="/wiki/Mental_disorder" title="Mental disorder">mental disorder</a>, is sent to prison twice and eventually dies of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brain_haemorrhage" title="Brain haemorrhage">brain haemorrhage</a> in 1996. Arthur Fowler was one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Arthur is a member of the first family of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Beale/Fowler_family" title="The Beale/Fowler family">the Beales and Fowlers</a>, and Holland took the inspiration for some of the series' earliest characters from his own London family and background. Arthur's original character outline as written by Smith and Holland appeared in an abridged form in their book, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EastEnders_books" title="EastEnders books">EastEnders: The Inside Story</a></i>.</p>

<p><b>Reginald "Reg" Cox</b>, played by Johnnie Clayton, is a resident of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>. The first episode of <i>EastEnders</i> on 19 February 1985 centres upon pensioner Reg's death. According to scriptwriter <a href="/wiki/Colin_Brake" title="Colin Brake">Colin Brake</a>, the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>, wanted to start the serial "with a bang, throwing the audience into the middle of life in [the fictional setting of] <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>". The Reg Cox storyline was deemed as a good starting point, allowing various members of the community to be involved with or comment on the circumstances of the old man's murder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10years_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-10years-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first ever scene of the programme, the characters of <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>), <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Treacher" title="Bill Treacher">Bill Treacher</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>) are shown breaking down the door of Reg's flat. In Holland and Smith's book, <i>The Inside Story</i>, the authors have outlined their original interpretation of the scene: "[EastEnders] starts with a bang, as a size ten boot kicks down the door that's locked from the inside. The tiny, dirty and foul-smelling council-flat behind the battered door belongs to Reg Cox (known locally as "the-old-boy", and a cantankerous bastard at the best of times) who hasn't been seen around [<a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>] for days.... Once the door's down, three men rush into the gloomy main-room.... They find the old boy sitting in his favourite armchair beside the gas fire (which isn't on) — and he's very nearly dead.... By the end of the episode the old boy will have been removed from the square and taken to Intensive Care, and the entire community will be rife with gossip, which spreads round the houses like the plague. Via the gossip we're able to piece together the events leading up to the discovery of Reg"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Storylines">Storylines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Storylines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reg lived alone at 23b Albert Square. On the morning of 19 February 1985, <a href="/wiki/Naima_Jeffery" title="Naima Jeffery">Naima Jeffery</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shreela_Ghosh" title="Shreela Ghosh">Shreela Ghosh</a>), who runs the shop on Bridge Street, became concerned about Reg after he had failed to make his daily milk purchase for the past three days and her husband, <a href="/wiki/Saeed_Jeffery" title="Saeed Jeffery">Saeed Jeffery</a> (<a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson_(actor)" title="Andrew Johnson (actor)">Andrew Johnson</a>), mentions this to <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>), publican of the pub next door. Den and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Treacher" title="Bill Treacher">Bill Treacher</a>) roused <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>), who lived downstairs from Reg, to let them into the house and the three of them kick in the door to Reg's room when he fails to answer their knocking. Den speaks the opening lines of the first episode, "Stinks in here, dunnit?", followed by the discovery of an unconscious Reg slumped in his armchair in the lounge of his neglected house. Den runs to get the local doctor, <a href="/wiki/Harold_Legg" title="Harold Legg">Harold Legg</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leonard_Fenton" title="Leonard Fenton">Leonard Fenton</a>), and calls for an ambulance. On 21 February 1985, they learn that Reg had been severely beaten and died from injuries which had gone unnoticed when he was found. It is eventually revealed that <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cotton" title="Nick Cotton">Nick Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/John_Altman_(actor)" title="John Altman (actor)">John Altman</a>) was behind the beating when he was attempting to steal Reg's war medals.</p>
<p>Reg, who was 59 years old when he died, was not a popular figure in Albert Square. He is referred to as a 'cantankerous old <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/git" title="wikt:git">git</a>' by Arthur, called a 'miserable so and so' by <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Skinner" title="Ethel Skinner">Ethel Skinner</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gretchen_Franklin" title="Gretchen Franklin">Gretchen Franklin</a>) and a 'nasty old man' by his downstairs neighbour, <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue Osman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sandy_Ratcliff" title="Sandy Ratcliff">Sandy Ratcliff</a>). But despite this, <a href="/wiki/Lou_Beale" title="Lou Beale">Lou Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anna_Wing" title="Anna Wing">Anna Wing</a>) causes havoc around the Square, accusing Sue of neglect for allowing him to lie dying in his flat for days, without checking up on him. The character also appears in the Christmas special <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CivvyStreet" title="CivvyStreet">CivvyStreet</a></i>, set during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, shown on 22 December 1988. The teenage Reg is played by Marc Tufano. He has gone <a href="/wiki/Desertion" title="Desertion">AWOL</a> and sells dodgy gear during the Second World War.</p>
<p>In 2014, Reg's murderer, Nick, used the name 'Reg Cox' as an alias after he faked his death. In 2015, Nick died in the same place that Reg died 30 years ago after admitting to his mother, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dot_Branning" title="Dot Branning">Dot Branning</a> (<a href="/wiki/June_Brown" title="June Brown">June Brown</a>), that he killed Reg.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethel_Skinner">Ethel Skinner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ethel Skinner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Skinner" title="Ethel Skinner">Ethel Skinner</a></div>
<p><b>Ethel May Skinner</b> played by <a href="/wiki/Gretchen_Franklin" title="Gretchen Franklin">Gretchen Franklin</a>, is an elderly resident of Walford. Ethel Skinner also features in a 1988 <i>EastEnders</i> special, entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CivvyStreet" title="CivvyStreet">CivvyStreet</a></i>, set on Albert Square during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, where the character is played by <a href="/wiki/Alison_Bettles" title="Alison Bettles">Alison Bettles</a>. Ethel is an <i>EastEnders</i> original character and in the early years she can often be found wandering the square with her adored pug <a href="/wiki/Willy_(EastEnders)" title="Willy (EastEnders)">Willy</a>. She and <a href="/wiki/Dot_Cotton" title="Dot Cotton">Dot Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/June_Brown" title="June Brown">June Brown</a>) are lifelong friends and although they wind each other up, they are completely dependent on each other. In fact Ethel trusts Dot so much that she even asks her to help her die in 2000, after she is diagnosed with inoperable cancer. Ethel's original character outline states that she was born in 1920. However, during the series this was altered to 1916 and for many years her birthday fell on 19 February. When Ethel comes back to Walford to die in 2000, she reveals to Dot that she had lied about her age for many years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pauline_Fowler">Pauline Fowler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pauline Fowler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pauline_Fowler" title="Pauline Fowler">Pauline Fowler</a></div>
<p><b>Pauline Fowler</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile-3">[3]</a></sup> played by <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Richard" title="Wendy Richard">Wendy Richard</a> between 1985 and 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gone_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gone-4">[4]</a></sup> is the wife of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Treacher" title="Bill Treacher">Bill Treacher</a>). Pauline was created by scriptwriter <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and producer <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a> as one of <i>EastEnders'</i> original characters. She makes her debut in the soap's first episode on 19 February 1985, and remains for twenty-one years and ten months, making her the second longest-running original character, surpassed only by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Adam_Woodyatt" title="Adam Woodyatt">Adam Woodyatt</a>). Pauline was a staple in the UK press during her time in <i>EastEnders</i>, representative of the symbiosis between Britain's soaps and tabloid newspapers. Widely read tabloids, such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sun_(newspaper)" title="The Sun (newspaper)">The Sun</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a></i>, would routinely publish articles about forthcoming developments in Pauline's storylines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Critical opinion on the character differs. She has been described as a "legend" and a <a href="/wiki/Cultural_icon" title="Cultural icon">television icon</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stage_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-stage-6">[6]</a></sup> but was also voted the 35th "most annoying person of 2006" (being the only fictional character to appear on the list).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Three_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Three-7">[7]</a></sup> The character is well known even outside the show's viewer-base, and away from the on-screen serial, Pauline has been the subject of television documentaries, behind-the-scenes books, tie-in novels and comedy sketch shows.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Harold_Legg">Harold Legg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Harold Legg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Harold_Legg" title="Harold Legg">Harold Legg</a></div>
<p><b>Doctor Harold Legg</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Fenton" title="Leonard Fenton">Leonard Fenton</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>'s original GP. He is widely trusted within the community, and is always on hand to dish out advice. Dr Legg appears as a regular character between 1985 and 1989, but continues to appear in a recurring role until 1997. He was officially retired in 1999 by executive producer <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Robinson_(producer)" title="Matthew Robinson (producer)">Matthew Robinson</a>, though he has made cameos since this time in 2000, 2004 and 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nadia_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nadia-8">[8]</a></sup> Dr. Harold Legg is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Dr. Legg is an attempt to represent the successive wave of Jewish immigrants that had settled in the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a> between 1881 and 1914 in order avoid the persecution that they were being subjected to in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> The second generation of East End born Jews (as Dr. Legg was meant to represent) prospered in the area until the 1930s when <a href="/wiki/Oswald_Mosley" title="Oswald Mosley">Oswald Mosley</a>'s British Union of Fascists was formed, and used violence to instil fear in the Jewish population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jew_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-jew-9">[9]</a></sup> As the Jewish community grew wealthier, many moved out of the East End to more affluent areas of London,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jew_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-jew-9">[9]</a></sup> just as the character of Dr. Legg had done on-screen when the show began; living in <a href="/wiki/Islington" title="Islington">Islington</a>, but commuting to his practice in Walford.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lou_Beale">Lou Beale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Lou Beale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lou_Beale" title="Lou Beale">Lou Beale</a></div>
<p><b>Louise Ada "Lou" Beale</b> played by <a href="/wiki/Anna_Wing" title="Anna Wing">Anna Wing</a>, is the matriarch of the <a href="/wiki/Beale_family" title="Beale family">Beale family</a>. The character is played by <a href="/wiki/Karen_Meagher" title="Karen Meagher">Karen Meagher</a> in the 1988 <i>EastEnders</i> special, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CivvyStreet" title="CivvyStreet">CivvyStreet</a></i>, set during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>. Lou Beale was the first <i>EastEnders</i> character to be created by series co-creator <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a>, taking the inspiration for some of the series' earliest characters from his own London family and background. Lou is the archetypal <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End</a> matriarch throughout <i>EastEnders</i> first three years. An intimidating force within the local community, she is the dowager of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>'s central family, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Fowler/Beale_Family" title="The Fowler/Beale Family">the Beales and Fowlers</a>. Never afraid to speak her mind, and woe betide anyone who manages to get on her wrong side, Lou has the respect of her friends and family, even if they do find her a bit of a nuisance at times. Born in the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End</a>, Lou lived in <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a> all her life. Albert and Lou came to their house on the corner of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>, number forty-five, on getting married during the 1930s. Lou remained in the house throughout the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> and brought up her children there. Her affinity and ties with the area mean that she tends to view Albert Square as her own and that gives her an excuse to intrude into people's business as she sees fit.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Willy">Willy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Willy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Willy_(EastEnders)" title="Willy (EastEnders)">Willy (EastEnders)</a></div>
<p><b>Willy</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Pug" title="Pug">Pug</a>, who appears in the first episode of the programme and remained in the show until 1992. He belongs to <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Skinner" title="Ethel Skinner">Ethel Skinner</a> who loves him dearly. Willy was cast less than a week before the filming of <i>EastEnders</i> began. From the beginning it was decided that the pensioner <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Skinner" title="Ethel Skinner">Ethel Skinner</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gretchen_Franklin" title="Gretchen Franklin">Gretchen Franklin</a>) was to have a <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Terrier" title="Yorkshire Terrier">Yorkshire Terrier</a> named Willy (after her dead husband). The company Janimals, who specialised in providing and training animals for television and films, was contacted by <i>EastEnders</i> co-creator/producer <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>, and informed her that they could provide an experienced Pug for the part (he had previously starred in an adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Swallows_and_Amazons" title="Swallows and Amazons">Swallows and Amazons</a></i>). The dog was brought to the studio to meet <a href="/wiki/Gretchen_Franklin" title="Gretchen Franklin">Gretchen Franklin</a>, the actress who played Ethel. She took a shine to him straight away and so the pug was cast as Willy. Willy and <a href="/wiki/Roly" title="Roly">Roly</a> the poodle shared a dressing room at <a href="/wiki/Elstree_Studios" title="Elstree Studios">Elstree Studios</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Saeed_Jeffery">Saeed Jeffery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Saeed Jeffery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Saeed_Jeffery" title="Saeed Jeffery">Saeed Jeffery</a></div>
<p><b>Saeed Jeffery</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson_(actor)" title="Andrew Johnson (actor)">Andrew Johnson</a>, is the original owner of the First til Last grocery store. He is never truly accepted in Walford, and his arranged marriage ends in divorce after it is discovered that he'd been making obscene phone calls to women. He leaves Walford in disgrace, leaving his business to his wife. Saeed Jeffery is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Saeed and his wife <a href="/wiki/Naima_Jeffery" title="Naima Jeffery">Naima</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shreela_Ghosh" title="Shreela Ghosh">Shreela Ghosh</a>) are the first <a href="/wiki/British_Asian" title="British Asian">Asian</a> characters to appear in the soap. Black and Asian characters were two ethnic minorities that had previously been under-represented in British soap before <i>EastEnders</i> aired. Holland and Smith knew that for the soap to succeed there needed to be a varied group of characters, so that several different sections of the audience had someone to identify with.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naima_Jeffery">Naima Jeffery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Naima Jeffery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Naima_Jeffery" title="Naima Jeffery">Naima Jeffery</a></div>
<p><b>Naima Jeffery</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Shreela_Ghosh" title="Shreela Ghosh">Shreela Ghosh</a>, is the wife of <a href="/wiki/Saeed_Jeffery" title="Saeed Jeffery">Saeed Jeffery</a> (<a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson_(actor)" title="Andrew Johnson (actor)">Andrew Johnson</a>). Naima tries to embrace <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">Western culture</a>, but this isn't easy since her family constantly try to force her to adhere to their ancient customs. Sick of being forced to live in an arranged marriage with a husband she loathes, Naima makes a stand and shames her family by filing for a divorce. Naima Jeffery is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Naima and her husband are the first <a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asian</a> characters to appear in the soap. Black and Asian characters were two ethnic minorities that had previously been under-represented in British soap before <i>EastEnders</i> aired. Holland and Smith knew that for the soap to succeed there needed to be a varied group of characters, so that several different sections of the audience had someone to identify with.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Angie_Watts">Angie Watts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Angie Watts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Angie_Watts" title="Angie Watts">Angie Watts</a></div>
<p><b>Angela "Angie" Watts</b> played by <a href="/wiki/Anita_Dobson" title="Anita Dobson">Anita Dobson</a>, appears from the first episode of the show until 1988 when the actress decided to quit and the character was written out. Angie is well known for her cheeky banter, her huge shaggy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Permanent_wave" title="Permanent wave">perm</a> and turning to alcohol during her stormy marriage to cheating <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>) which ends when he hands her divorce papers on Christmas Day, in an episode watched by a record-breaking 30.1 million viewers. Despite being the loud and feisty lady of Walford, and having a close relationship with her beloved adopted daughter <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Watts" title="Sharon Watts">Sharon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Letitia_Dean" title="Letitia Dean">Letitia Dean</a>), she doesn't have much real happiness during her time in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> and alcoholism finally claims her life in 2002 when she dies of <a href="/wiki/Cirrhosis" title="Cirrhosis">cirrhosis</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Liver" title="Liver">liver</a>. Angie Watts is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. The character of Angie was originally going to be named Pearl<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> and she, her husband and adopted daughter were to be the occupants of the soap's local pub, now famously known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Queen_Vic" title="The Queen Vic">The Queen Vic</a>. Holland, who had worked as a barman in his youth, called upon his own personal experiences to invent the Watts family and the pub they lived in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> Holland and Smith had always been critical of the way pubs had been portrayed on television feeling they lacked vitality and life, so they were determined that their pub and occupants were going to be more 'real'. The Watts were seen by Holland as integral to the show's success, partly because he had already guessed that the pub was going to be a monstrous battleground where emotions would run high on a regular basis, and also because the occupants would be providing the majority of the drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nick_Cotton">Nick Cotton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Nick Cotton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cotton" title="Nick Cotton">Nick Cotton</a></div>
<p><b>Nicholas Charles "Nick" Cotton</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/John_Altman_(actor)" title="John Altman (actor)">John Altman</a>, was a recurring character who appears in the soap's debut episode in February 1985, through to his last appearance where he died in 2015. Nick is the son of characters <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Cotton" title="Charlie Cotton">Charlie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hancock" title="Christopher Hancock">Christopher Hancock</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Dot_Cotton" title="Dot Cotton">Dot Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/June_Brown" title="June Brown">June Brown</a>), and the father of Charlie Cotton <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Cotton" title="Ashley Cotton">Ashley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dotty_Cotton" title="Dotty Cotton">Dotty Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/Molly_Conlin" title="Molly Conlin">Molly Conlin</a>). His storylines have seen him twice commit murder of Reg Cox and Eddie Royle respectively, succumb to a heroin addiction, and attempt to poison his own mother, causing the death of his son Ashley amongst numerous other misdemeanours. Nick is also the subject of a special spin-off episode, <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_EastEnders_television_spin-offs" title="List of EastEnders television spin-offs">The Return of Nick Cotton</a></i>, which aired in October 2000. He has been voted one of television's all-time most villainous characters in a <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> poll. He is seen as the shows original villain. Nick died of a heroin overdose on 13 February 2015 in the house he killed Reg Cox 30 years previous.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sue_Osman">Sue Osman</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sue Osman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue Osman</a></div>
<p><b>Sue Osman</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Ratcliff" title="Sandy Ratcliff">Sandy Ratcliff</a>, is the wife of <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>). Sue is one of the serial's original characters, appearing in its first episode on 19 February 1985 and departing on-screen in May 1989. Created by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>, Sue is portrayed as argumentative, insecure and tragic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EEHandbook_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-EEHandbook-10">[10]</a></sup> A pivotal storyline in the character's narrative is the cot-death of her son, which is one of the show's first controversial plots. During her four years on-screen, the character contends with a phantom pregnancy, marital breakdown and finally insanity. Ratcliff left the role in 1989 after press stories began circulating about her private life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-counselor_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-counselor-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pete_Beale">Pete Beale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Pete Beale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pete_Beale" title="Pete Beale">Pete Beale</a></div>
<p><b>Peter "Pete" Beale</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Dean_(actor)" title="Peter Dean (actor)">Peter Dean</a>, makes his first appearance in the programme's first episode, on 19 February 1985. The character was created by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a>, one of the creators of <i>EasEnders</i>; he was based on a member of Holland's family. Pete features in the soap for eight years as the local fruit and veg trader of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>; he is a member of the original focal clan in the serial, <a href="/wiki/Beale_family" title="Beale family">the Beales and Fowlers</a>. Pete is portrayed as a macho and somewhat insensitive individual who struggles to cope with emotion. Pete was axed from the soap in 1993 and departed in May that year after over eight years on-screen. The character was killed off-screen later that year following Peter Dean's public criticism of the BBC.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kathy_Beale">Kathy Beale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Kathy Beale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kathy_Beale" title="Kathy Beale">Kathy Beale</a></div>
<p><b>Katherine "Kathy" Mitchell</b> (also <b>Beale</b>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> played by <a href="/wiki/Gillian_Taylforth" title="Gillian Taylforth">Gillian Taylforth</a> between 1985 and 2000, and then from 2015 onwards, is the mother of <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Adam_Woodyatt" title="Adam Woodyatt">Adam Woodyatt</a>). Though it was never used on screen, in 2010 the BBC website named the character as <b>Kathy Sullivan</b> due to a marriage after she left the show in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Kathy Beale is one of the serial's original characters, appearing in the first episode of <i>EastEnders</i> on 19 February 1985. One of the longest-running original characters, Kathy remains in a prominent role, covering issues such as rape and domestic violence until April 1998, when Taylforth quit. A year later, in 1999, Taylforth returned for a temporary stint to aid the departure of actor <a href="/wiki/Ross_Kemp" title="Ross Kemp">Ross Kemp</a> who played her brother-in-law <a href="/wiki/Grant_Mitchell_(EastEnders)" title="Grant Mitchell (EastEnders)">Grant Mitchell</a>, and later in December 1999 for the wedding of her screen son Ian. She made her final appearance in January 2000. Despite various rumours of a possible return for Kathy in the press, this did not occur and the character was supposedly killed off-screen in a road accident in late February 2006 in a storyline to facilitate the return of her son <a href="/wiki/Ben_Mitchell_(EastEnders)" title="Ben Mitchell (EastEnders)">Ben</a> to his father <a href="/wiki/Phil_Mitchell" title="Phil Mitchell">Phil</a> (<a href="/wiki/Steve_McFadden" title="Steve McFadden">Steve McFadden</a>). In tribute to the character, the soap's café is named "Kathy's".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Kathy returned for the 30th anniversary episode in February 2015, when it was revealed that Kathy never died in a surprise showdown with her ex-husband, Phil.</p>

<p><b>Hassan Osman</b>, played by Michael Evangelou, is the first son of <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue Osman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sandy_Ratcliff" title="Sandy Ratcliff">Sandy Ratcliff</a>), who is only a baby during his short time in <i>EastEnders</i>. Hassan dies in his sleep on 20 June 1985; no official causes of death are given. Sue is left in a state of shock after her son's death. She remains in a trance-like state for weeks and refuses to eat, sleep, cry or acknowledge her grief. She later begins blaming herself unfairly for her son's sudden death. Her mental health continues to deteriorate and she becomes despondent towards Ali and their marriage disintegrates in a welter of depression. Sue eventually manages to come to terms with Hassan's death with the help of <a href="/wiki/Harold_Legg" title="Harold Legg">Harold Legg</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leonard_Fenton" title="Leonard Fenton">Leonard Fenton</a>), who takes the drastic measure of placing <a href="/wiki/Mary_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Mary Smith (EastEnders)">Mary Smith</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Linda_Davidson" title="Linda Davidson">Linda Davidson</a>) baby, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annie_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Annie Smith (EastEnders)">Annie Smith</a> (Zara Posener), in her lap, finally allowing her to acknowledge her pent-up sadness.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before <i>EastEnders</i> initially aired, creators <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a> had already decided that Sue and Ali Osman would be parents to a young baby named Hassan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> However, as further characters were invented they realised that there would be a total of four babies in the show: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annie_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Annie Smith (EastEnders)">Annie Smith</a> (Zara Posener), <a href="/wiki/Martin_Fowler_(EastEnders)" title="Martin Fowler (EastEnders)">Martin Fowler</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jon_Peyton_Price" title="Jon Peyton Price">Jon Peyton Price</a>), <a href="/wiki/Vicki_Fowler" title="Vicki Fowler">Vicki Fowler</a> (Emma Herry) and Hassan. It was decided that it would be impossible for the studios to cope with four babies and so they invented a storyline to eliminate one from the cast. During this time, the topic of <a href="/wiki/Sudden_infant_death_syndrome" title="Sudden infant death syndrome">sudden infant death syndrome</a> was prominent in the British press, partly due to an increase in casualties, but also because a doctor had gone public with the accusation that parents were to blame for the tragic occurrence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> Holland and Smith decided that covering this issue in the soap would be a good way of 'setting the record straight', and so it was decided that Sue and Ali's baby would die from cot death in the early months of the show. This was the first of many controversial storylines in <i>EastEnders'</i> history. After the storyline aired in June 1985, the show was praised by audience and press alike for the sensitive and unsensational way this harrowing subject was treated. The sudden tragedy came as a surprise to the audience, especially since the bereaved parents were a couple whose feuding, fighting ways had made them appear rather comic in the early episodes of the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10years_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-10years-1">[1]</a></sup> The British Cot Death Foundation initially feared that a soap opera would trivialise the subject and frighten new parents. They tried to stop the episodes from airing, but in the end they were pleased with the way the subject was handled, and provided back-up support after transmission to many viewers who wanted more information on the subject.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10years_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-10years-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sharon_Watts">Sharon Watts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Sharon Watts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Watts" title="Sharon Watts">Sharon Watts</a></div>
<p><b>Sharon Anne Watts</b> (also credited as <b>Mitchell</b> and <b>Rickman</b>), played by <a href="/wiki/Letitia_Dean" title="Letitia Dean">Letitia Dean</a>, is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Angie_Watts" title="Angie Watts">Angie Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anita_Dobson" title="Anita Dobson">Anita Dobson</a>). Sharon is a regular character in the first 10 years of the programme, after which she leaves and returns several times. Sharon is the adoptive daughter of the popular '<a href="/wiki/Supercouple" title="Supercouple">supercouple</a>'; Den and Angie. She was known to Den as his "princess" and he made sure suitors watched their step around the teenage Sharon. Even though she disapproved of her father's dodgy deals and his constant fighting with binge-drinker Angie, she loved them both nonetheless. She made a permanent return to the show in 2012 and is married to Phil Mitchell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ian_Beale">Ian Beale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Ian Beale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian Beale</a></div>
<p><b>Ian Albert Beale</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Woodyatt" title="Adam Woodyatt">Adam Woodyatt</a>, is the longest-serving character and the only remaining original character to have appeared continuously since the first episode on 19 February 1985. The character appeared in his 2,000th episode in the show on 26 March 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Ian Beale is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Ian is a member of the first family of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Beale/Fowler_family" title="The Beale/Fowler family">the Beales and Fowlers</a>, and Holland took the inspiration for some of the series' earliest characters from his own London family and background. Ian's original character outline as written by Smith and Holland appeared in an abridged form in their book, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EastEnders_books" title="EastEnders books">EastEnders: The Inside Story</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Michelle_Fowler">Michelle Fowler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Michelle Fowler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Fowler" title="Michelle Fowler">Michelle Fowler</a></div>
<p><b>Michelle Fowler</b> (also <b>Holloway</b>), played by <a href="/wiki/Susan_Tully" title="Susan Tully">Susan Tully</a>, is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Treacher" title="Bill Treacher">Bill Treacher</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Pauline_Fowler" title="Pauline Fowler">Pauline Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wendy_Richard" title="Wendy Richard">Wendy Richard</a>). Although she is one of the brighter people in <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>, that doesn't stop Michelle making some huge mistakes during her time in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>. Michelle has a habit of choosing the wrong men and her stubborn nature means that she rarely accepts anyone's help or listens to good advice. She is tough, feisty, determined, outspoken and never afraid to defend herself or her beliefs. Early on in the series, the character of Michelle becomes central to the programme and is the focus of a controversial storyline involving her teenage pregnancy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> Press interest in the show escalated to "record levels" as journalists continuously tried to predict who had fathered Michelle's baby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whodunnit" title="Whodunnit">whodunnit</a> fashion, the audience had been kept in the dark as to the real identity of the father and were given teasers implicating several residents on The Square.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> The audience finally discovered the culprit in episode 66 of the programme, October 1985. The episode was written by series co-creator/script editor Tony Holland and directed by co-creator/producer Julia Smith, and was considered to be a landmark episode in the show's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10years_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-10years-1">[1]</a></sup> Four possible suspects are seen leaving the Square in the early half of the episode: <a href="/wiki/Tony_Carpenter" title="Tony Carpenter">Tony Carpenter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oscar_James" title="Oscar James">Oscar James</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>), <a href="/wiki/Andy_O%27Brien_(EastEnders)" title="Andy O'Brien (EastEnders)">Andy O'Brien</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ross_Davidson" title="Ross Davidson">Ross Davidson</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10years_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-10years-1">[1]</a></sup> As Michelle waits by their rendezvous point a car pulls up and finally the fluffy white legs of <a href="/wiki/Roly" title="Roly">Roly</a> the poodle bound out of the car, and give it all away: Den Watts is the man meeting Michelle and it was he who had fathered her baby. The rest of the episode consists of just one long scene, where Den and Michelle discuss whether or not to keep the baby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10years_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-10years-1">[1]</a></sup> Up to that time it was the longest scene ever done in a soap-opera, lasting fifteen minutes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mark_Fowler">Mark Fowler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Mark Fowler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mark_Fowler" title="Mark Fowler">Mark Fowler</a></div>
<p><b>Mark Albert Fowler</b> is an original regular character in the series starting February 1985 but becomes a semi-regular after his original portrayer <a href="/wiki/David_Scarboro" title="David Scarboro">David Scarboro</a> was written out of the role in April 1985. Scarboro made brief returns to the role in 1986 and 1987. Scarboro committed suicide in April 1988. The role was recast in 1990, with <a href="/wiki/Todd_Carty" title="Todd Carty">Todd Carty</a> taking the role. From this point the character is a permanent fixture in the series and Carty remained in the role until the character was written out of the series in early 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Mark starts out as a delinquent teenager, but returns to Walford a changed man when he was 22. Contracting HIV forces him to grow up fast and accept his responsibilities. He frequently finds it difficult to accept the restrictions of the illness, which finally claims his life in April 2004.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roly">Roly</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Roly">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Roly" title="Roly">Roly</a></div>
<p><b>Roly</b> is an apricot coloured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_Poodle" title="Standard Poodle">Standard Poodle</a>, who appears in the first episode of the programme and remains in the show until 1993. Roly was cast less than a week before the filming of <i>EastEnders</i> began. From the beginning it was decided that the occupants of <a href="/wiki/The_Queen_Victoria" title="The Queen Victoria">The Queen Victoria</a> pub, <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angie_Watts" title="Angie Watts">Angie</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharon_Rickman" title="Sharon Rickman">Sharon Watts</a>, were to have an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Shepherd_Dog" title="German Shepherd Dog">Alsatian</a> named Prince.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> However, finding an Alsatian that was light enough in colour not to merge into the background of the set proved to be difficult. Eventually, the co-creator/producer of the show, <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>, was contacted by a company named Janimals, who specialised in providing and training animals for television and films. They had found a seven-month-old Poodle, called Roly, who might still be young enough to be trained. Training was said to take three weeks, and as the Poodle was a similar size to an Alsatian, Roly got the part. Roly was made the property of the BBC, given an ID card, and taken to live with Julia Smith at her home in London. Roly and <a href="/wiki/Willy_(EastEnders)" title="Willy (EastEnders)">Willy</a> the <a href="/wiki/Pug" title="Pug">pug</a> shared a dressing room at Elstree studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Tracey</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> (known to fans as <b>Tracey the barmaid</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>) is played by <a href="/wiki/Jane_Slaughter_(actress)" title="Jane Slaughter (actress)">Jane Slaughter</a>. She has appeared as a background character since the first episode on 19 February 1985, where she is seen working on the flower stall in Bridge Street Market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspytracy_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspytracy-22">[22]</a></sup> She is the longest-serving female member of the cast in terms of continuous appearances. (<a href="/wiki/Letitia_Dean" title="Letitia Dean">Letitia Dean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gillian_Taylforth" title="Gillian Taylforth">Gillian Taylforth</a> are also original cast members, but have taken long-term breaks from the show.)</p>
<p>Tracey starts working as a barmaid at <a href="/wiki/The_Queen_Victoria" title="The Queen Victoria">The Queen Victoria</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_house" title="Public house">public house</a>, in 1989. Her existence is largely to provide somebody to man the bar while one of the main characters resolves disputes, arguments or disagreements, or tends to the main plot of the episode. Despite featuring on the television programme frequently and occasionally having a line to say, her surname and most of her details are unknown. She has also featured in a few minor storylines. As well as being a barmaid, Tracey owns a flower stall on Bridge Street market. Her stall is regularly robbed by new characters who steal flowers for people they are meeting. She employs workers whilst she does shifts in the pub, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ina_Foot" title="Ina Foot">Ina Foot</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ina_Clare" title="Ina Clare">Ina Clare</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Slater" title="Jean Slater">Jean Slater</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gillian_Wright" title="Gillian Wright">Gillian Wright</a>). Tracey becomes good friends with <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Bates" title="Debbie Bates">Debbie Bates</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nicola_Duffett" title="Nicola Duffett">Nicola Duffett</a>), and is godmother to her daughter <a href="/wiki/Clare_Bates" title="Clare Bates">Clare Bates</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gemma_Bissix" title="Gemma Bissix">Gemma Bissix</a>). Tracey is often seen to be pleased at the prospect of additional overtime in The Queen Victoria when a Mitchell family wedding or other event is planned as she can always do with extra money.</p>
<p>On 14 April 2003 Tracey has a one-night stand with <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Rickman" title="Dennis Rickman">Dennis Rickman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nigel_Harman" title="Nigel Harman">Nigel Harman</a>), the adoptive half-brother of former pub owner <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Watts" title="Sharon Watts">Sharon Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Letitia_Dean" title="Letitia Dean">Letitia Dean</a>), when he arrives in Albert Square, despite the fact that she is married. The day after, he returns her knickers to her publicly over the bar. In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Mitchell_(EastEnders)" title="Sam Mitchell (EastEnders)">Sam Mitchell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kim_Medcalf" title="Kim Medcalf">Kim Medcalf</a>) breaks into the pub with a crowbar and Tracey discovers her, asking her to leave. Sam knocks Tracey out with the crowbar and drags her, unconscious, into the bathroom, locking her in. She goes downstairs to unearth the body of <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>) who is buried in the basement, and when the police arrive, Tracey is comforted by <a href="/wiki/Little_Mo_Mitchell" title="Little Mo Mitchell">Little Mo Mitchell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kacey_Ainsworth" title="Kacey Ainsworth">Kacey Ainsworth</a>). After Sam is released from prison on bail she apologises to Tracey, but Tracey appears shaken by the ordeal and scared of Sam.</p>
<p>On 7 August 2008, Tracey opens up when <a href="/wiki/Sean_Slater" title="Sean Slater">Sean Slater</a> (<a href="/wiki/Robert_Kazinsky" title="Robert Kazinsky">Robert Kazinsky</a>) questions why she is so quiet. She says she wants to keep herself to herself because she thinks that the Mitchells are all "stark raving mad" and that she does not fear any new barmaids as she had seen them come and go but she knows that she will always be behind the bar. She goes on to say that whoever lives in the pub is "cursed" in her opinion and that they will never be able to live happily together.</p>
<p>In March 2009, <a href="/wiki/Archie_Mitchell" title="Archie Mitchell">Archie Mitchell</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Larry_Lamb_(actor)" title="Larry Lamb (actor)">Larry Lamb</a>) makes Tracey a cleaner at The Queen Victoria, and she is pleased for the extra money. She attends <a href="/wiki/Peggy_Mitchell" title="Peggy Mitchell">Peggy Mitchell</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Barbara_Windsor" title="Barbara Windsor">Barbara Windsor</a>) hen party and Peggy and Archie's wedding on 2 April. On 16 April 2009, <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Carter" title="Shirley Carter">Shirley Carter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Linda_Henry" title="Linda Henry">Linda Henry</a>) accuses <a href="/wiki/Phil_Mitchell" title="Phil Mitchell">Phil Mitchell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Steve_McFadden" title="Steve McFadden">Steve McFadden</a>) of drinking again. However, Phil denies this, saying that he had broken a bottle of alcohol earlier, and Tracey backs him up. On 28 April 2009, Tracey answers the phone to <a href="/wiki/Minty_Peterson" title="Minty Peterson">Minty Peterson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cliff_Parisi" title="Cliff Parisi">Cliff Parisi</a>) questioning Phil's whereabouts from the night before. Tracey tells Phil about having to talk to the bank about some money issues. Following that, Phil pressures Tracey into talking about her feelings, supposedly for him, mentioning her husband in the process. Finally, she appears again towards the end of the episode wanting to help <a href="/wiki/Ben_Mitchell_(EastEnders)" title="Ben Mitchell (EastEnders)">Ben Mitchell</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlie_Jones_(actor)" title="Charlie Jones (actor)">Charlie Jones</a>) get to school as Phil is too drunk to assist him. Phil, flirting with Tracey, tells her that her husband will not mind.</p>
<p>On 30 April 2009, she finds <a href="/wiki/Heather_Trott" title="Heather Trott">Heather Trott</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Cheryl_Fergison" title="Cheryl Fergison">Cheryl Fergison</a>) lost inhaler. On 30 July, she phones in with a back injury, and Peggy says it was unlike her to skive so it must be serious. <a href="/wiki/Dot_Cotton" title="Dot Cotton">Dot Branning</a> (<a href="/wiki/June_Brown" title="June Brown">June Brown</a>) describes Tracey as the person who "sees everything but says very little". On 8 September 2009, Peggy tells Sam (now <a href="/wiki/Danniella_Westbrook" title="Danniella Westbrook">Danniella Westbrook</a>) that Tracey still holds a grudge against her. On 26 November Tracey covers for both Peggy and Phil when bailiffs ask for them, by pretending she is just a barmaid from an agency and does not know them. She then informs Ben he needs to tell Phil that the bailiffs are looking for him and want to repossess his car. On 24 December, Archie Mitchell and <a href="/wiki/Janine_Butcher" title="Janine Butcher">Janine Butcher</a> (<a href="/wiki/Charlie_Brooks" title="Charlie Brooks">Charlie Brooks</a>) take over the pub, ousting Peggy and her family. Tracey is unhappy with the way Archie and Janine treated Peggy and the rest of the Mitchell family, and the straw that broke the camel's back comes the next day when Janine tells Tracey to put some tinsel in her hair and asks her to keep an eye on the Christmas dinner. Tracey leaves a note saying "Gone fishing", implying that she resigned. The next day, Tracey is present when <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Mitchell" title="Ronnie Mitchell">Ronnie Mitchell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Samantha_Womack" title="Samantha Womack">Samantha Womack</a>) is arrested on suspicion of Archie's murder, and looks shocked. She also attends <a href="/wiki/Jean_Slater" title="Jean Slater">Jean Slater</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Gillian_Wright" title="Gillian Wright">Gillian Wright</a>) birthday party on 11 January 2010. When <a href="/wiki/Mo_Harris" title="Mo Harris">Mo Harris</a> (<a href="/wiki/Laila_Morse" title="Laila Morse">Laila Morse</a>) opens a betting circle on the identity of Archie's killer, she lists Tracey as the rank outsider, giving her the nickname "Silent Assassin".</p>
<p>On 18 January 2010, Tracey appears behind the bar in a non-speaking capacity for The Queen Victoria's reopening. On 22 January, the pub's new owner, <a href="/wiki/Roxy_Mitchell" title="Roxy Mitchell">Roxy Mitchell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Rita_Simons" title="Rita Simons">Rita Simons</a>), gives Tracey a warning for arriving two hours late, but Peggy explains that she asked her to come in late as she has done a lot of unpaid overtime recently. Later, Roxy sacks Tracey because she wants the pub to have younger bar staff. Peggy demands that Roxy beg Tracey to come back for the evening shift as workers like Tracey are 'gold dust'. Peggy tells Tracey on the phone that she is not sacked but Tracey hangs up. Tracey decides to take Roxy to an unfair dismissal tribunal, so Roxy says she will audition a new barmaid, giving Tracey a chance but making sure she loses. However, when Roxy realises she does not know how to run a pub, she gives Tracey her job back. Tracey suggests a curry night, and Roxy is happy to leave Tracey in charge of it, saying it is the most she has ever heard her speak. On that same day, viewers find out that Tracey and Shirley Carter are good friends. In March 2010, <a href="/wiki/Billie_Jackson" title="Billie Jackson">Billie Jackson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Devon_Anderson" title="Devon Anderson">Devon Anderson</a>) holds up The Queen Victoria, asking Tracey for cash and saying he has a gun. She fills his bag with cash but Phil takes it off her. In September 2010, following the fire at The Queen Victoria, Tracey starts working at <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian Beale</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Adam_Woodyatt" title="Adam Woodyatt">Adam Woodyatt</a>) fish and chip shop. The next month, <a href="/wiki/Alfie_Moon" title="Alfie Moon">Alfie Moon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shane_Richie" title="Shane Richie">Shane Richie</a>) offers Tracey her old job back at The Queen Victoria, along with <a href="/wiki/Stacey_Slater" title="Stacey Slater">Stacey Branning</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lacey_Turner" title="Lacey Turner">Lacey Turner</a>), after he reopens the pub.</p>
<p>In January 2011, during a conversation with <a href="/wiki/Kat_Slater" title="Kat Slater">Kat Moon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jessie_Wallace" title="Jessie Wallace">Jessie Wallace</a>), it is revealed that Tracey has a son who lives with his father, as she and her husband have split up. When <a href="/wiki/David_Wicks" title="David Wicks">David Wicks</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michael_French" title="Michael French">Michael French</a>) returned for his mother, Pat's, funeral, he comments to his daughter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bianca_Butcher" title="Bianca Butcher">Bianca Butcher</a> (<a href="/wiki/Patsy_Palmer" title="Patsy Palmer">Patsy Palmer</a>) that he was amazed Tracey was still working in The Vic, which prompted Bianca to say that Tracey was one of the few things in the world that was reliable. Tracey's next appearance in speech was on Thursday 14 June 2012, she gave Jean her job back on the flower stall. However Tracey later sacks Jean after she rows with customers <a href="/wiki/Michael_Moon_(EastEnders)" title="Michael Moon (EastEnders)">Michael Moon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Steve_John_Shepherd" title="Steve John Shepherd">Steve John Shepherd</a>) and Janine Butcher. In August 2012, after <a href="/wiki/Billy_Mitchell_(EastEnders)" title="Billy Mitchell (EastEnders)">Billy Mitchell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Perry_Fenwick" title="Perry Fenwick">Perry Fenwick</a>) announces to the customers in The Vic that Sharon has returned, only Tracey greets her and meets her son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dennis_Rickman_Jr" title="Dennis Rickman Jr">Dennis Rickman Jr</a>.</p>
<p>In December 2013, Phil sells the pub to <a href="/wiki/Mick_Carter" title="Mick Carter">Mick Carter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Danny_Dyer" title="Danny Dyer">Danny Dyer</a>) and when Tracey arrives to work her shift, Mick's wife, <a href="/wiki/Linda_Carter" title="Linda Carter">Linda Carter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kellie_Bright" title="Kellie Bright">Kellie Bright</a>) tells her that she is not wanted anymore. Shirley witnesses this and tells Linda that Tracey is like part of the furniture at the Vic and she is then later seen when she and Linda become friends and she resumes her job at The Vic. In April 2014, the market merges with <i>Spring Lane Market</i> and this brings in a new florist, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pam_Coker" title="Pam Coker">Pam Coker</a> (Lin Blakely), however when discussing the competition with Alfie, Pam states she has no competition. This implies Tracey no longer runs a flower stall.</p>
<p>In September 2015, Tracey is shocked when she answers The Queen Victoria telephone to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kathy_Sullivan_(EastEnders)" title="Kathy Sullivan (EastEnders)">Kathy Sullivan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gillian_Taylforth" title="Gillian Taylforth">Gillian Taylforth</a>), who is believed to be dead. Kathy asks Tracey to pass on an address to Phil. After being conforted by Mick, she finds Phil. Phil tries to persuade her it was a joke but Tracey is not convinced and knows it was Kathy. She gives him Kathy's whereabouts. The following month, Tracey finally meets Kathy again and welcomes her home. Eight months later, Tracey welcomes another old friend back to Walford as she greets Peggy, when she rides around the square in a milk float.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_2">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Being one of the only four remaining original cast members in the show, Slaughter said that when she joined the show "she recognised that EastEnders could run and be something very exciting" and that she 'never dreamed [that it would last] 25 years' but she 'certainly knew it was special.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-interview-23">[23]</a></sup> When asked to pick her favourite storyline she said, "In terms of actually watching and seeing scenes, I've been lucky being in The Vic. I've seen so many good ones – with the Mitchells, with Phil, with Peggy – I've been very, very lucky to see so many, that it's nigh on impossible to single any out. They've all involved different people – I could go on and on and on!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-praises_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-praises-24">[24]</a></sup> She's always there whereas other cast members such as Adam [Woodyatt] – who's been there as long as I have – wouldn't necessarily have a storyline that puts him in The Vic at all."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspytracy_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspytracy-22">[22]</a></sup> She also added, "For me as a character, it has to be Tracey speaking to <a href="/wiki/Sean_Slater" title="Sean Slater">Sean Slater</a>. It was hysterical. For someone who never says anything, she suddenly had an opinion about everything, which I just loved. It was written by Christopher Reason and done so well. To get someone who only occasionally says things like 'out the back' and make what she says make sense – he did a fantastic job. That was very memorable because everyone was so supportive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-interview-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>A 2010 video on the official website of <i>EastEnders</i> spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders:_E20" title="EastEnders: E20">EastEnders: E20</a></i> features the character <a href="/wiki/Fatboy_(EastEnders)" title="Fatboy (EastEnders)">Fatboy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ricky_Norwood" title="Ricky Norwood">Ricky Norwood</a>) giving his "Survival Tips in Walford!", one of which is to not talk to Tracey, as "she goes on, and on, and on, and on."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2010, Slaughter said that it would not make sense to make Tracey into "this talkative, all-singing, all-dancing person with a fully-fledged family and surname."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspytracy_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspytracy-22">[22]</a></sup> Slaughter described her character as 'lovely', 'clever', 'loyal but strong' and 'opinionated'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspytracy_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspytracy-22">[22]</a></sup> Jane Simons from <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">The Mirror</a></i> described her as 'silent'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worth_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-worth-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a></i> asked producer <a href="/wiki/Diederick_Santer" title="Diederick Santer">Diederick Santer</a> what's in store for Tracey, he said, "You just have to watch out for any script that Christopher Reason writes. Chris is obsessed with Tracey and with Jane Slaughter, who plays her. He gives her loads to do, which is fantastic. When we give her more to do, she absolutely delivers. We think she's fab."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Santer_interview_May_2009_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Santer_interview_May_2009-26">[26]</a></sup> When it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Kirkwood_(producer)" title="Bryan Kirkwood (producer)">Bryan Kirkwood</a> was taking over from Santer, Slaughter was asked what she hoped Kirkwood would do to Tracey saying, "I would just be happy if he knows the character and likes the character and thinks he could use her. I think there is potential to use her but like I said, not in a ridiculously nonsensical way, but I think it's also down to the public. It sounds like I'm deferring my opinion but I'm not, I think the public actually would like to know. "</p>
<p>Tracey was one of the 'outsiders' in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Who_Killed_Archie" title="Who Killed Archie">Who Killed Archie</a> storyline with many viewers thinking it could be her. In a poll with <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a></i> 7.9% thought Tracey killed <a href="/wiki/Archie_Mitchell" title="Archie Mitchell">Archie Mitchell</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Larry_Lamb_(actor)" title="Larry Lamb (actor)">Larry Lamb</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poll_result_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-poll_result-27">[27]</a></sup> and in a second poll, 7.1% thought it was her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poll_result2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-poll_result2-28">[28]</a></sup> When asked if she thought Tracey could kill she said, "I wouldn't think she's that dark but who knows – she could be. I've heard there are odds at the bookies to be had for her but why? Why would she have killed him? Because he's threatening The Vic? Is it The Vic she protects or the family?".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-interview-23">[23]</a></sup> It was later revealed that bookmakers, <a href="/wiki/William_Hill_(bookmaker)" title="William Hill (bookmaker)">William Hill</a> had slashed the odds on Tracey being the killer, with 7/1 thinking she killed Archie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slashed_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-slashed-29">[29]</a></sup> The killer was later revealed to be <a href="/wiki/Stacey_Slater" title="Stacey Slater">Stacey Slater</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lacey_Turner" title="Lacey Turner">Lacey Turner</a>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dek Hogan from <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a></i> hailed Tracey when she said a line and said it was 'such a rare event that it’s always worthy of a celebration. Fair play, she did mumble it a bit and it was so unmemorable that I’ve forgotten what it was she actually said but it was a line nonetheless.' He also said that she, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winston_(EastEnders)" title="Winston (EastEnders)">Winston</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ulric_Browne" title="Ulric Browne">Ulric Browne</a>) and other background characters should have a daytime soap, saying it would be a ratings winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-underused_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-underused-30">[30]</a></sup> Kris Green also from <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a></i> said he wants to ask scriptwriters to give more lines to Tracey,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kg_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-kg-31">[31]</a></sup> as well as calling Tracey's scenes with <a href="/wiki/Sean_Slater" title="Sean Slater">Sean Slater</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rob_Kazinsky" title="Rob Kazinsky">Rob Kazinsky</a>) 'iconic'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-video_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-video-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Speaking of the reaction viewers have to the character of Tracey, she said, My son was telling me the other day there's a [Facebook group] that says 'Let Tracey Speak' with 47,000 members. It just blows my head. I said, 'Oh, Ollie – I think you mean 4,700' and he went, 'No mum, forty seven thousand!' <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lofty_Holloway">Lofty Holloway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Lofty Holloway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lofty_Holloway" title="Lofty Holloway">Lofty Holloway</a></div>
<p><b>George "Lofty" Holloway</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Watt_(actor)" title="Tom Watt (actor)">Tom Watt</a>, is one of the serial's original characters, making his first appearance in the third episode, 26 February 1985. Lofty is generally depicted as a meek, luckless and hapless victim. A long running storyline concerns his relationship with the character <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Fowler" title="Michelle Fowler">Michelle Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Susan_Tully" title="Susan Tully">Susan Tully</a>). Their unhappy marriage finally disintegrates after Michelle has an abortion, and Lofty leaves Walford for a new start. The character's final appearance is in the episode first aired on 19 April 1988. Lofty was one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Both felt that to help complete the community there was a need for a character in his early twenties. He had to be someone a bit different. Not brash and confident like a lot of the older men, and not boisterous like the younger ones. A loner, maybe someone forced to be a loner. A person who "stuck out like a sore thumb". Someone that was happiest in a group but still couldn't find one that he fit in with. Tony Holland had previously been in the army and found that ex-soldiers had these problems when they tried to reintegrate as civilians. So they decided that Lofty would be an ex-soldier, forced to quit because of his asthma. He was happiest in the army and felt incomplete without the group setting, the all-male camaraderie and even the security of the uniformity that the army provides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Detective Sergeant Terry Rich</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Gary_Whelan" title="Gary Whelan">Gary Whelan</a>, first appears in the series on 26 February 1985. He investigates the death of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reg_Cox" title="Reg Cox">Reg Cox</a> (Johnnie Clayton), who had died the previous week, and his death is believed to be a murder. DS Rich of the <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a> CID arrives in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> to conduct an inquiry.</p>
<p>The following week, a <a href="/wiki/Badge" title="Badge">badge</a> that <a href="/wiki/Lofty_Holloway" title="Lofty Holloway">Lofty Holloway</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tom_Watt" title="Tom Watt">Tom Watt</a>) is wearing on his jumper catches the eye of <a href="/wiki/Harold_Legg" title="Harold Legg">Dr Legg</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leonard_Fenton" title="Leonard Fenton">Leonard Fenton</a>), who recognises it as one that he'd seen in Reg's collection of Nazi badges from the war. A few hours later, Rich arrives at <a href="/wiki/The_Queen_Victoria" title="The Queen Victoria">The Queen Victoria</a> where Lofty is tending bar and questions him about how he got the badge. Lofty reluctantly admits that he'd bought it from <a href="/wiki/Mark_Fowler" title="Mark Fowler">Mark Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/David_Scarboro" title="David Scarboro">David Scarboro</a>) for five pounds. When questioned by Detective Sergeant Rich, Mark insists that he found the badge on the street. However, on 28 March, Rich learns that Mark had obtained the badge from <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cotton" title="Nick Cotton">Nick Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/John_Altman_(actor)" title="John Altman (actor)">John Altman</a>) and 'turned over' Nick's place to find the remains of Reg's collection along with other incriminating evidence. Nick Cotton then flees Walford, but is arrested the following month for the murder of Reg Cox, although he is never charged.</p>
<p>Terry returns in 1987 to investigate the case of the 'Walford attacker', who has assaulted several different female inhabitants of Albert Square. In April of that year, the attacker picks the wrong victim, <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Wilkins" title="Debbie Wilkins">Debbie Wilkins</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shirley_Cheriton" title="Shirley Cheriton">Shirley Cheriton</a>), who manages to ward him off using self-defence, which leads to his arrest. Terry is impressed with the way Debbie conducted herself so he asks her if she would help him out on some police business. Terry asks Debbie to go to a French restaurant with him on an undercover operation to expose French restaurants using English waiters. Debbie agrees, but whilst on their mock date they bond and when they return to The Queen Victoria they make a dinner date for the next night to a Chinese restaurant and they are soon inseparable. Debbie's flat-mate <a href="/wiki/Naima_Jeffery" title="Naima Jeffery">Naima Jeffery</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shreela_Ghosh" title="Shreela Ghosh">Shreela Ghosh</a>) seems to dislike Terry immensely, but this doesn't perturb Debbie, and when Terry asks her to marry him, she gleefully accepts. The following month Terry is transferred to another division, so Debbie decides to leave Walford with him. He leaves with her on 14 May 1987.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tony_Carpenter">Tony Carpenter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Tony Carpenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tony_Carpenter" title="Tony Carpenter">Tony Carpenter</a></div>
<p><b>Tony Carpenter</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Oscar_James" title="Oscar James">Oscar James</a>, is a jovial character, who tries to establish a successful business and steady home for his family, but nothing he does is ever good enough for his nagging wife. Tony is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Tony was originally intended to be named Alan, and his son <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_Carpenter" title="Kelvin Carpenter">Kelvin Carpenter</a> was originally named Kevin. They are the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_(people)" title="Black (people)">black</a> characters to appear in the soap. Black and Asian characters were two ethnic minorities that had previously been under-represented in British soap before <i>EastEnders</i> aired. Holland and Smith knew that for the soap to succeed there needed to be a varied group of characters, so that several different sections of the audience had someone to identify with. Additionally, if the programme was to be realistic, it had to reflect the cross-section of society that actually existed in the real location. For these reasons, different sexes, ages, classes, religions and races were all included in the original character line-up. Both Holland and Smith had been at the forefront of the move towards 'integrated casting' in television and had encountered an array of ethnic diversities in the process. Even though the ethnic minority groups were deemed the hardest to research, Holland and Smith called upon their contacts to relay information about their origins and lifestyles and were then able to portray Walford's most recent immigrants more realistically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mary_Smith">Mary Smith</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Mary Smith">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mary_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Mary Smith (EastEnders)">Mary Smith (EastEnders)</a></div>
<p><b>Theresa "Mary" Smith</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Linda_Davidson" title="Linda Davidson">Linda Davidson</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Punk_subculture" title="Punk subculture">punk</a>, and is <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>'s original wild child. She often makes life difficult for herself due to her stubborn, defensive nature and she tends to feel that everyone around her is out to get her. In fact, Mary is her own worst enemy and most of her misfortune is down to her irresponsible behaviour and her inability to heed good advice. Mary Smith is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Mary's original character outline as written by Smith and Holland appeared in an abridged form in their book, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EastEnders_books" title="EastEnders books">EastEnders: The Inside Story</a></i>. As Holland and Smith wanted a diverse cross-section from the East End community, it was decided that one of the main cast had to be a young, single mother, and as <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk music</a> was prominent in British culture at the time, they decided to use a <a href="/wiki/Punk_fashion" title="Punk fashion">punk image</a> for the character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup> Holland and Smith decided to cast an unknown actress in the role. They chose <a href="/wiki/Linda_Davidson" title="Linda Davidson">Linda Davidson</a>, who was the right age and had been brought up in northern England and therefore had an accent that would befit the character's background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Annie Smith</b>, played by Zara Posener and Jenna Alembick from 1985 to 1986 and by Samantha Crown from 1986 to 1988, is the young daughter of <a href="/wiki/Punk_subculture" title="Punk subculture">punk</a> <a href="/wiki/Mary_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Mary Smith (EastEnders)">Mary Smith</a> (<a href="/wiki/Linda_Davidson" title="Linda Davidson">Linda Davidson</a>). Her father was a member of a punk group of which Mary was a groupie, and had no contact with her.</p>
<p>Annie is three months old when she arrives, along with Mary, on 5 March 1985 and initially resides with her mother at 23b <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>. Mary is an incredibly irresponsible mother and Annie is always getting lumbered on neighbours when Mary gets tired of looking after her. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dot_Branning" title="Dot Branning">Dot Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/June_Brown" title="June Brown">June Brown</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue Osman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sandy_Ratcliff" title="Sandy Ratcliff">Sandy Ratcliff</a>) are the usual victims, but almost everyone on <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">the Square</a> has a turn of looking after Annie. Sue Osman, in particular, grows extremely fond of Annie following the death of her son, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hassan_Osman" title="Hassan Osman">Hassan</a>. After bottling up her grief for months, she is forced to hold little Annie, which finally allows her to acknowledge her pent-up sadness.</p>
<p>Annie is once left alone in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Launderette" title="Launderette">launderette</a> by accident while Mary goes drinking in <a href="/wiki/The_Queen_Victoria" title="The Queen Victoria">The Queen Vic</a>. <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Wilkins" title="Debbie Wilkins">Debbie Wilkins</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shirley_Cheriton" title="Shirley Cheriton">Shirley Cheriton</a>) finds her and returns her to her mother, along with a few choice words for Mary. Mary also has a tendency to leave Annie home alone whilst she goes out to prostitute herself at night. On one such occasion, Annie throws her blanket at the electric fan heater, causing a fire. Luckily, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Treacher" title="Bill Treacher">Bill Treacher</a>) manages to rescue her but as a result, Annie is removed from Mary's care and given to her grandparents, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Chris Smith (EastEnders)">Chris</a> (<a href="/wiki/Allan_O%27Keefe" title="Allan O'Keefe">Allan O'Keefe</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edie_Smith" title="Edie Smith">Edie Smith</a> (<a href="/wiki/Eileen_O%27Brien_(actress)" title="Eileen O'Brien (actress)">Eileen O'Brien</a>), who live in <a href="/wiki/Stockport" title="Stockport">Stockport</a>. With the help of Mary's boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Rod_Norman" title="Rod Norman">Rod Norman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Christopher_McHallem" title="Christopher McHallem">Christopher McHallem</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Carmel_Jackson" title="Carmel Jackson">Carmel Jackson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Judith_Jacob" title="Judith Jacob">Judith Jacob</a>), Annie is eventually reunited with her mother, although it takes a lot of persuading to make Edie return her, who feels that Mary is not capable of caring for a young child. Annie spends Christmas Day 1987 in hospital after her intoxicated grandfather, Chris, abducts her and crashes his car into a wall in an attempt to take her back to Stockport. Luckily, Annie is unharmed. Eventually, Mary decides to leave Albert Square for good with Annie. They left in May 1988 and have not been seen since.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kelvin_Carpenter">Kelvin Carpenter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Kelvin Carpenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_Carpenter" title="Kelvin Carpenter">Kelvin Carpenter</a></div>
<p><b>Kelvin Carpenter</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Paul_J._Medford" title="Paul J. Medford">Paul J. Medford</a>, is an original <i>EastEnders</i> character. A bright spark and full of initiative, he opens several businesses in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> and even forms a band. He is a bit of a heartbreaker in <i>EastEnders</i> early years, but he eventually has his heart broken in return, when his middle-aged girlfriend jilts him. Always a bit too intelligent for <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>, Kelvin eventually leaves for university and has since married a model wife and has become a songwriter. Kelvin Carpenter was one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Kelvin was originally intended to be named Kevin, and his father <a href="/wiki/Tony_Carpenter" title="Tony Carpenter">Tony Carpenter</a> was originally named Alan. They are the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_(people)" title="Black (people)">black</a> characters to appear in the soap.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Andy_O.27Brien">Andy O'Brien</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Andy O'Brien">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Andy_O%27Brien_(EastEnders)" title="Andy O'Brien (EastEnders)">Andy O'Brien (EastEnders)</a></div>
<p><b>Andrew "Andy" O'Brien</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Ross_Davidson" title="Ross Davidson">Ross Davidson</a>, is one of the original characters created for the series. Andy makes his appearance one month after the show first broadcast in March 1985. Portrayed as altruistic and middle-classed, Andy and his partner <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Wilkins" title="Debbie Wilkins">Debbie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shirley_Cheriton" title="Shirley Cheriton">Shirley Cheriton</a>) are an attempt to represent gentrification of the East End. Despite Davidson claiming that there had been plans for his character, Andy became the first regular character in <i>EastEnders</i> to be killed-off. Davidson claims this was due to an altercation between himself and Executive Producer and show creator, <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. His death scene aired in August 1986.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Debbie_Wilkins">Debbie Wilkins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Debbie Wilkins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Wilkins" title="Debbie Wilkins">Debbie Wilkins</a></div>
<p><b>Deborah "Debbie" Wilkins</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Cheriton" title="Shirley Cheriton">Shirley Cheriton</a> is <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>'s first upwardly mobile character. She has an on/off relationship with her ill-fated boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Andy_O%27Brien_(EastEnders)" title="Andy O'Brien (EastEnders)">Andy O'Brien</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ross_Davidson" title="Ross Davidson">Ross Davidson</a>). Andy and she tend to be a bit too pretentious for the working class locals of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>. Debbie Wilkins is one of the original twenty-three characters invented by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. The character of Debbie along with her boyfriend Andy are an attempt by Holland and Smith to represent the influx of upwardly mobile people that were opting to move to the usually working-class areas of the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gentrification" title="Gentrification">Gentrification</a> of the East End was on the increase in the 1980s, and in Holland's experience, the new, wealthier residents were never welcomed or truly accepted within the community, and this was what he hoped to convey on-screen with these two characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Big Ron</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Ron_Tarr" title="Ron Tarr">Ron Tarr</a>, is an overweight man that works in <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a> market and appears as a background character from 1985 to 1997. Ron is involved in a few storylines, including one where he has a heart attack after he is pushed by a <a href="/wiki/Robbery" title="Robbery">mugger</a> in the market.</p>
<p>The actor Ron Tarr died of cancer in October 1997 and was appearing in pre-recorded episodes several weeks after his death. To explain the character's absence, he was given an off-screen storyline in early 1998 where he wins <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>500,000 on the <a href="/wiki/National_Lottery_(United_Kingdom)" title="National Lottery (United Kingdom)">National Lottery</a> and emigrates to Spain. In a report about Tarr's death in the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a></i>, journalist Chris Hughes stated the character had "a cult following".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mirror_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-mirror-34">[34]</a></sup> Ron could have appeared in a special episode of the popular science fiction series, <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Dimensions_in_Time" title="Dimensions in Time">Dimensions in Time</a></i> (1993). The episode was specially created as part of BBC's annual fund-raising event, <a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need" title="Children in Need">Children in Need</a>. Viewers were asked to phone in and vote which <i>EastEnders</i> character, <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Salter" title="Mandy Salter">Mandy Salter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nicola_Stapleton" title="Nicola Stapleton">Nicola Stapleton</a>) or Ron, would appear in the show and save <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_(Doctor_Who)" title="Doctor (Doctor Who)">The Doctor</a> from certain death. Two versions were filmed for each voting outcome, but the Mandy version won with 56% of the vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Ron's first line in the series is, "All right, Den?", said to <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>), five months after Ron first appeared.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mirror_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-mirror-34">[34]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Chris Smith</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Allan_O%27Keefe" title="Allan O'Keefe">Allan O'Keefe</a>, is the father of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Mary Smith (EastEnders)">Mary Smith</a> (<a href="/wiki/Linda_Davidson" title="Linda Davidson">Linda Davidson</a>). A haulage driver from <a href="/wiki/Stockport" title="Stockport">Stockport</a>, he is first seen in <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a> in May 1985 when he comes to visit his wayward daughter. Mary (or Theresa as she was known to Chris) had left <a href="/wiki/Stockport" title="Stockport">Stockport</a> to escape her family, so she isn't pleased when Chris arrives and tries to persuade her to return with him so he can help bring up her young daughter. After several ill-fated attempts, Mary sends him packing.</p>
<p>In 1987, Mary begins prostituting herself. She leaves her young baby, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annie_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Annie Smith (EastEnders)">Annie</a>, at home alone while she works. On one occasion, Annie throws a blanket out of her cot straight on to an electric fan heater, causing a fire. Annie is rescued but Mary's neighbour, <a href="/wiki/Dot_Cotton" title="Dot Cotton">Dot Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/June_Brown" title="June Brown">June Brown</a>), phones Chris, who returns to Walford and takes Annie to live with him and his wife, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edie_Smith" title="Edie Smith">Edie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Eileen_O%27Brien_(actress)" title="Eileen O'Brien (actress)">Eileen O'Brien</a>), in Stockport.</p>
<p>Mary's boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/Rod_Norman" title="Rod Norman">Rod Norman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Christopher_McHallem" title="Christopher McHallem">Christopher McHallem</a>), tries to help Mary sort her life out so she can get her daughter back. Even though Mary seems to be better, Edie has grown so attached to Annie that she is unwilling to relinquish care. By December, Chris finally decides that Annie should be reunited with Mary. Edie is unhappy about this and tells Chris that he can only come home when he brings Annie back. On Christmas Eve 1987, after desperately trying to convince Mary to return to Stockport, a very <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drunk" title="Drunk">drunk</a> Chris abducts Annie and tries to drive her home, only to crash into a wall at the local <a href="/wiki/Bed_and_breakfast" title="Bed and breakfast">bed and breakfast</a> on Bridge Street. Mary is forced to spend Christmas Day in hospital so Annie can be monitored. Chris desperately tries to make amends for his mistake, but she can't forgive him until Annie is given the all clear later that day, so a jubilant Mary accepts her father's apology.</p>
<p>In early 1988, Chris makes plans to open up a haulage company at a disused tyre shop in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>, but finds out that his driving licence is to be suspended for 18 months following his drink driving accident. Nevertheless, he contacts his friend, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harry_Jameson" title="Harry Jameson">Harry Jameson</a> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Dutton_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anthony Dutton (actor) (page does not exist)">Anthony Dutton</a>) to go into partnership and even manages to persuade <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>) to provide some capital. However, Ali pulls out of the deal and Chris turns to alcohol, regularly drinking himself into a stupor to drown his sorrows. A concerned Mary then decides to contact her mother, who returns to Walford to help her husband. By May, Chris succeeds in opening the haulage company open by borrowing money from Walford Investments – the money lending organisation of <a href="/wiki/The_Firm_(EastEnders)" title="The Firm (EastEnders)">The Firm</a> — but the antics of his daughter, who starts to use drugs again and neglect Annie, cause further family strife. In a bid to help Mary out, Chris offers her a job at the haulage company but Mary gets annoyed by her parents' interference, and after sabotaging her father's office with red paint, she and Annie leave Walford.</p>
<p>Chris stays in Walford to run his business, employing Rod as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bookkeeper" title="Bookkeeper">bookkeeper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Cotton" title="Charlie Cotton">Charlie Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hancock" title="Christopher Hancock">Christopher Hancock</a>) as lorry driver, although he is extremely unreliable. However, Chris is still short of drivers, so he starts doing deliveries himself, despite having a suspended licence. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darren_Roberts_(EastEnders)" title="Darren Roberts (EastEnders)">Darren Roberts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gary_McDonald_(actor)" title="Gary McDonald (actor)">Gary McDonald</a>), who had had several run-ins with Chris regarding the theft of his <a href="/wiki/Backhoe" title="Backhoe">JCB</a> earlier that year, informs the police that he is driving illegally and Chris is fined <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>1000. This severely cripples his business and he is unable to repay his loan. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gregory_Mantel" title="Gregory Mantel">Gregory Mantel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pavel_Douglas" title="Pavel Douglas">Pavel Douglas</a>), a member of The Firm, then arrives on the Square, looking for Chris and forces him to sign the business over to The Firm, leaving Chris no choice but to return to Stockport. His last appearance is in August 1988. The premises of the haulage company are eventually bought by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Butcher" title="Frank Butcher">Frank Butcher</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mike_Reid_(actor)" title="Mike Reid (actor)">Mike Reid</a>), who converts it into the car lot.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mehmet_Osman">Mehmet Osman</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: Mehmet Osman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mehmet_Osman" title="Mehmet Osman">Mehmet Osman</a></div>
<p><b>Mehmet Osman</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Haluk_Bilginer" title="Haluk Bilginer">Haluk Bilginer</a>, is a recurring character appearing from 1985 to 1989. Mehmet is portrayed as a charmer, rogue and a serial womaniser. He was conceptualised by the creators of <i>EastEnders</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Holland" title="Tony Holland">Tony Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Smith" title="Julia Smith">Julia Smith</a>. Mehmet, the brother of original character <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali Osman</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>), is part of a well-intentioned attempt to represent the proportion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Cypriot" title="Turkish Cypriot">Turkish Cypriots</a> who had immigrated to England and settled in the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a>. Holland and Smith knew that for the soap to succeed there needed to be a varied group of characters, so that several different sections of the audience had someone to identify with. Additionally, if the programme was to be realistic, it had to reflect the cross-section of society that actually existed in the real location. For these reasons, different sexes, ages, classes, religions and races were all included in the original character line-up. Both Holland and Smith had been at the forefront of the move towards 'integrated casting' in television and had encountered an array of ethnic diversities in the process. Even though the ethnic minority groups were deemed the hardest to research, Holland and Smith called upon their social contacts to relay information about their own origins and lifestyles, which they say allowed them to portray Walford's most recent immigrants more realistically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidestory_2-16"><a href="#cite_note-insidestory-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Hannah Carpenter</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Sally_Sagoe" title="Sally Sagoe">Sally Sagoe</a>, is a religious woman who is active in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pentecostal_Church" title="Pentecostal Church">Pentecostal Church</a> but lost all tolerance for her fun-loving husband, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Carpenter" title="Tony Carpenter">Tony Carpenter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oscar_James" title="Oscar James">Oscar James</a>). After they agreed to divorce, their son, <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_Carpenter" title="Kelvin Carpenter">Kelvin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Paul_J._Medford" title="Paul J. Medford">Paul J. Medford</a>), moves to the Square to live with his father in February 1985, while their daughter, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cassie_Carpenter" title="Cassie Carpenter">Cassie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Delanie_Forbes" title="Delanie Forbes">Delanie Forbes</a>), stays with her mother. Hannah's first appearance in <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a> is in June 1985 and throughout the year she shows up occasionally to converse with Tony about issues concerning their children. Hannah constantly looks down on Tony's lifestyle and career, so their meetings are never pleasant.</p>
<p>When Hannah meets the smooth talking corporate lawyer, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neville_Agard" title="Neville Agard">Neville Agard</a> (Gordon Case), she invites him to move into her house. Eventually, Neville buys the house and they announce their intention to wed. However, when she discovers that Neville has a violent temper and has beaten her daughter with a riding <a href="/wiki/Whip" title="Whip">whip</a>, she confronts him and after a row results in violence she leaves him and turns up on Tony's doorstep at number 3 <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> in April 1986. Tony is furious and goes to confront Neville, returning later covered in blood. Hannah is adamant that she will not return to Neville, so she and Cassie come to live with Tony and they decide to try to give their marriage another chance.</p>
<p>Their reconciliation is not a happy one and it isn't long before Hannah had returns to her nagging ways. She is unhappy to be living in a house that is in the throes of renovation and she constantly harangues Tony to do better and try harder. She also manages to upset her children by trying to put a stop to her son's relationship with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carmel_Roberts" title="Carmel Roberts">Carmel Roberts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Judith_Jacob" title="Judith Jacob">Judith Jacob</a>) and forcing Cassie to go to <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a>.</p>
<p>In 1987 Hannah begins to become disillusioned with Walford after getting accosted by both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rezaul_Gabir" title="Rezaul Gabir">Rezaul Gabir</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tanveer_Ghani" title="Tanveer Ghani">Tanveer Ghani</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Mehmet_Osman" title="Mehmet Osman">Mehmet Osman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Haluk_Bilginer" title="Haluk Bilginer">Haluk Bilginer</a>). She is also stalked one night by a man known as the Walford attacker, who is guilty of assaulting several female inhabitants of the Square. Hannah is shaken but unharmed. She later relays to Tony that she fears that Mehmet is the culprit, because he had made a pass at her previously. Tony is furious, and he along with <a href="/wiki/Den_Watts" title="Den Watts">Den Watts</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Grantham" title="Leslie Grantham">Leslie Grantham</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Pete_Beale" title="Pete Beale">Pete Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Peter_Dean_(actor)" title="Peter Dean (actor)">Peter Dean</a>) go to track Mehmet down and teach him a lesson. After Hannah discovers that her husband is guilty of attacking Mehmet, she takes the moral high-ground and denounces his barbaric actions (Despite previously being happy for Tony to resort to violence against Neville). Hannah then decides that their marriage is over and so she leaves Walford in February that year to go and live with her sister in another part of London. She is not seen again.</p>

<p><b>Ernie Mears</b> wooed <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Skinner" title="Ethel Skinner">Ethel Skinner</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gretchen_Franklin" title="Gretchen Franklin">Gretchen Franklin</a>) during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> but after she married his good friend William Skinner, he disappeared from their lives. Many years later, Ernie confesses to Ethel that he fled because he feared that he would either go insane being around an unobtainable Ethel, or that they would do something to hurt William if their association had continued.</p>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Adam_Woodyatt" title="Adam Woodyatt">Adam Woodyatt</a>) becomes interested in learning to <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">box</a> in July 1985, Ethel joins him on his first trip to the gym, as boxing is her favourite sport. <p>Upon learning that it is Ethel and that she is now <a href="/wiki/Widow" title="Widow">widowed</a>, Ernie asks her on a date and a few weeks later asks her to marry him. However, the offer is conditional. Ernie is <a href="/wiki/Allergy" title="Allergy">allergic</a> to dogs and <a href="/wiki/Willy_(EastEnders)" title="Willy (EastEnders)">Willy</a> the <a href="/wiki/Pug" title="Pug">pug</a>, Ethel's constant companion for the past 9 years, would have to go. Ethel seriously considers Ernie's offer but gently declines, after which Ernie departs broken hearted again. His last appearance is on 15 August 1985.</p>

<p><b>Detective Sergeant Roy Quick</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Fielding" title="Douglas Fielding">Douglas Fielding</a>, first arrives in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> on 20 August 1985 in order to put his old adversary, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cotton" title="Nick Cotton">Nick Cotton</a> (<a href="/wiki/John_Altman_(actor)" title="John Altman (actor)">John Altman</a>), in prison. He fails at this, but finds the woman he wants to marry, <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Wilkins" title="Debbie Wilkins">Debbie Wilkins</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shirley_Cheriton" title="Shirley Cheriton">Shirley Cheriton</a>), who is seeing <a href="/wiki/Andy_O%27Brien_(EastEnders)" title="Andy O'Brien (EastEnders)">Andy O'Brien</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ross_Davidson" title="Ross Davidson">Ross Davidson</a>) in an <a href="/wiki/Open_relationship" title="Open relationship">open relationship</a>. Roy tries to woo Debbie, sending her bouquets of flowers and demonstrating his <a href="/wiki/Jujutsu" title="Jujutsu">jujutsu</a> techniques in order to impress her. Andy becomes jealous of their friendship and tries to make them feel as uncomfortable as possible.</p>
<p>After a few months of platonic dating, Roy eventually asks Debbie to marry him. A surprised Debbie turns the proposal down, but Roy continues to pester her until Debbie tells him that she can't marry him as she has only been using him to make Andy jealous. Debbie's rejection adds to Roy's dissatisfaction with the course his life has taken. He feels he is in a dead-end career with the police force and so he decides to leave to work for a security firm. His parting remarks to the citizenry in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Queen_Vic" title="The Queen Vic">The Queen Vic</a> public house are "You lot deserve each other!" His last appearance is in July 1986.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Martin_Fowler">Martin Fowler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: Martin Fowler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Martin_Fowler_(EastEnders)" title="Martin Fowler (EastEnders)">Martin Fowler (EastEnders)</a></div>
<p><b>Martin Albert Fowler</b>, played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jon_Peyton_Price" title="Jon Peyton Price">Jon Peyton Price</a> from 1985 to 1996 and <a href="/wiki/James_Alexandrou" title="James Alexandrou">James Alexandrou</a> from 1996 to 2007, is the younger son of <a href="/wiki/Pauline_Fowler" title="Pauline Fowler">Pauline</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wendy_Richard" title="Wendy Richard">Wendy Richard</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fowler" title="Arthur Fowler">Arthur Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Treacher" title="Bill Treacher">Bill Treacher</a>), born in July 1985. His considerably older siblings are <a href="/wiki/Mark_Fowler" title="Mark Fowler">Mark</a> (<a href="/wiki/David_Scarboro" title="David Scarboro">David Scarboro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Todd_Carty" title="Todd Carty">Todd Carty</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Fowler" title="Michelle Fowler">Michelle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Susan_Tully" title="Susan Tully">Susan Tully</a>). Martin is the first baby to be born in the serial. His grandmother <a href="/wiki/Lou_Beale" title="Lou Beale">Lou</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anna_Wing" title="Anna Wing">Anna Wing</a>) is extremely fond of him, and insists that he is named after her late husband <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albert_Beale" title="Albert Beale">Albert</a>. Pauline and Arthur are against this, and for several months the baby remains unnamed, although Lou continues to refer to him as Albert. The baby's name is finally revealed as Martin Albert Fowler at his christening in October 1985, which satisfies Lou. Martin grows up facing numerous family upsets, including his father's mental breakdown and imprisonment and his parents' temporary separation in 1993, which Martin takes badly. As Martin ages he becomes surly and increasingly troublesome.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_Wicks">Simon Wicks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_EastEnders_characters_(1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: Simon Wicks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Simon_Wicks" title="Simon Wicks">Simon Wicks</a></div>
<p><b>Simon "Wicksy" Wicks</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%A9" title="Né">né</a> <b>Beale</b>), played by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Berry" title="Nick Berry">Nick Berry</a>, appears between 1985 and 1990. Wicksy was introduced to take on some of the more adult storylines that had been scripted for another character, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Fowler" title="Mark Fowler">Mark Fowler</a>; Mark's actor <a href="/wiki/David_Scarboro" title="David Scarboro">David Scarboro</a> had left the serial prematurely due to personal problems. Wicksy was the soap's first male, pin-up and proved extremely popular with female fans. An early storyline sees Wicksy perform a song in the serial, "<a href="/wiki/Every_Loser_Wins" title="Every Loser Wins">Every Loser Wins</a>", which was subsequently released as a single in 1986 and reached number one in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_singles_chart" title="UK singles chart">UK singles chart</a>. One of Wicksy's most prominent storylines is his adultery with <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Beale" title="Cindy Beale">Cindy Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michelle_Collins" title="Michelle Collins">Michelle Collins</a>), and a subsequent feud with Cindy's husband <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian</a> (<a href="/wiki/Adam_Woodyatt" title="Adam Woodyatt">Adam Woodyatt</a>). Nick Berry quit the role in 1990, fearful of typecasting, and after five years on-screen, Wicksy departed in December that year.</p>

<p><b>Sheena Menell</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Dulice_Liecier" title="Dulice Liecier">Dulice Liecier</a>, first appears on 15 October 1985 when her taxicab breaks down in Bridge Street and she wanders into the café and asks <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue Osman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sandy_Ratcliff" title="Sandy Ratcliff">Sandy Ratcliff</a>) where she might find another one. Sue tells her that her husband, <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>), might be able to take her where she needs to go and so <a href="/wiki/Mary_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Mary Smith (EastEnders)">Mary Smith</a> (<a href="/wiki/Linda_Davidson" title="Linda Davidson">Linda Davidson</a>), who is in the café at the time, offers to show her the way to Number 23 <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> in search of Ali.</p>

Sheena had once been in a similar situation to Mary when she found herself pregnant and alone, although she opted to abort the baby, which she had always regretted. Mary is impressed that Sheena earned £150 per week as a <a href="/wiki/Stripper" title="Stripper">stripper</a>. Weary of trying to live on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Social_Security" title="Department of Social Security">DSS</a> payments she receives, Mary eagerly accepts Sheena's offer to help her find work as a stripper. Sheena also gives Mary dancing lessons.</p>
<p>Whilst out working, Mary leaves her daughter, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annie_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Annie Smith (EastEnders)">Annie</a>, in Sheena's care, but she is caught by Sue 'entertaining' a man in Mary's room with Annie in close proximity. Sheena then leaves Annie alone while she goes for a drink in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Queen_Vic" title="The Queen Vic">The Queen Vic</a> with her guest. When Mary finds out about Sheena's behaviour she calls her a "sluttish bitch" and tells her that Annie could end up in care because of her irresponsible actions. This is the last time Sheena was seen.</p>

<p><b>Guizin Osman</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Ishia_Bennison" title="Ishia Bennison">Ishia Bennison</a>, is the long suffering wife of the philandering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Cypriot" title="Turkish Cypriot">Turkish Cypriot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mehmet_Osman" title="Mehmet Osman">Mehmet Osman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Haluk_Bilginer" title="Haluk Bilginer">Haluk Bilginer</a>). She first arrives in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a> in October 1985 when she comes to inform her sister-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Sue_Osman" title="Sue Osman">Sue</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sandy_Ratcliff" title="Sandy Ratcliff">Sandy Ratcliff</a>), that her husband, <a href="/wiki/Ali_Osman" title="Ali Osman">Ali</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nejdet_Salih" title="Nejdet Salih">Nejdet Salih</a>), had <a href="/wiki/Gambling" title="Gambling">gambled</a> away the <a href="/wiki/Mortgage_loan" title="Mortgage loan">mortgage</a> money for the home they were planning to buy.</p>
<p>Guizin and her three children, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emine_Osman" title="Emine Osman">Emine</a> (Pelin Ahmet), Rayif and Murat, later move in with her brother-in-law and his family in 1987, after her husband gambles away their home and deserts them. She works for Ali and Sue in the café for a few months until her husband returns and she becomes a partner in the business. As part owner of the café, she hires <a href="/wiki/Ian_Beale" title="Ian Beale">Ian Beale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Adam_Woodyatt" title="Adam Woodyatt">Adam Woodyatt</a>), who later becomes the sole owner.</p>
<p>Guizin and Mehmet's marriage is highly turbulent and they are always involved in intense arguments, mainly concerning money and gambling. Guizin is well aware of Mehmet's flaws and <a href="/wiki/Adultery" title="Adultery">adultery</a>, but unlike Sue, she is willing to turn a blind eye to most of her husband's dealings in order to keep the marriage together because, in her community, that's what a wife is expected to do. Guizin may put up with a lot from Mehmet, but she is certainly no pushover, and is more than willing to defend herself and her family if anyone should cross her. She has numerous rows with Sue over the years and she nearly comes to blows with <a href="/wiki/Donna_Ludlow" title="Donna Ludlow">Donna Ludlow</a> (<a href="/wiki/Matilda_Ziegler" title="Matilda Ziegler">Matilda Ziegler</a>) after she informs her that Mehmet had been trying to seduce her on <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a> 1987. Yet more animosity arises when she discovers that Mehmet has been sleeping with prostitute <a href="/wiki/Mary_Smith_(EastEnders)" title="Mary Smith (EastEnders)">Mary Smith</a> (<a href="/wiki/Linda_Davidson" title="Linda Davidson">Linda Davidson</a>), but although Guizin gets extremely angry, she ends up forgiving Mehmet for the sake of the family. However, there is one affair that Guizin is not willing to forgive Mehmet for, and it happens to be the only one that he didn't actually have. After Sue discovers that Ali had slept with Donna Ludlow, she kisses Mehmet in front of Ali for revenge, causing a huge fight between the brothers. Ali tells Guizin about Sue and Mehmet's suspected affair, making Guizin furious. She viciously attacks Mehmet in the middle of the Square and leaves for <a href="/wiki/Northern_Cyprus" title="Northern Cyprus">Northern Cyprus</a>, taking her children with her. Her last appearance is in March 1989. Mehmet leaves England soon after to attempt to patch up their relationship.</p>
<p>Bennison has "mixed memories" about her role in <i>EastEnders</i>, commenting in 2003: "it was the start of the soap and the fame was very instant for everybody. You're in everybody's front room and everyone feels they own a bit of you. I'll never forget having to sign people's sickbags on the hovercraft to France. My daughter wouldn't walk down the street with me at the time. She would stay 10 paces behind. I still get recognised from <i>EastEnders</i>. I can't believe it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Queen_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Queen-37">[37]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Cassie Carpenter</b>, played by <a href="/wiki/Delanie_Forbes" title="Delanie Forbes">Delanie Forbes</a>, appears from 1985 to 1986. In an early episode, before Cassie appears, her brother refers to his sister as being seven years old, but when the character was actually seen later in the year, it was decided to increase her age to eleven, so a young actress who would be capable of handling the part.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10years_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-10years-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cassie is the daughter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hannah_Carpenter" title="Hannah Carpenter">Hannah</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sally_Sagoe" title="Sally Sagoe">Sally Sagoe</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Carpenter" title="Tony Carpenter">Tony Carpenter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oscar_James" title="Oscar James">Oscar James</a>) and the little sister of <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_Carpenter" title="Kelvin Carpenter">Kelvin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Paul_J._Medford" title="Paul J. Medford">Paul J. Medford</a>). Cassie's parents are separated and she initially resides with her mother and her mother's new boyfriend, a corporate lawyer named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neville_Agard" title="Neville Agard">Neville Agard</a> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gordon_Case&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gordon Case (page does not exist)">Gordon Case</a>). Cassie first appears in November 1985 when she comes to visit her father in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Square" title="Albert Square">Albert Square</a>. Cassie loves her father and finds her parents' separation difficult, particularly as she doesn't get on with her mother's boyfriend.</p>
<p>In April 1986 Hannah and Cassie arrive on Tony's doorstep with the disturbing news that Neville has been beating Cassie with a riding <a href="/wiki/Whip" title="Whip">whip</a>. Both Hannah and Cassie then come to live in <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a> with Tony and Kelvin, and Cassie is delighted when her parents decided to give their marriage another chance.</p>
<p>Cassie is a mischievous youngster who, like her brother, is bright. In May 1986 she gets into trouble with her school for bullying another girl, and is in even more trouble with her parents for trying to hide it from them. Tony later catches her smoking <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">cannabis</a> with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Fowler" title="Mark Fowler">Mark Fowler</a> (<a href="/wiki/David_Scarboro" title="David Scarboro">David Scarboro</a>). He is furious and the Carpenters refuse to speak to any of the Fowler family because of it. After a subsequent meeting with Cassie's headmaster, Hannah decides that the only solution is to send Cassie away to <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a>. Cassie is against the idea but nevertheless, she is sent away in September that year. A few weeks later the Carpenters hear news that Cassie has run away from school and is nowhere to be found. She is discovered the following day, stealing food from <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Fowler" title="Michelle Fowler">Michelle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Susan_Tully" title="Susan Tully">Susan Tully</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Lofty_Holloway" title="Lofty Holloway">Lofty Holloway</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Tom_Watt_(actor)" title="Tom Watt (actor)">Tom Watt</a>) kitchen in Albert Square, and returns to the school.</p>
<p>Cassie appears infrequently after this, although she does play a Herald <a href="/wiki/Angel" title="Angel">Angel</a> in the Walford <a href="/wiki/Nativity_of_Jesus" title="Nativity of Jesus">Nativity</a> play in December 1986. This is her last appearance, but after her parents attempt at a reconciliation fails, Hannah leaves Walford and Cassie goes to live with her. Tony visits her occasionally, before leaving Walford too in 1987.</p>
