<p>The <b><i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i>-class</b> destroyers (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 광개토대왕급 구축함, <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: 廣開土大王級驅逐艦), often called <b>KD-I class</b>, are <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a>, but are classified by some as <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigates</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> operated by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Navy" title="Republic of Korea Navy">Republic of Korea Navy</a>. It was the first phase of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ROKN" title="ROKN">ROKN</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Korean_Destroyer_eXperimental" title="Korean Destroyer eXperimental">KDX</a> program, in moving the ROK Navy from a coastal defence force to a <a href="/wiki/Blue-water_navy" title="Blue-water navy">blue-water navy</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gwanggaeto_the_Great-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The KDX-I was designed to replace the old destroyers in the ROKN that were transferred from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Navy" title="US Navy">US Navy</a> in the 1950s and 1960s. It was thought to be a major turning point for the ROKN in that the launching of the first KDX-I meant that ROKN finally had a capability to project power far from its shores. After the launching of the ship, there was a massive boom in South Korean international participation against piracy and <a href="/wiki/Military_operations_other_than_war" title="Military operations other than war">military operations other than war</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gwanggaeto_the_Great-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weapon_Systems">Weapon Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gwanggaeto_the_Great-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Weapon Systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The primary weapon deployed by <i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i>-class vessels is the Super Lynx helicopter, which acts in concert with shipboard sensors to seek out and destroy submarines at long distances from the ships. The <i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i> class also carries a close-in anti-submarine weapon in the form of the Mark 46 torpedoes, launched from triple torpedo tubes in launcher compartments either side of the forward end of the helicopter hangar. A secondary anti-shipping role is supported by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RGM-84_Harpoon" title="RGM-84 Harpoon">RGM-84 Harpoon</a> surface-to-surface missile, mounted in two quadruple launch tubes at the main deck level between the funnel and the helicopter hangar. For anti-aircraft self-defense, the <i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i> class carries 16 RIM-7P <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_Sparrow" title="Sea Sparrow">Sea Sparrow</a>. The <i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i> class also carries two 30mm <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_CIWS" title="Goalkeeper CIWS">Goalkeeper</a> to provide a shipboard point-defense against incoming anti-ship missiles and aircraft. The main gun on the <a href="/wiki/Forecastle" title="Forecastle">forecastle</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Otobreda_127/54_Compact" title="Otobreda 127/54 Compact">OTO Melara 127</a> gun.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Propulsion">Propulsion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gwanggaeto_the_Great-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Propulsion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i> class is powered by two <a href="/wiki/General_Electric_LM2500" title="General Electric LM2500">General Electric LM2500-30 gas turbines</a> and two SsangYong 20V 956 TB 82 diesel engines. The <i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i> class can reach a maximum speed of 30 knots.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction">Construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gwanggaeto_the_Great-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All <i>Gwanggaeto the Great</i>-class destroyers were built by the <a href="/wiki/Daewoo" title="Daewoo">Daewoo</a> Heavy Industries Co., Inc. at <a href="/wiki/Geoje" title="Geoje">Geoje</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. In 1989, Daewoo Heavy Industries began working on the 4,000-ton destroyer which is now the secondary destroyer of the Korean navy, and the achievement was made through DSME's 100% design engineering for the first time in Korea.</p>
