<p><i><b>Lamium amplexicaule</b></i>, commonly known as <b>henbit dead-nettle</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BSBI07_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BSBI07-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>common henbit</b>, or <b>greater henbit</b>, is a species of <i><a href="/wiki/Lamium" title="Lamium">Lamium</a></i> native to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>
<p>It is a low-growing <a href="/wiki/Annual_plant" title="Annual plant">annual plant</a> growing to 10â25Â cm (3.9â9.8Â in) tall, with soft, finely hairy stems. The <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> are opposite, rounded, 2â3Â cm (0.79â1.18Â in) diameter, with a lobed margin. The <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> are pink to purple, 1.5â2Â cm (0.59â0.79Â in) long. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Specific_name_(botany)" title="Specific name (botany)">specific name</a> refers to the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_botanical_terms" title="Glossary of botanical terms">amplexicaul</a> leaves (leaves grasping the stem).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lamium_amplexicaule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Henbit dead-nettle is an annual herb with a sprawling habit and short erect squarish, lightly hairy stems. It grows to a height of about 10 to 30Â cm (4 to 12Â in). The leaves are in opposite pairs, often with long internodes. The lower leaves are stalked and the upper ones stalkless, often fused, and clasping the stems. The blades are hairy and kidney-shaped, with rounded teeth. The flowers are relatively large and form a few-flowered terminal spike with axillary whorls. The calyx is regular with five lobes and closes up after flowering. The <a href="/wiki/Petal" title="Petal">corolla</a> is purplish-red, fused into a tube 15 to 20Â mm (0.6 to 0.8Â in) long. The upper lip is convex, 3 to 5Â mm (0.12 to 0.20Â in) long and the lower lip has three lobes, two small side ones and a larger central one 1.5 to 2.5Â mm (0.06 to 0.10Â in) long. There are four stamens, two long and two short. The <a href="/wiki/Gynoecium" title="Gynoecium">gynoecium</a> has two fused carpels and the fruit is a four-chambered <a href="/wiki/Schizocarp" title="Schizocarp">schizocarp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatureGate_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NatureGate-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>This plant flowers very early in the spring even in northern areas, and for most of the winter and the early spring in warmer locations such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean_region" title="Mediterranean region">Mediterranean region</a>. At times of year when there are not many pollinating insects, the flowers self-pollinate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatureGate_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NatureGate-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lamium_amplexicaule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Henbit dead-nettle is probably native to the Mediterranean region but has spread around the world as an arable weed. It is found growing in bare places, gardens, fields and waste places.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatureGate_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NatureGate-2">[2]</a></sup> It propagates freely by <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seed</a> and is regarded as a minor <a href="/wiki/Weed" title="Weed">weed</a>. Sometimes entire fields will be reddish-purple with its flowers before spring ploughing. Where common, it is an important <a href="/wiki/Nectar" title="Nectar">nectar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pollen" title="Pollen">pollen</a> plant for <a href="/wiki/Bee" title="Bee">bees</a>, especially <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honeybee" title="Honeybee">honeybees</a>, where it helps start the spring build up.</p>
<p>It is widely <a href="/wiki/Naturalisation_(biology)" title="Naturalisation (biology)">naturalised</a> in eastern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and elsewhere, where it is often considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a>. However, its attractive appearance, edibility and readiness to grow in many climates often mean it is permitted to grow when other 'weeds' are not.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lamium_amplexicaule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The leaves, stem, and flowers of the plant are edible and have a slightly sweet and peppery flavor. Henbit can be eaten raw or cooked.</p>
