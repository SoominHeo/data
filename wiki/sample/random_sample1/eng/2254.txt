<p><i><b>Flueggea suffruticosa</b></i> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Phyllanthaceae" title="Phyllanthaceae">Phyllanthaceae</a>. It is one of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_herbology#50_fundamental_herbs" title="Chinese herbology">50 fundamental herbs</a> used in <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_medicine" title="Traditional Chinese medicine">traditional Chinese medicine</a>, where it has the name <i>yÄ« yÃ¨ qiÅ«</i> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E4%B8%80" title="wiktionary:ä¸">ä¸</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8F%B6" title="wiktionary:å¶">å¶</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%A7%8B" title="wiktionary:ç§">ç§</a></span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Securinega suffruticosa contains the alkaloid <i>securinine</i> which has been regarded as a CNS stimulant since the 1960s, and was used to study other related effects on the CNS as a GABAA receptor antagonist in Russia and China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
