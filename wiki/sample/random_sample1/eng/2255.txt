<p>The <b>Cantonese Wikipedia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">粵文維基百科</span>; <a href="/wiki/Jyutping" title="Jyutping">Jyutping</a>: <i><span lang="yue-jyutping" xml:lang="yue-jyutping">Jyut<sup>6</sup>man<sup>4</sup>wai<sup>4</sup>gei<sup>1</sup>baak<sup>3</sup>fo<sup>1</sup></span></i>) is the <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a>-language edition of Wikipedia, run by the <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a>. It started on 25 March 2004</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cantonese_Wikipedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Written_Cantonese" title="Written Cantonese">Written Cantonese</a></div>
<p>Cantonese, a major variety of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese language</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_China" title="Southern China">southern China</a>, is the <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">lingua franca</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangdong_Province" title="Guangdong Province">Guangdong Province</a> and other neighboring areas, including <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a>. Cantonese has the most well-developed written form of all Chinese varieties apart from the standard-Mandarin variety and Classical Chinese.</p>
<p>With the advent of the computer and standardization of character sets specifically for Cantonese, many printed materials in predominantly Cantonese speaking areas of the world cater to their population using these written Cantonese characters.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cantonese_Wikipedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Cantonese name of Wikipedia was decided in April 2006, following a vote. In a similar manner to the existing <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Wikipedia" title="Chinese Wikipedia">Chinese Wikipedia</a>, the name of Cantonese Wikipedia (Traditional Chinese: 維基百科; Simplified Chinese: 维基百科; <a href="/wiki/Jyutping" title="Jyutping">jyutping</a> "wai<sup>4</sup> gei<sup>1</sup> baak<sup>3</sup> fo<sup>1</sup>") means "Wiki Encyclopedia". The Chinese transcription of "Wiki" is composed of two characters: 維/维, whose ancient sense refers to 'ropes or webs connecting objects', and alludes to the 'Internet'; and 基, meaning the 'foundations of a building', or 'fundamental aspects of things in general'. The name can be interpreted as 'the encyclopedia that connects the fundamental knowledge of humanity'.</p>
<p>The discussion in 2006 also decided the Cantonese name of the word "Cantonese" in the wiki. The result was "粵語". In a vote in August to September 2013 it has been decided that the name be changed to "粵文", which literally means Cantonese inscription.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ISO_code">ISO code</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cantonese_Wikipedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: ISO code">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the time of establishment, there was no ISO code for Cantonese. Thus, zh-yue was used as the domain. However, some did not like the idea, as Yue is the Mandarin name of Cantonese. The native name is Yuet. Cantonese preserves <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entering_tone" title="Entering tone">entering tone</a> while Mandarin does not. Some suggested using Cantonese instead, however zh-yue was chosen as the final domain name.</p>
<p>The ISO code for Cantonese is yue. Using the ISO code instead of current domain has been submitted as a proposal to Wikimedia, however, as of yet, no action has been taken to implement this change.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Native_name">Native name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cantonese_Wikipedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Native name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are various native names of Cantonese, namely Kwang Tung Wa (廣東話), Yuet Yu (粵語), and Kwang Fu Wa (廣府話). Cantonese Wikipedians thus use the three names interchangeably, namely 廣東話維基百科, 粵語維基百科, and 廣府話維基百科. There is no consensus on the native name.</p>

<p>Cantonese flourishes in <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a> in China, and Hong Kong, and <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>. Some Cantonese speakers are native to <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, Singapore, <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, Thailand, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, Canada, United Kingdom, or the United States.</p>
<p>Hong Kong owns the largest written Cantonese assets and does not have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_firewall_of_China" title="Great firewall of China">internet censorship</a>. Fittingly, most editors are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Konger" title="Hong Konger">Hong Kongers</a>.</p>
<p>Starting from a small community, Cantonese Wikipedians have formed a consensus to follow, by default, the policies, guidelines, and customs of the <a href="/wiki/English_Wikipedia" title="English Wikipedia">English Wikipedia</a>, wherever they are applicable. This consensus has provided stability and allows Wikipedians to concentrate on content.</p>
<p>As of 26 January 2017, the Cantonese Wikipedia has over 1,101,605 edits, over 50,551 articles, and more than 131,853 registered users, 9 of whom are administrators.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Script">Script</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cantonese_Wikipedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Script">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two different scripts of written Cantonese, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese" title="Traditional Chinese">Traditional Chinese</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese" title="Simplified Chinese">Simplified Chinese</a>. Cantonese Wikipedia uses Traditional following Hong Kong and Macau in their traditional usage. Some Cantonese specific characters can be typed in Traditional characters only. Simplified characters will be converted to Traditional ones manually.</p>
<p>For Simplified character users, an online converter to Simplified character is provided for viewing the content.</p>
