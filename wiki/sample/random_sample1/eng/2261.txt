<p><b>Guangzhou Railway Station</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_character" title="Chinese character">Chinese</a>: 广州火车站; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">Pinyin</a>: Guǎngzhōu Huǒchēzhàn) serves the city of <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, China. It sits on the high speed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangshen_railway" title="Guangshen railway">Guangshen railway</a> just west of <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_East_Railway_Station" title="Guangzhou East Railway Station">Guangzhou East</a>. As one of the busiest railway stations in southern China, Guangzhou station is known for its notorious safety record.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/China_Railway_High-speed" title="China Railway High-speed">CRH</a> trains from Guangzhou serves <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a> six times daily.</p>
<p>Services from this station include trains to <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jingguang_railway" title="Jingguang railway">Jingguang railway</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Lhasa" title="Lhasa">Lhasa</a>. It is served by the <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Metro" title="Guangzhou Metro">Guangzhou Metro</a> on Line 2 and Line 5.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guangzhou_Railway_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As early as the 1950s, the provincial government saw the need for a central train station in Guangzhou but the plans were shelved until 1974 due to the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>. On the 4<span class="nowrap"> </span>April, 1979 with the normalisation of relations with Hong Kong, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangdong_Through_Train_(MTR)" title="Guangdong Through Train (MTR)">through-train</a> service that was suspended for over twenty-five years was resumed. Through-train services were moved to <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_East_Railway_Station" title="Guangzhou East Railway Station">Guangzhou East</a> in 1996 due to increasing security problems.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Metro_services">Metro services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guangzhou_Railway_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Metro services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Interchange_station" title="Interchange station">interchange</a> <a href="/wiki/Metro_station" title="Metro station">station</a> of Line 2 and Line 5 of the <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Metro" title="Guangzhou Metro">Guangzhou Metro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is located at the underground of the west square of Guangzhou Railway Station, located at the junction of Huanshi Road West (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">环市西路</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Huanshi Xilu</span></i> and Renmin Road North (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">人民北路</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Renmin Beilu</span></i>) in <a href="/wiki/Yuexiu_District" title="Yuexiu District">Yuexiu District</a>. It started operations on December 29, 2002, (Line 2) and December 2009 (Line 5) respectively. Its area is about 10000 square metres and it is the second largest metro station in Guangzhou.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
