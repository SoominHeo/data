<p>The <b>bullhead sharks</b> are a small <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">order</a> (<b>Heterodontiformes</b>) of <a href="/wiki/Basal_(phylogenetics)" title="Basal (phylogenetics)">basal</a> modern <a href="/wiki/Shark" title="Shark">sharks</a> (<a href="/wiki/Neoselachii" title="Neoselachii">Neoselachii</a>). The nine living species are placed in a single genus, <i><b>Heterodontus</b></i>, in the family <b>Heterodontidae</b>. All are relatively small, with the largest species being just 150 cm (59 in) in adult length. They are bottom feeders in tropical and subtropical waters.</p>
<p>The Heterodontiforms appear in the fossil record in the Early <a href="/wiki/Jurassic" title="Jurassic">Jurassic</a>, well before any of the other <a href="/wiki/Galeomorphii" title="Galeomorphii">Galeomorphii</a>, a group that includes all modern sharks except the <a href="/wiki/Squalidae" title="Squalidae">dogfish</a> and its relatives. However, they have never been common, and their origin probably lies even further back.</p>

<p>The bullhead sharks are morphologically rather distinctive. The mouth is located entirely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anterior" title="Anterior">anterior</a> to the orbits. Labial cartilages are found in the most anterior part of the mouth. Nasoral grooves are present, connecting the external nares to the mouth. The nasal capsules are trumpet-shaped and well-separated from orbits. Circumnarial <a href="/wiki/Skin_fold" title="Skin fold">skin folds</a> are present, but the rostral process of the <a href="/wiki/Neurocranium" title="Neurocranium">neurocranium</a> (braincase) is absent, although a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Precerebral_fossa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Precerebral fossa (page does not exist)">precerebral fossa</a> is present. Finally, the braincase bears a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Supraorbital_crest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Supraorbital crest (page does not exist)">supraorbital crest</a>.</p>
<p>The eyes lack a <a href="/wiki/Nictitating_membrane" title="Nictitating membrane">nictitating membrane</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Spiracle" title="Spiracle">spiracle</a> is present, but small. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dorsum_(biology)" title="Dorsum (biology)">dorsal</a> ends of the fourth and fifth branchial arches are attached, but not fused into a "pickaxe" as in <a href="/wiki/Lamniformes" title="Lamniformes">lamniform sharks</a>. Heterodontiforms have two <a href="/wiki/Dorsal_fin" title="Dorsal fin">dorsal fins</a>, with fin spines, as well as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anal_fin" title="Anal fin">anal fin</a>. The dorsal and anal fins also contain basal <a href="/wiki/Cartilage" title="Cartilage">cartilages</a>, not just fin rays.</p>
<p>Bullhead Sharks have distinctive small spikes on the front of their dorsal fins. These are rumoured to be poisonous, but no further scientific tests have been done to prove this rumor true or false.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Species">Species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bullhead_shark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nine living species of bullhead shark are described, with another potential undescribed species in Baja California:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Horn_shark" title="Horn shark">Heterodontus francisci</a></i> <small>(<a href="/wiki/Charles_Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Girard" title="Charles Frédéric Girard">Girard</a>, 1855)</small> (horn shark)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Crested_bullhead_shark" title="Crested bullhead shark">Heterodontus galeatus</a></i> <small>(<a href="/wiki/Albert_G%C3%BCnther" title="Albert Günther">Günther</a>, 1870)</small> (crested bullhead shark)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Japanese_bullhead_shark" title="Japanese bullhead shark">Heterodontus japonicus</a></i> <small>(<a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Miklouho-Maclay" title="Nicholas Miklouho-Maclay">Maclay</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/William_John_Macleay" title="William John Macleay">W. J. Macleay</a>, 1884)</small> (Japanese bullhead shark)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Mexican_hornshark" title="Mexican hornshark">Heterodontus mexicanus</a></i> <small>(<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Leighton_R._Taylor_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Leighton R. Taylor Jr. (page does not exist)">L. R. Taylor</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Castro-Aguirre" title="José Luis Castro-Aguirre">Castro-Aguirre</a>, 1972)</small> (Mexican hornshark)</li>
