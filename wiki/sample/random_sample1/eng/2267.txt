<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Huai of Xia</h1>

<p><b>Huai</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">槐</span>; read <b>Hui</b> according to <a href="/wiki/Sima_Zhen" title="Sima Zhen">Sima Zhen</a>) was the eighth king of the semi-legendary <a href="/wiki/Xia_dynasty" title="Xia dynasty">Xia dynasty</a> of ancient China, who possibly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_monarchs" title="List of Chinese monarchs">ruled 44 years</a>. His other name is <b>Fen</b> (芬).</p>
<p>Huai got his throne in the year of <i>Wuzi</i> (<i>戊子</i>), after his father <a href="/wiki/Zhu_of_Xia" title="Zhu of Xia">Zhu</a> had died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 3rd year of his reign, nine <a href="/wiki/Barbarian" title="Barbarian">barbarians</a> came to his capital.</p>
<p>In the 16th year of his <a href="/wiki/Regime" title="Regime">regime</a>, the minister Luobo (洛伯) fought with minister Fengyi (冯夷) at He. In the 33rd year of his regime, he assigned the son of Kunwu as minister in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yousu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yousu (page does not exist)">Yousu</a> (有苏).</p>
<p>He created the poem and music of Huantu in the 36th year of his reign.</p>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Records_of_the_Grand_Historian" title="Records of the Grand Historian">Records of the Grand Historian</a></i>, he ruled 26 years,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but 44 years according to the <i><a href="/wiki/Bamboo_Annals" title="Bamboo Annals">Bamboo Annals</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Mang_of_Xia" title="Mang of Xia">Mang</a>.</p>
