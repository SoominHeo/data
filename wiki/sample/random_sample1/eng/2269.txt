<p><b>Göreme</b> (<small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Turkic_dialects" title="Help:IPA for Turkic dialects">[ˈɟøɾeme]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>: <i>Κόραμα</i>, <b>Kòrama</b>), located among the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fairy_chimney" title="Fairy chimney">fairy chimney</a>" <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_formation" title="Rock formation">rock formations</a>, is a town in <a href="/wiki/Cappadocia" title="Cappadocia">Cappadocia</a>, a historical region of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. It is in the <a href="/wiki/Nev%C5%9Fehir_Province" title="Nevşehir Province">Nevşehir Province</a> in Central <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a> and has a population of around 2,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Former names of the town have been Korama, Matiana, Maccan or Machan, and Avcilar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> When Göreme Valley nearby was designated an important tourist destination, a "center" for all tourism in Cappadocia, the name of the town was changed to Göreme for practical reasons.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%C3%B6reme_National_Park" title="Göreme National Park">Göreme National Park</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://tr.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6reme_Tarih%C3%AE_Mill%C3%AE_Park%C4%B1" title="tr:Göreme Tarihî Millî Parkı">tr</a>)</span> (Göreme Tarihî Millî Parkı in Turkish) was added to the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Heritage_List" title="World Heritage List">World Heritage List</a> in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The time that the Göreme was first settled is unclear, but it could date back as the <a href="/wiki/Hittites" title="Hittites">Hittite era</a>, between 1800 and 1200 B.C. The location was central between rivaling empires, such as the Greeks and Persians, leading the natives to tunnel into the rock to escape the political turmoil. During the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman era</a>, the area became home to <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christians</a> retreating from Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Christianity prevailed as the primary religion in the region, which is evident from many rock churches that can still be seen today.</p>
<p>Among Göreme's historically important sites are Ortahane, Durmus Kadir, Yusuf Koc and Bezirhane churches, in addition to the richly decorated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokali_Kilise" title="Tokali Kilise">Tokali Kilise</a>, the Apple Church, and a number of homes and pigeon houses carved straight into the rock formations in the town.</p>
