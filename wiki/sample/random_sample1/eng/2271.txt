<p><i><b>Kaijū</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">怪獣</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>kaijū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (from Japanese "strange beast")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a Japanese film genre that features <a href="/wiki/Monster" title="Monster">monsters</a>, usually attacking major cities and engaging the military and other monsters in battle. It is a subgenre of <i><a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a></i> (special effects-based) entertainment.</p>
<p>Related terms include <i>kaijū eiga</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">怪獣映画</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>kaijū eiga</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Monster_movie" title="Monster movie">monster movie</a>)</span>, a <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> featuring giant monsters or a single monster; <i>kaijin</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">怪人</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, referring to roughly <a href="/wiki/Humanoid" title="Humanoid">humanoid</a> monsters)</span>; and <i>daikaiju</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">大怪獣</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>daikaijū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, giant <i>kaiju</i>)</span>, specifically meaning the larger variety of monsters.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Godzilla" title="Godzilla">Godzilla</a> is an example of a <i>daikaiju</i>; others include <a href="/wiki/Mothra" title="Mothra">Mothra</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_Ghidorah" title="King Ghidorah">King Ghidorah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechagodzilla" title="Mechagodzilla">Mechagodzilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rodan" title="Rodan">Rodan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gamera" title="Gamera">Gamera</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyaos" title="Gyaos">Gyaos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daimajin" title="Daimajin">Daimajin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gappa" title="Gappa">Gappa</a>. The term <i>ultra-kaiju</i> is longhand for <i>kaiju</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Ultra_Series" title="Ultra Series">Ultra Series</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Toho" title="Toho">Toho</a> has produced a variety of <i>kaiju</i> films over the years (many of which featured Godzilla and Mothra) but other Japanese studios contributed to expanding the genre in Japan by producing films and shows of their own, including <a href="/wiki/Daiei_Film" title="Daiei Film">Daiei Film Co., Ltd.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Pictures" title="Kadokawa Pictures">Kadokawa Pictures</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tsuburaya_Productions" title="Tsuburaya Productions">Tsuburaya Productions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shochiku" title="Shochiku">Shochiku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nikkatsu" title="Nikkatsu">Nikkatsu</a> studios.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">Concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaiju&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaiju are typically modeled after conventional animals, <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mythological_creature" title="Mythological creature">mythological creatures</a>; however, there are more exotic examples.</p>
<p>Monsters famous in English stories, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vampires" title="Vampires">vampires</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Werewolves" title="Werewolves">werewolves</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mummies" title="Mummies">mummies</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zombie_(fictional)" title="Zombie (fictional)">zombies</a>, fall into this category. <a href="/wiki/Frankenstein%27s_monster" title="Frankenstein's monster">Frankenstein's monster</a> was a starring kaiju in the <a href="/wiki/Toho" title="Toho">Toho</a> films <i><a href="/wiki/Frankenstein_Conquers_the_World" title="Frankenstein Conquers the World">Frankenstein Conquers the World</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_War_of_the_Gargantuas" title="The War of the Gargantuas">The War of the Gargantuas</a></i>.</p>
<p>Kaiju are sometimes depicted as <a href="/wiki/Cannon_fodder" title="Cannon fodder">cannon fodder</a> serving a greater evil. Some kaiju are elite warriors which serve as the right-hand man to the greater villain and are destroyed by the heroic forces. Others have a neutral alignment, only seeking to destroy buildings and other structures. During the early eras of <i>tokusatsu</i>, "heroic" monsters were rarely seen in <i>daikaiju eiga</i> films, and it was not until later when television <i>tokusatsu</i> productions began using <i>kaiju</i> which aided the hero, saved civilians, or demonstrated some kind of complex personality. These kaiju adopted many classic monster traits, appearing as the "Misunderstood Creature". Some kaiju hung out with the heroes and provided comedy relief, in contrast to the darker approach to these characters from more mature franchises. Godzilla, arguably the most well known of the <i>daikaiju</i>, has played the roles of hero, villain, and force of nature in the course of his existence, one of the few kaiju of any type to be depicted in multiple roles and having those around him react in different ways, depending on how the creature itself was being presented in the films.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References_in_culture">References in culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaiju&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: References in culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In the Japanese language original of <i><a href="/wiki/Cardcaptor_Sakura" title="Cardcaptor Sakura">Cardcaptor Sakura</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Sakura_Kinomoto" title="Sakura Kinomoto">Sakura's</a> brother <a href="/wiki/List_of_Cardcaptor_Sakura_characters#Toya_Knimoto" title="List of Cardcaptor Sakura characters">Toya</a> likes to tease her by regularly calling her "kaiju", relating to her noisily coming down from her room for breakfast every morning.</li>
<li>In the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)">Star Wars: The Clone Wars</a></i>, there is a story arc composed of two episodes entitled "The Zillo Beast" and "The Zillo Beast Strikes Back", mostly influenced by Godzilla films, in which a huge reptilian beast is transported from its homeworld Malastare to the city-covered planet Coruscant, where it breaks loose and goes on a rampage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Jedi" title="Return of the Jedi">Return of the Jedi</a></i>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rancor" title="Rancor">rancor</a> was originally to be played by an actor in a suit similar to the way how kaiju films like Godzilla were made. However, the rancor was eventually portrayed by a puppet filmed in high speed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>In <i>The Simpsons: <a href="/wiki/Treehouse_of_Horror_VI" title="Treehouse of Horror VI">Treehouse of Horror VI</a> - Attack of the 50-Foot Eyesores,</i> Homer goes to Lard Lad Donuts; unable to get a "Colossal Doughnut" as advertised, he steals Lard Lad's Donut, awakening other giant advertising statues that come to life to terrorize Springfield. When Lard Lad awakes, he makes a Godzilla roar. <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_del_Toro" title="Guillermo del Toro">Guillermo del Toro</a> directed the <i><a href="/wiki/Treehouse_of_Horror_XXIV" title="Treehouse of Horror XXIV">Treehouse of Horror XXIV</a></i> couch gag which made multiple references to <a href="/wiki/Godzilla" title="Godzilla">Godzilla</a> and other Kaiju-based characters, including his own <i>Pacific Rim</i> characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Crank:_High_Voltage" title="Crank: High Voltage">Crank: High Voltage</a></i>, there is a sequence parodying Kaiju films using the same practical effects techniques used for Tokusatsu films such as miniatures and suitmation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the 2013 film <i><a href="/wiki/Pacific_Rim_(film)" title="Pacific Rim (film)">Pacific Rim</a></i>, "Kaiju" is the moniker bestowed upon giant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inter-dimensional" title="Inter-dimensional">inter-dimensional</a> monsters that <a href="/wiki/Alien_invasion" title="Alien invasion">invade Earth</a> and attempt to <a href="/wiki/Human_extinction" title="Human extinction">exterminate humanity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>Kaiju-Bird Monster was the alt-mode of Decepticon Leader Emperor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Deathsaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Deathsaurus (page does not exist)">Deathsaurus</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Transformers:_Victory" title="Transformers: Victory">Transformers: Victory</a></i> anime.</li>
<li>A series of cards in the <a href="/wiki/Yu-Gi-Oh!_Trading_Card_Game" title="Yu-Gi-Oh! Trading Card Game">Yu-Gi-Oh! Trading Card Game</a> called "Kaiju" are inspired, both in name and/or visually, by multiple kaiju from <i>Godzilla</i>-related films, including <a href="/wiki/Mothra" title="Mothra">Mothra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gamera" title="Gamera">Gamera</a>, and direct monsters of (previous) said series - <a href="/wiki/Gigan" title="Gigan">Gigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kumonga" title="Kumonga">Kumonga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rodan" title="Rodan">Rodan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MechaGodzilla" title="MechaGodzilla">MechaGodzilla</a>, and <a href="/wiki/King_Ghidorah" title="King Ghidorah">King Ghidorah</a>.</li>
</ul>
