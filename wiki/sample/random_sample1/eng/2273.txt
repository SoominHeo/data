<p>In <a href="/wiki/Algebraic_topology" title="Algebraic topology">algebraic topology</a> the <b>cap product</b> is a method of adjoining a <a href="/wiki/Chain_(algebraic_topology)" title="Chain (algebraic topology)">chain</a> of degree <i>p</i> with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cochain" title="Cochain">cochain</a> of degree <i>q</i>, such that <i>q</i> ≤ <i>p</i>, to form a composite chain of degree <i>p</i> − <i>q</i>. It was introduced by <a href="/wiki/Eduard_%C4%8Cech" title="Eduard Čech">Eduard Čech</a> in 1936, and independently by <a href="/wiki/Hassler_Whitney" title="Hassler Whitney">Hassler Whitney</a> in 1938.</p>
