<p><b>Kyōtamba</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">京丹波町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kyōtanba-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Funai_District,_Kyoto" title="Funai District, Kyoto">Funai District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Prefecture" title="Kyoto Prefecture">Kyoto Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Its name comes from the first syllable of <i>Kyōto</i> and the former town of <i>Tamba</i>, a namesake of the historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tamba_Province" title="Tamba Province">Tamba Province</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2008, the town has an estimated population of 17,312. The total area is 303.07 km².</p>
<p>The town was founded on October 11, 2005 by the merger of the former towns of <a href="/wiki/Tanba,_Kyoto" title="Tanba, Kyoto">Tanba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mizuho,_Kyoto" title="Mizuho, Kyoto">Mizuho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wachi,_Kyoto" title="Wachi, Kyoto">Wachi</a>, all from <a href="/wiki/Funai_District,_Kyoto" title="Funai District, Kyoto">Funai District</a>.</p>
