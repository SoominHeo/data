<p><b>Federal elections</b> were held in <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> on 15 September 1957. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Union_(Germany)" title="Christian Democratic Union (Germany)">Christian Democratic Union</a> and its <a href="/wiki/CDU/CSU" title="CDU/CSU">longtime ally</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Social_Union_in_Bavaria" title="Christian Social Union in Bavaria">Christian Social Union in Bavaria</a>, won a sweeping victory, taking 277 seats in the <a href="/wiki/Bundestag" title="Bundestag">Bundestag</a> to win the first--and to date, only--absolute majority for a single German parliamentary group in a free election.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Issues_and_Campaign">Issues and Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_German_federal_election,_1957&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Issues and Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Federal Chancellor Adenauer had some solid advantages over his Social Democratic opponent, <a href="/wiki/Erich_Ollenhauer" title="Erich Ollenhauer">Erich Ollenhauer</a>: West Germany had become fully sovereign in 1955, it had joined the <a href="/wiki/European_Economic_Community" title="European Economic Community">European Economic Community</a> in March 1957, its economy grew steadily with a very low unemployment, and most West Germans felt clearly more prosperous and more secure than in 1949 or 1953. Although the West German economic growth was more directly enhanced by the <a href="/wiki/Social_market_economy" title="Social market economy">social market economy</a> policies of Economics Minister <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_Erhard" title="Ludwig Erhard">Ludwig Erhard</a>, most non-socialistically inclined West German voters gave Adenauer the credit for it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The All-German Bloc lost all of their seats.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_German_federal_election,_1957&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Konrad_Adenauer" title="Konrad Adenauer">Konrad Adenauer</a> led the CDU-CSU coalition to a landslide victory. The CDU-CSU won an outright majority—to date, the only time a German party has been elected to a majority government in a free election (the CDU and CSU sit as a single bloc in the Bundestag).</p>
