<p><b>Pope Leo XI</b> (2 June 1535 – 27 April 1605), born <b>Alessandro Ottaviano de' Medici</b>, was <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> from 1 to 27 April 1605. His pontificate is one of the briefest in history having lasted under a month. He was from the prominent <a href="/wiki/House_of_Medici" title="House of Medici">House of Medici</a> originating from <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Leo_XI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Leo_XI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alessandro Ottoviano de' Medici was born in <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-catholic_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-catholic-2">[2]</a></sup> as the son of Francesca Salviati and <a href="/wiki/Ottaviano_de%27_Medici" title="Ottaviano de' Medici">Ottaviano</a>. He was the great-nephew of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Leo_X" title="Pope Leo X">Pope Leo X</a>. Alessandro's father died when he was a child and he was home schooled by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_Preachers" title="Order of Preachers">Dominican</a> priest, Vincenzo Ercolano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Medici felt the call to the priesthood but his mother opposed his vocation since he was the only male in the family. To discourage this, she sent him to the court of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duke of Tuscany">Grand Duke of Tuscany</a>, who knighted him as a knight of San Stefano. He later travelled to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> in 1560 where he commenced a lifelong friendship and collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Philip_Neri" title="Philip Neri">Philip Neri</a>, future saint. It was Neri who predicted that he would ascend to the pontificate. Medici's mother died in 1566 at which point he continued his studies to become a priest. This led to his ordination on 22 July 1567.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Priesthood">Priesthood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Leo_XI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Priesthood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:127px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alessandro_Algardi,_monumento_a_leone_XI_Medici,_1644,_01.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3648" data-file-width="2502" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Alessandro_Algardi%2C_monumento_a_leone_XI_Medici%2C_1644%2C_01.JPG/125px-Alessandro_Algardi%2C_monumento_a_leone_XI_Medici%2C_1644%2C_01.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Alessandro_Algardi%2C_monumento_a_leone_XI_Medici%2C_1644%2C_01.JPG/188px-Alessandro_Algardi%2C_monumento_a_leone_XI_Medici%2C_1644%2C_01.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Alessandro_Algardi%2C_monumento_a_leone_XI_Medici%2C_1644%2C_01.JPG/250px-Alessandro_Algardi%2C_monumento_a_leone_XI_Medici%2C_1644%2C_01.JPG 2x" width="125"/></a>

<p>Alessandro served as the Florentine <a href="/wiki/Ambassador" title="Ambassador">ambassador</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_V" title="Pope Pius V">Pope Pius V</a> from 1569 to 1584 and was later appointed by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_XIII" title="Pope Gregory XIII">Pope Gregory XIII</a> as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Pistoia" title="Bishop of Pistoia">Bishop of Pistoia</a> in 1573. In March 1573 after the appointment he received episcopal consecration in Rome. He was later made the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Florence" title="Archbishop of Florence">Archbishop of Florence</a> in 1574.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Medici was elevated into the cardinalate in 1583 and Pope Sixtus V made the <a href="/wiki/Santi_Quirico_e_Giulitta" title="Santi Quirico e Giulitta">Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quirico e Giulitta</a>: a title he received on 9 January 1584. It was a titular church reverted from its previous name of <a href="/wiki/San_Ciriaco_alle_Terme_Diocleziane" title="San Ciriaco alle Terme Diocleziane">San Ciriaco alle Terme Diocleziane</a>. In the period after this, he would opt for other titular churches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-catholic_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-catholic-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1596 <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_VIII" title="Pope Clement VIII">Pope Clement VIII</a> sent him as the papal legate to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maria_de%27_Medici" title="Maria de' Medici">Maria de' Medici</a> was queen. He remained there until 1598 when he received word of his appointment as the Prefect of the Congregation of Bishops and Regulars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pontificate">Pontificate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Leo_XI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pontificate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Papal_election">Papal election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Leo_XI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Papal election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Papal_conclave,_March_1605" title="Papal conclave, March 1605">Papal conclave, March 1605</a></div>
<p>On 14 March 1605, eleven days after the death of Clement VIII, 62 cardinals entered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papal_election" title="Papal election">conclave</a>. Prominent among the candidates for the papacy were the great <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">historian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cesare_Baronius" title="Cesare Baronius">Cesare Baronius</a> and the famous <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Jesus" title="Society of Jesus">Jesuit</a> controversialist <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bellarmine" title="Robert Bellarmine">Robert Bellarmine</a>, future saint.</p>
<p>But <a href="/wiki/Pietro_Aldobrandini" title="Pietro Aldobrandini">Pietro Aldobrandini</a>, the leader of the Italian party among the cardinals, allied with the French cardinals and brought about the election of Alessandro against the express wish of King <a href="/wiki/Philip_III_of_Spain" title="Philip III of Spain">Philip III of Spain</a>. King <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_France" title="Henry IV of France">Henry IV of France</a> is said to have spent 300,000 <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cu" title="Écu">écus</a> in the promotion of Alessandro's candidacy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 April 1605, Cardinal Alessandro de' Medici was elected as pope. He chose to be called Leo XI in honor of his uncle <a href="/wiki/Pope_Leo_X" title="Pope Leo X">Pope Leo X</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-catholic_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-catholic-2">[2]</a></sup> He was crowned on 10 April 1605 by the protodeacon, Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Sforza_(cardinal)" title="Francesco Sforza (cardinal)">Francesco Sforza</a> and he took possession of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basilica_of_Saint_John_Lateran" title="Basilica of Saint John Lateran">Basilica of Saint John Lateran</a> on 17 April 1605.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Leo_XI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When he was elected, Leo XI was almost seventy years of age, and he died twenty seven days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> His death came as a result of fatigue and cold in the ceremony of taking possession of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lateran_Basilica" title="Lateran Basilica">Basilica of St John Lateran</a>. He was called <i>Papa Lampo</i> ("Lightning Pope") because his papacy was so short.</p>
