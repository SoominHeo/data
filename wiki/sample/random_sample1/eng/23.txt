<p><b>Overflow</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">オーバーフロー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōbāfurō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (stylized <b>0verflow</b>) is a Japanese video game division of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stack_Ltd.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stack Ltd. (page does not exist)">Stack Ltd.</a> specializing in the production of mature <a href="/wiki/Interactive_fiction" title="Interactive fiction">interactive fiction</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annlast_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-annlast-2">[2]</a></sup> The Overflow label is known for its game franchise <i><a href="/wiki/School_Days_(visual_novel)" title="School Days (visual novel)">School Days</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annlast_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-annlast-2">[2]</a></sup> The company headquarters are located in the Sugishō Building <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">杉商ビル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sugishō Biru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in <a href="/wiki/Kanda,_Tokyo" title="Kanda, Tokyo">Kanda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiyoda,_Tokyo" title="Chiyoda, Tokyo">Chiyoda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stackabout_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-stackabout-1">[1]</a></sup> On February 10, 2012, Overflow confirmed that <i>Shiny Days</i>, a remake of <i>Summer Days</i>, will be the last title produced by the brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annlast_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-annlast-2">[2]</a></sup> The team also reassured customers that it was not going bankrupt and would continue to support their line of video games even after the release of <i>Shiny Days</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annlast_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-annlast-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Overflow's <i>School Days</i> video game was adapted into a 12-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annlast_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-annlast-2">[2]</a></sup> Both <i>School Days</i> and <i>Cross Days</i> have also received <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptations.</p>
