<p><b>Pope Lucius III</b> (c. 1100 ‚Äì 25 November 1185), born <b>Ubaldo Allucingoli</b>, reigned from 1 September 1181 to his death in 1185.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ce_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ce-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Lucius_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A native of the independent republic of <a href="/wiki/Lucca" title="Lucca">Lucca</a>, he was born c. 1100 (perhaps 1097) as Ubaldo, son of Orlando. He is commonly referred to as a member of the aristocratic family of <b>Allucingoli</b>, but this is not proven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He had close ties to the <a href="/wiki/Cistercians" title="Cistercians">Cistercians</a>, but it seems that he never joined the order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was named cardinal by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Innocent_II" title="Pope Innocent II">Pope Innocent II</a> in December 1138, initially as cardinal-deacon of San Adriano, then (in May 1141) as cardinal-priest of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Prassede" title="Santa Prassede">Santa Prassede</a>. <a href="/wiki/Pope_Adrian_IV" title="Pope Adrian IV">Pope Adrian IV</a> promoted him to the rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_Bishop_of_Ostia_and_Velletri" title="Cardinal Bishop of Ostia and Velletri">Cardinal Bishop of Ostia and Velletri</a> in December 1158. He was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dean_of_the_Sacred_College_of_Cardinals" title="Dean of the Sacred College of Cardinals">dean of the Sacred College of Cardinals</a> and one of the most influential cardinals under his predecessor <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_III" title="Pope Alexander III">Pope Alexander III</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Election_and_papacy">Election and papacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Lucius_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Election and papacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Papal_election,_1181" title="Papal election, 1181">Papal election, 1181</a></div>
<p>After being <a href="/wiki/Papal_election,_1181" title="Papal election, 1181">elected Pope</a> in 1181, he lived at <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> from November 1181 to March 1182, but dissensions in the city compelled him to pass the remainder of his pontificate in exile, mainly at <a href="/wiki/Velletri" title="Velletri">Velletri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anagni" title="Anagni">Anagni</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verona,_Italy" title="Verona, Italy">Verona</a>.</p>
<p>He disputed with the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Frederick_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick I, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick I</a> over the disposal of the territories of the late Countess <a href="/wiki/Matilda_of_Tuscany" title="Matilda of Tuscany">Matilda of Tuscany</a>. The controversy over the succession to the inheritance of the Countess had been left unsettled by an agreement of 1177, and the Emperor proposed in 1182 that the <a href="/wiki/Curia" title="Curia">Curia</a> should renounce its claim, receiving in exchange two-tenths of the imperial income from Italy, one-tenth for the Pope and the other tenth for the cardinals. Lucius consented neither to this proposition nor to another compromise suggested by Frederick I the next year, nor did a personal discussion between the two potentates at Verona in October 1184 lead to any definite result.</p>
<p>During the conflict between Frederick I and the papacy, the problem of heresy required a political solution. In 1184, Pope Lucius III decreed <i><a href="/wiki/Ad_abolendam" title="Ad abolendam">Ad abolendam</a></i> that all "counts, barons, rectors, [and] consuls of cities and other places" who did not join in the struggle against heresy when called upon to do so would be excommunicated and their territories placed under interdict ‚Äì and declared that these provisions joined the apostolic authority of the church with the sanction of imperial power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the meantime other causes of disagreement appeared when the Pope refused to comply with Frederick I's wishes as to the regulation of German episcopal elections which had taken place during the schism, especially as regards an election to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/See_of_Trier" title="See of Trier">See of Trier</a> in 1183 contested between the papal candidate <a href="/wiki/Folmar_of_Karden" title="Folmar of Karden">Folmar of Karden</a> and the imperial candidate Rudolf of Wied.</p>
<p>In pursuance of his anti-imperial policy, Lucius declined in 1185 to crown <a href="/wiki/Henry_VI,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Henry VI, Holy Roman Emperor">Henry of Hohenstaufen</a> as Frederick I's <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Romans" title="King of the Romans">destined successor</a>, and the breach between the Empire and the Curia became wider on questions of Italian politics.</p>
<p>In November 1184 Lucius held a synod at Verona which condemned the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cathars" title="Cathars">Cathars</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paterines" title="Paterines">Paterines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waldensians" title="Waldensians">Waldensians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arnoldists" title="Arnoldists">Arnoldists</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anathema" title="Anathema">anathematized</a> all those declared as <a href="/wiki/Heresy" title="Heresy">heretics</a> and their abettors. Contrary to what is often said, he did not institute the <a href="/wiki/Inquisition" title="Inquisition">Inquisition</a>, which was not created until the reign of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_IX" title="Pope Gregory IX">Pope Gregory IX</a> in 1234.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Lucius_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1185 preparations began for the <a href="/wiki/Third_Crusade" title="Third Crusade">Third Crusade</a> in answer to the appeals of King <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_IV_of_Jerusalem" title="Baldwin IV of Jerusalem">Baldwin IV of Jerusalem</a>. Before they were completed, Lucius III died in Verona.</p>
