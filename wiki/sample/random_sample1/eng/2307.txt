<p><b>Pope Sixtus III</b> (died 18 August 440) was <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> from 31 July 432 to his death in 440.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ce-sixtus_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ce-sixtus-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Sixtus_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name of Sixtus is often connected with a great building boom in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>: <a href="/wiki/Santa_Sabina" title="Santa Sabina">Santa Sabina</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Aventine_Hill" title="Aventine Hill">Aventine Hill</a> was dedicated during his pontificate and he built <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Maria_Maggiore" title="Santa Maria Maggiore">Santa Maria Maggiore</a>, whose dedication to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mary,_the_mother_of_Jesus" title="Mary, the mother of Jesus">Mary</a> the Mother of God reflected his acceptance of the <a href="/wiki/Ecumenical_council" title="Ecumenical council">Ecumenical council</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_Council_of_Ephesus" title="First Council of Ephesus">Ephesus</a> which closed in 431. At that council, the debate over Christ's human and divine natures turned on whether Mary could legitimately be called the "Mother of God" or only "Mother of Christ". The council gave her the Greek title <i><a href="/wiki/Theotokos" title="Theotokos">Theotokos</a></i> (literally "God-bearer", or "Mother of God"), and the dedication of the large church in Rome is a response to that.</p>
<p>Prior to being made Pope, Sixtus was a patron of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pelagius_(British_monk)" title="Pelagius (British monk)">Pelagius</a>, who was later condemned as a heretic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of his main concerns was in restoring peace between <a href="/wiki/Cyril_of_Alexandria" title="Cyril of Alexandria">Cyril of Alexandria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrians</a>.</p>
<p>He also maintained the rights of the Pope over <a href="/wiki/Illyria" title="Illyria">Illyria</a> and the position of the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">archbishop</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thessalonica" title="Thessalonica">Thessalonica</a> as head of the local Illyrian church.</p>
<p>His feast is kept on 28 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ce-sixtus_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ce-sixtus-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
