<p><b>Pope John IX</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Ioannes IX</i></span>; died January 900) was <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> from January 898 to his death in 900.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_John_IX&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Little is known about John IX before he became Pope. Born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tivoli,_Italy" title="Tivoli, Italy">Tivoli</a> in an unknown year, he was ordained as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_St._Benedict" title="Order of St. Benedict">Benedictine priest</a> by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Formosus" title="Pope Formosus">Pope Formosus</a>. With the support of the powerful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Dukes_of_Spoleto" title="List of Dukes of Spoleto">House of Spoleto</a> he was elected Pontiff in early 898 following the sudden death of Pope <a href="/wiki/Pope_Theodore_II" title="Pope Theodore II">Theodore II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CE_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Pope">As Pope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_John_IX&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: As Pope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>With a view to diminish the violence of faction in Rome, John held several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synods" title="Synods">synods</a> in Rome and elsewhere in 898. They not only confirmed the judgment of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Theodore_II" title="Pope Theodore II">Pope Theodore II</a> in granting <a href="/wiki/Christian_burial" title="Christian burial">Christian burial</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pope_Formosus" title="Pope Formosus">Pope Formosus</a>, but also at a council held at <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a> decreed that the records of the synod held by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Stephen_VI" title="Pope Stephen VI">Pope Stephen VI</a> which had condemned him should be burned. Re-ordinations were forbidden, and those of the clergy who had been degraded by Stephen were restored to the ranks from which he had deposed them.</p>
<p>To keep their independence, which was threatened by the Germans, the Slavs of Moravia appealed to John to let them have a hierarchy of their own. Ignoring the complaints of the German hierarchy, John sanctioned the consecration of a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_bishop" title="Metropolitan bishop">metropolitan</a> and three bishops for the Church of the Moravians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CE_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-CE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finding that it was advisable to cement the ties between the empire and the papacy, John IX gave unhesitating support to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lambert_of_Spoleto" title="Lambert of Spoleto">Lambert</a> in preference to <a href="/wiki/Arnulf_of_Carinthia" title="Arnulf of Carinthia">Arnulf</a> during the Synod of Rome, and also induced the council to determine that henceforth the consecration of the Popes should take place only in the presence of the imperial legates. The sudden death of Lambert shattered the hopes which this alliance seemed to promise.</p>
<p>John IX died in the year 900 and was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IV" title="Pope Benedict IV">Pope Benedict IV</a> (900‚Äì903).</p>
