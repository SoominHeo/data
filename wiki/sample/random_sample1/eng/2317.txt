<p><b>Pope Clement III</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Clemens III</i></span>; 1130 – 20 March 1191), born <b>Paulino</b> (or <b>Paolo</b>) <b>Scolari</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> reigned from 19 December 1187 to his death in 1191.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardinal">Cardinal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Clement_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cardinal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Roman by birth, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_III" title="Pope Alexander III">Pope Alexander III</a> appointed him in succession Archpriest of the patriarchal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liberian_Basilica" title="Liberian Basilica">Liberian Basilica</a>, Cardinal-deacon of Sergio e Bacco, and finally <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_bishop_of_Palestrina" title="Cardinal bishop of Palestrina">Cardinal bishop of Palestrina</a> in December 1180. He appears as signatory of the <a href="/wiki/Papal_bull" title="Papal bull">papal bulls</a> issued between 15 October 1179 and 11 December 1187.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pope">Pope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pope_Clement_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly after his accession at the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Papal_election,_December_1187" title="Papal election, December 1187">papal election</a> of December 1187, Clement succeeded in allaying the conflict which had existed for half a century between the Popes and the citizens of Rome, with an agreement by which the citizens were allowed to elect their <a href="/wiki/Magistrate" title="Magistrate">magistrates</a>, while the nomination of the governor of the city remained in the hands of the Pope. On 31 May 1188 he concluded a treaty with the Romans which removed long standing difficulties, thus returning the Papacy to Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERockwell1911_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERockwell1911-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Clement also inherited a depleted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/College_of_cardinals" title="College of cardinals">college of cardinals</a>, consisting of no more than twenty cardinals. He orchestrated three series of promotions (March 1188, May 1189 and October 1190) that resulted in over thirty new cardinals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>He pushed King <a href="/wiki/Henry_II_of_England" title="Henry II of England">Henry II of England</a> and King <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_France" title="Philip II of France">Philip II of France</a> to undertake the <a href="/wiki/Third_Crusade" title="Third Crusade">Third Crusade</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In April 1189, Clement made peace with the Emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_I_Barbarossa" title="Frederick I Barbarossa">Frederick I Barbarossa</a>.</p>
<p>He settled a controversy with King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_I_of_Scotland" title="William I of Scotland">William I of Scotland</a> concerning the choice of the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">archbishop</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Andrews" title="St. Andrews">St. Andrews</a>, and on 13 March 1188 removed the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scotland" title="Church of Scotland">Scottish church</a> from the legatine jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_York" title="Archbishop of York">Archbishop of York</a>, thus making it independent of all save Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERockwell1911_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERockwell1911-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In spite of agreeing to crown <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Henry_VI,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Henry VI, Holy Roman Emperor">Henry VI</a>, Clement III angered him by bestowing <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a> on <a href="/wiki/Tancred,_King_of_Sicily" title="Tancred, King of Sicily">Tancred</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Roger_III,_Duke_of_Apulia" title="Roger III, Duke of Apulia">Roger III, Duke of Apulia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The crisis was acute when the Pope died in the latter part of March 1191.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERockwell1911_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERockwell1911-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
