<p>A <b>papal legate</b> or <b>Apostolic legate</b> (from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Roman</a> title <i><a href="/wiki/Legatus" title="Legatus">legatus</a></i>) is a personal representative of the <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a> to foreign nations, or to some part of the Catholic Church. He is empowered on matters of <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" title="Catholicism">Catholic Faith</a> and for the settlement of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical" title="Ecclesiastical">ecclesiastical</a> matters.</p>
<p>The legate is appointed directly by the pope (the bishop of Rome, head of the Catholic Church and head of state of the papal states). Hence a legate is usually sent to a government, a sovereign or to a large body of believers (such as a national church) or to take charge of a major religious effort, such as an (ecumenical) council, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusade" title="Crusade">crusade</a> to the Holy Land, or even against a heresy such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cathars" title="Cathars">Cathars</a>.</p>
<p>The term <i>legation</i> is applied both to a legate's mandate and to the territory concerned (such as a state, or an ecclesiastical province). The relevant adjective is <i>legatine</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/High_Middle_Ages" title="High Middle Ages">High Middle Ages</a>, papal legates were often used to strengthen the links between Rome and the many parts of <a href="/wiki/Christendom" title="Christendom">Christendom</a>. More often than not, legates were learned men and skilled diplomats who were not from the country they were accredited to. The Italian-born <a href="/wiki/Guala_Bicchieri" title="Guala Bicchieri">Guala Bicchieri</a> served as papal legate to England in the early 13th century and played a major role in both the English government and church at the time. By the <a href="/wiki/Late_Middle_Ages" title="Late Middle Ages">Late Middle Ages</a> it had become more common to appoint native clerics to the position of legate within their own country, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_Wolsey" title="Cardinal Wolsey">Cardinal Wolsey</a> acting as legate to the court of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII of England</a>. The reason for this switch in policy could be attributed to a change in attitude on the eve of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Reformation" title="The Reformation">The Reformation</a>; by this point, foreign men representing the papacy would be more likely to reinforce dissent than bring <a href="/wiki/Christendom" title="Christendom">Christendom</a> closer together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pagden_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pagden-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag is a non sequitur, and needs to be integrated into the text in a way that is meaningful to the reader. (April 2016)"><i>non sequitur</i></span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Papal legates often summoned <a href="/wiki/Legatine_council" title="Legatine council">legatine councils</a>, which dealt with church government and other ecclesiastical issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> According to Pope <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_VII" title="Pope Gregory VII">Gregory VII</a>, writing in the <i><a href="/wiki/Dictatus_papae" title="Dictatus papae">Dictatus papae</a></i>, a papal legate "presides over all bishops in a council, even if he is inferior in rank, and he can pronounce sentence of deposition against them".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson-3">[3]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, a legatine council was the usual means that a papal legate imposed his directives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diplomatic_Ranks">Diplomatic Ranks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Diplomatic Ranks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are several ranks of papal legates in diplomacy, some of which are no longer used.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Apostolic_nuncio">Apostolic nuncio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Apostolic nuncio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1020" data-file-width="1023" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG/220px-Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG/330px-Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG/440px-Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Francesco_Commendone" title="Giovanni Francesco Commendone">Giovanni Francesco Commendone</a>, sometime papal nuncio to <a href="/wiki/Urbino" title="Urbino">Urbino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferrara" title="Ferrara">Ferrara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parma" title="Parma">Parma</a>, and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.</div>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuncio" title="Nuncio">Nuncio</a></div>
<p>The most common form of papal legate today is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuncio" title="Nuncio">apostolic nuncio</a>, whose task it is to strengthen relations between the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> in a particular country and at the same time to act as the diplomatic representative of the Holy See to the government of that country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collinge_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Collinge-4">[4]</a></sup> An apostolic nuncio is generally equivalent in rank to that of <a href="/wiki/Ambassador" title="Ambassador">ambassador</a> extraordinary and <a href="/wiki/Plenipotentiary" title="Plenipotentiary">plenipotentiary</a>, although in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_countries" title="Catholic countries">Catholic countries</a> the nuncio often ranks above ambassadors in diplomatic protocol. A nuncio performs the same functions as an ambassador and has the same diplomatic privileges. Under the 1961 <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Convention_on_Diplomatic_Relations" title="Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations">Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations</a>, to which the Holy See is a party, a nuncio is an ambassador like those from any other country. The Vienna Convention allows the host state to grant seniority of precedence to the nuncio over others of ambassadorial rank accredited to the same country, and may grant the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dean_of_the_Diplomatic_Corps" title="Dean of the Diplomatic Corps">deanship of that country's diplomatic corps</a> to the nuncio regardless of seniority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pro-nuncio">Pro-nuncio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pro-nuncio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Pro-nuncio was a term used from 1965 to 1991 for a papal diplomatic representative of full ambassadorial rank accredited to a country that did not accord him precedence over other ambassadors and <i><a href="/wiki/De_jure" title="De jure">de jure</a></i> deanship of the diplomatic corps. In those countries, the papal representative's precedence within the corps is exactly on a par with that of the other members of ambassadorial rank, so that he becomes dean only on becoming the senior member of the corps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beal_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beal-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Apostolic_delegate">Apostolic delegate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Apostolic delegate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For countries with which the Holy See has no diplomatic relations, an apostolic delegate is sent to serve as a liaison with the Catholic Church in that country, though not accredited to its government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collinge_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Collinge-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legati"><i>Legati</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legati">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Legatus_a_latere"><i>Legatus a latere</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legatus a latere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This highest rank (literally "from the (Pope's) side", i.e. "intimately" trusted) is normally awarded to a priest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">cardinal rank</a>. It is an exceptional investiture and can either be focused or broad in scope. The legate <i>a latere</i> is the alter ego of the Pope, and as such, possesses full plenipotentiary powers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Princes2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Princes2-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Livingstone_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Livingstone-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Legatus_natus"><i>Legatus natus</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legatus natus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Literally "born legate", i.e. not nominated individually but <i>ex officio</i>, namely a bishop holding this rank as a privilege of his see, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">archbishops</a> of <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Canterbury</a> (pre-<a href="/wiki/English_Reformation" title="English Reformation">Reformation</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Prague" title="Archbishop of Prague">Prague</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Esztergom" title="Archbishop of Esztergom">Esztergom</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Udine" title="Archbishop of Udine">Udine</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Salzburg" title="Archbishop of Salzburg">Salzburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Gniezno" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Gniezno">Gniezno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Cologne" title="Archbishop of Cologne">Cologne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Princes2_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Princes2-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Livingstone_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Livingstone-8">[8]</a></sup> The <i>legatus natus</i> would act as the pope's representative in his province, with a <i>legatus a latere</i> only being sent in extraordinary circumstances. Although limited in their jurisdiction compared to <i>legati a latere</i>, a <i>legatus natus</i> were not subordinate to them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tayler_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tayler-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Legatus_missus"><i>Legatus missus</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Legatus missus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Literally "sent legate", possessing limited powers for the purpose of completing a specific mission. This commission is normally focused in scope and of short duration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Princes2_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Princes2-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Livingstone_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Livingstone-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gubernatorial_legates">Gubernatorial legates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_legate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Gubernatorial legates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some administrative (temporal) provinces of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papal_states" title="Papal states">Papal states</a> in (mostly central) Italy were governed by a Papal Legate. This has been the case in <a href="/wiki/Benevento" title="Benevento">Benevento</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Pontecorvo" title="Pontecorvo">Pontecorvo</a> (of Campagna e Marittima/ of Frosinone) and in <a href="/wiki/Viterbo" title="Viterbo">Viterbo</a>. In four cases, including <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a>, this post was awarded exclusively to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">Cardinals</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Velletri" title="Velletri">Velletri</a> post was created for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bartolommeo_Pacca" title="Bartolommeo Pacca">Bartolommeo Pacca</a>.</p>
<p>The title could be changed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apostolic_Delegate" title="Apostolic Delegate">Apostolic Delegate</a>, as happened in Frosinone (for Pontecorvo) in 1827.</p>

<dt>Papal diplomacy</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuncio" title="Nuncio">Nuncio</a> – an envoy whose diplomatic status is recognized by the receiving state – usually a <a href="/wiki/Titular_bishop" title="Titular bishop">titular archbishop</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internuncio" title="Internuncio">Internuncio</a> – a lower rank than Nuncio for a papal diplomatic representative, a title historically used at a time when states sent to some less important countries diplomatic representatives, called Envoys or Ministers, lower in rank than Ambassadors.</li>
