<p>The <b>1959 <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asian_Games" title="Southeast Asian Games">Southeast Asian Peninsular Games</a></b>, officially known as the 1st Southeast Asian Peninsular Games was the first and inaugural edition of the biennial <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> for <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asian</a> athletes, organised by the SEAP Games Federation. It was held in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> from 12 - 17 December 1959 with 12 sports featured in the games. Cambodia, one of the six founding members of the SEAP Games Federation, did not compete in the inaugural edition.</p>
<p>The choice of Thailand as host followed the successful proposition and creation of the games as suggested by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Luang_Sukhum_Nayaoradit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Luang Sukhum Nayaoradit (page does not exist)">Luang Sukhum Nayaoradit</a>, then Vice-President of the <a href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Committee_of_Thailand" title="National Olympic Committee of Thailand">National Olympic Committee of Thailand</a>, hence for the first time and first among all Southeast Asian nations, Thailand hosted the Southeast Asian Peninsular Games, then known as the Southeast Asian Games.</p>
<p>The final medal tally was led by host Thailand, followed by its neighbouring countries, Burma and Malaya.</p>
