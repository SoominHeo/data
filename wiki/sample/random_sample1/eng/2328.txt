<p><b>Google TV</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Smart_TV" title="Smart TV">smart TV</a> platform from <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> co-developed by <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Logitech" title="Logitech">Logitech</a> that was launched in October 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gannounce_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gannounce-1">[1]</a></sup> with official devices initially made by Sony and Logitech.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glaunch_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-glaunch-2">[2]</a></sup> Google TV integrates the <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> operating system and the <a href="/wiki/Google_Chrome" title="Google Chrome">Google Chrome</a> <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a> to create an <a href="/wiki/Interactive_television" title="Interactive television">interactive television</a> overlay on top of existing online video sites to add a <a href="/wiki/10-foot_user_interface" title="10-foot user interface">10-foot user interface</a>, for a smart TV experience.</p>
<p>Google TV's first generation devices were all based on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/X86_architecture" title="X86 architecture">x86 architecture</a> processors and were created and commercialized by <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Logitech" title="Logitech">Logitech</a>. The second generation of devices are all based on <a href="/wiki/ARM_architecture" title="ARM architecture">ARM architecture</a> processors and with additional partners including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG" title="LG">LG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vizio" title="Vizio">Vizio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hisense" title="Hisense">Hisense</a>. In 2013, more second generation Google TV-supported devices were announced by new partners, including <a href="/wiki/Hisense" title="Hisense">Hisense</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netgear" title="Netgear">Netgear</a>, <a href="/wiki/TCL_Corporation" title="TCL Corporation">TCL</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asus" title="Asus">Asus</a>, some of which including <a href="/wiki/3D_television" title="3D television">3D video</a> support.</p>
<p>Google TV was succeeded in June 2014 by <a href="/wiki/Android_TV" title="Android TV">Android TV</a>, a new platform which shares closer ties with the Android platform (in particular, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Android_5.0" title="Android 5.0">Android 5.0</a>) and has a revamped user experience integrating with <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_Graph" title="Knowledge Graph">Knowledge Graph</a>, and providing <a href="/wiki/Chromecast" title="Chromecast">casting</a> support from mobile devices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-V_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-V-4">[4]</a></sup> While a "small subset" of Google TV devices will be upgraded to the Android TV platform, the majority will not. In June 2014, the Google TV <a href="/wiki/Software_development_kit" title="Software development kit">SDK</a> was pulled, ending any future software development for existing devices and effectively <a href="/wiki/Deprecation" title="Deprecation">deprecating</a> the platform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-verge-googletvdead_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-verge-googletvdead-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Google TV leverages many of Google’s existing products. Google TV’s operating system, a customized version of <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a>, provides the underlying foundation, allowing developers to create applications that extend the system’s functionality. Google’s Chrome browser provides a gateway to the Internet, allowing consumers to browse web sites and watch television, in tandem. Consumers can access <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>, <a href="/wiki/CNBC" title="CNBC">CNBC</a>, and content from other providers through the Chrome browser. Android and <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> smartphones and tablet computers may be used as remote controls for Google TV. Google TV products ship with wireless remote controls with a full <a href="/wiki/QWERTY" title="QWERTY">QWERTY</a> keypad. An update in November 2011, allowed access to <a href="/wiki/Google_Play" title="Google Play">Google Play</a> and enabled search to find content from live TV, Netflix, YouTube, HBO GO, Amazon, and more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Apps">Apps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Apps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Xyologic has compiled a list of the early Google TV apps with the largest number of installations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As of November 2012, the most installed apps are <a href="/wiki/Napster" title="Napster">Napster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Radio" title="Pandora Radio">Pandora Radio</a> and <a href="/wiki/CNBC" title="CNBC">CNBC</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Partners">Partners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Partners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Google TV platform was provided by Google to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OEM" title="OEM">OEMs</a> for incorporation into their consumer products. The first generation of consumer devices were produced by <a href="/wiki/Logitech" title="Logitech">Logitech</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>. The second generation of consumer devices were produced by Sony,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG" title="LG">LG</a> (see <a href="/wiki/LG_L9" title="LG L9">LG L9</a> <a href="/wiki/System_on_a_chip" title="System on a chip">SoC</a>), <a href="/wiki/Vizio" title="Vizio">Vizio</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hisense" title="Hisense">Hisense</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NetGear" title="NetGear">NetGear</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asus" title="Asus">Asus</a>. The third generation of consumer devices was announced by LG at 2013 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_CES" title="International CES">International CES</a>, with the announcement of their TV models.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competitors_and_controversies">Competitors and controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Competitors and controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Smart_TV" title="Smart TV">Smart TV</a></div>
<p>Cable providers as well as content providers have been slow to warm to Google TV. <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>, Fox,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a> have blocked Google TV enabled devices from accessing their web content since Google TV's launch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytBlock_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytBlock-54">[54]</a></sup> As of November 22, 2010, Google TV devices are blocked from accessing any programs offered by <a href="/wiki/Viacom" title="Viacom">Viacom</a>’s subsidiaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnetBlock_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnetBlock-55">[55]</a></sup> Of the cable and satellite providers, only <a href="/wiki/Dish_Network" title="Dish Network">Dish Network</a> (formerly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Echostar" title="Echostar">Echostar</a>), had embraced Google TV and promoted it by offering customers a discount on the Logitech Revue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dishNetwork_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-dishNetwork-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2014, Google filed a <a href="/wiki/Uniform_Domain-Name_Dispute-Resolution_Policy" title="Uniform Domain-Name Dispute-Resolution Policy">UDRP</a> case against the owner of domain names androidtv.com and xbmcandroidtv.com. The domain names were owned Exo Level, Inc. and were registered with <a href="/wiki/GoDaddy" title="GoDaddy">GoDaddy</a> in November 2006. In March 2014, Google’s case was denied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_TV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>2010 March – Media outlets reported that the Google TV project was underway, although the partnering companies did not confirm their involvement in the venture until later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msspec_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-msspec-65">[65]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eweek_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-eweek-66">[66]</a></sup></li>
<li>2010 May 20 – The Google TV project was officially announced at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Google_I/O" title="Google I/O">Google I/O</a> conference on May 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engadget_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-engadget-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-appmarket.tv_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-appmarket.tv-68">[68]</a></sup> Google stated that the new platform would be incorporated directly into new high-definition television sets and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray Disc</a> players by <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>, although set-top boxes would also be developed by <a href="/wiki/Logitech" title="Logitech">Logitech</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The company indicated that the new systems developed by Sony and Logitech would be powered by <a href="/wiki/Intel_Atom" title="Intel Atom">Intel Atom</a> based <a href="/wiki/List_of_Intel_Atom_microprocessors#CE_processors" title="List of Intel Atom microprocessors">CE4100</a> consumer electronics <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/System-on-a-chip" title="System-on-a-chip">system-on-chip</a>. It also stated that a "fully optimized" viewer experience would be available through the <a href="/wiki/Dish_Network" title="Dish Network">Dish Network</a>, although the platform would operate through any provider.</li>
<li>2010 May – Sony announced that it would be releasing its Google-enabled Sony Internet TV product lineup in the fall of 2010, including standalone TV models and set-top units with integrated Blu-ray Disc drives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></li>
<li>2010 November 10 – Dish Network announced that its Google TV solution<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> was available to customers. Dish Network's Google TV solution, which requires a <a href="/wiki/Digital_video_recorder" title="Digital video recorder">DVR</a> integration service, includes the <a href="/wiki/Logitech" title="Logitech">Logitech</a> Revue with Google TV, a small set-top box.</li>
<li>2010 December 15 – Google announces the first Google TV update, with some bug fixes and four major improvements: namely <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> streaming catalog, Dual View, Remote Control App for Android Phones and Movies search results.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></li>
<li>2011 May 10 – At Google I/O it is announced that Google TV devices will be updated to Android 3.1 Honeycomb, will have access to the Android Market, and have some application updates.</li>
<li>2011 June 18 – Google acquires <a href="/wiki/SageTV" title="SageTV">SageTV</a>, a home media software developer.</li>
<li>2011 August 26 – Google has announced Google TV would be officially released in Europe in January 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></li>
<li>2011 October 28 – Google TV starts the roll-out of Google TV 2.0, with the Android 3.1 operating system and access to the Market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></li>
<li>2012 January 8 – It is announced that the new generation of Google TV devices will run on a variety of <a href="/wiki/ARM_architecture" title="ARM architecture">ARM</a> <a href="/wiki/System_on_a_chip" title="System on a chip">SoC</a> designs. One of the key suppliers is <a href="/wiki/Marvell_Technology_Group" title="Marvell Technology Group">Marvell</a> with their Armada 1500<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> platform.</li>
<li>2012 January 10 – Sony Corporation plans to bring Google TV to Canada in Summer 2012. No exact released date has been announced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> Sony marketing director, Stephane Labrousse has stated that Sony will start selling Google TV based products in Europe in September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></li>
<li>2012 April 19 – Google updates the TV and Movies app to include social features<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></li>
<li>2012 June 25 – Sony plans to release Google TV in Europe in September 2012 with the network media player <i>NSZ-GS7</i> and the Google TV-integrated <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> player <i>NSZ-GS9</i> where it will first be released in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></li>
<li>2012 October 12 – Google video demo of Google TV version 3 is leaked. Version 3 features include an improved <i>Primetime</i> TV guide application, as well as a companion Android application. Voice based TV and web search for models that include a microphone. A Movies &amp; TV Play Store application which will offer, for purchase, streaming video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></li>
<li>2013 January – Google TV version 3 begins to be rolled out to most Google TV devices.</li>
<li>2013 May 15 – Google announces that 2nd generation Google TV devices (using ARM CPUs) will be eligible to receive an update to Android 4.2.2 and the latest version of Android Chrome instead of Desktop Chrome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></li>
<li>2013 October 10 – Report suggests Google will rebrand Google TV to Android TV and that approximately one million Google TV devices are in use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GigaOm-Oct13_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-GigaOm-Oct13-84">[84]</a></sup></li>
<li>2013 October 11 – Google confirms Google TV version 4 will eventually receive native Chromecast like functionality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></li>
<li>2013 October 24 – LG televisions with Google TV begin to receive the Android 4.2.2 update.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
