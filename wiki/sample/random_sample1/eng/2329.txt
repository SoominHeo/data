<p><b>Google Map Maker</b> is a mapping and map editing service launched by <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> in June 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In geographies where it is hard to find providers of good map data, user contributions can be used to increase map quality. Changes to Google Map Maker are intended to appear on <a href="/wiki/Google_Maps" title="Google Maps">Google Maps</a> only after sufficient review by Google moderators. Google Map Maker is used at Google <a href="/wiki/Mapathon" title="Mapathon">Mapathon</a> events held annually.</p>
<p>In November 2016, it was announced that Google Map Maker would be retired in March 2017 and merged with Google Maps itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interface">Interface</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_Map_Maker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Interface">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Users are able to draw features directly onto a map where the borders have already been drawn, and can add features such as <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">roads</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway" title="Railway">railways</a>, <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a> and so on. In addition, users can add specific buildings and services onto the map such as local <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">businesses</a> and services. At first glance the site appears identical to Google Maps, and the three views (map, satellite and hybrid) are available which allow users to view the map data, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satellite_image" title="Satellite image">satellite image</a> of the region or a combination of both.</p>
<p>Using the find or browse tools, contributors are able to add to and edit existing features on the map. Three kinds of drawing tools are available: placemark (a single point of interest on the map), line (for drawing roads, railways, rivers, and the like) and <a href="/wiki/Polygon" title="Polygon">polygon</a> (for defining boundaries and borders, adding parks, lakes and other large features). The approach encouraged by users and by Google is to trace features such as roads from the existing satellite imagery. This approach is not useful in areas with poor satellite imagery, and users consequently create less map data in those areas.</p>
<p>New users' contributions are moderated by more experienced users or reviewers at Google to ensure quality and prevent <a href="/wiki/Vandalism" title="Vandalism">vandalism</a>. As users make more successful contributions, their edits are less closely monitored and may be published on the map straight away. Certain larger features may take a long time to appear on the map as they are waiting to be rendered by the server.</p>
<p>Contributors can assign areas of the map as their 'neighbourhood', that is an area they know well enough to make detailed contributions to. Users can also moderate the contributions of others within their neighbourhood. This information is private; the neighbourhood a user selects is not publicly associated with the users' account.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pulse_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-pulse-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_vandalism_incident">2015 vandalism incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_Map_Maker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2015 vandalism incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In April 2015, Google removed user-created Map Maker content that showed an "<a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> robot urinating on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apple_logo" title="Apple logo">Apple logo</a>" and a separate feature saying "Google review policy is crap", after they were discovered on <a href="/wiki/Google_Maps" title="Google Maps">Google Maps</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Following the "large scale prank", Google disabled auto-approval and user moderation, and on 8 May, the Map Maker team announced that editing would be temporarily disabled worldwide from 12 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_News_12_May_2015_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News_12_May_2015-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Google reenabled the service in six countries in August 2015, with plans to enable in other countries in the following weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6country_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-6country-9">[9]</a></sup> On 10 August, editing was re-opened to Bangladesh, Brazil, Canada, India, the Philippines, and Ukraine; Google will rely on regional moderators to review edits as an extra precaution, in addition to automated and human moderation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 26 August, Google Map Maker re-opened to 45 more countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Google_Map_Maker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Map Maker requires contributors to grant Google a "... perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, and non-exclusive license to reproduce, adapt, modify, translate, publish, publicly perform, publicly display, distribute, and create derivative works of the User Submission".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> While Google provides a form to request data downloads,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> it provides no programmatic access to data. Thus large contributors to Map Maker, such as the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>'s project partnering with Google have come under criticism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
