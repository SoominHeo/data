<p><b>Google Japanese Input</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">Google 日本語入力</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gūguru Nihongo Nyūryoku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an <a href="/wiki/Input_method" title="Input method">input method</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> for the entry of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_writing_system" title="Japanese writing system">Japanese text</a> on a computer. Since its dictionaries are generated automatically from the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>, it is much easier to type <a href="/wiki/Personal_name" title="Personal name">personal names</a>, Internet slang, neologisms and related terms.</p>
<p>Google also releases an <a href="/wiki/Open-source_software" title="Open-source software">open-source</a> version without stable releases or quality assurance under the name <b>mozc</b>. As it is open source, it can be used on Linux-based systems, whereas Google Japanese Input is limited to Windows, MacOS, and Chrome OS. It does not use Google's closed-source algorithms for generating dictionary data from online sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
