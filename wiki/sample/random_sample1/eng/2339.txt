<p><b>Kunohe</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">九戸村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kunohe-mura</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Villages_of_Japan" title="Villages of Japan">village</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kunohe_District,_Iwate" title="Kunohe District, Iwate">Kunohe District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iwate_Prefecture" title="Iwate Prefecture">Iwate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of February 2014, the village had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 6,145 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 45.8 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area was 134.05  km<sup>2</sup>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kunohe,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kunohe is located in north-central Iwate Prefecture, surrounded by the Kitakami Mountains.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kunohe,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Kunohe was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a>, dominated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nambu_clan" title="Nambu clan">Nambu clan</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, who ruled <a href="/wiki/Hachinohe_Domain" title="Hachinohe Domain">Hachinohe Domain</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>. The villages of Toda, ibonai and Esashika within Kita-Kunohe District were created on April 1, 1889. Kita-Kunohe District and Minami-Kunohe Districts merged to form Kunohe District on April 1, 1897. The three villages merged to form the modern village of Kunohe on April 1, 1955.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kunohe,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Kunohe is based on agriculture, the production of charcoal and animal husbandry, primarily poultry.</p>
