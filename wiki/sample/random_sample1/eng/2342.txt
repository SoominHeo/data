<p><b>Koo Dae-sung</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 구대성; born August 2, 1969 in <a href="/wiki/Daejeon" title="Daejeon">Daejeon</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>) is a Korean professional left-handed <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a>. He formerly played for the <a href="/wiki/Hanwha_Eagles" title="Hanwha Eagles">Hanwha Eagles</a> in the <a href="/wiki/KBO_League" title="KBO League">KBO League</a>. He was also formerly with the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orix_BlueWave" title="Orix BlueWave">Orix BlueWave</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a>. He announced his retirement from Korean baseball on August 15, 2010. He played for the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Blue_Sox" title="Sydney Blue Sox">Sydney Blue Sox</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Baseball_League" title="Australian Baseball League">Australian Baseball League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koo is a left-handed pitcher despite being born right-handed. As a child, he suffered an injury which forced him to become left-handed. He broke his right arm so badly that he had to use his left hand primarily. Koo attended <a href="/wiki/Hanyang_University" title="Hanyang University">Hanyang University</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hanwha_Eagles">Hanwha Eagles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hanwha Eagles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Koo began his professional career in 1993 with the <a href="/wiki/Hanwha_Eagles" title="Hanwha Eagles">Hanwha Eagles</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">Korea Baseball Organization</a>. In 1996, he won both a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_Glove_Award" title="Gold Glove Award">Gold Glove Award</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Most_Valuable_Player" title="Most Valuable Player">MVP</a> of the KBO with a 1.88 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">ERA</a>, 18 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Win_(baseball)" title="Win (baseball)">wins</a>, 24 <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">saves</a> and 183 <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a> in 139 <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings pitched</a>.</p>
<p>Koo played for the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> national Olympic team in the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000</a> games. He led his team to a <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze medal</a> in 2000 by pitching a 3-1 <a href="/wiki/Complete_game" title="Complete game">complete game</a> victory over Japan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Orix_BlueWave">Orix BlueWave</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Orix BlueWave">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, Koo joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orix_BlueWave" title="Orix BlueWave">Orix BlueWave</a> of the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Pacific_League" title="Pacific League">Pacific League</a>. Though he was a <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">reliever</a> throughout his professional career, he became a <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starter</a> for the team in 2002, recording the second best ERA on the team. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orix_BlueWave" title="Orix BlueWave">Orix BlueWave</a> merged with the <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Kintetsu_Buffaloes" title="Osaka Kintetsu Buffaloes">Osaka Kintetsu Buffaloes</a> in 2004, and he took the opportunity to announce his decision to play in the major leagues.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_Mets">New York Mets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New York Mets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, the New York Mets signed Koo as a free agent. He pitched well as a reliever during <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> and was selected for the Mets' active roster as the season began. Despite his 35 years of age, and 12 years experience in Korea and Japan, he was considered a <a href="/wiki/Rookie" title="Rookie">rookie</a> by Major League Baseball in his first year. He made his Major League Debut on April 4, 2005 against the Cincinnati Reds, and pitched a perfect 8th inning, in the process collecting his first big league strikeout.</p>
<p>Koo's most memorable moment as a Major Leaguer occurred on May 21, 2005, when he recorded his only major league hit, a <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a> off of <a href="/wiki/Randy_Johnson" title="Randy Johnson">Randy Johnson</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>. This came right when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_TV" title="Fox TV">Fox TV</a> analyst <a href="/wiki/Tim_McCarver" title="Tim McCarver">Tim McCarver</a> commented on how Koo's <a href="/wiki/Plate_appearance" title="Plate appearance">plate appearance</a> was "the biggest give-up <a href="/wiki/At_bat" title="At bat">at bat</a>". Koo had taken two pitches and sat in his first at-bat, leading everyone to believe that he was ready to go up just to sit down again. On a 1-and-1 count, Koo crushed a deep line drive right over <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Williams" title="Bernie Williams">Bernie Williams</a>'s head one hop of the wall in right-center field. Upon reaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_base" title="Second base">second base</a>, the home crowd and his teammates in the dugout cheered and chanted "KOOOO!" When asked about what he thought about the hit, Koo responded, "Other people say that I swung my bat with my eyes closed. But, of course I saw it –I hit it! It has been 18 years since I batted last – when I was in high school. Occasionally I went to the batting cages and swung the bat a couple of times. But, there really wasn’t any difference."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A slow runner with little base running experience, Koo advanced to third base on a <a href="/wiki/Sacrifice_bunt" title="Sacrifice bunt">sacrifice bunt</a> dropped by the following hitter, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Reyes_(shortstop)" title="José Reyes (shortstop)">José Reyes</a>. However, after Koo slowed down approaching third base, he re-accelerated and ran towards a vacant home plate left uncovered as Reyes was getting thrown out at first base. Yankees' catcher <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Posada" title="Jorge Posada">Jorge Posada</a> promptly ran towards home in a foot race with Koo. Upon retrieving the thrown ball from first base, Posada applied the tag onto Koo's left shoulder as he slid head first towards home plate on a close play. Home plate umpire <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Meriwether" title="Chuck Meriwether">Chuck Meriwether</a> called Koo safe on the play. With that, the home crowd once again cheered and chanted his last name. His teammates enthusiastically greeted him, with celebrations ranging from his teammates brushing the dirt off his uniform to <a href="/wiki/Doug_Mientkiewicz" title="Doug Mientkiewicz">Doug Mientkiewicz</a> fanning him with a towel. On his reception by his teammates, Koo said that "they said a lot of things. I just couldn't understand what. I think they were saying 'Good job.'" As of the 2008 season, it was his final Major League at-bat.</p>
<p>Unfortunately, it was later revealed that Koo suffered a shoulder injury during this play while sliding into homeplate. His stats suffered as well as a result thus forcing him to return to South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Korea">Return to Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 2, 2006, Koo rejoined the Hanwha Eagles when the Mets sold his contract to them.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sydney_Blue_Sox">Sydney Blue Sox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sydney Blue Sox">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In press conference on 22 August, Koo revealed he was to play for the Sydney Blue Sox in the Australian Baseball League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Koo made his debut in the opening game of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Australian_Baseball_League_regular_season" title="2010–11 Australian Baseball League regular season">2010–11 Australian Baseball League regular season</a>. With his team up 1-0 against the <a href="/wiki/Canberra_Cavalry" title="Canberra Cavalry">Canberra Cavalry</a>, he recorded the final out by striking out <a href="/wiki/Nick_Kimpton" title="Nick Kimpton">Nick Kimpton</a> to earn the first <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">save</a> in the Australian Baseball League. He finished the season with 12 saves, 1.00 ERA and .144 opposition average against, this stellar season led Koo to be awarded Reliever of the Year.</p>
<p>After spending the first two seasons as the Blue Sox <a href="/wiki/Closer_(baseball)" title="Closer (baseball)">closer</a>, he was shifted into the 8th inning role when <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Williams_(baseball)" title="Matthew Williams (baseball)">Matthew Williams</a> took over as the team's closer. However, in the 2013–14 season, Williams moved to the <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Bite" title="Adelaide Bite">Adelaide Bite</a> and Koo was once again named Sydney's closer, posting a stellar 2.08 ERA with 11 saves.</p>
<p>In four seasons of the Australian Baseball League, Koo has a 2.16 ERA with 31 saves.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_Australia">Team Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Team Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Koo was selected for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_national_baseball_team" title="Australian national baseball team">Australian national baseball team</a> in two spring training exhibition matches against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koo_Dae-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koo is married, has two children, and resides in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>. He plays for <a href="/wiki/Penrith,_New_South_Wales" title="Penrith, New South Wales">Penrith</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_Major_League" title="New South Wales Major League">New South Wales Major League</a>.</p>
