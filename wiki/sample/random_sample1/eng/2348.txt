<p>The <b>Kure Line</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">呉線</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kuresen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway line operated by <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR West) within <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima_Prefecture" title="Hiroshima Prefecture">Hiroshima Prefecture</a> in Japan. It begins at <a href="/wiki/Mihara_Station" title="Mihara Station">Mihara Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mihara,_Hiroshima" title="Mihara, Hiroshima">Mihara</a> and terminates at <a href="/wiki/Kaitaichi_Station" title="Kaitaichi Station">Kaitaichi Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kaita,_Hiroshima" title="Kaita, Hiroshima">Kaita</a>. It is one of the main lines of JR West. The section between <a href="/wiki/Mihara_Station" title="Mihara Station">Mihara Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Station" title="Hiro Station">Hiro Station</a> is branded the "Setouchi Sazanami Line" (<a href="/wiki/Seto_Inland_Sea" title="Seto Inland Sea">Seto Inland Sea</a> Ripple Line) as the sightseeing line. On the section between Mihara Station and Hiro Station, most trains operate from Hiro Station through Mihara Station to <a href="/wiki/Itozaki_Station" title="Itozaki Station">Itozaki Station</a>. On the section between Hiro Station and Kaitaichi Station, most trains operate from Hiro Station through Kaitaichi Station to <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima_Station" title="Hiroshima Station">Hiroshima Station</a> or <a href="/wiki/Iwakuni_Station" title="Iwakuni Station">Iwakuni Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>New 2- and 3-car <a href="/wiki/227_series" title="227 series">227 series</a> electric trains are scheduled to be introduced on the Kure Line from around 2015, replacing older 115 series trains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-page20140619_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-page20140619-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The Kaitashi - Kure section was opened as a private line in 1903, and leased to the Sanyo Railway Co. the following year, before being nationalised in 1906. Individual section opening dates are given below.</p>
<p>The Mihara - Sumani section opened in 1930, and the two sections were connected in 1935. Work to duplicate the line at Kure commenced in 1940, but was abandoned due to material shortages in 1945.</p>
<p>The entire line was electrified in 1970, and freight services ceased in 1986.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kure_Line">Kure Line</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Kure Line">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Opened the line between <a href="/wiki/Kaitaichi_Station" title="Kaitaichi Station">Kaitaichi Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kure_Station" title="Kure Station">Kure Station</a> including <a href="/wiki/Yano_Station" title="Yano Station">Yano Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saka_Station" title="Saka Station">Saka Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tenn%C5%8D_Station_(Hiroshima)" title="Tennō Station (Hiroshima)">Tennō Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoshiura_Station" title="Yoshiura Station">Yoshiura Station</a> on December 27, 1903.</li>
<li>Opened temporary station (<a href="/wiki/Hamasaki_Station" title="Hamasaki Station">Hamasaki Station</a>) on July 28, 1904.</li>
<li>Rent for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanyo_Railway" title="Sanyo Railway">Sanyo Railway</a> from December 1, 1904.</li>
<li>Nationalized from December 1, 1906.</li>
<li>Named "Kure Line" as one of national rail ways on October 12, 1909.</li>
<li>Opened <a href="/wiki/Koyaura_Station" title="Koyaura Station">Koyaura Station</a> and Closed Hamasaki Station on May 1, 1914.</li>
<li>Opened temporary station (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Akihamasaki_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Akihamasaki Station (page does not exist)">Akihamasaki Station</a>) on July 21, 1926.</li>
<li>Opened temporary station (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karyugahama_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karyugahama Station (page does not exist)">Karyugahama Station</a>) on July 7, 1928.</li>
<li>Extended the line from Kure Station to <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Station" title="Hiro Station">Hiro Station</a> and opened <a href="/wiki/Akiaga_Station" title="Akiaga Station">Akiaga Station</a> and Hiro Station on March 24, 1935.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sango_Line">Sango Line</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sango Line">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Opened Sango Line between <a href="/wiki/Mihara_Station" title="Mihara Station">Mihara Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sunami_Station" title="Sunami Station">Sunami Station</a> on March 19, 1930.</li>
<li>Extended the line from Sunami Station to <a href="/wiki/Akisaizaki_Station" title="Akisaizaki Station">Akisaizaki Station</a> on April 28, 1931.</li>
<li>Extended the line from Akisaizaki Station to <a href="/wiki/Takehara_Station" title="Takehara Station">Takehara Station</a> including <a href="/wiki/Tadanoumi_Station" title="Tadanoumi Station">Tadanoumi Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cnori_Station" title="Ōnori Station">Ōnori Station</a> on July 10, 1932.</li>
<li>Extended the line from Takehara Station to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsuuchinoumi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mitsuuchinoumi Station (page does not exist)">Mitsuuchinoumi Station</a> including <a href="/wiki/Yoshina_Station" title="Yoshina Station">Yoshina Station</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akimitsu_Station" title="Akimitsu Station">Akimitsu Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazahaya_Station" title="Kazahaya Station">Kazahaya Station</a> on January 17, 1935.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Integration">Integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Integrated Sango Line into Kure Line and opened <a href="/wiki/Ato_Station" title="Ato Station">Ato Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akikawajiri_Station" title="Akikawajiri Station">Akikawajiri Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigata_Station" title="Nigata Station">Nigata Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kawaraishi_Station" title="Kawaraishi Station">Kawaraishi Station</a> on November 24, 1935.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_integration">After integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: After integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Closed Kawaraishi Station on December 1, 1940.</li>
<li>Renamed Mitsuuchinoumi Station to <a href="/wiki/Yasuura_Station" title="Yasuura Station">Yasuura Station</a> on May 1, 1946.</li>
<li>Renamed Akimitsu Station to <a href="/wiki/Akitsu_Station_(Hiroshima)" title="Akitsu Station (Hiroshima)">Akitsu Station</a> on December 20, 1949.</li>
<li>Re-opened Kawaraishi Station on August 1, 1958.</li>
<li>Closed temporary stations (Karyugahama Station and Akihamasaki Station) on August 1, 1967.</li>
<li>Electrified the rail way on September 15, 1970.</li>
<li>Stopped cargo services on December 1, 1986.</li>
<li>Privatized as JR West on April 1, 1987.</li>
<li>Opened <a href="/wiki/Kure-Portopia_Station" title="Kure-Portopia Station">Kure-Portopia Station</a> on March 19, 1992.</li>
<li>Opened <a href="/wiki/Akinagahama_Station" title="Akinagahama Station">Akinagahama Station</a> on October 1, 1994.</li>
<li>Opened <a href="/wiki/Karugahama_Station" title="Karugahama Station">Karugahama Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mizushiri_Station" title="Mizushiri Station">Mizushiri Station</a> and moved Kawaraishi Station on February 7, 1999.</li>
<li>Opened <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinhiro_Station" title="Shinhiro Station">Shinhiro Station</a> on March 23, 2002.</li>
</ul>
