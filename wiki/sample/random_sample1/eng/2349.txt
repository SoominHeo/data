<p>The <b>bombing of <a href="/wiki/Kure,_Hiroshima" title="Kure, Hiroshima">Kure</a></b> and surrounding areas by United States and British <a href="/wiki/Naval_aviation" title="Naval aviation">naval aircraft</a> in late July 1945 led to the sinking of most of the surviving large warships of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> (IJN). The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Third_Fleet" title="United States Third Fleet">United States Third Fleet</a>'s attacks on <a href="/wiki/Kure_Naval_Arsenal" title="Kure Naval Arsenal">Kure Naval Arsenal</a> and nearby ports on 24, 25, and 28 July sank an <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a>, three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battleships" title="Battleships">battleships</a>, five <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cruisers" title="Cruisers">cruisers</a>, and several smaller warships. During the same period the <a href="/wiki/British_Pacific_Fleet" title="British Pacific Fleet">British Pacific Fleet</a> attacked other targets in the <a href="/wiki/Seto_Inland_Sea" title="Seto Inland Sea">Inland Sea</a> region and sank two <a href="/wiki/Type_D_escort_ship" title="Type D escort ship">escort ships</a> and several smaller vessels as well as damaging an <a href="/wiki/Escort_carrier" title="Escort carrier">escort carrier</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prelude">Prelude</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bombing_of_Kure_(July_1945)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Prelude">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 1945 the IJN's remaining large warships were concentrated near the major naval base of Kure. The ships were effectively immobilized due to fuel shortages and were being used only as stationary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-aircraft" title="Anti-aircraft">anti-aircraft</a> batteries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RN_p223_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RN_p223-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">Admiral</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_S._McCain,_Sr." title="John S. McCain, Sr.">John S. McCain, Sr.</a>, the commander of the <a href="/wiki/Fast_Carrier_Task_Force" title="Fast Carrier Task Force">Fast Carrier Task Force</a>, strongly opposed attacking Kure as he and his staff believed that the ships only posed a minor threat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halsey265_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Halsey265-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his memoirs Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Halsey,_Jr." title="William Halsey, Jr.">Halsey</a> gave four reasons for why he attacked Kure despite McCain's objections. Firstly, he believed that the attack would boost US morale and retaliate for the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">Attack on Pearl Harbor</a> in December 1941, secondly it would ensure that the Japanese could not disrupt the planned <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> invasion of <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a>, thirdly it would prevent Japan from using its fleet as a bargaining point to secure better peace terms and finally that he had been ordered to conduct the attack by his superior officer, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fleet_Admiral_(United_States)" title="Fleet Admiral (United States)">Fleet Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Chester_W._Nimitz" title="Chester W. Nimitz">Chester W. Nimitz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halsey265_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Halsey265-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite operating as a task group of the US Third Fleet, the British Pacific Fleet was excluded from the attack on Kure so that Britain would not be able to claim a part in destroying the Japanese fleet. The BPF was instead used to attack airfields and the port of <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RN_p223_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-RN_p223-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halsey265_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Halsey265-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kure had been subjected to several major attacks by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-29_Superfortress" title="B-29 Superfortress">B-29 Superfortress</a> bombers of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> in 1945, prior to the US Navy's attack in late July. The Hiro Naval Aircraft Factory was successfully bombed on 5 May, <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">naval mines</a> were laid in the approaches to the port on 30 March and 5 May and 40 percent of the city was destroyed in a major air raid on 1 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Participating in the attacks were Task Force 38 for the Americans and Task Force 37 for the British. Task Force 37 included the carriers HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Formidable_(67)" title="HMS Formidable (67)"><i>Formidable</i> (67)</a>, <a href="/wiki/HMS_Indefatigable_(R10)" title="HMS Indefatigable (R10)"><i>Indefatigable</i> (R10)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/HMS_Victorious_(R38)" title="HMS Victorious (R38)"><i>Victorious</i> (R38)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rohwer.2C_p._424_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rohwer.2C_p._424-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle">Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bombing_of_Kure_(July_1945)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The Third Fleet's attack against Kure began on 24 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> US carrier aircraft flew 1,747 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sorties" title="Sorties">sorties</a> on this day against Japanese targets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morison331_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morison331-7">[7]</a></sup> The attacks were successful, and resulted in the sinking of aircraft carrier <a href="/wiki/Japanese_aircraft_carrier_Amagi" title="Japanese aircraft carrier Amagi"><i>Amagi</i></a>, and the cruiser <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_%C5%8Cyodo" title="Japanese cruiser Ōyodo"><i>Ōyodo</i></a>, which at this time was acting as the Combined Fleet's flagship. The battleships <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Hy%C5%ABga" title="Japanese battleship Hyūga"><i>Hyūga</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Ise" title="Japanese battleship Ise"><i>Ise</i></a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Haruna" title="Japanese battleship Haruna"><i>Haruna</i></a>, the heavy cruisers <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Tone_(1937)" title="Japanese cruiser Tone (1937)"><i>Tone</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Aoba" title="Japanese cruiser Aoba"><i>Aoba</i></a>, and the outdated armored training cruisers <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Iwate" title="Japanese cruiser Iwate"><i>Iwate</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Izumo" title="Japanese cruiser Izumo"><i>Izumo</i></a> were all heavily damaged and settled in shallow water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The shallow anchorage precluded the use of <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedoes</a>. The US aircraft attempted to reduce their losses from the large number of anti-aircraft guns in the area by the use of variable time-fused bombs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RN_p223_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-RN_p223-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rohwer.2C_p._424_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rohwer.2C_p._424-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>The British Pacific Fleet's attacks against Osaka and targets in the Inland Sea damaged escort carrier <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_aircraft_carrier_Kaiyo" title="Japanese aircraft carrier Kaiyo"><i>Kaiyo</i></a> and sank the <a href="/wiki/Type_D_escort_ship" title="Type D escort ship">escort ships <i>No. 4</i> and <i>No. 30</i></a> for the loss of four aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RN_p223_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-RN_p223-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>US strikes against Kure resumed on 28 July and resulted in the further damaging of the battleships <i>Ise</i> and <i>Haruna</i>, and the heavy cruiser <i>Aoba</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RN_p223_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-RN_p223-2">[2]</a></sup> The aircraft carrier <a href="/wiki/Japanese_aircraft_carrier_Katsuragi" title="Japanese aircraft carrier Katsuragi"><i>Katsuragi</i></a> which had largely escaped attack in the earlier raid, and the unserviceable light aircraft carrier <a href="/wiki/Japanese_aircraft_carrier_Ry%C5%ABh%C5%8D" title="Japanese aircraft carrier Ryūhō"><i>Ryūhō</i></a> were attacked, with <i>Katsuragi</i> suffering heavy damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morison331_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Morison331-7">[7]</a></sup> These air strikes were among the largest conducted by the US Navy during the war, and were the most destructive of shipping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morison331_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Morison331-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The USAAF also launched an attack of the Japanese ships at Kure on 28 July. This raid was made up of 79 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-24_Liberator" title="B-24 Liberator">B-24 Liberators</a> based on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>. Four bomb hits were made upon the beached cruiser <i>Aoba</i>. The bomb strikes further damaged the vessel, and caused her stern to be broken off. The raid suffered the loss of two B-24s shot down and 14 others suffered damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allied losses included 102 aircrew and 133 planes lost in combat or accidents during the attacks. These losses were higher than those suffered by the Third Fleet in most of its operations, and were the result of the heavy anti-aircraft defences around Kure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halsey264_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Halsey264-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Allied attacks on Kure and the inland sea left the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Nagato" title="Japanese battleship Nagato"><i>Nagato</i></a> at <a href="/wiki/Yokosuka,_Kanagawa" title="Yokosuka, Kanagawa">Yokosuka</a> as the only remaining capital ship in Japan's inventory. The destruction of her <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleships</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heavy_cruiser" title="Heavy cruiser">heavy cruisers</a> at Kure was seen by British official historian <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Roskill" title="Stephen Roskill">Stephen Roskill</a> as avenging the losses suffered by the United States at Pearl Harbor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roskill374_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Roskill374-10">[10]</a></sup> The attacks allowed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean_Fleet_(Russia)" title="Pacific Ocean Fleet (Russia)">Soviet Pacific Fleet</a> to operate without fear of interdiction in the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frank_p158_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frank_p158-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
