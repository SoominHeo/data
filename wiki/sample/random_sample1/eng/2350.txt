<p><b>Kure Naval Arsenal</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">呉海軍工廠</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kure Kaigun Kosho</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was one of four principal naval <a href="/wiki/Shipyard" title="Shipyard">shipyards</a> owned and operated by the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Naval_Arsenal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kure_Naval_District" title="Kure Naval District">Kure Naval District</a> was established at <a href="/wiki/Kure,_Hiroshima" title="Kure, Hiroshima">Kure, Hiroshima</a> in 1889, as the second of the naval districts responsible for the defense of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_home_islands" title="Japanese home islands">Japanese home islands</a>. Along with the establishment of the navy base, a ship repair facility was also constructed, initially by moving the equipment from the Onohama shipyards near <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>. Construction was supervised by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/O-yatoi_gaikokujin" title="O-yatoi gaikokujin">French engineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Louis-%C3%89mile_Bertin" title="Louis-Émile Bertin">Louis-Émile Bertin</a>. The first warship constructed at Kure, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miyako_(ship)" title="Miyako (ship)">Miyako</a></i>, was launched in 1897. The "Kure Shipyards" were officially renamed the "Kure Naval Arsenal" in 1903.</p>
<p>Kure developed into one of the largest shipbuilding facilities in the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a>, capable of working with the largest vessels. The Arsenal included a major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steel_works" title="Steel works">steel works</a> (built with British assistance), and also facilities for producing <a href="/wiki/Naval_artillery" title="Naval artillery">naval artillery</a> and projectiles. The <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleships</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Yamato" title="Japanese battleship Yamato">Yamato</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Nagato" title="Japanese battleship Nagato">Nagato</a></i> were designed and constructed at Kure.</p>
<p>The facilities of the Kure Naval Arsenal were repeatedly targeted for bombing by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and over 70% of its buildings and equipment was destroyed.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Surrender_of_Japan" title="Surrender of Japan">surrender of Japan</a> in 1945, the Kure Naval Arsenal was turned over to civilian hands.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_facilities">Current facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kure_Naval_Arsenal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The extensive dry dock, ship building, repair and engineering facilities are now owned and operated by <a href="/wiki/Japan_Marine_United" title="Japan Marine United">Japan Marine United</a>, one of Japan's largest merchant marine and naval shipbuilders.</p>
