<p><b>Kurohime Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">黒姫駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kurohime-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinano_Railway_Kita-Shinano_Line" title="Shinano Railway Kita-Shinano Line">Shinano Railway Kita-Shinano Line</a> in Kashiwabara in the town of <a href="/wiki/Shinano,_Nagano" title="Shinano, Nagano">Shinano, Nagano</a>, Japan, operated by the <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership#Japan" title="Public–private partnership">third-sector</a> railway operator <a href="/wiki/Shinano_Railway" title="Shinano Railway">Shinano Railway</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurohime_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kurohime Station is served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinano_Railway_Kita-Shinano_Line" title="Shinano Railway Kita-Shinano Line">Shinano Railway Kita-Shinano Line</a> and is 28.9 kilometers from the terminus of the line at <a href="/wiki/Nagano_Station" title="Nagano Station">Nagano Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurohime_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of one ground-level <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> connected by a footbridge.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurohime_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 1 May 1888 as Kashiwabara Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">柏原駅</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. It was renamed Kurohime on 1 October 1968.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>From 14 March 2015, with the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_Shinkansen" title="Hokuriku Shinkansen">Hokuriku Shinkansen</a> extension from <a href="/wiki/Nagano_Station" title="Nagano Station">Nagano</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kanazawa_Station" title="Kanazawa Station">Kanazawa</a>, local passenger operations over sections of the Shinetsu Main Line and Hokuriku Main Line running roughly parallel to the new shinkansen line were reassigned to <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership#Japan" title="Public–private partnership">third-sector</a> railway operating companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj371_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-dj371-1">[1]</a></sup> From this date, Kurohime Station was transferred to the ownership of the third-sector operating company Shinano Railway.</p>
