<p><b>Gurgaon</b>, officially: <b>Gurugram</b>, is a city in the Indian state of <a href="/wiki/Haryana" title="Haryana">Haryana</a> and is located near the <a href="/wiki/National_Capital_Region_(India)" title="National Capital Region (India)">National Capital Region of India</a>. It is 32 kilometres (20 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a> and 268 kilometres (167 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Chandigarh" title="Chandigarh">Chandigarh</a>, the state capital. As of 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Gurugram had a population of 876,824.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1lakh_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-1lakh-1">[1]</a></sup> Witnessing rapid urbanization, Gurugram has become a leading financial and industrial hub with the third highest <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> in India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-percapita3_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-percapita3-2">[2]</a></sup> The city's economic growth story started when the leading Indian automobile manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Maruti_Suzuki" title="Maruti Suzuki">Maruti Suzuki India Limited</a> established a manufacturing plant in Gurgaon in the 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-3">[3]</a></sup> Today, Gurugram has local offices for more than 250 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The land came to be known as <i>Gurugram</i> (<a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a>: गुरुग्राम, lit. <i>village of the <a href="/wiki/Guru" title="Guru">Guru</a></i>) which over time became changed to <i>Gurgaon</i> (गुड़गाँव). The village still exists within the modern day city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 April 2016, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_Haryana" title="Chief Minister of Haryana">Chief Minister of Haryana</a> <a href="/wiki/Manohar_Lal_Khattar" title="Manohar Lal Khattar">Manohar Lal Khattar</a> announced a proposal to officially rename the city <i>Gurugram</i>, subject to the approval of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Haryana" title="Government of Haryana">Haryana cabinet</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Union Government</a>. He argued that the new name would help to preserve the "rich heritage" of the city by emphasizing its history and association with Dronacharaya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indianexpress-gurugram_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-indianexpress-gurugram-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hs-gurugram_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-hs-gurugram-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times-paradox_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-times-paradox-8">[8]</a></sup> On 27 September 2016, Manohar Lal Khattar officially announced that the Union Government had approved the name change, and thus the city and district would henceforth be known as Gurugram.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toi-nameofficial_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-toi-nameofficial-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gurgaon was historically inhabited by the <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> people and in early times, it formed a part of an extensive kingdom ruled over by <a href="/wiki/Rajput" title="Rajput">Rajputs</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yaduvanshi_Rajputs" title="Yaduvanshi Rajputs">Yaduvansi</a> or <a href="/wiki/Yadav" title="Yadav">Yadav</a> clan. The <a href="/wiki/Yadav" title="Yadav">Yadav</a> were defeated by <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_of_Ghor" title="Muhammad of Ghor">Muhammad of Ghor</a> in 1196, but for two centuries they sturdily resisted the Muhammadian domination and they were subjected to punitive expedition. Under the rule of <a href="/wiki/Firuz_Shah_Tughlaq" title="Firuz Shah Tughlaq">Feroz Shah Tughlaq</a>, several were converted to Islam. This was followed by the invasion of <a href="/wiki/Timur" title="Timur">Timur</a> and the land was ruled by Khanzadas. It was then annexed by Babur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> During <a href="/wiki/Akbar" title="Akbar">Akbar</a>'s reign, Gurgaon fell within the governing regions of <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agra" title="Agra">Agra</a>. As the <a href="/wiki/Mughal_Empire" title="Mughal Empire">Mughal Empire</a> started to decline, the place was torn between contending powers. By 1803 most of it came under the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British rule</a> through the treaty of Surji Arjungaon with Sindhia. The town was first occupied by the cavalry unit posted to watch the army of <a href="/wiki/Begum_Samru" title="Begum Samru">Begum Samru</a> of Sirdhana. It became a part of the <a href="/wiki/District" title="District">district</a>, which was divided into units called <a href="/wiki/Pargana" title="Pargana"><i>parganas</i></a>. These units were given to petty chiefs for the military service rendered by them. Eventually these units came under direct control of the British, with the last major administrative change in 1836. After the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857" title="Indian Rebellion of 1857">Revolt of 1857</a>, it was transferred from the <a href="/wiki/North-Western_Provinces" title="North-Western Provinces">North-Western Provinces</a> to <a href="/wiki/Punjab_Province_(British_India)" title="Punjab Province (British India)">Punjab Province</a>. In 1861, the district, of which Gurgaon was a part of, was rearranged into five <a href="/wiki/Tehsil" title="Tehsil">tehsils</a> Gurgaon, <a href="/wiki/Ferozepur_Jhirka" title="Ferozepur Jhirka">Ferozepur Jhirka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuh_(city)" title="Nuh (city)">Nuh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palwal" title="Palwal">Palwal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rewari" title="Rewari">Rewari</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and the modern day city came under the control of Gurgaon teshil. In 1947, Gurgaon became a part of <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(India)" title="Independence Day (India)">independent India</a> and fell under the <a href="/wiki/States_and_union_territories_of_India" title="States and union territories of India">Indian state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_India" title="Punjab, India">Punjab</a>. In 1966, the city came under the administration of <a href="/wiki/Haryana" title="Haryana">Haryana</a> with the creation of the new state.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gurugram is located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gurugram_district" title="Gurugram district">Gurugram district</a> in the Indian state of Haryana and is situated in the south eastern part of the state, and <a href="/wiki/North_India" title="North India">northern part</a> of the country. The city is located on the border with Delhi with New Delhi to its north east. The city has a total area of 738.8 square kilometres (285.3 sq mi)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Topography">Topography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Topography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The average land elevation is 217 metres (712 ft) above sea level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>, Gurugram experiences a <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a>-influenced <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (Cwa).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotel_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-hotel-14">[14]</a></sup> The city experiences four distinct seasons - spring (February - March), summer (April - August), fall/autumn (September - October) and winter (November - January), along with the monsoon season setting in towards the later half of the summer. Summers, from early April to mid October, are typically hot and humid, with an average daily June high temperature of 40 °C (104 °F). The season experiences heat indices easily breaking 43 °C (109 °F). Winters are cold and foggy with few sunny days, and with a December daytime average of 3 °C (37 °F). The <a href="/wiki/Western_Disturbance" title="Western Disturbance">Western Disturbance</a> brings some rain in winters that further adds to the chill. Spring and autumn are mild and pleasant seasons with low humidity. The monsoon season usually starts in the first week of July and continues till August. Thunderstorms are not uncommon during the Monsoon. The average annual rainfall is approximately 714 millimetres (28.1 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotel_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-hotel-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>Gurugram municipal corporation area has an estimated population of 876,824 as per 2011 India census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1lakh_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-1lakh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Gurugram has architecturally noteworthy buildings in a wide range of styles and from distinct time period. Gurugram's skyline with its many skyscrapers is nationally recognized, and the city has been home to several tall buildings with modern planning. Gurugram has an estimated 1,100 residential highrises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ht1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-ht1-15">[15]</a></sup> The average cost of a 93-square-metre (1,000 sq ft) two-bedroom apartment at a decent condominium in Gurugram is at least $160,130 (₹10,000,000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ht1_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-ht1-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>Gurugram is divided into 36 wards, with each ward further divided into blocks. The housing type in the city consists largely of attached housing, though a large number of attached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multi-dwelling_unit" title="Multi-dwelling unit">multi-dwelling units</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Apartment" title="Apartment">apartments</a>, condominiums and <a href="/wiki/Tower_block" title="Tower block">high rise residential towers</a> are getting popular. The top five condominiums in the city, as rated by <a href="/wiki/The_Times_of_India" title="The Times of India">The Times of India</a>, are: Aralias, Hamilton Court, The World Spa, Gurugram One and Raheja Atlantis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parks">Parks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Parks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gurugram has a complex park system, with various lands operated by the <a href="/wiki/Haryana_Urban_Development_Authority" title="Haryana Urban Development Authority">Haryana Urban Development Authority</a>. The key parks are Leisure Valley Park in Sector 29, which is spread over 15 hectares (36 acres); Tau Devi Lal Biodiversity Botanical Garden in Sector 52; Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Park in Sector 14, popularly known as HUDA Garden; Tau Devi Lal park in Sector 23; and Aravali Biodiversity Park on MG Road. However, most of the parks in Gurugram are small and ill-maintained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages_and_dialect">Languages and dialect</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Languages and dialect">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The main language spoken in Gurugram is <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>, though a segment of the population understands and speaks the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. The dialect used in Hindi is similar to that of Delhi, and is considered neutral, though the regional influences from the states of Haryana, <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bihar" title="Bihar">Bihar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_India" title="Punjab, India">Punjab</a> adds an accent to the language. English is spoken with an Indian accent, with a primarily North Indian influence. Since Gurugram has a large number of international <a href="/wiki/Call_centre" title="Call centre">call centres</a>, the employees are usually given formal training in neutral pronunciation in order to be understandable to native English speakers. <a href="/wiki/Haryanvi_language" title="Haryanvi language">Haryanvi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_language" title="Punjabi language">Punjabi</a> are other popular languages spoken in the city. The other regional languages include <a href="/wiki/Mewati_language" title="Mewati language">Mewati</a> and Haryanvi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> is a predominant religion among the city's population. Gurugram also includes adherents of <a href="/wiki/Sikhism" title="Sikhism">Sikhism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jainism" title="Jainism">Jainism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá'í Faith">Bahá'í</a>, among others. There are several places of worship for major religions in Gurugram, including <a href="/wiki/Hindu_temple" title="Hindu temple">Hindu temples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gurdwara" title="Gurdwara">gurdwaras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosques</a> and <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">churches</a>.</p>
<p>Sheetla Mata Mandir is a famous temple located in district Gurugram of Haryana state of India. It is a temple dedicated to the wife of Guru Dronacharya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The temple hosts fairs regularly and huge number of people come to seek blessings of Sheetla Mata every year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city has two major sports stadiums: <a href="/wiki/Tau_Devi_Lal_Stadium" title="Tau Devi Lal Stadium">Tau Devi Lal Stadium</a> in Sector 38, which has facilities for <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>, <a href="/wiki/Football" title="Football">football</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">athletics</a> as well as a sports hostel, and <a href="/wiki/Nehru_Stadium,_Gurgaon" title="Nehru Stadium, Gurgaon">Nehru Stadium</a> which is designed for football and athletics. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amity_United_FC" title="Amity United FC">Amity United FC</a> is a tenant of Tau Devi Lal Stadium. Gurugram district has nine golf courses, and is described as the "heart of India's golfing country".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joginder_Rao" title="Joginder Rao">Joginder Rao</a>, a domestic cricket player was from Gurugram.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gurgoan5.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2472" data-file-width="3296" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Gurgoan5.JPG/260px-Gurgoan5.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Gurgoan5.JPG/390px-Gurgoan5.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Gurgoan5.JPG/520px-Gurgoan5.JPG 2x" width="260"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gurgoan5.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>Once a small dusty agricultural village, Gurugram has now emerged to become the city with the third highest per capita income in India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-percapita3_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-percapita3-2">[2]</a></sup> Popularly known as Millennium City,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Gurugram has the presence of about 250 or 50% of the Fortune 500 companies. Gurugram's proximity to Delhi means easy access to political decision makers. Maruti Suzuki Private Limited was the first company that set up a manufacturing unit in the city in 1970s making cars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-3">[3]</a></sup> Eventually, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DLF_Limited" title="DLF Limited">DLF Limited</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Real_estate" title="Real estate">real estate</a> company acquired vast stretches of land in the city. The first major American brand to set up a unit in Gurugram was <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> in 1997. GE's setup in Gurugram prompted other companies, both international as well as domestic, to follow suit. Today, Gurugram has emerged as one of the most important offshoring centers in the world,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc1_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc1-23">[23]</a></sup> providing outsourcing solutions in software, IT, service and sales through delivery facilities and call centers. However, due to the lack of proper public transport and the inability of most of the employees to afford a personal vehicle, most of the call centers provide pooled-in cars to and from their offices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc1_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc1-23">[23]</a></sup> Apart from <a href="/wiki/Business_process_outsourcing" title="Business process outsourcing">Business process outsourcing</a> and IT sectors, the city is home to several other companies that specialize in domain expertise. <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens Industry Software</a>, in Gurugram Business Park, made a portfolio of design software that was used by <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> to digitally design, simulate and assemble the vehicle before any physical prototypes were built.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc1_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbc1-23">[23]</a></sup> Various international companies, including <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agilent_Technologies" title="Agilent Technologies">Agilent Technologies</a>, have chosen Gurugram to be their Indian corporate headquarters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc1_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-bbc1-23">[23]</a></sup> All the major companies in the city depend on their own backup, given the fact that Gurugram does not have reliable power and water supply, public transport and utilities. <a href="/wiki/Retail" title="Retail">Retail</a> is an important industry in Gurugram, with the presence of 26 shopping malls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Real estate is a major force in the city's economy. Gurugram is home to some of the nation's most valuable real estate.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/All_Nippon_Airways" title="All Nippon Airways">All Nippon Airways</a>, a Japanese airline, maintains its Delhi sales office in the Time Tower in Gurugram.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_government">Law and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Law and government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gurugram is governed by the Municipal Corporation of Gurugram which follows a Mayor-Council system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crime">Crime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Crime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The crime level is rising in Gurugram. The city reported 89 homicides in 2012. Another growing concern is the increase in number of rapes, robberies, assaults, burglaries, and thefts, including motor vehicle thefts. The total vehicle thefts registered by the police in Gurugram in 2014 was 3,638.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The police department in Gurugram is headed by the Commissioner of Police - Gurugram Police, which forms a part of the <a href="/wiki/Haryana_Police" title="Haryana Police">Haryana Police</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and reports to the Haryana state government. Sandeep Khirwar (IPS) is the Commissioner of Police in Gurugram. Gurugram Police has a separate traffic police department headquartered in sector 51.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Fire protection within the city limits is provided by Municipal Corporation of Gurugram through four fire stations, located in sector 29, sector 37, Udyog Vihar and Bhim Nagar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<p>The city's public school system is managed by the government of Haryana, and administered by <a href="/wiki/Haryana_Board_of_School_Education" title="Haryana Board of School Education">Haryana Board of School Education</a>. The city also has a large number of private schools, where education is often expensive and the quality usually better than the government schools. Schools like <a href="/wiki/The_Shri_Ram_School" title="The Shri Ram School">The Shri Ram School - Aravali</a>, the Ardee World School, Sector 52, The Heritage School, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dayanand_Anglo-Vedic_Schools_System" title="Dayanand Anglo-Vedic Schools System">DAV Public School</a> Sector 14 and <a href="/wiki/Amity_International_School,_Gurgaon" title="Amity International School, Gurgaon">Amity International School</a> are among the top 10 schools in the city, according to the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Hindustan_Times" title="Hindustan Times">Hindustan Times</a> - C fore Top Schools Survey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are several universities and institutes located in Gurugram and its nearby areas, that form a part of Gurugram district including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ansal_Institute_of_Technology" title="Ansal Institute of Technology">Ansal Institute of Technology</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITM_University" title="ITM University">ITM University</a>, Sector 23A; GD Goenka University, Sohna Road; <a href="/wiki/KR_Mangalam_University" title="KR Mangalam University">KR Mangalam University</a>, Sohna Road; <a href="/wiki/Amity_University" title="Amity University">Amity University</a>, Manesar; <a href="/wiki/Apeejay_Education_Society" title="Apeejay Education Society">Apeejay Stya University</a>, Sohna; <a href="/wiki/BML_Munjal_University" title="BML Munjal University">BML Munjal University</a>, NH8; <a href="/wiki/Shree_Guru_Gobind_Singh_Tricentenary_University" title="Shree Guru Gobind Singh Tricentenary University">Shree Guru Gobind Singh Tricentenary University</a>, Budhera;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/National_Brain_Research_Centre" title="National Brain Research Centre">National Brain Research Centre</a>, Manesar. Gurugram is also home to one of India's top ranked business schools, <a href="/wiki/Management_Development_Institute" title="Management Development Institute">Management Development Institute</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roadways">Roadways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Roadways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The major highway that links Gurugram is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Highway_8" title="National Highway 8">National Highway 8</a>, the road that runs from Delhi to Mumbai. While the 27.7-kilometre (17.2 mi) Delhi-Gurugram border-Kherki Dhaula stretch has been developed as an expressway, the rest is expanded to six lanes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The second Highway Is Dwarka Expressway, Which Starts from Gurugram and linked to New Delhi's various major Inter cities.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Intercity_rail">Intercity rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Intercity rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Gurgaon_railway_station" title="Gurgaon railway station">Gurugram railway station</a> operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Railway_Zone_(India)" title="Northern Railway Zone (India)">Northern Railway</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indian_Railways" title="Indian Railways">Indian Railways</a>. The rail station that forms a part of the larger Indian railways network, where trains connect Gurugram to <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> and other important cities in India like <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ahmedabad" title="Ahmedabad">Ahmadabad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaipur" title="Jaipur">Jaipur</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Delhi_Metro">Delhi Metro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Delhi Metro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>There are five stations served by <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Metro" title="Delhi Metro">Delhi Metro</a> located on the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Line_(Delhi_Metro)" title="Yellow Line (Delhi Metro)">Yellow Line</a>, which are <a href="/wiki/HUDA_City_Centre_metro_station" title="HUDA City Centre metro station">HUDA City Centre</a>, <a href="/wiki/IFFCO_Chowk_metro_station" title="IFFCO Chowk metro station">IFFCO Chowk</a>, <a href="/wiki/MG_Road_metro_station" title="MG Road metro station">MG Road</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sikandarpur_metro_station" title="Sikandarpur metro station">Sikanderpur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guru_Dronacharya_metro_station" title="Guru Dronacharya metro station">Guru Droncharya</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rapid_Metro">Rapid Metro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Rapid Metro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ggnm.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="194" data-file-width="259" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Ggnm.jpg/220px-Ggnm.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e0/Ggnm.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ggnm.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Rapid_MetroRail_Gurgaon" title="Rapid MetroRail Gurgaon">Rapid Metro Gurugram</a> near <a href="/wiki/Phase_2_metro_station" title="Phase 2 metro station">Phase 2</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Currently <a href="/wiki/Rapid_MetroRail_Gurgaon" title="Rapid MetroRail Gurgaon">Rapid Metro Gurugram</a> have six stations, connecting <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Line_(Delhi_Metro)" title="Yellow Line (Delhi Metro)">Yellow Line</a> of <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Metro" title="Delhi Metro">Delhi Metro</a> at <a href="/wiki/Sikandarpur_metro_station" title="Sikandarpur metro station">Sikandarpur metro station</a>, which are <a href="/wiki/Sikandarpur_metro_station" title="Sikandarpur metro station">Sikanderpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phase_2_metro_station" title="Phase 2 metro station">Phase 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vodafone_Belvedere_Towers_metro_station" title="Vodafone Belvedere Towers metro station">Vodafone Belvedere Towers</a>, <a href="/wiki/IndusInd_Bank_Cyber_City_metro_station" title="IndusInd Bank Cyber City metro station">IndusInd Bank Cyber City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Micromax_Moulsari_Avenue_metro_station" title="Micromax Moulsari Avenue metro station">Micormax Moulsari Avenue</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phase_3_metro_station" title="Phase 3 metro station">Phase 3</a>. Two stations, Shankar Chowk and Gateway tower, are scheduled to become operational soon. The first phase of Rapid Metro became operational in November 2013 and covers a distance of 3.3 mi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Two more phases of the project are in the pipeline and would take the total number of subway stations in Gurugram to 16. An estimated 33,000 people ride Rapid Metro everyday, which provides an exclusive elevated transit service with three coach trains that run in a loop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airways">Airways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Airways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Airport">Airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Gurugram is served by <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi_International_Airport" title="Indira Gandhi International Airport">Indira Gandhi International Airport</a>, though the airport is just outside the city limits and located within the jurisdiction of Delhi near National Highway 8. The airport is one of the busiest airports in India and provides domestic and international air connectivity.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transit_systems">Transit systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Transit systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transit">Public transit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Public transit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In November, 2013, Gurugram launched an <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ciclovia" title="Ciclovia">Ciclovia</a></i>-inspired initiative known as <i>Raahgiri Day</i>—in which a corridor of streets are closed to motor vehicle traffic on Sunday mornings to encourage the use of non-motorized transport and participation in outdoor leisure activities. Gurugram was the first city in India to implement such a program, followed by New Delhi, and later Noida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timesofindia-raahgiri_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-timesofindia-raahgiri-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gurugram is also expected to get India's first <a href="/wiki/Personal_rapid_transit" title="Personal rapid transit">Pod Taxis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Utilities">Utilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurgaon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Utilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Electricity in Gurugram is provided by government owned <a href="/wiki/Dakshin_Haryana_Bijli_Vitran_Nigam" title="Dakshin Haryana Bijli Vitran Nigam">Dakshin Haryana Bijli Vitran Nigam</a>. Gurugram has power consumer base of 360,000 with average power load of 700-800 MW.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> There are frequent power outages in the city, especially during the peak consumption season of summer. Apart from the power deficit, the equipment used by the power department like transformers, panels and transmission lines is either old or overburdened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
