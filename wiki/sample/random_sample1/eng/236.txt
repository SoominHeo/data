<p>The <b>1960 Summer Olympics medal table</b> is a list of <a href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Committee" title="National Olympic Committee">National Olympic Committees</a> ranked by the number of <a href="/wiki/Medal" title="Medal">medals</a> won during the <a href="/wiki/1960_Summer_Olympics" title="1960 Summer Olympics">1960 Summer Olympics</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> from August 25 to September 11, 1960. A total of 5,338 athletes from 83 countries participated in these Games, competing in 150 events in 17 <a href="/wiki/Olympic_sports" title="Olympic sports">sports</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympic.org_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-olympic.org-1">[1]</a></sup> The bronze medal Iraq's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdul_Wahid_Aziz" title="Abdul Wahid Aziz">Abdul Wahid Aziz</a> won in men's lightweight weightlifting is the only Olympic medal (Summer or Winter) Iraq has ever won, currently the longest drought for any country who has won at least one Olympic medal.</p>
<p>Athletes from 44 countries won at least one medal, leaving 39 countries in blank in the medal table. The <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> was the country which conquered the highest number of gold medals (43), silver medals (29), bronze medals (31) and overall medals (103). <a href="/wiki/British_West_Indies_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="British West Indies at the 1960 Summer Olympics">British West Indies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Republic of China at the 1960 Summer Olympics">Republic of China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Ethiopia at the 1960 Summer Olympics">Ethiopia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghana_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Ghana at the 1960 Summer Olympics">Ghana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Iraq at the 1960 Summer Olympics">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Morocco at the 1960 Summer Olympics">Morocco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Singapore_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Singapore at the 1960 Summer Olympics">Singapore</a> won the first medals in their Olympic history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Report-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medal_table">Medal table</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1960_Summer_Olympics_medal_table&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Medal table">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ranking in this table is based on information provided by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IOC_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-4">[4]</a></sup> although that organization does not officially recognize global ranking per country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympic.org_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-olympic.org-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The countries are ranked by the number of gold medals won by the athletes of that country. If countries are tied, first the number of silver medals is taken into consideration and then the number of bronze medals. If, after the above, countries are still tied, equal ranking is given and they are listed alphabetically.</p>
<p>A total of 150 events in 17 sports were awarded in Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics" title="Gymnastics">gymnastics</a>, two extra gold medals were awarded in the men's <a href="/wiki/Pommel_horse" title="Pommel horse">pommel horse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vault_(gymnastics)" title="Vault (gymnastics)">long horse vault</a> events because of ties and one additional bronze medal was awarded in the <a href="/wiki/Rings_(gymnastics)" title="Rings (gymnastics)">Rings</a> event. Because of this, two fewer silver medals were awarded, though an extra silver was awarded in the women's <a href="/wiki/High_jump" title="High jump">high jump</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a> events, two bronze medals were awarded in each weight class, so the total number of bronze medals is greater than the total number of gold and silver medals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
