<p>The <b>Kumamoto Domain</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">熊本藩</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kumamoto-han</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, also known as <b>Higo Domain</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">肥後藩</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Higo-han</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_(Japan)" title="Han (Japan)">domain</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. It was associated with <a href="/wiki/Higo_Province" title="Higo Province">Higo Province</a> in modern-day <a href="/wiki/Kumamoto_Prefecture" title="Kumamoto Prefecture">Kumamoto Prefecture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-explorer_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-explorer-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Han_system" title="Han system">han system</a>, Kumamoto was a <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">political</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economic</a> abstraction based on periodic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cadastral" title="Cadastral">cadastral</a> surveys and projected agricultural yields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In other words, the domain was defined in terms of <i><a href="/wiki/Kokudaka" title="Kokudaka">kokudaka</a></i>, not land area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This was different from the <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudalism</a> of the West.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kumamoto_Domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The domain was centered at the Kumamoto Castle in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kumamoto,_Kumamoto" title="Kumamoto, Kumamoto">Kumamoto</a>.</p>
<p>Under the Hosokawa, with an income of 540,000 koku, the Kumamoto domain was one of the largest in <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a>, second only to the <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Domain" title="Satsuma Domain">Satsuma Domain</a>, and excluding the lands held by the Tokugawa and Matsudaira clans, the fourth-largest in Japan after the <a href="/wiki/Kaga_Domain" title="Kaga Domain">Kaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Domain" title="Satsuma Domain">Satsuma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sendai_Domain" title="Sendai Domain">Sendai</a> domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
