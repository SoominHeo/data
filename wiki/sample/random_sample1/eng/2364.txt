<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kumaso</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Kumaso</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">熊襲</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> were a people of ancient <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, believed to have lived in the south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> until at least the <a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">Nara period</a>. <a href="/wiki/William_George_Aston" title="William George Aston">William George Aston</a>, in his translation of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nihongi" title="Nihongi">Nihongi</a></i>, says <i>Kumaso</i> refers to two separate tribes, <i>Kuma</i> (meaning "bear") and <i>So</i> (written with the character for "attack" or "layer on").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In his translation of the <i><a href="/wiki/Kojiki" title="Kojiki">Kojiki</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Basil_Hall_Chamberlain" title="Basil Hall Chamberlain">Basil Hall Chamberlain</a> records that the region is also known simply as <i><a href="/wiki/Soo_District,_Kagoshima" title="Soo District, Kagoshima">So district</a></i>, and elaborates on the <i>Yamato</i>-centric description of a "bear-like" people, based on their violent interactions or physical distinctiveness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (The people called <i><a href="/wiki/Tsuchigumo" title="Tsuchigumo">tsuchigumo</a></i> by the Yamato people provide a better-known example of the transformation of other tribes into legendary monsters. <i>Tsuchigumo</i>—the monstrous "ground spider" of legend—is speculated to refer originally to the native pit dwellings of that people.) As the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yamato_(people)" title="Yamato (people)">Yamato</a> pushed southward, the Kumaso people were either assimilated or exterminated. The last leader of the Kumaso, Torishi-Kaya, aka Brave of Kahakami, was assassinated in the winter of 397 by Prince <a href="/wiki/Yamato_Takeru" title="Yamato Takeru">Yamato Takeru</a> of Yamato,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> who was disguised for this as a woman at a banquet.</p>
<p>Geographically, Aston records that the Kumaso domain encompassed the historical provinces of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hy%C5%ABga_province" title="Hyūga province">Hyūga</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%8Csumi_province" title="Ōsumi province">Ōsumi</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satsuma_province" title="Satsuma province">Satsuma</a> (contemporaneous with Aston's translation), or present-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miyazaki_prefecture" title="Miyazaki prefecture">Miyazaki</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kagoshima_prefecture" title="Kagoshima prefecture">Kagoshima</a> prefectures.</p>
<p>The word Kuma ('Bear') survives today as <a href="/wiki/Kumamoto_Prefecture" title="Kumamoto Prefecture">Kumamoto Prefecture</a> ('source of the bear'), and <a href="/wiki/Kuma_District,_Kumamoto" title="Kuma District, Kumamoto">Kuma District, Kumamoto</a>. Kuma District is known for a distinct dialect, Kuma Dialect.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People_of_the_Kumaso_mentioned_in_the_Nihongi">People of the Kumaso mentioned in the Nihongi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kumaso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: People of the Kumaso mentioned in the Nihongi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Torishi-Kaya (aka Brave of Kahakami): a leader of the Kumaso<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Atsukaya: a leader of the Kumaso<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Sakaya: a leader of the Kumaso<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>Ichi-fukaya: <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Keik%C5%8D" title="Emperor Keikō">Emperor Keikō</a> married her 82 AD and in the same year put her to death, since she was involved in the assassination which killed her father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>Ichi-kaya: younger sister of Ichi-fukaya<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
