<p><b>Kusangi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">草薙駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kusanagi eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">train station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shimizu-ku,_Shizuoka" title="Shimizu-ku, Shizuoka">Shimizu-ku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka,_Shizuoka" title="Shizuoka, Shizuoka">Shizuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka_Prefecture" title="Shizuoka Prefecture">Shizuoka Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is operated by <a href="/wiki/Central_Japan_Railway_Company" title="Central Japan Railway Company">Central Japan Railway Company</a> (JR Central). The station is 174.2 rail kilometers from <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_history">Station history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kusanagi_Station_(JR_Central)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Station history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Signal_box" title="Signal box">signal box</a> was established on the site of the present Kusanagi Station on April 10, 1911. Kusanagi Station was opened on April 3, 1926. From 1930, the station had both passenger and freight operations; however, freight operations were discontinued from 1967. The current station building dates from 1973. Kusanagi Station serves the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Shizuoka" title="University of Shizuoka">University of Shizuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka_Prefectural_Museum_of_Art" title="Shizuoka Prefectural Museum of Art">Shizuoka Prefectural Museum of Art</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shizuoka_Prefectural_Central_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shizuoka Prefectural Central Library (page does not exist)">Shizuoka Prefectural Central Library</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shizuoka_Railway_Shizuoka-Shimizu_Line" title="Shizuoka Railway Shizuoka-Shimizu Line">Shizuoka-Shimizu</a> local railway line's via <a href="/wiki/Kusanagi_Station_(Shizuoka_Railway)" title="Kusanagi Station (Shizuoka Railway)">Kusanagi Station</a> is a three-minute walk away.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kusanagi_Station_(JR_Central)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kusanagi Station has two opposing <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a> serving Track 1 and Track 2, with <a href="/wiki/Headshunt" title="Headshunt">headshunts</a>, allowing for tracks for express trains to pass in between, and an <a href="/wiki/Overpass" title="Overpass">overpass</a> connecting the platforms. The station building has automated <a href="/wiki/Ticket_machine" title="Ticket machine">ticket machines</a>, <a href="/wiki/TOICA" title="TOICA">TOICA</a> automated <a href="/wiki/Turnstile" title="Turnstile">turnstiles</a> and a manned "Green Window" service counter.</p>
