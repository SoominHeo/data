<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savior&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mahdi" title="Mahdi">Mahdi</a>, the prophesied redeemer of Islam who will rule for seven, nine or nineteen years</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Maitreya" title="Maitreya">Maitreya</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Messiah" title="Messiah">Messiah</a>, a saviour or liberator of a group of people, most commonly in the Abrahamic religions
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Messiah_in_Judaism" title="Messiah in Judaism">Messiah in Judaism</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christ" title="Christ">Messiah in Christianity</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Redeemer_(Christianity)" title="Redeemer (Christianity)">Redeemer (Christianity)</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Zoroastrian tradition envisions three future saviors, including <a href="/wiki/Saoshyant" title="Saoshyant">Saoshyant</a>, [sou-shyuhnt] a figure of Zoroastrian eschatology who brings about the final renovation of the world, the Frashokereti</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Soter" title="Soter">Soter</a>, derives from the Greek epithet <i>σωτήρ</i> (sōtēr), meaning a saviour, a deliverer</li>
</ul>
