<p><b>Kushima</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">串間市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kushima-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_Prefecture" title="Miyazaki Prefecture">Miyazaki Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The city was founded on November 3, 1954.</p>
<p>As of 2003, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 22,727 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 77.06 persons per km². The total area is 294.91 km².</p>
<p>The city is served by the <a href="/wiki/Nichinan_Line" title="Nichinan Line">Nichinan Line</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JR_Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="JR Kyūshū">JR Kyūshū</a> railway system, which links it to the west to <a href="/wiki/Shibushi,_Kagoshima" title="Shibushi, Kagoshima">Shibushi</a> in neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Prefecture" title="Kagoshima Prefecture">Kagoshima Prefecture</a> and to the north to the prefectural capital, <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyazaki" title="Miyazaki, Miyazaki">Miyazaki</a>.</p>
<p>Within the boundaries of the municipality (but some distance from its main built-up areas) lies the island of <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Djima" title="Kōjima">Kōjima</a>, known for the field studies site of the <a href="/wiki/Primate_Research_Institute" title="Primate Research Institute">Primate Research Institute</a>, where studies on wild-living <a href="/wiki/Japanese_macaque" title="Japanese macaque">Japanese macaque</a> monkeys are carried out.</p>
