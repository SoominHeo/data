<p><b>Kuwŏlsan</b>, or <b>Mount Kuwol</b>, is a mountain in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Hwanghae" title="South Hwanghae">South Hwanghae</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. The mountain takes its name from the ninth month of the <a href="/wiki/Lunar_calendar" title="Lunar calendar">lunar calendar</a>, because it is considered particularly attractive in that month. The mountain is a major summer resort in North Korea, attracting many domestic tourists.</p>
<p>Kuwolsan is home to the Sansong Revolutionary Site, the 9th century <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anak_Woljongsa" title="Anak Woljongsa">Woljong Temple</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Stupa" title="Stupa">stupas</a>, as well as the 4th century <a href="/wiki/Anak_Tomb_No._3" title="Anak Tomb No. 3">Anak Tomb No. 3</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuwolsan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The highest peak of the mountain - the highest point in the short Kuwol range - is 954 m above sea level. There are several scenic routes through the mountain area. Natural attractions include rock formations, waterfalls and natural pools. Much of the mountain is covered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate_broadleaf_and_mixed_forests" title="Temperate broadleaf and mixed forests">mixed broadleaf and coniferous forest</a> and protected in an 18,000 ha <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national park</a>. Some 1100 ha has been identified by <a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">BirdLife International</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Important_Bird_Area" title="Important Bird Area">Important Bird Area</a> (IBA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bli_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bli-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
