<p><b>Gujō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">郡上市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gujō-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Gifu_Prefecture" title="Gifu Prefecture">Gifu Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of July 2011, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 43,960 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 43 persons per km². The total area is 1,030.79 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guj%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The modern city of Gujō was established on March 1, 2004, from the merger of the towns of <a href="/wiki/Hachiman,_Gifu" title="Hachiman, Gifu">Hachiman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shirotori,_Gifu" title="Shirotori, Gifu">Shirotori</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yamato,_Gifu" title="Yamato, Gifu">Yamato</a>, and the villages of <a href="/wiki/Meih%C5%8D,_Gifu" title="Meihō, Gifu">Meihō</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minami,_Gifu" title="Minami, Gifu">Minami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Takasu,_Gifu" title="Takasu, Gifu">Takasu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wara,_Gifu" title="Wara, Gifu">Wara</a> (all from <a href="/wiki/Guj%C5%8D_District,_Gifu" title="Gujō District, Gifu">Gujō District</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-merger_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-merger-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Waterways">Waterways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guj%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Waterways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gujō's famous pristine waterways operate the same way as they did in the 17th century. The canals, fountains, and waterways are still used for washing rice, vegetables, and laundry. Townspeople cooperate to keep the canals clean and the water fresh; as a result of their efforts, Gujō's drinking water is a source of local pride.</p>
<p>Gujō Hachiman is in a valley where three major fast-running rivers meet: the Yoshida, Nagara and Kodara.</p>
<p>Visitors come to enjoy the pure mineral water that flows from every tap in town. The clean water and sound environmental practices translate into good food and drink. <a href="/wiki/Ayu" title="Ayu">Ayu</a>, soba, and sake all depend on water for their flavor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gujohachiman.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gujohachiman.com-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some residents can be seen using the town's unique system of small waterways to wash laundry and dishes following a very strict set of rules that describe what may be washed where. This practice has survived for centuries and ensures that all households have access to clean water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gujohachiman.com_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-gujohachiman.com-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Guj.C5.8D_Odori">Gujō Odori</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guj%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gujō Odori">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

Gujō Odori</div>

<p>The famous dance festival — <a href="/wiki/Guj%C5%8D_Odori" title="Gujō Odori">Gujō Odori</a> — attracts many visitors. The festival started over 400 years ago and continues today. During the four days of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Obon" title="Obon">Obon</a> in mid-August, dances continue all night.</p>
<p>The dance begins on the same night as Kyoto's <a href="/wiki/Gion_Matsuri" title="Gion Matsuri">Gion Festival</a> and continues for 30 nights. It begins at the Yasaka Shrine and moves to another one each night. The Gujō Odori Preservation Society tell musical stories through an "ohayashi" which consists of a soloist, a <a href="/wiki/Shamisen" title="Shamisen">shamisen</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Taiko" title="Taiko">taiko</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Shakuhachi" title="Shakuhachi">shakuhachi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city.gujo.gifu.jp_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-city.gujo.gifu.jp-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Listeners participate by dancing around the stage. During "Urabon" (August 13 to 16) the dancing continues until 5:00am. More than 20,000 visitors come to town for the odori.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city.gujo.gifu.jp_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-city.gujo.gifu.jp-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_replicas">Food replicas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guj%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Food replicas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gujō is a leading producer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fake_food_in_Japan" title="Fake food in Japan">food replicas</a> in Japan. Many of the food replicas, used by restaurants to decorate their windows and inform patrons of their dishes, are produced here.</p>
