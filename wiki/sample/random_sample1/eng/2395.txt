<p><b>Kuji</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">久慈郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kuji-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Ibaraki_Prefecture" title="Ibaraki Prefecture">Ibaraki Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Town">Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuji_District,_Ibaraki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Daigo,_Ibaraki" title="Daigo, Ibaraki">Daigo</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger">Merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuji_District,_Ibaraki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>On December 1, 2004 the town of <a href="/wiki/Kanasag%C5%8D,_Ibaraki" title="Kanasagō, Ibaraki">Kanasagō</a>, and the villages of <a href="/wiki/Satomi,_Ibaraki" title="Satomi, Ibaraki">Satomi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suifu,_Ibaraki" title="Suifu, Ibaraki">Suifu</a> merged into the expanded city of <a href="/wiki/Hitachi%C5%8Dta,_Ibaraki" title="Hitachiōta, Ibaraki">Hitachiōta</a>.</li>
</ul>
