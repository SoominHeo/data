<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kuki Yoshitaka</h1>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">In this <a href="/wiki/Japanese_name" title="Japanese name">Japanese name</a>, the family name is Kuki.</div>
<p><b>Kuki Yoshitaka <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">九鬼 嘉隆</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></b> (1542 – November 17, 1600) was a naval commander during Japan's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sengoku" title="Sengoku">Sengoku</a> Period, under <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a>, and later, <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyoshi" title="Toyotomi Hideyoshi">Toyotomi Hideyoshi</a>. He was also the ninth headmaster of the Kuki family's school of martial arts, <a href="/wiki/Kukishin-ry%C5%AB" title="Kukishin-ryū">Kukishin-ryū</a> and thus a very skilled warrior.</p>
<p>In the 1570s, Kuki allied himself with <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a>, and commanded his fleet, supporting land-based attacks on the <a href="/wiki/Ikk%C5%8D-ikki" title="Ikkō-ikki">Ikkō-ikki</a>. In 1574, his aid ensured a victory for <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Nobunaga</a> in his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Nagashima" title="Siege of Nagashima">third attempt</a> to attack the <a href="/wiki/Nagashima" title="Nagashima">Nagashima</a> fortress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1576, he was defeated at <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Kizugawaguchi" title="Battles of Kizugawaguchi">Kizugawaguchi</a> by the <a href="/wiki/M%C5%8Dri_clan" title="Mōri clan">Mōri clan</a> fleet, but 1578 brought victory in the second <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Kizugawaguchi" title="Battles of Kizugawaguchi">Battle of Kizugawaguchi</a>, in which Kuki used 'iron ships' to repel the arrows and musket balls of the opposing Mōri clan's ships.</p>
<p>In 1587, he led <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyoshi" title="Toyotomi Hideyoshi">Toyotomi Hideyoshi's</a> fleet in a campaign in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Konishi_Yukinaga" title="Konishi Yukinaga">Konishi Yukinaga</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakizaka_Yasuharu" title="Wakizaka Yasuharu">Wakizaka Yasuharu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kat%C5%8D_Yoshiaki" title="Katō Yoshiaki">Katō Yoshiaki</a>. Three years later, along with Wakizaka Yasuharu and Kato Yoshiaki he went on to lead the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Odawara_(1590)" title="Siege of Odawara (1590)">Siege of Odawara</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Shimoda" title="Siege of Shimoda">Siege of Shimoda</a>. He continued in his role as commander of Hideyoshi's fleet, launching an invasion of <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> in 1592 from his flagship <i>Nipponmaru</i>. He was severely defeated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Myeongryang" title="Battle of Myeongryang">Battle of Myeongryang</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sekigahara" title="Battle of Sekigahara">Battle of Sekigahara</a>, Kuki Yoshitaka fought alongside the Toyotomi forces, while his son <a href="/wiki/Kuki_Moritaka" title="Kuki Moritaka">Kuki Moritaka</a> joined the opposing force, under <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a>. Following Tokugawa's victory, his son successfully guaranteed Yoshitaka's safety from Ieyasu. In a turn of fate, Yoshitaka committed <a href="/wiki/Seppuku" title="Seppuku">seppuku</a> before the news reached him.</p>
