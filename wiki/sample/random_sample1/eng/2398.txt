<p><i><b>A Goofy Movie</b></i> is a 1995 American <a href="/wiki/Animation" title="Animation">animated</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">musical</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Road_film" title="Road film">road</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/DisneyToon_Studios" title="DisneyToon Studios">DisneyToon Studios</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disney_Television_Animation" title="Disney Television Animation">Walt Disney Television Animation</a>. The film features characters from <a href="/wiki/The_Disney_Afternoon" title="The Disney Afternoon">The Disney Afternoon</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Goof_Troop" title="Goof Troop">Goof Troop</a></i>; the film itself acts as a <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> to the TV show. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Lima" title="Kevin Lima">Kevin Lima</a>, the film's plot revolves around the father-son relationship between <a href="/wiki/Goofy" title="Goofy">Goofy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Goof" title="Max Goof">Max</a> as Goofy believes that he's losing Max.</p>
<p>The film was released theatrically on April 7, 1995 by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Studios_Motion_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures">Buena Vista Pictures Distribution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Pictures">Walt Disney Pictures</a> to mixed reviews from critics and moderate box office success. The film was dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Pat_Buttram" title="Pat Buttram">Pat Buttram</a>, who died during production. A <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/An_Extremely_Goofy_Movie" title="An Extremely Goofy Movie">A Goofy Movie 2: An Extremely Goofy Movie</a></i>, was released in February 2000.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Goofy" title="Goofy">Goofy</a> is the single father of a teenage boy named <a href="/wiki/Max_Goof" title="Max Goof">Max Goof</a> in the town of Spoonerville, Ohio, though the two have a tense relationship. On the last day of school before summer vacation, Max and his best friends <a href="/wiki/Pete_Junior" title="Pete Junior">P.J.</a> and Robert "Bobby" Zimmeruski hijack the auditorium stage in the middle of Principal Mazur's speech, creating a small concert where Max performs, while costumed as the pop singer Powerline. The performance succeeds in making Max a school celebrity and impressing his love interest, Roxanne; but he, P.J. and Bobby are sent to Mazur's office. Roxanne speaks with Max and agrees to go with him to a party where Powerline's concert will be aired live. However, Mazur exaggerates these events to Goofy and forewarns him that Max's actions may result in him facing <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">capital punishment</a>.</p>
<p>Goofy decides to take Max on a fishing trip to Lake Destiny, Idaho, following a map route he and his father took years ago, and the two go into his <a href="/wiki/Station_wagon" title="Station wagon">station wagon</a>. However, he is oblivious to what Max is planning to do with Roxanne. Max stops by Roxanne's house to call off their date, but when Roxanne says she will just have to go with someone else, Max instead fabricates a story about his father knowing Powerline; he tells her he will be on stage at the concert.</p>
<p>Despite his son's objections, Goofy plans his own trip, with initially disastrous results. Max hurts his father's feelings after his father humiliates him at an <a href="/wiki/Opossum" title="Opossum">opossum</a>-based theme park. While camping, <a href="/wiki/Pete_(Disney)" title="Pete (Disney)">Pete</a> and P.J. join them. Following Pete's advice to keep Max under control, Goofy takes his son fishing and performs the Perfect Cast fishing technique, luring <a href="/wiki/Bigfoot" title="Bigfoot">Bigfoot</a> to their camp. Pete and P.J. flee, leaving Goofy and Max to spend the night with Bigfoot. At night, while Goofy is still asleep, Max alters the map route to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, where the concert is taking place.</p>
<p>The next morning, Goofy decides to make Max the navigator of the trip. The two go to several locations that satisfy both of them. They stop by a motel where they meet Pete and P.J. again. When Pete overhears a conversation between Max and P.J., he tells Goofy that Max has tricked him in traveling to Los Angeles. The next day, Goofy and Max come to a junction: One leading to <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a>, the other to <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. Max chooses the route to California, making Goofy stop the car and storm off in anger. With the brake loose, the car drives off on its own; Goofy and Max chase after it and end up at a river. Goofy reveals that no matter how old Max gets, he will always be his son and the two reconcile with each other. After learning that Max had promised Roxanne that he would be at the concert, Goofy decides to take him to Los Angeles. The two nearly plummet down a waterfall to their deaths, but Max fortunately saves Goofy, using the Perfect Cast technique.</p>
<p>Goofy and Max get to Los Angeles and they end up onstage and dance with Powerline, watched by Pete, P.J. and Roxanne on separate televisions. Goofy and Max later return to Roxanne's house in their damaged car. Max tells the truth to Roxanne, though she accepts it and admits she always had feelings for him, ever since he first said, "Ahyuck!"; thus, a relationship starts between them. Goofy's car suddenly explodes due to the damage it has sustained, ejecting Goofy in the process, but he safely falls through the porch roof of Roxanne's house, and Max proceeds to introduce him to Roxanne.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>A Goofy Movie</i> was the directorial debut for Disney crewmember <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Lima" title="Kevin Lima">Kevin Lima</a>, who went on to direct the Disney films <i><a href="/wiki/Tarzan_(1999_film)" title="Tarzan (1999 film)">Tarzan</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/102_Dalmatians" title="102 Dalmatians">102 Dalmatians</a></i> (2000), and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enchanted_(2007_film)" title="Enchanted (2007 film)">Enchanted</a></i> (2007).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1995, Lima said that "Instead of just keeping Goofy one-dimensional as he's been in the past, we wanted to give an emotional side that would add to the emotional arc of the story. We wanted the audience to see his feelings instead of just his antics."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Austin_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Austin-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The main characters of this film, specifically <a href="/wiki/Goofy" title="Goofy">Goofy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Goof" title="Max Goof">Max Goof</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pete_(Disney)" title="Pete (Disney)">Pete</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pete_Junior" title="Pete Junior">PJ</a>, are based on their incarnations in the <i><a href="/wiki/Goof_Troop" title="Goof Troop">Goof Troop</a></i> television show, albeit slightly older: Max and PJ are high-school aged rather than middle-schoolers. However, other characters that had been established in <i>Goof Troop</i> do not appear in this film, such as Pete's wife Peg, his daughter Pistol, and pets Waffles and Chainsaw. Goofy and Pete retain their classic looks from the 1940s cartoons as opposed to the looks that they had in the 1950s cartoons and <i>Goof Troop</i>.</p>
<p>Although based upon a Disney TV series, <i>A Goofy Movie</i> was jointly produced by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Animation_Studios" title="Walt Disney Animation Studios">Walt Disney Feature Animation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disney_Television_Animation" title="Disney Television Animation">Walt Disney Television Animation</a>, <a href="/wiki/DisneyToon_Studios" title="DisneyToon Studios">Disney MovieToons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animation_studios_owned_by_The_Walt_Disney_Company#Disney_Animation_France" title="Animation studios owned by The Walt Disney Company">Walt Disney Animation France S.A</a>. and <a href="/wiki/Animation_studios_owned_by_The_Walt_Disney_Company#Disney_Animation_Australia" title="Animation studios owned by The Walt Disney Company">Walt Disney Animation Australia</a>. Pre-production was done at the main WDFA studio in <a href="/wiki/Burbank,_California" title="Burbank, California">Burbank, California</a>, starting as early as mid-1993. The animation work was done at Walt Disney Animation France in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris,_France" title="Paris, France">Paris, France</a> supervised by <a href="/wiki/Paul_and_Ga%C3%ABtan_Brizzi" title="Paul and Gaëtan Brizzi">Paul and Gaëtan Brizzi</a>, with additional scenes animated at Disney's studio in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney, Australia</a> under the direction of Steve Moore, and clean-up work done at the main Burbank studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Additional clean-up/animation was done by Phoenix Animation Studios in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_ink_and_paint" title="Digital ink and paint">digital ink and paint</a> by the Pixibox studio in France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-credits_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-credits-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The score for <i>A Goofy Movie</i> was provided by <a href="/wiki/Carter_Burwell" title="Carter Burwell">Carter Burwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Don_Davis_(composer)" title="Don Davis (composer)">Don Davis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Brown" title="Bobby Brown">Bobby Brown</a> was the original choice for Powerline and had some songs recorded but was cut due to drug problems. Some of the songs Bobby did for the movie were revamped and ended up on his <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_(Bobby_Brown_album)" title="Forever (Bobby Brown album)">Forever</a></i> album. The songs "I 2 I" and "Stand Out" were performed by <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Tevin_Campbell" title="Tevin Campbell">Tevin Campbell</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack_album" title="Soundtrack album">soundtrack album</a> for <i>A Goofy Movie</i> was released by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records" title="Walt Disney Records">Walt Disney Records</a> on March 18, 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitchell_Musso" title="Mitchell Musso">Mitchell Musso</a> covered the song "Stand Out" for the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DisneyMania_7" title="DisneyMania 7">DisneyMania 7</a></i> album, which was released on March 9, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disneymania7amazon_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-disneymania7amazon-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>A Goofy Movie</i> was originally scheduled for a November 1994 theatrical release,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> but production setbacks resulted in a push-back to 1995, while <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">The Lion King</a></i> was reissued to fill in for the film's absence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The film's premiere took place on April 5, 1995 at the AMC Pleasure Island in Lake Buena Vista, and was attended by director Kevin Lima and voice stars Bill Farmer and Jenna von Oy. On the 7th, it was released nationwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was first released on <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">VHS</a> home video on September 6, 1995, and included a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Parachute_Express" title="Parachute Express">Parachute Express</a> song <i>Doctor Looney's Remedy</i> on their video, <i>Come Sing with Us</i>. In the UK, it was released in theaters succeeding the Mickey Mouse short <i><a href="/wiki/Runaway_Brain" title="Runaway Brain">Runaway Brain</a></i> on October 18, 1996 and on VHS in 1997. It was reissued on June 20, 2000, along with a <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> version as part of the short-lived <a href="/wiki/List_of_Walt_Disney_and_Buena_Vista_video_releases#Walt_Disney_Gold_Classic_Collection" title="List of Walt Disney and Buena Vista video releases">Walt Disney Gold Classic Collection</a>. To date, this film and <i><a href="/wiki/Doug%27s_1st_Movie" title="Doug's 1st Movie">Doug's 1st Movie</a></i> are the only two Disney animated films produced in <a href="/wiki/Widescreen" title="Widescreen">widescreen</a> that have <a href="/wiki/Pan_and_scan" title="Pan and scan">pan and scan</a>-only <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_1" title="Region 1">Region 1</a> DVD releases (not counting separate widescreen and pan and scan DVD releases of the two Disney/<a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar</a> films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredibles" title="The Incredibles">The Incredibles</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cars_(film)" title="Cars (film)">Cars</a></i>). However, the film's <a href="/wiki/PAL" title="PAL">PAL</a> and <a href="/wiki/NTSC" title="NTSC">NTSC</a> (Japan) counterpart does have a non-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anamorphic" title="Anamorphic">anamorphic</a> widescreen DVD, and the film is available in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letterbox" title="Letterbox">letterbox</a> presentation on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laserdisc" title="Laserdisc">Laserdisc</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>A Goofy Movie</i> holds a score of 53% <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on reviews from 15 critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Todd_McCarthy" title="Todd McCarthy">Todd McCarthy</a> criticized the film's score, calling the six featured songs "unmemorable". He also felt that the personality of Goofy's character, while agreeable enough in support, proved a bit over the top for a headliner, and that "by any reasonable reckoning, he's distinctly overbearing and selfish, and responds with a bland dismissal to any opinion offered up by his son." McCarthy praised the film's technical aspects, calling them "crisp and clean".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Louis_Black" title="Louis Black">Louis Black</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Austin_Chronicle" title="The Austin Chronicle">The Austin Chronicle</a></i> summed up his review by saying the film was "bland, a barely television-length cartoon stretched out to fill a feature, and not much fun."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Austin_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Austin-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siskel_and_Ebert" title="Siskel and Ebert">Siskel and Ebert</a> both approved of the movie, praising the color scheme and the "sweet" father-son plot and gave it a "Three stars".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>A Goofy Movie</i> was considered a relative success for Disney, opening in 2,159 theaters at #2 on its opening weekend with $6,129,557 - held from the #1 spot only because of the Will Smith blockbuster <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boys_(1995_film)" title="Bad Boys (1995 film)"><i>Bad Boys</i></a> that opened the same weekend, with $15,523,358 in box office returns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BadBoysBoxOffice_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-BadBoysBoxOffice-17">[17]</a></sup> It ultimately ended its run at the US box office grossing $35,348,597 - coming in as the 51st highest-grossing domestic film in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gross_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-gross-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was nominated for "<a href="/wiki/Annie_Award_for_Best_Animated_Feature" title="Annie Award for Best Animated Feature">Best Animated Feature</a>" in the production categories and "Best Production Design", "Best Storyboarding", "Best Music", and "Best Animation" in the individual categories at the 23rd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annie_Awards" title="Annie Awards">Annie Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A sequel to this film was released in 2000, titled <i><a href="/wiki/An_Extremely_Goofy_Movie" title="An Extremely Goofy Movie">A Goofy Movie 2: An Extremely Goofy Movie</a></i>. The sequel involves Max's freshman year in college. Characters that returned for the sequel were Goofy, Max, PJ, Pete, and Bobby, but Roxanne, Max's love interest, is absent from the sequel and is not referenced. Roxanne later appeared in the television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_House_of_Mouse" title="Disney's House of Mouse">House of Mouse</a></i> in the episode titled "Max's Embarrassing Date", where she was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Grey_DeLisle" title="Grey DeLisle">Grey DeLisle</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Kellie_Martin" title="Kellie Martin">Kellie Martin</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reunion">Reunion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Goofy_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reunion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On August 14, 2015, a 20th anniversary reunion for the film occurred at the <a href="/wiki/D23_(Disney)" title="D23 (Disney)">D23 Expo</a> at <a href="/wiki/Anaheim_Convention_Center" title="Anaheim Convention Center">Anaheim Convention Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Surprisinglyawesome_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Surprisinglyawesome-19">[19]</a></sup> Those in attendance included Bill Farmer, Jason Marsden, Jim Cummings, Rob Paulsen, Jenna von Oy, and producer Don Hahn. Wallace Shawn, Pauly Shore, and director Kevin Lima also sent video messages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Surprisinglyawesome_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Surprisinglyawesome-19">[19]</a></sup> The panel also included musical performances from Farmer, Marsden, and Tevin Campbell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Surprisinglyawesome_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Surprisinglyawesome-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
