<p><b>Goo Ha-ra</b> (born January 13, 1991), better known mononymously as <b>Hara</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She is best known for being a former member of South Korean <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/Kara_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kara (South Korean band)">Kara</a> and for portraying Choi Da-hye, the president's daughter, on <i><a href="/wiki/City_Hunter_(TV_series)" title="City Hunter (TV series)">City Hunter</a></i> in 2011. Hara debuted as a solo artist in July 2015 with extended play <i><a href="/wiki/Alohara_(Can_You_Feel_It%3F)" title="Alohara (Can You Feel It?)">Alohara (Can You Feel It?)</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goo_Hara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Goo Ha-ra was born on January 13, 1991,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russell2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russell2014-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Gwangju" title="Gwangju">Gwangju</a>, South Korea. Before debuting, she participated in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM_Entertainment" title="SM Entertainment">SM</a>'s youth appearance tournament in 2005. In 2007, she auditioned for <a href="/wiki/JYP_Entertainment" title="JYP Entertainment">JYP</a>, however, she lost the runoff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was also an online shopping mall model, having been scouted after her photos were uploaded onto DSP's homepage. Thus, Hara became a member of <a href="/wiki/Kara_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kara (South Korean band)">Kara</a>. She is currently attending <a href="/wiki/Sungshin_Women%27s_University" title="Sungshin Women's University">Sungshin Women's University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 5, 2010, during an appearance at <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS'</a> <a href="/wiki/Strong_Heart_(TV_series)" title="Strong Heart (TV series)">Strong Heart</a> she admitted to getting <a href="/wiki/Dental_surgery" title="Dental surgery">dental</a> and minor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmetic_surgery" title="Cosmetic surgery">cosmetic</a> surgery on her face. She confessed to receiving one filler injection on her nose and had her teeth fixed. She stated that she had always had double eyelids, but had surgery to get them more defined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goo_Hara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.932016:_Kara">2008–2016: Kara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goo_Hara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2016: Kara">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kara_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kara (South Korean band)">Kara (South Korean band)</a></div>
<p>Kara debuted on March 29, 2007. In 2008, however, a member, Kim Sunghee, left the group. DSP Entertainment replaced her with two new members, Goo Hara and <a href="/wiki/Kang_Ji-young" title="Kang Ji-young">Kang Ji-young</a>, and Kara made a comeback with <a href="/wiki/Rock_U" title="Rock U">Rock U</a>, which changed their image from "Hip-hop" to "cute and playful".</p>
<p>On December 4, 2008, Kara had a comeback on <i>M! Countdown</i> with "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretty_Girl_(song)" title="Pretty Girl (song)">Pretty Girl</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In February 2009, Kara released their second mini-album "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honey_(Kara_song)" title="Honey (Kara song)">Honey</a>". "Honey" became the group's first No. 1 single when it topped the charts on <i>M! Countdown</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">The Music Trend</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late July 2009, Kara revealed their second album named <i><a href="/wiki/Revolution_(Kara_album)" title="Revolution (Kara album)">Revolution</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Due to the popularity of their songs "<a href="/wiki/Wanna_(song)" title="Wanna (song)">Wanna</a>" and "Mister", Kara's overall popularity increased. Kara started promotions with their third mini-album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lupin_(song)" title="Lupin (song)">Lupin</a></i> in February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2010, Kara officially debuted in Japan with "Mister". After their successful debut in Japan, Kara released their fourth mini-album <i><a href="/wiki/Jumping_(EP)" title="Jumping (EP)">Jumping</a></i> in November 2010.</p>
<p>On January 19, 2011, it was announced that Goo Hara would be terminating her contract with DSP Entertainment along with three other members of Kara, and a lawsuit was filed on their behalf. Later that day, it was announced that Goo Hara would discontinue her involvement with the suit and had rejoined the company,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heraldsuit_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-heraldsuit-10">[10]</a></sup> as she apparently was not fully aware of the lawsuit's details.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_activities">Solo activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goo_Hara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 2008, she appeared along with the other Kara members in an episode of <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> sitcom <i>Here He Comes</i>, having the most prominent role as a female gang leader.</p>
<p>In October 2009, she became a cast member in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a> 2TV reality show <i><a href="/wiki/Invincible_Youth" title="Invincible Youth">Invincible Youth</a></i> (청춘불패).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On March 14, 2011, it was also announced that Hara would make her acting debut in SBS <i><a href="/wiki/City_Hunter_(TV_series)" title="City Hunter (TV series)">City Hunter</a></i>, after filming the Japanese sitcom <i>Urakara</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> She starred as Choi Da-hye, the daughter of South Korea's president. She also appeared as a female lead in A-JAX's "Never Let Go" music video.</p>
<p>In November 2011, she and <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Jung" title="Nicole Jung">Nicole Jung</a> became the new MCs for SBS <i>Inkigayo</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> They left the show on August 19, 2012.</p>
<p>She recorded a song titled "Secret Love" which she performed during Kara's first concert tour, <a href="/wiki/Karasia" title="Karasia">Karasia</a>. The music video featured ZE:A's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yim_Si-wan" title="Yim Si-wan">Siwan</a>, and they acted as a lovely couple.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2013, she collaborated with Japanese musician and songwriter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fukuyama_Masaharu" title="Fukuyama Masaharu">Fukuyama Masaharu</a> to record a song called "Magic of Love" in Korean under the project group HARA+. The song was used as a soundtrack for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FujiTV" title="FujiTV">FujiTV</a>'s drama <i>Galileo</i>, which Masaharu starred in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Later on, Goo Hara had a cameo appearance in <i>Galileo 2</i> as a company employee.</p>
<p>In October 2013, she was appointed as the blood donation ambassador for the Hanmaum Blood Bank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her own reality show titled "On &amp; Off"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> aired on December 29, 2014 on MBC Music.</p>
<p>In January 2015, it was revealed that she was chosen to MC a new KBS idol beauty show, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Style_For_You_(TV_series)" title="A Style For You (TV series)">A Style For You</a></i> along with Super Junior's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Heechul" title="Kim Heechul">Kim Heechul</a>, EXID's <a href="/wiki/Hani_(singer)" title="Hani (singer)">Hani</a> and SISTAR's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoon_Bora" title="Yoon Bora">Bora</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>She said during an interview in April 2015 that after KARA's comeback in May, she will debut as a solo with her first album in July.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goo_Hara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 28, 2011, <a href="/wiki/DSP_Media" title="DSP Media">DSP Media</a> announced that Hara was in a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Yong_Jun-hyung" title="Yong Jun-hyung">Yong Jun-hyung</a> from <a href="/wiki/Beast_(South_Korean_band)" title="Beast (South Korean band)">Beast</a>. DSP Media also announced, "They do have good feelings towards one another, but they're still getting to know each other."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 27, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Cube_Entertainment" title="Cube Entertainment">Cube Entertainment</a> and DSP Media announced that Hara and Jun-hyung had ended their relationship. DSP Media said that the two "naturally drifted apart".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
