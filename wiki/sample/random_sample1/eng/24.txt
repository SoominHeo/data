<p><b>1,000,000,000</b> (one billion, <a href="/wiki/Long_and_short_scales" title="Long and short scales">short scale</a>; one thousand million or milliard, yard,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> long scale) is the <a href="/wiki/Natural_number" title="Natural number">natural number</a> following 999,999,999 and preceding 1,000,000,001. One billion can also be written as <b>b</b> or <b>bn</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EUStyleGuide_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EUStyleGuide-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Scientific_notation" title="Scientific notation">scientific notation</a>, it is written as <b>1 × 10<sup>9</sup></b>.</p>
<p>Previously in <a href="/wiki/British_English" title="British English">British English</a> (but not in <a href="/wiki/Comparison_of_American_and_British_English#Numbers" title="Comparison of American and British English">American English</a>), the word "billion" referred exclusively to a million millions (1,000,000,000,000). However, this is no longer as common as earlier, and the word has been used to mean one thousand million (1,000,000,000) for some time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The alternative term "one thousand million" is mainly used in the U.K., or countries such as Spain that uses "one thousand million" as one million million constitutes a billion. The worded figure, as opposed to the numerical figure (one thousand million/$1,000,000,000) is used to differentiate between "one thousand million" or "one billion".</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Asian_numbering_system" title="South Asian numbering system">South Asian numbering system</a>, it is known as 100 <a href="/wiki/Crore" title="Crore">crore</a> or 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Asian_numbering_system" title="South Asian numbering system">Arab</a>.</p>
<p>The term <i><b>milliard</b></i> can also be used to refer to 1,000,000,000; whereas "milliard" is seldom used in English, variations on this name often appear in <a href="/wiki/Long_and_short_scales" title="Long and short scales">other languages</a> (e.g. Slovenian <i>Miljarda</i>, Hungarian (Magyar) <i>milliárd</i>, Indonesian <i>miliar</i>, Polish <i>miliard</i>, Danish <i>milliard</i>, Spanish <i><span lang="es" xml:lang="es">millardo</span></i>, French <i>milliard</i>, Italian <i>miliardo</i>, Icelandic <i>milljarður</i>, German <i>Milliarde</i>, Hebrew <span lang="he" xml:lang="he">מיליארד</span>, Finnish <i>miljardi</i>, Dutch <i>miljard</i>, Croatian <i>milijarda</i>, Serbian милијарда, Bulgarian милиард, Russian миллиард, Czech <i>miliarda</i>, Arabic <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">مليار</span>, Romanian <i>miliard</i>, Swedish <i>miljard</i>, Norwegian <i>milliard</i>, Turkish <i>milyar</i>, Esperanto <i>miliardo</i>).</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SI_prefix" title="SI prefix">SI prefix</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giga" title="Giga">giga</a> indicates 1,000,000,000 times the base unit. Despite this, B remains the common abbreviation for this number.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>See <a href="/wiki/Orders_of_magnitude_(numbers)" title="Orders of magnitude (numbers)">Orders of magnitude (numbers)</a> for larger numbers; and <a href="/wiki/Long_and_short_scales" title="Long and short scales">long and short scales</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sense_of_scale">Sense of scale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sense of scale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The facts below give a sense of how large 1,000,000,000 (10<sup>9</sup>) is in the context of time according to current scientific evidence:</p>
<ul>
<li>10<sup>9</sup> seconds is 114 days short of 32 calendar years (≈ 31.7 years).</li>
<li>About 10<sup>9</sup> minutes ago, the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> was flourishing and Christianity was emerging. (10<sup>9</sup> minutes is roughly 1,901 years.)</li>
<li>About 10<sup>9</sup> hours ago, modern human beings and their ancestors were living in the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a> (more precisely, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Paleolithic" title="Middle Paleolithic">Middle Paleolithic</a>). (10<sup>9</sup> hours is roughly 114,080 years.)</li>
<li>About 10<sup>9</sup> days ago, <i><a href="/wiki/Australopithecus" title="Australopithecus">Australopithecus</a></i>, an ape-like creature related to an ancestor of modern humans, roamed the African <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savannas</a>. (10<sup>9</sup> days is roughly <span class="nowrap">2.738 million</span> years.)</li>
<li>About 10<sup>9</sup> months ago, <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaurs</a> walked the Earth during the late <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a>. (10<sup>9</sup> months is roughly <span class="nowrap">83.3 million</span> years.)</li>
<li>About 10<sup>9</sup> years—a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gigaannus" title="Gigaannus">gigaannus</a>—ago, the first <a href="/wiki/Multicellular_organism" title="Multicellular organism">multicellular</a> <a href="/wiki/Eukaryote" title="Eukaryote">eukaryotes</a> appeared on Earth.</li>
<li>It takes approximately 95 years to count from one to one billion in a single sitting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Universe" title="Universe">universe</a> is thought to be about <a href="/wiki/Age_of_the_universe" title="Age of the universe">13.8 × 10<sup>9</sup> years old.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Distance">Distance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>10<sup>9</sup> inches is 15,783 miles (25,400 km), more than halfway around the world and thus sufficient to reach any point on the globe from any other point.</li>
<li>10<sup>9</sup> metres (called a gigameter) is almost three times the distance from the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a>.</li>
<li>10<sup>9</sup> kilometres is over six times the distance from the Earth to the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finance">Finance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Finance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The possession of assets with total value of 10<sup>9</sup> <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">United States dollars</a> would place a person among the <a href="/wiki/Billionaire" title="Billionaire">world's wealthiest individuals</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Area">Area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>A billion square inches would be a square about one half mile on a side.</li>
<li>A piece of finely woven bed sheet cloth that contained a billion holes would measure about 500 square feet (46 m<sup>2</sup>), large enough to cover a moderate sized apartment.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Volume">Volume</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Volume">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>There are a billion cubic millimeters in a cubic meter and there are a billion cubic meters in a cubic kilometer.</li>
<li>A billion grains of table salt or granulated sugar would occupy a volume of about 2.5 cubic feet (0.071 m<sup>3</sup>).</li>
<li>A billion cubic inches would be a volume comparable to a large commercial building slightly larger than a typical supermarket.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>As of July 2016, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> has sold one billion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPhones" title="IPhones">iPhones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> This makes the iPhone one of the most successful product lines in history, surpassing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Playstation" title="Sony Playstation">Sony Playstation</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rubik%27s_Cube" title="Rubik's Cube">Rubik's Cube</a>.</li>
<li>As of July 2016, <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> has 1.71 billion users.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature">Nature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Nature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>A small mountain, slightly larger than <a href="/wiki/Stone_Mountain" title="Stone Mountain">Stone Mountain</a> Georgia, United States, would weigh (have a mass of) a billion tons.</li>
<li>There are billions of worker ants in the largest ant colony in the world,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> which covers 4,000 miles of the Mediterranean coast.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Count">Count</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Count">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>A</b> is a cube; <b>B</b> consists of 1000 cubes of type A, <b>C</b> consists of 1000 Bs; and <b>D</b> consists of 1000 Cs. Thus there are <span class="nowrap">1 million</span> As in C; and 1,000,000,000 As in D.</p>
