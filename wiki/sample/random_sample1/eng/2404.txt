<p>The <i><b>Ditadura Nacional</b></i> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[ditɐˈðuɾɐ nɐsiuˈnaɫ]</a></span>, <i><b>National Dictatorship</b></i>) was the name of the <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> regime that started in 1928 after re-election of General <a href="/wiki/%C3%93scar_Carmona" title="Óscar Carmona">Óscar Carmona</a> to the post of President.</p>
<p>The preceding military dictatorship period that started after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/28th_May_1926_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="28th May 1926 coup d'état">28th May 1926 coup d'état</a>, is known as <i><b>Ditadura Militar</b></i> (<i>Military Dictatorship</i>). After adopting a new constitution in 1933, the regime changed its name to <i><a href="/wiki/Estado_Novo_(Portugal)" title="Estado Novo (Portugal)">Estado Novo</a></i> (New State).</p>
<p>It is considered that the <i>Ditadura Nacional</i>, together with the <i><a href="/wiki/Estado_Novo_(Portugal)" title="Estado Novo (Portugal)">Estado Novo</a></i>, forms the historical period of the Portuguese Second Republic (1926-1974).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ditadura_Militar.2C_1926-1928">Ditadura Militar, 1926-1928</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ditadura_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ditadura Militar, 1926-1928">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/28th_May_1926_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="28th May 1926 coup d'état">28th May 1926 coup d'état</a></div>
<p>The military coup of May 28 easily seized power. Very soon dictatorship closes Parliament, bans all political parties and institutes censorship but is rocked by instability as hardline officers force out liberals. During this time no one clear leader emerges, as dictatorship is controlled by a coalition of lower-rank military officers, some of whom are <a href="/wiki/Integralismo_Lusitano" title="Integralismo Lusitano">Integralists</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After republican Prime minister and President resign on May 30, naval officer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mendes_Cabe%C3%A7adas_J%C3%BAnior" title="José Mendes Cabeçadas Júnior">José Mendes Cabeçadas Júnior</a> assumes both posts, but after conflicts with other coup leaders is forced to resign on June 17. He is followed by General <a href="/wiki/Manuel_de_Oliveira_Gomes_da_Costa" title="Manuel de Oliveira Gomes da Costa">Gomes da Costa</a>, who assumed post of Prime minister and President. The figurehead general does not want to establish a military dictatorship and is forced out on July 9 by General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_%C3%93scar_Carmona" title="António Óscar Carmona">António Óscar de Fragoso Carmona</a> who assumes both highest offices of the state. Carmona continues as Prime minister until April 18, 1928 and retains the post of President until his death on April 18, 1951. During 1927 there are failed coup attempts from the left and right wing movements. On August 12, 1927 junior officers break into cabinet meeting and start shooting. Carmona government regains control and imposes stronger military discipline, however, the attackers are not punished, they are sent to army posts in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Angola" title="Portuguese Angola">Portuguese Angola</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ditadura_Nacional.2C_1928-1933">Ditadura Nacional, 1928-1933</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ditadura_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ditadura Nacional, 1928-1933">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 25, 1928 Carmona organizes Presidential election and is elected to a five-year term. On April 18 he appoints <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Vicente_de_Freitas" title="José Vicente de Freitas">José Vicente de Freitas</a> as the new Prime minister. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Oliveira_Salazar" title="Antonio de Oliveira Salazar">Antonio de Oliveira Salazar</a> becomes Minister of Finances. The conflicts between the military officers and Catholic wing represented by Salazar increase to a point where the whole Freitas government resigns on July 8, 1929 and only Salazar keeps his ministerial post in the new cabinet of <a href="/wiki/Artur_Ivens_Ferraz" title="Artur Ivens Ferraz">Artur Ivens Ferraz</a>. Salazar's influence begins to grow at the expense of military officers who begin to lose their political power. After a conflict with Salazar in 1930 Ferraz was replaced by General <a href="/wiki/Domingos_Oliveira" title="Domingos Oliveira">Domingos Oliveira</a> who allowed Salazar to play ever increasing role in finances and politics.</p>
<p>By 1930 military dictatorship had stabilized Portugal and had to think about the future. In what form was the dictatorship to continue? The answer was provided by Salazar, who became Prime minister on July 5, 1932 and in 1933 instituted the <a href="/wiki/Estado_Novo_(Portugal)" title="Estado Novo (Portugal)">Estado Novo</a> regime.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline.2C_1928-1933">Timeline, 1928-1933</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ditadura_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Timeline, 1928-1933">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>1928</b>
<ul>
<li>February, The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Commission_for_the_Propaganda_of_the_Dictatorship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Commission for the Propaganda of the Dictatorship (page does not exist)">Comissão de Propaganda da Ditadura</a> (Commission for the Propaganda of the Dictatorship) is created.</li>
<li>March 17, The <i>Police of Information</i> of Porto and Lisbon are fused.</li>
<li>April 18, General <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Vicente_de_Freitas" title="José Vicente de Freitas">José Vicente de Freitas</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Portugal" title="List of Prime Ministers of Portugal">Prime Minister</a>.</li>
<li>April 26, <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Oliveira_Salazar" title="António de Oliveira Salazar">António de Oliveira Salazar</a> becomes Minister of Finance for the 2nd time.</li>
<li>General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_%C3%93scar_Carmona" title="António Óscar Carmona">António Óscar de Fragoso Carmona</a> remains <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Portugal" title="List of Presidents of Portugal">President of the Republic</a>.</li>
<li><i>Acordo Missionário</i> (Missionary Agreement) between the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church_in_Portugal" title="Catholic Church in Portugal">Catholic Church</a> and the Portuguese Republic, giving special status to the action of the Catholic Church in Portugal's colonies.</li>
<li>Failed Republican revolutionary attempt against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ditadura_Nacional_(Portugal)" title="Ditadura Nacional (Portugal)">Ditadura Nacional</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Communist_Party" title="Portuguese Communist Party">Portuguese Communist Party</a>'s Main Office is closed.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><b>1929</b>
<ul>
<li>July 8, <a href="/wiki/Artur_Ivens_Ferraz" title="Artur Ivens Ferraz">Artur Ivens Ferraz</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Portugal" title="List of Prime Ministers of Portugal">Prime Minister</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Religious_institute" title="Religious institute">religious institutes</a> are again permitted in Portugal.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Communist_Party" title="Portuguese Communist Party">Portuguese Communist Party</a> is reorganized under <a href="/wiki/Bento_Ant%C3%B3nio_Gon%C3%A7alves" title="Bento António Gonçalves">Bento Gonçalves</a>. Adapting the Party to its new illegal status, the reorganization creates a net of clandestine cells to avoid the wave of detentions.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><b>1930</b>
<ul>
<li>January 21, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domingos_da_Costa_e_Oliveira" title="Domingos da Costa e Oliveira">Domingos da Costa e Oliveira</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Portugal" title="List of Prime Ministers of Portugal">Prime Minister</a>.</li>
<li>The <i>Acto Colonial</i> (Colonial Act) is published, defining the status of Portuguese colonies (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Angola" title="Portuguese Angola">Portuguese Angola</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabinda_(province)" title="Cabinda (province)">Cabinda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Cape_Verde" title="Portuguese Cape Verde">Portuguese Cape Verde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Guinea" title="Portuguese Guinea">Portuguese Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="Portuguese São Tomé and Príncipe">Portuguese São Tomé and Príncipe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Mozambique" title="Portuguese Mozambique">Portuguese Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_India" title="Portuguese India">Portuguese India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Timor" title="Portuguese Timor">Portuguese Timor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Macau" title="Portuguese Macau">Portuguese Macau</a>).</li>
<li>The fundamental principles of the new regime are presented by <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Oliveira_Salazar" title="António de Oliveira Salazar">António de Oliveira Salazar</a> in the 4th anniversary of the <i>28th of May Revolution</i>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><b>1932</b>
<ul>
<li>July 5, <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Oliveira_Salazar" title="António de Oliveira Salazar">António de Oliveira Salazar</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Portugal" title="List of Prime Ministers of Portugal">Prime Minister</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><b>1933</b>
<ul>
<li>A new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_Constitution" title="Portuguese Constitution">Constitution</a> is approved in a referendum, defining Portugal as a <a href="/wiki/Corporatism" title="Corporatism">Corporative</a>, <a href="/wiki/One-party_state" title="One-party state">Single Party</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transcontinental_nation" title="Transcontinental nation">Multi-continental country</a> (in Europe, Africa, Asia and <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>).</li>
<li>An authoritarian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-wing" title="Right-wing">right-wing</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dictatorial" title="Dictatorial">Dictatorial</a> regime entitled <b><a href="/wiki/Estado_Novo_(Portugal)" title="Estado Novo (Portugal)">Estado Novo</a></b> is installed.</li>
<li>The Single Party <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uni%C3%A3o_Nacional" title="União Nacional">União Nacional</a> (National Union) is created.</li>
<li>The <i>Estatuto do Trabalho Nacional</i> (Code of National Labour) is published, prohibiting all free trade unions.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Secret_police" title="Secret police">Political Police</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PVDE" title="PVDE">PVDE</a> (<i>Polícia de Vigilância e de Defesa do Estado</i>; State Defense and Vigilance Police) is created.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Censorship" title="Censorship">Censorship</a>, particularly of the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">Mass media</a>, is systematic.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
