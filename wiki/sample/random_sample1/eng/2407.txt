<p>The <b>Main Directorate of State Security</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Glavnoe Upravlenie Gosudarstvennoi Bezopasnosti</span>, Главное управление государственной безопасности, ГУГБ, <b>GUGB</b>) was the name of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intelligence_service" title="Intelligence service">intelligence service</a> and <a href="/wiki/Secret_police" title="Secret police">secret police</a> from July 1934 to April 1943. It was run under the auspices of the Peoples Commissariat of Internal Affairs (<a href="/wiki/NKVD" title="NKVD">NKVD</a>). Its first head was first deputy of People's Commissar of Internal Affairs (then - <a href="/wiki/Genrikh_Yagoda" title="Genrikh Yagoda">Genrikh Yagoda</a>), Commissar 1st rank of State Security <a href="/wiki/Yakov_Agranov" title="Yakov Agranov">Yakov Agranov</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Main_Directorate_of_State_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Main Directorate of State Security evolved from the Joint State Political Directorate (or. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OGPU" title="OGPU">OGPU</a>). On February 3, 1941, the <a href="/wiki/Military_counterintelligence_of_the_Soviet_Army" title="Military counterintelligence of the Soviet Army">Special Sections (or. OO)</a> of the GUGB-<a href="/wiki/NKVD" title="NKVD">NKVD</a> (responsible for counter-intelligence in the military) became part of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Navy" title="Navy">Navy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">RKKA</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RKKF" title="RKKF">RKKF</a>, respectively). The GUGB was removed from the NKVD and renamed People's Commissariat of State Security or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NKGB" title="NKGB">NKGB</a>.</p>
<p>Following the outbreak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the NKVD and NKGB were reunited on July 20, 1941 and counter-intelligence was returned to the NKVD in January 1942. In April 1943 it was again transferred to the Narkomat of Defence and Narkomat of the Navy, becoming <a href="/wiki/SMERSH" title="SMERSH">SMERSH</a> (from Smert' Shpionam or "Death to Spies"); at the same time, the GUGB was again separated from the <a href="/wiki/NKVD" title="NKVD">NKVD</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NKGB" title="NKGB">NKGB</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="GUGB_heads">GUGB heads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Main_Directorate_of_State_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: GUGB heads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the end of 1937 GUGB was the most powerful and influential organ in <a href="/wiki/NKVD" title="NKVD">NKVD</a> structure. GUGB departments (or Sections) dealt with - intelligence, internal security, counter-intelligence, protection of government and secret communications.<br/>
First chief of GUGB was <a href="/wiki/Yakov_Agranov" title="Yakov Agranov">Yakov Agranov</a>, Commissar 1st rank of State Security and first deputy of People's Commissar of Internal Affairs. Next chief of GUGB from April 15, 1937 to September 8, 1938 was komkor <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Frinovsky" title="Mikhail Frinovsky">Mikhail Frinovsky</a>, he was succeeded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lavrenty_Beria" title="Lavrenty Beria">Lavrenty Beria</a>, then just promoted to Commissar 1st rank of State Security. When Beria become <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Commissar_of_Internal_Affairs" title="People's Commissar of Internal Affairs">People's Commissar of Internal Affairs</a> (head of NKVD), Commissar 3rd rank of State Security <a href="/wiki/Vsevolod_Merkulov" title="Vsevolod Merkulov">Vsevolod Merkulov</a> become his first deputy and new and last head of GUGB.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Main_Directorate_of_State_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="GUGB_Ranks">GUGB Ranks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Main_Directorate_of_State_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: GUGB Ranks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The GUGB had a unique system of ranks, a blend of position-rank system used in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">army</a> and personal ranks used in <a href="/wiki/Militsiya" title="Militsiya">Militsiya</a>; the rank insignia was also very distinct. Even though insignia introduced in 1937 followed the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> collar patch patterns, it assigned them very different ranks for GUGB and <a href="/wiki/Internal_Troops" title="Internal Troops">Internal Troops</a>/political/specialist branches, with GUGB rank placed at least one grade higher than a similar army equivalent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>When GUGB and Militsiya ranks were replaced with military ranks and insignia in February 1943, Major to Sergeant ranks were aligned with Colonel to Junior Lieutenant, and Senior Major and up were replaced with various degrees of Commissar. In 1945, General Commissar <a href="/wiki/Lavrentiy_Beria" title="Lavrentiy Beria">Lavrentiy Beria</a> received the rank of the <a href="/wiki/Marshal_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Marshal of the Soviet Union">Marshal of the Soviet Union</a>, and other GUGB Commissars received the ranks from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_of_the_Army_(USSR)" title="General of the Army (USSR)">Generals of the Army</a> to Major General.</p>
<p>The ranks of GUGB used in 1937–1943 are as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>генеральный комиссар ГБ – <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=General_Commissar_of_State_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="General Commissar of State Security (page does not exist)">General Commissar of State Security</a></li>
<li>комиссар ГБ 1-го ранга – <a href="/wiki/Commissar" title="Commissar">Commissar</a> 1st rank of State Security</li>
<li>комиссар ГБ 2-го ранга – Commissar 2nd rank of State Security</li>
<li>комиссар ГБ 3-го ранга – Commissar 3rd rank of State Security</li>
<li>старший майор ГБ – Senior Major of State Security</li>
<li>майор ГБ – Major of State Security</li>
<li>капитан ГБ – Captain of State Security</li>
<li>старший лейтенант ГБ – Senior Lieutenant of State Security</li>
<li>лейтенант ГБ – Lieutenant of State Security</li>
<li>младший лейтенант ГБ – Junior Lieutenant of State Security</li>
<li>сержант ГБ –  Sergeant of State Security</li>
</ul>
