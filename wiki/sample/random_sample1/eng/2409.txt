<p>The <b>National Day of the People's Republic of China</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_China" title="Public holidays in China">public holiday in the People's Republic of China</a> to celebrate their <a href="/wiki/National_day" title="National day">national day</a>, and is celebrated annually on October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The PRC was founded on September 21, 1949, with a ceremony celebrating the forming of the Central People's Government taking place in <a href="/wiki/Tiananmen_Square" title="Tiananmen Square">Tiananmen Square</a> on October 1 that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Central People's Government passed the <i>Resolution on the National Day of the People's Republic of China</i> on December 2, 1949, and declared that October 1 is the National Day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The National Day marks the start of only one of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Week_(China)" title="Golden Week (China)">Golden Weeks</a> in the PRC. However, there have been some <a href="/wiki/Golden_Week_(China)#Controversy" title="Golden Week (China)">recent controversies</a> over whether Golden Weeks should be kept.</p>
<p>The National Day is celebrated throughout <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> with a variety of <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a>-organized festivities, including fireworks and concerts. Public places, such as <a href="/wiki/Tiananmen_Square" title="Tiananmen Square">Tiananmen Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, are decorated in a festive theme. Portraits of revered leaders, such as <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, are publicly displayed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
