<p>The <b>National Theatre</b> in <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> (<b><a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <span lang="no" xml:lang="no"><i>Nationaltheatret</i></span></b>) is one of <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>'s largest and most prominent venues for performance of dramatic arts.</p>
<p>The theatre had its first performance on 1 September 1899 but can trace its origins to <a href="/wiki/Christiania_Theatre" title="Christiania Theatre">Christiania Theatre</a>, which was founded in 1829. There were three official opening performances, on subsequent days in September: first, selected pieces by <a href="/wiki/Ludvig_Holberg" title="Ludvig Holberg">Ludvig Holberg</a>, then <i><a href="/wiki/An_Enemy_of_the_People" title="An Enemy of the People">An Enemy of the People</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Ibsen" title="Henrik Ibsen">Henrik Ibsen</a>, and on the third day <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sigurd_I_of_Norway" title="Sigurd I of Norway">Sigurd Jorsalfar</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B8rnstjerne_Bj%C3%B8rnson" title="Bjørnstjerne Bjørnson">Bjørnstjerne Bjørnson</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Theatre_(Oslo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The National Theatre was founded as a private institution and weathered several financial crises until 1929, when the Norwegian government started providing modest support. A number of famous Norwegians have served as artistic directors for the theatre, but <a href="/wiki/Vilhelm_Krag" title="Vilhelm Krag">Vilhelm Krag</a> who took over in 1911, is credited as having brought the theatre into its "golden age."</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NationalTheatretBack.jpg"><img alt="NationalTheatretBack.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/NationalTheatretBack.jpg/220px-NationalTheatretBack.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/NationalTheatretBack.jpg/330px-NationalTheatretBack.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/NationalTheatretBack.jpg/440px-NationalTheatretBack.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NationalTheatretBack.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The theatre is often considered the home for Ibsen's plays, and most of his works have been performed here. Notable is also the children's Christmas play, <i>The Journey to the Christmas Star</i> (<span lang="no" xml:lang="no"><i>Reisen til Julestjernen</i></span>), written by the then theatre's finance director <a href="/wiki/Sverre_Brandt" title="Sverre Brandt">Sverre Brandt</a> and performed for the first time in 1924.</p>
<p>The main building is centrally located between the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Palace,_Oslo" title="Royal Palace, Oslo">Royal Palace, Oslo</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Norway" title="Parliament of Norway">Parliament of Norway</a>. It is served by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Theatre_Station" title="National Theatre Station">National Theatre Station</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Theatre_metro_station" title="National Theatre metro station">National Theatre metro stations</a>. It was designed by <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Bull" title="Henrik Bull">Henrik Bull</a>. The theatre organisation manages four stages: the main stage (<i><span lang="no" xml:lang="no">Hovedscenen</span></i>), the amphitheatre (<i><span lang="no" xml:lang="no">Amfiscenen</span></i>) and Painting Parlour (<i><span lang="no" xml:lang="no">Malersalen</span></i>) within the main building. The fourth is the Torshov Theatre (<i><span lang="no" xml:lang="no">Torshovteatret</span></i>) in the <a href="/wiki/Torshov" title="Torshov">Torshov</a> district of Oslo. As of 2009<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=National_Theatre_(Oslo)&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the theatre's artistic director was <a href="/wiki/Hanne_T%C3%B8mta" title="Hanne Tømta">Hanne Tømta</a>.</p>
