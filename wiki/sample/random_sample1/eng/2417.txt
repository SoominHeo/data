<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Affiliation">Affiliation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Dance_Company_of_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Affiliation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The dance company has been affiliated with <a href="/wiki/National_Theater_of_Korea" title="National Theater of Korea">National Theater of Korea</a> located in the neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Jangchung-dong" title="Jangchung-dong">Jangchung-dong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jung-gu,_Seoul" title="Jung-gu, Seoul">Jung-gu</a>, central of Seoul where the National Dance Company of Korea mainly has performed as well as the <a href="/wiki/National_Drama_Company_of_Korea" title="National Drama Company of Korea">National Drama Company of Korea</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Changguk" title="Changguk">National Changguk Company Of Korea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Orchestra_Company_of_Korea" title="National Orchestra Company of Korea">National Orchestra Company of Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncyKorea_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EncyKorea-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Britannica_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Britannica-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_Work">History and Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Dance_Company_of_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History and Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>National Dance Company of Korea was established by the director, Im Seong-nam (임성남) and 12 other dancers in February 1962. Since then, it has been a representative dance company in South Korea with the purpose to create new dances based on Korean traditional dance and to perform the works. By participating in various international festivals, National Dance Company has presented the world, its rich repertoire.<br/>
From October 2006 to April 2007 <i>Soul, Sunflower</i>, the dance production of National Dance Company and the German Musical group <i>Saltacello</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Schindler_(musician)" title="Peter Schindler (musician)">Peter Schindler</a>) has been several sold-out appearances in Seoul. This production is a unique cultural cooperation between Germany and Korea and re-create the Korean dance <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Korea#Salp.27uri" title="Music of Korea">Salpuri</a> with a modern interpretation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><br/>
Nearly since fifty years, the National Dance Company of Korea has expressed the feeling of Korean humans and the aesthetically appeal of Korean culture in dance performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
