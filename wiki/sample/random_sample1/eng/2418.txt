<p>The <b>National Museum of Denmark</b> (Nationalmuseet) in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> is <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmarkâs</a> largest museum of <a href="/wiki/Cultural_history" title="Cultural history">cultural history</a>, comprising the histories of Danish and foreign cultures, alike. The museum's main building is located a short distance from <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%B8get" title="StrÃ¸get">StrÃ¸get</a> at the center of Copenhagen. It contains exhibits from around the world, from <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> to <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Additionally, the museum sponsors SILA - The Greenland Research Centre at the National Museum of Denmark to further <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeological</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthropology" title="Anthropology">anthropological</a> research in <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The museum has a number of national commitments, particularly within the following key areas: <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethnology" title="Ethnology">ethnology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Numismatics" title="Numismatics">numismatics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethnography" title="Ethnography">ethnography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_science" title="Natural science">natural science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architectural_conservation" title="Architectural conservation">conservation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a>, building <a href="/wiki/Antiquarian" title="Antiquarian">antiquarian</a> activities in connection with the <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">churches</a> of <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, as well as the handling of the DanefÃ¦ (the National Treasures).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exhibitions">Exhibitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Museum_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Exhibitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The museum covers 14,000 years of <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a>, from the reindeer-hunters of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice_Age" title="Ice Age">Ice Age</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Vikings</a>, and works of religious art from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_Age" title="Middle Age">Middle Ages</a>, when the church was highly significant in Danish life. Danish coins from Viking times to the present and coins from ancient <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, as well as examples of the <a href="/wiki/Coin" title="Coin">coinage</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Currencies" title="Currencies">currencies</a> of other <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">cultures</a>, are exhibited also. The National Museum keeps Denmarkâs largest and most varied collection of objects from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient" title="Ancient">ancient</a> cultures of Greece and Italy, the <a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a> and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. For example, it holds a collection of objects that were retrieved during the Danish excavation of <a href="/wiki/Tell_Shemshara" title="Tell Shemshara">Tell Shemshara</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> in 1957.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Exhibits are also shown on who the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danish_people" title="Danish people">Danish people</a> are and were, stories of everyday life and special occasions, stories of the Danish state and nation, but most of all stories of different peopleâs lives in Denmark from 1560 to 2000.</p>
<p>The Danish pre-history section was re-opened in May 2008 after years of renovating.</p>
<p>In 2013, a major exhibition on the <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a> was opened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Margrethe" title="Queen Margrethe">Queen Margrethe</a>. It has toured to other museums, including the <a href="/wiki/British_Museum" title="British Museum">British Museum</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
