<p>The <b>Korea National Maritime Museum</b> (called 국립해양박물관 in Korean) is a Korean <a href="/wiki/Maritime_museum" title="Maritime museum">maritime museum</a> and the third largest museum in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea" title="Republic of Korea">Republic of Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3rd_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-3rd-1">[1]</a></sup> The museum was inaugurated on July 9, 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-open_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-open-2">[2]</a></sup> and is located in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dongsam-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dongsam-dong (page does not exist)">Dongsam-dong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeongdo-gu" title="Yeongdo-gu">Yeongdo-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>.</p>
<p>The museum exhibits more than 12,000 maritime relics, including the ‘<a href="/wiki/Joseon_Tongsinsa" title="Joseon Tongsinsa">Joseon Missional Ship</a>’ which is the largest replica in South Korea - half the size of the actual ship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exhi_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-exhi-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
