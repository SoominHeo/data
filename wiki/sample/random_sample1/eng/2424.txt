<p>The <b>National Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Partido Nacional</i></span>, PN), also known as the <b>White Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Partido Blanco</i></span>), is a major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-wing" title="Right-wing">right-wing</a> <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, currently the major opposition party to the ruling <a href="/wiki/Broad_Front_(Uruguay)" title="Broad Front (Uruguay)">Frente Amplio</a> government.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Party_(Uruguay)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was formed on 10 August 1836. Together with the <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a> <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Party_(Uruguay)" title="Colorado Party (Uruguay)">Colorado Party</a>, it is one of the two traditional groupings dating back to the nineteenth century. <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Oribe" title="Manuel Oribe">Manuel Oribe</a> was its founder. In the <a href="/wiki/Uruguayan_War" title="Uruguayan War">civil war of 1864–65</a> it was ousted by the Colorados who were in power until 1958. In 1872 the party changed its name from White Party (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Partido Blanco</i></span>) to National Party.</p>
<p>During the mid-20th century a very peculiar phenomenon occurred: a splinter group known as <a href="/wiki/Independent_National_Party_(Uruguay)" title="Independent National Party (Uruguay)">Independent National Party</a> was active between 1931 and 1959 but, although they were de facto a separate party, they permanently insisted that "there is only one National Party".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_elections">2004 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Party_(Uruguay)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2004 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Uruguay" title="Elections in Uruguay">2004 national elections</a>, the National Party won 36 seats out of 99 in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Uruguay" title="Chamber of Deputies of Uruguay">Chamber of Deputies</a> and 11 seats out of 31 in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Uruguay" title="Senate of Uruguay">Senate</a>. Its presidential candidate, <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Larra%C3%B1aga" title="Jorge Larrañaga">Jorge Larrañaga</a>, obtained the same day 35.1% of the valid, popular vote.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_elections">2009 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Party_(Uruguay)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Uruguay" title="Elections in Uruguay">2009 national elections</a>, the National Party won 31 seats out of 99 in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Uruguay" title="Chamber of Deputies of Uruguay">Chamber of Deputies</a> and 9 seats out of 31 in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Uruguay" title="Senate of Uruguay">Senate</a>. Its presidential candidate, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Alberto_Lacalle" title="Luis Alberto Lacalle">Luis Alberto Lacalle</a>, obtained on October 25 29.07% of the valid, popular vote.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_elections">2014 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Party_(Uruguay)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the 2014 elections, its presidential candidate was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luis_Lacalle_Pou" title="Luis Lacalle Pou">Luis Lacalle Pou</a>.</p>
