<p>The <b>National Institute of Korean History</b> (<b>NIKH</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> national organization in charge of researching, collecting, compiling, promoting the study of historical materials on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_history" title="Korean history">Korean history</a>. It was established as <i>Guksagwan</i> (국사관 國史館) in March 1946, one year after the liberation of Korea and was changed to the current name in 1949.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncyKorea_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EncyKorea-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2012, the Institute announced that they will translate the <a href="/wiki/Annals_of_the_Joseon_Dynasty" title="Annals of the Joseon Dynasty">Annals of the Joseon Dynasty</a> into English by the year 2033. With an initial budget of ₩500 million, they planned to start work in 2014 but estimated that a budget of ₩40 billion is needed to complete the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
