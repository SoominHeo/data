<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Locally simply connected space</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, a <b>locally simply connected space</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Topological_space" title="Topological space">topological space</a> that admits a <a href="/wiki/Base_(topology)" title="Base (topology)">basis</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simply_connected" title="Simply connected">simply connected</a> sets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Every locally simply connected space is also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Locally_path-connected" title="Locally path-connected">locally path-connected</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Locally_connected" title="Locally connected">locally connected</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hawaiian-Earring-250.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="250" data-file-width="250" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Hawaiian-Earring-250.jpg/220px-Hawaiian-Earring-250.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d2/Hawaiian-Earring-250.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hawaiian-Earring-250.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hawaiian earring</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Circle" title="Circle">circle</a> is an example of a locally simply connected space which is not simply connected. The <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_earring" title="Hawaiian earring">Hawaiian earring</a> is a space which is neither locally simply connected nor simply connected. The <a href="/wiki/Cone_(topology)" title="Cone (topology)">cone</a> on the Hawaiian earring is <a href="/wiki/Contractible_space" title="Contractible space">contractible</a> and therefore simply connected, but still not locally simply connected.</p>
<p>All <a href="/wiki/Topological_manifold" title="Topological manifold">topological manifolds</a> and <a href="/wiki/CW_complex" title="CW complex">CW complexes</a> are locally simply connected. In fact, these satisfy the much stronger property of being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Locally_contractible_space" title="Locally contractible space">locally contractible</a>.</p>
<p>A strictly weaker condition is that of being <a href="/wiki/Semi-locally_simply_connected" title="Semi-locally simply connected">semi-locally simply connected</a>. Both locally simply connected spaces and simply connected spaces are semi-locally simply connected, but neither converse holds.</p>
