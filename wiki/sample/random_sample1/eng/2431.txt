<p><b>Hitachi Seaside Park</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">国営ひたち海浜公園</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kokuei Hitachi Kaihinkōen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a public park in <a href="/wiki/Hitachinaka,_Ibaraki" title="Hitachinaka, Ibaraki">Hitachinaka, Ibaraki</a>, Japan.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hitachi_Seaside_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Covering an area of 190 hectares, the park features blooming flowers around the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibarakiguide_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibarakiguide-1">[1]</a></sup> The park has become known for its <a href="/wiki/Nemophila" title="Nemophila">baby blue-eyes</a> flowers, with the blooming of 4.5 million of the translucent-petaled blue flowers in the spring drawing tourists. In addition to the annual "Nemophila Harmony", the park features a million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daffodil" title="Daffodil">daffodils</a>, 170 varieties of <a href="/wiki/Tulip" title="Tulip">tulips</a>, and many other flowers. The park includes cycling trails and a small amusement park with a <a href="/wiki/Ferris_wheel" title="Ferris wheel">Ferris wheel</a>.</p>
