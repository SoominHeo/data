<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Guozijian</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Guozijian</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">国子监</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">國子監</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">guózǐjiàn</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">kuo<sup>2</sup>-tzu<sup>3</sup>-chien<sup>4</sup></span></i>; literally: "School for the Sons of the State"),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yuan_194_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yuan_194-1">[1]</a></sup> sometimes translated as the National School, National Academy, Imperial Academy, Imperial University, Imperial College, Imperial Central School, National University,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoteTwitchett1988_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-MoteTwitchett1988-2">[2]</a></sup> etc., was the national central institution of higher learning in Chinese dynasties after the <a href="/wiki/Sui_dynasty" title="Sui dynasty">Sui</a>. It was the highest institution of learning in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>'s traditional educational system, with the function of administration of education. In Vietnam, the Imperial Academy (<a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>: Quốc Tử Giám) existed after the <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_dynasty" title="Lý dynasty">Lý dynasty</a>. Several notable chairmans of Guozijian in Vietnam history are Chu Văn An, Nguyễn Phi Khanh, <a href="/wiki/V%C5%A9_Mi%C3%AAn" title="Vũ Miên">Vũ Miên</a>, and <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA_Qu%C3%BD_%C4%90%C3%B4n" title="Lê Quý Đôn">Lê Quý Đôn</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guozijian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Kwo-tze-keen,_or_National_University.jpg"><img alt="The Kwo-tze-keen, or National University.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3452" data-file-width="2822" height="269" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/The_Kwo-tze-keen%2C_or_National_University.jpg/220px-The_Kwo-tze-keen%2C_or_National_University.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/The_Kwo-tze-keen%2C_or_National_University.jpg/330px-The_Kwo-tze-keen%2C_or_National_University.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/The_Kwo-tze-keen%2C_or_National_University.jpg/440px-The_Kwo-tze-keen%2C_or_National_University.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Kwo-tze-keen,_or_National_University.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Formerly it was called the <a href="/wiki/Taixue" title="Taixue">Taixue</a>, while Taixue for Gongsheng (<i>tribute students</i>) from the populace was still part of Guozijian, along with Guozixue for noble students. The central schools of taixue were established as far back as 3 CE, when a standard nationwide school system was established and funded during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ping_of_Han" title="Emperor Ping of Han">Emperor Ping of Han</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Since <a href="/wiki/Sui_dynasty" title="Sui dynasty">Sui dynasty</a> it was called Guozijian (Kuotzuchien).</p>
<p>At the Guozijian, law, math, calligraphy, equestrianism, and archery were emphasized by the Ming <a href="/wiki/Hongwu_Emperor" title="Hongwu Emperor">Hongwu Emperor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoteTwitchett1988_2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MoteTwitchett1988_2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Guozijian was shut down in 1905.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> From then on the European <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> system, based on the earlier <a href="/wiki/Medieval_university" title="Medieval university">medieval university</a>, was adopted.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations">Locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guozijian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1178" data-file-width="1800" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg/220px-Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg/330px-Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg/440px-Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Imperial_Academy_of_Hue.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Entrance of the Imperial Academy in Huế, central Vietnam</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Guozijian were located in the national capital of each dynasty, such as <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kaifeng" title="Kaifeng">Kaifeng</a>. In early years of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming" title="Ming">Ming</a>, Guozijian was in <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>, and then there were two capitals, thus there were two Guozijian, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanking_Kuotzuchien" title="Nanking Kuotzuchien">one in Nanjing</a> and one in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing" title="Qing">Qing</a>, Guozijian was in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Guozijian_(Beijing)" title="Guozijian (Beijing)">Guozijian in Beijing</a>, located at the <a href="/wiki/Guozijian_Street" title="Guozijian Street">Guozijian Street</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Dongcheng_District,_Beijing" title="Dongcheng District, Beijing">Dongcheng District</a>, was the imperial college during the Yuan, Ming and Qing dynasties; most of the current buildings were built during the Ming Dynasty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It is the last Guozijian in China.</p>
