<p>The <b>Federation of International Trade Associations</b> (<b>FITA</b>) based in <a href="/wiki/Reston,_Virginia" title="Reston, Virginia">Reston, Virginia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York,_New_York" title="New York, New York">New York, New York</a> USA was founded in 1984 by Nelson Joyner to foster the international trade community providing benefits and services. It fosters international trade by seeking to strengthen the role of trade associations and bi-lateral chamber of commences. This was the mission of <b>Nelson Joyner</b> In 1994, Nelson's Director of Business Development, created affinity programs to generate non-due revenue with Fortune 500 Corporations including 1992 Sprint (7 years) endorsement headed by Jessica Cannon, key negations development executive. FITA Services, a private for-profit company was established. Such members as The National Export Companies were earning over $100k yearly from the partner programs implemented as the affinity communication contract was creatively using member association and Global Business Roundtable. With retirement, Nelson Joyner gave Barney Lahrer the portion of webmaster and previous secretary Kim Park the organization which now has anp the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. FITA is the strategic partner of the United States Commercial Service for e-commerce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>Really Useful Resources for International Trade Professionals,</i> a bi-weekly newsletter including tips on useful Web sites and articles about international trade and markets.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Commercial_Service" title="United States Commercial Service">United States Commercial Service</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ppp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ppp-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Trade_and_Investment" title="UK Trade and Investment">UK Trade &amp; Investment (UKTI)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Trade_Development_Council" title="Hong Kong Trade Development Council">Hong Kong Trade Development Council</a> (HKTDC) have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Private_Partnership" title="Public Private Partnership">public-private partnership (PPP)</a> agreements with FITA under which they contribute market research and other reports on <a href="/wiki/GlobalTrade.net" title="GlobalTrade.net">GlobalTrade.net</a>. Similar agreements are in effect with <a href="/wiki/NASBITE_International" title="NASBITE International">NASBITE International</a>, the European International Business Academy and other associations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Market_Reports_and_Country_Profiles_for_International_Trade">Market Reports and Country Profiles for International Trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Federation_of_International_Trade_Associations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Market Reports and Country Profiles for International Trade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2009, the Federation of International Trade Associations signed a public-private partnership with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Commercial_Service" title="United States Commercial Service">United States Commercial Service</a>, the trade promotion arm of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Commerce" title="United States Department of Commerce">United States Department of Commerce (DoC)</a>, thereby sanctioning the publication of the DoC’s market studies on <a href="/wiki/GlobalTrade.net" title="GlobalTrade.net">GlobalTrade.net</a>. In August 2010, FITA signed another similar PPP with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Trade_and_Investment" title="UK Trade and Investment">UK Trade &amp; Investment</a>.</p>
<p>GlobalTrade.net is a knowledge resource service where experts in international trade can post their articles; the content is vetted by an editing team. Content includes international trade experts' analyses, market surveys, tips, white papers, country profiles, experts’ views, webinars, news flows, video tutorials and presentations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_Service_Providers_for_International_Trade">Business Service Providers for International Trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Federation_of_International_Trade_Associations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business Service Providers for International Trade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along with the publication of market surveys and other expert content, <a href="/wiki/GlobalTrade.net" title="GlobalTrade.net">GlobalTrade.net</a> contains a free database of business service providers useful for <a href="/wiki/Import" title="Import">importers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exporters</a>. The site’s business service providers include international marketing consultants, trade finance companies, banks, freight forwarders, quality control firms, lawyers, accountants, customs brokers, international trade instructors and insurance providers for their international operations. GlobalTrade.net allows business service providers to create a personal profile and to facilitate contact with the international trade market; all content posted by experts and global trade service providers is vetted. GlobalTrade.net was launched on November 15, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Professionals use the website to find information and business service providers for their international trade operations. <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, the United States, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> are presently the most popular countries. Categories of service providers include legal, trade support organizations, transportation and logistics, taxes and accounting, marketing and communications, sales and distribution and travel services.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Partner_Marketplaces">International Partner Marketplaces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Federation_of_International_Trade_Associations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International Partner Marketplaces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FITA has signed partnership accords with three major B2B marketplaces: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alibaba.com" title="Alibaba.com">Alibaba.com</a>, Kompass and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Register" title="Thomas Register">Thomasnet</a>: ranked 1st, 13th and 18th in the world respectively by bridgat.com (December 2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
