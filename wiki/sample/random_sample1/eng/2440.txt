<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Inter-Parliamentary Union</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Inter-Parliamentary Union</b> (<b>IPU</b>; <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Union Interparlementaire (UIP)</i></span>) is a global <a href="/wiki/Inter-parliamentary_institution" title="Inter-parliamentary institution">inter-parliamentary institution</a> established in 1889 by <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Passy" title="Frédéric Passy">Frédéric Passy</a> (<a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Randal_Cremer" title="William Randal Cremer">William Randal Cremer</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>). It was the first permanent forum for political multilateral negotiations. Initially, the organization was for individual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">parliamentarians</a>, but has since transformed into an international organization of the parliaments of sovereign states. The national parliaments of 170 countries are members of the IPU, and 11 regional parliamentary assemblies are associate members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-members_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-members-1">[1]</a></sup> The IPU has permanent <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_observers#Other_entities" title="United Nations General Assembly observers">observer status at the United Nations General Assembly</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_organizations_with_consultative_status_to_the_United_Nations_Economic_and_Social_Council" title="List of organizations with consultative status to the United Nations Economic and Social Council">general consultative status with the Economic and Social Council</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-Parliamentary_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eight leading personalities of the IPU have received <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prizes</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li>1901: <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Passy" title="Frédéric Passy">Frédéric Passy</a> (France)</li>
<li>1902: <a href="/wiki/Charles_Albert_Gobat" title="Charles Albert Gobat">Charles Albert Gobat</a> (Switzerland)</li>
<li>1903: <a href="/wiki/Randal_Cremer" title="Randal Cremer">Randal Cremer</a> (United Kingdom)</li>
<li>1908: <a href="/wiki/Fredrik_Bajer" title="Fredrik Bajer">Fredrik Bajer</a> (Denmark)</li>
<li>1909: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auguste_Marie_Fran%C3%A7ois_Beernaert" title="Auguste Marie François Beernaert">Auguste Marie François Beernaert</a> (Belgium)</li>
<li>1913: <a href="/wiki/Henri_La_Fontaine" title="Henri La Fontaine">Henri La Fontaine</a> (Belgium)</li>
<li>1921: <a href="/wiki/Christian_Lous_Lange" title="Christian Lous Lange">Christian Lange</a> (Norway)</li>
<li>1927: <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Buisson" title="Ferdinand Buisson">Ferdinand Buisson</a> (France)</li>
</ul>
<p>The organisation's initial objective was the arbitration of conflicts. The IPU played an important part in setting up the <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Court_of_Arbitration" title="Permanent Court of Arbitration">Permanent Court of Arbitration</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>. Over time, its mission has evolved towards the promotion of <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> and inter-parliamentary dialogue. The IPU has worked for establishment of institutions at the inter-governmental level, including the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, an organization with which it cooperates and with which it has permanent observer status.</p>

<p>Numerous bodies have expressed interest in the possibility of transforming the IPU into a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Parliamentary_Assembly" title="United Nations Parliamentary Assembly">United Nations Parliamentary Assembly</a>, among them the <a href="/wiki/Committee_for_a_Democratic_UN" title="Committee for a Democratic UN">Committee for a Democratic UN</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Bundestag" title="German Bundestag">German Bundestag</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_International" title="Socialist International">Socialist International</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">Liberal International</a> also considers this as an option.</p>
<p>The headquarters of the union have been moved several times since its inception. Locations:</p>
<ul>
<li>1892–1911: <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a> (Switzerland)</li>
<li>1911–1914: <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> (Belgium)</li>
<li>1914–1920: <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> (Norway)</li>
<li>1921 – permanent in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a> (Switzerland)</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-Parliamentary_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Regional parliamentary assemblies may be admitted by the Governing Council as Associate Members</li>
<li>Every Parliament constituted in conformity with the laws of a sovereign State whose population it represents and on whose territory it functions may request affiliation to the Inter-Parliamentary Union. The decision to admit or readmit a Parliament shall be taken by the Governing Council.</li>
</ul>
<p>It is the duty of the Members of the IPU to submit the resolutions of the IPU within their respective Parliament, in the most appropriate form; to communicate them to the Government; to stimulate their implementation and to inform the IPU Secretariat, as often and fully as possible, particularly in its annual reports, as to the steps taken and the results obtained.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organs">Organs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-Parliamentary_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Organs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IPU_Building2010.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/IPU_Building2010.jpg/220px-IPU_Building2010.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/IPU_Building2010.jpg/330px-IPU_Building2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/IPU_Building2010.jpg/440px-IPU_Building2010.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

Headquarters of the IPU in Geneva (2010)</div>
</div>

<p>The organs of the Inter-Parliamentary Union are:</p>
<ul>
<li>the Assembly, meets twice a year. The Assembly shall be composed of parliamentarians designated as delegates by the Members of the Union. The Assembly is assisted in its work by Standing Committees, whose number and terms of reference are determined by the Governing Council; Standing Committees shall normally prepare reports and draft resolutions for the Assembly. No one delegate may record more than ten votes.</li>
<li>the Governing Council. The Governing Council shall normally hold two sessions a year. The Governing Council shall be composed of three representatives from each Member of the Union. The term of office of a member of the Governing Council shall last from one Assembly to the next and all the members of the Governing Council must be sitting members of Parliament. The Governing Council shall elect the President of the Inter-Parliamentary Union for a period of three years. It elects the members of the Executive Committee and appoints the Secretary General of the Union.</li>
<li>the Executive Committee. The Executive Committee shall be composed of the President of the Inter-Parliamentary Union, fifteen members belonging to different Parliaments (elected by the Governing Council; not less than twelve shall be elected from among the members of the Governing Council) and the President of the Coordinating Committee of the Meeting of Women Parliamentarians. The fifteen elected seats will be assigned to the geopolitical groups. Only parliamentarians from States where women have both the right to vote and the right to stand for election are eligible to the Executive Committee. The Executive Committee shall be the administrative organ of the Inter-Parliamentary Union.</li>
<li>and the Secretariat. The Secretariat of the Union comprises the totality of the staff of the organisation under the direction of the Secretary General of the Union.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Association of Secretaries General of Parliaments shall be a consultative body of the Inter-Parliamentary Union.</p>

<ul>
<li>The President of the Inter-Parliamentary Union is Mr. <a href="/wiki/Saber_Hossain_Chowdhury" title="Saber Hossain Chowdhury">Saber Hossain Chowdhury</a> (Bangladesh), elected to this post for a three-year mandate on 16 October 2014 at the 195th session of the Governing Council of the IPU in Geneva.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>As of March 2014, the Secretary General of IPU is Mr. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Chungong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Martin Chungong (page does not exist)">Martin Chungong</a> (Cameroon).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup> He is the first African to hold the post in the IPU's 125-year history. He took over from Mr. <a href="/wiki/Anders_Johnsson_(jurist)" title="Anders Johnsson (jurist)">Anders B. Johnsson</a> (Sweden), who had been in the post since 1998 until his retirement in June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amendments_to_the_Statutes">Amendments to the Statutes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-Parliamentary_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Amendments to the Statutes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Any proposal to amend the Statutes shall be submitted in writing to the Secretariat of the Union at least three months before the meeting of the Assembly. The Secretariat will immediately communicate all such proposals to the Members of the Union. The consideration of such proposed amendments shall be automatically placed on the agenda of the Assembly.</p>
<p>Any sub-amendments shall be submitted in writing to the Secretariat of the Union at least six weeks before the meeting of the Assembly. The Secretariat will immediately communicate all such sub-amendments to the Members of the Union.</p>
<p>After hearing the opinion of the Governing Council, expressed through a simple majority vote, the Assembly shall decide on such proposals by a two-thirds majority vote.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_IPU_and_the_United_Nations">The IPU and the United Nations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-Parliamentary_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The IPU and the United Nations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IPU marked the 50th anniversary of the United Nations, in 1995, by holding a special session in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Headquarters" title="United Nations Headquarters">General Assembly Hall</a> before the start of the session,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A50561_page2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A50561_page2-9">[8]</a></sup> where they planned for closer cooperation with the United Nations. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_Resolution" title="United Nations General Assembly Resolution">General Assembly Resolution</a> passed during that session requested the Secretary-General to put this into action.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_ARES5015_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES5015-10">[9]</a></sup> An agreement was signed between the IPU and the Secretary-General on 24 July 1996 and subsequently ratified by a General Assembly Resolution, where the United Nations recognizes IPU as the world organization of parliaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A51402_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A51402-11">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_ARES517_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES517-12">[11]</a></sup> <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Pursuant" title="wikt:Pursuant">Pursuant</a> to this resolution, the Secretary-General submitted a report <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A52456_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A52456-13">[12]</a></sup> which was noted with appreciation by the General Assembly, who requested further strengthening of cooperation and another report.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_ARES527_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES527-14">[13]</a></sup> This report detailed the measures that had been taken, including opening a liaison office in New York, and cooperation on issues such land-mines and the promotion of representative democracy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A53458_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A53458-15">[14]</a></sup> Following an entire morning of debate<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A53PV46_1998_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A53PV46_1998-16">[15]</a></sup> the General Assembly passed a resolution which simply stated that it "looks forward to continued close cooperation".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_ARES5313_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES5313-17">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following year (1999) the Secretary-General reported on an increased number of areas of cooperation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A54379_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A54379-18">[17]</a></sup> the issue was debated for an entire afternoon<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A54PV41_1999_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A54PV41_1999-19">[18]</a></sup> (interrupted by a minute of silence held for tribute to <a href="/wiki/Vazgen_Sargsyan" title="Vazgen Sargsyan">Vazgen Sargsyan</a>, the Prime Minister of <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> who had just at that time been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999_Armenian_parliament_shooting" title="1999 Armenian parliament shooting">killed by gunmen</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup> and passed a resolution requesting the IPU be allowed to address the Millennium General Assembly directly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_ARES5412_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES5412-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following another report,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A55409_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A55409-22">[21]</a></sup> and another half-day debate,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_A55PV55_2000_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A55PV55_2000-23">[22]</a></sup> the General Assembly welcomed the IPU declaration entitled "The Parliamentary vision for international cooperation at the dawn of the third millennium" and called for the Secretary-General to explore new and further ways in which the relationship could be strengthened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_ARES5519_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES5519-24">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 November 2002 the IPU was granted <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_observers" title="United Nations General Assembly observers">observer status</a> to the General Assembly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_ARES5732_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES5732-25">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the Resolution <i>59/19, Cooperation between the United Nations and the Inter-Parliamentary Union</i>, the UN General Assembly takes note of the recommendations in regard to engaging parliamentarians more systematically in the work of the United Nations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-26">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The final declaration of the Second World Conference of Speakers of Parliament, hosted at United Nations headquarters, took place in September 2005, was entitled <i>Bridging the democracy gap in international relations: A stronger role for parliament</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the Resolution adopted by the UN General Assembly, <i>61/6, Cooperation between the United Nations and the Inter-Parliamentary Union</i>, on 27 November 2006, it calls for <i>the further development of the annual parliamentary hearing at the United Nations and other specialized parliamentary meetings in the context of major United Nations meetings as joint United Nations-Inter-Parliamentary Union events</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-28">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Every year during the fall session of the General Assembly the IPU organises a Parliamentary Hearing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[28]</a></sup> A resolution on cooperation between the United Nations and the IPU allowed for circulation of official IPU documents in the General Assembly.</p>
<p>UN and the IPU cooperate closely in various fields, in particular peace and security, economic and social development, international law, human rights, and democracy and gender issues, but IPU has not obtained the status of UN General Assembly subsidiary organ.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fourth_World_Conference_of_Speakers_of_Parliament">Fourth World Conference of Speakers of Parliament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-Parliamentary_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Fourth World Conference of Speakers of Parliament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The fourth world conference on <a href="/wiki/Seventieth_anniversary_of_the_United_Nations" title="Seventieth anniversary of the United Nations">UN 70th anniversary</a> marked by <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-Moon</a> as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN70" title="UN70">UN70</a>" was organised in September 2015 where Speakers of all IPU(<i><b>Inter-Parliamentary Union</b></i>) member parliaments and of non-member parliaments were invited from across the world. The theme was on peace, democracy, and development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[29]</a></sup></p>
