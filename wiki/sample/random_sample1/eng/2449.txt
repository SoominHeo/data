<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Korea National Assembly Building</h1>

The Korean National Assembly Building in <a href="/wiki/Yeouido" title="Yeouido">Yeouido</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <b>National Assembly Proceeding Hall</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">국회의사당</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">國會議事堂</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Gukhoe-uisadang</span></i>) is the building that serves as the location of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_South_Korea" title="National Assembly of South Korea">National Assembly of the Republic of Korea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislative branch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_South_Korea" title="Government of South Korea">South Korean national government</a>. It is located at <a href="/wiki/Yeouido" title="Yeouido">Yeouido-dong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeongdeungpo-gu" title="Yeongdeungpo-gu">Yeongdeungpo-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.</p>
<p>The current building was completed in 1975. Before 1975 the South Korean government used the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_General_Government_Building,_Seoul" title="Japanese General Government Building, Seoul">General-Government Building</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plenary_chamber" title="Plenary chamber">plenary chamber</a> has seating for 400 people, ostensibly in preparation for new lawmakers in case <a href="/wiki/Korean_reunification" title="Korean reunification">Korean reunification</a> actually occurs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
