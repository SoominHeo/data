<p><b>Materiel</b> (from the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> <i>matériel</i> for equipment or hardware, related to the word material, and sometimes so spelled in English) is <a href="/wiki/Military_technology" title="Military technology">military technology</a> and supplies in <a href="/wiki/Military_supply_chain_management" title="Military supply chain management">military</a> and <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">commercial</a> <a href="/wiki/Supply_chain_management" title="Supply chain management">supply chain management</a>.</p>
<p>In a military context, materiel relates to the specific needs of a force to complete a specific <a href="/wiki/Military_operation" title="Military operation">mission</a>. The term is also often used in a general sense ("men and materiel") to describe the needs of a functioning army. Materiel management consists of continuing actions relating to planning, organizing, directing, coordinating, controlling, and evaluating the application of resources to ensure the effective and economical support of military forces. It includes provisioning, cataloging, requirements determination, acquisition, distribution, maintenance, and disposal. The terms "materiel management", "materiel control", "inventory control", "inventory management", and "supply management" are synonymous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Military materiel is often shipped to and used in severe climates without controlled warehouses and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Material_handling_equipment" title="Material handling equipment">material handling equipment</a>. <a href="/wiki/Packaging_and_labeling" title="Packaging and labeling">Packaging and labeling</a> often needs to meet stringent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technical_specifications" title="Technical specifications">technical specifications</a> to help ensure proper delivery and final use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Materiel in the <a href="/wiki/Distribution_(business)" title="Distribution (business)">commercial distribution</a> context comprises the items being moved by the services of or as the products of the business, as distinct from those involved in operating the business itself.</p>
