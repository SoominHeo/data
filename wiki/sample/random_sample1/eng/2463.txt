<p><b>Guliston</b> also spelled as <b>Gulistan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Uzbek_language" title="Uzbek language">Uzbek</a>: <span lang="uz" xml:lang="uz"><i><b>Guliston</b> / Гулистoн</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Гулистан</span>), formerly known as <i>Mirzachul</i> (Russian: <i>Мирзачуль</i>, until 1961), is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Sirdaryo_Region" title="Sirdaryo Region">Sirdaryo Region</a> in eastern <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. It lies in the southeastern part of the Mirzachül (formerly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golodnaya_Steppe" title="Golodnaya Steppe">Golodnaya</a>) steppe, 75 miles (120 km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Tashkent" title="Tashkent">Tashkent</a>. It has an approx. population of 77,300. The main industry in the area is cottonpicking.</p>
