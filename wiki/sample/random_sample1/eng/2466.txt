<p><i><b>Platycarya</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angiosperms" title="Angiosperms">flowering plants</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Juglandaceae" title="Juglandaceae">Juglandaceae</a>, usually treated as comprising a single species <i><b>Platycarya strobilacea</b></i>, though one to two additional species are accepted by some authors. It is native to eastern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foc_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-foc-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foc1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-foc1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">tree</a> growing to 15 m tall. The <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> are usually pinnate, 15â30Â cm long with 7â15 leaflets (rarely simple, or with up to 23 leaflets), the terminal leaflet present; the leaflets are 3â11Â cm long and 1.5â3.5Â cm broad. The <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> are <a href="/wiki/Catkin" title="Catkin">catkins</a>; the male (pollen) catkins are 2â15Â cm long, the female catkins 2.5â5Â cm long at maturity, hard and woody, superficially resembling a <a href="/wiki/Conifer_cone" title="Conifer cone">conifer cone</a> with spirally arranged scales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foc_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-foc-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foc1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-foc1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Galloyl_pedunculagin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Galloyl pedunculagin (page does not exist)">Galloyl pedunculagin</a> can be found in <i>P. strobilacea</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
