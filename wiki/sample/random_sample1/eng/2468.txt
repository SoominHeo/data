<p><b>Lake Kussharo</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">屈斜路湖</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kussharo-ko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caldera_lake" title="Caldera lake">caldera lake</a> in <a href="/wiki/Akan_National_Park" title="Akan National Park">Akan National Park</a>, eastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hokkaid%C5%8D" title="Hokkaidō">Hokkaidō</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As with many geographic names in Hokkaidō, the name derives from the <a href="/wiki/Ainu_language" title="Ainu language">Ainu language</a>. It is the largest caldera lake in Japan in terms of surface area, and sixth largest lake in Japan. It is also the largest lake in Japan to freeze over completely in winter.</p>

<p>The lake’s central island, Nakajima (not to be confused with another island of the same name in <a href="/wiki/Lake_T%C5%8Dya" title="Lake Tōya">Lake Tōya</a>), is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Composite_volcano" title="Composite volcano">composite volcano</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volcanic_gases" title="Volcanic gases">Volcanic gases</a> render the lake water <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acidic" title="Acidic">acidic</a>, and it supports few fish except in areas where inflowing streams dilute the water. <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_trout" title="Rainbow trout">Rainbow trout</a>, which are also resistant to fairly acidic water, have been artificially introduced. In 1951, a rare form of <a href="/wiki/Cicada" title="Cicada">cicada</a> (<i>Oncotympana maculaticollis</i>) was discovered, and is now protected by the government. The lake is also on the migratory path of the <a href="/wiki/Whooper_swan" title="Whooper swan">whooper swan</a>.</p>
<p>Along the lake shore are several outdoor hot springs and a sand beach with naturally heated sand and hot ground water. Wakoto Peninsula extending into the lake has a number of active sulfurous vents.</p>
<p>The lake is also known as Japan’s <a href="/wiki/Loch_Ness" title="Loch Ness">Loch Ness</a>, after numerous reported sightings on a <a href="/wiki/Lake_monster" title="Lake monster">lake monster</a> dubbed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kusshii" title="Kusshii">Kusshii</a> by the press from 1973.</p>
