<p><b>Gonggar County</b>, also <b>Gongkar</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tibetan_script" title="Tibetan script">Tibetan</a>: གོང་དཀར་རྫོང་ <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tibetan_transliteration_(Wylie)" title="Tibetan transliteration (Wylie)">Wylie</a>: gong dkar rdzong; <a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">贡嘎县</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Gònggá Xiàn</span></i>), is a county of <a href="/wiki/Shannan,_Tibet" title="Shannan, Tibet">Shannan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</a>, one of the 12 counties of the prefecture. It has under its jurisdiction 5 towns, 3 townships, 43 administrative villages (neighbourhood committees) and 168 village development committees and contains notable landmarks such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gonggar_Choide_Monastery" title="Gonggar Choide Monastery">Gonggar Choide Monastery</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Gonggar_Dzong" title="Gonggar Dzong">Gonggar Dzong</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Lhasa_Gonggar_Airport" title="Lhasa Gonggar Airport">Gonggar Airport</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gonggar" title="Gonggar">Gonggar</a> town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Buckley_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Buckley-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legend">Legend</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonggar_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Legend">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fable narrated about this county is that the "first Tibetan was born to the fairy girl, Ruosa and her monkey lover".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonggar_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>History of the county came into limelight with the establishment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sakyapa" title="Sakyapa">Sakyapa</a> school of <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Buddhism" title="Tibetan Buddhism">Tibetan Buddhism</a>. Among the many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dzongs" title="Dzongs">dzongs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monasteries" title="Monasteries">monasteries</a> established in the 14th century the best known are the Gonngar Dzong and the Gonggar Choede Monastery.</p>
<p>In recent history of the county, during the Cultural Revolution of the 1950s, Gonggar County was the scene of armed rebellion between the <a href="/wiki/Kamba_people" title="Kamba people">Kamba</a> rebels (under the banner of “four rivers and six mountain ranges”) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peoples_Liberation_Army" title="Peoples Liberation Army">Peoples Liberation Army</a> of China (PLA); PLA troops were killed and their convoy of army vehicles ambushed here.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-armed_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-armed-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonggar_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gonggar County is located in southern Tibet and has a total area of 2,283 square kilometres (881 sq mi). The <a href="/wiki/Yarlung_Tsangpo_River" title="Yarlung Tsangpo River">Yarlung Tsangpo River</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brahmaputra_River" title="Brahmaputra River">Brahmaputra River</a>), the mother river of Tibet, flows through the Gonggar County and among other counties of Shannan, and is thus rich in water resources. Set in west to east direction, the county is a plateau land with an average altitude of 3,750 metres (12,300 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup> The Gonggar County is situated at a location where Yarlung Tsangpo River is very wide. It is for this reason that the Gonggar Airport was constructed at this location. The airport lies to the west of Rawa-me, which is the capital of the county at the entry of the Namrab Valley, 96 kilometres (60 mi) from Lhasa and 87 kilometres (54 mi) from Tsetang, the capital of Shannan. The county is bounded by the Mount Chuwaru (held sacred in Tibet) opposite to Chusul, the Gompa La (4,794 kilometres (2,979 mi)) Pass to the south on the route to the sacred Yamdrok Lake in West Tibet, on the east to the limits of the Dal Valley on the south bank of the Brahmaputra and Dorje Monastery on the north bank. The county also envelops the southern side valleys of Namrab and Drib counties and the northern side valleys of Leuchung and Trango counties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Climate</dt>
</dl>
<p>The county has monsoon climate. It has four climatic seasons. It has a long winter and hardly any summer season. The average temperature varies from 7.2–8.5 °C (45.0–47.3 °F), with the warmest month average temperature being 15.8 °C (60.4 °F) and the coldest month average temperature is −2.2 °C (28.0 °F). The average annual precipitation is 356.6 millimetres (14.04 in) with 29.9 rainy days. The annual sunshine hours are 3,194 and frost-free period is 142 days. It is subject to natural disasters from frequent storms with 40 days of gale days on an average concentrated in February–April. Solar radiation has been recorded as 7,710 MJ/m<sup>2</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Vegetation</dt>
</dl>
<p>The forest area in the county is 7.8 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mu_(unit_of_area)" title="Mu (unit of area)">mu</a> and of grassland area is 282 mu. The main crops and vegetables grown are barley, winter wheat, spring wheat, pea, broad bean, rape, radish, potato, Yuan cabbage, tomatoes and cauliflower. Medicinal plants and products in the county are creeping <a href="/wiki/Cypress" title="Cypress">cypress</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Opulus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Opulus (page does not exist)">opulus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ginseng" title="Ginseng">ginseng</a> fruits, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snow_lotus" title="Snow lotus">snow lotus</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_caterpillar_fungus" title="Chinese caterpillar fungus">Chinese caterpillar fungus</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fritillaria" title="Fritillaria">Fritillaria</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Musk" title="Musk">musk</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seabuckthorn" title="Seabuckthorn">seabuckthorn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Fauna</dt>
</dl>
<p>The wildlife species recorded in the county are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yak" title="Yak">wild yak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kiang" title="Kiang">wild ass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_antelope" title="Tibetan antelope">Tibetan antelope</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tibetan_gazelle" title="Tibetan gazelle">Tibetan gazelle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Black-necked_crane" title="Black-necked crane">black-necked cranes</a>. The livestock are mainly yak, pianniu, cattle, goats, sheep, horses, donkeys, mules and pigs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonggar_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county has predominantly an agricultural economy and is considered the grain base of Shannan. Tourism also adds to the economic development of the county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Economic progress is witnessed in the county in all sectors of economy. In recent years, the county has held five Trade fairs and 3 cultural fairs to promote local products and Tibetan handicrafts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trade_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-trade-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Energy</dt>
</dl>
<p>The energy resources of the county consist of the two functioning hydropower stations with generating capacity of 530 MWh. The Yamzhog pumped storage power station with installed capacity of 90 MW is under construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Handicrafts</dt>
</dl>
<p>The famous apron, which the Tibetan married women wear, is a handicraft of the Gonggar County in the Jiedexiu area and is known as "Jed Show Apron". The county is synonymous with this apron, which has a distinctive and characteristic style and it is produced here for the last 500 or 600 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clothing_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clothing-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonggar_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gonggar County has a geographical area of 2,283 square kilometres (881 sq mi), with a total population of 50,000 (2003), which gives a population density of 21 per km<sup>2</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maps_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-maps-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonggar_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As the cradle of ancient culture and its enviable position in the rich history of Tibet, Gonggar County has unique and rich historical monuments, natural geological features such as lake and caves, scenic regions and religiously linked mountains.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Dorjezha monastery</dt>
</dl>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1127" data-file-width="1502" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG/220px-Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG/330px-Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG/440px-Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Museum_of_Ethnology_Vienna_002.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fifth_Dalai_Lama" title="Fifth Dalai Lama">Fifth Dalai Lama</a>, instrumental in enlarging the Dorjezha Monastery</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dorjezha_Monastery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dorjezha Monastery (page does not exist)">Dorjezha Monastery</a>, situated on a hilltop, blends well with the rock setting and is named after the rock known as Vraja, the green coloured gemstones found behind the monastery. The literal meaning of ‘Dorjezha’ is “Diamond Hill.” It was initially built on modest lines, on the right bank of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yarlung_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yarlung River (page does not exist)">Yarlung River</a> by a wealthy local man known as Doxie Dorje. The monastery was destroyed completely in 1717 during Zungar invasion of this area. It was, however, rebuilt in 1718. Further, the monastery was supported by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fifth_Dalai_Lama" title="Fifth Dalai Lama">Fifth Dalai Lama</a> and consequently, it underwent major improvements and became one of the famous monasteries of Tibet. At the peak of its popularity 2000 monks of <a href="/wiki/Nyingma" title="Nyingma">Nyingma</a> sect resided here; it holds the distinction of being known as the only ancestral monastery of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sakyapa" title="Sakyapa">Sakyapa</a> sect. It was substantially damaged during the Cultural Revolution. However, it was rebuilt in 1978. At present, 29 monks reside here, and most of them are from the Gonggar County, except one monk who is from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Renbo_County&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Renbo County (page does not exist)">Renbo County</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shigatse_Prefecture" title="Shigatse Prefecture">Shigatse Prefecture</a>. The Management system established is democratic, with each individual member of the Management Commission made responsible for particular tasks such as religious activities, finance and daily matters. It maintains close interaction with the Choqin Monastery (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dege_County" title="Dege County">Dege County</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> province) also of the Nyigma sect and both follow the same teaching and practicing of Buddhist tenets. In this monastery, the practice of the “Reincarnation of the Living Buddha” is followed and hence it has the name of <i>Renchenqinmo Dorjeza Jambeilobsam Gyinmeinamzogyacho</i> and the present Living Buddha is the 10th Reincarnation in this lineage, and as result this monastery has huge reputation among the adherents of <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Buddhism" title="Tibetan Buddhism">Tibetan Buddhism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magazine_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Magazine-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazangjia_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gazangjia-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Mao Zedong statue</dt>
</dl>
<p>A large statue of <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a> has been installed in the Gonggar County in Gonggar town. The statue was erected by contributions made by the people of <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a> Province, where Mao was born. The statue, erected in honour of the Chairman Mao, was in gratitude for the people of his hometown. The statue is erected on a 5.16 metres (16.9 ft) pedestal is 7.1 metres (23 ft) tall and weighs 35 tonnes and made in granite. It cost 480,000 yuan (US$60,000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mao_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mao-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Gyaideshiu town</dt>
</dl>
<p>Gyaideshiu town (also spelt Jiedexiu Town) is famous for the Gyaideshiu 'Bangdian' apron not only among women in Tibet but also is in demand in Nepal, India, Bhutan and Western Europe. The town is located in the county, 16 kilometres (9.9 mi) to its east on the highway towards <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zetang" title="Zetang">Zetang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magazine_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Magazine-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apron_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-apron-10">[10]</a></sup> Bangdian" is an apron of China's Tibetan ethnic group with historic heritage of 1,500 years of hand weaving. This weaving technique is designated as "China's state-level intangible cultural heritage." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apron_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-apron-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonggar_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two National Highways passing through the county, apart from several rural network of roads. The county is also famous for the <a href="/wiki/Lhasa_Gonggar_Airport" title="Lhasa Gonggar Airport">Lhasa Gonggar Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gonggar_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-Gonggar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
