<p><b><a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a></b> competed in the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> for the first time at the <b><a href="/wiki/1968_Summer_Olympics" title="1968 Summer Olympics">1968 Summer Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, from 12 to 27 October 1968.</p>
<p>Comité Olímpico de El Salvador sent a total of 60 athletes to the Games, 52 men and 8 women, to compete in 7 sports. Swimmer <a href="/wiki/Salvador_Vilanova" title="Salvador Vilanova">Salvador Vilanova</a> was selected to carry his nation's flag during the opening ceremony.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competitors">Competitors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Salvador_at_the_1968_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Competitors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Comité Olímpico de El Salvador selected a team of 60 athletes, 52 men and 8 women, to compete in 7 sports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1968MEX_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-1968MEX-1">[1]</a></sup> The contingent marks the largest in El Salvador's Olympic participation. Trap shooter <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Soundy" title="Roberto Soundy">Roberto Soundy</a>, at age 68, was the oldest athlete of the team, while swimmer <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Guerrero" title="Rubén Guerrero">Rubén Guerrero</a> was the youngest at age 13. Each respectively holds the distinction of being the all-time oldest and youngest Olympic participants from El Salvador.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1968MEX_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-1968MEX-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
