<p><b>Gongliao District</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">貢寮區</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Gòngliáo Qū</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Pe%CC%8Dh-%C5%8De-j%C4%AB" title="Pe̍h-ōe-jī">Pe̍h-ōe-jī</a>: <i><span lang="nan" xml:lang="nan">Kòng-liâu-khu</span></i>; literally: "gift hut") is a rural <a href="/wiki/District_(Taiwan)" title="District (Taiwan)">district</a> in the eastern part of <a href="/wiki/New_Taipei_City" title="New Taipei City">New Taipei City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the most eastern district on the main <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Island_of_Taiwan" title="Island of Taiwan">island of Taiwan</a> and at its most eastern tip is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_of_San_Diego" title="Cape of San Diego">Cape of San Diego</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gongliao_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Japanese_rule" title="Taiwan under Japanese rule">period of Japanese rule</a>, Gongliao was called Kōryō Village <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">貢寮庄</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, and was governed under Kīrun District, <a href="/wiki/Taihoku_Prefecture" title="Taihoku Prefecture">Taihoku Prefecture</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gongliao_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of the district is approximately 99.97 square kilometers. There are about 14,077 people living in the district.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fulong_Beach">Fulong Beach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gongliao_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fulong Beach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Fulong Beach</b>, also known as Fulong Bathing Beach (福隆海水浴場), is located at Fulong Village, Gongliao Township, New Taipei City at Northeastern Taiwan. It is the outlet of the Shuang River.</p>
<p>The sands there are golden, which is rare in Taiwan. Nearby is a camping site named Longmen. This beach is a favorite in the summer for people living in northern Taiwan. It is also a popular area for surfing, windsurfing, and fishing.</p>
<p>The Shuang River divides the beach into inner and outer parts. There is a footbridge named Rainbow Bridge (彩虹橋) linking the two parts. However, the watercourse of the river varies every year to some extent; sometimes the bridge is functional; other times it is not, because the seaside end would be in the river or in the sea.</p>
<p>Since 2000, the Hohaiyan Rock Festival (貢寮國際海洋音樂祭) has been held here for 3 to 5 days in the summer. Normally, it takes place in mid-July, but sometimes it has been delayed due to typhoon interruption or damage to the band shell facilities. Also the watercourse of the Shuang River sometimes changes shape or size, influencing the location of these temporary band shells.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aodi.2C_Taiwan">Aodi, Taiwan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gongliao_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Aodi, Taiwan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Aodi</b> (sometimes Audi, Aoti, Auti; <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">澳底</span>) is a small fishing village in Gongliao. It is a popular tourist destination owing to its scenic setting and many seafood restaurants. It is also close to the controversial <a href="/wiki/Lungmen_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Lungmen Nuclear Power Plant">Lungmen Nuclear Power Plant</a>, under construction since 1999. A nearby beach, Yenliao (鹽寮), was the site of the first landing for the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Taiwan_(1895)" title="Japanese invasion of Taiwan (1895)">Japanese invasion of Taiwan</a> in 1895.</p>
