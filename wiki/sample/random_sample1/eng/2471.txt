<p>The <b>Gongshan muntjac</b> (<i>Muntiacus gongshanensis</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msw3_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-msw3-3">[3]</a></sup> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Muntjac" title="Muntjac">muntjac</a> (a type of <a href="/wiki/Deer" title="Deer">deer</a>) living in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gongshan" title="Gongshan">Gongshan</a> mountains in northwestern <a href="/wiki/Yunnan" title="Yunnan">Yunnan</a>, southeast <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>. Recently, it has also been found to occur in <a href="/wiki/Arunachal_Pradesh" title="Arunachal Pradesh">Arunachal Pradesh</a> in northeastern India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The earlier references of occurrence of the <a href="/wiki/Hairy-fronted_muntjac" title="Hairy-fronted muntjac">hairy-fronted muntjac</a> <i>Muntiacus crinifrons</i> in Arunachal Pradesh are actually Gongshan muntjakc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> More recently, genetic studies have shown it to be very closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Hairy-fronted_muntjac" title="Hairy-fronted muntjac">hairy-fronted muntjac</a>, possibly close enough to be considered the same species despite different coloration, though this position is disputed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup> Ongoing <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a> is a major threat to its survival.</p>
