<p><i><b>Beyond the Realm of Conscience</b></i> is a 2009 Hong Kong television series. Produced by <a href="/wiki/Mui_Siu-ching" title="Mui Siu-ching">Mui Siu-ching</a>, the serial is one of two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">TVB</a> productions to celebrate along with the channel's 42nd anniversary, the other being <i><a href="/wiki/Born_Rich_(TV_series)" title="Born Rich (TV series)">Born Rich</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The drama aired five days a week on the TVB network with 45-minute episodes starting October 19, 2009.</p>
<p>Set in the latter years of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>, <i>Beyond the Realm of Conscience</i> tells the story of palace maid Lau Sam-ho and her relations in the Imperial Palace, beginning with her experiences in the Imperial Household Bureau (尚宮局). The series title roughly means "Plots in the Palace" and is a play on the phrase (攻心計), a homonym which roughly means "Plots that target and attack the heart".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gong Choi-king is forced back into the palace with her daughter, Lau Sam-ho and their servant, Yiu Kam-ling. Gong Choi-King comes from a very talented background of jewellery making, therefore she was assigned the task of making a beautiful, gold hairpiece in the shape of a phoenix, with a luminous pearl as the eye and the feathers of a river kingfisher as the tail for the Empress Dowager. However, during the Empress' daily walk around the imperial garden, the phoenix headpiece suddenly weeps tears of blood. Taking this as a sign of bad luck, she throws the hairpiece away and demands the jewellery maker to be severely punished. Gong Choi-King suffers harsh beating as her punishment and eventually passes away. Before passing away, she tells Sam-ho to always remember the significance of her name("Speak good words, do good deeds, show good will."). (Sam-ho literally means three goodness) She also tells her to help and work together with Kam-ling as if they are related sisters.</p>
<p>Around the time of Choi-king's death, Dowager Concubine Cheng's son, Lee Yi (prince) encounters the six years old Sam-ho. He showed kindness to Sam-ho by giving her some white flowers from Choi-king's favourite tree, the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Viburnum" title="Viburnum">viburnum</a> (瓊花), so she can take it to her dying mother. Empress Dowager was still very angry because of the hair piece and asked the new head of the Jewellery department, Yuen Chui-wan to shatter it. However, Yuen Chui-wan believed that the hairpiece is a one of a kind masterpiece, therefore she injures her own hand to protect it. She tells Empress Dowager that the hair piece is full of bad luck and if it is shattered all the bad luck will merge with the air. Taking this as an advantage Empress Dowager forces Dowager Concubine Cheng to wear the hair piece and tells her because of her good spirit she can block the bad luck from the entering the palace. After a short period of time bad luck begins to happen to Dowager Concubine Cheng and her son Lee Yi. The story was told that Lee Yi fell off a tree, while trying to reach for his kite. The fall injured his head causing his brain to remain as a 6-year-old boy forever. Lee Yi uses this mental illness as an excuse to escape the imminent death threat from the Empress Dowager, Sam-ho gives him some sweet lotus seed candy to comfort him on the journey. Because of this, the young prince begins to develop a small affection for her.</p>
<p>The Imperial Household Bureau is responsible for managing and directing all household services to the Emperor and the Imperial family of the Tang Dynasty. It is made up of four departments, namely the Houses of Jewelry, Attire, Food, and Furnishing. Sam-ho and Kam-ling are introduced into the bureau in their childhood and are brought up as the disciples in the palace. The two girls are very diligent. Yuen Tsui-wan, Head of the Jewels, and Chung Suet-ha, Head of Attire, are both very fond of Sam-ho for she is a kind-hearted young girl and shows great capabilities in the decorative arts of jewellery and silk work. Both of them fight bitterly to have Sam-ho in their departments. In the end, Sam-ho is assigned to Department of Attire while, Kam-Ling is assigned to the Department of Jewelry.</p>
<p>Years later, Sam-ho meets Ko Hin-yeung, a palace scholar and also a chess teacher, the two shares a secret love compassion for one and another. The young prince Yi, has now grown up and he is returning to the palace. Later on in the series he becomes the Emperor after an evading assassinations with the help of Sam-ho and Hin-yeung. He promotes Hin-yeung as the Palace General because of his faithfulness. Sam-ho later becomes the Head of the Jewelry Department. Even though the Emperor has deep feeling for Sam-ho and wants to have Sam-ho as his concubine, he decides to bury his feeling for her when he discovers that Hin-yeung and Sam-ho are deeply in love and that they were planning to leave the palace but stayed to help the Emperor.</p>
<p>Sam-ho and Kam-ling continue to enjoy a very close friendship, treating each other as sisters and this provokes jealousy among other palace girl servants. Time and time again some of them will try to get Sam-ho and Kam-ling into trouble. However, Sam-ho's honesty and kind attitude endears many powerful people in the palace and they always come to her aid. Whereas, Kam-ling uses her cunning mind to get rid of her enemies. As time passed, Kam-ling realised in order to protect herself from the bullying and to be able to survive in the palace she has to be someone who is powerful and respected. Therefore, she slowly works on a scheme to gain favour of the Empress Dowager Cheng, Yi's mother. She was eventually chosen as one of the Yi's concubines. From there she works on a bigger scheme to get rid of all her competitors, which eventually includes Sam-ho.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Lau Sam-Ho</b> - Sam-Ho is a kind-hearted maid who took her mother and father's teachings to heart. Her parents taught her to speak kind words, do good deeds, and bear a kind heart. She always cares for and helps everyone in the palace to the best of her abilities. She is very close with Yiu Kam-Ling, with whom she has sworn sisterhood. She is the love interest of both Ko Hin-Yeung and Emperor Lee Yi. Sam-ho started her service in the Department of Embroidery, but was eventually promoted to be the Head of Jewellery Proceedings. She was later chosen as Lee Yi's concubines because she was pregnant with Hin-Yeung's child. Lee Yi made her his concubine to save her.</p>
<p><b>Yiu Kam-Ling</b> - Kam-Ling initially starts out as a maid in the Department of Jewellery. During her time in the department, she suffers and perpetrates back-stabbing and torture. Her suffering leads her to believe that she has to plot, manipulate, and formulate schemes in order to survive and avoid further suffering in the palace. She eventually marries Emperor Lee Yi and becomes his concubine. She continuously schemes to destroy her competitors. She eventually became delusional and insane after Emperor Lee Yi punished her with lifelong confinement to her palace pavilion for all her evil deeds.</p>
<p><b>Yuen Chui-Wan</b> - In the beginning, Chui-Wan serves as Deputy Leader of Jewellery Proceedings. Her dedication and hard work are eventually recognized, and she is promoted to serve as Head of the same department. Chui-Wan is a self spoken, wise woman who never accuses anyone without proof. Throughout the series, she is made Head of Furnishing Proceedings and Head of Embroidery Proceedings. She is in a feud with Suet-Ha (Michelle Yim) because she accuses her of tampering with her food by adding an ingredient to which she was allergic. As a result of her sickness, she was not permitted to leave the palace with her lover, General Man. It is later revealed that she has a son with General Man. By series end, it's implied that Bo Gut-chong took their son out of the palace and left him in the care of an outside healer.</p>
<p><b>Chung Suet-Ha</b> - Suet-Ha starts out as Head of Embroidery Proceedings. She mistakes Chiu-wan for sabotaging her many years ago, causing her permit to leave the palace to be revoked. As a result, she was unable to see her dying mother. This starts a feud between the two friends who have once sworn sisterhood. She always opposes Chui-Wan. They eventually reconcile their differences. She is later promoted to be the Head of Palace Proceedings. When she thinks that Chui-Wan killed her to be husband purposely, she begins to try to make Chui-Wan suffer and wants to get revenge for her to-be husband. They reconciled by the end of the series and remained friends.</p>
<p><b>Ko Hin-Yeung</b> - The Emperor's personal guard. This role is assigned to him because of his loyalty to the emperor. He is skilled in martial arts, and even taught Lee Yi several moves that save his life. He also saves Emperor Lee Yi twice from the assassins sent by the previous emperor and the grand empress. He is the lover of Sam-ho and later marries her. They have a daughter in the last episode.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_basis">Historical basis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Historical basis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Parts of the plot of the drama series are inspired or based on historical facts or beliefs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dramatized_historical_figures">Dramatized historical figures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Dramatized historical figures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Guo_(Tang_dynasty)" title="Empress Dowager Guo (Tang dynasty)">Empress Dowager Guo</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Susan_Tse" title="Susan Tse">Susan Tse</a>) - Empress Dowager and Grand Empress Dowager Guo is based on a historical figure of the same name, who was recorded in history as being the rival of <a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Zheng" title="Empress Dowager Zheng">Empress Dowager Zheng</a>.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Zheng" title="Empress Dowager Zheng">Empress Dowager Zheng</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Mary_Hon" title="Mary Hon">Mary Hon</a>) - based on a historical figure of the same name, initially a maid to the Imperial Household who caught the Emperor's attentions and was elevated to the status of an imperial concubine.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xu%C4%81nzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuānzong of Tang">Emperor Xuānzong of Tang</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Moses_Chan" title="Moses Chan">Moses Chan</a>) - based on Emperor Xuānzong who succeeded Empress Dowager Guo's grandson <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Wuzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Wuzong of Tang">Emperor Wuzong of Tang</a> as Emperor of the Tang.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Emperor_Wuzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Wuzong of Tang">Emperor Wuzong of Tang</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Edwin_Siu" title="Edwin Siu">Edwin Siu</a>) - based on the Emperor Wuzong who succeeded his brother <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Wenzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Wenzong of Tang">Emperor Wenzong of Tang</a> as Emperor of the Tang.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Emperor_Muzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Muzong of Tang">Emperor Muzong of Tang</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Eric_Li_(actor)" title="Eric Li (actor)">Eric Li (actor)</a>) - based on the Emperor that preceded by <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xianzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xianzong of Tang">Emperor Xianzong of Tang</a> and succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Jingzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Jingzong of Tang">Emperor Jingzong of Tang</a>. Neither of the latter two are featured in the series.</p>
<p><b>Li Jun, Prince of Qi</b> 杞王李峻 (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeffrey_Wong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeffrey Wong (page does not exist)">Jeffrey Wong</a>) - based on the eldest son of Emperor Wuzong, of whom nothing is known, in the series he assisted Emperor Xuānzong in defeating the character of Ma Yuan Zhi in return for the throne which he rejected in the end.</p>
<p><b>Consort Wang</b> 王貴妃 (<a href="/wiki/Tracy_Ip" title="Tracy Ip">Tracy Ip</a>) - based on the favourite concubine of Emperor Wuzong, <a href="/wiki/Consort_Wang_(Wuzong)" title="Consort Wang (Wuzong)">Consort Wang (Wuzong)</a> 王賢妃. Consort Wang was never titled <i>guifei</i> in reality. During her lifetime she held the position of a <i>cairen</i> which was a lower rank of imperial concubine than a <i>guifei</i>. She was posthumously honoured as <i>yinfei</i> by Emperor Xuãnzong, who was in reality moved by Consort Wang's devotion to her husband.</p>
<p><b>Ma Yuan Zhi</b> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Kwok_Lun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lee Kwok Lun (page does not exist)">Lee Kwok Lun</a>) - loosely based on a trusted and powerful eunuch of the same name under the rule of Emperor Xuãzong. The character portrayed by Lee Kwok Lun is a combination of two powerful figures, the eunuch Ma Yuan Zhi and military statesman Ma Zhi, who were known dually as the "Two Mas".</p>
<p><b>Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Li_Deyu" title="Li Deyu">Li Deyu</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Lau_Dan" title="Lau Dan">Lau Dan</a>) - based on Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Li_Deyu" title="Li Deyu">Li Deyu</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Trivia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager" title="Empress Dowager">Empress Dowager</a> or Grand Empress Dowager Guo was resented by Concubine Dowager (later Empress Dowager) Zheng. The two women were rivals for a long period of time, especially after the ascension of the latter's son Emperor Xuãnxong. The Grand Empress Dowager was disrespected by Empress Dowager Zheng and Lee Yi to such an extent that she really attempted suicide by throwing herself of the tower.</li>
<li>Grand Empress Dowager Guo tried to commit suicide as she failed to demand respect from Emperor Xuānzong and Empress Dowager Zheng. She tried to jump from the Qinzheng Tower, but failed. That night, she died, though the cause of her death remains a mystery. It is suspected that Emperor Xuãnzong hashed her put to death.</li>
<li>Consort Wang was in the rank of <i>cairen</i> instead of <i>guifei</i> during her lifetime as her family was neither prominent nor noble. It was only after her death that she was elevated to the rank of <i>yinfei</i>. It is traditionally held that Consort Wang and her husband were truly in love with each other.</li>
<li>Instead of being sentenced to death by Empress Dowager Guo, in history she committed suicide because she was saddened by the death of her spouse. This moved even the jealous concubines who resented the devotion she had from her husband, and even Emperor Xuãnzong himself.</li>
<li>Li Yi is believed to have sought refuge at a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taoist" title="Taoist">Taoist</a> monastery at some point, though this is not a confirmed historical fact.</li>
<li>Li Yi was thought to have had low intelligence and confidence before he ascended to the throne. However, after his ascension he revealed extraordinary abilities as a political leader.</li>
<li>Emperor Wuzong did suffer during the latter part of his reign in health due to alchemists' pills. In the series, he was given a pill by the fictional <i>huifei</i> who thought it would be of benefit to him. Many of the Tang Emperors actually believed in the purported health benefits of these pills, though modern scientific evidence reveals that these pills were toxic.</li>
<li>Emperor Wuzong was killed by ambitious powerful <a href="/wiki/Eunuch" title="Eunuch">eunuchs</a> that held immense power at the latter part of the Tang Dynasty, which was already nearing its end during the time setting of the series. The eunuchs in the series killed him to place Li Yi on the throne because they thought that he would be easy to control, as the Prince of Guang put on a facade.</li>
<li>The Imperial Household Bureau was one of the six Imperial Bureaus of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>.</li>
<li>The series is set during the latter years of the Tang Dynasty, around about half a century before the Tang Dynasty's end. The Tang Empire at this time was controlled by powerful, ambitious, <a href="/wiki/Political_corruption" title="Political corruption">corrupt</a> eunuchs, and the Tang Emperors gradually lost more and more power to them. The Tang Dynasty reached its peak in culture and power during the reign of Emperor Xuānzong's ancestor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Emperor Xuanzong</a>'s reign, before it was sent into decline by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/An_Shi_Rebellion" title="An Shi Rebellion">An Shi Rebellion</a> during Xuanzong's reign. Emperor Xuānzong was an Emperor during the decline of the Tang Empire, and he was the last strong ruler of the Tang Dynasty.</li>
<li>In the television drama, <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoism</a> appears as the initially as the religion and accepted philosophy of the imperial family and imperial government. However, after Li Yi comes to the throne, <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> and Buddhist philosophy is discussed by the characters in their dialogue, and Buddhist <a href="/wiki/Worship" title="Worship">worship</a> took the place of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taoist" title="Taoist">Taoist</a> worship. Emperor Xuānzong revived Buddhist worship that was unsupported by the few Emperors before him. Buddhism flourished under the Tang Empire, and it was during this golden age in Chinese history that the system of adapting to the three schools of <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a>, Taoism and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Buddhism" title="Chinese Buddhism">Chinese Buddhism</a> was solidified for the first time. This ideology of adapting to the philosophies of all three would last for millennia. Confucianism was promoted primarily for its appreciation of various virtues, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collectivist" title="Collectivist">collectivist</a> ideals of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_family" title="Chinese family">family</a> units and interdependence between the people and units of the state. Taoism was promoted for its appreciation of <a href="/wiki/Nature" title="Nature">nature</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balance_(ability)" title="Balance (ability)">balance</a> and moderation in all things, as opposed to the <a href="/wiki/Legalism_(Chinese_philosophy)" title="Legalism (Chinese philosophy)">Legalist</a> ideals of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_China" title="Ancient China">Ancient</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_nobility" title="Chinese nobility">feudal</a> China. Buddhism was imported as an <a href="/wiki/Indian_religions" title="Indian religions">Indian</a> faith and remains hugely popular from the Tang Dynasty.</li>
<li>In history Empress Dowager Zheng outlived Li Yi and saw her grandson <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Yizong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Yizong of Tang">Emperor Yizong of Tang</a> ascend the throne as a grand empress dowager. As the eldest son of Li Yi, in fact, Yizong was born 14 years before Li Yi's ascension But all through the series, Li Yi is seen as childless. In the series, Emoress Dowager Zheng is portrayed more sympathetically in a tilt towards the rights of the <a href="/wiki/Working_class" title="Working class">working class</a> than the picture of a resentful and socially ambitious empress dowager drawn by historians. Historians often view Empress Dowager Guo with sympathy in contrast, as a woman who was born into nobility.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_inaccuracies">Cultural inaccuracies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Cultural inaccuracies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>During the Tang Dynasty, there were actually a limited number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chairs" title="Chairs">chairs</a>. Within the drama, chairs are excessively common.</li>
<li>The Tang palace had six imperial bureaus. In the drama, food and clothing is prepared under the Imperial Household Bureau, while in fact they were prepared within their own, individual bureaus.</li>
<li>Members of the imperial <a href="/wiki/Harem" title="Harem">harem</a> would not have referred to each other as "sisters" during the Tang dynasty and earlier, a common misconception.</li>
<li>Parents of girls working as servants in the palace were not allowed to vocally recognise their daughters.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> player is dressed in Japanese clothing from a later period in Japanese history.At the time, early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kimonos" title="Kimonos">kimonos</a> inspired by <a href="/wiki/Hanfu" title="Hanfu">hanfu</a> were already popular amongst Japanese women (Japanese culture was very different from China's before the Japanese began importing Chinese cultural elements and forms of government around 500CE) though it wasn't quite as common amongst Japanese men.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the successful <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">Korean</a> blockbuster drama, <a href="/wiki/Dae_Jang_Geum" title="Dae Jang Geum">Jewel in the Palace</a>, reached a final peak 50 rating points, and topped the chart in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> in 2005, Hong Kong had been waiting for another breathtaking production to the likes of Jewel in the Palace. After In 2004, the highly successful TVB drama series War and Beauty gripped a huge audience and gained excellent ratings. Producer Mui Siu Ching, who is highly familiar to this type of Darker-plot, with her experiences in dramas such as <a href="/wiki/Forensic_Heroes" title="Forensic Heroes">Forensic Heroes</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Brink_of_Law" title="The Brink of Law">The Brink of Law</a>, moved straight to commission this drama production which genre to costume series revolving around the conflicts and struggle within the inner palace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In late 2008, The <a href="/wiki/Sales" title="Sales">Sale</a> <a href="/wiki/Presentation" title="Presentation">Presentation</a> finally confirmed with an important casts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The more <a href="/wiki/Casting" title="Casting">Casting</a> then introduced at the costume fitting on February 17, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The series was originally planned to start filming in December 2008, but the executives felt it had potential and so it became a grand production; filming was pushed to February 2009 with the script modified from the original 25 episodes to become 33, and new costumes were especially designed for the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The original working title known as <i>Palace Schemes</i> linked the story to scheming hearts of Ladies in the Royal palace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Although its Chinese title kept on the same meaning, Official English title changed to <i>Beyond the realm of conscience</i>,to prolific more catchy and lure more viewer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Furthermore, when initial script was first submitted, it was temporarily called <i>Lau Sam Ho</i>, represent <a href="/wiki/Charmaine_Sheh" title="Charmaine Sheh">Charmaine Sheh</a>'s role. On the way to production after the sale presentation successfully release, The Script writer managed to change, possible for the current drama in contrast with its illustration. It led over 50% of storyline from the teaser to change as almost of scene in sale presentation not able to exist despite the main plot still provided. It yielded the some development in casting, costume, setting, character and screenplay as well.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series is in a <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> format popular throughout <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>. It revolves around <a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">palace</a> scheming, which has been featured in numerous <a href="/wiki/TVB" title="TVB">TVB</a> television series before Beyond the Realm of Conscience and also beyond <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, China and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> in dramas from <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">Korea</a> and other East Asian countries These series were made very popular in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> possibly by <a href="/wiki/War_and_Beauty" title="War and Beauty">War and Beauty</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/TVB" title="TVB">TVB</a> in 2004 also as an <a href="/wiki/Anniversary" title="Anniversary">anniversary</a> grand production. However, Beyond the Realm of Conscience is rather a series that carries the message that good (Lau Sam Ho, <a href="/wiki/Charmaine_Sheh" title="Charmaine Sheh">Charmaine Sheh</a>) always beats evil (Yiu Kam Ling, <a href="/wiki/Tavia_Yeung" title="Tavia Yeung">Tavia Yeung</a>. <a href="/wiki/War_and_Beauty" title="War and Beauty">War and Beauty</a> depicts the lives of four imperial concubines who all scheme to rise.</p>
<p>Beyond the Realm of Conscience tells the story of the life of a protagonist who struggles to survive in the <a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">palace</a>, but rises in status through a kind heart. Beyond the Realm of Conscience, however, is far shorter in length. The television series is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Costume_drama" title="Costume drama">costume drama</a>, using elaborate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Costumes" title="Costumes">costumes</a> and sets to attract audiences. However, Beyond the Realm of Conscience also uses historical figures an events in its plot, such as <a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Guo_(Tang_dynasty)" title="Empress Dowager Guo (Tang dynasty)">Grand Empress Dowager Guo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beyond_the_Realm_of_Conscience&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initial audience anticipation of the series before airing was mostly positive, reported as being a highly anticipated drama following its sales presentation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Some audience also looked forward to <a href="/wiki/Tavia_Yeung" title="Tavia Yeung">Tavia Yeung</a> being cast as a <a href="/wiki/Villain" title="Villain">villain</a> for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Some viewers also criticised <a href="/wiki/Charmaine_Sheh" title="Charmaine Sheh">Charmaine Sheh</a>'s character of "Lau Sam Ho" as being "too perfect" and therefore unrealistic.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tavia_Yeung" title="Tavia Yeung">Tavia Yeung</a>'s acting skills initially received criticism from viewers saying that she was not "evil" enough for her role as "Yiu Gam Ling", however by the end of the series she won praise for her facial acting, which showed off her villainy. Her role as "Yiu Gam Ling" was a favorite to win the best actress award in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TVB_Anniversary_Awards_(2009)" title="TVB Anniversary Awards (2009)">2009 TVB Anniversary Awards</a>, however rather than <i>Best Actress</i> she won the awards for <i>Best Performance</i> and <i>My Favorite Female Character</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Netizens also strongly praised her character, which replaced the character of the Empress Dowager, portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Susan_Tse" title="Susan Tse">Susan Tse</a>, as the lead villain in the series from episode 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Charmaine also received praise for her acting, in particular in the final episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The series also received acclaim from critics for its elaborate costumes and plot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The comparison of the series to TVB's 2004 <a href="/wiki/War_and_Beauty" title="War and Beauty">War and Beauty</a> received much retaliation from fans. The plotting and scheming of the characters in <i>Beyond the Realm of Conscience</i> was repeatedly inferior to that of <i>War and Beauty</i>. Others argued that the series was superior in portraying the vulnerability of those less powerful. Charmaine and <a href="/wiki/Moses_Chan" title="Moses Chan">Moses Chan</a>, who starred in both series, defended the popularity of both series.</p>
<p>Ratings wise the drama performed extremely well from the start, the first episode received an index rating of 34 points (2.14 million viewers) and peaking at 36 points (2.29 million viewers), breaking <a href="/wiki/TVB" title="TVB">TVB</a>’s 2003 record for a first episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The second week of the series was better received, with a peak of 41 points for episode 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> It maintained a steady progress in subsequent weeks. <a href="/wiki/Charmaine_Sheh" title="Charmaine Sheh">Charmaine Sheh</a> also hoped that <i>Beyond the Realm of Conscience</i>, would break <i>Jewel in the Palace’s</i> record. For the final episode the series managed to receive 50 points for the scene in which the characters of <i>Sam Ho</i> and <i>Ko Hin Yeung</i> were reunited. The announcement that the drama attracted 1% more viewers than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewel_in_the_Palace" title="Jewel in the Palace">Jewel in the Palace</a>, with 98% of viewers against the formers's 97%, pleased both drama's producer and cast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and meant that <i>Beyond the Realm of Conscience</i> finished as <a href="/wiki/TVB" title="TVB">TVB</a>'s most viewed series for 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
