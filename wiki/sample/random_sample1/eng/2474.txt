<p><b>Golog</b> (or <b>Guoluo</b>) <b>Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">果洛藏族自治州</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Guǒluò Zàngzú Zìzhìzhōu</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_alphabet" title="Tibetan alphabet">Tibetan</a>: <span lang="bo" style="font-family: 'Qomolangma-Dunhuang', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchen', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchung', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Suring', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sutung', 'Qomolangma-Title', 'Qomolangma-Subtitle', 'Qomolangma-Woodblock', 'DDC Uchen', Kailash, Jomolhari, 'TCRC Youtso Unicode', 'Tibetan Machine Uni', 'Noto Sans Tibetan', 'Microsoft Himalaya'; font-size:1.25em; vertical-align:top; word-wrap: break-word;">མགོ་ལོག་བོད་རིགས་རང་སྐྱོང་ཁུལ་</span>, <a href="/wiki/Wylie_transliteration" title="Wylie transliteration">Wylie</a>: <i>Mgo-log Bod-rigs rang-skyong-khul</i>) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_prefecture" title="Autonomous prefecture">autonomous prefecture</a> occupying the southeastern corner of <a href="/wiki/Qinghai" title="Qinghai">Qinghai</a> province, People's Republic of China. The prefecture has an <a href="/wiki/Area" title="Area">area</a> of 76,312 km<sup>2</sup> (29,464 sq mi) and its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">seat</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Maq%C3%AAn_County" title="Maqên County">Maqên County</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golog_Tibetan_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Golog Prefecture is located in the southeastern part of <a href="/wiki/Qinghai" title="Qinghai">Qinghai</a>, in the upper basin of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gyaring_Lake" title="Gyaring Lake">Gyaring Lake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ngoring_Lake" title="Ngoring Lake">Ngoring Lake</a> on the western edge of the prefecture are considered to be the source of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a>. However, these lakes do receive water from rivers that flow from locations even further west, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qumarleb_County" title="Qumarleb County">Qumarleb County</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Yushu_Tibetan_Autonomous_Prefecture" title="Yushu Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture">Yushu Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture</a>.</p>
<p>The lay of the land of the prefecture is largely determined by the <a href="/wiki/Amne_Machin" title="Amne Machin">Amne Machin</a> mountain range (max elevation 6,282 m), which runs in the general northwest- to-southeast direction across the entire prefecture, and beyond. The existence of the ridge results in one of the great bends of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a>, which first flows for several hundreds of kilometers toward the east and southeast along through the entire Golog Prefecture, along the southern side of the Amne Machin Range, until it reaches the borders of Gansu and Sichuan; it and then turns almost 180 degrees and flows toward the northwest for 200–300 km (120–190 mi) through several prefectures of the northeastern Qinghai, forming a section of the northeastern border of the Golog prefecture.</p>
<p>Several sections of the <a href="/wiki/Sanjiangyuan_National_Nature_Reserve" title="Sanjiangyuan National Nature Reserve">Sanjiangyuan ("Sources of Three Rivers") National Nature Reserve</a> are within the prefecture.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golog_Tibetan_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Construction for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golog_Airport" title="Golog Airport">Golog Airport</a> began in September 2012 and the airport opening date is 1 July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>3,000 km (1,900 mi) of new roads are expected to be built by 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
