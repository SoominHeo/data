<p><b>Church discipline</b> is the practice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Censuring" title="Censuring">censuring</a> church members when they are perceived to have <a href="/wiki/Christian_views_on_sin" title="Christian views on sin">sinned</a> in hope that the offender will <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Repentance_(theology)" title="Repentance (theology)">repent</a> and be <a href="/wiki/Reconciliation_(theology)" title="Reconciliation (theology)">reconciled</a> to God and the church. It is also intended to protect other church members from the influence of sin. <a href="/wiki/Excommunication" title="Excommunication">Excommunication</a> is usually considered a last resort if a person does not repent of their sin.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Catholic_Church_discipline">Catholic Church discipline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_discipline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Catholic Church discipline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Congregation_for_the_Doctrine_of_the_Faith" title="Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith">Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith</a> is the oldest of the nine congregations of the Roman Curia. Among the most active of these major Curial departments, it oversees Catholic doctrine. The CDF is the modern name for what used to be the Holy Office of the Inquisition.</p>
<p>According to Article 48 of the Apostolic Constitution on the Roman Curia, Pastor Bonus, promulgated by Pope John Paul II on June 28, 1988: "the duty proper to the Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith is to promote and safeguard the doctrine on the faith and morals throughout the Catholic world: for this reason everything which in any way touches such matter falls within its competence."</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Congregation_for_Divine_Worship_and_the_Discipline_of_the_Sacraments" title="Congregation for Divine Worship and the Discipline of the Sacraments">Congregation for Divine Worship and the Discipline of the Sacraments</a> is the congregation of the Roman Curia that handles most affairs relating to liturgical practices of the Latin Catholic Church as distinct from the Eastern Catholic Churches and also some technical matters relating to the Sacraments.</p>
<p>In some contexts, <i>church discipline</i> may refer to the rules governing an ecclesiastical <a href="/wiki/Ordination" title="Ordination">order</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priests</a> or <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monks</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Clerical_celibacy" title="Clerical celibacy">clerical celibacy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protestant_Church_discipline">Protestant Church discipline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_discipline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Protestant Church discipline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along with preaching and proper administration of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sacraments" title="Sacraments">sacraments</a>, <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestants</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Reformation</a> considered it one of the <a href="/wiki/Marks_of_the_Church" title="Marks of the Church">marks of a true church</a>. Church discipline is mentioned several times in the Bible<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In I Corinthians 5 and other passages, the Bible teaches that sin if not dealt with in a congregation can contaminate other members of the body of Christ, as leaven spreads through bread. This was an important doctrine in the development of different branches of the <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Brethren" title="Plymouth Brethren">Plymouth Brethren</a> movement. It is also an important topic of discussion in many churches today. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Confession_of_Faith" title="Westminster Confession of Faith">Westminster Confession of Faith</a> sees the three steps of church discipline as being "admonition", "suspension from the sacrament of the Lord's Supper for a season" and then finally <a href="/wiki/Excommunication" title="Excommunication">excommunication</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fundamentalist_Corrective_Church_discipline">Fundamentalist Corrective Church discipline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_discipline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fundamentalist Corrective Church discipline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ultimate authority resides in Christ, who authorizes the Church to use it as needed. (Matthew 18:17)</p>
<p><b>Corrective Discipline</b> is for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Troublemakers and those who sow discord. (Romans 16:17)</li>
<li>The unruly and disorderly. (1st Thessalonians 5:14)</li>
<li>Those who disobey the great <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctrines" title="Doctrines">doctrines</a> of the faith. (2nd Thessalonians 3:13-14)</li>
<li>Those who deny the great doctrines of the faith. (1st Timothy 6:3-4)</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Procedures in Discipline</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Arrange a private meeting with the offender. (Matthew 18:15)</li>
<li>If a private meeting fails, meet with them and several witnesses. (Matthew 18:16)</li>
<li>Admonish and warn them. (Titus 3:10)</li>
<li>As a final resort, bring the matter up to the whole church. (Matthew 18:17)</li>
<li>Remove their membership, and avoid them. (Romans 16:17)</li>
<li>Be ready to forgive them when <a href="/wiki/Repentance" title="Repentance">repentance</a> occurs. (2nd Corinthians 2:7)</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Purpose of Discipline</b></p>
<ul>
<li>To maintain the standards of the church to a watching world. (Matthew 5:13-16)</li>
<li>To keep sin from spreading throughout the church. (Joshua 7:3); (1st Corinthians 5:6-7)</li>
<li>Help the guilty person find their way to God. (2nd Corinthians 2:6-8)</li>
<li>To escape God's judgment upon habitually sinning saints. (1st Corinthians 11:30)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
