<p><b>Taika Gon</b> (権 泰夏, originally <b>Tae-ha Kwon</b>, 권 태하, June 2, 1906 – October 10, 1971) was a Korean runner. He competed in the marathon at the 1932 Olympics and finished in ninth place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He competed for Japan under his Japanese name as <a href="/wiki/Korea_under_Japanese_rule" title="Korea under Japanese rule">Korea</a> was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Empire" title="Japanese Empire">Japanese Empire</a> at the time. The name is based on the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a> pronunciation of his Korean <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">hanja</a> name.</p>
