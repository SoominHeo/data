<p>The <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CcTLD" title="CcTLD">ccTLD</a>) <b>.io</b> is assigned to the <a href="/wiki/British_Indian_Ocean_Territory" title="British Indian Ocean Territory">British Indian Ocean Territory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The .io domain is administered by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Internet_Computer_Bureau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Internet Computer Bureau (page does not exist)">Internet Computer Bureau</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_registry" title="Domain name registry">domain name registry</a> company based in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>'s ad targeting currently treats .io as a <a href="/wiki/Generic_top-level_domain" title="Generic top-level domain">generic top-level domain</a> (gTLD) because "users and webmasters frequently see [the domain] more generic than country-targeted".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The .io domain extension has existed since 1997.</p>
<p>The first .io domain was registered in 1998, when <a href="/wiki/Levi_Strauss_%26_Co." title="Levi Strauss &amp; Co.">Levi Strauss &amp; Co.</a> registered the domain levi.io.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">Specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Labels for .io domains may only contain <a href="/wiki/Alphanumeric" title="Alphanumeric">alphanumeric</a> characters and hyphens and must be between 3 and 63 characters long. Domain names cannot begin with, or end with, a hyphen symbol, and may not contain two consecutive hyphens.The entire domain name may not contain more than 253 characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfc1034_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-rfc1034-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The right to administer domain names is given to approved organisations by the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Assigned_Numbers_Authority" title="Internet Assigned Numbers Authority">Internet Assigned Numbers Authority</a> (IANA). The Internet Computer Bureau (ICB) administers .io domains. This <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_registry" title="Domain name registry">domain name registry</a> is a British company, and operates for this purpose under the name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Network_information_center" title="Network information center">NIC</a>.IO. The company also holds the rights to sell <a href="/wiki/.sh" title=".sh">.sh</a>-domain and <a href="/wiki/.ac" title=".ac">.ac</a>-domains, the top-level domains for the South Atlantic islands of Saint Helena and Ascension, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Registration_and_restrictions">Registration and restrictions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Registration and restrictions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both individuals and organisations are allowed to register .io-domains.</p>
<p>In order to register .io domains you do not need to be registered or established in the British Indian Ocean territory. In order to register third-level domains such as <i>xyz.com.io</i> you have to be an inhabitant of the area. Any <a href="/wiki/Second-level_domain" title="Second-level domain">second-level domains</a> used by NIC.IO and <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domains</a> can not be used as a third-level domain. For example, the domain "com.com.io", "org.com.io" and "biz.com.io" are all restricted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nic.io_rules_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-nic.io_rules-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Domain names in .io may not be used "for any purpose that is sexual or pornographic or that is against the statutory laws of any Nation". Breaching this requirement "NIC.IO reserves the right to immediately deactivate the offending registration".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nic.io_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nic.io-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dot-io domains may be registered for a minimum of one year, and a maximum of 5 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Domain names in .io are currently priced higher than those in other TLDs. Registering available .io-domains starts from around Â£30 / $32.88 US per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nic.io_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-nic.io-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Startups">Startups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Startups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The .io gTLD has become a popular alternative for <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startup companies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_services">Online services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Online services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>.io domains are often used for <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open-source_software" title="Free and open-source software">open source</a> projects, APIs (such as put.io API and pen.io API) and online services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rub.io is a shortened URL that was used for <a href="/wiki/Marco_Rubio_presidential_campaign,_2016" title="Marco Rubio presidential campaign, 2016">the 2016 U.S. presidential campaign</a> of <a href="/wiki/Marco_Rubio" title="Marco Rubio">Marco Rubio</a>. Likewise, hrc.io is a shortened URL used for <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton_presidential_campaign,_2016" title="Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2016">2016 U.S. presidential campaign</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Games">Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some popular browser-based games such as <a href="/wiki/Agar.io" title="Agar.io">agar.io</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slither.io" title="Slither.io">slither.io</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wings.io" title="Wings.io">wings.io</a> use the .io ccTLD. <a href="/wiki/Itch.io" title="Itch.io">itch.io</a> is a video game distribution service.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.io&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to a <a href="/wiki/Gigaom" title="Gigaom">Gigaom</a> interview with Paul Kane, chairman of the Internet Computer Bureau, the domain name registry is required to give some of its profits to the British government, for administration of the <a href="/wiki/British_Indian_Ocean_Territory" title="British Indian Ocean Territory">British Indian Ocean Territory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gigaom_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-gigaom-10">[10]</a></sup> As no indigenous <a href="/wiki/Chagossians" title="Chagossians">Chagossians</a> remain in the territory, the only residents being about 4,000 United States military personnel and contractors,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> it is contended that the indigenous Chagossians do not receive any monetary benefit from the sale of the domain names.</p>
<p>After being questioned as a result of the interview, the British Government denied receiving any funds from the sale of .io domain names, and argued that consequently, the profits could not be shared with the Chagossians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
