<p><b>Gjúki</b> (also <i>Gebicca</i>, <i>Gifica</i>, <i>Gibica</i>, <i>Gebicar</i>, <i>Gibicho</i> or <i>Gippich</i>) was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Burgundy" title="King of Burgundy">King of the Burgundians</a> in the late 4th century until his death in or around 407. He was the father of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gundomar" title="Gundomar">Gundomar I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giselher_of_Burgundy" title="Giselher of Burgundy">Giselher</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gundaharius" title="Gundaharius">Gunther</a>.</p>
<p>He is mentioned in <a href="/wiki/Widsith" title="Widsith">Widsith</a> as <i>Gifica</i> and as <i>Gjúki</i> in the eddic poem <i><a href="/wiki/Atlakvi%C3%B0a" title="Atlakviða">Atlakviða</a></i>, where he was the father of Gunnar (see <a href="/wiki/Gunther" title="Gunther">Gunther</a>). As one of the earliest kings of the <a href="/wiki/Nibelung" title="Nibelung">Nibelungs</a>, the clan is called the Gjúkungar.</p>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Prose_Edda" title="Prose Edda">Prose Edda</a>,</i> <a href="/wiki/Snorri_Sturluson" title="Snorri Sturluson">Snorri Sturluson</a> says that Gjúki was the father of sons Gunnar and <a href="/wiki/Hagen_(legend)" title="Hagen (legend)">Hogni</a> and a daughter <a href="/wiki/Gudrun" title="Gudrun">Gudrun</a>. Gotthorm (slayer of Sigurd) is his stepson from his wife Grimhild's previous marriage.</p>
<p>The <i>Prose Edda</i> mentions Gudny, a second daughter of Gjúki and Grimhild. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gudrunarkvida" title="Gudrunarkvida">Gudrunarkvida</a>, this second daughter is named Gullrond.</p>
