<p><b>Cho Kyu-hyun</b> (born February 3, 1988),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russell2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russell2014-1">[1]</a></sup> better known mononymously as <b>Kyuhyun</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_people" title="Korean people">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. He is best known as a member of South Korean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boy_group" title="Boy group">boy group</a> <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior" title="Super Junior">Super Junior</a>, its sub-groups <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-K.R.Y." title="Super Junior-K.R.Y.">Super Junior-K.R.Y.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-M" title="Super Junior-M">Super Junior-M</a> and a former member of the South Korean ballad group <a href="/wiki/SM_the_Ballad" title="SM the Ballad">S.M. the Ballad</a>. He is one of the first four Korean artists to appear on Chinese postage stamps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stamp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-stamp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kyuhyun was born in Nowon, a district in Seoul. Kyuhyun has an older sister named Cho Ara. Kyuhyun's father is in the education business and owns a school. Kyuhyun's mother also runs an art academy and a guesthouse called 'MOM House' located at Myeongdong. Kyuhyun was initially urged to study law and would have been a lawyer if he had not become a singer. His original plans were abandoned once Kyuhyun joined a band in high school, where he became more interested in music and singing. His father was against his dream of singing but allowed him to do so if he did well on his college entrance exams and was accepted into a university. Kyuhyun enrolled in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyunghee_University" title="Kyunghee University">Kyunghee University</a> with a major in Post-Modern Music. He received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> in 2013 and is currently enrolled in the university's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master%27s" title="Master's">master's</a> program. Kyuhyun won third place at the <i>Chin Chin Singing Competition</i> in 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9309:_Debut_with_Super_Junior.2C_K.R.Y_and_Super_Junior-M">2006–09: Debut with Super Junior, K.R.Y and Super Junior-M</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–09: Debut with Super Junior, K.R.Y and Super Junior-M">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior" title="Super Junior">Super Junior</a>, <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-K.R.Y." title="Super Junior-K.R.Y.">Super Junior-K.R.Y.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-M" title="Super Junior-M">Super Junior-M</a></div>
<p>Kyuhyun officially debuted in May 2006 as part of Super Junior, formerly "Super Junior 05", the first generation of the 12-member rotational boy band, who had debuted in November 2005 with the single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TWINS_(Knock_Out)" title="TWINS (Knock Out)">TWINS (Knock Out)</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In March 2006, SM Entertainment began to recruit new members for the next generation. However, plans changed and the company declared a halt in forming future Super Junior generations. Following his addition to the group, they dropped the suffix "05" and became officially credited as Super Junior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kyuhyun made his first appearance as a part-time model of Super Junior in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/News_broadcast" title="News broadcast">news broadcast</a> on 23 May 2006 where the group introduced their new single, <i><a href="/wiki/U_(Super_Junior_song)" title="U (Super Junior song)">U</a></i>. His debut performance was on 27 May at <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s <i>I-Concert</i> with "U", which was also the group's first comeback performance as a permanent group. The CD single "<a href="/wiki/U_(Super_Junior_song)" title="U (Super Junior song)">U</a>" was released on 7 June 2006 and was their most successful single until the release of "<a href="/wiki/Sorry,_Sorry_(Super_Junior_song)" title="Sorry, Sorry (Super Junior song)">Sorry, Sorry</a>" in March 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2006, Kyuhyun, along with <a href="/wiki/Yesung" title="Yesung">Yesung</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryeowook" title="Ryeowook">Ryeowook</a>, was placed in Super Junior's first subgroup, <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-K.R.Y." title="Super Junior-K.R.Y.">Super Junior-K.R.Y.</a>. The trio specialize in ballads and debuted on 5 November on <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>'s <i>Music Bank</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Kyuhyun made contributions to the soundtrack of the tvN drama <a href="/wiki/Hyena_(soundtrack)" title="Hyena (soundtrack)">Hyena</a>, released in November 2006, with a solo track titled "Smile". Along with fellow member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryeowook" title="Ryeowook">Ryeowook</a>, Kyuhyun was featured in song "Wish" (소원), in labelmate <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">TVXQ</a>'s repackaged edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Mirotic" title="Mirotic">Mirotic</a></i> in 2008.</p>
<p>In April 2008, Kyuhyun was put into the seven-member sub-group <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior-M" title="Super Junior-M">Super Junior-M</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Mandopop" title="Mandopop">Mandopop</a> subgroup of <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior" title="Super Junior">Super Junior</a> for the Chinese music industry. They debuted in China at the 8th Annual Music Chart Awards with the release of their first music video, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U_(Super_Junior-M_song)" title="U (Super Junior-M song)">U</a>" on 8 April 2008. This was followed by the release of the debut Chinese-language <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Me_(Super_Junior-M_album)" title="Me (Super Junior-M album)">Me</a></i> in selected provinces in China on 23 April and 2 May in Taiwan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Kyuhyun also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/The_Grace_(band)" title="The Grace (band)">The Grace</a> in early 2008 to perform "Just For One Day" (하루만) for <a href="/wiki/The_Grace_(band)" title="The Grace (band)">The Grace</a>'s first album <i><a href="/wiki/Hanbeon_Deo,_OK%3F" title="Hanbeon Deo, OK?">Hanbeon Deo, OK?</a></i>.</p>
<p>In July 2009, Kyuhyun sang his first solo track. He was featured on Yoo Youngsuk's 20th anniversary tribute album, with a remake of Yoo's song "7 Years of Love" (7년간의 사랑). He first performed it on 12 July 2009 on the television programme <i>Yoo Hee Yeol's Sketch Book</i>. The song was released as a digital single and on Yoo's album on 30 June, with all proceeds going to <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>. He and bandmate <a href="/wiki/Lee_Donghae" title="Lee Donghae">Donghae</a> performed "Happy Bubble", a commercial theme song for facial wash product "Happy Bath" with actress <a href="/wiki/Han_Ji-min" title="Han Ji-min">Han Ji-min</a>, with a music video released on 19 August 2009.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9313:_S.M._The_Ballad_and_Solo_activities">2010–13: S.M. The Ballad and Solo activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–13: S.M. The Ballad and Solo activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 6 January 2010, Kyuhyun's solo track "Listen To You", for the Korean drama <i>Pasta</i> was released. It peaked at number one on music portals' real time charts. On 5 August, his third solo song "Hope, a dream that never sleeps" (희망은 잠들지 않는 꿈) for the television drama <i>Baker King, Kim Tak Goo</i> was released.</p>

<p>In November 2010, Kyuhyun along with label mates <a href="/wiki/TRAX_(band)" title="TRAX (band)">TRAX</a>’s Jay, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SHINee" title="SHINee">SHINee</a>’s Jonghyun, and new singer Jino formed the project group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM_The_Ballad" title="SM The Ballad">SM The Ballad</a>. It is described as their label's <i>vocal unit group</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> They released their debut album, <i>Miss You</i> on 29 November 2010 and debuted on the music programme <a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a> on 28 November. Their debut album includes a solo track, "Love Again" by Kyuhyun. In 2014, however, he along with TRAX's Jay and Jinho left the group S.M.The Ballad and the group made their comeback with 7 new members, Jonghyun being the only original member remaining in the group.</p>
<p>From 15 December 2010 to mid-January 2011, he rotated as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%27Artagnan" title="D'Artagnan">D'Artagnan</a> with <a href="/wiki/TRAX_(band)" title="TRAX (band)">TRAX</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_(Korean_singer)" title="Typhoon (Korean singer)">Jay "Typhoon" Kim</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Three_Musketeers_(musical)" title="The Three Musketeers (musical)">The Three Musketeers</a></i>, where they co-starred with Yoo Jun Sang, Im Ki Jun, Kim Mu Yul and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dana_Hong" title="Dana Hong">Dana Hong</a> at Chungmu Art Hall, Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He placed first in an on-line poll for the <i>Musical Rising Star Award</i> at the <i>2010 Golden Ticket Awards</i>, for his performance in the role of D'Artagnan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goldenticket_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-goldenticket-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Kyuhyun contributed to group mate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siwon" title="Siwon">Siwon</a>'s drama <i>Poseidon</i> with the track "The Way to Break Up". It was composed by Yoon Jong Shin and included in his monthly project. Kyuyhun then sang "Late Autumn" for Yoon's November monthly project. Kyuhyun also appeared on KBS's <i>Immortal Songs 2</i> replacing his band mate <a href="/wiki/Yesung" title="Yesung">Yesung</a>, winning two rounds.</p>
<p>Kyuhyun was one of four regular MCs on Super Junior's <i>Foresight</i> and in September he replaced fellow member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heechul" title="Heechul">Heechul</a> as a regular MC on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goldfish_(TV_series)" title="Goldfish (TV series)">Radio Star</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He also appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/We_Got_Married" title="We Got Married">We Got Married</a></i> as part of <i>Fighting Junior</i>, supporting fellow member <a href="/wiki/Leeteuk" title="Leeteuk">Leeteuk</a> and on-screen wife actress <a href="/wiki/Kang_So-ra" title="Kang So-ra">Kang So-ra</a>. On 28 January 2012, Kyuhyun was cast as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frank_Abagnale_Jr." title="Frank Abagnale Jr.">Frank Abagnale Jr.</a>, rotating with <a href="/wiki/Um_Ki-joon" title="Um Ki-joon">Um Ki-joon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jeong-hoon" title="Kim Jeong-hoon">Kim Jeong-hoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Park_Gwang-hyun" title="Park Gwang-hyun">Park Gwang-hyun</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Key_(singer)" title="Key (singer)">Key</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shinee" title="Shinee">Shinee</a> in the Korean production of the Broadway musical <i><a href="/wiki/Catch_Me_If_You_Can_(musical)" title="Catch Me If You Can (musical)">Catch Me If You Can</a></i>. The musical ran from 28 March to 10 June at the Blue Square, Samsung Card Hall, in <a href="/wiki/Hannam-dong" title="Hannam-dong">Hannam-dong</a>, Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>From December 2012 to February 2013, he was cast as Frank Abagnale Jr. for the re-run of musical <i>Catch Me If You Can</i>. From March 2013 to April 2013, he was cast as D'Artagnan for the re-run of musical The three musketeers.</p>
<p>On 11 May 2013, it was announced that Kyuhyun would replace labelmate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SHINee" title="SHINee">SHINee</a>‘s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choi_Minho_(singer)" title="Choi Minho (singer)">Minho</a> as the new MC of KBS 2TV‘s ‘Mamma Mia‘.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The first broadcast with him as the main MC was on 19 May 2013. In June 2013, he featured in Super Junior-M's Henry solo song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trap_(Henry_Lau_song)" title="Trap (Henry Lau song)">Trap</a>" alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SHINee" title="SHINee">SHINee</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Taemin" title="Lee Taemin">Taemin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In August 2013, Kyuhyun graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyunghee_University" title="Kyunghee University">Kyunghee University</a>'s postmodern music department with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a>. News sources reported the graduations of Kyuhyun and classmate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Yeonji" title="Kim Yeonji">Kim Yeonji</a> as unusual examples of entertainment stars taking their academic work seriously, about which Kyuhyun remarked, "I worked hard to get into university, so I was very attached."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Solo_debut">2014–present: Solo debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Solo debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 18 January to 23 February 2014, Kyuhyun starred in the musical adaptation of MBC's <a href="/wiki/Moon_Embracing_the_Sun" title="Moon Embracing the Sun">Moon Embracing the Sun</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Seohyun" title="Seohyun">Seohyun</a>. The musical was performed at the Seoul Arts Center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>In May 2014, it was officially announced that Kyuhyun was to star in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Singin%27_in_the_Rain_(musical)" title="Singin' in the Rain (musical)">Singin' In The Rain</a></i> as Don Lockwood, with <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sunny_(singer)" title="Sunny (singer)">Sunny</a> cast as the female lead. The musical was produced by SM C&amp;C, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a>, and ran from 5 June to 22 June 2014 at Chungmu Art Hall in Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>During August 2014, Kyuhyun made a comeback with his fellow <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior" title="Super Junior">Super Junior</a> members in their studio album, <a href="/wiki/Mamacita_(Super_Junior_album)" title="Mamacita (Super Junior album)">Mamacita</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In August 2014, Kyuhyun was cast in his fifth musical, <i>The Days</i>. In 'The Days', he played the second lead character Mooyeong, a witty and free-spirited security guard for Blue House as well as friend of Jeong Hak. Along with Kyuhyun, actors Kim Seung Dae, Oh Jeong Hyuk, and <a href="/wiki/Ji_Chang-wook" title="Ji Chang-wook">Ji Chang Wook</a> played Mooyeong. It ran from October 21, 2014 to January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was officially announced on November 6, 2014 that Kyuhyun would be the next SM Entertainment male solo artist. The album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/At_Gwanghwamun" title="At Gwanghwamun">At Gwanghwamun</a></i> was released on November 13, 2015, making Kyuhyun the first Super Junior member to have a solo debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Kyuhyun made his first music show appearance on KBS2's Music Bank on November 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Just two hours after its online release, the solo album's title track "At Gwanghwamun" started to top several major music sites and in 12 hours Kyuhyun achieved an 'all-kill' by placing his song "At Gwanghwamun" at the top of all nine digital Korean charts. All of his tracks made their way to top 10 on most music sites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2014, it was announced that Kyuhyun would be starring as Prince Phillip for the musical <i>Robin Hood</i>. He will take turns to play the role with <a href="/wiki/Beast_(South_Korean_band)" title="Beast (South Korean band)">BEAST</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoseob" title="Yoseob">Yang Yoseob</a> and actor Park Sunghwan. The musical opened in January 2015 at the D-Cube Arts Center in Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<p>In early 2015 it was announced that Kyuhyun was to take part in a news-based <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a> reality show entitled <i>Fluttering India</i> (두근두근 인도) along with <a href="/wiki/Changmin" title="Changmin">TVXQ's Changmin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choi_Minho_(singer)" title="Choi Minho (singer)">SHINee's Minho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jong-hyun" title="Lee Jong-hyun">CNBLUE's Jonghyun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Sung-kyu" title="Kim Sung-kyu">INFINITE'S Sungkyu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suho" title="Suho">Suho</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EXO_(band)" title="EXO (band)">EXO</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Filming took place in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> during February and the first episode was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a> on April 10, 2015. It has been announced that following series will take place with the cast travelling to different locations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 March, it was announced that singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Lee_Moon-se" title="Lee Moon-se">Lee Moon Se</a> had chosen Kyuhyun to be his new duet partner in his new album, <i>New Direction</i>. Their duet track, <i>She is Coming</i>, was released on April 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September, SM Entertainment announced Kyuhyun would be hold his first solo concert <b>And It's Fall Again</b>, as part of SM Entertainment's concert series <i>The Agit</i>. The concert was held from November 6–8 and 13–15 at the SMTOWN Theatre in SMTOWN’s Coex Artium. His 6 days concert tickets were sold out in 46 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kyuhyun released second <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fall,_Once_Again" title="Fall, Once Again">Fall, Once Again</a></i> with the title track <i>A Million Pieces</i> on October 14, following his comeback performances on music program <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i> on October 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> His next single, <i>The Day We Felt The Distance</i> was released on November 3.</p>
<p>From November 2015 to January 2016, Kyuhyun acted in the 15th anniversary production of the <a href="/wiki/Werther" title="Werther">Werther</a> musical as the main role Werther. He shared the role with senior actors <a href="/wiki/Um_Ki-joon" title="Um Ki-joon">Um Ki-joon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jo_Seung-woo" title="Jo Seung-woo">Jo Seung-woo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Kyuhyun also participated in the OST album of the musical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>From April 11 to June 5, Kyuhyun held solo Japan tour <b>Super Junior Kyuhyun Japan Tour 2016 ~Knick Knack~</b> on several city Fukuoka, Sapporo, Osaka, Hiroshima, Yokohama, Nagoya, and Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Following released debut Japanese single, <i>Celebration ~Bridge to You~</i> on May 25, 2016<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 22, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM_Entertainment" title="SM Entertainment">SM Entertainment</a> announced that Kyuhyun will take a break due to vocal cord nodules. He will also not take part in the ongoing musical, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mozart" title="Mozart">Mozart</a> and 2 weeks recording of <a href="/wiki/Radio_Star_(TV_series)" title="Radio Star (TV series)">Radio Star</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2016, it was announced that his upcoming album will be included Sung Si-kyung's composed song and plans to released in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Kyuhyun will be performed new track from his upcoming album on his concert <b>Autumn Memories: Story of a Novelist</b> from October 29 to 30 at Ehwa Womans University and Busan from November 5 to 6 at Bexco Auditorium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> His third EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Waiting,_Still" title="Waiting, Still">Waiting, Still</a></i> was released on November 10. The album has including double title track, <i>Blah Blah</i> was produced by popular singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Jong-shin" title="Yoon Jong-shin">Yoon Jong-shin</a> and <i>Still</i> was produced by popular ballad singer <a href="/wiki/Sung_Si-kyung" title="Sung Si-kyung">Sung Si-kyung</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 12, Kyuhyun's first Japanese album <i>One Voice</i> and his second Japan tour <b>Super Junior-Kyuhyun Japan Tour 2017 ~One Voice~</b> was announced. The album will be released on February 8, 2017. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The album includes five new song, Japanese single <i><a href="/wiki/Celebration_%EF%BD%9EKimini_Kakeru_Hashi%EF%BD%9E" title="Celebration ～Kimini Kakeru Hashi～">Celebration ～Kimini Kakeru Hashi～</a></i> (include B-side track: <i>Lost my Way</i> and <i>Beautiful</i>) and Japanese version of <i>At Gwanghwamun</i> and <i>Blah Blah</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On November 22, His second Japan tour schedule were revealed. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car_accident">Car accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Car accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the early morning of April 19, 2007, Kyuhyun along with Super Junior members <a href="/wiki/Leeteuk" title="Leeteuk">Leeteuk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shindong" title="Shindong">Shindong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eunhyuk" title="Eunhyuk">Eunhyuk</a>, and two managers were all hospitalized due to a serious car accident when a front tire burst and the car hit a guard rail while returning from the <i>Super Junior Kiss the Radio</i> show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Kyuhyun, who was sitting behind the driver's seat when the accident occurred, was the most injured and had a fractured hip, <a href="/wiki/Pneumothorax" title="Pneumothorax">pneumothorax</a> from broken ribs, and facial scratches and bruises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>The doctor told Kyuhyun's parents that to repair his pierced lungs he would have to <a href="/wiki/Tracheotomy" title="Tracheotomy">make a hole</a> in Kyuhyun's neck thus ending his career as a singer; Kyuhyun's father was the first to immediately reject the operation, stating that Kyuhyun would rather die than not be able to sing, and pleaded that the doctor find another way to repair his son's lungs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kyuhyun_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-kyuhyun-49">[49]</a></sup> He was in a coma for four days and was told by doctors that he, "had about a 20 percent chance to live."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Kyuhyun spent 6 days in the ICU before he was moved to a regular hospital room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Kyuhyun no longer needed the respiratory machine to support his breathing, though it was said that he was only able to walk on his own one month after the accident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kyuhyun_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-kyuhyun-49">[49]</a></sup> Kyuhyun was discharged on July 5, 2007 after 78 days.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_endorsements">Commercial endorsements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cho_Kyuhyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Commercial endorsements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Together with Super Junior/Super Junior-M as a whole group, Kyuhyun has endorsed for various brands such as <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a>, Yamaha(New Fino), 12Plus, SPAO, Kyochon Chicken, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG" title="LG">LG</a> phone, Tony Moly, Lotte Duty Free, Bonrich. In 2013, Kyuhyun and other SM artists <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoona" title="Yoona">Yoona</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choi_Minho_(singer)" title="Choi Minho (singer)">Choi Minho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sulli" title="Sulli">Sulli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seohyun" title="Seohyun">Seohyun</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siwon" title="Siwon">Siwon</a> become the main models for <a href="/wiki/SK_Telecom" title="SK Telecom">SK Telecom</a>. He endorsed Masita Seaweed for 6 years from 2011-2016. In 2016, he was chosen to be the representative of Samdasoo mineral water with SNSD's Taeyeon and A24 skincare products.</p>
