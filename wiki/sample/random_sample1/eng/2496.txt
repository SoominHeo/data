<p><i><b>A Scene at the Sea</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">あの夏、いちばん静かな海。</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ano natsu, ichiban shizukana umi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "That summer, the calmest ocean")</span> is a 1991 Japanese film written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Takeshi_Kitano" title="Takeshi Kitano">Takeshi Kitano</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Scene_at_the_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deaf" title="Deaf">deaf</a> garbage collector, played by <a href="/wiki/Claude_Maki" title="Claude Maki">Claude Maki</a>, is determined to learn how to <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surf</a>—and does so almost at the expense of the girl he loves.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Scene_at_the_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This movie was a break from previous Kitano fare in that it features no gangsters or police. However, Kitano did return to darker themes in his next film, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonatine_(1993_film)" title="Sonatine (1993 film)">Sonatine</a></i>, as well as many later works. In the film, Kitano develops his more delicate, romantic side along with his trademark deadpan approach. In 2002 the Japanese filmmaker directed a similar movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Dolls_(2002_film)" title="Dolls (2002 film)">Dolls</a></i>, a romantic tale about three pairs of lovers.</p>
<p>This film marks the first collaboration between Kitano and composer <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hisaishi" title="Joe Hisaishi">Joe Hisaishi</a>, who had previously created the acclaimed soundtracks of many of <a href="/wiki/Hayao_Miyazaki" title="Hayao Miyazaki">Hayao Miyazaki</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Nausica%C3%A4_of_the_Valley_of_the_Wind_(film)" title="Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind (film)">Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind</a></i>. Hisaishi would go on to compose the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">OSTs</a> for all of Kitano's films until <i>Dolls</i>, after which their collaboration ended.</p>
<p>Occasional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Office_Kitano" title="Office Kitano">Office Kitano</a> actor, Claude Maki, who plays the mute main character, went on to appear in Kitano's film <i><a href="/wiki/Brother_(2000_film)" title="Brother (2000 film)">Brother</a></i> as Ken, a Japanese-American punk set to become leader of a <a href="/wiki/Yakuza" title="Yakuza">Yakuza</a> clan. In <i>Brother</i>, Claude speaks mostly in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American-English" title="American-English">American-English</a> with some occasional Japanese.</p>
