<p><i><b>Waiting in the Summer</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">あの夏で待ってる</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ano Natsu de Matteru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>Waiting in That Summer</i>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series animated by <a href="/wiki/J.C.Staff" title="J.C.Staff">J.C.Staff</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Genco" title="Genco">Genco</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneon" title="Geneon">Geneon</a>, and directed by <a href="/wiki/Tatsuyuki_Nagai" title="Tatsuyuki Nagai">Tatsuyuki Nagai</a>. The screenplay was written by <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Dsuke_Kuroda" title="Yōsuke Kuroda">Yōsuke Kuroda</a>, with original character design by Taraku Uon, who both contributed in the creation of the <i>Please!</i> franchise (<i><a href="/wiki/Please_Teacher!" title="Please Teacher!">Please Teacher!</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Please_Twins!" title="Please Twins!">Please Twins!</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A 12-episode anime television series aired in Japan between January and March 2012 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aichi_Television_Broadcasting" title="Aichi Television Broadcasting">TV Aichi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Broadcasting_System" title="Kyoto Broadcasting System">KBS</a>. Sentai Filmworks has licensed the anime for release in North America. The staff of the anime returned to produce an <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">original video animation</a> episode in August 2014. A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation illustrated by Pepako Dokuta was serialized in <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Media_Works" title="ASCII Media Works">ASCII Media Works</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Daioh" title="Dengeki Daioh">Dengeki Daioh</a></i> magazine.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waiting_in_the_Summer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Kaito Kirishima <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">霧島 海人</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kirishima Kaito</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Nobunaga_Shimazaki" title="Nobunaga Shimazaki">Nobunaga Shimazaki</a></span></dd>
<dd>The male protagonist is a student in his first year of high school. He is an avid videographer and constantly out recording on an 8mm handheld camera that he inherited from his late grandfather. Others sometimes refer to him as "The Director". He is seemingly aware of Kanna's feelings for him, nonetheless he develops a genuine love for Ichika. He also has frequent daydreams of conversing with Ichika and talking out loud sometimes during them. When Ichika revealed her true identity to him, it didn't bother him since he had a thought that she might be from outer space. Kaito and Ichika later confessed to each other and began to date. Before Ichika left to go back to her planet, he promised to love her forever. It is shown that before Kaito and his friends graduated from high school, they completed the film, adding a scene that reveals that Ichika was able to return to Earth again.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Ichika Takatsuki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">貴月 イチカ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takatsuki Ichika</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Haruka_Tomatsu" title="Haruka Tomatsu">Haruka Tomatsu</a></span></dd>
<dd>The female protagonist is an alien whose ship accidentally crash-lands on Earth, thus causing Kaito to sustain fatal injuries. Since her bodily cells consist of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanomachine" title="Nanomachine">nanomachines</a> the capacity of which is medical treatment, she saves him by transferring her cells to him through kissing and initially remains with him in order to be certain that he is in condition. Being an alien unaccustomed to life on Earth, her actions may seem weird in the eyes of others, but she is quick to correct herself if only to hide the fact that she is an alien. She was aware of Kaito's feelings, but had yet to respond with her own. She is kind, polite, friendly and an odd cook (because of her bizarre, yet still edible food combination choices). Like her best friend, Lemon Yamano, she is a third-year student in Kaito's high school. Ichika later revealed herself that she is alien to Kaito and her friends, but it didn't bother them at all, especially Kaito who had a thought she was from outer space. With Kanna's help and influence, she confessed to Kaito and began dating him. Ichika said that she would continue loving Kaito, even after she left. At the end of the series, Ichika is shown in the final scene of the completed film, revealing that she was able to return to Earth again.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Kanna Tanigawa <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">谷川 柑菜</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tanigawa Kanna</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waiting_in_the_Summer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Waiting in the Summer</i> <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> TV series directed by <a href="/wiki/Tatsuyuki_Nagai" title="Tatsuyuki Nagai">Tatsuyuki Nagai</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/J.C.Staff" title="J.C.Staff">J.C.Staff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Genco" title="Genco">Genco</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-staff_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-staff-2">[2]</a></sup> began airing in Japan on January 10, 2012 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aichi_Television_Broadcasting" title="Aichi Television Broadcasting">TV Aichi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Broadcasting_System" title="Kyoto Broadcasting System">KBS</a> television networks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The screenplay was written by <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Dsuke_Kuroda" title="Yōsuke Kuroda">Yōsuke Kuroda</a>, who originally conceived the series with original character designer Taraku Uon. Masayoshi Tanaka based the character design used in the anime on Uon's concepts. The music was mainly produced by Maiko Iuchi, with other members of <a href="/wiki/I%27ve_Sound" title="I've Sound">I've Sound</a>, and the sound director is Jin Aketagawa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-staff_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-staff-2">[2]</a></sup> The opening theme is "Sign" sung by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ray_(Japanese_singer)" title="Ray (Japanese singer)">Ray</a>, featuring lyrics written by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kotoko_(singer)" title="Kotoko (singer)">Kotoko</a> and composed by <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Orito" title="Shinji Orito">Shinji Orito</a> of <a href="/wiki/Key_(company)" title="Key (company)">Key</a>. The ending theme is "Vidro Moyō" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ビードロ模様</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> sung by <a href="/wiki/Nagi_Yanagi" title="Nagi Yanagi">Nagi Yanagi</a>, who also wrote the lyrics, and is composed by Tomoyuki Nakazawa of I've Sound. The single for "Sign" was released on February 8, 2012, and the single for "Vidro Moyō" was released on February 29, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goods_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-goods-4">[4]</a></sup> Sentai Filmworks has licensed the anime for release in North America, and <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a> is streaming the series in North and South America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> licensed the series for release in Australia and New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The staff of the anime returned to produce an <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">original video animation</a> episode released with the Blu-ray box set on August 29, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet_radio_show">Internet radio show</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waiting_in_the_Summer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Internet radio show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Internet_radio" title="Internet radio">internet radio</a> show to promote <i>Waiting in the Summer</i> called <i>Ichika to Rinon no Natsu Machi Radio</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">イチカとりのんのなつまちラジオ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ichika and Rinon's Waiting for Summer Radio</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> had a pre-broadcast on December 26, 2011. It ran for 12 weekly broadcasts between January 16 and April 30, 2012. Produced by Hibiki Radio Station, the show was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Haruka_Tomatsu" title="Haruka Tomatsu">Haruka Tomatsu</a> (the voice of Ichika Takatsuki) and <a href="/wiki/Rina_Hidaka" title="Rina Hidaka">Rina Hidaka</a> (the voice of Rinon).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Printed_media">Printed media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waiting_in_the_Summer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Printed media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation illustrated by Pepako Dokuta was serialized between the March 2012 and February 2013 issues of <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Media_Works" title="ASCII Media Works">ASCII Media Works</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Daioh" title="Dengeki Daioh">Dengeki Daioh</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The first <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volume was released on March 27, 2012; two volumes have been released as of August 27, 2012. The third and final volume will be released on February 27, 2013. <a href="/wiki/Media_Factory" title="Media Factory">Media Factory</a> published two <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novels</a>, written by Ichika Toyogawa with illustrations by Taraku Uon: the first on March 22, 2012 and the second on July 23, 2012. An art book titled <i>The Art of Natsu de Matteru</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">The Art of あの夏で待ってる</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was published by Mitsumura Suiko Shoin on July 18, 2012.</p>
