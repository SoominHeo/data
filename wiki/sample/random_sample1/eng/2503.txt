<p>In <a href="/wiki/Norse_mythology" title="Norse mythology">Norse mythology</a>, <b>Grani</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horse</a> owned by the hero <a href="/wiki/Sigurd" title="Sigurd">Sigurd</a>. He is the horse that Sigurd receives through advice from the Norse god (<a href="/wiki/Odin" title="Odin">Odin</a>). Grani is a descendant of Odin's own steed, <a href="/wiki/Sleipnir" title="Sleipnir">Sleipnir</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attestations">Attestations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Attestations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In chapter 13 of <i><a href="/wiki/V%C3%B6lsunga_saga" title="Völsunga saga">Völsunga saga</a></i>, the hero Sigurðr is on his way to a wood when he meets a long-bearded old man he had never seen before. Sigurd tells the old man that he is going to choose a horse, and asks the old man to come with him to help him decide. The old man says that they should drive the horses down to the river Busiltjörn. The two drive the horses down into the deeps of Busiltjörn, and all of the horses swim back to land but a large, young, and handsome gray horse that no one had ever mounted. The grey-bearded old man says that the horse is from "Sleipnir's kin" and that "he must be nourished heedfully, for it will be the best of all horses". The old man vanishes. Sigurd names the horse Grani, and the narrative adds that the old man was none other than Odin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MORRIS54_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MORRIS54-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Archeological_evidence">Archeological evidence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Archeological evidence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grani is believed to be depicted on several of the <a href="/wiki/Sigurd_stones" title="Sigurd stones">Sigurd stones</a>, which depict imagery from the legend of Sigurd the dragon slayer, including the inscription on <a href="/wiki/Sigurd_stones#S.C3.B6_327" title="Sigurd stones">Sö 327</a> in Gök, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dermanland_County" title="Södermanland County">Södermanland County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In Norse <a href="/wiki/Iconography" title="Iconography">iconography</a>, the depiction of a horse carrying a chest was sufficient to represent Grani carrying the treasure after Sigurd had slain the dragon <a href="/wiki/Fafnir" title="Fafnir">Fafnir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This is supported by a <a href="/wiki/Kenning" title="Kenning">kenning</a> in a Norse poem that refers to "Grani's beautious burden,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> indicating a common understanding of the <a href="/wiki/Motif_(narrative)" title="Motif (narrative)">motif</a>.</p>

<p>In Wagner's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ring_cycle" title="Ring cycle">Ring cycle</a> of operas the name (as "Grane") is given to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Br%C3%BCnnhilde" title="Brünnhilde">Brünnhilde</a>'s horse.</p>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Digimon_Tamers" title="Digimon Tamers">Digimon Tamers</a></i>, Grani was the name of the refitted 'Ark', which was used as a steed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guilmon" title="Guilmon">Gallantmon</a> late in the series. It was directly stated in episode 47 that it was named by its creators after <a href="/wiki/Sigurd" title="Sigurd">Siegfried</a>'s horse, Grani. Grani would later be used to upgrade Gallantmon into his Crimson Mode during the finale of the series and in one of the theatrical movies.</p>
