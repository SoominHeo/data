<p><i><b>Gradius IV: Fukkatsu</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">グラディウスIV 復活</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gradiusu fō: Fukkatsu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, Gradius IV: Resurrection)</span> is the fourth <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade</a> installment in a series of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scrolling_shooters" title="Scrolling shooters">scrolling shooter</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a> developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Konami_Computer_Entertainment_Tokyo" title="Konami Computer Entertainment Tokyo">Konami Computer Entertainment Tokyo</a>. It was preceded by <i><a href="/wiki/Gradius_III" title="Gradius III">Gradius III</a></i>, released in <a href="/wiki/1989_in_video_gaming" title="1989 in video gaming">1989</a>, although spinoffs had been released prior to it such as <i><a href="/wiki/Gradius_Gaiden" title="Gradius Gaiden">Gradius Gaiden</a></i>. This title brings a considerable graphical upgrade, particularly with the use of colored lighting. In addition, there have been several additions and removals to the vaulted weapons system; specifically, the edit mode has been removed and an online ranking system was added.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gradius_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The core gameplay of <i>Gradius IV</i> remains relatively unchanged. However, several changes were made on the weapons system in the transition from <i><a href="/wiki/Gradius_III" title="Gradius III">Gradius III</a></i>. First of all, the edit mode has been completely eliminated, as are the "Snake Option" (s.option), the "Reduce" shield type, and the seventh powerup category. The seventh powerup category was the one that was labeled "!" and would produce detrimental results, such as reducing the speed or eliminating the weapons of the ship.</p>
<p>In this game there are a total of six configurations, the first four of which are migrated from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gradius_II:_Gofer%27s_Ambition" title="Gradius II: Gofer's Ambition">Gradius II: Gofer's Ambition</a></i>. The two new configurations are as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>Configuration: The missile mode is a vertical mine that is released above and below the ship. After a short delay, the mines explode, yielding a large explosion. The double mode is the standard 45-degree angle split. The laser mode is a thin <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armor-piercing" title="Armor-piercing">armor-piercing</a> round that can penetrate multiple enemies.</li>
<li>Configuration 6: The missile mode is the flying torpedo, which produces two missiles that fly forward in front of the ship. The double mode is the tailgun, seen in other configurations. The laser is the twin laser seen in <i><a href="/wiki/Gradius_III" title="Gradius III">Gradius III</a></i>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Starting a tradition that is continued in <i><a href="/wiki/Gradius_V" title="Gradius V">Gradius V</a></i>, this game introduced an online-ranking system. After a player's game was over, a password was presented. This password, which was hashed scoring information, could be uploaded to a Konami website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This was only available in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and was discontinued on August 27 of <a href="/wiki/1999_in_video_gaming" title="1999 in video gaming">1999</a>. This was similarly done for the <i>Gradius III and IV</i> console release. Score recording was disabled approximately a year after its arcade brethren.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gradius_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Gradius IV</i> never received a direct home port of its arcade version. It was included in the <i>Gradius III and IV</i> compilation for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> in 2000 and as a part of <i><a href="/wiki/Gradius_Collection" title="Gradius Collection">Gradius Collection</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> in 2006. The former also includes a boss rush mode (play all the game's bosses in a time attack fashion) and a stage select. A <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_64" title="Nintendo 64">Nintendo 64</a> version titled <i>Gradius 64</i> just planned released for 1999 but was cancelled.</p>
