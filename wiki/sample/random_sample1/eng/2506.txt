<p><i><b>Gravitholus</b></i> (meaning 'heavy dome') was a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a> from the late <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> period (<a href="/wiki/Campanian" title="Campanian">Campanian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faunal_stage" title="Faunal stage">stage</a>, around 75 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mya_(unit)" title="Mya (unit)">million years ago</a>). It was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pachycephalosaur" title="Pachycephalosaur">pachycephalosaur</a>, a type of dinosaur with a thick skull made of hardened bone. It lived in what is now <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, Canada, and was described in 1979 by W. P. Wall and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Galton" title="Peter Galton">Peter Galton</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Type_species" title="Type species">type species</a> is <i>Gravitholus albertae</i>. There is some debate amongst paleontologists as to whether the animal represents a distinct genus, or if it may be synonymous with <i><a href="/wiki/Stegoceras" title="Stegoceras">Stegoceras</a></i>. Recent publications indicate it may be a valid genus.</p>
