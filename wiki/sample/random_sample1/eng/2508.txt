<p><b>Graells's tamarin</b>, <i>Saguinus graellsi</i>, is a species of <a href="/wiki/Tamarin" title="Tamarin">tamarin</a> from the northwestern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Rainforest" title="Amazon Rainforest">Amazon</a> in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> and northeastern <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. It has often been treated as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Black-mantled_tamarin" title="Black-mantled tamarin">black-mantled tamarin</a>, but differs from that species in having a dull <a href="/wiki/Olive_(color)" title="Olive (color)">olive</a>-brown (no reddish-orange) lower back, rump and thighs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The two are often said to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sympatric" title="Sympatric">sympatric</a> in Colombia (a major argument for treating them as separate species), though the accuracy of such reports has been questioned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
