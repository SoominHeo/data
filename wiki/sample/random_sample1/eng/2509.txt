<p>The <b>Graian Alps</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it" xml:lang="it"><i>Alpi Graie</i></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[ˈalpi ˈɡraːje]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Alpes grées</i></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[alp ɡʁe]</a></span>) are a mountain range in the western part of the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Graian_Alps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Graie</i> comes from the <i>Graioceli</i> <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> tribe, which dwelled in the area surrounding the <a href="/wiki/Mont_Cenis" title="Mont Cenis">Mont Cenis</a> pass and the <a href="/wiki/Vi%C3%B9" title="Viù">Viù</a> valley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Other sources claim that the name comes from the Celtic "Graig" meaning rock/stone, literally the Rocky Mountains<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Graian_Alps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Graian Alps are located in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (<a href="/wiki/Rh%C3%B4ne-Alpes" title="Rhône-Alpes">Rhône-Alpes</a>), <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Aosta_Valley" title="Aosta Valley">Aosta Valley</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> (western <a href="/wiki/Valais" title="Valais">Valais</a>).</p>
<p>The French side of the Graian Alps is drained by the river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Is%C3%A8re_River" title="Isère River">Isère</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tarentaise_Valley" title="Tarentaise Valley">Tarentaise</a> valley) and its tributary <a href="/wiki/Arc_(Savoie)" title="Arc (Savoie)">Arc</a> (<a href="/wiki/Maurienne" title="Maurienne">Maurienne</a> valley), and by the <a href="/wiki/Arve" title="Arve">Arve</a>. The Italian side is drained by the rivers <a href="/wiki/Dora_Baltea" title="Dora Baltea">Dora Baltea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orco" title="Orco">Orco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stura_di_Lanzo" title="Stura di Lanzo">Stura di Lanzo</a>, tributaries of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Po_River" title="Po River">Po</a>.</p>
<p>The Graian Alps can also be divided into the following four groups:</p>
<ul>
<li>the Mont Blanc group (north of the Little St Bernard Pass)</li>
<li>the Central group (the watershed between the Little St Bernard Pass and the Col du Mont Cenis)</li>
<li>the Western or French group,</li>
<li>and the Eastern or Italian group.</li>
</ul>
