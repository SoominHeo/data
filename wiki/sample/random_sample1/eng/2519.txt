<p><b>Grasshopper Manufacture Inc.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">の株式会社グラスホッパー・マニファクチュア</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>No Kabushikigaisha Gurasuhoppā Manifakuchua</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">video game developer</a> founded on 30 March 1998 in <a href="/wiki/Suginami" title="Suginami">Suginami</a>, Japan. Grasshopper gained mainstream attention in 2005 for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nintendo_GameCube" title="Nintendo GameCube">Nintendo GameCube</a> and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Killer7" title="Killer7">Killer7</a></i>. In addition to <i>Killer7</i>, they have developed <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michigan:_Report_From_Hell" title="Michigan: Report From Hell">Michigan: Report From Hell</a></i> (released in Japan, Europe) and a number of Japan-only titles. Grasshopper Manufacture was also responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Contact_(video_game)" title="Contact (video game)">Contact</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/No_More_Heroes_(video_game)" title="No More Heroes (video game)">No More Heroes</a></i> and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/No_More_Heroes_2:_Desperate_Struggle" title="No More Heroes 2: Desperate Struggle">No More Heroes 2: Desperate Struggle</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Shadows_of_the_Damned" title="Shadows of the Damned">Shadows of the Damned</a></i>. The company is headed by <a href="/wiki/Goichi_Suda" title="Goichi Suda">Goichi Suda</a>, also known as Suda51, and is noted for its original and imaginative titles - ones that are also fraught with financial risk. Potential losses are often made up by the development of games based on popular anime franchises, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Champloo:_Sidetracked" title="Samurai Champloo: Sidetracked">Samurai Champloo: Sidetracked</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blood%2B:_One_Night_Kiss" title="Blood+: One Night Kiss">Blood+: One Night Kiss</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a>: Sound Impact</i>.</p>
<p>In May 2007, Suda announced during a speech at the 2007 Game Developers Conference that Grasshopper was at the time working on three titles for the Wii, two of which have now been released: <i>No More Heroes</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatal_Frame_IV" title="Fatal Frame IV">Fatal Frame IV</a></i>. There is no information on the status of the third Wii game in development then.</p>
<p>Grasshopper was said to be working on an <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> title, and have presented a concept for a <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> game called <i>Kurayami</i>, a non-linear action adventure inspired by the worrying and confused universe of the Czech writer <a href="/wiki/Franz_Kafka" title="Franz Kafka">Franz Kafka</a>, whom Goichi Suda admires. This was later renamed <i><a href="/wiki/Shadows_of_the_Damned" title="Shadows of the Damned">Shadows of the Damned</a></i>.</p>
<p>On 30 January 2013, Grasshopper Manufacture was acquired by <a href="/wiki/GungHo_Online_Entertainment" title="GungHo Online Entertainment">GungHo Online Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
