<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Grand Slam (professional wrestling)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">For other uses, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Grand_Slam_(disambiguation)" title="Grand Slam (disambiguation)">Grand Slam (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>Grand Slam</b> is an accomplishment in <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a>. It is a distinction given to a professional wrestler who has won four specific <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Championship_(professional_wrestling)" title="Championship (professional wrestling)">championships</a> within a promotion throughout the course of their career. National promotions that recognize this include <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a> (TNA). Notable independent promotions include <a href="/wiki/Florida_Championship_Wrestling" title="Florida Championship Wrestling">Florida Championship Wrestling</a> (FCW). The four titles typically feature three levels of singles championships and a <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">tag team</a> championship. These singles championships include a primary, secondary, and tertiary championship.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="WWE">WWE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Slam_(professional_wrestling)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: WWE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shawn_Michaels_at_WrestleMania_XXIV.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="781" data-file-width="628" height="218" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Shawn_Michaels_at_WrestleMania_XXIV.jpg/175px-Shawn_Michaels_at_WrestleMania_XXIV.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Shawn_Michaels_at_WrestleMania_XXIV.jpg/263px-Shawn_Michaels_at_WrestleMania_XXIV.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Shawn_Michaels_at_WrestleMania_XXIV.jpg/350px-Shawn_Michaels_at_WrestleMania_XXIV.jpg 2x" width="175"/></a>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> (formerly WWF), the term "Grand Slam" was originally used by <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Michaels" title="Shawn Michaels">Shawn Michaels</a> to describe himself upon winning the <a href="/wiki/WWE_European_Championship" title="WWE European Championship">European Championship</a> on September 20, 1997. Michaels previously held the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Championship" title="WWE Championship">WWF Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">Intercontinental Championship</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/World_Tag_Team_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Tag Team Championship (WWE)">Tag Team Championship</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Nash" title="Kevin Nash">Diesel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWEMichaels_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-WWEMichaels-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2001, WWF.com indicated that the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hardcore_Championship" title="WWE Hardcore Championship">Hardcore Championship</a> was an acceptable substitute for the European Championship in the Grand Slam. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glenn_Jacobs" title="Glenn Jacobs">Kane</a>, who had defeated <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a> for the Intercontinental Championship at <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2001)" title="Judgment Day (2001)">Judgment Day</a> on May 20, 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kane1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-kane1-2">[2]</a></sup> was acknowledged as a Grand Slam winner as he had "become the only superstar in World Wrestling Federation history that has held the Intercontinental title as well as the Hardcore, Tag Team and WWF titles".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kane1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kane1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kane2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kane2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2006, <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Angle" title="Kurt Angle">Kurt Angle</a> was noted as being a former Grand Slam winner on WWE.com, having won the WWE, WWE Tag Team, Intercontinental, and European Championship, indicating that WWE considers the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Raw Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Championship</a> to be an acceptable substitute for the World Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWEAngle_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WWEAngle-5">[5]</a></sup> In August 2007, WWE.com published an article listing Shawn Michaels' championship reigns that completed the Grand Slam. They included the WWE, World Heavyweight, World Tag Team, Intercontinental, and European Championship. The inclusion of the <a href="/wiki/World_Heavyweight_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)">World Heavyweight Championship</a> indicated that WWE considered the title to be an acceptable substitute for the WWE Championship in completing the Grand Slam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWEMichaels_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-WWEMichaels-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/ECW_One_Night_Stand_(2006)" title="ECW One Night Stand (2006)">ECW One Night Stand</a> in June 2006, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Van_Dam" title="Rob Van Dam">Rob Van Dam</a> became the first superstar acquired by WWE after the purchase of <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Championship Wrestling">World Championship Wrestling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Extreme_Championship_Wrestling" title="Extreme Championship Wrestling">Extreme Championship Wrestling</a> in 2001 to complete the Grand Slam when he defeated <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a> for the WWE Championship. <a href="/wiki/Booker_T_(wrestler)" title="Booker T (wrestler)">Booker T</a> became the second star acquired by the purchase to complete the Grand Slam when he defeated Rey Mysterio for the World Heavyweight Championship at <a href="/wiki/The_Great_American_Bash_(2006)" title="The Great American Bash (2006)">The Great American Bash</a> in July 2006. Booker has held the World Tag Team, Intercontinental, and Hardcore titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RVDGS_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-RVDGS-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_31" title="WrestleMania 31">WrestleMania 31</a> in 2015, WWE established an updated version of the Grand Slam consisting of the four then-active men's titles in WWE: the WWE, Intercontinental, <a href="/wiki/WWE_United_States_Championship" title="WWE United States Championship">United States</a>, and WWE Tag Team Championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wwenew6_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-wwenew6-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-six_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-six-8">[8]</a></sup> Seven wrestlers have been recognized as Grand Slam winners under these new parameters (including four who were already recognized as Grand Slam winners under the original guidelines). With the brand extension re-established, WWE indicated in 2017 that the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Universal_Championship" title="WWE Universal Championship">WWE Universal Championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE SmackDown Tag Team Championship">SmackDown Tag Team Championship</a> are acceptable substitutes for their counterpart titles as part of the Grand Slam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seven_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-seven-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of January 24, 2017, 15 wrestlers have been recognized as WWE Grand Slam winners under its various incarnations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Slam_(professional_wrestling)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1980" data-file-width="1483" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg/175px-AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg/263px-AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg/350px-AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg 2x" width="175"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AJ_Styles_World_Champion_January_2010.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/A.J._Styles" title="A.J. Styles">A.J. Styles</a> – the first <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">TNA</a> Grand Slam winner, and the only one to win all of TNA's Grand Slam eligible titles</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a> (TNA) Grand Slam winner was crowned on March 15, 2009 at TNA's <a href="/wiki/Destination_X_(2009)" title="Destination X (2009)">Destination X</a> <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_TNA_pay-per-view_events" title="List of TNA pay-per-view events">event</a>. At said event, then three-time <a href="/wiki/Triple_Crown_(professional_wrestling)#Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Triple Crown (professional wrestling)">TNA Triple Crown champion</a> <a href="/wiki/A.J._Styles" title="A.J. Styles">A.J. Styles</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Booker_T_(wrestler)" title="Booker T (wrestler)">Booker T</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNA_Global_Championship" title="TNA Global Championship">TNA Legends Championship</a>. On the March 19 episode of TNA's primary television program, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Impact_Wrestling" title="Impact Wrestling">TNA Impact!</a></i>, announcer <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tenay" title="Mike Tenay">Mike Tenay</a> stated that Styles had become the first TNA Grand Slam winner by capturing the <a href="/wiki/NWA_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="NWA World Heavyweight Championship">World Heavyweight</a> (NWA or <a href="/wiki/TNA_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="TNA World Heavyweight Championship">TNA</a>), <a href="/wiki/NWA_World_Tag_Team_Championship" title="NWA World Tag Team Championship">World Tag Team</a> (NWA or <a href="/wiki/TNA_World_Tag_Team_Championship" title="TNA World Tag Team Championship">TNA</a>), <a href="/wiki/TNA_X_Division_Championship" title="TNA X Division Championship">TNA X Division</a>, and TNA Legends Championships (The Legends Championship has subsequently been renamed the Global, Television, and King of the Mountain Championship).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TNAgrandslam1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-TNAgrandslam1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TNAgrandslam2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-TNAgrandslam2-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following is a list of TNA Grand Slam winners with dates indicating when the wrestler first won the respective championship. Under TNA's definition of the Grand Slam, wrestlers are eligible to be a multiple Grand Slam winner each time they complete a new circuit. Thus far, only A.J. Styles has won the Grand Slam on more than one occasion, as well as being the only one to have held every eligible championship to qualify for the Grand Slam.</p>
<p>On August 15, 2016, the TNA King of the Mountain Championship was once again retired when Lashley unified the title into his TNA World Heavyweight Championship. As a result of which, once again, the number of potential Grand Slam Champions in TNA is limited to former Legends/Global/Television/King of the Mountain Champions.</p>
<p>It is unknown at this time if the <a href="/wiki/Impact_Grand_Championship" title="Impact Grand Championship">Impact Grand Championship</a> is considered as an acceptable tertiary championship for the Grand Slam.</p>
