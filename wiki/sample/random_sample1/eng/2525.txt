<p><b>Grand Kru County</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Liberia" title="Counties of Liberia">county</a> in the southeastern portion of <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a>. One of 15 counties that comprise the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_division" title="Administrative division">first-level of administrative division</a> in the nation, it has eighteen <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Liberia" title="Districts of Liberia">districts</a>. Organized in 1984, its capital is <a href="/wiki/Barclayville" title="Barclayville">Barclayville</a>. The area of the county measures 3,895 square kilometres (1,504 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2008_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-census2008-1">[1]</a></sup> As of the 2008 Census, it had a population of 57,106, making it the least populous county in Liberia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2008_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-census2008-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Grand Kru's County Superintendent is Rosalind Sneh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The county is bordered by <a href="/wiki/River_Gee_County" title="River Gee County">River Gee County</a> to the northeast, <a href="/wiki/Sinoe_County" title="Sinoe County">Sinoe County</a> to the northwest, and <a href="/wiki/Maryland_County" title="Maryland County">Maryland County</a> to the southeast. The southern part of Grand Kru borders the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Kru_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grand Kru was created in 1984/1985 by the merger of the territories of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sasstown_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sasstown Territory (page does not exist)">Sasstown</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kru_Coast_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kru Coast Territory (page does not exist)">Kru Coast</a>. Kru Coast territory had previously been part of Maryland County, and Sasstown territory had previously been part of Sinoe county. As of the 1984 Census, the county had a population of 62,791.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2008_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-census2008-1">[1]</a></sup> A UN Mission in Liberia report issued in April 2005 estimated that the population was 71,000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Kru_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The primary language is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kru_language" title="Kru language">Kru language</a>, which is spoken in several significantly different dialects around the county. Some areas identify heir language as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buah" title="Buah">Buah</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kplio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kplio (page does not exist)">Kplio</a>. <a href="/wiki/Liberian_English" title="Liberian English">Liberian English</a> is the language of school instruction.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Kru_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area has few roads so it has had limited business or residential development. Because of the long period of war since the late 20th century, few investments were made in infrastructure. In June 2005 the UN Integrated Regional Information Network reported that the roads in Grand Kru had decayed and become overgrown by dense bush, rendering them impassable except on foot. The bridge across the Nu River at Barclaville had been destroyed during warfare.</p>
<p>The residents have a subsistence farming economy, based on slash-and-burn techniques of clearing the bush. The most important crops are upland <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arecaceae" title="Arecaceae">palm nuts</a>, and along the coast, fishing. Feed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corn" title="Corn">corn</a> is grown at higher elevations farther inland. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" title="Sugar cane">Sugar cane</a> and several varieties of <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">bananas</a> grow in the wetland areas. Cash crops include <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_bean" title="Cocoa bean">cocoa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kola_nut" title="Kola nut">kola</a>. Locally grown <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piassava_palm" title="Piassava palm">piassava palm</a> are widely used for construction, mats and baskets. Many tropical fruits, domestic and wild, grow in the region including <a href="/wiki/Orange_(fruit)" title="Orange (fruit)">oranges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lime_(fruit)" title="Lime (fruit)">limes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">mangos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soursop" title="Soursop">soursop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Breadfruit" title="Breadfruit">breadfruit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a>.</p>
<p>The people keep <a href="/wiki/Poultry" title="Poultry">poultry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a> and <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goats</a> to supplement their diets and to use for their products; the animals are found in and around most villages. Most animals are of pygmy variety, as larger varieties die quickly due to the heat and humidity.</p>
<p>Prior to the civil war, the region's extensive <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforests</a> contained a wide variety of wildlife including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wild_pig" title="Wild pig">wild pigs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bongo_(antelope)" title="Bongo (antelope)">bongo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dik-dik" title="Dik-dik">dik-dik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pangolin" title="Pangolin">pangolin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civet" title="Civet">civet</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pygmy_hippo" title="Pygmy hippo">pygmy hippo</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_buffalo" title="African buffalo">African buffalo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colobus_monkey" title="Colobus monkey">colobus monkey</a>, all of which are hunted for food and hides. Also found are <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a>, and very small populations of forest <a href="/wiki/Elephant" title="Elephant">elephants</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leopard" title="Leopard">leopards</a>. The warfare disrupted their habitats; in addition, many animals were killed in the course of the war and their populations have fallen.</p>
