<p><b>Grant Mandel Rosenmeyer</b> (born July 3, 1991) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a> best known for his roles in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Tenenbaums" title="The Royal Tenenbaums">The Royal Tenenbaums</a></i> and on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FOX" title="FOX">FOX</a> television <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Beene" title="Oliver Beene">Oliver Beene</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grant_Rosenmeyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rosenmeyer first acted on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macduff_(thane)" title="Macduff (thane)">Macduff</a>'s son in <i><a href="/wiki/Macbeth" title="Macbeth">Macbeth</a></i> and as <a href="/wiki/Gavroche" title="Gavroche">Gavroche</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables_(musical)" title="Les Misérables (musical)">Les Misérables</a></i>. In 2001, he made his feature film debut as Ari Tenenbaum, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Stiller" title="Ben Stiller">Ben Stiller</a>'s son, in <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Tenenbaums" title="The Royal Tenenbaums">The Royal Tenenbaums</a></i>, for which he received a <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a> nomination.</p>
<p>From 2003-2004, Rosenmeyer played the title character in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FOX" title="FOX">FOX</a> television <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Beene" title="Oliver Beene">Oliver Beene</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Levitan" title="Steven Levitan">Steven Levitan</a>. He has made guest appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="Late Night with Conan O'Brien">Late Night with Conan O'Brien</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monk_(TV_Series)" title="Monk (TV Series)">Monk</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jonny_Zero" title="Jonny Zero">Jonny Zero</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Bloods_(TV_series)" title="Blue Bloods (TV series)">Blue Bloods</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Crazy_Ex-Girlfriend_(TV_series)" title="Crazy Ex-Girlfriend (TV series)">Crazy Ex-Girlfriend</a></i>, among others. He also appeared as Wilson, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Make-A-Wish" title="Make-A-Wish">Make-A-Wish</a> patient that blackmails <a href="/wiki/Larry_David" title="Larry David">Larry David</a>, in a memorable episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Curb_Your_Enthusiasm" title="Curb Your Enthusiasm">Curb Your Enthusiasm</a></i>. Additional film credits include Young Mordechai in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hebrew_Hammer" title="The Hebrew Hammer">The Hebrew Hammer</a></i>.</p>
<p>In June 2009, Rosenmeyer's short film <i>Fugue</i> won the award for Best Student Film at the Connecticut Film Festival. He co-wrote and starred in <i>The Smut Locker</i>, a short comedy which garnered a <a href="/wiki/CINE" title="CINE">CINE</a> Golden Eagle Award in 2014. That same year, his feature script <i>The Defectives</i> was a semifinalist for the <a href="/wiki/Nicholl_Fellowships_in_Screenwriting" title="Nicholl Fellowships in Screenwriting">Nicholl Fellowships in Screenwriting</a>.</p>
<p>He appeared alongside <a href="/wiki/Julia_Roberts" title="Julia Roberts">Julia Roberts</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">George Clooney</a> in the 2016 film <i><a href="/wiki/Money_Monster" title="Money Monster">Money Monster</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Jodie_Foster" title="Jodie Foster">Jodie Foster</a>. He also plays the male lead opposite <a href="/wiki/Lindsey_Shaw" title="Lindsey Shaw">Lindsey Shaw</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic comedy</a> <i>Temps</i>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Cinequest_Film_Festival" title="Cinequest Film Festival">Cinequest Film Festival</a> in March 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grant_Rosenmeyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rosenmeyer was born in <a href="/wiki/Manhasset,_New_York" title="Manhasset, New York">Manhasset</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, the son of Debra and Colin Rosenmeyer. He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Ridgefield,_Connecticut" title="Ridgefield, Connecticut">Ridgefield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> and earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tisch_School_of_the_Arts" title="Tisch School of the Arts">Tisch School of the Arts</a> in 2012.</p>
<p>He currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.</p>
