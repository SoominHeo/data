<p><b>Greg McLean</b> is an Australian film director, producer and writer. He came to world attention in 2005 with his debut feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_Creek_(film)" title="Wolf Creek (film)">Wolf Creek</a></i>, creating one of Australia's most memorable and horrific characters, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mick_Taylor_(fictional_character)" title="Mick Taylor (fictional character)">Mick Taylor</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/John_Jarratt" title="John Jarratt">John Jarratt</a>). The long-awaited sequel to his first feature, <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_Creek_2" title="Wolf Creek 2">Wolf Creek 2</a></i> was released February 2013. Mclean also wrote, directed and produced <i><a href="/wiki/Rogue_(film)" title="Rogue (film)">Rogue</a></i> (2007) and was executive producer of <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Hill_(film)" title="Red Hill (film)">Red Hill</a></i> (2010) and <i><a href="/wiki/Crawlspace_(2012_film)" title="Crawlspace (2012 film)">Crawlspace</a></i> (2012). He is also the author of two novels about the fictional character Mick Taylor; Wolf Creek: Origins and Wolf Creek: Desolation Game and the four-part comic book series <i>Dark Axis: Secret Battles of WW2</i> and the graphic novel <i>Sebastian Hawks – Creature Hunter.</i> In 2016, his latest film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Darkness_(film)" title="The Darkness (film)">The Darkness</a></i>, was released to theaters, and a <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_Creek_(TV_series)" title="Wolf Creek (TV series)">Wolf Creek</a></i> TV series was released on Australian streaming service <a href="/wiki/Stan_(company)" title="Stan (company)">Stan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_McLean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the production notes for film <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_Creek_(film)" title="Wolf Creek (film)">Wolf Creek</a></i>, "after training as a fine artist specializing in painting, McLean attended the National Institute of Dramatic Art (NIDA), completing a graduate diploma in directing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-productionnotes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-productionnotes-1">[1]</a></sup> In his early career he worked with theatre director <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armfield" title="Neil Armfield">Neil Armfield</a>, and with <a href="/wiki/Baz_Luhrmann" title="Baz Luhrmann">Baz Luhrmann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Martin_(designer)" title="Catherine Martin (designer)">Catherine Martin</a> at <a href="/wiki/Opera_Australia" title="Opera Australia">Opera Australia</a>.</p>
<p>McLean's first short film, <i>Plead</i>, won a Gold award from the Australian Cinematographers Society (ACS). His short film <i>ICQ</i> screened at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_International_Independent_Film_and_Video_Festival" title="New York International Independent Film and Video Festival">New York International Independent Film and Video Festival</a>, winning "Best Director of a Short Film". Under his production company GMF (Greg McLean Film), McLean produced television commercials and similar work.</p>
<p>McLean's first full-length film was the 2005 Australian independent horror film <i>Wolf Creek</i>. Reviews were mixed, but it achieved a cult following. In the United States, it received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NC-17" title="NC-17">NC-17</a> rating until being edited to obtain an <a href="/wiki/Motion_Picture_Association_of_America_film_rating_system" title="Motion Picture Association of America film rating system">R rating</a>.</p>
<p>Mclean's next film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rogue_(2007_film)" title="Rogue (2007 film)">Rogue</a></i>, in 2007, was about a deadly salt-water crocodile attacking an international group of tourists in Australia's Northern Territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was one of the candidates to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Paranormal_Activity_2" title="Paranormal Activity 2">Paranormal Activity 2</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> McLean produced the Justin Dix thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Crawlspace_(2012_film)" title="Crawlspace (2012 film)">Crawlspace</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2013, McLean returned as writer, producer and director for <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_Creek_2" title="Wolf Creek 2">Wolf Creek 2</a></i>, the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_Creek_(film)" title="Wolf Creek (film)">Wolf Creek</a></i>. John Jarrett reprised his role as serial killer Mick Taylor, and co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Corr" title="Ryan Corr">Ryan Corr</a>.</p>
<p>McLean also joined <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Ben_Hall" title="The Legend of Ben Hall">The Legend of Ben Hall</a></i> as an Executive Producer in February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, McLean directed the poorly received horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Darkness_(film)" title="The Darkness (film)">The Darkness</a></i>. His next films are <i><a href="/wiki/The_Belko_Experiment" title="The Belko Experiment">The Belko Experiment</a></i> and then <i>Jungle</i>.</p>
