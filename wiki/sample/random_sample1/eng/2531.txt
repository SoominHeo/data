<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Gregg Hoffman</b> (June 11, 1963 – December 4, 2005), born in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>, was a film producer responsible for developing <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_(2004_film)" title="Saw (2004 film)">Saw</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_II" title="Saw II">Saw II</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SawIINotes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SawIINotes-1">[1]</a></sup> He studied communications, law and economics at <a href="/wiki/American_University" title="American University">American University</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C" title="Washington, D.C">Washington, D.C</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Hoffman was working on <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_III" title="Saw III">Saw III</a></i> and other films for <a href="/wiki/Twisted_Pictures" title="Twisted Pictures">Twisted Pictures</a> when he died in a hospital in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood,_California" title="Hollywood, California">Hollywood, California</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_causes" title="Natural causes">natural causes</a>. He was 42 years old at his death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The character of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Hoffman_(Saw_series)" title="Mark Hoffman (Saw series)">Mark Hoffman</a> was named in his memory.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The movie <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Silence" title="Dead Silence">Dead Silence</a></i> (2007) was dedicated to him. He was also thanked in the movie <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gross_misconduct_(1993_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gross misconduct (1993 film) (page does not exist)">Gross misconduct</a></i> and mentioned as dedicatee for <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_III" title="Saw III">Saw III</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
