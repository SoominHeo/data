<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_flag" title="National flag">national</a> <b>flag of Grenada</b> was adopted upon independence from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, 7 February 1974. The flag was designed by <a href="/wiki/Anthony_C._George" title="Anthony C. George">Anthony C. George</a> of Soubise in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew_Parish,_Grenada" title="Saint Andrew Parish, Grenada">Saint Andrew Parish</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Civil_ensign" title="Civil ensign">civil ensign</a> is the same, except for a 1:2 rather than 3:5 ratio. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naval_ensign" title="Naval ensign">naval ensign</a> is based on the <a href="/wiki/White_Ensign" title="White Ensign">British White Ensign</a>, with this flag in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_terminology" title="Flag terminology">canton</a> (and extending into the cross).</p>
<p>The six stars in the red border represent <a href="/wiki/Parishes_of_Grenada" title="Parishes of Grenada">the country's six parishes</a>, with the middle star, encircled by a red disk, representing Carriacou and Petite Martinique. The symbol in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_terminology" title="Flag terminology">hoist</a> represents a clove of <a href="/wiki/Nutmeg" title="Nutmeg">nutmeg</a>, one of the principal crops of Grenada. It also represents a link to Grenada's former name, which was the "Isle of Spice".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The red colour of the flag stands for courage and vitality, gold for wisdom and warmth, and green for vegetation and agriculture.</p>
