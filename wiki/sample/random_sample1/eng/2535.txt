<p><b>Grace Jane Gummer</b> (born May 9, 1986) is an American actress. She received a <a href="/wiki/Theatre_World_Award" title="Theatre World Award">Theatre World Award</a> for her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadway_(theatre)" title="Broadway (theatre)">Broadway</a> debut in the 2011 revival of <a href="/wiki/Arcadia_(play)" title="Arcadia (play)">Arcadia</a>. Her television work includes recurring roles on the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Newsroom_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Newsroom (U.S. TV series)">The Newsroom</a></i> (2013–14), and the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Freak_Show" title="American Horror Story: Freak Show">American Horror Story: Freak Show</a></i> (2014–15), as well as regular roles on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Extant_(TV_series)" title="Extant (TV series)">Extant</a></i> (2014–15) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USA_(television)" title="USA (television)">USA</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Robot_(TV_series)" title="Mr. Robot (TV series)">Mr. Robot</a></i> (2016–present).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grace_Gummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gummer was born in New York City, New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The daughter of actress <a href="/wiki/Meryl_Streep" title="Meryl Streep">Meryl Streep</a> and sculptor <a href="/wiki/Don_Gummer" title="Don Gummer">Don Gummer</a>, she grew up in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> with older brother <a href="/wiki/Henry_Wolfe_Gummer" title="Henry Wolfe Gummer">Henry Wolfe Gummer</a>, older sister <a href="/wiki/Mamie_Gummer" title="Mamie Gummer">Mamie Gummer</a> and younger sister <a href="/wiki/Louisa_Gummer" title="Louisa Gummer">Louisa Gummer</a>.</p>
<p>Gummer is of Swiss-German, German-English-Irish extraction through her mother and of Norwegian-German ancestry through her father.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grace_Gummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gummer (billed as Jane Grey) appeared with Streep in the 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_House_of_the_Spirits_(film)" title="The House of the Spirits (film)">The House of the Spirits</a></i>, as the younger version of her mother's character, Clara del Valle. After graduating from her mother's <i><a href="/wiki/Alma_mater" title="Alma mater">alma mater</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Vassar_College" title="Vassar College">Vassar College</a> with a degree in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_History" title="Art History">Art History</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, she made her stage debut in 2008 in Lukas Bärfuss's <i>The Sexual Neuroses of Our Parents</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> From 2010-11, she starred as Anna Moore on the <a href="/wiki/TeenNick" title="TeenNick">TeenNick</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Gigantic_(TV_series)" title="Gigantic (TV series)">Gigantic</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> which premiered October 8, 2010.</p>
<p>Gummer appeared in the 2010 film <i>Meskada</i>, in which she played the role of Nat Collins, before playing Abby in <i>Bashert</i> (2010) with <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Costanzo" title="Paulo Costanzo">Paulo Costanzo</a>. She also had a small role as a student in <a href="/wiki/Julia_Roberts" title="Julia Roberts">Julia Roberts</a>' class in <i><a href="/wiki/Larry_Crowne" title="Larry Crowne">Larry Crowne</a></i> (2011), which was directed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the spring of 2011, she performed the role of Chloe Coverly in the revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Arcadia_(play)" title="Arcadia (play)">Arcadia</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Stoppard" title="Tom Stoppard">Tom Stoppard</a> at the Ethel Barrymore Theatre in New York.</p>
<p>Gummer appeared as Katie Rand on the hit <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Smash_(TV_series)" title="Smash (TV series)">Smash</a></i> on March 26, 2012, in the episode entitled "<a href="/wiki/The_Coup_(Smash)" title="The Coup (Smash)">The Coup</a>". The character Katie Rand is a humanitarian attempting to bring peace to her divorcing parents, including mother played by <a href="/wiki/Anjelica_Huston" title="Anjelica Huston">Anjelica Huston</a>.</p>
<p>Gummer had a recurring role on the 2013 television series <i><a href="/wiki/Zero_Hour_(2013_TV_series)" title="Zero Hour (2013 TV series)">Zero Hour</a></i> and joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Newsroom_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Newsroom (U.S. TV series)">The Newsroom</a></i> beginning in its second season, which premiered on July 14, 2013.</p>
<p>In 2014 and 2015, Gummer starred alongside Halle Berry on both seasons of the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Extant_(TV_series)" title="Extant (TV series)">Extant</a></i>.</p>
<p>On January 29, 2016, it was announced that Gummer had joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Robot_(TV_series)" title="Mr. Robot (TV series)">Mr. Robot</a></i> as a series regular in the role of FBI field agent Dominique “Dom” DiPierro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
