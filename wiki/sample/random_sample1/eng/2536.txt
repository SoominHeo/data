<p><b>Grace Sewell</b>, known <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mononym" title="Mononym">mononymously</a> as <b>Grace</b>, is an Australian singer and songwriter. She is best known for "<a href="/wiki/You_Don%27t_Own_Me#Grace_version" title="You Don't Own Me">You Don't Own Me</a>", a cover version of the 1963 <a href="/wiki/Lesley_Gore" title="Lesley Gore">Lesley Gore</a> song "<a href="/wiki/You_Don%27t_Own_Me" title="You Don't Own Me">You Don't Own Me</a>", produced by <a href="/wiki/Quincy_Jones" title="Quincy Jones">Quincy Jones</a> and featuring <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NashvilRCA_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NashvilRCA-3">[3]</a></sup> The song, a single from her debut album with Regime Music Societe<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>, was a <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a> "top 10 most viral track"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMail10top_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMail10top-4">[4]</a></sup> and a number-one hit in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewsAustralia_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NewsAustralia-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grace_(Australian_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grace is from <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a>, Australia, and attended <a href="/wiki/All_Hallows%27_School" title="All Hallows' School">All Hallows' School</a> and Our Lady of Lourdes Catholic Primary School, Sunnybank. She grew up listening to musical artists such as <a href="/wiki/Smokey_Robinson" title="Smokey Robinson">Smokey Robinson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janis_Joplin" title="Janis Joplin">Janis Joplin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Bassey" title="Shirley Bassey">Shirley Bassey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NashvilRCA_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NashvilRCA-3">[3]</a></sup> Grace comes from a family of musicians. Her grandparents toured with the Bee Gees and the <a href="/wiki/Bee_Gees" title="Bee Gees">Gibb Brothers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NashvilRCA_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-NashvilRCA-3">[3]</a></sup> Her brother <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Sewell" title="Conrad Sewell">Conrad Sewell</a> is also a singer, best known for featuring on <a href="/wiki/Kygo" title="Kygo">Kygo</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Firestone_(song)" title="Firestone (song)">Firestone</a>" and for his solo hit "<a href="/wiki/Start_Again" title="Start Again">Start Again</a>". When "Start Again" hit number one on the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA Charts</a> in June 2015, Conrad and Grace became the first Australian born siblings in the history of the charts to hit number one as separate acts.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grace_(Australian_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She performed for <i>Dropout Live UK</i>, singing a cover of "<a href="/wiki/Do_It_like_a_Dude" title="Do It like a Dude">Do It like a Dude</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jessie_J" title="Jessie J">Jessie J</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Grace signed with Regime Music Societe<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> in 2015 and began recording in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy</a> and Quincy Jones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewsAustralia_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-NewsAustralia-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Grace released "You Don't Own Me" in March 2015 as a tribute to and cover of the original singer, <a href="/wiki/Lesley_Gore" title="Lesley Gore">Lesley Gore</a>, who had recently died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheSourceUpcomeing_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheSourceUpcomeing-7">[7]</a></sup> Grace recorded the song with rapper <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Quincy_Jones" title="Quincy Jones">Quincy Jones</a> returning to co-produce the track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADirectSOW_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ADirectSOW-8">[8]</a></sup> It was her first single with RCA Records. The song became a top 10 viral track on Spotify in April 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IStatetop10_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-IStatetop10-9">[9]</a></sup> and debuted at number 14 on the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA Singles Chart</a> in May 2015, later peaking at number 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Australia_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Australia-10">[10]</a></sup> The song was used to promote the Australian TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Child_(TV_series)" title="Love Child (TV series)">Love Child</a></i>, and was used in Christmas advertisements for <a href="/wiki/House_of_Fraser" title="House of Fraser">House of Fraser</a> in the UK. In 2016, the song was used in the trailer for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Suicide_Squad_(film)" title="Suicide Squad (film)">Suicide Squad</a></i>.</p>
<p>"You Don't Own Me" is included on her debut album, scheduled for release in spring 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheSourceUpcomeing_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-TheSourceUpcomeing-7">[7]</a></sup> Grace also co-wrote a track for the album with <a href="/wiki/Fraser_T_Smith" title="Fraser T Smith">Fraser T Smith</a>, which features additional writing and production contributions from <a href="/wiki/Parker_Ighile" title="Parker Ighile">Parker Ighile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diane_Warren" title="Diane Warren">Diane Warren</a>, and Quincy Jones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HMMB_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-HMMB-11">[11]</a></sup> In September, she released the official single version of "Boyfriend Jeans" which had been included as a demo version on her debut EP, <i>Memo</i>. Grace was the featured artist on <i><a href="/wiki/Live_from_Daryl%27s_House" title="Live from Daryl's House">Live from Daryl's House</a></i> on 16 June 2016. Grace's debut studio album, <i>FMA</i>, which stands for <i>Forgive My Attitude</i>, was released on 1 July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
