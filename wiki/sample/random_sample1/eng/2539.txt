<p><i><b>The Great Wall</b></i> is a 2016 <a href="/wiki/Epic_film" title="Epic film">epic</a> <a href="/wiki/Historical_fiction" title="Historical fiction">historical fiction</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a>-<a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure</a> <a href="/wiki/Monster_movie" title="Monster movie">monster</a> film directed by <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Yimou" title="Zhang Yimou">Zhang Yimou</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Gilroy" title="Tony Gilroy">Tony Gilroy</a>, Carlo Bernard, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Miro" title="Doug Miro">Doug Miro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Brooks" title="Max Brooks">Max Brooks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Zwick" title="Edward Zwick">Edward Zwick</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Herskovitz" title="Marshall Herskovitz">Marshall Herskovitz</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jing_Tian" title="Jing Tian">Jing Tian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Pascal" title="Pedro Pascal">Pedro Pascal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willem_Dafoe" title="Willem Dafoe">Willem Dafoe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andy_Lau" title="Andy Lau">Andy Lau</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> began on March 30, 2015, in <a href="/wiki/Qingdao" title="Qingdao">Qingdao</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The film was released in China on December 15, 2016, by <a href="/wiki/Le_Vision_Pictures" title="Le Vision Pictures">Le Vision Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/China_Film_Group_Corporation" title="China Film Group Corporation">China Film Group Corporation</a> and is scheduled to be released in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on February 17, 2017, by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Pictures</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Song_dynasty" title="Song dynasty">Song dynasty</a>, a few miles north of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Wall_of_China" title="Great Wall of China">Great Wall</a>, William, Tovar, and three other survivors from a European mercenary group searching for <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder" title="Gunpowder">black powder</a> are pursued by <a href="/wiki/Khitan_people" title="Khitan people">Khitan</a> bandits, who have already killed the rest of them. Upon escaping they seek refuge in a cave but are then attacked by a shadowy monster, leaving only William and Tovar alive, the former having slashed off the monster's hand. The next day, they stumble upon the Great Wall and are taken prisoner by Chinese soldiers, who are led by General Shao, Commander Lin, and Strategist Wang.</p>
<p>The officers have been preparing for a monster invasion which happens every sixty years, and have trained five special units: Bear Troop, Tiger Troop, Eagle Troop, Deer Troop and the Crane Troop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defenders_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-defenders-6">[6]</a></sup> However, they are shocked upon seeing the monster's hand, identified as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Taotie" title="Taotie">Taotie</a> species, as they believed the invasion was still weeks away. Suddenly, a wave of Taoties attack the Great Wall, and the soldiers respond with their own special attacks.</p>
<p>The Eagle Troops specialized in archery,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defenders_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-defenders-6">[6]</a></sup> the Tiger Troops in ballista and catapults launching burning rocks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defenders_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-defenders-6">[6]</a></sup> Lin's Crane Troop consisted exclusively of female soldiers jumping off the Wall to spear the Taoties before being pulled back with elastic cords.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defenders_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-defenders-6">[6]</a></sup> The heavily armed and armoured Bear Troops engaged in melee while the Deer Troop served as the cavalry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defenders_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-defenders-6">[6]</a></sup> The Taoties are more intelligent and numerous than thought, and kill some of the defenders, but are repelled with William and Tovar's assistance, which earns the soldiers' respect. In the middle of the fight, William also saved and made acquaintance with Peng Yong, a kitchen soldier who is not skilled in fighting. Later, William and Tovar meet Sir Ballard, a European who, like them, had ventured east twenty-five years ago in search of black powder, which might be sold for a fortune back in Europe. However, like them, Ballard was also taken prisoner and has been serving as an English and Latin teacher. The three foreigners discuss plans to steal black powder from the storeroom and flee while the soldiers are occupied by battle.</p>
<p>During the night, two monsters manage to climb the Great Wall and kill a guard. Shao and Lin lead a platoon to investigate, but they are ambushed and Shao is killed, leaving Lin in charge. Around this time, an envoy from the capital, <a href="/wiki/Kaifeng" title="Kaifeng">Bianliang</a>, arrives with an ancient scroll, which suggests that the monsters are pacified by magnets. To test this theory, William suggests that Lin should capture a monster alive using harpoons laced with sedatives, and delays his escape plans to help. At the next battle, several monsters are ensnared, and William bravely slides down a chain to ensure that an ensnared monster is not freed by other monsters. Tovar delays his escape to assist William, to Ballard's disappointment. The soldiers resist the monsters by fighting vertically along the Wall while tied to a chain, and by using spinning mechanical blades. As the monsters are too numerous, Lin resorts to using black powder rockets, whose existence had been a secret. After the battle, the captured monster is taken to the capital by the envoy. A short while later, a hole is discovered in the base of the Great Wall, which the monsters have used as a bypass, a reflection of their cleverness. While Lin investigates, Tovar and Ballard try to escape and knock William unconscious for resisting. William is then arrested by the Chinese soldiers after they discovered the escapade of his friends but Peng Yong pleads for William, saying he witnessed William trying to stop Tovar and Ballard from leaving. Some distance away, Ballard betrays and abandons Tovar, but is himself captured by the Khitans, who, not knowing what the barrels of gunpowder were, accidentally ignite a barrel killing themselves and Ballard. Tovar reclaims the horses but is recaptured by Chinese riders.</p>
<p>Knowing that the monsters are nearing the capital, Lin orders the use of an unstable technology, namely hot-air balloons, to reach the capital more quickly. Most of the balloons catch fire and the soldiers fall to their deaths. William decides to fight for the Chinese and was about to board the last balloon when Peng Yong comes up to him and asks to come along. William commends Peng Yong for being brave, unlike what everyone else sees him as. William boards the last balloon with Wang and Peng Yong, following Lin to the capital, where they find most of the city already ruined. The attack on the city happened quicker than expected because the palace officials woke the subdued Taotie up unintentionally, allowing it to communicate with its herd to direct them to the city.</p>
<p>On the other hand, Wang thinks of a plan to kill the queen Taotie by tying explosives to the captured Taotie from earlier and giving the Taotie meat to deliver to the queen. While transporting the Taotie, the troops are attacked by other Taoties above them, including Peng Yong. Peng Yong manages to save himself using a shield but is wounded. William, Lin and Wang decides to leave Peng Yong there, promising to come back for him. However, other Taoties broke through the door and Peng Yong saves them by detonating an explosive, destroying himself together with the Taoties.</p>
<p>The three people climb a tower to shoot a black powder rocket to detonate the explosives, during which Wang is killed. Two rockets fail, but the third succeeds after William throws a magnetic stone at the queen right before the rocket hits. In the aftermath, Lin is promoted to regional General, and William forgoes a reward in exchange for freeing Tovar. The two are allowed to return to Europe and a contingent of cavalry are sent to protect them.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 18, 2014, Chinese director <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Yimou" title="Zhang Yimou">Zhang Yimou</a> was set to direct the epic film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On November 6, 2014, Legendary East's CEO, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Loehr" title="Peter Loehr">Peter Loehr</a>, confirmed that the film would be made with a budget of $135 million, that it would be completely in English and it would be the first English-language film directed by Zhang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BudgetVariety_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-BudgetVariety-10">[10]</a></sup> The film originally came from Legendary CEO <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Tull" title="Thomas Tull">Thomas Tull</a>, who conceived the idea with <a href="/wiki/World_War_Z" title="World War Z">World War Z</a> writer <a href="/wiki/Max_Brooks" title="Max Brooks">Max Brooks</a>. <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic" title="Industrial Light &amp; Magic">Industrial Light &amp; Magic</a> (ILM) will be responsible for the visual effects and <a href="/wiki/Weta_Workshop" title="Weta Workshop">Weta Workshop</a> will contribute practical effects (namely, weapons and props) to ensure a high quality film reminiscent of Hollywood productions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-china.org.cn_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-china.org.cn-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:35%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>The company and I have been preparing for <i>Great Wall</i> for a long time. It is an action blockbuster. The reason I took the <i>Great Wall</i> project is that there have been requests in the last 10 or 20 years. Now the production is big enough and really appealing. And, very importantly, it has Chinese elements in it.</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"><a href="/wiki/Zhang_Yimou" title="Zhang Yimou">Zhang Yimou</a>, director<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yimou-details_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yimou-details-12">[12]</a></sup></cite></div>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> of the film began on March 30, 2015, on location in <a href="/wiki/Qingdao" title="Qingdao">Qingdao</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It is the most expensive film ever shot entirely in China with a budget of $135 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NovemberRelease_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NovemberRelease-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BudgetVariety_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-BudgetVariety-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Three walls were built during production as they could not shoot on the actual Great Wall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-14">[14]</a></sup> During the filmmaking, the director said the most impressive part for him was the presence of so many translators to handle communication as he assembled an international crew for the filming. More than 100 on-set translators worked with the various cast and crew members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film's score is composed by <a href="/wiki/Ramin_Djawadi" title="Ramin Djawadi">Ramin Djawadi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The first track called "Nameless Order" was released on December 14, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Great Wall</i> was released in China on December 15, 2016. It is scheduled to be released on February 17, 2017, in the United States by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Pictures</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FebruaryRelease_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FebruaryRelease-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Great Wall</i> released its first trailer in July 2016. The trailer shows views of the Great Wall in fog, thousands of soldiers on a battlefield ready for war and a mysterious monster, as well as the roster views of the cast including <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andy_Lau" title="Andy Lau">Andy Lau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-china.org.cn_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-china.org.cn-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>A new song from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leehom_Wang" title="Leehom Wang">Leehom Wang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tan_Weiwei" title="Tan Weiwei">Tan Weiwei</a> was released on November 15, 2016, to promote <i>The Great Wall.</i> <i>Bridge of Fate</i> was composed by pop singer Wang with lyrics written by <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Fang_(lyricist)" title="Vincent Fang (lyricist)">Vincent Fang</a>, a long time collaborator of singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jay_Chou" title="Jay Chou">Jay Chou</a>. Female rocker Tan Weiwei joined Wang for a duet, but with two different vocal styles. Wang sang pop, while Tan performed a traditional <a href="/wiki/Qinqiang" title="Qinqiang">Qinqiang</a> - a folk Chinese opera style from <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a> Province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Chinese pop diva <a href="/wiki/Jane_Zhang" title="Jane Zhang">Jane Zhang</a> released another new English song <i>Battle Field</i> and its promotional music video for <i>The Great Wall</i> on November 22, 2016. The song was composed by King Logan and <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a>'s keyboardist <a href="/wiki/PJ_Morton" title="PJ Morton">PJ Morton</a> and written by Josiah "JoJo" Martin and Jane Zhang. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Universal Pictures &amp; Legendary Entertainment also debuted eight character posters of the film on November 17, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Great Wall</i> opened on December 15, 2016 in China and made $24.3 million on Friday and $67.4 million in its opening weekend. In the second weekend it grossed $26.1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frater_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frater-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film has an approval rating of 44% based on 9 reviews, with an average rating of 5.8/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 48 out of 100, based on 4 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Wall_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to some of the characters, including a main character played by <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a>, being white in a movie set in medieval China, the film has been accused of <a href="/wiki/Whitewashing_in_film" title="Whitewashing in film">whitewashing</a> and using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_savior_narrative" title="White savior narrative">white savior narrative</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Director Zhang Yimou clarified that Matt Damon is not playing a role that was originally conceived for a Chinese actor, he further criticized detractors for not being "armed with the facts" and stated that:</p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:90%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>“In many ways ‘The Great Wall’ is the opposite of what is being suggested. For the first time, a film deeply rooted in Chinese culture, with one of the largest Chinese casts ever assembled, is being made at tentpole scale for a world audience. I believe that is a trend that should be embraced by our industry.”</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"><a href="/wiki/Zhang_Yimou" title="Zhang Yimou">Zhang Yimou</a>, director<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiewire.com_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiewire.com-28">[28]</a></sup></cite></div>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<p>As the film's largest investor, the <a href="/wiki/Wanda_Group" title="Wanda Group">Wanda Group</a> (owner of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legendary_Pictures" title="Legendary Pictures">Legendary Pictures</a>) has good relationships with the Chinese government and the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a>. In December 2016, users of film review website <a href="/wiki/Douban" title="Douban">Douban</a> rated <i>The Great Wall</i> 5.4 out of 10, which is considered very low. On <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maoyan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maoyan (page does not exist)">Maoyan</a>, another movie review aggregator, the 'professional score' is only 4.9 out of 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xx_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-xx-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haodu_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-haodu-30">[30]</a></sup> On December 28, the Communist Party's official media outlet <i><a href="/wiki/People%27s_Daily" title="People's Daily">People's Daily</a></i> published an article on its website severely criticizing Douban and Maoyan for doing harm to the Chinese movie industry with their bad reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On the same day, Maoyan took down its 'professional score' for <i>The Great Wall</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xx_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-xx-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
