<p><b>Gretchen Mol</b> (born November 8, 1972) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> and former model. She is known for her roles in the films <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rounders_(1998_film)" title="Rounders (1998 film)">Rounders</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_(film)" title="Celebrity (film)">Celebrity</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/3:10_to_Yuma_(2007_film)" title="3:10 to Yuma (2007 film)">3:10 to Yuma</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thirteenth_Floor" title="The Thirteenth Floor">The Thirteenth Floor</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_Bettie_Page" title="The Notorious Bettie Page">The Notorious Bettie Page</a></i>, where she played the <a href="/wiki/Bettie_Page" title="Bettie Page">title character</a>. She also appeared as Gillian Darmody in <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Empire" title="Boardwalk Empire">Boardwalk Empire</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gretchen_Mol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mol was born in <a href="/wiki/Deep_River,_Connecticut" title="Deep River, Connecticut">Deep River, Connecticut</a>, where her mother, Janet (née Morgan), is an artist and teacher, and her father was a school teacher at <a href="/wiki/RHAM_High_School" title="RHAM High School">RHAM</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She went to high school with Broadway actor <a href="/wiki/Peter_Lockyer" title="Peter Lockyer">Peter Lockyer</a>, with whom she performed in school musicals and plays. Her brother, Jim Mol, is a director and editor in the film industry. Mol attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_American_Musical_and_Dramatic_Academy" title="The American Musical and Dramatic Academy">The American Musical and Dramatic Academy</a> and graduated from the <a href="/wiki/William_Esper_Studio" title="William Esper Studio">William Esper Studio</a>. After <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_stock" title="Summer stock">summer stock</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>, she took a job for a while as an usher at Angelika Film Center. She was living in a <a href="/wiki/Hell%27s_Kitchen,_Manhattan" title="Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan">Hell's Kitchen</a> walk-up when she was noticed by a talent agent who spotted her working as a hat check girl at Michael's Restaurant in New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broadway.com_interview_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Broadway.com_interview-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gretchen_Mol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mol's acting career began in <a href="/wiki/Summer_stock_theatre" title="Summer stock theatre">summer stock theatre</a> in Vermont where she played a variety of roles including <i><a href="/wiki/Godspell" title="Godspell">Godspell</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/110_In_The_Shade" title="110 In The Shade">110 In The Shade</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broadway.com_interview_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Broadway.com_interview-3">[3]</a></sup> She played Jenny in <a href="/wiki/Neil_LaBute" title="Neil LaBute">Neil LaBute</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shape_of_Things" title="The Shape of Things">The Shape of Things</a></i> on stage in both London and New York in 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in a role she reprised in the film version, released in 2003. The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> critic <a href="/wiki/Ben_Brantley" title="Ben Brantley">Ben Brantley</a>, in his review of the play (which he disliked),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> wrote, "[Mol] gives by far the most persuasive performance as the unworldly Jenny, and you wind up feeling for her disproportionately, only because she seems to be entirely there, in the present tense". In 2004, Mol spent a year singing and dancing as Roxie in the Broadway production of <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_(musical)" title="Chicago (musical)">Chicago</a></i>. In 2014–15, Mol played the role of Emily in the Broadway debut of the Pulitzer Prize winning <a href="/wiki/Ayad_Akhtar" title="Ayad Akhtar">Ayad Akhtar</a> play <i><a href="/wiki/Disgraced" title="Disgraced">Disgraced</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gretchen_Mol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1994, Mol was spotted by photographer David William Powell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He photographed her in New York's <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">Central Park</a> and replaced her unrepresentative portfolio with professional-looking black-and-white images which landed her on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/W_(magazine)" title="W (magazine)">W</a></i> within weeks, and foreshadowed her "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/It_Girl" title="It Girl">It Girl</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Bettie_Page" title="Bettie Page">Bettie Page</a>" looks. Shortly afterwards, she ended her brief modeling career and entered acting full-time.</p>
<p>While major roles have been sporadic, Mol has been in more than 30 feature films. She made her film debut in <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>'s 1996 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Girl_6" title="Girl 6">Girl 6</a>.</i> She said, "I was auditioning for <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i> and I was happy I got a Spike Lee movie, which was a tiny part, but all of a sudden I had Spike Lee on my resume. I didn't audition for <a href="/wiki/Supporting_actor" title="Supporting actor">day player</a> anymore".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broadway.com_interview_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Broadway.com_interview-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <i>Girl 6</i>, New York filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Abel_Ferrara" title="Abel Ferrara">Abel Ferrara</a> took notice and cast her in two movies, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Funeral_(1996_film)" title="The Funeral (1996 film)">The Funeral</a></i> (1996) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Rose_Hotel_(1998_film)" title="New Rose Hotel (1998 film)">New Rose Hotel</a></i> (1998). She had a small role in <i><a href="/wiki/Donnie_Brasco_(film)" title="Donnie Brasco (film)">Donnie Brasco</a></i> (1998), but by now, she was being typecast as "the girlfriend", which she attempted to change by taking a role opposite <a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Music_from_Another_Room_(film)" title="Music from Another Room (film)">Music from Another Room</a></i> (1998), a romantic comedy. The film went virtually unnoticed by critics and audiences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_bio_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_bio-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1998, she appeared in several notable films including <i><a href="/wiki/Rounders_(film)" title="Rounders (film)">Rounders</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_(film)" title="Celebrity (film)">Celebrity</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio" title="Leonardo DiCaprio">Leonardo DiCaprio</a>. In 1998, she also came to prominence when she was featured on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (magazine)">Vanity Fair</a></i>, dubbed the "It Girl of the Nineties" by the magazine.</p>
<p>For her second film with Woody Allen, 1999's <i><a href="/wiki/Sweet_and_Lowdown" title="Sweet and Lowdown">Sweet and Lowdown</a>,</i> she played a minor role which the <i>Greenwich Village Gazette</i> called "notable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She played the female lead role in the 1999 film <i>The Thirteenth Floor</i>. She played the victim of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Con_artist" title="Con artist">con artist</a> in the 2003 film, <i>Heavy Put-Away</i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Terry_Southern" title="Terry Southern">Terry Southern</a> story. In 2006, she shared the lead in a romantic comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/Puccini_for_Beginners" title="Puccini for Beginners">Puccini for Beginners</a></i>, in which her character has a <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a> affair.</p>
<p>Mol worked with <a href="/wiki/Mary_Harron" title="Mary Harron">Mary Harron</a> for two years as the director struggled to finance <i>The Notorious Bettie Page</i>: "I kind of felt like I lived with it for a while; certainly not as long as Mary Harron did but I got a good chance to really feel like I knew something about Bettie so by the time the role was mine and I was on set, I was pretty confident. I felt like I really worked for it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next year, 2007, was one of her busiest, with four films in production or in release, including a remake of <i><a href="/wiki/3:10_to_Yuma_(2007_film)" title="3:10 to Yuma (2007 film)">3:10 to Yuma</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/An_American_Affair" title="An American Affair">An American Affair</a></i>, in which her character, Catherine Caswell, has an affair with <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>. When released in February 2009, the film was harshly criticized by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> critic Stephen Holden, though he said that Mol's part was "quite well acted".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2008, she began filming <i><a href="/wiki/Tenure_(film)" title="Tenure (film)">Tenure</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, working opposite <a href="/wiki/Luke_Wilson" title="Luke Wilson">Luke Wilson</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andrew_Daly" title="Andrew Daly">Andrew Daly</a>. Though it had received some good reviews after being screened at several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_festivals" title="Film festivals">film festivals</a>, it was released <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> in February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gretchen_Mol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mol's first television work was in a <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> commercial. Mol had a small role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maggie_Tilton" title="Maggie Tilton">Maggie Tilton</a> in the 1996 miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Man%27s_Walk" title="Dead Man's Walk">Dead Man's Walk</a></i>, based on the Larry McMurtry novel. She also was in a few episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_City" title="Spin City">Spin City</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_bio_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_bio-8">[8]</a></sup> She was the star of the short-lived David E. Kelley series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Girls_club_(TV_series)" title="Girls club (TV series)">Girls Club</a></i> (2002), a drama about three women lawyers. The series was not well received and it was cancelled after two episodes.</p>
<p>She appeared in two TV remakes of classic films: <i>Picnic</i> (2000), in the role of Madge Owens, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_Ambersons_(film)#Adaptations" title="The Magnificent Ambersons (film)">The Magnificent Ambersons</a></i> as Lucy Morgan (2002). She made a <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Hall_of_Fame" title="Hallmark Hall of Fame">Hallmark Hall of Fame</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_movie" title="Television movie">television movie</a> in January 2007, starring in <i>The Valley of Light</i>, a story set in post-World War II based on a novel by Terry Kay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It was her second Hallmark production. She had a minor role in <i>Calm at Sunset</i> in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>She played Norah in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Memory_Keeper%27s_Daughter" title="The Memory Keeper's Daughter">The Memory Keeper's Daughter</a></i> which aired in the U.S. on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifetime_Television" title="Lifetime Television">The Lifetime Channel</a> in the U.S. in April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>She played Annie in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Life_on_Mars_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Life on Mars (U.S. TV series)">Life on Mars</a></i>, the U.S. remake of the British show <a href="/wiki/Life_on_Mars_(UK_TV_series)" title="Life on Mars (UK TV series)">of the same name</a>. It started airing in the U.S. on October 9, 2008 and ran 17 episodes, concluding on April 1, 2009.</p>
<p>She had a recurring role on <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Empire" title="Boardwalk Empire">Boardwalk Empire</a></i> as Gillian Darmody, a showgirl at the Beaux Arts and mother to gangster <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Darmody" title="Jimmy Darmody">Jimmy Darmody</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Pitt" title="Michael Pitt">Michael Pitt</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gretchen_Mol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Interviewed by the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> in Baltimore in December 2006, Mol commented about how she maintained her confidence as an actress: "It is an ongoing struggle. Confidence is something that sometimes you have and sometimes you don't. And the older you get, hopefully, the more you have some tools to at least fake it".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>She married film director <a href="/wiki/Tod_Williams_(filmmaker)" title="Tod Williams (filmmaker)">Kip Williams</a> on June 1, 2004. Their first child, Ptolemy John Williams, was born September 10, 2007. On February 17, 2011, Mol gave birth to their second child, daughter Winter Morgan Williams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> While raising Ptolemy, Mol has only taken jobs close to her home in New York City. "I told my agent I didn't want to work in L.A., even if it was the greatest job in the world. I didn't want to compromise."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mol serves as the national spokesperson in the United States for the PMD Foundation, which funds research and awareness of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pelizaeus-Merzbacher_disease" title="Pelizaeus-Merzbacher disease">Pelizaeus-Merzbacher disease</a>, a neurological disorder afflicting children worldwide. Mol became involved with PMD after one of her cousins died from <a href="/wiki/Amyotrophic_lateral_sclerosis" title="Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis">amyotrophic lateral sclerosis</a> (familiarly known as "Lou Gehrig's Disease").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
