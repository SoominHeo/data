<p><b>Gregg Alan Rolie</b> (born June 17, 1947, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>, United States) is an American singer and keyboardist. Rolie served as lead singer of the bands <a href="/wiki/Santana_(band)" title="Santana (band)">Santana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Journey_(band)" title="Journey (band)">Journey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abraxas_Pool" title="Abraxas Pool">Abraxas Pool</a> – all of which he co-founded. He also helmed rock group <a href="/wiki/The_Storm_(American_band)" title="The Storm (American band)">The Storm</a>, and currently performs with his Gregg Rolie Band. Rolie is a two-time inductee of the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a>, having been inducted both as a member of Santana in 1998 and as a member of Journey in 2017.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gregg_Rolie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to Santana, Rolie played with a group called William Penn and his Pals while attending <a href="/wiki/Ellwood_P._Cubberley_High_School" title="Ellwood P. Cubberley High School">Cubberley High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>, circa 1965.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A year after graduating from high school in 1965, Rolie joined <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a> and others to form the Santana Blues Band, which was later shortened simply to <a href="/wiki/Santana_(band)" title="Santana (band)">Santana</a>. As a co-founding member of Santana, Rolie was part of the band's first wave of success, including an appearance at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woodstock_Festival" title="Woodstock Festival">Woodstock Music and Art Festival</a> in 1969 and central roles in several hit albums. He is perhaps best known for being their original lead vocalist, with his voice appearing on well-known Santana songs such as "<a href="/wiki/Black_Magic_Woman" title="Black Magic Woman">Black Magic Woman</a>" (US #4), "<a href="/wiki/Oye_Como_Va" title="Oye Como Va">Oye Como Va</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No_One_To_Depend_On" title="No One To Depend On">No One To Depend On</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Evil_Ways" title="Evil Ways">Evil Ways</a>". He also became well known for his skill on the <a href="/wiki/Hammond_organ" title="Hammond organ">Hammond B3 organ</a>, with solos on many of the aforementioned hits. However, persistent differences with Carlos Santana regarding the musical direction of the band led Rolie to leave at the end of 1971.</p>
<p>In 1973 Rolie joined a new band with ex-Santana guitarist <a href="/wiki/Neal_Schon" title="Neal Schon">Neal Schon</a>. This became <a href="/wiki/Journey_(band)" title="Journey (band)">Journey</a>. Starring in a lineup that featured Schon, <a href="/wiki/Aynsley_Dunbar" title="Aynsley Dunbar">Aynsley Dunbar</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Tickner" title="George Tickner">George Tickner</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ross_Valory" title="Ross Valory">Ross Valory</a>, he was keyboardist for the band's first six albums. On <i><a href="/wiki/Journey_(Journey_album)" title="Journey (Journey album)">Journey</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Look_into_the_Future" title="Look into the Future">Look into the Future</a></i>, he was lead vocalist, and on <i><a href="/wiki/Next_(Journey_album)" title="Next (Journey album)">Next</a></i> he shared those duties with guitarist Neal Schon. After <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_Perry_(musician)" title="Steve Perry (musician)">Steve Perry</a> joined the band in 1977, Rolie sang co-lead vocals on several songs on the albums <i><a href="/wiki/Infinity_(Journey_album)" title="Infinity (Journey album)">Infinity</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Evolution_(Journey_album)" title="Evolution (Journey album)">Evolution</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Departure_(Journey_album)" title="Departure (Journey album)">Departure</a></i>.</p>
<p>After leaving Journey in 1980, Rolie released several solo albums, including the eponymous <i>Gregg Rolie</i> in 1985. This album featured the song "I Wanna Go Back," which later became a hit for <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Money" title="Eddie Money">Eddie Money</a>, and included contributions from <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wolf" title="Peter Wolf">Peter Wolf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neal_Schon" title="Neal Schon">Neal Schon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Craig_Chaquico" title="Craig Chaquico">Craig Chaquico</a>. A second solo effort, <i>Gringo</i>, was released in 1987.</p>
<p>Rolie formed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Storm_(band)" title="The Storm (band)">The Storm</a> in 1991 with <a href="/wiki/Steve_Smith_(musician)" title="Steve Smith (musician)">Steve Smith</a> (replaced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Wikso&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ron Wikso (page does not exist)">Ron Wikso</a> after the recording of the first album) and <a href="/wiki/Ross_Valory" title="Ross Valory">Ross Valory</a> of Journey. Similar to his work with Journey and Steve Perry, Rolie played keyboards and was a co-lead vocalist on several tracks of the band's first, eponymous, album, which hit #3 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> albums chart and spawned the Top Ten single "I've Got A Lot To Learn About Love." Despite this success, their second album, recorded in 1993, was shelved, due to the industry's shifting focus to favor rap and alternative music audiences. It finally saw limited release in 1996, and in 1998, Rolie and other former members of Santana, including Neal Schon, briefly reunited as <a href="/wiki/Abraxas_Pool" title="Abraxas Pool">Abraxas Pool</a>, releasing one album.</p>
<p>When Schon left to lead a re-formed Journey later that year, Rolie and Ron Wikso began work in 1999, on a Gregg Rolie solo CD, titled "Roots", which eventually led to the forming of the Gregg Rolie Band. Besides Rolie and Wikso, "Roots" featured appearances by <a href="/wiki/Neal_Schon" title="Neal Schon">Neal Schon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alphonso_Johnson" title="Alphonso Johnson">Alphonso Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Amato" title="Dave Amato">Dave Amato</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Adrian_Areas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Adrian Areas (page does not exist)">Adrian Areas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Carabello" title="Michael Carabello">Michael Carabello</a>. The Gregg Rolie Band saw <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurt_Griffey" title="Kurt Griffey">Kurt Griffey</a> taking over guitar duties and the addition of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wally_Minko&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wally Minko (page does not exist)">Wally Minko</a> as a second keyboardist. They recorded a live CD at Sturgis called "Rain Dance", which was released in 2009.</p>
<p>In 2010, Rolie released "Five Days" and subsequently formed a duo with <a href="/wiki/Alan_Haynes" title="Alan Haynes">Alan Haynes</a>, which eventually led to the formation of the Gregg Rolie Quartet, with the addition of long time collaborator/drummer, Ron Wikso and bassist Evan "Sticky" Lopez.</p>
<p>In 2012–2014 he toured as a member of <a href="/wiki/Ringo_Starr" title="Ringo Starr">Ringo Starr</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Ringo_Starr_%26_His_All-Starr_Band" title="Ringo Starr &amp; His All-Starr Band">All Starr Band</a> performing Santana hits <a href="/wiki/Black_Magic_Woman" title="Black Magic Woman">Black Magic Woman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evil_Ways" title="Evil Ways">Evil Ways</a> and Everybody's Everything. The band also included <a href="/wiki/Toto_(band)" title="Toto (band)">Toto</a> guitarist <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lukather" title="Steve Lukather">Steve Lukather</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todd_Rundgren" title="Todd Rundgren">Todd Rundgren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Page_(musician)" title="Richard Page (musician)">Richard Page</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rivera" title="Mark Rivera">Mark Rivera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gregg_Bissonette" title="Gregg Bissonette">Gregg Bissonette</a>. On February 2, 2013 Carlos Santana confirmed that he would reunite his classic lineup, most of whom played Woodstock with him in 1969. Santana said of Rolie, "I'm pretty sure Gregg's going to do it." Speaking in 2012 of such a reunion, Rolie told Radio.com "it's just a matter of putting it together and going and doing it. I would do it. I think it's a great idea. People would love it. It could be great!"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2016, as part of Santana's original line-up they released their fourth album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Santana_IV" title="Santana IV">Santana IV</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gregg_Rolie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rolie is a proponent of music education for children. In 2005, he signed on as an official supporter of Little Kids Rock, a nonprofit organization that provides free musical instruments and instruction to children in underserved public schools throughout the U.S.A. He sits on the organization's Honorary board of directors.</p>
