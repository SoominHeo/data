<p><b>Greenwich Island</b> (variant historical names <i>Sartorius Island</i>, <i>Berezina Island</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> 24 km (15 mi) long and from 0.80 to 9.66 km (0.5 to 6 mi) (average 5.23 km or 3.25 mi) wide, lying between <a href="/wiki/Robert_Island" title="Robert Island">Robert Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Livingston_Island" title="Livingston Island">Livingston Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands" title="South Shetland Islands">South Shetland Islands</a>. Surface area 142.7 square kilometres (55.1 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The name Greenwich Island dates back to at least 1821 and is now established in international usage.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> base <a href="/wiki/Captain_Arturo_Prat_Base" title="Captain Arturo Prat Base">Arturo Prat</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuadorian</a> base <a href="/wiki/Maldonado_Base" title="Maldonado Base">Pedro Vicente Maldonado</a> are situated on the northeast and north coast of the island respectively.</p>
