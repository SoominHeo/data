<p>The <b>Bank of Greece</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Τράπεζα της Ελλάδος</span> <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Greek transliteration"><i>Trapeza tis Elladas</i></span>, abbreviated ΤτΕ) is the <a href="/wiki/Central_bank" title="Central bank">central bank</a> of <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. Its headquarters is located in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> on <a href="/wiki/Panepistimiou_Street" title="Panepistimiou Street">Panepistimiou Street</a>, but it also has several branches across the country. It was founded in 1927 and its operations started officially in 1928. The building that houses it headquarters until this day was completed ten years later, in 1938.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Bank of Greece is listed on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athens_Exchange" title="Athens Exchange">Athens Exchange</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bank_of_Greece&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bank of Greece, a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_System_of_Central_Banks" title="European System of Central Banks">European System of Central Banks</a> (ESCB), is the national <a href="/wiki/Central_bank" title="Central bank">central bank</a> of Greece and was established by Law 3424/7 December 1927. The shares of the Bank of Greece are registered and have been listed on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athens_Exchange" title="Athens Exchange">Athens Exchange</a> since 12 June 1930.</p>
<p>It is a partially state owned S.A. share company with special privileges, with special restrictions and duties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It cannot operate as a commercial bank and the percentage of shares that can be under Greek state ownership cannot exceed 35%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> (initially this limit was 10%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>). It has a staff of more than 3,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Employee" title="Employee">employees</a>.</p>
<p>The primary objective of the Bank of Greece is to ensure <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Price_stability" title="Price stability">price stability</a> in Greece. It also supervises the private banks and acts as a treasurer and fiscal agent for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_government" title="Greek government">Greek government</a>. After law 3867/2010 was passed, the Bank of Greece is also responsible for supervising private insurance companies, merging in the Committee for the Supervision of Insurance Companies established by law 3229/2004.</p>
<p>Its <a href="/wiki/Euro_banknotes" title="Euro banknotes">Euro banknotes</a> printer identification code is <b>Y</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bank of Greece also sells <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_sovereign" title="Gold sovereign">gold sovereigns</a>.</p>

<p><small><sup>a</sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Axis_occupation_of_Greece" title="Axis occupation of Greece">Axis occupation of Greece</a> (1941–44), Governor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kyriakos_Varvaresos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kyriakos Varvaresos (page does not exist)">Kyriakos Varvaresos</a> followed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_government_in_exile" title="Greek government in exile">Greek government in exile</a> to London. The <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_State_(1941%E2%80%9344)" title="Hellenic State (1941–44)">collaborationist governments</a> in Greece fired Varvaresos in 1941 and appointed first <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Miltiadis_Negrepontis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Miltiadis Negrepontis (page does not exist)">Miltiadis Negrepontis</a> as Governing Counsellor (April 24, 1941 – July 3, 1941) and then <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitrios_Santis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dimitrios Santis (page does not exist)">Dimitrios Santis</a> (July 3, 1941 – January 20, 1943) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Theodoros_Tourkovasilis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Theodoros Tourkovasilis (page does not exist)">Theodoros Tourkovasilis</a> (April 19, 1943 – April 13, 1944) as Governors. Nevertheless, after the liberation, all dismissals and appointments by occupation-era governments concerning members of the administration of the Bank of Greece were declared null and void.</small></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deputy_Governors">Deputy Governors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bank_of_Greece&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Deputy Governors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>deputy governor</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">υποδιοικητής, <i>ypodioikētés</i></span>) is the Bank's second-in-line officer. Traditionally, the Deputy Governors' main remit is administration, whereas Governors supervise monetary policy at large.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
