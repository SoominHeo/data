<p>The <b>United States Senate elections, 1972</b> was an election for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> coinciding with the landslide <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1972" title="United States presidential election, 1972">re-election</a> of Republican President <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a>. Despite Nixon's victory, <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrats</a> increased their majority by two seats. After the election, Democrats held 56 seats and <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republicans</a> held 42 seats, with 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_of_New_York" title="Conservative Party of New York">Conservative</a> and 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" title="Independent (politician)">independent</a> Senator. This was the first election that citizens at least 18 years of age (instead of 21 and older) could vote due to the recent passage of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/26th_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="26th Amendment to the United States Constitution">26th Amendment</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gains_and_losses">Gains and losses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Senate_elections,_1972&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gains and losses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Democratic pickups included open seats in Kentucky and South Dakota, and defeats of Senators <a href="/wiki/Gordon_L._Allott" title="Gordon L. Allott">Gordon L. Allott</a> of Colorado, <a href="/wiki/J._Caleb_Boggs" title="J. Caleb Boggs">J. Caleb Boggs</a> of Delaware, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Miller_(politician)" title="Jack Miller (politician)">Jack Miller</a> of Iowa, and <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Chase_Smith" title="Margaret Chase Smith">Margaret Chase Smith</a> of Maine.</p>
<p>Republican pickups included open seats in New Mexico, North Carolina, and Oklahoma, and the defeat of incumbent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_B._Spong,_Jr." title="William B. Spong, Jr.">William B. Spong, Jr.</a> of Virginia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appointment">Appointment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Senate_elections,_1972&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Appointment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1974, <a href="/wiki/William_B._Saxbe" title="William B. Saxbe">William B. Saxbe</a> of Ohio resigned to become <a href="/wiki/United_States_Attorney_General" title="United States Attorney General">Attorney General</a>, and Democrat <a href="/wiki/Howard_Metzenbaum" title="Howard Metzenbaum">Howard Metzenbaum</a> was appointed to replace him. This is not included in the party balances.</p>
