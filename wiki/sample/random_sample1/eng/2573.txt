<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gvardeysk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tapiau.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="468" data-file-width="730" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Tapiau.jpg/220px-Tapiau.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Tapiau.jpg/330px-Tapiau.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Tapiau.jpg/440px-Tapiau.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tapiau.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
14th Century Teutonic Order Castle</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Peter_of_Dusburg" title="Peter of Dusburg">Peter of Dusburg</a> wrote of a settlement known as <b>Tapiow</b>, first documented in 1254, and the neighboring fort <i>Surgurbi</i> built by 1265.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oesterley_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Oesterley-5">[5]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Old_Prussian_language" title="Old Prussian language">Old Prussian</a> names were derived from the words <i>tape</i>, <i>teplu</i>, <i>toplu</i>, <i>tapi</i>, meaning "warm", and <i>sur garbis</i>, meaning "around the mountain". During the 13th century <a href="/wiki/Prussian_Crusade" title="Prussian Crusade">Prussian Crusade</a>, the area was conquered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teutonic_Knights" title="Teutonic Knights">Teutonic Knights</a>. To protect <a href="/wiki/Sambia_Peninsula" title="Sambia Peninsula">Samland</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Nadruvians" title="Nadruvians">Nadruvians</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scalvians" title="Scalvians">Scalvians</a>, the crusaders built a wooden fort between the <a href="/wiki/Deyma_River" title="Deyma River">Deime</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pregolya_River" title="Pregolya River">Pregel Rivers</a> in 1283–1290. This was replaced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tapiau_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tapiau Castle (page does not exist)">Tapiau Castle</a>, a stone <a href="/wiki/Ordensburg" title="Ordensburg">Ordensburg</a>, in 1351.</p>
<p>The settlement gradually became known by the <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> crusaders as <b>Tapiau</b>. <a href="/wiki/Vytautas" title="Vytautas">Vytautas</a>, the later <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Lithuania" title="List of rulers of Lithuania">Grand Duke of Lithuania</a>, was baptized in Tapiau in 1385. After the transfer of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Masters_of_the_Teutonic_Knights" title="Grand Masters of the Teutonic Knights">Grand Master</a>'s seat from <a href="/wiki/Malbork_Castle" title="Malbork Castle">Marienburg</a> to <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg_Castle" title="Königsberg Castle">Königsberg</a>, Tapiau became the site of the Order's archives and library from 1469 to 1722.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tapiau_print.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="820" height="172" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Tapiau_print.jpg/220px-Tapiau_print.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Tapiau_print.jpg/330px-Tapiau_print.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Tapiau_print.jpg/440px-Tapiau_print.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tapiau_print.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
16th century depiction of Tapiau</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Tapiau became a part of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Prussia" title="Duchy of Prussia">Duchy of Prussia</a> in 1525. The Tapiau Castle was often used as a second residence of the Prussian dukes; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albert,_Duke_in_Prussia" title="Albert, Duke in Prussia">Albert of Prussia</a> died there in 1568. It became a part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a> in 1701, receiving <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">town privileges</a> from King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_I_of_Prussia" title="Frederick William I of Prussia">Frederick William I of Prussia</a> in 1722.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was a part of the Prussian Province of <a href="/wiki/East_Prussia" title="East Prussia">East Prussia</a> and was administered in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Landkreis_Wehlau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Landkreis Wehlau (page does not exist)">Landkreis Wehlau</a> (1818–1945). Tapiau became a part of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Unification_of_Germany" title="Unification of Germany">unification of Germany</a> in 1871.</p>
<p>Unlike most other towns in northern East Prussia, Tapiau was largely undamaged during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Following the war's end in 1945, it was annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and renamed Gvardeysk ("guard town") in 1946.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-gr-6">[6]</a></sup> Of the town's German population of more than 9,000, those who had not fled or been killed in the final months of the war were <a href="/wiki/Flight_and_expulsion_of_Germans_(1944%E2%80%9350)" title="Flight and expulsion of Germans (1944–50)">expelled</a> and gradually replaced by Soviet settlers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gvardeysk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Gvardeysk serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gvardeysky_District" title="Gvardeysky District">Gvardeysky District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KaliningradO_admlaw_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-KaliningradO_admlaw-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is, together with one <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural locality</a> (the settlement of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Prigorodnoye,_Gvardeysky_District,_Kaliningrad_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Prigorodnoye, Gvardeysky District, Kaliningrad Oblast (page does not exist)">Prigorodnoye</a>), incorporated within Gvardeysky District as the <b><a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">town of district significance</a> of Gvardeysk</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KaliningradO_admlist_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-KaliningradO_admlist-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of municipal divisions</a>, since June 11, 2014, the territories of the town of district significance of Gvardeysk and of four <a href="/wiki/Selsoviet" title="Selsoviet">rural okrugs</a> of Gvardeysky District are incorporated as Gvardeysky Urban Okrug.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KaliningradOGvardeyskyD_mun_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-KaliningradOGvardeyskyD_mun-2">[2]</a></sup> Before that, the town of district significance was incorporated within Gvardeysky Municipal District as <b>Gvardeyskoye Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KaliningradOGvardeyskyD_mun_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-KaliningradOGvardeyskyD_mun-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gvardeysk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> of Gvardeysk depicts a bare hand holding a sword amongst clouds, beneath a golden sun. When the town was known as Tapiau before 1946, the golden sun also included the <a href="/wiki/Tetragrammaton" title="Tetragrammaton">Tetragrammaton</a> (<i>Jehova-Sonne</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gvardeysk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sights of Gvardeysk include a church from 1502 and the ruined <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tapiau_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tapiau Castle (page does not exist)">Tapiau Castle</a>, reconstructed into an orphanage in 1879. It has been used as a prison since 1945.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gvardeysk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: People">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tapiau's most famous resident was the German Impressionist painter <a href="/wiki/Lovis_Corinth" title="Lovis Corinth">Lovis Corinth</a> (1858–1925), who donated the painting <i>Golgatha</i> for the altar of the town's church in 1910; the painting disappeared near the end of World War II. The house where Corinth was born still stands in Gvardeysk. Other notable people associated with the town include <a href="/wiki/Albert,_Duke_of_Prussia" title="Albert, Duke of Prussia">Albert, Duke of Prussia</a> (1490–1568), who died in Tapiau.</p>
