<p><b>Gwen Torrence</b> (born June 12, 1965) is a retired <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" title="Sprint (running)">sprinter</a> and Olympic <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medalist</a>. She was born in <a href="/wiki/Decatur,_Georgia" title="Decatur, Georgia">Decatur, Georgia</a>. She attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbia_High_School_(Decatur,_GA)" title="Columbia High School (Decatur, GA)">Columbia High School</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a>.</p>
<p>Torrence won medals at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Olympics" title="Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a>, <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="IAAF World Championships in Athletics">Outdoor</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships_in_Athletics" title="IAAF World Indoor Championships in Athletics">Indoor World Championships</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games" title="Pan American Games">Pan American Games</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goodwill_Games" title="Goodwill Games">Goodwill Games</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_University_Games" title="World University Games">World University Games</a>.</p>
<p>In 1988, Torrence achieved a tie with <a href="/wiki/Evelyn_Ashford" title="Evelyn Ashford">Evelyn Ashford</a> in the 55 m race at the U.S. national indoor championships. She also had many battles both on the track and in the press with <a href="/wiki/Florence_Griffith_Joyner" title="Florence Griffith Joyner">Florence Griffith Joyner</a>.</p>
<p>In the 200 m at the <a href="/wiki/1995_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1995 World Championships in Athletics">1995 World Championships</a>, she was disqualified for stepping out of her lane after finishing first. This left her idol, <a href="/wiki/Merlene_Ottey" title="Merlene Ottey">Merlene Ottey</a>, to be promoted to first place.</p>
