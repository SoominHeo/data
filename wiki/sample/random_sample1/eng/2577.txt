<p><b>Xavier Beauvois</b> (<small>French: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ɡzavje bovwa]</a></span>; born 20 March 1967) is a French actor, <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xavier_Beauvois&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His film <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Forget_You%27re_Going_to_Die" title="Don't Forget You're Going to Die">Don't Forget You're Going to Die</a></i> was entered into the <a href="/wiki/1995_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="1995 Cannes Film Festival">1995 Cannes Film Festival</a> where it won the <a href="/wiki/Jury_Prize_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Jury Prize (Cannes Film Festival)">Jury Prize</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival-cannes.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-festival-cannes.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>His film <i><a href="/wiki/Of_Gods_and_Men_(film)" title="Of Gods and Men (film)">Of Gods and Men</a></i> received the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Grand Prix (Cannes Film Festival)">Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Prize_of_the_Ecumenical_Jury" title="Prize of the Ecumenical Jury">Prize of the Ecumenical Jury</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2010 Cannes Film Festival">2010 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The film was also selected as France's submission for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/83rd_Academy_Awards" title="83rd Academy Awards">83rd Academy Awards</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83rdAcademy_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-83rdAcademy-4">[4]</a></sup> but it did not make the final shortlist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oscar_Shortlist_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oscar_Shortlist-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>His 2014 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Ran%C3%A7on_de_la_gloire" title="La Rançon de la gloire">La Rançon de la gloire</a></i> was selected to compete for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Lion" title="Golden Lion">Golden Lion</a> at the <a href="/wiki/71st_Venice_International_Film_Festival" title="71st Venice International Film Festival">71st Venice International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Venice_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Venice-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadline_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deadline-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xavier_Beauvois&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is married to film editor Marie-Julie Maille. They have two sons, Arthur, who was born in August 1992, and Antoine, born May 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
