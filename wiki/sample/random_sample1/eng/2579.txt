<p><b>Alpha Apodis</b> (Alpha Aps, α Apodis, α Aps) is the brightest <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> in the southern <a href="/wiki/Circumpolar_constellation" title="Circumpolar constellation">circumpolar constellation</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apus_(constellation)" title="Apus (constellation)">Apus</a>, with an <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">apparent magnitude</a> of approximately 3.825.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kaler_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-kaler-8">[8]</a></sup> It had the Greek alpha designation at part of the constellation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johann_Bode" title="Johann Bode">Johann Bode</a> called Apis Indica in his 1603 <i><a href="/wiki/Uranometria" title="Uranometria">Uranometria</a></i> star atlas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ridpath_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ridpath-9">[9]</a></sup> With a declination of –79°, this is a <a href="/wiki/Circumpolar_star" title="Circumpolar star">circumpolar star</a> for much of the southern hemisphere. It can be identified on the night sky by drawing an imaginary line through <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Centauri" title="Alpha Centauri">Alpha Centauri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Circini" title="Alpha Circini">Alpha Circini</a> then extending it toward the south celestial pole.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moore2000_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-moore2000-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Giant_star" title="Giant star">giant star</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> of K2.5III, indicating that this star has consumed the hydrogen at its core and has <a href="/wiki/Stellar_evolution" title="Stellar evolution">evolved</a> away from the <a href="/wiki/Main_sequence" title="Main sequence">main sequence</a>. It has expanded to an estimated radius of about 48 times the <a href="/wiki/Solar_radius" title="Solar radius">radius of the Sun</a> and is emitting 980 times the <a href="/wiki/Solar_luminosity" title="Solar luminosity">Sun's luminosity</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Photosphere" title="Photosphere">photosphere</a> has an <a href="/wiki/Effective_temperature" title="Effective temperature">effective temperature</a> of 4,256 K, giving the star the characteristic orange hue of a K-type star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csiro_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-csiro-11">[11]</a></sup> Based upon <a href="/wiki/Parallax" title="Parallax">parallax</a> measurements, this star is roughly 447 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light-years" title="Light-years">light-years</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa474_2_653_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa474_2_653-1">[1]</a></sup> It is not known to have a companion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras389_2_869_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-mnras389_2_869-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Apodis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> caused by adaptation of the European southern hemisphere constellations into the Chinese system, <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">異雀</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Yì Què</span>), meaning <i><a href="/wiki/Chinese_constellations#The_Southern_Asterisms" title="Chinese constellations">Exotic Bird</a></i>, refers to an asterism consisting of α Apodis, <a href="/wiki/Zeta_Apodis" title="Zeta Apodis">ζ Apodis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iota_Apodis" title="Iota Apodis">ι Apodis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beta_Apodis" title="Beta Apodis">β Apodis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Apodis" title="Gamma Apodis">γ Apodis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delta_Octantis" title="Delta Octantis">δ Octantis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delta_Apodis" title="Delta Apodis">δ<sup>1</sup> Apodis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eta_Apodis" title="Eta Apodis">η Apodis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Epsilon_Apodis" title="Epsilon Apodis">ε Apodis</a>. Consequently, α Apodis itself is known as <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">異雀八</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Yì Què bā</span>, English: the Eighth Star of Exotic Bird.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
