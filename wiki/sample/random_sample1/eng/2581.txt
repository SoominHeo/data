<p><i><b>Sailor Moon S: The Movie</b></i>, known in Japan as <i><b>Pretty Soldier Sailor Moon S The Movie</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">劇場版 美少女戦士セーラームーンＳ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gekijōban Bishōjo Senshi Sērā Mūn Sūpā</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and <i><b>Sailor Moon S the Movie: Hearts in Ice</b></i> in the Pioneer English adaptation, is a 1994 Japanese animated film directed by Hiroki Shibata and written by Sukehiro Tomita. It is the second film in the <i><a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon" title="Sailor Moon">Sailor Moon</a></i> series. The film is adapted from a side story of the original <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series created by <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">Naoko Takeuchi</a>, <i>The Lover of Princess Kaguya</i>. The storyline is loosely based on "<a href="/wiki/The_Snow_Queen" title="The Snow Queen">The Snow Queen</a>" fairytale by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hans_Christen_Andersen" title="Hans Christen Andersen">Hans Christen Andersen</a>.</p>
<p>The film was released on December 4, 1994 in Japan as part of the Winter '94 Toei Anime Fair.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_S:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An extraterrestrial entity named Princess Snow Kaguya <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">プリンセス・スノー・カグヤ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Purinsesu Sunō Kaguya</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> arrives on Earth in an attempt to freeze it, but a fragment of her comet has been lost and she is unable to proceed without it. She has her henchwomen, the Snow Dancers, search for the missing fragment. In Tokyo, a young astronomer named Kakeru Ōzora <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">宇宙 翔</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōzora Kakeru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> finds the fragment and keeps it in his observatory to study it further.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the Sailor Guardians are enjoying a day in the Juban Shopping District. <a href="/wiki/List_of_Sailor_Moon_characters#Luna" title="List of Sailor Moon characters">Luna</a> falls ill and decides to go back to Usagi's house. Along the way, she collapses while crossing the road, and is almost hit by a car, but is rescued and nursed to health by Kakeru. Luna then develops strong romantic feelings for him, even kissing him on the cheek in his sleep, leaving Artemis devastated. Luna herself ends up with <a href="/wiki/Unrequited_love" title="Unrequited love">unrequited love</a> because it is revealed that Kakeru himself has a girlfriend, an astronaut named Himeko Nayotake <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">名夜竹 姫子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nayotake Himeko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, and more importantly, because Luna is a cat. The two are devastated because Himeko is oblivious to Kakeru's belief of <a href="/wiki/The_Tale_of_the_Bamboo_Cutter" title="The Tale of the Bamboo Cutter">Princess Kaguya</a>'s existence. Later, after finding herself unable to reconcile her differences with Kakeru, Himeko leaves on a space mission.</p>
<p>The fragment of the comet attaches itself to his life force, and begins slowly stealing his life-force energy, causing him to become very ill. Kaguya later steals the shard, but because it is linked to his life-force, he is brought even closer to death when Kaguya throws the shard into the ocean and creates an enormous ice crystal that will continue to draw away Kakeru's life force energy completely. She and her Snow Dancers then begin freezing the entire Earth. The Sailor Guardians attempt to stop her, but every time they kill the Snow Dancers, Kaguya keeps reviving them using the crystal. Just before Kaguya could kill the soldiers, Usagi tries to stop her and uses the Holy Grail to transform into Super Sailor Moon. She uses her <i>Rainbow Moon Heartache</i> attack but is easily overpowered by Kaguya's strength and power. Determined to protect the Earth, Usagi prepares to activate the Legendary Silver Crystal's immense energy and power. The eight Sailor Guardians, along with Chibiusa, combine their powers and abilities all at once to activate the Legendary Silver Crystal, and it hits Kaguya head-on. The Silver Crystal's power also destroys Kaguya's Snow Dancers, the ice crystal in the ocean, as well as her comet.</p>
<p>With peace once again returned to the Earth, Usagi wishes for Luna to become Princess Kaguya. Concerned about Himeko's safety, Kakeru wanders in the snowstorm and is saved by Luna at the exact point Kakeru saved her, transformed into a beautiful human woman. She takes him near the moon, where Himeko, on her space mission, witnesses the phenomenon and realizes that Princess Kaguya is real. Luna tells him that he needs to start focusing on his relationship with Himeko, and the two kiss. After returning to the Earth, Kakeru takes up Luna's advice and meets Himeko at the airport, where the two reconcile. Artemis meets up with Luna and the cats reconcile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_S:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sailor Moon S: The Movie</i> is based on the 135-page side story "Princess Kaguya's Lover" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">かぐや姫の恋人</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>"Kaguya hime no Koibito"</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, written and illustrated by series creator <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">Naoko Takeuchi</a> and later published by Kodansha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pp_138.E2.80.94141_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pp_138.E2.80.94141-2">[2]</a></sup> Dissatisfied that she had left the production of the previous <i>Sailor Moon</i> film to others, Takeuchi envisioned "Princess Kaguya's Lover" as the plot of <i>Sailor Moon S: The Movie</i>, and proceeded to write the story "all in one go."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pp_138.E2.80.94141_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pp_138.E2.80.94141-2">[2]</a></sup> She modeled the antagonist after an <a href="/wiki/Art_Deco" title="Art Deco">Art Deco</a> antique named "Salome", while the Snow Dancers are modeled after a German china piece, which Takeuchi thought resembled "a character dancing in a snowstorm."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pp_178.E2.80.949_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pp_178.E2.80.949-3">[3]</a></sup> On July 8, 1994, she traveled to the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">Kennedy Space Center</a> in Florida as part of her research; there, she watched the launch of space shuttle <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Columbia" title="Space Shuttle Columbia">Columbia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pp_138.E2.80.94141_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Pp_138.E2.80.94141-2">[2]</a></sup> She enjoyed working on the film, and liked the overall result, particularly Luna's transformation sequence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pp_138.E2.80.94141_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Pp_138.E2.80.94141-2">[2]</a></sup> The film was <a href="/wiki/Open_matte" title="Open matte">soft matted</a> for its theatrical release, as it was animated in 4:3 <a href="/wiki/Aspect_ratio" title="Aspect ratio">aspect ratio</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_release">English release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_S:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: English release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was first released in North America on VHS by Pioneer Entertainment on August 31, 1999 in Japanese with English subtitles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Pioneer later released the film to uncut bilingual DVD on May 23, 2000 alongside another VHS release containing an edited version of the English dub.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vhs_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-vhs-5">[5]</a></sup> Pioneer re-released their DVD on January 6, 2004 under their "Geneon Signature Series" line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The DVDs later fell out of print when Pioneer/Geneon lost the license to the film. The edited version was also shown on TV in Canada on <a href="/wiki/YTV_(TV_channel)" title="YTV (TV channel)">YTV</a> and in the US on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toonami" title="Toonami">Toonami</a> block on November 9, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The English dub was produced in association with Optimum Productions in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, and featured most of the original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DiC_Entertainment" title="DiC Entertainment">DiC</a> English cast reprising their roles. The edited version of the dub was censored for content and replaced the music with cues from the DiC version of the first two seasons of the anime. The uncut version of the dub was only seen on the bilingual DVD, featured no censorship, and all of the original Japanese music was left intact, with the exception of the DiC theme song being used.</p>
<p>In 2014, the film was re-licensed for an updated English-language release in North America by <a href="/wiki/Viz_Media" title="Viz Media">Viz Media</a>, who has plans to produce a new English dub of the film in association with <a href="/wiki/Studiopolis" title="Studiopolis">Studiopolis</a> in Los Angeles, California and re-release it on DVD and Blu-ray.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It has also been licensed in Australia and New Zealand by <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_S:_The_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Animerica" title="Animerica">Animerica</a></i> noted that the film incorporates aspects of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_folklore" title="Japanese folklore">Japanese folklore</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tale_of_the_Bamboo_Cutter" title="The Tale of the Bamboo Cutter">The Tale of the Bamboo Cutter</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">竹取物語</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Taketori Monogatari</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuki_Onna" title="Yuki Onna">Yuki Onna</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">雪女</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, snow woman)</span> in the antagonist's character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
