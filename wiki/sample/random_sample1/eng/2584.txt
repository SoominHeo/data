<p><i><b>Pokémon the Movie: Diancie and the Cocoon of Destruction</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ポケモン・ザ・ムービーXY 「破壊の繭とディアンシー」</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Pokemon Za Mūbī Ekkusu Wai "Hakai no Mayu to Dianshī"</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "Pokémon the Movie XY: Diancie and the Cocoon of Destruction")</span> is a 2014 Japanese <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> film directed by <a href="/wiki/Kunihiko_Yuyama" title="Kunihiko Yuyama">Kunihiko Yuyama</a>. It is the 17th <a href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_films" title="List of Pokémon films"><i>Pokémon</i> anime movie</a> and the first movie in the XY Series. The movie was released in Japanese theaters on July 19, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The movie features the Jewel <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon" title="Pokémon">Pokémon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diancie" title="Diancie">Diancie</a>, alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xerneas" title="Xerneas">Xerneas</a>, the Life Pokémon, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yveltal" title="Yveltal">Yveltal</a>, the Destruction Pokémon. It premiered in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> in theaters on November 8, 2014. The movie aired on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on November 8, 2014. It was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> on December 17, 2014. It was also released on DVD format in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on February 17, 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pok%C3%A9mon_the_Movie:_Diancie_and_the_Cocoon_of_Destruction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a country called "Diamond Domain" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ダイヤモンド鉱国</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Daiyamondo Kōkoku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kokoku_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kokoku-3">[Note 1]</a></sup> lies the powerful Heart Diamond <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">聖なるダイヤ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Seinaru Daiya</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Holy Diamond")</span>. The Heart Diamond served as the kingdom's source of energy. It also maintained the Ore Country for centuries. Many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbink" title="Carbink">Carbink</a> live in the Diamond Domain. This includes their princess known as the Jewel Pokémon named Diancie. Diancie created the Heart Diamond. Diancie no longer has the power to control the Heart Diamond. Diancie's country is falling into chaos as a result of losing control of the Heart Diamond.</p>
<p>One day, Diancie meets with Ash and his friends. Diancie asks them to go on a journey to find the Life Pokémon named Xerneas in order to restore the Heart Diamond. Standing in their way is the Pokémon of Destruction named Yveltal who once stole all of the life in the Kalos Region. During their journey, they are attacked by Team Rocket and the jewel thieves named Marilyn Flame and Ninja Riot. Diancie and Ash and his friends are aided by Millis Steel and her father named Argus. Millis and Argus Steel are thieves that want to use Diancie's power. They also encounter the Cocoon Of Destruction who is known as Yveltal.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pok%C3%A9mon_the_Movie:_Diancie_and_the_Cocoon_of_Destruction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The movie was first announced after the theatrical release of <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_the_Movie:_Genesect_and_the_Legend_Awakened" title="Pokémon the Movie: Genesect and the Legend Awakened">Pokémon the Movie: Genesect and the Legend Awakened</a></i>, with a short trailer shown. Later in December 2013, the January 2014 issue of <a href="/wiki/CoroCoro_Comic" title="CoroCoro Comic">CoroCoro Comic</a> confirmed the title of the movie, alongside the official website's announcement with the official trailer featured on the official movie website. The first poster of the movie shows all the confirmed Mega Evolutions of some Pokémon in <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_X_and_Y" title="Pokémon X and Y"><i>Pokémon X</i> and <i>Y</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup> The February issue of CoroCoro states that the movie will revolve around the "Magnificent Battle" between Xerneas and Yveltal as well as the hidden meaning to the title, related to the Destruction Pokémon. It also notes that many Legendary Pokémon and several Mega Evolutions will appear in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Cocoon of Destruction was shown alongside the Pikachu Short <i>Pikachu, What's This Key?</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ピカチュウ、これなんのカギ？</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Pikachū, Kore Nan no Kagi?</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on which will feature the new Pokémon <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Klefki" title="Klefki">Klefki</a>. The magazine also reveals that it will also feature <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jirachi" title="Jirachi">Jirachi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victini" title="Victini">Victini</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manaphy" title="Manaphy">Manaphy</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darkrai" title="Darkrai">Darkrai</a> as special guests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film made its English language debut on November 8, 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> in the United States, and in theaters in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. In addition, a prequel to the movie, titled <i>Diancie: Princess of the Diamond Domain</i> made its debut on the American Pokémon Trading Card Game website on November 5, 2014 with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbink" title="Carbink">Carbink</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pangoro" title="Pangoro">Pangoro</a>, and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ariados" title="Ariados">Ariados</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Setting">Setting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pok%C3%A9mon_the_Movie:_Diancie_and_the_Cocoon_of_Destruction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Setting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film's official setting was based on <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, with locations include the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottawa,_Canada" title="Ottawa, Canada">Ottawa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rideau_Canal" title="Rideau Canal">Rideau Canal</a>, the town of Merrickville, Skydeck Tower, the Thousand Islands of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence_River" title="Saint Lawrence River">Saint Lawrence River</a>, the city of <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quebec,_Canada" title="Quebec, Canada">Quebec</a> and the town of <a href="/wiki/Niagara-on-the-Lake" title="Niagara-on-the-Lake">Niagara-on-the-Lake</a>. The castle from the opening theme is the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_Frontenac" title="Château Frontenac">Château Frontenac</a> and the fight occurs on Terrace Dufferin. Director Yuyama stated that it is the first time since <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_the_Movie:_Black%E2%80%94Victini_and_Reshiram_and_White%E2%80%94Victini_and_Zekrom" title="Pokémon the Movie: Black—Victini and Reshiram and White—Victini and Zekrom">Pokémon the Movie: Black—Victini and Reshiram and White—Victini and Zekrom</a></i> that he and his crew traveled to a foreign country for inspiration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guest_characters">Guest characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pok%C3%A9mon_the_Movie:_Diancie_and_the_Cocoon_of_Destruction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Guest characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Diancie <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ディアンシー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Dianshī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>: Known as the Jewel Pokémon, Diancie is the princess of the Diamond Domain, home to Carbink that keep it peaceful as well the Heart Diamond that keeps it safe from outsiders. However, Diancie is growing weak, as is the Heart Diamond. She seeks out Ash and his friends to help her find the Life Pokémon Xerneas to restore her life energy, but on the way they are attacked by the Destruction Pokémon Yveltal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> Diancie is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Marika_Matsumoto" title="Marika Matsumoto">Marika Matsumoto</a> in the original Japanese version<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> and by <a href="/wiki/Caitlin_Glass" title="Caitlin Glass">Caitlin Glass</a> in the English dub.</li>
<li>Argus Steel <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">アルガス・スティール</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Argus Steel</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>: The father of Millis Steel. A man who loves chocolate and owns a blimp. He wants to use Diancie's power to create diamonds. He is voiced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Reiji_Nakagawa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Reiji Nakagawa (page does not exist)">Reiji Nakagawa</a> in Japanese and by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pete_Zarustica" title="Pete Zarustica">Pete Zarustica</a> in English.</li>
<li>Millis Steel <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ミリス・スティール</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Millis Steel</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>: A travelling trainer whose Pokémon is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chesnaught" title="Chesnaught">Chesnaught</a>. She and her father help rescue Diancie only to reveal they want to use her power to their advantage and they only rescued her so that Ash and his friends could help her to make real diamonds and then they could kidnap her. She is voiced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dko_Nakagawa" title="Shōko Nakagawa">Shōko Nakagawa</a> in Japanese and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brittney_Lee_Hamilton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brittney Lee Hamilton (page does not exist)">Brittney Lee Hamilton</a> in English.</li>
<li>Marilyn Flame <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">マリリン・フレーム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Marilyn Flame</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and Ninja Riot <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ニンジャ・ライオット</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ninja Riot</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>: A pair of jewel thieves who attack Diancie. Director Kunihiko Yuyama stated that they would be more lighthearted than previous movie villains, using interactions and exchanges to provide humor. Yuyama also stated that Marilyn Flame was designed with "more sex appeal than any earlier character, to give the adults a little bonus". Marilyn Flame's partner Pokémon is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delphox" title="Delphox">Delphox</a> while Ninja Riot's is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greninja" title="Greninja">Greninja</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> Marilyn Flame and Ninja Riot are voiced by <a href="/wiki/Rika_Adachi" title="Rika Adachi">Rika Adachi</a> and <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dichi_Yamadera" title="Kōichi Yamadera">Kōichi Yamadera</a>, respectively. Their respective English voice actors are <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lianne_Marie_Dobbs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lianne Marie Dobbs (page does not exist)">Lianne Marie Dobbs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Thompson_(voice_actor)" title="Marc Thompson (voice actor)">Marc Thompson</a>.</li>
</ul>
