<p><b>Myalgia</b>, or <a href="/wiki/Muscle" title="Muscle">muscle</a> <a href="/wiki/Pain" title="Pain">pain</a>, is a symptom of many diseases and disorders. The most common causes are the overuse or over-stretching of a muscle or group of muscles. Myalgia without a traumatic history is often due to <a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">viral infections</a>. Longer-term myalgias may be indicative of a <a href="/wiki/Metabolic_myopathy" title="Metabolic myopathy">metabolic myopathy</a>, some nutritional deficiencies or <a href="/wiki/Chronic_fatigue_syndrome" title="Chronic fatigue syndrome">chronic fatigue syndrome</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">Causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myalgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most common causes of myalgia are overuse, <a href="/wiki/Injury" title="Injury">injury</a> or <a href="/wiki/Strain_(injury)" title="Strain (injury)">strain</a>. However, myalgia can also be caused by diseases, disorders, medications, or as a response to a <a href="/wiki/Vaccination" title="Vaccination">vaccination</a>. It is also a sign of acute rejection after heart transplant surgery.</p>
<p>The most common causes are:</p>
<div class="div-col columns column-count column-count-2" style="-moz-column-count: 2; -webkit-column-count: 2; column-count: 2;">
<ul>
<li>Injury or trauma, including sprains, hematoma</li>
<li>Overuse: using a muscle too much, too often, including protecting a separate injury</li>
<li>Chronic tension</li>
</ul>
<p>Muscle pain occurs with:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rhabdomyolysis" title="Rhabdomyolysis">Rhabdomyolysis</a>, associated with:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">Viral</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Compression_injury&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Compression injury (page does not exist)">Compression injury</a></li>
<li>Drug-related, esp <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fibrates" title="Fibrates">fibrates</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statins" title="Statins">statins</a>, occ <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACE_inhibitors" title="ACE inhibitors">ACE inhibitors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a>, some retro-viral drugs</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hypokalemia" title="Hypokalemia">Severe potassium deficiency</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fibromyalgia" title="Fibromyalgia">Fibromyalgia</a></li>
<li>Auto-immune disorders, including:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mixed_connective_tissue_disease" title="Mixed connective tissue disease">Mixed connective tissue disease</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Systemic_lupus_erythematosus" title="Systemic lupus erythematosus">Systemic lupus erythematosus</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Polymyalgia_rheumatica" title="Polymyalgia rheumatica">Polymyalgia rheumatica</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Polymyositis" title="Polymyositis">Polymyositis</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dermatomyositis" title="Dermatomyositis">Dermatomyositis</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiple_Sclerosis" title="Multiple Sclerosis">Multiple Sclerosis</a> (this is neurologic pain localised to myotome)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ehlers-Danlos_syndrome" title="Ehlers-Danlos syndrome">Ehlers-Danlos syndrome</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Infections, including:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Influenza" title="Influenza">Influenza</a> (the flu)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lyme_disease" title="Lyme disease">Lyme disease</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Babesiosis" title="Babesiosis">Babesiosis</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">Malaria</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Toxoplasmosis" title="Toxoplasmosis">Toxoplasmosis</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dengue_Fever" title="Dengue Fever">Dengue Fever</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hemorrhagic_fever" title="Hemorrhagic fever">Hemorrhagic fever</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Muscle_abscess&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Muscle abscess (page does not exist)">Muscle abscess</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polio" title="Polio">Polio</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_spotted_fever" title="Rocky Mountain spotted fever">Rocky Mountain spotted fever</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Trichinosis" title="Trichinosis">Trichinosis</a> (roundworm)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebola" title="Ebola">Ebola</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Reaction to Statin drugs</li>
<li>Other
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Postorgasmic_illness_syndrome" title="Postorgasmic illness syndrome">Postorgasmic illness syndrome</a> (POIS)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Balon2005_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Balon2005-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wylie2015_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wylie2015-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GARD_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GARD-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Overuse">Overuse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myalgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overuse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Overuse of a muscle is using it too much, too soon and/or too often.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MedlinePlus_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MedlinePlus-4">[4]</a></sup> Examples are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Repetitive_strain_injury" title="Repetitive strain injury">Repetitive strain injury</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Injury">Injury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myalgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Injury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The most common causes of myalgia by <a href="/wiki/Injury" title="Injury">injury</a> are: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sprains" title="Sprains">sprains</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strain_(injury)" title="Strain (injury)">strains</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MedlinePlus_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-MedlinePlus-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Autoimmune">Autoimmune</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myalgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Autoimmune">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
