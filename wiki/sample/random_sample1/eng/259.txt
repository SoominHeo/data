<p><b>ARJ</b> (<b>A</b>rchived by <b>R</b>obert <b>J</b>ung) is a software tool designed by Robert K. Jung for creating high-efficiency <a href="/wiki/Data_compression" title="Data compression">compressed file</a> archives. ARJ is currently on version 2.86 for DOS and 3.20 for Windows and supports 16-bit, 32-bit and 64-bit Intel architectures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>ARJ was one of two mainstream<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (October 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> archivers for <a href="/wiki/DOS" title="DOS">DOS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> during the early and mid-1990s, with <a href="/wiki/PKZIP" title="PKZIP">PKZIP</a> being its competition. Parts of ARJ were covered by <span><a class="external text" href="//www.google.com/patents/US5140321" rel="nofollow">U.S. Patent 5,140,321</a></span>. Generally ARJ was less popular than PKZIP, but it did enjoy a niche market during the <a href="/wiki/Bulletin_board_system" title="Bulletin board system">BBS</a> era and in the <a href="/wiki/Warez_scene" title="Warez scene">warez scene</a>. This was largely due to ARJ's creation and handling of multi-volume archives (archives which are split into smaller files which are then suitable for dial-up transfers and <a href="/wiki/Floppy_disk" title="Floppy disk">floppy</a> distribution) being more robust than PKZIPs.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="File_format_support_in_other_software">File format support in other software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ARJ&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: File format support in other software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ARJ compressed files with the <a href="/wiki/Filename_extension" title="Filename extension">Filename extension</a> .arj can be unpacked with various tools other than the ARJ software. There exists a free software re-implement of the tool. The software <a href="/wiki/7-Zip" title="7-Zip">7-Zip</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zipeg" title="Zipeg">Zipeg</a> can also unpack .arj files.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> For Mac there exists a standalone utility UnArjMac.</p>
