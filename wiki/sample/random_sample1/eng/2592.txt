<p><b>Glen Drover</b> (born May 25, 1969) is a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> musician from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Missisauga" title="Missisauga">Missisauga</a>, Ontario, Canada, where he currently resides. Drover is best known as the former lead guitarist in <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a> and <a href="/wiki/King_Diamond_(band)" title="King Diamond (band)">King Diamond</a>, as well as his own band <a href="/wiki/Eidolon_(band)" title="Eidolon (band)">Eidolon</a>, along with his brother <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Drover" title="Shawn Drover">Shawn Drover</a> who also performed with Megadeth.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Drover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Glen Drover began playing guitar as a child, and was joined at age 10 by his brother <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Drover" title="Shawn Drover">Shawn</a> on drums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_with_Glen_Drover_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Interview_with_Glen_Drover-1">[1]</a></sup> Influenced by <a href="/wiki/Tony_MacAlpine" title="Tony MacAlpine">Tony MacAlpine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Di_Meola" title="Al Di Meola">Al Di Meola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Randy_Rhoads" title="Randy Rhoads">Randy Rhoads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greg_Howe" title="Greg Howe">Greg Howe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Romeo" title="Michael Romeo">Michael Romeo</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Lynch_(musician)" title="George Lynch (musician)">George Lynch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_DeMartini" title="Warren DeMartini">Warren DeMartini</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Gilmour" title="David Gilmour">David Gilmour</a>, the Drover brothers formed the <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash</a>/<a href="/wiki/Power_metal" title="Power metal">power metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Eidolon_(band)" title="Eidolon (band)">Eidolon</a> in 1996, releasing seven albums to date. Glen is currently teaching guitar lessons at Musicians Choice.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="King_Diamond_.281998.E2.80.932000.29">King Diamond (1998–2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Drover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: King Diamond (1998–2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1998, Drover joined <a href="/wiki/King_Diamond" title="King Diamond">King Diamond</a>, and appeared on the <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_God_(album)" title="House of God (album)">House of God</a></i> album.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Megadeth_.282004.E2.80.932008.29">Megadeth (2004–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Drover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Megadeth (2004–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2004, Drover joined <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a>, bringing along his brother Shawn. Following an extensive world tour, Drover contributed to Megadeth's 2007 release, <i><a href="/wiki/United_Abominations" title="United Abominations">United Abominations</a></i> as the lead guitarist and with co-writing credits for one song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LionsShareLucemFero_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-LionsShareLucemFero-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2008 Glen Drover left Megadeth to focus on family life; the constant touring was starting to have a negative effect on him. His last show with Megadeth was on November 18, 2007 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brisbane,_Australia" title="Brisbane, Australia">Brisbane, Australia</a>. Commenting on leaving the band, Drover said "I am aware of the rumors that I left Megadeth to focus on family life and my family life has always been my priority. In the end, I was unhappy with the situation, which magnified me wanting to spend more time with my family and realizing that it's time for me to move on to the next chapter in my musical career, I have a lot of great memories and met a ton of great people along the way, both fans and people in the industry."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drover_leaves_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Drover_leaves-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metalusion_.282008.E2.80.93present.29">Metalusion (2008–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Drover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Metalusion (2008–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Glen collaborated with <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phan_Fort%C3%A9" title="Stéphan Forté">Stéphan Forté</a> of the band <a href="/wiki/Adagio_(band)" title="Adagio (band)">Adagio</a> on a 10 track instrumental CD.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The album, titled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metalusion" title="Metalusion">Metalusion</a>, was recorded sporadically over a 2-plus-year period. It was released on April 5, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The group decided to call the band/project Metalusion, which is the name of the first album. The next studio album, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Between_Sea_And_Sky&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Between Sea And Sky (page does not exist)">Between Sea And Sky</a> was scheduled to be released in the summer of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Testament_.282008.2C_2010.29">Testament (2008, 2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Drover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Testament (2008, 2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 22, 2008, <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a> announced that they had recruited guitarist Glen Drover to fill in on their upcoming Mexican tour dates with <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">Judas Priest</a>, due to <a href="/wiki/Alex_Skolnick" title="Alex Skolnick">Alex Skolnick</a>'s prior commitment to the <a href="/wiki/Trans-Siberian_Orchestra" title="Trans-Siberian Orchestra">Trans-Siberian Orchestra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Also in March 2010, during the time of him trying to finally finish his album, Glen Drover played with Testament on the Megadeth, Testament, Exodus tour in the US and Canada, due to Alex Skolnick being away again during this time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Queensr.C3.BFche_.282012.2C_2013.29">Queensrÿche (2012, 2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Drover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Queensrÿche (2012, 2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After lead singer <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Tate" title="Geoff Tate">Geoff Tate</a> was fired from the band <a href="/wiki/Queensr%C3%BFche" title="Queensrÿche">Queensrÿche</a> in June 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-testimony_tate_E_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-testimony_tate_E-9">[9]</a></sup> Tate announced his own lineup on September 1, 2012, which included Drover,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soundspike_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-soundspike-10">[10]</a></sup> but on November 23, 2012, Drover left the band, stating: "I was very excited about doing this gig initially, but learned in the past week or so that it's in my best interest not to do this right now. (...) I sincerely wish the Geoff and the band all the luck on what they do in the future."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Drover would later explain that: “[t]he musical direction of where Geoff wanted to go wasn't what I wanted to go forward with. I'm more into the first five Queensrÿche albums, the original template of the band. For me, when I think of the band, I think of those records."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-madness_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-madness-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 5, 2013, Drover surprised many,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-madness_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-madness-12">[12]</a></sup> when he <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweeted</a>: "Going up onstage with the REAL Queensryche and play "Take Hold of the Flame" this thursday at the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Concert_Theatre" title="Phoenix Concert Theatre">Phoenix</a> in Toronto. going to be fun stuff!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> By this, he meant the version of Queensrÿche that had expelled Tate and replaced him with <a href="/wiki/Todd_La_Torre" title="Todd La Torre">Todd La Torre</a>. This performance was held on March 7, 2013, in which "Take Hold of the Flame" from Queensrÿche's 1984 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Warning_(Queensr%C3%BFche_album)" title="The Warning (Queensrÿche album)">The Warning</a></i> was played as an encore. Drover later stated that calling this version of Queensrÿche "the real Queensrÿche", "was poor wording on my part. (...) It wasn't a slap at anyone in any way, shape or form. I have no problems with anybody on either side. It was the wrong choice of wording."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-madness_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-madness-12">[12]</a></sup> In April 2014, a settlement granted the sole rights to the Queensrÿche name to the band fronted by La Torre.</p>
<p>In 2014, Drover collaborated with La Torre on a single called "<a href="/wiki/Discordia_(song)" title="Discordia (song)">Discordia</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-discordia_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-discordia-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
