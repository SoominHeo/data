<p><b>Glen Winter</b> is a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Television_director" title="Television director">television director</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cinematographer" title="Cinematographer">cinematographer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">producer</a>.</p>
<p>He is well known for his work on <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">The WB</a>/<a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smallville_(TV_series)" title="Smallville (TV series)">Smallville</a></i>; as well as for his significant contributions to the <i><a href="/wiki/Arrowverse" title="Arrowverse">Arrowverse</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Winter began his career as a filmloader on <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rydell" title="Mark Rydell">Mark Rydell</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Intersection_(1994_film)" title="Intersection (1994 film)">Intersection</a></i>. He worked on several films as a camera operator such as; <i>The Crow: Stairway To Heaven</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/MVP:_Most_Vertical_Primate" title="MVP: Most Vertical Primate">MVP: Most Vertical Primate</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Air_Bud:_Seventh_Inning_Fetch" title="Air Bud: Seventh Inning Fetch">Air Bud: Seventh Inning Fetch</a></i>. As a cinematographer his work included: <i><a href="/wiki/Stone_of_Destiny_(film)" title="Stone of Destiny (film)">Stone of Destiny</a></i>, <i>Rags</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Heat_(2002_film)" title="Dead Heat (2002 film)">Dead Heat</a></i>.</p>
<p>He serves as the director of the pilot episode of <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Supergirl_(TV_series)" title="Supergirl (TV series)">Supergirl</a></i>, which premiered in late 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Supergirl1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Supergirl1-1">[1]</a></sup> The series was developed by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Normal_(TV_series)" title="The New Normal (TV series)">The New Normal</a></i> co-creator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ali_Adler" title="Ali Adler">Ali Adler</a>, <i>Arrow</i> and <i>The Flash</i> executive producers <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Kreisberg" title="Andrew Kreisberg">Andrew Kreisberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greg_Berlanti" title="Greg Berlanti">Greg Berlanti</a>. Now in its second season on The CW, Winter has directed 7 episodes, in total, of the series.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smallville"><i>Smallville</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Smallville">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winter had a mainstay position on the <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a> prequel series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smallville_(TV_series)" title="Smallville (TV series)">Smallville</a></i>, on which he first served as second unit director of photography, and then series cinematographer. He later went on to direct 12 episodes, including "Cyborg", introducing the <a href="/wiki/Cyborg_(comics)" title="Cyborg (comics)">titular hero</a>, "Fallout", "Crimson", "Blue", "Traveler", "Committed", "Legion", first collaboration with DC Comics' <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Johns" title="Geoff Johns">Geoff Johns</a> and introduces the <a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Super-Heroes" title="Legion of Super-Heroes">titular group of heroes from the future</a>; "Infamous", "Idol", featuring the <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Twins" title="Wonder Twins">Wonder Twins</a>, the first part of the TV movie "<a href="/wiki/Absolute_Justice" title="Absolute Justice">Absolute Justice</a>", "Hostage", and "Shield", showcasing the supervillain <a href="/wiki/Deadshot" title="Deadshot">Deadshot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmo1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmo1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrow.2C_The_Flash_and_Legends_of_Tomorrow"><i>Arrow</i>, <i>The Flash</i> and <i>Legends of Tomorrow</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arrow, The Flash and Legends of Tomorrow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:300px; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div style="margin:0px 2em;">
<p>In <i>Legends</i>, we had a couple of complicated action sequences that we didn't have a lot of time to do. This is a part of the process that I actually really enjoy. You seemingly have something that is unshootable in the time and money, where you have to go, "Okay, how do we make this shootable and still make it dynamic?" I just break it down into variable chunks. I want to put the audience in the middle of the action. I try to think of a way to shoot each action beat within the scene so it's not necessarily always the same. I'll shoot one piece off a crane. I'll do one piece hand-to-hand. I'll do one piece off a camera car. I just see the movie in my head.</p>
</div>
<div style="position: absolute; left: 0px; top: 0px; text-align: left; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; font-size: large; color: gray;">“</div>
<div style="position: absolute; right: 0px; bottom: 0px; text-align: left; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; font-size: large; color: gray;">”</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: right;">—Winter on his direction style of <i><a href="/wiki/Legends_of_Tomorrow" title="Legends of Tomorrow">Legends of Tomorrow</a></i>, and beyond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WinterPilot_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WinterPilot-3">[3]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>In spring 2012, Winter signed on to another <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a> produced series, <i><a href="/wiki/Arrow_(TV_series)" title="Arrow (TV series)">Arrow</a></i>. After starting as series cinematographer, he segued to episode directing. His first effort was written by frequent collaborator, and DC Comics CCO, <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Johns" title="Geoff Johns">Geoff Johns</a>. Entitled "Dead to Rights", the installment was met with fan and critical acclaim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DtR1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DtR1-4">[4]</a></sup> He went on to direct second season episodes "Broken Dolls", "Blind Spot", and "The Promise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmo1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmo1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He helmed <i>Arrow</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s third season premiere, "The Calm".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calm1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-calm1-5">[5]</a></sup> He then directed the first episode of 2015, "Left Behind".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arrow10_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Arrow10-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Starting with the fourth season premiere, "Green Arrow", Winter was named a producer on <i>Arrow</i>.</p>
<p>He served as the director of photography on the pilot of <i>Arrow</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s sister-series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flash_(2014_TV_series)" title="The Flash (2014 TV series)">The Flash</a></i>. He went on to direct the fourth episode, "Going Rogue", in which the supervillain <a href="/wiki/Captain_Cold" title="Captain Cold">Captain Cold</a> is introduced. He next helmed the series' 8th installment, which features a crossover event with <i>Arrow</i>, "Flash vs. Arrow"; and the 13th "The Nuclear Man", featuring the exploration of the <a href="/wiki/Firestorm_(comics)" title="Firestorm (comics)">titular hero</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flash4_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flash4-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flash8director_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flash8director-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flash8_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flash8-9">[9]</a></sup> During the latter portion of the season, he was named as producer.</p>
<p>In September 2015, it was confirmed that Winter would helm the pilot for the <i>Arrow</i>/<i>Flash</i> spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/Legends_of_Tomorrow" title="Legends of Tomorrow">Legends of Tomorrow</a></i>. The series focuses on a band of tag-team heroes and villains made up from the <a href="/wiki/Arrowverse" title="Arrowverse">Arrowverse</a>, traveling through space and time to stop the maniacal <a href="/wiki/Vandal_Savage" title="Vandal Savage">Vandal Savage</a>. It was later confirmed that Winter would also serve as a producer on the series. Winter said of directing the 2-part pilot episode, he was most proud of a technically challenging battle involving <a href="/wiki/Atom_(Ray_Palmer)" title="Atom (Ray Palmer)">The Atom</a>. "He flies out of [Martin] Stein's pocket. [The creators] write, "Atom basically comes in and shoots up the place." I'm like, "Oh, my god. How am I going to do that? What is that? What does that even look like?" I decided I didn't want to do it in a bunch of cuts. I wanted it to feel like one flowing, big piece. I shot it on the camera car. I shot it on high-res at 6k. We did it in one take."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WinterPilot_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-WinterPilot-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ep101_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ep101-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
