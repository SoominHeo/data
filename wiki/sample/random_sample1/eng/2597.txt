<p><b>Glory</b> (stylized as <b>GLORY</b>) and formerly <b>Glory World Series</b> is an international <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxing</a> <a href="/wiki/Promoter_(entertainment)" title="Promoter (entertainment)">promotion</a> company founded in 2012 by Glory Sports International Pte Ltd. partners <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Andurand" title="Pierre Andurand">Pierre Andurand</a>, Scott Rudmann and Total Sports Asia. The company has its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glory_(kickboxing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a>, historically the world's premier kickboxing organization, began suffering from extreme financial difficulties in 2011, Total Sports Asia, TSA CEO Marcus Luer, London-based French investor Pierre Andurand, and Scott Rudmann of Nectar Capital attempted to buy the brand. When K-1 was instead sold to Gunil "Mike" Kim and EMCOM Entertainment Inc., Pierre Andurand, TSA, and the other investors decided to start a brand new label and kickboxing organisation called GLORY, and bought <a href="/wiki/United_Glory" title="United Glory">United Glory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Glory" title="Golden Glory">Golden Glory</a> and <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(kickboxing)" title="It's Showtime (kickboxing)">It's Showtime</a> in order to secure a top event production team, and to secure all the top fighters in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Andurand" title="Pierre Andurand">Pierre Andurand</a>, Marcus Luer, and Scott Rudmann derived the name 'GLORY' for the new brand from <a href="/wiki/Golden_Glory" title="Golden Glory">Golden Glory</a>.</p>
<p>The name <i>GLORY</i> has caused some confusion to arise as to Golden Glory's role with the new organization. GLORY has clarified that Golden Glory, like the It's Showtime organization, was bought and absorbed by the investment team headed by <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Andurand" title="Pierre Andurand">Pierre Andurand</a>, and not the other way around.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A number of people formerly involved with It's Showtime, the Golden Glory team and the United Glory event became GLORY consultants, though Simon Rutz, the founder and former owner of It's Showtime, has departed the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>GSI signed most of the world's top kickboxers such as <a href="/wiki/Peter_Aerts" title="Peter Aerts">Peter Aerts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Remy_Bonjasky" title="Remy Bonjasky">Remy Bonjasky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Semmy_Schilt" title="Semmy Schilt">Semmy Schilt</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gokhan_Saki" title="Gokhan Saki">Gokhan Saki</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Ghi%C5%A3%C4%83" title="Daniel Ghiţă">Daniel Ghiţă</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Kraus" title="Albert Kraus">Albert Kraus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Petrosyan" title="Giorgio Petrosyan">Giorgio Petrosyan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoshihiro_Sato" title="Yoshihiro Sato">Yoshihiro Sato</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pat_Barry_(kickboxer)" title="Pat Barry (kickboxer)">Pat Barry</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glory_(kickboxing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GLORY Sports International has its offices in UK, Netherlands, Japan, Malaysia, Singapore, and US. The company includes unprecedented mix of accomplished entrepreneurs and senior level executives from the diverse worlds of finance, sports marketing, television, technology and martial arts fighting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2012, GLORY entered into a partnership with the <a href="/wiki/ONE_Championship" title="ONE Championship">ONE Championship</a> <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> promotions to share fighters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2013, GLORY announced former WWE Executive VP Andrew Whitaker to the position of the global CEO of Glory Sports International.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2013, after having an inaugural first event in USA at NY (<a href="/wiki/Glory_9:_New_York" title="Glory 9: New York">Glory 9: New York</a>) on June 22, 2013, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SPIKE_TV" title="SPIKE TV">SPIKE TV</a> signed a multi-year agreement to broadcast GLORY kickboxing events.</p>
<p>In August 2014, GLORY announced Jon Franklin has been appointed as new CEO of Glory Sports International, after broadcast deal extended with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spike_TV" title="Spike TV">Spike TV</a> in May 2014 to cover 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2016, GLORY announced a new multi-year deal with <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
