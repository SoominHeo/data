<p><i><b>Globe Trekker</b></i> (sometimes called <i><b>Pilot Guides</b></i> in Australia and <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, and originally broadcast as <i><b>Lonely Planet</b></i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Adventure_travel" title="Adventure travel">adventure tourism</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> produced by Pilot Productions. The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> series was inspired by the <a href="/wiki/Lonely_Planet" title="Lonely Planet">Lonely Planet</a> travelbooks and began airing in 1994. <i>Globe Trekker</i> is broadcast in over 40 countries across six continents. The program won over 20 international awards, including six American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cable_Ace" title="Cable Ace">Cable Ace</a> awards.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Program_Synopsis">Program Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Trekker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Program Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Each episode features a host, called a <i>traveller</i>, who travels with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camera_crew" title="Camera crew">camera crew</a> to a country—often, a relatively exotic locale—and experiences the sights, sounds, and culture that the location has to offer. Special episodes feature in-depth <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ape" title="Ape">ape</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">dive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shopping" title="Shopping">shopping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travel" title="Travel">journey</a>, <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a>, <a href="/wiki/Festival" title="Festival">festival</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a> guides.</p>
<p>The show often goes far beyond popular tourist destinations in order to give viewers a more authentic look at local culture. Presenters usually participate in different aspects of regional life, such as attending a traditional wedding or visiting a mining community. They address the viewer directly, acting as tourists-turned-tour guides, but are also filmed interacting with locals and discovering interesting locations in (mostly) unrehearsed sequences. <i>Globe Trekker</i> also sometimes includes brief interviews with <a href="/wiki/Backpacking_(travel)" title="Backpacking (travel)">backpackers</a> who share tips on independent travel in that particular country.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_details">Production details</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Trekker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production details">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It usually takes 12 to 15 weeks to complete an <a href="/wiki/Episode" title="Episode">episode</a> of the show from start to finish, including at least four weeks of <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a>, three weeks of planning and preparation (speaking to tourist boards, making travel arrangements), two to three weeks of actual filming, and three weeks of <a href="/wiki/Film_editing" title="Film editing">editing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Post-production" title="Post-production">post-production</a>.</p>
<p>The presenter is often accompanied by five or six members of <a href="/wiki/Film_crew" title="Film crew">camera and production crew</a>, who almost never appear on camera. Specifically, the crew consists of a <a href="/wiki/Camera_operator" title="Camera operator">camera operator</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Utility_sound_technician" title="Utility sound technician">sound technician</a>, plus a <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">producer</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Television_director" title="Television director">director</a>, who scout locations two weeks before filming. A <a href="/wiki/Driving" title="Driving">driver</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aviator" title="Aviator">pilot</a>, or other type of facilitator is often hired locally. A presenter and crew almost never spend more than three nights in one particular area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theme_music">Theme music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Trekker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Theme music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series is known for its distinctive theme and background music, which consists largely of <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">instrumental</a>, <a href="/wiki/Downtempo" title="Downtempo">downtempo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_dance" title="Electronic dance">electronic dance</a> compositions containing global <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a> elements. Most of the music is written for the show by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Ritchie_(producer)" title="Ian Ritchie (producer)">Ian Ritchie</a>, Michael Conn, Colin Winston-Fletcher, Makoto Sakamoto, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jesper_Mattsson_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jesper Mattsson (musician) (page does not exist)">Jesper Mattsson</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_Luscombe" title="Stephen Luscombe">Stephen Luscombe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pandit_Dinesh" title="Pandit Dinesh">Pandit Dinesh</a>.</p>
