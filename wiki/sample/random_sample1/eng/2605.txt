<p><b>Gliese 673</b> is an orange <a href="/wiki/Main_sequence" title="Main sequence">dwarf</a> <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Ophiuchus" title="Ophiuchus">Ophiuchus</a>. It has a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> of K7V.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras403_4_1949_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-mnras403_4_1949-2">[2]</a></sup> Main sequence stars with this spectra have a <a href="/wiki/Mass" title="Mass">mass</a> in the range of 60-70% of <a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass">solar mass</a> (<a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass"><i>M</i><sub>☉</sub></a>) (comparable to the members of the binary star system <a href="/wiki/61_Cygni" title="61 Cygni">61 Cygni</a>).</p>
<p>This star is relatively near our <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> at a distance of about 25 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_year" title="Light year">light years</a>. In spite of this proximity, however, it is still too faint to be viewed by the unaided eye. It is considered a slowly rotating star with a relatively high <a href="/wiki/Proper_motion" title="Proper motion">proper motion</a>.</p>
