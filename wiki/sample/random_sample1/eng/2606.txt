<p><b>Gliese 682</b> or <b><a href="/wiki/Gliese_Catalogue_of_Nearby_Stars" title="Gliese Catalogue of Nearby Stars">GJ</a> 682</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Red_dwarf" title="Red dwarf">red dwarf</a>. It is listed as the 49th-nearest known star to the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a>, being about 16 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_year" title="Light year">light years</a> away from the Earth. Even though it is close by, it is dim with an <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a> of 10.95 and thus requires a relatively powerful <a href="/wiki/Telescope" title="Telescope">telescope</a> to be seen. It is located in the constellation of <a href="/wiki/Scorpius" title="Scorpius">Scorpius</a>, near the bright star <a href="/wiki/Theta_Scorpii" title="Theta Scorpii">Theta Scorpii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIMBAD_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-SIMBAD-1">[1]</a></sup> It has two candidate planets, one of which is in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitable_zone" title="Habitable zone">habitable zone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
