<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">1976 Winter Olympics medal table</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <a href="/wiki/1976_Winter_Olympics" title="1976 Winter Olympics">1976 Winter Olympics</a>, officially known as the XII Olympic Winter Games, was a winter <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck" title="Innsbruck">Innsbruck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, from 4 to 15 February 1976. A total of 1,123 athletes representing 37 <a href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Committee" title="National Olympic Committee">National Olympic Committees</a> (NOC) participated in 37 events from 10 different <a href="/wiki/Olympic_sports" title="Olympic sports">sports and disciplines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IOC_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-1">[1]</a></sup> Two events were contested for the first time: the <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the 1976 Winter Olympics">figure skating</a> discipline of <a href="/wiki/Ice_dancing" title="Ice dancing">ice dancing</a>, and the men's 1,000 metres in <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Speed skating at the 1976 Winter Olympics">speed skating</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IOC_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ellensburg_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ellensburg-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sixteen NOCs won at least one medal, and twelve of them secured at least one gold. The <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Soviet Union at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Soviet Union</a> clinched the first place in the gold and overall medal counts, with 13 and 27, respectively. Moreover, the Soviet team also collected the most silver (6) and bronze (8) medals. The host nation, <a href="/wiki/Austria_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Austria at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Austria</a>, concluded its participation with a total of six medals (two golds, two silvers, and two bronzes).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Liechtenstein at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Liechtenstein</a> won its first Olympic medals: two bronzes by <a href="/wiki/Willi_Frommelt" title="Willi Frommelt">Willi Frommelt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hanni_Wenzel" title="Hanni Wenzel">Hanni Wenzel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Alpine skiing at the 1976 Winter Olympics">alpine skiing</a> slalom events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liechtenstein_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-liechtenstein-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanni_Wenzel_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hanni_Wenzel-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/East_Germany_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="East Germany at the 1976 Winter Olympics">East German</a> athletes achieved gold medal sweeps in <a href="/wiki/Luge_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Luge at the 1976 Winter Olympics">luge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bobsleigh_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Bobsleigh at the 1976 Winter Olympics">bobsleigh</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GDR_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GDR-5">[5]</a></sup> which accounted for five of the country's seven Olympic titles. Alpine skier <a href="/wiki/Rosi_Mittermaier" title="Rosi Mittermaier">Rosi Mittermaier</a> won the women's downhill and slalom events to give <a href="/wiki/West_Germany_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="West Germany at the 1976 Winter Olympics">West Germany</a>'s two gold medals in these Games. She came close to winning a third in the giant slalom, which would have made her the first woman to win all three Olympic alpine skiing events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IOC_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rosi_Mittermaier_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rosi_Mittermaier-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Cross-country skiing at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Cross-country skier</a> <a href="/wiki/Helena_Takalo" title="Helena Takalo">Helena Takalo</a> contributed three of <a href="/wiki/Finland_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Finland at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Finland</a>'s seven medals by reaching podium place finishes in all of the women's events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ellensburg_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ellensburg-2">[2]</a></sup> Two <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Netherlands at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Dutch</a> speed skaters were responsible for five of their country's six medals: <a href="/wiki/Piet_Kleine" title="Piet Kleine">Piet Kleine</a> won the men's 10,000 metres and came second in the 5,000 metres, while <a href="/wiki/Hans_van_Helden" title="Hans van Helden">Hans van Helden</a> secured bronze medals in the same events and also in the 1,500 metres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Before these Games, Great Britain's last Winter Olympic medal had been a gold at the <a href="/wiki/1964_Winter_Olympics" title="1964 Winter Olympics">1964 Games</a>, also held in Innsbruck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Twelve years later in the same city, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Great Britain at the 1976 Winter Olympics">British</a> team won again a single gold medal, this time by figure skater <a href="/wiki/John_Curry" title="John Curry">John Curry</a> in the men's singles with a still-standing record total score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IOC_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-John_Curry_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-John_Curry-9">[9]</a></sup> The two bronze medals won by <a href="/wiki/Sweden_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Sweden at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Swedish</a> athletes were not enough to prevent this team's performance from becoming the weakest ever in the nation's Winter Olympic history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Andorra_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Andorra at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Andorra</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="San Marino at the 1976 Winter Olympics">San Marino</a> sent their first delegations to the Winter Olympics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-San_Marino_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-San_Marino-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andorra_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andorra-12">[12]</a></sup> whereas the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_at_the_1976_Winter_Olympics" title="Republic of China at the 1976 Winter Olympics">Republic of China</a> competed for the second and last time before its return as <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei_at_the_1984_Winter_Olympics" title="Chinese Taipei at the 1984 Winter Olympics">Chinese Taipei at the 1984 Winter Olympics</a>; none of these teams medalled.</p>

<p>The medal table is based on information provided by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> (IOC) and is consistent with IOC convention in its published medal tables. By default, the table is ordered by the number of gold medals won by an NOC. The number of silver medals is taken into consideration next and then the number of bronze medals. If nations are still tied, equal ranking is given and they are listed alphabetically.</p>
