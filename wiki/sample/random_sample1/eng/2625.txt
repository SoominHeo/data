<p><b>Geumcheon District (Geumcheon-gu)</b> is one of the <a href="/wiki/Seoul#Administrative_divisions" title="Seoul">25 <i>gu</i></a> (districts) of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. It was created from southern parts of <i>Guro-gu</i> and tiny sections from <a href="/wiki/Gwangmyeong" title="Gwangmyeong">Gwangmyeong</a> in 1995. Its district office is located in front of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siheung_Station" title="Siheung Station">Siheung Station</a>, now Geumcheon-gu Office Station, in Siheung-dong.</p>
<p>Geumcheon-gu is located in the southwest corner of the city, south of the <a href="/wiki/Han_River_(Korea)" title="Han River (Korea)">Han River</a>. It is bordered on the west by the Anyang River, and partially on the east by Gwanak Mountain, a dominating part of Seoul's southern skyline.</p>
<p>Many technology companies are housed in Geumcheon-gu and several large headquarters are located here, albeit the income level of Seoulites here is lower than average. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeongbu_railway" title="Gyeongbu railway">Gyeongbu railway</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seoul_station" title="Seoul station">Seoul station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Busan_Station" title="Busan Station">Busan Station</a> passes through, as well as Seoul Subway Lines 1 and 7.</p>
<p>The mayor of this district has been Cha Sung-su (차성수) since July 2010.</p>
