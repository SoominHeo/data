<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Gigaku</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">伎楽</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, also known as <b><i>kure-gaku</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">呉楽</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup> refers to an extinct genre of masked drama-dance performance, imported into Japan during the <a href="/wiki/Asuka_period" title="Asuka period">Asuka period</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigaku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Records state that it was introduced during the 20th year of reign of <a href="/wiki/Empress_Suiko" title="Empress Suiko">Empress Suiko</a> (612.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cambridge_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cambridge-2">[2]</a></sup>) by a certain <b>Mimaji</b> (Mimaji <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">味摩之</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heibonsha_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-heibonsha-3">[3]</a></sup> from Kudara kingdom (<b><a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a></b>), one of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Three Kingdoms of Korea</a>.</p>
<p>The masked dance was performed in silent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mime" title="Mime">mime</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup> to the accompaniment of music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup> The flute, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Waist_drum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Waist drum (page does not exist)">waist drum</a> (or hip drum;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup> (<i>yōko</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">腰鼓</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>), also known as <i>kuretsuzumi</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">呉鼓</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>"Wu(China) drum"</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup>), and <i>shōban</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鉦盤</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a type of <a href="/wiki/Gong" title="Gong">gong</a>, were the three instruments used in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nara_Period" title="Nara Period">Nara Period</a>, though the gong was superseded by a type of cymbal (<i>dobyōshi</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">銅鈸子</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>) in the early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heian_Period" title="Heian Period">Heian Period</a> (9th century).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tokyomus_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tokyomus-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>About the only surviving description of the performance comes from the musical treatise forming a part of the <i><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ky%C5%8Dkunsh%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kyōkunshō (page does not exist)">Kyōkunshō</a><sup class="noprint" style="font-style: normal;">[<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%95%99%E8%A8%93%E6%8A%84" title="ja:教訓抄">ja</a>]</sup></b></i> (『教訓抄』; "Selections for Instructions and Admonition" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup>) authored by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Koma_no_Chikazane&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Koma no Chikazane (page does not exist)">Koma no Chikazane</a><sup class="noprint" style="font-style: normal;">[<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koma_no_Chikazane" title="ja:Koma no Chikazane">ja</a>]</sup> (died 1242).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup> According to this, the <i>netori</i> or tuning of instruments signals the start, followed by a prelude of instruments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup> Then there is a parading of the whole cast, both dancers and instrumentalists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heibonsha_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-heibonsha-3">[3]</a></sup> It has been speculated that the character mask named <i><b>Chidō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">治道</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></b></i> "Govern the way" probably took position at the front of the parade,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho-chido_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-chido-6">[6]</a></sup> especially as this mask is listed first off in the assets ledgers (Shizaichō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">資材帳</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>) for some of the temples that house gigaku masks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho-chido_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-chido-6">[6]</a></sup> The program opens with the Lion Dance (<a href="/wiki/Lion_dance" title="Lion dance">Shishimai</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup> and solo dances by the Duke of Wu,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup> wrestler, the birdman <a href="/wiki/Karura" title="Karura">karura</a>, and the Brahman priest.</p>
<p>There are two wrestler archetype characters, the <b>Kongō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">金剛</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> or "Vajra-yakṣa" who is open-mouthed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho-kongo_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-kongo-7">[7]</a></sup> and the <b><a href="/wiki/Rikishi" title="Rikishi">Rikishi</a></b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">力士</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> who is closed mouthed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho-kongo_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-kongo-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hayashiya_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-hayashiya-8">[8]</a></sup> These two are said to be analogous to the two <a href="/wiki/Nio" title="Nio">Niō</a> or guardian gate statues, who respectively form the open and closed <a href="/wiki/A-un" title="A-un">A-un</a> shapes in their mouths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho-kongo_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-kongo-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hayashiya_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-hayashiya-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the exaggerated features of many of the masks, the content of the play is described as being farcical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup> Indeed, the two-part play of the <b>Kuron</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">崑崙</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (or Konron; Chinese:<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kunlun_nu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kunlun nu (page does not exist)">Kunlun nu</a> which denotes a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_man" title="Black man">black man</a> or <a href="/wiki/Negrito" title="Negrito">negrito</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup>) and the Rikishi (wrestler or "Strong Man") is outright obscene.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-araki_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-araki-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the ribald performance, the lascivious Kuron falls in lust for the Gojo (Wu woman or Chinese maiden), and expresses his desire by holding up his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phallic" title="Phallic">phallic</a> prop called <i>marakata</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">陽物(マラカタ)</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, and beating it with his <a href="/wiki/Hand_fan" title="Hand fan">hand fan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hayashiya_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-hayashiya-8">[8]</a></sup> The comic dance maneuvers are referred to as <i>marafuri-mai</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">マラフリ舞</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>"phallus-swinging dance"</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. In subsequent development, the Kuron is subdued by the Rikishi who binds the Kuron by his equipment (<i>marakata</i>), and drags him along by the noose around his manhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hayashiya_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-hayashiya-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The gigaku is closely related to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sangaku_(theater)" title="Sangaku (theater)">sangaku</a> (散楽), and may be considered a variant imported via the Korean route, rather than more directly from China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shincho_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-shincho-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<ul>
