<p>A <b>memorial</b> is an object which serves as a focus for memory of something, usually a person (who has died) or an event. Popular forms of memorials include landmark objects or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_objects" title="Art objects">art objects</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculptures</a>, <a href="/wiki/Statue" title="Statue">statues</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fountain" title="Fountain">fountains</a>, and even entire <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">parks</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most common type of memorial is the <a href="/wiki/Headstone" title="Headstone">gravestone</a> or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Memorial_plaque" title="Memorial plaque">memorial plaque</a>. Also common are <a href="/wiki/War_memorial" title="War memorial">war memorials</a> commemorating those who have died in <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">wars</a>. Memorials in the form of a <a href="/wiki/Cross" title="Cross">cross</a> are called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intending_cross" title="Intending cross">intending crosses</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Online_memorial" title="Online memorial">Online memorials</a> and tributes are becoming increasingly popular especially with the increase in <a href="/wiki/Natural_burial" title="Natural burial">natural burial</a> where the laying of gravestones, or memorial plaques, is often not permitted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>When somebody has died, the family may request that a memorial gift (usually <a href="/wiki/Money" title="Money">money</a>) be given to a designated <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charity</a>, or that a tree be planted in memory of the person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sometimes, when a high school student has died, the memorials are placed in the form of a <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship</a>, to be awarded to high-achieving students in future years.</p>
