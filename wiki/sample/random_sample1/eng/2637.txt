<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kinomoto, Shiga</h1>

<p><b>Kinomoto</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">木之本町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kinomoto-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Ika_District,_Shiga" title="Ika District, Shiga">Ika District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shiga_Prefecture" title="Shiga Prefecture">Shiga Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It was the center of the district. In <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shizugatake" title="Battle of Shizugatake">Battle of Shizugatake</a> broke out at a mountain in the west of Kinomoto.</p>
<p>As of 2003, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 8,795 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 99.45 persons per km². The total area was 88.44 km².</p>
<p>On January 1, 2010, Kinomoto, along with the towns of <a href="/wiki/Kohoku,_Shiga" title="Kohoku, Shiga">Kohoku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Torahime,_Shiga" title="Torahime, Shiga">Torahime</a> (both from <a href="/wiki/Higashiazai_District,_Shiga" title="Higashiazai District, Shiga">Higashiazai District</a>), and the towns of <a href="/wiki/Nishiazai,_Shiga" title="Nishiazai, Shiga">Nishiazai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Takatsuki,_Shiga" title="Takatsuki, Shiga">Takatsuki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yogo,_Shiga" title="Yogo, Shiga">Yogo</a> (all from <a href="/wiki/Ika_District,_Shiga" title="Ika District, Shiga">Ika District</a>), was merged into the expanded city of <a href="/wiki/Nagahama,_Shiga" title="Nagahama, Shiga">Nagahama</a>. <a href="/wiki/Higashiazai_District,_Shiga" title="Higashiazai District, Shiga">Higashiazai District</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ika_District,_Shiga" title="Ika District, Shiga">Ika District</a> were both dissolved as a result of this merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Plannedmerge_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Plannedmerge-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
