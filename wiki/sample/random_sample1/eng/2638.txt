<p>The <i><b>Kinosaki</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">きのさき</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a limited express train service operated by <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR West). One of the services making up JR West's "Big X Network", it connects <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyoto_Station" title="Kyoto Station">Kyoto Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kinosaki_Onsen_Station" title="Kinosaki Onsen Station">Kinosaki Onsen Station</a> via the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanin_Main_Line" title="Sanin Main Line">Sanin Main Line</a>. The color associated with the service is purple, also the line color of the <a href="/wiki/Sagano_Line" title="Sagano Line">Sagano Line</a> which it uses for part of its journey.</p>

<p><i>Kinosaki</i> services are operated with <a href="/wiki/287_series" title="287 series">287 series</a> <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple unit</a> (EMU) trains based at Fukuchiyama Depot. Some services operate as combined <i>Kinosaki</i> and <i>Maizuru</i> services. The 287 series EMUs were introduced on these services from 12 March 2011, replacing 183 series sets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kyotoshimbun20091002_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-kyotoshimbun20091002-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/289_series" title="289 series">289 series</a> EMUs converted from former dual-voltage <a href="/wiki/683_series" title="683 series">683 series</a> trainsets were introduced on <i>Kinosaki</i> services from 31 October 2015, replacing the remaining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JNR" title="JNR">JNR</a>-era 381 series trains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hobidas20150821_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-hobidas20150821-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kinosaki_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>Kinosaki</i> service is named after the service's terminus, formerly known only as Kinosaki Station. Also, when the service began, the neighborhood in which the station stood was known as Kinosaki. Both are derived from the region's well-known area of hot springs.</p>
<ul>
<li>March 1, 1962: the <i>Kinosaki</i> service begins as two daily round trips between Kyoto and Kinosaki.</li>
<li>April 20, 1963: the <i>Kinosaki</i> running from Fukuchiyama Station is renamed to <i>Tango</i>. Daily service consists of four round trips per day.</li>
<li>March 5, 1966: the <i>Kinosaki</i> and <i>Tango</i> become express services.</li>
<li>November 10, 1968: <i>Hakuto</i> services departing Kyoto no longer connected with more cars at Ōsaka station. That service becomes the <i>Daisen</i>. The <i>Kinosaki</i> name is discontinued, and <i>Tango</i> services are increased to seven round trips daily. All <i>Tango</i> services are classified as express.</li>
<li>December 1990: 183 series trains providing seasonal express service between Osaka and Kinosaki are once again called <i>Kinosaki</i>.</li>
<li>1995: seasonal express service ends. The following year, it is decided that permanent limited express <i>Kinosaki</i> service will be restored between Kyoto and Kinosaki.</li>
<li>March 18, 2007: All cars become non-smoking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrr2010_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrr2010-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>March 16, 2013: From the start of the revised timetable, 183 series EMUs are withdrawn from <i>Kinosaki</i> services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20130317_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20130317-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
