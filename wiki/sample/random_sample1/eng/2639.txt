<p><b>Ginoza</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">宜野座村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ginoza-son</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Okinawan_language" title="Okinawan language">Okinawan</a>: <i>Jinuza</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_villages_in_Japan" title="List of villages in Japan">village</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kunigami_District,_Okinawa" title="Kunigami District, Okinawa">Kunigami District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Prefecture" title="Okinawa Prefecture">Okinawa Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2013 the village had a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 5,544 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 180 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area of Ginoza is 31.28 square kilometres (12.08 sq mi), and 50% of the land area of the village is used for United States military bases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-c_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-c-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pop-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza,_Okinawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a> for Ginoza (宜野座) mean "suitable field in which to sit".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza,_Okinawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ginoza is located on the eastern coast of the middle of the island of <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Island" title="Okinawa Island">Okinawa</a>. The village is located on the backbone of mountains that run north to south on Okinawa Island, and slopes gently to a broad coastline along the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Kanna_Dam" title="Kanna Dam">Kanna Dam</a> was completed in 1993.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza,_Okinawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Ginoza was historically agricultural. The area was, however, used as a retreat for members of the artistocratic class of Shuri, Naha, Toumai (Tomari); consequently, the area was strongly influenced, economic and culturally, by the central Ryukyuan state. In a census of Ginoza in 1903 half the town was registered as nobility, and half as commoners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-c_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-c-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ginoza became part of Okinawa Prefecture with its creation in 1879, and part of Kunigami District in 1896. In the administrative reorganization of Okinawa Prefecture in 1908 the Kinmu magiri was divided into two villages; the majority of the former magiri became Ginoza, and a small part was added to the present-day town of Kin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-c_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-c-2">[2]</a></sup> A large part of the population of Ginoza emigrated overseas before World War II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During World War 2 Ginoza village's schools were used as field hospitals. Bodies lie buried around the buildings to this day. Directly after the war Ginoza the south central part of Ginoza was home to a large concentration of refugees. The population of the village reached over 100,000, and the village was temporarily divided into six cities. The population of the village dropped rapidly after this period as Okinawans returned to their home villages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup> 50% of Ginoza remains occupied by United States military bases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Ginoza Village Museum opened in 1994.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza,_Okinawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agricultural production remains high in Ginoza. Like other areas of Okinawa, the village produces <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a>. Cut flowers production, which has spread rapidly to other municipalities in Okinawa Prefecture, has also been developed in Ginoza. The village produces of <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum" title="Chrysanthemum">chrysanthemums</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orchid" title="Orchid">orchids</a>. Tropical fruit, specifically <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapples</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">mangoes</a>, is also a developing part of the agricultural sector Ginoza. Additionally <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potatoes</a> are grown in Ginozan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rent from land used for military bases by the United States remains a large source of income in the Ginoza. The village government has attempted to diversify the economy of Ginoza, but dependence on rental income remains high.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-c_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-c-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza,_Okinawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_Autonomy_Law" title="Local Autonomy Law">Local Autonomy Law</a> of 1947 the government of Ginoza consists of an elected village council, an elected mayor, and administrative committees and departments under control of the mayor. Ginoza, with a population of under 2,000 residents, has a village council consisting of 12 members. Members of the council and the mayor serve a four-year term. The mayor of Ginoza is Atsushi Tōma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gikai_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gikai-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza,_Okinawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ginoza operates three elementary schools (Matsuda, Kanna, and Ginoza Elementary Schools) and one junior high school (Ginoza Junior High School). The <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Prefectural_Board_of_Education" title="Okinawa Prefectural Board of Education">Okinawa Prefectural Board of Education</a> operates <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ginoza High School (page does not exist)">Ginoza High School</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza,_Okinawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ginoza is crossed by <a href="/wiki/Japan_National_Route_329" title="Japan National Route 329">Japan National Route 329</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_highways_of_Japan" title="National highways of Japan">national highway</a> which connects <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nago,_Okinawa" title="Nago, Okinawa">Nago, Okinawa</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naha,_Okinawa" title="Naha, Okinawa">Naha, Okinawa</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Expressway" title="Okinawa Expressway">Okinawa Expressway</a>. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ginoza_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ginoza Interchange (page does not exist)">Ginoza Interchange</a> connects JNR 329 and the Okinawan Expressway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-n_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-n-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
