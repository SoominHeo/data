<p><b>Books Kinokuniya</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">紀伊國屋書店</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kinokuniya Shoten</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese bookstore chain operated by <b>Kinokuniya Company Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">株式会社紀伊國屋書店</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kabushiki-gaisha Kinokuniya Shoten</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, founded in 1927, with its first store located in <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku" title="Shinjuku">Shinjuku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. It means "Bookstore of <a href="/wiki/Kii_Province" title="Kii Province">Kii Province</a>". The company has its headquarters in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meguro,_Tokyo" title="Meguro, Tokyo">Meguro</a>, Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Books_Kinokuniya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kinokuniya was originally a lumber and charcoal dealer in <a href="/wiki/Yotsuya" title="Yotsuya">Yotsuya</a>; and after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1923_Great_Kanto_earthquake" title="1923 Great Kanto earthquake">1923 Great Kanto earthquake</a>, the business was moved westward to a new location in Shinjuku, where it was refashioned into a book store by former president Moichi Tanabe, opening with a staff of five in January 1927.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On the second floor was an art gallery.</p>
<p>The building burnt down in May 1945 during an air raid, but reopened in December 1945. Over the next few years, more Kinokuniya shops opened around Japan. In 1964, headquarters was established in Shinjuku (the current Shinjuku Main Store Building). The bookstore was nine stories and had two underground floors.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Store">Store</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Books_Kinokuniya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Store">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kinokuniya is the largest bookstore chain in Japan, with 56 shops around the country, in cities such as Osaka, Kyoto and Sapporo. Overall, it has more than 80 stores in Japan and overseas.</p>
<p>Its first overseas store opened in San Francisco in 1969. Several other bookstores have since opened in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, in cities including Los Angeles and New York. It then ventured into the Asia-Pacific market, opening its first store in Singapore (Liang Court Store) in 1983. Shops in Taiwan, Indonesia, Malaysia and Thailand followed suit. In 1996, Kinokuniya launched the first outlet in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neutral_Bay" title="Neutral Bay">Neutral Bay</a>. It later moved to its present location in <a href="/wiki/George_Street,_Sydney" title="George Street, Sydney">George Street</a> in the Central Business District.</p>
<p>Since 2000, Kinokuniya in the US has capitalised on the growing popularity of Japanese TV / <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> by stocking both English- and Japanese-language books /<a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>, as well as other Japanese TV / anime-related paraphernalia. The New York City branch in <a href="/wiki/Rockefeller_Center" title="Rockefeller Center">Rockefeller Center</a> was the best-known, encompassing, lengthwise, an entire city block. A new store has recently opened on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avenue_of_the_Americas" title="Avenue of the Americas">Avenue of the Americas</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Bryant_Park" title="Bryant Park">Bryant Park</a>, replacing the old store, which closed at the end of 2007.</p>
<p>Books Kinokuniya is known for the immense size of its bookshops. For 10 years its store in <a href="/wiki/Ngee_Ann_City" title="Ngee Ann City">Ngee Ann City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, was the largest bookshop in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_East_Asia" title="South East Asia">South East Asia</a>, until the opening of the new <a href="/wiki/Gramedia_(bookstore)" title="Gramedia (bookstore)">Gramedia</a> flagship store in Jakarta in 2007.</p>
<p>Fellow international bookstore chain <a href="/wiki/Page_One_(bookstore)" title="Page One (bookstore)">Page One</a> (headquartered in Singapore) began as the magazine agent for Kinokuniya but later became independent.</p>
