<p><b>Kinugawa River</b>, <b>Kinugawa</b>, or <b>Kinu River</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鬼怒川</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kinu-gawa</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a river on the main island of <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshu</a> in Japan. It flows from the north to the south on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_plain" title="Kantō plain">Kantō plain</a>, merging with <a href="/wiki/Tone_River" title="Tone River">Tone River</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">利根川</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tone-gawa</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. At 176.7 km in length, it is the longest tributary of Tone-gawa. From ancient times, it has been known to cause floods. The river starts in Kinu swamp in the city of <a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D,_Tochigi" title="Nikkō, Tochigi">Nikkō, Tochigi</a>, within <a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D_National_Park" title="Nikkō National Park">Nikkō National Park</a>.</p>
