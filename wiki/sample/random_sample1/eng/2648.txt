<p><b>COPEI</b>, also called <b>Social Christian Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Partido Socialcristiano</i></span>) or <b>Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Partido Verde</i></span>), is a <a href="/wiki/Christian_democracy" title="Christian democracy">Christian democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The acronym stands for <i>Comité de Organización Política Electoral Independiente</i> ("Independent Political Electoral Organization Committee"), but this provisional full name has fallen out of use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CrispLevMol_275_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CrispLevMol_275-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was founded on January 13, 1946, by <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Caldera" title="Rafael Caldera">Rafael Caldera</a>, who later served as president under the party's banner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CrispLevMol_275_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CrispLevMol_275-1">[1]</a></sup> Copei, along with <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Action_(Venezuela)" title="Democratic Action (Venezuela)">Democratic Action</a>, was the dominant Venezuelan party from 1958 to 1998. The only other Copei member to become president of Venezuela was <a href="/wiki/Luis_Herrera_Campins" title="Luis Herrera Campins">Luis Herrera Campins</a>, between 1979 and 1983. The party's leaders were Lorenzo Fernández, <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(Venezuelan_politician)" title="Eduardo Fernández (Venezuelan politician)">Eduardo Fernández</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oswaldo_%C3%81lvarez_Paz" title="Oswaldo Álvarez Paz">Oswaldo Álvarez Paz</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venezuelan_parliamentary_election,_2000" title="Venezuelan parliamentary election, 2000">2000 legislative elections</a> the party won 5 of 165 seats in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Venezuela" title="National Assembly of Venezuela">National Assembly</a>; 4 additional seats were won by an alliance with Democratic Action. In the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_parliamentary_election,_2005" title="Venezuelan parliamentary election, 2005">2005 legislative elections</a> Copei staged an electoral <a href="/wiki/Boycott" title="Boycott">boycott</a> and so did not win any seats in the National Assembly. In the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_parliamentary_election,_2010" title="Venezuelan parliamentary election, 2010">2010 parliamentary election</a>, Copei was part of the broad oppositional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coalition_for_Democratic_Unity" title="Coalition for Democratic Unity">Coalition for Democratic Unity</a> and won 8 of the 165 seats.</p>
