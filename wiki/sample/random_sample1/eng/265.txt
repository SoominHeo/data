<p>The <b>1976 Winter Paralympic Games</b> (Swedish: <i>Paralympiska vinterspelen 1976</i>) were the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Paralympics" title="Winter Paralympics">Winter Paralympics</a>. They were held in <a href="/wiki/%C3%96rnsk%C3%B6ldsvik" title="Örnsköldsvik">Örnsköldsvik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, from 21 to 28 February 1976. The <a href="/wiki/Disability" title="Disability">disabilities</a> included in this Paralympics were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blindness" title="Blindness">blindness</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amputee" title="Amputee">amputees</a>. Sixteen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Countries" title="Countries">countries</a> took part with 53 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There were competitions in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paralympic_alpine_skiing" title="Paralympic alpine skiing">Alpine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_cross-country_skiing" title="Paralympic cross-country skiing">Nordic skiing</a> for amputee and visually impaired athletes, and a demonstration event in ice sledge racing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Originally known as the <b>1st Winter Olympic Games for the Disabled</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
