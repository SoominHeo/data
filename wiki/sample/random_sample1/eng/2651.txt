<p><b>Girma Wolde-Giorgis</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ge%27ez" title="Ge'ez">Ge'ez</a>: ግርማ ወልደ ጊዮርጊስ; born December 1924) is an <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopian</a> politician who was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Ethiopia" title="List of Presidents of Ethiopia">President of Ethiopia</a> from 2001 to 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was the second person to hold the office of President since the founding of the Federal Republic of Ethiopia in 1995.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girma_Wolde-Giorgis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was elected President on 8 October 2001, as a relative unknown and a surprise choice, by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unanimous" title="Unanimous">unanimous</a> vote of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_People%27s_Representatives" title="House of People's Representatives">Ethiopian Parliament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The Ethiopian presidency is largely a symbolic office with little power. Most of the power is vested in the hands of the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Ethiopia" title="Prime Minister of Ethiopia">Prime Minister</a>. Presidents serve six-year <a href="/wiki/Term_of_office" title="Term of office">terms</a>. Girma was <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_presidential_election,_2007" title="Ethiopian presidential election, 2007">re-elected</a> as President on 9 October 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girma_Wolde-Giorgis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Girma was born in December 1924 in <a href="/wiki/Addis_Ababa" title="Addis Ababa">Addis Ababa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He first attended an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Orthodox_Church" title="Ethiopian Orthodox Church">Ethiopian Orthodox Church</a> school and later joined the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Teferi_Mekonnen_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Teferi Mekonnen School (page does not exist)">Teferi Mekonnen School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Addis_Ababa" title="Addis Ababa">Addis Ababa</a> where he followed his education until the Italian invasion. The school was then renamed "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scuola_Principe_di_Piemonte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scuola Principe di Piemonte (page does not exist)">Scuola Principe di Piemonte</a>" (Prince of Piedmonte School) for the Crown Prince of Italy, in Addis Ababa.</p>
<p>Between 1950 and 1952, he received certificates in <a href="/wiki/Management" title="Management">Management</a> (from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>), in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Traffic_Management" title="Air Traffic Management">Air Traffic Management</a> (in Sweden) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Traffic_Control" title="Air Traffic Control">Air Traffic Control</a> (in Canada) under a training programme sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization" title="International Civil Aviation Organization">International Civil Aviation Organization</a>. He was one of the first Ethiopians in the Ethiopian airforce dominated by American technicians. Girma tried to motivate Ethiopians to join the airlines and wrote a book on fundamentals. He was an activist and in the Inter Parliamentary Summit in Yugoslavia, he condemned the apartheid system in South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Girma speaks <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Afan_Oromo" title="Afan Oromo">Afan Oromo</a> (Oromiffa), <a href="/wiki/Amharic" title="Amharic">Amharic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> fluently.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girma_Wolde-Giorgis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Girma Wolde-Giorgis is from the plurality <a href="/wiki/Oromo_people" title="Oromo people">Oromo</a> ethnic group. He is married and has five children. Girma is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Orthodox_Tewahedo_Church" title="Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church">Ethiopian Orthodox Church</a>. He is widely renowned for his usual presence at Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church <a href="/wiki/Meskel" title="Meskel">Meskel Demera Festivals</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_service">Government service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girma_Wolde-Giorgis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Government service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1941: Enlisted at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ethiopian_Military_Radio_Communication&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ethiopian Military Radio Communication (page does not exist)">Ethiopian Military Radio Communication</a> set up by U.K.</li>
<li>1944: Graduated from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oletta_Military_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oletta Military Academy (page does not exist)">Oletta Military Academy</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sub-Lieutenant" title="Sub-Lieutenant">Sub-Lieutenant</a></li>
<li>1946: Joined the <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Air_Force" title="Ethiopian Air Force">Air Force</a> in and took various Air Management courses</li>
<li>1948: Became assistant teacher in <a href="/wiki/Air_navigation" title="Air navigation">air navigation</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flight_control" title="Flight control">flight control</a></li>
<li>1951: Became Director General of the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Planning at its establishment</li>
<li>1955: Became head of Civil Aviation of <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a> (Eritrea, at the time, was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federated" title="Federated">federated</a>)</li>
<li>1957: Assumed the post of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Director_General" title="Director General">Director General</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Civil_Aviation_Authority" title="Ethiopian Civil Aviation Authority">Ethiopian Civil Aviation Authority</a> and was Board member of the <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Airlines" title="Ethiopian Airlines">Ethiopian Airlines</a> during the same period.</li>
<li>1961: Became member of the lower house (Chamber of Deputies) of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Imperial_Ethiopian_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Imperial Ethiopian Parliament (page does not exist)">Imperial Ethiopian Parliament</a></li>
<li>Elected <a href="/wiki/Speaker_(politics)" title="Speaker (politics)">speaker</a> of the lower house (Chamber of Deputies) of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Imperial_Ethiopian_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Imperial Ethiopian Parliament (page does not exist)">Imperial Ethiopian Parliament</a> for three consecutive years.</li>
<li>Helped win a seat for the Ethiopian Parliament in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Parliamentary_Union" title="International Parliamentary Union">International Parliamentary Union</a> and attended conferences of the IPU in Switzerland, Denmark and former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a> and was elected as Vice President of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=52nd_Meeting_of_the_International_Parliamentary_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="52nd Meeting of the International Parliamentary Union (page does not exist)">52nd Meeting of the International Parliamentary Union</a>.</li>
<li>Served as Manager of the Import and Export Enterprise (IMPEX).</li>
<li>Served as deputy Commissioner of the Peace Programme drawn up in 1977 by the provisional military government of Ethiopia (Derg) to settle the Eritrean problem peacefully.</li>
<li>2000: Became member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Peoples%27_Representatives" title="House of Peoples' Representatives">House of Peoples' Representatives</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Democratic_Republic_of_Ethiopia" title="Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia">Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia</a> (FDRE) after winning in the <a href="/wiki/Becho" title="Becho">Becho</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woreda" title="Woreda">woreda</a> constituency, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mirab_Shewa_Zone" title="Mirab Shewa Zone">Mirab Shewa Zone</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oromia_Region" title="Oromia Region">Oromia Region</a>, as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_candidate" title="Independent candidate">independent candidate</a> in the second round elections</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Experience_in_non-governmental_offices">Experience in non-governmental offices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girma_Wolde-Giorgis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Experience in non-governmental offices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Between 1965 and 1974:</p>
<ul>
<li>Board member of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ethiopian_Chamber_of_Commerce&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ethiopian Chamber of Commerce (page does not exist)">Ethiopian Chamber of Commerce</a></li>
<li>Representative for Australian Trade Mission in Ethiopia</li>
<li>Founder and Director of the Ghibe Agricultural Association</li>
<li>Founder and Director of the Keffa and Illubabor Timber Processing Industry</li>
</ul>
<p>While in the then province of <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a> before 1990:</p>
<ul>
<li>President of the <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Red_Cross_Society" title="Ethiopian Red Cross Society">Ethiopian Red Cross Society</a>- Eritrea Branch (Asmara)</li>
<li>Board President of Cheshire Home</li>
<li>Managing Director of Leprosy Control Organization</li>
</ul>
<p>Upon returning to Addis Ababa in 1990, he served as Board Member of the Ethiopian Red Cross Society and Head of its International Logistics Department.</p>
<p>He launched an environmental protection association called <i>Lem Ethiopia</i> in March 1992 to date serving as Vice President of the Board of the Association.</p>
<p>Two days before Ethiopian Christmas, on January 5, 2014 he made a clear statement on Ethiopia TV, calling for pacification between Ethiopia and Eritrea, calling it his last personal task and fight. He is coordinating from his office a group of people trying to launch peace talks, after fifteen years of disagreements, culminating in the Ethio Erithrean war<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>of 1998-2000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_biography">Official biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girma_Wolde-Giorgis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Official biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Indian author Sivakumar K.P. has produced the official biography of Girma Wolde Giorgis. The book, <i>Under the Shade of a Gaashe</i>, was released on 15 July 2015 at the official residence of the former president.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Micro Business College is the publisher of the Ethiopian edition. The author acknowledges the role of <a class="external text" href="http://www.ethiopiaobserver.com/2016/10/the-life-of-girma-wolde-giorgis/" rel="nofollow">Abera Tilahun</a>, founder and president of Micros Business College in Ambo president of Micros Business College in Ambo in introducing him to the former president and financing the publishing of the book.</p>
