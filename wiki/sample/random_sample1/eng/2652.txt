<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Girsu was possibly inhabited in the <a href="/wiki/Ubaid_period" title="Ubaid period">Ubaid period</a> (5300-4800¬†BC), but significant levels of activity began in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_Dynastic_Period_of_Sumer" title="Early Dynastic Period of Sumer">Early Dynastic period</a> (2900-2335¬†BC). At the time of <a href="/wiki/Gudea" title="Gudea">Gudea</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Lagash" title="Lagash">Second Dynasty of Lagash</a>, Girsu became the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Lagash" title="Lagash">Lagash</a> kingdom and continued to be its religious center after political power had shifted to city of Lagash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Third_Dynasty_of_Ur" title="Third Dynasty of Ur">Ur III period</a>, Girsu was a major administrative center for the empire. After the fall of <a href="/wiki/Ur" title="Ur">Ur</a>, Girsu declined in importance, but remained inhabited until approximately 200¬†BC.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Archaeology">Archaeology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Girsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Archaeology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Issue_of_barley_rations.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2246" data-file-width="1944" height="254" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Issue_of_barley_rations.JPG/220px-Issue_of_barley_rations.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Issue_of_barley_rations.JPG/330px-Issue_of_barley_rations.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Issue_of_barley_rations.JPG/440px-Issue_of_barley_rations.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

An <a href="/wiki/Accounting" title="Accounting">account</a> of <a href="/wiki/Barley" title="Barley">barley</a> rations issued monthly to adults and children written in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuneiform" title="Cuneiform">Cuneiform</a> on clay tablet, written in year 4 of King <a href="/wiki/Urukagina" title="Urukagina">Urukagina</a> (circa 2350 BC). <p>Telloh was the first Sumerian site to be extensively excavated, at first under the French vice-consul at Basra, <a href="/wiki/Ernest_de_Sarzec" title="Ernest de Sarzec">Ernest de Sarzec</a>, from 1877 to 1900, followed by his successor <a href="/wiki/Gaston_Cros" title="Gaston Cros">Gaston Cros</a> from 1903‚Äì1909.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Excavations continued under Abb√© <a href="/wiki/Henri_de_Genouillac" title="Henri de Genouillac">Henri de Genouillac</a> in 1929‚Äì1931 and under <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Parrot" title="Andr√© Parrot">Andr√© Parrot</a> in 1931‚Äì1933.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It was at Girsu that the fragments of the <a href="/wiki/Stele_of_the_Vultures" title="Stele of the Vultures">Stele of the Vultures</a> were found. The site has suffered from poor excavation standards and also from illegal excavations. About 50,000 cuneiform tablets have been recovered from the site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
