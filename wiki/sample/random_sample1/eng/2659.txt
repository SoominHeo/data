<p><b>Kaela Kimura</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">木村 カエラ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kimura Kaera</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, born <b>Rie Kaela Kimura</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">木村 カエラ りえ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kimura Kaera Rie</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, (born October 24, 1984) is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> singer, songwriter, fashion model, and television presenter.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaela_Kimura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Adachi,_Tokyo" title="Adachi, Tokyo">Adachi, Tokyo</a>, Japan to a British father and Japanese mother, Kimura started working as a model in 2002 for the Japanese magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Seventeen_(Japanese_magazine)" title="Seventeen (Japanese magazine)">Seventeen</a></i>. She went on to host the morning television show <i>Saku Saku</i> from April 14, 2003 to March 31, 2006.</p>
<p>Kimura released her only <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">indie</a> single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Level_42_(song)" title="Level 42 (song)">Level 42</a>", but it was later released as her major label debut single on June 23, 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> after she signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbia_Music_Entertainment" title="Columbia Music Entertainment">Columbia Music Entertainment</a>. The song was used over the end credits of <i>Saku Saku</i>. On October 27, Kimura released her second single, "<a href="/wiki/Happiness!!!_(Kaela_Kimura_song)" title="Happiness!!! (Kaela Kimura song)">Happiness!!!</a>", which was followed by her debut album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaela" title="Kaela">Kaela</a></i>, on December 8, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the release of her third single, "<a href="/wiki/Rirura_Riruha" title="Rirura Riruha">Rirura Riruha</a>", on March 30, 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Kimura saw her fanbase grow considerably. The song was used in a series of <a href="/wiki/Vodafone" title="Vodafone">Vodafone</a> commercials in Japan and helped make her a household name. It sold over 100,000 copies, her best-selling single to date, and it also received a nomination for "Best Video" in the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards_Japan" title="MTV Video Music Awards Japan">MTV Video Music Awards Japan</a> 2005. Nearly seven months later, her next single, "<a href="/wiki/Beat_(Kaela_Kimura_song)" title="Beat (Kaela Kimura song)">Beat</a>", was released. The song was composed by <a href="/wiki/Tamio_Okuda" title="Tamio Okuda">Tamio Okuda</a>. The song had a more <a href="/wiki/Pop_punk" title="Pop punk">pop punk</a> influence. On January 18, 2006, Kimura released her fifth single, "<a href="/wiki/You_(Kaela_Kimura_song)" title="You (Kaela Kimura song)">You</a>". In March 2006, <i><a href="/wiki/Circle_(Kaela_Kimura_album)" title="Circle (Kaela Kimura album)">Circle</a></i> was released and debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> chart. She continued promoting <a href="/wiki/Kit_Kat" title="Kit Kat">Kit Kat</a> with a promotional single titled "Circle"; a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_Disc" title="Compact Disc">CD</a> with the song was sold along with the candy in a limited package. At this point, Kimura decided to leave the TV show, <i>Saku Saku</i>, to focus on her music career. Her last appearance as host was on March 31, 2006.</p>
<p>During 2006, Kimura became the lead vocalist of the rock band <a href="/wiki/Sadistic_Mika_Band" title="Sadistic Mika Band">Sadistic Mika Band</a>, which started as a collaboration for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirin_Brewery_Company" title="Kirin Brewery Company">Kirin</a> products promotion, and later resulting in the album <i>Narkissos</i> as Sadistic Mikaela Band. That year, she also covered <a href="/wiki/Roy_Orbison" title="Roy Orbison">Roy Orbison</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Oh,_Pretty_Woman" title="Oh, Pretty Woman">Oh, Pretty Woman</a></i>, which was used as the theme song of 2006 <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">Japanese television dramas</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Attention_Please" title="Attention Please">Attention Please</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Aya_Ueto" title="Aya Ueto">Aya Ueto</a>. She appeared on the program in a cameo role as an airline passenger in episode 10.</p>
<p>On June 28, a new single, "<a href="/wiki/Magic_Music_(Kaela_Kimura_song)" title="Magic Music (Kaela Kimura song)">Magic Music</a>", was released, followed by the release of "<a href="/wiki/Tree_Climbers" title="Tree Climbers">Tree Climbers</a>" three months later. Her eighth single, "<a href="/wiki/Snowdome_(song)" title="Snowdome (song)">Snowdome</a>", was released in conjunction with her third studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Scratch_(album)" title="Scratch (album)">Scratch</a></i>, on February 7, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The song "Snowdome" was composed by Japanese pop punk band <a href="/wiki/Beat_Crusaders" title="Beat Crusaders">Beat Crusaders</a>. The album <i>Scratch</i> topped the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> Albums Chart for two weeks and sold over 300,000 copies.</p>
<p>Kimura's ninth single, "Samantha", was released on July 18, 2007. The song was inspired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samantha_Stephens" title="Samantha Stephens">Samantha Stephens</a>, the protagonist from the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> TV series, <i><a href="/wiki/Bewitched" title="Bewitched">Bewitched</a></i>; and the music video features <a href="/wiki/Hanna-Barbera" title="Hanna-Barbera">Hanna-Barbera</a> designs. In September, a second <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> from her 'Scratch Tour' was released, followed by her tenth single, "Yellow", a month later. She released her eleventh single, "Jasper", a <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> song very different from her previous works, in early 2008. On April 2,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> her fourth album, <i>+1</i>, was released and debuted on the Oricon weekly albums chart at number three. After the album's release, Kimura hosted her '+1 TOUR' and released her first <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> compilation <i>Best Video 1</i>.</p>
<p>On September 10, 2008, she released her first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Double_A-side" title="Double A-side">double A-side</a> single "Moustache/memories (original version)" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">マスタッシュ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Masutasshu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. The first A-side was used in Lucido-L commercials, while the second was featured in the Japanese film <i>Paco to Maho no Ehon</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">パコと魔法の絵本</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <i>Paco and the Magical Picture Book</i>)</span>. Kimura released her first ballad, titled "Doko" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">どこ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Where")</span> on January 28, 2009, reaching number five on <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>, and her fourteenth single, "Banzai", on May 8, followed by her fifth studio album, <i>Hocus Pocus</i>, on June 24, celebrating her fifth anniversary as a singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaela_Kimura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 1, 2010, Kimura married actor <a href="/wiki/Eita" title="Eita">Eita</a> after becoming engaged in late 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Although the official marriage announcement was delayed due to work commitments. They have a son (born October 2010, though without a disclosed exact birthdate or name),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and a daughter (born October 2013)</p>
