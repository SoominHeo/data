<p><b>Saint Martin</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Saint-Martin</i></span>), officially the <b>Collectivity of Saint Martin</b> (<i><span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">Collectivité de Saint-Martin</span></i>) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity_of_France" title="Overseas collectivity of France">overseas collectivity of France</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>. With a population of 36,286 (as of Jan. 2011)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-population_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-population-2">[2]</a></sup> on an area of 53.2 square kilometres (20.5 sq mi), it encompasses the northern 60% of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_divided_islands" title="List of divided islands">divided island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">Saint Martin</a>, and some neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Islet" title="Islet">islets</a>, the largest of which is <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_Tintamarre" title="Île Tintamarre">Île Tintamarre</a>. The southern 40% of the island of Saint Martin constitute <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a>, since 2010 a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Countries_of_the_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Countries of the Kingdom of the Netherlands">constituent country</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>.</p>
<p>Before 2007, the French part of Saint Martin formed a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%C3%A9partement_d%27outre-mer" title="Département d'outre-mer">French overseas <i>région</i> and <i>département</i></a> of <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>. Saint Martin is separated from the island of <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Anguilla_Channel" title="Anguilla Channel">Anguilla Channel</a>. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Marigot,_Saint_Martin" title="Marigot, Saint Martin">Marigot</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_government">Politics and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Politics and government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saint Martin was for many years a French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commune_in_France" title="Commune in France">commune</a>, forming part of <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>, which is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%C3%A9partement_d%27outre-mer" title="Département d'outre-mer">overseas <i>région</i> and <i>département</i></a> of France. In 2003 the population of the French part of the island voted in favour of <a href="/wiki/Secession" title="Secession">secession</a> from Guadeloupe in order to form a separate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collectivit%C3%A9_d%27outre-mer" title="Collectivité d'outre-mer">overseas collectivity</a> (COM) of France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On 9 February 2007, the <a href="/wiki/French_Parliament" title="French Parliament">French Parliament</a> passed a bill granting COM status to both the French part of Saint Martin and (separately) the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint Barthélemy">Saint Barthélemy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The new status took effect on 15 July 2007, once the local assemblies were elected,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> with the second leg of the vote ultimately occurring on 15 July 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Saint Martin remains part of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The new governance structure befitting an overseas collectivity took effect on 15 July 2007 with the first session of the Territorial Council (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Conseil territorial</i></span>) and the election of <a href="/wiki/Louis-Constant_Fleming" title="Louis-Constant Fleming">Louis-Constant Fleming</a> as president of the Territorial Council. On 25 July 2008 Fleming resigned after being sanctioned by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_of_State_(France)" title="Council of State (France)">Conseil d'État</a> for one year over problems with his 2007 election campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 7 August, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frantz_Gumbs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frantz Gumbs (page does not exist)">Frantz Gumbs</a> was elected as President of the Territorial Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> However, his election was declared invalid on 10 April 2009 and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Gibbs" title="Daniel Gibbs">Daniel Gibbs</a> appointed as Acting President of the Territorial Council on 14 April 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Gumbs was reelected on 5 May 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before 2007, Saint Martin was coded as GP (Guadeloupe) in <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1" title="ISO 3166-1">ISO 3166-1</a>. In October 2007, it received the ISO 3166-1 code MF (alpha-2 code), MAF (alpha-3 code), and 663 (numeric code).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>coat of arms of the French overseas collectivity <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint-Martin" title="Saint-Martin">Saint-Martin</a></b> features a ship, a palm and a sun, and reads "Collectivité de Saint Martin".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The commune that existed until 22 February 2007, used similar arms but with the legend "Ville de Saint Martin".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The French part of the island has a land area of 53.2 square kilometres (20.5 sq mi). A local English-based <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands_Creole" title="Virgin Islands Creole">dialect</a> is spoken in informal situations on both the French and Dutch sides of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> At the January 2011 French census, the population in the French part of the island was 36,286<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-population_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-population-2">[2]</a></sup> (up from only 8,072 inhabitants at the 1982 census), which means a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 682 inhabitants per square kilometre (1,770/sq mi) in 2011.</p>
<p>During the 1980s, the population more than tripled; at the time, the collectivity was administered as a <a href="/wiki/Dependencies_of_Guadeloupe" title="Dependencies of Guadeloupe">part of Guadeloupe</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_Saint_Martin" title="Economy of Saint Martin">Economy of Saint Martin</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="500" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg/300px-Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg/450px-Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/54/Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>

Flags flying in Marigot harbour, Saint-Martin.</div>

<p>The official currency of Saint Martin is the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euro</a>, though the <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US dollar</a> is also widely accepted. Tourism is the main economic activity.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/INSEE" title="INSEE">INSEE</a> estimated that the total <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP</a> of Saint Martin amounted to 421 million euros in 1999 (US$449 million at 1999 exchanges rates; US$599 million at Oct. 2007 exchange rates).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GDP_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-GDP-18">[18]</a></sup> In that same year the GDP per capita of Saint Martin was 14,500 euros (US$15,500 at 1999 exchanges rates; US$20,600 at Oct. 2007 exchange rates), which was 39% lower than the average GDP per capita of <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_France" title="Metropolitan France">metropolitan France</a> in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GDP_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-GDP-18">[18]</a></sup> In comparison, the GDP per capita on the Dutch side of the island, Sint Maarten, was 14,430 euros in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The collectivity has seven public preschools (<i>maternelles</i>) and seven public elementary schools:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Preschools: Emile Choisy, Nina Duverly, Eli Gibs, Quartier d'Orléans 1, Sandy Ground, Hervé Williams 1, Hervé Williams 2</li>
<li>Elementary schools: Grand Case, Eveline Halley, Morne O'Reilly, Quartier d'Orléans 1, Quartier d'Orléans 2, Sandy Ground, Simeone Trott</li>
</ul>
<p>There are three junior high schools (<i>collège</i>) and one senior high school:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Junior highs: #1 Des Accords, #2 Soualiaga, #3 Quartier d'Orleans</li>

</ul>
