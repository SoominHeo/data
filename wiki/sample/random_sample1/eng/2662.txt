<p>The <b>Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German language</a>: <i>Ritterkreuz des Eisernen Kreuzes</i>), or simply the Knight's Cross (<i>Ritterkreuz</i>), and its variants were the highest awards in the military and paramilitary forces of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<p>The Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross was awarded for a wide range of reasons and across all ranks, from a senior commander for skilled leadership of his troops in battle to a low-ranking soldier for a single act of extreme battlefield bravery. Presentations were made to members of the three military branches of the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a> (the <i><a href="/wiki/German_Army_(Wehrmacht)" title="German Army (Wehrmacht)">Heer</a></i> (army), the <i><a href="/wiki/Kriegsmarine" title="Kriegsmarine">Kriegsmarine</a></i> (navy) and the <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a>), as well as the <a href="/wiki/Waffen-SS" title="Waffen-SS">Waffen-SS</a>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Reichsarbeitsdienst" title="Reichsarbeitsdienst">Reichsarbeitsdienst</a></i> (RAD—Reich Labour Service) and the <i><a href="/wiki/Volkssturm" title="Volkssturm">Volkssturm</a></i> (German national <a href="/wiki/Militia" title="Militia">militia</a>), along with personnel from other <a href="/wiki/Axis_powers" title="Axis powers">Axis powers</a>.</p>
<p>The award was instituted on 1 September 1939, at the onset of the German <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Poland" title="Invasion of Poland">Invasion of Poland</a>. A higher grade, the Oak Leaves to the Knight's Cross, was instituted in 1940. In 1941, two higher grades of the Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves were instituted: the Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves and Swords and the Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds). At the end of 1944 the final grade, the Knight's Cross with Golden Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds, was created.</p>
<p>Over 7,000 awards were made since its first presentation on 30 September 1939. Analysis of the <a href="/wiki/German_Federal_Archives" title="German Federal Archives">German Federal Archives</a> revealed evidence for 7,161 officially bestowed recipients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The German Federal Archives substantiate 863 awards of the Oak Leaves to the Knight's Cross, along with the 147 Swords and 27 Diamonds awards. The Golden Oak Leaves to the Knight's Cross was verifiably awarded only once, to <a href="/wiki/Hans-Ulrich_Rudel" title="Hans-Ulrich Rudel">Hans-Ulrich Rudel</a> on 29 December 1944.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_background">Historic background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historic background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IK_1813.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="297" data-file-width="238" height="212" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/IK_1813.jpg/170px-IK_1813.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/11/IK_1813.jpg 1.5x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IK_1813.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
1813 Iron Cross</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Prussian king <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friedrich_Wilhelm_III" title="Friedrich Wilhelm III">Friedrich Wilhelm III</a> established the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Cross" title="Iron Cross">Iron Cross</a> at the beginning of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_campaign_(Napoleonic_Wars)" title="German campaign (Napoleonic Wars)">German campaign</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>. The design was a silver-framed <a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">cast iron</a> cross on 13 March 1813.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Iron was a material which symbolised defiance and reflected the spirit of the age. The Prussian state had mounted a campaign steeped in patriotic rhetoric to rally their citizens to repulse the French occupation. To finance the army, the king implored wealthy Prussians to turn in their jewels in exchange for a men's cast-iron ring or a ladies' brooch, each bearing the legend "Gold I gave for iron" (<i>Gold gab ich für Eisen</i>).</p>
<p>With the outbreak of World War II on 1 September 1939, <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> in his role as commander in chief of the German armed forces decreed the renewal of the Iron Cross of 1939.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schaulen_p6_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schaulen_p6-3">[3]</a></sup> A new grade of the Iron Cross series was introduced, the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross. The Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross, without distinction, was awarded to officers and soldiers alike, conforming with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Socialist" title="National Socialist">National Socialist</a> slogan: "One people, one nation, one leader".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Knight.27s_Cross_grades">The Knight's Cross grades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Knight's Cross grades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The legal grounds for this decree had been established in 1937 with the German law of Titles, Orders and Honorary Signs (<i>Gesetz über Titel, Orden und Ehrenzeichen</i>) that made the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BChrer" title="Führer">Führer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reichskanzler" title="Reichskanzler">Reichskanzler</a> the only person who was allowed to award orders or honorary signs. The re-institution of the Iron Cross was therefore a Führer decree, which had political implication since the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles" title="Treaty of Versailles">Treaty of Versailles</a> had explicitly prohibited the creation of a military decoration, order or medal. The renewal for the first time had created an honorary sign of the entire German state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schaulen_p6_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Schaulen_p6-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the war progressed four additional grades were introduced to further distinguish those who had already won the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross or one of the higher grades and who continued to show merit in combat bravery or military success. The Knight's Cross was eventually awarded in five grades:</p>
<ul>
<li>Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross</li>
<li>Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves</li>
<li>Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves and Swords</li>
<li>Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds</li>
<li>Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Golden Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Knight.27s_Cross">Knight's Cross</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Knight's Cross">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross instituted on 1 September 1939. Its appearance was very similar to the Iron Cross. Its shape was that of a <a href="/wiki/Cross_patt%C3%A9e" title="Cross pattée">cross pattée</a>, a cross that has arms which are narrow at the center and broader at the perimeter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The most common Knight's Crosses were produced by the manufacturer Steinhauer &amp; Lück in <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCdenscheid" title="Lüdenscheid">Lüdenscheid</a>. The Steinhauer &amp; Lück crosses are stamped with the digits "800", indicating 800 grade silver, on the reverse side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Knight.27s_Cross_with_Oak_Leaves">Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves (<i>Ritterkreuz des Eisernen Kreuzes mit Eichenlaub</i>) was instituted on of 3 June 1940.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RGBl._I_S._849_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-RGBl._I_S._849-7">[7]</a></sup> Before the introduction of the Oak Leaves only 124 members of the Wehrmacht had received the Knight's Cross. Prior to Case Yellow (<span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fall_Gelb" title="Fall Gelb">Fall Gelb</a></i></span>), the attack on the Netherlands, Belgium and France, just 52 Knight's Crosses had been awarded. In May 1940 the number of presentations peaked. The timing for the introduction of the Oak Leaves is closely linked to Case Red (<span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i><a href="/wiki/Fall_Rot" title="Fall Rot">Fall Rot</a></i></span>), the second and decisive phase of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_France" title="Battle of France">Battle of France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Like the Knight's Cross to which it was added, the Oak Leaves clasp could be awarded for leadership, distinguished service or personal gallantry. The Oak Leaves, just like the 1813 Iron Cross and Grand Cross of the Iron Cross, was not a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Socialist" title="National Socialist">National Socialist</a> invention. They originally appeared in conjunction with the Golden Oak Leaves of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Eagle_Order" title="Red Eagle Order">Red Eagle Order</a>, which was the second highest Prussian order after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Eagle_Order" title="Black Eagle Order">Black Eagle Order</a>. The king also awarded the Oak Leaves together with the Pour le Mérite since 9 October 1813 for gallantry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Knight.27s_Cross_with_Oak_Leaves.2C_Swords_and_Diamonds">Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Knight's Cross with Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds (<i>Ritterkreuz des Eisernen Kreuzes mit Eichenlaub, Schwertern und Brillianten</i>) was instituted on 28 September 1941. The clasp was drilled out to accept the diamonds. The first recipients were <a href="/wiki/Werner_M%C3%B6lders" title="Werner Mölders">Werner Mölders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Galland" title="Adolf Galland">Adolf Galland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Presentation of the Diamonds came as a set and included the more elaborate A-piece and a second clasp with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhinestones" title="Rhinestones">rhinestones</a> for everyday wear, the B-piece.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The Diamonds were awarded 27 times during World War II. However three individuals never received a set of Diamonds. <a href="/wiki/Hans-Joachim_Marseille" title="Hans-Joachim Marseille">Hans-Joachim Marseille</a>, the fourth recipient, was killed in an aircraft crash prior to its presentation. The deteriorating situation and the end of the war prevented its presentation to <a href="/wiki/Karl_Mauss" title="Karl Mauss">Karl Mauss</a>, the 26th recipient and <a href="/wiki/Dietrich_von_Saucken" title="Dietrich von Saucken">Dietrich von Saucken</a>, the 27th and final recipient.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Knight.27s_Cross_with_Golden_Oak_Leaves.2C_Swords_and_Diamonds">Knight's Cross with Golden Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Knight's Cross with Golden Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Knight's Cross with Golden Oak Leaves, Swords and Diamonds (<i>Ritterkreuz des Eisernen Kreuzes mit Goldenem Eichenlaub, Schwertern und Brillianten</i>) was instituted on 29 December 1944.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RGBl._I_S._11_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-RGBl._I_S._11-15">[15]</a></sup>) Six sets of Golden Oak Leaves were manufactured, each consisting of an A-piece, made of 18 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carat_(purity)" title="Carat (purity)">Carat</a> gold with 58 real diamonds and a B-piece, made of 14 Carat with 68 real sapphires. One of these sets was presented to <a href="/wiki/Hans-Ulrich_Rudel" title="Hans-Ulrich Rudel">Hans-Ulrich Rudel</a> on 1 January 1945, the remaining five sets were taken to <a href="/wiki/Schloss_Klessheim" title="Schloss Klessheim">Schloss Klessheim</a>, where they were captured by the US forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>At first, the recipient of the Knight's Cross, or one of its higher grades, received a brief telegram informing him of the award of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross. Thereafter he received a <i>Vorläufiges Besitzzeugnis</i> (Preliminary Testimonial of Ownership). The award was also noted in the recipients <i>Soldbuch</i> (Soldiers Pay Book), his <i>Wehrpass</i> (Military Identification) and personnel records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The preliminary testimonial of ownership was followed by a more elaborate award document, the <i>Ritterkreuzurkunde</i> (Knight's Cross Certificate). Sometimes, Hitler's signature was a facsimile, but when time allowed, he did sign them. These documents for the Knight's Cross were used between 1939 and 1942, when the number of recipients made it possible only for documents of this type to be made for the higher grades of the order.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<td>Nomination<br/>

<div class="floatnone"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arrow_north.svg"><img alt="Arrow north.svg" data-file-height="903" data-file-width="365" height="67" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Arrow_north.svg/27px-Arrow_north.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Arrow_north.svg/41px-Arrow_north.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Arrow_north.svg/54px-Arrow_north.svg.png 2x" width="27"/></a></div>

<p>To qualify for the Knight's Cross, a soldier had to already hold the 1939 <a href="/wiki/Iron_Cross" title="Iron Cross">Iron Cross</a> First Class, though the Iron Cross First Class was awarded concurrently with the Knight's Cross in some cases. Unit commanders could also be awarded the medal for the exemplary conduct of the unit as a whole. Also, U-boat commanders could qualify for sinking 100,000 tons of shipping and <i>Luftwaffe</i> pilots could qualify for accumulating 20 "points" (with one point being awarded for shooting down a single-engine plane, two points for a twin-engine plane and three for a four-engine plane, with all points being doubled at night). It was issued from 1939 to 1945, with the requirements being gradually raised as the war went on.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Nominations for the Knight's Cross could be made at company level or higher. Commanders could not nominate themselves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In this instance the division adjutant made the recommendation. In the Luftwaffe the lowest level was the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geschwader" title="Geschwader">Geschwader</a></i> and in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kriegsmarine" title="Kriegsmarine">Kriegsmarine</a></i> the respective flotilla was authorized to make the nomination. It was also possible to nominate subordinated foreign units. The nomination by the troop had to be submitted in writing and in double copy. The format and the content were predefined. Every nomination contained the personal data, the rank and unit at the time of the act, since when the soldier held this position, the military service entry date, previous military decorations awarded and date of presentation, etc. For <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enlisted_soldier" title="Enlisted soldier">enlisted soldiers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noncommissioned_officer" title="Noncommissioned officer">noncommissioned officers</a> the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9sum%C3%A9" title="Résumé">résumé</a> had to be submitted as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scherzer_p31_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scherzer_p31-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The nomination had to be forwarded in writing by a courier up the official command chain. Every intermittent administrative office or commander between the nominating unit and the commander-in-chief of the respective Wehrmacht branch (commander-in-chief of the <i>Heer</i>, commander-in-chief of the <i>Luftwaffe</i> and commander-in-chief of the <i>Kriegsmarine</i> with their respective staff offices) had to give their approval along with a short comment. In exceptional cases, such as the nominated individual had sustained severe injuries or that the command chain had been interrupted, a nomination could be submitted via <a href="/wiki/Teleprinter" title="Teleprinter">teleprinter</a> communication.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scherzer_p31_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Scherzer_p31-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Approval_authority">Approval authority</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Approval authority">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1_September_1939_to_20_April_1945">1 September 1939 to 20 April 1945</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 1 September 1939 to 20 April 1945">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Administration/Berlin (preliminary decision) → Chief of the <a href="/wiki/Heerespersonalamt" title="Heerespersonalamt">Heerespersonalamt</a>/Berlin (preliminary decision) → <a href="/wiki/Oberkommando_der_Wehrmacht" title="Oberkommando der Wehrmacht">Oberkommando der Wehrmacht</a>-Department/Berlin (presenting) → Hitler (decision)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scherzer_p50_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scherzer_p50-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Army Personnel Branch Office was split due to the deteriorating war situation and was moved to <a href="/wiki/Marktschellenberg" title="Marktschellenberg">Marktschellenberg</a> in the time frame 21 to 24 April 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scherzer_p50_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Scherzer_p50-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="25_April_1945_to_30_April_1945_.28Hitler.27s_death.29">25 April 1945 to 30 April 1945 (Hitler's death)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 25 April 1945 to 30 April 1945 (Hitler's death)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Administration/Marktschellenberg (preliminary decision) → deputy Chief of the Heerespersonalamt/Marktschellenberg (preliminary decision) → Chief of the HPA/Berlin (preliminary decision) → OKW-Department/Berlin (presenting) → Hitler (decision)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="30_April_1945_onwards">30 April 1945 onwards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 30 April 1945 onwards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The approval authority of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross became confusing after Hitler's death on 30 April 1945. General <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Maisel" title="Ernst Maisel">Ernst Maisel</a>, deputy chief of Army Personnel Office, was authorized by the Presidential Chancellery to approve presentations of the Knights Cross effective as of 28 April 1945. Maisel, on 30 April, legally approved and conferred 33 Knight's Crosses, 29 nominations were rejected and four were deferred.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Hitler's death ended Maisel's authority to approve nominations. The authority to approve and make presentations was passed on to Hitler's successor as <i>Staatsoberhaupt</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Head_of_State" title="Head of State">Head of State</a>) <a href="/wiki/Karl_D%C3%B6nitz" title="Karl Dönitz">Karl Dönitz</a>, who held the title of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_Germany_(1919%E2%80%931945)" title="President of Germany (1919–1945)">Reichspräsident</a></i> (President) and Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scherzer_p63_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scherzer_p63-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="3_May_1945">3 May 1945</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 3 May 1945">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Teleprinter" title="Teleprinter">teleprinter</a> message dated 3 May 1945 was sent to the Commanders-in-Chief of those units still engaged in combat, empowering them to make autonomous presentations of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross themselves. The following decision making chains of command were possible at this time:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scherzer_p63_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Scherzer_p63-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Northern sector
<ul>
<li>Administration (preliminary decision) → Chief of the Heerespersonalamt/Flensburg (preliminary decision) → Chief of the OKW/Flensburg (presenting) → Dönitz/Flensburg (decision)</li>
<li>Commander-in-Chief North: <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Busch_(field_marshal)" title="Ernst Busch (field marshal)">Ernst Busch</a></li>
<li>Commander-in-Chief <a href="/wiki/Army_Group_Courland" title="Army Group Courland">Army Group Courland</a>: <a href="/wiki/Carl_Hilpert" title="Carl Hilpert">Carl Hilpert</a></li>
<li>Commander-in-Chief <a href="/wiki/Army_East_Prussia_(Wehrmacht)" title="Army East Prussia (Wehrmacht)">East Prussia</a>: <a href="/wiki/Dietrich_von_Saucken" title="Dietrich von Saucken">Dietrich von Saucken</a></li>
<li>Commander-in-Chief Norway: <a href="/wiki/Franz_B%C3%B6hme" title="Franz Böhme">Franz Böhme</a></li>
<li>Commander-in-Chief Denmark: <a href="/wiki/Georg_Lindemann" title="Georg Lindemann">Georg Lindemann</a></li>

</li>

<li>Commander-in-Chief <a href="/wiki/Army_Group_E" title="Army Group E">Army Group E</a>: <a href="/wiki/Alexander_L%C3%B6hr" title="Alexander Löhr">Alexander Löhr</a></li>
<li>Commander-in-Chief <a href="/wiki/Army_Group_Ostmark" title="Army Group Ostmark">Army Group Ostmark</a>: <a href="/wiki/Lothar_Rendulic" title="Lothar Rendulic">Lothar Rendulic</a></li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="D.C3.B6nitz-decree">Dönitz-decree</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Dönitz-decree">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Grand Admiral <a href="/wiki/Karl_D%C3%B6nitz" title="Karl Dönitz">Karl Dönitz</a>, as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_Germany_(1919%E2%80%931945)" title="President of Germany (1919–1945)">President of Germany</a> and Hitler's successor as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Head_of_State" title="Head of State">Head of State</a> and Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces, had declared that "All nominations for the bestowal of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross and their higher grades which have been received by the <i><a href="/wiki/Oberkommando_der_Wehrmacht" title="Oberkommando der Wehrmacht">Oberkommando der Wehrmacht</a></i> — staff of the Wehrmacht high command — until the capitulation becomes effective are approved, under the premise that all nominations are formally and correctly approved by the nominating authorities of the Wehrmacht, Heer including the Waffen-SS, Kriegsmarine and Luftwaffe all the way to the level of the <a href="/wiki/Field_army" title="Field army">field army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Army_group" title="Army group">army group</a> leadership."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>This "Dönitz-decree" (<i>Dönitz-Erlaß</i>) is most likely dated from 7 May 1945. Manfred Dörr, author of various publications related to the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross, requested legal counsel on this decree in 1988. The <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Dienststelle_(WASt)" title="Deutsche Dienststelle (WASt)">Deutsche Dienststelle (WASt)</a> came to the conclusion that this decree is unlawful and bears no legal justification. This blanket decree is not in line with the law governing the bestowal of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross which requires a case by case decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recipients">Recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross_recipients" title="List of Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross recipients">List of Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross recipients</a></div>
<p>Analysis of the <a href="/wiki/German_Federal_Archives" title="German Federal Archives">German Federal Archives</a> revealed evidence for 7,161 officially bestowed recipients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The German Federal Archives substantiate 863 awards of the Oak Leaves to the Knight's Cross, along with the 147 Swords and 27 Diamonds awards. Author Veit Scherzer concluded that every presentation of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross, or one of its higher grades, made until 20 April 1945 is verifiable in the <a href="/wiki/German_Federal_Archives" title="German Federal Archives">German Federal Archives</a>. The first echelon of the <i>Heerespersonalamt Abteilung P 5/Registratur</i> (Army Personnel Office Department P 5/Registry) was relocated from <a href="/wiki/Zossen" title="Zossen">Zossen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> to <a href="/wiki/Traunstein" title="Traunstein">Traunstein</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> on this day and the confusion regarding who can be considered a legitimate Knight's Cross recipient began.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<p>Hitler frequently made the presentations of the Oak Leaves and higher grades himself. The first presentations in 1940 and 1941 were made in the <a href="/wiki/Reich_Chancellery" title="Reich Chancellery">Reich Chancellery</a> in Berlin or at the <a href="/wiki/Berghof_(residence)" title="Berghof (residence)">Berghof</a> near <a href="/wiki/Berchtesgaden" title="Berchtesgaden">Berchtesgaden</a>. Beginning with <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a>, the invasion of the Soviet Union, the presentations were made at the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BChrer_Headquarters" title="Führer Headquarters">Führer Headquarters</a> "<a href="/wiki/Wolf%27s_Lair" title="Wolf's Lair">Wolf's Lair</a>" in <a href="/wiki/East_Prussia" title="East Prussia">East Prussia</a>, in the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wehrwolf" title="Wehrwolf">Wehrwolf</a>" near <a href="/wiki/Vinnytsia" title="Vinnytsia">Vinnytsia</a> in Ukraine, and at the Berghof. After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/July_20_plot" title="July 20 plot">July 20 plot</a>, the presentation were only made sporadically by Hitler himself. The last presentations by Hitler were made early in 1945 in the <i><a href="/wiki/F%C3%BChrerbunker" title="Führerbunker">Führerbunker</a></i> in Berlin. Senior commanders, like the commanders in chief of the <i>Kriegsmarine</i> and the Luftwaffe, and from the fall of 1944 also by the <i><a href="/wiki/Reichsf%C3%BChrer-SS" title="Reichsführer-SS">Reichsführer-SS</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Himmler" title="Heinrich Himmler">Heinrich Himmler</a>, made the presentations instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Association_of_Knight.27s_Cross_Recipients">Association of Knight's Cross Recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Association of Knight's Cross Recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Association of Knight's Cross Recipients (AKCR) (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German language</a>: <i>Ordensgemeinschaft der Ritterkreuzträger des Eisernen Kreuzes e.V. (OdR)</i>) is an association of highly decorated soldiers of both world wars. The association was founded in 1955 in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> by <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Keller" title="Alfred Keller">Alfred Keller</a>, Knight of the Order <i><a href="/wiki/Pour_le_M%C3%A9rite" title="Pour le Mérite">Pour le Mérite</a></i> and recipient of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross. Later, the recipients of the <a href="/wiki/Military_Merit_Cross_(Prussia)" title="Military Merit Cross (Prussia)">Prussian Golden Military Merit Cross</a>, or the <i>Pour le Mérite</i> for enlisted personnel, were included. The AKCR lists the awarding of 7318 Knight's Crosses, as well as 882 Oak Leaves, 159 Swords, 27 Diamonds, 1 Golden Oak Leaves and 1 <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross" title="Grand Cross of the Iron Cross">Grand Cross of the Iron Cross</a> for all ranks in the three branches of the Wehrmacht and the <a href="/wiki/Waffen-SS" title="Waffen-SS">Waffen-SS</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1999, German Minister of Defense <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Scharping" title="Rudolf Scharping">Rudolf Scharping</a> banned any official contacts between the Bundeswehr and the association, stating that it and many of its members shared neo-Nazi and revanchistic ideas which were not in conformity with the German constitution and Germany's postwar policies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-war">Post-war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Post-war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The German Law about Titles, Orders and Honorary Signs (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German language</a>: <i>Gesetz über Titel, Orden und Ehrenzeichen</i>) regulates the wearing of the Knight's Cross in post World War II Germany. German law prohibits wearing a swastika, so on 26 July 1957 the West German government authorized replacement Knight's Crosses with an Oak Leaf Cluster in place of the swastika, similar to the Iron Crosses of 1813, 1870 and 1914, which could be worn by World War II recipients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
