<p><i><b>Kisen</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">喜撰</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> also known as <i><b>Kisen Hōshi</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">喜撰法師</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was an early <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a> Buddhist monk <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">僧</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>sō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and poet. Little is known about his life other than that he lived in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ujiyama&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ujiyama (page does not exist)">Ujiyama</a>.</p>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Ki_no_Tsurayuki" title="Ki no Tsurayuki">Ki no Tsurayuki</a> wrote the Japanese preface <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仮名序</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>kanajo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Kokin_Wakash%C5%AB" title="Kokin Wakashū">Kokinshū</a></i>, he selected Kisen as one of the six poetic sages <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">六歌仙</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>rokkasen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> whose work was to be considered as superior. Tsurayuki says the following to comment on Kisen's work.</p>
<dl>
<dd>ことばかすかにして 始め終り たしかならず。</dd>
<dd><i>kotoba kasuka ni shite hajime wohari tashikanarazu.</i> (note: exact transcription using classical <a href="/wiki/Kana" title="Kana">kana</a> orthography)</dd>
<dd>The use of words is a delicate thing—from start to end it does not express the thing that actually is.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>いはば、 秋の月を見るに、 暁の雲に あへるが ごとし。</dd>
<dd><i>ihaba, aki no tsuki wo miruni, akatsuki no kumo ni aheru ga gotoshi.</i></dd>
<dd>That is to say, to speak of the autumn moon, one compares it to the clouds at dawn.</dd>
</dl>
<p>Kisen is sometimes said to be the author of the poetry collection <i>waka sakushiki</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">倭歌作式</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (also known as the <i>kisen-shiki</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">喜撰式</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>), but it is probably apocryphal and created well after the end of the <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a>.</p>
<p>The following two <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eika_(poetry)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eika (poetry) (page does not exist)">eika</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">詠歌</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> of Kisen are the only poems that can be confidently traced back to him.</p>
<dl>
<dd>わが庵は都の辰巳しかぞすむ世を宇治山と人はいふなり</dd>
<dd><i>waga iho ha miyako no tatsumi shika zo sumu yo wo ujiyama to hito ha ifu nari</i></dd>
<dd>Loosely: People say that I am a secluded hermit living in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ujiyama&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ujiyama (page does not exist)">Ujiyama</a>, but my hermitage is just to the southeast of the capital!</dd>
<dd>(<a href="/wiki/Kokin_Wakash%C5%AB" title="Kokin Wakashū">Kokinshū</a> 18:983; also in the <a href="/wiki/Ogura_Hyakunin_Isshu" title="Ogura Hyakunin Isshu">Ogura Hyakunin Isshu</a>)</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>木の間より見ゆるは谷の蛍かもいさりに海人の海へ行くかも</dd>
<dd><i>ki no ma yori miyuru ha tani no hotaru kamo isari ni ama no umi he yuku kamo</i></dd>
<dd>Loosely: What I see through the trees seems to be the fireflies <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">蛍</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>hotaru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> from the valley—or is that the luring fires <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">漁火</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>isaribi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> of the fishermen as they head to the sea?</dd>
<dd>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyokuy%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB" title="Gyokuyōshū">Gyokuyōshū</a> 400)</dd>
</dl>
