<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Godokoro</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Godokoro</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">碁所</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>godokoro</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a title that was given in Japan from the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> until the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>. In that period it was the highest official standing that could be attained by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go_player" title="Go player">go player</a>. Literally it is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metonym" title="Metonym">metonym</a>, meaning the 'go office'.</p>
<p>The title was created by <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a> and was given by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Commissioner_of_Temples_and_Shrines&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Commissioner of Temples and Shrines (page does not exist)">Commissioner of Temples and Shrines</a>. The Godokoro was required to be a <a href="/wiki/Meijin" title="Meijin">Meijin</a> and to be the best player in the land.</p>
<p>The Godokoro was responsible for approving rank promotions, mediating disputes between the <a href="/wiki/Four_go_houses" title="Four go houses">Four go houses</a>, being a Go tutor to the shogun, approving terms of challenge matches, and keeping the Go world running smoothly. The position was a lifetime commitment because the man who held the position was excused from playing any games that would cause him to lose the position of Godokoro or Meijin.</p>
