<p><b>Kishidan</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">氣志團</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, which means "The Knights", are a Japanese rock band. They have a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retro" title="Retro">retro</a> image, wearing <a href="/wiki/Japanese_school_uniform#Gakuran" title="Japanese school uniform">Japanese school uniforms</a> in the style of <a href="/wiki/B%C5%8Ds%C5%8Dzoku" title="Bōsōzoku">bōsōzoku</a>. The lead singer, Show Ayanocozey, was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ_OZMA" title="DJ OZMA">DJ OZMA</a>, until the persona was "retired" in December 2008. Kishidan announced a "comeback", after a three-year break, on January 27, 2009. The band had never officially split up, but had been taking a break while Show and Hikaru were working on the DJ OZMA side project. Their song, <i>Omae Dattanda</i> released on November 10, 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was used for the 11th ending theme song for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naruto_Shippuden" title="Naruto Shippuden">Naruto Shippuden</a>. They are signed to <a href="/wiki/Avex_Trax" title="Avex Trax">Avex Trax</a> and are managed by Sony Music Artists. Their song, <i>Warera Omou, Yue ni Warera Ari</i>, is used for the opening theme song for <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Ghost" title="Kamen Rider Ghost">Kamen Rider Ghost</a>, released on December 9, 2015</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage_personas">Stage personas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kishidan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stage personas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Their various uniforms, modeled after the short and long-coat bōsōzoku-preferred versions of old Japanese gakuran (boys' school uniforms), their matching bōsōzoku uniforms (the kind modeled on those worn by <a href="/wiki/Kamikaze" title="Kamikaze">kamikaze</a> pilots)—everything is done with a touch of humor and a nostalgic fondness for the gangs and values of days not-long gone by. Since the band started touring again in 2009, they have started wearing different styles of gakuran, as well as outfits from Tokyo-based fashion group, Saturday Nite.</p>
<p>The group's lyrics are predominantly about motorcycles, being with your gang, conflicts with school, and adolescent love. Though Kishidan has something of a comic approach to their presentation and performances, their songs are often sincere and about issues that are generally important to young people.</p>
<p>Kishidan's hit songs "One Night Carnival" and "Zoku" were featured in <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Osu!_Tatakae!_Ouendan" title="Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan">Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan</a>,</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Moero!_Nekketsu_Rhythm_Damashii_Osu!_Tatakae!_Ouendan_2" title="Moero! Nekketsu Rhythm Damashii Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan 2">Moero! Nekketsu Rhythm Damashii Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan 2</a></i>, respectively, which are games about ouendan (cheer squads). The song "One Night Carnival" (a cover version) is even used for a level that was inspired by one of Kishidan's live performances. Coincidentally, the characters wear long gakuran coats and dance like Kishidan. The dancing style is based on actual ouendan cheering routines, and long gakuran coats are often worn by ouendan cheer teams. It is possible that Kishidan's lively and powerful dance routines were inspired by the tradition of ouendan cheering, a subject that is unique to Japanese culture.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kishidan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A February 2011 appearance on <a href="/wiki/MTV_Japan" title="MTV Japan">MTV Japan</a>'s Mega Vector program was heavily criticized by the <a href="/wiki/Simon_Wiesenthal_Center" title="Simon Wiesenthal Center">Simon Wiesenthal Center</a> after the band appeared in costumes resembling <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi" title="Nazi">Nazi</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS" title="SS">SS</a> uniforms. The band's agency, <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment_Japan" title="Sony Music Entertainment Japan">Sony Music Artists</a>, issued a statement of apology, noting that the band members "deeply regretted" their attire and that the clothing "was not meant to carry any ideological meaning whatsoever."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Avex_Group" title="Avex Group">Avex Group</a>, the band's current record label, also sent an apology through its homepage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avex_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-avex-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:30%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>In regards to the costume worn by Kishidan on MTV Network Japan’s program, “Megavector”, although it was not meant to carry any ideological meaning whatsoever, we deeply regret and apologize for the distress it has caused Simon Wiesenthal Center and all concerned. Members of Kishidan also deeply regret and apologize to you in this matter. We have duly received the words of advice from Simon Wiesenthal Center and take them very seriously. Kishidan will never again use this costume and it will be disposed of immediately. We will not broadcast, transmit, or distribute the video recording of Kishidan's performance with the said costume, and the recording will be disposed of immediately. The companies and artist Kishidan sincerely apologize for the insensitive action.</p>
</div>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_show">Live show</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kishidan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kishidan embraces the theatrics of rock music in their live performance, and make extensive use of pop dancing and theatrics. Members of the band will sometimes pretend to perish mid-concert in battle with rival school gangs. Kishidan is also known to parody current popular Japanese songs, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matsudaira_Ken" title="Matsudaira Ken">Matsudaira Ken</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Matsuken_Samba_II" title="Matsuken Samba II">Matsuken Samba II</a>", and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakashima_Mika" title="Nakashima Mika">Nakashima Mika</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Glamorous_Sky" title="Glamorous Sky">Glamorous Sky</a>" from the film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Nana_(manga)" title="Nana (manga)">Nana</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_ateji">Use of ateji</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kishidan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Use of ateji">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since transferring to Avex, the band started to use <a href="/wiki/Ateji" title="Ateji">ateji</a> to promote their singles. In every promotional video, the band uses the ateji character for the <a href="/wiki/Avex_Trax" title="Avex Trax">Avex Trax</a> label, <b>影別苦須 虎津苦須</b>.</p>
