<p>The <b>Kia Granbird</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Coach_(bus)" title="Coach (bus)">high-decker coach</a> produced by the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors" title="Kia Motors">Kia</a>. It was derived from the <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Fuso_Truck_and_Bus_Corporation" title="Mitsubishi Fuso Truck and Bus Corporation">Mitsubishi Fuso</a> platform and for a brief period of time from former <a href="/wiki/Asia_Motors" title="Asia Motors">Asia Motors</a>. Production started in 1994, and the exterior look stayed the same until a cosmetic update in 2007 for 2008. The Granbird is manufactured at <a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors" title="Kia Motors">Kia</a>'s Gwangju plant, which also makes the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Soul" title="Kia Soul">Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kia_Sportage" title="Kia Sportage">Sportage</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kia_Rondo" title="Kia Rondo">Rondo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors" title="Kia Motors">Kia</a>'s commercial and military trucks. The Granbird is only sold in selected markets.</p>
