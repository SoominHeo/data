<p>The <b>Kia Rio</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Subcompact_car" title="Subcompact car">subcompact car</a> produced by the South Korean manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors" title="Kia Motors">Kia</a> since August 2000 and now in its third generation. Body styles have included a three and five-door <a href="/wiki/Hatchback" title="Hatchback">hatchback</a> and four-door <a href="/wiki/Sedan_(automobile)" title="Sedan (automobile)">sedan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Body_style" title="Body style">body styles</a>, equipped with <a href="/wiki/Inline-four_engine" title="Inline-four engine">inline-four</a> <a href="/wiki/Gasoline" title="Gasoline">gasoline</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel</a> engines, and <a href="/wiki/Front-wheel_drive" title="Front-wheel drive">front-wheel drive</a>.</p>
<p>The Rio replaced the first-generation <a href="/wiki/Kia_Pride" title="Kia Pride">Pride</a>—a rebadged version of the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Festiva" title="Ford Festiva">Ford Festiva</a>—and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kia_Avella" title="Kia Avella">Avella</a>, a subcompact sold as a <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> in some markets. A second generation was introduced in 2005 in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and 2006 in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, sharing its platform with the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Accent" title="Hyundai Accent">Hyundai Accent</a>, a subcompact manufactured by its sister <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Motor_Company" title="Hyundai Motor Company">Hyundai Motor Company</a>. In <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, the second generation was marketed as the New Pride.</p>
<p>In 2010, the Rio was facelifted to incorporate Kia's "Tiger Nose" <a href="/wiki/Grille#In_powered_vehicles" title="Grille">grille</a>, and for model year 2012, Kia introduced the third generation Rio.</p>

<p>The first-generation Kia Rio (referred to as the "DC") was offered in both four-door <a href="/wiki/Sedan_(automobile)" title="Sedan (automobile)">sedan</a> and five-door <a href="/wiki/Station_wagon" title="Station wagon">station wagon</a> body styles. When released, it was the least-expensive mass-produced car to be sold in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> While the Rio's styling and affordability were well-received, it was derided for its poor build quality and driving experience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The station wagon was marketed as the "Rio Cinco" in the United States, "Rio RX-<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">V</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, and "Rio Look" in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>. The sedan version was not sold in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and the station wagon was known as the "Rio" there. In Greece, both versions were sold as the "Rio".</p>
<p>The first-generation Rio offered only one engine for each model year in the USA: a 96 hp (72 kW) 1.5-litre <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DOHC" title="DOHC">DOHC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight-4" title="Straight-4">I4</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Four-stroke_cycle" title="Four-stroke cycle">gasoline engine</a> from 2001 through 2002. Then a larger version appeared, a 1.6-litre DOHC four-cylinder rated at 104 hp (78 kW) used for model years 2003 through 2005. All years offered a choice of a five-speed <a href="/wiki/Manual_transmission" title="Manual transmission">manual transmission</a> or a four-speed F4A-EL <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic</a>. An alternative of a 1.3-litre (1,343 cc) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SOHC" title="SOHC">SOHC</a> four-cylinder/eight-valve petrol, producing 75 hp (56 kW) was offered in some other countries, including the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and most of continental Europe. The USA-version five-door featured <a href="/wiki/Power_steering" title="Power steering">power steering</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Tachometer" title="Tachometer">tachometer</a>, optional on the sedan. The European version was marketed under several trim levels; standard equipment for all trims included a driver airbag. Additional features were available for promotion in Greece, including leather seats and car alarm. In Europe, the various national markets received different selections of the three engines.</p>
<p>The domestic Korean market versions did not include the 1.6-liter version, as the South Korean taxation system heavily penalizes cars of more than 1,500 cc. Claimed outputs for the domestic versions (JIS) were higher, at 84 PS (62 kW) for the little 1.3 and 108 PS (79 kW) for the DOHC 1.5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AR02_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AR02-5">[5]</a></sup> For the SOHC 1.5 95 PS (70 kW) was claimed. The first generation hatchback was marketed as the Rio RX-V in South Korea.</p>
<p>Safety features included <a href="/wiki/Seat_belt" title="Seat belt">seat belts</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Airbag" title="Airbag">airbag</a> for the driver. <a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_braking_system" title="Anti-lock braking system">ABS</a> was available as an option for entry-line cars but fitted by default for top of the range models (i.e., Rio LX, in the UK, and Rio LS, in Greece).</p>

<p>In 2002 the Rio received a mild redesign (for the 2003 model year in the US) including upgrades in engine, suspension, brakes and new exterior and interior styling. After this 2002 facelift the entire range received the "Rio SF" moniker in the domestic South Korean market, with "SF" standing for both "Science Fiction" and "Safety First".</p>
<p>In Europe, the <a href="/wiki/Facelift_(automotive)" title="Facelift (automotive)">facelifted</a> Rio received minor changes in external look and engine improvement of the 1.3 from 75 hp (56 kW) to 80 hp (60 kW) at 5,500 rpm, and 86 lb·ft (117 N·m) at 3,000 rpm.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> production of the facelifted Rio by <a href="/wiki/SAIPA" title="SAIPA">SAIPA</a> began in 2005. The cars were not equipped with ABS or airbags until 2011, powered by a version of the 1.5 litre engine which produces 96 hp (72 kW) at 5,500 rpm and 135 N·m (100 ft·lbf) torque at 4,500 rpm. In early 2012, the production ended.</p>

<p>Kia introduced the second generation (JB) in the early 2005 model year in Europe and for model year 2006 in North America, based on a redesigned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile_platform" title="Automobile platform">platform</a> shared with the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Accent" title="Hyundai Accent">Hyundai Accent</a>. The engine was upgraded to a 110 hp 1.6-litre DOHC <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight-4" title="Straight-4">four-cylinder</a> "Alpha II" engine now also shared with the Hyundai Accent. Other changes include a new exterior design, an increase in exterior size and interior volume, increased safety features and <a href="/wiki/Fuel_economy_in_automobiles" title="Fuel economy in automobiles">fuel economy</a>. The hatchback was renamed to <i>Rio5</i> in the USA and Canada.</p>
<p>Kia offered the 2006 sedan as the Rio and Rio LX, the LX trim included power steering, air conditioning, and <a href="/wiki/CD_player" title="CD player">CD player</a>. The Rio5 (USA) offered simply in SX trim level with leather-covered <a href="/wiki/Steering_wheel" title="Steering wheel">steering wheel</a>. As in the first generation, only the five-door hatchback version was offered in some <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> markets (including the UK). Three engines are available; a 1.6 litre <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DOHC" title="DOHC">DOHC</a> engine with 110 hp (82 kW), a lesser 1.4 litre version with 96 hp (72 kW) and a 1.5 L <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turbodiesel" title="Turbodiesel">Turbodiesel</a> engine with 109 hp. However, in the US market only the 1.6L gasoline engine is offered, in two body styles: a four-door-sedan and a five-door hatch. The European version's common rail diesel engine produces 110 PS (81 kW).</p>
<p>In 2007, an SX trim level was added to the Rio sedan with the same features supplied with the Rio5 SX.</p>
<p>In 2007, the South Korean government began testing approximately 4,000 pre-production Rio <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_electric_vehicle" title="Hybrid electric vehicle">hybrid-electric</a> models, with parent company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyundai_Kia_Automotive_Group" title="Hyundai Kia Automotive Group">Hyundai Kia Automotive Group</a> later announcing and subsequently withdrawing anticipated release dates for the hybrid model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>For 2008, Kia offered the LX trim level for the Rio5 (similar to the Rio LX sedan) and a limited production of Rio5 SX Tuner models, featuring Falken FK452 tires, Tanabe <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sway_bar" title="Sway bar">strut tower brace</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eibach_Federn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eibach Federn (page does not exist)">Eibach</a> lowering <a href="/wiki/Coil_spring" title="Coil spring">springs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the 2009 model year, all Rio and Rio5 trims received standard <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Satellite Radio</a> (complimentary for three months) and radios with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Serial_Bus" title="Universal Serial Bus">USB</a> port &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack_(connector)" title="Jack (connector)">auxiliary jack</a>, the auxiliary jack substituting for a <a href="/wiki/Bluetooth" title="Bluetooth">Bluetooth</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phone</a> connection for devices with an <a href="/wiki/Headphones" title="Headphones">earphone</a> jack, e.g., the <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">iPhone</a>. The Kia Rio ranked 5th in the "20 least expensive 2009 vehicles to insure" list by Insure.com. According to <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a>, the Rio is one of the least expensive vehicles to insure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>For the 2010 model year, the models received a <a href="/wiki/Facelift_(automotive)" title="Facelift (automotive)">facelift</a> in late 2009, adopting Kia's new <a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors#Design_emphasis_and_bowtie" title="Kia Motors">Tiger nose</a> grille. In addition, the steering wheel receives the same design as the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Soul" title="Kia Soul">Kia Soul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kia_Forte" title="Kia Forte">Kia Forte</a>, featuring optional Bluetooth hands-free phone operation, and the gauges cluster receives a new red backlit design. The headlights were modified slightly, with a darker appearance and parking lights and side-marker lights sharing the turn-signal housing. Side mouldings on the car became narrower and body-colored. For the UK version, the Rio looks exactly the same as the US version, except that only the <a href="/wiki/Hatchback" title="Hatchback">hatchback</a> variant is sold there. It has Kia's new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bowtie" title="Bowtie">bowtie</a> grille (for the 2010 model) and is powered by a 1.4 litre DOHC four-cylinder 16-valve petrol engine, or a 1.5 litre DOHC four-cylinder 16-valve diesel. They both have a five speed manual gearbox.</p>

<p>The third generation Kia Rio debuted on March 1, 2011, at the <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Motor_Show" title="Geneva Motor Show">Geneva Motor Show</a>, again based on the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Accent" title="Hyundai Accent">Hyundai Accent</a> and featuring a 1.4 L or 1.6 L <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Gamma_engine" title="Hyundai Gamma engine">Hyundai GDI Gamma engine</a>. The European market also receives the option of a 1.25L Petrol Engine, as well as a 1.4L or 1.1L CRDi Turbo Diesel Engine - the 1.1L engine having the lowest emissions and highest MPG of any non-electric or hybrid car currently on sale at time of writing; up to 88mpg on the EU combined cycle (73mpg in US gallons) and emitting only 85grams of CO2 per kilometer. This market, however, lacks the 1.6L engine, with the 1.4 being the most powerful on offer. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual and 6-speed automatic for the American market, with the European market receiving a 5-speed manual for the 1.25L Petrol engine, a 6-speed manual for the 1.4L Petrol, 1.1L and 1.4L Diesels and a 4-speed automatic for the 1.4L petrol "2" spec model in 5-door configuration. Intelligent stop go is an option on almost all available trim and engine levels.</p>
<p>The Rio in Europe also receives a 3-door model. It is mechanically identical to the 5 door, though its longer doors give it a more sporting appearance that appeals to younger audiences. The 3 door is also noticeably cheaper than the 5 door. A one time, limited delivery of 50 top-spec European 3-door models have been shipped to New Zealand based on the UK "3" trim and marketed as the Rio Sport EX. The imported European models have a better spec than the current car available in New Zealand, with some extra options added over the European car (such as keyless go), with the limited availability designed to make the car feel exclusive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011, the third generation Rio was awarded <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semperit_Irish_Car_of_the_Year" title="Semperit Irish Car of the Year">Semperit Irish Car of the Year</a></i> and in December was awarded <a href="/wiki/Carsguide.com.au" title="Carsguide.com.au">Carsguide</a>'s 2011 Car of the Year award as well, triumphing over many other vehicles including the likes of the <a href="/wiki/Range_Rover" title="Range Rover">Range Rover</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evoque" title="Evoque">Evoque</a> and the recently facelifted <a href="/wiki/Ford_Territory" title="Ford Territory">Ford Territory</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kia_Rio_GT">Kia Rio GT</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kia_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Kia Rio GT">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kia released a turbo charged performance model of the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Cee%27d" title="Kia Cee'd">Cee'd</a>, called the Pro_Cee'd GT in Europe in July 2013, and is the company's first ever <a href="/wiki/Hot_hatch" title="Hot hatch">hot hatch</a>. Rumours have surfaced around the internet that a Rio GT (based on what is known as the Rio 5-door in America, but using the 3-door European configuration) may go into production, giving Kia a true supermini hot hatch to rival the established class leaders; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ford_Fiesta_ST" title="Ford Fiesta ST">Ford Fiesta ST</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peugeot_208" title="Peugeot 208">Peugeot 208 GTi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renault_Clio_Renault_Sport" title="Renault Clio Renault Sport">Renaultsport Clio</a>. Rumours were backed up when the European VP for Kia, Benny Oeyen, mentioned that if the Cee'd GT is successful, Kia would put more GT models into production, including the Rio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> An unnamed Kia insider also stated the Rio as a candidate for GT treatment in future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fourth_generation_.282016-.29">Fourth generation (2016-)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kia_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fourth generation (2016-)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kia Motors presented the fourth generation Rio at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Paris_Motor_Show" title="Paris Motor Show">Paris Motor Show</a>. The new car is designed in Kia's California and Germany design centers, and will feature longer front overhangs, a bigger hood, and a more upright C-pillar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The hatchback model will be 15 mm longer, 5 mm wider, and 5 mm shorter in height than its predecessor. Production will begin by the end of 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
