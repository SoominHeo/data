<p>The <b>Kia Opirus</b> was an <a href="/wiki/Executive_car" title="Executive car">executive car</a> manufactured and marketed by <a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors" title="Kia Motors">Kia Motors</a> that was launched in April 2003 and was marketed globally under various nameplates, prominently as the <b>Amanti</b>. It was considered to be Kia's flagship vehicle.</p>
<p>As Kia's largest entry into the <a href="/wiki/Executive_car" title="Executive car">executive car</a> market, the Opirus/Amanti had been marketed in a single trim level and only as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sedan_(car)" title="Sedan (car)">sedan</a>. It shared some components with its now-defunct corporate cousin, the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Grandeur#XG300" title="Hyundai Grandeur">Hyundai Grandeur XG</a>, including its 3.5 L <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6 engine</a>. For 2007, the Kia Opirus received various upgrades, including suspension and styling revisions, and the addition of the same <a href="/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine" title="Internal combustion engine">engine</a> as the current <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Grandeur" title="Hyundai Grandeur">Hyundai Grandeur</a> (Azera), this time being a 3.8 L V6 engine.</p>
<p>In the U.S., the Amanti was recognized as the "Most Appealing Premium Midsize Car" by <a href="/wiki/J.D._Power_and_Associates" title="J.D. Power and Associates">J.D. Power and Associates</a>' 2005 Automotive Performance, Execution and Layout Study.</p>
<p>The 2007 Amanti outscored several luxury cars in <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Institute_for_Highway_Safety" title="Insurance Institute for Highway Safety">Insurance Institute for Highway Safety</a> (IIHS) side-impact crash testing, to earn the highest rating of Good.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of December 17, 2010, the Kia website no longer listed the Amanti as a production model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Many critics have noticed the apparent visual similarities to the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_E-Class_(W210)" title="Mercedes-Benz E-Class (W210)">Mercedes-Benz E-Class (W210)</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Successor">Successor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kia_Opirus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Successor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors" title="Kia Motors">Kia Motors</a> replaced the Oprius/Amanti with two vehicles. The <a href="/wiki/Kia_Cadenza" title="Kia Cadenza">Kia Cadenza</a> was designed to fill the <a href="/wiki/Executive_car" title="Executive car">executive car</a> classes, while the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kia_Quoris" title="Kia Quoris">Kia Quoris</a> was designed to fill the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Full-size" title="Full-size">full-size</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxury_car" title="Luxury car">luxury car</a> class.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kia_Cadenza" title="Kia Cadenza">Kia Cadenza</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kia_Cadenza" title="Kia Cadenza">Kia Cadenza</a>, officially unveiled on October 18, 2009, is completely redesigned to reflect Kia's new design style. It is based on the platform of the next-generation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyundai_Azera" title="Hyundai Azera">Hyundai Azera</a> (HG).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Cadenza will replace the Kia Amanti, and features many features found on more expensive luxury cars, such as a heated steering wheel, power extendable seat cushions, automatic windshield defogger, and cooled front seats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Like the Amanti, the Cadenza is only equipped with an <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a>.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kia_K9" title="Kia K9">Kia K9</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kia_K9" title="Kia K9">Kia K9</a> is an upscale full-size premium sedan from <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> automaker Kia. It will replace the Opirus sedan in the Korean market, and will follow in the product line of the Kia K5 and Kia K7. The car is a derivative of the BH-L (VI) platform shared with the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Equus" title="Hyundai Equus">Hyundai Equus</a>. The K9 is slightly shorter than the Equus, but has a more aggressive, sporty stance as well as a longer wheelbase with shorter front overhang dimensions. Kia's signature "Tiger nose" grill has been adopted in the front fascia design.</p>
