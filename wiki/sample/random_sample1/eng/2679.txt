<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Atmospheric temperature</h1>

<p><b>Atmospheric temperature</b> is a measure of <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> at different levels of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth's atmosphere">Earth's atmosphere</a>. It is governed by many factors, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insolation" title="Insolation">incoming solar radiation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Altitude" title="Altitude">altitude</a>. When discussing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surface_temperature" title="Surface temperature">surface temperature</a>, the annual <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_temperature_range" title="Atmospheric temperature range">atmospheric temperature range</a> at any geographical location depends largely upon the type of <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biome</a>, as measured by the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Temperature_versus_height">Temperature versus height</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atmospheric_temperature&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Temperature versus height">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Atmospheric_Temperature_Trend.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="610" height="197" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Atmospheric_Temperature_Trend.jpg/200px-Atmospheric_Temperature_Trend.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Atmospheric_Temperature_Trend.jpg/300px-Atmospheric_Temperature_Trend.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Atmospheric_Temperature_Trend.jpg/400px-Atmospheric_Temperature_Trend.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

These images show temperature trends in two thick layers of the atmosphere as measured by a series of satellite-based instruments between January 1979 and December 2005. The measurements were taken by Microwave Sounding Units and Advanced Microwave Sounding Units flying on a series of National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) weather satellites. The instruments record microwave energy emitted from oxygen molecules in the atmosphere. <div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lapse_rate" title="Lapse rate">lapse rate</a></div>
<p>In the Earth's atmosphere, temperature varies greatly at different heights relative to the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s surface. The coldest temperatures lie near the <a href="/wiki/Mesopause" title="Mesopause">mesopause</a>, an area approximately 85 km (53 mi) to 100 km (62 mi) above the surface. In contrast, some of the warmest temperatures can be found in the <a href="/wiki/Thermosphere" title="Thermosphere">thermosphere</a>, which receives strong <a href="/wiki/Ionizing_radiation" title="Ionizing radiation">ionizing radiation</a> at the level of the <a href="/wiki/Van_Allen_radiation_belt" title="Van Allen radiation belt">Van Allen radiation belt</a>.</p>
<p>Temperature varies as one moves vertically upwards from the Earth's Surface.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_temperature">Global temperature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atmospheric_temperature&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global temperature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Schumann_resonances#Global_temperature" title="Schumann resonances">Schumann resonances § Global temperature</a></div>
<p>The concept of a global temperature is commonly used in <a href="/wiki/Climatology" title="Climatology">climatology</a>, and denotes the average temperature of the Earth based on surface,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> near-surface or <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">tropospheric</a> measurements. These <a href="/wiki/Temperature_record" title="Temperature record">temperature records</a> and measurements are typically acquired using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satellite_temperature_record" title="Satellite temperature record">satellite</a> or <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_temperature_record" title="Instrumental temperature record">ground instrumental</a> temperature <a href="/wiki/Thermometer" title="Thermometer">measurements</a>, then usually compiled using a database or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_model" title="Computer model">computer model</a>. Long-term global temperatures in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paleoclimate" title="Paleoclimate">paleoclimate</a> are discerned using <a href="/wiki/Proxy_(climate)" title="Proxy (climate)">proxy data</a>.</p>
