<p><b>Guillaume Musso</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ɡijɔm myˈso]</a></span>) (born 6 June 1974) is a French novelist.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guillaume_Musso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Musso was born in 1974 in <a href="/wiki/Antibes" title="Antibes">Antibes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alpes-Maritimes" title="Alpes-Maritimes">Alpes-Maritimes</a>), France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After finishing high school, he left for the United States at the age of 19. He spent several months in New York City,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> living with other young foreigners and earning money by selling ice-cream. Then he came back to France, earned a degree in economics, and taught in high schools. His first published novel, <i>Skidamarink</i>, a thriller that opens with the theft of the <i>Mona Lisa</i> from the Musée du Louvre, was published in 2001.</p>
<p>After a car accident he became interested in near-death experiences and imagined a story about a man who returns to life after touching death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This became the novel <i>Afterwards...</i> published in 2004 by XO Editions, which sold more than 1 million copies in France and has been translated into 23 languages. <i><a href="/wiki/Afterwards" title="Afterwards">Afterwards</a></i>, the film directed by <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Bourdos" title="Gilles Bourdos">Gilles Bourdos</a> starring <a href="/wiki/John_Malkovich" title="John Malkovich">John Malkovich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Lilly" title="Evangeline Lilly">Evangeline Lilly</a> was released in France in January 2009 and then internationally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Musso wrote <i>Save Me</i> in 2005, <i>A Mix-up in Heaven</i> in 2006, <i>Lost and Found</i> in 2007, <i>One Day, Perhaps</i> in 2008, <i>Where Would I Be Without You?</i> in 2009,<i>Girl on Paper</i> in 2010 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <i>Call from an Angel</i> in 2011. In 2009, Musso was the #2 bestselling author in France, and according to a 2011 Edistat study he holds third place on the list of authors who have sold the most books in France since 2008, just after Stephenie Meyer and before Harlan Coben. Some 11 million copies of his novels have been sold worldwide and they have been translated into 34 languages.</p>
