<p><br/>
Year <b>157 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Caesar and Orestes</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 597 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 157 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=157_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=157_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=157_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthaginians</a>, prevented by their treaty with Rome from engaging in armed resistance, but equally guaranteed against any loss of territory, appeal to <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Rome</a> against the depredations of King <a href="/wiki/Masinissa" title="Masinissa">Masinissa</a> of <a href="/wiki/Numidia" title="Numidia">Numidia</a>. The Roman <a href="/wiki/Roman_censor" title="Roman censor">censor</a> <a href="/wiki/Cato_the_Elder" title="Cato the Elder">Marcus Porcius Cato</a> heads a commission which arbitrates a truce between Carthage and her former ally, Masinissa.</li>
<li>During his time in Carthage, Cato is so struck by the evidence of Carthaginian prosperity that he is convinced that the security of Rome now depends on the annihilation of Carthage. From this time on, Cato keeps repeating the cry "<a href="/wiki/Carthago_delenda_est" title="Carthago delenda est">Ceterum censeo Carthaginem esse delendam</a>" ("Moreover, I advise that Carthage must be destroyed") at the end of all his speeches, no matter what subject they concern.</li>
<li>After <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ariarathes_V" title="Ariarathes V">Ariarathes V</a> has been deposed from the <a href="/wiki/Cappadocia" title="Cappadocia">Cappadocian</a> throne by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seleucid" title="Seleucid">Seleucid</a> king <a href="/wiki/Demetrius_I_Soter" title="Demetrius I Soter">Demetrius I Soter</a> and has fled to Rome, the new king of Cappadocia, <a href="/wiki/Orophernes_of_Cappadocia" title="Orophernes of Cappadocia">Orophernes</a>, sends two ambassadors to Rome to join the Seleucid emissaries of Demetrius in opposing Ariarathes V's return to power. Despite their efforts, Ariarathes V is restored to his throne by the Romans. However, Rome allows Orophernes to reign jointly with him. The joint government, however, does not last long, as Ariarathes V becomes sole king of Cappadocia shortly afterwards.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jonathan_Maccabeus" title="Jonathan Maccabeus">Jonathan Maccabeus</a> is recognised by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seleucid" title="Seleucid">Seleucids</a> as a minor king within their dominions.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Births">Births</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=157_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Births">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gaius_Marius" title="Gaius Marius">Gaius Marius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman</a> general and politician who will be elected <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">consul</a> seven times; he will also introduce major reforms to the Roman army, authorising recruitment of landless citizens and reorganising the structure of the legions (d. <a href="/wiki/86_BC" title="86 BC">86 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanatruces" title="Sanatruces">Sanatruces</a> (also known as Sinatruces or Sanatruk), King of <a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthia</a> who will rule the Parthian Empire from around <a href="/wiki/77_BC" title="77 BC">77 BC</a> (approximate date) (d. c. <a href="/wiki/70_BC" title="70 BC">70 BC</a>)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deaths">Deaths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=157_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Deaths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
