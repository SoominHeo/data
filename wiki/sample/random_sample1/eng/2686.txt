<p><br/>
Year <b>171 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Crassus and Longinus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 583 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 171 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=171_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=171_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=171_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Epirus" title="Epirus">Epirus</a> joins <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedon" title="Macedon">Macedonia</a> in the latter's fight against <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Rome</a>. However, the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek</a> leagues remain neutral.</li>
<li>Thanks to the efforts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eumenes_II_of_Pergamum" title="Eumenes II of Pergamum">Eumenes II of Pergamum</a> while in Rome, the Romans declare war on Macedonia and send troops to <a href="/wiki/Thessaly" title="Thessaly">Thessaly</a>, thus beginning the <a href="/wiki/Third_Macedonian_War" title="Third Macedonian War">Third Macedonian War</a>. In the resulting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Callicinus" title="Battle of Callicinus">Battle of Callicinus</a> the Macedonians, led by their king, <a href="/wiki/Perseus_of_Macedon" title="Perseus of Macedon">Perseus</a>, are victorious over a Roman force led by <a href="/wiki/Consul" title="Consul">consul</a> <a href="/wiki/Publius_Licinius_Crassus_(consul_171_BC)" title="Publius Licinius Crassus (consul 171 BC)">Publius Licinius Crassus</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=171_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_colony" title="Roman colony">Roman colony</a> outside <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> is founded at <a href="/wiki/San_Roque,_C%C3%A1diz" title="San Roque, Cádiz">Carteia</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Hispania" title="Hispania">Hispania</a> after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iberian_peninsula" title="Iberian peninsula">Iberian</a>-born descendants of Roman soldiers appear before the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Senate" title="Roman Senate">Roman Senate</a> to request a town to live in and are given <a href="/wiki/San_Roque,_C%C3%A1diz" title="San Roque, Cádiz">Carteia</a>, which is named Colonia Libertinorum Carteia.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lucius_Postumius_Albinus_(consul_173_BC)" title="Lucius Postumius Albinus (consul 173 BC)">Lucius Postumius Albinus</a> is sent by Rome as an ambassador to King <a href="/wiki/Masinissa" title="Masinissa">Masinissa</a> of <a href="/wiki/Numidia" title="Numidia">Numidia</a>, and to the <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthaginians</a> in order to raise troops for the war against Perseus of Macedonia.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Parthia">Parthia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=171_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Parthia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mithradates_I" title="Mithradates I">Mithradates I</a> succeeds his brother <a href="/wiki/Phraates_I" title="Phraates I">Phraates I</a> as king of <a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthia</a>.</li>
</ul>
