<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=18th_century_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CodexOfHammurabi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="515" data-file-width="332" height="341" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/CodexOfHammurabi.jpg/220px-CodexOfHammurabi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/CodexOfHammurabi.jpg/330px-CodexOfHammurabi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b9/CodexOfHammurabi.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CodexOfHammurabi.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
An inscription of the <a href="/wiki/Code_of_Hammurabi" title="Code of Hammurabi">Code of Hammurabi</a>, one of the earliest known sets of laws</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><br/></p>
<ul>
<li><b>1800 BC</b>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iron_age" title="Iron age">Iron age</a> in India<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tewari_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tewari-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1800 BC</b>: Beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_Bronze_Age" title="Nordic Bronze Age">Nordic Bronze Age</a> in the period system devised by <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Montelius" title="Oscar Montelius">Oscar Montelius</a>.</li>
<li><b>1800 BC – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1300_BC" title="1300 BC">1300 BC</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Troy" title="Troy">Troy</a> VI flourishes.</li>
<li><b>c. 1800 BC</b>: Sedentary <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Mayan</a> communities in <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerica" title="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</a></li>
<li><b>c. 1800 BC</b>: <a href="/wiki/Hyksos" title="Hyksos">Hyksos</a> start to settle in the <a href="/wiki/Nile_Delta" title="Nile Delta">Nile Delta</a>. They had the capital at <a href="/wiki/Avaris" title="Avaris">Avaris</a> in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Nile_Delta" title="Nile Delta">Nile Delta</a>.</li>
<li>1800 BC Adichanallur urn-burial site in Tirunelveli district in Tamil Nadu. In 2004, a number of skeletons dating from around 3,800 years ago.</li>
<li>1800 BC Indo-Aryan migration</li>
<li><b>c. 1792 BC – 1750 BC</b>: (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_chronology" title="Middle chronology">middle chronology</a>) – <a href="/wiki/Hammurabi" title="Hammurabi">Hammurabi</a> rules <a href="/wiki/Babylonia" title="Babylonia">Babylonia</a> and has to deal with <a href="/wiki/Mari,_Syria" title="Mari, Syria">Mari</a>, which he conquers late in his career.</li>
<li><b>c. 1792 BC – 1750 BC</b>: (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_chronology" title="Middle chronology">middle chronology</a>) – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stela_of_Hammurabi" title="Stela of Hammurabi">Stela of Hammurabi</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Susa" title="Susa">Susa</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Shush,_Iran" title="Shush, Iran">Shush, Iran</a>) is made. It is now in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre" title="Musée du Louvre">Musée du Louvre</a>, Paris.</li>
<li><b>1787 BC – 1784 BC</b>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amorite" title="Amorite">Amorite</a> conquests of <a href="/wiki/Uruk" title="Uruk">Uruk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isin" title="Isin">Isin</a>.</li>
<li><b>1786 BC</b>: <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>: Queen <a href="/wiki/Sobekneferu" title="Sobekneferu">Sobekneferu</a> dies. End of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twelfth_dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Twelfth dynasty of Egypt">Twelfth Dynasty</a>, start of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thirteenth_dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Thirteenth dynasty of Egypt">Thirteenth Dynasty</a>, start of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fourteenth_dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Fourteenth dynasty of Egypt">Fourteenth Dynasty</a>.</li>
<li><b>1779 BC</b>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zimrilim" title="Zimrilim">Zimrilim</a>, the King of <a href="/wiki/Mari,_Syria" title="Mari, Syria">Mari</a>, starts to rule.</li>
<li><b>1770 BC</b>: <a href="/wiki/Babylon" title="Babylon">Babylon</a>, capital of <a href="/wiki/Babylonia" title="Babylonia">Babylonia</a> becomes the largest city of the world, taking the lead from <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Egypt" title="Thebes, Egypt">Thebes</a>, capital of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a>.<a class="external autonumber" href="http://geography.about.com/library/weekly/aa011201a.htm" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></li>
<li><b>1766 BC</b>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shang_Dynasty" title="Shang Dynasty">Shang</a> conquest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xia_Dynasty" title="Xia Dynasty">Xia Dynasty</a>. China.</li>
<li><b>1764 BC – 1750 BC</b>: Wars of <a href="/wiki/Hammurabi" title="Hammurabi">Hammurabi</a>.</li>
<li><b>1757 BC</b>: <a href="/wiki/Mari,_Syria" title="Mari, Syria">Mari</a> sacked by <a href="/wiki/Hammurabi" title="Hammurabi">Hammurabi</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zimrilim" title="Zimrilim">Zimrilim</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_palace_of_Mari" title="Royal palace of Mari">palace</a> is destroyed.</li>
<li><b>1757 BC</b>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zimrilim" title="Zimrilim">Zimrilim</a>, the King of <a href="/wiki/Mari,_Syria" title="Mari, Syria">Mari</a>, dies.</li>
<li><b>1750 BC</b>: <a href="/wiki/Hyksos" title="Hyksos">Hyksos</a> occupation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Egypt" title="Northern Egypt">Northern Egypt</a>.</li>
<li><b>1750 BC</b>: A colossal volcanic eruption at <a href="/wiki/Mount_Veniaminof" title="Mount Veniaminof">Mount Veniaminof</a>, Alaska.</li>
<li><b>c. 1750 BC</b>: <a href="/wiki/Vedic_period" title="Vedic period">Vedic period</a> starts in India.</li>
<li><b>c. 1750 BC</b>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Investiture_of_Zimrilim" title="Investiture of Zimrilim">Investiture of Zimrilim</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zimrilim" title="Zimrilim">Zimrilim</a>, King of <a href="/wiki/Mari,_Syria" title="Mari, Syria">Mari</a>, before the Goddess <a href="/wiki/Ishtar" title="Ishtar">Ishtar</a>), facsimile of a wall painting on mud plaster from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_palace_of_Mari" title="Royal palace of Mari">Zimrilim palace</a> at <a href="/wiki/Mari,_Syria" title="Mari, Syria">Mari</a> (modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tell_Hariri" title="Tell Hariri">Tell Hariri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>), Court 106, is made. It is now in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre" title="Musée du Louvre">Musée du Louvre</a>, Paris.</li>
<li><b>c. 1740–1720 BC</b>: reigns of pharaoh <a href="/wiki/Neferhotep_I" title="Neferhotep I">Neferhotep I</a> and his brother <a href="/wiki/Sobekhotep_IV" title="Sobekhotep IV">Sobekhotep IV</a>, marking the apex of the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egyptian</a> <a href="/wiki/Thirteenth_Dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Thirteenth Dynasty of Egypt">13th Dynasty</a>.</li>
<li><b>1749 BC – 1712 BC</b>: <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamian</a> Rebellions.</li>
<li>Early <a href="/wiki/Unetice_culture" title="Unetice culture">Unetice culture</a>, beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Minoan_civilization" title="Minoan civilization">Minoan civilization</a>: phase II of the Middle period (MM II).</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1700_BC" title="1700 BC">1700 BC</a>: The last species of <a href="/wiki/Mammoth" title="Mammoth">mammoth</a> became extinct on <a href="/wiki/Wrangel_Island" title="Wrangel Island">Wrangel Island</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1700_BC" title="1700 BC">1700 BC</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Indus Valley Civilization</a> comes to an end but is continued by the <a href="/wiki/Cemetery_H_culture" title="Cemetery H culture">Cemetery H culture</a></li>
<li>c. 1700 BC: <a href="/wiki/Minoan_civilization" title="Minoan civilization">Minoan</a> Old Palace period ends and Minoan Second Palace (Neopalatial) period starts in <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a>.</li>
<li>c. 1700 BC: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aegean_civilization" title="Aegean civilization">Aegean</a> metalworkers are producing decorative objects rivaling those of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Near_East" title="Ancient Near East">Ancient Near East</a> jewelers, whose techniques they seem to borrow.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1700_BC" title="1700 BC">1700 BC</a>: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lila-Ir-Tash&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lila-Ir-Tash (page does not exist)">Lila-Ir-Tash</a> started to rule the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elamite_Empire" title="Elamite Empire">Elamite Empire</a>.</li>
<li>c. 1700 BC: <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> starts in China.</li>
<li>c. 1700 BC: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shang_Dynasty" title="Shang Dynasty">Shang Dynasty</a> starts in China.</li>
<li><b>1800 BC – 1700 BC</b>: Decline of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Indus Valley Civilization</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Significant_persons">Significant persons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=18th_century_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Significant persons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hammurabi" title="Hammurabi">Hammurabi</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1792_BC" title="1792 BC">1792 BC</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1750_BC" title="1750 BC">1750 BC</a>), ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Babylonia" title="Babylonia">Babylonian Empire</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tang_of_Shang" title="Tang of Shang">Tang of Shang</a> overthrew emperor <a href="/wiki/Jie_of_Xia" title="Jie of Xia">Jie</a>, last ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Xia_dynasty" title="Xia dynasty">Xia Kingdom</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deaths">Deaths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=18th_century_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Deaths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>1750 BC</b>—<a href="/wiki/Hammurabi" title="Hammurabi">Hammurabi</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_chronology" title="Middle chronology">middle chronology</a>)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inventions.2C_discoveries.2C_introductions">Inventions, discoveries, introductions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=18th_century_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Inventions, discoveries, introductions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>c. 1700 BC</b>—Median date for the building of the <a href="/wiki/Phaistos_Disc#Dating" title="Phaistos Disc">Phaistos Disc</a>. Its purpose and meaning, and even its original geographical place of manufacture remains unknown, making it one of the most famous mysteries of archaeology.</li>
</ul>
