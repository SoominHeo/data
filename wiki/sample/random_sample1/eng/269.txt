<p>The <b>3rd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-Africa_Games" title="All-Africa Games">All-Africa Games</a></b> were played from July 13, 1978, to July 28, 1978, in <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>. 45 countries from 49 independent African countries participated in twelve sports.</p>
<p>At the closing ceremonies the torch was passed to <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>, Kenya to begin preparations for the IVth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-African_Games" title="All-African Games">All-African Games</a> in 1987.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_games">The games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Opening_ceremony">Opening ceremony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Opening ceremony">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The opening ceremony officially began on July 13, 1978. It was organized by <b>Mohamed Zerguini</b>, president of the <a href="/wiki/Algerian_Olympic_Committee" title="Algerian Olympic Committee">Algerian Olympic Committee</a> and member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a>. It was attended by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Morris,_3rd_Baron_Killanin" title="Michael Morris, 3rd Baron Killanin">Lord Killanin</a> (president of the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohamed_Mzali" title="Mohamed Mzali">Mohamed Mzali</a> (vice-president of the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">IOC</a>), <a href="/wiki/Masaji_Kiyokawa" title="Masaji Kiyokawa">Masaji Kiyokawa</a> (Vice Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">IOC</a>), the French Count <a href="/wiki/Jean_de_Beaumont" title="Jean de Beaumont">Jean de Beaumont</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">IOC</a> executif members <a href="/wiki/Juan_Antonio_Samaranch" title="Juan Antonio Samaranch">Juan Antonio Samaranch</a>, Mohamed Benjelloun, Lamine Keita, Mohamed Abel Halim and other members and 45 presidents or representatives of the African National Olympic Committees. And finally <b>Ignaty Novikov</b>, president of the <b>The Organizing Committees of the Olympic Games</b> (OCOGs) accompanied with representatives of Organizing Committees of the <a href="/wiki/1979_Mediterranean_Games" title="1979 Mediterranean Games">1979 Mediterranean Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Split,_Croatia" title="Split, Croatia">Split</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Closing_ceremony">Closing ceremony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Closing ceremony">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the conclusion of the games it was known where the next games would be held as Nairobi had come forth to offer to hold the games in 1987.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Henry_Rono" title="Henry Rono">Henry Rono</a>, the Kenyan star who had already set four world records on the track in 1978 won the 10,000 meters and 3000 meters steeplechase. <a href="/wiki/Filbert_Bayi" title="Filbert Bayi">Filbert Bayi</a> again took the 1500 meters gold medal. The <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algeria national football team</a> wins the gold medal of the games against <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana</a> wins the bronze medal.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1978_All-Africa_Games" title="Athletics at the 1978 All-Africa Games">Athletics at the 1978 All-Africa Games</a></div>
<p>Malian discus thrower <a href="/wiki/Namakoro_Niar%C3%A9" title="Namakoro Niaré">Namakoro Niaré</a> won his third title, being the only athlete to do so. Four athletes, two male and two female, won more than one event:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Kashief_Hassan" title="El Kashief Hassan">El Kashief Hassan</a>, Sudan (200 metres and 400 metres)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Afriyie" title="Hannah Afriyie">Hannah Afriyie</a>, Ghana (100 metres and 200 metres)</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Charlton_Ehizuelen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Charlton Ehizuelen (page does not exist)">Charlton Ehizuelen</a>, Nigeria (long jump and triple jump)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Modupe_Oshikoya" title="Modupe Oshikoya">Modupe Oshikoya</a>, Nigeria (high jump and long jump)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nagui_Asaad" title="Nagui Asaad">Nagui Asaad</a>, Egypt, won his second Gold medal in Shot Put of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Africa_Games" title="All Africa Games">All Africa Games</a> in 1978, <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, after his first in 1973, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Some new events were added: <a href="/wiki/Decathlon" title="Decathlon">Decathlon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pentathlon" title="Pentathlon">pentathlon</a>, for men and women respectively, as well as men's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_walking" title="Race walking">20 km road walk</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concerns_and_controversies">Concerns and controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Concerns and controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Egyptian_incident">Egyptian incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Egyptian incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Egyptian team, which had topped the medals table in the previous two games was called home by its government midway through the games after a brawl marred the football tournament. A fight between Libyans and Egyptians began at the end of the match after a Libyan player struck his opponent. The field became a ring between the players and leaders on both sides. It was reported that Algerian police did stop the violence. Live television broadcast the melee to Egypt. Egypt decided, as a result of these incidents, to withdraw from the competition and the Egyptian Prime minister called the team home immediately.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_meeting_of_Algiers">The meeting of Algiers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_All-Africa_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: The meeting of Algiers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The meeting gave those African nations associated with the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> a chance to address another brewing controversy; their proposed boycott of the <a href="/wiki/1978_Commonwealth_Games" title="1978 Commonwealth Games">1978 Commonwealth Games</a> which were to be held the following month in <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. The discussions were fruitful and the boycott was not carried out.</p>
