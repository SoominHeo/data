<p>Year <b>215 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Albinus/Marcellus/Verrucosus and Gracchus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 539 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 215 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=215_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=215_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Carthage">Carthage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=215_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Carthage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthaginians</a> fail to recapture <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=215_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The Carthaginian general, <a href="/wiki/Hannibal" title="Hannibal">Hannibal</a>, is denied any reinforcements from Spain for his forces now based in Italy by the activities of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman</a> general <a href="/wiki/Publius_Cornelius_Scipio" title="Publius Cornelius Scipio">Publius Cornelius Scipio</a> and his brother <a href="/wiki/Gnaeus_Cornelius_Scipio_Calvus" title="Gnaeus Cornelius Scipio Calvus">Gnaeus Cornelius Scipio Calvus</a>, who, in a battle at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dertosa" title="Dertosa">Dertosa</a> near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebro_River" title="Ebro River">Ebro River</a> effectively stop the Carthaginian general, <a href="/wiki/Hasdrubal_Barca" title="Hasdrubal Barca">Hasdrubal's</a> attempt to break through to Italy.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=215_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman</a> law, <i><a href="/wiki/Lex_Oppia" title="Lex Oppia">Lex Oppia</a></i>, is instituted by Gaius Oppius, a <a href="/wiki/Tribune" title="Tribune">tribune</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Plebs" title="Plebs">plebs</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">consulship</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fabius_Maximus" title="Fabius Maximus">Quintus Fabius Maximus Verrucosus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tiberius_Sempronius_Gracchus_(consul_215_BC)" title="Tiberius Sempronius Gracchus (consul 215 BC)">Tiberius Sempronius Gracchus</a>. The <i>Lex Oppia</i> is the first of a series of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sumptuary" title="Sumptuary">sumptuary</a> laws introduced in Rome. It not only restricts women's wealth, but also their displaying it.</li>
<li>The Roman general, <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Claudius_Marcellus" title="Marcus Claudius Marcellus">Marcus Claudius Marcellus</a>, again repulses an attack by Hannibal on the city of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nola_(215_BC)" title="Battle of Nola (215 BC)">Nola</a>.</li>
<li>Hannibal's forces occupy the cities of <a href="/wiki/Heraclea_Lucania" title="Heraclea Lucania">Heraclea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thurii" title="Thurii">Thurii</a>. However, Hannibal is unable to prevent the Romans from besieging <a href="/wiki/Capua" title="Capua">Capua</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=215_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Philip_V_of_Macedon" title="Philip V of Macedon">Philip V of Macedon</a> and Hannibal negotiate an alliance under which they pledge mutual support and defence. Specifically, they agree to support each other against Rome, and that Hannibal shall have the right to make peace with Rome, but that any peace would include Philip and that Rome would be forced to give up control of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corcyra" title="Corcyra">Corcyra</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollonia,_Illyria" title="Apollonia, Illyria">Apollonia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epidamnus" title="Epidamnus">Epidamnus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hvar" title="Hvar">Pharos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dimale" title="Dimale">Dimale</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_ancient_tribes_in_Illyria" title="List of ancient tribes in Illyria">Parthini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atintanians" title="Atintanians">Atintania</a> and to restore to <a href="/wiki/Demetrius_of_Pharos" title="Demetrius of Pharos">Demetrius of Pharos</a> all his lands currently controlled by Rome.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Seleucid_Empire">Seleucid Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=215_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Seleucid Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seleucid" title="Seleucid">Seleucid</a> king, <a href="/wiki/Antiochus_III_the_Great" title="Antiochus III the Great">Antiochus III</a>, crosses the <a href="/wiki/Taurus_Mountains" title="Taurus Mountains">Taurus</a>, uniting his forces with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attalus_I_Soter" title="Attalus I Soter">Attalus</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pergamum" title="Pergamum">Pergamum</a> and, in one campaign, deprives his rebel general, <a href="/wiki/Achaeus_(general)" title="Achaeus (general)">Achaeus</a>, of all his dominions and takes <a href="/wiki/Sardis" title="Sardis">Sardis</a> (with the exception of the citadel).</li>
</ul>
