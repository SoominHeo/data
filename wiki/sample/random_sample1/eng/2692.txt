<p><br/>
Year <b>22 BC</b> was either a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Common year starting on Sunday">common year starting on Sunday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">Monday</a> or <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">Tuesday</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Leap year starting on Sunday">leap year starting on Sunday</a> or <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Leap year starting on Saturday">Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a> (the sources differ, see <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar#Leap_year_error" title="Julian calendar">leap year error</a> for further information) and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Proleptic_Julian_calendar" title="Proleptic Julian calendar">Proleptic Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Marcellus and Arruntius</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 732 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 22 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=22_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=22_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lucius_Arruntius_the_Elder" title="Lucius Arruntius the Elder">Lucius Arruntius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Claudius_Marcellus_Aeserninus" title="Marcus Claudius Marcellus Aeserninus">Marcus Claudius Marcellus Aeserninus</a> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Early_Imperial_Roman_Consuls" title="List of Early Imperial Roman Consuls">Roman Consuls</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aemilius_Lepidus_Paullus" title="Aemilius Lepidus Paullus">Aemilius Lepidus Paullus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Munatius_Plancus" title="Lucius Munatius Plancus">Lucius Munatius Plancus</a> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_censors" title="List of censors">Censors</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> governor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aegyptus_(province)" title="Aegyptus (province)">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Petronius" title="Gaius Petronius">Gaius Petronius</a>, marches the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> with <a href="/wiki/Roman_legion" title="Roman legion">legions</a> <a href="/wiki/Legio_XXII_Deiotariana" title="Legio XXII Deiotariana">XXII <i>Deiotariana</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Legio_III_Cyrenaica" title="Legio III Cyrenaica">III <i>Cyrenaica</i></a>, and destroys the <a href="/wiki/Nubia" title="Nubia">Nubian</a> capital of <a href="/wiki/Napata" title="Napata">Napata</a>.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Artaxias_II" title="Artaxias II">Artaxias II</a> returns with support of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parthians" title="Parthians">Parthians</a> to <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and claims the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a>. <a href="/wiki/Artavasdes_I_of_Media_Atropatene" title="Artavasdes I of Media Atropatene">Artavasdes I</a> escapes to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Caesar Augustus</a> receives him.</li>
</ul>
