<p>Year <b>246 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Crassus and Licinus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 508 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 246 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=246_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Egypt">Egypt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=246_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Egypt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt's</a> <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_II_Philadelphus" title="Ptolemy II Philadelphus">Ptolemy II</a> dies and is succeeded by his son, <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_III_Euergetes" title="Ptolemy III Euergetes">Ptolemy III</a>. At the time of Ptolemy II's death, Egypt comprises the ancient kingdom of Egypt in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nile_Valley" title="Nile Valley">Nile Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyrene,_Libya" title="Cyrene, Libya">Cyrene</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judea" title="Judea">Judea</a> and the coast of southern <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> and a number of cities on the shores and islands of the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedon" title="Macedon">Macedonian</a> Ptolemies maintain their authority over their territories with a small <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">mercenary</a> army made up of Macedonians and Greeks.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=246_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The Ashokan pillar in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lauriya" title="Lauriya">Lauriya</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nandangarh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nandangarh (page does not exist)">Nandangarh</a> (from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maurya_period" title="Maurya period">Maurya period</a>) is made.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Seleucid_Empire">Seleucid Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=246_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Seleucid Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antiochus_II_Theos" title="Antiochus II Theos">Antiochus II</a> leaves <a href="/wiki/Berenice_(Seleucid_queen)" title="Berenice (Seleucid queen)">Berenice</a> in order to live again with his former wife <a href="/wiki/Laodice_I" title="Laodice I">Laodice</a> and his son <a href="/wiki/Seleucus_II_Callinicus" title="Seleucus II Callinicus">Seleucus</a>. However, Laodice poisons him and proclaims her son as King Seleucus II Callinicus, while her supporters in <a href="/wiki/Antioch" title="Antioch">Antioch</a> kill Berenice and her children who have taken refuge at Daphne, near Antioch, in <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>.</li>
<li>Berenice's brother, <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_III_Euergetes" title="Ptolemy III Euergetes">Ptolemy III</a>, sets about to avenge his sister's murder by invading Syria which begins the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Syrian_War" title="Third Syrian War">Third Syrian War</a> (also known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laodicean_War" title="Laodicean War">Laodicean War</a>). Ptolemy III's navy, perhaps with the aid of rebels in the cities, advances against Seleucus II's forces as far as <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a>, across the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hellespont" title="Hellespont">Hellespont</a>, and also captures some islands off the <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolian</a> coast.</li>
<li>Ptolemy III wins major victories over Seleucus II in Syria and Anatolia and briefly occupies Antioch. These victories are marred by the loss of the <a href="/wiki/Cyclades" title="Cyclades">Cyclades</a> to <a href="/wiki/Antigonus_II_Gonatas" title="Antigonus II Gonatas">Antigonus II Gonatas</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Andros_(246_BC)" title="Battle of Andros (246 BC)">Battle of Andros</a>.</li>
<li>Seleucus II Callinicus' mother, Laodice attempts to take control over the Seleucid Empire by insisting that Seleucus II make his younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Antiochus_Hierax" title="Antiochus Hierax">Antiochus Hierax</a>, co-regent and give him all the Seleucid territory in <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a>. Antiochus promptly declares independence and begins fighting a war with his brother.</li>
<li>In order to secure the <a href="/wiki/Bactria" title="Bactria">Bactrian</a> King <a href="/wiki/Diodotus_I" title="Diodotus I">Diodotus'</a> friendship, Seleucus II Callinicus arranges the marriage of one of his sisters to King Diodotus.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=246_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>With <a href="/wiki/Hamilcar_Barca" title="Hamilcar Barca">Hamilcar Barca</a> wearing the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Romans</a> down in <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, the Romans, by private subscription, build another fleet with the aim of regaining command of the sea.</li>
<li>In Rome, the number of <a href="/wiki/Praetor" title="Praetor">praetors</a> is increased from one to two. The second praetor is appointed to relieve the backlog of judicial business and to give the Republic a magistrate with Imperium who can field an army in an emergency when both consuls are away fighting a war.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=246_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>An irrigation canal approximately one hundred miles long is built across the current-day province of <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, greatly adding to the agricultural productivity of the area and to the military potency of the <a href="/wiki/Qin_dynasty" title="Qin dynasty">Qin dynasty</a>.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arsinoe_III_of_Egypt" title="Arsinoe III of Egypt">Arsinoe III</a>, queen of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a> from <a href="/wiki/220_BC" title="220 BC">220 BC</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_III_Euergetes" title="Ptolemy III Euergetes">Ptolemy III</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berenice_II_of_Egypt" title="Berenice II of Egypt">Berenice II</a> (d. <a href="/wiki/204_BC" title="204 BC">204 BC</a>)</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deaths">Deaths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=246_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Deaths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_II_Philadelphus" title="Ptolemy II Philadelphus">Ptolemy II Philadelphus</a>, king of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a> from 285 BC, second king of the Ptolemaic dynasty, who has extended his power by skilful diplomacy, developed agriculture and commerce, and made <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> a leading centre of the arts and sciences (b. <a href="/wiki/308_BC" title="308 BC">308 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antiochus_II_Theos" title="Antiochus II Theos">Antiochus II Theos</a>, king of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seleucid" title="Seleucid">Seleucid</a> dominions in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> from <a href="/wiki/261_BC" title="261 BC">261 BC</a>. He has spent much of his reign at war with <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a>, recovering much of the territory in <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a> lost in earlier wars between the Ptolemaic and Seleucid dynasties (b. c. <a href="/wiki/287_BC" title="287 BC">287 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Berenice_(Seleucid_queen)" title="Berenice (Seleucid queen)">Berenice</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_II_Philadelphus" title="Ptolemy II Philadelphus">Ptolemy II Philadelphus</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arsinoe_I_of_Egypt" title="Arsinoe I of Egypt">Arsinoe</a>, wife of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seleucid" title="Seleucid">Seleucid</a> ruler <a href="/wiki/Antiochus_II_Theos" title="Antiochus II Theos">Antiochus II Theos</a>, supplanting his first wife, <a href="/wiki/Laodice_I" title="Laodice I">Laodice</a>, whose children she has persuaded him to bar from the succession to the throne in favour of her own</li>
</ul>
