<p>Year <b>286 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Corvus (or Potitus) and Paetus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 468 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 286 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=286_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=286_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=286_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>As <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demetrius_Poliorcetes" title="Demetrius Poliorcetes">Demetrius Poliorcetes</a> and his army are chased across <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Taurus_Mountains" title="Taurus Mountains">Taurus Mountains</a> by the armies of <a href="/wiki/Lysimachus" title="Lysimachus">Lysimachus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seleucus_I_Nicator" title="Seleucus I Nicator">Seleucus</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greece</a> his son <a href="/wiki/Antigonus_II_Gonatas" title="Antigonus II Gonatas">Antigonus</a> meets with success. <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_I_Soter" title="Ptolemy I Soter">Ptolemy's</a> fleet is driven off and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Athens" title="Ancient Athens">Athens</a> surrenders to Antigonus.</li>
<li>After allowing <a href="/wiki/Pyrrhus_of_Epirus" title="Pyrrhus of Epirus">Pyrrhus of Epirus</a> to remain in possession of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedon" title="Macedon">Macedonia</a> with the title of king, he is expelled by <a href="/wiki/Lysimachus" title="Lysimachus">Lysimachus</a> who declares himself its king in the place of Pyrrhus.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=286_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The new law, <a href="/wiki/Lex_Aquilia" title="Lex Aquilia">Lex Aquilia</a>, is enacted. This is a Roman law which provides compensation to the owners of property injured as a result of someone's fault.</li>
</ul>
