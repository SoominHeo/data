<p>Year <b>316 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Rutilus and Laenas</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 438 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 316 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Macedonian_Empire">Macedonian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Macedonian Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eumenes" title="Eumenes">Eumenes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antigonus_I_Monophthalmus" title="Antigonus I Monophthalmus">Antigonus</a>, rivals to Cassander for control of Macedonia, meet in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gabiene" title="Battle of Gabiene">Battle of Gabiene</a> in <a href="/wiki/Medes" title="Medes">Media</a> to the northeast of <a href="/wiki/Susa" title="Susa">Susa</a>. Antigonus defeats Eumenes, with the aid of <a href="/wiki/Seleucus_I_Nicator" title="Seleucus I Nicator">Seleucus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peithon" title="Peithon">Peithon</a> (the satraps of <a href="/wiki/Babylonia" title="Babylonia">Babylonia</a> and Media, respectively). The result is inconclusive. However, some of Eumenes' soldiers take matters into their own hands. Learning that Antigonus has captured many of their wives, children and the cumulative plunder of nearly 40 years of continuous warfare, they secretly open negotiations with Antigonus for their safe return. They hand over Eumenes and his senior officers to Antigonus in return for their baggage and families. Eumenes is put to death by Antigonus after a week's captivity.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cassander" title="Cassander">Cassander</a> returns from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peloponnesus" title="Peloponnesus">Peloponnesus</a> and defeats <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedon" title="Macedon">Macedonia</a>'s regent <a href="/wiki/Polyperchon" title="Polyperchon">Polyperchon</a> in battle. Cassander blockades <a href="/wiki/Olympias" title="Olympias">Olympias</a>, mother of the late <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Pydna" title="Pydna">Pydna</a>, where she surrenders. Cassander takes <a href="/wiki/Roxana" title="Roxana">Roxana</a> and his son <a href="/wiki/Alexander_IV_of_Macedon" title="Alexander IV of Macedon">Alexander IV of Macedon</a> into his custody.</li>
<li>Olympias is condemned to death by Cassander, but his soldiers refuse to carry out the sentence. She is eventually killed by relatives of those she has previously had executed.</li>
<li>Cassander marries <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thessalonica_of_Macedon" title="Thessalonica of Macedon">Thessaloniki</a>, half sister of Alexander. He has Alexander's widow, <a href="/wiki/Roxana" title="Roxana">Roxana</a> and son, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_IV_of_Macedon" title="Alexander IV of Macedon">Alexander IV of Macedon</a>, imprisoned at Amphipolis in Thrace. They are never to be seen alive again.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Greece" title="Thebes, Greece">Thebes</a>, which has been destroyed by Alexander the Great, begins to get rebuilt by Cassander with the help of the citizens of <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sicily">Sicily</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sicily">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Agathocles" title="Agathocles">Agathocles</a>, the new <a href="/wiki/Tyrant" title="Tyrant">tyrant</a> of <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_Sicily" title="Syracuse, Sicily">Syracuse</a>, extends his rule over the eastern part of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Romans</a>, with an eye to capturing <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">Apulia</a>, send an army (led by <a href="/wiki/Roman_dictator" title="Roman dictator">dictator</a> <a href="/wiki/Quintus_Fabius_Maximus_Rullianus" title="Quintus Fabius Maximus Rullianus">Quintus Fabius Maximus Rullianus</a>) to seize the town of <a href="/wiki/Lucera" title="Lucera">Lucera</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Samnites" title="Samnites">Samnites</a>. They are badly beaten in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lautulae" title="Battle of Lautulae">Battle of Lautulae</a> and the Samnites go on to reach within 32 kilometres of Rome.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/King_Huiwen_of_Qin" title="King Huiwen of Qin">King Hui</a> of <a href="/wiki/Qin_(state)" title="Qin (state)">Qin</a> decides on the advice of General Sima Cuo to invade and annex the ancient states of <a href="/wiki/Ba_(state)" title="Ba (state)">Ba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shu_(state)" title="Shu (state)">Shu</a> in Sichuan to increase Qin's agricultural output and obtain a strategic platform to defeat the state of <a href="/wiki/Chu_(state)" title="Chu (state)">Chu</a>.</li>
</ul>

</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deaths">Deaths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=316_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Deaths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><a href="/wiki/Eumenes" title="Eumenes">Eumenes</a>, Greek general and <a href="/wiki/Diadochi" title="Diadochi">diadochi</a> (b. c. (<a href="/wiki/362_BC" title="362 BC">362 BC</a>)</li>
