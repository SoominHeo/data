<p><br/>
Year <b>355 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Peticus and Poplicola</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 399 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 355 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=355_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=355_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Artaxerxes_III" title="Artaxerxes III">Artaxerxes III</a> of <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Persia</a> forces <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> to conclude a peace which requires the city to leave <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a> and to acknowledge the independence of its rebellious allies.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Archidamus_III" title="Archidamus III">Archidamus III</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Sparta</a> supports the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phocians" title="Phocians">Phocians</a> against <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Greece" title="Thebes, Greece">Thebes</a> in the "Sacred War".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chares_of_Athens" title="Chares of Athens">Chares</a>' war party in Athens is replaced by one under <a href="/wiki/Eubulus_(statesman)" title="Eubulus (statesman)">Eubulus</a> which favours peace. Eubulus restores the economic position of Athens without increasing the burden of taxation and improves the Athenian fleet while its docks and fortifications are repaired.</li>
</ul>
