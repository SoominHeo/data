<p><br/>
Year <b>385 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Tribunate of Capitolinus, Cornelius, Capitolinus, Papirius, Capitolinus and Fidenas</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 369 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 385 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
