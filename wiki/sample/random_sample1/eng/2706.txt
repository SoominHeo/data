<p>Year <b>43 BC</b> was either a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Common year starting on Sunday">common year starting on Sunday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">Monday</a> or <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">Tuesday</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Leap year starting on Sunday">leap year starting on Sunday</a> or <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Leap year starting on Monday">Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a> (the sources differ, see <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar#Leap_year_error" title="Julian calendar">leap year error</a> for further information) and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Proleptic_Julian_calendar" title="Proleptic Julian calendar">Proleptic Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Pansa and Hirtius</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 711 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 43 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=43_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=43_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consuls" title="Consuls">Consuls</a>: <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Vibius_Pansa_Caetronianus" title="Gaius Vibius Pansa Caetronianus">Gaius Vibius Pansa Caetronianus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aulus_Hirtius" title="Aulus Hirtius">Aulus Hirtius</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Senate" title="Roman Senate">Roman Senate</a> confirms Octavian as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Propraetor" title="Propraetor">propraetor</a> with joint responsibility for the campaign against Antony. Hirtius and Octavian mobilize <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Troops" title="Troops">troops</a> for the march to <a href="/wiki/Modena" title="Modena">Mutina</a>, while Pansa continues the <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">levy</a>. Embassy dispatched to treat with Antony.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaius_Antonius" title="Gaius Antonius">Gaius Antonius</a> is defeated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marcus_Junius_Brutus" title="Marcus Junius Brutus">Marcus Junius Brutus</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dyrrachium" title="Dyrrachium">Dyrrachium</a>. Brutus proceeds to secure his position in <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(Roman_province)" title="Macedonia (Roman province)">Macedonia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Cassius_Longinus" title="Gaius Cassius Longinus">Gaius Cassius Longinus</a> campaigns in <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> and defeats the army of <a href="/wiki/Publius_Cornelius_Dolabella" title="Publius Cornelius Dolabella">Publius Cornelius Dolabella</a> at <a href="/wiki/Latakia" title="Latakia">Laodicea</a>.</li>
<li>March – Vibius Pansa set out to link up with Hirtius and Octavian, bringing four <a href="/wiki/Roman_legion" title="Roman legion">legions</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Army_recruit" title="Army recruit">recruits</a>, having left one, the <i>legio urbana</i>, to defend <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_14" title="April 14">April 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Forum_Gallorum" title="Battle of Forum Gallorum">Battle of Forum Gallorum</a>: <a href="/wiki/Mark_Antony" title="Mark Antony">Mark Antony</a>, besieging <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Caesar</a>'s assassin <a href="/wiki/Decimus_Junius_Brutus_Albinus" title="Decimus Junius Brutus Albinus">Decimus Brutus</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutina" title="Mutina">Mutina</a>, defeats the forces of the consul <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Vibius_Pansa_Caetronianus" title="Gaius Vibius Pansa Caetronianus">Pansa</a>, but is then immediately defeated by the army of the other consul, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hirtius" title="Hirtius">Hirtius</a>. Both consuls are killed (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hirtius" title="Hirtius">Hirtius</a> does not die until after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mutina" title="Battle of Mutina">Battle of Mutina</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_21" title="April 21">April 21</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cicero" title="Cicero">Cicero</a>'s 14th and last <i><a href="/wiki/Philippic" title="Philippic">Philippic</a></i>.</li>
<li>Antony is again defeated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mutina" title="Battle of Mutina">Battle of Mutina</a> by a coalition of <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Octavian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Decimus_Junius_Brutus_Albinus" title="Decimus Junius Brutus Albinus">Decimus Brutus</a>, and the two consuls of the year.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Antony marches to <a href="/wiki/Parma" title="Parma">Parma</a> (which is sacked) and <a href="/wiki/Piacenza" title="Piacenza">Placentia</a>. He then crosses the <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_Alps" title="Ligurian Alps">Ligurian Alps</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vada_Sabatia" title="Vada Sabatia">Vada Sabatia</a>, 50 km (31 mi) south-west of <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a>, and joints with <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Aemilius_Lepidus_(triumvir)" title="Marcus Aemilius Lepidus (triumvir)">Aemilius Lepidus</a>, soon after Decimus Brutus is killed by <a href="/wiki/Outlaw" title="Outlaw">brigands</a>. The Senate declares Antony a <a href="/wiki/Hostis_humani_generis" title="Hostis humani generis">hostis</a>, an enemy of the state. <a href="/wiki/Sextus_Pompey" title="Sextus Pompey">Sextus Pompey</a> becomes supreme commander of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_navy" title="Roman navy">Roman navy</a> and Gaius Cassius <a href="/wiki/Proconsul" title="Proconsul">proconsul</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syria_(Roman_province)" title="Syria (Roman province)">Syria</a>.</li>
<li>Summer – Gaius Cassius captures <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">Rhodes</a> after refusing paying <a href="/wiki/Tribute" title="Tribute">tribute</a>. Their fleet is defeated by <a href="/wiki/Galley#Roman_Imperial_era" title="Galley">Roman galleys</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a>. He lands a military force on the island and plunders the city. Cassius puts to death 50 of the leading citizens and seizes all the <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> he could lay hands on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>July–August – Antony is again at the head of a large army; Octavian enters <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> in force without opposition. It is clear that Cicero's plan to divide them against each other has failed.</li>
<li>August 19th – <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Gaius Octavian</a> taking office as <a href="/wiki/Consul" title="Consul">consul</a>, the day before his 20th birthday, is prevailed to pass the <i>lex Pedia</i>, a law establishing the murder of Caesar as a capital crime.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_26" title="November 26">November 26</a> – Octavian meets Antony and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Aemilius_Lepidus_(triumvir)" title="Marcus Aemilius Lepidus (triumvir)">Lepidus</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bononia</a> and the three enter into an official five-year autocratic pact, the <a href="/wiki/Second_Triumvirate" title="Second Triumvirate">Second Triumvirate</a> (see <i><a href="/wiki/Lex_Titia" title="Lex Titia">lex Titia</a></i>). To cement their reconciliation Octavian agrees to marry <a href="/wiki/Clodia_Pulchra" title="Clodia Pulchra">Clodia</a>, a daughter of Antony's wife <a href="/wiki/Fulvia" title="Fulvia">Fulvia</a> by her former husband <a href="/wiki/Publius_Clodius_Pulcher" title="Publius Clodius Pulcher">Publius Clodius Pulcher</a>.</li>
<li>November – The triumvirs introduce <a href="/wiki/Proscription" title="Proscription">proscriptions</a> in which allegedly 130 senators and 2,000 <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Equestrian_order" title="Equestrian order">equites</a></i> are branded as <a href="/wiki/Outlaw" title="Outlaw">outlaws</a> and deprived of their property.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_7" title="December 7">December 7</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marcus_Tullius_Cicero" title="Marcus Tullius Cicero">Marcus Tullius Cicero</a> is killed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formiae" title="Formiae">Formiae</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Litter_(vehicle)" title="Litter (vehicle)">litter</a> going to the seaside, by a party led by Herennius (a <a href="/wiki/Centurion" title="Centurion">centurion</a>) and Popilius (a military <a href="/wiki/Tribune" title="Tribune">tribune</a>). His head and hands are displayed on the <a href="/wiki/Rostra" title="Rostra">Rostra</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forum_Romanum" title="Forum Romanum">Forum Romanum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deaths">Deaths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=43_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Deaths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>June – <a href="/wiki/Porcia_Catonis" title="Porcia Catonis">Porcia Catonis</a>, wife of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marcus_Junius_Brutus" title="Marcus Junius Brutus">Marcus Junius Brutus</a> (b. <a href="/wiki/70_BC" title="70 BC">70 BC</a>) (alternative date is July)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_7" title="December 7">December 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Cicero" title="Cicero">Cicero</a>, Roman politician and author (murdered) (b. <a href="/wiki/106_BC" title="106 BC">106 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atia_Balba_Caesonia" title="Atia Balba Caesonia">Atia Balba Caesonia</a>, niece of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a> and mother of <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a> (b. <a href="/wiki/85_BC" title="85 BC">85 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Decimus_Junius_Brutus_Albinus" title="Decimus Junius Brutus Albinus">Decimus Brutus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman</a> statesman (murdered) (b. c. <a href="/wiki/85_BC" title="85 BC">85 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Publius_Cornelius_Dolabella" title="Publius Cornelius Dolabella">Publius Cornelius Dolabella</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suffect" title="Suffect">suffect</a> <a href="/wiki/Consul" title="Consul">consul</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Assassination" title="Assassination">assassination</a> of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a> (b. <a href="/wiki/70_BC" title="70 BC">70 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hirtius" title="Hirtius">Hirtius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman</a> statesman (killed in battle) (b. c. <a href="/wiki/90_BC" title="90 BC">90 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Quintus_Tullius_Cicero" title="Quintus Tullius Cicero">Quintus Tullius Cicero</a> proscribed (b. <a href="/wiki/102_BC" title="102 BC">102 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gaius_Vibius_Pansa_Caetronianus" title="Gaius Vibius Pansa Caetronianus">Pansa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman</a> statesman (killed in battle)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Trebonius" title="Trebonius">Trebonius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assassination" title="Assassination">assassin</a> of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a> (murdered by <a href="/wiki/Publius_Cornelius_Dolabella" title="Publius Cornelius Dolabella">Publius Cornelius Dolabella</a>) (b. c. <a href="/wiki/92_BC" title="92 BC">92 BC</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Verres" title="Verres">Verres</a>, corrupt <a href="/wiki/Praetor" title="Praetor">praetor</a> (b. c. <a href="/wiki/120_BC" title="120 BC">120 BC</a>)</li>
