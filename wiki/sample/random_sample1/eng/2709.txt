<p>Year <b>460 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Poplicola and Sabinus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 294 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 460 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Persian_Empire">Persian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Persian Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a> revolts against <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Persian</a> rule. The Egyptian leader, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inaros" title="Inaros">Inaros</a>, asks <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> for assistance, which is willingly provided as Athens has plans to trade with and colonise Egypt. A force of 200 Athenian <a href="/wiki/Trireme" title="Trireme">triremes</a>, which is campaigning in Cyprus, is immediately ordered to Egypt to render assistance.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Achaemenes_(satrap)" title="Achaemenes (satrap)">Achaemenes</a>, Persian <a href="/wiki/Satrap" title="Satrap">satrap</a> (governor) of Egypt, is defeated and slain in a battle at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Papremis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Papremis (page does not exist)">Papremis</a>, on the banks of the Nile River, by Egyptian forces.</li>
<li>The construction of the ceremonial complex of <a href="/wiki/Apadana" title="Apadana">Apadana</a> (the audience hall of <a href="/wiki/Darius_I" title="Darius I">Darius I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xerxes_I" title="Xerxes I">Xerxes I</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Persepolis" title="Persepolis">Persepolis</a> is completed.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/First_Peloponnesian_War" title="First Peloponnesian War">First Peloponnesian War</a> breaks out between the <a href="/wiki/Delian_League" title="Delian League">Delian League</a> (led by <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>) and a Peloponnesian alliance (led by <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Sparta</a>), caused in part by Athens' alliance with <a href="/wiki/Megara" title="Megara">Megara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argos" title="Argos">Argos</a> and the subsequent reaction of Sparta. The Athenians have built long walls for the Megarans to their port at <a href="/wiki/Nisaea" title="Nisaea">Nisaea</a>, thereby earning the enmity of Megara's old rival <a href="/wiki/Corinth" title="Corinth">Corinth</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Argos" title="Argos">Argos</a> rises against <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Sparta</a>. <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> supports Argos and <a href="/wiki/Thessaly" title="Thessaly">Thessaly</a>. The small force that is sent by Sparta to quell the uprising in Argos is defeated by a joint Athenian and Argos force at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oenoe,_Attica" title="Oenoe, Attica">Oenoe</a>. (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Oenoe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Battle of Oenoe (page does not exist)">Battle of Oenoe</a>)</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Conflicts arise between the Roman <a href="/wiki/Patrician_(ancient_Rome)" title="Patrician (ancient Rome)">patricians</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plebeians" title="Plebeians">plebeians</a>. There is also a revolt by <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>'s slaves. During the revolt, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campidoglio" title="Campidoglio">Campidoglio</a> is held by the slaves for a lengthy period, along with the most important temples of Rome. It is during this revolt that <a href="/wiki/Consul" title="Consul">consul</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Publius_Valerius_Publicola_(460_BC)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Publius Valerius Publicola (460 BC) (page does not exist)">Publius Valerius Publicola</a> dies. The revolt only ends with the arrival of an army from <a href="/wiki/Tusculum" title="Tusculum">Tusculum</a>, led by Tusculan <a href="/wiki/Roman_dictator" title="Roman dictator">dictator</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lucius_Mamilius&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lucius Mamilius (page does not exist)">Lucius Mamilius</a>. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Quinctius_Cincinnatus" title="Lucius Quinctius Cincinnatus">Lucius Quinctius Cincinnatus</a> is appointed as consul to replace Publicola.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Siculi">Siculi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Siculi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ducetius" title="Ducetius">Ducetius</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hellenised" title="Hellenised">Hellenised</a> leader of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siculi" title="Siculi">Siculi</a>, an ancient people of <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, takes advantage of the confusion that follows the collapse of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyranny" title="Tyranny">tyranny</a> in <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_Sicily" title="Syracuse, Sicily">Syracuse</a> and other Sicilian states. With the support of the Syracusan democracy, he drives out the colonists of the former tyrant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hiero_I" title="Hiero I">Hieron</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catana" title="Catana">Catana</a> and restores it to its original inhabitants.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts">Arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=460_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Arts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polygnotos" title="Polygnotos">Polygnotos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Thasos" title="Thasos">Thasos</a> decorates the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Painted_Stoa" title="Painted Stoa">Painted Stoa</a>, on the north side of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Agora_of_Athens" title="Ancient Agora of Athens">Ancient Agora of Athens</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>The construction of a sculpture of a <i>Young Warrior</i> (subsequently found in the sea off <a href="/wiki/Riace" title="Riace">Riace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>) is begun and completed <a href="/wiki/450_BC" title="450 BC">approximately ten years later</a>. It is now preserved at the <a href="/wiki/Museo_Nazionale_della_Magna_Grecia" title="Museo Nazionale della Magna Grecia">Museo Archeologico Nazionale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reggio_Calabria" title="Reggio Calabria">Reggio Calabria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</li>
<li>The sculpture <i>Apollo with battling Lapiths and centaurs</i> is built on the west pediment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temple_of_Zeus" title="Temple of Zeus">Temple of Zeus</a> in <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Greece" title="Olympia, Greece">Olympia</a> (approximate date). Only fragments remain and are today preserved at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeological_museum" title="Archaeological museum">Archaeological museum</a> in Olympia.</li>
<li>A metope relief of <i>Athena, Heracles and Atlas</i> are made on a frieze in the Temple of Zeus in Olympia (approximate date). It is now preserved at the Archaeological museum in Olympia.</li>
<li>A statue of <a href="/wiki/Apollo" title="Apollo">Apollo</a> is cast (approximate date), of which today remains one leg, preserved at the <a href="/wiki/Louvre" title="Louvre">Louvre</a>, and the head, known as the <a href="/wiki/Chatsworth_Head" title="Chatsworth Head">Chatsworth Head</a>, preserved at the <a href="/wiki/British_Museum" title="British Museum">British Museum</a>.</li>
</ul>
