<p>The <b>1980 Winter Olympics</b>, officially known as the <b>XIII Olympic Winter Games</b> (French: Les <i>XIIIes Jeux olympiques d'hiver</i>), was a <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> which was celebrated from February 13, through February 24, 1980, in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Placid,_New_York" title="Lake Placid, New York">Lake Placid</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> of America. This was the second time the <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">Upstate New York</a> village hosted the Games, after <a href="/wiki/1932_Winter_Olympics" title="1932 Winter Olympics">1932</a>. The only other candidate city to bid for the Games was <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>-<a href="/wiki/Garibaldi,_British_Columbia" title="Garibaldi, British Columbia">Garibaldi</a>, British Columbia, Canada, which withdrew before the final vote.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_Olympic_mascots" title="List of Olympic mascots">mascot</a> of the Games was "<a href="/wiki/Roni_(mascot)" title="Roni (mascot)">Roni</a>", a <a href="/wiki/Raccoon" title="Raccoon">raccoon</a>. The mask-like rings on a raccoon's face recall the <a href="/wiki/Goggles" title="Goggles">goggles</a> and hats worn by many athletes in winter sports.</p>
<p>The sports were played at the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Center" title="Olympic Center">Olympic Center</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whiteface_Mountain" title="Whiteface Mountain">Whiteface Mountain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mt._Van_Hoevenberg_Olympic_Bobsled_Run" title="Mt. Van Hoevenberg Olympic Bobsled Run">Mt. Van Hoevenberg Olympic Bobsled Run</a>, the Olympic Ski Jumps, the Cascade Cross Country Ski Center, and the Lake Placid High School Speed Skating Oval.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Host_selection">Host selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Host selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The selection process for the 1980 Winter Olympics consisted of one bid, from <a href="/wiki/Lake_Placid,_New_York" title="Lake Placid, New York">Lake Placid</a>, United States. It was selected at the 75th IOC Session in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> on October 13, 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-votes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-votes-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Notable highlights included:</p>
<ul>
<li>The United States men's <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_1980_Winter_Olympics" title="Ice hockey at the 1980 Winter Olympics">ice hockey</a> team, composed mostly of collegiate players and not predicted to advance beyond group play, won the gold medal, defeating the heavily favored <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Soviet Union national ice hockey team">Soviet</a> team and <a href="/wiki/Finland_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Finland men's national ice hockey team">Finland</a> in the medal round. The United States team's 4–3 win over the Soviet team, which came into the 1980 Games having won 4 consecutive Olympic gold medals, became known as the "<a href="/wiki/Miracle_on_Ice" title="Miracle on Ice">Miracle on Ice</a>" in the US press. The win captured the hearts of Americans during a time of Cold War tensions, even though it was the win against <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> that captured the gold medal.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eric_Heiden" title="Eric Heiden">Eric Heiden</a> of the United States won gold in the 500m, 1000m, 1500m, 5000m, and 10,000m <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating_at_the_1980_Winter_Olympics" title="Speed skating at the 1980 Winter Olympics">speedskating</a> events, setting 4 Olympic records and 1 world record (10,000m) in the process and delivering 83% of the American gold medals (the only other gold, as noted above, was won by the hockey team). Heiden became the first to ever win all five speed skating events and is still the <a href="/wiki/List_of_multiple_Olympic_medalists_at_a_single_Games" title="List of multiple Olympic medalists at a single Games">only athlete to win 5 gold medals at one Winter Games</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ingemar_Stenmark" title="Ingemar Stenmark">Ingemar Stenmark</a> won both the giant slalom and the slalom.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hanni_Wenzel" title="Hanni Wenzel">Hanni Wenzel</a> won the women's giant slalom and slalom, making <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">Liechtenstein</a> the smallest country to produce an Olympic champion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ulrich_Wehling" title="Ulrich Wehling">Ulrich Wehling</a> of <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irina_Rodnina" title="Irina Rodnina">Irina Rodnina</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="USSR at the Winter Olympics">USSR</a> won their respective events for the third time, Wehling in <a href="/wiki/Nordic_combined" title="Nordic combined">Nordic combined</a> and Rodnina in <a href="/wiki/Pair_skating" title="Pair skating">pairs figure skating</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Tikhonov" title="Aleksandr Tikhonov">Aleksandr Tikhonov</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="USSR at the Winter Olympics">USSR</a> earned his fourth straight gold medal as part of the 4 x 7.5m <a href="/wiki/Biathlon" title="Biathlon">biathlon</a> relay team.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nikolay_Zimyatov" title="Nikolay Zimyatov">Nikolay Zimyatov</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="USSR at the Winter Olympics">USSR</a> earned three gold medals in cross-country skiing.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Robin_Cousins" title="Robin Cousins">Robin Cousins</a> won gold for Great Britain in the men's singles figure skating.</li>
<li>East Germany won the most medals overall (23) but had fewer golds (9) than the USSR (10).</li>
<li>In possibly the most dramatic duel of the games, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Wassberg" title="Thomas Wassberg">Thomas Wassberg</a> edged <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Juha_Mieto" title="Juha Mieto">Juha Mieto</a> in the 15 km cross-country skiing by 0.01 seconds, the closest margin of victory ever in Olympic <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing_at_the_1980_Winter_Olympics" title="Cross-country skiing at the 1980 Winter Olympics">cross-country skiing</a>. This led the <a href="/wiki/International_Ski_Federation" title="International Ski Federation">International Ski Federation</a> (ISF) to time all events to the nearest 1/10 second in the future.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Although they did not get any medals, the People's Republic of China entered the Olympics Games for the first time after the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">IOC</a> agreed to designate the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> "<a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a>".</li>
<li>Lake Placid 1980 marked the first use of artificial snow in Olympic competition.</li>
<li>Lake Placid 1980 was the last Olympics to be opened by the <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">Vice President of the United States</a>, as 1980 was a Presidential election year.</li>
</ul>

<p>Critics decried poorly planned transportation which left spectators waiting for hours in freezing weather, and some athletes complained about lousy accommodations at the Olympic village, where the dorms would later be turned into a prison, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Correctional_Institution,_Ray_Brook" title="Federal Correctional Institution, Ray Brook">FCI Ray Brook</a>. The supply and demand for event tickets never merged, as would-be buyers were unable to buy unsold tickets because only those who already had a ticket could enter ticket-sales areas at venues. Unsold tickets are still being sold as souvenirs at several local tourist shops, including the Olympic Regional Development Association's gift shop at the Olympic Arena.</p>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/Will_Rogers_Memorial_Hospital" title="Will Rogers Memorial Hospital">Will Rogers Memorial Hospital</a> was briefly used as press headquarters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv_ny_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv_ny-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Participating_nations">Participating nations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Participating nations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>37 <a href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Committee" title="National Olympic Committee">NOCs</a> participated. United States President <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a> had already called for the boycott of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Summer_Olympics" title="1980 Summer Olympics">1980 Summer Olympics</a> in Moscow, leading to a great deal of tension at the Games. Nevertheless, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a> and its allies participated (though the USSR didn't participate in the <a href="/wiki/1984_Summer_Olympics" title="1984 Summer Olympics">1984 Summer Olympics</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> made their Olympic debut at the games. The People's Republic of China and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> both made their Winter Olympic debut. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> had boycotted the Games over the IOC's recognition of the PRC as "China", and its request for the Republic of China to compete as "<a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Roni_(mascot)" title="Roni (mascot)">Roni (mascot)</a></div>
<p>Roni is the Olympic mascot of these Games, created by Don Moss. The mascot is a racoon, which is a familiar animal from the mountainous region of the Adirondacks where Lake Placid is situated. The name Roni comes from the word <i>racoon</i> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iroquoian" title="Iroquoian">Iroquoian</a>, the language of the native people from the region of the State of New York and Lake Placid and was chosen by Lake Placid school children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympic.org_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-olympic.org-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theme_song">Theme song</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Theme song">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The official theme song for the 1980 Winter Olympics was "<a href="/wiki/Give_It_All_You_Got" title="Give It All You Got">Give It All You Got</a>" by the American <a href="/wiki/Flugelhorn" title="Flugelhorn">flugelhorn</a> player <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Mangione" title="Chuck Mangione">Chuck Mangione</a>, who performed the song (along with the song "Pina Colada") live at the Closing Ceremony, with the Hamilton Philharmonic Orchestra (Canada).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
