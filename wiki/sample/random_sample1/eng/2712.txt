<p>Year <b>53 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Messalla and Calvinus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 701 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 53 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=53_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=53_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Consul" title="Consul">Consuls</a>: <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Valerius_Messalla_Rufus" title="Marcus Valerius Messalla Rufus">Marcus Valerius Messalla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gnaeus_Domitius_Calvinus" title="Gnaeus Domitius Calvinus">Gnaeus Domitius Calvinus</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthian</a> war:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marcus_Licinius_Crassus" title="Marcus Licinius Crassus">Crassus</a> sacks the Temple of <a href="/wiki/Hierapolis" title="Hierapolis">Hierapolis</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Temple_in_Jerusalem" title="Temple in Jerusalem">Temple in Jerusalem</a> on his way to engage the Parthians.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Carrhae" title="Battle of Carrhae">Battle of Carrhae</a>: Romans defeated, and Crassus killed, by Parthians led by <a href="/wiki/Surena" title="Surena">Surena</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallic_War" title="Gallic War">Gallic War</a>:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a> defeats a revolt led by <a href="/wiki/Ambiorix" title="Ambiorix">Ambiorix</a> near <a href="/wiki/Sambre" title="Sambre">Sabis</a> (Northern <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>).</li>
<li>At <a href="/wiki/Cenabum" title="Cenabum">Cenabum</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Orl%C3%A9ans" title="Orléans">Orléans</a>) Roman <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">merchants</a> are massacred by the <a href="/wiki/Carnutes" title="Carnutes">Carnutes</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vercingetorix" title="Vercingetorix">Vercingetorix</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Arverni" title="Arverni">Arverni</a> chieftain, leads a revolt against Caesar in Central Gaul.</li>
<li>Winter – Caesar enrols non-citizen soldiers in <a href="/wiki/Gallia_Narbonensis" title="Gallia Narbonensis">Gallia Transalpina</a>, genesis of <i><a href="/wiki/Legio_V_Alaudae" title="Legio V Alaudae">Legio V Alaudae</a></i>. He increases his army to ten <a href="/wiki/Roman_legion" title="Roman legion">legions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
