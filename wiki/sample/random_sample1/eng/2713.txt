<p>The <b>5th century BC</b> started the first day of <a href="/wiki/500_BC" title="500 BC">500 BC</a> and ended the last day of <a href="/wiki/401_BC" title="401 BC">401 BC</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ac.parthenon5.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="426" data-file-width="575" height="222" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Ac.parthenon5.jpg/300px-Ac.parthenon5.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Ac.parthenon5.jpg/450px-Ac.parthenon5.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/35/Ac.parthenon5.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>

The Parthenon of <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> seen from the hill of the Pnyx to the west. Location 23°43'35.69"E 37°58'17.39"N</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This century saw the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Pataliputra" title="Pataliputra">Pataliputra</a> as a capital of <a href="/wiki/Magadha" title="Magadha">Magadha</a> Empire. This city would later become the ruling capital of different Indian Kingdoms for about a thousand years. This period saw the rise of two great philosophical schools of the East, <a href="/wiki/Jainism" title="Jainism">Jainism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>. This period saw <a href="/wiki/Mahavira" title="Mahavira">Mahavira</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddha" title="Buddha">Buddha</a> spreading their respective teachings in Northern Plains of India. This essentially changed the socio-cultural and political dynamics of the region of South Asia. <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> would later go on to become one of the major world religions.</p>
<p>This period also saw the work of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yaska" title="Yaska">Yaska</a>, who created <a href="/wiki/Nirukta" title="Nirukta">Nirukta</a>, that would lay the foundation stone for Sanskrit grammar and is one of the oldest works on grammar known to mankind.</p>
<p>This century also saw the beginning of a period of philosophical brilliance among Western civilizations, particularly the <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a>, which would continue all the way through the <a href="/wiki/4th_century_BC" title="4th century BC">4th century</a> until the time of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-book-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Ancient</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_philosophy" title="Greek philosophy">Greek philosophy</a> developed during the 5th century BC, setting the foundation for Western ideology. In <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> and elsewhere in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> world, the 5th century marked a high point in the development of political institutions, art, architecture, and literature.</p>
<p>The century was also notable for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persian_Wars" title="Persian Wars">Persian Wars</a>, fought between the Greek cities and the vast <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Persian Empire</a>. Determined to punish <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> for supporting a revolt by conquered Greek cities in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a>, King <a href="/wiki/Darius_I" title="Darius I">Darius I</a> sent several armies against them, only to have his plans first thwarted by a storm and later by a defeat at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Marathon" title="Battle of Marathon">Battle of Marathon</a>. His son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xerxes_I_of_Persia" title="Xerxes I of Persia">Xerxes</a> attempted to finish the job 10 years later, and succeeded in capturing <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> and burning it to the ground, and was defeated later on land at <a href="/wiki/Plataea" title="Plataea">Plataea</a>. In the latter part of the century the Greeks became locked in a bitter <a href="/wiki/Peloponnesian_War" title="Peloponnesian War">war</a> among themselves, with the major cities Athens and <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Sparta</a> competing for absolute domination. The Spartans were victorious in this battle.</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/424_BC" title="424 BC">424 BC</a>: The Athenians try to capture <a href="/wiki/Megara" title="Megara">Megara</a>, but are defeated by the Spartans.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/424_BC" title="424 BC">424 BC</a>: The Spartan general <a href="/wiki/Brasidas" title="Brasidas">Brasidas</a> captures <a href="/wiki/Amphipolis" title="Amphipolis">Amphipolis</a>, which is a setback for Athens. <a href="/wiki/Thucydides" title="Thucydides">Thucydides</a> is held responsible for the Athenian failure and is ostracised. This gives him time to start writing his <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Peloponnesian_War" title="History of the Peloponnesian War">history book</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/423_BC" title="423 BC">423 BC</a>: The Athenians propose a cease-fire, which the Spartan general Brasidas ignores.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/422_BC" title="422 BC">422 BC</a>: The Spartans defeat the Athenians in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Amphipolis" title="Battle of Amphipolis">Battle of Amphipolis</a>, where the Athenian <a href="/wiki/Cleon" title="Cleon">Cleon</a> and the Spartan <a href="/wiki/Brasidas" title="Brasidas">Brasidas</a> are both killed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/421_BC" title="421 BC">421 BC</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Nicias" title="Peace of Nicias">Peace of Nicias</a> puts a temporary end to the hostilities between <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Sparta</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/420_BC" title="420 BC">420 BC</a>: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alicibiades&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alicibiades (page does not exist)">Alicibiades</a> is elected <a href="/wiki/Strategos" title="Strategos">strategos</a> of Athens and begins dominating Athenian politics.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="410s_BC"><a href="/wiki/410s_BC" title="410s BC">410s BC</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=5th_century_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 410s BC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/419_BC" title="419 BC">419 BC</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Nicias" title="Peace of Nicias">Peace of Nicias</a> is broken when Sparta defeats <a href="/wiki/Argos" title="Argos">Argos</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/418_BC" title="418 BC">418 BC</a>: The Spartans win a major victory over the Athenians in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mantinea_(418_BC)" title="Battle of Mantinea (418 BC)">Battle of Mantinea</a>, the biggest land battle of the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnesian_War" title="Peloponnesian War">Peloponnesian War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/416_BC" title="416 BC">416 BC</a>: The Athenians capture the island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melos" title="Melos">Melos</a> and treat the inhabitants with great cruelty.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/416_BC" title="416 BC">416 BC</a>: The Athenians adheres a plea of help from <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a> and starts planning an invasion of the island.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/415_BC" title="415 BC">415 BC</a>: The sacred <a href="/wiki/Herma" title="Herma">Hermae</a> busts in Athens are mutilated just before the expedition to Sicily is sent away. One of the culprits, <a href="/wiki/Andocides" title="Andocides">Andocides</a>, is captured and is forced to turn informer. He names the other mutilators, among them Alcibiades, who are sentenced to death in their absence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/415_BC" title="415 BC">415 BC</a>: Alcibiades defects from Athens to Sparta after having learned about his death sentence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/414_BC" title="414 BC">414 BC</a>: The Athenians try to make a breakthrough in <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_Expedition" title="Sicilian Expedition">their siege</a> of <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_Sicily" title="Syracuse, Sicily">Syracuse</a> but are defeated by the Spartans.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/413_BC" title="413 BC">413 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/Demosthenes_(general)" title="Demosthenes (general)">Demosthenes</a> suggests the Athenians leave Syracuse in order to return to Athens, where help is needed. However, <a href="/wiki/Nicias" title="Nicias">Nicias</a> refuses and they are again <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Syracuse_(415_BC)" title="Battle of Syracuse (415 BC)">defeated in battle</a> by the Spartans. Both Demosthenes and Nicias are killed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/413_BC" title="413 BC">413 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/Caria" title="Caria">Caria</a> allies itself with Sparta.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/412_BC" title="412 BC">412 BC</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Persian Empire</a> starts preparing an invasion of <a href="/wiki/Ionia" title="Ionia">Ionia</a> and signs a treaty with <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Sparta</a> about it.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/411_BC" title="411 BC">411 BC</a>: The democracy in Athens is overthrown and replaced by the oligarchic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Four_Hundred_(oligarchy)" title="The Four Hundred (oligarchy)">Council of Four Hundred</a>. This council is itself soon defeated and order is almost restored, when the Five Thousand start ruling. Early next year, they are also overthrown and the old democracy is restored.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/410_BC" title="410 BC">410 BC</a>: Athens regains control over its vital grain route from the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> by defeating Sparta in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cyzicus" title="Battle of Cyzicus">Battle of Cyzicus</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="400s_BC"><a href="/wiki/400s_BC_(decade)" title="400s BC (decade)">400s BC</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=5th_century_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 400s BC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/409_BC" title="409 BC">409 BC</a>: Athens recaptures <a href="/wiki/Byzantium" title="Byzantium">Byzantium</a>, thereby putting an end to its revolt against Athens and taking control of the whole <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bosporus" title="Bosporus">Bosporus</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/409_BC" title="409 BC">409 BC</a>: The city of <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">Rhodes</a> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/409_BC" title="409 BC">409 BC</a>: The Carthaginians invade <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/408_BC" title="408 BC">408 BC</a>: The Persian king, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darius_II_of_Persia" title="Darius II of Persia">Darius II</a>, decides to aid Sparta in the war and makes his son <a href="/wiki/Cyrus_the_Younger" title="Cyrus the Younger">Cyrus</a> a <a href="/wiki/Satrap" title="Satrap">satrap</a>. However, Cyrus starts collecting an army to benefit his own interests, rather than his father's.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/408_BC" title="408 BC">408 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/Alcibiades" title="Alcibiades">Alcibiades</a> returns to Athens in triumph after an absence of seven years.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/407_BC" title="407 BC">407 BC</a>: The Athenian fleet is routed by the Spartan one in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Notium" title="Battle of Notium">Battle of Notium</a>, which gives Alcibiades' opponents a reason to strip him of command. He never returns to Athens again.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/406_BC" title="406 BC">406 BC</a>: Athens defeats Sparta in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Arginusae" title="Battle of Arginusae">Battle of Arginusae</a> and the blockade of <a href="/wiki/Conon" title="Conon">Conon</a> is lifted.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/406_BC" title="406 BC">406 BC</a>: Sparta sues for peace, but Athens rejects this.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/406_BC" title="406 BC">406 BC</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthaginians</a> once again invade Sicily and return to Carthage with spoils of war, but also with the plague.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/405_BC" title="405 BC">405 BC</a>: The Spartan king <a href="/wiki/Pausanias_of_Sparta" title="Pausanias of Sparta">Pausanias</a> lays siege to <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, which makes the city start starving.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/405_BC" title="405 BC">405 BC</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dionysius_the_Elder" title="Dionysius the Elder">Dionysius the Elder</a> rises to power in <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_Sicily" title="Syracuse, Sicily">Syracuse</a>. He signs a peace with Carthage and starts consolidating and expanding his influence.</li>
<li>April 25, <a href="/wiki/404_BC" title="404 BC">404 BC</a>: Athens surrenders to Sparta, ending the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnesian_War" title="Peloponnesian War">Peloponnesian War</a>. Sparta introduces an oligarchic system, the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Tyrants" title="Thirty Tyrants">Thirty Tyrants</a>, in Athens.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/404_BC" title="404 BC">404 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a> rebels against Persian rule.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/403_BC" title="403 BC">403 BC</a>: Some exiled Athenians return to fight the Thirty Tyrants and restore democracy in Athens. They are, however, narrowly defeated by the Spartans in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Piraeus" title="Battle of Piraeus">Battle of Piraeus</a>. After this, the Spartan king <a href="/wiki/Pausanias_of_Sparta" title="Pausanias of Sparta">Pausanias</a> allows democracy to be restored in Athens.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/403_BC" title="403 BC">403 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/Thrasybulus" title="Thrasybulus">Thrasybulus</a> restores the Athenian democracy and grants an almost general amnesty.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/403_BC" title="403 BC">403 BC</a>: The Athenians adopt the Ionian alphabet.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/401_BC" title="401 BC">401 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/Cyrus_the_Younger" title="Cyrus the Younger">Cyrus the Younger</a> rebels against the Persian king <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artaxerxes_II" title="Artaxerxes II">Artaxerxes II</a> but is, however, eventually slain in battle.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/400_BC" title="400 BC">400 BC</a>: After Cyrus has been killed, his Greek mercenaries make their way back to Greece, where Sparta is so impressed with their feats in and march through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persia" title="Persia">Persia</a> that they declare war on the Persians.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/400_BC" title="400 BC">400 BC</a>: The Carthaginians occupy <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/400_BC" title="400 BC">400 BC</a>: The Egyptians successfully revolt against Persian rule.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/400_BC" title="400 BC">400 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> has its origins as far back as this time.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/400_BC" title="400 BC">400 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dmon_period" title="Jōmon period">Jōmon period</a> ends in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Japan" title="Ancient Japan">Ancient Japan</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inventions.2C_discoveries.2C_introductions">Inventions, discoveries, introductions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=5th_century_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Inventions, discoveries, introductions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">Cast iron</a> is first used in the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Wu_(state)" title="Wu (state)">Kingdom of Wu</a> with the innovation of the <a href="/wiki/Blast_furnace" title="Blast furnace">blast furnace</a>, and soon becomes widespread for agricultural tools and weapons during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warring_States" title="Warring States">Warring States</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Trebuchet" title="Trebuchet">Trebuchet</a> <a href="/wiki/Catapult" title="Catapult">catapult</a> is first used by followers of the Chinese philosopher <a href="/wiki/Mozi" title="Mozi">Mozi</a>.</li>
<li>The Greeks invent the <a href="/wiki/Anchor" title="Anchor">Anchor</a> with flukes.</li>
<li>The Greeks start to use <a href="/wiki/Shear_legs" title="Shear legs">shear-leg</a> cranes for construction and loading of ships.</li>
<li>The Greeks invent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linear_perspective" title="Linear perspective">linear perspective</a>.</li>
<li>The Greeks develop an indirect lost wax process for casting <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a>.</li>
<li>The Chinese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydraulic" title="Hydraulic">hydraulic</a> engineer <a href="/wiki/Ximen_Bao" title="Ximen Bao">Ximen Bao</a> (西門豹) oversees an enormous <a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">canal</a> system for agricultural <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>, while employed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquis_Wen_of_Wei" title="Marquis Wen of Wei">Marquis Wen of Wei</a> (文侯) (<a href="/wiki/445_BC" title="445 BC">445 BC</a>–<a href="/wiki/396_BC" title="396 BC">396 BC</a>).</li>
<li>The Chinese philosopher <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Kui_(legalism)" title="Li Kui (legalism)">Li Kui</a> writes the <i>Book of Law</i> (Fajing, 法经) in <a href="/wiki/407_BC" title="407 BC">407 BC</a>, the basis for the law codes of the following <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qin_Dynasty" title="Qin Dynasty">Qin Dynasty</a> and partially that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_Dynasty" title="Han Dynasty">Han Dynasty</a>.</li>
<li>Scholars commonly accept that the Hindu text the Bhagavad Gita was written.</li>
<li>Creation of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Foundry_Cup" title="Berlin Foundry Cup">Berlin Foundry Cup</a> (early 5th century).</li>
<li>The oldest known <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maya_Calendar" title="Maya Calendar">Maya Calendar</a></li>
</ul>
