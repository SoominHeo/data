<p>Year <b>67 BC</b> was a year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_calendar" title="Roman calendar">pre-Julian Roman calendar</a>. At the time it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Piso and Glabrio</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 687 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 67 BC for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=67_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=67_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Republic">Roman Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=67_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Consul" title="Consul">Consuls</a>: <a href="/wiki/Manius_Acilius_Glabrio_(consul_67_BC)" title="Manius Acilius Glabrio (consul 67 BC)">Manius Acilius Glabrio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Calpurnius_Piso_(consul_67_BC)" title="Gaius Calpurnius Piso (consul 67 BC)">Gaius Calpurnius Piso</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pompey" title="Pompey">Pompey</a>'s war against the pirates, he raises a fleet of 500 <a href="/wiki/Galley" title="Galley">warships</a> and fights with great success.</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Lex_Gabinia" title="Lex Gabinia">lex Gabinia</a></i> gives Pompey command of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> and its coasts for 50 miles inland for three years. He defeats the pirates in three months and pacifies <a href="/wiki/Cilicia" title="Cilicia">Cilicia</a>.</li>
<li>Pompey divides the Mediterranean into 13 zones – six in the West and seven in the East – to each of which he assigns a fleet under an <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a>.</li>
<li>Pompey offers the ex-pirates and their families <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clemency" title="Clemency">clemency</a>, he settled them in <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colonies</a> in eastern Mediterranean lands.</li>
<li>Pompey takes over the command of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucius_Lucullus" title="Lucius Lucullus">Lucius Lucullus</a> in the war against <a href="/wiki/Mithridates_VI_of_Pontus" title="Mithridates VI of Pontus">Mithridates VI</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Harvest" title="Harvest">reaping</a> the fruit of the latter's victories.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lex_Acilia_Calpurnia" title="Lex Acilia Calpurnia">Lex Acilia Calpurnia</a>: permanent exclusion from office in cases of electoral corruption.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lex_Roscia_theatralis" title="Lex Roscia theatralis">Lex Roscia theatralis</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Judea">Judea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=67_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Judea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hyrcanus_II" title="Hyrcanus II">Hyrcanus II</a> becomes king of <a href="/wiki/Judea" title="Judea">Judea</a>, for first time (until <a href="/wiki/66_BC" title="66 BC">66 BC</a>), on death of his mother, <a href="/wiki/Salome_Alexandra" title="Salome Alexandra">Salome Alexandra</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pontus">Pontus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=67_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pontus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Mithridates VI invades <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontus" title="Pontus">Pontus</a>. He besieged the Romans in <a href="/wiki/Chalcedon" title="Chalcedon">Chalcedon</a> (opposite <a href="/wiki/Byzantium" title="Byzantium">Byzantium</a>) and pressed westward along the south shores of the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara" title="Sea of Marmara">Sea of Marmara</a> to attack <a href="/wiki/Cyzicus" title="Cyzicus">Cyzicus</a>.</li>
<li>Lucullus disperse Mithridates's invading armies and launched a counter-offensive into Pontus, where he penetrates the chain of fortress towns that defended the kingdom.</li>
</ul>
