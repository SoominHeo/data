<p>The <b>3rd millennium BC</b> spans the years 3000 through 2000 BC. This period of time corresponds to the Early to Middle <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>, in which <a href="/wiki/Imperialism" title="Imperialism">imperialism</a>, or the desire to conquer, grew to prominence in the city-states of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and throughout <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. The civilization of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a> rose to a peak with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Kingdom" title="Old Kingdom">Old Kingdom</a>. <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">World population</a> is estimated to have doubled in the course of the millennium, to some 30 million people.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> occurred roughly between 3000 BC and 2500 BC. The previous millennium had seen the emergence of advanced, urbanized civilizations, new <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">metallurgy</a> extending the productivity of agricultural work, and highly developed ways of communication in the form of <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writing</a>. In the 3rd millennium BC, the growth of these riches, both intellectually and physically, became a source of contention on a political stage, and rulers sought the accumulation of more wealth and more power. Along with this came the first appearances of mega architecture, <a href="/wiki/Imperialism" title="Imperialism">imperialism</a>, organized absolutism and internal revolution.</p>
<p>The civilizations of <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Akkad_(city)" title="Akkad (city)">Akkad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> became a collection of volatile <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-states</a> in which warfare was common. Uninterrupted conflicts drained all available resources, energies and populations. In this millennium, larger empires succeeded the last, and conquerors grew in stature until the great <a href="/wiki/Sargon_of_Akkad" title="Sargon of Akkad">Sargon of Akkad</a> pushed his empire to the whole of <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> and beyond. It would not be surpassed in size until <a href="/wiki/Assyria" title="Assyria">Assyrian</a> times 1,500 years later.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Kingdom" title="Old Kingdom">Old Kingdom</a> of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_pyramids" title="Egyptian pyramids">Egyptian pyramids</a> were constructed and would remain the tallest and largest human constructions for thousands of years. Also in Egypt, <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">pharaohs</a> began to posture themselves as living <a href="/wiki/Deity" title="Deity">gods</a> made of an essence different from that of other <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> beings. Even in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, which was still largely <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">neolithic</a> during the same period, the builders of <a href="/wiki/Megalith" title="Megalith">megaliths</a> were constructing giant monuments of their own. In the <a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">Occident</a> during the 3rd millennium BC, limits were being pushed by architects and rulers.</p>
<p>Towards the close of the millennium, Egypt became the stage of the first popular revolution recorded in history. After lengthy wars, the Sumerians recognized the benefits of unification into a stable form of national government and became a relatively peaceful, well-organized, complex technocratic state called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3rd_dynasty_of_Ur" title="3rd dynasty of Ur">3rd dynasty of Ur</a>. This dynasty was later to become involved with a wave of <a href="/wiki/Nomad" title="Nomad">nomadic</a> invaders known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amorite" title="Amorite">Amorites</a>, who were to play a major role in the region during the following centuries.</p>

<p>Certain <a href="/wiki/4th_millennium_BC" title="4th millennium BC">4th millennium BC</a> events were precursors to the 3rd millennium BC:</p>
<ul>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3700_BC" title="3700 BC">3700 BC</a>: <a href="/wiki/Lothal" title="Lothal">Lothal</a> Advanced Civilization in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3650_BC" title="3650 BC">3650 BC</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minoan_culture" title="Minoan culture">Minoan culture</a> appeared on <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3200_BC" title="3200 BC">3200 BC</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3100_BC" title="3100 BC">3100 BC</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helladic_culture" title="Helladic culture">Helladic culture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cycladic_culture" title="Cycladic culture">Cycladic culture</a> both emerge in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>The 3rd millennium BC included the following key events:</p>
<ul>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>: First evidence of gold being used in the Middle East.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>: Nubian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-Group" title="A-Group">A-Group</a>, Ta-Seeti "kingdom" came to an end, possibly due to raids by <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_BC" title="2000 BC">3100 BC</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_vessel" title="Marine vessel">Mahabharat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kurukshetra_War" title="Kurukshetra War">Kurukshetra War</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>c.3112 BC- Birth Of King <a href="/wiki/Krishna" title="Krishna">Krishna</a> Central figure of Mahabharata and Kurukshetra war as per as <a class="external text" href="http://m.timesofindia.com/home/sunday-times/deep-focus/Silent-secrets-of-the-sea/articleshow/2351376.cms" rel="nofollow">Recent Marine Archeology, Scientific, Archeological, Astronomical and Under water Excavation Studies.</a></li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_BC" title="2000 BC">2000 BC</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_vessel" title="Marine vessel">Vessels</a> from <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> are made; they are now at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Museum" title="National Museum">National Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>.:</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2890_BC" title="2890 BC">2890 BC</a>: Second Dynasty of Egypt, reign of <a href="/wiki/Hotepsekhemwy" title="Hotepsekhemwy">Hotepsekhemwy</a>.</li>

<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2650_BC" title="2650 BC">2650 BC</a>: Reservoirs, script, metals and pottery used in the city of <a href="/wiki/Dholavira" title="Dholavira">Dholavira</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Indus Valley Civilization</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2300_BC" title="2300 BC">2300 BC</a>: Metals are used in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">Northern Europe</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> record a <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comet</a>.</li>
<li>Building of the Great <a href="/wiki/Pyramid" title="Pyramid">Pyramid</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giza" title="Giza">Giza</a> (<a href="/wiki/26th_century_BC" title="26th century BC">26th century BC</a>).</li>
<li>Sails used on <a href="/wiki/Ship" title="Ship">ships</a> (<a href="/wiki/20th_century_BC" title="20th century BC">20th century BC</a>).</li>
<li>First <a href="/wiki/Ziggurat" title="Ziggurat">ziggurats</a> built in <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumer</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a> civilizations enter <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> around 3000 BC.</li>
<li>Oldest known <a href="/wiki/Medicine_wheel" title="Medicine wheel">medicine wheel</a> constructed in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.</li>
<li>First <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">Copper</a> (~2500 BC) and then <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">Bronze</a> (~2000 BC) and other types of <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">metallurgy</a> are introduced to <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Domestication_of_the_horse" title="Domestication of the horse">Domestication of the horse</a> with the coming of <a href="/wiki/Proto-Indo-Europeans" title="Proto-Indo-Europeans">Indo-Europeans</a> in central Eurasia.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Chariot" title="Chariot">chariot</a> emerges in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a> just before 2000 BC.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Camel" title="Camel">camel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dromedary" title="Dromedary">dromedary</a>) domesticated (though widespread use took until mid-to-late <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium_BC" title="2nd millennium BC">2nd millennium BC</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Indoor plumbing and sewage in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Indus Valley Civilization</a>.</li>
<li>Sumerian medicine discovers the healing qualities of mineral springs.</li>
<li>Weaving loom known in Europe.</li>
<li>Sumerian numerical system based on multiples of 6 and 12.</li>
<li>Egyptians discover use of papyrus.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bow_(weapon)" title="Bow (weapon)">Bow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arrow" title="Arrow">arrow</a> used in warfare.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austronesian" title="Austronesian">Austronesian</a> peoples have developed lanteen sail, and the out-rigger as well as extensive development of celestial navigation systems.</li>
<li>Oldest known evidence of the <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_smoking" title="Cannabis smoking">inhalation of cannabis smoke</a>, as indicated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charred" title="Charred">charred</a> cannabis <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a> found in a <a href="/wiki/Ritual" title="Ritual">ritual</a> <a href="/wiki/Brazier" title="Brazier">brazier</a> at a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burial_site" title="Burial site">burial site</a> in present-day <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
