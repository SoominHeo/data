<p>The <b>4th millennium BC</b> spans the years 4000 through 3000 BC. Some of the major changes in human culture during this time included the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> and the invention of <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writing</a>, which played a major role in starting <a href="/wiki/Recorded_history" title="Recorded history">recorded history</a>.</p>

<p>The city states of <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumer</a> and the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a> were established and grew to prominence. <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">Agriculture</a> spread widely across <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">World population</a> in the course of the millennium doubled, approximately from 7 to 14 million people.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=4th_millennium_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> is in the <a href="/wiki/Uruk_period" title="Uruk period">Uruk period</a>, with emerging <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumerian</a> hegemony and development of "proto-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuneiform" title="Cuneiform">cuneiform</a>" <a href="/wiki/History_of_writing" title="History of writing">writing</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Base-60" title="Base-60">base-60</a> <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Astrology" title="Astrology">astrology</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legal_system" title="Legal system">civil law</a>, complex <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sailboat" title="Sailboat">sailboat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potter%27s_wheel" title="Potter's wheel">potter's wheel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wheel" title="Wheel">wheel</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chalcolithic" title="Chalcolithic">Chalcolithic</a> proceeds into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_Bronze_Age" title="Early Bronze Age">Early Bronze Age</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4000_BC" title="4000 BC">4000 BC</a>—First neolithic settlers in the island of <a href="/wiki/Santorini" title="Santorini">Thera</a> (<a href="/wiki/Santorini" title="Santorini">Santorini</a>), Greece, migrating probably from <a href="/wiki/Minoan_civilization" title="Minoan civilization">Minoan</a> <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a>.</li>
<li>c. 4000 BC—<a href="/wiki/Beaker_(archaeology)" title="Beaker (archaeology)">Beaker</a> from <a href="/wiki/Susa" title="Susa">Susa</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Shush,_Iran" title="Shush, Iran">Shush, Iran</a>) is made. It is now at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre" title="Musée du Louvre">Musée du Louvre</a>, Paris.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4000_BC" title="4000 BC">4000</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_BC" title="2000 BC">2000 BC</a>—People and animals, a detail of rock-shelter painting in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cogul&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cogul (page does not exist)">Cogul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lleida" title="Lleida">Lleida</a>, Spain, are painted. It is now at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_Arqueol%C3%B3gico_de_Barcelona&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Museo Arqueológico de Barcelona (page does not exist)">Museo Arqueológico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3900_BC" title="3900 BC">3900 BC</a>—<a href="/wiki/5.9_kiloyear_event" title="5.9 kiloyear event">5.9 kiloyear event</a>, one of the most intense <a href="/wiki/Aridification" title="Aridification">aridification</a> events during the <a href="/wiki/Holocene" title="Holocene">Holocene</a>. It ended the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic_Subpluvial" title="Neolithic Subpluvial">Neolithic Subpluvial</a> and likely initiated the most recent <a href="/wiki/Desiccation" title="Desiccation">desiccation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> desert, triggering <a href="/wiki/Human_migration" title="Human migration">migration</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_valley" title="River valley">river valleys</a>, such as from central <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nile_valley" title="Nile valley">Nile valley</a>.</li>
<li>Babylonian influence predominant in Mediterranean regions of Asia (to 2000 BC).</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, circa <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3600_BC" title="3600 BC">3600 BC</a>, first rupestrian art <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chiribiquete&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chiribiquete (page does not exist)">Chiribiquete</a> (<a href="/wiki/Caquet%C3%A1_Department" title="Caquetá Department">Caquetá</a>).</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3600_BC" title="3600 BC">3600 BC</a>—Construction of the <a href="/wiki/%C4%A0gantija" title="Ġgantija">Ġgantija</a> megalithic temple complex on the <a href="/wiki/Gozo" title="Gozo">Island of Gozo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>: the world's oldest extant unburied free-standing structures, and the world's oldest religious structures. (See <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6bekli_Tepe" title="Göbekli Tepe">Göbekli Tepe</a> for older, buried religious structures.)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3600_BC" title="3600 BC">3600</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3200_BC" title="3200 BC">3200 BC</a>—Construction of the first temple within the <a href="/wiki/Mnajdra" title="Mnajdra">Mnajdra</a> solar temple complex on <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, containing "furniture" such as stone benches and tables, that set it apart from other European megalith constructions.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3600_BC" title="3600 BC">3600</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>—Construction of the Ta' Ħaġrat and Kordin III temples on <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3500_BC" title="3500 BC">3500</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lost_wax" title="Lost wax">Metalcasting</a> began in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohenjodaro" title="Mohenjodaro">Mohenjodaro</a> area.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3500_BC" title="3500 BC">3500 BC</a>—Figures of a man and a woman, from <a href="/wiki/Cernavod%C4%83" title="Cernavodă">Cernavodă</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, are made. They are now at <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_Romanian_History" title="National Museum of Romanian History">National Historical Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3500_BC" title="3500 BC">3500</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3400_BC" title="3400 BC">3400 BC</a>—<a href="/wiki/Jar" title="Jar">Jar</a> with boat designs, from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hierakonpolis" title="Hierakonpolis">Hierakonpolis</a> (today in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Museum" title="Brooklyn Museum">Brooklyn Museum</a>) is created. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Predynastic_Egypt" title="Predynastic Egypt">Predynastic Egypt</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3500_BC" title="3500 BC">3500</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2340_BC" title="2340 BC">2340 BC</a>—First cities developed in Southern <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>. Inhabitants migrated from north.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuneiform" title="Cuneiform">cuneiform</a> script proper emerges from pictographic proto-writing in the later 4th millennium. Mesopotamia's "proto-literate" period spans the 35th to 32nd centuries. The first documents unequivocally written in the Sumerian language date to the 31st century, found at Jemdet Nasr.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3300_BC" title="3300 BC">3300</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2900_BC" title="2900 BC">2900 BC</a>—Construction of the <a href="/wiki/Newgrange" title="Newgrange">Newgrange</a> solar <a href="/wiki/Observatory" title="Observatory">observatory</a>/passage tomb in Ireland.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3300_BC" title="3300 BC">3300</a>—<a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> starts in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Indus Valley</a> (<a href="/wiki/Harappa" title="Harappa">Harappa</a>).</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3300_BC" title="3300 BC">3300 BC</a>—<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%96tzi_the_Iceman" title="Ötzi the Iceman">Ötzi the Iceman</a> dies near the present-day border between Austria and Italy, only to be discovered in 1991 buried in a <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glacier</a> of the <a href="/wiki/%C3%96tztal_Alps" title="Ötztal Alps">Ötztal Alps</a>. His <a href="/wiki/Cause_of_death" title="Cause of death">cause of death</a> is believed to be <a href="/wiki/Homicide" title="Homicide">homicide</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3200_BC" title="3200 BC">3250</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>—Construction of three megalithic temples at <a href="/wiki/Tarxien" title="Tarxien">Tarxien</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/32nd_century_BC" title="32nd century BC">3200</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2500_BC" title="2500 BC">2500 BC</a>—Construction of the <a href="/wiki/%C4%A6a%C4%A1ar_Qim" title="Ħaġar Qim">Ħaġar Qim</a> megalithic temple complex on <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, featuring both solar and lunar alignments.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3150_BC" title="3150 BC">3150 BC</a>—<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Predynastic_Egypt" title="Predynastic Egypt">Predynastic</a> period ended in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_Dynastic_Period_of_Egypt" title="Early Dynastic Period of Egypt">Early Dynastic</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_Dynastic_Period_of_Egypt" title="Early Dynastic Period of Egypt">Archaic</a>) period started (according to French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egyptologist" title="Egyptologist">Egyptologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Grimal" title="Nicolas Grimal">Nicolas Grimal</a>). The period includes 1st and 2nd Dynasties.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3150_BC" title="3150 BC">3150 BC</a> a lesser <a href="/wiki/Tollmann%27s_hypothetical_bolide" title="Tollmann's hypothetical bolide">Tollmann's hypothetical bolide</a> event may have occurred.</li>
<li>August 11, 3114 BC—start date of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayan_calendar" title="Mayan calendar">Mayan calendar</a>.</li>
<li>July 27, 3112 BC- Birth of King <a href="/wiki/Krishna" title="Krishna">Krishna</a>, Period of <a href="/wiki/Mahabharata" title="Mahabharata">Mahabharata</a> in India as per as <a class="external text" href="http://m.timesofindia.com/home/sunday-times/deep-focus/Silent-secrets-of-the-sea/articleshow/2351376.cms" rel="nofollow">Recent Scientific, Marine Archeology, Astro-Archeological and Underwater excavation Studies.</a></li>
<li>February 18, 3102 BC—Beginning of <a href="/wiki/Kali_Yuga" title="Kali Yuga">Kali Yuga</a> as per <a href="/wiki/Vedas" title="Vedas">Vedas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>c. 3100 BC—According to the legend, <a href="/wiki/Menes" title="Menes">Menes</a> (or perhaps, <a href="/wiki/Narmer" title="Narmer">Narmer</a>) unifies <a href="/wiki/Upper_Egypt" title="Upper Egypt">Upper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lower_Egypt" title="Lower Egypt">Lower Egypt</a>, and a new capital is erected at <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Egypt" title="Memphis, Egypt">Memphis</a>.</li>
<li>c. 3100 BC—<a href="/wiki/Narmer_Palette" title="Narmer Palette">Narmer Palette</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3100_BC" title="3100 BC">3100</a>–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2600_BC" title="2600 BC">2600 BC</a>—<a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> settlement at <a href="/wiki/Skara_Brae" title="Skara Brae">Skara Brae</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orkney_Islands" title="Orkney Islands">Orkney Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, is inhabited.</li>
<li>3051 BC <a href="/wiki/Methuselah_(tree)" title="Methuselah (tree)">The oldest currently (2013) living</a> non-clonal organism germinated in the Grove of the Ancients.</li>
<li>First to Fourth dynasty of <a href="/wiki/Kish_(Sumer)" title="Kish (Sumer)">Kish</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>.</li>
<li>Discovery of <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a>.</li>
<li>The beginnings of <a href="/wiki/Iberians" title="Iberians">Iberian</a> civilizations, arrival to the peninsula dating as far back as 4000 BC.</li>
<li>c. 3000 BC—First <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">pottery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Hormiga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Puerto Hormiga (page does not exist)">Puerto Hormiga</a> (<a href="/wiki/Magdalena_Department" title="Magdalena Department">Magdalena</a>), considered one of the first attempts of pottery of the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>. First settlement at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Badel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Puerto Badel (page does not exist)">Puerto Badel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bol%C3%ADvar_Department" title="Bolívar Department">Bolívar</a>).</li>
<li>Sumerian temple of Janna at Eridu erected.</li>
<li>Temple at <a href="/wiki/Al_Ubaidi" title="Al Ubaidi">Al-Ubaid</a> and tomb of Mes-Kalam-Dug built near <a href="/wiki/Ur" title="Ur">Ur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chaldea" title="Chaldea">Chaldea</a>.</li>
</ul>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_(period)" title="Atlantic (period)">Atlantic (period)</a></div>
<p>Based on studies by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glaciologist" title="Glaciologist">glaciologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Lonnie_Thompson" title="Lonnie Thompson">Lonnie Thompson</a>, professor at <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University" title="Ohio State University">Ohio State University</a> and researcher with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byrd_Polar_Research_Center" title="Byrd Polar Research Center">Byrd Polar Research Center</a>, a number of indicators shows there was a global change in climate 5,200 years ago, probably due to a drop in solar energy output as hypothesized by Ohio State University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Plants buried in the <a href="/wiki/Quelccaya_Ice_Cap" title="Quelccaya Ice Cap">Quelccaya Ice Cap</a> in the Peruvian Andes demonstrate the climate had shifted suddenly and severely to capture the plants and preserve them until now.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>A man trapped in an Alpine glacier ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%96tzi_the_Iceman" title="Ötzi the Iceman">Ötzi the Iceman</a>") is frozen until his discovery in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD5200_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SD5200-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Tree rings from Ireland and England show this was their driest period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD5200_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-SD5200-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Ice core records showing the ratio of two oxygen isotopes retrieved from the ice fields atop Africa's <a href="/wiki/Mount_Kilimanjaro" title="Mount Kilimanjaro">Mount Kilimanjaro</a>, a proxy for atmospheric temperature at the time snow fell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD5200_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-SD5200-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Major changes in plant pollen uncovered from lakebed cores in South America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD5200_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-SD5200-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Record lowest levels of <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> retrieved from ice cores from <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD5200_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-SD5200-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>End of the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic_Subpluvial" title="Neolithic Subpluvial">Neolithic Subpluvial</a>, start of <a href="/wiki/Desertification" title="Desertification">desertification</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> (<a href="/wiki/35th_century_BC" title="35th century BC">35th century BC</a>). North Africa shifts from a habitable region to a barren desert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD5200_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-SD5200-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inventions.2C_discoveries.2C_introductions">Inventions, discoveries, introductions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=4th_millennium_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Inventions, discoveries, introductions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sumerian_26th_c_Adab.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="576" data-file-width="556" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Sumerian_26th_c_Adab.jpg/170px-Sumerian_26th_c_Adab.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Sumerian_26th_c_Adab.jpg/255px-Sumerian_26th_c_Adab.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Sumerian_26th_c_Adab.jpg/340px-Sumerian_26th_c_Adab.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

Sumerian Cuneiform Script</div>
</div>

<ul>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4000_BC" title="4000 BC">4000 BC</a>—<a href="/wiki/Potter%27s_wheel" title="Potter's wheel">potter's wheel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>4000 BC—<a href="/wiki/Susa" title="Susa">Susa</a> is a center of <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">pottery</a> production.</li>
<li>c. 4000 BC—<a href="/wiki/Domestication_of_the_horse" title="Domestication of the horse">Horses are domesticated</a> in the western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eurasian_Steppes" title="Eurasian Steppes">Eurasian Steppes</a> in what is now northern <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> (see the <a href="/wiki/Botai_culture" title="Botai culture">Botai culture</a>).</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3500_BC" title="3500 BC">3500 BC</a>—<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2340_BC" title="2340 BC">2340 BC</a>; <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumer</a>: wheeled carts, <a href="/wiki/Potter%27s_wheel" title="Potter's wheel">potter's wheel</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=White_Temple_ziggurat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="White Temple ziggurat (page does not exist)">White Temple ziggurat</a>, bronze tools and weapons.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3250_BC" title="3250 BC">3250 BC</a>—potter's wheel appears in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Near_East" title="Ancient Near East">Ancient Near East</a>.</li>
<li>c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3138_BC" title="3138 BC">3138 BC</a> <a href="/wiki/Ljubljana_Marshes_Wheel" title="Ljubljana Marshes Wheel">Ljubljana Marshes Wheel</a> is a wooden <a href="/wiki/Wheel" title="Wheel">wheel</a> that was found in the <a href="/wiki/Ljubljana_Marshes" title="Ljubljana Marshes">Ljubljana Marshes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Slovenia_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Slovenia-6">[6]</a></sup> Radiocarbon dating showed that it is approximately 5,150 years old, which makes it the oldest wooden wheel yet discovered.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_BC" title="3000 BC">3000 BC</a>—<a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">Tin</a> is in use in Mesopotamia soon after this time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (March 2016)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Beginnings of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urbanisation" title="Urbanisation">urbanisation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.</li>
<li>First <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writings</a> in the cities of <a href="/wiki/Uruk" title="Uruk">Uruk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Susa" title="Susa">Susa</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuneiform_(script)" title="Cuneiform (script)">cuneiform</a> writings). <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_hieroglyphs" title="Egyptian hieroglyphs">Hieroglyphs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurgan_culture" title="Kurgan culture">Kurgan culture</a> of what is now <a href="/wiki/Southern_Federal_District" title="Southern Federal District">Southern Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>; possibly the first <a href="/wiki/Domestication_of_the_horse" title="Domestication of the horse">domestication of the horse</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sail" title="Sail">Sails</a> used in the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a>.</li>
<li>Construction in England of the <a href="/wiki/Sweet_Track" title="Sweet Track">Sweet Track</a>, the World's first known engineered roadway.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Drainage" title="Drainage">Drainage</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sewage_collection_and_disposal" title="Sewage collection and disposal">Sewage collection and disposal</a> created in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_civilization" title="Indus Valley civilization">Indus Valley civilization</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dam" title="Dam">Dams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">canals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stone_sculpture" title="Stone sculpture">stone sculptures</a> using <a href="/wiki/Inclined_plane" title="Inclined plane">inclined plane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lever" title="Lever">lever</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumer</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">Copper</a> was in use, both as tools and weapons.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">Bronze</a> was in use, specifically by the <a href="/wiki/Maykop_culture" title="Maykop culture">Maykop culture</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mastaba" title="Mastaba">Mastabas</a>, the predecessors of the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_pyramids" title="Egyptian pyramids">Egyptian pyramids</a>.</li>
<li>The earliest phase of the <a href="/wiki/Stonehenge" title="Stonehenge">Stonehenge</a> monument (a circular earth bank and ditch) dates to c. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3100_BC" title="3100 BC">3100 BC</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9ide_Fields" title="Céide Fields">Céide Fields</a> in Ireland, arguably the oldest field system in world, are developed.</li>
<li>Sumerian writing, done on clay tablets, shows about 2,000 pictographic signs.</li>
<li>White painted <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">pottery</a> in Egypt and southeastern Europe.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harp" title="Harp">Harps</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">flutes</a> played in Egypt.</li>
<li>Copper alloys used by Egyptians and Sumerians; smelting of gold and silver known.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lyre" title="Lyre">Lyres</a> and double clarinets (<a href="/wiki/Arghul" title="Arghul">arghul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mijwiz" title="Mijwiz">mijwiz</a>) played in Egypt.</li>
<li>Earliest known <a href="/wiki/Numeral_system" title="Numeral system">numerals</a> in Egypt.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Linen" title="Linen">Linen</a> is produced in the Middle East.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Calendars_and_chronology">Calendars and chronology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=4th_millennium_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Calendars and chronology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Maya_calendar" title="Maya calendar">Maya calendar</a> dates the <a href="/wiki/Creation_myth" title="Creation myth">creation</a> of the Earth to August 11 or August 13, 3114 BC (establishing that date as day zero of the <a href="/wiki/Maya_calendar" title="Maya calendar">Long Count</a> 13.0.0.0.0).</li>
<li>According to calculations of <a href="/wiki/Aryabhata" title="Aryabhata">Aryabhata</a> (6th century), the <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hindu</a> <a href="/wiki/Kali_Yuga" title="Kali Yuga">Kali Yuga</a> began at midnight (00:00) on 18 February 3102 BC. Consequently, Aryabhata dates the events of the <a href="/wiki/Mahabharata" title="Mahabharata">Mahabharata</a> to around 3137 BC.</li>
<li>7 October 3761 BC—<a href="/wiki/Epoch_(reference_date)" title="Epoch (reference date)">Epoch</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hebrew_Calendar" title="Hebrew Calendar">Hebrew Calendar</a> (introduced in the 12th century).</li>
<li>3929 BC—Creation according to <a href="/wiki/John_Lightfoot" title="John Lightfoot">John Lightfoot</a> based on the Old Testament of the Bible, and often associated with the <a href="/wiki/Ussher_chronology#Lightfoot.27s_Creation" title="Ussher chronology">Ussher chronology</a>.</li>
<li>1 January 4000 BC—<a href="/wiki/Epoch_(reference_date)" title="Epoch (reference date)">Epoch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Freemasonry" title="Freemasonry">Masonic</a> calendar's <a href="/wiki/Anno_Lucis" title="Anno Lucis">Anno Lucis</a> era.</li>
</ul>
