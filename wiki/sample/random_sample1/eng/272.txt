<p>The <b>United States Senate elections, 1980</b> coincided with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a>'s victory in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._presidential_election,_1980" title="U.S. presidential election, 1980">presidential election</a>. Reagan's large margin of victory over incumbent <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a> <a href="/wiki/Reagan%27s_coattails" title="Reagan's coattails">pulled in</a> many <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> voters and gave a huge boost to <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> Senate candidates.</p>
<p>The Republicans gained a net of 12 seats from the Democrats and gained control of the Senate, 53–46–1. Majority and minority leaders <a href="/wiki/Robert_Byrd" title="Robert Byrd">Robert Byrd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Howard_Baker" title="Howard Baker">Howard Baker</a> exchanged places. This election marked the first time since <a href="/wiki/United_States_elections,_1954" title="United States elections, 1954">1954</a> that the Republican Party controlled either house of Congress. Despite large gains, Republican Senate candidates actually lost the popular vote.</p>
<p>This was the largest Senate swing since <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1958" title="United States Senate elections, 1958">1958</a>, and was the largest Republican gain since <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1946" title="United States Senate elections, 1946">1946</a>, when Republicans also picked up 12 seats.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gains_and_losses">Gains and losses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Senate_elections,_1980&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gains and losses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Without losing any seats, the Republicans took open seats in <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_Senators_from_Alabama" title="List of United States Senators from Alabama">Alabama</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_Senators_from_Alaska" title="List of United States Senators from Alaska">Alaska</a>, and <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_Senators_from_Florida" title="List of United States Senators from Florida">Florida</a>, and unseated nine incumbents: <a href="/wiki/Herman_Talmadge" title="Herman Talmadge">Herman Talmadge</a> (D-GA), <a href="/wiki/Frank_Church" title="Frank Church">Frank Church</a> (D-ID), <a href="/wiki/Birch_Bayh" title="Birch Bayh">Birch E. Bayh II</a> (D-IN), <a href="/wiki/John_Culver" title="John Culver">John Culver</a> (D-IA), <a href="/wiki/John_A._Durkin" title="John A. Durkin">John A. Durkin</a> (D-NH), <a href="/wiki/Robert_Burren_Morgan" title="Robert Burren Morgan">Robert Morgan</a> (D-NC), 1972 presidential nominee <a href="/wiki/George_McGovern" title="George McGovern">George S. McGovern</a> (D-SD), <a href="/wiki/Warren_Magnuson" title="Warren Magnuson">Warren Magnuson</a> (D-WA), and <a href="/wiki/Gaylord_Nelson" title="Gaylord Nelson">Gaylord Nelson</a> (D-WI) (see <a href="/wiki/Reagan%27s_coattails" title="Reagan's coattails">Reagan's coattails</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_changes">Later changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Senate_elections,_1980&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1982, <a href="/wiki/Harrison_A._Williams" title="Harrison A. Williams">Harrison A. Williams</a> (D-NJ) resigned from the Senate rather than face a vote on his expulsion over the <a href="/wiki/Abscam" title="Abscam">Abscam</a> scandal. He was replaced by Republican <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_F._Brady" title="Nicholas F. Brady">Nicholas F. Brady</a>. This is not reflected in the party totals.</p>
