<p><b>Terence Michael Joseph</b> "<b>Geezer</b>" <b>Butler</b> (born 17 July 1949) is an English musician and songwriter. Butler is best known as the bassist<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and primary lyricist of the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath</a>. He has also recorded with <a href="/wiki/Heaven_%26_Hell_(band)" title="Heaven &amp; Hell (band)">Heaven &amp; Hell</a>, <a href="/wiki/GZR" title="GZR">GZR</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy Osbourne</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geezer_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Butler received the nickname "Geezer" at approximately age eight, because he "used to call everybody Geezer" at school. "It was just a slang term for a man." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Butler grew up in an working-class<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Irish_Catholic" title="Irish Catholic">Irish Catholic</a> family<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iommibook_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-iommibook-4">[4]</a></sup> and was heavily influenced by the writing of <a href="/wiki/Aleister_Crowley" title="Aleister Crowley">Aleister Crowley</a> as a teenager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iommibook_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-iommibook-4">[4]</a></sup> Butler formed his first band, Rare Breed, in the autumn of 1967, with <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">John "Ozzy" Osbourne</a> soon joining as lead vocalist. Butler dated a girl who lived near <a href="/wiki/Tony_Iommi" title="Tony Iommi">Tony Iommi</a>, and Iommi's earliest memories of Butler involved seeing him walking past his house in Birmingham quite often to visit her. Later, Iommi and Butler became acquainted when their bands played at a nearby nightclub.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iommibook_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-iommibook-4">[4]</a></sup> Separated for a time, Osbourne and Butler reunited in the blues foursome, Polka Tulk, along with guitarist Iommi and drummer <a href="/wiki/Bill_Ward_(musician)" title="Bill Ward (musician)">Bill Ward</a>. They renamed their band Earth, but after finding a band in the small-time English circuit with the same name, soon adopted <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath</a> in early 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Inspired by <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a>, Butler played rhythm guitar in his pre-Sabbath days, including with Rare Breed. When Sabbath was formed, Iommi made it clear that he would not want to play with another guitarist, so Butler moved to bass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ampeg_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ampeg-6">[6]</a></sup> Butler lists <a href="/wiki/Jack_Bruce" title="Jack Bruce">Jack Bruce</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cream_(band)" title="Cream (band)">Cream</a> as his biggest influence as a bassist. Iommi described Butler as being "from another planet" in the band's early days; he took <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LSD" title="LSD">LSD</a>, wore Indian hippie dresses, and was very peaceful.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iommibook_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-iommibook-4">[4]</a></sup> At the time Black Sabbath was formed, Butler was studying to become an accountant, and this training resulted in him managing the band's finances in the early days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iommibook_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-iommibook-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Butler briefly left Black Sabbath during the recording of their 1980 album <i><a href="/wiki/Heaven_and_Hell_(Black_Sabbath_album)" title="Heaven and Hell (Black Sabbath album)">Heaven and Hell</a></i> to deal with personal problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotten_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-hotten-7">[7]</a></sup> He again left the band in 1984 after touring in support of their 1983 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Born_Again_(Black_Sabbath_album)" title="Born Again (Black Sabbath album)">Born Again</a></i>. In 1988 he joined his former Sabbath bandmate Osbourne to take part in the <i><a href="/wiki/No_Rest_for_the_Wicked_(Ozzy_Osbourne_album)" title="No Rest for the Wicked (Ozzy Osbourne album)">No Rest for the Wicked</a></i> World Tour. Butler re-joined Black Sabbath in 1991 for the reunion of the <i><a href="/wiki/Mob_Rules_(album)" title="Mob Rules (album)">Mob Rules</a></i> line-up, but again quit the group after the <i><a href="/wiki/Cross_Purposes" title="Cross Purposes">Cross Purposes</a></i> tour in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1995 Butler joined with Osbourne to play on the <i><a href="/wiki/Ozzmosis" title="Ozzmosis">Ozzmosis</a></i> album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-black_science_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-black_science-8">[8]</a></sup> After recording <i>Ozzmosis</i>, he formed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G/Z/R" title="G/Z/R">G/Z/R</a>, issuing <i>Plastic Planet</i> in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plastic_planet_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-plastic_planet-9">[9]</a></sup> His next solo album, <i>Black Science,</i> followed in 1997. Butler returned to Sabbath once more for the 1997 edition of <a href="/wiki/Ozzfest" title="Ozzfest">Ozzfest</a>, and has remained with the band since. In 2005 he released <i><a href="/wiki/Ohmwork" title="Ohmwork">Ohmwork</a></i>, his third solo album. In October 2006 it was announced that Butler, along with <a href="/wiki/Tony_Iommi" title="Tony Iommi">Tony Iommi</a>, would be reforming the <i><a href="/wiki/Dehumanizer" title="Dehumanizer">Dehumanizer</a></i>-era Black Sabbath line-up with <a href="/wiki/Vinny_Appice" title="Vinny Appice">Vinny Appice</a> and Ronnie James Dio, under the name <a href="/wiki/Heaven_%26_Hell_(band)" title="Heaven &amp; Hell (band)">Heaven &amp; Hell</a> to differentiate between the reunited touring band fronted by Osbourne, and the current Sabbath line-up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geezer_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Butler is married to Gloria Butler, who managed <a href="/wiki/Heaven_%26_Hell_(band)" title="Heaven &amp; Hell (band)">Heaven &amp; Hell</a>. He also shares his Los Angeles home with several cats, of whom he has posted pictures on his website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His son, Biff Butler, was the frontman in the <a href="/wiki/Nu_metal" title="Nu metal">nu metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Apartment_26" title="Apartment 26">Apartment 26</a>. Butler's other son James is a graduate of Oxford and resides in London.</p>
<p>Butler is a life long supporter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_Football_Club" title="Aston Villa Football Club">Aston Villa Football Club</a>, and during Black Sabbath's induction to the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a>, Butler is heard shouting "Up the Villa" as the members of the band left the stage.</p>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy Osbourne</a>'s autobiography <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Ozzy" title="I Am Ozzy">I Am Ozzy</a></i>, Geezer "never uses foul language." An avowed <a href="/wiki/Veganism" title="Veganism">vegan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guitarworld_peta_ad_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-guitarworld_peta_ad-12">[12]</a></sup> Butler appeared in an advertisement for <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals</a> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guitarworld_peta_ad_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-guitarworld_peta_ad-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>He urged fans to boycott <a href="/wiki/Fortnum_%26_Mason" title="Fortnum &amp; Mason">Fortnum and Mason's</a> until they remove <a href="/wiki/Foie_gras" title="Foie gras">foie gras</a> from their shelves. Butler said,"I've seen some outrageous things in my time, but watching those poor birds suffer simply so that their diseased livers can be sold on your shop floor is horrific!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2015, Butler was briefly detained after a bar brawl in Death Valley, California and charged with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Misdemeanor_assault" title="Misdemeanor assault">misdemeanor assault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_intoxication" title="Public intoxication">public intoxication</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vandalism" title="Vandalism">vandalism</a>. He was released following detoxification and a citation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_legacy">Style and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geezer_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Style and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Butler is noted as being one of the first bassists to use a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wah_pedal" title="Wah pedal">wah pedal</a> and to down-tune his instrument (from the standard E-A-D-G to the lower C#-F#-B-E), as exemplified on Black Sabbath's <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_Reality" title="Master of Reality">Master of Reality</a></i> album, to match Iommi who had started tuning his guitar to C# (a minor third down).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ampeg_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ampeg-6">[6]</a></sup> During the band's Ozzy Osbourne era, Butler wrote almost all of the band's lyrics, drawing heavily upon his fascination with religion, science-fiction, fantasy and horror, and musings on the darker side of human nature that posed a constant threat of global annihilation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Butler is regarded as one of the most influential bassists in <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a>. <a href="/wiki/Billy_Sheehan" title="Billy Sheehan">Billy Sheehan</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr._Big_(band)" title="Mr. Big (band)">Mr. Big</a> said: "He’s a founding father of a whole genre of music and a man who really set the bar early on to be such an integral part of the sound and song structure of Sabbath".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire-15">[15]</a></sup> In Mick Wall's biography of <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a> entitled <i>Run to the Hills</i>, founder <a href="/wiki/Steve_Harris_(musician)" title="Steve Harris (musician)">Steve Harris</a> recalls: "I distinctly remember trying to play along to Black Sabbath's "Paranoid" – at first I just could not get it. I threw the guitar on my bed and walked out in a huff, but the next day I came back, picked it up and played it all the way through note-for-note! Once I got going, I started getting into bass-lines with a bit more subtlety to them...".</p>
<p>Former <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> bassist <a href="/wiki/Jason_Newsted" title="Jason Newsted">Jason Newsted</a>, who defined him his "number 1 influence", stated: "All true metal bassists look up to Geezer as a pioneer and Godfather of our chosen instrument. The best, ever".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rex_Brown" title="Rex Brown">Rex Brown</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kill_Devil_Hill_(band)" title="Kill Devil Hill (band)">Kill Devil Hill</a> asserted: "He’s a legend. He’s everything. Geezer is so much of an influence on me.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire-15">[15]</a></sup> Other bassists such as <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Burton" title="Cliff Burton">Cliff Burton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Les_Claypool" title="Les Claypool">Les Claypool</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_DiGiorgio" title="Steve DiGiorgio">Steve DiGiorgio</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alex_Webster" title="Alex Webster">Alex Webster</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Myung" title="John Myung">John Myung</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Lee_Middleton" title="Johnny Lee Middleton">Johnny Lee Middleton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Greg_Smith_(American_musician)" title="Greg Smith (American musician)">Greg Smith</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> cited Butler as a significant influence on their style.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geezer_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Butler currently endorses <a href="/wiki/Lakland" title="Lakland">Lakland</a> basses and has his own signature model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lakland_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-lakland-24">[24]</a></sup> For amplification, Butler is endorsed by <a href="/wiki/Hartke_Systems" title="Hartke Systems">Hartke</a> bass amplification, <i>Kilo</i> bass head and 4X10 HyDrive cabinet. In the past, he has been known to use Ampeg SVT &amp; B-15 bass amps and Fender, Dan Armstrong Plexi, Rickenbacker, Yamaha BB, Vigier and B.C. Rich Basses.</p>
