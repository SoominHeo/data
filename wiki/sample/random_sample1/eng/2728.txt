<p><b>Kikuchi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">菊池郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kikuchi-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kumamoto_Prefecture" title="Kumamoto Prefecture">Kumamoto Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koshi,_Kumamoto" title="Koshi, Kumamoto">Koshi</a> merger (but with 2003 population statistics), the district has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 58,300 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 427 persons per square kilometer. The total area is 136.66 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers">Mergers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kikuchi_District,_Kumamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dd><i>See <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merger_and_dissolution_of_municipalities_of_Japan" title="Merger and dissolution of municipalities of Japan">merger and dissolution of municipalities of Japan</a>.</i></dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>On March 22, 2005 the towns of <a href="/wiki/Shichijo,_Kumamoto" title="Shichijo, Kumamoto">Shichijō</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shisui,_Kumamoto" title="Shisui, Kumamoto">Shisui</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Kyokushi,_Kumamoto" title="Kyokushi, Kumamoto">Kyokushi</a> merged into the expanded <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kikuchi,_Kumamoto" title="Kikuchi, Kumamoto">Kikuchi</a>.</li>
<li>On February 27, 2006 the towns of <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dshi,_Kumamoto" title="Kōshi, Kumamoto">Kōshi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nishigoshi,_Kumamoto" title="Nishigoshi, Kumamoto">Nishigōshi</a> merged to form the new city of <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dshi,_Kumamoto" title="Kōshi, Kumamoto">Kōshi</a>.</li>
</ul>
