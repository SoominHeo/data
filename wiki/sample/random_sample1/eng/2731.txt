<p><b>Kitano-chō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">北野町</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> or <b>Kitano Ijinkan</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">北野異人館</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a historical district in <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyogo_Prefecture" title="Hyogo Prefecture">Hyogo Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, which contains a number of foreign residences from the late <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji</a> and early <a href="/wiki/Taish%C5%8D_period" title="Taishō period">Taishō</a> eras of <a href="/wiki/History_of_Japan" title="History of Japan">Japanese history</a>. While the term <i>ijinkan</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">異人館</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> can refer to any foreign residence of this period in Japan, it usually refers to those of Kitano given the number and high concentration of those that remain. <i>Ijinkan</i> districts exist in other locales (notably <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hakodate" title="Hakodate">Hakodate</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nagasaki,_Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki, Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>), but due to <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">war</a> and natural disasters, these districts are not as well preserved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>While some of the houses still serve as residences, many are open to tourists, making Kitano-chō one of the principal <a href="/wiki/Tourist_attraction" title="Tourist attraction">tourist attractions</a> in Kobe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
