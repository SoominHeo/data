<p><b>Kitauwa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">北宇和郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kitauwa-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Ehime_Prefecture" title="Ehime Prefecture">Ehime Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 2004, the district has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 17,299. The total area is 340.37 km².</p>
<p>The district has two towns:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kihoku,_Ehime" title="Kihoku, Ehime">Kihoku</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Matsuno,_Ehime" title="Matsuno, Ehime">Matsuno</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kitauwa_District,_Ehime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Due to 1878 land reforms, the district was founded when it broke off from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uwa_District,_Ehime" title="Uwa District, Ehime">Uwa District</a>. (2 towns, 31 villages)</li>
<li>July 1, 1895 — The village of Tsushima broke up into the villages of Takachika and Iwamatsu. (2 towns, 32 villages)</li>
<li>July 1, 1899 — The village of Kiyomitsu broke up into the villages of Mimaki and Kiyomitsu. (2 towns, 33 villages)</li>
<li>May 1, 1917 — The village of Maruho merged into the town of Uwajima. (2 towns, 32 villages)</li>
<li>October 3, 1919 — The village of Iwamatsu gained town status. (3 towns, 31 villages)</li>
<li>August 1, 1921 — The town of Uwajima and the village of Yawata merged to form the city of Uwajima. (2 towns, 30 villages)</li>
<li>September 1, 1934 — The village of Kushima merged into the city of Uwajima. (2 towns, 29 villages)</li>
<li>February 11, 1938 — The village of Tamajiri merged into the town of Yoshida. (2 towns, 28 villages)</li>
<li>September 10, 1938 — The village of Takachika merged into the town of Iwamatsu. (2 towns, 27 villages)</li>
<li>November 10, 1940 — The village of Akeharu was renamed and gained town status to become the town of Matsumaru. (3 towns, 26 villages)</li>
<li>November 10, 1941 — The village of Asahi was renamed and gained town status to become the town of Chikanaga. (4 towns, 25 villages)</li>
<li>April 1, 1951 — Parts of the village of Kitanada merged into the town of Iwamatsu.</li>
<li>October 10, 1954 — The villages of Mima, Narutae, and Futana merged to become the town of Mima. (5 towns, 22 villages)</li>
<li>February 11, 1955 — The villages of Kiyomitsu, Mimami, Kitanada, Hataji, Shimonada the town of Iwamatsu merged to form the town of Tsushima. (5 towns, 17 villages)</li>
<li>March 1, 1955 — The villages of Tachima, Kisakata, and Okuna merged into the town of Yoshida. (5 towns, 14 villages)</li>
<li>March 31, 1955
<ul>
<li>Parts of the village of Takamitsu and the village of Tamatsu from <a href="/wiki/Higashiuwa_District,_Ehime" title="Higashiuwa District, Ehime">Higashiuwa District</a> merged into the town of Yoshida.</li>
<li>The villages of Takamitsu (excluding parts) and Miura merged into the city of Uwajima. (5 towns, 12 villages)</li>
<li>The villages of Yoshifuji, Aiji, Izumi, Mishima, and the town of Chikanaga merged to become the town of Hiromi. (5 towns, 8 villages)</li>
<li>The village of Yoshinobu and the town of Matsumaru merged to become the town of <a href="/wiki/Matsuno,_Ehime" title="Matsuno, Ehime">Matsuno</a>. (5 towns, 7 villages)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>January 1, 1957 — The village of Kuno merged into the city of Uwajima. (5 towns, 6 villages)</li>
<li>April 1, 1958 — The villages of Shitaba, Komobuchi, Yusu, Tojima and Hiburijima merged to become the village of Uwaumi. (5 towns, 2 villages)</li>
<li>August 1, 1958 — Parts of the town of Hiromi merged into the town of Mima.</li>
<li>April 1, 1974 — The village of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Uwaumi,_Ehime&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Uwaumi, Ehime (page does not exist)">Uwaumi</a> merged into the city of Uwajima. (5 towns, 1 village)</li>
<li>January 1, 2005 — The town of <a href="/wiki/Hiromi,_Ehime" title="Hiromi, Ehime">Hiromi</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/Hiyoshi,_Ehime" title="Hiyoshi, Ehime">Hiyoshi</a> merged to become the town of <a href="/wiki/Kihoku,_Ehime" title="Kihoku, Ehime">Kihoku</a>. (5 towns)</li>
<li>August 1, 2005 — The towns of <a href="/wiki/Mima,_Ehime" title="Mima, Ehime">Mima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tsushima,_Ehime" title="Tsushima, Ehime">Tsushima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoshida,_Ehime" title="Yoshida, Ehime">Yoshida</a> merged into the expanded city of Uwajima. (2 towns)</li>
</ul>
