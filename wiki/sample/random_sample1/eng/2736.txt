<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kitakanbara District, Niigata</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Kitakanbara</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">北蒲原郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kitakanbara-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of April 1, 2005, the district has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 13,528. The total area is 37.99 km².</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Towns_and_villages">Towns and villages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kitakanbara_District,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Towns and villages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 1, 2005 - the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakajo,_Niigata" title="Nakajo, Niigata">Nakajō</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/Kurokawa,_Niigata" title="Kurokawa, Niigata">Kurokawa</a> merged to form the city of <a href="/wiki/Tainai,_Niigata" title="Tainai, Niigata">Tainai</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Seir%C5%8D,_Niigata" title="Seirō, Niigata">Seirō</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kitakanbara_District,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote plainlinks" role="note">This list is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Lists#Incomplete_lists" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Lists">incomplete</a>; you can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kitakanbara_District,_Niigata&amp;action=edit">expanding it</a>.</div>
<ul>
<li>On January 1, 1947 - the town of <a href="/wiki/Shibata,_Niigata" title="Shibata, Niigata">Shibata</a> became a city. After it gained city status, Shibata and <a href="/wiki/Niigata,_Niigata" title="Niigata, Niigata">Niigata</a> merged to form the city known as Shibata. The surrounding areas and the district continued to lose more areas.</li>
<li>On November 1, 1970 - the town of <a href="/wiki/Toyosaka,_Niigata" title="Toyosaka, Niigata">Toyosaka</a> (part of <a href="/wiki/Niigata,_Niigata" title="Niigata, Niigata">Niigata</a> as of March 21, 2005) gained city status.</li>
<li>On July 7, 2003 - the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyoura,_Niigata" title="Toyoura, Niigata">Toyoura</a> merged into the city of <a href="/wiki/Shibata,_Niigata" title="Shibata, Niigata">Shibata</a>.</li>
<li>On April 1, 2004 - the towns of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suibara,_Niigata" title="Suibara, Niigata">Suibara</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yasuda,_Niigata" title="Yasuda, Niigata">Yasuda</a>, and the villages of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyogase,_Niigata" title="Kyogase, Niigata">Kyogase</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sasakami,_Niigata" title="Sasakami, Niigata">Sasakami</a> merged to create the city of <a href="/wiki/Agano,_Niigata" title="Agano, Niigata">Agano</a>.</li>
<li>On May 1, 2005 - the town of <a href="/wiki/Shiunji,_Niigata" title="Shiunji, Niigata">Shiunji</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/Kajikawa,_Niigata" title="Kajikawa, Niigata">Kajikawa</a> were also merged into the city of <a href="/wiki/Shibata,_Niigata" title="Shibata, Niigata">Shibata</a>.</li>
<li>On September 1, 2005 - the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakajo,_Niigata" title="Nakajo, Niigata">Nakajō</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/Kurokawa,_Niigata" title="Kurokawa, Niigata">Kurokawa</a> merged to form the city of <a href="/wiki/Tainai,_Niigata" title="Tainai, Niigata">Tainai</a>; which left <a href="/wiki/Seir%C5%8D,_Niigata" title="Seirō, Niigata">Seirō</a> as the only town in Kitakanbara District.</li>
</ul>
