<p><b>Gitega</b> (formerly <b>Kitega</b>) is the second largest city in <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, lying east of <a href="/wiki/Bujumbura" title="Bujumbura">Bujumbura</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gitega&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Gitega_Province" title="Gitega Province">Gitega Province</a>, one of the 17 provinces of Burundi. It is the home of the Burundi's <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_Gitega" title="National Museum of Gitega">National Museum of Gitega</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Gitega" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Gitega">Archdiocese of Gitega</a>. Several <i><a href="/wiki/Karyenda" title="Karyenda">karyenda</a></i> royal drum <a href="/wiki/Sanctuary" title="Sanctuary">sanctuaries</a> are located in the area, as well as the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ibwami" title="Ibwami">ibwami</a></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_court" title="Royal court">royal court</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gitega&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 29 April 1972, <a href="/wiki/Ntare_V_of_Burundi" title="Ntare V of Burundi">Ntare V of Burundi</a>, the country's last <i><a href="/wiki/Mwami" title="Mwami">Mwami</a></i> (King), was killed in Gitega.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Capital_city">Capital city</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gitega&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Capital city">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2007, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_Burundi" title="President of Burundi">President of Burundi</a> <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Nkurunziza" title="Pierre Nkurunziza">Pierre Nkurunziza</a> announced that Burundi was planning to bring back its capital city to Gitega, saying that it is in a better location for a capital than Bujumbura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
