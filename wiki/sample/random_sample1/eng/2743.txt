<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gihon (Ryukyu)</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Gihon <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">義本</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></b> (c. 1204 - c. 1260), also known as <b>Yoshimoto</b> or as <b>Yiben</b> in Chinese,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a king<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ry%C5%ABky%C5%AB_Islands" title="Ryūkyū Islands">Ryūkyū Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gihon was the third and last of the Shunten Lineage. He succeeded his father <a href="/wiki/Shunbajunki" title="Shunbajunki">Shunbajunki</a> at the age of 44, in 1248.</p>
<p>Gihon's reign was marked by terrible disasters, including famine, epidemics, and devastating typhoons. Around 1254, he appointed a young lord by the name of <a href="/wiki/Eiso_(Ryukyu)" title="Eiso (Ryukyu)">Eiso</a> to be Regent (<i><a href="/wiki/Sessei" title="Sessei">Sessei</a></i>), and to aid in managing these disasters. When Gihon abdicated in 1259 or 1260, he "withdrew into the forest alone."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Eiso succeeded him as "king" and began a new royal lineage. The precise location, date, and circumstances of Gihon's death are unknown, though it is safe to assume he died shortly after his abdication. Local legends allege that he was last seen at the cliffs of Hedo Point <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hedo-misaki" title="Hedo-misaki">Hedo-misaki</a>, the northernmost point on <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Island" title="Okinawa Island">Okinawa Island</a>.</p>
