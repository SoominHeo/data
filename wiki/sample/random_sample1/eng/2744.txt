<p><b>Kee Wah Bakery</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">奇華餅家有限公司</span>) is a chain of <a href="/wiki/Bakery" title="Bakery">bakery</a> stores in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>), <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> (<a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>). </p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kee_Wah_Bakery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kee Wah Bakery was founded by Mr. Wong Yip Wing in 1938 as a small grocery store in a corner of Shanghai Street. A few years later World War II began and the store was forced to closed. At that time one of the employees used some simple tools and an old oil barrel as an improvised oven, and began baking pastries to sell to the boat dwellers on the harbor. This marked the beginning of Kee Wah Bakery. Since then, Kee Wah has become one of Hong Kong’s oldest bakeries, and has made its name in Chinese Wedding Cakes and Mooncakes. In 1997 the baton of the business were passed to the second generation; Mr Kevin S Wong revamped the company’s branding and product packaging by engaging renowned graphic designer Mr Suen Siu Wah to overhaul the corporate identity, product packaging, outlet interiors and staff uniforms. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Headquarter is located at Kee Wah Industrial Building, 666 Castle Peak Road, Cheung Sha Wan, with production lines; There is also a production centre in Taipo Industrial Estate which started in 2009.</p>
<p>Hong Kong actor <a href="/wiki/Eric_Tsang" title="Eric Tsang">Eric Tsang</a> is the endorser for Kee Wah Mooncake and Kee Gift Series. Taiwanese actress <a href="/wiki/Annie_Liu" title="Annie Liu">Annie Liu</a> is the endorser for Kee Wah Chinese Bridal Cakes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Kee Wah Bakery in California was established in 1985 and continues to produce freshly baked goods daily. Many well known favorites are the <a href="/wiki/Egg_tart" title="Egg tart">egg tarts</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pineapple_bun" title="Pineapple bun">pineapple buns</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaboration">Collaboration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kee_Wah_Bakery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Collaboration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, Hong Kong lifestyle retail store <a href="/wiki/Goods_of_Desire" title="Goods of Desire">G.O.D.</a> collaborated with Kee Wah Bakery to design <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mooncakes" title="Mooncakes">mooncakes</a> for the Chinese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-autumn_festival" title="Mid-autumn festival">Mid-Autumn Festival</a>. G.O.D. came up with presenting the traditional treats in the shape of <a href="/wiki/Buttocks" title="Buttocks">bottoms</a> in eight different designs, but still filled with traditional white lotus seed paste and salted yolks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Kee Wah opened its first nostalgia-themed store in Hong Kong in Ocean Park’s “Old Hong Kong” attraction. Based on Kee Wah’s flagship store in Shanghai during the 40s, the décor and ambience of the store aims to offer visitors a chance to reminiscence the past while tasting fresh baked traditional pastries and snacks. A series of co-branded products are launched which are available for sale not only inside the Park but also in other Kee Wah outlets in Hong Kong.</p>
