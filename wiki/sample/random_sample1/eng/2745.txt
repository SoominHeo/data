<p><i><b>Empress Ki</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">기황후</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">奇皇后</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Gi Hwanghu</span></i>) is a television series starring <a href="/wiki/Ha_Ji-won" title="Ha Ji-won">Ha Ji-won</a> as the titular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empress_Ki" title="Empress Ki">Empress Ki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It aired on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> from October 28, 2013 to April 29, 2014 on Mondays and Tuesdays at 21:55 for 51 episodes.</p>
<p>The early <a href="/wiki/Working_title" title="Working title">working title</a> was <i>Hwatu</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">화투</span>; lit. "Battle of Flowers") but the title was changed to prevent confusion with the similarly named <a href="/wiki/Hanafuda" title="Hanafuda">playing cards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Empress_Ki_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series revolves around Ki Seung-nyang, a <a href="/wiki/Goryeo" title="Goryeo">Goryeo</a>-born woman who ascends to power despite the restrictions of the era's class system, and later marries <a href="/wiki/Toghon_Tem%C3%BCr" title="Toghon Temür">Toghon Temür</a> to become an <a href="/wiki/Empress_Gi" title="Empress Gi">empress</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Yuan_dynasty" title="Yuan dynasty">Yuan dynasty</a>, instead of her first love, Wang Yu. The series depicts her loves and political ambitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_inaccuracy">Historical inaccuracy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Empress_Ki_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Historical inaccuracy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As Empress Ki was portrayed as a brave warrior in the drama, historians were worried that the audience would overlook the fact that the real-life Empress Ki was responsible for attacking her native land. The portrayal of the ancient kingdom of Goryeo also makes it seem weak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<div class="NavHead" style="background:transparent;text-align:left;background:#D8D8D8;">Ratings in the Philippines </div>
<div class="NavContent" style="background:transparent;text-align:left;">
<p>It aired on <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a> from October 20, 2014 to April 14, 2015, on weeknights (Monday to Thursday) at 10:00 PM <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Standard_Time" title="Philippine Standard Time">PST</a> for 100 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Each episode runs 1 hour including commercial breaks. It was pre-empted on December 31, 2014 to give way to the <i>Countdown to 2015 GMA New Year Special</i> and on April 2 to 3, 2015 to give way on the <i>Holy Week</i> programming. The entire series was dubbed in <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a>. It was the highest rated Korean drama in the Philippines for 2015. It was re-aired on <a href="/wiki/GMA_News_TV" title="GMA News TV">GMA News TV</a> from July 12, 2016 to January 20, 2017.</p>
