<p>The <b>guenons</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/ən/ 'on' in 'button'">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span>) are the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><b>Cercopithecus</b></i> of <a href="/wiki/Old_World_monkey" title="Old World monkey">Old World monkeys</a>. Not all members of this genus have the word "guenon" in their common names; also, because of changes in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_classification" title="Scientific classification">scientific classification</a>, some monkeys in other genera may have common names that include the word "guenon". Nonetheless, the use of the term guenon for monkeys of this genus is widely accepted.</p>
<p>All members of the genus are <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a>, and most are forest monkeys. Many of the species are quite local in their ranges, and some have even more local subspecies. Many are threatened or endangered because of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a>. The species currently placed in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Chlorocebus" title="Chlorocebus">Chlorocebus</a></i>, such as <a href="/wiki/Vervet_monkey" title="Vervet monkey">vervet monkeys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Green_monkey" title="Green monkey">green monkeys</a>, were formerly considered as a single species in this genus, <i>Cercopithecus aethiops</i>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a>, the word "guenon" is apparently of <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In French, <i>guenon</i> was the common name for all species and individuals, both males and females, from the genus <i>Cercopithecus</i>. In all other monkey and apes species, the French word <i>guenon</i> only designates the females.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hybrids">Hybrids</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guenon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hybrids">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
