<p>The <b>1984 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Women%27s_Championship" title="UEFA Women's Championship">European Competition for Women's Football</a></b> was won by <a href="/wiki/Sweden_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Sweden women's national football team">Sweden</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kicks_from_the_penalty_mark" title="Kicks from the penalty mark">penalties</a> against <a href="/wiki/England_women%27s_national_football_team" title="England women's national football team">England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It comprised four qualifying groups, the winner of each going through to the semi-finals which were played over two legs, home and away. As only sixteen teams took part (less than half the membership of <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> at the time), the competition could not be granted official status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Matches comprised two halves of 35 minutes, played with a size four <a href="/wiki/Football_(ball)" title="Football (ball)">football</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
