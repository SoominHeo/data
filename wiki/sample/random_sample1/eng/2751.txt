<p><b>Kintetsu Group Holdings Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">近鉄グループホールディングス株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kintetsu gurūpu hōrudeingusu kabushiki gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, referred to as Kintetsu GHD <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">近鉄GHD</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a Japanese railway <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> which primarily owns the <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Railway" title="Kintetsu Railway">Kintetsu Railway</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_World_Express" title="Kintetsu World Express">Kintetsu World Express</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Department_Store" title="Kintetsu Department Store">Kintetsu Department Store</a>, and its other 141 corporations, which are collectively known as Kintetsu Group.</p>
<p>Its subsidiaries operates tourism, real estate, and shipping companies, and has a major rail car-building operation <a href="/wiki/Kinki_Sharyo" title="Kinki Sharyo">Kinki Sharyo</a> which produces trains used in Japan, the United States, Egypt and Hong Kong.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kintetsu_Group_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kinki Nippon Railways Co., Ltd. <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">近畿日本鉄道株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kinki nippon tetsudo kabushiki gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a passenger rail transit company in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kinki_Region" title="Kinki Region">Kinki</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokai_region" title="Tokai region">Tokai regions</a>, was founded after Kansai Express Railways merged with Nankai Railways on June 1, 1944. Kinki Nippon Railways changed its legal name in English to Kintetsu Corporation on June 28, 2003. On April 1, 2015, the corporation, was restructured into a holding company, splitting its railway, real eastate, logistics and retail, and recreation service divisions. Kintetsu Corporation also changed the legal name to Kintetsu Group Holdings Co., Ltd. on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[1]</a></sup></p>
