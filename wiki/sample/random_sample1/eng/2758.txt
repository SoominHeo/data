<p><b>Gillian MacLaren Jacobs</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myfdb1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-myfdb1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born October 19, 1982)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interviewmag_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-interviewmag-3">[3]</a></sup> is an American actress. She is known for portraying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britta_Perry" title="Britta Perry">Britta Perry</a> on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Screen" title="Yahoo! Screen">Yahoo! Screen</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Community_(TV_series)" title="Community (TV series)">Community</a></i> and stars as Mickey on the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_(TV_series)" title="Love (TV series)">Love</a></i>. Jacobs has also had a recurring role on the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Girls_(TV_series)" title="Girls (TV series)">Girls</a></i> and has appeared in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Gardens_of_the_Night" title="Gardens of the Night">Gardens of the Night</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lookalike_(2014_film)" title="The Lookalike (2014 film)">The Lookalike</a></i> (2014) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don%27t_Think_Twice_(film)" title="Don't Think Twice (film)"><i>Don't Think Twice</i></a> (2016).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gillian_Jacobs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jacobs was born in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>. Her mother, Martina Magenau Jacobs, is the director of alumni relations at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Heinz_College" title="Heinz College">Heinz College</a>. Her father, William F. Jacobs Jr., was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Investment_banker" title="Investment banker">investment banker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myfdb1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-myfdb1-1">[1]</a></sup> She was raised in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mt._Lebanon,_Pennsylvania" title="Mt. Lebanon, Pennsylvania">Mt. Lebanon, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pittsburghmagazine_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-pittsburghmagazine-4">[4]</a></sup> She is of <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Her family owned the <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Koehler_Eagle_Brewery" title="Jackson Koehler Eagle Brewery">Jackson Koehler Eagle Brewery</a>, established in 1847 in <a href="/wiki/Erie,_Pennsylvania" title="Erie, Pennsylvania">Erie, Pennsylvania</a>, where her grandfather, John Martin Magenau Jr., served as President and CEO until its closure in 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jacobs began studying acting at the age of eight, and attended acting classes on Saturdays at the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Playhouse" title="Pittsburgh Playhouse">Pittsburgh Playhouse</a> while growing up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pittsburghmagazine_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-pittsburghmagazine-4">[4]</a></sup> She performed with the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Public_Theater" title="Pittsburgh Public Theater">Pittsburgh Public Theater</a>, where she was a perennial contender in the Public's <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a> Monologue Contest, leading her to be cast as <a href="/wiki/Titania" title="Titania">Titania</a> in its production of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night's Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After graduating from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mt._Lebanon_High_School" title="Mt. Lebanon High School">Mt. Lebanon High School</a> in 2000, Jacobs moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to attend the <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard School</a>, where she was a member of the Drama Division's Group 33. She graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">Bachelor of Fine Arts</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pittsburghmagazine_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-pittsburghmagazine-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gillian_Jacobs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Jacobs' first acting role came as Adele Congreve on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Book_of_Daniel_(TV_series)" title="The Book of Daniel (TV series)">The Book of Daniel</a></i>. Although she played Kim in the pilot of <i><a href="/wiki/Traveler_(TV_series)" title="Traveler (TV series)">Traveler</a></i>, the role was given to <a href="/wiki/Pascale_Hutton" title="Pascale Hutton">Pascale Hutton</a> when <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> acquired the series. Jacobs subsequently made guest appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/Fringe_(TV_series)" title="Fringe (TV series)">Fringe</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i>. In 2006, she starred in an <a href="/wiki/Off-Off-Broadway" title="Off-Off-Broadway">Off-Off-Broadway</a> production of Chris Denham's <i>cagelove</i>. While overall critical reaction to the play was negative, Jacobs earned praise in several reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> advised readers to "remember the name of Gillian Jacobs, a stunning Juilliard graduate who has the glow of a star in the making".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jacobs has appeared in theatrical productions such as <i>The Fabulous Life of a Size Zero</i> (2007), <i>A Feminine Ending</i> (2007),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and <i>The Little Flower of East Orange</i> (2008).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interviewmag_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-interviewmag-3">[3]</a></sup> Her film work includes <i><a href="/wiki/Blackbird_(2007_film)" title="Blackbird (2007 film)">Blackbird</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Choke_(film)" title="Choke (film)">Choke</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Gardens_of_the_Night" title="Gardens of the Night">Gardens of the Night</a></i> (both 2008), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Box_(2009_film)" title="The Box (2009 film)">The Box</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Think_Twice" title="Don't Think Twice">Don't Think Twice</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Brother_Nature_(film)" title="Brother Nature (film)">Brother Nature</a></i> (both 2016).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In March 2009, Jacobs joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> single-camera comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Community_(TV_series)" title="Community (TV series)">Community</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britta_Perry" title="Britta Perry">Britta Perry</a>, a high school dropout who aspires to become a psychologist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <i>Community</i> was canceled by NBC on May 9, 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and later that month, it was reported that Jacobs had landed a recurring role as Mimi-Rose Howard in the fourth season of the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Girls_(TV_series)" title="Girls (TV series)">Girls</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2014, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Screen" title="Yahoo! Screen">Yahoo! Screen</a> picked up <i>Community</i> for a sixth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It was announced on September 16, 2014 that Jacobs had been cast to star as Mickey in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> original comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_(TV_series)" title="Love (TV series)">Love</a></i>, which premiered in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2015, the documentary <i>The Queen of Code</i> about <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Rear_admiral_(United_States)" title="Rear admiral (United States)">rear admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Grace_Hopper" title="Grace Hopper">Grace Hopper</a> was directed by Jacobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Also in 2015 she co-starred in the thriller film <i>Visions</i> with <a href="/wiki/Jim_Parsons" title="Jim Parsons">Jim Parsons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_Fisher" title="Isla Fisher">Isla Fisher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anson_Mount" title="Anson Mount">Anson Mount</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gillian_Jacobs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
