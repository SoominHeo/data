<p><i><b>Castaway on the Moon</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">김씨 표류기</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Kimssi Pyoryugi</span></i>; lit. "Mr Kim's drifting experience": 표류: drifting; hanja: 漂流記) is a 2009 South Korean <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy_film" title="Romantic comedy film">romantic comedy film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hae-jun" title="Lee Hae-jun">Lee Hae-jun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is a love story between a suicidal man turned castaway on <a href="/wiki/Bamseom" title="Bamseom">Bamseom</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Han_River_(Korea)" title="Han River (Korea)">Han River</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Agoraphobia" title="Agoraphobia">agoraphobic</a> woman who is addicted to <a href="/wiki/Cyworld" title="Cyworld">Cyworld</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castaway_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim Seong-geun (<a href="/wiki/Jung_Jae-young" title="Jung Jae-young">Jung Jae-young</a>) is deep in debt and his life seems completely hopeless. He jumps off a bridge into the <a href="/wiki/Han_River_(Korea)" title="Han River (Korea)">Han River</a> and washes up on the shore of <a href="/wiki/Bamseom" title="Bamseom">Bamseom</a>, which lies directly below the bridge. After searching the island he finds it is filled mostly with vegetation and surrounded by the city but too far to shout and he can't swim. He finds a duck-shaped boat and begins to like living on the island, free of his debt and worries of city life, though it is not easy.</p>
<p>As he learns to survive on the island, his cries for help scrawled in the sand are seen by Kim Jung-yeon (<a href="/wiki/Jung_Ryeo-won" title="Jung Ryeo-won">Jung Ryeo-won</a>), an <a href="/wiki/Agoraphobia" title="Agoraphobia">agoraphobe</a> who spots him while engaging in her nightly habit of photographing the moon. They soon begin exchanging messages, with Jung-yeon venturing out of her house to throw bottled messages onto the island, and Seong-geun writing his replies in the sand.</p>
<p>A torrential storm arrives destroying Seong-geun's farm and sweeps away the possessions he has collected, following which he is found by a group of workers sent to clean up litter on the island. He boards a bus in the city where Jung-yeon, who, after overcoming her anxiety and running across the bridge to find him, manages to finally meet him.</p>
