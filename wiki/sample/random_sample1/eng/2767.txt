<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kim Kyung-cheon</h1>

<p><b>Kim Kyung-cheon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">김경천</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">金擎天</span>; June 5, 1888 – January 2, 1942) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_people" title="Korean people">Korean</a> independence activist and military leader. Several sources believe <a href="/wiki/North_Korean" title="North Korean">North Korean</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a> stole his identity after his death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1888, he was born in a rich, <a href="/wiki/Yangban" title="Yangban">Yangban</a>-traditioned family in <a href="/wiki/South_Hamgyong_Province" title="South Hamgyong Province">South Hamgyong Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pukchong_County" title="Pukchong County">Pukchong County</a>, as the fifth son of his father Kim Ding-Hyung. His original name was Kim Eung-cheon (Korean: 김응천, Chinese: 金應天). In 1909 he married You Jeong. He later entered the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army_Academy" title="Imperial Japanese Army Academy">Imperial Japanese Army Academy</a> and graduated in 1911, attaing the rank of cavalry lieutenant in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a>. In June 1919 he fled to <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a> along with Ji Cheong-cheon to join the <a href="/wiki/Korean_independence_movement" title="Korean independence movement">Korean independence movement</a>, working as a trainer but after only six months he communicated with some Korean activists and moved to <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> to fight under Kim Kyu, who was renowned for victory over a Japanese battalion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His main operation after arriving in Vladivostok was fighting off Japanese-supported Chinese militias. In this period he chose Kim Kyung-cheon as a <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonym</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a>, his troops managed to impress Red Army commanders with good discipline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In January 1923 he attended the conference of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_Provisional_Government" title="Korean Provisional Government">Korean Provisional Government</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> and decided to create a communist Korean regime which would be based in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. However, the <a href="/wiki/Communist_International" title="Communist International">Comintern</a> denied the "republic's" legitimacy as an independent entity, leading Ji to leave the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> while Kim remained. During the <a href="/wiki/Great_Purge" title="Great Purge">Great Purge</a>, Kim was arrested for protesting against <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a>'s Korean Dislocation policy and eventually died in a Soviet prison. He and <a href="/wiki/Ji_Cheong-cheon" title="Ji Cheong-cheon">Ji Cheong-cheon</a> and Shin Dong-cheon were called "3 cheons of South Manchuria" (南满三天). He was also referred to as 擎天金将軍 (경천 김장군) (literally meaning Heaven-supporting General Kim) by Koreans in Manchuria.</p>
<p>Lee Myung-young published a book <i>The Legend of Kim Il-sung</i> in 1974 in which she asserted that the original General Kim Il-sung was an Imperial Japanese Army Academy graduate.</p>
