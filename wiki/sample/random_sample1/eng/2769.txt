<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">Amateur career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Kwang-sun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim won the gold medal in the Men's <a href="/wiki/Flyweight" title="Flyweight">Flyweight</a> (51 kg) category at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. He was a two-time <a href="/wiki/Boxing_World_Cup" title="Boxing World Cup">Boxing World Cup</a> champion as well.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pro_career">Pro career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Kwang-sun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pro career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim turned pro in 1990 and after only five fights took on <a href="/wiki/Humberto_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="Humberto González">Humberto González</a> in 1992 for the <a href="/wiki/World_Boxing_Council" title="World Boxing Council">WBC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_Flyweight" title="Light Flyweight">Light Flyweight</a> title. Although he was leading in the fight, he lost in a 12th round TKO. In 1993 he took on <a href="/wiki/Michael_Carbajal" title="Michael Carbajal">Michael Carbajal</a> for the <a href="/wiki/World_Boxing_Council" title="World Boxing Council">WBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_Boxing_Federation" title="International Boxing Federation">IBF</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_Flyweight" title="Light Flyweight">Light Flyweight</a> title but was TKO'd in the 7th round. He retired after the bout with a record of 6-2-0.</p>
