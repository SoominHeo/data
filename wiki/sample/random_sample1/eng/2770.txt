<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Kwang-soo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim is a Professor and Director at the Molecular Neurobiology Laboratory, <a href="/wiki/McLean_Hospital" title="McLean Hospital">McLean Hospital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Medical_School" title="Harvard Medical School">Harvard Medical School</a>. He has over 20 year experiences to investigate molecular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neurobiology" title="Neurobiology">neurobiology</a> of <a href="/wiki/Midbrain" title="Midbrain">midbrain</a> <a href="/wiki/Dopamine" title="Dopamine">dopamine</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neuronal" title="Neuronal">neuronal</a> system in health and disease, focusing on elucidating the genetic network of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intrinsic" title="Intrinsic">intrinsic</a> signaling molecules and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrinsic" title="Extrinsic">extrinsic</a> transcription factors for development and maintenance of dopamine neurons. He is also investigating <a href="/wiki/Stem_cell" title="Stem cell">stem cell</a> biology and has pioneered to generate human induced pluripotent stem (iPS) cells by protein-based reprogramming methods and demonstrated that these protein-iPS cells can efficiently generate functional dopamine neurons. He is currently focused on translating his neuroscience and stem cell research to potential therapeutic development for brain disorders such as <a href="/wiki/Parkinson%27s_disease" title="Parkinson's disease">Parkinson's disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_disease" title="Alzheimer's disease">Alzheimer's disease</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inflammatory_diseases" title="Inflammatory diseases">inflammatory diseases</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
