<p><b>Kim Kuk-tae</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">김국태</span>; 27 August 1924 – 13 December 2013) was an elder <a href="/wiki/Apparatchik" title="Apparatchik">apparatchik</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Workers' Party of Korea">Workers' Party of Korea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ruling_party" title="Ruling party">ruling party</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>.</p>
<p>Kim was born in <a href="/wiki/North_Hamgyong_Province" title="North Hamgyong Province">North Hamgyong Province</a> as the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a>'s trusted general <a href="/wiki/Kim_Chaek" title="Kim Chaek">Kim Chaek</a>. He attended North Korea's most prestigious schools for cadres, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mangyongdae_Revolutionary_School" title="Mangyongdae Revolutionary School">Mangyongdae Revolutionary School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung_University" title="Kim Il-sung University">Kim Il-sung University</a>, and started working in the <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Workers' Party of Korea">Workers' Party of Korea</a> from the late 1940s. In 1963, he was appointed general to serve as deputy director of the General Political Bureau of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Army" title="Korean People's Army">Korean People's Army</a> to 1968, where he worked to consolidate Kim Il-sung's political control over the army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wordpress_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-wordpress-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1968 he was made an alternate member of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea">WPK Central Committee</a> (promoted to full member at the 5th Party Congress in 1970) and director of the Propaganda and Agitation Department, where he worked closely with <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-il" title="Kim Jong-il">Kim Jong-il</a>. As the propaganda department fell under the future leader's control, Kim was transferred to director of the Culture Department in 1971 and president of the Kim Il-sung Higher Party School in 1976, but then he apparently fell out of favour and was exiled as ambassador to <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wordpress_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-wordpress-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kim Kuk-tae was called back to North Korea in 1980 to arrange preparations of the <a href="/wiki/6th_Congress_of_the_Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="6th Congress of the Workers' Party of Korea">6th Party Congress</a>. He even received the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Kim_Il-sung" title="Order of Kim Il-sung">Order of Kim Il-sung</a> in 1982. He was promoted again to a number of leading positions, including director of the Propaganda Department (1983–1984), director of the Education Department (1984–1985), director of the Cadres Department (1985–1990), president of the Higher Party School (1990–1992).</p>
<p>In 1993 Kim was made secretary for party cadre affairs and director of the Cadres Department, with overall responsibility over personnel appointment and management, and was considered to be a close aide of Kim Jong-il.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He also served as chairman of the Deputies' Credential Screening Committee of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_People%27s_Assembly" title="Supreme People's Assembly">Supreme People's Assembly</a> for two terms (1998–2003, and 2009–2013), himself being a deputy since 1967. In 2010 he was transferred to chairman of the WPK Central Control Commission and promoted to the top decision-making <a href="/wiki/Politburo_of_the_Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Politburo of the Workers' Party of Korea">Politburo</a>. Although he was ranked number 7 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_and_funeral_of_Kim_Jong-il" title="Death and funeral of Kim Jong-il">committee that organized the funeral</a> of Kim Jong-il in December 2011, he kept a low profile under <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-un" title="Kim Jong-un">Kim Jong-un</a>, mainly disappearing from public after summer 2012. He died on 13 December 2013 from <a href="/wiki/Heart_failure" title="Heart failure">heart failure</a> after a five-decade career, and was <a href="/wiki/Lying_in_state" title="Lying in state">laid in state</a> in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Workers%27_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Central Workers' Hall (page does not exist)">Central Workers' Hall</a> (home of the <a href="/wiki/General_Federation_of_Trade_Unions_of_Korea" title="General Federation of Trade Unions of Korea">General Federation of Trade Unions of Korea</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">Pyongyang</a> before being buried in the <a href="/wiki/Patriotic_Martyrs%27_Cemetery" title="Patriotic Martyrs' Cemetery">Patriotic Martyrs' Cemetery</a>.</p>
