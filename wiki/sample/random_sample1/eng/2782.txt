<p><b>Kim Min-goo</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 김민구; born June 24, 1991 in <a href="/wiki/Suwon" title="Suwon">Suwon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeonggi-do" title="Gyeonggi-do">Gyeonggi-do</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> professional basketball player who currently plays for the <a href="/wiki/Jeonju_KCC_Egis" title="Jeonju KCC Egis">Jeonju KCC Egis</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Basketball_League" title="Korean Basketball League">Korean Basketball League</a> (KBL).<br/>
At the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIBA_Asia_Championship" title="2013 FIBA Asia Championship">2013 FIBA Asia Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>, he was named to the tournament's <a href="/wiki/2013_FIBA_Asia_Championship#Awards" title="2013 FIBA Asia Championship">All-Star Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Min-goo_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a freshman at <a href="/wiki/Kyung_Hee_University" title="Kyung Hee University">Kyung Hee University</a>, Kim became the team's starting <a href="/wiki/Combo_guard" title="Combo guard">guard</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> leading his team to the 2010 Korea University Basketball League Finals.</p>
<p>As a sophomore during the 2011 season Kim averaged 19.1 points, 5.9 assists and 8.4 rebounds,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mk_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mk-3">[3]</a></sup> often citing as the best guard in the college league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-universiade_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-universiade-4">[4]</a></sup> He was named the Regular Season MVP and helped his team win the national championship. In August 2011, Kim was selected for the South Korean collegiate national team to compete in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Summer_Universiade" title="2011 Summer Universiade">2011 Summer Universiade</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-universiade_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-universiade-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a junior during the 2012 season Kim averaged 22.6 points, 5.8 assists and 6.1 rebounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mk_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mk-3">[3]</a></sup> He was named the Regular Season MVP for the second year in a row and helped his team win its second national championship.</p>
<p>As a senior in 2013 Kim was selected for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korea_national_basketball_team" title="Korea national basketball team">South Korean national team</a> to compete in the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIBA_Asia_Championship" title="2013 FIBA Asia Championship">2013 FIBA Asia Championship</a>. During the competition Kim served as the backup <a href="/wiki/Shooting_guard" title="Shooting guard">shooting guard</a>, manning the position when starter <a href="/wiki/Cho_Sung-min_(basketball)" title="Cho Sung-min (basketball)">Cho Sung-Min</a> was not in the game. However, Kim averaged 12.7 points, 2.7 assists and 4.1 rebounds in only 19.9 minutes per game, and topped the entire tournament with 25 three-point field goals made in nine games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreatimes_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-koreatimes-5">[5]</a></sup> Although Team Korea failed to win the championship losing to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in the semifinal game, they clinched the slot for next year's <a href="/wiki/2014_FIBA_Basketball_World_Cup" title="2014 FIBA Basketball World Cup">FIBA World Cup</a> defeating <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a> 75-57 in the third place game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In the semifinal game against the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, Kim scored 27 points on 9-of-15 shooting overall and 5-of-11 from the three-point range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the third-place game against <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a>, Kim led Team Korea with a game-high 21 points and shot 5-of-10 from the three-point range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
