<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Bo-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cerezo_Osaka">Cerezo Osaka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Bo-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cerezo Osaka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Kim dropped out of <a href="/wiki/Hongik_University" title="Hongik University">Hongik University</a> and signed a three-year contract for <a href="/wiki/J1_League" title="J1 League">J1 League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Cerezo_Osaka" title="Cerezo Osaka">Cerezo Osaka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After he was loaned out to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League_Division_2" title="J. League Division 2">J. League Division 2</a> side <a href="/wiki/Oita_Trinita" title="Oita Trinita">Oita Trinita</a> for the 2010 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> he returned to Cerezo Osaka. During the 2011 season, Kim scored 8 goals. In 2012, while <a href="/wiki/Levir_Culpi" title="Levir Culpi">Levir Culpi</a> resigned and Cerezo Osaka appointed <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9rgio_Soares" title="Sérgio Soares">Sérgio Soares</a> as the new manager, he remained a key player for the team, scoring 7 goals in 15 matches. When he left the team to participate in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, he was the fourth leading goal scorer in <a href="/wiki/J1_League" title="J1 League">J1 League</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardiff_City">Cardiff City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Bo-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cardiff City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 July 2012, his agents confirmed that his club <a href="/wiki/Cerezo_Osaka" title="Cerezo Osaka">Cerezo Osaka</a>, had entered talks with Welsh <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> over a possible transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 12 July, <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> and Cerezo Osaka agreed to a fee believed to be around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pound_Sterling" title="Pound Sterling">£</a>2.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The deal was completed on 27 July, when Kim signed a three-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He made his debut on 18 September in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Millwall_F.C." title="Millwall F.C.">Millwall</a> coming on for <a href="/wiki/Craig_Noone" title="Craig Noone">Craig Noone</a>. Kim's first start for Cardiff came against <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> in a 4–0 win at <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_Stadium" title="Cardiff City Stadium">Cardiff City Stadium</a> on 27 October. Kim scored his first goal for Cardiff in their 4–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> on 7 December. His second was the opening goal at <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> in a 2–1 victory for Cardiff on 19 January.</p>
<p>On 17 April 2013, Cardiff City won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012-13_Football_League_Championship" title="2012-13 Football League Championship">Championship</a> title and were promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>, making Kim the 12th Premier League player from Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He scored his first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_United" title="Manchester United">Manchester United</a> on 24 November 2013, to secure a late 2–2 home draw with an injury time headed equaliser.</p>
<p>On 24 January 2015, Cardiff City announced Kim's contract had been terminated by mutual consent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wigan_Athletic">Wigan Athletic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Bo-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wigan Athletic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 6 February 2015, <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> announced that Kim agreed a contract until the end of 2014–15 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Bo-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim's international career began in 2009 as a member of the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_under-20_football_team" title="South Korea national under-20 football team">South Korea U-20</a>. He participated in the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>, scoring 2 goals in 4 matches. After the U-20 World Cup, he was selected by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Myung-Bo" title="Hong Myung-Bo">Hong Myung-Bo</a>, then the head coach of the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_under-23_football_team" title="South Korea national under-23 football team">South Korea U-23 team</a>.</p>
<p>On 9 January 2010, Kim made his first international appearance for <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a> in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Zambia_national_football_team" title="Zambia national football team">Zambia</a> and participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_World_Cup" title="2010 World Cup">2010 World Cup</a> later that year. He scored his first and second goals on 12 June 2012, in a World Cup qualifying match against <a href="/wiki/Lebanon_national_football_team" title="Lebanon national football team">Lebanon</a>.</p>
<p>Kim was included in Hong's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> and contributed to winning the bronze medal. He scored the winning goal against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> via a spectacular left-foot volley shot and helped his side advance to the knockout stage. He was also included in the team of the tournament for his outstanding performances.</p>
