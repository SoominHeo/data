<p><b>Kim Song-i</b> (born 10 August 1994) is a North Korean <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis" title="Table tennis">table tennis</a> player. As of December 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kim_Song-i&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, she is ranked 30th in the world based on ITTF rankings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kim plays a highly defensive <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis_styles" title="Table tennis styles">style</a> with plenty of slice, occasionally injecting pace with top spin strokes from the forehand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new._Birt_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-new._Birt-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 in Helsingborg she won the Women's Singles title at the Swedish Open.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She won the bronze medal for North Korea at the <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_singles" title="Table tennis at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Women's singles">2016 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_Rio_Olympics">2016 Rio Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Song-i&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2016 Rio Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the third round, she surprisingly beat Japan's <a href="/wiki/Kasumi_Ishikawa" title="Kasumi Ishikawa">Kasumi Ishikawa</a>, ranked number six in the world.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITTF_Olympic_Rankings_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITTF_Olympic_Rankings-6">[6]</a></sup> In the quarter finals, she went on to beat Singaporean <a href="/wiki/Yu_Mengyu" title="Yu Mengyu">Yu Mengyu</a>, ranked 13th, with a score of 4-2 before losing to second-ranked <a href="/wiki/Ding_Ning" title="Ding Ning">Ding Ning</a> in the semifinals with a score of 4-1. In the bronze medal match, she beat Japan's <a href="/wiki/Ai_Fukuhara" title="Ai Fukuhara">Ai Fukuhara</a> with a score of 4-1 to take the bronze medal of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_Olympics" title="2016 Olympics">2016 Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rio2016com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rio2016com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
