<p><b>Kim Soo-yong</b> (born September 23, 1929) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a>. Kim made his debut in 1958 with <i>A Henpecked Husband</i> and directed more than 100 movies through 1999 with <i>Scent of Love</i> (2000). He made many popular commercial films of the past decades, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Sad_Story_of_Self_Supporting_Child" title="Sad Story of Self Supporting Child">Sad Story of Self Supporting Child</a></i> (aka <i>Sorrow in the Heavens</i>) (1965) as well as some 50 literary movies based on popular Korean novels such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sea_Village" title="The Sea Village">The Sea Village</a></i> (1965) and <i>Mist</i> (1967).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
