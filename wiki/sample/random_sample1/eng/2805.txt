<p><b>Kim Young-kwang</b> (born January 11, 1987) is a South Korean actor and model. Kim began his entertainment career as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Model_(profession)" title="Model (profession)">model</a> and has walked the runways of world-renowned designers such as <a href="/wiki/Alexander_McQueen" title="Alexander McQueen">Alexander McQueen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vivienne_Westwood" title="Vivienne Westwood">Vivienne Westwood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Etro" title="Etro">Etro</a>. In 2008, he was the first Asian model to walk <a href="/wiki/Dior_Homme" title="Dior Homme">Dior Homme</a>'s show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As an actor, Kim has starred in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Runway_Cop" title="Runway Cop">Runway Cop</a></i> (2012) and <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Young_Bloods" title="Hot Young Bloods">Hot Young Bloods</a></i> (2014),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television dramas</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Pinocchio_(2014_TV_series)" title="Pinocchio (2014 TV series)">Pinocchio</a></i> (2014), <i><a href="/wiki/D-Day_(TV_series)" title="D-Day (TV series)">D-Day</a></i> (2015) and <i><a href="/wiki/Sweet_Stranger_and_Me" title="Sweet Stranger and Me">Sweet Stranger and Me</a></i> (2016).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Young-kwang_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim's father was a veteran of the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, who died when Kim was in the sixth grade. Kim's family struggled financially and it was rumoured that he had to start delivering newspapers at a young age to help relieve the financial burden. When he was nineteen years he randomly received an opportunity to become a model for an advertisement which opened doors for him to become a runway model and later debut as an actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12th December 2013, Kim quietly enlisted to his mandatory <a href="/wiki/Military_service" title="Military service">military service</a>. However, he only had to be enlisted for six months as a public service soldier, the reason being that his father had been a <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a> veteran, as well as an acknowledged person of national merit. Thus, as a child of a person considered to be of national merit, Kim was required to serve only six months of military service. Kim was discharged in June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Young Kwang maintains close friendship with <a href="/wiki/Lee_Soo-hyuk" title="Lee Soo-hyuk">Lee Soo-hyuk</a>, and also other fellow model-turned-actor <a href="/wiki/Kim_Woo-bin" title="Kim Woo-bin">Kim Woo-bin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Jong-hyun" title="Hong Jong-hyun">Hong Jong-hyun</a> whom he has worked with them onscreen in <i><a href="/wiki/White_Christmas_(TV_series)" title="White Christmas (TV series)">White Christmas</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
