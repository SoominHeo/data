<p><i><b>"O"-Jung.Ban.Hap.</b></i> (stylized as <b>"O"-正.反.合.</b>), sometimes referred to as <b>"O"-Union</b> or just <b>O</b>, is the third Korean studio album (fourth overall) by South Korean pop group <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">TVXQ</a>, released on September 29, 2006 by <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a>. In a year when Korean music sales had slumped, it was the highest selling album in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> in 2006 and won the group several prominent awards in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The album stayed charted within the top 50 throughout 2007. By 2014, it sold over 475,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaon2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaon2010-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaon2011_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaon2011-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaon1212_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaon1212-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaon1311_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaon1311-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaon1403_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaon1403-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album's name <i>"O"-Jung.Ban.Hap</i>, literally translated to <i>"O"-Justice.Opposition.Solution</i>, is based on <a href="/wiki/Georg_Wilhelm_Friedrich_Hegel" title="Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel">Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel</a>'s theory of <a href="/wiki/Thesis,_antithesis,_synthesis" title="Thesis, antithesis, synthesis">thesis, antithesis, synthesis</a>. The album's mononymous <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> has lyrics that regard global conflicts with a plea for peace and resistance. The album has a balance of fast tempo songs and ballads.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Versions_and_repackaging">Versions and repackaging</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%22O%22-Jung.Ban.Hap.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Versions and repackaging">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was released in 4 different versions and packaging, versions <b>A</b> and <b>B</b>, <b>C</b> and <b>D</b>. Versions A and B promote the title song """O"-正.反.合.", while versions C and D promote the fantasy theme of the song "Balloons" and contain new songs and a <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> in each. The initial versions of the album with the original track listing was released in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD" title="CD">CD</a> and <a href="/wiki/Compact_Cassette" title="Compact Cassette">cassette</a> formats.</p>
<p>For the version D, the label company did a unique box packaging, with special deck of cards with members' pictures wearing costumes included with the CD and DVD specials.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, the album was released under the names <i>"O"-Jing.Fan.Hap.</i> and <i>"O"-Zheng.Fan.He.</i> respectively, while in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> the name of the album is <i>"O"-Sei.Han.Go.</i>. The Japanese release does not contain the repackaged versions that are available in the Korean release.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%22O%22-Jung.Ban.Hap.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22O.22-Jung.Ban.Hap">"O"-Jung.Ban.Hap</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%22O%22-Jung.Ban.Hap.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title='Edit section: "O"-Jung.Ban.Hap'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The lead single is a fast-tempo fusion of <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">electronic trance</a>. The music video took several days to complete as its footage was shot in several different countries. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U-Know" title="U-Know">U-Know Yunho</a>'s footage was shot in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micky_(singer)" title="Micky (singer)">Micky Yoochun</a>'s and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Max_(singer)" title="Max (singer)">Max Changmin</a>'s in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hero_(singer)" title="Hero (singer)">Hero Jaejoong</a>'s in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xiah" title="Xiah">Xiah Junsu</a>'s in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. The music video showcases intense and unique dance choreography with stylized outfits.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Balloons">Balloons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%22O%22-Jung.Ban.Hap.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Balloons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In drastic contrast to the lead single, "Balloons" (<i>Pungseon</i> in Korean) is a <a href="/wiki/Bubblegum_pop" title="Bubblegum pop">bubbly song</a> that features TVXQ members in animal costumes, made to directly appeal to the younger generation, as well as older generations familiar with the original "Balloons" by Five Fingers (an old Korean band). The lyrics talk about innocent childhood dreams and memories and how people tend to forget the importance of those dreams as they grow older.</p>
<p>In the lyrics, "yellow balloons" line was changed to "red balloons" to coordinate with the colour that represents TVXQ's fanbase <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_(TVXQ)" title="Cassiopeia (TVXQ)">Cassiopeia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Performances">Performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%22O%22-Jung.Ban.Hap.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In three short months after the album's release, TVXQ were invited to perform on many stages of music shows, award shows, and concerts. TVXQ performed their third album's songs a few times in 2007 as well, since a fourth Korean album had not been released.</p>
