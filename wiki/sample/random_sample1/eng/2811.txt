<p><b>Kim Won-jin</b> (born August 24, 1984, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>) is a South Korean <a href="/wiki/%C3%89p%C3%A9e" title="Épée">épée</a> fencer who currently coaches at the Seoul Physical Education High School . Kim Won-jin has placed in the top 8 at many international events, winning both the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games" title="Asian Games">Asian Games</a> and Asian Championships twice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Infostrada_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Infostrada-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He won the 2006 Asian Games in the mens individual épée. He says the inspirational figure in his life is Shim Jae-sung, another South Korean épéeist.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>He fences left-handed and is 1.78 metres (5 ft 10 in) tall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Infostrada_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Infostrada-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
