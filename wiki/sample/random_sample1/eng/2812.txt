<p><b>Kim Yu-seok</b> (born September 22, 1966) is a South Korean actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He starred in the films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Power_of_Kangwon_Province" title="The Power of Kangwon Province">The Power of Kangwon Province</a></i> (1998),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Isle" title="The Isle">The Isle</a></i> (2000), <i>Possible Changes</i> (2005),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Long and Winding Road</i> (2006),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <i>Family Matters</i> (2006), as well as the <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television series</a> <i>Reservation for Love</i> (2002), <i>Thank You, My Life</i> (2006), <i>My Lovely Fool</i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/White_Lies_(TV_series)" title="White Lies (TV series)">White Lies</a></i> (2008) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_King%27s_Dream" title="The King's Dream">The King's Dream</a></i> (2012).</p>
