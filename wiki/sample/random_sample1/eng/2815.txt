<p><b>Kim Yoon-hye</b> (born May 24, 1991) is a South Korean actress. She first appeared on a 2002 magazine cover of <a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue Girl Korea</a>, and also modeled for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Asia" title="MTV Asia">MTV Asia</a> in 2005 before starring in several <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a>. She made her acting debut under the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Woori</b>, which means "us" in <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>, making it difficult for her name to be searched on internet portals. She reverted to using her real name Kim Yoon-hye during the promotions for horror-comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_Sweepers" title="Ghost Sweepers">Ghost Sweepers</a></i> in 2012. Kim next starred in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">TV dramas</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Heartstrings_(TV_series)" title="Heartstrings (TV series)">Heartstrings</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>I Need a Fairy</i> (also known as <i>Sent from Heaven</i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Flower_Boys_Next_Door" title="Flower Boys Next Door">Flower Boys Next Door</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2013, she garnered positive reviews for her portrayal of a cold and aloof high school girl in romantic thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Steel_Cold_Winter" title="Steel Cold Winter">Steel Cold Winter</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
