<p>The <b>Order of Kim Il-sung</b> is the highest <a href="/wiki/Orders_and_medals_of_North_Korea" title="Orders and medals of North Korea">order of North Korea</a>, along with the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Kim_Jong-il" title="Order of Kim Jong-il">Order of Kim Jong-il</a>, and only second to one honorary title, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hero_of_Labour" title="Hero of Labour">Hero of Labour</a>.</p>
<p>The order was instituted in 1972 during a reform of the North Korean honors system. Its history is not fully known, but the order was initially round, being changed to a five-pointed star design later, and the picture of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a> updated in 2012.</p>
<p>Recipients can be individuals or organizations, who have contributed to the cause of <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communism</a>. It is traditionally awarded on 15 April, the <a href="/wiki/Day_of_the_Sun" title="Day of the Sun">Day of the Sun</a>, the birthday of Kim Il-sung. Relatively few are awarded, totaling at least 600, to highlight the high symbolic status of the order. Recipients include <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-il" title="Kim Jong-il">Kim Jong-il</a>, who received it four times. He was supposed to be the recipient of the first award in 1972, but according to North Korean sources, he initially refused.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Order_of_Kim_Il-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The North Korean system of orders and medals saw periods of expansion and stagnation through the 1950s and 1960s, but in the early 1970s, major additions were made. Of these, the most important one was the addition of the Order of Kim Il-sung to the list of titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weiser2016_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Weiser2016-1">[1]</a></sup> The order was instituted on 20 March 1972<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENorth_Korea_Handbook2002960_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENorth_Korea_Handbook2002960-2">[2]</a></sup> on the occasion of the 60th <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birthday_of_Kim_Il-sung" title="Birthday of Kim Il-sung">birthday of Kim Il-sung</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38no_Here_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-38no_Here-3">[3]</a></sup> At about the same time, North Korea also started awarding watches with Kim Il-sung's autograph on them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weiser2016_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Weiser2016-1">[1]</a></sup> According to North Korean sources, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-il" title="Kim Jong-il">Kim Jong-il</a> was to be the first recipient of the order, but he declined it and received the honor only in 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38no_Here_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-38no_Here-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, all orders bearing the picture of Kim Il-sung, including the Order of Kim Il-sung, were redesigned with a newer picture of Kim. At some point before that, the order had become a five-pointed star, being formerly round. It is possible that all old versions were recalled and changed to the new one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weiser2016_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Weiser2016-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eligibility">Eligibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Order_of_Kim_Il-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Eligibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The order is traditionally awarded annually on 15 April, the birthday of Kim Il-sung.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seoul2002_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Seoul2002-4">[4]</a></sup> It is awarded for "outstanding services to the Republic of the Korean nation and communism".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Order_of_Kim_Il_Sung_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Order_of_Kim_Il_Sung-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Out of all North Korean orders, it is awarded the most sparingly, reflecting its high symbolic status. At least 600 have been awarded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weiser2016_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Weiser2016-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Precedence">Precedence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Order_of_Kim_Il-sung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Precedence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The order is the highest of the <a href="/wiki/Orders_and_medals_of_North_Korea" title="Orders and medals of North Korea">order of North Korea</a>, along with the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Kim_Jong-il" title="Order of Kim Jong-il">Order of Kim Jong-il</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Order_of_Kim_Jong_Il_Instituted_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Order_of_Kim_Jong_Il_Instituted-6">[6]</a></sup> although out of titles the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hero_of_Labour" title="Hero of Labour">Hero of Labour</a> is considered higher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weiser2016_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Weiser2016-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_National_Flag" title="Order of the National Flag">Order of the National Flag</a> is one rank lower.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seoul2002_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Seoul2002-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are related prices called the <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung_Prize" title="Kim Il-sung Prize">Kim Il-sung Prize</a>, as well as prizes for youth and children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weiser2016_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Weiser2016-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
