<p><b>Kim Il-Jin</b> (born 1956, <a href="/wiki/Manpo" title="Manpo">Manpo</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>) is a North Korean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conductor_(music)" title="Conductor (music)">conductor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was born in Manpo, a city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jagang" title="Jagang">Jagang</a> Province, but he grew up mainly in <a href="/wiki/Wonsan" title="Wonsan">Wonsan</a>. He studied <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cello</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang_University_of_Music_and_Dance" title="Pyongyang University of Music and Dance">Pyongyang University of Music and Dance</a>.</p>
<p>After graduating in 1977, Kim joined the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mansudae_Arts_Troupe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mansudae Arts Troupe (page does not exist)">Mansudae Arts Troupe</a> as a cellist but he turned to conducting shortly after. He went to <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and studied conducting at the <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Conservatory" title="Moscow Conservatory">Moscow Conservatory</a>.</p>
<p>In 1985, Kim participated in the <a href="/wiki/Herbert_von_Karajan" title="Herbert von Karajan">Herbert von Karajan</a> International Conductors Competition in <a href="/wiki/West_Berlin" title="West Berlin">West Berlin</a> and took second prize. He is the only North Korean to have won this competition.</p>
<p>After returning to North Korea, Kim rejoined the Mansudae Arts Troupe as a conductor. He also rehearsed the Troupe's famous Merited Female Instrumental Ensemble and founded the <a href="/wiki/Isang_Yun_Orchestra" title="Isang Yun Orchestra">Isang Yun Orchestra</a> in 1990.</p>
<p>Kim received a title of '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merited_Artist" title="Merited Artist">Merited Artist</a>' in 1988.</p>
