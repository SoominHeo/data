<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Korean_name" title="Korean name">Korean name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Korean_family_names" title="List of Korean family names">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Kim_(Korean_surname)" title="Kim (Korean surname)"><i>Kim</i></a>.</div>
<p><b>Kim Jong-chul</b> (born 25 September 1981) is the son of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-il" title="Kim Jong-il">Kim Jong-il</a>, the former leader of <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> (Democratic People's Republic of Korea). His older half-brother is <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-nam" title="Kim Jong-nam">Kim Jong-nam</a>. His younger brother is <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-un" title="Kim Jong-un">Kim Jong-un</a>, now the leader of North Korea.</p>
<p>In 2007, Jong-chul was appointed deputy chief of a leadership division of the Workers' Party. However, on 15 January 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Yonhap" title="Yonhap">South Korean News Agency</a> reported that Kim Jong-il appointed his youngest son, Jong-un, to be his successor, passing over Jong-nam and Jong-chul.</p>
<p>These reports were supported in April 2009 when Kim Jong-un assumed a low-level position within the ruling <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_Workers%27_Party" title="Korean Workers' Party">Workers' Party of Korea</a>, since Kim Jong-il was groomed by his own father, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a>, in a similar way before becoming North Korean leader in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Son_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Son-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Jong-chul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim Jong-chul was born in 1981. He is the middle son of Kim Jong-il and companion <a href="/wiki/Ko_Yong-hui" title="Ko Yong-hui">Ko Yong-hui</a>, who died in 2004. Until 2001, it was assumed that Kim Jong-il's eventual heir would be his eldest son, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-nam" title="Kim Jong-nam">Kim Jong-nam</a>, whose mother was <a href="/wiki/Song_Hye-rim" title="Song Hye-rim">Song Hye-rim</a>. But in May 2001, Kim Jong-nam was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Jong_Nam#2001_incident" title="Kim Jong Nam">arrested</a> at <a href="/wiki/Narita_International_Airport" title="Narita International Airport">New Tokyo International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, travelling on a forged <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic_passport" title="Dominican Republic passport">Dominican Republic passport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2007_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2007-2">[2]</a></sup> He was held and then deported to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. The incident caused Kim Jong-il to cancel a planned visit to China because of the embarrassment to both countries. As a result of this incident, Kim Jong-nam had fallen from favour. He was later reported to be living in <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heir_apparent">Heir apparent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Jong-chul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Heir apparent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2003, moves began to raise the profile of Kim Jong-chul. The <a href="/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Army" title="Korean People's Army">Korean People's Army</a> began a propaganda campaign using the slogan "The Respected Mother is the Most Faithful and Loyal Subject to the Dear Leader Comrade Supreme Commander". Since the "Respected Mother" was described as "‍[devoting] herself to the personal safety of the comrade supreme commander", and "‍[assisting] the comrade supreme commander nearest to his body", Western analysts assume that the "Respected Mother" was <a href="/wiki/Ko_Yong-hui" title="Ko Yong-hui">Ko Yong-hui</a>, mother of Kim Jong-chul and Kim Jong-un.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity.org_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity.org-3">[3]</a></sup> A similar campaign was launched in praise of Kim Jong-il's mother during the later years of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a>'s life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity.org_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity.org-3">[3]</a></sup> This suggested that Kim Jong-chul, despite his youth, had emerged with Army backing to be a serious contender to succeed his father.</p>
<p>However, <a href="/wiki/Kenji_Fujimoto" title="Kenji Fujimoto">Kenji Fujimoto</a>, Kim Jong-il's personal <a href="/wiki/Sushi" title="Sushi">sushi</a> chef, wrote in his memoir, <i>I Was Kim Jong-il's Cook</i>, that Kim Jong-il thought Jong-chul was "no good because he is like a little girl". Fujimoto believed Kim Jong-il favoured his youngest son, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-un" title="Kim Jong-un">Kim Jong-un</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 June 2009, it was reported that Kim Jong-chul had been passed over as his younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-un" title="Kim Jong-un">Kim Jong-un</a>, was to succeed his father as the head of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_Workers%27_Party" title="Korean Workers' Party">Korean Workers' Party</a> and <i>de facto</i> head of state of North Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-successor_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-successor-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.93present">2011–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Jong-chul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim Jong-chul was reportedly spotted in Singapore on 14 February 2011, where he was attending an <a href="/wiki/Eric_Clapton" title="Eric Clapton">Eric Clapton</a> concert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In late 2011, his father died and his younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-un" title="Kim Jong-un">Kim Jong-un</a>, succeeded his father as the head of state. He was again apparently spotted attending two additional Clapton concerts on successive days at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> in London, in May 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Lee Yun-keol (as reported by <i><a href="/wiki/Wen_Wei_Po" title="Wen Wei Po">Wen Wei Po</a></i>), chairman of the North Korea Strategy Information Service Center, Kim Jong-chul personally led the arrest of his uncle <a href="/wiki/Jang_Sung-taek" title="Jang Sung-taek">Jang Sung-taek</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WCN_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-WCN-8">[8]</a></sup> Some analysts believe that this signals an expanded role for Kim Jong-chul in the North Korean regime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
