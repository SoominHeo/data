<p><a href="/wiki/Daejang" title="Daejang">General</a> (Ret.) <b>Kim Tae-young</b>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Army" title="Republic of Korea Army">ROKA</a>, was the 34th Chairman of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joint_Chiefs_of_Staff_(Republic_of_Korea)" title="Joint Chiefs of Staff (Republic of Korea)">Joint Chiefs of Staff</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_South_Korea" title="Military of South Korea">Republic of Korea Armed Forces</a> and 42nd Republic of Korea Minister of National Defense.</p>
<p>General Kim graduated from the Republic of Korea Military Academy in 1972. He previously served as Commanding General, 6th Artillery Brigade (1997–1998), Commanding General, 35th Infantry Division (1999–2000), Commanding General, Capital Defense Command (2004–2005), and Chief Director, Joint Operations Headquarters, Joint Chiefs of Staff (2005–2006).</p>
<p>Prior to assuming the position of Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff in 2008, he was Commanding General of the First ROK Army.</p>
<p>In September 2009, he replaced Lee Sang-Hee as the 42nd Republic of Korea Minister of National Defense.</p>
<p>Despite being considered as a good military officer with his skill from both inside &amp; outside of the R.O.K. Military, he started to suffer strong criticism after the <a href="/wiki/ROKS_Cheonan_sinking" title="ROKS Cheonan sinking">sinking of ROKS Cheonan</a>. On May 2010, he offered his resignation to President <a href="/wiki/Lee_Myung-bak" title="Lee Myung-bak">Lee Myung-bak</a>, although it wasn't accepted.</p>
<p>On 25 November 2010, he stepped down from his post as Minister of Defense in the wake of criticism he received for his handling of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shelling_of_Yeonpyeong" title="Shelling of Yeonpyeong">shelling of Yeonpyeong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The next day it was announced that his successor would be <a href="/wiki/Kim_Kwan-jin" title="Kim Kwan-jin">Kim Kwan-Jin</a>, his JCS Chairmanship predecessor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
