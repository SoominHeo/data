<p><i><b>...ing</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">아이엔지</span>) is a 2003 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> film starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Im_Soo-jung_(actress)" title="Im Soo-jung (actress)">Im Soo-jung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Rae-won" title="Kim Rae-won">Kim Rae-won</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Mi-sook" title="Lee Mi-sook">Lee Mi-sook</a> and directed by Lee Eon-hee. The title refers to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Present_continuous_tense" title="Present continuous tense">present continuous tense</a> in English.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...ing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Min-ah (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Im_Soo-jung_(actress)" title="Im Soo-jung (actress)">Im Soo-jung</a>) is a young woman who has become reserved and aloof to the world as a result of her chronic illness and deformed hand. The film quietly portrays the unconventional, yet endearing relationship between Min-ah and her mother Mi-sook (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Mi-sook" title="Lee Mi-sook">Lee Mi-sook</a>), as well as Min-ah's development as she is befriended by the high-spirited and carefree photographer Young-jae (<a href="/wiki/Kim_Rae-won" title="Kim Rae-won">Kim Rae-won</a>) who moves into their apartment complex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
