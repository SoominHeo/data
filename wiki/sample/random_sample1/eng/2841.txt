<p><b>Kim Hyun-ki</b> (born February 9, 1983) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ski_jumper" title="Ski jumper">ski jumper</a> who has competed since 1998.</p>
<p>Competing in four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympics</a>, he earned his best finish of eighth in the team large hill event at <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a> in <a href="/wiki/2002_Winter_Olympics" title="2002 Winter Olympics">2002</a> while his best individual finish was 31st in the individual large hill event at those same games.</p>
<p>Kim's best finish at the <a href="/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships" title="FIS Nordic World Ski Championships">FIS Nordic World Ski Championships</a> was tenth in the team normal hill event at <a href="/wiki/Oberstdorf" title="Oberstdorf">Oberstdorf</a> in <a href="/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_2005" title="FIS Nordic World Ski Championships 2005">2005</a> while his best individual finish was 38th in the individual normal hill at those same games. He finished 30th in the individual event of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIS_Ski-Flying_World_Championships_2006" title="FIS Ski-Flying World Championships 2006">FIS Ski-Flying World Championships 2006</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kulm_(venue)" title="Kulm (venue)">Kulm</a>.</p>
<p>Kim's best individual World Cup finish was 21st in a large hill event in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> in 2006. He has two individual victories in lesser events from 2009.</p>
<p>He holds negative record for highest amount of fallouts in qualifications in World Cup with 101 fallouts.</p>
