<p><b>Kim Ho-sun</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">김호선</span>; born 9 March 1941) is a South Korean <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">producer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a> who has enjoyed both critical and box office success.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Ho-sun_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kim Ho-sun was born 9 March 1941 in <a href="/wiki/South_Hamgyong_Province" title="South Hamgyong Province">South Hamgyong Province</a>, now part of <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. He dropped out of <a href="/wiki/Kyung_Hee_University" title="Kyung Hee University">Kyung Hee University</a> and made his directorial debut in 1974 with <i>Hwannyeo</i>.</p>
<p>A member of the so-called "Visual Age" of Korean directors, Kim made some of the bestselling Korean films of the 1970s. The <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Korea" title="Cinema of Korea">local Korean cinema industry</a> in the 1970s had to face the challenges of foreign imports, increased television ownership, and the stifling censorship of the military dictatorship of <a href="/wiki/Park_Chung-hee" title="Park Chung-hee">Park Chung-hee</a>.</p>
<p>Kim began his directing career making what are known as "hostess films," one of the few genres that the censors allowed some latitude to, these were melodramas that portrayed the lives of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bargirls" title="Bargirls">bargirls</a> and their milieu. Kim showed that despite the formulaic restrictions the censors placed on filmmaking, locally made films could still be individual and sell well, and in doing so inspired his fellow filmmakers. In terms of tickets sold, <i><a href="/wiki/Winter_Woman" title="Winter Woman">Winter Woman</a></i> that he directed in 1977 was the bestselling domestic film of the 1970s,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and would hold the record until <i><a href="/wiki/General%27s_Son" title="General's Son">General's Son</a></i> in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KoFA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-KoFA-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2007, Kim has been a senior advisor to the Korea Film Directors' Society (KFDS).</p>
