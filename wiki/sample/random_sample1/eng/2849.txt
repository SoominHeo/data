<p><b>Cao Lãnh</b> (<small>Vietnamese: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Vietnamese" title="Help:IPA for Vietnamese">[kaːw˧ lan˧˨˧]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Cao_Lanh.ogg" title="File:Cao Lanh.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/39/Cao_Lanh.ogg" title="Cao Lanh.ogg">listen</a>)</small>) is the capital city of <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_Th%C3%A1p_Province" title="Đồng Tháp Province">Đồng Tháp Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>. It is located at around <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Cao_L%C3%A3nh&amp;params=10_28_2_N_105_37_49_E_region:VN_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">10°28′2″N</span> <span class="longitude">105°37′49″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">10.46722°N 105.63028°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">10.46722; 105.63028</span></span></span></a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cao_L%C3%A3nh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the French colonial period Cao Lãnh found some supporters for the nationalist appeal of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%E1%BA%A7n_Huy_Li%E1%BB%87u&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trần Huy Liệu (page does not exist)">Trần Huy Liệu</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/%C4%90%C3%B4ng_Ph%C3%A1p_Th%E1%BB%9Di_B%C3%A1o" title="Đông Pháp Thời Báo">Đông Pháp Thời Báo</a></i> (<i>Indochina Times</i>) founded in May 1923.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Later, in initial resistance to the French in 1945, the <a href="/wiki/Vi%E1%BB%87t_Nam_Qu%E1%BB%91c_D%C3%A2n_%C4%90%E1%BA%A3ng" title="Việt Nam Quốc Dân Đảng">Việt Nam Quốc Dân Đảng</a> and others in the South formed into the Third Division of the popular army. Cao Lãnh was where the troops were reorganized into 23 units each of 500-600 men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to 1975, Cao Lãnh was the capital of the Kiến Phong province, in the <a href="/wiki/Mekong_Delta" title="Mekong Delta">Mekong Delta</a> region of <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a>. In February 1976, Kien Phong was merged with <a href="/wiki/Sa_%C4%90%C3%A9c_Province" title="Sa Đéc Province">Sa Đéc Province</a> to become Đồng Tháp Province. <a href="/wiki/Sa_%C4%90%C3%A9c" title="Sa Đéc">Sa Đéc</a> became the capital city of the new province. Cao Lãnh replaced Sa Đéc as the capital since April 24, 1994, and became a city since October 2007.</p>
