<p><i><b>Ribes</b></i> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/iː/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iː</span><span title="'z' in 'Zion'">z</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of about 150 known species<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christenhusz-Byng2016_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christenhusz-Byng2016-4">[4]</a></sup> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angiosperms" title="Angiosperms">flowering plants</a> native throughout the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate">temperate</a> regions of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>. It is usually treated as the only genus in the family <b>Grossulariaceae</b>, but a few taxonomists place the <a href="/wiki/Gooseberry" title="Gooseberry">gooseberry</a> species in a separate genus of <i>Grossularia</i>. Seven <a href="/wiki/Subgenus" title="Subgenus">subgenera</a> are recognized.</p>
<p>Sometimes <i>Ribes</i> is instead included in the family <a href="/wiki/Saxifragaceae" title="Saxifragaceae">Saxifragaceae</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultivation">Cultivation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ribes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cultivation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The genus <i>Ribes</i> includes the edible <b>currants</b> (<a href="/wiki/Blackcurrant" title="Blackcurrant">blackcurrant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redcurrant" title="Redcurrant">redcurrant</a>, <a href="/wiki/White_currant" title="White currant">white currant</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Gooseberry" title="Gooseberry">gooseberry</a>, and several hybrid varieties. It should not be confused with the dried currant used in cakes and puddings, which is a small-fruited cultivar of <a href="/wiki/Grape" title="Grape">grape</a> (<a href="/wiki/Zante_currant" title="Zante currant">Zante currant</a>). <i>Ribes</i> gives its name to the popular blackcurrant cordial <a href="/wiki/Ribena" title="Ribena">Ribena</a>.</p>
<p>The genus also includes the group of ornamental plants collectively known as the flowering currants, for instance <i><a href="/wiki/Ribes_sanguineum" title="Ribes sanguineum">R. sanguineum</a></i>.</p>
<p>There are restrictions on growing some <i>Ribes</i> species in some U.S. states, as they are the main alternate host for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Pine_Blister_Rust" title="White Pine Blister Rust">White Pine Blister Rust</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medicinal">Medicinal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ribes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Medicinal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blackfoot Indians used blackcurrant root <i>(<a href="/wiki/Ribes_hudsonianum" title="Ribes hudsonianum">Ribes hudsonianum</a>)</i> for the treatment of kidney diseases and menstrual and menopausal problems. Cree Indians used the fruit of <i><a href="/wiki/Ribes_glandulosum" title="Ribes glandulosum">Ribes glandulosum</a></i> as a fertility enhancer to assist women in becoming pregnant.</p>
<p>Currant root and seeds are high in <a href="/wiki/Gamma-Linolenic_acid" title="Gamma-Linolenic acid">gamma-Linolenic acid</a> (GLA). GLA has been clinically verified as an effective treatment for pre-menstrual syndrome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ribes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Currants are used as food plants by the <a href="/wiki/Larva" title="Larva">larvae</a> of some <a href="/wiki/Lepidoptera" title="Lepidoptera">Lepidoptera</a> species.</p>
