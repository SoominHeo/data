<p><b>Sūrat Qaf</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">سورة ق</span>‎‎, "The Letter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Q%C4%81f" title="Qāf">Qāf</a>") is the 50th <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sura" title="Sura">sura</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qur%27an" title="Qur'an">Qur'an</a> with 45 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ayat" title="Ayat">ayat</a>. The sura that opens with the single discrete Arabic letter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%D9%82" title="ق">QĀF</a>.</p>
<p>The name of Surah is taken from the letter Kaf in the beginning of the first verse. Sure is associated with the Kafdag (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cafcuh" title="Cafcuh">Cafcuh</a>) in mystical tafsir.</p>
<p>According to the traditions of the Qaf, it is the name of a mountain from the green emerald surrounding the Earth, and Allah is swearing in the name of this mountain and the Qur'an at the beginning of the sura.</p>
<p>It deals predominantly with the <a href="/wiki/Resurrection" title="Resurrection">Resurrection</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Day_of_Judgement" title="Day of Judgement">Day of Judgement</a>. Reference is made to previous generations of disbelievers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> both to warn the <a href="/wiki/Kafir" title="Kafir">disbelievers</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Makkah</a> and to reassure the <a href="/wiki/Prophet" title="Prophet">Prophet</a>. Creation is cited as an indication of God’s ability to bring the dead to life again,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and emphasis is placed on the powerlessness of man on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Day_of_Resurrection" title="Day of Resurrection">Day of Judgement</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The surah both opens and closes with mention of the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>. This chapter is thematically linked with the previous one because the previous chapter teaches the etiquette among Muslims, and this one teaches the proper behaviors for non-believers. It is thematically linked with the next chapter through increasing reference to the Hereafter; the primary subject of the next chapter.</p>
