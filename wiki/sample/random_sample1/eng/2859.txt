<p><i><b>Boys Over Flowers</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">꽃보다 남자</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">꽃보다男子</span>) is a 2009 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> television series starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Min-ho_(actor_born_1987)" title="Lee Min-ho (actor born 1987)">Lee Min-ho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ku_Hye-sun" title="Ku Hye-sun">Ku Hye-sun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Hyun-joong" title="Kim Hyun-joong">Kim Hyun-joong</a> (of <a href="/wiki/SS501" title="SS501">SS501</a>), <a href="/wiki/Kim_Bum" title="Kim Bum">Kim Bum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Joon" title="Kim Joon">Kim Joon</a> (of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T-Max" title="T-Max">T-Max</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Kim_So-eun" title="Kim So-eun">Kim So-eun</a>. It aired on <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a> from January 5 to March 31, 2009 on Mondays and Tuesdays at 22:00 for 25 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is based on <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo_manga" title="Shōjo manga">shōjo</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Boys_Over_Flowers" title="Boys Over Flowers">Boys Over Flowers</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">花より男子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hana Yori Dango</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> written by <a href="/wiki/Yoko_Kamio" title="Yoko Kamio">Yoko Kamio</a>. The series is the fifth television adaptation of the manga following the Taiwanese <i><a href="/wiki/Meteor_Garden" title="Meteor Garden">Meteor Garden</a></i> and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Meteor_Garden_II" title="Meteor Garden II">Meteor Garden II</a></i>, and the Japanese <i><a href="/wiki/Hana_Yori_Dango_(TV_series)" title="Hana Yori Dango (TV series)">Hana Yori Dango</a></i> and its sequel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hana_Yori_Dango_Returns_(TV_series)" title="Hana Yori Dango Returns (TV series)">Hana Yori Dango Returns</a></i>. It spins the modern-day <a href="/wiki/Cinderella" title="Cinderella">Cinderella</a> tale of a poor, but spunky schoolgirl at an exclusive academy who attracts the interest of four rich and handsome guys of the school collectively known as F4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boys_Over_Flowers_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hardworking Geum Jan-di (<a href="/wiki/Ku_Hye-sun" title="Ku Hye-sun">Ku Hye-sun</a>) attends the exclusive Shinhwa High School through a scholarship she obtains. She finds her shallow, privileged classmates unbearable, especially the notorious F4, consisting of four rich and handsome men: the arrogant Gu Jun-pyo (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Min-ho_(actor,_born_1987)" title="Lee Min-ho (actor, born 1987)">Lee Min-ho</a>), the quiet Yoon Ji-hoo (<a href="/wiki/Kim_Hyun-joong" title="Kim Hyun-joong">Kim Hyun-joong</a>), the charming So Yi-jung (<a href="/wiki/Kim_Bum" title="Kim Bum">Kim Bum</a>), and the flirtatious Song Woo-bin (<a href="/wiki/Kim_Joon" title="Kim Joon">Kim Joon</a>). They are considered to be the Kings of the school and no one dared to challenge them. However, after witnessing her friend being bullied, Jan-di told off F4's leader Gu Jun-pyo, thus becoming entangled with him. Through their interactions, the proud and persistent Jun Pyo falls for feisty Jan Di, but instead she shares a connection with the quiet and sensitive Ji Hoo. Through Jan Di's encouragement, Ji Hoo goes abroad to join his model girlfriend Min Seo-hyun, and in his absence, Jan Di and Jun Pyo grow closer. Jun Pyo and Jan Di slowly begin to fall in love. Soon, Jan Di realizes that it is Jun Pyo that she truly loves and not Ji Hoo. But numerous difficulties arise, threatening their relationship. Jun Pyo's mother, a cruel and selfish businesswoman, who is also the President of Shinhwa group, disapproves of Jan Di's lowly upbringing and tries her best to split them up. A newly single Ji Hoo also returns to Korea, prepared to declare his feelings for Jan Di. Many obstacles stand in their relationship, but there is also true love blossoming between these two young hearts. Will their love survive?</p>

<dd>The leader of F4 and heir to Shinhwa Group, one of the most powerful organization in South Korea. Raised by his older sister and servants, as his parents tend to be overwhelmingly busy, he grew up an arrogant and conceited person who believes that there was nothing that could not be done with money. Deep down, however, he has a heart of gold masked by his inability to express his feelings. He torments Jan-di after she stands up to his bullying ways, but ends up falling in love with her.</dd>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_from_the_manga">Differences from the manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boys_Over_Flowers_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Differences from the manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In the manga, the characters are in high school in season 1, but F4 (minus Domyoji) attends Eitoku University in season 2. In the Korean version, the characters are also in high school, but the F4 move on to Shinhwa University from episode 13 onwards. Shinhwa High School and Shinhwa University are located within the same campus.</li>
<li>The relationship between the two characters Sojiroh and Yuki (Yi-jeong and Ga-eul in the Korean version) is merely hinted. In the Korean version, their relationship gradually develops throughout the series, having their own significant plotline instead of acting as a subplot.</li>
<li>Yoon Ji-hoo has a more significant role in Geum Jan-di's life than the Taiwanese and Japanese versions, more strongly depicted as a romantic companion for most of the drama.</li>
<li>Woo-bin's family does not appear in this version, nor is he only interested in older women.</li>
<li>How Geum Jan-di came to attend the same school as F4 is changed completely. In the manga version, Makino's parents sent her to Eitoku against her wishes to attend another high school with her middle school friends because they thought in order to increase chances for Makino to marry a rich guy, she will need to go to a school full of rich people. In the Japanese TV series, Makino decides to apply for Eitoku after seeing Shizuka Todou at the briefing session, wanting to become an amazing person like her. Yet in the Korean version, Jan-di saves a student from committing suicide while delivering laundry (her family owns a dry-cleaning business), and in order to subdue the negative press from the incident, Shinhwa Group offers her a scholarship to the school. Geum Jan-di is reluctant to attend and at first declines, but complied because of parental pressure and because the school has a state-of-the-art swimming pool (she is a competitive swimmer).</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boys_Over_Flowers_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Boys Over Flowers</i> attracted high viewership ratings and buzz throughout South Korea during its broadcast in 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and created another <a href="/wiki/Korean_Wave" title="Korean Wave">Korean Wave</a> throughout Asia. The cast members became household names and shot to stardom overnight, and after the series ended, several of them became the faces of various endorsements and advertisements. The show is credited with launching the career of its lead actor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Min-ho_(actor_born_1987)" title="Lee Min-ho (actor born 1987)">Lee Min-ho</a>, who had previously appeared in a small number of low-budget high school dramas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>During its broadcast, the series influenced South Korean men to take their appearances more seriously to copy the <a href="/wiki/Metrosexual" title="Metrosexual">metrosexual</a> or "pretty boy" image (<i>kkotminam</i>, lit. "men as beautiful as flowers") of the F4 characters in the drama. This led to an increase in South Korean males wearing <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Preppy#Fashion" title="Preppy">preppy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Resort_wear" title="Resort wear">cruise</a> outfits, and clothing in traditionally more feminine looks like the color pink and floral prints.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The drama's local filming locations became tourist attractions, such as Damyang Dynasty Country Club in <a href="/wiki/South_Jeolla_Province" title="South Jeolla Province">South Jeolla Province</a>; Ragung <a href="/wiki/Hanok" title="Hanok">hanok</a> hotel in <a href="/wiki/Tourism_in_Gyeongju" title="Tourism in Gyeongju">Silla Millennium Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gyeongju" title="Gyeongju">Gyeongju</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Gyeongsang_Province" title="North Gyeongsang Province">North Gyeongsang Province</a>; Hilton Namhae Gold &amp; Spa Resort in <a href="/wiki/South_Gyeongsang_Province" title="South Gyeongsang Province">South Gyeongsang Province</a>; <a href="/wiki/Grand_Hyatt_Seoul" title="Grand Hyatt Seoul">Grand Hyatt Seoul</a> ice rink; <a href="/wiki/Lotte_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Lotte Hotels &amp; Resorts">Lotte Hotel World</a>'s Emerald Room; Farmer's Table in <a href="/wiki/Gyeonggi_Province" title="Gyeonggi Province">Heyri</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Yangpyeong_County" title="Yangpyeong County">Yangpyeong</a> English Village. The overseas locations featured such as <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> also became sought-after holiday destinations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series' international popularity extended to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and among others. The cast members held various sold-out events across Asia, such as concerts and fan meetings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Seoul chapter of the Young Women's Christian Association (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_YWCA" title="World YWCA">YWCA</a>) criticized the series for being the epitome of <a href="/wiki/Economic_materialism" title="Economic materialism">materialism</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cinderella_complex" title="Cinderella complex">Cinderella complex</a>, saying it sets a bad example for Korean dramas by depicting <a href="/wiki/School_violence" title="School violence">school violence</a>, and teenagers indulging in pleasure and <a href="/wiki/Prejudice" title="Prejudice">prejudice</a> toward others based on their appearance and <a href="/wiki/Social_class" title="Social class">social class</a>. The YWCA report also singled out the leading female character (Geum Jan-di) for being passive and dependent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<div class="NavContent" style="background:transparent;text-align:left;">
<p>It aired on <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> from May 11 to August 21, 2009, on weeknights at 5:45 PM <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/PST" title="PST">PST</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Each episode runs 45 minutes including commercial breaks. It was initially aired at 10:00 PM PST but was later moved to an earlier timeslot due to its high ratings. The entire series was dubbed in Filipino. It was the number 1 show on its timeslot throughout its run and is listed as one of the highest rated Korean drama in the Philippines.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcast">International broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boys_Over_Flowers_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>: A special preview of the series aired on March 21, 2009. The series then officially aired on Hallyu Channel <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a> every Sunday at 7:30 p.m. starting on April 12, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It also aired on terrestrial channel <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS</a> and its cable channel <a href="/wiki/BS-TBS" title="BS-TBS">BS-TBS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>: It aired on <a href="/wiki/China_Television" title="China Television">CTV</a> beginning May 10, 2009, on Sundays at 10 p.m. <a href="/wiki/Gala_Television" title="Gala Television">GTV</a> also aired the series starting from May 16, on Saturdays at 9 p.m.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>: It aired on <a href="/wiki/Mediacorp" title="Mediacorp">Mediacorp</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MediaCorp_Channel_U" title="MediaCorp Channel U">Channel U</a> starting from May 16, 2009 every Saturday at 9.30 p.m., together with Taiwan's <a href="/wiki/Gala_Television" title="Gala Television">GTV</a> (Channel U broadcast the Taiwan version).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>: It aired on May 10, 2009 on Hong Kong's biggest television network, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">Television Broadcasts Limited</a>, specifically its channel intended for a younger audience <a href="/wiki/TVB_J2" title="TVB J2">TVB J2</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>: It aired on <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> on May 11, 2009. It was first re-aired on UHF channel <a href="/wiki/Studio_23" title="Studio 23">Studio 23</a> on April 19, 2010. And now its re-aired on cable channel <a href="/wiki/Jeepney_TV" title="Jeepney TV">Jeepney TV</a> on August 18, 2014. It is also available to stream with subtitles on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>: It aired on <a href="/wiki/Indosiar" title="Indosiar">Indosiar</a> every Monday and Tuesday at 10.30 p.m., starting from June 1, 2009 and It aired on <a href="/wiki/RCTI" title="RCTI">RCTI</a> every Monday to Friday at 01.30 p.m., starting from January 2, 2015. It is also available to stream with subtitles on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>: It aired on <a href="/wiki/Channel_7_(Thailand)" title="Channel 7 (Thailand)">BBTV Channel 7 Bangkok</a> every Saturday and Sunday at 9.15 a.m. starting from July 4, 2009. Repeat telecast every Monday to Thursday at 3.10 a.m. starting from December 30, 2015. It is also available to stream with subtitles on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a>.</li>
<li>Hong Kong-based-pan-Asian TV channel <a href="/wiki/Xing_Kong" title="Xing Kong">Xing Kong</a> aired the series on July 8, 2009, as part of the "<i>Boys Over Flowers</i> Trilogy" along with the Taiwanese and Japanese versions.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>: It aired on H1 (Hanoi Television) beginning June 24, 2009, from Mondays to Thursdays at 9 p.m. The copyright was held by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%C3%AD_Vi%E1%BB%87t_Media_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trí Việt Media Corporation (page does not exist)">TVM</a> and it also aired on <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">HTV3</a> every weekend at 9:00 p.m. beginning August 22, 2009.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>: It aired on <a href="/wiki/8TV_(Malaysia)" title="8TV (Malaysia)">8TV</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astro_(Malaysian_satellite_television)" title="Astro (Malaysian satellite television)">Astro</a> channel 708), starting from December 16, 2009, dubbed in Mandarin with Malay subtitles. Later on, in 2015, it has re-broadcast again at NTV7 Monday to Friday on 12:00 am until 1:00 pm. It is also available to stream with subtitles on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a>.</li>
<li>Canada: It aired in Vancouver on SHAW Multicultural Channel (119) weekdays from 7:30 to 8:00 p.m. with English subtitles.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>: It began airing on September 3, 2010 on Viva Platina Channel, 2 episodes every Friday.</li>
<li>United States: <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a> started streaming the full 25 episodes in August 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>: It aired on TV5 in 2010.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>: It began airing in January 2011 on BTV.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>: It began airing on May 5, 2011 on <a href="/wiki/Panamericana_Televisi%C3%B3n" title="Panamericana Televisión">Panamericana Televisión</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>: It began airing on June 19, 2011 on SERTV, Channel 11.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>: It aired on <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_TV" title="Ecuador TV">Ecuador TV</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>: It began airing on October 12, 2011 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euforia_Lifestyle_TV" title="Euforia Lifestyle TV">Euforia Lifestyle TV</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>: It began airing on October 19, 2011 on Puerto Rico TV.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>: It aired on El Arna.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>: It began airing on April 16, 2012 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Etc...TV" title="Etc...TV">Etc...TV</a> and sister station <a href="/wiki/Mega_(Chilean_television_channel)" title="Mega (Chilean television channel)">MEGA</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>: It began airing on December 1, 2012 on <a href="/wiki/TRT_Okul" title="TRT Okul">TRT Okul</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>: It began airing on March 23, 2013 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MBC_4_(Middle_East)" title="MBC 4 (Middle East)">MBC 4</a>, dubbed as <i>Ayam al Zohor</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>: It began airing on May 12, 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Puthuyugam_TV" title="Puthuyugam TV">Puthuyugam TV</a>, daily from 7:00-8:00 p.m. (with reruns at 10:30-11:30 p.m., and the next day at 2:00-3:00 p.m.), dubbed in <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> as <a href="/wiki/K-series_(TV_series)" title="K-series (TV series)">K-series</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>: It premiered on August 11, 2014 on <a href="/wiki/TV_Derana" title="TV Derana">TV Derana</a>, Monday to Friday 8:30 p.m - 9:00 p.m, dubbed in Sinhala. It is also available to stream with subtitles on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a>.</li>
</ul>
