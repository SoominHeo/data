<p>The domain name <b>jobs</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Sponsored_top-level_domain" title="Sponsored top-level domain">sponsored top-level domain</a> (sTLD) in the <a href="/wiki/Domain_Name_System" title="Domain Name System">Domain Name System</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>. As indicated by its name, the domain is restricted to employment-related sites.</p>
<p>The domain was approved by <a href="/wiki/ICANN" title="ICANN">ICANN</a> on April 8, 2005 as part of the second group of new TLD applications submitted in 2004. It was installed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DNS_root" title="DNS root">DNS root</a> in September 2005, and began accepting registrations later in the year.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Intended_use">Intended use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.jobs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Intended use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The intended use of the domain <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">jobs</span> is for companies and organizations to register some version of their corporate names and use it for a site aimed at those seeking employment with that company, or, <i>.jobs</i> can target a specific market. For example, manufacturing.jobs could represent employment for a specific corporation, or market sector.</p>
<p>In 2010, Employ Media LLC applied to ICANN to extend the charter under which Employ Media is authorized to sell the <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.jobs</span> domains. If accepted, Employ Media plans to create hundreds of thousands and perhaps a million new, niche job boards and sell domains such as Chicago.jobs to third parties. The International Association of Employment Web Sites and dozens of other employment services organizations have objected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It has been suggested that subdomains of other domains, such as <i>jobs.example.com</i>, can be used without any new registrations on the part of the companies involved. However, there is no general consensus among companies or industry sectors for any such naming scheme, like there is for the <b>www</b> prefix and other prefixes, so the proponents of <span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">.jobs</span> argue the domain can gain a market presence by allowing recruiters to communicate a simple, direct URL destination of employment opportunities to job seekers.</p>
<p>In 2013, Employ Media partnered with Cnjobs Technology which is headed by Dr. Renjun Bao to expand to greater China.</p>
