<p>The <b>Cladoniaceae</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichenized</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fungi" title="Fungi">fungi</a> in the order <a href="/wiki/Lecanorales" title="Lecanorales">Lecanorales</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OoA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-OoA-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reindeer_moss" title="Reindeer moss">reindeer moss</a> and cup lichens (<i><a href="/wiki/Cladonia" title="Cladonia">Cladonia</a></i>) belong to this family. The latter genus, which comprises about 400 species, forms a major part of the diet of large mammals in <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a> ecosystems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cannon_2007_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cannon_2007-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
