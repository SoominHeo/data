<p><i><b>Springtime</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">꽃피는 봄이 오면</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Ggotpineun bomi omyeon</span></i>; lit. <i><b>When Spring Comes</b></i>) is a 2004 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> film starring <a href="/wiki/Choi_Min-sik" title="Choi Min-sik">Choi Min-sik</a> as a struggling musician who takes a job as a music teacher in a rural mining town outside of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Springtime_(2004_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To trumpeter Hyun-woo, life seems to remain forever locked in winter. In desperation, Hyunwoo signs up for a position teaching a children’s wind ensemble at a small junior high school in a distant Dogye village. Worn-out instruments, tarnished trophies and frayed certificates testify to the poor conditions of this ragtag group. This leads Hyun-woo, together with his students, to take on a seemingly impossible challenge.</p>
