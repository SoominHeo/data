<p><i><b>My Way</b></i>, released in France as <i><b>Cloclo</b></i>, is a 2012 French <a href="/wiki/Biographical_film" title="Biographical film">biographical</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> about the life of French singer, songwriter and entertainer <a href="/wiki/Claude_Fran%C3%A7ois" title="Claude François">Claude François</a>. It is co-written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Florent_Emilio_Siri" title="Florent Emilio Siri">Florent Emilio Siri</a>, and stars <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%A9mie_Renier" title="Jérémie Renier">Jérémie Renier</a> as François.</p>
<p><i>Cloclo</i> is referring to François' nickname, while the international name was chosen due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Way_(song)" title="My Way (song)">eponymous song</a> popularized by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a>, but originally co-written, co-composed and performed as "<a href="/wiki/Comme_d%27habitude" title="Comme d'habitude">Comme d'habitude</a>" by François. The film follows the life of the singer from his childhood in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> to his accidental death in 1978.</p>
