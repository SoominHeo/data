<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kazuo Nagano</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Kazuo Nagano</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">永野 一男</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nagano Kazuo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, August 1, 1952 – June 18, 1985)</span> was <a href="/wiki/Chairman" title="Chairman">chairman</a> of the board at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Toyota_Shoji&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Toyota Shoji (page does not exist)">Toyota Shoji</a> (unrelated to <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">the car manufacturing company</a>) which was responsible for swindling 3,855 people, mostly elderly, out of 12 billion <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">yen</a>. In 1985, two men – Masakazu Yano and Atsuo Iida<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> – entered his house in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> through the window while at least 40 reporters watched,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> proceeding to stab him to death<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> with a <a href="/wiki/Bayonet" title="Bayonet">bayonet</a>. One of the murderers received an 8-year jail sentence while the other received a 10-year sentence.</p>
