<p><b>Jamaica</b> competed in the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympic_Games" title="Winter Olympic Games">Winter Olympic Games</a> for the first time at the <b><a href="/wiki/1988_Winter_Olympics" title="1988 Winter Olympics">1988 Winter Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Calgary" title="Calgary">Calgary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. They competed in one sport, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bobsledding" title="Bobsledding">Bobsledding</a>, in both the two-man and four-man events and finished outside the medal places in both competitions. Athletes were recruited from the <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Defence_Force" title="Jamaica Defence Force">Jamaica Defence Force</a>, which saw <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dudley_Stokes" title="Dudley Stokes">Dudley Stokes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Devon_Harris" title="Devon Harris">Devon Harris</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_White_(bobsledder)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Michael White (bobsledder) (page does not exist)">Michael White</a> become the first members of the team. Caswell Allen was the fourth man, but was injured prior to the start of the Olympics and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Stokes_(bobsledder)" title="Chris Stokes (bobsledder)">Chris Stokes</a>, who was only in Canada to support his brother and new team-mate Dudley.</p>
<p>The two-man event took place first, with Dudley Stokes and White finishing in 30th position overall. American television stations aired footage of the four-man Jamaican team, and despite crashing during the third run and finishing last overall, went on to inspire the 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Cool_Runnings" title="Cool Runnings">Cool Runnings</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_at_the_Olympics" title="Jamaica at the Olympics">Jamaica at the Olympics</a></div>
<p>The Jamaica Olympic Association was formed in 1936,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> but due to the cancellation of the <a href="/wiki/1940_Summer_Olympics" title="1940 Summer Olympics">1940</a> and <a href="/wiki/1944_Summer_Olympics" title="1944 Summer Olympics">1944 Olympics</a> due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, the first Games they competed in was the <a href="/wiki/1948_Summer_Olympics" title="1948 Summer Olympics">1948 Summer Olympics</a> in London. They have appeared at every Summer Games since,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamsports_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jamsports-2">[2]</a></sup> including at the <a href="/wiki/1960_Summer_Olympics" title="1960 Summer Olympics">1960 Games</a> in which they appeared as part of the combined team of the <a href="/wiki/British_West_Indies_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="British West Indies at the 1960 Summer Olympics">British West Indies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They have won medals at every Summer Games they have competed at, with the exception of the <a href="/wiki/1956_Summer_Olympics" title="1956 Summer Olympics">1956</a> and <a href="/wiki/1964_Summer_Olympics" title="1964 Summer Olympics">1964 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamsports_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-jamsports-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bobsleigh">Bobsleigh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bobsleigh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bobsleigh_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics" title="Bobsleigh at the 1988 Winter Olympics">Bobsleigh at the 1988 Winter Olympics</a></div>
<p>Businessmen <a href="/wiki/George_B._Fitch" title="George B. Fitch">George B. Fitch</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_Maloney_(businessman)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William Maloney (businessman) (page does not exist)">William Maloney</a> proposed the idea of a Jamaican bobsleigh team after seeing a local <a href="/wiki/Baggage_cart" title="Baggage cart">pushcart</a> derby in Jamaica. The President of the Jamaica Olympic Association at the time supported the idea, and so preparations were made to hire athletes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup> Advertisements were placed describing "dangerous and rigorous" trials which would form the basis of the country's first bobsleigh team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frozenintime_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-frozenintime-5">[5]</a></sup> However, recruitment proved to be problematic and so the <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Defence_Force" title="Jamaica Defence Force">Jamaica Defence Force</a> was asked for volunteers. This resulted in the first team of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dudley_Stokes" title="Dudley Stokes">Dudley Stokes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Devon_Harris" title="Devon Harris">Devon Harris</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_White_(bobsledder)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Michael White (bobsledder) (page does not exist)">Michael White</a>. These three were selected as part of the team in October 1987, with team-mate Casewell Allen added later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>With funding provided by the Fitch and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica_Tourist_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jamaica Tourist Board (page does not exist)">Jamaica Tourist Board</a>, training was conducted in Canada and Austria in preparation for the <a href="/wiki/1988_Winter_Olympics" title="1988 Winter Olympics">1988 Winter Olympics</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sepp_Haidacher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sepp Haidacher (page does not exist)">Sepp Haidacher</a> was recruited as a coach, and the team began to be featured in North American media with a comical angle. An agreement was reached with the <a href="/wiki/International_Bobsleigh_and_Skeleton_Federation" title="International Bobsleigh and Skeleton Federation">Fédération International de Bobsleigh et de Tobogganing</a> in order to allow for entrance in both the four-man and the two-man events at the Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup> Once in Calgary, the team conducted test runs on a frozen lake in order to get used to the conditions, but Allen fell and was injured. <a href="/wiki/Chris_Stokes_(bobsledder)" title="Chris Stokes (bobsledder)">Chris Stokes</a>, who was only in Canada in order to support his brother Dudley, was added to the four-man team three days before the first run having never been in a bobsleigh before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frozenintime_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-frozenintime-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first event which Jamaica competed in was the two-man bobsleigh, where Dudley Stokes and Michael White became Jamaica's first Winter Olympians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup> In their first run, they finished in 34th position, ahead of the second <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics" title="New Zealand at the 1988 Winter Olympics">New Zealand team</a>, both of the pairs from <a href="/wiki/Portugal_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics" title="Portugal at the 1988 Winter Olympics">Portugal</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Virgin_Islands_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics" title="U.S. Virgin Islands at the 1988 Winter Olympics">U.S. Virgin Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexico_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics" title="Mexico at the 1988 Winter Olympics">Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They improved in the second run, moving up to 22nd place,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> but were in 31st place on the third run and finished only one place higher in 30th place during the fourth and final run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Overall the duo finished in 30th place out of the 41 teams competing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the elimination of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics#Ice_hockey" title="United States at the 1988 Winter Olympics">United States ice hockey team</a>, American television stations needed to fill airtime and chose to focus on the Jamaican bobsleigh team in the four-man event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup> The first run ended poorly, as when Dudley Stokes jumped into the bobsleigh, the <a href="/wiki/Crash_bar" title="Crash bar">push-bar</a> in the sleigh broke,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frozenintime_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-frozenintime-5">[5]</a></sup> resulting in the team coming in third from last in 24th place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On their second attempt, the team ranked second to last, due in part to White struggling to crouch down properly in his seat, remaining almost upright through the first corner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was the events of the third run for which the team became best known. Stokes injured his shoulder prior to the race, but decided to continue with the run. The team set the seventh-fastest start for all competitors. At the turn called the "Kreisel", Stokes lost control of the bobsleigh and it careened into the wall of the track, and flipped over on top of the four athletes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup> The four team members climbed out and pushed the bobsleigh to the end of the track, before they carried it off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frozenintime_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-frozenintime-5">[5]</a></sup> The team did not compete in the fourth run of the event,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and subsequently were listed as not finishing the event and therefore were placed in the last place overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story of the Jamaican bobsleigh team at the 1988 Winter Olympics was turned into the 1993 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Cool_Runnings" title="Cool Runnings">Cool Runnings</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crashingstart_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-crashingstart-4">[4]</a></sup> However, the film was only loosely based on actual events, with real life coach Pat Brown later saying that the team had never experienced any of the animosity from the other teams as depicted in the movie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frozenintime_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-frozenintime-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>All of the team members returned for the <a href="/wiki/1992_Winter_Olympics" title="1992 Winter Olympics">1992 Winter Olympics</a>, however Harris only competed in the two-man event, with his place in the four-man team taken by newcomer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_McIntosh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ricky McIntosh (page does not exist)">Ricky McIntosh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Harris and the Stokes brothers would continue to compete at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Games</a> in the bobsleigh events until the <a href="/wiki/1998_Winter_Olympics" title="1998 Winter Olympics">1998 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nagano,_Nagano" title="Nagano, Nagano">Nagano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
