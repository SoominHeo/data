<p><b>Nagashima Spa Land</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ナガシマスパーランド</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nagashima Supā Rando</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a major <a href="/wiki/Amusement_park" title="Amusement park">amusement park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kuwana,_Mie" title="Kuwana, Mie">Kuwana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It features several <a href="/wiki/Roller_coaster" title="Roller coaster">roller coasters</a>, thrill rides, and kid rides, a giant <a href="/wiki/Ferris_wheel" title="Ferris wheel">Ferris wheel</a>, and a water park. As of 2012, Nagashima Spa Land is the 18th most visited amusement park in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ferris_wheel">Ferris wheel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagashima_Spa_Land&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ferris wheel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nagashima Spa Land is the home of Aurora Wheel, a giant <a href="/wiki/Ferris_wheel" title="Ferris wheel">Ferris wheel</a>. It is 90 metres (295 ft) tall<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and 83 metres (272 ft) in diameter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents">Incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagashima_Spa_Land&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On October 19, 2003, one of the <a href="/wiki/Steel_Dragon_2000" title="Steel Dragon 2000">Steel Dragon 2000</a> trains lost a wheel, resulting in a guest in the water park suffering a broken hip.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The ride was <a href="/wiki/Standing_but_not_operating" title="Standing but not operating">standing but not operating</a> (SBNO) until sturdier wheels were installed and the ride reopened in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
