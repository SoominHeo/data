<p><b>Yuto Nagatomo</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">長友 佑都</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nagatomo Yūto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 12 September 1986)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FC_Tokyo">FC Tokyo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: FC Tokyo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nagatomo officially signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Tokyo" title="F.C. Tokyo">FC Tokyo</a> in 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> while at Meiji. But he made an appearance at <a href="/wiki/J.League_Cup" title="J.League Cup">J.League Cup</a> as Special Designated Youth Player in 2007. Nagatomo made 84 appearances in his four years at FC Tokyo.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cesena">Cesena</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cesena">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2010, Nagatomo was signed on loan by the newly promoted <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> side <a href="/wiki/A.C._Cesena" title="A.C. Cesena">A.C. Cesena</a>, where he played the first half of the season as Cesena's starting left back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Before moving to Italy, Nagatomo gave a speech to 25,000 supporters just after the match involving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Tokyo" title="F.C. Tokyo">F.C. Tokyo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vissel_Kobe" title="Vissel Kobe">Vissel Kobe</a> on 17 July 2010. After being signed on a permanent deal, Nagatomo was then sold to Internazionale.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internazionale">Internazionale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Internazionale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to his impressive performances for both Cesena and <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2011 AFC Asian Cup">2011 Asian Cup</a>, he was fully signed by Internazionale in January 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> But before making any league appearances he was sent straight out on loan to <a href="/wiki/A.C._Cesena" title="A.C. Cesena">Cesena</a> in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Davide_Santon" title="Davide Santon">Davide Santon</a> also on loan, becoming the first East Asian player to sign with Internazionale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nagatomo, wearing number 55, made his debut for Inter on 6 February 2011, replacing <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Sneijder" title="Wesley Sneijder">Wesley Sneijder</a> in the Serie A match against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>, which Inter won 5–3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the following game, Nagatomo came on as a late substitute against <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, before starting in both of the following games against <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a>. Nagatomo has shown his versatility by playing right back against Sampdoria on 27 February 2011, and finishing the whole 90 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 6 March 2011, Nagatomo scored his first goal for Inter in the 5–2 trashing of <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Houssine_Kharja" title="Houssine Kharja">Kharja</a> assist with a swivel and a finish into the roof of the net.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 March, to show solidarity with his home country in light of the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">catastrophic earthquake and tsunami</a> which took place the previous week he paraded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_flag" title="Japanese flag">Japanese flag</a> (with the writing "<a href="/wiki/You%27ll_Never_Walk_Alone" title="You'll Never Walk Alone">You'll Never Walk Alone</a>", a chant of his former team in Tokyo) around the pitch during a <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Inter had drawn German club side <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> in the quarter-finals of Champions League. Nagatomo, along with international teammate <a href="/wiki/Atsuto_Uchida" title="Atsuto Uchida">Atsuto Uchida</a> who plays for Schalke became the second and third Japanese footballer to play in the quarter-finals of the UEFA Champions league (<a href="/wiki/Keisuke_Honda" title="Keisuke Honda">Keisuke Honda</a> was the first playing for <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a> during <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2009–10 UEFA Champions League">2009–10 season</a>).</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> season was over, <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a> made an agreement with <a href="/wiki/A.C._Cesena" title="A.C. Cesena">Cesena</a> of making Yuto's move permanent in a cash plus player exchange deal. Nagatomo was valued at €10.95 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inter2011bilancio_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inter2011bilancio-14">[14]</a></sup> however part of the fee was paid via half of the registration rights of <a href="/wiki/Luca_Garritano" title="Luca Garritano">Luca Garritano</a> (€700,000)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inter2011bilancio_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Inter2011bilancio-14">[14]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Luca_Caldirola" title="Luca Caldirola">Luca Caldirola</a> (€2.5 million).</p>
<p>Nagatomo started his second Inter Milan season by playing 62 minutes in the opening <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">Serie A</a> match against <a href="/wiki/U.S._Citt%C3%A0_di_Palermo" title="U.S. Città di Palermo">Palermo</a>, ended in a 4–3 away defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 27 September 2011, Nagatomo provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Giampaolo_Pazzini" title="Giampaolo Pazzini">Giampaolo Pazzini</a> by nutmegging Aleksei Berezutski before driving the ball across goal during the 3–2 away win against <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League group stage">group stage</a> match of <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He scored his first goal of the season on 10 December 2011 during the 2–0 home win against Fiorentina in the matchday 15 of Serie A.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He was again in the scoresheet three days later as he headed home an <a href="/wiki/Ricky_%C3%81lvarez" title="Ricky Álvarez">Ricky Álvarez</a> cross in the 1–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In the last match of 2011 against <a href="/wiki/U.S._Lecce" title="U.S. Lecce">Lecce</a> at home, Nagatomo produced a "man of the match" performance by providing two assists in an eventual 4–1 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nagatomo enjoyed his most productive season under <a href="/wiki/Walter_Mazzarri" title="Walter Mazzarri">Walter Mazzarri</a>, scoring five goals, along with six assists during the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 April 2016, Nagatomo agreed a contract extension with the club, signing until June 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nagatomo earned his first international cap for <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> on 24 May 2008 in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast_national_football_team" title="Ivory Coast national football team">Ivory Coast</a>. His first international goal was scored in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Syria_national_football_team" title="Syria national football team">Syria</a> held on 13 November 2008. Nagatomo was also a member of the Japan U-21 team for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_FIFA_World_Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was called up for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> for Japan, and was named in <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Ars%C3%A8ne_Wenger" title="Arsène Wenger">Arsène Wenger</a>'s best XI of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_AFC_Asian_Cup">2011 AFC Asian Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2011 AFC Asian Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nagatomo was selected as part of the Japan squad for the <a href="/wiki/2011_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2011 AFC Asian Cup">2011 AFC Asian Cup</a> by coach <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Zaccheroni" title="Alberto Zaccheroni">Alberto Zaccheroni</a>. He featured in every game Japan played in the competition and helped them defeat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_football_team" title="Australia national football team">Australia</a> in the final, thus earning his first international honour.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_FIFA_Confederations_Cup">2013 FIFA Confederations Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2013 FIFA Confederations Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was included in <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Zaccheroni" title="Alberto Zaccheroni">Alberto Zaccheroni</a>'s 23-man Japan squad for the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2013 FIFA Confederations Cup">2013 FIFA Confederations Cup</a> in which he featured in all three games Japan played before they were eliminated in the group stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_FIFA_World_Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuto_Nagatomo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2014 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nagatomo was part of Japan's national squad to play in the 2014 World Cup in Brazil. He started in all three group matches but Japan was eliminated in the group stage.</p>
