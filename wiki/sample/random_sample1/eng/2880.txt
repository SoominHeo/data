<p><b>Nagato-Awano Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">長門粟野駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nagato-Awano-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanin_Main_Line" title="Sanin Main Line">Sanin Main Line</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hohoku,_Yamaguchi" title="Hohoku, Yamaguchi">Hohoku</a> area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shimonoseki,_Yamaguchi" title="Shimonoseki, Yamaguchi">Shimonoseki, Yamaguchi</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR West).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagato-Awano_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of an <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> serving two tracks. The platform is accessible via an uncovered <a href="/wiki/Footbridge" title="Footbridge">footbridge</a>. There is a toilet available near the station building, which is located on the north side of the tracks. Though managed by the Nagato Railroad Bureau, there are no station staff members.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagato-Awano_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>7 December 1930 - The opening of the extension of the <a href="/wiki/Mine_Line" title="Mine Line">Mine Line</a>, as it was then, from <a href="/wiki/Nagato-Furuichi_Station" title="Nagato-Furuichi Station">Nagato-Furuichi Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Agawa_Station" title="Agawa Station">Agawa Station</a>. Services for passenger and freight trains commence.</li>
<li>24 February 1933 - Areas of the Mine line including Nagato-Awano Station are incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/San%27in_Main_Line" title="San'in Main Line">San'in Main Line</a>.</li>
<li>1 June 1963 - The service of freight trains is cancelled.</li>
<li>1 April 1987 - Under the privatisation of Japan's railways, Nagato-Awano Station becomes part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_area">Local area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagato-Awano_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Local area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are several small shops around the station. The station is located in the village of Awano, which is at the north end of <a href="/wiki/Shimonoseki" title="Shimonoseki">Shimonoseki</a> city, and roughly 1 kilometre East from the border of <a href="/wiki/Nagato,_Yamaguchi" title="Nagato, Yamaguchi">Nagato</a> city.</p>
<ul>
<li>Awano Port (Awano Fishing Harbour) - Former ferry port that linked to Kuzu in <a href="/wiki/Yuya,_Yamaguchi" title="Yuya, Yamaguchi">Yuya</a> (of the former <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Ctsu_District,_Yamaguchi" title="Ōtsu District, Yamaguchi">Ōtsu District</a>)across the Yuya Bay.</li>
<li>Awano Post Office</li>
<li>Awano River - The <a href="/wiki/Aonori" title="Aonori">aonori</a> collected near the mouth of the river is a local speciality.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan_National_Route_191" title="Japan National Route 191">Japan National Route 191</a></li>
</ul>
