<p><b>Na Kyung-won</b> (나경원, born 6 December 1963) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> politician and lawyer. She was a member of the conservative <a href="/wiki/Saenuri_Party" title="Saenuri Party">Saenuri Party</a> (formerly the Grand National Party). She was one of two candidates of the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_by-elections,_2011" title="South Korean by-elections, 2011">October 2011 Seoul mayoral by-election</a> after <a href="/wiki/Oh_Se-hoon" title="Oh Se-hoon">Oh Se-hoon</a> resigned his position as <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Seoul" title="Mayor of Seoul">mayor</a>, but lost the election to <a href="/wiki/Park_Won-soon" title="Park Won-soon">Park Won-soon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She started her political career as a special aide for women's affairs under <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hoi-chang" title="Lee Hoi-chang">Lee Hoi-chang</a> in 2002 during the presidential election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Na did not run for the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_legislative_election,_2012" title="South Korean legislative election, 2012">2012 legislative election</a> due to allegations that her husband <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jae-ho" title="Kim Jae-ho">Kim Jae-ho</a> had been involved in a clandestine deal with a prosecutor from the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Prosecutors%27_Office_of_the_Republic_of_Korea" title="Supreme Prosecutors' Office of the Republic of Korea">Supreme Prosecutors' Office</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She subsequently ran as a candidate for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongjak-gu" title="Dongjak-gu">Dongjak</a> B in <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_by-elections,_2014" title="South Korean by-elections, 2014">a July 2014 by-election</a>, and claimed victory against the <a href="/wiki/Justice_Party_(South_Korea)" title="Justice Party (South Korea)">Justice Party</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Roh_Hoe-chan" title="Roh Hoe-chan">Roh Hoe-chan</a>, by a difference of 929 votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
