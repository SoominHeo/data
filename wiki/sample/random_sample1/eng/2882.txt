<p><b>Mizuho Athletic Stadium</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">名古屋市瑞穂公園陸上競技場</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nagoya-shi Mizuho Kōen Rikujō Kyōgijō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, since April 2015 also <b>Paloma Mizuho Park Athletic Stadium</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> due to a <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a> deal with <a href="/wiki/Gas_heater" title="Gas heater">gas heater</a> construction company <i>Paloma</i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Multi-purpose_stadium" title="Multi-purpose stadium">multi-purpose stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>, Japan.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paloma_Mizuho_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is used mostly for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> matches and is the part-time home stadium of <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Grampus" title="Nagoya Grampus">Nagoya Grampus</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Stadium" title="Toyota Stadium">Toyota Stadium</a>. The stadium holds 27,000 people and was built in 1941.</p>
<p>It is distinct from <a href="/wiki/Mizuho_Rugby_Stadium" title="Mizuho Rugby Stadium">Mizuho Rugby Stadium</a> which has a capacity of 15,000 and is used mainly for rugby, including <a href="/wiki/Top_League" title="Top League">Top League</a> games.</p>
