<p><b>Nadir Belhadj</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><b>نذير بلحاج</b></span> ‎‎, born 18 June 1982) is an <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algerian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player who plays as a left wingback or as a left winger. He is well known for his pace and stamina.</p>
<p>An Algerian international, Belhadj was a former <a href="/wiki/France_national_youth_football_team" title="France national youth football team">French youth international</a> having played for the French Under-18's for a couple of games in 2000. Belhadj was among the first footballers to profit from the 2004 change in <a href="/wiki/FIFA_eligibility_rules" title="FIFA eligibility rules">FIFA eligibility rules</a> as he had played as a French youth international. After his switch of national allegiance to Algeria, he was called up to an unofficial friendly on 30 March 2004 against <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> club <a href="/wiki/R.A.A._Louvi%C3%A9roise" title="R.A.A. Louviéroise">R.A.A. Louviéroise</a>. He made his senior international debut in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/China_national_football_team" title="China national football team">China</a> on 28 April 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He featured for Algeria at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2010 Africa Cup of Nations">2010 Africa Cup of Nations</a>, reaching the semi-finals, and the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>. On 4 May 2012, he announced his international retirement, ending his career with 54 caps and 4 goals.</p>
<p>Belhadj played a vital role for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qatari" title="Qatari">Qatari</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Sadd" title="Al Sadd">Al Sadd</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Asian_Champions_League" title="2011 Asian Champions League">2011 Asian Champions League</a> final, by beating <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeonbuk_Hyundai_Motors" title="Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors">Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors</a> 4–2 in a penalty shoot-out. Belhadj scored the decisive penalty after the game had finished 2–2 in regulation time, holding his nerve to beat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Min-Sik" title="Kim Min-Sik">Kim Min-Sik</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> This was the first win for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qatari" title="Qatari">Qatari</a> club since the <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> started in 2003, and ended five consecutive triumphs for <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadir_Belhadj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadir_Belhadj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leftback" title="Leftback">left back</a> who can also operate as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winger_(soccer)" title="Winger (soccer)">winger</a>, Belhadj began his career at <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> club Lens, where he failed to make the first-team. He joined <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a> <a href="/wiki/FC_Gueugnon" title="FC Gueugnon">FC Gueugnon</a> on loan in 2002 and after making 26 league appearances signed permanently in 2003. Belhadj played 36 more Ligue 2 games for Gueugnon before moving to <a href="/wiki/CS_Sedan_Ardennes" title="CS Sedan Ardennes">CS Sedan</a> in 2004. Making more than thirty appearances in each of his two full seasons with Sedan in Ligue 2, gaining promotion in the second.</p>
<p>In January 2007 Belhadj was bought by French champions <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Olympique Lyonnais</a> for €3.24 million and signed a lucrative four and a half-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was loaned back to <a href="/wiki/CS_Sedan_Ardennes" title="CS Sedan Ardennes">CS Sedan</a> until the end of the season and joined <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a> again in the summer of 2007. He made just 9 appearances for the club, being blocked at the left-back position by World Cup champion <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Grosso" title="Fabio Grosso">Fabio Grosso</a>, and left the club in January. On 7 January 2008, it was confirmed that Belhadj had signed a three and a half-year deal with his former club Lens for a fee of €3.6 million.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadir_Belhadj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Portsmouth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 September 2008, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> had signed Belhadj on a season-long loan with an option to make the deal permanent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He made his Portsmouth debut on 13 September coming on as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> at <a href="/wiki/Fratton_Park" title="Fratton Park">Fratton Park</a> where Portsmouth went from a goal down to come back and win 2–1, with Belhadj playing a key role on the left with <a href="/wiki/Armand_Traor%C3%A9" title="Armand Traoré">Armand Traoré</a>. He also started in Portsmouth's first ever European match, a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_S.C." title="Vitória S.C.">Vitória de Guimarães</a> and set <a href="/wiki/Jermain_Defoe" title="Jermain Defoe">Jermain Defoe</a> up with a cross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He also scored a long range goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunderland_F.C." title="Sunderland F.C.">Sunderland</a> away from home in a 2–1 win, which was the first win for new manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tony_Adams_(footballer)" title="Tony Adams (footballer)">Tony Adams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Belhadj played a part in Portsmouth's 2–2 draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Milan" title="AC Milan">Milan</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_2008-09" title="UEFA Cup 2008-09">UEFA Cup</a>. On 30 December, Belhadj signed a four and a half-year deal with Pompey, costing €4.5 million paid to Lens,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> keeping him at the club until 2013. He officially became a Portsmouth player on 1 January 2009 when the winter transfer window opened. On 27 January 2009, Belhadj was sent off for the first time for Portsmouth when he picked up 2 yellow cards in the 1–0 defeat against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although he has not always kept a first team place, his recall to face <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> on 20 December 2009 was an inspired move by <a href="/wiki/Avram_Grant" title="Avram Grant">Avram Grant</a> as Belhadj was able to keep former Pompey favourite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glen_Johnson_(English_footballer)" title="Glen Johnson (English footballer)">Glen Johnson</a> under control. Belhadj scored the first goal in the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> A highlight of the 2009–10 season for Belhadj was a goal against local rivals <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> on 13 February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Belhadj's last appearance for Portsmouth was as a substitute in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FA_Cup_Final" title="2010 FA Cup Final">2010 FA Cup final</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Al_Sadd">Al Sadd</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadir_Belhadj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Al Sadd">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the 2011 summer transfer market several clubs showed interest, such as Celtic, Wigan and Wolves with Al Sadd rejecting all proposals as they viewed Belhadj as an important member of the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He helped guide his team to the <a href="/wiki/2011_AFC_Champions_League" title="2011 AFC Champions League">2011 AFC Champions League</a> title, defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeonbuk_Hyundai_Motors" title="Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors">Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors</a> in the final where he scored the game winning penalty in the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">shootout</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2013, Belhadj was invited by <a href="/wiki/Lekhwiya_SC" title="Lekhwiya SC">Lekhwiya</a> to play in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>Belhadj began his international career in 2000 by representing France at the Under-18 level, making just two appearances for the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 March 2004, Belhadj made his unofficial debut for the <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algerian national team</a> in a 0–0 friendly against <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> club <a href="/wiki/R.A.A._Louvi%C3%A9roise" title="R.A.A. Louviéroise">R.A.A. Louviéroise</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 28 April 2004, he made his official debut for the team in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/China_national_football_team" title="China national football team">China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 5 June 2007, Belhadj got his first international goal in a friendly 4–3 loss to <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He scored two goals in that game beating goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Abbondanzieri" title="Roberto Abbondanzieri">Roberto Abbondanzieri</a> twice from free kicks.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2010_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2010 Africa Cup of Nations">2010 Africa Cup of Nations</a> he was sent off in the semi-final against <a href="/wiki/Egypt_national_football_team" title="Egypt national football team">Egypt</a> with a straight card, and received a two match competitive international suspension, ruling him out of the match against <a href="/wiki/Tanzania_national_football_team" title="Tanzania national football team">Tanzania</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2012 African Cup of Nations">2012 African Cup of Nations</a> qualifying stages.</p>
<p>Since the 2010 World Cup and signing with Al-Saad in the Qatar Stars League, Nadir has seen his place at left back for the national side slip away to revelation <a href="/wiki/Djamel_Mesbah" title="Djamel Mesbah">Djamel Mesbah</a>, ever since his calamitous appearance against the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic_national_football_team" title="Central African Republic national football team">Central African Republic</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 4 May 2012, Belhadj announced his international retirement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
