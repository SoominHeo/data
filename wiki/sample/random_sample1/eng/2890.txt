<p>Since the first <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">human spaceflight</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, citizens of 40 countries have flown in space. For each nationality, the launch date of the first mission is listed. The list is based on the nationality of the person at the time of the launch. Only 3 of the 40 "first flyers" have been women (<a href="/wiki/Helen_Sharman" title="Helen Sharman">Helen Sharman</a> for the United Kingdom in 1991, <a href="/wiki/Anousheh_Ansari" title="Anousheh Ansari">Anousheh Ansari</a> for Iran in 2006, and <a href="/wiki/Yi_So-yeon" title="Yi So-yeon">Yi So-yeon</a> for South Korea in 2008). Only three nations (Soviet Union/Russia, U.S., China) have launched their own manned spacecraft, with the Soviets/Russians and the American programs providing rides to other nations' astronauts. Twenty-six "first flights" occurred on Soviet or Russian flights while the United States carried thirteen.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_space_travel_by_nationality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ol>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_SovietUnion"></span> The first cosmonaut, Yuri Gagarin, held <a href="/wiki/Citizenship" title="Citizenship">citizenship</a> in both the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_SFSR" title="Russian SFSR">Russian SFSR</a>, according to the applicable provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Constitution of the Soviet Union">Constitution of the Soviet Union</a>. In 1991, the Soviet Union dissolved, and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. At the time of the dissolution, <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Krikalev" title="Sergei Krikalev">Sergei Krikalev</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Alexandrovich_Volkov_(cosmonaut)" title="Alexander Alexandrovich Volkov (cosmonaut)">Alexander Volkov</a> were orbiting <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> on <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">Mir</a>, having been launched into orbit as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_citizen" title="Soviet citizen">Soviet citizens</a>, and having returned to the Earth as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_citizenship" title="Russian citizenship">Russian citizens</a>. Kaleri and Viktorenko were the first Russians to be launched into orbit as Russian citizens only, their launch having occurred subsequent to the dissolution of the Soviet Union.</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_SovietUnion"></span> Under <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale" title="Fédération Aéronautique Internationale">Fédération Aéronautique Internationale</a> rules, the Vostok missions are not deemed true spaceflights, as cosmonauts did not land with the spacecraft (they ejected from the spacecraft and landed separately). The first Soviet mission that did fulfill this requirement was <a href="/wiki/Voskhod_1" title="Voskhod 1">Voskhod 1</a>. (Reference: <cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.fai.org/sporting_code/sc08.pdf" rel="nofollow">"FAI Astronautic Records Commission – Sporting Code Section 8"</a> <span style="font-size:85%;">(PDF)</span>. <i>Fédération Aéronautique Internationale</i><span class="reference-accessdate">. Retrieved <span class="nowrap">2006-04-09</span></span>.</cite><span class="Z3988" title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3ATimeline+of+space+travel+by+nationality&amp;rft.atitle=FAI+Astronautic+Records+Commission+%93+Sporting+Code+Section+8&amp;rft.genre=unknown&amp;rft_id=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.fai.org%2Fsporting_code%2Fsc08.pdf&amp;rft.jtitle=F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration+A%C3%A9ronautique+Internationale&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Ajournal"><span style="display:none;"> </span></span>) However, despite this issue, the FAI does recognize <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">Yuri Gagarin</a> as the first <i>person</i> to complete a spaceflight. (Reference: <cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.fai.org/awards/award.asp?id=9" rel="nofollow">"FAI Award: The Yuri A. Gagarin Gold Medal"</a>. <i>Fédération Aéronautique Internationale</i><span class="reference-accessdate">. Retrieved <span class="nowrap">2007-10-10</span></span>.</cite><span class="Z3988" title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3ATimeline+of+space+travel+by+nationality&amp;rft.atitle=FAI+Award%3A+The+Yuri+A.+Gagarin+Gold+Medal&amp;rft.genre=unknown&amp;rft_id=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.fai.org%2Fawards%2Faward.asp%3Fid%3D9&amp;rft.jtitle=F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration+A%C3%A9ronautique+Internationale&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Ajournal"><span style="display:none;"> </span></span>)</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_UnitedStates"></span> Shepard's spaceflight was suborbital. The first <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> to be launched into Earth orbit was <a href="/wiki/John_Glenn" title="John Glenn">John Glenn</a>, on 20 February 1962.</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Czechoslovakia"></span> In 1993, <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> dissolved and was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>.</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Germany"></span> In 1978, both Jähn himself and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Democratic_Republic" title="German Democratic Republic">German Democratic Republic</a> pronounced him the "first <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> in space", rather than the first "citizen of the German Democratic Republic in space". In 1990, the states of the former East Germany acceded to the <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">Federal Republic of Germany</a>. On 22 January 1992, Ulf Merbold again traveled into space, now representing the <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">reunited Germany within the Federal Republic of Germany</a>. Jähn is, nevertheless, still considered the first German in space, even in the states of the Federal Republic of Germany that comprised the former West Germany.</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_SpaceTourists"></span> This person flew as a commercial, non-governmental space traveller. Apart from Akiyama and Sharman, these space travellers are known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_tourist" title="Space tourist">space tourists</a>.</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Israel"></span> Ilan Ramon was the first Israeli to go into space, but Ramon died during reentry during the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Columbia_disaster" title="Space Shuttle Columbia disaster">Space Shuttle Columbia disaster</a>. Again, this was not deemed a complete spaceflight under FAI rules.</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Ansari"></span> Although recognized as an Iranian citizen by Iranian law, Ansari is also an American citizen and was prohibited from wearing Iranian state symbols by both the United States and Russian governments.</li>
</ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_claims">Other claims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_space_travel_by_nationality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other claims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The above list uses the nationality at the time of launch. Lists with differing criteria might include the following people:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pavel_Popovich" title="Pavel Popovich">Pavel Popovich</a>, first launched 12 August 1962, was the first Ukrainian-born man in space. At the time, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> was a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Soviet_Socialist_Republics" title="Union of Soviet Socialist Republics">Union of Soviet Socialist Republics</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(astronaut)" title="Michael Collins (astronaut)">Michael Collins</a>, first launched 18 July 1966 was born in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> to American parents and was an American citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Anders" title="William Anders">William Anders</a>, American citizen, first launched 21 December 1968, was the first <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>-born man in space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Shatalov" title="Vladimir Shatalov">Vladimir Shatalov</a>, first launched 14 January 1969, was the first Kazakh-born man in space. At the time, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> was a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Soviet_Socialist_Republics" title="Union of Soviet Socialist Republics">Union of Soviet Socialist Republics</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Pogue#Special_honors" title="William Pogue">Bill Pogue</a>, first launched 16 November 1973, as an inductee to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/5_Civilized_Tribes" title="5 Civilized Tribes">5 Civilized Tribes</a> Hall of Fame can lay claim to being the first <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> in space. See <a href="/wiki/John_Herrington" title="John Herrington">John Herrington</a> below regarding technicality of tribal registration.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pyotr_Klimuk" title="Pyotr Klimuk">Pyotr Klimuk</a>, first launched 18 December 1973, was the first Belorussian-born man in space. At the time, <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> was a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Soviet_Socialist_Republics" title="Union of Soviet Socialist Republics">Union of Soviet Socialist Republics</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Dzhanibekov" title="Vladimir Dzhanibekov">Vladimir Dzhanibekov</a>, first launched 16 March 1978, was the first Uzbek-born man in space. At the time, <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a> was a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Soviet_Socialist_Republics" title="Union of Soviet Socialist Republics">Union of Soviet Socialist Republics</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_D._Scully-Power" title="Paul D. Scully-Power">Paul D. Scully-Power</a>, first launched 5 October 1984, was born in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, but was an American citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Taylor_Wang" title="Taylor Wang">Taylor Gun-Jin Wang</a>, first launched 29 April 1985, was born in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> to Chinese parents, but was an American citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lodewijk_van_den_Berg" title="Lodewijk van den Berg">Lodewijk van den Berg</a>, first launched 29 April 1985, was born in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, but was an American citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Patrick_Baudry" title="Patrick Baudry">Patrick Baudry</a>, first launched 17 June 1985, was born in French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cameroun" title="Cameroun">Cameroun</a> (now part of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>), but was a French citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shannon_Lucid" title="Shannon Lucid">Shannon Lucid</a>, first launched 17 June 1985, was born in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> to American parents of European descent, and was an American citizen when she went into space.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franklin_Chang-Diaz" title="Franklin Chang-Diaz">Franklin Chang-Diaz</a>, first launched 12 January 1986, was born in <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>, but was an American citizen when he went into space<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Musa_Manarov" title="Musa Manarov">Musa Manarov</a>, first launched 21 December 1987, was the first Azerbaijan-born man in space. At the time, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> was a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Soviet_Socialist_Republics" title="Union of Soviet Socialist Republics">Union of Soviet Socialist Republics</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anatoly_Solovyev" title="Anatoly Solovyev">Anatoly Solovyev</a>, first launched 7 June 1988, was the first Latvian-born man in space. At the time, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> was a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Soviet_Socialist_Republics" title="Union of Soviet Socialist Republics">Union of Soviet Socialist Republics</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergei_Konstantinovich_Krikalev" title="Sergei Konstantinovich Krikalev">Sergei Konstantinovich Krikalev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Aleksandrovich_Volkov" title="Aleksandr Aleksandrovich Volkov">Aleksandr Aleksandrovich Volkov</a> became Russian rather than Soviet citizens while still in orbit aboard Mir, making them the first purely Russian citizens in space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/James_H._Newman" title="James H. Newman">James H. Newman</a>, American citizen, first launched 12 September 1993, was born in the portion of the <a href="/wiki/Trust_Territory_of_the_Pacific_Islands" title="Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands">United Nations Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands</a> that is now the <a href="/wiki/Federated_States_of_Micronesia" title="Federated States of Micronesia">Federated States of Micronesia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Talgat_Musabayev" title="Talgat Musabayev">Talgat Musabayev</a>, first launched 1 July 1994, was born in the <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic">Kazakh SSR</a> and is known in Kazakhstan as the "first cosmonaut of independent Kazakhstan",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but was a Russian citizen when he went into space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Frederick_W._Leslie" title="Frederick W. Leslie">Frederick W. Leslie</a>, American citizen, first launched 20 October 1995, was born in <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal_Zone" title="Panama Canal Zone">Panama Canal Zone</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carlos_I._Noriega" title="Carlos I. Noriega">Carlos I. Noriega</a>, first launched 15 May 1997, was born in <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, but was an American citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bjarni_Tryggvason" title="Bjarni Tryggvason">Bjarni Tryggvason</a>, first launched 7 August 1997, was born in <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, but was a Canadian citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Salizhan_Sharipov" title="Salizhan Sharipov">Salizhan Sharipov</a>, first launched 22 January 1998, was born in <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a> (then the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirghiz_SSR" title="Kirghiz SSR">Kirghiz SSR</a>), but was a Russian citizen when he went into space. Sharipov is of <a href="/wiki/Uzbeks" title="Uzbeks">Uzbek</a> ancestry.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Philippe_Perrin" title="Philippe Perrin">Philippe Perrin</a>, first launched 5 June 2002, was born in <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, but was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> citizen when he went into space.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_Herrington" title="John Herrington">John Herrington</a>, an American citizen first launched 24 November 2002, is the first <i>tribal registered</i> <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> in space (<a href="/wiki/Chickasaw" title="Chickasaw">Chickasaw</a>). See also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Pogue" title="William Pogue">Bill Pogue</a> above.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Yurchikhin" title="Fyodor Yurchikhin">Fyodor Yurchikhin</a>, first launched 7 October 2002, was born in <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> (then the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgian_SSR" title="Georgian SSR">Georgian SSR</a>). He was a Russian citizen at the time he went into space and is of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontian_Greek" title="Pontian Greek">Pontian Greek</a> descent.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Joseph_M._Acaba" title="Joseph M. Acaba">Joseph M. Acaba</a>, first launched 15 March 2009, was born in the U.S. <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> to American parents of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> descent.</li>
</ul>
