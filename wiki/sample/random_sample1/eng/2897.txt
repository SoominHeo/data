<p><b>Tsingy de Namoroka Strict Nature Reserve</b>, also known as Namoroka National Park, is a strict nature reserve located in the northwestern part of <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mahajanga_Province" title="Mahajanga Province">Mahajanga Province</a>, specifically, the <a href="/wiki/Soalala_District" title="Soalala District">Soalala District</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsingy_de_Namoroka_Strict_Nature_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Namoroka Strict Nature Reserve was established in 1927<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdpa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-wdpa-1">[1]</a></sup> and became a special reserve in 1966. It forms a complex with the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Baie_de_Baly_National_Park" title="Baie de Baly National Park">Baie de Baly National Park</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_and_geography">Climate and geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsingy_de_Namoroka_Strict_Nature_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate and geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located in the northwestern part of Madagascar about 50 km south of <a href="/wiki/Soalala" title="Soalala">Soalala</a>, Tsingy de Namoroka has a dry season lasting about seven months with a rainy season lasting only five months. The resulting precipitation is about 115 cm per year, while the average temperature hovers around 25°C.</p>
<p>The park is known for its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karst_topography" title="Karst topography">tsingy</a> walls, caves, canyons, and natural swimming pools.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fauna">Fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsingy_de_Namoroka_Strict_Nature_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like much of Madagascar, Tsingy de Namoroka is known for its abundant and diverse wildlife. Of its over 81 species of birds, 31 are <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to Madagascar with 23 other species endemic to Madagascar and other neighboring islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tsingy de Namoroka is also home to over 30 species of reptiles, five types of frogs, and 16 mammals, including eight lemurs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wildmadagascar_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-wildmadagascar-3">[3]</a></sup> Specifically, Namoroka is home to the following lemur species:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wildmadagascar_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-wildmadagascar-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Von_der_Decken%27s_sifaka" title="Von der Decken's sifaka">Von der Decken's sifaka</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Red_lemur" title="Red lemur">Red lemur</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eastern_lesser_bamboo_lemur" title="Eastern lesser bamboo lemur">Eastern lesser bamboo lemur</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masoala_fork-crowned_lemur" title="Masoala fork-crowned lemur">Masoala fork-crowned lemur</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gray_mouse_lemur" title="Gray mouse lemur">Gray mouse lemur</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Milne-Edwards%27_sportive_lemur" title="Milne-Edwards' sportive lemur">Milne-Edwards' sportive lemur</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fat-tailed_dwarf_lemur" title="Fat-tailed dwarf lemur">Fat-tailed dwarf lemur</a></li>
