<p><b>Sematophyllaceae</b> is a family of <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">mosses</a>. It includes 40 <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a> with a total of about 150 species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fna_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-fna-1">[1]</a></sup> They occur nearly worldwide, especially in tropical and temperate regions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fna_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-fna-1">[1]</a></sup> These mosses are known commonly as <b>signal mosses</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-delta_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-delta-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>These species form yellow to yellow-green mats with reddish stems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fna_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-fna-1">[1]</a></sup> They grow on rocks in wet or humid places.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-delta_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-delta-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
