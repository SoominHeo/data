<p><b>Nabari</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">名張市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nabari-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in central <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, bordering on <a href="/wiki/Nara_Prefecture" title="Nara Prefecture">Nara Prefecture</a> to the west.</p>
<p>As of August 2015, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 78,190 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 603 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area was 129.77 square kilometres (50.10 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nabari,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area around Nabari is part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Iga_Province" title="Iga Province">Iga Province</a>, and Nabari developed from the <a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">Nara period</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Shukuba" title="Shukuba">post station</a> on the Ise Kaido, or highway from the capital to the <a href="/wiki/Ise_Grand_Shrine" title="Ise Grand Shrine">Ise Grand Shrines</a>.</p>
<p>Nabari Town in Nabari District in Mie Prefecture was established on April 1, 1889. The district was merged with Iga District in 1896 to form Naga District. The modern city of Nabari was established on March 31, 1954. Voters rejected a proposal to merge with the neighboring city of Iga in February 2003.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nabari,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Nabari is mixed, with agriculture (especially for fruits) and <i><a href="/wiki/Sake" title="Sake">sake</a></i> production, along with light manufacturing and chemicals production predominant. Due to its proximity to Osaka and Nara, the city is also increasing becoming a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedroom_community" title="Bedroom community">bedroom community</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nabari,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><a href="/wiki/Kogakkan_University" title="Kogakkan University">Kogakkan University</a> – Nabari branch campus</li>

</ul>
