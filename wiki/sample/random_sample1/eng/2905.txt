<p><b>Navashino</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Нава́шино</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nizhny_Novgorod_Oblast" title="Nizhny Novgorod Oblast">Nizhny Novgorod Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the right bank of the <a href="/wiki/Oka_River" title="Oka River">Oka River</a>, 158 kilometers (98 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Nizhny_Novgorod" title="Nizhny Novgorod">Nizhny Novgorod</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>, its population was 16,416.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navashino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town of Navashino was founded in 1957 by merging two neighboring settlements of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mordovshchikovo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mordovshchikovo (page does not exist)">Mordovshchikovo</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lipnya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lipnya (page does not exist)">Lipnya</a> and named after the nearby <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">railway station</a> of Navashino.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A railway was built in the area in 1912.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navashino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, it is, together with fifty <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural localities</a>, incorporated as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">town of oblast significance</a> of Navashino</b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Nizhny_Novgorod_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Nizhny Novgorod Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NizhnyNovgorodO_admlaw_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-NizhnyNovgorodO_admlaw-2">[2]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the town of oblast significance of Navashino is incorporated as <b>Navashinsky Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NizhnyNovgorodONavashinskyUO_mun_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-NizhnyNovgorodONavashinskyUO_mun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until May 2015, the town served as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Navashinsky_District" title="Navashinsky District">Navashinsky District</a> and, within the framework of administrative divisions, was incorporated within that district as a <a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">town of district significance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NizhnyNovgorodO_oldadmlist_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NizhnyNovgorodO_oldadmlist-10">[10]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, it was incorporated as Navashino Urban Settlement within Navashinsky Municipal District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NizhnyNovgorodONavashinskyD_mun_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-NizhnyNovgorodONavashinskyD_mun-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navashino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Joint-stock_company#Russia" title="Joint-stock company">OAO</a> <i>Okskaya Sudoverf</i> is headquartered in Navashino. The shipbuilding company, originating from a local shipyard founded in 1907, builds boats for Volga and sea navigation. Some of their tankers are to be sold to customers as far afield as <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Petronas" title="Petronas">Petronas</a>, which plans to use the vessels at its <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian</a> subsidiary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navashino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A new road bridge over the <a href="/wiki/Oka_River" title="Oka River">Oka River</a> was opened in October 2009 near Navashino.</p>
