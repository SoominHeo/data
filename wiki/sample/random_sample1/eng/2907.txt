<p>The <i><b>nabal</b></i> is a long, straight <a href="/wiki/Brass" title="Brass">brass</a> horn used in Korean traditional music. As the instrument has no valves or finger holes it is not a melodic instrument but rather plays a single sustained tone. The precise frequency of the tone produced can be quite different depending on the size of the individual instrument.</p>
<p>The <i>nabal</i> has historically been used primarily in the military procession music called <i><a href="/wiki/Daechwita" title="Daechwita">daechwita</a></i>, as well as in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nongak" title="Nongak">nongak</a></i> (rural farmers' music) to signal the beginning and end of performances. Here is the link that shows you how kereans use Nabal. <a class="external free" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=csevjEQhu4s" rel="nofollow">https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=csevjEQhu4s</a></p>
