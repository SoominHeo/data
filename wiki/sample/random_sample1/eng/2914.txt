<p><b>Navinchandra Ramgoolam</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Star_and_Key_of_the_Indian_Ocean" title="Order of the Star and Key of the Indian Ocean">GCSK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_College_of_Physicians" title="Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians">FRCP</a> (born 14 July 1947) is a former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Mauritius" title="Prime Minister of Mauritius">Prime Minister of Mauritius</a> and leader of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Mauritius)" title="Labour Party (Mauritius)">Labour Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lab_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-lab-1">[1]</a></sup> He was <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition_(Mauritius)" title="Leader of the Opposition (Mauritius)">Leader of the Opposition</a> from 1991 to 1995. He served as Prime Minister for the first time from December 1995 until September 2000, and became Leader of the Opposition again from October 2000 to 4 July 2005. On 5 July 2005, he became prime minister for a second term after his <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Sociale" title="Alliance Sociale">Alliance Sociale</a> won the general elections. He served again as Prime Minister from 2005 to 2014, when he was defeated.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Navin Ramgoolam was born on 14 July 1947 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Seewoosagur_Ramgoolam" title="Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam">Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam</a> and his wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lady_Sushil_Ramgoolam" title="Lady Sushil Ramgoolam">Lady Sushil Ramjoorawon</a>. Navin Ramgoolam belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Kurmi" title="Kurmi">Kurmi</a> community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He attended the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_Curepipe" title="Royal College Curepipe">Royal College Curepipe</a> from 1960 to 1966 and proceeded to study medicine in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> between 1968 and 1975, where he obtained the <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Medicine,_Bachelor_of_Surgery#Ireland" title="Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery">LRCP&amp;SI</a> licentiates from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Physicians_of_Ireland" title="Royal College of Physicians of Ireland">Royal College of Physicians of Ireland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Surgeons_in_Ireland" title="Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland">Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland</a>.</p>
<p>When his father, the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_General_of_Mauritius" title="Governor General of Mauritius">Governor General</a> and former Prime Minister, died in December 1985, Ramgoolam was on the point of immigrating to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> when Sir <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Satcam_Boolell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Satcam Boolell (page does not exist)">Satcam Boolell</a> (then leader of Labour Party) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Berenger" title="Paul Berenger">Paul Berenger</a> (the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_Militant_Movement" title="Mauritian Militant Movement">Mauritian Militant Movement</a>) convinced him to return assume the leadership of the Labour party. He subsequently served as a medical doctor at Dr A.G Jeetoo Hospital in Mauritius from 1985 to 1987. In 1987, he started his law studies at <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">The London School of Economics and Political Science</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a>. Upon completion of his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Laws" title="Bachelor of Laws">LLB</a> degree in 1990, he returned to Mauritius to face the electorate as the new leader of the Labour party in the <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_1991" title="Mauritian general election, 1991">1991 general election</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1991_elections_and_first_candidacy">1991 elections and first candidacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1991 elections and first candidacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_1991" title="Mauritian general election, 1991">Mauritian general election, 1991</a></div>
<p>The leadup to the <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_1991" title="Mauritian general election, 1991">general elections</a> held in 1991 saw a realignment of the country's major political parties. The <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_Militant_Movement" title="Mauritian Militant Movement">Mauritian Militant Movement</a> (MMM), which had been the main opposition party, decided to contest the election as an ally of Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Anerood_Jugnauth" title="Anerood Jugnauth">Anerood Jugnauth</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Militant_Socialist_Movement" title="Militant Socialist Movement">Militant Socialist Movement</a> (MSM). Ramgoolam's Labour Party and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ga%C3%ABtan_Duval&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gaëtan Duval (page does not exist)">Gaëtan Duval</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Parti_Mauricien_Social_D%C3%A9mocrate" title="Parti Mauricien Social Démocrate">Parti Mauricien Social Démocrate</a> (PMSD), both of which had been allied with the MSM in <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_1987" title="Mauritian general election, 1987">the previous election</a>, formed a separate alliance, along with the <a href="/wiki/Rodrigues_People%27s_Organisation" title="Rodrigues People's Organisation">Rodrigues People's Organisation</a> (OPR). A major campaign issue was the proposal of the MSM-MMM alliance to cut the country's ties with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Monarchy" title="British Monarchy">British Monarchy</a> and declare a republic with a <a href="/wiki/President_of_Mauritius" title="President of Mauritius">President</a> as head of state. Ramgoolam opposed this, arguing that the Presidential office would be too ceremonial, leaving all executive powers effectively in the hands of the Prime Minister.</p>
<p>The election turned out to be a landslide for Jugnauth. The <a href="/wiki/MSM/MMM" title="MSM/MMM">MSM/MMM</a> alliance won 57 of the 62 directly elected seats. Of the 5 seats won by Ramgoolam's alliance, 2 were held by the OPR and 3 by his own Labour Party (<a href="/wiki/Arvin_Boolell" title="Arvin Boolell">Arvin Boolell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vasant_Bunwaree" title="Vasant Bunwaree">Vasant Bunwaree</a> and himself, being elected in constituency <a href="/wiki/Constituencies_of_Mauritius" title="Constituencies of Mauritius">5, Triolet &amp; Pamplemoussess</a>). Following his defeat, he took leave as Leader of the Opposition and returned to London to complete his law vocational course at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inns_of_Court_School_of_Law" title="Inns of Court School of Law">Inns of Court School of Law</a>. He was called to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_Bar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="United Kingdom Bar (page does not exist)">United Kingdom Bar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inner_Temple" title="Inner Temple">Inner Temple</a> in 1993.</p>
<p>In 1993, Prime Minister Jugnauth asked the <a href="/wiki/Speaker_(politics)" title="Speaker (politics)">Speaker</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Mauritius" title="National Assembly of Mauritius">National Assembly</a> to declare Ramgoolam's seat vacant as he had not attended Parliament for three consecutive momeths. The Speaker referred the case to the <a href="/wiki/Judiciary_of_Mauritius" title="Judiciary of Mauritius">Court</a> but the Judge found that it was unconstitutional.</p>
<p>Shortly afterwards, Jugnauth dismissed Bérenger from the Cabinet after leaning that Ramgoolam had hosted him at a private dinner at his Riverwalk residence. Bérenger and 10 MMM members joined Ramgoolam on the opposition benches. Although the MMM caucus was larger than the Labour Party caucus, he agreed to let Ramgoolam continue as Leader of the Opposition.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1995_elections">1995 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1995 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_1995" title="Mauritian general election, 1995">Mauritian general election, 1995</a></div>
<p>The Labour Party and the MMM went on to forge an alliance to contest the <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_1995" title="Mauritian general election, 1995">1995 elections</a>. The alliance won all 60 directly elected seats from the mainland (with Labour taking 35 seats and the MMM 25). Ramgoolam became Prime Minister with Bérenger as his Deputy. The coalition soon fractured, however, and Ramgoolam dismissed Bérenger in 1997. Bérenger then became Leader of the Opposition, while Ramgoolam formed a one-party government.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_elections">2000 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_2000" title="Mauritian general election, 2000">Mauritian general election, 2000</a></div>
<p>Jugnauth and Bérenger formed a new alliance to contest the <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_2000" title="Mauritian general election, 2000">2000 elections</a>. Part of the agreement was to allow Jugnauth to serve for the first three years of the five-year term, then resign to assume the Presidency and allow Bérenger to complete his unexpired term. Ramgoolam, for his part, formed an alliance with the <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_Party_of_Xavier-Luc_Duval" title="Mauritian Party of Xavier-Luc Duval">Mauritian Party of Xavier-Luc Duval</a> (PMXD), a breakaway from the PMSD led by <a href="/wiki/Xavier-Luc_Duval" title="Xavier-Luc Duval">Xavier-Luc Duval</a>, the son of Sir <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ga%C3%ABtan_Duval&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gaëtan Duval (page does not exist)">Gaëtan Duval</a>. The MSM/MMM alliance won 54 of the 60 directly elected mainland seats. Ramgoolam, who had retained his own seat, became Leader of the Opposition.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_elections">2005 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2005 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His Alliance Social won the general elections against the <a href="/wiki/MSM/MMM" title="MSM/MMM">MSM/MMM</a> outgoing government. He was again appointed as prime minister with a majority of 38 out of 60 seats. His alliance also won the local/municipal elections in 2006 where the <a href="/wiki/MSM/MMM" title="MSM/MMM">MSM/MMM</a> was severely defeated. These consecutive defeats and internal instability caused the break-up of the <a href="/wiki/MSM/MMM" title="MSM/MMM">MSM/MMM</a> coalition.</p>
<p>As the MSM had more seats than the MMM and <a href="/wiki/Pravind_Jugnauth" title="Pravind Jugnauth">Pravind Jugnauth</a> was not elected, <a href="/wiki/Nando_Bodha" title="Nando Bodha">Nando Bodha</a> was appointed as Leader of the Opposition.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_elections">2010 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_2010" title="Mauritian general election, 2010">Mauritian general election, 2010</a></div>
<p>With the <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_2010" title="Mauritian general election, 2010">2010 elections</a> approaching, Ramgoolam decided in 2008 to support Jugnauth for re-election as <a href="/wiki/President_of_Mauritius" title="President of Mauritius">President</a>, to forestall a possible return by Jugnauth to parliamentary politics, where Ramgoolam viewed him as a potential threat. Jugnauth's condition for accepting the offer was an alliance between the Labour Party and the MSM. At Ramgoolam's insistence, the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_de_L%27Avenir" title="Alliance de L'Avenir">Alliance de L'Avenir</a> also included the PMSD, into which the PMXD, and its leader, Xavier-Luc Duval, had merged; seven of the sixty parliamentary candidates would come from the PMSD).</p>
<p>The Alliance de L'Avenir won 41 of the 60 directly elected seats. Ramgoolam remained Prime Minister and <a href="/wiki/Pravind_Jugnauth" title="Pravind Jugnauth">Pravind Jugnauth</a>, son of Sir Anerood Jugnauth, became his Deputy. Following the involvement of some members of the MSM in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Medpoint_Scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Medpoint Scandal (page does not exist)">Medpoint Scandal</a>, however, Ramgoolam dismissed the MSM from the government.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_elections">2014 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_2014" title="Mauritian general election, 2014">Mauritian general election, 2014</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mauritian_general_election,_2014" title="Mauritian general election, 2014">general elections</a> originally scheduled for 2015 were brought forward to December 2014. The Labour Party made a new alliance with the MMM, proposing a constitutional amendment to upgrade the Presidency to a less ceremonial role. Ramgoolam and Bérenger, the MMM leader, claimed that the election was a referendum on the proposal, which they called the Second Republic. If the alliance one more than 45 of the 60 directly elected mainland seats, the Constitution would be amended; Ramgoolam would run for the Presidency and Bérenger would succeed him as Prime Minister.</p>
<p>Ramgoolam and Berenger were opposed by the MSM-led <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_Lepep&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alliance Lepep (page does not exist)">Alliance Lepep</a>, which also included the PMSD, which had been Ramgoolam's coalition partner, and a new party called <a href="/wiki/Muvman_Liberater" title="Muvman Liberater">Muvman Liberater</a>, formed by a defector from the MMM. The Alliance Lepep, which opposed the proposal for the Second Republic, won 47 seats out of 60. The Labour-MMM alliance won only 13 seats, 9 from the MMM and 4 from the Labour Party. Ramgoolam lost his seat. On 12 December 2014, he resigned as Prime Minister of Mauritius.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_arrest">2015 arrest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2015 arrest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2015, Ramgoolam was arrested at his residence at <a href="/wiki/Port_Louis" title="Port Louis">Port Louis</a>. Police questioned him about a burglary which took place at his Roche Noires residence in 2011 and allegations that he had asked a businessman to make a false report of the break in to the local police station asserting he was not there at the time. Police are also investigating the alleged suicide in custody of a suspect in the burglary. The police pathologist has repeatedly said that the death was a suicide and Ramgoolam has denied any foul play, accusing his critics of trying to discredit him. On the same day, Police searched his house at Riverwalk and found Rs 220 Million, nearly half of it in foreign currencies. Some of the money was contained in envelopes or wrappers with good-will messages for the election and Ramgoolam has said that the monies represented election donations to his Party. No law prevents such donations in Mauritius. However, he was arrested on two charges, <i>conspiracy to do a wrongful act</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Money_laundering" title="Money laundering">money laundering</a>.</i> He was held in custody and released on <a href="/wiki/Bail" title="Bail">bail</a> the following day. On 14 February, Ramgoolam announced that he was taking leave from the activities of the Labour Party to devote himself to his legal battle and defend his honour. He accused unnamed adversaries of spreading misinformation with the intention to put an end to the Labour Party. He designated <a href="/wiki/Arvin_Boolell" title="Arvin Boolell">Arvin Boolell</a> as the new spokesman for the Labour Party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defi_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-defi-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 May 2015, he was arrested again on three charges and was granted bail after spending two nights in police detention.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ramgoolam has received several accolades and honours. In 1998 he was made an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honorary_Fellow" title="Honorary Fellow">Honorary Fellow</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics_and_Political_Science" title="London School of Economics and Political Science">London School of Economics and Political Science</a> (LSE), Dr Honoris Causa by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Mauritius" title="University of Mauritius">University of Mauritius</a>, Dr Honoris Causa from <a href="/wiki/Aligarh_Muslim_University" title="Aligarh Muslim University">Aligarh Muslim University</a>, India and Dr Honoris Causa by the <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru_University,_Delhi" title="Jawaharlal Nehru University, Delhi">Jawaharlal Nehru University</a>, India in October 2005. Other awards he attained are the <a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Honour" title="Legion of Honour">Grand Officier de la Legion d’Honneur</a> from France in March 2006, the Honorary Freeman of Rodrigues from Rodrigues Regional Assembly in March 2007, The Wilberforce Medal from Wilberforce Lecture Trust, Hull, United Kingdom in June 2007, The Rajiv Gandhi Award from <a href="/wiki/Mumbai_Regional_Congress_Committee" title="Mumbai Regional Congress Committee">Mumbai Regional Congress Committee</a>, India in August 2007, The Pravasi Bharatiya Samman Award from <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Government of the Republic of India</a> in January 2008 as well as <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Star_and_Key_of_the_Indian_Ocean" title="Order of the Star and Key of the Indian Ocean">Grand Commander of the Order of the Star and Key of the Indian Ocean</a> (GCSK) from the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Mauritius" title="Government of Mauritius">Government of the Republic of Mauritius</a> in March 2008. In Paris, Ramgoolam received the Prix Louise Michel, awarded generally each year to a high personality in recognition of his or her outstanding contribution in the political field. He was made Doctor of Science (Honoris Causa) by the Padmashree Dr D. Y. Patil University, Mumbai, India in February 2009, <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_College_of_Physicians" title="Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians">Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians</a> (FRCP) of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Physicians" title="Royal College of Physicians">Royal College of Physicians</a>, London in May 2009, Honorary Doctor by <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire_University" title="Staffordshire University">Staffordshire University</a>, United Kingdom in July 2010, Order of the Rule of Law by the <a href="/wiki/World_Jurist_Association" title="World Jurist Association">World Jurist Association</a>, Bethesda, Maryland, USA in April 2011, Overseas Bencher by the <a href="/wiki/Inner_Temple" title="Inner Temple">Inner Temple</a>, United Kingdom in April 2011 and Doctor of Laws (Honoris Causa) by the <a href="/wiki/Kurukshetra_University" title="Kurukshetra University">Kurukshetra University</a>, Haryana, India in February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cur_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cur-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navin_Ramgoolam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Navin Ramgoolam is the son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Seewoosagur_Ramgoolam" title="Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam">Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam</a> who was the sixth <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Mauritius" title="Governor-General of Mauritius">Governor General</a> of Mauritius, as well as the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Minister" title="Chief Minister">Chief Minister</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Mauritius" title="Prime Minister of Mauritius">Prime Minister</a> of Mauritius. His mother is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lady_Sushil_Ramgoolam" title="Lady Sushil Ramgoolam">Lady Sushil Ramgoolam</a> (1922-1984). Navin is married to <a href="/wiki/Veena_Ramgoolam" title="Veena Ramgoolam">Veena Ramgoolam</a></p>
