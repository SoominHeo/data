<p><b>Nayanthara</b> (born <b>Diana Mariam Kurian</b>; 18 November 1984) is an Indian film actress who predominantly appears in <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a> films along with a few <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malayalam_language" title="Malayalam language">Malayalam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Telugu_language" title="Telugu language">Telugu</a> films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She is the recipient of <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_Nayanthara" title="List of awards and nominations received by Nayanthara">numerous awards</a>.</p>
<p>Nayanthara made her acting debut in the 2003 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Kerala" title="Cinema of Kerala">Malayalam film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Manassinakkare" title="Manassinakkare">Manassinakkare</a></i> with Jayaram and <i><a href="/wiki/Vismayathumbathu" title="Vismayathumbathu">Vismayathumbathu</a></i> with Super Star Mohanlal (2004) . She also acted in a supporting role in super hit malayalam filim <a href="/wiki/Natturajavu" title="Natturajavu">Natturajavu</a> before foraying into <a href="/wiki/Tamil_cinema" title="Tamil cinema">Tamil</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Andhra_Pradesh" title="Cinema of Andhra Pradesh">Telugu cinema</a>. She made her debut in Tamil cinema with <i><a href="/wiki/Ayya_(2005_Tamil_film)" title="Ayya (2005 Tamil film)">Ayya</a></i> (2005) and Telugu with <i><a href="/wiki/Lakshmi_(2006_film)" title="Lakshmi (2006 film)">Lakshmi</a></i> (2006). Both were successful. After which she was the female lead in numerous commercially successful Tamil and Telugu films like <i><a href="/wiki/Chandramukhi" title="Chandramukhi">Chandramukhi</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Dubai_Seenu" title="Dubai Seenu">Dubai Seenu</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Tulasi_(film)" title="Tulasi (film)">Tulasi</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Billa_(2007_film)" title="Billa (2007 film)">Billa</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Yaaradi_Nee_Mohini" title="Yaaradi Nee Mohini">Yaaradi Nee Mohini</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Aadhavan" title="Aadhavan">Aadhavan</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/Adhurs" title="Adhurs">Adhurs</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Simha_(film)" title="Simha (film)">Simha</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Boss_Engira_Bhaskaran" title="Boss Engira Bhaskaran">Boss Engira Bhaskaran</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Sri_Rama_Rajyam" title="Sri Rama Rajyam">Sri Rama Rajyam</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Raja_Rani_(2013_film)" title="Raja Rani (2013 film)">Raja Rani</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Arrambam" title="Arrambam">Arrambam</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Thani_Oruvan" title="Thani Oruvan">Thani Oruvan</a></i> (2015), <i><a href="/wiki/Maya_(2015_Indian_film)" title="Maya (2015 Indian film)">Maya</a></i> (2015), <i><a href="/wiki/Naanum_Rowdy_Dhaan" title="Naanum Rowdy Dhaan">Naanum Rowdy Dhaan</a></i> (2015), <i><a href="/wiki/Babu_Bangaram" title="Babu Bangaram">Babu Bangaram</a></i> (2016) and <i><a href="/wiki/Iru_Mugan" title="Iru Mugan">Iru Mugan</a></i> (2016), making her the highest paid actress in South Indian cinema.</p>
<p>In 2010, she also made her <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a> film debut through the Kannada-Telugu bilingual film <i><a href="/wiki/Super_(2010_Indian_film)" title="Super (2010 Indian film)">Super</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmibeat.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmibeat.com-8">[8]</a></sup> her only appearance in a Kannada film to-date. Her portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Sita" title="Sita">Sita</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Sri_Rama_Rajyam" title="Sri Rama Rajyam">Sri Rama Rajyam</a></i> (2011) earned her the <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Telugu" title="Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Telugu">Filmfare Award for Best Telugu Actress</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nandi_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Nandi Award for Best Actress">Nandi Award for Best Actress</a>. She won the <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Tamil" title="Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Tamil">Filmfare Award for Best Tamil Actress</a> for her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Raja_Rani_(2013_film)" title="Raja Rani (2013 film)">Raja Rani</a></i> (2013). <i>Kochi Times</i> listed her in the "List of 15 Most Desirable Women in 2014".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nayanthara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nayanthara was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bengaluru" title="Bengaluru">Bengaluru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes1-4">[4]</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Malayali" title="Malayali">Malayali</a> <a href="/wiki/Malankara_Orthodox_Syrian_Church" title="Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church">Orthodox Christian</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> parents Kurian Kodiyattu and Omana Kurian who hail from an ancestral family Kodiyatt in <a href="/wiki/Tiruvalla" title="Tiruvalla">Tiruvalla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Telegraph_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Telegraph-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Her brother, Leno, who is nine years elder to her, lives in <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> As her father was an <a href="/wiki/Indian_Air_Force" title="Indian Air Force">Indian Air Force</a> official, she studied in various parts of India, mainly in <a href="/wiki/North_India" title="North India">North India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> She did her schooling in <a href="/wiki/Jamnagar" title="Jamnagar">Jamnagar</a>, Gujarat and <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Tiruvalla" title="Tiruvalla">Tiruvalla</a>, she studied at Balikamadom Girls Higher Secondary School and then attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marthoma_College" title="Marthoma College">Marthoma College</a> for her <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Literature" title="English Literature">English Literature</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nayanthara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut_in_Malayalam_cinema_.282003.E2.80.9304.29">Debut in Malayalam cinema (2003–04)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nayanthara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Debut in Malayalam cinema (2003–04)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While studying at college, Nayanthara worked part-time as a model. She was spotted by director <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sathyan_Anthikkad" title="Sathyan Anthikkad">Sathyan Anthikkad</a>, who had seen some of her modelling assignments and approached her to play a pivotal role in his film <i><a href="/wiki/Manassinakkare" title="Manassinakkare">Manassinakkare</a></i> (2003).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff2005_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-rediff2005-15">[15]</a></sup> Although she turned down the offer initially, as she was not interested in films, she gave in eventually and agreed to do "just that one film".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff2005_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-rediff2005-15">[15]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Manassinakkare" title="Manassinakkare">Manassinakkare</a></i> went on to become a high financial success and she continued to receive acting offers. Her both releases in 2004, <i><a href="/wiki/Natturajavu" title="Natturajavu">Natturajavu</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Shaji_Kailas" title="Shaji Kailas">Shaji Kailas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fazil_(director)" title="Fazil (director)">Fazil</a>'s psychological thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Vismayathumbathu" title="Vismayathumbathu">Vismayathumbathu</a></i> saw her co-starring alongside <a href="/wiki/Mohanlal" title="Mohanlal">Mohanlal</a>; while she played the protagonist's adopted sister in the former, she portrayed a ghost in the latter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Vismayathumbathu" title="Vismayathumbathu">Vismayathumbathu</a></i>, in particular, was lauded, with critics claiming that she had "stolen the thunder with her author-backed role",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and was "the revelation of the film".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> She also acted in <i><a href="/wiki/Thaskaraveeran_(2005_film)" title="Thaskaraveeran (2005 film)">Thaskara Veeran</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rappakal" title="Rappakal">Rappakal</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_in_Tamil_and_Telugu_cinema_.282005.E2.80.9307.29">Work in Tamil and Telugu cinema (2005–07)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nayanthara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Work in Tamil and Telugu cinema (2005–07)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nayanthara subsequently started appearing in Tamil and Telugu films. In 2005, she was cast in <a href="/wiki/Hari_(director)" title="Hari (director)">Hari's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Ayya_(2005_Tamil_film)" title="Ayya (2005 Tamil film)">Ayya</a></i>, debuting in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tamil_film_industry" title="Tamil film industry">Tamil film industry</a>. Behindwoods.com stated that she had made a "sensational debut in Tamil",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> while a reviewer from Nowrunning.com said that her "presence with her beautiful smile is crowd winning".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> While still shooting for <i>Ayya</i>, she was selected for the <a href="/wiki/Comedy_horror" title="Comedy horror">comedy horror</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Chandramukhi" title="Chandramukhi">Chandramukhi</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> after its director <a href="/wiki/P._Vasu" title="P. Vasu">P. Vasu</a>'s wife had seen <i>Manassinakkare</i> and recommended her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The film ran for over 800 days in theatres,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> eventually turning Nayanthara into one of the most-sought after actresses in Tamil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-24">[24]</a></sup> Later that year she appeared in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_R_Murugadoss" title="A R Murugadoss">A R Murugadoss</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Ghajini_(2005_film)" title="Ghajini (2005 film)">Ghajini</a></i>, in which she played a secondary female character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-24">[24]</a></sup> Nayanthara then accepted to appear in an <a href="/wiki/Item_number" title="Item number">item number</a> as herself in the <a href="/wiki/Perarasu" title="Perarasu">Perarasu</a>-directed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masala_(film_genre)" title="Masala (film genre)">masala film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Sivakasi_(film)" title="Sivakasi (film)">Sivakasi</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nayanthara's first release in 2006 was <i><a href="/wiki/Kalvanin_Kadhali_(2006_film)" title="Kalvanin Kadhali (2006 film)">Kalvanin Kadhali</a></i>. Indiaglitz.com stated her performance was the "strength of the film".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> She next made her debut in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Andhra_Pradesh" title="Cinema of Andhra Pradesh">Telugu</a>, starring in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Lakshmi_(2006_film)" title="Lakshmi (2006 film)">Lakshmi</a></i>, following which she performed in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boss,_I_Love_You" title="Boss, I Love You">Boss, I Love You</a></i>. Three Tamil films in which she enacted the lead female characters – <i><a href="/wiki/Vallavan" title="Vallavan">Vallavan</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Thalaimagan" title="Thalaimagan">Thalaimagan</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/E_(film)" title="E (film)">E</a></i> – were released simultaneously during Deepavali 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> All three films opened to mixed reviews;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-29">[29]</a></sup> In <i>Vallavan</i>, she portrayed a lecturer who falls in love with a student younger than her. Sify wrote: "Nayanthara virtually walks away with the film and has never looked so beautiful. She looks gorgeous especially in songs and does justice to her well-etched out role".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The science fiction thriller <i>E</i> featured Nayanthara in the role of a bar dancer. Reviewers from Rediff stated that she made an impact,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Indiaglitz said she was "very adequate and impressive in a slightly complicated role",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and that she had "come up with a good performance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-29">[29]</a></sup> In <i>Thalaimagan</i>, she played a news reporter, with critics agreeing that she did not have much to do in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_recognition_and_widespread_success_.282007.E2.80.932011.29">Public recognition and widespread success (2007–2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nayanthara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Public recognition and widespread success (2007–2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nayanthara regained her star billing in Kollywood acting in the <a href="/wiki/Vishnuvardhan_(director)" title="Vishnuvardhan (director)">Vishnuvardhan</a>-directed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gangster_film" title="Gangster film">gangster film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Billa_(2007_film)" title="Billa (2007 film)">Billa</a></i> (2007). A remake of the <a href="/wiki/Billa_(1980_film)" title="Billa (1980 film)">same-titled 1980 Tamil film</a>, it went on to become a success at the box office<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (November 2015)">vague</span></a></i>]</sup>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> while Nayanthara received many accolades for her performance as Sasha in her new glamour look.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sify" title="Sify">Sify</a> heaped praise on her, describing her as a "show stopper". The reviewer further wrote: "Nayanthara has gone full throttle to look her sexiest best [...] She has a beautiful body which she flaunts daringly [...] and is also able to bring out the cold aloofness and bitterness of her character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Similarly a critic from Nowrunning.com noted that she looked "great in mini-skirts, jacket, dark glasses and tall boots".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008 she had five releases, four of which were in Tamil. Her first release, the family entertainer <i><a href="/wiki/Yaaradi_Nee_Mohini" title="Yaaradi Nee Mohini">Yaaradi Nee Mohini</a></i>. Behindwoods.com wrote: "Nayan dispels her bombshell act and proves that she can more than just that. She breaks into tears when needed, shows vicious contempt when rubbed the wrong way in the name of love, and looks endearing in songs". Nowrunning.com stated that she "exhibits her acting skills in full measure [and] gives a moving performance in emotional scenes",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Sify" title="Sify">Sify</a>'s critic wrote that she looked "sensational and has done a great job in perhaps the meatiest role she has done so far".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> She won the <a href="/wiki/Vijay_Award_for_Favourite_Heroine" title="Vijay Award for Favourite Heroine">Vijay Award for Favourite Heroine</a> for second consecutive year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> as well as a nomination for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Best_Actress_Award_(Tamil)" title="Filmfare Best Actress Award (Tamil)">Best Actress prize</a> at the <a href="/wiki/56th_Filmfare_Awards_South" title="56th Filmfare Awards South">56th Filmfare Awards South</a>. Her subsequent releases were <i><a href="/wiki/Kuselan" title="Kuselan">Kuselan</a></i> , <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sathyam_(2008_film)" title="Sathyam (2008 film)">Sathyam</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Villu_(film)" title="Villu (film)">Villu</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Aegan" title="Aegan">Aegan</a></i> . In 2009 she released <i><a href="/wiki/Aadhavan" title="Aadhavan">Aadhavan</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2010, all her releases, which featured her as the female lead, turned out to be commercial successes: she had five box office hits in the four Southern languages – <i><a href="/wiki/Adhurs" title="Adhurs">Adhurs</a></i> (Telugu) <i><a href="/wiki/Bodyguard_(2010_film)" title="Bodyguard (2010 film)">Bodyguard</a></i> (Malayalam), and <i><a href="/wiki/Simha_(film)" title="Simha (film)">Simha</a></i> (Telugu), <i><a href="/wiki/Boss_Engira_Bhaskaran" title="Boss Engira Bhaskaran">Boss Engira Bhaskaran</a></i> (Tamil) and <i><a href="/wiki/Super_(2010_Indian_film)" title="Super (2010 Indian film)">Super</a></i> (Kannada, Telugu).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> She won the <a href="/wiki/Asianet_Film_Awards#Best_Actress" title="Asianet Film Awards">Asianet Award for Best Actress</a> for her performance in <i>Bodyguard</i>. The latter three, in particular, were particularly good for Nayanthara, with <i>Simha</i> becoming one of the highest-grossing Telugu films of the year and <i>Boss Engira Bhaskaran</i> releasing to positive reviews, and becoming a financial success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Upendra_(actor)" title="Upendra (actor)">Upendra</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Super_(2010_Indian_film)" title="Super (2010 Indian film)">Super</a></i>, which marked her debut into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Karnataka" title="Cinema of Karnataka">Kannada film industry</a> opened to rave reviews, while her performance was also praised by critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Her performances in <i>Simha</i>, <i>Boss Engira Bhaskaran</i> and <i>Super</i> eventually fetched her nominations for the Filmfare Best Actress Award in the respective languages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndiaGlitz_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndiaGlitz-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiaglitz.com_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiaglitz.com-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-51">[51]</a></sup> She also starred in <a href="/wiki/Shyamaprasad" title="Shyamaprasad">Shyamaprasad</a>'s critically acclaimed Malayalam film <i><a href="/wiki/Elektra_(2010_film)" title="Elektra (2010 film)">Elektra</a></i>. Her performance was well appreciated by critics when it was screened at the <a href="/wiki/International_Film_Festival_of_India" title="International Film Festival of India">International Film Festival of India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The film was also screened at the <a href="/wiki/Dubai_International_Film_Festival" title="Dubai International Film Festival">Dubai International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her only release in 2011 was the mythological film <i><a href="/wiki/Sri_Rama_Rajyam" title="Sri Rama Rajyam">Sri Rama Rajyam</a></i> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bapu_(artist)" title="Bapu (artist)">Bapu</a>, in which she played the role of <a href="/wiki/Sita" title="Sita">Sita</a>. She received critical acclaim for her performance in the film with Rediff.com noting that "Nayanthara is the surprise package of the film. As Sita, she too has played the role of her lifetime. She gave a fine understated performance conveying a kaleidoscope of emotions",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> while Sify commented "Nayanthara gave a fitting answer to all, unleashing grace and accomplishing the assignment with absolute perfection."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Subsequently, she was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Nandi_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Nandi Award for Best Actress">Nandi Award for Best Actress</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Telugu_Actress" title="Filmfare Award for Best Telugu Actress">Filmfare Award for Best Telugu Actress</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> The film was dubbed in the media as her <a href="/wiki/Swan_song" title="Swan song">swan song</a> and considered to be her last release before her marriage as she was reported to quit acting thereafter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comeback_and_stardom_.282012.E2.80.93present.29">Comeback and stardom (2012–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nayanthara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Comeback and stardom (2012–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Choosing to make a comeback, Nayanthara began shooting for <a href="/wiki/Krish_(director)" title="Krish (director)">Krish</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Krishnam_Vande_Jagadgurum" title="Krishnam Vande Jagadgurum">Krishnam Vande Jagadgurum</a></i> (2012) after an eleven month <a href="/wiki/Sabbatical" title="Sabbatical">sabbatical</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> She portrayed the role of a journalist and her performance was praised, with a reviewer from <a href="/wiki/Sify" title="Sify">Sify</a> stating that "Nayanthara does not play the normal commercial glam-doll for a change and she is good" and "her chemistry with Rana is perfect".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The following year, she won critical acclaim for her performance in <a href="/wiki/Atlee_(director)" title="Atlee (director)">Atlee</a>'s romantic drama <i><a href="/wiki/Raja_Rani_(2013_film)" title="Raja Rani (2013 film)">Raja Rani</a></i> (2013), winning both the <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Tamil" title="Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Tamil">Filmfare</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vijay_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Vijay Award for Best Actress">Vijay Award for Best Actress</a>. Playing the role of a wife in an unhappy marriage, a critic from Sify.com noted "Nayanthara gets a well-written role and she couldn`t ask for a better character than Regina for her return after a three-year hiatus", adding "she looks good, throws herself into the role, approaches her part with restraint and minimalist fuss, and turns in a sincere performance", calling her role as "unquestionably the film`s biggest strength".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Likewise, another reviewer from Behindwoods.com stated the role was "Nayanthara’s career best performance till date" and it was a "great comeback by the actress with a role which gave her ample scope".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Her next release, <a href="/wiki/Vishnuvardhan_(director)" title="Vishnuvardhan (director)">Vishnuvardhan</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Arrambam" title="Arrambam">Arrambam</a></i> (2013) featuring <a href="/wiki/Ajith_Kumar" title="Ajith Kumar">Ajith Kumar</a> in the lead, also won acclaim with a reviewer from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rediff" title="Rediff">Rediff</a> stating that "Nayanthara looks gorgeous and does full justice to her character", while Behindwoods's critic stated that "Nayanthara puts out a fiery face when required and also showcase her oomph and style in many scenes, adding "this superstar continues to shine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> Both films performed well commercially and were amongst the highest grossing Tamil films of 2013.</p>
<p>Nayanthara's rising popularity as an actress led to her being cast in female-centric films, a genre considered a rarity in South Indian cinema. <a href="/wiki/Sekhar_Kammula" title="Sekhar Kammula">Sekhar Kammula</a> cast her in the titular role of his bilingual <i><a href="/wiki/Anaamika" title="Anaamika">Anaamika</a></i> (2014), a remake of the Hindi film <i><a href="/wiki/Kahaani" title="Kahaani">Kahaani</a></i> (2012), which saw Nayanthara portray an IT professional who moves to <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad" title="Hyderabad">Hyderabad</a> to look for her missing husband. Sify.com labelled her performance in the film "a one-woman show by her in a difficult role" stating "she nails the character perfectly whether it in the initial scenes showing her anguish or in the stunning climax, where she takes a brave decision", though in contrast, Rediff.com stated her "performance is lacklustre in the first half".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> While <i>Anaamika</i> did middling business, her next female-centric project, the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Maya_(2015_Indian_film)" title="Maya (2015 Indian film)">Maya</a></i> (2015), emerged as a profitable venture commercially. Portraying a single mother with a mysterious past, Nayanthara won acclaim for her performance and her decision to portray an unconventional character, with Sify.com adding "Nayanthara is just fabulous and her classier side has been perfectly unleashed in <i>Maya</i> and that "it’s a treat to watch Nayanthara re-inventing herself by performing in an author backed role".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Along with strong, character roles, Nayanthara continued to appear in action and comedy films, including the successful <i><a href="/wiki/Bhaskar_the_Rascal" title="Bhaskar the Rascal">Bhaskar the Rascal</a></i> (2015) and <i><a href="/wiki/Thani_Oruvan" title="Thani Oruvan">Thani Oruvan</a></i> (2015), during the same period. She then went on to win her second Filmfare Award for her role of a deaf and mute girl seeking revenge in <a href="/wiki/Vignesh_Shivan" title="Vignesh Shivan">Vignesh Shivan</a>'s black comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Naanum_Rowdy_Dhaan" title="Naanum Rowdy Dhaan">Naanum Rowdy Dhaan</a></i> (2015). About her performance, Sify.com's reviewer noted "Nayanthara’s Kadambarai is a well-etched out role, the actress is effortlessly charming and she does full justice", and "infuses the part with the right portions of vulnerability, restraint, and quiet strength, delivering a performance that is nothing short of perfect".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Following the success of the film, Nayanthara was referred to in the media as the "Lady Superstar".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>In her first release of 2016, Nayanthara portrayed a rape victim in <i><a href="/wiki/Puthiya_Niyamam" title="Puthiya Niyamam">Puthiya Niyamam</a></i> (2016) opposite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mammooty" title="Mammooty">Mammooty</a>, with a critic from the IBTimes stating "Nayantara's role as Vasuki Iyer is one of her career's best until now and her decision to dub in her own voice for her character is worthy of appreciation".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> She continued to play diverse roles, appearing as a village-based school teacher in <i><a href="/wiki/Thirunaal" title="Thirunaal">Thirunaal</a></i> (2016), a spy in the commercially successful <a href="/wiki/Vikram_(actor)" title="Vikram (actor)">Vikram</a>-starrer <i><a href="/wiki/Iru_Mugan" title="Iru Mugan">Iru Mugan</a></i> (2016) and the queen of a fantasy kingdom in <i><a href="/wiki/Kaashmora" title="Kaashmora">Kaashmora</a></i> (2016). In 2017, she will appear in a series of female-centric films, where she plays the main lead. Her first release is likely to be the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Dora_(2017_film)" title="Dora (2017 film)">Dora</a></i>, which will be followed by the social drama <i><a href="/wiki/Aramm" title="Aramm">Aramm</a></i>, where she portrays a district collector. She also plays the main character in <a href="/wiki/Chakri_Toleti" title="Chakri Toleti">Chakri Toleti</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kolaiyuthir_Kaalam" title="Kolaiyuthir Kaalam">Kolaiyuthir Kaalam</a></i>, the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Imaikkaa_Nodigal" title="Imaikkaa Nodigal">Imaikkaa Nodigal</a></i> and Bharath Krishnamachari's <i>Ner Vazhi</i>, amongst other upcoming releases.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nayanthara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nayanthara was born and brought up as a <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Christians" title="Saint Thomas Christians">Syrian Christian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Telegraph_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Telegraph-11">[11]</a></sup> On 7 August 2011, she converted to <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Arya_Samaj" title="Arya Samaj">Arya Samaj</a> Temple in Chennai. She went through <i><a href="/wiki/Shuddhi" title="Shuddhi">Shuddhi Karma</a></i>, a procedure involving a Vedic purification and a <i><a href="/wiki/Homa_(ritual)" title="Homa (ritual)">Homam</a></i> where Nayanthara chanted the hymns from Veda and <i><a href="/wiki/Gayatri_Mantra" title="Gayatri Mantra">Gayatri Mantra</a></i> under the guidance of the priest. After the purification ceremony, a Certificate of Conversion to Hinduism was issued to her and her stage name, Nayanthara, became her official name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> She also visited a number of holy places, including <a href="/wiki/Haridwar" title="Haridwar">Haridwar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rishikesh" title="Rishikesh">Rishikesh</a> in May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nayanthara started dating actor-director <a href="/wiki/Prabhu_Deva" title="Prabhu Deva">Prabhu Deva</a> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (June 2016)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2010, Prabhu Deva's wife Latha filed a petition at the family court, seeking directions against Deva from living in a relationship with Nayanthara and requesting a reunion with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> Furthermore, she threatened to go on <a href="/wiki/Hunger_strike" title="Hunger strike">hunger strike</a> if Prabhu Deva married Nayanthara, while several women's organisations conducted protests against Nayanthara for bringing disrepute to the Tamil culture, burning an <a href="/wiki/Effigy" title="Effigy">effigy</a> of her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> In 2012, Nayanthara confirmed that she had ended her relationship with Deva.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Times_Of_India_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Times_Of_India-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Times_Of_India_76-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Times_Of_India-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
