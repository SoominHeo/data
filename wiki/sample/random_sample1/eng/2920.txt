<p><b>Ronaldo Aparecido Rodrigues</b> (born 10 September 1982), commonly known as <b>Naldo</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazilian_people" title="Brazilian people">Brazilian</a>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a>. From 2007 to 2009, he made four appearances for the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazilian national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naldo_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Naldo.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1177" data-file-width="717" height="246" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Naldo.jpg/150px-Naldo.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Naldo.jpg/225px-Naldo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Naldo.jpg/300px-Naldo.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>

<p>Naldo began his football career playing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RS_Futebol_Clube" title="RS Futebol Clube">RS Futebol</a> at the youth level before playing for them professionally. After two years at RS Futebol, Naldo joined <a href="/wiki/Esporte_Clube_Juventude" title="Esporte Clube Juventude">Juventude</a> for one season, where, playing alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thiago_Silva_(footballer)" title="Thiago Silva (footballer)">Thiago Silva</a>, he made thirty-six appearances and scored eight goals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Werder_Bremen">Werder Bremen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naldo_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Werder Bremen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His talent in Brazil led to Naldo signing with <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a> in the summer of 2005, to replace <a href="/wiki/Val%C3%A9rien_Isma%C3%ABl" title="Valérien Ismaël">Valérien Ismaël</a> who left for <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> for €3 million and shortly after was given the number four shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NaldoKreiszeitung_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NaldoKreiszeitung-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Furthermore, he was capable of playing across the back line. In the opening game of the season, Naldo made his debut in a 5–2 win over <a href="/wiki/Arminia_Bielefeld" title="Arminia Bielefeld">Arminia Bielefeld</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NaldoKreiszeitung_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NaldoKreiszeitung-2">[2]</a></sup> His first goal came in November 2005, in a 6–1 win over <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">Wolfsburg</a>. After missing one match, due to injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Naldo scored his second goal of the season, in a 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/1._FC_K%C3%B6ln" title="1. FC Köln">FC Köln</a> on 11 December 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In his first season, Naldo played in all but two of Bremen's Bundesliga games and in seven of their eight <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> fixtures, along with two appearances in Champions League Qualifiers.</p>
<p>In the 2006–07 season, Naldo started the season well and scored his first goal in a 6–0 win over <a href="/wiki/VfL_Bochum" title="VfL Bochum">Bochum</a> on 14 October 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Naldo scored a hat-trick against <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a>, which ended as a 6–2 win for Werder Bremen on 9 December 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Naldo scored his first UEFA Champions League goal in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/PFC_Levski_Sofia" title="PFC Levski Sofia">Levski Sofia</a> on 18 October 2006. The third place in the Champions League group stage secured Werder Bremen a place in the UEFA Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In Bremen's first game in the competition, he scored in a 3–1 first-leg win over <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> which helped Bremen reach the next round despite losing 3–1 in the second leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The club advanced through the knock-out stage until they were eliminated in the semi-final by <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">Espanyol</a>. With one game left at the end of the season, Naldo scored an own goal, which handed victory to Eintracht Frankfurt, in a 2–1 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Just as in his first season, Naldo played in all but two of Bremen's Bundesliga games in 2006–07 season, as well as in every European fixture. At the end of the season, Naldo extended his contract with the club until 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the 2007–08 season, Naldo remained in the first team spotlight, where he formed a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Per_Mertesacker" title="Per Mertesacker">Per Mertesacker</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and scored his first goal of the season in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> on 27 October 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> During the 2007–08 season, Naldo was involved in an incident at training after clashing with <a href="/wiki/Boubacar_Sanogo" title="Boubacar Sanogo">Boubacar Sanogo</a>. However, manager Schaaf played down the incident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Once again, Naldo played in all but two of Bremen's Bundesliga games in the 2007–08 season, due to suspension,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and in all their <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> fixtures, along with two in Champions League qualifiers.</p>
<p>In the 2008–09 season, Naldo scored with his right foot his first goal of the season in a 5–2 win over Bayern Munich, the first time Bremen won at the Allianz Arena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Naldo soon suffered a setback due to injuries during the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> as well as receiving a red card on 1 March 2009 against Bayern Munich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In the UEFA Cup round of sixteen, he scored the only goal in the first leg win over <a href="/wiki/AS_Saint-%C3%89tienne" title="AS Saint-Étienne">Saint-Étienne</a>. He scored in the <a href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="2009 UEFA Cup Final">2009 UEFA Cup Final</a> to level the game at 1–1, however his team went on to lose the game 2–1 to <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a> after extra time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> After the match, Naldo was seen crying uncontrollably.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NaldoKreiszeitung_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NaldoKreiszeitung-2">[2]</a></sup> At the end of the season, Naldo played in all but six of Bremen's Bundesliga games in 2006–07 season, in seven of their eight <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> fixtures, and all of the UEFA Cup fixtures. Despite not winning the UEFA Cup, the club would, nevertheless, win the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_DFB-Pokal" title="2008–09 DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a> after beating <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a> 1–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>Ahead of the 2009–10 season, Naldo was linked with a move to <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> side <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The transfer speculation ended when he signed a contract extension to keep him at the club until 2013. Manager <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Schaaf" title="Thomas Schaaf">Thomas Schaaf</a> was delighted with Naldo's new deal due to the fact that he was then considered as a key player at Werder Bremen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Naldo started the season well when he scored his first goal of the season, in a 5–0 win over <a href="/wiki/1._FC_Union_Berlin" title="1. FC Union Berlin">Union Berlin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> However, Naldo suffered a thigh injury and missed the first two matches of the opening season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Upon his return, Naldo scored two goals in two matches, with both games a victory against <a href="/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Borussia Mönchengladbach">Borussia Mönchengladbach</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Hertha_BSC" title="Hertha BSC">Hertha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On 20 March 2010, Naldo captained Werder Bremen for the first time against Bochum and provided an assist to one of the goals in a 3–2 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In the first leg of the fourth round of Europa League, he scored twice in a 6–3 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Aktobe" title="FC Aktobe">Aktobe</a> and the club would go through to the group stage in a 2–0 win in the second leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_DFB-Pokal" title="2009–10 DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a>, Naldo scored three goals in the competition and the club reached the final. In the final, against Bayern Munich, he started in the central defence with <a href="/wiki/Per_Mertesacker" title="Per Mertesacker">Per Mertesacker</a>, in a 4–0 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Towards the end of the 2009–10 season, Naldo sustained a serious knee injury in a match against Wolfsburg on 17 April 2010, after a challenge with <a href="/wiki/Grafite" title="Grafite">Grafite</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He suffered a bone edema on the right knee, but recovered after taking painkillers and played the remaining fixture for the club towards the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> However, in the 2010–11 season, he was absent throughout the season as he had not made a single appearance that season. At the time, it was reported that his injury might require surgery, as the exact cause was not known.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He had many surgeries in Germany<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> but went back to Brazil to have the surgery on his knee, after the club's management was against having German doctors. He was operated on by the Brazil national football team physician, José Luiz Runco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> An operation on his knee revealed that he damaged his <a href="/wiki/Cartilage" title="Cartilage">cartilage</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> This injury would take at least nine months to recover and the doctors feared that the cartilage damage could later lead to other knee problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>

<p>He made his comeback in mid August 2011, appearing for Bremen in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_St._Pauli" title="FC St. Pauli">St. Pauli</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> and subsequently returned to Bundesliga action on 19 September 2011, after coming on as a substitute in a late minute 2–0 win over Hamburger SV, where he played his first leaue match in fifteen months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> One month later, he told <i>Kreiszeitung Syke</i> that he was pleased with overcoming his injury nightmare, and felt happy on his return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Naldo scored his first goal in over a year in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart" title="VfB Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> on 27 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Naldo then injured his knee ligament and was sidelined for months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> During the recovery, Naldo was linked with a return to Brazil, as supposedly <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Internacional" title="Sport Club Internacional">Internacional</a> was interested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> But the move was rejected by Werder Bremen and he was convinced to stay by Schaaf after expressing he desire to leave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> After making his return, Naldo scored in a 2–2 draw against Borussia Mönchengladbach on 10 April 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the 2011–12 season ended, Naldo's future at Werder Bremen was uncertain despite he was intent keen staying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> In May, Naldo revealed that the club had not yet received offers for him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wolfsburg">Wolfsburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naldo_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wolfsburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 July 2012, Naldo moved to <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">Wolfsburg</a> on a four-year contract for a fee of €3 million, which would rise to €4m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>Naldo made his competitive debut in a 5–0 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Sch%C3%B6nberg_95" title="FC Schönberg 95">Schönberg 95</a> and setting a goal for <a href="/wiki/Ivica_Oli%C4%87" title="Ivica Olić">Ivica Olić</a>, who would score a hat-trick in the first round of <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_DFB-Pokal" title="2012–13 DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a> and made his league debut in the opening season in a 1–0 win over Stuttgart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> His performance was criticised by manager <a href="/wiki/Felix_Magath" title="Felix Magath">Felix Magath</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> As a result, Naldo was demoted to the second team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Magath would be sacked after only five points in eight matches (and no goals and points in the last four games). After that, Naldo returned to the first team. Despite suffering from a hamstring injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Naldo made thirty-one appearances and scored six times.</p>
<p>In the 2013–14 season, Naldo started the season well when he scored his first goal of the season in a 4–0 win over Schalke 04 on 17 August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Naldo then was given a captaincy in number of games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Having made thirty-three appearances for the club, Naldo signed a new two-year contract on 16 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2014–15 season, Naldo captained in the opening game of the season, as Bayern Munich beat Wolfsburg 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> He also was captain in the next game and scored his first goal, in a 2–2 draw against Frankfurt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Naldo also scored in the Europa League round of sixteen match, in a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Naldo then played the whole 90 minutes in the <a href="/wiki/2015_DFB-Pokal_Final" title="2015 DFB-Pokal Final">DFB-Pokal Final</a> against Brussia Dortmund and was involved in an assist that led <a href="/wiki/Luiz_Gustavo" title="Luiz Gustavo">Luiz Gustavo</a> scoring an equaliser, with Wolfsburg eventually winning 3–1 for their first cup honour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> Naldo made thirty-two appearances and scored seven goals in the 2014–15 season.</p>
<p>In the 2015–16 season, Naldo was given the captaincy following <a href="/wiki/Diego_Benaglio" title="Diego Benaglio">Diego Benaglio</a>'s absence<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> and played 120 minutes as captain in the <a href="/wiki/2015_DFL-Supercup" title="2015 DFL-Supercup">DFL-Supercup</a> against Bayern Munich. Wolfsburg won 5–4 on penalty shootout. On 8 December 2015, Naldo scored two goals,in a 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, which saw them eliminated from the Champions League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Naldo was first team regular at Wolfsburg until he suffered a shoulder injury during a 2–0 loss against Bayern Munich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Naldo finished the 2015–16 season, making twenty-nine appearances for the club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Schalke_04">Schalke 04</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naldo_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Schalke 04">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 May 2016, it was announced that Naldo would join <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> on a two-year deal, after his contract with Wolfsburg expired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Upon joining the club, Naldo criticised the club's sporting director Klaus Allofs for not giving him a new contract at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Naldo was previously linked with a move to Manchester United before joining Schalke 04.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naldo_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2007, Naldo was called up by his then Brazil Manager <a href="/wiki/Dunga" title="Dunga">Dunga</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil squad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> Despite not playing, Naldo was then included in Brazil's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2007 Copa América">Copa América</a>, which Brazil went on to win, once again defeating <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Final" title="2007 Copa América Final">final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Naldo made his full debut in England's first match at the new Wembley Stadium on 1 June 2007 as they drew 1–1 with <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since his debut, Naldo was called up by the national football team numerous times, but did not play.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naldo_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He plays as a versatile two-footed central defender. He stands at 1.98 metres tall and this height, combined with his jumping and heading ability, makes him a big threat in aerial contests. He is also well known for his set piece skill and according to <a href="/wiki/Dunga" title="Dunga">Dunga</a>, who coached him at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2007 Copa América">2007 Copa América</a>, he can hit the ball in excess of 60 mph using the cannonball free kick method, associated with other pros such as <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Carlos" title="Roberto Carlos">Roberto Carlos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)" title="Alex (footballer, born 1982)">Alex</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adriano_(footballer,_born_1982)" title="Adriano (footballer, born 1982)">Adriano</a>.</p>
<p>During his time at Werder Bremen, he was the club's fan favourite in the regular team. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Tim_Wiese" title="Tim Wiese">Tim Wiese</a> described him as a best central defender in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> His manager at Werder Bremen, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Schaaf" title="Thomas Schaaf">Thomas Schaaf</a>, described him as "probably the most talented defender in Brazil".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naldo_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naldo is a devout <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> and has a large back-piece tattoo depicting <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> on the cross with two angels on either side, one holding a guitar the other holding a heart. Over the tattoo it says in Portuguese "De Todo o Nos Livrai time Amém" ("Deliver us from evil, Amen"). The tattoo was done by Frank Kassebaum and describing it Naldo states "I am very religious, and have faith in God. It is to my family and will always protect me." In addition, Naldo has the names of his wife Carla and his son Naldinho tattooed on his forearms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2014, Naldo received a German citizenship, having lived in Germany since 2005, as well, as his wife and his two children, Naldinho (born in 2005) and Liz (born in 2009).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NaldoKreiszeitung_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-NaldoKreiszeitung-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> Naldo also speaks fluent Portuguese and German.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his first few weeks at Bremen, fans likened him to an Ox due to his immense height and athletic build, as a result he was nicknamed The Beast, a name shared with players Julio Baptista, Jonathan Parkin, Brian Jensen and Adebayo Akinfenwa.</p>
