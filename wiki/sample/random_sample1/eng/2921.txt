<p><i><b>My Hero Academia</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">僕のヒーローアカデミア</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Boku no Hīrō Akademia</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dhei_Horikoshi" title="Kōhei Horikoshi">Kōhei Horikoshi</a>. It has been serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Jump" title="Weekly Shōnen Jump">Weekly Shōnen Jump</a></i> since July 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and 11 volumes have been collected in <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> format. The series has been licensed for English-language release by <a href="/wiki/Viz_Media" title="Viz Media">Viz Media</a>, and began serialization in their weekly digital manga anthology <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Shonen_Jump_(magazine)" title="Weekly Shonen Jump (magazine)">Weekly Shonen Jump</a></i> on February 9, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The story follows Izuku Midoriya, a boy born without <a href="/wiki/Superpower_(ability)" title="Superpower (ability)">superpowers</a> in a world where they are the norm, but who still dreams of becoming a <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> himself, and is scouted by the world's greatest hero who shares his powers with Izuku after recognizing his value and enrolls him in a high school for heroes in training. The manga was adapted into an <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series; the first season aired from April 3 to June 26, 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and a second season is scheduled to premiere in April 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2Anc_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-S2Anc-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2Apr_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-S2Apr-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Hero_Academia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Izuku Midoriya is a regular middle school student in a world where people with superpowers known as "Quirks" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">個性</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kosei</i>)</span> are the norm. However, he dreams of one day becoming a Hero <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ヒーロー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hīrō</i>)</span>, despite being bullied by his classmates for not having a Quirk. After being the only one to try and save his childhood bully, Katsuki, from a Villain <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">敵（ヴィラン）</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Viran</i>)</span>, All Might, the world's greatest Hero, bestows upon him his own quirk, "One For All". The story follows Izuku's entrance into U.A. High School <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">雄英高校</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yūei Kōkō</i>)</span>, a school that cultivates the next generation of Heroes.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Hero_Academia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Izuku Midoriya <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">緑谷 出久</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Midoriya Izuku</i>)</span> / Deku <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">デク</span>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Daiki_Yamashita" title="Daiki Yamashita">Daiki Yamashita</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anime_News_Network_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Anime_News_Network-10">[10]</a></sup> (Vomic and Anime) (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Justin_Briner" title="Justin Briner">Justin Briner</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-funi_blog_cast_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-funi_blog_cast-11">[11]</a></sup> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Nicknamed Deku <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">デク</span>)</span> (an alternate spelling of his name and which means "one who can't do anything"), he is a timid but kind boy who is extremely knowledgeable about heroes and dreams to be one himself like his idol, All Might, despite lacking a Quirk due to the trait required to have one being too diluted to be passed on in his family. However, when he steps up to save his childhood acquaintance Katsuki from an evil villain, All Might bestows upon him his Quirk One For All <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ワン・フォー・オール</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Wan Fō Ōru</i>)</span>, a Quirk that becomes more powerful the more people it is given to, and results in the user being able to call upon great physical strength. At first, Izuku was only able to use the full power of his strength for specific moments, resulting in him severely damaging whichever part of his body he used. However he has since learned to channel the power throughout his body rather than in short bursts, but currently can only withstand around 8% of it. He eventually managed to perfect its use in his legs, creating a kick-related fighting style unique from All Might's punching style. Despite these limitations, he makes up for it with a keen sense of strategy (having written at least thirteen notebooks on other heroes, including his peers) and an instinctive desire to save people. He initially dislikes his nickname, but starts to like it once Ochako comments that she likes his nickname, as it also means "one who never gives up". Because of this, he instantly decided to use it as his Hero name, much to his own dismay.</dd>
</dl>

<dd>Katsuki is one of Izuku's childhood acquaintances and classmates in class 1-A, whom Izuku calls Kacchan <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">かっちゃん</span>)</span>. He possesses the Quirk Explosion <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">爆破</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Bakuha</i>)</span>, which allows his palm sweat to become nitroglycerin and explode. He has made fun of Izuku for lacking a Quirk since childhood, despite them initially being close friends, and also the one who gave him his nickname as an insult. It was only after he grew up being praised by people around him for his Quirk and achievements that he became more egotistical and thought that others, particularly Izuku, are below him. He started to bully and became truly hostile to Izuku when his attempts to reach out to Katsuki out of concern are misinterpreted by Katsuki into thinking he was looking down on him as someone in need of saving. In the beginning of the series, he thinks that he is the strongest hero, and dreams of becoming richer and more famous than All Might. He stops picking on Izuku after the latter's attempt to save him from a villain, though he truly starts reflecting on himself only after Izuku defeated him in their battle training and seeing that many students possess greater and better skills than him. However, Katsuki's aggressive and conceited behaviour draws the attention of The League of Villains, who decide to kidnap him in order to convince him to join their ranks, but he is rescued with help from Izuku and some other classmates.</dd>
</dl>

Due to this, Ochako aspires to become a hero for money so she can give her parents an easy life, after her father dissuaded her from following their footsteps in order for her to achieve her own goals. Ochako originally meets Izuku prior to U.A.'s entrance exam, after she stops him from falling over when he tripped on his own foot. This caused Izuku to develop a large crush on her (as he had "talked" to a girl), to the point of blushing and stammering every time he is with her. Izuku eventually returns the favor by saving her during the entrance exam, and several other characters later deduce that Ochako returns Izuku's feelings, with her even showing signs after a class mate suspected it with her try to deny it.</dd>

He is a sinister man who, due to a prior confrontation with All Might, has a massive scar covering most of his face above his lower lip. As the name implies anything he touches disintegrates in seconds, but all five of his fingers need to make contact otherwise it doesn't take effect. He doesn't like Stain and believes they'll never get along, despite the Alliance's desire to have him join ranks. He eventually becomes obsessed with Izuku, believing that the two of them are polar opposites and that to truly break Izuku and the society he represents, he must kill All Might. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Hero_Academia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_My_Hero_Academia_chapters" title="List of My Hero Academia chapters">List of My Hero Academia chapters</a></div>
<p><i>My Hero Academia</i> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dhei_Horikoshi" title="Kōhei Horikoshi">Kōhei Horikoshi</a>. It began its serialization in the manga magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Jump" title="Weekly Shōnen Jump">Weekly Shōnen Jump</a></i> in July 2014. As of June 9, 2016 the series been collected into nine <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2Anc_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-S2Anc-8">[8]</a></sup> The series is licensed for English language release in North America by <a href="/wiki/Viz_Media" title="Viz Media">Viz Media</a>, who published the first volume on August 4, 2015. As the series is published in Japan, it is also released simultaneously in English digitally by Viz Media's Weekly Shounen Jump. As of December 1, 2016 six volumes have been released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2Anc_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-S2Anc-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Hero_Academia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 29, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> announced that <i>My Hero Academia</i> would receive an anime adaption produced by <a href="/wiki/Bones_(studio)" title="Bones (studio)">Studio Bones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-otakutale.com_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-otakutale.com-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the anime announced, TOHO Group registered domain name "heroaca.com" as the anime website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-otakutale.com_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-otakutale.com-24">[24]</a></sup> The anime is directed by Kenji Nagasaki, written by <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Dsuke_Kuroda" title="Yōsuke Kuroda">Yōsuke Kuroda</a>, and feature character designs by Yoshikiko Umakoshi and music composed by <a href="/wiki/Yuki_Hayashi_(composer)" title="Yuki Hayashi (composer)">Yuki Hayashi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animenewsnetwork.com_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-animenewsnetwork.com-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The anime premiered on <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Broadcasting_System" title="Mainichi Broadcasting System">MBS</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Japan_News_Network" title="Japan News Network">Japan News Network</a> stations in '<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nichigo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nichigo (page does not exist)">Nichigo</a>' timeslot at 5pm on Sundays in Japan. The opening theme is "The Day", performed by <a href="/wiki/Porno_Graffitti" title="Porno Graffitti">Porno Graffitti</a> and the ending theme is "Heroes", performed by Brian the Sun. In March 2016 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funimation_Entertainment" title="Funimation Entertainment">Funimation Entertainment</a> announced they had licensed the series for streaming services, home and broadcast release as well as obtaining the merchandise rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>A second season was announced in the <i>Weekly Shōnen Jump</i> magazine's 30th issue of 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2Anc_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-S2Anc-8">[8]</a></sup> It will premiere in April 2017 on <a href="/wiki/Nippon_TV" title="Nippon TV">NTV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Telecasting_Corporation" title="Yomiuri Telecasting Corporation">YTV</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2TVNetworks_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-S2TVNetworks-3">[3]</a></sup> with the staff and cast from the first season returning to reprise their roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2Apr_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-S2Apr-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game">Video game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Hero_Academia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Video game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A video game based on the anime was announced in November 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The game is developed by <a href="/wiki/Bandai_Namco_Entertainment#Bandai_Namco_Studios" title="Bandai Namco Entertainment">Bandai Namco Studios</a> and published by <a href="/wiki/Bandai_Namco_Entertainment" title="Bandai Namco Entertainment">Bandai Namco Entertainment</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_3DS" title="Nintendo 3DS">Nintendo 3DS</a> handheld game console, which was released in Japan on May 19, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Hero_Academia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is nominated for the 8th <a href="/wiki/Manga_Taish%C5%8D" title="Manga Taishō">Manga Taishō</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Before the anime adaptation's premiere, manga author <a href="/wiki/Masashi_Kishimoto" title="Masashi Kishimoto">Masashi Kishimoto</a> praised Kōhei Horikoshi's work, believing it would be a success overseas; Horikoshi, meanwhile, has cited Kishimoto's <i><a href="/wiki/Naruto" title="Naruto">Naruto</a></i> series as a primary source of inspiration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Volume 1 reached the 7th place on the weekly <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> manga chart with 71,575 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> It sold out almost immediately on its first printing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Volume 2 reached the 6th place, with 167,531 copies<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and, by January 18, 2015, had sold 205,179 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>The story has been noted to take inspiration from elements in <a href="/wiki/Superhero_comics" title="Superhero comics">superhero comics</a>, such as the aesthetics of its characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alex Osborn of <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> gave the anime series positive marks, saying "The first season of <i>My Hero Academia</i> delivers thirteen episodes of fantastic action, elevated by a heartfelt story that's wrapped around a core cast of memorable and relatable characters." Osborn went on to state that the villains were underdeveloped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
