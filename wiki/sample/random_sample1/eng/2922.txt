<p><i><b>Naegleria</b></i> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɪər/ 'ear' in 'near'">ɪər</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Protozoa" title="Protozoa">protozoa</a>. The genus was named after French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zoologist" title="Zoologist">zoologist</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mathieu_Naegler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mathieu Naegler (page does not exist)">Mathieu Naegler</a>. <i>Naegleria</i> is a microscopic <a href="/wiki/Amoeba" title="Amoeba">amoeba</a> that can cause a very rare, but severe, infection of the brain. The amoeba is commonly found in warm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a> (for example, lakes, rivers, and hot springs) and soil. Only one <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Naegleria_fowleri" title="Naegleria fowleri">Naegleria fowleri</a></i> infects <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Naegleria fowleri</i> infects people by entering the body through the nose. This typically occurs when people go swimming or diving in warm freshwater places, like lakes and rivers. In very rare instances, <i>Naegleria</i> infections may also occur when contaminated water from other sources (such as inadequately chlorinated swimming pool water or heated tap water &lt; 47 °C) enters the nose. Once the amoeba enters the brain, it causes a usually fatal infection called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primary_amoebic_meningoencephalitis" title="Primary amoebic meningoencephalitis">primary amoebic meningoencephalitis</a> (PAM).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Naegleria are also notable because some species change their form based on the chemistry of their surroundings, transitioning from an immobile cell to a flagellated cell.</p>
<p>The amoeba lives as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oocyst" title="Oocyst">cyst</a> on the sediments of the bottom of freshwater like lakes, ponds, and rivers.</p>
<p>Species include:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Naegleria_fowleri" title="Naegleria fowleri">Naegleria fowleri</a></i>, which causes a rare and generally fatal disease in humans</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Naegleria_gruberi" title="Naegleria gruberi">Naegleria gruberi</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Naegleria_lovaniensis" title="Naegleria lovaniensis">Naegleria lovaniensis</a></i></li>
</ul>
