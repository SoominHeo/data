<p>The <b>Niagara Peninsula</b> is the portion of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ontario" title="Southern Ontario">Southern Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, lying between the south shore of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a> and the north shore of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">Lake Erie</a>. Technically an <a href="/wiki/Isthmus" title="Isthmus">isthmus</a> rather than a <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> it stretches from the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_River" title="Niagara River">Niagara River</a> in the east to <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton, Ontario</a>, in the west. The population of the peninsula is roughly 1,000,000 people. The region directly across the Niagara River and Lake Erie in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_State" title="New York State">New York State</a> is known as the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Frontier" title="Niagara Frontier">Niagara Frontier</a>. The broader <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Niagara_Region" title="Buffalo Niagara Region">Buffalo Niagara Region</a> includes the Niagara Peninsula, the Niagara Frontier, and the city of <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The greater part of the peninsula is incorporated as the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Municipality_of_Niagara" title="Regional Municipality of Niagara">Regional Municipality of Niagara</a>. Cities in the region include <a href="/wiki/St._Catharines" title="St. Catharines">St. Catharines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls,_Ontario" title="Niagara Falls, Ontario">Niagara Falls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thorold" title="Thorold">Thorold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Port_Colborne" title="Port Colborne">Port Colborne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welland" title="Welland">Welland</a>. Towns include <a href="/wiki/Niagara-on-the-Lake" title="Niagara-on-the-Lake">Niagara-on-the-Lake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_Ontario" title="Lincoln, Ontario">Lincoln</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pelham,_Ontario" title="Pelham, Ontario">Pelham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grimsby,_Ontario" title="Grimsby, Ontario">Grimsby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fort_Erie,_Ontario" title="Fort Erie, Ontario">Fort Erie</a>, as well as the townships Wainfleet and <a href="/wiki/West_Lincoln,_Ontario" title="West Lincoln, Ontario">West Lincoln</a>. The remainder of the peninsula encompasses parts of the <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">City of Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haldimand_County" title="Haldimand County">Haldimand County</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area was originally inhabited by a <a href="/wiki/First_Nations" title="First Nations">First Nations</a> people called the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neutral_Indian" title="Neutral Indian">Neutrals</a>", so named for their practice of trading goods such as flint arrowhead blanks with both of the feuding regional powers, the <a href="/wiki/Wyandot_people" title="Wyandot people">Wyandot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iroquois" title="Iroquois">Iroquois</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Neutrals were wiped out by the Iroquois c. 1650 as the latter sought to expand their fur-trapping territory as part of the <a href="/wiki/Beaver_Wars" title="Beaver Wars">Beaver Wars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> From this point until the arrival of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Empire_Loyalists" title="United Empire Loyalists">United Empire Loyalists</a> following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_War_of_Independence" title="American War of Independence">American War of Independence</a>, the region was only sporadically inhabited,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> as the Iroquois did not establish permanent settlements in the area.</p>
<p>The Niagara Peninsula then became one of the first areas settled in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper Canada</a> by British Loyalists in the late 18th century. The capital of the new colony was established with the founding of <a href="/wiki/Niagara-on-the-Lake" title="Niagara-on-the-Lake">Niagara-on-the-Lake</a>, then called Newark. Many <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> immigrants settled in the peninsula, but by the 1800s, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_people" title="Italian people">Italian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> immigrants heavily populated the peninsula and were the chief sources of immigrants followed by <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish_people" title="Polish people">Polish</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europeans</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2015)">which?</span></a></i>]</sup>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Following the agricultural period of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> settlement, the Niagara area became an important industrial centre, with water-powered mills joined later by hydro-electric power generation in <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls,_Ontario" title="Niagara Falls, Ontario">Niagara Falls</a> and electricity-intensive industry in both Niagara Falls and <a href="/wiki/St._Catharines" title="St. Catharines">St. Catharines</a>. While agriculture – especially fruit farming along the shore of Lake Ontario – remains important to this day, it was joined in the 19th century by industrial developments. A succession of <a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">canals</a> were built to connect the markets and mineral resources of the upper <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_Seaway" title="St. Lawrence Seaway">St. Lawrence Seaway</a> (See also <a href="/wiki/Welland_Canal" title="Welland Canal">Welland Canal</a>). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Motors_Corporation" title="General Motors Corporation">General Motors</a> built a considerable presence in St. Catharines with auto plants and a foundry, and a number of auto-parts manufacturers followed. Dry docks were also built at <a href="/wiki/Port_Weller,_Ontario" title="Port Weller, Ontario">Port Weller</a> on Lake Ontario.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Heavy industry has been diminishing for the past decade or more primarily due to the slow-down of the North American automotive manufacturers. Thousands of jobs have been lost at long-time area employers such as <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">General Motors</a>, <a href="/wiki/TRW_Inc." title="TRW Inc.">Thompson Products</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Deere" title="John Deere">Deere &amp; Company</a>, Dana Canada Corp, Port Weller Drydocks, Domtar Papers and Gallagher Thorold Paper. Because of this, local municipalities have been forced to look at new and diversified opportunities to prevent an exodus of well trained staff.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visiting">Visiting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Visiting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hospitality and tourism has attracted numerous visitors to the area for more than 150 years primarily thanks to <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls" title="Niagara Falls">Niagara Falls</a>. New development beginning during the mid-1990s has spun off an upscale hospitality boom throughout the whole Niagara Peninsula.</p>
<p>Today, more than 10 million guests<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> visit the peninsula annually to see the beauty of the Falls and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Niagara_Parks" title="Niagara Parks">Niagara Parks</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ecotourism" title="Ecotourism">Ecotourism</a> has become more popular with more people finding and exploring out of the way places such as the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Escarpment" title="Niagara Escarpment">Niagara Escarpment</a>, named a world <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biosphere_Reserve" title="Biosphere Reserve">Biosphere Reserve</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in 1990.</p>
<p>Another area of major tourism growth in the past thirty years has been the expansion of the grape and wine industry. The Niagara Peninsula is one of four recognized viticultural areas by the <a href="/wiki/Vintners_Quality_Alliance" title="Vintners Quality Alliance">VQA</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_wine" title="Ontario wine">Ontario wine</a> industry. The many European-style <a href="/wiki/Winery" title="Winery">wineries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vineyard" title="Vineyard">vineyards</a> have played a major role in attracting visitors seeking a unique cultural experience. Most of the local wineries offer full tours of their facilities with a few offering onsite dining featuring unique Canadian cuisine paired with their own <a href="/wiki/Vintners_Quality_Alliance" title="Vintners Quality Alliance">VQA</a> vintages. It is common for many of these wineries' world-class chefs to use fresh ingredients that are grown or acquired from local farms in season. Some wineries also feature live music and theatrical performances in the vineyard during the summer months. Visitors come during the coldest months of the year (usually December to February) to watch some varieties of grapes being harvested and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pressed_(wine)" title="Pressed (wine)">pressed</a> outdoors in the vineyard as part of the process of creating the sweetest, and among the most expensive,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> wine on earth – <a href="/wiki/Ice_wine" title="Ice wine">ice wine</a>. A few Niagara Peninsula wineries have won the most prestigious international awards<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> for their ice wine products, many of which are only available from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vintner" title="Vintner">vintner</a>.</p>
<p>There is an official <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.winesofontario.org" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> Wine Routes Guide for those that wish to self-drive while transportation companies offering wine tours operate out of major <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bed_and_breakfast" title="Bed and breakfast">bed and breakfast</a> establishments in <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls" title="Niagara Falls">Niagara Falls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niagara-on-the-Lake" title="Niagara-on-the-Lake">Niagara-on-the-Lake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>. (Driving from downtown Toronto to the Niagara wine region is about two hours.)</p>
<p>Another major attraction for the well travelled looking for cultural activities is the famous <a href="/wiki/Shaw_Festival" title="Shaw Festival">Shaw Festival</a> Theater (named for playwright <a href="/wiki/George_Bernard_Shaw" title="George Bernard Shaw">George Bernard Shaw</a>) located in the town of <a href="/wiki/Niagara-on-the-Lake" title="Niagara-on-the-Lake">Niagara-on-the-Lake</a>. A resident repertory company of actors uses three theatres during a six-month season. Niagara-on-the-Lake is also the location of <a href="/wiki/Fort_George,_Ontario" title="Fort George, Ontario">Fort George</a>, a British-built and -occupied fort during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>. It was rebuilt for the public during the 1960s and is open during the summer months. Other key historical locations nearby include: <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Brock" title="Isaac Brock">Brock</a>'s Monument, the <a href="/wiki/Laura_Secord" title="Laura Secord">Laura Secord</a> Monument and the battlefield sites of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Queenston_Heights" title="Battle of Queenston Heights">Battle of Queenston Heights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lundy%27s_Lane" title="Battle of Lundy's Lane">Battle of Lundy's Lane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chippawa" title="Battle of Chippawa">Battle of Chippawa</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Living">Living</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Living">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The region's moderate year-round climate, in addition to its close proximity to the United States for easy road and air access to the southern U.S., makes it a popular<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> retirement destination. In fact, the Niagara Peninsula has both the highest density and growth rate of seniors for any region within Ontario.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The highest percentage of seniors to the total population is located within the city of <a href="/wiki/Port_Colborne" title="Port Colborne">Port Colborne</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>During the early 1990s a major telecommunications highway between metropolitan Toronto and the U.S. was upgraded to become one of North America's fastest fiber backbones.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It passes through the heart of the Niagara Peninsula and enters the U.S. at <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>. This gave Niagara the advantage of having direct access to the backbone and attracting many new professional call centers.</p>
<p>Compared with the cities of <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton</a> and most Ontario municipalities with populations similar in size to the whole of the Niagara Peninsula, the average cost of living is very reasonable.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The cost of housing, both owned and rented, is lower.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The three major cities, <a href="/wiki/St._Catharines" title="St. Catharines">St. Catharines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls,_Ontario" title="Niagara Falls, Ontario">Niagara Falls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welland" title="Welland">Welland</a> are mostly urban with most needed services available locally. The remainder of the peninsula, especially to the far west and south, is either partially urban or almost entirely rural.</p>
<p>Centres of higher education are <a href="/wiki/Brock_University" title="Brock University">Brock University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niagara_College" title="Niagara College">Niagara College</a>, both offering undergraduate and post-graduate studies in many disciplines.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The major roadway bisecting the peninsula is the <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_Way" title="Queen Elizabeth Way">Queen Elizabeth Way</a> (QEW). This freeway connects <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>, in the United States. It is one of the major thoroughfares for the North American trucking industry and is responsible for supporting the carriage of nearly one third of all goods imported and exported.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The second major roadway is <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Highway_406" title="Ontario Highway 406">Highway 406</a> which begins at the QEW in west St. Catharines and ends approximately 30 km south in the city of Welland. Another shorter freeway is <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Highway_405" title="Ontario Highway 405">Highway 405</a>, named the General Isaac Brock Parkway in 2006. It begins at the QEW in Niagara-on-the-Lake, just east of St. Catharines, and ends about 15 km away at <a href="/wiki/Queenston,_Ontario" title="Queenston, Ontario">Queenston, Ontario</a>, where it connects to an international bridge that crosses into the United States at <a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_New_York" title="Lewiston, New York">Lewiston, New York</a>. This is also a major travel zone for the Canada/US trucking industry.</p>
<p>All cities and some towns in the peninsula have taxi services while St. Catharines, Niagara Falls and Welland all have a local transit commission. There is also one major airbus company that services Toronto, Hamilton and Buffalo airports exclusively. Most cities and some towns also have very limited inter-city bus services operated mostly by Greyhound and Coach Canada. A specialized inter-city regional bus service, owned and operated by the Regional government, began operation in late 2006 but is restricted to those requiring transport to medical appointments throughout the region and have no other means of transportation. (Other restrictions apply.) The region hopes to have a fully integrated region-level transit system by the end of the decade.</p>
<p>Niagara Falls, St. Catharines and Grimsby are all connected to the CN railway line. Via Rail offers limited daily commuter and weekend service between these three peninsula municipalities and Toronto and many points between. <a href="/wiki/Via_Rail" title="Via Rail">Via Rail</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> also offer daily southbound service from Toronto to New York City with stops at the same stations.</p>
