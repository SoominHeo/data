<p><b>Niagara-on-the-Lake</b> (<a href="/wiki/Canada_2011_Census" title="Canada 2011 Census">2011</a> population 15,400) is a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ontario" title="Southern Ontario">Southern Ontario</a> where the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_River" title="Niagara River">Niagara River</a> meets <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Municipality_of_Niagara,_Ontario" title="Regional Municipality of Niagara, Ontario">Niagara Region</a> of the southern part of the province of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>. It is across the Niagara River from <a href="/wiki/Youngstown,_New_York" title="Youngstown, New York">Youngstown, New York</a>, US. It is the only town in Canada that has a <a href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor" title="Lord Mayor">Lord Mayor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lordy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-lordy-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara-on-the-Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The settlement, known from about 1781 as <b>Butlersburg</b>, in honour of Colonel John Butler, the commander of Butler's Rangers, was renamed <b>West Niagara</b> to distinguish it from <a href="/wiki/Fort_Niagara" title="Fort Niagara">Fort Niagara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maritimehistoryofthegreatlakes.ca_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-maritimehistoryofthegreatlakes.ca-3">[3]</a></sup> It was a British military base and haven for pro-British <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">loyalists</a> fleeing the United States during the volatile aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InfoNiagara.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-InfoNiagara.com-4">[4]</a></sup> Renamed <b>Newark</b> by <a href="/wiki/List_of_lieutenant_governors_of_Ontario" title="List of lieutenant governors of Ontario">Lieutenant-Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Graves_Simcoe" title="John Graves Simcoe">John Graves Simcoe</a> in 1792, he made it the first capital of <a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper Canada</a> (now the province of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>), The first provincial parliament was convened at the <a href="/wiki/Navy_Hall" title="Navy Hall">Navy Hall</a> on September 17, 1792.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InfoNiagara.com_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-InfoNiagara.com-4">[4]</a></sup> Due to Newark's close proximity to the Canada–US border, Simcoe moved the capital in 1797 to <a href="/wiki/York,_Upper_Canada" title="York, Upper Canada">York</a> and Newark was renamed 'Niagara' in 1798.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maritimehistoryofthegreatlakes.ca_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-maritimehistoryofthegreatlakes.ca-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Niagara played a central role in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>. Niagara was taken in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fort_George" title="Battle of Fort George">Battle of Fort George</a> by American forces after a two-day bombardment by cannons from Fort Niagara and the American Fleet, followed by a fierce battle. Later in the war, the town was razed and burnt to the ground by American soldiers as they withdrew to Fort Niagara. Undaunted by this setback, often referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Fort_Niagara#Burning_of_Newark" title="Capture of Fort Niagara">"burning of Newark,"</a> the citizens rebuilt the town after the war, with the residential quarter around Queen Street and toward King Street, where the new Court House was rebuilt using material out of range of Fort Niagara's cannons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InfoNiagara.com_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-InfoNiagara.com-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1859 the town built its first public school, <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Public_School" title="Niagara Public School">Niagara Public School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The town's present name was adopted around 1880 as a Postal Address to distinguish the town from <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls,_Ontario" title="Niagara Falls, Ontario">Niagara Falls</a>. The name was not officially adopted until 1970, when the Town of Niagara and the Township of Niagara merged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InfoNiagara.com_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-InfoNiagara.com-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_sites">Historic sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara-on-the-Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historic sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most of the former military sites, such as <a href="/wiki/Fort_George,_Ontario" title="Fort George, Ontario">Fort George</a>, <a href="/wiki/Navy_Hall" title="Navy Hall">Navy Hall</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Butler%27s_Barracks" title="Butler's Barracks">Butler's Barracks</a>, have been restored. Fort George's restoration was done as a "Make Work Project," guided by plans from the Royal Engineers during the Great Depression of the 1930s, an early example of historic preservation. Fort George National Historic Site is a focal point in a collection of War of 1812 sites which, collectively, are managed by <a href="/wiki/Parks_Canada" title="Parks Canada">Parks Canada</a> under the name Niagara National Historic Sites. That administrative name includes several national historic sites: <a href="/wiki/Fort_Mississauga" title="Fort Mississauga">Fort Mississauga</a>, Mississauga Point Lighthouse (1804, the first on the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a>), <a href="/wiki/Navy_Hall" title="Navy Hall">Navy Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butler%27s_Barracks" title="Butler's Barracks">Butler's Barracks</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Queenston_Heights" title="Queenston Heights">Queenston Heights</a>.</p>

<p>Niagara-on-the-Lake teems with historical plaques, many national and provincial, reflecting its significance in the establishment of many of the province's institutions. Among these were its first newspaper, lending library, parliament, historical museum, and governing body for the legal profession. Critical battles in the defence of Upper Canada took place here, at Queenston, including one in which heroine <a href="/wiki/Laura_Secord" title="Laura Secord">Laura Secord</a> gained her fame. The town gave many black Americans their first taste of freedom, both as a stop on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Underground_railroad" title="Underground railroad">Underground railroad</a> for those travelling further into Upper Canada, and as a refuge in its own right. Its stock of Regency and Classical Revival buildings, considered the best in the country from the post-War of 1812 period, led the Historic Sites and Monuments Board of Canada to recommend the town's historic district be designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Sites_of_Canada" title="National Historic Sites of Canada">National Historic Site of Canada</a>, a designation which was approved in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The historic centre had been designated as a provincial Heritage Conservation District under the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Heritage_Act" title="Ontario Heritage Act">Ontario Heritage Act</a> in 1986. Although it did not make the final list, the Historic District was considered for nomination as a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara-on-the-Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Niagara-on-the-Lake is within the federal electoral district of <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls_(electoral_district)" title="Niagara Falls (electoral district)">Niagara Falls</a>, currently represented in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_House_of_Commons" title="Canadian House of Commons">Canadian House of Commons</a> by <a href="/wiki/Rob_Nicholson" title="Rob Nicholson">Rob Nicholson</a>, and the provincial electoral district of <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls_(provincial_electoral_district)" title="Niagara Falls (provincial electoral district)">Niagara Falls</a>, represented in the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Ontario" title="Legislative Assembly of Ontario">Legislative Assembly of Ontario</a> by <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Gates" title="Wayne Gates">Wayne Gates</a>.</p>
<p>It is the only municipality in Canada whose elected leader is designated as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor" title="Lord Mayor">Lord Mayor</a>, a title most common in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lordy_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-lordy-2">[2]</a></sup> Popular legend suggests <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward,_Duke_of_Kent_and_Strathearn" title="Prince Edward, Duke of Kent and Strathearn">Prince Edward, Duke of Kent and Strathearn</a> bestowed the title on the mayor of Niagara during a visit to the town in the early 1800s, in recognition of the town's history as the first capital of Upper Canada;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lordy_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-lordy-2">[2]</a></sup> however, there is no record of a mayor using it until Jerry Mussen in the early 1920s, and even afterward the title was used only irregularly until the Regional Municipality of Niagara Act of 1969 legislated that "The mayor of the Town of Niagara-on-the-Lake shall be known as the Lord Mayor."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lordy_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-lordy-2">[2]</a></sup> The town's Lord Mayor is Patrick Darte, who was first elected in the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Region_municipal_elections,_2014" title="Niagara Region municipal elections, 2014">2014 municipal election</a>; previous Lord Mayors have included Dave Eke, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Burroughs" title="Gary Burroughs">Gary Burroughs</a>, Art Viola, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Dietsch" title="Mike Dietsch">Mike Dietsch</a>, Stan Ignatczyk, Jim Marino, Wilbert Dick, <a href="/wiki/Jake_Froese" title="Jake Froese">Jake Froese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frederick_S._Goring" title="Frederick S. Goring">Fred Goring</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lordy_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-lordy-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>The town is home to the <a href="/wiki/Shaw_Festival" title="Shaw Festival">Shaw Festival</a>, a series of theatrical productions featuring the works of <a href="/wiki/George_Bernard_Shaw" title="George Bernard Shaw">George Bernard Shaw</a>, his contemporaries, or plays about his era (1856–1950), running from April to November. The festival operates three theatres in the centre of town: the Festival, Royal George, and Court House theatres, and features one of a repertory acting company, scenic staff, and collection of resident and guest directors considered some of the best in the English-speaking world.</p>
<p>Along the Niagara Parkway is <a href="/wiki/RiverBrink_Art_Museum" title="RiverBrink Art Museum">RiverBrink Art Museum</a>, a popular tourist destination in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queenston" title="Queenston">Queenston</a>. It is home to a unique collection of over 1,400 artworks and artefacts by Canadian and international artists, assembled by Samuel E. Weir. Completed in 1970, the building features Georgian-style architecture, including a mansard roof and gabled windows. It served as Weir's country residence, and was converted into an art museum following his death in 1981.</p>
<p>The surrounding region enjoys a comparatively mild climate thanks to the adjoining lakes, and excellent soil for fruit production, for which it has become one of Canada's centres. In particular, NOTL has grown into a major viticultural region. Visitors flock to dozens of nearby wineries, including those making the world's largest volumes of <a href="/wiki/Ice_wine" title="Ice wine">ice wine</a>. The town is also known for its gardens, art galleries, antique shops, and golf courses. There are many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotels" title="Hotels">hotels</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inns" title="Inns">inns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bed_and_breakfast" title="Bed and breakfast">bed and breakfasts</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vacation_rentals" title="Vacation rentals">vacation rentals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Day_spa" title="Day spa">spas</a> in the area.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_Amenities">Local Amenities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara-on-the-Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Local Amenities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Niagara on the Lake boasts a wide variety of local soccer fields, 2 swimming pools, a community centre, baseball diamonds, hockey rinks, and lacrosse fields. The main pool, Niagara Pool, Is located off of King Street. It is the home of the Niagara on the lake Electrifying Eels Swim Team and boasts 2 high diving boards, change rooms, and a beautiful view of the park surrounding the pool. Swimming lessons, water aerobics, and many youth programs are held at the pool during the summer months. The Niagara on the Lake Soccer Club is the organization which runs all soccer in the town, and is a leading club within the Niagara Region.</p>

<p>Only 15% of the population is under 14 years of age. Those over 65 years of age number 22.6% and constitute a fast-growing population. The town has seen growth of almost 1% yearly, partially due to a large number of <a href="/wiki/Retirement" title="Retirement">retirees</a> moving to the town.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara-on-the-Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Niagara-on-the-Lake can be reached by <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_Way" title="Queen Elizabeth Way">Queen Elizabeth Way</a>. Public transportation is served by <a href="/wiki/Niagara-on-the-Lake_Transit" title="Niagara-on-the-Lake Transit">Niagara-on-the-Lake Transit</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognition">Awards and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara-on-the-Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Awards and recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Town of Niagara was the site of the <a href="/wiki/8th_World_Scout_Jamboree" title="8th World Scout Jamboree">8th World Scout Jamboree</a> in 1955. Over 11,000 Scouts from 71 countries attended the Jamboree. It was the first to be held outside Europe and had the theme "Jamboree of New Horizons". Niagara-on-the-Lake was named the Prettiest Town in Canada in 1996 by <a href="/wiki/Communities_in_Bloom" title="Communities in Bloom">Communities in Bloom</a>, a nationwide beautification programme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The town is now a popular tourist destination, located at the northern terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Parkway" title="Niagara Parkway">Niagara Parkway</a>, a scenic drive and biking/walking path.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities">Communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara-on-the-Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In addition to the primary town site of Niagara-on-the-Lake, the Old Town, the town also includes the villages of Glendale, Homer, McNab, <a href="/wiki/Queenston,_Ontario" title="Queenston, Ontario">Queenston</a>, St. Davids, Niagara-on-the-Green and Virgil.</p>
<p>Glendale is located near the junction of the Queen Elizabeth Way <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/QEW" title="QEW">QEW</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Highway_405" title="Ontario Highway 405">Highway 405</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highway_55_(Ontario)" title="Highway 55 (Ontario)">Highway 55</a>, and adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Welland_Canal" title="Welland Canal">Welland Canal</a>. It is home to the Niagara-on-the-Lake campus of <a href="/wiki/Niagara_College" title="Niagara College">Niagara College</a>.</p>
<p>Virgil is just south-west of the Old Town of Niagara-on-the-Lake, where most of the tourism takes place. The community has a large <a href="/wiki/Mennonite" title="Mennonite">Mennonite</a> community, who settled the area in the early to mid 20th-century from Russia. Virgil has a large sports park, serving as the centre of Niagara-on-the-Lake's bustling hockey, softball, lacrosse and soccer leagues, two arenas, three baseball diamonds and a skate park. Once a year, on the Victoria Day weekend in May, the community holds its "Virgil Stampede". The festival includes rides, attractions and its annual soccer start-up tournament. Virgil's educational institutions are St. Michael's Elementary School and Crossroads Public School, which opened in September 2011, amalgamating the now-closed Virgil and Colonel John Butler Public Schools. The town's only secondary school, Niagara District, was closed by the District School Board of Niagara in 2010.</p>
<p>The Old Town also had an elementary school on King Street: Parliament Oak Public School. It was on the site of the signing of the Act Against Slavery of 1793, by the first legislative session of the parliament of Upper Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>The school was closed on June 25, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>St. Davids Public School serves Junior Kindergarten to Grade 8 students in the southern part of the municipality. St. Michael Catholic elementary school serves Junior Kindergarten to Grade 8 students in the southern part of the municipality. Crossroads Elementary School serves Junior Kindergarten to Grade 8 students in the southern part of the municipality. High school students now take a bus to Laura Secord in St. Catharines, or to A.N. Myer in Niagara Falls.</p>
