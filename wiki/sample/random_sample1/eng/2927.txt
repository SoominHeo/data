<p><i><b>Knight Rider</b></i> is a 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">series</a> that follows the 1982 <a href="/wiki/Knight_Rider_(1982_TV_series)" title="Knight Rider (1982 TV series)">TV series of the same title</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Glen_A._Larson" title="Glen A. Larson">Glen A. Larson</a> and the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Knight_Rider_(2008_film)" title="Knight Rider (2008 film)">television movie</a>. The series stars <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bruening" title="Justin Bruening">Justin Bruening</a> as Mike Traceur, the estranged son of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Knight_(Knight_Rider)" title="Michael Knight (Knight Rider)">Michael Knight</a>. The series also stars <a href="/wiki/Deanna_Russo" title="Deanna Russo">Deanna Russo</a> as Sarah Graiman, Traceur's former girlfriend and love interest. Sarah is the daughter of Charles Graiman. Graiman, played by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Davison" title="Bruce Davison">Bruce Davison</a>, is the creator of a new generation of <a href="/wiki/KITT" title="KITT">KITT</a> (Knight Industries Three Thousand), which is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Val_Kilmer" title="Val Kilmer">Val Kilmer</a>. The series was in production for just one season.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight_Rider_(2008_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the spring of 2007, <a href="/wiki/NBCUniversal" title="NBCUniversal">NBCUniversal</a> chief executive <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Zucker" title="Jeff Zucker">Jeff Zucker</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Ben_Silverman" title="Ben Silverman">Ben Silverman</a> as chief NBC programmer, and asked him to help bring NBC out of last place in network ratings. One of Silverman's early moves was to try to revive old franchises like <a href="/wiki/Knight_Rider_(franchise)" title="Knight Rider (franchise)"><i>Knight Rider</i></a> and <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gladiators_(2008_TV_series)" title="American Gladiators (2008 TV series)">American Gladiators</a></i>. Silverman, in a deal endorsed by <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford Motor Company</a>, the car supplier, approved production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In advance of its anticipated weekly run, the new <i>Knight Rider</i> series was introduced with a <a href="/wiki/Knight_Rider_(2008_film)" title="Knight Rider (2008 film)">television movie</a> on February 17, 2008. The new series premiered on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> on September 24, 2008 and led the network's Wednesday evening lineup, along with <i><a href="/wiki/Deal_or_No_Deal" title="Deal or No Deal">Deal or No Deal</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Lipstick_Jungle_(TV_series)" title="Lipstick Jungle (TV series)">Lipstick Jungle</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On October 21, 2008, NBC gave the show a full season order of 22 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> All of the episodes that aired on TV are also available on the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Network" title="PlayStation Network">PlayStation Network</a> Video Store,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xbox_Live_Marketplace" title="Xbox Live Marketplace">Xbox Live Marketplace</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-knightriderxbox_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-knightriderxbox-5">[5]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hulu_Plus" title="Hulu Plus">Hulu Plus</a> (available only for computer streaming, not TV or mobile streaming),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The episodes are available in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">HD</a> format and <a href="/wiki/Standard-definition_television" title="Standard-definition television">SD</a> format.</p>
<p>On November 10, 2008, NBC reformatted the show in an effort to more closely resemble the storyline of its predecessor. Yancey Arias, Bruce Davison, and Sydney Tamiia Poitier's characters were removed from the series after the end of their original thirteen episode commitment starting with a two-part episode scheduled to air in January 2009. The show continued with a more character-driven focus on Michael and KITT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywoodreporter_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodreporter-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 3, 2008, <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> reduced its season order to seventeen episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seasonorderreduced_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-seasonorderreduced-9">[9]</a></sup> The series finale aired on March 4, 2009. When NBC's 2009-2010 schedule was announced on May 19, 2009, it did not include <i>Knight Rider</i>, effectively signaling the cancellation of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EOnline01_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-EOnline01-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Futon01_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Futon01-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW01_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW01-13">[13]</a></sup> Following the announcement, NBC was criticised and petitions to continue the series were made, but they failed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="KITT">KITT</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knight_Rider_(2008_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: KITT">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/KITT" title="KITT">KITT</a></div>
<p>For the series premiere, many new features in KITT were introduced in addition to those seen on the <a href="/wiki/Knight_Rider_(2008_film)#Knight_Industries_Three_Thousand_.28K.I.T.T..29" title="Knight Rider (2008 film)">pilot movie</a>. As an homage to the original KITT's Super Pursuit Mode, the new KITT transforms into Attack Mode<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> - a significantly more aggressive version than the Attack Mode depicted in the pilot movie, which consisted primarily of an extended rear <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spoiler_(automotive)" title="Spoiler (automotive)">spoiler</a>. KITT also transforms into a <a href="/wiki/Ford_F-Series" title="Ford F-Series">Ford F-150 FX4</a> pickup truck for off-road purposes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Ford_E-Series" title="Ford E-Series">Ford E-150</a> van, <a href="/wiki/Ford_Flex" title="Ford Flex">Ford Flex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ford_Crown_Victoria_Police_Interceptor" title="Ford Crown Victoria Police Interceptor">Ford Crown Victoria Police Interceptor</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Ford_Mustang_(first_generation)#1969.E2.80.931970" title="Ford Mustang (first generation)">1969 Mach 1 Mustang</a> for disguise purposes. The series also demonstrated capabilities such as KITT's Turbo Boost, allowing KITT to briefly become airborne, and submergibility, maintaining system integrity and life support for occupants while underwater. Also installed in KITT are a grappling hook, a laser that originates from his scanner bar, double mini-guns, defensive flares, offensive missiles, parachute, a dart gun, a sonic inhibitor and an <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_pulse#Non-nuclear_electromagnetic_pulse" title="Electromagnetic pulse">EMP</a> weapon.</p>
<p>The interior of the vehicle has been reworked, with the single display screen on the <a href="/wiki/Center_console_(automobile)" title="Center console (automobile)">center console</a> replaced by <a href="/wiki/Touchscreen" title="Touchscreen">touch-enabled</a> <a href="/wiki/Head-up_display" title="Head-up display">head-up displays</a> spanning the entire interior surface of the windshield. The removal of the center console screen has also seen KITT's "presence" indicator, reminiscent of the original series KITT's voice modulator, appearing in a pulsing orb mounted high in the middle of the dashboard with a dot in the middle that shifts over to "look" at whoever he's talking to. Other interior changes include a more conventional <a href="/wiki/Steering_wheel" title="Steering wheel">steering wheel</a>, replacing the previously seen 3/4 steering wheel, blue lighting in the dashboard over the driver's-side instrumentation and the passenger-side console that becomes red whenever KITT is in Attack Mode, and a standard Mustang rear seat instead of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super-computer" title="Super-computer">super-computer</a> laden rear seat of the movie KITT. The interior of 4x4 Mode uses the same customized Mustang dashboard, but the F-150's passenger and driver's seats. KITT also carries a backup mainframe that he can reboot to in the event his primary is damaged. KITT's mechanics are high-tech industrial robots named Katie, Lisa, and Hank. They scan KITT for damages and interact with the human actors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>KITT's technology includes a surface screen program, enabling the hood to operate as a touchscreen display, similar to the internal head-up displays, a printer in the passenger-side console, and a 3D Object Generator in the rear passenger compartment, as well as a self-destruct program.</p>
