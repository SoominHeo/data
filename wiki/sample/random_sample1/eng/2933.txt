<p><b>Insook Bhushan</b>, born <b>Na In-Sook</b> in 1952, is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">Korean-</a><a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis" title="Table tennis">table tennis</a> player. She was a member of the South Korean women's team that won the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/1971_World_Table_Tennis_Championships" title="1971 World Table Tennis Championships">1971 World Championships</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/1973_World_Table_Tennis_Championships" title="1973 World Table Tennis Championships">1973 World Championships</a>, she was limited to the role of "non-playing captain" of the gold medal-winning South Korean women's team. In 1974, she emigrated to the USA, where her father had established an import-export business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The following year, she married the architect Shekhar Bhushan and in 1980 became a US citizen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1976 and 1991, Bhushan dominated the <a href="/wiki/USA_Table_Tennis_Champions" title="USA Table Tennis Champions">US National Championships</a>, winning the title in women's singles 11 times, women's doubles 11 times, and mixed doubles 8 times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1977, she won the women's singles title at the US Open.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bhushan returned to her birthplace for the <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis_at_the_1988_Summer_Olympics" title="Table tennis at the 1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Olympics</a>, where she competed in women's singles and women's doubles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also competed in women's singles at the <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis_at_the_1992_Summer_Olympics" title="Table tennis at the 1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Olympics</a> and represented the USA in 6 World Championships. She participated in 3 <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis_at_the_Pan_American_Games" title="Table tennis at the Pan American Games">Pan American Games</a>, winning 8 gold medals (including 3 consecutive gold medals in women's singles) and 1 silver medal.</p>
<p>Bhushan retired from competition after the 1992 Olympics and was inducted into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USATT" title="USATT">USATT</a> Hall of Fame in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bhushan has a degree in accounting from the University of Colorado at Denver<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and works as an accountant in the Colorado Department of Military and Veterans Affairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She has two sons, Austin (born in 1980) and Kevin (born in 1985).</p>
