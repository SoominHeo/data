<p><b>Nile Gregory Rodgers, Jr.</b> (born September 19, 1952) is an American record producer, songwriter, musician, composer, arranger and guitarist. He is the lead guitarist and co-founding member with <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Edwards" title="Bernard Edwards">Bernard Edwards</a> of the band <a href="/wiki/Chic_(band)" title="Chic (band)">Chic</a>, which has been active since 1976 and was one of the most successful groups of the <a href="/wiki/Disco" title="Disco">disco</a> era. He is famed for his "chucking" rhythm guitar style.</p>
<p>Although he recorded three solo albums during the 1980s and 1990s, and one as part of the short-lived group Outloud, Rodgers has found more success in collaboration as producer and performer with many artists including <a href="/wiki/Sister_Sledge" title="Sister Sledge">Sister Sledge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheena_Easton" title="Sheena Easton">Sheena Easton</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Ferry" title="Bryan Ferry">Bryan Ferry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, <a href="/wiki/INXS" title="INXS">INXS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spoons" title="Spoons">Spoons</a>, and more recently, <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disclosure_(band)" title="Disclosure (band)">Disclosure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Smith_(singer)" title="Sam Smith (singer)">Sam Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nervo_(duo)" title="Nervo (duo)">Nervo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laura_Mvula" title="Laura Mvula">Laura Mvula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sigala" title="Sigala">Sigala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keith_Urban" title="Keith Urban">Keith Urban</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>. On April 7, 2017, Rodgers will be inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> and receive an Award for Musical Excellence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His mother became pregnant at age 13, and gave birth to him in New York City. His parents and step-father were all <a href="/wiki/Beat_Generation" title="Beat Generation">Beat-niks</a>, and heavy heroin users throughout his childhood. With music all around him in his childhood, he tried out all of his school's band instruments, before a love of <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> drew him towards the electric guitar, and especially the <a href="/wiki/Fender_Stratocaster" title="Fender Stratocaster">Fender Stratocaster</a>.</p>
<p>He began his career as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_musician" title="Studio musician">session guitarist</a> in New York, touring with the <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i> band, led by <a href="/wiki/Joe_Raposo" title="Joe Raposo">Joe Raposo</a>, in his teens, and then working in the <a href="/wiki/House_band" title="House band">house band</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harlem,_Manhattan" title="Harlem, Manhattan">Harlem</a>'s world-famous <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Theater" title="Apollo Theater">Apollo Theater</a>, playing behind <a href="/wiki/Screamin%27_Jay_Hawkins" title="Screamin' Jay Hawkins">Screamin' Jay Hawkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maxine_Brown_(soul_singer)" title="Maxine Brown (soul singer)">Maxine Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_E._King" title="Ben E. King">Ben E. King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Betty_Wright" title="Betty Wright">Betty Wright</a>, Earl Lewis and the Channels, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliament_Funkadelic" title="Parliament Funkadelic">Parliament Funkadelic</a>, and many other legendary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> artists.</p>
<p>In his teens Rodgers was a member of the Harlem chapter of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Panther_Party_for_Self-Defense" title="Black Panther Party for Self-Defense">Black Panther Party for Self-Defense</a>. He remains friendly with numerous former Panthers, including Professor <a href="/wiki/Jamal_Joseph" title="Jamal Joseph">Jamal Joseph</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Nile met bassist <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Edwards" title="Bernard Edwards">Bernard Edwards</a> in 1970 while working as a touring musician for the <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i> stage show. Together they formed The Big Apple Band, and worked as back-up musicians for the vocal group <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_(band)" title="New York City (band)">New York City</a> ("<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Doin%27_Fine_Now" title="I'm Doin' Fine Now">I'm Doin' Fine Now</a>"). New York City's one hit allowed them to tour extensively, even opening for <a href="/wiki/The_Jackson_5" title="The Jackson 5">The Jackson 5</a> on the American leg of their first world tour in 1973. The band dissolved after their second album failed to yield a hit, but Nile and Bernard joined forces with drummer <a href="/wiki/Tony_Thompson_(drummer)" title="Tony Thompson (drummer)">Tony Thompson</a>, and worked and recorded as a Funk Rock band called The Boys, which played numerous gigs up and down the East Coast. Despite major label interest in their demos, they could not get a record deal when the record companies discovered they were black, as they thought that black rock artists would be too hard to promote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The band continued playing mostly local bars.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_of_Chic">Formation of Chic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Formation of Chic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the Big Apple Band, Rodgers and Edwards worked with <a href="/wiki/Ashford_%26_Simpson" title="Ashford &amp; Simpson">Ashford &amp; Simpson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luther_Vandross" title="Luther Vandross">Luther Vandross</a>, and many others. Since another New York artist, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Murphy" title="Walter Murphy">Walter Murphy</a>, had a band also called <a href="/wiki/Walter_Murphy" title="Walter Murphy">The Big Apple Band</a>, Rodgers and Edwards were forced to change their band's name to avoid confusion. Thus, in 1977 the band was renamed as <a href="/wiki/Chic_(band)" title="Chic (band)">Chic</a>. Rodgers has stated that a major inspiration was a <a href="/wiki/Roxy_Music" title="Roxy Music">Roxy Music</a> gig he saw in London:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>It was a totally immersive artistic experience – the crowd were fly, the band were fly, the music was textual, they were saying 'come into my world'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:342px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CHIC.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="428" data-file-width="658" height="221" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/CHIC.jpg/340px-CHIC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/CHIC.jpg/510px-CHIC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/67/CHIC.jpg 2x" width="340"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CHIC.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Chic_(band)" title="Chic (band)">Chic</a> in 1970s: Luci Martin, Nile Rodgers, Bernard Edwards, Alfa Anderson (photo by <a href="/wiki/Jill_Furmanovsky" title="Jill Furmanovsky">Jill Furmanovsky</a>).</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Between gigs, they recorded their first album with then-boss <a href="/wiki/Luther_Vandross" title="Luther Vandross">Luther Vandross</a>, who provided background vocals on the group's early recordings. The band scored numerous top ten hits and helped propel <a href="/wiki/Disco" title="Disco">disco</a> to new levels of popularity. Chic's chart-topping songs "<a href="/wiki/Le_Freak" title="Le Freak">Le Freak</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I_Want_Your_Love_(Chic_song)" title="I Want Your Love (Chic song)">I Want Your Love</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Everybody_Dance_(Chic_song)" title="Everybody Dance (Chic song)">Everybody Dance</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Dance,_Dance,_Dance_(Yowsah,_Yowsah,_Yowsah)" title="Dance, Dance, Dance (Yowsah, Yowsah, Yowsah)">Dance, Dance, Dance</a>", "<a href="/wiki/My_Forbidden_Lover" title="My Forbidden Lover">My Forbidden Lover</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Good_Times_(Chic_song)" title="Good Times (Chic song)">Good Times</a>" became club/pop/R&amp;B standards. "Le Freak" was composed after being turned away from <a href="/wiki/Studio_54" title="Studio 54">Studio 54</a>, despite being invited by <a href="/wiki/Grace_Jones" title="Grace Jones">Grace Jones</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEShapiropp.158.E2.80.939_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEShapiropp.158.E2.80.939-5">[5]</a></sup> "Le Freak" was <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>' only triple platinum selling single for more than 35 years and "Good Times" shot to the No. 1 spot in spite of the "<a href="/wiki/Disco_Demolition_Night" title="Disco Demolition Night">Disco Sucks</a>" backlash in 1979.</p>
<p>The success of Chic's first singles led Atlantic to offer Rodgers and Edwards the opportunity to produce any act on its roster. They chose <a href="/wiki/Sister_Sledge" title="Sister Sledge">Sister Sledge</a>, whose 1979 album, <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_Family_(album)" title="We Are Family (album)">We Are Family</a></i>, peaked at No. 3 and remained on the charts well into 1980. The first two singles, "<a href="/wiki/He%27s_the_Greatest_Dancer" title="He's the Greatest Dancer">He's the Greatest Dancer</a>" and the title cut "<a href="/wiki/We_Are_Family_(song)" title="We Are Family (song)">We Are Family</a>" both reached No. 1 on the R&amp;B chart, and No. 6 and No. 2, respectively on the Pop chart. "He's the Greatest Dancer" was sampled in 1998 on <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gettin%27_Jiggy_Wit_It" title="Gettin' Jiggy Wit It">Gettin' Jiggy Wit It</a>".</p>
<p>As the Chic Organization's tight sound became increasingly sought-after, Rodgers and Edwards began record production with numerous artists, together or individually.</p>
<p>The combination of Nile's cocaine addiction, insomnia and Bernards family later separated the pair as partners as the circumstances lead Nile to work at an increasing rate whilst Bernard began to slow down.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1980, Rodgers and Edwards wrote and produced the album <i><a href="/wiki/Diana_(album)" title="Diana (album)">Diana</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a>, yielding the smash hits "<a href="/wiki/Upside_Down_(Diana_Ross_song)" title="Upside Down (Diana Ross song)">Upside Down</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Coming_Out" title="I'm Coming Out">I'm Coming Out</a>". Chic's song "<a href="/wiki/Good_Times_(Chic_song)" title="Good Times (Chic song)">Good Times</a>" played a pivotal role in the explosion of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop music</a>, as an interpolation of the song's bass line and the record's string-section sample was the bedrock of <a href="/wiki/The_Sugarhill_Gang" title="The Sugarhill Gang">The Sugarhill Gang</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Rapper%27s_Delight" title="Rapper's Delight">Rapper's Delight</a>", the first multiple-platinum Hip Hop single. The Chic Organization produced the smash hit "Spacer" for French disco act <a href="/wiki/Sheila_(and)_B._Devotion" title="Sheila (and) B. Devotion">Sheila and B. Devotion</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deborah_Harry" title="Deborah Harry">Deborah Harry</a>'s 1981 solo album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koo_Koo" title="Koo Koo">Koo Koo</a></i>.</p>
<p>Chic dissolved in 1983 after its final Atlantic album on their contract, <i><a href="/wiki/Believer_(Chic_album)" title="Believer (Chic album)">Believer</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Soup_for_One_(soundtrack)" title="Soup for One (soundtrack)">Soup For One</a></i> (a film soundtrack). At that time Rodgers began a solo career producing his first album <i><a href="/wiki/Adventures_in_the_Land_of_the_Good_Groove" title="Adventures in the Land of the Good Groove">Adventures in the Land of the Good Groove</a></i>.</p>
<p>Rodgers produced the 1983 album <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Talkback_(Spoons_album)" title="Talkback (Spoons album)"><i>Talkback</i></a>, by Canadian New Wave/New Romantic band <a href="/wiki/Spoons_(band)" title="Spoons (band)">Spoons</a>, which included the hits "Old Emotions" and "The Rhythm".</p>
<p>Rodgers produced <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s biggest selling album <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Dance_(David_Bowie_album)" title="Let's Dance (David Bowie album)">Let's Dance</a></i> with several hit singles including "<a href="/wiki/China_Girl_(song)" title="China Girl (song)">China Girl</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Modern_Love_(song)" title="Modern Love (song)">Modern Love</a>" and the title track, "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Dance_(David_Bowie_song)" title="Let's Dance (David Bowie song)">Let's Dance</a>". He produced the single "<a href="/wiki/Original_Sin_(INXS_song)" title="Original Sin (INXS song)">Original Sin</a>" by <a href="/wiki/INXS" title="INXS">INXS</a>. <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a> worked extensively with Rodgers after he remixed their largest selling hit single, "<a href="/wiki/The_Reflex" title="The Reflex">The Reflex</a>" in 1984 and followed up with "<a href="/wiki/The_Wild_Boys_(song)" title="The Wild Boys (song)">The Wild Boys</a>" on their 1984 live album <i><a href="/wiki/Arena_(Duran_Duran_album)" title="Arena (Duran Duran album)">Arena</a></i>. It was rumored that Rodgers had become a member of <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a> when they released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Notorious_(Duran_Duran_album)" title="Notorious (Duran Duran album)">Notorious</a></i>, but he only acted as a co-producer and session musician on that album.</p>
<p>That same year he produced <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s blockbuster album <i><a href="/wiki/Like_a_Virgin_(album)" title="Like a Virgin (album)">Like a Virgin</a></i>, spawning her two signature hits "<a href="/wiki/Material_Girl" title="Material Girl">Material Girl</a>" and the album's title track, "<a href="/wiki/Like_a_Virgin_(song)" title="Like a Virgin (song)">Like a Virgin</a>". The album saw an unofficial reunion of Chic's core members, with Rodgers, Edwards, drummer Tony Thompson and keyboard player Robert Sabino all contributing. Rodgers also joined <a href="/wiki/Robert_Plant" title="Robert Plant">Robert Plant</a>'s platinum selling studio band <a href="/wiki/The_Honeydrippers" title="The Honeydrippers">The Honeydrippers</a>, on the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Honeydrippers:_Volume_One" title="The Honeydrippers: Volume One">The Honeydrippers: Volume One</a></i>. This period sparked Rodgers' interest in soundtracks, the first of which were <i><a href="/wiki/Alphabet_City_(film)" title="Alphabet City (film)">Alphabet City</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gremlins" title="Gremlins">Gremlins</a></i> ("Out Out" – <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gabriel" title="Peter Gabriel">Peter Gabriel</a>), <i><a href="/wiki/Against_All_Odds_(film)" title="Against All Odds (film)">Against All Odds</a></i> ("<a href="/wiki/Against_All_Odds_(soundtrack)" title="Against All Odds (soundtrack)">Walk Through the Fire</a>" – Peter Gabriel), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/That%27s_Dancing" title="That's Dancing">That's Dancing</a></i> ("Invitation to Dance" – <a href="/wiki/Kim_Carnes" title="Kim Carnes">Kim Carnes</a>), <i><a href="/wiki/White_Nights_(1985_film)" title="White Nights (1985 film)">White Nights</a></i> (numerous songs) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fly_(1986_film)" title="The Fly (1986 film)">The Fly</a></i> ("Help Me") – <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Ferry" title="Bryan Ferry">Bryan Ferry</a>).</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nile_Rodgers_and_Madonna_Live_Aid_1985.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="667" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Nile_Rodgers_and_Madonna_Live_Aid_1985.jpg/300px-Nile_Rodgers_and_Madonna_Live_Aid_1985.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Nile_Rodgers_and_Madonna_Live_Aid_1985.jpg/450px-Nile_Rodgers_and_Madonna_Live_Aid_1985.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Nile_Rodgers_and_Madonna_Live_Aid_1985.jpg/600px-Nile_Rodgers_and_Madonna_Live_Aid_1985.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>

<p>In 1985, Rodgers produced albums for <a href="/wiki/Sheena_Easton" title="Sheena Easton">Sheena Easton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Beck" title="Jeff Beck">Jeff Beck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thompson_Twins" title="Thompson Twins">Thompson Twins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a>, and many others, while still finding time to perform at <a href="/wiki/Live_Aid" title="Live Aid">Live Aid</a> with Madonna and the Thompson Twins. He was awarded No. 1 Singles Producer in the World in <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine to close out the year.</p>
<p>In 1986, he produced <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Notorious_(Duran_Duran_album)" title="Notorious (Duran Duran album)">Notorious</a></i> album, which yielded a No. 2 title track hit, "<a href="/wiki/Notorious_(Duran_Duran_song)" title="Notorious (Duran Duran song)">Notorious</a>". During a live set, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Le_Bon" title="Simon Le Bon">Simon Le Bon</a> introduced Rodgers by saying, "Well, this band went through a difficult time and it might not have made it if it weren't for this gentleman." Rodgers contributed to numerous other projects and appearances with members of the band throughout the 1980s. He also produced albums for <a href="/wiki/Grace_Jones" title="Grace Jones">Grace Jones</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth_Wind_and_Fire" title="Earth Wind and Fire">Earth Wind and Fire</a>'s vocalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phillip_Bailey" title="Phillip Bailey">Phillip Bailey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Al_Jarreau" title="Al Jarreau">Al Jarreau</a>. Rodgers performed on "<a href="/wiki/Higher_Love" title="Higher Love">Higher Love</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Steve_Winwood" title="Steve Winwood">Steve Winwood</a>, and records for <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howard_Jones_(musician)" title="Howard Jones (musician)">Howard Jones</a>, and <a href="/wiki/David_Sanborn" title="David Sanborn">David Sanborn</a>. He then worked on the soundtrack for <a href="/wiki/Laurie_Anderson" title="Laurie Anderson">Laurie Anderson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Home_of_the_Brave_(1986_film)" title="Home of the Brave (1986 film)">Home of the Brave</a></i>.</p>
<p>Rodgers formed the short-lived experimental band Outloud in 1987, with <a href="/wiki/David_Letterman" title="David Letterman">David Letterman</a>'s guitarist, composer, and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Felicia_Collins" title="Felicia Collins">Felicia Collins</a> and acclaimed French session musician, producer, composer, and keyboardist <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Saisse" title="Philippe Saisse">Philippe Saisse</a>; the trio released a single album, <i>Out Loud</i>, on Warner Bros. Records.</p>
<p>In 1988, Rodgers composed his first orchestral soundtrack for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Coming_to_America" title="Coming to America">Coming to America</a></i> (the second highest-grossing film of the year) starring <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Murphy" title="Eddie Murphy">Eddie Murphy</a>. Rodgers followed this with soundtracks for <i>White Hot</i> (the world's first Hi-Def feature motion picture), and <i><a href="/wiki/Earth_Girls_Are_Easy" title="Earth Girls Are Easy">Earth Girls Are Easy</a></i>. The latter would pair him with <a href="/wiki/The_B-52%27s" title="The B-52's">The B-52's</a>. In 1989 he co-produced their comeback multi-platinum album <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmic_Thing" title="Cosmic Thing">Cosmic Thing</a></i>, which had the hit singles "<a href="/wiki/Love_Shack" title="Love Shack">Love Shack</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Roam" title="Roam">Roam</a>", "<a href="/wiki/(Shake_That)_Cosmic_Thing" title="(Shake That) Cosmic Thing">Cosmic Thing</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Deadbeat_Club" title="Deadbeat Club">Deadbeat Club</a>". That year he also produced <i><a href="/wiki/Workin%27_Overtime" title="Workin' Overtime">Workin' Overtime</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a>' return to <a href="/wiki/Motown" title="Motown">Motown</a>, a deal that scored her an executive position at the label, along with releases by the <a href="/wiki/Dan_Reed_Network" title="Dan Reed Network">Dan Reed Network</a>, Slam, and <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>'s compilation, <i><a href="/wiki/Decade:_Greatest_Hits" title="Decade: Greatest Hits">Decade</a></i>, which was appropriately titled. It was the most successful decade for Rodgers and also for many of the artists he worked with.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="718" height="276" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg/220px-Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg/330px-Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg/440px-Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nilerodgersatlecrib.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Nile Rodgers at his Le Crib Studios, 1999</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In September 1990, Epic Records released the Rodgers produced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vaughan_Brothers" title="Vaughan Brothers">Vaughan Brothers</a> album, <i>Family Style</i>, shortly after the untimely death of guitar virtuoso <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Ray_Vaughan" title="Stevie Ray Vaughan">Stevie Ray Vaughan</a>. Early in this decade he also produced projects for <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Clapton" title="Eric Clapton">Eric Clapton</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_B-52s" title="The B-52s">The B-52s</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Lee_Roth" title="David Lee Roth">David Lee Roth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ric_Ocasek" title="Ric Ocasek">Ric Ocasek</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Dan_Reed_Network" title="Dan Reed Network">Dan Reed Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cathy_Dennis" title="Cathy Dennis">Cathy Dennis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patty_Griffin" title="Patty Griffin">Patty Griffin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harris_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harris-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jimmie_Vaughan" title="Jimmie Vaughan">Jimmie Vaughan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Stray_Cats" title="The Stray Cats">The Stray Cats</a> and many other artists, along with continuing soundtrack work on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thelma_and_Louise" title="Thelma and Louise">Thelma and Louise</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cool_World" title="Cool World">Cool World</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Beavis_and_Butt-head_Experience" title="The Beavis and Butt-head Experience">The Beavis and Butt-head Experience</a></i> (co-writer of "Come to Butt-head"). After a 1992 birthday party where Rodgers, <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Edwards" title="Bernard Edwards">Bernard Edwards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Shaffer" title="Paul Shaffer">Paul Shaffer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anton_Fig" title="Anton Fig">Anton Fig</a> played old Chic hits to rapturous response. Rodgers and Edwards reformed a new version of Chic. They recorded a fresh crop of material for the album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chic-Ism" title="Chic-Ism">Chic-Ism</a></i> and performed live worldwide.</p>
<p>In 1995, he played on the track "Money" from <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/HIStory:_Past,_Present_and_Future,_Book_I" title="HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book I">HIStory</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1996, Rodgers released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Chic_Freak_and_More_Treats" title="Chic Freak and More Treats">Chic Freak and More Treats</a></i> in Japan, containing re-recordings of old Chic material with guest vocalists including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashford_and_Simpson" title="Ashford and Simpson">Ashford and Simpson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taja_Sevelle" title="Taja Sevelle">Taja Sevelle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Le_Bon" title="Simon Le Bon">Simon Le Bon</a> and Christopher Max. Bernard Edwards also appeared, alongside regular Chic vocalist <a href="/wiki/Sylver_Logan_Sharp" title="Sylver Logan Sharp">Sylver Logan Sharp</a> and drummer <a href="/wiki/Omar_Hakim" title="Omar Hakim">Omar Hakim</a>.</p>

<p>In April 1996, Rodgers was honored as the JT Super Producer of the Year. He performed with <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Edwards" title="Bernard Edwards">Bernard Edwards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sister_Sledge" title="Sister Sledge">Sister Sledge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Winwood" title="Steve Winwood">Steve Winwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Le_Bon" title="Simon Le Bon">Simon Le Bon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slash_(musician)" title="Slash (musician)">Slash</a> in a series of commemorative concerts in Japan, which provided a career retrospective. Unfortunately, Rodgers found his longtime musical partner and close friend, <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Edwards" title="Bernard Edwards">Bernard Edwards</a> dead in his hotel room the morning after their last performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A year later Rodgers returned to Japan to pay homage to his fallen partner. In 1999, Rodgers released <i><a href="/wiki/Live_at_the_Budokan_(Chic_album)" title="Live at the Budokan (Chic album)">Live at the Budokan</a></i>, a live recording of the April 17, 1996 JT Super Producer concert and made the decision not to make any overdubs or changes in order to keep the purity of his last performance with Edwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>He started playing live concerts again while composing and producing music for film soundtracks: <i><a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Cop_III" title="Beverly Hills Cop III">Beverly Hills Cop III</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Chips" title="Blue Chips">Blue Chips</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flintstones_(film)" title="The Flintstones (film)">The Flintstones</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Feeling_Minnesota" title="Feeling Minnesota">Feeling Minnesota</a></i> (working with <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>), to name but a few.</p>
<p>In 1997, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Notorious_BIG" title="Notorious BIG">Notorious BIG</a> released "<a href="/wiki/Mo_Money_Mo_Problems" title="Mo Money Mo Problems">Mo Money Mo Problems</a>" sampling Rodgers &amp; Edwards' song "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Coming_Out" title="I'm Coming Out">I'm Coming Out</a>" from Diana Ross's 1980 platinum album, <a href="/wiki/Diana_(album)" title="Diana (album)"><i>diana</i></a>. "Mo Money, Mo Problems" topped the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> for two weeks and was nominated for the 1998 <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group">Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group</a>.</p>
<p>In 1998, Rodgers founded <a href="/wiki/Sumthing_Distribution" title="Sumthing Distribution">Sumthing Else Music Works</a> record label and <a href="/wiki/Sumthing_Distribution" title="Sumthing Distribution">Sumthing Distribution</a>, an independent music label distributor. Sumthing focuses on distributing a fast-growing new genre: video game soundtracks. Its titles include the complete <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_(series)" title="Halo (series)">Halo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a></i> franchises and other well-known Triple-A game soundtracks like <i><a href="/wiki/Gears_of_War" title="Gears of War">Gears of War</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Borderlands_(video_game)" title="Borderlands (video game)">Borderlands</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rodgers focused on many soundtrack projects, film and video games alike. Among them were: <i><a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_2" title="Rush Hour 2">Rush Hour 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Snow_Dogs" title="Snow Dogs">Snow Dogs</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Semi-Pro" title="Semi-Pro">Semi-Pro</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">Will Ferrell</a>, who co-wrote the title song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_Me_Sexy" title="Love Me Sexy">Love Me Sexy</a>" with Rodgers. In 2002–03 he co-produced <i><a href="/wiki/Astronaut_(Duran_Duran_album)" title="Astronaut (Duran Duran album)">Astronaut</a></i>, with the original five members of <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>.</p>

<p>Also in 2002, Rodgers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>'s compilation CD <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Hot_and_Riot" title="Red Hot and Riot">Red Hot and Riot</a></i>. The CD, a tribute to the music and positive social message of afropop pioneer, <a href="/wiki/Fela_Kuti" title="Fela Kuti">Fela Kuti</a>, featured Rodgers on remakes of Kuti songs "Water No Get Enemy" and "Zombie (Part Two)". Rodgers appeared on "Water No Get Enemy" alongside influential hip-hop and R&amp;B artists, <a href="/wiki/D%27Angelo" title="D'Angelo">D'Angelo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macy_Gray" title="Macy Gray">Macy Gray</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Soultronics" title="The Soultronics">The Soultronics</a>, and on "Zombie (Part Two)" with famous jazz trumpeter <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hargrove" title="Roy Hargrove">Roy Hargrove</a>. All proceeds of the CD were donated to charities working towards greater AIDS awareness.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> prompted Rodgers to create the <a href="/wiki/We_Are_Family_Foundation" title="We Are Family Foundation">We Are Family Foundation</a> (WAFF) to help promote the healing process. To begin, he organized a re-recording of the song he and Edwards wrote for <a href="/wiki/Sister_Sledge" title="Sister Sledge">Sister Sledge</a> called "<a href="/wiki/We_Are_Family_(song)" title="We Are Family (song)">We Are Family</a>" with more than 200 musicians, celebrities, and personalities. Director <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a> filmed the "We Are Family" music video and director <a href="/wiki/Danny_Schechter" title="Danny Schechter">Danny Schechter</a> filmed a documentary depicting the recording sessions called <i>The Making and Meaning of We Are Family</i>. The film was chosen as a <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a> Special Selection in 2002. Rodgers then produced another "We Are Family" music video involving more than 100 beloved children's television characters. The children's music video airs as a public service announcement on <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> stations promoting a common humanity and celebrating the vision of a global family.</p>
<p>Rodgers received the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NARAS" title="NARAS">NARAS</a>) NY Chapter's Governor's Lifetime Achievement Award and the Heroes Award. On September 19, 2005, he was honored at the Dance Music Hall of Fame in New York when he was inducted for his many outstanding achievements as a producer, along with former bandmate Bernard Edwards.</p>
<p>Chic has been nominated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_%26_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock &amp; Roll Hall of Fame">Rock &amp; Roll Hall of Fame</a> 11 times – 2003, 2006, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2011, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016 and 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Rodgers served as co-musical director for the tribute concert to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ahmet_Erteg%C3%BCn" title="Ahmet Ertegün">Ahmet Ertegün</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Montreux_Jazz_Festival" title="Montreux Jazz Festival">Montreux Jazz Festival</a> in the summer of 2006. The concert included performances by Chic, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Plant" title="Robert Plant">Robert Plant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Winwood" title="Steve Winwood">Steve Winwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Nicks" title="Stevie Nicks">Stevie Nicks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Rock" title="Kid Rock">Kid Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_E._King" title="Ben E. King">Ben E. King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chaka_Khan" title="Chaka Khan">Chaka Khan</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Duke" title="George Duke">George Duke</a> (co-music director), <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Nutini" title="Paolo Nutini">Paolo Nutini</a> and many other artists who were signed to Ertegün's Atlantic Records. A PBS documentary, <i>Atlantic Records: The House That Ahmet Built</i>, uses footage from this show, as it was one of the last times Ertegün would be captured on video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhino_Records" title="Rhino Records">Rhino Records</a> released a four-CD <a href="/wiki/Box_set" title="Box set">box set</a>, <i>Nile Rodgers Presents The Chic Organization, Volume 1: Savoir Faire</i>, which for the first time collected tracks from all of the acts produced by The Chic Organization up to their original break-up in 1983. Rodgers provided sleevenotes for the set, which was also reissued in 2013.</p>
<p>Rodgers' critically acclaimed autobiography entitled <i>Le Freak: An Upside Down Story of Family, Disco, and Destiny</i> was published by <a href="/wiki/Spiegel_%26_Grau" title="Spiegel &amp; Grau">Spiegel &amp; Grau</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Random_House" title="Random House">Random House</a> imprint, in late 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It was named one of the top 10 of the <i>25 Greatest Rock Memoirs of All Time</i> by <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> "This is a rich, warm tale of a fascinating life in the golden age of New York – and pop."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> – <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a>.</p>
<p>He has additionally stayed active in music since the reunion of The Chic Organization by touring as <a href="/wiki/Chic_(band)" title="Chic (band)">CHIC</a> ft. Nile Rodgers and producing live events. In recent years, he has continued to collaborate with an array of musicians including his 2014 three-time <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy</a> Award winning collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disclosure_(band)" title="Disclosure (band)">Disclosure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sam_Smith_(singer)" title="Sam Smith (singer)">Sam Smith</a> among many others.</p>
<p>In October 2011, Rodgers worked with singer <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a> in New York on a song entitled "Shady" for Lambert's second album, <i>Trespassing</i>. Both Rodgers and Lambert tweeted enthusiastically about the collaboration, and Rodgers also mentioned it on his blog <i>Planet C</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2012, Rodgers announced that he was collaborating with electronic band <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a> for their latest album, "teasing out their R&amp;B influences".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> During a live interview for the UMFTV at the 2013 Ultra Music Festival in Miami, Rodgers confirmed that he was working with Daft Punk,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> as well as recording multiple tracks with <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a>, and mentioned work with Chase &amp; Status and <a href="/wiki/Felix_da_Housecat" title="Felix da Housecat">Felix da Housecat</a>.</p>
<p>On March 31, 2013 <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a> broadcast a documentary about his music life, <i>Nile Rodgers: The Hitmaker</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> starting from the point that Chic was formed until the present, including stories about his involvement in producing numerous hits and albums for some of the world's best artist and bands.</p>
<p>True to his word, Rodgers performed on <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a>'s chart-topping album <i><a href="/wiki/Random_Access_Memories" title="Random Access Memories">Random Access Memories</a></i>. Rodgers co-wrote and played guitar on three tracks: "<a href="/wiki/Give_Life_Back_to_Music" title="Give Life Back to Music">Give Life Back to Music</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Lose_Yourself_to_Dance" title="Lose Yourself to Dance">Lose Yourself to Dance</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Get_Lucky_(Daft_Punk_song)" title="Get Lucky (Daft Punk song)">Get Lucky</a>". On April 21, 2013 "Get Lucky" entered the official <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> at No. 3, despite having been available for little more than 24 hours, and rose to No. 1 on April 28, 2013. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Official_Charts_Company" title="Official Charts Company">Official Charts Company</a>, Rodgers said, "I've had big records and Number 1s; I have had records that were Number 1 in the United States but nowhere else… I've had records which have done well [in the UK], but not in the States. But to have this ubiquitous record, that is a hit everywhere… It's amazing to me! I'm out on the road and I can hear it wherever I go. I'm flabbergasted!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodgers declined to perform "Get Lucky" live until performing it live with Daft Punk,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> but at Chic's live shows in 2013, the record would usually be played immediately after the group's set, with Rodgers remaining on stage to lead the crowd in clapping and singing along. After Rodgers performed the song with <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a> in January 2014, <a href="/wiki/Get_Lucky_(Daft_Punk_song)" title="Get Lucky (Daft Punk song)">"Get Lucky"</a> was added to the setlist for Chic's 2014 live dates.</p>

<p>On June 28, 2013 CHIC ft. Nile Rodgers' performance at the Glastonbury Festival in the UK was aired live on BBC. The performance was given a five star review by <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a> with the headline, "The West Holts stage saw some of the greatest pop music ever made, expertly played by a ferociously well-drilled band<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> ". <a href="/wiki/Noel_Gallagher" title="Noel Gallagher">Noel Gallagher</a> from <a href="/wiki/Oasis_(band)" title="Oasis (band)">Oasis</a> also commented, "My favourite act at this year's Glastonbury, when I went, was not the Rolling Stones, as great as they were; was not the Arctic Monkeys, as good as they were; was not Disclosure, as good as they were; but it was Chic. They were fucking mega. Absolutely out of this world."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2013, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhino_Records" title="Rhino Records">Rhino Records</a> released <i>Nile Rodgers Presents The Chic Organization</i> <a href="/wiki/Up_All_Night_(The_Chic_Organization_album)" title="Up All Night (The Chic Organization album)"><i>Up All Night</i></a> <i>(The Greatest Hits)</i>, a compilation album featuring songs written, played or produced by Rodgers and Bernard Edwards for Chic and various artists including Diana Ross, Sister Sledge, Carly Simon, Debbie Harry, Johnny Mathis, Sheila &amp; B. Devotion and Norma Jean Right. <i>Up All Night</i> reached No. 2 on the UK Compilation Album Chart for the week ending July 13, 2013. In October 2013, Rhino released <i>Nile Rodgers Presents</i> <i>The Chic Organization</i> <a href="/wiki/Up_All_Night_(The_Chic_Organization_album)" title="Up All Night (The Chic Organization album)"><i>Up All Night</i></a> <i>(The Greatest Hits Disco Edition)</i> that includes a medley taken from CHIC ft. Nile Rodgers' live performance at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glastonbury</a> Festival.</p>
<p>In August 2013, the track "Mandou Bem" was released by the Brazilian band <a href="/wiki/Jota_Quest" title="Jota Quest">Jota Quest</a> with the participation of Nile Rodgers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodgers co-produced and played guitar on <a href="/wiki/Tensnake" title="Tensnake">Tensnake</a>'s track "Love Sublime" featuring Nile Rodgers and <a href="/wiki/Fiora_(musician)" title="Fiora (musician)">Fiora</a>.</p>
<p>On October 10, 2013, Nile Rodgers and Bernard Edwards were nominated to the <a href="/wiki/Songwriters_Hall_of_Fame" title="Songwriters Hall of Fame">Songwriters Hall of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>CHIC ft. Nile Rodgers, opened <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i> live show on November 2, 2013 for Disco week. They performed a medley of hits including "Le Freak", "He's The Greatest Dancer" and "Good Times".</p>
<p>On January 26, 2014 Rodgers performed <a href="/wiki/Get_Lucky_(Daft_Punk_song)" title="Get Lucky (Daft Punk song)">"Get Lucky"</a> with <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a> on the Grammy Awards, along with <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, with a medley including elements of Chic's "<a href="/wiki/Le_Freak" title="Le Freak">Le Freak</a>" and Wonder's "<a href="/wiki/Another_Star" title="Another Star">Another Star</a>". Rodgers won three Grammy Awards for his work with Daft Punk and <i><a href="/wiki/Random_Access_Memories" title="Random Access Memories">Random Access Memories</a></i> including Best Pop Duo/Group Performance, Record of the Year and Album of the Year.</p>
<p>In March 2014, Rolling Stone named Nile Rodgers one of the 50 Most Important People in EDM,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> stating, "The full scope of Nile Rodgers' career is still hard to fathom, and it's not just ongoing, it's in overdrive."</p>
<p>On May 8, 2014 "The Nile Rodgers Global Creator's Award" was presented to Rodgers by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Williams_(songwriter)" title="Paul Williams (songwriter)">Paul Williams</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Music_Week" title="Canadian Music Week">Canadian Music Week</a> Festival in Toronto. The Award was originally called "The Global Creator's Award" and it was renamed in 2014 in honor of Rodgers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 21, 2014, the IMS (International Music Summit) Legends Award was presented to Rodgers in Ibiza by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simon_LeBon" title="Simon LeBon">Simon LeBon</a>. Rodgers said upon receiving the award: "I am extremely honored to follow <a href="/wiki/Pete_Tong" title="Pete Tong">Pete Tong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Cox" title="Carl Cox">Carl Cox</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatboy_Slim" title="Fatboy Slim">Fatboy Slim</a> as the fourth recipient of the IMS Legends Award."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 22, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Marr" title="Johnny Marr">Johnny Marr</a> presented Rodgers with the Ivor Novello Awards' "Special International Award". "You hear his heart with his right hand and his soul with his left." Marr remarked upon giving Nile the award. "I hope you feel loved now because you are. You showed what a guitar player could be and what music could be, and always carried yourself with such dignity, grace and positivity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 4, 2014, CHIC ft. Nile Rodgers headlined the 2014 Essence Festival curated by <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>. Special guests performing with Chic during a segment of the show that highlighted Chic's songwriting and production work for other artists, were <a href="/wiki/Kathy_Sledge" title="Kathy Sledge">Kathy Sledge</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sister_Sledge" title="Sister Sledge">Sister Sledge</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/We_Are_Family_(song)" title="We Are Family (song)">We Are Family</a>", <a href="/wiki/Janelle_Mon%C3%A1e" title="Janelle Monáe">Janelle Monáe</a> for Sister Sledge's "<a href="/wiki/He%27s_the_Greatest_Dancer" title="He's the Greatest Dancer">He's the Greatest Dancer</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a> for <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Dance_(David_Bowie_song)" title="Let's Dance (David Bowie song)">Let's Dance</a>".</p>
<p>Rodgers' solo single "Do What You Wanna Do (<a href="/wiki/International_Music_Summit" title="International Music Summit">IMS</a> Anthem)" was released on August 10, 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> to benefit his We Are Family Foundation.</p>
<p>CHIC ft. Nile Rodgers headlined Bestival on September 7, 2014 on the Isle of Wight, UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Rodgers told Bestival founder, Rob da Bank, he and CHIC would headline if he built the world's largest disco ball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_Book_of_World_Records" title="Guinness Book of World Records">Guinness Book of World Records</a> verified Bestival's disco ball as the largest ever built at 10.33 meters in diameter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> nominated CHIC for the 9th time with their 2015 nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 16, 2014, The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Grammys" title="The Grammys">GRAMMY's</a> announced that CHIC's hit single "Le Freak" would be inducted into The GRAMMY Hall of Fame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nile Rodgers was the 2015 Honoree of the Producers and Engineers Wing of the Recording Academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In February 2015, he won the <a href="/wiki/Goldene_Kamera" title="Goldene Kamera">Goldene Kamera</a> award for musical lifetime achievement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2015, it was announced that Nile Rodgers was working with <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a> for her new Christmas album. It was also announced that Rodgers and <a href="/wiki/Grace_Jones" title="Grace Jones">Grace Jones</a> would be supporting acts for Minogue at the <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">British Summer Time</a> on July 21, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 20, 2015, Rodgers released <a href="/wiki/Chic_(band)" title="Chic (band)">CHIC</a>'s first single in more than 23 years, "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Be_There_(Chic_song)" title="I'll Be There (Chic song)">I'll Be There</a>", with a live streamed concert from The Roundhouse in London, UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The song, created by Rodgers from original CHIC outtake tapes from the 70s, features original band members', the late bassist <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Edwards" title="Bernard Edwards">Bernard Edwards</a> and drummer <a href="/wiki/Tony_Thompson_(drummer)" title="Tony Thompson (drummer)">Tony Thompson</a>, and vocalists Alfa Anderson and Luci Martin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The music video was filmed by fashion photographers/filmmakers, <a href="/wiki/Inez_and_Vinoodh" title="Inez and Vinoodh">Inez and Vinoodh</a>, and stars supermodel <a href="/wiki/Karlie_Kloss" title="Karlie Kloss">Karlie Kloss</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The song went to #1 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Dance/Club_Play_Chart" title="Billboard Dance/Club Play Chart">Billboard's Dance Music Chart</a>, June 20, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015 pretended to be a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Busker" title="Busker">busker</a> on London's <a href="/wiki/South_Bank" title="South Bank">South Bank</a>. Rodgers admitted that he 'Didn't draw much of a crowd until I started playing dance music."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 4 and 5, 2015, Rodgers launched FOLD Festival (FreakOut! Let's Dance). A multi-day event with a diverse line-up of artists including <a href="/wiki/Beck" title="Beck">Beck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaka_Kahn" title="Chaka Kahn">Chaka Kahn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keith_Urban" title="Keith Urban">Keith Urban</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janelle_Mon%C3%A1e" title="Janelle Monáe">Janelle Monáe</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ginger_Minj" title="Ginger Minj">Ginger Minj</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CHIC_(band)" title="CHIC (band)">CHIC</a> and many others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Rodgers announced this event is to now take place on a yearly basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodgers was presented with the 2015 BMI Icon Award at the BMI R&amp;B / Hip Hop Awards on August 28, 2015 in Los Angeles, CA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodgers collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> to remake CHIC's hit, "I Want Your Love" for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Ford" title="Tom Ford">Tom Ford</a>'s Spring/Summer 2016 collection video in lieu of a fashion show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodgers and <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Edwards" title="Bernard Edwards">Bernard Edwards</a> were inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Songwriters_Hall_of_Fame" title="Songwriters Hall of Fame">Songwriters Hall of Fame</a> on June 9, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodgers was named as 2016's main BBC Music Day Ambassador.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Media_Centre_13_May_2016_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Media_Centre_13_May_2016-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Front_Row_2_June_2016_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Front_Row_2_June_2016-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodgers was presented with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_GQ" title="British GQ">British GQ</a> Magazine's ICON Of The Year Award on September 6, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a> feat. Nile Rodgers song "<a href="/wiki/Telepathy_(Christina_Aguilera_song)" title="Telepathy (Christina Aguilera song)">Telepathy</a>" goes number one on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> Dance Charts, November 12, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> The song was written for <a href="/wiki/The_Get_Down" title="The Get Down">The Get Down</a> on <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a>.</p>
<p>Rodgers will be inducted into the 2017 class of the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> for Musical Excellence. "It's sort of bittersweet," says Rodgers. "I'm quite flattered that they believed that I was worthy, but my band Chic didn't win. They plucked me out of the band and said, 'You're better than Chic.' That's wacky to me ... I am flattered and I think it's cool, but I feel like somebody put me in the lifeboat and told my family they can't get in." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_.22Hitmaker.22">The "Hitmaker"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title='Edit section: The "Hitmaker"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rodgers plays a 1960 Fender <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stratocaster" title="Stratocaster">Stratocaster</a> with a 1959 neck, affectionately nicknamed "The Hitmaker", which he acquired as a trade-in at a small shop in <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach,_Florida" title="Miami Beach, Florida">Miami Beach, Florida</a>. Exceptionally light, it has a maple fingerboard and bears a well-worn white finish. Rodgers claims that it does not sound like any other Stratocaster in the world. In his autobiography, <i>Le Freak, An Upside Down Story of Family, Disco and Destiny</i>, Rodgers stated:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>One day, following a gig in Miami, Nard got me to trade in my prized jazz guitar, a hollow-bodied Gibson Barney Kessel, for a sleek solid-bodied Fender Stratocaster, the six-string equivalent of trading in a Range Rover for a Porsche. The local act that opened for us played on our equipment, and their guitar player sounded better than I did on my own amplifier. Nard convinced me it was the guitar that made the difference. His soul-man makeover plan was working. He came to my room to admire my new guitar and showed me the style the other guy had played on my amp. He fingered the chords with his left hand, and his right hand would continuously play sixteen notes to the bar while accenting the main parts of the rhythm. He called it "chucking." Bernard used to be a guitar player before he switched to bass, and one lesson was all I needed. For the next few nights straight, while my roommate pursued all manner of trysts, I was having a love affair in the bathroom with my new ax. In just a few days, I'd emerge as a chucking funk guitarist who knew more jazz chord inversions than most of my R&amp;B counterparts.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Fender Custom Shop introduced a limited edition Nile Rodgers Hitmaker Stratocaster guitar, a recreation of Rodgers' own guitar, in January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nile_Rodgers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2011 Rodgers revealed on his website that he had aggressive prostate cancer, which had been diagnosed in October 2010. As a result, he started a cancer blog called "Walking on Planet C" detailing his status and upcoming projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 29, 2013, Rodgers posted on Twitter that he had been given the all-clear regarding his cancer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In November 2013, he gave an interview to <i><a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News">BBC News</a></i> talking about Daft Punk, Madonna, Diana Ross and beating cancer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
