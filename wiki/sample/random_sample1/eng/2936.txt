<p><i><b>Najas</b></i>, the <b>water-nymphs</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> or <b>naiads</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aquatic_plant" title="Aquatic plant">aquatic plants</a>. It is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_(distribution)" title="Cosmopolitan (distribution)">cosmopolitan</a> in distribution, first described for modern science by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linnaeus" title="Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a> in 1753. Until 1997, it was rarely placed in the <a href="/wiki/Hydrocharitaceae" title="Hydrocharitaceae">Hydrocharitaceae</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tanaka_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tanaka-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-les_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-les-3">[3]</a></sup> and was often taken as constituting (by itself) the family <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Najadaceae" title="Najadaceae">Najadaceae</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/APG_II_system" title="APG II system">APG II system</a>, of 2003 (unchanged from the <a href="/wiki/APG_system" title="APG system">APG system</a>, of 1998), places the genus in family <a href="/wiki/Hydrocharitaceae" title="Hydrocharitaceae">Hydrocharitaceae</a>, in the order <a href="/wiki/Alismatales" title="Alismatales">Alismatales</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monocots" title="Monocots">monocots</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apg2003_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-apg2003-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
