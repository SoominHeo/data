<p><b>Nadzeya Ostapchuk</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be" xml:lang="be">Надзея Мікалаеўна Астапчук</span>, <i>Nadzeya Mikalayeuna Astapchuk</i>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Надежда Остапчук</span>, <i>Nadezhda Ostapchuk;</i> born October 28, 1980)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAAFbio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAAFbio-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarusian</a> <a href="/wiki/Shot_put" title="Shot put">shot putter</a>. She briefly became the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_shot_put" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's shot put">Olympic Champion</a> in 2012, but was subsequently stripped of the title for failing a drug test and the <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medal</a> was awarded to <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> shot putter <a href="/wiki/Valerie_Adams" title="Valerie Adams">Valerie Adams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-2">[2]</a></sup> She was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="World Championships in Athletics">World Champion</a> in 2005, but in March 2013, the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_of_Athletics_Federations" title="International Association of Athletics Federations">IAAF</a> reported that her drug test sample from that event had been retested and found to be positive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ostapchuk was <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze medallist</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Beijing_Olympics" title="2008 Beijing Olympics">2008 Beijing Olympics</a> (but was subsequently disqualified from that too, because of a doping violation), and was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="IAAF World Indoor Championships">World Indoor</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Athletics_Championships" title="European Athletics Championships">European Champion</a> in 2010. Her overall personal best of 21.70 m that year is the fourth best women's all-time distance indoors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She is a four-time runner-up at the World Indoor Championships and a three-time World Championships <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medallist</a> outdoors. She holds the Championship record for the former event, with her winning throw of 20.85 m in 2010. In continental competition, she was the <a href="/wiki/2005_European_Athletics_Indoor_Championships" title="2005 European Athletics Indoor Championships">2005 European Indoor</a> champion and came second at the <a href="/wiki/2006_European_Athletics_Championships" title="2006 European Athletics Championships">2006 European Athletics Championships</a>. In addition to her withdrawn medals in Beijing and London, she was fourth at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_Athens_Olympics" title="2004 Athens Olympics">2004 Athens Olympics</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadzeya_Ostapchuk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Stolin" title="Stolin">Stolin</a>, she was initially interested in <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> but the lack of a local team left her unable to pursue the sport further. Ostapchuk instead entered throwing events and her first international title came at the age of seventeen, as she won the shot put at the <a href="/wiki/1998_World_Junior_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1998 World Junior Championships in Athletics">1998 World Junior Championships in Athletics</a>. She was dominant at the younger levels and won at the <a href="/wiki/1999_European_Athletics_Junior_Championships" title="1999 European Athletics Junior Championships">1999 European Athletics Junior Championships</a> and then the <a href="/wiki/2001_European_Athletics_U23_Championships" title="2001 European Athletics U23 Championships">2001 European Athletics U23 Championships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She rose to top international level in the early 2000s, winning two consecutive <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medals</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="IAAF World Indoor Championships">IAAF World Indoor Championships</a> and also finished as runner-up at the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2003 World Championships in Athletics">2003 World Championships in Athletics</a>. She finished fourth at her first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Olympics" title="Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_shot_put" title="Athletics at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Women's shot put">2004</a>, but reached the peak of her discipline the following year, winning at the <a href="/wiki/2005_European_Athletics_Indoor_Championships" title="2005 European Athletics Indoor Championships">2005 European Athletics Indoor Championships</a> and then becoming <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_World_Championships_in_Athletics_-_Women%27s_Shot_Put" title="2005 World Championships in Athletics - Women's Shot Put">2005 World Champion</a>. Her career became overshadowed by <a href="/wiki/Valerie_Adams" title="Valerie Adams">Valerie Adams</a>, who succeeded her as World Champion in 2007, although she continued to win major medals, including silvers at the World Indoor and Outdoor Championships and a <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze medal</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Beijing_Olympics" title="2008 Beijing Olympics">2008 Beijing Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>Her personal best is 21.70 m thrown at the Belarusian championships in 2010, making her the third best indoor thrower on the all-time lists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She scored her first world indoor title at the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="2010 IAAF World Indoor Championships">2010 IAAF World Indoor Championships</a>, throwing a championship record of 20.85 m. She won further honours at the <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Cup_Winter_Throwing" title="2010 European Cup Winter Throwing">2010 European Cup Winter Throwing</a> meeting, easily winning gold ahead of compatriot <a href="/wiki/Natallia_Mikhnevich" title="Natallia Mikhnevich">Natallia Mikhnevich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She defeated the reigning champion, <a href="/wiki/Natallia_Mikhnevich" title="Natallia Mikhnevich">Natallia Mikhnevich</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Athletics_Championships" title="2010 European Athletics Championships">2010 European Athletics Championships</a> to claim her first outdoor European Championship. She won all six of the <a href="/wiki/IAAF_Diamond_League" title="IAAF Diamond League">IAAF Diamond League</a> meetings that she competed in that year, becoming the inaugural women's shot put trophy winner, and suffered just one defeat in the entirety of 2010 – a runner-up placing behind Valerie Adams at the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_Continental_Cup" title="2010 IAAF Continental Cup">2010 IAAF Continental Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Knee_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Knee-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>She decided to miss the indoor section of 2011 and instead struck a balance between training and resting her left knee. She had endured ongoing pain in the joint but remarked "I didn’t want to do something radical and have surgery as there is no guarantee of a speedy recovery".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Knee_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Knee-8">[8]</a></sup> Her first competition of 2012 came at the Belarusian indoor championships and she demonstrated her form with a world-leading mark of 20.70 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She had a best throw of 20.42 m at the <a href="/wiki/2012_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="2012 IAAF World Indoor Championships">2012 IAAF World Indoor Championships</a> and this brought her the silver medal behind Adams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Turning to the outdoor season, she won at the <a href="/wiki/2012_European_Cup_Winter_Throwing" title="2012 European Cup Winter Throwing">2012 European Cup Winter Throwing</a> and reached 20.53 m to win the first leg of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_Diamond_League" title="2012 Diamond League">2012 Diamond League</a> in Doha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She managed only third at the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Gala" title="Golden Gala">Golden Gala</a> at the end of May, but threw a Belarusian outdoor record of 21.13 m in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a> two weeks later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> She threw further in July, having marks of 21.32 m, then 21.39 m in <a href="/wiki/Grodno" title="Grodno">Grodno</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Immediately prior to the Olympics, she had a world-leading throw of 21.58 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Doping">Doping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadzeya_Ostapchuk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Doping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ostapchuk won gold at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in London, the day after which the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Belarus" title="President of Belarus">President of Belarus</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alyaksandr_Lukashenka" title="Alyaksandr Lukashenka">Alyaksandr Lukashenka</a>, awarded her the Third-class Order of the Fatherland in recognition of her “high professionalism, outstanding sports achievements and victory at the 30th Summer Olympic Games”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> However, she was later disqualified from the medal for testing positive for a banned substance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianAug13_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianAug13-17">[17]</a></sup> She was tested twice, once on 5 August and again the following day after she had finished first. The IOC said both samples indicated the presence of the anabolic agent <a href="/wiki/Metenolone" title="Metenolone">metenolone</a>. New Zealand's <a href="/wiki/Valerie_Adams" title="Valerie Adams">Valerie Adams</a> was subsequently awarded the gold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianAug13_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianAug13-17">[17]</a></sup> Russia's <a href="/wiki/Yevgeniya_Kolodko" title="Yevgeniya Kolodko">Yevgeniya Kolodko</a> was moved up to win the silver medal, and China's <a href="/wiki/Gong_Lijiao" title="Gong Lijiao">Gong Lijiao</a> moved to take the bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> She received a one-year ban after her coach, Alexander Yefimov, admitted to spiking her coffee with metenolone without her knowledge after being worried about her performance, stating that he thought the drug would be clear before the tests were administered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2013, Ostapchuk was stripped of her 2005 World Championship title after samples from the 2005 event were retested by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAAF" title="IAAF">IAAF</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2014 IAAF announced that she was sanctioned to four years ineligibility, ending 14 August 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and that her results from 13 August 2005 to 12 August 2007 and since 5 August 2012 were annulled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 January 2017 it was announced that because of a doping violation she had been disqualified from the 2008 Olympic Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
