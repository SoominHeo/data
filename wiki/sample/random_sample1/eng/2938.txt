<p><b>Naselle</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> (CDP) in <a href="/wiki/Pacific_County,_Washington" title="Pacific County, Washington">Pacific County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The population was 419 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>. Though very near <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a>'s estuary, the valley's Naselle River flows west into nearby <a href="/wiki/Willapa_Bay" title="Willapa Bay">Willapa Bay</a> and then into the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. Close about the town lie the evergreen-covered <a href="/wiki/Willapa_Hills" title="Willapa Hills">Willapa Hills</a>. The river's name has been spelled Nasel and Nasal. An early settler along the river called it the Kenebec. The name comes from the Nisal Indians, a <a href="/wiki/Chinookan_peoples" title="Chinookan peoples">Chinookan</a> tribe formerly residing on the river.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naselle,_Washington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">[1]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 377 people, 160 households, and 110 families residing in the CDP. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 164.6 people per square mile (63.6/km²). There were 184 housing units at an average density of 80.3/sq mi (31.0/km²). The racial makeup of the CDP was 92.57% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.27% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 2.12% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.53% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, and 4.51% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 0.53% of the population. 33.2% were of <a href="/wiki/Finns" title="Finns">Finnish</a>, 10.0% <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, 7.9% <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> and 7.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_states" title="United states">American</a> ancestry according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Census_2000" title="Census 2000">Census 2000</a>.</p>
<p>There were 160 households out of which 31.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 59.4% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 6.3% had a female householder with no husband present, and 31.3% were non-families. 25.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 17.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.36 and the average family size was 2.85.</p>
<p>In the CDP the population was spread out with 23.3% under the age of 18, 5.0% from 18 to 24, 22.5% from 25 to 44, 26.5% from 45 to 64, and 22.5% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 44 years. For every 100 females there were 95.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 90.1 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the CDP was $35,769, and the median income for a family was $40,250. Males had a median income of $36,875 versus $18,125 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the CDP was $17,714. About 4.0% of families and 4.7% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 7.5% of those under age 18 and 2.1% of those age 65 or over.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_culture">History and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naselle,_Washington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The community first flourished as a logging town, and logging remains the dominant private industry. Settled primarily by <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavian</a> immigrants, the community has maintained this cultural heritage despite a declining percentage of traditional family names. Since 1982, Naselle has hosted a "Finnish-American Folk Festival" every other year, and in 2006 co-hosted, with the nearby city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astoria,_OR" title="Astoria, OR">Astoria, OR</a>, the national festival FinnFest USA.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jennifer_L._Holm" title="Jennifer L. Holm">Jennifer L. Holm</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newbery_Honor" title="Newbery Honor">Newbery Honor</a>-winning novel <i>Our Only May Amelia</i> is set in pioneer Naselle, then spelled "Nasel", and is based on Holm family documents of life as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finnish-American" title="Finnish-American">Finnish-American</a> frontier family.</p>
<p>Past and present residents of note include <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Wirkkala" title="Oscar Wirkkala">Oscar Wirkkala</a>, logger and inventor; <a href="/wiki/Wilho_Saari" title="Wilho Saari">Wilho Saari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kantele" title="Kantele">kantele</a> performer and teacher and <a href="/wiki/Rex_Ziak" title="Rex Ziak">Rex Ziak</a> historian.</p>
