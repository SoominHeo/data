<p><b>Imratul Najwa Binti Abdul Latif</b> (born May 23, 1995), better known as <b>Najwa Latif</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Malaysians" title="Malaysians">Malaysian</a> singer and <a href="/wiki/Teen_idol" title="Teen idol">teen idol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-five_minutes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-five_minutes-2">[2]</a></sup> She became popular after her first song, "Cinta Muka Buku" (Facebook Love).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Najwa_Latif&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Najwa learned how to play guitar in 2009 when she was 14 years old, and afterwards began writing music for songs with her sister Ika, who wrote the lyrics. In September 2010, Najwa began uploading cover songs to YouTube; the first was a cover of <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Baby_(Justin_Bieber_song)" title="Baby (Justin Bieber song)">Baby</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-narrecords_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-narrecords-3">[3]</a></sup> In March 2011, she was signed to NAR Records and her first official single, "Cinta Muka Buku", was released in June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A pre-release video of the song was uploaded to YouTube. According to Najwa, this single is very meaningful to her, as it marked her first steps into the Malaysian music industry as well as to finally fulfill her dream and ambition of becoming a singer. It was also a gift for her fans who had long-awaited her single and showed their support towards her long before she became an established singer. The official music video of "Cinta Muka Buku" received more than 6 million views in just over six months, and is the most viewed music video in Malaysia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-narrecords_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-narrecords-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2013, Chinese idol singer <a href="/wiki/Wang_Leehom" title="Wang Leehom">Wang Leehom</a> (王力宏) invited Najwa Latif to be a special guest at his concert at <a href="/wiki/Genting_Highlands" title="Genting Highlands">Genting Highlands</a> in Malaysia. They performed the song "All the Things You Don't Know"(你不知道的事).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Najwa_Latif&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Najwa Latif is the third of four siblings <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and was born in Batu Pahat, Johor, Malaysia. All members of her family are musically talented.</p>
<p>Najwa also did well academically, and achieved 7A 1B in <a href="/wiki/Penilaian_Menengah_Rendah" title="Penilaian Menengah Rendah">Penilaian Menengah Rendah</a> (PMR) when she was 15 years old. She received her secondary education at Sekolah Menengah Kebangsaan (P) in Temenggong Ibrahim, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batu_Pahat" title="Batu Pahat">Batu Pahat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johor" title="Johor">Johor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sijil_Pelajaran_Malaysia" title="Sijil Pelajaran Malaysia">Sijil Pelajaran Malaysia</a> (SPM), where she achieved 4A. She is currently studying the Bachelor of English course at <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/UNITAR" title="UNITAR">UNITAR International University</a>, majoring in the Teaching of English as a Second Language (TESL).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-five_minutes_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-five_minutes-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
