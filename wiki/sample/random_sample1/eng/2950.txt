<p><b>Nakatsu</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">中津市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nakatsu-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> on the northern border of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cita_Prefecture" title="Ōita Prefecture">Ōita Prefecture</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a>, Japan. The city is on the border with <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Prefecture" title="Fukuoka Prefecture">Fukuoka Prefecture</a>. Nakatsu was founded on April 20, 1929.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>As of 2008, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 84,179 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 171 people per km². The total area is 491.09 km².<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakatsu,_%C5%8Cita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nakatsu is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_castles" title="Japanese castles">castle</a> town centered on Nakatsu Castle, which is open to visitors. The modernised interior comprises a museum with <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> armor, old maps and information about <a href="/wiki/Rangaku" title="Rangaku">Rangaku</a> (Dutch studies), for which Nakatsu was an important center in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edo_era" title="Edo era">Edo era</a>. There are views from the top of the castle keep.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Visitors can see the residence of the most celebrated member of the Okudaira clan of Nakatsu, <a href="/wiki/Fukuzawa_Yukichi" title="Fukuzawa Yukichi">Fukuzawa Yukichi</a>, and the memorial hall there. It is close to the castle and a 15-minute walk from the station.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Nakatsu's Mt. Hachimen was home to the popular music festival, <a href="/wiki/Concert_on_the_Rock" title="Concert on the Rock">Concert on the Rock</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakatsu,_%C5%8Cita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nakatsu is in the northwest corner of Ōita Prefecture, Kyushu. To the east of Nakatsu is <a href="/wiki/Usa,_%C5%8Cita" title="Usa, Ōita">Usa</a> and to the southwest is <a href="/wiki/Hita,_%C5%8Cita" title="Hita, Ōita">Hita</a>. Nakatsu touches the border of Fukuoka Prefecture on its west. To the northeast is the <a href="/wiki/Seto_Inland_Sea" title="Seto Inland Sea">Suohnada Sea</a>. The city covers an area of 491.09 square kilometers, 80% of which is mountainous. There is a wide stretch of flat, agricultural land which begins at the mouth of Yamakuni River and extends to Mt. Hiko.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakatsu,_%C5%8Cita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1587: <a href="/wiki/Kuroda_Yoshitaka" title="Kuroda Yoshitaka">Kuroda Yoshitaka</a> (Josui) built Nakatsu Castle as a flatland castle near the Yamaguni River.</li>
<li>April 1925: The village of Ōe was merged with Toyoda to become the town of Nakatsu.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>April 1919: The village of Ogusu and the town of Nakatsu were merged to become the city of Nakatsu.</li>
<li>August 1933: The villages of Tsurui, Ōhata and Josui were merged into Nakatsu.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>April 1951: The village of Miho was merged into Nakatsu.</li>
<li>October 1954: The village of Wada was merged into Nakatsu.</li>
<li>February 1955: The village of Imazu was merged into Nakatsu.</li>
<li>March, 2005: The towns of <a href="/wiki/Hon%27yabakei,_%C5%8Cita" title="Hon'yabakei, Ōita">Hon'yabakei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yabakei,_%C5%8Cita" title="Yabakei, Ōita">Yabakei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yamakuni,_%C5%8Cita" title="Yamakuni, Ōita">Yamakuni</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Sank%C5%8D,_%C5%8Cita" title="Sankō, Ōita">Sankō</a> (all from <a href="/wiki/Shimoge_District,_%C5%8Cita" title="Shimoge District, Ōita">Shimoge District</a>) were all merged into Nakatsu.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
