<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nakauonuma District, Niigata</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Nakauonuma</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">中魚沼郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nakauonuma-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of April 1, 2005, the district has a population of 11,754. The area is now 372.80 km².</p>
<p>The district has one village</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tsunan,_Niigata" title="Tsunan, Niigata">Tsunan</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakauonuma_District,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In 1954, the town of <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkamachi,_Niigata" title="Tōkamachi, Niigata">Tōkamachi</a> gained status after merging with three villages and later gained more land by merging surrounding villages. There were some villages from the northside of the district merged into the city of <a href="/wiki/Ojiya,_Niigata" title="Ojiya, Niigata">Ojiya</a>.</li>
<li>On April 1, 2005 the town of <a href="/wiki/Kawanishi,_Niigata" title="Kawanishi, Niigata">Kawanishi</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Nakasato,_Niigata" title="Nakasato, Niigata">Nakasato</a> merged with the city of <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkamachi,_Niigata" title="Tōkamachi, Niigata">Tōkamachi</a>, leaving the district with the village of <a href="/wiki/Tsunan,_Niigata" title="Tsunan, Niigata">Tsunan</a>.</li>
</ul>
