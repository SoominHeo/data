<p><b>Megumi Nakajima</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">中島 愛</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nakajima Megumi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born June 5, 1989)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doi_top_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-doi_top-2">[2]</a></sup> is a Filipino-Japanese <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting_in_Japan" title="Voice acting in Japan">voice actress</a> and singer, currently represented by the <a href="/wiki/Stardust_Promotion" title="Stardust Promotion">Stardust Promotion</a> talent agency. She was born to a Japanese father and a Filipina mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2003, she participated in the "Self Stardust Promotion" contest and passed, thereby affiliating her to the agency. In 2007, she auditioned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victor_Entertainment" title="Victor Entertainment">Victor Entertainment</a> sanctioned "Victor Vocal &amp; Voice Audition" and was chosen to voice <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranka_Lee" title="Ranka Lee">Ranka Lee</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Macross_Frontier" title="Macross Frontier">Macross Frontier</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Her voice was also sampled to create the Vocaloid named <a href="/wiki/Megpoid" title="Megpoid">Megpoid</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megumi_Nakajima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mamegu_Live_at_LA_(2).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3571" data-file-width="2381" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Mamegu_Live_at_LA_%282%29.jpg/220px-Mamegu_Live_at_LA_%282%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Mamegu_Live_at_LA_%282%29.jpg/330px-Mamegu_Live_at_LA_%282%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Mamegu_Live_at_LA_%282%29.jpg/440px-Mamegu_Live_at_LA_%282%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Nakajima made her debut performance as a professional artist at the "Macross 25th Anniversary Concert" on April 18, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Her first vocal recordings appeared in the compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Macross_Frontier_albums#O.S.T.1_Nyan_FRO." title="List of Macross Frontier albums">Macross Frontier O.S.T.1 Nyan FRO.</a></i> on June 4, 2008. Her first solo live performance was a mini-concert titled "Ranka Lee = Megumi Nakajima Mini Live @ Formo… Not! Venus Fort☆" (ランカ・リー＝中島愛 ミニライブ＠フォルモ…ぢゃないよ！ヴィーナスフォート☆ <i>Ranka Rī = Nakajima Megumi Mini Raibu @ Forumo... Janai yo! Vīnasu Fōto☆</i>) which was performed on June 14, 2008. She made her single debut on June 25, 2008 with the single "<a href="/wiki/List_of_Macross_Frontier_albums#Seikan_Hik.C5.8D" title="List of Macross Frontier albums">Seikan Hikō</a>". Her first major concert was the "Super Dimension Super Live" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">超時空スーパーライブ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Chō Jikū Sūpā Raibu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> concert held on July 27, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nakajima won the award for Best Musical Performance in the third <a href="/wiki/Seiyu_Awards" title="Seiyu Awards">Seiyu Awards</a> held on March 7, 2009 for her performance as Ranka Lee in the song "Seikan Hikō" of <i>Macross F</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nakajima's first single, "Tenshi ni Naritai" was released in 2009 and was the first single to be released outside of her role from <i>Macross F</i>. It was followed up by the single "Nostalgia" two months later. She was the voice of Miku (The Strongest Akikan) and the role of bad girl in the series <i><a href="/wiki/Akikan!" title="Akikan!">Akikan!</a></i>. Nakajima after that, participated in the series <a href="/wiki/Basquash!" title="Basquash!">Basquash!</a>, giving life to the character of Citron that along with Rouge (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomatsu_Haruka" title="Tomatsu Haruka">Tomatsu Haruka</a>) and Violette (<a href="/wiki/Saori_Hayami" title="Saori Hayami">Saori Hayami</a>) formed the pop group Eclipse, this was dissolved at the end of the series; after it, began work on the series <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%A4mpfer" title="Kämpfer">Kämpfer</a></i> as Sakura Kaede<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> (who unlike her previous roles, she fight for the person who love, but still retains its characteristic tenderness), while representing Chiho and Chise Mihara in <i><a href="/wiki/Kobato" title="Kobato">Kobato</a></i>. At the end of 2009, Nakajima voiced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranka_Lee" title="Ranka Lee">Ranka Lee</a> in the first movie of the <i><a href="/wiki/Macross_Frontier" title="Macross Frontier">Macross Frontier</a></i> franchise, <i>Macross Frontier the Movie: The False Songstress</i> (マクロスF 虚空歌姫～イツワリノウタヒメ～, Macross Frontier Gekijō-ban ~Itsuwari no Utahime~?). . In 2010, Nakajima played a Lyra, a celestial spirit that belongs to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucy_Heartfilia" title="Lucy Heartfilia">Lucy Heartfilia</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Fairy_Tail" title="Fairy Tail">Fairy Tail</a></i> series. In February 2011, Nakajima returned to the movie screen to voice <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranka_Lee" title="Ranka Lee">Ranka Lee</a> in the second <i><a href="/wiki/Macross_Frontier" title="Macross Frontier">Macross Frontier</a></i> movie, <i>Macross Frontier the Movie: The Wings of Goodbye</i> (マクロスF 恋離飛翼～サヨナラノツバサ～, Macross Frontier Gekijō-ban ~Sayonara no Tsubasa~?).</p>
<p>On December 2, 2013, Stardust Promotion announced that Nakajima would go on an indefinite hiatus from her music career after the release of her album <i>Thank You</i> and a final concert on March 20, 2014, although she would continue to be active in the voice-acting industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 1, 2016, Nakajima's management announced that she would return from her hiatus to perform the song "Watashi no Sekai", which will be used as the ending theme to the 2017 anime television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C5%ABka_(manga)" title="Fūka (manga)">Fūka</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fuuka_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-fuuka-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
