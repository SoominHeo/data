<p><b>Nakata Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">中田駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nakata-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">JR East</a> <a href="/wiki/Gon%C5%8D_Line" title="Gonō Line">Gonō Line</a> located in the city of <a href="/wiki/Tsugaru,_Aomori" title="Tsugaru, Aomori">Tsugaru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aomori_Prefecture" title="Aomori Prefecture">Aomori Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is 116.9 rail kilometers from the southern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> of the Gonō Line at <a href="/wiki/Higashi-Noshiro_Station" title="Higashi-Noshiro Station">Higashi-Noshiro Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nakata Station has a single <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> serving a single track. There is no station building, only a small weather shelter on the platform. The station is unattended.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nakata Station was opened on November 11, 1956 as a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> station in the former village of <a href="/wiki/Morita,_Aomori" title="Morita, Aomori">Morita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nishitsugaru_District,_Aomori" title="Nishitsugaru District, Aomori">Nishitsugaru District</a>. With the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of JNR on April 1, 1987, the station came under the operational control of JR East.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surrounding_area">Surrounding area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Surrounding area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is surrounded by rice fields, and <a href="/wiki/Mount_Iwaki" title="Mount Iwaki">Mount Iwaki</a> is clearly visible from the platform.</p>
