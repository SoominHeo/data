<p><b>Nakhon Si Thammarat</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">นครศรีธรรมราช</span>, <small>pronounced</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA for Thai and Lao">[náʔkʰɔːn sǐː tʰammarâːt]</a></span>, alternative English transliteration: <b>Nakhon Sri Thammarat</b> from Pali <i>Nagara Sri Dhammaraja</i>) is a city (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thesaban_nakhon" title="Thesaban nakhon">thesaban nakhon</a></i>) in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Thailand" title="Southern Thailand">southern Thailand</a>, capital of the <a href="/wiki/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Province" title="Nakhon Si Thammarat Province">Nakhon Si Thammarat Province</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoe_Mueang_Nakhon_Si_Thammarat" title="Amphoe Mueang Nakhon Si Thammarat">Nakhon Si Thammarat District</a>. It is about 610 km (380 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, on the east coast of the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a>. The city was the administrative center of southern Thailand during most of its history. Originally a coastal city, silting moved the coastline away from the city. The city has a much larger north to south extension than west to east, which dates back to its original location on a flood-save dune. The modern city centre on the train station is north of Old Town. As of 2005, the city had a population of 105,417.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakhon_Si_Thammarat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Thai_honorifics#Si.2FSri" title="Thai honorifics">Thai honorific <i>Sri</i> or <i>Si</i></a> is from <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> <a href="/wiki/Sri" title="Sri">Sri</a>; <span lang="th-Latn" xml:lang="th-Latn"><i>Thamma</i></span>, from <a href="/wiki/Dharma" title="Dharma">Dharma</a>; <span lang="th-Latn" xml:lang="th-Latn"><i>rat</i></span>, from <a href="/wiki/Raja" title="Raja">Raja</a>. <i>Dhammaraja</i> means "righteous ruler", an important <a href="/wiki/Theravada" title="Theravada">Theravada</a> concept.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakhon_Si_Thammarat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Chi_Tu" title="Chi Tu">Chi Tu</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LocationMalayPeninsula.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="115" data-file-width="250" height="115" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e1/LocationMalayPeninsula.png" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LocationMalayPeninsula.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Location of <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It is one of the most ancient cities of Thailand, previously the Kingdom of Ligor, and contains many buildings and ruins of historical significance.</p>
<p>The king of <a href="/wiki/Srivijaya" title="Srivijaya">Srivijaya</a> "had established a foothold on the Malay Peninsular at Ligor" by 775, where he "built various edifices, including a sanctuary dedicated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddha" title="Buddha">Buddha</a> and to the <a href="/wiki/Bodhisattva" title="Bodhisattva">Bodhisattvas</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Padmapani" title="Padmapani">Padmapani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vajrapani" title="Vajrapani">Vajrapani</a>." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coedes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Coedes-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:84–85,91</sup></p>
<p>With the fall of the Siamese capital of <a href="/wiki/Ayutthaya_Kingdom" title="Ayutthaya Kingdom">Ayutthaya</a> in 1767 it regained independence, but returned to its allegiance on the founding of <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>. In the 17th century British, Portuguese, and Dutch merchants set up factories there and carried on an extensive trade. Its origins are not fully known. Most historians recognize the <a href="/wiki/Tambralinga" title="Tambralinga">Tambralinga</a> Kingdom of Chinese records as a precursor of Nakhon Si Thammarat.</p>
<p>The town chronicles of this time are hardly separable from legend, but they do tell of an abandonment and refounding of the town, which would explain the break in history between Tambralinga and Nakhon Si Thammarat.</p>
<p>References to a country named <b>Poling</b> appear in Chinese chronicles from the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang dynasty</a> period down to the early <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a>. Many scholars identify Poling with <b>Maling</b> and <b>Danmaling</b> was one of the member-states of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanfoqi" title="Sanfoqi">Sanfoqi</a> (the Chinese equivalent to <a href="/wiki/Srivijaya" title="Srivijaya">Srivijaya</a>) in the central part of the Malayu Peninsula or today southern Thailand.</p>
<p>Poling may also be equated to the Tambralingarat (Tambralinga State) that appears in Indian sources. By the end of the 12th century, <a href="/wiki/Tambralinga" title="Tambralinga">Tambralinga</a> had become independent of <a href="/wiki/Srivijaya" title="Srivijaya">Srivijaya</a> Kingdom. Its rapid rise to prominence from the 13th century to the beginning of 14th century, <a href="/wiki/Tambralinga" title="Tambralinga">Tambralinga</a> had occupied the entire <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a> and become one of the dominant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South-east_Asian" title="South-east Asian">south-east Asian</a> states. By the end of the 14th century, Tambralinga had become a part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siam" title="Siam">Siam</a> (now Thailand) named Nakhon Si Thammaraj.</p>
<p>At the time of the <a href="/wiki/Sukhothai_Kingdom" title="Sukhothai Kingdom">Sukhothai Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Kingdom" title="Nakhon Si Thammarat Kingdom">Nakhon Si Thammarat Kingdom</a> was already listed as one of the kingdoms under control of the Thai, which it has remained during most of its history. It was usually known as <b>Ligor</b> to European merchants in the 16th century.</p>
<p>During the period of the <a href="/wiki/Taksin#Five_separate_states" title="Taksin">five separate states</a> following the fall of Ayutthaya in 1767, the Prince of Nakhon Si Thammarat made an abortive bid for independence, but was <a href="/wiki/Pardon" title="Pardon">pardoned</a> by <a href="/wiki/Taksin" title="Taksin">Taksin</a> and retired to <a href="/wiki/Thonburi" title="Thonburi">Thonburi</a>.</p>
<p>At the end of the 19th century, the kingdom was finally fully absorbed into Siam by converting it into the <a href="/wiki/Monthon_Nakhon_Si_Thammarat" title="Monthon Nakhon Si Thammarat">Monthon Nakhon Si Thammarat</a>. When the <a href="/wiki/Monthon" title="Monthon">monthon</a> system was abolished in 1932, the town became a provincial capital.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakhon_Si_Thammarat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nakhon Si Thammarat has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest_climate" title="Tropical rainforest climate">tropical rainforest climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Af</i>). Temperatures remain very warm to hot throughout the year. While some rain falls in all months, it is drier in February and March when about 90 millimetres (3.5 in) of rain falls in each month, and wetter in October to December when very heavy rain may fall; November sees 631 millimetres (24.8 in) of rain on average each year.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Points_of_interest">Points of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakhon_Si_Thammarat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Points of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wat_Phra_Mahathat_Woramahawihan"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wat_Phra_Mahathat_Woramahawihan,_Nakhon_Si_Thammarat" title="Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan, Nakhon Si Thammarat">Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakhon_Si_Thammarat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Chedi_Phra_Baromathat.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1700" data-file-width="1100" height="232" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Chedi_Phra_Baromathat.jpg/150px-Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Chedi_Phra_Baromathat.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Chedi_Phra_Baromathat.jpg/225px-Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Chedi_Phra_Baromathat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Chedi_Phra_Baromathat.jpg/300px-Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_Chedi_Phra_Baromathat.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Chedi Phra Baromathat</div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Wat" title="Wat">Wat</a> Phra Mahathat <a href="/wiki/Vihara" title="Vihara">Vihan</a> (Thai วัดพระมหาธาตุวรมหาวิหาร) is the most important temple of Nakhon Si Thammarat and southern Thailand. It was constructed at the time of the founding of the town, and contains a tooth <a href="/wiki/Relic" title="Relic">relic</a> of Buddha. The 78 m high <a href="/wiki/Stupa" title="Stupa">chedi</a> is surrounded by 173 smaller ones. While the chedi is now in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lankan</a> style, it is said to be built on top of an earlier <a href="/wiki/Srivijaya" title="Srivijaya">Srivijaya</a> style chedi. The chedi was renovated in early 2009 and now appears like new.</p>
<p>At the base of the chedi is a gallery named <i>Viharn Tap Kaset</i>, decorated with many Buddha statues and elephant heads emerging from the chedi. <i>Viharn Phra Song Ma</i> are the buildings which contain the staircase which leads to a walkway around the chedi above the gallery. At the bottom of the staircase are demon giants (<a href="/wiki/Yaksha" title="Yaksha">yak</a>) as guardians. Adjoining to the north is the <i>Viharn Kien</i>, which contains a small temple museum.</p>
<p>South of the chedi is the large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phra_ubosot" title="Phra ubosot">ubosot</a> building, the <i>Viharn Luang</i>. <a href="/wiki/Thai_temple_art_and_architecture#Sanghawat" title="Thai temple art and architecture">Monk living quarters</a> are across the street in a separate temple, Wat Na Phra Boromathat.</p>
<p>The chedi is the symbol of the Nakhon Si Thammarat Province, present on the seal of the province. It is also displayed on the <a href="/wiki/Thai_baht" title="Thai baht">25 satang</a> coin.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_wall">City wall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakhon_Si_Thammarat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: City wall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1100" data-file-width="1700" height="129" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg/200px-Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg/300px-Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg/400px-Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nakhon_Si_Thammarat_city_wall.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Northern Gate</div>
</div>

<p>The city chronicle mentions a fortification when the town was refounded in 1278. Restorations were recorded at the time of King <a href="/wiki/Ramesuan_(king_of_Ayutthaya)" title="Ramesuan (king of Ayutthaya)">Ramesuan</a> (14th century), as well as King <a href="/wiki/Narai" title="Narai">Narai</a> (1686). The latter was supported by the French engineer M. de la Mare.</p>
<p>The walls spread 456 m from east to west, and 2238 m north to south, thus enclosing an area of about one square kilometre. The northern wall had only one gate, called <i>Prathu Chai Nua</i> or <i>Prathu Chai Sak</i>. The southern wall had only one gate. To the east there were three gates, which connected the town with the sea. To the west were five gates. Today only the northern gate still exists, together with a short stretch of the northern city wall.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Educational_institutions">Educational institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nakhon_Si_Thammarat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Educational institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kindergarten and primary schools: Anuban Na Nakhon Utit School is a government-run school with kindergarten through grade 6. The school operates both Thai and English programmes.</p>
<p>Srithammarat Suksa School is the largest kindergarten and primary school. They offer nursery-grade 6 classes on all three campuses in the city. They also offer the largest English program housed on a separate campus. Srithammart is often referred to as "AMC" or "EP AMC".</p>
<p>Secondary schools: Nakhon Si Thammarat has three large schools: Benjamarachutit School and <a href="/wiki/Kanlayanee_Si_Thammarat_School" title="Kanlayanee Si Thammarat School">Kanlayanee Si Thammarat School</a>, both public secondary schools with grades 7 to 12, and <a href="/wiki/Srithammarat_Suksa_School" title="Srithammarat Suksa School">Srithammarat Suksa School</a>, the largest private pre-kindergarten through grade 12 school.</p>
<p>Vocational Colleges: Nakhon Si Thammarat has numerous vocational colleges, the most notable being Nakhon Sri Thammaratt Technical College (Technic), and Nakhon Si Thammarat Vocational college (Acheewa).</p>
<p>Universities: Nakhon Si Thammarat has two universities: <a href="/wiki/Walailak_University" title="Walailak University">Walailak University</a> (the largest university in Thailand) and Nakhon Si Thammarat Rajaphat University.</p>
