<p><b>Naklo</b> (<small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Slovene" title="Help:IPA for Slovene">[ˈnaːklɔ]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Naklas</i></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leksikon_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leksikon-2">[2]</a></sup>) is the largest town and the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Municipality_of_Naklo" title="Municipality of Naklo">Municipality of Naklo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>. In addition to the main settlement, formerly known as Veliko Naklo<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leksikon_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Leksikon-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Großnaklas</i></span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leksikon_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Leksikon-2">[2]</a></sup> it includes the hamlets of Malo Naklo (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Kleinnaklas</i></span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leksikon_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Leksikon-2">[2]</a></sup> Pivka (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Piuka</i></span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leksikon_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Leksikon-2">[2]</a></sup> and Podreber.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Savnik_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Savnik-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naklo,_Naklo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naklo was first mentioned in written sources in 1241 as <i>Nacel</i> (and as <i>Nakel</i> in 1252, <i>Nachil</i> in 1317, <i>Nakal</i> in 1320, <i>Nackel</i> in 1323, and <i>Nakel</i> in 1328). The name <i>Naklo</i> appears elsewhere in Slovenia and in other Slavic countries—for example, <a href="/wiki/Nak%C5%82o,_Cz%C4%99stochowa_County" title="Nakło, Częstochowa County">Nakło</a> (Poland) and <a href="/wiki/N%C3%A1klo" title="Náklo">Náklo</a> (Czech Republic). The name is derived from <i>*nakъlo</i>, a fused form that has lost inflection from the prepositional phrase <i>*na kъlě</i> 'on a (sandy) spit in a river', thus referring to the location of the settlement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The town was known as <i>Naklas</i> in German in the past.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leksikon_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Leksikon-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naklo,_Naklo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naklo was plundered by Ottoman forces in 1475. In June 1809 the town was plundered by French troops. Banditry was a long-term problem in the area, and local bandits took shelter in the nearby <a href="/wiki/Udin_Woods" title="Udin Woods">Udin Woods</a> (<a href="/wiki/Slovene_language" title="Slovene language">Slovene</a>: <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>Udin boršt</i></span>). Naklo had one of the first running water systems installed in Slovenia, in the 18th century, due to the efforts of Jurij Voglar (1651–1717), who willed his property in order to achieve this goal. During the Second World War, German forces surrounded Naklo with a network of bunkers and wire barriers, and they established a headquarters for an <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">SS</a> regiment in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Savnik_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Savnik-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Church">Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naklo,_Naklo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The local church is dedicated to Saint Peter. It is a Baroque structure and was renovated after a fire in 1843. The altar painting is by Leopold Layer (1752–1828), and the Stations of the Cross were painted by Janez Wolf (1825–1884).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Savnik_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Savnik-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
