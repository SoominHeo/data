<p><b>Natalia Noemi Keery-Fisher</b> (born 15 August 1986), professionally known as <b>Teddy Sinclair</b> or, previously, <b>Natalia Kills</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-real_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-real-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDB_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. In 2008, record executive and producer <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a> signed her to his label <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am_Music_Group" title="Will.i.am Music Group">will.i.am Music Group</a>, an imprint of <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.</p>
<p>As Natalia Kills, Sinclair released her debut album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perfectionist_(Natalia_Kills_album)" title="Perfectionist (Natalia Kills album)">Perfectionist</a></i>, in 2011, and her second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_(Natalia_Kills_album)" title="Trouble (Natalia Kills album)">Trouble</a></i>, in September 2013. In 2014, she married boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Willy_Moon" title="Willy Moon">Willy Moon</a>, who is a singer on the same label. In 2015 the couple were featured as judges on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_series_2)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand series 2)">second season</a> of the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand TV series)">New Zealand version</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor" title="The X Factor">The X Factor</a></i>, but after the season's first live show they were fired after their scathing remarks towards a contestant were deemed unacceptable by broadcaster <a href="/wiki/TV3_(New_Zealand)" title="TV3 (New Zealand)">TV3</a>. As of July 2015, she now goes by and performs under her legal name Teddy Sinclair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2016, she started a three piece girl band, <a href="/wiki/Cruel_Youth_(band)" title="Cruel Youth (band)">Cruel Youth</a>. Their first song, "Mr. Watson", was released on Soundcloud in February, followed by "Diamond Days" in April. Their third single ,"Hatefuck", was released early September along with the announcement of their debut EP <i><a href="/wiki/%2B30mg" title="+30mg">+30mg</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/59th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="59th Annual Grammy Awards">59th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, she received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_R%26B_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best R&amp;B Song">Grammy Award for Best R&amp;B Song</a> nomination for co-writing <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Kiss_It_Better_(Rihanna_song)" title="Kiss It Better (Rihanna song)">Kiss It Better</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natalia_Kills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1986.E2.80.932010:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1986â2010: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natalia_Kills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1986â2010: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sinclair was born Natalia Noemi Keery-Fisher in <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, to a British father of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jamaicans_of_African_ancestry" title="Jamaicans of African ancestry">Afro-Jamaican</a> heritage and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uruguayan_people" title="Uruguayan people">Uruguayan</a> mother,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and attended <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Girls%27_Grammar_School" title="Bradford Girls' Grammar School">Bradford Girls' Grammar School</a>. Her family left Bradford when she was a child and she spent her early years travelling between England, Jamaica and Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>She ran away from home when she was 14 and shifted her focus away from acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-real_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-real-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_York_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_York-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Style_Icon_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Style_Icon-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuff_NZ_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stuff_NZ-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Digital-16">[16]</a></sup> She has described her teenage years as "degenerate," stating that she tried to set her ex-boyfriend's house on fire while both were in it. She had frequent legal trouble and periodically experienced suicidal depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_York_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-New_York-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> She was also briefly involved with a religious cult.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her acting debut was in <i>New Voices</i>. She had a regular role as Sima on the TV <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/All_About_Me" title="All About Me">All About Me</a></i> and as Amy Franks in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s radio drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Archers" title="The Archers">The Archers</a></i>. She began writing songs at 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-DS-19">[19]</a></sup> After finishing with <i>The Archers</i> in 2003, She began pursuing a music career and had a significant breakthrough as "Candy Rapper" in 2003, when she won a <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> MC Battle in <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">Leeds</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-real_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-real-5">[5]</a></sup> She signed to the UK record company Adventures in Music and released her first single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don%27t_Play_Nice" title="Don't Play Nice">Don't Play Nice</a>" under the name Verbalicious in February 2005. Sinclair told <i><a href="/wiki/W_(magazine)" title="W (magazine)">W</a></i> that her childhood nickname from her mother was "Verbal" because she talked and sang a lot. She adopted the name and variations of it as her stage name at some points during her career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmag_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmag-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, while writing music for films,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gigwise_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-gigwise-21">[21]</a></sup> she posted <i>Womannequin,</i> a demo she produced and wrote under the name Natalia Cappuccini, to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MySpace" title="MySpace">MySpace</a>. The EP received 2 million plays on the website, and she reached the top of the unsigned artists chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmag_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-wmag-20">[20]</a></sup> During this time, using the name Verse, she also co-wrote and appeared on "<a href="/wiki/They_Talk_Shit_About_Me" title="They Talk Shit About Me">They Talk Shit About Me</a>" by French recording artist <a href="/wiki/M._Pokora" title="M. Pokora">M. Pokora</a> and met Guillaume Doubet, who would go on to direct many of her videos, in Paris. Sinclair moved to Los Angeles in 2008. Arriving without money or a place to live, Sinclair "lived out of these motels, bumming around, trying to work with or make songs with anyone who would have me for a couple of hours in the studio". At the time she says she did awful things adding "I was just trying to survive and I was too young to know better."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-r1-22">[22]</a></sup> While in L.A., a DJ introduced her to <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a> after hearing her demo and learning that she did not have a record deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In January 2009, will.i.am signed her to his record label, <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am_Music_Group" title="Will.i.am Music Group">will.i.am Music Group</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gigwise_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-gigwise-21">[21]</a></sup> "We had a really good creative chemistry," she said in a 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> interview.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard_bill_clinton_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard_bill_clinton-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9314:_Perfectionist.2C_Trouble_and_marriage">2010â14: <i>Perfectionist</i>, <i>Trouble</i> and marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natalia_Kills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010â14: Perfectionist, Trouble and marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sinclair adopted the stage name Natalia Kills from the interjection "you killed it!",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvr_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-mvr-26">[26]</a></sup> after her record company advised her that her legal name, "Natalia Cappuccini", was "indescribable."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gigwise_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-gigwise-21">[21]</a></sup> Kills released her debut album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perfectionist_(Natalia_Kills_album)" title="Perfectionist (Natalia Kills album)">Perfectionist</a></i> in Germany in April 2011 following the release of her first single, "<a href="/wiki/Mirrors_(Natalia_Kills_song)" title="Mirrors (Natalia Kills song)">Mirrors</a>", which hit the German Top 10. The album was described as a concept album by Sinclair, claiming that everybody is a perfectionist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-DS-19">[19]</a></sup> The album was accompanied by four singles, "Mirrors", "<a href="/wiki/Wonderland_(Natalia_Kills_song)" title="Wonderland (Natalia Kills song)">Wonderland</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Free_(Natalia_Kills_song)" title="Free (Natalia Kills song)">Free</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kill_My_Boyfriend" title="Kill My Boyfriend">Kill My Boyfriend</a>". Through 2010 and 2011 Sinclair opened for <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robyn" title="Robyn">Robyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kesha" title="Kesha">Kesha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Eyed_Peas" title="Black Eyed Peas">Black Eyed Peas</a> over various tours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmag_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-wmag-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvr_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-mvr-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Two tracks from <i>Perfectionist,</i> "Free" feat. will.i.am, and "Mirrors," hit the Top 10 in Europe and achieved sales of over 750,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Digital-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Sinclair was featured in <a href="/wiki/LMFAO" title="LMFAO">LMFAO</a>'s single, "<a href="/wiki/Champagne_Showers" title="Champagne Showers">Champagne Showers</a>," and in <a href="/wiki/Junior_Caldera" title="Junior Caldera">Junior Caldera</a>'s single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lights_Out_(Go_Crazy)" title="Lights Out (Go Crazy)">Lights Out (Go Crazy)</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 September 2012, Sinclair unveiled the video for "Controversy" to start promotion of her second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_(Natalia_Kills_album)" title="Trouble (Natalia Kills album)">Trouble</a></i>. The album was centered on her troubled childhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Sinclair attended an after-party for the <a href="/wiki/2013_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2013 MTV Video Music Awards">2013 MTV Video Music Awards</a>, held on 25 August 2013 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York_City" title="Brooklyn, New York City">Brooklyn, New York City</a>, where she performed various songs from <i>Trouble</i>. Two days later, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Outta_Time" title="Outta Time">Outta Time</a>" was released as the album's second promotional recording free of charge in a <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a> newsletter,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> coinciding with the US release of <i>Trouble</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <i>Trouble</i> was officially released on 3 September 2013 as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> in the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ca_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-ca-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 April 2014, fashion editor Alex Catarinella officially declared to <i><a href="/wiki/BlackBook" title="BlackBook">BlackBook</a></i> magazine that Sinclair would be married to <a href="/wiki/Willy_Moon" title="Willy Moon">Willy Moon</a> on 23 May, in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> During an interview she gave to New Zealand-edited fashion magazine <i>Black</i>, Kills said she had just moved in with Moon and that she had been recording a few songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On 31 May 2014, she was invited to charity event <a href="/wiki/Life_Ball" title="Life Ball">Life Ball</a>, which took place in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, Austria,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> mostly for a live performance kicking off the event, and a concert. On 29 June, she made an appearance at the <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> <a href="/wiki/Gay_pride" title="Gay pride">gay pride</a> festival,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Minneapolis_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Minneapolis-38">[38]</a></sup> where she appeared on stage dressed as a bride. In mid-October 2014, while interviewed for <a href="/wiki/Paper_(magazine)" title="Paper (magazine)"><i>Paper</i> Magazine</a>, she confirmed the future release of new material.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paper_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Paper-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present:_The_X_Factor.2C_Collaborations_and_Cruel_Youth">2015âpresent: <i>The X Factor</i>, Collaborations and Cruel Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natalia_Kills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015âpresent: The X Factor, Collaborations and Cruel Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sinclair and husband Willy Moon were featured as judges and mentors on season 2 of the New Zealand version of <i>The X Factor</i>. They were the mentors of the Boys and Groups categories respectively. During the first live show, in a highly publicized scandal, the two made scathing remarks towards contestant Joe Irvine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-herald_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-herald-40">[40]</a></sup> Sinclair described Irvine as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doppelganger" title="Doppelganger">doppelganger</a> over his alleged copying of Moon's hairstyle and dress sense, deeming him a "laughing stock", overtly "cheesy" and "disgusting",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> while Moon compared Irvine to the fictional killer <a href="/wiki/Norman_Bates" title="Norman Bates">Norman Bates</a>. The comments led to widespread condemnation on social media, including a petition to have Sinclair fired from the show, which reportedly received over 70,000 signatures within 24 hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The show's sponsors, franchise owner and judges <a href="/wiki/Melanie_Blatt" title="Melanie Blatt">Melanie Blatt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stan_Walker" title="Stan Walker">Stan Walker</a> also voiced disapproval of the incident, and Moon and Sinclair were both fired from the show the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Sinclair was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Bassingthwaighte" title="Natalie Bassingthwaighte">Natalie Bassingthwaighte</a>, a former judge on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Australian_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (Australian TV series)">Australian version of the show</a>, while Moon was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Shelton_Woolright" title="Shelton Woolright">Shelton Woolright</a>.</p>
<p>After departing <i>The X Factor</i>, it was revealed in the media that Sinclair had been dropped by her record label, <a href="/wiki/Cherrytree_Records" title="Cherrytree Records">Cherrytree Records</a>, shortly before appearing on the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Sinclair confirmed to <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> that she was no longer with Cherrytree Records, but that she was still with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sinclair worked with <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> on her album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rebel_Heart_(Madonna_album)" title="Rebel Heart (Madonna album)">Rebel Heart</a></i>, co-writing the song "<a href="/wiki/Holy_Water_(Madonna_song)" title="Holy Water (Madonna song)">Holy Water</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rebel_Heart_album_credits_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rebel_Heart_album_credits-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Sinclair's song âProblemâ from her album <a href="/wiki/Trouble_(Natalia_Kills_album)" title="Trouble (Natalia Kills album)">Trouble</a> (released in 2013) was featured in the film <a class="external text" href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2848292/soundtrack" rel="nofollow">soundtrack</a> for <a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_2" title="Pitch Perfect 2">Pitch Perfect 2</a> which hit Number 1 in the album charts <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> and won an American Music Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sinclair worked with <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a> on her eighth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Anti_(album)" title="Anti (album)">Anti</a></i>, co-writing the single "<a href="/wiki/Kiss_It_Better_(Rihanna_song)" title="Kiss It Better (Rihanna song)">Kiss It Better</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2016, Sinclair started a band, <a href="/wiki/Cruel_Youth_(band)" title="Cruel Youth (band)">Cruel Youth</a>, with (her husband) Moon. Their first song, "Mr. Watson", was released on Soundcloud in February, that was then followed by "Diamond Days" in April, which was also released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">ITunes</a>. Their third single ,"Hatefuck", was released in early September along with the release of their debut EP <a href="/wiki/%2B30mg" title="+30mg">+30mg</a> on 16 September.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_influences">Style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natalia_Kills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sinclair has mentioned <a href="/wiki/Kate_Bush" title="Kate Bush">Kate Bush</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alanis_Morissette" title="Alanis Morissette">Alanis Morissette</a> as her most important musical influences, describing them as emotional artists "who write honestly about their opinions and experiences."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The singer also stated she considered <a href="/wiki/Gwen_Stefani" title="Gwen Stefani">Gwen Stefani</a>, who is a musical and stylistic inspiration, as her "hero",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> adding that <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanity_6" title="Vanity 6">Vanity 6</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a> have already inspired her during live performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvr_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-mvr-26">[26]</a></sup> Sinclair explores music that is both confessional and bold when looking for inspiration, she reveals: "I don't listen to music to let go, I listen to dive in", listing <a href="/wiki/Hole_(band)" title="Hole (band)">Hole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Manson" title="Marilyn Manson">Marilyn Manson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> among her favorite artists and bands with whom she grew up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> According to Jon O'Brien of <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a>, Sinclair gradually composed a musical tone incorporating "the theatrical dance-pop of <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>, 1940s <a href="/wiki/Film_noir" title="Film noir">film noir</a>, and the electro-gloom of <a href="/wiki/Depeche_Mode" title="Depeche Mode">Depeche Mode</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> She also reported that she has always been a pop music fan, especially in evoking contemporary "new age" artists as <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lana_Del_Rey" title="Lana Del Rey">Lana Del Rey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marina_and_the_Diamonds" title="Marina and the Diamonds">Marina and the Diamonds</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ivy_Levan" title="Ivy Levan">Ivy Levan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lorde" title="Lorde">Lorde</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sinclair claims to be influenced by her own experiences. She is also inspired by cinema when composing songs. In this regard, she said during an interview for <i>MKR Magazine</i>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I'm a huge fan of <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Kubrick" title="Stanley Kubrick">Stanley Kubrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Tarantino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gaspar_No%C3%A9" title="Gaspar NoÃ©">Gaspar NoÃ©</a>. I love anything that's beautiful and disturbing and horrifying and gorgeous at the same time. I do get inspired, definitely. I don't listen to a lot of music except my own, but I definitely like music a lot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-r1-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
