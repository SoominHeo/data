<p><b>Nathan Allan de Souza</b> (born 13 March 1996), simply known as <b>Nathan</b>, is a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/SBV_Vitesse" title="SBV Vitesse">Vitesse</a> on loan from <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>. Mainly an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a>, he can also play as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">left winger</a> or as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Second_striker" title="Forward (association football)">second striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_(footballer,_born_1996)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atl.C3.A9tico_Paranaense">Atlético Paranaense</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_(footballer,_born_1996)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atlético Paranaense">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Blumenau" title="Blumenau">Blumenau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(state)" title="Santa Catarina (state)">Santa Catarina</a>, Nathan joined <a href="/wiki/Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Paranaense" title="Clube Atlético Paranaense">Atlético Paranaense</a>'s prolific youth setup in 2009, at the age of 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was promoted to the main squad in 2014, initially appearing with the under-23 team in <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Paranaense" title="Campeonato Paranaense">Campeonato Paranaense</a>. His debut came on 29 January in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Copa_Libertadores_first_stage" title="2014 Copa Libertadores first stage">first stage</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a> away at <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Cristal" title="Sporting Cristal">Sporting Cristal</a>, playing ten minutes in place of <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Cirino" title="Marcelo Cirino">Marcelo Cirino</a> in a 1–2 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A week later in the second leg, he came on as a substitute and had his attempt saved in the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout" title="Penalty shootout">penalty shootout</a>, although his team advanced nonetheless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nathan made his <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Série A</a> debut on 22 May, coming on as a second-half <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> for <a href="/wiki/Paulinho_Dias" title="Paulinho Dias">Paulinho Dias</a> in a 1–1 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Corinthians_Paulista" title="Sport Club Corinthians Paulista">Corinthians</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In October, after rejecting a contract renewal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the club and the player went to court, with him being demoted back to the under-23s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chelsea">Chelsea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_(footballer,_born_1996)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chelsea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 July 2015, Nathan joined <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> for an undisclosed fee rumoured to be in the region of £4.5 million, having initially confirming he had signed in May 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Vitesse">Loan to Vitesse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_(footballer,_born_1996)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Loan to Vitesse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Nine days after signing for Chelsea, it was announced that Nathan was join to Dutch side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vitesse_Arnhem" title="Vitesse Arnhem">Vitesse Arnhem</a> on loan, along with <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Brown" title="Isaiah Brown">Isaiah Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Baker_(footballer)" title="Lewis Baker (footballer)">Lewis Baker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He made his debut on 6 August in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> third qualifying round second leg against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> at the <a href="/wiki/GelreDome" title="GelreDome">GelreDome</a>, replacing Baker for the final 21 minutes of a 0–2 home defeat for a 0–5 aggregate elimination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He made his <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> debut three days later in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Willem_II_(football_club)" title="Willem II (football club)">Willem II</a>, replacing Brown with 25 minutes to spare. On 14 August he scored his first goal, again as a substitute, concluding a 3–0 home win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roda_JC" title="Roda JC">Roda JC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 4 October, Nathan scored his second goal for the club in a 5–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Groningen" title="FC Groningen">FC Groningen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 June 2016, Nathan's loan at Vitesse was extended for the 2016/17 campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On the opening day of 2016/17 season, Nathan scored a brace against <a href="/wiki/Willem_II_(football_club)" title="Willem II (football club)">Willem II</a> in a 4–1 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_(footballer,_born_1996)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nathan has represented Brazil in both <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-17_football_team" title="Brazil national under-17 football team">under-17</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-20_football_team" title="Brazil national under-20 football team">under-20</a> levels. He made his debut with the under-17s during the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2013 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2013 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a>, scoring five goals, including braces in the 6–1 group stage wins against <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_under-17_football_team" title="Slovakia national under-17 football team">Slovakia</a> and hosts <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_national_under-17_football_team" title="United Arab Emirates national under-17 football team">United Arab Emirates</a>. He scored their equaliser in the 1–1 draw against holders <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_under-17_football_team" title="Mexico national under-17 football team">Mexico</a> in the quarter-finals and netted in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout" title="Penalty shootout">penalty shootout</a>, but Mexico won 11–10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Nathan was voted as the second best player of the tournament, winning the Silver Ball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was also included in <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Gallo" title="Alexandre Gallo">Alexandre Gallo</a>'s 23-man squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2015_South_American_Youth_Championship" title="2015 South American Youth Championship">2015 South American Youth Championship</a> held in Uruguay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Nathan appeared in all matches during the competition, scoring his first goal on 4 February, netting the first in a 5–0 routing over <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_under-20_football_team" title="Peru national under-20 football team">Peru</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_(footballer,_born_1996)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tim_Vickery" title="Tim Vickery">Tim Vickery</a>, a reporter on South American football, called Nathan "a playmaker who can thread a little pass". He said that although he performed well as an under-17 international, his performances at under-20 were below standard, concluding that "it’s difficult to see what he has done in the last season to justify" interest from Chelsea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bleacher_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-bleacher-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
