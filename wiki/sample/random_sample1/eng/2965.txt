<p><b>Nathuram Vinayak Godse</b> (19 May 1910 – 15 November 1949) assassinated <a href="/wiki/Mahatma_Gandhi" title="Mahatma Gandhi">Mahatma Gandhi</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a> on 30 January 1948, shooting Gandhi in the chest three times at point blank range. Godse, an advocate of <a href="/wiki/Hindu_nationalism" title="Hindu nationalism">Hindu nationalism</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">Pune</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a>, thought Gandhi favored the political demands of India's <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_India" title="Partition of India">partition of India</a>. He plotted the assassination with <a href="/wiki/Narayan_Apte" title="Narayan Apte">Narayan Apte</a> and six others. After a trial that lasted over a year, Godse was sentenced to death on 8 November 1949. Although pleas for commutation were made by Gandhi's two sons, <a href="/wiki/Manilal_Gandhi" title="Manilal Gandhi">Manilal Gandhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ramdas_Gandhi" title="Ramdas Gandhi">Ramdas Gandhi</a>, they were turned down by India's prime minister <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru" title="Jawaharlal Nehru">Jawaharlal Nehru</a>, deputy prime minister <a href="/wiki/Vallabhbhai_Patel" title="Vallabhbhai Patel">Vallabhbhai Patel</a> and the Governor-General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chakravarti_Rajagopalachari" title="Chakravarti Rajagopalachari">Chakravarti Rajagopalachari</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-godse-commutation_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-godse-commutation-1">[1]</a></sup> and Godse was hanged in the Ambala Jail on 15 November 1949.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bandyopadhyay2009_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bandyopadhyay2009-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Nathuram Vinayakrao Godse was born at the nativity mission center, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pune_District" title="Pune District">Pune District</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Chitpavan" title="Chitpavan">Chitpavan</a> <a href="/wiki/Brahmin" title="Brahmin">Brahmin</a> family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His father, Vinayak Vamanrao Godse, was a postal employee; his mother was Lakshmi (née Godavari). At birth, he was named Ramachandra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Nathuram was given his name because of an unfortunate incident. Before he was born, his parents had three sons and a daughter, with all three boys dying in their infancy. Fearing a curse that targeted male children, young Ramachandra was brought up as a girl for the first few years of his life, including having his nose pierced and being made to wear a nose-ring (<i>nath</i> in Marathi). It was then that he earned the nickname "Nathuram" (literally "Ram with a nose-ring"). After his younger brother was born, they switched to treating him as a boy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nosering_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nosering-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Godse attended the local school at Baramati through the fifth standard, after which he was sent to live with an aunt in <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">Pune</a> so that he could study at an English-language school. During his school days, he highly respected Gandhi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nathuram.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="320" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Nathuram.jpg/200px-Nathuram.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Nathuram.jpg/300px-Nathuram.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e9/Nathuram.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

<p>Godse dropped out of high school and became an activist with Hindu nationalist organizations <a href="/wiki/Rashtriya_Swayamsevak_Sangh" title="Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh">Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh</a> (RSS) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindu_Mahasabha" title="Hindu Mahasabha">Hindu Mahasabha</a>, although the exact dates of his membership are uncertain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSSRefutal_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-RSSRefutal-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Godse started a <a href="/wiki/Marathi_language" title="Marathi language">Marathi language</a> newspaper for the Hindu Mahasabha called <i>Agrani</i>, which some years later was renamed <i>Hindu Rashtra</i>. Godse rejected Gandhi's philosophy, believing Gandhi repeatedly sabotaged the interests of Hindus by using the "fasting unto death" tactic on many issues. In Godse's view, Gandhi was giving in to Muslim interests in ways that seemed unfair and anti-national.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Assassination_of_Mahatma_Gandhi">Assassination of Mahatma Gandhi</span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Mahatma_Gandhi" title="Assassination of Mahatma Gandhi">Assassination of Mahatma Gandhi</a></div>
<p>Godse approached Gandhi on 30 January 1948 during the evening prayer at 17:17. When Godse bowed, one of the girls flanking and supporting Gandhi, said to Godse, "Brother, Bapu is already late" and tried to put him off, but he pushed her aside and shot Gandhi in the chest three times at <a href="/wiki/Point-blank_range" title="Point-blank range">point-blank range</a> with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beretta_M_1934" title="Beretta M 1934">Beretta M 1934</a> semi-automatic pistol. Raghu Nayak, who worked as a gardener at the Birla House, was the first to chase Godse and overpower him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Gandhi was taken to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birla_House" title="Birla House">Birla House</a>, where he died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trial_and_execution">Trial and execution</span></h3>
<p>Godse was put on trial at the Punjab High Court, at <a href="/wiki/Peterhoff,_Shimla" title="Peterhoff, Shimla">Peterhoff, Shimla</a>. On 8 November 1949, he was sentenced to death. Although pleas for commutation were made by Gandhi's two sons, <a href="/wiki/Manilal_Gandhi" title="Manilal Gandhi">Manilal Gandhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ramdas_Gandhi" title="Ramdas Gandhi">Ramdas Gandhi</a>, they were turned down by India's prime minister <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru" title="Jawaharlal Nehru">Jawaharlal Nehru</a>, deputy prime minister <a href="/wiki/Vallabhbhai_Patel" title="Vallabhbhai Patel">Vallabhbhai Patel</a> and the Governor-General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chakravarti_Rajagopalachari" title="Chakravarti Rajagopalachari">Chakravarti Rajagopalachari</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-godse-commutation_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-godse-commutation-1">[1]</a></sup> and Godse was hanged at Ambala Jail on 15 November 1949.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bandyopadhyay2009_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bandyopadhyay2009-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span></h3>
<p>Millions of Indians mourned Gandhi's assassination; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindu_Mahasabha" title="Hindu Mahasabha">Hindu Mahasabha</a> was vilified and the <a href="/wiki/Rashtriya_Swayamsevak_Sangh" title="Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh">Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh</a> was temporarily banned. However, investigators could find no evidence that the RSS bureaucracy had formally sponsored or even knew of Godse's plot. The ban on the RSS was lifted in 1949. To this day it denies any connection with Godse and disputes the claim that he was a member.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSSRefutal_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-RSSRefutal-8">[8]</a></sup> However, Godse's brother <a href="/wiki/Gopal_Godse" title="Gopal Godse">Gopal Godse</a> claims that all the Godse brothers were members of the RSS at the time of the assassination. According to Gopal Godse:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>“All the brothers were in the RSS. Nathuram, Dattatreya, myself and Govind. You can say we grew up in the RSS rather than in our home. It was like a family to us. Nathuram had become a baudhik karyavah [intellectual worker] in the RSS. He has said in his statement that he left the RSS. He said it because Golwalkar and the RSS were in a lot of trouble after the murder of Gandhi. But he did not leave the RSS.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frontline_1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-frontline_1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attempts_at_rehabilitation">Attempts at rehabilitation</span></h3>
<p>In 2014, following the <a href="/wiki/Bharatiya_Janata_Party" title="Bharatiya Janata Party">Bharatiya Janata Party</a>'s rise to power, the Hindu Mahasabha began attempts to rehabilitate Godse and portray him as a patriot. It requested Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a> to install the bust of Godse. It created a documentary film <i>Desh Bhakt Nathuram Godse</i> (Patriot Nathuram Godse) for release on the death anniversary of Gandhi on 30 January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mahasabha_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-mahasabha-14">[14]</a></sup> There were attempts to build a temple for Godse and to celebrate 30 January as a <i>Shaurya Diwas</i> ("Bravery Day").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> A civil suit was filed in Pune Court asking for a ban on the documentary film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_art">In art</span></h2>
