<p><b>Napoli Centrale</b> (<i>Naples Central Station</i>) is the main <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> and in southern Italy and the sixth largest station in Italy in terms of passenger flow with an annual ridership of 50 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gs1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gs1-1">[1]</a></sup> It is located next to Piazza Garibaldi to the east of the old city. It is the primary rail terminus and station for Naples, and serves <a href="/wiki/Trenitalia" title="Trenitalia">Trenitalia</a> national railways and EAV and has an underground section known as <i>Stazione di Napoli Piazza Garibaldi</i> (Naples Garibaldi Piazza station), which is served by the metropolitan trains of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_2_(Naples_metro)" title="Line 2 (Naples metro)">line 2</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Napoli_Centrale_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first station on the site was built in 1866 and designed by the architect <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Alvino" title="Enrico Alvino">Enrico Alvino</a>. The current station was designed in 1954 by <a href="/wiki/Pier_Luigi_Nervi" title="Pier Luigi Nervi">Pier Luigi Nervi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carlo_Cocchia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carlo Cocchia (page does not exist)">Carlo Cocchia</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Battaglini&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Massimo Battaglini (page does not exist)">Massimo Battaglini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Zevi" title="Bruno Zevi">Bruno Zevi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Giulio_De_Luca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Giulio De Luca (page does not exist)">Giulio De Luca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Piccinato" title="Luigi Piccinato">Luigi Piccinato</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseph_Vaccaro" title="Joseph Vaccaro">Joseph Vaccaro</a> on the site of the old railway station and overlooks the square dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">Giuseppe Garibaldi</a>. The project was completed in 1960. The station is currently being rebuilt.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Napoli_Centrale_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has 25 tracks. It is connected to Rome by high-speed trains on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rome%E2%80%93Naples_high-speed_railway_line" title="Rome–Naples high-speed railway line">Rome–Naples high-speed railway line</a> as well as slower trains on the original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rome%E2%80%93Cassino%E2%80%93Naples_railway_line" title="Rome–Cassino–Naples railway line">Rome–Cassino–Naples line</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rome%E2%80%93Formia%E2%80%93Naples_railway_line" title="Rome–Formia–Naples railway line">Rome–Formia–Naples <i>Direttissima</i></a> opened in 1927. It is connected to Salerno and southern Italy by the traditional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naples%E2%80%93Salerno_railway_line" title="Naples–Salerno railway line">Naples–Salerno line</a> and the recently opened <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naples%E2%80%93Salerno_high-speed_railway_line" title="Naples–Salerno high-speed railway line">Naples–Salerno high-speed line</a> used by long distance trains.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Napoli_Piazza_Garibaldi_railway_station">Napoli Piazza Garibaldi railway station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Napoli_Centrale_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Napoli Piazza Garibaldi railway station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Below the mainline station is an underground station named Napoli Piazza Garibaldi railway station, for the <a href="/wiki/Villa_Literno%E2%80%93Napoli_Gianturco_railway" title="Villa Literno–Napoli Gianturco railway">Villa Literno–Napoli Gianturco railway</a>, used by the metropolitan train of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_2_(Naples_metro)" title="Line 2 (Naples metro)">line 2</a>. There is also a connection to the nearby <a href="/wiki/Circumvesuviana" title="Circumvesuviana">Circumvesuviana</a> <i>Napoli Piazza Garibaldi</i>.</p>
