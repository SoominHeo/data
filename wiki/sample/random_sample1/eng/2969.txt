<p><b>Nahas Gideon Angula</b> (born 22 August 1943<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pro_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pro-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KD_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-KD-2">[2]</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibian</a> politician who served as the third <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Namibia" title="Prime Minister of Namibia">Prime Minister of Namibia</a> from 21 March 2005 to 4 December 2012. He was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Hage_Geingob" title="Hage Geingob">Hage Geingob</a> in a cabinet reshuffle after the 2012 SWAPO congress. He subsequently served as Minister of Defence from 2012 to 2015.</p>
<p>Angula is a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_West_Africa_People%27s_Organization" title="South West Africa People's Organization">South West Africa People's Organization</a> (SWAPO). He was Minister of Education, Sport, and Culture from 1990 to 1995 and Minister of Higher Education from 1995 to 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nahas_Angula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Angula was born in Onyaanya, <a href="/wiki/Oshikoto_Region" title="Oshikoto Region">Oshikoto Region</a>. He was in exile from Namibia from 1965 to 1989, and during this time he worked for Radio Zambia from 1973 to 1976 and for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> as a civil servant from 1976 to 1980 before becoming a SWAPO organizer in 1980. He was in charge of SWAPO voter registration in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pro_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pro-1">[1]</a></sup> Immediately prior to independence, Angula was a SWAPO member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constituent_Assembly_of_Namibia" title="Constituent Assembly of Namibia">Constituent Assembly of Namibia</a>, which was in place from November 1989 to March 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Angula became a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Namibia" title="National Assembly of Namibia">National Assembly</a> in 1990. He was Minister of Education, Sport, and Culture from 1990 to 1995 and Minister of Higher Education from 1995<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pro_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Pro-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KD_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-KD-2">[2]</a></sup> to 2005.</p>
<p>Angula received the highest number of votes, 395 (tied with <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Ekandjo" title="Jerry Ekandjo">Jerry Ekandjo</a>), in the election to the Central Committee of <a href="/wiki/SWAPO" title="SWAPO">SWAPO</a> at the party's August 2002 congress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was one of three candidates who sought SWAPO's nomination as its presidential candidate in May 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He placed third in the first round, receiving 137 votes, while <a href="/wiki/Hifikepunye_Pohamba" title="Hifikepunye Pohamba">Hifikepunye Pohamba</a> received 213 and <a href="/wiki/Hidipo_Hamutenya" title="Hidipo Hamutenya">Hidipo Hamutenya</a> received 166, and he was therefore excluded from the second round. Those who supported Angula backed Pohamba almost without exception in the second round of voting, and Pohamba was victorious.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 21, 2005, when Pohamba was sworn in as President of Namibia, he announced that he was appointing Angula as Prime Minister of Namibia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-New-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Hage_Geingob" title="Hage Geingob">Hage Geingob</a> was re-elected as SWAPO Vice-President on 2 December 2012, and thus confirmed as SWAPO's 2014 presidential candidate, President Pohamba appointed Geingob to replace Angula as Prime Minister on 4 December 2012. Angula was instead appointed as Minister of Defense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Amidst a push for new faces in the National Assembly, Angula opted not to seek a spot on the SWAPO list for the 2014 election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_personal_life">Education and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nahas_Angula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Angula received a master's degree in education from <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Evangelical_Lutheran_Church_in_Namibia" title="Evangelical Lutheran Church in Namibia">Lutheran church</a>, as has been his family for generations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
