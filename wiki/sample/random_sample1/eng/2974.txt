<p><b>Naganeupseong</b>(樂安邑城, 낙안읍성) is a Korean fortress in Nagan-myeon, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suncheon,_South_Korea" title="Suncheon, South Korea">Suncheon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeollanamdo" title="Jeollanamdo">Jeollanamdo</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nagan_castle.jpg"><img alt="Nagan castle.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Nagan_castle.jpg/220px-Nagan_castle.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Nagan_castle.jpg/330px-Nagan_castle.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Nagan_castle.jpg/440px-Nagan_castle.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nagan_castle.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Naganeupseong is the only remaining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon</a> dynasty castle in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeollanamdo" title="Jeollanamdo">Jeollanamdo</a>. It finely has the old images of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon</a> folk village and castle, with about 100 houses and government offices. Nowadays, there still are 85 households and 229 people living there. It has the biggest feature that it was built on the plain fields and has the narrow stone walls around the castle.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naganeupseong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was firstly built in 1397 <a href="/wiki/Taejo_of_Joseon" title="Taejo of Joseon">Taejo</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon</a>, by the general Kim Bin-gil after the defeat of <a href="/wiki/Wokou" title="Wokou">Wokou</a> to protect its area. It was surrounded by a sand wall but changed to a stone wall during king <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sejong" title="Sejong">Sejong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon</a> dynasty because of its difficulty to protect its area against the Japanese invaders <a href="/wiki/Wokou" title="Wokou">Wokou</a>.</p>
