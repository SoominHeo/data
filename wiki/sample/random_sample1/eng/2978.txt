<p><b>Marianne Elisabeth Nordqvist</b> (<small>Swedish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[mariˌan ɛˈliːsabɛt ˈnuːɖˈkvɪst]</a></span>) (born 18 May 1962 in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>), known by her stage name <b>Nanne Grönvall</b> (<small>Swedish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[ˌnanːɛ ˈɡrøːnˈvalː]</a></span>), is a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>-<a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nanne_Gr%C3%B6nvall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grönvall's musical career started in the 1980s group Sound of Music. The trio participated twice in the Swedish <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a> selections, <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</a>, finishing fourth both times; in 1986 with "Eldorado" and in 1987 with "Alexandra". Over the following ten years she established herself as a songwriter in Melodifestivalen, writing two songs for the 1992 competition and the 1995 runner-up "<a href="/wiki/Det_vackraste" title="Det vackraste">Det vackraste</a>" ("The Most Beautiful Thing") performed by <a href="/wiki/Cecilia_Vennersten" title="Cecilia Vennersten">Cecilia Vennersten</a>.</p>
<p>From 1991 till 1997, she was one of the lead vocalists of the group <a href="/wiki/One_More_Time_(band)" title="One More Time (band)">One More Time</a> with her husband <i>Peter Grönvall</i> and <a href="/wiki/Maria_R%C3%A5dsten" title="Maria Rådsten">Maria Rådsten</a>. They won the Melodifestivalen 1996 final with the folk-ballad "<a href="/wiki/Den_vilda" title="Den vilda">Den vilda</a>" ("The Wild One"). It finished third (out of 23) in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1996" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1996">Eurovision Song Contest 1996</a> behind Norway and winners Ireland.</p>
<p>Boosted by this, Nanne began a solo career following the last album of One More Time in 1997. In 1998 she returned to Melodifestivalen with "Avundsjuk" ("Envious"), a song about her hatred of successful people. For the Melodifestivalen performance of the song, she wore a purple wig and ears reminiscent of Star Trek's <a href="/wiki/Spock" title="Spock">Mr. Spock</a>. The regional juries placed the song fourth. She participated in the United Kingdom's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Making_Your_Mind_Up_(TV_series)" title="Making Your Mind Up (TV series)">Song for Europe</a> selection for Eurovision in 2001 and finished fourth (last) with "Men", written by <a href="/wiki/Kimberley_Rew" title="Kimberley Rew">Kimberley Rew</a> (formerly of <a href="/wiki/Katrina_and_the_Waves" title="Katrina and the Waves">Katrina and the Waves</a>). In 2003, she returned to Melodifestivalen with "Evig kärlek" ("Eternal Love"), a somewhat peculiar mix of <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Techno_music" title="Techno music">techno</a>. Maria Rådsten was a backing singer for the song. However, the song did not appeal to the voting public, and failed to qualify for the final.</p>
<p>In 2005, Nanne made her sixth appearance as a performer at Melodifestivalen, singing a song co-written with <a href="/wiki/Ingela_Forsman" title="Ingela Forsman">Ingela 'Pling' Forsman</a>. Even though it was initially rejected by the jury selecting the entries, "Håll om mig" ("Embrace me") was chosen as a <a href="/wiki/Wild_card_(sports)" title="Wild card (sports)">wildcard</a> for the competition, due to the lack of female songwriters participating. It was performed at the semifinal in <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4xj%C3%B6" title="Växjö">Växjö</a> and qualified for the final with ease. It was considered the favourite, albeit in a close contest. The song finished a disappointing third with the regional juries, but won the national televote by a surprisingly huge margin, scoring a total of 209 points. This left it three points behind the overall winner, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Stenmarck" title="Martin Stenmarck">Martin Stenmarck</a>. It went on to top the Swedish single chart for two weeks and stayed in the top three for eight weeks, selling more than 10,000 copies (<a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">gold</a> throughout Sweden).</p>
<p>In June 2006, Nanne canceled her tour and announced that she was suffering from <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a>, which she recovered from some months later.</p>
<p>Nanne entered Melodifestivalen again in 2007 with the song "Jag måste kyssa dig" ("I Must Kiss You"), written by herself and Ingela 'Pling' Forsman. On 17 February 2007, Nanne Grönvall finished third in the Melodifestivalen semifinal, thus qualifying for the "second chance" round. On 3 March 2007, Nanne was eliminated from the "Second chance round" after the first voting round in which she was up against <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Uggla" title="Magnus Uggla">Magnus Uggla</a>.</p>
<p>She competed as a celebrity dancer in <a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Dance_2015" title="Let's Dance 2015">Let's Dance 2015</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nanne_Gr%C3%B6nvall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Melodifestivalen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gronvall has performed at <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</a> on seven occasions:</p>
<ul>
<li>1986: "Eldorado" (as a member of Sound of Music) - 4th</li>
<li>1987: "Alexandra" (as a member of Sound of Music) - 4th</li>
<li>1996: "Den vilda" (as a member of One More Time) - 1st</li>
<li>1998: "Avundsjuk" ("Envious") - 4th</li>
<li>2003: "Evig kärlek" ("Eternal Love") - semifinal</li>
<li>2005: "Håll om mig" ("Embrace Me") - 2nd</li>
<li>2007: "Jag måste kyssa dig" ("I Must Kiss You") - second chance round</li>
</ul>
