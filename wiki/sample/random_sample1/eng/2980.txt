<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nanbu Shigenao</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">In this <a href="/wiki/Japanese_name" title="Japanese name">Japanese name</a>, the family name is Nanbu.</div>
<p><b>Nanbu Shigenao <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南部重直</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></b> (1606–1664) was a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">daimyo</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, who was the head of the <a href="/wiki/Nanbu_clan" title="Nanbu clan">Nanbu clan</a> and the 2nd lord of the <a href="/wiki/Morioka_Domain" title="Morioka Domain">Morioka Domain</a>. He was the 3rd son of <a href="/wiki/Nanbu_Toshinao" title="Nanbu Toshinao">Nanbu Toshinao</a>, and was born in <a href="/wiki/Edo" title="Edo">Edo</a>. During his tenure, <a href="/wiki/Morioka_Castle" title="Morioka Castle">Morioka Castle</a> was completed.</p>
