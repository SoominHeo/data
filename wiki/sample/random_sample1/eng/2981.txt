<p><b>Nansen passports</b> were internationally recognized <a href="/wiki/Refugee_travel_document" title="Refugee travel document">refugee travel documents</a>, first issued by the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stateless_person" title="Stateless person">stateless</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Refugees" title="Refugees">refugees</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nansen_passport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first Nansen passports were issued following an international agreement reached at the Intergovernmental Conference on Identity Certificates for Russian Refugees, convened by <a href="/wiki/Fridtjof_Nansen" title="Fridtjof Nansen">Fridtjof Nansen</a> in Geneva from July 3, 1922 to July 5, 1922 in his role as High Commissioner for Refugees for the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> By 1942, they were honoured by governments in 52 countries. Approximately 450,000 Nansen passports were provided<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> to stateless people and refugees who needed travel documents but could not obtain one from a national authority.</p>
<p>The Nansen passport was originally provided to refugees from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_civil_war" title="Russian civil war">Russian civil war</a>. It is estimated that about 800,000 Russian refugees had become stateless when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lenin" title="Lenin">Lenin</a> revoked citizenship for all Russian expatriates in 1921.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1933, the arrangement was broadened to also include <a href="/wiki/Armenians" title="Armenians">Armenian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turkish</a> refugees.</p>
<p>Following Nansen's death in 1930, the passport was handled by the Nansen International Office for Refugees within the League of Nations. At that point the passport no longer included a reference to the 1922 conference, but were issued in the name of the League. The office was closed in 1938; passports were thereafter issued by a new agency, the Office of the High Commissioner for Refugees in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nobel_Peace_Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nansen_passport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nobel Peace Prize">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Nansen_International_Office_for_Refugees" title="Nansen International Office for Refugees">Nansen International Office for Refugees</a> was awarded the 1938 <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> for its efforts to establish the Nansen passports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Today">Today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nansen_passport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While Nansen passports are no longer issued, existing national and supranational authorities, including the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, issue travel documents for stateless people and refugees, including <a href="/wiki/Certificate_of_identity" title="Certificate of identity">certificates of identity</a> (or "alien's passports") and <a href="/wiki/Refugee_travel_document" title="Refugee travel document">refugee travel documents</a>.</p>

<p><small><sup>1</sup> A) Includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_Dependencies" title="Crown Dependencies">Crown Dependencies</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">British Overseas Territories</a>, and <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">former British plantations, crown colonies, colonies, protectorates, protected states, mandates, trust territories and other British possessions</a>.<br/>
B) The Isle of Man, Jersey and Guernsey are not part of the European Union, but Manxmen and Channel Islanders are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citizens_of_the_European_Union" title="Citizens of the European Union">citizens of the European Union</a>; the Isle of Man, Jersey and Guernsey, and Manxmen and Channel Islanders themselves (unless they qualify and apply for recognition of a change in status), are however excluded from the benefits of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Four_Freedoms_(European_Union)" title="Four Freedoms (European Union)">Four Freedoms of the European Union</a>.<br/>
C) The Government of the United Kingdom also issue passports to British nationals who are not British citizens with the right of abode in the United Kingdom and who are also not otherwise citizens of the European Union.</small></p>
<p><small><sup>2</sup> Open border with <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen Area</a>.</small></p>
<p><small><sup>3</sup> <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_transcontinental_countries" title="List of transcontinental countries">transcontinental country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Asia" title="Northern Asia">Northern Asia</a>. The vast majority of its population (80%) lives in <a href="/wiki/European_Russia" title="European Russia">European Russia</a>, therefore Russia as a whole is included as a European country here.</small></p>
<p><small><sup>4</sup> <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_transcontinental_countries" title="List of transcontinental countries">transcontinental country</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and Southeast Europe. Turkey has a small part of its territory (3%) in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Europe" title="Southeast Europe">Southeast Europe</a> called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Thrace" title="Turkish Thrace">Turkish Thrace</a>.</small></p>
<p><small><sup>5</sup> <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Abkhazia" title="Abkhazia">Abkhazia</a>; <a href="/wiki/South_Ossetia" title="South Ossetia">South Ossetia</a>) are <a href="/wiki/List_of_transcontinental_countries" title="List of transcontinental countries">transcontinental countries</a>. Both have a small part of their territories in the European part of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a>.</small></p>
<p><small><sup>6</sup> <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_transcontinental_countries" title="List of transcontinental countries">transcontinental country</a>. Kazakhstan has a small part of its territories located west of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urals" title="Urals">Urals</a> in Eastern Europe.</small></p>
<p><small><sup>7</sup> <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh" title="Nagorno-Karabakh">Nagorno-Karabakh</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> (<a href="/wiki/Northern_Cyprus" title="Northern Cyprus">Northern Cyprus</a>) are entirely in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southwest_Asia" title="Southwest Asia">Southwest Asia</a> but having socio-political connections with Europe.</small></p>
<p><small><sup>8</sup> <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_transcontinental_countries" title="List of transcontinental countries">transcontinental country</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a>. Egypt has a small part of its territory in Western Asia called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sinai_peninsula" title="Sinai peninsula">Sinai peninsula</a>.</small></p>
