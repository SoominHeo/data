<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Presidential Palace (Nanjing)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Presidential Palace</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%80%BB" title="wikt:总">总</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%BB%9F" title="wikt:统">统</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%BA%9C" title="wikt:府">府</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">總統府</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Zǒngtǒng fǔ</span></i>, lit. "President's House") in <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, housed the Office of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="President of the Republic of China">President of the Republic of China</a> since 1927 until the republic was relocated to <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> in 1949. It is now a museum called the <b>China Modern History Museum</b>. It is located at No.292 Changjiang Road (formerly Lin Sen Road), in the <a href="/wiki/Xuanwu_District,_Nanjing" title="Xuanwu District, Nanjing">Xuanwu District</a> of Nanjing.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ming_and_Qing_era">Ming and Qing era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ming and Qing era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a>, this house was to the west of the <a href="/wiki/Ming_Palace" title="Ming Palace">Ming Palace</a>, built by a duke. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a>, it became the Office of the <a href="/wiki/Viceroy_of_Liangjiang" title="Viceroy of Liangjiang">Viceroy of Two Lower Yangtze Provinces</a>, the chief government official in charge of what is today <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anhui" title="Anhui">Anhui</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>. When <a href="/wiki/Qianlong_Emperor" title="Qianlong Emperor">Qianlong</a> visited the Lower Yangtze, he chose this office to be the detached palace together with the Imperial Silk House. Today, "Daxinggong" (lit. the Great Detached Palace) Station of Nanjing Metro Line 2 is named after it.</p>
<p>In 1853, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiping_Revolution" title="Taiping Revolution">Taiping Revolution</a> forces led by <a href="/wiki/Hong_Xiuquan" title="Hong Xiuquan">Hong Xiuquan</a> occupied <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>. The palace was expanded and converted into a luxurious palace for Hong, called the <b>Palace of the Heavenly King</b>, or the <b>Celestial Palace</b>.</p>
<p>In 1864, Qing imperial forces re-took Nanjing. Commander <a href="/wiki/Zeng_Guofan" title="Zeng Guofan">Zeng Guofan</a> ordered to destroy most of the palace by fire. He had a new palatial residence in 1870 and later imposing government buildings for the Qing Governor-General erected in the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical style</a>, and in accordance with contemporary protocol.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Presidential_Palace">Presidential Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Presidential Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Xinhai_Revolution" title="Xinhai Revolution">Xinhai Revolution</a> in 1911, <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen" title="Sun Yat-sen">Sun Yat-sen</a> was sworn in at the former Governor-General's palace, now the "Presidential Palace," as the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="President of the Republic of China">provisional President of the Republic of China</a>. He kept offices here for a while.</p>
<p>However, China soon fell into the post-revolution <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warlord_era" title="Warlord era">Warlord era</a>, and the Palace was not officially used by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912-49)" title="Republic of China (1912-49)">Republic of China</a> until 1927, when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Expedition_(1926%E2%80%931927)" title="Northern Expedition (1926–1927)">Northern Expedition</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> (KMT) captured Nanjing. As the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nationalist_Government" title="Nationalist Government">Nationalist Government</a>, they transformed the Presidential Palace and adjacent buildings into the "Headquarters of the Nationalist Government of the Republic of China." Premier <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek" title="Chiang Kai-shek">Chiang Kai-shek</a> had his office in the palace.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">Second Sino-Japanese War</a> (1937–1945), Chiang Kai-shek's government fled to <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chungking</a>, and the Headquarters was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Wang_Jingwei" title="Wang Jingwei">Wang Jingwei</a>, who collaborated with the invading Japanese. Following the Japanese surrender in 1945, Chiang Kai-shek's Nationalist Government reoccupied the Headquarters—Presidential Palace for governing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912-49)" title="Republic of China (1912-49)">Republic of China</a>. In 1947, the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Constitution of the Republic of China">Constitution of the Republic of China</a> was promulgated. and the "Headquarters of the Nationalist Government of the Republic of China" was renamed the "Presidential Palace."</p>
<p>In April 1949, near the end of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a>, the Communist forces captured Nanjing and the Presidential Palace. Chiang Kai-shek's government fled to <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a> declared the establishment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> with capital in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> on 1 October 1949. The Presidential Palace building was then used for Jiangsu Provincial Government.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museum">Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the late 1980s the former Presidential Palace was transformed into the China Modern History Museum, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_museum" title="History museum">history museum</a> exclusively about the 20th century <a href="/wiki/History_of_China#Modern_China" title="History of China">history of modern China</a>, about the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="History of the Republic of China">history of the Republic of China</a> (1912-1949) and the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="History of the People's Republic of China">history of the People's Republic of China</a>.</p>
<p>In 2005, the Chairman of Kuomintang, <a href="/wiki/Lien_Chan" title="Lien Chan">Lien Chan</a>, visited the museum in the Presidential Palace on his trip to Mainland China, marking a symbolic return of the party to the Palace for the first time in 58 years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located in the capital city of the Republic of China, Presidential Palace is an important historical landmark in Nanjing and one of the largest surviving complex of modern Chinese (1840-1949) buildings. It mainly consists of the central axis, the west garden and the east garden.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_Axis">Central Axis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Central Axis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_Gate">Main Gate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Main Gate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="1067" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG/220px-Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG/330px-Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG/440px-Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Presidental_Palace_at_Nanjing_main_gate.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The main gate, built in 1929</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It was the site of the main gate of the Viceroy's Office. During the period of Taiping Rebellion, it became the site of the Gate of True God's Glorious Light, the main gate of the Heavenly King's Palace, which was destroyed soon after the imperial troops captured the palace. In 1870, the gate was rebuilt as the main gate of the viceroy's office, and demolished when the <a href="/wiki/National_Revolutionary_Army" title="National Revolutionary Army">NRA</a> captured Nanjing in 1927.</p>
<p>The current gate, rebuilt in 1929, was a western-style gate with ionic columns. Two stone lions stood on the both sides of the gate. As the HQ of the Nationalist Government, the sign on the gate read "國民政府" (Nationalist Government) from 1929 to 1937 and from 1946 to 1948. During the Japanese occupation of Nanking, it served as the main gate of Legislative Yuan and Control Yuan of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wang_Jingwei_regime" title="Wang Jingwei regime">Wang Jingwei regime</a>. After the constitutionalism and the presidential election in 1948, the sign changed into "總統府" (Presidential Palace) until now. Nowadays, it is not only a significant symbol in downtown Nanjing, but also served as the main entrance to the Presidential Palace.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_Hall">Main Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Main Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace,_Nanjing,_Aug_2016.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing%2C_Aug_2016.jpg/220px-Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing%2C_Aug_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing%2C_Aug_2016.jpg/330px-Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing%2C_Aug_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing%2C_Aug_2016.jpg/440px-Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing%2C_Aug_2016.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Main_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace,_Nanjing,_Aug_2016.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
South facade of the main hall</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Just north of the main gate, the main hall is the first hall of the Viceroy's Palace. It was rebuilt in 1870 on the original site of the Supreme Hall of the Glorious Light,the most luxurious hall of the Heavenly King's Palace during the period of Taiping Heavenly Kingdom. It was the place where the viceroy or the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Xiuquan" title="Hong Xiuquan">Heavenly King</a> held the ceremonies. In January 1912, the inarguration ceremony of the Provisional President was held in the "Warm Pavilion" just on the northwest side of the main hall. From 1927, it was used for government functions by the Nationalist Government and the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="President of the Republic of China">Office of the President</a>.</p>
<p>The main hall is a traditional Chinese-style hall. At the top of the hall, there is a board on which the four Chinese characters "天下為公" ("All is equal under heaven") was written by Dr <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen" title="Sun Yat-sen">Sun Yat-sen</a>. Behind the main hall there is a corridor which connects it to the second hall. On the west of the corridor stands the auditorium, which was built on the ruins of the "Warm Pavilion". Heavenly King's Throne Room is located on the east side of the corridor.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Hall">Second Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Second Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The second hall, also known as the central hall, rebuilt in late 19th century, was the site of the Heavenly King's inner court. During the period of the Nationalist Government, some minor ceremonies were held in the hall. There is an aisle that lead the visitors to the reception hall.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_Hall">Reception Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Reception_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace,_Nanjing_(2016).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Reception_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/220px-Reception_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Reception_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/330px-Reception_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Reception_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/440px-Reception_Hall_of_the_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

South facade of the reception hall</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Also known as the "八字廰" (literally "Hall of Character Eight"), the western-style reception hall of the Presidential Palace was built in 1917 by <a href="/wiki/Feng_Guozhang" title="Feng Guozhang">Feng Guozhang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Vice President of the Republic of China">Vice President of the Republic of China</a> at that time. After the Northern Expedition of the National Revoluntionary Army, it became the reception rooms for the civil and foreign guests of the Nationalist Government. Before the Chinese Civil War began, Some negotiations between <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">CPC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">KMT</a> were signed in this hall. In addition, Chiang Kai-shek, Lin Sen and Li Tsung-jen also rested here before the ceremonies began frequently. President Chiang Kai-shek occasionally worked here instead of the Concrete House.</p>
<p>The Kirin Gate is a red gate situated on the north of the reception hall. This gate would open only if the president went through this place. The gate leads to the House of the Government Affairs, the first office building of the Chairman of the Nationalist Government.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="House_of_the_Government_Affairs">House of the Government Affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: House of the Government Affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President,_Nanjing_(2016).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/220px-Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/330px-Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/440px-Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Museum_of_the_President_and_the_Vice_President,_Nanjing_(2016).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
North facade of the House of the Government Affairs</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The House of the Government Affairs, also known as the House of the Government Officials and the House of Documentary Affairs, was built in 1925 by the "Nanking Warlord" <a href="/wiki/Sun_Chuanfang" title="Sun Chuanfang">Sun Chuanfang</a>. It was served as the Kiangsu military governor's office firstly. After 1928, it became the office building of the Chairman of the Nationalist Government and other government officials until the Concrete House was accomplished in 1935. As Chiang Kai-shek was the chairman from 1928 to 1931, there was a room in the second floor once served as Chiang's office.</p>
<p>From 1946 to 1949, this house was converted into the House of the Government Affairs. The Ministry of the Government Affairs mainly dealt with the works of writing articles and checking confidential documents. Chen Bulei, an official of the government held the house as his office. Today, the house is served as the Museum of the President and the Vice President, with some collections from the Nationalist Government. Out of the house, there is a courtyard that leads to the Concrete House, one of the most important buildings in the palace.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Concrete_House">Concrete House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Concrete House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Concrete_House_in_Presidential_Palace,_Nanjing_(2016).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="2448" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/The_Concrete_House_in_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/220px-The_Concrete_House_in_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/The_Concrete_House_in_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/330px-The_Concrete_House_in_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/The_Concrete_House_in_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg/440px-The_Concrete_House_in_Presidential_Palace%2C_Nanjing_%282016%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

South facade of the Concrete House</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Concrete House, also known as "Zichao House" and the "Presidential Building", was one of the most important buildings in the complex. Situated on the northernmost part in the palace, the six-storey house with the structure of reinforced concrete was one of the tallest buildings in Nanking in the 1930s. The construction started in 1934, and finished in December 1935. <a href="/wiki/Lin_Sen" title="Lin Sen">Lin Sen</a> used this house as the office of Chairman of the Nationalist Government until the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nanking" title="Battle of Nanking">Japanese troops captured Nanking</a>. From 1940 to 1945, this Japanese-occupied house was converted into the office of the Legislative Yuan and the Control Yuan of the <a href="/wiki/Reorganized_National_Government_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Reorganized National Government of the Republic of China">Reorganized National Government</a>. In 1946, Chiang's Nationalist Government re-took Nanking, then used this house as the office of the Nationalist Government for the second time. After the presidential inarguration ceremony in 1948, it became the seat of the president of the ROC. In April 1949, the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> took control of this house. The house lost the position of the president's seat from then on. From December 1949, the function of this house was shifted into the <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Office_Building,_Taiwan" title="Presidential Office Building, Taiwan">Presidential Office Building of Taipei</a>. In Mainland China, <a href="/wiki/Zhongnanhai" title="Zhongnanhai">Zhongnanhai</a> became the seat of the central government and the official residence of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="President of the People's Republic of China">President of Mainland China</a> since October 1949.</p>
<p>Two cedars were planted in 1934 by Chairman Lin Sen on both sides of the courtyard in the front of the Concrete House. The cedar on the east side was dead in 2005 and was cut down in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The house was designed by Yu Binglie, who was the head of the department of architecture, <a href="/wiki/Nanjing_University" title="Nanjing University">National Central University</a>. Inside the house, the first floor was served as the office of the Secretary-General to the President, the highest-ranking official in the Office of the President who supervised the staff of the Office. The presidential office and vice presidential office were both on the second floor. The meeting room of the State Affair Council was located on the third floor.</p>

<p>The Presidential Office was the office of the president of the ROC since 20 May 1948. It is also known as the Office of Chiang Kai-shek because Chiang was the only president who used this office as the presidential office. Situated on the southeast corner of the second floor, it composed of three rooms. The middle one was served as the principal working place of the president. Inside the middle room, a huge secretaire was put sideways next to the window, with a French-made droplight above. And above the brown couch, a huge photograph of Chiang was hung on the side wall. This office is one of the most popular sites for the visitors in the Presidential Palace.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Vice_Presidential_Office">Vice Presidential Office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Vice Presidential Office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>the vice presidential office lied opposite the presidential office, also known as <a href="/wiki/Li_Zongren" title="Li Zongren">Li Tsung-jen</a>'s office. As well as the presidential office, it was served as the office of the vice president since 20 May 1948. But Li Tsung-jen, the vice president usually worked in his residence in <a href="/wiki/Gulou_District,_Nanjing" title="Gulou District, Nanjing">Gulou District</a> instead of this office to avoid meeting Chiang Kai-shek, who were not getting along well with each other.</p>

<p>The West Garden, also called Xu Garden, is a classical southern Chinese garden located just on the west of the Presidential Palace's central axis, which was considered as the finest work of the gardens in Nanjing together with <a href="/wiki/Zhan_Garden_(Nanjing)" title="Zhan Garden (Nanjing)">Zhanyuan Garden</a>. Centered on the Taiping Lake (literally "Pacific Lake"), Various pavilions and attics built in 1870s, including the Marble Boat, the Paulownia Melody House, the Tower of Beautiful Sunset, the Ripples Pavilion, the Provinsional President's Living Room and the Attic of Joy are all located on the shore of the lake.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Marble_Boat">The Marble Boat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: The Marble Boat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Marble Boat is the oldest surviving structure in the Presidential Palace complex, which was built when <a href="/wiki/Qianlong_Emperor" title="Qianlong Emperor">Qianlong</a> visited Kiangning. To please the emperor, the official told him that the name of the structure was called the "unmoored boat", as the metaphor of the firmness of the Qing dynasty's regime. The 14.5-meter-long timber-mimic stony boat is one of the best-known structures in the garden.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Attic_of_Joy">Attic of Joy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Attic of Joy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Attic of Joy, alternatively called the Pavilion that Forgetting to Fly and the Waterside Pavilion, was rebuilt in 1870 on the eastern lakeside of the garden. The government official once worked here were surprised at the attractive view and believed that "if the birds saw the view here, they would have so much joy that they could forget how to fly". That was the origin of the name.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Staff_House">General Staff House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: General Staff House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The General Staff House was built in 1935, which was the seat of <a href="/wiki/He_Yingqin" title="He Yingqin">Ho Ying-chin</a>, the General Staff of the Nationalist Government. Its function now is the exhibition hall of the theme "Sun Yat-sen and the Provisional Government in Nanjing".</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Commission_House">Commission House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Commission House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Commission House, variously called the West Garden Hall and the Office of the Provisional President, was built in 1909 by <a href="/wiki/Duanfang" title="Duanfang">Duanfang</a>, the 80th <a href="/wiki/Viceroy_of_Liangjiang" title="Viceroy of Liangjiang">Viceroy of Liangjiang</a>, who visited the Europe just before the construction started. The construction finished in 1910, during the period of <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Renjun" title="Zhang Renjun">Zhang Renjun</a>, the last Viceroy of Liangjiang. From 1 Januanry 1912, the house was used as the office of the provisional president by Sun Yat-sen. The first cabinet council of the provisional government was held in the house as well. Since 1927, the house was served as the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Military_Affairs_Commission" title="Military Affairs Commission">Military Affairs Commission</a>, which grasped the real power of the Nationalist Government. Today this yellow house is also a popular tuorist attraction in the Presidential Palace.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_East_Garden">The East Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: The East Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG/220px-Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG/330px-Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG/440px-Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Garden1-of-zhongtongfu.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The East Garden</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The East Garden, also known as the Restored Garden, was the additional garden of the viceroy's office. During the period of Taiping Rebellion, it became the east garden of the Celestial Palace. After the troops of Qing dynasty captured the palace, the east garden was totally destroyed. When the Nationalist Government came to Nanjing, some minor office buildings attached to the Executive Yuan were built on the site, which were all demolished inthe 2000s. In 2003, the garden was partly rebuilt according to the historical information. Some other buildings, like the warehouse, were rebuilt at the same time.</p>
<p>The Executive Houses, the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Yuan" title="Executive Yuan">Executive Yuan</a> from 1928 to 1937 were located on the north side of the garden.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Houses">Executive Houses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Palace_(Nanjing)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Executive Houses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Executive_Yuan_Building,_North_Block,_Nanjing,_2016.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Executive_Yuan_Building%2C_North_Block%2C_Nanjing%2C_2016.jpg/220px-Executive_Yuan_Building%2C_North_Block%2C_Nanjing%2C_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Executive_Yuan_Building%2C_North_Block%2C_Nanjing%2C_2016.jpg/330px-Executive_Yuan_Building%2C_North_Block%2C_Nanjing%2C_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Executive_Yuan_Building%2C_North_Block%2C_Nanjing%2C_2016.jpg/440px-Executive_Yuan_Building%2C_North_Block%2C_Nanjing%2C_2016.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Executive_Yuan_Building,_North_Block,_Nanjing,_2016.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
North Executive House</div>

</div>
<p>The Executive Houses were the seat of the Executive Yuan from 1928 to 1937. In November 1937, the Executive Yuan was moved to the city of Chungking. The houses were served as the offices of Ministry of Railways and Ministry of Transportation and Communication in the period of Wang Jingwei regime and later served as the seat of Ministry of Social Affairs and Ministry of Water Conservancy.</p>
<p>The Executive Houses were divided into two blocks. The North Executive House, also known as the North Block of the Executive Yuan Building, mainly housed the office of the Premier from 1928 to 1934 until the South Executive House was finished working. The South Executive House, also known as the South Block of the Executive Yuan Building, mainly housed the office from 1934 to 1937 until the Nationalists lost Nanking and fled to Chungking. Premiers including Chiang Kai-shek, Wang Jingwei, <a href="/wiki/H._H._Kung" title="H. H. Kung">Kung Hsiang-hsi</a> and <a href="/wiki/T._V._Soong" title="T. V. Soong">Soong Tse-ven</a> once worked in the premier's office in the South Block.</p>

<p>The building is accessible within walking distance north of <a href="/wiki/Daxinggong_Station" title="Daxinggong Station">Daxinggong Station</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nanjing_Metro" title="Nanjing Metro">Nanjing Metro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
