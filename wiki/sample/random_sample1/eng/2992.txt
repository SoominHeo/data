<p><b>Nankai Broadcasting Co., Ltd</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南海放送株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nankai Hōsō Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, also known as <b>RNB</b>, is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_network" title="Broadcast network">broadcast network</a> affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Nippon_News_Network" title="Nippon News Network">Nippon News Network</a> (NNN). Their headquarters are located in <a href="/wiki/Matsuyama,_Ehime" title="Matsuyama, Ehime">Matsuyama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ehime_Prefecture" title="Ehime Prefecture">Ehime Prefecture</a>.</p>

<ul>
<li>TV: <a href="/wiki/Nippon_News_Network" title="Nippon News Network">Nippon News Network</a> (NNN)</li>
<li>RADIO: <a href="/wiki/Japan_Radio_Network" title="Japan Radio Network">Japan Radio Network</a> (JRN), <a href="/wiki/National_Radio_Network_(Japan)" title="National Radio Network (Japan)">National Radio Network</a> (NRN)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nankai_Broadcasting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>August 1, 1953: Nankai Broadcasting's radio license is applied.</li>
<li>September 28, 1953: Nankai Broadcasting is established.</li>
<li>October 1, 1953: Nankai Broadcasting commences broadcasting.</li>
<li>December 1, 1958: Television broadcasts commence.</li>
<li>October 1, 1964: Color broadcasts commence.</li>
<li>December 10, 1969: All Fuji TV shows move to the newly launched FNN station EBC.</li>
<li>May 13, 1970: Nankai Broadcasting exchanges footage of the Setouchi Sea Jack incident with Hiroshima Telecasting.</li>
<li>March 15, 1980: AM stereo broadcasts are conducted.</li>
<li>February 21, 1988: RNB transmits the Ehime Marathon for the first time.</li>
<li>July 19, 1990: RNB starts dual audio broadcasting for certain shows.</li>
<li>March 2000: <b>Club N</b>, a mobile site, opens.</li>
<li>June 21, 2006: Digital terrestrial broadcasts commence.</li>
<li>July 24, 2011: Analog terrestrial broadcasts conclude.</li>
<li>April 2012: RNB opens their official Facebook page.</li>
<li>December 1, 2014: A new FM station, <b>Fnam</b>, launches.</li>
</ul>
