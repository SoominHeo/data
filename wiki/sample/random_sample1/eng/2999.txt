<p><b>Antarctican dollars</b> are collectors' items produced by the "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctica_Overseas_Exchange_Office&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Antarctica Overseas Exchange Office (page does not exist)">Antarctica Overseas Exchange Office</a>" in the appearance of a national money for the continent of <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>. The bills are not <a href="/wiki/Legal_tender" title="Legal tender">legal tender</a> in Antarctica nor any other continent or nation. They are mostly sold for the equivalent of their face value in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Dollars" title="United States Dollars">United States Dollars</a>.</p>
<p>The Antarctica Overseas Exchange Office states it uses a portion of all proceeds from the sale of Antarctican dollars to fund organizations seeking to undertake research and humanitarian projects in the Antarctic region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Symes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Symes-1">[1]</a></sup> The unofficial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Currency_code" title="Currency code">currency code</a> "AQD" is based on "AQ", the <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1_alpha-2" title="ISO 3166-1 alpha-2">ISO 3166-1 alpha-2</a> country code for Antarctica.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_series">2007 series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctican_dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2007 series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two types of the 1 dollar; The general issue of March 1, 2007 and the commemorative issue of November 23, 2007 noting the sinking of the Antarctic vessel "M/S Explorer".</p>
<p>Two Dollars; July 30, 2007</p>
<p>Three Dollars; General issue of March 1, 2007 and the commemorative issue of December 14, 2007 noting the International Polar Year 2007-2008.</p>
<p>These issues have a 12/31/2012 expiration date.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_series">2008 series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctican_dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2008 series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>1, 2, 3, 5, and 20 dollar notes have been issued in polymer.</p>
<p>50 and 100 dollars were designed for the 2010 release.</p>
<p>The 1, 2, 3 and 5 dollar issues have a 12/31/2012 expiration date.</p>
<p>The 20 dollar issue has an expiration of 12/31/2013.</p>
<p>As of November 1, 2009, the 50 and 100 dollar issues have not been circulated. This means that any notes out there are issued notes of the 2001 edition or are non-original specimens with no value whatsoever.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_series">2009 series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antarctican_dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2009 series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>10 dollars first issued March 29, 2009 and expires in 2016.</p>
<p>The 200110 dollar issue has an expiration date of 12/31/2010.</p>
