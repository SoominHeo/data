<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sea Mither</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Sea Mither</b>, or <b>Mither of the Sea</b>, is a mythical being of <a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orcadian</a> folklore that lives in the sea during summer, when she confines the demonic <a href="/wiki/Nuckelavee" title="Nuckelavee">nuckelavee</a> to the ocean depths. Each spring she battles with her arch-enemy Teran, another spirit of Orcadian legend capable of causing severe winter storms, to gain control of the seas and the weather. Eventually Sea Mither overcomes Teran and sends him to the depths of the ocean, but the effort of keeping him confined there along with her other benevolent labours during the summer exhaust her, until in the autumn Teran takes advantage of her weakness to wrest control from her once again.</p>
<p>Stories of the Sea Mither and Teran are among Orkney's oldest legends, perhaps invented to explain the vagaries of weather and other naturally occurring events. In <a href="/wiki/Shetland" title="Shetland">Shetland</a> fishermen petition Sea Mither to afford them protection from the <a href="/wiki/Devil" title="Devil">Devil</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_Mither&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Mither</i> is defined in the <i><a href="/wiki/Dictionary_of_the_Older_Scottish_Tongue" title="Dictionary of the Older Scottish Tongue">Dictionary of the Older Scottish Tongue</a></i> as the Scots variant of "mother",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DOSL_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DOSL-1">[1]</a></sup> which may particularly reflect oral Orcadian use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Orkneyjar_mither_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Orkneyjar_mither-2">[2]</a></sup> The name of her opponent, <i>Teran</i>, is local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orkney_dialect" title="Orkney dialect">Orkney dialect</a> meaning "furious anger",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070-3">[3]</a></sup> and may be a derivative of <i>tyrren</i>, <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Norse</a> for "angry".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Orkneyjar_mither_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Orkneyjar_mither-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Folk_beliefs">Folk beliefs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_Mither&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Folk beliefs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Description_and_common_attributes">Description and common attributes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_Mither&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description and common attributes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sea Mither is a spirit of summer days that quells the turbulent sea waters around the northern isles of Scotland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019-4">[4]</a></sup> Shetland islanders, particularly fishermen, seek her protection from the <a href="/wiki/Devil" title="Devil">Devil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200023_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200023-5">[5]</a></sup> Control of the seas is maintained by Teran, the spirit of winter, until Sea Mither arrives around the time of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vernal_equinox" title="Vernal equinox">vernal equinox</a> in mid-March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070-3">[3]</a></sup> Both spirits are invisible to humans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071-6">[6]</a></sup> Teran is her arch-enemy and the pair fight bitterly, often for weeks as she tries to gain control.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070-3">[3]</a></sup> Their arguments cause gale force winds and heavy tumultuous seas as she tries to wrest control from him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070-3">[3]</a></sup> Teran's screeches are carried by the howling gales as the two spirits try to oust each other.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019-4">[4]</a></sup> The period of the spring combat between the pair is termed the <i>Vore tullye</i> or the "spring struggle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070-3">[3]</a></sup> Eventually Sea Mither overcomes Teran, relegating him to the depths of the ocean;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020-7">[7]</a></sup> inclement summer weather is caused by Teran's attempts to escape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>During summer months the Sea Mither also keeps the demonic <a href="/wiki/Nuckelavee" title="Nuckelavee">nuckelavee</a> creature confined,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison1891131_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison1891131-8">[8]</a></sup> and undertakes benevolent labours: she empowers aquatic creatures with the ability to reproduce;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071-6">[6]</a></sup> warms and calms the seas;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019-4">[4]</a></sup> and instils a softer song-like quality to the gentle summer breeze.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071-6">[6]</a></sup> According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folklorist" title="Folklorist">folklorist</a> and Orkney resident, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Traill_Dennison" title="Walter Traill Dennison">Walter Traill Dennison</a>, during Sea Mither's reign in summer the conditions reported by islanders may have "tempted one to believe that the Orkney archipelago had become the islands of the blessed."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071-6">[6]</a></sup> But the continual work she undertakes to keep everything calm and the strain of maintaining control over Teran gradually tires her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>As autumn approaches, Teran takes advantage of Sea Mither's exhaustion to break free, and conflict between the two starts again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071-6">[6]</a></sup> The power struggles cause the weather to change with darkening skies and howling winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020-7">[7]</a></sup> This time, Teran triumphs in the conflict termed the <i>Gore vellye</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189071-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[a]</a></sup> Control of the ocean and weather is returned to Teran and Sea Mither is forced to leave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200020-7">[7]</a></sup> No details are given as to where she spends the winter, but during the storms caused by Teran the fishermen were consoled that Sea Mither would return refreshed and powerful in the spring, to again oust Teran from his malevolent grip over the seas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200071_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200071-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_Mither&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Orcadian tales were strongly influenced by Scandinavian mythology with a blending of traditional <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> stories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMuir201410_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMuir201410-11">[10]</a></sup> Folklorist and writer <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Marwick" title="Ernest Marwick">Ernest Marwick</a> describes the Sea Mither and Teran as "pure personifications of nature."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019-4">[4]</a></sup> Several ancient myths were based upon the natural elements of the turbulent and ever changing sea surrounding Orkney,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OJ_The_Sea_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-OJ_The_Sea-12">[11]</a></sup> but the stories of the two spirits are among the oldest legends on the islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarwick200019-4">[4]</a></sup> People had to be able to explain the vagaries of weather and other natural life cycles without the benefit of science; Traill Dennison hypothesises that this is why "the imagination of some half savage" may have formed the foundations of the myth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraill_Dennison189070-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
