<p><b>Namdaemunno</b> is a major <a href="/wiki/Thoroughfare" title="Thoroughfare">thoroughfare</a> in the central districts of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and a two-way road consisting of 8 lanes. With a 2 km length and a 40∼50m width, Namdaemunno originates at <a href="/wiki/Bosingak" title="Bosingak">Bosingak</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jongno-gu" title="Jongno-gu">Jongno-gu</a> and terminates at <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Station" title="Seoul Station">Seoul Station</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jung-gu,_Seoul" title="Jung-gu, Seoul">Jung-gu</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyber_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-encyber-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Historical buildings on this street include the <a href="/wiki/Gwangtonggwan" title="Gwangtonggwan">Gwangtonggwan</a>, the oldest continuously operating bank building in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was registered as one of city's protected monuments on March 5, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tong_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tong-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
