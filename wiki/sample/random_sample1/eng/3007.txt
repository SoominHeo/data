<p><b>South Bohemia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>: <span lang="cs" xml:lang="cs"><i>Jihočeský kraj</i></span>) is an administrative unit (<i><a href="/wiki/Regions_of_the_Czech_Republic" title="Regions of the Czech Republic">kraj</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, located mostly in the southern part of its historical land of <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemia</a>, with a small part in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Moravia" title="Moravia">Moravia</a>. The western part of the South Bohemian Region is former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prachens" title="Prachens">Prachens</a> (Prácheňsko), a huge archaic region with distinctive features with its capital, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%ADsek" title="Písek">Písek</a>. In 2011, there were 623 municipalities in the region, whereof 54 had a status of town.</p>

<p>The region borders (from the west clockwise) the regions <a href="/wiki/Plze%C5%88_Region" title="Plzeň Region">Plzeň</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_Bohemian_Region" title="Central Bohemian Region">Central Bohemia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vysocina_Region" title="Vysocina Region">Vysocina</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Moravian_Region" title="South Moravian Region">South Moravia</a>. To the south it borders <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> and Germany. Until 30 May 2001, the region was named as <span lang="cs" xml:lang="cs"><i>Budějovický kraj</i></span> or <span lang="cs" xml:lang="cs"><i>Českobudějovický kraj</i></span>, after its capital, <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%A9_Bud%C4%9Bjovice" title="České Budějovice">České Budějovice</a>. The South Bohemian Region is further divided into seven districts: <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%A9_Bud%C4%9Bjovice_District" title="České Budějovice District">České Budějovice District</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%BD_Krumlov_District" title="Český Krumlov District">Český Krumlov District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jind%C5%99ich%C5%AFv_Hradec_District" title="Jindřichův Hradec District">Jindřichův Hradec District</a>, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%ADsek_District" title="Písek District">Písek District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prachatice_District" title="Prachatice District">Prachatice District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strakonice_District" title="Strakonice District">Strakonice District</a> and <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A1bor_District" title="Tábor District">Tábor District</a>.</p>
<p>Thanks to its geographical location and natural conditions the region belongs to the areas where the first settlements began to appear in the distant past. In the past centuries, the South Bohemia was famous for fishpond cultivation and forestry. The region has been industrialized since the beginning of the twentieth century. Nowadays, it is an attractive destination due to its natural and culturally historical richness. The travel industry has been recently the fastest growing industry in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The region was established based on the constitutional lax No. 347/97 of Collections concerning the formation of higher territorial administrative units. The region and its authorities are specified by Act No. 129-2000 of Collections concerning regions, which came into effect on the day of the regional authorities elections, or on January 1, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population_and_area">Population and area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Bohemian_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Population and area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The total area of the region is 10,056 km² which is 12.8% of the total area of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>. As of September 2013, South Bohemia's population is 636,459 and with only 63 people per 1 km² the region has the lowest population density in the whole country.</p>
<p>64.2% of region’s population lives in towns or cities. One third of the inhabitants live in the five largest municipalities. Only 4% of region’s population lives in municipalities with less than 200 inhabitants.</p>
<p>In 2011, the average age in the region is 41.2 years. Approximately 11% of inhabitants who were 15 years or older had a university degree (in 2001 this was 8%). According to 2011 census, 20.6% of inhabitants in the region believe in god (however, almost half of the people did not answer this question). <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Bohemian_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg/220px-Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg/330px-Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg/440px-Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ple%C5%A1n%C3%A9_jezero.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Plešné jezero</i> (Plešné Lake)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>South Bohemia consists of the South Bohemian Basin, České Budějovice Basin and Třeboň Basin. The <a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Forest" title="Bohemian Forest">Bohemian Forest</a> (Czech: <i>Šumava</i>) is a low range of mountains situated in the south-west. Other highlands that can be found in the region are the Brdy Highlands (in the very northern part of the region), the Central-Bohemian Highlands (Czech: <i>Středočeská vrchovina</i>, in the northern part of the region), the <a href="/wiki/Bohemian-Moravian_Highlands" title="Bohemian-Moravian Highlands">Bohemian-Moravian Highlands</a> (Czech: <i>Českomoravská vrchovina</i>, eastern part of the region) and the Novohradské Mountains (in the south of the region). The highest elevation in the region is the 1,378-metre (4,521 ft) high <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Plech%C3%BD&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Plechý (page does not exist)">Plechý</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Forest" title="Bohemian Forest">Bohemian Forest</a>, the lowest elevation with 350 metres <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">above sea level</a> is at the <a href="/wiki/Orl%C3%ADk_Dam" title="Orlík Dam">Orlík Dam</a>.</p>
<p>The region is located in the drainage basin of <a href="/wiki/Vltava" title="Vltava">Vltava</a> river. Other significant rivers are <a href="/wiki/Mal%C5%A1e" title="Malše">Malše</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lu%C5%BEnice" title="Lužnice">Lužnice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otava_(river)" title="Otava (river)">Otava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ne%C5%BE%C3%A1rka" title="Nežárka">Nežárka</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lomnice_(river)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lomnice (river) (page does not exist)">Lomnice</a>. South Bohemia is famous for its countless ponds. In the past, more than seven thousand ponds were established across the region. With its 489 ha <a href="/wiki/Ro%C5%BEmberk_Pond" title="Rožmberk Pond">Rožmberk</a> is the largest one, followed by Bezdrev (450 ha) and Horusice pond (415 ha). In the 20th century, a series of dams was constructed on Vltava river. <a href="/wiki/Lipno_Dam" title="Lipno Dam">Lipno Dam</a> is the largest dam in the Czech Republic and has area of 4,870ha. Other dams in the region are <a href="/wiki/Orl%C3%ADk_Dam" title="Orlík Dam">Orlík Dam</a>, Římov Dam and Hněvkovice Dam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A big part of the <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0umava_National_Park" title="Šumava National Park">Šumava National Park</a> is situated in South Bohemia. The Bohemian Forest is a popular holiday destination because it is excellent hiking country. Most interesting natural and cultural sights are connected with more than 500 km of summer marked trails and many bike trails.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Bohemian_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The climate in South Bohemia is of a transitional Central European type. It is affected alternatively by an oceanic influence from the west, and a continental influence from the east. Therefore, the weather can be variable. Most of the South Bohemian region belongs to the mild, warm and wet zone and at altitudes above 750 m this passes to mild and cool. The warmest month is usually July, with temperatures averaging between 17 and 18 °C in valley areas. In higher localities (over 900 m) the temperatures can drop below 14 °C. Days with temperatures above 25 °C are most frequently in valley-basins, and the area around the confluence of the rivers Lužnice and Vltava (Moldau): on average there are 40-50 such days a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Bohemian_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2560" data-file-width="1049" height="244" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg/100px-Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg/150px-Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg/200px-Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Budvar_UK_retouched.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
500ml bottle of Budweiser Budvar, as marketed in the UK</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 2010, the region produced 5% of the national <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GDP" title="GDP">GDP</a>. The GDP per capita is 84% of the national average, which is the 8th highest among all regions. In 2011, the business sector in the South Bohemian Region comprises 159,000 entities, 114,000 of which were sole traders. There are more than 300 thousand people employed in the region, whereof 31% in industry, 13% in trade and 10% in construction sector. The average salary in the region in September 2013 was CZK 21,768 (approximately EUR 850). The unemployment rate in September 2013 was 6.05%.</p>
<p>The Region does not rank among key industrial areas of the Czech Republic. The industrial production is concentrated particularly in the area of <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%A9_Bud%C4%9Bjovice" title="České Budějovice">České Budějovice</a> and in <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A1bor_District" title="Tábor District">Tábor District</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strakonice_District" title="Strakonice District">Strakonice District</a>. Food and drink processing industries plays a significant role in the region. Other important sectors are the automotive industry, production of machinery and appliances, and also the textile and clothing industries. Recently, the travel industry has become an important sector in the region.</p>
<p>The Czech Academy of Sciences, whose institutes operate all over the region, provides a good base for the development of science. These institutes focus mostly on biology and ecology. Scientific work is also part of the activities of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_South_Bohemia" title="University of South Bohemia">University of South Bohemia</a> (Czech: Jihočeská univerzita) with its headquarters in České Budějovice and Jindřichův Hradec.</p>
<p>Approximately 11% of the national agricultural production is produced in the South Bohemian Region. The agricultural sector focuses on plant production, mostly on growing cereals, oil plants and potatoes. In animal husbandry, the breeding of cattle and pigs prevails. Fishpond cultivation has a long tradition in South Bohemia. Fish husbandry in the total area of 25 000 ha makes up about 50% of the total national production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Bohemian_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Holasovice1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="430" data-file-width="1321" height="81" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Holasovice1.jpg/250px-Holasovice1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Holasovice1.jpg/375px-Holasovice1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Holasovice1.jpg/500px-Holasovice1.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Holasovice1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rustic baroque in <a href="/wiki/Hola%C5%A1ovice" title="Holašovice">Holašovice</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The region is famous for its small villages with a pond in the middle. These are generally built in the style of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rustic_Baroque&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rustic Baroque (page does not exist)">Rustic Baroque</a>, also known as South Bohemian Baroque:</p>
<p>"Rustic Baroque is a term for the unique architecture of South Bohemia. The local folk bricklayer masters Martin Paták and František Šoch created a new type of South Bohemian farmhouse with an ornate frontispiece in the middle of the 19th century. A typical building of this style is a massive rustic farmhouse with two richly decorated frontispieces, which are joined by an arched gate with small doors. The marshland frontispieces are beautifully decorated, they have lavish contours, the surface is usually divided by allusive decorative columns, completed by arches, stylised hearts, four-leafed clovers, meadow flowers."</p>
