<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Southern Romance languages</h1>

<p>The <b>Southern Romance languages</b> make up a hypothetical sub-group of the family of <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">Romance languages</a> suggested by <a href="/wiki/Ethnologue" title="Ethnologue">Ethnologue</a> but with little support among linguists.</p>
<p>According to Ethnologue, the language group would include <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">Sardinian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">Corsican</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallurese_language" title="Gallurese language">Gallurese</a> dialect, and the <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">dialects</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sassarese_language" title="Sassarese language">Sassarese</a>. In mainstream linguistics, Corsican is instead considered closest to the <a href="/wiki/Tuscan_dialect" title="Tuscan dialect">Tuscan dialect</a> of Italian and closer to standard <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> than many other <a href="/wiki/Regional_Italian" title="Regional Italian">Italian dialects</a>, while Sardinian is considered a different language in its own right.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification.5B1.5D">Classification<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Romance_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification[1]">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>Corsican</b>:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">Corsican</a> (<i>corsu</i>) - Corsica;</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallurese" title="Gallurese">Gallurese</a> (<i>gadduresu</i>) - Sardinia;</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sassarese_language" title="Sassarese language">Sassarese</a></b> (<i>sassaresu</i>), transitional between Corsican and Sardinian - Sardinia.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">Sardinian</a></b> (<i>sardu</i>):
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logudorese_language" title="Logudorese language">Logudorese</a> (<i>sardu logudoresu</i>) - Sardinia;
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logudorese_language" title="Logudorese language">Nuorese</a> (<i>sardu nugoresu</i>) - Sardinia;</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campidanese_language" title="Campidanese language">Campidanese</a> (<i>sardu campidanesu</i>) - Sardinia.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
