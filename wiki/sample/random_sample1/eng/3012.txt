<p>The <b>Southern Governorate</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">المحافظة الجنوبية</span>‎‎; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_transliteration" title="Arabic transliteration">transliterated</a>: Al-Muḥāfaẓat al-Janūbīyah) has the largest area of the four <a href="/wiki/Governorate" title="Governorate">governorates</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>, and the smallest population (91,450).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It includes parts of Bahrain's old municipalities - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Mintaqah_al_Gharbiyah" title="Al Mintaqah al Gharbiyah">Al Mintaqah al Gharbiyah</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ar_Rifa%27_wa_al_Mintaqah_al_Janubiyah" title="Ar Rifa' wa al Mintaqah al Janubiyah">Ar Rifa' wa al Mintaqah al Janubiyah</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hawar_Islands" title="Hawar Islands">Juzur Hawar</a> (the Hawar Islands). It is the least populated of the regions on Bahrain and the least economically productive, per capita.</p>
<p>The Governor of the Southern Governorate is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shaikh_Abdullah_Bin_Rashid_Al_Khalifa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shaikh Abdullah Bin Rashid Al Khalifa (page does not exist)">Shaikh Abdullah Bin Rashid Al Khalifa</a>, the son of The Minister of Interior Lt. Gen. Sh. Rashid bin Abdulla Al Khalifa.</p>
