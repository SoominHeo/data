<p>The <b>Southwestern Front</b> was a name given to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Front_(Soviet_Army)" title="Front (Soviet Army)">Front</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Army_group" title="Army group">Army group</a> sized military <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formation_(military)" title="Formation (military)">formation</a>) by the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army" title="Imperial Russian Army">Imperial Russian Army</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>, by the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a>, and by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.</p>
<p>The Southwestern Front in this article describes several distinct organizations during the Second World War. This Red Army formation was first created on June 22, 1941 from the <b>Kiev Special Military District</b>. The western boundary of the front in June 1941 was 865Â km long, from the <a href="/wiki/Pripyat_River" title="Pripyat River">Pripyat River</a> and the town of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vlodava&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vlodava (page does not exist)">Wlodawa</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prut_River" title="Prut River">Prut River</a> and the town of <a href="/wiki/Lipcani" title="Lipcani">Lipkany</a> at the border with Romania. It connected to the north with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_Western_Front" title="Soviet Western Front">Western Front</a>, which extended to the Lithuanian border, and to the south with the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Front_(Soviet_Union)" title="Southern Front (Soviet Union)">Southern Front</a>, which extended to the city of <a href="/wiki/Odessa" title="Odessa">Odessa</a> on the Black Sea.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operational_history">Operational history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southwestern_Front_(Soviet_Union)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operational history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Southwestern Front was on the main axis of attack by the German <a href="/wiki/Army_Group_South" title="Army Group South">Army Group South</a> during <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a>. At the outbreak of war with Germany, the Front contained the Soviet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/5th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="5th Army (Soviet Union)">5th</a>, <a href="/wiki/6th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="6th Army (Soviet Union)">6th</a>, <a href="/wiki/26th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="26th Army (Soviet Union)">26th</a>, and <a href="/wiki/12th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="12th Army (Soviet Union)">12th</a> Armies along the frontier. <a href="/wiki/16th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="16th Army (Soviet Union)">16th</a> and <a href="/wiki/19th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="19th Army (Soviet Union)">19th</a> Armies were in reserve behind the forward forces. These forces took part in the tank battles in western <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> and were surrounded and destroyed at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Uman" title="Battle of Uman">Battle of Uman</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kiev_(1941)" title="Battle of Kiev (1941)">Battle of Kiev (1941)</a> in August and September 1941.</p>
<p>The Front was immediately re-established with new forces. During the period of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Moscow" title="Battle of Moscow">Battle of Moscow</a> it was under the command of Marshal Timoshenko, and included from north to south the <a href="/wiki/40th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="40th Army (Soviet Union)">40th</a>, <a href="/wiki/21st_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="21st Army (Soviet Union)">21st</a>, <a href="/wiki/38th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="38th Army (Soviet Union)">38th</a> and <a href="/wiki/6th_Army_(Soviet_Union)" title="6th Army (Soviet Union)">6th</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Soviet_armies" title="List of Soviet armies">Armies</a>. It was formally disbanded on July 12, 1942 and the forces transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Stalingrad_Front" title="Stalingrad Front">Stalingrad Front</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Front_(Soviet_Union)" title="Southern Front (Soviet Union)">Southern Front</a>.</p>
<p>The Front was reformed from reserve armies on October 22, 1942. It was renamed the <a href="/wiki/3rd_Ukrainian_Front" title="3rd Ukrainian Front">3rd Ukrainian Front</a> on October 20, 1943. 3rd Ukrainian Front's first operations were the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Dnieper" title="Battle of the Dnieper">Battle of the Dnieper</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kiev_(1943)" title="Battle of Kiev (1943)">Battle of Kiev (1943)</a>.</p>
