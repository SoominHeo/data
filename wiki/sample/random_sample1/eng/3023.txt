<p>The <b>South Asian Games</b> (<b>SAF Games</b>, <b>SAG</b>, or <b>SA games</b>, &amp; formerly known as <b>South Asian Federation Games</b>) are a biennial <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> held among the athletes from <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. The governing body of these games is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=South_Asian_Sports_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="South Asian Sports Council (page does not exist)">South Asian Sports Council</a> (SASC), formed in 1983. At present, SAG are joined by eight members namely <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">Bhutan</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.</p>
<p>The first South Asian Games were hosted by <a href="/wiki/Kathmandu" title="Kathmandu">Kathmandu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> in 1984 and have since been held every two years except for some occasions. In 2004, it was decided in the 32nd meeting of South Asian Sports Council to rename the games from South Asian Federation Games to South Asian Games as the officials believed the word <i><a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">Federation</a></i> was diminishing the emphasis on event and acting as a barrier in attracting crowd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> These Games are often hyped as the South Asian version of <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>. <a href="/wiki/2016_South_Asian_Games" title="2016 South Asian Games">XII South Asian Games</a> are being held at Guwahati and Shillong from 5 February to 16 February 2016.</p>
