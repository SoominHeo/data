<p><b>Camarines Sur</b> (<a href="/wiki/Central_Bikol_language" title="Central Bikol language">Central Bikol</a>: <span lang="bcl"><i>Habagatan na Camarines</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a>: <span lang="fil"><i>Timog Camarines</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Philippines" title="Provinces of the Philippines">province</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Region" title="Bicol Region">Bicol</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_the_Philippines" title="Regions of the Philippines">Region</a> in <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">Luzon</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Pili,_Camarines_Sur" title="Pili, Camarines Sur">Pili</a> and the province borders <a href="/wiki/Camarines_Norte" title="Camarines Norte">Camarines Norte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quezon" title="Quezon">Quezon</a> to the northwest, and <a href="/wiki/Albay" title="Albay">Albay</a> to the south. To the east lies the island province of <a href="/wiki/Catanduanes" title="Catanduanes">Catanduanes</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Maqueda_Channel" title="Maqueda Channel">Maqueda Channel</a>.</p>
<p>Camarines Sur is the largest among the six provinces in the Bicol Region both by population and land area. Its territory includes two cities: <a href="/wiki/Naga,_Camarines_Sur" title="Naga, Camarines Sur">Naga</a>, the lone chartered city, as the province's and the region's religious, cultural, financial, commercial, industrial and business center; and <a href="/wiki/Iriga" title="Iriga">Iriga</a>, a component city, as the center of the Rinconada area and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rinconada_Bikol" title="Rinconada Bikol">Riŋkonāda</a> Language. Within the province lies <a href="/wiki/Lake_Buhi" title="Lake Buhi">Lake Buhi</a>, where the smallest commercially harvested fish, the <a href="/wiki/Sinarapan" title="Sinarapan">Sinarapan</a> (<i>Mistichthys luzonensis</i>), can be found.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camarines_Sur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 1569, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luis_Enriquez_de_Guzman" title="Luis Enriquez de Guzman">Luis Enriquez de Guzman</a>, a member of the expedition led by Maestro de Campo <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mateo_de_Saz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mateo de Saz (page does not exist)">Mateo de Saz</a> and Captain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_de_Goiti" title="Martin de Goiti">Martin de Goiti</a>, led a group which crossed from <a href="/wiki/Burias_(island)" title="Burias (island)">Burias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ticao_Island" title="Ticao Island">Ticao</a> islands and landed on a coastal settlement called <i>Ibalon</i> in what is now the province of <a href="/wiki/Sorsogon" title="Sorsogon">Sorsogon</a>. From this point another expedition was sent to explore the interior and founded the town of <i>Camalig</i>.</p>
<p>In 1573, Spanish <i>conquistador</i> <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Salcedo" title="Juan de Salcedo">Juan de Salcedo</a> penetrated the <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Peninsula" title="Bicol Peninsula">Bicol Peninsula</a> from the north as made it as far south as Libon, establishing the settlement of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_de_Libon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Santiago de Libon (page does not exist)">Santiago de Libon</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jose_Maria_Pe%C3%B1aranda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jose Maria Peñaranda (page does not exist)">Jose Maria Peñaranda</a>, the first governor of <a href="/wiki/Albay" title="Albay">Albay</a> and a military engineer, was made <i>coregidor</i> of the province on May 14, 1834. He constructed public buildings and built roads and bridges.</p>
<p>The entire <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Peninsula" title="Bicol Peninsula">Bicol Peninsula</a> was organized as one province with two divisions, Camarines in the northwest and Ibalon in the southeast. In 1636, the two <i>partido</i>s were separated.</p>
<p>Known centuries ago as the <i>Tierra de Camarines</i>, the province is distinctly Spanish-founded settlement. Its name having been derived from <i>camaronchones</i> or <i>camarines</i>, a Spanish word for <i>kamalig</i> referring to small nipa or bamboo-made <a href="/wiki/Nipa_hut" title="Nipa hut">huts</a> by the natives.</p>
<p>In 1574, <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Philippines" title="Governor-General of the Philippines">Governor General</a> <a href="/wiki/Guido_de_Lavezaris" title="Guido de Lavezaris">Guido de Lavezaris</a> referred Camarines Sur to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Spain" title="King of Spain">King of Spain</a> as <i>Los Camarines</i>, after the abundance of <i>camarin</i>s-rice granaries - which were conspicuous features of the area.</p>
<p>Spanish colonizers later subjugated its people and denominated the area into two distinct aggrupations. The southern portion comprising the area south of the present town of <a href="/wiki/Camalig,_Albay" title="Camalig, Albay">Camalig</a> (in Albay), Sorsogon, the islands of <a href="/wiki/Masbate_Island" title="Masbate Island">Masbate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catanduanes" title="Catanduanes">Catanduanes</a>, and the area, which is now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partido_(Philippines)" title="Partido (Philippines)">Partido</a> in present day, then called <i>Partido de Ibalon</i>. The northern, upper portion, which included from the present day Camalig town in Albay, and all towns of Camarines Sur and <a href="/wiki/Camarines_Norte" title="Camarines Norte">Camarines Norte</a>, was called <i>Partido de Camarines</i>.</p>
<p><i>Partido de Camarines</i> was partitioned into <i>Camarines Sur</i> and <i>Camarines Norte</i> in 1829, and thereafter underwent fusion, annexations and re-partitions until March 19, 1919 when two provinces, jointly called <i><a href="/wiki/Ambos_Camarines" title="Ambos Camarines">Ambos Camarines</a></i>, were finally separated with their present boundaries by decree of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=First_Philippine_Legislature&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="First Philippine Legislature (page does not exist)">First Philippine Legislature</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Revolution" title="Philippine Revolution">Philippine Revolution</a> started in Ambos Camarines when <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Elias_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Elias Angeles (page does not exist)">Elias Angeles</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Felix_Plazo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Felix Plazo (page does not exist)">Felix Plazo</a>, Filipino corporals in the Spanish Army, sided with revolutionists and fought the local Spanish forces on September 17, 1898. Governor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vicente_Zaidin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vicente Zaidin (page does not exist)">Vicente Zaidin</a> capitulated to the revolutionists on the following day. With the arrival of General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vicente_Lukban" title="Vicente Lukban">Vicente Lukban</a>, the revolutionary government in the Bicol Region was established.</p>
<p>The American forces occupied the Bicol Peninsula in January 1900. In March of the same year. General <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=John_M._Bell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="John M. Bell (page does not exist)">John M. Bell</a> was made the military governor of the southeastern Luzon. Civil government was finally established in Ambos Camarines in April 1901.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Camarines Sur came under <a href="/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_the_Philippines" title="Japanese occupation of the Philippines">Japanese occupation</a> in late December 1941, following the capture of Naga City on December 18, a few days after the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Legaspi" title="Japanese invasion of Legaspi">Japanese invasion of Legaspi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hyper_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hyper-3">[3]</a></sup> Guerrilla units were organized by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wenceslao_Q._Vinzons" title="Wenceslao Q. Vinzons">Wenceslao Q. Vinzons</a> that waged underground operations against the Japanese troops stationed in Camarines Sur. After the capture of Vinzons on July 8, 1942, the guerrilla movement was carried on by Lieutenant <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Francisco_Boayes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Francisco Boayes (page does not exist)">Francisco Boayes</a> and by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tangcong_Vaca_Guerrilla_Unit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tangcong Vaca Guerrilla Unit (page does not exist)">Tangcong Vaca Guerrilla Unit</a> organized by Elias Madrid, Juan Miranda and Leon Aureus. In April 1945, Camarines Sur was finally liberated from the Japanese invaders against the combined Filipino and American troops in 1945.</p>
<p>On March 8, 1942, the famous Tangcong Vaca Guerrilla Unit (TVGU) was organized in San Nicolas, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canaman" title="Canaman">Canaman</a> with Juan Miranda as the Commanding Officer, Leon Aureus as the Executive Officer and Elias Madrid as the Finance Officer. Among the numerous Canamanons who joined-up soon afterwards either in the unit’s intelligence or combat components were Jose and Antonio Madrid, Mamerto Sibulo, Andres Fortaleza, Marcos Severo, Damaso Avenilla, Federico Crescini, Nicolas Vargas, Venancio Begino, Eugenio Ragodon, Juan Pachica, Santiago Amaro, Jose Gervas, Pedro Angeles, Aproniano Lopez, Andres Alzate, Modesto Sanchez, Blas Alcantara, Andres Aguilar, Florencio Frondozo, Alfredo de la Torre and Flaviano Estrada.</p>
<p>The military general headquarters and military camp bases of the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Commonwealth_Army" title="Philippine Commonwealth Army">Philippine Commonwealth Army</a> were active on January 3, 1942 to June 30, 1946 and the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Constabulary" title="Philippine Constabulary">Philippine Constabulary</a> was active on October 28, 1944 to June 30, 1946 in the province of Camarines Sur. The Filipino soldiers of the Philippine Commonwealth Army and Philippine Constabulary were spearheading the local military special operations in Bicol Region with the Bicolano guerrilla units decisively aiding them.</p>
<p>In 1945, Filipino and American troops along with the Bicolano guerrillas, liberated Camarines Sur from the Japanese forces towards the end of WWII. Local Filipino troops of the 5th, 51st, 52nd, 53rd, 55th, 56th and 57th Infantry Division of the Philippine Commonwealth Army and the 5th Infantry Regiment of the Philippine Constabulary were involved in the liberation efforts.</p>
<p>Naga City, the former capital of Camarines Sur, was founded in 1573 as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ciudad_de_Nueva_C%C3%A1ceres" title="Ciudad de Nueva Cáceres">Nueva Cáceres</a>, namesake of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_C%C3%A1ceres" title="Province of Cáceres">Province in Spain</a> and among the original five royal cities of the colony. It was designated as the provincial capital by virtue of <i>Philippine Legislative Act No. 2711</i> approved on March 10, 1917 until June 6, 1955, when <a href="/wiki/Pili,_Camarines_Sur" title="Pili, Camarines Sur">Pili</a>, the adjoining town, was declared the Provincial Capital by virtue of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_Act" title="Republic Act">Republic Act</a> 1336</i> up to the present time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camarines_Sur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Camarines Sur covers a total area of 5,497.03 square kilometres (2,122 sq mi)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nscb_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nscb-5">[5]</a></sup> occupying the central section of the <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Region" title="Bicol Region">Bicol Region</a> in <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">Luzon</a>. The province borders <a href="/wiki/Camarines_Norte" title="Camarines Norte">Camarines Norte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quezon" title="Quezon">Quezon</a> to the northwest, and <a href="/wiki/Albay" title="Albay">Albay</a> to the south. To the east lies the <a href="/wiki/Maqueda_Channel" title="Maqueda Channel">Maqueda Channel</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terrain">Terrain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camarines_Sur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Terrain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Camarines Sur occupies the central section of the <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Peninsula" title="Bicol Peninsula">Bicol Peninsula</a>. With a land area of 5,266.8 square kilometres (2,034 sq mi), it is the largest province in the <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Region" title="Bicol Region">Bicol Region</a>. At the center of the province is the Bicol Plain, surrounded by mountains which include <a href="/wiki/Mount_Isarog" title="Mount Isarog">Mount Isarog</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mount_Iriga" title="Mount Iriga">Mount Iriga</a>. The eastern portion of the province lies on the mountainous <a href="/wiki/Caramoan_Peninsula" title="Caramoan Peninsula">Caramoan Peninsula</a>, which faces the island of <a href="/wiki/Catanduanes" title="Catanduanes">Catanduanes</a> to the east.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bicol_River" title="Bicol River">Bicol River</a> drains the central and southern parts of the province into the <a href="/wiki/San_Miguel_Bay" title="San Miguel Bay">San Miguel Bay</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mount_Iriga" title="Mount Iriga">Mount Asog</a> is surrounded by three lakes: <a href="/wiki/Lake_Buhi" title="Lake Buhi">Buhi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Bato" title="Lake Bato">Bato</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Baao" title="Lake Baao">Baao</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camarines_Sur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The climate in Camarines Sur, like most of the rest of the country, is very tropical. It is dry from March to May and wet the rest of the year Annual average rainfall is 2,565 millimetres (101 in). Camarines Sur has an average temperature of 27.0 °C (80.6 °F) and a <a href="/wiki/Relative_humidity" title="Relative humidity">relative humidity</a> of 25.8%. Based from Aera Tranquilo</p>

<p>The population of Camarines Sur in the 2015 census was 1,952,544 people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSO15-05_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-NSO15-05-2">[2]</a></sup> with a density of 360 inhabitants per square kilometre or 930 inhabitants per square mile.</p>
<p>During the May 2010 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>, there were 1,822,371 residents in Camarines Sur, making it the most populous in the region. The census also stated that Camarines Sur had 288,172 households with an average household size of 5.37 persons, significantly higher than the national average of 4.99. The annual growth rate was 1.86%, much lower than the national growth rate of 2.36%. This rate of growth will double the population of Camarines Sur in 8 years.</p>
<div style="color: #000000; background: none; overflow: hidden; page-break-after: avoid; font-size: 1.17em; font-weight: bold; margin-top: 0.3em; margin-bottom: 0; line-height: 1.6; padding-top: 0.5em; padding-bottom: 0;">Religion</div>
<p>The religion of the province is predominantly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_the_Philippines" title="Roman Catholicism in the Philippines">Roman Catholicism</a> followed by 93%<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> of the population. Other religions professed by the people include the Aglipayan Church, Iglesia Ni Cristo (INC), Baptist, Church of Christ of the Latter Day Saints (Mormons), Jehovah's Witnesses, Methodists and other smaller Christian groups. Islam is also present in the province with their mosques stood in large population areas.</p>
<div style="color: #000000; background: none; overflow: hidden; page-break-after: avoid; font-size: 1.17em; font-weight: bold; margin-top: 0.3em; margin-bottom: 0; line-height: 1.6; padding-top: 0.5em; padding-bottom: 0;">Languages</div>
<p>The main languages spoken in Camarines Sur are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coastal_Bikol_languages" title="Coastal Bikol languages">Coastal Bikol</a> (especially <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Bikol" title="Central Bikol">Central Bikol</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inland_Bikol_languages" title="Inland Bikol languages">Inland Bikol</a> group of languages. The latter is a group of languages that includes <a href="/wiki/Albay_Bikol_language" title="Albay Bikol language">Albay Bikol</a> group and <a href="/wiki/Rinconada_Bikol_language" title="Rinconada Bikol language">Rinconada Bikol</a>, while the former just consists dialects. A dialect of Coastal Bikol, called <i>Coastal Bikol-Partido</i> is used in the eastern portion of the province around <a href="/wiki/Lagonoy_Gulf" title="Lagonoy Gulf">Lagonoy Gulf</a>, and another dialect called <i>Coastal Bikol-Central</i> is spoken around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naga_City,_Camarines_Sur" title="Naga City, Camarines Sur">Naga City</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rinconada_Bikol_language" title="Rinconada Bikol language">Rinconada Bikol</a> also known as Riŋkonāda (under the umbrella of Inland Bikol group of languages), is used by most people in the Rinconada area or district of the province especially in <a href="/wiki/Nabua,_Camarines_Sur" title="Nabua, Camarines Sur">Nabua</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iriga_City" title="Iriga City">Iriga City</a> and by people of Rinconada in diaspora. <i><a href="/wiki/Albay_Bikol_language" title="Albay Bikol language">Buhinon</a></i> (one of the languages of Albay Bikol group, another member of Inland Bikol), is a minority language spoken in the town of Buhi and around <a href="/wiki/Lake_Buhi" title="Lake Buhi">Lake Buhi</a>. Del Gallego is the only town in the province that has a majority of population that speaks <a href="/wiki/Tagalog_language" title="Tagalog language">Tagalog</a>. Most inhabitants of Camarines Sur understand Filipino and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>.</p>
<p>The Canaman dialect of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Bikol" title="Central Bikol">Central Bikol</a> variant of <i>Coastal Bikol</i> spoken in <a href="/wiki/Canaman,_Camarines_Sur" title="Canaman, Camarines Sur">Canaman, Camarines Sur</a> is said to be the "purest" form of Bikol (according to Jesuit anthropologist Frank Lynch, S.J.), though most linguists just consider it as the standard form of Central Bikol language since other Coastal Bikol languages, Rinconada Bikol and Buhinon (both Inland Bikol) are separate languages.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camarines_Sur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Camarines Sur is mostly agriculture-based. 29 of the 35 towns are agricultural and produce rice, corn, feedmeal, freshwater fish, livestock, coconut, sugar, abacá, and water-lily.</p>
<p>Entrepreneurs engage in trading, often branching out towards neighboring provinces in the south as local demand might be limited by the 3rd to 5th income-class municipalities. Handicrafts are the major source of rural income, providing a fairly large share in the small-scale industries of the province. Forestry and papermaking are other sources of livelihood. The manufacture of abacá products such as Manila hemp, hats, bags, mats, and slippers is one of the main sources of income in the rural areas. Fishing is also done along both shores of the province. Tourism, primarily because of Caramoan and <a href="/wiki/Mount_Isarog" title="Mount Isarog">Mount Isarog</a>, also generates income for Camarines Sur.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Naga,_Camarines_Sur" title="Naga, Camarines Sur">Naga City</a> and several towns have a tri-economy or three-base economy: commerce, industry, and agriculture. As the main center in the Bicol Region, all of the products from other provinces in the region are brought to Naga City. It has four major industries: the manufacture of jewelry and gifts/toys/housewares, and processing of pineapple and coconut. Naga also has vast cornfields, rice fields, and water lily farms all over the city.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Calabanga,_Camarines_Sur" title="Calabanga, Camarines Sur">Calabanga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cabusao,_Camarines_Sur" title="Cabusao, Camarines Sur">Cabusao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libmanan,_Camarines_Sur" title="Libmanan, Camarines Sur">Libmanan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sipocot,_Camarines_Sur" title="Sipocot, Camarines Sur">Sipocot</a> have similar economies to Naga City. Calabanga has commerce from goods moving out of Naga City, and is the trade center for the towns of <a href="/wiki/Tinambac,_Camarines_Sur" title="Tinambac, Camarines Sur">Tinambac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goa,_Camarines_Sur" title="Goa, Camarines Sur">Goa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Siruma,_Camarines_Sur" title="Siruma, Camarines Sur">Siruma</a>. Calabanga also has fishing from the Quipayo Fishing Center (the largest in Bicol), and vast productions of corn, sugar, and rice, which benefit from a large granary. Libmanan has 156 hectares of ricefields and cornfields, and fishing along its coastline connecting the towns of <a href="/wiki/Ragay,_Camarines_Sur" title="Ragay, Camarines Sur">Ragay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pasacao,_Camarines_Sur" title="Pasacao, Camarines Sur">Pasacao</a>; Libmanan also has a commercial district. Sipocot has an agricultural base economy, with an abundant stock of native chicken (Sipocot's OTOP) and wide production of calamansi and other vegetables, while also serving as trading post for towns of Cabusao, Ragay and Mercedes (Camarines Norte). Fish products from these towns are received by Sipocot. Other towns not mentioned have a fishing industry as the main base of their economy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourist_attractions">Tourist attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camarines_Sur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tourist attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>19th-century churches – There are a number of century old-churches in <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_Nueva_Ecija" title="San Jose, Nueva Ecija">San Jose</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sag%C3%B1ay" title="Sagñay">Sagñay</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Pe%C3%B1afrancia_Shrine" title="Our Lady of Peñafrancia Shrine">Our Lady of Peñafrancia Church</a> – Completed in 1750, this two-century-old church is a site of pilgrimage located in Naga.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lake_Buhi" title="Lake Buhi">Lake Buhi</a> - Created by volcanic activity, this isolated lake is famous for unique organisms including the world's smallest commercially harvested fish.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mount_Isarog" title="Mount Isarog">Mount Isarog</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_Asog" title="Mount Asog">Mount Asog</a> – Two potentially active volcanoes with hiking trails to explore rich biodiversity.</li>
<li>Beaches of Sagñay, Sabang (Partido) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caramoan,_Camarines_Sur" title="Caramoan, Camarines Sur">Caramoan</a> – These black and white sand beaches are shielded by coral reefs.</li>
<li>Pasacao – Known for its beaches as "the Summer Capital of Cam. Sur".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MOSQUOTE" title="Wikipedia:MOSQUOTE"><span title="The text near this tag needs citation.
