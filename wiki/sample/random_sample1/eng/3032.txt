<p><b>Namhangdaegyo</b> (남항대교) is a girder bridge in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>. It spans <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeongdo-gu" title="Yeongdo-gu">Yeongdo-gu</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seo-gu,_Busan" title="Seo-gu, Busan">Seo-gu</a> and is 1.8 km long in total with four lanes of traffic (two in each direction).</p>

<li>July 9, 2008 opened to the public</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namhangdaegyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bridge was constructed as part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Busan_Thoroughfare&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Southern Busan Thoroughfare (page does not exist)">Southern Busan Thoroughfare</a> (부산남부순환로) initiative. It has significantly reduced time between Yeongdo Island and western Busan. Initially, a trip from Seo-gu to Yeongdo-gu (or vice versa) was 7 km long and would take 30 minutes. Now, it is only 2 km long and takes 3 minutes.</p>
