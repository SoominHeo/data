<p>The <b>1st <a href="/wiki/East_Asian_Games" title="East Asian Games">East Asian Games</a></b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> from May 9 to May 18, 1993. The main stadium for the inaugural edition of the games was the <a href="/wiki/Hongkou_Football_Stadium" title="Hongkou Football Stadium">Hongkou Football Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shanghai also hosted a number of sports-themed exhibitions to coincide with the games, including exhibitions of <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamp" title="Postage stamp">stamps</a>. A total of 9 nations competed in a programme of twelve sports. <a href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias" title="Julio Iglesias">Julio Iglesias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wei_Wei_(singer)" title="Wei Wei (singer)">Wei Wei</a> performed a duet at the games closing ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The hosts, China, topped the medal table, winning almost two-thirds of the <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medals</a> on offer. <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> were the second- and third-most successful nations.</p>
