<p><i><b>Listen to My Word</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">내 얘길 들어봐</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Nae Yaegil Deureobwa</span></i>) is the fourth <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) by South Korean girl group <a href="/wiki/Oh_My_Girl" title="Oh My Girl">Oh My Girl</a>. It was released by WM Entertainment on August 1, 2016, distributed by <a href="/wiki/LOEN_Entertainment" title="LOEN Entertainment">LOEN Entertainment</a>. The album contains four <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">covers</a> of classic <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> songs, including <a href="/wiki/Papaya_(band)" title="Papaya (band)">Papaya</a>'s "Listen to My Word (A-ing)" featuring reggae duo Skull and <a href="/wiki/Haha_(entertainer)" title="Haha (entertainer)">Haha</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Listen_to_My_Word&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 15, 2016, Oh My Girl's agency WM Entertainment announced that the group would be releasing a special summer album in August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On July 31, Oh My Girl introduced the album through a live broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver</a>'s V app, singing a medley of all four songs on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v_report_1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-v_report_1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v_report_2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-v_report_2-3">[3]</a></sup> The digital album was released on August 1, along with a music video for the title track, "Listen to My Word (A-ing)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itunes_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The CD version of the album was released on August 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-synnara_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-synnara-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Listen_to_My_Word&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All four songs on the album are covers of classic <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> songs, re-arranged to have a more contemporary sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v_report_2_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-v_report_2-3">[3]</a></sup> The title track, "Listen to My Word (A-ing)", is a cover of the hit song by girl group <a href="/wiki/Papaya_(band)" title="Papaya (band)">Papaya</a>, originally released in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It features <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Skull_(singer)" title="Skull (singer)">Skull</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haha_(entertainer)" title="Haha (entertainer)">Haha</a> and has reggae influences and a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> beat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> "Midsummer Night's Christmas", originally released in 1990 by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Jung-hyun_(singer_born_1966)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lee Jung-hyun (singer born 1966) (page does not exist)">Lee Jung-hyun</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EC%9D%B4%EC%A0%95%ED%98%84_(1966%EB%85%84)" title="ko:이정현 (1966년)">ko</a>)</span>, has a piano melody and <a href="/wiki/Bossa_nova" title="Bossa nova">bossa nova</a> drum rhythms. "Je T'aime" was originally released by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hey_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hey (singer) (page does not exist)">Hey</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%ED%95%B4%EC%9D%B4" title="ko:해이">ko</a>)</span> in 2001, and has <a href="/wiki/Modern_rock" title="Modern rock">modern rock</a> influences. "Lies You Can See" was originally released in by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Hyun-chul_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kim Hyun-chul (singer) (page does not exist)">Kim Hyun-chul</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EA%B9%80%ED%98%84%EC%B2%A0_(%EA%B0%80%EC%88%98)" title="ko:김현철 (가수)">ko</a>)</span> in 1998, and is in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philly_soul" title="Philly soul">Philly soul</a> genre. All four songs feature a new rap written by Mimi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-synnara_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-synnara-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Listen_to_My_Word&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a> for July 31 – August 6, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weekly_gaon_album_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-weekly_gaon_album-9">[9]</a></sup> The title track charted at number 15 on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Digital_Chart" title="Gaon Digital Chart">Gaon Digital Chart</a> that same week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
