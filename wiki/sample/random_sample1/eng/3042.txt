<p><i><b>Oreimo</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺妹</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, short for <i><b>Ore no Imōto ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺の妹がこんなに可愛いわけがない</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>My Little Sister Can't Be This Cute</i>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drama_CD_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Drama_CD-3">[3]</a></sup> and also known as <i><b>Ore no Imōto</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺の妹</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novel</a> series written by <a href="/wiki/Tsukasa_Fushimi" title="Tsukasa Fushimi">Tsukasa Fushimi</a>, with illustrations provided by Hiro Kanzaki. The series includes 12 novels released between August 2008 and June 2013. A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation drawn by Sakura Ikeda was serialized in <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Media_Works" title="ASCII Media Works">ASCII Media Works</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_G%27s_Magazine" title="Dengeki G's Magazine">Dengeki G's Magazine</a></i>. A 12-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation produced by <a href="/wiki/Anime_International_Company" title="Anime International Company">AIC</a> aired in Japan between October and December 2010. Four additional episodes, which offer a different ending to the broadcast series, were streamed monthly via the official website and other video streaming sites between February and May 2011, before being released on Blu-ray Disc and DVD in June and July 2011. A 13-episode second anime season produced by <a href="/wiki/A-1_Pictures" title="A-1 Pictures">A-1 Pictures</a>, titled <i>Ore no Imōto ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai.</i> (with a period at the end), aired between April and June 2013. An additional three episodes were streamed online on August 18, 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kyosuke Kosaka, a normal 17-year-old high school student living in <a href="/wiki/Chiba,_Chiba" title="Chiba, Chiba">Chiba</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> has not gotten along with his younger sister Kirino in years. For longer than he can remember, Kirino has ignored his comings and goings and looked at him with spurning eyes. It seemed as if the relationship between Kyōsuke and his sister, now fourteen, would continue this way forever. One day however, Kyosuke finds a DVD case of a <a href="/wiki/Magical_girl" title="Magical girl">magical girl</a> <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> which had fallen in his house's entrance way. To Kyosuke's surprise, he finds a hidden <a href="/wiki/Eroge" title="Eroge">eroge</a> inside the case and he soon learns that both the DVD and the game belong to Kirino. That night, Kirino brings Kyosuke to her room and reveals herself to be an <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">otaku</a> with an extensive collection of <a href="/wiki/Moe_(slang)" title="Moe (slang)">moe</a> anime and younger sister-themed eroge she has been collecting in secret. Kyosuke quickly becomes Kirino's confidant for her secret hobby. The series then follows Kyosuke's efforts to help his sister to reconcile her personal life with her secret hobbies, while restoring their broken relationship and coming to terms with their true feelings for each other.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Kyosuke_Kosaka">Kyosuke Kosaka</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">高坂 京介</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kōsaka Kyōsuke</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Yuichi_Nakamura_(voice_actor)" title="Yuichi Nakamura (voice actor)">Yuichi Nakamura</a>, Mami Fujita (young)</span></dd>
<dd>Kyosuke is the 17 year-old main character of <i>Oreimo</i>. He has a distant relationship with his sister, and feels that this will never change. However, this suddenly changes when he finds one of her <a href="/wiki/Eroge" title="Eroge">eroge</a> and, after confronting her with it, learns that she is an <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">otaku</a>. Through this experience he realizes that he knows very little about his sister, including her being a model as well as an exemplary student. He decides to become more of an older brother to her. Despite Kyosuke being older, his sister is generally the dominant person in their relationship, with Kyosuke only standing up to her in truly serious situations. Kyosuke initially claims to prefer a relatively peaceful and simple life, but grows to enjoy spending time with his sister as well as the drama she causes to the point where he becomes lonely when she is not around. Kyosuke helped Ruri make friends and joins the Game Research Club with her during his third year when Ruri becomes his junior. Kyosuke dates Ruri during summer vacation but she breaks up with him. In the PSP game <i>Ore no Imōto ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai Portable ga Tsuzuku Wake ga Nai</i>, Kyosuke is the son of his current mother's sibling.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>In the past, Kyosuke had good grades and was the fastest runner in his elementary school. He also had the tendency to shoulder everything himself, thinking that there was nothing that he could not achieve, and often said "Leave it to me". However, after a hiking accident, Kyosuke was convinced by Manami that he should not try so hard at everything, which drastically changed his personality. He turned into someone who only desired to live a normal lifestyle, and his studies and athleticism worsened over time.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Kirino_Kosaka">Kirino Kosaka</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">高坂 桐乃</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kōsaka Kirino</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Ayana_Taketatsu" title="Ayana Taketatsu">Ayana Taketatsu</a></span></dd>
<dd>Kirino is Kyosuke's 14 year-old younger sister. She is normally mature and independent for her age. However this is a facade and she only reveals her true personality (immature, abusive and ungrateful with an aggressive, <i><a href="/wiki/Tsundere" title="Tsundere">tsundere</a></i>-like character) to Kyosuke, whom she orders to play her games and care for the characters, only to accuse him of being a pervert and a <i><a href="/wiki/Lolicon" title="Lolicon">lolicon</a></i>. She is secretly an otaku with an obsession for "little sister"-themed eroge, as well as the children's anime series <i>Stardust Witch Meruru</i>. She works as a magazine model and later becomes a novelist, the funds from which she uses to support her hobby. Despite her interests, she states she does not know why she started liking eroge and denies having a brother complex. Nevertheless, she frequently forces Kyosuke to spend time with her, whether it is playing games or taking her out, but denies that she actually enjoys the time they spend together.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Kirino is also shown to become frustrated whenever Kyosuke is with or shows interest in other girls. She even goes so far as to sabotage his time with another girl. Despite this, she is genuinely grateful to her brother. Although she states that she only likes eroge because of their cute characters, she shows a perverted side of herself when playing those games. She uses the screen name Kiririn when with her otaku friends. She used to admire Kyosuke in the past, whom she saw as a perfect brother. Kyosuke started distancing himself from her during elementary school. Kirino once tried to chase after Kyosuke (who ran away to play with his friends) but she tripped and cried in disappointment. This spurred her to start working hard on her academics and sports. She was also severely disappointed in Kyosuke's change in personality after the incident with Akimi.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Ruri_Gok.C5.8D">Ruri Gokō</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">五更 瑠璃</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gokō Ruri</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>) /</span> <span id="Kuroneko">Kuroneko</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">黒猫</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. Black Cat)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Kana_Hanazawa" title="Kana Hanazawa">Kana Hanazawa</a></span></dd>
<dd>Ruri is a 15 year-old otaku girl who lives near Kirino and later enrolls in Kyosuke's school. She often wears <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gothic_lolita" title="Gothic lolita">gothic lolita</a> clothing based on a character from her favorite anime, even during hot weather, and will sometimes add cat ears and a tail. She calls herself Kuroneko (black cat) and wears contact lenses to make her blue eyes appear red. Due to both of their <i>tsundere</i>-type personalities, she constantly clashes with Kirino. However, both girls genuinely care for each other and value the time they spend together. Like Kirino, Kuroneko is very prideful but is willing to swallow her pride to achieve a goal. Unlike Kirino, who likes little sister-themed games and <a href="/wiki/Magical_girl" title="Magical girl">magical girl</a> anime, Kuroneko enjoys teen-centered fantasy anime. She often mentions "dark magic", claiming that she has it. She is noted to have a very large vocabulary - Kirino once comments that she needed to use a dictionary to translate her novel.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Kuroneko has two younger sisters, Hinata and Tamaki, whom she cares for dearly, although they and her mother worry about Ruri when she is in otaku mode. At school, Kuroneko wears normal clothes and at home takes care of her younger sisters. She later joins the video game club after she enrolls into Kyosuke's school; she is very good at playing video games. She dates Kyosuke for over summer vacation before breaking up with him. However, she still harbors feelings for Kyosuke. Kyosuke later rejects her feelings and she accepts that they will not be together.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Saori_Makishima">Saori Makishima</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">槇島 沙織</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Makishima Saori</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>) /</span> <span id="Saori_Vageena">Saori Vageena</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">沙織・バジーナ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Saori Bajīna</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Hitomi_Nabatame" title="Hitomi Nabatame">Hitomi Nabatame</a></span></dd>
<dd>Saori is the leader of the "Anime Girls Unite!" group on the Internet, whom Kirino encounters in a meet-up. She wears very thick glasses (shown as swirls) and speaks with an exaggerated tone. She is a fan of <i><a href="/wiki/Gundam" title="Gundam">Gundam</a></i> (her alias is named after the character, <a href="/wiki/Char_Aznable" title="Char Aznable">Quattro Bajeena</a>) and is also a collector of their model kits. Despite her general appearance in public, Saori is actually from a wealthy family and speaks formally when not with her friends. As Kyosuke notes, Saori is drastically different in person as compared to how she communicates over the Internet or the phone.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>According to Kyosuke, she dresses as a stereotypical otaku, but has the body of a supermodel. She is tall and usually has to have cosplay costumes tailor made for her. Despite being a middle school girl around Kirino and Kuroneko's ages, she is around the same height as Kyosuke, which surprises him when he first meets her. Despite her cheery attitude, Saori is quite lonely, living by herself, and has always feared that one day her true friends (Kirino, Kyosuke, and Ruri) will leave her in a similar way that her older sister (who married and moved away) left her. The reason she set up a hobby group of her own is that she was "just the sister of a member" of her sister's hobby group "Pretty Garden," and was severely disappointed and angry when the group eventually dissolved after her sister moved away.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Ayase Aragaki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">新垣 あやせ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aragaki Ayase</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Saori_Hayami" title="Saori Hayami">Saori Hayami</a></span></dd>
<dd>Ayase is Kirino's classmate who is her best friend and also works as a model. She admired Kirino for being seemingly perfect in every way and she used to have a crush on Kyosuke, thinking of him as Kirino's caring brother. She was greatly disappointed when she found out about Kirino's otaku hobby and how Kyosuke was aware of it and helped support it. Ayase later reconciled with Kirino, but not as much with Kyosuke. Ayase is normally a friendly, nice, and refined girl, but becomes agitated and somewhat violent at the thought that she is being lied to, which she hates above all else. She also believes what she hears on the news, thus explaining her disapproval of anime and manga, calling it a disgusting hobby, especially eroge, due to the media's portrayal that otaku will eventually become criminals. Once she develops a dislike and contempt for someone, she shows a much ruder and even hostile side of herself. She stubbornly maintains a strong dislike and disrespect for Kyosuke, convincing herself that he is really the pervert she persistently calls him. She becomes good friends with Manami and often confides private matters to her. She eventually overcomes her prejudice and harbors romantic feelings for Kyosuke.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Manami Tamura <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">田村 麻奈実</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tamura Manami</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Satomi_Sat%C5%8D" title="Satomi Satō">Satomi Satō</a></span></dd>
<dd>Manami is Kyosuke's childhood friend. She wears glasses and is described as "plain", something she views as a compliment from Kyosuke. She has a crush on Kyosuke and blushes whenever he compliments her. Due to the amount time they spend together, people around them assume that they are dating. Kyosuke explains that although he does not have feelings for her beyond being close friends, for some reason he does not like the idea of Manami having a boyfriend. Kyosuke is unaware of the many times that Manami has attempted to become closer to him by trying to do things that couples do and how she is willingly doing things to please him in any way, such as cooking and cleaning. According to Kyosuke, her speech patterns resemble those of a grandmother or an old lady. After Kyosuke's accident it was Manami who convinced him to stop trying his best in everything, and made him into the average person today, which severely disappointed Kirino and caused the rift between her and Kirino. She manages to make up with Kirino after Kyosuke gets them to talk. Manami later confesses to Kyosuke but he rejects her.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Kanako Kurusu <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">来栖 加奈子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kurusu Kanako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Yukari_Tamura" title="Yukari Tamura">Yukari Tamura</a></span></dd>
<dd>Kanako is Kirino and Ayase's fellow friend, classmate and model, who sometimes swaps between a cute and a bratty personality. She is a talented singer who dreams of becoming an idol and attends many auditions. She frequently goes to karaoke and is capable of perfectly remembering any song that she has heard. She is unaware of Kirino's hobby and generally finds anime childish and otaku disgusting, though on one occasion she was tricked into cosplaying as Kirino's favorite anime character, Meruru, for a contest and won. Despite winning the contest, she was disgusted by what she had gone through, but liked the idea of being praised and the event even helped her career. She later realized that Kyosuke, who acted as her manager, was Kirino's brother. She also asked Manami to teach her to cook in order to be able to cook for her parents.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Sena Akagi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">赤城 瀬菜</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Akagi Sena</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Mariya_Ise" title="Mariya Ise">Mariya Ise</a></span></dd>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Light_novels">Light novels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Light novels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Oreimo</i> began as a <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novel</a> series written by <a href="/wiki/Tsukasa_Fushimi" title="Tsukasa Fushimi">Tsukasa Fushimi</a>, with illustrations provided by Hiro Kanzaki. <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Media_Works" title="ASCII Media Works">ASCII Media Works</a> published 12 novels under their <a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Bunko" title="Dengeki Bunko">Dengeki Bunko</a> <a href="/wiki/Imprint_(trade_name)" title="Imprint (trade name)">imprint</a> between August 10, 2008 and June 7, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation based on the light novels drawn by Sakura Ikeda was serialized in ASCII Media Works' <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_G%27s_Magazine" title="Dengeki G's Magazine">Dengeki G's Magazine</a></i> between the March 2009 and May 2011 issues. Four <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes were released under ASCII Media Works' <a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Comics" title="Dengeki Comics">Dengeki Comics</a> <a href="/wiki/Imprint_(trade_name)" title="Imprint (trade name)">imprint</a> between October 27, 2009 and April 27, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The third volume was released in regular and special editions; the special edition came bundled with an Ayase Aragaki figurine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The manga has been licensed in North America by <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">Dark Horse Comics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> A follow-up manga, <i>Ore no Kōhai ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺の後輩がこんなに可愛いわけがない</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>My Junior Can't Be This Cute</i>)</span>, began serialization in the July 2011 issue of <i>Dengeki G's Magazine</i> and features Ruri Goko as the main heroine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The manga ended serialization in the magazine's May 2014 issue and continued serialization in <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_G%27s_Comic" title="Dengeki G's Comic">Dengeki G's Comic</a></i> between the June 2014 and July 2015 issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G.27s-Comic-June-2014_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-G.27s-Comic-June-2014-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The first volume was released on May 26, 2012, in regular and special editions; the special edition came bundled with a Kuroneko figurine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Six <i>tankōbon</i> volumes were released under ASCII Media Works' Dengeki Comics imprint between May 26, 2012 and July 27, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drama_CD_and_radio_shows">Drama CD and radio shows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Drama CD and radio shows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_drama" title="Audio drama">drama CD</a>, based on events from the first and third volumes of the novels as well as a newly written scenario by Tsukasa Fushimi, was released by ASCII Media Works on March 31, 2010. The CD stars <a href="/wiki/Ayana_Taketatsu" title="Ayana Taketatsu">Ayana Taketatsu</a> as Kirino and <a href="/wiki/Yuichi_Nakamura_(voice_actor)" title="Yuichi Nakamura (voice actor)">Yūichi Nakamura</a> as Kyōsuke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drama_CD_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Drama_CD-3">[3]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Internet_radio" title="Internet radio">Internet radio</a> show titled <i>Ore no Imōto ga (Radio de mo) Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺の妹が(ラジオでも)こんなに可愛いわけがない</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>There's No Way My Little Sister (Radio Version) Can Be This Cute</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> to promote the anime adaptation streamed 24 episodes between August 13, 2010 and July 22, 2011 via the official website of the anime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official-web-radio_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-web-radio-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The show was hosted by Ayana Taketatsu, who plays Kirino in the anime, and <a href="/wiki/Kana_Hanazawa" title="Kana Hanazawa">Kana Hanazawa</a>, who plays Kuroneko.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official-web-radio_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-official-web-radio-18">[18]</a></sup> A second Internet radio show to promote the second season of the anime started on April 11, 2013. The show was again hosted by Taketatsu and Hanazawa. Even though the show was limited to the Japanese speaking audience, the e-mails and comments from fans outside Japan were also accepted and sometimes read during the show, and the official website of the show has been translated into English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Oreimo_episodes" title="List of Oreimo episodes">List of Oreimo episodes</a></div>
<p>A 12-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation produced by <a href="/wiki/Anime_International_Company" title="Anime International Company">AIC</a> and directed by Hiroyuki Kanbe aired in Japan between October 3 and December 19, 2010. The series was released over eight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">BD</a>/DVD compilation volumes, each holding two episodes, between December 22, 2010 and July 27, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Four <a href="/wiki/Original_net_animation" title="Original net animation">original net animation</a> episodes were streamed through the official website, as well as several other websites such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nico_Nico_Douga" title="Nico Nico Douga">Nico Nico Douga</a>, Showtime Japan, and MovieGate, which began on February 22, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode12_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode12-24">[24]</a></sup> The first two were released on June 27, 2011, together with the seventh BD/DVD volume and the last two were released on July 27, 2011, together with the eighth. These episodes feature a break in the original story arc starting at episode 12<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode12_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Episode12-24">[24]</a></sup> and offer an alternate ending from the TV broadcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The anime retains the voice cast from the drama CD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-anime-news_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-anime-news-26">[26]</a></sup> The opening theme for the anime is "<a href="/wiki/Irony_(ClariS_song)" title="Irony (ClariS song)">Irony</a>" by <a href="/wiki/ClariS" title="ClariS">ClariS</a> and is composed by Kz of <a href="/wiki/Livetune" title="Livetune">Livetune</a>, while each episode features a different ending theme sung by one of the voice actors. The music of the anime is composed by <a href="/wiki/Satoru_K%C5%8Dsaki" title="Satoru Kōsaki">Satoru Kōsaki</a> and a soundtrack was released on January 12, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official-music_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-music-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Aniplex_of_America" title="Aniplex of America">Aniplex of America</a> began streaming and simulcasting the series in North America through <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> (ANN), but security issues involving the illegal leaking of episode two online resulted in the stream being placed on hold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The stream of <i>Oreimo</i> returned to ANN with the first four episodes on November 8, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Aniplex of America released the series on an English-subtitled DVD box set in October 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The anime is licensed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MVM_Films" title="MVM Films">MVM Films</a> in the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>A second 13-episode anime season, titled <i>Ore no Imōto ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai.</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺の妹がこんなに可愛いわけがない。</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, with a period at the end)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and produced by <a href="/wiki/A-1_Pictures" title="A-1 Pictures">A-1 Pictures</a>, aired between April 7 and June 30, 2013 and was simulcast by <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> An additional three episodes were screened at <a href="/wiki/Otakon" title="Otakon">Otakon</a> 2013 on August 9, 2013, and then streamed worldwide on August 18. The episodes were simulcast by Crunchyroll at the same time as the Japanese broadcast and were available until August 31, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Despite the change in animation studio from the first season, the second season has the same staff as the first.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-10-07-12_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-10-07-12-36">[36]</a></sup> The opening theme is "<a href="/wiki/Reunion_(ClariS_song)" title="Reunion (ClariS song)">Reunion</a>" by ClariS,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> while a contest was held for the second season's ending themes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> This series has also been licensed by Aniplex of America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> A-1 Pictures collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Urban_Monorail" title="Chiba Urban Monorail">Chiba Urban Monorail</a> to promote the second season by making an <i>Oreimo</i>-themed monorail train, which remained in operation until September 30, 2013. In addition to decorating the exterior of the train, the anime characters were featured in in-train advertising to provide passengers with helpful tips about local sightseeing facilities and shops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oreimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Visual_novel"></span> A <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> <a href="/wiki/Visual_novel" title="Visual novel">visual novel</a> titled <i>Ore no Imōto ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai Portable</i> was developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bandai_Namco_Games" title="Bandai Namco Games">Bandai Namco Games</a> and released on January 27, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The limited edition bundle included an OAD, a sister-talk bonus CD, and an additional game for the PSP. A <a href="/wiki/Fighting_game" title="Fighting game">fighting game</a> titled <i>Shin Imōto Taisen Siscalypse</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">真妹大戦シスカリプス</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>True Little Sister War, Siscalypse</i>)</span>, based on the fictional game in the series, was released by Kadokawa Contents Gate on the <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> MobaGe Service on January 20, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> A second PSP title, <i>Ore no Imōto ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai Portable ga Tsuzuku Wake ga Nai</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺の妹がこんなに可愛いわけがない ポータブルが続くわけがない</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>There's No Way "My Little Sister Can't Be This Cute Portable" Could Continue</i>)</span>, was released on May 17, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The opening theme of the second PSP game is "Nexus" by ClariS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> game, <i>Ore no Imōto ga Konna ni Kawaii Wake ga Nai Happy End</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">俺の妹がこんなに可愛いわけがない。 ハッピーエンド</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was released on September 26, 2013 in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Kirino also makes a cameo appearance in the <i><a href="/wiki/A_Certain_Magical_Index" title="A Certain Magical Index">A Certain Magical Index</a></i> PSP game,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> being another game based on a light novel series published by ASCII Media Works. Kirino also appears as a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Bunko:_Fighting_Climax" title="Dengeki Bunko: Fighting Climax">Dengeki Bunko: Fighting Climax</a></i>, with Kuroneko as a support character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
