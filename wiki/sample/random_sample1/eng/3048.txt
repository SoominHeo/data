<p>The <b>Naidaijin</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">内大臣</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, usually translated as Inner Minister—also known as the Minister of the Center <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">内大臣</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>uchi no otodo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>—was a significant post in the Imperial Court as re-organized under the <a href="/wiki/Taih%C5%8D_Code" title="Taihō Code"><i>Taihō</i> Code</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-t425_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-t425-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Meiji_period_official">Pre-Meiji period official</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naidaijin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-Meiji period official">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The role, rank and authority of the <i>naidaijin</i> varied, however, throughout pre-<a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji</a> history.</p>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Ritsury%C5%8D" title="Ritsuryō">ritsuryō</a></i> system, the Minister of the Center was inferior only to the <a href="/wiki/Sadaijin" title="Sadaijin">Minister of the Left</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Udaijin" title="Udaijin">Minister of the Right</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meiji_period_official">Meiji period official</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naidaijin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Meiji period official">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The office developed a different character in the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>. In 1885, the title was reconfigured to mean the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Keeper_of_the_Privy_Seal_of_Japan" title="Lord Keeper of the Privy Seal of Japan">Lord Keeper of the Privy Seal of Japan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Court_(royal)" title="Court (royal)">Imperial Court</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In that year, the office of prime minister or chief minister of the initial restoration government was the <i>Daijō-daijin</i>, <a href="/wiki/Sanj%C5%8D_Sanetomi" title="Sanjō Sanetomi">Sanjō Sanetomi</a>. In December, Sanjō petitioned the emperor to be relieved of his office; and he was then immediately appointed <i>Naidaijin</i>, or Lord Keeper of the Privy Seal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The office of the Privy Seal was identical with the old <i>Naidaijin</i> only in the sense of the Japanese title—not in terms of function or powers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Unterstein_in_German_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Unterstein_in_German-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Meiji_period_official">Post-Meiji period official</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naidaijin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-Meiji period official">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The nature of the office evolved in the <a href="/wiki/Taish%C5%8D_period" title="Taishō period">Taishō</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dwa_period" title="Shōwa period">Shōwa</a> periods. The title was abolished on November 24, 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
