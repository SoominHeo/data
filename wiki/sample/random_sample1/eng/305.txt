<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">1993 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/FINA_World_Swimming_Championships_(25_m)" title="FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)">FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)</a></div>
<p>The <b>1st Short Course Swimming World Championships</b> were organized by <a href="/wiki/FINA" title="FINA">FINA</a> and held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca,_Spain" title="Palma de Mallorca, Spain">Palma de Mallorca, Spain</a>, December 2–5, 1993. The meet featured competition in a <a href="/wiki/Short_course" title="Short course">short course</a> (25 meter) pool. During the championships, a total of 12 world records were broken: 10 in the women's events and 2 in men's events, both in relays.</p>
<p>China won the most events, ten, all in women's events. <a href="/wiki/Le_Jingyi" title="Le Jingyi">Le Jingyi</a> won two individual and three relay events to travel home with five golds; <a href="/wiki/Dai_Guohong" title="Dai Guohong">Dai Guohong</a> went home with four golds and one silver. In the men's events, the USA and Australia won three events each. <a href="/wiki/Tripp_Schwenk" title="Tripp Schwenk">Tripp Schwenk</a> of the USA garnered three golds, winning the two backstroke events and also swimming on the USA's winning medley relay team. Also winning 2 events each on the men's side were <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Scherer" title="Fernando Scherer">Fernando Scherer</a> of Brazil (100m freestyle; 400m freestyle relay), <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Kowalski" title="Daniel Kowalski">Daniel Kowalski</a> of Australia (400m and 1500m freestyles). Belgium and Moldova won their only medals at a Short Course Worlds (through 2006), while <a href="/wiki/Franck_Esposito" title="Franck Esposito">Franck Esposito</a> won France's only gold to date when he won the 200 butterfly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
