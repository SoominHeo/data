<p>The <b>National League Central</b> is one of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>'s six divisions. This division was created in 1994, by moving two teams from the <a href="/wiki/National_League_West" title="National League West">National League West</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>) and three teams from the <a href="/wiki/National_League_East" title="National League East">National League East</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a>).</p>
<p>When the division was created in 1994, the Pirates were originally supposed to stay in the East while the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> were to be moved to the Central from the West. However, the Braves, wanting to form a natural rivalry with the expansion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> (a rivalry that as of today has yet to develop, largely due to the Marlins being noncompetitive except for their two World Series-winning seasons), requested to be moved to the East instead. Despite the Marlins offering to go to the Central, the Pirates instead gave up its spot in the East to the Braves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Since then, the Pirates have tried several times unsuccessfully to be placed back in the East.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1998, the NL Central became the largest division in Major League Baseball when the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a> were moved in from the <a href="/wiki/American_League_Central" title="American League Central">American League Central</a>. In 2013, the Astros moved to the <a href="/wiki/American_League_West" title="American League West">American League West</a>.</p>
<p>This division has been dominated by the Cardinals, who have accounted for ten of the 22 division championships, plus three wild card wins. Aside from the Cardinals, the Astros and Cubs have the most division championships with four, as well as two wild card wins each. The Reds have three division titles, along with one wild card win. The Brewers have one divisional championship, with one wild card win. The Pirates have not won the division since the division was created, but possesses three wild card wins. The 2013 win was the team's first playoff berth since 1992.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_League_Championships">National League Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_Central&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: National League Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The division has produced six National League Pennant winners: St. Louis in 2004, 2006, 2011 and 2013, Houston in 2005, and the Cubs in 2016. In both 2004 and 2005 the American League Champions swept the World Series. The <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> won the 2006 World Series in five games, won the 2011 World Series in seven games, and lost the 2013 World Series in six games. The <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> won the 2016 World Series in seven games.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wild-card_winners">Wild-card winners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_Central&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Wild-card winners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_National_League_Wild_Card_winners" title="List of National League Wild Card winners">List of National League Wild Card winners</a></div>
<p>The wild card was introduced in 1994 and was initially assigned to the team with the best record in each league that did not win its division. The first year of implementation was 1995 as a player strike prematurely ended the 1994 season. Since implementation, each NL Central teams has won the wild card. In 2012, a second wild card was added to post-season play.</p>
