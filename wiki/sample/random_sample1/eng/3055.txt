<p>In <a href="/wiki/Finance" title="Finance">finance</a>, <b>intrinsic value</b> refers to the <a href="/wiki/Value_(economics)" title="Value (economics)">value</a> of a company, <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> or <a href="/wiki/Product_(business)" title="Product (business)">product</a> determined through <a href="/wiki/Fundamental_analysis" title="Fundamental analysis">fundamental analysis</a> without reference to its <a href="/wiki/Market_value" title="Market value">market value</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is also frequently called <b>fundamental value</b>. It is ordinarily calculated by summing the <a href="/wiki/Discounting" title="Discounting">discounted</a> future income generated by the asset to obtain the <a href="/wiki/Present_value" title="Present value">present value</a>. It is worthy to note that this term may have different meanings for different assets.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Options">Options</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intrinsic_value_(finance)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Options">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Option_(finance)" title="Option (finance)">option</a> is said to have intrinsic value if the option is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In-the-money" title="In-the-money">in-the-money</a>. When <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Out-of-the-money" title="Out-of-the-money">out-of-the-money</a>, its intrinsic value is <i>zero</i>.</p>
<p>The intrinsic value for an in-the-money option is calculated as the absolute value of the difference between the current <a href="/wiki/Price" title="Price">price</a> (<i>S</i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Underlying" title="Underlying">underlying</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Strike_price" title="Strike price">strike price</a> (<i>K</i>) of the option.</p>

<p>For example, if the <a href="/wiki/Strike_price" title="Strike price">strike price</a> for a call option is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">USD</a> $1 and the price of the underlying is USD 1.20, then the option has an intrinsic value of USD 0.20.</p>
<p>The value of an option is the sum of its intrinsic and its <a href="/wiki/Option_time_value" title="Option time value">time value</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equity">Equity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intrinsic_value_(finance)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Equity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Valuation_using_discounted_cash_flows" title="Valuation using discounted cash flows">Valuation using discounted cash flows</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Burr_Williams#Theory" title="John Burr Williams">John Burr Williams § Theory</a></div>
<p>Stocks are assumed to be <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">equity</a> instruments because they are supposed to represent ownership<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> interest in the company. However, the 'equity' label is somewhat questionable. Class C<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> common stocks for example, do not have any voting rights or dividend privileges. They are still considered equity instruments by finance professionals, but shareholders are not entitled to the earnings of the underlying company and lack the right to voice an opinion.</p>
<p>In valuing equity, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Securities_analyst" title="Securities analyst">securities analysts</a> may use <a href="/wiki/Fundamental_analysis" title="Fundamental analysis">fundamental analysis</a>—as opposed to <a href="/wiki/Technical_analysis" title="Technical analysis">technical analysis</a>—to estimate the intrinsic value of a company. Here the "intrinsic" characteristic considered is the expected <a href="/wiki/Cash_flow" title="Cash flow">cash flow</a> production of the company in question. Intrinsic value is therefore defined to be the <a href="/wiki/Present_value" title="Present value">present value</a> of all expected future net cash flows to the company; it is calculated via <a href="/wiki/Discounted_cash_flow" title="Discounted cash flow">discounted cash flow</a> valuation. This is not a proven theorem or a validated theory, but a general assumption.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>An alternative, though related approach, is to view intrinsic value as the value of a business' ongoing operations, as opposed to its accounting based <a href="/wiki/Book_value" title="Book value">book value</a>, or break-up value. <a href="/wiki/Warren_Buffett" title="Warren Buffett">Warren Buffett</a> is known for his ability to calculate the intrinsic value of a business, and then buy that business when its price is at a discount to its intrinsic value.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_estate">Real estate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intrinsic_value_(finance)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Real estate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In valuing <a href="/wiki/Real_estate" title="Real estate">real estate</a>, a similar approach may be used. The "intrinsic value" of real estate is therefore defined as the net <a href="/wiki/Present_value" title="Present value">present value</a> of all future net <a href="/wiki/Cash_flow" title="Cash flow">cash flows</a> which are foregone by buying a piece of real estate instead of renting it in perpetuity. These cash flows would include rent, inflation, maintenance and property taxes. This calculation can be done using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gordon_model" title="Gordon model">Gordon model</a>.</p>
