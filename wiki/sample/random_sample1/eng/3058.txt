<p><b>Nathalie Kelley</b> (born October 5, 1985) is a Peruvian Australian actress. She is best known for her debut role as Neela in the 2006 action film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious:_Tokyo_Drift" title="The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift">The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift</a></i>. Kelley later had supporting roles on television, include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dani_Alvarez" title="Dani Alvarez">Dani Alvarez</a> in ABC procedural <i><a href="/wiki/Body_of_Proof" title="Body of Proof">Body of Proof</a></i> (2011-2012), and Grace in the Lifetime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Unreal_(TV_series)" title="Unreal (TV series)">Unreal</a></i> (2015).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathalie_Kelley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nathalie Kelley was born in <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">Lima</a>, Peru, to a Peruvian mother and an <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News.com.au_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-News.com.au-1">[1]</a></sup> She moved to <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> when she was 2 years old. From a young age, she dreamed about being an actress. She attended <a href="/wiki/North_Sydney_Girls_High_School" title="North Sydney Girls High School">North Sydney Girls High School</a> on Sydney's <a href="/wiki/North_Shore_(Sydney)" title="North Shore (Sydney)">North Shore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News.com.au_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-News.com.au-1">[1]</a></sup> When she was 16 she became a salsa dancer, which helped her get the money to finish her education. The "Miss Latin America competition" also earned her a much-needed $1,000, before she took a year off and went to Brazil. The things Kelley saw there prompted her to shun show business and come home to launch herself into a social science degree at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_New_South_Wales" title="University of New South Wales">University of New South Wales</a>. She volunteered with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indigenous_Australian" title="Indigenous Australian">Indigenous Australian</a> youth in the inner-city suburb of <a href="/wiki/Redfern,_New_South_Wales" title="Redfern, New South Wales">Redfern</a> before moving to Los Angeles and eventually becoming a film actress.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathalie_Kelley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005, <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i> executive producers <a href="/wiki/Brad_Kern" title="Brad Kern">Brad Kern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Spelling" title="Aaron Spelling">Aaron Spelling</a> and E. Duke Vincent developed a one-hour pilot for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_WB_Television_Network" title="The WB Television Network">The WB</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed#Mermaid" title="Charmed">Mermaid</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News.com.au_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-News.com.au-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Kelley was cast as the series protagonist Nikki, a mermaid who is rescued by a young man when she washes ashore in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>. The pilot was not picked up as a result of The WB and <a href="/wiki/UPN" title="UPN">UPN</a> merging into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" title="The CW Television Network">The CW</a>; the resulting network passed on the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News.com.au_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-News.com.au-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Kelley has since appeared on both <i><a href="/wiki/Lone_Star_(TV_series)" title="Lone Star (TV series)">Lone Star</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>.</p>
<p>In June 2006, Kelley received her breakout role as Neela in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious:_Tokyo_Drift" title="The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift">The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift</a></i>, the third installment of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious_(film_series)" title="The Fast and the Furious (film series)">The Fast and the Furious</a></i> franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News.com.au_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-News.com.au-1">[1]</a></sup> In March 2008, Kelley appeared in the low-budget drama crime film <i><a href="/wiki/Loaded_(2008_film)" title="Loaded (2008 film)">Loaded</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Metcalfe" title="Jesse Metcalfe">Jesse Metcalfe</a>. In March 2011, Kelley had a small role in the retro comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Home_Tonight_(film)" title="Take Me Home Tonight (film)">Take Me Home Tonight</a></i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/Topher_Grace" title="Topher Grace">Topher Grace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Faris" title="Anna Faris">Anna Faris</a> and fellow Australian <a href="/wiki/Teresa_Palmer" title="Teresa Palmer">Teresa Palmer</a>. The film was shot in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in 2007 but its release was delayed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a> for unknown reasons. The following month, Kelley appeared as the protagonist in the low-budget horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Urban_Explorer" title="Urban Explorer">Urban Explorer</a></i>, which follows a group of four urban explorers who explore an underground world beneath metropolitan Berlin.</p>
<p>In 2010, Kelley played the love interest in the music video for the <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Just_the_Way_You_Are_(Bruno_Mars_song)" title="Just the Way You Are (Bruno Mars song)">Just the Way You Are</a>".</p>
<p>In August 2011, Kelley signed on in a major recurring role in the second season of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> crime drama television series <i><a href="/wiki/Body_of_Proof" title="Body of Proof">Body of Proof</a></i>. Kelley appeared as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dani_Alvarez" title="Dani Alvarez">Dani Alvarez</a> in ten episodes and made her final appearance in the episode "<a href="/wiki/List_of_Body_of_Proof_episodes#Season_2" title="List of Body of Proof episodes">Going Viral Part One</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Kelley played a vampire princess in the video clip "Luna Llena" from Puerto Rican reggaeton duo <a href="/wiki/Baby_Rasta_%26_Gringo" title="Baby Rasta &amp; Gringo">Baby Rasta &amp; Gringo</a>. In 2014, she won a regular role in the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Unreal_(TV_series)" title="Unreal (TV series)">Unreal</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
