<p><b>Nancy Jebet Langat</b> (born 22 August 1981) is a <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenyan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_distance_track_event" title="Middle distance track event">middle distance</a>, runner who specialises in the <a href="/wiki/1500_metres" title="1500 metres">1500 metres</a>. She won the <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medal</a> in the 1500 m at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>, in a personal best time of 4:00.23.</p>
<p>She also competed at the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2005 World Championships in Athletics">2005 World Championships</a>, without reaching the finals. In her younger days she mainly competed in the <a href="/wiki/800_metres" title="800 metres">800 metres</a>, and was successful as a junior.</p>
<p>Langat won gold at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> and became the second Kenyan woman to win an Olympic gold. The first one was <a href="/wiki/Pamela_Jelimo" title="Pamela Jelimo">Pamela Jelimo</a>, who won the 800 m at the same Olympics. At the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2009 World Championships in Athletics">2009 World Championships</a>, Langat did not advance past the semifinals, but finished the season with a win at the <a href="/wiki/2009_IAAF_World_Athletics_Final" title="2009 IAAF World Athletics Final">World Athletics Final</a>.</p>
<p>In 2010 Langat won the prestigious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diamond_League" title="Diamond League">Diamond League</a> tally for the 1500 m. She went undefeated in all of her races against the best runners in the world. He performance at the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_Continental_Cup" title="2010 IAAF Continental Cup">2010 IAAF Continental Cup</a> was a controversial one, as she attempted to pull back <a href="/wiki/Hind_Dehiba" title="Hind Dehiba">Hind Dehiba</a> as the two duelled in the final straight. Langat fell and finished last.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She claimed an 800/1500 m middle distance double at the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2010_Commonwealth_Games" title="Athletics at the 2010 Commonwealth Games">2010 Commonwealth Games</a>, however, and was chosen as the <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_Sports_Personality_of_the_Year" title="Kenyan Sports Personality of the Year">Kenyan Sportswoman of the Year</a> in December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Langat had less success on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Diamond_League" title="2011 Diamond League">2011 Diamond League</a> circuit: on her two appearances, at the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Gala" title="Golden Gala">Golden Gala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prefontaine_Classic" title="Prefontaine Classic">Prefontaine Classic</a>, she came fifth both times. She topped the podium in the 1500 m at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2011_Military_World_Games" title="Athletics at the 2011 Military World Games">2011 Military World Games</a>, but was eliminated in the semi-finals at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2011 World Championships in Athletics">2011 World Championships in Athletics</a>. She suffered a knee injury in 2012 and missed the opportunity to defend her Olympic title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nancy_Langat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nancy Langat is married to <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a> runner <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Cheruiyot" title="Kenneth Cheruiyot">Kenneth Cheruiyot</a>. Her oldest son turned six on the day that she won Olympic Gold. She was recruited by <a href="/wiki/Kenya_Air_Force" title="Kenya Air Force">Kenya Air Force</a> and is based at the <a href="/wiki/Moi_Air_Base" title="Moi Air Base">Moi Air Base</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Her father <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_Langat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joseph Langat (page does not exist)">Joseph Langat</a> was an international level long-distance runner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
