<p><b>Natasha Anne Bedingfield</b> (born 26 November 1981) is a British singer and songwriter. Bedingfield released her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Unwritten" title="Unwritten">Unwritten</a></i>, in 2004, which contained primarily up-tempo pop songs and was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusicreview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicreview-1">[1]</a></sup> It enjoyed international success with more than 2.3Â million copies sold worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sales-2">[2]</a></sup> Bedingfield received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Female_Pop_Vocal_Performance" title="Best Female Pop Vocal Performance">Best Female Pop Vocal Performance</a> for the title track "<a href="/wiki/Unwritten_(song)" title="Unwritten (song)">Unwritten</a>", and at the 2005 and 2006 <a href="/wiki/Brit_Awards" title="Brit Awards">Brit Awards</a> she was nominated for Best British Female Artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <i>Unwritten</i> also produced her only UK number one, "<a href="/wiki/These_Words" title="These Words">These Words</a><i>.</i> Her second album, <i><a href="/wiki/N.B._(album)" title="N.B. (album)">N.B.</a></i> (2007), yielded the UK top 10 singles "<a href="/wiki/I_Wanna_Have_Your_Babies" title="I Wanna Have Your Babies">I Wanna Have Your Babies</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Soulmate_(song)" title="Soulmate (song)">Soulmate</a>". <i>N.B.</i> was not released in North America, but six tracks from it were included with seven new ones and released in 2008 as her third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/N.B._(album)" title="N.B. (album)">Pocketful of Sunshine</a></i>, with the singles "<a href="/wiki/Love_Like_This_(Natasha_Bedingfield_song)" title="Love Like This (Natasha Bedingfield song)">Love Like This</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Pocketful_of_Sunshine" title="Pocketful of Sunshine">Pocketful of Sunshine</a>" earning success on the charts. In December 2010, Bedingfield released her third album in North America, named <i><a href="/wiki/Strip_Me" title="Strip Me">Strip Me</a></i>. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> ranked Bedingfield number 66 on the list of 100 Greatest Women in Music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natasha_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bedingfield was born in <a href="/wiki/Haywards_Heath" title="Haywards Heath">Haywards Heath</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Sussex" title="West Sussex">West Sussex</a> (later moving to <a href="/wiki/Lewisham" title="Lewisham">Lewisham</a>, south <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>) to New Zealand-born Molly and John Bedingfield, who worked in the international charity sector focused on inner-city projects in education, reconciliation, addiction rehab and longterm community development, alongside local government initiatives and faith-based communities in Europe, Asia and Latin America. Bedingfield has three siblings: brothers <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bedingfield" title="Daniel Bedingfield">Daniel</a>, Joshua and a younger sister, <a href="/wiki/Nikola_Rachelle" title="Nikola Rachelle">Nikola</a>. Her interest in music was encouraged by her parents and she took guitar and piano lessons when she was younger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emi_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-emi-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2009)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup> When Bedingfield was a teenager, she and her siblings, Daniel and Nikola, formed the dance/electronic group, The DNA Algorithm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The group provided Bedingfield with the opportunity to explore different musical genres and expand her songwriting abilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She provided vocals for the group which primarily performed <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> music about independence and empowerment, themes that would later be found in her own solo compositions. At age 14, Bedingfield sang vocals on Origins <i>Refined Intricacy</i> (Steelyard Records).</p>
<p>Bedingfield attended a year at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Greenwich" title="University of Greenwich">University of Greenwich</a>, where she studied <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emi_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-emi-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sundaytimes_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-sundaytimes-9">[9]</a></sup> then left to concentrate on singing and songwriting. At first she recorded demos in the garages of friends who had recording studios, which she presented to record companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2009)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup> Throughout the 1990s and early 2000s, Bedingfield composed and recorded songs for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hillsong_London" title="Hillsong London">Hillsong London Church</a>. In 2004, her recordings appeared on the church's live album <i><a href="/wiki/Shout_God%27s_Fame" title="Shout God's Fame">Shout God's Fame</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_music" title="Children's music">children's album</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesus_Is_My_Superhero" title="Jesus Is My Superhero">Jesus Is My Superhero</a></i> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hillsong_Music" title="Hillsong Music">Hillsong Music Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natasha_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.9306:_Early_years_and_Unwritten">2001â06: Early years and <i>Unwritten</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natasha_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2001â06: Early years and Unwritten">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bedingfield was spotted in London by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Winkler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nathan Winkler (page does not exist)">Nathan Winkler</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Tafaro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Michael Tafaro (page does not exist)">Michael Tafaro</a>, song writing and production duo who were looking for a singer to demo their compositions and productions. They went on to record an albums worth of material over the course of the following 2 years. As Daniel Bedingfield's career took off doors started opening for Natasha and she already had songs to demonstrate her talents. The song "<a href="/wiki/The_One_That_Got_Away_(Natasha_Bedingfield_song)" title="The One That Got Away (Natasha Bedingfield song)">The One That Got Away</a>" written by Nathan Winkler (Co-written by Natasha Bedingfield, Steve Kipner, Andrew Frampton and Michael Tafaro) was the only song that made it onto her album "Unwritten"</p>

<p>Bedingfield was introduced to Phonogenic founder and A&amp;R Paul Lisberg by the artist's manager Gary Wilson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-12">[12]</a></sup> Lisberg was initially hesitant, later telling <a href="/wiki/HitQuarters" title="HitQuarters">HitQuarters</a>: "[They] were good but weren't right for us ... "<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-12">[12]</a></sup> However, it was when, at a meeting together, Lisberg heard her singing ability and creative vocal ad-libbing first-hand that he became excited by Bedingfield's potential. Label interest was later confirmed when a trial studio session together with writers Steve Kipner and Andrew Frampton produced songs that were in keeping with Lisberg's vision for Phonogenic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-12">[12]</a></sup> Bedingfield signed a recording contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMG" title="BMG">BMG</a> UK &amp; Ireland, through its imprint <a href="/wiki/Phonogenic_Records" title="Phonogenic Records">Phonogenic Records</a>, in July 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her debut studio album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unwritten_(album)" title="Unwritten (album)">Unwritten</a></i> was released in September 2004 and features collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kipner" title="Steve Kipner">Steve Kipner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danielle_Brisebois" title="Danielle Brisebois">Danielle Brisebois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Lachey" title="Nick Lachey">Nick Lachey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Reilly" title="Jim Reilly">James Reilly</a>, Andrew Frampton, <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Wilkins" title="Wayne Wilkins">Wayne Wilkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kara_DioGuardi" title="Kara DioGuardi">Kara DioGuardi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guy_Chambers" title="Guy Chambers">Guy Chambers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Leonard" title="Patrick Leonard">Patrick Leonard</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Bizarre_(rapper)" title="Bizarre (rapper)">Bizarre</a>. The album contained uptempo <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> songs and influences from <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusicreview_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicreview-1">[1]</a></sup> Bedingfield's <a href="/wiki/Lyrics" title="Lyrics">lyrics</a> focused on independence, opportunism and female empowerment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emi_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-emi-6">[6]</a></sup> Reviews of the album were generally positive<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and it was described as a "well-made pop album for the new millennium" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusicreview_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicreview-1">[1]</a></sup> The album debuted on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> at number one and reached the top thirty in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It sold well, reaching multi-platinum status in the United Kingdom<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and Gold status in the United States<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The first single released from the album was "<a href="/wiki/Single_(Natasha_Bedingfield_song)" title="Single (Natasha Bedingfield song)">Single</a>", which reached number three on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The song's lyrics and accompanying music video depict Bedingfield's single lifestyle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capgains_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-capgains-18">[18]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/These_Words" title="These Words">These Words</a>" was released as the album's second single. The song became Bedingfield's first UK number one and US top twenty single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Unwritten_(song)" title="Unwritten (song)">Unwritten</a>" was released as the album's third single in 2004, the song reached number six on the UK Singles Chart and it also became the most played song on US radio in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/I_Bruise_Easily" title="I Bruise Easily">I Bruise Easily</a>" was released as the album's fourth single, it did not perform as well as its predecessors but was still a moderate success, reaching number twelve on the UK Singles Chart.</p>
<p>At the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Brit_Awards" title="Brit Awards">Brit Awards</a>, Bedingfield was nominated for four awards, though did not win any of her nominated categories. She was also nominated for an award at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Brit_Awards" title="2006 Brit Awards">2006 Brit Awards</a>, and in 2007, she was nominated for "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Female_Pop_Vocal_Performance" title="Best Female Pop Vocal Performance">Best Female Pop Vocal Performance</a>" at the <a href="/wiki/49th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="49th Annual Grammy Awards">2007 Grammy Awards</a>, though was again unsuccessful in winning in any of her nominated categories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>Bedingfield's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/N.B._(album)" title="N.B. (album)">N.B.</a></i>, was released in Europe in April 2007. In North America (the US and Canada), the album was released under the title "Pocketful of Sunshine" in January 2008. The album received mixed reviews by critics and peaked at number nine in the UK. "<a href="/wiki/I_Wanna_Have_Your_Babies" title="I Wanna Have Your Babies">I Wanna Have Your Babies</a>", the album's <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a>, was moderately successful, reaching number seven in the UK, number eight in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and the top fifty in various other markets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-babies_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-babies-22">[22]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Soulmate_(song)" title="Soulmate (song)">Soulmate</a>" was released as the album's second single on 2 July 2007 and also peaked at number seven in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> To promote <i>N.B.</i>, Bedingfield embarked on the <a href="/wiki/FutureSex/LoveShow" title="FutureSex/LoveShow">FutureSex/LoveShow</a> tour in May 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2010, "Pocketful of Sunshine" received major exposure as the somewhat mocked soundtrack to the American teenage comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Easy_A" title="Easy A">Easy A</a></i>.</p>
<p>On 1 July 2007, Bedingfield performed "Unwritten" at the <a href="/wiki/Concert_for_Diana" title="Concert for Diana">Concert for Diana</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, London, an event which celebrated the life of <a href="/wiki/Diana,_Princess_of_Wales" title="Diana, Princess of Wales">Princess Diana</a> almost 10 years after her death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The North American version of her second album featured six songs from <i><a href="/wiki/N.B._(album)" title="N.B. (album)">N.B.</a></i>. The album was released on 22 January 2008, after the lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Love_Like_This_(Natasha_Bedingfield_song)" title="Love Like This (Natasha Bedingfield song)">Love like This</a>", was released in September 2007 and charted at No. 11 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. The title track was released as the second single in February 2008. The single peaked at No. 5 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. On 18 January 2008, Bedingfield's UK tour was cancelled for the second time, one month before it was supposed to start, so she could spend more time promoting her album in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bad_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-bad-27">[27]</a></sup> In August 2008, Bedingfield and other singers including <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="BeyoncÃ©">BeyoncÃ©</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Etheridge" title="Melissa Etheridge">Melissa Etheridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leona_Lewis" title="Leona Lewis">Leona Lewis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> recorded the charity single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Just_Stand_Up" title="Just Stand Up">Just Stand Up</a>" produced by <a href="/wiki/Babyface_(musician)" title="Babyface (musician)">Babyface</a> and <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a>, to support "<a href="/wiki/Stand_Up_to_Cancer" title="Stand Up to Cancer">Stand Up to Cancer</a>". On 5 September 2008, the singers performed it live on TV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9311:_Strip_Me_and_Strip_Me_Away">2009â11: <i>Strip Me</i> and <i>Strip Me Away</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natasha_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009â11: Strip Me and Strip Me Away">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Bedingfield stated on her blog that she would start work on her new album in early 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> She was confirmed to be writing for the new album while on holiday in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>; she said "I'm here for a couple of weeks to do a bit of writing. It is such an inspiring place to write music. I love coming here as often as I can, usually once a year to fuel up on ideas for my next album".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> She also confirmed she was working with Brian Kennedy, who produced/co wrote <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>'s hit single "<a href="/wiki/Disturbia_(song)" title="Disturbia (song)">Disturbia</a>" and was writing songs with him while on tour<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> with a view to having the album released by the end of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Bedingfield also stated that <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Sparro" title="Sam Sparro">Sam Sparro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Elizondo" title="Mike Elizondo">Mike Elizondo</a> would be working on the record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bedingfield confirmed that the album would be ready for fall 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In an interview with IN:DEMAND Producer, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Tedder" title="Ryan Tedder">Ryan Tedder</a> said "I'm gonna try and bring Natasha Bedingfield back here in the UK with some quirky, kinda weird stuff".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On 18 March Bedingfield stated on her official site that she was in L.A. and she was putting the finishing touches on her album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Touch_(Natasha_Bedingfield_song)" title="Touch (Natasha Bedingfield song)">Touch</a>", the lead single from the album, was made available as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> 18 May 2010. Bedingfield performed the song on the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i> on 24 May 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> "Touch" was officially sent to US radio stations on 29 June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On 15 July Bedingfield announced via Twitter that the title of her new album would be <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strip_Me_(Natasha_Bedingfield_album)" title="Strip Me (Natasha Bedingfield album)">Strip Me</a></i>. The album was originally scheduled for release on 9 November 2010, but was released on 7 December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The second single, also titled "<a href="/wiki/Strip_Me_(song)" title="Strip Me (song)">Strip Me</a>", was sent to US radio on 30 August 2010 and it became available as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> on 21 September 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> It was written by Bedingfield, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Tedder" title="Ryan Tedder">Ryan Tedder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Wilkins" title="Wayne Wilkins">Wayne Wilkins</a>. The song was selected to head the soundtrack for the 2010 comedy<i><a href="/wiki/Morning_Glory_(2010_film)" title="Morning Glory (2010 film)">Morning Glory</a></i>. Bedingfield appeared on rapper <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>'s debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pink_Friday" title="Pink Friday">Pink Friday</a></i>, on a track called "Last Chance", and on <a href="/wiki/Rascal_Flatts" title="Rascal Flatts">Rascal Flatts</a>' album <i><a href="/wiki/Nothing_Like_This" title="Nothing Like This">Nothing Like This</a></i> on a track called "<a href="/wiki/Easy_(Rascal_Flatts_song)" title="Easy (Rascal Flatts song)">Easy</a>", released as the album's third single. On 31 December 2010/1 January 2011, Bedingfield appeared on <a href="/wiki/Dick_Clark%27s_New_Year%27s_Rockin%27_Eve" title="Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve">Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve</a>.</p>
<p>In 2010, Bedingfield joined forces with Avon as a celebrity judge for Avon Voices,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Avon's first ever global, online singing talent search for women and songwriting competition for men and women. On 6 April 2011, Bedingfield visited Radio Hamburg and confirmed that the song "Pocketful of Sunshine" (originally the second single from her US album "Pocketful of Sunshine") will be the first European single from the forthcoming European release of <i>Strip Me</i>. The European version of <i>Strip Me</i> will be called <i>Strip Me Away</i>. The lead single "Pocketful of Sunshine" was released on 13 May in Germany. The album "Strip Me Away" will be released in June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Bedingfield was featured on <a href="/wiki/Simple_Plan" title="Simple Plan">Simple Plan</a>'s new single from their new album <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Your_Heart_On!" title="Get Your Heart On!">Get Your Heart On!</a></i>, released on 21 June 2011. The song, "<a href="/wiki/Jet_Lag_(song)" title="Jet Lag (song)">Jet Lag</a>" was released on 26 April 2011 as the band's first single from their fourth album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> On 5 July, Bedingfield performed her song "Weightless" from her "Strip Me" album on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">Filipina</a> singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charice" title="Charice">Charice</a> confirmed that Bedingfield has written a song for her called "Lighthouse", which is included on her second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Infinity_(Charice_album)" title="Infinity (Charice album)">Infinity</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> On 9 November 2011, Bedingfield wowed American audiences when she joined Rascal Flatts to perform "Easy," on the Country Music Awards show on ABC. Bedingfield has released a Christmas song 'Shake Up Christmas',<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> which has been confirmed to be on the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> Christmas television advertisements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of April 2012, Bedingfield announced that she is working on her fourth studio album, titled "The Next Chapter" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> mentioning her plans to release the album worldwide,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> not just focusing on America. On 11 September, Bedingfield was featured on <a href="/wiki/Lifehouse_(band)" title="Lifehouse (band)">Lifehouse</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Between_the_Raindrops" title="Between the Raindrops">Between the Raindrops</a>". On 13 May 2013, she appeared as a guest judge on the New Zealand version of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_series_1)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand series 1)">The X Factor</a></i> alongside her brother <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bedingfield" title="Daniel Bedingfield">Daniel Bedingfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The album will feature production from <a href="/wiki/RedOne" title="RedOne">RedOne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Luke" title="Dr. Luke">Dr. Luke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benny_Blanco" title="Benny Blanco">Benny Blanco</a>, Paul Williams and <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Altman" title="Marshall Altman">Marshall Altman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Bedingfield composed the music of "Non mi ami", a single by <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer-Songwriter" title="Singer-Songwriter">singer-songwriter</a> <a href="/wiki/Giorgia_(singer)" title="Giorgia (singer)">Giorgia</a>, included on the album <i>Senza Paura</i>. In 2014, Bedingfield contributed to the soundtrack for the <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> animated film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pirate_Fairy" title="The Pirate Fairy">The Pirate Fairy</a></i>, with a track titled "Who I Am".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> On 13 January 2015, Bedingfield released a charity single with Philosophy Skin Care titled "Hope".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> With the release of "Hope", Bedingfield announced via Twitter that she will become more publicly active again, promising "many more music releases to come".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> The release of the music video for "Hope" followed a day later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> She has since collaborated with Belgian record producer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basto_(musician)" title="Basto (musician)">Basto</a>. The duo released a single titled "Unicorn" in March 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-musical_projects">Non-musical projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natasha_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Non-musical projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bedingfield has donated time and money to organisations such as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Angels&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Global Angels (page does not exist)">Global Angels</a>, an international children's charity founded by her mother, Molly Bedingfield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-global_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-global-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> She became associated with the organisation in 2006 and said she hoped to help "people around the world, particularly children, who live in conditions that would horrify us".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bedingfield is now an ambassador for Global Angels and in November 2006 she visited India for three weeks in support of the charity. She visited an <a href="/wiki/Orphanage" title="Orphanage">orphanage</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Refugee_camp" title="Refugee camp">refugee camp</a> for former child prostitutes in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> to learn more about the situation and the conditions in these areas. Bedingfield later expressed her shock at what she had witnessed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youmag_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-youmag-60">[60]</a></sup> Video diaries filmed during her trip were posted on her official website in early 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2009)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup> She is also an advocate for Stop the Traffik, a global coalition which works to end <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trafficking_in_human_beings" title="Trafficking in human beings">human trafficking</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-global_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-global-57">[57]</a></sup> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Product_Red" title="Product Red">(RED) campaign</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emi_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-emi-6">[6]</a></sup> In a 2008 rockumentary, Call+Response, headed by Justin Dillon, Bedingfield performed acoustic versions of "Unwritten" and "Soulmate" in support of the film's cause: a movement against current slavery and <a href="/wiki/Human_trafficking" title="Human trafficking">human trafficking</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2009)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Bedingfield showed interest in acting and made her debut in the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> video game <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/From_Russia_with_Love_(video_game)" title="From Russia with Love (video game)">From Russia with Love</a></i> in November 2005. She voiced the character Elizabeth Stark, the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">British Prime Minister's</a> daughter who is kidnapped in the opening sequence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamer_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-gamer-63">[63]</a></sup> Bedingfield commented that she would like to do more acting, but only if the film "was good enough, and it was a role that [would] fit me."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamer_63-1"><a href="#cite_note-gamer-63">[63]</a></sup> She has made an appearance in the seventh season finale of the Canadian television series <i><a href="/wiki/Degrassi:_The_Next_Generation" title="Degrassi: The Next Generation">Degrassi: The Next Generation</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Bedingfield also guest starred on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_(TV_channel)" title="Nickelodeon (TV channel)">Nickelodeon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">mockumentary</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Naked_Brothers_Band_(TV_series)" title="The Naked Brothers Band (TV series)">The Naked Brothers Band</a>'</i>s <i><a href="/wiki/Christmas_Special_(TV_special)" title="Christmas Special (TV special)">Christmas Special</a>,</i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Whoopi_Goldberg" title="Whoopi Goldberg">Whoopi Goldberg</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leon_G._Thomas_III" title="Leon G. Thomas III">Leon Thomas III</a>. She has made guest appearances on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Lipstick_Jungle_(TV_series)" title="Lipstick Jungle (TV series)">Lipstick Jungle</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>More recently, she guest starred on Nickelodeon's <i><a href="/wiki/True_Jackson,_VP" title="True Jackson, VP">True Jackson, VP</a></i> in November 2009. She sang the song "These Words". In 2012, Bedingfield made an appearance in three <a href="/wiki/List_of_Web_Therapy_webisodes#Season_4" title="List of Web Therapy webisodes">episodes</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Web_Therapy" title="Web Therapy">Web Therapy</a></i> fourth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> In December 2012, <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> announced that Bedingfield would perform at their 2012 <a href="/wiki/VH1_Divas" title="VH1 Divas">VH1 Divas</a> show, a concert benefiting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Save_The_Music_Foundation" title="Save The Music Foundation">Save The Music Foundation</a> charity. Bedingfeld will perform <a href="/wiki/Deee-Lite" title="Deee-Lite">Deee-Lite</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Groove_Is_in_the_Heart" title="Groove Is in the Heart">Groove Is in the Heart</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Bootsy_Collins" title="Bootsy Collins">Bootsy Collins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iggy_Azalea" title="Iggy Azalea">Iggy Azalea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> On 22 September 2014, Bedingfield performed a song titled "Love Song to the Earth" at the <a href="/wiki/Equator_Prize" title="Equator Prize">United Nations 2014 Equator Prize Gala</a> in New York City. The song is a special anthem she and Toby Gad co-wrote for the <a href="/wiki/Climate_Summit" title="Climate Summit">United Nations Climate Summit 2014</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natasha_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

The couple celebrated their wedding in <a href="/wiki/Malibu,_California" title="Malibu, California">Malibu, California</a> on 21 March 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people.com_69-1"><a href="#cite_note-people.com-69">[69]</a></sup></p>

<p>Bedingfield's debut album <i>Unwritten</i> contains primarily uptempo pop songs and was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusicreview_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicreview-1">[1]</a></sup> It enjoyed international success with over three million copies sold worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> In 2007, she received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination for "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Female_Pop_Vocal_Performance" title="Best Female Pop Vocal Performance">Best Female Pop Vocal Performance</a>" for the song "<a href="/wiki/Unwritten_(song)" title="Unwritten (song)">Unwritten</a>", the third single from the album of the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grammy_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-grammy-72">[72]</a></sup> Bedingfield's second album <i>N.B.</i> yielded the singles "<a href="/wiki/Soulmate_(song)" title="Soulmate (song)">Soulmate</a>"; "<a href="/wiki/Say_It_Again_(Natasha_Bedingfield_song)" title="Say It Again (Natasha Bedingfield song)">Say It Again</a>"; and "<a href="/wiki/I_Wanna_Have_Your_Babies" title="I Wanna Have Your Babies">I Wanna Have Your Babies</a>", written and produced by Natasha Bedingfield, <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Wilkins" title="Wayne Wilkins">Wayne Wilkins</a>, Andrew Frampton and Steve Kipner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> Bedingfield has received four nominations from the BRIT Awards but has won none.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brit_74-0"><a href="#cite_note-brit-74">[74]</a></sup> As of July 2008, she has won five awards from eleven nominations.</p>
