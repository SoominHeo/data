<p><i><b>You're All Surrounded</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">너희들은 포위됐다</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">너희들은 包圍됐다</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Neohuideureun Powidwaetda</span></i>) is a 2014 <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">South Korean television series</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Lee_Seung-gi" title="Lee Seung-gi">Lee Seung-gi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cha_Seung-won" title="Cha Seung-won">Cha Seung-won</a>, <a href="/wiki/Go_Ara" title="Go Ara">Go Ara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ahn_Jae-hyun" title="Ahn Jae-hyun">Ahn Jae-hyun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Park_Jung-min_(actor)" title="Park Jung-min (actor)">Park Jung-min</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oh_Yoon-ah" title="Oh Yoon-ah">Oh Yoon-ah</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sung_Ji-ru" title="Sung Ji-ru">Sung Ji-ru</a>. It aired on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> from May 7 to July 17, 2014 on Wednesdays and Thursdays at 21:55 for 20 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=You%27re_All_Surrounded&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After witnessing his mother's murder at a young age, Kim Ji-yong (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Seung-gi" title="Lee Seung-gi">Lee Seung-gi</a>) became a detective in order to investigate his mother's death. He later changed his name to Eun Dae-gu to be unnoticed by his mother's killer. On his way, he reunites with Eo Soo-sun (<a href="/wiki/Go_Ara" title="Go Ara">Go Ara</a>), whom attended the same middle school and gradually develops a romantic relationship. At the same time, he spies on his team leader, Seo Pan-seok (<a href="/wiki/Cha_Seung-won" title="Cha Seung-won">Cha Seung-won</a>), believing that he is related to the killer of his mother's case. As he unravels the truth behind the incident 11 years ago, he learns about his past, identity as well as making deep connections with people around him.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=You%27re_All_Surrounded&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=You%27re_All_Surrounded&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lee_Seung-gi" title="Lee Seung-gi">Lee Seung-gi</a> as Eun Dae-gu / Kim Ji-yong<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ahn_Do-gyu" title="Ahn Do-gyu">Ahn Do-gyu</a> as young Ji-yong</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>He is brusque, intelligent (with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IQ" title="IQ">IQ</a> of 150), and has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photographic_memory" title="Photographic memory">photographic memory</a>. His problem with authority stems from his painful past; as a young boy named Kim Ji-yong living in <a href="/wiki/Masan" title="Masan">Masan</a>, his mother was murdered in front of his eyes when she attempted to testify as a witness to a crime. To avoid getting killed himself, he changed his name to Eun Dae-gu and grew up in an orphanage. He eventually becomes a detective to bring his mother's killer to justice, while spying on his boss Seo Pan-seok, whom he believes conspired with his mother's killer.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cha_Seung-won" title="Cha Seung-won">Cha Seung-won</a> as Seo Pan-seok<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The short-tempered captain of the <a href="/wiki/Violent_crime" title="Violent crime">Violent Crimes</a> Unit. A legend at the precinct, Pan-seok is known for closing the toughest cases and catching the most notorious criminals. But he has never forgotten a long-ago unsolved case involving the murder of a witness and the subsequent disappearance of her son, Kim Ji-yong, a case which is inextricably linked to his own family tragedy: the accidental death of his young son.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Go_Ara" title="Go Ara">Go Ara</a> as Eo Soo-sun
<ul>
<li>Ji Woo as young Soo-sun</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Bold and persistent, Soo-sun applied seven times before being accepted into the police academy. She is the only female in the new class of officers. Soo-sun and Ji-yong went to the same middle school in Masan, but she initially doesn't recognize him in his present incarnation as Dae-gu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ahn_Jae-hyun" title="Ahn Jae-hyun">Ahn Jae-hyun</a> as Park Tae-il</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Tae-il is secretive and laidback, but has a way with the ladies. He was in his first year of <a href="/wiki/Residency_(medicine)" title="Residency (medicine)">residency</a> before deciding to leave medicine and become a detective, a decision his family disapproves of which led to his estrangement from them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Park_Jung-min_(actor)" title="Park Jung-min (actor)">Park Jung-min</a> as Ji Gook</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>A geeky and talkative guy whose sole reason for applying to the force was because he wanted to live in <a href="/wiki/Gangnam_(Seoul)" title="Gangnam (Seoul)">Gangnam</a>. He unexpectedly becomes best friends with Tae-il, and develops a crush on Soo-sun.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oh_Yoon-ah" title="Oh Yoon-ah">Oh Yoon-ah</a> as Kim Sa-kyung</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Sa-kyung is a tenacious detective with strong convictions, whose personal goal is to pave the way for female cops to work in her precinct without being <a href="/wiki/Sexism" title="Sexism">discriminated</a> against. She is also Pan-seok's ex-wife, and the divorced couple are forced to work together when she is transferred to his police station as the new head detective of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Missing_Person" title="Missing Person">Missing Persons</a> Unit. She divorced him due to the grief and anger she felt over their son's death, but upon meeting again at the precinct, Sa-kyung and Pan-seok's love for each other resurfaces and they begin dating again.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sung_Ji-ru" title="Sung Ji-ru">Sung Ji-ru</a> as Lee Eung-do</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Pan-seok's older colleague who looks upon the new recruits affectionately.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seo_Yi-sook&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seo Yi-sook (page does not exist)">Seo Yi-sook</a> as Kang Seok-soon</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The chief of police. She secretly sponsored and encouraged Dae-gu for eleven years up until police academy, but may have suspicious ties to Assemblyman Yoo, a crooked politician.</dd>
</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=You%27re_All_Surrounded&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While filming an action scene on June 9, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Seung-gi" title="Lee Seung-gi">Lee Seung-gi</a> was poked in his left eye by a prop knife, causing <a href="/wiki/Corneal_abrasion" title="Corneal abrasion">corneal damage</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Intraocular_hemorrhage" title="Intraocular hemorrhage">intraocular hemorrhage</a>. His injury required Lee to rest for a few days, which resulted in the preemption of the 10th episode; a special aired instead on June 11, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
