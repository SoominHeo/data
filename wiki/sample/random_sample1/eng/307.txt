<p><b>. NA</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CcTLD" title="CcTLD">ccTLD</a>) for <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> corresponding to the two letter code from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISO-3166" title="ISO-3166">ISO-3166</a> standard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iso_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-iso-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The registry accredits both Namibian and foreign registrars. Registrars access the Registry and register domains using either a web-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GUI" title="GUI">GUI</a> or the industry standard <a href="/wiki/Extensible_Provisioning_Protocol" title="Extensible Provisioning Protocol">EPP</a> protocol.</p>
<p>.NA was established on 8 May 1991. The ccTLD manager is NA-NiC (Namibian Network Information Centre).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iana_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-iana-3">[3]</a></sup> The Namibian Parliament passed a Communications Act in 2009 containing various provisions regarding the ccTLD;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> however as of the beginning of 2015 these had not yet entered into force.</p>
<p>Registrations are available at both the second level or at the third level beneath various names that include some apparently redundant choices (e.g., both <b>.co.na</b> and <b>.com.na</b> for commercial entities).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_registration_costs">Domain registration costs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Domain registration costs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Domain registration prices to the end-user are now set by registrars in competition with each other. Wholesale prices (the cost to the registrars) depend on the level at which a registration is made (i.e. whether at second-level or a third-level registration) and also whether the registrant is domestic or foreign. The second-level is considered 'premium', so the cheapest domains would be a registration by a local organisation at third-level (such as the NamNumbers telephone directoryt at TELECOM.COM.NA<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telecom_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-telecom-5">[5]</a></sup> whilst the highest prices are paid by non-Namibian entities registering at the second-level (such as BRITISHCOUNCIL.NA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britishcouncil_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-britishcouncil-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On occasion, it has been suggested that <b>. NA</b> may have some unofficial use as meaning <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> but there is little or no evidence for this. NA-NiC is a member of CoCCA and uses their Dispute Resolution.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Secure_DNS">Secure DNS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Secure DNS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>.NA is an early adopter of the <a href="/wiki/Domain_Name_System_Security_Extensions" title="Domain Name System Security Extensions">Domain Name System Security Extensions</a>, the. NA root zone having been signed with DNSSEC since 1 September 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icann_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-icann-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
