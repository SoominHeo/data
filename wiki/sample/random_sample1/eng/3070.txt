<p><i><b>Missing You</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: 널 기다리며) is a 2016 South Korean revenge <a href="/wiki/Thriller_film" title="Thriller film">thriller film</a> written and directed by Mo Hong-jin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ki_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ki-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hancinema_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-hancinema-1">[1]</a></sup> It was released in South Korea on March 10, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hancinema_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-hancinema-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Missing_You_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Hee-Joo (<a href="/wiki/Shim_Eun-kyung" title="Shim Eun-kyung">Shim Eun-Kyung</a>) was 7-years-old her father was murdered. Her father worked as a detective and was working on a serial killer case. The killer, Ki-Bum (<a href="/wiki/Kim_Sung-oh" title="Kim Sung-oh">Kim Sung-Oh</a>), was eventually arrested, but found guilty for only one murder. 15 years later, Ki-Bum is released from prison. Detective Dae-Young (<a href="/wiki/Yoon_Je-moon" title="Yoon Je-moon">Yoon Je-Moon</a>) worked on the original case with Hee-Joo's father and he has not given up getting a murder conviction for his former partner. Meanwhile, Hee-Joo has waited patiently for Kim-Bum's release.</p>
