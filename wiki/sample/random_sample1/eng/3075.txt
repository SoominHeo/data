<p><i><b>Day of the Woman</b></i> (better known by its re-release title, <i><b>I Spit on Your Grave</b></i>) is a 1978 American <a href="/wiki/Rape_and_revenge_film" title="Rape and revenge film">rape-and-revenge</a> <a href="/wiki/Exploitation_film" title="Exploitation film">exploitation</a>-<a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a>. The film received a limited release, with a wider release in 1980.</p>
<p>The film is noted for its controversy and prominent film critics condemned the film for its graphic violence, <a href="/wiki/Nudity" title="Nudity">nudity</a>, obscene language, and lengthy depictions of <a href="/wiki/Gang_rape" title="Gang rape">gang rape</a> which take up 30 minutes of the film's run-time, and the motion picture remains controversial to this day, even being considered to be one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_films_considered_the_worst" title="List of films considered the worst">worst films ever made</a>. The film was named in 2010 one of <a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)"><i>Time</i></a>'s Top 10 Ridiculously Violent Movies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was <a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_(2010_film)" title="I Spit on Your Grave (2010 film)">remade</a> that same year. <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> famously reviewed the film and called it a "vile bag of garbage". He gave it the rare score of 0 of 4 stars.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Spit_on_Your_Grave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> <a href="/wiki/Short_story" title="Short story">short story</a> writer <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Hills" title="Jennifer Hills">Jennifer Hills</a> (<a href="/wiki/Camille_Keaton" title="Camille Keaton">Camille Keaton</a>) rents an isolated cottage in <a href="/wiki/Kent,_Connecticut" title="Kent, Connecticut">Kent, Connecticut</a> on Park Hill Lane near the <a href="/wiki/Housatonic_River" title="Housatonic River">Housatonic River</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Litchfield_County" title="Litchfield County">Litchfield County</a> countryside to write her first novel. The arrival in town of the attractive and independent young woman attracts the attention of Johnny (Eron Taber), the gas station manager, and Stanley (Anthony Nichols) and Andy (Gunter Kleemann), two unemployed men who hang around the gas station. Jennifer has her groceries delivered by Matthew (Richard Pace), who is mildly mentally disabled. Jennifer is wearing tight clothing and Matthew peeps at her breasts. Matthew is friends with the other three men and reports back to them about the beautiful woman he met, claiming he saw her breasts.</p>
<p>Stanley and Andy start cruising by the cottage in their boat and prowl around the house at night. One day, while Jennifer is relaxing in her canoe, they surprise her in their speedboat and tow her to shore. As she tries to escape, she is met by Johnny, while Matthew hides in bushes nearby. She realizes they planned her abduction so Matthew can lose his virginity. Jennifer fights back but is chased by the men through the forest, they then rip her <a href="/wiki/Bikini" title="Bikini">bikini</a> off and hold her to the ground. But Matthew refuses to have sex with her, so Johnny rapes her. They allow her to escape but track her down shortly afterward. Andy proceeds to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anally" title="Anally">anally</a> rape her; after she crawls back to her house, they attack her again. Matthew finally rapes her after drinking alcohol, but he says that he can not reach orgasm with the other men watching him. The other men ridicule her book and rip up the manuscript, and Stanley sexually assaults her. She passes out; but, after the men leave, Johnny realizes she is a witness to their crimes and orders Matthew to stab her to death. Matthew cannot bring himself to do this, so he dabs the knife in the blood on her cheek and returns to the other men, claiming he has killed her.</p>
<p>In the following days, a <a href="/wiki/Psychological_trauma" title="Psychological trauma">traumatized</a> Jennifer pieces both herself and her manuscript back together. She goes to church and asks for forgiveness for what she plans to do. The men learn Jennifer has survived and beat Matthew up for deceiving them. Jennifer calls in a grocery order, knowing Matthew will deliver it. He takes the groceries and a knife. At the cabin, Jennifer entices him to have sex with her under a tree. As he becomes oblivious to his surroundings, she strings a noose around his neck and hangs him, then cuts the rope and his body drops into the shallow edge of the lake.</p>
<p>At the gas station, Jennifer seductively directs Johnny to enter her car. She stops halfway to her house, points a handgun at him, and orders him to remove all his clothing. Johnny insists the rapes were her fault because she enticed the men by parading around in revealing clothing. She pretends to believe this as Johnny grabs the gun from her hand and throws it to the ground. She invites him back to her cottage for a hot bath, where she gives him a <a href="/wiki/Handjob" title="Handjob">handjob</a>. When Johnny says that Matthew has been reported missing, Jennifer states that she killed Matthew; and, as he nears orgasm, Jennifer takes the knife Matthew brought with him from its hiding place under the bathmat and severs Johnny's genitals. She then leaves the bathroom, locks the door behind her, and listens to classical music as Johnny screams and bleeds to death. She later dumps the body in the basement and burns his clothes in the fireplace.</p>
<p>Stanley and Andy learn that Johnny is missing and take their boat to the shore of Jennifer's cabin. Andy goes ashore with an axe. Jennifer swims out to the boat and climbs aboard before Stanley realizes she is there and pushes him overboard. Andy tries to attack her when she speeds past him in the boat, but she escapes with the axe. Andy swims out to rescue Stanley, but Jennifer plunges the axe into Andy's back, killing him. While the outboard motor is off, Stanley moves towards the boat and grabs hold of the motor to climb aboard, begging Jennifer not to kill him. She repeats the same words that he used against her during the sexual assaults: "Suck it, bitch!" Jennifer then starts the motor and traps Stanley in the spinning propeller, disemboweling him. Jennifer then speeds away in the boat as the film ends.</p>

<p>The house that Jennifer rents and its grounds, the river and the gas station, were all located in <a href="/wiki/Kent,_Connecticut" title="Kent, Connecticut">Kent, Connecticut</a>. The waterway is a section of the <a href="/wiki/Housatonic_River" title="Housatonic River">Housatonic River</a>. The house in real life was owned by Zarchi's friend Nouri Haviv, who photographed this film. Most of the house interior scenes were also shot inside this house.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Zarchi first visited the house while developing the script and its ambience and location influenced the development of the story.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Title">Title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Spit_on_Your_Grave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Title">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film's original title was <i>Day of the Woman</i>. It was also shown under the title <i>I Hate Your Guts</i> and <i>The Rape and Revenge of Jennifer Hill</i>. The title was changed to <i>I Spit on Your Grave</i> for the 1980 re-release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Zarchi was unable to find a distributor, so he distributed the film himself. It played a number of engagements in rural <a href="/wiki/Drive-in_theater" title="Drive-in theater">drive-in theaters</a>, but only for brief runs each time, and Zarchi barely made back what he spent in advertising. In 1980, it was picked up for distribution by the Jerry Gross Organization. A condition of this re-release was that they could change the title to anything they wished. It was at this time the film was retitled <i>I Spit on Your Grave</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Spit_on_Your_Grave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>I Spit on Your Grave</i> received negative reviews from critics. Film critic <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> gave the film a thumbs down, referring to it as "a vile bag of garbage...without a shred of artistic distinction," adding that "Attending it was one of the most depressing experiences of my life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He mentioned in his review a female member of the audience (one of many people who randomly talked aloud) who had "feminist solidarity for the movie's heroine". He wrote, "I wanted to ask if she'd been appalled by the movie's hour of rape scenes". Ebert was also one of many to cite the movie's poor production quality as a weakness in addition to the scenes he found offensive, stating "The story of 'I Spit on Your Grave' is told with moronic simplicity. These horrible events are shown with an absolute minimum of dialogue, which is so poorly recorded that it often cannot be heard. There is no attempt to develop the personalities of the characters - they are, simply, a girl and four men, one of them mentally retarded. The movie is nothing more or less than a series of attacks on the girl and then her attacks on the men, interrupted only by an unbelievably grotesque and inappropriate scene in which she enters a church and asks forgiveness for the murders she plans to commit".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Ebert also included it on his "most hated" list and considered it to be the worst movie ever made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Both Ebert and fellow critic <a href="/wiki/Gene_Siskel" title="Gene Siskel">Gene Siskel</a> blasted the movie on their television program <i><a href="/wiki/Sneak_Previews" title="Sneak Previews">Sneak Previews</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In a later episode, Siskel and Ebert chose the film as the worst film of 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Siskel would join Ebert in calling the film one of the worst ever made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Critic Luke Y. Thompson of <i><a href="/wiki/New_Times_LA" title="New Times LA">The New Times</a></i> stated that "defenders of the film have argued that it's actually pro-woman, due to the fact that the female lead wins in the end, which is sort of like saying that cockfights are pro-rooster because there's always one left standing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiton_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spiton-9">[9]</a></sup> Film critic <a href="/wiki/Mark_Kermode" title="Mark Kermode">Mark Kermode</a> has opined that it is "deeply, deeply problematic at the very best of times" and is not as interesting as earlier exploitation films such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_House_on_the_Left_(1972_film)" title="The Last House on the Left (1972 film)">The Last House on the Left</a></i>. Critic David Keyes named it the worst film of the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiton_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spiton-9">[9]</a></sup> This led to the film's removal from a major theatre.</p>
<p><i>Encyclopedia of Horror</i> notes that the film attracted much debate for and against, frequently involving people who clearly had not actually seen the film. "The men are so grossly unattractive and the rapes so harrowing, long-drawn-out and starkly presented it is hard to imagine most male spectators identifying with the perpetrators, especially as the film's narrative structure and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mise_en_sc%C3%A8ne" title="Mise en scène">mise-en-scene</a></i> force the spectator to view the action from Keaton's point of view. Further, there is no suggestion that she 'asked for it' or enjoyed it, except, of course, in the rapists' own perceptions, from which the film is careful to distance itself." The book continues that the scenes of revenge were "grotesquely misread by some critics" as Jennifer only "pretends to have enjoyed the rape so as to lure the men to their destruction", and that in these scenes the film is critiquing "familiar male arguments about women 'bringing it on themselves'" as "simply sexist, self-excusing rhetoric and are quite clearly presented as such".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milne.2C_Tom_1986._p_329_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Milne.2C_Tom_1986._p_329-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The initial criticism was followed by reappraisals of the film. Michael Kaminski's 2007 article for the website "Obsessed with Film", titled "Is 'I Spit on Your Grave' Really a Misunderstood Feminist Film?" argues that, when understood within the context in which director Zarchi was inspired to make it, the movie may be equally appropriate to analyze as "feminist wish-fulfillment" and a vehicle of personal expression reacting to violence against women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>A reappraisal was made by <a href="/wiki/Carol_J._Clover" title="Carol J. Clover">Carol J. Clover</a> in the third chapter of her 1992 book <i><a href="/wiki/Men,_Women,_and_Chainsaws" title="Men, Women, and Chainsaws">Men, Women, and Chainsaws</a></i>. Clover notes that she and others like her "appreciate, however grudgingly, the way in which [the movie's] brutal simplicity exposes a mainspring of popular culture." Clover further argues that the film's sympathies are entirely with Jennifer, that the male audience is meant to identify with her, and not with the attackers, and that the point of the film is a <a href="/wiki/Sadomasochism" title="Sadomasochism">masochistic</a> identification with pain used to justify the bloody <a href="/wiki/Catharsis" title="Catharsis">catharsis</a> of revenge. Clover wrote that in her opinion the film owes a debt to <i><a href="/wiki/Deliverance" title="Deliverance">Deliverance</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The British feminist <a href="/wiki/Julie_Bindel" title="Julie Bindel">Julie Bindel</a>, who was involved in pickets outside cinemas in Leeds when the film was released, has said that she was wrong about the film and that it is a feminist film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Censorship_and_film_bans">Censorship and film bans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Spit_on_Your_Grave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Censorship and film bans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many nations, such as <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>, banned the film altogether, claiming that it "glorified violence against women". <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> initially banned the film, but in the 1990s decided to allow its individual provinces to decide whether to permit its release. Since 1998, some provinces (such as <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>) have released the film, with a rating that reflects its content.</p>
<p>The censored American version of the film was released in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> in 1982 with an R 18+ rating. In 1987, the film survived an appeal to ban it. It continued to be sold until 1997, when another reclassification caused its ban in Australia. In 2004, the full uncut version was awarded an R 18+, lifting the seven-year ban. The Office of Film and Literature Classification justified this decision by reasoning that <a href="/wiki/Castration" title="Castration">castration</a> is not sexual violence (Australian censorship law forbids the release of films that depict scenes of sexual violence as acceptable or justified).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the film was branded a "<a href="/wiki/Video_nasty" title="Video nasty">video nasty</a>". It appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Director_of_Public_Prosecutions" title="Director of Public Prosecutions">Director of Public Prosecutions</a>'s list of prosecutable films until 2001, when a heavily cut version which extensively edited the rape scenes was released with an <a href="/wiki/18_certificate" title="18 certificate">18 certificate</a>. All subsequent releases of the film have received similar cuts.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, the uncut version of the film (101 minutes) was classified in 1984 as R20 with the descriptive note "Contains graphic violence, content may disturb". Other versions with shorter running times (96 minutes) were also classified in 1984 and 1985, and received the same classification.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Irish_Film_Board" title="The Irish Film Board">The Irish Film Board</a> has again banned the film from sale. Having been banned for many years in the country, the new Blu-ray and DVD uncensored edition has been prohibited from purchase by retailers due to the nature of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zarchi.27s_inspiration_and_responses_to_criticism">Zarchi's inspiration and responses to criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Spit_on_Your_Grave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Zarchi's inspiration and responses to criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the commentary for the Millennium Edition, Zarchi said he was inspired to produce the film after helping a young woman who had been raped in New York. He tells of how he, a friend, and his daughter were driving by a park when they witnessed a young woman crawling out of the bushes bloodied and naked (he later learned the young woman was taking a common shortcut to her boyfriend's house when she was attacked). They collected the traumatized girl, returned the daughter home, and quickly decided it was best to take the girl to the police rather than a hospital, lest the attackers escape and find further victims.</p>
<p>They quickly decided that they made the wrong decision — the officer, whom Zarchi described as "not fit to wear the uniform", delayed taking her to the hospital and instead insisted that she follow formalities such as giving her full name (and the spelling), even though her jaw had been broken and she could hardly speak. Zarchi insisted the officer take her to the hospital and he eventually complied. Soon afterwards the woman's father wrote both Zarchi and his friend a letter of thanks for helping his daughter. The father offered a reward, which Zarchi refused.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the same commentary, Zarchi denied that the film was exploitative, and that the violent nature of the film was necessary to tell the story. He described actress Camille Keaton as "brave" for taking on the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel_and_remakes">Sequel and remakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Spit_on_Your_Grave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sequel and remakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was followed by the unofficial <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Savage_Vengeance" title="Savage Vengeance">Savage Vengeance</a></i> (the title card on the movie was misspelled as <i>Savage Vengance</i>) (1993) in which <a href="/wiki/Camille_Keaton" title="Camille Keaton">Camille Keaton</a> (under the alias of Vickie Kehl for unknown reasons) reprises the role of Jennifer. However, no scenes from <i>I Spit on Your Grave</i> were used for the flashbacks. The film barely went for 65 minutes, and received extremely negative reviews from critics and fans alike due to major flaws in the film. Zarchi allegedly sued director Donald Farmer, which resulted in a "change" in the character Jennifer's name - instead of reshooting, they blanked the audio when an actor said her last name. Keaton allegedly walked out of filming, which gives somewhat of an explanation as to why the film ended so suddenly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>CineTel Films acquired rights to remake <i><a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_(2010_film)" title="I Spit on Your Grave (2010 film)">I Spit on Your Grave</a></i>, which had a Halloween 2010 worldwide theatrical release. The remake was produced by CineTel president and CEO Paul Hertzberg and Lisa Hansen, with Jeff Klein, Alan Ostroff, Gary Needle and Zarchi as executive producers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-remake_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-remake-19">[19]</a></sup> Steven R. Monroe directed, with newcomer <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Butler" title="Sarah Butler">Sarah Butler</a> starring as Jennifer. The follow up <i><a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_2" title="I Spit on Your Grave 2">I Spit on Your Grave 2</a></i> was released on September 20, 2013, starring Jemma Dallender, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Absolom" title="Joe Absolom">Joe Absolom</a>, Yavor Baharov, and Aleksandar Alekiov. It was directed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_R._Monroe" title="Steven R. Monroe">Steven R. Monroe</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Fenton" title="Thomas Fenton">Thomas Fenton</a> and Neil Elman. A second sequel, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_III:_Vengeance_is_Mine" title="I Spit on Your Grave III: Vengeance is Mine">I Spit on Your Grave III: Vengeance is Mine</a></i>, arrived in 2015.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The official sequel <i><a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave:_Deja_Vu" title="I Spit on Your Grave: Deja Vu">I Spit on Your Grave: Deja Vu</a></i>, directed by original director <a href="/wiki/Meir_Zarchi" title="Meir Zarchi">Meir Zarchi</a>, was finished in October 2015 with <a href="/wiki/Camille_Keaton" title="Camille Keaton">Camille Keaton</a> reprising her role as Jennifer Hills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sequel_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-sequel-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Spit_on_Your_Grave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film received its first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> release on 20 September 2010 in the United Kingdom from 101 Films. It was released in an 'Ultimate Collector's Edition', containing the film on both Blu-ray and DVD, a collector's booklet and poster. It is the most complete version released uncut in the UK, but it is not uncut - cuts of almost three minutes were required for an '18' rating <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> to the rape scenes (previous UK releases were cut by over seven minutes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>). It was also released alongside the <a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_(2010_film)" title="I Spit on Your Grave (2010 film)">remake</a> in a 'Limited Collector's Edition' on 7 February 2011 in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> It was released on 8 February 2011 in the United States from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starz_Home_Entertainment" title="Starz Home Entertainment">Starz</a>/<a href="/wiki/Anchor_Bay_Entertainment" title="Anchor Bay Entertainment">Anchor Bay Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In Australia, the film was released on 16 March 2011 as a 'Director's Cut' edition.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
