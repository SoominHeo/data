<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Río Negro (Uruguay)</h1>

<p>The <b>Río Negro</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈri.o ˈneɣɾo]</a></span>, <i>Black River</i>) is the most important river in <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iahs_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-iahs-1">[1]</a></sup> It originates in the southern highlands of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, just east of <a href="/wiki/Bag%C3%A9" title="Bagé">Bagé</a>, and flows west across the entire width of Uruguay to the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_River" title="Uruguay River">Uruguay River</a>. The course of the Río Negro across Uruguay effectively divides the south of the country from the north. The Río Negro's principals tributaries are <a href="/wiki/Y%C3%AD_River" title="Yí River">Yí River</a> and Tacuarembó River.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:307px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flooding_along_the_Rio_Negro,_Uruguay.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1366" data-file-width="2049" height="203" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Flooding_along_the_Rio_Negro%2C_Uruguay.jpg/305px-Flooding_along_the_Rio_Negro%2C_Uruguay.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Flooding_along_the_Rio_Negro%2C_Uruguay.jpg/458px-Flooding_along_the_Rio_Negro%2C_Uruguay.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Flooding_along_the_Rio_Negro%2C_Uruguay.jpg/610px-Flooding_along_the_Rio_Negro%2C_Uruguay.jpg 2x" width="305"/></a>

Flooding along the river</div>

<p>The river is dammed near <a href="/wiki/Paso_de_los_Toros" title="Paso de los Toros">Paso de los Toros</a>, creating the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lago_Rinc%C3%B3n_del_Bonete" title="Lago Rincón del Bonete">Rincón del Bonete Reservoir</a>, also called the Gabriel Terra Reservoir or the Rio Negro Reservoir. With a surface area of about 420 square miles (1,100 km<sup>2</sup>), it is the largest <a href="/wiki/Reservoir" title="Reservoir">reservoir</a> in Uruguay and an installed capacity of 160 MW.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-britannica-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Downstream from the Rincón del Bonete Reservoir, there are two more dams, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baygorria_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baygorria Dam (page does not exist)">Baygorria Dam</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Constituci%C3%B3n_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Constitución Dam (page does not exist)">Constitución Dam</a> at <a href="/wiki/Palmar,_Uruguay" title="Palmar, Uruguay">Palmar</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which generate hydroelectric power for Uruguay with 108 MW and 333 MW, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-britannica-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Río Negro's <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">drainage basin</a> size is about 69,700 square kilometres (26,900 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iahs_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-iahs-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
