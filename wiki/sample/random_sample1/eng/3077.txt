<p><b>Negidals</b> (<i>негидальцы</i> in <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>; self designation: <i>элькан бэйэнин</i>, or elkan bayenin meaning "local people." The ethnonym Negidal is a Russification of the Ewenki term ngegida, which means "coastal people.") are a people in the <a href="/wiki/Khabarovsk_Krai" title="Khabarovsk Krai">Khabarovsk Krai</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, who live along the <a href="/wiki/Amgun_River" title="Amgun River">Amgun River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amur_River" title="Amur River">Amur River</a>. Their language belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Tungusic_languages" title="Tungusic languages">Tungusic language family</a> and is very close to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evenk_language" title="Evenk language">Evenk language</a>. The Negidals are <a href="/wiki/Evenks" title="Evenks">Evenks</a> by origin, who settled along the Amgun and interbred with the <a href="/wiki/Nivkh_people" title="Nivkh people">Nivkhs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanais" title="Nanais">Nanais</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulchs" title="Ulchs">Ulchs</a>. They are officially considered <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Orthodox Christians</a>, but preserved their own <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animistic" title="Animistic">animistic</a> beliefs and <a href="/wiki/Shamanism" title="Shamanism">shamanism</a>. According to the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>, there were 567 Negidals in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, 147 of which still spoke the <a href="/wiki/Negidal_language" title="Negidal language">Negidal language</a>. According to the 2010 census there were 513 Negidals in Russia, 75 of which still spoke the Negidal language.</p>
<p>52 Negidals live in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>. Of these, 31 declared <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Negidal" title="Negidal">Negidal</a> as their native language, 11 are Russian speaking and for 9 the mother tongue is some other language.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stats_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-stats-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
