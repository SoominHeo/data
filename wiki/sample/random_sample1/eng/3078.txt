<p><b>Nena</b> (<small>pronouncedÂ </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[ËnÉªna]</a></span>; born <b>Gabriele Susanne Kerner</b>, 24 March 1960) is a German singer-songwriter, actress, and comedian who rose to international fame in 1983 with the <a href="/wiki/Neue_Deutsche_Welle" title="Neue Deutsche Welle">New German Wave</a> song "<a href="/wiki/99_Luftballons" title="99 Luftballons">99 Luftballons</a>". In 1984, she re-recorded this song in English as "99 Red Balloons".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nena_(band)" title="Nena (band)">Nena</a> was also the name of the band with whom she released the song. The re-recording of some of her old songs rekindled her career in 2002. In 2007, she co-founded <a href="/wiki/Neue_Schule_Hamburg" title="Neue Schule Hamburg">Neue Schule Hamburg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gabriele Susanne Kerner (Nena) was born on 24 March 1960 in <a href="/wiki/Hagen" title="Hagen">Hagen</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> while her family lived in the nearby town of <a href="/wiki/Breckerfeld" title="Breckerfeld">Breckerfeld</a>. She spent the earliest part of her childhood in Breckerfeld and later lived in Hagen. She acquired her nickname, based on the Spanish word "niÃ±a", or "little girl", at the age of three, while on a vacation to Spain with her parents. In 1977 she left high school before graduation, and in the three following years she was trained as a goldsmith.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_beginnings">Musical beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nena's musical career began on 2 July 1979 when guitarist Rainer Kitzmann founded <a href="/wiki/The_Stripes" title="The Stripes">The Stripes</a> band and, on the basis of having seen her dancing at a local disco, asked her to audition for the position of the lead singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>The group had a minor hit with the song "Ecstasy", but never achieved mainstream success and disbanded on 3 March 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In May 1982 Nena and her then-boyfriend <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolf_Brendel" title="de:Rolf Brendel">Rolf Brendel</a> moved to <a href="/wiki/West_Berlin" title="West Berlin">West Berlin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pop.2Frocky_staralbum_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pop.2Frocky_staralbum-5">[5]</a></sup> where they met future band members guitarist <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Karges" title="Carlo Karges">Carlo Karges</a>, keyboard player <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uwe_Fahrenkrog-Petersen" title="Uwe Fahrenkrog-Petersen">Uwe Fahrenkrog-Petersen</a>, and bass player <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Dehmel" title="JÃ¼rgen Dehmel">JÃ¼rgen Dehmel</a>. Together, they formed the band <a href="/wiki/Nena_(band)" title="Nena (band)">Nena</a>. In 1982 "Nena" released their first single, "<a href="/wiki/Nena_(album)#.22Nur_getr.C3.A4umt.22" title="Nena (album)">Nur getrÃ¤umt</a>", which became an instant hit in Germany after the band appeared on the German television show <i><a href="/wiki/Musikladen" title="Musikladen">Musikladen</a></i> on 21 August 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pop.2Frocky_staralbum_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pop.2Frocky_staralbum-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The single reportedly sold 40,000 copies the day after the song appeared on the show and reached <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>2</span> in the German charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HosfeldP.C3.B6lking2007_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-HosfeldP.C3.B6lking2007-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1983, the band released its first album <i><a href="/wiki/Nena_(album)" title="Nena (album)">Nena</a></i>, which contained the singles "<a href="/wiki/99_Luftballons" title="99 Luftballons">99 Luftballons</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Nena_(album)#.22Leuchtturm.22" title="Nena (album)">Leuchtturm</a>". "99 Luftballons" became a number one hit in West Germany and the Netherlands in 1983 and went on to major international chart success the following year, an English version hitting <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>1</span> in the UK and the original German version hitting <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>2</span> in the US, behind <a href="/wiki/Van_Halen" title="Van Halen">Van Halen</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Jump_(Van_Halen_song)" title="Jump (Van Halen song)">Jump</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_charts_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-German_charts-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1984, <a href="/wiki/Casey_Kasem" title="Casey Kasem">Casey Kasem</a>'s radio show <i><a href="/wiki/American_Top_40" title="American Top 40">American Top 40</a></i> introduced a "mixed" version of the song, "splicing" the German and English versions together. It was also a huge hit in many other countries, and to this day it is one of the best-known German rock songs in many parts of the world.</p>
<p>In May 1984 while on a tour in the UK, Nena made the headlines of the British red-top press for having unshaven armpits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At the time this was not uncommon in continental Europe but was considered unusual in English-speaking countries to the extent that some people consider it explains the commercial failure there of the follow-ups to "99 Luftballons".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Nena, baffled by the attention generated, asked the girlfriend of her manager to shave her and has remained clean shaven ever since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Referring to the "huge indignation" the issue raised, Nena, in her memoires published in 2005, wrote, "Can a girl from Hagen, who dreams of the big wide world and is in love with <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a>, have no idea that girls can't under any circumstances have hair under the arm? Yes she can. I simply had no idea!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although "99 Luftballons" was Nena's only hit in the English-speaking world, the band continued to enjoy success in several European countries in the following years. Nena's next international single "Just a Dream" (an English language re-issue of "Nur getrÃ¤umt") reached <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>70</span> in the UK charts in 1984; it had "Indianer" on the B-side. A dance version of "Just a Dream" was released in the 1990s to a new audience and became a club anthem. The band split in 1987, and Nena went solo thereafter.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1989:_Launch_of_solo_career">1989: Launch of solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1989: Launch of solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nena's first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Wunder_gescheh%27n" title="Wunder gescheh'n">Wunder gescheh'n</a></i> was released on 5 November 1989. The title track (German for "Miracles Happen"), composed by Nena herself, relates to the fact that Nena was at the time pregnant with twins, but release of the album that appeared just four days before the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a> (on 9 November) and the fact that she performed the song at the end of the <i><a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konzert_f%C3%BCr_Berlin" title="de:Konzert fÃ¼r Berlin">Konzert fÃ¼r Berlin</a></i> three days later has ever since associated it with that historic event. It was to prove to be her last major hit of the 20th century as throughout the 1990s her albums and singlesÂ â  although often critically acclaimedÂ â  were less commercially successful. In 1993, following the indifferent performance of her second solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Bongo_Girl" title="Bongo Girl">Bongo Girl</a></i>, Sony decided not to renew Nena's recording contract, and the label which distributed her third, RMG Music Entertainment, disappeared shortly afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2002, Nena celebrated her 20th anniversary on stage with the album <i><a href="/wiki/Nena_feat._Nena" title="Nena feat. Nena">Nena feat. Nena</a></i>, a disc consisting of newly arranged recordings of her hits from the 1980s. This album marked a "comeback" for Nena, and spawned a number of successful chart entries. The remake of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anyplace,_Anywhere,_Anytime" title="Anyplace, Anywhere, Anytime">Anyplace, Anywhere, Anytime</a>" as an English-German duet with <a href="/wiki/Kim_Wilde" title="Kim Wilde">Kim Wilde</a> was a hit in various European countries, reaching the <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>1</span> spot in the Netherlands and Austria, and <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>3</span> in Germany, in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_charts_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-German_charts-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Having regalvanised her career by virtue of her 1980s hits, Nena reestablished herself as a force with entirely new material with the 2005 album <i><a href="/wiki/Willst_du_mit_mir_gehn" title="Willst du mit mir gehn">Willst du mit mir gehn</a></i> which quickly achieved platinum status and climbed to <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>2</span> in the German charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_charts_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-German_charts-8">[8]</a></sup> The first single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Willst_du_mit_mir_gehn#.22Liebe_ist.22" title="Willst du mit mir gehn">Liebe ist</a>", reached <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>1</span> on the German charts in early 2005, and was the theme song for a German telenovela, <i><a href="/wiki/Verliebt_in_Berlin" title="Verliebt in Berlin">Verliebt in Berlin</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_charts_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-German_charts-8">[8]</a></sup> It reached the top position 22 years after "99 Luftballons", the longest span between first and last number one in German chart history (see <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liste_der_Nummer-eins-Hits_in_Deutschland#K.C3.BCnstler_mit_den_meisten_Jahren_zwischen_der_ersten_und_letzten_Nummer-eins-Single" title="de:Liste der Nummer-eins-Hits in Deutschland">Liste der Nummer-eins-Hits in Deutschland</a>).</p>
<p>In October 2007, Nena released a new album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Cover_Me_(album)" title="Cover Me (album)">Cover Me</a></i>, made up entirely of cover songs. <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" title="Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rammstein" title="Rammstein">Rammstein</a> are three of the bands covered. She also released the single, "<a href="/wiki/Young_Folks#In_popular_culture" title="Young Folks">Ich kann nix dafÃ¼r</a>" in April 2007 for the film, <i><a href="/wiki/Vollidiot" title="Vollidiot">Vollidiot</a></i>, and her cover of "<a href="/wiki/She%27s_a_Rainbow" title="She's a Rainbow">She's a Rainbow</a>" by the Rolling Stones in the US and the UK. In 2009 she recorded and released a new version of her hit song "99 Luftballons", which more closely follows the 1980s original, in contrast to her 2002 version. This song was first performed in Germany on 6 September 2009. Some parts of the new version are in French.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Since_2009:_Own_record_label">Since 2009: Own record label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Since 2009: Own record label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2009, Nena's releases have been published by her own record label, The Laugh &amp; Peas Company, which also promotes the work of her daughter's group (Adameva) and that of one of her protegÃ©'s from <i>The Voice of Germany</i>, <a href="/wiki/Sharron_Levy" title="Sharron Levy">Sharron Levy</a>.</p>
<p>Nena released a new single on 4 September 2009, called "<a href="/wiki/Made_in_Germany_(Nena_album)#.22Wir_sind_wahr.22" title="Made in Germany (Nena album)">Wir sind wahr</a>", and a new album on the 23rd: <i><a href="/wiki/Made_in_Germany_(Nena_album)" title="Made in Germany (Nena album)">Made in Germany</a></i>. The autobiographical ballad from the album "<a href="/wiki/Made_in_Germany_(Nena_album)#.22In_meinem_Leben.22" title="Made in Germany (Nena album)">In meinem Leben</a>" became her fourteenth Top 10 hit in Germany, taking her to 12th position in the all-time list of <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liste_der_Top-10-Singles_in_Deutschland" title="de:Liste der Top-10-Singles in Deutschland">top-ten hits in Germany</a>, the third highest placed German act and top German female. She has developed an interest in the teachings of Indian mystic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osho_(Bhagwan_Shree_Rajneesh)" title="Osho (Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh)">Osho (Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh)</a> in recent years, and practises his meditation methods, an impression of which is featured in the "Wir sind wahr" video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> She also collaborated with the popular techno-rap artists <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Die_Atzen" title="de:Die Atzen">Die Atzen</a> to produce a new single, "Strobo Pop". In 2011, she contributed vocals to the track "Let Go Tonight" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_Costner_and_Modern_West" title="Kevin Costner and Modern West">Kevin Costner and Modern West</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2012 Nena released her 11th solo studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Du_bist_gut" title="Du bist gut">Du bist gut</a></i>, which peaked at <span class="nowrap">No.<span class="nowrap">â</span>2</span> in the German charts, although the tracks released as singles from the album were not as successful as those from her previous albums since her 2002 "comeback".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_charts_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-German_charts-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nena's next album, <a href="/wiki/Oldschool_(Nena_album)" title="Oldschool (Nena album)"><i>Oldschool</i></a>, which was produced by the German rapper and hip hop artist <a href="/wiki/Samy_Deluxe" title="Samy Deluxe">Samy Deluxe</a>, was released on 27 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oldschool_.28album.29_info_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oldschool_.28album.29_info-17">[17]</a></sup> Distribution rights for the album were agreed with Sony Music, 22 years after the company dropped Nena as a recording artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The album maintained Nena's 21st century chart success pattern (Top 5 in Germany, Top 10 in Austria, Top 20 in Switzerland) but the first two singles released from the album failed to chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Austrian_charts_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Austrian_charts-19">[19]</a></sup> However, for the first time in Nena's career, another track from the album ("Magie"), which was not released as a single, crept into the lower echelons of the German singles chart solely by virtue of downloads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_charts_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-German_charts-8">[8]</a></sup> Then, in April 2016, fourteen months after <i>Oldschool'</i>s release, the third single from the album, "Genau jetzt", returned Nena to the German Top 30 singles chart for the first time in six years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_charts_8-6"><a href="#cite_note-German_charts-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Since 1997 (and as of 2016, with dates announced for 2017) Nena has toured Germany and its neighbouring countries annually, typically performing between 15 and 50 concerts every year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2003, Nena took the stage during the <a href="/wiki/Howard_Jones_(musician)" title="Howard Jones (musician)">Howard Jones</a> 20th Anniversary concert at the <a href="/wiki/Shepherd%27s_Bush_Empire" title="Shepherd's Bush Empire">Shepherd's Bush Empire</a> in London to sing "99 Luftballons", with both German and English lyrics. In 2004, Nena went on stage at the <a href="/wiki/Mayday_(music_festival)" title="Mayday (music festival)">Mayday Music Festival</a>, in Dortmund, accompanied by the techno duo <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toktok" title="de:Toktok">Toktok</a>. She sang the song "Bang Bang", and was later joined on stage by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westbam" title="Westbam">DJ Westbam</a> to perform "Oldschool Baby". In March 2015 Nena promoted the release of the <i>Oldschool</i> album with a "club tour" of 16 smaller venues (for 200 to 700 people) including her former primary school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubtour_2015_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clubtour_2015-21">[21]</a></sup> A recording of one of these concertsÂ â  at the <a href="/wiki/SO36" title="SO36">SO36</a> club in Berlin on 4 March 2015Â â  was released as Nena's sixth live album <i><a href="/wiki/Live_at_SO36" title="Live at SO36">Live at SO36</a></i> on 4 March 2016, exactly one year later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Live_at_SO36_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Live_at_SO36-22">[22]</a></sup> Later in 2015 Nena announced her intention to perform live in the US for the first time by way of a similar "club tour", which was initially planned for the spring of 2016 before taking place as a 3-date "mini tour" in September and October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ex_Berliner_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ex_Berliner-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Besides her singing career, she has also acted in the 1983 musical comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Gib_Gas_%E2%80%93_Ich_will_Spass_(1983_film)" title="Gib Gas â Ich will Spass (1983 film)">Gib Gas â Ich will SpaÃ</a></i> (released in the US as <i>Hangin' Out</i>) opposite fellow musician <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markus_(Musiker)" title="de:Markus (Musiker)">Markus MÃ¶rl</a>, and voiced the character <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saphira" title="Saphira">Saphira</a> for the German dub of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Eragon_(film)" title="Eragon (film)">Eragon</a></i> and the role of the Princess for the German version of <i><a href="/wiki/Arthur_and_the_Invisibles" title="Arthur and the Invisibles">Arthur and the Invisibles</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Tokio_Hotel" title="Tokio Hotel">Tokio Hotel</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bill_Kaulitz" title="Bill Kaulitz">Bill Kaulitz</a>. Nena's contributions are her disparate accounts of various episodes in her life. Most of Thesenfitz's contributions are excerpts from interviews of many of Nena's friends, co-workers, and relatives.</p>
<p>Since becoming a mother, Nena has released a number of albums consisting of songs for children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In the 1990s she hosted several TV shows, including <i>Metro</i> and <i>Countdown Grand Prix</i>, the German preselection for the <i><a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a></i>, in 1998.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences_and_other_interests_and_pursuits">Influences and other interests and pursuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Influences and other interests and pursuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In addition to citing the <a href="/wiki/Ramones" title="Ramones">Ramones</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Look_In_21.2F4.2F84_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Look_In_21.2F4.2F84-33">[33]</a></sup> David Bowie and <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Harry" title="Debbie Harry">Debbie Harry</a> as early influences, Nena's main artistic source of inspiration and her favourite band is the Rolling Stones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ex_Berliner_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ex_Berliner-23">[23]</a></sup> Nena has covered their songs in concert and on her 2007 <i>Cover Me</i> album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cover_me_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cover_me-34">[34]</a></sup> In 1984 Nena said, "If I hadn't started listening to the Rolling Stones when I was 12 I'd have been a different person, a secretary or something like that."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_5.2F5.2F84_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-NME_5.2F5.2F84-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Nena said she disliked the English version of "99 Luftballons" because it was too "blatant" and that the Nena band was neither political nor a protest band,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_5.2F5.2F84_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-NME_5.2F5.2F84-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_Mirror_10.2F3.2F84_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Record_Mirror_10.2F3.2F84-36">[36]</a></sup> she has occasionally publicly lent her name in support of political causes. She supports "Ehe fÃ¼r alle" (marriage for everyone) and, as a mother of five, a campaign against the German government's plans to scale back the state provision of midwifery services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hebammen_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hebammen-37">[37]</a></sup> Nena has been a member of various charity ensembles and supergroups including "Band fÃ¼r Afrika" (Germany's equivalent of <a href="/wiki/Band_Aid_(band)" title="Band Aid (band)">Band Aid</a>) in 1985 and "Marlon und Freunde" in 2006, whose Top 10 hit in Germany "Lieber Gott" raised funds for flood victims. Nena also supports local causes and in 2015 ran (in socks) 2.5 kilometres (approximately 1.5 miles) in aid of a Stuttgart children's hospice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nena is a vegetarian and won the <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">PETA</a> (Germany) award for "Sexiest Vegetarian of the Year 2010".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> She believes in Jesus and God and says that thanking him for every new day is the cornerstone of her spirituality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>From time to time suspicions of drug use have surfaced, particularly in light of Nena's high energy levels on stage. However, in his 2014 biography of the Nena band, Rolf Brendel, its drummer and Nena's boyfriend at the time said that although other band members experimented with drugs, Nena never did and frowned upon it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bild_.E2.80.93_Brendel_book_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bild_.E2.80.93_Brendel_book-42">[42]</a></sup> Brendel added, "Nena is naturally stoned, she doesn't need to take drugs." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bild_.E2.80.93_Brendel_book_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bild_.E2.80.93_Brendel_book-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nena and the band's drummer Rolf Brendel split up in 1987, Nena's feelings movingly described in the song "<a href="/wiki/Eisbrecher_(Nena_album)#.22Jetzt_bist_du_weg.22" title="Eisbrecher (Nena album)">Jetzt bist du weg</a>" ("Now You Are Gone") from the band's final album, <i><a href="/wiki/Eisbrecher_(Nena_album)" title="Eisbrecher (Nena album)">Eisbrecher</a></i>. Nena then started dating Swiss actor Benedict Freitag shortly after the band separated in 1987. Benedict is the son of the German actress <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maria_Becker" title="de:Maria Becker">Maria Becker</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> and Austrian-Swiss actor <a href="/wiki/Robert_Freitag" title="Robert Freitag">Robert Freitag</a>. The couple had three children together, the first being Christopher Daniel, who was born disabled allegedly due to medical mistakes made during the birth that caused Nena to go into <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrest" title="Cardiac arrest">cardiac arrest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Claudia_Thesenfitz_2005_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-Claudia_Thesenfitz_2005-31">[31]</a></sup> He died at the age of 11 months. In 1990 they had twins, Larissa Marie and Sakias Manuel. After splitting with Freitag, Nena started a relationship with drummer and music producer Philipp Palm from <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>, with whom she had two children, Samuel Vincent (1995), and Simeon Joel (1997). The couple and the four children currently live in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>. On Christmas Day 2009, Nena became a grandmother when Larissa gave birth to a girl, Carla Maria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Only two days later, Nena's son Sakias became a father to a boy named Noah.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> A third grandchild (Victor, son of Larissa) was born in September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
