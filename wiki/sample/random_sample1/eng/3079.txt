<p><b>Princess Ariane of the Netherlands, Princess of Orange-Nassau</b> (Ariane Wilhelmina Máxima Inés; born 10 April 2007) is the third and youngest daughter of <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">King Willem-Alexander</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queen_M%C3%A1xima_of_the_Netherlands" title="Queen Máxima of the Netherlands">Queen Máxima</a>. Princess Ariane is a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_Royal_House" title="Dutch Royal House">Dutch Royal House</a> and currently third in the <a href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_Dutch_throne" title="Line of succession to the Dutch throne">line of succession to the Dutch throne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Ariane_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Princess Ariane was born in the Bronovo Hospital in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a> at 21:56 local time<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> on 10 April 2007 as the third child and third daughter of <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">King Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queen_M%C3%A1xima_of_the_Netherlands" title="Queen Máxima of the Netherlands">Queen Máxima of the Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Jan_Peter_Balkenende" title="Jan Peter Balkenende">Balkenende</a> addressed the nation shortly afterwards and said both mother and child were healthy and doing well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The next morning, Her father appeared on television with his new daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The names of the baby were announced on 13 April, when the birth was registered in The Hague.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-names_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-names-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Princess Ariane was <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptised</a> in the Abbey Church in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a> on 20 October 2007. <a href="/wiki/Vicar" title="Vicar">Vicar</a> Deodaat van der Boon used water from the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_River" title="Jordan River">Jordan River</a> to baptize the princess, who was wearing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christening_gown" title="Christening gown">christening gown</a> that <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Wilhelmina of the Netherlands">Princess Wilhelmina</a> first wore in 1880.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Over 850 guests were invited to attend, including Princess Máxima's parents and Dutch prime minister <a href="/wiki/Jan_Peter_Balkenende" title="Jan Peter Balkenende">Jan Peter Balkenende</a>. Her godparents are Valeria Delger, Inés Zorreguieta, <a href="/wiki/Guillaume,_Hereditary_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Guillaume, Hereditary Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Hereditary Grand Duke Guillaume of Luxembourg</a>, Tijo Baron Collot d’Escury, and Anton Friling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Princess Ariane has two elder sisters, the princesses <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Catharina-Amalia_of_the_Netherlands" title="Princess Catharina-Amalia of the Netherlands">Catharina-Amalia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Alexia_of_the_Netherlands" title="Princess Alexia of the Netherlands">Alexia</a>. She speaks Dutch, English and some Spanish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hospitalisation">Hospitalisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Ariane_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hospitalisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 2 May 2007, Princess Ariane was admitted to the <a href="/wiki/Leiden_University" title="Leiden University">Leiden University</a> medical centre with a suspected <a href="/wiki/Lung" title="Lung">lung</a> infection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She was released from the hospital on 5 May 2007 after treatment for her bacterial and viral infection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 13 June 2007, <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">Prince Willem-Alexander</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Maxima_of_the_Netherlands" title="Queen Maxima of the Netherlands">Princess Máxima</a> released a thank you note "not only for the congratulations upon Ariane's birth but also for the best wishes they received upon her hospitalisation" and released a third official picture with their newborn daughter. The couple reportedly received over 30,000 letters of well-wishers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 8 October 2009, Princess Ariane was again admitted to a hospital, in which she had to stay for one night, due to a respiratory infection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles.2C_styles_and_arms">Titles, styles and arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Ariane_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Titles, styles and arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<dt>Notes</dt>
<dd>Princess Ariane uses the same coat of arms as her sisters, <a href="/wiki/Catharina-Amalia,_Princess_of_Orange" title="Catharina-Amalia, Princess of Orange">Princess Catharina-Amalia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Alexia_of_the_Netherlands" title="Princess Alexia of the Netherlands">Princess Alexia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coatsofarms_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-coatsofarms-16">[16]</a></sup></dd>
<dt>Escutcheon</dt>
<dd>Quarterly: I and IV azure billety or, a lion with coronet also or armed and langued gules, holding in his dexter paw a sword argent hilted or, and in his sinister seven arrows argent pointed and bound together or, which is of the Kingdom of the Netherlands; II and III or, a horn azure opened and bound gules, which is of the first House of Orange; an inescutcheon or bearing a castle of three towers gules flanked on each side by a poplar tree au naturel, and a river azure flowing from the base, ondoyant to the gate of the castle, which is of the house of Zorreguieta in Argentina.</dd>
