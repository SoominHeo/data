<p>The <b>coat of arms of the Kingdom of the Netherlands</b> was originally adopted in 1815 and later modified in 1907. The arms are a composite of the arms of the former <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> and the arms of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Nassau" title="House of Nassau">House of Nassau</a>, it features a checkered shield with a lion grasping a sword in one hand and a bundle of arrows in the other and is the heraldic symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_Netherlands" title="Monarchy of the Netherlands">monarch</a> (currently <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Willem-Alexander" title="King Willem-Alexander">King Willem-Alexander</a>) and the country. The monarch uses a version of the arms with a mantle (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl"><i>Koninklijk wapen</i></span>) while the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Netherlands" title="Government of the Netherlands">government</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> uses a smaller version without the mantle (cloak) or the pavilion, sometimes even only the shield and crown are used (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl"><i>Rijkswapen</i></span>). The current components of the coats of arms were regulated by <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Wilhelmina of the Netherlands">Queen Wilhelmina</a> in a royal decree of 10 July 1907, affirmed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Juliana" title="Queen Juliana">Queen Juliana</a> in a royal decree of 23 April 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1980_Decree_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-1980_Decree-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Blazon" title="Blazon">blazon</a> is follows:</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Azure_(heraldry)" title="Azure (heraldry)">Azure</a>, billetty <a href="/wiki/Or_(heraldry)" title="Or (heraldry)">Or</a> a <a href="/wiki/Lion_(heraldry)" title="Lion (heraldry)">lion</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Coronet" title="Coronet">coronet</a> Or armed and langued <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">Gules</a> holding in his dexter paw a <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">sword</a> <a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">Argent</a> hilted Or and in the sinister paw seven <a href="/wiki/Arrow" title="Arrow">arrows</a> Argent pointed and bound together Or. (The seven arrows stand for the seven provinces of the Union of Utrecht.) The <a href="/wiki/Shield" title="Shield">shield</a> is crowned with the (Dutch) royal <a href="/wiki/Crown_(headgear)" title="Crown (headgear)">crown</a> and supported by two lions</i> Or armed and langued gules<i>. They stand on a scroll Azure with the text (</i>Or<i>) "Je Maintiendrai" (<small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ʒə mɛ̃.tjɛ̃.dʁe]</a></span>, French for "I shall uphold".)</i></p>
<p>The monarch places this coat of arms on a mantle <i>gules</i> lined with <a href="/wiki/Ermine_(heraldry)" title="Ermine (heraldry)">ermine</a>. Above the mantle is a pavilion <i>gules</i> again topped with the royal crown.</p>
<p>In the royal decree it is stated that male successors may replace the crown on the shield with a <a href="/wiki/Helmet#Heraldry" title="Helmet">helm</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">crest</a> of Nassau. (For a description see below at <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands#Nassau" title="Coat of arms of the Netherlands">"Nassau"</a>.)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_origin_of_the_coat_of_arms">History and origin of the coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History and origin of the coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This version of the coat of arms has been in use since 1907 but differs only slightly from the version that was adopted in 1815. From 1815 until 1907 all the lions wore the royal crown and the supporting lions were facing.</p>
<p>The royal arms were adopted by the first king of The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_I_of_the_Netherlands" title="William I of the Netherlands">William I</a>, when he became king after the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a> in 1815. As king, he adopted a coat of arms that combined elements of his family’s (<a href="/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau" title="House of Orange-Nassau">Orange-Nassau</a>) coat of arms and that of the former <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> that existed from 1581 until 1795.</p>
<p>From his family arms he used the <i>azure, billetty or</i> with <i>a lion rampant or</i> of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Nassau" title="House of Nassau">Nassau</a> (blue shield, lion, billets). The "Je Maintiendrai" motto represents the Orange family since it came into the family with the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Orange" title="Prince of Orange">princedom of Orange</a> as "Je Maintiendrai <a href="/wiki/Chalon-sur-Sa%C3%B4ne" title="Chalon-sur-Saône">Châlons</a>". These elements are also found in the arms of king <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_England" title="William III of England">William III</a>, who was also king of England, Scotland &amp; Ireland (1689–1702). From the arms of the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estates-General_of_the_Netherlands" title="Estates-General of the Netherlands">States General</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Republic of the United Provinces</a> he took the lion with a coronet, sword and arrows. The arrows symbolize the seven provinces that made up the Republic, the sword the determination to defend their liberty, and the coronet their sovereignty. William replaced the coronet with a royal crown. In 1907, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Wilhelmina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Queen Wilhelmina of the Netherlands">Queen Wilhelmina</a> returned to an open coronet.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Counts_of_Nassau">Counts of Nassau</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Counts of Nassau">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arms_of_Nassau.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="367" data-file-width="301" height="244" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Arms_of_Nassau.svg/200px-Arms_of_Nassau.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Arms_of_Nassau.svg/300px-Arms_of_Nassau.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Arms_of_Nassau.svg/400px-Arms_of_Nassau.svg.png 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arms_of_Nassau.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Arms of Nassau: <i>Azure billetty or, a lion rampant of the last armed and langued gules</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The arms of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Nassau" title="House of Nassau">Nassau</a> has existed since about 1250. There are two versions of the Nassau arms, representing the two main branches. This is a result of two brothers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walram_II_of_Nassau" title="Walram II of Nassau">count Walram II</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otto_I_of_Nassau" title="Otto I of Nassau">count Otto I</a>, agreeing to divide their father's (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_II_of_Nassau" title="Henry II of Nassau">Henry II</a>) lands between them in 1255. The line of Walram added a crown to the lion in the Nassau arms to make it different from the lion used by the line of Otto.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_monarchy" title="Dutch monarchy">kings and queens of the Netherlands</a> are descendants of count Otto. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Grand Dukes of Luxemburg</a> are descendants of count Walram. They also still use "Nassau" in their arms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Both lines are now extinct in the male line.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Helmet#Heraldry" title="Helmet">helm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">crest</a> that can be used in the Royal arms by the male successors to the throne (and is in fact being used by some male members of the Royal Family) is: "On a (ceremonial) helmet, with bars and decoration Or and <a href="/wiki/Mantling" title="Mantling">mantling</a> Azure and Or, issuing from a <a href="/wiki/Coronet" title="Coronet">coronet</a> Or, a pair of wings joined <a href="/wiki/Sable_(heraldry)" title="Sable (heraldry)">Sable</a> each with an arched bend Argent charged with three leaves of the lime-tree stems upward <a href="/wiki/Vert_(heraldry)" title="Vert (heraldry)">Vert</a>".</p>
<p>This crest is used by the descendants of Otto and differs from the crest used by the descendants of Walram. But in the royal decree of 1815 the crest issuing from a crown on the Dutch Royal Arms was the one used by the Walram line. Why this was done is not sure. Maybe due to the "mistake" this crest was hardly used.</p>
<p>The crest of the Walram-line is: Between two trunks Azure billetty Or a sitting lion Or. The trunks are probably a misinterpretation of two cow horns, a crest that is frequently used in German <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">heraldry</a>. On the Grand Coat of Arms of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Grand Duke of Luxemburg</a> the lion is crowned, armed and langued Gules.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Princedom_of_Orange">The Princedom of Orange</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Princedom of Orange">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The motto has been used by every "ruling" member of the Nassau family, who was also the prince of Orange since it came into the family with the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Orange" title="Prince of Orange">Princedom of Orange</a> in 1530. Count <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_Nassau-Breda" title="Henry III of Nassau-Breda">Henry III of Nassau-Breda</a>, who was living in the Low Countries, was married to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claudia_of_Ch%C3%A2lon" title="Claudia of Châlon">Claudia Orange-Châlon</a>. Her brother, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philibert_of_Ch%C3%A2lon" title="Philibert of Châlon">Philibert of Châlon</a>, was the last Prince of Orange from the House of Châlon. When he died in 1530, Henry's and Claudia's son René of Nassau-Breda inherited the Princedom on condition that he used the name and coat of arms of the Châlon family. History knows him therefore as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_of_Ch%C3%A2lon" title="René of Châlon">René of Châlon</a>. With this inheritance came the "Je Maintiendrai Châlons" motto into the Nassau family. René died in 1544 without leaving a child. His cousin William of Nassau-Dillenburg inherited all of René's lands. William became William of Orange (in English better known under his nickname <a href="/wiki/William_the_Silent" title="William the Silent">William the Silent</a>) and the founder of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau" title="House of Orange-Nassau">House of Orange-Nassau</a>. William first changed the motto to "Je Maintiendrai Nassau". Later he (or his sons) dropped the family name from the motto.</p>
<p>The horn from the arms of the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Orange" title="Prince of Orange">Princedom of Orange</a> is not used in the coat of arms of the kingdom but is part of the personal arms and flags of many members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_monarchy" title="Dutch monarchy">royal family</a>. See for example the <a class="external text" href="http://www.koninklijkhuis.nl/dsc?s=obj&amp;c=getobject&amp;objectid=8418&amp;getastype=org&amp;!dsname=minazkh" rel="nofollow">image</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Standard_of_the_Netherlands" title="Royal Standard of the Netherlands">Royal Standard of the Netherlands</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Dutch_Republic">The Dutch Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Dutch Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The sword and arrows originated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habsburg" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> rulers.</p>

<p>The lion, as representing the <a href="/wiki/Burgundian_Netherlands" title="Burgundian Netherlands">Burgundian Netherlands</a>, first appears as a <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">crest</a> on the tomb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philip_the_Handsome" title="Philip the Handsome">Philip the Handsome</a>. Later <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V</a> added the sword. The arrows where used, on coins etc., since the early 16th century to represent the <a href="/wiki/Seventeen_Provinces" title="Seventeen Provinces">Seventeen Provinces</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_countries" title="Low countries">low countries</a> under control of Charles V. In 1578, during the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years' War">Eighty Years' War</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estates-General_of_the_Netherlands" title="Estates-General of the Netherlands">States General</a> ordered a new great seal representing the lion, the sword and the 17 arrows combined. Although only seven provinces remained free from Spain, this seal stayed in use until 1795.</p>
<p>After the completion of its forming in 1584 the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Republic of the Seven United Provinces</a> used as its arms: Or a crowned lion Gules armed and langued Azure, holding in his dexter paw a sword and in the sinister paw seven arrows tight together Azure. The colours of this version where derived from the most important of the seven provinces, the county of Holland (its arms are still in use since being adopted by the <a href="/wiki/Count_of_Holland" title="Count of Holland">counts of Holland</a> c. 1198).</p>
<p>After c. 1668 the colours where reversed and the arms became Gules a crowned lion Or armed and langued Azure holding in his dexter paw a sword Argent hilted Or and in the sinister paw seven arrows Argent pointed and tight together Or.</p>
<p>The arrows symbolize the seven provinces that made up the Republic, the sword the determination to defend their liberty, and the coronet their sovereignty.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Netherlands_Maiden" title="Netherlands Maiden">Netherlands Maiden</a></div>
<p>In 1795, with French help, the last <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stadholder" title="Stadholder">Stadholder</a> <a href="/wiki/William_V,_Prince_of_Orange" title="William V, Prince of Orange">William V</a> was forced to flee and the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> (1795–1806) was proclaimed. At first this had no influence on the use of the arms of the former Republic. However, the following year the lion, that had served for approximately 280 years, was replaced by an allegoric image of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Netherlands_Maiden" title="Netherlands Maiden">“Dutch maiden of Freedom”</a>.</p>
<p>The replacement of the Batavian Republic with the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Holland" title="Kingdom of Holland">Kingdom of Holland</a> (1806–1810) saw the first return of the lion of the States General. <a href="/wiki/Louis_Bonaparte" title="Louis Bonaparte">Louis Napoleon Bonaparte</a> (brother of the French Emperor <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>) used as King Louis I a coat of arms that quartered the Dutch lion with the <a href="/wiki/French_Imperial_Eagle" title="French Imperial Eagle">French Imperial Eagle</a>. After the emperor Napoleon abolished the Kingdom of Holland in 1810 the lion again had to leave the stage and the Imperial Eagle was the only image in use.</p>
<p>In 1813 the French were forced out of the Netherlands and the son of the last Stadholder, <a href="/wiki/William_I_of_the_Netherlands" title="William I of the Netherlands">William VI / I</a> was proclaimed 'Sovereign Prince' (1813–1815). To symbolize his new status he assumed a new coat of arms. In it the old lion with the sword and arrows made his second reappearance, now with a Royal crown upon his head. Again it was placed in de prime locations of a quartered shield (I and IV quarter). In the II and III quarter where the arms of Châlon-Orange-Geneve, the arms of Nassau (Otto) where placed on an escutcheon in the center of the shield.</p>
<p>The final retirement of the Republican lion came in 1815 with the establishment of the “<a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="United Kingdom of the Netherlands">United Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>”. Because this new kingdom comprised not only of the lands of the former Dutch Republic but also of the former Austrian or <a href="/wiki/Southern_Netherlands" title="Southern Netherlands">Southern Netherlands</a> it was also not appropriate to continue the use of the old arms. First a combination with the arms of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Brabant" title="Duchy of Brabant">Brabant</a> (Sable a Lion Or, now the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Belgium" title="Coat of arms of Belgium">coat of arms of Belgium</a>) was considered. In the end the attributes, the sword, arrows and crown, were placed in the care of his older “colleague” from Nassau to symbolize the union between the (now Royal) House of Nassau and the Netherlands. As seen above, this is still the basis of the current coat of arms.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Versions_and_variants">Versions and variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Versions and variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Various versions of the Dutch Royal Arms are used by <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_Netherlands" title="Cabinet of the Netherlands">Government</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">Parliament</a> and courts. Government and its agencies generally use a simplified version of the Royal Arms without the mantle, the pavilion and the topped royal crown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This simplified Royal Arms also feature on the cover of <a href="/wiki/Dutch_passport" title="Dutch passport">passports</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_of_the_Netherlands" title="List of diplomatic missions of the Netherlands">embassies and consulates</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The versions used by the Legislature and its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chambers_of_parliament" title="Chambers of parliament">chambers</a> shown the Royal Arms with the Royal Crown and a buckled dark-blue strap that bears the name of the Parliament or each chamber <i>Staten-Generaal</i> (<a href="/wiki/States_General_of_the_Netherlands" title="States General of the Netherlands">States General</a>), <i>Eerste Kamer der Staten-Generaal</i> (<a href="/wiki/Senate_(Netherlands)" title="Senate (Netherlands)">Senate</a>), <i>Tweede Kamer der Staten-Generaal</i> (<a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_(Netherlands)" title="House of Representatives (Netherlands)">House of Representatives</a>) in gold letters surrounding the shield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
