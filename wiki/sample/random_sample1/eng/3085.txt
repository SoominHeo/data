<p>The <b>Minister-President of the Netherlands</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl" xml:lang="nl"><i>Minister-president van Nederland</i></span>), commonly referred to in English as the <b>Prime Minister</b>, is the head of the executive arm of the Dutch government and the <a href="/wiki/Chairman" title="Chairman">chairman</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_of_Ministers_of_the_Netherlands" title="Council of Ministers of the Netherlands">Council of Ministers of the Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlement1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-parlement1-3">[3]</a></sup> He is the <i>de facto</i> <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> of the Netherlands and coordinates the policy of the government. The current prime minister is <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rutte" title="Mark Rutte">Mark Rutte</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prime_Minister_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although he is the most important political figure in the Netherlands, the Prime Minister is not as powerful as his <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">British</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_Germany_(Federal_Republic)" title="Chancellor of Germany (Federal Republic)">German</a> counterparts. This is mainly because, due to the Dutch system of <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">proportional representation</a>, it is nearly impossible for one party to get a majority of seats in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_the_Netherlands" title="House of Representatives of the Netherlands">House of Representatives</a> (lower house). Therefore, the government is always a coalition between two or more parties. Because of his limited powers, the prime minister is described as <a href="/wiki/Primus_inter_pares" title="Primus inter pares">primus inter pares</a> (first among equals).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlement1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-parlement1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a result of the constitutional review of 1983, the position of Prime Minister was inscribed into the Dutch constitution for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> According to the constitution, the Government is constituted by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Netherlands" title="King of the Netherlands">King</a> and the ministers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The constitution stipulates the Prime Minister chairs the council of ministers (article 45) and is appointed by royal decree (article 43). The royal decree of their own appointment and those of the other ministers are to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Countersignature" title="Countersignature">countersigned</a> by the Prime Minister (article 48).</p>
<p>The Prime Minister chairs the weekly meetings of the council of ministers and has the power to set the agenda of these meetings. The prime minister is also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_General_Affairs" title="Ministry of General Affairs">Minister of General Affairs</a> (<i>Minister van Algemene Zaken</i>), which takes an important role in coordinating policy and is responsible for the Government Information Service (Dutch: <i>Rijksvoorlichtingsdienst</i>). The Prime Minister is also responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_Netherlands" title="Monarchy of the Netherlands">royal house</a> and has a weekly meeting with the King on government policy. Informally the Prime Minister functions as the "face" of the cabinet to the public. After the meetings of the cabinet on Friday, the Prime Minister hosts a press conference on the decisions of the cabinet and current affairs. The Prime Minister also has some functions in international affairs, attending the <a href="/wiki/European_Council" title="European Council">European Council</a> every six months and maintaining bilateral contacts. The Prime Minister's office is a hexagon shaped tower, named "The Little Tower" Het <a href="/wiki/Torentje" title="Torentje">Torentje</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Binnenhof" title="Binnenhof">Binnenhof</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>. The official residence (which is only used for official functions) is the <a href="/wiki/Catshuis" title="Catshuis">Catshuis</a>.</p>

<p>Conventionally, the party with the largest number of seats in the Second Chamber will initiate coalition talks after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_general_election" title="Dutch general election">elections</a>. These negotiations are concluded by means of a so-called "government agreement". The Second Chamber will have a debate about this agreement and, if this agreement meets sufficient support, the leader of the party with the largest number of seats in the Second Chamber will be instituted as <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_Netherlands#Formation" title="Cabinet of the Netherlands">formator</a> of the cabinet. He or she usually appoints her/himself Prime Minister. A minister from the smaller coalition party usually becomes <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister_of_the_Netherlands" title="Deputy Prime Minister of the Netherlands">Deputy Prime Minister</a> of the cabinet. If there is a third party in the coalition, one of its ministers will become the second <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister_of_the_Netherlands" title="Deputy Prime Minister of the Netherlands">Deputy Prime Minister</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>For a list of historic Prime Ministers, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_the_Netherlands" title="List of Prime Ministers of the Netherlands">List of Prime Ministers of the Netherlands</a>.</i> <i>For a list of Prime Ministers by age, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_the_Netherlands_by_age" title="List of Prime Ministers of the Netherlands by age">List of Prime Ministers of the Netherlands by age</a>.</i> <i>For a list of Prime Ministers by religious affiliations, see <a href="/wiki/Religious_affiliations_of_Prime_Ministers_of_the_Netherlands" title="Religious affiliations of Prime Ministers of the Netherlands">Religious affiliations of Prime Ministers of the Netherlands</a>.</i></p>
<p>Gradually the Prime Minister became an official function of government leader, taken by the political leader of the largest party. Since 1848 the role of the first minister has become relevant. In that year the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Netherlands" title="Constitution of the Netherlands">Constitution of the Netherlands</a> was amended to make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministerial_responsibility" title="Ministerial responsibility">ministers responsible</a> to parliament, rather than - as hitherto - being responsible to the King, who acted as the leader of cabinet. Until 1901 the position chair of the council of ministers officially rotated between ministers. Between 1901 and 1945 the position formally still rotated but prominent politicians were able to claim a rotation period of four years. In 1937 a separate Ministry of General Affairs was instituted which was informally linked to the Prime Minister. <a href="/wiki/Barend_Biesheuvel" title="Barend Biesheuvel">Barend Biesheuvel</a> (1971-1973) was the last Prime Minister who was not the political leader of the largest party in cabinet, but actually of the third largest. In 1983 the function of Prime Minister was laid down in the constitution.</p>

<p>The position of the Prime Minister has been enforced by the creation of the <a href="/wiki/European_Council" title="European Council">European Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In November 2006, the rules of procedure of the council of ministers was changed to allow the Prime Minister to put any item on the agenda of the council, whereas before he had to wait for a minister to take the initiative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A change of the rules of procedure of the cabinet in July 2008 allowed the Prime Minister to direct other ministers on the costs of the Royal House, which are covered by several ministries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Countries_of_the_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands">Countries of the Kingdom of the Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prime_Minister_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Countries of the Kingdom of the Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Prime Minister is also Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, and therefore also deals with matters affecting the other countries <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a> in the Kingdom. The independent cabinets of Aruba, Curaçao, and Sint Maarten also have their own prime ministers: <a href="/wiki/Mike_Eman" title="Mike Eman">Mike Eman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Aruba" title="Prime Minister of Aruba">Prime Minister of Aruba</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ben_Whiteman" title="Ben Whiteman">Ben Whiteman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Prime Minister of Curaçao">Prime Minister of Curaçao</a>), and <a href="/wiki/William_Marlin" title="William Marlin">William Marlin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Sint_Maarten" title="Prime Minister of Sint Maarten">Prime Minister of Sint Maarten</a>). The Council of Ministers of the Kingdom of the Netherlands includes <a href="/wiki/Minister_Plenipotentiary_(Netherlands)" title="Minister Plenipotentiary (Netherlands)">Minister Plenipotentiary</a> from the other countries of the Kingdom. These are not included in the government of the Kingdom.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deputies">Deputies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prime_Minister_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Deputies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister_of_the_Netherlands" title="Deputy Prime Minister of the Netherlands">Deputy Prime Minister of the Netherlands</a></div>
<p>The King appoints <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister" title="Deputy Prime Minister">Deputy Prime Ministers</a>. Conventionally all of the junior partners in the coalition get one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister" title="Deputy Prime Minister">Deputy Prime Minister</a>; they are ranked according to the size of their respective parties. When the Prime minister is not present at a cabinet meeting, the senior deputy present chairs it. In the current <a href="/wiki/Second_Rutte_cabinet" title="Second Rutte cabinet">Second Rutte cabinet</a> <a href="/wiki/Lodewijk_Asscher" title="Lodewijk Asscher">Lodewijk Asscher</a> the <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister_of_the_Netherlands" title="Deputy Prime Minister of the Netherlands">Deputy Prime Minister of the Netherlands</a> chairs those meetings.If the Prime Minister and the deputies are absent, the oldest member of the cabinet chairs the meeting. In the current <a href="/wiki/Second_Rutte_cabinet" title="Second Rutte cabinet">Second Rutte cabinet</a> <a href="/wiki/Henk_Kamp" title="Henk Kamp">Henk Kamp</a> the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Economic_Affairs_(Netherlands)" title="Ministry of Economic Affairs (Netherlands)">Minister of Economic Affairs</a> chairs those meetings.</p>
