<p><b>Nemanja Matić</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_alphabet" title="Serbian Cyrillic alphabet">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: Немања Матић; born 1 August 1988) is a Serbian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for English club <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbia national team</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sway_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sway-3">[3]</a></sup> He is known for his consistent performances on the pitch and his combative style of play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Matić began his career at <a href="/wiki/FK_Jedinstvo_Ub" title="FK Jedinstvo Ub">Jedinstvo Ub</a> and Slovak side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MFK_Ko%C5%A1ice" title="MFK Košice">MFK Košice</a> before joining Chelsea for £1.5 million in 2009. Used sparingly during his first spell at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a>, he spent the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Eredivisie" title="2010–11 Eredivisie">2010–11 season</a> on loan at Dutch club <a href="/wiki/SBV_Vitesse" title="SBV Vitesse">Vitesse</a>, and in January 2011 he moved to <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> in a swap deal involving <a href="/wiki/David_Luiz" title="David Luiz">David Luiz</a>. Matić returned to Chelsea three years later, for £21 million. He has been a full international for Serbia since 2008, earning over 25 caps.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mati%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mati%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0abac" title="Šabac">Šabac</a>, Matić began his senior career at <a href="/wiki/FK_Kolubara" title="FK Kolubara">Kolubara</a> in 2005. He signed for Slovak club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MFK_Ko%C5%A1ice" title="MFK Košice">Košice</a> in January 2007 and stayed at the club until the summer of 2009. While living in the country, he also received Slovak citizenship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chelsea">Chelsea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mati%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chelsea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 August 2009, Matić signed for <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> from MFK Košice for a fee of around £1.5 million on a four-year contract, having been on trial at <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He was given the number 24 shirt by Chelsea but was not able to make his debut immediately due to the injury he picked up at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2009 under-21 championships</a>. He was on the bench for Chelsea's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> on 23 September 2009, but did not take any part in the 1–0 victory. Matić made his <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut for Chelsea on 21 November, coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Florent_Malouda" title="Florent Malouda">Florent Malouda</a> in the 69th minute in a 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 August 2010, Matić transferred to <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> club <a href="/wiki/SBV_Vitesse" title="SBV Vitesse">Vitesse</a> on a one-year loan, together with teammates <a href="/wiki/Slobodan_Rajkovi%C4%87" title="Slobodan Rajković">Slobodan Rajković</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matej_Dela%C4%8D" title="Matej Delač">Matej Delač</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benfica">Benfica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mati%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Benfica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nemanja_Mati%C4%87.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="409" data-file-width="186" height="264" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Nemanja_Mati%C4%87.jpg/120px-Nemanja_Mati%C4%87.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Nemanja_Mati%C4%87.jpg/180px-Nemanja_Mati%C4%87.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6a/Nemanja_Mati%C4%87.jpg 2x" width="120"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nemanja_Mati%C4%87.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Matić with <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> in 2012</div>

<p>On 31 January 2011, Matić was transferred to Portuguese club <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> along with €25 million (£21M) in a swap deal for Brazilian centre back <a href="/wiki/David_Luiz" title="David Luiz">David Luiz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Under Benfica manager <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Jesus" title="Jorge Jesus">Jorge Jesus</a>, Matić was converted from a <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a> into a defensive midfielder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-interview-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 April 2012, Matić started for Benfica in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Ta%C3%A7a_da_Liga_Final" title="2012 Taça da Liga Final">2012 Taça da Liga Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Gil_Vicente_F.C." title="Gil Vicente F.C.">Gil Vicente</a>, where he won his first trophy with the <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>-based club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-interview-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 January 2013, Matić scored Benfica's first goal in a 2–2 draw with <i><a href="/wiki/O_Cl%C3%A1ssico" title="O Clássico">O Clássico</a></i> rivals <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The next day, he was awarded with a contract extension lasting until 2018, with the buyout clause set at €45 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Benfica's second-leg <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Europa_League_knockout_phase#Round_of_32" title="2012–13 UEFA Europa League knockout phase">Round of 32</a> Europa League clash with <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a>, Matić headed in a late goal after a cross from <a href="/wiki/Lima_(footballer)" title="Lima (footballer)">Lima</a> to seal a 3–1 aggregate victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Matić was an integral member throughout the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Primeira_Liga" title="2012–13 Primeira Liga">2012–13 Primeira Liga</a> campaign as Benfica looked to claim the club's first league championship since <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Primeira_Liga" title="2009–10 Primeira Liga">2010</a>. Going into the penultimate game of the season against Porto, both teams were undefeated in league play,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> but a stoppage-time winner from substitute <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_Mateus_de_Oliveira" title="Kelvin Mateus de Oliveira">Kelvin</a> gave Porto a 2–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 May, Matić faced former employers Chelsea in the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2013 UEFA Europa League Final">Europa League</a> final in Amsterdam but was on the losing side as Benfica fell to a 2–1 defeat, courtesy of a last minute <a href="/wiki/Branislav_Ivanovi%C4%87" title="Branislav Ivanović">Branislav Ivanović</a> header.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 4 July 2013, he was named <a href="/wiki/LPFP_Primeira_Liga_Player_of_the_Year" title="LPFP Primeira Liga Player of the Year">Primeira Liga Player of the Year</a> after having won the monthly award three times over the course of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref1-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_S.L._Benfica_season" title="2013–14 S.L. Benfica season">2013–14 season</a>, Matić played half of the season, with 22 matches and 3 goals, he helped Benfica to win the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Primeira_Liga" title="2013–14 Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> and <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="2013–14 Taça de Portugal">Taça de Portugal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On 13 January 2014, Matić placed second in the 2013 <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Pusk%C3%A1s_Award#2013" title="FIFA Puskás Award">FIFA Puskás Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Chelsea">Return to Chelsea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mati%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to Chelsea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chelsea_6_Maribor_0_Champions_League_(14979406193).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2808" data-file-width="2808" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Chelsea_6_Maribor_0_Champions_League_%2814979406193%29.jpg/220px-Chelsea_6_Maribor_0_Champions_League_%2814979406193%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Chelsea_6_Maribor_0_Champions_League_%2814979406193%29.jpg/330px-Chelsea_6_Maribor_0_Champions_League_%2814979406193%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Chelsea_6_Maribor_0_Champions_League_%2814979406193%29.jpg/440px-Chelsea_6_Maribor_0_Champions_League_%2814979406193%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Matić in action in Chelsea's 6–0 win over <a href="/wiki/NK_Maribor" title="NK Maribor">Maribor</a> on 21 October 2014</div>

<p>On 15 January 2014, Matić rejoined Chelsea for a fee of £21 million (€25M),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> on a five-and-a-half-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He made his second debut for the club four days later, replacing <a href="/wiki/Willian_(footballer)" title="Willian (footballer)">Willian</a> in a 3–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 3 February, Matić made his first <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Premier_League" title="2013–14 Premier League">Premier League</a> start in Chelsea's fixture away at championship rivals <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He was named <a href="/wiki/Man_of_the_match" title="Man of the match">man of the match</a> by <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> as Chelsea ended City's unbeaten run at the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Manchester_Stadium" title="City of Manchester Stadium">City of Manchester Stadium</a> with a 1–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Matić scored his first Premier League goal on 30 August 2014, in a 6–3 win at <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> A month later, Matić scored the only goal as Chelsea won away at <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting CP</a> in the group stage of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> on 5 November in the same competition, his goal earned Chelsea a 1–1 draw at <a href="/wiki/NK_Maribor" title="NK Maribor">Maribor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Matić was given a straight red card in Chelsea's 1–1 home draw against <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> on 21 February 2015 for pushing over <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Barnes" title="Ashley Barnes">Ashley Barnes</a> following a high tackle by him, which resulted in the bending of Matić's shin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On appeal, his suspension was shortened to two matches, including the <a href="/wiki/2015_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2015 Football League Cup Final">League Cup Final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 April, Matić was named as one of four midfielders in the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Team_of_the_Year" title="PFA Team of the Year">PFA Team of the Year</a>, alongside teammate <a href="/wiki/Eden_Hazard" title="Eden Hazard">Eden Hazard</a>. Four more Chelsea players were included in the selection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-six_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-six-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 September 2015, Matić scored his first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Premier_League" title="2015–16 Premier League">2015–16 Premier League</a> season against Everton, but it was not enough to save Chelsea from a 3–1 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Matić was sent off for two yellow cards in the first half of Chelsea's 2–1 loss at <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on 24 October 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mati%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Matić's debut for the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-21_football_team" title="Serbia national under-21 football team">Serbia under-21 team</a> came in a match against <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_under-21_football_team" title="Denmark national under-21 football team">Denmark</a> on 11 October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> After three matches and two goals for the Serbia under-21 squad, he was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbian senior squad</a> and made his debut in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> on 14 December 2008 in a 1–0 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Matić took part in the 2009 UEFA Euro under-21 Championship, where he played 85 minutes in the first group match against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy</a> in a 0–0 draw. Matić, however, injured himself after contact with Italian forward <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Giovinco" title="Sebastian Giovinco">Sebastian Giovinco</a>. He broke the fifth metatarsal in his right foot and was forced to undergo surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2012, Matić announced he would not play for the Serbian national team while <a href="/wiki/Sini%C5%A1a_Mihajlovi%C4%87" title="Siniša Mihajlović">Siniša Mihajlović</a> was the coach. The reason he gave for this decision is that he felt he was not given the right opportunity by the coach not playing a single minute in his last five call-ups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On 6 September 2013, Matić returned to his national team on a <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_A#Serbia_v_Croatia" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group A">2014 FIFA World Cup qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a>, being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sent_off#Association_football" title="Sent off">sent off</a> in the 75th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>He scored his first international goal on 29 March 2015 in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying</a> match away to <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_da_Luz" title="Estádio da Luz">Estádio da Luz</a>, equalising via an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overhead_kick" title="Overhead kick">overhead kick</a>, albeit in a 1–2 defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On 11 October, in a loss against the same opponents by the same score at the <a href="/wiki/Partizan_Stadium" title="Partizan Stadium">Partizan Stadium</a>, Matić received a straight red card within a minute of teammate <a href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Kolarov" title="Aleksandar Kolarov">Aleksandar Kolarov</a>'s dismissal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mati%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Matić's younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Uro%C5%A1_Mati%C4%87" title="Uroš Matić">Uroš</a>, is also a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Matić is of partial <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonian</a> descent as his grandfather, on his mother's side, is from <a href="/wiki/Volkovija" title="Volkovija">Volkovija</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
