<p><b>Neven Subotić</b> (<small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[něʋen sûbotitɕ]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Sh-Neven_Suboti%C4%87.oga" title="File:Sh-Neven Subotić.oga"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/ff/Sh-Neven_Suboti%C4%87.oga" title="Sh-Neven Subotić.oga">listen</a>)</small>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic" title="Serbian Cyrillic">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: Heвeн Cубoтић; born 10 December 1988) is a Serbian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre back</a> for the German <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> club <a href="/wiki/1._FC_K%C3%B6ln" title="1. FC Köln">1. FC Köln</a> on loan from <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He made his first-team debut in the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Bundesliga" title="2006–07 Bundesliga">2006–07 season</a> for <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">1. FSV Mainz 05</a>. In 2008, he signed with Borussia Dortmund.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Banja_Luka" title="Banja Luka">Banja Luka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a>, to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bosnian_Serb" title="Bosnian Serb">Bosnian Serb</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> parents, father Željko from the village of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kula%C5%A1i&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kulaši (page does not exist)">Kulaši</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://bs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kula%C5%A1i" title="bs:Kulaši">bs</a>)</span> and mother Svjetlana from <a href="/wiki/Brestovo_(Doboj)" title="Brestovo (Doboj)">Brestovo</a>, Subotić spent his early childhood in <a href="/wiki/Prnjavor,_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Prnjavor, Bosnia and Herzegovina">Prnjavor</a>. In 1990, when Subotić was two years old, his father went to Germany in search of work. In 1994, with the <a href="/wiki/Bosnian_War" title="Bosnian War">Bosnian War</a> raging, the rest of the family including five-year-old Neven, his sister and their mother joined their father in Germany, settling in <a href="/wiki/Sch%C3%B6mberg,_Calw" title="Schömberg, Calw">Schömberg</a>. Neven took up football at the age of seven with the local outfit TSV Schwarzenberg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-si.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-si.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the late 1990s, the Subotić family's residence authorization in Germany expired and in order to avoid being deported back to Bosnia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> they opted to move to the United States in 1999. They settled in <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>, Utah, where Subotić's father had a cousin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-si.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-si.com-3">[3]</a></sup> Subotić played football with Sparta Gold and Impact Black youth clubs.</p>
<p>Within two years, the family was on the move again, this time to <a href="/wiki/Bradenton,_Florida" title="Bradenton, Florida">Bradenton, Florida</a>, so that Subotić's sister Natalija could pursue a tennis career at the Nick Bollettieri Tennis Academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-si.com_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-si.com-3">[3]</a></sup> The city also happens to be the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IMG_Soccer_Academy" title="IMG Soccer Academy">base</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_U-17_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States U-17 men's national soccer team">United States under-17 national team</a>. Subotić trained on his own in G.T. Bray Park, where he got spotted by Keith Fulk, one of the American team's assistant coaches who then informed the team's head coach, <a href="/wiki/John_Ellinger" title="John Ellinger">John Ellinger</a>, about Subotić. After arranging a tryout, they offered Subotić a spot in the residency camp. At the time, he was not attached to any club sides, but eventually started playing with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Florida" title="University of South Florida">University of South Florida</a> team.</p>
<p>While playing with the under-17 team in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, Subotić was approached by player agent Steve Kelly, who inquired about his career plans and offered a possibility of playing in Europe. After impressing at the tryout for <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">1. FSV Mainz 05</a>, young Subotić was on his way there, initially playing for the club's youth and fourth-division teams (1. FSV Mainz 05 II).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-si.com_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-si.com-3">[3]</a></sup> Subotić holds a <a href="/wiki/German_passport" title="German passport">German passport</a>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_passport" title="American passport">American passport</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_passport" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina passport">Bosnian passport</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Serbian_passport" title="Serbian passport">Serbian passport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neven_Suboti.C4.87_for_Germany_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neven_Suboti.C4.87_for_Germany-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1._FSV_Mainz_05">1. FSV Mainz 05</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1. FSV Mainz 05">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Subotić made his professional debut for <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">1. FSV Mainz 05</a> in the last match of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Bundesliga" title="2006–07 Bundesliga">2006–07 season</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>, as his team was relegated from the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_2._Bundesliga" title="2007–08 2. Bundesliga">following season</a>, he seized a starting role in what was the leagueÄs best defense, conceding only 37 goals. The team finished in fourth place, missing the promotion back to Bundesliga by only two points.</p>
<p>In the 2008 summer off-season, Mainz head coach <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Klopp" title="Jürgen Klopp">Jürgen Klopp</a> got a job coaching <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> and was instrumental in bringing Subotić along with him.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Borussia_Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Borussia Dortmund">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>On 4 June 2008, it was announced that Subotić had signed with <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> on a five-year contract. He debuted for his new club in the unofficial <a href="/wiki/DFL-Supercup" title="DFL-Supercup">DFL-Supercup</a> match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>, which Dortmund won 2–1. Subotić's defensive displays quickly turned a lot of heads. In December 2008, he was named in the ideal XI team of the first part of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Bundesliga" title="2008–09 Bundesliga">2008–09 Bundesliga</a> season, alongside such defensive stars as <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BAcio" title="Lúcio">Lúcio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Lahm" title="Philipp Lahm">Philipp Lahm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neven_Suboti.C4.87_izabrao_Srbiju_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neven_Suboti.C4.87_izabrao_Srbiju-6">[6]</a></sup> In addition to confident and effective defensive displays far beyond his years, Subotić was also a factor on the offensive end, scoring six league goals throughout the season.</p>
<p>In June 2009, he signed a new contract which lasts until summer 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Bundesliga" title="2009–10 Bundesliga">2009–10 Bundesliga</a> season, Subotić was one of only four players in the entire league to play every minute of every league match.</p>
<p>On 15 December 2010, Subotić scored his first ever goal in the European competition, scoring a header off a corner at <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Europa_League_group_stage" title="2010–11 UEFA Europa League group stage">Europa League</a> to tie the score at 2–2. The match was a decider on which of the two teams would go on from the group stage, as Borussia needed to win away while Sevilla was fine even with a draw. It ended 2–2, however, and Borussia were eliminated.</p>
<p>On 27 July 2013, Subotić won the <a href="/wiki/2013_DFL-Supercup" title="2013 DFL-Supercup">2013 DFL-Supercup</a> with Dortmund 4–2 against rivals <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 May 2015 he signed a new contract until the end of the 2017–18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 April 2016, it was announced that Subotić would miss the remainder of Dortmund's season as he was suffering from <a href="/wiki/Thrombosis" title="Thrombosis">thrombosis</a> in his arm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At the end of the season he announced via his <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> account that he would be leaving Dortmund.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 June 2016, Subotić confirmed that he planned to leave the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_level">Youth level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Youth level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Subotić was part of the squad selected and coached by <a href="/wiki/John_Hackworth" title="John Hackworth">John Hackworth</a> for the U.S. entry in the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_U-17_World_Championship" title="2005 FIFA U-17 World Championship">2005 FIFA U-17 World Championship</a> during September 2005 in <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. Sixteen years of age at the time, he entered all three group matches as a substitute during the final five minutes. In the quarter-final versus the Netherlands, where the U.S. team lost 0–2 and were eliminated, he played from the start but received a red card in the 73rd minute.</p>
<p>He has also made two appearances for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_U-20_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States U-20 men's national soccer team">USA U-20 team</a>. What would prove to be his last one took place in November 2006 in a friendly after which head coach <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Rongen" title="Thomas Rongen">Thomas Rongen</a> criticized Subotić, who had signed for Mainz several months earlier, for "not accelerating over there to the point where we feel he belongs on the [U.S.] team."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-internationalfuture_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-internationalfuture-13">[13]</a></sup> Next summer, Rongen controversially did not select him to play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_U-20_World_Cup" title="2007 U-20 World Cup">2007 U-20 World Cup</a> in Canada, picking defenders such as <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Sturgis" title="Nathan Sturgis">Nathan Sturgis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Wallace_(soccer)" title="Anthony Wallace (soccer)">Anthony Wallace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Julian_Valentin" title="Julian Valentin">Julian Valentin</a>, Ofori Sarkodie, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Ward_(soccer)" title="Tim Ward (soccer)">Tim Ward</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Amaechi_Igwe" title="Amaechi Igwe">Amaechi Igwe</a> ahead of Subotić, a decision that has resulted in Rongen receiving a fair amount of criticism. While Rongen insisted that a groin injury was to blame,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> there has been considerable speculation since then that Subotić was so stung by Rongen's criticism that he decided to reconsider his international future:</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Full_squad">Full squad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Full squad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Neven_subotich.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2989" data-file-width="2181" height="240" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Neven_subotich.JPG/175px-Neven_subotich.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Neven_subotich.JPG/263px-Neven_subotich.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Neven_subotich.JPG/350px-Neven_subotich.JPG 2x" width="175"/></a>

<p>Subotić was eligible to represent the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-si.com_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-si.com-3">[3]</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> During September 2008, when the issue of his national team eligibility first started getting wider coverage in the football media, playing for <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a> or even <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> were mentioned as possibilities as well. Both options, however, were soon revealed to be media concoctions more than anything else. Bosnia and Herzegovina fell out of the running early as Subotić publicly rejected overtures by the country's football officials and head coach <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_Bla%C5%BEevi%C4%87" title="Miroslav Blažević">Miroslav Blažević</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>By late fall 2008, it became clear that only Serbia figured in his international choice. Citing sources close to Subotić's family, many reports in the Serbian press appeared about his decision to play for Serbia already being made, but waiting for the right moment to state it publicly since he had until 10 December 2009 (his 21st birthday) to declare his choice. In mid-December 2008, Subotić informed national team head coach <a href="/wiki/Radomir_Anti%C4%87" title="Radomir Antić">Radomir Antić</a> about his decision to represent Serbia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neven_Suboti.C4.87_izabrao_Srbiju_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Neven_Suboti.C4.87_izabrao_Srbiju-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Then in late December 2008, Subotić notified the US Soccer Federation about his decision, the first official step in the process of changing national team attachment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_World_Cup_qualifying">2010 World Cup qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010 World Cup qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 March 2009, Subotić made his debut for the Serbian national team in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_7" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group 7">2010 World Cup qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constan%C5%A3a" title="Constanţa">Constanţa</a> that Serbia won 2–3. Coming on as a 65th-minute substitute for striker <a href="/wiki/Marko_Panteli%C4%87" title="Marko Pantelić">Marko Pantelić</a> with Serbia leading 1–3, Subotić played the slightly altered central defensive role as Antić looked to protect the lead for the remaining 30 minutes in the crucial qualifying match. Afterwards, head coach Antić was very supportive of his new young defender, comparing him to <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Hierro" title="Fernando Hierro">Fernando Hierro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> After starting against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a> in a friendly, Subotić's first competitive start took place against <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_football_team" title="Austria national football team">Austria</a> on 6 June just after the end of club season, partnering <a href="/wiki/Nemanja_Vidi%C4%87" title="Nemanja Vidić">Nemanja Vidić</a> in central defence (if only for one half, since Vidić got injured and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Rukavina" title="Antonio Rukavina">Antonio Rukavina</a>) — the contest in which Serbia displayed a very lethargic overall team effort and was lucky to win 1–0 after numerous defensive breakdowns. Four days later, on 10 June 2009, Subotić scored his first goal for Serbia in a fairly straightforward 2–0 win over the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands_national_football_team" title="Faroe Islands national football team">Faroe Islands</a>. For the next qualifying match, at the end of summer against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, Serbia's chance to seal qualification by winning at home, Subotić got benched and didn't enter the contest at all, as Antić preferred <a href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Lukovi%C4%87" title="Aleksandar Luković">Aleksandar Luković</a> to partner Vidić on this occasion. The same central defensive pairing started the next qualifier against Romania at home (another chance to automatically qualify with a win after failing to beat the French), while Subotić got a chance to run out for the last 15 minutes as a sub for Vidić with the score 2–0 for Serbia as it eventually clinched the World Cup spot with a 5–0 thrashing. In the last meaningless qualifier against <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a>, Subotić played the full 90 minutes in a free role on the wing as Antić looked to experiment and try different setups without any fear of dropping points.</p>
<p>Though he came in with high praise and even higher hopes, Subotić's initial performances for Serbia weren't stellar. Following unconvincing defensive displays and fairly frequent mistakes, he had to deal with plenty of criticism in the Serbian press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_World_Cup">2010 World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2010 World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> in South Africa, Subotić was left out of the starting lineup for the opening group stage match against <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana</a>, with Nemanja Vidić and Aleksandar Luković the preferred choices for central defensive spots by coach Antić. Subotić's benching came as a result of his poor starting displays in the friendlies leading up to the World Cup against <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_football_team" title="New Zealand national football team">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon</a>. During the second half of the Ghana match, however, Luković received a red card and was sent off, forcing Antić to bring Subotić into the game for the remaining 14 minutes.</p>
<p>Due to Luković's one-match suspension as a result of the red card, Subotić started the next match in a must-win situation versus <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>, putting in a confident and effective performance against German forwards who despite mounting great pressure spells throughout the match ultimately failed to score as Serbia recorded a famous 1–0 win. Playing against the players he knew well from Bundesliga, Subotić showed excellent positional play with several crucial reactions, including the clearance in front of <a href="/wiki/Mesut_%C3%96zil" title="Mesut Özil">Mesut Özil</a> to prevent the rebound following goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Stojkovi%C4%87" title="Vladimir Stojković">Vladimir Stojković</a>'s penalty save on <a href="/wiki/Lukas_Podolski" title="Lukas Podolski">Lukas Podolski</a>.</p>
<p>For the group decider against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_football_team" title="Australia national football team">Australia</a>, Subotić was back on the bench as Luković returned from suspension.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Euro_2012_qualifying">Euro 2012 qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Euro 2012 qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Subotić returned in the starting lineup for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012_qualifying_Group_C" title="UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying Group C">qualifiers</a> that began under beleaguered head coach Antić, who was serving a four-match touchline ban as a result of the verbal altercation with the Uruguayan referee at the World Cup. The youngster played the full 90 minutes at Faroe Islands in the opener and also in the following match versus <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia</a>. Subotić's somewhat inconsistent form continued as he established a strong presence in central defence with Vidić, but also the duo's hesitant reaction to <a href="/wiki/Milivoje_Novakovi%C4%87" title="Milivoje Novaković">Milivoje Novaković</a>'s run off the ball led to Slovenian go-ahead goal. Following the Slovenian draw, Antić was fired and <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Petrovi%C4%87" title="Vladimir Petrović">Pižon Petrović</a> took over the job. Subotić got called up for the new coach's debut match against <a href="/wiki/Estonia_national_football_team" title="Estonia national football team">Estonia</a>, but was an unused sub as Serbia disastrously lost a competitive match at home for the first time in nine years. Petrović gave Subotić a starting assignment for the next qualifier away at <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> in October 2010, but the infamous contest in <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a> got interrupted after six minutes due to rioting by the traveling Serbian hooligans, which resulted in an automatic 3–0 loss for Serbia.</p>
<p>As the qualifying resumed in late March 2011, Subotić got the start at home against <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national football team">Northern Ireland</a>. Since center back Luković announced his retirement from international football before this match, 22-year-old Subotić essentially became an automatic first-team choice. However, partnering <a href="/wiki/Milan_Bi%C5%A1evac" title="Milan Biševac">Milan Biševac</a> in central defence (on instead of injured Vidić), Subotić again had a shaky performance as the duo failed to properly deal with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brunt" title="Chris Brunt">Chris Brunt</a>'s set piece cross that led to <a href="/wiki/Gareth_McAuley" title="Gareth McAuley">Gareth McAuley</a>'s headed goal. Furthermore, Subotić picked up an injury during the match that ruled him out of the next qualifier four days later at Estonia.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_World_Cup_qualifying">2014 World Cup qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2014 World Cup qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With <a href="/wiki/Sini%C5%A1a_Mihajlovi%C4%87" title="Siniša Mihajlović">Siniša Mihajlović</a>'s May 2012 arrival to the head coaching post, Subotić featured in a few preparation friendlies and even scored a goal in the loss to <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>, but was omitted from the final friendly at home versus the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> reportedly due to injury.</p>
<p>Once the 2014 World Cup qualifying cycle began in September 2012, Subotić was benched for the opening match away at <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> as the duo of Biševac and 19-year-old <a href="/wiki/Matija_Nastasi%C4%87" title="Matija Nastasić">Matija Nastasić</a> got the nod in central defence. Three days later, at home versus <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a>, the same situation repeated.</p>
<p>The following qualifier was a big test at home versus group favourites <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a>, and it still brought no change in Subotić's status, as Mihajlović continued with Nastasić and Biševac as his preferred central defenders. Despite creating many opportunities, Serbia failed to convert them, losing the match 0–3 in the end. The heartbreaking loss brought Mihajlović media criticism, including that over his continual omission of Subotić. Four days later, however, playing <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_national_football_team" title="Macedonia national football team">Macedonia</a> away in <a href="/wiki/Skopje" title="Skopje">Skopje</a> in a must-win situation, Mihajlović once again left Subotić on the bench without a minute of action. In a match of poor quality, amid reports of discord within the squad stemming from the Belgium loss, Serbia conceded a late penalty and disastrously lost 0–1 thus almost losing a chance of getting one of the top two spots in the group with only four points from four match at the winter break.</p>
<p>Throughout October 2012, Subotić continued with stellar performances for Borussia, both in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Bundesliga" title="2012–13 Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>, leading to more questions and criticism for Mihajlović in the Serbian press over continually leaving the player on the bench in national team matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Asked about it explicitly in interviews, Mihajlović said, "Subotić missed the final friendly versus Ireland because of injury. He was supposed to start against Scotland, but out of the five training sessions we held leading up to the match, he did very poorly at two of them. I decided to give Biševac a chance in the match at <a href="/wiki/Hampden_Park" title="Hampden Park">Hampden Park</a> and he was excellent. Then, before the Belgium match, I said a had two question marks over the starting lineup and one of them was if I should start Subotić. Now Biševac picked up a second yellow card of the qualifying, and Neven will start the next match in March versus <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a>, if he shows commitment in training, of course. It is now up to him to make that spot his again. Second part of the problem is that both Subotić and Nastasić aren't very vocal during matches, Biševac is the only one who talks and commands the back line. Neven is a very important player for us, and of course I'm counting on him, but these things happen – I had it happen to me as a player on occasion that a spot competitor on the squad makes the most of an opportunity coach gave him thus relegating me temporarily to the bench to wait for my next opportunity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Euro_2016_qualifying">Euro 2016 qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Euro 2016 qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Subotić did not play in qualification matches for Euro 2016. After new Serbian coach <a href="/wiki/Dick_Advocaat" title="Dick Advocaat">Dick Advocaat</a> announced his squad for qualifying rounds against Armenia and Albania, he also announced that Subotić may have finished his national career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
