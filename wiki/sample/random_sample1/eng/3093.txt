<p>The <b>University of Nebraska</b> system is the public <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> system in the state of <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>, United States. Founded in 1869 with one campus in <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_Nebraska" title="Lincoln, Nebraska">Lincoln</a>, the system now has four university campuses and operates a two-year technical agriculture college.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/University_of_Nebraska%E2%80%93Lincoln" title="University of Nebraska–Lincoln">University of Nebraska–Lincoln</a>, which is the state's flagship, land-grant, Carnegie Research I/Research-Extensive university. Founded in 1869 as the University of Nebraska.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/University_of_Nebraska_Omaha" title="University of Nebraska Omaha">University of Nebraska Omaha</a>, which is the state's public urban university. Founded in 1908 as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omaha_University" title="Omaha University">Omaha University</a>, the school joined the University of Nebraska system in 1968.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/University_of_Nebraska_at_Kearney" title="University of Nebraska at Kearney">University of Nebraska at Kearney</a>, which is the state's university especially focused on undergraduates, and in a smaller setting. Founded in 1905 as the Nebraska State <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Normal_School" title="Normal School">Normal School</a> at Kearney, the school joined the University of Nebraska system in 1991.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/University_of_Nebraska_Medical_Center" title="University of Nebraska Medical Center">University of Nebraska Medical Center</a> (located in <a href="/wiki/Omaha,_Nebraska" title="Omaha, Nebraska">Omaha</a>). The medical school was founded in 1880, and it became a part of the University of Nebraska system in 1902.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nebraska_College_of_Technical_Agriculture" title="Nebraska College of Technical Agriculture">Nebraska College of Technical Agriculture</a> (located in <a href="/wiki/Curtis,_Nebraska" title="Curtis, Nebraska">Curtis, Nebraska</a>), founded in 1965, is a two-year degree granting institution managed by the University of Nebraska pursuant to a 1994 agreement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nebraska_Institutes">Nebraska Institutes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Nebraska_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nebraska Institutes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The University of Nebraska has four interdisciplinary, University-wide institutes operating across the University of Nebraska system.</p>
<ul>
<li>Buffett Early Childhood Institute</li>
<li>National Strategic Research Institute</li>
<li>Robert B. Daugherty Water for Food Institute</li>
<li>Rural Futures Institute</li>
</ul>
<p>Two of NU's campuses also partner in the <a href="/wiki/Peter_Kiewit_Institute" title="Peter Kiewit Institute">Peter Kiewit Institute</a>, a facility in Omaha, Nebraska that houses academic programs from both the University of Nebraska-Lincoln's College of Engineering and the University of Nebraska at Omaha's College of Information Science and Technology.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="NU_Online_Education">NU Online Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Nebraska_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: NU Online Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Online Worldwide is the virtual connection point to more than 100 online degrees, certificates, endorsements, and minors offered by the four campuses of the University of Nebraska system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>University of Nebraska High School is an accredited, university-based online high school. The online college preparatory curriculum allows students to earn high school credit or a diploma from anywhere around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Nebraska_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 11, 1857, a group of citizens received permission from the <a href="/wiki/Nebraska_Territorial_Legislature" title="Nebraska Territorial Legislature">Nebraska Territorial Legislature</a> to found the University of Nebraska at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saratoga,_Nebraska" title="Saratoga, Nebraska">Saratoga, Nebraska</a>. However, when they did not complete the task of meeting in Saratoga and establishing a campus within one year they lost their permission to charter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Today's University of Nebraska was founded in 1869 in <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_Nebraska" title="Lincoln, Nebraska">Lincoln, Nebraska</a>. For the first 99 years of its existence, the University was synonymous with the Lincoln campus. In 1902, Omaha Medical College became part of the University of Nebraska system. The University reorganized into its present form in 1968 when it took control of the municipal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Omaha" title="University of Omaha">University of Omaha</a>, which became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Nebraska_at_Omaha" title="University of Nebraska at Omaha">University of Nebraska at Omaha</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kearney_State_College" title="Kearney State College">Kearney State College</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nebraska_at_Kearney" title="University of Nebraska at Kearney">University of Nebraska at Kearney</a>, became the fourth member of the University of Nebraska system in 1991.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Nebraska_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Effective April 13, 2015, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Hank_M._Bounds" title="Hank M. Bounds">Hank M. Bounds</a> was appointed the 7th president of the University of Nebraska system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> President Bounds was previously the commissioner of higher education for the state of Mississippi.</p>
<p>The Board of Regents consists of eight voting members elected by district for six-year terms, and four non-voting student Regents, one from each campus, who serve during their tenure as student body president. The board supervises the general operations of the university, and the control and direction of all expenditures.</p>
<p><i>Members of the Board of Regents are</i>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Tim Clare: District 1 in the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_Nebraska" title="Lincoln, Nebraska">Lincoln</a> area</li>
<li>Howard Hawks: District 2 in greater <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omaha" title="Omaha">Omaha</a></li>
<li>Jim Pillen: District 3 in northeastern Nebraska</li>
<li>Robert Whitehouse: District 4 in the Omaha area</li>
<li>Robert Schafer: District 5 in southeast Nebraska, including part of Lincoln</li>
<li>Kent Schroeder District 6 in central Nebraska,</li>
