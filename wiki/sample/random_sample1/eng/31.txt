<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1041&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1041">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1041&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1041">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zirid" title="Zirid">Zirid</a> dynasty rejects <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shi%27ite" title="Shi'ite">Shi'ite</a> obedience and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatimid" title="Fatimid">Fatimid</a> domination and recognizes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbasids" title="Abbasids">Abbasids</a> as their overlords.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1041&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1041">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_17" title="March 17">March 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Olivento" title="Battle of Olivento">Battle of Olivento</a> is fought near the river Olivento in <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">Apulia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Catepanate_of_Italy" title="Catepanate of Italy">Catepanate of Italy</a>. <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Normans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a> led by <a href="/wiki/William_Iron_Arm" title="William Iron Arm">William Iron Arm</a> are victorious against the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>'s army led by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dokeianos" title="Michael Dokeianos">Michael Dokeianos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catepanate_of_Italy#Catepans" title="Catepanate of Italy">Catepan of Italy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Harald_Hardrada" title="Harald Hardrada">Harald Hardrada</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_4" title="May 4">May 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Montemaggiore" title="Battle of Montemaggiore">Battle of Montemaggiore</a> is fought by the river <a href="/wiki/Ofanto" title="Ofanto">Ofanto</a> near <a href="/wiki/Cannae" title="Cannae">Cannae</a> in Apulia in the Catepanate of Italy. Normans and Lombards led by William Iron Arm are again victorious against the Byzantine forces led by Michael Dokeianos, Catepan of Italy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_10" title="December 10">December 10</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zoe_Porphyrogenita" title="Zoe Porphyrogenita">Zoe Porphyrogenita</a>, Empress Consort of Byzantium, elevates her adoptive son to the throne of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> as <a href="/wiki/Michael_V_Kalaphates" title="Michael V Kalaphates">Michael V Kalaphates</a> on the death of her husband <a href="/wiki/Michael_IV_the_Paphlagonian" title="Michael IV the Paphlagonian">Michael IV the Paphlagonian</a>.</li>
<li>The inland city of <a href="/wiki/Worcester" title="Worcester">Worcester</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a> rebels against the naval taxes of King <a href="/wiki/Harthacnut" title="Harthacnut">Harthacnut</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Edward_the_Confessor" title="Edward the Confessor">Edward the Confessor</a> returns from exile in <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Normandy" title="Duchy of Normandy">Normandy</a> to become the heir of his half-brother <a href="/wiki/Harthacnut" title="Harthacnut">Harthacnut</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_England" title="King of England">King of England</a>. He reduces the Navy from 60 to 32 ships this year due to the tax burden.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1041&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="1041">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The number of enlisted soldiers in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" title="Song Dynasty">Song Dynasty</a> Chinese military reaches well over 1,250,000 troops, an increase since <a href="/wiki/1022" title="1022">1022</a> when there were a million soldiers.</li>
</ul>

<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnus_I_of_Norway" title="Magnus I of Norway">Magnus I of Norway</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Danish_monarchs" title="List of Danish monarchs">King of Denmark</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Normans</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> move their capital to <a href="/wiki/Melfi" title="Melfi">Melfi</a>.</li>

<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Almoravids" title="Almoravids">Almoravids</a>, led by Abdullah Ibn Yasin, invade <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine-Arab_Wars" title="Byzantine-Arab Wars">Byzantine-Arab Wars</a>: The Byzantines conquer Edessa, returning to Christian hands after 400 years of Islamic rule.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1044">1044</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/1044" title="1044">1044</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1044&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1044&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<ul>
<li>September – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Pope Benedict IX</a> is forced out of office and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sylvester_III" title="Sylvester III">Sylvester III</a> elected to succeed him.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Anawrahta" title="Anawrahta">Anawrahta</a> seizes the throne of <a href="/wiki/Bagan" title="Bagan">Pagan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> military treatise of the <i><a href="/wiki/Wujing_Zongyao" title="Wujing Zongyao">Wujing Zongyao</a></i> is written and compiled by scholars Zeng Gongliang (曾公亮), Ding Du (丁度), and Yang Weide (楊惟德) during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" title="Song Dynasty">Song Dynasty</a>. It is the first book in history to include formulas for <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder" title="Gunpowder">gunpowder</a> and its use for various bombs (thrown by sling or <a href="/wiki/Trebuchet" title="Trebuchet">trebuchet</a> catapult). It also describes the double-<a href="/wiki/Piston" title="Piston">piston</a> pump <a href="/wiki/Flamethrower" title="Flamethrower">flamethrower</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Thermoremanent_magnetization" title="Thermoremanent magnetization">thermoremanence</a> compass, a few decades before <a href="/wiki/Shen_Kuo" title="Shen Kuo">Shen Kuo</a> wrote of the first known <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic" title="Magnetic">magnetic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mariner" title="Mariner">mariners</a> <a href="/wiki/Compass" title="Compass">compass</a>. Although emphasizing the importance of many weapons, it reserves high respect for the <a href="/wiki/Crossbow" title="Crossbow">crossbow</a> and ability of crossbowmen to fell charging units of <a href="/wiki/Nomad" title="Nomad">nomadic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">cavalrymen</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1045">1045</span></h3>

<ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/Edward_the_Confessor" title="Edward the Confessor">Edward the Confessor</a> marries <a href="/wiki/Edith_of_Wessex" title="Edith of Wessex">Edith of Wessex</a> and begins construction of <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_20" title="January 20">January 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Sylvester_III" title="Pope Sylvester III">Pope Sylvester III</a> becomes the 146th pope, succeeding <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Benedict IX</a>, who abdicated during the previous year.</li>
<li>April – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Sylvester_III" title="Pope Sylvester III">Pope Sylvester III</a> is deposed (election deemed invalid); <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Pope Benedict IX</a> is elected once more, becoming the 147th pope.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_5" title="May 5">May 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_VI" title="Pope Gregory VI">Pope Gregory VI</a> becomes the 148th pope, following the resignation of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Pope Benedict IX</a> in exchange for money. There are growing allegations that <a href="/wiki/Simony" title="Simony">simony</a> takes place during Gregory VI's reign.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Movable_type" title="Movable type">Movable type</a> <a href="/wiki/Printing" title="Printing">printing</a> is invented by <a href="/wiki/Bi_Sheng" title="Bi Sheng">Bi Sheng</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Lingxiao_Pagoda" title="Lingxiao Pagoda">Lingxiao Pagoda</a> of China is built during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" title="Song Dynasty">Song Dynasty</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Qingli_Reforms" title="Qingli Reforms">Qingli Reforms</a>, put forth by the Chinese statesmen <a href="/wiki/Ouyang_Xiu" title="Ouyang Xiu">Ouyang Xiu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fan_Zhongyan" title="Fan Zhongyan">Fan Zhongyan</a> in <a href="/wiki/1043" title="1043">1043</a>, are halted by their conservative ministerial peers, but would later influence reform efforts under <a href="/wiki/Wang_Anshi" title="Wang Anshi">Wang Anshi</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1046">1046</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/1046" title="1046">1046</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1046&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1046&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_5" title="March 5">March 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Nasir_Khusraw" title="Nasir Khusraw">Nasir Khusraw</a> begins the seven-year <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle Eastern</a> journey which he later describes in his book <i>Safarnameh</i>.</li>
<li>July – Ex-<a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Pope Benedict IX</a> gives up a renewed attempt to reclaim the papal throne.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_20" title="December 20">December 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_VI" title="Pope Gregory VI">Pope Gregory VI</a> is accused of <a href="/wiki/Simony" title="Simony">simony</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Sutri" title="Council of Sutri">Council of Sutri</a>, and abdicates as pope.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_25" title="December 25">December 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_II" title="Pope Clement II">Pope Clement II</a> succeeds <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_VI" title="Pope Gregory VI">Pope Gregory VI</a> as the 149th <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a> and crowns <a href="/wiki/Henry_III,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Henry III, Holy Roman Emperor">Henry III</a> as <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a>.</li>
<li>The first contact occurs between the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuks" title="Seljuks">Seljuks</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vatha" title="Vatha">Vatha</a> Pagan Revolt in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Hungary</a>: <a href="/wiki/Gerard_of_Csan%C3%A1d" title="Gerard of Csanád">Gerard of Csanád</a> is martyred in <a href="/wiki/Buda" title="Buda">Buda</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Munjong_of_Goryeo" title="Munjong of Goryeo">Munjong</a> is crowned 11th King of <a href="/wiki/Goryeo" title="Goryeo">Goryeo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>).</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ealdred_(bishop)" title="Ealdred (bishop)">Ealdred</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Worcester" title="Bishop of Worcester">Bishop of Worcester</a>, leads troops from <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a> on an unsuccessful punitive raid against <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a> leaders <a href="/wiki/Gruffydd_ap_Rhydderch" title="Gruffydd ap Rhydderch">Gruffydd ap Rhydderch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhys_ap_Rhydderch" title="Rhys ap Rhydderch">Rhys ap Rhydderch</a> and Gruffydd ap Llywelyn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>The original <a href="/wiki/Hildesheim_Cathedral" title="Hildesheim Cathedral">Hildesheim Cathedral</a> is largely destroyed by fire.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" title="Song Dynasty">Song Dynasty</a> <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> judge and magistrate <a href="/wiki/Bao_Zheng" title="Bao Zheng">Bao Zheng</a> (Bao Qingtian) writes a <a href="/wiki/Memorial_to_the_throne" title="Memorial to the throne">memorial to the throne</a>, warning about governmental corruption and a foreseeable <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a> of the Chinese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iron_industry" title="Iron industry">iron industry</a>, if increasingly poorer families continued to be listed on the register for iron-smelting households (while more rich households avoid being listed for fear of financial calamity). Apparently the government heeds the warning, and produces more <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> product by the year <a href="/wiki/1078" title="1078">1078</a> than China ever had before.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1048&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="1048">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_16" title="July 16">July 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Pope Benedict IX</a> is driven from <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> by German troops, ending his third and final <a href="/wiki/Pontificate" title="Pontificate">pontificate</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_17" title="July 17">July 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Damasus_II" title="Pope Damasus II">Pope Damasus II</a> succeeds <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Benedict IX</a> as the 151st <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a>.</li>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place_3">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1049&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1049">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa_2">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1049&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1049">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>Beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Banu_Hilal" title="Banu Hilal">Banu Hilal</a> invasions in the Maghrib. They are organized by the Egyptian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatimid" title="Fatimid">Fatimid</a> dynasty to punish their former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zirid" title="Zirid">Zirid</a> vassals.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia_3">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1049&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1049">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>A truce is declared between the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_Turks" title="Seljuk Turks">Seljuk Turks</a>.</li>
<li>Construction of the large <a href="/wiki/Iron_Pagoda" title="Iron Pagoda">Iron Pagoda</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kaifeng" title="Kaifeng">Kaifeng</a>, China, is completed during this year of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" title="Song Dynasty">Song Dynasty</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_3">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1049&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="1049">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Pisa" title="Republic of Pisa">Republic of Pisa</a> successfully completes the conquest of <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Andalusian</a> occupiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/History_of_Ireland_(800%E2%80%931169)" title="History of Ireland (800–1169)">Viking Irish</a> raiders ally with <a href="/wiki/Gruffydd_ap_Rhydderch" title="Gruffydd ap Rhydderch">Gruffydd ap Rhydderch</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Gwent" title="Kingdom of Gwent">Gwent</a> in raiding along the <a href="/wiki/River_Usk" title="River Usk">River Usk</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ealdred_(bishop)" title="Ealdred (bishop)">Ealdred</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Worcester" title="Bishop of Worcester">Bishop of Worcester</a>, is unsuccessful in driving them off.</li>
</ul>
