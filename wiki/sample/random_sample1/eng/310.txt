<p>The <b>1996 CONCACAF Gold Cup</b> was the third edition of the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="CONCACAF Gold Cup">Gold Cup</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football (soccer)</a> championship of North America, Central America and the Caribbean (<a href="/wiki/CONCACAF" title="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</a>).</p>
<p>The tournament went back to the United States and <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>; the games were hosted by <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaheim" title="Anaheim">Anaheim</a>. The format of the tournament changed from <a href="/wiki/1993_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="1993 CONCACAF Gold Cup">1993</a>: it was expanded to nine teams, separated into three groups of three and played in January as opposed to the 1993 edition which was played in July.</p>
<p>The top team in each group, plus the best second-place finisher would advance to the semifinals. For the first time, a non-CONCACAF team was invited: <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, who sent their Under-23 side. <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a> won their second straight Gold Cup, beating the Brazilians 2-0 in the final.</p>
