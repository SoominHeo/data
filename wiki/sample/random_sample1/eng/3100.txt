<p>The <b>Neoplan Skyliner</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Double-decker_bus" title="Double-decker bus">double-deck</a> <a href="/wiki/Multi-axle_bus" title="Multi-axle bus">multi-axle</a> luxury touring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coach_(vehicle)" title="Coach (vehicle)">coach</a> built by <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Bus_manufacturing" title="Bus manufacturing">coach manufacturer</a> <a href="/wiki/Neoplan" title="Neoplan">Neoplan</a>. It was introduced in 1964.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neoplan_Skyliner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In 1964, the founder's second son, Konrad Auwärter, developed a <a href="/wiki/Double-decker_bus" title="Double-decker bus">double-deck design</a> for a service bus as part of his dissertation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan61-64_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan61-64-5">[5]</a></sup> The 'Do-Bus' design had extremely low weight, and could carry over 100 passengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan61-64_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan61-64-5">[5]</a></sup> It also featured a low-frame front <a href="/wiki/Axle" title="Axle">axle</a> with forward-mounted <a href="/wiki/Steering" title="Steering">steering</a> gear that permitted a low flat floor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan61-64_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan61-64-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NeoplanHistory_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NeoplanHistory-6">[6]</a></sup> The double-deck principle was applied to coach design, creating a revolutionary high-capacity comfortable vehicle for touring. This vehicle was known as the Skyliner,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan65-71_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan65-71-7">[7]</a></sup> and created an image for the company that differentiated the Neoplan brand from its competitors, and created interest in its products across Europe.</p>
<p>A second manufacturing facility opened in <a href="/wiki/Pilsting" title="Pilsting">Pilsting</a> in 1973,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NeoplanLocations_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-NeoplanLocations-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan72-75_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan72-75-9">[9]</a></sup> and a third opening in <a href="/wiki/Kumasi" title="Kumasi">Kumasi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a> in December 1974<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NeoplanLocations_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-NeoplanLocations-8">[8]</a></sup> to support a large order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan72-75_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan72-75-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The double-deck Skyliner concept was taken to its ultimate conclusion in 1975, with the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Neoplan_Jumbocruiser" title="Neoplan Jumbocruiser">Jumbocruiser</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan72-75_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan72-75-9">[9]</a></sup> a double-deck articulated coach 18 metres (59 ft 1 in) long<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NeoplanHistory_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-NeoplanHistory-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan72-75_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan72-75-9">[9]</a></sup> and 4 metres (13 ft 1 in) high.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan72-75_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan72-75-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Neoplan_Spaceliner&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Neoplan Spaceliner (page does not exist)">Spaceliner</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan76-81_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan76-81-10">[10]</a></sup> introduced in 1979 took the Cityliner concept of passenger floor level above the driver, and extended the actual floor above the cab. This kept the height of the vehicle lower than a double-decker, at 3.65 metres (12 ft 0 in), but still allowed installation of toilets, kitchens or sleep cabins below the passenger compartment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neoplan76-81_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Neoplan76-81-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>SCRTD of Los Angeles, operated Neoplan Skyliners during the 1980s and early 1990s. Antelope Valley Transit Authority operated Skyliners on their Lancaster-Los Angeles route during the late 1990s early 2000s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UK">UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neoplan_Skyliner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Neoplan Skyliner also gained popularity in the United Kingdom. <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Group" title="Stagecoach Group">Stagecoach</a> bought some Skyliners during the 1980s to facilitate its new coaching division developed during the company's early years. In 2004, it bought 25 more N122/3L Skyliners for its modern coaching divisions the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_to_London_coach_route" title="Oxford to London coach route">Oxford Tube</a> and <a href="/wiki/Megabus_(Europe)" title="Megabus (Europe)">Megabus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-broch2005_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-broch2005-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Trathens_Travel_Services" title="Trathens Travel Services">Trathens Travel Services</a> of <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> also bought some Skyliners in 2005 for its <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> contracts. However, many of these were displaced following the accident with a National Express Skyliner (see below) - when some <a href="/wiki/Plaxton_Panther" title="Plaxton Panther">Plaxton Panthers</a> were bought in 2007.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications_.282009.29">Specifications (2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neoplan_Skyliner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Specifications (2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Neoplan Skyliner has undergone a process of evolution in specification since its 1964 introduction. As of 2009<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Neoplan_Skyliner&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the double-deck Skyliner is available in two lengths: the Skyliner C and the Skyliner L.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup> The shorter C version used to be 12.00 <a href="/wiki/Metre" title="Metre">metres</a> (39 <a href="/wiki/Foot_(unit)" title="Foot (unit)">ft</a> 4 <a href="/wiki/Inch" title="Inch">in</a>) long, but is now 12.44 metres (40 ft 10 in); and the Skyliner L is 13.79 metres (45 ft 3 in) long, and includes a correspondingly longer <a href="/wiki/Wheelbase" title="Wheelbase">wheelbase</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup> The Skyliner C is designed to seat 69 passengers plus two crew (driver and steward), whereas the Skyliner L seats 77 plus two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/Powertrain" title="Powertrain">powertrain</a> detail, current Skyliners use an MAN <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel engine</a> sourced from <a href="/wiki/Neoplan" title="Neoplan">Neoplan</a> parent company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MAN_Nutzfahrzeuge" title="MAN Nutzfahrzeuge">MAN Nutzfahrzeuge AG</a>. This Euro4 rated <a href="/wiki/Diesel_fuel" title="Diesel fuel">diesel fueled</a> <a href="/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine" title="Internal combustion engine">internal combustion engine</a> is the 12.5 litre MAN D2676 LOH 02 <a href="/wiki/Straight-six_engine" title="Straight-six engine">straight-six</a> (R6) <a href="/wiki/Common_rail" title="Common rail">common rail</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turbodiesel" title="Turbodiesel">turbodiesel</a> with <a href="/wiki/Intercooler" title="Intercooler">intercooler</a>, which <a href="/wiki/Engine_displacement" title="Engine displacement">displaces</a> 12,412 <a href="/wiki/Cubic_centimetre" title="Cubic centimetre">cubic centimetres</a> (757.4 <a href="/wiki/Cubic_inch" title="Cubic inch">cu in</a>), and utilises <a href="/wiki/Multi-valve" title="Multi-valve">four valves</a> per <a href="/wiki/Cylinder_(engine)" title="Cylinder (engine)">cylinder</a> and cylinder-<a href="/wiki/Fuel_injection#Direct_injection" title="Fuel injection">direct fuel injection</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-overview_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-engines-overview-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-pic_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-engines-pic-4">[4]</a></sup> It uses a <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bosch_GmbH" title="Robert Bosch GmbH">Bosch</a> EDC7 electronic <a href="/wiki/Engine_control_unit" title="Engine control unit">engine control unit</a>, and also uses a 'PM-KAT' <a href="/wiki/Catalytic_converter" title="Catalytic converter">catalytic converter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-overview_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-engines-overview-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-pic_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-engines-pic-4">[4]</a></sup> It produces a <a href="/wiki/Deutsches_Institut_f%C3%BCr_Normung" title="Deutsches Institut für Normung">DIN</a>-rated <a href="/wiki/Motive_power" title="Motive power">motive</a> <a href="/wiki/Power_(physics)" title="Power (physics)">power</a> output of 353 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kilowatt" title="Kilowatt">kilowatts</a> (480 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metric_horsepower" title="Metric horsepower">PS</a>; 473 <a href="/wiki/Horsepower" title="Horsepower">hp</a>) at 1,900 <a href="/wiki/Revolutions_per_minute" title="Revolutions per minute">revolutions per minute</a> (rpm), and generates a <a href="/wiki/Torque" title="Torque">torque</a> turning force of 2,300 <a href="/wiki/Newton_metre" title="Newton metre">newton metres</a> (1,696 <a href="/wiki/Pound-foot_(torque)" title="Pound-foot (torque)">lbf·ft</a>) at 1,050-1,400 rpm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-overview_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-engines-overview-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-pic_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-engines-pic-4">[4]</a></sup> The engine is mounted upright in a <a href="/wiki/Longitudinal_engine" title="Longitudinal engine">longitudinal</a> orientation at the <a href="/wiki/Rear-engine_design" title="Rear-engine design">rear</a> of the coach,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-overview_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-engines-overview-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engines-pic_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-engines-pic-4">[4]</a></sup> and transmits its output to the rear <a href="/wiki/Wheel" title="Wheel">roadwheels</a> via a twelve-speed TipMatic <a href="/wiki/ZF_Friedrichshafen" title="ZF Friedrichshafen">ZF</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_ZF_transmissions#12-speed_AMT" title="List of ZF transmissions">AS Tronic</a> <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_transmission" title="Semi-automatic transmission">automated manual transmission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>An interesting design on the Skyliner is its 'tag' third <a href="/wiki/Axle" title="Axle">axle</a>. This is able to articulate, during steering movements - and this helps reduce <a href="/wiki/Tire" title="Tire">tyre</a> wear,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup> which can afflict dual rear axle vehicles. It features <a href="/wiki/Disc_brake" title="Disc brake">disc brakes</a> all round, and utilises <a href="/wiki/Independent_suspension" title="Independent suspension">independent suspension</a> on the front and trailing third axles, whilst the driven axle is a solid version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Standard safety features include '<a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_braking_system" title="Anti-lock braking system">electronically regulated braking system</a>' (EBS) with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brake_Assist" title="Brake Assist">braking assistant</a> (BA) and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_stability_control" title="Electronic stability control">Electronic Stability Programme</a> (ESP).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup> It also features a <a href="/wiki/Governor_(device)#Automotive_industry" title="Governor (device)">speed-limiting device</a> called 'maximum speed control' (MSC), and is fitted with an <a href="/wiki/Event_data_recorder" title="Event data recorder">accident data recorder</a> (ADR).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup> An optional feature is the '<a href="/wiki/Lane_departure_warning_system" title="Lane departure warning system">lane guard system</a>' (LGS), which will vibrate the drivers seat should the coach drift out of its lane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Its <a href="/wiki/Gross_vehicle_weight_rating" title="Gross vehicle weight rating">maximum permissible weight</a> is 26,000 kilograms (57,320 lb), and can have a total luggage volume of up to 7.9 <a href="/wiki/Cubic_metre" title="Cubic metre">cubic metres</a> (279 <a href="/wiki/Cubic_foot" title="Cubic foot">cu ft</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skyliner_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-Skyliner-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents">Accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neoplan_Skyliner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2003 near Nažidla in <a href="/wiki/South_Bohemian_Region" title="South Bohemian Region">South Bohemian Region</a> a Neoplan Skyliner overturned on <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> <a href="/wiki/European_route_E55" title="European route E55">European route E55</a>, killing nineteen people and one man died two years later due to the injuries sustained in the crash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Thirty-four people were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It has been the biggest bus accident in history of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2003 in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> a Neoplan Skyliner overturned on a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> highway, killing twenty-eight people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> A report by French investigators recommended that drivers of double-deck coaches should receive special training because of the vehicle's relative instability. A separate study, presented to the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe in the same year reported that high-sided coaches were much more likely to overturn in crashes than standard single-deck coaches. The German manufacturer added a safety system, known as electronic stability control, which brakes each wheel separately to prevent overturning in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unstable_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-unstable-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2007 a Neoplan Skyliner operated by <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> overturned and crashed on the <a href="/wiki/M25_motorway" title="M25 motorway">M25 motorway</a> near <a href="/wiki/Heathrow_Airport" title="Heathrow Airport">Heathrow Airport</a>, killing two passengers;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> a third passenger died six-months later due to the injuries sustained in the crash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The company temporarily withdrew all twelve Skyliners from service pending investigations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The crash was deemed to have been caused by travelling at excessive speed and the driver was charged with three counts of causing death by dangerous driving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He was subsequently jailed for five years and banned from driving for three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC-jail_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-jail-22">[22]</a></sup> No safety issues were found.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The electronic stability control system which had been introduced on new vehicles in 2006 was not used on this vehicle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unstable_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-unstable-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
