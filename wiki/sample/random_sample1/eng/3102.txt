<p><b>Nathan Jones</b> (born 21 August 1969) is an Australian <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Powerlifting" title="Powerlifting">powerlifting</a> champion, <a href="/wiki/Strongman_(strength_athlete)" title="Strongman (strength athlete)">strongman</a> and retired <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a>. He held the <a href="/wiki/WWA_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWA World Heavyweight Championship">WWA World Heavyweight Championship</a> <a href="/wiki/WWA_World_Heavyweight_Championship#Title_history" title="WWA World Heavyweight Championship">once</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_All-Stars" title="World Wrestling All-Stars">World Wrestling All-Stars</a>, and aligned himself with <a href="/wiki/The_Undertaker" title="The Undertaker">The Undertaker</a> in <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Entertainment</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jones was born in Gold Coast, Queensland, Australia. Before his career as a wrestler, Jones was sentenced to 16 years in 1987 at the age of 18 to <a href="/wiki/Boggo_Road_Gaol" title="Boggo Road Gaol">Boggo Road Gaol</a> for eight armed robberies between 1985–1987, two of which occurred in <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>. During the robberies, he became one of Australia's most wanted and ended up serving seven years in a maximum-security prison. In 1994, Jones was given one year work release before being discharged at the age of 25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> While in prison, he was introduced to the sport of <a href="/wiki/Powerlifting" title="Powerlifting">powerlifting</a>. He is also alleged to have begun taking steroids during this time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lists_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-lists-5">[5]</a></sup> Within a short space of time, he became the National Powerlifting Champion of Australia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Strongman_career">Strongman career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Strongman career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon his release, Jones also began competing in <a href="/wiki/Strongman_(strength_athlete)" title="Strongman (strength athlete)">strongman</a> contests and during this time, he was dubbed "The Megaman". As the reigning Australia's Strongest Man, he entered the World Strength Championship at <a href="/wiki/Callander" title="Callander">Callander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> on 29–30 July 1995. He took first place, topping a field that included 1993 <a href="/wiki/World%27s_Strongest_Man" title="World's Strongest Man">World's Strongest Man</a> <a href="/wiki/Gary_Taylor_(strongman)" title="Gary Taylor (strongman)">Gary Taylor</a>. The following weekend, he competed in the <a href="/wiki/World_Muscle_Power_Classic" title="World Muscle Power Classic">World Muscle Power Classic</a> held at Mintlaw, <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire_(historic)" title="Aberdeenshire (historic)">Aberdeenshire</a>, Scotland. That contest was won by <a href="/wiki/Magn%C3%BAs_Ver_Magn%C3%BAsson" title="Magnús Ver Magnússon">Magnús Ver Magnússon</a>, with Nathan finishing fifth in a field of twelve competitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jones next took part in the 1995 <a href="/wiki/World%27s_Strongest_Man" title="World's Strongest Man">World's Strongest Man</a> contest. After quickly defeating <a href="/wiki/Phil_Martin_(highland_games)" title="Phil Martin (highland games)">Phil Martin</a> twice in the <a href="/wiki/Arm_wrestling" title="Arm wrestling">arm wrestling</a> event in the qualifying heat, Jones was then matched against <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Samuelsson" title="Magnus Samuelsson">Magnus Samuelsson</a>, who had been <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>'s arm wrestling champion for several years and who would become the World's Strongest Man in 1998. Samuelsson won the first round. In the second round Jones refused to go down and pulled with his opposite arm, twisting his body. This resulted in breaking the arm he was using to wrestle (a spiral fracture of the <a href="/wiki/Humerus" title="Humerus">humerus</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and he subsequently was out of the competition. Jones returned to Strongman competition in 1996 winning the <a href="/wiki/World_Strongman_Challenge" title="World Strongman Challenge">World Strongman Challenge</a>, and placed third in a World's Strongest Man qualifying heat behind <a href="/wiki/Magn%C3%BAs_Ver_Magn%C3%BAsson" title="Magnús Ver Magnússon">Magnús Ver Magnússon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jorma_Ojanaho" title="Jorma Ojanaho">Jorma Ojanaho</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixed_martial_arts_career">Mixed martial arts career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mixed martial arts career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He also participated in a <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> match at <a href="/wiki/Pride_Fighting_Championships" title="Pride Fighting Championships">KRS-Pride</a>'s debut event <a href="/wiki/Pride_1" title="Pride 1">Pride 1</a> in October 1997, facing Japanese <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Sumo" title="Sumo">grand sumo champion</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koji_Kitao" title="Koji Kitao">Koji Kitao</a>. Jones began the match taunting him with a nimble spin kick, and went to catch Kitao in a standing <a href="/wiki/Guillotine_choke" title="Guillotine choke">guillotine choke</a>. However, the sumo wrestler caught one of Nathan's knee strikes and took him down, landing in <a href="/wiki/Side_control" title="Side control">side control</a> position. Kitao then worked an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keylocks" title="Keylocks">americana</a> and Jones immediately tapped out.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_All-Stars_.282001.E2.80.932002.29">World Wrestling All-Stars (2001–2002)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: World Wrestling All-Stars (2001–2002)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After working as a <a href="/wiki/Bodyguard" title="Bodyguard">bodyguard</a> for <a href="/wiki/Millionaire" title="Millionaire">multimillionaire</a> <a href="/wiki/Rene_Rivkin" title="Rene Rivkin">Rene Rivkin</a>, Jones began a career in wrestling during this time. Jones first gained fame working in <a href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_All-Stars" title="World Wrestling All-Stars">World Wrestling All-Stars</a>, making a large impression at the first WWA <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> "<a href="/wiki/List_of_WWA_pay-per-view_events#The_Inception" title="List of WWA pay-per-view events">Inception</a>", where he was accompanied to the ring by <a href="/wiki/Rove_McManus" title="Rove McManus">Rove McManus</a>. However he was defeated after Rove was smashed with <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Jarrett" title="Jeff Jarrett">Jeff Jarrett</a>'s guitar and Jones was hit with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Stroke_(wrestling_maneuver)" title="The Stroke (wrestling maneuver)">Stroke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his time in the WWA, Nathan won the <a href="/wiki/WWA_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWA World Heavyweight Championship">WWA World Heavyweight Championship</a> on 7 April 2002 before losing the belt to <a href="/wiki/Scott_Steiner" title="Scott Steiner">Scott Steiner</a> only 5 days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pro_Wrestling_Zero1-Max_.282002.29">Pro Wrestling Zero1-Max (2002)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pro Wrestling Zero1-Max (2002)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the title loss, Jones began performing for <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Zero1" title="Pro Wrestling Zero1">Pro Wrestling Zero1-Max</a> in June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches2-10">[10]</a></sup> On 20 October, Jones and <a href="/wiki/Jon_Heidenreich" title="Jon Heidenreich">Jon Heidenreich</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Masato_Tanaka" title="Masato Tanaka">Masato Tanaka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shinjiro_Otani" title="Shinjiro Otani">Shinjiro Otani</a> to win the <a href="/wiki/NWA_Intercontinental_Tag_Team_Championship" title="NWA Intercontinental Tag Team Championship">NWA Intercontinental Tag Team Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches2_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches2-10">[10]</a></sup> On 25 October, Jones and Heidenreich successfully retained the title against <a href="/wiki/Deuce_(wrestler)" title="Deuce (wrestler)">Jimmy Snuka Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvester_Terkay" title="Sylvester Terkay">The Predator</a> before losing the title the next day to <a href="/wiki/Naoya_Ogawa" title="Naoya Ogawa">Naoya Ogawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shinya_Hashimoto" title="Shinya Hashimoto">Shinya Hashimoto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches2_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches2-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Entertainment_.282002.E2.80.932003.29">World Wrestling Entertainment (2002–2003)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Entertainment (2002–2003)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Jones initially signed his contract with <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Entertainment</a> (WWE), he was unable to work in the United States because of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visa_(document)" title="Visa (document)">visa</a> issues stemming from his criminal history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ceiling_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ceiling-11">[11]</a></sup> In late 2002 and early 2003, Jones began performing in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Dark_match" title="List of professional wrestling terms">dark matches</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">World Wrestling Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches2_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches2-10">[10]</a></sup> He was originally promoted as a "<a href="/wiki/Hannibal_Lecter" title="Hannibal Lecter">Hannibal Lecter</a>" type character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ceiling_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ceiling-11">[11]</a></sup> On the April 10, 2003 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown!</a></i>, Jones made his televised WWE in-ring debut and defeated <a href="/wiki/Bill_DeMott" title="Bill DeMott">Bill DeMott</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon debuting, Jones' original character was nixed, and he was put into a storyline with <a href="/wiki/The_Undertaker" title="The Undertaker">The Undertaker</a> as his protégé and helper during Undertaker's feud with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Bloom" title="Matt Bloom">A-Train</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Wight" title="Paul Wight">The Big Show</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ceiling_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-ceiling-11">[11]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XIX" title="WrestleMania XIX">WrestleMania XIX</a>, Jones and Undertaker were scheduled to face Big Show and A-Train in a tag team match, but at the last minute, the match was made a handicap match instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ceiling_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-ceiling-11">[11]</a></sup> On screen, Jones was attacked pre-match and left injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-1">[1]</a></sup> Near the closing moments of the match, Jones reappeared and attacked Big Show, enabling Undertaker to pin A-Train for the victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-1">[1]</a></sup> Jones was then sent to <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Valley_Wrestling" title="Ohio Valley Wrestling">Ohio Valley Wrestling</a> to improve his skills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ceiling_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-ceiling-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jones was then absent from television for several months until returning in the fall of 2003 as a <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">villain</a> known by the nickname "The Colossus of Boggo Road", a title referring to his time spent in <a href="/wiki/Boggo_Road_Gaol" title="Boggo Road Gaol">Boggo Road Gaol</a>. He was paired with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Heyman" title="Paul Heyman">Paul Heyman</a> on-screen and given the role as Smackdown General Manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ceiling_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-ceiling-11">[11]</a></sup> He was also immediately placed into <a href="/wiki/Brock_Lesnar" title="Brock Lesnar">Brock Lesnar</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series_(2003)" title="Survivor Series (2003)">Survivor Series</a> team, which also included A-Train, Big Show, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_Morgan_(wrestler)" title="Matt Morgan (wrestler)">Matt Morgan</a> in order to compete against Lesnar's rival <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Angle" title="Kurt Angle">Kurt Angle</a> and his team. At Survivor Series, Jones was eliminated by Angle after he submitted to the <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#Ankle_lock" title="Professional wrestling holds">Ankle lock</a>. Team Lesnar was defeated by Team Angle and Jones was then relegated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#R" title="List of professional wrestling terms">interfere</a> in Lesnar's matches along with the other members of Team Lesnar. He was also placed in matches by either Lesnar or Heyman to aid them. On 6 December 2003, Jones quit the company due to the rigor of WWE's heavy travel schedules while on tour in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perth,_Western_Australia" title="Perth, Western Australia">Perth, Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-WWE_and_retirement">Post-WWE and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nathan_Jones_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-WWE and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After leaving WWE, Jones was scheduled to make his pro wrestling return in 2004 at the Australian Wrestling Supershow III, booked against Mark Mercedes. Jones <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No-show_(professional_wrestling)" title="No-show (professional wrestling)">did not appear</a> as advertised and a <a href="/wiki/Battle_royal" title="Battle royal">battle royal</a> was held following an angry <a href="/wiki/Shoot_(professional_wrestling)" title="Shoot (professional wrestling)">shoot</a> promo on Jones by Mercedes and promoter <a href="/wiki/Andy_Raymond" title="Andy Raymond">Andy Raymond</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, he wrestled three matches for <a href="/wiki/World_Series_Wrestling" title="World Series Wrestling">World Series Wrestling</a>. On 5 October, he defeated Lee Star and on 7 October and 8 October, he defeated Mark Hilton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CageMatches_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-CageMatches-9">[9]</a></sup> After his last match with Hilton, Jones immediately retired from wrestling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cage_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Cage-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Jones signed with <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a>, but nerve damage caused by a cement truck hitting his left arm kept him sidelined for the first three months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-market_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-market-3">[3]</a></sup> He never wrestled for the promotion.</p>
