<p>The <b>Nezu Museum</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">根津美術館</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nezu bijutsukan</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, formerly known as the Nezu Institute of Fine Arts, is an art museum in the <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">Minato</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The museum houses the private collection of pre-modern Japanese and East Asian art of <a href="/wiki/Nezu_Kaichir%C5%8D" title="Nezu Kaichirō">Nezu Kaichirō</a> (1860–1940). The museum foundation was established on the death of the founder Nezu in 1940 and exhibitions were first opened to the public in 1941. The museum collection was stored away from central Tokyo during the second world war and escaped the destruction suffered by the estate property in the bombing of May 1945. Exhibitions were restarted after the war in 1946.</p>
<p>Closed due to large-scale renovation and renewal from 2006 onwards, the museum re-opened in fall 2009 with a completely new museum building by the Japanese architect <a href="/wiki/Kengo_Kuma" title="Kengo Kuma">Kengo Kuma</a>.</p>
<p>Included in the collection are a pair of <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> <a href="/wiki/Folding_screen" title="Folding screen">folding screens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Iris_(plant)" title="Iris (plant)">Irises</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ogata_K%C5%8Drin" title="Ogata Kōrin">Ogata Kōrin</a>. It also includes other paintings of renown, <a href="/wiki/Calligraphy" title="Calligraphy">calligraphy</a>, sculpture, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceramics_(art)" title="Ceramics (art)">ceramics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Textiles" title="Textiles">textiles</a> and archaeological materials, as well as objects in lacquer, metal, and wood. The collection also consists of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_bronzes" title="Chinese bronzes">Chinese bronzes</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shang" title="Shang">Shang</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhou_Dynasty" title="Zhou Dynasty">Zhou</a> dynasties. The Nezu Museum is also known for its extensive, Japanese-style garden.</p>
