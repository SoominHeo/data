<p><b>Necrophilia</b>, also called <b>thanatophilia</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Sexual_attraction" title="Sexual attraction">sexual attraction</a> or sexual act involving <a href="/wiki/Cadaver" title="Cadaver">corpses</a>. The attraction is classified as a <a href="/wiki/Paraphilia" title="Paraphilia">paraphilia</a> by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diagnostic_and_Statistical_Manual" title="Diagnostic and Statistical Manual">Diagnostic and Statistical Manual</a></i> (DSM) of the <a href="/wiki/American_Psychiatric_Association" title="American Psychiatric Association">American Psychiatric Association</a>. The term was coined by the Belgian <a href="/wiki/Alienist" title="Alienist">alienist</a> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Guislain" title="Joseph Guislain">Joseph Guislain</a>, who first used it in a lecture in 1850. It derives from the Greek words <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BD%CE%B5%CE%BA%CF%81%CF%8C%CF%82" title="wikt:νεκρός">νεκρός</a></i> (<i>nekros</i>; "dead") and <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%86%CE%B9%CE%BB%CE%AF%CE%B1" title="wikt:φιλία">φιλία</a></i> (<i>philia</i>; "love").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goodwin_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-goodwin-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rosman and Resnick (1989) reviewed information from 34 cases of necrophilia describing the individuals' motivations for their behaviors: these individuals reported the desire to possess a non-resisting and non-rejecting partner (68%), reunions with a romantic partner (21%), sexual attraction to corpses (15%), comfort or overcoming feelings of isolation (15%), or seeking self-esteem by expressing power over a homicide victim (12%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosman_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rosman-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Singular accounts of necrophilia in history are sporadic, though written records suggest the practice was present within <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a>. <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a> writes in <a href="/wiki/Histories_(Herodotus)" title="Histories (Herodotus)"><i>The Histories</i></a> that, to discourage intercourse with a corpse, ancient <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptians</a> left deceased beautiful women to decay for "three or four days" before giving them to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embalmers" title="Embalmers">embalmers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-klaf_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-klaf-5">[5]</a></sup> Herodotus also alluded to suggestions that Greek tyrant <a href="/wiki/Periander" title="Periander">Periander</a> had defiled the corpse of his wife, employing a metaphor: "Periander baked his bread in a cold oven."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Acts of necrophilia are depicted on ceramics from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moche_(culture)" title="Moche (culture)">Moche</a> culture, which reigned in northern <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> from the first to eighth century CE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A common theme in these artifacts is the masturbation of a male skeleton by a living woman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hittites" title="Hittites">Hittite</a> law from the 16th century BC through to the 13th century BC explicitly permitted sex with the dead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Around 1850, Belgian physician <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Guislain" title="Joseph Guislain">Joseph Guislain</a> coined the word <i>nécrophiles</i> in a lecture about mental illness, with reference to infamous contemporary necrophile <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Bertrand" title="François Bertrand">François Bertrand</a>. The term was popularized about a decade later by psychiatrist <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9n%C3%A9dict_Morel" title="Bénédict Morel">Bénédict Morel</a>, who also discussed Bertrand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goodwin_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-goodwin-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Richard_von_Krafft-Ebing" title="Richard von Krafft-Ebing">Richard von Krafft-Ebing</a> included necrophilia in his 1886 <i>Psychopathia Sexualis</i>. Krafft-Ebing based his conclusions on the cases of Bertrand and <a href="/wiki/Victor_Ardisson" title="Victor Ardisson">Victor Ardisson</a>, and suggested that Bertrand's necrophilia was caused by congenital feeble-mindedness and early <a href="/wiki/Masturbation" title="Masturbation">masturbation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a modern example, <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Dahmer" title="Jeffrey Dahmer">Jeffrey Dahmer</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">serial killer</a> who suffered from necrophilia. In order to be aroused, he had to murder his victims before performing sexual intercourse with them. Dahmer stated that he only killed his victims because they wanted to leave after having sex, and would be angry with him for drugging them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> British serial killer <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Nilsen" title="Dennis Nilsen">Dennis Nilsen</a> is also considered to have been a necrophiliac.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Diagnostic_and_Statistical_Manual_of_Mental_Disorders" title="Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders">Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders</a>, fifth edition (<a href="/wiki/DSM-5" title="DSM-5">DSM-5</a>), recurrent, intense sexual interest in corpses can be diagnosed under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Other_Specified_Paraphilic_Disorder" title="Other Specified Paraphilic Disorder">Other Specified Paraphilic Disorder</a> (necrophilia) when it causes marked distress or impairment in important areas of functioning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DSM-5_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-DSM-5-13">[13]</a></sup> A ten-tier classification of necrophilia exists:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aggrawal_classification_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-aggrawal_classification-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li>Role players: People who get aroused from <a href="/wiki/Sexual_roleplay" title="Sexual roleplay">pretending</a> their live partner is dead during sexual activity.</li>
<li>Romantic necrophiliacs: Bereaved people who remain attached to their dead lover's body.</li>
<li>Necrophilic fantasizers: People who fantasize about necrophilia, but never actually have sex with a corpse.</li>
<li>Tactile necrophiliacs: People who are aroused by touching or stroking a corpse, without engaging in intercourse.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sexual_fetishism" title="Sexual fetishism">Fetishistic</a> necrophiliacs: People who remove objects (e.g., panties or a tampon) or body parts (e.g., a finger or genitalia) from a corpse for sexual purposes, without engaging in intercourse.</li>
<li>Necromutilomaniacs: People who derive pleasure from mutilating a corpse while masturbating, without engaging in intercourse.</li>
<li>Opportunistic necrophiliacs: People who normally have no interest in necrophilia, but take the opportunity when it arises.</li>
<li>Regular necrophiliacs: People who preferentially have intercourse with the dead.</li>
<li>Homicidal necrophiliacs: People who commit <a href="/wiki/Lust_murder" title="Lust murder">murder</a> in order to have sex with the dead.</li>
<li>Exclusive necrophiliacs: People who have an exclusive interest in sex with the dead, and cannot perform at all for living partners.</li>
</ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Humans">Humans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Humans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Necrophilia is often assumed to be rare, but no data for its prevalence in the general population exists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Some necrophiles only fantasize about the act, without carrying it out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosman_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rosman-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1958, Klaf and Brown commented that, although rarely described, necrophiliac <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_(psychology)" title="Fantasy (psychology)">fantasies</a> may occur more often than is generally supposed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-klaf_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-klaf-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rosman and Resnick (1989) reviewed 122 cases of necrophilia. The sample was divided into genuine necrophiles, who had a persistent attraction to corpses, and pseudo-necrophiles, who acted out of opportunity, sadism, or transient interest. Of the total, 92% were male and 8% were female. 57% of the genuine necrophiles had occupational access to corpses, with morgue attendant, hospital orderly, and cemetery employee being the most common jobs. The researchers theorized that either of the following situations could be antecedents to necrophilia:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosman_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-rosman-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li>The necrophile develops poor <a href="/wiki/Self-esteem" title="Self-esteem">self-esteem</a>, perhaps due in part to a significant loss;
<dl>
<dd>(a) They are very fearful of rejection by others and they desire a sexual partner who is incapable of rejecting them; and/or</dd>
<dd>(b) They are fearful of the dead, and transform their fear — by means of <a href="/wiki/Reaction_formation" title="Reaction formation">reaction formation</a> — into a desire.</dd>
</dl>
</li>
<li>They develop an exciting fantasy of sex with a corpse, sometimes after exposure to a corpse.</li>
</ol>
<p>The authors reported that, of their sample of genuine necrophiles:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosman_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-rosman-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>68% were motivated by a desire for an unresisting and unrejecting partner;</li>
<li>21% by a want for reunion with a lost partner;</li>
<li>15% by sexual attraction to dead people;</li>
<li>15% by a desire for comfort or to overcome feelings of isolation; and</li>
<li>12% by a desire to remedy low self-esteem by expressing power over a corpse.</li>
</ul>
<p>IQ data was limited, but not abnormally low. About half of the sample had a personality disorder, and 11% of true necrophiles were psychotic. Rosman and Resnick concluded that their data challenged the conventional view of necrophiles as generally psychotic, mentally deficient, or unable to obtain a consenting partner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosman_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-rosman-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_animals">Other animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Necrophilia has been observed in mammals, birds, reptiles and frogs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-demattos_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-demattos-17">[17]</a></sup> In 1960, Robert Dickerman described necrophilia in <a href="/wiki/Ground_squirrel" title="Ground squirrel">ground squirrels</a>, which he termed "Davian behavior" in reference to a <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">limerick</a> about a necrophiliac miner named Dave. The label is still used for necrophilia in animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-costa_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-costa-18">[18]</a></sup> Certain species of arachnids and insects practice <a href="/wiki/Sexual_cannibalism" title="Sexual cannibalism">sexual cannibalism</a>, in which the female cannibalizes her male mate prior to, during, or after copulation.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kees_Moeliker" title="Kees Moeliker">Kees Moeliker</a> made one observation while he was sitting in his office at the <a href="/wiki/Natural_History_Museum_Rotterdam" title="Natural History Museum Rotterdam">Natural History Museum Rotterdam</a>, when he heard the distinctive thud of a bird hitting the glass facade of the building. Upon inspection, he discovered a drake (male) <a href="/wiki/Mallard" title="Mallard">mallard</a> lying dead outside the building. Next to the downed bird there was a second drake mallard standing close by. As Moeliker observed the couple, the living drake pecked at the corpse of the dead one for a few minutes then mounted the corpse and began copulating with it. The act of necrophilia lasted for about 75 minutes, in which time, according to Moeliker, the living drake took two short breaks before resuming with copulating behavior. Moeliker surmised that at the time of the collision with the window the two mallards were engaged in a common pattern in <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">duck</a> behavior called "attempted <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">rape</a> flight". "When one died the other one just went for it and didn't get any negative feedback — well, didn't get any feedback," according to Moeliker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mallard_Duck_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mallard_Duck-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MallardDuck_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-MallardDuck-20">[20]</a></sup> Necrophilia had previously only been reported in heterosexual mallard pairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mallard_Duck_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mallard_Duck-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a short paper known as "Sexual Habits of the Adélie Penguin", <a href="/wiki/George_Murray_Levick" title="George Murray Levick">George Murray Levick</a> described mating with dead females in the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Adare" title="Cape Adare">Cape Adare</a> <a href="/wiki/Rookery" title="Rookery">rookery</a>, the largest group of <a href="/wiki/Ad%C3%A9lie_penguin" title="Adélie penguin">Adélie penguins</a>, in 1911 and 1912.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russel_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-russel-22">[22]</a></sup> This is nowadays ascribed to lack of experience of young penguins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GD2012_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-GD2012-23">[23]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Gentoo_penguin" title="Gentoo penguin">gentoo penguin</a> was observed attempting to have intercourse with a dead penguin in 1921.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>A male <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_sea_lion" title="New Zealand sea lion">New Zealand sea lion</a> was once observed attempting to copulate with a dead female <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Zealand_fur_seal" title="New Zealand fur seal">New Zealand fur seal</a> in the wild. The sea lion nudged the seal repeatedly, then mounted her and made several pelvic thrusts. Approximately ten minutes later, the sea lion became disturbed by the researcher's presence, dragged the corpse of the seal into the water and swam away while holding it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> A male <a href="/wiki/Sea_otter" title="Sea otter">sea otter</a> was observed holding a female sea otter underwater until she drowned, and then repeatedly copulating with her carcass. Several months later, the same sea otter was again observed copulating with the carcass of a different female.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Copulation with a dead female <a href="/wiki/Pilot_whale" title="Pilot whale">pilot whale</a> by a captive male pilot whale has been observed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and possible sexual behavior between two male <a href="/wiki/Humpback_whale" title="Humpback whale">humpback whales</a>, one dead, has also been reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1983, a male <a href="/wiki/Rock_dove" title="Rock dove">rock dove</a> was observed copulating with the corpse of a dove that had shortly before had its head crushed by a car.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_2:_Electric_Boogaloo_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_2:_Electric_Boogaloo-30">[30]</a></sup> In 2001, a researcher laid out <a href="/wiki/Sand_martin" title="Sand martin">sand martin</a> corpses to attract flocks of other sand martins. In each of six trials, 1-5 individuals from flocks of 50-500 were observed attempting to copulate with the dead sand martins. This occurred one to two months after the breeding season; since copulation outside the breeding season is uncommon among birds, the researcher speculated that the lack of resistance by the corpses stimulated the behavior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dale_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-dale-31">[31]</a></sup> Charles Brown observed at least ten <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cliff_swallow" title="Cliff swallow">cliff swallows</a> attempt to copulate with a road-killed cliff swallow in the space of 15 minutes. He commented, "This isn't the first time I've seen cliff swallows do this; the bright orange rump sticking up seems to be all the stimulus these birds need."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Necrophilia has also been reported in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_swallow" title="European swallow">European swallow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grey-backed_sparrow-lark" title="Grey-backed sparrow-lark">grey-backed sparrow-lark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stark%27s_lark" title="Stark's lark">Stark's lark</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russel_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-russel-22">[22]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Snow_goose" title="Snow goose">snow goose</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> A Norwegian television report showed a male <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybrid</a> between a <a href="/wiki/Black_grouse" title="Black grouse">black grouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_capercaillie" title="Western capercaillie">western capercaillie</a> kill a male black grouse before attempting to copulate with it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dale_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-dale-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<p>Necrophilia has been documented in various lizard species, including the <a href="/wiki/Giant_ameiva" title="Giant ameiva">giant ameiva</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-costa_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-costa-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leopard_lizard" title="Leopard lizard">leopard lizard</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Holbrookia_maculata" title="Holbrookia maculata">Holbrookia maculata</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> There are two reports of necrophilic behavior in the sleepy lizard (<i><a href="/wiki/Tiliqua_rugosa" title="Tiliqua rugosa">Tiliqua rugosa</a></i>). In one, the partner of a male lizard got caught in fencing wire and died. The male continued to display courtship behavior towards his partner two days after her death. This lizard's necrophilia was believed to stem from its strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monogamous_pairing_in_animals" title="Monogamous pairing in animals">monogamous</a> bond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In one study of <a href="/wiki/Argentine_black_and_white_tegu" title="Argentine black and white tegu">black and white tegu</a> lizards, two different males were observed attempting to court and copulate with a single female corpse on two consecutive days. On the first day, the corpse was freshly dead, but by the second day it was bloating and emitting a strong putrefying odor. The researcher attributed the behavior to <a href="/wiki/Sex_pheromone" title="Sex pheromone">sex pheromones</a> still acting on the carcass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sazima_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-sazima-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Male <a href="/wiki/Garter_snake" title="Garter snake">garter snakes</a> often copulate with dead females.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> One case has been reported in the <i><a href="/wiki/Bothrops_jararaca" title="Bothrops jararaca">Bothrops jararaca</a></i> snake with a dead <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_American_rattlesnake" title="South American rattlesnake">South American rattlesnake</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-klauber718_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-klauber718-41">[41]</a></sup> The prairie rattlesnake (<i><a href="/wiki/Crotalus_viridis" title="Crotalus viridis">Crotalus viridis</a></i>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Helicops_carinicaudus" title="Helicops carinicaudus">Helicops carinicaudus</a></i> snake have both been seen attempting to mate with decapitated females, presumably attracted by still-active <a href="/wiki/Sex_pheromone" title="Sex pheromone">sex pheromones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-klauber718_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-klauber718-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Male <a href="/wiki/Crayfish" title="Crayfish">crayfish</a> sometimes copulate with dead crayfish of either sex, and in one observation even with a dead crayfish of a different species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>In frogs, it has been observed in the <a href="/wiki/Foothill_yellow-legged_frog" title="Foothill yellow-legged frog">foothill yellow-legged frog</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> the yellow <a href="/wiki/Fire-bellied_toad" title="Fire-bellied toad">fire-bellied toad</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Common_frog" title="Common frog">common frog</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mollov_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-mollov-47">[47]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_spotted_frog" title="Oregon spotted frog">Oregon spotted frog</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Dendropsophus_columbianus" title="Dendropsophus columbianus">Dendropsophus columbianus</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rhinella_jimi" title="Rhinella jimi">Rhinella jimi</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-demattos_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-demattos-17">[17]</a></sup> The film <i><a href="/wiki/Cane_Toads:_An_Unnatural_History" title="Cane Toads: An Unnatural History">Cane Toads: An Unnatural History</a></i> shows a male toad copulating with a female toad that had been run over by a car. It goes on to do this for eight hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Necrophilic <a href="/wiki/Amplexus" title="Amplexus">amplexus</a> in frogs may occur because males will mount any pliable object the size of an adult female. If the mounted object is a live frog not appropriate for mating, it will vibrate its body or vocalize a call to be released. Dead frogs cannot do this, so they may be held for hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mollov_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-mollov-47">[47]</a></sup> The Amazonian frog species <i><a href="/wiki/Rhinella_proboscidea" title="Rhinella proboscidea">Rhinella proboscidea</a></i> sometimes practices what has been termed "functional necrophilia". Males grasp the corpse of a dead female and squeeze it until <a href="/wiki/Oocyte" title="Oocyte">oocytes</a> are ejected, and then fertilize them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legality">Legality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Article 212 of the Brazilian Penal Code (federal Decree-Law No 2.848) states as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bpc_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-bpc-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p><br/></p>
<blockquote>
<p>Art. 212 - To abuse a cadaver or its ashes:<br/>
Penalty: detention, from 1 to 3 years, plus fine.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><br/></p>
<p>Although sex with a corpse is not explicitly mentioned, a person who has sex with a corpse may be convicted of a crime under the above Article. The legal asset protected by such Article isn't the corpse's objective honor, but the feeling of good memories, respect and veneration that living people keep about the deceased person: these persons are considered passive subjects of the corpse's violation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Section 297 of the Indian Penal Code entitled "Trespassing on burial places, etc", states as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ipc_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-ipc-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>Whoever, with the intention of wounding the feelings of any person, or of insulting the religion of any person, or with the knowledge that the feelings of any person are likely to be wounded, or that the religion of any person is likely to be insulted thereby,</p>
<p>commits any trespass in any place of worship or on any place of sculpture, or any place set apart from the performance of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funeral_rite" title="Funeral rite">funeral rites</a> or as a depository for the remains of the dead, or offers any indignity to any human corpse, or causes disturbance to any persons assembled for the performance of funeral ceremonies,</p>
shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to one year, or with fine, or with both.</blockquote>
<p>Although sex with a corpse is not explicitly mentioned, a person who has sex with a corpse may be convicted under the above section. <a href="/wiki/Section_377_of_the_Indian_Penal_Code" title="Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code">Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code</a> could also be invoked.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Under Section 150 of the New Zealand <a href="/wiki/Crimes_Act_1961" title="Crimes Act 1961">Crimes Act 1961</a>, it is an offence for there to be "misconduct in respect to human remains." Subsection (b) elaborates that this applies if someone "improperly or indecently interferes with or offers indignity to any dead human body or human remains, whether buried or not." This statute is therefore applicable to sex with corpses and carries a potential two-year prison sentence, although it should be noted that there is no case law as yet that would apply the aforementioned statute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Section 14 of the <a href="/wiki/Criminal_Law_(Sexual_Offences_and_Related_Matters)_Amendment_Act,_2007" title="Criminal Law (Sexual Offences and Related Matters) Amendment Act, 2007">Criminal Law (Sexual Offences and Related Matters) Amendment Act, 2007</a> prohibits the commission of a sexual act with a corpse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Until codified by the act it was a <a href="/wiki/Common_law_offence" title="Common law offence">common law offence</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necrophilia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sexual penetration with a corpse was made illegal under the <a href="/wiki/Sexual_Offences_Act_2003" title="Sexual Offences Act 2003">Sexual Offences Act 2003</a>. This is defined as depictions of "sexual interference with a human corpse" as well as actual scenes (see also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extreme_pornography" title="Extreme pornography">extreme pornography</a>). As of the <a href="/wiki/Criminal_Justice_and_Immigration_Act_2008" title="Criminal Justice and Immigration Act 2008">Criminal Justice and Immigration Act 2008</a>, it is also illegal to possess explicit and realistic depictions of sexual interference with a human corpse, electronic or otherwise.</p>
