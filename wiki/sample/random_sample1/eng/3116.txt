<p><b>Nexen Heroes Baseball Club</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">넥센 히어로즈 야구단</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. They are a member of the <a href="/wiki/KBO_League" title="KBO League">KBO League</a> which culminates in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Series" title="Korean Series">Korean Series</a>. The Heroes mascot is <i>Teokdori</i> (<span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">턱돌이</span>; Mr. Jaw). Like other baseball teams in South Korea, the team is known primarily by the name of the sponsor, occasionally as their nickname, and never as the city in which they are based. The Heroes play their home games at <a href="/wiki/Gocheok_Sky_Dome" title="Gocheok Sky Dome">Gocheok Sky Dome</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gocheok-dong" title="Gocheok-dong">Gocheok-dong</a>, a neighborhood located in the southwestern part of Seoul.</p>
<p>In 2014, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Futures_League" title="Korea Baseball Futures League">Futures League</a> squad changed its name to <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hwaseong_Heroes" title="Hwaseong Heroes">Hwaseong Heroes</a></b> (<span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko"><a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%ED%99%94%EC%84%B1_%ED%9E%88%EC%96%B4%EB%A1%9C%EC%A6%88" title="ko:화성 히어로즈">화성 히어로즈</a></span>) differentiating from the first string.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nexen_Heroes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The franchise was originally known as the Sammi SuperStars and had subsequent incarnations as the Chungbo Pintos and Pacific Dolphins. The team was renamed the <b><a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Unicorns" title="Hyundai Unicorns">Hyundai Unicorns</a></b> after being sold to <a href="/wiki/Hyundai" title="Hyundai">Hyundai</a> in 1996, and was relocated from <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a> to <a href="/wiki/Suwon" title="Suwon">Suwon</a>. The Unicorns won the KBO championship four times (1998, 2000, 2003 and 2004), with only the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Kia Tigers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Lions" title="Samsung Lions">Samsung Lions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doosan_Bears" title="Doosan Bears">Doosan Bears</a> having a better record of championship victories. But in 2008, the team was disbanded. After that, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Centennial_Investments&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Centennial Investments (page does not exist)">Centennial Investments</a> founded a new team called Woori Heroes. Unlike the other KBO League ballclubs, the Heroes are owned by a collection of individuals headed by Lee Chang-suk (founder), instead of being owned by its naming sponsor. But during the 2008 season, Woori broke the naming sponsor deal, citing that the Heroes has not paid the full founding fee to the KBO yet, forcing the team to trade most of its star players for cash. For the rest of 2008 season and the 2009 season, the team used only "Heroes" for its name. On February 8, 2010, naming rights were sold to <a href="/wiki/Nexen_Tire" title="Nexen Tire">Nexen Tire</a>.</p>
