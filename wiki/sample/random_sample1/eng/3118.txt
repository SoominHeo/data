<p><b>Nell Marie "Nellie" McKay</b> (born April 13, 1982<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) is a British-American <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>, and former <a href="/wiki/Stand-up_comedy" title="Stand-up comedy">stand-up comedian</a>, noted for her critically acclaimed albums, and for her Broadway debut in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Threepenny_Opera" title="The Threepenny Opera">The Threepenny Opera</a></i> (2006). Her music has showcased different genres, from <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disco" title="Disco">disco</a> to <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McKay was born in London<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-born_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-born-2">[2]</a></sup> to an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> father, writer-director <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_McKay" title="Malcolm McKay">Malcolm McKay</a>, and an American mother, actress <a href="/wiki/Robin_Pappas" title="Robin Pappas">Robin Pappas</a>. She holds dual citizenship. While growing up, she lived with her mother in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harlem,_New_York" title="Harlem, New York">Harlem, New York</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Washington" title="Olympia, Washington">Olympia, Washington</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Mount_Pocono,_Pennsylvania" title="Mount Pocono, Pennsylvania">Mount Pocono, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cabaret_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-cabaret-3">[3]</a></sup> She studied jazz voice at the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_School_of_Music" title="Manhattan School of Music">Manhattan School of Music</a>, but did not graduate. Her performances at various New York City music venues, including the Sidewalk Cafe and Joe's Pub, drew attention from record labels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-born_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-born-2">[2]</a></sup> She signed with Columbia Records.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.932006">2004â2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004â2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Get_Away_from_Me"><i>Get Away from Me</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Get Away from Me">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The recording sessions for McKay's debut album <i>Get Away from Me</i> took place in August 2003 with <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Emerick" title="Geoff Emerick">Geoff Emerick</a> as producer. Emerick was known for working as the Beatles' engineer on such albums as <i>Revolver</i> and <i>Abbey Road</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The title is a play on <a href="/wiki/Norah_Jones" title="Norah Jones">Norah Jones</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Come_Away_with_Me" title="Come Away with Me">Come Away with Me</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cabaret_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-cabaret-3">[3]</a></sup> McKay is said to be the first woman to release a <a href="/wiki/Double_album" title="Double album">double album</a> as her first release. Originally, her contract with Columbia called for 13 songs, but McKay aggressively lobbied her label for a double album, including bottles of wine, a <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_PowerPoint" title="Microsoft PowerPoint">PowerPoint</a> slideshow, and a mock photo of her threatening Emerick with a gun. (Allmusic).</p>
<p><i>Get Away from Me</i> was released in February 2004. Jon Pareles of <i>The New York Times</i> called the album "a tour de force from a sly, articulate musician who sounds comfortable in any era".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The album was included on several "Best of 2004" lists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>McKay was one of the major breakout artists from the 2004 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SXSW" title="SXSW">SXSW</a> Festival and was a finalist in the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Shortlist_Music_Prize" title="Shortlist Music Prize">Shortlist Music Prize</a>. She toured the northern United States in July 2004 as an opening act on the first half of the Au Naturale tour co-headlined by <a href="/wiki/Alanis_Morissette" title="Alanis Morissette">Alanis Morissette</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barenaked_Ladies" title="Barenaked Ladies">Barenaked Ladies</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rumor_Has_It..."><i>Rumor Has It...</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rumor Has It...">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>McKay wrote and recorded several songs for the motion picture <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rumor_Has_It..." title="Rumor Has It...">Rumor Has It...</a>.</i> The songs were released on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> on 27 December 2005.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pretty_Little_Head"><i>Pretty Little Head</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pretty Little Head">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><i>Pretty Little Head</i> was released in the United States on 31 October 2006 on McKay's own label, Hungry Mouse, and was marketed by <a href="/wiki/SpinART_Records" title="SpinART Records">SpinART Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The album was the intended 23 tracks as originally planned, divided into two discs with a 44-page color booklet. Release of the album in other countries, including Canada, was delayed until 21 November 2006.</p>
<p>After SpinART declared bankruptcy in 2007, <i>Pretty Little Head</i> was released by Sony.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Threepenny_Opera"><i>The Threepenny Opera</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Threepenny Opera">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>McKay made her <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut as Polly Peachum in the <a href="/wiki/Roundabout_Theatre_Company" title="Roundabout Theatre Company">Roundabout Theatre Company</a>'s limited-run production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Threepenny_Opera" title="The Threepenny Opera">The Threepenny Opera</a></i>, co-starring with <a href="/wiki/Alan_Cumming" title="Alan Cumming">Alan Cumming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Dale" title="Jim Dale">Jim Dale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Charles_Rooney" title="Brian Charles Rooney">Brian Charles Rooney</a>. The role earned her a <a href="/wiki/Theatre_World_Award" title="Theatre World Award">Theatre World Award</a> for Outstanding Debut Performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.93present">2007âpresent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2007âpresent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Obligatory_Villagers"><i>Obligatory Villagers</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Obligatory Villagers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>McKay's third full-length studio release debuted on 25 September 2007. With both of her previous albums lasting over 60 minutes and spanning two discs each, <i><a href="/wiki/Obligatory_Villagers" title="Obligatory Villagers">Obligatory Villagers</a></i>, with only nine tracks (ten if purchased from <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>), totaling 30 minutes was her shortest release to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">[9]</a></sup> The album was produced on McKay's own label, Hungry Mouse, and released by <a href="/wiki/Vanguard_Records" title="Vanguard Records">Vanguard Records</a>.</p>
<p>The album was recorded at <a class="external text" href="http://www.redrockrecording.com" rel="nofollow">Red Rock Recording Studio</a> in the Pocono Mountains where McKay went to high school.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Normal_as_Blueberry_Pie_.E2.80.93_A_Tribute_to_Doris_Day"><i>Normal as Blueberry Pie â A Tribute to Doris Day</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Normal as Blueberry Pie â A Tribute to Doris Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On October 13, 2009 she released her fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Normal_as_Blueberry_Pie_%E2%80%93_A_Tribute_to_Doris_Day" title="Normal as Blueberry Pie â A Tribute to Doris Day">Normal as Blueberry Pie â A Tribute to Doris Day</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Verve_Records" title="Verve Records">Verve Records</a>. The album contains twelve covers of songs made famous by <a href="/wiki/Doris_Day" title="Doris Day">Day</a>, as well as one original tune. <a href="/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble" title="Barnes &amp; Noble">Barnes &amp; Noble</a> featured an exclusive edition, packaged with the bonus track "I Want To Be Happy". iTunes also featured an exclusive edition with a different bonus track, "I'll Never Smile Again".</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_Sweet_Mobile_Home"><i>Home Sweet Mobile Home</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Home Sweet Mobile Home">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On September 28, 2010 McKay and <a href="/wiki/Verve_Records" title="Verve Records">Verve Records</a> released her fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Home_Sweet_Mobile_Home" title="Home Sweet Mobile Home">Home Sweet Mobile Home</a>,</i> with original tracks. It was produced by McKay and her mother, Robin Pappas, with artistic input from <a href="/wiki/David_Byrne" title="David Byrne">David Byrne</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Old_Hats"><i>Old Hats</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Old Hats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2013, McKay appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> revue show <i><a href="/wiki/Old_Hats" title="Old Hats">Old Hats</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="My_Weekly_Reader"><i>My Weekly Reader</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: My Weekly Reader">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 24, 2015 McKay released her sixth album, <i>My Weekly Reader</i>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Covers_album" title="Covers album">covers album</a> of songs from the 1960s. Songs include <a href="/wiki/Moby_Grape" title="Moby Grape">Moby Grape</a>'s "Murder in My Heart for the Judge", <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Small_Faces" title="The Small Faces">The Small Faces</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Itchycoo_Park" title="Itchycoo Park">Itchycoo Park</a>", the <a href="/wiki/Steve_Miller_Band" title="Steve Miller Band">Steve Miller Band</a>'s "Quicksilver Girl", <a href="/wiki/Frank_Zappa" title="Frank Zappa">Frank Zappa</a>'s "Hungry Freaks, Daddy", <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>' "<a href="/wiki/If_I_Fell" title="If I Fell">If I Fell</a>", <a href="/wiki/The_Cyrkle" title="The Cyrkle">The Cyrkle</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Red_Rubber_Ball" title="Red Rubber Ball">Red Rubber Ball</a>", and <a href="/wiki/Herman%27s_Hermits" title="Herman's Hermits">Herman's Hermits</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Mrs._Brown,_You%27ve_Got_a_Lovely_Daughter" title="Mrs. Brown, You've Got a Lovely Daughter">Mrs. Brown, You've Got a Lovely Daughter</a>".</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On February 1, 2007 McKay joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurie_Anderson_(performance_artist)" title="Laurie Anderson (performance artist)">Laurie Anderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joan_Osborne" title="Joan Osborne">Joan Osborne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzanne_Vega" title="Suzanne Vega">Suzanne Vega</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Philharmonic" title="Brooklyn Philharmonic">Brooklyn Philharmonic</a> Orchestra for <i>Four Scored</i>, a single performance of reworked songs at the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Academy_of_Music" title="Brooklyn Academy of Music">Brooklyn Academy of Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>McKay also had a role in <i><a href="/wiki/P.S._I_Love_You_(film)" title="P.S. I Love You (film)">P.S. I Love You</a></i>, a 2007 film directed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_LaGravenese" title="Richard LaGravenese">Richard LaGravenese</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Swank" title="Hilary Swank">Hilary Swank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Butler" title="Gerard Butler">Gerard Butler</a>. McKay was cast as Ciara Reilly, sister of Holly Kennedy, played by Hilary Swank.</p>
<p>She has written three Christmas-themed songs, "A Christmas Dirge", "Take Me Away", and "Weed (All I Want for Christmas)".</p>
<p>McKay is featured in the song "How Are You?" on David Byrne and Fatboy Slim's concept album <i><a href="/wiki/Here_Lies_Love" title="Here Lies Love">Here Lies Love</a></i>, released in 2010. The album chronicles the life of Imelda Marcos, the widow of former Philippines President Ferdinand Marcos.</p>
<p>McKay, along with violinist <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Quint" title="Philippe Quint">Philippe Quint</a>, starred in and contributed music to the independent film <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Express&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Downtown Express (page does not exist)">Downtown Express</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/David_Grubin" title="David Grubin">David Grubin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her musical show "I Want to Live!" is based on the life of murderer <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Graham" title="Barbara Graham">Barbara Graham</a>, who also inspired a 1958 film with the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_views">Political views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nellie_McKay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Political views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McKay is a vocal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feminist" title="Feminist">feminist</a>, and wrote a satirical song relating to feminist issues called "Mother of Pearl".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> McKay "is a proud member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PETA" title="PETA">PETA</a>" (album notes); her song "Columbia Is Bleeding" dealt with the issue of <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>'s cruelty to animals. She wrote a 2004 song ("John John") about her feelings in favor of political candidate <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Nader" title="Ralph Nader">Ralph Nader</a> over Democratic presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/John_Kerry" title="John Kerry">John Kerry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has performed at events for the progressive radio station <a href="/wiki/WBAI" title="WBAI">WBAI</a>, Planned Parenthood, Farm Sanctuary,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and the Coalition to Ban Horse-Drawn Carriages, among many groups. McKay was one of several musicians to write a song in support of Georgia death row inmate <a href="/wiki/Troy_Davis" title="Troy Davis">Troy Davis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
