<p><b>Nefteyugansk</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Нефтеюга́нск</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Khanty-Mansi_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Khanty-Mansi Autonomous Okrug">Khanty-Mansi Autonomous Okrug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located south of the <a href="/wiki/Ob_River" title="Ob River">Ob River</a>, close to the larger city of <a href="/wiki/Surgut" title="Surgut">Surgut</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">122,855 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-3">[3]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">107,830 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-5">[5]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">93,930 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nefteyugansk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was founded on October 16, 1967, after an oil field had been discovered on a small forest clearing in the middle of the <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> marshland in 1961.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The main and the only big enterprise in the city, <i>Yuganskneftegaz</i>, was founded in February 1966. The name 'Yugansk' comes from the indigenous <a href="/wiki/Khanty_language" title="Khanty language">Khanty</a> name of a small river near the city, <i>neft'</i> means oil in Russian, and <i>gaz</i> is <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>. The economy of the city remains <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>-based, and was a major center for the Russian oil enterprise <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YUKOS" title="YUKOS">YUKOS</a></i>, which owned <i>Yuganskneftegaz</i>. In fact, the "Yu" in "YUKOS" comes from the "yu" in "Nefteyugansk" and therefore from "<a href="/wiki/Yuganskneftegaz" title="Yuganskneftegaz">Yuganskneftegaz</a>". The other three letters come from the oil-refining factory "Kuibyshev-Org-Sintez", situated in <a href="/wiki/Samara,_Russia" title="Samara, Russia">Samara</a>.</p>
<p>On June 26, 1998, city mayor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Petukhov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vladimir Petukhov (page does not exist)">Vladimir Petukhov</a> was shot dead on the way to his office. Before his murder, Petukhov had been on a <a href="/wiki/Hunger_strike" title="Hunger strike">hunger strike</a> demanding that the chairmen of municipal and district tax offices be dismissed from their positions and a criminal case against Yukos be filed on counts of <a href="/wiki/Tax_evasion" title="Tax evasion">tax evasion</a>. Petukhov's widow later on called for an investigation into <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Khodorkovsky" title="Mikhail Khodorkovsky">Mikhail Khodorkovsky</a>'s role in events. Back then Khodorkovsky was head of Yukos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On September 20, 2005 Dmitry Yegortsev, acting mayor of Nefteyugansk, was assaulted and wounded with a knife. After the stabbing of Yegortsev, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Igor_Gribanov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Igor Gribanov (page does not exist)">Igor Gribanov</a> took over City Hall as acting mayor. Just a few month later, he died of carbon monoxide poisoning at his home on January 6, 2006.<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_fire"><a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nefteyugansk#endnote_fire">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since January 2005, Yuganskneftegaz has been owned by the state-owned oil company <a href="/wiki/Rosneft" title="Rosneft">Rosneft</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nefteyugansk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Nefteyugansk serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nefteyugansky_District" title="Nefteyugansky District">Nefteyugansky District</a>, even though it is not a part of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref51_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Ref51-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is incorporated separately as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">city of okrug significance</a> of Nefteyugansk</b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Khanty%E2%80%93Mansi_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Administrative divisions of Khanty–Mansi Autonomous Okrug">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref51_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Ref51-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the city of okrug significance of Nefteyugansk is incorporated as <b>Nefteyugansk Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref52_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ref52-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nefteyugansk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is served by the <a href="/wiki/Surgut_International_Airport" title="Surgut International Airport">Surgut International Airport</a>.</p>
