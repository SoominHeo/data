<p><b>Nyalam County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">聂拉木县</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">聶拉木縣</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Nièlāmù Xiàn</span></i>; Tibetan: gnya' lam rdzong) is a county in <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shigatse_Prefecture" title="Shigatse Prefecture">Shigatse Prefecture</a>. It borders on Nepal.</p>
<p>The land area of the county is 7,903 km<sup>2</sup> (3,051 sq mi). The population as of 2003 was 10,000. The postal code for the county is 858300.</p>
<p>The county seat is in <a href="/wiki/Nyalam_Town" title="Nyalam Town">Nyalam Town</a>.</p>
<p>The other town of the county is <a href="/wiki/Zhangmu" title="Zhangmu">Dram</a>, also known under its Tibetan name Dram, or <a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a> Khasa. It is located near the border and is the point of entry from Nepal. At "merely" 2300 meters elevation about the sea level, Zhangmu has mild and humid subtropical climate, which is a rarity for Tibet.</p>
<p>It is one of the four counties that comprise the <a href="/wiki/Qomolangma_National_Nature_Preserve" title="Qomolangma National Nature Preserve">Qomolangma National Nature Preserve</a> (Nyalam, <a href="/wiki/Tingri_County" title="Tingri County">Tingri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dinggy%C3%AA_County" title="Dinggyê County">Dinggyê</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gyirong_County" title="Gyirong County">Kyirong</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
