<p><b>Nō Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">能生駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nō-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in <a href="/wiki/Itoigawa,_Niigata" title="Itoigawa, Niigata">Itoigawa, Niigata</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Tokimeki_Railway" title="Echigo Tokimeki Railway">Echigo Tokimeki Railway</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nō Station is served by the Nihonkai Hisui Line, and is 33.4 kilometers from the starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Ichiburi_Station" title="Ichiburi Station">Ichiburi</a> and 327.9 kilometers from <a href="/wiki/Maibara_Station" title="Maibara Station">Maibara Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of two <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> on an embankment, level with the second story of a two-story station building.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 16 December 1912, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Government_Railways" title="Japanese Government Railways">Japanese Government Railways</a> (JGR, JNR after 1949). It was relocated to its present location of 1 October 1961.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>From 14 March 2015, with the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_Shinkansen" title="Hokuriku Shinkansen">Hokuriku Shinkansen</a> extension from <a href="/wiki/Nagano_Station" title="Nagano Station">Nagano</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kanazawa_Station" title="Kanazawa Station">Kanazawa</a>, local passenger operations over sections of the Shinetsu Main Line and Hokuriku Main Line running roughly parallel to the new shinkansen line were reassigned to <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership#Japan" title="Public–private partnership">third-sector</a> railway operating companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj371_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-dj371-1">[1]</a></sup> From this date, Nō Station was transferred to the ownership of the third-sector operating company Echigo Tokimeki Railway.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2015, the station was used by an average of 367 passengers daily (boarding passengers only).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ETR2015stats_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ETR2015stats-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
