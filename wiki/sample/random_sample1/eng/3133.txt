<p><b>Noda</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">野田村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Noda-mura</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Villages_of_Japan" title="Villages of Japan">village</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kunohe_District,_Iwate" title="Kunohe District, Iwate">Kunohe District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iwate_Prefecture" title="Iwate Prefecture">Iwate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of February 2014, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 4,290 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 53.1 persons per km². The total area was 80.84  km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noda,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Noda is located in far north-eastern Iwate Prefecture, bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> to the east and the Kitakami Mountains to the west.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noda,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Noda was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a>, dominated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nambu_clan" title="Nambu clan">Nambu clan</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, who ruled <a href="/wiki/Hachinohe_Domain" title="Hachinohe Domain">Hachinohe Domain</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>. The village of Noda was created within Kita-Kunohe District on April 1, 1889. The area was swept by a <a href="/wiki/1896_Sanriku_earthquake" title="1896 Sanriku earthquake">tsunami in 1896</a>, which killed 261 villagers and destroyed 411 houses. Kita-Kunohe District and Minami-Kunohe Districts merged to form Kunohe District on April 1, 1897. The village again suffered from the effects of a <a href="/wiki/1933_Sanriku_earthquake" title="1933 Sanriku earthquake">tsunami in 1933</a>, which killed seven people and destroyed 58 houses. The <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a> created extensive damage.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noda,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The local economy is based on <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">commercial fishing</a> and to a lesser extent on agriculture. The area was formerly noted for its salt production.</p>
