<p>The <b>1996 Paralympic Games</b> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">USA</a> were held from August 16 to 25. It was the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paralympics" title="Paralympics">Paralympics</a> to get mass media sponsorship,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IPCgeninfo_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-IPCgeninfo-1">[1]</a></sup> and had a budget of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">USD</a> $81 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was the first Paralympic Games where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Sports_Federation_for_Persons_with_an_Intellectual_Disability" title="International Sports Federation for Persons with an Intellectual Disability">International Sports Federation for Persons with an Intellectual Disability</a> athletes were given full medal status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbol_and_mascot_of_the_games">Symbol and mascot of the games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Summer_Paralympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Symbol and mascot of the games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1996_Paralympic_Mascot_Blaze.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="110" data-file-width="100" height="110" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/4/44/1996_Paralympic_Mascot_Blaze.png" width="100"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1996_Paralympic_Mascot_Blaze.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
1996 Paralympic Mascot Blaze the Phoenix</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">mascot</a> for the Paralympic Summer Games in Atlanta 1996 was Blaze. Blaze was created by Trevor Stone Irvin of Irvin Productions in Atlanta.</p>
<p>Blaze is a <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_(mythology)" title="Phoenix (mythology)">phoenix</a>, a mythical bird that rises from ashes to experience a renewed life. The phoenix appears in Egyptian, Arabian, Chinese, Russian and Native American <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a> and in all instances symbolizes strength, vision, inspiration and survival. The phoenix was an ideal mascot for the 1996 Atlanta Paralympic Games and later for BlazeSports America, a nonprofit organization that is the direct legacy of the Games. The phoenix has long been the symbol of Atlanta’s rebirth after its devastation in the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. But most importantly, it is the personification of the will, perseverance and determination of youth and adults with physical disability to achieve full and productive lives. Blaze, with his bright colors, height and broad wing span, reflects the traits, identified in a focus group of athletes with disability, as those they believed best represented the drive to succeed of persons with physical disability who pursue sports as recreation and as a competitive endeavor. Today, Blaze is the most recognizable symbol of disability sport in America.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medal_count">Medal count</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Summer_Paralympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Medal count">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Paralympics_medal_table" title="1996 Summer Paralympics medal table">1996 Summer Paralympics medal table</a></div>
<p>A total of 1577 medals were awarded during the Atlanta games: 518 gold, 517 silver, and 542 bronze. The host country, the United States, topped the medal count with more gold medals, more bronze medals, and more medals overall than any other nation. Germany took the most silver medals, with 58.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
