<p>The <b>splendid perches</b> are a small family, <b>Callanthiidae</b> (about 14 species), of marine fishes in the order <a href="/wiki/Perciformes" title="Perciformes">Perciformes</a>. The family is largely restricted to the Indian and Pacific Oceans; the only species found outside these regions are <i>Callanthias legras</i> of the southeast Atlantic, and <i>C. ruber</i> of the East Atlantic and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>. These brightly coloured fishes are found in relatively deep waters; some species of <i>Callanthias</i> are the only to occur at depths within reach in normal <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diving</a>. As suggested by the scientific name Call<u>anthii</u>dae, they show some resemblance to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthias" title="Anthias">anthias</a> (subfamily Anthiinae, family Serranidae).</p>
