<p><i><b>Normanichthys crockeri</b></i>, the <b>mote sculpin</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Actinopterygii" title="Actinopterygii">ray-finned fish</a>, the only member of the family <b>Normanichthyidae</b>. It is found in tropical <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">South Pacific</a> waters, from <a href="/wiki/Chimbote" title="Chimbote">Chimbote</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isla_Mocha" title="Isla Mocha">Isla Mocha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, where it is found at depths from 37 to 200 m (121 to 656 ft). This fish reaches a length of up to 11 cm (4.3 in). Common names for the species in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> include <i>camotillo</i> (in Peru) and <i>bacaladillo</i> (in Chile).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Normanichthys_crockeri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Normanichthys crockeri</i> was <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">first described</a> by the American zoologist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Walton_Clark&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Howard Walton Clark (page does not exist)">Howard Walton Clark</a> in 1937, the generic name <i>Normanichthys</i> being given in honour of the British ichthyiologist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=John_Roxburgh_Norman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="John Roxburgh Norman (page does not exist)">John Roxburgh Norman</a> who was taking part in <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Investigations" title="Discovery Investigations">Discovery Investigations</a> at the time, undertaking research into whales and their ecology in the Southern Ocean. The fish seemed to have few affinities with other known species, and a new genus and family were erected to accommodate it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Normanichthys_crockeri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Normanichthys crockeri</i> is a fairly slender fish with a maximum length of about 11 cm (4.3 in), with the anus approximately halfway along the body. It has 1 spine and 5 pelvic fin rays, and 7 and 6 principal rays in the caudal fin. Internally, it has 36 to 37 <a href="/wiki/Myomere" title="Myomere">myomeres</a> (blocks of muscle).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
