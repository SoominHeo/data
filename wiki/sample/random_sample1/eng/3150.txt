<p><b>Norberto Bobbio</b> (<small>Italian: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[norˈbɛrto ˈbɔbbjo]</a></span>; 18 October 1909 – 9 January 2004) was an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_of_law" title="Philosophy of law">philosopher of law</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">political sciences</a> and a historian of political thought. He also wrote regularly for the <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>-based daily <i><a href="/wiki/La_Stampa" title="La Stampa">La Stampa</a></i>. Bobbio was a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_socialism" title="Liberal socialism">liberal socialist</a> in the tradition of <a href="/wiki/Piero_Gobetti" title="Piero Gobetti">Piero Gobetti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Rosselli" title="Carlo Rosselli">Carlo Rosselli</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_Calogero&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guido Calogero (page does not exist)">Guido Calogero</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Aldo_Capitini" title="Aldo Capitini">Aldo Capitini</a>. He was also strongly influenced by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Kelsen" title="Hans Kelsen">Hans Kelsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vilfredo_Pareto" title="Vilfredo Pareto">Vilfredo Pareto</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_views">Life and views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norberto_Bobbio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bobbio was born into what his <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">Guardian</a></i> obituary described as "a relatively wealthy, middle-class <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a> family" whose sympathies Bobbio would later characterize as "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/philo-" title="wikt:philo-">philo-</a><a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">fascist</a>, regarding fascism as a necessary evil against the supposedly greater danger of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolshevik" title="Bolshevik">Bolshevism</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gua130104_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gua130104-1">[1]</a></sup> In high school he met <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Foa" title="Vittorio Foa">Vittorio Foa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leone_Ginzburg" title="Leone Ginzburg">Leone Ginzburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Pavese" title="Cesare Pavese">Cesare Pavese</a>, and at the university he became a friend of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alessandro_Galante_Garrone&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alessandro Galante Garrone (page does not exist)">Alessandro Galante Garrone</a>.</p>
<p>In 1942, under the Fascist regime of <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a> and during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Bobbio joined the then illegal radical <a href="/wiki/Liberal_socialism" title="Liberal socialism">liberal socialist</a> party <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partito_d%27Azione" title="Partito d'Azione">Partito d'Azione</a> ("Party of Action") and was briefly imprisoned in 1943 and 1944. He ran unsuccessfully in the 1946 <a href="/wiki/Constituent_Assembly_of_Italy" title="Constituent Assembly of Italy">Constituent Assembly of Italy</a> elections. With the party's failure in a post-war Italy dominated by the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democracy_(Italy)" title="Christian Democracy (Italy)">Christian Democrats</a>, Bobbio left electoral politics and focused back in academia.</p>
<p>He was one of the major exponents of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-right_politics" title="Left-right politics">left-right political</a> distinctions, arguing that the Left believes in attempting to eradicate <a href="/wiki/Social_inequality" title="Social inequality">social inequality</a>, while the Right regards most social inequality as the result of ineradicable natural inequalities, and sees attempts to enforce social equality as utopian or authoritarian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A strong advocate of the <a href="/wiki/Rule_of_law" title="Rule of law">rule of law</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Separation_of_powers" title="Separation of powers">separation of powers</a>, and the limitation of powers, he was a socialist, but opposed to what he perceived as the anti-<a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Authoritarianism" title="Authoritarianism">authoritarian</a> elements in most of <a href="/wiki/Marxism" title="Marxism">Marxism</a>. He was a strong partisan of the "<a href="/wiki/Historic_Compromise" title="Historic Compromise">Historic Compromise</a>" between the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Communist_Party" title="Italian Communist Party">Italian Communist Party</a> and the Christian Democrats, and a fierce critic of <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Berlusconi" title="Silvio Berlusconi">Silvio Berlusconi</a>. Bobbio died in Turin, the same city in which he was born and lived most of his life.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_career_and_honors">Academic career and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norberto_Bobbio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academic career and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bobbio studied philosophy of law with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gioele_Solari&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gioele Solari (page does not exist)">Gioele Solari</a>; he later taught this curriculum in <a href="/wiki/Camerino" title="Camerino">Camerino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Padua" title="Padua">Padua</a>, and ultimately back in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a> as Solari's successor in 1948; from 1972 to 1984, he had a chair in the newly created faculty of political science in Turin.</p>
<p>He was a National Associate of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincean_Academy" title="Lincean Academy">Lincean Academy</a> and longtime director (together with <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Abbagnano" title="Nicola Abbagnano">Nicola Abbagnano</a>) of the <i>Rivista di Filosofia</i>. He became a Corresponding Associate of the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy" title="British Academy">British Academy</a> in 1966; in 1979 he was nominated as <a href="/wiki/Senator_for_life" title="Senator for life">Senator-for-life</a> by <a href="/wiki/President_of_Italy" title="President of Italy">Italian President</a> <a href="/wiki/Sandro_Pertini" title="Sandro Pertini">Sandro Pertini</a>. Bobbio received, among others, the <a href="/wiki/Balzan_Prize" title="Balzan Prize">Balzan Prize</a> in 1994 (for Law and Political Science: governments and democracy) and diplomas <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honoris_causa" title="Honoris causa">honoris causa</a></i> from the Universities of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Paris,_Nanterre" title="University of Paris, Nanterre">Paris (Nanterre)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Complutense_University_of_Madrid" title="Complutense University of Madrid">Madrid (Complutense)</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bologna" title="University of Bologna">Bologna</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamb%C3%A9ry_University" title="Chambéry University">Chambéry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_III_University_of_Madrid" title="Charles III University of Madrid">Madrid (Carlos III)</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Sassari" title="University of Sassari">Sassari</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Camerino" title="University of Camerino">Camerino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_University_of_Madrid" title="Autonomous University of Madrid">Madrid (Autónoma)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Buenos_Aires" title="University of Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>.</p>
<p>To celebrate the centenary of Norberto Bobbio's birth, a committee was established, constituted by more than a hundred Italian and international public institutions and intellectual figures, which formulated a wide-ranging programme of activities to promote dialogue and reflection on the thought and figure of Bobbio, and on the future of democracy, culture and civilisation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Celebrations were officially opened on 10 January 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Turin" title="University of Turin">University of Turin</a>.</p>
