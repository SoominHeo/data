<p><b>Norrbotten</b> (<small>Swedish: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[²nɔrːˌbɔtːɛn]</a></span>), known in English as <b>North <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Bothnia" title="Gulf of Bothnia">Bothnia</a></b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Sweden" title="Provinces of Sweden">Swedish province</a> (<i>landskap</i>) in northernmost <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. It borders south to <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten" title="Västerbotten">Västerbotten</a>, west to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lapland,_Sweden" title="Lapland, Sweden">Swedish Lapland</a>, and east to <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrbotten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The traditional <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Sweden" title="Provinces of Sweden">provinces of Sweden</a> serve no administrative or political purposes, but are historical and cultural entities. In this case, however, the county is older than the province. When the new national border to the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Finland" title="Grand Duchy of Finland">Grand Duchy of Finland</a> formed, the province split in two, and formed the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Kolari" title="Kolari">Kolari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muonio" title="Muonio">Muonio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pello" title="Pello">Pello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tornio" title="Tornio">Tornio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ylitornio" title="Ylitornio">Ylitornio</a>. However, Finnish Norrbotten is not recognized enough as its own historical province, so it's usually merged instead with <a href="/wiki/Ostrobothnia_(historical_province)" title="Ostrobothnia (historical province)">Ostrobothnia</a>, however leaving out Muonio to <a href="/wiki/Laponia_(historical_province_of_Finland)" title="Laponia (historical province of Finland)">Laponia</a>. The northernmost of the <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Sweden" title="Counties of Sweden">counties of Sweden</a> were created in 1810 consisting of the northern parts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lapland,_Sweden" title="Lapland, Sweden">Lappland</a> and <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten" title="Västerbotten">Västerbotten</a>. After that, northern Västerbotten has gradually evolved as a province of its own.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heraldry">Heraldry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrbotten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Heraldry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Not being one of the old historical <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Sweden" title="Provinces of Sweden">provinces of Sweden</a> Norrbotten had not been granted a coat of arms in the same way as the others. As recently as 1995, after decades of controversy, Norrbotten got its arms, thus recognized as a "real" province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrbotten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> Norrbotten was basically <a href="/wiki/Terra_nullius" title="Terra nullius">terra nullius</a>. The area was sparsely populated by <a href="/wiki/Sami_people" title="Sami people">Sami people</a>, who lived on hunting, fishing and reindeer herding. From the Middle Ages and forward, the Swedish kings tried hard to colonise and Christianize the area. Settlers from Finland, the most important of which were known as <a href="/wiki/Birkarls" title="Birkarls">Birkarls</a>, controlled the trade and even the taxing on the area long into the 16th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> From the mid-16th century, the area was more firmly tied to Sweden. An important sign of Swedish control was the large <a href="/wiki/Gammelstad_Church_Town" title="Gammelstad Church Town">Nederluleå stone church</a> from 1492. Still today, Finnish and Sami minorities live in Norrbotten and they have kept their culture and language.</p>
<p>Historically, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten" title="Västerbotten">Västerbotten</a> was the denomination for northern Sweden, together with the <a href="/wiki/Laponia_(historical_province)" title="Laponia (historical province)">Lappland</a> areas. The <i>Eastern Botten</i> or <a href="/wiki/Ostrobothnia_(region)" title="Ostrobothnia (region)">Ostrobothnia</a> was in the Finnish side.</p>
<p>Cultural identification in Sweden is closely related to the historical <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Sweden" title="Provinces of Sweden">provinces</a>, and the people in Norrbotten live in this sense in Northern <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten" title="Västerbotten">Västerbotten</a>, or <i>Norra Västerbotten</i>. In order not to be confused with people from Southern Västerbotten, i.e. the coastal region of <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten_County" title="Västerbotten County">Västerbotten County</a>, they started to identify themselves with the county rather than with the historical province.</p>
<p>Norrbotten had gradually become synonymous with the area that was previously referred to as northern Västerbotten. It started to evolve as a separate province. During the 20th century it got all of the symbols (animals, flowers etc.) which are assigned to the other provinces of Sweden. But there was still some resistance to the idea of Norrbotten being a province.</p>
<p>As recently as 1995, after decades of debate, Norrbotten was granted a <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>, thus at last being fully recognized as one of <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Sweden" title="Provinces of Sweden">provinces of Sweden</a>.</p>
<p>The coat of arms symbolizes the four large rivers in Norrbotten that drain into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Bothnia" title="Gulf of Bothnia">Gulf of Bothnia</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torne_River" title="Torne River">Torne River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalix_River" title="Kalix River">Kalix River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lule_River" title="Lule River">Lule River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pite_River" title="Pite River">Pite River</a>.</p>
<p>The summers can bring surprisingly warm temperatures for such northerly altitudes, and as a matter of fact, Norrbotten holds the all-time high record for the entire Norrland ; +37°C (98°F).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrbotten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of December 31, 2009, the population is 192,542,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-population_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-population-1">[1]</a></sup> distributed over 26,671 km<sup>2</sup>, which gives a density of 7.2 inhabitants/km<sup>2</sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrbotten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Norrbotten has around 8,000 ancient remains. Languages spoken in the province include <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a> (including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Swedish" title="North Swedish">North Swedish</a> regiolects), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Me%C3%A4nkieli" title="Meänkieli">Meänkieli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sami_language" title="Sami language">Sami</a>. Some Meänkieli speakers have gradually been considering themselves part of the <a href="/wiki/Kven_people" title="Kven people">Kven</a> people, which supposedly arrived to the area much earlier than the Swedish settlers.</p>
<p>The Church Village of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gammelstad" title="Gammelstad">Gammelstad</a> outside <a href="/wiki/Lule%C3%A5" title="Luleå">Luleå</a> has been named a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_heritage_site" title="World heritage site">World heritage site</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrbotten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Football in the province (and Norrbotten County as a whole) is administered by <a href="/wiki/Norrbottens_Fotbollf%C3%B6rbund" title="Norrbottens Fotbollförbund">Norrbottens Fotbollförbund</a>.</p>
