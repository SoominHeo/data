<p><b>Nordland</b> (<small>Norwegian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Norwegian" title="Help:IPA for Norwegian">[²nuːrlɑn]</a></span><small>  <span class="nowrap">(<img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5d/Nordland.ogg" title="Nordland.ogg">listen</a>)</span></small>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Sami_language" title="Northern Sami language">Northern Sami</a>: <span lang="se" xml:lang="se"><i>Nordlándda</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Norway" title="Counties of Norway">county</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Norway" title="Northern Norway">Northern Norway</a> region, bordering <a href="/wiki/Troms" title="Troms">Troms</a> in the north, <a href="/wiki/Nord-Tr%C3%B8ndelag" title="Nord-Trøndelag">Nord-Trøndelag</a> in the south, <a href="/wiki/Norrbotten_County" title="Norrbotten County">Norrbotten County</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten_County" title="Västerbotten County">Västerbotten County</a> to the southeast, and the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> (<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Sea" title="Norwegian Sea">Norwegian Sea</a>) to the west. The county was formerly known as <i>Nordlandene amt</i>. The county administration is in <a href="/wiki/Bod%C3%B8" title="Bodø">Bodø</a>. The remote Arctic island of <a href="/wiki/Jan_Mayen" title="Jan Mayen">Jan Mayen</a> has been administered from Nordland since 1995.</p>
<p>In the southern part is <a href="/wiki/Vega,_Norway" title="Vega, Norway">Vega</a>, listed on the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> list. The history of Nordland is a tale about the gifts from the sea: One of the most productive seas in the world providing food all year since ancient times, the same sea creates a climate more moderate than any other place in the arctic; even the bedrock itself enriched by sea living organisms millions of years ago in the geological past.</p>

<p>The county is divided into traditional districts. These are <a href="/wiki/Helgeland" title="Helgeland">Helgeland</a> in the south (south of the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a>), <a href="/wiki/Salten" title="Salten">Salten</a> in the centre, and <a href="/wiki/Ofoten" title="Ofoten">Ofoten</a> in the northeast. In the northwest lie the archipelagoes of <a href="/wiki/Lofoten" title="Lofoten">Lofoten</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vester%C3%A5len" title="Vesterålen">Vesterålen</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Trollfjord_from_the_opposite_coast_of_Raftsundet_3.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3456" data-file-width="5184" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Trollfjord_from_the_opposite_coast_of_Raftsundet_3.JPG/220px-Trollfjord_from_the_opposite_coast_of_Raftsundet_3.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Trollfjord_from_the_opposite_coast_of_Raftsundet_3.JPG/330px-Trollfjord_from_the_opposite_coast_of_Raftsundet_3.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Trollfjord_from_the_opposite_coast_of_Raftsundet_3.JPG/440px-Trollfjord_from_the_opposite_coast_of_Raftsundet_3.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

Autumn near <a href="/wiki/Trollfjord" title="Trollfjord">Trollfjord</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hadsel" title="Hadsel">Hadsel</a>. Nordland is home to innumerable fjords and fjord branches.</div>

<p>Nordland is located along the northwestern coast of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scandinavian_peninsula" title="Scandinavian peninsula">Scandinavian peninsula</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Norway" title="Northern Norway">Northern Norway</a>. Due to the large distance to the densely populated parts of Europe, this is one of the least <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">polluted</a> areas in Europe. Nordland extends about 500 km from <a href="/wiki/Nord-Tr%C3%B8ndelag" title="Nord-Trøndelag">Nord-Trøndelag</a> to <a href="/wiki/Troms" title="Troms">Troms</a>. The distance by road from <a href="/wiki/Bindal" title="Bindal">Bindal</a> in the far south of the county to <a href="/wiki/Andenes" title="Andenes">Andenes</a> on the northern tip is roughly 800 km. Nordland has a rugged coastline, with many <a href="/wiki/Fjord" title="Fjord">fjords</a>. From south to north, the main fjords are Bindalsfjord, <a href="/wiki/Vefsnfjord" title="Vefsnfjord">Vefsnfjord</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ranfjord" title="Ranfjord">Ranfjord</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saltfjord" title="Saltfjord">Saltfjord</a>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skjerstadfjord" title="Skjerstadfjord">Skjerstadfjord</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folda,_Nordland" title="Folda, Nordland">Folda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tysfjord" title="Tysfjord">Tysfjord</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ofotfjord" title="Ofotfjord">Ofotfjord</a> (the longest) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andfjord" title="Andfjord">Andfjord</a>, which is shared with Troms county. The best-known is perhaps the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vestfjorden_(Norway)" title="Vestfjorden (Norway)">Vestfjorden</a>, which is not really a fjord, but an open stretch of sea between the <a href="/wiki/Lofoten" title="Lofoten">Lofoten</a> island group and the mainland. The <a href="/wiki/Raftsundet" title="Raftsundet">Raftsundet</a> <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a>, with its famous branch <a href="/wiki/Trollfjord" title="Trollfjord">Trollfjord</a>, is the shortest waterway connecting Lofoten and Vesterålen. The <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelf</a> is very narrow west of Andenes, nowhere else in Norway is the deep ocean only a few kilometres from shore. <a href="/wiki/Saltstraumen" title="Saltstraumen">Saltstraumen</a> whirlpool is just southeast of <a href="/wiki/Bod%C3%B8" title="Bodø">Bodø</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moskenstraumen" title="Moskenstraumen">Moskenstraumen</a> is located in southern Lofoten.</p>

<p>Steep mountains near the sea and an almost flat lowland area in between the mountains and the sea (<i>Strandflaten</i>, coastal brim) is very typical for the long coastline in Nordland, and <i>Strandflaten</i> often continues out from the shore, the result is numerous islands (skerries), of which Helgeland have thousands; these islands are usually mountainous, but with smaller or larger <i>strandflate</i> areas. The southern part of Norways largest island (apart from <a href="/wiki/Svalbard" title="Svalbard">Svalbard</a>), <a href="/wiki/Hinn%C3%B8ya" title="Hinnøya">Hinnøya</a> is in Nordland, as is the third largest island, <a href="/wiki/Lang%C3%B8ya" title="Langøya">Langøya</a>. In the fjords, the coastal brim is much less developed: There might be a more gradual slope, with hills, towards the mountains, or no lowland at all. There are often <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valleys</a> at the head of fjords (the fjord is an extension of the valley), usually with a river at the centre of the valley.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Mo_i_Rana" title="Mo i Rana">Mo i Rana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mosj%C3%B8en" title="Mosjøen">Mosjøen</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Rognan" title="Rognan">Rognan</a> are situated in such valleys. Norway's second largest <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glacier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Svartisen" title="Svartisen">Svartisen</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the second largest lake, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8ssvatnet" title="Røssvatnet">Røssvatnet</a>, and the second deepest fjord, Tysfjord (897 m) are all located in Nordland. The largest river (waterflow) is <a href="/wiki/Vefsna" title="Vefsna">Vefsna</a> which forms the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Laksforsen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Laksforsen (page does not exist)">Laksforsen</a> waterfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Saltfjellet" title="Saltfjellet">Saltfjellet</a> mountain range forms a natural border between Helgeland and Salten, and is where the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a> cuts through the county. The western part of this mountain range is dominated by steep mountains and fjord inlets, with glaciers stretching towards the sea, while the eastern part of the mountains is more gentle and rounded, with some forested valleys, and is well suited for hiking. The interior of Nordland, towards the border with <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, is dominated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kj%C3%B8len_Mountains" title="Kjølen Mountains">Kjølen Mountains</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_Mountains" title="Scandinavian Mountains">Scandinavian Mountains</a>). The highest mountain in Nordland is <a href="/wiki/Oksskolten" title="Oksskolten">Oksskolten</a> (1,915 m /6,300 ft) in <a href="/wiki/Okstindan" title="Okstindan">Okstindan</a> range (<a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.hemnesturistforening.no/wp-content/uploads/2007/03/austerisen_bb.JPG" rel="nofollow">[3]</a>), the second highest is <a href="/wiki/Suliskongen" title="Suliskongen">Suliskongen</a> (1,907 m) in Fauske, and the third is <a href="/wiki/Storsteinfjellet" title="Storsteinfjellet">Storsteinfjellet</a> (1,894 m) in Narvik. <a href="/wiki/Stetind" title="Stetind">Stetind</a> in Tysfjord has been voted as Norway's national mountain.</p>
<p>There are many glaciers in the mountains, like <a href="/wiki/Bl%C3%A5mannsisen" title="Blåmannsisen">Blåmannsisen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Okstindbreen" title="Okstindbreen">Okstindbreen</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sulitjelma_Glacier" title="Sulitjelma Glacier">Sulitjelma Glacier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frostisen" title="Frostisen">Frostisen</a> - 7 of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_glaciers_in_Norway" title="List of glaciers in Norway">15 largest glaciers</a> in continental Norway are located in Nordland.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In the geological past, a collision with <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> pushed long slices of the seabed on top of the existing bedrock, today forming the bedrock from <a href="/wiki/Dovrefjell" title="Dovrefjell">Dovrefjell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trollheimen" title="Trollheimen">Trollheimen</a> south of <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a> stretching north in Trøndelag and through Nordland to justh north of <a href="/wiki/Troms%C3%B8" title="Tromsø">Tromsø</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Cambrian" title="Cambrian">Cambrian</a> - <a href="/wiki/Silur" title="Silur">Silurian</a> bedrock, much of it <a href="/wiki/Mica" title="Mica">mica</a> <a href="/wiki/Schist" title="Schist">schist</a>, is by far the largest area in Norway with soft bedrock rich in nutritions good for plant growth. It forms the bedrock in the fjord areas, while the islands off the coast and some of the easternmost areas along the border with Sweden are made up of hard bedrock (usually <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a>). In some areas, as in Tysfjord and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8rfold" title="Sørfold">Sørfold</a>, the bedrock is a mix of soft bedrock and hard granite. Much of the Lofoten mountains are of <a href="/wiki/Precambrian" title="Precambrian">precambrian</a> eruptive origin and 3.5 billion years old, among the oldest on earth. The youngest rock in Norway is on <a href="/wiki/And%C3%B8ya" title="Andøya">Andøya</a>, also known for its <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossils</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaurs</a> and other life forms. As the land was depressed by the <a href="/wiki/Ice_sheet" title="Ice sheet">ice sheet</a> in the ice age, substantial areas in the lowest altitudes was beneath the surface of the sea for thousands of years acquiring marine deposits. Due to <a href="/wiki/Post-glacial_rebound" title="Post-glacial rebound">post-glacial rebound</a>, this is now dry land, reaching 120 metres above sea level today in Saltdal, 100 m in Narvik and Brønnøysund, and 30–50 m in Lofoten and Vesterålen. <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">Limestone</a> is very common in Nordland, with many <a href="/wiki/Cave" title="Cave">caves</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> throughout the county, such as <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%B8nligrotta" title="Grønligrotta">Grønligrotta</a> in Rana. There are more caves in <a href="/wiki/Rana,_Norway" title="Rana, Norway">Rana</a> than any other area in northern Europe. In August 2006 the <a href="/wiki/Tjoarvekrajgge" title="Tjoarvekrajgge">Tjoarvekrajgge</a> cave in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8rfold" title="Sørfold">Sørfold</a> was explored and verified as the longest cave in <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> (22 km long); <a href="/wiki/Raggejavreraige" title="Raggejavreraige">Raggejavreraige</a> in Tysfjord is the deepest in Scandinavia and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Svarthamarhola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Svarthamarhola (page does not exist)">Svarthamarhola</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fauske" title="Fauske">Fauske</a> has the largest cavity. There are more than 900 caves in Nordland, one of these have a 70 m high waterfall (<a class="external text" href="http://www.dn.no/d2/reise/article1491042.ece?jgo=pos_rsiste" rel="nofollow">DN.no</a>). <a href="/wiki/Marble" title="Marble">Marble</a> is found in several locations. Fauske is sometimes referred to as the <i>marble capital</i>, and has exported marble worldwide (one customer being the UN building in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Nordland has a very mild climate for the high <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a>. Some islands, like <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Myken&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Myken (page does not exist)">Myken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tr%C3%A6na" title="Træna">Træna</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8st" title="Røst">Røst</a>, have average temperatures of c. 1 °C (33 °F) in their coldest month, 25 °C (45 °F) above average for the latitude. Nordland covers almost 5° latitude, but temperatures are moderated by proximity to the temperate sea; the annual mean only varies from 5.6 °C (42 °F) on the southern coast to 4 °C (39 °F) on the northern coast (1961–90). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_(season)" title="Summer (season)">Summers</a> are somewhat longer in the south. Winters are colder in the interior, fjords will moderate temperatures in nearby areas. Mountain areas are colder all year, with much longer winters - snow cover can persist all year at the higher mountains. Annual snow accumulation can exceed 5 m in the mountains - the main reason for the many glaciers in Nordland. Easterly winds give dry, sunny weather (the air must climb the Kjølen mountains), with warmth in summer and cold, clear air in winter. Southwesterly winds are common, bringing moist and mild air from the Atlantic ocean. Autumn and early winter is the wettest season along the coast of Nordland, while April - June on average is the driest. The strongest winds occur in late autumn and winter, as the Atlantic low pressure systems are strongest then. High pressure weather can occur in all seasons, and in summer this brings 24-hr sunshine north of the Arctic Circle.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p><a href="/wiki/Lur%C3%B8y" title="Lurøy">Lurøy</a> (115 m), west of Saltfjell, averages 2,935 mm precipitation annually; the wettest location in North Norway and in the world at such high latitude. Some of the wettest areas along the coast (a narrow band) north to <a href="/wiki/Glomfjord" title="Glomfjord">Glomfjord</a> fulfills the climatic criteria for a <a href="/wiki/Temperate_rainforest" title="Temperate rainforest">temperate rainforest</a> (<a class="external autonumber" href="http://staffwww.fullcoll.edu/tmorris/elements_of_ecology/images/biome_graph.jpg" rel="nofollow">[4]</a>). East of the mountains, <a href="/wiki/Saltdal" title="Saltdal">Saltdal</a> has a mean annual precipitation of less than 300 mm. Narvik in the northern part of Nordland averages 830 mm. Temperatures will generally drop 0.7 °C (1 °F) as elevation increases by 100 m. With mountains in almost all areas, <a href="/wiki/Alpine_tundra" title="Alpine tundra">alpine tundra</a> is common in Nordland.</p>
<p>Recent years have tended to be warmer in Nordland than the fairly cold 1961 - 1990 base period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Glomfjord in <a href="/wiki/Mel%C3%B8y" title="Meløy">Meløy</a>, approximately halfway up the coast of Nordland, has a long climate record and is situated in a rural area with little or no <a href="/wiki/Urban_heat_island" title="Urban heat island">urban heat island</a> effect, and is used as an official climate reference station. Glomfjord here represents the current climate in coastal areas (summers usually slightly warmer, winters often significantly colder in the inland).</p>

<p>Research using <a href="/wiki/Sediment" title="Sediment">sediment</a> in lakes near the Okstind Glacier has shown that the summer climate in Nordland was up to 2.5 °C warmer 9,000 to 6,000 years ago, and then slowly cooled - it was 0.5 °C warmer 2,000 years before present (see <a href="/wiki/Holocene_climatic_optimum" title="Holocene climatic optimum">Holocene climatic optimum</a>.). This research also concluded that the eastern Okstind Glacier did not melt completely during this warm period, the first glacier in Norway known to have survived since the <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">Ice age</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Light">Light</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Light">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The light conditions vary considerably from north to south; <a href="/wiki/Andenes" title="Andenes">Andenes</a> in the north will have midnight sun from 22 May to 20 July, and the sun is below the horizon from 28 November to 16 January (<a class="external text" href="http://www.gaisma.com/en/location/narvik.html" rel="nofollow">Narvik daylight</a>). In Bodø, the sun is above the horizon from 3 June to 8 July. Helgeland is situated south of the Arctic Circle: At <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">winter solstice</a> the sun is above the horizon approximately 3 hours a day (<a class="external text" href="http://www.gaisma.com/en/location/mosjoen.html" rel="nofollow">Mosjøen daylight</a>). There is not a true midnight sun in Helgeland, although the upper part of the sun disc will be above the horizon all night in June as far south as Mosjøen. With the transitional period with a short dusk included, there are three full months (from early May to early August) without darkness in Nordland.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature">Nature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Puffin2-1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="343" data-file-width="500" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Puffin2-1.jpg/220px-Puffin2-1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Puffin2-1.jpg/330px-Puffin2-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Puffin2-1.jpg/440px-Puffin2-1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Puffin2-1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8st" title="Røst">Røst</a> has the largest seabird colonies along the Norwegian coast.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The sea along the coast has a rich marine life, and the <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a> fisheries in Lofoten has lasted for more than 1000 years. In addition to <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pollachius_virens" title="Pollachius virens">coalfish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haddock" title="Haddock">haddock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">herring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anarhichadidae" title="Anarhichadidae">wolf fish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halibut" title="Halibut">halibut</a> are all common along the coast and in the fjords. Nordland also features many lakes. The largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deep_water_coral" title="Deep water coral">deep water coral</a> reef (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lophelia_pertusa" title="Lophelia pertusa">Lophelia pertusa</a>) in the world, the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8st_Reef" title="Røst Reef">Røst Reef</a>, 40 km long, is located west of Røst, and protected from <a href="/wiki/Trawling" title="Trawling">trawling</a> since 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The coast of Nordland has the highest density of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_tailed_sea_eagle" title="White tailed sea eagle">sea eagles</a> in Europe. There are millions of seabirds; the islands of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8st" title="Røst">Røst</a> has the largest colonies in Norway with about one-quarter of all seabirds in continental Norway, probably most famous for the <a href="/wiki/Puffin" title="Puffin">puffins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cormorant" title="Cormorant">cormorants</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lovund" title="Lovund">Lovund</a> also has a well-known colony of puffins.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sjurfjellet4.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1280" data-file-width="960" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Sjurfjellet4.jpg/220px-Sjurfjellet4.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Sjurfjellet4.jpg/330px-Sjurfjellet4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Sjurfjellet4.jpg/440px-Sjurfjellet4.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sjurfjellet4.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Marked hiking route in <a href="/wiki/Junkerdal_National_Park" title="Junkerdal National Park">Junkerdal National Park</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Otter" title="Otter">Otters</a> are very common along the coast and fjords, as is <a href="/wiki/Harbour_porpoise" title="Harbour porpoise">harbour porpoise</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harbour_seal" title="Harbour seal">harbour seals</a>; there are also <a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">seals</a>. The largest fish in the sea is the <a href="/wiki/Basking_shark" title="Basking shark">basking shark</a>, which were hunted in earlier days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The character birds along the coast, common in all areas including the fjords, are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_gull" title="Sea gull">sea gulls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_oystercatcher" title="Eurasian oystercatcher">Eurasian oystercatcher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arctic_tern" title="Arctic tern">Arctic terns</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Grey_heron" title="Grey heron">grey heron</a> has also become common. The <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_eagle-owl" title="Eurasian eagle-owl">Eurasian eagle-owl</a> has a stronghold along the Helgeland coast; predator birds like <a href="/wiki/Golden_eagle" title="Golden eagle">golden eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gyrfalcon" title="Gyrfalcon">gyrfalcon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_falcon" title="Peregrine falcon">peregrine falcon</a> nest in some inaccessible areas. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orca" title="Orca">Orcas</a> are common along the coast and in the Vestfjord-area (even in the fjords) in winter, and the world's largest predator, the <a href="/wiki/Sperm_whale" title="Sperm whale">sperm whale</a>, hunt for prey in the deep waters west of <a href="/wiki/And%C3%B8ya" title="Andøya">Andøya</a>. Tysfjord and Folda fjord is home to the worlds northernmost <a href="/wiki/Homarus_gammarus" title="Homarus gammarus">lobster</a> population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ahma_(Gulo_gulo)_10_kallerna.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1535" data-file-width="1770" height="191" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Ahma_%28Gulo_gulo%29_10_kallerna.jpg/220px-Ahma_%28Gulo_gulo%29_10_kallerna.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Ahma_%28Gulo_gulo%29_10_kallerna.jpg/330px-Ahma_%28Gulo_gulo%29_10_kallerna.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Ahma_%28Gulo_gulo%29_10_kallerna.jpg/440px-Ahma_%28Gulo_gulo%29_10_kallerna.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ahma_(Gulo_gulo)_10_kallerna.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The long inland mountain range with alpine tundra and subarctic forest is part of the core area for <a href="/wiki/Wolverine" title="Wolverine">wolverine</a> in western Europe.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norway_spruce" title="Norway spruce">Spruce</a> forest expansion have been blocked by Saltfjell and Kjølen mountains, thus spruce forest naturally grows only in Helgeland, but are commonly planted in the whole county for economic reasons, sparking some debate. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sitka_spruce" title="Sitka spruce">Sitka spruce</a> is also commonly planted in Nordland, particularly in coastal areas. The coastal areas belong to the <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_coastal_conifer_forests" title="Scandinavian coastal conifer forests">Scandinavian coastal conifer forests</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a>; some small areas have been classified as rainforest using botanical criteria as well as precipitation data. Inland areas up to the conifer treeline belong to the <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_and_Russian_taiga" title="Scandinavian and Russian taiga">Scandinavian and Russian taiga</a> ecoregion, while the highlands and mountains belong to the <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_Montane_Birch_forest_and_grasslands" title="Scandinavian Montane Birch forest and grasslands">Scandinavian Montane Birch forest and grasslands</a> ecoregion.</p>
<p>Other common trees in Nordland are <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rowan" title="Rowan">rowan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willow" title="Willow">willow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alnus_incana" title="Alnus incana">grey alder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prunus_padus" title="Prunus padus">bird cherry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aspen" title="Aspen">aspen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scots_pine" title="Scots pine">Scots pine</a>, more rarely seen is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wych_elm" title="Wych elm">wych elm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corylus_avellana" title="Corylus avellana">common hazel</a> (elm north to <a href="/wiki/Beiarn" title="Beiarn">Beiarn</a>, hazel north to <a href="/wiki/Steigen" title="Steigen">Steigen</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Small-leaved_lime" title="Small-leaved lime">small-leaved lime</a> north to Brønnøy, <a href="/wiki/Malus_sylvestris" title="Malus sylvestris">forest apple</a> north to <a href="/wiki/Moskenes" title="Moskenes">Moskenes</a>). The Nordland <a href="/wiki/Whitebeam" title="Whitebeam">whitebeam</a> (<i>Nordlandsasal</i>) is one of very few <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> trees in Norway, and only grows in <a href="/wiki/Bindal" title="Bindal">Bindal</a> in Nordland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the mountain areas in the interior, <a href="/wiki/Reindeer" title="Reindeer">reindeer</a> can be seen (these have <a href="/wiki/Sami_people" title="Sami people">Sami</a> owners), hunted by the indigenous wolverine. There are also a few <a href="/wiki/Brown_bear" title="Brown bear">brown bears</a> in the interior. The <a href="/wiki/Arctic_fox" title="Arctic fox">Arctic fox</a> is now in danger of extinction on the mainland, but a few are left in these mountain areas, particularly in <a href="/wiki/B%C3%B8rgefjell_National_Park" title="Børgefjell National Park">Børgefjell</a> mountains. <a href="/wiki/Red_fox" title="Red fox">Red foxes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moose" title="Moose">moose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hare" title="Hare">hares</a>, <a href="/wiki/Squirrel" title="Squirrel">squirrels</a>, small <a href="/wiki/Rodent" title="Rodent">rodents</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_pine_marten" title="European pine marten">pine marten</a> (inland valleys) and <a href="/wiki/Stoat" title="Stoat">stoats</a> are all common in the forests. As of 2008, there were a minimum of 50 <a href="/wiki/Lynx" title="Lynx">lynx</a> in Nordland<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> residing in the forests in all Nordland except Lofoten and Vesterålen. In lowland areas, <a href="/wiki/Roe_deer" title="Roe deer">roe deers</a> now occur in most of Nordland, and <a href="/wiki/Red_deer" title="Red deer">red deers</a> are seen in the southern part of Nordland. The <a href="/wiki/European_badger" title="European badger">badger</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Vipera_berus" title="Vipera berus">common viper</a> has their most northerly habitat in southerly coastal areas of Nordland. <a href="/wiki/Gray_wolf" title="Gray wolf">Grey wolves</a> have been observed in inland areas of <a href="/wiki/Helgeland" title="Helgeland">Helgeland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> There are few species of <a href="/wiki/Amphibian" title="Amphibian">amphibians</a>, although the <a href="/wiki/Common_frog" title="Common frog">common frog</a> is common in all Nordland except mountain areas, and the <a href="/wiki/Smooth_newt" title="Smooth newt">smooth newt</a> has its most northerly habitat in the world in the river <a href="/wiki/Vefsna" title="Vefsna">Vefsna</a>. There are seven national parks in, or partly in, Nordland. From south to north, these are <a href="/wiki/B%C3%B8rgefjell_National_Park" title="Børgefjell National Park">Børgefjell National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lomsdal%E2%80%93Visten_National_Park" title="Lomsdal–Visten National Park">Lomsdal–Visten National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saltfjellet%E2%80%93Svartisen_National_Park" title="Saltfjellet–Svartisen National Park">Saltfjellet–Svartisen National Park</a>, Junkerdal National Park, <a href="/wiki/Rago_National_Park" title="Rago National Park">Rago National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sjunkhatten_National_Park" title="Sjunkhatten National Park">Sjunkhatten National Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B8ysalen_National_Park" title="Møysalen National Park">Møysalen National Park</a>. The national park centre for Nordland is situated in Saltdal.</p>

<p>The key industries are fisheries and offshore petroleum exploration. Nordland is well known for the fishing of <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">codfish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fish_farming" title="Fish farming">fish farming</a> of <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">salmon</a>. Main export markets are <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> is important, mainly in the summer season, although there are some winter visitors looking for good <a href="/wiki/Skiing" title="Skiing">skiing</a> or wanting to see the Northern Lights, especially from February to April. Tourists are attracted by the scenic coast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> especially Lofoten, which is also visited by many cruise ships in the summer, while the rest of the county often is ignored by tourists. Mountain hiking is popular among natives and some tourists.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Whale_watching" title="Whale watching">Whale watching</a> attracts tourists to <a href="/wiki/And%C3%B8y" title="Andøy">Andøy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tysfjord" title="Tysfjord">Tysfjord</a> /<a href="/wiki/L%C3%B8dingen" title="Lødingen">Lødingen</a> /<a href="/wiki/Svolv%C3%A6r" title="Svolvær">Svolvær</a> area, and fishing is also popular along the coast and in the <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">salmon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trout" title="Trout">trout</a> rivers; there is also <a href="/wiki/Arctic_char" title="Arctic char">Arctic char</a> in some rivers. Saltstraumen has the world record for coalfish using a <a href="/wiki/Fishing_rod" title="Fishing rod">fishing rod</a>, and Røst has the world record for halibut, 202 kg (<a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.aftenposten.no/english/local/article2424400.ece" rel="nofollow">[5]</a>).</p>

<p>Farming is another regional economy, and consists mainly of <a href="/wiki/Dairy_farming" title="Dairy farming">dairy farming</a> and livestock such as <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a>, and domesticated reindeer graze the inland highlands. There is also some forestry, particularly in the Helgeland district, but also further north. In earlier days, grain was grown in Nordland (mostly <a href="/wiki/Barley" title="Barley">barley</a>, some <a href="/wiki/Oat" title="Oat">oats</a>). <a href="/wiki/Nordlandshest/Lyngshest" title="Nordlandshest/Lyngshest">Nordlandshest</a> is the smallest of the three Norwegian horse breeds. The <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Lundehund" title="Norwegian Lundehund">Norwegian Lundehund</a> was bred to hunt <a href="/wiki/Puffin" title="Puffin">puffins</a> and only saved from extinction due to a few remaining dogs in Lofoten.</p>
<p>There is a long history of mining. <a href="/wiki/Sulitjelma" title="Sulitjelma">Sulitjelma</a> has the largest <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> deposits in the county, as well as <a href="/wiki/Pyrite" title="Pyrite">pyrite</a>, the latter is also found in several other locations, but the mining here has been discontinued for economical reasons. In earlier days, <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a> was mined in the <a href="/wiki/Dunderland_Valley" title="Dunderland Valley">Dunderland Valley</a> in Rana, and until 2002 <a href="/wiki/Nickel" title="Nickel">nickel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivine" title="Olivine">olivine</a> was mined in <a href="/wiki/Ballangen" title="Ballangen">Ballangen</a>. There are several limestone, marble, and <a href="/wiki/Dolostone" title="Dolostone">dolostone</a> quarries, <a href="/wiki/Dolomite" title="Dolomite">dolomite</a> in Vefsn, Fauske, Sørfold and Ballangen. As of 2008, mining of <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bindal" title="Bindal">Bindal</a> is in testing stages (<a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.gexco.se/website1/sd_art-s1/86/Verdens%20Gang.pdf" rel="nofollow">[6]</a>). The port of <a href="/wiki/Narvik" title="Narvik">Narvik</a> has a direct rail connection to the well known and profitable <a href="/wiki/Kiruna" title="Kiruna">Kiruna</a>-<a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4llivare" title="Gällivare">Gällivare</a> <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a>-ore fields in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. There are many dams for hydroelectric power. Nordland has an increasing diverse economy, with fledgling research and development in aerospace and space exploration at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/And%C3%B8ya_Rocket_Range" title="Andøya Rocket Range">Andøya Rocket Range</a>, which primarily is known for its satellite launches. This vast province, Norway's second largest, almost the size of <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, traditionally was very important for NATO, and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Norwegian_Air_Force" title="Royal Norwegian Air Force">Royal Norwegian Air Force</a> has two squadrons of <a href="/wiki/General_Dynamics_F-16_Fighting_Falcon" title="General Dynamics F-16 Fighting Falcon">F-16</a> fighters stationed at <a href="/wiki/Bod%C3%B8_Airport" title="Bodø Airport">Bodø Airport</a>, and all its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P-3_Orion" title="P-3 Orion">P-3 Orion</a> maritime surveillance aircraft stationed at <a href="/wiki/And%C3%B8ya_Air_Station" title="Andøya Air Station">Andøya Air Station</a>. The decommissioning of closing military bases has led to a regional shift towards a new knowledge based economy.</p>

<p>There are local hospitals in <a href="/wiki/Mosj%C3%B8en" title="Mosjøen">Mosjøen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandnessj%C3%B8en" title="Sandnessjøen">Sandnessjøen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mo_i_Rana" title="Mo i Rana">Mo i Rana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bod%C3%B8" title="Bodø">Bodø</a> (the largest), <a href="/wiki/Gravdal,_Nordland" title="Gravdal, Nordland">Gravdal</a>, Narvik and <a href="/wiki/Stokmarknes" title="Stokmarknes">Stokmarknes</a>.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Bod%C3%B8_Airport" title="Bodø Airport">Bodø Airport</a> is the busiest airport, and a hub for many smaller airports in Nordland. <a href="/wiki/Harstad/Narvik_Airport,_Evenes" title="Harstad/Narvik Airport, Evenes">Harstad/Narvik Airport, Evenes</a> in the north also has non-stop flights to <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_route_E06" title="European route E06">E6</a> runs along the entire length of Nordland. There are many tunnels and bridges; some of the largest are <a href="/wiki/Helgeland_Bridge" title="Helgeland Bridge">Helgeland Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gims%C3%B8ystraumen_Bridge" title="Gimsøystraumen Bridge">Gimsøystraumen Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raftsund_Bridge" title="Raftsund Bridge">Raftsund Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skjomen_Bridge" title="Skjomen Bridge">Skjomen Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rombak_Bridge" title="Rombak Bridge">Rombak Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hadsel_Bridge" title="Hadsel Bridge">Hadsel Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sortland_Bridge" title="Sortland Bridge">Sortland Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/And%C3%B8y_Bridge" title="Andøy Bridge">Andøy Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saltstraumen_Bridge" title="Saltstraumen Bridge">Saltstraumen Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kjellingstraumen_Bridge" title="Kjellingstraumen Bridge">Kjellingstraumen Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8nn%C3%B8ysund_Bridge" title="Brønnøysund Bridge">Brønnøysund Bridge</a>. Even with improved communications, population numbers in Nordland has actually decreased slightly since 1990, as many young people move to larger cities in Norway. <a href="/wiki/Bod%C3%B8" title="Bodø">Bodø</a> is the only municipality in the county with a significant growth in population.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>There is evidence of human settlement in Nordland as far back as 10,500 years ago, about as early as in southern Norway. These <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a> people lived near the coast, often on islands and typically along <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">straits</a> near the open sea, with a rich provision of marine resources. Such archeological evidence has been found on <a href="/wiki/Vega,_Norway" title="Vega, Norway">Vega</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Leirfjord" title="Leirfjord">Leirfjord</a> and along <a href="/wiki/Saltstraumen" title="Saltstraumen">Saltstraumen</a>. There are at least 15 locations with prehistoric <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_carvings_in_Norway" title="Rock carvings in Norway">rock carvings</a> in Nordland, from Helgeland in the south to Narvik in the north (see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fosna-Hensbacka_culture" title="Fosna-Hensbacka culture">Fosna-Hensbacka culture</a>).</p>
<p>Some of the oldest houses known in Norway were excavated on Langhågan on the island <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sanna,_Tr%C3%A6na&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sanna, Træna (page does not exist)">Sanna</a> in Træna municipality;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> the oldest house was oval, 6 x 4.5 m, and is dated to 4000 BC. A 65 m long stone paved "<a href="/wiki/Trail" title="Trail">trail</a>" from the house down to a small harbour, today 23 m above sea level, is still visible.</p>
<p>The 4,600-year-old <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_drawings" title="Rock drawings">rock drawings</a> at <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8d%C3%B8y" title="Rødøy">Rødøy</a>, which depict a man on skis holding a stick, is the oldest known reference of skis being used. The location is somewhat surprising, as this island has unreliable snow cover, and the Scandinavian climate was warmer in the Stone Age. This rock drawing was used as a pictogram in the <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer" title="Lillehammer">Lillehammer</a> <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> in 1994.</p>
<p>The first agricultural culture has been dated to the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>. This culture left large burial cairns close to the sea, for instance in <a href="/wiki/Steigen" title="Steigen">Steigen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vestv%C3%A5g%C3%B8y" title="Vestvågøy">Vestvågøy</a>, and the northernmost location is around <a href="/wiki/Harstad" title="Harstad">Harstad</a> in southern Troms. These locations have significant areas of lowland suited for agriculture, they are close to the sea and they have many natural harbours.</p>

<p>The oldest remains of a boat ever found in Norway was discovered in a <a href="/wiki/Bog" title="Bog">bog</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8mna" title="Sømna">Sømna</a>. Known as <i>Haugvikbåten</i>, the well preserved piece, kept in the Museum of Science (<i>Vitenskapsmuseet</i>) in Trondheim, has been dated to 800 - 400 BC - the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_Bronze_Age" title="Nordic Bronze Age">Nordic Bronze Age</a> (<a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ntnu.no/vmuseet/fakark/forvaltning/Haugvikbaten%202006/haugvikbaten.html" rel="nofollow">[7]</a>).</p>
<p>For many generations Hålogaland had been the northernmost area of <a href="/wiki/Norsemen" title="Norsemen">Norse</a> settlement. The remnants of large longhouses near Borg (Vestvågøy) and in Steigen are dated to the Merovinger period (ca 600 AD). There are substantial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archeological" title="Archeological">archeological</a> evidence of a Norse <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a>-based culture along the coast from approximately 200 AD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>Nordland is regarded as part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C3%A1pmi_(area)" title="Sápmi (area)">Sápmi</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Sami_people" title="Sami people">Sami</a>, which are not of <a href="/wiki/Norsemen" title="Norsemen">Norse</a> origins, have lived in Nordland for at least 2000 years. Not just inland, but also along the fjords and, in the northern part of Nordland, even on the coast and larger islands, such as Hinnøya. There is a story in <a href="/wiki/Heimskringla" title="Heimskringla">Heimskringla</a> about a Viking which became a contender for the throne, <a href="/wiki/Sigurd_Slembe" title="Sigurd Slembe">Sigurd Slembe</a>. He was on the run from the king, and was helped by Sami in what is probably <a href="/wiki/L%C3%B8dingen" title="Lødingen">Lødingen</a> municipality today. The sami even built him a boat, which the saga mention was made from pine <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a> (<a href="/wiki/Viking_ships" title="Viking ships">viking ships</a> were typically made from oak wood, but oak does not grow naturally this far north). Tysfjord today is a centre for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lule_Sami" title="Lule Sami">Lule Sami</a> culture.</p>
<p>The current county of Nordland was part of the <a href="/wiki/Petty_kingdom" title="Petty kingdom">petty kingdom</a> of <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A5logaland" title="Hålogaland">Hålogaland</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Viking_Age" title="Viking Age">Viking era</a>. This kingdom also included the southern part of <a href="/wiki/Troms" title="Troms">Troms</a>.</p>
<p>In January 1432, an Italian trade ship going from <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a> to <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> was hit by a storm and taken far north where it was wrecked; some of the crew survived and came ashore on a small island near <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8st" title="Røst">Røst</a>. They were taken care of by the people of Røst. The story, told by one of the men, <a href="/wiki/Pietro_Querini" title="Pietro Querini">Pietro Querini</a>, gives one of very few descriptions of life in Nordland in this time period (<a class="external text" href="http://home.online.no/~boethius/senja/rost.htm" rel="nofollow">Querini - Norw txt</a>). Querini sees the people of Røst (in all 120 people) as very trusting and good <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic" title="Catholic">Catholics</a>, <i>the inner circle of paradise</i>. He also mentions a German priest, which they could talk to in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>. People lived from fishing cod and halibut, which they traded for other goods (like grain) in <a href="/wiki/Bergen" title="Bergen">Bergen</a> - the cod as <a href="/wiki/Stockfish" title="Stockfish">stockfish</a>. Each family also had about 5 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cow" title="Cow">cows</a>, and collected eggs from semi-domesticated <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">ducks</a>. The shipwrecked men did not lack food during their winter stay, usually fish, but Querini also mention, milk, meat, butter and pancakes. The people of Røst lived in round, wooden houses, and mostly used clothes made of <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a>. By late May, sailing in 24-hr daylight, the Italians were taken south to Bergen.</p>

<p>The largest ship disaster ever in Norway took place in 1944 when <a href="/wiki/MS_Rigel" title="MS Rigel"><i>Rigel</i></a> transporting German prisoners was bombed by RAF near <a href="/wiki/Sandnessj%C3%B8en" title="Sandnessjøen">Sandnessjøen</a> (Alstahaug), with more than 2,500 casualties. The ship was grounded on Rosøya, where it remained, half sunk, until it was demolished in 1970.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Petter_Dass" title="Petter Dass">Petter Dass</a> lived in <a href="/wiki/Alstahaug" title="Alstahaug">Alstahaug</a>, and the Nobel laureate author <a href="/wiki/Knut_Hamsun" title="Knut Hamsun">Knut Hamsun</a> grew up in <a href="/wiki/Hamar%C3%B8y" title="Hamarøy">Hamarøy</a> after his family moved there when he was 3 years old.</p>
<p>Nordland county is identical with the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_S%C3%B8r-H%C3%A5logaland" title="Diocese of Sør-Hålogaland">Diocese of Sør-Hålogaland</a>.</p>
