<p><b>Norman Mark Reedus</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_Reedus_FTM_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Norman_Reedus_FTM-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> (born January 6, 1969) is an American actor and model,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_Reedus_FTM_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Norman_Reedus_FTM-1">[1]</a></sup> best known for his role as <a href="/wiki/Daryl_Dixon" title="Daryl Dixon">Daryl Dixon</a> on the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_fiction" title="Horror fiction">horror</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and as Murphy MacManus in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boondock_Saints" title="The Boondock Saints">The Boondock Saints</a></i>. He has also acted in numerous films, appeared in and created several videos, provided video game voiceovers, and modeled for various <a href="/wiki/Fashion_design" title="Fashion design">fashion designers</a> (most recognizably <a href="/wiki/Prada" title="Prada">Prada</a> in the 1990s).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_Reedus_FTM_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Norman_Reedus_FTM-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-home_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-home-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Reedus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reedus was born in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Florida" title="Hollywood, Florida">Hollywood, Florida</a>, the son of Marianne (<i>née</i> Yarber), a teacher, and Ira Norman Reedus. He is of <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup> He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles, California</a>, and also lived in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He worked at a <a href="/wiki/Harley-Davidson" title="Harley-Davidson">Harley-Davidson</a> shop in <a href="/wiki/Venice,_Los_Angeles" title="Venice, Los Angeles">Venice, California</a>, and contributed artwork to various shows as a painter, photographer, sculptor, and video artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_Reedus_FTM_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Norman_Reedus_FTM-1">[1]</a></sup> He first acted in the play <i>Maps for Drowners</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Tiffany_Theater" title="Tiffany Theater">Tiffany Theater</a> on <a href="/wiki/Sunset_Boulevard" title="Sunset Boulevard">Sunset Boulevard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He was discovered at a party in Los Angeles, where someone asked him if he wanted to act in a play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Reedus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Reedus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Reedus' first film was in the 1997 Guillermo del Toro horror thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/Mimic_(film)" title="Mimic (film)">Mimic</a></i>, where he played the character Jeremy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup> He also had roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Harbor" title="Dark Harbor">Dark Harbor</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Ways_to_Sunday" title="Six Ways to Sunday">Six Ways to Sunday</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Deuces_Wild" title="Deuces Wild">Deuces Wild</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_II" title="Blade II">Blade II</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gossip_(2000_American_film)" title="Gossip (2000 American film)">Gossip</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/8mm_(film)" title="8mm (film)">8mm</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gangster_(film)" title="American Gangster (film)">American Gangster</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hero_Wanted" title="Hero Wanted">Hero Wanted</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Moscow_Chill" title="Moscow Chill">Moscow Chill</a></i>. In 2005, he had a bit part in the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Alvart" title="Christian Alvart">Christian Alvart</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Antibodies_(film)" title="Antibodies (film)">Antibodies</a></i> as a German policeman. In 2008, he starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Canyon_(2008_film)" title="Red Canyon (2008 film)">Red Canyon</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-home_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-home-6">[6]</a></sup> Reedus played Murphy MacManus in the 1999 movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boondock_Saints" title="The Boondock Saints">The Boondock Saints</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He reprised the role in the 2009 sequel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boondock_Saints_II:_All_Saints_Day" title="The Boondock Saints II: All Saints Day">The Boondock Saints II: All Saints Day</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He guest-starred on <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i> as Paige's (<a href="/wiki/Rose_McGowan" title="Rose McGowan">Rose McGowan</a>) boyfriend, Nate. Reedus also starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_Herman" title="Hello Herman">Hello Herman</a></i>, which opened nationwide and <a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">on-demand</a> on June 7, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watch_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-watch-12">[12]</a></sup> Reedus currently stars in the reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Ride_with_Norman_Reedus" title="Ride with Norman Reedus">Ride with Norman Reedus</a></i>, which premiered on June 12, 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Walking_Dead"><i>The Walking Dead</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Reedus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Walking Dead">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2010, Reedus began playing <a href="/wiki/Daryl_Dixon" title="Daryl Dixon">Daryl Dixon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i>, a horror drama about a group of friends and family members who fight to survive in a violent apocalyptic world populated with flesh-eating zombies and the few surviving humans, some of whom are even more diabolical and dangerous than the zombies themselves. The character was not originally in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(comics)" title="The Walking Dead (comics)">comic book series of the same name</a>, but was created specifically for Reedus after his audition for the character of <a href="/wiki/Merle_Dixon" title="Merle Dixon">Merle Dixon</a>. <i>The Walking Dead</i> comic creator <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kirkman" title="Robert Kirkman">Robert Kirkman</a> feels "absolutely blessed Reedus has honored the show with his presence, and the way he has come in and taken over that role and defined Daryl Dixon. A lot of Reedus's portrayal of the character in the <a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(season_1)" title="The Walking Dead (season 1)">first season</a> inspired all the writers to do what we did with him in the <a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(season_2)" title="The Walking Dead (season 2)">second season</a>. We love writing him and end up doing cool stuff with him." The drama has evolved into the highest-rated in cable history, smashing all previous records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Reedus was nominated for a 2012 <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actor">Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actor</a> for his portrayal of Dixon.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modeling.2C_music_videos.2C_and_art">Modeling, music videos, and art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Reedus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Modeling, music videos, and art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reedus has modeled for <a href="/wiki/Prada" title="Prada">Prada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Dell%27Acqua" title="Alessandro Dell'Acqua">Alessandro Dell'Acqua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">Durban</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Levi%27s" title="Levi's">Levi's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lexus" title="Lexus">Lexus</a>, and Morgenthal Fredrics. In 2015 Reedus posed for a <a href="/wiki/Cruelty_Free_International" title="Cruelty Free International">Cruelty Free International</a> ad in support of ending cosmetic tests on animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the mid-1990s, while working as a model, he appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> for "Wicked As It Seems" by <a href="/wiki/Keith_Richards" title="Keith Richards">Keith Richards</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Violently_Happy" title="Violently Happy">Violently Happy</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Flat_Top_(song)" title="Flat Top (song)">Flat Top</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Goo_Goo_Dolls" title="Goo Goo Dolls">Goo Goo Dolls</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Cat%27s_in_the_Cradle" title="Cat's in the Cradle">Cat's in the Cradle</a>" covered by <a href="/wiki/Ugly_Kid_Joe" title="Ugly Kid Joe">Ugly Kid Joe</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Strange_Currencies" title="Strange Currencies">Strange Currencies</a>" by <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Fake_Plastic_Trees" title="Fake Plastic Trees">Fake Plastic Trees</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>. In 1999 he appeared in the video for "Mean to Me" by <a href="/wiki/Tonic_(band)" title="Tonic (band)">Tonic</a>. Since achieving fame as an actor, he has also appeared in the videos for "<a href="/wiki/Judas_(Lady_Gaga_song)" title="Judas (Lady Gaga song)">Judas</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>, "Sun Down" by <a href="/wiki/Tricky_(musician)" title="Tricky (musician)">Tricky</a> (ft. Tirzah), "Gypsy Woman" by <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Duff" title="Hilary Duff">Hilary Duff</a> and "<a href="/wiki/No_Cities_to_Love" title="No Cities to Love">No Cities to Love</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Sleater-Kinney" title="Sleater-Kinney">Sleater-Kinney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is also a painter, sculptor, and photographer who currently shows his artwork in galleries in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-home_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-home-6">[6]</a></sup> He released a book of photography on October 31, 2013, called <i>The Sun's Coming Up... Like A Big Bald Head</i>, and in September 2014 announced a fan art compilation called <i>Thanks For All The Niceness</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Reedus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reedus reprised his <i>Walking Dead</i> character of Daryl Dixon in the 2013 video game <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead:_Survival_Instinct" title="The Walking Dead: Survival Instinct">The Walking Dead: Survival Instinct</a></i>, which is set within the universe of the television series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cvg-survivalinstinct_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-cvg-survivalinstinct-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reedus was to portray the main character in <a href="/wiki/Hideo_Kojima" title="Hideo Kojima">Hideo Kojima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_del_Toro" title="Guillermo del Toro">Guillermo del Toro</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Hills" title="Silent Hills">Silent Hills</a></i>—a horror game that was to serve as a reboot of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silent_Hill_(series)" title="Silent Hill (series)">Silent Hill</a></i> franchise. However, as a result of Kojima's exit from his previous employer, <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">Konami</a>, <i>Silent Hills</i> was effectively cancelled in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ars-hillscancelled_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ars-hillscancelled-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Reedus would ultimately resurface in a new Kojima project, <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Stranding" title="Death Stranding">Death Stranding</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gs-stranding_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-gs-stranding-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Reedus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reedus was in a five year relationship with supermodel <a href="/wiki/Helena_Christensen" title="Helena Christensen">Helena Christensen</a> from 1998 until 2003. Despite reports to the contrary, the two were never married.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Together they have a son, Mingus Lucien Reedus (born October 13, 1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The two have remained friends and share joint custody of their son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
