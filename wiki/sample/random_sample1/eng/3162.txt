<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mathematics">Mathematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Normal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mathematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are many unrelated notions of "normality" in mathematics. Non-mathematical subjects very frequently refer to <a href="/wiki/Normal_(geometry)" title="Normal (geometry)">Normal (geometry)</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Normal_Distribution" title="Normal Distribution">Normal Distribution</a>; see also the disambiguation pages for <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Normal_form_(disambiguation)" title="Normal form (disambiguation)">Normal form</a> and <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Normalization_(disambiguation)" title="Normalization (disambiguation)">Normalization</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Algebra_and_Number_Theory">Algebra and Number Theory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Normal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Algebra and Number Theory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Normal_subgroup" title="Normal subgroup">Normal subgroup</a>, a subgroup invariant under conjugation</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Normal_extension" title="Normal extension">Normal extensions</a> (or quasi-Galois) field extensions, splitting fields for a set of polynomials over the base field</li>
<li>Normal domain (<a href="/wiki/Integrally_closed_domain" title="Integrally closed domain">integrally closed domain</a>), a ring integrally closed in its fraction field
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Normal_ring" title="Normal ring">Normal ring</a>, a reduced ring whose localizations at prime ideals are integrally closed domains</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Normal_scheme" title="Normal scheme">Normal scheme</a>, an algebraic variety or scheme that meets certain conditions</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Normal variety, a projective variety embedded by a complete linear system, as in a <a href="/wiki/Rational_normal_scroll" title="Rational normal scroll">rational normal scroll</a> (unrelated to the concept of normal scheme above)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Normal_basis" title="Normal basis">Normal basis</a> (of a Galois extension), used heavily in cryptography</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Normal_order_of_an_arithmetic_function" title="Normal order of an arithmetic function">Normal order of an arithmetic function</a>, a type of asymptotic behavior useful in number theory</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Normal_degree" title="Normal degree">Normal degree</a>, a rational curve on a surface that meets certain conditions</li>
</ul>
