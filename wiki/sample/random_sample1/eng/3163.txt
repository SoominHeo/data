<p><b>Nomeidae</b>, the <b>driftfishes</b>, are a family of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perciform" title="Perciform">perciform</a> fishes found in tropical and subtropical waters throughout the world. The family includes about 16 species. The largest species, such as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_fathead&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cape fathead (page does not exist)">Cape fathead</a>, <i>Cubiceps capensis</i>, reach 1 m in length.</p>
<p>Several species are found in association with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siphonophore" title="Siphonophore">siphonophores</a> (which are colonies of tiny individual animals that have specialised functions which resemble jellyfish) such as the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_man_o%27_war" title="Portuguese man o' war">Portuguese man o' war</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Man-of-war_fish" title="Man-of-war fish">man-of-war fish</a>, <i>Nomeus gronovii</i>, is known to eat its tentacles and gonads, as well as feeding on other <a href="/wiki/Jellyfish" title="Jellyfish">jellyfishes</a>. Other species of driftfishes are associated with the floating seaweed <i><a href="/wiki/Sargassum" title="Sargassum">Sargassum</a></i>. The Cape fathead feeds mainly on <a href="/wiki/Salp" title="Salp">salps</a>. Some species of <i><a href="/wiki/Cubiceps" title="Cubiceps">Cubiceps</a></i> are occasionally caught on pelagic longlines set for swordfish.</p>
