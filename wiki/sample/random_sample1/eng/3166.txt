<p><b>Noomi Rapace</b> (<small>Swedish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[ˈnoːmɪ raˈpɑːs]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> née <b>Norén</b>; born 28 December 1979) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_people" title="Swedish people">Swedish</a> actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olsson2012_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Olsson2012-3">[3]</a></sup> She achieved fame with her portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Lisbeth_Salander" title="Lisbeth Salander">Lisbeth Salander</a> in the Swedish film adaptations of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennium_series" title="Millennium series"><i>Millennium</i> series</a>: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girl_with_the_Dragon_Tattoo_(2009_film)" title="The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (2009 film)">The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girl_Who_Played_with_Fire_(film)" title="The Girl Who Played with Fire (film)">The Girl Who Played with Fire</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girl_Who_Kicked_the_Hornets%27_Nest_(film)" title="The Girl Who Kicked the Hornets' Nest (film)">The Girl Who Kicked the Hornets' Nest</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheStar_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheStar-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2011, she was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Leading_Role" title="BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role">BAFTA Award for Best Actress</a> for her performance in <i>The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo</i>.</p>
<p>She is also known for playing Anna in <i><a href="/wiki/Daisy_Diamond" title="Daisy Diamond">Daisy Diamond</a></i> (2007), Leena in <i><a href="/wiki/Beyond_(2010_film)" title="Beyond (2010 film)">Beyond</a></i> (2010), Anna in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Monitor_(film)" title="The Monitor (film)">The Monitor</a></i> (2011), Madame Simza Heron in <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a></i> (2011), the lead role of Dr. Elizabeth Shaw in the <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a> science-fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/Prometheus_(2012_film)" title="Prometheus (2012 film)">Prometheus</a></i> (2012), Beatrice in <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Down" title="Dead Man Down">Dead Man Down</a></i> (2013), Nadia in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Drop_(film)" title="The Drop (film)">The Drop</a></i> (2014), and Raisa Demidova in <i><a href="/wiki/Child_44_(film)" title="Child 44 (film)">Child 44</a></i> (2015). Rapace will reprise her role of Dr. Elizabeth Shaw in <i><a href="/wiki/Alien:_Covenant" title="Alien: Covenant">Alien: Covenant</a></i> (2017).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noomi_Rapace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rapace was born in <a href="/wiki/Hudiksvall" title="Hudiksvall">Hudiksvall</a>, Sweden. Her mother, Nina Norén (born Kristina Norén; 1954), is a Swedish actress, and her father, Rogelio Durán (10 November 1953 – 4 November 2006), was a Spanish <a href="/wiki/Flamenco" title="Flamenco">Flamenco</a> singer from <a href="/wiki/Badajoz" title="Badajoz">Badajoz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She has said that her father may have been of part <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Romani</a> descent, and though she is "not sure if it is true," she has "always been interested in the culture."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Rapace's sister, Særún Norén, is a photographer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheStar_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-TheStar-4">[4]</a></sup> Rapace has said she saw her father only occasionally before his death. At the age of five, she moved from her native Sweden to <a href="/wiki/Fl%C3%BA%C3%B0ir" title="Flúðir">Flúðir</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> with her mother and stepfather. Two years later, she made her film debut in a minor role in the <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Iceland" title="Cinema of Iceland">Icelandic film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Shadow_of_the_Raven" title="In the Shadow of the Raven">In the Shadow of the Raven</a></i>. Rapace speaks fluent <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_language" title="Icelandic language">Icelandic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> in addition to her native <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noomi_Rapace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of seven, Rapace was given her first film role, a non-speaking part in the <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Iceland" title="Cinema of Iceland">Icelandic film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Shadow_of_the_Raven" title="In the Shadow of the Raven">In the Shadow of the Raven</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Hrafn_Gunnlaugsson" title="Hrafn Gunnlaugsson">Hrafn Gunnlaugsson</a>. This experience prompted her to be an actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She left home at age 15 and enrolled in a <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> theatre school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1996, she made her television debut playing the part of Lucinda Gonzales in the long-running soap series <i><a href="/wiki/Tre_kronor_(TV_series)" title="Tre kronor (TV series)">Tre kronor</a></i>. From 1998 to 1999, Rapace studied at the acting school Skara Skolscen. She has been engaged at Theater Plaza 2000–01, <a href="/wiki/Orion_Theatre" title="Orion Theatre">Orionteatern</a> 2001, <a href="/wiki/Teater_Galeasen" title="Teater Galeasen">Teater Galeasen</a> 2002, <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_City_Theatre" title="Stockholm City Theatre">Stockholms stadsteater</a> in 2003, as well as at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dramatic_Theatre" title="Royal Dramatic Theatre">Royal Dramatic Theatre</a>, all in Stockholm.</p>
<p>In 2007, she won acclaim for her award-winning portrayal of a troubled teen mother in the Danish film <i><a href="/wiki/Daisy_Diamond" title="Daisy Diamond">Daisy Diamond</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Simon_Staho" title="Simon Staho">Simon Staho</a>. She won the two top film awards in Denmark (the <a href="/wiki/Bodil_Awards" title="Bodil Awards">Bodil</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Award" title="Robert Award">Robert</a> prize) for Best Actress for her role in the film, which was also selected for the main competition at the <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n_International_Film_Festival" title="San Sebastián International Film Festival">San Sebastián International Film Festival</a>. The film received some criticism for the supposed abuse occurring to a baby actor during production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Girl_with_the_Dragon_Tattoo"><i>The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noomi_Rapace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, she played the role of <a href="/wiki/Lisbeth_Salander" title="Lisbeth Salander">Lisbeth Salander</a> in the Swedish-produced film adaptation of the best-selling novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girl_with_the_Dragon_Tattoo" title="The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo">The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo</a></i>, for which she won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guldbagge_Award" title="Guldbagge Award">Guldbagge Award</a> (Sweden's top award) and was nominated for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award" title="BAFTA Award">BAFTA Award</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Film_Award" title="European Film Award">European Film Award</a>. She reprised this role in the sequels <i>The Girl Who Played with Fire</i>, and <i>The Girl Who Kicked the Hornets' Nest</i> (all three were subsequently recut as a six-part miniseries aired on Swedish television called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennium_(TV_miniseries)" title="Millennium (TV miniseries)">Millennium</a></i>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hollywood">Hollywood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noomi_Rapace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hollywood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 September 2010, <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> reported that Rapace had been cast in <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a></i>, her first English-speaking role, as a French-<a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Romani</a> woman; the film was released in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her international fame has earned her leading roles in American cinema. She was cast in <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a>'s blockbuster hit <i><a href="/wiki/Prometheus_(2012_film)" title="Prometheus (2012 film)">Prometheus</a></i>, where she played the leading role, a scientist named Elizabeth Shaw. She had met Scott for the first time following the release of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennium_series#Swedish_films" title="Millennium series"><i>Millennium</i> trilogy</a> movies, at which point he expressed a willingness to work with her and encouraged her to improve her accent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <i>Prometheus</i> was released in June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2012, she appeared in a <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a> video for the single "<a href="/wiki/Doom_and_Gloom" title="Doom and Gloom">Doom and Gloom</a>" shot in the studios of the <a href="/wiki/Cit%C3%A9_du_Cin%C3%A9ma" title="Cité du Cinéma">Cité du Cinéma</a> by <a href="/wiki/Luc_Besson" title="Luc Besson">Luc Besson</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Denis,_Seine-Saint-Denis" title="Saint-Denis, Seine-Saint-Denis">Saint-Denis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, she starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Rachel_McAdams" title="Rachel McAdams">Rachel McAdams</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brian_De_Palma" title="Brian De Palma">Brian De Palma</a>'s erotic thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Passion_(2012_film)" title="Passion (2012 film)">Passion</a></i>, which is the English-language remake of 2010's French psychological thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Crime" title="Love Crime">Love Crime</a></i>. They both appeared in <i>Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</i>, but did not share scenes. Rapace also appeared in <a href="/wiki/Niels_Arden_Oplev" title="Niels Arden Oplev">Niels Arden Oplev</a>'s crime thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Down" title="Dead Man Down">Dead Man Down</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Isabelle_Huppert" title="Isabelle Huppert">Isabelle Huppert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colin_Farrell" title="Colin Farrell">Colin Farrell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood_Reporter_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood_Reporter-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Mail_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Mail-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, she appeared as Nadia in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Roskam" title="Michael Roskam">Michael Roskam</a>'s thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Drop_(film)" title="The Drop (film)">The Drop</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hardy" title="Tom Hardy">Tom Hardy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matthias_Schoenaerts" title="Matthias Schoenaerts">Matthias Schoenaerts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/James_Gandolfini" title="James Gandolfini">James Gandolfini</a>. On September 2014, she was the subject of the short film <i>A Portrait of Noomi Rapace</i>, directed by artist and designer <a href="/wiki/Aitor_Throup" title="Aitor Throup">Aitor Throup</a> and scored by <a href="/wiki/Flying_Lotus" title="Flying Lotus">Flying Lotus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In the same year, Rapace appeared in the video for the single "<a href="/wiki/Eez-eh" title="Eez-eh">eez-eh</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Kasabian" title="Kasabian">Kasabian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p>Rapace will be in the upcoming sci-fi thriller <i>Brilliance</i>, although her role is not known yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> She will also star in the spy thriller <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unlocked_(2016_film)" title="Unlocked (2016 film)">Unlocked</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Douglas" title="Michael Douglas">Michael Douglas</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Malkovich" title="John Malkovich">John Malkovich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Bloom" title="Orlando Bloom">Orlando Bloom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Toni_Collette" title="Toni Collette">Toni Collette</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> will lead the sci-fi/actioner <i>Whatever Happened To Monday?</i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Close" title="Glenn Close">Glenn Close</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willem_Dafoe" title="Willem Dafoe">Willem Dafoe</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and the sci-fi/thriller <i>Rupture</i> by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Shainberg" title="Steven Shainberg">Steven Shainberg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 May 2015, it was announced that she will play opera singer <a href="/wiki/Maria_Callas" title="Maria Callas">Maria Callas</a> in a biopic directed by <a href="/wiki/Niki_Caro" title="Niki Caro">Niki Caro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 5 November 2015, it was announced that Rapace is in talks to portray <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a> in a new biopic about the late singer's life directed by <a href="/wiki/Kirsten_Sheridan" title="Kirsten Sheridan">Kirsten Sheridan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noomi_Rapace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rapace married <a href="/wiki/Swedes" title="Swedes">Swedish</a> actor <a href="/wiki/Ola_Rapace" title="Ola Rapace">Ola Norell</a> in 2001. Together, they chose the surname "Rapace" after they were married, which means "bird of prey" in French.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anzeiger_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-anzeiger-28">[28]</a></sup> They have a son named Lev, who was born in 2004. In September 2010, the couple filed for divorce, taking effect in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Rapace had a relationship with Swedish kickboxer <a href="/wiki/Sanny_Dahlbeck" title="Sanny Dahlbeck">Sanny Dahlbeck</a>. They made their first public appearance as a couple at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTAs" title="BAFTAs">BAFTAs</a> on 8 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 2 October 2015, it was reported that the couple had broken up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
