<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">1998 FIFA World Cup Group A</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Group A of the 1998 FIFA World Cup</b> was one of eight groups of four teams competing at the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998 World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. The first matches were played on 10 June 1998 and the final games took place simultaneously on 23 June 1998.</p>
<p>The group consisted of world champions <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morocco_national_football_team" title="Morocco national football team">Morocco</a>. Matches took place at six different venues around France.</p>
<p>After Brazil beat Scotland and Morocco while Norway drew with Morocco and Scotland, Brazil had qualified as group winners with a game to spare. With ten minutes to go in the final two games, Morocco looked like they would take second place, because they were beating Scotland while Brazil were beating Norway. However, Norway scored two late goals to win and take the second qualifying place away from Morocco.</p>
