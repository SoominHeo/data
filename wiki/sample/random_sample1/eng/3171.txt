<p><b>Novorzhev</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Новорже́в</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Novorzhevsky_District" title="Novorzhevsky District">Novorzhevsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pskov_Oblast" title="Pskov Oblast">Pskov Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the <a href="/wiki/Sorot_River" title="Sorot River">Sorot River</a> 144 kilometers (89 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">3,695 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-5">[5]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">4,125 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-9">[9]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">5,050 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novorzhev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was founded in 1777 during <a href="/wiki/Catherine_the_Great" title="Catherine the Great">Catherine the Great</a>'s municipal reform as a town and the seat of newly created <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Novorzhevsky_Uyezd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Novorzhevsky Uyezd (page does not exist)">Novorzhevsky Uyezd</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pskov_Viceroyalty" title="Pskov Viceroyalty">Pskov Viceroyalty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-hist-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1796, when the viceroyalty was abolished and transformed into <a href="/wiki/Pskov_Governorate" title="Pskov Governorate">Pskov Governorate</a>, the uyezd was abolished as well; however, it was re-established in 1802.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-hist-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 1, 1927, the uyezds and governorates were abolished and Novorzhevsky District, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> in Novorzhev, was established as a part of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pskov_Okrug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pskov Okrug (page does not exist)">Pskov Okrug</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leningrad_Oblast" title="Leningrad Oblast">Leningrad Oblast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> It included parts of former Novorzhevsky Uyezd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NovorzhevskyDHist_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-NovorzhevskyDHist-12">[12]</a></sup> On July 23, 1930, the okrugs were also abolished and the districts were directly subordinated to the oblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> On January 29, 1935, the district was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Tver_Oblast" title="Tver Oblast">Kalinin Oblast</a>, and on February 5, Novorzhevsky District became a part of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Velikiye_Luki_Okrug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Velikiye Luki Okrug (page does not exist)">Velikiye Luki Okrug</a> of Kalinin Oblast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> one of the okrugs abutting the state boundaries of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. On May 11, 1937, the district was transferred to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Opochka_Okrug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Opochka Okrug (page does not exist)">Opochka Okrug</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> On February 5, 1941, the okrug was abolished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> Between July 17, 1941 and February 29, 1944, Novorzhev was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">German</a> troops.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1944, the town was completely destroyed but it was rebuilt after the war.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On August 23, 1944, the district was transferred to newly established Pskov Oblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novorzhev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Novorzhev serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Novorzhevsky_District" title="Novorzhevsky District">Novorzhevsky District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OKATO_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OKATO-2">[2]</a></sup> to which it is directly subordinated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Incorp_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Incorp-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> of Novorzhev is incorporated within Novorzhevsky Municipal District as <b>Novorzhev Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO_mun_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO_mun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novorzhev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novorzhev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A factory producing electric equipment and a textile factory operate in Novorzhev.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novorzhev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The town is connected by roads with <a href="/wiki/Ostrov,_Ostrovsky_District,_Pskov_Oblast" title="Ostrov, Ostrovsky District, Pskov Oblast">Ostrov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Opochka" title="Opochka">Opochka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pushkinskiye_Gory" title="Pushkinskiye Gory">Pushkinskiye Gory</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bezhanitsy" title="Bezhanitsy">Bezhanitsy</a>. There are also local roads.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_recreation">Culture and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novorzhev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Novorzhev contains six cultural heritage monuments classified as cultural and historical heritage of local significance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rkn_base_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rkn_base-14">[14]</a></sup> Most of them are monuments to soldiers fallen in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<p>The town hosts the Novorzhev History Museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
