<p><b>Novomoskovsk</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Новомоско́вск</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">city</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Novomoskovsky_District" title="Novomoskovsky District">Novomoskovsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tula_Oblast" title="Tula Oblast">Tula Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located at the source of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_River,_Russia" title="Don River, Russia">Don</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shat_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shat River (page does not exist)">Shat Rivers</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">131,386 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-8">[8]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">134,081 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-12">[12]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">146,302 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-13">[13]</a></sup> 143,000 (1974); 107,000 (1959); 76,000 (1939).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novomoskovsk,_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3072" data-file-width="2304" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg/220px-%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg/330px-%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg/440px-%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:%D0%98%D1%81%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%BA_%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BA%D0%B8_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The source of the Don River in Novomoskovsk</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city originated in the 18th century as the family manor of Counts <a href="/wiki/Bobrinsky" title="Bobrinsky">Bobrinsky</a>, who industrialized it towards the end of the 19th century. The city, under the name of <b>Bobriki</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Бобрики</span>) was officially established in 1930 and continued to develop as a <a href="/wiki/Lignite" title="Lignite">coal (lignite) mining</a> center throughout the Soviet period.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1933, it was renamed <b>Stalinogorsk</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Сталиногорск</span>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the city was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">German Army</a> from November 22, 1941 to December 11, 1941. In 1961, it was given its present name.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The city was awarded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Red_Banner_of_Labor" title="Order of the Red Banner of Labor">Order of the Red Banner of Labor</a> on January 14, 1971.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novomoskovsk,_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Novomoskovsk serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Novomoskovsky_District" title="Novomoskovsky District">Novomoskovsky District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TulaO_admlaw_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-TulaO_admlaw-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is, together with three <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural localities</a>, incorporated within Novomoskovsky District as <b>Novomoskovsk <a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">City Under District Jurisdiction</a></b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Note_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Note-2">[2]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the territories of Novomoskovsk Town Under District Jurisdiction and of thirteen rural okrugs of Novomoskovsky District are incorporated as Novomoskovsk Urban Okrug.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref904_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ref904-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TulaO_admlist_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-TulaO_admlist-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
