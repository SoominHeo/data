<p><b>Novokuznetsky District</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Новокузне́цкий райо́н</span>) is an administrative district (<a href="/wiki/Raion" title="Raion">raion</a>), one of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Kemerovo_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Kemerovo Oblast">nineteen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kemerovo_Oblast" title="Kemerovo Oblast">Kemerovo Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_adm_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO_adm-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, it is incorporated as <b>Novokuznetsky Municipal District</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_mun_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO_mun-2">[2]</a></sup> It is located in the center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. The area of the district is 13,040 square kilometers (5,030 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sa_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-sa-3">[3]</a></sup> Its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Novokuznetsk" title="Novokuznetsk">Novokuznetsk</a> (which is not administratively a part of the district).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_adm_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO_adm-1">[1]</a></sup> Population: <span class="nowrap">50,681 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> 50,812 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2002Census_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-2002Census-7">[7]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">39,436 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novokuznetsky_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 19, 2007 a <a href="/wiki/Ulyanovskaya_Mine_disaster" title="Ulyanovskaya Mine disaster">massive methane explosion</a> ripped through the Ulyanovskaya mine in Novokuznetsky District killing over a hundred people. The mine was the largest coal producing center in Kemerovo Oblast. It is the deadliest mining accident in recent history.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Novokuznetsky_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Novokuznetsky District is one of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Kemerovo_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Kemerovo Oblast">nineteen</a> in the oblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_adm_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO_adm-1">[1]</a></sup> The city of <a href="/wiki/Novokuznetsk" title="Novokuznetsk">Novokuznetsk</a> serves as its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a>, despite being incorporated separately as a <a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">city under oblast jurisdiction</a>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the districts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_adm_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO_adm-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the district is incorporated as <b>Novokuznetsky Municipal District</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_mun_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO_mun-2">[2]</a></sup> Novokuznetsk City Under Oblast Jurisdiction is incorporated separately from the district as Novokuznetsky Urban Okrug.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_mun_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO_mun-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
