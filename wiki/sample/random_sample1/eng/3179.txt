<p><b>Nose</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">能勢町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nose-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (<small>Japanese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Japanese" title="Help:IPA for Japanese">[nose]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> situated in <a href="/wiki/Toyono_District,_Osaka" title="Toyono District, Osaka">Toyono District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Prefecture" title="Osaka Prefecture">Osaka Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2009, the town has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 11,839 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 120 persons per km². The total area is 98.68 km².</p>

<p>Nose is noted for the "Noma Keyaki", a 1,000-year-old <a href="/wiki/Zelkova_serrata" title="Zelkova serrata">Keyaki</a> tree, 25 m tall, 11.95 m trunk circumference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noma_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-noma-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1837, there was an important peasant revolt in Nose, in the context of the <a href="/wiki/Tenp%C5%8D" title="Tenpō">Tenpō</a> famine (1833-1839), some months after <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cshio_Heihachir%C5%8D" title="Ōshio Heihachirō">Ōshio Heihachirō</a>’s riot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
