<p><b>À</b>, <b>à</b> (<a href="/wiki/A" title="A">a</a>-<a href="/wiki/Grave_accent" title="Grave accent">grave</a>) is a letter of the <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emilian-Romagnol_language" title="Emilian-Romagnol language">Emilian-Romagnol</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">Occitan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> languages consisting of the letter <a href="/wiki/A" title="A">A</a> of the <a href="/wiki/ISO_basic_Latin_alphabet" title="ISO basic Latin alphabet">ISO basic Latin alphabet</a> and a grave accent. À is also used in <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">Pinyin</a> transliteration. In most languages, it represents the vowel <i>a</i>. This letter is also a letter in <a href="/wiki/Taos_language" title="Taos language">Taos</a> to indicate a mid tone.</p>
<p>When denoting quantity, à means "each": "5 apples à $1" (one dollar each). That usage is based upon the French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preposition" title="Preposition">preposition</a> à and has evolved into the <a href="/wiki/At_sign" title="At sign">at sign (@)</a>. Sometimes, it is part of a surname: <a href="/wiki/Thomas_%C3%A0_Kempis" title="Thomas à Kempis">Thomas à Kempis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_Anne_%C3%A0_Beckett" title="Mary Anne à Beckett">Mary Anne à Beckett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Abbott_%C3%A0_Beckett" title="Gilbert Abbott à Beckett">Gilbert Abbott à Beckett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Arthur_%C3%A0_Beckett" title="Gilbert Arthur à Beckett">Gilbert Arthur à Beckett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_William_%C3%A0_Beckett" title="Arthur William à Beckett">Arthur William à Beckett</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_%C3%A0_Beckett" title="William à Beckett">William à Beckett</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_in_various_languages">Usage in various languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Usage in various languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Emilian-Romagnol">Emilian-Romagnol</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Emilian-Romagnol">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>À is used in <a href="/wiki/Emilian_dialect" title="Emilian dialect">Emilian</a> to represent short stressed [a], e.g. Bolognese dialect <i>sacàtt</i> [saˈkatː] "sack".</p>
