<p><b>North Yorkshire</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_county" title="Non-metropolitan county">non-metropolitan county</a> (or shire county) and larger <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial county</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is located primarily in the region of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_and_the_Humber" title="Yorkshire and the Humber">Yorkshire and the Humber</a> but partly in the region of <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East England</a>.</p>
<p>Created by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> it covers an area of 8,654 square kilometres (3,341 sq mi), making it the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_ceremonial_counties_of_England_by_area" title="List of ceremonial counties of England by area">largest county in England</a>. The majority of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales" title="Yorkshire Dales">Yorkshire Dales</a> and the <a href="/wiki/North_York_Moors" title="North York Moors">North York Moors</a> lie within North Yorkshire's boundaries, and around 40% of the county is covered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Parks" title="National Parks">National Parks</a>. The largest settlements are <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> (153,717), <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> (138,400), <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a> (73,576) and <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a> (61,749); the county town, <a href="/wiki/Northallerton" title="Northallerton">Northallerton</a>, has a population of 16,832.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions_and_environs">Divisions and environs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Divisions and environs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area under the control of the county council, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shire_county" title="Shire county">shire county</a>, is divided into a number of local government districts: <a href="/wiki/Craven" title="Craven">Craven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hambleton" title="Hambleton">Hambleton</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harrogate_(borough)" title="Harrogate (borough)">Harrogate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richmondshire" title="Richmondshire">Richmondshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryedale" title="Ryedale">Ryedale</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scarborough_(borough)" title="Scarborough (borough)">Scarborough</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Selby_(district)" title="Selby (district)">Selby</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NY-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Communities_and_Local_Government" title="Department for Communities and Local Government">Department for Communities and Local Government</a> considered reorganising North Yorkshire County Council's administrative structure by abolishing the seven district councils and the county council to create a North Yorkshire <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a>. The changes were planned to be implemented no later than 1 April 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This was rejected on 25 July 2007 so the County Council and District Council structure will remain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The largest settlement in the administrative county is <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a>, the second largest is Scarborough, while in the ceremonial county, the largest is <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>. The largest urban area within the ceremonial county is the Middlesbrough built-up area sub-division of Teesside.</p>
<p>York, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_(borough)" title="Middlesbrough (borough)">Middlesbrough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Redcar_and_Cleveland" title="Redcar and Cleveland">Redcar and Cleveland</a> are <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> boroughs which form part of the ceremonial county for various functions such as the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_North_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of North Yorkshire">Lord Lieutenant of North Yorkshire</a>, but do not come under county council control. Uniquely for a district in England, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockton-on-Tees_(borough)" title="Stockton-on-Tees (borough)">Stockton-on-Tees</a> is split between North Yorkshire and <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> for this purpose. Middlesbrough, Stockton-on-Tees, and Redcar and Cleveland boroughs form part of the <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East England</a> region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial county</a> area, including the unitary authorities, borders <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_features">Physical features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Physical features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_Yorkshire" title="Geology of Yorkshire">geology of North Yorkshire</a> is closely reflected in its landscape. Within the county are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_York_Moors_National_Park" title="North York Moors National Park">North York Moors</a> and most of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales" title="Yorkshire Dales">Yorkshire Dales</a>; two of eleven areas of countryside within England and Wales to be officially designated as <a href="/wiki/National_parks_of_England_and_Wales" title="National parks of England and Wales">national parks</a>. Between the North York Moors in the east and the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennine Hills</a> in the west lie the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Mowbray" title="Vale of Mowbray">Vales of Mowbray</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_York" title="Vale of York">York</a>. The Tees Lowlands lie to the north of the North York Moors and the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Pickering" title="Vale of Pickering">Vale of Pickering</a> lies to the south. Its eastern border is the North sea coast. The highest point is <a href="/wiki/Whernside" title="Whernside">Whernside</a>, on the Cumbrian border, at 736 metres (2,415 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The two major rivers in the county are the <a href="/wiki/River_Swale" title="River Swale">River Swale</a> and the <a href="/wiki/River_Ure" title="River Ure">River Ure</a>. The Swale and the Ure form the <a href="/wiki/River_Ouse,_Yorkshire" title="River Ouse, Yorkshire">River Ouse</a> which flows through York and into the Humber estuary. The River Tees forms part of the border between North Yorkshire and <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> and flows from upper <a href="/wiki/Teesdale" title="Teesdale">Teesdale</a> to <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stockton-on-Tees" title="Stockton-on-Tees">Stockton</a> and to the coast.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_local_government_in_Yorkshire" title="History of local government in Yorkshire">History of local government in Yorkshire</a></div>
<p>North Yorkshire was formed on 1 April 1974 as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, and covers most of the lands of the <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic</a> <a href="/wiki/North_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="North Riding of Yorkshire">North Riding</a>, as well as the northern half of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding</a>, the northern and eastern fringes of the <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> and the former <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>.</p>
<p>York became a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> independent of North Yorkshire on 1 April 1996,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and at the same time <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redcar_and_Cleveland" title="Redcar and Cleveland">Redcar and Cleveland</a> and areas of Stockton-on-Tees south of the river became part of North <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> for ceremonial purposes, having been part of <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_England" title="Cleveland, England">Cleveland</a> from 1974 to 1996.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>North Yorkshire is a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">non-metropolitan</a> county that operates a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabinet-style_council" title="Cabinet-style council">cabinet-style council</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire_County_Council" title="North Yorkshire County Council">North Yorkshire County Council</a>. The full council of 72 elects a council leader, who in turn appoints up to 9 more councillors to form the executive cabinet. The cabinet is responsible for making decisions in the County. The county council have their offices in the County Hall in <a href="/wiki/Northallerton" title="Northallerton">Northallerton</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county is affluent and has above average house prices. Unemployment is below average for the UK and claimants of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Job_Seekers_Allowance" title="Job Seekers Allowance">Job Seekers Allowance</a> is also very low compared to the rest of the UK at 2.7%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Agriculture is an important industry, as are mineral extraction and power generation. The county also has prosperous high technology, service and tourism sectors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_schools_in_North_Yorkshire" title="List of schools in North Yorkshire">List of schools in North Yorkshire</a></div>
<p>North Yorkshire <a href="/wiki/Local_education_authority" title="Local education authority">LEA</a> has a mostly <a href="/wiki/Comprehensive_school" title="Comprehensive school">comprehensive</a> education system with 42 state schools secondary (not including <a href="/wiki/Sixth_form_college" title="Sixth form college">sixth form colleges</a>) and 12 independent schools.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>North Yorkshire has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate">temperate</a> <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic climate</a>, like most of the UK. However, there are large climate variations within the county. The upper <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> border on a <a href="/wiki/Subarctic" title="Subarctic">Subarctic</a> climate, whereas the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Mowbray" title="Vale of Mowbray">Vale of Mowbray</a> has an almost <a href="/wiki/Semi-arid_climate" title="Semi-arid climate">Semi-arid climate</a>. Overall, with the county being situated in the east, it receives below average rainfall for the UK, but the upper Dales of the Pennines are one of the wettest parts of England, where in contrast the driest parts of the Vale of Mowbray are some of the driest areas in the UK. Summer temperatures are above average, at 22 °C, but highs can regularly reach up to 28 °C, with over 30 °C reached in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heat_waves" title="Heat waves">heat waves</a>. Winter temperatures are below average, with average lows of 1 °C. Snow and Fog can be expected depending on location, with the <a href="/wiki/North_York_Moors" title="North York Moors">North York Moors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> having snow lying for an average of between 45 and 75 days per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[14]</a></sup> Sunshine is most plentiful on the coast, receiving an average of 1650 hours a year, and reduces further west in the county, with the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> only receiving 1250 hours a year.</p>

<p>The County is served by <a href="/wiki/BBC_North_East_and_Cumbria" title="BBC North East and Cumbria">BBC North East and Cumbria</a>, and for more southerly parts of the county <a href="/wiki/BBC_Yorkshire" title="BBC Yorkshire">BBC Yorkshire</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Television" title="Yorkshire Television">Yorkshire Television</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyne_Tees_Television" title="Tyne Tees Television">Tyne Tees Television</a> are also received in most areas of the County, <a href="/wiki/Settle,_North_Yorkshire" title="Settle, North Yorkshire">Settle</a> and the Western part of the <a href="/wiki/Craven" title="Craven">Craven</a> area is served by <a href="/wiki/BBC_North_West" title="BBC North West">BBC North West</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Granada_Television" title="Granada Television">Granada Television</a>. <a href="/wiki/BBC_Tees" title="BBC Tees">BBC Tees</a> is broadcast to northern parts of the county, whist <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_York" title="BBC Radio York">BBC Radio York</a> is broadcast more widely. <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Leeds" title="BBC Radio Leeds">BBC Radio Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minster_FM" title="Minster FM">Minster FM</a> (Based in <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>) broadcast to southern parts of the county. New DAB stations have recently proliferated, such a <a class="external text" href="http://thevaleonline.com/" rel="nofollow">Vale Radio</a>, that broadcasts through 4 DAB transmitters to the whole of North Yorkshire. 56.7% of households in North Yorkshire have a DAB radio according to Ofcom.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main road through the county is the north-south <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A1(M)_motorway_(Great_Britain)" title="A1(M) motorway (Great Britain)">A1/A1(M)</a> which has gradually been upgraded to motorway status since the early 1990s. The only other motorways within the county are the short <a href="/wiki/A66_road#A66.28M.29" title="A66 road">A66(M)</a> near <a href="/wiki/Darlington" title="Darlington">Darlington</a> and a small stretch of the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a> close to <a href="/wiki/Eggborough" title="Eggborough">Eggborough</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NY-3">[3]</a></sup> The other nationally maintained trunk routes are the <a href="/wiki/A168_road" title="A168 road">A168</a>/<a href="/wiki/A19_road" title="A19 road">A19</a>, <a href="/wiki/A64_road" title="A64 road">A64</a>, the <a href="/wiki/A66_road" title="A66 road">A66</a> and <a href="/wiki/A174_road" title="A174 road">A174</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a> (ECML) bisects the county stopping at <a href="/wiki/Northallerton_railway_station" title="Northallerton railway station">Northallerton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thirsk_railway_station" title="Thirsk railway station">Thirsk</a> and <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a>. Passenger services on the ECML within the county are operated by <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Trains_East_Coast" title="Virgin Trains East Coast">Virgin Trains East Coast</a>, <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_Railway" title="Grand Central Railway">Grand Central</a>. TransPennine Express run services on the <a href="/wiki/York_to_Scarborough_Line" title="York to Scarborough Line">York to Scarborough Line</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northallerton%E2%80%93Eaglescliffe_Line" title="Northallerton–Eaglescliffe Line">Northallerton–Eaglescliffe Line</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_railway_station" title="Middlesbrough railway station">Middlesbrough</a>) that both branch off the ECML.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> operate the remaining lines in the county including commuter services on the <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Line" title="Harrogate Line">Harrogate Line</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airedale_Line" title="Airedale Line">Airedale Line</a> and York &amp; Selby Lines, of which the former two are covered by the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">Metro</a> ticketing area. Remaining branch lines operated by Northern include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Coast_Line" title="Yorkshire Coast Line">Yorkshire Coast Line</a> from Scarborough to Hull, the <a href="/wiki/Hull_to_York_Line" title="Hull to York Line">Hull to York Line</a> via <a href="/wiki/Selby_railway_station" title="Selby railway station">Selby</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley_Line" title="Tees Valley Line">Tees Valley Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/Darlington_railway_station" title="Darlington railway station">Darlington</a> to <a href="/wiki/Saltburn_railway_station" title="Saltburn railway station">Saltburn</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esk_Valley_Line" title="Esk Valley Line">Esk Valley Line</a> from Middlesbrough to <a href="/wiki/Whitby_railway_station" title="Whitby railway station">Whitby</a>. Last but certainly not least, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Settle-Carlisle_Line" title="Settle-Carlisle Line">Settle-Carlisle Line</a> runs through the west of the county with services again operated by Northern.</p>

<p>The county suffered badly under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beeching_Axe" title="Beeching Axe">Beeching</a> cuts of the 1960s. Places such as <a href="/wiki/Richmond_railway_station_(North_Yorkshire)" title="Richmond railway station (North Yorkshire)">Richmond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ripon_railway_station" title="Ripon railway station">Ripon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tadcaster_railway_station" title="Tadcaster railway station">Tadcaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helmsley_railway_station" title="Helmsley railway station">Helmsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pickering_railway_station" title="Pickering railway station">Pickering</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wensleydale" title="Wensleydale">Wensleydale</a> communities lost their passenger services. Notable lines closed were the <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_and_Whitby_Railway" title="Scarborough and Whitby Railway">Scarborough and Whitby Railway</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malton_and_Driffield_Railway" title="Malton and Driffield Railway">Malton and Driffield Railway</a> and the secondary main line between Northallerton and Harrogate via Ripon.</p>
<p>Heritage railways within North Yorkshire include the <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire_Moors_Railway" title="North Yorkshire Moors Railway">North Yorkshire Moors Railway</a> between <a href="/wiki/Pickering,_North_Yorkshire" title="Pickering, North Yorkshire">Pickering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grosmont,_North_Yorkshire" title="Grosmont, North Yorkshire">Grosmont</a>, which opened in 1973, the <a href="/wiki/Derwent_Valley_Light_Railway" title="Derwent Valley Light Railway">Derwent Valley Light Railway</a> near York, and the <a href="/wiki/Embsay_and_Bolton_Abbey_Steam_Railway" title="Embsay and Bolton Abbey Steam Railway">Embsay and Bolton Abbey Steam Railway</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Wensleydale_Railway" title="Wensleydale Railway">Wensleydale Railway</a>, which started operating in 2003, runs services between <a href="/wiki/Leeming_Bar_railway_station" title="Leeming Bar railway station">Leeming Bar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Redmire_railway_station" title="Redmire railway station">Redmire</a> along a former freight-only line. The medium-term aim is to operate into Northallerton station on the ECML once agreement can be reached with <a href="/wiki/Network_Rail" title="Network Rail">Network Rail</a>. In the longer term the aim is to reinstate the full line west via <a href="/wiki/Hawes" title="Hawes">Hawes</a> to <a href="/wiki/Garsdale_railway_station" title="Garsdale railway station">Garsdale</a> on the Settle-Carlisle line.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York railway station</a> is the largest station in the county with 11 platforms and is a major tourist attraction in its own right. The station is immediately adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/National_Railway_Museum" title="National Railway Museum">National Railway Museum</a>.</p>
<p>Long-distance coach services are operated by <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> and <a href="/wiki/Megabus_(Europe)" title="Megabus (Europe)">Megabus</a>. Local bus service operators include <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Bus_Company" title="Harrogate Bus Company">Harrogate Bus Company</a>, Scarborough &amp; District (<a href="/wiki/East_Yorkshire_Motor_Services" title="East Yorkshire Motor Services">East Yorkshire Motor Services</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Coastliner" title="Yorkshire Coastliner">Yorkshire Coastliner</a>, <a href="/wiki/First_York" title="First York">First York</a> and the local <a href="/wiki/Dales_%26_District" title="Dales &amp; District">Dales &amp; District</a>.</p>
<p>There are no major airports in the county itself but nearby airports include <a href="/wiki/Durham_Tees_Valley_Airport" title="Durham Tees Valley Airport">Durham Tees Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_Airport" title="Newcastle Airport">Newcastle</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robin_Hood_Airport_Doncaster_Sheffield" title="Robin Hood Airport Doncaster Sheffield">Robin Hood Doncaster Sheffield</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_International_Airport" title="Leeds Bradford International Airport">Leeds Bradford</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>North Yorkshire is home to several football clubs, including <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> who play in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> and <a href="/wiki/York_City_F.C." title="York City F.C.">York City</a> who play in The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vanarama_National_League" title="Vanarama National League">Vanarama National League</a>. <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Town_F.C." title="Harrogate Town F.C.">Harrogate Town</a> play in the <a href="/wiki/National_League_North" title="National League North">National League North</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whitby_Town_FC" title="Whitby Town FC">Whitby Town FC</a> have reached the FA cup first round seven times, and have played the likes of <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>, Wigan and Plymouth Argyle, they currently play in the Evo-Stik Premier league. Other lower league clubs include <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Railway_Athletic_F.C." title="Harrogate Railway Athletic F.C.">Harrogate Railway Athletic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northallerton_Town_F.C." title="Northallerton Town F.C.">Northallerton Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pickering_Town_F.C." title="Pickering Town F.C.">Pickering Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_Athletic_F.C." title="Scarborough Athletic F.C.">Scarborough Athletic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selby_Town_F.C." title="Selby Town F.C.">Selby Town</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tadcaster_Albion_F.C." title="Tadcaster Albion F.C.">Tadcaster Albion</a>.</p>
<p>No notable <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> teams hail from the county but <a href="/wiki/York_City_Knights" title="York City Knights">York City Knights</a> are a <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> team and play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rugby_League_National_Leagues" title="Rugby League National Leagues">Rugby League Championship</a>.</p>
<p>North Yorkshire is home to many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racecourses" title="Racecourses">racecourses</a>; these include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catterick_Bridge_Racecourse" title="Catterick Bridge Racecourse">Catterick Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redcar_Racecourse" title="Redcar Racecourse">Redcar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ripon_Racecourse" title="Ripon Racecourse">Ripon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thirsk_Racecourse" title="Thirsk Racecourse">Thirsk</a> and <a href="/wiki/York_Racecourse" title="York Racecourse">York</a>. It also has one <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor racing</a> circuit, <a href="/wiki/Croft_Circuit" title="Croft Circuit">Croft Circuit</a>; the circuit holds meetings of the <a href="/wiki/British_Touring_Car_Championship" title="British Touring Car Championship">British Touring Car Championship</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Superbike" title="British Superbike">British Superbike</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pickup_Truck_Racing" title="Pickup Truck Racing">Pickup Truck Racing</a> race series.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire County Cricket Club</a>, play a number of fixtures at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Marine_Road" title="North Marine Road">North Marine Road</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a>.</p>
<p>The ball game <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock-It-Ball" title="Rock-It-Ball">Rock-It-Ball</a> was developed in the county.</p>
