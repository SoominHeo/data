<p><b>Norsefire</b> is the fictional <a href="/wiki/Neo-fascism" title="Neo-fascism">fascist</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> ruling the United Kingdom in <a href="/wiki/Alan_Moore" title="Alan Moore">Alan Moore</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Lloyd_(comics)" title="David Lloyd (comics)">David Lloyd</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/V_for_Vendetta" title="V for Vendetta">V for Vendetta</a></i> comic book series.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norsefire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story presents a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Post-apocalyptic" title="Post-apocalyptic">post-apocalyptic</a> Britain that has narrowly avoided an international <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_warfare" title="Nuclear warfare">nuclear war</a> from 1988 on. As displayed during the story of <a href="/wiki/Evey_Hammond" title="Evey Hammond">Evey Hammond</a>, although the United Kingdom did not suffer any nuclear attacks, the effects of full-scale nuclear war on other countries had severe effects on the environment, and thus on <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>. This in its turn severely damaged the <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Economy of the United Kingdom">British economy</a> and mass riots broke out. As Evey relays to <a href="/wiki/V_(comics)" title="V (comics)">V</a>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist" title="Anarchist">anarchist</a> determined to destroy Norsefire, the government quickly collapsed and chaos overran the country.</p>
<p>The situation turned after several years (approximately 1992 in the story). From the madness of the violence came the ultra right-wing Norsefire regime: <a href="/wiki/Neo-fascism" title="Neo-fascism">fascists</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (similar to <a href="/wiki/Oswald_Mosley" title="Oswald Mosley">Oswald Mosley</a>'s <a href="/wiki/British_Union_of_Fascists" title="British Union of Fascists">British Union of Fascists</a>) that united with the surviving big companies and businesses, giving them the appearance of wealth and stability. However, while the Norsefire regime did indeed bring order back to the country, this order came at a cost: people who were not white, Christian, and heterosexual were rounded up and sent to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concentration_camp" title="Concentration camp">concentration camps</a>. Political <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leftist" title="Leftist">leftists</a> were also persecuted. With their potential enemies all removed within a short space of time, Norsefire began consolidating their power over the country. They began to sink their influence into the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a>, giving it more power and influence. They began promoting and demoting members of the clergy as they saw fit. They also took over the television companies, creating <i>NTV</i> (Norsefire Television), and implemented the technologies that would allow for a closely monitored society, including <a href="/wiki/Closed-circuit_television" title="Closed-circuit television">closed-circuit television</a>. In the novel the British monarchy continues under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zara_Phillips" title="Zara Phillips">Queen Zara</a>. No reference is made to the monarchy in the film, though <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/God_Save_the_King" title="God Save the King">God Save the King</a></i> is played during a television comedy sketch.</p>
<p>By the time the story of the graphic novel has begun, Norsefire has shut down the concentration camps and is essentially in complete control of society. Although competition exists between the varying branches of the state, they generally have complete control over the United Kingdom. The head of the party is <a href="/wiki/Adam_Susan" title="Adam Susan">Leader Adam Susan</a>, a self-proclaimed fascist who seems to worship <i>Fate</i>, the super-computer surveillance system that oversees the nation. Their control over the state soon faces a threat from V, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist" title="Anarchist">anarchist</a> protagonist of the story who seeks to overthrow the regime and allow the people to decide their own fate. By the end of the novel, every top government official and notable figure in Norsefire has died, except for Lewis Prothero who is driven insane by V and incapacitated by Norsefire early on in the novel, and Eric Finch who leaves Norsefire after he kills V and when Norsefire collapses.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motto">Motto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norsefire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Motto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A common recurring motto is "Strength Through Purity, Purity Through Faith" (or in the movie, "Strength Through Unity, Unity Through Faith"). The <a href="/wiki/British_Union_of_Fascists" title="British Union of Fascists">British Union of Fascists</a> also used a similar slogan, "Action within unity". Another <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maxim_(saying)" title="Maxim (saying)">maxim</a> often used by Norsefire as a salute is "England Prevails".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbol">Symbol</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norsefire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Symbol">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NorsefireFlag.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="1200" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/NorsefireFlag.svg/300px-NorsefireFlag.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/NorsefireFlag.svg/450px-NorsefireFlag.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/NorsefireFlag.svg/600px-NorsefireFlag.svg.png 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NorsefireFlag.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The flag of Norsefire as portrayed in the film version of <i>V for Vendetta</i>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the book, a blue "N" on a black flag is the symbol of the party. An "N" or "NF" are the only party symbols shown. Yet, as mentioned above, the first issue's cover also features a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_cross" title="Greek cross">Greek</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_cross" title="Latin cross">Latin cross</a> merged into a pair of wings rising from red flames. This could symbolize the "rise" of Britain from the ashes similar to that of a <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_(mythology)" title="Phoenix (mythology)">Phoenix</a>. It is notable that this symbol also uses the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Flag of the United Kingdom">British national colours</a>, red, white and blue.</p>
<p>In the film, the Norsefire symbol is a combination of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_England" title="Flag of England">flag of England</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lorraine_cross" title="Lorraine cross">Lorraine cross</a>. The symbol is shown on flags, police badges, <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanks" title="Tanks">tanks</a>, and army <a href="/wiki/Beret" title="Beret">beret</a> badges. Two versions exist, one with a red cross on a black background and another with a black cross on a red background.</p>
