<p><b>North Hollywood</b> is a combined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heavy_rail" title="Heavy rail">heavy rail</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subway_station" title="Subway station">subway station</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Bus_rapid_transit" title="Bus rapid transit">bus rapid transit</a> (BRT) station in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County_Metro_Rail" title="Los Angeles County Metro Rail">Los Angeles County Metro Rail</a> system. It is located at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Lankershim_Boulevard" title="Lankershim Boulevard">Lankershim Boulevard</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chandler_Boulevard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chandler Boulevard (page does not exist)">Chandler Boulevard</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Hollywood,_Los_Angeles,_California" title="North Hollywood, Los Angeles, California">North Hollywood</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. This station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Line_(Los_Angeles_Metro)" title="Red Line (Los Angeles Metro)">Red Line</a> subway service as well as the <a href="/wiki/Orange_Line_(Los_Angeles_Metro)" title="Orange Line (Los Angeles Metro)">Orange Line</a> BRT service. The station is the northern <a href="/wiki/Train_station#Terminus" title="Train station">terminus</a> (the last stop) of the Red Line, and the eastern terminus of the Orange Line in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metro_Liner_(Los_Angeles_County)" title="Metro Liner (Los Angeles County)">Los Angeles County Metro Liner</a> system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metro_Rail_and_BRT_service">Metro Rail and BRT service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Hollywood_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Metro Rail and BRT service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Red Line service hours are approximately from 5:00 AM until 12:45 AM daily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metro_Liner_(Los_Angeles_County)" title="Metro Liner (Los Angeles County)">Metro Liner</a> Orange Line BRT (bus rapid transitway) service hours are approximately from 4:00 AM until 1:00 AM daily.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Underground_passageway">Underground passageway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Hollywood_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Underground passageway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Metro constructed a second entrance on the west side of <a href="/wiki/Lankershim_Boulevard" title="Lankershim Boulevard">Lankershim Boulevard</a>, which would allow riders to connect between the Orange Line (surface bus rapid transit) and the Red Line (underground heavy rail) via an underground passageway. This underground connection was completed in August 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Hollywood_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>North Hollywood Metro station is located on Lankershim Boulevard, which forms the western border of the station and parking lot; it is bordered on its South side by Chandler Boulevard. It is one block South of Burbank Boulevard (but is not bordered by Burbank) and is one block West of <a href="/wiki/Vineland_Avenue" title="Vineland Avenue">Vineland Avenue</a> (but is not bordered by Vineland). The station is located in <a href="/wiki/North_Hollywood,_Los_Angeles" title="North Hollywood, Los Angeles">district of the same name</a> in the <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando_Valley" title="San Fernando Valley">San Fernando Valley</a> section of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transit-oriented_development">Transit-oriented development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Hollywood_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transit-oriented development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the opening of the station in 2000, <a href="/wiki/Transit-oriented_development" title="Transit-oriented development">transit-oriented developments</a> have begun to be constructed adjacent to the station. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=NoHo_Tower&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NoHo Tower (page does not exist)">NoHo Tower</a> (a 15-story apartment building) is across the street from the station and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=NoHo_Commons&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NoHo Commons (page does not exist)">NoHo Commons</a>, a multi-use complex which includes several floors of apartments above a level of retail. In September 2007, transportation officials approved NoHo Art Wave, the largest "transit-oriented" development in L.A. County history, consisting of a $1.3-billion apartment, retail and high-rise office tower complex totaling more than 1,700,000 square feet (160,000 m<sup>2</sup>) of development on 15.6 acres (63,000 m<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LAT092807_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-LAT092807-5">[5]</a></sup> That project did not start due to the recession but in 2016 a public-private partnership with the Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority was proposed on the 16 acres (6.5 ha) surrounding the station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lankershim_Depot">Lankershim Depot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Hollywood_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Lankershim Depot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2144" data-file-width="3000" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg/220px-Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg/330px-Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg/440px-Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Toluca_Southern_Pacific_Depot_and_Pacific_Electric_Lankershim_Station_view_from_east_2014-11-23.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Depot after restoration, 2014</div>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Railway" title="Southern Pacific Railway">Southern Pacific Railway</a> built the Lankershim Depot in 1896 on land that is adjacent to the current Orange Line platforms. It later served as a stop on the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Electric" title="Pacific Electric">Pacific Electric</a> system after its North Hollywood Line opened in 1911. In 2014, the station was restored for a cost of $3.6 million, with plans to convert it into a bike hub, museum, or retail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
