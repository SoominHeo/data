<p><b>Noah Lindsey Cyrus</b> (born January 8, 2000) is an American actress and singer. She is most known for voicing the title role in the English version of the animated feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Ponyo" title="Ponyo">Ponyo</a></i>. She is the youngest daughter of <a href="/wiki/Billy_Ray_Cyrus" title="Billy Ray Cyrus">Billy Ray Cyrus</a> and younger sister of <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noah_Cyrus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cyrus began her acting career at age three, playing "Gracie Hebert" for six episodes of the television show <i><a href="/wiki/Doc_(2001_TV_series)" title="Doc (2001 TV series)">Doc</a></i>. She was a background dancer in the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana:_The_Movie" title="Hannah Montana: The Movie">Hannah Montana: The Movie</a></i> and played small roles in six episodes of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_Original_Series" title="Disney Channel Original Series">Disney Channel Original Series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvcom_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tvcom-1">[1]</a></sup> She appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">straight-to-DVD</a> movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mostly_Ghostly_(film)" title="Mostly Ghostly (film)">Mostly Ghostly</a></i> where she was spotted as a Trick or Treater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Her first movie role was in 2008 in the English version of the anime feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Ponyo" title="Ponyo">Ponyo</a></i> in which she voiced the title character. She sang the English version of the theme song to the film with <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Jonas" title="Frankie Jonas">Frankie Jonas</a>. Between 2009 and 2010 Cyrus also ran a webshow with <a href="/wiki/Emily_Grace_Reaves" title="Emily Grace Reaves">Emily Grace Reaves</a>, called <i>The Noie and Ems Show</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singing_career">Singing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noah_Cyrus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 15, 2016, it was announced that Cyrus had inked a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Barry_Weiss" title="Barry Weiss">Barry Weiss</a> record label called RECORDS and signed a management deal with <a href="/wiki/Maverick_(company)" title="Maverick (company)">Maverick</a> under Adam Leber. Later that day, she released her debut single, a pop ballad titled, "<a href="/wiki/Make_Me_(Cry)" title="Make Me (Cry)">Make Me (Cry)</a>" featuring UK singer/producer <a href="/wiki/Labrinth" title="Labrinth">Labrinth</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noah_Cyrus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, Cyrus used her 13th birthday as an opportunity to raise funds for the ban of using horse-drawn carriages in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also posed in a <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">PETA</a> ad protesting the use of animal dissection in high school lab classes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
