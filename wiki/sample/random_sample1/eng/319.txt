<p>The <b>1998 FIFA World Cup Final</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> match that was played on 12 July 1998 at the <a href="/wiki/Stade_de_France" title="Stade de France">Stade de France</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Denis,_Seine-Saint-Denis" title="Saint-Denis, Seine-Saint-Denis">Saint-Denis</a> to determine the winner of the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998 FIFA World Cup</a>. The final was contested by <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, who were the defending champions having won the previous <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> four years earlier in <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and the host nation <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, who had reached the final of the tournament for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> France won the match 3–0 to claim the World Cup for the first time, with the timing of the match two days before <a href="/wiki/Bastille_Day" title="Bastille Day">Bastille Day</a> adding to the significance of the victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-France_98_BBC_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-France_98_BBC-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewind_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rewind-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a>, who was named man of the match, scored twice before half-time and <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Petit" title="Emmanuel Petit">Emmanuel Petit</a> added a third goal in the last minute. The match had an attendance in the region of 75,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-France_98_BBC_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-France_98_BBC-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On their way to the final, defending champions Brazil, coached by their former player <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1rio_Zagallo" title="Mário Zagallo">Mário Zagallo</a>, recorded victories over <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> (2–1) and <a href="/wiki/Morocco_national_football_team" title="Morocco national football team">Morocco</a> (3–0) to top their <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup#Group_stage" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">group</a> with 6 points from three matches, suffering a surprise 2–1 defeat at the hands of <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a> in their final group game. After a 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a> and a 3–2 success against <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a>, they reached the final with a penalty shoot-out victory over <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">the Netherlands</a>. As for France, they won their three group matches and defeated <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a> in the knockout stages on golden goals. They had a penalty-shootout with <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> in the quarter-finals, and defeated recently formed <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a> to reach the final.</p>
<p>The match also saw speculation on the condition of the Brazilian striker <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a>, who suffered a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seizure" title="Seizure">convulsive fit</a> on the eve of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mystery_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mystery-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After initially being left out of the team sheet, in spite of his physical state, it was announced just 72 minutes before kick-off that he was going to play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-France_98_BBC_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-France_98_BBC-4">[4]</a></sup> In the match, he sustained an injury in a clash with French goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Fabien_Barthez" title="Fabien Barthez">Fabien Barthez</a>. Although it was believed that the decision to play Ronaldo had backfired, it was understandable as the player had been a crucial member of the side throughout the tournament, having scored four goals and created three more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>France followed up their victory by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000_qualification" title="UEFA Euro 2000 qualification">qualifying</a> for and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000_Final" title="UEFA Euro 2000 Final">winning</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">UEFA Euro 2000</a> held in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Brazil took the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copa_America" title="Copa America">Copa America</a> title in <a href="/wiki/1999_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1999 Copa América">1999</a>, and then won the next FIFA World Cup in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> in <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Ronaldo went on to set the record for goals in World Cups, which was later broken by <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_Klose" title="Miroslav Klose">Miroslav Klose</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>For Brazil, this marked only the second time that they had lost a World Cup final, following their <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_v_Brazil_(1950_FIFA_World_Cup)" title="Uruguay v Brazil (1950 FIFA World Cup)">2–1 upset loss</a> to <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a> in the <i>de facto</i> final of the <a href="/wiki/1950_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1950 FIFA World Cup">1950 FIFA World Cup</a>. The 3–0 scoreline was also Brazil's previous largest loss at any World Cup match prior to their <a href="/wiki/Brazil_v_Germany_(2014_FIFA_World_Cup)" title="Brazil v Germany (2014 FIFA World Cup)">7–1 loss</a> to Germany in the semifinals of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_FIFA_World_Cup_Final&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brazil were drawn in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup_-_Group_A" title="1998 FIFA World Cup - Group A">Group A</a> for the group stages alongside <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco_national_football_team" title="Morocco national football team">Morocco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. They recorded victories over Scotland (2–1) and Morocco (3–0) to progress but lost their final game 2–1 to Norway.</p>
<p>They next faced Group B runners-up <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a> in the last-16 and comprehensively won 4–1, <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a> and <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Sampaio" title="César Sampaio">César Sampaio</a> each scoring twice. In the quarter-finals, they played <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a>, who had also won their previous game 4–1 (against <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a>), but Brazil won a tight game 3–2. Despite being 1–0 down to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Jorgensen" title="Martin Jorgensen">Martin Jorgensen</a> goal in the second minute, Brazil turned the game around in their favour with goals from <a href="/wiki/Bebeto" title="Bebeto">Bebeto</a> (11) and <a href="/wiki/Rivaldo" title="Rivaldo">Rivaldo</a> (27). <a href="/wiki/Brian_Laudrup" title="Brian Laudrup">Brian Laudrup</a> equalised for Denmark after 50 minutes but Brazil won the game 10 minutes later courtesy of a second from Rivaldo.</p>
<p>In the semi-finals, Brazil faced the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> in <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a>. The game finished 1–1 at full-time, <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a> scoring just after half-time and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Kluivert" title="Patrick Kluivert">Patrick Kluivert</a> equalising for the Netherlands in the 87th minute, and the score remained the same through extra-time. The match had to be settled by penalties which Brazil won 4–2 to reach their second successive World Cup final.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_FIFA_World_Cup_Final&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>France were drawn in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup_-_Group_C" title="1998 FIFA World Cup - Group C">Group C</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia_national_football_team" title="Saudi Arabia national football team">Saudi Arabia</a>. They started their campaign with an easy 3–0 win over South Africa followed by a convincing 4–0 win over Saudi Arabia. France secured top spot in their group courtesy of a 2–1 win over Denmark with goals from <a href="/wiki/Youri_Djorkaeff" title="Youri Djorkaeff">Youri Djorkaeff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Petit" title="Emmanuel Petit">Emmanuel Petit</a>.</p>
<p>In the second round they faced Group D runners-up <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a>. France won a close encounter 1–0 in extra time thanks to a golden goal scored by <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Blanc" title="Laurent Blanc">Laurent Blanc</a>. In the quarter-finals France faced <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> who had also scraped through to the quarter-finals with a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a>. A tense match ended 0–0 after extra time and France won 4–3 on penalties after Italy's <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Di_Biagio" title="Luigi Di Biagio">Luigi Di Biagio</a> struck his penalty onto the crossbar.</p>
<p>In the semi-finals, France faced tournament surprise <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a>. After a goal-less first half, Croatia took the lead in the first minute of the second half through <a href="/wiki/Davor_%C5%A0uker" title="Davor Šuker">Davor Šuker</a>, his fifth goal of the tournament. France responded immediately with <a href="/wiki/Lilian_Thuram" title="Lilian Thuram">Lilian Thuram</a> scoring his first international goal. Thuram then added a second twenty minutes from time to send France to their first ever World Cup final. The match ended in controversy however when <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Blanc" title="Laurent Blanc">Laurent Blanc</a> was sent off after a skirmish with Croatia's <a href="/wiki/Slaven_Bili%C4%87" title="Slaven Bilić">Slaven Bilić</a>. Bilić had sunk down to his knees, seemingly in pain. Replays showed, however, that there was minimal contact between the players. Blanc's expulsion meant he would miss the final.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Match">Match</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_FIFA_World_Cup_Final&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Match">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_FIFA_World_Cup_Final&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The build-up was dominated by the fitness of Brazil's star striker, <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a>, amid reports that he had suffered a pre-match fit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mystery_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mystery-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a> gave France the lead just before the half-hour mark with a header from an in-swinging corner from the right taken by Emmanuel Petit. Only minutes later, Ronaldo was put through on goal by a long ball from <a href="/wiki/Dunga" title="Dunga">Dunga</a>, but he could not get the better of the onrushing <a href="/wiki/Fabien_Barthez" title="Fabien Barthez">Fabien Barthez</a>, who collided with the Brazilian striker. Both needed assistance from the squad medics but quickly recovered. Brazil's superstar playmakers <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Ara%C3%BAjo" title="Leonardo Araújo">Leonardo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rivaldo" title="Rivaldo">Rivaldo</a> were kept quiet by <a href="/wiki/Didier_Deschamps" title="Didier Deschamps">Didier Deschamps</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Karembeu" title="Christian Karembeu">Christian Karembeu</a>. Zidane doubled France's advantage on the stroke of half-time with an almost identical goal, this time the corner came from the left. In the second half, Ronaldo had a chance to halve the deficit. The ball fell for him inside the penalty box, but he could only plant his shot into Barthez's arms. Midfielder <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Petit" title="Emmanuel Petit">Emmanuel Petit</a> wrapped up the scoring in the 90th minute, after receiving a through ball from his <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> team mate <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Vieira" title="Patrick Vieira">Patrick Vieira</a>, slotting the ball low into the net. France had to play the last 20 minutes with only 10 men with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Misconduct_(association_football)" title="Misconduct (association football)">dismissal</a> of <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Desailly" title="Marcel Desailly">Marcel Desailly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
