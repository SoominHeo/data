<p><b>Nozawa Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">野沢駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nozawa-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Ban%27etsu_West_Line" title="Ban'etsu West Line">Ban'etsu West Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nishiaizu,_Fukushima" title="Nishiaizu, Fukushima">Nishiaizu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yama_District,_Fukushima" title="Yama District, Fukushima">Yama District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Prefecture" title="Fukushima Prefecture">Fukushima Prefecture</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nozawa_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nozawa Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Ban%27etsu_West_Line" title="Ban'etsu West Line">Ban'etsu West Line</a>, and is located 106.2 rail kilometers from the official starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Driyama_Station_(Fukushima)" title="Kōriyama Station (Fukushima)">Kōriyama Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nozawa_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nozawa Station has a single <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> and a single <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a>, connected to the station building by an overhead passage.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nozawa_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nozawa Station opened on August 1, 1913. The station was absorbed into the JR East network upon the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on April 1, 1987.</p>
