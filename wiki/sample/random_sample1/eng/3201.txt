<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquis" title="Marquis">Marquis</a> <b>Nozu Michitsura</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">野津 道貫</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 17 December 1840 – 18 October 1908)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Field_Marshal_(Japan)" title="Field Marshal (Japan)">field marshal</a> and leading figure in the early <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a>. His wife was the younger sister of fellow general <a href="/wiki/Takashima_Tomonosuke" title="Takashima Tomonosuke">Takashima Tomonosuke</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nozu_Michitsura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nozu was born in <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima" title="Kagoshima">Kagoshima</a> as the second son of a low-ranking <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a></i> of the <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Domain" title="Satsuma Domain">Satsuma Domain</a>. He studied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_swordsmanship" title="Japanese swordsmanship">Japanese swordsmanship</a> under Yakumaru Kaneyoshi, a noted instructor within Satsuma Domain, and was appointed a company commander during in the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>. Nozu was at every major battle in the war, from the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toba%E2%80%93Fushimi" title="Battle of Toba–Fushimi">Battle of Toba–Fushimi</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aizu" title="Battle of Aizu">Battle of Aizu</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hakodate" title="Battle of Hakodate">Battle of Hakodate</a>.</p>
<p>After the war, Nozu went to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, and in March 1871, was appointed as a <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">major</a> in the 2nd Brigade of the fledgling Imperial Japanese Army. He was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_colonel" title="Lieutenant colonel">lieutenant colonel</a> in August 1872, and colonel in January 1874 upon his appointment as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_of_staff_(military)" title="Chief of staff (military)">chief of staff</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Guard_of_Japan" title="Imperial Guard of Japan">Imperial Guards Brigade</a>. From July to October 1876, Nozu traveled to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, where he attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centennial_Exhibition" title="Centennial Exhibition">Centennial Exhibition</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia</a>. Soon after his return to Japan, he had the unpleasant task of fighting against his former Satsuma clansmen in the <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Rebellion" title="Satsuma Rebellion">Satsuma Rebellion</a>. In February 1877, Nozu was appointed chief of staff of the 2nd Brigade, and was stationed in <a href="/wiki/Bungo_Province" title="Bungo Province">Bungo Province</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a> – the heartland of the rebellion, from May to August 1877.</p>
<p>In November 1878, Nozu was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Major_general" title="Major general">major general</a>, and subsequently served as commander of the Tokyo Military District. In February 1884, Nozu accompanied <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_War_of_Japan" title="Ministry of War of Japan">War Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cyama_Iwao" title="Ōyama Iwao">Ōyama Iwao</a>, on a year-long tour of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> to examine the military systems of various European nations. In July 1884, he was elevated to the title of <a href="/wiki/Baron" title="Baron">baron</a> (<i>danshaku</i>) in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i> peerage system by <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a>. From February to April 1885, Nozu was sent to <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a> <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Military_attach%C3%A9" title="Military attaché">military attaché</a>. On his return to Japan in May 1885, he was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_general" title="Lieutenant general">lieutenant general</a> and made commander of the <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima" title="Hiroshima">Hiroshima</a> Military District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 1888, with the reorganization of the Imperial Japanese Army into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_(army)" title="Division (army)">divisions</a> per the advice of <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/O-yatoi_gaikokujin" title="O-yatoi gaikokujin">military advisor</a> <a href="/wiki/Jakob_Meckel" title="Jakob Meckel">Jakob Meckel</a>, Nozu was made commander of the new <a href="/wiki/5th_Division_(Imperial_Japanese_Army)" title="5th Division (Imperial Japanese Army)">IJA 5th Division</a>, which saw combat under his command in the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Pyongyang_(1894)" title="Battle of Pyongyang (1894)">Battle of Pyongyang</a> (1894). In March 1895, Nozu was promoted to full <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General" title="General">general</a> and replaced General <a href="/wiki/Yamagata_Aritomo" title="Yamagata Aritomo">Yamagata Aritomo</a> as command-in-chief of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_First_Army" title="Japanese First Army">Japanese First Army</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a>. In August 1895, he was elevated to the title of <a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">count</a> (<i>hakushaku</i>).</p>
<p>After the end of the war, Nozu he successively held various military posts including Commander of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Guard_of_Japan" title="Imperial Guard of Japan">Imperial Guard Division</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inspectorate_General_of_Military_Training" title="Inspectorate General of Military Training">Inspector-General of Military Training</a>, and served as a Military Councilor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the start of the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a>, Nozu was assigned command of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Fourth_Army" title="Japanese Fourth Army">Japanese Fourth Army</a>, which played a crucial role in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mukden" title="Battle of Mukden">Battle of Mukden</a>. His title was also upgraded to <a href="/wiki/Viscount" title="Viscount">viscount</a> (<i>shishaku</i> ). At the end of the war, he received promotion to the post of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Field_Marshal_(Japan)" title="Field Marshal (Japan)">field marshal</a> in January 1906.</p>
<p>By Imperial appointment, Nozu served as a member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Peers_(Japan)" title="House of Peers (Japan)">House of Peers</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diet_of_Japan" title="Diet of Japan">Diet of Japan</a> from September 1907 until his death in October 1908. His title was also upgraded to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquis" title="Marquis">marquis</a> (<i>koshaku</i>) in 1907.</p>
<p>Nozu's decorations included the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Golden_Kite" title="Order of the Golden Kite">Order of the Golden Kite</a> (1st class) and the Grand Cordon of the Supreme <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Chrysanthemum" title="Order of the Chrysanthemum">Order of the Chrysanthemum</a>.</p>
<p>His grave is at the <a href="/wiki/Aoyama_Cemetery" title="Aoyama Cemetery">Aoyama Cemetery</a> in downtown Tokyo.</p>
