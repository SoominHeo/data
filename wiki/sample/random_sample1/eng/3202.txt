<p>The term <i><b>nose cone</b></i> is used to refer to the forwardmost section of a <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rocket</a>, guided <a href="/wiki/Missile" title="Missile">missile</a> or <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a>. The cone is shaped to offer minimum <a href="/wiki/Aerodynamics" title="Aerodynamics">aerodynamic</a> resistance. Nose cones are also designed for travel in and under water and in high-speed land vehicles.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rocket">Rocket</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nose_cone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rocket">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On a rocket vehicle it consists of a chamber or chambers in which a satellite, instruments, animals, plants, or auxiliary equipment may be carried, and an outer surface built to withstand high temperatures generated by <a href="/wiki/Aerodynamic_heating" title="Aerodynamic heating">aerodynamic heating</a>. Much of the fundamental research related to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hypersonic" title="Hypersonic">hypersonic</a> flight was done towards creating viable nose cone designs for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric_reentry" title="Atmospheric reentry">atmospheric reentry</a> of spacecraft and <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile" title="Intercontinental ballistic missile">ICBM</a> <a href="/wiki/Multiple_independently_targetable_reentry_vehicle" title="Multiple independently targetable reentry vehicle">reentry vehicles</a>.</p>
<p>In a <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellite</a> vehicle, the nose cone may become the satellite itself after separating from the final <a href="/wiki/Multistage_rocket" title="Multistage rocket">stage</a> of the rocket or it may be used to shield the satellite until orbital speed is accomplished, then separating from the satellite.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft">Aircraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nose_cone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aircraft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On airliners the nose cone is also a <a href="/wiki/Radome" title="Radome">radome</a> protecting the <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">weather radar</a> from aerodynamic forces.</p>
<p>The shape of the nose cone must be chosen for minimum drag so a <a href="/wiki/Solid_of_revolution" title="Solid of revolution">solid of revolution</a> is used that gives least resistance to motion. The article on <a href="/wiki/Nose_cone_design" title="Nose cone design">nose cone design</a> contains possible shapes and formulas.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hypersonic">Hypersonic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nose_cone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hypersonic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the extreme temperatures involved, nose cones for high-speed applications (e.g. hypersonic speeds or atmospheric reentry of <a href="/wiki/Orbit" title="Orbit">orbital</a> vehicles) have to be made of <a href="/wiki/Refractory" title="Refractory">refractory</a> materials. <a href="/wiki/Pyrolytic_carbon" title="Pyrolytic carbon">Pyrolytic carbon</a> is one choice, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reinforced_carbon-carbon" title="Reinforced carbon-carbon">reinforced carbon-carbon</a> composite or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HRSI" title="HRSI">HRSI</a> ceramics are other popular choices. Another design strategy is using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric_reentry#Ablative" title="Atmospheric reentry">ablative heat shields</a>, which get consumed during operation, disposing of excess heat that way. Materials used for ablative shields include, for example <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_phenolic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carbon phenolic (page does not exist)">carbon phenolic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polydimethylsiloxane" title="Polydimethylsiloxane">polydimethylsiloxane</a> <a href="/wiki/Composite_material" title="Composite material">composite</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silica" title="Silica">silica</a> filler and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber" title="Carbon fiber">carbon fibers</a>, or as in of some Chinese <a href="/wiki/Fanhui_Shi_Weixing" title="Fanhui Shi Weixing">FSW</a> reentry vehicles, <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a> <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In general, the constraints and goals for atmospheric reentry conflict with those for other high-speed flight applications; during reentry a high-drag blunt reentry shape is frequently used, which minimises the <a href="/wiki/Heat_transfer" title="Heat transfer">heat transfer</a> by creating a <a href="/wiki/Shock_wave" title="Shock wave">shock wave</a> that stands off from the vehicle, but some very-high-temperature materials may permit sharper-edged designs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nose_cone_design">Nose cone design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nose_cone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nose cone design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nose_cone_design" title="Nose cone design">Nose cone design</a></div>
<p>Given the problem of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerodynamic" title="Aerodynamic">aerodynamic</a> <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a> of the nose cone section of any vehicle or body meant to travel through a compressible fluid medium (such as a <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rocket</a> or <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missile" title="Missile">missile</a> or <a href="/wiki/Bullet" title="Bullet">bullet</a>), an important problem is the determination of the nose cone geometrical shape for optimum performance. For many applications, such a task requires the definition of a <a href="/wiki/Solid_of_revolution" title="Solid of revolution">solid of revolution</a> shape that experiences minimal resistance to rapid motion through such a fluid medium, which consists of elastic particles.</p>
