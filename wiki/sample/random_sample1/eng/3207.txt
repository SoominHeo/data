<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_finance">Law and finance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Exposure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Law and finance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Abandonment_(legal)" title="Abandonment (legal)">Abandonment</a>, multiple meanings in law</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Infant_exposure" title="Infant exposure">Infant exposure</a>, a form of child abandonment</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indecent_exposure" title="Indecent exposure">Indecent exposure</a>, the display of unclothed parts of the human body that is against local custom and law</li>
<li>Potential for <a href="/wiki/Damages" title="Damages">damages</a></li>
<li>An insurance company's potential to provide coverage under an <a href="/wiki/Insurance_policy" title="Insurance policy">insurance policy</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Market_exposure" title="Market exposure">Market exposure</a>, a measure of the proportion of money invested in the same industry sector</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Publicity" title="Publicity">Publicity</a>, an activity designed to rouse public interest</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Photography">Photography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Exposure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Photography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Exposure_(photography)" title="Exposure (photography)">Exposure (photography)</a>, the quantity of light or other radiation reaching a photographic film (or sensor), as determined by shutter speed and lens aperture</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Exposure_value" title="Exposure value">Exposure value</a>, a value given to all combinations of camera shutter speed and aperture that gives the same exposure</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_medicine">Science and medicine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Exposure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Science and medicine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Radiation_exposure" title="Radiation exposure">Radiation exposure</a>, a measure of the ionization of air due to ionizing radiation from photons, that is, gamma rays and X-rays</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Radiant_exposure" title="Radiant exposure">Radiant exposure</a>, the radiant energy received by a surface per unit area, or equivalently the irradiance of a surface integrated over time of irradiation</li>
<li>In experimental particle physics (in particular <a href="/wiki/Particle_beam" title="Particle beam">beam</a> experiments or <a href="/wiki/Flux" title="Flux">flux</a> measurements), the product of the detector mass times the duration of the experiment, sometimes also multiplied by a measure of the intensity of the incoming flux</li>
<li>In geology, an occurrence of a rock at the Earth's surface - an <a href="/wiki/Outcrop" title="Outcrop">outcrop</a></li>
<li>In biology, contact of an organism with a harmful agent (for example, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical" title="Chemical">chemicals</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Exposure_(heights)" title="Exposure (heights)">Exposure (heights)</a>, in climbing, the degree of risk in the event of a fall</li>
<li>A condition of very poor health or death resulting from lack of protection over prolonged periods under weather, extreme temperatures or dangerous substances, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Hypothermia" title="Hypothermia">hypothermia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunburn" title="Sunburn">sunburn</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Exposure_assessment" title="Exposure assessment">Exposure assessment</a>, in the context of epidemiology and risk assessment</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Exposure_therapy" title="Exposure therapy">Exposure therapy</a>, a treatment method for anxiety disorders</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mere-exposure_effect" title="Mere-exposure effect">Mere-exposure effect</a>, a psychological artefact</li>
</ul>
