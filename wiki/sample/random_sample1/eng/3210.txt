<p>The <b>Nokia Lumia 505</b> is a budget <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Nokia" title="Nokia">Nokia</a> that runs <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windows_Phone_7.8" title="Windows Phone 7.8">Windows Phone 7.8</a> operating system. It was announced in December 2012, as a <a href="/wiki/Telcel" title="Telcel">Telcel</a>-exclusive device and became available in early January 2013 in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wcentral_1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wcentral_1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Engadget_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Engadget-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In late January 2013 it was announced the Lumia 505 would also become available in Colombia, Chile and Peru on <a href="/wiki/Claro_Americas" title="Claro Americas">Claro Americas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wcentral_2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wcentral_2-4">[4]</a></sup> It is currently exclusive to the <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin American</a> market and has not been announced or released for any other markets.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_features">Primary features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nokia_Lumia_505&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Primary features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The primary features of the Lumia 505 are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nokia_505_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nokia_505-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>3.7in 800x480 <a href="/wiki/AMOLED" title="AMOLED">AMOLED</a> 252 PPI touchscreen display with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corning_Incorporated" title="Corning Incorporated">Corning</a> <a href="/wiki/Gorilla_Glass" title="Gorilla Glass">Gorilla Glass</a></li>
<li>8 MP camera</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VGA" title="VGA">VGA</a> video and photo recording</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_to_the_Lumia_510">Relationship to the Lumia 510</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nokia_Lumia_505&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Relationship to the Lumia 510">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lumia 505 is a market-exclusive sister device to the globally released <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lumia_510" title="Lumia 510">Lumia 510</a> and shares a number of specifications with the Lumia 510, including utilizing the same 800 MHz <a href="/wiki/Qualcomm_Snapdragon" title="Qualcomm Snapdragon">Qualcomm Snapdragon</a> processor and having only 256 MB of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Random_Access_Memory" title="Random Access Memory">RAM</a> and 4 GB internal <a href="/wiki/Flash_memory" title="Flash memory">flash memory</a>. The Lumia 505, however, has a slightly differing design, with a smaller 3.7 inch <a href="/wiki/AMOLED" title="AMOLED">AMOLED</a> screen (versus a 4.4 inch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LCD" title="LCD">LCD</a> screen on the Lumia 510), shorter battery life of 7.2 hours (versus 8.3 hours), and higher resolution 8 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megapixel" title="Megapixel">megapixel</a> camera (versus 5 megapixel camera).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wpcentral_3_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wpcentral_3-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nokia_510_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nokia_510-7">[7]</a></sup> The Lumia 505 is also available in a different set of colors from the Lumia 510 with the Lumia 505 being available in Magenta, Black and Red and the Lumia 510 being available in Black, White, Red, Cyan, &amp; Yellow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nokia_505_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nokia_505-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nokia_510_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nokia_510-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Availability">Availability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nokia_Lumia_505&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Availability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The phone was originally released for sale exclusively through <a href="/wiki/Telcel" title="Telcel">Telcel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> in the beginning of 2013 as a carrier exclusive for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MXN" title="MXN">MXN</a> $3,499 without a contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wcentral_1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wcentral_1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSMarena_Price_and_Latin_America_Release_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GSMarena_Price_and_Latin_America_Release-9">[9]</a></sup> In late January 2013, it was announced that the phone would also become available in Colombia, Chile and Peru on <a href="/wiki/Claro_Americas" title="Claro Americas">Claro Americas</a> becoming market exclusive to <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wcentral_2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wcentral_2-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSMarena_Price_and_Latin_America_Release_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-GSMarena_Price_and_Latin_America_Release-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
