<p><b>Nokia Lumia 710</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Windows_Phone_7" title="Windows Phone 7">Windows Phone 7</a> <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Its release is part of a change in company's direction which has resulted in a shift from <a href="/wiki/Symbian" title="Symbian">Symbian</a> platform towards <a href="/wiki/Windows_Phone" title="Windows Phone">Windows Phone</a> for smartphones. While the <a href="/wiki/Nokia_Lumia_800" title="Nokia Lumia 800">Nokia Lumia 800</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lumia_900" title="Lumia 900">Lumia 900</a> target the high-end of the smartphone marketplace, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lumia_610" title="Lumia 610">Lumia 610</a> and Lumia 710 are aimed at the <a href="/wiki/Feature_phone" title="Feature phone">mid-range price point</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This generation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nokia_Lumia" title="Nokia Lumia">Lumia</a> phones ship with Windows Phone 7.5 "Mango".</p>
<p>It has been released in Brazil and some Western European and Asian countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 14 December 2011, <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile" title="T-Mobile">T-Mobile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nokia" title="Nokia">Nokia</a> officially announced their plan to sell the Lumia 710 from 11 January 2012, for $349.99 prepaid or $49.99 with a 2-year contract with minimum of $60 per month plan and $35 per line activation fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At CES 2012, Nokia announced that Rogers would have exclusivity for the Lumia 710 in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The phone, alongside the <a href="/wiki/Nokia_Lumia_800" title="Nokia Lumia 800">Nokia Lumia 800</a>, is promised to get an update to add wi-fi hotspot capabilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 February 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Nokia_Lumia_720" title="Nokia Lumia 720">Nokia Lumia 720</a>, the successor of the Nokia Lumia 710, was presented. The improvements are <a href="/wiki/Windows_Phone_8" title="Windows Phone 8">Windows Phone 8</a>, dual-core 1 GHz Qualcomm S4 chipset, a front-facing VGA camera, a bigger 4.2 in display, a bigger 2000 mAh battery and support for up to 64GB <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MicroSD" title="MicroSD">microSD</a> cards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwmpoweruser.com.2Fnokia-officially-announces-nokia-lumia-720-mid-range-windows-phone-8-device.2F_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwmpoweruser.com.2Fnokia-officially-announces-nokia-lumia-720-mid-range-windows-phone-8-device.2F-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nokia_Lumia_710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Nokia Lumia 710 can be personalized with exchangeable back covers. It is available in black, white with black, white, cyan, fuchsia and yellow back covers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lumiainfo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-lumiainfo-1">[1]</a></sup> The device has the same 1.4 GHz processor, hardware acceleration and graphics processor as the Nokia Lumia 800.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>This phone also features Nokia's clear black display giving it a vivid resolution.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Software">Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nokia_Lumia_710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lumia 710 comes with four Nokia-exclusive applications not included by default in the Windows Phone OS: Nokia Drive, a free turn-by-turn navigation system; Nokia Maps; Nokia Music, a free streaming music service and music store; and App Highlights, a service suggesting software based on location and operator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nokia_Collection">Nokia Collection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nokia_Lumia_710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nokia Collection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Windows Phone market place contains a Nokia Collection section.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> As of April 2012, it contains the following additional applications: Nokia Transport, a location aver public transport schedule and navigation application, Creative Studio, a photo editing application, TuneIn Radio, a local and global radio streaming application, CNN, a news reader and video viewer for then CNN news network, WRC Live, an application to follow live timing and media from the FIA WRC series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nokia_Lumia_710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lumia 710 received mixed reviews, with some critics describing it as an affordable "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entry-level" title="Entry-level">entry-level</a>" smartphone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CNET" title="CNET">CNET</a> gave the phone 3.5 stars out of 5 and said that the 710, "has a smooth and engaging user interface, satisfying photo quality, and 720p HD video, all in a compact and affordable package." CNET UK said, "For a mid-range smart phone, the Nokia Lumia 710 has loads to offer -- it's well built, runs on easy-to-use Windows Phone, and comes with free music, sat-nav and mapping apps."</p>
<p>Some reviewers noted problems with the phone. The device had an issue with disconnecting calls, however, Nokia addressed the problem with a software update.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> TechRadar said the phone has a poor battery life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PCWorld_(magazine)" title="PCWorld (magazine)">PC World</a></i> said that "The Lumia 710's photos were mediocre."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> According to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=TechSpot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="TechSpot (page does not exist)">TechSpot</a>, "The Lumia 710's button problems don't end with the front of the phone, as the volume rocker and camera key on the right edge are very mushy and don't provide much feedback. The camera key, in particular, is quite hard to use, as it is very difficult to determine when you have pushed it down completely."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
