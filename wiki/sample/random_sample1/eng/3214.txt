<p><i><b>Noticias Caracol</b></i> (until 9 October 2007 <i>Caracol Noticias</i>) is the name for all the national newscast from the news division of <a href="/wiki/Caracol_Televisi%C3%B3n" title="Caracol Televisión">Caracol Televisión</a> which carries the same name. It is broadcast four times on weekdays and twice on weekends. In January 2015 the new director is Juan Roberto Vargas and the second director is Alberto Medina Lopez.</p>
<p>Before becoming a network, Caracol Televisión produced the morning newscast <i>7:30 Caracol</i> in the mid-1990s. Several well-known Colombian <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalists</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Palacios" title="Claudia Palacios">Claudia Palacios</a>, Érika Fontalvo, and <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Cristina_Uribe" title="María Cristina Uribe">María Cristina Uribe</a> were also news presenters of <i>Caracol Noticias</i>. <a href="/wiki/Yamid_Amat" title="Yamid Amat">Yamid Amat</a> was its director until March 2002.</p>
<p>The newsroom and studio was built in nine months before <i>Caracol Noticias’</i> first airing, on 10 July 1998. <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> company Imageneering acted as a consultant.</p>
<p>All <i>Noticias Caracol</i> newscasts are also aired live on <a href="/wiki/Caracol_TV_Internacional" title="Caracol TV Internacional">Caracol TV Internacional</a>, and on Caracol's U.S. television station <a href="/wiki/WGEN-TV" title="WGEN-TV">WGEN-TV</a> in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> (which is affiliated with <a href="/wiki/MundoMax" title="MundoMax">MundoMax</a>, a television network owned by Caracol's domestic rival <a href="/wiki/RCN_Televisi%C3%B3n" title="RCN Televisión">RCN Televisión</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sections">Sections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noticias_Caracol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Noticias Caracol</i> newscasts are divided in sections, grouped in three blocks: "serious" news (regional, national, world news, weather, health), sports, and entertainment. For the world news, <i>Noticias Caracol</i> has signed some agreements with services such as <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press_Television_News" title="Associated Press Television News">APTN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/CNN_en_Espa%C3%B1ol" title="CNN en Español">CNN en Español</a>.</p>
<p><i>Noticias Caracol</i> is also part of the <i>Alianza Informativa Latinoamericana</i> (AIL), with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ATV_(Peruvian_Network)" title="ATV (Peruvian Network)">ATV</a> (<a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ecuavisa" title="Ecuavisa">Ecuavisa</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>), <a href="/wiki/Sistema_Nacional_de_Televisi%C3%B3n_(Paraguay)" title="Sistema Nacional de Televisión (Paraguay)">SNT Cerro Corá</a> (<a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>), <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo_TV" title="Monte Carlo TV">Monte Carlo TV</a> (<a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telef%C3%A9" title="Telefé">Telefé</a> (<a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megavisi%C3%B3n" title="Megavisión">Megavisión</a> (<a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venevision" title="Venevision">Venevision</a> (<a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Azteca" title="TV Azteca">TV Azteca</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>), <a href="/wiki/Unitel_Bolivia" title="Unitel Bolivia">Unitel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>), TVN/TVMax (<a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>), <a href="/wiki/Teletica" title="Teletica">Teletica</a> (<a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>), <a href="/wiki/RedeTV!" title="RedeTV!">RedeTV!</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Band_News" title="Band News">Band News</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>), TV Doce (<a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Azteca" title="TV Azteca">TV Azteca</a> (<a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>), <a href="/wiki/WAPA-TV" title="WAPA-TV">WAPA</a> WAPA AMERICA (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>), Antena Latina/Noticias SIN (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rep%C3%BAblica_Dominicana" title="República Dominicana">República Dominicana</a>) and ATV CANAL 15 (<a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>). This alliance allows <i>Noticias Caracol</i> to use the services of journalists working for the member networks as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_correspondent" title="Foreign correspondent">foreign correspondents</a> in their respective countries, also the AIL allows <i>Noticias Caracol</i> to use all the technical resources of the other networks to provide a better and faster response to the news events. The AIL also has built its own news agency, where all the networks provide the most important news events in their own countries and feed them to Caracol TV, where they are edited and rebroadcast by satellite to all the members of this alliance. Since September 2012, Caracol Television has become an <a href="/wiki/European_News_Exchange" title="European News Exchange">ENEX (European News Exchange)</a> an alliance that started in Europe 20 years ago, which has more that 42 television stations (Europe, Asia and Americas). Some of the stories are available at its website as <a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">video on demand</a>, as well as at <i><a href="/wiki/El_Espectador" title="El Espectador">El Espectador</a>'</i>s website. Both media outlets are owned by <a href="/wiki/Julio_Mario_Santo_Domingo" title="Julio Mario Santo Domingo">Julio Mario Santo Domingo</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weekdays">Weekdays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noticias_Caracol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Weekdays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 5:45 a.m. newscast, presented by Catalina Gomez Sanchez and Juan Diego Alvira, focuses on national news—most of them from the day before—,sports, regional news, world news, and entertainment. On this newscast, guests are invited to the <a href="/wiki/Set_construction" title="Set construction">set</a> to be <a href="/wiki/Interview" title="Interview">interviewed</a> by the presenters. It includes a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_forecast" title="Weather forecast">weather forecast</a>, supported by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Instituto_de_Hidrolog%C3%ADa,_Meteorolog%C3%ADa_y_Estudios_Ambientales_de_Colombia" title="Instituto de Hidrología, Meteorología y Estudios Ambientales de Colombia">IDEAM</a>, Colombia's <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorological institute</a>, and a section named <i>¿Qué pasó con?</i>, where viewers can learn what happened with an old story previously covered on the newscast.</p>
<p>The 12:30 midday newscast, presented by <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B3nica_Jaramillo" title="Mónica Jaramillo">Mónica Jaramillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_de_la_Torre" title="Vanessa de la Torre">Vanessa de la Torre</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1bel_Lara" title="Mábel Lara">Mábel Lara</a>, lasts one hour, but until early 2011, when a new season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Price_Is_Right#International_versions" title="The Price Is Right">El precio es correcto</a></i> premiered, was the longest of all (it ended around 15:00). The newscast includes national, world and sports news, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Health" title="Health">health</a> section, in charge by doctor Fernanda Hernández, <i>Con-sumo cuidado</i> (a section on rights of the consumers), <i>Ojo ciudadano</i> (a section with denounces submitted by viewers), and <i>Caracol más cerca</i>, where a journalist travels around the country in order to show its diversity. The entertainment block is known as <i>Del otro mundo</i>.</p>
<p>The 7:00 p.m. newscast is <i>Noticias Caracol</i> flagship <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/News_show" title="News show">news show</a>, presented by Jorge Alfredo Vargas and <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Luc%C3%ADa_Fern%C3%A1ndez" title="María Lucía Fernández">María Lucía Fernández</a>, where the main events, stories and developments of the day are summarized. It has no health section but it does include a <i>Caracol más cerca</i> abridged section, and <i>Código Caracol</i>, hosted by Mábel Lara, where some political events and gossip are revealed.</p>
<p>The 11:30 p.m. newscast lasts 30 minutes and is presented by Alejandra Giraldo or Jennifer Montoya. It includes a <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> section called <i>Sala múltiple</i> presented by Lina María Arbeláez, instead the entertainment block, and a review of <i><a href="/wiki/El_Espectador" title="El Espectador">El Espectador</a>'</i>s main headlines for the next day. Its broadcasting time had been progressively moved from 21:30 to 22:00, 22:30, 23:00, 23:45, and midnight. It returned to the 22:00 time slot in January 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-victorsolano_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-victorsolano-1">[1]</a></sup> but it was moved half hour later since 23 June 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fiscalia_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fiscalia-2">[2]</a></sup> and again in 2011 to 22:45.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weekends">Weekends</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noticias_Caracol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Weekends">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Noticias Caracol</i> 12:30 midday and 7:00 p.m. newscasts on weekends are presented By Alejandra Giraldo and Jennifer Montoya. The 12:30 newscast was revamped since 18 April 2009, including, besides the news, specialized sections in law (<i>En todo su derecho</i>, by Silvia Corzo and later by Paola Bermúdez), technology (<i>Click</i>, by Juan Ignacio Velásquez), cinema (by Germán Espinel), cooking (<i>2 x 3</i>, by Pilar Schmitt), health (by Fernanda Hernández), music (by Adriana Tono), and culture (a weekend edition of <i>Sala múltiple</i>, by Lina María Arbeláez).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weekend_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-weekend-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 7:00 p.m. newscast tends to follow the three-block rule more conventionally. It includes a brief segment reviewing the main headlines for the following day’s edition of the <i>El Espectador</i> newspaper.</p>
