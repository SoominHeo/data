<p>The <b>Norfolk Admirals</b> are a professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team in the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a> which began play in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_ECHL_season" title="2015–16 ECHL season">2015–16 season</a>. Based in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk, Virginia</a> and affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Oilers" title="Edmonton Oilers">Edmonton Oilers</a>, the team plays its home games at the <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Scope" title="Norfolk Scope">Norfolk Scope</a>.</p>
<p>The Admirals replaced the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a> <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Admirals_(AHL)" title="Norfolk Admirals (AHL)">team of the same name</a>, which played from <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_AHL_season" title="2000–01 AHL season">2000</a> until <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_AHL_season" title="2014–15 AHL season">2015</a>, after which they moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a> and became the current incarnation of the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Gulls" title="San Diego Gulls">San Diego Gulls</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norfolk_Admirals_(ECHL)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 29, 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Anaheim_Ducks" title="Anaheim Ducks">Anaheim Ducks</a> announced that they would be moving their AHL affiliate, the <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Admirals_(AHL)" title="Norfolk Admirals (AHL)">Norfolk Admirals</a>, to San Diego as one of five charter members of the AHL's new Pacific Division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ahlsandiego_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ahlsandiego-1">[1]</a></sup> The next day, the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Oilers" title="Edmonton Oilers">Edmonton Oilers</a> announced that their outgoing ECHL team, the <a href="/wiki/Bakersfield_Condors_(1998%E2%80%932015)" title="Bakersfield Condors (1998–2015)">Bakersfield Condors</a> (who would also be supplanted by a new <a href="/wiki/Bakersfield_Condors" title="Bakersfield Condors">Bakersfield Condors</a> in the AHL), would move to Norfolk and take on the Admirals name and logo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-admiralsechl_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-admiralsechl-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Oilers_Entertainment_Group" title="Oilers Entertainment Group">Oilers Entertainment Group</a> had originally purchased the ECHL Condors in January 2014, one year prior to the announcement of the relocation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Admirals hockey in Norfolk actually began in the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Admirals" title="Hampton Roads Admirals">Hampton Roads Admirals</a> played from <a href="/wiki/1989%E2%80%9390_ECHL_season" title="1989–90 ECHL season">1989</a> until <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_ECHL_season" title="1999–2000 ECHL season">2000</a>, when the ownership group purchased an AHL franchise license, changed the name to the Norfolk Admirals, and began play as an AHL expansion team starting with the 2000–01 season. The original ECHL Admirals' run was very successful, winning three titles – the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riley_Cup" title="Riley Cup">Riley Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/1990%E2%80%9391_ECHL_season" title="1990–91 ECHL season">1991</a> and <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%9392_ECHL_season" title="1991–92 ECHL season">1992</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Cup" title="Kelly Cup">Kelly Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_ECHL_season" title="1997–98 ECHL season">1998</a>, an ECHL record which has since been tied by the <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Aces_(ECHL)" title="Alaska Aces (ECHL)">Alaska Aces</a> and the <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina_Stingrays" title="South Carolina Stingrays">South Carolina Stingrays</a>.</p>
<p>After one season, the Oilers announced they had sold the Admirals franchise to the Mango Media Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Admirals continued the Oilers affiliation under the new ownership.</p>
