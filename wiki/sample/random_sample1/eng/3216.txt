<p>The <b>Pucciniomycetes</b> (formerly known as the <b>Urediniomycetes</b>) are a <a href="/wiki/Class_(biology)" title="Class (biology)">class</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fungi" title="Fungi">fungi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pucciniomycotina" title="Pucciniomycotina">Pucciniomycotina</a> subdivision of the <a href="/wiki/Basidiomycota" title="Basidiomycota">Basidiomycota</a>. The class contains 5 <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">orders</a>, 21 <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">families</a>, 190 <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a>, and 8016 <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kirk2008_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kirk2008-3">[3]</a></sup> It includes several important <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phytopathology" title="Phytopathology">plant pathogens</a> causing forms of fungal <a href="/wiki/Rust_(fungus)" title="Rust (fungus)">rust</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pucciniomycetes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pucciniomycetes develop no <a href="/wiki/Basidiocarp" title="Basidiocarp">basidiocarp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karyogamy" title="Karyogamy">karyogamy</a> occurs in a thick-walled resting <a href="/wiki/Spore" title="Spore">spore</a> (<a href="/wiki/Teliospore" title="Teliospore">teliospore</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Meiosis" title="Meiosis">meiosis</a> occurs upon germination of teliospore. They have simple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Septal_pore" title="Septal pore">septal pores</a> without membrane caps and disc-like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitotic_spindle" title="Mitotic spindle">spindle</a> <a href="/wiki/Microtubule_organizing_center" title="Microtubule organizing center">pole bodies</a>. Except for a few species, the <a href="/wiki/Basidium" title="Basidium">basidia</a>, when present, are transversally septate. <a href="/wiki/Mannose" title="Mannose">Mannose</a> is the major <a href="/wiki/Cell_wall" title="Cell wall">cell wall</a> carbohydrate, <a href="/wiki/Glucose" title="Glucose">glucose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fucose" title="Fucose">fucose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rhamnose" title="Rhamnose">rhamnose</a> are the less prevalent neutral sugars and <a href="/wiki/Xylose" title="Xylose">xylose</a> is not present.</p>
