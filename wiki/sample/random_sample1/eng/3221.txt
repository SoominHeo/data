<p><b>Noksapyeong Station</b> is a subway station on the <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Subway_Line_6" title="Seoul Subway Line 6">Seoul Subway Line 6</a>. It is located on the eastern end of the <a href="/wiki/Yongsan_Garrison" title="Yongsan Garrison">Yongsan Garrison</a>, and the western end of <a href="/wiki/Itaewon" title="Itaewon">Itaewon</a>. It is the main station servicing the <a href="/wiki/Haebangchon" title="Haebangchon">Haebangchon</a> and Hoenamu-gil communities, which are known for having significant foreign populations.</p>
<p>This station has low ridership due to its location, but is noted for its impressive interior design. It has five underground levels, and a glass <a href="/wiki/Dome" title="Dome">dome</a> on top of the building lets in <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">sunlight</a> which permeates throughout the station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In addition to being featured in Korean movies and dramas, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weddings" title="Weddings">weddings</a> can be held there at no cost.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embassy" title="Embassy">embassy</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in South Korea is close to this station. <a href="/wiki/Namsan_(Seoul)" title="Namsan (Seoul)">Namsan</a> lies just to the north, a 20-minute walk from the station.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
