<p>The <b>Green Line</b> (French: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Ligne verte</i></span>), is one of the four lines of the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Metro" title="Montreal Metro">Montreal Metro</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, Quebec, Canada. The line runs through the commercial section of downtown Montreal underneath <a href="/wiki/De_Maisonneuve_Boulevard" title="De Maisonneuve Boulevard">Boulevard de Maisonneuve</a>, formerly Rue de Montigny. It runs mainly on a northeast to southwest axis with a connection to the Orange and Yellow Lines at <a href="/wiki/Berri-UQAM_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Berri-UQAM (Montreal Metro)">Berri-UQAM</a>, and with the Orange Line west of downtown at <a href="/wiki/Lionel-Groulx_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Lionel-Groulx (Montreal Metro)">Lionel-Groulx</a>.</p>
<p>The section between <a href="/wiki/Atwater_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Atwater (Montreal Metro)">Atwater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frontenac_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Frontenac (Montreal Metro)">Frontenac</a> was part of the initial network; the line was extended to <a href="/wiki/Honor%C3%A9-Beaugrand_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Honoré-Beaugrand (Montreal Metro)">Honoré-Beaugrand</a> in 1976, and to <a href="/wiki/Angrignon_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Angrignon (Montreal Metro)">Angrignon</a> in 1978. The extension to Honoré-Beaugrand was to provide easy access to <a href="/wiki/1976_Summer_Olympics" title="1976 Summer Olympics">1976 Summer Olympics</a> sites. Most stations are <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> stations, and a few have large video screens showing news, weather, advertisements, and the time until the next train arrives.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Line_(Montreal_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first stations, found on the section between <a href="/wiki/Atwater_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Atwater (Montreal Metro)">Atwater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Papineau_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Papineau (Montreal Metro)">Papineau</a>, opened on October 14, 1966. Several smaller sections were delayed by several months. On December 19, 1966, the line was further extended from Papineau to <a href="/wiki/Frontenac_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Frontenac (Montreal Metro)">Frontenac</a>, and two days later came the stopover <a href="/wiki/Beaudry_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Beaudry (Montreal Metro)">Beaudry</a> between <a href="/wiki/Berri-UQAM_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Berri-UQAM (Montreal Metro)">Berri-UQAM</a> and Papineau. On December 20, 1967, <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Back" title="Frédéric Back">Frédéric Back</a> completed his art piece <i>L'histoire de la musique à Montréal</i> (<i>The history of music in Montreal</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Place-des-Arts_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Place-des-Arts (Montreal Metro)">Place-des-Arts station</a>. This commissioned piece was the first artwork completed in the Metro system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTLenMetro_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTLenMetro-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The construction of the second phase began in 1971, when Montreal was awarded the bid to host the <a href="/wiki/1976_Summer_Olympics" title="1976 Summer Olympics">1976 Summer Olympics</a>. The goal was to have the ability to transport visitors from downtown to the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Park,_Montreal" title="Olympic Park, Montreal">Olympic Park</a> in the east end. The opening of the section between Frontenac and <a href="/wiki/Honor%C3%A9-Beaugrand_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Honoré-Beaugrand (Montreal Metro)">Honoré-Beaugrand</a> took place on June 6, 1976,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTLenMetro_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MTLenMetro-1">[1]</a></sup> six weeks before the start of the Summer Olympics. Green Line trains inaugurated an <a href="/wiki/Autopilot" title="Autopilot">autopilot</a> feature on November 8, 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTLenMetro_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-MTLenMetro-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The third expansion phase, between Atwater and <a href="/wiki/Angrignon_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Angrignon (Montreal Metro)">Angrignon</a>, came into operation on September 3, 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTLenMetro_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-MTLenMetro-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Line_(Montreal_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Line is served primarily by <a href="/wiki/MR-63" title="MR-63">MR-63</a> cars, although in recent years <a href="/wiki/MR-73" title="MR-73">MR-73</a> cars have begun appearing on the Green Line in anticipation of the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/MPM-10" title="MPM-10">MPM-10</a> stock in 2016 and the withdrawal of the MR-63 stock by 2018.</p>
