<p>The <b>Green Party Taiwan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">台灣綠黨</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Táiwān Lǜ Dǎng</span></i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwanese_Minnan" title="Taiwanese Minnan">Taiwanese</a>: Tâi-ôan Le̍k Tóng) is a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> established on 25 January 1996. Although the party is sympathetic to Taiwan nationalism and shares a number of centre-left positions with the <a href="/wiki/Pan-Green_Coalition" title="Pan-Green Coalition">Pan-Green Coalition</a>, the party emphasizes campaigning primarily on social and environmental issues. The party is not a member of, and should not be confused with, the Pan-Green Coalition. Green Party Taiwan is a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Green_Network" title="Asia-Pacific Green Network">Asia Pacific Greens Federation</a> and participates in the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_history">Electoral history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Electoral history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1996, Green Party Taiwan’s <a href="/wiki/Kao_Meng-ting" title="Kao Meng-ting">Kao Meng-ting</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Republic_of_China)" title="National Assembly (Republic of China)">National Assembly</a>. However, he left the party in 1997.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_legislative_election,_2008" title="Republic of China legislative election, 2008">2008 legislative election</a>, the Green Party of Taiwan formed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red-green_alliance" title="Red-green alliance">red-green coalition</a> with a labour-led organization Raging Citizens Act Now! (人民火大行動聯盟), but failed to win any seats.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_legislative_election,_2012" title="Republic of China legislative election, 2012">2012 legislative election</a>, Green Party Taiwan garnered 1.7% of the party vote. While still far short of the 5% threshold to win a seat in the legislature, this makes it the largest extraparliamentary party in Taiwan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its best showing is in <a href="/wiki/Orchid_Island" title="Orchid Island">Ponso no Tao</a> where Taiwan’s nuclear waste storage facility is located. There, the party collected 35.76% of the party votes due to its strong antinuclear stance.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_local_elections,_2014" title="Taiwan local elections, 2014">2014 local elections</a>, the party won two seats. Wang Hao-yu was elected to the Taoyuan City council and Jay Chou to the Hsinchu County Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwanese_general_election,_2016" title="Taiwanese general election, 2016">2016 general election</a>, the party ran in a coalition with the newly founded centre-left Social Democratic Party<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and fielded candidates in both constituency races and the nationwide party ballot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The coalition garnered 2.5% of the party vote without winning any seats.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
