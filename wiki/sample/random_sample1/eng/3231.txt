<p><b>Nông Đức Mạnh</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Nong_Duc_Manh.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a2/Nong_Duc_Manh.ogg" title="Nong Duc Manh.ogg">listen</a></span></span>; born 11 September 1940) is a Vietnamese politician and was the general secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Vietnam" title="Communist Party of Vietnam">Communist Party of Vietnam</a>, the most powerful position in the Vietnamese government, from 22 April 2001 to 19 January 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Although his official biography states that his parents were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tay_(people)" title="Tay (people)">Tày</a> peasants,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OfficialBio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-OfficialBio-2">[2]</a></sup> it has long been rumoured that Mạnh is the son of former North Vietnamese leader <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh" title="Ho Chi Minh">Hồ Chí Minh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rumor_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rumor-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mạnh was born in <a href="/wiki/C%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Dng_L%E1%BB%A3i" title="Cường Lợi">Cường Lợi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Na_R%C3%AC_District" title="Na Rì District">Na Rì District</a>, <a href="/wiki/B%E1%BA%AFc_K%E1%BA%A1n_Province" title="Bắc Kạn Province">Bắc Kạn Province</a>. His own son is Nông Quốc Tuấn, party secretary for Bắc Giang Province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%B4ng_%C4%90%E1%BB%A9c_M%E1%BA%A1nh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It has long been rumoured that Mạnh is the illegitimate son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H%E1%BB%93_Ch%C3%AD_Minh" title="Hồ Chí Minh">Hồ Chí Minh</a> (1890-1969) and Nông Thị Trưng (1920–2003), Hồ's housekeeper from 1941-42. This story may have been a factor in his selection as party boss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rumor_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rumor-3">[3]</a></sup> In a profile of Mạnh published in the official press immediately after he gained this position, Trưng was identified as his mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GioiMoi_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GioiMoi-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mạnh's official biography gives his date of birth as 11 September 1940,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> when Hồ was still in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theage_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-theage-6">[6]</a></sup> Ho returned to Vietnam in February 1941<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and met Trưng in July. Hồ wrote a four-line poem for Trưng in 1944, and gave her a notebook as "a token of my love".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>This poem was later taught to elementary school students. In April 2001, shortly after Mạnh was named as party boss, a reporter at a news conference asked him to confirm or deny the rumor. He responded, <i>"All Vietnamese people are the children of Uncle Hồ."</i> When asked again about the rumor in January 2002 by a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Asia" title="Time Asia">Time Asia</a></i> reporter, he denied he was Hồ's son and stated that his father was named Nông Văn Lai and his mother Hoàng Thị Nhị.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%B4ng_%C4%90%E1%BB%A9c_M%E1%BA%A1nh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1958-61, Mạnh attended the Hanoi Higher School. From 1962-63, he worked as a forestry supervisory technician in the Bắc Kạn Forestry Service. He joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_Party" title="Communist Party">Communist Party</a> on 5 July 1963 and received full membership on 10 July 1964. From 1963-65, Mạnh was the deputy chief of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%E1%BA%A1ch_Th%C3%B4ng" title="Bạch Thông">Bạch Thông</a> wood exploitation <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> team; he later returned to his studies, learning Russian at the Hanoi Foreign Languages College (from 1965–66). He traveled to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leningrad" title="Leningrad">Leningrad</a>, where he studied at the Forestry Institute until 1971. After returning to Vietnam, he became the deputy head of the Bắc Thái provincial forestry inspection board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OfficialBio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OfficialBio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1973-74, Mạnh served as director of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph%C3%BA_L%C6%B0%C6%A1ng" title="Phú Lương">Phú Lương</a> State Forestry Camp in Bắc Thái province. From 1974-76, Mạnh studied at the Nguyễn Ái Quốc High-Level Party School. From 1976-80, he served as the deputy director of the provincial forestry service and director of the construction company of the provincial forestry service. Rising through the party ranks, Mạnh was a member of the Bắc Thái Provincial Party Committee from 1976-83. In 1984, he was named deputy secretary of the committee, and in November 1986, the secretary of the committee. At the <a href="/wiki/6th_National_Congress_of_the_Communist_Party_of_Vietnam" title="6th National Congress of the Communist Party of Vietnam">6th National Congress</a> he was elected as an alternate member of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee" title="Central Committee">Central Committee</a>. At the sixth party plenum in March 1989, he was elevated to full central committee member. Since 1991, he has been in the <a href="/wiki/Politburo" title="Politburo">politburo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mạnh was selected General Secretary of the Communist Party in April 2001. His term was renewed in April 2006. He is the first Vietnamese party head with a university degree. He announced his plans for Vietnam's future as an industrialised country, to be completed by 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
