<p><b>Nong Khai</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">หนองคาย</span>, <small>pronounced</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA for Thai and Lao">[nɔ̌ːŋ kʰāːj]</a></span>) is the northernmost of the <a href="/wiki/Isan" title="Isan">northeastern</a> (Isan) <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Thailand" title="Provinces of Thailand">provinces</a> (<i>changwat</i>) of <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. Neighbouring provinces are (from east clockwise) <a href="/wiki/Bueng_Kan_Province" title="Bueng Kan Province">Bueng Kan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Nakhon_Province" title="Sakon Nakhon Province">Sakon Nakhon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Udon_Thani_Province" title="Udon Thani Province">Udon Thani</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Loei_Province" title="Loei Province">Loei</a>. To the north it borders <a href="/wiki/Vientiane_Province" title="Vientiane Province">Vientiane Province</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vientiane_(prefecture)" title="Vientiane (prefecture)">Vientiane Prefecture</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolikhamxai" title="Bolikhamxai">Bolikhamxai</a> of <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nong_Khai_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province is in the valley of the Mae Nam Kong (<a href="/wiki/Mekong" title="Mekong">Mekong</a> River), which also forms the border with Laos. There are highlands to the south. The Laotian capital <a href="/wiki/Vientiane" title="Vientiane">Vientiane</a> is only 25 kilometres away from the provincial capital of Nong Khai. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai-Lao_Friendship_Bridge" title="Thai-Lao Friendship Bridge">Thai-Lao Friendship Bridge</a>, which connects the two countries, was built jointly by the governments of Thailand, Laos, and Australia, and was opened in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nong_Khai_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Over the centuries the control of the province swung between the Thai Kingdom <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ayutthaya_kingdom" title="Ayutthaya kingdom">Ayutthaya</a>, and the Laotian kingdom <a href="/wiki/Lan_Xang" title="Lan Xang">Lan Xang</a>, as their respective powers ebbed and flowed in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TAT_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TAT-3">[3]</a></sup> See also the <a href="/wiki/Haw_wars" title="Haw wars">Haw wars</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nong_Khai_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of March 23, 2011, the province is subdivided into nine districts (<i><a href="/wiki/Amphoe" title="Amphoe">amphoe</a></i>). The districts are further subdivided into 62 subdistricts (<i><a href="/wiki/Tambon" title="Tambon">tambon</a></i>) and 705 villages (<i><a href="/wiki/Muban" title="Muban">muban</a></i>). The eight districts of <a href="/wiki/Bueng_Kan_Province" title="Bueng Kan Province">Bueng Kan</a> were districts of Nong Khai before, they were split as the newest province of Thailand.</p>
