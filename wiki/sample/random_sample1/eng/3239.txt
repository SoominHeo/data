<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nuh ibn Asad</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Nuh ibn Asad</b> (نوح بن اسد; d. 841/842) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samanid" title="Samanid">Samanid</a> ruler of <a href="/wiki/Samarkand" title="Samarkand">Samarkand</a> (819-841/2). He was a son of <a href="/wiki/Asad_ibn_Saman" title="Asad ibn Saman">Asad</a>.</p>
<p>In 819, Nuh was granted authority over the city of Samarkand by Caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Ma%27mun" title="Al-Ma'mun">Al-Ma'mun's</a> governor of <a href="/wiki/Greater_Khorasan" title="Greater Khorasan">Khurasan</a>, Ghassan ibn 'Abbad, as a reward for his support against the rebel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rafi%27_ibn_Laith" title="Rafi' ibn Laith">Rafi' ibn Laith</a>. In 839/840, Nuh captured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isfijab" title="Isfijab">Isfijab</a> and constructed a wall around it to protect the city from the nomadic pagan Turks living near the borders of the Samanid state. Nuh continued to rule over the city until his death in 841 or 842. Abdallah, the governor of Khurasan, then appointed two of Nuh's brothers, <a href="/wiki/Yahya_ibn_Asad" title="Yahya ibn Asad">Yahya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_ibn_Asad" title="Ahmad ibn Asad">Ahmad</a>, to jointly rule over Samarkand.</p>
