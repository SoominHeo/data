<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medal_table">Medal table</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Winter_Paralympics_medal_table&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Medal table">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Olympic_medal_table" title="Olympic medal table">Olympic medal table</a></div>
<p>The ranking in this table is based on information provided by the <a href="/wiki/International_Paralympic_Committee" title="International Paralympic Committee">International Paralympic Committee</a> (IPC) and is consistent with IPC convention in its published medal tables. By default, the table is ordered by the number of gold medals the athletes from a nation have won (in this context, a "nation" is an entity represented by a <a href="/wiki/National_Paralympic_Committee" title="National Paralympic Committee">National Paralympic Committee</a>). The number of silver medals is taken into consideration next and then the number of bronze medals. If nations are still tied, equal ranking is given and they are listed alphabetically by <a href="/wiki/List_of_IOC_country_codes" title="List of IOC country codes">IPC country code</a>.</p>
