<p><b>Numanohata Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">沼ノ端駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Numanohata-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Chitose_Line" title="Chitose Line">Chitose Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muroran_Main_Line" title="Muroran Main Line">Muroran Main Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tomakomai,_Hokkaido" title="Tomakomai, Hokkaido">Tomakomai, Hokkaido</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido_Railway_Company" title="Hokkaido Railway Company">Hokkaido Railway Company</a> (JR Hokkaido). The station is numbered "H17".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Numanohata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This station serves the Muroran Main and Chitose Lines. It is officially the terminus of the Chitose Line, although all trains terminate at <a href="/wiki/Tomakomai_Station" title="Tomakomai Station">Tomakomai Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Numanohata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of one <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> serving a total of three tracks. The station has automated ticket machines and <a href="/wiki/Kitaca" title="Kitaca">Kitaca</a> card readers (not equipped with regular ticket gates).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The station is unattended.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
