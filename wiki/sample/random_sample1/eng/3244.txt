<p><b>Dacryoadenitis</b> is <a href="/wiki/Inflammation" title="Inflammation">inflammation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lacrimal_gland" title="Lacrimal gland">lacrimal glands</a> (the tear-producing glands).</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes.2C_incidence.2C_and_risk_factors">Causes, incidence, and risk factors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dacryoadenitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Causes, incidence, and risk factors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acute_(medical)" title="Acute (medical)">Acute</a> dacryoadenitis is most commonly due to <a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">viral</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bacterium" title="Bacterium">bacterial</a> <a href="/wiki/Infection" title="Infection">infection</a>. Common causes include <a href="/wiki/Mumps" title="Mumps">mumps</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epstein-Barr_virus" title="Epstein-Barr virus">Epstein-Barr virus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staphylococcus" title="Staphylococcus">staphylococcus</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gonococcus" title="Gonococcus">gonococcus</a>.</p>
<p>Chronic dacryoadenitis is usually due to noninfectious inflammatory disorders. Examples include <a href="/wiki/Sarcoidosis" title="Sarcoidosis">sarcoidosis</a>, thyroid eye disease, and orbital pseudotumor.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symptoms">Symptoms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dacryoadenitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Symptoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Swelling of the outer portion of the upper lid, with possible redness and tenderness</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pain" title="Pain">Pain</a> in the area of swelling</li>
<li>Excess tearing or discharge</li>
<li>Swelling of <a href="/wiki/Lymph_node" title="Lymph node">lymph nodes</a> in front of the <a href="/wiki/Ear" title="Ear">ear</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Signs_and_tests">Signs and tests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dacryoadenitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Signs and tests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dacryoadenitis can be diagnosed by examination of the <a href="/wiki/Human_eye" title="Human eye">eyes</a> and lids. Special tests such as a <a href="/wiki/CT_scan" title="CT scan">CT scan</a> may be required to search for the cause. Sometimes <a href="/wiki/Biopsy" title="Biopsy">biopsy</a> will be needed to be sure that a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tumor" title="Tumor">tumor</a> of the lacrimal gland is not present.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatment">Treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dacryoadenitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>If the cause of dacryoadenitis is a viral condition such as mumps, simple rest and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warm_compresses" title="Warm compresses">warm compresses</a> may be all that is needed. For other causes, the treatment is specific to the causative disease.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prognosis">Prognosis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dacryoadenitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Prognosis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most patients will fully recover from dacryoadenitis. For conditions with more serious causes, such as sarcoidosis, the <a href="/wiki/Prognosis" title="Prognosis">prognosis</a> is that of the underlying condition.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Complications">Complications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dacryoadenitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Complications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Swelling may be severe enough to put pressure on the eye and distort <a href="/wiki/Visual_perception" title="Visual perception">vision</a>. Some patients first thought to have dacryoadenitis may turn out to have a <a href="/wiki/Malignancy" title="Malignancy">malignancy</a> of the lacrimal gland.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prevention">Prevention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dacryoadenitis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Prevention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mumps can be prevented by <a href="/wiki/Immunization" title="Immunization">immunization</a>. Gonococcus, bacteria can be avoided by the use of <a href="/wiki/Condom" title="Condom">condoms</a>. Most other causes cannot be prevented.</p>
