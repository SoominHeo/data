<p>The <b>Autonomous University of Nuevo León</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Universidad Autónoma de Nuevo León, UANL</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public university</a> with seven campuses across the Northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_state" title="Mexican state">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo León">Nuevo León</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. Founded as <b>University of Nuevo León</b> on 25 September 1933, it is the third largest public university in Mexico in terms of student population and the most important institution of higher learning in Northeastern Mexico, which offers the highest number of academic programs. It is also the oldest university in the state, it is currently headquartered in <a href="/wiki/San_Nicol%C3%A1s_de_los_Garza" title="San Nicolás de los Garza">San Nicolás de los Garza</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanlfolleto_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-uanlfolleto-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The UANL has seven distinct campuses: the Main Campus, which houses the Administration Building, Colleges of Law, Biological Sciences, Chemistry, Public Accounting and Philosophy, amongst others, as well as the Football Stadium, and other sport facilities. Other campuses include the Health Sciences Campus, the Mederos Humanities and Fine Arts campus, the Marin Agronomy Center, the Escobedo Agricultural Sciences Campus, the Linares Earth Sciences, and Forestry campus,as well as the Sabinas Hidalgo facilities, where extensions of the Colleges of Law, and Business are housed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanlfolleto_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-uanlfolleto-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The institution counts with 84 Libraries with a total of 2,238,000 Library volumes. It has 27 research facilities with 438 national researchers, 16 academic journals, 9 main campus bookstores, 25 student computer centers and 53 cafeterias.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanlfolleto_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-uanlfolleto-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The University has been ranked by various organizations as one of the best public universities in Mexico and Latin America, it has been ranked 4th place in a publication of the Best Universities Of Mexico 2014 by the Rankia Organization in Mexico,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rankiarank_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-rankiarank-7">[7]</a></sup> and is ranked as one of the 10 most recognized universities in Mexico by a number of organizations like <a href="/wiki/QS_World_University_Rankings" title="QS World University Rankings">QS World University Rankings</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanlrank_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-uanlrank-8">[8]</a></sup> and the Mexican journal "El Universal".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universidad_Aut%C3%B3noma_de_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation">Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universidad_Aut%C3%B3noma_de_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="767" data-file-width="1028" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg/220px-Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg/330px-Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg/440px-Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Colegio_Civil_Mty.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Civil Academy, one of the five institutions that joined efforts to create the University of Nuevo León in 1933.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The immediate forerunner of this public university dates back to 1859, when the Civil Academy (<i>Colegio Civil</i>) started its courses. This <a href="/wiki/Institution" title="Institution">institution</a> had been envisioned by <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Governor of Nuevo León">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Vidaurri" title="Santiago Vidaurri">Santiago Vidaurri</a> but was erected by his successor, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Silvestre_Aramberri&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="José Silvestre Aramberri (page does not exist)">José Silvestre Aramberri</a>, after the former was removed from office. The academy began to sponsor the Pharmacy and Medicine courses <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Eleuterio_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Eleuterio González">José Eleuterio González</a> had been teaching at the local Hospital of the Rosary for several decades. The academic offerings included courses in Law and Medicine, as well as a <a href="/wiki/University-preparatory_school" title="University-preparatory school">preparatory school</a>. Its first director was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_de_Jes%C3%BAs_D%C3%A1vila_y_Prieto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="José de Jesús Dávila y Prieto (page does not exist)">José de Jesús Dávila y Prieto</a>.</p>
<p>On 29 October 1932, the delegations of the state schools of Law, Medicine and Pharmacy; the Civil Academy and the <a href="/wiki/Normal_school" title="Normal school">normal school</a>, submitted a project to the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Congress of Nuevo León">Congress of Nuevo León</a> to organize a state university for the <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_Metropolitan_area" title="Monterrey Metropolitan area">Monterrey Metropolitan area</a>. On 7 November 1932, the state congress ordered to proceed with the request and on 25 September 1933 the University of Nuevo León was born.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanl-history_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-uanl-history-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In its first year, 1,864 students were schooled by 218 professors from the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(division)" title="Faculty (division)">faculties</a> of Medicine, Law, Engineering, and Chemistry, the Normal School, a preparatory school, the School of Nurses and Obstetricians and the Álvaro Obregón and Pablo Livas Industrial Schools. The institution became plagued with political disputes and almost two years later, on 25 September 1935, it was closed by state decree, only to reopen almost eight years later, on 13 September 1943.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanl-history_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-uanl-history-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion">Expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universidad_Aut%C3%B3noma_de_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The number of faculty and personnel began to grow and this prompted the construction of its own campus in 1958, the University City (<i>Ciudad Universitaria</i>), an academic complex located in <a href="/wiki/San_Nicol%C3%A1s_de_los_Garza" title="San Nicolás de los Garza">San Nicolás de los Garza</a>, a suburb to the state capital. In 1967, its open-air <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Universitario" title="Estadio Universitario">stadium</a> was finished. From 1968 to 1972, the University was hit once again by student protests and political disputes, and by 1971 the protests had forced the government to stop from interfering in its internal affairs and recognize a <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_university" title="Autonomous university">statute of autonomy</a> that became part of its current name: Autonomous University of Nuevo León (<i>Universidad Autónoma de Nuevo León</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanl-chron_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-uanl-chron-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the late 1990s the institution saw the necessity to rent its professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football (soccer)</a> team, which has maintained in the Mexican professional league (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tigres_de_la_UANL" title="Tigres de la UANL">Tigres de la UANL</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cemex-tigres_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-cemex-tigres-11">[11]</a></sup> and was later affected by a corruption scandal that involved its own rector but ended with no legal consequences for those accused.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universidad_Aut%C3%B3noma_de_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The university has seven campuses across the state:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>University City</b> (<i>Ciudad Universitaria</i>) in <a href="/wiki/San_Nicol%C3%A1s_de_los_Garza" title="San Nicolás de los Garza">San Nicolás de los Garza</a>, which houses thirteen <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(division)" title="Faculty (division)">faculty schools</a>; a 43,150-seat, open air <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Universitario" title="Estadio Universitario">stadium</a>; its two main libraries, computer labs, cafeterias and the main bookstore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_UANL_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-About_UANL-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Agricultural and Animal Sciences Campus</b>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Escobedo,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Escobedo, Nuevo León">Escobedo</a>, houses a local branch of both the <a href="/wiki/Agronomy" title="Agronomy">Agronomy</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veterinary_Medicine" title="Veterinary Medicine">Veterinary Medicine</a> faculty schools, a center for research and development in food sciences (CIDIA) and a center for business development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCA_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCA-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Health Sciences Campus</b>, in <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>, housing the Dentistry, Medicine, Nursing, Psychology, Public Health and Nutrition faculty schools, along the University Hospital and a health clinic for its personnel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_UANL_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-About_UANL-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Marin Campus</b> in <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADn,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Marín, Nuevo León">Marin</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Agronomy" title="Agronomy">Agronomy</a> faculty school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_UANL_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-About_UANL-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Mederos Campus</b>, in <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>, housing the Economics, Communication Studies, Music, Political Sciences, Stage Arts and Visual Arts faculty schools, along the university theater, a state branch of the <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Centers_Association" title="World Trade Centers Association">World Trade Centers Association</a> and the Bilingual Education Research Center (CIDEB).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_UANL_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-About_UANL-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Linares Campus</b> in the Southern municipality of <a href="/wiki/Linares,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Linares, Nuevo León">Linares</a>, housing the Earth Sciences, Forestry, and a branch of the Accounting and Business Administration faculty schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_UANL_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-About_UANL-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sabinas Hidalgo Campus</b> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sabinas_Hidalgo" title="Sabinas Hidalgo">Sabinas Hidalgo</a>, home of a local branch of Law and Business Administration faculty schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_UANL_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-About_UANL-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparatorias_.28High_Schools.29">Preparatorias (High Schools)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universidad_Aut%C3%B3noma_de_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Preparatorias (High Schools)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The university also counts with a 36 High Schools of which 7 of them are Technical High Schools, one is a Bilingual International High School, and the rest are 2 year High Schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanl-prepa_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-uanl-prepa-14">[14]</a></sup> 25 other schools that are not property of the UANL around the state are incorporated to the university, this means that these schools meet the norms established by the institution (a minimum infrastructure of libraries,laboratories,class rooms,school programs accepted by the UANL, and certified academic staff).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uanl-incorp_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-uanl-incorp-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>The current rector is <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Ancer_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Jesús Ancer Rodríguez">Jesús Ancer Rodríguez</a>, a former Director of the Faculty of Medicine and the university hospital, who serves as an interim after José Antonio González Treviño resigned to serve as the state's secretary of education in the cabinet of <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Medina_de_la_Cruz" title="Rodrigo Medina de la Cruz">Rodrigo Medina de la Cruz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rect-interino_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rect-interino-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The university also has a symphony orchestra, a chamber orchestra, and a band of regional music, <i>El Tigre</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universidad_Aut%C3%B3noma_de_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The UANL's teams are called the <a href="/wiki/Tiger" title="Tiger">Tigres</a>.</p>
<p>The American football team, named the <b>Auténticos Tigres</b>, is very successful in the <a href="/wiki/ONEFA" title="ONEFA">ONEFA</a>. The program has won three national championships (1974, 1977 and 2009), and four times has been runner-up. They play in the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Gaspar_Mass" title="Estadio Gaspar Mass">Estadio Gaspar Mass</a>.</p>
<p>UANL teams have also won the national <a href="/wiki/Universiade" title="Universiade">universiade</a> in 2006, 2007, 2008 and 2009 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The UANL installations include the Raymundo "Chico" Rivera Stadium (a football and athletic field), the Luis Eugenio Todd Gym, a baseball park, an indoor soccer pitch, and a <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> center with four courts<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The soccer team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tigres_de_la_UANL" title="Tigres de la UANL">Tigres de la UANL</a> was managed for the UANL before 1996, when the team's control passed to <a href="/wiki/Cemex" title="Cemex">Cemex</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Universitario" title="Estadio Universitario">Estadio Universitario</a> is located in the principal campus of the UANL, and occasionally is host of Auténticos Tigres games, and others activities of the university.</p>
<p>The University Olympic Aquatic Center <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_Acu%C3%A1tico_Ol%C3%ADmpico_Universitario&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Centro Acuático Olímpico Universitario (page does not exist)">Centro Acuático Olímpico Universitario</a> is one of the best aquatic centers of the country. It's also located in the main campus and there are swimming, water polo, diving and synchronized swimming teams.</p>
