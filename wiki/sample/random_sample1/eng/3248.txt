<p><b>Nuevo León</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈnweβo leˈon]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:NuevoLeon.ogg" title="File:NuevoLeon.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5a/NuevoLeon.ogg" title="NuevoLeon.ogg">listen</a>)</small>), or <b>New Leon</b>, officially the <b>Free and Sovereign State of Nuevo León</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Estado Libre y Soberano de Nuevo León</i></span>), is one of the 31 states which, with <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, compose the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_divisions_of_Mexico" title="Political divisions of Mexico">32 Federal Entities</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. It is divided into <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Municipalities of Nuevo León">51 municipalities</a> and its capital city is <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>.</p>
<p>It is located in <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Mexico" title="Northeastern Mexico">Northeastern Mexico</a>. It is bordered by the states of <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> to the north and east, <a href="/wiki/San_Luis_Potos%C3%AD" title="San Luis Potosí">San Luis Potosí</a> to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">Coahuila</a> to the west. To the north, Nuevo León has a 15 kilometer (9 mi) stretch of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S.%E2%80%93Mexico_border" title="U.S.–Mexico border">U.S.–Mexico border</a> adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.</p>
<p>The state was named after the <a href="/wiki/New_Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="New Kingdom of León">New Kingdom of León</a>, an administrative territory of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Spain" title="Viceroyalty of New Spain">Viceroyalty of New Spain</a>, which was itself named for the historic Spanish <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">Kingdom of León</a>.</p>
<p>Besides its capital, other important cities are <a href="/wiki/Guadalupe,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Guadalupe, Nuevo León">Guadalupe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Santa Catarina, Nuevo León">Santa Catarina</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Nicol%C3%A1s_de_los_Garza" title="San Nicolás de los Garza">San Nicolás de los Garza</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Garza_Garc%C3%ADa" title="San Pedro Garza García">San Pedro Garza García</a>, all of which are part of the <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_Metropolitan_area" title="Monterrey Metropolitan area">Monterrey Metropolitan area</a>.</p>

<p>Nuevo León was originally founded by conquistador <a href="/wiki/Alberto_del_Canto" title="Alberto del Canto">Alberto del Canto</a>, although frequent raids by <a href="/wiki/Chichimeca" title="Chichimeca">Chichimecas</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_Americans_(Americas)" title="Native Americans (Americas)">natives</a> of the north, prevented the establishment of almost any permanent settlements. Subsequent to the failure of del Canto to populate the area, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luis_Carvajal_y_de_la_Cueva" title="Luis Carvajal y de la Cueva">Luis Carvajal y de la Cueva</a>, at the head of a group of Portuguese and Spanish settlers who were of Jewish descent, requested permission from the Spanish King to attempt to settle the area which would be called the <a href="/wiki/New_Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="New Kingdom of León">New Kingdom of León</a> and would fail as well. Business rivals later persecuted the Carbajal family by sicking the Inquisition upon them in the 16th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It wasn't until 1596 under the leadership of Diego de Montemayor the colony became permanent. Nuevo Leon eventually became (along with the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">Coahuila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Santander" title="Nuevo Santander">Nuevo Santander</a> and <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>) one of the Eastern Internal Provinces in Northern New Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 19th century, Nuevo León was in a growth spurt and the bargain land deals attracted immigrants of <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">Slavic</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_people" title="Italian people">Italian</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jewish</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English-American" title="English-American">Anglo-American</a> origin. The capital of Nuevo León is <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>, the third largest city in Mexico with over four million residents. Monterrey is a modern and affluent city, and Nuevo León has long been one of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>'s most industrialized states.</p>

<p>Nuevo León has an extreme climate, and there is very little rainfall throughout the year. The territory covers 64,220 square kilometres (24,800 sq mi), and can be divided into three regions: a hot, dry region in the north, a temperate region in the mountains, and a semi-arid region in the south. The <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental" title="Sierra Madre Oriental">Sierra Madre Oriental</a> mountain range affects in an important way the lay of the land forming the Galeana and Doctor Arroyo plateaus, the Iguana, Picachos, Papagayos, and Santa Clara mountain ranges, and the Pilón, Ascensión, and Río Blanco valleys. As for hydrography, the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_River_(Tamaulipas)" title="San Juan River (Tamaulipas)">San Juan River</a> supplies the El Cuchillo dam, which provides water for <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a> and the metropolitan area. There are also the Cerro Prieto, La Boca, Vaquerías, Nogalitos, and Agualeguas dams. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Laguna_de_Labradores&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Laguna de Labradores (page does not exist)">Laguna de Labradores</a> is a major lake in Nuevo León, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pozo_del_Gavil%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pozo del Gavilán (page does not exist)">Pozo del Gavilán</a> is a natural well. Both are located in the <a href="/wiki/Galeana,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Galeana, Nuevo León">Galeana</a> municipality. The flora of the region includes brush and pastures in the low regions, and pine and oak trees in the mountains. The fauna includes <a href="/wiki/American_black_bear" title="American black bear">black bears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cougar" title="Cougar">mountain lions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peccary" title="Peccary">javelinas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prairie_dog" title="Prairie dog">prairie dogs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fox" title="Fox">foxes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coyote" title="Coyote">coyotes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/White-tailed_deer" title="White-tailed deer">white-tailed deer</a>, along with smaller species.</p>

<p>As of 2010, Nuevo León's population was about 4.653 million. Of these 4.653 million, nearly 80%, or about 3.7 million, of the state's population resides within the Monterrey Metropolitan area, making it the third largest metropolitan area in the country. Life expectancy in the state is high, being 73 years for men and 79 years for women.</p>
<p>Ninety-four percent of the total population occupy urban areas, one million of which are home-owners, and 98% have all utilities (running water, sewer systems and electric power). The remaining 2% is mostly the small indigenous population which is isolated and lives in the mountain regions.</p>
<p>Following the nation's tendency, a majority of the population identifies as being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a>.</p>
<p>Regarding ethnicity, the state has one of the highest European descended, white populations,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> a trend very apparent in Mexico's northern region. The majority of the people within the state are of Spanish, French, or German descent. Mestizos are also dominant in the state.</p>

<p>Highly industrialized, Nuevo León possesses a standard of living similar to that of countries such as Croatia, Slovakia or Poland. In 2007, the per capita GDP of the state was similar to that of the Asian Tiger of South Korea and even higher than that of some European Union states such as Slovakia and Hungary. At about $27,000, it was the highest GDP per capita (PPP) of any Mexican state (not counting the Federal District, which also has a very high per capita), and was therefore higher than the Mexican national average (2013 GDP per capita (PPP) national average was $15,700).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of its municipalities, <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Garza_Garc%C3%ADa" title="San Pedro Garza García">San Pedro Garza García</a>, is among the richest in the country in terms of per capita income. It is also home of powerful conglomerates, such as <a href="/wiki/Cemex" title="Cemex">Cemex</a> (one of the largest construction materials firms in the world), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bimbo_(company)" title="Bimbo (company)">Bimbo</a> (bakery and pastry), <a href="/wiki/Gruma" title="Gruma">Maseca</a> (food and grains), <a href="/wiki/Banorte" title="Banorte">Banorte</a> (the only high-street bank in Mexico wholly owned by Mexicans), <a href="/wiki/ALFA_(Mexico)" title="ALFA (Mexico)">ALFA</a> (Sigma, Alestra, Nemak, Alpek and Hylsa (recently bought by Ternium), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=I-service&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="I-service (page does not exist)">i-service</a> (HelpDesk), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vitro_SA&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vitro SA (page does not exist)">Vitro SA</a> (glass), <a href="/wiki/FEMSA" title="FEMSA">FEMSA</a> (Coca-Cola in Latin America), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cervecer%C3%ADa_Cuauht%C3%A9moc_Moctezuma" title="Cervecería Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma">Cervecería Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma</a> (brewers of <i>Sol</i>, <i>Tecate</i>, <i>XX</i>, <i>Bohemia</i>, <i>Indio</i> and <i>Nochebuena</i>).</p>

<p>Nuevo León also boasts a rich agricultural core, called the "orange belt", which comprises the municipalities of Allende, <a href="/wiki/Montemorelos" title="Montemorelos">Montemorelos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hualahuises" title="Hualahuises">Hualahuises</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Ter%C3%A1n" title="General Terán">General Terán</a> and <a href="/wiki/Linares,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Linares, Nuevo León">Linares</a>. Small but productive investments have been transforming traditional harvests (mainly based on orange and cereals) into agroindustrial developments that are producing increasing revenues for the local economy.</p>
<p>In contrast with the relative wealth of industrial Nuevo León and the orange belt, the Southern part of the state (municipalities of Galeana, <a href="/wiki/Aramberri,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Aramberri, Nuevo León">Aramberri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Arroyo" title="Doctor Arroyo">Doctor Arroyo</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mier_y_Noriega&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mier y Noriega (page does not exist)">Mier y Noriega</a>) remains rural and less productive. Most of The South of the state is at the mercy of a very dry weather that represents a major hurdle for agriculture and livestock.</p>
<p>As of 2010, Nuevo León’s economy represents 11.4% of Mexico’s total <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a> or 165 billion USD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Nuevo León's economy has a strong focus on export oriented manufacturing (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Maquiladora" title="Maquiladora">maquiladora</a> / <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=INMEX&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="INMEX (page does not exist)">INMEX</a>). As of 2005, 431,551 people are employed in the manufacturing sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Foreign direct investment in Nuevo León was 1,213.1 million USD for 2005.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In recent years, the state government has been making efforts in attracting significant investments in <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechatronics" title="Mechatronics">mechatronics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Information_and_communication_technologies" title="Information and communication technologies">information and communication technologies</a> fields with the creation of the Research and Technology Innovation Park PIIT (Parque de Investigación e Innovación Tecnológica), a technology park oriented in the development, innovation and research of sciences. The project is one of the key strategies within the Monterrey, City of Knowledge program. The park is located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Apodaca" title="Apodaca">Apodaca</a>, part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greater_Monterrey" title="Greater Monterrey">Greater Monterrey</a> at the 10 km of the highway to Monterrey’s International Airport. It consists of a total surface area of 70 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">Ha</a> (172 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acres" title="Acres">acres</a>), half of it already committed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26D" title="R&amp;D">R&amp;D</a> centers. The other 35 Ha (86 acres) are available for research and development centers, and for businesses that meet the Park’s objectives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<dl>
<dt>Official name</dt>
<dd><i>Estado Libre y Soberano de Nuevo León</i> (Free and Sovereign State of Nuevo León).</dd>
<dt>Official motto</dt>
<dd>Latin: <i>Semper Ascendens</i> (Always Ascending).</dd>
<dt>Type of government</dt>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">Republican</a> and representative according to 30th article of the local <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a>.</dd>
<dt>Executive</dt>
<dd>In 6 July 2003 <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">gubernatorial</a> election, <i>Alianza Ciudadana</i> – an electoral alliance between the <a href="/wiki/Institutional_Revolutionary_Party" title="Institutional Revolutionary Party">Institutional Revolutionary Party</a> (PRI) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Ecological_Party_of_Mexico" title="Green Ecological Party of Mexico">Green Ecological Party of Mexico</a> (PVEM) – regained control of the state from <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Fox" title="Vicente Fox">President Fox</a>'s party <a href="/wiki/National_Action_Party_(Mexico)" title="National Action Party (Mexico)">National Action Party</a> (PAN). The new governor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natividad_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Par%C3%A1s" title="Natividad González Parás">Natividad González Parás</a> of the PRI, was sworn in on 4 October 2003 for a period of six years.</dd>
<dt>Cabinet</dt>
<dd>Chosen directly by the Governor except for the General Comptroller and the State General Attorney, which are elected by Congress from a list of names provided by the Governor.</dd>
<dt>Legislative</dt>
<dd>The State has a unicameral chamber. The LXXI <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Congress of Nuevo León">Congress of Nuevo León</a> is composed of 42 <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies" title="Chamber of Deputies">deputies</a>, 26 of them chosen by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_Past_the_Post_electoral_system" title="First Past the Post electoral system">first-past-the-post</a> electoral districts and 16 of them by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proportional_Representation" title="Proportional Representation">proportional representation</a> on a party-list basis. The parties represented are the PRI with 15 deputies, the PAN with 22 deputies, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partido_del_Trabajo" title="Partido del Trabajo">Partido del Trabajo</a> (PT) with two deputies, the <a href="/wiki/Party_of_the_Democratic_Revolution" title="Party of the Democratic Revolution">Party of the Democratic Revolution</a> (PRD) with one, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Alliance_(Mexico)" title="New Alliance (Mexico)">New Alliance party</a> (PANAL) with two deputies.</dd>
<dt>Judiciary</dt>
<dd>Judicial power rests in the <a href="/wiki/Superior_Court_of_Justice_of_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Superior Court of Justice of Nuevo León">Superior Court of Justice of Nuevo León</a>, led by Minister <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gustavo_Adolfo_Guerrero_Guti%C3%A9rrez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gustavo Adolfo Guerrero Gutiérrez (page does not exist)">Gustavo Adolfo Guerrero Gutiérrez</a>.</dd>
<dt>Political parties</dt>
<dd>Official recognition is given by the State Electoral Commission to those parties getting more than 1.5% of the votes in the last election (Art.40 of the State Electoral Law), which are the ones represented in Congress.</dd>
</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nuevo León has many <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biomes</a>, which is why it has different climates. Some areas in the mountains are very cold in winter and temperate in summer. In the northern part of the state the climate is <a href="/wiki/Arid" title="Arid">arid</a> as a result of the proximity to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chihuahuan_desert" title="Chihuahuan desert">Chihuahuan desert</a>. Extreme high temperatures of 47 °C or more occur on the desert areas while winters are short and mild. In <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a> the climate is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semi-arid" title="Semi-arid">hot semi-arid</a> with extreme hot summers and mild winters. There is very little rainfall throughout the year, usually about 500 mm or less.</p>
