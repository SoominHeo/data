<p><b>Jun Seba</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">瀬葉淳</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Seba Jun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, February 7, 1974 – February 26, 2010)</span>, born in the <a href="/wiki/Nishi-Azabu,_Minato,_Tokyo" title="Nishi-Azabu, Minato, Tokyo">Nishi-Azabu</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">Minato</a>, was a Japanese record producer, DJ, composer and arranger who recorded under the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Nujabes</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ヌジャベス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nujabesu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. Nujabes (pronounced <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/u/ 'u' in 'situation'">u</span><span title="/dʒ/ 'j' in 'jam'">dʒ</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>) was an <a href="/wiki/Anagram" title="Anagram">anagram</a> of his name, being the reverse spelling of Seba Jun. He was the owner of two <a href="/wiki/Shibuya" title="Shibuya">Shibuya</a> record stores, T Records and Guinness Records, and founder of the independent label <a href="/wiki/Hydeout_Productions" title="Hydeout Productions">Hydeout Productions</a>. His style mixes hip hop and jazz samples. Nujabes collaborated with other Japanese artists such as <a href="/wiki/Uyama_Hiroto" title="Uyama Hiroto">Uyama Hiroto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shing02" title="Shing02">Shing02</a> (with whom he created the critically acclaimed "Luv(sic)" <a href="/wiki/Hexalogy" title="Hexalogy">hexalogy</a>), <a href="/wiki/Minmi" title="Minmi">Minmi</a>, and was also involved with America's <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground hip hop</a> scene through collaboration with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CYNE_(hip_hop_group)" title="CYNE (hip hop group)">CYNE</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apani_B" title="Apani B">Apani B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Five_Deez" title="Five Deez">Five Deez</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Substantial" title="Substantial">Substantial</a>, <a href="/wiki/CL_Smooth" title="CL Smooth">CL Smooth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Jon" title="Fat Jon">Fat Jon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Terry_Callier" title="Terry Callier">Terry Callier</a>. In his career he released three studio albums (<i><a href="/wiki/Metaphorical_Music" title="Metaphorical Music">Metaphorical Music</a></i> in 2003, <i><a href="/wiki/Modal_Soul" title="Modal Soul">Modal Soul</a></i> in 2005, and <i><a href="/wiki/Spiritual_State" title="Spiritual State">Spiritual State</a></i>, released posthumously in 2011) and two collection compilations (<i><a href="/wiki/Hydeout_Productions_1st_Collection" title="Hydeout Productions 1st Collection">Hydeout Productions 1st Collection</a></i> in 2003 and <i><a href="/wiki/Hydeout_Productions_2nd_Collection" title="Hydeout Productions 2nd Collection">2nd Collection</a></i> in 2007).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> One of his most recognizable works is that of the background music for <a href="/wiki/Shinichir%C5%8D_Watanabe" title="Shinichirō Watanabe">Shinichirō Watanabe's</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Champloo" title="Samurai Champloo">Samurai Champloo</a></i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Champloo_Music_Record:_Departure" title="Samurai Champloo Music Record: Departure">Music Record: Departure</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Champloo_Music_Record:_Impression" title="Samurai Champloo Music Record: Impression">Impression</a></i>) in 2004.</p>
<p>On February 26, 2010 Jun Seba died in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traffic_accident" title="Traffic accident">traffic accident</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tribute2015_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tribute2015-2">[2]</a></sup> Following his death, numerous tributes were made by other artists from around the world (including his own label Hydeout Productions).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nujabes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nujabes was best known for his approach to hip hop beats, often blending jazz influences into his songs creating a mellow, nostalgic and atmospheric sound. He sampled a wide range of instruments and genres to create a soothing vibe. In addition to Japanese artists like <a href="/wiki/Uyama_Hiroto" title="Uyama Hiroto">Uyama Hiroto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shing02" title="Shing02">Shing02</a>, L-Universe, and <a href="/wiki/Minmi" title="Minmi">Minmi</a>, Nujabes collaborated with various underground <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> hip hop acts such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CYNE_(hip_hop_group)" title="CYNE (hip hop group)">CYNE</a>, Cise Starr (as a solo apart from the hip-hop collective CYNE), <a href="/wiki/Apani_B" title="Apani B">Apani B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Five_Deez" title="Five Deez">Five Deez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Substantial_(rapper)" title="Substantial (rapper)">Substantial</a>, <a href="/wiki/CL_Smooth" title="CL Smooth">CL Smooth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Jon" title="Fat Jon">Fat Jon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terry_Callier" title="Terry Callier">Terry Callier</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> rapper Funky DL. He was also a member of the production duo Urbanforest, an experimental collaboration with Nao T.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nujabes was one of the most prolific contributors to the background music and soundtracks of <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Champloo" title="Samurai Champloo">Samurai Champloo</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> which blends a feudal Japan setting with modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anachronisms" title="Anachronisms">anachronisms</a>, especially in regard to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop culture</a> such as graffiti and rapping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nujabes collaborated with Shing02 on the critically acclaimed "Luv(sic)" <a href="/wiki/Hexalogy" title="Hexalogy">hexalogy</a>. They made parts 1-3 together, but after Seba's death many doubted that the series could ever be completed. However, parts 4 and 5 had already been completed, and were released shortly after Nujabes' death. Although part 6 was not among the previously completed tracks, it was released on February 26, 2013, 3 years after the day Seba had died. According to Shing02's official Facebook page, the instrumental to what would eventually be part 6 was discovered on Nujabes' cell phone a few weeks after his death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nujabes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 26, 2010, Nujabes was involved in a traffic accident upon exiting the <a href="/wiki/Shuto_Expressway" title="Shuto Expressway">Shuto Expressway</a> late at night. He was pronounced dead at a hospital in Shibuya Ward after efforts to revive him failed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nujabes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nujabes' death has elicited many tributes from other artists around the world. On <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a>, the New York based Digi Crates records have released a series of tribute albums performed by various artists in a style reminiscent of Seba's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In addition, Nujabes' former label <a href="/wiki/Hydeout_Productions" title="Hydeout Productions">Hydeout Productions</a> released a tribute album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Modal_Soul_Classics_II" title="Modal Soul Classics II">Modal Soul Classics II</a></i> featuring a number of former collaborators and with lyrics and song titles referencing select tracks from both <i><a href="/wiki/Modal_Soul" title="Modal Soul">Modal Soul</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Metaphorical_Music" title="Metaphorical Music">Metaphorical Music</a></i>, such as the track "Music is Ours", which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Direct_reference" title="Direct reference">directly references</a> "Music is Mine", the 5th track of <i>Modal Soul</i>.</p>
<p>Frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Shing02" title="Shing02">Shing02</a> has performed at many tribute concerts to the late DJ,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and has remarked that "Through his soulful music, Nujabes has touched so many people around the world, even beyond his dreams", and "[I] deeply regret the loss of a unique talent and a close friend."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013 Australian producer <a href="/wiki/Ta-ku" title="Ta-ku">Ta-ku</a> released the Tribute "25 Nights for Nujabes".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Daniel Hodgman, founder of hip hop culture website BonusCut, in a 2014 highlight of Nujabes' <a href="/wiki/Modal_Soul" title="Modal Soul">Modal Soul</a> has claimed that, "Nujabes may not be physically with us anymore, but through his music, his legacy and lust for life lives on". Co-founder Gus Navarro remarked that, "The music of the late producer Nujabes is something to hold onto and never let go."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
