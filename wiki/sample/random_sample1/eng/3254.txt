<p><i><b>Nunchi</b></i>, sometimes <i><b>noonchi</b></i>, is a Korean concept signifying the subtle art and ability to listen and gauge others' moods. In Western culture, nunchi could be described as the concept of <a href="/wiki/Emotional_intelligence" title="Emotional intelligence">emotional intelligence</a>. It is of central importance to the dynamics of <a href="/wiki/Interpersonal_relationship" title="Interpersonal relationship">interpersonal relationships</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <i>Nunchi</i> is literally translated as "eye-measure".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VegdahlHur2008_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-VegdahlHur2008-1">[1]</a></sup> It is closely related to the broader concept of <a href="/wiki/Paralanguage" title="Paralanguage">paralanguage</a>, however <i>nunchi</i> also relies on an understanding of one's status relative to the person with whom they're interacting. It can be seen as the embodiment of skills necessary to communicate effectively in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_context_culture" title="High context culture">high context culture</a>.</p>
<p>The concept of <i>nunchi</i>, and one's abundance or lack thereof, forms the basis of many common expressions and idioms. For example, a socially clumsy person can be described as <i>nunchi eoptta</i> (눈치 없다), meaning "absence of nunchi."</p>
