<p><b>New Blood Rising</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> produced by <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Championship Wrestling">World Championship Wrestling</a> that replaced WCW's August PPV event <a href="/wiki/Road_Wild" title="Road Wild">Road Wild</a> held from 1997 to 1999 on a Saturday. It took place on August 13, 2000 from the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coliseum" title="Pacific Coliseum">Pacific Coliseum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver, British Columbia</a>. The name is a reference to the <a href="/wiki/The_New_Blood" title="The New Blood">New Blood</a> faction within WCW (which dissolved before the pay-per-view). This WCW PPV event was held on a Sunday instead of a Saturday. Despite never being announced before or during the show, every match on the card was contested under no disqualification rules. In 2014, All WCW pay-per-views were made available on the WWE Network.</p>

<p>*"Canadian Rules" means that, instead of a traditional three-count, the referee must administer a five-count. Following the five-count, the competitor has to stay down for ten seconds. If both men are down for a ten-count for any reason, the first man who gets to his feet wins the match. In addition, there are no submissions and no count-outs.<br/></p>
<p>**After being absent from the pay-per-view due to a (kayfabe) mysterious motorcycle accident, Goldberg did not show up until the middle of the match. Then, in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worked_shoot" title="Worked shoot">worked shoot</a>, Goldberg walked out on the match, apparently refusing to take Kevin Nash's Jacknife Powerbomb.</p>
