<p>The <b>1998 <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games" title="Asian Games">Asian Games</a></b> (Thai: เอเชียนเกมส์ 1998), officially known as the <b>XIII Asiad</b>, was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multi-sport" title="Multi-sport">multi-sport</a> event celebrated in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> from December 6 – December 20, 1998, with 376 events in 36 <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games_sports" title="Asian Games sports">sports and disciplines</a> featured in the Games.</p>
<p>Bangkok was awarded the right on September 26, 1990, defeating <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> to host the Games. Bangkok was the first city to hosted the Asian Games for four times.</p>
<p>The final medal tally was led by <a href="/wiki/China_at_the_1998_Asian_Games" title="China at the 1998 Asian Games">China</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_at_the_1998_Asian_Games" title="South Korea at the 1998 Asian Games">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan_at_the_1998_Asian_Games" title="Japan at the 1998 Asian Games">Japan</a> and the host <a href="/wiki/Thailand_at_the_1998_Asian_Games" title="Thailand at the 1998 Asian Games">Thailand</a>. Thailand set a new record with 24 gold medals. In addition, Japanese Athletics <a href="/wiki/Koji_Ito" title="Koji Ito">Koji Ito</a> was announced as the <a href="/wiki/Most_valuable_player" title="Most valuable player">most valuable player</a> (MVP) of the Games., the games were deemed generally successful with the rising standard of competition amongst the Asian nations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Asian_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bidding">Bidding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Asian_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bidding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three cities bid for the Games. All three, Taipei (Chinese Taipei), Jakarta (Indonesia) and Bangkok (Thailand) submitted their formal bid in 1989.</p>
<p>The vote was held on September 27, 1990, at the China Palace Tower Hotel in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, during the OCA's general assembly. All 37 members voted, with voting held in secret ballot. It was announced that Bangkok won the rights. Though the vote results were not released, it was revealed that Bangkok won by 20-10-7.</p>
<p>After that, Bangkok was the first city to hosted the Asian Games for four times, after the <a href="/wiki/1966_Asian_Games" title="1966 Asian Games">1966</a>, <a href="/wiki/1970_Asian_Games" title="1970 Asian Games">1970</a> and <a href="/wiki/1978_Asian_Games" title="1978 Asian Games">1978</a> games and the first game was Bangkok hosted by the bidding.</p>
<p>19 votes were needed for selection.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emblem">Emblem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Asian_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Emblem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Official Emblem of the 13th <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games" title="Asian Games">Asian Games</a> elements from Asia in general and <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> in particular. It is based on the letter A, representing Asia and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">Athletes</a>. The Maha <a href="/wiki/Stupa" title="Stupa">Chedi</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Pagoda" title="Pagoda">pagoda</a> shape, represents Thailand, in particular. The pinnacle of the Maha Chedi symbolises the knowledge, intelligence and athletic prowess of Thailand's forefathers, which are second to none. The top is part of the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Council_of_Asia" title="Olympic Council of Asia">OCA</a> logo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Asian_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:13th_asiad_mascot.png"><img alt="13th asiad mascot.png" data-file-height="102" data-file-width="92" height="102" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/c6/13th_asiad_mascot.png" width="92"/></a></div>
<p>The official <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">Mascot</a> of the 13th Asian Games is an <a href="/wiki/Elephant" title="Elephant">elephant</a>. The elephant is a very distinctive animal which has lived with the people of <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> for many generations and is universally admired for its strengths and nobility. The mascot's name <b>Chai-Yo</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th">ไชโย</span>) (a Thai word meaning pleasure, gladness, success, unity and happiness) is usually shouted by a group of people to show their unity and solidarity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Participating_NOCs">Participating NOCs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Asian_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Participating NOCs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
