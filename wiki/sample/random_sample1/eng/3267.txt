<p><i><b>Heterometrus cyaneus</b></i>, the <b>Asian Blue Forest Scorpion</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scorpion" title="Scorpion">scorpions</a> belonging to the family <a href="/wiki/Scorpionidae" title="Scorpionidae">Scorpionidae</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heterometrus_cyaneus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Heterometrus cyaneus</i> can reach a length of 12–15 centimetres (4.7–5.9 in). These scorpions are dark black, with blue reflections. Body is strongly granulated. This species is classified as harmful, as the sting causes moderate to severe pain but without further consequences. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fa_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fa-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heterometrus_cyaneus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species is native to Borneo, Philippines and Indonesia. It can be found in tropical rainforests and wetland forests, usually under stones or fallen trees. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fa_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-fa-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
