<p><b>Newry and Mourne District Council</b> (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <span lang="ga"><i>Comhairle an Iúir agus Mhúrn</i></span>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a local council in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>. It merged with <a href="/wiki/Down_District_Council" title="Down District Council">Down District Council</a> in May 2015 under local government reorganisation in Northern Ireland to become <a href="/wiki/Newry,_Mourne_and_Down_District_Council" title="Newry, Mourne and Down District Council">Newry, Mourne and Down District Council</a>.</p>
<p>It included much of the south of <a href="/wiki/County_Armagh" title="County Armagh">County Armagh</a> and the south of <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">County Down</a> and had a population of over 99,000. Council headquarters were in <a href="/wiki/Newry" title="Newry">Newry</a>, the largest settlement and only city in the area; it has a population of 28,850. Other towns in the council area included <a href="/wiki/Crossmaglen" title="Crossmaglen">Crossmaglen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bessbrook" title="Bessbrook">Bessbrook</a> in County Armagh and <a href="/wiki/Warrenpoint" title="Warrenpoint">Warrenpoint</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rostrevor" title="Rostrevor">Rostrevor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilltown,_County_Down" title="Hilltown, County Down">Hilltown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annalong" title="Annalong">Annalong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kilkeel" title="Kilkeel">Kilkeel</a> (an important fishing port) in Down.</p>
<p>The council was formed in 1973 under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_(Northern_Ireland)_1972" title="Local Government Act (Northern Ireland) 1972">Local Government Act (Northern Ireland) 1972</a>. Its area was formed from Kilkeel, Newry and Warrenpoint <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">Urban Districts</a> and Kilkeel and Newry No. 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rural_District" title="Rural District">Rural Districts</a> in County Down, and from Newry No. 2 Rural District in County Armagh. From 1973 to 1985, the council area consisted of six electoral areas. In 1985, this was reduced to five electoral areas: Crotlieve, Fews, Newry Town, Slieve Gullion and The Mournes. One of its 30 wards, <a href="/wiki/Rathfriland" title="Rathfriland">Rathfriland</a>, was transferred to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Banbridge_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Banbridge Council (page does not exist)">Banbridge Council</a> in 1993. At the elections of 2005, 30 members were elected from the following political parties: 14 <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a>, 9 <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_and_Labour_Party" title="Social Democratic and Labour Party">Social Democratic and Labour Party</a> (SDLP), 3 <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">Ulster Unionist Party</a> (UUP), 1 <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">Democratic Unionist Party</a> (DUP), 2 Independents and 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Independence_Party" title="United Kingdom Independence Party">United Kingdom Independence Party</a> (UKIP).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_criticism">2014 criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newry_and_Mourne_District_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2014 criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In April 2014 the <a href="/wiki/Equality_Commission_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Equality Commission for Northern Ireland">Equality Commission for Northern Ireland</a> criticised Newry and Mourne District Council for retaining the name of a children's play park called after the <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Irish_Republican_Army" title="Provisional Irish Republican Army">IRA</a> hunger striker, <a href="/wiki/Raymond_McCreesh" title="Raymond McCreesh">Raymond McCreesh</a>. McCreesh had been arrested with a weapon used in the infamous <a href="/wiki/Kingsmill_massacre" title="Kingsmill massacre">Kingsmill massacre</a> of Protestant workmen. Twenty nationalist councillors - including six (non-republican) SDLP representatives - voted to uphold his name in the December 2012 vote, but unionists campaigned against the decision. The Equality Commission concluded that their investigation has found that little consideration appears to have been given by the council to the impact its decision, in this instance, might have on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulster_Protestant" title="Ulster Protestant">Ulster Protestants</a>/unionists or to the damage it might cause to good relations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Review_of_Public_Administration">Review of Public Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newry_and_Mourne_District_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Review of Public Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under the Review of Public Administration (RPA) the Council was due to merge with <a href="/wiki/Down_District_Council" title="Down District Council">Down District Council</a> in 2011 to form a single council for the enlarged area totalling 1539 km² and a population of 150,886.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The next election was due to take place in May 2009, but on 25 April 2008, <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Woodward" title="Shaun Woodward">Shaun Woodward</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a> announced that the scheduled 2009 district council elections were to be postponed until the introduction of the eleven new councils in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The merger will now take place in April 2015 to form <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newry_City,_Mourne_and_Down" title="Newry City, Mourne and Down">Newry City, Mourne and Down</a> District Council.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newry_and_Mourne_District_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area covered by Newry and Mourne District Council has a population of 99,480 residents according to the 2011 Northern Ireland census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
