<p><i><b>New Moon</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Romantic_fantasy" title="Romantic fantasy">romantic fantasy</a> novel by author <a href="/wiki/Stephenie_Meyer" title="Stephenie Meyer">Stephenie Meyer</a>, and is the second novel in the <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel_series)" title="Twilight (novel series)"><i>Twilight</i> series</a>. The novel continues the story of <a href="/wiki/Bella_Swan" title="Bella Swan">Bella Swan</a> and vampire <a href="/wiki/Edward_Cullen" title="Edward Cullen">Edward Cullen</a>'s relationship. When Edward leaves Bella after his brother attacks her, she is left heartbroken and depressed for months until <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Black" title="Jacob Black">Jacob Black</a> becomes her best friend and helps her fight her pain. However, her life twists once more when Jacob's nature reveals itself and Edward's sister decides to visit.</p>
<p>According to Meyer, the book is about losing true love.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-1">[1]</a></sup> The title refers to the darkest phase of the <a href="/wiki/Lunar_phase" title="Lunar phase">lunar cycle</a>, indicating that <i>New Moon</i> is about the darkest time of protagonist Bella Swan's life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAQ_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-2">[2]</a></sup> Meyer wrote the book before <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel)" title="Twilight (novel)">Twilight</a></i> was published. Writing the book was difficult for Meyer as she feared the readers' reaction to the book and often cried as she described Bella's pain.</p>
<p>The book was originally released in hardcover on September 6, 2006 with an initial print run of 100,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Run_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Run-3">[3]</a></sup> Upon its publication in the United States, <i>New Moon</i> was highly successful and moved quickly to the top of bestseller lists, becoming one of the most anticipated books of the year. It peaked at #1 on both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times_Best_Seller_list" title="New York Times Best Seller list"><i>New York Times</i> Best Seller list</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i>'s Top 150 Bestsellers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Children.27s_Books_-_New_York_Times_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Children.27s_Books_-_New_York_Times-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-database_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-database-5">[5]</a></sup> and was the biggest selling children's paperback of 2008 with over 5.3 million copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Moreover, <i>New Moon</i> was the best-selling book of 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday1-7">[7]</a></sup> and has been translated into 38 languages. A <a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_New_Moon" title="The Twilight Saga: New Moon">film adaptation of the book</a> was released on November 20, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-title_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-title-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reception for <i>New Moon</i> was more positive than that for its predecessor. Some criticized the middle section's pacing; critics generally, however, argued the novel was more mature in tone, praising the character development and its depiction of human emotion.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Bella_Swan" title="Bella Swan">Isabella Swan's</a> 18th birthday, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Cullen" title="Edward Cullen">Edward Cullen</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vampire_(Twilight)" title="Vampire (Twilight)">vampire</a> she loves, and his family throw her a birthday party. While unwrapping a gift, she gets a <a href="/wiki/Paper_cut" title="Paper cut">paper cut</a>, which causes Edward's adopted brother Jasper, to be overwhelmed by her blood's scent and he attempts to attack and feed on Bella. Trying to protect her, Edward and the Cullens move away from <a href="/wiki/Forks,_Washington" title="Forks, Washington">Forks</a>, but in an attempt to get Bella to move on, Edward tells her it is because he no longer loves her. This leaves Bella heartbroken and depressed.</p>
<p>In the months that follow, Bella learns that thrill-seeking activities, such as motorcycle riding and cliff-diving, allow her to "hear" Edward's voice in her head through her subconscious mind. She also seeks comfort in her deepening friendship with <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Black" title="Jacob Black">Jacob Black</a>, a cheerful companion who eases her pain over losing Edward. Sometime after losing Edward, Bella starts to enjoy Jacob's company and friendship. After spending some time with Bella, Jacob starts experiencing some huge, unexpected and drastic changes with his mood swings, body and personality. As Jacob undergoes a very long, painful and life altering transformation Bella and Charlie become concerned. A few weeks later, Bella notes that Jacob isn't as happy-go-lucky as he once was. She isn't so comfortable with the new Jacob and shortly thereafter she discovers that Jacob has unwillingly become a werewolf and that there are other tribe members who are werewolves too. Jacob and his pack protect Bella from the vampire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurent_(Twilight)" title="Laurent (Twilight)">Laurent</a> and also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victoria_(Twilight)" title="Victoria (Twilight)">Victoria</a>, who seeks revenge for her dead mate, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_(Twilight)" title="James (Twilight)">James</a>, whom the Cullens had killed (in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel)" title="Twilight (novel)">Twilight</a>)</i>. Jacob starts developing physical emotions towards Bella but she doesn't feel the same having experienced a life changing breakup with Edward, making him horribly sad and envious of Edward. Jacob then saves Bella from drowning after jumping off the cliff, and almost kisses her in the events following.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, a series of miscommunications leads Edward to believe that Bella has killed herself by jumping off a cliff. Distraught over her supposed <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>, Edward flees to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volterra,_Italy" title="Volterra, Italy">Volterra, Italy</a> to provoke the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volturi" title="Volturi">Volturi</a>, vampire royalty who are capable of killing him, though they refuse, deeming his mind reading ability to be too valuable. Alice Cullen, however, makes a surprise visit to Bella's house, which overwhelms Bella. Bella asks a series of questions, and Alice tells her that she saw Bella trying to kill herself. As Alice's visions about Edward change rapidly, Alice and Bella are unable to clearly understand whether Edward is or will be safe. So, they rush to Italy to prevent Edward from revealing himself to humans so the Volturi are forced to kill him, arriving just in time to stop him. Before leaving Italy, the Volturi tell Edward that Bella, a human who knows that vampires exist, must either be killed or transformed into a vampire to protect the secret. When they return to Forks, Edward tells Bella that he has always loved her and only left Forks to protect her. She forgives him, and the Cullens vote in favor of Bella being transformed into a vampire, to Rose and Edward's dismay. However, Jacob sternly reminds Edward about an important part of their treaty: if the Cullens bite a human for any reason, the treaty is over and the wolves will attack. When Bella reminds him that it's none of his concern as being a vampire is what she wants, Jacob reveals it is his business as she doesn't understand what's going to be at stake for her and the Cullens. Before he can continue warning her, they hear Charlie asking Bella to get inside the house at once. Jacob apologizes to Bella once more before leaving and the book ends with Charlie grounding her for running off to Italy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Meyer finished writing <i>Twilight</i>, she found herself writing multiple, hundred-page epilogues, and has said, "I quickly realized I wasn't ready to stop writing about Bella and Edward."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She began writing a sequel, which was entitled <i>Forever Dawn</i> and skipped over Bella's final year of high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Story-10">[10]</a></sup> While Meyer was still writing <i>Forever Dawn</i>, she learned that <i>Twilight</i> was going to be published and marketed as a young-adult novel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Story-10">[10]</a></sup> Wanting the next book to be aimed at a similar audience, she decided to write a new sequel, <i>New Moon</i>, which took place during Bella's senior year of high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Story-10">[10]</a></sup> Therefore, Meyer started writing the outline of the book and thinking of what her characters would do, and claims that she "swiftly regretted asking them for the story." She didn't like the idea of Edward leaving at first and tried to think of other plot options, but, in the end, she said that "she accepted the inevitability of it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-Story-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Meyer wrote <i>New Moon</i> in five months. She found the editing process "much longer and more difficult than the same process with <i>Twilight</i>." Also, unlike <i>Twilight</i>, which Meyer intended not to publish at first, she recognized that <i>New Moon</i> was going to be published and had what she described as a "horrible feeling much like stage fright" while writing. However, Meyer considers Jacob to be her favorite gift the book gave, as she liked the character a lot and wanted to expand his role and presence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-Story-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The confrontation with the Volturi in the clock tower at the end of the book was the first scene Meyer wrote. She did not want to use a real city as the location for the Volturi's residence, as she did with <a href="/wiki/Forks,_Washington" title="Forks, Washington">Forks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Volterra_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Volterra-11">[11]</a></sup> She decided to name her city "Volturin"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and chose a location in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuscany,_Italy" title="Tuscany, Italy">Tuscany, Italy</a> because it matched her vision of the city being "very old and relatively remote." However, when consulting a map, she found that there was a city called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volterra,_Italy" title="Volterra, Italy">Volterra</a> in the area where she had planned to place her imaginary city. Therefore, she chose Volterra and called it "a pretty creepy coincidence."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Volterra_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Volterra-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first draft of <i>New Moon</i> differed significantly from the manuscript published. Originally, Bella never found out that Jacob was a werewolf, and as a result, the seventy pages following Bella's discovery of Jacob's nature were missing. The epilogue was also different in title and content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Meyer found it difficult to write Bella's pain over Edward's departure and often cried while writing those parts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-Story-10">[10]</a></sup> She mentioned that she never suffered a heartbreak like Bella's, so she couldn't draw inspiration for her pain from personal experiences, but based it on how she thought she would feel if she lost a child,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> while insisting that it came from her character, who is "much more open—to both pain and joy." She claims that "the way she chose to cope with it" was unexpected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Meyer, the story was inspired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shakespeare" title="Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meridian_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-meridian-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_and_title">Cover and title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cover and title">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_art">Cover art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cover art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The cover art of <i>New Moon</i> was designed by Gail Doobinin and photographed by John Grant. Meyer has expressed on numerous occasions that she had no hand in choosing the cover,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cover_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cover-17">[17]</a></sup> and said that she does not like it. She described it as "a very lovely ruffled tulip that means nothing at all".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cover_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cover-17">[17]</a></sup> Originally, Meyer suggested a clock image for the cover as she saw "time" as one of the most important themes of the novel. However, the artwork team that designed the cover chose the image of a tulip losing one of its petals, aiming to represent Bella losing a drop of blood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Title">Title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Title">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When Meyer finished writing the book, she wanted a title that referred to a time of day to match <i>Twilight</i>. As it reflected the mood of the sequel, she titled the novel <i>New Moon</i>, "the darkest kind of night, a night with no moon", to refer to the darkest period of Bella's life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-Story-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_and_reception">Publication and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Publication and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales">Sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>New Moon</i> was published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Little,_Brown" title="Little, Brown">Little, Brown</a> in the USA on September 6, 2006 with an initial print run of 100,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Run_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Run-3">[3]</a></sup> Demand for the book was so high that <a href="/wiki/Advance_copy" title="Advance copy">advance reading copies</a> were being sold on <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay">eBay</a> for as high as $380.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <i>New Moon</i> immediately rose to the #1 position on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times_Best_Seller_list" title="New York Times Best Seller list"><i>New York Times</i> Best Seller list</a> for Children's Chapter Books<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Children.27s_Books_-_New_York_Times_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Children.27s_Books_-_New_York_Times-4">[4]</a></sup> in its second week on the list, displacing popular children's authors such as <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Paolini" title="Christopher Paolini">Christopher Paolini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Markus_Zusak" title="Markus Zusak">Markus Zusak</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and remained in that spot for eleven weeks. It spent over 47 weeks in total on the list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>New Moon</i> also remained on the <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> Best Seller list for over 150 weeks after entering the list two weeks after its release, later peaking at #1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-database_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-database-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a> ranked it at #29 on its 2007 top-selling books list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/Publishers_Weekly" title="Publishers Weekly">Publishers Weekly</a></i> reported that <i>New Moon</i> had sold 1.5 million copies throughout the USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In October 2008, the book was ranked #37 on <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a>'s</i> "Bestselling Books of Last 15 Years".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> According to <i>USA Today</i>, the book was also the second biggest-selling book of 2008 behind its prequel, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel)" title="Twilight (novel)">Twilight</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and the biggest-selling of 2009, giving the saga the top four positions on the list for two consecutive years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday1-7">[7]</a></sup> It was also ranked at #27 on the list of biggest-selling books of 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The novel received mostly positive reviews with some critics feeling that it dragged in the middle. Hillias J. Martin of <i><a href="/wiki/School_Library_Journal" title="School Library Journal">School Library Journal</a></i> praised the book, saying, "Less streamlined than <i>Twilight</i> yet just as exciting, <i>New Moon</i> will more than feed the bloodthirsty hankerings of fans of the first volume and leave them breathless for the third".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirkus" title="Kirkus">Kirkus</a></i> praised the novel, describing it as "an exciting page turner...This tale of tortured demon lovers entices."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amazon.com_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-amazon.com-28">[28]</a></sup> Moreover, Cindy Dobrez of <i><a href="/wiki/Booklist" title="Booklist">Booklist</a></i> gave <i>New Moon</i> a positive review, stating that Bella's dismay at being ordinary "will strike a chord even among girls who have no desire to be immortal, and like the vampires who watch Bella bleed with "fevered eyes," teens will relish this new adventure and hunger for more".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amazon.com_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-amazon.com-28">[28]</a></sup> Furthermore, Norah Piehl of <a class="external text" href="http://www.teenreads.com" rel="nofollow">Teenreads.com</a> thought that in the middle "the story sometimes drags, and readers may long for the vampires' return", though she believed that "<i>New Moon</i> will leave Meyer's many fans breathless for the sequel, as Bella finally understands everything that will be at stake if she makes the ultimate choice to give up her humanity and live, like the vampires, forever."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Anna Limber of <a href="/wiki/About.com" title="About.com">About.com</a> echoed Piehl, saying that "the middle section is a little slow" and some aspects of the story were "predictable". However, she gave the book 3.5 stars out of 5 and said that the novel as a whole "has a brooding and melancholy feel to it, capturing well the angst of its teenage characters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>New Moon</i> won the Senior <a href="/wiki/Young_Reader%27s_Choice_Award" title="Young Reader's Choice Award">Young Reader's Choice Award</a> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adaptations">Adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Moon_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_New_Moon" title="The Twilight Saga: New Moon">The Twilight Saga: New Moon</a></div>
<p>A film adaptation of <i>New Moon</i> was released on November 20, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-title_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-title-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> It is the sequel to 2008's <i><a href="/wiki/Twilight_(2008_film)" title="Twilight (2008 film)">Twilight</a></i>, which is based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel)" title="Twilight (novel)">previous novel</a> written by Meyer. The film starred <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Stewart" title="Kristen Stewart">Kristen Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Pattinson" title="Robert Pattinson">Robert Pattinson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Lautner" title="Taylor Lautner">Taylor Lautner</a>, reprising their roles as <a href="/wiki/Bella_Swan" title="Bella Swan">Bella Swan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Cullen" title="Edward Cullen">Edward Cullen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Black" title="Jacob Black">Jacob Black</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-_34-0"><a href="#cite_note--34">[34]</a></sup> In late November 2008, <a href="/wiki/Summit_Entertainment" title="Summit Entertainment">Summit Entertainment</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenlight" title="Greenlight">greenlit</a> the sequel, which was directed by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Weitz" title="Chris Weitz">Chris Weitz</a> with <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Rosenberg" title="Melissa Rosenberg">Melissa Rosenberg</a> returning as the screenwriter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The majority of the film was shot in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vancouver_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vancouver-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
