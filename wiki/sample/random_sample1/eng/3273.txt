<p><b>New Britain</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tok_Pisin_language" title="Tok Pisin language">Tok Pisin</a>: <span lang="tpi" xml:lang="tpi"><i>Niu Briten</i></span>), is the largest <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bismarck_Archipelago" title="Bismarck Archipelago">Bismarck Archipelago</a> (named after <a href="/wiki/Otto_von_Bismarck" title="Otto von Bismarck">Otto von Bismarck</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>. It is separated from the island of <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Dampier_Strait_(Papua_New_Guinea)" title="Dampier Strait (Papua New Guinea)">Dampier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitiaz_Strait" title="Vitiaz Strait">Vitiaz Straits</a> and from <a href="/wiki/New_Ireland_(island)" title="New Ireland (island)">New Ireland</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Channel_(Papua_New_Guinea)" title="St. George's Channel (Papua New Guinea)">St. George's Channel</a>. The main towns of New Britain are <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a>/<a href="/wiki/Kokopo" title="Kokopo">Kokopo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimbe" title="Kimbe">Kimbe</a>. The island is roughly the size of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. While the island was part of <a href="/wiki/German_New_Guinea" title="German New Guinea">German New Guinea</a>, it was named <i><b>Neupommern</b></i> ("New <a href="/wiki/Pomerania" title="Pomerania">Pomerania</a>").</p>

<p>New Britain extends from 148¬∞18'31" to 152¬∞23'57" E longitude and from 4¬∞08'25" to 6¬∞18'31" S latitude. It is crescent-shaped, approximately 520¬†km (320¬†mi) along its southeastern coastline, and from 29 to 146¬†km (18‚Äì91¬†miles) wide, not including a small central peninsula. The air-line distance from west to east is 477¬†km (296¬†mi). The island is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_by_area" title="List of islands by area">38th largest in the world</a>, with an area of 36,520¬†km<sup>2</sup> (14,100¬†sq¬†mi).</p>
<p>Steep cliffs form some sections of the coastline; in others the mountains are further inland, and the coastal area is flat and bordered by coral reefs. The highest point, at 2,438¬†m (7,999¬†ft), is Mount Sinewit in the <a href="/wiki/Baining_Mountains" title="Baining Mountains">Baining range</a> in the east. Most of the terrain is covered with tropical rainforest and several large rivers are fed by the high rainfall.</p>
<p>New Britain was largely formed by volcanic processes, and there are several active volcanoes on the island, including <a href="/wiki/Ulawun" title="Ulawun">Ulawun</a> (the highest volcano in Papua New Guinea), <a href="/wiki/Langila" title="Langila">Langila</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Garbuna_Group" title="Garbuna Group">Garbuna Group</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sulu_Range" title="Sulu Range">Sulu Range</a>, and the volcanoes <a href="/wiki/Tavurvur" title="Tavurvur">Tavurvur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vulcan_(volcano)" title="Vulcan (volcano)">Vulcan</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Rabaul_caldera" title="Rabaul caldera">Rabaul caldera</a>. A major eruption of Tavurvur in 1994 destroyed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_New_Britain" title="East New Britain">East New Britain</a> provincial capital of <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a>. Most of the town still lies under metres of ash, and the capital has been moved to nearby <a href="/wiki/Kokopo" title="Kokopo">Kokopo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>New Britain forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Islands_Region" title="Islands Region">Islands Region</a>, one of four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regions_of_Papua_New_Guinea" title="Regions of Papua New Guinea">regions of Papua New Guinea</a>. It comprises the mainland of two <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_New_Britain" title="East New Britain">East New Britain</a> with headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Kokopo" title="Kokopo">Kokopo</a> (formerly Rabaul)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_New_Britain" title="West New Britain">West New Britain</a> with headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Kimbe" title="Kimbe">Kimbe</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_history">Modern history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Modern history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dd><i>For the missionary history, see <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_Vicariate_of_Northern_Solomon_Islands" title="Apostolic Vicariate of Northern Solomon Islands">Apostolic Vicariate of Northern Solomon Islands</a></i></dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1700.E2.80.931914">1700‚Äì1914</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1700‚Äì1914">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/William_Dampier" title="William Dampier">William Dampier</a> became the first known <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> to visit New Britain on 27 February 1700: he dubbed the island with the Latin name <i>Nova Britannia</i>, (Eng: <i>New <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britain_(placename)" title="Britain (placename)">Britain</a></i>).</p>
<p>In November 1884, Germany proclaimed its <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a> over the New Britain Archipelago; the German colonial administration gave New Britain and <a href="/wiki/New_Ireland_(island)" title="New Ireland (island)">New Ireland</a> the names of Neupommern (or Neu-Pommern; "New <a href="/wiki/Pomerania" title="Pomerania">Pomerania</a>") and <a href="/wiki/New_Ireland_(island)" title="New Ireland (island)">Neumecklenburg</a> (or Neu-Mecklenburg; "New <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg" title="Mecklenburg">Mecklenburg</a>") respectively, and the whole island group was renamed the Bismarck Archipelago. New Britain became part of <a href="/wiki/German_New_Guinea" title="German New Guinea">German New Guinea</a>.</p>
<p>In 1909, the <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">indigenous</a> population was estimated at about 190,000; the foreign population at 773 (474 white). The expatriate population was practically confined to the northeastern <a href="/wiki/Gazelle_Peninsula" title="Gazelle Peninsula">Gazelle Peninsula</a>, which included the capital, <a href="/wiki/Kokopo" title="Kokopo">Herbertsh√∂he</a> (now Kokopo). At the time 5,448 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">hectares</a> (13,464 <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acres</a>) had been converted to plantations, primarily growing <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a>, cotton, coffee and rubber. Westerners avoided exploring the interior initially, believing that the indigenous peoples were warlike and would fiercely resist intrusions.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 September 1914, New Britain became the site of one of the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I#Opening_battles" title="World War I">earliest battles of World War I</a> when the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Naval_and_Military_Expeditionary_Force" title="Australian Naval and Military Expeditionary Force">Australian Naval and Military Expeditionary Force</a> landed on the island. They quickly overwhelmed the German forces and occupied the island for the duration of the war.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Between_the_world_wars">Between the world wars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Between the world wars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles" title="Treaty of Versailles">Treaty of Versailles</a> was signed in June 1919, where Germany was stripped on all its possessions outside <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. In 1920 the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> included New Britain along with the former German colony on New Guinea in the <a href="/wiki/Territory_of_New_Guinea" title="Territory of New Guinea">Territory of New Guinea</a>, a mandated territory of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Native_New_British_Islanders_1944.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="533" data-file-width="789" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Native_New_British_Islanders_1944.jpg/220px-Native_New_British_Islanders_1944.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Native_New_British_Islanders_1944.jpg/330px-Native_New_British_Islanders_1944.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Native_New_British_Islanders_1944.jpg/440px-Native_New_British_Islanders_1944.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Britain_Campaign" title="New Britain Campaign">New Britain Campaign</a></div>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a> attacked New Britain soon after the outbreak of hostilities in the Pacific Ocean. Strategic bases at <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kavieng" title="Kavieng">Kavieng</a> (<a href="/wiki/New_Ireland_(island)" title="New Ireland (island)">New Ireland</a>) were defended by a small Australian detachment, <a href="/wiki/Lark_Force" title="Lark Force">Lark Force</a>. During January 1942, the Japanese heavily bombed <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a>. On 23 January, <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy_Land_Forces" title="Imperial Japanese Navy Land Forces">Japanese marines</a> landed by the thousands, starting the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rabaul_(1942)" title="Battle of Rabaul (1942)">Battle of Rabaul</a>. The Japanese used Rabaul as a heavy base until 1944; it served as the key point for the failed invasion of <a href="/wiki/Port_Moresby" title="Port Moresby">Port Moresby</a> (May to November, 1942).</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Japanese_flags_are_displayed_by_the_weary_Marines.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="356" data-file-width="640" height="122" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Japanese_flags_are_displayed_by_the_weary_Marines.jpg/220px-Japanese_flags_are_displayed_by_the_weary_Marines.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Japanese_flags_are_displayed_by_the_weary_Marines.jpg/330px-Japanese_flags_are_displayed_by_the_weary_Marines.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Japanese_flags_are_displayed_by_the_weary_Marines.jpg/440px-Japanese_flags_are_displayed_by_the_weary_Marines.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>New Britain was invaded by the <a href="/wiki/1st_Marine_Division_(United_States)" title="1st Marine Division (United States)">U.S. 1st Marine Division</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Gloucester" title="Cape Gloucester">Cape Gloucester</a> area of the very western end of the island, and also by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">U.S. Army</a> soldiers at some other coastal points. As for Cape Gloucester, with its swamps and mosquitos, the marines said that it was "worse than <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal_Campaign" title="Guadalcanal Campaign">Guadalcanal</a>". They captured an airfield but accomplished little toward reducing the Japanese base at Rabaul.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Operation_Cartwheel" title="Operation Cartwheel">Allied plan</a> involved bypassing Rabaul by surrounding it with air and naval bases on surrounding islands and on New Britain itself. The adjacent island of <a href="/wiki/New_Ireland_(island)" title="New Ireland (island)">New Ireland</a> was bypassed altogether. Much of the story from the Japanese side, especially the two suicide charges by the Baalen group, are retold in <a href="/wiki/Shigeru_Mizuki" title="Shigeru Mizuki">Shigeru Mizuki</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Onward_Towards_Our_Noble_Deaths" title="Onward Towards Our Noble Deaths">Onward Towards Our Noble Deaths</a></i>. The factual telemovie <i><a href="/wiki/Sisters_of_War" title="Sisters of War">Sisters of War</a></i> recounts experiences of Australian army nurses and Catholic nuns during the conflict.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People_and_culture">People and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: People and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The indigenous people of New Britain fall into two main groups: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papuans" title="Papuans">Papuans</a>, who have inhabited the island for tens of thousands of years, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austronesian_people" title="Austronesian people">Austronesians</a>, who arrived around two thousand years ago. There are around ten Papuan languages spoken and about forty Austronesian languages, as well as <a href="/wiki/Tok_Pisin" title="Tok Pisin">Tok Pisin</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. The Papuan population is largely confined to the eastern third of the island and a couple of small enclaves in the central highlands. At <a href="/wiki/Jacquinot_Bay" title="Jacquinot Bay">Jacquinot Bay</a>, in the south-east, they live beside the beach where a waterfall crashes directly into the sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Die_Gartenlaube_(1882)_b_697.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2091" data-file-width="3198" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Die_Gartenlaube_%281882%29_b_697.jpg/220px-Die_Gartenlaube_%281882%29_b_697.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Die_Gartenlaube_%281882%29_b_697.jpg/330px-Die_Gartenlaube_%281882%29_b_697.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Die_Gartenlaube_%281882%29_b_697.jpg/440px-Die_Gartenlaube_%281882%29_b_697.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Die_Gartenlaube_(1882)_b_697.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
New Britain, c. 1882</div>

<p>The population of New Britain was 493,585 in 2010. Austronesian people make up the majority on the island. The major towns are <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a>/<a href="/wiki/Kokopo" title="Kokopo">Kokopo</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_New_Britain" title="East New Britain">East New Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimbe" title="Kimbe">Kimbe</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_New_Britain" title="West New Britain">West New Britain</a>.</p>
<p>New Britain hosts diverse and complex traditional cultures. While the <a href="/wiki/Tolai_people" title="Tolai people">Tolai</a> of the Rabaul area of East New Britain have a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matrilineal" title="Matrilineal">matrilineal</a> society, other groups are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patrilineal" title="Patrilineal">patrilineal</a> in structure. There are numerous traditions which remain active today, such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Duk-Duk" title="Duk-Duk">dukduk</a></i> secret society (also known as <i>tubuan</i>) in the Tolai area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Forests on New Britain have been rapidly destroyed in recent years, largely to clear land for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_palm" title="Oil palm">oil palm</a> plantations. Lowland <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a> has been hardest hit, with nearly a quarter of the forest below 100 m disappearing between 1989 and 2000. If those rates of <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a> continue, it is estimated that all forest below 200 m will be cleared by 2060.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
