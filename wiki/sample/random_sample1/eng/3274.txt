<p><b>Newsarama</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">website</a> that publishes news, interviews, and essays about the <a href="/wiki/American_comic_book" title="American comic book">American comic book</a> industry.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsarama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Newsarama began in the summer of 1995 as a series of <a href="/wiki/Internet_forum" title="Internet forum">Internet forum</a> postings on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prodigy_(ISP)" title="Prodigy (ISP)">Prodigy</a> comic book message boards by fan Mike Doran.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the forum postings, Doran shared comic book-related news items he had found across the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a> and, as these postings became more regular and read widely, he gave them the title "Prodigy Comic Book Newswire."</p>
<p>In January 1997, Doran began to post a version of the column titled <i>The Comics Newswire</i> on <a href="/wiki/Usenet" title="Usenet">Usenet</a>'s various rec.arts.comics communities. The name of the column evolved to <i>The Newswire</i>, and then to <i>CBI Newsarama</i>, before finally becoming <i>Newsarama</i> in 1998.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The posts quickly became popular due to the speed of reporting via the Internet.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This meant Doran could break stories faster than other comic book news sources that appeared in printed publications, as the printed stories had to be fully edited weeks before they were released.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> By the time other online comic journalists arrived on the scene, Newsarama already had become an established brand.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Although the column in its earliest forms reported both news and rumors, it later adopted a standard journalistic news approach.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Doran's postings left Usenet in 1998, becoming a Newsarama column on such websites as Mania.com, AnotherUniverse.com, Fandom.com and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comicon.com" title="Comicon.com">Comicon.com</a>. It then continued to become a semi-autonomous site, Newsarama, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Smith" title="Kevin Smith">Kevin Smith</a>'s ViewAskew.com network of sites in August 2002.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Three months later, Doran left Newsarama, which was by now its own website, to take a staff position at <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Matt Brady, a writer who had written extensively for the site, took over. Doran later returned to work at Newsarama, while Brady continued working as primary writer for the site.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The site left the ViewAskew.com network and became independent in early April 2006. Newsarama was acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Imaginova" title="Imaginova">Imaginova</a> corporation in October 2007.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> When Brady left the site in July 2009, Doran and Lucas Siegel stepped up to run it, with Siegel taking the position of Site Editor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The site was acquired by TopTenREVIEWS in October 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Newsarama has been quoted as a source of comic news by the mainstream media, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2006, <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> listed Newsarama as one of its "25 favorite online entertainment sites" in 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and as one of its "100 Greatest Websites" in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Newsarama originally maintained a registered member forum known as <i>talk@Newsarama</i>. In 2010, Newsarama closed down the forum and redirected readers to comment on the site's Facebook page.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Columnists">Columnists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsarama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Columnists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> <a href="/wiki/Joe_Quesada" title="Joe Quesada">Joe Quesada</a>'s column "Joe Fridays" (renamed "New Joe Fridays" in 2006 as a joke regarding Marvel's penchant for relaunching titles with the prefix "new") appeared weekly until 2008, when the column moved to MySpace.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Quesada then began writing the column "Cup of Joe" on <a href="/wiki/Comic_Book_Resources" title="Comic Book Resources">Comic Book Resources</a>. Former <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a> editor Michael Siglain contributed the weekly "5.2 About <a href="/wiki/52_(comics)" title="52 (comics)">52</a>", and in 2007, DC Executive Editor <a href="/wiki/Dan_DiDio" title="Dan DiDio">Dan DiDio</a> announced<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> he would write a column similar to "New Joe Fridays", focusing on the series <i>Countdown</i>. Didio has participated in the weekly "10 Answers and 1 Question" column for the site.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Regular columns have included "Animated Shorts" by Steve Fritz, "Write or Wrong" by Dirk Manning, "Best Shots" by reviewers from ShotgunReviews.com, "10 Answers and 1 Question with Dan DiDio", "Weekly Webbing", "Right to Assemble", covering Marvel's <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a> titles by Troy Brownfield, "Column . . . for JUSTICE" by Brownfield, covering <a href="/wiki/Justice_League" title="Justice League">Justice League</a> titles, "Getting Animated" and "Friday Flashback" by Brownfield and "Agent of S.T.Y.L.E." by Alan Kistler, covering the evolution of costumes and designs for different comic book characters.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Newsarama has also run a series of "Post Game" columns offering coverage and commentary of popular genre-related television programs on a regular basis. Covered shows include <i>Lost</i>, <i>Smallville</i>, <i>Batman: The Brave and the Bold</i>, <i>Fringe</i>, <i>Flash Forward</i>, and others.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsarama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2005, Michael Dean, writing in the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Comics_Journal" title="The Comics Journal">The Comics Journal</a></i>, studied Internet comic book industry news sources and evaluated Newsarama's journalistic performance. The study praised the site for the depth of coverage provided in some articles, but criticized its reliance on press releases and the "softness" of the questions asked in its interviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archive1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-archive1-6">[6]</a></sup> Dean focused on one story in particular, "<a href="/wiki/Diamond_Comic_Distributors" title="Diamond Comic Distributors">Diamond</a> Changes Thresholds" by Matt Brady.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Though he found the piece qualified as "journalism", Dean also found it: "contained factual inaccuracies, failed to get multiple points of view and sucked up to its corporate subject."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archive1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-archive1-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsarama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The site has been the recipient of a number of awards and award nominations, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagle_Awards#1999" title="Eagle Awards">1999 Eagle Award</a> nomination for Favorite Comics-Related Website (professional).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagle_Awards#2000" title="Eagle Awards">2000 Eagle Award</a> nomination for Favourite Comics-Related Website (professional).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagle_Awards#2004" title="Eagle Awards">2004 Eagle Award</a> for Favorite Comics E-Zine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagle_Awards#2005" title="Eagle Awards">2005 Eagle Award</a> nomination for Favourite Comics-Related Website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagle_Awards#2006" title="Eagle Awards">2006 Eagle Award</a> for Favorite Comics-Related Website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
