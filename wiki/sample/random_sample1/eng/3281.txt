<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_York_Red_Bulls_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The New York Red Bulls II were founded on January 21, 2015, when the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Red_Bulls" title="New York Red Bulls">New York Red Bulls</a> acquired the rights to start a <a href="/wiki/United_Soccer_League" title="United Soccer League">USL</a> club. Red Bulls II competes in the third tier of American soccer and serves as a reserve side for the first team as well as a developmental stage for young prospects and academy players. The club follows in the footsteps of many <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">MLS</a> clubs who created USL sides to help promote youth development throughout the nation. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on March 2, 2015, that former Metrostars and Red Bulls player, <a href="/wiki/John_Wolyniec" title="John Wolyniec">John Wolyniec</a> would be the club's new head coach. Wolyniec previously served as an assistant coach to the senior team and the academy. Former Red Bulls defender, <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Sekagya" title="Ibrahim Sekagya">Ibrahim Sekagya</a> will also serve on his coaching staff. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 19, 2015, the club announced the signing of their first ever player, and former academy standout, <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Adams" title="Tyler Adams">Tyler Adams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Following their first signing, the club's first roster consisted of senior team players who were loaned down, draft picks, academy players and local trialists. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> A week later, on March 28, the club played in their inaugural match, drawing 0-0 against the <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Rhinos" title="Rochester Rhinos">Rochester Rhinos</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Senior team loanee, <a href="/wiki/Anatole_Abang" title="Anatole Abang">Anatole Abang</a> scored the club's first ever goal in a 4-1 victory against <a href="/wiki/Toronto_FC_II" title="Toronto FC II">Toronto FC II</a>, marking the first victory in club history. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>After spending the majority of the 2015 and 2016 season playing at <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Arena_(New_Jersey)" title="Red Bull Arena (New Jersey)">Red Bull Arena</a>, the club announced on May 10, 2016 that their new home field would be at <a href="/wiki/Montclair_State_University" title="Montclair State University">Montclair State University</a>'s <a href="/wiki/MSU_Soccer_Park_at_Pittser_Field" title="MSU Soccer Park at Pittser Field">MSU Soccer Park at Pittser Field</a> starting in 2017. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club won their first ever trophy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> on September 7, 2016 claiming the Regular Season Title (the equivalent of the <a href="/wiki/Supporters%27_Shield" title="Supporters' Shield">Supporters' Shield</a>) 4-1 victory at <a href="/wiki/Harrisburg_City_Islanders" title="Harrisburg City Islanders">Harrisburg City Islanders</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On October 23, Red Bulls II won their first USL Cup after a 5-1 victory against <a href="/wiki/Swope_Park_Rangers" title="Swope Park Rangers">Swope Park Rangers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Arena_(New_Jersey)" title="Red Bull Arena (New Jersey)">Red Bull Arena</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
