<p>The <b>D Sixth Avenue Express</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> service in the <a href="/wiki/B_Division_(New_York_City_Subway)" title="B Division (New York City Subway)">B Division</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Subway" title="New York City Subway">New York City Subway</a>. Its route emblem, or "bullet", is colored <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orange_(color)" title="Orange (color)">bright orange</a> since it uses the <a href="/wiki/IND_Sixth_Avenue_Line" title="IND Sixth Avenue Line">IND Sixth Avenue Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <b>D</b> operates at all times between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwood_%E2%80%93_205th_Street_(IND_Concourse_Line)" title="Norwood – 205th Street (IND Concourse Line)">205th Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norwood,_Bronx" title="Norwood, Bronx">Norwood, Bronx</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coney_Island_%E2%80%93_Stillwell_Avenue_(New_York_City_Subway)" title="Coney Island – Stillwell Avenue (New York City Subway)">Stillwell Avenue</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coney_Island,_Brooklyn" title="Coney Island, Brooklyn">Coney Island, Brooklyn</a> via <a href="/wiki/IND_Concourse_Line" title="IND Concourse Line">Grand Concourse</a> in the Bronx, <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Eighth Avenue (Manhattan)">Central Park West</a> / <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Sixth Avenue (Manhattan)">Sixth Avenue</a> in Manhattan, the north side of the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Bridge" title="Manhattan Bridge">Manhattan Bridge</a>, and <a href="/wiki/BMT_Fourth_Avenue_Line" title="BMT Fourth Avenue Line">Fourth Avenue</a> / <a href="/wiki/BMT_West_End_Line" title="BMT West End Line">West End</a> in Brooklyn. The <b>D</b> runs express in Manhattan and makes all stops on the BMT West End Line in Brooklyn at all times. The <b>D</b> also makes all stops in the Bronx except when it runs express in the peak direction during rush hours. The <b>D</b> runs express on Fourth Avenue at all times except nights when it serves all stops.</p>

<p><b>D</b> service began on December 15, 1940 when the <a href="/wiki/IND_Sixth_Avenue_Line" title="IND Sixth Avenue Line">IND Sixth Avenue Line</a> opened. It ran from 205th Street, the Bronx to World Trade Center (at that time called <b>Hudson Terminal</b>) on the <a href="/wiki/IND_Eighth_Avenue_Line" title="IND Eighth Avenue Line">IND Eighth Avenue Line</a>, switching between the IND Sixth Avenue to the Eighth Avenue Lines just south of West Fourth Street – Washington Square.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>D</b> service was increased on October 24, 1949 in order to offset the loss off <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CC_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="CC (New York City Subway service)">CC</a></b> service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 29, 1951, peak direction express service in the Bronx was discontinued.</p>
<p>On October 30, 1954, a connection between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IND_South_Brooklyn_Line" title="IND South Brooklyn Line">IND South Brooklyn Line</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMT_Culver_Line" title="BMT Culver Line">BMT Culver Line</a> opened. <b>D</b> service was rerouted via these two lines to Coney Island – Stillwell Avenue with limited rush hour trains to <a href="/wiki/Church_Avenue_(IND_Culver_Line)" title="Church Avenue (IND Culver Line)">Church Avenue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1957 and 1959, limited rush hour trains ran express and/or local to <a href="/wiki/Euclid_Avenue_(IND_Fulton_Street_Line)" title="Euclid Avenue (IND Fulton Street Line)">Euclid Avenue</a>.</p>
<p>From December 4 to 27, 1962, a special service labeled <b>DD</b> was provided due to a water main break. It ran local from 205th Street, Bronx to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/59th_Street_%E2%80%93_Columbus_Circle_(IND_Eighth_Avenue_Line)" title="59th Street – Columbus Circle (IND Eighth Avenue Line)">59th Street – Columbus Circle</a>, then continued as a local down the Eighth Avenue Line to West Fourth Street, where it switched to the Sixth Avenue Line and continued on its normal route to Coney Island – Stillwell Avenue via the Culver Line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>On November 26, 1967, the <a href="/wiki/Chrystie_Street_Connection" title="Chrystie Street Connection">Chrystie Street Connection</a> opened, adding express service on the Sixth Avenue Line and connecting it with the north tracks of the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Bridge" title="Manhattan Bridge">Manhattan Bridge</a>. <b>D</b> service was switched over to <a href="/wiki/BMT_Brighton_Line" title="BMT Brighton Line">BMT Brighton Line</a> via this new connector. It became the express service weekdays to <a href="/wiki/Brighton_Beach_(BMT_Brighton_Line)" title="Brighton Beach (BMT Brighton Line)">Brighton Beach</a> and the local to Stillwell Avenue at other times. In Manhattan, it ran express from West 4th Street to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/34th_Street_%E2%80%93_Herald_Square_(IND_Sixth_Avenue_Line)" title="34th Street – Herald Square (IND Sixth Avenue Line)">34th Street</a> rush hours only (the <b><a href="/wiki/B_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="B (New York City Subway service)">B</a></b> used the express tracks to relay when it terminated at West 4th Street at other times).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It would become the full-time Sixth Avenue Express when non-rush hours <b><a href="/wiki/B_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="B (New York City Subway service)">B</a></b> service was extended to <a href="/wiki/57th_Street_(IND_Sixth_Avenue_Line)" title="57th Street (IND Sixth Avenue Line)">57th Street – Sixth Avenue</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:D_Train_(1986-1988).svg"><img alt="D Train (1986-1988).svg" data-file-height="75" data-file-width="75" height="75" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/D_Train_%281986-1988%29.svg/75px-D_Train_%281986-1988%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/D_Train_%281986-1988%29.svg/113px-D_Train_%281986-1988%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/D_Train_%281986-1988%29.svg/150px-D_Train_%281986-1988%29.svg.png 2x" width="75"/></a>

<p>When the north tracks of the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Bridge" title="Manhattan Bridge">Manhattan Bridge</a> closed on April 13, 1986 due to construction, the <b>D</b> service was divided and ran in two sections, one between Norwood-205th Street in the Bronx and 34th Street – Herald Square (the <b>Orange D</b>) while the other ran from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/57th_Street_%E2%80%93_Seventh_Avenue_(BMT_Broadway_Line)" title="57th Street – Seventh Avenue (BMT Broadway Line)">57th Street – Seventh Avenue</a> on the <a href="/wiki/BMT_Broadway_Line" title="BMT Broadway Line">BMT Broadway Line</a>, then express along the Broadway Line to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canal_Street_(BMT_Manhattan_Bridge_Line)" title="Canal Street (BMT Manhattan Bridge Line)">Canal Street</a>, then over the south tracks of the Manhattan Bridge into Brooklyn, and then along the Brighton Line to Stillwell Avenue (the <b>Yellow D</b>). At this time, <b>D</b>/<b><a href="/wiki/Q_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="Q (New York City Subway service)">Q</a></b> <a href="/wiki/Skip-stop" title="Skip-stop">skip-stop</a> service ran in Brooklyn on weekdays.</p>
<p>On December 11, 1988, the north tracks of the Manhattan Bridge reopened and the two sections of the <b>D</b> joined together running via Sixth Avenue Express. The <b>D</b> now ran as the full-time Brighton Local to Stillwell Avenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 1995, the north tracks were closed during midday and weekends and <b>D</b> service was cut south of 34th Street-Herald Square. On July 22, 2001, it was closed at all times and <b>D</b> service was cut again. In Brooklyn, it was replaced by <b><a href="/wiki/Q_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="Q (New York City Subway service)">Q</a></b> local service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/September_11,_2001_attacks" title="September 11, 2001 attacks">September 11, 2001</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/C_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="C (New York City Subway service)">C</a></b> service was suspended. On weekends, the <b>D</b> ran local on the Eighth Avenue Line north of 59th Street to fill in the gap in service caused by the suspension.</p>
<p>On February 22, 2004, full service on the Manhattan Bridge was restored and <b>D</b> trains were extended via the north tracks of the bridge to Brooklyn, replacing the <b><a href="/wiki/W_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="W (New York City Subway service)">W</a></b> as the <a href="/wiki/BMT_Fourth_Avenue_Line" title="BMT Fourth Avenue Line">Fourth Avenue Express</a> (late nights local) and <a href="/wiki/BMT_West_End_Line" title="BMT West End Line">West End Local</a> to Coney Island – Stillwell Avenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The <b>D</b> was moved to the West End Line instead of returning to the Brighton Line, which it had run on since 1967, in order to avoid running two separate (<b>B</b> and <b>D</b>) shortened services outside of weekdays. Moving the <b>D</b> to the West End Line meant it could now provide 24-hour service to both the Concourse Line in the Bronx and West End Line in Brooklyn. This eliminated the need to run late-night and/or weekend shuttles on either the West End or Concourse lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>From May 24, 2004 to Fall 2004, construction on the <a href="/wiki/IND_Concourse_Line" title="IND Concourse Line">IND Concourse Line</a>, required the suspension of <b>D</b> express service in the Bronx.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D_(New_York_City_Subway_service)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>'s 1966 song "<a href="/wiki/Visions_of_Johanna" title="Visions of Johanna">Visions of Johanna</a>" includes the lyric "In the empty lot where the ladies play blindman's bluff with the keychain/And the all-night girls, they whisper of escapades out on the <b>D</b> train." At the time, the <b>D</b> used the <a href="/wiki/IND_Culver_Line" title="IND Culver Line">IND Culver Line</a> to Coney Island.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Biz_Markie" title="Biz Markie">Biz Markie</a>'s song "Pickin' Boogers" from his debut album <a href="/wiki/Goin%27_Off" title="Goin' Off">Goin' Off</a> features the line "I was chillin one day/with my partner <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Kane</a>/headed up to the rooftop/ridin' the <b>D</b> train."</li>
<li>The opening track on Yoko Ono's 2009 album <i>Between My Head And The Sky</i> is titled 'Waiting For The <b>D</b> Train'. The <b>D</b> passes through <a href="/wiki/72nd_Street_(IND_Eighth_Avenue_Line)" title="72nd Street (IND Eighth Avenue Line)">72nd Street</a> (opposite Yoko's apartment in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dakota_Building" title="Dakota Building">Dakota Building</a>) but never stops there, as it is a local station.</li>
<li>In the late 1980s and early 1990s, numerous Top 10 Lists on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Night with David Letterman">Late Night with David Letterman</a></i> contained references to the <b>D</b> train.</li>
<li>The famous car-chase scene in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_French_Connection_(film)" title="The French Connection (film)">The French Connection</a></i> took place under the elevated tracks running from 86th Street into New Utrecht Avenue, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> (although at that time, the <b><a href="/wiki/B_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="B (New York City Subway service)">B</a></b> was serving the West End Line). Many of the actual street scenes, however, were shot in other areas, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bushwick" title="Bushwick">Bushwick</a>, Brooklyn.</li>
<li>The namesake street youth gang in the 1979 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Warriors_(film)" title="The Warriors (film)">The Warriors</a></i> travel on the <b>D</b>, throughout the film's opening credits, from their Coney Island turf to a meeting in the Bronx.</li>
<li>The service is mentioned in the song <i>Boogie Down</i> by <a href="/wiki/Man_Parrish" title="Man Parrish">Man Parrish</a> Ft. Freeze Force (MC John Ski) raps the following line: "You take the <b>D</b> to 205th Then go see me 'cause I got the gift And I'm the cool MC with the vicious sounds I'm not from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a></i>, but I still Boogie Down".</li>
<li>The service is mentioned in the song <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3_The_Hard_Way" title="3 The Hard Way">3 The Hard Way</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>. <a href="/wiki/Adam_Yauch" title="Adam Yauch">Adam Yauch</a> raps the following line: "Used to ride the <b>D</b> to beat the morning bell at Edward R. Murrow out on Avenue L..." (Referring to <a href="/wiki/Edward_R._Murrow_High_School" title="Edward R. Murrow High School">Edward R. Murrow High School</a>, where the <b>D</b> served the station closest to the school, <a href="/wiki/Avenue_M_(BMT_Brighton_Line)" title="Avenue M (BMT Brighton Line)">Avenue M</a>, until 2001, when it was replaced by the <b><a href="/wiki/Q_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="Q (New York City Subway service)">Q</a></b>).</li>
<li>The service is mentioned twice in the song <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-Boy_Bouillabaisse" title="B-Boy Bouillabaisse">Stop That Train</a></i> by the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mike_D" title="Mike D">Mike D</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adrock" title="Adrock">Adrock</a> rap the following line: "Same faces every day, but you don't know their names, party people going places on the <b>D</b> train". <a href="/wiki/Adam_Yauch" title="Adam Yauch">Adam Yauch</a> raps the following line: "Groggy-eyed and fried, and I'm headed for the station, <b>D</b> train ride to Coney Island vacation."</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Seinfeld_(character)" title="Jerry Seinfeld (character)">eponymous character</a> of <a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a> uses the <b>D</b> train to go to Coney Island in the episode <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Subway_(Seinfeld_episode)" title="The Subway (Seinfeld episode)">The Subway</a></i>.</li>
<li>The 1980s folk-pop trio <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Washington_Squares" title="The Washington Squares">The Washington Squares</a> includes a song titled "<b>D</b> Train" on their eponymous 1987 debut album.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Type_O_Negative" title="Type O Negative">Type O Negative</a> refer to the <b>D</b> train as the chosen transport to <a href="/wiki/Brighton_Beach" title="Brighton Beach">Brighton Beach</a>, where lead singer <a href="/wiki/Peter_Steele" title="Peter Steele">Peter Steele</a> will kill his girlfriend in their songs "<a href="/wiki/Slow,_Deep_and_Hard" title="Slow, Deep and Hard">Xero Tolerance</a>," "<a href="/wiki/The_Origin_of_the_Feces" title="The Origin of the Feces">Hey Pete</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/The_Origin_of_the_Feces" title="The Origin of the Feces">Kill You Tonight</a>".</li>
<li>It is also mentioned in an episode of <a href="/wiki/Penguins_of_Madagascar" title="Penguins of Madagascar">Penguins of Madagascar</a> called "Gone In A Flash" where the penguins go to rescue Maurice and have to travel through the subway system. Also, in episode "Dr. Blowhole's Revenge", when Julien is kidnaped by the lobsters, Mort starts his travel to save him by using the metro until Coney Island.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Man_Against_Crime" title="Man Against Crime">Man Against Crime</a> episode Third Rail (S4E19) starring <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Bellamy" title="Ralph Bellamy">Ralph Bellamy</a> was filmed on the <b>D</b> train and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/207th_Street_Yard" title="207th Street Yard">207th Street Yard</a> in 1953.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jean-Michel_Basquiat" title="Jean-Michel Basquiat">Jean-Michel Basquiat</a> generated much interest in his graffiti art, which took the form of spray-painted aphorisms that were targeted at the <b>D</b> train.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
