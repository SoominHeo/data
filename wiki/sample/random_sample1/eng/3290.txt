<p>The <b>Anglican Church in <a href="/wiki/Aotearoa" title="Aotearoa">Aotearoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a></b> is a church of the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican Communion</a> serving New Zealand, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cook_Islands" title="Cook Islands">Cook Islands</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Primate_(bishop)" title="Primate (bishop)">primate</a> of the church, known as the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_New_Zealand" title="Archbishop of New Zealand">Archbishop of New Zealand</a>, was <a href="/wiki/Brown_Turei" title="Brown Turei">Brown Turei</a>, until his sudden death in January 2017.</p>
<p>Since 1992, the church (formerly known as the <b>Church of the Province of New Zealand</b>) has consisted of three <i><a href="/wiki/Tikanga_M%C4%81ori" title="Tikanga Māori">tikanga</a></i> or cultural streams: <b>Aotearoa</b>, <b>New Zealand</b>, and <b>Polynesia</b>. The church's constitution says that, among other things, it is required to "maintain the right of every person to choose any particular cultural expression of the faith". As a result, the church's General Synod has agreed upon the development of the three-person primacy based on this three <i>tikanga</i> system. This saw Turei sharing the primacy with <a href="/wiki/Philip_Richardson_(bishop)" title="Philip Richardson (bishop)">Philip Richardson</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Winston_Halapua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Winston Halapua (page does not exist)">Winston Halapua</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership">Leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anglican_Church_in_Aotearoa,_New_Zealand_and_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The church has decided that three bishops shall share the position and style of <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">archbishop</a>, each representing one of the three tikanga. The three archbishops sharing the title of <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_New_Zealand" title="Archbishop of New Zealand">Archbishop of New Zealand</a> are: one vacancy for the head of <a href="/wiki/Te_P%C4%ABhopatanga_o_Aotearoa" title="Te Pīhopatanga o Aotearoa">Te Pīhopatanga o Aotearoa</a> which oversees churches for the indigenous <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori people</a> of New Zealand (following the death of <a href="/wiki/Brown_Turei" title="Brown Turei">Brown Turei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Aotearoa" title="Bishop of Aotearoa">Bishop of Aotearoa</a>); <a href="/wiki/Philip_Richardson_(bishop)" title="Philip Richardson (bishop)">Philip Richardson</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Taranaki" title="Bishop of Taranaki">Bishop of Taranaki</a>, representing the dioceses in New Zealand; and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Winston_Halapua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Winston Halapua (page does not exist)">Winston Halapua</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Polynesia" title="Bishop of Polynesia">Bishop of Polynesia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aotearoa">Aotearoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anglican_Church_in_Aotearoa,_New_Zealand_and_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aotearoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Te_P%C4%ABhopatanga_o_Aotearoa" title="Te Pīhopatanga o Aotearoa">Te Pīhopatanga o Aotearoa</a>, which Turei also served as <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">archbishop</a> or co-presiding bishop, oversees churches for the indigenous <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori people</a> of New Zealand. Aotearoa is made up of five <b>hui amorangi</b> or regional <a href="/wiki/Diocese" title="Diocese">bishoprics</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Manawa_O_Te_Wheke" title="Manawa O Te Wheke">Manawa O Te Wheke</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Te_Hui_Amorangi_ki_Te_Tairawhiti" title="Te Hui Amorangi ki Te Tairawhiti">Tairāwhiti</a> (East Coast Region)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tai_Tokerau" title="Tai Tokerau">Tai Tokerau</a> (Northern Region)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Upoko_O_Te_Ika" title="Upoko O Te Ika">Upoko O Te Ika</a> (Wellington/Taranaki)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Waipounamu" title="Waipounamu">Waipounamu</a> (South Island)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anglican_Church_in_Aotearoa,_New_Zealand_and_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tikanga of New Zealand, which serves non-Maori in New Zealand, is made up of seven <a href="/wiki/Diocese" title="Diocese">dioceses</a>:</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Anglican_Diocese_of_Auckland" title="Anglican Diocese of Auckland">Auckland</a></li>

<p>Formerly, the dioceses in New Zealand were led by a "senior bishop" elected from among the diocesan bishops. However, as the church moves towards a three-person primacy, the leader of the dioceses in New Zealand is elected as co-presiding bishop and styled as an archbishop. The current <a href="/wiki/P%C4%81keh%C4%81" title="Pākehā">Pākehā</a> co-presiding bishop is <a href="/wiki/Philip_Richardson_(bishop)" title="Philip Richardson (bishop)">Philip Richardson</a>, Bishop of Taranaki.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Polynesia">Polynesia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anglican_Church_in_Aotearoa,_New_Zealand_and_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Polynesia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Diocese of Polynesia, or the <i>Tikanga Pasefika</i>, headed by Bishop Winston Halapua,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> serves Anglicans in Fiji, Tonga, Samoa, and the Cook Islands. The diocese's first bishop was consecrated in 1908. The diocese's cathedral is Holy Trinity Cathedral in <a href="/wiki/Suva" title="Suva">Suva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>. As the province moves towards a three-person primacy, the Bishop of Polynesia is automatically a co-presiding bishop and styled as an archbishop. The Bishop of Polynesia is currently supported by three suffragan bishops: Bishop Winston Halapua, now the new Bishop of Polynesia, formerly lead the ministry to Polynesians in New Zealand, Bishop <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apimeleki_Nadoki_Qiliho" title="Apimeleki Nadoki Qiliho">Apimeleki Nadoki Qiliho</a> serves <a href="/wiki/Vanua_Levu" title="Vanua Levu">Vanua Levu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taveuni" title="Taveuni">Taveuni</a>, and Bishop Gabriel Sharma serves <a href="/wiki/Viti_Levu" title="Viti Levu">Viti Levu</a> West as well as the archdeacons of Suva and Ovalau, Samoa and American Samoa, and Tonga.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theological_training">Theological training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anglican_Church_in_Aotearoa,_New_Zealand_and_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Theological training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Residential theological training is carried out primarily at <a href="/wiki/St_John%27s_College,_Auckland" title="St John's College, Auckland">St John's College, Auckland</a>, which is also organised according to the three <i>tikanga</i> approach.</p>
<p>Theological training was formerly carried out by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/College_House_(University_of_Canterbury)" title="College House (University of Canterbury)">College House</a> in <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a>, but over time College House has become secularised as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hall_of_residence" title="Hall of residence">hall of residence</a> of the nearby <a href="/wiki/University_of_Canterbury" title="University of Canterbury">University of Canterbury</a>. While it still falls under the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Diocese_of_Christchurch" title="Anglican Diocese of Christchurch">Anglican Diocese of Christchurch</a> and has the extensive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theological_libraries_and_librarianship" title="Theological libraries and librarianship">theological holdings</a> in its library, it no longer trains ordinands.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Homosexuality">Homosexuality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anglican_Church_in_Aotearoa,_New_Zealand_and_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Homosexuality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Anglican Church of Aotearoa, New Zealand and Polynesia has no official policy on the issue<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and is one of the many churches of the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican Communion</a> considering same-sex unions. In 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Diocese_of_Auckland" title="Anglican Diocese of Auckland">Diocese of Auckland</a> voted in favour of ordaining partnered gay and lesbian priests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Congregations in the Auckland Diocese may offer a 'relationship blessing' for two partners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Diocese_of_Dunedin" title="Anglican Diocese of Dunedin">Dunedin Diocese</a> also provides a blessing for the relationship of "two people" irrespective of gender. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the diocese "Blessings of same-sex relationships are offered in line with Diocesan Policy and with the bishop's permission."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Dunedin Diocese also ordained an openly gay and partnered deacon<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Subsequently, the same deacon was ordained a priest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, some bishops and four dioceses supported a rite of blessing for same-sex unions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Motion 30, adopted by the 62nd General Synod on 14 May 2014, designated a working task group with the purpose of creating a "process and structure" that would allow the blessing of same-sex unions, while also upholding the traditional doctrine of marriage as the union of a man and a woman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, the committee responsible for developing the rites of blessing released its proposed liturgies for same-sex couples to be discussed by the General Synod.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The General Synod 2016 voted to 'receive' the report on blessings but left the option to "[lie] on the table" and the issue will be reviewed again in 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> "However, Synod did pass a constitutional change allowing bishops the right to authorize (sic) a service for use in his or her diocese".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
