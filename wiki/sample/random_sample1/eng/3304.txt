<p>The <b>Ulmaceae</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/Ê/ short 'u' in 'bud'">Ê</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/Ë/ primary stress follows">Ë</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/eÉª/ long 'a' in 'base'">eÉª</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/iË/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iË</span></span>/</a></span></span> are a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flowering_plants" title="Flowering plants">flowering plant</a> that includes the <a href="/wiki/Elm" title="Elm">elms</a> (genus <i>Ulmus</i>), and the <a href="/wiki/Zelkova" title="Zelkova">zelkovas</a> (genus <i>Zelkova</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Members of the family are widely distributed throughout the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate">north temperate zone</a>, and have a scattered distribution elsewhere except for <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stevens_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-stevens-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The family was formerly sometimes treated to include the <a href="/wiki/Celtis" title="Celtis">hackberries</a>, (<i>Celtis</i> and allies), but analysis by the <a href="/wiki/Angiosperm_Phylogeny_Group" title="Angiosperm Phylogeny Group">Angiosperm Phylogeny Group</a> members suggests that these genera are better placed in the related family <a href="/wiki/Cannabaceae" title="Cannabaceae">Cannabaceae</a>. The circumscription included in the taxobox is the one suggested by P. Stevens on his Missouri Botanical Garden Angiosperm Phylogeny Website and includes information from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Botanic_Gardens,_Kew" title="Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew">Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew</a> Vascular Plant Families and Genera list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stevens_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-stevens-3">[3]</a></sup> It generally is considered to include ca 7 genera and about 45 species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christenhusz-Byng2016_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christenhusz-Byng2016-4">[4]</a></sup> Some classifications also include the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Ampelocera" title="Ampelocera">Ampelocera</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ulmaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The family is a group of evergreen or deciduous trees and shrubs with <a href="/wiki/Mucilage" title="Mucilage">mucilagenous</a> substances in leaf and bark tissue. <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">Leaves</a> are usually alternate on the stems. The leaf blades are simple (not compound), with entire (smooth) or variously toothed margins, and often have an asymmetrical base. The flowers are small and either bisexual or unisexual.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> is an indehiscent <a href="/wiki/Samara_(fruit)" title="Samara (fruit)">samara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nut</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Drupe" title="Drupe">drupe</a>. <i>Ulmus</i> provides important <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timber" title="Timber">timber trees</a> mostly for <a href="/wiki/Furniture" title="Furniture">furniture</a>, and <i>U. rubra</i>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slippery_elm" title="Slippery elm">Slippery elm</a> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_herbalism" title="Medical herbalism">medicinal plant</a> known for the <a href="/wiki/Demulcent" title="Demulcent">demulcent property</a> of its inner bark. <i>Planera aquatica</i> is also a timber species. <i>Planera</i>, <i>Ulmus</i>, and <i>Zelkova</i> are all grown as <a href="/wiki/Ornamental_plant" title="Ornamental plant">ornamental trees</a>.</p>
