<p><b>Giuliano di Lorenzo de' Medici</b> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Garter" title="Order of the Garter">KG</a> (12 March 1479 – 17 March 1516) was an Italian nobleman, the third son of <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_de%27_Medici" title="Lorenzo de' Medici">Lorenzo the Magnificent</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Giuliano_de%27_Medici,_Duke_of_Nemours&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4326" data-file-width="3442" height="277" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg/220px-Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg/330px-Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg/440px-Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Florenz_-_Neue_Sakristei_Giuliano_II.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Giuliano's Statue in the Medici-Chapel, created by Michelangelo</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a>, his brothers were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piero_di_Lorenzo_de%27_Medici" title="Piero di Lorenzo de' Medici">Piero</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pope_Leo_X" title="Pope Leo X">Giovanni de' Medici</a>.</p>
<p>His older brother Piero was briefly the ruler of <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a> after Lorenzo's death, until the republican faction drove out the Medici in 1494. Giuliano moved therefore to <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medici" title="Medici">Medici</a> family was restored to power after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_League_(1495)" title="Holy League (1495)">Holy League</a> drove the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> forces that had supported the Florentine republicans from Italy. This effort was headed by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Giuliano reigned at Florence from 1512 to 1516.</p>
<p>He married Filiberta (1498–1524), daughter of <a href="/wiki/Philip_II,_Duke_of_Savoy" title="Philip II, Duke of Savoy">Philip II, Duke of Savoy</a>, on 22 February 1515, at the court of France, thanks to the intercession of his brother Giovanni, now Pope as Leo X, in the same year <a href="/wiki/Francis_I_of_France" title="Francis I of France">Francis I of France</a> (Filiberta's nephew) invested him with the title of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duc_de_Nemours" title="Duc de Nemours">Duke of Nemours</a> (which had recently reverted once again to the French crown) on the occasion. The French were also apparently grooming him for the throne of <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> (in which the French maintained a historical interest), when Giuliano died prematurely. He was succeeded in Florence by his nephew <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lorenzo_II_de%27_Medici" title="Lorenzo II de' Medici">Lorenzo</a>.</p>
<p>Giuliano left a single illegitimate son, <a href="/wiki/Ippolito_de%27_Medici" title="Ippolito de' Medici">Ippolito de' Medici</a>, who became a cardinal.</p>
<p>His portrait, painted in Rome by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raffaello_Santi" title="Raffaello Santi">Raphael</a> (a painter favored by Leo), shows Rome's <a href="/wiki/Castel_Sant%27Angelo" title="Castel Sant'Angelo">Castel Sant'Angelo</a> behind a curtain. (A studio version is at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Museum" title="Metropolitan Museum">Metropolitan Museum</a>.)</p>
<p>Giuliano's tomb in the <a href="/wiki/Medici_Chapel" title="Medici Chapel">Medici Chapel</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basilica_di_San_Lorenzo_di_Firenze" title="Basilica di San Lorenzo di Firenze">Church of San Lorenzo</a>, Florence, is ornamented with the <i><a href="/wiki/Night_(Michelangelo)" title="Night (Michelangelo)">Night</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Day_(Michelangelo)" title="Day (Michelangelo)">Day</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Michelangelo" title="Michelangelo">Michelangelo</a>, along with a <a href="/wiki/Portrait_of_Giuliano_de%27_Medici_(1479%E2%80%931516)" title="Portrait of Giuliano de' Medici (1479–1516)">statue of Giuliano</a> by Michelangelo. Due to the identical common name (<a href="/wiki/Giuliano_de%27_Medici" title="Giuliano de' Medici">Giuliano de' Medici</a>) which he shares with his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giuliano_di_Piero_de%27_Medici" title="Giuliano di Piero de' Medici">uncle</a>, whose tomb is also in the Medici Chapel, his tomb is often mistaken for that of his uncle.</p>
