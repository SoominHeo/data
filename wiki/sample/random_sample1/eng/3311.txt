<p><b>Niigata Kenmin FM Broadcast Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">新潟県民エフエム放送株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Niigata Kenmin Efu Emu Hōsō Kabushiki Gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Niigata Prefectural FM Broadcast Co., Ltd.")</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Frequency_modulation" title="Frequency modulation">FM</a> <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio station</a> under the name <b>FM Port</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エフエムポート</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Efu Emu Pōto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. It was founded in 2000.</p>
