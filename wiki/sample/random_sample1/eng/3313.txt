<p><b>Nineveh Governorate</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">نینوى</span>‎‎) is a <a href="/wiki/Governorates_of_Iraq" title="Governorates of Iraq">governorate</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> that contains the ancient Assyrian city of <a href="/wiki/Nineveh" title="Nineveh">Nineveh</a>. It was an integral part of <a href="/wiki/Assyria" title="Assyria">Assyria</a> from the 25th century BC to the 7th century AD, It has an area of 37,323 km<sup>2</sup> (14,410 sq mi) and an estimated population of 2,453,000 people in 2003. Its chief city and provincial capital is <a href="/wiki/Mosul" title="Mosul">Mosul</a>, which lies across the <a href="/wiki/Tigris" title="Tigris">Tigris</a> river from the ruins of ancient Nineveh. <a href="/wiki/Tal_Afar" title="Tal Afar">Tal Afar</a> is the second-biggest city. Before 1976, it was called <b>Mosul Province</b> and included the present-day <a href="/wiki/Dohuk_Governorate" title="Dohuk Governorate">Dohuk Governorate</a>.</p>
<p>An ethnically, religiously and culturally diverse region, it has been subject to attacks by the terrorist organization known as the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</a>, with Mosul being captured on 10 June 2014, and many places of worship and historic ruins and monuments destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A massive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mosul_(2016)" title="Battle of Mosul (2016)">offensive to retake the city</a>, dubbed Operation "We Are Coming, Nineveh" (<span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">قادمون يا نينوى</span>; <i>Qadimun Ya Naynawa</i>), began in October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atlantic1020_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-atlantic1020-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>Its two cities endured the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003_Invasion_of_Iraq" title="2003 Invasion of Iraq">2003 Invasion of Iraq</a> and emerged unscathed. In 2004, Mosul and Tal Afar were fierce between <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a>-led <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraqi_insurgency_(Iraq_War)" title="Iraqi insurgency (Iraq War)">Iraqi insurgency</a>. The insurgents moved to Nineveh after the <a href="/wiki/First_Battle_of_Fallujah" title="First Battle of Fallujah">Battle of Fallujah</a> in 2004.</p>
<p>After the invasion, the military of the province was led by (then Major General) <a href="/wiki/David_Petraeus" title="David Petraeus">David Petraeus</a> of the <a href="/wiki/101st_Airborne_Division" title="101st Airborne Division">101st Airborne Division</a> and later by (then Brigadier General) <a href="/wiki/Carter_Ham" title="Carter Ham">Carter Ham</a> as the multi-national brigade for Iraq. During the American civil head of the local office of the <a href="/wiki/Coalition_Provisional_Authority" title="Coalition Provisional Authority">Coalition Provisional Authority</a> was a US Foreign Service Officer and former Kurdish refugee to the States. Mustafa administered her nominees on the provincial council and through members of the Kashmoula family.</p>
<p>In June 2004, Osama Kashmoula became the interim governor of the province and in September of the same year he was assassinated en route to Baghdad. He was succeeded as interim Governor by <a href="/wiki/Duraid_Kashmoula" title="Duraid Kashmoula">Duraid Kashmoula</a>, who was elected governor in January 2005. Duraid Kashmoula resigned in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In April 2009, <a href="/wiki/Atheel_al-Nujaifi" title="Atheel al-Nujaifi">Atheel al-Nujaifi</a>, a hardline Arab nationalist and member of <a href="/wiki/Al-Hadba" title="Al-Hadba">Al-Hadba</a>, became governor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> While al-Nujaifi's Arab <a href="/wiki/Muttahidoon" title="Muttahidoon">Muttahidoon</a> bloc lost its majority to the Kurdish <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_List" title="Kurdistan List">Brotherhood and Coexistence Alliance List</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ninawa_governorate_election,_2013" title="Ninawa governorate election, 2013">2013 provincial election</a>, al-Nujaifi was reelected as governor by a larger Sunni Arab coalition<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> that was later formalized as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nahda_Bloc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nahda Bloc (page does not exist)">Nahda Bloc</a>.</p>
<p>In June 2014, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insurgents" title="Insurgents">insurgents</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</a> (known as ISIS or ISIL) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_Mosul_offensive" title="2014 Mosul offensive">overran the capital Mosul</a>, forcing an estimated 500,000 refugees to flee the area,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> including governor al-Nujaifi,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> who was subsequently deposed by the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Representatives_of_Iraq" title="Council of Representatives of Iraq">Iraqi Parliament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>While the Kurdish list proposed Hassan al-Allaf, an Arab affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_Islamic_Party" title="Iraqi Islamic Party">Islamic Party</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> the provincial council elected <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nofal_Hammadi_al-Sultan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nofal Hammadi al-Sultan (page does not exist)">Nofal Hammadi</a> (formerly Loyalty to Nineveh List) with the votes of the Nahdha bloc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>An <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mosul_(2016)" title="Battle of Mosul (2016)">offensive to retake Mosul</a> from ISIL control began in October 2016, with Iraqi and Kurdish soldiers supported by a U.S.-led coalition of 60 nations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atlantic1020_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-atlantic1020-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Borders">Borders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nineveh_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Borders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Neighboring Iraqi regions are <a href="/wiki/Dohuk_Governorate" title="Dohuk Governorate">Dohuk Governorate</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Erbil_Governorate" title="Erbil Governorate">Erbil Governorate</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/Saladin_Governorate" title="Saladin Governorate">Saladin Governorate</a> to the south-east, and <a href="/wiki/Al_Anbar_Governorate" title="Al Anbar Governorate">Al Anbar Governorate</a> to the south. In the west it shares a border with <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, mostly <a href="/wiki/Al-Hasakah_Governorate" title="Al-Hasakah Governorate">Al-Hasakah Governorate</a>, and also <a href="/wiki/Deir_ez-Zor_Governorate" title="Deir ez-Zor Governorate">Deir ez-Zor Governorate</a> in the south.</p>

<p>Nineveh Province is multiethnic. There are significant numbers of <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurds" title="Kurds">Kurds</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraqi_Turkmen" title="Iraqi Turkmen">Turkmens</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Yazidis" title="Yazidis">Yazidis</a> both in towns and cities, and in their own specific villages and regions. There are also numbers of <a href="/wiki/Armenians" title="Armenians">Armenians</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kawliya" title="Kawliya">Kawliya</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mandeans" title="Mandeans">Mandeans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shabak_people" title="Shabak people">Shabaks</a>.</p>
<p>In religion, the majority of Arabs are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunni_Muslim" title="Sunni Muslim">Sunni Muslim</a> while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraqi_Turkmen" title="Iraqi Turkmen">Turkmen</a> are mostly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shia_Muslim" title="Shia Muslim">Shi'ite</a>. Kurds are largely Sunni Muslim, although some follow the native <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yarsan" title="Yarsan">Yarsan</a> religion and a few are Christians. <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrians</a> are exclusively <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a>, with the, following either the <a href="/wiki/Chaldean_Catholic_Church" title="Chaldean Catholic Church">Chaldean Catholic Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Catholic_Church" title="Syriac Catholic Church">Syriac Catholic Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Orthodox_Church" title="Syriac Orthodox Church">Syriac Orthodox Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_Church_of_the_East" title="Assyrian Church of the East">Assyrian Church of the East</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Church_of_the_East" title="Ancient Church of the East">Ancient Church of the East</a>. <a href="/wiki/Armenians" title="Armenians">Armenians</a> are also exclusively <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> and mostly followers of the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church" title="Armenian Apostolic Church">Armenian Apostolic Church</a>. <a href="/wiki/Yazidis" title="Yazidis">Yazidis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shabak_people" title="Shabak people">Shabaks</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mandeans" title="Mandeans">Mandeans</a> follow their own religious beliefs: <a href="/wiki/Shabakism" title="Shabakism">Shabakism</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yazidism" title="Yazidism">Yazidism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mandaeism" title="Mandaeism">Mandaeism</a>. However, Arab Sunnis form a majority in the province.</p>
<p>The main language spoken is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>. Minority languages include <a href="/wiki/Neo-Aramaic_languages" title="Neo-Aramaic languages">Neo-Aramaic</a> dialects spoken by the Assyrians, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_language" title="Kurdish language">Kurdish</a> (to include dialects of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurmanji" title="Kurmanji">Kurmanji</a> like <a href="/wiki/Shengali" title="Shengali">Shengali</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shabaki_language" title="Shabaki language">Shabaki</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraqi_Turkman" title="Iraqi Turkman">Turkman</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mandic" title="Mandic">Mandic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_Assyrian_autonomous_region">Proposed Assyrian autonomous region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nineveh_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Proposed Assyrian autonomous region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nineveh_Plains" title="Nineveh Plains">Nineveh Plains</a></div>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (March 2015)">which?</span></a></i>]</sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leader" title="Leader">leaders</a> advocate an autonomous <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_homeland" title="Assyrian homeland">Assyrian homeland</a> within Nineveh Province for the indigenous Assyrians. Most National Iraqi leaders have not taken this plan seriously, but it has a strong proponent in the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Regional_Government" title="Kurdistan Regional Government">Kurdistan Regional Government</a> Minister of Finance <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarkis_Aghajan" title="Sarkis Aghajan">Sarkis Aghajan</a>, himself an Assyrian and a prominent figure in the Kurdish dominated government. Sarkis has claimed that the autonomous region he envisions would stretch from the Syrian border to the north at <a href="/wiki/Faysh_Khabur" title="Faysh Khabur">Faysh Khabur</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bakhdida" title="Bakhdida">Bakhdida</a>. He says it would have its own parliament, executive council, constitution, budget, logo, and flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
