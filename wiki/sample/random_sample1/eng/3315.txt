<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Neiman Marcus Fashion Award</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>The <a href="/wiki/Neiman_Marcus" title="Neiman Marcus">Neiman Marcus</a> Award for Distinguished Service in the Field of Fashion</b> was a yearly award created in 1938 by <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Marcus_Neiman" title="Carrie Marcus Neiman">Carrie Marcus Neiman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Marcus" title="Stanley Marcus">Stanley Marcus</a>. Unlike the <a href="/wiki/Coty_Award" title="Coty Award">Coty Award</a>, it was not limited to American-based fashion designers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcdowell_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcdowell-1">[1]</a></sup> Recipients of the Neiman Marcus Awards include <a href="/wiki/Haute_couture" title="Haute couture">couturiers</a>, non-American-based designers, journalists, manufacturers, and celebrities and style icons who had had a significant personal influence upon fashion such as <a href="/wiki/Grace_Kelly" title="Grace Kelly">Grace Kelly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grace_Mirabella" title="Grace Mirabella">Grace Mirabella</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcdowell_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mcdowell-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The award was typically presented to multiple recipients each year, rather than to a single individual, although <a href="/wiki/Adrian_(costume_designer)" title="Adrian (costume designer)">Adrian</a> was the sole winner in 1943, a feat repeated in 1957 by <a href="/wiki/Coco_Chanel" title="Coco Chanel">Coco Chanel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcdowell_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mcdowell-1">[1]</a></sup> From 1969 the awards became increasingly intermittent, with ceremonies held in 1973, 1979, 1980, 1984 and 1995, the last year in which the awards were presented. For the final ceremony, the founder, Stanley Marcus, received one of his own awards.</p>
