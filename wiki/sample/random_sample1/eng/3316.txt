<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nieman Foundation for Journalism</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>The Nieman Foundation for Journalism at Harvard University</b> is the primary journalism institution at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harvard" title="Harvard">Harvard</a>. It was founded in 1938 as the result of a $1.4 million bequest by Agnes Wahl Nieman, the widow of <a href="/wiki/Lucius_W._Nieman" title="Lucius W. Nieman">Lucius W. Nieman</a>, founder of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Milwaukee_Journal" title="The Milwaukee Journal">The Milwaukee Journal</a></i>. She stated the goal was "to promote and elevate the standards of journalism in the United States and educate persons deemed specially qualified for journalism." It is based at <a href="/wiki/Walter_Lippmann" title="Walter Lippmann">Walter Lippmann</a> House in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programs">Programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nieman_Foundation_for_Journalism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Nieman Foundation is best known as home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nieman_Fellow" title="Nieman Fellow">Nieman Fellows</a>, a group of journalists from around the world who come to Harvard for a year of study. Many noted journalists, and from 1959, also photojournalists, have been Nieman Fellows, including <a href="/wiki/John_Carroll_(actor)" title="John Carroll (actor)">John Carroll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dexter_Filkins" title="Dexter Filkins">Dexter Filkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Orlean" title="Susan Orlean">Susan Orlean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Caro" title="Robert Caro">Robert Caro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hodding_Carter" title="Hodding Carter">Hodding Carter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kirk" title="Michael Kirk">Michael Kirk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Jones_(journalist)" title="Alex Jones (journalist)">Alex Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Lewis" title="Anthony Lewis">Anthony Lewis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Maynard" title="Robert Maynard">Robert Maynard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allister_Sparks" title="Allister Sparks">Allister Sparks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Forman" title="Stanley Forman">Stanley Forman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hedrick_Smith" title="Hedrick Smith">Hedrick Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucia_Annunziata" title="Lucia Annunziata">Lucia Annunziata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Yardley" title="Jonathan Yardley">Jonathan Yardley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philip_Meyer" title="Philip Meyer">Philip Meyer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howard_Sochurek" title="Howard Sochurek">Howard Sochurek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huy_Duc" title="Huy Duc">Huy Duc</a>. It is considered the most prestigious fellowship program for journalists; Nieman Fellows have collectively won 101 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prizes" title="Pulitzer Prizes">Pulitzer Prizes</a>.</p>
<p>The foundation is also the home of <i><b>Nieman Reports</b></i>, a quarterly journal on journalism issues. The journal has been in publication for more than 60 years. For several years, ending in 2009, the foundation sponsored the annual <a class="external text" href="http://nieman.harvard.edu/NiemanFoundation/ProgramsAndPublications/NarrativeJournalism.aspx" rel="nofollow">Nieman Conference on Narrative Journalism</a>, the largest conference of its kind, which attracted hundreds of writers, filmmakers, and broadcasters to Boston. The narrative program now consists of a writing seminar for Fellows, and a public website, <a class="external text" href="http://niemanstoryboard.org/" rel="nofollow">Nieman Storyboard</a>, which covers storytelling across media. In 2004, the Foundation launched <a class="external text" href="http://www.niemanwatchdog.org/" rel="nofollow">Nieman Watchdog</a>, a web site intended to encourage more aggressive questioning of the powerful by news organizations. In 2008, the foundation created the <b>Nieman Journalism Lab</b>, an effort to investigate future models that could support quality journalism.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nieman_Foundation_for_Journalism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several prestigious literary or journalism awards are based at the Nieman Foundation. They include three given in connection with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbia_University_School_of_Journalism" title="Columbia University School of Journalism">Columbia University School of Journalism</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/J._Anthony_Lukas_Book_Prize" title="J. Anthony Lukas Book Prize">J. Anthony Lukas Book Prize</a> ($10,000, "recognizes superb examples of nonfiction writing that exemplify literary grace, a commitment to serious research and social concern")</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Mark_Lynton_History_Prize" title="Mark Lynton History Prize">Mark Lynton History Prize</a> ($10,000, awarded to the "book-length work of history, on any subject, that best combines intellectual or scholarly distinction with felicity of expression")</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/J._Anthony_Lukas_Book_Prize" title="J. Anthony Lukas Book Prize">J. Anthony Lukas Work-in-Progress Award</a> ($30,000, "given annually to aid in the completion of a significant work of nonfiction")</li>
</ul>
<p>Other awards based at Nieman include:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worth_Bingham_Prize_for_Investigative_Reporting" title="Worth Bingham Prize for Investigative Reporting">Worth Bingham Prize for Investigative Reporting</a> ($20,000, "honors investigative reporting of stories of national significance where the public interest is being ill-served")</li>
<li>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=I.F._Stone_Medal_for_Journalistic_Independence&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="I.F. Stone Medal for Journalistic Independence (page does not exist)">I.F. Stone Medal for Journalistic Independence</a> ("to a journalist whose work captures the spirit of independence, integrity, courage and indefatigability that characterized <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I.F._Stone" title="I.F. Stone">I. F. Stone's Weekly</a></i>")</li>
<li>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Lyons_Award_for_Conscience_and_Integrity_in_Journalism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Louis Lyons Award for Conscience and Integrity in Journalism (page does not exist)">Louis Lyons Award for Conscience and Integrity in Journalism</a> ("recognizes displays of conscience and integrity by individuals, groups or institutions in communications")</li>
<li>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Taylor_Family_Award_for_Fairness_in_Newspapers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Taylor Family Award for Fairness in Newspapers (page does not exist)">Taylor Family Award for Fairness in Newspapers</a> ($10,000, "recognizes fairness in newspaper reporting")</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Curators">Curators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nieman_Foundation_for_Journalism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Curators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The leader of the Nieman Foundation is known as its "curator" — a holdover from a brief moment after Agnes Wahl Nieman's death when her gift was to be used to build a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microfilm" title="Microfilm">microfilm</a> library of quality journalism. The foundation has appointed eight curators:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archibald_MacLeish" title="Archibald MacLeish">Archibald MacLeish</a>, 1938-1939</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Louis_M._Lyons" title="Louis M. Lyons">Louis M. Lyons</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nieman_Fellowship" title="Nieman Fellowship">Nieman Fellow</a> class of 1939), 1939-1964</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dwight_E._Sargent" title="Dwight E. Sargent">Dwight E. Sargent</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nieman_Fellowship" title="Nieman Fellowship">Nieman Fellow</a> class of 1951), 1964-1972</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_C._Thomson_Jr." title="James C. Thomson Jr.">James C. Thomson Jr.</a>, 1972-1984</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Howard_Simons" title="Howard Simons">Howard Simons</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nieman_Fellowship" title="Nieman Fellowship">Nieman Fellow</a> class of 1959), 1984-1989</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bill_Kovach" title="Bill Kovach">Bill Kovach</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nieman_Fellowship" title="Nieman Fellowship">Nieman Fellow</a> class of 1989), 1989-2000</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Robert_Giles" title="Robert Giles">Robert H. Giles</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nieman_Fellowship" title="Nieman Fellowship">Nieman Fellow</a> class of 1966), 2000- June 2011</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ann_Marie_Lipinski" title="Ann Marie Lipinski">Ann Marie Lipinski</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nieman_Fellowship" title="Nieman Fellowship">Nieman Fellow</a> class of 1990), 2011 -</li>
</ul>
