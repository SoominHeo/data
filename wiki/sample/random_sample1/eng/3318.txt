<p><b>Yuichi Nishimura</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">西村 雄一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nishimura Yūichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 17 April 1972)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Referee_(association_football)" title="Referee (association football)">referee</a>. He has refereed in the Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League_Division_1" title="J. League Division 1">J. League Division 1</a> since 1999 and has been a full international referee for <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> since 2004. He recently became noticed for his work as referee of the opening game in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> between hosts Brazil and Croatia and also <a href="/wiki/2014_AFC_Champions_League" title="2014 AFC Champions League">2014 AFC Champions League</a> second-leg <a href="/wiki/2014_AFC_Champions_League_Final" title="2014 AFC Champions League Final">final</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuichi_Nishimura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nishimura was the only <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> referee selected to officiate at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2008 African Cup of Nations">2008 African Cup of Nations</a> in Ghana along with <a href="/wiki/Assistant_referee_(association_football)" title="Assistant referee (association football)">assistant referees</a> Toru Sagara from Japan and Jeong Hae-Sang from South Korea.</p>
<p>He refereed the final of the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA Club World Cup">2010 FIFA Club World Cup</a> between <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a> and <a href="/wiki/TP_Mazembe" title="TP Mazembe">TP Mazembe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 November 2010, Nishimura was the referee for the final match of the <a href="/wiki/2010_AFC_Champions_League_Final" title="2010 AFC Champions League Final">2010 AFC Champions League Final</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seongnam_Ilhwa_Chunma" title="Seongnam Ilhwa Chunma">Seongnam Ilhwa Chunma</a> vs. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zob_Ahan_Isfahan_FC" title="Zob Ahan Isfahan FC">Zob Ahan</a>, at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Stadium_(Tokyo)" title="National Olympic Stadium (Tokyo)">National Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Nishimura officiated at the <a href="/wiki/2011_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2011 AFC Asian Cup">2011 AFC Asian Cup</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2012 Men's Olympic Football Tournament</a>, Nishimura officiated the group stage matches between Brazil and Belarus<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and host Great Britain and Uruguay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nishimura was appointed to the second leg of the <a href="/wiki/2014_AFC_Champions_League" title="2014 AFC Champions League">2014 AFC Champions League</a> Final in which <a href="/wiki/Al-Hilal_FC" title="Al-Hilal FC">Al-Hilal FC</a> faced <a href="/wiki/Western_Sydney_Wanderers_FC" title="Western Sydney Wanderers FC">Western Sydney Wanderers FC</a>. The match ended in a dramatic 0-0 upset which saw the Western Sydney Wanderers crowned AFC champions courtesy of their 1-0 victory in the first leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_FIFA_World_Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuichi_Nishimura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2010 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nishimura was preselected as a referee for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fifa2010_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-fifa2010-5">[5]</a></sup> He was the referee for the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a> against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> game at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>, along with fourth official Joel Aguilar and assistant referees Jeong Hae-Sang, and Toru Sagara.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YahooSports_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-YahooSports-6">[6]</a></sup> Nishimura produced the first red card of the tournament, when he sent Uruguay midfielder <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Lodeiro" title="Nicolás Lodeiro">Nicolás Lodeiro</a> off the field for two yellow cards. During <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_knockout_stage#Netherlands_vs_Brazil" title="2010 FIFA World Cup knockout stage">the first quarter-final game</a> between Brazil and the Netherlands at <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela_Bay_Stadium" title="Nelson Mandela Bay Stadium">Nelson Mandela Bay Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Port_Elizabeth" title="Port Elizabeth">Port Elizabeth</a>, Nishimura sent off <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Melo" title="Felipe Melo">Felipe Melo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> in the 73rd minute of the game. He was the 4th official for the final match of the World Cup, <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2010 FIFA World Cup Final">Netherlands vs. Spain</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_FIFA_World_Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuichi_Nishimura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Nishimura was chosen by FIFA to referee the opening game of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> between <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Group_A#Brazil_vs_Croatia" title="2014 FIFA World Cup Group A">Brazil–Croatia</a>. With this, he became only the third Japanese referee to officiate at two <a href="/wiki/List_of_FIFA_World_Cup_finals" title="List of FIFA World Cup finals">FIFA World Cups</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Shizuo_Takada" title="Shizuo Takada">Shizuo Takada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toru_Kamikawa" title="Toru Kamikawa">Toru Kamikawa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIFA_Nishimura_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA_Nishimura-7">[7]</a></sup> In the match, Nishimura became the first referee to use <a href="/wiki/Vanishing_spray" title="Vanishing spray">vanishing spray</a> at a World Cup finals, as well as to have access to <a href="/wiki/Goal-line_technology" title="Goal-line technology">goal-line technology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The game ended with a 3–1 victory for hosts <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>. Despite criticism,</p>
<p>Nishimura was appointed to two other games in the tournament as the <a href="/wiki/Assistant_referee_(association_football)#Fourth_official" title="Assistant referee (association football)">fourth official</a>: the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Group_E#Honduras_vs_Ecuador" title="2014 FIFA World Cup Group E">Group E match</a> between <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_national_football_team" title="Ecuador national football team">Ecuador</a> (2–1 win for Ecuador), and the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_knockout_stage#Third_place_play-off" title="2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage">third place play-off match</a> between Brazil and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> (3–0 win for the Netherlands).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nishimura_fourth_official_Honduras-Ecuador_Brazil-Netherlands_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nishimura_fourth_official_Honduras-Ecuador_Brazil-Netherlands-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nishimura_fourth_official_Honduras-Ecuador_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nishimura_fourth_official_Honduras-Ecuador-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nishimura_fourth_official_Brazil-Netherlands_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nishimura_fourth_official_Brazil-Netherlands-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yuichi_Nishimura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nishimura was chosen as the J. League Referee of the Year in 2009 and 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Nishimura was named Best Men's Referee of the Year by the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">Asian Football Confederation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFCAward_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-AFCAward-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
