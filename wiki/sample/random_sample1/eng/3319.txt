<p><b>Nishitsugaru District</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">西津軽郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nishitsugaru-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">rural district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Aomori_Prefecture" title="Aomori Prefecture">Aomori Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of September 2013, the district had an estimated population of 19,666 and an area of 831.85 km². All of the city of <a href="/wiki/Tsugaru,_Aomori" title="Tsugaru, Aomori">Tsugaru</a> was formerly part of Nishitsugaru District.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishitsugaru_District,_Aomori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of Nishitsugaru District was formerly part of <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a>. At the time of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_restoration" title="Meiji restoration">Meiji restoration</a> of 1868, the area consisted of one towns (Ajigasawa) and 218 villages, all formerly under the control of <a href="/wiki/Hirosaki_Domain" title="Hirosaki Domain">Hirosaki Domain</a>. Aomori Prefecture was founded on December 13, 1871, and Nishitsugaru District was carved out for former Tsugaru District on October 30, 1878.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_2">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishitsugaru_District,_Aomori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the establishment of the municipality system on April 1, 1889, Nishitsugaru District, was organized into one town (Ajigasawa) and 19 villages was established.</p>
<ul>
<li>1905 - Kizukuri was elevated to town status.</li>
<li>1926 - Fukaura was elevated to town status.</li>
<li>1955 – Mizumoto and Jusan villages were transferred to <a href="/wiki/Kitatsugaru_District,_Aomori" title="Kitatsugaru District, Aomori">Kitatsugaru District</a></li>
<li>On February 11, 2005 the town of <a href="/wiki/Kizukuri,_Aomori" title="Kizukuri, Aomori">Kizukuri</a> and the villages of <a href="/wiki/Inagaki,_Aomori" title="Inagaki, Aomori">Inagaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kashiwa,_Aomori" title="Kashiwa, Aomori">Kashiwa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morita,_Aomori" title="Morita, Aomori">Morita</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shariki,_Aomori" title="Shariki, Aomori">Shariki</a> merged forming the new city of <a href="/wiki/Tsugaru,_Aomori" title="Tsugaru, Aomori">Tsugaru</a>.</li>
<li>On March 31, 2005 the village of <a href="/wiki/Iwasaki,_Aomori" title="Iwasaki, Aomori">Iwasaki</a> merged into the town of <a href="/wiki/Fukaura,_Aomori" title="Fukaura, Aomori">Fukaura</a>.</li>
</ul>
