<p><b>Nishiyatsushiro</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">西八代郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nishiyatsushiro-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Yamanashi_Prefecture" title="Yamanashi Prefecture">Yamanashi Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of February 1, 2006, the district has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 17,878 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 238 persons per km². The total area is 75.07 km².</p>
<p>As of March 1, 2006, the district has only one town left.</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ichikawamisato,_Yamanashi" title="Ichikawamisato, Yamanashi">Ichikawamisato</a> (founded on October 1, 2005 by the mergers of the towns of <a href="/wiki/Ichikawadaimon,_Yamanashi" title="Ichikawadaimon, Yamanashi">Ichikawadaimon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mitama,_Yamanashi" title="Mitama, Yamanashi">Mitama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rokug%C5%8D,_Yamanashi" title="Rokugō, Yamanashi">Rokugō</a>)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishiyatsushiro_District,_Yamanashi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the Massive mergers of Showa, the east shore of Fujigawa belonged to Nishiyatsushiro District, and the west shore belonged to Minamikoma District.</p>
<ul>
<li>On February 11, 1955 when the town of Okawauchi merged with the town of Minobu from Minamikoma District and 3 towns to form the new "town of Minobu in Minamikoma District,"</li>
<li>On April 1, 1955 the village of Sakae merged with the village of Akatsukisawa from Minamikoma District to form the town of Nambu in Minamikoma District.</li>
<li>On September 30, 1956, parts of the village of Daido went to the town of Ajisawa in Minamikoma District.</li>
<li>On January 1, 1958, parts of the town of Ichikawadaimon were merged into the town of Ajisawa.</li>
<li>On April 1, 1958, parts of the town of Shimobe merged into the town of Nakatomi in Minamikoma District; Nishiyatsushiro District started to eat more areas by Minamikoma District.</li>
<li>On September 13, 2004, the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shimobe,_Yamanashi" title="Shimobe, Yamanashi">Shimobe</a> merged with the towns of <a href="/wiki/Minobu,_Yamanashi" title="Minobu, Yamanashi">Minobu</a> (former) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakatomi,_Yamanashi" title="Nakatomi, Yamanashi">Nakatomi</a>, both from <a href="/wiki/Minamikoma_District,_Yamanashi" title="Minamikoma District, Yamanashi">Minamikoma District</a>, to form the town of <a href="/wiki/Minobu,_Yamanashi" title="Minobu, Yamanashi">Minobu</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Minamikoma_District,_Yamanashi" title="Minamikoma District, Yamanashi">Minamikoma District</a>.</li>
<li>On October 1, 2005, the towns of <a href="/wiki/Ichikawadaimon,_Yamanashi" title="Ichikawadaimon, Yamanashi">Ichikawadaimon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mitama,_Yamanashi" title="Mitama, Yamanashi">Mitama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rokug%C5%8D,_Yamanashi" title="Rokugō, Yamanashi">Rokugō</a> merged to form the new town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ichikawamisato,_Yamanashi" title="Ichikawamisato, Yamanashi">Ichikawamisato</a>.</li>
<li>On March 1, 2006:
<ul>
<li>the northern part of <a href="/wiki/Kamikuishiki,_Yamanashi" title="Kamikuishiki, Yamanashi">Kamikuishiki</a> (the localities of <i>Furuseki, Kakehashi</i>) merged into the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dfu,_Yamanashi" title="Kōfu, Yamanashi">Kōfu</a></li>
<li>the southern part of Kamikuishiki (the localities of <i>Fujigane, Motosu, Shoji</i>) merged into the town of <a href="/wiki/Fujikawaguchiko,_Yamanashi" title="Fujikawaguchiko, Yamanashi">Fujikawaguchiko</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Minamitsuru_District,_Yamanashi" title="Minamitsuru District, Yamanashi">Minamitsuru District</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The district used to have the area of the east shore of Fujikawa, but due to these mergers, the district is now merely the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ichikawamisato,_Yamanashi" title="Ichikawamisato, Yamanashi">Ichikawamisato</a>.</p>
