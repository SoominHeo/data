<p><b>Nishikasugai</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">西春日井郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nishikasugai-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Aichi_Prefecture" title="Aichi Prefecture">Aichi Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About">About</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikasugai_District,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: About">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located on the central <a href="/wiki/Owari_Province" title="Owari Province">Owari Region</a>. The population is 22,194 people; the total area is 10.20 km² (as of March 1, 2007). Currently the district has the town of <a href="/wiki/Toyoyama,_Aichi" title="Toyoyama, Aichi">Toyoyama</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikasugai_District,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="About_2">About</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikasugai_District,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: About">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the old days, the district extended as far as Nagoya Castle, but since Taisho Era to the 1950s, most of the areas were merged into the city of <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>.</p>
<p>Recently, there were proposals to turn the entire district into a city but the merger was killed due to differences of opinion. After the merger was killed, the towns of <a href="/wiki/Kiyosu,_Aichi_(town)" title="Kiyosu, Aichi (town)">Kiyosu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shinkawa,_Aichi" title="Shinkawa, Aichi">Shinkawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nishibiwajima,_Aichi" title="Nishibiwajima, Aichi">Nishibiwajima</a> merged in 2005 (City of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiyosu,_Aichi" title="Kiyosu, Aichi">Kiyosu</a>). Again in 2006, the towns of <a href="/wiki/Nishiharu,_Aichi" title="Nishiharu, Aichi">Nishiharu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shikatsu,_Aichi" title="Shikatsu, Aichi">Shikatsu</a> merged into (City of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitanagoya,_Aichi" title="Kitanagoya, Aichi">Kitanagoya</a>). And most recently in 2009, the town of <a href="/wiki/Haruhi,_Aichi" title="Haruhi, Aichi">Haruhi</a> merged into Kiyosu. As a result of this merger, the district has only the town of <a href="/wiki/Toyoyama,_Aichi" title="Toyoyama, Aichi">Toyoyama</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li>November 1, 1963 - The village of Nishiharu gained town status. (5 towns, 2 villages)</li>
<li>April 1, 1972 - The village of Toyoyama gained town status. (6 towns, 1 village)</li>
<li>April 1, 1990 - The village of Haruhi gained town status. (7 towns)</li>
<li>July 7, 2005 - The towns of Kiyosu, Shinkawa and Nishibiwajima merged to form the City of Kiyosu. (4 towns)</li>
<li>March 20, 2006 - The towns of Nishiharu and Shikatsu merged to form the city of Kitanagoya. (2 towns)</li>
<li>October 1, 2009 - The town of <a href="/wiki/Haruhi,_Aichi" title="Haruhi, Aichi">Haruhi</a> <a href="/wiki/Municipal_mergers_and_dissolutions_in_Japan" title="Municipal mergers and dissolutions in Japan">merged</a> into the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiyosu,_Aichi" title="Kiyosu, Aichi">Kiyosu</a>. This leaves <b>Nishikasugai District</b> with one municipality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Plannedmerge_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Plannedmerge-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikasugai_District,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/J-Air" title="J-Air">J-Air</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Japan_Airlines" title="Japan Airlines">Japan Airlines</a>, is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Toyoyama,_Aichi" title="Toyoyama, Aichi">Toyoyama</a>, Nishikasugai District, in the <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Airfield" title="Nagoya Airfield">Nagoya Airfield</a> Terminal Building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nakanihon_Air_Service" title="Nakanihon Air Service">Nakanihon Air Service</a>, a general aviation operator, is also located in Toyoyama on the airport grounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
