<p><b>Nishikatsura</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">西桂町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nishikatsura-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Minamitsuru_District,_Yamanashi" title="Minamitsuru District, Yamanashi">Minamitsuru District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yamanashi_Prefecture" title="Yamanashi Prefecture">Yamanashi Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of November 2015, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 4,270 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 281 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area was 15.22 square kilometres (5.88 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikatsura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nishikatsura is situated in the Katsura River valley in southern Yamanashi Prefecture, near the northern foot of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Fuji" title="Mount Fuji">Mount Fuji</a>, and is the departure point for climbs of Mount Mitsutoge, which affords unhindered views of Mount Fuji. The area is also famous for pure, clean water.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikatsura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area around modern Nishikatsura has been settled since prehistoric times, and developed from the <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a>, as a hamlet on the pilgrimage route to Mount Fuji. During the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, all of <a href="/wiki/Kai_Province" title="Kai Province">Kai Province</a> was <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%8D" title="Tenryō">tenryō</a></i> territory under direct control of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>. During the cadastral reform of the early <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a> in July 1, 1889, the village of Katsura was created within Minamitsuru District, Yamanashi Prefecture. It was renamed Nishikatsura on June 3, 1893 after expansion through annexation of portions of neighboring villages, and was raised to town status on September 15, 1952.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikatsura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nishikatsura is home to numerous small textile plants, which manufacture neckties and umbrellas.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nishikatsura&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nishikatsura has one elementary school and one middle school.</p>
