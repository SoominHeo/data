</tr>
</table>
</div>
<p>The <b>Blue House raid</b> (also known in South Korea as the <b>January 21 Incident</b>) was an unsuccessful attempt by <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> commandos to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assassinate" title="Assassinate">assassinate</a> the <a href="/wiki/President_of_South_Korea" title="President of South Korea">South Korean president</a>, <a href="/wiki/Park_Chung-hee" title="Park Chung-hee">Park Chung-hee</a> in his residence at the <a href="/wiki/Blue_House" title="Blue House">Blue House</a>, on January 21, 1968.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_House_raid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The attack at the Blue House took place in the context of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_DMZ_Conflict_(1966%E2%80%9369)" title="Korean DMZ Conflict (1966–69)">Korean DMZ Conflict (1966–69)</a>, which in turn was influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>. Following the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_1967" title="South Korean presidential election, 1967">South Korean presidential election, 1967</a> and the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_legislative_election,_1967" title="South Korean legislative election, 1967">South Korean legislative election, 1967</a>, the North Korean leadership concluded that Park Chung Hee's domestic opposition no longer constituted a serious challenge to his rule. On June 28 – July 3, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea">Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea</a> held an extended plenum at which North Korean leader <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a> called on the cadres "to prepare to give assistance to the struggle of our South Korean brethren." In July 1967, a special squad of the recently established <a href="/wiki/Unit_124" title="Unit 124">Unit 124</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Army" title="Korean People's Army">Korean People's Army</a> (KPA) was entrusted with the task of assassinating Park. This decision was probably facilitated by the fact that in 1967, the Vietnam War entered a new stage of escalation, under which circumstances the U.S. military forces, preoccupied as they were with Vietnam, could not easily take retaliatory measures against North Korea. In 1965-1968, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea%E2%80%93Vietnam_relations" title="North Korea–Vietnam relations">North Korea-North Vietnam relations</a> were very close, and the DPRK provided substantial military and economic assistance to <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a>. North Korean propaganda sought to depict the post-1966 commando raids as a South Korean guerrilla movement akin to the <a href="/wiki/Viet_Cong" title="Viet Cong">Viet Cong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Szalontai_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Szalontai-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_House_raid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thirty-one men were handpicked from the elite all-officer Unit 124. This special operation <a href="/wiki/Commando" title="Commando">commando</a> unit trained for two years and spent their final 15 days rehearsing action on the objective in a full-scale <a href="/wiki/Mockup" title="Mockup">mockup</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Blue_House" title="Blue House">Blue House</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>These specially selected men were trained in infiltration and <a href="/wiki/Extraction_(military)" title="Extraction (military)">exfiltration</a> techniques, weaponry, navigation, airborne operations, amphibious infiltration, <a href="/wiki/Hand-to-hand_combat" title="Hand-to-hand combat">hand-to-hand combat</a> (with emphasis on <a href="/wiki/Knife_fight" title="Knife fight">knife fighting</a>), and concealment.</p>
<p>As <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Shin-Jo" title="Kim Shin-Jo">Kim Shin-Jo</a>, the only known surviving commando, stated, "It made us fearless—no one would think to look for us in a graveyard."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Their training was difficult and often in adverse conditions, such as running at a speed of eight miles an hour with sixty-six pound rucksacks over broken and unforgiving terrain, which sometimes resulted in injuries such as lost toes and feet from <a href="/wiki/Frostbite" title="Frostbite">frostbite</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Raid">Raid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_House_raid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Raid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Infiltration">Infiltration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_House_raid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Infiltration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 16, 1968, Unit 124 left their garrison at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yonsan" title="Yonsan">Yonsan</a>. On January 17, 1968, at 11 p.m., they infiltrated the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Demilitarized_Zone" title="Korean Demilitarized Zone">DMZ</a> by cutting through the fencing of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="2nd Infantry Division (United States)">U.S. Army's 2nd Infantry Division</a>'s sector. By 2 a.m. the next day they had set up camp at Morae-dong and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seokpo-ri&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seokpo-ri (page does not exist)">Seokpo-ri</a>. On January 19, at 5 a.m., after having crossed the <a href="/wiki/Imjin_River" title="Imjin River">Imjin River</a>, they set up camp on Simbong Mountain.</p>
<p>At 2 p.m., four brothers named Woo from Beopwon-ri were out cutting firewood and stumbled across the unit's camp. After a fierce debate over whether or not to kill the brothers, it was decided instead to try to indoctrinate them on the alleged benefits of <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communism</a> and they were released with a stern warning not to notify the police. However, the brothers immediately reported the presence of the unit to the Changhyeon police station in Beopwon-ri.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The unit broke camp and increased their pace to more than ten kilometers per hour, despite carrying 30 kilograms of equipment each, crossing Nogo Mountain and arriving at Bibong Mountain on January 20 at 7 a.m. Three battalions from the <a href="/wiki/25th_Infantry_Division_(South_Korea)" title="25th Infantry Division (South Korea)">South Korean 25th Infantry Division</a> began searching Nogo Mountain for the infiltrators, but they had already left the area. The unit entered <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> in two- and three-man cells on the night of January 20 and regrouped at the Seungga-sa Temple, where they made their final preparations for the attack.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the ROK (Republic of Korea) High Command added the 30th Infantry Division and Airborne Corps to the search and police began searching along <a href="/wiki/Hongje-dong" title="Hongje-dong">Hongje-dong</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeongreung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeongreung (page does not exist)">Jeongreung</a>, and Bukak Mountain. Given the increased security measures that had been implemented throughout the city and realizing their original plan had little chance of success, the team leader improvised a new plan.</p>
<p>Changing into <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Army" title="Republic of Korea Army">Republic of Korea Army</a> (ROKA) uniforms of the local 26th Infantry Division, complete with the correct unit insignia (which they had brought with them), they formed up and prepared to march the last mile to the Blue House, posing as ROKA soldiers returning from a counter-infiltration patrol. The unit marched along Segeomjeong Road near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jahamun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jahamun (page does not exist)">Jahamun</a> toward the Blue House, passing several National Police and ROKA units en route.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflict">Conflict</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_House_raid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Conflict">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At 10 p.m. on January 21, 1968, the unit approached the Segeomjeong–Jahamun checkpoint less than 100 meters from the Blue House, where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jongro" title="Jongro">Jongro</a> police chief Choi Gyushik approached the unit and began to question them. When he grew suspicious of their answers, he drew his pistol and was shot by members of the unit who started firing and throwing grenades at the checkpoint. After several minutes of shooting, the unit dispersed, with some heading off to <a href="/wiki/Inwangsan" title="Inwangsan">Inwang Mountain</a>, Bibong Mountain, and <a href="/wiki/Uijeongbu" title="Uijeongbu">Uijeongbu</a>. Police Chief Choi and Assistant Inspector Jung Jong-su were killed in the firefight; one commando was captured but managed to commit suicide. Before the night was over, 92 South Koreans had become casualties of the firefight, among them nearly two-dozen civilians who were on a bus that passed through the line of fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 22, 1968, the ROK Army's 6th Corps began a massive sweep operation to capture or kill any members of the unit. Soldiers from the 92nd Regiment, 30th Infantry Division captured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Shin-Jo" title="Kim Shin-Jo">Kim Shin-Jo</a>, who had been hiding in a civilian's house near Inwang Mountain. The 30th Battalion, Capital Defense Command, killed four commandos in <a href="/wiki/Buam-dong,_Seoul" title="Buam-dong, Seoul">Buam-dong</a> and on Bukak Mountain.</p>
<p>On January 23, the 26th Infantry Division's Engineer Battalion killed one commando on <a href="/wiki/Dobongsan" title="Dobongsan">Dobong Mountain</a>. On January 24, 1968, the 26th Infantry Division and <a href="/wiki/1st_Infantry_Division_(South_Korea)" title="1st Infantry Division (South Korea)">1st Infantry Division</a> soldiers killed twelve commandos near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seongu-ri&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seongu-ri (page does not exist)">Seongu-ri</a>. On January 25, three commandos were killed near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Songchu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Songchu (page does not exist)">Songchu</a>. On January 29, six commandos were killed near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papyeong" title="Papyeong">Papyeong</a> Mountain.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Casualties">Casualties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_House_raid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Casualties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the course of this assassination attempt, South Korean casualties totaled 26 killed and 66 wounded, including about two dozen civilians. Four Americans also were killed in attempts to block the escaping infiltrators from crossing the DMZ.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Of the 31 members of Unit 124, twenty-nine were killed; one, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Shin-Jo" title="Kim Shin-Jo">Kim Shin-Jo</a>, was captured,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and the other one, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Jae-kyung" title="Park Jae-kyung">Park Jae-kyung</a> escaped to North Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The bodies of the members of Unit 124 killed in the raid were subsequently buried in the <a href="/wiki/Cemetery_for_North_Korean_and_Chinese_Soldiers" title="Cemetery for North Korean and Chinese Soldiers">Cemetery for North Korean and Chinese Soldiers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_House_raid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 22, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Command" title="United Nations Command">United Nations Command</a> (UNC) requested that a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_Armistice_Commission" title="Military Armistice Commission">Military Armistice Commission</a> (MAC) meeting be held to discuss the raid. The UNC requested the meeting for January 23, but the North Koreans asked for a day's delay. On January 23, the <a href="/wiki/USS_Pueblo_(AGER-2)" title="USS Pueblo (AGER-2)">USS <i>Pueblo</i> (AGER-2)</a>, a technical research ship of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, was captured by North Korea. Consequently, the MAC meeting held on January 24 had to deal not only with the raid but also with the <i>Pueblo</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s capture. To a considerable extent, the seizure of the <i>Pueblo</i> diverted U.S. and international attention from the Blue House raid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Blue House raid occurred on the same day when the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Khe_Sanh" title="Battle of Khe Sanh">Battle of Khe Sanh</a> started in Vietnam and on January 31 the <a href="/wiki/Tet_Offensive" title="Tet Offensive">Tet Offensive</a> broke out across South Vietnam, making any U.S. support for South Korean retaliation unlikely. In Saigon, Viet Cong guerrillas attempted to assassinate President <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_Thi%E1%BB%87u" title="Nguyễn Văn Thiệu">Nguyễn Văn Thiệu</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Independence_Palace" title="Independence Palace">Independence Palace</a> but were quickly beaten back. Some writers have suggested that due to the similarities of both attacks by an almost identical number of commandos (31 in Seoul and 34 in Saigon, respectively) that the North Korean leaders had a certain insight into Vietnamese Communist military operations, and wanted to take advantage of the Vietnam War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Szalontai_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Szalontai-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson" title="Lyndon B. Johnson">President Johnson</a> regarded the seizure of the <i>Pueblo</i> and the timing of the Tet Offensive to have been coordinated to divert U.S. resources away from Vietnam and to force the South Koreans to withdraw their two Divisions and Marine Brigade from South Vietnam. Unlike President Johnson, <a href="/wiki/Charles_H._Bonesteel_III" title="Charles H. Bonesteel III">General Bonesteel</a> saw no such connection. He regarded the Blue House Raid as having been planned at the highest levels in North Korea, while the seizure of the <i>Pueblo</i> seemed merely opportunistic and the timing of the Tet Offensive as helpful but coincidental.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In response to the assassination attempt, the South Korean government organized the ill-fated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unit_684" title="Unit 684">Unit 684</a>. This group was intended to assassinate the leader of North Korea, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a>. However, following an improvement in intra-Korean relations, the unit's assassination mission was cancelled and in 1971 the Unit revolted and most of its members were killed.</p>
<p>In May 1972, Kim Il-sung expressed regret and claimed that the Blue House Raid "was entirely plotted by extreme leftists and did not reflect my intent or that of the Party" to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KCIA" title="KCIA">KCIA</a> head <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hu-rak" title="Lee Hu-rak">Lee Hu-rak</a> during their meeting in Pyongyang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
