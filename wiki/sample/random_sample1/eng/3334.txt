<p>Viscount <b>Niwa Nagakuni <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">丹羽長国</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Niwa Nagakuni</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>;</b> (May 22, 1834 – January 15, 1904) was a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">daimyo</a> of the late Edo period who ruled <a href="/wiki/Nihonmatsu_Domain" title="Nihonmatsu Domain">Nihonmatsu han</a> and is famous for his leadership of the domain during the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niwa_Nagakuni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nagakuni, known in his childhood as Hōzō (保蔵) was born in Nihonmatsu on May 22, 1834, the 6th son of <a href="/wiki/Niwa_Nagatomi" title="Niwa Nagatomi">Niwa Nagatomi</a>. On November 15, 1858<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> he succeeded to the family headship upon his father's retirement. He continued the joint coastal defense mission at Tomitsu (together with <a href="/wiki/Aizu" title="Aizu">Aizu</a> han) begun by his father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.E6.AD.B4.E5.8F.B2.E3.81.AE.E5.8B.89.E5.BC.B7.E3.83.BB.E4.B8.B9.E7.BE.BD.E6.B0.8F_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-.E6.AD.B4.E5.8F.B2.E3.81.AE.E5.8B.89.E5.BC.B7.E3.83.BB.E4.B8.B9.E7.BE.BD.E6.B0.8F-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1860, he sent forces for security duty in Kyoto. This, as well as a fire in his castle town, seriously drained his resources. These expenses were compounded by the economic difficulties the domain faced following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenpo_famine" title="Tenpo famine">Tenpo famines</a>, as well as bureaucratic corruption. As a result, Nihonmatsu was utterly economically paralyzed by the end of the Edo period. Despite this, it joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ouetsu_Reppan_Domei" title="Ouetsu Reppan Domei">Northern Confederation of Allied Domains</a> in 1868, and fought against the forces of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_Government" title="Meiji Government">Meiji Government</a>. However, Nihonmatsu was defeated, and forced to give up 50,000 <i><a href="/wiki/Koku" title="Koku">koku</a></i> of its holdings. Also, as per the government's conditions, Nagakuni retired, and his adopted son <a href="/wiki/Niwa_Nagahiro" title="Niwa Nagahiro">Niwa Nagahiro</a> (brother of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yonezawa_han" title="Yonezawa han">Yonezawa han</a> daimyo <a href="/wiki/Uesugi_Mochinori" title="Uesugi Mochinori">Uesugi Mochinori</a>) succeeded him.</p>
<p>Nagakuni held the title of <i>Sakyō-dayū</i> (左京大夫) and the junior 4th court rank, lower grade (<i>jū shi-i no ge</i> 従四位下).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.E6.AD.B4.E5.8F.B2.E3.81.AE.E5.8B.89.E5.BC.B7.E3.83.BB.E4.B8.B9.E7.BE.BD.E6.B0.8F_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-.E6.AD.B4.E5.8F.B2.E3.81.AE.E5.8B.89.E5.BC.B7.E3.83.BB.E4.B8.B9.E7.BE.BD.E6.B0.8F-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the Meiji era, Nagakuni lived to see the Niwa clan recover some of its fortunes in society, and received the title of viscount (子爵 <i>shishaku</i>). He died in 1904.</p>
