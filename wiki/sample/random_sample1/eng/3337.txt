<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rivers and ponds take up one third of the city's territory. Nizhny Tagil spans 22 kilometers (14 mi) from north to south and 21 kilometers (13 mi) from east to west. The city is built around the extinct <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> Lisya Mountain. This mountain with a watchtower on its top is a symbol of the city. Another mountain, Medved-Kamen, is located in the northern part of the city and is 100 meters (330 ft) high. High rock walls break into the <a href="/wiki/Tagil_River" title="Tagil River">Tagil River</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Neighboring cities include <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">Yekaterinburg</a> 130 kilometers (81 mi) to the south, <a href="/wiki/Serov_(town)" title="Serov (town)">Serov</a> and Priobye in the north, <a href="/wiki/Perm" title="Perm">Perm</a> in the west, and <a href="/wiki/Alapayevsk" title="Alapayevsk">Alapayevsk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Verkhnyaya_Salda" title="Verkhnyaya Salda">Verkhnyaya Salda</a> in the east.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The altitude of the city varies from 170 to 380 meters (560 to 1,250 ft). This makes Nizhny Tagil one of the rare natural store-rooms on the Earth.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="(October 2015)">why?</span></a></i>]</sup> There are many mineral deposits containing 63 elements of the <a href="/wiki/Periodic_table" title="Periodic table">periodic table</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The history of Nizhny Tagil begins with the opening of the Vysokogorsky iron ore quarry in 1696. The deposits were particularly rich, and included lodes of pure magnetic iron. The surrounding landscape provided everything needed for a successful and productive mining and smelting operation — rivers for transport, forests for fuel, and suitable climate.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Over the following decades, the city developed as one of the early centers of Russian industrialization, and it has been a major producer of <a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">cast iron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The first Russian steam <a href="/wiki/Locomotive" title="Locomotive">locomotive</a> was constructed there in 1833, and the father-and-son engineers who developed it, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cherepanovs" title="Cherepanovs">Yefim and Miron Cherepanovs (Черепанов)</a>, were in 1956 commemorated by an 8-meter (26 ft) bronze statue (executed by sculptor A. S. Kondratyev and architect A. V. Sotnikov) which stands in the center of the Theatrical Square in the heart of downtown.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>According to some sources, the copper for the skin of the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">Statue of Liberty</a> was mined and refined in Nizhny Tagil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2007, a mass grave with 30 murdered girls and women was found near Nizhny Tagil. They had been abducted in the city by a prostitution gang between 2002 and 2006. See <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nizhny_Tagil_mass_murder_(2002-2007)" title="Nizhny Tagil mass murder (2002-2007)">Nizhny Tagil mass murder (2002-2007)</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A frightful, fictionalized description of Nizhny Tagil and these murders appears in “<a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Sanction" title="The Bourne Sanction">The Bourne Sanction</a>,” by Eric Van Lustbader, chapters 28, 30, 34 and 39.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of the administrative divisions</a>, Nizhny Tagil serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Prigorodny_District,_Sverdlovsk_Oblast" title="Prigorodny District, Sverdlovsk Oblast">Prigorodny District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OKATO_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-OKATO-1">[1]</a></sup> even though it is not a part of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OKATO2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OKATO2-2">[2]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is, together with twenty-three <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural localities</a>, incorporated separately as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">City</a> of Nizhny Tagil</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OKATO2_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-OKATO2-2">[2]</a></sup>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Sverdlovsk_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Sverdlovsk Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SverdlovskO_adm_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-SverdlovskO_adm-10">[10]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the City of Nizhny Tagil is incorporated as <b>Nizhny Tagil Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SverdlovskO_mun_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-SverdlovskO_mun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city is divided into three city districts: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Leninsky_City_District,_Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Leninsky City District, Nizhny Tagil (page does not exist)">Leninsky</a>, encompassing the city center and Nizhnetagilsky Pond; <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tagilstroyevsky_City_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tagilstroyevsky City District (page does not exist)">Tagilstroyevsky</a>, a comparatively small section at the north part of town; and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dzerzhinsky_City_District,_Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dzerzhinsky City District, Nizhny Tagil (page does not exist)">Dzerzhinsky</a>, a sizable section to the east of the city center principally consisting of apartment buildings and other residences.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A large portion of the land within the bounds of the city is dominated by the facilities of the various factories located in this industrial city, including those of Oxochem.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Nizhny Tagil is an industrial center of the Middle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urals" title="Urals">Urals</a>. Such highly power-intensive industries as <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">ferrous metallurgy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, and metal working are well-developed in the city. A total of 606 manufacturing companies operate in Nizhny Tagil.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nizhny_Tagil_Iron_and_Steel_Plant" title="Nizhny Tagil Iron and Steel Plant">Nizhny Tagil Iron and Steel Plant</a> (<i>Nizhnetagilsky Metallurgichesky Kombinat</i>, <i>NTMK</i>) is a leading <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> steel company.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Uralvagonzavod" title="Uralvagonzavod">Uralvagonzavod</a></i> (<i>UVZ</i>) is well known in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> as the main producer of modern <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">tanks</a> on the territory of the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and Russia. It is the largest <a href="/wiki/Main_battle_tank" title="Main battle tank">main battle tank</a> manufacturer in the world. The <a href="/wiki/T-72" title="T-72">T-72</a>, <a href="/wiki/T-90" title="T-90">T-90</a>, and <a href="/wiki/T-14_Armata" title="T-14 Armata">T-14</a> are produced in the city.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> As of 2016 an economic slowdown in Russia had resulted in diminished demand for civilian products such as train cars, but strong demand continued for tanks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT022416_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT022416-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are resources to manufacture medical tools for traumatology at the Nizhny Tagil medical tools plant.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/42nd_Rocket_Division" title="42nd Rocket Division">42nd Rocket Division</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strategic_Rocket_Forces" title="Strategic Rocket Forces">Strategic Rocket Forces</a> is based here,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> equipped with 36 <a href="/wiki/RT-2PM_Topol" title="RT-2PM Topol">Topol</a> nuclear missiles.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expo-Center">Expo-Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Expo-Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nizhny Tagil is one of centers of exhibition activity in the Middle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urals" title="Urals">Urals</a>. Nizhny Tagil Institute of Metals Testing was the host of the international exhibitions such as Ural Expo Arms / <a href="/wiki/Russian_Expo_Arms" title="Russian Expo Arms">Russian Expo Arms</a>, Russian Defense Expo (2001 and 2002).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nizhny Tagil is known for its decorative trays.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Demidov" title="Demidov">Demidovs</a>' initiatives in the area of culture had a favorable influence on the development of Tagil community into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urals" title="Urals">Urals</a>' most important cultural center. In the 19th century, a library and the museum of natural history and antiquity were opened.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The city has a network of 28 libraries servicing 75,000 readers every year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Tagil museums include the old regional history museum, the museum of Fine Arts, and a number of new museums opened in the 1990s: the museum of tray painting art, the museum of lifestyle and handicrafts representing the starting point of a new ethnographic complex.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Demidov Park, a new cultural and historical project, is planned to be built in the city.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Nizhny Tagil has been chosen to host international Urals' Industrial Heritage conferences and workshops.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="during what years? (October 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>City theatrical life is represented by three professional theaters: the National <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D._N._Mamin-Sibiryak" title="D. N. Mamin-Sibiryak">D. N. Mamin-Sibiryak</a> Academic Drama Theater, a puppet theater, community theaters, and the actor department of Nizhny Tagil College of Arts, which has been training actors and actresses for Nizhny Tagil and oblast scenes for two years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="note that FOUR are listed. The college provides amateurs, right? Sentence needs to be re-written (October 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Several Maximum-security prisons surround the town, and most town residents have close connections to them. When prisoners are released from the prisons, they are not given their train fare, and most remain in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizhny_Tagil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rail lines and highways connect the city to the rest of the country.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[ <i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="need to name rail lines, and highways. no airport">(October 2015)"&gt;clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The city is served by the <a href="/wiki/Salka_Airport" title="Salka Airport">Salka Airport</a>, located 17 kilometers (11 mi) northeast of the city. It was a military base until 1994.
