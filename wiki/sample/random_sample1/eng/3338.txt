<p><b>Nizwa</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">نزوى</span>‎‎ Nizwā) is the largest city in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ad_Dakhiliyah_Region" title="Ad Dakhiliyah Region">Ad Dakhiliyah Region</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a> and was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oman_proper" title="Oman proper">Oman proper</a>. Nizwa is about 140 km (1.5 hours) from <a href="/wiki/Muscat,_Oman" title="Muscat, Oman">Muscat</a>. The population is estimated at around 72,000 people including the two areas of Burkat Al Mooz and <a href="/wiki/Jebel_Akhdar_(Oman)" title="Jebel Akhdar (Oman)">Al Jabel Al Akhdar</a>.</p>
<p>Nizwa is one of the oldest cities in Oman and it was once a center of trade, religion, education and art. Its Jama (grand mosque) was formerly a center for <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamic</a> learning. Nizwa acquired its importance because it has been an important meeting point at the base of the Western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hajar_Mountains" title="Hajar Mountains">Hajar Mountains</a>. Set amid a verdant spread of date palms, it is strategically located at the crossroads of routes linking the interior with <a href="/wiki/Muscat,_Oman" title="Muscat, Oman">Muscat</a> and the lower reaches of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dhofar" title="Dhofar">Dhofar</a> thus serving as the link for a large part of the country. Today, Nizwa is a diverse prosperous place with numerous agricultural, historical and recreational aspects. Nizwa is a center for <a href="/wiki/Date_palm" title="Date palm">date</a> growing and is the market place for the area.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historians cannot agree on the origins of the name of the city. Some suggest the name was derived from the Arabic verb (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">انزوا</span>‎‎) which means being alone. Others say that the city was named after an old water spring.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nizwa was the capital of Oman in the 6th and 7th centuries AD. With its deep connection to the root of Islam, Nizwa possesses a number of renowned <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosques</a>, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qaboos_of_Oman" title="Qaboos of Oman">Sultan Qaboos</a> Jama (Friday mosque), So'al Mosque built in the 2nd century AH (9th century AD), Ash-Shawathinah Mosque in Uqr and Ash-Sharja Mosque. There are also Al-Ain Mosque, Ash-Sheikh Mosque and Shuraij Mosque in Tanuf built in 377 AH (around 1000 AD).</p>
<p>In the early 1950s, the large round tower of the ancient fort built in the center of the town was bombed and rocketed by the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>, who were called in to assist the then-reigning <a href="/wiki/Sultan" title="Sultan">Sultan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Said_bin_Taymour" title="Said bin Taymour">Said bin Taymour</a> in suppressing a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revolt" title="Revolt">revolt</a> by leaders of the interior <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imamate_of_Oman" title="Imamate of Oman">Imamate of Oman</a>. The conflict was driven by a struggle for shares in Oman's newly discovered <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a> wealth.</p>
<p>Nizwa has become a more modern city since 1970 under the reign of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qaboos_of_Oman" title="Qaboos of Oman">Sultan Qaboos</a>. Improvements include connections to Muscat via a two-lane highway, which has increased tourism. Communications have been improved to include broadband access, and the city is home to a substantial hospital. It is also a hub for education including a Technical College, College of Applied Sciences, The University of Nizwa, and the training academy for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Oman_Police" title="Royal Oman Police">Royal Oman Police</a>. There are now four hotels and tourism is promoted in the area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mountains surround Nizwa from every side and there are some outstanding mountain scenery close by. Nizwa has an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arid_climate" title="Arid climate">arid climate</a> under <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen's climate classification</a>. In the winter from November until March the climate is pleasant with temperatures as low as 12 C in January. In the summer, the climate is hot and dry with temperatures reaching 45 C in July.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions">Attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main tourist attractions in the city are Nizwa <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort" title="Fort">Fort</a>, the traditional <a href="/wiki/Souq" title="Souq">Souq</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Falaj" title="Falaj">Falaj</a> Daris. In the 1990s, the Jama, the fort and the souq which sit next to each other in the centre were renovated using the same traditional materials. In 1993 Nizwa won the award of 'Organisation of Arab Cities'.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nizwa_Fort">Nizwa Fort</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nizwa Fort">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nizwa_Fort" title="Nizwa Fort">Nizwa Fort</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nizwa8.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Nizwa8.jpg/220px-Nizwa8.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Nizwa8.jpg/330px-Nizwa8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Nizwa8.jpg/440px-Nizwa8.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nizwa8.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Nizwa's enormous fort</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Nizwa fort was built in the 1668 AD by Imam Sultan Bin Saif Al Ya'rubi. It is Oman's most visited national monument. The fort was the administrative seat of authority for the presiding Imams and Walis in times of peace and conflict. The main bulk of the fort took about 12 years to complete and was built above an underground stream. The fort is a reminder of the town's significance through turbulent periods in Oman's long history. It was a formidable stronghold against raiding forces that desired Nizwa's abundant natural wealth and its strategic location at the crossroads of vital routes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nizwa_Souq">Nizwa Souq</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nizwa Souq">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nizwa_(2).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2244" data-file-width="3365" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Nizwa_%282%29.jpg/220px-Nizwa_%282%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Nizwa_%282%29.jpg/330px-Nizwa_%282%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Nizwa_%282%29.jpg/440px-Nizwa_%282%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nizwa_(2).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Nizwa souk</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city, famous for its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Handicrafts" title="Handicrafts">handicrafts</a> and agricultural products, has an expansive souq with an array of products. It is one of the most important in the country besides <a href="/wiki/Muttrah" title="Muttrah">Muttrah</a>. The souq bustles with vendors selling everything from meat, fish, fruits and vegetables to spices, dates, gold and silverware. Nizwa is renowned for its silver jewelry which is considered to be the best in the country. Its people are masters in Khanjar making (curved dagger), recognised for its distinctive style and patterns. They also make copper ware, coffee pots, <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">swords</a>, leather goods and <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">pottery</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Falaj_Daris">Falaj Daris</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Falaj Daris">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Falaj" title="Falaj">Falaj</a> Daris (a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>) is the largest falaj in Oman and is the life maintainer of Nizwa. It provides the surrounding countryside with much needed water for the plantations. Al Ghantuq and Dhoot are two other important falajs in Nizwa. Farming is widely practiced and the town's immense palm farms stretches for eight kilometers along the course of two <a href="/wiki/Wadi" title="Wadi">wadis</a> (Kalbouh and Al Abiadh). Also in practice are red sugar processing and hide tanning.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historically, Nizwa was known for producing <a href="/wiki/Mat" title="Mat">mats</a> from <a href="/wiki/Straw" title="Straw">straw</a>. As of 1920, the city was described as having a "thriving" <a href="/wiki/Metalworking" title="Metalworking">metalworking</a> industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prothero99_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Prothero99-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tanuf">Tanuf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nizwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tanuf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A short drive from Nizwa centre is the old village of Tanuf, known for its seasonal <a href="/wiki/Waterfall" title="Waterfall">waterfalls</a>. Waterfalls in Tanuf are unique to the area within the steep mountain sides and the water reservoir.</p>
