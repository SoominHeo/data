<p><b>Toshihiro Nikai</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">二階 俊博</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nikai Toshihiro</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born February 17, 1939)</span> is a Japanese politician. He was the <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Economy,_Trade_and_Industry_(Japan)" title="Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry (Japan)">Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry</a>. A member of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Japan)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Japan)">Liberal Democratic Party</a> (LDP), Nikai is currently serving in his eighth term in the Lower House representing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakayama" title="Wakayama">Wakayama</a>'s Third District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lineup_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lineup-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nikai was born in <a href="/wiki/Wakayama_Prefecture" title="Wakayama Prefecture">Wakayama Prefecture</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Chuo_University" title="Chuo University">Chuo University</a>, graduating with a <a href="/wiki/Law_degree" title="Law degree">law degree</a> in 1961. He initially worked as secretary for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saburo_Endo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saburo Endo (page does not exist)">Saburo Endo</a>, a Diet member from Shizuoka. After Endo's death, Nikai returned to Wakayama, where he won a seat on the prefectural assembly in 1975. He was elected to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_(Japan)" title="House of Representatives (Japan)">House of Representatives</a> in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lineup_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lineup-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was a member of <a href="/wiki/Noboru_Takeshita" title="Noboru Takeshita">Noboru Takeshita</a>'s faction within the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Japan)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Japan)">Liberal Democratic Party</a>, but left the party in 1993 to join the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Renewal_Party" title="Japan Renewal Party">Japan Renewal Party</a> (<i>Shinseitō</i>). As a member of the JRP, he served as Vice-Minister of Transportation under <a href="/wiki/Morihiro_Hosokawa" title="Morihiro Hosokawa">Morihiro Hosokawa</a> in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lineup_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Lineup-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was later a member of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(Japan,_1998)" title="Liberal Party (Japan, 1998)">Liberal Party</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_Party_of_Japan_(1996)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Conservative Party of Japan (1996) (page does not exist)">Conservative Party</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Conservative_Party" title="New Conservative Party">New Conservative Party</a>, independent parties in coalition with the LDP. As Secretary-General of the NCP and part of the governing coalition, he served as Minister of Transportation under <a href="/wiki/Keiz%C5%8D_Obuchi" title="Keizō Obuchi">Keizō Obuchi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoshir%C5%8D_Mori" title="Yoshirō Mori">Yoshirō Mori</a>. After the NCP merged with the LDP in 2003, Nikai became an LDP member again, and was appointed Director of the General Affairs Bureau in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lineup_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Lineup-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Junichiro_Koizumi" title="Junichiro Koizumi">Junichiro Koizumi</a> tapped Nikai to head the Diet committee in charge of the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japan_Post" title="Japan Post">Japan Post</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Following the general elections of that year, on October 31, Koizumi selected Nikai to head the <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Economy,_Trade_and_Industry_(Japan)" title="Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry (Japan)">Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry</a>, traditionally one of the most highly valued cabinet portfolios.</p>
<p>Later, under Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Yasuo_Fukuda" title="Yasuo Fukuda">Yasuo Fukuda</a>, Nikai was returned to the post of Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry on August 1, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Nikai is known to have strong ties with Chinese leaders and accompanied relief supplies to <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> after the earthquake there in June 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lineup_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Lineup-1">[1]</a></sup> In the Cabinet of Prime Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taro_Aso" title="Taro Aso">Taro Aso</a>, appointed on 24 September 2008, Nikai was retained as Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
