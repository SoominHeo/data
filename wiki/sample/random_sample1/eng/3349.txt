<p><b>Nicholas Robinson-Baker</b> (born 24 June 1987) is an English diver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Robinson-Baker became interested in diving when he attended a boxing camp near <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_National_Sports_Centre" title="Crystal Palace National Sports Centre">Crystal Palace</a> and used the pool there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diving_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-diving-2">[2]</a></sup> He participated in the <a href="/wiki/Diving_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Diving at the 2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>, where he was placed 7th overall in the <a href="/wiki/Diving_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_synchronized_3_metre_springboard" title="Diving at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's synchronized 3 metre springboard">synchronised 3m springboard</a> event with his diving partner <a href="/wiki/Ben_Swain" title="Ben Swain">Ben Swain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He has won gold, silver and bronze medals on the World Series tour,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with a gold in 2009 in 3m synchro with Ben Swain at the World Series leg in Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, he partnered with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Mears_(diver)" title="Chris Mears (diver)">Chris Mears</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Diving_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_synchronized_3_metre_springboard" title="Diving at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's synchronized 3 metre springboard">synchronised 3 metre springboard</a> event and came fifth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They have also won a number of gold medals at National Cup and National Championships as well as a silver at the World Series in Mexico. At the <a href="/wiki/2014_Commonwealth_Games" title="2014 Commonwealth Games">2014 Commonwealth Games</a> he won bronze in the 3m springboard synchro with <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Woodward" title="Freddie Woodward">Freddie Woodward</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>He retired from diving in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He described languages as a life passion, and speaks Russian, Albanian, and Romanian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diving_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-diving-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
