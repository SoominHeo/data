<p><b>January 14</b> is the 14th day of the year in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 351 days remaining until the end of the year (352 in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>). This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Monday, Thursday or Saturday (58 in 400 years each) than on Tuesday or Wednesday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Friday or Sunday (56).</p>
<p>In the 20th and 21st centuries the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a> is 13 days behind the Gregorian calendar, thus January 14 is sometimes celebrated as New Year's Day (<a href="/wiki/Old_New_Year" title="Old New Year">Old New Year</a>) by religious groups who use the Julian calendar.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=January_14&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1301" title="1301">1301</a> – <a href="/wiki/Andrew_III_of_Hungary" title="Andrew III of Hungary">Andrew III of Hungary</a> dies, ending the <a href="/wiki/%C3%81rp%C3%A1d_dynasty" title="Árpád dynasty">Árpád dynasty</a> in Hungary.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1343" title="1343">1343</a> – <a href="/wiki/Arno%C5%A1t_of_Pardubice" title="Arnošt of Pardubice">Arnošt of Pardubice</a> becomes the last <a href="/wiki/List_of_bishops_and_archbishops_of_Prague" title="List of bishops and archbishops of Prague">bishop of Prague</a> and, subsequently, the first Archbishop of Prague.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1539" title="1539">1539</a> – Spain annexes <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1639" title="1639">1639</a> – The "<a href="/wiki/Fundamental_Orders_of_Connecticut" title="Fundamental Orders of Connecticut">Fundamental Orders</a>", the first written <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> that created a <a href="/wiki/Forms_of_government" title="Forms of government">government</a>, is adopted in <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1761" title="1761">1761</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Third_Battle_of_Panipat" title="Third Battle of Panipat">Third Battle of Panipat</a> is fought in India between the <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghans</a> under <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Shah_Durrani" title="Ahmad Shah Durrani">Ahmad Shah Durrani</a> and the Marathas.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1784" title="1784">1784</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ratification_Day_(United_States)" title="Ratification Day (United States)">Ratification Day, United States</a> - Congress ratifies the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1783)" title="Treaty of Paris (1783)">Treaty of Paris</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1814" title="1814">1814</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Kiel" title="Treaty of Kiel">Treaty of Kiel</a>: <a href="/wiki/Frederick_VI_of_Denmark" title="Frederick VI of Denmark">Frederick VI of Denmark</a> cedes Norway to Sweden in return for <a href="/wiki/Pomerania" title="Pomerania">Pomerania</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1822" title="1822">1822</a> – <a href="/wiki/Greek_War_of_Independence" title="Greek War of Independence">Greek War of Independence</a>: <a href="/wiki/Acrocorinth" title="Acrocorinth">Acrocorinth</a> is captured by <a href="/wiki/Theodoros_Kolokotronis" title="Theodoros Kolokotronis">Theodoros Kolokotronis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demetrios_Ypsilantis" title="Demetrios Ypsilantis">Demetrios Ypsilantis</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1858" title="1858">1858</a> – <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_III" title="Napoleon III">Napoleon III</a> of France escapes an assassination attempt.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1907" title="1907">1907</a> – An <a href="/wiki/1907_Kingston_earthquake" title="1907 Kingston earthquake">earthquake in Kingston, Jamaica</a> kills more than 1,000 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1911" title="1911">1911</a> – <a href="/wiki/Roald_Amundsen" title="Roald Amundsen">Roald Amundsen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Amundsen%27s_South_Pole_expedition" title="Amundsen's South Pole expedition">South Pole expedition</a> makes landfall on the eastern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Ross_Ice_Shelf" title="Ross Ice Shelf">Ross Ice Shelf</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1938" title="1938">1938</a> – Norway claims <a href="/wiki/Queen_Maud_Land" title="Queen Maud Land">Queen Maud Land</a> in Antarctica.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a> – <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>: Japan begins <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ke" title="Operation Ke">Operation Ke</a>, the successful operation to evacuate its forces from <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal_Campaign" title="Guadalcanal Campaign">Guadalcanal Campaign</a>.</li>
<li>1943 – World War II: <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> begin the <a href="/wiki/Casablanca_Conference" title="Casablanca Conference">Casablanca Conference</a> to discuss strategy and study the next phase of the war.</li>
<li>1943 – World War II: Franklin D. Roosevelt becomes the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> to travel by <a href="/wiki/Airplane" title="Airplane">airplane</a> while in office when he travels from <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> to <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> to meet with Winston Churchill.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1950" title="1950">1950</a> – The first prototype of the <a href="/wiki/Mikoyan-Gurevich_MiG-17" title="Mikoyan-Gurevich MiG-17">MiG-17</a> makes its maiden flight.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1952" title="1952">1952</a> – NBC's long-running morning news program <i><a href="/wiki/Today_(U.S._TV_program)" title="Today (U.S. TV program)">Today</a></i> debuts, with host <a href="/wiki/Dave_Garroway" title="Dave Garroway">Dave Garroway</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1953" title="1953">1953</a> – <a href="/wiki/Josip_Broz_Tito" title="Josip Broz Tito">Josip Broz Tito</a> is inaugurated as the first <a href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_state_of_Yugoslavia" title="List of heads of state of Yugoslavia">President of Yugoslavia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1954" title="1954">1954</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Motor_Car_Company" title="Hudson Motor Car Company">Hudson Motor Car Company</a> merges with <a href="/wiki/Nash-Kelvinator" title="Nash-Kelvinator">Nash-Kelvinator</a> Corporation forming the <a href="/wiki/American_Motors" title="American Motors">American Motors</a> Corporation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1957" title="1957">1957</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kripalu_Maharaj" title="Kripalu Maharaj">Kripalu Maharaj</a> was named fifth <a href="/wiki/Jagadguru" title="Jagadguru">Jagadguru</a> (world teacher) after giving seven days of speeches before 500 Hindu scholars.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1960" title="1960">1960</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Bank_of_Australia" title="Reserve Bank of Australia">Reserve Bank of Australia</a>, the country's <a href="/wiki/Central_bank" title="Central bank">central bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banknote" title="Banknote">banknote</a> issuing authority, is established.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1967" title="1967">1967</a> – <a href="/wiki/Counterculture_of_the_1960s" title="Counterculture of the 1960s">Counterculture of the 1960s</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Human_Be-In" title="Human Be-In">Human Be-In</a> takes place in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco, California</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Golden_Gate_Park" title="Golden Gate Park">Golden Gate Park</a>, launching the <a href="/wiki/Summer_of_Love" title="Summer of Love">Summer of Love</a>.</li>
