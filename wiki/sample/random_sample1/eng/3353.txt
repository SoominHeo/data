<p>The <b>Nikon 1 J1</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Nikon_1_series" title="Nikon 1 series">Nikon 1 series</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_speed_photography" title="High speed photography">high-speed</a> <a href="/wiki/Mirrorless_interchangeable-lens_camera" title="Mirrorless interchangeable-lens camera">mirrorless interchangeable-lens camera</a> with 1" sensor size launched by <a href="/wiki/Nikon" title="Nikon">Nikon</a> on September 21, 2011. It is a new model that focuses on high-performance, portability and versatility. Nikon lists the estimated selling price of the Nikon 1 J1 One-Lens Kit in the United States at $649.95. Released on October 20, 2011, this kit comes with the 1 NIKKOR VR 10-30mm f/3.5-5.6 lens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nikon_1_J1_home_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nikon_1_J1_home-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>This camera integrates <a href="/wiki/Nikon_1_series#New_technologies" title="Nikon 1 series">many new technologies</a> and is designed for ease of use, sporting only four shooting modes: <a href="/wiki/Image" title="Image">Still Images, Moving Images</a>, Motion Snapshot and Smart Photo Selector and only two buttons for Power, <a href="/wiki/Shutter_(photography)" title="Shutter (photography)">Shutter</a> and Record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-product_brochure_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-product_brochure-4">[4]</a></sup> Nikon 1 J1 has an all-new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nikon_1_mount" title="Nikon 1 mount">Nikon 1 lens mount</a>, which has made lens changes simpler and quicker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Photo_blog_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Photo_blog-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The successor is the <a href="/wiki/Nikon_1_J2" title="Nikon 1 J2">J2</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Taiwan_park.jpg"><img alt="Ugly-ass pineapple" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3872" data-file-width="2592" height="254" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Taiwan_park.jpg/170px-Taiwan_park.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Taiwan_park.jpg/255px-Taiwan_park.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Taiwan_park.jpg/340px-Taiwan_park.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

Photograph taken with Nikon 1 J1</div>

<p>The Nikon 1 J1 uses interchangeable lenses with Nikon 1 mount.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It features a 2.7x lens focal length and 10.1 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pixels" title="Pixels">pixels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The camera has <a href="/wiki/1080p" title="1080p">1080p</a> Full HD movie capability, as well as slow motion movie that films at a speed of 400-1,200fps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> While in movie mode, users can shoot photographs, as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Videos have been tested and show to be sensitive to wind noise and will detect the sound of zoom on the lenses that allow this. While it does not produce the quality of true HD, it is still impressive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_Camera_Review_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Digital_Camera_Review-10">[10]</a></sup> Slow-motion video has lower resolution than the HD option, and the quality is adequate for sites like YouTube.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Photo_blog_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Photo_blog-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motion_Snapshot">Motion Snapshot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Motion Snapshot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the touch of the shutter, the camera takes a high-resolution still, plus a one-second movie vignette. Upon playback, this creates a motion series in slow motion, accompanied by music. This feature can be used to capture <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Landscape" title="Landscape">landscape</a>, photographs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This mode is really only interesting when used on the camera. Once uploaded to a computer, the video plays at normal speed and without the sound produced on the camera. The images and the video are saved as different files, but can be combined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Photo_blog_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Photo_blog-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Auto-focus">Auto-focus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Auto-focus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Nikon 1 J1 boasts very effective <a href="/wiki/Autofocus" title="Autofocus">autofocus</a> system with a 73-point <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Focal_plane" title="Focal plane">focal plane</a>. The autofocus extensively and thoroughly monitors the subject and tracks it to ensure the elements are sharply preserved. It automatically works to improve output in low-light situations, as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Reviewers say that Nikon stands behind its words and users agree that the Nikon 1 J1 has incredible speed when focusing in normal light, but when light levels drop, the speed of the focus differs. While still quicker than other models, it is slower than in good light conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Photo_blog_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Photo_blog-5">[5]</a></sup> The shutter lag time is equal to that of other cameras in the class and while AF acquisition time is slower than other cameras, it is still fairly quick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_Camera_Review_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Digital_Camera_Review-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smart_Photo_Selector">Smart Photo Selector</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Smart Photo Selector">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon pressing the shutter speed, the camera takes 20 shots at 30<a href="/wiki/Frame_rate" title="Frame rate">fps</a>. From those 20, the camera then selects the best 5, and from that reduces that selection to one for a final photo recommendation. The camera selects the best pictures based on criteria such as the subject being out of frame, out of focus, blurred or having its eyes closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This is useful in situations where the user is photographing fast actions and quick moments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Photo_blog_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-Photo_blog-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nikon_EXPEED">Nikon EXPEED</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nikon EXPEED">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EXPEED" title="EXPEED">EXPEED 3</a> is Nikon’s newest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Image_processing_engine" title="Image processing engine">processing engine</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scene_Recognition_Mode">Scene Recognition Mode</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Scene Recognition Mode">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This setting determines which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_digital_camera_modes" title="List of digital camera modes">mode</a> the camera should capture the photograph in. It automatically will choose between portrait, landscape, <a href="/wiki/Close-up" title="Close-up">close-up</a> and night portrait depending on lighting, subject, scenery and closeness. The camera will automatically detect these settings, and choose accordingly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Picture_Control">Picture Control</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Picture Control">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It allows users to retouch photographs in-camera, before uploading them to a computer or printing them. Users can choose from Standard, Neutral, <a href="/wiki/Color_photography" title="Color photography">Vivid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monochrome_photography" title="Monochrome photography">Monochrome</a>, Portrait and Landscape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="D-Lighting">D-Lighting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikon_1_J1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: D-Lighting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Active D-Lighting optimizes images regarding balanced shadows and high-lights. While users are able to adjust <a href="/wiki/Exposure_value" title="Exposure value">exposure settings</a>, the camera will help to correct the images or can work on its own to correct images itself. Without D-lighting, most cameras will determine lighting based on one section of the photo, which can drastically distort lighting and make the photograph appear dark and lifeless. With Active D-lighting, the Nikon 1 J1 corrects the exposure based on lighting in the entire frame and creates more lively and vivid images.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>Since its release in October 2011, there have been many reviews of the Nikon 1 J1. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PC_World_(magazine)" title="PC World (magazine)">PCWorld</a> gave the camera a positive review mentioning only minor drawbacks, such as the camera not coming with a user manual and not having external <a href="/wiki/Flash_(photography)" title="Flash (photography)">flash</a> (although cameras exported since February 2012 now come with a User Manual on both print and CD as standard). When compared to photographs from a <a href="/wiki/Nikon_D3" title="Nikon D3">Nikon D3</a>, the J1 held its own, exhibiting perfect exposure and vibrant colors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PCWorld_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-PCWorld-17">[17]</a></sup> They also state the flash does not live up to expected standards and users need to increase the intensity when using it from a distance. The lens also locks easily and can become a hassle when trying to smoothly take photographs, but is fine once the user gets the hang of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PCWorld_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-PCWorld-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Photography blog has reviewed the camera and has generally good things to say. They do note the peculiar layout of the dials and buttons on the back of the camera. They offer that the dial should have more options on it, rather than forcing the user to delve into menus to get to settings that would normally appear on the setting dial. They also point out that because the lenses for this camera do not have manual focusing rings, the manual focus that the camera does have is rudimentary at best. According to the review, it lags in wake-up time from sleep mode, picture review right after taking a picture is too long and its lack of ports make it uncompatible with other Nikon 1 accessories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Photo_blog_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-Photo_blog-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/TechRadar" title="TechRadar">TechRadar.com</a> mentioned that the 10 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megapixel" title="Megapixel">megapixel</a> camera was slightly lagging because of its boundaries on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sensor_resolution" title="Sensor resolution">sensor resolution</a>. Nikon believes that 12 megapixels is enough for its full-frame cameras, making the 10 megapixel appeal of this camera a slight drawback when considering the potential of this camera.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tech_radar_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-tech_radar-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Digital Camera Review says that the camera is good overall, but not a must-have for enthusiasts and Nikon fans. In the article it is stated that the J1 and V1 are not meant to replace <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DSLR" title="DSLR">DSLRs</a> but are meant for users who wish to take pictures at any moment without having to resort to many lenses and a large-bodied DSLR. The review advises users to have a spare battery on hand for a full day of shooting and be wary when the <a href="/wiki/Battery_indicator" title="Battery indicator">battery indicator</a> gets to a single segment. They also mention that cost and lack of viewfinder is a major drawback for this camera.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_Camera_Review_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Digital_Camera_Review-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
