<p><b>Nicole Hassler</b> (1 June 1941 – 19 November 1996) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating" title="Figure skating">figure skater</a>. She was the 1963 <a href="/wiki/World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="World Figure Skating Championships">World bronze medalist</a>, the 1963 <a href="/wiki/European_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="European Figure Skating Championships">European silver medalist</a> and the 1964-1966 European bronze medalist. She represented <a href="/wiki/France_at_the_1960_Winter_Olympics" title="France at the 1960 Winter Olympics">France at the 1960 Winter Olympics</a>, where she placed 11th and <a href="/wiki/France_at_the_1964_Winter_Olympics" title="France at the 1964 Winter Olympics">at the 1964 Winter Olympics</a>, where she placed 4th.</p>
<p>She was born in <a href="/wiki/Chamonix" title="Chamonix">Chamonix</a> and was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Hassler" title="Albert Hassler">Albert Hassler</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
