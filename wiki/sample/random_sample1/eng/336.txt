<p><b>January 18</b> is the 18th day of the year in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 347 days remaining until the end of the year (348 in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>). This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Monday, Wednesday or Friday (58 in 400 years each) than on Saturday or Sunday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Tuesday or Thursday (56).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=January_18&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/350" title="350">350</a> – General <a href="/wiki/Magnentius" title="Magnentius">Magnentius</a> deposes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Emperor" title="Roman Emperor">Roman Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Constans" title="Constans">Constans</a> and proclaims himself Emperor.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/474" title="474">474</a> – Seven-year-old <a href="/wiki/Leo_II_(emperor)" title="Leo II (emperor)">Leo II</a> succeeds his maternal grandfather <a href="/wiki/Leo_I_the_Thracian" title="Leo I the Thracian">Leo I</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine_emperor" title="Byzantine emperor">Byzantine emperor</a>. He dies ten months later.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/532" title="532">532</a> – <a href="/wiki/Nika_riots" title="Nika riots">Nika riots</a> in <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> fail.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1126" title="1126">1126</a> – <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Huizong_of_Song" title="Emperor Huizong of Song">Emperor Huizong</a> abdicates the Chinese throne in favour of his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Qinzong_of_Song" title="Emperor Qinzong of Song">Emperor Qinzong</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1486" title="1486">1486</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Henry_VII_of_England" title="Henry VII of England">Henry VII of England</a> marries <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_of_York" title="Elizabeth of York">Elizabeth of York</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward IV</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1535" title="1535">1535</a> – Spanish conquistador <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pizarro" title="Francisco Pizarro">Francisco Pizarro</a> <a href="/wiki/History_of_Lima" title="History of Lima">founds Lima</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1562" title="1562">1562</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_IV" title="Pope Pius IV">Pope Pius IV</a> reopens the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Trent" title="Council of Trent">Council of Trent</a> for its third and final session.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1591" title="1591">1591</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Naresuan" title="Naresuan">Naresuan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ayutthaya_Kingdom" title="Ayutthaya Kingdom">Siam</a> kills Crown Prince <a href="/wiki/Mingyi_Swa" title="Mingyi Swa">Mingyi Swa</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a> in <a href="/wiki/Single_combat" title="Single combat">single combat</a>, for which this date is now observed as <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_Armed_Forces" title="Royal Thai Armed Forces">Royal Thai Armed Forces</a> day.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1670" title="1670">1670</a> – <a href="/wiki/Henry_Morgan" title="Henry Morgan">Henry Morgan</a> captures <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1701" title="1701">1701</a> – <a href="/wiki/Frederick_I_of_Prussia" title="Frederick I of Prussia">Frederick I</a> crowns himself King of <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a> in <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg" title="Königsberg">Königsberg</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1778" title="1778">1778</a> – <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> is the first known European to discover the <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Islands" title="Hawaiian Islands">Hawaiian Islands</a>, which he names the "Sandwich Islands".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1788" title="1788">1788</a> – The first elements of the <a href="/wiki/First_Fleet" title="First Fleet">First Fleet</a> carrying 736 convicts from <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a> to Australia arrive at <a href="/wiki/Botany_Bay" title="Botany Bay">Botany Bay</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1866" title="1866">1866</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wesley_College,_Melbourne" title="Wesley College, Melbourne">Wesley College, Melbourne</a>, is established.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1871" title="1871">1871</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wilhelm_I_of_Germany" title="Wilhelm I of Germany">Wilhelm I of Germany</a> is proclaimed <i>Kaiser Wilhelm</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Hall_of_Mirrors" title="Hall of Mirrors">Hall of Mirrors</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Versailles" title="Palace of Versailles">Palace of Versailles</a> (France) towards the end of the <a href="/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War" title="Franco-Prussian War">Franco-Prussian War</a>. Wilhelm already had the title of <a href="/wiki/German_Emperor" title="German Emperor">German Emperor</a> since the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_German_Confederation_1871" title="Constitution of the German Confederation 1871">constitution of 1 January 1871</a>, but he had hesitated to accept the title.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1884" title="1884">1884</a> – Dr. <a href="/wiki/William_Price_(physician)" title="William Price (physician)">William Price</a> attempts to <a href="/wiki/Cremation" title="Cremation">cremate</a> the body of his infant son, Jesus Christ Price, setting a legal precedent for cremation in the United Kingdom.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1886" title="1886">1886</a> – Modern <a href="/wiki/Field_hockey" title="Field hockey">hockey</a> is born with the formation of The Hockey Association in England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1896" title="1896">1896</a> – An <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">X-ray</a> <a href="/wiki/X-ray_generator" title="X-ray generator">generating machine</a> is exhibited for the first time by H. L. Smith.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1903" title="1903">1903</a> – A transmitter in <a href="/wiki/Wellfleet,_Massachusetts" title="Wellfleet, Massachusetts">Wellfleet, Massachusetts</a> sends the <a href="/wiki/Invention_of_radio" title="Invention of radio">first transatlantic radio transmission</a> to originate in the United States.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Plaque at site says 19 Jan; 'sources vary'? (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1911" title="1911">1911</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eugene_B._Ely" title="Eugene B. Ely">Eugene B. Ely</a> lands on the deck of the <a href="/wiki/USS_Pennsylvania_(ACR-4)" title="USS Pennsylvania (ACR-4)">USS <i>Pennsylvania</i></a> stationed in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a>, the first time an aircraft landed on a ship.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1913" title="1913">1913</a> – <a href="/wiki/First_Balkan_War" title="First Balkan War">First Balkan War</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Navy" title="Hellenic Navy">Greek</a> flotilla defeats the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Navy" title="Ottoman Navy">Ottoman Navy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lemnos_(1913)" title="Battle of Lemnos (1913)">Naval Battle of Lemnos</a>, securing the islands of the Northern <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a> for Greece.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1915" title="1915">1915</a> – Japan issues the "<a href="/wiki/Twenty-One_Demands" title="Twenty-One Demands">Twenty-One Demands</a>" to the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%9349)" title="Republic of China (1912–49)">Republic of China</a> in a bid to increase its power in East Asia.</li>
