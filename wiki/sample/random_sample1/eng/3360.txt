<p><b>Nicolas Alexis Julio Nkoulou Ndoubena</b> (born 27 March 1990), commonly referred to as <b>Nicolas Nkoulou</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroonian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for French <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> club <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicolas_Nkoulou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AS_Monaco">AS Monaco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicolas_Nkoulou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: AS Monaco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nkoulou began his career at <a href="/wiki/Kadji_Sports_Academy" title="Kadji Sports Academy">Kadji Sports Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Douala" title="Douala">Douala</a>, the same academy that produced the likes of <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Eto%27o" title="Samuel Eto'o">Samuel Eto'o</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Idriss_Carlos_Kameni" title="Idriss Carlos Kameni">Carlos Kameni</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Djemba-Djemba" title="Eric Djemba-Djemba">Eric Djemba-Djemba</a>. In late 2007, it was announced that he was acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Principality" title="Principality">principality</a>-based side <a href="/wiki/AS_Monaco_FC" title="AS Monaco FC">AS Monaco</a>. He agreed to a three-year professional contract with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He spent the latter portion of the 2007–08 season playing with Monaco's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Championnat_de_France_Amateurs" title="Championnat de France Amateurs">CFA</a> squad, before joining the squad for preseason in July 2008, where he was handed the number 3 kit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>NKoulou made two appearances during the pre-season starting their opening friendly match against fellow Ligue 1 side <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_FC" title="Toulouse FC">Toulouse</a> and also starting against Croatian side <a href="/wiki/NK_Zagreb" title="NK Zagreb">NK Zagreb</a>. He made his professional debut on 13 September 2008 in a 2–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Lorient" title="FC Lorient">Lorient</a> starting the match before being substituted in the 65th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Though he made 24 appearance in the 2008–09 season,</p>
<p>The 2009–10 season saw NKoulou to play in defensive midfielder for the most of the season under the new management of <a href="/wiki/Guy_Lacombe" title="Guy Lacombe">Guy Lacombe</a> and newly signing, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Puygrenier" title="Sébastien Puygrenier">Sébastien Puygrenier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As the 2009–10 season progressed, NKoulou signed a new contract with the club, keeping him until 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and was then reverted back to central defence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> NKoulou also played in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Coupe_de_France_Final" title="2010 Coupe de France Final">Coupe de France Final</a>, coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Costa" title="Eduardo Costa">Eduardo Costa</a> in the 111th minutes, in a 1–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">PSG</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and went on to make 23 appearance in the 2009–10 season despite being committed to the African Cup of Nation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2010–11 season, NKoulou found himself in the transfer speculation and ended up staying at the club despite fallen out with Lacombe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> NKoulou continued remain in the first team, playing in the defensive midfielder position for the most of the season and central defence. Despite suffering from injuries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and suspension,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> NKoulou finished the 2010–11 season, making 30 appearance. However, following their relegation to <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a>, NKoulou was among key players to be expected to leave the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>His performances with Monaco have led to him being scouted by several prominent <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> clubs. <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> clubs <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> sides <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sevilla_F.C." title="Sevilla F.C.">Sevilla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> champions <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Olympique Lyonnais</a> have all declared their interests in the Cameroonian defender.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> During his time at AS Monaco, NKoulou made 78 appearance and has started every match since playing the full 90 minutes in them all. He has yet to score a goal.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympique_Marseille">Olympique Marseille</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicolas_Nkoulou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Olympique Marseille">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>On 29 June 2011, NKoulou passed his medical and signed for four years with <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a> for worth €3.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Marseille and Lyon found themselves competing over their interest signing NKoulou before he chose Marseille.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>After missing two matches, due to suspension he received while at Monaco,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> NKoulou made his Marseille debut on 14 August 2011, in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/AJ_Auxerre" title="AJ Auxerre">Auxerre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In the next match against <a href="/wiki/AS_Saint-%C3%89tienne" title="AS Saint-Étienne">Saint Étienne</a> on 21 August 2011, Nkoulou was involved in an incident with <a href="/wiki/Florent_Sinama_Pongolle" title="Florent Sinama Pongolle">Florent Sinama Pongolle</a>. But following the reviewing by the Disciplinary Committee of the LFP, he was received a three match ban.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> While serving his suspension in the league, he made his Champions League debut on Matchday 1 of the Group–stage, helping a club kept a clean sheet, in a 1–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos</a> on 13 September 2011 and went on to play ten Champions League Matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Eight days later on 21 September 2011, Nkolou made his return to the first team, in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Evian_Thonon_Gaillard_F.C." title="Evian Thonon Gaillard F.C.">Evian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> His performance throughout October saw him earned the club’s player of the month of October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Nkolou continued to remain in the first team for the 2011–12 season despite another suspension (which he got a two match ban against <a href="/wiki/Dijon_FCO" title="Dijon FCO">Dijon</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>) and a knee injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Despite this, Nkoulou helped the club win against Lyon 1–0 <a href="/wiki/2012_Coupe_de_la_Ligue_Final" title="2012 Coupe de la Ligue Final">in the final of Coupe de la Ligue</a>, winning the cup for their first piece of silverware since 1981, as well as, being named Man of the Match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> After finishing the 2011–12 appearance, making 46 appearance in all competition, Nkoulou was named <a href="/wiki/Troph%C3%A9es_UNFP_du_football" title="Trophées UNFP du football">Trophées UNFP du football</a>'s Team of the Year of <a href="/wiki/Troph%C3%A9es_UNFP_du_football#2012" title="Trophées UNFP du football">2012</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Prix_Marc-Vivien_Fo%C3%A9#2012" title="Prix Marc-Vivien Foé">Prix Marc-Vivien Foé Award</a>, but finished as runner–up to <a href="/wiki/Youn%C3%A8s_Belhanda" title="Younès Belhanda">Younès Belhanda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Nevertheless, Nkoulou was awarded 2011–12 Olympique de Marseille Player of the Season.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In the 2012–13 season saw Nkoulou found himself in the transfer speculation when the club began to suffer financial trouble, but stayed at the club<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and insisting on honouring his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Nkoulou was ever–present in the 2012–13 season, having played all the match in the league and scored his first goal for the club, in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Troyes_AC" title="Troyes AC">Troyes</a> on 3 March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> During the season, Nkoulou suffered a knee ligament injury and was sidelined for weeks, but managed to return to the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> At the end of the 2012–13 season, Nkoulou was named <a href="/wiki/Troph%C3%A9es_UNFP_du_football" title="Trophées UNFP du football">Trophées UNFP du football</a>'s Team of the Year of <a href="/wiki/Troph%C3%A9es_UNFP_du_football#2012" title="Trophées UNFP du football">2013</a> for the second time running.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the 2013–14 season, Nkoulou stated he is prepared to leave Marseille in the summer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> prompting attracted interests from his former club, Monaco,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> who were just promoted back to Ligue 1, and Arsenal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> After the club set a higher price tag on him,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Nkoulou remained at the club and started a bad start when he conceded a goal, in a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/En_Avant_de_Guingamp" title="En Avant de Guingamp">Guingamp</a> in the opening game of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Despite this, Nkoulou redeem himself soon after to continouslly remain in the first team under the new management of <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lie_Baup" title="Élie Baup">Élie Baup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On 4 January 2014, Nkoulou extended his contract with Marseille, keeping him until 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> As the 2013–14 season progressed, Nkoulou suffered a knee injury, which kept him sidelined for one match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> After making his return, Nkoulou went on to score two goals later in the season against Saint Étienne<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Sochaux-Montb%C3%A9liard" title="FC Sochaux-Montbéliard">Sochaux</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Nkoulou later finished the 2013–14 season, making 46 appearance and scoring two times in all competition.</p>
<p>Ahead of the 2014–15 season, the club were keen on selling Nkoulou,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> but following a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/F.C._Bari_1908" title="F.C. Bari 1908">Bari</a>, it appeared hinted that he wanted to stay at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> His good performance against Guingamp on 23 August 2014 earned him Team of the Week on Matchday 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Then on 19 October 2014, Nkoulou scored his first goal of the season, in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_FC" title="Toulouse FC">Toulouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Two weeks later, on 2 November 2014, Nkoulou scored again, in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/RC_Lens" title="RC Lens">Lens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> However, as the 2014–15 season, his appearance was soon restricted to 26 appearance in competition, due to injuries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> and international commitment with Cameroon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> After two months without playing, Nkoulou made his first team return on 12 April 2015, in a 1–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/FC_Girondins_de_Bordeaux" title="FC Girondins de Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> and went on to help the finished the season in the 4th place at two points of a qualification in the Champions League.</p>
<p>Ahead of the 2015–16 season, Nkoulou’s contract set to expire and haven’t renewed his contract,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> though his future remained undecided.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> In the 2015–16 season, Nkoulou made his first appearance of the season, having missed through opening game of the season, in a 1–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Stade_de_Reims" title="Stade de Reims">Reims</a> on 16 August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In a match against <a href="/wiki/Lille_OSC" title="Lille OSC">Lille</a> on 29 January 2016, Nkoulou captained Marseille for the first time following <a href="/wiki/Steve_Mandanda" title="Steve Mandanda">Steve Mandanda</a>’s absent, which they drew 1–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> In a match against Saint-Étienne on 21 February 2016, Nkoulou received a straight red card in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> In addition to his disciplinary issues, Nkoulou was suspended three times in every <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Coupe_de_France" title="2015–16 Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> matches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Nkoulou started in the <a href="/wiki/2016_Coupe_de_France_Final" title="2016 Coupe de France Final">Coupe de France Final</a>, which they lost 4–2 against PSG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> At the end of the 2015–16 season, the club faced a difficult season with three different managers during the season and finished the league in thirteenth place and finished the season, making 41 appearance in all competition.</p>
<p>With his contract expiring at the end of the 2015–16 season, Nkoulou attracted interests around clubs, such as, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF" title="Málaga CF">Málaga</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS_Lazio" title="SS Lazio">Lazio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> However, on 27 June 2016, Nkoulou began talks with a move to stay in France by joining Lyon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympique_Lyon">Olympique Lyon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicolas_Nkoulou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Olympique Lyon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After spending five years at Marseille, with his contract at the club expired, Nkoulou moved to <a href="/wiki/Choc_des_Olympiques" title="Choc des Olympiques">rivals</a>’ <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyon" title="Olympique Lyon">Lyon</a> on a four–year contract on 29 June 2016; coincidentally was the day he joined Marseille.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Upon joining the club, Nkoulou stated he joined Lyon to achieve greater things and was given number 3 shirt, which he quoted: ‘’“The No. 3? It is a wink to those close to me, because I am the youngest of three children.”’’<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nkoulou played his first official match for Lyon at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Troph%C3%A9e_des_Champions" title="2016 Trophée des Champions">Trophée des Champions</a>, making his first start, in a 4–1 loss against Paris Saint–Germain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> A week later, he made his league debut for the club, in the opening game of the season, in a 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/AS_Nancy" title="AS Nancy">Nancy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> In his first match against Marseille for the first time, which saw them drew 0–0 on 18 September 2016, Nkoulou received boos from Marseille supporters during the match every time he touched the ball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> Around the same time he was competing with <a href="/wiki/Mouctar_Diakhaby" title="Mouctar Diakhaby">Mouctar Diakhaby</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> Nkoulou’s performance soon came under criticism by observers, prompting Manager <a href="/wiki/Bruno_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9sio" title="Bruno Génésio">Bruno Génésio</a> defended him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicolas_Nkoulou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>NKoulou was included in the <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon U-23</a> squad that participated in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>. He helped the squad reach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_-_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics - Men's tournament">the knockout round</a>, where they were eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>.</p>

<p>NKoulou was called up by the <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">national team</a> for the first time on 4 November 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> He would make his international debut in the senior squad on 19 November 2008 in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a>, which they lost 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<p>NKoulou selected for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2010 African Cup of Nations">2010 African Cup of Nations</a> by Cameroon National coach <a href="/wiki/Paul_Le_Guen" title="Paul Le Guen">Paul Le Guen</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> and went on to play four times in the cup competitions. Later in 2010, N’Koulou was called up again for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> and made his World Cup debut, making his first start and playing 90 minutes, in a 1–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> N’Koulou played all three matches in the World Cup, as Cameroon were eliminated in the Group Stage.</p>
<p>After captain Samuel Eto'o and vice–captain <a href="/wiki/Eyong_Enoh" title="Eyong Enoh">Eyong Enoh</a> were suspended for five and two matches respectively by Cameroon Football Federation for their action of boycotting a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algeria</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> Nkoulou was appointed as captain of Cameroon on a temporary basis and captained his first national match, in a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau_national_football_team" title="Guinea-Bissau national football team">Guinea-Bissau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following Eto’o refused to return to the national squad in late-August 2012, The Cameroon Football Federation stripped him of his captaincy in response and Nkoulou was appointed as a new captain of Cameroon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>, Nkoulou was among 23 players to be included for the squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> Nkoulou played all three matches and retained his captaincy, as Cameroon struggled in the World Cup after losing three matches that saw them eliminated in the World Cup. The following year, Nkoulou was called up into the squad by the national team for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2015 Africa Cup of Nations">African Cup of Nations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two years later on 26 March 2016, Nkoulou scored his first international goal, in a 2–2 draw against South Africa, the team he made his national debut eight years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicolas_Nkoulou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nkoulou said when not playing football, he enjoy listening to music and that he doesn’t drink alcohol, even he’s with friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nkoulou is friends with his national and then Marseille team–mate, <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phane_Mbia" title="Stéphane Mbia">Stéphane Mbia</a>, who considered him as a brother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></p>
