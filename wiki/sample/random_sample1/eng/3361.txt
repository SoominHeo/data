<p><b>Nicola Pavarini</b> (born 24 February 1974 in <a href="/wiki/Brescia" title="Brescia">Brescia</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>, who last played as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma F.C.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Pavarini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pavarini started his career at <a href="/wiki/Brescia_Calcio" title="Brescia Calcio">Brescia</a>. After he made his first team debut in the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_Serie_B" title="1996–97 Serie B">1996–97 Serie B</a> season, he was loaned to Serie C1 clubs <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.S._Gualdo" title="S.S. Gualdo">Gualdo</a> and <a href="/wiki/A.C._Cesena" title="A.C. Cesena">Cesena</a>.</p>
<p>In summer 2001, he left for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Acireale" title="A.S. Acireale">Acireale</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C2" title="Serie C2">Serie C2</a>, where his started to play regularly in the second season. in summer 2003 he moved back to Serie B for <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Marco_Amelia" title="Marco Amelia">Marco Amelia</a> who left Livorno on loan.</p>
<p>Pavarini was signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reggina_Calcio" title="Reggina Calcio">Reggina</a> in summer 2004, along with new signing <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Salvatore_Soviero&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Salvatore Soviero (page does not exist)">Salvatore Soviero</a> to compete the first choice. In 2005–06 season, he became new signing <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Pelizzoli" title="Ivan Pelizzoli">Ivan Pelizzoli</a> backup. But due to his injury, he managed to play 18 Serie A games.</p>
<p>In June 2006, he was signed by another Seire A club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Siena" title="A.C. Siena">Siena</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Mirante" title="Antonio Mirante">Antonio Mirante</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marco_Fortin" title="Marco Fortin">Marco Fortin</a>. But he played as Austrian international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Manninger" title="Alexander Manninger">Alexander Manninger</a>'s backup. In January 2007, he was exchanged with <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Benussi" title="Francesco Benussi">Francesco Benussi</a> of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Lecce" title="U.S. Lecce">U.S. Lecce</a>, joining the Serie B team on loan.</p>
<p>In August 2007, he joined Parma and became first choice after the 2008 departure of <a href="/wiki/Luca_Bucci" title="Luca Bucci">Luca Bucci</a>. He won promotion back to Serie A in the 2008–09, but lost his place in the team to new signing <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Mirante" title="Antonio Mirante">Antonio Mirante</a> in the summer of 2009 and made just five league appearances in the following two seasons. After injuries to Mirante in 2011–12, Pavarini made 10 league appearances.</p>
<p>On 15 May 2014, Pavarini announced his retirement at the end of the season after seven years at Parma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
