<p><b>Nicola Pozzi</b> (born 30 June 1986) is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who currently plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Pozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Milan">Milan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Pozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Milan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Signed by <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> from then-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> side <a href="/wiki/A.C._Cesena" title="A.C. Cesena">Cesena</a> in January 2004, he was successively loaned to <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pescara_Calcio" title="Pescara Calcio">Pescara</a>, before being sent on loan to <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli F.C.</a> for two consecutive seasons (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005-06_Serie_A" title="2005-06 Serie A">2005–06</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006-07_Serie_A" title="2006-07 Serie A">2006–07</a>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Empoli">Empoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Pozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Empoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007–08 season, his status was changed to co-owned by both sides, for €750,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (that season Empoli also signed <a href="/wiki/Luca_Antonini" title="Luca Antonini">Luca Antonini</a> (€1M), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lino_Marzoratti" title="Lino Marzoratti">Lino Marzoratti</a> (€0.75M) and <a href="/wiki/Ignazio_Abate" title="Ignazio Abate">Ignazio Abate</a> (€0.9M) in co-ownership deals), and on 16 June 2008 Empoli got full ownership for €3.25 million (Pozzi) and €1.5 million (Marzoratti )<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Empoli2008bilancio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Empoli2008bilancio-3">[3]</a></sup> in exchange of Antonini (€2.75 million)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Empoli2008bilancio_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Empoli2008bilancio-3">[3]</a></sup> and Abate (€2 million)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Empoli2008bilancio_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Empoli2008bilancio-3">[3]</a></sup> who return to AC Milan, effectively made Emppoli paid €3.4 million in net for Pozzi and Marzoratti, as well as the special loan of Abate and Antonini.</p>
<p>On 9 December 2007, he scored all four goals in Empoli's 4–1 victory over Cagliari. However, he suffered a season-ending knee injury after netting a brace in a 3–1 defeat of Napoli on February 17, and will be sidelined for six months, effectively ruling him out of the <i>Azzurrini</i> squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Olympics" title="2008 Olympics">2008 Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>Pozzi also represented Italy at the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Under-21 level</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sampdoria">Sampdoria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Pozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sampdoria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Monday 31 August 2009 <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> announced via their website the loan signing of Pozzi for €180,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Empoli2010bilancio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Empoli2010bilancio-4">[4]</a></sup> with the option to make the signing permanent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In June 2010, Sampdoria decided to sign him outright,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> for €5.2 million in a 5-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Empoli2010bilancio_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Empoli2010bilancio-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parma">Parma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Pozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Parma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After playing five seasons well at Sampdoria, Pozzi moved to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a> on 31 January 2014, with <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Okaka" title="Stefano Okaka">Stefano Okaka</a> moved to opposite direction,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">signing a three-and-a-half year deal.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The deal was a cashless swap, as both Pozzi and Okaka were valued for €2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 February 2015 Pozzi joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Chievo_Verona" title="A.C. Chievo Verona">A.C. Chievo Verona</a> in a temporary deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vicenza">Vicenza</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Pozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Vicenza">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 August 2015 he was signed by <a href="/wiki/Vicenza_Calcio" title="Vicenza Calcio">Vicenza</a> in a 2-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He took no.9 shirt from <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Pettinari" title="Stefano Pettinari">Stefano Pettinari</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
