<p><b>Nicolás Federico Spolli</b> (<small>locally: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[eʔˈpoli]</a></span>, <small>Italian: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[ˈspɔlli]</a></span>; born 20 February 1983 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosario" title="Rosario">Rosario</a>) is an Argentine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defender_(football)" title="Defender (football)">defender</a> for Italian club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Chievo_Verona" title="A.C. Chievo Verona">Chievo</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicol%C3%A1s_Spolli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newell.27s_Old_Boys">Newell's Old Boys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicol%C3%A1s_Spolli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Newell's Old Boys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Spolli began his youth career with hometown club, <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's Old Boys</a>. In 2005, he was promoted to the senior squad. At the club he was soon a regular in the starting eleven, and by 2009, he made over 100 appearances and also hit the back of the net nearly ten times. He was a fan favourite as well.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Calcio_Catania">Calcio Catania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicol%C3%A1s_Spolli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Calcio Catania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2009, <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Calcio Catania</a> sporting director, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pietro_Lo_Monaco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pietro Lo Monaco (page does not exist)">Pietro Lo Monaco</a> confirmed that the <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicilian</a> club were deep into negotiations with Newell's for the defender and on 25 July 2009, the deal was finalized and Spolli officially became a Catania player. He was Catania's ninth transfer of the 2009 summer transfer window, and proved himself as an integral part of Catania's starting eleven, early in his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> career. The player made 26 Serie A appearances in his first season, netting one goal, helping <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a> to an impressive 12th-place finish in the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> and also a record points total for the club in a top flight season. In his next two seasons with the club, Spolli and his team continued their recent success and endured three additional seasons of record point totals in Serie A, making it five consecutive seasons of this achievement.</p>
<p>Spolli is currently part of a record-breaking Catania outfit that picked up 56 points from 38 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012-13_Serie_A" title="2012-13 Serie A">Serie A</a> matches. This performance saw the club also break its record number of home victories in a single season, its record number of victories overall in a single top flight campaign, as well as its record points total in Serie A for the fifth consecutive season.</p>
