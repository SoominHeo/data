<p><b>Nikolay Mihaylov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Николай Михайлов</span>; born 28 June 1988) is a <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who currently plays as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> for Turkish side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mersin_%C4%B0dmanyurdu_SK" title="Mersin İdmanyurdu SK">Mersin İdmanyurdu</a> and for the <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgarian national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Mihaylov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mihaylov's father is former goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Borislav_Mikhailov" title="Borislav Mikhailov">Borislav Mihaylov</a>, the player with the most international appearances for Bulgaria (after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stilyan_Petrov" title="Stilyan Petrov">Stilyan Petrov</a>) and head of the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Football_Union" title="Bulgarian Football Union">Bulgarian Football Union</a>, and his grandfather was Biser Mihaylov, who also played in goal for Levski in the 1960s. <a href="/wiki/Maria_Petrova_(rhythmic_gymnast)" title="Maria Petrova (rhythmic gymnast)">Maria Petrova</a> a prominent world champion in rhythmic gymnastics has been his stepmother since 1998.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Mihaylov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Levski_Sofia">Levski Sofia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Mihaylov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Levski Sofia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Talented Mihaylov started as a youngster with home country top team Levski Sofia, making his full 90 minutes debut at the age of just 17 years old in the UEFA cup match against Auxerre. He nevertheless wasn't popular with their fans. Mikhaylov's poor performances in some matches, particularly his blunder in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> game against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Werder_Bremen" title="Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a> on 31 October 2006, provoked a negative campaign among Levski supporters who associated his career with protection from his influential father.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Mihaylov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2007, he almost joined <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">ACF Fiorentina</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> but instead transferred to Liverpool on a three-year contract, with an option for a further two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liverpool_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-liverpool-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Mihaylov was acquired by Liverpool as a talent, whom might become their first team keeper in the future. As at that time Liverpool's first choice keeper was <a href="/wiki/Pepe_Reina" title="Pepe Reina">Pepe Reina</a> with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Itandje" title="Charles Itandje">Charles Itandje</a> as back up. And by 14 July it became clear that the transfer had hit a problem; Mihaylov wouldn't qualify for a British work permit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As a consequence the Bulgarian U-21 International joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> on a one-year loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At FC Twente is the 19-year-old Mihaylov to compete with <a href="/wiki/Sander_Boschker" title="Sander Boschker">Sander Boschker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cees_Paauwe" title="Cees Paauwe">Cees Paauwe</a> for first team action. Although not playing any matches for the first team, Twente did recognize his talent and in 2008 they extended his loan with a year to August 2009.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Twente">Twente</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Mihaylov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Twente">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 August 2008 finally made his debut for <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> in their 4–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Emirates_Stadium" title="Emirates Stadium">Emirates Stadium</a> in the second leg of the third qualifying round for the UEFA Champions League and went on to make his <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> debut in a 1–1 draw at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roda_JC" title="Roda JC">Roda</a> on 30 August 2008. On 14 March 2009, Mihaylov made his second appearance for Twente in the Dutch league against Willem II, playing the full 90 minutes and earning himself a booking, managing to keep a clean sheet in Twente's 2–0 away win. On 29 July 2009, Mihaylov came on as a substitute for midfielder <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Perez" title="Kenneth Perez">Kenneth Perez</a>, following the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_card#Red_card" title="Penalty card">dismissal</a> of starting keeper <a href="/wiki/Sander_Boschker" title="Sander Boschker">Sander Boschker</a> in the 27th minute of Twente's away game against <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting CP</a> in the first leg of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> third qualifying round. He stopped the penalty taken by <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Moutinho" title="João Moutinho">João Moutinho</a> and made a number of key saves to keep the scores level at 0–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Mihaylov played the full 90 minutes in the return leg and was not at fault for the <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> side's <a href="/wiki/Last-minute_goal" title="Last-minute goal">last-minute</a> equalizing goal, which allowed <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting CP</a> to advance on the <a href="/wiki/Away_goals_rule" title="Away goals rule">away goals rule</a>.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> had become impressed with Mihaylov, not only his talent, but also his progress did convince them. And after several years of being loaned out to <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a>, on 5 February 2010 he officially was transferred for a fee of €1.8m from <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> to <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">Twente</a>. He signed a contract for three years with the Dutch club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the 2010–11 season, he has established himself as the starting goalkeeper for <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a>. Having played in the Champions League, Mihaylov impressed with great appearances in the competition that season, among others by stopping a penalty-shot. In the Champions League FC Twente drew to play against his "nightmare" club <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a>, as it were mentioned by the Bulgarian media. He only conceded one goal in the two matches played against Werder. With further appearances against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur" title="Tottenham Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</a> and the previous year's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a> in the group, Twente managed on their debut in the Champions League nevertheless to end at third place, allowing continuation in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>. Twente was defeated in the quarterfinals by <a href="/wiki/Villarreal_CF" title="Villarreal CF">Villareal</a>, after having defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubin_Kazan" title="Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</a>.</p>
<p>Mihaylov was recently quoted saying that Liverpool now have retained an option to buy him back from Twente, as he has played enough games for his native country's national team to qualify for a work permit.</p>
<p>On 23 December 2011, Mihaylov attended a ceremony in <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>, where he was honoured with the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="Bulgarian Footballer of the Year">Bulgarian Footballer of the Year</a> award (<a href="/wiki/Stiliyan_Petrov" title="Stiliyan Petrov">Stiliyan Petrov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dimitar_Berbatov" title="Dimitar Berbatov">Dimitar Berbatov</a> came second and third respectively). The last Bulgarian goalkeeper to have received this distinction is his father <a href="/wiki/Borislav_Mikhailov" title="Borislav Mikhailov">Borislav Mihailov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mersin">Mersin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Mihaylov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mersin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mihaylov signed for Turkish club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mersin_%C4%B0dmanyurdu_SK" title="Mersin İdmanyurdu SK">Mersin İdmanyurdu</a> on 1 September 2014 on a two-year contract as a free agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He made his debut in a 3–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Kemer_Tekirovaspor" title="Kemer Tekirovaspor">Kemer Tekirovaspor</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Cup" title="Turkish Cup">Turkish Cup</a> on 29 September. His <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="Süper Lig">Süper Lig</a> debut came on 22 November, in a 2–2 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Akhisar_Belediyespor" title="Akhisar Belediyespor">Akhisar Belediyespor</a>. On 27 December, Mihaylov played his second league game in a 1–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)">Fenerbahçe</a> at <a href="/wiki/%C5%9E%C3%BCkr%C3%BC_Saraco%C4%9Flu_Stadium" title="Şükrü Saracoğlu Stadium">Şükrü Saracoğlu Stadium</a>, in which he saved a penalty from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diego_Ribas_da_Cunha" title="Diego Ribas da Cunha">Diego</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Mihaylov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mihaylov made his senior international debut in the 5–1 defeat by <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kirin_Cup" title="Kirin Cup">Kirin Cup</a> in Japan in May 2006. On 12 August 2009, he earned his second cap, playing the full 90 minutes in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Latvia_national_football_team" title="Latvia national football team">Latvia</a>, which <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> won 1–0. Mihaylov did well to parry a dangerous <a href="/wiki/Vit%C4%81lijs_Astafjevs" title="Vitālijs Astafjevs">Vitālijs Astafjevs</a> shot in the second half, but was hardly tested during the game. He earned his third cap on 10 October 2009, after replacing injured <a href="/wiki/Dimitar_Ivankov" title="Dimitar Ivankov">Dimitar Ivankov</a> in the 1:4 away loss to <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_national_football_team" title="Cyprus national football team">Cyprus</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification">World Cup qualifier</a>. Mihaylov was the starting goalkeeper for Bulgaria during the country's first two reasonably unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying">qualifiers</a>. He played as the main custodian for Bulgaria during the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2014 World Cup qualifiers</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Vladislav_Stoyanov" title="Vladislav Stoyanov">Vladislav Stoyanov</a> was selected for the last two matches - against <a href="/wiki/Armenia_national_football_team" title="Armenia national football team">Armenia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_football_team" title="Czech Republic national football team">Czech Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
