<p><b>Nikolay Gennadiyevich Basov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Никола́й Генна́диевич Ба́сов</span>; 14 December 1922 – 1 July 2001) was a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Physicist" title="Physicist">physicist</a> and educator. For his fundamental work in the field of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quantum_electronics" title="Quantum electronics">quantum electronics</a> that led to the development of <a href="/wiki/Laser" title="Laser">laser</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maser" title="Maser">maser</a>, Basov shared the 1964 <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics" title="Nobel Prize in Physics">Nobel Prize in Physics</a> with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Prokhorov" title="Alexander Prokhorov">Alexander Prokhorov</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Hard_Townes" title="Charles Hard Townes">Charles Hard Townes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-i-lasers_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-i-lasers-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Basov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Basov was born in the town <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usman_(town)" title="Usman (town)">Usman</a>, now in <a href="/wiki/Lipetsk_Oblast" title="Lipetsk Oblast">Lipetsk Oblast</a> in 1922.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AC_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AC-2">[2]</a></sup> He finished school in 1941 in <a href="/wiki/Voronezh" title="Voronezh">Voronezh</a>, and was later called for military service at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuibyshev_Military_Medical_Academy" title="Kuibyshev Military Medical Academy">Kuibyshev Military Medical Academy</a>. In 1943 he left the academy and served in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AC_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-AC-2">[2]</a></sup> participating in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> with the <a href="/wiki/1st_Ukrainian_Front" title="1st Ukrainian Front">1st Ukrainian Front</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Basov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Basov graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moscow_Engineering_Physics_Institute" title="Moscow Engineering Physics Institute">Moscow Engineering Physics Institute</a> (MEPhI) in 1950. He then held a professorship at MEPhI and also worked in the <a href="/wiki/Lebedev_Physical_Institute" title="Lebedev Physical Institute">Lebedev Physical Institute</a> (LPI), where he defended a dissertation for the <i><a href="/wiki/Candidate_of_Sciences" title="Candidate of Sciences">Candidate of Sciences</a></i> degree (equivalent to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD">PhD</a>) in 1953 and a dissertation for the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Sciences" title="Doctor of Sciences">Doctor of Sciences</a></i> degree in 1956. Basov was the Director of the LPI in 1973–1988. He was elected as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corresponding_member" title="Corresponding member">corresponding member</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR_Academy_of_Sciences" title="USSR Academy of Sciences">USSR Academy of Sciences</a> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Russian Academy of Sciences">Russian Academy of Sciences</a> since 1991) in 1962 and <a href="/wiki/Academician" title="Academician">Full Member</a> of the Academy in 1966. In 1967, he was elected a Member of the Presidium of the Academy (1967—1990), and since 1990 he was the councillor of the Presidium of the USSR Academy of Sciences. He was Honorary President and Member of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=International_Academy_of_Science,_Munich&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="International Academy of Science, Munich (page does not exist)">International Academy of Science, Munich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was the head of the laboratory of quantum radiophysics at the LPI until his death in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-i-lasers_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-i-lasers-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Missile_defense">Missile defense</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Basov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Missile defense">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Basov's contributions to the development of the laser and maser, which won him the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> in 1964, led to new missile defense initiatives seeking to employ them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Basov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He entered politics in 1951<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and became a member of parliament (the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_of_the_Union" title="Soviet of the Union">Soviet of the Union</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union">Supreme Soviet</a>) in 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AC_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-AC-2">[2]</a></sup> Following <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._President" title="U.S. President">U.S. President</a> <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a>'s speech on <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Defense_Initiative" title="Strategic Defense Initiative">SDI</a> in 1983, Basov signed a letter along with other Soviet scientists condemning the initiative, which was published in the New York Times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TS_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-TS-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1985 he declared the Soviet Union was capable of matching SDI proposals made by the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TS_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-TS-6">[6]</a></sup> <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon.
