<p><b>Nikolay Gerasimovich Kuznetsov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Никола́й Гера́симович Кузнецо́в</span>; July 24, 1904 – December 6, 1974) was a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Navy" title="Soviet Navy">Soviet naval</a> officer who achieved the rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union">Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union</a> and served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Commissar" title="People's Commissar">People's Commissar</a> of the Navy during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">The Second World War</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Russian_Navy" title="Russian Navy">Russian</a> aircraft carrier <a href="/wiki/Russian_aircraft_carrier_Admiral_Kuznetsov" title="Russian aircraft carrier Admiral Kuznetsov"><i>Admiral Kuznetsov</i></a> is named in his honor.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Kuznetsov_(officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_and_advancement">Early years and advancement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Kuznetsov_(officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years and advancement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nikolay Gerasimovich Kuznetsov was born into a peasant family of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_people" title="Serbian people">Serbian</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> descent in the village of <a href="/wiki/Medvedki" title="Medvedki">Medvedki</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kotlassky_District" title="Kotlassky District">Kotlassky District</a> of <a href="/wiki/Arkhangelsk_Oblast" title="Arkhangelsk Oblast">Arkhangelsk Oblast</a>.</p>
<p>In 1919, Kuznetsov joined the Northern Dvina Naval Flotilla, after adding two years to his age in order to be eligible to serve. In 1920 he was stationed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrograd" title="Petrograd">Petrograd</a> and in 1924, as a member of a naval unit, he attended the funeral ceremony of <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Vladimir Lenin</a>. That same year he joined the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Communist Party of the Soviet Union">Bolshevik Party</a>.</p>
<p>Upon graduation from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frunze_Higher_Naval_School" title="Frunze Higher Naval School">Frunze Higher Naval School</a> in 1926, Kuznetsov served on the <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Soviet_cruiser_Chervona_Ukraina" title="Soviet cruiser Chervona Ukraina"><i>Chervona Ukraina</i></a> , first as watch officer and then as First Lieutenant. In 1932 he graduated from the Naval College after studying operational tactics. Upon graduation he was offered one of two options – a desk job with the general staff or a command post on a ship.</p>
<p>Kuznetsov successfully applied for the post of executive officer on the <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Soviet_cruiser_Krasnyi_Kavkaz" title="Soviet cruiser Krasnyi Kavkaz"><i>Krasnyi Kavkaz</i></a> . Within a year the young officer earned his next promotion. In 1934 he returned to the <i>Chervona Ukraina</i>, this time as her commander. Under Kuznetsov, the ship became an outstanding example of discipline and organization, quickly drawing attention to her young captain.</p>
<p>From September 5, 1936 to August 15, 1937, Kuznetsov was the naval attaché and chief naval advisor to <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Republican Spain</a>. Serving during the early stages of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>, he developed a strong dislike of <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">fascism</a>.</p>
<p>On returning home, on January 10, 1938, he was promoted to the rank of flag officer, 2nd rank, and given command of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Fleet_(Russia)" title="Pacific Fleet (Russia)">Pacific Fleet</a>. While in this position, he came face to face with <a href="/wiki/Great_Purge#Purge_of_the_army" title="Great Purge">Stalin's purge of the military</a>. Kuznetsov himself was never implicated, but many of the officers under his command were. Kuznetsov resisted the purges at every step, and his intervention saved the lives of many Soviet officers.</p>
<p>On April 28, 1939, Kuznetsov, still only thirty-four, was appointed the People's Commissar (Minister) of the Navy, a post he would hold for the duration of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>. In 1939, despite the Stalin policy against the <a href="/wiki/Military_Engineering-Technical_University" title="Military Engineering-Technical University">Nikolaevsky Engineering Academy</a>, Nikolay Gerasimovich Kuznetsov ordered the return of the Naval Engineering faculty from Moscow, and created the Military Engineering-Technical University to educate engineers for the construction of naval bases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1941, he ordered the creation near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leningrad" title="Leningrad">Leningrad</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Special_forces" title="Special forces">special forces</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commando_frogmen" title="Commando frogmen">company RON</a>, the first Soviet underwater special force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Second_World_War">The Second World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Kuznetsov_(officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Second World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kuznetsov played a crucial role during the first hours of the war – at this pivotal moment, his resolve and blatant disregard for orders averted the destruction of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Navy" title="Soviet Navy">Soviet Navy</a>. By June 21, 1941, Kuznetzov was convinced of the inevitability of war with Nazi Germany. On the same day <a href="/wiki/Semyon_Timoshenko" title="Semyon Timoshenko">Semyon Timoshenko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgy_Zhukov" title="Georgy Zhukov">Georgy Zhukov</a> issued a directive prohibiting Soviet commanders from responding to "German provocations". The Navy, however, constituted a distinct ministry (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narkomat" title="Narkomat">narkomat</a>), and thus Kuznetsov held a position which was technically outside the direct chain of command. He utilized this fact in a very bold move.</p>
<p>Shortly after midnight on the morning of June 22, Kuznetsov ordered all Soviet fleets to battle readiness. At 3:15 am that same morning, the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a> began <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchubel200733.E2.80.9334_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchubel200733.E2.80.9334-4">[4]</a></sup> The Soviet Navy was the only branch of the military in the highest state of combat readiness at the start of the initial German push.</p>
<p>In the following two years, Kuznetsov's primary concern was the protection of the Caucasus from a German invasion. Throughout the war, the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> remained the primary theater of operations for the Soviet Navy. During the war years Kuznetsov honed Soviet methods of <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_warfare" title="Amphibious warfare">amphibious assault</a>. In May 1944 he was given the rank of Admiral of the Fleet – a newly created position initially equated to that of a four-star general. In the same year, Kuznetsov was given the title of <a href="/wiki/Hero_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Hero of the Soviet Union">Hero of the Soviet Union</a>. On May 31, 1945, his rank was equated to the rank of <a href="/wiki/Marshal_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Marshal of the Soviet Union">Marshal of the Soviet Union</a> with a similar insignia.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_first_fall">The first fall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Kuznetsov_(officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The first fall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1946 to 1947 he was the Deputy Minister of the USSR Armed Forces and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief" title="Commander-in-Chief">Commander-in-Chief</a> of the Naval Forces.</p>
<p>In 1947 he was removed from his post on Stalin's orders and in 1948 he, as well as several other admirals were put on trial by the Naval Tribunal. Kuznetsov was demoted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice-admiral" title="Vice-admiral">vice-admiral</a>, while the other admirals received prison sentences of varying length.</p>
<p>In 1951 Stalin ended Kuznetsov's pariah status, once again placing him in command of the Navy (as the Minister of the Navy of the USSR), but without restoring his military rank, which was returned to him upon Stalin's death in 1953. In the same year, he became the First Deputy Minister of Defense of the USSR. In 1955, Kuznetsov was made Commander-in-Chief of the Naval Forces. His rank was raised to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union">Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union</a> and he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Marshal%27s_Star" title="Marshal's Star">Marshal's Star</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_second_fall_and_retirement">The second fall and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Kuznetsov_(officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The second fall and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His newfound prominence brought him into direct conflict with Marshal Zhukov, with whom he had clashed during the war years. On December 8, 1955, using the loss of the <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleship</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_battleship_Novorossiysk" title="Soviet battleship Novorossiysk"><i>Novorossiysk</i></a> as a pretext, Zhukov removed the Admiral from his post; in February 1956 Kuznetsov was again demoted to the rank of vice-admiral, retired and expressly forbidden "any and all work connected with the navy."</p>
<p>During his retirement he wrote and published many essays and articles, as well as several longer works, including his memoirs and an officially sanctioned book, "With a Course for Victory", which dealt with the Patriotic War. His memoirs, unlike those of many other prominent leaders, were written by him personally and are noted for their style.</p>
<p>Kuznetsov also authored several books on the war, on Stalin's repressions, and on the navy which were published <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Posthumous_work" title="Posthumous work">posthumously</a>. In these he was highly critical of the Party's interference in the internal affairs of the military, and insisted that "the state must be ruled by law."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rehabilitation_and_legacy">Rehabilitation and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolay_Kuznetsov_(officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rehabilitation and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the retirement of Zhukov in 1957, and of Khrushchev in 1964, a group of naval veterans began a campaign to restore Kuznetsov's rank, with all benefits, and to make him one of the General Inspectors of the Ministry of Defence. Invariably, these requests fell on deaf ears, particularly on those of Kuznetsov's successor, Admiral <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Gorshkov" title="Sergey Gorshkov">Gorshkov</a>. Not until July 26, 1988 did the <a href="/wiki/Presidium_of_the_Supreme_Soviet" title="Presidium of the Supreme Soviet">Presidium of the Supreme Soviet</a> of the USSR reinstate Kuznetsov to his former rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union">Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union</a>. Kuznetzov is now recognized as one of the most prominent men in the history of the Soviet and, today, of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Navy" title="Russian Navy">Russian Navy</a>.</p>
