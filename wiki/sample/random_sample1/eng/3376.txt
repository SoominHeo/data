<p><b>Niccolò II d'Este</b> (1338 – 26 March 1388) was lord of <a href="/wiki/Ferrara" title="Ferrara">Ferrara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Modena" title="Modena">Modena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parma" title="Parma">Parma</a> from 1361 until his death.</p>
<p>He was the son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Obizzo_III_d%27Este" title="Obizzo III d'Este">Obizzo III</a>, who had ruled in Ferrara from 1317 to 1352. After inheriting his lands from Aldobrandino III, he allied with <a href="/wiki/Padua" title="Padua">Padua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Verona" title="Verona">Verona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mantua" title="Mantua">Mantua</a> against <a href="/wiki/Bernab%C3%B2_Visconti" title="Bernabò Visconti">Bernabò Visconti</a> and, after a meeting at Viterbo, he managed to obtain also the support of Pope <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_V" title="Urban V">Urban V</a> (1367).</p>
<p>During Niccolò's reign, Ferrara started to gain a reputation as an art city. He commissioned to <a href="/wiki/Bartolino_da_Novara" title="Bartolino da Novara">Bartolino da Novara</a> the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Castello_Estense" title="Castello Estense">Castello Estense</a> after a popular revolt in 1385.</p>
