<p><b>Nikuman</b> (肉まん; derived from 肉饅頭 <i>niku</i> (meat) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manju_(food)" title="Manju (food)"><i>manjū</i></a>) is the Japanese name for the Chinese <i><a href="/wiki/Baozi" title="Baozi">baozi</a></i> (包子) made from flour <a href="/wiki/Dough" title="Dough">dough</a>, and filled with cooked ground <a href="/wiki/Pork" title="Pork">pork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beef" title="Beef">beef</a> or other ingredients. It is a kind of <b>chūka man</b> (中華まん lit. Chinese-style steamed bun) also known in English as <b>pork buns</b>.</p>
<p>Nikuman are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steamed" title="Steamed">steamed</a> and often sold as <a href="/wiki/Street_food" title="Street food">street food</a>. During festivals, they are frequently sold and eaten. From about August or September, through the winter months until roughly the beginning of April, Nikuman are available at <a href="/wiki/Convenience_store" title="Convenience store">convenience stores</a>, where they are kept hot.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_varieties">Other varieties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikuman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Other varieties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i>Butaman</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">豚まん</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>butaman</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> — essentially an equivalent to <i>nikuman</i>, this name is more common in the <a href="/wiki/Kansai_region" title="Kansai region">Kansai region</a>.</li>
<li><i>Anman</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">あんまん</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>anman</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> — the ingredients consist of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azuki_bean" title="Azuki bean">azuki beans</a> (<i>koshian</i> or <i>tsubuan</i>). Lard and sesame oil are typically added to increase flavor and taste. Similar to Chinese <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Doushabao&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Doushabao (page does not exist)">Doushabao</a>.</li>
<li><i>Kare-man</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">カレーまん</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>karēman</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> — <a href="/wiki/Turmeric" title="Turmeric">turmeric</a> or <a href="/wiki/Food_coloring" title="Food coloring">food coloring</a> is added to the skin to make it yellow. The ingredients are the same as meat buns or pork buns with <a href="/wiki/Curry" title="Curry">curry</a>-style flavoring. There is also curry man similar to <a href="/wiki/Curry_bread" title="Curry bread">curry bread</a> or dry curry.</li>
</ul>
