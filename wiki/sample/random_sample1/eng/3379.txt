<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Niki Reiser</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Niki Reiser</b> (born 12 May 1958) is a Swiss film score composer and flautist. He is considered one of the most outstanding film composers of the German-speaking countries, winning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Film_Award" title="German Film Award">German Film Award</a> five times. His debut was in 1986 the score for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Du_mich_auch" title="Du mich auch">Du mich auch</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Dani_Levy" title="Dani Levy">Dani Levy</a>, for whom he has composed all further scores. He had his breakthrough with the music for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Beyond_Silence_(1996_film)" title="Beyond Silence (1996 film)">Beyond Silence</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Link" title="Caroline Link">Caroline Link</a> in 1996.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niki_Reiser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Niki Reiser was born in 1958 in <a href="/wiki/Aargau" title="Aargau">Aargau</a> in Switzerland, the son of a pastor and a nurse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zff_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-zff-1">[1]</a></sup> Before he came to <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a> age twelve, where he has lived since, he spent four years in <a href="/wiki/Schaffhausen" title="Schaffhausen">Schaffhausen</a>. In addition to classical flute lessons a child, he invented his own melodies on the piano. In his youth he played in several bands, composed pieces for them and for theater performances at school.</p>
<p>In the 1970s, Reiser studied classical music with an emphasis on flute in Basel. From 1980 to 1984 he studied in Boston at the <a href="/wiki/Berklee_College_of_Music" title="Berklee College of Music">Berklee College of Music</a> jazz and classical music with a focus on film music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zff_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-zff-1">[1]</a></sup> From 1984 to 1986, Reiser lived partly in Europe, mostly in Switzerland, where he continued his studies in composition in Basel. In workshops in the U.S., he met <a href="/wiki/Ennio_Morricone" title="Ennio Morricone">Ennio Morricone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Goldsmith" title="Jerry Goldsmith">Jerry Goldsmith</a> who influenced his music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-2">[2]</a></sup> During this time he composed his first pieces for movies. During this period he also toured as a jazz flutist with the ensemble <i>People</i>, which he had co-founded.</p>
<p>In 1986, Reiser moved back to Europe where he met the film director <a href="/wiki/Dani_Levy" title="Dani Levy">Dani Levy</a>. He composed the music for his first film <i>Du mich auch</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zff_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-zff-1">[1]</a></sup> The film was a surprising success throughout Europe and was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Cam%C3%A9ra_d%27Or" title="Caméra d'Or">Golden Camera</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cannes_International_Film_Festival" title="Cannes International Film Festival">Cannes International Film Festival</a> in 1987. This encounter started a lasting friendship, resulting in Reiser composing the music for all of Levy's films.</p>
<p>At that time Reiser joined the klezmer band <a href="/wiki/Kolsimcha_%E2%80%93_The_World_Quintet" title="Kolsimcha – The World Quintet">Kolsimcha</a> as a flutist, and composed several pieces for the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miz_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-miz-3">[3]</a></sup> On two world tours he played with the band<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-klezmer_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-klezmer-4">[4]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/Montreux_Jazz_Festival" title="Montreux Jazz Festival">Montreux Jazz Festival</a>, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-winter_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-winter-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Reiser realised that it was impossible to be a flutist and a composer at the same time, he concentrated on composition. In addition to working with Levy, he also composed for <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Link" title="Caroline Link">Caroline Link</a>, whom he met in 1996. His breakthrough came with the music for her movie <i>Beyond Silence</i>. For her film <i><a href="/wiki/Nowhere_in_Africa" title="Nowhere in Africa">Nowhere in Africa</a></i>, Reiser studied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_music" title="African music">African music</a> in Nairobi. The influences of indigenous Kenyan music can be seen in the score he composed for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-America2004_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-America2004-6">[6]</a></sup> In addition to cinema and TV films, he also wrote music for the theater. In 2008 he worked again for Link for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Im_Winter_ein_Jahr" title="Im Winter ein Jahr">Im Winter ein Jahr</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-winter_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-winter-5">[5]</a></sup> The score won both the <a href="/wiki/Preis_der_deutschen_Filmkritik" title="Preis der deutschen Filmkritik">Preis der deutschen Filmkritik</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Filmpreis" title="Deutscher Filmpreis">Deutscher Filmpreis</a> of 2009.</p>
<p>Reiser has his studio in Basel's Gundeldingerfeld. He is on the board of the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Filmakademie" title="Deutsche Filmakademie">Deutsche Filmakademie</a> since its inception in 2003.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niki_Reiser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2012, Reiser was awarded the German film prize <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Filmpreis" title="Deutscher Filmpreis">Deutscher Filmpreis</a> five times. In 2005, he was the 12th recipient of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rheingau_Music_Prize" title="Rheingau Music Prize">Rheingau Music Prize</a>, as "a composer who repeatedly and majestically succeeds in meeting the particular dramaturgical needs entailed by film scores, and thus suffusing each individual work with its own unique sound".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miz_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-miz-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>1997 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayerischer_Filmpreis" title="Bayerischer Filmpreis">Bayerischer Filmpreis</a> for <i>Jenseits der Stille</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Beyond_Silence_(1996_film)" title="Beyond Silence (1996 film)">Beyond Silence</a></i>)</li>
<li>1997 Deutscher Filmpreis for <i>Jenseits der Stille</i></li>
<li>1999 Deutscher Filmpreis for <i>Meschugge</i> and <i>Pünktchen und Anton</i></li>
<li>2001 Film music prize of the <a href="/wiki/SUISA" title="SUISA">SUISA</a> foundation for <i>Kalt ist der Abendhauch</i></li>
<li>2002 Deutscher Filmpreis for <i>Nirgendwo in Afrika</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Nowhere_in_Africa" title="Nowhere in Africa">Nowhere in Africa</a></i>)</li>
<li>2004 <a href="/wiki/Preis_der_deutschen_Filmkritik" title="Preis der deutschen Filmkritik">Preis der deutschen Filmkritik</a> for <i>Das fliegende Klassenzimmer</i></li>
<li>2005 Deutscher Filmpreis for <i><a href="/wiki/Alles_auf_Zucker!" title="Alles auf Zucker!">Alles auf Zucker!</a></i></li>
<li>2005 Rheingau Music Prize</li>
<li>2008 Bayerischer Filmpreis for <i>Liebesleben</i></li>
<li>2009 Preis der deutschen Filmkritik for <i>Im Winter ein Jahr</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/A_Year_Ago_in_Winter" title="A Year Ago in Winter">A Year Ago in Winter</a></i>)</li>
<li>2009 Deutscher Filmpreis for <i>Im Winter ein Jahr</i></li>
<li>2011 Film music prize of the SUISA foundation <i>Das Blaue vom Himmel</i></li>
</ul>
