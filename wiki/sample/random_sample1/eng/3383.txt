<p>The <i><b>ninjatō</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">忍者刀</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, <i><b>ninjaken</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">忍者剣</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, or <i><b>shinobigatana</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">忍刀</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> ,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> are allegedly the preferred weapon that the <a href="/wiki/Ninja" title="Ninja">Shinobi</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feudal_Japan" title="Feudal Japan">feudal Japan</a> carried. It is portrayed by modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_Schools_of_Ninjutsu" title="Modern Schools of Ninjutsu">ninjutsu</a> practitioners (including <a href="/wiki/Masaaki_Hatsumi" title="Masaaki Hatsumi">Masaaki Hatsumi</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MH_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MH-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_K._Hayes" title="Stephen K. Hayes">Stephen K. Hayes</a>) and is prominently featured in <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">popular culture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKH_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SKH-4">[4]</a></sup> Replicas of this weapon are also prominently on display in both the Koka Ninja Village Museum in <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dka,_Shiga" title="Kōka, Shiga">Kōka, Shiga</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iga-ry%C5%AB_Ninja_Museum" title="Iga-ryū Ninja Museum">Iga-ryū Ninja Museum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iga,_Mie" title="Iga, Mie">Iga, Mie</a>. Historically, there is no physical evidence for the existence of this "<a href="/wiki/Katana" title="Katana">katana</a>-like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Short_sword" title="Short sword">short sword</a> legendarily used by ninja",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> though it is believed that they are based on the design of the <i><a href="/wiki/Wakizashi" title="Wakizashi">wakizashi</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Chokut%C5%8D" title="Chokutō">chokutō</a></i> swords.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DK_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-DK-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ninjat%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because of the lack of any physical evidence or antique swords from the <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> (16th to 19th century) matching the description of the ninjatō,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DK_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-DK-1">[1]</a></sup> the history of the weapon can only be reliably chronicled from the 20th century onwards.</p>
<ul>
<li>1964: The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iga-ry%C5%AB_Ninja_Museum" title="Iga-ryū Ninja Museum">Iga-ryū Ninja Museum</a> in Japan, which houses replicas of the sword, is established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> That same year, the swords appeared in <i>Shinobi no Mono Kirigakure Saizō</i> (忍びの者 霧隠才蔵) and <i>Shinobi no Mono Zoku Kirigakure Saizō</i> (忍びの者 続・霧隠才蔵), the 4th and 5th entries in the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Jidaigeki" title="Jidaigeki">jidaigeki</a> movie series <i><a href="/wiki/Shinobi_no_Mono" title="Shinobi no Mono">Shinobi no Mono</a></i>, released in theaters in Japan.</li>
<li>1973: Ads selling newly manufactured and imported ninja swords appear in the American magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Belt_(magazine)" title="Black Belt (magazine)">Black Belt</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>1981: Books containing references to the sword written by <a href="/wiki/Masaaki_Hatsumi" title="Masaaki Hatsumi">Masaaki Hatsumi</a>, the founder of the <a href="/wiki/Bujinkan" title="Bujinkan">Bujinkan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MH_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MH-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_K._Hayes" title="Stephen K. Hayes">Stephen K. Hayes</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKH_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-SKH-4">[4]</a></sup> an American who studied under Hatsumi in 1975,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> are published.</li>
<li>1983: The first Hollywood film to feature the ninjatō, <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_of_the_Ninja" title="Revenge of the Ninja">Revenge of the Ninja</a></i>, is released in theaters.</li>
<li>1984: The first American television production to feature these swords, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Master_(TV_series)" title="The Master (TV series)">The Master</a></i>, is broadcast on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ninjat%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Moden_depiction_of_ninja_with_ninjato_(ninja_sword).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="523" data-file-width="930" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Moden_depiction_of_ninja_with_ninjato_%28ninja_sword%29.jpg/220px-Moden_depiction_of_ninja_with_ninjato_%28ninja_sword%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Moden_depiction_of_ninja_with_ninjato_%28ninja_sword%29.jpg/330px-Moden_depiction_of_ninja_with_ninjato_%28ninja_sword%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Moden_depiction_of_ninja_with_ninjato_%28ninja_sword%29.jpg/440px-Moden_depiction_of_ninja_with_ninjato_%28ninja_sword%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The ninjatō is typically depicted as being a short sword, often portrayed as having a straight blade (similar to that of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shikomizue" title="Shikomizue">shikomizue</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>) with a square guard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DK_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-DK-1">[1]</a></sup> Usually of a length "less than 60 cm", the rest of the sword is comparatively "thick, heavy and straight". Hayes suggests that the typical description of the ninjatō could be due to ninja having to forge their own blades from slabs of steel or iron with the cutting edge being ground on a stone, with straight blades being easier to form than the much more refined curved traditional <a href="/wiki/Japanese_sword" title="Japanese sword">Japanese sword</a>. His second possible reason for ninjatō being described as a straight-bladed, rather short sword could be that the ninja were emulating one of the patron Buddhist deities of ninja families, <a href="/wiki/Acala" title="Acala">Fudo Myo-oh</a>, who is depicted brandishing a straight-bladed short sword similar to a <a href="/wiki/Chokut%C5%8D" title="Chokutō">chokutō</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Turnbull_(historian)" title="Stephen Turnbull (historian)">Stephen Turnbull</a>, a historian specializing in the military history of Japan indicates of historical ninja: "The most important ninja weapon was his sword. This was the standard Japanese fighting sword or katana ... for convenience the ninja would choose a blade that was shorter and straighter than usual."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ninjat%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the lack of historical evidence regarding the existence of the ninjatō, techniques for usage in a martial context are largely speculative. When used in film and stage, ninjatō are depicted as being shorter than a katana with a straight blade but they are utilized in a "nearly identical" manner as the katana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Books and other written materials have described a number of possible ways to use the sword including "fast draw techniques centered around drawing the sword and cutting as a simultaneous defensive or attacking action",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> with "a thrust fencing technique",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WJ_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-WJ-14">[14]</a></sup> and with a "reverse grip".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The scabbards were often said to have been used for various purposes such as a respiration pipe (snorkel) in underwater activities or for secretly overhearing conversations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WJ_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-WJ-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The scabbard is also said to have been longer than the blade of the ninjatō in order to hide various objects such as chemicals used to blind pursuers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tsuba" title="Tsuba">tsuba</a> (hand guard) of the ninjato is often described as being larger than average and square instead of the much more common round tsuba. One theory on the ninjatō tsuba size and shape is that it was used as a tool, the sword would be leaned against a wall and ninja would use the tsuba as a step to extend his normal reach, the sword would then be retrieved by pulling it up by the sageo (saya cord).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
