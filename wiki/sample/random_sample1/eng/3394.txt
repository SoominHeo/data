<p>The <b>Nissan Fuga</b> (Japanese: 日産・フーガ <i>Nissan Fūga</i>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fullsize_car" title="Fullsize car">full-size</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxury_car" title="Luxury car">luxury</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sedan_(car)" title="Sedan (car)">sedan</a> produced by Japanese automaker <a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan">Nissan</a> since October 2004. It is built on a wider, stretched wheelbase version of the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_FM_platform" title="Nissan FM platform">Nissan FM platform</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cima" title="Nissan Cima">Nissan Cima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nissan_President" title="Nissan President">Nissan President</a> were discontinued in August 2010, the Fuga became Nissan's <a href="/wiki/Flagship#Automotive" title="Flagship">flagship</a> vehicle. In North America and Europe, the Fuga is sold as the second and third-generation <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M</a> (Q70 from 2014), where it has been the flagship of the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti" title="Infiniti">Infiniti</a> luxury division of Nissan since 2006.</p>
<p>First shown as the Fuga Concept at the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Motor_Show" title="Tokyo Motor Show">Tokyo Motor Show</a>, the Fuga is a replacement for the long running <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cedric" title="Nissan Cedric">Nissan Cedric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Gloria" title="Nissan Gloria">Nissan Gloria</a> series of cars and later succeeded the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cima" title="Nissan Cima">Nissan Cima</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_President" title="Nissan President">Nissan President</a>. The name of the vehicle is the Italian word for the <a href="/wiki/Fugue" title="Fugue">fugue</a>, a composition musical form. The name Fuga was chosen to suggest that the long, storied histories of the Cedric, Gloria, Cima, and President are being combined into a new vehicle.</p>

<p>When the Prince (Nissan) Gloria was first introduced in 1959, it was based on a stretched version of the original Prince (Nissan) Skyline, which was first introduced in 1957. Nissan used this approach again for the Fuga, using the Skyline platform again. The Fuga was available at Japanese Nissan dealerships called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nissan_Motor_Company#Japan" title="Nissan Motor Company">Nissan Red Stage</a></i> and <i>Nissan Blue Stage</i> until <i>Blue Stage</i> and <i>Red Stage</i> dealerships were combined into one Japanese Nissan dealership network utilized internationally in 2005.</p>
<p>Nissan wanted to create a fresh approach started by the Cedric/Gloria and focus its efforts to compete with European executive mid-sized sports sedans, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_5_series" title="BMW 5 series">BMW 5 series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audi_A6" title="Audi A6">Audi A6</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_E-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz E-Class">Mercedes-Benz E-Class</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_S-Type" title="Jaguar S-Type">Jaguar S-Type</a>. The Fuga's primary competitor from Toyota is the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Crown" title="Toyota Crown">Toyota Crown</a> Athlete Series, but also competes with the <a href="/wiki/Lexus_GS" title="Lexus GS">Lexus GS</a>.</p>
<p>The Fuga trim levels began with "XV" and ended with "GT" as the top trim package, equivalent to the previous "Brougham" and "Gran Turismo" versions of the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cedric" title="Nissan Cedric">Cedric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Gloria" title="Nissan Gloria">Gloria</a>.</p>
<p>In markets outside Japan it is sold as the second-generation <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M</a> (M35/45). The Infiniti M became the flagship of the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti" title="Infiniti">Infiniti</a> luxury division after the 2006 model year, as the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q" title="Infiniti Q">Infiniti Q</a> (the Cima's counterpart in certain markets) was discontinued. However the Cima would remain in production for the Japanese domestic market and it is 2" longer than the Fuga which slots below it in the lineup.</p>
<p>The Fuga is also used as an uplevel <a href="/wiki/Taxicab" title="Taxicab">taxi</a> in Japan.</p>
<p>Mitsuoka Motors, a small Japanese automobile company, uses the Nissan Fuga as a base for their Galue-III luxury sedan which has been produced since 2005.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nissan_Fuga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Aluminum is used in the body, hood, trunk lid and is also used in the doors internally to reduce weight. The front suspension uses a double wishbone setup, also to reduce unsprung weight, and to improve vehicle handling. This type of suspension was previously used on the Cedric/Gloria until 1983, when that platform began to use MacPherson struts for the front suspension, but still uses the multilink rear suspension which was introduced on the Cedric/Gloria in 1979. The Fuga uses aluminum alloy extensively in both the front and rear suspension components.</p>
<p>The GT sports package includes Nissan's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4WS" title="4WS">4WS</a> version of <a href="/wiki/HICAS" title="HICAS">HICAS</a> to improve stability and handling, taking advantage of the 53:47 weight distribution.</p>
<p>A driver aid system used in Japan, called Distance Control Assistance, pushes the gas pedal against the foot when the DVD navigation with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GPS" title="GPS">GPS</a> determines that an unsafe speed is being maintained. If the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_cruise_control_system" title="Autonomous cruise control system">Autonomous cruise control system</a> is being used, the Distance Control Assistance will reduce speed automatically, and will warn the driver that an adjustment is being made with an audible bell sound.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nissan_Fuga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the Fuga was first introduced for sale October 14, 2004 the advertising campaign used a musical work in the <a href="/wiki/Fugue" title="Fugue">fugue</a> style. The work is titled "<a class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qbymu2-Mc2k" rel="nofollow">Vortex</a>", composed and arranged by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Akihiko_Matsumoto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Akihiko Matsumoto (page does not exist)">Akihiko Matsumoto</a>, and was specially commissioned for Nissan. The music was played in three movements by a full orchestra for 7 minutes and 42 seconds, and a recording was given to dealers in Japan as a promotional DVD-CD gift for customers called "<a class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g1xSq2-BeHc&amp;feature=related" rel="nofollow">Fuga The Movie and Premium Soundtrack</a>", free of charge.</p>
<p>The DVD and CD package was not available for private sale, with the DVD providing a gallery of images, and descriptions of key technologies such as how the adaptive cruise control functions, various safety technologies like the Blind Spot camera, which is a camera installed on the passenger side side view mirror displaying a view of the curb on the in-dash monitor, and a movie with the Fuga on a mountain road, using the "Vortex" soundtrack. The DVD is recorded in Dolby 5.1 surround sound.</p>

<p>The second generation <a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan">Nissan</a> Fuga, introduced as the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M</a>, was previewed at the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Pebble_Beach_Concours_d%27Elegance" title="Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance">Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The new Fuga debuted at the 2009 Tokyo Motor Show. The production version went on sale across Japan starting on December 21, 2009. It is sold in overseas markets under the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti" title="Infiniti">Infiniti</a> nameplate as the third-generation M (M37/M56/35h/30d). The second generation Fuga's body design (styled by Hideo Komuro in 2007), took on a more organic look.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ulrich_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ulrich-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caranddriver.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-caranddriver.com-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and was a resurrection of a 1960s and 1970s appearance called "<a href="/wiki/Coke_bottle_styling" title="Coke bottle styling">coke bottle styling</a>" that was used on the 1970s <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cedric" title="Nissan Cedric">Nissan Cedric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Gloria" title="Nissan Gloria">Nissan Gloria</a>.</p>

<p>The Y51 series <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M</a> / Nissan Fuga introduced November 2009 is only 50 mm (2.0 in) shorter than the F50 series <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cima" title="Nissan Cima">Nissan Cima</a>, with the same width dimensions of 1,845 mm (6.1 ft) and height of 1,515 mm (5.0 ft) compared to the Cima's of 1,490 mm (4.9 ft) height. On August 2010, the Fuga became Nissan's flagship with the announcement of the cancellation of the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cima" title="Nissan Cima">Nissan Cima</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_President" title="Nissan President">Nissan President</a>. The Fuga is 115 mm (4.5 in) shorter than the Nissan President, and the Fuga has a longer wheelbase at 2,900 mm (114.2 in), compared to the wheelbase on the President and Cima at 2,870 mm (113.0 in). When the Fuga assumed the role as flagship sedan of Nissan Japan August 2010, it became the first time that Nissan didn't sell a premium luxury V8 sedan in Japan; the Fuga's North American cousin, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M#Third_generation_.282010.E2.80.93.29" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M56</a>, is offered with a <a href="/wiki/Nissan_VK_engine#VK56VD" title="Nissan VK engine">V8</a> shared with the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_QX56" title="Infiniti QX56">Infiniti QX56</a>.</p>
<p>World first <a href="/wiki/Wrong-way_driving_warning" title="Wrong-way driving warning">Wrong-way driving warning</a> debuted on the Fuga hybrid in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The VK45DE V8 engine used in the previous generation is no longer offered in Japan, neither is the succeeding <a href="/wiki/Nissan_VK_engine#VK56VD" title="Nissan VK engine">VK56VD V8</a> which is only found on the Fuga's North American counterpart, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M#Third_generation_.282011.E2.80.93.29" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M56</a>. The larger 5.6L engine incurs a higher rate of <a href="/wiki/Road_tax#Japan" title="Road tax">road tax liability</a> which hampers sales. The <a href="/wiki/Nissan_VQ_engine#VQ35HR" title="Nissan VQ engine">VQ35HR</a> 3.5 L V6 Hybrid is now the Fuga's third engine option, utilizing the <a href="/wiki/Atkinson_cycle" title="Atkinson cycle">Atkinson cycle</a>. The electric motor, installed in parallel between the engine and the transmission, produces 68 PS (50 kW; 67 bhp) from 1.3kWh <a href="/wiki/Lithium-ion_battery" title="Lithium-ion battery">lithium-ion batteries</a> that are expected to have a service life of 10 years. The batteries are installed upright behind the rear seats. The Fuga Hybrid introduced Nissan's first in-house developed electric hybrid technology, and Nissan claims it will double the <a href="/wiki/Fuel_economy_in_automobiles" title="Fuel economy in automobiles">fuel economy</a> of its gasoline-powered version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NissanHybrid_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-NissanHybrid-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>A driving mode selector knob has been installed as standard equipment on all models, situated below the transmission gear lever and between the heated and ventilated front seat controls, providing four selections labeled "Standard", "Sport", "Eco", and "Snow", allowing the 7-speed transmission, engine and various systems to optimize driving based on varying conditions. To minimize exhaust noise intrusion into the passenger compartment from the engine at low RPMs, "Active Noise Control" has been installed. Double-piston shock absorbers, developed by Kayaba Industry Company, have replaced the ones previously supplied by <a href="/wiki/Hitachi" title="Hitachi">Hitachi</a>.</p>
<p>Trim levels offered in Japan start with the 250GT, 250GT Type P, 250GT "A" package; 370GT, 370GT FOUR (AWD), 370GT FOUR (AWD) "A" package, 370GT Type S, with the 370VIP at the top of the range. The 370GT Type S uses 20" Potenza RE050A 245/40 tires, with other trim packages using Dunlop SP Sport Maxx TT 245/50 tires on 18" with all wheels consisting of aluminum alloy.</p>
<p>Some of the optional interior equipment includes heated and ventilated front seats, power reclining rear seats, power <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_(furniture)" title="Ottoman (furniture)">ottoman</a> for the rear passenger opposite the driver, and HDD navigation combined with a <a href="/wiki/Telematics" title="Telematics">telematics</a> subscription service called "<a href="/wiki/CarWings" title="CarWings">CarWings</a>" in Japan.</p>
<p>Safety technology has also been introduced, such as Lane Departure Prevention, which uses a small camera mounted above the rearview mirror to gauge distance from the lane markers, and will lightly tap selected brakes when necessary to help ease the car back into the correct lane. It will allow for unimpeded lane changes when the turn signal is used, and will yield control to the driver at all times.</p>
<p>Blind Spot Intervention System uses sensors to provide a visual warning in the side view mirror. If the car senses that movement towards the obstacle is occurring, it will provide an audible warning and will apply opposite side brakes to guide the vehicle into the correct lane.</p>
<p>The Nissan Fuga Hybrid, together with the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Leaf" title="Nissan Leaf">Nissan Leaf</a>, will include Nissan's new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vehicle_Sound_for_Pedestrians" title="Vehicle Sound for Pedestrians">Vehicle Sound for Pedestrians</a> system to alert pedestrians, the blind and others to their presence when the hybrid is operating at low speeds in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-electric_mode" title="All-electric mode">all-electric mode</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NissanNoise_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-NissanNoise-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AutoBlogNoise_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-AutoBlogNoise-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
