<p><b>Nikkō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">日光市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nikkō-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Tochigi_Prefecture" title="Tochigi Prefecture">Tochigi Prefecture</a>, in the northern <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of May 2015, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 84,197 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 58.1 persons per km². Its total area was 1,449.83 km². it is a popular destination for Japanese and international tourists. Attractions include the <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleum</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">shogun</a> <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D_T%C5%8Dsh%C5%8D-g%C5%AB" title="Nikkō Tōshō-gū">Nikkō Tōshō-gū</a>) and that of his grandson <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Iemitsu" title="Tokugawa Iemitsu">Iemitsu</a> (Iemitsu-byō Taiyū-in), and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Futarasan_Shrine" title="Futarasan Shrine">Futarasan Shrine</a>, which dates to the year 767. There are also many famous hot springs (<i><a href="/wiki/Onsen" title="Onsen">onsen</a></i>) in the area. Elevations range from 200 to 2,000 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NC2007_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NC2007-1">[1]</a></sup> The Japanese saying <i>"Never say 'kekkou' until you've seen Nikko"</i>—<i>kekko</i> meaning beautiful, magnificent or "I am satisfied"—is a reflection of the beauty and sites in Nikkō.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lohn1983_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lohn1983-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Nikkō covers a large area (1,449.83 km²) of rural northwestern Tochigi approximately 140 km north of <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> and 35 km west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Utsunomiya,_Tochigi" title="Utsunomiya, Tochigi">Utsunomiya</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Tochigi_Prefecture" title="Tochigi Prefecture">Tochigi Prefecture</a>. It is the third-largest city (by area) in Japan, behind <a href="/wiki/Takayama,_Gifu" title="Takayama, Gifu">Takayama</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamamatsu,_Shizuoka" title="Hamamatsu, Shizuoka">Hamamatsu</a>.</p>
<p>Both the <a href="/wiki/Watarase_River" title="Watarase River">Watarase River</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kinugawa_River" title="Kinugawa River">Kinugawa River</a> pass through the city. <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ch%C5%ABzenji" title="Lake Chūzenji">Lake Chūzenji</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kegon_Falls" title="Kegon Falls">Kegon Falls</a> lie in Nikkō, as does the <a href="/wiki/Nikko_Botanical_Garden" title="Nikko Botanical Garden">Nikko Botanical Garden</a>. The city's many mountains and waterfalls have made it an important source of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_power" title="Hydroelectric power">hydroelectric power</a>. The area has also been used for mining <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aluminum" title="Aluminum">aluminum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Concrete" title="Concrete">concrete</a>.</p>
<p>The weather in Nikkō is fairly similar to that of the northern island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hokkaid%C5%8D" title="Hokkaidō">Hokkaidō</a> even though Nikkō is much closer to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> than Hokkaidō. The elevation of Nikkō plays an important role in this fact. It will usually get cooler as one ascends the mountain. The average temperature of Nikkō is around 7 °C (44 °F) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with the warmest months reaching only about 22 °C (72 °F)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the coldest reaching down to about -8 °C (17 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikk%C5%8D,_Tochigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nikkō experiences a humid continental climate (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Dfb</i>) with cold, snowy winters and predominantly mild, very wet summers. Nikkō is much cooler than nearby parts of Honshu because it is situated at an altitude of 1298m above sea level.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikk%C5%8D,_Tochigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1901_Japanese_View_and_Map_of_Nikko,_Japan_-_Geographicus_-_Nikko-japan-1901.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2872" data-file-width="4000" height="158" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/1901_Japanese_View_and_Map_of_Nikko%2C_Japan_-_Geographicus_-_Nikko-japan-1901.jpg/220px-1901_Japanese_View_and_Map_of_Nikko%2C_Japan_-_Geographicus_-_Nikko-japan-1901.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/1901_Japanese_View_and_Map_of_Nikko%2C_Japan_-_Geographicus_-_Nikko-japan-1901.jpg/330px-1901_Japanese_View_and_Map_of_Nikko%2C_Japan_-_Geographicus_-_Nikko-japan-1901.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/1901_Japanese_View_and_Map_of_Nikko%2C_Japan_-_Geographicus_-_Nikko-japan-1901.jpg/440px-1901_Japanese_View_and_Map_of_Nikko%2C_Japan_-_Geographicus_-_Nikko-japan-1901.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Shōdō Shōnin <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">勝道上人</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> established the temple of <a href="/wiki/Rinn%C5%8D-ji" title="Rinnō-ji">Rinnō-ji</a> in 766, followed by the temple of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ch%C5%ABzen-ji&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chūzen-ji (page does not exist)">Chūzen-ji</a> in 784. The village of Nikkō developed around these temples. The shrine of <a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D_T%C5%8Dsh%C5%8D-g%C5%AB" title="Nikkō Tōshō-gū">Nikkō Tōshō-gū</a> was completed in 1617 and became a major draw of visitors to the area during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. It is known as the burial place of the famous Japanese <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">shogun</a> <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a>. A number of new roads were built during this time to provide easier access to Nikkō from surrounding regions. <a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D_T%C5%8Dsh%C5%8D-g%C5%AB" title="Nikkō Tōshō-gū">Nikkō Tōshō-gū</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Futarasan_Shrine" title="Futarasan Shrine">Futarasan Shrine</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rinn%C5%8D-ji" title="Rinnō-ji">Rinnō-ji</a> now form the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Shrines_and_Temples_of_Nikk%C5%8D" title="Shrines and Temples of Nikkō">Shrines and Temples of Nikkō</a></i>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a> Nikkō developed as a mountain resort, and became particularly popular among foreign visitors to Japan. The <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> began service to Nikkō in 1890 with the <a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D_Line" title="Nikkō Line">Nikkō Line</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Railway" title="Tobu Railway">Tobu Railway</a> in 1929 with its <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dbu_Nikk%C5%8D_Line" title="Tōbu Nikkō Line">Nikkō Line</a>.</p>
<p>Nikkō was incorporated as a town in 1889, (part of <a href="/wiki/Kamitsuga_District,_Tochigi" title="Kamitsuga District, Tochigi">Kamitsuga District</a>). It was upgraded to city status in 1954 after merging with the neighboring village of <a href="/wiki/Okorogawa,_Tochigi" title="Okorogawa, Tochigi">Okorogawa</a>.</p>
<p>On March 20, 2006, Nikkō absorbed the old city of <a href="/wiki/Imaichi,_Tochigi" title="Imaichi, Tochigi">Imaichi</a>, the town of <a href="/wiki/Ashio,_Tochigi" title="Ashio, Tochigi">Ashio</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Kamitsuga_District,_Tochigi" title="Kamitsuga District, Tochigi">Kamitsuga District</a>), the town of <a href="/wiki/Fujihara,_Tochigi" title="Fujihara, Tochigi">Fujihara</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Kuriyama,_Tochigi" title="Kuriyama, Tochigi">Kuriyama</a> (both from <a href="/wiki/Shioya_District,_Tochigi" title="Shioya District, Tochigi">Shioya District</a>), to create what is officially the new and expanded city of Nikkō. The new city hall is located at the former Imaichi City Hall; the former Nikkō City Hall is now known as Nikkō City Hall-Nikkō Satellite Office.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikk%C5%8D,_Tochigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nikkō is heavily dependent on tourism to its historical and scenic sites and hot spring resorts. Hydroelectric power production, food processing, and the metals industry remain important components of the economy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikk%C5%8D,_Tochigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nikko has 23 <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary schools</a>, 15 <a href="/wiki/Middle_school" title="Middle school">middle schools</a> and three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">high schools</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikk%C5%8D,_Tochigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg/220px-Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg/330px-Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg/440px-Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nikko_Kirifuri_skate_center2009.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Nikkō Kirifuri skate center</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Nikkō is the home city to the <a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D_Ice_Bucks" title="Nikkō Ice Bucks">Nikkō Ice Bucks</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Asia_League_Ice_Hockey" title="Asia League Ice Hockey">Asia League Ice Hockey</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nikk%C5%8D_Kirifuri_Ice_Arena" title="Nikkō Kirifuri Ice Arena">Nikkō Kirifuri Ice Arena</a> hosted the Women's <a href="/wiki/Ice_Hockey_World_Championships" title="Ice Hockey World Championships">Ice Hockey World Championships</a> (Division I) in April 2007.</p>
<p>There is also a <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating" title="Speed skating">speed skating</a> oval.</p>
