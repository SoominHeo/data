<p><i><b>Dagashi Kashi</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">だがしかし</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series by Kotoyama. It has been serialized in <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan" title="Shogakukan">Shogakukan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen_manga" title="Shōnen manga"><i>shōnen</i> manga</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Sunday" title="Weekly Shōnen Sunday">Weekly Shōnen Sunday</a></i> since June 2014 and has been collected in six <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes. A <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novel</a> adaptation titled <i>Dagashi Kashi: Mō Hitotsu no Natsu Yasumi</i>, written and illustrated by Manta Aisora, was published in a single volume by Shogakukan on December 18, 2015 under their <a href="/wiki/Gagaga_Bunko" title="Gagaga Bunko">Gagaga Bunko</a> imprint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series adaptation by <a href="/wiki/Feel_(company)" title="Feel (company)">Feel</a> aired in Japan between January 7, 2016 and March 31, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> "Dagashi Kashi" means "cheap sweets candy", but if read "Daga Shikashi", it also means "however".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dagashi_Kashi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>Kokonotsu Shikada <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">鹿田 ココノツ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shikada Kokonotsu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Atsushi_Abe" title="Atsushi Abe">Atsushi Abe</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Todd_Haberkorn" title="Todd Haberkorn">Todd Haberkorn</a> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Kokonotsu and his father Yō live in a countryside town where they run a small sweets store. He aspires to be a manga artist, an ambition that puts him at odds with his father who wants him to inherit the store which has been run by the family for eight generations. He constantly finds himself manning the cash register, more often than not a result of his father's trickery. After Hotaru's arrival, he also has to put up with her antics as well. He is nicknamed <i>Kokonatsu</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ココナツ</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, "Coconuts")</span>. His given name is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kun%27yomi" title="Kun'yomi">Kun'yomi</a> pronunciation of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_numerals" title="Japanese numerals">Japanese numeral</a> <span class="t_nihongo_kanji"><span lang="ja">九</span></span>, <i>Kokonotsu</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ここのつ</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, "Ninth")</span>; it works as a pun, referring to his being the ninth generation of the family that will run the store.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Hotaru Shidare <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">枝垂 ほたる</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shidare Hotaru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Ayana_Taketatsu" title="Ayana Taketatsu">Ayana Taketatsu</a> (Japanese); Tabitha Ray (English)</span></dd>
<dd>An eccentric girl who is very passionate about sweets and snacks. Her father owns a famous snacks company and she came to Shikada store to recruit Yō. As Yō will not leave as long as Kokonotsu is unwilling to succeed him as Shikada storekeeper, she quickly strikes a deal with him: she will convince Kokonotsu to take over the store in return for his employment. From then on she tries to persuade Kokonotsu using many different ways such as games, stories, and riddles.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Saya Endō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">遠藤 サヤ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Endō Saya</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Manami_Numakura" title="Manami Numakura">Manami Numakura</a> (Japanese); Sara Ragsdale (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Kokonotsu's classmate and childhood friend who runs a nearby cafe. She has a longstanding crush on Kokonotsu. At first alarmed by Hotaru's presence, she quickly befriends her, as their goals are aligned: Hotaru wishes Kokonotsu to run the store while she wants him to keep staying in town. Saya has a hidden talent of toy mastery. At one point she defeats a well-prepared Hotaru at a <a href="/wiki/Menko" title="Menko">Menko</a> game, an event that leads Hotaru to start addressing her with suffix <i>shi</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">師</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, an antiquated designation used by disciples to their masters.</dd>
<dd>She is the younger twin sister of Tō.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Tō Endō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">遠藤 豆</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Endō Tō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Tatsuhisa_Suzuki" title="Tatsuhisa Suzuki">Tatsuhisa Suzuki</a> (Japanese); Justin Pate (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Older twin brother of Saya and a good friend of Kokonotsu. A laid back person who usually wears a Hawaiian shirt and sunglasses. He and Saya run a cafe named "Cafe Endō".</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Yō Shikada <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">鹿田 ヨウ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shikada Yō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Keiji_Fujiwara" title="Keiji Fujiwara">Keiji Fujiwara</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Inman" title="Jeremy Inman">Jeremy Inman</a> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Kokonotsu's father as well as the current owner of Shikada store. He is desperate to make his son the 9th head of the shop so that his legacy will continue.
