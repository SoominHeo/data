<p><b>Dagmara "Daga" Wozniak</b> (<b>Woźniak</b>) (born July 1, 1988) is an American <a href="/wiki/Sabre_(fencing)" title="Sabre (fencing)">saber</a> <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Wozniak was named to the U.S. Olympic team at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in women's saber competition as a substitute and as a competitor at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fencingteam_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-fencingteam-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-workethic_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-workethic-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of the summer of 2016, she was ranked as one of the top 10 saber fencers in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She represented the <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="United States at the 2016 Summer Olympics">United States at the 2016 Summer Olympics</a> in Rio de Janeiro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dagmara_Wozniak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her parents are Gregory and Irena Wozniak and she has an older sister, Zuzanna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Born in <a href="/wiki/Wroc%C5%82aw" title="Wrocław">Wrocław</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, Wozniak immigrated with her parents to the United States from Poland when she was one year old. The family settled in <a href="/wiki/Avenel,_New_Jersey" title="Avenel, New Jersey">Avenel, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wozniak graduated from <a href="/wiki/Colonia_High_School" title="Colonia High School">Colonia High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Woodbridge_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Woodbridge Township, New Jersey">Woodbridge Township, New Jersey</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She went on to attend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._John%27s_University_(New_York)" title="St. John's University (New York)">St. John's University</a>, where she majored in Biology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fencing_career">Fencing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dagmara_Wozniak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fencing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wozniak began fencing at age 9 at the Polish American Fencing School (PAFS) in <a href="/wiki/Linden,_New_Jersey" title="Linden, New Jersey">Linden, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> She has trained at the Manhattan Fencing Center since high school, and her coach is <a href="/wiki/Yury_Gelman" title="Yury Gelman">Yury Gelman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> She won three team medals at the Junior World Fencing Championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wozniak was an Olympic alternate in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Olympic_Games" title="2008 Olympic Games">2008 Olympic Games</a> in Beijing but did not compete. She became a full member of the U.S. team at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_Olympic_Games" title="2012 Olympic Games">2012 Olympic Games</a> and placed eighth, losing to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sophia_Velikaia" title="Sophia Velikaia">Sofiya Velikaya</a> of Russia in the quarterfinals by the score of 15-13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-workethic_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-workethic-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> It was later revealed that she had fenced with a partially <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torn_Achilles_tendon" title="Torn Achilles tendon">torn Achilles tendon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>After winning bronze medals in the team competition at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2011 World Fencing Championships">2011 World Fencing Championships</a>, and the 2012 and 2013 Senior World Championships, Wozniak won a gold medal with Team USA in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of the summer of 2016, she was ranked among the top 10 saber fencers in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> She is represented the <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="United States at the 2016 Summer Olympics">United States at the 2016 Summer Olympics</a> in Rio de Janeiro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The team won a bronze medal.</p>
