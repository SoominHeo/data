<p><b>Naoki Tanaka</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">田中 直紀</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tanaka Naoki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 19 June 1940 as Naoki Suzuki)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Japan" title="Democratic Party of Japan">Democratic Party of Japan</a>, a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Councillors" title="House of Councillors">House of Councillors</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diet_of_Japan" title="Diet of Japan">Diet</a> (national legislature), from <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefectural</a> electorate. He is a native of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kanazawa,_Ishikawa" title="Kanazawa, Ishikawa">Kanazawa, Ishikawa</a> and graduate of <a href="/wiki/Keio_University" title="Keio University">Keio University</a>.</p>
<p>When he married <a href="/wiki/Makiko_Tanaka" title="Makiko Tanaka">Makiko Tanaka</a> he changed his family name to hers. He was elected to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_(Japan)" title="House of Representatives (Japan)">House of Representatives</a> for the first time in 1983. After losing his seat in 1996, he was elected to the House of Councillors for the first time in 1998.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
