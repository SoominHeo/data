<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>González</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Güiza</i>.</div>
<p><b>Daniel 'Dani' González Güiza</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[daˈnjel ˈɣwiθa]</a></span>; born 17 August 1980) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz_CF" title="Cádiz CF">Cádiz CF</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Late_bloomer" title="Late bloomer">late bloomer</a>, he made a name for himself at the age of 27, being crowned <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> top scorer with <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">Mallorca</a>. Subsequently he helped the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish national team</a> win <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">Euro 2008</a>, and signed with <a href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)">Fenerbahçe</a> in Turkey shortly afterwards.</p>
<p>Over the course of eight seasons Güiza amassed Spanish top division totals of 141 games and 52 goals, mainly with Mallorca and <a href="/wiki/Getafe_CF" title="Getafe CF">Getafe</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Having started playing on the streets of his hometown of <a href="/wiki/Jerez_de_la_Frontera" title="Jerez de la Frontera">Jerez de la Frontera</a> in <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz" title="Cádiz">Cádiz</a>, Güiza used to hide behind trees in an attempt to avoid the running, despite being then coached by boyhood idol <a href="/wiki/Kiko_(Spanish_footballer)" title="Kiko (Spanish footballer)">Kiko</a>, who begun his professional career with local <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz_CF" title="Cádiz CF">Cádiz CF</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LOWE_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-LOWE-1">[1]</a></sup> He eventually made it into his local club <a href="/wiki/Xerez_CD" title="Xerez CD">Xerez CD</a> and, after one season there, first showed his eye for goal at <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a> side <a href="/wiki/Dos_Hermanas_CF" title="Dos Hermanas CF">Dos Hermanas CF</a>, loaned immediately after <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a> bought him. Such performances saw him make his debut in <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> for the latter at the age of 19, but he would fail to perform, being relegated to <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca_B" title="RCD Mallorca B">the reserves</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUE_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CUE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Güiza was then loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Recreativo_de_Huelva" title="Recreativo de Huelva">Recreativo de Huelva</a>, but could not find a secure place in the team, and moved soon after to <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona_B" title="FC Barcelona B">FC Barcelona B</a> in the same predicament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUE_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-CUE-2">[2]</a></sup> Subsequently he joined <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_de_Murcia" title="Ciudad de Murcia">Ciudad de Murcia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>, where his career saw a turning point with two consecutive top-five finishes in the top scorers' list (runner-up in the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2004–05 Segunda División">2004–05 season</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUE_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-CUE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Getafe">Getafe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Getafe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This impressed <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schuster" title="Bernd Schuster">Bernd Schuster</a> enough to sign Güiza for <a href="/wiki/Getafe_CF" title="Getafe CF">Getafe CF</a> in the summer of 2005. He was an instant first-choice at his new team and became recognised as a top flight striker, scoring 27 goals in 70 competitive games – seven in nine <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> appearances – while the team from the <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> outskirts achieved back-to-back ninth-league places.</p>
<p>The club's president, Ángel Torres, described him as "the best finisher in the first division after <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUE_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-CUE-2">[2]</a></sup> On 10 May 2007, he was one of three players on target in a 4–0 home defeat of <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> for <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Copa_del_Rey" title="2006–07 Copa del Rey">the semifinals</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>, which meant qualification for the decisive match for the first time ever after a 6–5 aggregate win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mallorca">Mallorca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mallorca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After four years, Güiza returned to Mallorca in a <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>5 million move, brokered by his wife and agent <a href="/wiki/Nuria_Berm%C3%BAdez" title="Nuria Bermúdez">Nuria Bermúdez</a>, experiencing a breakthrough season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LOWE_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-LOWE-1">[1]</a></sup> On 20 April 2008 he scored a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> against <a href="/wiki/Real_Murcia" title="Real Murcia">Real Murcia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and finished the campaign as <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_La_Liga" title="2007–08 La Liga">the league</a>'s top scorer by scoring 27 goals in 37 matches, a feat achieved without a single <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty kick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUE_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-CUE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Club coach <a href="/wiki/Gregorio_Manzano" title="Gregorio Manzano">Gregorio Manzano</a> said: "In 24 years of coaching I've never had the winner of the <i><a href="/wiki/Pichichi_Trophy" title="Pichichi Trophy">Pichichi</a></i> in my team. Dani is the top goalscorer in our league, and deserves extra credit for doing it without taking penalties, and he is also worthy of selection to Euro 2008".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUE_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-CUE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fenerbah.C3.A7e">Fenerbahçe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fenerbahçe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 8 July 2008, Güiza signed for <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> club <a href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)">Fenerbahçe SK</a> for €14 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In his first month he managed to score twice in the <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="Süper Lig">Süper Lig</a>, adding another two in the club's <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2008–09 UEFA Champions League">run</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>.</p>
<p>Güiza ended a long scoring drought on 22 March 2009 with an early goal against <a href="/wiki/Bursaspor" title="Bursaspor">Bursaspor</a>, as Fenerbahçe lost 1–2 in the dying minutes of the game. Prior to the beginning of <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="2009–10 Süper Lig">2009–10</a> he scored three against <a href="/wiki/Budapest_Honv%C3%A9d_FC" title="Budapest Honvéd FC">Budapest Honvéd FC</a> in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Europa_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round#First_leg_3" title="2009–10 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round">the third qualifying round</a> of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>, in a 5–1 home win (6–2 on aggregate), netting his first in the domestic competition in matchday one against <a href="/wiki/Denizlispor" title="Denizlispor">Denizlispor</a>, in the first minute of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his first two seasons in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, Güiza helped the team to consecutive <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Cup" title="Turkish Cup">Turkish Cup</a> finals, netting in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Turkish_Cup" title="2008–09 Turkish Cup">2008–09 edition</a> in a 2–4 loss against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K" title="Beşiktaş J.K">Beşiktaş JK</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Getafe">Return to Getafe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Getafe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 August 2011, one day after his 31st birthday, Güiza signed with former club Getafe for €6 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> agreeing to a three-year contract shortly after. He was regularly played in <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_La_Liga" title="2011–12 La Liga">the first season in his second spell</a>, starting in 15 of his appearances, but failed to reproduce his previous form, scoring just three goals.</p>
<p>On 6 November 2012, Güiza signed for <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Super_League" title="Malaysia Super League">Malaysia Super League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Johor_Darul_Ta%27zim_F.C." title="Johor Darul Ta'zim F.C.">Johor Darul Ta'zim F.C.</a> on a season-long loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> His move, along with the acquisition of former <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">S.S. Lazio</a> winger <a href="/wiki/Simone_Del_Nero" title="Simone Del Nero">Simone Del Nero</a> and seven <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_national_football_team" title="Malaysia national football team">Malaysian internationals</a>, was part of the club's major restructuring plans upon the appointment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tunku_Ismail_Idris" title="Tunku Ismail Idris">Tunku Ismail Idris</a>, the Crown Prince of <a href="/wiki/Johor" title="Johor">Johor</a>, as president;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> based on past achievements and honours the player, at the time of his signing, was arguably the most prolific footballer to have competed in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Güiza made his first competitive appearance and league debut for his new team on 8 January 2013, in an opening day away game against <a href="/wiki/Pahang_FA" title="Pahang FA">Pahang FA</a>: despite a 2–3 shock defeat, he provided the <a href="/wiki/Assist_(football)" title="Assist (football)">assists</a> for both of his side's goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He scored his first competitive goal on 15 January in an away game against treble-winners <a href="/wiki/Kelantan_FA" title="Kelantan FA">Kelantan FA</a>, with an expertly curled <a href="/wiki/Direct_free_kick" title="Direct free kick">free-kick</a> from 25 yards out as the game ended 1–1;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> three days later he netted from open play in a home game against <a href="/wiki/2012_Malaysia_Cup" title="2012 Malaysia Cup">2012 Malaysia Cup</a> runners-up <a href="/wiki/ATM_FA" title="ATM FA">ATM FA</a>, with a volleyed lob from the edge of the penalty area in an eventual 2–2 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Güiza played his last match for Darul Takzim on 6 April 2013 in a 1–2 loss against <a href="/wiki/Selangor_FA" title="Selangor FA">Selangor FA</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Malaysia_FA_Cup" title="2013 Malaysia FA Cup">2013 Malaysia FA Cup</a>, after his loan was ended prematurely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Both he and Del Nero left the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cerro_Porte.C3.B1o">Cerro Porteño</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cerro Porteño">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Güiza joined <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Porte%C3%B1o" title="Cerro Porteño">Cerro Porteño</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He made his debut on 14 September of that year, as a 69th-minute substitute against <a href="/wiki/Sportivo_Carapegu%C3%A1" title="Sportivo Carapeguá">Sportivo Carapeguá</a>, scoring four minutes later in an eventual 4–0 home win<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> as the team went on to win its 30th <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Paraguayan Primera División">Paraguayan Primera División</a> title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He also featured in <a href="/wiki/2014_Copa_Libertadores" title="2014 Copa Libertadores">the following year</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a>, netting three times to help to progress from the group stage and leading to interest from other clubs, to which he responded: "My heart is here, from day one they have shown me affection, my heart is what drives me, so I'm staying here"; however, new manager <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Astrada" title="Leonardo Astrada">Leonardo Astrada</a> did not have the player in his plans, which led to his release in March 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="C.C3.A1diz">Cádiz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cádiz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2015, Güiza returned to his native Andalusia, signing for Cádiz. His arrival drew the ire of fans, due to disparaging remarks he had made about the club a decade ago while playing for local rivals Xerez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 June 2016, Güiza scored the only goal at <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9rcules_CF" title="Hércules CF">Hércules CF</a> in the second leg of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B_playoffs" title="2016 Segunda División B playoffs">play-off finals</a> (2–0 on aggregate), helping the club return to the second division after six years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spain_Euro_08_celebration_1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="856" data-file-width="1280" height="161" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Spain_Euro_08_celebration_1.jpg/240px-Spain_Euro_08_celebration_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Spain_Euro_08_celebration_1.jpg/360px-Spain_Euro_08_celebration_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Spain_Euro_08_celebration_1.jpg/480px-Spain_Euro_08_celebration_1.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>

<p>Güiza was a <a href="/wiki/Late_bloomer" title="Late bloomer">late bloomer</a> on the international stage, having been first called up by <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> on 8 November 2007 (aged 27) for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying">qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national football team">Northern Ireland</a>, as the national team was already qualified for the finals in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. He made his international debut against the latter on 21 November, in a 1–0 home win in <a href="/wiki/Las_Palmas" title="Las Palmas">Las Palmas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Güiza was subsequently selected to the final squad of 23 but, despite being the league's top scorer in that year, still doubted his chances of being picked: "I was really scared I'd miss out, because being the league's top scorer doesn't guarantee you a place".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUE_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-CUE-2">[2]</a></sup> On 18 June 2008 he made his debut in the tournament, and scored Spain's winning goal against <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greece</a> during the dying moments of the last match group stage match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In the quarterfinals he came on as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a>, missing the team's fourth attempt in an eventual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout_(association_football)" title="Penalty shootout (association football)">penalty shootout</a> win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the semifinal match against <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a>, Güiza netted his second international goal, in a 3–0 win: after receiving a pass from <a href="/wiki/Cesc_F%C3%A0bregas" title="Cesc Fàbregas">Cesc Fàbregas</a>, he quickly side-flicked it over the goalkeeper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He played 15 minutes in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_Final" title="UEFA Euro 2008 Final">the final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> after replacing <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Torres" title="Fernando Torres">Fernando Torres</a> (who scored the game's only goal), and came very close to assisting <a href="/wiki/Marcos_Senna" title="Marcos Senna">Marcos Senna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 October 2008 Güiza came on as a late substitute against <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a>, and soon after assisted <a href="/wiki/David_Villa" title="David Villa">David Villa</a> who scored the winning goal in a close 2–1 contest for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">qualifiers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>After an article circulated in January 2009 involving details about personal life and behaviour in training, Güiza's international career future was questioned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JEOPARDY_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-JEOPARDY-29">[29]</a></sup> Such reports on his behaviour became debatable due to the unreliable sources and any rumours of him being dropped from the Spain team were put to rest when <a href="/wiki/Vicente_del_Bosque" title="Vicente del Bosque">Vicente del Bosque</a> called him up for his services for the 2010 World Cup qualification matches against <a href="/wiki/Turkey_national_football_team" title="Turkey national football team">Turkey</a>, where he solidified his superb performance against Belgium, once again coming on in the dying minutes and assisting a last-minute winner during the match held in Istanbul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 June 2009, Güiza was selected to the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2009 FIFA Confederations Cup">2009 FIFA Confederations Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On the 28th he came from the bench as the national team trailed hosts <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a> (0–1) in the third-place match, and responded with a spectacular brace in the last two minutes as Spain eventually won the game in <a href="/wiki/Overtime_(sports)" title="Overtime (sports)">extra-time</a> (3–2).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_G%C3%BCiza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1701" data-file-width="1134" height="263" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg/175px-NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg/263px-NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg/350px-NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg 2x" width="175"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NURIA_BERMUDEZ_REDU.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Güiza's partner and agent, <a href="/wiki/Nuria_Berm%C3%BAdez" title="Nuria Bermúdez">Nuria Bermúdez</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Güiza is of <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Romani (Gypsy)</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He was introduced as "the greatest promise in Spain", but also "the greatest party-goer" when he first joined Ciudad de Murcia, with club coach <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Lillo" title="Juan Manuel Lillo">Juan Manuel Lillo</a> stating that he had "too many birds flying round in his head".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LOWE_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-LOWE-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Güiza suffered a divorce from his first wife Rocío with whom he had his first child, which gained a lot of publicity in the Spanish press. Furthermore, he admitted that "the night confuses me" and, after joining Getafe, he started to take advantage of the night life in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, suffering a <a href="/wiki/Stomach" title="Stomach">stomach</a> infection as a result and subsequently being forced to go on a diet consisting of liquids alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LOWE_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-LOWE-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his time at Getafe, Güiza met <a href="/wiki/Nuria_Berm%C3%BAdez" title="Nuria Bermúdez">Nuria Bermúdez</a>. She sat for the qualifications to become an official <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> football agent, which she passed scoring 29 points, just five below the top and went on to become Güiza's agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> She also obtained the highest score for a female football agent in Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Speaking of his relationship with the <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a>, Getafe coach Schuster said: "Ever since Dani's been with Nuria, he turns up to training and it doesn't look like he's slept under a bridge". On 1 December 2007, Bermúdez gave birth to Güiza's second son, also named Daniel, at <i>Policlínica Miramar</i> in <a href="/wiki/Palma,_Majorca" title="Palma, Majorca">Palma, Majorca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before Euro 2008, Güiza promised the media that if he were to win the competition, he would then propose to Bermúdez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In January 2009, the Spanish media began to publicise problems between his family and Bermúdez, with his mother apparently threatening to slit Bermúdez's throat for being a "thief".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Bermúdez then left Turkey with son Daniel, amongst claims the latter was ignored by the father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Güiza's ex-wife, Rocío Aranda, revealed that the two had met up just hours before the start of the match between <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> and Spain on 11 February 2009. According to Aranda, they met up in the hotel bar and went on to have sexual intercourse in one of the bedrooms. Subsequently, it was rumoured that Spain coach Del Bosque would drop him from the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JEOPARDY_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-JEOPARDY-29">[29]</a></sup> Güiza spoke against this in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Marca_(newspaper)" title="Marca (newspaper)">Marca</a></i> saying: "The truth is, I hadn't seen my son in a while so I went to see him in the hotel, I have to take advantage of such opportunities to see my children. To make money, she [his ex-wife] makes up stories. I spoke with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Hierro" title="Fernando Hierro">Hierro</a> and I explained the situation. I told him that I had to see my son and I wanted to be with him. I explained everything. I tried my best to be with my son and the truth is the whole story has not come out well in the press."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
