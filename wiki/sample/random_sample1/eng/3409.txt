<p><b>Daniel Olbrychski</b> (<small>Polish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Polish" title="Help:IPA for Polish">[ɔlˈbrɨxskʲi]</a></span>; born 27 February 1945) is a <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> actor best known for leading roles in several <a href="/wiki/Andrzej_Wajda" title="Andrzej Wajda">Andrzej Wajda</a> movies and also known for playing a defector and spymaster Vassily Orlov, alongside Hollywood actress <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Jolie" title="Angelina Jolie">Angelina Jolie</a> in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Salt_(2010_film)" title="Salt (2010 film)">Salt</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Olbrychski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Olbrychski was born in <a href="/wiki/%C5%81owicz" title="Łowicz">Łowicz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. He attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gimnazjum_i_Liceum_im._Stefana_Batorego_(Warsaw,_Poland)" title="Gimnazjum i Liceum im. Stefana Batorego (Warsaw, Poland)">Gimnazjum i Liceum im. Stefana Batorego (Warsaw, Poland)</a>.</p>
<p>In 1971 he won the award for Best Actor at the <a href="/wiki/7th_Moscow_International_Film_Festival" title="7th Moscow International Film Festival">7th Moscow International Film Festival</a> for his role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Birch_Wood" title="The Birch Wood">The Birch Wood</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moscow1971_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moscow1971-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He played one of the leading roles in Volker Schlöndorff's film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tin_Drum" title="The Tin Drum">The Tin Drum</a></i> based on <a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCnter_Grass" title="Günter Grass">Günter Grass</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tin_Drum" title="The Tin Drum">Die Blechtrommel</a></i>. He also appeared in one of the ten short films in <a href="/wiki/Krzysztof_Kie%C5%9Blowski" title="Krzysztof Kieślowski">Krzysztof Kieślowski</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dekalog" title="Dekalog">Dekalog</a></i> and had a small role in <a href="/wiki/The_Unbearable_Lightness_of_Being" title="The Unbearable Lightness of Being">the film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unbearable_Lightness_of_Being" title="The Unbearable Lightness of Being">The Unbearable Lightness of Being</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1986 Olbrychski received the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Legion_of_Honor" title="French Legion of Honor">French Legion of Honor</a> (L'Ordre national de la Légion d'honneur). In 2007 he received the <a href="/wiki/Stanislavsky_Award" title="Stanislavsky Award">Stanislavsky Award</a> at the <a href="/wiki/29th_Moscow_International_Film_Festival" title="29th Moscow International Film Festival">29th Moscow International Film Festival</a> for the outstanding achievement in the career of acting and devotion to the principles of <a href="/wiki/Stanislavski%27s_system" title="Stanislavski's system">Stanislavsky's</a> school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moscow2007_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moscow2007-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>In addition to acting, Olbrychski has been well known for his abilities as an athlete. A keen horse-rider and very able with the <a href="/wiki/Sabre" title="Sabre">sabre</a>, Olbrychski personally performed most of the stunt scenes in his movies.</p>
<p>He is the father of two sons, Rafał Olbrychski (actor, born 26 February 1971) by Monika Dzienisiewicz-Olbrychska, and Viktor by German actress <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Sukowa" title="Barbara Sukowa">Barbara Sukowa</a>. He also has a daughter named Weronika (b. 1982) by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zuzanna_%C5%81apicka-Olbrychska&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zuzanna Łapicka-Olbrychska (page does not exist)">Zuzanna Łapicka-Olbrychska</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Andrzej_%C5%81apicki" title="Andrzej Łapicki">Andrzej Łapicki</a>.</p>
<p>He was selected to be on the jury for the Cinéfondation and short films sections of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2015 Cannes Film Festival">2015 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
