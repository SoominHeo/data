<p><b>Daniel Van Buyten</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈdaːnijɛl vɑm ˈbœytə(n)]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> born 7 February 1978) is a Belgian former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre_back" title="Defender (association football)">centre back</a>. Nicknamed "Big Dan",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Van Buyten was known for his uncompromising style of play, exploiting both his physical strength and aerial ability.</p>
<p>Beginning at <a href="/wiki/R._Charleroi_S.C." title="R. Charleroi S.C.">Charleroi</a> as a striker, Van Buyten's career took off when he converted to a centre back, playing for a series of top flight clubs, including <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>. Throughout his career, Van Buyten was also a regular starter for the <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium national team</a>, amassing 83 caps in an international career that spanned 13 years and which included participation at two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>Van Buyten became the first-ever Belgian winner of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> when Bayern Munich claimed the European crown in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Van_Buyten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Van Buyten joined <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a> in the summer of 2001, leaving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">Belgian</a> side <a href="/wiki/Standard_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Standard Liège">Standard Liège</a> with whom he had been with since 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After two-and-a-half productive seasons with the club, on 31 January 2004 Van Buyten agreed to a short term loan agreement with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>, signing for the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Manchester_City_F.C._season" title="2003–04 Manchester City F.C. season">season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He proved to be a success in his half-season loan deal at the club, garnering praise from his manager, specifically in his performance against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>. But former manager <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Keegan" title="Kevin Keegan">Kevin Keegan</a> conceded that, "I don't think we will keep him at the end of the season as I feel he will go to a big club then."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Van Buyten returned to Marseille at the end of his loan agreement and was signed by <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> club <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a> in June 2004 to replace outgoing captain <a href="/wiki/Nico-Jan_Hoogma" title="Nico-Jan Hoogma">Nico-Jan Hoogma</a>, agreeing to a four-year deal with the German side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bayern_Munich">Bayern Munich</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Van_Buyten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bayern Munich">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2006, <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> announced that they had acquired Van Buyten from Hamburg and had signed him to a contract lasting through the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_FC_Bayern_Munich_season" title="2009–10 FC Bayern Munich season">2009–10 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Van Buyten had a highly successful first season at the <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavarian</a> club, forming an effective centre-back partnership with <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BAcio" title="Lúcio">Lúcio</a>. Van Buyten scored his first goal for Bayern against <a href="/wiki/FC_Energie_Cottbus" title="FC Energie Cottbus">Energie Cottbus</a> on 9 December 2006, scoring the game-winning goal of a 2–1 victory for <i>Die Roten</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Van Buyten then scored his second goal for the club on 26 January 2007, levelling the sides at 1–1 but Bayern fell to <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> by a score of 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>Arguably Van Buyten's most valuable contribution in his debut season, however, came against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> on 3 April 2007, in the first-leg of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2006–07 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> quarter-final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Van Buyten scored two away goals to level the match and give Bayern Munich the advantage in the tie. In the quarter-final encounter against Milan, he scored twice with his left foot, once in the 78th minute to take Bayern to 1–1 and then he scored a stoppage time half-volley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 February 2010, Bayern Munich announced that Van Buyten had signed a new contract that would keep him at the club until 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> During the second half of the 2010–11 season, Van Buyten was mostly relegated to bench player status, but recovered his starting spot towards the end of the season. He was part of the squad that blitzed <a href="/wiki/FC_St._Pauli" title="FC St. Pauli">FC St. Pauli</a> on 11 May 2011, netting a goal in an 8–1 victory that condemned the <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>-based side to relegation to the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 December 2011, in the match between Bayern Munich and <a href="/wiki/1._FC_K%C3%B6ln" title="1. FC Köln">1. FC Köln</a>, Van Buyten struck a free-kick so hard that the ball burst.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 30 April 2012, Van Buyten signed a one-year extension with Bayern Munich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite the arrival of centre-back <a href="/wiki/Dante_(footballer)" title="Dante (footballer)">Dante</a> from <a href="/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Borussia Mönchengladbach">Borussia Mönchengladbach</a> in the summer of 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Van Buyten became an integral member of the back-line for the Bavarians during the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_FC_Bayern_Munich_season" title="2012–13 FC Bayern Munich season">2012–13 campaign</a>, part of a three-man rotation in the centre of defense with Dante and <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_Boateng" title="Jérôme Boateng">Jérôme Boateng</a>. On 6 April 2013, with a 1–0 defeat of <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a> thanks to a goal from <a href="/wiki/Bastian_Schweinsteiger" title="Bastian Schweinsteiger">Bastian Schweinsteiger</a>, Van Buyten celebrated his third <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Bundesliga" title="2012–13 Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> championship with Bayern, clinching the club's 23rd national title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Van Buyten played the full 90 minutes in the club's 3–0 second leg semi-final victory over <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_La_Liga" title="2012–13 La Liga">Spanish</a> champions <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> on 1 May, clinching a resounding 7–0 aggregate victory and a place in the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2013 UEFA Champions League Final">Champions League</a> final against fellow Bundesliga side Borussia Dortmund.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Van Buyten is the first Belgian player in a Champions League final.</p>
<p>After heavily been linked with big spenders <a href="/wiki/AS_Monaco_FC" title="AS Monaco FC">Monaco</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Van Buyten signed a further contract extension with Bayern Munich on a one-year rolling contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Having been a part member of the squad under manager <a href="/wiki/Pep_Guardiola" title="Pep Guardiola">Pep Guardiola</a>, and despite scoring against <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Van Buyten was rumored to join <a href="/wiki/1._FC_K%C3%B6ln" title="1. FC Köln">1. FC Köln</a> on a pre-contract agreement, though this was denied by a club official.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> After the end of the season, the club did not renew his contract, leaving him a free agent to sign with another club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Belgian_First_Division_A" title="Belgian First Division A">Belgian Pro League</a> club <a href="/wiki/R.S.C._Anderlecht" title="R.S.C. Anderlecht">Anderlecht</a> offered him a two-year contract in August 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> but he rejected it, stating that he had retired from competitive football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Van_Buyten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Van Buyten was called up to the national team in 2001 and received his first cap for <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a> on 28 February 2001 against <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_6" title="2002 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group 6">2002 FIFA World Cup qualifying</a> match,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> starting in a 10–1 thrashing of the nation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> His first goal for the national team came in just his second cap, heading home the equalizing goal in a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> on 24 March 2001, earning a crucial point for ten-man Belgium in World Cup qualifying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>After good performances with Marseille in France, the young defender was included in the 23-man roster for the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He played the full game in Belgium's opening match of the tournament, a 2–2 draw with co-hosts <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> on 4 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In the second group match, Belgium stuttered to a surprising 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Tunisia_national_football_team" title="Tunisia national football team">Tunisia</a> on 10 June while Van Buyten booked for the Belgians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Van Buyten helped Belgium pick up their first win of the tournament and in doing so progression to the knockout stage, stopping a late flurry from <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a> to secure a 3–2 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Finishing as runners-up in the group, Belgium were dealt a tough draw with four-time champions <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Despite a valiant effort, Van Buyten and the Belgians bowed out in the round of 16 as the duo of <a href="/wiki/Rivaldo" title="Rivaldo">Rivaldo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a> led Brazil to a 2–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The South Americans would eventually be crowned champions with a defeat of <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2002 FIFA World Cup Final">final</a> on 30 June by the same scoreline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following Belgium's impressive display at the World Cup in Japan and South Korea, much was expected of the small nation in the qualifying phase of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004_qualifying_Group_8" title="UEFA Euro 2004 qualifying Group 8">UEFA Euro 2004</a> tournament and the country was placed in Pot A amongst the best teams in the European federation. Van Buyten played in all eight qualifying games for the national team<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> but Belgium failed to make it to the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004" title="UEFA Euro 2004">final tournament</a>, missing out on second spot in the group to <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a> on the head-to-head rule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 11 February 2009, he scored a rare double as the Belgians sunk <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia</a> 2–0 in a friendly match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Seven months later, on 9 September, Van Buyten notched Belgium's only goal as Belgium slumped to a disappointing 2–1 defeat to lowly <a href="/wiki/Armenia_national_football_team" title="Armenia national football team">Armenia</a> in <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_5" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group 5">FIFA World Cup qualifying</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The loss meant that Belgium lost any hope of qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, with head coach <a href="/wiki/Franky_Vercauteren" title="Franky Vercauteren">Franky Vercauteren</a> resigning minutes after final whistle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a> qualification, Belgium were placed in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012_qualifying_Group_A" title="UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying Group A">Group A</a> with the likes of Germany, <a href="/wiki/Turkey_national_football_team" title="Turkey national football team">Turkey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_football_team" title="Austria national football team">Austria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The defender netted a brace away to Turkey on 7 September 2010, but despite his best efforts, Belgium fell to a 3–2 loss as captain <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Kompany" title="Vincent Kompany">Vincent Kompany</a> was sent off for two bookable offenses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Van Buyten was once again an integral member for the Belgians in yet another qualifying campaign, featuring in seven of the ten qualifying matches for the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Belgium narrowly missed out in qualifying for the playoff-round, finishing two points behind Turkey with a 3–1 loss to Germany in the final qualifying round game on 11 October 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2014 World Cup qualifying</a>, the veteran defender made five appearances as Belgium reached its first tournament in 12 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 May 2014, Van Buyten was named in Belgium's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 World Cup</a>, in Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> At the age of 36, he started in the team's first game of the tournament, a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algeria</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Van_Buyten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Van Buyten's father, Franz, was a popular Belgian catcher/wrestler in Europe from 1960 to 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> His mother, Renate, is German.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bank_theft_incident">Bank theft incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Van_Buyten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bank theft incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While he was with Manchester City, he, along with players <a href="/wiki/Djamel_Belmadi" title="Djamel Belmadi">Djamel Belmadi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Mat%C3%ADas_Vuoso" title="Vicente Matías Vuoso">Matías Vuoso</a>, were the victim of theft perpetrated by two bankers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> In total, bank workers at the Co-operative Bank stole more than <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>350,000 from the accounts of the three players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2006, the bank workers, Paul Sherwood, a cashier, and Paul Hanley, his supervisor, were jailed for 32 months and 12 months respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
