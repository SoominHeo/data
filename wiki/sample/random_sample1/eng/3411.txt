<p><b>Daniel Bozhilov Petrov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Даниел Божилов Петров</span>) (born 8 September 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Varna" title="Varna">Varna</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxer</a>. He won a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">Barcelona Olympics in 1992</a> and a gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">Atlanta Olympics in 1996</a> in the category Light <a href="/wiki/Flyweight" title="Flyweight">Flyweight</a>.</p>
<p>Petrov began his career in <a href="/wiki/Varna" title="Varna">Varna</a>, Bulgaria, but then went to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavia_Sofia_Sports_Club" title="Slavia Sofia Sports Club">Slavia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>). He became a champion of the country several times. In 1993 he captured the European title in <a href="/wiki/1993_European_Amateur_Boxing_Championships" title="1993 European Amateur Boxing Championships">Bursa, Turkey</a>. Two years later he won the world title at the <a href="/wiki/1995_World_Amateur_Boxing_Championships" title="1995 World Amateur Boxing Championships">1995 World Amateur Boxing Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, followed by the European title a year later in <a href="/wiki/1996_European_Amateur_Boxing_Championships" title="1996 European Amateur Boxing Championships">Vejle, Denmark</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gold_medal_controversy">Gold medal controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Petrov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Gold medal controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The gold medal match where Petrov won against Velasco from the Philippines was hailed as "The Robbery in Atlanta". <a href="/wiki/Mansueto_Velasco" title="Mansueto Velasco">Mansueto Velasco</a> would hit Daniel Petrov with telling blows, and the scores would be counted for Petrov who was barely throwing any punches, was on a joy ride to an Olympic gold medal. It appeared that the judges were pressing the buttons on their electronic scoring equipment for the wrong boxer. Petrov was still awarded the gold medal despite the numerous protests.</p>
