<p><b>Daniel Jarque González</b> (1 January 1983 – 8 August 2009) was a Spanish <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>.</p>
<p>He played his entire career with <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">Espanyol</a>, and was named <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">team captain</a> one month before his death from a heart attack, at the age of 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MOURN_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MOURN-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Jarque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, and a product of local <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">RCD Espanyol</a>'s youth system, Jarque made his <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> debut with his hometown club on 20 October 2002, in a match against <a href="/wiki/Recreativo_de_Huelva" title="Recreativo de Huelva">Recreativo de Huelva</a> during <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_La_Liga" title="2002–03 La Liga">2002–03</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After 15 appearances in two seasons combined, he went on to become a defensive mainstay for a team that <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Copa_del_Rey" title="2005–06 Copa del Rey">won the 2006 edition</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> on 18 September 2005, he scored the game's only goal to help the hosts defeat <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_La_Liga" title="2006–07 La Liga">2006–07</a>, after renewing his contract until 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Jarque appeared in 14 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> games as Espanyol <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_UEFA_Cup" title="2006–07 UEFA Cup">reached</a> the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="2007 UEFA Cup Final">final</a>, lost on <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalties</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a>. In <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_La_Liga" title="2005–06 La Liga">the previous league campaign</a>, he contributed with four league goals as the <i>Pericos</i> finished 15th.</p>
<p>At the start of <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_La_Liga" title="2009–10 La Liga">2009–10</a>, Jarque relieved <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Tamudo" title="Raúl Tamudo">Raúl Tamudo</a> of his <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> duties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_tributes">Death and tributes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Jarque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death and tributes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to several online Spanish newspapers, Jarque was found dead during Espanyol's pre-season, in <a href="/wiki/Coverciano" title="Coverciano">Coverciano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a>, Italy. On 8 August 2009 he was on the phone with his girlfriend in the team hotel when he suffered a <a href="/wiki/Myocardial_infarction" title="Myocardial infarction">heart attack</a>. Some other news sources say that Jarque's girlfriend, who was seven months into her pregnancy,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> reported his absence to his roommate <a href="/wiki/Ferran_Corominas" title="Ferran Corominas">Ferran Corominas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MOURN_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MOURN-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 August 2009, <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> player <a href="/wiki/Cesc_F%C3%A0bregas" title="Cesc Fàbregas">Cesc Fàbregas</a>, a former team-mate of Jarque in the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">Spanish under-21 side</a>, dedicated his second goal in his team's 6–1 <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> win at <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> to him, by raising a shirt with his name and number (#21, which he wore mainly as a professional).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The homage was again paid the following month – as Espanyol won the first match at their new <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estadi_Cornell%C3%A0-El_Prat" title="Estadi Cornellà-El Prat">Estadi Cornellà-El Prat</a></i> – against <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF" title="Málaga CF">Málaga CF</a> – by 2–1 scorer <a href="/wiki/Iv%C3%A1n_Alonso" title="Iván Alonso">Iván Alonso</a>, as Jarque's pregnant girlfriend was also giving birth to the couple's daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 July 2010, during the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2010 FIFA World Cup Final">final</a>, his close friend <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Iniesta" title="Andrés Iniesta">Andrés Iniesta</a> celebrated his 116th-minute winning goal by removing his jersey to reveal an undershirt with the sentence "Dani Jarque siempre con nosotros" ("Dani Jarque, always with us").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Two years later, after Spain conquered <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, Fàbregas again donned a T-shirt during the trophy celebrations to remember Jarque's death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Espanyol <a href="/wiki/Ciutat_Esportiva_Dani_Jarque" title="Ciutat Esportiva Dani Jarque">training ground and B team stadium</a> was also renamed in his memory.</p>
