<p><b>Daniele Portanova</b> (born 17 December 1978) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">footballer</a>, who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defender_(football)" title="Defender (football)">defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/S.S._Robur_Siena" title="S.S. Robur Siena">Robur Siena</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Portanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Portanova started his professional career at third division side <a href="/wiki/Fermana_F.C." title="Fermana F.C.">Fermana F.C.</a>, debuting in 1996 and leaving for then Serie B side <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</a>. He started the first four games of the season but managed just two more minutes of football for the rest of the season after that. This prompted him to leave Genoa for third division side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Avellino_1912" title="A.S. Avellino 1912">A.S. Avellino 1912</a> on loan for the 1999/2000 season. After a disappointing time at Avellino, where he managed just nine appearances, Genoa sold the young defender to then third division side <a href="/wiki/A.C.R._Messina" title="A.C.R. Messina">A.C.R. Messina</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Messina">Messina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Portanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Messina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Portanova immediately became a vital member of the Messina side which achieved promotion in the 2000/01 season to Serie B. He made 18 appearances in 2001/02, missing 15 rounds in the middle of the season due to an injury. The 2002/03 season was the best of Portanova's career to date, with the defender managing 34 appearances and even scored four goals for the side.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Napoli">Napoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Portanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Napoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His strong form the season before prompted southern heavyweights <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">S.S.C. Napoli</a> to make a move for the player. He made 31 appearances in his first and only season for the club as the side finished 14th in Serie B.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A.C._Siena">A.C. Siena</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Portanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: A.C. Siena">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daniele's continued good form in the second division of Italy finally led to a move to Serie A where he was picked up by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Siena" title="AC Siena">AC Siena</a>. He made 26 appearances and even managed four goals in the 2004/05 season, as the side finished 14th, comfortably clear of relegation. The next season, Daniele continued as a mainstay at Siena, making 32 appearances as the side finished 15th. In August 2009, Portanova was moved to <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Siena" title="AC Siena">AC Siena</a> after 162 appearances and 10 goals for Siena over five years. His departure was very much mourned by fans who came to love the player over his time at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bologna">Bologna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Portanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bologna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Portanova moved to Bologna in a swap deal as <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Terzi" title="Claudio Terzi">Claudio Terzi</a> moved the other way. He made 36 appearances in his debut season for the club, scoring his first goal in a 2-0 win over Livorno in round 5 of the 09/10 Serie A season. The next season he continued as a main part of the first team, scoring a single goal in 34 appearances, the winner in a 1-0 victory over Catania. The next season, Portanova's good form and leadership helped his side finish 9th in Serie A, despite struggling in recent seasons. He scored four goals from defence in 35 appearances including one in a 1-1 draw against Juventus.</p>
<p>Portanova was banned from football for four months in the summer of 2012 as a result of failing to report attempted match fixing. Notably, the Italian FA did not actually ban Portanova for match fixing or attempting to fix a football match, simply for failing to report a significant conversation the player overheard.</p>
<p>Portanova returned to action in round 17 as he faced former side Napoli. In a wonderful return to football, Daniele scored an 89th-minute winner as his struggling side managed an unlikely 3-2 win over the third placed Neapolitans.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Genoa">Return to Genoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Portanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Genoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Portanova returned to Genoa on the 30th of January, 2013, right on transfer deadline day, for an undisclosed fee. The Genovese club brought the player in with an attempt to turn around their season as they were hovering just above relegation. In the end, Portanova and company did just enough as Genoa finished six points clear of relegation. In the off season of 2013, Daniele was appointed as captain of his new club. He made 22 appearances that season, struggling towards the end of the campaign due to injuries and loss of form and was released by the Grifone at the end of the 2013/14 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Siena">Return to Siena</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Portanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Siena">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The player signed for Robur Siena after an extended time without a club. Siena were recently bankrupt and Daniele still signed, despite the club being in Serie D and offering him a very small wage.</p>
