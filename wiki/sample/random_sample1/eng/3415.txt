<p><b>Danijel Pranjić</b> (<small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[dǎnijel prǎːɲit͡ɕ]</a></span>; born 2 December 1981) is a Croatian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Koper" title="FC Koper">FC Koper</a>. Being a versatile left-footed player, he can play all across the left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defender_(football)#Wing-back" title="Defender (football)">wing</a> and can also be used as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central</a> midfielder.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danijel_Pranji%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pranjić started his career at <a href="/wiki/NK_Papuk_Orahovica" title="NK Papuk Orahovica">Papuk</a>. He played for <a href="/wiki/NK_NA%C5%A0K" title="NK NAŠK">NAŠK</a>, Papuk, <a href="/wiki/NK_Beli%C5%A1%C4%87e" title="NK Belišće">Belišće</a>, and <a href="/wiki/NK_Osijek" title="NK Osijek">Osijek</a> before making his move to a bigger stage at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NK_Dinamo_Zagreb" title="NK Dinamo Zagreb">Dinamo Zagreb</a> in 2004. While at Dinamo Zagreb, he also played regularly during their 2004–05 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> campaign, making six appearances and scoring one goal in the competition.</p>
<p>In 2005, he was transferred to Dutch <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> side <a href="/wiki/SC_Heerenveen" title="SC Heerenveen">SC Heerenveen</a>. His good performances as an offensive winger secured him a return to international football with <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a> in 2007. He started the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Eredivisie" title="2008–09 Eredivisie">2008–09 Eredivisie</a> season well, netting four goals in his first three league matches. Pranjić scored his first <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">Dutch Cup</a> on 12 November 2008 in a huge 7–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Haaglandia" title="Haaglandia">Haaglandia</a>. On 31 January 2009, he made the lone goal in a Heerenveen victory at <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">AFC Ajax</a>. A month later, he scored a late penalty that gave Heerenveen a 3–2 win at <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>.</p>
<p>He won the Dutch Cup with Heerenveen at the end of the 2008–09 season, which turned out to be his last match for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In June 2009, <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> announced Pranjić would join the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He struggled to make an impact in his first season with the club, starting only 14 matches and losing out to first choice left-back <a href="/wiki/Holger_Badstuber" title="Holger Badstuber">Holger Badstuber</a>. Due to his utility and injuries to other teammates, however, he started an increasing number of games in his second season with the Bavarian club.</p>
<p>On 13 July 2012, after the expiration of his Bayern contract, Pranjić was announced as a new signing for Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> team <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Clube de Portugal</a>. He played nine games for <i>Os Leões</i> before joining Spanish <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> club <a href="/wiki/Celta_de_Vigo" title="Celta de Vigo">Celta de Vigo</a> in January 2013 on loan until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sporting_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 August 2013, Pranjić and Sporting terminated the contract in mutual agreement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 September 2013, it was officially announced that Pranjić had signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a> for three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He made his professional debut in a 1–1 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Platanias_F.C." title="Platanias F.C.">Platanias F.C.</a></p>
<p>On 11 September 2016 Pranjić joined Slovenian club <a href="/wiki/FC_Koper" title="FC Koper">FC Koper</a> on free transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koper_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-koper-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danijel_Pranji%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pranjić started his international career with the <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_under-21_football_team" title="Croatia national under-21 football team">Croatian national under-21 football team</a>, winning a total of 15 international caps and scoring one goal for the team between 2002 and 2004.</p>
<p>On 16 November 2004, Pranjić made his full international debut for <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a> in a friendly match against the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a>. After two years of absence from the national team, he was called up for a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a> in February 2007. Later that year, he also appeared in three <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2008 qualifiers</a>.</p>
<p>Asked to play a defensive role by Croatia coach <a href="/wiki/Slaven_Bili%C4%87" title="Slaven Bilić">Slaven Bilić</a>, although still predominantly a midfielder in his club career, Pranjić was the only Croatian to start all four games at the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> finals. Pranjić provided an assist to <a href="/wiki/Darijo_Srna" title="Darijo Srna">Darijo Srna</a> to score the opener in Croatia's 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> and an assist to <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Klasni%C4%87" title="Ivan Klasnić">Ivan Klasnić</a> to score the only goal in their next game, a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a>.</p>
<p>He kept his place as a regular in the national team during their unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">qualifying campaign for the 2010 FIFA World Cup</a>, appearing in a total of nine matches.</p>
<p>On 28 September 2012, Pranjić retired from the national team in protest to the call up of Brazilian-born player <a href="/wiki/Sammir" title="Sammir">Sammir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 November 2013, Kovač called up Pranjić to the national team after <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Strini%C4%87" title="Ivan Strinić">Ivan Strinić</a> sustained an injury that made him ineligible for the playoffs against Iceland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pranjić exceptional season year with <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos</a> was his passport to be called to the final 23-man squad for <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
