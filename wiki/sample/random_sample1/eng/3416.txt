<p><b>Daniel Munthe Agger</b> (<small>Danish: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Danish" title="Help:IPA for Danish">[d̥anjəl ˈɑɡ̊ɐ]</a></span>; born 12 December 1984) is a Danish retired professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tattoo" title="Tattoo">tattoo</a> <a href="/wiki/Tattoo_artist" title="Tattoo artist">artist</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8ndby_IF" title="Brøndby IF">Brøndby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and captained the <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark national team</a>. Agger was described as "a fine reader of the game, comfortable on the ball and blessed with a ferocious shot".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was the 2007 and 2012 <a href="/wiki/Danish_Football_Player_of_the_Year" title="Danish Football Player of the Year">Danish Football Player of the Year</a>.</p>
<p>He started his senior career with Brøndby in July 2004, winning the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Superliga" title="Danish Superliga">Danish Superliga</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Cup" title="Danish Cup">Danish Cup</a>, before moving to Liverpool in January 2006. He made 175 <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> appearances for the club (232 total) and won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/FA_Community_Shield" title="FA Community Shield">Community Shield</a>. Agger returned to Brøndby for personal reasons in August 2014, and retired two years later at the age of 31.</p>
<p>A full international since 2005, Agger earned 75 caps and scored 12 goals for Denmark. He represented the nation at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, captaining his country at the latter.</p>

<p>In July 2004, he was moved from the youth squad to the first team, following the departure of <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Swedish international</a> defender <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Jakobsson" title="Andreas Jakobsson">Andreas Jakobsson</a>. Agger quickly established himself not only as a first team regular, but as one of the key components in the team that won the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Danish_Superliga" title="2004–05 Danish Superliga">2004–05 Danish Superliga</a> championship. After the first half of the season Agger was named 2004 "talent of the year" by Spillerforeningen (the Danish equivalent of the <a href="/wiki/Professional_Footballers%27_Association" title="Professional Footballers' Association">English PFA</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfc_stats_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-lfc_stats-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>An injury sustained in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Danish_Superliga" title="2005–06 Danish Superliga">2005–06 Danish Superliga</a> season in September forced him out for the remainder of 2005.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On 6 December 2005 and at only 20 years of age, Agger was awarded the Danish "talent of the year" for all sports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfc_stats_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-lfc_stats-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9306_season">2005–06 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2005–06 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Agger was long linked to a transfer to a big club, and in the January 2006 <a href="/wiki/Transfer_window" title="Transfer window">transfer window</a>, he got his wish and was looking to join <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> team <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, then holders of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-echo_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-echo-6">[6]</a></sup> Agger did not travel with his Brøndby teammates for their winter training camp in January and on 12 January 2006, he signed a 4½ year contract with Liverpool. The <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>6,000,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transfer_deal" title="Transfer deal">transfer deal</a> made him the most expensive footballer sold by a Danish club to a foreign club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dr_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-dr-7">[7]</a></sup> During his first half-season at Liverpool, injuries limited Agger to four first-team appearances, and he missed the club's victory in the <a href="/wiki/2006_FA_Cup_Final" title="2006 FA Cup Final">2006 FA Cup Final</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9307_season">2006–07 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2006–07 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Daniel_Agger.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1532" data-file-width="2048" height="187" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Daniel_Agger.jpg/250px-Daniel_Agger.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Daniel_Agger.jpg/375px-Daniel_Agger.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Daniel_Agger.jpg/500px-Daniel_Agger.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Daniel_Agger.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Agger in action against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> in April 2007</div>

<p>Agger played the full game as Liverpool won the <a href="/wiki/2006_FA_Community_Shield" title="2006 FA Community Shield">2006 FA Community Shield</a> against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Still adapting to the English game, he established himself in Liverpool's first team squad, competing with Liverpool vice-captain <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Carragher" title="Jamie Carragher">Jamie Carragher</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Finland_national_football_team" title="Finland national football team">Finnish</a> team captain <a href="/wiki/Sami_Hyypi%C3%A4" title="Sami Hyypiä">Sami Hyypiä</a> for the two starting places in the central defence. Agger scored his first goal for Liverpool on 26 August 2006 in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>. Agger was allowed to run unchallenged toward the <a href="/wiki/Spion_Kop_(stadiums)" title="Spion Kop (stadiums)">Kop</a>-end goal and scored from 35 yards (32 m) out. Liverpool manager <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Ben%C3%ADtez" title="Rafael Benítez">Rafael Benítez</a> commented he was not surprised and that Agger has frequently scored such goals in training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfc_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-lfc-9">[9]</a></sup> The goal was named <a href="/wiki/Goal_of_the_Month_(England)" title="Goal of the Month (England)">Goal of the Month</a> for August by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Match_of_the_Day" title="Match of the Day">Match of the Day</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and later Liverpool's Premiership Goal of the Season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 4 October 2006, he was given the <a href="/wiki/Professional_Footballers%27_Association" title="Professional Footballers' Association">PFA's</a> fan award for impressive and solid displays in September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LFCProfile_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-LFCProfile-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agger scored his second goal for the club in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">Football League Cup</a> away game against <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> with a volley from close range during the seven minutes of first-half stoppage time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> His third was against Arsenal, scoring a header in a 4–1 victory played on 31 March 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In the first semi-final of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2006–07 UEFA Champions League">2006–07 Champions League</a>, Agger received some criticism as Liverpool lost to <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> 1–0, after Chelsea striker <a href="/wiki/Didier_Drogba" title="Didier Drogba">Didier Drogba</a> managed to dribble past him before making the assist to win the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-14">[14]</a></sup> In the second leg of the semi-finals, Agger answered his critics by scoring another curler from a smartly-taken <a href="/wiki/Steven_Gerrard" title="Steven Gerrard">Steven Gerrard</a> free-kick from just outside the penalty area, and helping Liverpool keep a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clean_sheet" title="Clean sheet">clean sheet</a> as the team beat Chelsea 1–0 on 1 May 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-14">[14]</a></sup> He went on to play in the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2007 UEFA Champions League Final">2007 UEFA Champions League Final</a> which Liverpool lost 2–1 to <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9308_season">2007–08 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2007–08 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Agger started the 2007 pre-season scoring two goals in four games, including a left footed-finish against Hong Kong outfit <a href="/wiki/South_China_AA" title="South China AA">South China</a>. However, he suffered a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metatarsal" title="Metatarsal">metatarsal</a> injury in September, again losing his place to Hyypiä.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In January 2008, Agger began training and was attempting to regain match fitness, but a recurrence of the injury in his second metatarsal again ruled him out of action.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> After being examined by several specialists, it was reported that Agger would miss the remainder of the season to undergo surgery on his foot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.9309_season">2008–09 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2008–09 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Agger returned to full training in the pre-season at Melwood and on 12 July 2008, Agger made his playing return as a substitute in Liverpool's friendly win over neighbours <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tranmere_Rovers" title="Tranmere Rovers">Tranmere Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Agger stated after the match: "I am almost starting my career at Liverpool all over again".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Agger was in the starting line up in Liverpool's first game of the season against Standard Liège in the Champions League qualifiers. The game ended 0–0. Agger then found himself sidelined the rest of August and much of September, and was not even included in the squad to face Manchester United. This was followed by rumours of a verbal exchange between Agger and Liverpool manager Benítez. However, Benítez insisted that there was no rift between Agger and himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a month without playing, Agger returned to the starting line up against <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a> in the League Cup. Agger played the full 90 minutes and marked his return by scoring the first goal from a free kick outside the box. Liverpool won the game 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:176px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Daniel_Agger_2011.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1876" data-file-width="1052" height="310" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Daniel_Agger_2011.jpg/174px-Daniel_Agger_2011.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Daniel_Agger_2011.jpg/261px-Daniel_Agger_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Daniel_Agger_2011.jpg/348px-Daniel_Agger_2011.jpg 2x" width="174"/></a>

<p>Profiting in part from Škrtel's injury, Agger returned to the starting line up against Wigan for his first Premier League match of the season. Agger was, however, at least partly to blame for Wigan taking the lead when he lost the ball to Zaki after a pass from goalkeeper Reina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_sport1_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_sport1-23">[23]</a></sup> Agger redeemed himself by setting up Dirk Kuyt for the equaliser.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_sport1_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_sport1-23">[23]</a></sup> Liverpool eventually won the match 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_sport1_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_sport1-23">[23]</a></sup> On 11 April 2009, Agger scored the third goal for Liverpool from 30 yards against Blackburn Rovers. Liverpool went on to win 3–0. Agger celebrated, like the others who had scored in the match, by pointing to the sky in memory of victims in the <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_disaster" title="Hillsborough disaster">Hillsborough disaster</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2009, Agger signed a new five-year deal with Liverpool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310_season">2009–10 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2009–10 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Agger underwent surgery for a recurring back injury in August 2009 and made his return on 25 October 2009 for the 2–0 win over rivals <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 28 February 2010, Agger played his 100th competitive game for Liverpool, playing in the 2–1 home win against <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Agger played another 14 times in 2009–10, including scoring his seventh goal for the club, an outstanding backheel, against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and also helped Liverpool reach the semi finals of the same competition.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311_season">2010–11 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2010–11 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:188px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Daniel_Agger_2011_Singapore.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="918" data-file-width="611" height="279" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Daniel_Agger_2011_Singapore.jpg/186px-Daniel_Agger_2011_Singapore.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Daniel_Agger_2011_Singapore.jpg/279px-Daniel_Agger_2011_Singapore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Daniel_Agger_2011_Singapore.jpg/372px-Daniel_Agger_2011_Singapore.jpg 2x" width="186"/></a>

<p>Agger appeared against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> on 15 August 2010, but got concussed in the second half of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> As a result of the concussion, he missed the next few games. He also had other injury problems which kept him out until December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Martin_%C5%A0krtel" title="Martin Škrtel">Martin Škrtel</a> was preferred rather than Agger under <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a> and he threatened to quit the club with <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> reportedly interested in him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> After the departure of Hodgson, he featured in most of the games under <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Dalglish" title="Kenny Dalglish">Kenny Dalglish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> However, Agger was ruled out for the remainder of the season, following a knee injury sustained in the match against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> on 2 March 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> At the end of the season, he had only managed 21 appearances for the Reds in all competitions.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312_season">2011–12 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2011–12 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 1 August 2011, he scored two goals in a pre-season friendly against <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A5lerenga_Fotball" title="Vålerenga Fotball">Vålerenga Fotball</a>, the first a powerful header from a corner, the second a half-volley, also from a corner. Agger returned from injury against <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> in a friendly match in which Liverpool lost 1–0. On 29 October 2011, Daniel Agger played his 100th Premier League game for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in a 0–2 win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion" title="West Bromwich Albion">West Bromwich Albion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:267px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Daniel_Agger_20111205.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2901" data-file-width="4343" height="177" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Daniel_Agger_20111205.JPG/265px-Daniel_Agger_20111205.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Daniel_Agger_20111205.JPG/398px-Daniel_Agger_20111205.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Daniel_Agger_20111205.JPG/530px-Daniel_Agger_20111205.JPG 2x" width="265"/></a>

<p>On 28 January 2012, Agger opened the scoring against Manchester United in the fourth round FA Cup tie, defeating them 2–1. It was his first Liverpool goal since 2010, when he scored in the Europa League tie against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a>. On 26 February 2012, he helped Liverpool to win their first trophy since 2006 as they won the <a href="/wiki/2012_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2012 Football League Cup Final">2012 Football League Cup Final</a> but injured his ribs during the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On 7 April 2012, Agger made his return from injury as a second-half substitute against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a> at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Enrique" title="José Enrique">José Enrique</a> and hitting the crossbar with a header late on which led to the equalizer in a 1–1 draw. On 8 May 2012, Agger scored his first Premier League goal of the season in a 4–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> which was also the last home game of the season for Liverpool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313_season">2012–13 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2012–13 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Agger made his first start of the league campaign in a 3–0 defeat to West Bromwich Albion on 18 August, in which he conceded a penalty and was sent off for a foul on <a href="/wiki/Shane_Long" title="Shane Long">Shane Long</a>. Although the penalty was saved, Liverpool were unable to hold on and slipped to a disappointing defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On 5 October, Agger signed a new long term contract with Liverpool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On 12 November, Agger was named 2012 Danish Player of the Year for the second time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On 1 December 2012, Agger scored against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> at Anfield, with a 43rd-minute header giving Liverpool a 1–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> On 30 December, Agger scored a beautiful header against QPR, assisted by a cross from captain Steven Gerrard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Agger made it 3–0 which also was the final score. Agger reached his 200th appearance for Liverpool on 21 February 2013 in a home Europe League tie against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zenit_St._Petersburg" title="Zenit St. Petersburg">Zenit St. Petersburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> With Liverpool trailing 2–0 from the first leg, the Reds recorded a 3–1 win at Anfield, but were eliminated on away goals.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Daniel_Agger_Liverpool.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2492" data-file-width="2081" height="240" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Daniel_Agger_Liverpool.jpg/200px-Daniel_Agger_Liverpool.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Daniel_Agger_Liverpool.jpg/300px-Daniel_Agger_Liverpool.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Daniel_Agger_Liverpool.jpg/400px-Daniel_Agger_Liverpool.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

Agger for Liverpool in 2014</div>

<p>On 9 August 2013, it was confirmed that Agger was to become Liverpool's vice-captain,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> replacing the retired <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Carragher" title="Jamie Carragher">Jamie Carragher</a>. This came shortly after reports had surfaced that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_F.C." title="Barcelona F.C.">Barcelona</a> had made a bid to sign the Dane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Agger assisted Daniel Sturridge's goal with a flick-on header in the 1–0 victory against Manchester United.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Agger did however, see his game time cut short due to <a href="/wiki/Mamadou_Sakho" title="Mamadou Sakho">Mamadou Sakho</a> being preferred in the same role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On 1 January 2014, Agger scored his first goal of the season, meeting a Philippe Coutinho corner with his head in a 2-0 win over Hull. During much of 2014, Agger was left out of the team, with Sakho partnering Skrtel in defence. Agger was restored to the starting line up on 11 May 2014 in Liverpool's end-of-season match at home to Newcastle. With the Reds trailing 1-0, Agger scored the equaliser by meeting a Steven Gerrard free kick at the back post.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Br.C3.B8ndby">Return to Brøndby</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Return to Brøndby">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 August 2014, Liverpool confirmed that Agger had rejoined his previous club Brøndby IF on a two-year deal, which involved a considerable pay cut, for a fee of £3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Despite offers in England and other European countries, he wanted to play in a country with a less physically demanding league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-retire-49">[49]</a></sup> He told Denmark's <a href="/wiki/TV3_(Denmark)" title="TV3 (Denmark)">TV3</a> that Liverpool manager <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Rodgers" title="Brendan Rodgers">Brendan Rodgers</a> was unappreciative of his efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-retire-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 June 2016, at the age of 31, Agger announced his retirement at Brøndby at the expiration of his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_49-2"><a href="#cite_note-retire-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NED-DEN_Euro_2012_(26).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="436" data-file-width="656" height="199" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/NED-DEN_Euro_2012_%2826%29.jpg/300px-NED-DEN_Euro_2012_%2826%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/NED-DEN_Euro_2012_%2826%29.jpg/450px-NED-DEN_Euro_2012_%2826%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/NED-DEN_Euro_2012_%2826%29.jpg/600px-NED-DEN_Euro_2012_%2826%29.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NED-DEN_Euro_2012_(26).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Agger rising for a header against the Netherlands at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a></div>

<p>Agger made his debut for Denmark on 2 June 2005, playing the full 90 minutes of a 1–0 friendly win over <a href="/wiki/Finland_national_football_team" title="Finland national football team">Finland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tampere" title="Tampere">Tampere</a>. Agger spent the next national team match on the bench before playing the full 4–1 friendly win on 17 August against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>, alongside Per Nielsen. On 6 September, he scored his first international goal in a 6–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a> in the nation's unsuccessful campaign to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>. In May 2006, he played 10 games and scored 3 goals for the <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_under-21_football_team" title="Denmark national under-21 football team">Danish under-21 national team</a>, for which he was selected to play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2006 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2006 European Under-21 Championship</a> tournament.</p>
<p>Agger scored on 2 June 2007 in a 3–3 home draw against rivals Sweden for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> qualification; the game was abandoned in the last minute for crowd misbehaviour and awarded to the Swedes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made eight appearances in qualification for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>, scoring once in a 3–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a>. In the finals in South Africa, Agger scored an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a> to open a 2–0 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> in the first game; <a href="/wiki/Christian_Poulsen" title="Christian Poulsen">Christian Poulsen</a> had headed the ball into him while trying to clear <a href="/wiki/Robin_van_Persie" title="Robin van Persie">Robin van Persie</a>'s cross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> He played the other two fixtures in a group stage exit.</p>
<p>Agger first captained Denmark on 11 August 2010 in a 2–2 home friendly draw with neighbours <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>. He was given the armband a year later as Poulsen suffered from stress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Under Agger's captaincy, the Danes qualified for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a> in Poland and Ukraine, and he was again a fixture in a group stage elimination.</p>
<p>On 15 October 2013, Agger scored two penalties in a 6–0 home win against the Maltese in <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> qualification, securing second place in their group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> The following 28 May, from the penalty spot in added time, he scored the only goal of a win over Sweden in a match celebrating the 125th anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Football_Association" title="Danish Football Association">Danish Football Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Denmark didn't qualify for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2016" title="Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a>, and so Agger revealed his intention to end his active playing career in 2016, which would also see his international retirement.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agger selected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volbeat_(band)" title="Volbeat (band)">Volbeat</a>'s <i>The Garden's Tale</i> as his choice for the "LFC Athens 2007 Playlist" in the buildup of Liverpool's <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2007 UEFA Champions League Final">2007 UEFA Champions League Final</a> appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2010, Agger married girlfriend Sofie Nielsen in Denmark. The couple wore matching white outfits for the wedding, which was followed by a reception at the Nimb Hotel in Copenhagen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> They have two sons.</p>
<p>In September 2014, Agger donated 20,000 kroner, about £2100, to the Danish homeless national football side to help pay for travel expenses so the team could make it to the <a href="/wiki/Homeless_World_Cup" title="Homeless World Cup">Homeless World Cup</a> in Chile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Agger_Foundation">The Agger Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: The Agger Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agger founded The Agger Foundation in 2012 aiming to help children in need.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tattoos">Tattoos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Agger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Tattoos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Daniel_Agger_YNWA_Tattoo.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="321" data-file-width="581" height="122" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Daniel_Agger_YNWA_Tattoo.jpg/220px-Daniel_Agger_YNWA_Tattoo.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Daniel_Agger_YNWA_Tattoo.jpg/330px-Daniel_Agger_YNWA_Tattoo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Daniel_Agger_YNWA_Tattoo.jpg/440px-Daniel_Agger_YNWA_Tattoo.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Daniel Agger has "YNWA" tattoed on his knuckles.</div>

<p>Outside of football, Agger is a qualified <a href="/wiki/Tattoo" title="Tattoo">tattoo</a> artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> He has numerous tattoos including a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Viking</a> on his upper right arm, a large drawing of Vikings and a graveyard that spans his whole back,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> which includes his birth date and the Latin motto "<a href="/wiki/List_of_Latin_phrases_(M)" title="List of Latin phrases (M)">Mors certa, hora incerta</a>" ("death is certain, its hour is uncertain"), forming a <a href="/wiki/Memento_mori" title="Memento mori">memento mori</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> He also features an intricate band around his left elbow which includes the phrase "Succes er at leve som man selv vil" ("Success is to live as you wish" in <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>). In August 2012, during a period of intense speculation regarding his future at the club, Agger had the letters "YNWA" tattooed on the fingers of his right hand, referencing the anthem of Liverpool F.C., "<a href="/wiki/You%27ll_Never_Walk_Alone" title="You'll Never Walk Alone">You'll Never Walk Alone</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
