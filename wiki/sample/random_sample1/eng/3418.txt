<p><b>Danilo Gabriel de Andrade</b>, or simply <b>Danilo</b> (also known as <b>ZiDanilo</b>) (born June 11, 1979 in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Gotardo" title="São Gotardo">São Gotardo</a>), is a Brazilian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Corinthians_Paulista" title="Sport Club Corinthians Paulista">Corinthians</a>.</p>
<p>Had some of his most successful years with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sao_Paulo_FC" title="Sao Paulo FC">Sao Paulo FC</a> that resulted in a move to <a href="/wiki/Kashima_Antlers" title="Kashima Antlers">Kashima Antlers</a>. At <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo FC</a> he was nicknamed Zidanilo (in homage to <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a>) and was known for his technique, playmaking abilities and goals that he scored. Returns to Brazil for playing in <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Corinthians_Paulista" title="Sport Club Corinthians Paulista">Sport Club Corinthians Paulista</a>, being also again national, continental and world champion. Danilo is one of the most important player of Corinthians team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><br/>
According to Alexandre Lozetti, a columnist for GloboEsporte.com, "<i>Danilo is the Greatest football player who has never played or been selected for a National Team</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
