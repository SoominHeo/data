<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danilo_Pereira_(Portuguese_footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Bissau" title="Bissau">Bissau</a>, Guinea-Bissau as the oldest child of Quinta Djata, a nurse, Pereira and his family moved to Portugal when he was five. He played his youth football with three clubs in the <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> area, finishing his formation with <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a>.</p>
<p>Benfica subsequently decided against signing Pereira to a professional contract, and Italian side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma F.C.</a> acquired him on a <a href="/wiki/Free_transfer_(association_football)" title="Free transfer (association football)">free transfer</a>. He spent the second half of the 2010–11 season on loan to <a href="/wiki/Aris_Thessaloniki_F.C." title="Aris Thessaloniki F.C.">Aris Thessaloniki F.C.</a> in Greece and, upon his return to the <i><a href="/wiki/Stadio_Ennio_Tardini" title="Stadio Ennio Tardini">Stadio Ennio Tardini</a></i>, made his first appearance in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> on 21 December 2011, coming on as a late <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> in a 3–3 home draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catania_Calcio" title="Catania Calcio">Catania Calcio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Eredivisie" title="2012–13 Eredivisie">2012–13</a> Pereira was loaned again, spending the campaign with <a href="/wiki/Roda_JC_Kerkrade" title="Roda JC Kerkrade">Roda JC Kerkrade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and being first-choice to help his team narrowly avoid relegation. On 1 August 2013 he broke all ties with Parma and returned to Portugal, joining <a href="/wiki/C.S._Mar%C3%ADtimo" title="C.S. Marítimo">C.S. Marítimo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 July 2015, after two seasons in <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a> as first-choice, Danilo signed a four-year contract with <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> also from the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a>, with a release clause of €40 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danilo_Pereira_(Portuguese_footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pereira chose to represent <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> internationally. He was part of the <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_under-20_football_team" title="Portugal national under-20 football team">under-20 side</a> that finished second at the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> playing all seven games (six complete) and scoring in the 2–0 semifinal win over <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-20_football_team" title="France national under-20 football team">France</a>.</p>
<p>Pereira made his senior international debut on 31 March 2015, replacing fellow debutant <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_Silva" title="Bernardo Silva">Bernardo Silva</a> after 62 minutes of an eventual 0–2 <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_national_football_team" title="Cape Verde national football team">Cape Verde</a> in <a href="/wiki/Estoril" title="Estoril">Estoril</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was also selected for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a> squad by manager <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Santos_(Portuguese_footballer)" title="Fernando Santos (Portuguese footballer)">Fernando Santos</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> scoring his first goal in the last warm-up game, a 7–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Estonia_national_football_team" title="Estonia national football team">Estonia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> on 8 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
