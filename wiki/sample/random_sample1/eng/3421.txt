<p><b>Dadih</b> (<a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a>: <span lang="id" xml:lang="id"><i>Dadih</i></span>), a traditional fermented <a href="/wiki/Milk" title="Milk">milk</a> popular among people of <a href="/wiki/West_Sumatra" title="West Sumatra">West Sumatra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, is made by pouring fresh <a href="/wiki/Raw_milk" title="Raw milk">raw</a> unheated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domestic_buffalo" title="Domestic buffalo">buffalo</a> milk into a <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a> tube capped with a <a href="/wiki/Banana_leaf" title="Banana leaf">banana leaf</a>, and allowing it to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fermentation_(food)" title="Fermentation (food)">ferment</a> spontaneously at room temperature for two days.</p>
<p>The milk is fermented by indigenous <a href="/wiki/Lactobacillales" title="Lactobacillales">lactic bacteria</a> of the buffalo milk. Its natural fermentation provides different strains of indigenous lactic bacteria involved in each fermentation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The natural indigenous lactic acid bacteria observed in dadih could be derived from the bamboo tubes, buffalo milk or banana leaves involved in milk fermentation.</p>
<p>Dadih is usually eaten for breakfast, mixed together with <i>ampiang</i> (traditional glutinous rice krispies) and <a href="/wiki/Palm_sugar" title="Palm sugar">palm sugar</a>. Dadih can also be eaten with hot rice and <a href="/wiki/Sambal" title="Sambal">sambal</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some studies on probiotic properties of indigenous strains isolated from dadih fermented milk demonstrated to exhibit antimutagenic, acid, and bile tolerance as well as antipathogenic properties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Natural wild strains isolated from dadih show inhibitory, competitive and displacing properties against pathogens, and they are promising candidates for future <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Probiotics" title="Probiotics">probiotics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Viable cells of <i><a href="/wiki/Lactobacillus_plantarum" title="Lactobacillus plantarum">Lactobacillus plantarum</a></i> strains from dadih as well as active metabolism play important roles in removing microcystin-LR, cyanobacterial toxin, and natural wild strain of <i>Lactobacillus plantarum</i> from dadih has the highest removal abilities as compared to the other commercial probiotic strains. This finding offers new and economical tools for decontaminating microcystin containing water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
