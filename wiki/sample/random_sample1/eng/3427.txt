<p><b>Dalian Yifang Football Club</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">大连一方足球俱乐部</span>) is a professional <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club that currently participates in the <a href="/wiki/China_League_One" title="China League One">China League One</a> division under licence from the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Football_Association" title="Chinese Football Association">Chinese Football Association</a> (CFA). The team is currently owened by Dalian Yifang Group Co. Ltd. based in <a href="/wiki/Dalian" title="Dalian">Dalian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a> and their home stadium is <a href="/wiki/Dalian_Sports_Center_Stadium" title="Dalian Sports Center Stadium">Dalian Sports Center Stadium</a> with a capacity of 61,000. The club was founded in September 20, 2009 by Dalian Aerbin Group Co. Ltd and started from the third tier of the Chinese football pyramid, the <a href="/wiki/China_League_Two" title="China League Two">China League Two</a>. Winning two consecutive league title in the 2nd and 3rd tier professional football leagues, they were promoted to the top tier in <a href="/wiki/2012_Chinese_Super_League" title="2012 Chinese Super League">2012 Chinese Super League</a> season where they experienced their highest ever placing of fifth in the same season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dalian_Yifang_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dalian_Aerbin_.282009_-_2015.29">Dalian Aerbin (2009 - 2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dalian_Yifang_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Dalian Aerbin (2009 - 2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 20, 2009, Dalian Aerbin Group Co. Ltd. established a new professional football club named Dalian Aerbin (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">大连阿尔滨</span>) and hired former <a href="/wiki/China_national_football_team" title="China national football team">Chinese</a> footballer <a href="/wiki/Li_Ming_(footballer,_born_1971)" title="Li Ming (footballer, born 1971)">Li Ming</a> to become the club manager. The club's name <b>Aerbin</b> comes from the <a href="/wiki/Manchu_language" title="Manchu language">Manchu language</a> meaning <i>a place with water</i>, which is also the name of a small town in Jinzhou District of Dalian where Dalian Aerbin Group Co. Ltd locates. They would soon move into the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dalian_University_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dalian University Stadium (page does not exist)">Dalian University Stadium</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Dalian_Development_Area" title="Dalian Development Area">Dalian Development Area</a> and bring in <a href="/wiki/Chi_Shangbin" title="Chi Shangbin">Chi Shangbin</a> as their co-manager and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Xianlu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sun Xianlu (page does not exist)">Sun Xianlu</a> as their Head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Starting in the third tier league, the club made their debut in <a href="/wiki/2010_China_League_Two" title="2010 China League Two">2010 China League Two</a> season. The club brought in established top tier <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a> players such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guo_Hui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guo Hui (page does not exist)">Guo Hui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chang_Lin" title="Chang Lin">Chang Lin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yang_Lin_(footballer)" title="Yang Lin (footballer)">Yang Lin</a>. The quality of these players helped the club win their regional division section and later the league title over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Songjiang" title="Tianjin Songjiang">Tianjin Songjiang</a> as the club won the championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the following season, the club hired its first foreign coach, Bulgarian manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Stankov_(manager)" title="Aleksandar Stankov (manager)">Aleksandar Stankov</a>. Dalian Aerbin F.C. set up a surprising winning streak and won the <a href="/wiki/2011_China_League_One" title="2011 China League One">2011 China League One</a> championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> With its meteoric rise to the Super League, the club decided to use the 30,775 seater <a href="/wiki/Jinzhou_Stadium" title="Jinzhou Stadium">Jinzhou Stadium</a> as its home stadium and shared it with their local rival <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dalian_Shide" title="Dalian Shide">Dalian Shide</a> as well as signing a more experienced manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chang_Woe-Ryong" title="Chang Woe-Ryong">Chang Woe-Ryong</a> who had previously succeeded in the Chinese Super League with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qingdao_Jonoon" title="Qingdao Jonoon">Qingdao Jonoon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The club initially struggled in the league and the club brought in <a href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Stanojevi%C4%87" title="Aleksandar Stanojević">Aleksandar Stanojević</a> as the head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> By July 11, 2012 Dalian Aerbin brought in former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_F.C." title="Barcelona F.C.">Barcelona F.C.</a> player <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seydou_Keita_(footballer)" title="Seydou Keita (footballer)">Seydou Keita</a> who departed on a free transfer from to ensure their position within the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Stanojevć managed the club successfully and Dalian Aerbin F.C. was actually the "champion" in the 2nd half of the league. The club finally ranked 5th in 2012.</p>
<p>On 30 November 2012, Aerbin Group acquired the local rival <a href="/wiki/Dalian_Shide_F.C." title="Dalian Shide F.C.">Dalian Shide F.C.</a> by taking on responsibility of their 330 million <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">RMB</a> debt after Dalian Shide's chairman <a href="/wiki/Xu_Ming" title="Xu Ming">Xu Ming</a> was arrested for bribing and corruption.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In hopes of bringing in a harmonious merger of the two teams, former Dalian Shide manager <a href="/wiki/Xu_Hong" title="Xu Hong">Xu Hong</a> was brought in for the start of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Chinese_Super_League" title="2013 Chinese Super League">2013 Chinese Super League</a>. However, after only 63 days in charge he had to resign after the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Football_Association" title="Chinese Football Association">Chinese Football Association</a> found that he manipulated a match while as a manager at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sichuan_Guancheng" title="Sichuan Guancheng">Sichuan Guancheng</a> and was given a 5-year suspension from all football activity, which forced Li Ming to start the season as their caretaker manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Chinese Football Association call off this merger according to the regulation, and decided that former Dalian Shide player shall join free market, while Dalian Aerbin could only sign them through normal transfer, 5 at most, instead of taking over the whole team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> This incident caused Aerbin to faced some serious financial problems, being unable to pay the salaries, bonuses, or even maintenance of the stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> At the end of <a href="/wiki/2014_Chinese_Super_League" title="2014 Chinese Super League">2014 Chinese Super League</a>, Aerbin was relegated to China League One.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dalian_Yifang_.282015_-_.29">Dalian Yifang (2015 - )</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dalian_Yifang_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dalian Yifang (2015 - )">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Back to the China League One, Dalian Aerbin sold some valuable players trying to cover the expense, but still couldn't afford the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 8 July 2015, Dalian Yifang Group Co. Ltd bought majority shares of the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This purchase is believed to be promoted by <a href="/wiki/Wang_Jianlin" title="Wang Jianlin">Wang Jianlin</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Wanda_Group" title="Wanda Group">Wanda Group</a>,which brought <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dalian_Wanda_F.C." title="Dalian Wanda F.C.">Dalian Wanda F.C.</a> to fame, and is a main shareholder of the Yifang Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The club fail to promote back to the first tier after finishing the 3rd place of the league in the 2015 season. They changed their name to Dalian Yifang F.C. (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">大连一方</span>) in December 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
