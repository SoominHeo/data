<p>The <b>2000 Summer Olympics medal table</b> is a list of <a href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Committee" title="National Olympic Committee">National Olympic Committees</a> ranked by the number of <a href="/wiki/Medal" title="Medal">medals</a> won during the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a>, held in Sydney, Australia, from 15 September to 1 October 2000. A total of 10,651 athletes from 199 nations (with <a href="/wiki/Individual_Olympic_Athletes_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Individual Olympic Athletes at the 2000 Summer Olympics">four individual athletes</a> from <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a>) competed in 300 events in 28 sports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympicsorg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-olympicsorg-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Athletes from 80 countries won at least one medal, leaving 119 countries without a medal. The United States won the most medals overall with 91, as well as the most gold (35) medals. Host nation Australia finished the Games with 58 medals overall (16 gold, 25 silver, and 17 bronze).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympicsorg_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-olympicsorg-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a> won a gold medal for the first time in their Olympic histories, while <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> won their first ever Olympic medals, a silver in <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">taekwondo</a>, a bronze in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">athletics</a>, a bronze in <a href="/wiki/Wrestling" title="Wrestling">wrestling</a> and a bronze in <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judo</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympicsorg_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-olympicsorg-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_medal_rankings" title="Olympic medal rankings">Olympic medal rankings</a></div>
<p>The ranking in this table is based on information provided by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympicsorg_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-olympicsorg-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ranking sorts by the number of gold medals earned by a country—in this context, an entity represented by a National Olympic Committee. The number of silver medals is taken into consideration next and then the number of bronze medals. If, after the above, countries are still tied, equal ranking is given and they are listed alphabetically.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_in_medal_standings">Changes in medal standings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Summer_Olympics_medal_table&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Changes in medal standings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the Games, <a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanian</a> gymnast <a href="/wiki/Andreea_R%C4%83ducan" title="Andreea Răducan">Andreea Răducan</a> won the gold in <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_artistic_individual_all-around" title="Gymnastics at the 2000 Summer Olympics – Women's artistic individual all-around">women's artistic individual all-around</a>, so she stripped her gold medal after she tested a banned positive substance. As so, her teammates <a href="/wiki/Simona_Am%C3%A2nar" title="Simona Amânar">Simona Amânar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maria_Olaru" title="Maria Olaru">Maria Olaru</a>, originally won silver and bronze, upgraded to gold and silver, respectively. While <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> gymnast <a href="/wiki/Liu_Xuan_(gymnast)" title="Liu Xuan (gymnast)">Liu Xuan</a> moved up to bronze.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since the closing of the 2000 Games, the medal results changed following a <a href="/wiki/Doping_at_the_Olympic_Games" title="Doping at the Olympic Games">doping scandal</a> involving five-time Olympic medal winner <a href="/wiki/Marion_Jones" title="Marion Jones">Marion Jones</a>. The American <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" title="Sprint (running)">sprinter</a> was stripped of her 1 gold and two bronze medals, by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a>, after confessing, on 5 October 2007, she had taken the <a href="/wiki/Anabolic_steroid" title="Anabolic steroid">anabolic steroid</a> <a href="/wiki/Tetrahydrogestrinone" title="Tetrahydrogestrinone">tetrahydrogestrinone</a> before competing in Sydney.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Along with the other four medals, the women's 100 metres gold medal has not yet been reallocated, because the presumed recipient, <a href="/wiki/Ekaterini_Thanou" title="Ekaterini Thanou">Ekaterini Thanou</a> of Greece, was given a two-year ban for missing a drug test just before the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> However, Jones' teammates on the relay teams had their medals reinstated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 August 2008, the International Olympic Committee stripped the gold medal from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="United States at the 2000 Summer Olympics">U.S.</a> men's 4x400-metre relay team after <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Pettigrew" title="Antonio Pettigrew">Antonio Pettigrew</a> admitted to taking <a href="/wiki/Erythropoietin" title="Erythropoietin">EPO</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_growth_hormone" title="Human growth hormone">human growth hormone</a>. On 21 July 2012, the IOC decided to reallocate the gold, silver and bronze medals to the teams from Nigeria, Jamaica and the Bahamas that finished behind the US team, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 February 2010, The <i><a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a></i> reported that one of the members of the Chinese Gymnastic team was found to be under the minimum age limit set for competition. The governing body of the event, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Gymnastics_Federation" title="International Gymnastics Federation">International Gymnastics Federation</a>, reported that it determined <a href="/wiki/Dong_Fangxiao" title="Dong Fangxiao">Dong Fangxiao</a> to be 14 during the 2000 Olympics. The minimum age for competition was 16. The IGF invalidated the results of the competition in relation to the disqualified athlete. On 28 April 2010, the International Olympic Committee formally stripped the Chinese team of its bronze medal in the team event. The United States, which originally placed fourth, was awarded the bronze.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 January 2013, U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyclist" title="Cyclist">cyclist</a> <a href="/wiki/Lance_Armstrong" title="Lance Armstrong">Lance Armstrong</a> was stripped of his <a href="/wiki/Cycling_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_road_time_trial" title="Cycling at the 2000 Summer Olympics – Men's road time trial">bronze medal</a> from the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOC" title="IOC">IOC</a> because of his involvement in <a href="/wiki/Doping_in_sport" title="Doping in sport">doping</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
