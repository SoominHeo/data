<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Darmstadt Madonna</i></h1>

<p>The <i><b>Darmstadt Madonna</b></i> (also known as the <i><b>Madonna of Jakob Meyer zum Hasen</b></i>) is an oil painting by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Holbein_the_Younger" title="Hans Holbein the Younger">Hans Holbein the Younger</a>. Completed in 1526 in <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a>, the work shows the Bürgermeister of Basel <a href="/wiki/Jakob_Meyer_zum_Hasen" title="Jakob Meyer zum Hasen">Jakob Meyer zum Hasen</a>, his first wife (who had died earlier), his current wife, and his daughter grouped around the Madonna and infant Jesus. The meaning of the two other male figures on the left side is, like the overall iconography of the image, not entirely clear. The image testified to the resolutely Catholic faith of the Bürgermeister, who actively opposed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reformation" title="Reformation">Reformation</a>.</p>
<p>The painting was influenced by Italian Renaissance religious painting, with elements of Netherlandish portrait painting. Earlier located in <a href="/wiki/Darmstadt" title="Darmstadt">Darmstadt</a>, hence its title, the work has been on temporary loan to the <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A4del" title="Städel">Städelschen Kunstinstitut</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_am_Main" title="Frankfurt am Main">Frankfurt am Main</a> since 2004 and until 2011. As of 2012, the painting is at display in the <i>Johanniterkirche</i> in <a href="/wiki/Schw%C3%A4bisch_Hall" title="Schwäbisch Hall">Schwäbisch Hall</a>.</p>
