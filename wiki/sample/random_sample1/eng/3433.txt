<p><b>Darío Cvitanich</b> (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: Dario Cvitanić; born 16 May 1984) is an Argentine <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">football striker</a> who will play for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami_F.C." title="Miami F.C.">Miami FC</a> in the upcoming <a href="/wiki/2016_North_American_Soccer_League_season" title="2016 North American Soccer League season">2016 NASL season</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Banfield">Banfield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Banfield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cvitanich started his career in <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Banfield" title="Club Atlético Banfield">Club Atlético Banfield</a>'s youth and made his debut for the club's first team at the age of 19 on 20 October 2003 in a 3–1 defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olimpo_de_Bah%C3%ADa_Blanca" title="Olimpo de Bahía Blanca">Olimpo de Bahía Blanca</a>. He scored a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> in a 4–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's Old Boys</a> on 27 April 2007. He played, and scored, for Banfield in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores_2007" title="Copa Libertadores 2007">Copa Libertadores 2007</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He also served as a loyal member of Banfield during his early youth years in Argentina. Just prior to leaving his childhood side, he excelled his final loyalty by scoring 13 times during Banfield's run at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007-08_in_Argentine_football#Torneo_Clausura_.28.22Closing.22_Tournament.29" title="2007-08 in Argentine football">Clausura 2008</a> tournament.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax">Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cvitanich signed for <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>, although he did not leave Banfield until the end of the 2008 season. He was labelled the successor to <a href="/wiki/Klaas-Jan_Huntelaar" title="Klaas-Jan Huntelaar">Klaas-Jan Huntelaar</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and scored his first goal against <a href="/wiki/NAC_Breda" title="NAC Breda">NAC Breda</a> on 12 December 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Following the match against NAC, Cvitanich scored his first brace and his first hat-trick in subsequent weeks against <a href="/wiki/De_Graafschap" title="De Graafschap">De Graafschap</a> and <a href="/wiki/ADO_Den_Haag" title="ADO Den Haag">ADO Den Haag</a>. He scored his first hat-trick of the 2009–10 season against NAC Breda.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Pachuca">Loan to Pachuca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Loan to Pachuca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cvitanich joined Mexican side <a href="/wiki/C.F._Pachuca" title="C.F. Pachuca">Pachuca</a> on a year's loan on 1 January 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He helped the Mexican side to win their fourth <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_CONCACAF_Champions_League" title="2009–10 CONCACAF Champions League">CONCACAF Champions League</a> title, and returned to <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> on 3 November 2010, helping the club win their 30th Dutch league title.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Boca_Juniors">Loan to Boca Juniors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Loan to Boca Juniors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cvitanich signed with <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> in July 2011, on a one-year loan deal. He scored his first goal for Boca on 28 August 2011, against San Lorenzo de Almagro. Cvitanich scored again in the last games of the tournament against Godoy Cruz, two goals against Banfield and one against All Boys. He won the 2011 Argentine Torneo Apertura with Boca Juniors, as well as help the team to place in the final of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Copa_Libertadores" title="2012 Copa Libertadores">2012 Copa Libertadores</a>, where they faced <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Corinthians_Paulista" title="Sport Club Corinthians Paulista">Corinthians</a> from Brazil. Cvitanich was also voted <a href="/wiki/2012_Copa_Libertadores" title="2012 Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a> player of the week, in the 10th week of the tournament in April.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="OGC_Nice">OGC Nice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: OGC Nice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 July 2012, Cvitanich joined French <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> outfit <a href="/wiki/OGC_Nice" title="OGC Nice">OGC Nice</a> on a three-year contract.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Pachuca">Return to Pachuca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Pachuca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 January 2015, Cvitanich joined Mexican <a href="/wiki/Liga_MX" title="Liga MX">Liga MX</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> club <a href="/wiki/C.F._Pachuca" title="C.F. Pachuca">Pachuca</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After having a second knee injury and as a consequence missed the rest of the tournament, Pachuca agreed to released him from the team and hired <a href="/wiki/Franco_Jara" title="Franco Jara">Franco Jara</a> as his replacement.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cvitanich was born in Argentina with distant Croatian heritage. He established himself as a quality footballer in his home country playing for Banfield in the Argentine Primera División. After much consideration he eventually pledged to play for the <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia national team</a>, stating that "such an opportunity only comes once and you must take it".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It became known on 14 January 2009 that he couldn't play for <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a>, because the <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> rules do not allow nationality to be taken from great grandparents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dar%C3%ADo_Cvitanich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cvitanich obtained a Croatian passport in August 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He has dual citizenship, holding both Argentinean and Croatian passports. The surname <b>Cvitanić</b> has his origin on the island of <a href="/wiki/Bra%C4%8D" title="Brač">Brač</a>. During the last century, every tenth citizen of the island was one of the family of the Cvitanić´s.</p>
