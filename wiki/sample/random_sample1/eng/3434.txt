<p>The <b>Tama Zoological Park</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">多摩動物公園</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tama Dōbutsukōen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Zoo" title="Zoo">zoo</a>, owned by the government of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolis" title="Tokyo Metropolis">Tokyo Metropolis</a>, and located in <a href="/wiki/Hino,_Tokyo" title="Hino, Tokyo">Hino, Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Tama Zoo was opened on May 5, 1958, originally as part of <a href="/wiki/Ueno_Zoo" title="Ueno Zoo">Ueno Zoo</a>. The zoo aims to use its large site (52 ha, compared to the 14.3 ha of <a href="/wiki/Ueno_Zoo" title="Ueno Zoo">Ueno Zoo</a>) to show its animals moving in a more free and natural environment.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_attractions">Major attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tama_Zoological_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Major attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asiatic_Garden">Asiatic Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tama_Zoological_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Asiatic Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt><a href="/wiki/Orangutan" title="Orangutan">Orangutan</a> Skywalk</dt>
<dd>Connects orangutan enclosures with towers and wire ropes. Total length of 150 metres.</dd>
<dt>Raptor Enclosure</dt>
<dd>A giant dome enclosure that enables birds of prey such as <a href="/wiki/White-tailed_eagle" title="White-tailed eagle">white-tailed eagles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golden_eagle" title="Golden eagle">golden eagles</a> to be viewed flying freely.</dd>
<dt><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malayan_Tapir" title="Malayan Tapir">Malayan Tapir</a> Enclosure</dt>
<dd>Enables visitors to view Malayan tapirs swimming via a glass window.</dd>
<dt><a href="/wiki/Mole_(animal)" title="Mole (animal)">Mole</a> House</dt>
<dd>A man-made mole burrow made of glass-covered earth and metal tunnels. Also exhibits the <i>Sorex minutissimus hawkeri</i>, a subspecies of the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_least_shrew" title="Eurasian least shrew">Eurasian least shrew</a> that is the smallest <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammal</a> found in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</dd>
<dt>Asian Swampland</dt>
<dd>Reproduces the habitats of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_rhinoceros" title="Indian rhinoceros">Indian rhinoceros</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_water_buffalo" title="Asian water buffalo">Asian water buffalo</a>, and various wading birds.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="African_Garden">African Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tama_Zoological_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: African Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Lion Bus</dt>
<dd>Enables visitors to view <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lions</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Safari" title="Safari">safari</a> atmosphere.</dd>
<dt>Savannah</dt>
<dd>A large enclosure featuring <a href="/wiki/Giraffe" title="Giraffe">giraffes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grevy%27s_zebra" title="Grevy's zebra">Grevy's zebras</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scimitar-horned_oryx" title="Scimitar-horned oryx">scimitar-horned oryx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ostrich" title="Ostrich">ostriches</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pelican" title="Pelican">pelicans</a>.</dd>
<dt><a href="/wiki/Chimpanzee" title="Chimpanzee">Chimpanzee</a> Forest</dt>
<dd>Includes a 15-metre-high tower and vending machine, to showcase the athletic and mental abilities of the <a href="/wiki/Chimpanzee" title="Chimpanzee">chimpanzee</a>.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_Garden">Australian Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tama_Zoological_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Australian Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Koala House</dt>
<dd>Exhibits marsupials such as <a href="/wiki/Sugar_glider" title="Sugar glider">sugar gliders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Common_ringtail_possum" title="Common ringtail possum">ringtail possums</a>, in addition to <a href="/wiki/Koala" title="Koala">koalas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wombat" title="Wombat">wombats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emu" title="Emu">emus</a>.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Insectarium">Insectarium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tama_Zoological_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Insectarium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Main Building</dt>
<dd>A building modeled after a <a href="/wiki/Dragonfly" title="Dragonfly">dragonfly</a>, which contains exhibits relating to the life of insects.</dd>
<dt>Butterfly House</dt>
<dd>A large greenhouse that visitors can enter to observe various species of <a href="/wiki/Butterfly" title="Butterfly">butterflies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grasshopper" title="Grasshopper">grasshoppers</a> all year round.</dd>
</dl>
