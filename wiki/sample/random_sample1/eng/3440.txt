<p>The <b>Dabie Mountains</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">大别山</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Dàbié Shān</span></i>) are a major mountain range located in central <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. Running northwest-to-southeast, they form the main watershed between the <a href="/wiki/Huai_River" title="Huai River">Huai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yangtze" title="Yangtze">Yangtze</a> rivers. The range also marks the boundary between <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> Province and its neighbors of <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> (to the north) and <a href="/wiki/Anhui" title="Anhui">Anhui</a> (to the east).</p>
<p>The western part of Dabie Mountains has a low elevation of only 1,000–1,300 feet (300–400 meters), though there are a few peaks rising to 3,000 feet (900 meters). The eastern part is higher, averaging more than 3,300 feet (1,000 meters). The highest peak is <a href="/wiki/Mount_Tianzhu" title="Mount Tianzhu">Mount Tianzhu</a> at 5,820 feet (1,774 meters), with several others topping 5,000 feet (1,500 meters) including the 1,729.13 metres (5,673.0 ft) high <a href="/wiki/Tiantangzhai" title="Tiantangzhai">Tiantangzhai</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landscape">Landscape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dabie_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Landscape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG/220px-Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG/330px-Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG/440px-Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dabie_Mountains_landscape_-_P1050200.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Dabie Mountains in <a href="/wiki/Jinzhai_County" title="Jinzhai County">Jinzhai County</a>, Anhui, seen from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hewu_Railway" title="Hewu Railway">Hewu Railway</a></div>
</div>

<p>The range is heavily forested (about 85% coverage) and yields valuable <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Cork_(material)" title="Cork (material)">cork</a> oak, making it China's chief cork-producing area. The region is very poor, and subsistence agriculture is the heart of its economy with rice and tea predominating.</p>
<p>The main transportation route across the higher range is from <a href="/wiki/Macheng" title="Macheng">Macheng</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huangchuan" title="Huangchuan">Huangchuan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>, through the <a href="/wiki/Huai_River" title="Huai River">Huai River</a> valley. The main rail and road crossings go north from <a href="/wiki/Wuhan" title="Wuhan">Wuhan</a>, through the lower elevations.</p>
<p>In 2009, the high-speed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hewu_Passenger_Railway" title="Hewu Passenger Railway">Hewu Passenger Railway</a> opened, connecting <a href="/wiki/Hefei" title="Hefei">Hefei</a> (capital of Anhui) with <a href="/wiki/Wuhan" title="Wuhan">Wuhan</a> (capital of Hubei) along a short direct route. It uses a number of tunnels when crossing the Dabie range.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dabie_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Geologically, the range is a complex, stressed structure, making it and the surrounding area subject to earthquakes. In late 2005, a 5.7 magnitude earthquake centered in the northwest corner of <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> province, just south of <a href="/wiki/Jiujiang" title="Jiujiang">Jiujiang</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Yangtze" title="Yangtze">Yangtze</a> killed at least 15 people, injured hundreds, and left several thousand people homeless. It caused tremors in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qichun" title="Qichun">Qichun</a> County in <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> province and beyond, even reaching as far as the provincial capital of <a href="/wiki/Wuhan" title="Wuhan">Wuhan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Forest_Park">International Forest Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dabie_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International Forest Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This scenic park centered on Tiantangzhai is a key tourist attraction in the highest part of the Dabie mountain range. Total area of the park is 300 square kilometers. Located in <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> province's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luotian" title="Luotian">Luotian</a> County and close to the border with Anhui Province, the park is about 68 kilometers from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luotian" title="Luotian">Luotian</a> town, 140 kilometers from <a href="/wiki/Macheng" title="Macheng">Macheng</a>, 150 kilometers from <a href="/wiki/Huangshi" title="Huangshi">Huangshi</a>, and 210 kilometers from the Hubei provincial capital, <a href="/wiki/Wuhan" title="Wuhan">Wuhan</a>.</p>
