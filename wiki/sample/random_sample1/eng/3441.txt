<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davide_Gavazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Sondrio" title="Sondrio">Sondrio</a>, Lombardy, Gavazzi started his career at Lombard team <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Como" title="Calcio Como">Como</a>. He was the member of the reserve in 2003–04<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to 2004–05 season. The club bankrupted in 2005 and a new company was admitted to <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Serie_D" title="2005–06 Serie D">2005–06 Serie D</a>. Gavazzi joined the new club and played for 2½ seasons. Gavazzi won <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Serie_D" title="2007–08 Serie D">2007–08 Serie D</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2007%E2%80%9308_Coppa_Italia_Serie_D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2007–08 Coppa Italia Serie D (page does not exist)">2007–08 Coppa Italia Serie D</a>. However Gavazzi left the club in mid-season, for <a href="/wiki/A.C._Renate" title="A.C. Renate">Renate</a>. Renate was the losing semi-finalists of 2009 promotion playoffs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vicenza">Vicenza</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davide_Gavazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Vicenza">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2009 Gavazzi was signed by Serie B club <a href="/wiki/Vicenza_Calcio" title="Vicenza Calcio">Vicenza Calcio</a>.</p>
<p>On 24 August 2012 he took no.7 shirt previously worn by <a href="/wiki/Pasquale_Maiorino" title="Pasquale Maiorino">Pasquale Maiorino</a>, who changed to no.11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sampdoria">Sampdoria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davide_Gavazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sampdoria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 7 September 2012, Gavazzi was signed by Serie A club <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">U.C. Sampdoria</a> for €1.2 million in 4-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Vicenza acquired <a href="/wiki/Franco_Semioli" title="Franco Semioli">Franco Semioli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marco_Padalino" title="Marco Padalino">Marco Padalino</a> outright for free, as well as the loan of Gavazzi and <a href="/wiki/Zsolt_Laczk%C3%B3" title="Zsolt Laczkó">Zsolt Laczkó</a>. In January 2013 Vicenza released Gavazzi from loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Gavazzi played 7 times for Sampdoria in Serie A, all in <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Serie_A" title="2013–14 Serie A">2013–14 season</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ternana_.28loan.29">Ternana (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davide_Gavazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ternana (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 January 2014, he was loaned to Serie B side <a href="/wiki/Ternana_Calcio" title="Ternana Calcio">Ternana Calcio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 August 2014 the loan was renewed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Avellino">Avellino</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Davide_Gavazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Avellino">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 July 2015 he was signed by <a href="/wiki/U.S._Avellino_1912" title="U.S. Avellino 1912">Avellino</a> in a 3-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
