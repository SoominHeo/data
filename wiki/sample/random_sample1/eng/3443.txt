<p><b>David F. Sandberg</b> (born 1981) is a Swedish <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">filmmaker</a>. He is best known for his collective <a href="/wiki/No_budget_film" title="No budget film">no-budget</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror</a> <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short films</a> under his "ponysmasher" alias, and for his 2016 debut <i><a href="/wiki/Lights_Out_(2016_film)" title="Lights Out (2016 film)">Lights Out</a></i>, based on his 2013 acclaimed horror short <a href="/wiki/Lights_Out_(2013_film)" title="Lights Out (2013 film)">of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toro_.27Lig_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-toro_.27Lig-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_and_life">Career and life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_F._Sandberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career and life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before <i>Lights Out</i>, Sandberg was a freelance animator and maker of documentary and short films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sandberg20160809_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sandberg20160809-2">[2]</a></sup> He was deeply in debt in 2013, and said the short film <i><a href="/wiki/Lights_Out_(2013_film)" title="Lights Out (2013 film)">Lights Out</a></i> changed his life in "every way imaginable". Sandberg released his <a href="/wiki/Lights_Out_(2016_film)" title="Lights Out (2016 film)">feature film of the same name</a> in 2016 based on his 2013 film and received critical praise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lati_HowD_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-lati_HowD-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forb_.27Lig_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-forb_.27Lig-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is set to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Annabelle_2" title="Annabelle 2">Annabelle 2</a></i>, the sequel to 2014 supernatural horror film, <i><a href="/wiki/Annabelle_(film)" title="Annabelle (film)">Annabelle</a></i>, set to be released in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-holl_HowN_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-holl_HowN-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pare_.E2.80.98Ann_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-pare_.E2.80.98Ann-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sandberg stars <a href="/wiki/Lotta_Losten" title="Lotta Losten">Lotta Losten</a> in many of his short films, including <i>Lights Out</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worl_Ligh_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-worl_Ligh-7">[7]</a></sup> They married in 2013.</p>
