<p><b>David di Elias Alemu Tommaso</b> (October 6, 1979 – November 29, 2005) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Di_Tommaso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Di Tommaso was born in <a href="/wiki/%C3%89chirolles" title="Échirolles">Échirolles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Is%C3%A8re" title="Isère">Isère</a>. His father <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Di_Tommaso" title="Pascal Di Tommaso">Pascal Di Tommaso</a> and uncle <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Di_Tommaso&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Louis Di Tommaso (page does not exist)">Louis Di Tommaso</a> both played in <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grenoble_Foot_38" title="Grenoble Foot 38">Grenoble Foot 38</a> in the 1980s. His younger brother <a href="/wiki/Yohan_Di_Tommaso" title="Yohan Di Tommaso">Yohan Di Tommaso</a> is also a professional footballer.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Di_Tommaso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He started his career at French side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AS_Monaco" title="AS Monaco">AS Monaco</a>, where he helped the club win the 2000 <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">league</a> title. He then played in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> and was selected for the French national youth football team. In 2001, di Tommaso transferred to <a href="/wiki/CS_Sedan_Ardennes" title="CS Sedan Ardennes">Sedan</a>, which sold him to <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Utrecht" title="FC Utrecht">FC Utrecht</a> in 2004. In his first season there, the fans named him the club's player of the year.</p>
<p>His last match was on November 27, a 1–0 victory against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ajax_Amsterdam" title="Ajax Amsterdam">Ajax Amsterdam</a>, one of FC Utrecht's main rivals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nlll_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nlll-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Di_Tommaso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Di Tommaso died after a <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrest" title="Cardiac arrest">cardiac arrest</a> in his sleep at the age of 26 in <a href="/wiki/De_Meern" title="De Meern">De Meern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. He is survived by his wife and son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcs_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcs-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_his_death">After his death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Di_Tommaso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: After his death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 1, a meeting of supporters at <a href="/wiki/Stadion_Galgenwaard" title="Stadion Galgenwaard">Stadion Galgenwaard</a> was held, to honour Di Tommaso (DiTo for short). At least 14,000 fans showed up. Among the speakers were chairman <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Willem_van_Dop&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jan Willem van Dop (page does not exist)">Jan Willem van Dop</a>, coach <a href="/wiki/Foeke_Booy" title="Foeke Booy">Foeke Booy</a> and captain <a href="/wiki/Jean-Paul_de_Jong" title="Jean-Paul de Jong">Jean-Paul de Jong</a>. Besides Di Tommaso's relatives and FC Utrecht's main squad, all of the clubs' youth teams were among the visitors. They were told that number 4, the number worn by Di Tommaso, would be retired from use. Sedan, Di Tommaso's former club, had already announced that number 29, the number Di Tommaso used when he played for the club, would be retired.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Di_Tommaso_Trophy">Di Tommaso Trophy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Di_Tommaso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Di Tommaso Trophy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2006, FC Utrecht's player of the year award is called the Di Tommaso trophy in honour of David Di Tommaso. The trophy is voted for by the fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
