<p><b>David Mateos Ramajo</b> (born 22 April 1987) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for American club <a href="/wiki/Orlando_City_SC" title="Orlando City SC">Orlando City SC</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Mateos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_Madrid">Real Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Mateos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Real Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, Mateos joined <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>'s youth system at the age of 12. In the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2007–08 Segunda División B">2007–08 season</a> he made his senior debuts, playing that and the following two years with <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_Castilla" title="Real Madrid Castilla">the B-team</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">third division</a>.</p>
<p>In late January 2010, Mateos was called to the main squad by <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Pellegrini" title="Manuel Pellegrini">Manuel Pellegrini</a> for a <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_de_La_Coru%C3%B1a" title="Deportivo de La Coruña">Deportivo de La Coruña</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 3 April of the same year, he also made the final list for the game against <a href="/wiki/Racing_de_Santander" title="Racing de Santander">Racing de Santander</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but failed to leave <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">the bench</a> on either occasion.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_La_Liga" title="2010–11 La Liga">2010–11</a> pre-season, Real Madrid's first-team coach, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a>, said:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>It's a shame Mateos can't join us because I like him a lot. He defends well and knows how to instigate play from the back. That's a trait which we need here at Madrid. With the difficulties we have it is a pity that he can not play, I would like him to be part of the first team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The coach's statements instigated Real Madrid to sign the player to a professional contract<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and, subsequently, Mateos was called for the first match of the season against <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After the departure of <a href="/wiki/Rafael_van_der_Vaart" title="Rafael van der Vaart">Rafael van der Vaart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royston_Drenthe" title="Royston Drenthe">Royston Drenthe</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> and <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9rcules_CF" title="Hércules CF">Hércules CF</a> respectively, he was promoted to the first team, being awarded the number 15 shirt previously worn by the latter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mateos made his official debut for Real Madrid on 23 November 2010, against <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">AFC Ajax</a> in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Champions League">the campaign</a>'s <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, playing ten minutes in the 4–0 away win. After the match, he said: "I will never forget this day."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 January 2011, <a href="/wiki/AEK_Athens_F.C." title="AEK Athens F.C.">AEK Athens F.C.</a> signed Mateos on a six-month loan contract, with the <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> side having an option to retain the player for an extra year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the summer, however, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Real_Zaragoza" title="Real Zaragoza">Real Zaragoza</a> in the same predicament,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> making his top flight debut on 11 September in a 0–0 draw at <a href="/wiki/Rayo_Vallecano" title="Rayo Vallecano">Rayo Vallecano</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but having <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_La_Liga" title="2011–12 La Liga">the season</a> marred by injuries overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late June 2012, Mateos accepted a return to Castilla for <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2012–13 Segunda División">2012–13</a>, after the reserves <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2011–12 Segunda División B">promoted</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">second level</a> after an absence of five years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ferencv.C3.A1ros">Ferencváros</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Mateos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ferencváros">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 September 2013, Mateos signed for <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> club <a href="/wiki/Ferencv%C3%A1rosi_TC" title="Ferencvárosi TC">Ferencvárosi TC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He scored two goals in 21 games in <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Nemzeti_Bajnoks%C3%A1g_I" title="2013–14 Nemzeti Bajnokság I">his first season</a>, helping his team to the third position.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Orlando_City">Orlando City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Mateos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Orlando City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 July 2015, Mateos joined <a href="/wiki/Orlando_City_SC" title="Orlando City SC">Orlando City SC</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>, with <a href="/wiki/General_manager" title="General manager">general manager</a> Paul McDonough describing him as a "well-versed" player who he expected to "make an impact right away".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
