<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>Álvarez</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Aguirre</i>.</div>
<p><b>David Álvarez Aguirre</b> (born 5 February 1984), known as <b>Kily</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatoguinean</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for Spanish club La Madalena Morcín mainly as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Wide_midfielder" title="Midfielder">right midfielder</a> but also as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kily_%C3%81lvarez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kily was born in <a href="/wiki/Avil%C3%A9s" title="Avilés">Avilés</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_people" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> mother and an <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatoguinean</a> father, never knowing the latter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LNE_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-LNE-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kily_%C3%81lvarez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After finishing his formation with the club, Kily started playing with <a href="/wiki/Real_Oviedo" title="Real Oviedo">Real Oviedo</a>, having the honour of scoring its first-ever goal in <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">Tercera División</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LNE_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-LNE-1">[1]</a></sup> He spent his entire career in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_football_league_system" title="Spanish football league system">Spanish lower leagues</a>, also representing <a href="/wiki/UP_Langreo" title="UP Langreo">UP Langreo</a> (two spells),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_B" title="Atlético Madrid B">Atlético Madrid B</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Orihuela_CF" title="Orihuela CF">Orihuela CF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Novelda_CF" title="Novelda CF">Novelda CF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marino_de_Luanco" title="Marino de Luanco">Marino de Luanco</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/SD_Noja" title="SD Noja">SD Noja</a>, <a href="/wiki/CD_Cudillero" title="CD Cudillero">CD Cudillero</a> and <a href="/wiki/L%27Entregu_CF" title="L'Entregu CF">L'Entregu CF</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kily_%C3%81lvarez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kily made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea_national_football_team" title="Equatorial Guinea national football team">Equatorial Guinea</a> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was selected in the squad that appeared at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2012 Africa Cup of Nations">2012 Africa Cup of Nations</a> on home soil,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> where on 25 January he scored arguably the country's most important goal, netting in the <a href="/wiki/Last-minute_goal" title="Last-minute goal">90th minute</a> for a 2–1 group stage win against <a href="/wiki/Senegal_national_football_team" title="Senegal national football team">Senegal</a>, which meant the hosts qualified to the quarter-finals at the expense of their opponents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
