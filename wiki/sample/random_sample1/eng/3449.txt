<p><b>David Marcelo Pizarro Cortés</b> (born 11 September 1979) is a Chilean professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. He is a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">centre midfielder</a>, who can operate in a holding position or an attacking position in the hole behind the strikers. Pizarro is known for his vision, passing, dribbling skills and set piece ability.</p>
<p>During Pizarro's stay in Italian football, he won one <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> title (<a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Serie_A" title="2005–06 Serie A">2005–06</a>), three <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> titles and two <a href="/wiki/Supercoppa_Italiana" title="Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a> titles. His Italian nickname is "<i>Pek</i>", diminutive of "pequeño", meaning "small" in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, because of his short height (1.68 meters).</p>
<p>He plays for the <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile national team</a>, for which he won the Bronze medal at <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a>, and played at two <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa América</a>. He made his full debut in 1999, playing at <a href="/wiki/1999_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1999 Copa América">that year's</a> <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa América</a>, and was part of the Chilean squad which won its first ever tournament in <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">2015</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_.2F_Inter">Early career / Inter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Pizarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career / Inter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pizarro started his career at <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Wanderers" title="Santiago Wanderers">Santiago Wanderers</a> in his homeland <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valpara%C3%ADso,_Chile" title="Valparaíso, Chile">Valparaíso</a>. After a season playing at <i>Seaport Team</i> since his promotion to first-adult team in 1997, he was transferred to Italy’s <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese Calcio</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>. In 2001 he was loaned to Chilean powerhouse <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_de_Chile_(football_club)" title="Universidad de Chile (football club)">Universidad de Chile</a> for gain experience. Once back in <a href="/wiki/Friuli" title="Friuli">Friuli</a> he settled as the team starter central midfielder, performing well during his five seasons there.</p>
<p>On 14 July 2005, Pizarro was transferred to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">F.C. Internazionale Milano</a> for a reported fee of €10 million (plus half of <a href="/wiki/Goran_Pandev" title="Goran Pandev">Goran Pandev</a> rights, which Lazio bought a year later for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%E2%82%AC" title="€">€</a>4m) on a four-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> There, not repeating his successful campaigns at Udinese and being overshadowed Argentine star <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Ver%C3%B3n" title="Juan Sebastián Verón">Juan Sebastián Verón</a>, won a <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Serie_A" title="2005–06 Serie A">league title</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Supercoppa_Italiana" title="Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roma">Roma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Pizarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 19 August 2006, <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">A.S. Roma</a> acquired Pizarro's 50% transfer rights and they reached four-year deal in a €6.5 million transfer fee in a co-ownership deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On his arrival he chose the number 7 shirt and reunited with <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese Calcio</a> coach <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Spalletti" title="Luciano Spalletti">Luciano Spalletti</a>.</p>
<p>On 12 September 2006, Pizarro scored his first competitive goal for the club in a 4–0 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League_2006-07" title="UEFA Champions League 2006-07">Champions League</a> victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaktar_Donetsk" title="Shaktar Donetsk">Shaktar Donetsk</a> at 89th minute. Five days later, he netted a twice in a 3–1 <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> won over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Siena" title="AC Siena">AC Siena</a>. He helped Roma win the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Coppa_Italia" title="2006–07 Coppa Italia">2006–07 Coppa Italia</a> title where was one of the team top-scorers with three goals. On 20 June 2007, Roma announced that they paid the rest of 50% Pizarro rights to Inter to remain him at <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>–based team until 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The next season he was an undisputed starter with <a href="/wiki/Daniele_De_Rossi" title="Daniele De Rossi">Daniele De Rossi</a> on midfield, making over thirty league appearances as well as in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007-08_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2007-08 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> where he scored a goal in a 2–1 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid" title="Real Madrid">Real Madrid</a> to seal the team’s advance to quarterfinals. Nevertheless, during the season Pizarro once again won the <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a>.</p>
<p>In October 2009, Pizarro signed another contract extension that originally would keep him in the team until June 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He finished the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Serie_A" title="2009–10 Serie A">2009–10 season</a> with two goals and eight assists in 31 appearances, helping Roma finish second in Serie A behind eventual winners Inter.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_City">Manchester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Pizarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manchester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Pizarro signed on loan for <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> from Roma on 31 January 2012 until the end of the season, where manager <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mancini" title="Roberto Mancini">Roberto Mancini</a> had previously managed Pizarro at Inter Milan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He made his debut four days later as an added-time substitute for <a href="/wiki/Adam_Johnson_(footballer)" title="Adam Johnson (footballer)">Adam Johnson</a> in City's 3–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>, becoming the first Chilean to play for Manchester City. On 22 February, he came off the bench to score and had an assist against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Porto" title="F.C. Porto">Porto</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Europa_League" title="Europa League">Europa League</a> as City won 4–0 in the second leg of the tie, having won 2–1 in the away leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 May 2012, Manchester City were crowned Premier League champions for the 2011–12 season after beating <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Pizarro made five league appearances that season, which was not enough for a medal. Pizzaro loan spell with Manchester City came to an end and returned to Roma, where he vowed not to move again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiorentina">Fiorentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Pizarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fiorentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 August 2012, Pizarro transferred to <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and signed a two-year deal with the club. Pizarro played 83 times for Fiorentina, and scored four goals.</p>
<p>He was an unused substitute in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2014 Coppa Italia Final">2014 Coppa Italia Final</a>, which Fiorentina lost 3–1 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSC_Napoli" title="SSC Napoli">Napoli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Santiago_Wanderers">Santiago Wanderers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Pizarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Santiago Wanderers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He decided to return to Chile with <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Wanderers" title="Santiago Wanderers">Santiago Wanderers</a> on a two-year contract.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Pizarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Pizarro began his international career in 1999. After being one of the best players of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_American_Youth_Championship" title="South American Youth Championship">South American Youth Championship</a> that year, <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Acosta" title="Nelson Acosta">Nelson Acosta</a> gave him an opportunity in the senior team.</p>
<p>He played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999_Copa_America" title="1999 Copa America">1999 Copa America</a> and also the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a>, where he won the bronze medal. He also participated in the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2002 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">2002</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2006 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">2006</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Cup_qualifiers" title="World Cup qualifiers">World Cup qualifiers</a>, with Chile failing to qualify on both occasions.</p>
<p>After failing to qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> in Germany, Pizarro announced his retirement from international football, listing several reasons for his decision. He disliked ex-coach <a href="/wiki/Juvenal_Olmos" title="Juvenal Olmos">Juvenal Olmos</a> and ex-association president Reinaldo Sánchez.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Also, he believed the players on the national team played solely to gain popularity. He also said he wished to spend more time with his family.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 20 May 2013 he was called up to the national team for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">2014 World Cup qualifiers</a>, returning to the Chile national squad after having meetings with head coach <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Sampaoli" title="Jorge Sampaoli">Jorge Sampaoli</a>. Thus ending an eight-year absence from international football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Then after failing to be named in Chile’s list of 23 for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a>, he was part of <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">2015 Copa América</a> historical champions team.</p>
