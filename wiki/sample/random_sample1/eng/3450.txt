<p><i><b><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="ar ALA-LC transliteration">DhabÄ«á¸¥ah</span></b></i> (or <b>zabiha</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">Ø°ÙØ¨ÙÙÙØ­ÙØ©</span>ââ <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="ar ALA transliteration">dhabÄ«á¸¥ah</span></i> <small>IPA:Â </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Arabic" title="Help:IPA for Arabic">[Ã°Ã¦ËbiËÄ§É]</a></span>, 'slaughter'<sup>(noun)</sup>) is, in <a href="/wiki/Sharia" title="Sharia">Islamic law</a>, the prescribed method of <a href="/wiki/Ritual_slaughter" title="Ritual slaughter">ritual slaughter</a> of all lawful <a href="/wiki/Halal" title="Halal">halal</a> animals (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goats" title="Goats">goats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cows" title="Cows">cows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicken" title="Chicken">chicken</a>) excluding locusts, fish, and most sea-life. Unlawful animals like pigs, dogs, lions, bears, etc. are not allowed to be slaughtered or zabihah. This method of slaughtering lawful animals has several conditions to be fulfilled. The butcher must be <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, the name of <a href="/wiki/God_in_Islam" title="God in Islam">God</a> or "In the name of God" (<i><a href="/wiki/Basmala" title="Basmala">Bismillah</a></i>) must be called by the butcher upon slaughter of each halal animal separately, and it should consist of a swift, deep incision with a very sharp knife on the throat, cutting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_pipe" title="Wind pipe">wind pipe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jugular_vein" title="Jugular vein">jugular veins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carotid_artery" title="Carotid artery">carotid arteries</a> of both sides but leaving the <a href="/wiki/Spinal_cord" title="Spinal cord">spinal cord</a> intact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The precise details of the slaughtering method arise from <a href="/wiki/Sunnah" title="Sunnah">Islamic tradition</a> educated by <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_in_Islam" title="Muhammad in Islam">Muhammad</a>, himself. It is used to comply with the conditions stated in the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Forbidden for you are carrion, and blood, and <a href="/wiki/Pork" title="Pork">flesh of swine</a>, and that which has been slaughtered while proclaiming the name of any other than God, and one killed by strangling, and one killed with blunt weapons, and one which died by falling, and that which was gored by the horns of some animal, and one eaten by a wild beast, except those whom you slaughter; and that which is slaughtered at the altar and that which is distributed by the throwing of arrows [for an omen]; this is an act of sin.</p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>ââ<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Qur%27an_(English_translation)#Al-Maidah_-_The_Table_Spread" title="wikisource:Qur'an (English translation)">al-MÄÊ¼idah 5:3</a></cite></div>
</blockquote>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Slaughtering_process">Slaughtering process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhabihah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Slaughtering process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The slaughtering process referred to as dhabÄ«á¸¥ah is regulated by a set of rules intended to ensure the health of the animal to be slaughtered and conformance to Islamic religious law, which is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hadith" title="Hadith">hadiths</a>.</p>

<p>The following verses of the Quran mention the items which are forbidden to be eaten in Islam; however other have cited many other reasons that discourage the consumption of blood, pork, and carrion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>"He has only forbidden you what dies of itself, and blood, and flesh of swine, and that over which any other (name) than (that of) Allah has been invoked; but whoever is driven to necessity, not desiring, nor exceeding the limit, no sin shall be upon him; surely Allah is Forgiving, Merciful."</p>

This day have those who reject faith given up all hope of your religion: yet fear them not but fear Me. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prerequisites">Prerequisites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhabihah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prerequisites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the laws of dhabÄ«á¸¥ah á¸¥alÄl, certain prerequisites must be met before an animal is slaughtered:</p>
<ul>
<li>The animal must not be a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haraam#Food_and_intoxicants" title="Haraam">forbidden substance as per the Quran</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>The slaughter itself must be done by a sane (mentally competent) adult Muslim, Jew, or Christian as they are considered "<a href="/wiki/People_of_the_Book" title="People of the Book">People of the Book</a>" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">Ahl al-KitÄb</span>ââ); this is a point of consensus amongst Muslim scholars. They differ, however, concerning whether <a href="/wiki/Sabians" title="Sabians">Sabians</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zoroastrians" title="Zoroastrians">Zoroastrians</a> are also included under <a href="/wiki/People_of_the_Book" title="People of the Book">People of the Book</a> as stated in Surat al-MaÊ¼idah, ayah 5. Modern authors<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2011)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> have proposed that Christians who follow the Pauline doctrine of the Trinity are not included in this dispensation.</li>
<li>All Muslim scholars hold that mentioning God's name at the time of slaughter is a must; they differ as to whether or not forgetting to do so or leaving it off intentionally at the time of slaughter renders the sacrifice void and the meat thus forbidden for consumption. They also agree that if other than God's name is mentioned then this would be forbidden, due to the verse</li>
</ul>
<p>5:3 "Forbidden for you are carrion, and blood, and flesh of swine, and that which has been slaughtered while proclaiming the name of any other than God, and one killed by strangling, and one killed with blunt weapons, and one which died by falling, and that which was gored by the horns of some animal, and one eaten by a wild beast, except those whom you slaughter; and that which is slaughtered at the altar and that which is distributed by the throwing of arrows [for an omen]; this is an act of sin." ââ al-MaÊ¼idah 5:3 "Eat not of that (meat) on which Allah's name has not been pronounced." [al-AnÊ»am 6:121] Thus an <a href="/wiki/Animal_slaughter" title="Animal slaughter">animal slaughtered</a> by a Christian who did not mention anything at the time of slaughter would be permissible to some, while other scholars would hold it impermissible.</p>
<ul>
<li>In order for these traditions to take part in the United States, certain governmental regulations, enforced by acts, such as the <a href="/wiki/Humane_Slaughter_Act" title="Humane Slaughter Act">Humane Slaughter Act</a> require various prerequisites to be in place before any ritual or slaughter of any kind be permitted. This includes faiths such as Muslim, for Halal processing, and Jewish, for Kosher processing. Regulations include that the animal be completely insensitive to pain before killing it, typically by a technique like <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Electronarcosis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Electronarcosis (page does not exist)">electronarcosis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<p>The act of slaughtering itself is preceded by mentioning the name of God. Invoking the name of God at the moment of slaughtering is sometimes interpreted as acknowledgment of God's right over all things and thanking God for the sustenance he provides: it is a sign the food is taken not in sin or in gluttony, but to survive and praise Allah, as the most common blessing is, "Bismillah," or "In the name of God".</p>
<p>Thus the slaughter itself is preceded by the words "In the name of Allah (<i><a href="/wiki/Basmala" title="Basmala">Bismillah</a></i>)". It is not regarded appropriate to use the phrase "<a href="/wiki/Basmala" title="Basmala">Bismillah al Raá¸¥mÄn Al Raá¸¥Ä«m</a>" (In the name of God the Beneficent the Merciful) in this situation, because slaughtering is an act of subdual rather than mercy.</p>
<p>According to Islamic tradition, the animal is brought to the place of slaughter and laid down gently so as to not injure it. It is <a href="/wiki/Sunnah" title="Sunnah">Sunnah</a> but not <a href="/wiki/Fard" title="Fard">Fard</a> that the head of the animal be facing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qiblah" title="Qiblah">Qiblah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The blade must be kept hidden until the very last moment while the jugular of the animal is felt. The conventional method used to slaughter the animal involves cutting the large arteries in the neck along with the <a href="/wiki/Esophagus" title="Esophagus">esophagus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trachea" title="Trachea">trachea</a> with one swipe of a non-serrated blade. Care must be taken that the nervous system is not damaged, as this may cause the animal to die before <a href="/wiki/Exsanguination" title="Exsanguination">exsanguination</a> has taken place. During the swipe of the blade, the head must not be decapitated. While blood is draining, the animal is not handled until it has died. While this is an acceptable method, the Egyptian Fatwaa Committee has agreed that an animal can be rendered insensible to pain via <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Electronarcosis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Electronarcosis (page does not exist)">electronarcosis</a> and still be halal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> It's also important to note that these guidelines also respect the laws in place by the United States government to allow the practice to be permissible in the United States.</p>
<p>It is also compulsory that each animal must be slaughtered individually and in seclusion. In a <a href="/wiki/Poultry_farming" title="Poultry farming">poultry farm</a> or <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse" title="Slaughterhouse">slaughter house</a>, one animal must not witness another animal being slaughtered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>This method adheres to Islamic law (it ensures the animal does not die by any of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haraam" title="Haraam">Haraam</a> methods) and helps to effectively drain blood from the animal. This may be important because the consumption of blood itself is forbidden in Islam;<sup>[<a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>Â <a class="external text" href="https://quran.com/2:173" rel="nofollow">2:173</a>]</sup> however, it is not clear that bleeding the animal removes all traces of blood from the carcass, so the meat may remain unclean. In fact it is stated by Islamic authorities that it is only necessary to drain "most" of the blood from the animal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inducing_unconsciousness">Inducing unconsciousness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhabihah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Inducing unconsciousness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stunning the animal with a <a href="/wiki/Captive_bolt_pistol" title="Captive bolt pistol">bolt-gun</a>, as is the standard practice in USDA FSIS inspected slaughtering houses, may cause instant death. Muslims regard meat from such a slaughter to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haraam" title="Haraam">haraam</a>, considering such meat as <a href="/wiki/Carrion" title="Carrion">carrion</a>.</p>
<p>It is for these reasons that there are ongoing questions and conversations within the North American Muslim community as to whether meat processed in these slaughter houses meets the standard of "halal" (as opposed to dhabÄ«á¸¥ah). At center of this debate is the doubt as to whether this meat could qualify under the allowed category of the food of the People of the Book (Jewish and Christians). The first consideration being that standard slaughtering methods could cause the animal to die in a way other than slaughter (death through exsanguination).</p>
<p>Debate continues among <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faqih" title="Faqih">Muslim jurists</a> and the general Muslim population about whether or not stunning, anesthetics, or other forms of inducing unconsciousness in the animal prior to slaughter are permissible per Islam. Several halal food authorities have more recently permitted the use of a recently developed <a href="/wiki/Fail-safe" title="Fail-safe">fail-safe</a> system of head-only stunning where the shock is less painful and non-fatal and where it is possible to reverse the procedure and revive the animal after the shock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Stunning">Stunning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhabihah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Stunning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>According to the British Halal Food Authority, <a href="/wiki/Stunning" title="Stunning">stunning</a> is permissible. However, there are different forms of stunning; some of which are prohibited, and some are permitted. According to the HFA, the following are prohibited:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stunning" title="Stunning">Captive-bolt stunning</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stunning" title="Stunning">Percussion stunning</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stunning" title="Stunning">Gas stunning</a></li>
</ul>
<p>There are two types of stunning that the Halal Food Authority approves:</p>
<ul>
<li>Water-bath Stun â for <a href="/wiki/Poultry" title="Poultry">poultry</a> only - where birds are dragged through water with an electric stunner within. Stated that sometimes they are not rendered fully paralyzed after the water bath because they lift their bodies up while struggling and do not make full contact with the stunner. Furthermore, the birds come out of the electrified bath paralyzed, but not stunned. Karen Davis of United Poultry Concerns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li>Electric-Tong Stun â for larger animals, e.g. sheep, cow, camel, goat, etc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The tong would have to stay attached to the head when the bovine falls. The neck to neck position must never be used because the current may fail to go through the brain. A headholder that holds the animal's head up when the body falls is strongly recommended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li>Electronarcosis is also often used for larger animals, dulling the animal's senses before the slaughtering and has been agreed upon as licit by the Egyptian Fawtwa Committee, which is a committee that has the authority to declare what is permissible and not in the ritual slaughter techniques of halal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies_on_animal_welfare">Controversies on animal welfare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhabihah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversies on animal welfare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bans_on_ritual_slaughter" title="Bans on ritual slaughter">Bans on ritual slaughter</a></div>
<p>Opponents of dhabÄ«á¸¥ah á¸¥alÄl, most notably some animal welfare groups, contend that some methods of slaughter "cause severe suffering to animals" compared to when the animal is stunned before slaughter, which is why the Egyptian Fatwaa Committee has agreed to the electronarcosis. In the United Kingdom, the government funded an independent advisory body <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farm_Animal_Welfare_Council" title="Farm Animal Welfare Council">Farm Animal Welfare Council</a> but recommended that conventional dhabÄ«á¸¥ah (along with <a href="/wiki/Shechita" title="Shechita">kosher slaughter (sheá¸¥itah)</a>) without prior stunning be abolished. The FAWC chairwoman of the time, Dr Judy MacArthur Clark, said, "This is a major incision into the animal and to say that it doesn't suffer is quite ridiculous." According to Dr Peter Jinman, president of the British Veterinary Association, vets are "looking at what is acceptable in the moral and ethical society we live."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Muslims counter this by referring to the problems connected with stunning and the benefits of slaughter without stunning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asidcom_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-asidcom-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is argued that this method of slaughter without prior stunning leaves the <a href="/wiki/Spinal_cord" title="Spinal cord">spinal cord</a>, and thus the capacity to feel pain until death, intact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The UK <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farm_Animal_Welfare_Council" title="Farm Animal Welfare Council">Farm Animal Welfare Council</a> says that the method by which kosher and halal meat is produced causes severe suffering to animals and it should be banned immediately.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> According to FAWC it can take up to two minutes for cattle to bleed to death, thus amounting to animal abuse. <a href="/wiki/Compassion_in_World_Farming" title="Compassion in World Farming">Compassion in World Farming</a> also supported the recommendation saying "We believe that the law must be changed to require all animals to be stunned before slaughter."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The UK government rejected its recommendations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Various research papers on cattle slaughter collected by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compassion_In_World_Farming" title="Compassion In World Farming">Compassion In World Farming</a> mention that "after the throat is cut, large clots can form at the severed ends of the carotid arteries, leading to occlusion of the wound (or "ballooning" as it is known in the slaughtering trade). Nick Cohen wrote in the <i>New Statesman</i>, "Occlusions slow blood loss from the carotids and delay the decline in blood pressure that prevents the suffering brain from blacking out. In one group of calves, 62.5 per cent suffered from ballooning. Even if the cut to the neck is clean, blood is carried to the brain by vertebral arteries and it keeps cattle conscious of their pain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> "Experiments carried out by the principal of the Swedish Veterinary Institute (<i>VeterinÃ¤rhÃ¶gskolan</i>) by order of the Swedish government in 1925 and published in 1928 determined that the blood carried to the brain by the vertebral arteries in bovines is reduced after slaughter by the Jewish method shehitah from 1/30 to 1/40, and on the basis of this and one other experiment Professor Axel Sahlstedt declared the method humane and not cruel. However, on the basis of other experiments that had showed different results, Sahlstedt recommended post-stunning as standard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, in a study between 1974 and 1978 <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Schulze" title="Wilhelm Schulze">Wilhelm Schulze</a> and his colleagues carried out a study at the School of Veterinary Medicine, Hannover University in Germany: "Attempts to Objectify Pain and Consciousness in Conventional (<a href="/wiki/Captive_bolt_pistol" title="Captive bolt pistol">captive bolt pistol</a> stunning) and Ritual (knife) Methods of Slaughtering Sheep and Calves"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> is reported on Islamic websites<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> to have concluded that "the Islamic way of slaughtering is the most humane method of slaughter and that captive bolt stunning, practiced in the West, causes severe pain to the animal." However, recent studies have countered the Schulze study, which is dated and relied on older EEG measurement techniques. Dr. Schulze himself also warned in his report that the stunning technique may not have functioned properly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Halal and kosher butchers deny that their method of killing animals is cruel and expressed anger over the FAWC recommendation,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Majid Katme of the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_Council_of_Britain" title="Muslim Council of Britain">Muslim Council of Britain</a> also disagreed, stating that "it's a sudden and quick haemorrhage. A quick loss of blood pressure and the brain is instantaneously starved of blood and there is no time to start feeling any pain."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2008, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DEFRA" title="DEFRA">Food and Farming minister</a> in the UK, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Rooker" title="Lord Rooker">Lord Rooker</a>, stated that halal and kosher meat should be labeled when it is put on sale, so that members of the public can decide whether or not they want to buy food from animals that have been bled to death. He was quoted as saying, "I object to the method of slaughter ... my choice as a customer is that I would want to buy meat that has been looked after, and slaughtered in the most humane way possible." The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RSPCA" title="RSPCA">RSPCA</a> supported Lord Rooker's views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization_of_the_United_Nations" title="Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations">Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Humane_Society_International" title="Humane Society International">Humane Society International</a>, "the animals that are slaughtered according to kosher and halal should be securely restrained, particularly the head and neck, before cutting the throat" as "movements (during slaughter) results in a poor cut, bad bleeding, slow loss of consciousness, if at all, and pain."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Europe, the DIALREL project addressed religious slaughter issues by gathering and disseminating information and by encouraging dialogue between the spiritual and scientific communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Funding for DIALREL was provided by The European Commission, and it began functioning in November 2006. DIALREL produced many fact sheets and ultimately published a final report in 2010, "Report on good and adverse practices - Animal welfare concerns in relation to slaughter practices from the viewpoint of veterinary sciences."</p>
<p>Certain Muslim and Jewish communities expressed frustration with the process of dialogue skewed for non-religious audiences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Research undertaken in 2010 by <a href="/wiki/Meat_%26_Livestock_Australia" title="Meat &amp; Livestock Australia">Meat &amp; Livestock Australia</a> on animal pain and distress concluded, "technologies available to alleviate such suffering overwhelmingly supports the use of pre-slaughter stunning".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, in the United Kingdom, concern has been expressed regarding the slaughtering of animals without prior stunning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ritual_slaughter_in_other_religions">Ritual slaughter in other religions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhabihah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ritual slaughter in other religions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Followers of some religions are prohibited from consuming meat slaughtered in the fashion described above. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rehat_Maryada" title="Rehat Maryada">Rehat Maryada</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sikhism" title="Sikhism">Sikhism</a> states that in Sikhism, "consumption of any meat killed in a ritualistic manner" is strictly <a href="/wiki/Prohibitions_in_Sikhism" title="Prohibitions in Sikhism">prohibited</a>, therefore prohibiting both halal and kosher meat.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Judaism">Judaism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhabihah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Judaism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Comparison_of_Islamic_and_Jewish_dietary_laws" title="Comparison of Islamic and Jewish dietary laws">Comparison of Islamic and Jewish dietary laws</a></div>
<p>There are many similarities between the rules concerning dhabihah and <a href="/wiki/Shechita" title="Shechita">shechita</a>, Jewish ritual slaughter. The word <i>dhabihah</i> is linguistically similar to the Hebrew term ××× <i>zevaá¸¥</i> (sacrifice).</p>
<p>Muslims are divided as to whether or not Jewish slaughter suffices as a replacement for Islamic dhabihah halal. Some claim that Jewish slaughter leaves out the takbÄ«r (saying "God is great") and changes the method of slaughter; thus, their meat is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haraam" title="Haraam">haraam</a>. Others claim that the slaughtering processes are similar enough in practice and in theory to render animals slaughtered by Jewish laws as halal.</p>
<p>Jeremiah J Berman wrote in 1941: "At the present day in most of the Islamic world Muslims purchase Jewish meat, though they will not buy Christian meat. This is true in Istanbul, Beirut, Jerusalem and Mogador. Contemporary Muslims in these cities consider Jewish slaughtering as fulfilling all the requirements of their law, while they regard the slaughtering performed by Christians as done in contravention thereof. In Yemen ... Jewish meat is not acceptable." Berman also reports that Jewish meat slaughtered in Salonica (Thessaloniki) was not acceptable to Muslims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shehitah_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-shehitah-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>To be kosher, fit for consumption by those of the Jewish faith, meat must be slaughtered by a Jewish shohet who holds a licence from a rabbi and has been examined on the laws of shechitah. This alone means that halal meat is forbidden to those of the Jewish faith. The requirements for the shape of the knife are more severe, the knife must be free from a single nick and the method of cutting is exactly defined. In addition there is an inspection of the lungs (bedikah) that mammals must pass, which Muslims do not have.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shehitah_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-shehitah-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
