<p>The <b>Daxi culture</b> (5000–3300 BC) was a <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> culture centered in the <a href="/wiki/Three_Gorges" title="Three Gorges">Three Gorges</a> region around the middle <a href="/wiki/Yangtze" title="Yangtze">Yangtze</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The culture ranged from western <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> to eastern <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pearl_River_Delta" title="Pearl River Delta">Pearl River Delta</a>. The site at Daxi, located in the <a href="/wiki/Qutang_Gorge" title="Qutang Gorge">Qutang Gorge</a> around <a href="/wiki/Wushan_County,_Chongqing" title="Wushan County, Chongqing">Wushan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a>, was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Nels_C._Nelson" title="Nels C. Nelson">Nels C. Nelson</a> in the 1920s. Many key archaeological sites from the Daxi culture, including the site at Daxi, will be inundated or destroyed after the completion of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Gorges_Dam" title="Three Gorges Dam">Three Gorges Dam</a>.</p>
<p>Daxi sites are typified by the presence of <i>dou</i> (cylindrical bottles), white <i>pan</i> (plates), and red pottery. The Daxi people cultivated <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> extensively. Daxi sites were some of the earliest in China to show evidence of moats and walled settlements.</p>
<p>The Daxi culture showed evidence of cultural interactions with the <a href="/wiki/Yangtze_River_Delta" title="Yangtze River Delta">Yangtze River Delta</a> region. The white <i>pan</i> artefacts from the culture were discovered at several Yangtze River Delta sites, including the type site of the <a href="/wiki/Majiabang_culture" title="Majiabang culture">Majiabang culture</a>. Conversely, <a href="/wiki/Jade" title="Jade">jade</a> artefacts at Daxi sites show possible influence from the Yangtze River Delta region. The Daxi culture was followed by the <a href="/wiki/Qujialing_culture" title="Qujialing culture">Qujialing culture</a>.</p>
