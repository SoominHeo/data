<p>The <b>Greater Khingan Range</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">大兴安岭</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">大興安嶺</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Dà Xīng'ānlǐng</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a>: <span lang="mn" xml:lang="mn">Их Хянганы нуруу</span>; <a href="/wiki/Manchu_language" title="Manchu language">Manchu</a>: <i>Amba Hinggan</i>), is a volcanic mountain range in northeast <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The range extends roughly 1,200 kilometres (750 mi) from northeast to southwest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Khingan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area has an elevation of 1,200 to 1,300 metres (3,900 to 4,300 ft), and the highest peak reaches 2,035 metres (6,677 ft). The range is much broader in the north, at 306 kilometres (190 mi), than it is in the south, at 97 kilometres (60 mi). It was formed during the Jurassic Period (roughly 200 to 145 million years ago), and is essentially a tilted <a href="/wiki/Fault_block" title="Fault block">fault block</a>; its ancient fault line forms its eastern edge, facing the Northeast Plain. The ranges are markedly asymmetrical, with a sharp eastern face and a more gentle western slope down to the Mongolian Plateau at an elevation of 790 to 1,000 metres (2,590 to 3,280 ft). The eastern slopes are more heavily dissected by the numerous tributaries of the <a href="/wiki/Nen_River" title="Nen River">Nen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songhua_River" title="Songhua River">Songhua</a> rivers, but generally the mountains are rounded with flat peaks. The ranges are composed largely of igneous rocks (i.e., formed through the solidification of magma).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Khingan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its slopes are a relatively rich grazing area. The <a href="/wiki/Khitan_people" title="Khitan people">Khitan</a> people lived on the eastern slopes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mote_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mote-2">[2]</a></sup> before establishing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liao_Dynasty" title="Liao Dynasty">Liao Dynasty</a> in the tenth century. On the western slopes lived the nomadic people, who raised sheep and camels and used the Mongolian plateau for their pastoralist economy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mote_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mote-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Much of the area is inhabited by peoples speaking Mongol and, in the north, <a href="/wiki/Tungusic_languages" title="Tungusic languages">Tungusic languages</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Oroqen_people" title="Oroqen people">Oroqen people</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Evenks" title="Evenks">Evenk people</a>. Logging continues to be the major economic activity.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
