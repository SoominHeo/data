<p><b>Kazuyoshi Tatsunami</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">立浪 和義</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tatsunami Kazuyoshi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born August 19, 1969 in <a href="/wiki/Settsu,_Osaka" title="Settsu, Osaka">Settsu, Osaka</a>)</span> is a former Japanese professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Chunichi_Dragons" title="Chunichi Dragons">Chunichi Dragons</a>, having played his professional career for them since his debut in <a href="/wiki/1988_in_baseball" title="1988 in baseball">1988</a>. He was drafted in the first round in the <a href="/wiki/1987_in_baseball" title="1987 in baseball">1987</a> NPB Draft.</p>
<p>Tatsunami holds the all-time NPB <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a> record with 487, and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Meikyukai" title="Meikyukai">Meikyukai</a> (Association of Great Players, or Golden Players Club).</p>
<p>After the 2009 season, Tatsunami retired, having seen his skills and playing time diminish for the last few seasons. He retired with a career .285 batting average, 171 home runs, 1,037 RBI, and 2,480 hits.</p>
