<p><b>Tatsuno Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">辰野駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tatsuno-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tatsuno,_Nagano" title="Tatsuno, Nagano">Tatsuno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kamiina_District,_Nagano" title="Kamiina District, Nagano">Kamiina District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagano_Prefecture" title="Nagano Prefecture">Nagano Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> jointly operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JR_Central" title="JR Central">JR Central</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JR_East" title="JR East">JR East</a>. It is managed by JR East.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno_Station_(Nagano)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tatsuno Station is served by the old route of <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Main_Line" title="Chūō Main Line">Chūō Main Line</a> (Okaya-Shiojiri branch) and is 9.5 kilometers from the branching point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Okaya_Station" title="Okaya Station">Okaya Station</a> and 219.9 from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo Station</a>. It is also a station on the <a href="/wiki/Iida_Line" title="Iida Line">Iida Line</a>, and is 195.7 kilometers from the terminus of that line at <a href="/wiki/Toyohashi_Station" title="Toyohashi Station">Toyohashi Station</a></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno_Station_(Nagano)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of two ground-level <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> serving four tracks, connected to the station building by a footbridge. The station has a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midori_no_Madoguchi" title="Midori no Madoguchi">Midori no Madoguchi</a></i> staffed ticket office.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno_Station_(Nagano)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 11 June 1906. The current station building was completed in 1983. With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR East.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno_Station_(Nagano)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2015, the station was used by an average of 569 passengers daily (boarding passengers only).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast2015stats_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast2015stats-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
