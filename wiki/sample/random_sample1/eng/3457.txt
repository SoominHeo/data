<p><b>Tatsuno</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">辰野町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tatsuno-machi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Towns_of_Japan" title="Towns of Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kamiina_District,_Nagano" title="Kamiina District, Nagano">Kamiina District</a> in south-central <a href="/wiki/Nagano_Prefecture" title="Nagano Prefecture">Nagano Prefecture</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%ABbu_region" title="Chūbu region">Chūbu region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 October 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno,_Nagano&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 19,236 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 114 persons per km². Its total area was 169.20 square kilometres (65.33 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno,_Nagano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tatsuno is located in the Ina Valley of south-central Nagano Prefecture, bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Kiso_Mountains" title="Kiso Mountains">Kiso Mountains</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%AB_River" title="Tenryū River">Tenryū River</a> flows through the town, which is surrounded by mountains and is rich in a natural environment. It is takes about 3 hours from <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, 2 hours from <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>, 5 hours from <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> by train and equally by car. Part of the town is within the borders of the <a href="/wiki/Enrei_%C5%8Cj%C5%8D_Prefectural_Natural_Park" title="Enrei Ōjō Prefectural Natural Park">Enrei Ōjō Prefectural Natural Park</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Yokokawa_Dam" title="Yokokawa Dam">Yokokawa Dam</a> is located in Tatsuno.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno,_Nagano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Tatsuno was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Shinano_Province" title="Shinano Province">Shinano Province</a>. The village of Inatomi established on April 1, 1889 by the establishment of the municipalities system, and was elevated to town status on January 1, 1947, changing its name to Tatsuno at that time. The neighboring village of Asahi was annexed on April 1, 1955, followed by Kawashima on September 30, 1956 and Ono n March 31, 1961.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuno,_Nagano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tatsuno has five public elementary schools and five public middle school operated by the town government, and one high school operated the Nagano Prefectural Board of Education. The <a href="/wiki/Shinshu_Honan_Junior_College" title="Shinshu Honan Junior College">Shinshu Honan Junior College</a> us also located in Tatsuno.</p>
