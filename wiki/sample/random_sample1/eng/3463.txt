<p><b>Down District Council</b> is a Local Council in <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">County Down</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>. It is set to merge with <a href="/wiki/Newry_and_Mourne_District_Council" title="Newry and Mourne District Council">Newry and Mourne District Council</a> in May 2015 under local government reorganisation in Northern Ireland to become <a href="/wiki/Newry,_Mourne_and_Down_District_Council" title="Newry, Mourne and Down District Council">Newry, Mourne and Down District Council</a>.</p>
<p>The Council is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Downpatrick" title="Downpatrick">Downpatrick</a>. Other towns in the Council area are <a href="/wiki/Ardglass" title="Ardglass">Ardglass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ballynahinch,_County_Down" title="Ballynahinch, County Down">Ballynahinch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castlewellan" title="Castlewellan">Castlewellan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clough" title="Clough">Clough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crossgar" title="Crossgar">Crossgar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dundrum,_County_Down" title="Dundrum, County Down">Dundrum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killough" title="Killough">Killough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killyleagh" title="Killyleagh">Killyleagh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle,_County_Down" title="Newcastle, County Down">Newcastle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saintfield" title="Saintfield">Saintfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seaforde" title="Seaforde">Seaforde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strangford" title="Strangford">Strangford</a>.</p>
<p>The Down District Council area consists of 4 electoral areas: Ballynahinch, Downpatrick, Newcastle and Rowallane. At the last election in 2011, 23 Councillors were elected from the following political parties: 9 <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_and_Labour_Party" title="Social Democratic and Labour Party">SDLP</a>, 5 <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a>, 3 <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">DUP</a>, 3 <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">UUP</a>, 1 <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party</a>, 1 <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Green Party in Northern Ireland">Green Party</a>, 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" title="Independent (politician)">Independent</a>. The Council Chairman for the 2011/2012 council term is Councillor Dermot Curran (SDLP) and the Vice Chair is Councillor Liam Johnston (Sinn Féin).</p>
<p>In elections for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westminster_Parliament" title="Westminster Parliament">Westminster Parliament</a> it is split between the <a href="/wiki/Strangford_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Strangford (UK Parliament constituency)">Strangford constituency</a> and the <a href="/wiki/South_Down_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="South Down (UK Parliament constituency)">South Down constituency</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_of_the_Council">Work of the Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Down_District_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Work of the Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Council has periodically had to provide funding to ensure that the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick_Visitor_Centre" title="Saint Patrick Visitor Centre">Saint Patrick Visitor Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Downpatrick" title="Downpatrick">Downpatrick</a> remains in operation. It has also funded a major promenade infrastructure regeneration in <a href="/wiki/Newcastle,_County_Down" title="Newcastle, County Down">Newcastle</a>. The new promenade has won a number of National awards including a <a href="/wiki/Civic_Trust_Awards" title="Civic Trust Awards">Civic Trust</a> Award for Excellence in the Public Realm.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Review_of_Public_Administration">Review of Public Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Down_District_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Review of Public Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under the Review of Public Administration (RPA) the Council was due to merge with <a href="/wiki/Newry_and_Mourne_District_Council" title="Newry and Mourne District Council">Newry and Mourne District Council</a> in 2011 to form a single council for the enlarged area totalling 1539 km² and a population of 150,886.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The first election to the new council was due to take place in May 2009, but on 25 April 2008, <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Woodward" title="Shaun Woodward">Shaun Woodward</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a> announced that the scheduled 2009 district council elections were to be postponed until the introduction of the eleven new councils in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The planned change was abandoned in 2010 and the most recent election took place in May 2011 under the existing boundaries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Down_District_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area covered by Down District Council has a population of 69,731 residents according to the 2011 Northern Ireland census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
