<p>Count <b>Leopold Joseph von Daun</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Leopold Joseph Maria, <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Count" title="Imperial Count">Reichsgraf</a> von und zu Daun</i></span>; 24 September 1705 – 5 February 1766), later <b>Prince of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thiano" title="Thiano">Thiano</a></b>, was an <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Austrian</a> field marshal of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Army_(Holy_Roman_Empire)" title="Imperial Army (Holy Roman Empire)">Imperial Army</a> in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Austrian_Succession" title="War of the Austrian Succession">War of the Austrian Succession</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">Seven Years' War</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Count_Leopold_Joseph_von_Daun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leopold Joseph Maria was born at <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, the son of Count <a href="/wiki/Wirich_Philipp_von_Daun" title="Wirich Philipp von Daun">Wirich Philipp von Daun</a> (1669–1741). The Daun noble family originated from the <a href="/wiki/Rhineland" title="Rhineland">Rhineland</a> and derived its name from the ancestral seat at <a href="/wiki/Daun,_Germany" title="Daun, Germany">Daun</a> south of the <a href="/wiki/High_Eifel" title="High Eifel">High Eifel</a> range.</p>
<p>He was intended for the church, but his natural inclination for the army, in which his father and grandfather had been distinguished generals, proved irresistible. In 1718 he served in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Quadruple_Alliance" title="War of the Quadruple Alliance">War of the Quadruple Alliance</a> in Sicily, in his father's regiment. He had already risen to the rank of <i><a href="/wiki/Oberst" title="Oberst">Oberst</a></i> (Colonel) when he saw further active service in Italy and on the Rhine in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Polish_Succession" title="War of the Polish Succession">War of the Polish Succession</a> (1734–35). He continued to add to his distinctions in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Russian%E2%80%93Turkish_War_(1735%E2%80%9339)" title="Austro-Russian–Turkish War (1735–39)">Turkish War</a> of 1737–39, in which he attained the rank of <i>Feldmarschallleutnant</i> (Major-General).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="War_of_the_Austrian_Succession">War of the Austrian Succession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Count_Leopold_Joseph_von_Daun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: War of the Austrian Succession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Austrian_Succession" title="War of the Austrian Succession">War of the Austrian Succession</a> from 1740, Daun distinguished himself by the careful leadership which was afterwards his greatest military quality.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Silesian_Wars" title="Silesian Wars">First Silesian War</a>, he was present at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chotusitz" title="Battle of Chotusitz">Chotusitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>, and led the advanced guard of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ludwig_Andreas_Khevenh%C3%BCller" title="Ludwig Andreas Khevenhüller">Khevenhüller</a>'s army in the victorious Danube campaign of 1743. Field Marshal <a href="/wiki/Otto_Ferdinand_von_Abensberg_und_Traun" title="Otto Ferdinand von Abensberg und Traun">Count Traun</a>, who succeeded Khevenhüller in 1744, thought equally highly of Daun, and entrusted him with the rearguard of the Austrian army when it escaped from the French to attack <a href="/wiki/Frederick_the_Great" title="Frederick the Great">Frederick the Great</a>. He held important commands in the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hohenfriedberg" title="Battle of Hohenfriedberg">Hohenfriedberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Soor" title="Battle of Soor">Soor</a>, and in the same year (1745) was promoted to the rank of <i>Feldzeugmeister</i> (Lieutenant General). After this he served in the Low Countries, and was present at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Val" title="Battle of Val">Battle of Val</a>. He was highly valued by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa_of_Austria" title="Maria Theresa of Austria">Maria Theresa</a>, who made him commandant of Vienna and a Knight of the Golden Fleece, and in 1754 he was elevated to the rank of <i>Feldmarschall</i> (Field-Marshal).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Seven_Years.27_War">Seven Years' War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Count_Leopold_Joseph_von_Daun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Seven Years' War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the interval of peace that preceded the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">Seven Years' War</a> he was engaged in carrying out an elaborate scheme for the reorganization of the Austrian army, and it was chiefly through his efforts that the <a href="/wiki/Theresian_Military_Academy" title="Theresian Military Academy">Theresian Military Academy</a> was established at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wiener-Neustadt" title="Wiener-Neustadt">Wiener-Neustadt</a> in 1751. He was not actively employed in the first campaigns of the war, but in 1757 he was placed at the head of the army which was raised to relieve <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>. On 18 June 1757 Daun decisively defeated Frederick for the first time in his career in the desperately fought <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kolin" title="Battle of Kolin">Battle of Kolin</a>. In commemoration of this brilliant exploit, the queen immediately instituted a military order bearing her name, and Daun was awarded the first Grand Cross of that order. The union of the relieving army with the forces of Prince Charles at Prague reduced Daun to the position of second in command, and in that capacity he took part in the pursuit of the <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussians</a> and the victory of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Breslau_(1757)" title="Battle of Breslau (1757)">Breslau</a>.</p>

<p>Frederick now reappeared and won the most brilliant victory of the age at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leuthen" title="Battle of Leuthen">Leuthen</a>. Daun was present on that field, but was not held accountable for the disaster, and when Prince Charles resigned his command, Daun was appointed in his place. With the campaign of 1758 began the war of manoeuvre in which Daun, though missing some opportunities to crush the Prussians through over-caution, at least maintained a steady and cool resistance to the fiery strategy of Frederick. In 1758 Major-General <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Gideon_von_Laudon" title="Ernst Gideon von Laudon">Laudon</a>, acting under Daun's instructions, forced the king to raise the siege of <a href="/wiki/Olomouc" title="Olomouc">Olmütz</a> (<a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Domstadtl" title="Battle of Domstadtl">Battle of Domstadtl</a>), and later in the same year Daun himself surprised Frederick at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hochkirch" title="Battle of Hochkirch">Battle of Hochkirch</a> and inflicted a severe defeat upon him (14 October). Despite the tactical success Daun achieved at Hochkirch, his failure to pursue Frederick allowed the Prussians to block his path into Silesia, meaning that his victory counted for little. In the following year the war of manoeuvre continued, and on 20 and 21 November he surrounded the entire corps of General <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_August_von_Finck" title="Friedrich August von Finck">Finck</a> at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Maxen" title="Battle of Maxen">Maxen</a>, forcing the Prussians to surrender. These successes were counterbalanced in the following year by the defeat of Laudon at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Liegnitz_(1760)" title="Battle of Liegnitz (1760)">Liegnitz</a>, which was attributed partly to the dilatoriness of Daun, and Daun's own subsequent defeat by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Joachim_von_Zieten" title="Hans Joachim von Zieten">Zieten</a>'s bold attack in the great <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Torgau" title="Battle of Torgau">Battle of Torgau</a>. In this engagement, Daun was so severely wounded that he had to return to Vienna to recuperate. However, Daun and his forces inflicted heavy casualties on the Prussians and were able to retreat in good order.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Count_Leopold_Joseph_von_Daun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1200" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg/220px-Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg/330px-Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg/440px-Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Statue_of_Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Statue of Leopold Josef Graf Daun, on the Maria Theresa memorial, Vienna</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Daun continued to command until the end of the war, and afterwards worked with the greatest energy at the reorganization of the imperial forces. In 1762 he had been appointed president of the <i><a href="/wiki/Hofkriegsrat" title="Hofkriegsrat">Hofkriegsrat</a></i>. By order of Maria Theresa, a monument to his memory was erected by <a href="/wiki/Balthasar_Ferdinand_Moll" title="Balthasar Ferdinand Moll">Balthasar Ferdinand Moll</a> in the church of the Augustinians, with an inscription describing him as the "saviour of her states." In 1888, the 56th Regiment of Austrian Infantry was named after him.</p>
<p>Called the Austrian <a href="/wiki/Fabius_Maximus" title="Fabius Maximus">Fabius Cunctator</a>, Daun has been criticized for the dilatoriness of his operations, but wariness was not misplaced when one faced a general like Frederick, who was quick and unpredictable. His inability to exploit a victory, on the other hand, might not be so easily excused. The relationship with his daring general Ernst Gideon von Laudon remained tense.</p>
<p>His cousin married the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquis_of_Pombal" title="Marquis of Pombal">Marquis of Pombal</a>, the noted Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">statesman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Count_Leopold_Joseph_von_Daun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Regarding personal names: Until 1919, <i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Graf" title="Graf">Graf</a></span></i> was a title, translated as <i><a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">Count</a></i>, not a first or middle name. The female form is <i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Gräfin</span></i>. In Germany since 1919, it forms part of family names. Regarding personal names: <i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrst" title="Fürst">Fürst</a></span></i> is a title, translated as '<a href="/wiki/Prince" title="Prince">Prince</a>', not a first or middle name. The feminine form is <i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Fürstin</span></i>.</p>
<p>For an overview of the system of military ranks that existed in the Austro-Hungarian Empire up to the first World War see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comparative_military_ranks_of_World_War_I" title="Comparative military ranks of World War I">Comparative military ranks of World War I</a></p>
