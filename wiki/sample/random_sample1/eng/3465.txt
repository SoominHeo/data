<p><i><b>Down to You</b></i> is a 2000 romantic comedy film about losing a first love. It was directed by Kris Isacsson. The main characters are Alfred 'Al' Connelly (played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freddie_Prinze,_Jr." title="Freddie Prinze, Jr.">Freddie Prinze, Jr.</a>), Imogen (<a href="/wiki/Julia_Stiles" title="Julia Stiles">Julia Stiles</a>), and Cyrus (<a href="/wiki/Selma_Blair" title="Selma Blair">Selma Blair</a>). The cast also includes <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Hatosy" title="Shawn Hatosy">Shawn Hatosy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Kutcher" title="Ashton Kutcher">Ashton Kutcher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosario_Dawson" title="Rosario Dawson">Rosario Dawson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucie_Arnaz" title="Lucie Arnaz">Lucie Arnaz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Winkler" title="Henry Winkler">Henry Winkler</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zak_Orth" title="Zak Orth">Zak Orth</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Down_to_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-notice" role="presentation">
<tr>
<td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w.svg"><img alt="Wiki letter w.svg" data-file-height="44" data-file-width="44" height="20" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6c/Wiki_letter_w.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6c/Wiki_letter_w.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6c/Wiki_letter_w.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td>
<td class="mbox-text"><span class="mbox-text-span">This article needs an improved <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:How_to_write_a_plot_summary" title="Wikipedia:How to write a plot summary">plot summary</a>. <small><i>(September 2015)</i></small></span></td>

<p>College sophomore Al Connelly (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freddie_Prinze,_Jr." title="Freddie Prinze, Jr.">Freddie Prinze, Jr.</a>) meets the girl of his dreams, freshman Imogen (<a href="/wiki/Julia_Stiles" title="Julia Stiles">Julia Stiles</a>), and true love abounds. The two engage on a whirlwind courtship—they pick a song based on the records from Al's parents, eat a cake together, and even make love. Imogen's fear of lost youth causes her to push away from Al, and they go their separate ways after Imogen cheats on Al at a party. Al attempts to rebound from the relationship, determined to forget Imogen, and he goes to desperate measures to do so, including suicide by shampoo and problems with his career. In the end, Imogen finds Al when she hears of his attempted suicide and she brings him a book cover that she illustrated featuring the two of them. The two reconcile their differences and get back together showing that love can be obtained. The story is told from the points of view of both Al and Imogen.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Down_to_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Down to You</i> opened at number 2 at the US box office and made $7.6 million in its opening weekend, behind <i><a href="/wiki/Next_Friday" title="Next Friday">Next Friday</a></i>. It went on to gross $24.4 million worldwide from a $35 million budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reports that 3% of 59 surveyed critics gave the film a positive review; the average rating is 3.2/10. The consensus reads: "Down to You is ruined by a bland, by-the-numbers plot and an awful script."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> rated it 13/100 based on 21 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
