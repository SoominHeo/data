<p><b>Dawei</b> (<a href="/wiki/Burmese_language" title="Burmese language">Burmese</a>: <span style="font-family:'Myanmar Text','Noto Sans Myanmar', Myanmar3, Myanmar2, Myanmar1, Padauk, mm3web, TharLon, 'Masterpiece Uni Sans', Parabaik, Yunghkio, Thanlwin, 'Win Uni Innwa', 'MyMyanmar Unicode', 'WinUni Innwa';">ထားဝယ်မြို့</span>; <a href="/wiki/MLC_Transcription_System" title="MLC Transcription System">MLCTS</a>: <i><span title="Transcription in Myanmar Language Commission Transcription System">hta: wai mrui.</span></i>, <small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Burmese" title="Help:IPA for Burmese">[dəwɛ̀ mjo̰]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Mon_language" title="Mon language">Mon</a>: <span lang="mnw" xml:lang="mnw"><span style="font-family: TharLon, 'Noto Sans Myanmar', 'Myanmar Text';">ဓဝဲါ</span></span>, <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[həwài]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">ทวาย</span>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">RTGS:</a> <i>Thawai</i>, <small>pronounced</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA for Thai and Lao">[tʰā.wāːj]</a></span>; formerly known as <b>Tavoy</b>), is a city in south-eastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Myanmar</a> and capital of <a href="/wiki/Tanintharyi_Region" title="Tanintharyi Region">Tanintharyi Region</a>, formerly Tenasserim Division, about 614.3 km (381.7 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Yangon" title="Yangon">Yangon</a> on the north bank of the <a href="/wiki/Dawei_River" title="Dawei River">Dawei River</a>. Population (2004 estimate), 139,900. It is at 14.09°N 98.20°E. Dawei is a port at the head of the Dawei River <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a>, 30 km (18.6 mi). from the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Sea" title="Andaman Sea">Andaman Sea</a>. As a result, the city is prone to flooding during the monsoon season. "Dawei" is also the name of one of Myanmar's 135 ethnic minorities.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dawei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area around the Dawei River estuary has been inhabited for centuries by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dawei_(ethinc_group)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dawei (ethinc group) (page does not exist)">Dawei</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mon_(ethnic_group)" title="Mon (ethnic group)">Mon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karen_people" title="Karen people">Kayin</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_(ethnic_group)" title="Thai (ethnic group)">Thai</a> mariners. From the 11th to 13th centuries, Dawei was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pagan_Empire" title="Pagan Empire">Pagan Empire</a>. From 1287 to 1564, Dawei became part of the <a href="/wiki/Sukhothai_Kingdom" title="Sukhothai Kingdom">Sukhothai Kingdom</a> and its successor <a href="/wiki/Ayutthaya_Kingdom" title="Ayutthaya Kingdom">Ayutthaya Kingdom</a> (Siam). From 1564 to 1594, Dawei was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toungoo_dynasty" title="Toungoo dynasty">Toungoo Kingdom</a> of Burma. Siam <a href="/wiki/Burmese%E2%80%93Siamese_War_(1594%E2%80%931605)" title="Burmese–Siamese War (1594–1605)">temporarily regained the city</a> between 1594 and 1614. From 1614 to the 1740s, Dawei was the southernmost city under Burmese authority, and was defended by a Burmese garrison. In the late 1740s during the Burmese civil war of 1740–1757, Dawei, along with the northern Tenasserim coast, was taken over by Siam. Burma <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burmese%E2%80%93Siamese_War_(1759%E2%80%931760)" title="Burmese–Siamese War (1759–1760)">regained the city</a> in 1760, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burmese%E2%80%93Siamese_War_(1765%E2%80%931767)" title="Burmese–Siamese War (1765–1767)">extended its control over the entire Tenasserim coast</a> in 1765. The Tenasserim coast was ceded to the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> after the <a href="/wiki/First_Anglo-Burmese_War" title="First Anglo-Burmese War">First Anglo-Burmese War</a> (1824–1826).</p>
<p>After independence in 1948, the city became part of the <a href="/wiki/Tanintharyi_Region" title="Tanintharyi Region">Tenasserim Division</a>, which also included today's <a href="/wiki/Mon_State" title="Mon State">Mon State</a>. In 1974, Mon State was carved out of Tenasserim and Dawei became the capital of the truncated Division. In 1989, the city's English name was changed from Tavoy to Dawei, and Tenasserim became Tanintharyi.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dawei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dawei features an extreme <a href="/wiki/Tropical_monsoon_climate" title="Tropical monsoon climate">tropical monsoon climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Am</i>), similar to <a href="/wiki/Sittwe" title="Sittwe">Sittwe</a> further north-west. There is a substantial dry season from November to April, but in the wet season the influence of local mountains causes Dawei to receive as much as 1,300 millimetres (51 in) precipitation per month. Apart from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Choc%C3%B3" title="El Chocó">the Chocó region</a> of Colombia, and the area around <a href="/wiki/Mount_Cameroon" title="Mount Cameroon">Mount Cameroon</a> in Africa, it is possibly the wettest lowland tropical region in the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dawei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Daweiairport.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="375" data-file-width="500" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Daweiairport.jpg/220px-Daweiairport.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Daweiairport.jpg/330px-Daweiairport.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Daweiairport.jpg/440px-Daweiairport.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Daweiairport.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Dawei_Airport" title="Dawei Airport">Dawei Airport</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Only recently was Dawei connected to the rest of <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> by road and rail. There are plans to construct a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deep_water_port" title="Deep water port">deep water port</a> in Dawei.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In November 2010, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Myanmar_Port_Authority" title="Myanmar Port Authority">Myanmar Port Authority</a> signed a US$8.6 billion deal with <a href="/wiki/Italthai_Industrial_Group" title="Italthai Industrial Group">Italian-Thai Development</a> to develop the seaport at Dawei.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dawei_Special_Economic_Zone" title="Dawei Special Economic Zone">Dawei Special Economic Zone</a> would become Myanmar's first <a href="/wiki/Special_economic_zone" title="Special economic zone">special economic zone</a> (SEZ), which includes plans to develop a 250 square kilometres (97 sq mi) industrial estate, with sea, land (railway and road) infrastructure links to Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam, as well as a gas pipeline to Thailand's <a href="/wiki/Kanchanaburi_Province" title="Kanchanaburi Province">Kanchanaburi Province</a> and commercial and residential developments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref2012103004_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref2012103004-6">[6]</a></sup> The development of the SEZ has been linked to land confiscations and land grabs from farmers of upwards of 63,768 acres (258.06 km<sup>2</sup>) (direct) and 153,919 acres (622.89 km<sup>2</sup>), potentially displacing 500,000 Dawei natives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tni_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tni-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>A transnational highway and a railway line across the <a href="/wiki/Tenasserim_Hills" title="Tenasserim Hills">Tenasserim Hills</a> connecting Dawei and <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a> are planned if the deep water port project goes ahead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> This port could significantly reduce <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>-bound traffic when completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dawei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dawei <a href="/wiki/Longyi" title="Longyi">longyis</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sarong" title="Sarong">sarong</a>) are one of the area's well-known products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The area produces <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubber" title="Rubber">rubber</a>, dried fish, and <a href="/wiki/Teak" title="Teak">teakwood</a>. It also produces <a href="/wiki/Cashew" title="Cashew">cashew nuts</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Betel_nut" title="Betel nut">betel nuts</a> and exports them through local traders to China, India, and Thailand. Dawei is also well known for its variety of tropical fruits such as <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapples</a>, a variety of <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">mangoes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mangosteen" title="Mangosteen">mangosteens</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Durian" title="Durian">durian</a>. There is one fruit called <i>zin thi</i> (in Dawei language), which can only be found in Dawei and surrounding areas; this fruit is sweet with a touch of sour.</p>
<p>Dawei is also getting a proposed special economic zone plus deep sea port called the <a href="/wiki/Dawei_Port_Project" title="Dawei Port Project">Dawei Port Project</a>. It is undergoing construction with companies providing a road link to Thailand, port construction, and a rail connection. It is partly being developed by Italian and Thai companies.</p>
