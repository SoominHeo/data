<p><i><b>Divergent</b></i> is a 2014 American <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_film" title="Science fiction film">science fiction</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Neil_Burger" title="Neil Burger">Neil Burger</a>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Divergent_(novel)" title="Divergent (novel)">novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Roth" title="Veronica Roth">Veronica Roth</a>. The film is the first installment in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series" title="The Divergent Series">The Divergent Series</a></i> and was produced by <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Fisher" title="Lucy Fisher">Lucy Fisher</a>, Pouya Shabazian, and <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Wick" title="Douglas Wick">Douglas Wick</a>, with a screenplay by <a href="/wiki/Evan_Daugherty" title="Evan Daugherty">Evan Daugherty</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Taylor" title="Vanessa Taylor">Vanessa Taylor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It stars <a href="/wiki/Shailene_Woodley" title="Shailene Woodley">Shailene Woodley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theo_James" title="Theo James">Theo James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Judd" title="Ashley Judd">Ashley Judd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jai_Courtney" title="Jai Courtney">Jai Courtney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ray_Stevenson_(actor)" title="Ray Stevenson (actor)">Ray Stevenson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Kravitz" title="Zoë Kravitz">Zoë Kravitz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miles_Teller" title="Miles Teller">Miles Teller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Goldwyn" title="Tony Goldwyn">Tony Goldwyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ansel_Elgort" title="Ansel Elgort">Ansel Elgort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Q" title="Maggie Q">Maggie Q</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The story takes place in a <a href="/wiki/Dystopia" title="Dystopia">dystopian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Post-apocalyptic" title="Post-apocalyptic">post-apocalyptic</a> <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> where people are divided into distinct factions based on human virtues. Beatrice Prior is warned that she is Divergent and thus will never fit into any one of the factions. She soon learns that a sinister plot is brewing in the seemingly perfect society.</p>
<p>Development of <i>Divergent</i> began in March 2011 when <a href="/wiki/Summit_Entertainment" title="Summit Entertainment">Summit Entertainment</a> picked up the film rights to the novel with <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Wick" title="Douglas Wick">Douglas Wick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Fisher" title="Lucy Fisher">Lucy Fisher</a>'s production company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Wagon_Entertainment" title="Red Wagon Entertainment">Red Wagon Entertainment</a>. <a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> began April 16, 2013, and concluded on July 16, 2013, with reshoots taking place from January 24–26, 2014. Production mostly took place in Chicago.</p>
<p><i>Divergent</i> was released on March 21, 2014, in the United States. The film received mixed reviews from critics, with praise going towards its performances, and criticism focused on its execution. Reviewers found the film to be generic and unoriginal, comparing it unfavorably to other <a href="/wiki/Young_adult_fiction" title="Young adult fiction">young adult fiction</a> adaptations. Despite this, the film was a financial success as it reached the #1 spot at the box office during its opening weekend. After its release, the film earned over US$288 million worldwide against its budget of US$85 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> on August 5, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series:_Insurgent" title="The Divergent Series: Insurgent">The Divergent Series: Insurgent</a></i>, was released on March 20, 2015 in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-debut-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a futuristic <a href="/wiki/Dystopia" title="Dystopia">dystopian</a> Chicago, society is divided into five <a href="/wiki/Factions_(Divergent)" title="Factions (Divergent)">factions</a>: Abnegation (the selfless), Amity (the peaceful), Candor (the honest), Dauntless (the brave), and Erudite (the intellectual). The remaining population are the Factionless, who have no status or privilege in this society. When children reach the age of 16, they undergo a <a href="/wiki/Human_serum_albumin" title="Human serum albumin">serum</a>-induced psychological aptitude test which indicates their best-suited faction, though they are allowed to choose any faction as their permanent group at the subsequent Choosing Ceremony.</p>
<p>Beatrice Prior (<a href="/wiki/Shailene_Woodley" title="Shailene Woodley">Shailene Woodley</a>) was born into Abnegation, which runs the government. Her father, Andrew Prior (<a href="/wiki/Tony_Goldwyn" title="Tony Goldwyn">Tony Goldwyn</a>), serves on the ruling council along with the head of Abnegation, Marcus Eaton (<a href="/wiki/Ray_Stevenson_(actor)" title="Ray Stevenson (actor)">Ray Stevenson</a>). Beatrice takes her test with a Dauntless woman named Tori Wu (<a href="/wiki/Maggie_Q" title="Maggie Q">Maggie Q</a>) as her <a href="/wiki/Proctor" title="Proctor">proctor</a>. Her results show equal attributes of multiple factions, meaning she is Divergent. Her divergence includes Abnegation, Erudite, and Dauntless. Tori records her results as Abnegation and warns her to keep the true result a secret, saying that because Divergents can think independently the government cannot control them and they are considered threats to the existing social order.</p>
<p>The next day at the Choosing Ceremony, Beatrice's brother Caleb Prior (<a href="/wiki/Ansel_Elgort" title="Ansel Elgort">Ansel Elgort</a>) chooses Erudite. After some indecision, Beatrice defects to Dauntless. After the ceremony, Beatrice meets Christina (<a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Kravitz" title="Zoë Kravitz">Zoë Kravitz</a>), Al (<a href="/wiki/Christian_Madsen" title="Christian Madsen">Christian Madsen</a>), and Will (<a href="/wiki/Ben_Lloyd-Hughes" title="Ben Lloyd-Hughes">Ben Lloyd-Hughes</a>), three other initiates from other factions who also chose Dauntless. Christina and Al are from Candor, and Will is from Erudite. The initiates' commitment and fearlessness are immediately tested, and Eric Coulter (<a href="/wiki/Jai_Courtney" title="Jai Courtney">Jai Courtney</a>), a brutal Dauntless leader, makes it clear that anyone not meeting Dauntless' high expectations will be expelled from the faction to become Factionless. Beatrice is the first to volunteer for a leap of faith from a tall building into a dark hole, landing in a net. When Four (<a href="/wiki/Theo_James" title="Theo James">Theo James</a>), a transfer initiates' instructor, asks her name, she shortens it to "Tris" leaving her Abnegation childhood behind.</p>
<p>Tris initially struggles in Dauntless training, ranking far below the cutoff after the first evaluation, but with Four's help slowly improves. Eric matches her against her nemesis — Peter (<a href="/wiki/Miles_Teller" title="Miles Teller">Miles Teller</a>) in a fight. Tris is soundly defeated and wakes up in the infirmary. Informed that she will miss the most important test, Capture The Flag, Tris leaves the infirmary and joins the other initiates, secures her team's victory, and makes the final cut.</p>
<p>In the next phase of training, the initiates face their worst fears in psychological simulations. Divergents are resistant to serums and simulations, so Tris excels at these tests, solving them in peculiar ways, but Four warns her to solve the challenges as a normal Dauntless would in order to hide her abilities.</p>
<p>Tris visits Caleb. She tells him that Erudite is planning to overthrow Abnegation and become the ruling faction. He doesn't believe her. On her return to Dauntless quarters, Tris is attacked by Al, Peter, and Drew before being rescued by Four. The next day Al pleads for Tris' forgiveness but she refuses, calling him a coward. He later kills himself by jumping into "The Chasm" rather than live with the shame.</p>
<p>To prepare her for the final test, Four takes Tris into his own fear simulations, where she learns that he was Tobias Eaton, the son of Marcus Eaton. After the simulation, they kiss. Tris then passes her test and is officially initiated into Dauntless. The Dauntless, including Tris and Four, are injected by Dauntless leadership with a serum supplied by Erudite which is supposedly for tracking, but is actually for mind control.</p>
<p>The next morning, the Dauntless prepare to execute Abnegations by the orders of the Erudites and the Dauntless leadership working with them. Divergents are unaffected by the mind control serum. When the mind controlled Dauntless army are preparing for Abnegation execution, a confused Divergent reveals himself by asking everyone what is happening and is shot immediately by Eric. Witnessing this Tris blends in with the army to avoid suspicion. On the train, she carefully makes her way next to Four. She stands next to him and after a few seconds he reveals himself as a Divergent to Tris for the first time by holding her hand and she is relieved. While the Dauntless move to raid Abnegation, Tris and Four separate from the group and attempt to locate Tris' parents. Eric is suspicious of Four and tests him by pointing his gun at Four. Tris breaks their cover and points her gun at Eric. Their identities as Divergents are revealed to the Dauntless leaders and they both get captured. Four is taken into custody while Tris is ordered to be executed. Her mother Natalie (<a href="/wiki/Ashley_Judd" title="Ashley Judd">Ashley Judd</a>) appears and liberates her. Tris now knows that her mother was also Dauntless. She tells Tris her father's whereabouts and that they have to get back to them immediately. Tris kills one of her friends, Will, and is really affected by the fact that she had to kill her friend to survive. Unfortunately, her mother is soon shot dead as they flee. Tris has to leave her mother's dead body to save herself from being killed.</p>
<p>Tris finds her father in hiding with several Abnegations, including Caleb and Marcus. The group sneaks into Dauntless headquarters, where Tris encounters Peter and forces him to lead them to the Erudite's control center. Her father sacrifices himself in a shootout, and Tris goes in alone to find Four, who is now under stronger mind control designed for Divergents and attacks her. Using her knowledge of his fears, she manages to wake him from the mind control and the two enter the central control room, where Erudite leader Jeanine (<a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a>) is about to have the Dauntless army execute the entire Abnegation faction. Tris uses a sample of the mind control serum on her to force her to cancel the program. The group escapes the compound and boards a train out of the complex.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-production">Pre-production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pre-production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2011, <a href="/wiki/Summit_Entertainment" title="Summit Entertainment">Summit Entertainment</a> picked up the rights for <i>Divergent</i> with <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Wick" title="Douglas Wick">Douglas Wick</a> and Lucy Fisher's production company Red Wagon Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Neil_Burger" title="Neil Burger">Neil Burger</a> was announced as the director on August 23, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Evan_Daugherty" title="Evan Daugherty">Evan Daugherty</a>, who co-wrote the screenplay with <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Taylor" title="Vanessa Taylor">Vanessa Taylor</a>, said, "I get hung up on the toughness of the movie but of equal importance is the love story between Tris and Four. It's inherently and inextricably linked to Tris's character journey. There will be plenty of sexual tension and chemistry, but it's important that all of that stuff doesn't just feel like it's thrown in, but that it all helps Tris grow as a character."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Daugherty further added, "It's tricky because the book is a very packed read with a lot of big ideas. So, distilling that into a cool, faithful two-hour movie is challenging. Not only do you have to establish five factions, but you have to acknowledge that there's a sixth entity, which is the divergent, and you also have the factionless. So there's a world that really has to be built out for the big screen... the movie is going to do it a little more efficiently."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Author <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Roth" title="Veronica Roth">Veronica Roth</a> said about the script of the film: "Reading a script is a really interesting experience. I'd never read a script before. I was really impressed by how closely it stuck to the general plot line of the book."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Initially, the budget of the film was $40 million but <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate" title="Lionsgate">Lionsgate</a> later increased it to $80 million (which finally changed to $85 million) due to the success of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film_series)" title="The Hunger Games (film series)">The Hunger Games</a></i>. Analyst Ben Mogil said, "Divergent is more similar to <i>Hunger Games</i> in that the company owns the underlying economics (i.e. production) and the budget (at $80[million]) is more manageable."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Theo_James_Divergent_premiere.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="501" data-file-width="333" height="301" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Theo_James_Divergent_premiere.jpg/200px-Theo_James_Divergent_premiere.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Theo_James_Divergent_premiere.jpg/300px-Theo_James_Divergent_premiere.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/52/Theo_James_Divergent_premiere.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Theo_James_Divergent_premiere.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Theo_James" title="Theo James">Theo James</a> was cast as Tobias "Four" Eaton, winning the role over several other considered actors.</div>

<p>On October 22, 2012 it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Shailene_Woodley" title="Shailene Woodley">Shailene Woodley</a> had landed the lead role of Beatrice "Tris" Prior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lucas_Till" title="Lucas Till">Lucas Till</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Reynor" title="Jack Reynor">Jack Reynor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Irvine" title="Jeremy Irvine">Jeremy Irvine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Pettyfer" title="Alex Pettyfer">Alex Pettyfer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brenton_Thwaites" title="Brenton Thwaites">Brenton Thwaites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Ludwig" title="Alexander Ludwig">Alexander Ludwig</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Bracey" title="Luke Bracey">Luke Bracey</a> were all considered for the role of Tobias "Four" Eaton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On March 15, 2013, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Theo_James" title="Theo James">Theo James</a> had been cast as Four.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew-four_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew-four-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Though James was 10 years older than the character when cast, Roth praised his casting: "I was sure within seconds: this was 'Four', no question. Theo is able to capture 'Four's' authority and strength, as well as his depth and sensitivity." She also mentions the chemistry between him and Shailene: "He is a perfect match for Shailene's incredibly strong presence as Tris. I'm thrilled!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The producers said about his casting: "We took our time to find the right actor to fill the role of Four, and Theo is definitely the perfect fit. Veronica have crafted a truly iconic character in Four and we cannot wait to begin production and bring him and this story to life for millions of fans around the world."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew-four_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-ew-four-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 11, 2013, it was announced that the roles of Tori, Christina, and Caleb had gone to <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Q" title="Maggie Q">Maggie Q</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Kravitz" title="Zoë Kravitz">Zoë Kravitz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ansel_Elgort" title="Ansel Elgort">Ansel Elgort</a> respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ray_Stevenson_(actor)" title="Ray Stevenson (actor)">Ray Stevenson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jai_Courtney" title="Jai Courtney">Jai Courtney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Eckhart" title="Aaron Eckhart">Aaron Eckhart</a> were announced to be in talks to join the cast on March 15, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Stevenson and Courtney joined the cast as Marcus Eaton and Eric.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> That same day, <a href="/wiki/Miles_Teller" title="Miles Teller">Miles Teller</a> was cast as Peter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a> was announced to be in talks on January 24, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Later, it was confirmed that she would portray Jeanine Matthews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Talking about playing the negative character for the first time, Winslet said, "I'm no idiot. The idea went through my head that I have never played a baddie before, I was almost kind of surprised." Since Winslet joined filming late, she used that distance from her co-stars to appear aloof on the first day of her shoot. "I wanted to break it and say, 'It's OK, I'm really fun. I promise.' But I thought, just for today, I'd let them think that I am a complete bitch."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-z_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-z-29">[29]</a></sup> Winslet was five months pregnant during the shooting of the film and her pregnancy was concealed using various props. According to Burger, "We had to be very strategic in the way that we shot her. She always had some sort of file or case in her hand that was sort of protecting, you know, her bump."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On March 25, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Lamb" title="Ben Lamb">Ben Lamb</a> was cast as Edward, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Lloyd-Hughes" title="Ben Lloyd-Hughes">Ben Lloyd-Hughes</a> as Will, and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Madsen" title="Christian Madsen">Christian Madsen</a> as Al.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In April 2013, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Judd" title="Ashley Judd">Ashley Judd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Goldwyn" title="Tony Goldwyn">Tony Goldwyn</a> joined the cast as Natalie and Andrew Prior, Beatrice's parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="944" data-file-width="800" height="260" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d5/Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg/220px-Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d5/Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg/330px-Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d5/Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg/440px-Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Giant_Wheel_Navy_Pier_Chicago.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Ferris wheel scene in <i>Divergent</i> was filmed at <a href="/wiki/Navy_Pier" title="Navy Pier">Navy Pier</a>.</div>

<p>Filming began in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> on April 16, 2013 and concluded on July 16, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Virtually all production photography took place in Chicago; many of the interior locations were filmed at Cinespace Chicago Film Studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Scenes for the "choosing ceremony" were shot at <a href="/wiki/Seventeenth_Church_of_Christ,_Scientist_(Chicago,_Illinois)" title="Seventeenth Church of Christ, Scientist (Chicago, Illinois)">17th Church of Christ, Scientist, Chicago</a> on East Wacker in downtown Chicago,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> with outdoor shots taken at <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_Court" title="Pioneer Court">Pioneer Court</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Filming also took place at the <a href="/wiki/Navy_Pier" title="Navy Pier">Navy Pier</a> Ferris Wheel; the entire area was treated with dirt and rust to appear more <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dystopian" title="Dystopian">dystopian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Additional scenes were shot at 57th Street and Ellis Avenue near the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> at Federal Street where fake <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago_%27L%27" title="Chicago 'L'">L tracks</a> were constructed<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and at <a href="/wiki/Michigan_Avenue_(Chicago)" title="Michigan Avenue (Chicago)">Michigan Avenue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Scenes were also filmed in the University of Chicago's <a href="/wiki/Joe_and_Rika_Mansueto_Library" title="Joe and Rika Mansueto Library">Joe and Rika Mansueto Library</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the Abnegation sector, the production team constructed the set on <a href="/wiki/Wells_Street_(Chicago)" title="Wells Street (Chicago)">Wells Street</a> in downtown Chicago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In late June, filming took place at 1500 S Western Avenue and 600 S. Wells Street, Chicago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In the last schedule, filming moved to the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_River" title="Chicago River">Chicago River</a> and continued at Lake Street Bridge, Wacker Drive and LaSalle Street Bridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Filming wrapped on July 16, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.27Divergent.27_hits_Comic-Con.21_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-.27Divergent.27_hits_Comic-Con.21-48">[48]</a></sup> On January 24, 2014, additional filming began in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, finishing two days later, which officially became the last day of shooting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.27Divergent.27_hits_Comic-Con.21_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-.27Divergent.27_hits_Comic-Con.21-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-production">Post-production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Post-production" title="Post-production">Post-production</a> work began after filming wrapped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> On July 18, Summit and Lionsgate issued a joint statement announcing the film would be released in <a href="/wiki/IMAX" title="IMAX">IMAX</a> format: "We're delighted to continue our successful collaboration with IMAX, with whom we have already partnered on the global blockbuster <i>Hunger Games</i> and <i>Twilight Saga</i> franchises, and we're especially pleased that we can introduce our newest young adult franchise, <i>Divergent</i>, to the movie going public in the premium IMAX format that celebrates its status as a special and memorable event."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Divergent:_Original_Motion_Picture_Soundtrack" title="Divergent: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack">Divergent: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack</a></div>
<p>The score for <i>Divergent</i> was composed by <a href="/wiki/Junkie_XL" title="Junkie XL">Junkie XL</a> and executive produced by his longtime mentor <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>. Ellie Goulding's Beating Heart was included in the film as original soundtrack and later the assisting video was also released from Goulding's YouTube channel. Randall Poster served as the film's music supervisor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The original soundtrack was released on March 11, 2014, while the Original Score of the film was released on March 18, 2014, by <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first image of Shailene Woodley as Beatrice "Tris" Prior was revealed by <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> on April 24, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> A few seconds sneak preview footage of the film was shown at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2013 Cannes Film Festival">2013 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> On June 7, <i>Entertainment Weekly</i> released a still of Theo James (Four) showing the Dauntless initiates around their new headquarters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> The magazine released several more stills on July 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> On July 16, <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> released the first image of Kate Winslet as Jeanine Matthews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-z_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-z-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 18, 2013 Summit held a sold-out <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con" title="San Diego Comic-Con">San Diego Comic-Con</a> panel in Hall H. <p>On August 22, 2013, a sneak peek of the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teaser_trailer" title="Teaser trailer">teaser trailer</a> was released by <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> The full teaser was released on August 25, 2013 during the pre-show of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Music_Awards" title="MTV Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Two official posters featuring Woodley and James as Tris and Four and highlighting their tattoos were released on September 23, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Neil Burger released the full official <a href="/wiki/Trailer_(promotion)" title="Trailer (promotion)">trailer</a> on November 13, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> On February 4, 2014 Shailene Woodley and Theo James released the final trailer for the film during their appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Divergent</i> earned $150,947,895 in North America, and $137,800,000 in other countries, for a worldwide total of $288,747,895.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-3">[3]</a></sup> Calculating in all expenses, <a href="/wiki/Deadline.com" title="Deadline.com">Deadline.com</a> estimated that the film made a profit of $71.87 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>On its opening weekend, the film attained the No. 1 spot in domestic and worldwide box office rankings by <a href="/wiki/Rentrak" title="Rentrak">Rentrak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> The film grossed $4.9 million in late night screenings, on Thursday March 20, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-debut-11">[11]</a></sup> On its opening day, the film grossed $22.8 million in the United States (including the Thursday night gross).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> <i>Divergent</i> accumulated $54,607,747 from 3,936 theaters at an average of $13,873 per theater, on its opening weekend in the United States and Canada<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> and grossed $1.7 million internationally from four territories, with a worldwide total of $56,307,747.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rentrak_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-rentrak-84">[84]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Critics"></span><span id="Critical_reception"></span><i>Divergent</i> received mixed reviews from critics. Review aggregator <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives the film a 40% rating based on reviews from 200 critics, with an average score of 5.4/10. The site's consensus states: "With an adherence to YA formula that undercuts its individualistic message, <i>Divergent</i> opens its planned trilogy in disappointingly predictable fashion."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gives the film a score of 48 based on reviews from 38 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> Audiences surveyed by CinemaScore were more receptive to the film, giving it a CinemaScore grade of "A". The survey group was 69% female and half of the audience was over 25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> The main criticisms of the film were its generic execution and predictable plot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> Bruce Diones of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i> called it, "barely diverting",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> and Jordan Adler of <i>We Got This Covered</i> said it was a "plodding and generic dystopian drama".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> Several critics have compared the film unfavorably with other <a href="/wiki/Young_adult_fiction" title="Young adult fiction">young adult fiction</a> adaptations such as <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">Harry Potter</a></i> and, predominantly, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film_series)" title="The Hunger Games (film series)">The Hunger Games</a></i>. Andrew Barker of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_Film_Reviews" title="Variety Film Reviews">Variety</a></i> said, "Unlike the “<i>Harry Potter</i>” series’ tangible, fully dimensional Hogwarts or “<i>The Hunger Games</i>’” colorfully variegated districts, “<i>Divergent</i>’s” vision of new Chicago doesn’t have much to distinguish it from a standard-issue post-apocalyptic pic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Travers" title="Peter Travers">Peter Travers</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> wrote that, "At least <i>The Hunger Games</i> spawned two terrific movies and a breakthrough star in <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lawrence" title="Jennifer Lawrence">Jennifer Lawrence</a>. Onscreen, <i>Divergent</i> ignites only indifference."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> However, Peter Debruge of <i>Variety</i> considered it a much better adaptation writing that, "[although] it shares a fair amount of DNA in common with “<i>The Hunger Games</i>,” it ranks as far superior."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<p>Though the film overall received a mixed reception, many critics praised Shailene Woodley and Theo James for their portrayals as Beatrice Prior and Tobias Eaton. According to Todd Gilchrist of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiewire" title="Indiewire">The Playlist</a>, "Woodley makes for more than uncertain enough of a hero to add detail and meaning to the implosion of this world", adding that "there's little artifice to her performance, and the mundane honesty of her reactions create a believability that the world would otherwise lack." Speaking of James's performance, Gilchrist adds that he "manages the considerable accomplishment of seeming like a real grown-up man" and that he "makes the character's transformation from hardass to collaborator seem natural, if inevitable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blogs.indiewire.com_97-0"><a href="#cite_note-blogs.indiewire.com-97">[97]</a></sup> Drew McWeeny at <a href="/wiki/HitFix" title="HitFix">HitFix</a> said, "it helps that [they] got Woodley and James in the leads. [Woodley's] like a walking empathy battery, wide-open emotionally, easy to read and enormously appealing", also adding that James is "incredibly natural onscreen".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup></p>
<p>Andrew Osmond of <i><a href="/wiki/SFX_(magazine)" title="SFX (magazine)">SFX</a></i> magazine was more receptive to the film, calling it "an often entertaining, and sometimes very interesting, piece of teen SF".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> Andy Lea of <i>Daily Star</i> wrote that "the satire is not as sharp as that of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film)" title="The Hunger Games (film)">The Hunger Games</a></i> and the training scenes borrow heavily from the much smarter <i><a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game_(film)" title="Ender's Game (film)">Ender's Game</a></i>. But it's also stylish, tense and surprisingly pacy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Orlando_Weekly" title="Orlando Weekly">Orlando Weekly</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Sam Allard gave the film a 3/5 rating, praising Shailene Woodley's performance by saying she "rescues and then raises up a film that could have been an utter disaster".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> Christy Lemire of RogerEbert.com agreed with this sentiment, writing, "the performances—namely from stars Shailene Woodley and Theo James and Kate Winslet in a juicy supporting role—always make the movie watchable and often quite engaging".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup> Michael O'Sullivan of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> felt that the film surpassed its source material, feeling that "it's rare that a movie is as good as the book on which it's based. It's even more unusual when it's better."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> Leigh Paatsch of the <i><a href="/wiki/Herald_Sun" title="Herald Sun">Herald Sun</a></i> dubbed the film a "solid first-up effort for the <i>Divergent</i> franchise",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup> and Margaret Pomeranz of <i><a href="/wiki/At_the_Movies_(Australian_TV_series)" title="At the Movies (Australian TV series)">At the Movies</a></i> gave praise to director Neil Burger, stating that he "handles the action with aplomb".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Playlist's Todd Gilchrist gave it a mixed review saying that it has "great ideas ... and some terrific character work, but it's given such uneven attention, alternately languished upon and glossed over".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blogs.indiewire.com_97-1"><a href="#cite_note-blogs.indiewire.com-97">[97]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>'s Matt Patches gave it 5.8 out of 10, citing its similarities to other young adult films and contrived Dauntless plot line. He praised lead actors Theo James and Shailene Woodley's performances, judging that they "add personality and physicality to the limp script they're acting out".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> Scott Mendelson of <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine echoed these sentiments, arguing that despite Woodley's excellent performance, "the generic story reduced a large portion of the mythology to irrelevancy". Mendelson believed that the film would please the novel's fanbase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequels">Sequels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Divergent_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Sequels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series:_Insurgent" title="The Divergent Series: Insurgent">The Divergent Series: Insurgent</a></div>
<p>On May 7, 2013, Summit Entertainment revealed that a sequel based on <i><a href="/wiki/Insurgent_(novel)" title="Insurgent (novel)">Insurgent</a></i> was in the works. Brian Duffield, writer of <i><a href="/wiki/Jane_Got_a_Gun" title="Jane Got a Gun">Jane Got a Gun</a></i>, was hired to write the script.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-111"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup> The sequel was released on March 20, 2015. The third film, based on the novel <i><a href="/wiki/Allegiant_(novel)" title="Allegiant (novel)">Allegiant</a></i>, was initially announced as a single film scheduled for release on March 18, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-113"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup> In December 2013, it was announced that Neil Burger will not return to direct the sequel, due to him still working on the first film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> On February 11, 2014, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Robert_Schwentke" title="Robert Schwentke">Robert Schwentke</a> would take Burger's place for the next installment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-debut-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-115"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-116"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> for <i>The Divergent Series: Insurgent</i> began in Atlanta on May 27, 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-117"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup> and concluded on September 6, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-118"><a href="#cite_note-118">[118]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 11, 2014, Summit Entertainment announced that the third film would split into two films titled <i>The Divergent Series: Allegiant – Part 1</i> and <i>The Divergent Series: Allegiant – Part 2</i>, with <i>Part 1</i> scheduled to be released on March 18, 2016 and <i>Part 2</i> scheduled to be released on June 9, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-119"><a href="#cite_note-119">[119]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-move_back_120-0"><a href="#cite_note-move_back-120">[120]</a></sup> On December 5, 2014, it was announced that Robert Schwentke would return to direct <i>Part 1</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-121"><a href="#cite_note-121">[121]</a></sup> On September 10, 2015, it was announced that the two films would be re-titled, with <i>Part 1</i> being renamed as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series:_Allegiant" title="The Divergent Series: Allegiant">Allegiant</a></i> and <i>Part 2</i> as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series:_Ascendant" title="The Divergent Series: Ascendant">Ascendant</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-122"><a href="#cite_note-122">[122]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 8, 2016, it was reported that Robert Schwentke, who directed both <i>Insurgent</i> and <i>Allegiant</i>, would not return to direct for the final film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-123"><a href="#cite_note-123">[123]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lee_Toland_Krieger" title="Lee Toland Krieger">Lee Toland Krieger</a> will replace Schwentke as the director of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-124"><a href="#cite_note-124">[124]</a></sup></p>
