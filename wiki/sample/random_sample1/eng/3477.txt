<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Taishō Tripiṭaka</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Taishō Tripiṭaka</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">大正新脩大藏經</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Dàzhèng Xīnxīu Dàzàngjīng</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <i>Taishō Shinshū Daizōkyō</i>; <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <i>Taishō Revised Tripiṭaka</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Taisho_Tripitaka_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Taisho_Tripitaka-1">[1]</a></sup> is a definitive edition of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Buddhist_canon" title="Chinese Buddhist canon">Chinese Buddhist canon</a> and its Japanese commentaries used by scholars in the 20th century. It was edited by <a href="/wiki/Takakusu_Junjiro" title="Takakusu Junjiro">Takakusu Junjiro</a> and others.</p>
<p>Volumes 1–85 are the literature, in which volumes 56–84 are Japanese Buddhist literature, written in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese" title="Traditional Chinese">traditional Chinese</a>. Volumes 86–97 are Buddhism related drawings, includes drawings of many <a href="/wiki/Buddhahood" title="Buddhahood">Buddhas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bodhisattva" title="Bodhisattva">bodhisattvas</a>. Volumes 98–100 are texts of different indexes of Buddhist texts in Japan. The 85 volumes of literature contains 5,320 individual texts, classified as follows.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Digitalization"><span id="Digitalization"></span>Digitalization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taish%C5%8D_Tripi%E1%B9%ADaka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Digitalization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The SAT Daizōkyō Text Database edition contains volumes 1–85. The Chinese Buddhist Electronic Text Association (CBETA) edition contains volumes 1–55 and 85. The Fomei edition (佛梅電子大藏經) contains texts in Classical Chinese other than <a href="/wiki/Nichiren_Buddhism" title="Nichiren Buddhism">Nichiren Buddhism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Volumes 86-100, although they were written in Classical Chinese, are actually by Japanese Buddhist scholars of the modern period.</p>
