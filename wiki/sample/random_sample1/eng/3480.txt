<p><b>Dianna Elise Agron</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywoodlife_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodlife-1">[1]</a></sup> born April 30, 1986) is an American actress, singer, dancer, and music video director. In 2006, Agron made her television debut as Jessica Grant on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_NY" title="CSI: NY">CSI: NY</a></i>. From 2006 to 2007, Agron had recurring roles on <i><a href="/wiki/Veronica_Mars" title="Veronica Mars">Veronica Mars</a></i> as Jenny Budosh, and <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_(TV_series)" title="Heroes (TV series)">Heroes</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Debbie_Marshall" title="Debbie Marshall">Debbie Marshall</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, Agron was cast as <a href="/wiki/Quinn_Fabray" title="Quinn Fabray">Quinn Fabray</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> musical comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Emmy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Emmy-2">[2]</a></sup> Agron has appeared in the films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunters_(2011_film)" title="The Hunters (2011 film)">The Hunters</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Number_Four_(film)" title="I Am Number Four (film)">I Am Number Four</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Family_(2013_film)" title="The Family (2013 film)">The Family</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Zipper_(film)" title="Zipper (film)">Zipper</a></i> (2015), and <i><a href="/wiki/Bare_(film)" title="Bare (film)">Bare</a></i> (2015).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dianna_Agron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agron was born in <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>, and raised in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Antonio,_Texas" title="San Antonio, Texas">San Antonio, Texas</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, the daughter of Mary (née Barnes) and Ronald S. Agron, a general manager of <a href="/wiki/Hyatt" title="Hyatt">Hyatt</a> hotels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gleesh_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-gleesh-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Movieline_01_2011_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Movieline_01_2011-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-womenshealthmag1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-womenshealthmag1-6">[6]</a></sup> Due to her father's career, Agron said she and her family lived in different hotels growing up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She has a younger brother, Jason. Her father was born to a Jewish family and her mother <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism" title="Conversion to Judaism">converted</a> to <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a>. Agron attended <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_school" title="Hebrew school">Hebrew school</a> and had a <a href="/wiki/Bar_and_Bat_Mitzvah" title="Bar and Bat Mitzvah">Bat Mitzvah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gleesh_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-gleesh-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref1-8">[8]</a></sup> Agron's paternal great-grandparents were Jewish immigrants from Eastern Europe, and according to her, their original surname, Agronsky, was altered by <a href="/wiki/Ellis_Island" title="Ellis Island">Ellis Island</a> officials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywoodlife_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodlife-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>When she was 15, she found out that her father had <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a>. In an interview for <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_(magazine)" title="Cosmopolitan (magazine)">Cosmopolitan</a></i>, she revealed, "Quite a bit changed after that. At that age, you don't see mortality in your parents." The disease caused her parents' relationship to fall apart, and they decided to separate, which was devastating for her and her younger brother. She stated, "I had to play therapist to my family... be the glue. Those kinds of things I'm not ready to speak about yet."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agron attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burlingame_Intermediate_School" title="Burlingame Intermediate School">Burlingame Intermediate School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burlingame_High_School_(California)" title="Burlingame High School (California)">Burlingame High School</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Emmy_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Emmy-2">[2]</a></sup> where she was in the Homecoming Court,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and played the part of Marty in <i><a href="/wiki/Grease_(musical)" title="Grease (musical)">Grease</a></i>; she was also involved in set design, costumes, and painting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grease_as_Marty_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grease_as_Marty-11">[11]</a></sup> She has been dancing since the age of three, focusing mainly in jazz and ballet, and later began hip-hop dancing. Agron then fell in love with musical theater, and often performed in local and school productions. She played <a href="/wiki/Dorothy_Gale" title="Dorothy Gale">Dorothy Gale</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_of_Oz_(1987_musical)" title="The Wizard of Oz (1987 musical)">The Wizard of Oz</a></i> in fifth grade,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and began teaching dance as a teenager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-womenshealthmag1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-womenshealthmag1-6">[6]</a></sup> She says she was not "popular" in the stereotypical sense in high school, though she had many friends from different cliques around the school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glee_Interview_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Glee_Interview-13">[13]</a></sup> As a teenager, Agron worked at a local boutique, where she "became enthralled with fashion," and was on her high school's yearbook committee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>Agron has appeared on television series such as <i><a href="/wiki/Shark_(TV_series)" title="Shark (TV series)">Shark</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Close_to_Home_(2005_TV_series)" title="Close to Home (2005 TV series)">Close to Home</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_NY" title="CSI: NY">CSI: NY</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Numb3rs" title="Numb3rs">Numb3rs</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Veronica_Mars" title="Veronica Mars">Veronica Mars</a></i>. She appeared as Harper in a 13-episode series of short films, <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Mall_World" title="It's a Mall World">It's a Mall World</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Milo_Ventimiglia" title="Milo Ventimiglia">Milo Ventimiglia</a>, that aired on MTV. She then had a recurring role on the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_(TV_series)" title="Heroes (TV series)">Heroes</a></i> as Debbie Marshall, the captain of the cheerleading squad at Costa Verde High School. After this role, she was only offered horror films that included nudity, and turned them all down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agron's most notable role to date is as <a href="/wiki/Quinn_Fabray" title="Quinn Fabray">Quinn Fabray</a>, a high school cheerleader, on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i>. Agron was the last primary actor to be cast, having won the role only days before the pilot began filming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huli_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-huli-17">[17]</a></sup> Agron said in a 2009 interview pertaining to her casting session, "I nearly bailed on my audition for the show. I was so nervous." Before being cast as Quinn, the show's producers wondered if she appeared too innocent. Agron said in an interview, "They told me to come back with straight hair and to dress sexier. Later that week, I started work."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dianna_Agron_2009_Interview_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dianna_Agron_2009_Interview-18">[18]</a></sup> She auditioned with <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Fly_Me_to_the_Moon" title="Fly Me to the Moon">Fly Me to the Moon</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huli_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-huli-17">[17]</a></sup> Quinn is described by Agron as <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Berry" title="Rachel Berry">Rachel Berry</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Lea_Michele" title="Lea Michele">Lea Michele</a>) enemy, and "terrible, the meanest girl."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouTube_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube-19">[19]</a></sup> The character was originally conceived as the antagonistic queen bee head cheerleader, a departure from Agron's actual high school experience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HitFix_Interview_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-HitFix_Interview-20">[20]</a></sup> The role saw Agron nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teen_Choice_Award" title="Teen Choice Award">Teen Choice Award</a> for Female Breakout Star in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> She and the other cast members were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_an_Ensemble_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series">Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series</a> in 2010, and were nominated in the same category the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>On July 23, 2012, it was reported that Agron was going to appear less frequently in <i>Glee</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s fourth season, being demoted to a guest star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Agron's character only appeared on three episodes: "<a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving_(Glee)" title="Thanksgiving (Glee)">Thanksgiving</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Naked_(Glee)" title="Naked (Glee)">Naked</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/I_Do_(Glee)" title="I Do (Glee)">I Do</a>". <p>In 2011, Agron appeared as Alice in the horror thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunters_(2011_film)" title="The Hunters (2011 film)">The Hunters</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> and as Sarah Hart in the science-fiction action thriller <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Number_Four_(film)" title="I Am Number Four (film)">I Am Number Four</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Agron appeared in the concert film of <i>Glee</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Glee:_The_3D_Concert_Movie" title="Glee: The 3D Concert Movie">Glee: The 3D Concert Movie</a></i>. She then played Belle Blake in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Family_(2013_film)" title="The Family (2013 film)">The Family</a></i>, opposite <a href="/wiki/Robert_De_Niro" title="Robert De Niro">Robert De Niro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee_Jones" title="Tommy Lee Jones">Tommy Lee Jones</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Pfeiffer" title="Michelle Pfeiffer">Michelle Pfeiffer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The film was released on September 13, 2013, and received mixed to negative reviews. <i>The Family</i> debuted at #2 in its first weekend with $14.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Critics praised Agron's performance. <p>In 2015, Agron appeared in the political thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Zipper_(film)" title="Zipper (film)">Zipper</a></i> as Dalia, the intern of a lawyer who is running for office. The film also starred <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Wilson_(American_actor)" title="Patrick Wilson (American actor)">Patrick Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lena_Headey" title="Lena Headey">Lena Headey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Cho" title="John Cho">John Cho</a>. <i>Zipper</i> premiered on January 27, 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>, and was released on August 28, 2015 in a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> and through <a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">video on demand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Agron then co-starred in the romantic comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Tumbledown_(2015_film)" title="Tumbledown (2015 film)">Tumbledown</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Jason_Sudeikis" title="Jason Sudeikis">Jason Sudeikis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Hall" title="Rebecca Hall">Rebecca Hall</a>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> That same year, she had her first lead role as Sarah Barton in the drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Bare_(film)" title="Bare (film)">Bare</a></i>, which also premiered at Tribeca. It was written and directed by Natalia Leite and follows a young girl living in a small desert town in Nevada as she becomes romantically involved with a female drifter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Paz_de_la_Huerta" title="Paz de la Huerta">Paz de la Huerta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Zylka" title="Chris Zylka">Chris Zylka</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louisa_Krause" title="Louisa Krause">Louisa Krause</a> also starred in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agron is part of the main cast of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Crash_(2017_film)" title="The Crash (2017 film)">The Crash</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Frank_Grillo" title="Frank Grillo">Frank Grillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/AnnaSophia_Robb" title="AnnaSophia Robb">AnnaSophia Robb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnie_Driver" title="Minnie Driver">Minnie Driver</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ed_Westwick" title="Ed Westwick">Ed Westwick</a>, which was released on January 13, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> She also stars in <i><a href="/wiki/Novitiate_(film)" title="Novitiate (film)">Novitiate</a></i>, portraying a young nun in a convent, opposite <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Leo" title="Melissa Leo">Melissa Leo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Qualley" title="Margaret Qualley">Margaret Qualley</a>, which is set to premiere at the <a href="/wiki/2017_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2017 Sundance Film Festival">2017 Sundance Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Agron is set to co-star in the thriller <i>Headlock</i>, with <a href="/wiki/Andy_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Andy García">Andy García</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bartha" title="Justin Bartha">Justin Bartha</a>, and <a href="/wiki/James_Frain" title="James Frain">James Frain</a>, and will portray the lead in the drama <i>Hollow in the Land</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dianna_Agron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Agron made her stage debut in the play <i><a href="/wiki/McQueen_(play)" title="McQueen (play)">McQueen</a></i>, based on the life of the designer <a href="/wiki/Alexander_McQueen" title="Alexander McQueen">Alexander McQueen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Stephen_Wight" title="Stephen Wight">Stephen Wight</a>). She portrayed the female lead, Dahlia, a fan who breaks into the designer's home to steal a dress and is caught by McQueen, The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantasy_(genre)" title="Fantasy (genre)">fantasy</a>-based play was written by <a href="/wiki/James_Phillips_(playwright)" title="James Phillips (playwright)">James Phillips</a> for the fifth anniversary of McQueen's death. The play ran from May 12 to June 6, 2015 at London's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._James_Theatre,_London" title="St. James Theatre, London">St. James Theatre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Agron was unable to reprise her role as Dahlia in the <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End theatre</a> re-run due to "filming commitments".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WET_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-WET-57">[57]</a></sup></p>

Jen Chaney of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> gave the song a "B-", and said it "worked much better than every track that preceded it" because it adapted the song to the show "instead of trying to out-Jackson Jackson."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WP2_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-WP2-64">[64]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> Joseph Brannigan Lynch called it "a nice summation of her character's journey, but not vocally impressive enough to justify listening to outside of the episode" and gave it a "B" rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW2_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW2-65">[65]</a></sup> Crystal Bell of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huffington_Post" title="Huffington Post">HuffPost TV</a></i> described it as a "blah performance", but Kate Stanhope of <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> said it was "sweet and reflective."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HP2_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-HP2-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVG2_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVG2-67">[67]</a></sup> Erica Futterman of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> wrote that it was "a tune well-suited for Quinn's sultry voice and the flipped meaning she gives the lyrics", and <i><a href="/wiki/TVLine" title="TVLine">TVLine</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> Michael Slezak had a similar take: he gave it an "A" rating and called it a "remarkably lovely fit" for her voice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS2_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-RS2-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVL2_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVL2-69">[69]</a></sup> She has also sung in many cast group songs and duets.</p>
<p>In 2011, when asked about if she is interested in a solo album, Agron said that she is more focused on acting. But later, in December 2013, she said it is not possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GaloreInterview_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-GaloreInterview-70">[70]</a></sup> In February 2013, Agron hosted her very first You, Me and Charlie concert in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, where she performed <a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Mac" title="Fleetwood Mac">Fleetwood Mac</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Dreams_(Fleetwood_Mac_song)" title="Dreams (Fleetwood Mac song)">Dreams</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Tina_Turner" title="Tina Turner">Tina Turner</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/What%27s_Love_Got_to_Do_with_It_(song)" title="What's Love Got to Do with It (song)">What's Love Got to Do with It</a>" featuring the band A House For Lions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> In November 2014, Agron, among many other international artists, was featured in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Children%27s_Fund" title="United Nations Children's Fund">United Nations Children's Fund</a> (<a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>)'s charity single "<a href="/wiki/Imagine_(John_Lennon_song)" title="Imagine (John Lennon song)">Imagine</a>" which was originally performed by <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July, 2015, Agron performed the <a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">The Star-Spangled Banner</a> for the 239th Anniversary of Independence at the <a href="/wiki/Winfield_House" title="Winfield House">Winfield House</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_videos">Music videos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dianna_Agron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Music videos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Agron directed the music video for "Body" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thao_%26_The_Get_Down_Stay_Down" title="Thao &amp; The Get Down Stay Down">Thao &amp; The Get Down Stay Down</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> On November 25, 2013, the music video of "<a href="/wiki/Just_Another_Girl_(The_Killers_song)" title="Just Another Girl (The Killers song)">Just Another Girl</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Killers" title="The Killers">The Killers</a> was released, in which Agron portrays the lead singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> Agron starred as a scorned bride in <a href="/wiki/Sam_Smith_(singer)" title="Sam Smith (singer)">Sam Smith</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Not_the_Only_One" title="I'm Not the Only One">I'm Not the Only One</a>" music video which was released on August 1, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> In 2014, Agron directed the music video for "Till Sunrise" by <a href="/wiki/Goldroom" title="Goldroom">Goldroom</a>. Her brother, Jason, and actress Gabby Haugh star as a romantic couple.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dianna_Agron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Agron wrote, starred in, directed, and executive-produced an unreleased <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy</a> film called <i><a href="/wiki/A_Fuchsia_Elephant" title="A Fuchsia Elephant">A Fuchsia Elephant</a></i>. The plot revolves around Agron's character. On the day before her eighteenth birthday, Charlotte Hill makes a decision to change. Not wanting to follow in the footsteps of her alcoholic mother, she enlists a sober partner named Michael (<a href="/wiki/Dave_Franco" title="Dave Franco">Dave Franco</a>) to help guide her. It was shot during the <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i> hiatus in the summer of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> That same year, Agron hosted a mini music festival for <a href="/wiki/826LA" title="826LA">826LA</a> called Chickens in Love.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> Agron hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GLAAD_Media_Awards" title="GLAAD Media Awards">GLAAD Media Awards</a> on June 2, 2012, in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> In 2012 and 2013, Agron appeared in the "Play As You Are" <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> campaign, ads for Art Academy: Lessons for Everyone!, and in spots for the puzzle-solving adventure Professor Layton and the Miracle Mask.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nintendo_3DS_Campaign_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nintendo_3DS_Campaign-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agron spoke at the 18th San Francisco Power of Choice Luncheon to celebrate the 40th Anniversary of Roe V. Wade – 40 years of Choice! on March 7, 2013, in San Francisco,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> and attended <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Choo" title="Jimmy Choo">Jimmy Choo</a> 2nd Annual 25 Most Powerful Stylists Luncheon on March 13, 2013 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Hollywood" title="West Hollywood">West Hollywood</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> For the February 2014 issue of the <i>Galore</i> magazine, Dianna worked as the photographer. Her brother, Jason Agron, also helped for the shoot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> After directing Thao &amp; The Get Down Stay Down's music video "Body", and her independent unreleased film <i>A Fuchsia Elephant</i>, Agron stated that she would like to continue directing different projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> In May 2015, she directed a video for <a href="/wiki/Tory_Burch" title="Tory Burch">Tory Burch</a>'s Paris collection that will come out later in the year and she was later selected by Burch to represent the brand at the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Met_Gala" title="Met Gala">Met Gala</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dianna_Agron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agron and her <i>Glee</i> co-star Lea Michele rented an apartment together in downtown <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in early 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> Agron underwent nose surgery twice to repair her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deviated_septum" title="Deviated septum">deviated septum</a>, once the result of a blow to the nose when she was fourteen in a party, and once in an accident while on tour for <a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)"><i>Glee</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> Rumors that she was engaged to actor <a href="/wiki/Alex_Pettyfer" title="Alex Pettyfer">Alex Pettyfer</a> in 2011 are unconfirmed. In late 2015, she became engaged to <a href="/wiki/Winston_Marshall" title="Winston Marshall">Winston Marshall</a>, from the band <a href="/wiki/Mumford_%26_Sons" title="Mumford &amp; Sons">Mumford &amp; Sons</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> They were married on October 15, 2016, in <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dianna_Agron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agron has supported <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PETA" title="PETA">PETA</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LGBT_rights" title="LGBT rights">LGBT rights</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> Using her Tumblr account as a starting point, Agron released her website You, Me and Charlie on December 12, 2011. Along with help from several other contributors, she writes and collects posts, which subjects vary from music, art, fashion, and daily inspiration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> The site serves as inspiration for many young artists. <i><a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (magazine)">Vanity Fair</a></i> has complimented the site, stating that the site is "full of sunshine, optimism, and pretty people."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> Agron hosted the GLAAD Media Awards on June 2, 2012 in San Francisco. Her co-stars of <i>Glee</i>, <a href="/wiki/Naya_Rivera" title="Naya Rivera">Naya Rivera</a> and Cory Monteith hosted them on March 24, 2012 in New York City. Following Rivera's tradition of auctioning off kisses to an audience member, she raised $5,500 for the campaign. Agron worked with <a href="/wiki/The_Trevor_Project" title="The Trevor Project">The Trevor Project</a> in 2012 to raise money in honor of her birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> Also in 2012, Agron visited the <a href="/wiki/Kampong_Cham_Province" title="Kampong Cham Province">Kampong Cham Center</a>, where she met children and teenage residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> On April 20, 2013, Agron's fans honored her by raising $10,200 for her birthday, benefiting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Somaly_Mam_Foundation" title="Somaly Mam Foundation">Somaly Mam Foundation</a> in effort to fight human trafficking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agron donated possessions to the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_Veterans_of_America" title="Vietnam Veterans of America">Vietnam Veterans of America</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> during August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> Agron also supports the initiative between <a href="/wiki/Camp_Wonder" title="Camp Wonder">Camp Wonder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cetaphil" title="Cetaphil">Cetaphil</a>, which is about children with chronic and life-threatening skin diseases to enjoy being a kid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup> On February 22, 2014, Agron participated along with other <i>Glee</i> stars in the <a href="/wiki/Young_Storytellers_Foundation" title="Young Storytellers Foundation">Young Storytellers Foundation</a> biggest show, <i>Glee Big Show</i>, which featured live performances of five scripts written by 5th Grade Young Storytellers to support art programs in public schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup> In June 2014, Agron joined with other celebrities and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_Slick" title="Big Slick">Big Slick</a> Foundation weekend charity event to help raise funds for Children's Mercy Hospital of <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>. Among the events Agron was involved in was a celebrity softball game, a private get-together for patients and celebrities, a bowling tournament, and a celebrity party and auction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> In December 2014, Agron provided services and live entertainment to United States troops and their families as part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Service_Organizations" title="United Service Organizations">United Service Organizations</a> tour at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bagram_air_field" title="Bagram air field">Bagram air field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup> Later that month, she attended to the 10th anniversary gala of a <a href="/wiki/ASmallWorld" title="ASmallWorld">ASmallWorld</a> and sold a kiss for more than $20,000. She donated the money to the <a href="/wiki/War_Child_(charity)" title="War Child (charity)">War Child</a> charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2016, she traveled with the UN to visit resettled <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrian</a> refugees in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> She has stated, in response to questions about her intent, that she "came here to help tell the stories of specific individuals because when you meet one of the 60 million forced to flee their home, and you put a name and a face to a number, the global refugee crisis becomes impossible to ignore."</p>
