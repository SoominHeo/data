<p><b>Taiki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大紀町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Taiki-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Watarai_District,_Mie" title="Watarai District, Mie">Watarai District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of September 2012, the town had an estimated population of 9,543 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 40.9 persons per km². The total area was 233.54 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiki,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taiki is located in eastern <a href="/wiki/Kii_Peninsula" title="Kii Peninsula">Kii Peninsula</a>, in south-central Mie Prefecture. The town is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Ise_Bay" title="Ise Bay">Ise Bay</a> to the east.</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Watarai,_Mie" title="Watarai, Mie">Watarai</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiki,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tamaki was the site of a <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_castle" title="Japanese castle">Japanese castle</a>, Tamaru Castle, initially built by the <a href="/wiki/Kitabatake_clan" title="Kitabatake clan">Kitabatake clan</a>, which dominated the surrounding area, including access to the <a href="/wiki/Ise_Grand_Shrine" title="Ise Grand Shrine">Ise Grand Shrines</a>. The castle was destroyed in the early <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>.</p>
<p>Taiki Town was formed on February 14, 2005 from the merger of the towns of <a href="/wiki/Kisei,_Mie" title="Kisei, Mie">Kisei</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmiya,_Mie" title="Ōmiya, Mie">Ōmiya</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cuchiyama,_Mie" title="Ōuchiyama, Mie">Ōuchiyama</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiki,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town serves as a commercial center for the surrounding region. <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">Commercial fishing</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dairy" title="Dairy">dairy</a> industry play important roles in the local economy.</p>
