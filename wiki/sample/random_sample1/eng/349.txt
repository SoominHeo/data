<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.ee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The top-level domain .ee was introduced in 1992 and was operated by <a href="/wiki/EENet" title="EENet">EENet</a> until July 2010. The administrator of .ee domain was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academia" title="Academia">academic</a> <a href="/wiki/Endel_Lippmaa" title="Endel Lippmaa">Endel Lippmaa</a>. There was a limit of one domain name per legal entity, and registrations of additional names to protect trademarks were specifically denied because "The domain name has no trademark status". As domains under .ee were meant to be an institution's identification on the Internet (like the register code in the commercial register), registration of additional domains in the defense of a trademark or a name form is not possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notrademarkstatus_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-notrademarkstatus-4">[4]</a></sup> Valid registrations were free of charge.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_relaunch">Domain relaunch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.ee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Domain relaunch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The new order of registration of .ee TLD became available on 5 July 2010. Estonian Internet Foundation took over .ee ccTLD registry functions from EENet. All existing domains registered before the transition had to be re-registered during a 6-month transition period which ended on 5 January 2011. Registration services can be done through accredited registrars listed on Estonian Internet Foundation website.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_relaunch">After relaunch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.ee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: After relaunch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>New .ee domain rules allow unlimited number of domains to be registered by private and legal persons directly under .ee. Foreign entities and persons can register .ee domains. Domain disputes are handled by Domain Disputes Committee. Domains can be registered and renewed for 1, 2 and 3 years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Registration_fee">Registration fee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.ee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Registration fee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Registration fee for registrars is set to 7, 14, 21 <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euros</a> plus tax in 2016 depending on the registration period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The final cost is influenced by competition on the market.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.ee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The new order of domain registering was met with great controversy. The relaunch of the registry was at one point delayed to be reassessed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> When the relaunch was finalized, the annual fee for a .ee domain was announced to be 285 Estonian kroons (~24 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>) plus <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">tax</a>, which was met with wide criticism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-epl-expensive_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-epl-expensive-7">[7]</a></sup> A list of shortcomings was presented in a public notice to Estonian Internet Foundation by <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B5nu_Samuel" title="TÃµnu Samuel">TÃµnu Samuel</a> and Jaan JÃ¤nesmÃ¤e, mentioning that the .ee domain had become the most expensive TLD in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-epl-expensive_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-epl-expensive-7">[7]</a></sup> Their cause gained over 800 supporters on Facebook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eic-goals_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-eic-goals-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">NGO</a> Estonian Internet Community was founded partly as a reaction to the controversial domain reform with 22 founding members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eic-goals_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-eic-goals-8">[8]</a></sup> Its board is made up of <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B5nu_Samuel" title="TÃµnu Samuel">TÃµnu Samuel</a>, Elver Loho and Heiti Kender. Recently it noted on its blog that several of its members had lost faith in the possibility of any sort of compromise and had independently written e-mails to <a href="/wiki/Internet_Assigned_Numbers_Authority" title="Internet Assigned Numbers Authority">IANA</a> asking them to step in to resolve the controversy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eic-iana_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-eic-iana-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 December 2015, it was announced that the annual fee for a .ee domain would be lowered to about 110 Estonian kroons (~8 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
