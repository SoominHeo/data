<p><b>Tomy Company, Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">株式会社タカラトミー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kabushikigaisha takaratomī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <b>Takara Tomy</b>)</span> is a Japanese entertainment company that makes children's toys and merchandise. It was created from a merger on 1 March 2006 of two companies: <b>Tomy</b> (founded in 1924 as Tomiyama, changing the name to Tomy in 1963<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomy-pdf_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tomy-pdf-3">[3]</a></sup>) and long-time rival <a href="/wiki/Takara" title="Takara">Takara</a> (founded in 1955).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomy-pdf_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tomy-pdf-3">[3]</a></sup> The company has its headquarters in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Katsushika,_Tokyo" title="Katsushika, Tokyo">Katsushika, Tokyo</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_corporate_name">History and corporate name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and corporate name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company made a pragmatic decision of which name to use after the merger, deciding to use "Tomy" in international subsidiaries and "Takara-Tomy<i>"</i> in Japan. They came to this decision because;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Tomy has built considerable brand recognition internationally (particularly in the area of infant and preschool products, where brand trust is very important).</li>
<li>The majority of Takara's international hit products (<a href="/wiki/Microman" title="Microman">Microman</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transformers_(toyline)" title="Transformers (toyline)">Transformers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_Beasts" title="Battle Beasts">Battle Beasts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beyblade" title="Beyblade">Beyblade</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-Daman" title="B-Daman">B-Daman</a> etc.) have been sold and branded by other toy companies such as <a href="/wiki/Hasbro" title="Hasbro">Hasbro</a></li>
<li>Changing their name would have been unproductive and expensive.</li>
</ul>
<p>Therefore, it seemed appropriate to keep a domestic English name which matched that of international subsidiaries.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>One of the editorial policies of many Western business publications is to ignore merger declarations and declare that one company is being bought by another. Therefore, in much of the Western media, the Takara-Tomy merger was characterised as a 'take-over' of Takara by Tomy. This assumption may have been made because several years of losses had put Takara in a financially weakened state at the time of the merger, although Takara did have significantly higher sales than Tomy. However, the management of Takara and Tomy had discussed merging several times, including at times when Takara appeared stronger. Under Japanese corporate law, the move was a merger of both companies on an equal basis.</p>
<p>There has been much post-merger media speculation about the control of brands from the Takara-Tomy merger. Much of this arose from the new use of a "TOMY" copyright on all packaging, including former Takara brands shipped by Hasbro. However, that was simply the result of a practical decision to use only the Tomy name in international subsidiaries. In Japan, <i>Takara-Tomy</i> continues to use both Tomy and Takara as distinct brand names on toy ranges which originated in each separate company, and most new toy ranges or stand-alone products now carry the new Takara-Tomy brand.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Takara" title="Takara">Takara</a> purchased a majority stake in <a href="/wiki/Tatsunoko_Production" title="Tatsunoko Production">Tatsunoko Production</a> in June 2005. The studio then became a complete subsidiary of Takara-Tomy, following the March 2006 merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Tomy UK was founded in 1982 for the sale and distribution of Tomy products in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and has brought toys such as Zoids, and games like <a href="/wiki/Pop-up_Pirate" title="Pop-up Pirate">Pop-up Pirate</a>, to the West with great success. Tomy UK's slogan has traditionally been "Trust Tomy". In 2006, Tomy UK launched a website on which consumers can buy online from Tomy's catalogue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In early 2011, Takara-Tomy acquired <a href="/wiki/RC2_Corporation" title="RC2 Corporation">RC2 Corporation</a> and RC2 sub-brand, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Learning_Curve_Brands" title="Learning Curve Brands">Learning Curve</a>, which included The First Years, <a href="/wiki/Lamaze_Infant_Development_System" title="Lamaze Infant Development System">Lamaze</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Compass" title="Compass">Compass</a>.</p>

<p>Takara-Tomy has manufactured a broad range of products based on its own properties which include, from the Tomy side: <a href="/wiki/Tomica" title="Tomica">Tomica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plarail" title="Plarail">Plarail</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoids" title="Zoids">Zoids</a>, Idaten Jump, <a href="/wiki/Nohohon_Zoku" title="Nohohon Zoku">Nohohon Zoku</a> and Tomy branded baby care products, and, from the Takara side: <a href="/wiki/Space_Pets" title="Space Pets">Space Pets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Choro-Q" title="Choro-Q">Choro-Q</a> (a.k.a. Penny Racers), <a href="/wiki/Transformers_(toy_line)" title="Transformers (toy line)">Transformers</a>, B-Daman, Koeda-chan (a.k.a. Treena) and <a href="/wiki/Microman" title="Microman">Microman</a>. The merged Takara-Tomy also produces and/or sells a wide variety of toy and game brands under license, such as Thomas &amp; Friends, Disney, Pokémon, Naruto, The Game of Life (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/Jinsei_Game" title="Jinsei Game">Jinsei Game</a>), Rockman (a.k.a. Megaman), <a href="/wiki/Mermaid_Melody_Pichi_Pichi_Pitch" title="Mermaid Melody Pichi Pichi Pitch">Mermaid Melody Pichi Pichi Pitch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirarin_Revolution" title="Kirarin Revolution">Kirarin Revolution</a>, Sugar Bunnies and Animal Crossing. Tomy's rights to these licenses vary by region. One of the first examples of product synergy for the merged company was the combining of <a href="/wiki/Takara" title="Takara">Takara</a>'s Jinsei Game (Game of Life) license and Tomy's Pokémon license to produce a Pokémon Jinsei Game.</p>
<p>Tomy sells many products worldwide, including baby and pre-school toys, <a href="/wiki/Baby_monitor" title="Baby monitor">baby monitors</a>, mechanical and electronic games, consumer electronics, children's <a href="/wiki/Handicraft" title="Handicraft">arts and crafts</a> products, and a vast range of toys suited to girls or boys. They make a large selection of <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon" title="Pokémon">Pokémon</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_the_Tank_Engine_and_Friends_merchandise#Tomy" title="Thomas the Tank Engine and Friends merchandise">Thomas the Tank Engine</a> merchandise. They also publish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Videogames" title="Videogames">videogames</a> in Japan (mostly based on <a href="/wiki/Zoids" title="Zoids">Zoids</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naruto" title="Naruto">Naruto</a> <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series), and are responsible for the distribution of some <a href="/wiki/Hasbro" title="Hasbro">Hasbro</a> products in Japan, such as <a href="/wiki/Play-Doh" title="Play-Doh">Play-Doh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenga" title="Jenga">Jenga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monopoly_(game)" title="Monopoly (game)">Monopoly</a>. The company was formerly responsible for distribution of the <a href="/wiki/My_Little_Pony" title="My Little Pony">My Little Pony</a> products in Japan before <a href="/wiki/Bushiroad" title="Bushiroad">Bushiroad</a> acquired the distribution rights to them starting with the franchise's <i><a href="/wiki/My_Little_Pony:_Friendship_Is_Magic" title="My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic">Friendship Is Magic</a></i> line (though <a href="/wiki/My_Little_Pony:_Friendship_Is_Magic" title="My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic">the animated television series</a> was owned by <a href="/wiki/Hasbro" title="Hasbro">Hasbro</a>). Later in 2015, after Bushiroad disowned the distribution rights, Sega Toys reacquired the rights to all generations of the franchise. In the 1960s, Tomy was a sponsor and toy maker for the television show <a href="/wiki/Giant_Robo_(tokusatsu)" title="Giant Robo (tokusatsu)">Giant Robo (tokusatsu)</a>.</p>
<p>They merged with Takara shortly before the bankruptcy of the mentioned company.</p>
