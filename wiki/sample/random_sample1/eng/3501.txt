<p><b>Daiki Takamatsu</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">高松 大樹</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takamatsu Daiki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (born September 8, 1981 in <a href="/wiki/Ube,_Yamaguchi" title="Ube, Yamaguchi">Ube, Yamaguchi</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> player. He is a forward and plays for <a href="/wiki/J3_League" title="J3 League">J3 League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Oita_Trinita" title="Oita Trinita">Oita Trinita</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jleague_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jleague-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daiki_Takamatsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Takamatsu was chosen as one of the Designated Players for Development by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League" title="J. League">J. League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan_Football_Association" title="Japan Football Association">JFA</a> in 1999 when he was a student of Tatara Gakuen High School. Because of this status, he was able to register as a <a href="/wiki/Sanfrecce_Hiroshima" title="Sanfrecce Hiroshima">Sanfrecce Hiroshima</a> player while he was still eligible to play for his high school club. However, he did not play any official match for Hiroshima.</p>
<p>After graduating from his high school in 2000, he joined J. League Division 2 side <a href="/wiki/Oita_Trinita" title="Oita Trinita">Oita Trinita</a>. He played 6 games and scored 1 goal in his first season. In the 2001 season, he became a regular playing 22 league games and scored 8 goals. The club was promoted to J.League Division 1 at the end of the 2002 season.</p>
<p>He spent 16 seasons with Oita, deciding to retire at the end of <a href="/wiki/2016_J3_League" title="2016 J3 League">2016 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daiki_Takamatsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was part of the Japanese 2004 Olympic football team, who exited in the first round, having finished fourth in group B, below group winners <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>.</p>
<p>The 2006 season turned out to be the most prolific season for him scoring 12 league goals. The reward was the first full international cap handed by national coach <a href="/wiki/Ivica_Osim" title="Ivica Osim">Ivica Osim</a> on November 15, 2006 in an <a href="/wiki/2007_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2007 AFC Asian Cup">2007 AFC Asian Cup</a> qualifier against <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia_national_football_team" title="Saudi Arabia national football team">Saudi Arabia</a> at <a href="/wiki/Sapporo_Dome" title="Sapporo Dome">Sapporo Dome</a> when he replaced <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Ganaha" title="Kazuki Ganaha">Kazuki Ganaha</a> in the 74th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
