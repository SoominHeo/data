<p>The <b>Praemium Imperiale</b> (lit. "World Culture Prize in Memory of His Imperial Highness Prince Takamatsu", <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">高松宮殿下記念世界文化賞</span>, <i>Takamatsu no miya denka kinen sekai bunka-shō</i>) is an international art prize awarded since 1989 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_family_of_Japan" title="Imperial family of Japan">imperial family of Japan</a> on behalf of the Japan Art Association in the fields of <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">theatre/film</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldberger_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Goldberger-2">[2]</a></sup> These are areas of achievement not covered by the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a>. The prizes are awarded for outstanding contributions to the development, promotion and progress of the arts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Information">Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Praemium_Imperiale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Praemium Imperiale is awarded in the memory of His Imperial Highness <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Takamatsu" title="Prince Takamatsu">Prince Takamatsu</a> (1905–1987), younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Hirohito" title="Hirohito">Emperor Shōwa</a> who reigned from 1926 through 1989. Prince Takamatsu was famous for his longtime support of the development, promotion and progress of arts in the world.</p>
<p>The laureates are announced each September; the prize presentation ceremony and related events are held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo,_Japan" title="Tokyo, Japan">Tokyo, Japan</a>, each November. The prize presentation ceremony is held in the presence of His Imperial Highness <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Hitachi" title="Prince Hitachi">Prince Hitachi</a>, President of the Japan Art Association, at the <a class="external text" href="http://www.meijikinenkan.gr.jp" rel="nofollow">Meiji Kinenkan</a> in Tokyo. Prince Hitachi presents the prizes to the selected laureates. The prize consists of a gold medal and 15 million Japanese yen, and was created by the <a href="/wiki/Fujisankei_Communications_Group" title="Fujisankei Communications Group">Fujisankei Communications Group</a>, which pays the expenses of around $3 million per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-select_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-select-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldberger_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Goldberger-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The laureates are annually recommended by international advisers, and decided by an anonymous committee of the Japan Art Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldberger_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Goldberger-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The advisers include <a href="/wiki/Yasuhiro_Nakasone" title="Yasuhiro Nakasone">Yasuhiro Nakasone</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_H._Luers" title="William H. Luers">William H. Luers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lamberto_Dini" title="Lamberto Dini">Lamberto Dini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Pinault" title="François Pinault">François Pinault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Patten" title="Chris Patten">Chris Patten</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Klaus-Dieter_Lehmann" title="Klaus-Dieter Lehmann">Klaus-Dieter Lehmann</a>. Honorary advisers include <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Chirac" title="Jacques Chirac">Jacques Chirac</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Rockefeller" title="David Rockefeller">David Rockefeller</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Rockefeller,_Jr." title="David Rockefeller, Jr.">David Rockefeller, Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helmut_Schmidt" title="Helmut Schmidt">Helmut Schmidt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_von_Weizs%C3%A4cker" title="Richard von Weizsäcker">Richard von Weizsäcker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grants_for_Young_Artists">Grants for Young Artists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Praemium_Imperiale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Grants for Young Artists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li>2004 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Young_Sound_Forum_of_Central_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Young Sound Forum of Central Europe (page does not exist)">Young Sound Forum of Central Europe</a></li>
<li>2003 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=De_Sono_Associazione_per_la_Musica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="De Sono Associazione per la Musica (page does not exist)">De Sono Associazione per la Musica</a>, Italy</li>
