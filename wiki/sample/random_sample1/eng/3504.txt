<p><b>Takasago</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">高砂市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takasago-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo_Prefecture" title="Hyōgo Prefecture">Hyōgo Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The city was founded on July 1, 1954. It lies between the cities of <a href="/wiki/Kakogawa,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" title="Kakogawa, Hyōgo">Kakogawa</a> to the east and <a href="/wiki/Himeji,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" title="Himeji, Hyōgo">Himeji</a> to the west, which is known for its <a href="/wiki/Himeji_Castle" title="Himeji Castle">castle</a>.</p>
<p>As of March 31, 2012, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 95,259, with 38,479 <a href="/wiki/Household" title="Household">households</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 2,769.16 persons per km². The total area is 34.40 km².</p>
<p>Takasago was a fishing town and a place known for its ports, but it has since been industrialized and many factories for major companies dot its skyline in the south, such as the Kobe Steel Takasago Manufacturing Complex, Mitsubishi Paper Mills, Takuma Power Plant, and Kikkoman Soy Sauce.</p>
<p>Major routes of transportation include the JR train line in the north and <a href="/wiki/Sanyo_Electric_Railway" title="Sanyo Electric Railway">Sanyo Densha</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">山陽電車</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> line to the south. The Meiki Kansen <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">明姫幹線</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the major highway connecting the cities of <a href="/wiki/Akashi,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" title="Akashi, Hyōgo">Akashi</a> and Himeji, running through Takasago and Kakogawa in the process. The <i><a href="/wiki/Shinkansen" title="Shinkansen">shinkansen</a></i> also runs through the heart of Takasago, but does not make any stops there.</p>
