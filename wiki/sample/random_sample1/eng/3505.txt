<p><b>Takasago International Corporation</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">高砂香料工業株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takasago Kōryō Kōgyō Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (<a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">TYO</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www2.tse.or.jp/tseHpFront/StockSearch.do?callJorEFlg=1&amp;method=&amp;topSearchStr=4914" rel="nofollow">4914</a>) is a major international producer of flavours and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fragrance" title="Fragrance">fragrances</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, with presence in 24 countries worldwide and net sales of $900 million in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Major competitors include <a href="/wiki/Firmenich" title="Firmenich">Firmenich</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Flavors_and_Fragrances" title="International Flavors and Fragrances">International Flavors and Fragrances</a>, <a href="/wiki/Givaudan" title="Givaudan">Givaudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frutarom" title="Frutarom">Frutarom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Symrise" title="Symrise">Symrise</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takasago_International_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Takasago International Corporation was founded on 9 February 1920 as the Takasago Perfumery Company Limited. In 1938 the headquarters of Takasago was moved to Taihoku (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>) and in 1940 a branch office and factory were set up in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>. In 1945, the Taipei headquarters and Shanghai office were taken over by <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%9349)" title="Republic of China (1912–49)">Republican China</a>. In 1951, the Takasago Chemical Company in Taipei was closed and a new company, Takasago Chemical Industry Company was founded. The new company later changed its name to Takasago Perfumery Industry Company and merged with Takasago Perfumery Company.</p>
<p>During the 1960s, Takasago established offices in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and their headquarters were moved to Hatchoubori 2-11, <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Chūō, Tokyo">Chūō Ward</a>. In 1963, Takasago Company Limited was listed in the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo 2nd stock market</a>, but by 1969 they were listed in the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo 1st stock market</a>. Since then the company has opened offices around the world including <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zurich" title="Zurich">Zurich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takasago_International_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, Takasago's Professor <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%8Dji_Noyori" title="Ryōji Noyori">Ryōji Noyori</a> won the 2001 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_for_Chemistry" title="Nobel Prize for Chemistry">Nobel Prize for Chemistry</a> for the study of chirally catalyzed hydrogenations.</p>
