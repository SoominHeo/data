<p><i><b>Takachiho</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">高千穂</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was the second and final <a href="/wiki/Naniwa-class_cruiser" title="Naniwa-class cruiser"><i>Naniwa</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Protected_cruiser" title="Protected cruiser">protected cruiser</a> built for the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle upon Tyne</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Armstrong_Whitworth" title="Armstrong Whitworth">Armstrong Whitworth</a> <a href="/wiki/Elswick,_Tyne_and_Wear" title="Elswick, Tyne and Wear">Elswick</a> shipyard in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. The name <i>Takachiho</i> comes from a mountain in the volcanic <a href="/wiki/Mount_Kirishima" title="Mount Kirishima">Kirishima</a> range between <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Prefecture" title="Kagoshima Prefecture">Kagoshima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_Prefecture" title="Miyazaki Prefecture">Miyazaki</a> prefectures in Japan, which was a prominent location in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_mythology" title="Japanese mythology">Japanese mythology</a>. <i>Takachiho</i> played a major role in the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a> of 1894–1895, but was lost in combat in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The revolutionary design of the "Elswick" protected cruiser, initially developed as a private-venture by Armstrong Whitworth in the mid-1880s, and implemented in the cruiser <i>Esmeralda</i> for the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Navy" title="Chilean Navy">Chilean Navy</a> (subsequently purchased by Japan as <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Izumi" title="Japanese cruiser Izumi"><i>Izumi</i></a>) was of great interest to Japan because of its high speed, powerful armament, armor protection and relatively low cost, especially since the Imperial Japanese Navy lacked the resources at the time to purchase modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pre-dreadnought" title="Pre-dreadnought">pre-dreadnought</a> <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBrooke199958-60_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrooke199958-60-1">[1]</a></sup> Pioneering Japanese <a href="/wiki/Naval_architecture" title="Naval architecture">naval architect</a> <a href="/wiki/Sas%C5%8D_Sach%C5%AB" title="Sasō Sachū">Sasō Sachū</a> requested that Armstrong Whitworth make modifications to the <i>Esmeralda</i> design to customize it for Japanese requirements, and two vessels, <i>Naniwa</i> and <i>Takachiho</i> were ordered under the 1883 fiscal year budget. When completed, <i>Naniwa</i> and <i>Takachiho</i> were considered the most advanced and most powerful cruiser in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJentsuraJungMickel1976_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJentsuraJungMickel1976-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEvansPeattie199715_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEvansPeattie199715-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Naniwa-class_cruiser" title="Naniwa-class cruiser">Naniwa-class cruiser</a></div>
<p>The design of <i>Naniwa</i> and <i>Takachiho</i> was based on a steel <a href="/wiki/Hull_(watercraft)" title="Hull (watercraft)">hull</a> with high <a href="/wiki/Freeboard_(nautical)" title="Freeboard (nautical)">freeboard</a> to increase seaworthiness. Compared with <i>Izumi,</i> the armor was stronger and the coal bunkers formed an additional shield around critical areas. The hull was split into multiple watertight compartments, with a double bottom. Both ships were equipped with <a href="/wiki/Naval_ram" title="Naval ram">naval rams</a> as was standard for the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChesneau1979226-227_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChesneau1979226-227-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Propulsion was by two horizontal two-cylinder double expansion <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam engines</a>, with six cylindrical boilers, which provided for a design speed of 18 knots (33 km/h; 21 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChesneau1979226-227_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChesneau1979226-227-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>As built, her <a href="/wiki/Main_battery" title="Main battery">main armament</a> initially consisted of two 260 mm (10 in) L/35 <a href="/wiki/Krupp" title="Krupp">Krupp</a> cannons mounted individually on rotating platforms in the <a href="/wiki/Bow_(ship)" title="Bow (ship)">bow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stern" title="Stern">stern</a>, with a supply of 200 rounds per gun. Secondary armament was initially six 150 mm (5.9 in) L/35 Krupp cannons mounted in semi-circular <a href="/wiki/Sponson" title="Sponson">sponsons</a> on the main deck, with 450 rounds per gun. Light armament included six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/QF_6_pounder_Hotchkiss" title="QF 6 pounder Hotchkiss">QF 6 pounder Hotchkiss</a> guns, ten <a href="/wiki/1-inch_Nordenfelt_gun" title="1-inch Nordenfelt gun">1-inch Nordenfelt guns</a> and four 11-mm, 10-barrel <a href="/wiki/Nordenfelt_gun" title="Nordenfelt gun">Nordenfelt guns</a> In addition, there were four 356 mm (14.0 in) <a href="/wiki/Robert_Whitehead" title="Robert Whitehead">Whitehead</a> <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_tube" title="Torpedo tube">torpedo tubes</a> mounted on the main deck. After the First Sino-Japanese War, both <i>Naniwa</i> and <i>Takachiho</i> were re-armed with eight Elswick <a href="/wiki/QF_6_inch_/40_naval_gun" title="QF 6 inch /40 naval gun">QF 6 inch /40 naval guns</a> in order to increase stability and standardize on ammunition for the fleet.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_record">Service record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Service record">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Takachiho</i> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Yokohama" title="Yokohama">Yokohama</a> in early July 1886, just one week after her sister ship, <i>Naniwa</i> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.</p>
<p>In 1893, <i>Naniwa</i> and <i>Takachiho</i> made two voyages to <a href="/wiki/Honolulu" title="Honolulu">Honolulu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> to provide protection for Japanese citizens and to indicate Japanese concern during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overthrow_of_the_Hawaiian_monarchy" title="Overthrow of the Hawaiian monarchy">Overthrow of the Hawaiian monarchy</a> by American marines and colonists. During the second voyage, marines from <i>Naniwa</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>’s cruiser <a href="/wiki/HMS_Champion_(1878)" title="HMS Champion (1878)">HMS <i>Champion</i></a> were asked to land to defend their respective citizens during the "<a href="/wiki/Black_Week_(Hawaii)" title="Black Week (Hawaii)">Black Week</a>" hysteria, when the <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_Hawaii" title="Provisional Government of Hawaii">Provisional Government of Hawaii</a> feared invasion by the United States to restore the legitimate government.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Sino-Japanese_War">First Sino-Japanese War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: First Sino-Japanese War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a>, <i>Takachiho</i> was present at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Yalu_River_(1894)" title="Battle of Yalu River (1894)">Battle of the Yellow Sea</a> under the command of <a href="/wiki/Yashiro_Rokur%C5%8D" title="Yashiro Rokurō">Yashiro Rokurō</a> as part of the "flying squadron" including <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Yoshino" title="Japanese cruiser Yoshino"><i>Yoshino</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Naniwa" title="Japanese cruiser Naniwa"><i>Naniwa</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Akitsushima" title="Japanese cruiser Akitsushima"><i>Akitsushima</i></a>, which sank the Imperial <a href="/wiki/Beiyang_Fleet" title="Beiyang Fleet">Beiyang Fleet</a> cruisers <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_cruiser_Jingyuan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chinese cruiser Jingyuan (page does not exist)"><i>Jingyuan</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_cruiser_Zhiyuan" title="Chinese cruiser Zhiyuan"><i>Zhiyuan</i></a>. <i>Takachiho</i> was subsequently used in patrols of the <a href="/wiki/Bohai_Sea" title="Bohai Sea">Bay of Bohai</a> and in operations off of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BCshunkou" title="Lüshunkou">Port Arthur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPaine2003133-134_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPaine2003133-134-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Takachiho</i> was among the Japanese fleet units that took part in the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Taiwan_(1895)" title="Japanese invasion of Taiwan (1895)">invasion of Taiwan</a> in 1895, and saw action on 3 June 1895 at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Keelung_(1895)" title="Battle of Keelung (1895)">bombardment of the Chinese coastal forts at Keelung</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interwar_years">Interwar years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Interwar years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Takachiho</i> was re-designated as a 2nd-class cruiser on 21 March 1898. Around 1900-1901, both her main battery and secondary battery of Krupp guns was replaced with smaller Elswick <a href="/wiki/QF_6_inch_/40_naval_gun" title="QF 6 inch /40 naval gun">QF 6 inch /40 naval guns</a> for stability, and for standardization of ammunition with other ships of the Japanese Navy.</p>
<p><i>Takachiho</i> was assigned to help cover the Japanese landings of Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naval_landing_forces" title="Naval landing forces">naval landing forces</a> which occupied the port city of <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a> in northern China during the <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a>, as part of the Japanese contribution to the <a href="/wiki/Eight-Nation_Alliance" title="Eight-Nation Alliance">Eight-Nation Alliance</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russo-Japanese_War">Russo-Japanese War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Russo-Japanese War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a> of 1904-1905, <i>Takachiho</i> was part of the Japanese squadron at the initial <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chemulpo_Bay" title="Battle of Chemulpo Bay">Battle of Chemulpo Bay</a> against the forward deployed units of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Navy" title="Imperial Russian Navy">Imperial Russian Navy</a> in Korea. On 10 March 1904, <i>Takachiho</i> and <i>Naniwa</i> assisted in the blockade of the Russian Pacific Squadron within the confines of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lushunkou" title="Lushunkou">Port Arthur</a> and subsequent naval <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Port_Arthur" title="Battle of Port Arthur">Battle of Port Arthur</a>. In June, <i>Takachiho</i> was assigned to patrols in the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a> but was unable to prevent cruisers from the Russian squadron based at <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a> from sinking Japanese transports. She subsequently joined the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Fleet_(Imperial_Japanese_Navy)" title="2nd Fleet (Imperial Japanese Navy)">IJN 2nd Fleet</a> under the overall command of Vice Admiral <a href="/wiki/Kamimura_Hikonoj%C5%8D" title="Kamimura Hikonojō">Kamimura Hikonojō</a> to search out the Russian cruisers. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_off_Ulsan" title="Battle off Ulsan">Battle off Ulsan</a>, she assisted in the sinking of the Russian cruiser <a href="/wiki/Russian_cruiser_Rurik_(1892)" title="Russian cruiser Rurik (1892)"><i>Rurik</i></a>, and in the rescue of her survivors. <i>Takachiho</i> was also present at the crucial final <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tsushima" title="Battle of Tsushima">Battle of Tsushima</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBrooke199958-60_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrooke199958-60-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Takachiho</i> was re-designated a 2nd class <a href="/wiki/Coastal_defence_ship" title="Coastal defence ship">Coastal defence ship</a> on 28 August 1912 and was converted into a <a href="/wiki/Minelayer" title="Minelayer">minelayer</a> and mine recovery training vessel. One of her 152-mm guns was removed to make room for 200 <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">naval mines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChesneau1979226-227_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChesneau1979226-227-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the Japanese declaration of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> on the German Empire on 23 August 1914, <i>Takachiho</i> was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I" title="Allies of World War I">Allied</a> blockade forces during the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Tsingtao" title="Siege of Tsingtao">Siege of Tsingtao</a>. On 17 October 1914 <i>Takachiho</i> was struck by a single <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedo</a> launched from the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_German_Navy" title="Imperial German Navy">Imperial German Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_boat" title="Torpedo boat">torpedo boat</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_S90&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="SMS S90 (page does not exist)"><i>S90</i></a> approximately 10 nautical miles (19 km; 12 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Jiaozhou_Bay" title="Jiaozhou Bay">Jiaozhou Bay</a> at approximate coordinates <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Japanese_cruiser_Takachiho&amp;params=35_55_N_120_24_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">35°55′N</span> <span class="longitude">120°24′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">35.917°N 120.400°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">35.917; 120.400</span></span></span></a></span> <i>Takachiho</i> sank with the loss of 271 officers and men (including Captain Ito Sukeyasu). There were only three survivors of the disaster, which was the largest single loss for Japanese forces during the entire war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBrooke199958-60_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrooke199958-60-1">[1]</a></sup> She was struck from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Navy_list" title="Navy list">navy list</a> on 29 October 1914.</p>
