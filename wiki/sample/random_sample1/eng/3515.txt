<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Takahashi Unions</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Takahashi Unions</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">高橋ユニオンズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takahashi Yunionzu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> were a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> team in <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Pacific_League" title="Pacific League">Pacific League</a> expansion team in 1954, they were brought into the league to increase the number of teams to eight. The team was stocked with players from the other Pacific League teams, including aging pitcher <a href="/wiki/Victor_Starffin" title="Victor Starffin">Victor Starffin</a>. In their three years of existence the team finished in the second division every season.</p>
<p>The Unions played their games at <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki_Stadium" title="Kawasaki Stadium">Kawasaki Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki,_Kanagawa" title="Kawasaki, Kanagawa">Kawasaki, Kanagawa</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Franchise_history">Franchise history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takahashi_Unions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Franchise history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Victor_Starffin.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1011" data-file-width="1334" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Victor_Starffin.jpg/220px-Victor_Starffin.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Victor_Starffin.jpg/330px-Victor_Starffin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Victor_Starffin.jpg/440px-Victor_Starffin.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Victor_Starffin.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Victor_Starffin" title="Victor Starffin">Victor Starffin</a> reaching 300 wins with the Takahashi Unions.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Pacific League had seven teams since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Baseball_League" title="Japan Baseball League">Japan Baseball League</a> split into the <a href="/wiki/Central_League" title="Central League">Central</a> and the Pacific League, but having an odd number of teams was inconvenient for scheduling and playoffs. Therefore, in 1953 it was decided that any team which finished below a winning percentage of .350 would be disbanded. However, all the teams finished above the mark (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Pearls" title="Kintetsu Pearls">Kintetsu</a> had the worst record at .410) — therefore the league decided to admit an eighth team, the Unions.</p>
<p>The Unions were owned by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ryutaro_Takahashi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ryutaro Takahashi (page does not exist)">Ryutaro Takahashi</a>, the former president of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dai-Nippon_Beer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dai-Nippon Beer (page does not exist)">Dai-Nippon Beer</a> and owner of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagles_Baseball_Club" title="Eagles Baseball Club">Eagles Baseball Club</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurowashi_Black_Eagles" title="Kurowashi Black Eagles">Kurowashi Black Eagles</a> from 1939 to 1941. Takahashi had originally wanted to name the Unions the "Takahashi Eagles" after himself and a beer that had been produced by Dai-Nippon. However, the teams's name ended up being chosen by a public vote.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The team became the <b>Tombo Unions</b> after <a href="/wiki/Tombow" title="Tombow">Tombow Pencil</a> bought a share of the team in 1955, but reverted to its original name after Tombow withdrew before the following year.</p>
<p>Starffin pitched for the team in 1954–1955, going a combined 15-34, but managing to notch his 300th career win with the team.</p>
<p>The Unions, now in financial trouble, only lasted one more year before they merged with the <a href="/wiki/Daiei_Stars" title="Daiei Stars">Daiei Stars</a> on February 26, 1957, to form the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daiei_Unions" title="Daiei Unions">Daiei Unions</a>.</p>
