<p><b>Takezawa Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">竹沢駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takezawa-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Hachik%C5%8D_Line" title="Hachikō Line">Hachikō Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ogawa,_Saitama" title="Ogawa, Saitama">Ogawa, Saitama</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takezawa_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Takezawa Station is served by the unelectrified <a href="/wiki/Hachik%C5%8D_Line" title="Hachikō Line">Hachikō Line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Komagawa_Station" title="Komagawa Station">Komagawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Takasaki_Station" title="Takasaki Station">Takasaki</a>, and is located 56.3 km from the starting point of the Hachikō Line at <a href="/wiki/Hachi%C5%8Dji_Station" title="Hachiōji Station">Hachiōji</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kawashima2011_Vol.11_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-kawashima2011_Vol.11-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takezawa_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of two opposed <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a> serving two tracks, which form a <a href="/wiki/Passing_loop" title="Passing loop">passing loop</a> on the single-track line. The station building is located on the south side<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kawashima2011_Vol.11_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-kawashima2011_Vol.11-2">[2]</a></sup> and the platforms are connected by a level crossing.</p>

<p>The station opened on 6 October 1934.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jreast-1">[1]</a></sup> The station building was rebuilt in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kawashima2011_Vol.11_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-kawashima2011_Vol.11-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takezawa_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2010, the station was used by an average of 31 passengers daily (boarding passengers only).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast2010stats_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast2010stats-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
