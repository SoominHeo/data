<p><b>Taki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">多気町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Taki-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of August 2015, the town had an estimated population of 14,846 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 144 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area was 103.06 square kilometres (39.79 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taki,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village of Ōka (相可) was established on April 1, 1889 during the establishment of municipalities in the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>. It was elevated to town status on June 20 1919, and changed its name to Taki after merging of the neighboring villages of Sana and Tsuda, both in <a href="/wiki/Taki_District,_Mie" title="Taki District, Mie">Taki District</a>, on March 30, 1955. The village of Nishi-Tokida was annexed on April 15, 1959. On January 1, 2006 the village was merged into Taki.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taki,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taki serves as a commercial center for the surrounding region. The major industrial employer is <a href="/wiki/Sharp_Corporation" title="Sharp Corporation">Sharp Corporation</a>. Noted agricultural products include <i>Kaki</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persimmons" title="Persimmons">persimmons</a> and green tea.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taki,_Mie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
