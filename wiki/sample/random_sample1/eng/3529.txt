<p><b>Dafne Schippers</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈdɑfnə ˈsxɪpərs]</a></span>; born 15 June 1992) is a Dutch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Track_and_field_athletics" title="Track and field athletics">athlete</a>, competing in <a href="/wiki/Heptathlon" title="Heptathlon">heptathlon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" title="Sprint (running)">sprints</a>. She is the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2015 World Championships in Athletics">2015 World Champion</a> and winner of 2016 Olympic silver at the <a href="/wiki/200_metres" title="200 metres">200 metres</a>. She holds the <a href="/wiki/List_of_European_records_in_athletics" title="List of European records in athletics">European record</a> of 21.63 seconds and is the third fastest woman of all time at this distance.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dafne_Schippers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dafne Schippers was born on 15 June 1992 in the city of <a href="/wiki/Utrecht" title="Utrecht">Utrecht</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> At age 9, she started competing in athletics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dafne_Schippers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schippers won <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medals</a> in the heptathlon at the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Junior_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2010 World Junior Championships in Athletics">2010 World Junior Championships in Athletics</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Athletics_Junior_Championships" title="2011 European Athletics Junior Championships">2011 European Athletics Junior Championships</a>. Two years later, she won gold in the <a href="/wiki/100_metres" title="100 metres">100 m</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Long_jump" title="Long jump">long jump</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2013_European_Athletics_U23_Championships" title="2013 European Athletics U23 Championships">2013 European Athletics U23 Championships</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2011 World Championships in Athletics">World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, she broke the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_records_in_athletics" title="Dutch records in athletics">Dutch national record</a> in the 200 m in the heats before finishing 9th in the semifinals, missing the final by 0.04 seconds. She is co-record holder in the <a href="/wiki/4_%C3%97_100_metres_relay" title="4 × 100 metres relay">4 × 100 m relay</a>.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2013 World Championships in Athletics">2013 World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, Schippers won the bronze medal in the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Championships_in_Athletics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_heptathlon" title="2013 World Championships in Athletics – Women's heptathlon">heptathlon</a>. She became the first Dutch woman to win a medal at the World Athletics Championships.</p>
<p>She improved the 200 m record during the heptathlon at the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Hypo-Meeting" title="Hypo-Meeting">Hypo-Meeting</a> in <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6tzis" title="Götzis">Götzis</a>, her time of 22.35 being one of the best 200 m performances ever in a heptathlon.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Schippers won gold medals at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_championships_athletics_2014" title="European championships athletics 2014">European championships of 2014</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014_European_Athletics_Championships_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_100_metres" title="2014 European Athletics Championships – Women's 100 metres">100 m</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2014_European_Athletics_Championships_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_200_metres" title="2014 European Athletics Championships – Women's 200 metres">200 m</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her success at the <a href="/wiki/2014_European_Athletics_Championships" title="2014 European Athletics Championships">2014 European Athletics Championships</a> prompted discussion over her long-term prospects and whether she should focus on sprinting, or continue her career in the heptathlon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015 Schippers announced via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that she would focus on sprinting in the run-up to the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2015 World Championships in Athletics">2015 World Championships in Athletics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the World Championships in Beijing Schippers won the <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medal</a> in the 100 m and gold in the 200 m. Her 200 m winning time of 21.63 seconds was a new <a href="/wiki/List_of_European_records_in_athletics" title="List of European records in athletics">European record</a> and made her the third fastest woman in history over that distance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>She won the <a href="/wiki/2016_European_Athletics_Championships_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_100_metres" title="2016 European Athletics Championships – Women's 100 metres">2016 European Athletics Championships – Women's 100 metres</a> in Amsterdam in 10.90, by 3 tenths of a second. At the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> she won the silver medal in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_200_metres" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Women's 200 metres">200 m</a> and finished fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_100_metres" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Women's 100 metres">100 m</a> final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
