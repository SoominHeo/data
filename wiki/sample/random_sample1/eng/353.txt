<p><b><a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a></b> competed at the <b><a href="/wiki/2002_Winter_Olympics" title="2002 Winter Olympics">2002 Winter Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City, Utah</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, winning its first two gold medals in the Winter Games. It was the nation's best performance at the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympic_Games" title="Winter Olympic Games">Winter Games</a> prior to the <a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics" title="2010 Winter Olympics">2010 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australia_at_the_2002_Winter_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Australia's first ever Winter Olympic gold, also the first such medal won by a competitor from any Southern Hemisphere country and semitropical nation was won by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steven_Bradbury_(speed_skater)" title="Steven Bradbury (speed skater)">Steven Bradbury</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2002_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_1000_metres" title="Short track speed skating at the 2002 Winter Olympics â Men's 1000 metres">1000 m short track speed skating</a>. Well off the pace of the medal favourites, Bradbury was positioned at the rear in the semifinal, only to see his competitors crash into each other, allowing him through to the final. Again well off the pace in the final all four other competitors crashed out at the final corner, leaving a shocked Bradbury to take the gold medal. Bradbury was also part of the 1994 relay team that won Australia's first Winter Olympics medal.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alisa_Camplin" title="Alisa Camplin">Alisa Camplin</a>, who hadn't won a world cup event before and was so nervous she didn't eat dinner the night before the final, won the second gold, in <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_skiing_at_the_2002_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_aerials" title="Freestyle skiing at the 2002 Winter Olympics â Women's aerials">aerial skiing</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jacqui_Cooper" title="Jacqui Cooper">Jacqui Cooper</a> was previously a favourite, but she injured herself before the competition.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jenny_Owens" title="Jenny Owens">Jenny Owens</a> had a top 10 result, finishing 9th in the combined downhill event, the second best performance by any Australian at an Olympic Games.</p>
<p>Australia competed in alpine skiing, figure skating, freestyle skiing, short track speed skating and snowboarding. This was the fewest sports Australia had competed in since 1984. No Australian <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiers</a> competed in the Olympics for the first time since 1976, and no bobsledders competed for the first time since Australia's debut in 1988. Australian bobsledder <a href="/wiki/Will_Alstergren" title="Will Alstergren">Will Alstergren</a> said "We also beat half the teams currently in Salt Lake City, but unfortunately we couldn't meet the very high standard of the AOC, the Australian Olympic Committee". Australian selection standards was also listed as a factor for Australia not competing in cross-country skiing at Salt Lake City.</p>
<p>During the 2002 Winter Olympics, the Australian broadcaster, the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Network" title="Seven Network">Seven Network</a>, included in its coverage an irreverent talk show called <a href="/wiki/The_Dream_with_Roy_and_HG#The_Ice_Dream" title="The Dream with Roy and HG">The Ice Dream</a>, which interviewed several celebrities and promoted the <a href="/wiki/Smiggin_Holes_2010_Winter_Olympic_bid" title="Smiggin Holes 2010 Winter Olympic bid">Smiggin Holes 2010 Winter Olympic bid</a>. Bradbury's and Camplin's triumphs were celebrated by <a href="/wiki/Australia_Post" title="Australia Post">Australia Post</a> issuing stamps of them, which followed on from them issuing stamps of Australian gold medallists at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Sydney Olympics</a>. They were produced by high-speed offset stamp-printing, unlike the digitally produced 2000 stamps. Bradbury's stamp was issued on 20 February, and Camplin's was issued on 22 February, four days after their respective victories. Each received <a href="/wiki/Australian_dollar" title="Australian dollar">A$</a>20,000 for the use of their image. Bradbury said "Should get me a car. I haven't had a car for a long time." and later described having a stamp issued as "a great honour". Camplin was also delighted, saying "For us to be put in with the summer Olympians who had their stamps and the previous 39 sporting legends who've had their stamps is amazing."</p>
