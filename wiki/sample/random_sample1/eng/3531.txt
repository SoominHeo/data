<p><b>Tahara</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">田原市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tahara-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Aichi_Prefecture" title="Aichi Prefecture">Aichi Prefecture</a>, Japan.</p>
<p>As of December 2015, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 61,850 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 324 persons per km². The total area was 191.12 square kilometres (73.79 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tahara is situated in southern <a href="/wiki/Aichi_Prefecture" title="Aichi Prefecture">Aichi Prefecture</a>, and occupies most of <a href="/wiki/Atsumi_Peninsula" title="Atsumi Peninsula">Atsumi Peninsula</a>. The peninsula is bounded on the north by <a href="/wiki/Mikawa_Bay" title="Mikawa Bay">Mikawa Bay</a> and to the south lies the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea" title="Philippine Sea">Philippine Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Situated as it is between those two bodies of water, Tahara has a warm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_climate" title="Maritime climate">maritime climate</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Tahara has been continuously occupied since prehistoric times. Archaeologists have found numerous remains from the <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dmon_period" title="Jōmon period">Jōmon period</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kofun" title="Kofun">burial mounds</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Kofun_period" title="Kofun period">Kofun period</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">Nara period</a>, the area was assigned to ancient <a href="/wiki/Atsumi_District,_Aichi" title="Atsumi District, Aichi">Atsumi</a> County, and was divided into several <i><a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Den" title="Shōen">shōen</a></i> during the <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Kamakura_period" title="Kamakura period">Kamakura period</a>, the area was noted for production of a certain type of pottery. During the <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a>, the area was under the control of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Toda_clan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Toda clan (page does not exist)">Toda clan</a>, who constructed <a href="/wiki/Tahara_Castle" title="Tahara Castle">Tahara Castle</a>. The Toda, who were allied with <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a> were dispossessed by <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyoshi" title="Toyotomi Hideyoshi">Toyotomi Hideyoshi</a>, but returned as <i><a href="/wiki/Daimy%C5%8D" title="Daimyō">daimyō</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Tahara_Domain" title="Tahara Domain">Tahara Domain</a> at the start of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. The Toda were later replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Miyake_clan" title="Miyake clan">Miyake clan</a>, who ruled until the end of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>. The noted scholar <a href="/wiki/Watanabe_Kazan" title="Watanabe Kazan">Watanabe Kazan</a> was from Tahara.</p>
<p>At the start of the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>, on October 1, 1889 Tahara was a collection of villages within <a href="/wiki/Atsumi_District,_Aichi" title="Atsumi District, Aichi">Atsumi District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aichi_Prefecture" title="Aichi Prefecture">Aichi Prefecture</a>. Tahara Village was elevated to town status on October 3, 1892 and Fukue village became Fukue Town on February 22, 1897. Fukue later changed its name to <a href="/wiki/Atsumi,_Aichi" title="Atsumi, Aichi">Atsumi Town</a> on April 15, 1955. On November 11, 1958, the village of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akabane,_Aichi" title="Akabane, Aichi">Akabane</a> was raised to town status.</p>
<p>The modern city of Tahara was established on August 20, 2003, from the merger the former town of Tahara, absorbing the town of Akabane (both from Atsumi District) to elevate city status. On October 1, 2005, the town of Atsumi (also from Atsumi District) was merged into Tahara. Therefore, Atsumi District was dissolved as a result of this merger.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tahara is a regional commercial center with a mixed economy of manufacturing and agriculture. Due to its long coastline, Tahara has many ports for <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">commercial fishing</a>. The main industrial employer is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_Motor_Corporation" title="Toyota Motor Corporation">Toyota Motor Corporation</a>, which has its award-winning <a href="/wiki/Tahara_plant" title="Tahara plant">Tahara plant</a> in Tahara which makes many <a href="/wiki/Lexus" title="Lexus">Lexus</a>-brand cars and some <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">Toyota</a> models. The <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Celica" title="Toyota Celica">Toyota Celica</a> was manufactured in Tahara from 1979 to 1999. Many <a href="/wiki/Lexus" title="Lexus">Lexus</a> models are manufactured within this plant, as are many <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">Toyota</a> models for domestic and international markets.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy">Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tahara has a <a href="/wiki/Consortium" title="Consortium">consortium</a> of companies investing in <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> needs. As of November 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, a new <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar energy</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_energy" title="Wind energy">wind energy</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_generation" title="Power generation">power generation</a> facility will provide 19,000 households with electricity on an infrequent basis that is dependent upon the weather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tahara has 20 elementary schools, seven middle schools and three high schools.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Highway">Highway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Highway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan_National_Route_259" title="Japan National Route 259">Japan National Route 259</a> is the main highway that runs the length of <a href="/wiki/Atsumi_Peninsula" title="Atsumi Peninsula">Atsumi</a> Peninsula. An alternate reading of the <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a> in this highway numeral designation is <i>ji-go-ku</i>. In Japanese, the word <i><a href="/wiki/Diyu" title="Diyu">jigoku</a></i> means <a href="/wiki/Hell" title="Hell">Hell</a>, and thus some locals refer to it as <i>ji-go-ku-douro</i>, or the "Highway to Hell". This term was encouraged by the perception of a higher fatality rate along the road, especially before it was widened and improved.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan_National_Route_42" title="Japan National Route 42">Japan National Route 42</a></li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_services">Bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Toyotetsu buses and city-operated environmentally friendly public transport facilitate access throughout Tahara, even to the westernmost point at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mikawa_Wan_Quasi-National_Park" title="Mikawa Wan Quasi-National Park">Cape Irago</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seaports">Seaports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahara,_Aichi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Seaports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From the Port of Irago, the <a href="/wiki/Ise-wan_Ferry" title="Ise-wan Ferry">Ise-wan Ferry</a> connects Tahara with the town of <a href="/wiki/Toba,_Mie" title="Toba, Mie">Toba</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mie_prefecture" title="Mie prefecture">Mie prefecture</a>. The ferry can accommodate motor vehicles. The ferry also docks at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ch%C5%ABbu_Centrair_International_Airport" title="Chūbu Centrair International Airport">Central Japan International Airport</a>, built on an artificial island in <a href="/wiki/Ise_Bay" title="Ise Bay">Ise Bay</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>.</p>
