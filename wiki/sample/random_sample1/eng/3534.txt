<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Dedicated short-range communications</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<p><b>Dedicated short-range communications</b> are one-way or two-way short-range to medium-range <a href="/wiki/Wireless" title="Wireless">wireless</a> communication channels specifically designed for automotive use<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and a corresponding set of protocols and standards.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dedicated_short-range_communications&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 1999, the United States <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">Federal Communications Commission</a> (FCC) allocated 75 MHz of spectrum in the 5.9 GHz band to be used by <a href="/wiki/Intelligent_transportation_system" title="Intelligent transportation system">intelligent transportation systems</a> (ITS).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In August 2008, the European Telecommunications Standards Institute (<a href="/wiki/ETSI" title="ETSI">ETSI</a>) allocated 30 MHz of spectrum in the 5.9 GHz band for ITS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 2003, it was used in Europe and Japan in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_toll_collection" title="Electronic toll collection">electronic toll collection</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> DSRC systems in Europe, Japan and U.S. are not compatible and include some very significant variations (5.8 GHz, 5.9 GHz or even infrared, different baud rates, and different protocols).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Singapore's <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Road_Pricing" title="Electronic Road Pricing">Electronic Road Pricing</a> scheme uses DSRC technology for road use measurement. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other possible applications were:</p>
<ul>
<li>Emergency warning system for vehicles</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cooperative_Adaptive_Cruise_Control" title="Cooperative Adaptive Cruise Control">Cooperative Adaptive Cruise Control</a></li>
<li>Cooperative Forward Collision Warning</li>
<li>Intersection collision avoidance</li>
<li>Approaching emergency vehicle warning (Blue Waves)</li>
<li>Vehicle safety inspection</li>
<li>Transit or emergency vehicle signal priority</li>
<li>Electronic parking payments</li>
<li>Commercial vehicle clearance and safety inspections</li>
<li>In-vehicle signing</li>
<li>Rollover warning</li>
<li>Probe data collection</li>
<li>Highway-rail intersection warning</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electronic_toll_collection" title="Electronic toll collection">Electronic toll collection</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Other short-range wireless protocols are <a href="/wiki/IEEE_802.11" title="IEEE 802.11">IEEE 802.11</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bluetooth" title="Bluetooth">Bluetooth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Communications,_Air-interface,_Long_and_Medium_range" title="Communications, Air-interface, Long and Medium range">CALM</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Standardization">Standardization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dedicated_short-range_communications&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Standardization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The European standardization organisation <a href="/wiki/European_Committee_for_Standardization" title="European Committee for Standardization">European Committee for Standardization</a> (CEN), sometimes in co-operation with the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) developed some DSRC standards:</p>
<ul>
<li>EN 12253:2004 Dedicated Short-Range Communication – Physical layer using microwave at 5.8 GHz (review)</li>
<li>EN 12795:2002 Dedicated Short-Range Communication (DSRC) – DSRC Data link layer: Medium Access and Logical Link Control (review)</li>
<li>EN 12834:2002 Dedicated Short-Range Communication – Application layer (review)</li>
<li>EN 13372:2004 Dedicated Short-Range Communication (DSRC) – DSRC profiles for RTTT applications (review)</li>
<li>EN ISO 14906:2004 Electronic Fee Collection – Application interface</li>
</ul>
<p>Each standard addresses different layers in the <a href="/wiki/OSI_model" title="OSI model">OSI model</a> communication stack.</p>
