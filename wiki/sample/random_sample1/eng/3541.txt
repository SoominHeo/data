<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Second moment of area</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">This article is about the geometrical property of an area, termed the second moment of area. For the moment of inertia dealing with the rotation of an object with mass, see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mass_moment_of_inertia" title="Mass moment of inertia">Mass moment of inertia</a>.</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">For a list, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_second_moments_of_area" title="List of second moments of area">List of second moments of area</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>2nd moment of area</b>, also known as <b>moment of inertia of plane area</b>, <b>area moment of inertia</b>, or <b>second area moment</b>, is a geometrical property of an area which reflects how its points are distributed with regard to an arbitrary axis. The second moment of area is typically denoted with either an <span><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y mw-math-element" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">

In both cases, it is calculated with a <a href="/wiki/Multiple_integral" title="Multiple integral">multiple integral</a> over the object in question. Its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Physical_unit" title="Physical unit">unit</a> of dimension is meters to fourth power, <a href="/wiki/Unit_of_length" title="Unit of length">m</a><sup>4</sup>.</p>
<p>In the field of <a href="/wiki/Structural_engineering" title="Structural engineering">structural engineering</a>, the second moment of area of the cross-section of a <a href="/wiki/Beam_(structure)" title="Beam (structure)">beam</a> is an important property used in the calculation of the beam's <a href="/wiki/Deflection_(engineering)" title="Deflection (engineering)">deflection</a> and the calculation of stress caused by a moment applied to the <a href="/wiki/Beam_(structure)" title="Beam (structure)">beam</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composite_shapes">Composite shapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Second_moment_of_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Composite shapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For more complex areas, it is often easier to divide the area into a series of "simpler" shapes. The second moment of area for the entire shape is the sum of the second moment of areas of all of its parts about a common axis. This can include shapes that are "missing" (i.e. holes, hollow shapes, etc.), in which case the second moment of area of the "missing" areas are subtracted, rather than added. In other words, the second moment of area of "missing" parts are considered negative for the method of composite shapes.</p>
