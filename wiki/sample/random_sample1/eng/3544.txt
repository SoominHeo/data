<p><b>China Danxia</b>, or <b>Danxia landform of China</b>, is the general name of the unique type of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landscapes" title="Landscapes">landscapes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danxia_landform" title="Danxia landform">Danxia landform</a>, formed from red <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> and characterised by steep <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cliffs" title="Cliffs">cliffs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which are caused by endogenous forces (including <a href="/wiki/Tectonic_uplift" title="Tectonic uplift">uplift</a>) and exogenous forces (including <a href="/wiki/Weathering" title="Weathering">weathering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">erosion</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2010, China Danxia was inscribed onto the <a href="/wiki/List_of_World_Heritage_Sites_in_China" title="List of World Heritage Sites in China">World Heritage List</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
