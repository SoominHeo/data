<p><b>Single Radio Voice Call Continuity</b> (<b>SRVCC</b>) provides an interim solution for handing over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VoLTE" title="VoLTE">VoLTE</a> (Voice over LTE) to 2G/3G networks. The voice calls on LTE network are meant to be packet switched calls which use <a href="/wiki/IP_Multimedia_Subsystem" title="IP Multimedia Subsystem">IMS</a> system to be made. To make it inter operable with existing networks, these calls are to be handed over to Circuit switched calls in GSM/WCDMA networks. QoS is ensured by SRVCC operators for calls made.</p>
<p>3GPP also standardized SRVCC to provide easy handovers from LTE network to GSM/UMTS network.</p>
