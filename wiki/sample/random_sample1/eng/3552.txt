<p><b>Dante</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ダンテ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a fictional character and the primary <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry" title="Devil May Cry">Devil May Cry</a></i> series created and published by <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capcomplatinum_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-capcomplatinum-3">[3]</a></sup> In the first four games, Dante is a <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">mercenary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Private_investigator" title="Private investigator">private investigator</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Demon" title="Demon">demon</a> hunting <a href="/wiki/Vigilante" title="Vigilante">vigilante</a> dedicated to exterminating them and other malevolent supernatural foes, a mission he follows in pursuit of those that killed his mother and corrupted his brother. He is the son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sparda" title="Sparda">Sparda</a>, a demon of great power and as a result of his heritage, he possesses numerous powers <a href="/wiki/Superhuman" title="Superhuman">beyond that of any human</a>, which he uses in combination with a variety of weapons to accomplish his goals. The character also appears in several <a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_(novels)" title="Devil May Cry (novels)"><i>Devil May Cry</i> novels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_3_(manga)" title="Devil May Cry 3 (manga)">manga</a> volumes; and is featured in the <a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry:_The_Animated_Series" title="Devil May Cry: The Animated Series">2007 anime television series</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Reboot_(fiction)" title="Reboot (fiction)">reboot</a> of the series by <a href="/wiki/Ninja_Theory" title="Ninja Theory">Ninja Theory</a> features him in an alternate universe as a young adult fighting against demons. Dante has also appeared as a guest character in multiple crossover games.</p>
<p>Named after the Italian poet <a href="/wiki/Dante_Alighieri" title="Dante Alighieri">Dante Alighieri</a>, the character was designed to fit <i>Devil May Cry</i> game designer <a href="/wiki/Hideki_Kamiya" title="Hideki Kamiya">Hideki Kamiya</a>'s vision of a "cool and stylish" man, and his personality was based on the title character of the manga series <i><a href="/wiki/Cobra_(manga)" title="Cobra (manga)">Cobra</a></i>. Across the series, Dante's portrayal has been modified to appeal to criticism aimed towards his role in the <a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_2" title="Devil May Cry 2">first sequel</a> with <i>Devil May Cry 3</i> featuring a younger and cockier Dante while <i>Devil May Cry 4</i> offered an older yet cocky characterization. While Capcom handled the character in the first four games, Ninja Theory was in charge of his persona in the reboot.</p>
<p>Dante has become one of the most popular characters in gaming, having been recognized for his role in the <i>Devil May Cry</i> series and various characteristics that have attracted gamers. However, Dante's redesign and characterization in the reboot was the subject of controversy amongst gamers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dante is a mercenary and private investigator specializing in <a href="/wiki/Paranormal" title="Paranormal">paranormal</a> cases, preferring those that call for demon slaying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He is muscular, has white/silver hair, icy-blue eyes and is usually seen wearing red <a href="/wiki/Duster_(clothing)" title="Duster (clothing)">dusters</a>/<a href="/wiki/Trench_coat" title="Trench coat">trench coats</a>. Dante's arsenal usually consists of <a href="/wiki/Firearm" title="Firearm">firearms</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melee" title="Melee">melee</a> weapons, including "Ebony and Ivory", twin handguns that never need reloading, as well as a variety of swords such as the "Rebellion" and "Force Edge", Devil Arms created by his father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGNDMC_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGNDMC-5">[5]</a></sup> The guns are handmade with "For Tony Redgrave, By.45 Art Warks" written on them (Tony Redgrave is the alias used by Dante).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He possesses superhuman strength, agility, stamina, reflexes, coordination, and resistance to injury that approaches invulnerability as a result of his half-demon heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This gives him the ability to enter a heightened state called "Devil Trigger". In this state, Dante possesses greater strength and speed, his health regenerates at a steady pace, and further abilities with his equipped melee weapon, including flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-booklet_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-booklet-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dante is one of the twin sons of Sparda, the demon knight who sided with humanity and drove back an invasion of the human world by demons roughly 2,000 years prior to the events of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> After Sparda's death, Dante and his identical twin brother <a href="/wiki/Vergil_(Devil_May_Cry)" title="Vergil (Devil May Cry)">Vergil</a> were raised by their human mother, <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Devil_May_Cry#Eva" title="Characters of Devil May Cry">Eva</a>. When Dante and Vergil were children, the family was attacked by demons, resulting in Eva's death. This event led to Dante's commitment to hunting demons in pursuit of those that killed his mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Dante is confident in his encounters with opponents, and frequently taunts enemies before battling them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gameinformer_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gameinformer-11">[11]</a></sup> With the exception of <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_4" title="Devil May Cry 4">Devil May Cry 4</a></i>, Dante says the phrase "Devils never cry" in every game, implying that no matter how much of a demon he may prove to be, his ability to cry makes his human side precious to him.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">Appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Devil_May_Cry_video_games"><i>Devil May Cry</i> video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Devil May Cry video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the original <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_(video_game)" title="Devil May Cry (video game)">Devil May Cry</a></i>, Dante is hired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trish_(Devil_May_Cry)" title="Trish (Devil May Cry)">Trish</a>, a mysterious woman who looks similar to his deceased mother, to stop the return of the devil king <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mundus_(character)" title="Mundus (character)">Mundus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, she is actually setting up events for Mundus' agents to kill Dante as he makes his way to Mundus himself. During the course of the game Dante is also reunited with his brother Vergil, who, under the control of Mundus, attempts to kill him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Trish eventually betrays Mundus to save Dante, and the pair work together to lock Mundus in the demon world. Afterwards, they become partners in Dante's demon-slaying business, now renamed "Devil Never Cry".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_2" title="Devil May Cry 2">Devil May Cry 2</a></i>, Dante is shown to have a habit of flipping a coin to make his decisions, although it is revealed at the end of the game that both sides of the coin are heads. Set some time after the first game, <i>Devil May Cry 2</i> focuses on aiding the character <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Devil_May_Cry#Lucia" title="Characters of Devil May Cry">Lucia</a> in defeating <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Devil_May_Cry#Arius" title="Characters of Devil May Cry">Arius</a>, an international businessman who uses demonic power and seeks to conquer the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> At the end of the game, Dante must go into the demon world to stop a demon lord from escaping, but the gate closes behind him and he is trapped. With no way back to the human world, Dante heads even deeper into the demon world on his motorcycle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The third game, <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_3:_Dante%27s_Awakening" title="Devil May Cry 3: Dante's Awakening">Devil May Cry 3: Dante's Awakening</a></i>, serves as a prequel to the first game and features a cockier and younger Dante. In the story, Dante is drawn out by his brother Vergil, who is attempting to reopen the portal to the demon world in order to obtain the full power of Sparda, which remains on the other side contained within the sword Force Edge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Along the way, Dante encounters <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Devil_May_Cry#Lady" title="Characters of Devil May Cry">Lady</a>, who is in pursuit of her father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arkham_(Devil_May_Cry)" title="Arkham (Devil May Cry)">Arkham</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> who is working with Vergil, but has plans of his own. In the end, Dante claims ownership of the Force Edge, while Vergil chooses to remain in the demon world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Inspired by Lady's courage and commitment to her own family, Dante continues his business with a greater sense of purpose. They become friends, and he decides to call his shop "Devil May Cry", after something Lady had said to comfort him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Released in 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry_4" title="Devil May Cry 4">Devil May Cry 4</a></i> is the first game in the series not to feature Dante as the primary character. Dante is seen as an <a href="/wiki/Antagonist" title="Antagonist">antagonist</a> by the game's lead character, <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Devil_May_Cry#Nero" title="Characters of Devil May Cry">Nero</a>, after he presumably kills Sanctus, the leader from The Order of the Sword in charge of hunting demons. Across the story, Dante befriends Nero when he decides to face The Order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> When Nero is kidnapped by Sanctus, the player controls Dante once again as he continues his fight against the Order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> After Nero is saved and Sanctus defeated, Dante entrusts Nero with Vergil's sword he has been wielding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reboot">Reboot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reboot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A reboot of <i>Devil May Cry</i> franchise, <i><a href="/wiki/DmC:_Devil_May_Cry" title="DmC: Devil May Cry">DmC: Devil May Cry</a></i>, features an alternative universe Dante with a very different appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-siliconera_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-siliconera-25">[25]</a></sup> Dante is featured as a young adult in his early twenties who is attacked by a seemingly <a href="/wiki/Sentience" title="Sentience">sentient</a> town known as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Limbo_City&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Limbo City (page does not exist)">Limbo City</a> populated by demons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignuk_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignuk-26">[26]</a></sup> During the reboot, Dante meets his brother Vergil, who is the leader of The Order – a rogue vigilante group trying to free the world from the demons. The city is controlled by the demon king, Mundus, who killed Dante's mother Eva (an angel) and later imprisoned his father (<a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Devil_May_Cry#Sparda" title="Characters of Devil May Cry">Sparda</a>, a demon and Mundus' former lieutenant) in an attempt to kill the children, since <a href="/wiki/Nephilim" title="Nephilim">Nephilim</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nephalem" title="Nephalem">half-angel, half-demon offspring</a>) held the power to kill Mundus. Dante joins Vergil's group to oppose Mundus, eventually taking him down and freeing humanity from the demons; however, Vergil's intentions to take Mundus' place cause a fight between the brothers which Dante wins and Vergil escapes. In the <a href="/wiki/Downloadable_content" title="Downloadable content">downloadable content</a> sequel, <i>Vergil's Downfall</i>, a replica of Dante appears to oppose Vergil in his quest for power but is killed in combat.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_appearances">Other appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dante has playable appearances in several games outside of the <i>Devil May Cry</i> series of games. In the <i>Viewtiful Joe</i> series, also created by <a href="/wiki/Hideki_Kamiya" title="Hideki Kamiya">Hideki Kamiya</a>, Dante is playable in the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/Viewtiful_Joe" title="Viewtiful Joe">Viewtiful Joe</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PSP</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/Viewtiful_Joe:_Red_Hot_Rumble" title="Viewtiful Joe: Red Hot Rumble">Viewtiful Joe: Red Hot Rumble</a></i>. In both games, he frequently has dialogue with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Viewtiful_Joe_characters#Blade_Master_Alastor" title="List of Viewtiful Joe characters">Alastor</a>, who is revealed to be the embodiment of the identically-named sword from <i>Devil May Cry</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He appears as an enemy and optional ally in the director's cut release of <i><a href="/wiki/Shin_Megami_Tensei:_Nocturne" title="Shin Megami Tensei: Nocturne">Shin Megami Tensei: Nocturne</a></i>. His inclusion was suggested by the <a href="/wiki/Atlus" title="Atlus">Atlus</a> staff due to how well Dante would fit in the game's plot and convinced Capcom to include him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> There is a Dante character card in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SNK_vs._Capcom_Card_Fighters_DS" title="SNK vs. Capcom Card Fighters DS">SNK vs. Capcom: Card Fighters DS</a></i>. He makes his second fighting game appearance as a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_vs._Capcom_3:_Fate_of_Two_Worlds" title="Marvel vs. Capcom 3: Fate of Two Worlds">Marvel vs. Capcom 3: Fate of Two Worlds</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GIJune_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-GIJune-30">[30]</a></sup> He was also set to appear in <i><a href="/wiki/Soulcalibur_III" title="Soulcalibur III">Soulcalibur III</a></i>, but did not make it into the final game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soulcaliIII_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-soulcaliIII-31">[31]</a></sup> He appears as a playable character in the tactical role-playing game <i><a href="/wiki/Project_X_Zone" title="Project X Zone">Project X Zone</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Demitri_Maximoff" title="Demitri Maximoff">Demitri Maximoff</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Darkstalkers" title="Darkstalkers">Darkstalkers</a></i> as his partner. He will return in the sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Project_X_Zone_2" title="Project X Zone 2">Project X Zone 2</a></i>, with Vergil as his partner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Hideki Kamiya, now in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platinum_Games" title="Platinum Games">Platinum Games</a>, has stated he was approached about having Sega character <a href="/wiki/Bayonetta_(character)" title="Bayonetta (character)">Bayonetta</a> included in this game, to which he refused as he wanted Bayonetta and Dante from <i>Devil May Cry</i> to meet "on his own terms". However, he has stated he has come to regret this decision due to realizing how the fans would have loved the two interacting, and that if there is a <i>Project X Zone 3</i> he would be all for Bayonetta's inclusion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Dante did fight Bayonetta in the popular online series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_Battle" title="Death Battle">Death Battle</a></i> in a fight to the death and won. Dante is a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/PlayStation_All-Stars_Battle_Royale" title="PlayStation All-Stars Battle Royale">PlayStation All-Stars Battle Royale</a></i>, using his appearance from <i>DmC: Devil May Cry</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The inclusion of his <i>DmC</i> persona over the classic one generated criticism by fans and <a href="/wiki/SuperBot_Entertainment" title="SuperBot Entertainment">SuperBot Entertainment</a> responded to them expecting them to enjoy the character's gameplay mechanics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dante also appears in many other pieces of media based on the video games. These include two <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novels</a> written by Shinya Goikeda featuring original stories; The first has a young Dante under the alias of "Tony Redgrave" as he is chased by assassins and <i>Devil May Cry 2</i> prequel that has him searching for a demonic statue known as the Beastheads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmazonDMC1_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmazonDMC1-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmazonDMC2_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmazonDMC2-38">[38]</a></sup> A <i>Devil May Cry 4</i> novel by writer Bingo Morihashi reprises Dante's role from the game but reveals his interest in Nero after finding him very similar to his brother, Vergil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> There is also a <i>Devil May Cry 3</i> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> that follows Dante before the game's events, a comic of the first game published by <a href="/wiki/Dreamwave_Productions" title="Dreamwave Productions">Dreamwave Productions</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> and an anime titled <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry:_The_Animated_Series" title="Devil May Cry: The Animated Series">Devil May Cry: The Animated Series</a></i> that follows his daily life as he solves cases involving demons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anime_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-anime-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The reboot version of Dante also appears in the prequel comic <i>The Chronicles of Vergil</i> when he receives the sword Rebellion to fight demons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In the <i>Sengoku Basara vs. Devil May Cry</i> stage he will be portrayed by Hiroki Suzuki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>The popularity of the <i>Devil May Cry</i> series lead to a line of action figures produced by Toycom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Actionfigures_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Actionfigures-45">[45]</a></sup> Japanese company <a href="/wiki/Kaiyodo" title="Kaiyodo">Kaiyodo</a> produced a similar line for <i>Devil May Cry 2</i> and a <i>Devil May Cry 3</i> Dante action figure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmazonDMC3_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmazonDMC3-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmazonDMC4_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmazonDMC4-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conception_and_design">Conception and design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Conception and design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dante debuted in <i>Devil May Cry</i>, a game originally intended to be a part of Capcom's <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a></i> franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-48">[48]</a></sup> Series' creator Hideki Kamiya wrote his name after rewriting the story and took it from <a href="/wiki/Dante_Alighieri" title="Dante Alighieri">Dante Alighieri</a>'s poem <i><a href="/wiki/Divine_Comedy" title="Divine Comedy">Divine Comedy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dmc3interview_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-dmc3interview-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dmcscenario_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-dmcscenario-50">[50]</a></sup> Kamiya has said that the title character from the manga series <i>Cobra</i> by <a href="/wiki/Buichi_Terasawa" title="Buichi Terasawa">Buichi Terasawa</a> served as the basis for Dante's personality. Kamiya based his idea of Dante on what he perceived as "Stylish" – wearing a long coat to make the character "showy" and a non-smoker, as Kamiya saw that as "more cool". The character wears red because in Japan it is a traditional color for a heroic figure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMCgraphic_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMCgraphic-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cvg.com_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-cvg.com-52">[52]</a></sup> Kamiya has also stated he perceives Dante as "a character that you would want to go out drinking with", someone who was not a show-off but would instead "pull some ridiculous, mischievous joke" to endear people to him. He added that this aspect was intended to make the character feel familiar to audiences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Although Kamiya was not the main writer from the first two <i>Devil May Cry</i> novels he viewed Shinya Goikeda's depiction of Dante similar to the one he wrote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the prequel game <i>Devil May Cry 3</i>, Dante's attitude was emphasized to reflect a younger and more arrogant character than in previous installments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tanaka_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tanaka-55">[55]</a></sup> The Devil Trigger forms for Dante was designed by <a href="/wiki/Kazuma_Kaneko" title="Kazuma Kaneko">Kazuma Kaneko</a> from <a href="/wiki/Atlus" title="Atlus">Atlus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGNKanekoDesign_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGNKanekoDesign-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GameSpotKanekoDesign_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-GameSpotKanekoDesign-57">[57]</a></sup> Gameplay-wise he was made to be easy to control contrasting Vergil who was harder yet stronger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dmc3interview_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-dmc3interview-49">[49]</a></sup> <i>Devil May Cry 4'</i>s producer <a href="/wiki/Hiroyuki_Kobayashi_(producer)" title="Hiroyuki Kobayashi (producer)">Hiroyuki Kobayashi</a> noted prior to the release of that game that they wanted to make Dante seem significantly more powerful than the game's other protagonist, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nero_(Devil_May_Cry)" title="Nero (Devil May Cry)">Nero</a>. This was done in order to create an evident difference between the strength of a "veteran" when compared to a "rookie". Another reason for this portrayal is based on the series' continuity, which dictated that Dante display the power he would possess after the events of the first game and its prequel, <i>Devil May Cry 3</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-very_long_interview_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-very_long_interview-58">[58]</a></sup> In this game Dante was designed by Tatsuya Yoshikawa who enjoyed the character in his original appearance to the point of calling him "the ultimate Hollywood action superstar." Yoshikawa discussed with the staff to make Dante older, more mature and more experienced to and have him look between his 30s and 40s. The design included multiple details such as Dante's stubble that reflected his "cool" personality as he did not care about shaving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>For both <i>Devil May Cry 3</i> and <i>Devil May Cry 4</i> Dante was voiced by Reuben Langdon who also did the motion capture for certain scenes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-langdon_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-langdon-60">[60]</a></sup> During the development of <i>Devil May Cry 3</i>, Langdon found that doing the motion capture of Dante was difficult to the multiple moves the character makes. Langdon often discussed with the staff about Dante's characterization that was different from the original one as they wanted "a different spin." Eventually, Langdon decided to do his own rendition of Dante as he was confused with the staff's suggestions. In retrospective, Langdon finds Dante "the most difficult, frustrating and yet rewarding character" he has ever played and stated he grew attached with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Langdon was told to make <i>Devil May Cry 4</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Dante similar to his <i>Devil May Cry 3</i> persona albeit more mature. Despite the staff's concerns for the difficulties of such portrayal, Langdon had no issues after choosing <a href="/wiki/Roy_Focker" title="Roy Focker">Roy Focker</a> from the anime series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Super_Dimension_Fortress_Macross" title="The Super Dimension Fortress Macross">The Super Dimension Fortress Macross</a></i> as his character model and noting he had almost the same age as Dante during production of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouTube_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redesign">Redesign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Redesign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>For <i>DmC: Devil May Cry</i>, a reboot of the series, Dante was completely redesigned as a result of comments by the Capcom staff. Dante's original design was originally meant to be similar to the ones from previous games, but Capcom told the <a href="/wiki/Ninja_Theory" title="Ninja Theory">Ninja Theory</a> staff it had to be completely different in order to appeal to a younger demographic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-63">[63]</a></sup> Motohide Eshiro stated this Dante was completely set apart from the previous Dantes which they expected would generate major criticism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> While the original Dante was designed from a Japanese perspective, the new one was made from a Western perspective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xbuttoninter_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-xbuttoninter-65">[65]</a></sup> After several drafts of Dante's new character,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_63-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-63">[63]</a></sup> the designers settled on a look as inspired by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film)" title="The Dark Knight (film)">The Dark Knight</a></i>. Dante's coat is not long, only coming down to his lower-back, his hair is shorter and is black; he has a Devil Trigger form which makes him look close to the classic Dante. Art designer Alessandro Taini drew the character as a child and explain with reboot's origin story why he has white hair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Director Tameem Antoniades denied rumors that his design was modelled after him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>For this game, Dante was made young, consumed by hatred and inexperienced resulting in a fighting style similar to a street brawler rather than that of a skilled swordsman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nowgamer_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-nowgamer-68">[68]</a></sup> As a result of the reboot's theme being "rebellion" most of Dante's actions are based around it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dmcrebootprev_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-dmcrebootprev-69">[69]</a></sup> Antoniades stated that Dante is about "being cool and making you feel cool when you're playing it," and, as such, they felt that his attire from previous games would be found comical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> Antoniades added he felt the classic Dante was not appealing anymore and when comparing the new Dante with the <a href="/wiki/Bayonetta_(character)" title="Bayonetta (character)">title character</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Bayonetta" title="Bayonetta">Bayonetta</a></i> he did not see her style to be what he wanted for the reboot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Antoniades responded to criticism stating they would not change it as it is supposed to fit within the game's setting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-preview_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-preview-72">[72]</a></sup> Additionally, he stated he would have liked this Dante in response to the criticism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dmcrebootprev_69-1"><a href="#cite_note-dmcrebootprev-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dante_(Devil_May_Cry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dante's confident and fearless attitude has gained him widespread popularity. When first introduced, <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Informer" title="Game Informer">Game Informer</a></i> described him as "one of the most bad-ass characters around," citing the contrast between his character and those previously seen in the <i>Resident Evil</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gameinformer_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gameinformer-11">[11]</a></sup> His design and personality received praise from numerous reviews, such as <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>, who referred to him as "a dark <a href="/wiki/Antihero" title="Antihero">antihero</a> kind of guy even a down-in-the-dumps, disgruntled teenager would love."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Review2_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-Review2-73">[73]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameSpy" title="GameSpy">GameSpy</a> described his appearance as "awe-inspiring to look at", feeling his attire was reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Price" title="Vincent Price">Vincent Price</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empire_(magazine)" title="Empire (magazine)">Empire</a></i> also ranked Dante on their list of the 50 greatest video game characters as 38th, adding Dante is "surely one of the coolest" ones in the history of video gaming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GamesRadar" title="GamesRadar">GamesRadar</a> named him "Mister 2001" in their article on the sexiest new characters of the decade, stating that compared to the female characters in <i>Devil May Cry</i>, Dante was the "hottest" of them all, due to his muscular build, hair, his fighting abilities, and his attitude.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> Dante was ranked third in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ScrewAttack" title="ScrewAttack">ScrewAttack</a>'s list of the "coolest" video game characters in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Screwattack_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-Screwattack-77">[77]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameDaily" title="GameDaily">GameDaily</a> named him one of their favorite Capcom characters, ranking him seventh and stating that the various aspects of the character made him an "unstoppable force".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> GamesRadar also listed him as one of the 25 best new characters of the decade, calling him "unforgettable", as well as stating that he had set the template for "smartass action heroes".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> In 2010, Dante came 23rd in a <i><a href="/wiki/Famitsu" title="Famitsu">Famitsu</a></i> poll featuring the most popular video game characters in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> He was also voted by <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Informer" title="Game Informer">Game Informer</a></i> as the 13th best character from the 2000s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> In 2012, GamesRadar stated that despite Dante's multiple characterizations "he's a guy who you'll always have a hell of a time playing with."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> Dante and Trish were also included in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Inquirer" title="The Inquirer">The Inquirer</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s list of the most memorable video game love teams, with comments focused on how the two join forces to defeat their enemies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> In 2014, <i><a href="/wiki/La_Nueva_Espa%C3%B1a" title="La Nueva España">La Nueva España</a></i> included this "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ephebe" title="wikt:ephebe">ephebe</a> that exudes testosterone from every pore" among the top ten sexiest video game characters of both genders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p>Additionally, Dante has also often been compared with the <a href="/wiki/Bayonetta_(character)" title="Bayonetta (character)">title character</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Bayonetta" title="Bayonetta">Bayonetta</a></i> especially in his <i>Devil May Cry 4</i> persona based on their personalities and actions that made the two of them appealing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> Reuben Langdon's performance as Dante in <i>Devil May Cry 3</i> and <i>Devil May Cry 4</i> received praise by GamesRadar for being the character's best voice actor noting that the two actors did not fit the character well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the other hand, Dante's characterization has often resulted in criticism. In <i>Devil May Cry 2</i>, Dante's cocky attitude was largely absent, which was one of the main criticisms of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gamespy_88-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gamespy-88">[88]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Railey.2C_Dave_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-Railey.2C_Dave-89">[89]</a></sup> Fan reactions to the new design of Dante in <i>DmC: Devil May Cry</i> were mostly negative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> Hideki Kamiya found him based on the modern's rebellious youth and said he missed the original Dante, but he still hoped that people would try it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> Dave Riley from <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> compared the reboot's Dante with the young Dante from <i>Devil May Cry 3</i> but criticized him for being "pretty generic adolescent power fantasy" as well as his use of swearing when confronting enemies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Railey.2C_Dave_89-1"><a href="#cite_note-Railey.2C_Dave-89">[89]</a></sup> Dante's voice actor, Reuben Langdon, expressed disappointment with Dante's characterization in the reboot despite his anticipation to it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-langdon_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-langdon-60">[60]</a></sup> In contrast, several websites writers criticized the fanbase's negative reactions stating they were solely influenced by the character's look and that his personality was very close to the classic Dante.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></p>
