<p><b>Daler Singh</b>, known by his stage name, <b>"Daler Mehndi"</b> (born 18 August 1967) is an Indian recording artist, musician, song writer, author, record producer, performer and environmentalist. He is credited with making <a href="/wiki/Bhangra_(music)" title="Bhangra (music)">Bhangra</a> popular worldwide, and setting up a parallel non-film music industry to the pre-Daler era’s then existent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bollywood_music" title="Bollywood music">Bollywood music</a>. He is best known for his energetic dance songs, his distinctive voice,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> turban and long flowing robes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daler Singh was born in Patna, Bihar on 18 August 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1991, he formed his own group composed of his brothers, cousins and musician friends. A.J Jaspal of IPCA gave Daler a professional set up and promoted him. In 1994, Jaspal sent Daler to compete at the annual Voice of Asia International Ethnic and Pop Music Contest at Azia Dynasys in <a href="/wiki/Almaty" title="Almaty">Almaty</a>, Kazakhstan. He represented India and was judged second amongst other two hundred contestants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Recording_for_Chaalis_Chauraasi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="439" data-file-width="585" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Recording_for_Chaalis_Chauraasi.jpg/220px-Recording_for_Chaalis_Chauraasi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Recording_for_Chaalis_Chauraasi.jpg/330px-Recording_for_Chaalis_Chauraasi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Recording_for_Chaalis_Chauraasi.jpg/440px-Recording_for_Chaalis_Chauraasi.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnasound" title="Magnasound">Magnasound</a> signed Daler Mehndi for a three album contract for three years. <i>Bolo Ta Ra Ra</i>, Mehndi's debut album, sold over 20 million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The album established Daler as a pop star,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> which also won him the Channel V's Best Indian Male Pop Artist Award. A year later, his second album under Magnasound, <i>Dardi Rab Rab</i>, was launched. This album surpassed the sales of <i>Bolo Ta Ra Ra</i>. He was nominated in three categories at <a href="/wiki/Channel_V" title="Channel V">Channel V</a> Music awards and won the Channel V Best Indian Male Artist Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1997 he released his third album <i>Balle Balle</i>. This album got him Channel V Awards in six categories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Mehndi composed the popular track "Na Na Na Re" for the Bollywood film <i><a href="/wiki/Mrityudata" title="Mrityudata">Mrityudata</a></i> where he featured on the big screen with <a href="/wiki/Amitabh_Bachchan" title="Amitabh Bachchan">Amitabh Bachchan</a>.</p>
<p>In 2000, Mehndi released his album <i>Ek Dana</i> with music label <a href="/wiki/Tips_Industries_Limited" title="Tips Industries Limited">TIPS Music</a>. The album had a mix of genres ranging from <a href="/wiki/Folk_music_of_Punjab" title="Folk music of Punjab">Folk</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_Music" title="Rock Music">Rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">Pop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> One of the more popular songs of the album <i>Sajan Mere Satrangiya</i> featured the Indian actress <a href="/wiki/Priyanka_Chopra" title="Priyanka Chopra">Priyanka Chopra</a> in the video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This video was released in <a href="/wiki/Miss_World_2010" title="Miss World 2010">the year</a> that Priyanka Chopra won the <a href="/wiki/Miss_World" title="Miss World">Miss World</a> title. Subsequently, Daler signed up with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a> in 2001 and released a new track "Kala Kauwa Kaat Khayega". In 2003 Daler forayed into playback singing for films. He started with the song "Ru-Ba-Ru" for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Maqbool" title="Maqbool">Maqbool</a></i>. The same year Daler and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.R_Rahman" title="A.R Rahman">A.R Rahman</a> came together to give another track "Nach Le" for <i><a href="/wiki/Lakeer_%E2%80%93_Forbidden_Lines" title="Lakeer – Forbidden Lines">Lakeer – Forbidden Lines</a></i>. He fused Rock with Bhangra with his next album titled <i>Mojaan Laen Do</i> and in 2004 he self produced <i>Shaa Ra Ra</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2014, <a href="/wiki/Deadmau5" title="Deadmau5">Deadmau5</a> stated on Twitter that he was eager to have Mehndi perform on his forthcoming show in India at the <a href="/wiki/Sunburn" title="Sunburn">Sunburn</a> Festival. And the outcome of bonding over social media and on the Coffee Run was a remix of Mehndi's 1998 hit "<a href="/wiki/Tunak_Tunak_Tun" title="Tunak Tunak Tun">Tunak Tunak Tun</a>" performed by the duo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On Navratri 2014, Mehndi composed and recorded his rendition of the 'Hanuman Chalisa' overnight while working at his studio in Delhi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Daler Mehndi, feeling inspired during the festival of Navratri, composed and record his rendition of the 'Hanuman Chalisa' in the DM Folk Studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September,2014, Mehndi released his new song <i>Aaja Mere Twitter Te</i> to encourage users to visit his Twitter account and Facebook page.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 November 2014, Mehndi released his album <i>Best of Gurbani</i> on the occasion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guru_Nanak_Jayanti" title="Guru Nanak Jayanti">Guru Nanak Jayanti</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tours">Tours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1998, Mehndi was signed by <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> for product endorsement and was featured in 30 concerts across India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He has performed worldwide including the US, Canada, Australia, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, UK, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a>, South Africa, East Africa, <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, Hong Kong, Singapore, <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 September 2013, Daler performed at the YouFest festival in Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He has had an advent with Canadian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_Dance_Music" title="Electronic Dance Music">Electronic Dance Music</a> (EDM) artist <a href="/wiki/Deadmau5" title="Deadmau5">Deadmau5</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lamborghini_Murci%C3%A9lago_LP670-4_SuperVeloce" title="Lamborghini Murciélago LP670-4 SuperVeloce">Lamborghini Murciélago LP670-4 SuperVeloce</a>, Daler featured on his tour in India and together they produced an EDM remixed version of "<a href="/wiki/Tunak_Tunak_Tun" title="Tunak Tunak Tun">Tunak Tunak Tun</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2014, Daler Mehndi was invited as the guest of honour along with U.S Congressman <a href="/wiki/Pete_Sessions" title="Pete Sessions">Pete Sessions</a> for the closing bell ceremony at <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> Stock Exchange at New York, after which he performed at the National Indian American Public Policy Institute Azadi Diwas (NIAPPI)celebrations on the occasion of the 65th <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(India)" title="Independence Day (India)">Independence Day</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Daler Mehndi delivered his first ever performance in Kuwait on 5 September. Welcomed by a packed audience at the Al Daiya Indoor Stadium, he broke his own record for the longest live performance without a break by performing for four and a half hours non-stop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The concert organised by the Indian Cultural Society (ICS) has been named 'Bolo Ta Ra Ra Ra' in honour of Daler Mehndi's globally popular track by the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the eve of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guru_Nanak_Jayanti" title="Guru Nanak Jayanti">Guru Nanak Jayanti</a> Daler Mehndi was invited to Ulhas Nagar to perform at the world's largest Prabhat Pheri.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2014 the singer visited Mandsaur, Madhya Pradesh for a performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2014 Daler Mehndi was invited to Nigeria for a performance to celebrate Diwali. This was the second time the musician has performed in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vocal_style_and_instruments">Vocal style and instruments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vocal style and instruments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daler Mehndi is credited for creating a genre called "Rabbabi", which is a combination of <a href="/wiki/Thumri" title="Thumri">Thumri</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sufi" title="Sufi">Sufi</a> and rock and creating an instrument called "Swar Mandir" which is an instrument that combines influences from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rabab" title="Rabab">Rabab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swarmandal" title="Swarmandal">Swarmandal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tanpura" title="Tanpura">Tanpura</a>. The instrument was crafted by Sanjay Rikhi Ram from Delhi and was launched on 10 February 2012 by the <a href="/wiki/Indian_classical_music" title="Indian classical music">Indian classical music</a> maestro, <a href="/wiki/Bharat_Ratna" title="Bharat Ratna">Bharat Ratna</a> awarded Pandit <a href="/wiki/Ravi_Shankar" title="Ravi Shankar">Ravi Shankar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_ventures">Business ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Business ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daler Mehndi launched his maiden Record Label 'DRecords' in the year 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The record label has several artists under its banner including Hussain Baksh and Safri Boys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_pop_culture">Foreign pop culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Foreign pop culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daler Mehndi's "<a href="/wiki/Tunak_Tunak_Tun" title="Tunak Tunak Tun">Tunak Tunak Tun</a>" has found popularity on the internet as a <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral video</a>. The video game company <a href="/wiki/Blizzard_Entertainment" title="Blizzard Entertainment">Blizzard Entertainment</a> incorporated the Tunak Tunak Tun dance as a character animation in their multiplayer role playing game <a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft" title="World of Warcraft">World of Warcraft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daler Mehndi is married to Taranpreet Kaur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He has four children – <a href="/wiki/Gurdeep_Mehndi" title="Gurdeep Mehndi">Gurdeep Mehndi</a>, Ajit Kaur Mehndi, Prabhjot Kaur Mehndi and Rabaab Kaur Mehndi. His daughter Ajit Kaur Mehndi is married to <a href="/wiki/Navraj_Hans" title="Navraj Hans">Navraj Hans</a>, son of Punjabi singer <a href="/wiki/Hans_Raj_Hans" title="Hans Raj Hans">Hans Raj Hans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Gurdeep Mehndi got engaged to his fellow actress from Delhi 1984, Jessica Singh in 2014.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daler_Mehndi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998 Mehndi launched the "<a href="/wiki/Daler_Mehndi_Green_Drive" title="Daler Mehndi Green Drive">Daler Mehndi Green Drive</a>", inducted in the Special Task Force of the Delhi Government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The drive has planted over 1.2 million saplings in and around Delhi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>He has performed for charity for <a href="/wiki/Imran_Khan" title="Imran Khan">Imran Khan</a>'s Shaukat Khanum Memorial Trust in Pakistan and helped raise $5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mehndi supports charitable causes such as cancer, <a href="/wiki/Thalassemia" title="Thalassemia">thalassemia</a> and AIDS, and has helped to bring up the homeless children of <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> as well as famine-stricken families in Kalahandi in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orissa,_India" title="Orissa, India">Orissa, India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> He financially supported the families of <a href="/wiki/Kargil" title="Kargil">Kargil</a> martyrs by donating to the Indian government's Kargil Victims' Relief Fund.</p>
<p>Mehndi was one of the first celebrities to go and visit the soldiers at Kargil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Mehndi has constructed earthquake resistant houses for the <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a> earthquake victims; he donated money and performed to raise money for Gujarat cyclone and quake victims, and for police welfare funds. He has donated rupees 10 million in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vadodra" title="Vadodra">Vadodra</a> for the victims of the cyclone in the government aid fund. He has taken upon himself the reconstruction of a block devastated in the Gujarat quake in Doodhiya village.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mehndi has set up a Daler Mehndi Food for Life Society in Chander Vihar, <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>, where a 24-hour kitchen provides food to the destitute and the underprivileged. Mehndi has constructed a Gurudwara Dukh Bhanjani Sahib where every morning people from all castes and religions join together and offer service in the name of god. Mehndi is the only known Indian artist to be invited by the President of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> as a state guest. He gifted his album <i>Bismillah</i> to the President Zardari of Pakistan and the funds generated from its sale were given to the President's charitable fund.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mehndi voiced his protest against International Basketball Federation's (<a href="/wiki/FIBA" title="FIBA">FIBA</a>) controversial ruling on the use of turbans while playing. The singer made a video to raise awareness about the issue and to have the policy changed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Mahendi was appointed as the brand ambassador of NIAPPI Business Council by <a href="/wiki/Shalabh_Kumar" title="Shalabh Kumar">Shalabh Kumar</a>.</p>
