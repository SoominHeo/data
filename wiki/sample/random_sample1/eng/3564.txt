<p><b>Damyang County</b> (<i>Damyang-gun</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_South_Korea" title="Administrative divisions of South Korea">county</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeollanam-do" title="Jeollanam-do">Jeollanam-do</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tourism_in_South_Korea" title="Tourism in South Korea">Tourism</a> is a major local industry. Notable local products include <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a> goods and strawberries.</p>
<p>Damyang is not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Danyang_County" title="Danyang County">Danyang</a>, which is located in eastern <a href="/wiki/North_Chungcheong_Province" title="North Chungcheong Province">North Chungcheong Province</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county comprises one <i>eup</i> (Damyang-eup, the seat), and eleven <i>myeon</i> (Bongsan-myeon, Changpyeong-myeon, Daedeok-myeon, Daejeon-myeon, Geumseong-myeon, Goseo-myeon, Mujeong-myeon, Nam-myeon, Subok-myeon, Wolsan-myeon, Yong-myeon).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bamboo">Bamboo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bamboo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Damyang is one of the northernmost places where <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a> can be found on the Korean peninsula,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and its bamboo forests are well-known among Koreans. Many touristic attractions have been created to leverage that reputation: a Bamboo Theme Park / Daenamugol, a Bamboo Museum, a Bamboo Festival, a park filled with green bamboo (Juknokwon - 죽녹원).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metasequoia">Metasequoia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Metasequoia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A stretch of <a href="/wiki/Metasequoia" title="Metasequoia">metasequoia</a> trees just outside Damyang-eup, planted during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Chung-Hee" title="Park Chung-Hee">Park Chung-Hee</a> administration in the 1970s, has a long walking path of about two kilometers. The trees are particularly striking in late October to early November when their leaves turn bright colors. The walk gained nationwide popularity after a romantic <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">drama</a> was shot here. It was even picked as one of the most beautiful roads in all of Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food">Food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Food">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Jeollanam-do enjoys a good reputation for its cuisine, and Damyang provides a large choice of restaurants.</li>
<li>The most popular specialities include Damyang ddeok galbi (<a href="/wiki/Tteokgalbi" title="Tteokgalbi">tteokgalbi</a>): this tasty and juicy marinated beef and pork mix, usually grilled at the table, is supposed to have originated either in Damyang or in <a href="/wiki/Gwangju" title="Gwangju">Gwangju</a>.</li>
<li>Bamboo foods served at restaurants in Damyang include daetongbap (rice cooked in bamboo), tea places bamboo tea and the salad of raw bamboo with spicy vinegar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">Literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several houses of famous scholars can be visited, and the Miam Diaries (Miam Yu Hui-chun: 1513-1577) are preserved in Daeduk-myon.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Damyang Bamboo Festival</li>
<li>The V <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomy_Olympiad" title="International Astronomy Olympiad">Asian-Pacific Astronomy Olympiad</a> took place in Damyang in October 2009.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dramas">Dramas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Dramas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Korean dramas have become a significant source of revenue for tourism, and many counties sponsor series or compete to host a few shootings, hoping to hit a jackpot similar to the localities 'blessed' with <i><a href="/wiki/Winter_Sonata" title="Winter Sonata">Winter Sonata</a>'</i>s visit. Damyang boasts its metasequoia path (see above), and a 'movie village'.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks_and_monuments">Landmarks and monuments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Landmarks and monuments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>They include the fortress of Geumseongsanseong, and various national treasures (pagodas, pavilions... - overall, 66 properties, including 15 at the national level and 51 at the provincial level).</p>
<p>Infrastructure has followed the natural assets, and tourists can stay in themed resorts with local features such as hot baths perfumed with bamboo.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soswaewon">Soswaewon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Soswaewon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A private garden built by Yang San-bo (1503–1557), <a href="/wiki/Soswaewon" title="Soswaewon">Soswaewon</a> (소쇄원) stretches along a charming streamlet dotted with pavilions particularly suitable for meditation. A refreshing spot in the summer heat.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juknokwon">Juknokwon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damyang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Juknokwon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Juknokwon was opened on May, 2003. This bamboo grove is 310,000<sup>2</sup> wide. There are 8 trails which have different themes. And it was filming location of '<a href="/wiki/R-Point" title="R-Point">R-Point</a>'.</p>
