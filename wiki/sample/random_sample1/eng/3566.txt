<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Yourself and Yours</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><i><b>Yourself and Yours</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">당신자신과 당신의 것</span>) is a 2016 <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> drama film directed by <a href="/wiki/Hong_Sang-soo" title="Hong Sang-soo">Hong Sang-soo</a>.</p>
<p>The plot revolves around the couple Yeong-soo (played by <a href="/wiki/Kim_Joo-hyuk" title="Kim Joo-hyuk">Kim Joo-hyuk</a>) and Min-jeong (played by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Yoo-young_(actress)" title="Lee Yoo-young (actress)">Lee Yoo-young</a>). After a fight between them, they decide to take a break in their relationship. The next day, Min-jeong vanishes without a trace. Instead a woman shows up looking exactly like Min-jeong. She seems interested in looking for "Mr. Right" and dating every man in the town where they live. One day Min-jeong returns.</p>
<p>In his review for <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i>, Scott Tobias described the plot as an "inspired reversal" of the film <i><a href="/wiki/That_Obscure_Object_of_Desire" title="That Obscure Object of Desire">That Obscure Object of Desire</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
