<p><b>Danny Barber</b> (born May 17, 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Bellflower,_California" title="Bellflower, California">Bellflower, California</a>) is a retired U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> who played one game with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MetroStars" title="MetroStars">MetroStars</a> in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>. He spent most of his career playing indoor soccer in the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Indoor_Soccer_League" title="Continental Indoor Soccer League">Continental Indoor Soccer League</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPSL_II" title="NPSL II">National Professional Soccer League</a> and second <a href="/wiki/Major_Indoor_Soccer_League_(2001%E2%80%9308)" title="Major Indoor Soccer League (2001–08)">Major Indoor Soccer League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Barber&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barber graduated from <a href="/wiki/Pacifica_High_School_(Garden_Grove,_California)" title="Pacifica High School (Garden Grove, California)">Pacifica High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Garden_Grove,_California" title="Garden Grove, California">Garden Grove, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He then attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNLV" title="UNLV">UNLV</a>, playing on the men's soccer team from 1989 to 1992. He holds the school's career assists record with 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional">Professional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Barber&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He joined the roster of the newly established <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Dustdevils" title="Las Vegas Dustdevils">Las Vegas Dustdevils</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Indoor_Soccer_League" title="Continental Indoor Soccer League">Continental Indoor Soccer League</a>. That season, the Dustdevils won the CISL championship<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He continued to play for the Dustdevils in 1995.</p>
<p>In February 1996, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MetroStars" title="MetroStars">MetroStars</a> selected Barber in the 3rd round (22nd overall) of the <a href="/wiki/1996_MLS_Supplemental_Draft" title="1996 MLS Supplemental Draft">1996 MLS Supplemental Draft</a>. He played one game for them.</p>
<p>In the fall of 1996, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Silverbacks" title="Cincinnati Silverbacks">Cincinnati Silverbacks</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPSL_II" title="NPSL II">National Professional Soccer League</a>. The Silverbacks folded following the 1997–1998 season and Barber moved to the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Blizzard" title="Buffalo Blizzard">Buffalo Blizzard</a>. He remained with the Blizzard until the team folded in the spring of 2001. He then began the 2001–2002 season with the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Blast" title="Baltimore Blast">Baltimore Blast</a> in the newly established <a href="/wiki/Major_Indoor_Soccer_League_(2001%E2%80%9308)" title="Major Indoor Soccer League (2001–08)">Major Indoor Soccer League</a>. He played twenty-one games for the Blast before being traded to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harrisburg_Heat_(NPSL/MISL)" title="Harrisburg Heat (NPSL/MISL)">Harrisburg Heat</a> in exchange for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lester_Felician&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lester Felician (page does not exist)">Lester Felician</a> on February 18, 2002. He finished the season in Harrisburg. On September 9, 2002, the Heat traded Barber, the rights to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Justin_Labrum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Justin Labrum (page does not exist)">Justin Labrum</a> and financial considerations to the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Sidekicks_(1984%E2%80%932004)" title="Dallas Sidekicks (1984–2004)">Dallas Sidekicks</a> in exchange for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Coria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marco Coria (page does not exist)">Marco Coria</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Genoni_Martinez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Genoni Martinez (page does not exist)">Genoni Martinez</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Raul_Salas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Raul Salas (page does not exist)">Raul Salas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He played nine games for the Sidekicks at the beginning of the 2002–2003 season before being released on October 14, 2002. On December 2, 2002 the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Blast" title="Baltimore Blast">Baltimore Blast</a> signed Barber to a 15-day/5-game contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He played four of those five games then was released. On July 23, 2003, the St. Louis Steamers selected Barber in the 11th Round of the 2003 MISL Expansion Draft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, he never played for the team.</p>
