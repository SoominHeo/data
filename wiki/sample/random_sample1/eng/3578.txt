<p><b>Daniel W. "Danny" Strong</b> (born June 6, 1974) is an American <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>, and producer. As an actor, Strong is best known for his roles as <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Levinson" title="Jonathan Levinson">Jonathan Levinson</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buffy_the_Vampire_Slayer_(TV_series)" title="Buffy the Vampire Slayer (TV series)">Buffy the Vampire Slayer</a></i> and Doyle McMaster in <i><a href="/wiki/Gilmore_Girls" title="Gilmore Girls">Gilmore Girls</a></i>. He also wrote the screenplays for <i><a href="/wiki/Recount_(film)" title="Recount (film)">Recount</a></i>, the HBO adaptation <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Change_(film)" title="Game Change (film)">Game Change</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Butler" title="The Butler">Lee Daniels' The Butler</a></i>, and the two-part finale of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film_series)" title="The Hunger Games (film series)">The Hunger Games</a></i> film trilogy, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay_%E2%80%93_Part_1" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 1">Mockingjay – Part 1</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay_%E2%80%93_Part_2" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 2">Mockingjay – Part 2</a></i>. Strong also is a co-creator, executive producer, and writer for the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_(2015_TV_series)" title="Empire (2015 TV series)">Empire</a></i>.</p>
<p>Strong has won two <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Emmy Awards</a>, two <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Writers Guild of America Award">Writers Guild of America Awards</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Producers Guild of America Award">Producers Guild of America Award</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/NAACP_Image_Award" title="NAACP Image Award">NAACP Image Award</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Strong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Strong grew up in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Beach,_California" title="Manhattan Beach, California">Manhattan Beach, California</a>. He was raised in a <a href="/wiki/American_Jews" title="American Jews">Jewish</a> family (of Lithuanian, Russian, and Polish origin).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He began acting at a young age. Strong rented videos from <a href="/wiki/Video_Archives" title="Video Archives">Video Archives</a>, and became friends with <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a>, who worked there as a clerk. "I would just literally sit and chat with him for 45 minutes, an hour at a time about movies, and he got me turned on to all these different movies that 10 year olds don't see."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Strong attended <a href="/wiki/Mira_Costa_High_School" title="Mira Costa High School">Mira Costa High School</a>, and then studied film and theatre at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Strong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Strong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Strong is known for playing <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Levinson" title="Jonathan Levinson">Jonathan Levinson</a> on the television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buffy_the_Vampire_Slayer_(TV_series)" title="Buffy the Vampire Slayer (TV series)">Buffy the Vampire Slayer</a></i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Geller" title="Paris Geller">Paris Geller</a>'s boyfriend Doyle McMaster on <i><a href="/wiki/Gilmore_Girls" title="Gilmore Girls">Gilmore Girls</a></i>, but he has also appeared in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Pleasantville_(film)" title="Pleasantville (film)">Pleasantville</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Minds" title="Dangerous Minds">Dangerous Minds</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Seabiscuit_(film)" title="Seabiscuit (film)">Seabiscuit</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">spoof</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shriek_If_You_Know_What_I_Did_Last_Friday_The_Thirteenth" title="Shriek If You Know What I Did Last Friday The Thirteenth">Shriek If You Know What I Did Last Friday The Thirteenth</a></i>, and was in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Sydney_White" title="Sydney White">Sydney White</a></i> as the Grumpy dork, Gurkin. Strong has also had guest parts in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Situation_comedy" title="Situation comedy">sitcoms</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Clueless_(TV_series)" title="Clueless (TV series)">Clueless</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/3rd_Rock_from_the_Sun" title="3rd Rock from the Sun">3rd Rock from the Sun</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Over_the_Top_(TV_series)" title="Over the Top (TV series)">Over the Top</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i> and has also guest-lectured in acting classes on finding a job as an actor. Most recently, Strong appeared in the popular <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Men" title="Mad Men">Mad Men</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mad_Men_characters#Danny_Siegel" title="List of Mad Men characters">Danny Siegel</a>, a young man with no talent, trying to break into the advertising industry, later making a career in Hollywood. Strong also appeared on the HBO series <a href="/wiki/Girls_(TV_series)" title="Girls (TV series)"><i>Girls</i></a> in the third and fourth seasons; and appeared on the fifth and sixth seasons of <a href="/wiki/Justified_(TV_series)" title="Justified (TV series)"><i>Justified</i></a> as Albert Ferkus, a rapist prison guard.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">Writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Strong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At 25, in the hopes of being the lead actor in his own film, Strong wrote a dark comedy about two men who kill an elderly man for his <a href="/wiki/Rent_regulation" title="Rent regulation">rent-controlled</a> apartment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duca_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-duca-4">[4]</a></sup> The film never materialized, but it sparked his desire to become a full-time writer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duca_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-duca-4">[4]</a></sup> His first successful script was <i><a href="/wiki/Recount_(film)" title="Recount (film)">Recount</a></i>, a film about the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._presidential_election,_2000" title="U.S. presidential election, 2000">2000 US Elections</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Jay_Roach" title="Jay Roach">Jay Roach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The film starred <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Spacey" title="Kevin Spacey">Kevin Spacey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laura_Dern" title="Laura Dern">Laura Dern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denis_Leary" title="Denis Leary">Denis Leary</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Hurt" title="John Hurt">John Hurt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Wilkinson" title="Tom Wilkinson">Tom Wilkinson</a> and premiered on May 25, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The script had been voted number one on the 2007 Hollywood Black List,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> a list of the "most liked" but unproduced scripts as voted on by the Hollywood community and insiders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Strong was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/60th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="60th Primetime Emmy Awards">2008 Emmy Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Writing_for_a_Miniseries,_Movie_or_a_Dramatic_Special" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Miniseries, Movie or a Dramatic Special">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Miniseries, Movie or a Dramatic Special</a> for <i>Recount</i>. The film was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Miniseries or Television Film">Best Television Miniseries or Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/66th_Golden_Globe_Awards" title="66th Golden Globe Awards">66th Golden Globe Awards</a> and won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Made_for_Television_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Made for Television Movie">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Made for Television Movie</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Writers Guild of America Award">Writers Guild of America Award</a> for Best Original Screenplay for a Television movie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Strong followed up <i>Recount</i> with the <a href="/wiki/Game_Change_(film)" title="Game Change (film)">2012 film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Change" title="Game Change">Game Change</a>,</i> based on the book written by <a href="/wiki/John_Heilemann" title="John Heilemann">John Heilemann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Halperin" title="Mark Halperin">Mark Halperin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The film starred Julianne Moore and aired on HBO on March 10, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2012, he won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Writing_for_a_Miniseries,_Movie_or_a_Dramatic_Special" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Miniseries, Movie or a Dramatic Special">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Miniseries, Movie or a Dramatic Special</a> for <i>Game Change</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The film was also awarded a <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Award" title="Peabody Award">Peabody Award</a>, which recognizes distinguished and meritorious public service by radio and television stations, networks, producing organizations and individuals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2012, he was hired to write the screenplay for the film adaptation of Dan Brown's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Symbol" title="The Lost Symbol">The Lost Symbol</a></i>, whose production has been stalled since 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Strong wrote the screenplay for the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Butler" title="The Butler">The Butler</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forest_Whitaker" title="Forest Whitaker">Forest Whitaker</a> starred in the film and it was directed by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Daniels" title="Lee Daniels">Lee Daniels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The script was voted on to the 2010 Hollywood Black List.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It was released in August 2013 and grossed over $100 million in the US box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He also had a cameo in the film.</p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:300px; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">

<p>I write characters of every race, gender and sexual orientation. I write some characters that are animals. That's just my job. To me, writing 'Empire' is not more difficult than Sarah Palin [in 'Game Change']. ... I mean, I'm not a Republican from Alaska.</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">Danny Strong, <i>Huffington Post</i> interview, June 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duca_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-duca-4">[4]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>In December 2013, Strong signed on to pen the new screenplay for the film adaptation of the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Guys_and_Dolls" title="Guys and Dolls">Guys and Dolls</a></i>, which originally premiered on Broadway in 1950.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Strong also co-created the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_(2015_TV_series)" title="Empire (2015 TV series)">Empire</a></i> with Daniels in 2014, for which he has written and directed multiple episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Strong co-wrote the two-part <i><a href="/wiki/Mockingjay#Film_adaptations" title="Mockingjay">Mockingjay</a></i>, the finale of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film_series)" title="The Hunger Games (film series)">The Hunger Games</a></i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay_-_Part_1" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay - Part 1">Part 1</a></i> was released on November 21, 2014, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay_-_Part_2" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay - Part 2">Part 2</a></i> was released on November 20, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
