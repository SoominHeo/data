<p><b>Daniel William John "Danny" Ings</b> (born 23 July 1992) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>.</p>
<p>Ings started his career in the youth team of <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> but was released as a schoolboy. He subsequently joined <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a>, progressing through their youth system, while also spending time at <a href="/wiki/Dorchester_Town_F.C." title="Dorchester Town F.C.">Dorchester Town</a> on loan. In 2011 Ings joined <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> with whom he won the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Football_League_Championship" title="2013–14 Football League Championship">2013–14 Championship</a> Player of the Year, and helped them achieve promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>. Following Burnley's relegation, and the expiry of his contract with the club, Ings moved to Liverpool in the summer of 2015, but his first season at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> was ended after two months due to injury.</p>
<p>Formerly an <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">under-21 international</a>, Ings made his senior debut for England in October 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Ings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bournemouth">Bournemouth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Ings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bournemouth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ings was born in <a href="/wiki/Winchester" title="Winchester">Winchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a> and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Netley" title="Netley">Netley, Hampshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burnley_FC_Player_Profiles:_Danny_Ings_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Burnley_FC_Player_Profiles:_Danny_Ings-2">[2]</a></sup> As a child he attended <a href="/wiki/Netley_Abbey" title="Netley Abbey">Netley Abbey Primary School</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamble_Community_Sports_College" title="Hamble Community Sports College">Hamble Community Sports College</a>. Ings dreamed of playing for local club Southampton, but was rejected for being too small.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> He signed for <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> from <a href="/wiki/Netley_Central_Sports_F.C." title="Netley Central Sports F.C.">Netley Central Sports</a>, before being released as a school boy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract-4">[4]</a></sup> After his release, Ings played for <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Itchen Tyro</a>. He subsequently signed a two-year apprentice contract with <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a> in May 2008, having progressed through the club's Centre of Excellence while he was still at secondary school at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamble_Community_Sports_College" title="Hamble Community Sports College">Hamble Community Sports College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_intake_for_coach_Joe_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_intake_for_coach_Joe-5">[5]</a></sup> He had also previously had experience playing for the reserve side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_intake_for_coach_Joe_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-New_intake_for_coach_Joe-5">[5]</a></sup> He made his professional debut for Bournemouth on 6 October 2009, in a 2–1 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/Northampton_Town_F.C." title="Northampton Town F.C.">Northampton Town</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Jason_Tindall" title="Jason Tindall">Jason Tindall</a> in the second half as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Northampton_2_-_1_Bournemouth_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Northampton_2_-_1_Bournemouth-6">[6]</a></sup> His second season in the youth team was blighted by a long-term abdominal injury, which restricted his playing time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Hot-shot_Ings_can_only_get_better_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Hot-shot_Ings_can_only_get_better-7">[7]</a></sup> He remained with the youth team until the summer of 2010, when he signed a short-term three-month contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He extended his contract in September 2010, until the end of the 2010–11 campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2010, he was sent on an initial one-month loan to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_South" title="Conference South">Conference South</a> side <a href="/wiki/Dorchester_Town_F.C." title="Dorchester Town F.C.">Dorchester Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ings_and_out_for_Magpies_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ings_and_out_for_Magpies-9">[9]</a></sup> His debut for the Magpies came on 11 September 2010, scoring the equaliser in a 2–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Ebbsfleet_United_F.C." title="Ebbsfleet United F.C.">Ebbsfleet United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ebbing_away_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ebbing_away-10">[10]</a></sup> His second goal for Dorchester came on 24 September, scoring a penalty in a 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Mangotsfield_United_F.C." title="Mangotsfield United F.C.">Mangotsfield United</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mangoes_crushed_by_Magpies_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mangoes_crushed_by_Magpies-11">[11]</a></sup> On 6 October, his loan was extended for a further month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loanee_Danny_extends_Magpies_stay_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Loanee_Danny_extends_Magpies_stay-12">[12]</a></sup> His next goal came in a 4–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Havant_%26_Waterlooville_F.C." title="Havant &amp; Waterlooville F.C.">Havant &amp; Waterlooville</a> as the Magpies were knocked out of the FA Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magpies.27_cup_dream_is_over_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Magpies.27_cup_dream_is_over-13">[13]</a></sup> On 16 October, he scored the second in a 2–0 league win over <a href="/wiki/Lewes_F.C." title="Lewes F.C.">Lewes</a>, after coming on as a late substitute for Giuseppe Sole.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loan_duo_strike_for_Magpies_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Loan_duo_strike_for_Magpies-14">[14]</a></sup> He got his fifth goal in a 2–1 away win over <a href="/wiki/Staines_Town_F.C." title="Staines Town F.C.">Staines Town</a>, scoring the opener.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moss_earns_treble_for_Magpies_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moss_earns_treble_for_Magpies-15">[15]</a></sup> His first goal in November came in a 2–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Maidenhead_United_F.C." title="Maidenhead United F.C.">Maidenhead United</a>, which ended an unbeaten run of six games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magpies.27_run_ended_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Magpies.27_run_ended-16">[16]</a></sup> On 12 November 2010, his loan was extended for a third time for an additional month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Danny_boy_delight_for_Magpies_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Danny_boy_delight_for_Magpies-17">[17]</a></sup> He scored the second in a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Weston-super-Mare_A.F.C." title="Weston-super-Mare A.F.C.">Weston-super-Mare</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Trophy" title="FA Trophy">FA Trophy</a> to help them progress to the next round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Away-day_joy_earns_.C2.A34.2C000_for_Magpies_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Away-day_joy_earns_.C2.A34.2C000_for_Magpies-18">[18]</a></sup> On 23 November, his spell at Dorchester was cut short as he was recalled from his loan by Bournemouth due to an injury crisis at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Danny_departs_Magpies_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Danny_departs_Magpies-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 November, he signed a new contract extension which lasted until 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Ings_signs_contract_extension_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Ings_signs_contract_extension-20">[20]</a></sup> He made his league debut and first start for Bournemouth on 28 December 2010, in a 2–0 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">Milton Keynes Dons</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a>, where he was substituted off in the first half for <a href="/wiki/Steve_Fletcher" title="Steve Fletcher">Steve Fletcher</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_Dons_2_-_0_Bournemouth_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-MK_Dons_2_-_0_Bournemouth-21">[21]</a></sup> He started to become a first team regular and scored his first professional goal against <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Town_F.C." title="Swindon Town F.C.">Swindon Town</a> on 1 February 2011, in a 3–2 win at <a href="/wiki/Dean_Court" title="Dean Court">Dean Court</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bournemouth_3_-_2_Swindon_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bournemouth_3_-_2_Swindon-22">[22]</a></sup> His form saw him rewarded with a new contract extension, lasting until 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract-4">[4]</a></sup> On 26 February 2011, he scored a header to equalise in 2–1 win away at <a href="/wiki/Dagenham_%26_Redbridge_F.C." title="Dagenham &amp; Redbridge F.C.">Dagenham &amp; Redbridge</a> in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dag_.26_Red_1_-_2_Bournemouth_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dag_.26_Red_1_-_2_Bournemouth-23">[23]</a></sup> On 1 April he added to his tally with a goal against <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a> in a 3–3 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peterborough_3_-_3_Bournemouth_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Peterborough_3_-_3_Bournemouth-24">[24]</a></sup> He went through a rich vein of goalscoring from in April, scoring four goals in three games. He scored an overhead kick to equalise in the last minute against <a href="/wiki/Tranmere_Rovers_F.C." title="Tranmere Rovers F.C.">Tranmere Rovers</a>, however, the Cherries went on to lose the game 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bournemouth_1_-_2_Tranmere_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bournemouth_1_-_2_Tranmere-25">[25]</a></sup> He also scored in a 2–0 away victory over <a href="/wiki/Notts_County_F.C." title="Notts County F.C.">Notts County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Notts_County_0_-_2_Bournemouth_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Notts_County_0_-_2_Bournemouth-26">[26]</a></sup> He scored his first brace in a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a>, including one penalty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yeovil_2_-_2_Bournemouth_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yeovil_2_-_2_Bournemouth-27">[27]</a></sup> His final goal for Bournemouth came in the last game of the season, in the play-off semi-final defeat to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>, as Bournemouth were knocked out on penalties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huddersfield_3_-_3_Bournemouth_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Huddersfield_3_-_3_Bournemouth-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer of 2011, he was linked with reported moves away from Bournemouth with <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> also had a £400,000 bid rejected by the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract-4">[4]</a></sup> In July 2011, he was rewarded with his fifth contract in twelve months, improving his deal to be one of the top earners at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-Cherries:_Boss_Lee_offers_Ings_brand_new_contract-4">[4]</a></sup> He remained at the club through pre-season and started in the first game of the season in a 3–0 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a>. It proved to be his final appearance for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charlton_3_-_0_Bournemouth_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Charlton_3_-_0_Bournemouth-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Burnley">Burnley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Ings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Burnley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Danny_Ings.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1698" data-file-width="691" height="418" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Danny_Ings.jpg/170px-Danny_Ings.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Danny_Ings.jpg/255px-Danny_Ings.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Danny_Ings.jpg/340px-Danny_Ings.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

<p>On 15 August 2011, Ings signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> for an undisclosed fee believed to be in the region of £1 million, on a four-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burnley_sign_Bournemouth_striker_Danny_Ings_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burnley_sign_Bournemouth_striker_Danny_Ings-30">[30]</a></sup> He rejoined former Bournemouth manager <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Howe" title="Eddie Howe">Eddie Howe</a> who had made the same move eight months earlier.</p>
<p>He made his debut on 14 February 2012, in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> at <a href="/wiki/Turf_Moor" title="Turf Moor">Turf Moor</a>, where he came on as a late substitute for <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Austin" title="Charlie Austin">Charlie Austin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burnley_2-0_Barnsley_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burnley_2-0_Barnsley-31">[31]</a></sup> His first start came a month later on 10 March 2012, in a 1–1 home draw with <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burnley_1-1_Crystal_Palace_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burnley_1-1_Crystal_Palace-32">[32]</a></sup> His first goal for Burnley came on 31 March, in a 5–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> at <a href="/wiki/Fratton_Park" title="Fratton Park">Fratton Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Portsmouth_1-5_Burnley_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Portsmouth_1-5_Burnley-33">[33]</a></sup> His second goal came in the following game, where he scored the equaliser in a 3–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burnley_1-3_Birmingham_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burnley_1-3_Birmingham-34">[34]</a></sup> He scored his third goal of the season in the final game of the season, a 30-yard strike in a 1–1 home draw with <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burnley_1-1_Bristol_City_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burnley_1-1_Bristol_City-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>He started the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Burnley_F.C._season" title="2012–13 Burnley F.C. season">2012–13</a> season as a first choice regular in pre-season following the sale of striker <a href="/wiki/Jay_Rodriguez" title="Jay Rodriguez">Jay Rodriguez</a> to <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>. He scored in friendly wins over <a href="/wiki/Altrincham_F.C." title="Altrincham F.C.">Altrincham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bury_F.C." title="Bury F.C.">Bury</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PHOTOS:_Altrincham_1.2C_Burnley_4_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-PHOTOS:_Altrincham_1.2C_Burnley_4-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BURY_0_BURNLEY_1_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-BURY_0_BURNLEY_1-37">[37]</a></sup> He suffered another serious knee injury in the final pre-season game, a 3–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_A.F.C." title="Rochdale A.F.C.">Rochdale</a>. He tore the knee cartilage in the opposite knee to the previous season which resulted in surgery, keeping him out of action for up to six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burnley.27s_Danny_Ings_faces_up_six_months_out_with_knee_injury_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burnley.27s_Danny_Ings_faces_up_six_months_out_with_knee_injury-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the departure of top-scorer <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Austin" title="Charlie Austin">Charlie Austin</a> to <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">QPR</a>, Ings became the main striker ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Burnley_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Burnley F.C. season">2013–14 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> He put together a string of impressive performances in the Clarets' pre-season campaign and scored two goals in the opening three league matches as well as a goal in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/York_City_F.C." title="York City F.C.">York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> For his continued good start to the season Ings was named the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship_Player_of_the_Month" title="Football League Championship Player of the Month">Championship Player of the Month</a> for October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2014, Ings won <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Awards#2014_Awards" title="Football League Awards">Championship Player of the Year</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Awards" title="Football League Awards">Football League Awards</a>, ahead of the two other nominees: Leeds United striker <a href="/wiki/Ross_McCormack" title="Ross McCormack">Ross McCormack</a> and Leicester City midfielder <a href="/wiki/Danny_Drinkwater" title="Danny Drinkwater">Danny Drinkwater</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> He ended the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Football_League_Championship" title="2013–14 Football League Championship">2013–14 Championship</a> season with 22 goals as Burnley finished second and gained promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 August 2014, Ings made his Premier League debut in a 3–1 loss to <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> at Turf Moor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> His first Premier League goal came in a 1–3 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> on 26 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 November 2014, Ings scored both of Burnley's goals in a 2–1 win at <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> for their first away win of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Premier_League" title="2014–15 Premier League">Premier League season</a>, his two goals coming in the space of two minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> He made his 100th league appearance for Burnley in a 1–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> on 13 December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>During January and February 2015, Ings scored five goals in six Premier League matches, helping the team to achieve draws with <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a>, and defeat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers" title="Queens Park Rangers">Queens Park Rangers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In May, he scored the winning goal in 1–0 victories over <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> respectively to end the season with 11 goals from 35 league appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Ings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 8 June 2015, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> announced that they had agreed on personal terms with Ings, subject to a medical,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> as well as a developmental fee due to Burnley for players under 24, to be negotiated between two clubs or determined by a tribunal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Under tribunal the fee would be decided by the <a href="/wiki/Professional_Football_Compensation_Committee" title="Professional Football Compensation Committee">Professional Football Compensation Committee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On 28 April 2016, the saga was finally settled. Liverpool would pay Burnley a record fee for a tribunal hearing: £6.5 million up front, with an extra £1.5 million in player performance-related bonuses. Burnley will also receive 20% of any future fee should Liverpool sell the striker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ings made his competitive debut on 29 August 2015 in a 3–0 defeat against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham</a> at Anfield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> He made his European debut for the club on 17 September in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> group stage match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Girondins_de_Bordeaux" title="Girondins de Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a>, as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Divock_Origi" title="Divock Origi">Divock Origi</a> in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Three days later, he replaced <a href="/wiki/Christian_Benteke" title="Christian Benteke">Christian Benteke</a> at half time and within three minutes scored his first Liverpool goal in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> On 4 October 2015, he opened a draw of the same score against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merseyside_Derby" title="Merseyside Derby">Merseyside Derby</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 October 2015, in his first training session under new manager <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Klopp" title="Jürgen Klopp">Jürgen Klopp</a>, Ings suffered an <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament_injury" title="Anterior cruciate ligament injury">anterior cruciate ligament injury</a> in his left knee and was ruled out for the remainder of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> However, Ings returned sooner than anticipated, making a substitute appearance in the final game of the season, a 1–1 draw away to West Bromwich Albion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Ings began the 2016-17 season playing in the reserves to help regain fitness. On 25 October 2016, Ings sustained damage to his right knee in a League Cup match against Tottenham Hotspur that would require another spell on the sidelines of up to 9 months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> He successfully underwent surgery to repair the damage to his knee and begun rehabilitation at <a href="/wiki/Melwood" title="Melwood">Melwood</a> on 9 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Ings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 3 October 2013, Ings received his first international call-up to the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England national under-21 team</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Southgate" title="Gareth Southgate">Gareth Southgate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> He made his debut a week later as a substitute in a 4–0 win away to <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_under-21_football_team" title="San Marino national under-21 football team">San Marino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> He won his second <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">cap</a> in the reverse fixture on 19 November, and scored twice as England won 9–0 against San Marino at the <a href="/wiki/New_Meadow" title="New Meadow">New Meadow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> He earned 13 caps and scored four goals for the under-21s from 2013 to 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 October 2015, Ings received his first international call-up to the full <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England national team</a> from manager <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a>, for the final <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying</a> matches against <a href="/wiki/Estonia_national_football_team" title="Estonia national football team">Estonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> He made his debut in the latter game on the 12th, replacing <a href="/wiki/Harry_Kane" title="Harry Kane">Harry Kane</a> after 59 minutes of an eventual 3–0 win in <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Ings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Off the field, Ings has gained a reputation for charitable acts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> In November 2014, he launched and funded the Danny Ings Disability Sport Project to provide football coaching to children with disabilities and learning difficulties after being inspired to do so by a young disabled Burnley fan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> His father Shayne Ings initially played as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a> and then as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">full-back</a> for Hampshire-based <a href="/wiki/Netley_Central_Sports_F.C." title="Netley Central Sports F.C.">Netley Central Sports</a>. He still works as a self-employed <a href="/wiki/Bricklayer" title="Bricklayer">bricklayer</a> and the family still live in the same house in <a href="/wiki/Netley" title="Netley">Netley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
