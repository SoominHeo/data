<p><b>Daniel John Bedingfield</b> (born 3 December 1979) is an English-New Zealand singer, songwriter and record producer. He is the brother of fellow singers <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Bedingfield" title="Natasha Bedingfield">Natasha Bedingfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nikola_Rachelle" title="Nikola Rachelle">Nikola Rachelle</a>. He was a judge on <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand TV series)">The X Factor New Zealand</a></i> in 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.9303:_Gotta_Get_Thru_This_and_international_breakthrough">2001–03: <i>Gotta Get Thru This</i> and international breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2001–03: Gotta Get Thru This and international breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He released his first single, "<a href="/wiki/Gotta_Get_Thru_This_(song)" title="Gotta Get Thru This (song)">Gotta Get Thru This</a>" in November 2001. It gathered momentum on <a href="/wiki/White_label" title="White label">white label</a> through the <a href="/wiki/UK_garage" title="UK garage">UK garage</a> music scene, and peaked at number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. He has since had two more number-one singles on the chart ― "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/If_You%27re_Not_The_One" title="If You're Not The One">If You're Not The One</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Never_Gonna_Leave_Your_Side" title="Never Gonna Leave Your Side">Never Gonna Leave Your Side</a>" ― as well as three further top ten hits. His debut album, also titled <i><a href="/wiki/Gotta_Get_Thru_This_(album)" title="Gotta Get Thru This (album)">Gotta Get Thru This</a></i>, which was recorded at home using Making Waves Audio software,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> peaked at number two on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> and went on to sell more than 4 million copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.9305:_Second_First_Impression">2004–05: <i>Second First Impression</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004–05: Second First Impression">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Bedingfield won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_BRIT_Awards" title="2004 BRIT Awards">BRIT Award</a> for Best British Male Artist. He then moved to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a> subsidiary label <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a> to release his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Second_First_Impression" title="Second First Impression">Second First Impression</a></i>, which reached number 8 in the UK. The album sold poorly compared to his first offering, though it did include two UK Top 20 singles, "<a href="/wiki/Nothing_Hurts_Like_Love" title="Nothing Hurts Like Love">Nothing Hurts Like Love</a>" (which reached number three) and "<a href="/wiki/Wrap_My_Words_Around_You" title="Wrap My Words Around You">Wrap My Words Around You</a>" (which peaked at number 12). The third and final single, "<a href="/wiki/The_Way_(Daniel_Bedingfield_song)" title="The Way (Daniel Bedingfield song)">The Way</a>", became his first to miss the top 40, reaching number 41. The album was also released in Singapore (featuring 'If You're Not The One' as a bonus track) but not the US.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9311:_Diversifying">2006–11: Diversifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–11: Diversifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In an interview with the <i>New Zealand Herald</i> in April 2007, Bedingfield said that, after a four-year hiatus, he had three albums recorded and awaiting release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10434738_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10434738-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since his last release in 2004, Bedingfield continued to work with other artists. He co-wrote the song "Works for Me!" for <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> finalist <a href="/wiki/David_Archuleta" title="David Archuleta">David Archuleta</a>. He has also worked with <a href="/wiki/Pixie_Lott" title="Pixie Lott">Pixie Lott</a>, and contributed guest vocals on a track by a former member of The Roots. In December 2009, he co-wrote material with <a href="/wiki/Tessanne_Chin" title="Tessanne Chin">Tessanne Chin</a> as well as recorded three songs with <a href="/wiki/Busy_Signal" title="Busy Signal">Busy Signal</a> at DJ Karim's Stainless Records studios in Kingston, Jamaica. Bedingfield provided backing vocals for the Kirsten Morrell track "He Walked In" from the album Ultraviolet,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> which was released in May 2010. In December 2010, Bedingfield spent a month in <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>, Israel, where he wrote and recorded a song with a local freak folk band, <a href="/wiki/The_Raw_Men_Empire" title="The Raw Men Empire">The Raw Men Empire</a>. He concluded his visit with a Tel Aviv gig, hosting Uzi Feinerman, The Raw Men Empire and a variety of other Israeli musicians. In July 2011, he recorded on a dancehall riddim called the Overproof Riddim. Like many of the songs on this riddim, it has been getting good rotation in Jamaica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_.22Rocks_Off.22_and_Stop_the_Traffik_.E2.80.93_Secret_Fear">2012: "Rocks Off" and <i>Stop the Traffik – Secret Fear</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title='Edit section: 2012: "Rocks Off" and Stop the Traffik – Secret Fear'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2012, Bedingfield released the single "Rocks Off" with an accompanying video on MTV which can be <a class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/danielbedingfld" rel="nofollow">viewed here</a>. "Rocks Off" was released alongside B-side "It's Not Me, It's You". On 24 April, Bedingfield released the EP <i><a href="/wiki/Stop_the_Traffik_%E2%80%93_Secret_Fear" title="Stop the Traffik – Secret Fear">Stop the Traffik – Secret Fear</a></i>, his first EP release since becoming independent. The EP featured both the previously released "Rocks Off" and "It's Not Me, Its You" as well as five other new tracks. The EP was followed by the release of Secret Fear as a single, the video features adult scenes and has gone on to win several awards.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313:_The_X_Factor_New_Zealand">2012–13: <i>The X Factor</i> New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012–13: The X Factor New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 December 2012, Bedingfield took up a role as a judge for the first New Zealand series of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_series_1)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand series 1)">The X Factor</a></i> joining <a href="/wiki/Stan_Walker" title="Stan Walker">Stan Walker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melanie_Blatt" title="Melanie Blatt">Melanie Blatt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ruby_Frost" title="Ruby Frost">Ruby Frost</a>. The series premiered on TV3 on 21 April 2013. Bedingfield mentored the Girls category. His sister Natasha appeared as an assistant during the Judges Retreats round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Bedingfield was the winning mentor of series one when his act Jackie Thomas won the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_Songwriting">2013–present: Songwriting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013–present: Songwriting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bedingfield is writing for a number of artists. He co-wrote "I Wanna Feel" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SecondCity" title="SecondCity">SecondCity</a>, which debuted number 1 on the UK Singles Chart on June 1, 2014, and stayed in the top 20 for the next six weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The song was Bedingfield's fourth number 1 in the UK. Bedingfield spent time working in the studio with K-pop group <a href="/wiki/Spica_(band)" title="Spica (band)">Spica</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> including co-producing their first English language single "I Did It". He was also the group's vocal director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2015 he co-wrote the song "Testify" which was included on <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor UK</a></i> <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_11)" title="The X Factor (UK series 11)">2014</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Ben_Haenow" title="Ben Haenow">Ben Haenow</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ben_Haenow_(album)" title="Ben Haenow (album)">self-titled debut studio album</a>.</p>
<p>From February 2016 he will appear in the West End musical <i>The War Of The Worlds</i> as The Artilleryman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The date of the event predicted near this tag has passed. (February 2016)">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bedingfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While on holiday visiting his parents in New Zealand in 2004, Bedingfield nearly lost his life in a serious car accident. As a result, he was left with severe head and neck injuries because of the car rolling on him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>His parents are leadership trainers and mentors in the voluntary sector and business world. His mother, Molly Bedingfield, is founder and CEO of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Angels&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Global Angels (page does not exist)">Global Angels</a>, for which Daniel is doing fundraising work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Bedingfield also helped to launch the <a href="/wiki/Stop_the_Traffik" title="Stop the Traffik">Stop the Traffik</a> coalition against modern day slavery.</p>
