<p><b>Daniel Amartey</b> (born 21 December 1994) is a Ghanaian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana national football team</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> and <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Amartey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Djurg.C3.A5rdens_IF">Djurgårdens IF</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Amartey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Djurgårdens IF">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Amartey started out playing for second tier club <a href="/wiki/International_Allies_F.C." title="International Allies F.C.">International Allies</a> in Ghana where he was spotted as a sixteen-year-old by <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Pehrsson" title="Magnus Pehrsson">Magnus Pehrsson</a> who was on a scouting trip in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> during his preparation to take over as manager of <a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens IF</a>. When Pehrsson became manager he secured the transfer rights for Amartey from the day he turned 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> To help prepare Amartey for his future permanent move to <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> in 2013 the club brought him over for shorter periods of time in both 2011 and 2012 where he got to play for the clubs U21 team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Amartey made his <a href="/wiki/Svenska_Cupen" title="Svenska Cupen">Svenska Cupen</a> debut on 3 March 2013 against <a href="/wiki/Ume%C3%A5_FC" title="Umeå FC">Umeå FC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He then made his league debut in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Allsvenskan" title="2013 Allsvenskan">2013 Allsvenskan</a> opening game away against <a href="/wiki/Helsingborgs_IF" title="Helsingborgs IF">Helsingborgs IF</a> on March 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Amartey was praised by the media for his strong start to the 2013 season, and foreign clubs like <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">FC Schalke 04</a> and <a href="/wiki/1._FC_Kaiserslautern" title="1. FC Kaiserslautern">1. FC Kaiserslautern</a> started to scout him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On May 26 he scored his first ever goal for the club when he headed home the 1–1 equalizer in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Svenska_Cupen_Final" title="2013 Svenska Cupen Final">2013 Svenska Cupen Final</a> which Djurgården eventually lost against <a href="/wiki/IFK_G%C3%B6teborg" title="IFK Göteborg">IFK Göteborg</a> on penalties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After his first season in the Swedish league as an eighteen-year-old Amartey was ranked as the 10th best player in the league by newspaper <a href="/wiki/Expressen" title="Expressen">Expressen</a> and 18th best by <a href="/wiki/Aftonbladet" title="Aftonbladet">Aftonbladet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In November 2013 Amartey confirmed that he was in talks with <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool F.C.</a> over a possible move to the English club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FC_Copenhagen">FC Copenhagen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Amartey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: FC Copenhagen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2014, Amartey moved to <a href="/wiki/F.C._Copenhagen" title="F.C. Copenhagen">F.C. Copenhagen</a> for a fee of €2.5 million plus add ons, and made his <a href="/wiki/Danish_Superliga" title="Danish Superliga">Superliga</a>-debut on 20 July in a match against <a href="/wiki/Silkeborg_IF" title="Silkeborg IF">Silkeborg IF</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leicester_City">Leicester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Amartey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Leicester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 22 January 2016, Amartey joined <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> on a four-and-a-half-year contract for a fee around the region of up to £6m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In his first season in his new country, Amartey featured five times as his team Leicester City won the Premier League title. He made his debut for the club on 27 February 2016, in 1-0 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwich_City" title="Norwich City">Norwich City</a> at the <a href="/wiki/King_Power_Stadium" title="King Power Stadium">King Power Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leicester_City_1-0_Norwich_City_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_City_1-0_Norwich_City-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2016-17 season, Amartey became a first team regular following the departure of <a href="/wiki/N%27Golo_Kant%C3%A9" title="N'Golo Kanté">N'Golo Kanté</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leicester_City.27s_Daniel_Amartey_filling_the_Kante_void_will_take_time_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_City.27s_Daniel_Amartey_filling_the_Kante_void_will_take_time-14">[14]</a></sup> partnering teammate <a href="/wiki/Danny_Drinkwater" title="Danny Drinkwater">Danny Drinkwater</a> in the central midfield role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daniel_Amartey_will_be_the_Leicester_star_to_replace_N.27Golo_Kante.2C_says_Copenhagen_boss_Stale_Solbakken_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daniel_Amartey_will_be_the_Leicester_star_to_replace_N.27Golo_Kante.2C_says_Copenhagen_boss_Stale_Solbakken-15">[15]</a></sup> On 17 December 2016, Amartey scored his first goal for Leicester in a 88th-minute equaliser against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stoke_City" title="Stoke City">Stoke City</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Bet365_Stadium" title="Bet365 Stadium">bet365 Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stoke_City_2-2_Leicester_City_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stoke_City_2-2_Leicester_City-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Amartey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2012 Amartey made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_under-20_football_team" title="Ghana national under-20 football team">Ghana national under-20 football team</a> in a game against <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_under-20_football_team" title="Nigeria national under-20 football team">Nigeria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He was also selected to play in the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a> but Djurgården wanted to keep him in Sweden since the tournament clashed with the <a href="/wiki/2013_Allsvenskan" title="2013 Allsvenskan">2013 Allsvenskan</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In January 2015, Amartey played in all the group matches for Ghana at the 2015 Africa Cup of Nations, where the Black Stars finished as runners-up.</p>
