<p><b>Danielle Rousseau</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i>, which chronicles the lives of over forty people after their plane crashes on a remote island somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">South Pacific</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> actress <a href="/wiki/Mira_Furlan" title="Mira Furlan">Mira Furlan</a> plays the scientist who shipwrecks on the island sixteen years prior to the crash of <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_Airlines" title="Oceanic Airlines">Oceanic</a> Flight 815. After Rousseau is killed in the <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_4)" title="Lost (season 4)">fourth season</a>, the American actress <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Farman" title="Melissa Farman">Melissa Farman</a> portrayed a younger version of the character in the <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_5)" title="Lost (season 5)">fifth season</a>. Furlan later reappears for one episode in the <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_6)" title="Lost (season 6)">sixth season</a>. Rousseau is a recurring on-island character who has appeared in nineteen episodes in seasons one through four, as well as one episode where her voice alone is heard, and her final episode in the <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_6)" title="Lost (season 6)">sixth season</a>.</p>
<p>The character, who is commonly known as "The French Woman" among the survivors on the island, is introduced early in the <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_1)" title="Lost (season 1)">first season</a>. Sixteen years prior to the plane crash, Rousseau was a member of a French research vessel, whose ship ran aground on the island. Two months after being stranded, Rousseau gave birth to her daughter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alex_(Lost)" title="Alex (Lost)">Alex</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tania_Raymonde" title="Tania Raymonde">Tania Raymonde</a>); however, Alex was taken by the island's original inhabitants, a group which Rousseau called the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Others_(Lost)" title="Others (Lost)">Others</a>". Rousseau's primary objective is to reunite with her daughter, which is achieved in the season three finale "<a href="/wiki/Through_the_Looking_Glass_(Lost)" title="Through the Looking Glass (Lost)">Through the Looking Glass</a>". The reunion is short-lived; Rousseau is shot and killed by a band of mercenaries in the middle of the fourth season.</p>
<p>The writers wrote Rousseau out of the series upon request by Furlan, who did not wish to travel to Hawaii to film her scenes, although she later called this an untrue rumor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Geekadelphia_interview_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Geekadelphia_interview-2">[2]</a></sup> The producers planned for Rousseau to get her own flashback episode in season four; however, this was delayed due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%932008_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike">2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike</a>. The writers stated that Rousseau would be in the fifth season, and her story would be told, but to say that it would be told through a flashback would be "disingenuous".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-futurestory_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-futurestory-3">[3]</a></sup> In the fifth season of <i>Lost</i>, a younger version of Rousseau is shown. Critical reaction to the character has been generally positive; however, many have expressed disappointment in the execution of Rousseau's death scene.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arc">Arc</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Arc">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prior_to_the_crash">Prior to the crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prior to the crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sixteen years before Oceanic 815 crashes on the island, Rousseau was a heavily pregnant member of a six-person crew aboard a French research vessel three days out of <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a>. The crew picked up a radio signal emanating from the South Pacific, which was a repeating set of <a href="/wiki/Mythology_of_Lost#The_Numbers" title="Mythology of Lost">six numbers</a>: 4, 8, 15, 16, 23 and 42. While attempting to find the source of the signal, the ship ran aground on rocks surrounding the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solitary_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Solitary-4">[4]</a></sup> Before reaching the shore, Rousseau and her team found Oceanic 815 survivor <a href="/wiki/Jin-Soo_Kwon" title="Jin-Soo Kwon">Jin-Soo Kwon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Daniel_Dae_Kim" title="Daniel Dae Kim">Daniel Dae Kim</a>) in the water, who had been time-travelling as a result of the time shifts which had been moving him and the flight survivors through the island's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Little_Prince_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Little_Prince-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rousseau later tells the survivors of Oceanic 815 that two months after their arrival, that the island natives, which she called the Others, carried a disease that the rest of her crew had contracted. She believed that this disease could not be allowed to reach the rest of the world, so she killed her colleagues, including her husband Robert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exodus:_Part_2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Exodus:_Part_2-6">[6]</a></sup> When Jin time-travels to the past, he witnesses a different version of Rousseau's past. It is shown that the Smoke Monster, not the Others, was responsible for the "sickness" that forced her to kill her crew. Rousseau confronts the father of her child, Robert, at gunpoint and accuses him of "being changed" inside the temple. Robert denies this, explaining that the Monster is a security system for the temple, and convinces Rousseau to lower her weapon. He attempts to shoot her when she does, but is shot by Rousseau. Rousseau also attempts to kill Jin, having witnessed him vanish moments after her team entered the Smoke Monster's lair, believing he has the disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-This_Place_is_Death_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-This_Place_is_Death-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rousseau hiked to the radio tower and changed the message from the numbers to a repeating distress call. Three days later, Rousseau gave birth to a daughter, Alexandra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Through_the_Looking_Glass_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Through_the_Looking_Glass-8">[8]</a></sup> One week after Alex's birth, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benjamin_Linus" title="Benjamin Linus">Benjamin Linus</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Emerson" title="Michael Emerson">Michael Emerson</a>) came and took Alex from Rousseau. Ben told Rousseau that the child would be safe with them, and that she was lucky for being left alive. Before leaving, Ben tells her that if she hears whispers, she should run the other way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-This_Place_is_Death_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-This_Place_is_Death-7">[7]</a></sup> After this, Rousseau began setting traps in the jungle with the hope of catching one of the people responsible for Alex's kidnapping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exodus:_Part_2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Exodus:_Part_2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_crash">After the crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After the crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rousseau makes her first appearance in the season one episode "<a href="/wiki/Solitary_(Lost)" title="Solitary (Lost)">Solitary</a>", where she captures <a href="/wiki/Sayid_Jarrah" title="Sayid Jarrah">Sayid</a> (<a href="/wiki/Naveen_Andrews" title="Naveen Andrews">Naveen Andrews</a>), one of the plane crash survivors. Rousseau tortures him until he eventually convinces her that he is not one of the Others. She allows Sayid to return to his camp, and cautions him to keep a close eye on the other survivors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solitary_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Solitary-4">[4]</a></sup> Over two weeks later, she comes across a delirious <a href="/wiki/Claire_Littleton" title="Claire Littleton">Claire</a> (<a href="/wiki/Emilie_de_Ravin" title="Emilie de Ravin">Emilie de Ravin</a>), who had been left in the jungle after being kidnapped by the Others. Noticing that Claire's shouting is alerting the Others to her presence, Rousseau knocks her unconscious and carries her back to the other survivors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maternity_Leave_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maternity_Leave-9">[9]</a></sup> Rousseau makes her next appearance in "<a href="/wiki/Numbers_(Lost)" title="Numbers (Lost)">Numbers</a>", where she shoots at <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Pace" title="Charlie Pace">Charlie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominic_Monaghan" title="Dominic Monaghan">Dominic Monaghan</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Hugo_%22Hurley%22_Reyes" title='Hugo "Hurley" Reyes'>Hurley</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jorge_Garcia" title="Jorge Garcia">Jorge Garcia</a>), suspecting them of being Others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Numbers_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Numbers-10">[10]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Exodus_(Lost)" title="Exodus (Lost)">season one finale</a>, set 43 days after the plane crash, Rousseau arrives on the beach and warns everyone that the Others are coming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exodus:_Part_1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Exodus:_Part_1-11">[11]</a></sup> After Claire gives birth to her son Aaron, Rousseau steals him and intends to swap him for her own child. When the Others do not come, Rousseau reluctantly returns him to Sayid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exodus:_Part_2_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Exodus:_Part_2-6">[6]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_2)" title="Lost (season 2)">season two</a>, set two weeks later, Rousseau informs Sayid that she has caught one of the Others, who later turns out to be Ben.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-One_of_Them_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-One_of_Them-12">[12]</a></sup> Later, Rousseau joins Claire and <a href="/wiki/Kate_Austen" title="Kate Austen">Kate Austen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Lilly" title="Evangeline Lilly">Evangeline Lilly</a>) who are searching for medicine for Claire's baby, in one of the Others' work stations. Disappointed that she has not found any trace of Alex, she begins to leave them when Claire tells her that she believes Alex helped her escape when the Others captured her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maternity_Leave_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Maternity_Leave-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Midway through <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_3)" title="Lost (season 3)">season three</a>, Kate persuades Rousseau to help some of the survivors find the Others' home, by informing her that Alex helped her escape from the Others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tricia_Tanaka_is_Dead_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tricia_Tanaka_is_Dead-13">[13]</a></sup> When they arrive, Rousseau abandons the group and watches from the bushes as Alex walks past.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Man_from_Tallahassee_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Man_from_Tallahassee-14">[14]</a></sup> A few days later, Rousseau leads the survivors to the radio tower in order for them to contact a nearby freighter. En route, they encounter Ben and Alex; Ben explains to.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Through_the_Looking_Glass_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Through_the_Looking_Glass-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_4)" title="Lost (season 4)">Season four</a> begins with the survivors dividing into two groups, with those who believe the people from the freighter to be dangerous, including Rousseau and Alex, joining <a href="/wiki/John_Locke_(Lost)" title="John Locke (Lost)">Locke</a> (<a href="/wiki/Terry_O%27Quinn" title="Terry O'Quinn">Terry O'Quinn</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Beginning_of_the_End_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Beginning_of_the_End-15">[15]</a></sup> After learning of the freighter crew's intentions to kill everyone on the island, Ben directs Rousseau, along with Alex and her boyfriend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#The_Others" title="Characters of Lost">Karl</a> (<a href="/wiki/Blake_Bashoff" title="Blake Bashoff">Blake Bashoff</a>), to head to the Temple where the rest of the Others are located. On their way, Rousseau and Karl are shot by unseen attackers, and both are killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_Kevin_Johnson_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meet_Kevin_Johnson-16">[16]</a></sup> The buried bodies of both are discovered by <a href="/wiki/Miles_Straume" title="Miles Straume">Miles</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ken_Leung" title="Ken Leung">Ken Leung</a>), <a href="/wiki/James_%22Sawyer%22_Ford" title='James "Sawyer" Ford'>Sawyer</a> (<a href="/wiki/Josh_Holloway" title="Josh Holloway">Josh Holloway</a>), and Claire after they leave Locke and travel back to the beach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Something_Nice_Back_Home_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Something_Nice_Back_Home-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flash-Sideways">Flash-Sideways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flash-Sideways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She's still the mother of Alex and lives with her. Alex introduces her mother to Dr. Linus because he's too hurt after being battered by Desmond. Danielle and Alex bring him home for dinner. While Alex is doing her homework, Danielle and Dr. Linus are talking about her. She tells him that since the death of her father when she was two, he's the closest to a father figure that she ever got. Dr. Linus is visibly touched.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mira3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="721" data-file-width="600" height="264" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Mira3.jpg/220px-Mira3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Mira3.jpg/330px-Mira3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Mira3.jpg/440px-Mira3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mira3.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Furlan in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, 2006</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Rousseau is named after French-Swiss philosopher <a href="/wiki/Jean-Jacques_Rousseau" title="Jean-Jacques Rousseau">Jean-Jacques Rousseau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-philosopher_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-philosopher-18">[18]</a></sup> Although Rousseau is French, actress Mira Furlan is Croatian, which led to speculation that Rousseau is not really from France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In one of the official <i>Lost</i> podcasts, producer <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cuse" title="Carlton Cuse">Carlton Cuse</a> joked, "any French woman who speaks with a Croatian accent and has been living in the woods for sixteen years eating bugs ... has got some issues, and those issues need to be overcome before she can actually go and embrace Alex".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastMar07_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastMar07-20">[20]</a></sup> Furlan understood Rousseau's decision to not meet her daughter, believing that no one can ever be prepared for that moment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-21">[21]</a></sup> Furlan met Raymonde for the first time just minutes before shooting their reunion scene in the season three finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-21">[21]</a></sup> The actors believed the scene to be a "huge moment" for their characters, which Furlan described as a "basic human moment."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-21">[21]</a></sup> Furlan was glad to have "such a beautiful partner," and noted their physical resemblance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Furlan is said to have asked for her character to be written out of the series because she did not want to travel to Hawaii to film her scenes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gloria_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gloria-22">[22]</a></sup> although she later called this an untrue rumor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Geekadelphia_interview_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Geekadelphia_interview-2">[2]</a></sup> The producers decided for Rousseau to be shot, with the possibility of her returning in <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_5)" title="Lost (season 5)">season five</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gloria_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gloria-22">[22]</a></sup> Co-show runners and executive producers <a href="/wiki/Damon_Lindelof" title="Damon Lindelof">Damon Lindelof</a> and Cuse originally intended for Rousseau to get her own flashback episode in season four,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> however as a result of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%932008_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike">Writer's Strike</a>, this did not come to pass. When asked whether Rousseau would have a flashback in season five, Lindelof said that Rousseau's story would "definitively" be revealed, but that "to use the word flashback would be disingenuous". Cuse revealed that they were trying to steer away from the word flashback and were "embracing a whole new word". He explained that "there will still [...] be flashbacks and flash-forwards on the show, but we're going to do something different this year. We're going to mix it up."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-futurestory_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-futurestory-3">[3]</a></sup> Instead of using flashbacks and flashforwards like in previous seasons, stories of the fifth season will play out both on and off the island stories in different points in time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comic_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-comic-24">[24]</a></sup> The writers were supposedly scheduling an appearance by Furlan for the fifth season, but Furlan did not end up making an appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> However, she returns in the form of Rousseau's "sideways" counterpart (who never crashed on the Island, lost Alex or went insane) in the sixth season.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a review of the first season episode "<a href="/wiki/Solitary_(Lost)" title="Solitary (Lost)">Solitary</a>", Chris Carabott of <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> commented that Rousseau appeared to be more emotionally fragile than in later seasons, and was like a "loose cannon".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignrosscar_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignrosscar-26">[26]</a></sup> Carabott found that as the series progressed and the mystery around the character was lessened, Rousseau was unable to deliver the same impact she made in her first appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignrosscar_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-ignrosscar-26">[26]</a></sup> In his review of "<a href="/wiki/Numbers_(Lost)" title="Numbers (Lost)">Numbers</a>", in which Rousseau makes her second appearance, Carabott described Hurley and Rousseau's encounter as "the episode's best moment", as "Hurley finds comfort with someone who we would least expect."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <i>Lost</i> co-creator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.J._Abrams" title="J.J. Abrams">J.J. Abrams</a> commended Furlan for giving the character "heart and soul", and managed to make her "identifiable and complex".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_21-4"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rousseau's unknown fate in "Meet Kevin Johnson" was heavily discussed among critics. Alan Sepinwall of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Star-Ledger" title="The Star-Ledger">The Star-Ledger</a></i> felt that the relationship between Rousseau and Alex after their reunion remained unexplored, which made Rousseau's death a wasted opportunity for a powerful death scene.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BuddyTV" title="BuddyTV">BuddyTV</a>'s Don Williams believed that Rousseau would survive, mainly because he had been waiting to see her flashback for four seasons, and thought her death would ensure it would not happen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Dan Compora from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SyFy_Portal" title="SyFy Portal">SyFy Portal</a> was disappointed in her death scene, noting, "I wasn't shocked to see Danielle get killed, but the scene felt like it was tossed in just in case this had to serve as the season finale." Compora felt that Rousseau deserved more screen time in the episode leading up to her death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a></i>'s Tim Goodman described it as "a rude and lazy way to get rid of Rousseau".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> John Kubicek, also of BuddyTV, found her death scene unsatisfactory, commenting, "Then we have that entirely tacked on scene at the end which felt so artificial, like I accidentally flipped over to a different show. While I agree Danielle Rousseau being shot with an arrow is shocking, is this really what the show has come to? Arbitrarily putting a recurring character's life in peril to pose as a cliffhanger?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
