<p><b>Danielle Nicole Panabaker</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVGuide-bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVGuide-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> (born September 19, 1987<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVGuide-bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TVGuide-bio-1">[1]</a></sup>) is an American actress. She began acting as a teenager and first came to prominence for her roles in the <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> films <i><a href="/wiki/Stuck_in_the_Suburbs" title="Stuck in the Suburbs">Stuck in the Suburbs</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Sky_High_(2005_film)" title="Sky High (2005 film)">Sky High</a></i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/Read_It_and_Weep" title="Read It and Weep">Read It and Weep</a></i> (2006), the latter alongside her younger sister <a href="/wiki/Kay_Panabaker" title="Kay Panabaker">Kay Panabaker</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_Falls_(miniseries)" title="Empire Falls (miniseries)">Empire Falls</a></i> (2005).</p>
<p>She came to wider attention as a cast member alongside <a href="/wiki/James_Woods" title="James Woods">James Woods</a> in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> legal drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Shark_(TV_series)" title="Shark (TV series)">Shark</a></i> (2006–08) and is also noted as a <a href="/wiki/Scream_queen" title="Scream queen">scream queen</a>, having starred in the psychological thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Brooks" title="Mr. Brooks">Mr. Brooks</a></i> (2007) and the horror films <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_the_13th_(2009_film)" title="Friday the 13th (2009 film)">Friday the 13th</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Crazies_(2010_film)" title="The Crazies (2010 film)">The Crazies</a></i> (2010), <a href="/wiki/John_Carpenter" title="John Carpenter">John Carpenter</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ward_(film)" title="The Ward (film)">The Ward</a></i> (2010), and <i><a href="/wiki/Piranha_3DD" title="Piranha 3DD">Piranha 3DD</a></i> (2012).</p>
<p>After recurring roles on the drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Necessary_Roughness_(TV_series)" title="Necessary Roughness (TV series)">Necessary Roughness</a></i> (2011–13), the crime series <i><a href="/wiki/Bones_(TV_series)" title="Bones (TV series)">Bones</a></i> (2012–13) and the crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Justified_(TV_series)" title="Justified (TV series)">Justified</a></i> (2014), Panabaker has guest-starred as Caitlin Snow on <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Arrow_(TV_series)" title="Arrow (TV series)">Arrow</a></i> since April 2014. She is currently reprising the role of <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_Flash_characters#Caitlin_Snow" title="List of The Flash characters">Caitlin Snow/Killer Frost</a> as a main character in the spin-off series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flash_(2014_TV_series)" title="The Flash (2014 TV series)">The Flash</a></i>, which premiered in October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Panabaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Panabaker was born in <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Georgia" title="Augusta, Georgia">Augusta, Georgia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> to Donna (née Mayock) and Harold Panabaker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Her younger sister <a href="/wiki/Kay_Panabaker" title="Kay Panabaker">Kay</a> was also an actress, and is currently working as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zoologist" title="Zoologist">zoologist</a>. As her father's sales job took them across the country, the family spent time in <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, and for a short time around Panabaker's <a href="/wiki/Kindergarten" title="Kindergarten">kindergarten</a> year, in <a href="/wiki/Orange,_Texas" title="Orange, Texas">Orange, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superstar_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-superstar-6">[6]</a></sup> She took a theater class at a summer camp, discovered her love of acting, and started acting in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Community_theater" title="Community theater">community theaters</a> at the age of 12, later auditioning for commercials.</p>
<p>After moving to <a href="/wiki/Naperville,_Illinois" title="Naperville, Illinois">Naperville, Illinois</a> in 2000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superstar_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-superstar-6">[6]</a></sup> Panabaker went to <a href="/wiki/Indian_Prairie_School_District_204" title="Indian Prairie School District 204">Crone Middle School</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Neuqua_Valley_High_School" title="Neuqua Valley High School">Neuqua Valley High School</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superstar_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-superstar-6">[6]</a></sup> participating in the <a href="/wiki/Individual_events_(speech)" title="Individual events (speech)">speech</a> team. She graduated from high school when she was only 14 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superstar_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-superstar-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shockya_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-shockya-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2003, feeling it was the only way to land acting roles regularly, Panabaker, her sister, and their mother relocated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>, so she could pursue an acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superstar_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-superstar-6">[6]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Glendale_Community_College_(California)" title="Glendale Community College (California)">Glendale Community College</a>, studying acting. In 2005, she earned her <a href="/wiki/Associate_degree" title="Associate degree">associate degree</a> and appeared on the national <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dean%27s_List" title="Dean's List">Dean's List</a>. In the fall of 2006, she began her senior year at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Los_Angeles" title="University of California, Los Angeles">University of California, Los Angeles</a>, from which she graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> in June 2007, again appearing on the Dean's List.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shockya_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-shockya-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Panabaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Panabaker followed her initial appearances in commercials with roles in television, including a part in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian_(TV_series)" title="The Guardian (TV series)">The Guardian</a></i> (for which she won a <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a>), as well as other <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television series</a> including <i><a href="/wiki/Malcolm_in_the_Middle" title="Malcolm in the Middle">Malcolm in the Middle</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Medium_(TV_series)" title="Medium (TV series)">Medium</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Summerland_(TV_series)" title="Summerland (TV series)">Summerland</a></i>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Disney_Channel_Original_Movies" title="List of Disney Channel Original Movies">Disney Channel Original Movie</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Stuck_in_the_Suburbs" title="Stuck in the Suburbs">Stuck in the Suburbs</a></i>. She also appeared in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifetime_Television" title="Lifetime Television">Lifetime Television</a> productions <i>Sex and the Single Mom</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mom_at_Sixteen" title="Mom at Sixteen">Mom at Sixteen</a></i>, as well as the well-reviewed <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_Falls_(miniseries)" title="Empire Falls (miniseries)">Empire Falls</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superstar_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-superstar-6">[6]</a></sup> She considers <i>Empire Falls</i> to be her big break, as it gave her the confidence to pursue her career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superstar_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-superstar-6">[6]</a></sup> In addition, Panabaker appeared in stage productions with roles in <a href="/wiki/Musical_theatre" title="Musical theatre">musical theater</a>, including <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Side_Story_(musical)" title="West Side Story (musical)">West Side Story</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pippin_(musical)" title="Pippin (musical)">Pippin</a></i>, <i>Once Upon A Time</i>, and <i>Beauty Lou and the Country Beast</i>. In 2004, she starred in the ABC film "Searching for David's Heart", alongside co-former Disney star Ricky Ullman. In 2005, Panabaker co-starred in two widely released theatrical films, <i><a href="/wiki/Sky_High_(2005_film)" title="Sky High (2005 film)">Sky High</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yours,_Mine_and_Ours_(2005_film)" title="Yours, Mine and Ours (2005 film)">Yours, Mine and Ours</a></i>. Her next role was in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Home_of_the_Giants" title="Home of the Giants">Home of the Giants</a></i> (2008), opposite <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Merriman" title="Ryan Merriman">Ryan Merriman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haley_Joel_Osment" title="Haley Joel Osment">Haley Joel Osment</a>. She also had a supporting role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Brooks" title="Mr. Brooks">Mr. Brooks</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Costner" title="Kevin Costner">Kevin Costner</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> original movie <i><a href="/wiki/Read_It_and_Weep" title="Read It and Weep">Read It and Weep</a></i>, she plays "Is", an alternate version of Jamie, who was played by her real-life sister Kay.</p>
<p>Panabaker was added to the cast of the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> television drama <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Shark_(TV_series)" title="Shark (TV series)">Shark</a></i>, as Julie Stark, the main character's daughter. She had a regular role in the series, which ran for two years. Although she sometimes only appeared in a scene or two an episode, and was absent from three episodes in the series run, she was billed second to the star, <a href="/wiki/James_Woods" title="James Woods">James Woods</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Panabaker played <a href="/wiki/List_of_Friday_the_13th_characters#Jenna" title="List of Friday the 13th characters">Jenna</a>, a main character of <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_the_13th_(2009_film)" title="Friday the 13th (2009 film)">Friday the 13th</a></i> (2009) along with <a href="/wiki/Jared_Padalecki" title="Jared Padalecki">Jared Padalecki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Panabaker next starred in the films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Crazies_(2010_film)" title="The Crazies (2010 film)">The Crazies</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ward_(film)" title="The Ward (film)">The Ward</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Panabaker starred as Katie Lapp, the lead character in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Hallmark_Channel_Original_Movies" title="List of Hallmark Channel Original Movies">Hallmark Movie</a> <i>The Shunning</i>, based on the novel of the same name by <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Lewis" title="Beverly Lewis">Beverly Lewis</a>. She was due to reprise her role in the 2013 sequel <i>The Confession</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> but a scheduling conflict forced her to drop out. The role was recast, going to <a href="/wiki/Katie_Leclerc" title="Katie Leclerc">Katie Leclerc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, she starred in another Hallmark Channel Original Movie entitled <i>Nearlyweds</i>. On May 5, 2013, Panabaker joined actors including <a href="/wiki/Philip_Baker_Hall" title="Philip Baker Hall">Philip Baker Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Pullman" title="Bill Pullman">Bill Pullman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Siff" title="Maggie Siff">Maggie Siff</a> in performing at Cedering Fox's WordTheatre, where they read aloud contemporary short fiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Panabaker starred in director <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bradley_D._King" title="Bradley D. King">Bradley D. King</a>'s award-winning science-fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Lapse_(film)" title="Time Lapse (film)">Time Lapse</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> for which she won the award for Best Actor/Actress at the 2014 London Independent Film Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Panabaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Panabaker volunteers for multiple organizations including The Art of Elysium, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unicef" title="Unicef">Unicef</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Young_Storytellers_Foundation" title="Young Storytellers Foundation">Young Storytellers Foundation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In July 2016, Panabaker announced that she is engaged to her longtime boyfriend Hayes Robbins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
