<p><b>Danielle Amy Louise Harold</b> (born 30 May 1992)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English actress, best known for her role as teenager <a href="/wiki/Lola_Pearce" title="Lola Pearce">Lola Pearce</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Harold&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before Harold's acting career began she was featured on the 2011 television series <i><a href="/wiki/Jamie%27s_Dream_School" title="Jamie's Dream School">Jamie's Dream School</a></i>. Harold got one GCSE as she had lost a year of school due to an illness and missed out on being able to study for GCSEs. On Jamie's Dream School, she was seen as one of the brightest of the students and was rewarded by <a href="/wiki/Jane_Poynter" title="Jane Poynter">Jane Poynter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Winston" title="Robert Winston">Robert Winston</a> with the chance to stay in the <a href="/wiki/Biosphere" title="Biosphere">biosphere</a> which Poynter had lived in for two years. She was also given a tour of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cambridge_University" title="Cambridge University">Cambridge University</a> by <a href="/wiki/David_Starkey" title="David Starkey">David Starkey</a> as he found her to be very intelligent and thought that it might be a good choice of university. However, she decided to go to acting school and auditioned for many soap operas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EastEnders"><i>EastEnders</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danielle_Harold&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: EastEnders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i> producers announced that Harold had been cast as <a href="/wiki/Lola_Pearce" title="Lola Pearce">Lola Pearce</a>, the granddaughter of established characters <a href="/wiki/Billy_Mitchell_(EastEnders)" title="Billy Mitchell (EastEnders)">Billy Mitchell</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julie_Perkins" title="Julie Perkins">Julie Perkins</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-3">[3]</a></sup> She made her first appearance in <i>EastEnders</i> on 12 July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Harold's first major storyline involved her character's pregnancy at the age of 16. In July 2012, the character gave birth in a special live broadcast, coinciding with her screen grandfather carrying the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Torch" title="Olympic Torch">Olympic Torch</a> through <a href="/wiki/Walford" title="Walford">Walford</a>. Harold is the first <i>EastEnders</i> actress to portray giving birth live on air, and the second British soap actress, after <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Jennie_McAlpine" title="Jennie McAlpine">Jennie McAlpine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015 it was announced that Harold would be leaving <i>EastEnders</i> later that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leaving_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-leaving-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
