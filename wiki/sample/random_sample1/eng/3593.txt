<p><b>Frederick "Fred" Douglas Rosser III</b> (born November 2, 1983),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Ring_name" title="Ring name">ring name</a> <b>Darren Young</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a> signed to <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> on the <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i> brand, but is currently on hiatus due to injury. He is a former one-time <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Raw Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Champion</a>.</p>
<p>Prior to signing with WWE, Rosser competed in Northeastern and Mid-Atlantic independent promotions including <a href="/wiki/Chaotic_Wrestling" title="Chaotic Wrestling">Chaotic Wrestling</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Wrestling_Association" title="East Coast Wrestling Association">East Coast Wrestling Association</a>, Independent Wrestling Federation and the <a href="/wiki/National_Wrestling_Alliance" title="National Wrestling Alliance">National Wrestling Alliance</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rosser was born in <a href="/wiki/Union_Township,_Union_County,_New_Jersey" title="Union Township, Union County, New Jersey">Union Township, New Jersey</a>. He began watching professional wrestling during the mid-1980s and became interested in becoming a professional wrestler as a teenager, wrestling in several <a href="/wiki/Backyard_wrestling" title="Backyard wrestling">backyard wrestling</a> federations. He attended <a href="/wiki/Union_High_School_(New_Jersey)" title="Union High School (New Jersey)">Union High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, where he played <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a>, both offensively and defensively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Varsallone-pg1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Varsallone-pg1-7">[7]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Fairleigh_Dickinson_University" title="Fairleigh Dickinson University">Fairleigh Dickinson University</a>, where he played football for a year before deciding to focus on his academic studies and professional wrestling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Varsallone-pg1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Varsallone-pg1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Training_and_independent_circuit_.282002.E2.80.932004.29">Training and independent circuit (2002â2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Training and independent circuit (2002â2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rosser became interested in becoming a professional wrestler as a teenager, wrestling in several backyard wrestling federations. He researched a number of wrestling schools before deciding on Camp IWF in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Paterson,_New_Jersey" title="West Paterson, New Jersey">West Paterson</a>, New Jersey. This was due in part to his employer, whose uncle was a friend of a local wrestler training there at the time, and helped him enroll in the wrestling school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECWAInterview_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ECWAInterview-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rosser made his professional debut in 2002 and spent several years on the independent circuit, including wrestling for the Independent Wrestling Federation and <a href="/wiki/Chaotic_Wrestling" title="Chaotic Wrestling">Chaotic Wrestling</a> during his early career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On May 17, 2003, he won his first major title when he defeated the IWF Heavyweight Championship in Woodland Park, New Jersey and held it for over half a year until losing to Roman on January 17, 2004. It was at a house show in Chaotic Wrestling that he was first noticed by promoter Jim Kettner and invited to compete for the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Wrestling_Association" title="East Coast Wrestling Association">East Coast Wrestling Association</a> where he made his debut in the summer of 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Shortly after entering the promotion, Rosser began feuding with <a href="/wiki/Prince_Nana" title="Prince Nana">Prince Nana</a> over the <a href="/wiki/ECWA_Mid_Atlantic_Championship" title="ECWA Mid Atlantic Championship">ECWA Mid Atlantic Championship</a> and eventually defeated him for the title<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECWAInterview_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-ECWAInterview-8">[8]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_Delaware" title="Newark, Delaware">Newark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> on September 18, 2004. He lost the title to Nick Malakai less than two months later.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Entertainment.2FWWE">World Wrestling Entertainment/WWE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Entertainment/WWE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_appearances_and_Florida_Championship_Wrestling_.282005.E2.80.932009.29">Early appearances and Florida Championship Wrestling (2005â2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Early appearances and Florida Championship Wrestling (2005â2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During 2005 and 2006, Rosser made several appearances for World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) on its weekly programs <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Heat" title="WWE Heat">Sunday Night Heat</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Velocity" title="WWE Velocity">Velocity</a></i> as well as in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dark_match" title="Dark match">dark matches</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Combat-Hooligans_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Combat-Hooligans-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WrestleView_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-WrestleView-12">[12]</a></sup> In May 2009, Rosser signed a WWE developmental contract and was sent to WWE's <a href="/wiki/Farm_team#Professional_wrestling" title="Farm team">developmental territory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida_Championship_Wrestling" title="Florida Championship Wrestling">Florida Championship Wrestling</a>, under the name <b>Darren Young</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Varsallone-pg2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Varsallone-pg2-13">[13]</a></sup> In FCW, Young was in a tag team called The South Beach Party Boys with <a href="/wiki/Percy_Watson" title="Percy Watson">Percy Watson</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Nexus_.282010.E2.80.932011.29">The Nexus (2010â2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Nexus (2010â2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Nexus_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Nexus (professional wrestling)">The Nexus</a></div>
<p>On February 16, 2010, it was announced that he would compete as part of the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT_(TV_series)" title="WWE NXT (TV series)">WWE NXT</a></i>, mentored by <a href="/wiki/CM_Punk" title="CM Punk">CM Punk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 23, 2010, Young made his debut on <i>NXT</i> with a character described as a "South Beach Party Boy", losing his debut match to <a href="/wiki/David_Otunga" title="David Otunga">David Otunga</a>. His storyline had mentor CM Punk showing apathy towards him, claiming he had no idea why he's in <i>NXT</i> and did not want to train Young unless he accepted his Straight-edge lifestyle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NXT022310_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-NXT022310-15">[15]</a></sup> Despite this, Punk and his disciples, <a href="/wiki/Luke_Gallows" title="Luke Gallows">Luke Gallows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Serena_Deeb" title="Serena Deeb">Serena</a>, helped Young win his rematch against Otunga the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Torch03022010_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Torch03022010-16">[16]</a></sup> Young was ranked eight in the first Pros' Poll. After the pros' poll, Young decided to join <a href="/wiki/The_Straight_Edge_Society" title="The Straight Edge Society">The Straight Edge Society</a> in an attempt to improve, only to change his mind before getting his hair shaved off. CM Punk decided to forgive him for standing up to him when he defeated Luke Gallows with Young's own hair on the line. Because of this victory, CM Punk had then been interested in Young's potential, but in turn started feuding with a jealous Luke Gallows. On May 11 in the second Pros poll, Young was ranked fifth out of the six remaining contestants and narrowly escaped elimination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The following week, however, Young was eliminated from the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>The week following the Season 1 finale, Young, along with the other season one <i>NXT</i> rookies turned <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heel</a> and interfered in the main event match on <i>Raw</i> between <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a> and Young's former Pro, CM Punk and attacked both wrestlers, the announce team and the <a href="/wiki/Ring_announcer" title="Ring announcer">ring announcer</a> <a href="/wiki/Justin_Roberts" title="Justin Roberts">Justin Roberts</a>, and dismantled the ring area and surrounding equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On the June 14, 2010 edition of <i>Raw,</i> the rookies attacked General Manager <a href="/wiki/Bret_Hart" title="Bret Hart">Bret Hart</a>, when he refused to give them contracts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The following week on <i>Raw,</i> <a href="/wiki/Vince_McMahon" title="Vince McMahon">Vince McMahon</a> fired Hart and announced that a new General Manager had been hired, one that had signed all seven season one NXT rookies to contracts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The following week, the group was named <a href="/wiki/The_Nexus_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Nexus (professional wrestling)">The Nexus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On the July 5 episode of <i>Raw</i>, The Nexus tried to attack John Cena as he confronted <a href="/wiki/Wade_Barrett" title="Wade Barrett">Wade Barrett</a> during their "truce" with one another. The segment ended with The Nexus leaving without Young, when other Raw wrestlers arrived to aid Cena, and Young was assaulted by Cena to end the show. Due to Cena's actions, the new general manager booked Cena in a 7 on 1 handicap match against The Nexus scheduled for the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Young did not participate due to injuries caused by Cena the previous week but The Nexus won the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He returned at <a href="/wiki/Money_in_the_Bank_(2010)" title="Money in the Bank (2010)">Money in the Bank</a> with the rest of The Nexus, sporting a new haircut and costing Cena the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Championship" title="WWE World Championship">WWE Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Sheamus" title="Sheamus">Sheamus</a>. After the match, Cena attacked Young and fellow Nexus teammate, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Tarver" title="Michael Tarver">Michael Tarver</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The Nexus continued to feud with Cena and the Raw roster, resulting in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Elimination_tag_team_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">seven-on-seven elimination tag team match</a> at <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam_(2010)" title="SummerSlam (2010)">SummerSlam</a>. Young was the first person to be eliminated from the match, when he submitted to <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bryan" title="Daniel Bryan">Daniel Bryan</a> in under a minute, and The Nexus lost the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On the next episode of <i>Raw</i> the members of The Nexus fought in individual matches against members of the victorious Team WWE with the stipulation that losing a match would have them exiled from the group. Young chose to face John Cena, but failed to win and was exiled and attacked as a result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Young returned on the September 6 episode of <i>Raw</i>, distracting Barrett and costing him a match against <a href="/wiki/Randy_Orton" title="Randy Orton">Randy Orton</a> thus turned Young <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">face</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On the October 4 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Young participated in a <a href="/wiki/Battle_royal_(professional_wrestling)" title="Battle royal (professional wrestling)">battle royal</a> to determine the number one contender to the WWE Championship, but was eliminated by The Nexus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Young had his first singles match since leaving The Nexus on the October 7 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Superstars" title="WWE Superstars">Superstars</a></i>, losing to <a href="/wiki/William_Regal" title="William Regal">William Regal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Young had his first win in singles competition on the October 14 airing of <i>Superstars</i> defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Col%C3%B3n" title="Eddie ColÃ³n">Primo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Young teamed up with Tatsu in a losing effort against <a href="/wiki/The_Usos" title="The Usos">The Usos</a> in mid November on two occasions. Young got his first victory of 2011 when he teamed with <a href="/wiki/Primo_(wrestler)" title="Primo (wrestler)">Primo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoshi_Tatsu" title="Yoshi Tatsu">Yoshi Tatsu</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Hart_Smith" title="David Hart Smith">David Hart Smith</a> to defeat <a href="/wiki/Zack_Ryder" title="Zack Ryder">Zack Ryder</a>, William Regal and The Usos on the January 13 episode of <i>Superstars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_NXT_.282011.E2.80.932012.29">Return to NXT (2011â2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to NXT (2011â2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In March 2011, Young was selected as one of six former <i>NXT</i> contestants to return to the show in its fifth season, <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT_(TV_series)#NXT_Redemption_.28season_5.29" title="WWE NXT (TV series)">NXT Redemption</a></i>. During this season, Young was mentored by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chavo_Guerrero,_Jr." title="Chavo Guerrero, Jr.">Chavo Guerrero</a>. In the season premiere, Young defeated Jacob Novak and won the first challenge to earn himself "redemption points".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On the April 12 episode of <i>NXT</i>, Young attacked <a href="/wiki/Titus_O%27Neil" title="Titus O'Neil">Titus O'Neil</a> after their match and tried to attack O'Neil's pro Hornswoggle, but was stopped by Guerrero.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He turned heel, when he attacked <a href="/wiki/Hornswoggle" title="Hornswoggle">Hornswoggle</a>, and later he and Guerrero faced O'Neil and different tag team partners on several occasions. On the April 19 edition of Redemption, Young faced Hornswoggle in single match. During their match, Chavo Guerrero gave Young a blindfold but In the end Hornswoggle won after a Tadpole splash. On the May 3 edition of Redemption, Young lost to his Pros Chavo Guerrero with Hornswoggle as a special guest referee. On the May 10 edition of Redemption, Hornswoggle didn't appear on NXT and accused by Hornswoggle Rookie's Titus O'Neil. The following week, It was revealed Chavo Guerrero kidnapped Hornswoggle and hid Hornswoggle in the box. Then, Guerrero and Young was attack Hornswoggle in the ring until O'Neil made a save. The feud was settled when Titus O'Neil and Darren Young faced each other in a No Disqualitication Match on the May 24 episode of NXT Redemption. The match ended with Titus O'Neil a victor after Hornswoggle interfered in a match which O'Neil attack Guerrero and Young. Guerrero was later released from WWE, leaving Young on his own. In August he formed an alliance with <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Carter_III" title="Ethan Carter III">Derrick Bateman</a> and the duo teamed up against O'Neil and <i>NXT</i> host <a href="/wiki/Matt_Striker" title="Matt Striker">Matt Striker</a>, but were defeated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Young then moved into a feud with Striker, defeating him on two consecutive occasions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> After Young attacked Striker following a handshake, William Regal intervened, saving Striker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Two weeks later, Young was defeated by Regal in a singles match. Young then formed an alliance with <a href="/wiki/JTG" title="JTG">JTG</a> and defeated Striker and O'Neil. After the match they were attacked by the Usos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Young was suspended for 30 days on October 5, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Following his suspension, Young returned on the November 16, 2011 episode of <i>NXT</i> and assaulted Titus O'Neil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Young was then able to get the better of O'Neil twice in tag team matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GutCheck_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-GutCheck-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The conclusion to Young's feud with O'Neil came when O'Neil defeated Young in a no disqualifications match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>

<p>O'Neil then turned heel and formed an alliance with Young. Young and O'Neil feuded with Percy Watson and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Riley" title="Alex Riley">Alex Riley</a> in February 2012, defeating them twice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In March, while he and O'Neil were feuding with the Usos, Young started chasing the affections of <a href="/wiki/Tamina_Snuka" title="Tamina Snuka">Tamina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the April 18 episode of <i>NXT</i>, Young and O'Neil were signed by <a href="/wiki/John_Laurinaitis" title="John Laurinaitis">John Laurinaitis</a> to the <i>SmackDown</i> brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> The team made their debut for the brand on the April 20 episode of <i>SmackDown</i> with a victory over the Usos. The team suffered their first team loss against the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Raw Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Champions</a> <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Kingston" title="Kofi Kingston">Kofi Kingston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ron_Killings" title="Ron Killings">R-Truth</a> on the May 18 edition of Smackdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/No_Way_Out_(2012)" title="No Way Out (2012)">No Way Out</a> pay-per-view in June, minutes before Young and O'Neil teamed up to defeat <a href="/wiki/Justin_Gabriel" title="Justin Gabriel">Justin Gabriel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Kidd" title="Tyson Kidd">Tyson Kidd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Primo_%26_Epico" title="Primo &amp; Epico">Primo &amp; Epico</a> and the Usos in a four-way #1 tag team match to earn a future shot at the WWE Tag Team Championship, after <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Washington" title="Abraham Washington">A.W.</a> turned on his clients, Epico and Primo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> On the July 16 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Young and O'Neil unsuccessfully challenged Kofi Kingston and R-Truth for the WWE Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On the August 10 episode of <i>SmackDown</i>, they defeated Primo and Epico in a #1 contenders match via disqualification when A.W. provoked Kingston and R-Truth, who were on commentary, into interfering in the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SmackDown081012_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-SmackDown081012-53">[53]</a></sup> That same day, A. W. was released from his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AWreleased_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-AWreleased-54">[54]</a></sup> Prime Time Players received their title shots at <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam_(2012)" title="SummerSlam (2012)">SummerSlam</a>, but were again defeated by Kingston and R-Truth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SummerSlam2012_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-SummerSlam2012-55">[55]</a></sup> On the September 7 edition of <i>Smackdown</i>, Prime Time Players defeated Primo and Epico and The Usos in a triple threat tag team match to earn another shot at the title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> But on <i>Raw</i> three days later, Prime Time Players lost their title shot to the team of Daniel Bryan and <a href="/wiki/Kane_(wrestler)" title="Kane (wrestler)">Kane</a> (soon to be known as <a href="/wiki/Team_Hell_No" title="Team Hell No">Team Hell No</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series_(2012)" title="Survivor Series (2012)">Survivor Series</a> pay-per-view, Young took part in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elimination_tag_team_match" title="Elimination tag team match">10 man elimination tag team match</a>, but was eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Rey_Mysterio" title="Rey Mysterio">Rey Mysterio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Young also competed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Royal_Rumble" title="2013 Royal Rumble">2013 Royal Rumble</a> entering at entrant 15, but was quickly eliminated by Kofi Kingston.</p>
<p>On the August 19, 2013 edition of <i>Raw</i>, the Prime Time Players turned face in a winning effort against the Real Americans (<a href="/wiki/Jack_Swagger" title="Jack Swagger">Jack Swagger</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antonio_Cesaro" title="Antonio Cesaro">Antonio Cesaro</a>), Darren Young's second face turn and Titus O'Neil first face turn. The face turn had been speculated about for some months, especially after Young's <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Legit" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">real-life</a> <a href="/wiki/Coming_out" title="Coming out">coming out</a> the previous week which received <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_media" title="Mainstream media">mainstream media</a> coverage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The team gained a push, challenging for the WWE Tag Team Championship, but lost a match to <a href="/wiki/The_Shield_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Shield (professional wrestling)">the Shield</a>. The duo also sided with other face wrestlers from the active roster, only to get punished by COO <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a> for preventing Daniel Bryan from being critically injured by Randy Orton. The Prime Time Players were amongst the select few ordered to battle The Shield in separately sanctioned 3 on 1 handicap matches on the September 20 edition of <i>SmackDown</i>, as punishment for their intervention.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles_competition_.282014.E2.80.932015.29">Singles competition (2014â2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Singles competition (2014â2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On the January 31, 2014 episode of <i>SmackDown</i> after Young was pinned in a tag team match against RybAxel (<a href="/wiki/Ryback" title="Ryback">Ryback</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Axel" title="Curtis Axel">Curtis Axel</a>), O'Neil attacked him and disbanded the team. On <i>SmackDown</i> the following week, Young attacked O'Neil backstage after his interview.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Young picked up a victory over <a href="/wiki/Damien_Sandow" title="Damien Sandow">Damien Sandow</a> on the February 14 edition of <i>SmackDown</i> before being attacked by O'Neil, which turned into a brawl and left O'Neil in tattered clothes. They fought at the <a href="/wiki/Elimination_Chamber_(2014)" title="Elimination Chamber (2014)">Elimination Chamber</a>, with the victor being O'Neil. However, Young defeated O'Neil in a rematch on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Main_Event" title="WWE Main Event">Main Event</a></i>. At <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XXX" title="WrestleMania XXX">WrestleMania XXX</a>, Young was in the <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_the_Giant" title="AndrÃ© the Giant">AndrÃ© the Giant</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_battle_royal" title="Professional wrestling battle royal">Memorial Battle Royal</a>, where he was eliminated by 3MB. At a WWE live event on April 12, Young was injured in a match against <a href="/wiki/Fandango_(wrestler)" title="Fandango (wrestler)">Fandango</a>, and on April 16, he underwent surgery to repair a torn ACL in his left knee that would take him out of action for four to six months.</p>

<p>On the January 5, 2015 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Young appeared with the rest of the WWE roster in the opening segment. His official return came on the February 16 episode of <i>Raw</i>, where Young and an unnamed independent wrestler, Kevin Kross, were scheduled to face <a href="/wiki/The_Ascension_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Ascension (professional wrestling)">The Ascension</a>, but were attacked by them before the match, leading to Young's former tag team partner Titus O'Neil making the save.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> This turned O'Neil face again and the Prime Time Players reunited. On the February 23 edition of <i>Raw</i>, The Prime Time Players defeated The Ascension to hand them their first win as part of the main roster, only to then being attacked by the two. Young participated in the 2nd Annual AndrÃ© the Giant Memorial Battle Royal at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_31" title="WrestleMania 31">WrestleMania 31</a>, where he was eliminated by Ryback.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/Elimination_Chamber_(2015)" title="Elimination Chamber (2015)">Elimination Chamber</a>, The Prime Time Players participated in the first ever tag team <a href="/wiki/Elimination_Chamber" title="Elimination Chamber">Elimination Chamber match</a> for the WWE Tag Team Championship, where they were the last team to be eliminated by the <a href="/wiki/The_New_Day_(wrestling)" title="The New Day (wrestling)">The New Day</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_E_Langston" title="Big E Langston">Big E</a>, Kofi Kingston and <a href="/wiki/Xavier_Woods" title="Xavier Woods">Xavier Woods</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Money_in_the_Bank_(2015)" title="Money in the Bank (2015)">Money in the Bank</a>, the The Prime Time Players defeated The New Day to win the title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Battleground_(2015)" title="Battleground (2015)">Battleground</a>, The Prime Time Players retained the title against The New Day but <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Drop" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">dropped</a> the title back to The New Day on August 23 at <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam_(2015)" title="SummerSlam (2015)">SummerSlam</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Basic_non-elimination_matches" title="Professional wrestling match types">fatalâfour way match</a>, also involving <a href="/wiki/The_Lucha_Dragons" title="The Lucha Dragons">The Lucha Dragons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primo_%26_Epico" title="Primo &amp; Epico">Los Matadores</a>. Young and O'Neil would invoke their rematch clause for the September 14 episode of <i>Raw</i>, which they lost.</p>
<p>Towards the end of 2015, O'Neil and Young began focusing on singles careers. Young teamed with Damien Sandow on the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(2016)" title="Royal Rumble (2016)">Royal Rumble 2016 Kickoff Show</a> in a Fatal 4-Way tag team match in a losing effort. On the February 2, 2016 taping of <i>Main Event</i>, the duo returned to team with The Usos to defeat the Ascension, <a href="/wiki/Cody_Rhodes" title="Cody Rhodes">Stardust</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Breeze" title="Tyler Breeze">Tyler Breeze</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> They have since quietly disbanded when O'Neil received a 60-day suspension after the February 8 episode of <i>Raw</i> after an alleged physical altercation with Vince McMahon. At <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_32" title="WrestleMania 32">WrestleMania 32</a>, Young entered the third AndrÃ© the Giant Memorial Battle Royal and came within the final three, being eliminated by Kane.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bob_Backlund.27s_prot.C3.A9g.C3.A9_.282016.E2.80.93present.29">Bob Backlund's protÃ©gÃ© (2016âpresent)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Bob Backlund's protÃ©gÃ© (2016âpresent)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On the May 5 episode of <i>SmackDown</i>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Backlund" title="Bob Backlund">Bob Backlund</a> agreed to be Young's life coach, in a campaign to "Make Darren Young Great Again", a parody of <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Make_America_Great_Again" title="Make America Great Again">Make America Great Again</a>" campaign slogan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Throughout the next couple of months on <i>Raw</i> and <i>SmackDown</i>, various vignettes featuring Young and Backlund aired, with Backlund assuming the role of Young's <a href="/wiki/Lifestyle_guru" title="Lifestyle guru">life coach</a>. In a "life lesson" segment on the July 4 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Backlund approved of Young adopting his famous <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#Crossface_chickenwing" title="Professional wrestling holds">Crossface chickenwing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Raw07042016_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-Raw07042016-67">[67]</a></sup> The week after on <i>Raw</i>, Young won an 18-man battle royal (after <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Crews" title="Apollo Crews">Apollo Crews</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baron_Corbin" title="Baron Corbin">Baron Corbin</a> eliminated themselves) to become the #1 contender for <a href="/wiki/The_Miz" title="The Miz">The Miz</a>'s <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">WWE Intercontinental Championship</a> at <a href="/wiki/Battleground_(2016)" title="Battleground (2016)">Battleground</a>. The following week on <i>Raw</i>, Young defeated <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Del_Rio" title="Alberto Del Rio">Alberto Del Rio</a>, despite interference from The Miz. On July 19, Young, with Backlund, was drafted to the <i>Raw</i> brand as a result of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_WWE_Draft" title="2016 WWE Draft">2016 WWE Draft</a>. At Battleground, Young faced The Miz in a match that resulted in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Countout" title="Countout">double-countout</a> after he applied the Crossface Chickenwing to Miz outside the ring to protect Bob from Miz and <a href="/wiki/Maryse_Ouellet" title="Maryse Ouellet">Maryse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>Young began a feud with his former tag team partner Titus O'Neil on the August 1 episode of <i>Raw</i>. O'Neil questioned Young's motives on being "great again". Later that night, he defeated Young when he pinned him while grabbing Young's tights. Afterwards, he got into a backstage altercation with Bob Backlund before Young came to the defense of Backlund by attacking O'Neil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The week after on <i>Raw</i>, Young defeated O'Neil by pinning him while grabbing O'Neil's tights, mimicking the way O'Neil defeated him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> On the August 15 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Young and O'Neil reconciled and were placed in a match against <a href="/wiki/Primo_%26_Epico" title="Primo &amp; Epico">The Shining Stars</a>. During the match, O'Neil attacked Young with a Clash of the Titus, allowing The Shining Stars to win the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> On the October 31 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Darren Young participated in the Battle Royal dressed up as his coach, Bob Backlund, in a losing effort. In January, Young suffered an arm injury while teaming with Sin Cara in a match against Primo &amp; Epico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rosser has cited <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Michaels" title="Shawn Michaels">Shawn Michaels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Steamboat" title="Ricky Steamboat">Ricky Steamboat</a> as his favorite wrestlers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Varsallone-pg2_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Varsallone-pg2-13">[13]</a></sup> In an interview released on August 15, 2013, he publicly discussed being <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">gay</a>.
