<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Taiwan Prefecture</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">臺灣府</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Táiwān Fǔ</span></i>) was a prefecture of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Qing_rule" title="Taiwan under Qing rule">Taiwan under Qing rule</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Henckaerts1996_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Henckaerts1996-1">[1]</a></sup> The prefecture was established by the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a> government in 1684,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-China:_five_thousand_years_of_history_and_civilization_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-China:_five_thousand_years_of_history_and_civilization-2">[2]</a></sup> after the island "became an integral part of the Chinese Empire" in 1683.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDavidson190363_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDavidson190363-3">[3]</a></sup> The Taiwan Prefecture Gazetteer (<span lang="zh">臺灣府志</span>; <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Taiwan Fu Zhi</span></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> documented it as part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujian_Province" title="Fujian Province">Fujian Province</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Teng2006_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Teng2006-5">[5]</a></sup> The Taiwan Prefecture Gazetteer was completed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gao_Gonggan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gao Gonggan (page does not exist)">Gao Gonggan</a> in 1695, the 34th year of the reign of the <a href="/wiki/Kangxi_Emperor" title="Kangxi Emperor">Kangxi Emperor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chinese_Studies_in_History_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chinese_Studies_in_History-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1684.E2.80.931723">1684–1723</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 1684–1723">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the Qing wrested the island from the control of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Tungning" title="Kingdom of Tungning">Kingdom of Tungning</a> in 1683, Taiwan was made a prefecture under the administration of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujian_Province" title="Fujian Province">Fujian Province</a>. The new Prefecture "was divided into three <i>hien</i> or districts":<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDavidson190364_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDavidson190364-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zhuluo_County" title="Zhuluo County">Zhuluo County</a>, the central western plains and the north;</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_County&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Taiwan County (page does not exist)">Taiwan County</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%87%BA%E7%81%A3%E7%B8%A3_(1684%E5%B9%B4-1887%E5%B9%B4)" title="zh:臺灣縣 (1684年-1887年)">zh</a>)</span>, around the prefectural seat at Taiwan-fu</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fongshan_County&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fongshan County (page does not exist)">Fongshan County</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%B3%B3%E5%B1%B1%E7%B8%A3_(%E8%87%BA%E7%81%A3)" title="zh:鳳山縣 (臺灣)">zh</a>)</span>, which took up much of present-day <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung" title="Kaohsiung">Kaohsiung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pingtung_County" title="Pingtung County">Pingtung County</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The aboriginal lands on the east coast were not under Qing jurisdiction (this area was called <i>Behind the Mountains</i> (<span lang="zh">後山</span>) by the Qing).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The seat of government, referred to as "Taiwan-fu" (<span lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%87%BA%E7%81%A3%E5%BA%9C%E5%9F%8E" title="zh:臺灣府城">臺灣府城</a></span>; <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Táiwān fǔchéng</span></i>), was located in modern-day <a href="/wiki/Tainan" title="Tainan">Tainan</a>, "which city had been in turn the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Formosa" title="Dutch Formosa">Dutch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Tungning" title="Kingdom of Tungning">Koxinga</a>, and the Chinese".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903244_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903244-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1723-1875">1723-1875</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1723-1875">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Four_Prefectures.2C_Two_Seaboard_Divisions">Four Prefectures, Two Seaboard Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Four Prefectures, Two Seaboard Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prefectures (from south to north):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDavidson190393_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDavidson190393-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Fongshan <i>hien</i>: one town, 8 Chinese villages, 73 uncivilized native villages, 8 civilized native villages</li>
<li>Kagi <i>hien</i>: one town, 4 Chinese villages, 22 uncivilized native villages, 8 civilized native villages</li>
<li>Changhwa <i>hien</i>: one town, 16 villages</li>
</ul>
<p>Seaboard Divisions:</p>
<ul>
<li>Pescadores <i>ting</i></li>
<li>Tamsui <i>ting</i>: one town, 132 farms, 70 native villages</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1875-1887">1875-1887</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1875-1887">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An administrative change occurred in 1875, when Imperial Commissioner <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shen_Pao-chen" title="Shen Pao-chen">Shen Pao-chen</a> demanded that another prefecture be added in Taiwan to revamp the administrative organization of the northern area of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SkinnerBaker1977_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-SkinnerBaker1977-11">[11]</a></sup> As a result, <a href="/wiki/Taipeh_Prefecture" title="Taipeh Prefecture">Taipeh Prefecture</a> was created from part of Taiwan Prefecture.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1887-1895">1887-1895</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1887-1895">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Province#Qing_Dynasty" title="Taiwan Province">Taiwan Province</a> (officially "Fujian-Taiwan-Province") was established in 1887. The new province was reorganized into four prefectures: Taipeh, Taiwan, <a href="/wiki/Tainan_Prefecture_(Qing_dynasty)" title="Tainan Prefecture (Qing dynasty)">Tainan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Taitung_Prefecture" title="Taitung Prefecture">Taitung</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903244_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903244-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Tainan Prefecture was created from part of Taiwan Prefecture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gordon2007_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gordon2007-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Chinese_times_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Chinese_times-14">[14]</a></sup> Thus Taiwan Prefecture was reduced to the area of central Taiwan only, composed of the modern-day <a href="/wiki/Miaoli_County" title="Miaoli County">Miaoli County</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taichung_City" title="Taichung City">Taichung City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nantou_County" title="Nantou County">Nantou County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Changhua_County" title="Changhua County">Changhua County</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yunlin_County" title="Yunlin County">Yunlin County</a>.</p>
<p>The new prefecture was divided into four counties and one subprefecture: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_County_(1887)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Taiwan County (1887) (page does not exist)">Taiwan County</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%87%BA%E7%81%A3%E7%B8%A3_(1887%E5%B9%B4-1895%E5%B9%B4)" title="zh:臺灣縣 (1887年-1895年)">zh</a>)</span>, Changhua County, Yunlin County, Miaoli County, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Puli_Subprefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Puli Subprefecture (page does not exist)">Puli Subprefecture</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%9F%94%E8%A3%8F%E7%A4%BE%E5%BB%B3" title="zh:埔裏社廳">zh</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903244_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903244-9">[9]</a></sup> The new prefecture seat was located at the central city of <i>Toatun</i> (大墩), which was also designated as the site of the new provincial capital, or <i>Taiwan-fu</i>. However, during construction of the new Taiwan-fu, the provincial capital was temporarily relocated to the city of Taipeh (<a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903245_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDavidson1903245-15">[15]</a></sup> One of the administrators of Taiwan Prefecture was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Raymund_Tu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Raymund Tu (page does not exist)">Raymund Tu</a>, a native priest of Taiwan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-David1985_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-David1985-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Four years after development of <i>Toatun</i> began, the seat of Taipeh (Taipei) was officially declared the provincial capital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engtaichung_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-engtaichung-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1895, with the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Shimonoseki" title="Treaty of Shimonoseki">Treaty of Shimonoseki</a> and the successful <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Taiwan_(1895)" title="Japanese invasion of Taiwan (1895)">Japanese invasion of Taiwan</a>, Taiwan Prefecture was abolished. <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Japanese_rule" title="Taiwan under Japanese rule">Under Japanese rule</a>, the province was abolished in favor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_divisions_of_Taiwan_(1895-1945)" title="Political divisions of Taiwan (1895-1945)">Japanese-style divisions</a>.</p>
