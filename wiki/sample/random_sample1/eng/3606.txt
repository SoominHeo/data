<p><b>Daewon C.I.</b> (대원씨아이, <i>Daewon Ssi Ai</i>, formerly Daiwon C.I.), short for Daewon Culture Industry, is a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Daewon_Media" title="Daewon Media">Daewon Media</a> founded in 1991. This <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> publisher releases domestic and imported comics, Newtype Korea Magazine, children's books, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_novels" title="Light novels">light novels</a>. With <a href="/wiki/Haksan_Culture_Company" title="Haksan Culture Company">Haksan Culture Company</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_Cultural_Publishers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seoul Cultural Publishers (page does not exist)">Seoul Cultural Publishers</a>, Daewon C.I. accounts for more than 50% of comics publications in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-accu_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-accu-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daewon_C.I.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daewon C.I. was founded in 1991 as the publishing arm of Daewon Media. Its initial publication was <i>Comic Champ</i> magazine in December of that year. In 1994, they launched <i>Young Champ</i>, and followed with two additional monthly magazines in 1995, which are no longer in print. <i>Issue</i> was also introduced that year as a biweekly magazine. The first <i>Newtype Korea</i> was published in 1999, and in 2002, <i>Mag X</i> was launched in Thailand. Its newest magazine, <i>Super Champ</i> debuted in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daiwon_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-daiwon-2">[2]</a></sup> They currently have a magazine circulation of over 2,000,000 per year and publish 11,000,000 collected volumes of comics per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kocca_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-kocca-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daewon_C.I.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comics">Comics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daewon_C.I.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Comics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daewon C.I. produces comics magazines that feature serialization of domestic and imported comics titles. Each magazine also has its own book imprint under which collected volumes of these comics are published. Its magazines are:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Comic_Champ" title="Comic Champ">Comic Champ</a></i>, a biweekly first published in 1991, aimed at adolescent and young teenage boys.</li>
<li><i>Young Champ</i>, an online-only biweekly first published in 1994, aimed at young adults (changed to an online-only magazine in 2009, merging Super Champ's titles).</li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Issue_(comics)" title="Issue (comics)">Issue</a></i>, a monthly first published in 1995, aimed at adolescent and teenage girls.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newtype_Korea">Newtype Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daewon_C.I.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Newtype Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Newtype Korea</i>, first published in July 1999, is a glossy magazine released monthly. It is the Korean version of <i><a href="/wiki/Newtype" title="Newtype">Newtype</a></i> from Japan, and covers Japanese and domestic TV, DVD, and theatrical animation. Much of the content is translated directly from Japanese, with added features emphasizing domestic Korean productions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Children.27s_books">Children's books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daewon_C.I.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Children's books">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daewon C.I. publishes entertainment and educational books for young children featuring a variety of characters. These characters include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winnie_the_Pooh" title="Winnie the Pooh">Winnie the Pooh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pororo_the_Little_Penguin" title="Pororo the Little Penguin">Pororo the Little Penguin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Digimon" title="Digimon">Digimon</a>.</p>
