<p>The <b>Popular Orthodox Rally</b> or <b>"People's Orthodox Alarm"</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: Λαϊκός Ορθόδοξος Συναγερμός, <i>Laikós Orthódoxos Synagermós</i>), often abbreviated to <b>ΛΑ.Ο.Σ</b> (LAOS) as a <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">pun</a> on the Greek word for <i>people</i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> radical <a href="/wiki/Right-wing_populism" title="Right-wing populism">right-wing populist</a> political party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gemenis_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gemenis-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hainsworth_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hainsworth-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Art_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Art-4">[4]</a></sup> It was founded and is led by journalist <a href="/wiki/Georgios_Karatzaferis" title="Georgios Karatzaferis">Georgios Karatzaferis</a>. Karatzaferis formed LAOS in 2000, a few months after he was expelled from the centre-right <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">New Democracy</a>.</p>
<p>In 2004, LAOS secured support from the <a href="/wiki/Party_of_Hellenism" title="Party of Hellenism">Party of Hellenism</a> and the Hellenic Women's Political Party. In 2005, LAOS absorbed the nationalist<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Front" title="Hellenic Front">Hellenic Front</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Independent_sources" title="Wikipedia:Independent sources"><span title="This claim needs a reference to a independent, third-party source. (February 2012)">third-party source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The youth branch of LAOS is the Youth of the Orthodox Rally (NEOS) (which is also a pun on the word for "youth" in Greek). The Popular Orthodox Rally was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Europe_of_Freedom_and_Democracy" title="Europe of Freedom and Democracy">Europe of Freedom and Democracy</a> (EFD) group in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Seventh_European_Parliament" title="Seventh European Parliament">7th European Parliament</a>, and was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Independent_Democrats_in_Europe" title="Alliance of Independent Democrats in Europe">Alliance of Independent Democrats in Europe</a> <a href="/wiki/European_political_party" title="European political party">Europarty</a> until the AIDE's dissolution in 2008.</p>
<p>The party failed to reach the 3% threshold of the popular vote in the <a href="/wiki/Greek_legislative_election,_2004" title="Greek legislative election, 2004">2004 elections</a>, with 2.2%; three months later it gained 4.12% of the vote and one seat in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Parliament_Election,_2004_(Greece)" title="European Parliament Election, 2004 (Greece)">2004 European Parliamentary Elections</a>. LAOS received 3.8% of the vote in the <a href="/wiki/Greek_legislative_election,_2007" title="Greek legislative election, 2007">2007 elections</a>, electing 10 members of parliament. In 2009 LAOS managed to <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2009_(Greece)" title="European Parliament election, 2009 (Greece)">elect</a> two representatives in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>, receiving 7.14% of the vote. After receiving 5.63% of the vote and electing 15 members of parliament in the <a href="/wiki/Greek_legislative_election,_2009" title="Greek legislative election, 2009">2009 elections</a>, LAOS dropped below the 3% threshold in <a href="/wiki/Greek_legislative_election,_May_2012" title="Greek legislative election, May 2012">2012</a> and failed to secure any seats in parliament.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology">Ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Popular_Orthodox_Rally&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ideology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the Popular Orthodox Rally, "the <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/demarcation" title="wikt:demarcation">demarcation</a> of the political world into the <a href="/wiki/Right-wing_politics" title="Right-wing politics">Right Wing</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_politics" title="Left-wing politics">Left Wing</a> is no longer relevant after the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>. Nowadays, everyone in every aspect of his or her everyday life is either in favour or against <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">Globalization</a>". The party claims to consist of radically diverse groups that span the entire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-Right_politics" title="Left-Right politics">Left-Right political spectrum</a>. Party president Karatzaferis, speaking on the 6th anniversary of the party's creation, stated "We are united in the only party that has in its ranks labourers and scientists, workers and the unemployed, leftists and rightists".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Independent_sources" title="Wikipedia:Independent sources"><span title="This claim needs a reference to a independent, third-party source. (February 2012)">third-party source needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Karatzaferis has described the Popular Orthodox Rally as "a profoundly democratic party", consisting of everything from a "pre-dictatorship Right" to a merger of Left and Right to a "Popular Liberalism" in official party literature. He has also stated that he supports "patriotism and social <a href="/wiki/Solidarity" title="Solidarity">solidarity</a>, taking from all ideologies and personalities I like. I don't care if it's called communism, liberalism or socialism."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nemecis_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-nemecis-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, the Popular Orthodox Rally is often characterized by opposing politicians and in the media as "far-right",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> "populist", "radical right",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> "right-wing"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-right_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-right-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and "nationalist". It has also been argued that its founding declaration (now withdrawn from the web) included antidemocratic, anti-parliamentary ideas, and the proposal that decisions should be taken by a council, which would include military officers and Church officials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The Popular Orthodox Rally began as a party with an Orthodox Christian religious identity, but also one with a radically nationalist political identity. Although it has since allegedly tried to 'moderate' the nationalist part of its appeal, with some of an extreme-nationalist or neo-fascist bent, such as <a href="/wiki/Konstantinos_Plevris" title="Konstantinos Plevris">Konstantinos Plevris</a>, then leaving the party to join <a href="/wiki/Patriotic_Alliance_(Greece)" title="Patriotic Alliance (Greece)">Patriotic Alliance</a> or other fringe political organizations, more extreme-nationalists have recently once again joined its ranks and been elected to parliament. Of the ten Popular Orthodox Rally candidates who entered the parliament in 2007, four are considered to be part of the "nationalist bloc": Makis Voridis, "Thanos" Plevris, Adonis Georgiadis, and Kiriakos Velopoulos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Amid the <a href="/wiki/Greek_government-debt_crisis" title="Greek government-debt crisis">Greek government-debt crisis</a>, the party supported the first bail-out in 2010 (the only parliamentary party apart from the governing PASOK),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> but thereafter voted against PASOK government on crucial votes, including the 29 June 2011 vote on austerity measures. After George Papandreou resigned in November 2011, LAOS participated along with PASOK and the ND in the government of national unity (the Papademos cabinet), but resigned from the government in February 2012 due to further austerity measures<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and amid declining popularity in polls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> LAOS failed to win any seats in either 2012 Greece parliamentary election, which can be attributed to its previous indecisive position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<p>The main points of the Popular Orthodox Rally platform are as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>No <a href="/wiki/Accession_of_Turkey_to_the_European_Union" title="Accession of Turkey to the European Union">accession of Turkey to the European Union</a></li>
<li>Ban immigration from outside the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and deport all illegal immigrants.</li>
<li>Opposition to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Constitution" title="European Constitution">European Constitution</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lisbon_Treaty" title="Lisbon Treaty">Lisbon Treaty</a></li>
<li>A strict stance in the <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_naming_dispute" title="Macedonia naming dispute">Macedonia naming dispute</a>; no recognition of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Republic of Macedonia</a> under any name that includes the term "Macedonia."</li>
<li>Drastic tax cuts for both individuals and small businesses.</li>
</ul>
