<p>The <b>United New Democratic Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 대통합민주신당, <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: 大統合民主新黨, <a href="/wiki/Abbreviation" title="Abbreviation">Abbreviation</a>: UNDP) was a political party of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. It was formed out of the <a href="/wiki/Uri_Party" title="Uri Party">Uri Party</a> and its resulting splinter groups. <a href="/wiki/Chung_Dong-young" title="Chung Dong-young">Chung Dong-young</a> was the UNDP candidate in the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_2007" title="South Korean presidential election, 2007">South Korean presidential election, 2007</a>; he lost to <a href="/wiki/Lee_Myung-bak" title="Lee Myung-bak">Lee Myung-bak</a>. On February 17, 2008, the party merged with the Democratic Party to form the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Democratic_Party_(Republic_of_Korea)" title="United Democratic Party (Republic of Korea)">United Democratic Party</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Brief_history">Brief history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_New_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Brief history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party was formed when loyalists to president Roh Moo-hyun in the <a href="/wiki/Uri_Party" title="Uri Party">Uri Party</a> chose to break ranks from other party members who showed lukewarm support for the administration. Some 80 out of 152 lawmakers of the Uri Party joined the new party, a conservative-liberal minority group from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_National_Party" title="Grand National Party">Hannara Party</a> (led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Son_Hak-Gyu" title="Son Hak-Gyu">Son Hak-Gyu</a>), and a group of progressive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_rights" title="Civil rights">civil rights</a>' group from outside South Korean politics also joined, seeking to complete political reforms.</p>
<p>As a result of merge with the <a href="/wiki/Uri_Party" title="Uri Party">Uri Party</a>, this party has been ranked as the biggest political party in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_National_Assembly" title="Korean National Assembly">legislative</a> by 140 of 298 seats (as of January 14, 2008). After receiving a 'shocked' result at the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_2007" title="South Korean presidential election, 2007">2007 presidential election</a>, the delegates of its party decided to elect a new leader, with adopting a '<a href="/wiki/Papal_conclave" title="Papal conclave">Papal conclave</a>'-style system. On January 11, with more than a half delegates' vote, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Son_Hak-Gyu" title="Son Hak-Gyu">Son Hak-Gyu</a> was elected to lead at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Korean_parliamentary_election,_2008" title="South Korean parliamentary election, 2008">2008 parliamentary election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Presidential_election_primary">Presidential election primary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_New_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Presidential election primary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_New_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>These list of key events has been managed by its public primary election committee(국민경선위원회).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>August 21 and August 22, 2007 - Official announcement has been made by its committee. Registration process has been done.</li>
<li>August 25, 2007 - The full list of official candidate for the primary has been announced. 'Cut-off' process begins.</li>
<li>August 27, 2007 - 'The policy debate' for its candidates has been done.</li>
<li>September 5, 2007 - The result of cut-off election (to nominate 5 candidates to go to its primary) will be announced.</li>
<li>September 5 to September 14, 2007 - Nationwide 'Policy debate'(tour) will be held in several cities.</li>
<li>September 15 to October 14, 2007 - The primary election will be held in 8 multi-regional areas. (<i>see 'Results' for more details</i>)</li>
<li>October 14, 2007 - <a href="/wiki/Chung_Dong-young" title="Chung Dong-young">Chung Dong-young</a> was elected as the official presidential candidate of the party.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules">Rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_New_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Cut-off process (August 25 ~ September 5)
<ul>
<li>The official result of cut-off process has been combining a result from public poll(50%), and a poll result from its 10,000 randomly selected delegates(50%). 'Public poll' has been conducted from all eligible voters (whether this party is in favour or not).</li>
<li>Top-5 candidates who earn more votes will join to its official primary<i>(Result has been announced-see 'Candidates')</i></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Primary election (September 15 ~ October 14)</li>
</ul>
<p>The official result of this primary combines all votes of these key methods. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First-past-the-post" title="First-past-the-post">First-past-the-post</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_voting" title="Electronic voting">Electronic voting</a> system is being used.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<ul>
<li>1. Regional Rounds from 8 multi-provincial divisions (see below)</li>
<li>2. The total number of '<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mobile_vote&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mobile vote (page does not exist)">Mobile vote</a>' cast</li>
<li>3. Public opinion polls which conducted from October 8 to 14</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Method 1 and 2 grants exactly 90% of results (Method 3 grants 10%). The official candidate of this party will be nominated on October 14, just after the last regional rounds finishes. Due to the executives of this party's decision, All candidates did not contested during the <a href="/wiki/Chuseok" title="Chuseok">Hangawi</a> holidays and the <a href="/wiki/Inter-Korean_Summit" title="Inter-Korean Summit">2007 Inter-Korean Summit</a> periods.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Candidates">Candidates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_New_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Candidates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of August 25, 2007, 9 politicians has been set up to their presidential bid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Before starting their official primary to the South Korean public, they have to access the 'cut-off' process to reduce from 9 to 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Later, the number of candidates reduced to 3 after ex-Health and Welfare Minister <a href="/wiki/Rhyu_Si-min" title="Rhyu Si-min">Rhyu Si-min</a> and former Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Han_Myeong-sook" title="Han Myeong-sook">Han Myeong-sook</a> quit the race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Here's a list of official candidates of the UNDP primary that announced on September 5, 2007, 05:30<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GMT" title="GMT">GMT</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Re-merge_with_Democratic_Party">Re-merge with Democratic Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_New_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Re-merge with Democratic Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February,2007, Kim-Han-Kil, who was the flood leader of Uri party, decided to defect Uri party(열린우리당) with 23 councilers. And, they formed the new party(their purpose was the destruction of Uri party(열린우리당), and the president Roh-Mu-Hyun, and his supporters).And some councilers of Uri party(열린우리당), supporters of Kim-Han-Kil, and supporters of Son-Hak-Gyu merged, and then they formed the United New Democracy Party(대통합민주신당).</p>
<p>On February 17, 2008, the UNDP merged with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2005)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2005)">Democratic Party</a> (민주당), forming the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Democratic_Party_(Republic_of_Korea)" title="United Democratic Party (Republic of Korea)">United Democratic Party</a> (통합민주당). This was four years after the <a href="/wiki/Uri_Party" title="Uri Party">Uri Party</a> (열린우리당)'s split from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennium_Democratic_Party" title="Millennium Democratic Party">Millennium Democratic Party</a> (새천년민주당). <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
