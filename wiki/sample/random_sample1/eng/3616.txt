<p>The <b>Grand and General Council</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it"><i>Consiglio Grande e Generale</i></span>) is the <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a>. The council has 60 members elected for a five-year term.</p>
<p>The country's electoral law is based on the electoral system of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cities_in_Italy" title="Cities in Italy">Italian municipalities</a>. Between 1945 and 2007, San Marino used <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">proportional representation</a>. A majority of at least 35 seats is given to the winning coalition of parties which receives an absolute majority of votes at the first or the eventual second round of elections. Within single coalitions, seats are divided between the parties using a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%27Hondt_system" title="D'Hondt system">D'Hondt system</a>. A 3.5% <a href="/wiki/Election_threshold" title="Election threshold">threshold</a> exists, together with guarantees for female candidates.</p>
<p>The Council appoints from among its members the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_State" title="Congress of State">Congress of State</a>, which is the executive branch of the government, and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Captains_Regent_of_San_Marino" title="List of Captains Regent of San Marino">Captains Regents</a>, who serve as the heads of state.</p>
<p>The Council is the equivalent of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Senate" title="Roman Senate">Roman Senate</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman Republic</a>.</p>
