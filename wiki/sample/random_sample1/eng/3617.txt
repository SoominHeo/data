<p><i><b>The Great Seer</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">대풍수</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani">大風水</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Dae Pung Su</span></i>, also known as <i><b>The Great Geomancer</b></i>) is a 2012 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> historical television series, starring <a href="/wiki/Ji_Sung" title="Ji Sung">Ji Sung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ji_Jin-hee" title="Ji Jin-hee">Ji Jin-hee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Song_Chang-eui" title="Song Chang-eui">Song Chang-eui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_So-yeon" title="Kim So-yeon">Kim So-yeon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Yoon-ji" title="Lee Yoon-ji">Lee Yoon-ji</a>. Set during the turbulent decline of <a href="/wiki/Goryeo" title="Goryeo">Goryeo</a>, it is about practicers of divination and the power that they hold over the fate of the country. It aired on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> from October 10, 2012 to February 7, 2013 on Wednesdays and Thursdays at 21:55 for 35 episodes.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Seer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Great Seer</i> begins during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Gongmin_of_Goryeo" title="Gongmin of Goryeo">King Gongmin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ryu_Tae-joon" title="Ryu Tae-joon">Ryu Tae-joon</a>). But despite being about seers, geomancers, divinators and the like, this drama is less about the fantasy and more about the political movers and shakers — people who had the power to advise, and therefore control, kings. <a href="/wiki/Taejo_of_Joseon" title="Taejo of Joseon">Yi Seong-gye</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ji_Jin-hee" title="Ji Jin-hee">Ji Jin-hee</a>) is the general who led the overthrow of <a href="/wiki/Goryeo" title="Goryeo">Goryeo</a> and established the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a>, becoming its first king.</p>
<p>Mok Ji-sang (<a href="/wiki/Ji_Sung" title="Ji Sung">Ji Sung</a>) is a gifted seer/geomancer, born with the ability to see into people’s pasts and futures. There are those who believe falsely that he has dark supernatural powers, thinking him possessed by ghosts. When he comes of age in the late Goryeo era, he becomes a scholar of <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">divination</a>, and a reader of geography, <a href="/wiki/Physiognomy" title="Physiognomy">faces</a>, and the like to tell fortunes — an area with much influence at the time. He eventually becomes a "king-maker," who holds the key to a major political shift in the overthrow of Goryeo and the rise of Joseon when he backs General Yi and effectively shapes the future of Korea as we know it.</p>
<p>Lee Jung-geun (<a href="/wiki/Song_Chang-eui" title="Song Chang-eui">Song Chang-eui</a>) is General Yi's other advisor and Ji-sang's rival. Hae-in (<a href="/wiki/Kim_So-yeon" title="Kim So-yeon">Kim So-yeon</a>) is a healer whose destiny is tied to General Yi, but she falls in love with the seer. Ban-ya (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Yoon-ji" title="Lee Yoon-ji">Lee Yoon-ji</a>) is a woman who was sold off as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gisaeng" title="Gisaeng">gisaeng</a> at a young age, but becomes a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concubine" title="Concubine">concubine</a> to King Gongmin's advisor, and bears a son. King Gongmin takes in that son as his, and the boy becomes <a href="/wiki/U_of_Goryeo" title="U of Goryeo">Woo of Goryeo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Min-ho_(actor_born_1993)" title="Lee Min-ho (actor born 1993)">Lee Min-ho</a>) — the king that General Yi dethrones in a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a>.</p>
