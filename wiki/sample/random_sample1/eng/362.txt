<p><b>.ar</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CcTLD" title="CcTLD">ccTLD</a>) for <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. It is administered by <a href="/wiki/NIC_Argentina" title="NIC Argentina">NIC Argentina</a>. Registering a .AR domain directly is not allowed, only the 8 second-level domains below are open to everyone, although a local presence in Argentina is required.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_level_domains">Second level domains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.ar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Second level domains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are currently 8 <a href="/wiki/Country_code_second-level_domain" title="Country code second-level domain">second-level domains</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><b>COM.AR</b> - Companies and individuals resident in Argentina</li>
<li><b>EDU.AR</b> - Educational institutions</li>
<li><b>GOB.AR</b>, <b>GOV.AR</b> - Local and national government</li>
<li><b>INT.AR</b> - International entities and representatives of foreign international organisations in Argentina</li>
<li><b>MIL.AR</b> - Military</li>
<li><b>NET.AR</b> - Providers of internet services licensed by the Comisión Nacional de Comunicaciones</li>
<li><b>ORG.AR</b> - Not for profit only</li>
<li><b>TUR.AR</b> - Tourism and travel companies licensed by the Ministerio de Turismo de la Nación</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_characters">Special characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.ar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Special characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2008, a resolution approved the use of special characters in domain names, including ñ, ç, á, é, í ó, ú, ä, ë, ï, ö, and ü. The .gob.ar domain was also approved for government entities (.gob stands for "gobierno", government in Spanish).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
