<p><b>South Korean–Turkish relations</b> are foreign relations between <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. Turkey has an embassy in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, while South Korea has an embassy in <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a> and an consulate-general in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>. Both countries are members of <a href="/wiki/G20" title="G20">G20</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korea%E2%80%93Turkey_relations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Turkey, namely the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Brigade" title="Turkish Brigade">Turkish Brigade</a> had supported South-Koreans against North-Korean forces at the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>. Because of this, the relationship between the two nations is sometimes referred as "South-Korean–Turkish brotherhood"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Kore–Türk dostluğu</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">한국 – 터키 우정</span>).</p>
<p>Through the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greek_genocide" title="Greek genocide">Greek genocide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_genocide" title="Assyrian genocide">Assyrian genocide</a> and the actual Turkish-Kurdish conflict and the political situation in Turkey, many intellectual Koreans have a bad image about Turkey. In 2015 South Korean Parliament Members Visited the Armenian Genocide Memorial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_relations">Economic relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korea%E2%80%93Turkey_relations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economic relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, both countries entered into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_trade_agreement" title="Free trade agreement">free trade agreement</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_cooperation">Military cooperation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korea%E2%80%93Turkey_relations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Military cooperation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Altay_(tank)" title="Altay (tank)">Altay tank</a>, a newly military tank of the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Armed_Forces" title="Turkish Armed Forces">Turkish Armed Forces</a>, was supported by <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Rotem" title="Hyundai Rotem">Hyundai Rotem</a> with technical support and assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
