<p>The <b>Korea Fair Trade Commission</b> (<b>KFTC</b>) is South Korea's regulatory authority for economic <a href="/wiki/Competition" title="Competition">competition</a>. It was established in 1981 within the Economic Planning Board. The establishing law was the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monopoly_Regulation_and_Fair_Trade_Act&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monopoly Regulation and Fair Trade Act (page does not exist)">Monopoly Regulation and Fair Trade Act</a> (MRFTA), Law No. 3320, December 31, 1980. In 1994, the Fair Trade Commission and its Secretariat were separated from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Planning_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Economic Planning Board (page does not exist)">Economic Planning Board</a> as an independent vice ministerial-level, central administrative organization. In 1996, the status of the KFTC Chairman was elevated from vice-ministerial to ministerial level.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Trade_Commission_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original KFTC had five commissioners from 1981 to 1990. This was increased to seven commissioners from 1990 to 1997. Since 1997 the KFTC has had nine commissioners, which includes a Chairman, a Vice-Chairman, and three other standing commissioners. There are four non-standing commissioners. The KFTC is supported in its work by a Secretariat.</p>
<p>The current KFTC Chairman is Jeong Jae-Chan.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Enforcement">Enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Trade_Commission_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Apart from the MRFTA, the KFTC enforces the following statutes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Adhesion Contract Act, Law No. 3922, December 31, 1986.</li>
<li>Fair Labelling and Advertising Act, Law No. 5814, February 5, 1999.</li>
<li>Omnibus Cartel Repeal Act, Law No. 5815, February 5, 1999.</li>
<li>Door-to-Door Sales Act, Law No. 4481, December 31, 1991.</li>
<li>Installment Transactions Act, Law No. 4480, December 31, 1991.</li>
<li>Fair Subcontract Transactions Act, Law No. 3799, December 31, 1984.</li>
<li>Fair Franchise Transactions Act, Law No. 6704, May 13, 2002.</li>
<li>Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce Act, Law No. 6687, March 30, 2002.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cases_tackled">Cases tackled</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Trade_Commission_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cases tackled">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Microsoft_decision">Microsoft decision</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Trade_Commission_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Microsoft decision">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 7, 2005, the KFTC reached the decision to order <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microsoft_Corporation" title="Microsoft Corporation">Microsoft Corporation</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Microsoft Korea (page does not exist)">Microsoft Korea</a>, inter alia, to unbundle certain tied products, including <a href="/wiki/Windows_Media_Player" title="Windows Media Player">Windows Media Player</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MSN_Messenger" title="MSN Messenger">MSN Messenger</a>, and to impose surcharges amounting to 33 billion won (31 million US dollars) for violation of the Monopoly Regulation and Fair Trade Act (MRFTA), including the abuse of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Market_dominant_position&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Market dominant position (page does not exist)">market dominant position</a> provision. On October 16, 2007, the Associated Press reported that Microsoft has stopped appealing the December 2005 decision and has withdrawn the appeal.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_music_price_fixing_scandal.5B1.5D.5B2.5D">2011 music price fixing scandal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Trade_Commission_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011 music price fixing scandal[1][2]">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 28, 2011, the KFTC declared, <i>"Through a meeting held from February 23rd through the 25th, we have decided to take appropriate action against the following 15 online music-related companies: <a href="/wiki/SK_Telecom" title="SK Telecom">SK Telecom</a>, <a href="/wiki/LOEN_Entertainment" title="LOEN Entertainment">LOEN Entertainment</a>, <a href="/wiki/KT_Corporation" title="KT Corporation">KT</a>, <a href="/wiki/KT_Music" title="KT Music">KT Music</a>, <a href="/wiki/CJ_E%26M_Music_Performance_Division" title="CJ E&amp;M Music Performance Division">Mnet Media</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neowiz" title="Neowiz">Neowiz Bugs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music Entertainment Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warner_Music_Group" title="Warner Music Group">Warner Music Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yejeon_Media" title="Yejeon Media">Yejeon Media</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pony_Canyon" title="Pony Canyon">Pony Canyon Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a>, <a href="/wiki/SBS_Contents_Hub" title="SBS Contents Hub">SBS Contents Hub</a>, King Pin Entertainment, Direct Media."</i></p>
<p>The FTC alleged that the companies decided to rig the market once non-DRM (<a href="/wiki/Digital_rights_management" title="Digital rights management">digital rights management</a>) digital songs were allowed to be downloaded, starting May 2008 (previously, only DRM songs were allowed on the market). Non-DRM songs allow for infinite downloads, which led companies to discontinue products and certificates that catered to non-DRM songs. Thirteen of the companies listed above also stopped distributing songs to services which offered infinite downloads, thus rigging the market.</p>
<p>A representative of the FTC revealed, <i>"In order to block competition coming from smaller businesses, the companies listed above rigged their own prices. By rigging the prices in compliance with one another, they placed great harm on the consumers and other businesses in the industry. Indeed, by equalizing the prices of their products, they've made the music industry believe that such a phenomenon was permanent. They have completely set up a blockade against the production of products that fit in different price categories. This is a violation of the consumer's right of choice."</i></p>
<p>The representative continued, <i>"With this action, we hope that consumers will be able to spend less in order to enjoy a greater diversity of products. This is the first action being taken for the online music industry. No one is free when prices are rigged, so we hope other companies support this effort."</i></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Recommendations">Recommendations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Trade_Commission_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Recommendations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The fines for each company are:</p>
<ul>
<li>SK Telecom (fined $1.96 million)</li>
<li>LOEN Entertainment (fined $9.69 million)</li>
<li>KT (fined $811,000)</li>
<li>KT Music (fined $1.16 million)</li>
<li>Mnet Media (fined $1.98 million)</li>
<li>Neowiz Bugs (fined $1.11 million)</li>
<li>Sony Music Entertainment (fined $1.19 million)</li>
<li>Universal Music (fined $814,000)</li>
<li>Warner Music Korea (fined $96,000)</li>
</ul>
<p>Five of the above companies (SK Telecom, LOEN Entertainment, KT Music, Mnet Media, and Neowiz Bugs) will be formally accused of their crimes.</p>
<p>LOEN Entertainment's CEO Shin Won-soo, KT Music's CEO Kim Min-wook and Mnet Media's CEO Kim Sung-soo will be prosecuted separately.</p>
