<p>The <b>South Korean women's national ice hockey team</b> represents <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> at the <a href="/wiki/International_Ice_Hockey_Federation" title="International Ice Hockey Federation">International Ice Hockey Federation</a>'s <a href="/wiki/IIHF_World_Women%27s_Championships" title="IIHF World Women's Championships">World Women's Ice Hockey Championship Division IIA</a>. The women's national team is controlled by <a href="/wiki/Korea_Ice_Hockey_Association" title="Korea Ice Hockey Association">Korea Ice Hockey Association</a>. As of 2013, South Korea has 184 female players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The South Korean women's national team is ranked 24th in the world.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Championships">World Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korea_women%27s_national_ice_hockey_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004 the South Korean women's hockey team was the first time involved in the <a href="/wiki/IIHF_World_Women%27s_Championships" title="IIHF World Women's Championships">World Championship</a> competition.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/2004_IIHF_Women%27s_World_Championship" title="2004 IIHF Women's World Championship">2004</a> – Finished in 27th place (6th in Division III, Demoted to Division IV)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2005_IIHF_Women%27s_World_Championship" title="2005 IIHF Women's World Championship">2005</a> – Finished in 27th place (1st in Division IV, Promoted to Division III)</li>
