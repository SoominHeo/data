<p><b>Presidential elections</b> were held in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> on 15 May 1956.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The result was a victory for <a href="/wiki/Syngman_Rhee" title="Syngman Rhee">Syngman Rhee</a>, who won 70.0% of the vote. Voter turnout was 94.4%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rhee, who at that time held a virtual monopoly on political power, was opposed by <a href="/wiki/Shin_Ik-hee" title="Shin Ik-hee">Shin Ik-hee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cho_Bong-am" title="Cho Bong-am">Cho Bong-am</a>. Shin died before the election by disease, and Cho campaigned on a platform of peaceful reunification in opposition to Rhee's policy of "March North and unify Korea".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Cho exceeded expectations by receiving over 30% of the vote. Three years later, Cho was accused of violating the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Security_Law_(South_Korea)" title="National Security Law (South Korea)">National Security Law</a> and executed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
