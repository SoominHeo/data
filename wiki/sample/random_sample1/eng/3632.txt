<p>The <b>Fourth Republic</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">제4공화국</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>:第四共和國; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revised_romanisation" title="Revised romanisation">revised romanisation</a>:je-sa gonghwaguk) was the government of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> between 1972 and 1981, regulated by the <b>Yusin Constitution</b> adopted in October 1972 and confirmed in a referendum on 21 November 1972. From 1972 to 1979, power was monopolized by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Chung_Hee" title="Park Chung Hee">Park Chung Hee</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republican_Party_(South_Korea)" title="Democratic Republican Party (South Korea)">Democratic Republican Party</a> under the highly centralized authoritarian "Yusin System". With <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Park_Chung_Hee" title="Assassination of Park Chung Hee">the assassination of Park</a> on 26 October 1979, the Republic entered a period of tumult and transition under the short-lived nominal presidency of <a href="/wiki/Choi_Kyu-hah" title="Choi Kyu-hah">Choi Kyu-hah</a>, controlled under severe escalating martial law and witnessing the <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_of_December_Twelfth" title="Coup d'état of December Twelfth">coup d'état of December Twelfth</a>, the violent unfolding of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwangju_Democratization_Movement" title="Gwangju Democratization Movement">Gwangju Democratization Movement</a> and its armed suppression, the <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_of_May_Seventeenth" title="Coup d'état of May Seventeenth">coup d'état of May Seventeenth</a> and presidency of <a href="/wiki/Chun_Doo-hwan" title="Chun Doo-hwan">Chun Doo-hwan</a>, and finally the transition to the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Republic_of_South_Korea" title="Fifth Republic of South Korea">Fifth Republic</a> under Chun in 1981.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fourth_Republic_of_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This period also saw continued dramatic economic growth, following the government's five-year plans. The government invested heavily in heavy industries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_relations">International relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fourth_Republic_of_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A variety of events in international diplomacy led the Park regime to reconsider its diplomatic position. Notable were the <a href="/wiki/Rapprochement" title="Rapprochement">normalization</a> of U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_relations" title="Diplomatic relations">diplomatic relations</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, which cast doubt on <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>'s ability to count on <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> backing from the United States. The South Korean government began to establish diplomatic relations with many countries, such as Canada. In addition, the first round of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> talks were held between <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. Park also announced plans for eventual reunification.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Yusin_Constitution">Yusin Constitution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fourth_Republic_of_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Yusin Constitution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The Fourth Republic was governed under the <i>Yusin Constitution</i>, also spelled <i>Yusin</i>. The term <i>Yusin</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">hangul</a>: <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ko:%EC%9C%A0%EC%8B%A0" title="wiktionary:ko:유신">유신</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a> means "rejuvenation" or "renewal", but it is also the term used to translate the "restoration" component of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> 明治維新, <i>Meiji-ishin</i>, meaning <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>. The significance of this allusion is in the "imperial" role which scholars have seen attached to the presidency under the Yusin Constitution, which effectively concentrated all governing power in Park's hands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 1963 Constitution limited a president to two four-year terms. However, Park won the right to run for a third term in 1971 when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_South_Korea" title="National Assembly of South Korea">National Assembly</a>, dominated by his allies in the Democratic Republican Party, amended the 1963 constitution to allow the incumbent president — Park himself — to run for three terms. He won a narrow victory over opposition candidate <a href="/wiki/Kim_Dae-jung" title="Kim Dae-jung">Kim Dae-jung</a>.</p>
<p>Shortly after being sworn in, Park declared a state of emergency "based on the dangerous realities of the international situation." In October 1972, he dissolved the National Assembly and suspended the Constitution. Work was then begun on a new constitution, which was approved in <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_constitutional_referendum,_1972" title="South Korean constitutional referendum, 1972">a referendum held on 21 November 1972</a>. Official results showed an implausible 92.3 percent of voters approved the new document.</p>
<p>The document was marked by the enormous powers granted to the president. He was elected for six years, with no limits on reelection. The people elected delegates to the National Conference for Unification, an electoral college that was charged with electing the president. The requirements for candidacy, however, were so stringent that only one candidate could be on the conference's ballot. He was vested with sweeping powers to suspend constitutional freedoms and rule by decree. He also had the right to appoint one-third of the National Assembly, effectively guaranteeing a parliamentary majority. For all intents and purposes, the Yusin Constitution turned Park's presidency into a legal dictatorship.</p>
<p>The provisions of the Yusin Constitution were greeted with widespread but ineffective protest. Park was elected without opposition in 1972 and 1978. It remained in effect until after Park's assassination in 1979.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Assassination_of_Park_and_breakdown">Assassination of Park and breakdown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fourth_Republic_of_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Assassination of Park and breakdown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The assassination of Park, on 26 October 1979, was a pivotal moment in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> history and a portentous one for the Yusin system. Park's Prime Minister, <a href="/wiki/Choi_Kyu-hah" title="Choi Kyu-hah">Choi Kyu-hah</a>, assumed power as acting president but was almost immediately marginalized by competing factions in the military. After the declaration of martial law following Park's death, General <a href="/wiki/Jeong_Seung-hwa" title="Jeong Seung-hwa">Jeong Seung-hwa</a> acted as the government's chief administrator and appointed <a href="/wiki/Chun_Doo-hwan" title="Chun Doo-hwan">Chun Doo-hwan</a> the same day to lead a Joint Investigation Headquarters.</p>
<p>On 27 October, Chun unilaterally assumed control of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KCIA" title="KCIA">KCIA</a> and the government intelligence apparatus. On 6 December, the National Council for Unification confirmed Choi Kyu-hah as President according to the framework of the Yusin Constitution, but six days later, Chun spearheaded a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_of_December_Twelfth" title="Coup d'état of December Twelfth">military coup</a>, forcibly arresting and detaining General Jeong. Choi had lost any meaningful authority in government by then, and Chun installed himself as Director of the KCIA early in 1980.</p>
<p>As growing social unrest came to a head in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwangju_Democratization_Movement" title="Gwangju Democratization Movement">Gwangju Democratization Movement</a>, Chun tightened martial law, suppressed protests with troops, and carried out a further <a href="/wiki/Self-coup" title="Self-coup">self-coup</a> over the course of year. Elected President by the continuing National Council for Unification, Chun progressively dismantled the institutions of the Fourth Republic, dissolving the National Assembly and establishing an emergency national security committee with himself as director. Finally, Chun promulgated a new constitution in 1981, pushing it through a referendum, thereby formally dissolving the Fourth Republic and Park's Yusin system.</p>
