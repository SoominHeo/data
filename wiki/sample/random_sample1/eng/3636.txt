<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Minister of Culture (South Korea)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <b>Minister of Culture</b> oversees the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Culture,_Sports_and_Tourism" title="Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism">Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism</a>. The ministry was established on 3 January 1990 by Presidential Decree Number 12895,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>under the <a href="/wiki/Roh_Tae-woo" title="Roh Tae-woo">Roh Tae-woo</a> Government. The first Minister of Culture was novelist <a href="/wiki/Lee_O-young" title="Lee O-young">Lee O-young</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
