<p><b><a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">Taekwondo</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a></b> were held in the Sports Pavilion at the <a href="/wiki/Faliro_Coastal_Zone_Olympic_Complex" title="Faliro Coastal Zone Olympic Complex">Faliro Coastal Zone Olympic Complex</a> in Athens, Greece where 124 competitors competed in eight events, four each for men and women.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medal_summary">Medal summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taekwondo_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Medal summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The host nation <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> failed to add to the gold won by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michalis_Mouroutsos" title="Michalis Mouroutsos">Michalis Mouroutsos</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, though <a href="/wiki/Alexandros_Nikolaidis" title="Alexandros Nikolaidis">Alexandros Nikolaidis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elisavet_Mystakidou" title="Elisavet Mystakidou">Elisavet Mystakidou</a> both won silver. <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a> (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>) won its first two gold medals ever at these events. <a href="/wiki/Steven_L%C3%B3pez" title="Steven López">Steven López</a> of the United States and <a href="/wiki/Chen_Zhong" title="Chen Zhong">Chen Zhong</a> of China each repeated as Olympic champions. <a href="/wiki/Hadi_Saei" title="Hadi Saei">Hadi Saei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huang_Chih-hsiung" title="Huang Chih-hsiung">Huang Chih-hsiung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Gentil" title="Pascal Gentil">Pascal Gentil</a> also won medal for the second time.</p>
