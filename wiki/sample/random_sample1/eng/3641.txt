<p>A <b>town</b> or <i><b>eup</b></i> is an administrative unit in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>; along with <a href="/wiki/Myeon_(administrative_division)" title="Myeon (administrative division)">township</a> (rural), a town (urban) is one of the divisions of a <a href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_South_Korea" title="List of counties in South Korea">county</a>, and of some <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_South_Korea" title="List of cities in South Korea">cities</a> with a population of less than 500,000. The main town or towns in a county—or the secondary town or towns within a city's territory—are designated as towns. Towns are subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Ri_(administrative_division)" title="Ri (administrative division)">village</a>. In order to form an town, the minimum population required is 20,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncyKorea_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EncyKorea-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Britannica_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Britannica-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doosan_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Doosan-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
