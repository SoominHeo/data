<p>This article gives an overview of <b>liberalism in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a></b>. It is limited to <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">parties</a> with substantial support, mainly proven by having had a representation in parliament.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Note: the word <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a> in South Korea is often used by conservative groupings (See <a href="/wiki/New_Right#South_Korea" title="New Right">New Right in South Korea</a>), in the European fashion. <a href="/wiki/Social_liberalism" title="Social liberalism">Liberal parties</a> (in the American sense of the term) tend to label themselves as "Democratic" or "Progressive" instead.</p>
<p>There has been a tradition of liberal parties since 1955, often organized around persons. Presently the <a href="/wiki/Minjoo_Party_of_Korea" title="Minjoo Party of Korea">Minjoo Party of Korea</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(South_Korea)" title="People's Party (South Korea)">People's Party</a> are the main successors of the liberal parties' tradition</p>
<p>"Liberal" movements in Korea also differ markedly from liberal movements elsewhere by strongly emphasizing <a href="/wiki/Patriotism" title="Patriotism">patriotism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_nationalism" title="Ethnic nationalism">ethnic nationalism</a> (especially with regard to <a href="/wiki/Korean_reunification" title="Korean reunification">Korean reunification</a>) instead of <a href="/wiki/Civic_nationalism" title="Civic nationalism">"civic," or "liberal nationalism"</a>, a characteristic shared with <a href="/wiki/Juche" title="Juche">North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This emphasis in "Liberalism" is closely associated with Pro-North Korea sentiments in the political sphere along with <a href="/wiki/Anti-Americanism" title="Anti-Americanism">anti-American ideology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In this critical respect "liberalism" in South Korea bears very little resemblance to "liberalism" as it is understood elsewhere in the world.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Christian_Social_Democratic_Party_to_Korea_Democratic_Party">Christian Social Democratic Party to Korea Democratic Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Christian Social Democratic Party to Korea Democratic Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1945: The <b><a href="/wiki/Korea_Democratic_Party" title="Korea Democratic Party">Christian Social Democratic Party</a></b> first political party formed since Korea was liberated from Japanese occupation.</li>
<li>1945: Renamed as <b><a href="/wiki/Korea_Democratic_Party" title="Korea Democratic Party">Korea Democratic Party</a></b>.</li>
<li>1949: merged with the Democratic National Party</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_National_Party">Democratic National Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Democratic National Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1955: merged with Democratic Party (1955) to form the <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Party_(South_Korea)" title="Democratic National Party (South Korea)">Democratic National Party</a></b></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_Party_.281955.29">Democratic Party (1955)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Democratic Party (1955)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1955: <a href="/wiki/Chang_Myon" title="Chang Myon">Chang Myon</a> founds the <b><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_1955)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 1955)">Democratic Party (1955)</a></b> (<i>Minju Dang</i>)</li>
<li>1957: Unity Party secedes from the Democratic Party (1955)</li>
<li>1961: Party is banned and splits into the Democratic Party (1963) and the Civil Rule Party</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_Rule_Party">Civil Rule Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Civil Rule Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1963: The <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rule_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Civil Rule Party (page does not exist)">Civil Rule Party</a></b> founded after the banned of Democratic Party (1955)</li>
<li>1967: merged with the Civil Rule Party and forms the New Democratic Korea Party</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_Party_.281963.29">Democratic Party (1963)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Democratic Party (1963)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1963: The <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_1963)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 1963) (page does not exist)">Democratic Party (1963)</a></b> founded after the banned of Democratic Party (1955)</li>
<li>1967: merged with the Democratic Party (1963) and forms the New Democratic Korea Party</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Democratic_Korea_Party">New Democratic Korea Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: New Democratic Korea Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1967: Party is refounded as the <b><a href="/wiki/New_Democratic_Party_(South_Korea)" title="New Democratic Party (South Korea)">New Democratic Party</a></b> (<i>Sinmin Dang</i>), led from 1971 by <a href="/wiki/Kim_Dae-jung" title="Kim Dae-jung">Kim Dae-jung</a>. When Kim fled to Japan, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Young-sam" title="Kim Young-sam">Kim Young-sam</a> became the party leader</li>
<li>1981: Party is banned and Kim Dae-jung is sentenced to the death penalty. Factions of the party continue as the <b><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Korea_Party" title="Democratic Korea Party">Democratic Korea Party</a></b> (<i>Minjuhanguk Dang</i>)</li>
<li>1985: Most of the party joins the ⇒ New Korean Democratic Party, a small and unsuccessful faction</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Korean_Democratic_Party">New Korean Democratic Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: New Korean Democratic Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1985: Lee Min Woo founds the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=New_Korean_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="New Korean Democratic Party (page does not exist)">New Korean Democratic Party</a></b> (<i>Sinhanminju Dang</i>), joined after the 1985 elections by the majority of the ⇒ Democratic Korea Party (including <a href="/wiki/Kim_Dae-jung" title="Kim Dae-jung">Kim Dae-jung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Young-sam" title="Kim Young-sam">Kim Young-sam</a>)</li>
<li>1987: The "two Kims" and their followers leave the party to form the ⇒ Reunification Democratic Party; the New Korean Democratic Party disappears</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reunification_Democratic_Party">Reunification Democratic Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reunification Democratic Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1987: <a href="/wiki/Kim_Dae-jung" title="Kim Dae-jung">Kim Dae-jung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Young-sam" title="Kim Young-sam">Kim Young-sam</a> leave the ⇒ New Korean Democratic Party and form the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Reunification_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Reunification Democratic Party (page does not exist)">Reunification Democratic Party</a></b> (<i>Tongil Minju Dang</i>)</li>
<li>1987: Kim Dae-jung leaves the Democratic Reunification Party and forms the ⇒ Party for Peace and Democracy</li>
<li>1990: The party merges with the conservative Democratic Justice Party and the New Democratic Republican Party into the conservative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Liberal_Party_(South_Korea)" title="Democratic Liberal Party (South Korea)">Democratic Liberal Party</a> (<i>Minju Jayu Dang</i>). A faction forms the ⇒ Democratic Party (1990)</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Party_for_Peace_and_Democracy_to_Democratic_Party_.281991.29">Party for Peace and Democracy to Democratic Party (1991)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Party for Peace and Democracy to Democratic Party (1991)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1987: <a href="/wiki/Kim_Dae-jung" title="Kim Dae-jung">Kim Dae-jung</a> leaves the ⇒ Reunification Democratic Party and forms the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Party_for_Peace_and_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Party for Peace and Democracy (page does not exist)">Party for Peace and Democracy</a></b> (<i>P'yonghwa Minjudang</i>)</li>
<li>1991: The party is reorganised into the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=New_United_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="New United Democratic Party (page does not exist)">New United Democratic Party</a></b> (<i>Sinminju Yeonhapdang</i>, <i>Sinmindang</i> for short)</li>
<li>1991: The party merges with the ⇒ Democratic Party (1990) and takes the name <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_1991)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 1991) (page does not exist)">Democratic Party (1991)</a></b> (<i>Minjudang</i>)</li>
<li>1995: Most of the party follows Kim into the ⇒ National Congress for New Politics or Democratic Party (2005), the Democratic Party (1991) disappears</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_Party_.281990.29">Democratic Party (1990)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Democratic Party (1990)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1990: A faction of the ⇒ Democratic Reunification Party forms the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_1990)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 1990) (page does not exist)">Democratic Party (1990)</a></b> (<i>Minjudang</i>)</li>
<li>1991: The party merges with the ⇒ Party for Peace and Democracy into the ⇒ New Democratic Party</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_Party_.281995.29">Democratic Party (1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Democratic Party (1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1995: A faction of the ⇒ Democratic Party (1990) forms the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_1995)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 1995) (page does not exist)">Democratic Party (1995)</a></b></li>
<li>1997: The party merges with the ⇒ National Congress for New Politics</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Congress_for_New_Politics_.281995.29">National Congress for New Politics (1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: National Congress for New Politics (1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>1995: Most of the ⇒ Democratic Party follows <a href="/wiki/Kim_Dae-jung" title="Kim Dae-jung">Kim Dae-jung</a> into the <b><a href="/wiki/National_Congress_for_New_Politics" title="National Congress for New Politics">National Congress for New Politics</a></b> (<i>Saejeongchi Gungminhoeui</i>) and succeeded in 1997 in electing Kim to the presidency of South Korea</li>
<li>2000: The party is merged with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=New_People_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="New People Party (page does not exist)">New People Party</a> to form the <b>Millennium Democratic Party</b> (<i>Sae Cheonnyeon Minjudang</i>, 새천년민주당)</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Millennium_Democratic_Party_to_Democratic_Party_.282000.29">Millennium Democratic Party to Democratic Party (2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Millennium Democratic Party to Democratic Party (2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>2003: After the election of its candidate <a href="/wiki/Roh_Moo-hyun" title="Roh Moo-hyun">Roh Moo-hyun</a> to the presidency, his followers leave the party and formed ⇒ Uri Party</li>
<li>2005: The party is renamed <b><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2000)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2000)">Democratic Party (2000)</a></b> (<i>Min-ju Dang</i>)</li>
<li>2007: the party split into two groups <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2007)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2007)">Democratic Party (2007)</a> and the other group merge with the <a href="/wiki/Uri_Party" title="Uri Party">Uri Party</a></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uri_Party_.282003.29">Uri Party (2003)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Uri Party (2003)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>2003: After the election of its candidate <a href="/wiki/Roh_Moo-hyun" title="Roh Moo-hyun">Roh Moo-hyun</a> to the presidency, his followers leave the Millennium Democratic Party and form the <b><a href="/wiki/Uri_Party" title="Uri Party">Uri Party</a></b> (<i>Yeollin Uri Dang</i>, 열린우리당), sometimes known as "Our Open Party." Lasted until August 19, 2007.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_Party_.282007.29_to_Centrist_Reformists_Democratic_Party">Democratic Party (2007) to Centrist Reformists Democratic Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Democratic Party (2007) to Centrist Reformists Democratic Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>2007:The <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2007)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2007)">Democratic Party (2007)</a> was found after the dissolution of <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2000)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2000)">Democratic Party (2000)</a>.</li>
<li>2007:The party is renamed <b><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2007)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2007)">Centrist Reformists Democratic Party</a></b></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_New_Democratic_Party_.282007.29">United New Democratic Party (2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: United New Democratic Party (2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>2007: Most members of the Uri Party, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Son_Hak-gyu" title="Son Hak-gyu">Son Hak-gyu</a>'s conservative-liberal benches, and a group of the civil movement organizations are united as a new political party called <b>The <a href="/wiki/United_New_Democratic_Party" title="United New Democratic Party">United New Democratic Party</a></b> (<i>Daetonghap Minju Sindang</i>, 대통합민주신당).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Democratic_Party_to_Democratic_Party_.282008.29">United Democratic Party to Democratic Party (2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: United Democratic Party to Democratic Party (2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 February 2008, the UNDP merged with the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2000)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2000)">Democratic Party</a> (민주당), forming the <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Democratic_Party_(Republic_of_Korea)" title="United Democratic Party (Republic of Korea)">United Democratic Party</a></b> (통합민주당). This was four years after the <a href="/wiki/Uri_Party" title="Uri Party">Uri Party</a> (열린우리당)'s split from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennium_Democratic_Party" title="Millennium Democratic Party">Millennium Democratic Party</a> (새천년민주당).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On July 2008 the party is renamed <b><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2008)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2008)">Democratic Party (2008)</a></b> (<i>Min-ju Dang</i>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_United_Party_to_Democratic_Party_.282011.29">Democratic United Party to Democratic Party (2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Democratic United Party to Democratic Party (2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 16, 2011, with the unity of <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2008)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2008)">Democratic Party</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_United_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Citizens United Party (page does not exist)">Citizens United Party</a> and cooperation of <a href="/wiki/Korean_Confederation_of_Trade_Unions" title="Korean Confederation of Trade Unions">Korean Confederation of Trade Unions</a>, formed <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_United_Party" title="Democratic United Party">Democratic United Party</a></b>. In 2013 the party is renamed <b><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2011)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2011)">Democratic Party</a></b> (민주당), while changed its official color from green to blue, the first time in 60 years.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Politics_Alliance_for_Democracy_to_The_Minjoo_Party_of_Korea">New Politics Alliance for Democracy to The Minjoo Party of Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_in_South_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: New Politics Alliance for Democracy to The Minjoo Party of Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 March 2014, the Democratic Party (2011) merged with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Political_Vision_Party" title="New Political Vision Party">New Political Vision Party</a> (새정치연합), forming the <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Politics_Alliance_for_Democracy" title="New Politics Alliance for Democracy">New Politics Alliance for Democracy</a></b> (<i>Saejeongchi Minju Yeonhap</i>, 새정치민주연합). In 2015 the party is renamed <b><a href="/wiki/Minjoo_Party_of_Korea" title="Minjoo Party of Korea">Minjoo Party of Korea</a></b> (더불어민주당). After merging <a href="/wiki/Minjoo_Party_(2014)" title="Minjoo Party (2014)">Minjoo Party (2014)</a> on 19 October 2016, the party's English name was changed into <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Korea" title="Democratic Party of Korea">Democratic Party of Korea</a>.</b></p>
<p>On 10 January 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Ahn_Cheol-soo" title="Ahn Cheol-soo">Ahn Cheol-soo</a> fraction of the split of the MPK to established the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(South_Korea)" title="People's Party (South Korea)">People's Party</a>.</p>
