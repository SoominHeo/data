<p>On 27 July 1989, a <a href="/wiki/Korean_Air" title="Korean Air">Korean Air</a> <a href="/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas_DC-10" title="McDonnell Douglas DC-10">DC-10</a> crashed while attempting to land in <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>, Libya. 75 of the 199 passengers and crew on board plus 4 persons on the ground were killed in the crash.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">Accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korean_Air_Flight_803&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Flight 803 was a scheduled International passenger service from <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> with intermediate stops in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeddah" title="Jeddah">Jeddah</a>, Saudi Arabia. While on final approach to Tripoli, the aircraft crashed into an orchard approximately 1.5 miles short of the runway 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The weather at the time of the crash consisted of heavy fog and visibility was between 100 and 800 feet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Korean Air Flight 803 was the second aviation disaster involving a DC-10 in less than two weeks. <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines_Flight_232" title="United Airlines Flight 232">United Airlines Flight 232</a> had crashed on 19 July 1989 while attempting an emergency landing in <a href="/wiki/Sioux_City,_Iowa" title="Sioux City, Iowa">Sioux City, Iowa</a> with 111 of 296 persons on board losing their lives.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korean_Air_Flight_803&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the crash Flight 803's pilot Kim Ho-jung was quoted as saying- "The airport was shrouded in dense fog and visibility was poor when I approached. I lost contact with the control tower for 15 minutes before the crash."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Libya's official news agency <a href="/wiki/Jamahiriya_News_Agency" title="Jamahiriya News Agency">JANA</a> reported that a Soviet airliner one hour before Flight 803 had rerouted to <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> rather than land in the fog.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Also the instrument landing system at Tripoli International Airport wasn't working at the time of the crash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>A Libyan court found the pilot and co-pilot guilty of neglect in December 1990. They were given prison sentences of two years and eighteen months respectively. In the case of the co-pilot the sentence was suspended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cause">Cause</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korean_Air_Flight_803&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cause">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cause of the crash was determined to be crew error in attempting a descent below decision height without the runway environment in sight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
