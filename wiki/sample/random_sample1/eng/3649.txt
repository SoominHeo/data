<p>The <b>Battle of Korea Strait</b> was a small naval battle fought on the first day of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, 25-26 June 1950, between the navies of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. A North Korean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Troop_transport" title="Troop transport">troop transport</a> carrying hundreds of soldiers attempted to land its cargo near <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a> but was encountered by a South Korean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patrol_ship" title="Patrol ship">patrol ship</a> and sunk. It was one of the first surface actions of the war and resulted in an important South Korean victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J._Marolda_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-J._Marolda-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NavSource_PC-823_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NavSource_PC-823-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle">Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Korea_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Korean War began with a massive North Korean invasion of South Korea which nearly overran the country. During the invasion, the <a href="/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Navy" title="Korean People's Navy">Korean People's Navy</a> was assigned to insert troops along the peninsula's southeastern coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NavSource_PC-823_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NavSource_PC-823-2">[2]</a></sup> The North Korean steamer was one of the vessels. Formerly an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> merchant ship of 1,000 tons, she was armed with <a href="/wiki/Machine_gun" title="Machine gun">machine guns</a> and loaded with 600 soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/766th_Independent_Infantry_Regiment_(North_Korea)" title="766th Independent Infantry Regiment (North Korea)">766th Independent Infantry Regiment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rottman171_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rottman171-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was early morning when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Korean_Navy" title="South Korean Navy">South Korean Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Submarine_chaser" title="Submarine chaser">submarine chaser</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ROKS_Baekdusan_(PC_701)" title="ROKS Baekdusan (PC 701)">ROKS <i>Bak Du San</i></a> spotted the lone enemy steamer eighteen miles from Busan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rottman171_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rottman171-3">[3]</a></sup> The chaser was the lead vessel of the South Korean Navy and was also formerly an American ship, built in 1944 as USS PC-823, decommissioned after World war II and transferred to the Merchant Marine Academy as "Ensign Whitehead" and finally sold to South Korea in 1949.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pak_Tu_San_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pak_Tu_San-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Bak Du San</i> first challenged the steamer with signal lights and received no response. But when the South Koreans turned on their searchlights, the North Koreans opened fire, hitting the <i>Bak Du San</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s bridge. The <a href="/wiki/Helmsman" title="Helmsman">helmsman</a> was killed and the <a href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_deck" title="Officer of the deck">officer of the deck</a> seriously wounded. <i>Bak Du San</i> returned fire with her main 3-inch <a href="/wiki/Anti-aircraft_warfare" title="Anti-aircraft warfare">anti-aircraft gun</a> and six .50-caliber machine guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pak_Tu_San_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pak_Tu_San-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the North Koreans began taking hits, the ship attempted to flee the engagement. In a running battle the <i>Bak Du San</i> chased the enemy steamer down and it sank near <a href="/wiki/Tsushima_Island" title="Tsushima Island">Tsushima Island</a> with heavy loss of life. The victory, though unintended, was a major strategic gain. Busan was vital but only lightly defended and if it had fallen the North Koreans would have been one step closer to completely overrunning the country.</p>
