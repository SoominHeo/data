<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Aschelminth</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Aschelminthes</b> (also known as <b>Aeschelminthes</b> "Nemathelminthes" "Nematodes"), closely associated with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platyhelminthes" title="Platyhelminthes">Platyhelminthes</a>, are an obsolete phylum of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pseudocoelomate" title="Pseudocoelomate">pseudocoelomate</a> and other similar animals that are no longer considered closely related and have been promoted to phyla in their own right. The term <b>Aschelminth</b> is now generally only used as an informal name for any member of the approximately ten different <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrate</a> phyla formerly included within Aschelminthes.</p>
<p>It is considered a polyphyletic group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid8524046_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid8524046-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subdivisions">Subdivisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aschelminth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Subdivisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although invertebrate experts<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> do not necessarily agree on these categorizations, groups that are generally incorporated into Aschelminthes include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Acanthocephala" title="Acanthocephala">Acanthocephala</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chaetognatha" title="Chaetognatha">Chaetognatha</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cycliophora" title="Cycliophora">Cycliophora</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gastrotrich" title="Gastrotrich">Gastrotricha</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kinorhyncha" title="Kinorhyncha">Kinorhyncha</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Loricifera" title="Loricifera">Loricifera</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nematoda" title="Nematoda">Nematoda</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nematomorpha" title="Nematomorpha">Nematomorpha</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Priapulida" title="Priapulida">Priapulida</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rotifer" title="Rotifer">Rotifera</a></li>
</ul>
<p>In addition, <a href="/wiki/Entoprocta" title="Entoprocta">Entoprocta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tardigrade" title="Tardigrade">Tardigrada</a> are sometimes included.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
