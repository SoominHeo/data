<p><b>Daniel Davis Blocker</b> (December 10, 1928 – May 13, 1972) was an American television actor and <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> veteran. He is best remembered for his role as Hoss Cartwright in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Western_fiction" title="Western fiction">western</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Bonanza" title="Bonanza">Bonanza</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Blocker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Blocker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blocker was born <b>Bobby Dan Davis Blocker</b> in <a href="/wiki/De_Kalb,_Texas" title="De Kalb, Texas">De Kalb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bowie_County,_Texas" title="Bowie County, Texas">Bowie County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, son of Ora "Shack" Blocker. (1895–1960) and his wife Mary Arizona Blocker, née Davis (1901–1998). The family moved to <a href="/wiki/O%27Donnell,_Texas" title="O'Donnell, Texas">O'Donnell</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Lubbock,_Texas" title="Lubbock, Texas">Lubbock</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Texas" title="West Texas">West Texas</a>, where they operated a store.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Former_Blocker_Store,_O%27Donnell,_Texas_IMG_1497.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3240" data-file-width="4320" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Former_Blocker_Store%2C_O%27Donnell%2C_Texas_IMG_1497.JPG/200px-Former_Blocker_Store%2C_O%27Donnell%2C_Texas_IMG_1497.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Former_Blocker_Store%2C_O%27Donnell%2C_Texas_IMG_1497.JPG/300px-Former_Blocker_Store%2C_O%27Donnell%2C_Texas_IMG_1497.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Former_Blocker_Store%2C_O%27Donnell%2C_Texas_IMG_1497.JPG/400px-Former_Blocker_Store%2C_O%27Donnell%2C_Texas_IMG_1497.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a>

<p>As a boy he attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texas_Military_Institute" title="Texas Military Institute">Texas Military Institute</a>, and in 1946 played football at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Baptist" title="Southern Baptist">Southern Baptist</a>-affiliated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hardin-Simmons_University" title="Hardin-Simmons University">Hardin-Simmons University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Abilene,_Texas" title="Abilene, Texas">Abilene</a>, Texas. In 1947, he transferred to and later graduated from <a href="/wiki/Sul_Ross_State_University" title="Sul Ross State University">Sul Ross State Teacher's College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alpine,_Texas" title="Alpine, Texas">Alpine</a>, Texas, where he was a star football player and then went on to earn a master's degree in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dramatic_arts" title="Dramatic arts">dramatic arts</a>.</p>
<p>Blocker was a high school English and drama teacher in <a href="/wiki/Sonora,_Texas" title="Sonora, Texas">Sonora</a>, Texas; a sixth-grade teacher and coach at Eddy Elementary School in <a href="/wiki/Carlsbad,_New_Mexico" title="Carlsbad, New Mexico">Carlsbad</a>, New Mexico; and then a teacher in California. He had worked as a <a href="/wiki/Rodeo" title="Rodeo">rodeo</a> performer and as a <a href="/wiki/Bouncer_(doorman)" title="Bouncer (doorman)">bouncer</a> in a beer bar while a student. He is remembered from his school days for his size of 6 ft 4 in (1.93 m) and weight of 300 pounds (140 kilograms; 21 stone 6 pounds), and for being good-natured despite his intimidating size.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._Army">U.S. Army</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Blocker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: U.S. Army">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blocker was <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">drafted</a> into the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> during the Korean War. He had basic training at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Polk" title="Fort Polk">Fort Polk</a>, Louisiana. He served as an <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> <a href="/wiki/Sergeant" title="Sergeant">sergeant</a> in F Company, 2nd Battalion, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/179th_Infantry_Regiment" title="179th Infantry Regiment">179th Infantry Regiment</a>, <a href="/wiki/45th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="45th Infantry Division (United States)">45th Infantry Division</a> in Korea, December 1951 to August 1952. He received a <a href="/wiki/Purple_Heart" title="Purple Heart">Purple Heart</a> for wounds in combat according to the June–July 2013 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Veterans_of_Foreign_Wars" title="Veterans of Foreign Wars">VFW Magazine</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blocker received the following military awards: the <a href="/wiki/Purple_Heart" title="Purple Heart">Purple Heart</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Defense_Service_Medal" title="National Defense Service Medal">National Defense Service Medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korean_Service_Medal" title="Korean Service Medal">Korean Service Medal</a> with two <a href="/wiki/Service_star" title="Service star"><span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>⁄<sub>16</sub></span>" bronze campaign stars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Presidential_Unit_Citation" title="Republic of Korea Presidential Unit Citation">Republic of Korea Presidential Unit Citation</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Service_Medal" title="United Nations Service Medal">United Nations Service Medal</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War_Service_Medal" title="Korean War Service Medal">Korean War Service Medal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Combat_Infantryman_Badge" title="Combat Infantryman Badge">Combat Infantryman Badge</a>.</p>

<p>In 1957, Blocker appeared in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Three_Stooges" title="Three Stooges">Three Stooges</a> short, <i><a href="/wiki/Outer_Space_Jitters" title="Outer Space Jitters">Outer Space Jitters</a></i>, having portrayed the part of "The Goon," billed as "Don Blocker". Dan Blocker made two appearances on the long-running <i><a href="/wiki/Gunsmoke" title="Gunsmoke">Gunsmoke</a></i> series: the first on August 25, 1956, in "Alarm at Pleasant Valley" and the second on October 18, 1958, in "Thoroughbreds". He also appeared in 1957 as Will in the episode, "A Time to Die" of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Brothers" title="Warner Brothers">Warner Brothers</a> western series, <i><a href="/wiki/Colt_.45_(TV_series)" title="Colt .45 (TV series)">Colt .45</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Wayde_Preston" title="Wayde Preston">Wayde Preston</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blocker was cast in 1957 in episodes of the <a href="/wiki/David_Dortort" title="David Dortort">David Dortort</a>-produced NBC series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Restless_Gun" title="The Restless Gun">The Restless Gun</a></i> as a <a href="/wiki/Blacksmith" title="Blacksmith">blacksmith</a> and as a cattleman planning to take his hard-earned profit to return to his family land in his native <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>. That same year, he had at least two roles as a <a href="/wiki/Bartender" title="Bartender">bartender</a> in an episode of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_syndication" title="Television syndication">syndicated</a> western-themed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crime_drama" title="Crime drama">crime drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Cochise" title="Sheriff of Cochise">Sheriff of Cochise</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/John_Bromfield" title="John Bromfield">John Bromfield</a> and in the film, <i>Gunsight Ridge</i>. Also in 1957, he appeared in an episode, "Land Beyond the Law", of <i>Cheyenne</i>, playing one of the outlaw minions (Pete). He also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rifleman" title="The Rifleman">The Rifleman</a></i>. In 1958, he played a prison guard and later had a recurring role as Tiny Budinger in the NBC western series <i><a href="/wiki/Cimarron_City_(TV_series)" title="Cimarron City (TV series)">Cimarron City</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/George_Montgomery_(actor)" title="George Montgomery (actor)">George Montgomery</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Smith_(actor)" title="John Smith (actor)">John Smith</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Totter" title="Audrey Totter">Audrey Totter</a>. He also was seen in "The Señorita Makes a Choice," a 1958 episode of <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Zorro_(1957_TV_series)" title="Zorro (1957 TV series)">Zorro</a></i> series.</p>
<p>In 1958, Blocker had a supporting role as Sergeant Broderick in "The Dora Gray Story" on NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Wagon_Train" title="Wagon Train">Wagon Train</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Linda_Darnell" title="Linda Darnell">Linda Darnell</a> in the title role and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Connors" title="Mike Connors">Mike Connors</a> as Miles Borden, a corrupt <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> lieutenant at an isolated western fort.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> That same year, he appeared in "Stagecoach Episode" of the NBC western, <i><a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Drum" title="Jefferson Drum">Jefferson Drum</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Richards_(baseball_player/actor)" title="Jeff Richards (baseball player/actor)">Jeff Richards</a>.</p>
<p>Blocker was cast as bearded poker-playing rodeo performer, Cloudy Sims, in the 1958 episode "Rodeo" on the <a href="/wiki/David_Janssen" title="David Janssen">David Janssen</a> crime drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_Diamond,_Private_Detective" title="Richard Diamond, Private Detective">Richard Diamond, Private Detective</a></i>. In the storyline, a rodeo performer named Ed Murdock, portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Van_Cleef" title="Lee Van Cleef">Lee Van Cleef</a>, is murdered before he can make his final performance at the annual event in <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1959, as <i>Bonanza</i> began its long network run, Blocker guest-starred in an episode of the <a href="/wiki/Keenan_Wynn" title="Keenan Wynn">Keenan Wynn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Mathias" title="Bob Mathias">Bob Mathias</a> NBC series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Troubleshooters_(1959_TV_series)" title="The Troubleshooters (1959 TV series)">The Troubleshooters</a></i>, an adventure program about unusual events surrounding an international construction company. Another 1959 role was as Del Pierce in "Johnny Yuma", the first episode of the ABC western series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rebel_(TV_series)" title="The Rebel (TV series)">The Rebel</a></i>, starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Adams_(actor)" title="Nick Adams (actor)">Nick Adams</a>.</p>

<p>Blocker's big break also came in 1959, when he was cast as Eric "Hoss" Cartwright<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dan_Blocker_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dan_Blocker-6">[6]</a></sup> on the long-running NBC television series <i><a href="/wiki/Bonanza" title="Bonanza">Bonanza</a></i>, and played the role until his death. Blocker said he portrayed the Hoss character with a <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Grellet" title="Stephen Grellet">Stephen Grellet</a> excerpt in mind: "We shall pass this way on Earth but once, if there is any kindness we can show, or good act we can do, let us do it now, for we will never pass this way again."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bfr2007_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bfr2007-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1963 Blocker starred with <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Come_Blow_Your_Horn_(film)" title="Come Blow Your Horn (film)">Come Blow Your Horn</a></i>, and worked with Sinatra again in 1968 in the "Tony Rome" film sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_in_Cement" title="Lady in Cement">Lady in Cement</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Kubrick" title="Stanley Kubrick">Stanley Kubrick</a> attempted to cast Blocker in his film <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Strangelove" title="Dr. Strangelove">Dr. Strangelove</a></i>, after <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sellers" title="Peter Sellers">Peter Sellers</a> elected not to add the role of Major T.J. "King" Kong to his multiple other roles, but according to the film's co-writer, <a href="/wiki/Terry_Southern" title="Terry Southern">Terry Southern</a>, Blocker's agent rejected the script. The role subsequently went to <a href="/wiki/Slim_Pickens" title="Slim Pickens">Slim Pickens</a>, who played the iconic scene of riding an atomic bomb down while waving his cowboy hat. In 1968, Blocker starred as John Killibrew, a blacksmith, who had convinced a number of settlers to follow him to California and founded the town of Arkana. This TV movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Something_for_a_Lonely_Man" title="Something for a Lonely Man">Something for a Lonely Man</a></i>, also featured <a href="/wiki/Susan_Clark" title="Susan Clark">Susan Clark</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Dehner" title="John Dehner">John Dehner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_Oates" title="Warren Oates">Warren Oates</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Don_Stroud" title="Don Stroud">Don Stroud</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1970, Blocker portrayed a love-shy <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/galoot" title="wikt:galoot">galoot</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cockeyed_Cowboys_of_Calico_County" title="Cockeyed Cowboys of Calico County">Cockeyed Cowboys of Calico County</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Nanette_Fabray" title="Nanette Fabray">Nanette Fabray</a> as a love prospect and a supporting cast featuring <a href="/wiki/Jim_Backus" title="Jim Backus">Jim Backus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Elam" title="Jack Elam">Jack Elam</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noah_Beery,_Jr." title="Noah Beery, Jr.">Noah Beery, Jr.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Rooney" title="Mickey Rooney">Mickey Rooney</a>. Blocker also appeared on NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flip_Wilson_Show" title="The Flip Wilson Show">The Flip Wilson Show</a></i> comedy hour.</p>
<p>Director <a href="/wiki/Robert_Altman" title="Robert Altman">Robert Altman</a> befriended Blocker while directing episodes of <i>Bonanza</i>. Years later, he cast Blocker as Roger Wade in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Long_Goodbye_(film)" title="The Long Goodbye (film)">The Long Goodbye</a></i>, but Blocker died before filming began. The role went to <a href="/wiki/Sterling_Hayden" title="Sterling Hayden">Sterling Hayden</a>, and the film was dedicated to Blocker.</p>
<p>Blocker started and received partial ownership in a successful chain of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ponderosa/Bonanza_Steakhouse" title="Ponderosa/Bonanza Steakhouse">Ponderosa/Bonanza Steakhouse</a> restaurants in exchange for serving (in character as Hoss) as their commercial spokesman and making personal appearances at franchises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dan_Blocker_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dan_Blocker-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Blocker was a <a href="/wiki/Free_Methodist_Church" title="Free Methodist Church">Free Methodist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He married Dolphia Parker, whom he had met while a student at Sul Ross State University. All of their four children's names begin with a 'D': Hollywood actor <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Blocker" title="Dirk Blocker">Dirk Blocker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">Hollywood producer</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Blocker" title="David Blocker">David Blocker</a>, and twin daughters Debra Lee (artist) and Danna Lynn. David Blocker won a 1998 Emmy for producing "Don King: Only in America."</p>
<p>Blocker was among <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a> celebrities who supported <a href="/wiki/Pat_Brown" title="Pat Brown">Pat Brown</a>'s re-election in 1966 as <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_California" title="Governor of California">governor of California</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ronald_W._Reagan" title="Ronald W. Reagan">Ronald W. Reagan</a>. In 1968, Blocker backed then-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Senator" title="U.S. Senator">U.S. Senator</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eugene_J._McCarthy" title="Eugene J. McCarthy">Eugene J. McCarthy</a> of Minnesota for the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> presidential nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bfr2007_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bfr2007-7">[7]</a></sup> Blocker later supported the eventual Democratic Party nominee, Vice President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hubert_H._Humphrey" title="Hubert H. Humphrey">Hubert H. Humphrey</a>, also of Minnesota, for the presidency against the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_M._Nixon" title="Richard M. Nixon">Richard M. Nixon</a>. He kept a house in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood</a>, California, and commuted to NBC. His 6,000-square-foot (560 m<sup>2</sup>) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tudorbethan_architecture" title="Tudorbethan architecture">Tudor</a>-style mansion in the <a href="/wiki/Hancock_Park,_Los_Angeles" title="Hancock Park, Los Angeles">Hancock Park, Los Angeles</a> area of Los Angeles is currently owned by <a href="/wiki/Rob_Zombie" title="Rob Zombie">Rob Zombie</a>.</p>
<p>On the 2010 <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> special, "Pioneers of Television: Westerns", actor <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Vogel" title="Mitch Vogel">Mitch Vogel</a>, who played the young brother, Jamie Cartwright, on <i>Bonanza</i>, said Blocker, "was so easy to get to know—the kind of guy you could go and have a beer with."</p>
<p>Blocker, a performance automobile fan, once owned a 1965 <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Chevelle" title="Chevrolet Chevelle">Chevrolet Chevelle</a> SS396 "Z-16" (RPO Z16 option) as <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet" title="Chevrolet">Chevrolet</a> was the commercial <a href="/wiki/Sponsor_(commercial)" title="Sponsor (commercial)">sponsor</a> of the show. He also owned a 1965 Huffaker Genie MK10 race car, nicknamed the "Vinegaroon". The car was run by Nickey Chevrolet in the 1965 and 1966 U.S. Road Racing Championship series, as well as the 1966 Can-Am championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Blocker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 13, 1972, Blocker died in Los Angeles at age 43 of a <a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_embolism" title="Pulmonary embolism">pulmonary embolism</a> following gall bladder surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The writers of <i>Bonanza</i> took the unusual step of referencing a major character's death in the show's storyline that autumn. <i>Bonanza</i> lasted another season without Hoss, as the 14th and final season ended on January 16, 1973. Blocker's remains are interred in a family plot in Woodsmen Cemetery, in <a href="/wiki/De_Kalb,_Texas" title="De Kalb, Texas">De Kalb, Texas</a>, although he had lived there only briefly. The common grave site is marked by a plain stone with the name "BLOCKER" engraved; three family members are buried beside him.</p>
<p>A "Dan Blocker Room" is on the second floor of the <a href="/wiki/O%27Donnell_Heritage_Museum" title="O'Donnell Heritage Museum">O'Donnell Heritage Museum</a> in O'Donnell, Texas, where he was brought up.</p>
