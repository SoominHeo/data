<p><i><b>Innocent Steps</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">댄서의 순정</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani">댄서의 純情</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Daenseoui sunjeong</span></i>; lit. "Dancer Purity") is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_South_Korean_films_of_2005" title="List of South Korean films of 2005">2005 South Korean film</a> directed by Park Young-hoon. Another English title for the movie is "Dancing princess".</p>
<p>There's a director's cut version of the movie featuring additional 17 minutes of footage and alternative cut of the dance scene at competition.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Innocent_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Former acclaimed dancer Na Young-sae (<a href="/wiki/Park_Gun-hyung" title="Park Gun-hyung">Park Gun-hyung</a>) attempts to make a comeback after his opponent, Hyun-soo (<a href="/wiki/Yoon_Chan" title="Yoon Chan">Yoon Chan</a>), purposely injures him at a dance competition. At the suggestion of dance studio manager Ma Sang-doo (<a href="/wiki/Park_Won-sang" title="Park Won-sang">Park Won-sang</a>), Young-sae then brings to Korea Jang Chae-min (<a href="/wiki/Moon_Geun-young" title="Moon Geun-young">Moon Geun-young</a>), an ethnic Korean from China whom he presumes is a renowned, talented dancer. To his surprise, Young-sae learns Chae-ryn knows nothing about dancing and her soon-to-be married, older sister, Jang Chae-min, is the talented dancer. With only three months until the national dance championship, Young-sae trains Chae-min, vowing to turn her into a world-class dancer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Innocent_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film received mixed to negative reviews. <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> reviewer, Derek Elley favorably compared the film to <i><a href="/wiki/Dance_with_the_Wind" title="Dance with the Wind">Dance with the Wind</a></i>, citing Moon Geun-young and Park Gun-hyung's performances, but wrote "the plot holds no water."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Koreanfilm.org</i> critic Tom Giammarco called the film "disappointing and cliche,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and Darcy Paquet credited the film's box office success to Moon's celebrity status and noted that the ending was disappointing: "We never even really get to see the knock-em-dead dance sequence that you'd expect."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remake">Remake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Innocent_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Remake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2015, Culture Cap Korea announced that it will co-produce a Chinese <a href="/wiki/Remake" title="Remake">remake</a>, which will cast a Chinese actor and a Korean actress. 60% of filming will take place in China, and 40% in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
