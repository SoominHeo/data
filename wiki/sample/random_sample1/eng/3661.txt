<p><i><b>Dance Dance Revolution Mario Mix</b></i>, known as <i><b>Dance Dance Revolution with Mario</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ダンスダンスレボリューション ウィズ マリオ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Dansu Dansu Reboryūshon Uizu Mario</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <i><b>Dancing Stage Mario Mix</b></i> in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, is a 2005 <a href="/wiki/Music_video_game" title="Music video game">music video game</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">Konami</a> and published by <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> for the <a href="/wiki/GameCube" title="GameCube">GameCube</a>. It is the first <i><a href="/wiki/Dance_Dance_Revolution" title="Dance Dance Revolution">Dance Dance Revolution</a></i> game to be released on a Nintendo <a href="/wiki/Video_game_console" title="Video game console">video game console</a> outside Japan.</p>
<p><i>Dance Dance Revolution Mario Mix</i> predominantly features characters, <a href="/wiki/Video_game_music" title="Video game music">music</a>, and locations from the <a href="/wiki/Mario_(franchise)" title="Mario (franchise)"><i>Mario</i> franchise</a>. The game was bundled with the <a href="/wiki/Dance_pad" title="Dance pad">dance pad</a> controller.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dance_Dance_Revolution#Gameplay" title="Dance Dance Revolution">Dance Dance Revolution Gameplay</a></div>
<p>Being run on a modified engine of <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Party_6" title="Mario Party 6">Mario Party 6</a></i>, the gameplay of <i>Dance Dance Revolution Mario Mix</i> follows the formula established in all <i><a href="/wiki/Dance_Dance_Revolution" title="Dance Dance Revolution">Dance Dance Revolution</a></i> games.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dance_Dance_Revolution_Mario_Mix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game opens with <a href="/wiki/Waluigi" title="Waluigi">Waluigi</a> stealing the four Music Keys, who can grant wishes, from Truffle Towers. However, when he tries to open the door to the room containing the Music Keys, three of them scatter across the <a href="/wiki/Mushroom_Kingdom" title="Mushroom Kingdom">Mushroom Kingdom</a>, and the fourth one is kept by Waluigi. From a distance, <a href="/wiki/Toad_(Nintendo)" title="Toad (Nintendo)">Toad</a> watches these events unfold and rushes to tell Mario or Luigi, depending on which character the player chose, who then rushes off to retrieve the missing Music Keys.</p>
<p>The keys are recovered by completing tasks for other characters who have found the scattered keys and then defeating them in a dance challenge. These characters are, in order, Waluigi, <a href="/wiki/Lakitu" title="Lakitu">Pirate Lakitu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enemies_in_the_Mario_series#Blooper" title="Enemies in the Mario series">Blooper</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enemies_in_the_Mario_series#Hammer_Bro." title="Enemies in the Mario series">Hammer Bros.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wario" title="Wario">Wario</a> and Freezie.</p>
<p>Toad and the player's character then return the Music Keys to Truffle Towers. Soon after, <a href="/wiki/Bowser_(character)" title="Bowser (character)">Bowser</a> steals the keys, but is followed by Toad and the player's chosen character. They enter Bowser's Castle to recapture the Music Keys, and are promptly challenged by Bowser. After defeating him in a dance-off, Bowser tells Toad and the player's character that he planned to use the Music Keys to fix his tone deafness. This prompts the player's character to use the Music Keys to turn the area around Bowser's Castle into a green field and induces a feeling to dance in everyone, with Toad realizing that this was how the Music Keys were supposed to be used as the game's ending sequence plays.</p>
