<p><i><b>Dallas</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_time" title="Prime time">prime time</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television</a> <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Cidre" title="Cynthia Cidre">Cynthia Cidre</a> and produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Horizon_Television" title="Warner Horizon Television">Warner Horizon Television</a> that aired on <a href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_channel)" title="TNT (TV channel)">TNT</a> from June 13, 2012, to September 22, 2014. The series was a <a href="/wiki/Revival_(television)" title="Revival (television)">revival</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmh080712_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cmh080712-1">[1]</a></sup>of the <a href="/wiki/Prime_time" title="Prime time">prime time</a> television <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <a href="/wiki/Dallas_(1978_TV_series)" title="Dallas (1978 TV series)">of the same name</a> that aired on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> from 1978 to 1991. The series revolved around the Ewings, a wealthy Dallas family in the oil and cattle-ranching industries.</p>
<p>The series brought back several stars of the original series, including <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Duffy" title="Patrick Duffy">Patrick Duffy</a> as <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Ewing" title="Bobby Ewing">Bobby Ewing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linda_Gray" title="Linda Gray">Linda Gray</a> as <a href="/wiki/Sue_Ellen_Ewing" title="Sue Ellen Ewing">Sue Ellen Ewing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Kercheval" title="Ken Kercheval">Ken Kercheval</a> as <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Barnes" title="Cliff Barnes">Cliff Barnes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Larry_Hagman" title="Larry Hagman">Larry Hagman</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.R._Ewing" title="J.R. Ewing">J.R. Ewing</a>. They were joined by the next generation of characters, including <a href="/wiki/Josh_Henderson" title="Josh Henderson">Josh Henderson</a> as <a href="/wiki/John_Ross_Ewing_III" title="John Ross Ewing III">John Ross Ewing III</a>, the son of J. R. and Sue Ellen Ewing, <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Metcalfe" title="Jesse Metcalfe">Jesse Metcalfe</a> as <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Ewing" title="Christopher Ewing">Christopher Ewing</a>, Bobby and Pam's adopted son, and <a href="/wiki/Julie_Gonzalo" title="Julie Gonzalo">Julie Gonzalo</a> as <a href="/wiki/Pamela_Rebecca_Barnes" title="Pamela Rebecca Barnes">Pamela Rebecca Barnes</a>, the daughter of Cliff Barnes and Afton Cooper.</p>
<p>The series was made for <a href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_channel)" title="TNT (TV channel)">TNT</a>, sister company to <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Television" title="Warner Bros. Television">Warner Bros. Television</a>, which has owned the original series since its purchase of <a href="/wiki/Lorimar_Television" title="Lorimar Television">Lorimar Television</a> (the original show's production company) in 1989. On July 8, 2011, after viewing the completed pilot episode, TNT gave a green light for the series with a 10-episode order,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew110708_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew110708-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time110711_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-time110711-3">[3]</a></sup> which premiered on June 13, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvnum120315_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvnum120315-4">[4]</a></sup> On June 29, 2012, TNT renewed <i>Dallas</i> for a second season consisting of 15 episodes, which premiered on January 28, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wise120629_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-wise120629-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sod120629_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sod120629-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew120629_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew120629-7">[7]</a></sup> On April 30, 2013, TNT renewed <i>Dallas</i> for a third season consisting of 15 episodes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> that premiered on Monday, February 24, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On October 3, 2014, the series was cancelled by TNT after three seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cancellation_.28THR_October_2014.29_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cancellation_.28THR_October_2014.29-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series revolves around the Ewings, a wealthy <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> family in the oil and cattle-ranching industries. It focuses mainly on <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Ewing" title="Christopher Ewing">Christopher Ewing</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jesse_Metcalfe" title="Jesse Metcalfe">Jesse Metcalfe</a>), the son of <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Ewing" title="Bobby Ewing">Bobby</a> (<a href="/wiki/Patrick_Duffy" title="Patrick Duffy">Patrick Duffy</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pamela_Barnes_Ewing" title="Pamela Barnes Ewing">Pamela Barnes Ewing</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peo110202_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-peo110202-13">[13]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Victoria_Principal" title="Victoria Principal">Victoria Principal</a>), and <a href="/wiki/John_Ross_Ewing_III" title="John Ross Ewing III">John Ross Ewing III</a> (<a href="/wiki/Josh_Henderson" title="Josh Henderson">Josh Henderson</a>), the son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.R._Ewing" title="J.R. Ewing">J.R.</a> (<a href="/wiki/Larry_Hagman" title="Larry Hagman">Larry Hagman</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Sue_Ellen_Ewing" title="Sue Ellen Ewing">Sue Ellen Ewing</a> (<a href="/wiki/Linda_Gray" title="Linda Gray">Linda Gray</a>). Both John Ross and Christopher were born during the original series' run and were featured in it as children (although played by different actors). Now grown up, John Ross has become almost a <a href="/wiki/Carbon_copy" title="Carbon copy">carbon copy</a> of his father, bent on oil, money, and power. Christopher, meanwhile, has become a lot like Bobby, in that he is more interested in the upkeep of Southfork Ranch. As an additional point of contention, Christopher is also becoming a player in <a href="/wiki/Alternative_energy" title="Alternative energy">alternative energy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Methane_clathrate" title="Methane clathrate">methane clathrate</a> recovery), thereby eschewing the oil business. However, John Ross is determined to resurrect the Ewings' former position in the oil industry. John Ross states in season one that he is JR's eldest child, which contradicts the storyline in the original series where JR's illegitimate older son James Beaumont appeared in seasons 12–13.</p>
<p>Alongside John Ross and Christopher, original series characters Bobby, JR, and Sue Ellen return as full cast members for the new series. Additional familiar characters, including JR's and Bobby's niece <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucy_Ewing_Cooper" title="Lucy Ewing Cooper">Lucy Ewing Cooper</a> (<a href="/wiki/Charlene_Tilton" title="Charlene Tilton">Charlene Tilton</a>), their half brother <a href="/wiki/Ray_Krebbs" title="Ray Krebbs">Ray Krebbs</a> (<a href="/wiki/Steve_Kanaly" title="Steve Kanaly">Steve Kanaly</a>), and Ewing family rival <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Barnes" title="Cliff Barnes">Cliff Barnes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ken_Kercheval" title="Ken Kercheval">Ken Kercheval</a>) appear occasionally as guest stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg1_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg1-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dspy110915_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-dspy110915-15">[15]</a></sup> Various other actors/characters from the original series also make appearances, including <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Landers" title="Audrey Landers">Audrey Landers</a> (<a href="/wiki/Afton_Cooper" title="Afton Cooper">Afton Cooper</a>), <a href="/wiki/Cathy_Podewell" title="Cathy Podewell">Cathy Podewell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cally_Harper_Ewing" title="Cally Harper Ewing">Cally Harper Ewing</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Deborah_Shelton" title="Deborah Shelton">Deborah Shelton</a> (<a href="/wiki/List_of_Dallas_characters#Other" title="List of Dallas characters">Mandy Winger</a>). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ted_Shackleford" title="Ted Shackleford">Ted Shackleford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joan_Van_Ark" title="Joan Van Ark">Joan Van Ark</a>, who first appeared on <i>Dallas</i> in the late 1970s before joining the spin-off series <i><a href="/wiki/Knots_Landing" title="Knots Landing">Knots Landing</a></i>, also returns as <a href="/wiki/Gary_Ewing" title="Gary Ewing">Gary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valene_Ewing" title="Valene Ewing">Valene Ewing</a>.</p>
<p>New main characters that made their appearances in the first season included Bobby's third wife, <a href="/wiki/Ann_Ewing_(Dallas)" title="Ann Ewing (Dallas)">Ann</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brenda_Strong" title="Brenda Strong">Brenda Strong</a>); Christopher's new wife, originally introduced as 'Rebecca Sutter' but later revealed to be <a href="/wiki/Pamela_Rebecca_Barnes" title="Pamela Rebecca Barnes">Pamela Rebecca Barnes Ewing</a> (<a href="/wiki/Julie_Gonzalo" title="Julie Gonzalo">Julie Gonzalo</a>), the daughter of Cliff Barnes and Afton Cooper; and <a href="/wiki/Elena_Ramos" title="Elena Ramos">Elena Ramos</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jordana_Brewster" title="Jordana Brewster">Jordana Brewster</a>), the daughter of Ewing family cook Carmen Ramos (<a href="/wiki/Marlene_Forte" title="Marlene Forte">Marlene Forte</a>), who is caught in a <a href="/wiki/Love_triangle" title="Love triangle">love triangle</a> with Christopher and John Ross. Harris Ryland (<a href="/wiki/Mitch_Pileggi" title="Mitch Pileggi">Mitch Pileggi</a>) plays Ann's villainous ex-husband. New main characters that made their appearances in the second season included Ann and Harris's daughter, Emma Ryland (<a href="/wiki/Emma_Bell" title="Emma Bell">Emma Bell</a>), and Elena Ramos's brother Drew Ramos (<a href="/wiki/Kuno_Becker" title="Kuno Becker">Kuno Becker</a>). In the second season, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judith_Brown_Ryland" title="Judith Brown Ryland">Judith Brown Ryland</a> (<a href="/wiki/Judith_Light" title="Judith Light">Judith Light</a>) joined the cast as Harris Ryland's controlling mother, while in season three <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Trevi%C3%B1o" title="Nicolas Treviño">Nicolas Treviño</a> (<a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Di_Pace" title="Juan Pablo Di Pace">Juan Pablo Di Pace</a>) joined as a childhood friend of Elena and Drew's who returns to help Cliff Barnes take over the Ewing oil company.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regular_cast">Regular cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regular cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>

R. and Sue Ellen. Ambitious and anxious to prove himself by following in his father's footsteps, he is determined to start drilling for oil at Southfork. In the episode 'Love &amp; Family' he marries Pamela Rebecca Barnes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jesse_Metcalfe" title="Jesse Metcalfe">Jesse Metcalfe</a> as <b><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Ewing" title="Christopher Ewing">Christopher Ewing</a></b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ds2jm_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ds2jm-17">[17]</a></sup> the adopted son of Bobby and his ex-wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pamela_Barnes_Ewing" title="Pamela Barnes Ewing">Pamela Barnes Ewing</a>. The biological son of Sue Ellen's younger sister <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Shepard" title="Kristin Shepard">Kristin Shepard</a>. In the pilot, after spending years in Asia researching alternative energies, Christopher returned to Southfork to get married. In the season 3 finale, he was apparently killed by a <a href="/wiki/Car_bomb" title="Car bomb">car bomb</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jordana_Brewster" title="Jordana Brewster">Jordana Brewster</a> as <b><a href="/wiki/Elena_Ramos" title="Elena Ramos">Elena Ramos</a></b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hit1jb_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-hit1jb-16">[16]</a></sup> the daughter of the Ewing family cook, and childhood friend of Christopher and John Ross, both of whom are in love with her. She has a master's degree in energy resources. In the season three finale, it was revealed that she is pregnant. She was escaping Nicolas with Christopher and was in a filling station bathroom checking a pregnancy test kit when she heard the car exploding, and ran out screaming Christopher's name.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Julie_Gonzalo" title="Julie Gonzalo">Julie Gonzalo</a> as <b><a href="/wiki/Pamela_Rebecca_Barnes" title="Pamela Rebecca Barnes">Pamela Rebecca Barnes</a></b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvl1102d_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvl1102d-18">[18]</a></sup> Under the alias of <b>Rebecca Sutter</b> she marries Christopher in the pilot and was pregnant with twins but miscarried them in "Guilt &amp; Innocence". It is revealed in the season one finale that she is <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Barnes" title="Cliff Barnes">Cliff Barnes</a>'s daughter with <a href="/wiki/Afton_Cooper" title="Afton Cooper">Afton Cooper</a>. In the episode "Love &amp; Family", her marriage to Christopher is finally annulled, and she and John Ross get married at the end of the episode. When she catches John Ross in an illicit affair with Emma Ryland, she purposely overdoses on sleeping pills. When she recovers, she informs him that while their marriage is over, she will not divorce him because they had no prenuptial agreement and she will not allow him to take half her shares in the company. Pamela, knowing that her father Cliff Barnes had ordered the explosion of the rig when he knew she was on board and caused her to lose her babies, got her revenge by visiting him in Mexican prison and giving him the title to Ewing land he coveted but not the pardon he had been given to release him from prison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg080912_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg080912-19">[19]</a></sup></li>

She has assumed the role of matriarch of Southfork while dealing with her ruthless brother-in-law <a href="/wiki/J._R._Ewing" title="J. R. Ewing">J. R. Ewing</a> and her ex-husband Harris Ryland. Most of the third season involved her complicated relationship with her daughter, former mother in law Judith and her mixed feelings for her ex-husband Harris. Note: Strong previously played a very minor character, being Cliff Barne's one-night-stand (episode 19 of season 10, 1987, see film bio of actress for source).</li>

A family man at heart and owner of the Southfork Ranch, Bobby is determined to keep the promise he made to his now-deceased mother: never to allow oil drilling on Southfork.</li>

R.'s ex-wife. Since leaving J. R., Sue Ellen has grown confident and influential with a budding career in politics and ran for governor. She still harbours feelings of guilt for using John Ross in revenge against J.R. during his childhood.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Larry_Hagman" title="Larry Hagman">Larry Hagman</a> as <b><a href="/wiki/J._R._Ewing" title="J. R. Ewing">J. R. A cunning and ruthless oil baron, J. R. has spent his recent years in a nursing home, being treated for clinical depression. Hagman died during production of season 2, signalling the on-screen death of J. R. Ewing. J.R. returned in 2014 in season 3 using unused footage of the character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HanMan_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-HanMan-25">[25]</a></sup></li>

Her birth name is revealed in the second season as <b>Emma Judith Ryland</b>. She starts romances with John Ross and Drew Ramos.</li>

Ruthless, narcissistic and always eager for more power, he has been shown enjoying tormenting his former wife, as well as trying to blackmail Sue Ellen and suing Bobby. It is revealed during the season two finale that Ryland's transport company is also a front for cocaine smuggling where the contraband is pressure molded in the guise of designer knockoff merchandise, in this case, women's shoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bell_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-bell-26">[26]</a></sup> In Season 3, Ryland is revealed as an operative with the CIA. Note: Pileggi previously played a very minor character named Evan Scott Morrisey who was in conflict with J. R. (4 episode appearance from 1989–90, see film bio of actor for source)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kuno_Becker" title="Kuno Becker">Kuno Becker</a> as <b>Drew Ramos</b> (season 3; recurring, season 2; main),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Elena's troubled brother. He witnessed his father's death, which turned him into an angry juvenile delinquent. He ended up enlisting in the military and after a tour in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> straightened him up, he found work on oil rigs all over the globe. He is on the run now after blowing up the Ewing rig. In the episode Denial, Anger, and Acceptance, it is revealed that he started the Southfork fire with an <a href="/wiki/Improvised_explosive_device" title="Improvised explosive device">IED</a> disguised as an air freshener (which served as a heat sensor) and using rocket fuel as a catalyst. He acted out of anger after learning of JR's deception from Elena; however, it must be known that Drew thought no one was home when he set the fire, just as he thought the rig would be unoccupied at the time the bomb went off. At first, Sue Ellen thought she started the fire after drunkenly setting fire to John Ross's wedding invitation and initially took the blame for it. Once Drew learned that Nicolas was using his sister as a means to an end and not because Nicolas truly wanted justice for the Ramos family, Drew threatened to expose him and then is executed by Luis, one of the cartel enforcers. Nicolas gave the order right after the official news that Sue Ellen started the fire, who made Drew's execution looked like an apparent suicide.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Di_Pace" title="Juan Pablo Di Pace">Juan Pablo Di Pace</a> as <b><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Trevi%C3%B1o" title="Nicolas Treviño">Nicolas Treviño</a></b> (season 3), born <b>Joaquin Reyes</b> a childhood friend of Elena and Drew Ramos who becomes a powerful self-made billionaire businessman from Mexico. He comes across as a good, genuine guy, even though there are darker parts of his personality which he's hiding. The episode Denial, Anger, and Acceptance reveals that he is an enforcer with the Mendez-Ochoa drug cartel, initiated into the cartel by its leader El Posolero, recruited into their inner circle, and later sent to Europe to finish his education where he developed his considerable math skills. His intention was to use Elena Ramos as his way into Ewing Global and use it as a front to launder cartel profits. He is later taken into custody by the U.S. Marshals during the season finale; he initially agrees to take down the Mendez-Ochoa cartel by testifying against their members. However, Elena found her brother Drew's St. Christopher medal in Nicolas' bag when she found her cell phone, and knowing that the only way he could have that was if he was present at his brother's murder, she shoots Nicolas with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Browning_Hi_Power" title="Browning Hi Power">Browning Hi Power</a> gun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

(who is dying of cancer) which results in Barnes' imprisonment). Throughout Season 3, he is cohorting with Elena and Nicolas to bring down the Ewings. In Season 3, she also runs an upscale brothel. The season 3 finale has a twist - she ends up collaborating with John Ross (after he had Emma released from the Mendez-Ochoa cartel) to become the Texas Railroad Commissioner after Bobby Ewing had to step down (where he and Sue Ellen regained the shares of Ewing Global).</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Fran_Kranz" title="Fran Kranz">Fran Kranz</a> as <b>Hunter McKay</b>- he's the grandson of original character <a href="/wiki/Carter_McKay" title="Carter McKay">Carter McKay</a>, played by George Kennedy. Hunter works with Nicolas Trevino to get Ewing Global stocks. He succeeds in obtaining the stocks in the episode Victims of Love and later found hanging in his loft (making it look like a suicide where in fact was a professional hit ordered by the Ochoa-Mendez cartel).</li>
<li>Antonio Jaramillo as <b>Luis</b> - he is seen in Season 3 as a Mendez-Ochoa cartel enforcer. In the episode Denial, Anger, and Acceptance, he is the mastermind who executes Drew Ramos while Nicolas becomes complicit (both he and Nicolas made Drew's execution as if he committed suicide). He also masterminds the kidnapping of Ann and Emma where he held them hostage in <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> where El Posolero and Nicolas arranged a meeting where he went rogue - during the season finale where John Ross (who collaborates with a CIA contractor) is about to take back Ewing Global from the cartel, he and the entire entourage are taken into custody by the Mexican Marines - he is executed in prison with El Posolero where his right-hand triggerman (who aligns with Nicolas) orders the hit.</li>
</ul>

<p>The first season premiered on June 13, 2012, and introduces the central characters of the show: John Ross Ewing III, Christopher Ewing, Elena Ramos, Rebecca Sutter, Ann Ewing, Bobby Ewing, Sue Ellen Ewing and J. R. Ewing. The main focus of the first season is the discovery of oil reserves on Southfork by John Ross and attempts by him and his father, J. R. to wrest the land from Bobby. Other storylines in this season include the love triangle between John Ross, Christopher and Elena, Christopher's marriage to Rebecca, Sue Ellen's plans to run for Governor of Texas and Bobby's health problems.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to <i>Dallas</i>, Cidre was best known for producing and writing episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Cane_(TV_series)" title="Cane (TV series)">Cane</a></i>, an American television drama that chronicled the lives and internal power struggles of a powerful and wealthy Cuban-American family running an immensely successful rum and sugar cane business in <a href="/wiki/South_Florida" title="South Florida">South Florida</a>. In 2010, TNT announced it would order a pilot for the continuation of the <i>Dallas</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvs100908_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvs100908-37">[37]</a></sup> The pilot was filmed in and around the city of <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> in early 2011. Production began in late August 2011 in Dallas on the remaining nine episodes in the first season order, based in studios constructed for the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Guys_(2010_TV_series)" title="The Good Guys (2010 TV series)">The Good Guys</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-up110711_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-up110711-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Executive producer Cynthia Cidre wrote the pilot script, while Michael M. Robin served as the director and executive producer for the pilot. David Jacobs reviewed Cidre's pilot script and gave his blessing to the new series though he has chosen not to participate in its production. A dispute erupted when the opening credits were originally planned to read "Developed by Cynthia Cidre, based on Dallas created by David Jacobs". But upon the determination of the <a href="/wiki/WGA_screenwriting_credit_system" title="WGA screenwriting credit system">Writers Guild of America's screenwriting credit system</a>, there are currently two separate credits: one listing Jacobs as the show's sole creator and another listing Cidre as the new show's developer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ForbesWritingCredits_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-ForbesWritingCredits-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>A sneak preview of the series, including clips from the pilot episode, aired on July 11, 2011, during an episode of TNT's <i><a href="/wiki/Rizzoli_%26_Isles" title="Rizzoli &amp; Isles">Rizzoli &amp; Isles</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time110711_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-time110711-3">[3]</a></sup> Patrick Duffy stated that the new show is "exactly the same [as the old show], but it's 2012. We consider this year 14 of the show. It's exactly as if [viewers] forgot which channel we were on."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leno20120612_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-leno20120612-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Continuity">Continuity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Continuity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The new series is a continuation of the old series following a 20-year break, during which the characters and their relationships continued unseen until today when the new series begins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> It does not take the events of the reunion TV movies <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas:_J.R._Returns" title="Dallas: J.R. Returns">Dallas: J.R. Returns</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas:_War_of_the_Ewings" title="Dallas: War of the Ewings">Dallas: War of the Ewings</a></i> into account. Instead, we find the characters having evolved over the last 20 years. Cynthia Cidre, show developer, has confirmed that the new series does not pick up from where the TV movies left off because the movies had tried to resolve lingering plotlines in less than two hours. It continues from the events of the 14th season, their development and consequences extrapolated to 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uldal11a_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-uldal11a-42">[42]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_crew">Production crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Production crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cynthia Cidre, Bruce Rasmussen, Michael M. Robin, Ken Topolsky and Bryan J. Raber served as executive producers for the show. Rasmussen had previously worked as the supervising producer with the hit TV series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roseanne_(TV_series)" title="Roseanne (TV series)">Roseanne</a></i>, for which he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe</a>.</p>
<p>In the first two seasons, <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Bochco" title="Jesse Bochco">Jesse Bochco</a> and Michael M. Robin were the most prolific directors, each directing five episodes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Unlike the original series, which did limited location shooting in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> but was filmed primarily in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, principal photography for the new series takes place in and around <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>. The new series also did location shooting at the actual <a href="/wiki/Southfork_Ranch" title="Southfork Ranch">Southfork Ranch</a> in the northern Dallas suburb of <a href="/wiki/Parker,_Texas" title="Parker, Texas">Parker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt120722_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt120722-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wfaa120712_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-wfaa120712-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Opening_sequence">Opening sequence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Opening sequence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The opening sequence features a shortened version of the original theme music, and echoes the original series opening with modernized shots of Dallas in sliding panels. Unlike the original series, the actors are not listed alphabetically and, for seasons 1 and 2, there are no images of the actors seen in the credits. Josh Henderson and Jesse Metcalfe alternate top billing, and the original stars are credited at the end ("with Patrick Duffy", "and Linda Gray", "and Larry Hagman as J. R. Ewing" until Hagman's death in season 2. The <i>Dallas</i> logo scrolls from left to right, rather than zooming upwards as it did on the original series. The sequence ends on a shot with the camera flying towards Southfork similar to the shot in the original titles where the camera flies over the gate towards Southfork. The season 3 titles feature the return of the iconic threeway split-screen opening, similar to those used in the original series for its first 11 years, with moving images of the actors. In addition, the <i>Dallas</i> season 3 logo zooms towards the screen as it did on the original series.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dallas_(2012_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Advance screening reviews of the series were generally positive from critics on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacr12_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-metacr12-45">[45]</a></sup> On June 29, 2012, TNT renewed <i>Dallas</i> for a second season consisting of 15 episodes, which premiered on January 28, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wise120629_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-wise120629-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sod120629_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-sod120629-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew120629_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ew120629-7">[7]</a></sup> The second season received positive notice, with a score of 82/100 from reviews on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
