<p><b>Murder Inc. Records</b> (formerly <b>The Inc. Records</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murder_Inc_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_and_success_.281999.E2.80.932003.29">Formation and success (1999–2003)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murder_Inc_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation and success (1999–2003)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After helping <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> with the success of <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a> gave Irv Gotti his own label under Def Jam. While watching <i><a href="/wiki/Biography_(TV_series)" title="Biography (TV series)">Biography</a></i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26E_(TV_channel)" title="A&amp;E (TV channel)">A&amp;E</a> during gangster week a Murder Inc. logo appeared on the screen which gave Irv Gotti an idea what to call his label. He got the inspiration because Murder Inc. put out hits and Irv wanted to put out hit records. In 1999, Murder Inc. released its first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Venni_Vetti_Vecci" title="Venni Vetti Vecci">Venni Vetti Vecci</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>. The album featured the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Holla_Holla" title="Holla Holla">Holla Holla</a>" and soon went platinum, which made Ja Rule and Murder Inc. among the most popular labels in the industry, respectively.</p>
<p>In 2000, the label began to associate themselves with <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Mo" title="Lil' Mo">Lil' Mo</a>, who would record two popular duets with Ja Rule, "<a href="/wiki/Put_It_on_Me_(Ja_Rule_song)" title="Put It on Me (Ja Rule song)">Put It on Me</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Cry_(Ja_Rule_song)" title="I Cry (Ja Rule song)">I Cry</a>". That same year, Irv Gotti released the first Murder Inc. compilation, <i><a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti_Presents:_The_Murderers" title="Irv Gotti Presents: The Murderers">Irv Gotti Presents: The Murderers</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>They would sign <a href="/wiki/Charli_Baltimore" title="Charli Baltimore">Charli Baltimore</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashanti_(entertainer)" title="Ashanti (entertainer)">Ashanti</a> during that year. Also in 2001, their friendship with Lil' Mo would grow sour because her tracks that were set to be released on Ja's third album were cut and replaced with duets with Ashanti and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>This would cause a minor feud with Lil' Mo as she cut ties with the label. The label would soar to new heights from mid 2001 to early 2003. Not only did Ja Rule release his multi-platinum selling album <i><a href="/wiki/Pain_Is_Love" title="Pain Is Love">Pain Is Love</a></i>, that release was soon followed up with Ashanti's multi-platinum debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Ashanti_(album)" title="Ashanti (album)">Ashanti</a></i>, which broke <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> and spawned three hits on the <i>Billboard</i> charts.</p>
<p>During that year's award shows, Ashanti and Ja Rule both performed numerous times and won big, along with fellow Inc. artists like Baltimore. Baltimore scored two hits that year with both Ashanti and Ja Rule ("<a href="/wiki/Down_Ass_Bitch" title="Down Ass Bitch">Down Ass Bitch</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Down_4_U" title="Down 4 U">Down 4 U</a>", also featuring Vita). During this time, Murder Inc. started associating with <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> as he started hinting to signing with the label and then collaborated with the Inc.'s top two artists on several records (such as "The Pledge").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Murder Inc. would even sign <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Brown" title="Bobby Brown">Bobby Brown</a> to the label. All of this success would soon come to an end despite Ja Rule's next album (<i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Temptation_(Ja_Rule_album)" title="The Last Temptation (Ja Rule album)">The Last Temptation</a></i>) still going platinum. It sold well but did not live up to its expectations.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The label left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Motown" title="Universal Motown">Universal Motown</a> searching for a major distribution deal.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>It is believed that much of the money involved in starting up the Murder Inc. label came from Irv Gotti and his associates' involvement in organized crime, specifically drug dealing. He had ties to incarcerated Queens drug kingpin Kenneth "Supreme" McGriff, who was a major investor in the label's beginnings.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feud_with_50_Cent_and_G_Unit_records">Feud with 50 Cent and G Unit records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murder_Inc_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Feud with 50 Cent and G Unit records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 2002, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> released his official debut single "<a href="/wiki/Wanksta" title="Wanksta">Wanksta</a>" and his debut album in 2003 '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27_(album)" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin' (album)">Get Rich or Die Tryin'</a>, which were both huge successes. 50 Cent's past with Ja Rule, Irv Gotti, and Murder Inc. had been less than pleasant and once he became big in the industry, he (along with his crew <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>) once again took aim at his long time rival and associates. G-Unit would then defame not just Ja Rule, Irv and the male rappers of Murder Inc. but also the female artists of the label, including Ashanti and Baltimore. Due to 50 Cent's rising popularity at the time, much of the public seemingly took his side of the feud while Ja Rule's popularity would start to wane. His next album, (<i><a href="/wiki/Blood_in_My_Eye" title="Blood in My Eye">Blood in My Eye</a></i>) took aim at 50 Cent and G-Unit but was a commercial disappointment.</p>
<p>Ashanti however, continued her reign as her sophomore album (<i><a href="/wiki/Chapter_II_(Ashanti_album)" title="Chapter II (Ashanti album)">Chapter II</a></i>) topped the charts and scored three hit singles (including "<a href="/wiki/Rain_on_Me" title="Rain on Me">Rain on Me</a>"). She received three <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy</a> nominations in late 2003 for the album. Baltimore, however, struggled to release her next album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Diary_(You_Think_You_Know)" title="The Diary (You Think You Know)">The Diary</a></i> before it was eventually shelved. She was still featured on the "Rain On Me" remix with Ashanti and Ja Rule.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Inc._.282003.E2.80.932007.29">The Inc. (2003–2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murder_Inc_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Inc. (2003–2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 14, 2003, Irv Gotti held a press conference about the recent changes at Murder Inc., the feuds, the scandal, the investigation from the IRS, and Ja Rule's troubles. In the end, he talked about changing the name from Murder Inc. to The Inc. Records. This became official in 2004, with the signing of <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_(singer)" title="Lloyd (singer)">Lloyd</a> and the release of his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Southside_(Lloyd_album)" title="Southside (Lloyd album)">Southside</a></i>.</p>
<p>Ja Rule would see his music return to form, with his gold-selling album <i><a href="/wiki/R.U.L.E." title="R.U.L.E.">R.U.L.E.</a></i>, and his popularity somewhat restored. Ashanti would also release her successful album, <i><a href="/wiki/Concrete_Rose" title="Concrete Rose">Concrete Rose</a></i>.</p>
<p>At the end of 2004, they were ordered to leave the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Def_Jam" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a> offices while the investigations were on. When the trials began in early 2005, Def Jam made them honor their contracts and release compilation albums; then they were not re-signed.</p>
<p>The Inc. Records spent the year of 2005 and parts of 2006 searching for a new home. Lorenzo first went to his friend Lyor Cohen at the <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> labels. Cohen offered a deal that would make Gotti the head of not only The Inc., but also <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records_Group" title="Atlantic Records Group">Atlantic Records Group</a>. In the deal it involved them buying The Inc. and Gotti getting paid the same as he did at Def Jam. The Inc. then ended talks with Warner Brothers as Gotti felt they would not get paid enough money.</p>
<p>Gotti had short talks with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>. It was beginning to seem as if they were going to sign with Atlantic, then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> offered Gotti a seven-year deal and 50 percent ownership of his materials and the label along with the job as A&amp;R which pays a large amount of money. A portion of the deal was financed by National Talent Agency out of Southern California.</p>
<p>The Inc. Records would sign on to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> (like Def Jam, a division of <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>) in early 2006 and begin recording their respective albums. Soon after settling in at Universal, Lorenzo would sign singer Ashley Joi and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_adult_contemporary" title="Modern adult contemporary">Adult alternative</a> singer/pianist <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Carlton" title="Vanessa Carlton">Vanessa Carlton</a>, who had been recently been recruited by Robert Krout and Christopher Dixon of National Talent shortly before the company's financial contribution.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decline_of_The_Inc._.282007.E2.80.932012.29">Decline of The Inc. (2007–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murder_Inc_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Decline of The Inc. (2007–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 1, 2007, Ja Rule would return to radio and television on <i><a href="/wiki/Sucker_Free" title="Sucker Free">Sucker Free</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, where he debuted his single "Uh Oh" with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and his return to recording music. Irv Gotti and Eastwood created The Inc. West with Eastwood serving as its president and Gotti on A&amp;R. Techniec also signed to the label and was appointed Vice President. Techniec's group Dynamic Certified also signed to the label.</p>
<p>Irv Gotti later gave Ja Rule his own label, Mpire Records.</p>
<p>In May 2009, Irv Gotti announced to MTV News that he was releasing Ashanti from her contract with The Inc. Just one month later in June 2009 singer <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_(singer)" title="Lloyd (singer)">Lloyd</a>—who had been with The Inc for five years—asked to be released from his contract. Lloyd cited the need for more control and change as reasons for his departure from the slowly shrinking label. "I'm ready for a change. There's no bad blood. I just feel I need to take more control over my career and get a fresh start. Hopefully Irv can understand my position," Lloyd explained in a press release.</p>
<p>"We find ourselves in limbo for the second time in three years," Henry Lee, Lloyd's manager said in a statement. "It's frustrating to know that opportunities to advance Lloyd's career are out there but we can't exploit them due to our current situation. Even though Lloyd is appreciative of the opportunity that has been afforded him by The Inc., he feels it's time for him to move on and seek opportunities that will allow him to build and enhance his brand."</p>
<p>Lloyd notified fans that an announcement regarding his "label situation" would be made earlier this week via his Twitter page writing, "I've been meeting with my management team and my attorneys for the past 2 days. Expect an announcement today regarding my label situation..."</p>
<p>That same year, Ja Rule announced that he was no longer signed to The Inc. as well, the same label he had been with since its beginnings in 1997. Ja Rule decided to go independent on his own label, Mpire Records, which is distributed through Fontana Distribution. Although Ja Rule had departed from The Inc., he revealed he was still on good terms with Gotti and the two worked together for Ja's next album, <i>Pain Is Love 2</i>. Baltimore soon joined suit and left the label in 2010 for new opportunities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_of_Murder_Inc._.282013.E2.80.93present.29">Return of Murder Inc. (2013–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murder_Inc_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return of Murder Inc. (2013–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2013, Murder Inc. was relaunched as an umbrella label under Irv Gotti's new label, Visionary. On September 27, Murder Inc. released its first songs in years, Ja Rule's "Fresh Out Da Pen" and "Everything", both produced by Murder Inc. Records/Visionary producers <a href="/wiki/Reefa" title="Reefa">Reefa</a> and Myles William.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xxlmag.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
