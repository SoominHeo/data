<p><i><b>The Guest</b></i> is a 2014 American <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror</a>-<a href="/wiki/Thriller_film" title="Thriller film">thriller</a> film directed and edited by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Wingard" title="Adam Wingard">Adam Wingard</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Simon_Barrett_(filmmaker)" title="Simon Barrett (filmmaker)">Simon Barrett</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Dan_Stevens" title="Dan Stevens">Dan Stevens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maika_Monroe" title="Maika Monroe">Maika Monroe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leland_Orser" title="Leland Orser">Leland Orser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheila_Kelley_(American_actress)" title="Sheila Kelley (American actress)">Sheila Kelley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Meyer" title="Brendan Meyer">Brendan Meyer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Reddick" title="Lance Reddick">Lance Reddick</a>. The film premiered at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2014 Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a> on January 17, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Wingard and Barrett similarly worked together for their previous film <i><a href="/wiki/You%27re_Next" title="You're Next">You're Next</a></i> (2011). The film was released in the United States on September 17, 2014 &amp; United Kingdom on September 5, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spencer and Laura Peterson with their children Luke and Anna are coping with the loss of their eldest son, Caleb, to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–present)">war in Afghanistan</a>. They are visited by David Collins, who claims to be a former soldier and Caleb's best friend. He announces to the family his reason of visit; to help Caleb take care of the family. David is polite, warm and friendly toward the family, and Laura offers to let him stay as long as he needs to.</p>
<p>David hears of Spencer's troubles at work and sees Luke return home with a mark on his face, caused by a gang of jock bullies in school. The next day, with Luke's help, David follows the bullying jock gang that attacked Luke to a bar, and humiliates them in a confrontation which erupts into a fight that injures all the bullies. He then uses his knowledge of the law, as well as a bribe, to convince the bartender not to tell anyone. David then goes to a party with a reluctant Anna, at which he saves her friend Kristen from her ex-boyfriend and then has sex with her, and also inquires about where to buy guns from Anna's friend Craig. On their way home, Anna offers to make David a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mix CD</a>.</p>
<p>David gives Luke some advice on dealing with bullies and gives him his <a href="/wiki/Butterfly_knife" title="Butterfly knife">butterfly knife</a>. He meets Craig and his friend to buy the gun, but then kills them both and takes all the weapons, including two grenades. An increasingly suspicious Anna calls the military base to ask about David. The call alerts a private corporation headed by Major Carver, who assembles a special forces team and heads to the Peterson's house. Anna is told that David had presumably died a week earlier. She then learns that Craig was killed and her boyfriend Zeke has been blamed for it. It is revealed that Spencer's boss died under mysterious circumstances, giving him the promotion he always wanted. Anna asks Luke to research the numbers David has called on his phone.</p>
<p>At school, one of the bullies assaults Luke again, and he retaliates, injuring the bully. After they are both sent to the principal's office, David arrives and blackmails the principal to give Luke a month of after-school detention with threats of litigation if the principal expels him. Luke tells David of Anna's suspicions, but promises not to investigate any further or tell anyone else. While David helps Laura with the laundry, Major Carver's team attack the house. David deftly kills all except Carver. Deciding his cover has been blown, David kills Laura. While driving away, he sees Spencer and kills him as well.</p>
<p>Carver picks up Anna and informs her of her parents' death. He also reveals that David was a soldier who was one of the test subjects for a medical experiment in the military and, being a former test subject of the said military program, he was "programmed" to kill anyone who might compromise his identity. David kills Kristen, destroys the restaurant she works at, then heads for the school to kill Luke. Carver and Anna head for the school, and enter a haunted house set up for the Halloween dance. David announces his presence by turning off the lights and playing Anna's mix CD, then kills Luke's teacher and Carver. Anna shoots David with Carver's gun, but David stabs her in the leg and attempts to choke her. Luke stabs David with his own butterfly knife. As he seemingly dies, David tells Luke that he's proud of him and that Luke did the right thing.</p>
<p>Sitting in an ambulance, Anna and Luke overhear the firefighters talking about discovering two charred corpses with no teeth. Anna sees a firefighter limp out from within, who turns to face her. Much to her horror, it is revealed that the firefighter is David himself.</p>

<p>Wingard directed the new thriller film with the script written by Simon Barrett. <a href="/wiki/Keith_Calder" title="Keith Calder">Keith Calder</a> and Jessica Wu produced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dan_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dan-5">[5]</a></sup> On March 7, 2014 <a href="/wiki/Picturehouse_(company)" title="Picturehouse (company)">Picturehouse</a> picked up the US distribution rights to the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The film was released into cinemas on September 5, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Casting choices focused on <a href="/wiki/Character_actor" title="Character actor">character actors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On June 11, 2013, actor <a href="/wiki/Dan_Stevens" title="Dan Stevens">Dan Stevens</a> signed to star in the role of David. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dan_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dan-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Maika_Monroe" title="Maika Monroe">Maika Monroe</a> was signed on June 26, to star opposite Stevens as Anna. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maika_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maika-9">[9]</a></sup> On July 8, two more were added to the cast, <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Meyer" title="Brendan Meyer">Brendan Meyer</a>, who played Luke, Monroe's character's younger brother, and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Reddick" title="Lance Reddick">Lance Reddick</a>, who played a Major in the Military Police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lance_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lance-10">[10]</a></sup> When casting Stevens, Barrett and Wingard were aware that he would initially be quite likable, both for his attractiveness and his character's importance to the plot. However, they said the title made the story obvious, and though audiences may cheer on his character for his entertainment value, he is clearly the antagonist of the film, something audiences will easily recognize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 17, 2013, the New Mexico Film Office announced the start of the production of <i>The Guest</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The filming finished at the end of August and was filmed in different locations such as <a href="/wiki/Moriarty,_New_Mexico" title="Moriarty, New Mexico">Moriarty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edgewood,_New_Mexico" title="Edgewood, New Mexico">Edgewood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Estancia,_New_Mexico" title="Estancia, New Mexico">Estancia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_rough_cut_and_test_screenings">Original rough cut and test screenings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Original rough cut and test screenings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to director Adam Wingard, the original rough cut of <i>The Guest</i> was over twenty minutes longer than the final theatrical cut. After this version was screened to a test audience, it was cut down to the running time very close to the runtime of the theatrical cut. After another test screening, the audience was confused at the sudden arrival of Major Richard Carver and his team at the Peterson home, which is why some scenes with Carver at KPG headquarters were added in final cut of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some of the scenes which were cut down or deleted completely after test screenings were scenes which explained what exactly "David" is, what happened to him, why he does some of the things he does in the film, and more details and information about the KPG program that he was involved in. These scenes were cut because the test audience felt that all of it was just too much information. Even Wingard and screenwriter Simon Barrett admitted that they hated explaining David's character and his background because they always wanted to leave much of the information about him ambiguous, which is why they were glad that those scenes were disliked by the test screening audience and had to be cut out of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 26, 2014, the first <a href="/wiki/Trailer_(promotion)" title="Trailer (promotion)">teaser trailer</a> was released,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> followed by another trailer on August 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Fearnet" title="Fearnet">Fearnet</a></i> praised the film's acting and overall remarked that it was "a slick, fast, fun thriller flick."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> As of March 2015, the film has a 90% score on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on 96 reviews. The site's consensus states: "Boasting enough intelligence to bolster its darkly violent thrills, <i>The Guest</i> offers another treat for genre fans from director Adam Wingard."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a 76/100 rating based on 29 critics, signifying "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> gave the film a B+ (with a community grade of B+ as well) and said that "Dumb fun is rarely this smartly delivered."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <i>Bloody Good Horror</i> gave the film an 8/10 and commented, "Mixing elements from such classics like <i>Halloween</i>, <i>The Terminator</i>, and using the framework of Molière's hidden-identity classic Tartuffe keeps this modern 80's thriller on par with some of the best homages seen in recent memory."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Guest_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film opened in the United Kingdom on 5 September 2014 in 274 cinemas. It finished in eighth place, grossing $511,040. After four weeks, the film grossed $1,352,579.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The film arrived in US markets on September 17 in 19 theaters. During the first weekend, the film took in $84,527. It expanded to more markets on October 10, increasing its screen count from eight to 53. It grossed $47,957 for the weekend, with a per-screen average of $905. After six weeks, the film grossed $332,890.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
