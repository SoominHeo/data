<p><i><b>The Gift</b></i> is a 2015 American-Australian <a href="/wiki/Psychological_thriller" title="Psychological thriller">psychological thriller</a> film written, co-produced, and directed by <a href="/wiki/Joel_Edgerton" title="Joel Edgerton">Joel Edgerton</a> in his <a href="/wiki/List_of_directorial_debuts" title="List of directorial debuts">directorial debut</a>, and co-produced by <a href="/wiki/Jason_Blum" title="Jason Blum">Jason Blum</a> and Rebecca Yeldham. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Jason_Bateman" title="Jason Bateman">Jason Bateman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Hall" title="Rebecca Hall">Rebecca Hall</a> as a couple intimidated by a past figure played by Edgerton.</p>
<p>It was released in the United States on August 7, 2015, by <a href="/wiki/STX_Entertainment" title="STX Entertainment">STX Entertainment</a>. The film received critical acclaim and box office receipts of $59 million over a $5 million budget.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A married couple, Simon and Robyn Callem, relocate from <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> suburb after Simon takes a new job. While out shopping, they run into Gordon "Gordo" Moseley, Simon's former high school classmate. Gordo begins dropping in unannounced and delivering gifts including <a href="/wiki/Koi" title="Koi">koi</a> for their pond. He makes Simon uncomfortable, but Robyn sees no problem.</p>
<p>Gordo invites the couple to his large and elegant home. He receives a phone call and leaves abruptly, making Simon more suspicious. When he returns, Simon tells him to stay away from his family. The next day, Robyn finds the koi dead and their dog missing. Simon drives to Gordo's house to confront him, but discovers it is not actually his home. In the days following, Robyn suspects she is not alone in the house while Simon is at work. Unable to sleep, she steals prescription pills from her neighbor and one morning faints.</p>
<p>The dog returns and Robyn receives a letter of apology from Gordo. She is puzzled by his writing that he was "willing to let bygones be bygones". Simon says he does not know what it refers to and refuses to make peace with Gordo, telling Robyn that her pills are making her paranoid.</p>
<p>Robyn becomes pregnant. At her baby shower, Simon's sister tells her that, at school, Simon and his friend Greg reported that Gordo was being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Child_molestation" title="Child molestation">molested</a>. Gordo was bullied afterwards and had to transfer. Robyn finds Greg, who reveals that the molestation story was fabricated by Simon, who was a bully in school. As a result of the story, Gordo's father thought he was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homosexual" title="Homosexual">homosexual</a> and nearly killed him. Robyn searches Simon's home office and discovers that he has run background checks on Gordo and someone named Danny McDonald.</p>
<p>Robyn learns Simon has beaten Danny for a promotion after troubling information was revealed from Danny's previous job. Robyn confronts Simon about Gordo, but he denies responsibility. Simon angrily blames Robyn's suggestions of reconciliation on her own past traumas of being bullied at school. At Robyn's behest, Simon tracks down Gordo and half-heartedly apologizes, but Gordo does not accept his apology. Furious, Simon attacks him.</p>
<p>Simon's promotion party is interrupted by Danny, who says Simon fabricated the information that ruined his career. Robyn goes into labor and gives birth to a boy. The next morning, Simon receives a call from his boss telling him that his fraud has been discovered and he will be fired. Robyn tells him that she wants to separate.</p>
<p>Simon goes home and finds another gift box, containing footage of Gordo preparing to <a href="/wiki/Sexual_assault" title="Sexual assault">sexually assault</a> Robyn after she fainted months earlier. Meanwhile, Gordo visits Robyn in the hospital to congratulate her, wearing his arm in a sling; he tells her Simon injured him. Simon rushes to the hospital but misses Gordo. Gordo calls him soon after, taunting him, refusing to confirm whether he raped Robyn, and admonishing him for his lies. Robyn, furious, refuses to let Simon see the baby. With his life and marriage destroyed, Simon breaks down. Gordo watches through a window and walks away, removing his arm sling.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The project was announced in August 2012, when it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Joel_Edgerton" title="Joel Edgerton">Joel Edgerton</a> had written a psychological thriller script titled <i>Weirdo</i>, with which Edgerton would also be making his directing debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> His inspirations for the screenplay included <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Hitchcock" title="Alfred Hitchcock">Alfred Hitchcock</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fatal_Attraction" title="Fatal Attraction">Fatal Attraction</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Haneke" title="Michael Haneke">Michael Haneke's</a> 2005 French film <i><a href="/wiki/Cach%C3%A9_(film)" title="Caché (film)">Caché</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wallstreet_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-wallstreet-4">[4]</a></sup> as well as <a href="/wiki/Park_Chan-wook" title="Park Chan-wook">Park Chan-wook</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vengeance_Trilogy" title="The Vengeance Trilogy">Vengeance Trilogy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> began on January 19, 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and ended on February 20, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> A majority of filming took place at a home in the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Hills" title="Hollywood Hills">Hollywood Hills</a> neighborhood, where <a href="/wiki/STX_Entertainment" title="STX Entertainment">STX Entertainment</a> also held promotional interviews for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitflix_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitflix-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was shot on an <a href="/wiki/Arri_Alexa" title="Arri Alexa">Arri Alexa</a> with <a href="/wiki/Canon_Inc." title="Canon Inc.">Canon</a> K35 lenses, and was filmed in 25 days, according to its cinematographer, Eduard Grau. Grau was recommended by <a href="/wiki/Nash_Edgerton" title="Nash Edgerton">Nash Edgerton</a>, who served as the film's <a href="/wiki/Stunt_coordinator" title="Stunt coordinator">stunt coordinator</a>, after the two were part of the production for the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short film</a> <i>Streetcar</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grau_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-grau-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-streetcar_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-streetcar-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stuntref_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-stuntref-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Collider.com" title="Collider.com">Collider.com</a>, Edgerton revealed that he did not start filming his acting role until two weeks into shooting (devoting that time, instead, solely to directing). As soon as he did, his older brother Nash assisted on set behind the camera. Edgerton completed shooting his role as Gordo in seven days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-collider_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-collider-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-production">Post-production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Post-production took eleven weeks from March–June 2015. Luke Doolan also edited <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance</a> winner <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_Kingdom_(film)" title="Animal Kingdom (film)">Animal Kingdom</a></i> (also starring Edgerton).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 20, 2015, STX Entertainment bought the United States distribution rights to the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> STX retitled the film <i>The Gift</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>STX initially set <i>The Gift</i> for domestic release on July 31, 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and later moved it to August 7, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Trailer_(promotion)" title="Trailer (promotion)">trailer</a> was released on April 1, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It premiered on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>'s live video streaming app <a href="/wiki/Periscope_(app)" title="Periscope (app)">Periscope</a>, making <a href="/wiki/STX_Entertainment" title="STX Entertainment">STX Entertainment</a> the first advertiser to work with Twitter on a campaign incorporating Periscope.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Gift</i> grossed $43.8 million in North America and $15.2 million in other territories for a worldwide total of $59 million, against a budget of $5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In its opening weekend, the film grossed $11.9 million, finishing third at the box office behind <i><a href="/wiki/Mission:_Impossible_%E2%80%93_Rogue_Nation" title="Mission: Impossible – Rogue Nation">Mission: Impossible – Rogue Nation</a></i> ($28.5 million) and fellow newcomer <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four_(2015_film)" title="Fantastic Four (2015 film)">Fantastic Four</a></i> ($25.7 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gift_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film has a rating of 93%, based on 158 reviews, with an average rating of 7.5/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "<i>The Gift</i> is wickedly smart and playfully subversive, challenging the audience's expectations while leaving them leaning on the edges of their seats."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gives the film a score of 77 out of 100, based on 31 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a>, audiences gave the film an average grade of "B" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Time_Out_(magazine)" title="Time Out (magazine)">Time Out</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Daisy Bowie-Sell praised <i>The Gift</i> as "darkly unnerving", commending its ending and comparing it positively to a similar film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hand_That_Rocks_the_Cradle_(film)" title="The Hand That Rocks the Cradle (film)">The Hand That Rocks the Cradle</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeout_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-timeout-32">[32]</a></sup> In a review for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiewire" title="Indiewire">Indiewire</a>, Katie Walsh lamented the film's use of common <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(genre)" title="Thriller (genre)">thriller</a> cliches and restraint towards the climax, but lauded Joel Edgerton's direction, concluding: "His deft, controlled maneuvering of plot, character, style, and tone is damn near perfect for his feature debut—even if it is in service of a very standard genre piece."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiewire_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiewire-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
