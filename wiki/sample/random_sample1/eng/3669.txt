<p><b>The Rasmus</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> rock band that formed in 1994 in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a> while the band members were still in upper comprehensive school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus-se_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus-se-6">[6]</a></sup> The original band members were <a href="/wiki/Lauri_Yl%C3%B6nen" title="Lauri Ylönen">Lauri Ylönen</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">lead singer</a>/<a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>), Eero Heinonen (<a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass</a>), Pauli Rantasalmi (<a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>) and Jarno Lahti (<a href="/wiki/Drum_kit" title="Drum kit">drums</a>). Jarno Lahti left the year after and was replaced by Janne Heiskanen in 1995. Heiskanen quit the band in 1998 and was soon replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aki_Hakala" title="Aki Hakala">Aki Hakala</a>. The Rasmus has sold 4 million albums worldwide, 310,000 copies in their native Finland alone. They have won numerous awards, both domestic and international.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_.281994.E2.80.931995.29">Formation (1994–1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation (1994–1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1st_(The_Rasmus_EP)" title="1st (The Rasmus EP)">1st (The Rasmus EP)</a></div>
<p>While still in highschool, Lauri Ylönen, Eero Heinonen, Pauli Rantasalmi and Jarno Lahti formed the band "Rasmus". The band played their first gig at their schools pre-Christmas party (23 December 1994). In 1995 Jarno left the band, and was replaced by Janne Heiskanen. In December 1995 they released their debut EP <a href="/wiki/1st_(The_Rasmus_EP)" title="1st (The Rasmus EP)"><i>1st</i></a>, including the songs "Funky Jam", "Myself", "Frog" and "Rakkauslaulu". In only a few weeks the EP had already sold 1000 copies. The EP was originally released independently through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teja_G._Records" title="Teja G. Records">Teja G. Records</a> but was re-released through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Music_Finland" title="Warner Music Finland">Warner Music Finland</a> after they signed with them early in 1996. The band also wrote the song "Don't Shut the Door" which would stay unused until it was re-written in 2009 as "<a href="/wiki/October_%26_April" title="October &amp; April">October &amp; April</a>" for their compilation album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_of_2001%E2%80%932009" title="Best of 2001–2009">Best of 2001–2009</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peep_.26_Playboys_.281996.E2.80.931997.29"><i>Peep</i> &amp; <i>Playboys</i> (1996–1997)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Peep &amp; Playboys (1996–1997)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Peep_(album)" title="Peep (album)">Peep (album)</a></div>
<p>In 1996 they released their debut album <a href="/wiki/Peep_(album)" title="Peep (album)"><i>Peep</i></a> through Warner Music Finland. The album was first released in Finland on 23 September, where it went Gold, and later in Estonia and Russia, and subsequently worldwide. Later in 1996 the band released two more EP's, <a href="/wiki/2nd_(The_Rasmus_EP)" title="2nd (The Rasmus EP)"><i>2nd</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/3rd_(The_Rasmus_EP)" title="3rd (The Rasmus EP)"><i>3rd</i></a>. Even though labeled as an EP, <i>3rd</i> went No. 8 on the Finnish Singles Chart later that year.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Playboys_(The_Rasmus_album)" title="Playboys (The Rasmus album)">Playboys (The Rasmus album)</a></div>
<p>Next year the band won an <a href="/wiki/Emma-gaala" title="Emma-gaala">EMMA</a> (the Finnish equivalent of Grammys) for the best new comer of 1996. Lauri and Pauli decided to quit school to focus fully on Rasmus and on 29 August they released their second album <a href="/wiki/Playboys_(The_Rasmus_album)" title="Playboys (The Rasmus album)"><i>Playboys</i></a>. It went Gold in Finland, and the single "Blue" also went Gold when it was released in May the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hell_of_a_Tester_and_musical_change_.281998.29"><i>Hell of a Tester</i> and musical change (1998)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hell of a Tester and musical change (1998)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hell_of_a_Tester" title="Hell of a Tester">Hell of a Tester</a></div>
<p>1998 marked the release of the third Rasmus album <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_of_a_Tester" title="Hell of a Tester">Hell of a Tester</a></i> (sometimes written <i>Hellofatester</i>). The album was released on 2 November and marks a change in their musical style from funk to more solid rock. The album received Gold status in Finland. The most popular single was "<a href="/wiki/Liquid_(The_Rasmus_song)" title="Liquid (The Rasmus song)">Liquid</a>", which was released in September, getting into the Top 40 on MTV Nordic. It was voted Single of the Year for 1998 by music critics and fans and the video won an award in 1999 at the Finnish Music Video Awards. Lauri wrote Liquid on <a href="/wiki/Lauttasaari" title="Lauttasaari">Lauttasaari</a> bridge. The band also wrote religious material which never saw daylight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Janne_leaves_the_band_and_Dynasty_is_born_.281999.29">Janne leaves the band and Dynasty is born (1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Janne leaves the band and Dynasty is born (1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dynasty_(association)" title="Dynasty (association)">Dynasty (association)</a></div>
<p>During 1999 Rasmus were headlining big festivals in Finland. Janne Heiskanen left the band after playing his last gig supporting <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>. Janne decided to retire himself in meditation in India, where he lived for some years. This left the band in disarray and were close to breaking up. Aki Hakala, who had previously sold merchandise for the band, was eventually asked to become the new drummer. Together with <a href="/wiki/Killer_(Finnish_band)" title="Killer (Finnish band)">Killer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kwan_(band)" title="Kwan (band)">Kwan</a> they formed the <a href="/wiki/Dynasty_(association)" title="Dynasty (association)">Dynasty association</a> a small, independent record label for recruiting new Finnish artists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Signing_with_Playground_Music_.282000.29">Signing with Playground Music (2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Signing with Playground Music (2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Playground_Music" title="Playground Music">Playground Music</a></div>
<p>In 2000 the band changed their name from "Rasmus" to "The Rasmus" to avoid confusion with the Swedish <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rasmus_(Swedish_musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rasmus (Swedish musician) (page does not exist)">DJ of the same name</a> (another artist, British Rasmus Gardell, has also used the name). In late 1999 The Cardigans' manager Petri H. Lundén recommended the band to Lars Tengroth, director of new Swedish independent label Playground Music. Tengroth went to see them play live in Helsinki and, as he later told <a href="/wiki/HitQuarters" title="HitQuarters">HitQuarters</a>, "fell in love with them straight away". Having released three albums in Finland and achieved modest success they were looking to develop their career outside of the country and Playground agreed to help them do this. In late 2000 the band began working with producers Martin Hansen &amp; Micke Nord in Sweden for their next album, ending up leaving their former funk sound completely behind and changing the style into straight forward pop rock. Once signed to Playground the band spent time developing their act to meet the demands of establishing themselves internationally. According to Tengroth: "It was like starting from scratch; we were creating a new band so to speak." They did a lot of things that were new to them – they spent a long time working on their songs before recording, they worked with producers for the first time, and they recorded outside of Finland. They already had a strong stage show but welcomed ideas in improving it further.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Into_.26_Hell_of_a_Collection_.282001.E2.80.932002.29"><i>Into</i> &amp; Hell of a Collection (2001–2002)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Into &amp; Hell of a Collection (2001–2002)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Into_(album)" title="Into (album)">Into (album)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hell_of_a_Collection" title="Hell of a Collection">Hell of a Collection</a></div>
<p>In October 2001 The Rasmus released <i><a href="/wiki/Into_(album)" title="Into (album)">Into</a>,</i> their fourth studio album, and first album for Playground Music. It was the first The Rasmus to be released in other European countries such as France and Spain. The album reached No. 1 in the Finnish album charts and spawned the singles "F-F-F-Falling", "Chill", "Madness" and "Heartbreaker/Days". All of these were released in 2001 apart from Heartbreaker/Days, which was released in 2002. For this fact and the name's change, some fans consider this album the first of "The Rasmus". In 2001 they also released their first compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_of_a_Collection" title="Hell of a Collection">Hell of a Collection</a></i>, composed of eighteen songs, nearly all of which were revamped versions of songs from the previous three albums. The exceptions are the final track, "Liquid" (Demo), which is a demo tape of their hit song "Liquid", and the songs "F-F-F-Falling" and "Chill" which were the first new songs released after Aki replaced Janne.</p>
<p>They won four EMMAs in 2002 for Best Group, Best Album, Best Pop/Rock Album and Best Song for "F-F-F-Falling".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dead_Letters_.282003.E2.80.932004.29"><i>Dead Letters</i> (2003–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Dead Letters (2003–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dead_Letters" title="Dead Letters">Dead Letters</a></div>
<p>On 21 March 2003 The Rasmus released their fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Letters" title="Dead Letters">Dead Letters</a></i>. <i>Dead Letters</i> sold 1.7 million copies worldwide and signified the band's major breakthrough, receiving 8 Gold and 6 Platinum <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">music certification awards</a>. Lead single "In the Shadows" received 6 gold and 2 platinum awards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Awards_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Awards-9">[9]</a></sup> selling over 1 million copies and breaking the record for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Performance_royalties" title="Performance royalties">performance royalties</a> received abroad on a Finnish composition (overtaking the works of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Sibelius" title="Jean Sibelius">Jean Sibelius</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hs.fi_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-hs.fi-10">[10]</a></sup> The commercial success of the album saw the band receiving numerous music awards across Europe, winning the 'Best Nordic Act' category in the <a href="/wiki/2003_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2003 MTV Europe Music Awards">2003 MTV Europe Music Awards</a>, and five Finnish EMMA awards for Best Group, Best Album, Best Video (In My Life), Best Artist and Export in 2004. They also won an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Echo_(music_award)" title="Echo (music award)">ECHO award</a> for best international newcomer and were awarded "Best International Artist" at the 2004 <a href="/wiki/MTV_Russia_Music_Awards" title="MTV Russia Music Awards">MTV Russia Music Awards</a>. "In The Shadows" was on the nominations list for the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Kerrang!_Award_for_Best_Single" title="Kerrang! Award for Best Single">Kerrang! Award for Best Single</a>.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Live_Letters" title="Live Letters">Live Letters</a></div>
<p>Such success led to extensive touring around the world. In 2004 they released their first live DVD <i><a href="/wiki/Live_Letters" title="Live Letters">Live Letters</a></i> which was filmed at Gampel Open Air in Switzerland on 21 August 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Awards_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Awards-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hs.fi_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-hs.fi-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hide_from_the_Sun_.282005.E2.80.932006.29"><i>Hide from the Sun</i> (2005–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Hide from the Sun (2005–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hide_from_the_Sun" title="Hide from the Sun">Hide from the Sun</a></div>
<p>On 12 September 2005 The Rasmus released their sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hide_from_the_Sun" title="Hide from the Sun">Hide from the Sun</a></i>. The album received Platinum status in Finland and sold more than 15,000 copies in the UK alone. Overall, Hide from the Sun sold 500,000 copies worldwide.</p>
<p>The band received a nomination for 'Best Rock Band' at the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eska_Awards" title="Eska Awards">ESKA awards</a>, and performed at the ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> They were also nominated in the 'Rock Album Of The Year', 'Song Of The Year' (No Fear), 'Best Finnish Band' categories at the <a href="/wiki/Emma-gaala" title="Emma-gaala">EMMA</a> awards 2006. Bassist Eero Heinonen released his solo album <i>Making Waves</i> with his side project Hay &amp; Stone on 20 September 2006. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Black_Roses_.282007.E2.80.932008.29"><i>Black Roses</i> (2007–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Black Roses (2007–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Black_Roses_(The_Rasmus_album)" title="Black Roses (The Rasmus album)">Black Roses (The Rasmus album)</a></div>
<p>The Rasmus continued their extensive touring throughout 2007. The band met <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Child" title="Desmond Child">Desmond Child</a> in Dominican Republic, who began working with the band on producing their seventh studio album. 2007 also marked the start of Eero Heinonen's career as a music video director.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Black_Roses_(The_Rasmus_album)" title="Black Roses (The Rasmus album)"><i>Black Roses</i></a> was released on 24 September 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It immediately replaced <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Magnetic" title="Death Magnetic">Death Magnetic</a></i> at the top of the Finnish Album Charts and went Gold after one week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album was originally planned to be released in March that year, but was delayed due to new songs the band still wanted to record, including the lead single, "Livin' in a World Without You". The album's sound is generally less heavy than that of <i>Hide from the Sun</i>. However, a few of the album's songs, such as "Ten Black Roses", "The Fight", and "Lost and Lonely", contain symphonic rock/metal elements. The album spawned a second single "Justify" in 2009. In total <i>Black Roses</i> sold 350,000 copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Best_of_2001.E2.80.932009_.282009.E2.80.932010.29"><i>Best of 2001–2009</i> (2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Best of 2001–2009 (2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_of_2001-2009" title="Best of 2001-2009">Best of 2001-2009</a></div>
<p>On 28 November 2009 they released their second compilation album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_of_2001%E2%80%932009" title="Best of 2001–2009">Best of 2001–2009</a></i>, featuring songs recorded between 2001 and 2009. While most of the songs were already released, it features a new unreleased song called "<a href="/wiki/October_%26_April" title="October &amp; April">October &amp; April</a>" which features vocals by <a href="/wiki/Anette_Olzon" title="Anette Olzon">Anette Olzon</a>. This song was recorded during the same time as <i>Black Roses</i>, but wasn't included as it didn't follow the album concept.</p>
<p>In early 2010, The Rasmus started working on their next album, which would be released by the band's personal record label due to their contract with their previous label dissolving at the end of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_World_.26_The_Rasmus_.282011.E2.80.932012.29">New World &amp; <i>The Rasmus</i> (2011–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: New World &amp; The Rasmus (2011–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/New_World_(Lauri_Yl%C3%B6nen_album)" title="New World (Lauri Ylönen album)">New World (Lauri Ylönen album)</a></div>
<p>On 30 March 2011 Lauri Ylönen released his solo album <a href="/wiki/New_World_(Lauri_Yl%C3%B6nen_album)" title="New World (Lauri Ylönen album)"><i>New World</i></a>, with the first single "Heavy" being released in February of that year. The material premiered the annual EMMA Gala on 26 February 2011. Ylönen stated that he wanted several songs that didn't fit The Rasmus to be released as a major record rather than a demo. The album was self-produced with their record label Dynasty Recordings. The album charted at a peak of No.2 on the Finnish album charts.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Rasmus_(album)" title="The Rasmus (album)">The Rasmus (album)</a></div>
<p>In 2011 the band signed a contract with Universal Music Finland. Shortly after, The Rasmus began producing their eighth studio album, lead singer Lauri Ylönen claimed they were aiming to revisit the sound of one of their earlier albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Into_(album)" title="Into (album)">Into</a></i>. The album's lead single, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=I%27m_a_Mess_(The_Rasmus_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="I'm a Mess (The Rasmus song) (page does not exist)">I'm a Mess</a>" was first performed on 25 February 2012 during the show to choose <a href="/wiki/Finland_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2012" title="Finland in the Eurovision Song Contest 2012">Finland's Eurovision act for 2012</a>, and was also the official song for the 2012 <a href="/wiki/European_Athletics_Championships" title="European Athletics Championships">European Athletics Championships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-14">[14]</a></sup> The band's <a href="/wiki/The_Rasmus_(album)" title="The Rasmus (album)">eighth studio album</a> was announced to be self-titled, and was released on 18 April 2012 in several European countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-therasmus.com_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-therasmus.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> '<a href="/wiki/Stranger_(The_Rasmus_song)" title="Stranger (The Rasmus song)">Stranger</a>', the second single from the album, was released on 15 May 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The album was re-issued as <i>The Rasmus tour edition</i> in autumn 2012, and featured the addition of the single "<a href="/wiki/Mysteria_(The_Rasmus_song)" title="Mysteria (The Rasmus song)">Mysteria</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The self-titled album later won two EMMA awards for "Best album cover" &amp; "Best music video (<i>I’m a Mess</i>)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Album_.282016-.29">New Album (2016-)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: New Album (2016-)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September and October 2016, The Rasmus posted updates from the studio showing them recording a new album which is likely to be out sometime in 2017. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_videos">Music videos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Music videos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Rasmus_discography#Music_videos" title="The Rasmus discography">The Rasmus music videos</a></div>
<p>The Rasmus have released a total of twenty music videos. Niklas Fronda and Fredrik Löfberg of Baranga Film have directed three of the band's videos, all from the <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Letters" title="Dead Letters">Dead Letters</a></i> album. <i>Dead Letters</i> is the album with most music videos: seven videos have been released from that album, including three versions of "In the Shadows". The videos of "Immortal" and "<a href="/wiki/Your_Forgiveness" title="Your Forgiveness">Your Forgiveness</a>" were directed by bassist Eero Heinonen. The first video they ever made was for "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funky_Jam" title="Funky Jam">Funky Jam</a>" in 1996.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lauri_Yl.C3.B6nen">Lauri Ylönen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Lauri Ylönen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lauri_Yl%C3%B6nen" title="Lauri Ylönen">Lauri Ylönen</a></div>
<p>He is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lead_singer" title="Lead singer">lead singer</a> of the band and was born Lauri Johannes Ylönen in Helsinki on 23 April 1979. His parents names are Matti and Liisa Ylönen and he has an older sister called Hanna, who was the muse of the song "P.S.", a song about Lauri's love/hate relationship with his sister. Lauri is the main songwriter and has also worked on various collaborations with fellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finnish_people" title="Finnish people">Finns</a> <a href="/wiki/Apocalyptica" title="Apocalyptica">Apocalyptica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ville_Valo" title="Ville Valo">Ville Valo</a> from the band <a href="/wiki/HIM_(Finnish_band)" title="HIM (Finnish band)">HIM</a>. He has also worked in the production of music for fellow Dynasty members <a href="/wiki/Killer_(Finnish_band)" title="Killer (Finnish band)">Killer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kwan_(band)" title="Kwan (band)">Kwan</a>, together with bandmate Pauli. In 2007, Lauri and other Dynasty members opened their own recording label, Dynasty Recordings. Lauri's nickname is "Lintu" which is the Finnish equivalent for bird which explains the crow feathers he once used to wear in his hair since just before their fifth album <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Letters" title="Dead Letters">Dead Letters</a></i> was released. He has been dying his hair black since early 2007 but dyed it blonde for their album <i>Black Roses</i>. Shortly after the band started touring for the <i>Black Roses Tour</i>, he changed his hair back to black. He appeared on MTV for the first time in July 2005 and three years later, he was also a presenter at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Europe_Music_Awards_2008" title="MTV Europe Music Awards 2008">2008 MTV Europe Music Awards</a>. In 2008 he composed the score to the Finnish thriller Blackout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He has a son named Julius Kristian Ylönen with Finnish <a href="/wiki/PMMP" title="PMMP">PMMP</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Paula_Vesala" title="Paula Vesala">Paula Vesala</a> who was born in April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In November 2010 he announced the release of his first solo album which will hit stores in March 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mesta.net_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mesta.net-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voice.fi_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Voice.fi-24">[24]</a></sup> Lauri is 1.69 m (5 ft 7 in) tall.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pauli_Rantasalmi">Pauli Rantasalmi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Pauli Rantasalmi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pauli is the <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitarist</a>, songwriter, producer and was born in Helsinki, Finland on 1 May 1979. He helped design the "dynasty" logo on four of his ESP custom guitars. He co-owns the record label Dynasty Recordings with Lauri Ylönen, who is the singer in the band. Together they have also produced one of the Finnish band Killer's albums before they split. Pauli has written and produced songs for several artists, including The Rasmus, Kwan, Killer, Pikku G and Anna Eriksson. Pauli has a Singaporean wife and his first child (a baby boy) was born on 13 August 2008 in Singapore, where he currently lives. Pauli is 1.75 m (5 ft 9 in) tall.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aki_Hakala">Aki Hakala</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Aki Hakala">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His full name is Aki Markus Hakala. He is the <a href="/wiki/Drummer" title="Drummer">drummer</a> and was born in <a href="/wiki/Espoo" title="Espoo">Espoo</a>, Finland, on 28 October 1979. He joined the band in 1999, after the former drummer, Janne Heiskanen, left the band to "find himself". Aki originally sold band merchandise at their shows and joined the band after jamming with Pauli. He says that the only thing he was ever good at was drumming, and he "sucked at everything else", although he has done some acting in the past in <i>Siamin tytöt</i> (a Finnish drama series) and can play the guitar. Before joining The Rasmus, he was a drummer for <a href="/wiki/Killer_(Finnish_band)" title="Killer (Finnish band)">Killer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kwan_(band)" title="Kwan (band)">Kwan</a>, (he was replaced by Risto). In 2006, Aki decided to finally have LASIK eye surgery, and ceased wearing his trademark black glasses. Early 2007, he stopped dying his hair black, opting instead for platinum blond. In July 2007 he welcomed his first daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Aki is 1.70 m (5 ft 7 in) tall.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eero_Heinonen">Eero Heinonen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Eero Heinonen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bass_guitarist" title="Bass guitarist">bassist</a> and was born Eero Aleksi Heinonen in Helsinki, Finland, on 27 November 1979. Eero is a follower of <a href="/wiki/Sahaja_Yoga" title="Sahaja Yoga">Sahaja Yoga</a> and is a quiet and well-balanced person, but also well known for playing tricks on bandmates and others. He was the first member of the band to get married and he is also a father of two young daughters, born in 2003 and 2005. After playing in Korpi Ensemble for many years he now has a new side project (since 2004) called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hay_and_Stone" title="Hay and Stone">Hay and Stone</a>, where he is bass player and lead singer. Since 2014 Eero has also played in the rock trio <a class="external text" href="https://soundcloud.com/aikuiset-lapset" rel="nofollow">Aikuiset Lapset</a> with Topi Jokinen and <a class="external text" href="http://www.mattipaalanen.com" rel="nofollow">Matti Paalanen</a>. Eero also plays the violin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dynasty">Dynasty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rasmus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Dynasty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dynasty_(association)" title="Dynasty (association)">Dynasty (association)</a></div>
<p>The Rasmus were part of a brotherhood of Finnish bands known as "Dynasty", along with the hip-hop band <a href="/wiki/Kwan_(band)" title="Kwan (band)">Kwan</a> and the now-separated modern rock band <a href="/wiki/Killer_(Finnish_band)" title="Killer (Finnish band)">Killer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Dynasty also have a studio in Helsinki, where they recruit new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finnish_people" title="Finnish people">Finnish</a> talent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Several members of Dynasty have tattoos with the Dynasty text; one can be found on Pauli's left and Lauri's right arm. Pauli and Eero also have instruments and gear bearing the Dynasty logo. Pauli has helped with the production of Kwan, winning an EMMA for Producer of the Year in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
