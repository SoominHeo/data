<p><i><b>The Last: Naruto the Movie</b></i> is the tenth overall <i><a href="/wiki/Naruto" title="Naruto">Naruto</a></i> film, made to commemorate the 15th anniversary of the franchise. It is also the first entry in the <i>Start of a New Era Project</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[Jp. 1]</a></sup>, and the first film to be an official part of the canon <i>Naruto</i> storyline, set between Ch. 699 and 700 of the original manga series.</p>
<p>The film premiered in theaters on 6 December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann20141022_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ann20141022-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup> It became the highest-grossing film in the franchise,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheLastTop_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheLastTop-5">[4]</a></sup> before being surpassed by its sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Boruto:_Naruto_the_Movie" title="Boruto: Naruto the Movie">Boruto: Naruto the Movie</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last:_Naruto_the_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two years after the Fourth Ninja World War and Kakashi Hatake becomes the Sixth Hokage, the moon is falling towards onto Earth and will soon collide. This crisis is caused by Toneri Ōtsutsuki a descendant of Hamura Ōtsutsuki of the Branch House who is determined to fulfill his "legacy" and punish mankind for abusing chakra over a millennia. During the Rinne Festival, as Hinata Hyuga knits a red scarf that Naruto Uzumaki used to wear since childhood, Sakura Haruno offers to help her, but Hinata doubtful while Naruto receive various gifts from everyone, including another scarf. Toneri infiltrates Konoha to kidnap Hinata but failed due to Naruto's intervention, though he manage to get Hanabi, Hinata's younger sister.</p>
<p>Naruto, Hinata, Sakura, Sai and Shikamaru Nara are assigned to rescue Hanabi, after Kakashi gives a special clock to Shikamaru that counts down to doomsday. Traveling in an abandoned village of the Ōtsutsuki Clan, Naruto recognizes the concept of love from Hinata's memory while being caught in a genjutsu and from spending more time with her. Toneri transplants Hanabi's eyes in himself to awaken the <a href="/wiki/Jutsu_(Naruto)#Tenseigan" title="Jutsu (Naruto)">Tenseigan</a> that his ancestors sealed over the last millennium and proposes Hinata; she "accepts" his offer, causing Naruto to fall into depression. Back on Earth, the villagers defend themselves and intercept the moon's meteorites, evacuating the civilians from Toneri's genocidal assault, while Sasuke Uchiha returns to protect Konoha.</p>
<p>After recovering for three days, Sakura reassures Naruto that Hinata truly loves him, and the group approaches Toneri's castle. Earlier, the spirit of Hamura contacted Hinata as the "Byakugan Princess" explaining Toneri has misinterpreted his decree. Toneri refuses to listen then he ruins the scarf and brainwashes Hinata. Invading Toneri's castle, Sakura and Sai secure Hanabi, while Shikamaru holds off Toneri's puppets, Naruto saves Hinata from the ceremony and destroys the Tenseigan altar. Toneri uses the remains to strengthen his power and slices the moon in half as Naruto engages him in Nine-Tails chakra mode. Naruto grasps a shred of the scarf and overpowers Toneri having the moon return to orbit. Taking back Hanabi's eyes, Toneri realizes the truth about Hamura's decree, and decides to stay on the moon to atone his sins. After Naruto tells Hinata the scarf he had earlier belonged to his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kushina_Uzumaki" title="Kushina Uzumaki">late mother</a>, they both confess their mutual love and kiss.</p>
<p>During the end credits, Naruto and Hinata get married, while his friends attend the wedding. In a <a href="/wiki/Post-credits_scene" title="Post-credits scene">post-credits scene</a> before the series' epilogue, the couple play with their children, Boruto and Himawari.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last:_Naruto_the_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film is directed by <a href="/wiki/Tsuneo_Kobayashi" title="Tsuneo Kobayashi">Tsuneo Kobayashi</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manga_author" title="Manga author">manga author</a> <a href="/wiki/Masashi_Kishimoto" title="Masashi Kishimoto">Masashi Kishimoto</a> is providing the story concept, character designs, and exercising complete editorial supervision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnimeNewsNetwork_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-AnimeNewsNetwork-7">[6]</a></sup> The film was first announced at <a href="/wiki/Jump_Festa" title="Jump Festa">Jump Festa</a> 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shinigami_List_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shinigami_List-8">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Film_Watch_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Film_Watch-9">[8]</a></sup> It was premiered on 6 December 2014, which made it the first movie in the franchise to be released two years after the previous film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnimeNewsNetwork_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-AnimeNewsNetwork-7">[6]</a></sup> Its first teaser was revealed on 31 July 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was heavily promoted in the lead up to the <i>Naruto</i> manga finale in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weekly_Shonen_Jump" title="Weekly Shonen Jump">Weekly Shonen Jump</a></i> magazine, with new concept designs and information revealed on a weekly basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> A new character voiced by <a href="/wiki/Jun_Fukuyama" title="Jun Fukuyama">Jun Fukuyama</a> made an appearance in the movie. Fukuyama also asked fans to enjoy the film once it is released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> A limited edition official movie <a href="/wiki/List_of_Naruto_print_media#Data_books" title="List of Naruto print media">data book</a> was distributed with the movie, and was to contain a one-shot movie tie-in chapter by Kishimoto. Maruo Kyōzuka wrote a novelization published by <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a> on 8 December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The male duo <a href="/wiki/Sukima_Switch" title="Sukima Switch">Sukima Switch</a> is performing the film's theme, "Hoshi no Utsuwa" ("Star Vessel"); producer Takuyuki Hirobe had asked the duo to make a song that invokes a gentle yet powerful outlook of the world in the making of the movie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann20141022_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ann20141022-3">[2]</a></sup> The single is set to be released on 3 December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated.">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup> A character CD song for Hinata Hyuga has also been announced as "Fuyu no Owari ni" ("At the End of Winter") by <a href="/wiki/Nana_Mizuki" title="Nana Mizuki">Nana Mizuki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last:_Naruto_the_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last:_Naruto_the_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Amy McNulty of <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a>, while criticizing the unengaging villain, overarching plot and score for having no impact, praised the top-notch animation and story for taking a more character-driven romance approach to it than previous films, concluding that "Despite the (awkward) title, the movie is not actually the final film in the franchise, but it's the last of an era and a fitting capstone for the series and its fandom."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ann-17">[16]</a></sup> Charles Solomon of the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> gave the film a positive review, saying that "If 'The Last' lacks some of the emotional punch of the previous feature, 'The Road to Ninja,' Kobayashi compensates with flamboyant visuals that mix CG, drawn animation and elegant calligraphic figures, fans should stay through the credits for a surprising final scene."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup> Naruto's romance with Hinata earned multiple positive reactions from the media to the point some wished <i>The Last</i> to be condensed so that their relationship was the main focus of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-ann-17">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last:_Naruto_the_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During its first weekend, <i>The Last</i> earned ¥515 million (US$4.35 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[20]</a></sup> The film has grossed <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">¥</a>1.29 billion after three weekends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup> And by the end of December 2014, it had earned about <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">¥</a>1.75 billion (US$14.76 million) and became the top-grossing feature film in the franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheLastTop_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-TheLastTop-5">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last:_Naruto_the_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Boruto:_Naruto_the_Movie" title="Boruto: Naruto the Movie">Boruto: Naruto the Movie</a></div>
<p>After the post-credits scene of The Last, it has shown a teaser trailer for a movie based on Naruto and Hinata's son, Boruto, along with Sasuke and Sakura's daughter, Sarada, and the new generation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup> Masashi Kishimoto is confirmed to be involved as a supervising executive producer, co-writer and character designer, conceiving the story about the next generation. The sequel is tied into the New Era Project. The film was released in Japan on 7 August 2015 and appeared with English subtitles in selected theaters in North America on 10 October 2015. Due to the film's success, it appeared in North America for a second time for a two night premiere on 18 and 19 December.</p>
