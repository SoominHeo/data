<p><b>The Wrestling Classic</b> was a <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Federation</a> (WWF) <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> event that took place on November 7, 1985, at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosemont_Horizon" title="Rosemont Horizon">Rosemont Horizon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwh_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-pwh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)#Main_event" title="Card (sports)">main event</a> was the finals of a 16-man <a href="/wiki/Single-elimination_tournament" title="Single-elimination tournament">tournament</a> where <a href="/wiki/Junkyard_Dog" title="Junkyard Dog">Junkyard Dog</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Randy_Savage" title="Randy Savage">Randy Savage</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwh_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-pwh-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Card_(sports)#Undercard" title="Card (sports)">undercard</a> consisted of a 16-man <a href="/wiki/Single-elimination_tournament" title="Single-elimination tournament">single-elimination</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tournament" title="Professional wrestling tournament">tournament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a> versus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%22Rowdy%22_Roddy_Piper" title='"Rowdy" Roddy Piper'>"Rowdy" Roddy Piper</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWF World Heavyweight Championship</a> and a contest where a fan won a <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Motors" title="Rolls-Royce Motors">Rolls Royce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwh_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-pwh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The event included no tag team match – something that would not be repeated on a WWF/WWE PPV event until the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_Royal_Rumble" title="2005 Royal Rumble">2005 Royal Rumble</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Tag_Team_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Tag Team Championship (WWE)">World Tag Team Champions</a> <a href="/wiki/The_Dream_Team_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Dream Team (professional wrestling)">The Dream Team</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brutus_Beefcake" title="Brutus Beefcake">Brutus Beefcake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greg_Valentine" title="Greg Valentine">Greg Valentine</a>) did not appear on the card. Former champions <a href="/wiki/The_Iron_Sheik" title="The Iron Sheik">The Iron Sheik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Volkoff" title="Nikolai Volkoff">Nikolai Volkoff</a> both wrestled as single competitors in the tournament, as did <a href="/wiki/The_British_Bulldogs" title="The British Bulldogs">The British Bulldogs</a> <a href="/wiki/Davey_Boy_Smith" title="Davey Boy Smith">Davey Boy Smith</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dynamite_Kid" title="Dynamite Kid">Dynamite Kid</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wrestling_Classic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Roddy_Piper.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="200" height="255" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Roddy_Piper.jpg/170px-Roddy_Piper.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1f/Roddy_Piper.jpg 1.5x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Roddy_Piper.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%22Rowdy%22_Roddy_Piper" title='"Rowdy" Roddy Piper'>"Rowdy" Roddy Piper</a>, who challenged <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWF World Heavyweight Championship</a> at Wrestling Classic</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The main <a href="/wiki/Feud_(professional_wrestling)" title="Feud (professional wrestling)">feud</a> heading into Wrestling Classic was between <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%22Rowdy%22_Roddy_Piper" title='"Rowdy" Roddy Piper'>"Rowdy" Roddy Piper</a>, with the two battling over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWF World Heavyweight Championship</a>. In 1985, Piper became the top <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heel</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_promotion" title="Professional wrestling promotion">promotion</a> after he spoke out against the burgeoning <a href="/wiki/1980s_professional_wrestling_boom" title="1980s professional wrestling boom">Rock 'n' Wrestling</a> connection, which led to a confrontation with Hogan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In February, they faced each other at <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> special <i><a href="/wiki/The_War_to_Settle_the_Score" title="The War to Settle the Score">The War to Settle the Score</a></i> for Hogan's WWF title, where Hogan retained his title by <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Disqualification" title="Professional wrestling">disqualification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Their feud led to the <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania#History" title="WrestleMania">development of WrestleMania</a>. In the main event of the <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_I" title="WrestleMania I">first-ever WrestleMania</a>, Hogan and celebrity <a href="/wiki/Mr._T" title="Mr. T">Mr. T</a> defeated Piper and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Orndorff" title="Paul Orndorff">Paul Orndorff</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Hogan feuded with Piper and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bob_Orton,_Jr." title="Bob Orton, Jr.">Bob Orton</a> for the rest of the year and often got disqualification victories over them,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWF6389Book_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WWF6389Book-5">[5]</a></sup> leading to a WWF title match between Hogan and Piper at the Wrestling Classic. A tournament was also promoted on WWF television which was to take place on the pay-per-view event.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Event">Event</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wrestling_Classic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Event">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> event featured a 16-man tournament dubbed "The Wrestling Classic". In the first round of the tournament, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Adonis" title="Adrian Adonis">Adrian Adonis</a> (with manager <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Hart" title="Jimmy Hart">Jimmy Hart</a>) defeated <a href="/wiki/Corporal_Kirchner" title="Corporal Kirchner">Corporal Kirchner</a> with a <a href="/wiki/DDT_(professional_wrestling)" title="DDT (professional wrestling)">DDT</a>. <a href="/wiki/Dynamite_Kid" title="Dynamite Kid">Dynamite Kid</a> then faced <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Volkoff" title="Nikolai Volkoff">Nikolai Volkoff</a> (without manager <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Blassie" title="Freddie Blassie">Freddie Blassie</a>); Dynamite Kid attacked Volkoff, who had just finished his customary singing of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Anthem_of_Russia" title="National Anthem of Russia">Soviet national anthem</a>, with a <a href="/wiki/Dropkick#Missile_dropkick" title="Dropkick">missile dropkick</a> from the top turnbuckle and then quickly scored the <a href="/wiki/Pin_(professional_wrestling)" title="Pin (professional wrestling)">pinfal</a> in a match that lasted about six seconds (the official time given was 9 seconds, equaling the record <a href="/wiki/King_Kong_Bundy" title="King Kong Bundy">King Kong Bundy</a> had set at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_I" title="WrestleMania I">WrestleMania</a>). <a href="/wiki/Randy_Savage" title="Randy Savage">Randy Savage</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Miss_Elizabeth" title="Miss Elizabeth">Miss Elizabeth</a>) then defeated "Polish Power" <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Putski" title="Ivan Putski">Ivan Putski</a>, though Savage had both his feet on the middle rope for leverage. <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Steamboat" title="Ricky Steamboat">Ricky Steamboat</a> faced <a href="/wiki/Davey_Boy_Smith" title="Davey Boy Smith">Davey Boy Smith</a> in a rare match pitting two fan favourites against each other. The match moved back and forth until Smith landed in the ropes trying attack Steamboat, but Steamboat sidestepped and Smith injured his <a href="/wiki/Groin" title="Groin">groin</a> and was unable to continue, so Steamboat was awarded the match by forfeit. <a href="/wiki/Junkyard_Dog" title="Junkyard Dog">Junkyard Dog</a> then faced <a href="/wiki/The_Iron_Sheik" title="The Iron Sheik">The Iron Sheik</a> (like Volkoff, the Sheik appeared without Freddie Blassie). The match began with Sheik <a href="/wiki/Chokehold" title="Chokehold">choking</a> JYD with his jacket. He applied a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#Camel_clutch" title="Professional wrestling holds">camel clutch</a> on JYD before releasing the hold. JYD quickly hit a <a href="/wiki/Headbutt" title="Headbutt">headbutt</a> on Sheik and <a href="/wiki/Pin_(professional_wrestling)" title="Pin (professional wrestling)">pinned</a> him to win the match. <a href="/wiki/Moondog_Spot" title="Moondog Spot">Moondog Spot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Terry_Funk" title="Terry Funk">Terry Funk</a>'s (with Jimmy Hart) match was next. It appeared that neither of them wanted to wrestle, so at Funk's suggestion they both left the ring. As Spot started to leave, Funk attacked him from behind and tried to re-enter the ring for a countout win, but Spot prevented him from entering the ring and himself won the match by <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Countout" title="Professional wrestling">countout</a> after Funk backdropped him into the ring before the ten count. <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">Intercontinental Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Tito_Santana" title="Tito Santana">Tito Santana</a> then faced <a href="/wiki/Don_Muraco" title="Don Muraco">The Magnificent Muraco</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Mr._Fuji" title="Mr. Fuji">Mr. Fuji</a>). Muraco appared to have pinned Santana but as his feet were on the ropes, the match continued and Santana rolled up the surprised Muraco with a quick <a href="/wiki/Pin_(professional_wrestling)#Small_package" title="Pin (professional wrestling)">small package</a>, scoring the pinfall. Santana suffered a groin injury that would hamper him later in the tournament. The final match of the first round pitted <a href="/wiki/Paul_Orndorff" title="Paul Orndorff">Paul Orndorff</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bob_Orton,_Jr." title="Bob Orton, Jr.">Bob Orton</a>. Orton was <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Disqualification" title="Professional wrestling">disqualified</a> after he hit Orndorff with his cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwh_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-pwh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The quarterfinals began with <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Adonis" title="Adrian Adonis">Adrian Adonis</a> facing Dynamite Kid. During the match, Adonis accidentally hit his <a href="/wiki/Manager_(professional_wrestling)" title="Manager (professional wrestling)">manager</a> Jimmy Hart and collided with Hart. Dynamite took advantage and pinned Adonis to win the match. <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Steamboat" title="Ricky Steamboat">Ricky Steamboat</a> then faced off against Randy Savage. The <a href="/wiki/Referee_(professional_wrestling)" title="Referee (professional wrestling)">referee</a> was distracted by Miss Elizabeth as Savage took advantage and pulled out <a href="/wiki/Brass_knuckles" title="Brass knuckles">brass knuckles</a> from his tights and hit Steamboat before pinning him to win the match. Junkyard Dog then defeated Moondog Spot after a head butt. Strangely, there was no referee for this match and JYD counted the 3 himself. Commentators <a href="/wiki/Gorilla_Monsoon" title="Gorilla Monsoon">Gorilla Monsoon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Ventura" title="Jesse Ventura">Jesse Ventura</a> then announced that a judge was at ringside and that JYD's count was legal. Tito Santana and Paul Orndorff fought to double countout, eliminating both wrestlers from the tournament in the process and allowing Junkyard Dog to advance to the finals. This led to Bobby Hennan's $50,000 storyline bounty on Paul Orndorff not being collected.</p>
<p>In a non-tournament match, <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a> defended his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWF Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Roddy_Piper" title="Roddy Piper">Roddy Piper</a>. Hogan applied a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#Bearhug" title="Professional wrestling holds">bearhug</a> on Piper. Piper responded with a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#Sleeper_hold" title="Professional wrestling holds">sleeper hold</a>. The referee was knocked out and Piper hit Hogan with a <a href="/wiki/Folding_chair" title="Folding chair">steel chair</a> before Hogan applied a sleeper hold of his own on Piper. Bob Orton came out and hit Hogan with his cast. The referee saw this and disqualified Piper in the process. Piper and Orton continued to assault Hogan until Paul Orndorff came out and cleared the ring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwh_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-pwh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The semifinal match between Randy Savage and Dynamite Kid was next. Dynamite Kid <a href="/wiki/Suplex#Superplex" title="Suplex">superplexed</a> Savage off the top rope but Savage countered it into an <a href="/wiki/Pin_(professional_wrestling)#Cradle" title="Pin (professional wrestling)">inside cradle</a> for the victory.</p>
<p>As part of the show, a contest was held where over 250,000 fans participated, one of which won a <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Motors" title="Rolls-Royce Motors">Rolls-Royce</a>. This segment was cut from the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Home_Video" title="WWE Home Video">Coliseum Home Video</a> release for time. However, it was restored when the show aired on <a href="/wiki/WWE_Classics_on_Demand" title="WWE Classics on Demand">WWE Classics on Demand</a>, WWE's archival <a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">video on demand</a> service.</p>
<p>In the finals of the tournament Junkyard Dog wrestled Randy Savage. JYD hit a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_throws#Back_body_drop" title="Professional wrestling throws">back body drop</a> on Savage off the top rope who landed on the arena floor. Savage was unable to get into the ring and was counted out. As a result, JYD won the match and the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwh_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-pwh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wrestling_Classic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many wrestlers received <a href="/wiki/Push_(professional_wrestling)" title="Push (professional wrestling)">pushes</a> by this tournament. <a href="/wiki/Junkyard_Dog" title="Junkyard Dog">Junkyard Dog</a>, the winner of this tournament got a push as he became the man to win the first-ever major tournament in WWF history. <a href="/wiki/Randy_Savage" title="Randy Savage">Randy Savage</a> went on to win both the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">WWF Intercontinental Championship</a> from <a href="/wiki/Tito_Santana" title="Tito Santana">Tito Santana</a> on February 8, 1986 and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWF Championship</a> in a similar tournament at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_IV" title="WrestleMania IV">WrestleMania IV</a> in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Steamboat" title="Ricky Steamboat">Ricky Steamboat</a> also won the Intercontinental Championship, by defeating Savage at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_III" title="WrestleMania III">WrestleMania III</a> in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dynamite_Kid" title="Dynamite Kid">Dynamite Kid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Davey_Boy_Smith" title="Davey Boy Smith">Davey Boy Smith</a> who together were the successful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Bulldogs" title="British Bulldogs">British Bulldogs</a> would go on to win the <a href="/wiki/World_Tag_Team_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Tag Team Championship (WWE)">WWF Tag Team Championship</a> at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_2" title="WrestleMania 2">WrestleMania 2</a> in 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The match between The Junkyard Dog and Moondog Spot from this event is one of only five matches to have ever been given the "-5 Star" rating by wrestling journalist <a href="/wiki/Dave_Meltzer" title="Dave Meltzer">Dave Meltzer</a> in the Wrestling Observer Newsletter.</p>
