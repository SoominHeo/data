<p><i><b>The Marine 2</b></i> is a 2009 American <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(genre)" title="Thriller (genre)">thriller film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Roel_Rein%C3%A9" title="Roel Reiné">Roel Reiné</a>, written by Christopher Borrelli and John Chapin Morgan, and produced by Michael Lake. The film stars are <a href="/wiki/Ted_DiBiase_Jr." title="Ted DiBiase Jr.">Ted DiBiase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Temuera_Morrison" title="Temuera Morrison">Temuera Morrison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lara_Cox" title="Lara Cox">Lara Cox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Coleby" title="Robert Coleby">Robert Coleby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Rooker" title="Michael Rooker">Michael Rooker</a>. It is the stand-alone sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marine" title="The Marine">The Marine</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a>, and it is the second in the <a href="/wiki/The_Marine_(film_series)" title="The Marine (film series)">film series</a>. This was Ted DiBiase's film debut. The film was released on DVD and Blu-ray in the United States on December 29, 2009.</p>
<p>The film was inspired by the <a href="/wiki/Dos_Palmas_kidnappings" title="Dos Palmas kidnappings">Dos Palmas kidnappings</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The film was produced by the films division of <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>, called <a href="/wiki/WWE_Studios" title="WWE Studios">WWE Studios</a>, and distributed in the United States by <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Marine_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joe Linwood (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ted_DiBiase,_Jr." title="Ted DiBiase, Jr.">Ted DiBiase</a>) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">US Marine</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Force_Recon" title="Force Recon">Force Recon</a> attempts to rescue his wife Robin (<a href="/wiki/Lara_Cox" title="Lara Cox">Lara Cox</a>), and other guests from a hotel which has been taken over by a gang of bloodthirsty terrorists. The Linwoods are vacationing at the lavish <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> resort when the terrorists invade during the hotel's grand opening. Joe escapes the initial onslaught and must find a way to save his wife and the other hostages.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Marine_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marine_3:_Homefront" title="The Marine 3: Homefront">The Marine 3: Homefront</a></i>, was released on March 5, 2013. It stars WWE wrestler <a href="/wiki/The_Miz" title="The Miz">Mike "The Miz" Mizanin</a> in the lead role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Randy_Orton" title="Randy Orton">Randy Orton</a> was initially cast in the lead role; however, due to his previous negative association with the Marine Corps, he was replaced by Mizanin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
