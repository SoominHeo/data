<p><b>The Muppets</b> are an <a href="/wiki/Ensemble_cast" title="Ensemble cast">ensemble cast</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puppet" title="Puppet">puppet</a> characters known for their <a href="/wiki/Self-referential_humor" title="Self-referential humor">self-aware</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burlesque" title="Burlesque">burlesque</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meta-reference" title="Meta-reference">meta-referential</a> style of <a href="/wiki/Variety_show" title="Variety show">variety</a>-<a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch comedy</a>. Created by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Henson" title="Jim Henson">Jim Henson</a> in 1955, they are the namesake for the <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> <a href="/wiki/Media_franchise" title="Media franchise">media franchise</a> that encompasses feature films, television series, music recordings, theme park attractions, print publications, merchandising, and other media works associated with the characters.</p>
<p>The Muppets debuted on the television program <i><a href="/wiki/Sam_and_Friends" title="Sam and Friends">Sam and Friends</a></i>, which aired from 1955 to 1961. After appearing on skits in several late night talk shows and advertising commercials during the 1960s, the Muppets began appearing on <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i> in 1969. The Muppets attained <a href="/wiki/Celebrity" title="Celebrity">celebrity status</a> and international recognition through their <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_role" title="Breakthrough role">breakout roles</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show" title="The Muppet Show">The Muppet Show</a></i> (1976–1981), a <a href="/wiki/Prime_time" title="Prime time">primetime television series</a> that garnered four <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> wins and twenty-one nominations during its five-year run. In the late 1970s and into the 1980s, the Muppets diversified into theatrical <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature films,</a> including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Movie" title="The Muppet Movie">The Muppet Movie</a></i> (1979), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Muppet_Caper" title="The Great Muppet Caper">The Great Muppet Caper</a></i> (1981), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Take_Manhattan" title="The Muppets Take Manhattan">The Muppets Take Manhattan</a></i> (1984). <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> began involvement with the Muppets in the late 1980s, seeking to acquire the characters from the <a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Henson_Company" title="The Jim Henson Company">Jim Henson Company</a>. The Muppets continued their presence in television and film in the 1990s with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Henson_Hour" title="The Jim Henson Hour">The Jim Henson Hour</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets_Tonight" title="Muppets Tonight">Muppets Tonight</a></i> (1996–98)—a series continuation of <i>The Muppet Show</i>—and three films, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Christmas_Carol" title="The Muppet Christmas Carol">The Muppet Christmas Carol</a></i> (1992), <i><a href="/wiki/Muppet_Treasure_Island" title="Muppet Treasure Island">Muppet Treasure Island</a></i> (1996), and <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets_from_Space" title="Muppets from Space">Muppets from Space</a></i> (1999). Disney acquired the rights to the Muppets in 2004, allowing the characters to gain broader public exposure than in previous years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Disney2004Press_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Disney2004Press-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agreement_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-agreement-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-purchase_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-purchase-3">[3]</a></sup> Under Disney's control, the Muppets enjoyed revitalized success, starring in two films—<i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_(film)" title="The Muppets (film)">The Muppets</a></i> (2011) and <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets_Most_Wanted" title="Muppets Most Wanted">Muppets Most Wanted</a></i> (2014)<i>—</i>as well as a short-lived <a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_(TV_series)" title="The Muppets (TV series)">primetime television series</a> on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a><i>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYFuzzy_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYFuzzy-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WallStreet_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WallStreet-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SeriesEnd_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-SeriesEnd-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Throughout their six decades of existence, the Muppets have been regarded as a staple of the entertainment industry and <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">popular culture</a> in the United States, receiving recognition from various cultural institutions and organizations, such as the <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Institute" title="American Film Institute">American Film Institute</a>, <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">Academy of Motion Pictures Arts and Sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress" title="Library of Congress">Library of Congress</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1950s.E2.80.931960s:_Beginnings">1950s–1960s: Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1950s–1960s: Beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jim_Henson_(1989).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="864" data-file-width="487" height="310" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Jim_Henson_%281989%29.jpg/175px-Jim_Henson_%281989%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Jim_Henson_%281989%29.jpg/263px-Jim_Henson_%281989%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Jim_Henson_%281989%29.jpg/350px-Jim_Henson_%281989%29.jpg 2x" width="175"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jim_Henson_(1989).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Under the guidance of creator and performer Jim Henson, the Muppets reached international recognition and celebrity status.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Muppets were created by puppeteer <a href="/wiki/Jim_Henson" title="Jim Henson">Jim Henson</a> in the 1950s, beginning with <a href="/wiki/Kermit_the_Frog" title="Kermit the Frog">Kermit the Frog</a>, who would become Henson's signature character. Originally conceived as characters aimed at an adult audience,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Henson stated that the term "Muppet" had been created as an amalgamation of the words "<a href="/wiki/Marionette" title="Marionette">marionette</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Puppet" title="Puppet">puppet</a>", but also claimed that it was actually a word he had coined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JimBio1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-JimBio1-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1955, the Muppets were introduced on <i><a href="/wiki/Sam_and_Friends" title="Sam and Friends">Sam and Friends</a></i>, a television program that aired on <a href="/wiki/WRC-TV" title="WRC-TV">WRC-TV</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." title="Washington D.C.">Washington D.C.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Conceptualized by Jim and eventual wife <a href="/wiki/Jane_Henson" title="Jane Henson">Jane Henson</a>, the series was notable for the being the first form of puppet media to not include a physical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proscenium_arch" title="Proscenium arch">proscenium arch</a> within which the characters are presented. Instead, Henson utilized the natural four-sided frame of a <a href="/wiki/Television_set" title="Television set">television set</a> that viewers would already be watching through as the program's theatre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JimBio2_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-JimBio2-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the 1960s, the characters—notably Kermit and <a href="/wiki/Rowlf_the_Dog" title="Rowlf the Dog">Rowlf the Dog</a>—appeared on skits in several <a href="/wiki/Late-night_talk_show" title="Late-night talk show">late-night talk shows</a> and advertising commercials, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ed_Sullivan_Show" title="The Ed Sullivan Show">The Ed Sullivan Show</a>.</i> Rowlf became the first Muppet with a regular spot on network television when he began appearing as <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Dean" title="Jimmy Dean">Jimmy Dean</a>'s sidekick on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jimmy_Dean_Show" title="The Jimmy Dean Show">The Jimmy Dean Show</a></i>. In 1966, <a href="/wiki/Joan_Ganz_Cooney" title="Joan Ganz Cooney">Joan Ganz Cooney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Morrisett" title="Lloyd Morrisett">Lloyd Morrisett</a> began developing an <a href="/wiki/Educational_television" title="Educational television">educational television</a> program targeted towards children and approached Henson to design several Muppet characters for the program. Produced by the <a href="/wiki/Sesame_Workshop" title="Sesame Workshop">Children's Television Workshop</a>, the show debuted as <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i> in 1969. Henson and his creative team performed and created several characters for the show in the years that followed; Henson waived his performance fee in exchange for retaining ownership rights to the Muppet characters created for the program. <i>Sesame Street</i> received critical acclaim, and the Muppets' involvement in the series was touted to be a vital component of the show's blossoming popularity, providing an "effective and pleasurable viewing" method of presentation for the series' educational curriculum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s:_The_Muppet_Show">1970s: <i>The Muppet Show</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1970s: The Muppet Show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By the early 1970s, the Muppets continued their presence in television, namely appearing in <a href="/wiki/The_Land_of_Gorch" title="The Land of Gorch"><i>The</i> <i>Land of Gorch</i></a> segments during the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>. As his involvement with <i>Sesame Street</i> continued, Henson mused about the possibility of creating a network television series featuring the Muppets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> However, unlike <i>Sesame Street</i>, which was geared towards a younger demographic and rooted in education, Henson pursued developing a series that would be focused purely on comedy and aimed more towards adults rather than children. Two <a href="/wiki/Pilot_(television_episode)" title="Pilot (television episode)">pilot specials</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Valentine_Show" title="The Muppets Valentine Show">The Muppets Valentine Show</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show:_Sex_and_Violence" title="The Muppet Show: Sex and Violence">The Muppet Show: Sex and Violence</a></i>, aired on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> in 1974 and 1975, respectively. After ABC passed on the pilots and no other major American network expressed interest in backing the project, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lew_Grade,_Baron_Grade" title="Lew Grade, Baron Grade">Lew Grade</a> approached Henson and agreed to produce the series for the British company <a href="/wiki/Associated_Television" title="Associated Television">Associated Television</a>. Debuting in 1976, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show" title="The Muppet Show">The Muppet Show</a></i> introduced characters such as <a href="/wiki/Miss_Piggy" title="Miss Piggy">Miss Piggy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fozzie_Bear" title="Fozzie Bear">Fozzie Bear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gonzo_(Muppet)" title="Gonzo (Muppet)">Gonzo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Animal_(Muppet)" title="Animal (Muppet)">Animal</a>, as well as showcasing regulars Kermit and Rowlf. Through its <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_syndication" title="Broadcast syndication">syndication</a>, <i>The Muppet Show</i> became increasingly popular due to its <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch comedy</a> <a href="/wiki/Variety_show" title="Variety show">variety</a> format, unique brand of humor, and prolific roster of <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest stars</a>. The show went on to receive twenty-one <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominations during its run, and winning four awards, including <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series">Outstanding Comedy Series</a> in 1978.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s.E2.80.931990s:_Continued_success">1980s–1990s: Continued success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1980s–1990s: Continued success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The success of <i>The Muppet Show</i> allowed <a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Henson_Company" title="The Jim Henson Company">Henson Associates</a> to diversify into theatrical <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature films</a> based on the Muppets and went on to produce <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Movie" title="The Muppet Movie">The Muppet Movie</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Muppet_Caper" title="The Great Muppet Caper">The Great Muppet Caper</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Take_Manhattan" title="The Muppets Take Manhattan">The Muppets Take Manhattan</a></i>, which followed in 1979, 1981 and 1984, respectively. Altogether, the three films received four <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Award</a> nominations. By 1983, Henson had introduced another television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Fraggle_Rock" title="Fraggle Rock">Fraggle Rock</a></i>, which ran on <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> in the United States until 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the late 1980s, Henson entered discussions with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Eisner" title="Michael Eisner">Michael Eisner</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>, in which the latter would acquire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jim_Henson_Productions" title="Jim Henson Productions">Jim Henson Productions</a> and in turn, own the Muppets. Disney was interested in purchasing the company for $150 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In addition to the company and Muppet characters, Eisner expressed a desire to include the <i>Sesame Street</i> characters as part of the acquisition. Henson, declined the proposal, however, consistently referring to such a motive as a "non-starter" for the deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> As discussions between the two companies continued, Henson and <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Imagineering" title="Walt Disney Imagineering">Walt Disney Imagineering</a> preemptively began developing Muppet-themed attractions for the <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Hollywood_Studios" title="Disney's Hollywood Studios">Disney-MGM Studios</a> at <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World" title="Walt Disney World">Walt Disney World</a>. However, negotiations broke off after Jim Henson's death in 1990. Nevertheless, Disney entered into a <a href="/wiki/License" title="License">licensing</a> agreement with Jim Henson Productions for permission to use the characters in the theme parks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The following year, <a href="/wiki/Muppet*Vision_3D" title="Muppet*Vision 3D">Muppet*Vision 3D</a> debuted at Disney-MGM Studios, the only attraction to come to fruition from the original Imagineering plans. Still interested in the franchise, Disney co-produced the fourth and fifth Muppet films, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Christmas_Carol" title="The Muppet Christmas Carol">The Muppet Christmas Carol</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Muppet_Treasure_Island" title="Muppet Treasure Island">Muppet Treasure Island</a></i>, with Jim Henson Productions in 1992 and 1996, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYFuzzy_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYFuzzy-4">[4]</a></sup> Following that, the characters starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets_Tonight" title="Muppets Tonight">Muppets Tonight</a></i> which ran on ABC from 1996 to 1998 and a sixth film, <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets_from_Space" title="Muppets from Space">Muppets from Space</a></i>, released by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Pictures" title="Columbia Pictures">Columbia Pictures</a> in 1999.</p>
<p>In 2000, Henson Productions was sold to <a href="/wiki/Constantin_Medien" title="Constantin Medien">EM.TV &amp; Merchandising AG</a> for $680 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Following the sale, EM.TV was plagued with financial problems and the Henson family purchased the company back in 2003, with the exception of the rights to the <i>Sesame Street</i> characters, which had been sold by EM.TV to <a href="/wiki/Sesame_Workshop" title="Sesame Workshop">Sesame Workshop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYFuzzy_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYFuzzy-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s:_Disney_acquisition">2000s: Disney acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000s: Disney acquisition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fourteen years after initial negotiations began, Disney purchased the Muppet <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">intellectual properties</a> from the Jim Henson Company for $75 million on February 17, 2004. The acquisition consisted of the rights and trademarks to the Muppets and <i><a href="/wiki/Bear_in_the_Big_Blue_House" title="Bear in the Big Blue House">Bear in the Big Blue House</a></i> characters, as well as to the Muppet film and television library.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Disney2004Press_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Disney2004Press-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agreement_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-agreement-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-purchase_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-purchase-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ForbesPurchase_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ForbesPurchase-21">[21]</a></sup> Exceptions included the <i>Sesame Street</i> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Sesame_Street_Muppets" title="List of Sesame Street Muppets">characters</a>—as they were previously sold to Sesame Workshop<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>—the <i>Fraggle Rock</i> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Fraggle_Rock_characters" title="List of Fraggle Rock characters">characters</a>, which were retained by Henson, and the <a href="/wiki/Film_distribution" title="Film distribution">distribution rights</a> to <i>The Muppets Take Manhattan</i>, <i>Muppets from Space</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Kermit%27s_Swamp_Years" title="Kermit's Swamp Years">Kermit's Swamp Years</a>,</i> which remained with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures_Entertainment" title="Sony Pictures Entertainment">Sony Pictures Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ForbesPurchase_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-ForbesPurchase-21">[21]</a></sup> As part of the acquisition, Disney formed the <a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Studio" title="The Muppets Studio">Muppets Studio</a>, a wholly owned subsidiary responsible for managing the characters and franchise. As a result, the term "Muppet" became a legal <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademark</a> owned by Disney, although Sesame Workshop continues to apply the term to their characters, and archival footage of Kermit, under an exclusive license from Disney.</p>
<p>The Jim Henson Company retains the rights to a number of productions featuring the Disney-owned Muppet characters, including <i><a href="/wiki/Emmet_Otter%27s_Jug-Band_Christmas" title="Emmet Otter's Jug-Band Christmas">Emmet Otter's Jug-Band Christmas</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Christmas_Toy" title="The Christmas Toy">The Christmas Toy</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sesame_Street:_20_and_Still_Counting" title="Sesame Street: 20 and Still Counting">Sesame Street: 20 and Still Counting</a></i>, <i>Henson's Place</i>, <i>Billy Bunny's Animal Songs</i>, the original <i><a href="/wiki/Dog_City" title="Dog City">Dog City</a></i> special, and <i>Donna's Day</i>. While some of these specials have since been released uncut, current releases of <i>Emmet Otter's Jug-Band Christmas</i> and <i>The Christmas Toy</i> have removed the appearances by Kermit the Frog.</p>
<p>Disney began gradually reintroducing the franchise to the mainstream in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYFuzzy_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYFuzzy-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WallStreet_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-WallStreet-5">[5]</a></sup> As a method of regaining a wider audience, Disney began to produce and air their own comedy shorts on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. After the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bohemian_Rhapsody_(The_Muppets)" title="Bohemian Rhapsody (The Muppets)">Muppets: Bohemian Rhapsody</a>" was posted on the Muppet Studios' YouTube channel, it ultimately gained 50 million views and took home two <a href="/wiki/Webby_Award" title="Webby Award">Webby Awards</a>. Videos are being posted on the site regularly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> That same year, the Muppets starred in a web series with <a href="/wiki/Cat_Cora" title="Cat Cora">Cat Cora</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Kitchen_with_Cat_Cora" title="The Muppets Kitchen with Cat Cora">The Muppets Kitchen With Cat Cora</a></i>, where cooking demonstrations are shown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> A television special, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Muppets_Christmas:_Letters_to_Santa" title="A Muppets Christmas: Letters to Santa">A Muppets Christmas: Letters to Santa</a></i>, premiered on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> on December 17, 2008. It was released on DVD on September 29, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In 2010, Disney used the Muppets to promote their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volunteerism" title="Volunteerism">volunteerism</a> program at the company's theme parks.That same year, a Halloween special featuring the Muppets was expected to air on ABC in October 2010 but was shelved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s:_Resurgence">2010s: Resurgence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s: Resurgence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, the Muppets were featured in an <a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_(film)" title="The Muppets (film)">eponymously-titled seventh film</a>, intended to serve as a "creative <a href="/wiki/Reboot_(fiction)" title="Reboot (fiction)">reboot</a>" for the characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarMojo_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-VarMojo-27">[27]</a></sup> Disney had been furthering development on a Muppet film since 2008 when it considered adapting an unused screenplay written by Jerry Juhl. Directed by <a href="/wiki/James_Bobin" title="James Bobin">James Bobin</a>, written by <a href="/wiki/Jason_Segel" title="Jason Segel">Jason Segel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Stoller" title="Nicholas Stoller">Nicholas Stoller</a>, and starring Segel, <a href="/wiki/Amy_Adams" title="Amy Adams">Amy Adams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Cooper" title="Chris Cooper">Chris Cooper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rashida_Jones" title="Rashida Jones">Rashida Jones</a>, the film was met with widespread critical acclaim, commercial success, and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> win for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Original Song">Best Original Song</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> During the film's publicity campaign, the Muppets appeared in promotional advertisements and in effusive marketing efforts by Disney and were also featured in a promotional video for <a href="/wiki/Google%2B" title="Google+">Google+</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In March of the following year, the Muppets received a collective star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HollywoodWalk_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-HollywoodWalk-30">[30]</a></sup> That same year, the Muppets hosted a <i><a href="/wiki/Just_for_Laughs" title="Just for Laughs">Just for Laughs</a></i> comedy gala in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the successful performance of <i>The Muppets</i>, Disney greenlit a sequel in March 2012, with Bobin and Stoller returning to direct and write, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets_Most_Wanted" title="Muppets Most Wanted">Muppets Most Wanted</a></i> was released on in 2014 with <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Gervais" title="Ricky Gervais">Ricky Gervais</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tina_Fey" title="Tina Fey">Tina Fey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Burrell" title="Ty Burrell">Ty Burrell</a> in supporting roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Disney_Theatrical_Productions" title="Disney Theatrical Productions">Disney Theatrical Productions</a> revealed in 2013 that a live show based on the Muppets was in active development and that a 15-minute show had been conducted by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Schumacher" title="Thomas Schumacher">Thomas Schumacher</a> to see how the technical components would work out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> <i>Muppets Moments</i>, a series of interstitial shorts, premiered on <a href="/wiki/Disney_Junior" title="Disney Junior">Disney Junior</a> on April 3, 2015. The short-form series features conversations between the Muppets and young children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the release of <i>Muppets Most Wanted</i>, Disney was interested in expanding the Muppets' presence across various media platforms, particularly in television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Discussions for a new <a href="/wiki/Prime_time" title="Prime time">primetime series</a> began internally within the Muppets Studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarMojo_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-VarMojo-27">[27]</a></sup> By April 2015, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Prady" title="Bill Prady">Bill Prady</a> was commissioned to write a script for a pilot with the working title, <i>Muppets 2015</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In May 2015, ABC announced that it had greenlit a new primetime television series titled, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_(TV_series)" title="The Muppets (TV series)">The Muppets</a></i>, co-created by Prady and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Kushell" title="Bob Kushell">Bob Kushell</a>, and directed by <a href="/wiki/Randall_Einhorn" title="Randall Einhorn">Randall Einhorn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wagmeister_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wagmeister-39">[39]</a></sup> The series premiered on September 22, 2015, in the United States, and ended on March 1, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SeriesEnd_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-SeriesEnd-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>

<p>Famous Muppets from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show" title="The Muppet Show">The Muppet Show</a></i> and related spin-offs include <a href="/wiki/Kermit_the_Frog" title="Kermit the Frog">Kermit the Frog</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miss_Piggy" title="Miss Piggy">Miss Piggy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fozzie_Bear" title="Fozzie Bear">Fozzie Bear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gonzo_(Muppet)" title="Gonzo (Muppet)">Gonzo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rowlf_the_Dog" title="Rowlf the Dog">Rowlf the Dog</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scooter_(Muppet)" title="Scooter (Muppet)">Scooter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rizzo_the_Rat" title="Rizzo the Rat">Rizzo the Rat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pepe_the_King_Prawn" title="Pepe the King Prawn">Pepe the King Prawn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bunsen_Honeydew" title="Bunsen Honeydew">Bunsen Honeydew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beaker_(Muppet)" title="Beaker (Muppet)">Beaker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Statler_and_Waldorf" title="Statler and Waldorf">Statler and Waldorf</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Chef" title="Swedish Chef">Swedish Chef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Eagle" title="Sam Eagle">Sam Eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walter_(Muppet)" title="Walter (Muppet)">Walter</a>, and the band <a href="/wiki/Dr._Teeth_and_The_Electric_Mayhem" title="Dr. Teeth and The Electric Mayhem">Dr. Teeth and The Electric Mayhem</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Dr._Teeth_and_The_Electric_Mayhem#Dr._Teeth" title="Dr. Teeth and The Electric Mayhem">Dr. Teeth</a> on keyboard, <a href="/wiki/Animal_(Muppet)" title="Animal (Muppet)">Animal</a> on the drums, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Teeth_and_The_Electric_Mayhem#Floyd_Pepper" title="Dr. Teeth and The Electric Mayhem">Floyd Pepper</a> on bass, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Janice_(muppet)" title="Janice (muppet)">Janice</a> on lead guitar, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Teeth_and_The_Electric_Mayhem#Zoot" title="Dr. Teeth and The Electric Mayhem">Zoot</a> on saxophone, and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Teeth_and_The_Electric_Mayhem#Lips" title="Dr. Teeth and The Electric Mayhem">Lips</a> on trumpet. <p>Television shows featuring Muppets have included <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jimmy_Dean_Show" title="The Jimmy Dean Show">The Jimmy Dean Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show" title="The Muppet Show">The Muppet Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fraggle_Rock" title="Fraggle Rock">Fraggle Rock</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bear_in_the_Big_Blue_House" title="Bear in the Big Blue House">Bear in the Big Blue House</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Henson_Hour" title="The Jim Henson Hour">The Jim Henson Hour</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets_Tonight" title="Muppets Tonight">Muppets Tonight</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Statler_and_Waldorf:_From_the_Balcony" title="Statler and Waldorf: From the Balcony">Statler and Waldorf: From the Balcony</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_(TV_series)" title="The Muppets (TV series)">The Muppets</a></i>. A recurring adult-oriented cast of Muppets (in a setting known as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Land_of_Gorch" title="The Land of Gorch">The Land of Gorch</a></i>) were featured throughout the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>. Guest stars on some of these programs have occasionally had Muppet versions of themselves. It was a regular practice for the first few episodes of <i>The Muppet Show</i>, and <a href="/wiki/ZZ_Top" title="ZZ Top">ZZ Top</a>, among others, have appeared as Muppet versions of themselves on <i>Sesame Street</i>. Muppet versions of real people have also appeared in other shows, such as in <i><a href="/wiki/30_Rock" title="30 Rock">30 Rock</a></i>.</p>
<p>The puppet characters of other Henson productions, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Labyrinth_(film)" title="Labyrinth (film)">Labyrinth</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Crystal" title="The Dark Crystal">The Dark Crystal</a></i>, are not considered Muppets,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> as they were made by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Henson%27s_Creature_Shop" title="Jim Henson's Creature Shop">Jim Henson's Creature Shop</a>, rather than by Henson's Muppet Workshop. The puppet casts of <i><a href="/wiki/Puppet_Up!" title="Puppet Up!">Puppet Up!</a></i> and <i>Tinseltown</i> are also not Muppets as they were made by the Jim Henson Company after the sale of the Muppets to Disney in 2004. The <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> character, <a href="/wiki/Yoda" title="Yoda">Yoda</a>, was performed and voiced by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Oz" title="Frank Oz">Frank Oz</a>, one of Henson's regular performers, and is often genericized as a Muppet in media and reference works; he is not, however, and Henson's organization was not involved in the character's conception.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Performers">Performers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Performers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the Muppets' formation, Jim and Jane Henson were the group's only performers. In 1961, Jane retired to focus on raising their children. Seeking additional performers, Jim came into contact with <a href="/wiki/Frank_Oz" title="Frank Oz">Frank Oz</a> that same year. Although interested, Oz declined participation due to his young age and commitment to high school, and instead suggested <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Juhl" title="Jerry Juhl">Jerry Juhl</a>, a fellow puppeteer who worked alongside Oz at the Vagabond Puppet Theater in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>. Upon graduating, Oz subsequently joined in August 1963. When <i>The Muppet Show</i> began, the main cast of performers grew to include Henson, Oz, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Goelz" title="Dave Goelz">Dave Goelz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Nelson" title="Jerry Nelson">Jerry Nelson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hunt_(puppeteer)" title="Richard Hunt (puppeteer)">Richard Hunt</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Steve_Whitmire" title="Steve Whitmire">Steve Whitmire</a>, while Juhl became head writer for the series. From <i>The Muppet Show</i> onwards, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Clash" title="Kevin Clash">Kevin Clash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kathryn_Mullen" title="Kathryn Mullen">Kathryn Mullen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louise_Gold" title="Louise Gold">Louise Gold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karen_Prell" title="Karen Prell">Karen Prell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caroll_Spinney" title="Caroll Spinney">Caroll Spinney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Henson" title="Brian Henson">Brian Henson</a> performed several minor characters and often assisted the main performers with puppeteering. Nearly all of the aforementioned puppeteers performed characters across a variety of media, including <i>The Muppet Show,</i> <i>Sesame Street</i>, <i>Fraggle Rock</i>, and other Henson-related projects.</p>
<p>Jim Henson, Hunt, and Nelson continued performing until their deaths in 1990, 1992, and 2012, respectively. Whitmire and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Barretta" title="Bill Barretta">Bill Barretta</a>, who became one of the group's main performers in the 1990s, adopted Henson's characters. Hunt's characters remained without a stable performer throughout the 1990s and 2000s, until <a href="/wiki/David_Rudman" title="David Rudman">David Rudman</a> began performing such characters in the late 2000s. Oz continued performing until his retirement from puppeteering in 2000; <a href="/wiki/Eric_Jacobson" title="Eric Jacobson">Eric Jacobson</a> took over his characters two years after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarMojo_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-VarMojo-27">[27]</a></sup> At Nelson's behest, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Vogel_(puppeteer)" title="Matt Vogel (puppeteer)">Matt Vogel</a> gradually assumed performing duties for his characters beginning in 2008. The Muppets are currently performed by a cast of seven principal puppeteers: Whitmire, Jacobson, Goelz, Barretta, Rudman, Vogel, and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Linz" title="Peter Linz">Peter Linz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarMojo_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-VarMojo-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_performance">Design and performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Design and performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The majority of the Muppets are designed as a combination of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rod_puppets" title="Rod puppets">rod puppets</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hand_puppets" title="Hand puppets">hand puppets</a>. A common facial design for a Muppet is a character with a very large mouth and big protruding eyes. Since Disney's acquisition of the Muppets, newer models of the characters are produced and maintained by <a href="/wiki/Puppet_Heap" title="Puppet Heap">Puppet Heap</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barnes1_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-barnes1-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>The puppets are often molded or carved out of various types of foam, and then covered with fleece, fur, or other felt-like material. Muppets may represent humans, <a href="/wiki/Anthropomorphism" title="Anthropomorphism">anthropomorphic</a> animals, realistic animals, robots, anthropomorphic objects, extraterrestrial creatures, mythical beings or other unidentified, newly imagined creatures, monsters, or abstract characters.</p>
<p>Muppets are distinguished from <a href="/wiki/Ventriloquism" title="Ventriloquism">ventriloquist</a> "dummies"/"puppets", which are typically animated only in the head and face, in that their arms or other features are also mobile and expressive. Muppets are typically made of softer materials. They are also presented as being independent of the puppeteer, who is usually not visible—hidden behind a set or outside of the camera frame. Using the camera frame as the "stage" was an innovation of the Muppets. Previously on television, there would typically be a stage hiding the performers, as if in a live presentation. Sometimes they are seen full-bodied. This is done by using invisible strings to move the characters' bodies and mouths, and then adding the voices later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since Disney's acquisition of the Muppets, newer models of the characters are produced and maintained by <a href="/wiki/Puppet_Heap" title="Puppet Heap">Puppet Heap</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barnes1_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-barnes1-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>The puppeteer, often dubbed as the "Muppet performer", holds the Muppet above his head or in front of his body, with one hand operating the head and mouth and the other manipulating the hands and arms, either with two separate control rods or by "wearing" the hands like gloves. One consequence of this design is that most Muppets are left-handed as the puppeteer uses his right hand to operate the head while operating the arm rod with his left hand. There are many other common designs and means of operation. In advanced Muppets, several puppeteers may control a single character; the performer who controls the mouth usually provides the voice for the character. As technology has evolved, the Jim Henson team and other puppeteers have developed an enormous variety of means to operate Muppets for film and television, including the use of suspended rigs, internal motors, remote radio control, and computer enhanced and superimposed images. Creative use of a mix of technologies has allowed for scenes in which Muppets appear to be riding a bicycle, rowing a boat, and even dancing on-stage with no puppeteer in sight.</p>
<p>Muppets tend to develop, as writer Michael Davis put it, "organically", meaning that the puppeteers take time, often up to a year, slowly developing their characters and voices. Muppets are also, as Davis said, "test-driven, passed around from one Henson troupe member to another in the hope of finding the perfect human-Muppet match".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> When interacting with Muppets, children tended to act as though the Muppets were living creatures, even when they could see the puppeteers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Muppets appear at the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Parks_and_Resorts" title="Walt Disney Parks and Resorts">Walt Disney Parks and Resorts</a>, having first made appearances at <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World" title="Walt Disney World">Walt Disney World</a> in 1990. Their first featured attraction, <i>Here Comes the Muppets</i>, was a live stage show that opened shortly after Jim Henson's death and ran at <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Hollywood_Studios" title="Disney's Hollywood Studios">Disney's Hollywood Studios</a> (known then as Disney-MGM Studios) for a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-48">[48]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Muppet*Vision_3D" title="Muppet*Vision 3D">Muppet*Vision 3D</a>, a 4D film attraction that uses <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio-animatronic" title="Audio-animatronic">audio-animatronic</a> Muppets and 4D effects, then opened at Disney's Hollywood Studios on May 16, 1991. The attraction is notable for being the final Muppets project to be produced by Jim Henson. The attraction exists in <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Hollywood_Studios#Muppets_Courtyard" title="Disney's Hollywood Studios">Muppets Courtyard</a>, a section of the park themed to the Muppets, which also features themed restaurants, gift shops, and restrooms. Muppet*Vision 3D had a subsequent opening at <a href="/wiki/Disney_California_Adventure" title="Disney California Adventure">Disney California Adventure</a>, on February 8, 2001, and remained there until its closure in 2014.</p>
<p>In addition to their main presence at Disney's Hollywood Studios, the Muppets also appear in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Present...Great_Moments_in_American_History" title="The Muppets Present...Great Moments in American History">Great Moments in American History</a></i>, a live show at the <a href="/wiki/Magic_Kingdom" title="Magic Kingdom">Magic Kingdom</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Muppet_Mobile_Lab" title="Muppet Mobile Lab">Muppet Mobile Lab</a> at <a href="/wiki/Epcot" title="Epcot">Epcot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> The latter attraction is a free-roving vehicle with <a href="/wiki/Audio-Animatronics" title="Audio-Animatronics">audio-animatronics</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bunsen_Honeydew" title="Bunsen Honeydew">Bunsen Honeydew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beaker_(Muppet)" title="Beaker (Muppet)">Beaker</a>. As part of Disney's Living Character Initiative, it premiered in 2007 at Epcot<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:5_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-:5-51">[51]</a></sup> and was later previewed at Disney California Adventure and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Disneyland" title="Hong Kong Disneyland">Hong Kong Disneyland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:6_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-:6-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, the Muppets were the face of the "<a href="/wiki/Give_a_Day,_Get_a_Disney_Day" title="Give a Day, Get a Disney Day">Give a Day, Get a Disney Day</a>" charity campaign. Guests could register for a select service activity on the Disney website, and in return for completing the service work, participants could print a voucher for a free one-day admission ticket to <a href="/wiki/Disneyland" title="Disneyland">Disneyland</a> or Walt Disney World Resort. The Muppets appeared in television and print ads for the campaign and were featured prominently on the campaign's website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>Disney has released numerous <a href="/wiki/Disney_pin_trading" title="Disney pin trading">collector pins</a> featuring the Muppets since 2004. These include Limited Edition pins, <a href="/wiki/Hidden_Mickey" title="Hidden Mickey">Hidden Mickey</a> pin collections, mystery pin sets, 2008 pin sets promoting <i>The Muppets</i>, cast lanyard pins, and assorted individual rack pins. Over 100 pins displaying the characters have been released overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Publishing">Publishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Muppets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Publishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the late 1970s, numerous Muppet-related comic books have been released over the years. The first <a href="/wiki/Comic_strip" title="Comic strip">comic strips</a> based on the Muppets appeared on September 21, 1981, in over 500 daily newspapers, just months after <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show" title="The Muppet Show">The Muppet Show</a></i> ended its five-year run. <i>The Muppets Comic Strip</i> was printed daily from 1981 to 1986. By the end of its initial run, the comic strip was seen in over 660 newspapers worldwide. Special strips were also created in color, exclusively for issues of <i>Muppet Magazine</i>.</p>
<p>The only film in the franchise to see a comic book adaptation was <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Take_Manhattan" title="The Muppets Take Manhattan">The Muppets Take Manhattan</a></i>. The <a href="/wiki/The_Muppets_Take_Manhattan#Marketing" title="The Muppets Take Manhattan">comic book series</a> was adapted by Marvel Comics in 1984, as the 68-page story in Marvel Super Special No. 32, August. The adaptation was later re-printed into three limited series issues, released under Marvel's <a href="/wiki/Star_Comics" title="Star Comics">Star Comics</a> imprint (November 1984 – January 1985).</p>
<p>In the wake of the success of the <i><a href="/wiki/Muppet_Babies" title="Muppet Babies">Muppet Babies</a></i> television show, Star Comics began releasing the <a href="/wiki/Muppet_Babies#Comics" title="Muppet Babies">Muppet Babies comic book</a> title on a bi-monthly basis. These were original stories, not adaptations of the show's episodes.</p>
<p>In the final <i><a href="/wiki/Disney_Adventures" title="Disney Adventures">Disney Adventures</a></i> issue, with a cover date of November 2007, a one-page story single strip focusing on Fozzie Bear, Smedley, Statler, and Waldorf (with a cameo by Scooter) was released. <a href="/wiki/Roger_Langridge" title="Roger Langridge">Roger Langridge</a> wrote and drew the comics intending it to be more long running.</p>
<p>In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Boom!_Studios" title="Boom! Studios">Boom! Studios</a> began publishing <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show_(comics)" title="The Muppet Show (comics)">The Muppet Show</a></i>, a mini-series based on the eponymous television show and written and drawn by <a href="/wiki/Roger_Langridge" title="Roger Langridge">Roger Langridge</a>. An ongoing series titled <i>The Muppet Show: The Comic Book</i> followed and ran for eleven issues. Additionally, Boom! Studios also published Muppet fairy-tale comic adaptations similar to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Christmas_Carol" title="The Muppet Christmas Carol">The Muppet Christmas Carol</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Muppet_Treasure_Island" title="Muppet Treasure Island">Muppet Treasure Island</a></i>. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> took over the publishing duties for the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>A comic strip by <a href="/wiki/Guy_Gilchrist" title="Guy Gilchrist">Guy Gilchrist</a> and Brad Gilchrist circulated in newspapers during the 1980s. Many of the strips were compiled in various book collections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Muppet Magazine</i> was published from 1983 to 1989. The magazine took on the format of being "by" the Muppets more than about them and had such features as celebrity interviews and comic stories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>

<p>The popularity of the Muppets has been so expansive that the characters have been viewed by the media as real celebrities in their own right.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The Muppets have received their own collective star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>, with Kermit having his own individual star as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HollywoodWalk_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-HollywoodWalk-30">[30]</a></sup> The characters have also presented at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Awards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Awards</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> made <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearances</a> in such feature films as <i><a href="/wiki/Rocky_III" title="Rocky III">Rocky III</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/An_American_Werewolf_in_London" title="An American Werewolf in London">An American Werewolf in London</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Magorium%27s_Wonder_Emporium" title="Mr. Magorium's Wonder Emporium">Mr. Magorium's Wonder Emporium</a></i>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> and have been interviewed on the <a href="/wiki/News_magazine" title="News magazine">news magazine</a> <i><a href="/wiki/60_Minutes" title="60 Minutes">60 Minutes</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Kermit_the_Frog" title="Kermit the Frog">Kermit the Frog</a> was interviewed early on in <a href="/wiki/Jon_Stewart" title="Jon Stewart">Jon Stewart</a>'s run on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Show" title="The Daily Show">The Daily Show</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> guest hosted <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show" title="The Tonight Show">The Tonight Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Extreme_Makeover:_Home_Edition" title="Extreme Makeover: Home Edition">Extreme Makeover: Home Edition</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Funniest_Home_Videos" title="America's Funniest Home Videos">America's Funniest Home Videos</a></i> and an <a href="/wiki/April_Fools%27_Day" title="April Fools' Day">April Fools' Day</a> edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Larry_King_Live" title="Larry King Live">Larry King Live</a></i>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> and has served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Marshal" title="Grand Marshal">Grand Marshal</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tournament_of_Roses_Parade" title="Tournament of Roses Parade">Tournament of Roses Parade</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> The characters also appeared in-character on such sitcoms and dramas as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Show" title="The Cosby Show">The Cosby Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a>,</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Torkelsons" title="The Torkelsons">The Torkelsons</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The music video for the <a href="/wiki/Weezer" title="Weezer">Weezer</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Keep_Fishin%27" title="Keep Fishin'">Keep Fishin'</a>" is premised on the band performing on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show" title="The Muppet Show">The Muppet Show</a></i> and features appearances by several characters. On September 28, 2005, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">United States Postal Service</a> released a <i>Jim Henson and the Muppets</i> postage stamp series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> The Muppets also appeared on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dick_Clark%27s_New_Year%27s_Rockin%27_Eve_with_Ryan_Seacrest" title="Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve with Ryan Seacrest">Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve</a></i> for the 2008 countdown on December 31, 2007. Kermit, Rizzo, and others welcomed in the new year with a series of messages to welcome viewers back from the advertising breaks. After one such segment, with Kermit in <a href="/wiki/Times_Square" title="Times Square">Times Square</a>, co-host <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Seacrest" title="Ryan Seacrest">Ryan Seacrest</a> thanked his pal "Kerms" for the help bringing in '08.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> Miss Piggy has appeared as a guest on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_Craig_Ferguson" title="The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson">The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson</a></i> and Kermit the Frog appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Squares" title="Hollywood Squares">Hollywood Squares</a></i> and as one of the celebrity commentators on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s <i>I Love</i> documentary series.</p>
<p>On July 25, 2007, the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Puppetry_Arts" title="Center for Puppetry Arts">Center for Puppetry Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> announced the opening of a new Jim Henson Wing, which will house anywhere from 500 to 700 retired Muppets. The new wing will also include films, sketches, and other materials from the Jim Henson Company archives. The wing was originally slated to open in 2012 but has been delayed by a lack of funding and rescheduled for a possible 2014 or 2015 debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>Muppet-like and Muppet-inspired puppets star in the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a>-winning Broadway musical <i><a href="/wiki/Avenue_Q" title="Avenue Q">Avenue Q</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a>'s film, <i><a href="/wiki/Meet_the_Feebles" title="Meet the Feebles">Meet the Feebles</a></i> is another parody of the Muppets. A vomit-spewing Kermit the Frog was a recurring character on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="Late Night with Conan O'Brien">Late Night with Conan O'Brien</a></i>, and the Muppets were frequently preempted at the beginning of episodes for the Canadian series <i><a href="/wiki/You_Can%27t_Do_That_on_Television" title="You Can't Do That on Television">You Can't Do That on Television</a>.</i> Seth Green's short-lived show <i><a href="/wiki/Greg_the_Bunny" title="Greg the Bunny">Greg the Bunny</a></i> was about sentient hand-puppets working in a Muppet-like children's show. Many other films and television shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Guy" title="Family Guy">Family Guy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Robot_Chicken" title="Robot Chicken">Robot Chicken</a></i> have referenced the Muppets.</p>
