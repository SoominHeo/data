<p><i><b>The Big Year</b></i> is a 2011 American <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/David_Frankel" title="David Frankel">David Frankel</a>, written by <a href="/wiki/Howard_Franklin" title="Howard Franklin">Howard Franklin</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Jack_Black" title="Jack Black">Jack Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Martin" title="Steve Martin">Steve Martin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Owen_Wilson" title="Owen Wilson">Owen Wilson</a>. It was based on the nonfiction book <i>The Big Year: A Tale of Man, Nature and Fowl Obsession</i> which was written by journalist Mark Obmascik. The book followed three men on a quest for a <a href="/wiki/Big_year" title="Big year">Big Year</a> - a competition among <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birders" title="Birders">birders</a> to see who can see and identify the greatest number of species of birds in North America (north of Mexico) in a calendar year. The film uses the same premise with fictional characters loosely modeled on the actual personalities.</p>
<p>Filming took place from May to July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TBYRef2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TBYRef2-3">[3]</a></sup> The film was released on October 14, 2011, in the United States and was a <a href="/wiki/Box_office_bomb" title="Box office bomb">box office bomb</a>, grossing only $7.4 million against its $41 million budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TBYRef1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TBYRef1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The film follows three seasoned birders who each set out to achieve a Big Year. They are Brad Harris (<a href="/wiki/Jack_Black" title="Jack Black">Jack Black</a>), a 36-year-old computer programmer based in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>; Stu Preissler (<a href="/wiki/Steve_Martin" title="Steve Martin">Steve Martin</a>), founder and CEO of a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> company bearing his name; and a roofing contractor named Kenny Bostick (<a href="/wiki/Owen_Wilson" title="Owen Wilson">Owen Wilson</a>), who holds the current Big Year record of 732 birds.</p>
<p>Bostick is obsessively possessive of his record, but his third wife Jessica (<a href="/wiki/Rosamund_Pike" title="Rosamund Pike">Rosamund Pike</a>) is concerned; this was supposed to be the year they focused on conceiving a child. She also believes that Bostick's birding obsession is what destroyed his previous marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brad is a skilled birder who can identify nearly any species solely by sound. He hates his job maintaining the operational software of a nuclear power plant in <a href="/wiki/Lansdowne,_Pennsylvania" title="Lansdowne, Pennsylvania">Lansdowne, Pennsylvania</a>. Living with his parents after a failed marriage, an aborted career at Dell, and dropping out of grad school, he is a "typical Jack Black underachiever".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-5">[5]</a></sup> He hopes that doing a Big Year will give him a sense of purpose and possibly even make his father proud of him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McCarthy_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-McCarthy-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stu is the founder and CEO of an enormous <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>-based chemical conglomerate which he built from the ground up, starting in his garage. After decades of corporate success, he is ready to retire to <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a> with his architect wife. Fear of an empty schedule led him to come back from a previous retirement, but now he wants to leave his company in the hands of his two lieutenants (Kevin Pollak and Joel McHale). The company is in the middle of complicated negotiations to merge with a competitor, so his two anointed successors keep calling him back to New York for important meetings; to some extent he is a "prisoner of his own success".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-5">[5]</a></sup> A Big Year has been his lifelong dream and he's pursuing it with the full support of his wife.</p>
<p>The movie portrays various incidents that take place while the trio compete with each other to achieve the world record of sighting the highest number of birds during the Big year event.</p>
<p>All three birders are coming to understand the cost of their birding obsessions. The Big year will result in Brad developing an all new bonding with his parents and makes Stu's bonding with his family even better. On the other hand Bostick's third marriage will also be on it's way to break.</p>
<p>Bostick races home from yet another birding trip to keep an important appointment with his wife at a fertility clinic. He is literally at the front door of the clinic when he receives a report of a sighting of a <a href="/wiki/Snowy_owl" title="Snowy owl">snowy owl</a>, his most coveted and elusive bird. Despite the fact that his wife is waiting inside to have her eggs harvested, fertilized and implanted after undergoing months of hormone injections, he speeds back to the airport and phones in an obviously made-up excuse for his absence. His wife returns alone to their big house, and screams in frustration inside the empty nursery. When Bostick finally returns home after the (fruitless) search for the snowy owl, she tells him she still loves him but can't be his wife anymore.</p>
<p>As the year draws to a close, Stu enjoys his newborn grandson; Brad finds a new relationship with fellow bider Ellie who had just broken up with her boyfriend ; and Bostick finally finds the Snowy Owl that eluded him for a long time, however his obsession of being the best birder in the world cost him his marriage.</p>
<p>At the end of Big year Stu and Brad are very close friends. Stu is now happily retired and Brad is happy man with his new girl friend Ellie who also share a lot of interest in Birding. Stu and Brad congratulate each other on "a very Big Year.", after they each sighted 700+ bird species that year.</p>
<p>The Big Year results are published and Stu phones Brad with the news. Bostick is first with 755, a new record; Brad came in second; Stu was fourth. Brad opines that "<i>he</i> got more birds, but <i>we</i> got more everything," as he looks at Ellie, who has come for a weekend visit. Stu smiles, looking at his wife.</p>
<p>The film ends with Brad and Ellie cozily birding together on a rocky coastline, while Brad confesses that birding is no longer the biggest part of his life. Stu, contented in retirement, is hiking with his toddler grandson (already enamored by birds) in the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rockies</a>. And Bostick is on a birding adventure in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, alone and gazing wistfully at a happy couple walking with their newborn child.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Big_Year&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> was done from May 3 to July 30, 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Jack Black's fall on <a href="/wiki/Attu_Island" title="Attu Island">Attu Island</a> was unscripted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (July 2012)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:The_Big_Year#Dubious" title="Talk:The Big Year">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Big_Year&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film received mixed reviews from critics. The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood_Reporter" title="Hollywood Reporter">Hollywood Reporter</a></i> described it as a "genial, amusing and somewhat unfathomable" film;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McCarthy_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-McCarthy-6">[6]</a></sup> the <i><a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Post-Gazette" title="Pittsburgh Post-Gazette">Pittsburgh Post-Gazette</a></i> called it a "gentle, light-hearted comedy" about "people trying to be the best, following their dreams and enjoying the wonder of birds".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It holds a 40% rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> with the consensus stating: "Though made with care and affection for its characters, <i>The Big Year</i> plods along, rarely reaching any comedic heights." <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> polls reported that the average grade moviegoers gave the film was a "B-minus" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was a box office failure, despite the established stars like Martin, Black and Wilson as the leads. Based on a budget of $41 million, it took in just $7.4 million in ticket sales worldwide according to Box Office Mojo.</p>
