<p><b>The Struts</b> are an English rock band from <a href="/wiki/Derby" title="Derby">Derby, Derbyshire</a>. The band consists of vocalist Luke Spiller, guitarist Adam Slack, bassist Jed Elliott, and drummer Gethin Davies. Formed in 2009, the original lineup was composed of Spiller, Slack, bassist Jamie Binns and drummer Rafe Thomas.</p>
<p>The band's influences include <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" title="Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Def_Leppard" title="Def Leppard">Def Leppard</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Smiths" title="The Smiths">The Smiths</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oasis_(band)" title="Oasis (band)">Oasis</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Libertines" title="The Libertines">The Libertines</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Strokes" title="The Strokes">The Strokes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/My_Chemical_Romance" title="My Chemical Romance">My Chemical Romance</a>. The Struts have been described as "unabashedly over the top retro-fetishist classic rock" and "glamourous and dangerous".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_Bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic_Bio-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popular_anti_social_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Popular_anti_social-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hits_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hits-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Struts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Singer-songwriter Luke Spiller was raised in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> family. His exposure to music was mainly limited to <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel music</a>, until the age of seven, when he discovered <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Off_the_Wall_(album)" title="Off the Wall (album)">Off the Wall</a></i>. Inspired by Jackson, his first aspirations were to be a contemporary dancer, but by the age of 11, he was listening to <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Cohen" title="Leonard Cohen">Leonard Cohen</a>, and in addition to pretending to be Jackson in front of a mirror, he was imitating <a href="/wiki/Bon_Scott" title="Bon Scott">Bon Scott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>. He began playing in bands as a teenager, only starting to sing at age 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popular_anti_social_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Popular_anti_social-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gettothefront.co.uk_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-gettothefront.co.uk-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Birmingham_Mail_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Birmingham_Mail-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMN_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-WMN-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Radio-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Spiller, then living in <a href="/wiki/Clevedon" title="Clevedon">Clevedon</a>, met Derby-based guitarist/songwriter Adam Slack, who had also been playing with bands since his teens. At the time, each of their bands were "coming apart," and Slack and Spiller quickly connected musically. Spiller moved to Derby, where he and Slack lived, wrote, and recorded together for nearly three years. They recruited mutual friends Jamie Binns and Rafe Thomas to play bass and drums. In 2012, Binns and Thomas were replaced with bassist Jed Elliott and drummer Gethin Davies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coatsville_Times_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Coatsville_Times-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>The band chose the name The Struts based on an offhand comment made at a rehearsal. Spiller said in a 2015 interview, "I was just moving around and somebody was like, 'you strut around a lot.' From then on, we knew we had a great name. We couldn't believe it hadn't been taken."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popular_anti_social_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Popular_anti_social-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Birmingham_Mail_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Birmingham_Mail-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMN_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-WMN-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Radio-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band played frequently throughout the UK. They soon developed a following, and released their first single "I Just Know" in June 2012. Signed by Future Records, they were transferred to <a href="/wiki/Virgin_EMI_Records" title="Virgin EMI Records">Virgin EMI</a> after Future's demise and subsequently put out a four-track EP titled <i>Kiss This</i>.</p>
<p>The Struts toured extensively in England and France, and in 2014 performed for 80,000 people as the opener for <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a> in Paris. That summer, they did a 24-date tour, which included a gig at the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight_Festival" title="Isle of Wight Festival">Isle of Wight Festival</a>. Spiller wore clothing designed for him by <a href="/wiki/Zandra_Rhodes" title="Zandra Rhodes">Zandra Rhodes</a>, who formerly dressed Freddie Mercury. The production of Rhodes' clothing for Spiller was later featured on the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> documentary <i>Oh You Pretty Things: The Story of Music and Fashion</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11">[11]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Derby_Telegraph_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Derby_Telegraph-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>As The Struts reputation as a live band grew, their audience increased, and their shows were frequently sold-out. Spiller's talent as a frontman was often noted by the press and reviews described him as "a force of nature" and as a "physical, mesmerising entertainer... vocally strong, expressive and blessed with excellent range". Spiller's looks and stage presence lead Roger Goodgroves of <i>Get To The Front</i> to describe him as "the musical love child of Freddie Mercury and Mick Jagger".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gettothefront.co.uk_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-gettothefront.co.uk-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mike_Oldfield" title="Mike Oldfield">Mike Oldfield</a> recruited Spiller to sing on his 2014 album <i><a href="/wiki/Man_on_the_Rocks" title="Man on the Rocks">Man on the Rocks</a></i>. "He did a much better job of sounding like a rock star than me," Oldfield said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-music_news_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-music_news-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Struts first television performance was on <a href="/wiki/Vintage_TV_(TV_channel)" title="Vintage TV (TV channel)">Vintage TV</a>'s 'Live Sessions,' where they appeared alongside established acts including <a href="/wiki/Toyah_(band)" title="Toyah (band)">Toyah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nik_Kershaw" title="Nik Kershaw">Nik Kershaw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judie_Tzuke" title="Judie Tzuke">Judie Tzuke</a>. The show was recorded in January 2014 at the Bedford in Balham and aired on March 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The band played two tracks, 'Kiss This' and 'Could Have Been Me'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_Wants" title="Everybody Wants">Everybody Wants</a></i>, their debut studio album, came out in July 2014. It charted in the UK and received positive reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Derby_Telegraph_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Derby_Telegraph-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In early 2015, The Struts signed to <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polydor" title="Polydor">Polydor</a> and in August of that year they released the EP <i>Have You Heard</i>, their first U.S. release. They began a tour of the States in support of the record in September. 20 dates were sold out, including the band's Los Angeles debut at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Troubador" title="The Troubador">The Troubador</a>, which sold out in 30 minutes. "Could Have Been Me", the lead single, hit No. 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a> Viral Top 50, No. 4 on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> rock singles chart, and in the Top 5 on the U.S. alternative and active rock charts. The video for the track, directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jonas_Ackerland" title="Jonas Ackerland">Jonas Ackerland</a>, had more than 1.5 million views within two months of its Vevo debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hits_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hits-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Struts relocated to Los Angeles in early 2015. Their first television appearance in America was on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i> in August 2015, and in September they were named "Band on the Verge" by <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i>. In December 2015 they performed on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Seth_Meyers" title="Late Night with Seth Meyers">Late Night with Seth Meyers</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vanyaland_Kimmel_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vanyaland_Kimmel-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2016, it was announced that The Struts would release a reissue of <i>Everybody Wants</i> on 4 March on Freesolo/Interscope Records/Universal Music Canada with five songs not included on the original UK release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-press_release_about_the_new_album_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-press_release_about_the_new_album-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes_Style_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes_Style-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2016, they opened for the band <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a> for their Not In This Lifetime... tour at AT&amp;T Park.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Struts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Struts' bassist Jed Elliot has been dating <a href="/wiki/Little_Mix" title="Little Mix">Little Mix</a>'s member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jade_Thirlwall" title="Jade Thirlwall">Jade Thirlwall</a> since January 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> According to Jed, they met in August 2015 after their mutual friend <a href="/wiki/Jake_Roche" title="Jake Roche">Jake Roche</a>, from the band <a href="/wiki/Rixton_(band)" title="Rixton (band)">Rixton</a>, introduced them in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Luke Spiller's girlfriend is model Laura Cartier Millon. They have been dating since February 14, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
