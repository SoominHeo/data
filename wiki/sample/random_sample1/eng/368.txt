<p>The <b>2005 FIFA Confederations Cup</b> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> tournament was the seventh <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="FIFA Confederations Cup">FIFA Confederations Cup</a>. It was held in Germany between 15 June and 29 June 2005, as a prelude to the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>. The tournament was won by <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> winners <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, who defeated <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> 4–1 in the final <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waldstadion_(Frankfurt)" title="Waldstadion (Frankfurt)">Waldstadion</a> in <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>. The final was a rematch of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa América</a> final also won by <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>. It was Brazil's second win at the Confederations Cup.</p>

<p>Originally, <a href="/wiki/Kaiserslautern" title="Kaiserslautern">Kaiserslautern</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fritz-Walter-Stadion" title="Fritz-Walter-Stadion">Fritz-Walter-Stadion</a> was also intended as a venue. However, on 27 May 2004, city authorities withdrew from the bidding process, citing added costs to complete the stadium on time as the reason for the withdrawal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>All five venues were reused for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
