<p><i><b>The Strain</b></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Horror_fiction" title="Horror fiction">horror</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> television series that premiered on <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> on July 13, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was created by <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_del_Toro" title="Guillermo del Toro">Guillermo del Toro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Hogan" title="Chuck Hogan">Chuck Hogan</a>, based on their <a href="/wiki/The_Strain" title="The Strain">novel trilogy of the same name</a>. <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cuse" title="Carlton Cuse">Carlton Cuse</a> serves as executive producer and showrunner. Del Toro and Hogan wrote the pilot episode, "<a href="/wiki/Night_Zero_(The_Strain)" title="Night Zero (The Strain)">Night Zero</a>", which del Toro directed. A thirteen-episode first season was ordered on November 19, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-series_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-series-2">[2]</a></sup> The pilot episode premiered at the ATX Television Festival in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>, in early June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 6, 2014, FX renewed <i>The Strain</i> for a 13-episode second season which premiered on July 12, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-S2-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2_Premiere_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-S2_Premiere-5">[5]</a></sup> On August 7, 2015, FX renewed <i>The Strain</i> for a 10-episode third season which premiered on August 28, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10_episodes_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-10_episodes-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> FX renewed the series for a fourth and final season on September 27, 2016, slated to air in summer 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-final_season_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-final_season-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_cast">Main cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Strain_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Corey_Stoll" title="Corey Stoll">Corey Stoll</a> as Dr. Ephraim "Eph" Goodweather, the head of the <a href="/wiki/Centers_for_Disease_Control_and_Prevention" title="Centers for Disease Control and Prevention">CDC</a> Canary Team in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stoll_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stoll-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/David_Bradley_(actor)" title="David Bradley (actor)">David Bradley</a> as Professor Abraham Setrakian, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holocaust" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a> survivor turned New York <a href="/wiki/Pawnbroker" title="Pawnbroker">pawnbroker</a>. Jim Watson portrays a young Setrakian in <a href="/wiki/Flashback_(narrative)" title="Flashback (narrative)">flashbacks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bradley_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-bradley-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalnews_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-globalnews-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/M%C3%ADa_Maestro" title="Mía Maestro">Mía Maestro</a> as Dr. Nora Martinez, a member of the CDC Canary Team and Eph's closest ally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> (seasons 1–2)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durand" title="Kevin Durand">Kevin Durand</a> as Vasiliy Fet, a once-solitary rat exterminator of <a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainian descent</a>, who is caught up in the fight for humanity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Hyde" title="Jonathan Hyde">Jonathan Hyde</a> as Eldritch Palmer/The Master, an elderly billionaire invalid driven to obtain immortality. Charlie Gallant portrays a young Palmer in flashbacks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydesammel_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-hydesammel-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Sammel" title="Richard Sammel">Richard Sammel</a> as Thomas Eichhorst, an undead vampire acolyte of the Master who has a 70-year history with Setrakian. In his former life, he was a <a href="/wiki/Nazism" title="Nazism">Nazi</a> commander at the <a href="/wiki/Treblinka_extermination_camp" title="Treblinka extermination camp">Treblinka extermination camp</a> in <a href="/wiki/Occupation_of_Poland_(1939%E2%80%9345)" title="Occupation of Poland (1939–45)">German-occupied</a> <a href="/wiki/General_Government" title="General Government">Poland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydesammel_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-hydesammel-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sean_Astin" title="Sean Astin">Sean Astin</a> as Jim Kent, a CDC administrator who works with Eph and Nora.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> (season 1, guest season 2)</li>
<li>Jack Kesy as Gabriel Bolivar/The Master, a philandering rock star with an appetite for recreational drugs and is one of the Master's vampire acolytes before becoming the Master's host body. (seasons 1–2, recurring season 3)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Strain_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Strain_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="612" data-file-width="408" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c6/The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg/200px-The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c6/The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg/300px-The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c6/The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg/400px-The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Strain_promotional_poster.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Promotional poster which was withdrawn from circulation after a number of complaints.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poster_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-poster-36">[36]</a></sup></div>

</div>
<p>In 2006, del Toro pitched <i>The Strain</i> as a television series, but negotiations broke down when the network president at <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox Broadcasting Company</a> asked him to make it a comedy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> An agent suggested expanding the concept as a novel series instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadline_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deadline-38">[38]</a></sup> Del Toro asked Chuck Hogan to co-write the series, explaining: "I've written short stories in Spanish and English. I've written screenplays. But I'm not good at forensic novels. I'm not good at hazmat language and that CSI-style precision. When <a href="/wiki/Bram_Stoker" title="Bram Stoker">Stoker</a> wrote <i><a href="/wiki/Dracula" title="Dracula">Dracula</a></i>, it was very modern, a <i><a href="/wiki/CSI_(franchise)" title="CSI (franchise)">CSI</a></i> sort of novel. I wanted to give <i>The Strain</i> a procedural feel, where everything seems real."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Hogan agreed after reading a page and a half of del Toro's 12-page project outline; the duo collaborated for the first year on a handshake, with no contract or publishing deal in place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The first installment, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Strain" title="The Strain">The Strain</a></i>, was released in 2009. It was followed by 2010's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Fall_(del_Toro_/_Hogan_novel)" title="The Fall (del Toro / Hogan novel)">The Fall</a></i> and 2011's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_Eternal" title="The Night Eternal">The Night Eternal</a></i>.</p>
<p>After the first book's publication, studios and networks began making offers for the film and television adaptation rights, but del Toro and Hogan declined, not wanting a screen version to influence the way they were writing the books. After the third book's publication, the authors talked with every cable network that had expressed interest. FX was deemed most suitable because they wanted to follow the books closely and liked the idea of <i>The Strain</i> as a close-ended series consisting of three to five seasons. Del Toro stated that the first two novels can be covered by one season each, while <i>The Night Eternal</i> may be split into two or three seasons. The author is also open to creative detours that may develop as the series goes on, possibly incorporating material cut from the books. Del Toro intends to direct as many episodes as his busy schedule allows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadline_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-Deadline-38">[38]</a></sup> Before the series order was announced, FX gave the writing team the go-ahead to script another ten episodes, which del Toro rewrote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> FX president <a href="/wiki/John_Landgraf" title="John Landgraf">John Landgraf</a> has stated that the series will consist of "39–65 episodes, no less, no more", adding "What if a television show could be just the length that is optimal for that story?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Regarding the style of the show's production, del Toro stated: "Basically I'm trying to do what I do in my movies which is to show it as a reality, but as a reality that is stylized. It's not like <i>CSI</i> or <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire" title="The Wire">The Wire</a></i>, it's real but it feels a little stylized. But the way the camera work will be is very realistic. We want to keep the camera very documentary even if the look of the show is not. The look of the show is very designed. The style of the camera and the storytelling will be very loose. It will evolve from that feel of reality, and little by little we want to evolve into more stylish, horror feel that requires smoother camera moves, more suspense and atmosphere-driven moments so it will be a mixture. I don't think that mixture has been seen a lot on TV."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>FX ordered a thirteen-episode first season for the series on November 19, 2013, and announced that the series would premiere in July 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-series_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-series-2">[2]</a></sup> In August 2015, del Toro and Cuse announced a five-season plan for the series after the third season renewal, Cuse said, "From the very beginning, we had really talked about the first book being the first season, the second book being the next two seasons and the third book the last two seasons". He also said that beyond the first and second seasons, which are 13 episodes each, the remaining seasons were planned to each consist of 10 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> However, in September 2016, FX announced the series' fourth season would be its last. Cuse stated, "After finishing the writing on season three, Guillermo, Chuck and I looked at our remaining story and felt the best version could be told in one more season."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-final_season_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-final_season-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Strain_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Del Toro and Cuse cast the series together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HitFix1_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-HitFix1-45">[45]</a></sup> Stoll was cast as Eph Goodweather based on his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_in_Paris" title="Midnight in Paris">Midnight in Paris</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HitFix1_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-HitFix1-45">[45]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Hurt" title="John Hurt">John Hurt</a> played Abraham Setrakian in the original version of the pilot, but later dropped out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> The role was recast with <a href="/wiki/David_Bradley_(actor)" title="David Bradley (actor)">David Bradley</a> and his scenes were reshot with Bradley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bradley_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-bradley-11">[11]</a></sup> Del Toro had previously expressed interest in casting <a href="/wiki/Roy_Dotrice" title="Roy Dotrice">Roy Dotrice</a>, who played Setrakian in several live-action advertisements for the first novel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durand" title="Kevin Durand">Kevin Durand</a>, who appeared in Cuse's previous show <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i>, was cast as Vasiliy Fet, although the character was originally created with regular del Toro collaborator <a href="/wiki/Ron_Perlman" title="Ron Perlman">Ron Perlman</a> in mind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HitFix1_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-HitFix1-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Lee_Smith" title="Lauren Lee Smith">Lauren Lee Smith</a> was originally cast as Kelly Goodweather, but was ultimately replaced by <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Brown_(actress)" title="Natalie Brown (actress)">Natalie Brown</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Del Toro wanted <a href="/wiki/Doug_Jones_(actor)" title="Doug Jones (actor)">Doug Jones</a> for a major role, but the actor had to decline because of scheduling conflicts; he later played the Ancient and the Master's previous incarnation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sean_Astin" title="Sean Astin">Sean Astin</a> was cast as Jim Kent because del Toro and Cuse "thought it would be funny to have the most famous sidekick of all times be a betrayer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HitFix1_45-3"><a href="#cite_note-HitFix1-45">[45]</a></sup> For the second season, <a href="/wiki/Max_Charles" title="Max Charles">Max Charles</a> replaced Ben Hyland in the series regular role of Zach Goodweather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zach_recast_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-zach_recast-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Strain_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The pilot episode began principal photography on September 17, 2013, in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Shooting of the pilot was finished on October 31, 2013. FX ordered 13 episodes. Season one was expected to film from November 25, 2013, to April 30, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> A full writing staff was hired to script subsequent episodes. FX reportedly committed $500,000 to creature creation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stoll_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stoll-10">[10]</a></sup> Twelve swords used in the series were provided by <a href="/wiki/Missoula,_Montana" title="Missoula, Montana">Missoula, Montana</a>-based bladesmith company Zombie Tools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Production began for the second season in Toronto in November 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Strain_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The original key art for the series prominently featured a worm, a vector of the vampiric virus, burrowing into a person's eye. Following complaints from members of the public, FX announced that they would replace the artwork on several of their billboards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poster_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-poster-36">[36]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Strain_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first season received generally positive reviews from critics and has a <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> rating of 72 out of 100 based on 38 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the first season holds an 87% "Certified Fresh" rating with an average score of 7.6 out of 10 based on 55 reviews. Its consensus states "<i>The Strain</i> makes the most of its familiar themes through an effective mix of supernatural thrills and B-movie gore – though it may not appeal to everyone".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second season received generally positive reviews and has a Metacritic rating of 66 out of 100 based on 8 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> On Rotten Tomatoes, the season has a rating of 79% with an average score of 7.2 out of 10 based on 19 reviews. Its consensus states "The Strain's gory action helps compensate for an unfocused narrative, while the show's political and philosophical subtext add necessary heft for adult viewers."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>The third season has received generally positive reviews and has a Metacritic rating of 62 out of 100 based on 5 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
