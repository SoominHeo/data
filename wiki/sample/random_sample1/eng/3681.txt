<p><i><b>The Wedding Ringer</b></i> is a 2015 <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> directed and co-written by Jeremy Garelick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Wedding_Ringer_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Wedding_Ringer-5">[5]</a></sup> It stars <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart" title="Kevin Hart">Kevin Hart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Josh_Gad" title="Josh Gad">Josh Gad</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kaley_Cuoco" title="Kaley Cuoco">Kaley Cuoco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Wedding_Ringer_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Wedding_Ringer-5">[5]</a></sup> The film was produced by Adam Fields, <a href="/wiki/Will_Packer_Productions" title="Will Packer Productions">Will Packer Productions</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miramax_Films" title="Miramax Films">Miramax Films</a>, distributed by <a href="/wiki/Screen_Gems" title="Screen Gems">Screen Gems</a>, and released on January 16, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite receiving generally negative reviews, the film was a box office success, grossing over $79 million against a $23 million budget.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jimmy provides <a href="/wiki/Groomsman" title="Groomsman">best man</a> services, through The Best Man Inc., for guys who don't have the friends necessary for a wedding. Doug Harris, a successful tax attorney, and his fiancee Gretchen Palmer are planning for their wedding day. Doug becomes frantic searching for a best man, and is referred to Jimmy's company by his party planner, Edmundo.</p>
<p>Doug asks Jimmy to pull off a "Golden Tux" (seven groomsmen) to match with Gretchen's bridesmaids, which has never been done before. After hearing Doug's plea, Jimmy agrees to be his best man for a fee of $50,000 and all expenses paid. When going over the formalities for the wedding, Doug tells Jimmy his name will be <a href="/wiki/Bic_(company)" title="Bic (company)">Bic</a> <a href="/wiki/Mitchum" title="Mitchum">Mitchum</a>.</p>
<p>Jimmy recruits three of his friends as groomsmen. Fitzgibbons, a criminal who escaped from a federal prison, agrees to be a groomsman because there will be seven bridesmaids to hit on. Lurch agrees in order to get away from his nagging wife. Reggie agrees because there will be good food. Jimmy, his secretary Doris, Fitzgibbons, Lurch, and Reggie, interview people willing to fill the four remaining spots based on their "party trick distractions." They choose Kip, a sexy man with a stutter, Endo, who has three testicles, Bronstein, who can dislocate and relocate his shoulder, and Otis, who can say every sentence backwards.</p>
<p>Doug tells Gretchen that "Bic" flew in from El Salvador for the wedding. Gretchen insists that Bic comes to a family brunch. Doug tells Jimmy that he must act as a military priest. At the brunch, Doug becomes nervous and almost blows his own cover until Jimmy accidentally sets Grandma on fire. They take her to the emergency room, and Jimmy makes up a lie to Ed that Doug used to play football. Ed challenges Jimmy and Doug to a football game with some of his old college teammates who will be at the wedding.</p>
<p>Doug meets his groomsmen, whom Doris has given fake identities based on the last names of famous <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> sports figures—<a href="/wiki/Jim_Plunkett" title="Jim Plunkett">Plunkett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Rambis" title="Kurt Rambis">Rambis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Garvey" title="Steve Garvey">Garvey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lyle_Alzado" title="Lyle Alzado">Alzado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Don_Drysdale" title="Don Drysdale">Drysdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rod_Carew" title="Rod Carew">Carew</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Dickerson" title="Eric Dickerson">Dickerson</a>. Jimmy takes Doug and the groomsmen on fake photo shoots of skydiving, scuba-diving, running a marathon, and climbing mountains.</p>
<p>When Doug begins to have doubts, they visit Edmundo, who tells him that the only key is to please Gretchen and her mother Lois and nothing else matters. To prove how good he is at being a best man, Jimmy takes Doug to a wedding where the best man makes a terrible speech. After, they have drinks and show off their dance moves. Jimmy reveals he once made an excellent best man speech for a minor friend which led to his career as a wedding ringer. Doug reveals that his father was an international tax attorney and moved frequently, so Doug never got to make friends. When his parents died, Doug took over the business, and work consumed him, leaving him without a best man. Jimmy drives Doug home, and reiterates that they are in a business relationship, and Doug, although hurt, agrees.</p>
<p>Jimmy is reminded by Doris that he needs a real friend for himself, and he is motivated to succeed on completing Doug's wedding.</p>
<p>The groomsmen kidnap Doug to his outrageous bachelor party. He is introduced to Nadia, who tries to seduce him, but Doug instead befriends her. A prank involving peanut butter, a blindfolded Doug's genitals and a <a href="/wiki/Basset_Hound" title="Basset Hound">basset hound</a> goes awry, requiring the groomsmen and Nadia to rush Doug to a hospital. When Doug wakes up the next day, Nadia kisses him goodbye, and hints she would like to know him better. Later, the groomsmen play football with Ed and his college football friends, including <a href="/wiki/Joe_Namath" title="Joe Namath">Joe Namath</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Riggins" title="John Riggins">John Riggins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ed_%22Too_Tall%22_Jones" title='Ed "Too Tall" Jones'>Ed "Too Tall" Jones</a>. A mud bowl ensues and Ed blows out his knee on the last play.</p>
<p>At the rehearsal dinner, Gretchen's bridesmaids sing a song, while Doug's groomsmen create a slideshow of the fake pictures they previously took, winning Gretchen over. That night, Gretchen, speaking to Doug, notices Bic razors and Mitchum deodorant in their cabinets. She recognizes the familiarity in the last names of the groomsmen and deduces the scheme. She asks Doug about it but he brushes it off, saying Gretchen is paranoid, to which she reluctantly agrees.</p>
<p>On the day of the wedding, the family priest cancels. Doug hatches an idea where Jimmy, already introduced as a military priest, officiates the nuptials. At the wedding reception, Jimmy congratulates Gretchen, who exclaims that the wedding is a disaster because her grandmother has third degree burns, her dad's knee is blown out, the food is bad, and she isn't marrying the man she loves. She confesses that she only married Doug because he is a nice man and can easily provide the lavish lifestyle she wants. Doug overhears and tells Jimmy that he can't go through with the wedding, but Jimmy dismisses this.</p>
<p>As Jimmy gives his best man speech, Doug stops it and reveals he and Gretchen aren't married since "Bic" is not a licensed officiant. He also tells everyone that his groomsmen are fake. Doug pays Jimmy his $50,000 fee and they accept each other's friendship. Gretchen is upset that her wedding is ruined. Ed's college friends make peace with Doug and Jimmy and tell them they were good players. Jimmy gets a date with Gretchen's sister Allison.</p>
<p>As they leave, Jimmy has an idea. They cash in Doug's first class honeymoon tickets to <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a>, going instead on a guy trip where the groomsmen, Edmundo, Doris, and Nadia, who begins her romance with Doug, party in the plane, while Lurch says that he "<a href="/wiki/Hugo_%22Hurley%22_Reyes" title='Hugo "Hurley" Reyes'>has a bad feeling about this flight</a>" as a reference to his character in the "LOST" TV show.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film evolved from a spec script titled "The Golden Tux" that was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Dimension_Films" title="Dimension Films">Dimension Films</a> as early as 2002, at that time Todd Phillips, director of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hangover" title="The Hangover">The Hangover</a></i> was attached as a producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The film went through many revisions as many actors came and left the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-empireonline.com_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-empireonline.com-10">[10]</a></sup> By 2013, Screen Gems and Miramax had agreed to produce the film, which by then had both Kevin Hart and Josh Gad attached <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and the name had officially changed to <i>The Wedding Ringer</i> and production had begun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was officially given a date of February 6, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> However this was later moved to January 16 after <a href="/wiki/Ride_Along_(film)" title="Ride Along (film)">Ride Along,</a> another Kevin Hart comedy, proved viable for Sony during the previous year's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_King,_Jr._Day" title="Martin Luther King, Jr. Day">MLK</a> weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was originally set in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> but was rewritten for <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> after the producers won a $2.8-million California tax credit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_Times1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Times1-15">[15]</a></sup> Filming took place in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Monica" title="Santa Monica">Santa Monica</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marina_del_Rey" title="Marina del Rey">Marina del Rey</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> over the course of 38 days in the fall 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_Times1_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Times1-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sony Pictures Entertainment released the first official trailer for <i>The Wedding Ringer</i> on June 19, 2014, a "Best Friends" official trailer on September 25, 2014 and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_band" title="Red band">Restricted</a> "Holiday" trailer on November 20, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 8, 2014, Kevin Hart announced through his <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account that he was "doing a college tour" to help promote <i>The Wedding Ringer</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Hart said that during this promotional tour, "he is focusing on college students because they are big social-media users" and can help promote the new film. He also encouraged the students to "tweet" about the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Wedding Ringer</i> was released on DVD and Blu-ray on April 28, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Wedding Ringer</i> grossed $64.5 million in North America and $15.3 million in other territories for a worldwide total of $79.8 million against a budget of $23 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was released in North American theaters on January 16, 2015. It grossed $20.6 million in its opening weekend, finishing second at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-23">[23]</a></sup> For the 4-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MLK_Day" title="MLK Day">MLK Holiday</a> weekend, the film grossed a total of $24.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wedding_Ringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Wedding Ringer</i> received generally negative reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> the film holds a rating of 28%, based on 97 reviews, with an average rating of 4.4/10. The critical consensus reads, "Kevin Hart and Josh Gad might be two great comedians that go great together, but there's little evidence of it on display in <i>The Wedding Ringer</i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 35 out of 100, based on 27 critics, indicating "generally unfavorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> audiences gave the film a grade of "A–" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>IGN gave the film a 6.2/10, stating that "the movie is actually pretty funny."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> ABCNews gave the movie 3.5/5 stars, stating "Hart and Gad make you laugh simply by showing up. Add a moderately funny script, and <i>The Wedding Ringer</i> is a winner."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> gave the film 1.5 out of 4 stars, acknowledging the chemistry between Hart and Gad but criticizing the "cockamamie premise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Film review blog <i>Movie Metropolis</i> was more critical, saying that it felt like Hart was on autopilot for much of the film's running time and the deeper storyline got lost in a mess of slapstick humour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Movie_Metropolis_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Movie_Metropolis-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
