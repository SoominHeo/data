<p><b>The Chinese Room</b> (formerly <b>thechineseroom</b>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newustcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-newustcr-1">[1]</a></sup> is a British <a href="/wiki/Independent_video_game_development" title="Independent video game development">independent video game development</a> studio best known for working on experimental <a href="/wiki/First-person_(video_games)" title="First-person (video games)">first-person</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">games</a>, such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_2" title="Half-Life 2">Half-Life 2</a></i> mod <i><a href="/wiki/Dear_Esther" title="Dear Esther">Dear Esther</a></i>, and co-developing its full-fledged <a href="/wiki/Dear_Esther#Remake" title="Dear Esther">remake</a>. The company originated as a <a href="/wiki/Mod_(video_gaming)" title="Mod (video gaming)">mod</a> team for <i>Half-Life 2</i>, based at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Portsmouth" title="University of Portsmouth">University of Portsmouth</a> in 2007, and is named after <a href="/wiki/John_Searle" title="John Searle">John Searle</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Chinese_room" title="Chinese room">Chinese room thought experiment</a>, however, the <a href="/wiki/Origami" title="Origami">origami</a> logo is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">japanese</a> symbol.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_history">Development history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chinese_Room&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mods">Mods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chinese_Room&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Chinese Room's first three projects were two mods for <i>Half-Life 2</i>, named <i>Antlion Soccer</i> and <i>Dear Esther</i>, and a <i>Doom 3</i> mod titled <i>Conscientious Objector</i>. The modding project was backed by the <a href="/wiki/Arts_and_Humanities_Research_Council" title="Arts and Humanities Research Council">Arts and Humanities Research Council</a>. Of these, <i>Dear Esther</i> became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cult_hit" title="Cult hit">cult hit</a>. In 2009, The Chinese Room developed <i><a href="/wiki/Korsakovia" title="Korsakovia">Korsakovia</a></i>, which was a <a href="/wiki/Survival_horror" title="Survival horror">survival horror</a> mod.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Games">Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chinese_Room&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <i>Korsakovia</i>, The Chinese Room worked with Robert Briscoe to develop a remake of <i>Dear Esther</i>, this time as a full-fledged video game title, distributed through <a href="/wiki/Valve_Corporation" title="Valve Corporation">Valve Corporation</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Steam_(software)" title="Steam (software)">Steam</a> distribution service. This stand-alone version of the mod received several IGF nominations, such as the Seamus McNally Grand Prize, Excellence in Visual Arts and Audio, and the Nuovo Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It finally won for Excellence in Visual Art.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The remake featured improved graphics, but was based on the same engine as the previous mods, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Source_engine" title="Source engine">Source</a>. The game was released in early 2012 and recouped its $55,000 investment in less than six hours of release, when 16,000 copies of the game were sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> One week after launch, the game reached 50,000 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2012, The Chinese Room announced that they began development on <i><a href="/wiki/Amnesia:_A_Machine_for_Pigs" title="Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs">Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs</a></i>, a survival horror game and indirect sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Amnesia:_The_Dark_Descent" title="Amnesia: The Dark Descent">Amnesia: The Dark Descent</a></i>. This project was produced by the makers of the original game, <a href="/wiki/Frictional_Games" title="Frictional Games">Frictional Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was later released on 10 September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Chinese Room also began work on their newest title, <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody%27s_Gone_to_the_Rapture" title="Everybody's Gone to the Rapture">Everybody's Gone to the Rapture</a></i> alongside the development of <i>Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Initially developed on PC, the team was afraid it would not receive enough funding from fans. They instead partnered with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Computer_Entertainment" title="Sony Computer Entertainment">Sony Computer Entertainment</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SCE_Santa_Monica_Studio" title="SCE Santa Monica Studio">Santa Monica Studio</a> to produce the game. It was re-revealed at <a href="/wiki/Gamescom" title="Gamescom">Gamescom</a> 2013 during Sony's conference as a <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a> exclusive. The title was finally released on 11 August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
