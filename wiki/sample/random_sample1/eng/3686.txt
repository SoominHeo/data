<p><b>The Canadas</b> is the collective name for <b><a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper Canada</a></b> and <b><a href="/wiki/Lower_Canada" title="Lower Canada">Lower Canada</a></b>, two <a href="/wiki/British_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="British colonization of the Americas">British historical colonies</a> in present-day <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turner2011_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Turner2011-1">[1]</a></sup> They were both created by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constitutional_Act_of_1791" title="Constitutional Act of 1791">Constitutional Act of 1791</a> and abolished in 1841 with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Upper_and_Lower_Canada" title="Union of Upper and Lower Canada">union of Upper and Lower Canada</a>.</p>
<p>Their names reflected their positions relative to the headwaters of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a>, the same relationship as between <a href="/wiki/Upper_Egypt" title="Upper Egypt">Upper Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lower_Egypt" title="Lower Egypt">Lower Egypt</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Lower Canada covered the south-eastern portion of the modern-day Province of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, Canada, and (until 1809) the <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a> region of the modern-day Province of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland and Labrador</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turner2011_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Turner2011-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>Upper Canada covered what is now the southern portion of the Province of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, and the lands bordering <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Bay" title="Georgian Bay">Georgian Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Superior" title="Lake Superior">Lake Superior</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turner2011_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Turner2011-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>The northern part of the current provinces of Ontario and Quebec was at that time part of <a href="/wiki/Rupert%27s_Land" title="Rupert's Land">Rupert's Land</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Canadas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The British colony of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Quebec_(1763%E2%80%931791)" title="Province of Quebec (1763–1791)">Province of Quebec</a> was divided by the Constitutional Act of 1791 into two separate provinces, with the <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_River" title="Ottawa River">Ottawa River</a> forming a part of the border.</p>
<p>The creation of Upper Canada was in response to the desire expressed by recently arrived <a href="/wiki/United_Empire_Loyalist" title="United Empire Loyalist">United Empire Loyalist</a> settlers for British institutions and laws, especially British laws of land tenure.</p>
<p>The political structure of the colonies was changed after <a href="/wiki/John_Lambton,_1st_Earl_of_Durham" title="John Lambton, 1st Earl of Durham">Lord Durham's</a> 1838 <i><a href="/wiki/Report_on_the_Affairs_of_British_North_America" title="Report on the Affairs of British North America">Report on the Affairs of British North America</a></i> recommended they be merged into a single colonial province.</p>
<p>Lower Canada, Upper Canada and their legislatures were abolished by the British <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Union_1840" title="Act of Union 1840">Act of Union 1840</a> of July 23, 1840 (which came into force in early 1841) and united as one political entity, the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Canada" title="Province of Canada">Province of Canada</a>.</p>
