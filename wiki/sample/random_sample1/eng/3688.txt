<p><b>The Tea Party</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock</a> band with <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a>, <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music_of_India" title="Music of India">Indian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middle_Eastern_music" title="Middle Eastern music">Middle Eastern</a> influences, dubbed "<a href="/wiki/Music_of_Morocco" title="Music of Morocco">Moroccan</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">roll</a>" by the media. Active throughout the 1990s and up until 2005, the band re-formed in 2011. The Tea Party released eight albums on <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> Music Canada, selling 1.6 million records worldwide, and achieving a No. 1 Canadian single "<a href="/wiki/Heaven_Coming_Down" title="Heaven Coming Down">Heaven Coming Down</a>" in 1999.</p>
<p>The Tea Party toured Canada on twenty-one occasions and Australia on twelve. In November 2002, the band toured Canada with symphony orchestras reinterpreting a decade's worth of shared songwriting. Breaking up in 2005 due to creative differences, band members nevertheless re-united in 2011 to play several Canadian tour dates during the summer. The band decided to continue after the tour and has now re-formed. The band has since released a double live album, recorded on their 2012 tour of Australia. Video shot during this tour was released as a Live DVD/Blu-ray, "The Tea Party: Live From Australia" in 2013. The Tea Party has also been writing and recording new original material since Fall 2012, and their latest album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ocean_at_the_End" title="The Ocean at the End">The Ocean at the End</a></i>, was released in September 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.281990.E2.80.931995.29">Early years (1990–1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years (1990–1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Tea Party was formed in 1990 by <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Martin_(Canadian_musician)" title="Jeff Martin (Canadian musician)">Jeff Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Chatwood" title="Stuart Chatwood">Stuart Chatwood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Burrows" title="Jeff Burrows">Jeff Burrows</a> after a marathon jam session at the <a href="/wiki/Cherry_Beach_Sound" title="Cherry Beach Sound">Cherry Beach Rehearsal Studios</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>. Each member had previously played together during their teenage years in a number of different bands in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor, Ontario</a>, where they were originally from. They had decided to name their new group The Tea Party after the infamous hash sessions of famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beat_generation" title="Beat generation">Beat generation</a> poets <a href="/wiki/Allen_Ginsberg" title="Allen Ginsberg">Allen Ginsberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kerouac" title="Jack Kerouac">Jack Kerouac</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Burroughs" title="William Burroughs">William Burroughs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Tea Party released their <a href="/wiki/The_Tea_Party_(album)" title="The Tea Party (album)">eponymous debut album</a> in 1991, distributing it through their own label Eternal Discs. The album drew influences from psychedelic rock and blues, and was produced by Martin; album production was something Martin would continue with for all of The Tea Party's albums, as a way of giving the band complete artistic control. In 1993 The Tea Party signed to EMI Music Canada and released their first major label recording, <i><a href="/wiki/Splendor_Solis_(album)" title="Splendor Solis (album)">Splendor Solis</a></i>. The band employed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_tuning" title="Open tuning">open tunings</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goblet_drums" title="Goblet drums">goblet drums</a> (Dumbek) to create Indian-style sounds, something they continued to employ throughout their career, while continuing in a blues influenced style. In 1994 the album released in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, with the single "<a href="/wiki/Save_Me_(The_Tea_Party_song)" title="Save Me (The Tea Party song)">Save Me</a>" launching the band's career in the country. The band gained the support of national radio station <a href="/wiki/Triple_J" title="Triple J">Triple J</a>, enabling the band's first tour, with "Save Me" becoming a staple of their setlists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuart_Chatwood.2C_In_Tangents_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2000.2C_CD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga._2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stuart_Chatwood.2C_In_Tangents_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2000.2C_CD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga.-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success_.281995.E2.80.932000.29">Success (1995–2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Success (1995–2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Further developing The Tea Party's sound in 1995, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Edges_of_Twilight" title="The Edges of Twilight">The Edges of Twilight</a></i> was recorded with an array of Indian and Middle-eastern instrumentation. "<a href="/wiki/Sister_Awake" title="Sister Awake">Sister Awake</a>", the third single from the album, defines what the band set out to do, combining three-piece rock compositions with music from the world. "Sister Awake" is an acoustically based arrangement on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/12-string_guitar" title="12-string guitar">12-string guitar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sitar" title="Sitar">sitar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarod" title="Sarod">sarod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pump_organ" title="Pump organ">harmonium</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goblet_drums" title="Goblet drums">goblet drums</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuart_Chatwood.2C_In_Tangents_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2000.2C_CD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga._2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stuart_Chatwood.2C_In_Tangents_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2000.2C_CD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga.-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>The Edges of Twilight</i> is The Tea Party's most commercially successful album; with sales exceeding 270,000 units, the album is certified double platinum in Canada and platinum in Australia.</p>
<p>Upon returning from successful tours in Canada, Europe and Australia in 1996, The Tea Party recorded <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alhambra_(album)" title="Alhambra (album)">Alhambra</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/Enhanced_CD" title="Enhanced CD">enhanced CD</a> which features acoustic re-recordings of songs from <i>The Edges of Twilight</i>, and followed its release with a brief tour of Canada called "Alhambra Acoustic and Eclectic". English folk musician <a href="/wiki/Roy_Harper" title="Roy Harper">Roy Harper</a> appeared on <i>The Edges of Twilight</i> reciting a poem and on <i>Alhambra</i> providing vocals for the song "Time".</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Transmission_(The_Tea_Party_album)" title="Transmission (The Tea Party album)">Transmission</a></i> released in 1997 saw The Tea Party's first foray into <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a> with a sampled world music foundation and thrust. <i>Transmission</i> is a collection of aggressive songs influenced by upheavals around the band; the firing of their management and the feeling of a lack of support from their record company. Epitomising the feelings were the first single "<a href="/wiki/Temptation_(The_Tea_Party_song)" title="Temptation (The Tea Party song)">Temptation</a>" and the album's title song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuart_Chatwood.2C_In_Tangents_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2000.2C_CD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga._2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stuart_Chatwood.2C_In_Tangents_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2000.2C_CD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga.-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Triptych_(The_Tea_Party_album)" title="Triptych (The Tea Party album)">Triptych</a></i> followed in 1999, the first single "<a href="/wiki/Heaven_Coming_Down" title="Heaven Coming Down">Heaven Coming Down</a>" rose to No. 1 on Canadian radio. The Tea Party's music took on a more orchestral sound, maturing from the blues base. <i><a href="/wiki/Live_at_the_Enmore_Theatre" title="Live at the Enmore Theatre">Live at the Enmore Theatre</a></i>, the band's first live album was released through Australian radio station <a href="/wiki/Triple_J" title="Triple J">Triple J</a> during the band's tour for <i>Triptych</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years_.282000.E2.80.932005.29">Later years (2000–2005)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later years (2000–2005)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band released a singles compilation called <i><a href="/wiki/Tangents:_The_Tea_Party_Collection" title="Tangents: The Tea Party Collection">Tangents: The Tea Party Collection</a></i> in 2000, and then a DVD compilation of music videos (which Martin remixed in <a href="/wiki/Surround_sound" title="Surround sound">surround sound</a>) called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Illuminations_(DVD)" title="Illuminations (DVD)">Illuminations</a></i> in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Band_biography:_Illuminations_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2001.2C_DVD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga._3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Band_biography:_Illuminations_The_Tea_Party_Collection_2001.2C_DVD.2C_EMI_Music_Canada.2C_Mississauga.-3">[3]</a></sup> was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Metalworks_Studios" title="Metalworks Studios">Metalworks Studios</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mississauga,_Ontario" title="Mississauga, Ontario">Mississauga, Ontario</a>. The Tea Party released <i><a href="/wiki/The_Interzone_Mantras" title="The Interzone Mantras">The Interzone Mantras</a></i> later in 2001, and in November 2002 joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symphony_orchestras" title="Symphony orchestras">symphony orchestras</a> across Canada in adapting their live show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Tea_Party_2004.2C_Band_biography._4-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Tea_Party_2004.2C_Band_biography.-4">[4]</a></sup> The Interzone Mantras was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Metalworks_Studios" title="Metalworks Studios">Metalworks Studios</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mississauga,_Ontario" title="Mississauga, Ontario">Mississauga, Ontario</a>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Seven_Circles" title="Seven Circles">Seven Circles</a></i>, the band's final album, was released in 2004. Both <i>The Interzone Mantras</i> and <i>Seven Circles</i> saw the band return to their earlier sound with maturity.</p>
<p>In October 2005 The Tea Party disbanded due to creative differences, with Martin abruptly announcing he was beginning a solo career. Afterward Chatwood stated on the band's forum "that Jeff Burrows and myself are sincerely sorry for the way this was handled. As far as Jeff Burrows and myself were concerned, the band was taking an extended break."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chatwood.2C_S._2005.2C_Stuart_Chatwood_statement._5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chatwood.2C_S._2005.2C_Stuart_Chatwood_statement.-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-breakup_.282006.E2.80.932011.29">Post-breakup (2006–2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-breakup (2006–2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006 Chatwood continued to compose the <i><a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Persia" title="Prince of Persia">Prince of Persia</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> soundtracks for <a href="/wiki/Ubisoft_Montreal" title="Ubisoft Montreal">Ubisoft Montreal</a>.</p>
<p>Burrows joined <a href="/wiki/Rush_(band)" title="Rush (band)">Rush</a>'s Geddy Lee and Alex Lifeson, and other Canadian musicians as drummer in the one-off project the <a href="/wiki/Big_Dirty_Band" title="Big Dirty Band">Big Dirty Band</a> as well as presenting the midday shift on <a href="/wiki/CKUE-FM" title="CKUE-FM">The Rock</a>, a radio station in Windsor. In 2008 Burrows announced that he, <a href="/wiki/Edwin_(musician)" title="Edwin (musician)">Edwin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Turner_(musician)" title="Mike Turner (musician)">Mike Turner</a> and Amir Epstein would form the band <a href="/wiki/Crash_Karma" title="Crash Karma">Crash Karma</a>, recording their debut album in early 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morano.2C_J_2008.2C_Edwin_Forms_Band_With_Ex-Members_Of_The_Tea_Party_And_Our_Lady_Peace.2C_Chartattack._6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morano.2C_J_2008.2C_Edwin_Forms_Band_With_Ex-Members_Of_The_Tea_Party_And_Our_Lady_Peace.2C_Chartattack.-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harper.2C_K_2009.2C_Edwin.2FTea_Party.2FOLP_Band_Is_For_Real.2C_Chartattack._7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harper.2C_K_2009.2C_Edwin.2FTea_Party.2FOLP_Band_Is_For_Real.2C_Chartattack.-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Martin moved to Ireland and recorded his debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Exile_and_the_Kingdom_(album)" title="Exile and the Kingdom (album)">Exile and the Kingdom</a></i>, which was released in Canada and Australia in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bliss.2C_K_2006.2C_Jeff_Martin_in-studio_session.2C_Jam.21_Music._8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bliss.2C_K_2006.2C_Jeff_Martin_in-studio_session.2C_Jam.21_Music.-8">[8]</a></sup> He has toured parts of Europe, Canada and Australia, and released two live albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Live_in_Brisbane_2006" title="Live in Brisbane 2006">Live in Brisbane 2006</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Live_in_Dublin_(Jeff_Martin_album)" title="Live in Dublin (Jeff Martin album)">Live in Dublin</a></i>, in November 2006 and May 2007, respectively. In August 2008, Martin announced the formation of his new band, <a href="/wiki/The_Armada_(band)" title="The Armada (band)">The Armada</a>. In 2010, The Armada broke up. Jeff Martin founded a new band called Jeff Martin 777 with Jay Cortez on bass and Malcolm Clark on drums. Their debut album <i>The Ground Cries Out</i> was released on March 1 in Canada and made it to No. 51 in the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart" title="Canadian Albums Chart">Canadian Albums Chart</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reunion">Reunion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reunion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 April 2011, an official <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page was launched with a biography section that closed with "The band is scheduled to play a select number of dates in 2011. No other info was available at this time." The following day, the local radio station K106.3 announced the Sarnia Bayfest lineup including The Tea Party, which was later confirmed by drummer Jeff Burrows on his radio show on 13 April. Burrows further stated that a Canadian summer tour was in the works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INXS_to_close_the_show_for_Big_Music_Fest...._9-0"><a href="#cite_note-INXS_to_close_the_show_for_Big_Music_Fest....-9">[9]</a></sup> The band played several shows in Canada over that summer.</p>
<p>On 6 August 2011, during a show in Lévis, the band had implied that they were going to be back together permanently. Martin said "We are the Tea Party and we're here to stay. And we're never going away again." In December of that year, during a show at the <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9tropolis_(concert_hall)" title="Métropolis (concert hall)">Métropolis</a> in Montreal, the band announced that they would hopefully record a new album in 2012 and that there would be no more break up; Martin confirmed earlier implications by stating that "The Tea Party is back for good".</p>
<p>On 22 February 2013 The Tea Party announced on its website that "The band have convened in Australia to write and record the next chapter in the Tea Party saga. Stay tuned for more updates." On 19 March 2014 The Tea Party announced via its Facebook page that the title of their forthcoming album will be <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ocean_at_the_End" title="The Ocean at the End">The Ocean at the End</a></i>. It was officially released in Canada on 8 September 2014.</p>
<p>The band has recently announced they will be going on a 30 date anniversary tour for their 4th studio album Transmission that was released 20 years ago. The tour begins February 3rd and they will be performing the album in its entirety along with some of their greatest hits. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_name">Domain name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Domain name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2011, <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> reported that political groups associated with the <a href="/wiki/Tea_Party_movement" title="Tea Party movement">Tea Party movement</a> were trying to purchase the band's domain name. Stuart Chatwood stated <i>"So much damage has been done to our name by the political movement that we're considering selling (The domain)"</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It is estimated the band could sell the domain for over $1M U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The band purchased the domain in 1993, and has since added the phrase "No politics... Just Rock and Roll" to their site in order to distance themselves from the political movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On October 15, 2011, Sedo announced that they will be exclusively listing the domain for sale on their online marketplace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In February 2012, however, a post appeared on the website's news section that said: "The band has not sold teaparty.com."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_awards_and_nominations">Video awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Tea_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Video awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Tea Party were nominated for 22 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MuchMusic_Video_Awards" title="MuchMusic Video Awards">MuchMusic Video Awards</a>, and were awarded three People's Choice Awards for Favourite Music Video: two for "The River" and one for "The Bazaar".
