<p><b>Douglas Eric</b> "<b>Doug</b>" <b>Liman</b> (born July 24, 1965) is an American <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a> and <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">producer</a> best known for <i><a href="/wiki/Swingers_(1996_film)" title="Swingers (1996 film)">Swingers</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Identity_(2002_film)" title="The Bourne Identity (2002 film)">The Bourne Identity</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._%26_Mrs._Smith_(2005_film)" title="Mr. &amp; Mrs. Smith (2005 film)">Mr. &amp; Mrs. Smith</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Jumper_(2008_film)" title="Jumper (2008 film)">Jumper</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Fair_Game_(2010_film)" title="Fair Game (2010 film)">Fair Game</a></i> (2010), and <i><a href="/wiki/Edge_of_Tomorrow" title="Edge of Tomorrow">Edge of Tomorrow</a></i> (2014).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doug_Liman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Liman was born in New York City, the son of Ellen (née Fogelson), a painter and writer, and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_L._Liman" title="Arthur L. Liman">Arthur L. Liman</a>, a lawyer well known for his public service, which included serving as chief counsel for the Senate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iran-Contra_Affair" title="Iran-Contra Affair">Iran-Contra</a> hearings. He has two siblings, Emily and Lewis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Liman was raised Jewish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liman began making <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short films</a> while still in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Junior_high_school" title="Junior high school">junior high school</a> and studied at <a href="/wiki/International_Center_of_Photography" title="International Center of Photography">International Center of Photography</a> in New York City. While attending <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a>, he helped to co-found the student-run <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">cable television</a> station BTV and served as its first station manager. With the help of a major grant through his father's connections from the now-defunct CBS Foundation, he also co-founded the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_College_Broadcasters" title="National Association of College Broadcasters">National Association of College Broadcasters</a> (NACB), the first trade association geared to student-staffed radio and television stations, in 1988. Liman attended the graduate program at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>, where he was tapped to helm his first project in 1993, the comedy <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(genre)" title="Thriller (genre)">thriller</a> <i>Getting In/Student Body</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doug_Liman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Liman became attached to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Swingers_(1996_film)" title="Swingers (1996 film)">Swingers</a></i> when its screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Jon_Favreau" title="Jon Favreau">Jon Favreau</a> turned down offers from studios who wanted to cast established actors. The director agreed to cast Favreau and his friends (<a href="/wiki/Vince_Vaughn" title="Vince Vaughn">Vince Vaughn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Livingston" title="Ron Livingston">Ron Livingston</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Van_Horn" title="Patrick Van Horn">Patrick Van Horn</a>) in this comedy about struggling actors amid the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">L.A.</a> club milieu. The result was a $250,000 dialogue-propelled film that became a <a href="/wiki/Sleeper_hit" title="Sleeper hit">sleeper hit</a> and critical success. In addition to establishing a cult following, it jump-started the careers of the featured actors. Liman sold the film to <a href="/wiki/Miramax" title="Miramax">Miramax</a> for $5.5 million; shortly after the sale, Favreau refused to speak to Liman. They rekindled their friendship years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liman's next effort, <i><a href="/wiki/Go_(1999_film)" title="Go (1999 film)">Go</a></i> (1999), tracked the events of one night through three different points of view as plot lines diverged and reconverged; Liman was also the film's <a href="/wiki/Cinematographer" title="Cinematographer">cinematographer</a>. The film made a profit at the box office grossing $28.4 million worldwide against a $6.5 million budget.</p>
<p>In 1999, Liman shot a commercial for Nike in which <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods" title="Tiger Woods">Tiger Woods</a>, without letting the ball touch the ground, repeatedly bounced a ball on his club and then drove it into the distance.</p>

<p>Liman enjoyed further commercial success when he directed the action thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Identity_(2002_film)" title="The Bourne Identity (2002 film)">The Bourne Identity</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/2002_in_film" title="2002 in film">2002</a>), an adaptation of author <a href="/wiki/Robert_Ludlum" title="Robert Ludlum">Robert Ludlum</a>'s novel. The film that Liman delivered lacked sufficient action sequences to satisfy test groups of young males, so <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a> required him to shoot almost twenty minutes of replacement scenes. Liman remained with the Bourne franchise through three of its next four installments (2004's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Supremacy_(film)" title="The Bourne Supremacy (film)">The Bourne Supremacy</a></i>, 2007's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Ultimatum_(film)" title="The Bourne Ultimatum (film)">The Bourne Ultimatum</a></i> and 2016's <i><a href="/wiki/Jason_Bourne_(film)" title="Jason Bourne (film)">Jason Bourne</a></i>), but served instead as an <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive producer</a> while <a href="/wiki/Paul_Greengrass" title="Paul Greengrass">Paul Greengrass</a> took over directing duties. Building on his success, Liman executive produced and directed the pilot episode (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Premiere_(The_O.C._episode)" title="Premiere (The O.C. episode)">Premiere</a></i>) as well as the second episode (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Model_Home" title="The Model Home">The Model Home</a></i>) of the successful Fox prime time drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_O.C." title="The O.C.">The O.C.</a></i> (2003–2007). Liman produced and directed a series of comedy shorts for the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a> Film Project and <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes Film Festival</a> entitled <i>Indie Is Great</i>.</p>
<p>Liman also directed <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._%26_Mrs._Smith_(2005_film)" title="Mr. &amp; Mrs. Smith (2005 film)">Mr. &amp; Mrs. Smith</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/2005_in_film" title="2005 in film">2005</a>), a comedic thriller about an increasingly distant married couple, both secretly assassins, who are hired to kill each other. The film, his most commercially successful to date, is also well known for the off-screen romance that developed between stars <a href="/wiki/Brad_Pitt" title="Brad Pitt">Brad Pitt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Jolie" title="Angelina Jolie">Angelina Jolie</a> after making the film. In 2005, Liman signed on to direct the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilot_episode" title="Pilot episode">pilot episode</a> of <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s television series <i><a href="/wiki/Heist_(TV_series)" title="Heist (TV series)">Heist</a></i>, which is about a season-long attempt to rob three jewelry stores on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills" title="Beverly Hills">Beverly Hills</a>' swanky <a href="/wiki/Rodeo_Drive" title="Rodeo Drive">Rodeo Drive</a>.</p>
<p>His <a href="/wiki/Jumper_(2008_film)" title="Jumper (2008 film)">film adaptation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Steven_Gould" title="Steven Gould">Steven Gould</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/Jumper_(novel)" title="Jumper (novel)">Jumper</a></i> was released in <a href="/wiki/2008_in_film" title="2008 in film">2008</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, he co-founded the website <i>30ninjas.com</i> which is geared towards fans of action movies and television, gaming, extreme sports and viral videos. He also maintains a blog on the site.</p>
<p>He directed 2010's <i><a href="/wiki/Fair_Game_(2010_film)" title="Fair Game (2010 film)">Fair Game</a></i>, about the <a href="/wiki/Plame_affair" title="Plame affair">Plame affair</a>, which competed for the <a href="/wiki/Palme_d%27Or" title="Palme d'Or">Palme d'Or</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2010 Cannes Film Festival">2010 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywoodreporter_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodreporter-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Liman directed and produced <i><a href="/wiki/I_Just_Want_My_Pants_Back" title="I Just Want My Pants Back">I Just Want My Pants Back</a>,</i> a television series that aired on MTV. He produces <i><a href="/wiki/Covert_Affairs" title="Covert Affairs">Covert Affairs</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Suits_(TV_series)" title="Suits (TV series)">Suits</a></i>, two original series on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a>. He directed the film adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Sakurazaka" title="Hiroshi Sakurazaka">Hiroshi Sakurazaka</a> novel, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_You_Need_is_Kill" title="All You Need is Kill">All You Need is Kill</a></i>, released as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edge_of_Tomorrow_(film)" title="Edge of Tomorrow (film)">Edge of Tomorrow</a></i> (2014). His next film is <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mena_(film)" title="Mena (film)">Mena</a></i> (2017).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In August 2016, Liman signed on to direct <i>Dark Universe</i>, a film set within the <a href="/wiki/DC_Extended_Universe" title="DC Extended Universe">DC Extended Universe</a> and based on superhero team <a href="/wiki/Justice_League_Dark" title="Justice League Dark">Justice League Dark</a> after leaving the adaptation of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gambit_(2016_film)" title="Gambit (2016 film)">Gambit</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most of his career has been associated with the production company, Hypnotic. He is co-owner with <a href="/wiki/Dave_Bartis" title="Dave Bartis">Dave Bartis</a>, whom he met as an undergraduate at Brown University where they co-founded BTV and NACB.</p>
<p>Liman serves on the boards of the Legal Action Center, <a href="/wiki/Symphony_Space" title="Symphony Space">Symphony Space</a> in New York City, and is actively involved in the Arthur Liman undergraduate fellowship program.</p>
