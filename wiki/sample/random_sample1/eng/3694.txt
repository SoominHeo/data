<p><b>Stephen Dougray Scott</b> (born 26 November 1965), better known as <b>Dougray Scott</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/uː/ long 'oo' in 'food'">uː</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span>), is a Scottish actor.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dougray_Scott&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scott was born in <a href="/wiki/Glenrothes" title="Glenrothes">Glenrothes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fife" title="Fife">Fife</a>, the son of Elma, a nurse, and Alan Scott, an <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salesperson" title="Salesperson">salesperson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He attended Auchmuty High School. Adopting his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> from his grandmother's surname, "Dougray", he enrolled in a foundation course in drama before going on to attend the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Welsh_College_of_Music_%26_Drama" title="Royal Welsh College of Music &amp; Drama">Royal Welsh College of Music &amp; Drama</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> from 1984 to 1987; here, he earned the college's Most Promising Drama Student award. In 1988, he moved to London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dougray_Scott&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scott began his acting career in national theatre, television, and puppet shows. He appeared in his first role on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Soldier_Soldier" title="Soldier Soldier">Soldier Soldier</a></i> and made his film debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_Town" title="Twin Town">Twin Town</a></i>. His first major film roles were as Prince Henry in <i><a href="/wiki/Ever_After" title="Ever After">Ever After</a></i> and the hero in <a href="/wiki/Enigma_(2001_film)" title="Enigma (2001 film)">the film adaptation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Harris_(novelist)" title="Robert Harris (novelist)">Robert Harris</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/Enigma_(novel)" title="Enigma (novel)">Enigma</a></i>. In 2000, he signed up to play the villain in <i><a href="/wiki/Mission:_Impossible_II" title="Mission: Impossible II">Mission: Impossible II</a></i> (hand-picked by star <a href="/wiki/Tom_Cruise" title="Tom Cruise">Tom Cruise</a>) but was also due to play <a href="/wiki/Wolverine_(character)" title="Wolverine (character)">Wolverine</a> in the feature film version of <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men_(film)" title="X-Men (film)">X-Men</a></i>. When <i>Mission: Impossible II</i> went over schedule, Scott was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Jackman" title="Hugh Jackman">Hugh Jackman</a> in <i>X-Men</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BrisT090409_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BrisT090409-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the 2002 release of <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Another_Day" title="Die Another Day">Die Another Day</a></i>, Scott was a candidate to replace <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Brosnan" title="Pierce Brosnan">Pierce Brosnan</a> as <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DR220506_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DR220506-4">[4]</a></sup> Despite the numerous reports that <a href="/wiki/Eon_Productions" title="Eon Productions">Eon Productions</a> was leaning towards naming Scott as the new Bond in the 007 adventure <i><a href="/wiki/Casino_Royale_(2006_film)" title="Casino Royale (2006 film)">Casino Royale</a></i>, the role eventually went to <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Craig" title="Daniel Craig">Daniel Craig</a>. In January 2006, he appeared in the miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ten_Commandments_(miniseries)" title="The Ten Commandments (miniseries)">The Ten Commandments</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Moses" title="Moses">Moses</a>. He also appeared in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Heist_(TV_series)" title="Heist (TV series)">Heist</a></i>, which aired in March 2006 for five episodes. From 2006 until 2007, he starred in the third season of <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Teri_Hatcher" title="Teri Hatcher">Teri Hatcher</a>'s character's new love interest. In 2007, he played the primary antagonist in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Hitman_(2007_film)" title="Hitman (2007 film)">Hitman</a></i>, based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitman_(video_game)" title="Hitman (video game)">popular video game</a>. He appeared in the title role(s) of a modern re-telling of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Jekyll_and_Mr._Hyde" title="Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde">Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde</a></i>. In May 2009, it was announced that he was to play ex-con Michael O' Connor in the new <a href="/wiki/Raidi%C3%B3_Teilif%C3%ADs_%C3%89ireann" title="Raidió Teilifís Éireann">RTÉ</a>-<a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Father_%26_Son_(TV_serial)" title="Father &amp; Son (TV serial)">Father &amp; Son</a></i>.</p>
<p>Scott starred in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Day_of_the_Triffids_(2009_TV_series)" title="The Day of the Triffids (2009 TV series)">The Day of the Triffids</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tel110209_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tel110209-5">[5]</a></sup> The drama was broadcast in December 2009 as part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_1" title="BBC 1">BBC 1</a> Christmas schedule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2011, Scott featured in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Love%27s_Kitchen" title="Love's Kitchen">Love's Kitchen</a></i> alongside his wife <a href="/wiki/Claire_Forlani" title="Claire Forlani">Claire Forlani</a>, who played his love interest in the film. On the opening weekend in the UK it took just £121 in June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In April 2011, Scott portrayed football manager <a href="/wiki/Matt_Busby" title="Matt Busby">Matt Busby</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/United_(2011_film)" title="United (2011 film)">United</a></i>, which was centred on the <a href="/wiki/Munich_air_disaster" title="Munich air disaster">Munich air disaster</a> of 1958, in which Busby was badly injured but survived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Busby's family were reportedly "incensed" by Scott's portrayal, with Busby's son commenting that his father's character "had the appearance of a gangster rather than a football manager" and wore an <a href="/wiki/Overcoat" title="Overcoat">overcoat</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trilby_hat" title="Trilby hat">trilby hat</a> instead of a <a href="/wiki/Tracksuit" title="Tracksuit">tracksuit</a>, despite Busby famously being the first "tracksuit" manager of the post-<a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2013, he guest starred as para-psychological researcher/ghost hunter (and War Hero) Alec Palmer in the <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Hide_(Doctor_Who)" title="Hide (Doctor Who)">Hide</a>" alongside <a href="/wiki/Matt_Smith_(actor)" title="Matt Smith (actor)">Matt Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Coleman" title="Jenna Coleman">Jenna Coleman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Raine" title="Jessica Raine">Jessica Raine</a>.</p>
<p>Scott signed up to play the main villain in <i><a href="/wiki/Taken_3" title="Taken 3">Taken 3</a></i>, hand-picked by star <a href="/wiki/Liam_Neeson" title="Liam Neeson">Liam Neeson</a>) in 2015.</p>
<p>In March 2016, Scott became the voice of Orby in adverts for British oil and gas company <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2016, Scott appeared in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Fear_the_Walking_Dead" title="Fear the Walking Dead">Fear the Walking Dead</a></i> playing the character Thomas Abigail, the business partner and lover of Strand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dougray_Scott&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scott is the father of twins, Eden and Gabriel Trevis Scott (b. 1998), with former wife <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Trevis" title="Sarah Trevis">Sarah Trevis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 8 June 2007, he married actress <a href="/wiki/Claire_Forlani" title="Claire Forlani">Claire Forlani</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scott is an avid fan of <a href="/wiki/Hibernian_F.C." title="Hibernian F.C.">Hibernian Football Club</a>, stemming from a great uncle being a scout for the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He is a celebrity supporter of the <a href="/wiki/British_Red_Cross" title="British Red Cross">British Red Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/WaterAid" title="WaterAid">WaterAid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scott and Forlani have a son, Milo Thomas Scott, born 27 December 2014.</p>
