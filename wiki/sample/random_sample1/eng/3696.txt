<p><b>Douglas County</b> (county code <b>DG</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">county</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the county population was 110,826,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QF_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-QF-1">[1]</a></sup> making it the fifth-most populous county in Kansas. Its <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> and most populous city is <a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Kansas" title="Lawrence, Kansas">Lawrence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Kansas" title="History of Kansas">History of Kansas</a></div>
<p>For many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennia" title="Millennia">millennia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> was inhabited by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nomadic" title="Nomadic">nomadic</a> <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a>. From the 16th century to 18th century, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">Kingdom of France</a> claimed ownership of large parts of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. In 1762, after the <a href="/wiki/French_and_Indian_War" title="French and Indian War">French and Indian War</a>, France secretly ceded <a href="/wiki/New_France" title="New France">New France</a> to <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, per the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fontainebleau_(1762)" title="Treaty of Fontainebleau (1762)">Treaty of Fontainebleau</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1802, Spain returned most of the land to France, but keeping title to about 7,500 square miles. In 1803, most of the land for <a href="/wiki/History_of_Kansas" title="History of Kansas">modern day Kansas</a> was acquired by the United States from France as part of the 828,000 square mile <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Purchase" title="Louisiana Purchase">Louisiana Purchase</a> for 2.83 <a href="/wiki/Penny_(United_States_coin)" title="Penny (United States coin)">cents</a> per <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acre</a>.</p>
<p>In 1854, the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_Territory" title="Kansas Territory">Kansas Territory</a> was organized, then in 1861 <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a> became the 34th <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a>. In 1855, <b>Douglas County</b> was established. Douglas County was opened for settlement on May 15, 1854, and was named for <a href="/wiki/Stephen_A._Douglas" title="Stephen A. Douglas">Stephen A. Douglas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> a senator from <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>. The county was practically at the center of the <a href="/wiki/Bleeding_Kansas" title="Bleeding Kansas">Bleeding Kansas</a> years as leaders in <a href="/wiki/Lecompton,_Kansas" title="Lecompton, Kansas">Lecompton</a>, the territorial capital, wanted <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a> to be a slave state and leaders in Lawrence wanted Kansas to be a free state. Because of this, multiple events took place, including the drafting of the <a href="/wiki/Lecompton_Constitution" title="Lecompton Constitution">Lecompton Constitution</a> admitting Kansas as a slave state, the <a href="/wiki/Sacking_of_Lawrence" title="Sacking of Lawrence">sacking of Lawrence</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Black_Jack" title="Battle of Black Jack">Battle of Black Jack</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The first railroad in Douglas County, the Union Pacific, was built through that territory in 1864.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the county has a total area of 475 square miles (1,230 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 456 square miles (1,180 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 19 square miles (49 km<sup>2</sup>) (4.0%) is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-5">[5]</a></sup> It is the fifth-smallest county in Kansas by land area. Much of its northern boundary is defined by the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_River" title="Kansas River">Kansas River</a>, which flows through Lawrence and provides <a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">hydropower</a> at the <a class="external text" href="http://abyss.kgs.ku.edu/pls/abyss/pubcat.phd1.View_Photo?f_id=298&amp;f_hd=Y" rel="nofollow">Bowersock Dam</a>.</p>

<p>Douglas County comprises the Lawrence, KS <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Metropolitan Statistical Area">Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>, which is also included in the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>-<a href="/wiki/Overland_Park,_Kansas" title="Overland Park, Kansas">Overland Park</a>-<a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Kansas" title="Kansas City, Kansas">Kansas City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">MO</a>-KS <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_metropolitan_area" title="Kansas City metropolitan area">Combined Statistical Area</a>.</p>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" title="2000 United States Census">2000 census</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-11">[11]</a></sup> there were 99,962 people, 38,486 households, and 21,167 families residing in the county. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 219 people per square mile (84/km²). There were 40,250 housing units at an average density of 88 per square mile (34/km²). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">racial makeup</a> of the county was 86.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_American" title="White American">White</a>, 4.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_American" title="Black American">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a>, 2.6% <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a>, 3.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_American" title="Asian American">Asian</a>, 0.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_American" title="Pacific Islander American">Pacific Islander</a>, 1.2% from other races, and 2.7% from <a href="/wiki/Multiracial" title="Multiracial">two or more races</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic" title="Hispanic">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino" title="Latino">Latino</a> of any race were 3.3% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 38,486 <a href="/wiki/Household" title="Household">households</a> out of which 27.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 43.1% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.5% had a female householder with no husband present, and 45.0% were non-families. 28.5% of all households were made up of individuals and 5.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.37 and the average family size was 2.97.</p>
<p>In the county the population was spread out with 20.4% under the age of 18, 26.4% from 18 to 24, 28.3% from 25 to 44, 16.9% from 45 to 64, and 7.9% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 27 years. For every 100 females there were 98.70 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 97.70 males.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Median_household_income" title="Median household income">median income for a household</a> in the county was $37,547, and the median income for a family was $53,991. Males had a median income of $35,577 versus $27,225 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the county was $19,952. About 6.2% of families and 15.9% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 9.0% of those under age 18 and 7.3% of those age 65 or over.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Douglas County is currently served by county commissioners Mike Gaughan, Nancy Thellman, and Jim Flory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Democratic state representatives of the county include John Wilson (10th District), <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Ballard" title="Barbara Ballard">Barbara Ballard</a> (44th District), and Dennis Highberger (46th District); Republican state representatives include Connie O'Brien (42nd District), <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Sloan_(Kansas_politician)" title="Thomas Sloan (Kansas politician)">Tom Sloan</a> (45th District) and Ken Corbet (54th District). The three state senators representing the county, <a href="/wiki/Marci_Francisco" title="Marci Francisco">Marci Francisco</a> (2nd District), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Holland_(Kansas_politician)" title="Tom Holland (Kansas politician)">Tom Holland</a> (3rd District), and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hensley" title="Anthony Hensley">Anthony Hensley</a> (19th District), are all Democrats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_highways">Major highways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Major highways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interstate_70_(Kansas)" title="Interstate 70 (Kansas)">Interstate 70</a>, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_Turnpike" title="Kansas Turnpike">Kansas Turnpike</a>, runs east to west just north of Lawrence.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Route_59_(Kansas)" title="U.S. Route 59 (Kansas)">U.S. Highway 59</a> runs north to south through the middle of the county and the middle of Lawrence.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Route_40_(Kansas)" title="U.S. Route 40 (Kansas)">U.S. Highway 40</a> virtually follows the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Trail" title="Oregon Trail">Oregon Trail</a> heading west out of Lawrence.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Route_56_(Kansas)" title="U.S. Route 56 (Kansas)">U.S. Highway 56</a> runs east to west in the southern half of the county, going through <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_City,_Kansas" title="Baldwin City, Kansas">Baldwin City</a> and skirts the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_Trail" title="Santa Fe Trail">Santa Fe Trail</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/K-10_(Kansas_highway)" title="K-10 (Kansas highway)">K-10</a> runs from the I-70 Lecompton Exchange along the south and west border of Lawrence to US-59 then north until 23rd Street where it heads east out of town into Johnson County.</li>
<li>Other major highways include: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Route_24_(Kansas)" title="U.S. Route 24 (Kansas)">US-24</a> which is in Grant township leading from Leavenworth to Jefferson County; <a href="/wiki/K-32_(Kansas_highway)" title="K-32 (Kansas highway)">K-32</a> starts just outside Lawrence and leads into Leavenworth County and <a href="/wiki/K-33_(Kansas_highway)" title="K-33 (Kansas highway)">K-33</a> is in extreme southeast Douglas County and leads into Franklin County.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unified_school_districts">Unified school districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Unified school districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Douglas County is served by seven school districts.</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.usd497.org/" rel="nofollow">Lawrence USD 497</a> serves <a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Kansas" title="Lawrence, Kansas">Lawrence</a> and the Clinton Lake area.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.usd348.com/" rel="nofollow">Baldwin City USD 348</a> serves <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_City,_Kansas" title="Baldwin City, Kansas">Baldwin City</a> and most of southern Douglas County.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.usd343.net/" rel="nofollow">Perry-Lecompton USD 343</a> serves <a href="/wiki/Lecompton,_Kansas" title="Lecompton, Kansas">Lecompton</a> and most of northwest Douglas County.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.eudoraschools.org/" rel="nofollow">Eudora USD 491</a> serves <a href="/wiki/Eudora,_Kansas" title="Eudora, Kansas">Eudora</a> and the northeast part of the county.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.usd434.org/" rel="nofollow">Santa Fe Trail USD 434</a> covers the southwest part of the county.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.wellsville-usd289.org/" rel="nofollow">Wellsville USD 289</a> covers extreme southeast Douglas County.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.snh450.k12.ks.us/" rel="nofollow">Shawnee Heights USD 450</a> services the extreme western part of the county including <a href="/wiki/Big_Springs,_Kansas" title="Big Springs, Kansas">Big Springs</a>.</li>
</ul>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kansas" title="University of Kansas">University of Kansas</a>'s main campus is located in Lawrence as is <a href="/wiki/Haskell_Indian_Nations_University" title="Haskell Indian Nations University">Haskell Indian Nations University</a>. <a href="/wiki/Baker_University" title="Baker University">Baker University</a>, the state's oldest university, is located in Baldwin City.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parks">Parks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Parks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Clinton_Lake_(Kansas)" title="Clinton Lake (Kansas)">Clinton Lake</a>, completed in 1980, offers boating, fishing and other water sports and various parks surrounding the lake provides camping and trails for mountain biking, hiking and horseback riding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lone Star Lake is a small country lake to the southwest of Lawrence offers fishing, boating and camping. Just northwest of Baldwin City is Douglas State Fishing Lake which provides hunting, fishing and limited camping. Other parks around the county include <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Black_Jack" title="Battle of Black Jack">Black Jack Park</a> which includes the Ivan Boyd Prairie Preserve and Robert Hall Pearson Memorial Park, Broken Arrow Park in Lawrence and Wells Overlook Park just south of Lawrence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Major events in the county include the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maple_Leaf_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maple Leaf Festival (page does not exist)">Maple Leaf Festival</a> in Baldwin City every third full weekend in October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lecompton,_Kansas" title="Lecompton, Kansas">Lecompton</a>'s Territorial Days take place every year in June<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and Lawrence has many parades throughout the year including <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Patrick%27s_Day" title="St. Patrick's Day">St. Patrick's Day</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_Townships">Historic Townships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_County,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Historic Townships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Douglas_County_1889.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="235" data-file-width="274" height="189" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/41/Douglas_County_1889.gif/220px-Douglas_County_1889.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/4/41/Douglas_County_1889.gif 1.5x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The county originally had only four townships. <a href="/wiki/Lecompton_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Lecompton Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Lecompton</a> comprised the area of Lecompton, <a href="/wiki/Kanwaka_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Kanwaka Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Kanwaka</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Clinton Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Clinton</a> townships; Washington took the place of <a href="/wiki/Marion_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Marion Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Marion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willow_Springs_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Willow Springs Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Willow Springs</a> townships; <a href="/wiki/Wakarusa_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Wakarusa Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Wakarusa</a> comprised both Wakarusa and <a href="/wiki/Eudora_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Eudora Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Eudora</a> townships; and Calhoun was the original name of <a href="/wiki/Palmyra_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Palmyra Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Palmyra</a> township. <a href="/wiki/Grant_Township,_Douglas_County,_Kansas" title="Grant Township, Douglas County, Kansas">Grant</a> township was annexed from <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Kansas" title="Jefferson County, Kansas">Jefferson County</a> in 1874.</p>
