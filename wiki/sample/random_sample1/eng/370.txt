<p>The <b>10th <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="IAAF World Championships in Athletics">World Championships in Athletics</a></b>, under the auspices of the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_of_Athletics_Federations" title="International Association of Athletics Federations">International Association of Athletics Federations</a> (IAAF), were held in the <a href="/wiki/Helsinki_Olympic_Stadium" title="Helsinki Olympic Stadium">Olympic Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> (6 August 2005 – 14 August 2005), the site of the first IAAF World Championships in 1983. One theme of the 2005 championships was <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_athletics" title="Paralympic athletics">paralympic</a> events, some of which were included as <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">exhibition</a> events. Much of the event was played in extremely heavy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_World_Championships_in_Athletics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bidding">Bidding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_World_Championships_in_Athletics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bidding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The original winning bid for the competition was for <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> but the cost to build the required stadium at <a href="/wiki/Picketts_Lock" title="Picketts Lock">Picketts Lock</a> and host the event was deemed too expensive by the government. <a href="/wiki/UK_Athletics" title="UK Athletics">UK Athletics</a> suggested to move the host city to <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> (using <a href="/wiki/Don_Valley_Stadium" title="Don Valley Stadium">Don Valley Stadium</a>), but the IAAF stated that having London as the host city was central to their winning the bid. The championships bidding process was reopened as a result. The United Kingdom's withdrawal as host was the first case for a major sporting event in a developed country since <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>'s withdrawal as host of the <a href="/wiki/1976_Winter_Olympics" title="1976 Winter Olympics">1976 Winter Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Helsinki was considered by many to be the outsider in the race to host the games with rival bids being presented by <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>; <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Opening_ceremony">Opening ceremony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_World_Championships_in_Athletics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Opening ceremony">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Apocalyptica" title="Apocalyptica">Apocalyptica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nightwish" title="Nightwish">Nightwish</a> performed at the opening ceremony of the event over a heavy rainfall. <a href="/wiki/Geir_R%C3%B6nning" title="Geir Rönning">Geir Rönning</a>, Finland's <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2005" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2005">Eurovision Song Contest 2005</a> entrant, sang "Victory" the official song of the 2005 IAAF World Championships.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_World_Championships_in_Athletics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the addition of the women's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-distance_track_event" title="Long-distance track event">3000 metres steeplechase</a> to the schedule, that year's program of events was closer to parity for women and men. With the exception of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_walking" title="Race walking">50 km walk</a> the women competed in practically the same events as the men. Two differences remaining from before, though, were the short hurdles race (100 metres for women vs. 110 metres for men), and the multi-event competition (<a href="/wiki/Heptathlon" title="Heptathlon">heptathlon</a> for women vs. <a href="/wiki/Decathlon" title="Decathlon">decathlon</a> for men).</p>
<p>Since the first <a href="/wiki/1983_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1983 World Championships in Athletics">World Championships in Helsinki 1983</a>, seven new events have been added for women:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-distance_track_event" title="Long-distance track event">10000 metres</a>, introduced in <a href="/wiki/1987_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1987 World Championships in Athletics">1987</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-distance_track_event" title="Long-distance track event">5000 metres</a>, replaced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_distance_track_event" title="Middle distance track event">3000 metres</a> in <a href="/wiki/1995_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1995 World Championships in Athletics">1995</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Triple_jump" title="Triple jump">triple jump</a>, introduced in <a href="/wiki/1993_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1993 World Championships in Athletics">1993</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_walking" title="Race walking">20 km walk</a> introduced in <a href="/wiki/1999_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1999 World Championships in Athletics">1999</a>, replaced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_walking" title="Race walking">10 km walk</a> that first appeared in <a href="/wiki/1987_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1987 World Championships in Athletics">1987</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pole_vault" title="Pole vault">pole vault</a>, introduced in <a href="/wiki/1999_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1999 World Championships in Athletics">1999</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hammer_throw" title="Hammer throw">hammer throw</a>, introduced in <a href="/wiki/1999_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1999 World Championships in Athletics">1999</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-distance_track_event" title="Long-distance track event">3000 metres steeplechase</a>, introduced in 2005</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Drug_testing">Drug testing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_World_Championships_in_Athletics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Drug testing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IAAF conducted their largest ever anti-doping program at an athletics event for the championships, with 705 athletes subjected to a total 884 of tests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> There were two athletes who failed drugs tests: Indian <a href="/wiki/Discus_throw" title="Discus throw">discus thrower</a> <a href="/wiki/Neelam_Jaswant_Singh" title="Neelam Jaswant Singh">Neelam Jaswant Singh</a> tested positive for the stimulant <a href="/wiki/Pemoline" title="Pemoline">pemoline</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vladyslav_Piskunov" title="Vladyslav Piskunov">Vladyslav Piskunov</a>, a Ukrainian <a href="/wiki/Hammer_throw" title="Hammer throw">hammer thrower</a>, tested positive for the steroid <a href="/wiki/Drostanolone" title="Drostanolone">drostanolone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Singh received a two-year ineligibility ban,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> while Piskunov received a life ban from athletics as this was his second offence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2013, the IAAF announced that re-testing of samples taken during these championships revealed that five medal winners had proved positive for banned substances. The athletes involved were Belarusian <a href="/wiki/Nadzeya_Ostapchuk" title="Nadzeya Ostapchuk">Nadzeya Ostapchuk</a> (shot put gold), Belarusian <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Tsikhan" title="Ivan Tsikhan">Ivan Tsikhan</a> (hammer throw gold), Russian <a href="/wiki/Olga_Kuzenkova" title="Olga Kuzenkova">Olga Kuzenkova</a> (hammer throw gold), Russian <a href="/wiki/Tatyana_Kotova" title="Tatyana Kotova">Tatyana Kotova</a> (long jump silver) and Belarus's <a href="/wiki/Vadim_Devyatovskiy" title="Vadim Devyatovskiy">Vadim Devyatovskiy</a> (men's hammer silver).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-6">[6]</a></sup> Belarusian <a href="/wiki/Andrei_Mikhnevich" title="Andrei Mikhnevich">Andrei Mikhnevich</a> (shot put 6th) had also tested positive and was disqualified.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAAF2013_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAAF2013-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

Before the final event, Klüft's lead was 18 points and Barber needed to beat her by 1.5 sec in the 800 m to win the gold medal. <p>To commemorate the 2005 World Championships in Athletics the Finnish government issued a high value commemorative euro coin, the €20 <a href="/wiki/Euro_gold_and_silver_commemorative_coins_(Finland)#2005_coinage" title="Euro gold and silver commemorative coins (Finland)">10th IAAF World Championships in Athletics commemorative coin</a>, minted in 2005. The obverse of the coin features <a href="/wiki/Helsinki_Olympic_Stadium" title="Helsinki Olympic Stadium">Helsinki Olympic Stadium</a> and above the stadium random waves express the feeling of the games.</p>
