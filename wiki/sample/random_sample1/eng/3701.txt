<p><b>Dăbuleni</b> (<small>Romanian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Romanian" title="Help:IPA for Romanian">[dəbuˈlenʲ]</a></span>) is a town of <a href="/wiki/Dolj_County" title="Dolj County">Dolj County</a>, known for the sandy areas surrounding it, since the 1980s have become part of an 80,000 hectares (800 km², 310 mi²) desert, known as the Oltenian Sahara. The town is the only place in Europe where an official <b>Sand Museum</b> exists. It was declared a town in 2004 (Law no. 83/2004). If the north part of the town is a desert, the south part, a <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> flooding area known as the "Melon Kingdom", from which the "Dăbuleni" melons, are famous in all <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>.</p>
<p>One village, Chiașu, is administered by the town.</p>
