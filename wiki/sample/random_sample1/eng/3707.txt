<p><b>The Dirty Mac</b> were a <a href="/wiki/One-off" title="One-off">one-time</a> English <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> consisting of <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Clapton" title="Eric Clapton">Eric Clapton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keith_Richards" title="Keith Richards">Keith Richards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Mitchell" title="Mitch Mitchell">Mitch Mitchell</a> that Lennon put together for <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">the Rolling Stones</a>' TV special titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones_Rock_and_Roll_Circus" title="The Rolling Stones Rock and Roll Circus">The Rolling Stones Rock and Roll Circus</a></i>. Recorded on 11 December 1968, this was the first time since the formation of <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a> that Lennon, who was still in the group, had performed in public without them. The Dirty Mac recorded a rendition of the Lennon-penned Beatles track "<a href="/wiki/Yer_Blues" title="Yer Blues">Yer Blues</a>" and then went on to back up <a href="/wiki/Yoko_Ono" title="Yoko Ono">Yoko Ono</a> and violinist <a href="/wiki/Ivry_Gitlis" title="Ivry Gitlis">Ivry Gitlis</a> on a track called "Whole Lotta Yoko" (essentially an extended blues jam on top of which Ono improvised free-form vocalizations). The name, thought of by Lennon, was a play on "<a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Mac" title="Fleetwood Mac">Fleetwood Mac</a>" who at that time were a very popular band in the United Kingdom. When asked what type of <a href="/wiki/Guitar_amplifier" title="Guitar amplifier">guitar amp</a> Lennon would like to use for the performance his answer was "One that plays".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1996, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones_Rock_and_Roll_Circus_(album)" title="The Rolling Stones Rock and Roll Circus (album)">The Rolling Stones Rock and Roll Circus</a></i>, the album of the event, was issued, concurrently with a home video of the event. The DVD issue followed in 2004.</p>
