<p><b>Dufferin</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Toronto_TTC_stations" title="List of Toronto TTC stations">subway station</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bloor%E2%80%93Danforth_line" title="Bloor–Danforth line">Bloor–Danforth line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It is located at <a href="/wiki/Dufferin_Street" title="Dufferin Street">Dufferin Street</a> just north of <a href="/wiki/Bloor_Street" title="Bloor Street">Bloor Street West</a>. It opened in 1966 as part of the original segment of the subway line. Wi-Fi service is available at this station. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture_and_art">Architecture and art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dufferin_(TTC)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Architecture and art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG/220px-Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG/330px-Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG/440px-Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dufferin_Station-Renovated-Stairs.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A renovated staircase featuring a multicoloured tile design.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>When the station first opened, the entrance on the west side of Dufferin Street had a tile back wall, a three sided glass and aluminium enclosure entrance at ground level, an opaque flat roof, red signage, and a red accent stripe. In 1974, the Dovercourt Baptist Church constructed the abutting new red brick facility and senior's residence (New Horizons Tower).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The entrance on the east side of the street is inset into the westerly facade of the Bloor Dufferin Medical Centre. Inside, smooth, unadorned green-coloured rectangular wall tiles were used, with a strip of narrower black tiles near the ceiling, and terrazzo floor tiles.</p>
<p>The 2010-2014 modernization of the station significantly modifies the original west entrance, updating signage, adding an elevator for accessibility, adding ceiling height and additional glazed elements, and adding a canopy covering the sidewalk. A canopy has also been added to the Bloor Dufferin Medical Centre to shelter the east side bus stops, and two additional exits have been added to Russet Ave, one block west of the main entrances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A public art component, titled “Something Happens Here” by Eduardo Aquino and Karen Shanski of spmb was also added, consisting of colourful mosaics of highly pixelated images of activity from the surrounding communities. The mosaics also include numerous metallic tiles featuring local logos, icons and historical references.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_modernization">Station modernization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dufferin_(TTC)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Station modernization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dufferin Station was upgraded and modernized under the TTC's <i>Station Modernization</i> program. Work commenced in September 2009 and was fully complete in November 2014, taking over twice the projected time to complete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The "modernization" included the installation of street level elevators for accessibility, the installation of a second entrance from Russett Avenue, two second exits (one on the northwest corner of Russet Avenue and Bloor Street, and the other on the northeast corner, providing direct access from the westbound platform), rebuilding of the bus waiting area, and new and modernized station finishes, art and lighting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The two second exits opened earlier than other improvements, in August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
