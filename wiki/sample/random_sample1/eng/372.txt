<p><b><a href="/wiki/March" title="March">March</a></b> <b><a href="/wiki/2006" title="2006">2006</a></b> was the third month of that common year. The month, which began on a <a href="/wiki/Wednesday" title="Wednesday">Wednesday</a>, ended on a <a href="/wiki/Friday" title="Friday">Friday</a> after 31 days.</p>

<dt>Arts and culture</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>In the fictional CW network drama <i><a href="/wiki/One_Tree_Hill_(TV_series)" title="One Tree Hill (TV series)">One Tree Hill</a></i>, this date marks a school shooting which took the lives of two people and drastically changed the dynamic of the show. It has been called one of the most essential and touching episodes in high school drama history.</li>
</ul>

<dt>Business and economy</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>A member of the board of directors of major German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steel_manufacturing" title="Steel manufacturing">steel manufacturing</a> company <a href="/wiki/ThyssenKrupp" title="ThyssenKrupp">ThyssenKrupp</a> AG says the company is "examining all its options," and may not complete its proposed acquisition of Canadian steel company <a href="/wiki/Dofasco" title="Dofasco">Dofasco</a>. <a class="external text" href="http://news.moneycentral.msn.com/provider/providerarticle.asp?feed=AP&amp;Date=20060301&amp;ID=5546668" rel="nofollow">(MSN Money)</a></li>

<td class="description">
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> voted 89–10 to renew the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USA_PATRIOT_Act" title="USA PATRIOT Act">USA PATRIOT Act</a> after two extensions. In its vote next week, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> will likely also vote to renew the Act, analysts say. <a class="external text" href="http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/11638713/" rel="nofollow">(MSNBC)</a></li>
<li>President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>, on his first visit to India, issues a joint statement with Indian Prime Minister on their growing strategic partnership, emphasising their agreement on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S.-India_Civil_Nuclear_Cooperation_Initiative" title="U.S.-India Civil Nuclear Cooperation Initiative">civil nuclear cooperation</a>. An estimated crowd of 100,000 people protest against Bush in <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>. <a class="external text" href="http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/articleshow/1434573.cms" rel="nofollow">(Times of India)</a> <a class="external text" href="http://www.khaleejtimes.com/DisplayArticle.asp?xfile=data/subcontinent/2006/March/subcontinent_March35.xml&amp;section=subcontinent" rel="nofollow">(Khaleej Times)</a> <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080223140835/http://www.forbes.com/work/feeds/afx/2006/03/02/afx2564440.html" rel="nofollow">(Forbes)</a> <a class="external text" href="http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/articleshow/1418449.cms" rel="nofollow">(Times of India)</a> <a class="external text" href="http://edition.cnn.com/2006/WORLD/asiapcf/03/02/bush.india.visit/index.html" rel="nofollow">(CNN)</a></li>
<li>A shipwreck from the 14th century was found buried in <a href="/wiki/Riddarfj%C3%A4rden" title="Riddarfjärden">Riddarfjärden</a> Bay in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. If the ship is well preserved, there are plans to remove it from the waters. <a class="external text" href="http://abcnews.go.com/Technology/wireStory?id=1681476" rel="nofollow">(ABC)</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alaksandar_Kazulin" title="Alaksandar Kazulin">Alaksandar Kazulin</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belarusian_Social_Democratic_Party" title="Belarusian Social Democratic Party">Social Democratic Party</a> candidate for the office of <a href="/wiki/President_of_Belarus" title="President of Belarus">President of Belarus</a>, was detained by <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a> police after he was rejected entrance to a congress hosted by current leader <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a>. Kazulin also suffered injuries during the course of his detention, which is still being enforced, though the <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_2006" title="Belarusian presidential election, 2006">elections</a> will commence in 17 days. <a class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4766024.stm" rel="nofollow">(BBC)</a></li>
<li>Traces of a prehistoric, 8,000-year-old civilization are found in <a href="/wiki/Shahrud" title="Shahrud">Shahrud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. The discoveries included ovens, craft workshops, and other evidence of settlements. <a class="external text" href="http://www.payvand.com/news/06/mar/1023.html" rel="nofollow">(Payvand)</a></li>
<li>Televangelist <a href="/wiki/Pat_Robertson" title="Pat Robertson">Pat Robertson</a> loses his bid for re-election to the board of directors of the <a href="/wiki/National_Religious_Broadcasters" title="National Religious Broadcasters">National Religious Broadcasters</a>. <a class="external text" href="http://www.dailypress.com/news/local/virginia/dp-sou--robertson-evange0302mar02,0,2148019.story?coll=dp-headlines-virginia" rel="nofollow">(Associated Press)</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dubai_Ports_World_controversy" title="Dubai Ports World controversy">Dubai Ports World controversy</a>: The United States urges the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> to end its boycott of <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>: "The Bush administration said yesterday it is pressing the United Arab Emirates to drop its economic boycott of Israel – a major sticking point in the proposed takeover of key U.S. ports by a UAE-owned firm." <a class="external text" href="http://www.washtimes.com/world/20060301-113324-4242r.htm" rel="nofollow">(The Washington Times)</a></li>
<li>Sir <a href="/wiki/Menzies_Campbell" title="Menzies Campbell">Menzies Campbell</a> has been elected the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_leaders_of_the_Liberal_Democrats" title="List of leaders of the Liberal Democrats">leader of the Liberal Democrats</a>. <a class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/4763646.stm" rel="nofollow">(BBC)</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/European_Central_Bank" title="European Central Bank">European Central Bank</a> raises <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a> base interest rates by 0.25% to 2.5%. The move affects the 12 members of the <a href="/wiki/Eurozone" title="Eurozone">Eurozone</a>. <a class="external text" href="http://news.ft.com/cms/s/e728076c-a9ce-11da-96ea-0000779e2340.html" rel="nofollow">(FT)</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>: Masked gunmen, since revealed to be <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenyan</a> police, attack the offices of leading newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Standard_(Kenya)" title="The Standard (Kenya)">The Standard</a></i> and its television station <a href="/wiki/Kenya_Television_Network" title="Kenya Television Network">KTN</a>, following their report that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heads_of_State_of_Kenya" title="Heads of State of Kenya">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Mwai_Kibaki" title="Mwai Kibaki">Mwai Kibaki</a> held secret meetings with key opposition figure <a href="/wiki/Kalonzo_Musyoka" title="Kalonzo Musyoka">Kalonzo Musyoka</a>. <a class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/4765250.stm" rel="nofollow">(BBC)</a> <a class="external text" href="http://www.alertnet.org/thenews/newsdesk/IRIN/594f371980df55020e96a8268a9aa506.htm" rel="nofollow">(Reuters)</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Black_site#Investigations_in_France_concerning_CIA_flights" title="Black site">CIA flights</a>: French newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Figaro" title="Le Figaro">Le Figaro</a></i> reveals that the <a href="/wiki/Attorney_general" title="Attorney general">attorney general</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bobigny" title="Bobigny">Bobigny</a> has opened up an investigation concerning the landing of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">CIA</a> flight in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_Bourget_Airport" title="Le Bourget Airport">Le Bourget Airport</a> following a complaint deposed at the end of December 2005 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NGO" title="NGO">NGOs</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Human_Rights_Leagues" title="International Federation of Human Rights Leagues">International Federation of Human Rights Leagues</a> and the French <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligue_des_droits_de_l%27homme" title="Ligue des droits de l'homme">Ligue des droits de l'homme</a></i>. <a class="external text" href="http://www.lefigaro.fr/france/20060302.FIG000000200_la_france_enquete_sur_les_avions_de_la_cia.html" rel="nofollow">(Le Figaro)</a></li>
<li>Just two days before <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">U.S. President</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. <li>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">UN</a>'s refugee agency, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Refugees" title="United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees">UNHCR</a>, says it has been ordered to leave <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a> within one month. <a class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/4826278.stm" rel="nofollow">(BBC)</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Belarusian_presidential_election,_2006" title="Belarusian presidential election, 2006">Belarusian presidential election, 2006</a>: <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a> has been re-elected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Belarusian_rulers" title="List of Belarusian rulers">president</a> of <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> with 82.6 percent of all votes, in an election which is considered by many to have been rigged.
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe" title="Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe">Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe</a>, which monitored the election, concluded that the presidential election failed to meet OSCE commitments for democratic elections. <a class="external text" href="http://news.yahoo.com/s/nm/20060320/wl_nm/belarus_dc" rel="nofollow">(Reuters)</a> <a class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4823800.stm" rel="nofollow">(BBC)</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a> also monitored the election and declared that the election was fair and that the results must be respected. <a class="external text" href="http://news.monstersandcritics.com/europe/article_1148866.php/Russia_Belarus_election_was_fair_and_open" rel="nofollow">(Monsters and Critics)</a></li>
<li>The United States and <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> condemn the elections. The White House, which has previously labeled Mr Lukashenko a dictator, says it does not accept the results. The EU says it is likely that it will impose sanctions. <a class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4824642.stm" rel="nofollow">(BBC)</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>At 0730 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Time_Zones" title="Australian Time Zones">AEST</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Cyclone" title="Tropical Cyclone">Tropical Cyclone</a> <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Larry" title="Cyclone Larry">Larry</a> makes landfall near <a href="/wiki/Innisfail,_Queensland" title="Innisfail, Queensland">Innisfail</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, Australia, with wind gusts of 290 km/h (180 mph) recorded, which would make it a Category 5 storm on the Australian scale for severity of cyclones. <a class="external text" href="http://www.theage.com.au/news/National/Cyclone-Larry-rips-through-north-Qld/2006/03/20/1142703243509.html" rel="nofollow">(AAP)</a></li>
<li>Russian President <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> visits Beijing on energy talks with Chinese President <a href="/wiki/Hu_Jintao" title="Hu Jintao">Hu Jintao</a>. <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080219112640/http://www.forbes.com/markets/feeds/afx/2006/03/20/afx2608814.html" rel="nofollow">(Forbes)</a></li>
<li>Charles, <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales" title="Prince of Wales">Prince of Wales</a>, and his wife, Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall, begin official visit to <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> and India.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vernal_Equinox" title="Vernal Equinox">Vernal Equinox</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autumnal_Equinox" title="Autumnal Equinox">Autumnal Equinox</a> occurred at 18:26 UTC.</li>
