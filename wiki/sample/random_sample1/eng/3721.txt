<p><b>Duncan James</b> (born <b>Duncan Matthew James Inglis</b>; 7 April 1978)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English singer, actor and television presenter. He is best known as a member of the boy band <a href="/wiki/Blue_(English_band)" title="Blue (English band)">Blue</a> from 2000 to 2005 and again from 2009 following their reformation. He is also known for playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryan_Knight_(Hollyoaks)" title="Ryan Knight (Hollyoaks)">Ryan Knight</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Hollyoaks" title="Hollyoaks">Hollyoaks</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>James was born in <a href="/wiki/Salisbury" title="Salisbury">Salisbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-1">[1]</a></sup> He grew up an <a href="/wiki/Only_child" title="Only child">only child</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a> and was raised primarily by his mother and grandparents after his father abandoned his mother before he was born.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was raised a "strict Catholic"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notw_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-notw-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mailonline_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mailonline-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and educated at <a href="/wiki/Dumpton_School" title="Dumpton School">Dumpton School</a> (where his grandfather worked as a music teacher),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Milldown School (<a href="/wiki/Blandford_Forum" title="Blandford Forum">Blandford Forum</a>), <a href="/wiki/Corfe_Hills_School" title="Corfe Hills School">Corfe Hills School</a> (<a href="/wiki/Broadstone,_Dorset" title="Broadstone, Dorset">Broadstone</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Sidmouth_College" title="Sidmouth College">Sidmouth College</a> (East Devon). At the age of 15 he played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puck_(Shakespeare)" title="Puck (Shakespeare)">Puck</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night's Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>. He played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr_Watson" title="Dr Watson">Dr Watson</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a></i> for Sidmouth Youth Theatre. James also worked for <a href="/wiki/Haven_Holidays" title="Haven Holidays">Haven Holidays</a> as a Havenmate on the Isle of Wight and at West Bay in Dorset.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.932005:_Career_with_Blue">2000–2005: Career with Blue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000–2005: Career with Blue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Blue_(English_band)" title="Blue (English band)">Blue (English band)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Blue_4.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1060" data-file-width="1497" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Blue_4.jpg/200px-Blue_4.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Blue_4.jpg/300px-Blue_4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Blue_4.jpg/400px-Blue_4.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

<p>James, along with <a href="/wiki/Antony_Costa" title="Antony Costa">Antony Costa</a>, convinced <a href="/wiki/Lee_Ryan" title="Lee Ryan">Lee Ryan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simon_Webbe" title="Simon Webbe">Simon Webbe</a> in early 2000 to form the group Blue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Blue's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>-influenced pop allowed the group to achieve commercial success in the United Kingdom and many other countries, including Ireland, Portugal, Belgium, Italy, France, Australia and New Zealand. The band released their debut single "<a href="/wiki/All_Rise_(song)" title="All Rise (song)">All Rise</a>" in May 2001 and it reached No. 4 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. Their follow up single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Too_Close_(Blue_song)" title="Too Close (Blue song)">Too Close</a>", was released in August 2001 peaking at No. 1 on the UK Singles Chart. They went on to achieve a second number one in November with the ballad "<a href="/wiki/If_You_Come_Back" title="If You Come Back">If You Come Back</a>". The album <i><a href="/wiki/All_Rise_(Blue_album)" title="All Rise (Blue album)">All Rise</a></i> was released in time for Christmas and reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>, eventually selling in excess of 1.8 million albums in the UK. The final single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Fly_By_II" title="Fly By II">Fly By II</a>", reached No. 6 in March 2002.</p>
<p>Their second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Love_(Blue_album)" title="One Love (Blue album)">One Love</a></i>, was released in October 2002; it entered the UK Albums Chart at No. 1 and sold more than 150,000 copies. Three singles were released from the album: "<a href="/wiki/One_Love_(Blue_song)" title="One Love (Blue song)">One Love</a>", which peaked at No. 3, "<a href="/wiki/Sorry_Seems_to_Be_the_Hardest_Word" title="Sorry Seems to Be the Hardest Word">Sorry Seems to Be the Hardest Word</a>", featuring vocals from <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, which peaked at No. 1, and "<a href="/wiki/U_Make_Me_Wanna_(Blue_song)" title="U Make Me Wanna (Blue song)">U Make Me Wanna</a>", which peaked at No. 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Their third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Guilty_(Blue_album)" title="Guilty (Blue album)">Guilty</a></i>, was released in autumn 2003; it too entered the UK Albums Chart at No. 1, and sold 100,000 copies in the week of release. The album spawned four singles, including "Guilty", which peaked at No. 2, "Signed, Sealed, Delivered, I'm Yours", featuring vocals from <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angie_Stone" title="Angie Stone">Angie Stone</a>, which peaked at No. 11, "Breathe Easy", which peaked at No. 4, and "Bubblin'", which peaked at No. 9.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>A compilation album, <i>Best of Blue</i>, followed in July 2005; this included three tracks only previously performed in Japan: "The Gift", "It's Alright" and "Elements". By the indication of the album booklet, a live version of "Lonely This Christmas" from CD:UK was intended to be track seven on the album; however, this was removed from the track listing for unknown reasons. The album was available in several European countries, as well as Japan, Thailand<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and China; however it was a commercial failure, and failed to chart anywhere. The album was not released in the UK, despite copies being widely available.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="(September 2013)">why?</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.932010:_Future_Past.2C_stage_and_television">2006–2010: <i>Future Past</i>, stage and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–2010: Future Past, stage and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>James returned to the music scene in May 2006 with his solo single "Sooner or Later", releasing the video in early May and planning to release the single commercially on 5 June 2006. The video reached No. 2 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Box_(UK_TV_channel)" title="The Box (UK TV channel)">The Box</a> and No. 5 on <a href="/wiki/The_Hits" title="The Hits">The Hits</a>. However, the single was no commercial success and entered the British charts at No. 35, before slipping down to No. 72 in its second week. "Can't Stop a River" was James's next single. Released on 21 August 2006, the track would only reach No. 59 in the UK and No. 97 in Ireland. In continental Europe, where "Can't Stop a River" was released a day earlier than in the UK, it similarly proved unpopular, dropping out of the singles charts in many countries such as France, Turkey, Germany, and Belgium. James' album also fell short, selling less than 1,000 copies in the UK in its first week (and failing to reach the Top 50). The album sold a total of 412,350 copies around the world. Although his debut album did not do as well as hoped (except in Italy, where it reached No. 2 on the chart and was certified as <i>platinum</i> for shipments of 80,000 copies), on 2 February 2007, music channel B4 premièred the video for "Amazed", his new single which was scheduled to be released 12 March 2007. However, there was once again a lack of interest. The release was cancelled and James was dropped from his recording contract. He has since announced he has quit his solo career. In 2007 he appeared in the West End production of <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_(musical)" title="Chicago (musical)">Chicago</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He completed ten more weeks in <i>Chicago</i> from 10 December 2007 until 9 February 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He appeared in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bill" title="The Bill">The Bill</a></i> entitled "The Morning After", which aired on 1 January 2009. It was confirmed in February 2009 that James had been selected to replace <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denise_Van_Outen" title="Denise Van Outen">Denise Van Outen</a> as presenter of <i><a href="/wiki/Grease:_the_School_Musical" title="Grease: the School Musical">Grease: the School Musical</a></i>, the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Hairspray:_The_School_Musical" title="Hairspray: The School Musical">Hairspray: The School Musical</a></i>. Filming commenced in early 2009. In May 2009 James announced the UK's voting in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2009" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2009">Eurovision Song Contest</a>. He presented <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah-Oke" title="Hannah-Oke">Hannah-Oke</a></i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_UK" title="Disney Channel UK">Disney Channel UK</a>, a singing dancing contest based on the popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i>. He also appeared on <i>Celebrity <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Are_You_Smarter_Than_A_10_Year_Old%3F_(UK)" title="Are You Smarter Than A 10 Year Old? (UK)">Are You Smarter Than A 10 Year Old?</a></i> and raised £10,000 for his charities. In 2006, James appeared with the other members of the boyband <a href="/wiki/Blue_(English_band)" title="Blue (English band)">Blue</a> on 26 June episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Mayhem" title="Ministry of Mayhem">Ministry of Mayhem</a></i>, where James and the other band members were all hypnotised by UK <a href="/wiki/Stage_hypnosis" title="Stage hypnosis">stage hypnotist</a> David Days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>James has worked occasionally as a television presenter, having presented the 95.8 Capital FM's <i><a href="/wiki/Party_in_the_Park" title="Party in the Park">Party in the Park</a></i> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince%27s_Trust" title="Prince's Trust">Prince's Trust</a> (2003 and 2004), and <a href="/wiki/ITV2" title="ITV2">ITV2</a>'s coverage of the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Soapstar_Superstar" title="Soapstar Superstar">Soapstar Superstar</a></i>, along with <a href="/wiki/Jayne_Middlemiss" title="Jayne Middlemiss">Jayne Middlemiss</a>. His other presenting work includes <i>Pop City Live</i>, <i>Variety Club Showbusiness Awards 2004</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/T4_(Channel_4)" title="T4 (Channel 4)">T4</a> Movie Special: Spider-Man 2</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Record_of_the_Year" title="The Record of the Year">Record of the Year</a>: Downloaded</i> and <a href="/wiki/GMTV" title="GMTV">GMTV</a>'s <i>Entertainment Today</i>.</p>
<p>He took part on ITV1's <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_on_Ice" title="Dancing on Ice">Dancing on Ice</a></i> in 2007, when he made it to the grand final alongside rugby player <a href="/wiki/Kyran_Bracken" title="Kyran Bracken">Kyran Bracken</a> and actress <a href="/wiki/Clare_Buckfield" title="Clare Buckfield">Clare Buckfield</a>. He came third in the contest, although was unable to perform his <a href="/wiki/Bolero" title="Bolero">Bolero</a> performance. In 2007 James played himself in the pilot episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Plus_One_(TV_series)" title="Plus One (TV series)">Plus One</a></i>, which was part of <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Comedy_Showcase" title="Comedy Showcase">Comedy Showcase</a></i>. A full series was broadcast in 2009, in which he played a broad <a href="/wiki/Caricature" title="Caricature">caricature</a> of himself called "Duncan From Blue". He made an appearance on a new show for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_One" title="Sky One">Sky One</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records_Smashed" title="Guinness World Records Smashed">Guinness World Records Smashed</a></i>, performing with <a href="/wiki/Festival4Stars" title="Festival4Stars">Festival4Stars</a> contestants.<sup class="reference" In 2008 he became an Ambassador of <a href="/wiki/Festival4Stars" title="Festival4Stars">Festival4Stars</a> talent competition after he judged a national final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival4stars.com_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-festival4stars.com-13">[13]</a></sup> James was cast as one of the leads in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde_(musical)" title="Legally Blonde (musical)">Legally Blonde</a></i> (adapted from the film <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde" title="Legally Blonde">Legally Blonde</a></i>) as it transferred from Broadway to London's West End in December 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He played opposite <a href="/wiki/Sheridan_Smith" title="Sheridan Smith">Sheridan Smith</a> as Elle, and <a href="/wiki/Aoife_Mulholland" title="Aoife Mulholland">Aoife Mulholland</a> as Brooke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In December 2009, James appeared as Warner Huntington III in the West End production of <i>Legally Blonde</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He appeared as a celebrity guest on 14 August 2009 on <i><a href="/wiki/...Cooks!" title="...Cooks!">Daily Cooks Challenge</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2010 he presented the quiz show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scream_if_You_Know_the_Answer!" title="Scream if You Know the Answer!">Scream if You Know the Answer!</a></i> where the contestants have to answer questions whilst on rollercoasters. In July 2010 he was a judge on <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Stop_Believing_(TV_series)" title="Don't Stop Believing (TV series)">Don't Stop Believing</a></i>, a talent show for choirs. He has his own radio show on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/95.8_Capital_FM" title="95.8 Capital FM">95.8 Capital FM</a>, every Sunday from 7–10 pm. In 2009 he briefly made an appearance on an episode of the UK reality show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/What_Katie_Did_Next" title="What Katie Did Next">What Katie Did Next</a></i>, about the life of model <a href="/wiki/Katie_Price" title="Katie Price">Katie Price</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.93present:_Return_with_Blue">2011–present: Return with Blue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011–present: Return with Blue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="United Kingdom in the Eurovision Song Contest">represented the UK</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="2011 Eurovision Song Contest">2011 Eurovision Song Contest</a> in <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> with the song "<a href="/wiki/I_Can_(Blue_song)" title="I Can (Blue song)">I Can</a>" coming in 11th place with 100 points. Blue released their fourth studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roulette_(Blue_album)" title="Roulette (Blue album)">Roulette</a></i> on 25 January 2013 with "<a href="/wiki/Hurt_Lovers" title="Hurt Lovers">Hurt Lovers</a>" as the lead single. On 21 February 2013, it was confirmed that the group would be joining <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Reunion" title="The Big Reunion">The Big Reunion</a></i> in which six groups from the past, including <a href="/wiki/Liberty_X" title="Liberty X">Liberty X</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atomic_Kitten" title="Atomic Kitten">Atomic Kitten</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/5ive" title="5ive">5ive</a>, reform for a one-off gig. Beginning in May 2013, the group were to tour Great Britain and Ireland with the other groups in <a href="/wiki/The_Big_Reunion_(concerts)" title="The Big Reunion (concerts)">The Big Reunion</a> concert series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 27 March 2013 the group announced they would embark on their first headlining tour later on in the year, their first tour in nearly ten years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to an interview he gave to <a href="/wiki/Gabby_Logan" title="Gabby Logan">Gabby Logan</a> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_5_Live" title="BBC Radio 5 Live">BBC Radio 5 Live</a> on 28 July 2007, he is a great-grandson of <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Chapman" title="Herbert Chapman">Herbert Chapman</a>, the manager of the <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> football team of the 1930s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>He has a daughter named Tianie Finn (born 26 February 2005) by former girlfriend Claire Grainger. He defined himself as <a href="/wiki/Bisexuality" title="Bisexuality">bisexual</a> in an interview with the <i><a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World" title="News of the World">News of the World</a></i> on 12 July 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notw_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-notw-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mailonline_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mailonline-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> although in 2012 interviews identified himself as gay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
