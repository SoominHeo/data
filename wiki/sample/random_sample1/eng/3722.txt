<p><b>Duncan Zowie Haywood Jones</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buckley_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-buckley-1">[1]</a></sup> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ʌ/ short 'u' in 'bud'">ʌ</span><span title="/ŋ/ 'ng' in 'sing'">ŋ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'z' in 'Zion'">z</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'h' in 'hi'">h</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/ʊ/ or /ə/ 2nd 'u' in 'beautiful'">ᵿ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/dʒ/ 'j' in 'jam'">dʒ</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="'z' in 'Zion'">z</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born 30 May 1971)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buckley_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-buckley-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English film director, film producer, and screenwriter. He is best known for directing the science fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/Moon_(film)" title="Moon (film)">Moon</a></i> (2009), winning the <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Outstanding_Debut_by_a_British_Writer,_Director_or_Producer" title="BAFTA Award for Outstanding Debut by a British Writer, Director or Producer">BAFTA Award for Outstanding Debut by a British Writer, Director or Producer</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Source_Code" title="Source Code">Source Code</a></i> (2011) and <i><a href="/wiki/Warcraft_(film)" title="Warcraft (film)">Warcraft</a></i> (2016). He is the son of late English singer <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a> and American model <a href="/wiki/Angie_Bowie" title="Angie Bowie">Angie Bowie</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jones was born in <a href="/wiki/Bromley" title="Bromley">Bromley, London</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buckley_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-buckley-1">[1]</a></sup> the only child from the marriage of <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a> and his first wife, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angela_Bowie" title="Angela Bowie">Angela</a>, a Cyprus-born American former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Model_(profession)" title="Model (profession)">model</a>. His birth prompted Bowie to write the songs "<a href="/wiki/Kooks_(song)" title="Kooks (song)">Kooks</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Oh!_You_Pretty_Things" title="Oh! You Pretty Things">Oh! You Pretty Things</a>" for his 1971 album <i><a href="/wiki/Hunky_Dory" title="Hunky Dory">Hunky Dory</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RockRoyalty_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RockRoyalty-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jones spent time growing up in London, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vevey,_Switzerland" title="Vevey, Switzerland">Vevey, Switzerland</a>, where he attended the first and second grade at the Commonwealth American School (now the International School of Lausanne) in <a href="/wiki/Lausanne" title="Lausanne">Lausanne</a>. When the Bowies divorced in February 1980, David Bowie was granted custody of nine-year-old Jones (who was then known as "Zowie"), and he visited his mother on his school holidays until ending contact with her at age 13. They remain estranged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> At age 14, he enrolled in the Scottish boarding school <a href="/wiki/Gordonstoun" title="Gordonstoun">Gordonstoun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the age of 12, Zowie decided that he preferred to be called "Joey", and used this nickname until shortening it to "Joe" in his later teen years. The press reported that he went by "Joe" in 1992 when attending his father's wedding to Iman. At this wedding he was the best man.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hello_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hello-5">[5]</a></sup> Whilst growing up, Jones dreamed of becoming a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a>, and his father frequently praised what he called Jones' "natural strength."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heni_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-heni-6">[6]</a></sup> He reverted to his birth name around the age of 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 1995, Jones graduated with a bachelor's degree in philosophy from the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Wooster" title="College of Wooster">College of Wooster</a>. He then pursued a Ph.D degree at <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_University" title="Vanderbilt University">Vanderbilt University</a> in Tennessee, but left before completion to attend <a href="/wiki/London_Film_School" title="London Film School">London Film School</a>, where he then graduated as a director in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jones was one of many cameramen at his father's widely televised 50th birthday party directed by Englishman <a href="/wiki/Tim_Pope" title="Tim Pope">Tim Pope</a> at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> in 1997 and also at two BowieNet concerts at <a href="/wiki/Roseland_Ballroom" title="Roseland Ballroom">Roseland Ballroom</a> in New York City in June 2000. He was also the in-game cinematics director for the political simulator <i><a href="/wiki/Republic:_The_Revolution" title="Republic: The Revolution">Republic: The Revolution</a></i>, as well as scripting elements of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Jones directed the 2006 campaign for the <a href="/wiki/French_Connection_(clothing)" title="French Connection (clothing)">French Connection</a> fashion label. The concept of 'Fashion vs Style' was to re-invigorate the brand and move it away from the former incarnation of FCUK, which style pundits believed had become tired and overused.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fcukoff_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-fcukoff-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-byebeattie_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-byebeattie-10">[10]</a></sup> The advert debuted in the week ending 20 February 2006 and featured two women (representing fashion and style) fighting and briefly kissing each other. The advertisement generated 127 complaints to the <a href="/wiki/Advertising_Standards_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Advertising Standards Authority (United Kingdom)">Advertising Standards Authority</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-byebeattie_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-byebeattie-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jones's first feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/Moon_(film)" title="Moon (film)">Moon</a></i>, was nominated for seven <a href="/wiki/British_Independent_Film_Awards_2009" title="British Independent Film Awards 2009">British Independent Film Awards</a> in 2009, and won two, Best British Independent Film, and the Douglas Hickox Award for Best British Director on their debut feature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-4">[4]</a></sup> It was also nominated for two <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy_Film_and_Television_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="British Academy Film and Television Arts (page does not exist)">BAFTA Awards</a> at the 2010 ceremony, winning Jones the <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Outstanding_Debut_by_a_British_Writer,_Director_or_Producer" title="BAFTA Award for Outstanding Debut by a British Writer, Director or Producer">BAFTA Award for Outstanding Debut by a British Writer, Director or Producer</a>. There have been 19 other nominations and wins for the film, from film festivals and societies, including the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy_Film_and_Television_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="British Academy Film and Television Arts (page does not exist)">BAFTA</a> <a href="/wiki/Carl_Foreman" title="Carl Foreman">Carl Foreman</a> Award.</p>
<p>He directed the <a href="/wiki/Summit_Entertainment" title="Summit Entertainment">Summit Entertainment</a> project <i><a href="/wiki/Source_Code" title="Source Code">Source Code</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> a science-fiction thriller from Vendome Pictures, which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Gordon" title="Mark Gordon">Mark Gordon</a>. <i>Source Code</i> was released on DVD and Blu-ray on 26<span class="nowrap"> </span>July 2011 in the United States.</p>
<p>On 30 January 2013, Jones was announced to direct the <a href="/wiki/Warcraft_(film)" title="Warcraft (film)"><i>Warcraft</i> film adaptation</a>, which was released in the summer of 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Hollywood_Reporter_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Hollywood_Reporter-12">[12]</a></sup> In November 2015, Jones announced his next film after <i>Warcraft</i> will be the science fiction thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Mute_(2017_film)" title="Mute (2017 film)">Mute</a></i>, starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Skarsgard" title="Alexander Skarsgard">Alexander Skarsgard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Rudd" title="Paul Rudd">Paul Rudd</a>. Jones had been developing the project for years and described it as a "spiritual sequel" to <i>Moon</i>, inspired by <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_Runner" title="Blade Runner">Blade Runner</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The film, set in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> forty years in the future, will follow a mute bartender investigating his partner's disappearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Jones announced on his Twitter that filming would begin on 28 September 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Liberty Films UK confirmed that principal photography had begun that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duncan_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jones announced his engagement to photographer Rodene Ronquillo on 28 June 2012. They married on 6 November 2012. On the same day, Ronquillo was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a>. The couple have been campaigning to raise awareness for the disease and for early diagnosis since then.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In February 2016, Jones and Ronquillo announced on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that they were expecting their first child, due that June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 10 July 2016, Ronquillo gave birth to their son Stenton David Jones. Jones announced his birth on 29 July 2016.</p>
<p>Jones is the half-brother of Alexandria "Lexi" Jones (born 2000) from his father's second marriage to the fashion model <a href="/wiki/Iman_(model)" title="Iman (model)">Iman</a>, and the half-brother of Stacia Larranna Celeste Lipka (born 1980) from his mother's relationship with musician Drew Blood (real name Andrew Lipka). He also has a <a href="/wiki/Sibling#Stepsibling" title="Sibling">stepsister</a>, Zulekha Haywood (born 1978), who is the daughter of Iman and former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA">NBA</a> basketball player <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Haywood" title="Spencer Haywood">Spencer Haywood</a>, Iman's second husband.</p>
