<p><b>Dennis Schröder</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈʃʀøːdɐ]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> born September 15, 1993) is a German professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). Standing at 6 ft 1 in (1.85 m), he plays at the <a href="/wiki/Point_guard" title="Point guard">point guard</a> position. Schröder formerly played for <a href="/wiki/SG_Braunschweig" title="SG Braunschweig">SG Braunschweig</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phantoms_Braunschweig" title="Phantoms Braunschweig">Phantoms Braunschweig</a>. He is widely regarded as one of the best talents in German basketball<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and has drawn many comparisons to fellow NBA <a href="/wiki/Point_guard" title="Point guard">point guard</a> <a href="/wiki/Rajon_Rondo" title="Rajon Rondo">Rajon Rondo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Phantoms_Braunschweig_.282010.E2.80.932013.29">Phantoms Braunschweig (2010–2013)</span></h3>
<p>Schröder started playing professional basketball in 2010 for <a href="/wiki/SG_Braunschweig" title="SG Braunschweig">SG Braunschweig</a>, <a href="/wiki/Farm_team" title="Farm team">farm team</a> of <a href="/wiki/Basketball_L%C3%B6wen_Braunschweig" title="Basketball Löwen Braunschweig">Phantoms Braunschweig</a>. In his first season with SUM Baskets Braunschweig, he averaged 7.8 points, 2.1 assists and 1.6 rebounds per game in a 2nd-tier German League. In the 2011–12 season he made a breakthrough, averaging 17.8 points and 6.7 assists over 23 regular season games. His team went in the playoffs where he averaged 18.8 points and 5.1 assists over 4 games. In the same season, he also played 30 games for Phantoms Braunschweig of the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_Bundesliga" title="Basketball Bundesliga">German League</a>, averaging 2.3 points, 0.7 assists and 0.8 rebounds in about 8 minutes per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2012–13 season he played 32 games for Phantoms Braunschweig, averaging 12 points, 3.2 assists and 2.5 rebounds in 25 minutes per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> For the season, he was named the League's Most Improved Player as well as Best Young German Player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Awards_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Awards-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Schröder was announced to play at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Nike_Hoop_Summit" title="Nike Hoop Summit">Nike Hoop Summit</a> for the World Select Team. Before officially playing at the Nike Hoop Summit, Schröder decided to declare for the <a href="/wiki/2013_NBA_draft" title="2013 NBA draft">2013 NBA draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On April 20, 2013, Schröder led his team to a 112-98 win. He finished with 18 points, 6 assists and 2 rebounds in 29 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlanta_Hawks_.282013.E2.80.93present.29">Atlanta Hawks (2013–present)</span></h3>
<p>On June 27, 2013, Schröder was selected with the 17th overall pick in the <a href="/wiki/2013_NBA_draft" title="2013 NBA draft">2013 NBA draft</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a>. On July 11, 2013, he signed with the Hawks and joined them for the <a href="/wiki/2013_NBA_Summer_League" title="2013 NBA Summer League">2013 NBA Summer League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On December 4, 2013, he was assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bakersfield_Jam" title="Bakersfield Jam">Bakersfield Jam</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_D-League" title="NBA D-League">NBA D-League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On December 16, 2013, he was recalled by the Hawks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014, Schröder re-joined the Hawks for the <a href="/wiki/2014_NBA_Summer_League" title="2014 NBA Summer League">2014 NBA Summer League</a>. On October 30, 2014, the Hawks exercised their third-year team option on Schröder's rookie scale contract, extending the contract through the 2015–16 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On December 22, 2014, he scored a then career-high 22 points in a 105–102 win over the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Participating in the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Rising_Stars_Challenge" title="Rising Stars Challenge">Rising Stars Challenge</a> on February 13, Schröder recorded 13 points, 9 assists and 3 steals as <i>Team World</i> defeated <i>Team USA</i>, 121–112.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> His performance was lauded by ESPN analysts, who drew comparisons to "a young Tony Parker" and called Schröder a "magician out there".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On March 15, 2015, he scored a career-high 24 points and tied his career high of 10 assists as he started in place of <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Teague_(basketball)" title="Jeff Teague (basketball)">Jeff Teague</a> and helped the Hawks defeat the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a>, 91–86.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 2, 2016, Schröder scored a playoff career-high 27 points in a Game 1 loss to the Cleveland Cavaliers during the 2016 conference semi-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 26, 2016, Schröder signed a four-year, $70 million contract extension with the Hawks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On November 8, 2016, he scored a then career-high 28 points in a 110–106 win over the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On November 30, he scored 14 of his career-high 31 points in the fourth quarter of the Hawks' 109–107 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He surpassed his career-high mark again on December 9, scoring 33 points in a 114–110 win over the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="German_national_team">German national team</span></h2>

<p>Schröder has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_basketball_team" title="Germany national basketball team">German national under-18</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_basketball_team" title="Germany national basketball team">German national under-20</a> teams. He played in the <a href="/wiki/2012_FIBA_Europe_Under-20_Championship" title="2012 FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship">2012 FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship</a> and helped the German team to fifth place, averaging 6.1 points, 2 assists and 1.8 rebounds in 14.6 minutes on the court. On 27 July 2014, he made his debut for the senior <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_basketball_team" title="Germany national basketball team">Germany national basketball team</a> in a game against <a href="/wiki/Finland_national_basketball_team" title="Finland national basketball team">Finland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Schröder is the son of a German father and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gambia" title="Gambia">Gambian</a> mother. He stated that he started focusing on his basketball career after his father died in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He and his brother were both heavily involved with <a href="/wiki/Skateboarding" title="Skateboarding">skateboarding</a> until Dennis found basketball at age 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> His agent is former German basketball national player <a href="/wiki/Ademola_Okulaja" title="Ademola Okulaja">Ademola Okulaja</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Schröder is <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
