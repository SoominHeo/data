<p><b>Denilson Martins Nascimento</b> (born 4 September 1976), simply known as <b>Denilson</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Bonsucesso_Futebol_Clube" title="Bonsucesso Futebol Clube">Bonsucesso Futebol Clube</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denilson_Martins_Nascimento&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Salvador,_Bahia" title="Salvador, Bahia">Salvador, Bahia</a>, Denilson moved abroad at the age of 20. He was, however, limited to <a href="/wiki/Reserve_team" title="Reserve team">reserve team</a> football with both <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain FC</a>, and signed with Portuguese club <a href="/wiki/C.F._Uni%C3%A3o_de_Lamas" title="C.F. União de Lamas">C.F. União de Lamas</a> in January 1998.</p>
<p>After six years in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>, mainly at the service of <a href="/wiki/Al_Shabab_(Dubai)" title="Al Shabab (Dubai)">Al Shabab (Dubai)</a>, and a brief stint in the <a href="/wiki/Liga_MX" title="Liga MX">Liga MX</a> with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atlas" title="Club Atlas">Club Atlas</a>, Denilson joined <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daejeon_Citizen_F.C." title="Daejeon Citizen F.C.">Daejeon Citizen F.C.</a> initially on a three-month contract, returning later for one 1/2 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He helped fellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-League" title="K-League">K-League</a> team <a href="/wiki/Pohang_Steelers" title="Pohang Steelers">Pohang Steelers</a> finish third at the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA Club World Cup">2009 FIFA Club World Cup</a>, scoring four goals in three games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 December 2009 Denilson signed for <a href="/wiki/FC_Bunyodkor" title="FC Bunyodkor">FC Bunyodkor</a> in Uzbekistan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> partnering <a href="/wiki/Rivaldo" title="Rivaldo">Rivaldo</a> at that and his following club, <a href="/wiki/Mogi_Mirim_Esporte_Clube" title="Mogi Mirim Esporte Clube">Mogi Mirim Esporte Clube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He continued playing well into his 30's, making his debut in the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_B" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série B">Série B</a> at the age of 35 with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guarani_Futebol_Clube" title="Guarani Futebol Clube">Guarani Futebol Clube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
