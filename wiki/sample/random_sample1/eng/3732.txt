<p><i><b>Corpse Princess</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">屍姫</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shikabane Hime</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by Yoshiichi Akahito. Premiering in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monthly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Gangan" title="Monthly Shōnen Gangan">Monthly Shōnen Gangan</a></i> on April 12, 2005, the series centers on the "Corpse Princess" Makina Hoshimura, an <a href="/wiki/Undead" title="Undead">undead</a> girl who is hunting down 108 undead corpses in order to gain entry into heaven with the help of a secret society of anti-corpse Buddhist monks.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Feel_(company)" title="Feel (company)">Feel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gainax" title="Gainax">Gainax</a> partnered together to adapt the series into a thirteen episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series. The first season, <i>Aka</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">赫</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <i>Red</i>)</span>, premiered on <a href="/wiki/AT-X_(company)" title="AT-X (company)">AT-X</a> on October 2, 2008. A second season, <i>Kuro</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">玄</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <i>Black</i>)</span>, aired between January and March 2009. The anime is licensed for North American distribution and release by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funimation_Entertainment" title="Funimation Entertainment">Funimation Entertainment</a>, which has already started releasing the episodes in subtitled form as <i>Shikabane Hime</i> through various online distribution companies and through its own website.</p>
<p>The series made its North American television debut on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FUNimation_Channel" title="FUNimation Channel">FUNimation Channel</a> on November 15, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corpse_Princess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After being murdered, Makina Hoshimura turns into a living "Shikabane Hime" ("Corpse Princess"). Armed with dual MAC-11 submachine guns, she must kill 108 other Shikabane in order to gain entry into heaven. She is assisted in this task by Keisei Tagami, a Buddhist monk with links to an anti-corpse group known as the Kōgon Sect. Her ultimate goal is to avenge the death of her family, by destroying the Shikabane known as the Seven Stars.</p>

<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Nana Akiyama (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Luci_Christian" title="Luci Christian">Luci Christian</a> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Makina is a main character and a contracted "living corpse". She is tasked with killing 108 corpses to gain entrance to heaven, which is later revealed to be a hoax. She has formidable physical skills and her weapon of choice are two <a href="/wiki/MAC-11" title="MAC-11">Ingram MAC-11</a> sub-machine pistols. Her Contracted Monk was Keisei Tagami. Later in the story, it is revealed that her family discovered the process of making a Shikabane Hime and was killed by the Seven Stars whom made Makina into a Shikabane. Later on, after Keisei's death, Ouri became her new Contracted Monk, although she refused to accept him at first.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Ouri_Kagami">Ouri Kagami</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">花神 旺里</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kagami Ōri</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Tatsuya Hasome (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Dismuke" title="Aaron Dismuke">Aaron Dismuke</a> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>A normal high school boy who was raised at Keisei Tagami's temple. He treats Keisei as his brother even though they are not blood related. Ouri is able to appear where the Shikabane are because of a mysterious black cat who began to talk to him. After the second time talking to the cat, Ouri knew that when he saw the cat he had to follow it, leading him to the corpses. He has also been nicknamed by the children at the temple "Hanagami". Later on after Keisei's death, he became Makina's new Contracted Monk. In the anime, it is strongly implied that he is in love with Makina.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Keisei_Tagami">Keisei Tagami</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">田神 景世</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tagami Keisei</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>

<dd>Keisei Tagami was a Buddhist priest under the anti-corpse organization known as the Kōugon Sect. He found Ouri Kagami as a three-year-old child and raised him at his temple orphanage. Keisei was an orphan himself, raised by Makina Hoshimura's father. When the Hoshimura family was brutally murdered, he agreed to become Makina's Contracted Monk. He was something of a pervert, often offering (or forcing upon) Ouri sexual posters or skin mags. He died near the end of the first season.</dd>
</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corpse_Princess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>Shikabane</dt>
<dd>Sometimes referred to as corpses. When a person dies with a powerful regret or obsession, they may become a Shikabane. As a Shikabane, they are undead but retain some degree of memory and intelligence. Their appearance may also differ greatly from that of a human. Shikabane are given superhuman strength and a unique ability or attribute called a curse. Shikabane have no feeling towards the living and tend to kill them as driven by their regret or obsession. A Shikabane can be killed by destroying or otherwise severing their brains.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Shikabane Hime</dt>
<dd>Shikabane Hime are Shikabane that follow the Kōgon Sect. So far only girls of a certain age group can become a Shikabane Hime. Each of them is partnered with a monk, who provides them with life energy called Rune. In exchange, the Shikabane Hime act as shields for their monk. They are used to hunt and kill rogue Shikabane. In fact, once they kill 108 Shikabane, they receive the reward of going to Heaven (this number may be from the number of sins in Buddhism). Shikabane Hime keep their human form but do still have their own curses that occasionally manifest, and unlike rogue Shikabane, Shikabane Hime are not driven by their regrets or obsessions to the same degree of madness. If their partner monk dies and a replacement is not forthcoming, however, the Shikabane Hime usually die or become Shikabane. Despite their contribution to the fight against the rogue Shikabane, they are shunned by most of the Kōgon Sect followers due to their nature as Shikabane.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Contracted Monk</dt>
<dd>Contracted Monks are the partner monks of the Shikabane Hime. They usually provide their Shikabane Hime with support and are linked by a bond.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Curse</dt>
<dd>A curse is a unique power that a Shikabane possess. Generally, it has something to do with their obsession or regret.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Investigator</dt>
<dd>A monk who acts as support for Contracted Monks. They usually do not have a contract of their own.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corpse_Princess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corpse_Princess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series premiered in Japan in the April 12, 2005 issue of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monthly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Gangan" title="Monthly Shōnen Gangan">Monthly Shōnen Gangan</a></i> where it is still on-going. Individual chapters are collected and published in <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes by <a href="/wiki/Square_Enix" title="Square Enix">Square Enix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComicList_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ComicList-2">[2]</a></sup> The first volume was released on August 22, 2005; as of November 2014, twenty-three volumes have been released. A limited edition version of volume 17 including a drama CD was released in April 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComicList_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ComicList-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corpse_Princess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Corpse_Princess_episodes" title="List of Corpse Princess episodes">List of Corpse Princess episodes</a></div>
<p>Produced by <a href="/wiki/Feel_(company)" title="Feel (company)">Feel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gainax" title="Gainax">Gainax</a>, <i>Shikabane Hime: Aka</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">屍姫 赫</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Corpse Princess: Red</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a thirteen episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation of the manga series. It premiered in Japan on <a href="/wiki/AT-X_(company)" title="AT-X (company)">AT-X</a> on October 2, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-anime-news_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-anime-news-3">[3]</a></sup> The episodes also air on <a href="/wiki/Nippon_BS_Broadcasting" title="Nippon BS Broadcasting">BS11</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Television_Broadcasting" title="Chiba Television Broadcasting">Chiba TV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Broadcasting_System" title="Kyoto Broadcasting System">KBS Kyoto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun_Television" title="Sun Television">Sun TV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Television" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Television">Tokyo MX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Television_Aichi_Broadcasting" title="Television Aichi Broadcasting">TV Aichi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Television_Kanagawa" title="Television Kanagawa">TV Kanagawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_Saitama" title="Television Saitama">TV Saitama</a>. The opening and ending themes, "Beautiful fighter" and "My story", had a single release from <a href="/wiki/Angela_(band)" title="Angela (band)">angela</a> on November 12, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series is licensed for North American distribution and release by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funimation_Entertainment" title="Funimation Entertainment">Funimation Entertainment</a> which is releasing the series as <i>Shikabane Hime</i>. On October 24, 2008, the thirteen episodes began airing online with English subtitles through Funimation's official YouTube, <a href="/wiki/Joost" title="Joost">Joost</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hulu.com" title="Hulu.com">Hulu.com</a> channels, with higher end downloadable versions released on the company's own website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Funimation noted that they hope this relatively quick release through online means will help prevent piracy. Traditionally, according to the president of Funimation Entertainment Gen Fukunaga, "by the time a licensing deal is signed to bring a series from Japan to the U.S. the episodes are already available as illegal downloads."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On January 18, 2011, British anime distributor <a href="/wiki/Manga_Entertainment" title="Manga Entertainment">Manga Entertainment</a> announced that they will release the first season <i>Aka</i>, on DVD in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>A second season, titled <i>Shikabane Hime: Kuro</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">屍姫 玄</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Corpse Princess: Black</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, aired in Japan between January and March 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Episode 26 of the series, or <i>Kuro</i> 13, was released as a DVD only episode in August 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
