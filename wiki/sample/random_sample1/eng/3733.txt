<p><b>Teradacho Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">寺田町駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Teradachō-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Loop_Line" title="Osaka Loop Line">Osaka Loop Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tenn%C5%8Dji-ku,_Osaka" title="Tennōji-ku, Osaka">Tennōji-ku, Osaka</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR West).</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teradach%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg/220px-Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg/330px-Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg/440px-Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Teradacho_Station_Old_Board.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The old station sign uncovered in August 2015</div>

<p>The station opened on 16 July 1932. Following the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR West.</p>
<p>In August 2015, an old station sign painted directly on the wall and dating from the 1930s or 1940s was uncovered during renovation work, and this was subsequently preserved under a clear plastic sheet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asahi20150904_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-asahi20150904-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20151113_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20151113-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
