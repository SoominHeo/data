<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Daisuke Terasawa</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Daisuke Terasawa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">寺沢大介</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Terasawa Daisuke</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 10 June 1959 in <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Mangaka" title="Mangaka">manga artist</a>. He is best known for writing <a href="/wiki/Cooking" title="Cooking">cooking</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Mister_Ajikko" title="Mister Ajikko">Mister Ajikko</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Shota_no_Sushi" title="Shota no Sushi">Shota no Sushi</a></i>, which earned him twice the <a href="/wiki/Kodansha_Manga_Award" title="Kodansha Manga Award">Kodansha Manga Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen" title="Shōnen">shōnen</a> in 1988 and 1996 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Both series have been adapted as dramas, the latter as a live-action television series, the former as the first cooking <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
