<p>The <b>Derbi barceloní</b> (<small>Catalan pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Catalan" title="Help:IPA for Catalan">[ˈdɛrβi βərsəɫuˈni]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Derbi barcelonés</i></span>, <small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈderβi βarθeloˈnes]</a></span>; "Barcelonian Derby"), is the name given to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> matches between <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">RCD Espanyol</a>, both of them from <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Barcelona" title="Province of Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Focus_carries_Barcelona_to_derby_win_at_Espanyol_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Focus_carries_Barcelona_to_derby_win_at_Espanyol-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_history">Background and history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derbi_barcelon%C3%AD&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">Espanyol</a>, being one of the clubs granted royal patronage, was founded exclusively by Spanish football fans, unlike the multinational nature of Barça's primary board. The founding message of the club was clearly anti-Barcelona, and they disapprovingly saw FC Barcelona as a team of foreigners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ball.2C_Phil._pp._86-87_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ball.2C_Phil._pp._86-87-2">[2]</a></sup> The rivalry was strengthened by what Catalonians saw as a provocative representative of Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Their original ground was in the affluent district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estadi_de_Sarri%C3%A0" title="Estadi de Sarrià">Sarrià</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the dictatorships of <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Primo_de_Rivera" title="Miguel Primo de Rivera">Miguel Primo de Rivera</a> (1923–1930) and <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a> (1939–1975), Espanyol was seen by the vast majority of Barcelona's citizens as a club which cultivated a kind of compliance to the central authority, in stark contrast to Barça's revolutionary spirit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Also in the 1960s and '70s, while FC Barcelona acted as a social integrator for immigrants from poorer regions of Spain in search for a better life, Espanyol drew their support mainly from sectors close to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franco_regime" title="Franco regime">regime</a> such as policemen, military officers, civil servants and career fascists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1918, Espanyol started a counter-petition against autonomy, which at that time had become a pertinent issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ball.2C_Phil._pp._86-87_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ball.2C_Phil._pp._86-87-2">[2]</a></sup> Later on, an Espanyol supporter group would join the Falangists in the Spanish Civil War, siding with the fascists. Despite these differences in ideology, the <i>derbi</i> has always been more relevant to Espanyol supporters than Barcelona ones due to the difference in objectives. In recent years the rivalry has become less political, as Espanyol translated its official name and anthem from Spanish to Catalan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ball.2C_Phil._pp._86-87_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ball.2C_Phil._pp._86-87-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Though it is the most played local derby in the history of La Liga, it is also the most unbalanced, with Barcelona overwhelmingly <a href="#Head_to_head">dominant</a>. In the league table, Espanyol has only managed to finish above Barça on three occasions in almost 70 years and the only all-Catalan Copa del Rey final was won by Barça in 1957. Espanyol has the consolation of achieving the largest margin win with a 6–0 in 1951. Espanyol achieved a 2–1 win against Barça during the 2008–09 season, becoming the first team to defeat Barcelona at Camp Nou in their treble-winning <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_FC_Barcelona_season" title="2008–09 FC Barcelona season">season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Barça's <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Messi" title="Lionel Messi">Lionel Messi</a> has scored 16 goals against Espanyol, the most in the history of the derby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Focus_carries_Barcelona_to_derby_win_at_Espanyol_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Focus_carries_Barcelona_to_derby_win_at_Espanyol-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
