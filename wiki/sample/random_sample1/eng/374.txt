<p>The <b>2006 South American Women's Football Championship</b> (Campeonato Sudamericano de Fútbol Femenino 2006) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> tournament was played between November 10 and November 26, 2006 at <a href="/wiki/Mar_del_Plata" title="Mar del Plata">Mar del Plata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-conmebol_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-conmebol-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The winner and runner-up from this <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_American_Women%27s_Football_Championship" title="South American Women's Football Championship">South American Women's Football Championship</a>'s third edition qualify to the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA Women's World Cup">2007 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>. The winner also qualifies for the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament">2008 Olympics football tournament</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_round">Final round</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_South_American_Women%27s_Football_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Final round">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Argentina and Brazil qualified to the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA Women's World Cup">2007 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>. Argentina and Brazil also qualified to the 2008 Olympics, although second placed Brazil had to play an <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_qualification#Inter-continental_play-off" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's qualification">Inter-continental play-off</a>, which they won against Ghana eventually.</p>
