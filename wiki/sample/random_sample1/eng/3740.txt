<p><b>Demikhovo Machinebuilding Plant</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMZhistory_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMZhistory-1">[1]</a></sup> or <b>Demikhovsky Engineering Plant</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Демиховский машиностроительный завод</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Russian" title="Romanization of Russian">translit.</a> </small><i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Russian transliteration">Demikhovsky mashinostroitelny zavod</span></i>, abbreviated <b>DMZ</b>) is a Russian <a href="/wiki/Open_joint-stock_company" title="Open joint-stock company">open joint-stock company</a> that makes <a href="/wiki/Train" title="Train">trains</a>. It has been awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Red_Banner_of_Labour" title="Order of the Red Banner of Labour">Order of the Red Banner of Labour</a>.</p>
<p>The company became part of <a href="/wiki/Transmashholding" title="Transmashholding">Transmashholding</a> in accordance with the Decree of the Governor of Moscow Region Boris Gromov of 20 July 2001, submitted to the departmental submission to the Committee on Transport, Moscow region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Since its inception in May 1935, the profile of Demikhov changed several times. There were activities in chemical engineering and the peat industry. But since 1992, the main products manufactured are <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple unit</a> trains on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3000_V_DC" title="3000 V DC">3000 V DC</a> and <a href="/wiki/25_kV_AC_railway_electrification" title="25 kV AC railway electrification">25 kV VAC</a> at 50 Hz.</p>
<p>During its life, Demikhov has produced seventeen different types of trains and produced over 3,000 examples, operated in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMZhistory_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-DMZhistory-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006 Demikhovsky produced and sold 571 trains of types <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ED4M&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ED4M (page does not exist)">ED4M</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ED9M&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ED9M (page does not exist)">ED9M</a>, and <a href="#ED4_ED4MK">ED4MK</a>). Various spare parts sold for nearly $40 million. In 2006 the company presented its new development, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ED4MKM&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ED4MKM (page does not exist)">ED4MKM</a>.</p>
<p>In 2007 Demikhovsky produced and sold 630 electric trains of types ED4M, ED9M, and ED9MK.</p>
<p>In 2008 the electric <a href="/wiki/Intermodal_passenger_transport" title="Intermodal passenger transport">intermodal passenger transport</a> train ED4MKM-AERO was certified.</p>
