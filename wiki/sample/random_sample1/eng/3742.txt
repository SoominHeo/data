<p><b>Devin Star Tailes</b> (born July 2, 1989), better known as <b>Dev</b> (sometimes stylized as <b>DEV</b> or <b>dEV</b>), is an American singer, songwriter, rapper, model and radio host. She was discovered by the record label, Indie-Pop, and <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a> via Myspace after her friend Shane Crislip, posted her singing to some beats. Dev's song "<a href="/wiki/Booty_Bounce" title="Booty Bounce">Booty Bounce</a>" was sampled in <a href="/wiki/Far_East_Movement" title="Far East Movement">Far East Movement</a>'s hit single "<a href="/wiki/Like_a_G6" title="Like a G6">Like a G6</a>" which reached number 1 in the US, and sold over four million downloads in the country.</p>
<p>Dev was signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Republic" title="Universal Republic">Universal Republic</a> in October 2010, and released her debut single "<a href="/wiki/Bass_Down_Low" title="Bass Down Low">Bass Down Low</a>" in November 16, 2010 which reached the top 10 in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. Her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_the_Sun_Came_Up" title="The Night the Sun Came Up">The Night the Sun Came Up</a></i> was released on March 27, 2012. The second single, "<a href="/wiki/In_the_Dark_(Dev_song)" title="In the Dark (Dev song)">In the Dark</a>", peaked at number 11 in the US chart, making it her most successful solo single to date. Dev in 2014 released two <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EPs</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bittersweet_July" title="Bittersweet July">Bittersweet July</a></i> and the second part as a follow up to part one. She's a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_host" title="Radio host">radio host</a> of the popular radio <a href="/wiki/Beats_1" title="Beats 1">Beats 1</a> by Apple, founded in 2015.</p>
<p>In mid 2016, Dev started promoting her album with an opening single called <i>#1</i> featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nef_The_Pharaoh" title="Nef The Pharaoh">Nef The Pharaoh</a> which reached the top 15 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhythmic" title="Rhythmic">Rhythmic</a> songs chart.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dev_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dev was born to Lisa, a real estate agent, and Riki Tailes, a painting contractor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Record-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bulletin-2">[2]</a></sup> She has two younger sisters, Sierra Sol (born September 13, 1994) and Maezee Luna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Record-1">[1]</a></sup> Dev is of Portuguese and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_people" title="Mexican people">Mexican</a> descent. She grew up in the city of <a href="/wiki/Manteca,_California" title="Manteca, California">Manteca</a>. At the age of four, she started swimming and was part of the U.S Olympic development program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Record-1">[1]</a></sup> She attended Brock Elliott Elementary and graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sierra_High_School,_Manteca,_California" title="Sierra High School, Manteca, California">Sierra High School</a> in 2007, where she was a member of band and choir. She attended <a href="/wiki/San_Joaquin_Delta_College" title="San Joaquin Delta College">San Joaquin Delta College</a>, studying English and art history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Record-1">[1]</a></sup> She went to her senior formal with Ryan Fisher, who is currently a professional baseball player for the Miami Marlins.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dev_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In 2009 she made a cover of <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>' "Blonde Trick" and, after a friend posted the song into MySpace, the production team <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a> noticed it and contacted Dev.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Record-1">[1]</a></sup> To Fashion Indie Magazine she said: "I decided to put up this song on MySpace. [...] it was basically a "diss track" and I didn't expect anything out of it. It was just to make me feel good like how music usually do but Niles [of the Cataracs] came across to my page and was really into my voice and they kind of got me into singing and opened up my eyes to business opportunities".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fashion_Indie_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fashion_Indie-3">[3]</a></sup> She dropped out of college in her first year to pursue her singing career and was then signed by the Los Angeles-based record label and management team, Indie-Pop, who also discovered The Cataracs. Six months later, Dev and The Cataracs' song "2Nite" began to gain exposure on the radio, the television channel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MtvU" title="MtvU">MtvU</a> and the <i>Billboard</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Airplay" title="Hot Dance Airplay">Hot Dance Airplay</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Record-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2009, Dev moved to Los Angeles to produce music with the Cataracs and work directly with Indie-Pop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bulletin-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, the Cataracs produced "<a href="/wiki/Like_a_G6" title="Like a G6">Like a G6</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Far_East_Movement" title="Far East Movement">Far East Movement</a> and decided to use a verse from Dev's single "<a href="/wiki/Booty_Bounce" title="Booty Bounce">Booty Bounce</a>" as the chorus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-4">[4]</a></sup> The two songs were made in the same summer, at the time where Dev and the Cataracs "were grinding it out in the studio pretty tough together," making a couple of "simplistic random tracks."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fashion_Indie_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fashion_Indie-3">[3]</a></sup> "Like a G6", which features Far East Movement and Dev, was released in April 2010, and reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">US <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LikeaG6_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-LikeaG6-5">[5]</a></sup> The song went on to sell over four million downloads in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In August 2010, a music video was made for "<a href="/wiki/Booty_Bounce" title="Booty Bounce">Booty Bounce</a>" directed by Ethan Lader, and uploaded it onto YouTube, where it has generated over twenty million views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood-7">[7]</a></sup> In October 2010, Dev was signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Republic" title="Universal Republic">Universal Republic</a> and her first official single, "<a href="/wiki/Bass_Down_Low" title="Bass Down Low">Bass Down Low</a>", was serviced to rhythmic radio stations in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It was released digitally in select territories on December 6, 2010 and one day later in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The song reached sixty one on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 chart and number two on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Songs" title="Heatseekers Songs">Heatseekers Songs</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BassDownLow_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-BassDownLow-11">[11]</a></sup> In Canada, the song peaked at number thirty five in its fifth week on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> chart. However, "Bass Down Low" saw massive success in the United Kingdom, where it peaked at number ten on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9313:_The_Night_the_Sun_Came_Up">2011–13: <i>The Night the Sun Came Up</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dev_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–13: The Night the Sun Came Up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 25, 2011, she released the single "<a href="/wiki/In_the_Dark_(Dev_song)" title="In the Dark (Dev song)">In the Dark</a>". The song enjoyed commercial success in the United States, peaking at number 11 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart and the summit of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Hot Dance Club Songs</a> and peaked in the top forty in Canada, Australia, Denmark, Ireland, Scotland and the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The song's music video features shots of black-painted hands that touch Dev while she is standing naked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Dev's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_the_Sun_Came_Up" title="The Night the Sun Came Up">The Night the Sun Came Up</a></i> has been released in United Kingdom on September 20, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fashion_Indie_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Fashion_Indie-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Since 2009, Dev has been working on her debut album with main producers, The Cataracs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Record-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bulletin-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itsthecataracs.com_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-itsthecataracs.com-16">[16]</a></sup> In March 2011, Dev and The Cataracs traveled to Costa Rica for three weeks where they recorded and produced most of the material for the new album. She said of the album's genres, "typical pop music is not what I am only good at doing; I have a lot of ideas and hip-hop influence records, some electro ballads."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fashion_Indie_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Fashion_Indie-3">[3]</a></sup> In March 2011, <a href="/wiki/New_Boyz" title="New Boyz">New Boyz</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Backseat_(song)" title="Backseat (song)">Backseat</a>", featuring Dev and The Cataracs, was released. The song peaked at number twenty-six on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Backseat_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Backseat-17">[17]</a></sup> She was featured on <a href="/wiki/Demi_Lovato" title="Demi Lovato">Demi Lovato</a>'s album "<a href="/wiki/Unbroken_(Demi_Lovato_album)" title="Unbroken (Demi Lovato album)">Unbroken</a>" on a song called "Who's That Boy". She featured on a song by British boy band <a href="/wiki/JLS" title="JLS">JLS</a>, "<a href="/wiki/She_Makes_Me_Wanna" title="She Makes Me Wanna">She Makes Me Wanna</a>", which peaked at the top of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> in the United Kingdom.</p>

<p>Dev also released two more promotional singles through Twitter, "Poison" and "Call Me" both covers of Bell Biv Devoe and Blondie respectively. She also featured in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a> artist <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a>'s album, <i>Nothing But the Beat</i> on a song called "I Just Wanna F" with <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afrojack" title="Afrojack">Afrojack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Dev has also featured on a single by Swedish singer <a href="/wiki/Eric_Saade" title="Eric Saade">Eric Saade</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Hotter_Than_Fire" title="Hotter Than Fire">Hotter Than Fire</a>" which was released on November 2, 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. Dev is featured on a remix of <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>'s song "Bad Girl" by <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a>, featured on their Japanese remix album and Dev was also featured in Timbaland's promotional single "Break Ya Back". The American version of <i>The Night the Sun Came Up</i> was released on March 26, 2012 in the United States, which include new tracks compared to previous releases of the album. Dev has teamed up with singer <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique Iglesias</a> on a track called "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naked_(Dev_song)" title="Naked (Dev song)">Naked</a>" which is produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a> and was released as the third single of the repackaged album. It reached the first position in Mexico and the second position in the US dance chart.</p>
<p>At the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a>, Dev revealed that she was currently working on her second album; "I'm using a lot more live instrumentation for this album", stating "I had such a great time making a very electronic-based album for the first one, but you know, I'm just moving to different things, different sounds, so we'll see."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Her track "<a href="/wiki/Bass_Down_Low" title="Bass Down Low">Bass Down Low</a>" was featured during match intervals of Volleyball, Beach Volleyball and Basketball among others during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>. In 2013 Universal Republic Records announced that they were going defunct, all artists from the label moved to <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In November 1, she has posted on her official YouTube a new song, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiss_it_(dev_single)" title="Kiss it (dev single)">Kiss It</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Sage_the_Gemini" title="Sage the Gemini">Sage the Gemini</a> produced by <a href="/wiki/Hit-Boy" title="Hit-Boy">Hit-Boy</a>. While "Kiss It" will release on December 17, 2013. A remix version was released featured <a href="/wiki/Casey_Veggies" title="Casey Veggies">Casey Veggies</a> in place of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sage_The_Gemini" title="Sage The Gemini">Sage The Gemini</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2013, Dev also collaborated with singer <a href="/wiki/Mia_Martina" title="Mia Martina">Mia Martina</a> for her single <i>Danse</i> which gained popularity in Latin and dance charts throughout spring 2014.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Bittersweet_July_and_second_album">2014–present: <i>Bittersweet July</i> and second album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dev_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Bittersweet July and second album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the middle of 2014, after the release of her previously single "Kiss It", Dev left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Republic" title="Universal Republic">Universal Republic</a> and decided to release music on her own, without a label. On May 5, 2014 Dev released an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> called <i>Not All Love Songs Have To Be So Sad</i> with NanosauR. The EP was released via iTunes on July 31, 2014. On August 7, 2014, Dev has posted a snippet of a new song on her <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> account called "Kids" and revealed that <i><a href="/wiki/Bittersweet_July" title="Bittersweet July">Bittersweet July</a></i> will be available for pre-order on August 11. It is set to be released September 23 by Rica Lyfe Records, her own label. On November 5, 2014 "<a href="/wiki/Honey_Dip_(Dev_song)" title="Honey Dip (Dev song)">Honey Dip</a>" was confirmed as the first single from the EP, the video was directed by the Californian rapper <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn</a>. On December 6, 2014 Dev announced the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Bittersweet_July" title="Bittersweet July">Bittersweet July, Pt. 2</a></i> as a follow up to Part 1 and made it available for pre-order along with the download of "The Night is Young". The EP was released via iTunes on December 15. The two EPs will subsequently put together in one album but it will not be considered as a real album. The third track from the second part, "<a href="/wiki/Parade_(Dev_song)" title="Parade (Dev song)">Parade</a>", was released as the lead single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015 Dev collaborated with female electro duo <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NERVO_(duo)" title="NERVO (duo)">NERVO</a> for their single "Hey Ricky" which was scheduled to be released with an attached video in April 2015. The single will feature also <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn</a> who directed the video too. A snippet of the video leaked on March 23, 2015. The video, released on VEVO on June 2, 2015 went viral in a few days making the song topping the real-time US chart. In December 2015 Dev was also featured as the main vocalist on the club hit "Electric Walk" by electro-house producer Nytrix. The song peaked at eight on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Dance_Chart" title="Billboard Dance Chart">Billboard Dance Chart</a>.</p>
<p>On January 25, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Idolator_(website)" title="Idolator (website)">Idolator</a> posted a snippet of a song called "Lowkey" and wrote that on January 27, <a href="/wiki/Ebro_Darden" title="Ebro Darden">Ebro Darden</a> will premiere Dev's first single on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beats1" title="Beats1">Beats1</a> from her upcoming second album set to be released the same year. Dev worked mainly with her friend-producer NanosauR, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Tozer" title="Dave Tozer">Dave Tozer</a> and Rey Reel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> "Lowkey" was released on March 8, 2016. The song was prasied by the critic and compared to <a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez" title="Selena Gomez">Selena Gomez</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Good_For_You_(song)" title="Good For You (song)">Good For You</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2016 Dev was featured as the main vocalist on "We Rock It" by <a href="/wiki/Sander_Kleinenberg" title="Sander Kleinenberg">Sander Kleinenberg</a>. The song reached the first position on the <a href="/wiki/Beatport" title="Beatport">Beatport</a> House chart.</p>
<p>In August 2016 Dev signed with <a href="/wiki/Barry_Weiss" title="Barry Weiss">Barry Weiss</a> record label called RECORDS and started promoting her album with an opening single called <i>#1</i> featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nef_The_Pharaoh" title="Nef The Pharaoh">Nef The Pharaoh</a> which reached the top 15 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhythmic" title="Rhythmic">Rhythmic</a> songs chart.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dev_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dev's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electro_music" title="Electro music">electro</a> style and "sing-talk" vocal style have been compared to fellow pop singer <a href="/wiki/Kesha" title="Kesha">Kesha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Idolator_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Idolator-26">[26]</a></sup> Dev responded to this, saying, "I'm confident enough in what I do that people will see me for who I am ... I get why people compare us but once people see me live they won't connect us as much."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood-7">[7]</a></sup> While comparing her to Kesha, <i><a href="/wiki/Idolator_(website)" title="Idolator (website)">Idolator</a></i> wrote that "Dev's beats come off a bit more mellow and her songs a little bit more electro than pop."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Idolator_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Idolator-26">[26]</a></sup> In response to being compared to both Kesha and <a href="/wiki/Uffie" title="Uffie">Uffie</a>, Dev said, "I just feel that I have a lot more to say and the ability to have an eclectic sound, I'm not bashing either artist, but I'm going to be able to give you a little bit more than what you've heard on those records. When my album comes out, hopefully I won't have to hear another reference or comparison."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fashion_Indie_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Fashion_Indie-3">[3]</a></sup> Much of Dev's music is inspired by rap music, as she believes that fans respond to "pop music but with rap roots". She said, "I've always been into hip-hop. <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> changed my life when I was younger."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood-7">[7]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> compared her "futuristic" and "pulsing" sound to underground <a href="/wiki/Electroclash" title="Electroclash">electroclash</a> musicians <a href="/wiki/Peaches_(musician)" title="Peaches (musician)">Peaches</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yelle" title="Yelle">Yelle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood-7">[7]</a></sup> Dev's musical genres have been cited as <a href="/wiki/Crunk" title="Crunk">electro-crunk</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Electropop" title="Electropop">electropop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Idolator_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-Idolator-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dev_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2011, Dev publicly announced that she and her fiancé Jimmy Gorecki were expecting a baby girl due December 24, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Dev gave birth to her daughter Emilia Lovely on December 9, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Dev aired an MTV Special about her life as a new mom and how she is juggling being a mother and a performer at the same time. The show also shed light on her daughter (Emilia Lovely Gorecki), who was born with a condition called <a href="/wiki/Gastroschisis" title="Gastroschisis">gastroschisis</a>, and how her daughter overcame it and grew stronger every day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ppdirt_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-ppdirt-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is also a supporter of <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-psdev_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-psdev-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
