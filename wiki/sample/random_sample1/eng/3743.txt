<p><b>Debra Lynn Winger</b> (born May 16, 1955) is an American actress. She has been nominated three times for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Actress">Academy Award for Best Actress</a>; for <i><a href="/wiki/An_Officer_and_a_Gentleman" title="An Officer and a Gentleman">An Officer and a Gentleman</a></i> (1982), <i><a href="/wiki/Terms_of_Endearment" title="Terms of Endearment">Terms of Endearment</a></i> (1983), and <i><a href="/wiki/Shadowlands_(1993_film)" title="Shadowlands (1993 film)">Shadowlands</a></i> (1993). She won the <a href="/wiki/National_Society_of_Film_Critics_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="National Society of Film Critics Award for Best Actress">National Society of Film Critics Award for Best Actress</a> for <i>Terms of Endearment</i>, and the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_International_Film_Festival" title="Tokyo International Film Festival">Tokyo International Film Festival Award for Best Actress</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/A_Dangerous_Woman_(1993_film)" title="A Dangerous Woman (1993 film)">A Dangerous Woman</a></i> (1993). Her other film roles include <i><a href="/wiki/Urban_Cowboy" title="Urban Cowboy">Urban Cowboy</a></i> (1980), <i><a href="/wiki/Legal_Eagles" title="Legal Eagles">Legal Eagles</a></i> (1986), <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Widow_(1987_film)" title="Black Widow (1987 film)">Black Widow</a></i> (1987), <i><a href="/wiki/Betrayed_(1988_film)" title="Betrayed (1988 film)">Betrayed</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Forget_Paris" title="Forget Paris">Forget Paris</a></i> (1995), and <i><a href="/wiki/Rachel_Getting_Married" title="Rachel Getting Married">Rachel Getting Married</a></i> (2008). In 2012, she made her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadway_(theatre)" title="Broadway (theatre)">Broadway</a> debut in the original production of the <a href="/wiki/David_Mamet" title="David Mamet">David Mamet</a> play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Anarchist_(play)" title="The Anarchist (play)">The Anarchist</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Winger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Debra Lynn Winger was born in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Heights,_Ohio" title="Cleveland Heights, Ohio">Cleveland Heights, Ohio</a>, into an <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox Jewish</a> family, to Robert Winger, a meat packer, and Ruth (née Felder), an office manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Over the years, she told many interviewers that she <a href="/wiki/Kibbutz_volunteer" title="Kibbutz volunteer">volunteered on an Israeli kibbutz</a>, sometimes even saying she had trained with the <a href="/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces" title="Israel Defense Forces">Israel Defense Forces</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but in a 2008 interview she said she was merely on a typical youth tour that visited the kibbutz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After returning to the United States, she was involved in a car accident and suffered a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cerebral_hemorrhage" title="Cerebral hemorrhage">cerebral hemorrhage</a>; as a result, she was left partially paralyzed and blind for ten months, having initially been told that she would never see again. With time on her hands to think about her life, she decided that, if she recovered, she would move to California and become an actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Winger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Winger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winger's first acting role was as "Debbie" in the 1976 <a href="/wiki/Sexploitation_film" title="Sexploitation film">sexploitation film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Slumber_Party_%2757" title="Slumber Party '57">Slumber Party '57</a></i>. Her next role was as Diana Prince's younger sister Drusilla (<a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girl" title="Wonder Girl">Wonder Girl</a>) in three episodes of <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s TV series, <i><a href="/wiki/Wonder_Woman_(TV_series)" title="Wonder Woman (TV series)">Wonder Woman</a></i>. The producers had wanted her to appear more often, but she refused, fearing that the role would hurt her fledgling career. This was followed by a guest role in Season 4 of the TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Police_Woman_(TV_series)" title="Police Woman (TV series)">Police Woman</a></i> in 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her first starring role was in <i><a href="/wiki/Thank_God_It%27s_Friday_(film)" title="Thank God It's Friday (film)">Thank God It's Friday</a>,</i> followed by her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Urban_Cowboy" title="Urban Cowboy">Urban Cowboy</a></i> in 1980 with <a href="/wiki/John_Travolta" title="John Travolta">John Travolta</a>, for which she received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> nomination and a pair of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> nominations (for Best Performance by an Actress and Best New Star). In 1982 she co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Nolte" title="Nick Nolte">Nick Nolte</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Cannery_Row_(film)" title="Cannery Row (film)">Cannery Row</a></i> and with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Gere" title="Richard Gere">Richard Gere</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/An_Officer_and_a_Gentleman" title="An Officer and a Gentleman">An Officer and a Gentleman</a></i>, for which she was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Actress">Academy Award for Best Actress</a>. She was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Actress">Academy Award for Best Actress</a> twice more: for <i><a href="/wiki/Terms_of_Endearment" title="Terms of Endearment">Terms of Endearment</a></i> in 1983 (which was awarded to her co-star, <a href="/wiki/Shirley_MacLaine" title="Shirley MacLaine">Shirley MacLaine</a>, who played her mother in the film) and for <i><a href="/wiki/Shadowlands_(1993_film)" title="Shadowlands (1993 film)">Shadowlands</a></i> in 1993, for which she also received her second BAFTA nomination. Her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Dangerous_Woman_(1993_film)" title="A Dangerous Woman (1993 film)">A Dangerous Woman</a></i> earned a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> nomination for Best Actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ART-bio_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ART-bio-9">[9]</a></sup> Her voice was used (along with others) for the main character of the 1982 <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/E.T._the_Extra-Terrestrial" title="E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial">E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial</a>.</i></p>
<p>Over the years Winger acquired a reputation for being outspoken and sometimes difficult to work with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-12">[12]</a></sup> She has expressed her dislike of <i><a href="/wiki/An_Officer_and_a_Gentleman" title="An Officer and a Gentleman">An Officer and a Gentleman</a></i> (and her costar <a href="/wiki/Richard_Gere" title="Richard Gere">Richard Gere</a>), for which she refused to do any publicity,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-12">[12]</a></sup> and several other of her films, and has been dismissive of some of her co-stars and directors. Commenting on her past attitudes, Winger said in 2009, "Most bad behavior comes from insecurity. Even though I loved what I was doing, I didn't always know I could pull it off. (...) I took [my insecurities out] on everybody. But in my defense, I never fought about the size of my trailer or things like that; it was always about the work".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Walters" title="Barbara Walters">Barbara Walters</a> interviewed <a href="/wiki/Bette_Davis" title="Bette Davis">Bette Davis</a> in 1986, Davis said "I see a great deal of myself in Debra Winger."</p>
<p>Winger was going to play Peggy Sue in <i><a href="/wiki/Peggy_Sue_Got_Married" title="Peggy Sue Got Married">Peggy Sue Got Married</a></i> but was forced to back out just before production began when she injured her back in a bicycle accident. The injury affected her ability to work for several months afterward. She was cast in the lead role in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_League_of_their_Own" title="A League of their Own">A League of their Own</a></i> but dropped out and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Geena_Davis" title="Geena Davis">Geena Davis</a>. It was later reported that the main reason Winger chose to leave the film was her refusal to work with singer/actress <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Other starring roles during this period included <i><a href="/wiki/Legal_Eagles" title="Legal Eagles">Legal Eagles</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Made_in_Heaven_(1987_film)" title="Made in Heaven (1987 film)">Made in Heaven</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_Wins_(film)" title="Everybody Wins (film)">Everybody Wins</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sheltering_Sky_(film)" title="The Sheltering Sky (film)">The Sheltering Sky</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Leap_of_Faith_(film)" title="Leap of Faith (film)">Leap of Faith</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Widow_(1987_movie)" title="Black Widow (1987 movie)">Black Widow</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Betrayed_(1988_film)" title="Betrayed (1988 film)">Betrayed</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wilder_Napalm" title="Wilder Napalm">Wilder Napalm</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Dangerous_Woman_(1993_film)" title="A Dangerous Woman (1993 film)">A Dangerous Woman</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1995 Winger decided to take a hiatus from acting. In 2002 she said, "I wanted out for years. I got sick of hearing myself say I wanted to quit. It's like opening an interview with 'I hate interviews!' Well, get out! I stopped reading scripts and stopped caring. People said, 'We miss you so much.' But in the last six years, tell me a film that I should have been in. The few I can think of, the actress was so perfect".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> After making <i><a href="/wiki/Forget_Paris" title="Forget Paris">Forget Paris</a></i> in 1995 she was absent from the screen for six years before returning in 2001 with <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Bad_Love" title="Big Bad Love">Big Bad Love</a></i>, written and directed by her husband, Arliss Howard, and also marking Winger's debut as a producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>During her film hiatus, Winger had the female lead in the <a href="/wiki/American_Repertory_Theater" title="American Repertory Theater">American Repertory Theater</a>'s production of <a href="/wiki/Anton_Chekhov" title="Anton Chekhov">Anton Chekhov</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Ivanov_(play)" title="Ivanov (play)">Ivanov</a></i> from November 1999 to January 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ART-Ivanov_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ART-Ivanov-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001 a critically acclaimed documentary film titled <i><a href="/wiki/Searching_for_Debra_Winger" title="Searching for Debra Winger">Searching for Debra Winger</a></i> was made by <a href="/wiki/Rosanna_Arquette" title="Rosanna Arquette">Rosanna Arquette</a> and released in 2002 after Winger returned to film acting. She subsequently starred in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_(2003_film)" title="Radio (2003 film)">Radio</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Eulogy_(film)" title="Eulogy (film)">Eulogy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sometimes_in_April" title="Sometimes in April">Sometimes in April</a></i> and received positive reviews for portraying <a href="/wiki/Anne_Hathaway" title="Anne Hathaway">Anne Hathaway</a>'s estranged mother in <i><a href="/wiki/Rachel_Getting_Married" title="Rachel Getting Married">Rachel Getting Married</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>She earned an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nomination for her title role in the television film <i><a href="/wiki/Dawn_Anna" title="Dawn Anna">Dawn Anna</a></i> in 2005, directed by Arliss Howard. In 2010 she returned to television, making a guest appearance as a high school principal in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> She also joined the cast of HBO's <i><a href="/wiki/In_Treatment_(U.S._TV_series)" title="In Treatment (U.S. TV series)">In Treatment</a></i> as one of the three patients featured in the third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her latest acting project is a starring role in the upcoming film <i>In the Woods</i>, the first installment of <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Elster" title="Jennifer Elster">Jennifer Elster</a>'s multimedia, experimental film series <i>The Being Experience</i>, planned for multi-platform release in 2013 and 2014, also including: <a href="/wiki/Terrence_Howard" title="Terrence Howard">Terrence Howard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Matthews" title="Dave Matthews">Dave Matthews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Wainwright" title="Rufus Wainwright">Rufus Wainwright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karen_Black" title="Karen Black">Karen Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Will_Shortz" title="Will Shortz">Will Shortz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liya_Kebede" title="Liya Kebede">Liya Kebede</a>, <a href="/wiki/Questlove" title="Questlove">Questlove</a>, <a href="/wiki/Famke_Janssen" title="Famke Janssen">Famke Janssen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moby" title="Moby">Moby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gale_Harold" title="Gale Harold">Gale Harold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paz_de_la_Huerta" title="Paz de la Huerta">Paz de la Huerta</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jorgen_Leth" title="Jorgen Leth">Jorgen Leth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosie_Perez" title="Rosie Perez">Rosie Perez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aubrey_de_Grey" title="Aubrey de Grey">Aubrey de Grey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Cumming" title="Alan Cumming">Alan Cumming</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> She also stars opposite <a href="/wiki/Sam_Elliott" title="Sam Elliott">Sam Elliott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Kutcher" title="Ashton Kutcher">Ashton Kutcher</a> in the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> multi-cam comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ranch_(TV_series)" title="The Ranch (TV series)">The Ranch</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_pursuits">Other pursuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Winger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other pursuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995 Winger performed in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_of_Oz_in_Concert:_Dreams_Come_True" title="The Wizard of Oz in Concert: Dreams Come True">The Wizard of Oz in Concert: Dreams Come True</a></i> a television musical performance of the popular 1939 MGM film at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_Center" title="Lincoln Center">Lincoln Center</a> to benefit the <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Defense_Fund" title="Children's Defense Fund">Children's Defense Fund</a>. Her roles in that television special were the "Cyclone" narrator and the <a href="/wiki/Wicked_Witch_of_the_West" title="Wicked Witch of the West">Wicked Witch of the West</a>. It was originally broadcast on both <a href="/wiki/TBS_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="TBS (U.S. TV channel)">TBS</a> and <a href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_channel)" title="TNT (TV channel)">TNT</a>.</p>
<p>During her hiatus from the film industry, Winger spent a semester as a <a href="/wiki/Teaching_assistant" title="Teaching assistant">teaching fellow</a> at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2008 Winger wrote a book based on her personal recollections titled <i>Undiscovered</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> She has shown her support for reconciliation between Arabs and Jews in Israel by visiting the bilingual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hand_in_Hand:_Center_for_Jewish_Arab_Education_in_Israel" title="Hand in Hand: Center for Jewish Arab Education in Israel">Hand in Hand</a> schools (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galil_Jewish-Arab_School" title="Galil Jewish-Arab School">Galilee Jewish-Arab School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hand_in_Hand_%E2%80%93_Bridge_over_the_Wadi" title="Hand in Hand – Bridge over the Wadi">Gesher al HaWadi School</a>) where, in 2008, she stated she would "dedicate the next bit of my life to these schools".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>As 2009 president of the <a href="/wiki/Zurich_Film_Festival" title="Zurich Film Festival">Zurich Film Festival</a> jury, Winger joined other members of the Hollywood film community to speak out against the arrest and prosecution of director <a href="/wiki/Roman_Polanski" title="Roman Polanski">Roman Polanski</a> who was accused of raping a 13-year-old girl in the 1970s, criticizing Switzerland's government for "philistine collusion" in arresting him so many years later, as he was en route to attend the Zurich festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010 Debra Winger was co-executive producer of the Academy Award-nominated documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/Gasland" title="Gasland">Gasland</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> She was also the executive producer of the 2012 documentary <i>Bel Borba Aqui</i> about the life and works of Brazilian graphic artist <a href="/wiki/Bel_Borba" title="Bel Borba">Bel Borba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Winger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Winger's three-year relationship with actor Andrew Rubin ended in 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> From 1983 to 1985 Winger dated <a href="/wiki/Bob_Kerrey" title="Bob Kerrey">Bob Kerrey</a>, at the time the Governor of Nebraska, whom she met while filming <i>Terms of Endearment</i> in <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_Nebraska" title="Lincoln, Nebraska">Lincoln, Nebraska</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Winger has also dated her <i>Cannery Row</i> and <i>Everybody Wins</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Nick_Nolte" title="Nick Nolte">Nick Nolte</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1986 to 1990 she was married to actor <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Hutton" title="Timothy Hutton">Timothy Hutton</a> with whom she had a son, Noah Hutton, a documentary filmmaker born in 1987. The marriage ended in divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1996 she married actor/director <a href="/wiki/Arliss_Howard" title="Arliss Howard">Arliss Howard</a>, whom she met on the set of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Wilder_Napalm" title="Wilder Napalm">Wilder Napalm</a></i>. Their son, Gideon Babe Ruth Howard (known as Babe), was born in 1997. She is stepmother to Sam Howard, Arliss's son from his prior marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winger has had feuds with some of her co-stars, notably <a href="/wiki/Richard_Gere" title="Richard Gere">Richard Gere</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/An_Officer_and_a_Gentleman" title="An Officer and a Gentleman">An Officer and a Gentleman</a></i> (1982) and <a href="/wiki/Shirley_MacLaine" title="Shirley MacLaine">Shirley MacLaine</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Terms_of_Endearment" title="Terms of Endearment">Terms of Endearment</a></i> (1983).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
