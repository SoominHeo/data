<p><b>Debbi Wilkes</b> (born December 16, 1946) is a Canadian former <a href="/wiki/Pair_skating" title="Pair skating">pair skater</a>. With skating partner <a href="/wiki/Guy_Revell" title="Guy Revell">Guy Revell</a>, she became a two-time <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="Canadian Figure Skating Championships">Canadian national</a> champion, the 1963 <a href="/wiki/North_American_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="North American Figure Skating Championships">North American</a> champion, and the <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_1964_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the 1964 Winter Olympics">1964 Olympic</a> silver medalist.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbi_Wilkes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilkes married Bruce McEwan and lives in Toronto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MES140205_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MES140205-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbi_Wilkes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the ice by the age of five, Wilkes took up pairs at age ten and skated with her first partner until he quit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MES140205_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MES140205-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>She began skating with <a href="/wiki/Guy_Revell" title="Guy Revell">Guy Revell</a>, six years her elder, in 1958 after meeting at the Unionville skating carnival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MES140205_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-MES140205-1">[1]</a></sup> Though their height difference was adequate at the start of their partnership, by the time she was 17, in 1963, her height was 5'5" to Revell's 5'4".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icetime_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-icetime-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SM6305_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SM6305-3">[3]</a></sup> They were coached by Bruce Hyland at Crosby Arena and represented the Unionville Skating Club throughout their career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MES140205_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-MES140205-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wilkes fell from a lift while posing for press photographs prior to the <a href="/wiki/1963_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1963 World Figure Skating Championships">1963 World Championships</a>, hitting the ice head-first and fracturing her skull.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icetime_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-icetime-2">[2]</a></sup> The pair had to withdraw from the competition.</p>
<p>Wilkes/Revell were awarded the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_1964_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the 1964 Winter Olympics">1964 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck" title="Innsbruck">Innsbruck</a>, while gold went to <a href="/wiki/Ludmila_Belousova" title="Ludmila Belousova">Ludmila Belousova</a> / <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Protopopov" title="Oleg Protopopov">Oleg Protopopov</a> and silver to <a href="/wiki/Marika_Kilius" title="Marika Kilius">Marika Kilius</a> / <a href="/wiki/Hans-J%C3%BCrgen_B%C3%A4umler" title="Hans-Jürgen Bäumler">Hans-Jürgen Bäumler</a>. After taking the bronze at the <a href="/wiki/1964_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1964 World Figure Skating Championships">1964 World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund">Dortmund</a>, the pair parted ways — Wilkes decided to pursue an education while Revell elected to tour professionally in ice shows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icetime_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-icetime-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1966, Wilkes/Revell were informed that the silver medalists in Innsbruck, <a href="/wiki/Marika_Kilius" title="Marika Kilius">Marika Kilius</a> / <a href="/wiki/Hans-J%C3%BCrgen_B%C3%A4umler" title="Hans-Jürgen Bäumler">Hans-Jürgen Bäumler</a> of Germany, had been disqualified after an International Olympic Committee investigation found they had signed pro contracts before the Olympics. IOC executive James Worrall presented Wilkes/Revell with the silver medals during the Canadian Championships in Peterborough, Ont. Later, Kilius/Baumler were reinstated in the record books, but the medals were never redistributed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icetime_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-icetime-2">[2]</a></sup> In December 2013, after an investigation by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, the International Olympic Committee confirmed that Kilius/Bäumler and Wilkes/Revell share the 1964 Olympic silver medal and Joseph/Joseph of the United States are the bronze medalists. Despite the information on its website over the years, the IOC stated that this was intended to be the official result since 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT131213_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT131213-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After retiring from competition, Wilkes became a television skating analyst, an author, a coach, and Skate Canada's Director of Marketing and Sponsorship.</p>
