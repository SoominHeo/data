<p><b>Desmond David Hume</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i> portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ian_Cusick" title="Henry Ian Cusick">Henry Ian Cusick</a>. Desmond's name is a tribute to <a href="/wiki/David_Hume" title="David Hume">David Hume</a>, the famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empiricist" title="Empiricist">empiricist</a> philosopher. Desmond was not a passenger of Flight 815. He had been stranded on the island three years prior to the crash as the result of a shipwreck. Desmond eventually leaves the Island with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#Oceanic_Six" title="Characters of Lost">Oceanic 6</a> and is reunited with his love <a href="/wiki/Penny_Widmore" title="Penny Widmore">Penny Widmore</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sonya_Walger" title="Sonya Walger">Sonya Walger</a>).</p>
<p>It appears that Desmond was the "package" that <a href="/wiki/Charles_Widmore" title="Charles Widmore">Charles Widmore</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alan_Dale" title="Alan Dale">Alan Dale</a>) was talking about on Hydra Island. It was later revealed by Charles Widmore that Desmond was Jacob's "fail-safe", meaning that in case the <a href="/wiki/Man_in_Black_(Lost)" title="Man in Black (Lost)">Man in Black</a> killed all of Jacob's candidates, Desmond would then be used to destroy the island.</p>
<p>Desmond was one of the show's most popular characters. In 2006 Cusick's portrayal was nominated for an Emmy. In 2007 a two-week-long tournament style competition for <i>Lost'</i>s best character with over six thousand voters hosted by the <i>Washington Post</i> voted Desmond the winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desmond_Hume&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Desmond is named after <a href="/wiki/David_Hume" title="David Hume">David Hume</a>, a Scottish <a href="/wiki/Philosopher" title="Philosopher">philosopher</a> who discussed the ideas of <a href="/wiki/Free_will" title="Free will">free will</a> and <a href="/wiki/Determinism" title="Determinism">determinism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-2">[2]</a></sup> These ideas are reflected in Desmond's time travel where he meets Ms. Hawking, an old lady who explains that the universe has a specific way in which things must take place, anywhere that things go off course, the universe will correct itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flashes_Before_Your_Eyes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flashes_Before_Your_Eyes-3">[3]</a></sup> Cusick was originally hired for only three episodes in the beginning of season two, but he then returned in the finale and became a regular cast member from the third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the episode "The Constant", Desmond appears in two ways. In 1996, Desmond has short hair and no facial hair and in 2004, Desmond sports long and untamed hair with a full beard. Cusick did not cut his hair; it was hidden underneath a short-haired wig by "really talented hair and makeup folks", according to Carlton Cuse. All freighter scenes were shot before Cusick shaved most of his beard for the 1996 scenes. A fake beard was glued onto Cusick for the episodes "<a href="/wiki/Ji_Yeon" title="Ji Yeon">Ji Yeon</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Meet_Kevin_Johnson" title="Meet Kevin Johnson">Meet Kevin Johnson</a>" while his beard grew back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Desmond is often distinguished by his cheery attitude and his habit of referring to people as "Brother".</p>
<p>Henry Ian Cusick was not named a regular cast member in the press release for season six,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nov19PR_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nov19PR-6">[6]</a></sup> but he is one and was credited as such in the episodes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desmond_Hume&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In 2006 <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ian_Cusick" title="Henry Ian Cusick">Henry Ian Cusick</a> was the only <i>Lost</i> actor to be nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Emmy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He lost out in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Guest_Actor_-_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actor - Drama Series">Award for Outstanding Guest Actor</a> to <a href="/wiki/Christian_Clemenson" title="Christian Clemenson">Christian Clemenson</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Eric Goldman from <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> thought Desmond's flashbacks were "some of the more interesting flashbacks of the [second] season", finding Cusick's portrayal of Desmond as "likable" and "sympathetic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> IGN's Chris Carabott complimented Cusick's performance in "Flashes Before Your Eyes", particularly liking the chemistry between Cusick and Sonya Walger, as well as between Cusick and Alan Dale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-2">[2]</a></sup> Maureen Ryan of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Chicago_Tribune" title="The Chicago Tribune">The Chicago Tribune</a></i> thought Cusick's performance in the fourth season episode "The Constant" was "especially spine-tingling".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Gary Susman from <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> described Desmond's storyline as "the most emotionally satisfying character arc of season 4", feeling he deserved another Emmy nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Critic Kelly Woo, from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Squad" title="TV Squad">TV Squad</a></i>, placed him on #1 on her list of "Seven new characters that worked".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Penny and Desmond's relationship has been received positively by critics. In <i>Entertainment Weekly</i>, Alejandro Garay wrote, "One of my favorite episodes of 2008 was <i>Lost</i>’s 'The Constant.' It was a beautiful episode that made us fans fall in love with the show even more. The creators managed to build momentum with smart narrative, by using the romance card to develop such a complicated topic as time traveling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Maureen Ryan of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Chicago_Tribune" title="The Chicago Tribune">The Chicago Tribune</a></i> praised Penny and Desmond's phone call in "<a href="/wiki/The_Constant" title="The Constant">The Constant</a>", saying "a classic <i>Lost</i> moment" in Desmond and Penny's phone call.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Verne Gay of <i><a href="/wiki/Newsday" title="Newsday">Newsday</a></i> called it an emotional release, "I actually cried when Penny and Desmond finally... connected" and "there wasn't one, single, solitary false note".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Jeff Jensen of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> named the phone call between Desmond and Penny the best moment of the season excluding any moments from the then yet-to-air <a href="/wiki/There%27s_No_Place_Like_Home#Parts_2_.26_3" title="There's No Place Like Home">season finale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Erin Martell of <a href="/wiki/AOL" title="AOL">AOL</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Squad" title="TV Squad">TV Squad</a> said that "The Constant" strengthened her love for Desmond and Penny's story, saying "my heart won't break if none of [Jack, Kate, Sawyer and Juliet] end up together [but] if Desmond and Penny don't reunite, I will be devastated."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Jay Glatfelter of <a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a> said Penny and Desmond had "the best love story on the show and dare I say on television today".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Karla Peterson of <i><a href="/wiki/The_San_Diego_Union-Tribune" title="The San Diego Union-Tribune">The San Diego Union-Tribune</a></i> wrote that "[I was] almost as touched and relieved by the[ir] reunion as Desmond and Penny are"; however.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Ben Rawson-Jones of <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> wrote that "a refreshing shift in Lost's tone enabled loyal viewers to have their hearts warmed by the long distance smoochfest between Desmond and his beloved Penny".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>'</i>s Chris Carabott praised the on-screen chemistry of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ian_Cusick" title="Henry Ian Cusick">Henry Ian Cusick</a> (Desmond) and <a href="/wiki/Sonya_Walger" title="Sonya Walger">Sonya Walger</a> (Penelope), stating that "their on screen chemistry makes the love between the two characters real and makes their inevitable breakup that much harder to handle.".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carabott_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-carabott-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
