<p><b>Erick Orrosquieta</b> (born August 30, 1991), commonly known by his stage name <b>Deorro</b>, is a Mexican American DJ signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultra_Records" title="Ultra Records">Ultra Records</a>. He formerly used the name <b>TON!C</b>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deorro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9312:_Early_beginnings">2005–12: Early beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deorro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2005–12: Early beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Deorro began his DJ career by playing at local gigs when he was 14. He was born in Los Angeles to Mexican parents. By the time Deorro turned 17, he was already producing his own tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2012, he was asked by DJ <a href="/wiki/Chuckie_(DJ)" title="Chuckie (DJ)">Chuckie</a> to remix his track "Make Some Noise". Deorro's remix landed in the top 50 on Beatport. He has since remixed tracks from multiple international famed DJs such as <a href="/wiki/Steve_Aoki" title="Steve Aoki">Steve Aoki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laidback_Luke" title="Laidback Luke">Laidback Luke</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Emery" title="Gareth Emery">Gareth Emery</a>, among others.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_Breakthrough">2013–present: Breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deorro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013–present: Breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, he released the track "Yee" on <a href="/wiki/Hardwell" title="Hardwell">Hardwell</a>'s label, Revealed Recordings, which charted in Austria, Belgium, France, Germany, Netherlands, and Switzerland. On 18 March 2014 he released the single "Freak" with <a href="/wiki/Steve_Aoki" title="Steve Aoki">Steve Aoki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diplo" title="Diplo">Diplo</a>. On 24 March 2014 he released the single "<a href="/wiki/Flashlight_(R3hab_and_Deorro_song)" title="Flashlight (R3hab and Deorro song)">Flashlight</a>" with <a href="/wiki/R3hab" title="R3hab">R3hab</a>. He released the single "<a href="/wiki/Five_Hours" title="Five Hours">Five Hours</a>" in April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The track charted in Austria, Belgium, France, Netherlands, Norway, Sweden and Switzerland. On 17 May 2014, Deorro announced on his Twitter page he was going to take a break from DJing in order to have a bigger focus in making music and expanding his record label, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Panda_Funk&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Panda Funk (page does not exist)">Panda Funk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He guest-starred on the MTV Series <a href="/wiki/Teen_Wolf" title="Teen Wolf">Teen Wolf</a> as the DJ. In August 2014 he released the single "Rambo" with J-Trick. On 18 October 2014, Deorro received the award for highest new entry on the 'DJmag top 100 DJs 2014' contest, he hit spot 19. He also played a half-hour set at the 'DJmag top 100 DJs' event, which took place during ADE in Amsterdam, the Netherlands. In December 2014 he released the single "Perdoname". In March 2015 he released a third version of "<a href="/wiki/Five_Hours" title="Five Hours">Five Hours</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> titled "<a href="/wiki/Five_Hours" title="Five Hours">Five More Hours</a>", the song has charted in Ireland, Netherlands and Sweden.</p>
