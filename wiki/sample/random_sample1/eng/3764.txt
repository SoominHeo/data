<p><b>David Michael Sabo</b>, nicknamed <b>"The Snake,"</b> is a rock guitarist who plays in the heavy metal band <a href="/wiki/Skid_Row_(American_band)" title="Skid Row (American band)">Skid Row</a>. He is co-guitarist with <a href="/wiki/Scotti_Hill" title="Scotti Hill">Scotti Hill</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Sabo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sabo was born on September 16, 1964, in <a href="/wiki/Perth_Amboy,_New_Jersey" title="Perth Amboy, New Jersey">Perth Amboy, New Jersey</a>. He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Sayreville,_New_Jersey" title="Sayreville, New Jersey">Sayreville</a>, down the street from <a href="/wiki/Jon_Bon_Jovi" title="Jon Bon Jovi">Jon Bon Jovi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> His mother, Dorothy, or "Mama Snake" as she is known, raised him and his brothers on her own. Influenced heavily by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_n%27_roll" title="Rock n' roll">rock n' roll</a> music of the era, including some <a href="/wiki/Motown" title="Motown">Motown</a>, he found himself listening to the likes of <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">Judas Priest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Van_Halen" title="Van Halen">Van Halen</a>. From an early age, Snake was a gifted athlete, even having scouts coming out to see him play. At the age of 13 he saw Kiss, which acted a catalyst for Snake to give up sports and focus his time on music and learning guitar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His mother had bought his brother a $40 guitar which he soon lost interest in, paving the way for Sabo to take up the instrument at the age of 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was the original lead guitar player of <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a>, playing several local shows with them before guitarist <a href="/wiki/Richie_Sambora" title="Richie Sambora">Richie Sambora</a> replaced him in 1983.</p>
<p>He is also the uncle of former <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Tennis_Professionals" title="Association of Tennis Professionals">ATP</a> Tennis professional Matt Sabo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A chance meeting with local bass player <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Bolan" title="Rachel Bolan">Rachel Bolan</a> in the Garden State Music Store where he was employed set him in the direction of forming the band that would earn him fame. Their partnership would not only prove to be the right step with their songwriting, but for friendship as well, as they still consider themselves to be best friends.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Skid_Row">Skid Row</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Sabo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Skid Row">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Skid Row is an U.S.-American hard rock/heavy metal band which became one of the flagship bands of the late 1980s hard rock scene. They were one of the most successful hard rock bands of the era until they were eclipsed by the <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> <a href="/wiki/Grunge" title="Grunge">grunge</a> bands in the early 1990s. The band was not named after <a href="/wiki/Gary_Moore" title="Gary Moore">Gary Moore</a>'s first band, <a href="/wiki/Skid_Row_(Irish_band)" title="Skid Row (Irish band)">Skid Row</a>.</p>
<p>Skid Row was formed in New Jersey in 1986 by Bolan and Sabo. The pair added guitarist Scotti Hill, drummer Rob Affuso, and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Matt_Fallon" title="Matt Fallon">Matt Fallon</a> who was later replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Bach" title="Sebastian Bach">Sebastian Bach</a> in 1987. The band began playing shows in clubs throughout the eastern United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the assistance of friend Jon Bon Jovi, Sabo secured a record deal for Skid Row with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1989, the band released their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Skid_Row_(album)" title="Skid Row (album)">Skid Row</a></i>, which was an instant success, mainly due to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_ballad" title="Power ballad">power ballads</a>. The record went multi-platinum and produced the hit singles "<a href="/wiki/18_and_Life" title="18 and Life">18 and Life</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I_Remember_You_(Skid_Row_song)" title="I Remember You (Skid Row song)">I Remember You</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Youth_Gone_Wild" title="Youth Gone Wild">Youth Gone Wild</a>". Skid Row turned in a heavier direction on their #1, double-platinum follow-up 1991 album. Sabo has worked as assistant producer on many of the band's songs.</p>
<p>Sabo composed music for the 2006 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Still_Life_(2006_film)" title="The Still Life (2006 film)">The Still Life</a></i>.</p>
