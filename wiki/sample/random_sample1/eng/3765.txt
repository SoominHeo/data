<p><b>Dave Evans</b> (born 20 July 1953) is a Welsh-born Australian singer who briefly sang for the Australian <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a> in 1973–1974.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Evans_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evans was born in the Welsh town of <a href="/wiki/Carmarthen" title="Carmarthen">Carmarthen</a>, and moved to Australia at the age of five with his parents. They settled in the Queensland city of <a href="/wiki/Townsville" title="Townsville">Townsville</a>, and later moved to <a href="/wiki/Charters_Towers" title="Charters Towers">Charters Towers</a>, where he formed his first band at the age of seventeen. He later moved to Sydney.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="AC.2FDC">AC/DC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Evans_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: AC/DC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evans was one of several members of AC/DC before the band matured and began to play all original music, along with <a href="/wiki/Colin_Burgess_(musician)" title="Colin Burgess (musician)">Colin Burgess</a> and <a href="/wiki/Larry_Van_Kriedt" title="Larry Van Kriedt">Larry Van Kriedt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was a member of the band from its inception in November 1973 until September 1974 before officially being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Bon_Scott" title="Bon Scott">Bon Scott</a> in October 1974. During his time with AC/DC, Evans recorded one single, a Young/Young composition ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Can_I_Sit_Next_to_You_Girl#.22Can_I_Sit_Next_to_You.2C_Girl.22_.281974.29" title="Can I Sit Next to You Girl">Can I Sit Next To You, Girl</a>"/ "Rocking in the Parlour") which was released in Australia and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. A low-budget promotional video for the demo was also shot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-markprindle_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-markprindle-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Music_Legends_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Music_Legends-5">[5]</a></sup> It peaked nationally at number 50 on the Aria (<a href="/wiki/Kent_Music_Report" title="Kent Music Report">Kent</a>) charts but reached the top 10 in South Australia and Western Australia and then disappeared. The song was later re-recorded with Bon Scott. They replaced Evans with Scott while changing their sound from glam rock to harder blues rock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Evans has cited jealousy and a physical altercation with the manager during a heavy national tour as the reason for his dismissal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-markprindle_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-markprindle-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrk_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrk-7">[7]</a></sup> and has also said in interviews that the early history of AC/DC is unjustly ignored.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrk_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrk-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_AC.2FDC">After AC/DC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Evans_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After AC/DC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After AC/DC, Evans joined Newcastle band <a href="/wiki/Rabbit_(band)" title="Rabbit (band)">Rabbit</a>, replacing original singer Greg Douglas. Rabbit released 6 singles and two albums between 1975 and 1977 with the second albnum, Too Much Rock N Roll released throughout Europe and Japan. His other bands included <i>Dave Evans &amp; Hot Cockerel and Dave Evans Thunder Down Under which released self titled album on Reaction Records.</i></p>
<p>Evans also released a live recording, <i>A Hell of a Night</i>, which was a memorial gig for Bon Scott. It was recorded on the 20th anniversary of Scott's death, with Melbourne AC/DC tribute band Thunderstruck. Since then he has released six solo cd's.</p>
<p>In June 2012, Evans performed as guest vocalist for the Norwegian band Barbed Wire on a mini AC/DC tribute tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The tour started with a concert on Trondheim Rock. Barbed Wire played as opening act to Twisted Sister. Evans then flew back to the UK for a 12-date headline tour, performing songs from <i>Judgement Day</i> and cover versions of classic AC/DC tracks written by the band after his departure.</p>
<p>In June/July 2013, Evans returned to the UK for an 11-date headline tour, celebrating 40 years of AC/DC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He released a single "Little Headbanger" from <i>Judgement Day</i> as a promo music video for the tour, but the music once again failed to make any charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He also played four dates with Long Way to the Top – the AC/DC Spectacular.</p>
<p>Dave Evans returned to Europe In May 2014. He performed in <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a> in a show called "Ukraine still ROCKS!" and was supported by local AC/DC tribute band D/C Train.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The show in Kiev was held in the Bike-Rock-N-Roll Club Route 66<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> on 24 May. Evans called it a historic event for him and rock music as a whole. In the same year the singer played in Brazil with his South American Badasses in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A3" title="Paranã">Paranã</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_Island" title="Santa Catarina Island">Santa Catarina</a>.</p>
