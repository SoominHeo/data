<p><b>Dave Pensado</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>-winning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mix_engineer" title="Mix engineer">mix engineer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sound_on_sound_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sound_on_sound-1">[1]</a></sup> His career began in Atlanta in the 1970s and 1980s doing live and studio sound engineering. He has lived in Los Angeles since 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sound_on_sound_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sound_on_sound-1">[1]</a></sup> He mixes between 200 and 250 songs a year at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Larrabee_Studios&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Larrabee Studios (page does not exist)">Larrabee Studios</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sound_on_sound_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-sound_on_sound-1">[1]</a></sup> When recording, he usually records at Echo Bar Studios in North Hollywood.</p>
<p>On September 12, 2011, he started a weekly show called <i>Pensado's Place</i> with Herb Trawick. The show is about engineering techniques and interviews a guest Mixer, Producer or Artist each episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 20, 2013, Dave Pensado and Herb Trawick hosted an event at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vintage_King&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vintage King (page does not exist)">Vintage King</a> <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> called "Pensado Gear Expo 2013".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pensado_gear_expo_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pensado_gear_expo-3">[3]</a></sup> More than one thousand people from around the world attended. 22-time Grammy Award winning engineer, <a href="/wiki/Al_Schmitt" title="Al Schmitt">Al Schmitt</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Steven_Slate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Steven Slate (page does not exist)">Steven Slate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_da_Kid" title="Alex da Kid">Alex da Kid</a>, Ryan Hewitt, and Robert Duncan<sup class="plainlinks noprint Inline-Template" style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="external text" href="http://dispenser.homenet.org/~dispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py?page=Dave_Pensado&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn" rel="nofollow"><span title="Link needs disambiguation. (March 2014)">disambiguation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> were a few of the featured guests of the expo.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Pensado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pensado mixes exclusively in the box, which means he uses very little outboard gear and programs everything in his computer. Apart from the very occasional <a href="/wiki/Logic_Pro" title="Logic Pro">Logic</a> session, he uses <a href="/wiki/Pro_Tools" title="Pro Tools">Pro Tools</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sound_on_sound_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-sound_on_sound-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Pensado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pensado won a Grammy Award, alongside fellow engineers <a href="/wiki/Jaycen_Joshua" title="Jaycen Joshua">Jaycen Joshua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuk_Harrell" title="Kuk Harrell">Kuk Harrell</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_Grammy_Awards" title="2009 Grammy Awards">2008</a> <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Contemporary_R%26B_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Contemporary R&amp;B Album">Best Contemporary R&amp;B Album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Growing_Pains_(Mary_J._Blige_album)" title="Growing Pains (Mary J. Blige album)">Growing Pains</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grammy_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-grammy-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
