<p><b>David Lee Gallagher</b> (born February 9, 1985) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. Beginning a prolific career as a <a href="/wiki/Child_actor" title="Child actor">child actor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Child_model" title="Child model">model</a> at the age of two, Gallagher is a five-time <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a> nominee and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teen_Choice_Award" title="Teen Choice Award">Teen Choice Award</a> winner, best known for his role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simon_Camden" title="Simon Camden">Simon Camden</a> on the long-running television series <i><a href="/wiki/7th_Heaven_(TV_series)" title="7th Heaven (TV series)">7th Heaven</a></i>, as well as for his feature film roles; as Mikey Ubriacco in <i><a href="/wiki/Look_Who%27s_Talking_Now" title="Look Who's Talking Now">Look Who's Talking Now</a></i>, as Kevin Harper in <i><a href="/wiki/Angels_in_the_Endzone" title="Angels in the Endzone">Angels in the Endzone</a></i> and as <a href="/wiki/Richie_Rich_(comics)" title="Richie Rich (comics)">Richie Rich</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Richie_Rich%27s_Christmas_Wish" title="Richie Rich's Christmas Wish">Richie Rich's Christmas Wish</a></i>. Gallagher is also well known for the voice of <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Kingdom_Hearts#Riku" title="Characters of Kingdom Hearts">Riku</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_Hearts" title="Kingdom Hearts">Kingdom Hearts</a></i> video game series.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Gallagher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gallagher was born in <a href="/wiki/College_Point,_Queens" title="College Point, Queens">College Point, New York</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to Elena Gallagher<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup> (née Lopez) and Darren James Gallagher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His parents separated when he was a baby and his mother remarried Vincent Casey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-4">[4]</a></sup> Gallagher is of Cuban descent on his mother's side and Irish descent on his father's side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-4">[4]</a></sup> He has four younger half-siblings, Michelle (b. 1988), Kelly (b. 1991), Kyle (b. 1995) and Killian (b. 1997).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Killian was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autism</a> and as a result David is an active supporter and spokesman for the organization <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cure_Autism_Now" title="Cure Autism Now">Cure Autism Now</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Chaminade_College_Preparatory_School_(California)" title="Chaminade College Preparatory School (California)">Chaminade College Preparatory School (California)</a> in 2003<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">[6]</a></sup> and enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>, where he majored in film and television studies, graduating in May 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Gallagher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gallagher began acting at the age of 2, first modeling for print advertorials in and around New York City which eventually led to commercial work as an actor. He appeared in many television commercials as a toddler for products such as <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Foods" title="Tyson Foods">Tyson Foods</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fisher_Price" title="Fisher Price">Fisher Price</a>. At age 8 he briefly had a recurring role on the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Loving_(TV_series)" title="Loving (TV series)">Loving</a></i>.</p>
<p>His film debut came in 1993 when he won the role of Mikey in the sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Look_Who%27s_Talking_Now" title="Look Who's Talking Now">Look Who's Talking Now</a></i>, playing the son of <a href="/wiki/John_Travolta" title="John Travolta">John Travolta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirstie_Alley" title="Kirstie Alley">Kirstie Alley</a>. In 1995 he played in a production of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Christmas_Carol" title="A Christmas Carol">A Christmas Carol</a></i> on Broadway. He also starred in several TV movies, including <i>Bermuda Triangle</i>.</p>
<p>In 1996, he reunited with John Travolta for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Phenomenon_(film)" title="Phenomenon (film)">Phenomenon</a></i>. Later that year, he was cast as Simon Camden in the family drama series <i>7th Heaven</i>. <i>7th Heaven</i> remained on the air for 11 seasons, making it the longest-running family drama in television history,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and also became the highest-rated show on The WB Network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>He filmed a few movies during hiatuses from filming, including the direct-to-video <i><a href="/wiki/Richie_Rich%27s_Christmas_Wish" title="Richie Rich's Christmas Wish">Richie Rich's Christmas Wish</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Secrets_(2001_film)" title="Little Secrets (2001 film)">Little Secrets</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rachel_Wood" title="Evan Rachel Wood">Evan Rachel Wood</a>. In 2003, during <i>7th Heaven</i> 's 8th season, David left the show in order to attend college full-time. However, he returned to the series part-time during season 9 and for what was thought to be the 10th and final season. In late 2006 <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a> unexpectedly picked up the show for an 11th season. David opted not to renew his contract.</p>
<p>In 2005 David starred in and co-produced a low-budget adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Picture_of_Dorian_Gray" title="The Picture of Dorian Gray">The Picture of Dorian Gray</a></i>.</p>
<p>His first role after leaving <i>7th Heaven</i> was a guest stint on <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(TV_series)" title="Numbers (TV series)">Numb3rs</a></i>, playing serial killer Buck Winters in September 2006, a role which he reprised in January 2009. He also appeared in the season 6 opener of <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i>, playing another suspected killer. He also starred in the horror sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Boogeyman_2" title="Boogeyman 2">Boogeyman 2</a></i>, which went straight to DVD. In July 2008, Gallagher guest-starred on the TNT drama <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_Grace_(TV_series)" title="Saving Grace (TV series)">Saving Grace</a></i> as Paul Shapiro, a troubled young man who was the suspect in a murder investigation. His next guest appearance was in an October 2008 episode of the FOX crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Bones_(TV_series)" title="Bones (TV series)">Bones</a></i> as Ryan Stephenson, the son of a man who later became a woman. Then in November 2008 he appeared in an episode of the CBS show <i><a href="/wiki/Without_a_Trace" title="Without a Trace">Without a Trace</a></i> as a suspect named Jeff Ellis who was thought to be responsible for the disappearance of a local nurse. He's also notable for having voiced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riku_(Kingdom_Hearts)" title="Riku (Kingdom Hearts)">Riku</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_Hearts" title="Kingdom Hearts">Kingdom Hearts</a></i> video game franchise and for portraying Seiji Amasawa in the English dub version of <a href="/wiki/Studio_Ghibli" title="Studio Ghibli">Studio Ghibli</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whisper_of_the_Heart_(film)" title="Whisper of the Heart (film)">Whisper of the Heart</a></i>. He appeared on the second-to-last episode of the series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Deep_End_(TV_series)" title="The Deep End (TV series)">The Deep End</a></i>, where he played a man charged with second-degree murder although he is innocent.</p>
<p>In March 2012, David has been announced to star with <a href="/wiki/Jake_Weber" title="Jake Weber">Jake Weber</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComingSoon_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ComingSoon-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShockTillYouDrop_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ShockTillYouDrop-10">[10]</a></sup> in <i>Scared of the Dark</i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Takashi_Shimizu" title="Takashi Shimizu">Takashi Shimizu</a>.</p>
