<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lowery_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Lowery is the eldest of nine children. His family moved to Irving when he was seven, after his father received a job teaching theology at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Dallas" title="University of Dallas">University of Dallas</a>. Just out of <a href="/wiki/Irving_High_School" title="Irving High School">Irving High School</a>, he wrote and directed his first film, <i>Lullaby</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lowery_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Lowery had his breakthrough with his first feature film, <i>St. Nick</i>, which follows two runaway children abandoned by their guardians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The film played in <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It won the Texas Filmmaker Award at the 2009 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AFI_Dallas_International_Film_Festival" title="AFI Dallas International Film Festival">AFI Dallas International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2011, he wrote and directed the short film entitled <i>Pioneer</i>, which played at <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Lowery directed his second feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/Ain%27t_Them_Bodies_Saints" title="Ain't Them Bodies Saints">Ain't Them Bodies Saints</a></i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/Casey_Affleck" title="Casey Affleck">Casey Affleck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rooney_Mara" title="Rooney Mara">Rooney Mara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The film was nominated for the Grand Jury Prize at the 2013 Sundance Film Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It was selected to compete at the <a href="/wiki/International_Critics%27_Week" title="International Critics' Week">International Critics' Week</a> section of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2013 Cannes Film Festival">2013 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to having edited films such as <a href="/wiki/Amy_Seimetz" title="Amy Seimetz">Amy Seimetz</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Sun_Don%27t_Shine" title="Sun Don't Shine">Sun Don't Shine</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Shane_Carruth" title="Shane Carruth">Shane Carruth</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Upstream_Color" title="Upstream Color">Upstream Color</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> he also co-wrote <i><a href="/wiki/Pit_Stop_(2013_film)" title="Pit Stop (2013 film)">Pit Stop</a></i> with director <a href="/wiki/Yen_Tan" title="Yen Tan">Yen Tan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2014_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2014 Cannes Film Festival">2014 Cannes Film Festival</a>, it was announced that Lowery would write and direct the film adaptation of 2012 National Book Award finalist <i><a href="/wiki/The_Yellow_Birds" title="The Yellow Birds">The Yellow Birds</a></i> written by war veteran <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Powers" title="Kevin Powers">Kevin Powers</a>. The film was eventually directed by <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Moors" title="Alexandre Moors">Alexandre Moors</a> and stars <a href="/wiki/Jack_Huston" title="Jack Huston">Jack Huston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tye_Sheridan" title="Tye Sheridan">Tye Sheridan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alden_Ehrenreich" title="Alden Ehrenreich">Alden Ehrenreich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Aniston" title="Jennifer Aniston">Jennifer Aniston</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2016, it was announced Lowery would direct an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_(TV_series)" title="Breakthrough (TV series)">Breakthrough</a></i> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Channel" title="National Geographic Channel">National Geographic Channel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In November 2016, it was announced Lowery would re-team with <a href="/wiki/Rooney_Mara" title="Rooney Mara">Rooney Mara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Casey_Affleck" title="Casey Affleck">Casey Affleck</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/A_Ghost_Story" title="A Ghost Story">A Ghost Story</a></i> a project secretly shot over the summer of 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_influences_for_Ain.27t_Them_Bodies_Saints">Style and influences for <i>Ain't Them Bodies Saints</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lowery_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Style and influences for Ain't Them Bodies Saints">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Lowery was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Claire_Denis" title="Claire Denis">Claire Denis</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/35_Shots_of_Rum" title="35 Shots of Rum">35 Shots of Rum</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Altman" title="Robert Altman">Robert Altman</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/McCabe_%26_Mrs._Miller" title="McCabe &amp; Mrs. Miller">McCabe &amp; Mrs. Miller</a></i> is one of his all-time favorite films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He was also inspired by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Thomas_Anderson" title="Paul Thomas Anderson">Paul Thomas Anderson</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Fincher" title="David Fincher">David Fincher</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
