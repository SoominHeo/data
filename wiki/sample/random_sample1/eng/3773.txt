<p><b>David Lee</b> (born April 29, 1983) is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). He played <a href="/wiki/College_basketball" title="College basketball">college basketball</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_men%27s_basketball" title="Florida Gators men's basketball">Florida Gators</a> before he was drafted 30th overall by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2005_NBA_draft" title="2005 NBA draft">2005 NBA draft</a>. In 2010, Lee was <a href="/wiki/Sign_and_trade" title="Sign and trade">signed and traded</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a>. Lee is a two-time All-Star and <a href="/wiki/All-NBA_Team" title="All-NBA Team">All-NBA Team</a> member, and won an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_championship" title="NBA championship">NBA championship</a> with the Warriors in 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>. He went to middle school at <a href="/wiki/John_Burroughs_School" title="John Burroughs School">John Burroughs School</a> and high school at <a href="/wiki/Chaminade_College_Preparatory_School_(Missouri)" title="Chaminade College Preparatory School (Missouri)">Chaminade College Preparatory School</a>, both in St. Louis suburbs. Naturally left-handed, Lee became essentially <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ambidextrous" title="Ambidextrous">ambidextrous</a> when he broke his left arm and learned to play right-handed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Lee was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McDonald%27s_All_American" title="McDonald's All American">McDonald's All American</a> and won the 2001 Slam Dunk competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Lee was also named a first-team high school All-American by <i><a href="/wiki/Parade_(magazine)" title="Parade (magazine)">Parade</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee accepted an athletic scholarship to attend the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a> in Gainesville, Florida, where he played for coach <a href="/wiki/Billy_Donovan" title="Billy Donovan">Billy Donovan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_men%27s_basketball" title="Florida Gators men's basketball">Florida Gators basketball</a> team from 2001 to 2005. As a freshman in 2002, he was named to the All-<a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Conference" title="Southeastern Conference">Southeastern Conference</a> (SEC) freshman team by the conference's coaches. During his sophomore season in 2002–03, he averaged 11.5 points and 7 rebounds a game. Lee was named a second-team All-SEC selection in his junior in 2003–04, while averaging 13 points and 7 rebounds per game.</p>
<p>Lee's senior year, he was joined by incoming freshmen and future NBA players <a href="/wiki/Al_Horford" title="Al Horford">Al Horford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corey_Brewer" title="Corey Brewer">Corey Brewer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taurean_Green" title="Taurean Green">Taurean Green</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joakim_Noah" title="Joakim Noah">Joakim Noah</a>. The Gators went on to win the <a href="/wiki/2005_SEC_Men%27s_Basketball_Tournament" title="2005 SEC Men's Basketball Tournament">2005 SEC Men's Basketball Tournament</a> – the Gators' first <a href="/wiki/SEC_Men%27s_Basketball_Tournament" title="SEC Men's Basketball Tournament">SEC Men's Basketball Tournament</a> championship – by defeating the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Kentucky_Wildcats_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2004–05 Kentucky Wildcats men's basketball team">Kentucky Wildcats</a> 70–53 in the tournament final.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_Knicks_.282005.E2.80.932010.29">New York Knicks (2005–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New York Knicks (2005–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9306">2005–06</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2005–06">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Lee was selected by the Knicks with the team's second first-round pick (30th overall) in the <a href="/wiki/2005_NBA_draft" title="2005 NBA draft">2005 NBA draft</a>, following <a href="/wiki/Channing_Frye" title="Channing Frye">Channing Frye</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Lee signed with the team on July 1, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:David_LeeNYK.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2336" data-file-width="3504" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/David_LeeNYK.jpg/220px-David_LeeNYK.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/David_LeeNYK.jpg/330px-David_LeeNYK.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/David_LeeNYK.jpg/440px-David_LeeNYK.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:David_LeeNYK.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lee in 2007</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Lee was installed as a starting forward for a stretch of 13 games in December 2005 and January 2006. Lee posted 23 points on 10–11 shooting, along with 15 rebounds and three steals in 52 minutes as the Knicks went on to defeat the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a> in triple overtime on January 2, 2006. Lee averaged 5.1 points (59.6%) and 4.5 rebounds per game in his rookie season while averaging 16.9 minutes of playing time in 67 games.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9307">2006–07</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2006–07">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>With an injury to <a href="/wiki/Channing_Frye" title="Channing Frye">Channing Frye</a>, Lee started his first game of the season against the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bulls" title="Chicago Bulls">Chicago Bulls</a> on November 28, 2006. On December 16, 2006, he was one of 10 players ejected in the <a href="/wiki/Knicks%E2%80%93Nuggets_brawl" title="Knicks–Nuggets brawl">Knicks–Nuggets brawl</a>. However, he was not involved in the brawl and was not suspended by the NBA. On December 20, 2006 in a double-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overtime_(sport)" title="Overtime (sport)">overtime</a> game against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlotte_Bobcats" title="Charlotte Bobcats">Charlotte Bobcats</a> with 0.1 seconds left, Lee scored the winning basket on a tip-in without breaking the league's so-called <a href="/wiki/Trent_Tucker_Rule" title="Trent Tucker Rule">Trent Tucker Rule</a>, whereby a player cannot catch and shoot a successful field goal with less than three tenths of a second remaining on the clock. At the <a href="/wiki/2007_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2007 NBA All-Star Game">All-Star break</a>, Lee had averaged 11.1 points on 61.05% shooting (first in the league), an 80.0% free throw percentage, 10.8 rebounds (8th in NBA) and 1.8 assists in 30.9 minutes a game. On February 16, 2007, Lee, playing for the Sophomores, was named the Most Valuable Player in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rookie_Challenge" title="Rookie Challenge">Rookie Challenge</a>, finishing with 30 points on 14 of 14 shooting from the field and 11 rebounds. On February 23, 2007, in a game against the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a>, Lee came down on, future teammate, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Bogut" title="Andrew Bogut">Andrew Bogut</a>'s foot and sprained his ankle. The original diagnosis was for him to only miss a few days, but nearly three weeks after the injury he was still unable to play. He was reexamined and the doctor found that he had a much more severe sprain than was originally diagnosed. Lee played only sporadic minutes for the remaining games of the season.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9308">2007–08</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2007–08">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the 2007–08 season, Lee continued to develop into a major contributor off the bench for the Knicks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.9309">2008–09</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2008–09">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>David Lee set a career high in points, with 37. He also became the first Knicks player with 30 points and 20 rebounds in a game since <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Ewing" title="Patrick Ewing">Patrick Ewing</a> had 34 points and 25 rebounds on February 23, 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 9, in a match-up against the Chicago Bulls, Lee became only the 11th Knick ever to score 10 consecutive double-doubles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310">2009–10</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2009–10">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On February 11, Lee was chosen to replace <a href="/wiki/Allen_Iverson" title="Allen Iverson">Allen Iverson</a> to represent the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Conference_(NBA)" title="Eastern Conference (NBA)">East team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2010_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2010 NBA All-Star Game">2010 NBA All-Star Game</a>. He became the first Knick to make the All-Star team since <a href="/wiki/Allan_Houston" title="Allan Houston">Allan Houston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latrell_Sprewell" title="Latrell Sprewell">Latrell Sprewell</a> appeared in the <a href="/wiki/2001_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2001 NBA All-Star Game">2001 NBA All-Star Game</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On April 2, Lee recorded the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triple-double" title="Triple-double">triple-double</a> of his career, putting up 37 points, 20 rebounds, and 10 assists against the Golden State Warriors. He became the first player since <a href="/wiki/Kareem_Abdul-Jabbar" title="Kareem Abdul-Jabbar">Kareem Abdul-Jabbar</a> in 1976 to record at least 35 points, 20 rebounds and 10 assists in a game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At the end of the season, Lee became an unrestricted free agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He had recorded career highs in points per game, as well as assists per game.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Golden_State_Warriors_.282010.E2.80.932015.29">Golden State Warriors (2010–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Golden State Warriors (2010–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lee became an unrestricted free agent in the 2010 off-season. On July 9, he was <a href="/wiki/Sign_and_trade" title="Sign and trade">signed and traded</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> in a deal that involved <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Randolph" title="Anthony Randolph">Anthony Randolph</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelenna_Azubuike" title="Kelenna Azubuike">Kelenna Azubuike</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronny_Turiaf" title="Ronny Turiaf">Ronny Turiaf</a> and a future second-round pick traded to the Knicks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Lee's contract under the deal pays him $79.54 million over six years, an average of $13.3 million per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spotrac_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-spotrac-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311">2010–11</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2010–11">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the 2010–11 season, Lee appeared in 73 games (all starts), averaging 16.5 points, 9.8 rebounds, 3.2 assists and 1.01 steals in 36.1 minutes per contest.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312">2011–12</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2011–12">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On February 7, 2012, Lee recorded his second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triple-double" title="Triple-double">triple-double</a>, scoring 25 points and getting 11 rebounds and 10 assists in a loss against the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_Thunder" title="Oklahoma City Thunder">Oklahoma City Thunder</a>. Over the course of the 2011–12 season, he averaged over 20 points per game, the second such season in his NBA career.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313">2012–13</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2012–13">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2237" data-file-width="2223" height="221" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg/220px-Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg/330px-Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg/440px-Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Andrew_Bogut_and_David_Lee.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lee with <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Bogut" title="Andrew Bogut">Andrew Bogut</a> in 2013</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On January 24, 2013, Lee was named to the <a href="/wiki/2013_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2013 NBA All-Star Game">2013 NBA All-Star Game</a> as a reserve representing the <a href="/wiki/Western_Conference_(NBA)" title="Western Conference (NBA)">Western Conference</a>. He was the Warriors' first All-Star since <a href="/wiki/Latrell_Sprewell" title="Latrell Sprewell">Latrell Sprewell</a> in 1997. Lee recorded a career high 22 rebounds against the Spurs, and recorded his third triple double against the Charlotte Bobcats with 23 points, 11 rebounds, and 11 assists. Lee finished the season with averages of 18.5 points and 11.2 rebounds per game, and led the league in <a href="/wiki/Double_(basketball)#Double-double" title="Double (basketball)">double-doubles</a> with 56. Also, he was named to the All-NBA Third Team. His performance was crucial for the Warriors to reach the <a href="/wiki/2013_NBA_Playoffs" title="2013 NBA Playoffs">playoffs</a>, where they were to face the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Nuggets" title="Denver Nuggets">Denver Nuggets</a>.</p>
<p>Lee injured his hip during the fourth quarter of his first playoff game. An <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MRI" title="MRI">MRI</a> later revealed a "complete tear of his right hip flexor". Lee finished the game with 10 points and 14 rebounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Despite the injury, which was expected to end his season, Lee returned for the sixth game of the series as the Warriors upset the Nuggets. However, the Warriors were defeated by the <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a> in six games in the next round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314">2013–14</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2013–14">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On February 2, 2014, Lee ended his double digit scoring streak at 123 games with 8 points in a 91-75 loss to the Charlotte Bobcats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315:_Injury-riddled_season_and_first_NBA_Championship">2014–15: Injury-riddled season and first NBA Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2014–15: Injury-riddled season and first NBA Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Lee suffered a strained left hamstring in the final game of the preseason, which sidelined him for 24 out of the first 25 games of the season. Lee was replaced in the Warriors' starting lineup by <a href="/wiki/Draymond_Green" title="Draymond Green">Draymond Green</a>, who continued to start even after Lee recovered. The highest-paid player on the Warriors roster at $15 million, Lee became a reserve for the first time since early in his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On January 27, 2015, he scored a season-high 24 points in a 111-113 overtime loss to the Chicago Bulls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Lee won his first NBA championship with the Warriors after they defeated the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2015_NBA_Finals" title="2015 NBA Finals">2015 NBA Finals</a> in six games.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boston_Celtics_.282015.E2.80.932016.29">Boston Celtics (2015–2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Boston Celtics (2015–2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 27, 2015, Lee was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a> in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Wallace" title="Gerald Wallace">Gerald Wallace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Babb" title="Chris Babb">Chris Babb</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> as Golden State was seeking to offload his salary given his limited role on the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He made his debut for the Celtics in the team's season opener against the Philadelphia 76ers on October 28, recording 8 points and 5 rebounds as a starter in a 112–95 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On February 19, 2016, he was waived by the Celtics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dallas_Mavericks_.282016.29">Dallas Mavericks (2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Dallas Mavericks (2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 22, 2016, Lee signed with the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He made his debut for the Mavericks two days later, recording four points and four rebounds off the bench in a loss to the Oklahoma City Thunder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In his second game for the Mavericks on February 26, he recorded 14 points and 14 rebounds in 25 minutes off the bench in a 122–116 overtime win over the Denver Nuggets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On March 25, Lee returned to Oracle Arena for the first time since being traded from the Warriors and got a standing ovation during a pre-game ceremony in which he received his 2015 championship <a href="/wiki/NBA_Championship_ring" title="NBA Championship ring">ring</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Lee missed the Mavericks' first two playoff games against the Oklahoma City Thunder due to a right foot injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="San_Antonio_Spurs_.282016.E2.80.93present.29">San Antonio Spurs (2016–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: San Antonio Spurs (2016–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 2, 2016, Lee signed with the <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On October 25, the season opener, he made his debut for the Spurs in a 129–100 win over the former team <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a>. He recorded six points, six rebounds and two assists in 11 minutes off the bench.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_appearances">Television appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Television appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007 Lee appeared on the game show <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Feud" title="Family Feud">Family Feud</a></i>, in an NBA Players vs Mothers week, to raise money for charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On April 26, 2009, he appeared on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celebrity_Apprentice" title="Celebrity Apprentice">NBC's <i>Celebrity Apprentice</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Lee made a cameo in an episode of the now-canceled series <i><a href="/wiki/Lipstick_Jungle_(TV_series)" title="Lipstick Jungle (TV series)">Lipstick Jungle</a></i>, playing himself. In the 2009 movie <i><a href="/wiki/When_in_Rome_(2010_film)" title="When in Rome (2010 film)">When in Rome</a></i>, David Lee made a cameo appearance. He also appeared in a segment of the 2009 version of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Electric_Company_(2009_TV_series)" title="The Electric Company (2009 TV series)">The Electric Company</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee is a <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In 2009, Lee, along with several other NBA players, joined the Hoops for St. Jude charity program benefiting the St. Jude Children's Research Hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
