<p><b>David James Elliott</b> (born September 21, 1960) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">Canadian</a> actor who was the star of the series <i><a href="/wiki/JAG_(TV_series)" title="JAG (TV series)">JAG</a></i>, playing lead character <a href="/wiki/Harmon_Rabb" title="Harmon Rabb">Harmon Rabb Jr.</a> from 1995 to 2005.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_James_Elliott&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Elliott was born David William Smith<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> on September 21, 1960, in <a href="/wiki/Milton,_Ontario" title="Milton, Ontario">Milton, Ontario</a>, Canada, the son of Pat Farrow, an office manager, and Arnold Smith, a heating and plumbing wholesale contractor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was the middle child of three boys. During his teenaged years, he was part of a band, quitting <a href="/wiki/Milton_District_High_School" title="Milton District High School">Milton District High School</a> in his final year to pursue his dream of becoming a rock star. However, at age 19, he realized this would not happen and returned to finish high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After graduating from high school, he attended <a href="/wiki/Ryerson_University" title="Ryerson University">Ryerson University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, auditioned for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stratford_Festival_of_Canada" title="Stratford Festival of Canada">Stratford Shakespearean Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> and was accepted as a member of its Young Company.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_James_Elliott&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Subsequently moving to Los Angeles, he took the stage name David James Elliott, having found an actor was already named David Smith. He appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Police_Academy_3:_Back_in_Training" title="Police Academy 3: Back in Training">Police Academy 3: Back in Training</a></i> in 1986, and subsequently in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Legal_(Canadian_TV_series)" title="Street Legal (Canadian TV series)">Street Legal</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Knots_Landing" title="Knots Landing">Knots Landing</a></i> as Bill Nolan, and in 1993's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Untouchables_(1993_TV_series)" title="The Untouchables (1993 TV series)">The Untouchables</a></i> as Agent Paul Robbins. The following year, Elliott landed a recurring role on the hit series <i><a href="/wiki/Melrose_Place" title="Melrose Place">Melrose Place</a></i>, playing Terry Parsons, and a guest appearance in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a></i> as "Carl" the anti<a href="/wiki/Abortion" title="Abortion">abortion</a> furniture mover.</p>
<p>He married Canadian-born actress <a href="/wiki/Nanci_Chambers" title="Nanci Chambers">Nanci Chambers</a> in 1992. She also appeared in <i>JAG</i> as the ambitious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_JAG#Lieutenant_Loren_Singer.2C_USN_.28JAGC.29" title="Characters of JAG">Lt. Loren Singer</a>. They have a daughter, Stephanie (b. March 15, 1993 in Chicago) and a son, Wyatt (b. March 3, 2003 in Los Angeles). David and Nanci both became U.S. citizens.</p>
<p>In 1995, Elliott landed the role he would hold for 10 years, the role of naval aviator-turned JAG lawyer <a href="/wiki/Harmon_Rabb" title="Harmon Rabb">Harmon Rabb, Jr.</a> in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>/<a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> television drama <i><a href="/wiki/JAG_(TV_series)" title="JAG (TV series)">JAG</a></i>. His tenure on <i>JAG</i> ended in 2005 with the series finale.</p>
<p>In 1996, David Elliott starred in the made-for-cable movie <i>Holiday Affair</i> with <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Gibb" title="Cynthia Gibb">Cynthia Gibb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVguide_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVguide-4">[4]</a></sup> This is a remake of the 1949 classic <i><a href="/wiki/Holiday_Affair" title="Holiday Affair">Holiday Affair</a></i> which starred <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mitchum" title="Robert Mitchum">Robert Mitchum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Janet_Leigh" title="Janet Leigh">Janet Leigh</a>. In 2005, Elliott starred in a <a href="/wiki/Television_in_Canada" title="Television in Canada">Canadian</a> television movie <i>The Man Who Lost Himself</i>, which was based on the true story of <a href="/wiki/Terry_Evanshen" title="Terry Evanshen">Terry Evanshen</a>, a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Canadian_football" title="Canadian football">football</a> player who was in a car accident and lost his memory due to severe head injuries. <a href="/wiki/Nanci_Chambers" title="Nanci Chambers">Nanci Chambers</a> also appeared in this movie as a doctor. In October 2006, Elliott joined the cast of legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/Close_to_Home_(2005_TV_series)" title="Close to Home (2005 TV series)">Close to Home</a></i> (2005–2007) as Chief Deputy <a href="/wiki/Prosecutor" title="Prosecutor">Prosecutor</a> James Conlon. During 2008, he had a recurring role in the Canadian series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guard_(TV_series)" title="The Guard (TV series)">The Guard</a></i>. He starred in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sci_Fi_Channel_(United_States)" title="Sci Fi Channel (United States)">Sci Fi Channel</a>'s four-hour <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Knights_of_Bloodsteel" title="Knights of Bloodsteel">Knights of Bloodsteel</a></i> (2009). Elliott was cast as an Internet millionaire in <i>Terror Trap</i>, directed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dan Garcia (page does not exist)">Dan Garcia</a>. Elliott also starred in <i>Dad's Home</i> (2010), a widowed advertising executive who loses his job and becomes a stay-at-home dad for his two children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HollywoodReporter_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-HollywoodReporter-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 19, 2010, Elliott replaced <a href="/wiki/Neal_McDonough" title="Neal McDonough">Neal McDonough</a> as the male lead in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Scoundrels_(TV_series)" title="Scoundrels (TV series)">Scoundrels</a></i>. In 2010, Elliott was cast as FBI Agent Russ Josephson on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_NY" title="CSI: NY">CSI: NY</a></i>, a recurring role as Detective <a href="/wiki/Jo_Danville" title="Jo Danville">Jo Danville</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Sela_Ward" title="Sela Ward">Sela Ward</a>) ex-husband, a role which he first portrayed in January, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Elliott also co-starred as Ripp Cockburn, the husband of <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Chenoweth" title="Kristin Chenoweth">Kristin Chenoweth</a>'s character Carlene, on ABC's comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/GCB_(TV_series)" title="GCB (TV series)">GCB</a></i>. In 2014, Elliott appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Men_(season_7)" title="Mad Men (season 7)">Mad Men (season 7)</a></i> as Dave Wooster. In 2015, Elliott guest starred in the TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(TV_series)" title="Scorpion (TV series)">Scorpion</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2015, Elliott played actor <a href="/wiki/John_Wayne" title="John Wayne">John Wayne</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Trumbo_(2015_film)" title="Trumbo (2015 film)">Trumbo</a></i>.</p>
