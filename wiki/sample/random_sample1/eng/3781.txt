<p><b>David Choi</b> (born March 22, 1986) is an American musician and YouTube video producer based in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allkpop_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allkpop-1">[1]</a></sup> Choi's original songs and pop covers have been heard on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>, FOX, <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>, <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26E_(TV_channel)" title="A&amp;E (TV channel)">A&amp;E</a>, <a href="/wiki/E!" title="E!">E!</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travel_Channel" title="Travel Channel">Travel Channel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Style_Network" title="Style Network">Style Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_Network" title="Food Network">Food Network</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a>, as well as in commercials internationally. He has worked with companies such as <a href="/wiki/Kellogg%27s" title="Kellogg's">Kellogg's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starburst_(confectionery)" title="Starburst (confectionery)">Starburst</a>, the <a href="/wiki/American_Cancer_Society" title="American Cancer Society">American Cancer Society</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._C._Penney" title="J. C. Penney">J. C. Penney</a>, and Asian online fashion and beauty retailer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YesStyle" title="YesStyle">YesStyle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chang_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chang-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yang_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yang-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Santiago_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Santiago-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Quinton_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Quinton-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Choi released his debut album, <i>Only You</i>, in October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-last.fm_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-last.fm-6">[6]</a></sup> His second album, <i>By My Side</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> was released on May 19, 2010, and his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_and_Ever_(David_Choi_album)" title="Forever and Ever (David Choi album)">Forever and Ever</a></i> was released on October 25, 2011.</p>
<p>As of December 3, 2012, David Choi's YouTube Channel had 929,061 subscribers, 117,774,875 total video views, and more than 13 million channel views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> As of November 2011, he was YouTube's 15th-most-subscribed musician and 62nd-most-subscribed user overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-subs_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-subs-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> As of February 3, 2015, he has 138,238 followers on Twitter account.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Choi previously worked as a songwriter and producer at <a href="/wiki/Warner/Chappell_Music" title="Warner/Chappell Music">Warner/Chappell Music</a>, which signed him on after his participation in an <a href="/wiki/American_Society_of_Composers,_Authors_and_Publishers" title="American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers">American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers</a> (ASCAP) workshop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allkpop_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-allkpop-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>His humorous song "YouTube, A Love Song" has received over 2.8 million views. The music videos for his singles "Won't Even Start" and "That Girl", which are posted on both his and producers <a href="/wiki/Wong_Fu_Productions" title="Wong Fu Productions">Wong Fu Productions</a>' YouTube channels, has received a total of over 5 million views. He also posts non-music <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vlog" title="Vlog">vlogs</a> on his second channel. Choi often appears in online videos with fellow YouTube personalities, mostly playing a humorous character with a straight face. YouTube personalities, like <a href="/wiki/Shane_Dawson" title="Shane Dawson">Shane Dawson</a>, Eric Feng, and <a href="/wiki/Wong_Fu_Productions" title="Wong Fu Productions">Wong Fu Productions</a>, often use Choi's music in their videos.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Childhood_and_education">Childhood and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Childhood and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Choi was born in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a>, on March 22, 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_American" title="Korean American">of Korean descent</a> and comes from a musical family: His parents, Ray and Jane Choi, own a music store named "Grace Store," and his father plays the <a href="/wiki/Autoharp" title="Autoharp">autoharp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Story_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-My_Story-13">[13]</a></sup> With an artistic mother and musical father, Choi was surrounded by music growing up and does not think he would have pursued music today without these influences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>At a young age, Choi was required to play the piano and violin, an experience that he did not enjoy. In fact, David would get in trouble by his parents for not practicing. He said that his parents would yell at him to "Go practice in [his] room for 30 minutes or [he would be] in big trouble!!!!" Choi claims that if he did not practice he would get physically punished. He does not remember much about practicing his violin; however, he does recall wanting to smash it several times. The Internet star says that his hate for practicing made him hate everything about the violin and piano to the point where he even began to hate music itself. Even though it was not his favorite thing to do, Choi does admit that it was nice when he got to leave class twice a week to attend his orchestra class. David says that class was easier for him than it was for some of the other kids because he had already covered some of the material through his private lessons. The unlikely musician thus became the concertmaster of his school orchestra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Story_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-My_Story-13">[13]</a></sup> He would play classical and jazz music, and never being exposed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop_(music)" title="Pop (music)">pop</a> song until sixth grade, when he heard <a href="/wiki/Smash_Mouth" title="Smash Mouth">Smash Mouth</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/All_Star_(song)" title="All Star (song)">All Star</a>". Soon after, he discovered "<a href="/wiki/All_the_Small_Things" title="All the Small Things">All the Small Things</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Blink-182" title="Blink-182">blink-182</a> and attempted to play the song on guitar. David began to listen to more and more pop songs and fell in love with the <a href="/wiki/Backstreet_Boys" title="Backstreet Boys">Backstreet Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boyz_II_Men" title="Boyz II Men">Boyz II Men</a>, and Japanese pop. In junior high David would stay up late listening to the radio, recording the songs, and began to make his own mix tapes. David then began to write his own songs and his first instrumental piece was a song titled "Lost Memory", written on the piano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Even after discovering pop music David stayed in the school orchestra. Pressured by his parents, David was forced to audition for the Gifted And Talented Education (GATE) orchestra. David rehearsed with this orchestra every Tuesday.</p>
<p>Choi eventually stopped taking violin lessons in high school and commenced <a href="/wiki/Jazz_piano" title="Jazz piano">jazz piano</a> lessons in ninth grade. It is at this time that Choi began composing music, and won his first award after submitting an instrumental pop song for a contest.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_.282002.E2.80.9306.29">Early career (2002–06)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career (2002–06)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Choi's original compositions were recorded digitally with the use of a program called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acid_Pro" title="Acid Pro">Acid Pro</a>. Choi taught himself how to use the program within several days of downloading it,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Story_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-My_Story-13">[13]</a></sup> and he learned a few songwriting tips from a friend.</p>
<p>In 2004, Choi won the grand prize in <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s <i>Mashup</i> contest<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, where David Bowie himself chose the grand prize winner. David appeared in <i>USA Weekend Magazine</i> alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a>, after he received the grand prize for the magazine's <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon_Songwriting_Contest" title="John Lennon Songwriting Contest">John Lennon Songwriting Contest</a> for teens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-last.fm_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-last.fm-6">[6]</a></sup> These achievements marked the start of Choi's musical career: He was signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Chappell_Music" title="Warner Chappell Music">Warner Chappell Music</a>, where he was initially placed as a staff songwriter and producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Story_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-My_Story-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Choi's humorous YouTube love song, "YouTube (A Love Song)", was uploaded on December 30, 2006, and brought him "overnight" fame. Choi followed the success of "YouTube (A Love Song)" with other humorous songs, such as "It's Rad To Pick Your Nose" and "I Think I Like You": "My Company" is considered a "half-humorous, half-love" song. Unbeknownst to him, his YouTube love song was posted on the homepage of YouTube. He found out when a friend told him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Story_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-My_Story-13">[13]</a></sup> He soon received requests for new songs, a live performance, and an album. He wanted to release his songs in an album, but as he was already technically a signed songwriter/producer at that time: He had to go through legal procedures and accept a variety of contractual conditions. Thus, he left Warner and stayed unsigned to carry on with his own album release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Story_13-5"><a href="#cite_note-My_Story-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Only_You_.282007.E2.80.9308.29"><i>Only You</i> (2007–08)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Only You (2007–08)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Choi released his first album in 2008. Entitled <i>Only You</i>, it contained mainly love songs, notably "Love", "Won't Even Start", "Something To Believe," and "Only You". In 2009, he released a Korean special edition of the album, containing three bonus tracks. "Won't Even Start" became his hit single<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Naming_conventions_(geographic_names)" title="Wikipedia:Naming conventions (geographic names)"><span title="The geographic scope near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2013)">where?</span></a></i>]</sup>, and he released a music video for it, collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Wong_Fu_Productions" title="Wong Fu Productions">Wong Fu Productions</a> in producing his first <b>official</b> music video.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_My_Side_.282009.E2.80.9310.29"><i>By My Side</i> (2009–10)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By My Side (2009–10)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On February 21, 2009, he performed at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kollaboration_(talent_show)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kollaboration (talent show) (page does not exist)">annual Kollaboration</a> talent show,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allkpop_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-allkpop-1">[1]</a></sup> and within a space of half a year (4th quarter 2009 to 1st Quarter 2010), David compiled composed songs into his second album, <i>By My Side,</i> released on May 18, 2010. He released a music video for its lead single "That Girl" on the same day. The music video was produced by him and his friends via <a href="/wiki/Wong_Fu_Productions" title="Wong Fu Productions">Wong Fu Productions</a>. It starred Julia Chang as 'that girl' opposite him, and it was his second collaboration with the production company, and<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WongFu_New_Music_Video_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-WongFu_New_Music_Video-16">[16]</a></sup> He also released a Korean edition, with two bonus tracks: "Valentines" and "My Company". On January 14, 2014, he released the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a>-English version of <i>By My Side</i> with Indonesian singer <a href="/wiki/Maudy_Ayunda" title="Maudy Ayunda">Maudy Ayunda</a>. <p>During his 2011 tours, Choi sang two songs he wrote the year before, "You Were My Friend" and "This and That is Life". He has said that a third album will include these two songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On July 7, 2011, David released his third music video, for his single "By My Side", once again a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Wong_Fu_Productions" title="Wong Fu Productions">Wong Fu Productions</a>. David also started to help his father, Ray Choi, in uploading YouTube videos of him playing the <a href="/wiki/Autoharp" title="Autoharp">autoharp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> His song "I Choose Happiness", of which the instrumental version is used in <a href="/wiki/KevJumba" title="KevJumba">KevJumba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wong_Fu_Productions" title="Wong Fu Productions">Wong Fu Productions</a> videos, was also chosen to be used in the 2011 film <i><a href="/wiki/Lucky_(2011_film)" title="Lucky (2011 film)">Lucky</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Colin_Hanks" title="Colin Hanks">Colin Hanks</a>. It was also featured in an episode of <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Make_It_or_Break_It" title="Make It or Break It">Make It or Break It</a></i>. David Choi released his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_and_Ever_(David_Choi_album)" title="Forever and Ever (David Choi album)">Forever and Ever</a></i> on October 25, 2011.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tours">Tours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Choi's first tour,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_tour_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-first_tour-20">[20]</a></sup> from November 12 to 19, 2009, was in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_West_Coast" title="United States West Coast">West Coast</a> cities of <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, Los Angeles, San Francisco, <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, as well as in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver, British Columbia</a>, Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tour_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-tour-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2010 Choi embarked on his second tour singing live in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_East_Coast" title="United States East Coast">East Coast</a> cities of Washington DC, New York, <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, and Chicago as well as <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto, Ontario</a>, Canada. The year after, he visited those few cities again.</p>
<p>In August 2010, David Choi went to Singapore for vacation and to tour. He was there for eleven days, and on his vacation day, he went to <a href="/wiki/Sentosa" title="Sentosa">Sentosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Night_Safari,_Singapore" title="Night Safari, Singapore">Night Safari</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-David_Choi_in_Singapore_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-David_Choi_in_Singapore-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2011, he went on a short tour around North America again: "Spring Tour" included <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Chicago, and Hawaii. He also frequented <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>.</p>
<p>In summer 2011, David announced another tour – his first continental Asia-Pacific tour (with a focus on Southeast Asia). He went to Hong Kong, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, Singapore, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> and Australia. In Australia, David was supported by Australian acts <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Jae" title="Amelia Jae">Amelia Jae</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jayesslee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jayesslee (page does not exist)">Jayesslee</a>. In Singapore, he had <a href="/wiki/Inch_Chua" title="Inch Chua">Inch Chua</a> do introductory songs and sing his and Kina Grannis' "My Time With You". Again on this tour, David went to small venues, like the 245-capacity Esplanade Recital Studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Asia_Pacific_Tour_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Asia_Pacific_Tour-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>On his tours, David has meet-and-greet sessions with his fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2012, David announced his Forever and Ever USA/Canada tour. He went to 20 cities across the US and Canada, starting in Phoenix on February 2 and ending in Chicago on April 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In May, David started on his third tour to the Asia-Pacific: <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a>, Sydney, <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>, Kuala Lumpur, Hong Kong, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> and Tokyo. In September 2012, he and <a href="/wiki/Clara_Chung" title="Clara Chung">Clara Chung</a> embarked on their Fall Tour presented by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YesStyle" title="YesStyle">YesStyle</a>, visiting 19 cities all over the US and Canada, starting in Los Angeles and ending in <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genre_and_lyrics">Genre and lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Genre and lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>David Choi's music genre falls mostly under the modern "<a href="/wiki/Ballad" title="Ballad">ballad</a>" genre, his songs being slow, mostly about love and relationships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-video2.channelnewsasia.com_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-video2.channelnewsasia.com-26">[26]</a></sup> He describes his own music as "poppy with a hint of jazz and folk."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> His songs have been described as "often to melt girls' hearts."</p>
<p>He often uses happy melodies, for example in "Something to Believe"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and "My Time with You".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_image">Public image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Choi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Public image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Choi has been portrayed by "<a href="/wiki/Netizen" title="Netizen">netizens</a>" and fans as sad and solemn person, with a poker face: Such perceptions were bolstered by the absence of images of Choi smiling. In response to such claims, Choi publicly promised that he would not smile until he reached 77,777 subscribers on YouTube (this goal was subsequently accomplished).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
