<p><b>David Paymer</b> (born August 30, 1954) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_director" title="Television director">television director</a>. He has been in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Saturday_Night" title="Mr. Saturday Night">Mr. Saturday Night</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quiz_Show_(1994_film)" title="Quiz Show (1994 film)">Quiz Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Searching_for_Bobby_Fischer" title="Searching for Bobby Fischer">Searching for Bobby Fischer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/City_Slickers" title="City Slickers">City Slickers</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%D1%8Fazy_People" title="Cяazy People">Crazy People</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/State_and_Main" title="State and Main">State and Main</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Payback_(1999_film)" title="Payback (1999 film)">Payback</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Shorty" title="Get Shorty">Get Shorty</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carpool_(film)" title="Carpool (film)">Carpool</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_American_President_(film)" title="The American President (film)">The American President</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_Thirteen" title="Ocean's Thirteen">Ocean's Thirteen</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Drag_Me_to_Hell" title="Drag Me to Hell">Drag Me to Hell</a></i>. Paymer was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor</a> in 1992 for <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Saturday_Night" title="Mr. Saturday Night">Mr. Saturday Night</a>.</i> He played the lead role as the Boss in <i><a href="/wiki/Bartleby_(2001_film)" title="Bartleby (2001 film)">Bartleby</a></i>, an adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Herman_Melville" title="Herman Melville">Herman Melville</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Bartleby,_the_Scrivener" title="Bartleby, the Scrivener">Bartleby, the Scrivener</a>." He played a <a href="/wiki/Mafia" title="Mafia">mob</a> boss in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Line_of_Fire_(2003_TV_series)" title="Line of Fire (2003 TV series)">Line of Fire</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Paymer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paymer was born in <a href="/wiki/Oceanside,_New_York" title="Oceanside, New York">Oceanside, New York</a>, the son of Sylvia, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Travel_agent" title="Travel agent">travel agent</a>, and Marvin Paymer, a <a href="/wiki/Pianist" title="Pianist">pianist</a> and musical director<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> who originally worked in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scrap_metal" title="Scrap metal">scrap metal</a> business. The two divorced in 1973.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Paymer is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jewish</a>; his mother was born in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> and left for the United States because of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi" title="Nazi">Nazi</a> occupation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Paymer has a brother, Steve, and graduated from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">BA</a> in theater and psychology. Paymer is married to Liz Georges, with whom he has two children.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Paymer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paymer's acting career began in 1979 with the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_In-Laws_(1979_film)" title="The In-Laws (1979 film)">The In-Laws</a></i>. Paymer has appeared in over 90 films in his career. He has also appeared in many popular television shows, including <i><a href="/wiki/Taxi_(TV_series)" title="Taxi (TV series)">Taxi</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Days" title="Happy Days">Happy Days</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/L.A._Law" title="L.A. Law">L.A. Law</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_Whisperer" title="Ghost Whisperer">Ghost Whisperer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i>. He has had recurring roles on <i><a href="/wiki/Cheers" title="Cheers">Cheers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Murphy_Brown" title="Murphy Brown">Murphy Brown</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Larry_Sanders_Show" title="The Larry Sanders Show">The Larry Sanders Show</a></i>. He has also appeared in such films as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amistad_(1997_film)" title="Amistad (1997 film)">Amistad</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/City_Hall_(film)" title="City Hall (film)">City Hall</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/RFK_(film)" title="RFK (film)">RFK</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_West_(miniseries)" title="Into the West (miniseries)">Into the West</a></i>. Paymer has also dabbled into the world of animation, lending his voice to the character "Sheldon" in the <a href="/wiki/Kids%27_WB" title="Kids' WB">Kids' WB</a> cartoon series <i><a href="/wiki/Channel_Umptee-3" title="Channel Umptee-3">Channel Umptee-3</a></i> and in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balto_3:_Wings_of_Change" title="Balto 3: Wings of Change">Balto 3: Wings of Change</a> as neurotic <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Terrier" title="Welsh Terrier">Welsh Terrier</a> Mel.</p>
<p>Paymer has directed episodes of several popular television series, including <i><a href="/wiki/Gilmore_Girls" title="Gilmore Girls">Gilmore Girls</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Tree_Hill_(TV_series)" title="One Tree Hill (TV series)">One Tree Hill</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Melrose_Place_(2009_TV_series)" title="Melrose Place (2009 TV series)">Melrose Place</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Everwood" title="Everwood">Everwood</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_%26_Sisters_(2006_TV_series)" title="Brothers &amp; Sisters (2006 TV series)">Brothers &amp; Sisters</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bunheads" title="Bunheads">Bunheads</a></i>. He also appears in a cameo for an AT&amp;T commercial in 2011.</p>
