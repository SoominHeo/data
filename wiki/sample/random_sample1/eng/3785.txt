<p><b>David Taylor Price</b> (born August 26, 1985) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). Price was selected <a href="/wiki/List_of_first_overall_Major_League_Baseball_draft_picks" title="List of first overall Major League Baseball draft picks">first overall</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_Draft" title="2007 Major League Baseball Draft">2007 Major League Baseball Draft</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</a> and made his MLB debut in September 2008. He has also played for the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>.</p>
<p>Price pitched out of the <a href="/wiki/Bullpen" title="Bullpen">bullpen</a> during the Rays run through the <a href="/wiki/2008_Major_League_Baseball_season#Postseason" title="2008 Major League Baseball season">2008 playoffs</a>. Just weeks after his first appearance in the big leagues, he earned a memorable <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">save</a> in Game 7 of the <a href="/wiki/2008_American_League_Championship_Series" title="2008 American League Championship Series">ALCS</a>, helping his club reach their first <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a>. Price became a full-time <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting pitcher</a> in 2009. In his second full season, he was named the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> starter for the <a href="/wiki/2010_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2010 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">2010 All-Star Game</a> and finished second in the voting for the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a>. He won his first Cy Young Award in 2012 after a tight race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Rays traded Price to the Tigers during the 2014 season. When the Tigers fell out of the postseason race in 2015, they traded him to the Blue Jays, who won their division and advanced to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ALCS" title="ALCS">ALCS</a>. That following off-season, the Boston Red Sox signed Price to a seven-year, $217 million contract.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>David Taylor Price was born in <a href="/wiki/Murfreesboro,_Tennessee" title="Murfreesboro, Tennessee">Murfreesboro, Tennessee</a>, to Bonnie and Debbie Price.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup> He has two brothers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup> He began playing <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> in his early childhood. He grew up as an <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> fan and idolized <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Justice" title="David Justice">David Justice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davidson_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Davidson-3">[3]</a></sup> He attended Cooperstown Dreams Park when he was a teenager. As a teenager, he was a standout on the <a href="/wiki/Blackman_High_School" title="Blackman High School">Blackman High School</a> baseball and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-priceright_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-priceright-4">[4]</a></sup> In his high school career, he compiled a 0.43 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">earned run average</a> (ERA) and 151 <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a>. He was the recipient of the Rutherford County MVP Pitcher (2003 and 2004), the Co-District 7AAA Pitcher of the Year with Michael Alcorn his senior season, and the Rutherford County Male Athlete of the Year in 2002, 2003 and 2004. He played in the 2004 High School All-America Game in <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque,_New_Mexico" title="Albuquerque, New Mexico">Albuquerque, New Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though Price was drafted in the 19th round by the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> out of high school, he did not sign, and instead chose to attend <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_University" title="Vanderbilt University">Vanderbilt University</a> on an academic scholarship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He pitched for <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_Commodores_baseball" title="Vanderbilt Commodores baseball">the baseball team</a> for three seasons.</p>

<p>In his freshman season in 2005, Price was honored as a Freshman All-American by both <i><a href="/wiki/Baseball_America" title="Baseball America">Baseball America</a></i> and Collegiate Baseball. That year he went 2–4 with a 2.86 ERA in 19 games, compiling 92 strikeouts in 69<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings pitched.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup> Still, frustrated with his college workload, Price almost quit baseball to work at a <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a> in his hometown but was talked out of it by his coach, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Corbin" title="Tim Corbin">Tim Corbin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, Price posted a 9–5 record with a 4.16 ERA in 110<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings pitched. He set a school single-season record in strikeouts with 155 while <a href="/wiki/Base_on_balls" title="Base on balls">walking</a> only 43 batters. Over a span of six starts early in the season, he recorded 10 or more strikeouts each game, including a 17-strikeout performance in a game against <a href="/wiki/University_of_Arkansas" title="University of Arkansas">Arkansas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup> That year, he was one of five finalists for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Spikes_Award" title="Golden Spikes Award">Golden Spikes Award</a> and a semifinalist for the <a href="/wiki/Roger_Clemens_Award" title="Roger Clemens Award">Roger Clemens Award</a>. He was also named to the third-team All-American by the National Collegiate Baseball Writers Association, first-team All-South Region by the <a href="/wiki/American_Baseball_Coaches_Association" title="American Baseball Coaches Association">American Baseball Coaches Association</a> and second-team All-<a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Conference" title="Southeastern Conference">SEC</a> by the coaches in that conference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a junior he tallied an 11–1 record with a 2.63 ERA. He was the nation's top strikeout pitcher, striking out 194 batters over 133<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings, breaking his own school record. His last start against SEC competition came against Mississippi State in the SEC Baseball Tournament. Price pitched a complete game, going nine innings while giving up five hits, two runs, and striking out 11 batters with no walks. His last start at the college level came against Austin Peay in Vanderbilt's first game in the 2007 Nashville Regional. Against Austin Peay, Price retired 17 batters via the strikeout in nine innings of work while yielding just five hits, one run, and two walks. Price won several major national awards, including college baseball's top honor, the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Dick_Howser_Trophy" title="Dick Howser Trophy">Dick Howser Trophy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup> and the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Brooks_Wallace_Award" title="Brooks Wallace Award">Brooks Wallace Award</a>. David Price was the first player to have a clean sweep of all the college baseball awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_National_Team">United States National Team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States National Team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the summer of 2005, Price pitched for the <a href="/wiki/USA_Baseball" title="USA Baseball">United States National Team</a>. He went 2–0 with a 1.26 ERA in five games, tallying 39 strikeouts and 13 walks over 28<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings. On August 2, 2005, he pitched a <a href="/wiki/Complete_game" title="Complete game">complete game</a> <a href="/wiki/Shutout" title="Shutout">shutout</a> over <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, only allowing five <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hits</a>. In the summer of 2006, Price helped the United States take home the gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/World_University_Baseball_Championship" title="World University Baseball Championship">World University Baseball Championship</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. In eight starts for the United States, Price was 5–1 with a 0.20 ERA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vu_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-vu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Draft_and_minor_league_career">Draft and minor league career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Draft and minor league career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Price signed his first professional contract on August 15, 2007. The six-year contract was worth $11.25 million ($8.5 million guaranteed), including a $5.6 million signing bonus. After the contract was approved by the MLB, he was added to the Devil Rays <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/40-man_roster" title="40-man roster">40-man roster</a> before being optioned to the minor leagues to begin his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The total value of Price’s bonus was the largest in draft history. The signing bonus was second-largest in draft history, behind only the $6.1 million <a href="/wiki/Justin_Upton" title="Justin Upton">Justin Upton</a> received from the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> as the top overall pick in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_MLB_Draft" title="2005 MLB Draft">2005 MLB Draft</a>. The guaranteed value was the third-highest in draft history, trailing only <a href="/wiki/Mark_Prior" title="Mark Prior">Mark Prior</a> ($10.5 million) and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Teixeira" title="Mark Teixeira">Mark Teixeira</a> ($9.5 million) from the 2001 draft. Price made his <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> debut against the Yankees, <a href="/wiki/Hit_by_pitch" title="Hit by pitch">hitting</a> the first batter and striking out the next three in one inning of work. His <a href="/wiki/Fastball" title="Fastball">fastball</a> averaged 98 mph.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Price was first assigned to pitch for the <a href="/wiki/Vero_Beach_Devil_Rays" title="Vero Beach Devil Rays">Vero Beach Devil Rays</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_A-Advanced" title="Class A-Advanced">Class A-Advanced</a> <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_League" title="Florida State League">Florida State League</a>. He started six games for Vero Beach, compiling a record of 4–0. He had an ERA of 1.82 with 37 strikeouts in 34<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings pitched.</p>
<p>In his second start in Vero Beach, Price pitched against <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Pedro Martínez">Pedro Martínez</a>. After the game, Martínez said of Price:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>He's amazing, that kid. He's amazing... that kid is very mature for his time in [the pros], and very talented. That kid did a hell of a job of throwing first-pitch strikes and pounding the strike zone and jamming hitters. I was watching that. He did it like a big leaguer. He had such a command. Right there, I'm challenging you. I'm going to do what I gotta do without any fear. That's the kind of talent you love to see.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>After moving up through the Rays minor league system to the <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_Biscuits" title="Montgomery Biscuits">Montgomery Biscuits</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_AA" title="Class AA">Class AA</a> <a href="/wiki/Southern_League_(baseball)" title="Southern League (baseball)">Southern League</a> in July, Price was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Durham_Bulls" title="Durham Bulls">Durham Bulls</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_AAA" title="Class AAA">Class AAA</a> <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a> on August 9, 2008. After compiling an 11–0 combined record at Single-A and Double-A, Price received his first professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loss_(baseball)" title="Loss (baseball)">loss</a> in his first start at Triple-A Durham.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tampa_Bay_Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tampa Bay Rays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_season">2008 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2008 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Price made his major league debut with the Tampa Bay Rays on September 14, 2008, against the <a href="/wiki/2008_New_York_Yankees_season" title="2008 New York Yankees season">Yankees</a>. The first MLB hit he allowed was a home run by <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> shortstop <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jeter" title="Derek Jeter">Derek Jeter</a>; Jeter would later record his 3,000th hit by again homering off Price in 2011. Price pitched 5<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings of <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">relief</a>; he made his first major league start with the Rays on September 22, 2008, against the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a>. He was the winning pitcher in Game 2 of the <a href="/wiki/2008_American_League_Championship_Series" title="2008 American League Championship Series">2008 ALCS</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>, earning a post-season victory before winning a regular season game. Price recorded the final four outs in Game 7 of the 2008 ALCS, earning his first career save (regular season or postseason; postseason statistics are recorded separately) and eliminating the defending champion Red Sox. Price then pitched 2<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings in Game 2 of the <a href="/wiki/2008_World_Series" title="2008 World Series">2008 World Series</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a>; he gave up two earned runs but nevertheless earned his second postseason save. The next day, Price introduced <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> at a campaign rally in Tampa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_season">2009 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2009 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On May 30, 2009, Price collected his first major league regular season win in a 5–2 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> in which he struck out 11 in under 6 innings. He finished the season with a 10–7 record, a 4.42 ERA, 102 strikeouts, and 54 walks in 23 starts.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_season">2010 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2010 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the 2010 season, Price was the first American League pitcher to reach 10 wins, on June 15. On that date, he was 10–2 and led the league with a 2.31 ERA. Price was selected as the American League starting pitcher for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_MLB_All-Star_Game" title="2010 MLB All-Star Game">2010 All-Star Game</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>He finished the 2010 season tied for second in the American League (with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Lester" title="Jon Lester">Jon Lester</a>) in wins (19) and in third place in ERA (2.72). He was eighth in the league in <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a> with 188.</p>
<p>He lost Games 1 and 5 of the <a href="/wiki/2010_American_League_Division_Series" title="2010 American League Division Series">2010 American League Division Series</a> against <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Lee" title="Cliff Lee">Cliff Lee</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_season">2011 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2011 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During a game against the Yankees on July 9, 2011, Price allowed a home run to <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jeter" title="Derek Jeter">Derek Jeter</a>, which was Jeter's 3,000th career hit. Price finished 2011 with a 12–13 record, 218 strikeouts, and a 3.49 ERA in 34 games started.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_season:_Cy_Young_Award_season">2012 season: Cy Young Award season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2012 season: Cy Young Award season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2012, Price went 20–5 on the season (tying for the league lead in wins) with a league-leading 2.54 ERA, two main reasons why he won the AL <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a>. Price pitched a complete game shutout against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels" title="Los Angeles Angels">Los Angeles Angels</a> on April 24, 2012, with the final score 5–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Price pitched in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_MLB_All-Star_Game" title="2012 MLB All-Star Game">2012 MLB All-Star Game</a>. Price got his 20th win against the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> on September 30, making him the first 20-win pitcher in Rays franchise history. In recording the win, he became the youngest American League pitcher (aged 27) to earn 20 wins in a season since 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2013, Price went 10-8 on the season with a 3.33 ERA, and had an American League leading and career-best four complete games. In the <a href="/wiki/2013_American_League_Wild_Card_tie-breaker_game" title="2013 American League Wild Card tie-breaker game">2013 American League Wild Card tie-breaker</a> against the Texas Rangers, Price pitched a complete game to lead the Rays into the postseason.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_season">2014 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2014 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>From June 4 to 25, Price had five consecutive starts with at least ten strikeouts, becoming only the eighth pitcher in major league history to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 6, Price was named to his fourth All-Star team; he did not participate in this All-Star game due to pitching in a game two days prior, avoiding pitching on only one day of rest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Rodney" title="Fernando Rodney">Fernando Rodney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before a July 31 trade to the Detroit Tigers, Price had a record of 11–8, with a 3.11 ERA, 189 strikeouts, and 23 walks in 23 starts with the Rays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Detroit_Tigers">Detroit Tigers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Detroit Tigers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_season_with_the_Tigers">2014 season with the Tigers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2014 season with the Tigers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On July 31, Price was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a> in a three-team deal that sent <a href="/wiki/Drew_Smyly" title="Drew Smyly">Drew Smyly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Franklin_(baseball)" title="Nick Franklin (baseball)">Nick Franklin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Willy_Adames" title="Willy Adames">Willy Adames</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Austin_Jackson" title="Austin Jackson">Austin Jackson</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In his Tigers debut against the New York Yankees on August 5, Price pitched 8 <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings, allowing three runs, striking out ten, and walking none.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 21, in his first game against his former team, the Tampa Bay Rays, Price allowed one hit and an unearned run with one out in the first inning, then retired the final 23 batters he faced, but suffered a complete game loss. Price is the first pitcher in the major leagues to lose a complete game, one-hitter or better, with no earned runs since <a href="/wiki/Andy_Hawkins" title="Andy Hawkins">Andy Hawkins</a> lost a no-hitter for the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> on July 1, 1990. It was the first time since 1914 that a pitcher has lost a complete game, one-hit, no walk start without allowing an earned run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 27, in a game against the Yankees, Price allowed eight runs and twelve hits, including nine straight in the third inning of an 8–4 loss. He is the first pitcher since <a href="/wiki/Bob_Forsch" title="Bob Forsch">Bob Forsch</a> in 1989 to allow nine straight hits. Only three previous times in major league history had a pitcher allowed 12 hits in two innings or fewer. The others being <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Estes" title="Shawn Estes">Shawn Estes</a> in 2003, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Podres" title="Johnny Podres">Johnny Podres</a> in 1963 and <a href="/wiki/George_Uhle" title="George Uhle">George Uhle</a> in 1929.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the Tigers, Price went 4–4 over the final two months of the season, with a 3.59 ERA and 82 strikeouts in <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">77<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings. On September 28, the last day of the regular season, Price pitched <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">7<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> shutout innings against the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a>, giving up four hits, striking out eight, and earning the 3–0 win, to help the Tigers clinch their fourth consecutive AL Central Division title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Price finished the 2014 regular season with a 15–12 record, 3.26 ERA and 1.08 WHIP. He led all of major league baseball in innings pitched (<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">248<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span>), batters faced (1,009) and strikeouts (271).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_season">2015 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 2015 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On January 16, 2015, Price and the Tigers avoided arbitration agreeing on a $19.75 million salary for the 2015 season, setting a record for the largest one-year deal for an arbitration-eligible player prior to free agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Price was named the Tigers opening day starter for 2015 by manager <a href="/wiki/Brad_Ausmus" title="Brad Ausmus">Brad Ausmus</a>, ending teammate <a href="/wiki/Justin_Verlander" title="Justin Verlander">Justin Verlander</a>'s streak of seven consecutive opening day starts. He responded by pitching <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">8<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> shutout innings in the Tigers 4–0 win over the Minnesota Twins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 6, 2015, Price was named to the <a href="/wiki/2015_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2015 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">2015 All-Star Game</a>, his fifth such honor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Price pitched a scoreless fourth inning in the game, striking out two, and earning the win for the AL.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He entered the All-Star Break with a 9–2 record, 2.38 ERA and 115 strikeouts.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toronto_Blue_Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Toronto Blue Jays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_Season_with_Blue_Jays">2015 Season with Blue Jays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2015 Season with Blue Jays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On July 30, 2015, Price was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a> for <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Norris_(baseball)" title="Daniel Norris (baseball)">Daniel Norris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Boyd_(baseball)" title="Matt Boyd (baseball)">Matt Boyd</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jairo_Labourt" title="Jairo Labourt">Jairo Labourt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Price made his debut for the Blue Jays on August 3. He threw 119 pitches through 8 innings, recording 11 strikeouts &amp; a winning decision, while yielding only 3 hits, 2 walks, and 1 earned run in a 5-1 victory over the Minnesota Twins at <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Centre" title="Rogers Centre">Rogers Centre</a>. His 11 strikeouts tied <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_(right-handed_pitcher)" title="José Núñez (right-handed pitcher)">José Núñez</a>'s team record for most strikeouts in a Blue Jays' pitching debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On September 5, Price earned his 100th career win, by beating the Baltimore Orioles 5–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> He was scratched from his final start of the season on October 1, as the Blue Jays had clinched the AL East the day prior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Price made 11 starts for the Blue Jays in 2015, and went 9–1 with a 2.30 ERA and 87 strikeouts in 74<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-BR-31">[31]</a></sup> Price started Game 1 of the <a href="/wiki/2015_American_League_Division_Series" title="2015 American League Division Series">ALDS</a> for the Blue Jays, taking the loss and bringing his career postseason record to 1–6. He would earn his second career postseason win on October 12, pitching 3 innings in relief of <a href="/wiki/R.A._Dickey" title="R.A. Dickey">R.A. Dickey</a>'s game 4 start.</p>
<p>Price finished the 2015 season with an 18–5 record and a 2.45 ERA. Price finished second in Cy Young Award balloting to <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Keuchel" title="Dallas Keuchel">Dallas Keuchel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boston_Red_Sox">Boston Red Sox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Boston Red Sox">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_Season">2016 Season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: 2016 Season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On December 4, 2015, Price signed a seven-year, $217 million contract with the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Price recorded a win in his debut with the Red Sox on April 5, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Price's first season was markedly different from the first to second half. In his first 18 starts, Price compiled an 8-6 record with a 4.64 ERA. He finished the season at 3.99 ERA with a 17-9 record. He led the AL with 230.0 IP and 227 hits allowed. 228 Strikeouts also marked his second highest in his career, after his 2014 season split between Tampa and Detroit.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scouting_report">Scouting report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Price_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Scouting report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Price uses a wide variety of pitches and complements them with excellent velocity. He throws two fastballs in the 93–96 mph range (tops out at 98 mph), a <a href="/wiki/Four-seam_fastball" title="Four-seam fastball">four-seamer</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Two-seam_fastball" title="Two-seam fastball">two-seamer</a>. His two-seamer is his lead pitch against right-handed and left-handed hitters, although he uses it more often against lefties. Price also throws a <a href="/wiki/Cutter_(baseball)" title="Cutter (baseball)">cutter</a> at 90-92 mph, used mainly against right-handed hitters (especially in 2-strike counts). He rounds out his repertoire with a <a href="/wiki/Changeup" title="Changeup">changeup</a> he uses mainly against right-handed hitters but also features to left-handed hitters (83–86) and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spike_curve" title="Spike curve">spike curveball</a> at 78–81.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brooks_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-brooks-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Price tends to set the pace early in games, taking 5–7 seconds to pitch the ball after it is returned by the catcher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davidson_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Davidson-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milb2007_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-milb2007-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
