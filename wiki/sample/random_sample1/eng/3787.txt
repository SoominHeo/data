<p><b>David William Huddleston</b> (September 17, 1930 – August 2, 2016) was an American actor. An <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nominee, Huddleston had a prolific television career, and appeared in many films including <i><a href="/wiki/Blazing_Saddles" title="Blazing Saddles">Blazing Saddles</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Crime_Busters" title="Crime Busters">Crime Busters</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Claus:_The_Movie" title="Santa Claus: The Movie">Santa Claus: The Movie</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Lebowski" title="The Big Lebowski">The Big Lebowski</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Huddleston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Huddleston was born in <a href="/wiki/Vinton,_Virginia" title="Vinton, Virginia">Vinton, Virginia</a>, the son of Ismay Hope (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Married_and_maiden_names" title="Married and maiden names">née</a> Dooley) and Lewis Melvin Huddleston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was briefly an officer in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> before beginning his formal education in acting at the prestigious <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Dramatic_Arts" title="American Academy of Dramatic Arts">American Academy of Dramatic Arts</a>. Huddleston attended <a href="/wiki/Fork_Union_Military_Academy" title="Fork Union Military Academy">Fork Union Military Academy</a> for high school (Class of 1949) and is listed among the school's prominent alumni.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Huddleston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Known mainly as a character actor, Huddleston starred in the title role of 1985's big-budget film <i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Claus:_The_Movie" title="Santa Claus: The Movie">Santa Claus: The Movie</a></i>, which featured a top-billed <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Moore" title="Dudley Moore">Dudley Moore</a> as an elf. One of Huddleston's first roles came in the 1968 drama <i><a href="/wiki/A_Lovely_Way_to_Die" title="A Lovely Way to Die">A Lovely Way to Die</a></i>. Shortly afterward the actor became a frequent guest star on several of the leading television series of the 1960s and 1970s, among them, <i><a href="/wiki/Adam-12" title="Adam-12">Adam-12</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Then_Came_Bronson" title="Then Came Bronson">Then Came Bronson</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gunsmoke" title="Gunsmoke">Gunsmoke</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bewitched" title="Bewitched">Bewitched</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bonanza" title="Bonanza">Bonanza</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cannon" title="Cannon">Cannon</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/McMillan_%26_Wife" title="McMillan &amp; Wife">McMillan &amp; Wife</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Waltons" title="The Waltons">The Waltons</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rookies" title="The Rookies">The Rookies</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Medical_Center_(TV_series)" title="Medical Center (TV series)">Medical Center</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Fu_(TV_series)" title="Kung Fu (TV series)">Kung Fu</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Emergency!" title="Emergency!">Emergency!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Spencer%27s_Pilots" title="Spencer's Pilots">Spencer's Pilots</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mary_Tyler_Moore_Show" title="The Mary Tyler Moore Show">The Mary Tyler Moore Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Police_Woman_(TV_series)" title="Police Woman (TV series)">Police Woman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-O" title="Hawaii Five-O">Hawaii Five-O</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Walker,_Texas_Ranger" title="Walker, Texas Ranger">Walker, Texas Ranger</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie%27s_Angels" title="Charlie's Angels">Charlie's Angels</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sanford_and_Son" title="Sanford and Son">Sanford and Son</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Practice_(1976_TV_series)" title="The Practice (1976 TV series)">The Practice</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rockford_Files" title="The Rockford Files">The Rockford Files</a></i>. He appeared in an unaired episode of the short-lived 1974 series <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Land_(TV_series)" title="The New Land (TV series)">The New Land</a></i> and in the episode "The Nomads" from the 1977 series <i><a href="/wiki/Quinn_Martin%27s_Tales_of_the_Unexpected" title="Quinn Martin's Tales of the Unexpected">Quinn Martin's Tales of the Unexpected</a></i> (known in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> as <i>Twist in the Tale</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He starred as J. T. Kallikak in the short-lived <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Situation_comedy" title="Situation comedy">situation comedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kallikaks" title="The Kallikaks">The Kallikaks</a></i> later in 1977, and in 1979 he played the title role in the short-lived situation comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Hizzonner" title="Hizzonner">Hizzonner</a></i> as a small-town mayor. Among Huddleston's notable feature film credits prior to <i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Claus:_The_Movie" title="Santa Claus: The Movie">Santa Claus: The Movie</a></i> are his co-starring roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Blazing_Saddles" title="Blazing Saddles">Blazing Saddles</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/McQ" title="McQ">McQ</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Klansman" title="The Klansman">The Klansman</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breakheart_Pass_(1975_film)" title="Breakheart Pass (1975 film)">Breakheart Pass</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Smokey_and_the_Bandit_II" title="Smokey and the Bandit II">Smokey and the Bandit II</a></i>.</p>
<p>Huddleston resumed his television career with roles in various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_movies" title="Television movies">television movies</a>, among them <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heat_Wave!_(1974_TV_Movie)" title="Heat Wave! (1974 TV Movie)">Heat Wave!</a></i> (1974); <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Oregon_Trail_(1976_TV_movie)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Oregon Trail (1976 TV movie) (page does not exist)">The Oregon Trail</a></i> (1976); <i>Shark Kill</i> (1976); <i>Kate Bliss and the Ticker Tape Kid</i> (1978); <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Reunion_(film)" title="Family Reunion (film)">Family Reunion</a></i> (1981); <i>Computercide</i> (1982); and <i>M.A.D.D.: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mothers_Against_Drunk_Drivers" title="Mothers Against Drunk Drivers">Mothers Against Drunk Drivers</a></i> (1983). For much of the 1980s, Huddleston also starred in a series of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_commercials" title="Television commercials">television commercials</a> for the Citrus Hill brand of <a href="/wiki/Orange_juice" title="Orange juice">orange juice</a>. Huddleston's post-<i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Claus:_The_Movie" title="Santa Claus: The Movie">Santa Claus</a></i> career has found him making occasional co-starring roles, in <i>Spot Marks the X</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Finnegan_Begin_Again" title="Finnegan Begin Again">Finnegan Begin Again</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Frantic_(film)" title="Frantic (film)">Frantic</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Life_with_Mikey" title="Life with Mikey">Life with Mikey</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Lebowski" title="The Big Lebowski">The Big Lebowski</a></i> in which he played the title role, and <i><a href="/wiki/G-Men_from_Hell" title="G-Men from Hell">G-Men from Hell</a></i>. Later, he also had a recurring role as Albert "Gramps" Arnold, the paternal grandfather of the protagonist in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wonder_Years" title="The Wonder Years">The Wonder Years</a></i>. Huddleston appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Next_Generation" title="Star Trek: The Next Generation">Star Trek: The Next Generation</a></i> as the train conductor in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Emergence_(Star_Trek:_The_Next_Generation)" title="Emergence (Star Trek: The Next Generation)">Emergence</a>". He also appeared twice on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_West_Wing_(TV_series)" title="The West Wing (TV series)">The West Wing</a></i> as Max Lobell, a Republican Senator who allies with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jed_Bartlet" title="Jed Bartlet">Jed Bartlet</a> on the issue of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campaign_finance_reform" title="Campaign finance reform">campaign finance reform</a>. His performance as Benjamin Franklin in a Boston stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/1776_(musical)" title="1776 (musical)">1776</a></i> is referenced in the book <i><a href="/wiki/Assassination_Vacation" title="Assassination Vacation">Assassination Vacation</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Vowell" title="Sarah Vowell">Sarah Vowell</a>, referring to him as "the actor who played The Big Lebowski in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Lebowski" title="The Big Lebowski">The Big Lebowski</a></i>." In 2009 he appeared in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Locker_13" title="Locker 13">Locker 13</a></i>. Huddleston was also featured in the 2010 special <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Always_Sunny_in_Philadelphia" title="It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia">It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia</a>: It's a Very Sunny Christmas</i> released directly to DVD, Blu-ray and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Online_distribution" title="Online distribution">Digital Download</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Huddleston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
