<p><b>David Hungate</b> (born August 5, 1948) is a bass guitarist, producer, and arranger noted as a member of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop-rock" title="Pop-rock">pop-rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Toto_(band)" title="Toto (band)">Toto</a> from 1977 to 1982 and rejoining in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Along with most of his Toto bandmates, Hungate did sessions on a number of hit albums of the 1970s, including <a href="/wiki/Boz_Scaggs" title="Boz Scaggs">Boz Scaggs</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Silk_Degrees" title="Silk Degrees">Silk Degrees</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Alice_Cooper" title="Alice Cooper">Alice Cooper</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/From_the_Inside_(Alice_Cooper_album)" title="From the Inside (Alice Cooper album)">From the Inside</a></i>.</p>
<p>Hungate played on Toto's first four records, including multiple Grammy award-winning album, <i><a href="/wiki/Toto_IV" title="Toto IV">Toto IV</a></i>. He left the band shortly after its release for a career as a session musician in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>. Hungate, who plays many instruments including guitar, trumpet, drums, and piano, has arranged, produced and recorded with several country artists such as <a href="/wiki/Chet_Atkins" title="Chet Atkins">Chet Atkins</a>. In 1998, Hungate rejoined his former TOTO bandmates in Europe for a series of live shows promoting Toto XX: 1977–1997, an archive release of previously unreleased studio &amp; live songs. He was also a primary member of <a href="/wiki/Album-oriented_rock" title="Album-oriented rock">AOR</a> <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> Mecca fronted by Joe Vana and <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Frederiksen" title="Dennis Frederiksen">Fergie Frederiksen</a>, the latter also of Toto fame. In 1990 he released a solo album entitled "Souvenir". <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Porcaro" title="Jeff Porcaro">Jeff Porcaro</a> played drums on some of the tracks on the album. In 1995, Hungate also played bass on all the songs on <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>'s second album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Woman_in_Me_(Shania_Twain_album)" title="The Woman in Me (Shania Twain album)">The Woman in Me</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></i></p>
<p>Hungate attended the College of Music at <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Texas" title="University of North Texas">North Texas State University</a>. He played bass in their jazz ensemble, the <a href="/wiki/One_O%27Clock_Lab_Band" title="One O'Clock Lab Band">One O'Clock Lab Band</a>, including a performance at the 1970 <a href="/wiki/Montreux_Jazz_Festival" title="Montreux Jazz Festival">Montreux Jazz Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is the son of the late US Congressman (and later Federal District Judge) <a href="/wiki/William_L._Hungate" title="William L. Hungate">William L Hungate</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2012, Hungate still resides in Nashville and is still an active and highly sought-after studio musician and on occasion tours with top acts such as <a href="/wiki/Vince_Gill" title="Vince Gill">Vince Gill</a>. As of 2014, he has rejoined <a href="/wiki/Toto_(band)" title="Toto (band)">Toto</a> in a touring capacity due to the departure of the touring bass player, Nathan East (who was, like <a href="/wiki/Leland_Sklar" title="Leland Sklar">Leland Sklar</a>, filling in for regular bassist <a href="/wiki/Mike_Porcaro" title="Mike Porcaro">Mike Porcaro</a> who was forced to retire due to illness). At first, it was announced that he would retire after the 2014 tour, but he will start 2015 touring with Toto, as stated by Lukather in October, due to Porcaro's death. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He also contributed some bass guitar on <a href="/wiki/Toto_XIV" title="Toto XIV">Toto XIV</a>.</p>
<p>In 2015, it was announced that David Hungate will no longer play with Toto. He will be replaced by <a href="/wiki/Leland_Sklar" title="Leland Sklar">Leland Sklar</a> when Toto tours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
