<p><b>Grace Martine Tandon</b> (born October 24, 1998), professionally known as <b>Daya</b> (pronounced <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="/./ syllable break">.</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>), is an American singer and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106.9_The_Q_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-106.9_The_Q-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She is signed to <a href="/wiki/Artbeatz" title="Artbeatz">Artbeatz</a>, Z Entertainment, and <a href="/wiki/RED_Distribution" title="RED Distribution">RED Distribution</a>, and released her self-titled debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP), <i><a href="/wiki/Daya_(EP)" title="Daya (EP)">Daya</a></i>, on September 4, 2015, which includes the song "<a href="/wiki/Hide_Away_(Daya_song)" title="Hide Away (Daya song)">Hide Away</a>", which has peaked at number 23 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iTunes_1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-iTunes_1-4">[4]</a></sup> She released her debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Sit_Still,_Look_Pretty_(album)" title="Sit Still, Look Pretty (album)">Sit Still, Look Pretty</a></i> on October 7, 2016. In 2017, Daya was nominated for the Grammy Award for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Dance_Recording" title="Best Dance Recording">Best Dance Recording</a> for her work on the song "Don't Let Me Down" by <a href="/wiki/The_Chainsmokers" title="The Chainsmokers">The Chainsmokers</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daya_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daya was born in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> and grew up in the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Mt._Lebanon_Township,_Allegheny_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Mt. Lebanon Township, Allegheny County, Pennsylvania">Mount Lebanon, Pennsylvania</a>. Her grandfather is <a href="/wiki/Indian_Americans" title="Indian Americans">Indian American</a>, and her stage name Daya (<small>Hindustani pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Hindi_and_Urdu" title="Help:IPA for Hindi and Urdu">[d̪əjɑː]</a></span>) is the word for "compassion" or "kindness".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In grade school she attended St. Bernard School, later she attended <a href="/wiki/Mt._Lebanon_School_District#Mt._Lebanon_High_School" title="Mt. Lebanon School District">Mt. Lebanon High School</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lebo_Mag_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lebo_Mag-6">[6]</a></sup> where she graduated in June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-South_Hills_Living_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-South_Hills_Living-7">[7]</a></sup> At the age of 3, Daya began to learn the piano, eventually converting to jazz piano at the age of eleven. By this time, she had also learned to play the guitar, ukulele, saxophone, and flute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lebo_Mag_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lebo_Mag-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At eleven, Daya enrolled as a student at the Accelerando Music Conservatory, owned by Christina Chirumbolo,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> in Pittsburgh. There, she met songwriter and producer Gino Barletta,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106.9_The_Q_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-106.9_The_Q-1">[1]</a></sup> a colleague of Chirumbolo who visited the school as a lecturer. Chirumbolo and Barletta founded INSIDE ACCESS, a music camp, the two worked with Daya eventually inviting her to Los Angeles in February 2015 to work on her original material.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daya_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daya's professional career began when her parents accompanied her to Los Angeles to work with Christina Chirumbolo and Gino Barletta, founders of INSIDE ACCESS by Accelerando, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leland_(Brett_McLaughlin)" title="Leland (Brett McLaughlin)">Brett McLaughlin</a>, Britten Newbill and Noisecastle III at <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Records" title="Paramount Records">Paramount Recording Studios</a>. It was during one of these writing sessions that her debut single "Hide Away" was written and recorded, and Barletta consequently introduced Daya to Steve Zap of Z Entertainment. Daya, who was a junior at the time, said she didn't think anything would happen for a while, and went back to high school the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-South_Hills_Living_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-South_Hills_Living-7">[7]</a></sup> A radio promotions veteran, Zap liked the song and was interested in helping to promote the singer, leading to forming an independent label with Barletta called <a href="/wiki/Artbeatz" title="Artbeatz">Artbeatz</a>.</p>
<p>Daya released her song "Hide Away" on April 22, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouTube_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube-9">[9]</a></sup> The song was well-received online, enjoyed support from a number of notable bloggers including <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Oakley" title="Tyler Oakley">Tyler Oakley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Perez_Hilton" title="Perez Hilton">Perez Hilton</a>, the latter commenting "There is something very special about Daya's voice".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Jason Lipshutz of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> also featured the single on their official website, labelling it "a gorgeous debut".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Daya made her television debut performing "Hide Away" on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today!_with_Kathie_Lee_and_Hoda" title="Today! with Kathie Lee and Hoda">Today with Kathie Lee and Hoda</a></i> on August 21, 2015 as Elvis Duran's "Artist to Watch".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She returned to <i><a href="/wiki/Today_(U.S._TV_program)" title="Today (U.S. TV program)">Today</a></i> for their summer concert series on June 28, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following a successful debut, Daya released her self-titled EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Daya_(EP)" title="Daya (EP)">Daya</a></i>, which features six songs, including "Hide Away", on September 4, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iTunes_1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-iTunes_1-4">[4]</a></sup> The EP was premiered in full a day early by <i>Billboard</i>, and debuted at number 161 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, propelling "Hide Away" to number 40 on the <i>Billboard</i> Pop Songs chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_200_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_200-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 30, 2015, Daya released the physical version of her debut EP through <a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Daya was the opening act for American <a href="/wiki/Pop-rap" title="Pop-rap">pop-rap</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Jack_%26_Jack" title="Jack &amp; Jack">Jack &amp; Jack</a>'s US tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2016, she featured on <a href="/wiki/The_Chainsmokers" title="The Chainsmokers">The Chainsmokers</a>' song "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Let_Me_Down_(The_Chainsmokers_song)" title="Don't Let Me Down (The Chainsmokers song)">Don't Let Me Down</a>", which eventually peaked at number 3 on the Hot 100, becoming her second top 40 entry and her first top 10. She also released the second single from her self-titled EP, "<a href="/wiki/Sit_Still,_Look_Pretty" title="Sit Still, Look Pretty">Sit Still, Look Pretty</a>", which debuted at number 100 and peaked at number 28, her third top 40.</p>
<p>Daya was invited to perform at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> Easter Egg Roll, where she and her family met President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> and the First Lady <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Obama" title="Michelle Obama">Michelle Obama</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-South_Hills_Living_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-South_Hills_Living-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 15, 2016, Daya released her song "<a href="/wiki/Words_(Daya_song)" title="Words (Daya song)">Words</a>" as her third single from her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Sit_Still,_Look_Pretty_(album)" title="Sit Still, Look Pretty (album)">Sit Still, Look Pretty</a></i>.</p>
<p>On December 6, 2016, it was announced that "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Let_Me_Down_(The_Chainsmokers_song)" title="Don't Let Me Down (The Chainsmokers song)">Don't Let Me Down</a>" had been nominated for a <a href="/wiki/59th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="59th Annual Grammy Awards">Grammy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
