<p><b>Data science</b>, also known as <b>data-driven science</b>, is an interdisciplinary field about scientific processes and systems to extract <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> or insights from <a href="/wiki/Data" title="Data">data</a> in various forms, either structured or unstructured,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> which is a continuation of some of the data analysis fields such as <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">data mining</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Predictive_analytics" title="Predictive analytics">predictive analytics</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> similar to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knowledge_Discovery_in_Databases" title="Knowledge Discovery in Databases">Knowledge Discovery in Databases</a> (KDD).</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turing_award" title="Turing award">Turing award</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Jim_Gray_(computer_scientist)" title="Jim Gray (computer scientist)">Jim Gray</a> imagined data science as a "fourth paradigm" of science (<a href="/wiki/Empirical_research" title="Empirical research">empirical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basic_research" title="Basic research">theoretical</a>, computational and now data-driven) and asserted that "everything about science is changing because of the impact of information technology" and the <a href="/wiki/Information_explosion" title="Information explosion">data deluge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TansleyTolle2009_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TansleyTolle2009-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BellHey2009_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BellHey2009-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Data_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Data science employs techniques and theories drawn from many fields within the broad areas of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Operations_research" title="Operations research">operations research</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Information_science" title="Information science">information science</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Signal_processing" title="Signal processing">signal processing</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Probability_models" title="Probability models">probability models</a>, <a href="/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Statistical_learning_theory" title="Statistical learning theory">statistical learning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">data mining</a>, <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Data_engineering" title="Data engineering">data engineering</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pattern_recognition_and_learning" title="Pattern recognition and learning">pattern recognition and learning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Data_visualization" title="Data visualization">visualization</a>, <a href="/wiki/Predictive_analytics" title="Predictive analytics">predictive analytics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uncertainty_modeling" title="Uncertainty modeling">uncertainty modeling</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Data_warehousing" title="Data warehousing">data warehousing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Data_compression" title="Data compression">data compression</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_programming" title="Computer programming">computer programming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_performance_computing" title="High performance computing">high performance computing</a>. Methods that scale to <a href="/wiki/Big_data" title="Big data">big data</a> are of particular interest in data science, although the discipline is not generally considered to be restricted to such big data, and big data technologies are often focused on organizing and preprocessing the data instead of analysis. The development of <a href="/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning</a> has enhanced the growth and importance of data science.</p>
<p>Data science affects academic and applied research in many domains, including <a href="/wiki/Machine_translation" title="Machine translation">machine translation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Speech_recognition" title="Speech recognition">speech recognition</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robotics" title="Robotics">robotics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Search_engines" title="Search engines">search engines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Digital_economy" title="Digital economy">digital economy</a>, but also the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_sciences" title="Biological sciences">biological sciences</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_informatics" title="Medical informatics">medical informatics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Health_care" title="Health care">health care</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_sciences" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">humanities</a>. It heavily influences <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> and <a href="/wiki/Finance" title="Finance">finance</a>. From the business perspective, data science is an integral part of competitive intelligence, a newly emerging field that encompasses a number of activities, such as <a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">data mining</a> and <a href="/wiki/Data_analysis" title="Data analysis">data analysis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_scientist">Data scientist</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Data_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Data scientist">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Data scientists use their data and analytical ability to find and interpret rich data sources; manage large amounts of data despite hardware, software, and bandwidth constraints; merge data sources; ensure consistency of datasets; create visualizations to aid in understanding data; build mathematical models using the data; and present and communicate the data insights/findings. They are often expected to produce answers in days rather than months, work by exploratory analysis and rapid iteration, and to produce and present results with dashboards (displays of current values) rather than papers/reports, as statisticians normally do.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Data Scientist" has become a popular occupation with <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_Review" title="Harvard Business Review">Harvard Business Review</a> dubbing it "The Sexiest Job of the 21st Century" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/McKinsey_%26_Company" title="McKinsey &amp; Company">McKinsey &amp; Company</a> projecting a global excess demand of 1.5 million new data scientists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Universities are offering masters courses in data science.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Shorter private bootcamps are also offering data science certificates including student-paid programs like <a href="/wiki/General_Assembly_(school)" title="General Assembly (school)">General Assembly</a> to employer-paid programs like <a href="/wiki/The_Data_Incubator" title="The Data Incubator">The Data Incubator</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>The term "data science" (originally used interchangeably with "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Datalogy" title="Datalogy">datalogy</a>") has existed for over thirty years and was used initially as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Naur" title="Peter Naur">Peter Naur</a> in 1960. In 1974, Naur published <i>Concise Survey of Computer Methods</i>, which freely used the term data science in its survey of the contemporary data processing methods that are used in a wide range of applications. In 1996, members of the International Federation of Classification Societies (IFCS) met in Kobe for their biennial conference. Here, for the first time, the term data science is included in the title of the conference ("Data Science, classification, and related methods").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 1997, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.F._Jeff_Wu" title="C.F. Jeff Wu">C.F. Jeff Wu</a> gave the inaugural lecture entitled "Statistics = Data Science?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cfjwutk_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-cfjwutk-14">[14]</a></sup> for his appointment to the H. C. Carver Professorship at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cfjwu01_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-cfjwu01-15">[15]</a></sup> In this lecture, he characterized statistical work as a trilogy of data collection, data modeling and analysis, and decision making. In his conclusion, he initiated the modern, non-computer science, usage of the term "data science" and advocated that statistics be renamed data science and statisticians data scientists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cfjwutk_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-cfjwutk-14">[14]</a></sup> Later, he presented his lecture entitled "Statistics = Data Science?" as the first of his 1998 P.C. Mahalanobis Memorial Lectures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cfjwu02_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-cfjwu02-16">[16]</a></sup> These lectures honor <a href="/wiki/Prasanta_Chandra_Mahalanobis" title="Prasanta Chandra Mahalanobis">Prasanta Chandra Mahalanobis</a>, an Indian scientist and statistician and founder of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Statistical_Institute" title="Indian Statistical Institute">Indian Statistical Institute</a>.</p>
<p>In 2001, William S. Cleveland introduced data science as an independent discipline, extending the field of statistics to incorporate "advances in computing with data" in his article "Data Science: An Action Plan for Expanding the Technical Areas of the Field of Statistics," which was published in Volume 69, No. 1, of the April 2001 edition of the International Statistical Review / Revue Internationale de Statistique.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cleveland01_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-cleveland01-17">[17]</a></sup> In his report, Cleveland establishes six technical areas which he believed to encompass the field of data science: multidisciplinary investigations, models and methods for data, computing with data, pedagogy, tool evaluation, and theory.</p>
<p>In April 2002, the International Council for Science: Committee on Data for Science and Technology (CODATA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ics12_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ics12-18">[18]</a></sup> started the <i>Data Science Journal</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dsj12_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-dsj12-19">[19]</a></sup> a publication focused on issues such as the description of data systems, their publication on the internet, applications and legal issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dsj02_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-dsj02-20">[20]</a></sup> Shortly thereafter, in January 2003, Columbia University began publishing <i>The Journal of Data Science</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jds03_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-jds03-21">[21]</a></sup> which provided a platform for all data workers to present their views and exchange ideas. The journal was largely devoted to the application of statistical methods and quantitative research. In 2005, The National Science Board published "Long-lived Digital Data Collections: Enabling Research and Education in the 21st Century" defining data scientists as "the information and computer scientists, database and software and programmers, disciplinary experts, curators and expert annotators, librarians, archivists, and others, who are crucial to the successful management of a digital data collection" whose primary activity is to "conduct creative inquiry and analysis."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2013, the IEEE Task Force on Data Science and Advanced Analytics <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> was launched, and the first international conference: IEEE International Conference on Data Science and Advanced Analytics was launched in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2015, the International Journal on Data Science and Analytics <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> was launched by Springer to publish original work on data science and big data analytics.</p>
<p>In 2008,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/DJ_Patil" title="DJ Patil">DJ Patil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Hammerbacher" title="Jeff Hammerbacher">Jeff Hammerbacher</a> used the term "data scientist" to define their jobs at LinkedIn and Facebook, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Data_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although use of the term "data science" has exploded in business environments, many academics and journalists see no distinction between data science and <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a>. Writing in <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a>, Gil Press argues that data science is a <a href="/wiki/Buzzword" title="Buzzword">buzzword</a> without a clear definition and has simply replaced “<a href="/wiki/Business_analytics" title="Business analytics">business analytics</a>” in contexts such as graduate degree programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GilPress_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-GilPress-27">[27]</a></sup> In the question-and-answer section of his keynote address at the Joint Statistical Meetings of <a href="/wiki/American_Statistical_Association" title="American Statistical Association">American Statistical Association</a>, noted applied statistician <a href="/wiki/Nate_Silver" title="Nate Silver">Nate Silver</a> said, “I think data-scientist is a sexed up term for a statistician....Statistics is a branch of science. Data scientist is slightly redundant in some way and people shouldn’t berate the term statistician.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Software">Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Data_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_statistical_packages" title="List of statistical packages">List of statistical packages</a></div>
<p>In the 2010-2011 time frame, data science software reached an inflection point where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_source" title="Open source">open source</a> software started supplanting proprietary software.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_source" title="Open source">open source</a> software enables modifying and extending the software, and it allows sharing of the resulting algorithms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
