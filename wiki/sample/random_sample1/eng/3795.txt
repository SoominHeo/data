<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_meanings">Common meanings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Date&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Common meanings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Date, a social activity which may be associated with:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dating" title="Dating">Dating</a>, a form of courtship, which may include any social activity undertaken by, typically, two persons with the aim of assessing each other's suitability as a partner</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Double_date" title="Double date">Double date</a>, a social date in which two couples participate</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Group_date" title="Group date">Group date</a>, a modern pattern for dating wherein a group of single men and a group of single women organise a night out, with the hope of forming romantic partnerships</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Meeting" title="Meeting">Meeting</a> over a meal (e.g., "breakfast date", "brunch date", "coffee date", "dinner date", "drinks date", "lunch date") or for a planned activity (e.g, "bowling date", "golf date", "ski date", "tennis date"), for social and/or business reasons</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Play_date" title="Play date">Play date</a>, an arranged appointment for children to get together for a few hours</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Date_(fruit)" title="Date (fruit)">Date (fruit)</a>, the fruit of the date palm (<i>Phoenix dactylifera</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Calendar_date" title="Calendar date">Calendar date</a>, a day on a calendar, which may also be used in:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Date_(metadata)" title="Date (metadata)">Date (metadata)</a>, a representation term or class associated with a data element</li>
<li>DATE command, a <a href="/wiki/System_time" title="System time">system time</a> command on DOS, OS/2, and Microsoft Windows operating systems for displaying the current date</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dating_methodologies_in_archaeology" title="Dating methodologies in archaeology">Dating methodologies in archaeology</a>, for an archaeological artifact</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">Old Style and New Style dates</a>, to indicate the <a href="/wiki/Adoption_of_the_Gregorian_calendar" title="Adoption of the Gregorian calendar">adoption of the Gregorian calendar</a> and change of start of year in Great Britain and it's colonies.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Radiometric_dating" title="Radiometric dating">Radiometric dating</a>, a family of techniques used to determine of the approximate period of origin of an object, e.g. "to carbon-date an artifact"</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
