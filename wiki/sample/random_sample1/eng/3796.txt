<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Date_A_Live&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Date_A_Live_characters" title="List of Date A Live characters">List of Date A Live characters</a></div>
<p>The series begins with a strange phenomenon called a "spatial quake" devastating the center of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>, resulting at least 150 million casualties. For the next 30 years, smaller spatial quakes plague the world on irregular basis. In the present, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shido_Itsuka" title="Shido Itsuka">Shido Itsuka</a>, a seemingly ordinary high schooler comes across a mysterious girl at the <a href="/wiki/Ground_zero" title="Ground zero">ground zero</a> of a spatial quake. He learns from his adoptive sister Kotori that the girl is one of the "Spirits" from different dimensions who are the real cause of the spatial quakes that occur when they manifest themselves in the real world. He also learns that Kotori is the commander of the airship <i>Fraxinus</i>, crewed by the organization Ratatoskr, and is recruited to make use of his mysterious ability to seal the Spirits' powers thus stopping them from being a threat to mankind. However, there is a catch: to seal a Spirit's power, he must make her fall in love with him and make her kiss him. Shido's intelligent classmate, Origami Tobiichi, is a sergeant of the AST (Anti-Spirit Team), a special unit designed to suppress the threat posed by Spirits. As Shido successfully keeps sealing more and more Spirits, he gains allies to help him with his dates with other Spirits. Shido also increases the competition among them for his attention and affection, much to his chagrin.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Date_A_Live&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A manga adaption of Date A Live with illustrations by Ringo began on April 16 of 2012. Due to Ringo having health problems, the manga was cancelled after 6 chapters. The adaptation ended covering only a part of Shido's first date with Tohka.</p>
<p>A new manga adaptation began serialisation in the January 2014 issue of Shōnen Ace, this time with illustrations done by Sekihiko Inui. It was recently announced that the third volume of the manga will be the last. The third volume will be shipped by Kadokawa throughout Japan this winter.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Date_A_Live&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Date_A_Live_episodes" title="List of Date A Live episodes">List of Date A Live episodes</a></div>
<p>The anime adaptation is directed by Keitaro Motonaga and produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIC_PLUS%2B" title="AIC PLUS+">AIC PLUS+</a>. The series was streamed in lower quality on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nico_Nico_Douga" title="Nico Nico Douga">Niconico</a>, with each episode available a week before its TV premiere. The first episode was streamed on March 31 and aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo_MX" title="Tokyo MX">Tokyo MX</a> on April 6, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The final, 12th episode was streamed on Niconico on June 16 and aired on Tokyo MX on June 22.</p>
<p>The opening theme is titled "Date A Live" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">デート・ア・ライブ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Dēto A Raibu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> sung by <a href="/wiki/Sweet_ARMS" title="Sweet ARMS">sweet ARMS</a>, a vocal group consisting <a href="/wiki/Iori_Nomizu" title="Iori Nomizu">Iori Nomizu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Misuzu_Togashi" title="Misuzu Togashi">Misuzu Togashi</a>, Kaori Sadohara, and Misato. The series makes use of four ending themes: "Hatsukoi Winding Road", by Kayoko Tsumita, Risako Murai and Midori Tsukimiya; "Save The World", "Save My Heart" and "Strawberry Rain" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ストロベリーレイン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, all three by Iori Nomizu.</p>
<p>Following the TV broadcast of the final episode of the first season, a second season is set for April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The opening theme is sung by <a href="/wiki/Sweet_ARMS" title="Sweet ARMS">sweet ARMS</a> titled "Trust in You" and the ending theme is sung by Kaori Sadohara titled "Day to Story".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The animation production will be held by <a href="/wiki/Production_IMS" title="Production IMS">Production IMS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2staffs_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-S2staffs-9">[9]</a></sup> An unaired episode will be bundled with the third volume of the <i>Date A Live Encore</i> short story collection to be released on December 9, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Date_A_Live&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

A <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Vita" title="PlayStation Vita">PlayStation Vita</a> version of the game was released in Summer 2015, and features new characters and scenarios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another video game titled <i>Date A Live: Ars Install</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">或守インストール</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was also announced and features another new character named Maria Ars <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">或守 鞠亜</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Arusu Maria</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Suzuko_Mimori" title="Suzuko Mimori">Suzuko Mimori</a>. The game was released on June 26, 2014. <i><a href="/wiki/Famitsu" title="Famitsu">Famitsu</a></i> gave the <i>Ars Install</i> game a review score of 30/40.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>A new installment for both <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> games, named <i>Date A Live Twin Edition: Rio <a href="/wiki/Reincarnation" title="Reincarnation">Reincarnation</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">デート・ア・ライブ Twin Edition 凜緒リンカーネイション</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Dēto A Raibu Tsuin Edishon Rio Rinkāneishon</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> produced by <a href="/wiki/Compile_Heart" title="Compile Heart">Compile Heart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sting_Entertainment" title="Sting Entertainment">Sting Entertainment</a> was released on July 30, 2015 for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Vita" title="PlayStation Vita">PlayStation Vita</a>. It is a de facto sequel with new characters and new scenarios. The game will features the Yamai Sisters, Miku Izayoi, Rinne Sonogami, Maria Ars, as well as Marina Ars, and a new original character named Rio <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">凜緒</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rio</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Ayane_Sakura" title="Ayane Sakura">Ayane Sakura</a>. A promotional video was shown at the events of Date A Fes II.</p>
