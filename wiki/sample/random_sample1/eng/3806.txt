<p><b>Prince Valdemar of Denmark</b>, <small><a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Tower_and_Sword" title="Order of the Tower and Sword">GCTE</a></small> (27 October 1858 in <a href="/wiki/Bernstorff_Palace" title="Bernstorff Palace">Bernstorff Palace</a> – 14 January 1939 in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>) was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Royal_Family" title="Danish Royal Family">Danish Royal Family</a>, the third son and youngest child of <a href="/wiki/Christian_IX_of_Denmark" title="Christian IX of Denmark">Christian IX of Denmark</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Louise_of_Hesse-Kassel" title="Louise of Hesse-Kassel">Louise of Hesse-Kassel</a>. Valdemar was the younger brother of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederik_VIII_of_Denmark" title="Frederik VIII of Denmark">Frederik VIII of Denmark</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Alexandra_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Queen Alexandra of the United Kingdom">Queen Alexandra of the United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_I_of_Greece" title="George I of Greece">George I of Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Feodorovna_(Dagmar_of_Denmark)" title="Maria Feodorovna (Dagmar of Denmark)">Empress Maria Feodorovna of Russia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Thyra_of_Denmark" title="Princess Thyra of Denmark">Crown Princess Thyra of Hanover</a>, and also grandfather of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne_of_Romania" title="Queen Anne of Romania">Queen Anne of Romania</a>, the wife of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Michael_I_of_Romania" title="King Michael I of Romania">King Michael I of Romania</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Valdemar_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Christian9%26Valdemar.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="426" data-file-width="243" height="263" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Christian9%26Valdemar.jpeg/150px-Christian9%26Valdemar.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Christian9%26Valdemar.jpeg/225px-Christian9%26Valdemar.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7a/Christian9%26Valdemar.jpeg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Christian9%26Valdemar.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Prince Valdemar with his father <a href="/wiki/Christian_IX_of_Denmark" title="Christian IX of Denmark">Christian IX of Denmark</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Prince Valdemar was born on 27 October 1858 at <a href="/wiki/Bernstorff_Palace" title="Bernstorff Palace">Bernstorff Palace</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gentofte" title="Gentofte">Gentofte</a> north of <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>. His father was <a href="/wiki/Christian_IX_of_Denmark" title="Christian IX of Denmark">Prince Christian of Denmark</a>, later King Christian IX. His mother was <a href="/wiki/Louise_of_Hesse-Kassel" title="Louise of Hesse-Kassel">Princess Louise of Hesse-Kassel</a>. He was baptised on 21 December 1858.</p>
<p>In March 1863, the Danish royal family, including four-year-old Valdemar, traveled to London for the wedding of his eldest sister, <a href="/wiki/Alexandra_of_Denmark" title="Alexandra of Denmark">Alexandra</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Edward_VII" title="Edward VII">Prince of Wales</a>, the son and heir of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>. That November, his parents succeeded to the throne of Denmark following the death of <a href="/wiki/Frederick_VII_of_Denmark" title="Frederick VII of Denmark">King Frederick VII</a>.</p>
<p>Prince Valdemar received his early education from tutors. In the summer of 1874, he accompanied his father during his visit to <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Millennium" title="Millennium">millennium</a> celebrations. After his <a href="/wiki/Confirmation" title="Confirmation">confirmation</a> in 1874, as was customary for princes at that time, he started a military education and entered the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Danish_Naval_Academy" title="Royal Danish Naval Academy">naval college</a>. In 1879, he was <a href="/wiki/Sub-lieutenant" title="Sub-lieutenant">sub-lieutenant</a> and in 1880 <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a>. In the following years, he participated in several naval expeditions. From 1883, Valdemar lived at Bernstorff Palace near Copenhagen with his nephew <a href="/wiki/Prince_George_of_Greece_and_Denmark" title="Prince George of Greece and Denmark">Prince George of Greece</a>, who had been taken as a boy to Denmark to be enlisted in the Danish royal navy, and be consigned to the care of Valdemar, who was an admiral in the Danish fleet. Feeling abandoned by his father on this occasion, George would later describe to his fiancée the profound attachment he developed for his uncle from that day forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Valdemar_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:La_princesa_Maria_d%27Orleans,_princesa_de_Dinamarca.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="396" data-file-width="250" height="238" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/La_princesa_Maria_d%27Orleans%2C_princesa_de_Dinamarca.jpg/150px-La_princesa_Maria_d%27Orleans%2C_princesa_de_Dinamarca.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/La_princesa_Maria_d%27Orleans%2C_princesa_de_Dinamarca.jpg/225px-La_princesa_Maria_d%27Orleans%2C_princesa_de_Dinamarca.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6c/La_princesa_Maria_d%27Orleans%2C_princesa_de_Dinamarca.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:La_princesa_Maria_d%27Orleans,_princesa_de_Dinamarca.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Prince Valdemar's wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marie_of_Orl%C3%A9ans_(1865%E2%80%931909)" title="Marie of Orléans (1865–1909)">Princess Marie of Orléans</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>He married <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marie_of_Orl%C3%A9ans_(1865%E2%80%931909)" title="Marie of Orléans (1865–1909)">Princess Marie d'Orleans</a> on 20 October 1885 in a civil ceremony in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt1-2">[2]</a></sup> They had a religious ceremony on 22 October 1885 at the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_d%27Eu" title="Château d'Eu">Château d'Eu</a>, the residence of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philippe,_Count_of_Paris" title="Prince Philippe, Count of Paris">Prince Philippe, Count of Paris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyt1-2">[2]</a></sup> Among the most notable attendants were Valdemar's mother <a href="/wiki/Louise_of_Hesse-Kassel" title="Louise of Hesse-Kassel">Queen Louise of Denmark</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_VII_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Edward VII of the United Kingdom">Prince</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexandra_of_Denmark" title="Alexandra of Denmark">Princess of Wales</a>, and Marie's parents the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert,_duke_of_Chartres" title="Robert, duke of Chartres">Duke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Fran%C3%A7oise_of_Orl%C3%A9ans_(1844%E2%80%931925)" title="Princess Françoise of Orléans (1844–1925)">Duchess</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chartres" title="Chartres">Chartres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt1_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-nyt1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The wedding was believed by one source to have been politically arranged, and in France, it was believed that the Count of Paris (the bride's uncle) was personally responsible for the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt1_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-nyt1-2">[2]</a></sup> However, the same source claimed that "there was every reason to believe that [it was] a genuine love match".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt1_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-nyt1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the time of their marriage, it was decided that any sons would be brought up in Valdemar's Lutheran faith, while any daughters would be raised as Catholics, the faith of their mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-F199_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-F199-3">[3]</a></sup> The couple's four sons were consequently Lutherans, while their only daughter, Margaret was raised a Catholic and married a Catholic prince.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-F199_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-F199-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Valdemar_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Valdemar had a lifelong naval career, but things could have been much different for the prince. He was offered two European thrones, those of Bulgaria and Norway, but declined them due to international pressures.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He was the first president of the <a href="/wiki/Seamen%27s_Association_of_1856" title="Seamen's Association of 1856">Seamen's Association of 1856</a>.</p>
<p>He died on 14 January 1939 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yellow_Palace,_Copenhagen" title="Yellow Palace, Copenhagen">Yellow Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> and was buried in <a href="/wiki/Roskilde_Cathedral" title="Roskilde Cathedral">Roskilde Cathedral</a>. He was the last surviving child of Christian IX.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Issue">Issue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Valdemar_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Issue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Count_Aage_of_Rosenborg" title="Count Aage of Rosenborg">Prince Aage of Denmark</a> (1887–1940), married Matilda Calvi dei conti di Bergolo in 1914 and had one son</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_Axel_of_Denmark" title="Prince Axel of Denmark">Prince Axel of Denmark</a> (1888–1964), married <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaretha_of_Sweden" title="Princess Margaretha of Sweden">Margaretha of Sweden</a> in 1919 and had two sons</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Count_Erik_of_Rosenborg" title="Count Erik of Rosenborg">Prince Erik of Denmark</a> (1890–1950), married Lois Frances Booth in Ottawa, Canada 1924, divorced 1937, and had a son and a daughter.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Count_Viggo_of_Rosenborg" title="Count Viggo of Rosenborg">Prince Viggo of Denmark</a> (1893–1970), created Count of Rosenborg, married Eleonora Green, and had no children</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret_of_Denmark" title="Princess Margaret of Denmark">Princess Margaret of Denmark</a> (1895–1992), married <a href="/wiki/Prince_Ren%C3%A9_of_Bourbon-Parma" title="Prince René of Bourbon-Parma">Prince René of Bourbon-Parma</a>. Her daughter <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne_of_Romania" title="Queen Anne of Romania">Anne</a> was the wife of <a href="/wiki/Michael_I_of_Romania" title="Michael I of Romania">Michael</a>, former King of Romania.</li>
