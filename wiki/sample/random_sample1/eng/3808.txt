<p>This is a <b>list of <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Denmark" title="Monarchy of Denmark">Danish monarchs</a></b>, that is, the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Denmark" title="Monarchy of Denmark">Kings and Queens regnant of Denmark</a>. This includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>The Kingdom of Denmark (up to 1397)
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">Personal union</a> of Denmark and Norway (1380–1397)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Kalmar_Union" title="Kalmar Union">Kalmar Union</a> (1397–1536)
<ul>
<li>Union of Denmark, Norway and Sweden (1397–1523)</li>
<li>Union of Denmark and Norway (1523–1536)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>The Kingdom of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denmark-Norway" title="Denmark-Norway">Denmark-Norway</a> (1536–1814)</li>
<li>The Kingdom of Denmark (1814–ongoing)
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> (since the union between <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> in 1380; independent in a personal union with Denmark 1918–1944; a sovereign republic since 1944)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> (since the union between <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> in 1380; effective Danish control began in 1721; integrated into the Danish realm in 1953; internal home rule introduced 1979; self-determination assumed in 2009; Greenland has two out of 179 seats in the Danish parliament <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folketinget" title="Folketinget">Folketinget</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a> (since the union between <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> in 1380; County of Denmark 1816–1948; internal home rule introduced 1948; The Faroe Islands have two out of 179 seats in the Danish parliament <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folketinget" title="Folketinget">Folketinget</a>)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The house of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Oldenburg" title="House of Oldenburg">Oldenburg</a> held the Danish Crown between 1448 and 1863, when it passed to the house of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Gl%C3%BCcksburg" title="Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg">Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg</a>, a cadet branch of the same house, patrilineally descended from King <a href="/wiki/Christian_III_of_Denmark" title="Christian III of Denmark">Christian III of Denmark</a>. The kingdom had been elective (although the eldest son or brother of the previous king was usually elected) until 1660, when it became hereditary and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Absolutism_(European_history)" title="Absolutism (European history)">absolutist</a>. Until 1864 Denmark was also united in a personal union with the duchies of <a href="/wiki/Holstein" title="Holstein">Holstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saxe-Lauenburg" title="Saxe-Lauenburg">Saxe-Lauenburg</a>, and in a political and personal union with the Duchy of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schleswig" title="Schleswig">Schleswig</a>.</p>

</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chochilaicus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chochilaicus (page does not exist)">Chochilaicus</a>—see <a href="/wiki/Hugleik" title="Hugleik">Hugleik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hygelac" title="Hygelac">Hygelac</a>—<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr> 515 AD, First Danish King mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Gregory_of_Tours" title="Gregory of Tours">Gregory of Tours</a> (538–594).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Hugleik, according to the written sources suffered a defeat in 515 during a naval expedition to the Frankish Empire. Hugleik is the first Danish king mentioned in European sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ongendus" title="Ongendus">Ongendus</a> (<i>Angantyr</i>): <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 710</span> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Willibrord" title="Saint Willibrord">Saint Willibrord</a> wrote about when he visited the Danes, at the time ruled by Ongendus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Harald, named as former king in relating 9th century events, perhaps model for legendary <a href="/wiki/Harald_Wartooth" title="Harald Wartooth">Harald Wartooth</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sigfred" title="Sigfred">Sigfred</a>: 780s–790s</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gudfred" title="Gudfred">Gudfred</a>: 804–810 mentioned as Danish king in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Heiligen" title="Treaty of Heiligen">Treaty of Heiligen</a> 811.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Hemming" title="King Hemming">Hemming</a>: 810–811/812 The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Heiligen" title="Treaty of Heiligen">Treaty of Heiligen</a> was signed in 811 between the Danish King Hemming and <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>Sigfred, nephew of Gudfred, and Anulo (<i>Anlaufr</i>), nephew of Harald, fought for throne and both were killed, perhaps model for legendary <a href="/wiki/Sigurd_Hring" title="Sigurd Hring">Sigurd Hring</a>: <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 812</span></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harald_Klak" title="Harald Klak">Harald Klak</a> and his brothers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ragnfrid" title="Ragnfrid">Ragnfrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hemming_Halfdansson" title="Hemming Halfdansson">Hemming Halfdansson</a>: 812–813 and again from 819/827. From 826 he and his household lived in exile with the Frankish emperor <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_Pious" title="Louis the Pious">Louis the Pious</a>, he was baptized by the bishop of Mainz in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ingelheim" title="Ingelheim">Ingelheim</a> am Rhein. The last reference of Harald in the written sources are in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annals_of_Fulda" title="Annals of Fulda">Annals of Fulda</a> this records his execution for treason in 852.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>Sons of Gudfred: 814–820s
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Horik_I" title="Horik I">Horik I</a>: (814) 827–854. king of the Danes (at first ruling jointly with his unnamed brothers). The Frankish annals mention Horik on numerous occasions during the next couple of decades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Horik_II" title="Horik II">Horik II</a>: 850s–860s. He is believed to have been the immediate successor of Horik I, but the annals are silent about the name of the Danish king for a few years after the disaster of 854. In 857, Horik II allowed Rorik to occupy the part of the kingdom between the sea and the <a href="/wiki/Eider" title="Eider">Eider</a>. Horik II was still alive in 864, when a letter was addressed to him by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Nicholas_I" title="Pope Nicholas I">Pope Nicholas I</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>Late 9th century kings
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bagsecg" title="Bagsecg">Bagsecg</a>: <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 860s–871</span></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Halfdan_Ragnarsson" title="Halfdan Ragnarsson">Halfdan</a>: 871–877</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sigfred" title="Sigfred">Sigfred</a>: <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 873–891</span> It is generally assumed that he was the immediate successor of Horik II, although that is not certain. His year of succession is unknown, but it was between 864 (when Horik II was still king) and his first appearance as king in the Frankish annals in 873. Sigifrid was baptized in 882.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Godfrid,_Duke_of_Frisia" title="Godfrid, Duke of Frisia">Gudfred</a>: 880s</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heiligo" title="Heiligo">Heiligo</a> (<i>Halga</i>): 890s</li>
<li>The "<a href="/wiki/House_of_Olaf" title="House of Olaf">House of Olaf</a>": late 9th century and early 10th century.. This dynasty is mentioned in the two <a href="/wiki/Sigtrygg_Runestones" title="Sigtrygg Runestones">Sigtrygg Runestones</a> raised shortly after 934. The two inscriptions are evidence for the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Olaf" title="House of Olaf">House of Olaf</a> controlling at least part of Denmark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Olof_the_Brash" title="Olof the Brash">Olof</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gyrd_and_Gnupa" title="Gyrd and Gnupa">Gyrd and Gnupa</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sigtrygg_Gnupasson" title="Sigtrygg Gnupasson">Sigtrygg</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harthacnut_I_of_Denmark" title="Harthacnut I of Denmark">Harthacnut I</a> (<i>Hardeknud</i>): 936–940</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sigurd_Snake-in-the-Eye" title="Sigurd Snake-in-the-Eye">Sigurd Snake-in-the-Eye</a> (da: Sigurd Orm-i-øje or Snogeøje). Mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Chronicon_Roskildense" title="Chronicon Roskildense">Chronicon Roskildense</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ragnarssona_%C3%BE%C3%A1ttr" title="Ragnarssona þáttr">Ragnarssona þáttr</a>. Said to be king of <a href="/wiki/Zealand" title="Zealand">Zealand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scania" title="Scania">Scania</a>, and son of <a href="/wiki/Ragnar_Lodbrok" title="Ragnar Lodbrok">Ragnar Lodbrok</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ragnar_Lodbrok" title="Ragnar Lodbrok">Ragnar Lodbrok</a> was a legendary king probably in the 9th century, is mentioned in multiple sources, but the sources are wildly inconsistent.</li>
</ul>
