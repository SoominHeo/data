<p>The <b>UEFA European Under-21 Championship 2007</b> were held from 10 June 2007 to 23 June 2007. It is the 16th staging of <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>'s <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="UEFA European Under-21 Championship">European Under-21 Championship</a>.</p>
<p>For the first time, a host nation was chosen ahead of the competition – the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Netherlands</a>, who are also the defending champions, were exempt from qualifying. The host nation was chosen on 15 December 2005. <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> also submitted bids. Of UEFA's 52 member countries, only the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a> did not compete in the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship_qualification" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification">qualification round</a> for the tournament. That left 50 nations attempting to qualify for the seven remaining spots at the finals.</p>
<p>This was the first time that the competition's final matches took place in an odd-numbered year. <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> took this decision with the will to give more visibility to the tournament, since during even-numbered years the competition was clouded by the approaching <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> or the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship" title="UEFA European Championship">UEFA European Championship</a>.</p>
<p>The tournament also served as the European qualifying tournament for the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>, with a place for the four semi-finalists of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, as England is not an Olympic nation, their team was ineligible for the Olympics. Therefore, with the event of <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England</a> reaching the semi-finals, a <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship#Olympic_Qualifying_Play-Off" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">fifth-place playoff</a> was necessary.</p>
<p>In this competition, a new UEFA penalty shootout record was established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The semi final between <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">The Netherlands U21</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England U21</a> finished in 1-1. No less than 32 penalties had to be taken before the tie was decided. The Netherlands U21 won 13-12,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and went on to win the tournament by defeating <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-21_football_team" title="Serbia national under-21 football team">Serbia U21</a> 4-1 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the final, some commotion arose in the Netherlands because several Dutch internationals with <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> roots carried the flag of Suriname with them during the ceremony of the European Champion in the <a href="/wiki/Euroborg" title="Euroborg">Euroborg</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Dutch coach <a href="/wiki/Foppe_de_Haan" title="Foppe de Haan">Foppe de Haan</a> expressed the actions of these players as "inappropriate".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>In order to make the switch from even to odd-numbered years, the 2007 edition was a shortened version, condensed from a two-year campaign into a one-year campaign. Therefore, a completely new qualification format was devised to eliminate the weaker nations early, and saw a decrease in the size and duration of qualification groups.</p>

<p>The draw for the finals took place in <a href="/wiki/Arnhem" title="Arnhem">Arnhem</a> on 24 November 2006, putting the eight qualifying nations into two groups. The provisional dates for the group games are 10 June to 17 June 2007, with <a href="/wiki/Heerenveen" title="Heerenveen">Heerenveen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arnhem" title="Arnhem">Arnhem</a> each staging a semi-final on 20 June. The final took place on Saturday 23 June at the <a href="/wiki/Euroborg" title="Euroborg">Euroborg stadium</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Groningen_(city)" title="Groningen (city)">Groningen</a>.</p>
<p>The opening game was between the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_under-21_football_team" title="Israel national under-21 football team">Israel</a> at 10 June.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympic_Qualifying_Play-Off">Olympic Qualifying Play-Off</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Olympic Qualifying Play-Off">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2007 European Under-21 Championship also doubled up as the European qualifying round for the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Olympic football tournament</a>. Europe's four places at the Olympic tournament were to be filled by the four semi-finalists. However, because England, one of the semi-finalists, do not compete independently at the Olympics, a play-off between Portugal and Italy, the two third-placed teams in each group, decided the fourth European qualifier. Italy defeated Portugal through a penalty shoot-out booking their Olympic place in Beijing.</p>
