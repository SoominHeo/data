<p><b>Bamidele Jermaine</b> "<b>Dele</b>" <b>Alli</b> (born 11 April 1996) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Premier_League" title="English Premier League">English Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> and the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England national team</a>.</p>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes" title="Milton Keynes">Milton Keynes</a>, he joined the youth system at <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">Milton Keynes Dons</a> aged 11 and broke into the first team five years later, during the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Football_League" title="2012–13 Football League">2012–13</a> season. Over the next two-and-a-half years he made 88 official appearances for the team, scoring 24 goals. He signed for Tottenham Hotspur in February 2015 for an initial fee of £5 million, being loaned back to the Dons for the remainder of the season. In his first full campaign at <a href="/wiki/White_Hart_Lane" title="White Hart Lane">White Hart Lane</a>, Alli was voted the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a>. In January 2016 he was ranked 8th in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gazzetta_dello_Sport" title="Gazzetta dello Sport">Gazzetta dello Sport</a> list of the 50 best U20 footballers and in October of the same year he was ranked as the best U21 talent in world football by <a href="/wiki/FourFourTwo" title="FourFourTwo">FourFourTwo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dele played for the England <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-17_football_team" title="England national under-17 football team">U17</a>, <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-18_football_team" title="England national under-18 football team">U18</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-19_football_team" title="England national under-19 football team">U19</a> teams. He made his senior debut in 2015 and was selected for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>.</p>

<p>Dele joined the youth system at <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">Milton Keynes Dons</a> when he was 11 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014-3">[3]</a></sup> He made his debut for the first team as a sixteen-year-old on 2 November 2012, coming on as a 64th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Jay_O%27Shea" title="Jay O'Shea">Jay O'Shea</a> in a 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Southern_Football_League" title="Southern Football League">Southern Football League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_City_F.C." title="Cambridge City F.C.">Cambridge City</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> first round at Milton Road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cambridge_City_0-0_MK_Dons_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cambridge_City_0-0_MK_Dons-6">[6]</a></sup> His first touch in professional football was a back-heeled pass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014-3">[3]</a></sup> His first goal came in the replay against Cambridge eleven days later, where he scored in a 6–1 win at <a href="/wiki/Stadium_mk" title="Stadium mk">Stadium mk</a> on his first start.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_Dons_6-1_Cambridge_City_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-MK_Dons_6-1_Cambridge_City-7">[7]</a></sup> He made his league debut in the 2–3 defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coventry_City" title="Coventry City">Coventry City</a> at home on 29 December, where he played 71 minutes before being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Zeli_Ismail" title="Zeli Ismail">Zeli Ismail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_Dons_2-3_Coventry_City_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-MK_Dons_2-3_Coventry_City-8">[8]</a></sup> His only other league appearance in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Football_League_One" title="2012–13 Football League One">2012–13 Football League One</a> season, came as a second-half substitute for <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Bamford" title="Patrick Bamford">Patrick Bamford</a> in the last game of the season, a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Stevenage_F.C." title="Stevenage F.C.">Stevenage</a> at <a href="/wiki/Broadhall_Way" title="Broadhall Way">Broadhall Way</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stevenage_0-2_MK_Dons_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stevenage_0-2_MK_Dons-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dele_Alli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The 2013–14 season saw Dele break into the MK Dons first team on a regular basis. He started the Dons' first league game of the season, a 0–0 draw away at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shrewsbury_Town" title="Shrewsbury Town">Shrewsbury Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shrewsbury_Town_0-0_MK_Dons_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shrewsbury_Town_0-0_MK_Dons-10">[10]</a></sup> In his first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> appearance, Dele scored to help MK Dons beat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northampton_Town" title="Northampton Town">Northampton Town</a> 2–0 and thus progress to the second round of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_Dons_2-0_Northampton_Town_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-MK_Dons_2-0_Northampton_Town-11">[11]</a></sup> On 28 September, Alli scored his first professional league goal in the 4–1 thrashing of Stevenage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_Dons_4-1_Stevenage_F.C._12-0"><a href="#cite_note-MK_Dons_4-1_Stevenage_F.C.-12">[12]</a></sup> After being in and out of the first team picture in late 2013 due to injury setbacks, Dele would go on and establish himself as a first-choice player in 2014. He scored the opening goal in the 3–2 win over Shrewsbury Town at Stadium mk on 11 January, with a header from <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Gleeson" title="Stephen Gleeson">Stephen Gleeson</a>'s pinpoint pass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MK_Dons_3-2_Shrewsbury_Town_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-MK_Dons_3-2_Shrewsbury_Town-13">[13]</a></sup> On 11 March, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Notts_County" title="Notts County">Notts County</a> at <a href="/wiki/Meadow_Lane" title="Meadow Lane">Meadow Lane</a>, aged 17 years and 11 months old, Dele scored a hat-trick to guide the 'Dons to a 3–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Notts_County_1-3_MK_Dons_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Notts_County_1-3_MK_Dons-14">[14]</a></sup> His next and final goal of the 2013–14 season, came on 5 April against Coventry City at the <a href="/wiki/Sixfields_Stadium" title="Sixfields Stadium">Sixfields Stadium</a>; Dele struck a thunderous volley from 25 yards out as MK Dons defeated Coventry, 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coventry_City_1-2_MK_Dons_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry_City_1-2_MK_Dons-15">[15]</a></sup> He made 37 appearances in all competitions during the 2013–14 season, scoring 7 times, with 33 appearances and 6 goals in the league.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dele_Alli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>With the departure of Gleeson to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birmingham_City" title="Birmingham City">Birmingham City</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> in June 2014, Dele became the first-choice central midfield partner to <a href="/wiki/Darren_Potter" title="Darren Potter">Darren Potter</a>. He started the season brightly, playing the first league game of the season as the Dons overcame a 2–0 deficit to defeat Gillingham, 4–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He then helped the team to a 3–1 win over arch-enemies, <a href="/wiki/AFC_Wimbledon" title="AFC Wimbledon">AFC Wimbledon</a>, in the League Cup first round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> His first goal of the 2014–15 season came in the first away league game, a 3–2 defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peterborough_United" title="Peterborough United">Peterborough United</a>, where he scored a tap-in from <a href="/wiki/Will_Grigg" title="Will Grigg">Will Grigg</a>'s deflected shot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 August, Dele played the full 90 minutes in the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Football_League_Cup" title="2014–15 Football League Cup">League Cup</a> second round as MK Dons recorded a historic 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It was reported that numerous scouts from top clubs across Europe attended the game to watch him play, including representatives from <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In the game after the 4–0 League Cup win over Manchester United, Dele continued his rich vein of form with a goal curled in from the edge of the box in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Crawley_Town_F.C." title="Crawley Town F.C.">Crawley Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> After the international break, Dele played 77 minutes in the 5–3 win against Barnsley, assisting the Dons second goal and scoring their third goal himself with a chip over the Barnsley goalkeeper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 September, Dele extended his contract with MK Dons until June 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Two days later, in a game against <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a>, he scored a hat-trick and achieved one assist in what turned out to be a 6–1 victory for the Dons. It was the second hat-trick of his career and the first he achieved at home, which also led to him winning the Man of the Match award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tottenham_Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dele_Alli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tottenham Hotspur">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>On 2 February 2015, Dele signed for <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in the last hours of the mid-season transfer window on a five-and-a-half-year deal for an initial fee of £5 million.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Milton_Keynes_Dons_.28loan.29">Milton Keynes Dons (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dele_Alli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Milton Keynes Dons (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Following his transfer to Tottenham Hotspur, Dele was immediately loaned back to Milton Keynes Dons for the remainder of the 2014–15 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On 19 April, he was chosen as the Young Player of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Awards" title="Football League Awards">Football League Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-27">[27]</a></sup> The season ended on 3 May with Milton Keynes Dons promoted automatically to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> as runners-up behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bristol_City" title="Bristol City">Bristol City</a>, after a 5–1 home win over relegated <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-one_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-one-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dele_Alli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 8 August 2015, Dele made his Tottenham debut against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> as a substitute in a 1–0 defeat away at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a>, playing the last 13 minutes in place of <a href="/wiki/Eric_Dier" title="Eric Dier">Eric Dier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Two weeks later, he scored his first goal for the club after coming on for <a href="/wiki/Christian_Eriksen" title="Christian Eriksen">Christian Eriksen</a> in the 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 September, Dele made his first start for Tottenham in a 1–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Stadium_of_Light" title="Stadium of Light">Stadium of Light</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On 2 November, he started and scored the second goal in an eventual 3–1 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Six days later he started his first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_London_Derby" title="North London Derby">North London Derby</a> alongside fellow England youngster Dier in central midfield, and was awarded Man of the Match in the 1–1 draw between rivals Arsenal and Tottenham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 5 December 2015, he scored his third goal for Tottenham in the 2015–16 campaign in a 1–1 draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion" title="West Bromwich Albion">West Bromwich Albion</a> at <a href="/wiki/The_Hawthorns" title="The Hawthorns">The Hawthorns</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following an impressive start to his Premier League career scoring five goals and making three assists in his first 18 league matches, he was rewarded with a new long-term contract until 2021 on 12 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Eleven days later he scored a 25-yard volley in a 3–1 win at <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>; <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC Sport</a> pundit and former Spurs player <a href="/wiki/Garth_Crooks" title="Garth Crooks">Garth Crooks</a> wrote "Well, I've seen some glorious goals scored in my time watching football matches but I doubt whether I will see a goal scored with such individual flair, and by a 19-year-old, as Dele Alli's goal at <a href="/wiki/Selhurst_Park" title="Selhurst Park">Selhurst Park</a> - it was sheer class".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On 13 April, he was named on the six-man shortlist for <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_in_English_football" title="2015–16 in English football">2015–16</a>'s <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 April 2016, he scored his first brace for Tottenham in a 0–4 away win at <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> reaching ten goals in his debut Premier League season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> He was voted the season's <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PFAPlayer_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-PFAPlayer-40">[40]</a></sup> On 28 April, Dele was banned by the Football Association for three matches, effectively ending his Premier League season, for an off-the-ball incident against West Brom in which he punched midfielder <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Yacob" title="Claudio Yacob">Claudio Yacob</a> in the stomach. He later apologised for the incident on Twitter, stating, "Gutted that my season is over. Shouldn't have reacted like I did. Will learn from this and come back stronger."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dele_Alli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Ahead of the season, Dele changed his kit name to his given name, saying that he had "no connection" to his legal surname due to his separation from his mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reason_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-reason-42">[42]</a></sup> His first goal of the season came in a 4-0 win against <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke</a> on 10 September 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Alli followed this up with a goal against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> in a 2-0 win on 2 October 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> and equalized late for a 1-1 draw against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> on 15 October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> He continued his fine form after scoring 3 consecutive braces against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> and the table toppers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C" title="Chelsea F.C">Chelsea</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> and broke the 13 game winning streak of Chelsea, who could have shared a record of 14 game winning streak with <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> had they defeated Tottenham.</p>

<p>Alli has made several appearances at U17 and U18 levels for England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> On 27 August 2014, Alli was called up to the England U19 squad following an impressive start to the campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> He made his debut for England U19 in the 1–1 draw against Germany U19. In the game, Alli assisted the opening goal feeding a through ball to <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Fewster" title="Bradley Fewster">Bradley Fewster</a> who gave England a 1–0 lead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2015, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/John_Fashanu" title="John Fashanu">John Fashanu</a> would try to convince Alli to play for Nigeria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> However, on 1 October of that year, he was included in <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> squad for the final <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying</a> matches against <a href="/wiki/Estonia_national_football_team" title="Estonia national football team">Estonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> He made his debut against the former on 9 October, coming on as a late substitute for <a href="/wiki/Ross_Barkley" title="Ross Barkley">Ross Barkley</a> in a 2–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 November 2015, Alli made his first start for the England senior team, scoring the opening goal from a long range shot to beat Spurs teammate, goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Lloris" title="Hugo Lloris">Hugo Lloris</a> in a 2–0 win against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> He was again named in the starting line-up for England's friendly match against world champions <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> on 26 March 2016. Alli was named man of the match by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="British Broadcasting Corporation">BBC</a> as England recovered from 0–2 down to win 3–2 at the Berlin <a href="/wiki/Olympiastadion_(Berlin)" title="Olympiastadion (Berlin)">Olympiastadion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dele_Alli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dele was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes" title="Milton Keynes">Milton Keynes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>, to a Yoruba Nigerian father Kenny<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> and English mother Denise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Kenny moved to the United States a week after Dele's birth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_story_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph_story-60">[60]</a></sup> Dele went to <a href="/wiki/Stantonbury_Campus" title="Stantonbury Campus">Stantonbury Campus</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-61">[61]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_Radcliffe_School" title="The Radcliffe School">The Radcliffe School</a> in Wolverton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dele was initially brought up by his mother, who suffered from alcohol problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_story_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph_story-60">[60]</a></sup> At the age of 13, he moved into the family home of Alan and Sally Hickford, parents of another young footballer with MK Dons and whom he refers to as his "adoptive parents" although he was never legally adopted by them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_story_60-2"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph_story-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> In the summer of 2016, Dele elected to stop wearing the name "Alli" on his match shirts because he felt no connection with the Alli family name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reason_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-reason-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dele is a close friend of <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> team mate <a href="/wiki/Eric_Dier" title="Eric Dier">Eric Dier</a>, with their relationship being described as a "<a href="/wiki/Bromance" title="Bromance">bromance</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dele was a Liverpool fan growing up, with <a href="/wiki/Steven_Gerrard" title="Steven Gerrard">Steven Gerrard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Puncheon" title="Jason Puncheon">Jason Puncheon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yaya_Tour%C3%A9" title="Yaya Touré">Yaya Touré</a> being his childhood idols.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport_19Sep2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
