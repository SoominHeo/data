<p>The <b><i>Delhi</i>-class destroyers</b> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guided-missile_destroyer" title="Guided-missile destroyer">guided-missile destroyers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy" title="Indian Navy">Indian Navy</a>. Three ships of this class are in active service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-in-destroyers_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-in-destroyers-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>Delhi</i>-class vessels are the third-largest warships to be fully designed and built in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, after the <a href="/wiki/Kolkata-class_destroyer" title="Kolkata-class destroyer"><i>Kolkata</i>-class destroyers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Shivalik-class_frigate" title="Shivalik-class frigate"><i>Shivalik</i>-class frigates</a>. They were built at <a href="/wiki/Mazagon_Dock_Limited" title="Mazagon Dock Limited">Mazagon Dock Limited</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Delhi-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The development of the ship began as "Project 15" in 1977. The ships were designed indigenously and built by Mazagon Docks Limited (MDL) at a cost of <a href="/wiki/Indian_rupee" title="Indian rupee">Rs</a> 7 Billion each.</p>
<p>The <i>Delhi</i> class has both Soviet and Western design influences, incorporating elements of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sovremenny-class_destroyer" title="Sovremenny-class destroyer"><i>Sovremenny</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Rajput-class_destroyer" title="Rajput-class destroyer"><i>Rajput</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Kashin-class_destroyer" title="Kashin-class destroyer">Kashin-II</a>-class destroyers, and the <a href="/wiki/Godavari-class_frigate" title="Godavari-class frigate"><i>Godavari</i>-class</a> frigate.</p>
<p>These vessels are fully fitted with flag facilities. The <i>Delhi</i> class is also capable of operating in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear,_biological_and_chemical_warfare" title="Nuclear, biological and chemical warfare">NBC</a> environment. <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">Radar</a> cross-section reduction is presumed to be minimal, to the extent that some sharp angles have been flattened.</p>
<p>In the main air defence role, a pair of 2 3S-90 launchers – one installed forward of the bridge and the other atop the dual helicopter hangar – are fitted with the Shtil SAM system. The Shtil system consists of the Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/9K37M1-2_Buk-M1-2" title="9K37M1-2 Buk-M1-2">Shtil</a> missile and 24 such missiles are carried in a below-decks magazine. The launchers elevate up to 70° but have a limited firing arc of 30° within the centreline. The launcher groups require a crew of 20 men and weigh about 50 tons.</p>
<p>The <i>Delhi</i> class is being upgraded with the Rafael <a href="/wiki/Barak_1" title="Barak 1">Barak 1</a> point air defence missile system, which overcomes the limited firing arc of the Shtil system. It has an eight-cell vertical launch system and the missile command-to-line-of-sight (CLOS) radar guidance with a range from 500 m (1,600 ft) to 10 km (6.2 mi). The missile's maximum range is 32 km (20 mi). The ship has a surveillance capacity of over 350 km (220 mi) and can sterilise an area of 250 km (160 mi).</p>
<p>The ships also have a quadruple 533 mm torpedo launcher, which can also be used to launch SS-N-15 'Starfish' or possibly SS-N-16 'Stallion' ASW missiles, so is capable of hitting targets ranging from 50 km (31 mi) to 120 km (75 mi). It is also equipped with two RBU-6000 anti-submarine rocket launchers with 12 tubes. Their range is 6 km (3.7 mi) and the maximum engagement depth is 500 m (1,600 ft).</p>
<p>Each ship can support two <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopters</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Westland_Sea_King" title="Westland Sea King">Sea King</a> or the <a href="/wiki/HAL_Dhruv" title="HAL Dhruv">HAL Dhruv</a>. The Sea King helicopters are equipped with a Super Searcher radar and primarily used in air surveillance and anti-submarine roles. The helicopters are capable of flying four and half hours non-stop. The <a href="/wiki/HAL_Dhruv" title="HAL Dhruv">HAL Dhruv</a> helicopters are primarily for utility roles. INS <i>Delhi</i> has a crew of approximately 30 officers and 350 sailors.</p>
<p>The ships are equipped with four chaff launch systems and the <a href="/wiki/Bharat_Electronics_Limited" title="Bharat Electronics Limited">BEL</a> Ajanta radar interceptor. The TQN-2 jamming system is supplied by the Italian company, Elettronica. The ship's hull mounted active search sonar capability is based on the TSM2633 by Thales Underwater Systems or the APSOH by Bharat. The <i>Delhi</i> class also has a variable depth sonar (VDS), Model 15-750 designed by Fathom Oceanology of Mississauga, Canada with a 15 feet (4.6 m) fish on 750 feet (230 m) of faired cable. The first VDS handling system was built by Fathom and the fish utilized a Westinghouse low frequency sonar. Fathom was acquired by Indal Canada which established subsequent manufacture of VDS handling systems by Indal and Garden Reach of India.</p>
