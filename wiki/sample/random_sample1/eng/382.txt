<p>The <b>11th <a href="/wiki/Pan_Arab_Games" title="Pan Arab Games">Pan Arab Games</a></b> took place in <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> from 11 – 26 November 2007. A total of 22 countries participated in 32 events. These games were the largest in the games' history; seven new events were introduced and 2000 more athletes participated than in previous years, making them 8000 Arab athletes. It was also the first time that some of the Arab football federations participated with their first teams.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Games">Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Pan_Arab_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Opening_ceremony">Opening ceremony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Pan_Arab_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Opening ceremony">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The opening ceremony started with the Egyptian Singer <a href="/wiki/Tamer_Hosny" title="Tamer Hosny">Tamer Hosny</a> singing a new song for Arab unity, and then the entrance of several Arab Leaders with <a href="/wiki/Hosni_Mubarak" title="Hosni Mubarak">Hosni Mubarak</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudanese</a> President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omar_Hassan_al-Bashir" title="Omar Hassan al-Bashir">Omar Hassan al-Bashir</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestinian_Authority" title="Palestinian Authority">Palestinian</a> President <a href="/wiki/Mahmoud_Abbas" title="Mahmoud Abbas">Mahmoud Abbas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemeni</a> President <a href="/wiki/Ali_Abdullah_Saleh" title="Ali Abdullah Saleh">Ali Abdullah Saleh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraqi</a> President <a href="/wiki/Jalal_Talabani" title="Jalal Talabani">Jalal Talabani</a>, and the Saudi Crown Prince <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_bin_Abdul_Aziz" title="Sultan bin Abdul Aziz">Sultan bin Abdul Aziz</a>. This was followed by the entrance of the 22 participating teams. The opening word was given to several Arab Sport Federation Chiefs, and the Secretary general of the <a href="/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amr_Moussa" title="Amr Moussa">Amr Moussa</a>, and ended with Egyptian President Mubarak's opening words.</p>
<p>The ceremony was divided into eight parts; it started with the Narration of the Abraham's Trip in the desert, and the founding of the Ka'aba, thus <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a>, and Arab Unity under <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, and stressed on the power of Words in the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>, and then it showed several dances showing the Arab Literature and Arts, then showed performances explaining the Arab's leadership in philosophy, mathematics, astronomy, medicine, gardening and music. Throughout the performance, several Arab scientists, poets, philosophers, mathematicians and travellers were mentioned as well as several verses of the Quran, and how it helped Arabs to achieve such glorious achievements. The Ceremony was wrapped up with the Egyptian National Anthem then an Arab Song called "Amgad Ya Arab" (Glories o Arabs).</p>
