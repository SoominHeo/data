<p><i><b>The Crucible</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">도가니</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Dogani</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/McCune%E2%80%93Reischauer" title="McCune–Reischauer">MR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Togani</span></i>; also known as <i><b>Silenced</b></i>) is a 2011 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Korean_film" title="South Korean film">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> based on <a href="/wiki/The_Crucible_(novel)" title="The Crucible (novel)">the novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Gong_Ji-young" title="Gong Ji-young">Gong Ji-young</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> starring <a href="/wiki/Gong_Yoo" title="Gong Yoo">Gong Yoo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jung_Yu-mi_(actress_born_1983)" title="Jung Yu-mi (actress born 1983)">Jung Yu-mi</a>. It is based on actual events that took place at <a href="/wiki/Gwangju_Inhwa_School" title="Gwangju Inhwa School">Gwangju Inhwa School</a> for the hearing-impaired, where young <a href="/wiki/Deaf_culture" title="Deaf culture">deaf students</a> were the victims of repeated sexual assaults by faculty members over a period of five years in the early 2000s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Depicting both the crimes and the court proceedings that let the teachers off with minimal punishment, the film sparked public outrage upon its September 2011 release, which eventually resulted in a reopening of the investigations into the incidents. With over 4 million people in Korea having watched the film, the demand for legislative reform reached all the way to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_South_Korea" title="National Assembly of South Korea">National Assembly</a>, where a revised bill, dubbed the <i>Dogani Bill</i>, was passed in late October 2011 to abolish the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_limitations" title="Statute of limitations">statute of limitations</a> for sex crimes against minors and the disabled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gateway_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-gateway-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Crucible_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film sparked public outcry over lenient court rulings, prompting police to reopen the case and lawmakers to introduce bills for the human rights of the vulnerable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Four out of the six teachers at the <a href="/wiki/Gwangju_Inhwa_School" title="Gwangju Inhwa School">Gwangju Inhwa School</a> for whom serious punishment was recommended by the education authority were reinstated after they escaped punishment under the statute of limitations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Only two of them were convicted of repeated rapes of eight young students and received jail terms of less than a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> 71-year-old ex teacher Kim Yeong-il recently claimed that two children had died when the incident took place in 1964, after which he was beaten and forced to resign his job by the vice principal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Two months after the film's release and resulting controversy, <a href="/wiki/Gwangju" title="Gwangju">Gwangju City</a> officially shut down the school in November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In July 2012, the Gwangju District Court sentenced the 63-year-old former administrator of <a href="/wiki/Gwangju_Inhwa_School" title="Gwangju Inhwa School">Gwangju Inhwa School</a> to 12 years in prison for sexually assaulting an 18-year-old student in April 2005. He was also charged with physically abusing another 17-year-old student who had witnessed the crime (the victim reportedly attempted to commit suicide afterward). The administrator, only identified by his surname Kim, was also ordered to wear an electronic anklet for 10 years following his release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Crucible_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kang In-ho (<a href="/wiki/Gong_Yoo" title="Gong Yoo">Gong Yoo</a>) is the newly appointed art teacher at Benevolence Academy, a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Deaf_school&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Deaf school (page does not exist)">deaf school</a> for children in the fictional city of Mujin, <a href="/wiki/North_Jeolla_Province" title="North Jeolla Province">North Jeolla Province</a>. He has a dark past - his wife was a high school student who committed suicide and his daughter is under the care of his mother. He is excited to teach his new students, yet the children are aloof and distant, trying to avoid running into him as much as possible. In-ho does not give up, however, trying to show the kids that he cares. When the children finally open up, In-ho faces the shocking and ugly truth about the school and what the students have been enduring in secret: the children are being physically and sexually abused by their teachers. When he decides to fight for the children’s rights and expose the crimes being committed at the school, In-ho teams up with human rights activist Seo Yoo-jin (<a href="/wiki/Jung_Yu-mi_(actress_born_1983)" title="Jung Yu-mi (actress born 1983)">Jung Yu-mi</a>). But he and Yoo-jin soon realize the school’s principal and teachers, and even the police, prosecutors and churches in the community are actually trying to cover up the truth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In addition to using "privileges of former post" (<a href="/wiki/Jeon-gwan_ye-u" title="Jeon-gwan ye-u">Jeon-gwan ye-u</a>) the accused do not hesitate to lie and bribe their way to get very light sentences.</p>

<p>In Korea the film ranked #1 for three consecutive weeks and grossed ₩7.8 billion in its first week of release<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-review_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-review-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and grossed a total of ₩35 billion after ten weeks of screening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the film's release, the bestselling book of the same name by author <a href="/wiki/Gong_Ji-young" title="Gong Ji-young">Gong Ji-young</a>, which first recounted the crimes and provided the bulk of the film’s content, topped national bestseller lists for the first time in two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gateway_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-gateway-5">[5]</a></sup> Ruling conservative political party <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_National_Party" title="Grand National Party">Grand National Party</a> (GNP) then called for an investigation into Gong Ji-young for engaging in "political activities", a move that was met with public derision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>It received the Audience Award at the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Udine" title="Udine">Udine</a> Far East Film Festival in Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Conversations about the film and its impact re-emerged when the Samsung Economic Research Institute (SERI) released its annual survey of the year’s top ten consumer favorites on December 7, 2011. Based on a poll of market analysts and nearly 8,000 consumers, SERI’s "Korea’s Top Ten Hits of 2011" ranked <i>The Crucible</i> among the year’s top events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gateway_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-gateway-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_release">International release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Crucible_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film's international title is <i>Silenced</i>. On November 4, 2011, the film was released in select theaters in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huntington_Beach" title="Huntington Beach">Huntington Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>. It has been reviewed by <a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
