<p><b>Yūto Tonokawa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">都乃河 勇人</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tonokawa Yūto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born January 25)</span> (<a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonym</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> scenario writer originally from <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. He had worked for the <a href="/wiki/Visual_novel" title="Visual novel">visual novel</a> studio <a href="/wiki/Key_(company)" title="Key (company)">Key</a> under <a href="/wiki/VisualArt%27s" title="VisualArt's">VisualArt's</a>. He began working with Key on their sixth game <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Busters!" title="Little Busters!">Little Busters!</a></i> and wrote the scenarios for two of the main heroines: Komari Kamikita, and Yuiko Kurugaya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erogamescape_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-erogamescape-1">[1]</a></sup> Tonokawa also wrote the scenario for Sasami Sasasegawa in <i>Little Busters! Ecstasy</i>. He provided the lyrics to one of the ending themes from the same game, "Alicemagic", as his first job as a <a href="/wiki/Lyricist" title="Lyricist">lyricist</a>. Tonokawa wrote the scenarios for Chihaya Ohtori and Sizuru Nakatsu in Key's ninth game <i><a href="/wiki/Rewrite_(visual_novel)" title="Rewrite (visual novel)">Rewrite</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rewrite_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rewrite-2">[2]</a></sup> Among Key's staff, Tonokawa updated the company's official <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blog</a> the most often.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Despite his accomplishments, Tonokawa has referred to himself as "only an incompetent writer."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In July 2008, Tonokawa was living in the same apartment building in <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Prefecture" title="Osaka Prefecture">Osaka</a> that <a href="/wiki/Jun_Maeda" title="Jun Maeda">Jun Maeda</a> had once lived in, though he did not know this when he moved into the building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Tonokawa resigned from Key in 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Y%C5%ABto_Tonokawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Y%C5%ABto_Tonokawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yūto Tonokawa started writing while in junior-high school, though these were only <a href="/wiki/Short_story" title="Short story">short stories</a>. He stated himself that while he never read much, he did read <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novels</a> and modern literature while in junior-high. While in high school, Tonokawa read works by the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher" title="Philosopher">philosophers</a> <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8ren_Kierkegaard" title="Søren Kierkegaard">Søren Kierkegaard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_Wittgenstein" title="Ludwig Wittgenstein">Ludwig Wittgenstein</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_folklore" title="Japanese folklore">Japanese folklorists</a> <a href="/wiki/Kunio_Yanagita" title="Kunio Yanagita">Kunio Yanagita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shinobu_Orikuchi" title="Shinobu Orikuchi">Shinobu Orikuchi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Tonokawa at one point wanted to be either a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seiy%C5%AB" title="Seiyū">voice actor</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mangaka" title="Mangaka">manga author</a>, and it was not until after he entered high school that he decided to become a scenario writer. Since he wanted to participate in a job that told stories, he feels that either way he has achieved that goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> While still in school, Tonokawa experimented on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KiriKiri" title="KiriKiri">KiriKiri</a> <a href="/wiki/Scripting_language" title="Scripting language">scripting</a> <a href="/wiki/Game_engine" title="Game engine">engine</a> and made an amateur "novel game", but it did not have visuals or music. He felt it was a good learning experience as a scenario writer since he discovered how different it is to write regular stories compared to writing scenarios for a game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Y%C5%ABto_Tonokawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Originally, Tonokawa came to <a href="/wiki/VisualArt%27s" title="VisualArt's">VisualArt's</a> and applied under a different brand under the publishing company, but coincidentally the timing was just right since <a href="/wiki/Jun_Maeda" title="Jun Maeda">Jun Maeda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Key_(company)" title="Key (company)">Key</a> was looking for another writer to join the team, which ultimately led to Tonokawa joining Key as a scenario writer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Tonokawa was able to initially catch the attention of the application examiners with a 600-page <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9sum%C3%A9" title="Résumé">résumé</a> featuring a story centering on a female protagonist, but since there was no market for this format at the time, he later filled out a regular application.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> As noted by himself, Tonokawa started working for Key almost immediately after graduating from university,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and he sees this as a major turning point in his life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Tonokawa said that Maeda was a major influence on his life, and if not for Maeda, he would not be where he is today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Originally, Tonokawa was supposed to work in the role of an outsourced writer who would help Maeda as a scenario assistant, but was brought in-house due to a quick need for a new writer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Due to Maeda stepping down as the main scenario writer for Key after the production of <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Busters!" title="Little Busters!">Little Busters! Ecstasy</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Tonokawa was the sole writer within Key at the time.</p>
<p>At the time Tonokawa joined Key in August 2005, the team was working on debugging <i><a href="/wiki/Tomoyo_After:_It%27s_a_Wonderful_Life" title="Tomoyo After: It's a Wonderful Life">Tomoyo After: It's a Wonderful Life</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He started working with Key on their sixth game <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Busters!" title="Little Busters!">Little Busters!</a></i> and wrote the scenarios for two of the main heroines: Komari Kamikita, and Yuiko Kurugaya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erogamescape_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-erogamescape-1">[1]</a></sup> Tonokawa also wrote the scenario for Sasami Sasasegawa in <i>Little Busters! Ecstasy</i>. He provided the lyrics to one of the ending themes from <i>Little Busters!</i>, "Alicemagic", as his first job as a <a href="/wiki/Lyricist" title="Lyricist">lyricist</a>, though feels that composing music is difficult.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Tonokawa also wrote some of the additional story for Takafumi and Kanako included in the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> version of <i>Tomoyo After: It's a Wonderful Life</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Tonokawa wrote the scenarios for Chihaya Ohtori and Sizuru Nakatsu in Key's ninth game <i><a href="/wiki/Rewrite_(visual_novel)" title="Rewrite (visual novel)">Rewrite</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rewrite_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rewrite-2">[2]</a></sup> Tonokawa resigned from Key in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
