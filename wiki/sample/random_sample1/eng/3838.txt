<p><b>Tōdō Takatora</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">藤堂 高虎</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, February 16, 1556 – November 9, 1630)</span> was a Japanese <i><a href="/wiki/Daimy%C5%8D" title="Daimyō">daimyō</a></i> from the <a href="/wiki/Azuchi%E2%80%93Momoyama_period" title="Azuchi–Momoyama period">Azuchi–Momoyama</a> to <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo periods</a>. He rose from relatively humble origins as an <a href="/wiki/Ashigaru" title="Ashigaru">ashigaru</a> (a foot soldier) to become a <i>daimyō</i>. During his lifetime he changed his feudal master seven times and worked for ten people, but in the end he rendered loyalty to <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a>, who became his last master.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhk_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhk-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsu_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tsu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Tōdō Takatora was promoted rapidly under <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hidenaga" title="Toyotomi Hidenaga">Hashiba Hidenaga</a>, the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyoshi" title="Toyotomi Hideyoshi">Toyotomi Hideyoshi</a>, and he participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_invasions_of_Korea_(1592%E2%80%931598)" title="Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–1598)">invasions of Korea</a> as a commander of Toyotomi's fleet. His fiefdom at that time was <a href="/wiki/Iyo_Province" title="Iyo Province">Iyo</a>-<a href="/wiki/Uwajima,_Ehime" title="Uwajima, Ehime">Uwajima</a>. During the Edo period, the wealth of each fiefdom was measured as a volume of rice production in <i><a href="/wiki/Koku" title="Koku">koku</a></i>. Iyo-Uwajima was assessed at 70,000 koku.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhk_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nhk-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsu_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tsu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sekigahara" title="Battle of Sekigahara">Battle of Sekigahara</a> in 1600, although he was one of Toyotomi's main generals, he sided with Tokugawa Ieyasu. After the war he was given a larger fiefdom, Iyo-<a href="/wiki/Imabari,_Ehime" title="Imabari, Ehime">Imabari</a>, assessed at 200,000 koku. Later in life he was made lord of <a href="/wiki/Tsu_Domain" title="Tsu Domain">Tsu</a> (with landholdings in <a href="/wiki/Iga_Province" title="Iga Province">Iga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ise_Province" title="Ise Province">Ise</a>), a domain of 320,000 koku.</p>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Akai_Naomasa" title="Akai Naomasa">Akai Naomasa</a>, some members of the Akai clan became retainers to the Tōdō house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tōdō Takatora is also famous for excellence in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_castle" title="Japanese castle">castle design</a>. He is said to have been involved in building as many as twenty castles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhk_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-nhk-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsu_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-tsu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

In Samurai Warriors 4, he is portrayed as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azai" title="Azai">Azai</a> retainer and a close friend of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Ctani_Yoshitsugu" title="Ōtani Yoshitsugu">Yoshitsugu Ōtani</a> before joining the Toyotomi.</li>
