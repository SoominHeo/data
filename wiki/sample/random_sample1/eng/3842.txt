<p><b>Dorogobuzh</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Дорогобу́ж</span>; <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl" xml:lang="pl"><i>Dorohobuż</i></span>) is a historic <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dorogobuzhsky_District" title="Dorogobuzhsky District">Dorogobuzhsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Smolensk_Oblast" title="Smolensk Oblast">Smolensk Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, straddling the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dnieper_River" title="Dnieper River">Dnieper River</a> and located 125 kilometers (78 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Smolensk" title="Smolensk">Smolensk</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">10,720 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">12,250 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-8">[8]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">12,254 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dorogobuzh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First mentioned in 1150, it was established as a fortress defending eastern approaches to <a href="/wiki/Smolensk" title="Smolensk">Smolensk</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1508, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasily_III_of_Russia" title="Vasily III of Russia">Vasily III</a> sent Italian masters to build a wooden fort there. Dorogobuzh was ravaged during the <a href="/wiki/Time_of_Troubles" title="Time of Troubles">Time of Troubles</a>, with its population reduced to ten people in 1614. Between 1611 and 1668, it belonged to <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a> as a part of <a href="/wiki/Smolensk_Voivodeship" title="Smolensk Voivodeship">Smolensk Voivodeship</a>. Subsequently, it survived other foreign invasions and numerous fires.</p>

<p>Dorogobuzh was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">German Army</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> first from October 5, 1941 to February 15, 1942 (when it was liberated by three partisan detachments and some Soviet troops), then again from June 7, 1942 to September 1, 1943.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dorogobuzh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Dorogobuzh serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dorogobuzhsky_District" title="Dorogobuzhsky District">Dorogobuzhsky District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SmolenskO_admlist_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-SmolenskO_admlist-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is incorporated within Dorogobuzhsky District as <b>Dorogobuzhskoye <a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">Urban Settlement</a></b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SmolenskO_admlist_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-SmolenskO_admlist-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, this administrative unit also has urban settlement status and is a part of Dorogobuzhsky Municipal District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SmolenskODorogobuzhskyD_mun_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-SmolenskODorogobuzhskyD_mun-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
