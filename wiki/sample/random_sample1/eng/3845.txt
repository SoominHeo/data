<p><b>Dordrecht</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈdɔrdrɛxt]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Nl-Dordrecht.ogg" title="File:Nl-Dordrecht.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/ff/Nl-Dordrecht.ogg" title="Nl-Dordrecht.ogg">listen</a>)</small>), colloquially <b>Dordt</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[dɔrt]</a></span>, historically in English named <b>Dort</b>, is a city and municipality in the western <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, located in the province of <a href="/wiki/South_Holland" title="South Holland">South Holland</a>. It is the fourth largest city of the province, having a population of 118,782 in 2014. The municipality covers the entire Dordrecht Island, also often called <i>Het Eiland van Dordt</i> (the Island of Dordt), bordered by the rivers <a href="/wiki/Oude_Maas" title="Oude Maas">Oude Maas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beneden_Merwede" title="Beneden Merwede">Beneden Merwede</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nieuwe_Merwede" title="Nieuwe Merwede">Nieuwe Merwede</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hollands_Diep" title="Hollands Diep">Hollands Diep</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dordtsche_Kil" title="Dordtsche Kil">Dordtsche Kil</a>. Dordrecht is the largest and most important city in the <a href="/wiki/Drechtsteden" title="Drechtsteden">Drechtsteden</a> and is also part of the <a href="/wiki/Randstad" title="Randstad">Randstad</a>, the main <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a> in the Netherlands. Dordrecht is the oldest city in the <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">Holland</a> area and has a rich history and culture.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Dordrecht comes from <i>Thuredriht</i> (ca 1120), <i>Thuredrecht</i> (ca 1200). The name seems to mean 'thoroughfare'; a ship-canal or -river through which ships were pulled by rope from one river to another, as here from the Dubbel to the Merwede, or vice versa. Earlier etymologists had assumed that the 'drecht' suffix came from Latin 'trajectum', a ford, but this was rejected in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The Drecht is now supposed to have been derived from 'draeg', which means to pull, tow or drag. Inhabitants of Dordrecht are <i>Dordtenaren</i> (singular: <i>Dordtenaar</i>). Dordrecht is informally called <i>Dordt</i> by its inhabitants. In earlier centuries, Dordrecht was a major trade port, well known to British merchants, and was called Dort in English.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_History">Early History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city was formed along the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thure_river&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thure river (page does not exist)">Thure river</a>, in the midst of peat swamps. This river was a branch of the river Dubbel, which is part of the massive <a href="/wiki/Rhine%E2%80%93Meuse%E2%80%93Scheldt_delta" title="Rhine–Meuse–Scheldt delta">Rhine–Meuse–Scheldt delta</a> complex, near the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bagijnhof" title="Bagijnhof">Bagijnhof</a>. Around 1120 reference to Dordrecht was made by a remark that count <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dirk_IV_of_Holland" title="Dirk IV of Holland">Dirk IV of Holland</a> was murdered in 1049 near "<i>Thuredrech</i>".</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="790" data-file-width="1079" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg/200px-Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg/300px-Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg/400px-Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Aelbert_Cuyp_001.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
View of Dordrecht, by <a href="/wiki/Aelbert_Cuyp" title="Aelbert Cuyp">Aelbert Cuyp</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Dordrecht was granted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_rights_in_the_Netherlands" title="City rights in the Netherlands">city rights</a> by <a href="/wiki/William_I,_Count_of_Holland" title="William I, Count of Holland">William I, Count of Holland</a>, in 1220, making it the oldest city in the present province of <a href="/wiki/South_Holland" title="South Holland">South Holland</a>. In fact, <a href="/wiki/Geertruidenberg" title="Geertruidenberg">Geertruidenberg</a> was the first city in the historical county of <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">Holland</a> to receive city rights, but this municipality currently is part of the province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noord-Brabant" title="Noord-Brabant">Noord-Brabant</a>.</p>
<p>In the 12th and 13th centuries, Dordrecht developed into an important market city because of its strategic location. It traded primarily in <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cereals" title="Cereals">cereals</a>. Dordrecht was made even more important when it was given <a href="/wiki/Staple_right" title="Staple right">staple right</a> in 1299.</p>
<p>In 1253 a <a href="/wiki/Latin_school" title="Latin school">Latin school</a> was founded in Dordrecht. It still exists today as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Johan_de_Witt_Gymnasium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Johan de Witt Gymnasium (page does not exist)">Johan de Witt Gymnasium</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johan_de_Witt-gymnasium" title="nl:Johan de Witt-gymnasium">nl</a>)</span> and is the oldest <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasium</a> in the Netherlands. From 1600 to 1615 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gerhard_Johann_Vossius" title="Gerhard Johann Vossius">Gerhard Johann Vossius</a> was <a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a> at this school.</p>
<p>On 18–19 November 1421, the <a href="/wiki/St._Elizabeth%27s_flood_(1421)" title="St. Elizabeth's flood (1421)">Saint Elisabeth's flood</a> flooded large parts of southern Holland, causing Dordrecht to become an island. It was commonly said that over 10,000 people died in the flood, but recent research indicates that it was probably less than 200 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Union_of_Dordrecht">Union of Dordrecht</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Union of Dordrecht">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1747" data-file-width="2000" height="192" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg/220px-Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg/330px-Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg/440px-Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dordrecht_vanDeventer.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Dordrecht around 1565, by Jacob van Deventer</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1572, four years into the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Revolt" title="Dutch Revolt">Dutch Revolt</a>, representatives of all the cities of Holland, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watergeuzen" title="Watergeuzen">Watergeuzen</a>, represented by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_II_de_la_Marck" title="William II de la Marck">William II de la Marck</a>, gathered in Dordrecht to hold the <i>Eerste Vrije Statenvergadering</i> ("First Assembly of the Free <a href="/wiki/States_of_Holland_and_West_Friesland" title="States of Holland and West Friesland">States</a>"), also known as the <i>Unie van Dordrecht</i> ("Union of Dordrecht"). This secret meeting, called by the city of Dordrecht, was a rebellious act since only <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">King Philip II</a> or his <a href="/wiki/Stadtholder" title="Stadtholder">stadtholder</a>, at that time the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_Alva" title="Duke of Alva">Duke of Alva</a>, were allowed to call a meeting of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/States_of_Holland" title="States of Holland">States of Holland</a>.</p>
<p>During the meeting, the organization and financing of the rebellion against the Spanish occupation was discussed, Phillip II was unanimously denounced, and <a href="/wiki/William_the_Silent" title="William the Silent">William of Orange</a> was chosen as the rightful <a href="/wiki/Stadtholder" title="Stadtholder">stadtholder</a> and recognized as the official leader of the revolt. Orange, represented at the meeting by his assistant <a href="/wiki/Philips_of_Marnix,_Lord_of_Saint-Aldegonde" title="Philips of Marnix, Lord of Saint-Aldegonde">Philips of Marnix</a>, was promised financial support of his struggle against the Spanish and at his own request, <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_religion" title="Freedom of religion">freedom of religion</a> was declared in all of Holland.</p>
<p>The gathering is regarded as the first important step towards the free and independent <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Other important gatherings such as the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Brussels" title="Union of Brussels">Union of Brussels</a> (1577) and the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Utrecht" title="Union of Utrecht">Union of Utrecht</a> (1579) paved the way for official independence of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a>, declared in the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Abjuration" title="Act of Abjuration">Act of Abjuration</a> in 1581.</p>
<p>The Union of Dordrecht was held in an Augustinian monastery, nowadays simply called <i>het Hof</i> ("the Court"). The room in which the meeting was held is called <i>de Statenzaal</i> ("The Hall of States") and features a <a href="/wiki/Stained_glass" title="Stained glass">stained glass</a> window in which the coats of arms of the twelve cities that were present at the meeting can be seen.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Synod_of_Dordrecht">Synod of Dordrecht</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Synod of Dordrecht">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Synodedordrecht.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="471" height="299" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Synodedordrecht.jpg/220px-Synodedordrecht.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Synodedordrecht.jpg/330px-Synodedordrecht.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Synodedordrecht.jpg/440px-Synodedordrecht.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Synodedordrecht.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synod_of_Dordrecht" title="Synod of Dordrecht">Synod of Dordrecht</a> 1618/19</div>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Synod_of_Dort" title="Synod of Dort">Synod of Dort</a></div>
<p>From November 13, 1618 to May 9, 1619, an important <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reformed" title="Reformed">Dutch Reformed Church</a> assembly took place in Dordrecht, referred to as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synod_of_Dordrecht" title="Synod of Dordrecht">Synod of Dordrecht</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Synod_of_Dort_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Synod_of_Dort-9">[9]</a></sup> The synod attempted, and succeeded, to settle the theological differences of opinion between the central tenets of <a href="/wiki/Calvinism" title="Calvinism">Calvinism</a>, and a new school of thought within the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed_Church" title="Dutch Reformed Church">Dutch Reformed Church</a> known as <a href="/wiki/Arminianism" title="Arminianism">Arminianism</a>, named for its spiritual leader <a href="/wiki/Jacobus_Arminius" title="Jacobus Arminius">Jacobus Arminius</a>. Arminius' followers were also commonly known as <a href="/wiki/Remonstrants" title="Remonstrants">Remonstrants</a>, after the 1610 <a href="/wiki/Five_Articles_of_Remonstrance" title="Five Articles of Remonstrance">Five Articles of Remonstrance</a> which outlined their points of dissent from the church's official doctrine. They were opposed by the Contra-Remonstrants, or the Gomarists, who were led by Dutch theologian <a href="/wiki/Franciscus_Gomarus" title="Franciscus Gomarus">Franciscus Gomarus</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Years%27_Truce" title="Twelve Years' Truce">Twelve Years' Truce</a>, this in essence purely theological conflict between different factions of the church had in practice spilled over into politics, dividing society along ideological lines, and threatening the existence of the young republic by repeatedly bringing it to the brink of civil war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The synod was attended by Gomarist Dutch delegates and also by delegates from Reformed churches in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Though it was originally intended that the synod would bring agreement on the doctrine of predestination among all the Reformed churches, in practice this Dutch synod was mainly concerned with problems facing the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed_Church" title="Dutch Reformed Church">Dutch Reformed Church</a>.</p>
<p>The opening sessions dealt with a new Dutch translation of the Bible, a catechism, and the censorship of books. The synod then called upon representatives of the Remonstrants to express their beliefs. The Remonstrants refused to accept the rules established by the synod and eventually were expelled from the church.</p>
<p>The synod then studied the theology of the Remonstrants and declared that it was contrary to Scripture. The <a href="/wiki/Canons_of_Dort" title="Canons of Dort">Canons of Dort</a> were produced; they discussed in detail in five sections the errors of the Remonstrants that were rejected as well as the doctrines that were affirmed. The doctrines affirmed were that predestination is not conditional on belief; that Christ did not die for all; the total depravity of man; the irresistible grace of God; and the impossibility of falling from grace. These canons of Dort, along with the Belgic Confession and the Heidelberg Catechism, remain the theological basis of the Reformed Churches in the Netherlands.</p>
<p>Following the synod, two hundred Remonstrant ministers were deposed from their office, of which eighty were banished. The political leaders of the Remonstrant movement were arrested and one of them beheaded on May 14, 1619.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It was only after the death of Prince Maurice in 1625 that the persecution of the Remonstrants ceased.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Patriots_movement">The Patriots movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Patriots movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1780 to 1787, Dordrecht was home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patriots_(faction)" title="Patriots (faction)">Patriots</a> faction which intended to remove the hereditary <a href="/wiki/Stadtholder" title="Stadtholder">Stadtholder</a> position held by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau" title="House of Orange-Nassau">House of Orange-Nassau</a>. The Netherlands was after all a republic <i><a href="/wiki/De_jure" title="De jure">de jure</a></i>. Soon after, more cities followed and <a href="/wiki/William_V,_Prince_of_Orange" title="William V, Prince of Orange">William V</a> fled from Holland. But his brother-in-law, King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_II_of_Prussia" title="Frederick William II of Prussia">Frederick William II of Prussia</a>, came to the aid of William V and on 18 September 1787, Dordrecht capitulated to Prussian troops. The Patriots were defeated and Willem V was restored in his position as Stadtholder.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_history">Modern history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Modern history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In the 18th century, the importance of Dordrecht began to wane, and <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> became the main city in the region. Yet throughout the centuries, Dordrecht held a key position in the defense of Holland. It hosted an army division well into the 20th century. During the <a href="/wiki/Mobilization" title="Mobilization">mobilization</a> of August 1939, infantry and artillery were sent to Dordrecht to defend the island. When the Germans invaded the Netherlands on the 10th of May 1940 German paratroopers landed in Dordrecht. After fierce fighting they overtook the bridges Dordrecht-Moerdijk and Dordrecht-Zwijndrecht. Many buildings in Dordrecht were destroyed.</p>
<p>At the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, during the winter of 1944–45, Dordrecht and its surroundings were in the middle between the opposing armies. The border between occupied and liberated regions ran along the <a href="/wiki/Hollands_Diep" title="Hollands Diep">Hollands Diep</a>. Dordrecht was finally liberated by the Canadian Army.</p>
<p>In 1970, the municipality <a href="/wiki/Dubbeldam" title="Dubbeldam">Dubbeldam</a> (then ca. 10,000 inhabitants) and the southern part of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Sliedrecht" title="Sliedrecht">Sliedrecht</a> were incorporated into Dordrecht, making Dordrecht Island one municipality.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Districts">Districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dordrecht is divided into 27 districts, neighbourhoods and <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlets</a>:</p>

<p>In 2008, around 17% of the population of Dordrecht was of non-Western origin. Between 2005 and 2008, this number has not changed. The allochthonous population consists for a large part of young people while the indigenous population has started to age very quickly. Over six thousand <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turks</a> live in Dordrecht, many of whom are originally from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kayapinar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kayapinar (page does not exist)">Kayapinar</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:292px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dordrecht,_by_Edmond_de_Schampheleer,_1873.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2056" data-file-width="3088" height="193" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Dordrecht%2C_by_Edmond_de_Schampheleer%2C_1873.JPG/290px-Dordrecht%2C_by_Edmond_de_Schampheleer%2C_1873.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Dordrecht%2C_by_Edmond_de_Schampheleer%2C_1873.JPG/435px-Dordrecht%2C_by_Edmond_de_Schampheleer%2C_1873.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Dordrecht%2C_by_Edmond_de_Schampheleer%2C_1873.JPG/580px-Dordrecht%2C_by_Edmond_de_Schampheleer%2C_1873.JPG 2x" width="290"/></a>

<p>Because of its long and important role in Dutch history, Dordrecht has a rich culture. The medieval city centre is home to over 950 monuments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The city also houses 7 historic churches and 6 museums in a relatively small area and hosts many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Festivals" title="Festivals">festivals</a> and events every year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_interest">Places of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Places of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Grote_Kerk,_Dordrecht.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="658" height="190" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Grote_Kerk%2C_Dordrecht.jpg/250px-Grote_Kerk%2C_Dordrecht.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Grote_Kerk%2C_Dordrecht.jpg/375px-Grote_Kerk%2C_Dordrecht.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Grote_Kerk%2C_Dordrecht.jpg/500px-Grote_Kerk%2C_Dordrecht.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Grote_Kerk,_Dordrecht.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Grote Kerk and one of the many canals (New Harbor)</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>The <b>Onze-Lieve-Vrouwe-Kerk</b> ('Our Dear Lady Church') or simply the <b><a href="/wiki/Grote_Kerk,_Dordrecht" title="Grote Kerk, Dordrecht">Grote Kerk</a></b> ('Big Church') was built between 1285 and 1470. The 65-meter tower contains a carillon with 67 bells including one weighing 9830 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kilos" title="Kilos">kilos</a>, making it the heaviest bell in the Netherlands.</li>
<li>The <b>Augustijnenkerk</b> ('Church of the Augustins') was built around 1293 and is currently owned by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed_Church" title="Dutch Reformed Church">Dutch Reformed Church</a>. The church includes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augustinian" title="Augustinian">Augustinian</a> Monastery <i>het Hof</i> ('the Court') which was built in 1275 and was the location of the First Assembly of the Free States.</li>
<li>The <b>Nieuwkerk</b> ('New Church') or St Nicolaas Kerk was built in 1175 and is, ironically considering its name, the oldest building in Dordrecht.</li>
<li>The <b>Munt van Holland</b> ('Mint of Holland'), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mint_(coin)" title="Mint (coin)">mint</a> built in 1366. The majority of the coins used in the region of <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">Holland</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Middle_Ages" title="The Middle Ages">the Middle Ages</a> were struck here. Nowadays, the building houses a <a href="/wiki/Music_school" title="Music school">music school</a>.</li>
<li><b>Kyck over den Dyck</b> ('View over the Dike'), the last <a href="/wiki/Windmill" title="Windmill">windmill</a> in Dordrecht. It was built in 1612 and used to produce <a href="/wiki/Malt" title="Malt">malt</a> that was used by Dordts <a href="/wiki/Brewery" title="Brewery">beer brewers</a>.</li>
<li>The <b><a href="/wiki/Groothoofdspoort" title="Groothoofdspoort">Groothoofdspoort</a></b> ('Big Head's Gate') is the original <a href="/wiki/City_gate" title="City gate">city gate</a> of Dordrecht, built in the 14th and 15th centuries. It is situated at the point where the rivers the <a href="/wiki/Meuse" title="Meuse">Meuse</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Merwede" title="Merwede">Merwede</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> meet.</li>
<li>Arend Maartenshof (Arend Maarten's Court), built in 1625.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/City_Hall,_Dordrecht" title="City Hall, Dordrecht">Stadhuis city hall</a>, built in 1383.</li>
<li>Statue of <a href="/wiki/Ary_Scheffer" title="Ary Scheffer">Ary Scheffer</a> (1861), by Joseph Mezzara.</li>
<li>Statue of <a href="/wiki/Johan_de_Witt" title="Johan de Witt">Johan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_de_Witt" title="Cornelis de Witt">Cornelis de Witt</a> (1918), by Toon Depuis.</li>
<li>River quais.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">Canals</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">Merchant</a> houses.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museums">Museums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Museums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following museums are located in Dordrecht:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dordrechts_Museum" title="Dordrechts Museum">Dordrechts Museum</a>, informally called <i>Schilderijenmuseum</i> (the paintings museum). Every summer, its garden, known as <i>de Museumtuin</i> (the Museum garden), hosts the showing of several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_house" title="Art house">art house</a> films that gained significant attention in the previous year. Re-opened in late 2010 after an extensive renovation.</li>
<li>Simon van Gijnmuseum, named after honorary citizen Simon van Gijn and winner of the museum prize 2004–2005, awarded by the <a href="/wiki/Prins_Bernhard_Cultuurfonds" title="Prins Bernhard Cultuurfonds">Prins Bernhard Cultuurfonds</a>.</li>
<li>Nationaal Landschapskundig Museum, (National Museum of <a href="/wiki/Landscaping" title="Landscaping">Landscaping</a>)</li>
<li>Lips Slotenmuseum, (Lips Museum of Locks) The museum has closed down and it is not known when or indeed if the inventory will be on display again.</li>
<li>Museum 1940–1945, also known as <i>het Verzetsmuseum</i> (the Resistance museum).</li>
<li>Naaimachinemuseum Dordrecht, (Museum of Sewing machines Dordrecht)</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_and_festivals">Events and festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Events and festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dordrecht hosts around 20 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cultural" title="Cultural">cultural</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historical" title="Historical">historical</a> events and festivals each year. The city won the title of "Best events city of the year" in 2003<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and was nominated for the same title in 2004 and 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Dordt in Stoom</i> (literally: Dordt in steam) is the biggest steam event in Europe during which historical steam trains, steam boats etc. can be seen in action. It is organized every other year and attracts a quarter of a million visitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Attention is also paid to Dordrecht’s <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a> during <i>Kunstrondje Dordt</i> (literally: Little art circle Dordt) and <i>Dordt Monumenteel</i> (Dordt Monumental), which attracts around 100.000 visitors every year.</p>
<p>Dordrecht hosts the second largest book market<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and the largest <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> market in the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Wantijfestival</i> is an out-doors music festival that has been held annually in the second week of June since 1995. It takes place in the Wantij park and attracts around 35.000 people each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Wantij park also hosts the <i>Wantijconcerten</i> (Wantij concerts) that are held every Monday night in July and August. Other popular music festivals held in Dordrecht are the <i>World Jazz dagen</i> (World Jazz days) held annually in August or September, the <i>Dancetour</i> or <i>Boulevard of Dance</i>, which takes place on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koninginnedag" title="Koninginnedag">Queensday</a>, <i>Big Rivers Festival</i>, a film, music, poetry and theatre festival held in June, and the Cello festival, held every four years in the weekend of <a href="/wiki/Feast_of_the_Ascension" title="Feast of the Ascension">the Ascension</a>.</p>
<p>Dordrecht’s annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funfair" title="Funfair">funfair</a> is among the highest-ranked in the Netherlands and the International Puppet festival, held in June, includes performances from artists from all over the world, including Italy, Germany, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, Israel and the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>A website with a list of the festivals in the city Dordrecht: <a class="external text" href="http://www.dordrechtfestivals.nl/" rel="nofollow">Dordrecht Festivals</a></p>

<p>During <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnaval</a>, Dordrecht is called <i>Ooi- en Ramsgat</i> (Ewe's and Ram's hole), and its inhabitants are <i>Schapenkoppen</i> (Sheepheads). This name originates from an old folk story. Import of meat or cattle was taxed in the 17th century. To avoid having to pay, two men dressed up a sheep they had bought outside the city walls, attempting to disguise it as a man. The sheep was discovered because it bleated as the three men (two men and one sheep) passed through the city wall gate. There is a special monument of a man and his son trying to hold a sheep disguised as a man between them, that refers to this legend. The logo of Dordrecht’s professional football club <a href="/wiki/FC_Dordrecht" title="FC Dordrecht">FC Dordrecht</a> includes the head of a ram and its supporters are known to sing <i>Wij zijn de Dordtse schapenkoppen</i> (we are the Dorsts sheep heads) during matches. There is also a cookie called <i>Schapenkop</i> (sheep head) which is a specialty of Dordrecht.</p>
<p>There are many more legends about Dordrecht. One of them is about Saint Sura, a young woman who planned on building an entire church with only three coins in her purse. She was murdered because of her supposed wealth.</p>
<p>Another legend is about the house called <i>de Onbeschaamde</i> (the Unembarrassed). It is about the three brothers Van Beveren who each wanted to build a house and decided to make a bet on who would dare to place the most risqué statue on their façade. One of the brothers, Abraham van Beveren, placed a naked little boy on his façade. However, the house that supposedly won has an empty façade today because, according to the story, the statue was so risqué that it was removed.</p>
<p>A well known saying about Dordrecht is <i>Hoe dichter bij Dordt, hoe rotter het wordt</i> (the closer to Dordrecht, the more rotten it gets). The previous mayor Noorland added to that; <i>maar ben je er eenmaal in, dan heb je het prima naar je zin</i> (but once you’re in it, you’re perfectly content). The saying can probably be explained as follows; traffic used to go by water and whoever came close to Dordrecht was obliged, according to <a href="/wiki/Staple_right" title="Staple right">staple right</a>, to display their merchandise for a couple of days before being allowed to sail on. This caused loss of time and caused products to become rotten. Another explanation is derived from <i><a href="/wiki/Zaltbommel" title="Zaltbommel">Bommel</a> is rommel, bij <a href="/wiki/Tiel" title="Tiel">Tiel</a> is niet viel en hoe dichter bij Dordt hoe rotter het wordt</i> which is supposed to be said by farmers describing the bad quality of the land close to the rivers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meuse_(river)" title="Meuse (river)">Maas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waal_(river)" title="Waal (river)">Waal</a>, only suitable for harvesting <a href="/wiki/Reed_bed" title="Reed bed">reed</a>.</p>

<p>The Sliedrechtse Biesbosch, east of Dordrecht, and the Dordtse Biesbosch, south of Dordrecht, together form the Hollandse Biesbosch which is a part of the national park the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biesbosch" title="Biesbosch">Biesbosch</a>, one of the largest national parks in the Netherlands and one of the last freshwater tide areas in Europe. The Dordtse Biesbosch has several recreational areas that are used for <a href="/wiki/Walking" title="Walking">walking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rowing_(sport)" title="Rowing (sport)">rowing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_swimming" title="Human swimming">swimming</a>.</p>
<p>There are also several parks near the city, such as Merwepark and Wantijpark.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dordts">Dordts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Dordts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dordts is a dialect of <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> traditionally spoken by the working class of Dordrecht. It is categorized under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollandic" title="Hollandic">Hollandic</a> accents but also has characteristics of <a href="/wiki/Zeelandic" title="Zeelandic">Zeelandic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brabantian" title="Brabantian">Brabantian</a>.</p>
<p>Typical features of Dordts are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Using the <a href="/wiki/Diminutive" title="Diminutive">diminutive</a> <a href="/wiki/Suffix" title="Suffix">suffix</a> <i>-ie</i> or <i>-tie</i> in cases where standard Dutch uses <i>-je</i>. (e.g. Standard Dutch: appeltje (“little apple”) Dordts: appeltie)</li>
<li>Words borrowed from Brabantian such as <i>akkerdere</i> (“lit. to knock or to fit, fig. “to get along”)</li>
<li>The Dutch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diphthongs" title="Diphthongs">diphthongs</a> <i>ei</i> and <i>ui</i> tend to be pronounced more like <i>èè</i> and <i>öö</i>. Recently, the <i>ei</i>-sound has started to be pronounced more like <i>ai</i>.</li>
</ul>
<p>In the 20th century, Dordts has slowly started to disappear as more and more people have started speaking <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_Dutch" title="Standard Dutch">standard Dutch</a>. The strongest Dordts dialect is nowadays found in the working-class neighborhoods bordering the city centre.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 November 1992 and again on 12 November 2011, the official arrival of the popular holiday figure <a href="/wiki/Sinterklaas" title="Sinterklaas">Sinterklaas</a> was held in Dordrecht and broadcast on national television.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1383" data-file-width="2048" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg/260px-Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg/390px-Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg/520px-Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg 2x" width="260"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Zeehavengebied_Dordrecht.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Harbor area of Dordrecht, seen from the Leeuwenhoekweg</div>

<p>The current economy of Dordrecht is based on ship building, wood industry, and steel industry. The city has the sixth largest sea port in the Netherlands. One of the largest employers on Dordrecht Island is <a href="/wiki/DuPont" title="DuPont">DuPont de Nemours (Nederland) B.V.</a> It has 9 factories here with a workforce of 900 people.</p>
<p>Together with Zwijndrecht, Papendrecht, and Sliedrecht (the "<a href="/wiki/Drechtsteden" title="Drechtsteden"><i>Drecht</i> Cities</a>"), Dordrecht is planning a new ambitious vision to revitalize the region's economy, moving from industry to more business services.</p>
<p>In development are the "Learning" and "Health" Business Parks. The Learning Park is intended to have 60,000 m<sup>2</sup> (645,834.63 sq ft) of space for educational institutions. In the Health Park, a wide range of health services will be located, with the Dordwijk Campus of the Albert Schweitzer Hospital as focal point. Other services include a blood bank, pharmacy, and mental health clinic.</p>
<p>Near the Health Park a new Sport Park will be built. A new large sport centre, the equivalent size of 3 soccer fields, is planned complete with gyms, skating rink, and a pool. Next to this Sport Park, a huge outdoor event terrain will be established.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping">Shopping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dordrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Shopping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shopping in the centre of Dordrecht is centred around the Voorstraat, the Sarisgang and the Statenplein (Staten square). The Voorstraat is 1,200 metres (1,300 yd) long, making it the longest shopping street in the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Markets are held every Friday and Saturday on the Statenplein and in the Sarisgang and on Tuesday in Dubbeldam.</p>
