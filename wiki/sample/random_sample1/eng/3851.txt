<p><b>Domagnano</b> is a municipality in <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a>. It has 3,319 inhabitants (October 2013) in an area of 6.62 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domagnano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It borders the San Marino municipalities <a href="/wiki/Faetano" title="Faetano">Faetano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borgo_Maggiore" title="Borgo Maggiore">Borgo Maggiore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serravalle_(San_Marino)" title="Serravalle (San Marino)">Serravalle</a> and the Italian municipality <a href="/wiki/Coriano" title="Coriano">Coriano</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domagnano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Domagnano, previously known as Montelupo (mountain of wolves) after its coat of arms, was already colonized by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_times" title="Roman times">Roman times</a> and was first mentioned in c. 1300. It is located on <a href="/wiki/Monte_Titano" title="Monte Titano">Monte Titano</a> and you can see the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a> from here. The so-called <a href="/wiki/Domagnano_Treasure" title="Domagnano Treasure">Domagnano Treasure</a> was found in the castle here in the late nineteenth century. It is now split between a number of museums, but the largest portions of the treasure are kept at the <a href="/wiki/Germanisches_Nationalmuseum" title="Germanisches Nationalmuseum">Germanisches Nationalmuseum</a> in Nuremberg and the <a href="/wiki/British_Museum" title="British Museum">British Museum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
