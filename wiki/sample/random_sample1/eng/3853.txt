<p><b>Gekkota</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infraorder" title="Infraorder">infraorder</a> of <a href="/wiki/Squamata" title="Squamata">squamate</a> <a href="/wiki/Reptile" title="Reptile">reptiles</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suborder" title="Suborder">suborder</a> <a href="/wiki/Scleroglossa" title="Scleroglossa">Scleroglossa</a>, comprising all <a href="/wiki/Gecko" title="Gecko">geckos</a> and the limbless "snake-lizards" of family <a href="/wiki/Pygopodidae" title="Pygopodidae">Pygopodidae</a>. The legless lizards of the family <a href="/wiki/Dibamidae" title="Dibamidae">Dibamidae</a>, also referred to as blind lizards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> have occasionally been counted as gekkotans, but recent molecular phylogenies suggest otherwise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gekkota is composed of seven families:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Family <a href="/wiki/Carphodactylidae" title="Carphodactylidae">Carphodactylidae</a></li>
<li>Family <a href="/wiki/Diplodactylidae" title="Diplodactylidae">Diplodactylidae</a></li>
<li>Family <a href="/wiki/Eublepharidae" title="Eublepharidae">Eublepharidae</a></li>
<li>Family <a href="/wiki/Gekkonidae" title="Gekkonidae">Gekkonidae</a></li>
<li>Family <a href="/wiki/Phyllodactylidae" title="Phyllodactylidae">Phyllodactylidae</a></li>
<li>Family <a href="/wiki/Pygopodidae" title="Pygopodidae">Pygopodidae</a></li>
