<p><b>Tomata</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">苫田郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tomata-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Okayama_Prefecture" title="Okayama Prefecture">Okayama Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2003, the district has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 20,631 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 33.22 persons per km². The total area is 621.03 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger">Merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomata_District,_Okayama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>On February 28, 2005 the town of <a href="/wiki/Kamo,_Okayama" title="Kamo, Okayama">Kamo</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Aba,_Okayama" title="Aba, Okayama">Aba</a> merged into the city of <a href="/wiki/Tsuyama,_Okayama" title="Tsuyama, Okayama">Tsuyama</a>.</li>
<li>On March 1, 2005 the town of <a href="/wiki/Okutsu,_Okayama" title="Okutsu, Okayama">Okutsu</a>, and the villages of <a href="/wiki/Kamisaibara,_Okayama" title="Kamisaibara, Okayama">Kamisaibara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tomi,_Okayama" title="Tomi, Okayama">Tomi</a> were merged into the expanded town of <a href="/wiki/Kagamino,_Okayama" title="Kagamino, Okayama">Kagamino</a>.</li>
</ul>
