<p><b>Domenico "Mimmo" Criscito</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[doˈmeniko ˈmimmo ˈkriʃʃito]</a></span>; born 30 December 1986) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</a>. Prior to joining Zenit, Criscito played for Italian clubs <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>. A full international since 2009, he represented the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italian national team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>. A versatile player, Criscito is effective both offensively and defensively,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tifoazzurro_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tifoazzurro-2">[2]</a></sup> and is also capable of playing as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Wing-back" title="Defender (association football)">wing-back</a>, or as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>, a position which he often occupied in his early career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vivoazzurro_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-vivoazzurro-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tuttojuve_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tuttojuve-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A native of southern Italy, Criscito moved up north at age 13 to pursue his football career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CriscitoMarchisio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CriscitoMarchisio-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He started his professional career at <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>, for whom he made his debut in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> in June 2003 at the age of 16. In the summer of 2004, Genoa sold 50% of Criscito and <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Volpe" title="Francesco Volpe">Francesco Volpe</a> contracts to <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> for €1.9 million, with 50% contract of <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Nocerino" title="Antonio Nocerino">Antonio Nocerino</a> was sold to Genoa for €450,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juventus">Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Criscito played for Juventus's <i>Primavera</i> team alongside Italy U-21 teammates <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Marchisio" title="Claudio Marchisio">Claudio Marchisio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paolo_De_Ceglie" title="Paolo De Ceglie">Paolo De Ceglie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Giovinco" title="Sebastian Giovinco">Sebastian Giovinco</a>, and together they won the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campionato_Primavera" title="Campionato Primavera">Campionato Primavera</a>. In the 2006–07 season, he returned to Genoa and established himself as one of the top young defenders in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>. In January, Juventus bought the remaining 50% of his contract for a combined price of €7.5 million. This was paid by the co-ownership of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Masiello" title="Andrea Masiello">Masiello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abdoulay_Konko" title="Abdoulay Konko">Konko</a> to <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>, and €5.25 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He also signed a new contract with Juventus that would last until 30 June 2011.</p>
<p>Criscito made his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut for <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> on 25 August 2007, in Juve's comeback game in Serie A versus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Livorno_Calcio" title="Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a>, a crushing 5–1 victory. However, he endured a difficult time at the Turin club. He was deemed "too soft" to be a centre-back after <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Totti" title="Francesco Totti">Francesco Totti</a> scored twice in six minutes in the September game, a 2–2 draw, against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> from his side of the pitch. Criscito was substituted at half-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CriscitoMarchisio_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-CriscitoMarchisio-5">[5]</a></sup> After that, he found himself behind <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Legrottaglie" title="Nicola Legrottaglie">Nicola Legrottaglie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Chiellini" title="Giorgio Chiellini">Giorgio Chiellini</a> in the pecking order due to their outstanding form.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Genoa">Return to Genoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Genoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Not content to remain on the bench, Criscito opted to return to <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> on loan in the opening days of the winter transfer window. In the summer of 2008, he was sent on loan to Genoa again for €1 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> with the Ligurian club having the option to purchase half of his contract for €5.5 million. In February 2009, he scored his first goal since returning to Genoa, the winner against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Palermo" title="US Palermo">Palermo</a>. His co-ownership agreement was renewed for the 2009–10 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He was very impressive after re-joining Genoa, who were back in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> by then, and he retained a place in the starting line-up as <a href="/wiki/Gian_Piero_Gasperini" title="Gian Piero Gasperini">Gian Piero Gasperini</a>'s first choice left-back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> However, he did not rule out a return to Juventus after being linked to a permanent return to replace <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Grosso" title="Fabio Grosso">Fabio Grosso</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In September against <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a>, Criscito was shown a straight red card for allegedly swearing at the referee after being penalized for a foul on <a href="/wiki/Christian_Maggio" title="Christian Maggio">Christian Maggio</a>, but Gasperini later defended the 23-year-old saying that he was angry at himself, not the referee. The appeal to rescind the red card was turned down, but his ban was reduced to 2–3 matchdays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 June 2010, Juventus announced that the Criscito's remaining 50% registration rights were sold for another €6 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> However co-currently 50% registration rights of <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Bonucci" title="Leonardo Bonucci">Leonardo Bonucci</a> was signed from Genoa via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AS_Bari" title="AS Bari">Bari</a> for €8 million.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zenit">Zenit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Zenit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 June 2011, Criscito signed for Russian Premier League club <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit</a> for €11 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He penned a 5-year contract with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He made his debut for the club on 6 August in a 2–0 away victory against <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a>, providing an assist for teammate <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Kerzhakov" title="Aleksandr Kerzhakov">Aleksandr Kerzhakov</a>. He ended the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Russian_Premier_League" title="2011–12 Russian Premier League">season as a league champion</a>, his first career silverware.</p>
<p>On 12 July 2015, he scored the winning penalty in a 4–2 shoot-out victory in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Russian_Super_Cup" title="2015 Russian Super Cup">2015 Russian Super Cup</a> over <a href="/wiki/FC_Lokomotiv_Moscow" title="FC Lokomotiv Moscow">Lokomotiv Moscow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 14 November 2006, Criscito made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy Under-21</a> squad in a match against the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_under-21_football_team" title="Czech Republic national under-21 football team">Czech Republic</a>. He appeared in two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">European Under-21 Championships</a>, in <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2007</a> and <a href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2009</a>, reaching the semi-finals of the letter tournament. In 2008 he also represented Italy at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>On 12 August 2009, he made his senior <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">national team</a> debut in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> After a promising season, he was named in <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Lippi" title="Marcello Lippi">Marcello Lippi</a>'s 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a>, where he was the starting left-back; Italy were eliminated in the first round of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Criscito was named to Italy's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a> squad, but withdrew from the team after he came under investigation due to the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Italian_football_scandal" title="2011–12 Italian football scandal"><i>Scommessopoli</i> match fixing scandal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Manager Prandelli explained his withdrawing Criscito from the Italy squad for Euro and said <i>"I am not taking Criscito because he would have been under pressure which no human being can bear, The other reason is that he could have been summoned by the prosecutor at any moment, before a match"</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Criscito went on to criticize the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIGC" title="FIGC">Italian FA</a> for the decision to drop him despite the limited selection of left-backs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Several months later he was cleared of all charges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Criscito is a tactically versatile player, who is capable of being both an effective attacking and defensive threat as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">full-back</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tifoazzurro_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tifoazzurro-2">[2]</a></sup> He began his career as a left-footed <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a>, but he was moved to the position of left-back while playing for <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>, where he was also employed as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Wing-back" title="Defender (association football)">wing-back</a> or as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">wide-midfielder</a> on occasion under manager <a href="/wiki/Gian_Piero_Gasperini" title="Gian Piero Gasperini">Gian Piero Gasperini</a> in Genoa's 3–4–3 formation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vivoazzurro_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-vivoazzurro-3">[3]</a></sup> Criscito has been described as a quick defender, with good technique, distribution, and man-marking ability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tuttojuve_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-tuttojuve-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domenico_Criscito&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
