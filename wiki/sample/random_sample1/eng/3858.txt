<p><b>Tsuyoshi Domoto</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">堂本 剛</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Dōmoto Tsuyoshi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (born April 10, 1979) is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_idol" title="Japanese idol">Japanese idol</a>, singer, songwriter, actor, and television personality. Along with <a href="/wiki/Koichi_Domoto" title="Koichi Domoto">Koichi Domoto</a> (with whom he has no blood-relation), he is a member of <a href="/wiki/KinKi_Kids" title="KinKi Kids">KinKi Kids</a>, which is a Japanese duo under the management of <a href="/wiki/Johnny_%26_Associates" title="Johnny &amp; Associates">Johnny &amp; Associates</a> and the record holder of <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> for having the record of the most number of consecutive number-one singles since their debut single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13consecutive_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-13consecutive-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsuyoshi_Domoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Nara,_Nara" title="Nara, Nara">Nara</a>, Domoto joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johnny%27s_Entertainment" title="Johnny's Entertainment">Johnny's Entertainment</a> on May 5, 1991, which also the first time he met his future bandmate <a href="/wiki/Koichi_Domoto" title="Koichi Domoto">Koichi Domoto</a>. The opportunity came while without his knowing, his mother and his 6-years-older sister sent his resume to the office. Domoto and his bandmate <a href="/wiki/Koichi_Domoto" title="Koichi Domoto">Koichi Domoto</a> first worked together as backdancers for <a href="/wiki/Hikaru_Genji_(band)" title="Hikaru Genji (band)">Hikaru Genji</a>, who were holding a concert at <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Arena" title="Yokohama Arena">Yokohama Arena</a>, and has since then been partnered up for magazine photoshoots, music acts and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">drama</a> projects. The duo starred in their first drama together called <i>Ningen Shikkaku</i> in 1994, in which Domoto was awarded Best Newcomer in the Television Drama Academy Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvawards_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvawards-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>With <a href="/wiki/Koichi_Domoto" title="Koichi Domoto">Koichi Domoto</a>, he eventually made his debut in 1997 as <a href="/wiki/KinKi_Kids" title="KinKi Kids">KinKi Kids</a> with a double release of a single "Garasu no Shōnen" and an album <i><a href="/wiki/A_Album" title="A Album">A Album</a></i>, both of which went on to sell more than a million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1997mil_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-1997mil-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 29, 2002, Domoto released his first solo single, "Machi/Dekiai Logic", in which he penned and composed all the tracks. The single was used as the theme song for Domoto's lead-role drama <i>Yume no California</i> and it reached number 1 in the weekly chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In the following month, Domoto started his career as an illustrator by publishing a book in collaboration with writer Shizuka Iziyuuin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-book1-5">[5]</a></sup> They collaborated again for another book in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-book2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Domoto appeared in the 2003 drama <i>Moto Kare</i>, in which he co-starred for the second time with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryoko_Hirosue" title="Ryoko Hirosue">Ryoko Hirosue</a> after their hit drama <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_Snow" title="Summer Snow">Summer Snow</a></i> in 2000. In April 2004, Domoto once again provided the theme song for his lead-role drama <i>Home Drama</i>. This year, he won the 'Best Jeanist' award. The following year, Domoto acted in a movie, <i>Fantastipo</i>, with fellow Johnny's personality <a href="/wiki/Taichi_Kokubun" title="Taichi Kokubun">Taichi Kokubun</a>. He paired with <a href="/wiki/Toraji_Haiji" title="Toraji Haiji">Toraji Haiji</a> to release the theme song for the movie. The same year, Domoto starred in <i>Hoshi ni Negai o</i> (a.k.a. <i>Wish Upon A Star</i>), a TV drama produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuji_TV" title="Fuji TV">Fuji TV</a> based on the true story of planetarium engineer <a href="/wiki/Takayuki_Ohira" title="Takayuki Ohira">Takayuki Ohira</a> who designed Megastar II – the world's most advanced planetarium projector.</p>
<p>In 2006, Domoto put his acting career on hold and focused more on his music career. He began to release his solo work under the project name Endlicheri-Endlicheri. During his Endlicheri-Endlicheri era, Domoto released three singles which all topped the weekly charts and made him the second male soloist after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masahiko_Kondo" title="Masahiko Kondo">Masahiko Kondo</a> to have achieved five consecutive number one singles. He then renamed his solo project <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/244_Endli-x" title="244 Endli-x">244 Endli-x</a> (pronounced "Tsuyoshi Endorikkusu") in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He announced that he would go on his first concert tour as 244 Endli-x soon after, which began on March 29, 2008 at the Yokohama Arena and ended in Sendai on May 8, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At the end of April, Domoto was appointed to be the first special ambassador for Nara tourism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Domoto once again changed his solo project name in 2009 to Tsuyoshi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">剛紫</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and simultaneously released a single and an album on his birthday, April 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the same year, he released another single, "Rain", on September 9, which topped the Oricon Single Weekly Charts. Domoto also announced his collaboration with the hat brand CA4LA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Known as the fashion leader with unique style, Domoto was chosen by famous sports brand <a href="/wiki/Adidas_Originals" title="Adidas Originals">Adidas Originals</a> to be the image character for its new campaign BE Originals which start on February 12, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-adidas_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-adidas-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 6, 2011, Domoto released his ninth single, "Eni o Yuite", which was recorded at shrines believed to have connections to Nara's entertainment and music industries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Another single, "Niji no Uta", was released in September 2011.</p>
