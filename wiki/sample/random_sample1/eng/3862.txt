<p><b>Dominic Adiyiah</b> (born 29 November 1989) is a <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghanaian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who last played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/Thai_League" title="Thai League">Thai League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Nakhon_Ratchasima_F.C." title="Nakhon Ratchasima F.C.">Nakhon Ratchasima</a>. Adiyiah comes from <a href="/wiki/Offinso" title="Offinso">Offinso</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_Region" title="Ashanti Region">Ashanti Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Adiyiah began his football career at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feyenoord_Ghana" title="Feyenoord Ghana">Feyenoord Ghana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-world_soccer_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-world_soccer-1">[1]</a></sup> He spent several years in Gomoa Fetteh, where the Academy is based, before transferring to <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Lions_F.C." title="Heart of Lions F.C.">Heart of Lions</a> in 2007. After making his debut in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghana_Premier_League" title="Ghana Premier League">Ghana Premier League</a> with the club based in <a href="/wiki/Kpandu" title="Kpandu">Kpandu</a>, at the end of the 2007–08 season he was named Most Exciting Player of the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-most_Exciting_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-most_Exciting-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fredrikstad_.282008.E2.80.932010.29">Fredrikstad (2008–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fredrikstad (2008–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2008, Adiyiah made the move to Europe, being signed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Norwegian top flight</a> club <a href="/wiki/Fredrikstad_FK" title="Fredrikstad FK">Fredrikstad</a> for a fee of only £100k (approx. €125k as of that date).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-world_soccer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-world_soccer-1">[1]</a></sup> He made his debut for the club in an away match against <a href="/wiki/Aalesunds_FK" title="Aalesunds FK">Aalesund</a>, on 30 August. He went scoreless in four appearances during the 2008 season, as the team finished second in the league.</p>
<p>The following season he scored twice during the training camp, but has not scored in the four league matches he appeared.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He also made his debut in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a>, in the second leg of the third qualifying round against <a href="/wiki/Lech_Pozna%C5%84" title="Lech Poznań">Lech Poznań</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Milan_.282010.E2.80.932012.29">Milan (2010–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Milan (2010–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late October 2009, after impressing in the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup">U-20 World Cup</a>, Adiyiah was reported to be close to joining <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>. Speculation increased as Fredrikstad announced they had received a bid €500k for the young striker from a big European club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The rumour was eventually confirmed by Milan CEO <a href="/wiki/Adriano_Galliani" title="Adriano Galliani">Adriano Galliani</a> on 1 November,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and six days later the Ghanaian signed his new contract, as he had passed routine medical examinations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The move was ratified on 2 January 2010, the first day of the winter transfer window. However Adiyiah was still not able to play, since he was called up by the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana national team</a> to take part in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2010 Africa Cup of Nations">African Nations Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Upon his return, a month later, he also secured an Italian work permit, which he was still lacking, thus becoming definitively eligible to play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Despite this, he was never selected by head coach <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Ara%C3%BAjo" title="Leonardo Araújo">Leonardo</a> during the remainder of the 2009–10 season. On 25 June 2012, Adiyiah completed a three-year <a href="/wiki/Association_football_contracts" title="Association football contracts">contract</a> with Arsenal Kyiv, ending his three years career with AC Milan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ghana_striker_Dominic_Adiyiah_joins_Arsenal_Kiev_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ghana_striker_Dominic_Adiyiah_joins_Arsenal_Kiev-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partizan_.28loan.29">Partizan (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Partizan (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Subsequently, Adiyiah was sent to <a href="/wiki/Serbian_SuperLiga" title="Serbian SuperLiga">Serbian SuperLiga</a> club <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">Partizan</a> on another loan deal until the end of the season<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> where he joined his national teammate <a href="/wiki/Prince_Tagoe" title="Prince Tagoe">Prince Tagoe</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kar.C5.9F.C4.B1yaka_.28loan.29">Karşıyaka (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Karşıyaka (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 2011 summer transfer window he signed a loan-deal with <a href="/wiki/Kar%C5%9F%C4%B1yaka_S.K." title="Karşıyaka S.K.">Karşıyaka</a> who play in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bank_Asya_1._Lig" title="Bank Asya 1. Lig">Bank Asya 1. Lig</a>, Turkey's second-tier league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arsenal_Kyiv_.282012.E2.80.932013.29">Arsenal Kyiv (2012–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Arsenal Kyiv (2012–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>However, in February 2012 he was called back from Karşıyaka, due to lack of playing time and <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loaned</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="Ukrainian Premier League">Ukrainian Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Arsenal_Kyiv" title="FC Arsenal Kyiv">Arsenal Kyiv</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 25 June 2012, Adiyiah completed a three-year <a href="/wiki/Association_football_contracts" title="Association football contracts">contract</a> with Arsenal Kyiv, after spending three years with AC Milan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ghana_striker_Dominic_Adiyiah_joins_Arsenal_Kiev_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ghana_striker_Dominic_Adiyiah_joins_Arsenal_Kiev-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atyrau">Atyrau</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Atyrau">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2014, Adiyiah signed for <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan_Premier_League" title="Kazakhstan Premier League">Kazakhstan Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Atyrau" title="FC Atyrau">FC Atyrau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nakhon_Ratchasima">Nakhon Ratchasima</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Nakhon Ratchasima">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2015, Adiyiah signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Nakhon_Ratchasima_F.C." title="Nakhon Ratchasima F.C.">Nakhon Ratchasima F.C.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> After a successful where Nakon Ratchasima reached 8th place Adiyiah announced that he would be leaving the club at the end of his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He latter changed his mind however and agreed to stay at Nakhon Ratchasima for another season.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ghana_U-20">Ghana U-20</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Ghana U-20">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Adiyiah started to be called up by the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_under-20_football_team" title="Ghana national under-20 football team">Black Satellites</a> in 2008, making his debut on 30 March, in a match against <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Niger_national_under%E2%80%9320_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Niger national under–20 football team (page does not exist)">Niger</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and also participating in the <a href="/wiki/2008_WAFU_U-20_Championship" title="2008 WAFU U-20 Championship">WAFU U-20 Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The following year, he was part of the squad that won the <a href="/wiki/2009_African_Youth_Championship" title="2009 African Youth Championship">African Youth Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> His successful 2009 was not over: in October, he took part also in the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup">U-20 World Cup</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> as the team went on winning the trophy, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup#Awards" title="2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup">Golden Shoe</a> for the topscorer with 8 goals in 7 games and was also named the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup#Awards" title="2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup">Most Valuable Player</a> of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awards_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-awards-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ghana">Ghana</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Adiyiah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Ghana">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Adiyiah earned his first call-up with the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Black Stars</a> on 3 November 2009, shortly after the U-20 triumph, for a <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_CAF_Third_Round" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification – CAF Third Round">World Cup qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Mali_national_football_team" title="Mali national football team">Mali</a>, to be played on 15 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> However he was left as an unused substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He did make his senior debut three days later though, in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Angola_national_football_team" title="Angola national football team">Angola</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In January 2010, he was part of the Ghanaian team that reached the final at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2010 Africa Cup of Nations">African Nations Cup</a>. Despite making only two substitute appearances, he showed glimpses of a player for the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June of the same year, Adiyiah was called up to take part in the upcoming <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a>, to be played in South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He made his debut in the competition during the last match of the group stage against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>, coming off the bench for the final minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> After being left as an unused substitute in the round of 16, he was sent on in the quarter-finals against <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a>. In the very last minute of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra_time" title="Extra time">extra time</a>, Adiyiah's header was saved on the line by Uruguyan striker <a href="/wiki/Luis_Su%C3%A1rez" title="Luis Suárez">Luis Suárez</a>, who palmed the ball away with both his hands, resulting in a penalty kick for Ghana and Suarez being sent off. However, <a href="/wiki/Asamoah_Gyan" title="Asamoah Gyan">Asamoah Gyan</a> smashed the penalty against the bar and the game went to the shootout. Adiyiah took the fourth penalty with his team trailing 2–3, but had his shot saved; <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Abreu" title="Sebastián Abreu">Sebastián Abreu</a> seized the chance by subsequently scoring the deciding penalty for Uruguay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
