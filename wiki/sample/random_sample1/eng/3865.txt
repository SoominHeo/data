<p><b>Dominic Ball</b> (born 2 August 1995) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a> on loan from <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a>. He plays primarily as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre back</a>, however, Ball can also play as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a> or at full-back.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Ball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tottenham_and_loan_moves">Tottenham and loan moves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Ball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tottenham and loan moves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> youth graduate, he joined the club's youth setup in 2011 from <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rangers_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rangers-2">[2]</a></sup> and appeared in 17 matches during his first campaign, scoring one goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spurs_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spurs-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ball appeared with the under-18s and with the under-21s in 2013–14, being also named on the bench in a 2–2 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> away draw against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> on 20 March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was also an unused <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> in two further matches for Spurs in 2014–15, a 1–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">Partizan</a> and a 2–1 away win against <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>.</p>
<p>On 22 January 2015, Ball was loaned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a> side <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_United_F.C." title="Cambridge United F.C.">Cambridge United</a> until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He made his professional debut five days later, starting in a 1–1 home draw against <a href="/wiki/Dagenham_%26_Redbridge_F.C." title="Dagenham &amp; Redbridge F.C.">Dagenham &amp; Redbridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was used mainly as a right-back, making two assists in his time at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 August 2015, Ball signed a season-long loan deal to <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Championship" title="Scottish Championship">Scottish Championship</a> team <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a>, reuniting with his Watford academy manager <a href="/wiki/Mark_Warburton" title="Mark Warburton">Mark Warburton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rangers_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rangers-2">[2]</a></sup> At Rangers he helped them return to the Scottish Premier League when they won the Championship and also picked up honours when they won the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Challenge_Cup" title="Scottish Challenge Cup">Scottish Challenge Cup</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rotherham_United">Rotherham United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Ball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rotherham United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 August 2016, Ball signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a> on a three-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Peterborough_United_loan">Peterborough United loan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Ball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Peterborough United loan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 3 January 2017, Ball joined <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a> on loan until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Ball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After being capped by Northern Ireland in the under-15, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_under-16_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national under-16 football team">under-16</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_under-17_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national under-17 football team">under-17</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_under-19_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national under-19 football team">under-19</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national under-21 football team">under-21</a> levels, Ball switched his allegiance to England in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spurs_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spurs-3">[3]</a></sup> He has represented England up to U-20 level, making six appearances at that level.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Ball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ball was born in <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City" title="Welwyn Garden City">Welwyn Garden City, Hertfordshire</a>. Ball has an older brother, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Ball_(footballer)" title="Matt Ball (footballer)">Matt</a>, who is also a footballer, while his father Tim was academy <a href="/wiki/Business_manager" title="Business manager">business manager</a> at Watford.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rangers_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rangers-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
