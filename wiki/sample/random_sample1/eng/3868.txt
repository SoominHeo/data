<p><b>Tobata-ku</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">戸畑区</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_Japan" title="Wards of Japan">ward</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kitakyūshū">Kitakyūshū</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Prefecture" title="Fukuoka Prefecture">Fukuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is the smallest ward of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kitakyūshū">Kitakyūshū</a> city at 16.66 km². The population was 64,330 as of the national census in 2000.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_Geography">History and Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobata-ku,_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG/220px-Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG/330px-Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG/440px-Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Waka-To_O-hashi.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Waka-To O-hashi bridge from the Tobata side of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dokai_wan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dokai wan (page does not exist)">Dokai wan</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1899 Tobata-cho (Tobata town) was created, and in 1924 Tobata-shi (Tobata city). The city was merged into <a href="/wiki/Kitakyushu" title="Kitakyushu">Kitakyushu</a> in 1963. Officially Tobata became a ward of Kitakyushu on April 1, 1963.</p>
<p>Part of Tobata ward faces the sea inlet or <a href="/wiki/Firth" title="Firth">firth</a> (Scottish English) called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dokai_wan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dokai wan (page does not exist)">Dokai wan</a>, and the surrounding outer sea is called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hibiki_nada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hibiki nada (page does not exist)">Hibiki nada</a>. The red-painted bridge between Tobata and Wakamatsu wards is called the Waka-To Ohashi (Great Bridge of Waka-To) and was the first suspension bridge in Asia, completed in 1963. On September 30, 2005 it passed into the control of <a href="/wiki/Kitakyushu" title="Kitakyushu">Kitakyushu city</a>.</p>
<p>A new tunnel is being constructed in 2005-8 between Tobata and Wakamatsu wards to link the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hibiki_container_terminal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hibiki container terminal (page does not exist)">Hibiki container terminal</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kokura_Kita-ku,_Kitakyushu" title="Kokura Kita-ku, Kitakyushu">Kokura</a>.</p>
<p>Much of this ward, especially the part facing the open sea, is taken up by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Steel" title="Nippon Steel">Nippon Steel</a> (Shin Nihon Seitetsu) foundries, blast furnaces, coaling docks etc. though the numbers employed there are greatly reduced since the heyday of steelmaking in the 1960s, from about 25,000 to 8,000 employees.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congregation_of_Notre_Dame" title="Congregation of Notre Dame">Congregation of Notre Dame</a> has a community in Tobata.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_Tobata">Cultural Tobata</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobata-ku,_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cultural Tobata">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Industrial_Club" title="West Japan Industrial Club">West Japan Industrial Club</a> (Matsumoto-tei, a house originally built for and owned by Mr. Matsumoto), a large house and garden adjoining Yomiya park, was designed by <a href="/wiki/Tatsuno_Kingo" title="Tatsuno Kingo">Tatsuno Kingo</a> who also designed <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo Station</a> as a monument to Japan's victory in the Russo-Japanese War. A scene from the 2003 movie "Spy Sorge" (director Masahiro Shinoda) about <a href="/wiki/Richard_Sorge" title="Richard Sorge">Richard Sorge</a> was shot in the garden,</p>
<p>Nearby <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_Institute_of_Technology" title="Kyushu Institute of Technology">Kyushu Institute of Technology</a> (founded by Mr. Matsumoto in 1907) has its oldest campus (the faculty of engineering) in Tobata.</p>
<p>The most important annual folk-cultural event is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tobata_Gion" title="Tobata Gion">Tobata Gion</a> festival designated a national intangible cultural asset, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitakyushu_city_art_museum" title="Kitakyushu city art museum">Kitakyushu city art museum</a> is also here. There are standing exhibits of Western and Japanese art and regularly changing art exhibitions.</p>
<p>Sayagatani field and athletic stadium was built by Yahata steel workers and is still used for ball sports (rugby, soccer etc.) and athletics.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobata-ku,_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The notable rugby player <a href="/wiki/Takuro_Miuchi" title="Takuro Miuchi">Takuro Miuchi</a> is from Tobata. There is a rugby ground at Astem no Mori, and another at Sayagatani. Miuchi first learned to play rugby at <a class="external text" href="http://www.geocities.jp/srs1980srs/" rel="nofollow">Sayagatani rugby school</a> and later at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yahata_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yahata High School (page does not exist)">Yahata High School</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport_.26_Facilities">Transport &amp; Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobata-ku,_Kitaky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport &amp; Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JR_Kyushu" title="JR Kyushu">JR Kyushu</a> rail stations on the <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Main_Line" title="Kagoshima Main Line">Kagoshima Main Line</a> (Kagoshima honsen): <a href="/wiki/Tobata_Station" title="Tobata Station">Tobata Station</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kyushu_Koudaimae_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kyushu Koudaimae Station (page does not exist)">Kyushu Koudaimae Station</a>, named after <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_Institute_of_Technology" title="Kyushu Institute of Technology">Kyushu Institute of Technology</a> which is seven minutes walk from there. Buses are run by the Nishitetsu company.</p>
<p>A small shopping mall,Tobata Aeon, is located next to JR Tobata station. It is a kind of local and down-market department store, as compared with Isetan and Izutsuya in nearby <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kokura_Kita" title="Kokura Kita">Kokura Kita</a> ward.</p>
<p>Wakato ferry is for passengers with bicycles. It is one of the shortest and cheapest ferries in Japan (three minutes, 100 yen one way).</p>
