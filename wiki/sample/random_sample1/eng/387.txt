<p>The <b>2008 Malaysian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>X Petronas Malaysian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 23 March 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sepang" title="Sepang">Sepang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. It was the 2nd race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 56-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from second position. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> finished second in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> car, with <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> third in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>.</p>
<p>It was an all-Ferrari front row, as <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with Räikkönen alongside. The McLarens of Kovalainen and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had been expected to line up behind the Ferraris on the second row, but both drivers were given five-place grid penalties post-qualifying for holding up <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> respectively, dropping them to eighth and ninth on the grid. Massa cut across his Ferrari teammate at the start, to stop him overtaking on the run to turn one. The top four of Massa, Räikkönen, Kubica and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> held station until the first round of pit stops. Räikkönen took the lead after these stops and after Massa retired following a spin into the gravel, the Finn cruised to a victory of nearly twenty seconds. Championship leader Hamilton could only finish in fifth position, after a tardy first stop on lap 19, with his McLaren team encountering a problem on his front right tyre and lost ground while trying to pass Webber.</p>
<p>Despite his fifth position, Hamilton kept the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers' Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> lead with 14 points, closely followed by Räikkönen and Heidfeld on 11. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors' Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren also kept their lead, with their five-point lead from <a href="/wiki/2008_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a> staying intact. BMW Sauber moved into second place on 19, with Ferrari in third on 11.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 22 drivers, in 11 teams of two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-race_classification_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-race_classification-1">[1]</a></sup> The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renault_F1" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-race_classification_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-race_classification-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to the race, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers' Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 10 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, and BMW Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was second with 8 points. Behind them in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was third with 6 points in a Williams, and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fourth with 5 points in a Renault. Hamilton's teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was fifth with 4 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-2">[2]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors' Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren–Mercedes were leading with 14 points, five points ahead of Williams, and BMW Sauber were third with 8 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:40%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"This morning I lost time because of a communication problem within the team, but we made up for it in the second session. The car seems to be going well and I am pleased with the work we have done. I worked mainly on the harder tyres, but I think that even with the softs, from what we have seen, we can be competitive. It is always difficult to say where we are compared to our rivals, but I think we can face the rest of the weekend with confidence."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, reflecting on his mishap in the first Friday session, and recovering to third in the second session.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FP_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FP-3">[3]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday morning and the second on Friday afternoon. Both sessions lasted 1 hour and 30 minutes with weather conditions dry throughout. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sporting_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the first session, Massa was fastest on a lap of 1:35.392, which was over a second faster than any of his rivals. Räikkönen ended up second, despite running out of fuel during the session, and Kovalainen ended up third, just under a tenth of a second slower than his fellow Finn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Also during the session, the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> machine of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> suffered a track rod failure on the curb of turn 12, and forced the team to show the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIA" title="FIA">FIA</a> clarification that their car design was safe for the Grand Prix, under Article 2.3 of the 2008 Formula One Technical Regulations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Coulthard sat out the second practice session, which saw Hamilton top the timesheets with a lap of 1:35.055, beating Massa by 0.151 seconds, with Räikkönen a further two tenths back in third.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In preparation for the qualifying session, many teams put their cars on <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">soft tyres</a> for the third practice session. The soft tyre gives better performance than the harder type of tyre but is less durable than the harder compound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-formula1.com-tyres_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-tyres-8">[8]</a></sup> Just like in Australia, the BMW of Heidfeld was fastest, setting a lap of 1:35.019, edging out the Ferraris of Räikkönen and Massa with <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s Toyota fourth, ending up just one-thousandth of a second behind Massa's time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>"The first corners of my last lap went smoothly, but then there were several cars driving very slowly on their in-laps. Apparently they had no information that the qualifying was still going on. Before Turn Four I lost a lot of time because both McLarens were cruising on the racing line. I think this has cost me about two tenths of a second, which would have meant being third instead of seventh. I just couldn't drive on the line I wanted and, even more importantly, could not brake were I wanted."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"><a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, on the McLaren drivers' antics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeidfeldPQ_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-HeidfeldPQ-10">[10]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>In qualifying, Massa earned his tenth career pole position, after recording a lap of 1:35.748 during the third and final session. He was joined on the front row by teammate Räikkönen, although the Finn's fastest time was more than half a second slower than that of Massa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autosport-quali_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-quali-11">[11]</a></sup> Third and fourth had originally gone to the McLarens of Kovalainen and Hamilton, however post-qualifying, both drivers were demoted five places on the grid for dangerous driving; having completed their flying laps, the McLaren pair and other drivers slowed to conserve fuel on their in-laps, and impeded <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renault_F1" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> whilst they were both on flying laps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McLarens_demoted_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-McLarens_demoted-12">[12]</a></sup> This led to a new rule being introduced for <a href="/wiki/2008_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain</a> giving drivers a set time to complete in-laps in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newrule_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-newrule-13">[13]</a></sup> McLaren's misfortune was to the gain of the Toyota of Trulli, both victims of the impeding (Heidfeld and Alonso), Heidfeld's teammate Kubica and Webber's Red Bull machine. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> rounded out the top ten in his Toyota.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autosport-quali_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-quali-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Malaysia_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="163" data-file-width="399" height="90" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Malaysia_2.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Malaysia_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Malaysia_2.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Malaysia_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/30/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Malaysia_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Malaysia_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> won the race by nearly twenty seconds, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>At the start Massa came across the track to prevent Räikkönen from overtaking him. Further back, Hamilton was able to move up to fifth and Webber up to fourth. Also on the first lap, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> spun out of the race. A crash between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and Glock's Toyota happened a few corners later, in which Rosberg destroyed the front wing of his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW30" title="Williams FW30">Williams FW30</a> and forced Glock to retire from the race. On lap four, Alonso and Heidfeld overtook the Red Bull of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> for ninth and eighth respectively. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> was forced to retire two laps later when he ran off the track and onto the grass, with hydraulic problems.</p>
<p>By lap nine Hamilton was within two seconds of Webber and the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of Massa and Räikkönen had opened up a lead on the rest of the field. Both set new fastest lap times over the next four laps. Webber pitted on lap 16, Massa on lap 17 and Räikkönen on lap 18, coming out ahead of Massa. Hamilton pitted on lap 19 and the team had a problem getting his front right tyre off, forcing him to stop for almost twenty seconds, and in the process he lost around twelve seconds<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pitstops_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-14">[14]</a></sup> to the Ferraris, meaning he came out behind Webber in 11th.</p>
<p>Räikkönen took the lead for the first time when Kubica pitted on lap 21. He would open up a lead of almost four seconds in the next six laps. Hamilton was up to seventh by lap 26 but was still unable to overtake Webber. Hamilton was within half a second of him by lap 29 but this forced him to brake harder and lose time to the rest of the field. Massa spun out of the race on lap 31 getting trapped in the gravel. This gave Räikkönen a lead of over 20 seconds from the next driver Kubica. Hamilton finally overtook Webber on lap 40 when he pitted. Vettel's engine failed one lap later and he was forced to retire. Hamilton would stay ahead of Webber after his second pit stop on lap 44. He was now six seconds behind Trulli in fourth and began to close that margin in the closing laps.</p>
<p>Alonso in eighth now began closing in on Webber. Hamilton was within two seconds of Trulli with two laps to go but was unable to pass him before the end of the race. Räikkönen won the race by nearly twenty seconds from Kubica who had his best ever finish in second; Kovalainen came third.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> earned their first fastest lap,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg110-111_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg110-111-16">[16]</a></sup> giving Heidfeld a record in "most races before first fastest lap" with 134, surpassing the former record of 114 held by <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>.</p>
