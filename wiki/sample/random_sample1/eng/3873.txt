<p>The <b>Tobu 6050 series</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">東武6050系</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōbu 6050-kei</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple unit</a> (EMU) train type operated on "Rapid" services by the <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Japan#Major_private_railways" title="Rail transport in Japan">private railway</a> operator <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Railway" title="Tobu Railway">Tobu Railway</a> and connecting <a href="/wiki/Aizu_Railway" title="Aizu Railway">Aizu Railway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yagan_Railway" title="Yagan Railway">Yagan Railway</a> in Japan since 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-private2012_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-private2012-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobu_6050_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 6050 series design was created from 1985 by rebuilding former <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tobu_6000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tobu 6000 series (page does not exist)">6000 series</a> EMUs dating from 1964 with new steel bodies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj264_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dj264-2">[2]</a></sup> The trains were required to satisfy fire resistance regulations applying to the long tunnel on the Aizu Kinugawa Line ahead of the start of through running operations from 9 October 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj264_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-dj264-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Livery">Livery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobu_6050_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Livery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Trains are painted in "jasmine white" with "sunny coral orange" and "purple ruby" lining.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-private2012_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-private2012-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formations">Formations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobu_6050_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fleet of 33 two-car sets is formed as shown below.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrrprivate2011_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrrprivate2011-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<table class="wikitable">
<tr>

<td>Tc</td>

<tr>

<td>6250</td>
</tr>
</table>
<ul>
<li>The Mc cars are each fitted with one scissors-type pantograph. (Sets 6153-6156 and 6173 have two pantographs)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrrprivate2011_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-jrrprivate2011-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Yagan Railway set (6050-100 series) cars are numbered 6110x and 6210x.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrrprivate2011_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-jrrprivate2011-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The sole Aizu Railway set (6050-200 series) cars are numbered 61201 and 62201.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrrprivate2011_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-jrrprivate2011-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Tc cars are equipped with a toilet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrrprivate2011_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-jrrprivate2011-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobu_6050_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg/220px-Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg/330px-Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg/440px-Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tobu_EC_6000_at_Asakusa_Sta2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Original 6000 series EMU, August 1980</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>All 44 former 6000 series cars were rebuilt between September 1985 and October 1986 as sets 6151 to 6172, with the first sets entering revenue service from 15 November 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj264_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-dj264-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the popularity of the new trains, a further 8 new 2-car sets were built, including one set for the Yagan Railway, enabling all "Rapid" services between Asakusa and Shimo-Imaichi to be increased to 6-car formations from the 9 August 1988 timetable revision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj264_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-dj264-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A further two newly built sets were added to the Yagan Railway fleet coinciding with the start of Aizu Kinugawa Line services, and one new set was delivered to the Aizu Railway in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj264_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-dj264-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two 6050 series sets, 6177 and 6178, were rebuilt in September 2012 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J-TREC" title="J-TREC">J-TREC</a> in Yokohama to become <a href="/wiki/Tobu_634_series" title="Tobu 634 series">634 series</a> sets for use on <i>Skytree Train</i> services from 27 October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20120928_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20120928-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
