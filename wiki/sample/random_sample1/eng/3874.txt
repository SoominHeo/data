<p><b>Dobryanka</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Добря́нка</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Perm_Krai" title="Perm Krai">Perm Krai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the bank of the <a href="/wiki/Kama_Reservoir" title="Kama Reservoir">Kama Reservoir</a>, 61 kilometers (38 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Perm" title="Perm">Perm</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Krai" title="Krai">krai</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">33,686 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-3">[3]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">36,436 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-6">[6]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">35,317 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dobryanka,_Perm_Krai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Considered to be one of the oldest inhabited localities in Perm Krai, Dobryanka was first mentioned in 1623.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1752, an <a href="/wiki/Ironworks" title="Ironworks">ironworks</a> was built at the mouth of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dobryanka_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dobryanka River (page does not exist)">Dobryanka River</a>, and a settlement developed around it. In 1943, the settlement was granted town status.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The town served as the administrative center of Dobryansky District, which was abolished in 1993.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dobryanka,_Perm_Krai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, it is, together with the <a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement" title="Urban-type settlement">work settlement</a> of <a href="/wiki/Polazna" title="Polazna">Polazna</a> and 110 <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural localities</a>, incorporated as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">town of krai significance</a> of Dobryanka</b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Perm_Krai" title="Administrative divisions of Perm Krai">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref127_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Ref127-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the town of Dobryanka, together with 8 rural localities, is incorporated as <a href="/wiki/Dobryanskoye_Urban_Settlement" title="Dobryanskoye Urban Settlement">Dobryanskoye Urban Settlement</a> within Dobryansky Municipal District and serves as the municipal district's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref847_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Ref847-2">[2]</a></sup> The work settlement of Polazna and the remaining 102 rural localities are grouped into one urban settlement and six rural settlements within Dobryansky Municipal District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref847_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Ref847-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dobryanka,_Perm_Krai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Perm_GRES&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Perm GRES (page does not exist)">Perm GRES</a>—a huge power station with a 330-meter (1,080 ft) tall chimney—is located in Dobryanka.</p>

Опубликован: "Бюллетень Законодательного Собрания и администрации Пермской области", №12, I часть, 9 декабря 2004 г.</span> (Legislative Assembly of Perm Oblast. Law #1743-358 of November 10, 2004 <i>On Establishing the Borders and on Granting the Status to the Municipal Formations of the Administrative Territory of the Town of Dobryanka of Perm Krai</i>, as amended by the Law #273-PK of December 14, 2013 <i>On Amending Various Laws of Perm Oblast, Komi-Permyak Autonomous Okrug, Perm Krai</i>. Effective as of the day which is ten days following the day of the official publication.).</li>
</ul>
