<p><b>Tobishima</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">飛島</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is an inhabited island located in the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a>, administered part of <a href="/wiki/Sakata,_Yamagata" title="Sakata, Yamagata">Sakata, Yamagata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The island, 2.75 km² in area, had 275 inhabitants as of 2005. The island has no airport, and access is normally by ferry to the city of Sakata on the mainland. The islanders are dependent mainly on <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">commercial fishing</a> and seasonal tourism.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobishima_(Yamagata)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tobishima is approximately 3 kilometres (1.9 mi) by 1 kilometre (0.62 mi). It is located 39 kilometres (21 nmi) west of <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshu</a>. The highest point is Takamori-yama ( 高森山 ) at 68 metres (223 ft)</p>
<p>The island is mostly flat, with a gentle rise from the east coast to the west coast, the west coast is characterized by steep cliffs. The port facilities are therefore located on the east coast and along with most of the population. Due to the effects of the warm Tsushima current, the island has relatively mild weather year-round average annual temperatures of 12 °C; even snowfall of 10 cm is rare. The island is covered with evergreen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deciduous_tree" title="Deciduous tree">deciduous trees</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/Eurya_japonica" title="Eurya japonica">Eurya japonica</a>.</i> The island also serves as an important nesting grounds for some 270 species of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Migratory_bird" title="Migratory bird">migratory birds</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tobishima_(Yamagata)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tobishima has been populated for several thousand years, with archaeological finds from the early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jomon_period" title="Jomon period">Jomon period</a> dated to 6000–7000 years ago. In the late <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a>, the island was controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Abe_clan" title="Abe clan">Abe clan</a>, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Kiyohara_clan" title="Kiyohara clan">Kiyohara clan</a>. During the 15th century, it was a base for the <i><a href="/wiki/Wokou" title="Wokou">wakō</a></i> pirates. In the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, it was part of the holdings of the <a href="/wiki/Sakai_clan" title="Sakai clan">Sakai clan</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shonai_Domain" title="Shonai Domain">Shonai Domain</a> and an occasional port for the <i><a href="/wiki/Kitamaebune" title="Kitamaebune">kitamaebune</a></i> coastal trading vessels.</p>
<p>As of April 1950, the village of Tobishima and thus the island was annexed by the city of Sakata, Yamagata.</p>
