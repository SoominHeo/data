<p><b>Dosa</b> is a type of pancake made from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fermentation_(food)" title="Fermentation (food)">fermented</a> <a href="/wiki/Batter_(cooking)" title="Batter (cooking)">batter</a>. It is not a crepe. Its main ingredients are <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_gram" title="Black gram">black gram</a>. Dosa is a typical part of the <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South Indian</a> diet and popular all over the Indian subcontinent. Traditionally, Dosa is served hot along with <a href="/wiki/Sambar_(dish)" title="Sambar (dish)">sambar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chutney" title="Chutney">chutney</a>. It can be consumed with <a href="/wiki/Idli_podi" title="Idli podi">idli podi</a> as well.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dosa is indigenous to <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South India</a>; its exact birthplace in that region is a matter of conjecture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charmine2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Charmine2013-1">[1]</a></sup> According to food historian <a href="/wiki/K._T._Achaya" title="K. T. Achaya">K. T. Achaya</a>, dosa (as <i>dosai</i>) was already in use in <a href="/wiki/Tamilakam" title="Tamilakam">ancient Tamil country</a> around the 1st century AD, as per references in the <a href="/wiki/Sangam_literature" title="Sangam literature">Sangam literature</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KTAchaya_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-KTAchaya-2">[2]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/P._Thankappan_Nair" title="P. Thankappan Nair">P. Thankappan Nair</a>, dosa originated in the <a href="/wiki/Udupi" title="Udupi">Udupi</a> town of present-day Karnataka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In popular tradition, the origin of dosa is linked to Udupi, probably because of the dish's association with the <a href="/wiki/Udupi_cuisine" title="Udupi cuisine">Udupi restaurants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charmine2013_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Charmine2013-1">[1]</a></sup> Also, the original Tamil dosa was softer and thicker. The thinner and crispier version of dosa, which became popular all over India, was first made in present-day <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vir2004_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vir2004-4">[4]</a></sup> A recipe for dosa (as <i>dosaka</i>) can be found in <i><a href="/wiki/Manasollasa" title="Manasollasa">Manasollasa</a></i>, a 12th-century Sanskrit encyclopedia compiled by <a href="/wiki/Someshvara_III" title="Someshvara III">Someshvara III</a>, who ruled from present-day Karnataka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>Dosa is high in <a href="/wiki/Carbohydrate" title="Carbohydrate">carbohydrates</a> and contains no added sugars or saturated fats. As its key ingredients are rice and <a href="/wiki/Vigna_mungo" title="Vigna mungo">black gram</a>, it is also a good source of <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">protein</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The fermentation process increases the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vitamin_B" title="Vitamin B">vitamin B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitamin_C" title="Vitamin C">vitamin C</a> content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> There are also instant mix products for making dosa which usually contain higher amounts of rice.</p>

<p>A mixture of <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_gram" title="Black gram">black grams</a> that has been soaked in water is ground finely to form a batter. Some add a handful of <a href="/wiki/Fenugreek" title="Fenugreek">fenugreek</a> seeds soaked along with the rice. The proportion of rice to lentils is generally 4:1 or 5:1. The batter is allowed to ferment overnight. After the overnight fermentation, the batter is mixed with water to get the desired thickness. The batter is then ladled onto a hot <i><a href="/wiki/Tava" title="Tava">tava</a></i> (griddle) greased with oil or <i><a href="/wiki/Ghee" title="Ghee">ghee</a></i> (clarified butter). It is spread out evenly with the base of a ladle or bowl to form a pancake. A dosa is served hot, either folded in half or rolled like a wrap. It is also usually served with chutney and <a href="/wiki/Sambar_(dish)" title="Sambar (dish)">sambar</a>. The mixture of black grams and rice can be replaced with highly refined wheat flour or <a href="/wiki/Semolina" title="Semolina">semolina</a>.</p>

<p>Dosa can be stuffed with fillings of vegetables and sauces to make a quick meal. They are typically served with a vegetarian <a href="/wiki/Side_dish" title="Side dish">side dish</a> which varies according to regional and personal preferences. Common side items are:</p>

<li>Dry chutney (<a href="/wiki/Idli_podi" title="Idli podi">Idli podi</a> or molagapodi): a powder of spices and sometimes desiccated coconut</li>

</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Masala_dosa">Masala dosa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Masala dosa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Masala_dosa" title="Masala dosa">Masala dosa</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Masala_Thosai.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1692" data-file-width="1959" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Masala_Thosai.jpg/200px-Masala_Thosai.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Masala_Thosai.jpg/300px-Masala_Thosai.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Masala_Thosai.jpg/400px-Masala_Thosai.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Masala_Thosai.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Masala_dosa" title="Masala dosa">Masala dosa</a> as served in <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a></div>

<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Masala_dosa" title="Masala dosa">masala dosa</a></i> is made by stuffing a dosa with a lightly cooked filling of potatoes, fried onions and spices. The dosa is wrapped around an onion and potato curry or <a href="/wiki/Spice_mix" title="Spice mix">masala</a>. Masala dosa was listed as number 49 on <i>World's 50 most delicious foods</i> compiled by <i>CNN Go</i> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_foods">Related foods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Related foods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Uttapam" title="Uttapam">Uttapam</a>: a dosa-like dish made from the same batter. Unlike a dosa, which is crisp and unlike relatively soggy crepes, it is a thick pancake mostly topped with diced onions, tomatoes, cilantro or cheese. Uttapam is sometimes characterized as an Indian pizza.</li>
<li>Pesarattu: a dosa-like preparation prepared from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_gram" title="Green gram">green gram</a>, which is typically served with a ginger and tamarind chutney</li>
<li>Adai: a dosa-like dish prepared from a combination of lentils, namely <i>black gram, kadalai</i> (<a href="/wiki/Chickpea" title="Chickpea">Chickpea</a>) and green gram .</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Appam" title="Appam">Appam</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aappam" title="Aappam">aappam</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hopper_(food)" title="Hopper (food)">hopper</a>Â : a pancake prepared from a combination of patted rice batter. The center is thicker and the outer rim is very thin. Served with sweet coconut milk.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chakuli_pitha" title="Chakuli pitha">Chakuli pitha</a>: batter contains more black gram and less rice flour</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Apam_balik" title="Apam balik">Apam balik</a>: made from a mixture of flour, eggs, sugar, baking soda, coconut milk and water.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jianbing" title="Jianbing">Jianbing</a>: dosa like dish made in china</li>
</ul>
