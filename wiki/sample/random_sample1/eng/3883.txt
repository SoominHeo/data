<p>The <b>Toei Mita Line</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">都営地下鉄三田線</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Toei Chikatetsu Mita-sen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">subway</a> line of the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Bureau_of_Transportation" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Bureau of Transportation">Tokyo Metropolitan Bureau of Transportation</a> (Toei) network in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, Japan. The line runs between <a href="/wiki/Nishi-Takashimadaira_Station" title="Nishi-Takashimadaira Station">Nishi-Takashimadaira</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Itabashi,_Tokyo" title="Itabashi, Tokyo">Itabashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meguro_Station" title="Meguro Station">Meguro</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinagawa,_Tokyo" title="Shinagawa, Tokyo">Shinagawa</a>. Trains continue with direct service into the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dky%C5%AB_Meguro_Line" title="Tōkyū Meguro Line">Meguro Line</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tokyu_Corporation" title="Tokyu Corporation">Tokyu Corporation</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hiyoshi_Station_(Kanagawa)" title="Hiyoshi Station (Kanagawa)">Hiyoshi</a>. The portion between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shirokane-Takanawa_Station" title="Shirokane-Takanawa Station">Shirokane-Takanawa</a> and Meguro is shared with the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Namboku_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Namboku Line">Tokyo Metro Namboku Line</a>.</p>
<p>On maps and signboards, the line is shown in "blue" (<span style="color:#0079c2; background:#0079c2;">O</span>). Stations carry the letter "I" followed by a two-digit number.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toei_Mita_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Platforms on the Mita Line are equipped with chest-height <a href="/wiki/Automatic_platform_gate" title="Automatic platform gate">automatic platform gates</a> that open in sync with the train doors. The line was the first in the Tokyo subway system to have low barriers. The <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Namboku_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Namboku Line">Tokyo Metro Namboku Line</a> has used full-height <a href="/wiki/Platform_screen_doors" title="Platform screen doors">platform screen doors</a> since its opening.</p>
<p>The right-of-way and stations between Shirokane-Takanawa and Meguro are shared with the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Namboku_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Namboku Line">Tokyo Metro Namboku Line</a>. Under an agreement of both parties, the fare for this section is calculated on the Toei system for passengers traveling to stations on the Mita Line past Shirokane-Takanawa, using the Tokyo Metro system for those travelling on the Namboku Line past Shirokane-Takanawa, and on the system "most beneficial to the passenger" (presently the Tokyo Metro schedule) for travel solely on the shared section.</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Bureau_of_Transportation" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Bureau of Transportation">Tokyo Metropolitan Bureau of Transportation</a>, as of June 2009 the Mita Line is the ninth most crowded subway line in Tokyo, running at 164% capacity between <a href="/wiki/Nishi-Sugamo_Station" title="Nishi-Sugamo Station">Nishi-Sugamo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sugamo_Station" title="Sugamo Station">Sugamo</a> stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toei_Mita_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The Mita Line was first envisioned in 1957 as a northern branch of Line 5 (the present <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_T%C5%8Dzai_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Tōzai Line">Tōzai Line</a>), serving the section between Ōtemachi and Itabashi. Under a revised proposal in 1962, the line was made independent and its construction undertaken by the Tokyo Metropolitan Government. The new line (Line 6) was planned to run from <a href="/wiki/Gotanda_Station" title="Gotanda Station">Gotanda Station</a> on the southwestern side of the <a href="/wiki/Yamanote_Line" title="Yamanote Line">Yamanote Line</a> through central Tokyo, with its northern extensions via Yamatochō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大和町</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in Itabashi (near present <a href="/wiki/Itabashi-honch%C5%8D_Station" title="Itabashi-honchō Station">Itabashi-honchō</a>), diverting to <a href="/wiki/Kami-Itabashi_Station" title="Kami-Itabashi Station">Kami-Itabashi</a> and Shimura <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">志村</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (present <a href="/wiki/Takashimadaira_Station" title="Takashimadaira Station">Takashimadaira</a>). The southernmost portion, from <a href="/wiki/Sengakuji_Station" title="Sengakuji Station">Sengakuji</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nishi-Magome_Station" title="Nishi-Magome Station">Nishi-Magome</a> and Nishi-Magome depot, was to be shared with Line 1 (<a href="/wiki/Toei_Asakusa_Line" title="Toei Asakusa Line">Asakusa Line</a>); therefore, Line 6 would be <span class="nowrap">1,435 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">4 ft <span class="frac">8<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> in</span>) gauge.</p>
<p>Due to political considerations, the design of the Mita Line changed several times during the early 1960s. There were plans for it to run to <a href="/wiki/Toda,_Saitama" title="Toda, Saitama">Toda, Saitama</a>, to serve a boat-racing venue for the <a href="/wiki/1964_Summer_Olympics" title="1964 Summer Olympics">1964 Summer Olympics</a>. The government of Saitama also proposed the construction of a new subway line which would allow through service on the Mita Line as far as <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmiya_Station_(Saitama)" title="Ōmiya Station (Saitama)">Ōmiya Station</a>. In 1964, these plans were changed to allow the Mita Line to connect with the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dbu_T%C5%8Dj%C5%8D_Line" title="Tōbu Tōjō Line">Tōbu Tōjō Line</a> via a branch to be built by Tobu between Yamatomachi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大和町</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (now <a href="/wiki/Wak%C5%8Dshi_Station" title="Wakōshi Station">Wakōshi</a>), and Shimura, the northern end of Line 6. At the southern end the junction with the <a href="/wiki/Tokyu_Corporation" title="Tokyu Corporation">Tokyu</a> network would be via a connecting line, which would be constructed by Tokyu from Sengakuji to Kirigaya <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">桐ヶ谷</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dky%C5%AB_Ikegami_Line" title="Tōkyū Ikegami Line">Tōkyū Ikegami Line</a>; the route would continue to the then-<a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dky%C5%AB_Den-en-toshi_Line" title="Tōkyū Den-en-toshi Line">Den-en-toshi Line</a> and finally west, down to <a href="/wiki/Nagatsuta_Station" title="Nagatsuta Station">Nagatsuta</a>. As a result, the construction standards of Line 6 were based on those of Tobu and Tokyu (such as <span class="nowrap">1,067 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">3 ft 6 in</span>) gauge track and 20-meter-long cars). A depot was planned at Shimura, independent of the Nishi-Magome depot on Line 1.</p>
<p>However, both Tokyu and Tobu decided the following year to operate their through services with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teito_Rapid_Transit_Authority" title="Teito Rapid Transit Authority">Teito Rapid Transit Authority</a> (TRTA, now <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro" title="Tokyo Metro">Tokyo Metro</a>) lines instead. With no through service opportunities available the Tokyo Metropolitan Government began construction on the central portion of the line, leaving the plans for the Itabashi and Mita ends open for future development. This required an extension somewhere south of Seishōkō-mae <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">清正公前</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (present <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shirokane-Takanawa_Station" title="Shirokane-Takanawa Station">Shirokane-Takanawa</a>), probably to the then-Mekama Line of Tokyu (on the commencement of inter running to Mita and Namboku lines, the Mekama Line was divided into the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dky%C5%AB_Meguro_Line" title="Tōkyū Meguro Line">Meguro Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dky%C5%AB_Tamagawa_Line" title="Tōkyū Tamagawa Line">Tōkyū Tamagawa Line</a>) which competed with TRTA Line 7, later called the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Namboku_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Namboku Line">Tokyo Metro Namboku Line</a>.</p>
<p>The first segment of the line opened on December 27, 1968, between <a href="/wiki/Sugamo_Station" title="Sugamo Station">Sugamo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Takashimadaira_Station" title="Takashimadaira Station">Takashimadaira</a> (10.4 km). The line was extended a further 7.3 km south to <a href="/wiki/Hibiya_Station" title="Hibiya Station">Hibiya</a> on June 30, 1972 and 3.3 km further south to Mita on November 27, 1973. The northern 1.5 km extension (originally licensed to Tobu and later transferred to the Tokyo government bureau of transportation) was completed on May 6, 1976. For the next 24 years, the line operated between Mita and Nishi-Takashimadaira; the authorized Mita and Sengakuji section had been left uncompleted.</p>
<p>In 1985 the then-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Transport (Japan)">Ministry of Transport</a> finally settled the plan regarding the southern extension of the line, and shelved all plans for further extension to the north due to the development of the <a href="/wiki/Saiky%C5%8D_Line" title="Saikyō Line">Saikyō Line</a>. On September 26, 2000, the final 4 km segment from Mita to Meguro opened, and through service to the Meguro Line of Tokyu began. The line switched to driver-only train operation at the same time.</p>
