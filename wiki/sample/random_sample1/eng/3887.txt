<p><b>Toyooka Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">豊岡駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Toyooka-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">train station</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%AB_Hamanako_Railroad_Tenry%C5%AB_Hamanako_Line" title="Tenryū Hamanako Railroad Tenryū Hamanako Line">Tenryū Hamanako Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iwata,_Shizuoka" title="Iwata, Shizuoka">Iwata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka_Prefecture" title="Shizuoka Prefecture">Shizuoka Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is located 23.0 rail kilometers from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> of the line at <a href="/wiki/Kakegawa_Station" title="Kakegawa Station">Kakegawa Station</a></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_history">Station history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toyooka_Station_(Shizuoka)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Station history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Toyooka Station was established on June 1, 1940, when the section of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_National_Railways" title="Japan National Railways">Japan National Railways</a> Futamata Line was extended from <a href="/wiki/Ensh%C5%AB-Mori_Station" title="Enshū-Mori Station">Enshū-Mori Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kanasashi_Station" title="Kanasashi Station">Kanasashi Station</a>. Scheduled freight services were discontinued from February 1964. After the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of JNR on March 15, 1987, the station came under the control of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%AB_Hamanako_Railroad_Tenry%C5%AB_Hamanako_Line" title="Tenryū Hamanako Railroad Tenryū Hamanako Line">Tenryū Hamanako Line</a>. The station was rebuilt in 2003 through the efforts of the local Chamber of Commerce, and an additional platform was added at that time.</p>
