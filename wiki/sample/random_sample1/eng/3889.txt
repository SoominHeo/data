<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Toyotomi Kunimatsu</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">In this <a href="/wiki/Japanese_name" title="Japanese name">Japanese name</a>, the family name is Toyotomi.</div>
<p><b>Toyotomi Kunimatsu</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">豊臣 国松</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 1608 – June 19, 1615)</span> was a member of the Japanese clan of <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_clan" title="Toyotomi clan">Toyotomi</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> of the 17th century. Kunimatsu was famed for being the son of <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyori" title="Toyotomi Hideyori">Toyotomi Hideyori</a>, who was the son of <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyoshi" title="Toyotomi Hideyoshi">Toyotomi Hideyoshi</a>. In 1615 during the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Osaka" title="Siege of Osaka">Siege of Osaka</a>, Hideyori was defeated and committed suicide by <i><a href="/wiki/Seppuku" title="Seppuku">seppuku</a></i>, while his castle was taken by the forces of <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Hidetada" title="Tokugawa Hidetada">Tokugawa Hidetada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a>. Kunimatsu, who was seven years of age at the time, was captured by <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_clan" title="Tokugawa clan">Tokugawa</a> forces, and was later executed by <a href="/wiki/Decapitation" title="Decapitation">decapitation</a>.</p>
<p>Theories and rumors in Japan say he could have escaped through some secret tunnel, and Tokugawa then set up an execution of a decoy or body double (known as a <i>kagemusha</i>), to make official the extinction of Toyotomi's bloodline.</p>
