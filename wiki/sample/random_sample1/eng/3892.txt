<p><b>Doina Ofelia Melinte</b> (<small>Romanian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Romanian" title="Help:IPA for Romanian">[ˈdojna meˈlinte]</a></span>; née <b>Beșliu</b> on 27 December 1956) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanian</a> middle-distance runner. She competed at the 1980, 1984, 1988 and 1992 Olympics and won a gold and a silver medal in 1984. She held the world indoor title in 1987 and 1989 and the European indoor title in 1985, 1988 and 1990 in the 1500 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iaaf_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-iaaf-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doina_Melinte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She competed as Doina Beșliu at the 1980 Moscow Olympics and reached the semi-finals of the 800 metres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1982, now competing as Doina Melinte, she was the fastest woman in the world at 800 metres but only finished sixth in the final of that years <a href="/wiki/1982_European_Athletics_Championships" title="1982 European Athletics Championships">European Championships</a> in Athens. She also finished sixth in the 800 m at the <a href="/wiki/1983_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1983 World Championships in Athletics">1983 World Championships</a> in Helsinki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iaaf_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-iaaf-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the 1984 Los Angeles Olympics, she won a gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/800_metres" title="800 metres">800 metres</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Gallagher" title="Kim Gallagher">Kim Gallagher</a> of the USA and countrywoman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fi%C5%A3a_Lovin" title="Fiţa Lovin">Fiţa Lovin</a>, and a silver medal in the 1500 metres behind <a href="/wiki/Gabriella_Dorio" title="Gabriella Dorio">Gabriella Dorio</a> of Italy and ahead of another Romanian, <a href="/wiki/Maricica_Puic%C4%83" title="Maricica Puică">Maricica Puică</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup> She won a 1500 m bronze medal at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1986_European_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1986 European Championships in Athletics">1986 European Championships</a>, behind the Soviet pair of <a href="/wiki/Ravilya_Agletdinova" title="Ravilya Agletdinova">Ravilya Agletdinova</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tatyana_Samolenko" title="Tatyana Samolenko">Tatyana Samolenko</a>. In March 1987, she won the 1500 m at the <a href="/wiki/1987_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="1987 IAAF World Indoor Championships">World Indoor Championships</a> in Indianapolis, ahead of Samolenko. In September of that year, she won a bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/1987_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1987 World Championships in Athletics">World Championships</a> in Rome. She originally finished fourth but was promoted one position after the disqualification of original bronze medal winner <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Gasser" title="Sandra Gasser">Sandra Gasser</a>.</p>
<p>At her third Olympic Games in <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1988_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1988 Summer Olympics">Seoul 1988</a>, she finished a disappointing ninth in the 1500 m final won by her team-mate <a href="/wiki/Paula_Ivan" title="Paula Ivan">Paula Ivan</a>. In 1989, she defended her 1500 m <a href="/wiki/1989_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="1989 IAAF World Indoor Championships">World Indoor title</a> in Budapest, winning in a championship record of 4:04.79. In February 1990, she broke the world indoor mile record with 4:17.14 in East Rutherford. The record (as of 2014) still stands. A month later, she won her third <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Indoor_Athletics_Championships" title="European Indoor Athletics Championships">European Indoor title</a> over 1500 m in Glasgow. A medal favourite for the <a href="/wiki/1990_European_Athletics_Championships" title="1990 European Athletics Championships">1990 European Championships</a> in Split, she could only finish sixth in the 1500 m final. In 1991, she was fourth in the 1500 m finals at both the <a href="/wiki/1991_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="1991 IAAF World Indoor Championships">World Indoor Championships</a> in Seville and the <a href="/wiki/1991_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1991 World Championships in Athletics">World Championships</a> in Tokyo.</p>
<p>Melinte concluded her international career at the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1992_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Barcelona Olympics</a>, where she dropped out of the 1500 m final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cosr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-cosr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doina_Melinte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Melinte first wanted to be a gymnast or a ballerina, but did not have adequate conditions for training. She then played handball before changing to athletics. Around 1980–82 she married her coach Dorin Melinte, and after retiring from competitions in 1992 became a coach herself. In 2010–2012 she served as director of the National Agency for Youth and Sports (<a class="extiw" href="https://ro.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autoritatea_Na%C5%A3ional%C4%83_pentru_Sport_%C5%9Fi_Tineret" title="ro:Autoritatea Naţională pentru Sport şi Tineret">Autoritatea Naţională pentru Sport şi Tineret</a>) and later became vice president of the national anti-doping agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cosr_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-cosr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
