<p><b>Doink the Clown</b> is the name and persona originated by <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a> <a href="/wiki/Matt_Osborne" title="Matt Osborne">Matt Osborne</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">World Wrestling Federation</a> (WWF) and used there from 1992 to 1997, and sporadically thereafter. After Osborne left the WWF in late 1993, the character was played by five wrestlers in the WWF (later WWE). Osborne continued to sporadically perform as Doink until six months before his death in June 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Federation">World Wrestling Federation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Federation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Former WWE producer <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Prichard" title="Bruce Prichard">Bruce Prichard</a> said in an interview on <i>The Steve Austin Show</i> that <a href="/wiki/Road_Warrior_Hawk" title="Road Warrior Hawk">Michael Hegstrand</a> had originally conceived the idea of a miserable clown character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After making appearances in late 1992 in the crowd and at ringside, playing tricks on the fans and wrestlers, the Doink character made his in-ring debut in the WWF in 1993, originally wrestling as a technically sound <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heel</a>. Doink played cruel jokes on both fans and wrestlers in order to amuse himself and put them off guard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wwebio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-wwebio-3">[3]</a></sup> Some of his villainous pranks included tripping <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ray_Traylor" title="Ray Traylor">The Big Boss Man</a> with a tripwire, dumping water on <a href="/wiki/Marty_Jannetty" title="Marty Jannetty">Marty Jannetty</a> and attacking <a href="/wiki/Brian_Adams_(wrestler)" title="Brian Adams (wrestler)">Crush</a> with a loaded prosthetic arm. He clashed with Crush at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_IX" title="WrestleMania IX">WrestleMania IX</a>, a match which he won after the appearance of an identical Doink (played by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Keirn" title="Steve Keirn">Steve Keirn</a>) from underneath the ring. Doink also briefly feuded with <a href="/wiki/Randy_Savage" title="Randy Savage">Randy Savage</a> on an early edition of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Monday Night Raw</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Bret_Hart" title="Bret Hart">Bret Hart</a>, after substituting for <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lawler" title="Jerry Lawler">Jerry Lawler</a>, who faked an injury, at <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam_(1993)" title="SummerSlam (1993)">SummerSlam</a> in 1993.</p>
<p>Doink then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#T" title="List of professional wrestling terms">turned</a> on Lawler on the September 4 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWF_Wrestling_Challenge" title="WWF Wrestling Challenge">WWF Wrestling Challenge</a></i> in Lawler's The King's Court segment, making <a href="/wiki/Burger_King" title="Burger King">Burger King</a> jokes to amuse the crowd and eventually emptied a bucket of water over Lawler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wwebio_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-wwebio-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Matt_Osborne" title="Matt Osborne">Matt Osborne</a>, the original man behind Doink, was fired for re-occurring drug abuses, eventually leaving the <a href="/wiki/Gimmick_(professional_wrestling)" title="Gimmick (professional wrestling)">gimmick</a> (after bouncing through a few others) to Ray Liachelli (also known as Ray Apollo). Now as a fan favorite and with a new <a href="/wiki/Midget" title="Midget">midget</a> sidekick <a href="/wiki/Claude_Giroux_(wrestler)" title="Claude Giroux (wrestler)">Dink</a>, Doink was more of a <a href="/wiki/Comic_relief" title="Comic relief">comic relief</a> character, but continued to pull pranks on other wrestlers (albeit more harmless and silly than outright cruel), mostly on heels like Lawler and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Heenan" title="Bobby Heenan">Bobby Heenan</a>. Doink and Dink also battled with <a href="/wiki/Bam_Bam_Bigelow" title="Bam Bam Bigelow">Bam Bam Bigelow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luna_Vachon" title="Luna Vachon">Luna Vachon</a> in a feud that culminated at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_X" title="WrestleMania X">WrestleMania X</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wwebio_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-wwebio-3">[3]</a></sup> Doink would later encounter Jerry Lawler again in a match at <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series_(1994)" title="Survivor Series (1994)">Survivor Series</a>. In this match, Doink and Dink teamed with Wink and Pink to meet Lawler's dwarf team of 'little kings' Queazy, Cheezy, and Sleazy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wwebio_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-wwebio-3">[3]</a></sup> Eventually, Doink became a <a href="/wiki/Job_(professional_wrestling)" title="Job (professional wrestling)">jobber</a>, regularly losing to wrestlers like <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Jarrett" title="Jeff Jarrett">Jeff Jarrett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jinsei_Shinzaki" title="Jinsei Shinzaki">Hakushi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dan_Spivey" title="Dan Spivey">Waylon Mercy</a> and, in his final televised match in September 1995, to <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Hunter Hearst Helmsley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Doink reemerged one last time in 1997 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slammy_Awards" title="Slammy Awards">Slammy Awards</a> and was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Stone_Cold_Steve_Austin" title="Stone Cold Steve Austin">Stone Cold Steve Austin</a>, amidst crowd chants of "kill the clown".</p>
<p>Doink is a playable character in <a href="/wiki/Acclaim_Entertainment" title="Acclaim Entertainment">Acclaim</a>'s 1994 video game, <i><a href="/wiki/WWF_Raw_(video_game)" title="WWF Raw (video game)">WWF Raw</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Midway_Games" title="Midway Games">Midway</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/WWF_WrestleMania:_The_Arcade_Game" title="WWF WrestleMania: The Arcade Game">WWF WrestleMania: The Arcade Game</a></i> (1995).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extreme_Championship_Wrestling">Extreme Championship Wrestling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Extreme Championship Wrestling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following his departure from the WWF, Osborne appeared (as Matt Borne) in <a href="/wiki/Extreme_Championship_Wrestling" title="Extreme Championship Wrestling">Extreme Championship Wrestling</a> (ECW) for several matches as Doink in a blue and green clown suit, setting up an angle where ECW champion <a href="/wiki/Shane_Douglas" title="Shane Douglas">Shane Douglas</a> criticized Vince McMahon for turning a talented wrestler like Borne into a comic relief character, and claimed that he knew how to bring out Borne's full potential. Borne then made a few appearances with Douglas as "himself", sporting his face half-painted with the Doink makeup. His attitude insinuated that he had developed <a href="/wiki/Borderline_personality_disorder" title="Borderline personality disorder">borderline personality disorder</a> from having been forced to wrestle as a clown; after winning matches he would dress his opponent in clown accessories to humiliate them. His ring name under this gimmick was "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Born_again_(Christianity)" title="Born again (Christianity)">Borne Again</a>".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Entertainment">World Wrestling Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Since 1997, Doink has appeared sporadically in WWE. Ray Apollo returned to play him in the Gimmick Battle Royal at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_X-Seven" title="WrestleMania X-Seven">WrestleMania X-Seven</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On December 10, 2007, Doink played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_Borne" title="Matt Borne">Matt Borne</a> participated in a battle royal of 15 WWE alumni for the <i>Raw</i> 15th anniversary special episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a> dressed as Doink when he attacked <a href="/wiki/William_Regal" title="William Regal">William Regal</a> on the March 26, 2001 <i>Raw</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Played by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Dinsmore" title="Nick Dinsmore">Nick Dinsmore</a>, he showed up in the A.P.A. Bar Room Brawl at <a href="/wiki/Vengeance_(2003)" title="Vengeance (2003)">Vengeance</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was selected by Rhino to face <a href="/wiki/Chris_Benoit" title="Chris Benoit">Chris Benoit</a> on the July 31, 2003 <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smackdown!" title="Smackdown!">Smackdown!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-oww-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Played by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lombardi" title="Steve Lombardi">Steve Lombardi</a>, he wrestled <a href="/wiki/Rob_Conway" title="Rob Conway">Rob Conway</a> on an October 2005 episode of <i>Raw</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On June 2, 2007, Doink, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Dinsmore" title="Nick Dinsmore">Eugene</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glenn_Jacobs" title="Glenn Jacobs">Kane</a> defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Fatu" title="Eddie Fatu">Umaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viscera_(wrestler)" title="Viscera (wrestler)">Viscera</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_Fertig" title="Kevin Fertig">Kevin Thorn</a> on the 34th edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night%27s_Main_Event_results#Saturday_Night.27s_Main_Event_XXXIV" title="Saturday Night's Main Event results">Saturday Night's Main Event</a></i>. On the July 12, 2010 <i>Raw</i>, Doink teamed with <a href="/wiki/William_Regal" title="William Regal">William Regal</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primo_Col%C3%B3n" title="Primo Colón">Primo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zack_Ryder" title="Zack Ryder">Zack Ryder</a> to lose to <a href="/wiki/Santino_Marella" title="Santino Marella">Santino Marella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goldust" title="Goldust">Goldust</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kozlov" title="Vladimir Kozlov">Vladimir Kozlov</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a>, when he was pinned by Khali. On the July 2, 2012 <i>Raw</i>, he made a surprise return and lost to <a href="/wiki/Heath_Slater" title="Heath Slater">Heath Slater</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He reappeared on July 23, alongside several other WWE alumni, to help <a href="/wiki/Lita_(wrestler)" title="Lita (wrestler)">Lita</a> take down Slater on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_1000" title="WWE Raw 1000">WWE Raw 1000</a></i>, the one thousandth episode of <i>Raw</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independent_circuit">Independent circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Independent circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In early 2010, Osborne reinvented the Doink character to resemble <a href="/wiki/Heath_Ledger" title="Heath Ledger">Heath Ledger</a>'s portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Joker_(comics)" title="Joker (comics)">The Joker</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film)" title="The Dark Knight (film)">The Dark Knight</a></i>, nicknaming the incarnation 'Reborne Again'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebourne_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-rebourne-11">[11]</a></sup> The new character debuted on March 27 for ISPW in New Jersey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebourne_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-rebourne-11">[11]</a></sup> On May 23, 2010, Doink the Clown, portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Dusty_Wolfe" title="Dusty Wolfe">Dusty Wolfe</a>, interfered against <a href="/wiki/Skandor_Akbar" title="Skandor Akbar">Skandor Akbar</a> and his men Dr. Knuckles and Rommel. This caused them to lose the Wrecking Ball Wrestling tag titles. In retaliation Akbar called on the original Doink <a href="/wiki/Matt_Osborne" title="Matt Osborne">Matt Borne</a>. Wolfe and Borne were scheduled to meet on Aug. 15, 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-12">[12]</a></sup> Wolfe would later no show the event. On August 8, 2010, Borne, as Doink the Clown, won the Wrecking Ball Wrestling Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> On May 30, 2015, at MAGNUM Pro's Over The Top event in Omaha Nebraska, Doink (portrayed by Gary Withrow) participated in an Over The Top Battle Royal.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incarnations">Incarnations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Incarnations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wrestlers_who_used_the_Doink_gimmick">Wrestlers who used the Doink gimmick</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Wrestlers who used the Doink gimmick">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Seven men have officially used the Doink gimmick. Six wrestlers (including two tag-teams) have used the costume for storyline purposes, at various times.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Matt_Osborne" title="Matt Osborne">Matt Osborne</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-oww-8">[8]</a></sup> – the original Doink, left WWF in December 1993, died June 28, 2013.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Steve_Keirn" title="Steve Keirn">Steve Keirn</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-oww-8">[8]</a></sup> – wrestled as the "illusion" Doink at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_IX" title="WrestleMania IX">WrestleMania IX</a> and occasionally the "real" Doink at house shows.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Steve_Lombardi" title="Steve Lombardi">Steve Lombardi</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-oww-8">[8]</a></sup> – occasionally wrestled as Doink at house shows and dresses as Doink for various WWE appearances.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dusty_Wolfe" title="Dusty Wolfe">Dusty Wolfe</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-oww-8">[8]</a></sup> – wrestled as Doink in the NWA and other indie promotions.</li>
<li>John Maloof - wrestled as Doink in the WWF also after Matt Osbourne left the company</li>
<li>Ray Apollo – also wrestled as Doink in WWF after Osborne left.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Maraldo" title="Mike Maraldo">Mike Maraldo</a> - wrestled <a href="/wiki/Raven_(wrestler)" title="Raven (wrestler)">Scotty Flamingo</a> as Doink on a <a href="/wiki/Smoky_Mountain_Wrestling" title="Smoky Mountain Wrestling">Smoky Mountain Wrestling</a> card in November 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Dinsmore" title="Nick Dinsmore">Nick Dinsmore</a> – wrestled as Doink on the July 31, 2003 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown!</a></i>, losing to <a href="/wiki/Chris_Benoit" title="Chris Benoit">Chris Benoit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oww_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-oww-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>Gary Withrow - wrestled as Doink on MAGNUM Pro's Over The Top event in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omaha" title="Omaha">Omaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wrestlers_who_used_Doink.27s_costume_for_storyline_purposes">Wrestlers who used Doink's costume for storyline purposes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doink_the_Clown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Wrestlers who used Doink's costume for storyline purposes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jeff_Jarrett" title="Jeff Jarrett">Jeff Jarrett</a> once dressed up as Doink to pull pranks on Dink.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Men_on_a_Mission" title="Men on a Mission">Men on a Mission</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Bushwhackers" title="The Bushwhackers">The Bushwhackers</a> wrestled as "The Four Doinks" at the <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series_(1993)" title="Survivor Series (1993)">1993 Survivor Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a> took on the persona of Doink in order to perform an ambush on <a href="/wiki/William_Regal" title="William Regal">William Regal</a>.</li>
</ul>
