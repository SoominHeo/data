<p><b>.me</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code_top-level_domain" title="Country code top-level domain">country code top-level domain</a> (ccTLD) for <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a>.</p>
<p>The <b>.me</b> top level domain replaced the <a href="/wiki/.yu" title=".yu">.yu</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>) and <a href="#.cg.yu">.cg.yu</a> domains previously used by <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <b>.me</b> registry is operated by doMEn,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> which won a contract to do so after a bid process conducted by the government of Montenegro and was launched through various accredited registrars around the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Montenegro declared its independence from <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a> on 3 June 2006, after a majority of Montenegrins supported independence in a <a href="/wiki/Montenegrin_independence_referendum,_2006" title="Montenegrin independence referendum, 2006">national referendum</a>. As a constituent part of that country, Montenegro had unofficially been using the <a href="#.cg.yu">.cg.yu</a> <a href="/wiki/Second-level_domain" title="Second-level domain">second-level domain</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/.cs" title=".cs">.cs</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (TLD) had been assigned to Serbia and Montenegro in 2003 following the breakup of <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>, but remained unused. Montenegro was assigned the <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1" title="ISO 3166-1">ISO 3166-1</a> two-letter code "ME", which was allocated by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) in September 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (most other possible abbreviations of Montenegro (<a href="/wiki/.mo" title=".mo">MO</a>, <a href="/wiki/.mn" title=".mn">MN</a>, <a href="/wiki/.mt" title=".mt">MT</a>, <a href="/wiki/.mg" title=".mg">MG</a> and <a href="/wiki/.mr" title=".mr">MR</a>) and its Montenegrin name Crna Gora (<a href="/wiki/.cg" title=".cg">CG</a>) having already been taken).</p>
<p>In September 2007, ICANN delegated the .me domain to the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Montenegro" title="Government of Montenegro">Government of Montenegro</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> with the former <a href="/wiki/.yu" title=".yu">.yu</a> domain to be operated temporarily by the <a href="/wiki/.rs" title=".rs">.rs</a> domain registry (<a href="/wiki/.rs" title=".rs">Serbian National Register of Internet Domain Names</a>) until its eventual abolition on or before 30 September 2009. Delegation of root <a href="/wiki/Name_server" title="Name server">name servers</a> was approved by <a href="/wiki/Internet_Assigned_Numbers_Authority" title="Internet Assigned Numbers Authority">IANA</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> establishing .me. The .me domain became active on 24 September 2007 and a "Public Invitation for selection of the Agent for domain registration under the national Internet domain of Montenegro" was posted on 14 November. doMEn d.o.o.âa Montenegro-based joint venture whose partners are <a href="/wiki/Afilias" title="Afilias">Afilias</a>, <a href="/wiki/GoDaddy" title="GoDaddy">GoDaddy</a>.com, and ME-net d.o.o.âwas selected as the new registry operator.</p>
<p>On 6 May 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications_in_Montenegro#Internet" title="Telecommunications in Montenegro">General Sunrise</a> period for the .me registry began for all eligible trademark holders worldwide, and on 16 July 2008, registration was made available for all .me domains at various registrars.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_structure_for_.me">Domain structure for .me</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Domain structure for .me">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The .me extension was assigned to Montenegro as a country code after it became an independent nation in June 2006. However, the Montenegro government decided .me would be operated as a generic name after considering the potential worldwide appeal for the extension.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Third-level registrations are available to Montenegrin citizens and companies in the following zones:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>.co.me</b> - unlimited registration, but appropriate for corporations</li>
<li><b>.net.me</b> - unlimited registration, but appropriate for providers of Internet services</li>
<li><b>.org.me</b> - unlimited registration, but appropriate for civil organizations and associations</li>
<li><b>.edu.me</b> - Educational institutions, such as primary and high schools</li>
<li><b>.ac.me</b> - Academic and post-secondary organizations such as universities; University of Montenegro has ucg.ac.me as its primary domain</li>
<li><b>.gov.me</b> - State and Government authorities</li>
<li><b>.its.me</b> or <b>.priv.me</b> - unlimited registration, but appropriate for personal use</li>
</ul>
<p>Apart from the official .me hierarchy, there are a number of private <a href="/wiki/Second-level_domain" title="Second-level domain">second-level domains</a> powered by Namely PRO platform.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunrise.2C_land_rush.2C_and_open_registration_periods">Sunrise, land rush, and open registration periods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sunrise, land rush, and open registration periods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 6 May 2008, the .me registry opened the <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_period" title="Sunrise period">sunrise period</a> for all eligible trademark holders anywhere in the world.</p>
<p>Throughout June and July 2008, multiple land rush applications were received, which resulted in more than 2,500 land rush auctions that were held during July and August 2008. Go Live was opened on 17 July 2008 when registrations were made available for all unreserved .me <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domains</a> via various registrars.</p>
<p>During the .me land rush auction period, more than <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>2,000,000 was generated with names like insure.me and sync.me going to the highest bidder.</p>
<p>At the beginning of August 2008, 100,000 .me <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain names</a> were registered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Sources say part of the worldwide appeal for the .me domain extension is the marketing aspect.</p>
<p>In 2008 in terms of the number of Web pages indexed by <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> among all TLDs, .me sites have enjoyed the fastest growth with more than 50% a month. In the same year, .me topped its potential rivals <a href="/wiki/.mobi" title=".mobi">.mobi</a> and <a href="/wiki/.asia" title=".asia">.asia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alexa_Internet" title="Alexa Internet">Alexa Internet</a> top one million websites with 341 .me sites versus 233 <a href="/wiki/.mobi" title=".mobi">.mobi</a> sites and 86 <a href="/wiki/.asia" title=".asia">.asia</a> sites. Extra potential of .me is revealed considering the fact that <a href="/wiki/.mobi" title=".mobi">.mobi</a> was launched two years before .me and <a href="/wiki/.asia" title=".asia">.asia</a> four months before .me.</p>
<p>By the middle of February 2009, 200,000 .me <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain names</a> were registered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 May 2009, number of registered .me <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain names</a> rose to 250,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In less than a year .me became more popular than <a href="/wiki/.asia" title=".asia">.asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/.jobs" title=".jobs">.jobs</a>, <a href="/wiki/.coop" title=".coop">.coop</a>, <a href="/wiki/.aero" title=".aero">.aero</a>, <a href="/wiki/.int" title=".int">.int</a>, <a href="/wiki/.mil" title=".mil">.mil</a>, <a href="/wiki/.museum" title=".museum">.museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/.name" title=".name">.name</a>, <a href="/wiki/.pro" title=".pro">.pro</a>, <a href="/wiki/.tel" title=".tel">.tel</a>, <a href="/wiki/.travel" title=".travel">.travel</a>, and other 200 <a href="/wiki/Country_code_top-level_domain" title="Country code top-level domain">country code top-level domains</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 2010, over 320,000 .me domains had been purchased, making it the fastest-selling top-level domain in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Known_hacks">Known hacks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Known hacks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most .me <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain names</a> were purchased as <a href="/wiki/Domain_hack" title="Domain hack">domain hacks</a> in English and, to a lesser extent, Dutch; 71% of names were applied for by applicants in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Because of the possibility of owning pure noun and verb combinations, their prices have stayed high: during the sunrise period, insure.me went for $68,005, and judge.me sold for $8000 in 2011.</p>
<p>.me has been used as an abbreviation for <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Accusative_case" title="Accusative case">accusative case</a> of <i>ja</i> (<i>I</i> in <a href="/wiki/South_Slavic_languages" title="South Slavic languages">South Slavic languages</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Other examples of domain hacks include <a href="/wiki/DeviantArt" title="DeviantArt">deviantArt</a> (fav.me), <a href="/wiki/WordPress" title="WordPress">WordPress</a> (wp.me),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> the <i><a href="/wiki/Despicable_Me" title="Despicable Me">Despicable Me</a></i> Web site, and <a href="/wiki/MeetMe" title="MeetMe">MeetMe</a>. .me was used by the Canadian <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_of_Canada" title="Conservative Party of Canada">Conservative Party</a> for the website ignatieff.me, a site attacking <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ignatieff" title="Michael Ignatieff">Michael Ignatieff</a>, the leader of the opposition <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Canada" title="Liberal Party of Canada">Liberal Party</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Later, domain name ignatieff.me has expired and picked by one of the <a href="/wiki/Domain_name_speculation#domainer" title="Domain name speculation">domainers</a> who converted it into a political blog.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trademark_disputes">Trademark disputes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Trademark disputes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008 three <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademark</a> cases were filed with the <a href="/wiki/World_Intellectual_Property_Organization" title="World Intellectual Property Organization">World Intellectual Property Organization</a> Arbitration and Mediation Center. All three <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain names</a> (creditmutuel.me, porsche.me, and exxonmobil.me) were transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Plaintiff" title="Plaintiff">complainants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".cg.yu">.cg.yu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: .cg.yu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the introduction of <b>.me</b>, the most used domain in Montenegro was the <a href="/wiki/Second-level_domain" title="Second-level domain">second-level domain</a> .cg.yu under <a href="/wiki/.yu" title=".yu">.yu</a>, the Internet domain name of Montenegrin <a href="/wiki/Internet_service_provider" title="Internet service provider">Internet service provider</a> <a href="/wiki/Crnogorski_Telekom" title="Crnogorski Telekom">T-Crnogorski Telekom</a>, which was allowed to such organisations. The acronym "CG" was used because of the native name for Montenegro, Crna Gora. When <a href="/wiki/.yu" title=".yu">.yu</a> TLD was phased out, all e-mail addresses ending with @cg.yu were replaced with @t-com.me, and the Web sites moved, variously, mostly under .me.</p>
