<p><b>Tokyo University of Science</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">東京理科大学</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōkyō Rika Daigaku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, formerly "Science University of Tokyo", informally <i><b>Rikadai</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">理科大</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> or simply <i><b>Ridai</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">理大</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a private research university located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinjuku,_Tokyo" title="Shinjuku, Tokyo">Shinjuku, Tokyo</a>, Japan.It's currently ranked #3 in Private universities.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_University_of_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tokyo University of Science was founded in 1881 as The Tokyo Academy of Physics by 21 graduates of the Department of Physics in the Faculty of Science, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tokyo" title="University of Tokyo">University of Tokyo</a> (then the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Seven_Universities" title="National Seven Universities">Imperial University</a>). In 1883, it was renamed the Tokyo University of Physics, and in 1949, it attained university status and became the Tokyo University of Science.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daijisen_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-daijisen-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Affiliated_schools">Affiliated schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_University_of_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Affiliated schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The university has two affiliated four-year universities: <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_University_of_Science,_Yamaguchi" title="Tokyo University of Science, Yamaguchi">Tokyo University of Science, Yamaguchi</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanyo-Onoda,_Yamaguchi" title="Sanyo-Onoda, Yamaguchi">Sanyo-Onoda, Yamaguchi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_University_of_Science,_Suwa" title="Tokyo University of Science, Suwa">Tokyo University of Science, Suwa</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Chino,_Nagano" title="Chino, Nagano">Chino, Nagano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daijisen_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-daijisen-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
