<p>The <b>men's 100 metre breaststroke</b> event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 9–11 August at the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_National_Aquatics_Center" title="Beijing National Aquatics Center">Beijing National Aquatics Center</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing,_China" title="Beijing, China">Beijing, China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swimming_schedule_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Swimming_schedule-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Japan's <a href="/wiki/Kosuke_Kitajima" title="Kosuke Kitajima">Kosuke Kitajima</a> set a new world record of 58.91 to defend his Olympic title in the event. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Dale_Oen" title="Alexander Dale Oen">Alexander Dale Oen</a> powered home with a silver in 59.20, earning a first Olympic medal for Norway in swimming. Coming from seventh place in the final turn, France's <a href="/wiki/Hugues_Duboscq" title="Hugues Duboscq">Hugues Duboscq</a> managed to repeat a bronze from Athens four years earlier, in a time of 59.37.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100breast_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-100breast-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> U.S. swimmer and defending silver medalist <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Hansen" title="Brendan Hansen">Brendan Hansen</a> pulled off a fourth-place effort in 59.57, two-tenths of a second (0.20) behind Duboscq.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Australia's <a href="/wiki/Brenton_Rickard" title="Brenton Rickard">Brenton Rickard</a> finished fifth with a time of 59.74, and was followed in sixth spot by Russia's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Sloudnov" title="Roman Sloudnov">Roman Sloudnov</a>, bronze medalist in Sydney (2000), in a national record of 59.87. Ukraine's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Igor_Borysik" title="Igor Borysik">Igor Borysik</a> (1:00.20) and American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Gangloff" title="Mark Gangloff">Mark Gangloff</a> (1:00.24) rounded out the finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100breast_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-100breast-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Earlier, Dale Oen set a new Olympic record of 59.41 in the prelims, and eventually lowered it to 59.16 in the semifinals by the next morning's session.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He continued to claim the title at the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2011_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="Swimming at the 2011 World Aquatics Championships">2011 FINA World Championships</a>, but his life came to a tragic end on April 30, 2012. Shortly after training at altitude in <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>, Dale Oen was found unconscious in his apartment shower, and died suddenly from a cardiac arrest at Flagstaff Medical Centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swimming_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metre_breaststroke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Alirezaei" title="Mohammad Alirezaei">Mohammad Alirezaei</a> was due to race against <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tom_Be%27eri" title="Tom Be'eri">Tom Be'eri</a> in the fourth heat of the 100 metre breaststroke, but pulled out, apparently under the orders from officials of the Iranian delegation. Efraim Zinger, Olympic Committee of Israel General Secretary, criticized the withdrawal saying "Politics takes precedence over sport with the Iranians and the Olympic spirit is as far from them as east is far from west". Giselle Davies, director of communications for the IOC, said that Alirezaei withdrew because of sickness and submitted his case in writing to his Federation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At the 2004 Games in Athens, Iran's <a href="/wiki/Arash_Miresmaeili" title="Arash Miresmaeili">Arash Miresmaeili</a>, a two-time world <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judo</a> champion, refused to compete against Israel's <a href="/wiki/Ehud_Vaks" title="Ehud Vaks">Ehud Vaks</a> in the opening round of the 66 kg competition, later admitting that he made his decision to show solidarity for the Palestinian cause.</p>
