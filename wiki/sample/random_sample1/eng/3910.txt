<p><b>Tokio Marine Holdings, Inc.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社東京海上ホールディングス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kabushiki_kaisha" title="Kabushiki kaisha">Kabushiki-kaisha</a> Tōkyō Kaijō Hōrudingusu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">TYO</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www2.tse.or.jp/tseHpFront/StockSearch.do?callJorEFlg=1&amp;method=&amp;topSearchStr=8766" rel="nofollow">8766</a>, <a href="/wiki/OTC_Markets_Group" title="OTC Markets Group">OTC Pink</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www.otcmarkets.com/stock/TKOMY/quote" rel="nofollow">TKOMY</a>)</span>, is a multinational <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a> <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> headquartered in Tokyo, Japan. It is the largest <a href="/wiki/Property_insurance" title="Property insurance">property</a>/<a href="/wiki/Casualty_insurance" title="Casualty insurance">casualty insurance</a> group in Japan in terms of revenue and is the parent company for the Tokio Marine Group which employs 36,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> people in 38 countries worldwide.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokio_Marine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally founded in 1879 as Tokio Marine Insurance, it is the oldest insurance company in Japan. Millea Holdings was established in 2002 to become the parent company to Tokio Marine Insurance and <b>Nichido Fire Insurance</b> in preparation for their merger, before being renamed Tokio Marine Holdings in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tokio Marine acquired the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Insurance_Companies" title="Philadelphia Insurance Companies">Philadelphia Insurance Companies</a> for $4.7 billion in 2008, and acquired the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Delphi_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Delphi Financial Group (page does not exist)">Delphi Financial Group</a> for $2.66 billion in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-3">[3]</a></sup> In June 2015, Tokio Marine announced it would be acquiring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HCC_Insurance_Holdings" title="HCC Insurance Holdings">HCC Insurance Holdings</a> for $7.5 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Tokio Marine forecast that 46% of its profits would come from outside Japan following the HCC acquisition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
