<p><b>Tokuma Shoten Publishing Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">株式会社徳間書店</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kabushiki-gaisha" title="Kabushiki-gaisha">Kabushiki-gaisha</a> Tokuma Shoten</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a publisher in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, headquartered in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shibadaimon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shibadaimon (page does not exist)">Shibadaimon</a> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%8A%9D%E5%A4%A7%E9%96%80" title="ja:芝大門">芝大門</a>), <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">Minato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was established in 1954. The company was also the parent company for the film studio <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Pictures" title="Kadokawa Pictures">Daiei Motion Pictures</a>, bought in 1974, and the record label <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%BE%B3%E9%96%93%E3%82%B8%E3%83%A3%E3%83%91%E3%83%B3%E3%82%B3%E3%83%9F%E3%83%A5%E3%83%8B%E3%82%B1%E3%83%BC%E3%82%B7%E3%83%A7%E3%83%B3%E3%82%BA" title="ja:徳間ジャパンコミュニケーションズ">Tokuma Japan Communications</a>, bought in 1972, until both were sold off when <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yasuyoshi_Tokuma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yasuyoshi Tokuma (page does not exist)">Yasuyoshi Tokuma</a> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%BE%B3%E9%96%93%E5%BA%B7%E5%BF%AB" title="ja:徳間康快">徳間 康快</a> <i>Tokuma Yasuyoshi</i>), who established this company, died in 2000.</p>
<p>The company was one of the largest entertainment publishers until the 1990s. Some of their products included music, computer and game software, movies (including <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a>), magazines, <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> and books. One of their most famous subsidiaries was <a href="/wiki/Studio_Ghibli" title="Studio Ghibli">Studio Ghibli</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokuma_Shoten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1980s they reached an agreement with <a href="/wiki/Streamline_Pictures" title="Streamline Pictures">Streamline Pictures</a> to dub <a href="/wiki/Studio_Ghibli" title="Studio Ghibli">Studio Ghibli</a> films into English language for <a href="/wiki/Oneworld" title="Oneworld">Oneworld</a> member <a href="/wiki/Japan_Airlines" title="Japan Airlines">Japan Airlines</a>' Trans-Pacific flights.</p>
<p>In August 1996, Disney and Tokuma Shoten agreed that <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Studios_Motion_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures">Disney</a> would distribute international Tokuma's <a href="/wiki/Studio_Ghibli" title="Studio Ghibli">Studio Ghibli</a> animated films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tokuma Shoten Publishing Co. consolidated its media operations on June 1, 1997 by merging <a href="/wiki/Studio_Ghibli" title="Studio Ghibli">Studio Ghibli</a>, Tokuma Shoten Intermedia software and game magazine producer and Tokuma International Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Founder Yasuyoshi Tokuma died on September 20, 2000, and the management inclined rapidly. In October of that same year, the company sold off Daiei Motion Pictures, Tokuma Japan Communications and the headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Shiodome" title="Shiodome">Shiodome</a>. Meanwhile, Studio Ghibli (that was absorbed temporarily as the anime section of Tokuma Shoten in 1999) also left from the group, and became an independent company again in 2005.</p>
<p>Tokuma Japan Communications was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Daiichi_Kosho_Company" title="Daiichi Kosho Company">Daiichikosho</a> in October 2001, and Daiei Motion Pictures was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a> in November 2002.</p>
<p>Tokuma Shoten finished the arrangement of the debt by 2005, and is developing only the publication business now.</p>
