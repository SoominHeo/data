<p><b>Tokushima Vortis</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">徳島ヴォルティス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tokushima Vorutisu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club, currently playing in the <a href="/wiki/J2_League" title="J2 League">J2 League</a>. The team is located in <a href="/wiki/Tokushima,_Tokushima" title="Tokushima, Tokushima">Tokushima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokushima_Prefecture" title="Tokushima Prefecture">Tokushima Prefecture</a>. Their home stadium is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naruto_Athletic_Stadium" title="Naruto Athletic Stadium">Naruto Athletic Stadium</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Naruto,_Tokushima" title="Naruto, Tokushima">Naruto, Tokushima</a>.</p>
<p>The name, "Vortis" was named in 1997 (see below), and it was explained as a combination of <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> "<i>Vor</i>tice" (meaning <a href="/wiki/Whirlpool" title="Whirlpool">whirlpool</a>, after the famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naruto_whirlpool" title="Naruto whirlpool">Naruto whirlpool</a> in <a href="/wiki/Naruto_Strait" title="Naruto Strait">Naruto Strait</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-otsuka_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-otsuka-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokushima_Vortis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Founded in 1955 as <i><a href="/wiki/Otsuka_Pharmaceutical" title="Otsuka Pharmaceutical">Otsuka Pharmaceutical</a> Co., Ltd. Soccer Club</i>, VORTIS joined the J-League in 2005. They are still sponsored by Otsuka's best-known brand, <a href="/wiki/Pocari_Sweat" title="Pocari Sweat">Pocari Sweat</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_drink" title="Sports drink">sports drink</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-otsuka_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-otsuka-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>They were first promoted to the old <a href="/wiki/Japan_Soccer_League" title="Japan Soccer League">Japan Soccer League</a> Division 2 in 1989, but the company's reluctance to professionalize the team forced it to compete in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Football_League_(former)" title="Japan Football League (former)">former JFL</a> and current <a href="/wiki/Japan_Football_League" title="Japan Football League">JFL</a>. In the 1997 old JFL season, they first sported a <i>Vortis Tokushima</i> name, but the lack of fan interest at the time forced them to go back to the corporate identity. They finally adopted the <i>Tokushima Vortis</i> name for good after winning the new JFL championship in 2004 and being promoted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-goal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first season in J2 was naturally a difficult one for Vortis, but they surprised many sceptics with their determination and quality of play. The team rose as high as fourth place, at one point, before slipping down the table later in the season to finish ninth. In 2006, the team was forced to rebuild, as the players who took the team into the J.League began to hit the ceiling of their abilities, and made way for younger replacements. As a result, despite the encouragement of a local rivalry with Ehime FC, Tokushima drifted down-table, and they followed it up with a last-place finish in 2007 and 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goal_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-goal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013 they earned fourth place in J2, matching the same placement they had two years before in the division and twenty years before in the old JFL Division 1; this time they won the playoff, defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyoto_Sanga_F.C." title="Kyoto Sanga F.C.">Kyoto Sanga F.C.</a> in the final round at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Stadium_(Tokyo)" title="National Olympic Stadium (Tokyo)">National Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, thus becoming the first professional Shikoku football club to compete in the top division of their national league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until their promotion, they were the only former JSL member currently a member of the J. League which has never competed in the top tier of Japanese football. With promotion and the creation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League_Division_3" title="J. League Division 3">J. League Division 3</a> in 2014, the distinction will be taken over by <a href="/wiki/Blaublitz_Akita" title="Blaublitz Akita">Blaublitz Akita</a>.</p>
