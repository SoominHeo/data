<p>The <b>Independent Basketball Association</b> (IBA) is a semi-professional men's <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_league" title="Sports league">league</a> that began play in the fall of 2011. The six charter members were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_Creek_Knights" title="Battle Creek Knights">Battle Creek Knights</a>, Chicago Redline, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Splash" title="Gary Splash">Gary Splash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kankakee_County_Soldiers" title="Kankakee County Soldiers">Kankakee County Soldiers</a>, Lake County Stars and <a href="/wiki/Rockford_Riverdawgs" title="Rockford Riverdawgs">Rockford Riverdawgs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>IBA play two separate seasons each calendar year: a Spring season (March–June) and a Fall season (September–January). Teams compete in one or both league competitions.</p>
<p>Commissioner of the IBA is Barry Bradford, president of the Kankakee County Soldiers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Independent_Basketball_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first league championship (2011–12) was won by the Kankakee County Soldiers, who also won the 2012 Spring season championship.</p>
<p>In the Spring of 2012 the league expanded beyond its midwest footprint, adding a total of seven teams: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albany_Legends" title="Albany Legends">Albany Legends</a>, Kenosha Ballers, <a href="/wiki/Lansing_Capitals" title="Lansing Capitals">Lansing Capitals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lightning" title="Los Angeles Lightning">Los Angeles Lightning</a>, Malibu Pirates, Springfield Xpress<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Trotters" title="St. Louis Trotters">St. Louis Trotters</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Holland_Dream" title="Holland Dream">Holland Dream</a> were added to the IBA for its third season in 2012-13, bringing the league total to 14 teams.</p>
<p>IBA and <a href="/wiki/Premier_Basketball_League" title="Premier Basketball League">PBL</a> joined forces in 2013, but the merger was short-lived. Travel issues, questions of league rules and other issues were too much to overcome and the two parted ways<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> before the end of the Spring season.</p>
<p>2013-14 began with the addition of the Windy City Blazers, who played previously as the Windy City Monsters in the <a href="/wiki/American_Basketball_Association_(2000%E2%80%93present)" title="American Basketball Association (2000–present)">ABA</a>. Grand Rapids Fusion and <a href="/wiki/International_Racers" title="International Racers">Markham City Racers</a> entered league play for the 2014 Spring season.</p>
<p>In the Fall season of 2014-15 four teams (<a href="/wiki/Binghamton_Pioneers" title="Binghamton Pioneers">Binghamton Pioneers</a>, Jersey G-Force, Kansas City Kryptonite, and Toronto 3D) played a partial schedule in anticipation of full league membership in 2015. CyFair Cobras, Houston Xperience and <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Soul" title="Louisiana Soul">Louisiana Soul</a> also were to compete as "branding teams" in the Fall season. None played an IBA game.</p>
<p>In December 2014 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albany_Legends" title="Albany Legends">Schenectady Legends</a> captured their first IBA championship, defeating Kenosha 154-137.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albany_High_School_(Albany,_New_York)" title="Albany High School (Albany, New York)">Albany High School</a> graduate Lloyd "Pooh" Johnson score an IBA championship game record 61 points and was named the game MVP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kankakee County won their league-best sixth championship in the Fall of 2015 by avenging their finals loss to St. Louis for the Spring title.</p>
