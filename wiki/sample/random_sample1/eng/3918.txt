<p><b>Plaza Independencia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for <b>Independence Square</b>) is the name of <a href="/wiki/Montevideo" title="Montevideo">Montevideo</a>'s most important <a href="/wiki/Plaza" title="Plaza">plaza</a>. It separates <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Vieja,_Montevideo" title="Ciudad Vieja, Montevideo">Ciudad Vieja</a> from <a href="/wiki/Centro,_Montevideo" title="Centro, Montevideo">downtown</a> Montevideo, with the <a href="/wiki/Ciudadela,_Montevideo" title="Ciudadela, Montevideo">Gateway of The Citadel</a> on one side and the beginning of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/18_de_Julio_avenue" title="18 de Julio avenue">18 de Julio avenue</a> on the other.</p>
<p>In the center, the <a href="/wiki/Artigas_Mausoleum" title="Artigas Mausoleum">Artigas Mausoleum</a> dominates the perspective.</p>
<p>Many important buildings, such as the <a href="/wiki/Sol%C3%ADs_Theatre" title="Solís Theatre">Solís Theatre</a> and the workplaces of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Uruguay" title="President of Uruguay">President of Uruguay</a> (both the <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A9vez_Palace" title="Estévez Palace">Estévez Palace</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Executive_Tower" title="Executive Tower">Executive Tower</a>) are located by this square.</p>
<p>One of the characteristic buildings located by the square is <a href="/wiki/Palacio_Salvo" title="Palacio Salvo">Palacio Salvo</a>. This square was designed in the 1830s by <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Zucchi" title="Carlo Zucchi">Carlo Zucchi</a>, inspired in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rue_de_Rivoli,_Paris" title="Rue de Rivoli, Paris">Rue de Rivoli, Paris</a>. Three decades later it was redesigned by <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_Poncini" title="Bernardo Poncini">Bernardo Poncini</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Independencia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May and June 2009 an exhibition of the "<a href="/wiki/United_Buddy_Bears" title="United Buddy Bears">United Buddy Bears</a>" was held in the square, for the second on the American continent.</p>
