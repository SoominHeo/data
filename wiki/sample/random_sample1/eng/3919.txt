<p>The <b>CIS national football team</b> was a provisional national team of the <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Football Federation of the Soviet Union">Football Federation of the Soviet Union</a> in 1992. It was accepted that the team would represent the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a>. The CIS team was created as part of transformation that was planned to take place. As the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR_national_football_team" title="USSR national football team">USSR national team</a> had already booked a spot in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1992_European_Football_Championship" title="1992 European Football Championship">Euro 1992</a> through the 1991 qualification tournament, the only way to preserve the spot for the post-Soviet team was to take part in the competition as a unified team.</p>
<p>With the end of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_1992" title="Euro 1992">Euro 1992</a>, the team was transformed into the <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia national football team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Situation">Situation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CIS_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Situation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_the_CIS_(UEFA_Euro_1992).svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="900" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Flag_of_the_CIS_%28UEFA_Euro_1992%29.svg/220px-Flag_of_the_CIS_%28UEFA_Euro_1992%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Flag_of_the_CIS_%28UEFA_Euro_1992%29.svg/330px-Flag_of_the_CIS_%28UEFA_Euro_1992%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Flag_of_the_CIS_%28UEFA_Euro_1992%29.svg/440px-Flag_of_the_CIS_%28UEFA_Euro_1992%29.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

Flag used by the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">CIS</a> team at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_1992" title="Euro 1992">Euro 1992</a>.</div>

<p>As the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> has formally ceased to exist on 1 January 1992, so did all its organizations including the <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Football Federation of the Soviet Union">football federation</a>. The Association of Football Federations of CIS was formed on 11 January 1992 and was approved by FIFA two days later. <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Beethoven</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._9_(Beethoven)" title="Symphony No. 9 (Beethoven)">Symphony No. 9</a> was adopted as its anthem. Along with the Association, national federations of its members started to form and apply for international recognition.</p>
<p>The CIS national football team, previously known as the <i>USSR national football team</i>, completed its participation in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1992_European_Football_Championship" title="1992 European Football Championship">Euro 1992</a> in June 1992. It was disbanded soon thereafter and all its results were transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia national football team</a> that played its first game in August 1992.</p>
<p>The CIS national football team was coached by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anatoly_Byshovets" title="Anatoly Byshovets">Anatoly Byshovets</a>. The team failed to achieve success in the 1992 European Football Championship, finishing last in the group, but achieved two notable draws with <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>, before being beaten 3–0 by <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> in what turned out to be their last match.</p>

<p>In total, the CIS squad contained eight Russians, six Ukrainians (one born in Germany), a Georgian, a Belarusian, an Abkhazian, a Circassian, and an Ossetian. Caps included games played for the Soviet team as well as the CIS. Some players simultaneously played for other national teams such as <a href="/wiki/Kakhaber_Tskhadadze" title="Kakhaber Tskhadadze">Kakhaber Tskhadadze</a> (Georgia) and <a href="/wiki/Akhrik_Tsveiba" title="Akhrik Tsveiba">Akhrik Tsveiba</a> (Ukraine).</p>
<p>Practically all the players (marked in <b>bold</b>) played later for the <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia national football team</a>, which qualified for the <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994 FIFA World Cup</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Due to the incident with the <i>Letter of fourteeners</i> in November 1993 (because of the team's poor performance), <a href="/wiki/Igor_Shalimov" title="Igor Shalimov">Igor Shalimov</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Igor_Dobrovolsky" title="Igor Dobrovolsky">Igor Dobrovolsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Igor_Kolyvanov" title="Igor Kolyvanov">Igor Kolyvanov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Kiriakov" title="Sergei Kiriakov">Sergei Kiriakov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vasili_Kulkov" title="Vasili Kulkov">Vasili Kulkov</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Andrei_Kanchelskis" title="Andrei Kanchelskis">Andrei Kanchelskis</a> were excluded from the national team. <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Salenko" title="Oleg Salenko">Oleg Salenko</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andrei_Ivanov_(footballer_born_1967)" title="Andrei Ivanov (footballer born 1967)">Andrei Ivanov</a>, who also signed the letter, eventually withdrew their signatures. Tsveiba and Chernyshov were later called to the <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia national football team</a>.</p>
<p>Although almost one third of the team were from Ukraine, only two Ukrainian players and an Abkhazian (Akhrik Tsveiba) ever played for the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine national football team</a>, while another four chose to play for the Russian national team.</p>
