<p>The <b>men's 200 metre backstroke</b> event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 13–15 August at the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_National_Aquatics_Center" title="Beijing National Aquatics Center">Beijing National Aquatics Center</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing,_China" title="Beijing, China">Beijing, China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swimming_schedule_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Swimming_schedule-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Billed as the <i>Clash of the Titans</i>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lochte" title="Ryan Lochte">Ryan Lochte</a> stormed home on the final lap to defeat his teammate <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Peirsol" title="Aaron Peirsol">Aaron Peirsol</a> and claim his first individual Olympic gold medal. He touched the wall first in 1:53.94, faster than the world record he shared with Peirsol at 1:54.32.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-200back_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-200back-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Peirsol enjoyed a head-to-head battle against Lochte in the first 150 metres, but ended up only with a silver in 1:54.33. Russia's <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Vyatchanin" title="Arkady Vyatchanin">Arkady Vyatchanin</a> added a second bronze to his collection, finishing in a new European record of 1:54.93 to hold off Austria's <a href="/wiki/Markus_Rogan" title="Markus Rogan">Markus Rogan</a> (1:55.49), the silver medalist in Athens four years earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-200back_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-200back-3">[3]</a></sup> Earlier in the semifinals, Vyatchanin edged out Germany's <a href="/wiki/Helge_Meeuw" title="Helge Meeuw">Helge Meeuw</a> in a swimoff to secure a last spot for the top 8 final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Japan's <a href="/wiki/Ryosuke_Irie" title="Ryosuke Irie">Ryosuke Irie</a> finished fifth with a time of 1:55.72, and was followed in the sixth spot by Australia's <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Stoeckel" title="Hayden Stoeckel">Hayden Stoeckel</a>, who shared bronze medals with Vyatchanin in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metre_backstroke" title="Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metre backstroke">100 m backstroke</a>, in an Oceanian record of 1:56.39. Romania's <a href="/wiki/R%C4%83zvan_Florea" title="Răzvan Florea">Răzvan Florea</a> (1:56.52) and Great Britain's <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Tait" title="Gregor Tait">Gregor Tait</a> (1:57.00) rounded out the finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-200back_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-200back-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
