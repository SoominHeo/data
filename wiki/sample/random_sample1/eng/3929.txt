<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Deutsches Sprachdiplom Stufe I and II</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Deutsches Sprachdiplom der Kultusministerkonferenz</span></b> (<i>engl.: German Language Certificate of the Education Ministers Conference</i>) is an initiative of the <a href="/wiki/Kultusministerkonferenz" title="Kultusministerkonferenz">German education authorities</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Office_(Germany)" title="Foreign Office (Germany)">Foreign Office (Germany)</a> for the improvement of the knowledge of the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German language</a> in schools worldwide. The program has run since 1973<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and is accessible for high school students at accredited schools only. The program prepares the participants inter alia for a study in Germany in matters of language and cultural issues. It finishes with an exam and a certification on language competencies on level A2/B1 or B2/C1 of the <a href="/wiki/Common_European_Framework_of_Reference_for_Languages" title="Common European Framework of Reference for Languages">Common European Framework of Reference for Languages</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acknowledgement_in_Germany">Acknowledgement in Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Acknowledgement in Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Level A2/B1 of the Deutsches Sprachdiplom der Kultusministerkonferenz (DSD I) is considered by the responsible German authorities as proof that the pupil has reached a level of ability in German that is required for entry to a <a href="/wiki/Studienkolleg" title="Studienkolleg">Studienkolleg</a> in preparation for attending a university in Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Level B2/C1 of the Deutsches Sprachdiplom der Kultusministerkonferenz (DSD II) is considered proof that the graduates have reached a language level in German that is required to be able to directly start a study at a university in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In order to start studying in Germany, students may need to fulfill additional requirements beyond language competencies which can be researched for each individual country in the official database ANABIN.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About_the_program">About the program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: About the program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Currently there are about 51.000 students in over 65 countries participating in the exams each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Most of the accredited schools for level A2/B1 are situated in Russia, Poland, the United States, China and Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Leading countries offering exams for level B2/C1 of the Deutsches Sprachdiplom der Kultusministerkonferenz are Russia, Poland, Romania, Croatia and Hungary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> All together about 950 schools are accredited<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> by the steering committee of the Deutsches Sprachdiplom. The steering committee 'Zentraler Ausschuss für das Deutsche Sprachdiplom' accounts for the quality of the language certificate. Its members are representatives of the German Foreign Office, the German Federal Office of Administration, the Central Agency for Schools Abroad and the States in Germany. The committee is headed by Mr. Thomas Mayer of Bavaria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The exams of the Deutsches Sprachdiplom der Kultusministerkonferenz are prepared by the German Federal Office of Administration the Central Agency for Schools Abroad (ZfA) in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> which also administers a network of advisors on German around the world.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About_the_exam">About the exam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: About the exam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Having learned German in school for several years, high school students can join either the exams on the level A2/B1 or on level B2/C1 depending on the accreditations of their school. All exams are free of charge, fixed dates for the exams are in December and March (<a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>) and in August (<a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a>). Each candidate must be registered by the school and needs approval by the German side. The student is only allowed to take part twice on each level. The test comprises listening comprehension, reading comprehension, writing skills, and oral communication of the candidate. The written parts of the test are done in classes and the oral part individually in front of a commission. All four competencies must be completed with a certain score in order to obtain the diploma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In order to offer exams, each school must be registered and accredited by the German institutions. Schools are permitted to register and offer the A2/B1-exams or the B2/C1-exams separately or both together. The German institutions offer support for each participating school consisting of inter alia personal support, training and taking over the chair of the examination committee by personnel from Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exam_DSD_I_.28A2.2FB1.29">Exam DSD I (A2/B1)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Exam DSD I (A2/B1)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reading_Part">Reading Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reading Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This section tests the candidates <a href="/wiki/Reading_comprehension" title="Reading comprehension">reading comprehension</a> by presenting several short (25-80 words) and long (100-300 words) pieces of writing followed by comprehension questions with simple filling (<a href="/wiki/Multiple_choice" title="Multiple choice">multiple choice</a>, true/false, <a href="/wiki/Check_mark" title="Check mark">ticking</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloze_text" title="Cloze text">cloze texts</a>). The reading part consists of five individual parts and lasts for 70 min (60 min working time + 10 min transfer time).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Listening_Part">Listening Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Listening Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In this section, the candidate is presented with several recordings and asked to fill out comprehension questions regarding the selections played (multiple choice, true/false, ticking). The five individual parts of the listening section include short and simple dialogues of ca. 60 words, as well as descriptive monologues of ca. 450 words, and lasts for 50 min (40 min working time + 10 min transfer time).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_Part">Writing Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Writing Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In this section, the candidate is asked to prove his writing skills by tackling three central questions and a sample text. The working time for this section is 75 min.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Speaking_Part">Speaking Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Speaking Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This section is divided in two parts and lasts for 15 min. The first part consists of a guided conversation where the candidate is prompted by the examiner, and the second part consists of a short monologue on a defined subject followed by answering questions on the presented subject.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exam_DSD_II_.28B2.2FC1.29">Exam DSD II (B2/C1)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Exam DSD II (B2/C1)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reading_Part_2">Reading Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reading Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This section tests the candidates reading comprehension by presenting five short (70-80 words) and three long (400-750 words) pieces of writing, followed by comprehension questions with simple filling (multiple choice, true/false, <a href="/wiki/Check_mark" title="Check mark">ticking</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloze_text" title="Cloze text">cloze texts</a>). The reading part consists of five individual parts and lasts for 85 min (75 min working time + 10 min transfer time).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Listening_Part_2">Listening Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Listening Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In this section, the candidate is presented with several recordings to test the listening comprehension and lasts for 50 min (40 min working time + 10 min transfer time). The three individual parts of the listening section include a dialogue of ca. 700 words, a monologue of ca. 700 words, and four audio sequences of ca. 100 words. The candidate is asked to fill out comprehension questions regarding the selections played (multiple choice, mapping).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_Part_2">Writing Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Writing Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The candidate is asked to write texts tackling central questions and sample texts. The working time for this section is 120 min. Before the writing exam, the candidate receives a single page with questions and another one with a text and a graph. The candidate should write an introduction (ca. 2 sentences), summarize the text, describe the graph, write advantages and disadvantages of the topic (for example top level sport) and present their own opinion on the topic.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Speaking_Part_2">Speaking Part</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Speaking Part">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This section is divided in two parts and lasts for 40 min (20 min preparation time + 20 min exam time). In the first part, the candidate is expected to deliver a monologue on given keywords and to answer relevant questions in a free and spontaneous fashion. The second part consists of a monologue delivered on a chosen subject, followed by discussion of the presented subject.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scoring">Scoring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_Sprachdiplom_Stufe_I_and_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Scoring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Each of the four test sections (reading, listening, writing, and speaking) accounts for 25% of the exam. Per tested section, it is possible to acquire a maximum of 24 points, and, depending on the acquired points in each section, the language proficiency level of said section is decided. The DSD II is presented to the candidate if he/she reached B2-level or higher in all tested sections. Scores above B2 are presented as C1-scores on the diploma. The DSD I is presented to the candidate if he/she reached in all tested sections B1-level or higher of language proficiency in German. Below DSD I, a DSD A2 is presented to the candidate if he/she reached at least or higher A2-level of language proficiency in German in all tested sections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
