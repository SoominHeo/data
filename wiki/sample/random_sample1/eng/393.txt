<p>The <b>women's 100 metre breaststroke</b> event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 10–12 August at the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_National_Aquatics_Center" title="Beijing National Aquatics Center">Beijing National Aquatics Center</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing,_China" title="Beijing, China">Beijing, China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swimming_schedule_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Swimming_schedule-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After winning a silver medal in Sydney and a bronze in Athens, Australia's <a href="/wiki/Leisel_Jones" title="Leisel Jones">Leisel Jones</a> stormed home on the final lap to claim an elusive gold in the event. She established an Olympic record of 1:05.17, just eight-hundredths of a second (0.08) off her global standard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100breast_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-100breast-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Coming from fifth place in the turn, U.S. swimmer <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Soni" title="Rebecca Soni">Rebecca Soni</a> earned a silver medal in 1:06.73.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Finishing fourth from the Olympic trials, she inherited a place in the event after <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Hardy" title="Jessica Hardy">Jessica Hardy</a>'s sudden withdrawal from the Games because of a doping irregularity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Mirna_Juki%C4%87" title="Mirna Jukić">Mirna Jukić</a> posted a time of 1:07.34 to settle for the bronze, holding off Russia's <a href="/wiki/Yuliya_Yefimova" title="Yuliya Yefimova">Yuliya Yefimova</a> (1:07.43) to fourth place by almost a tenth of a second (0.10).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100breast_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-100breast-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Megan_Jendrick" title="Megan Jendrick">Megan Jendrick</a>, former Olympic champion from Sydney in 2000, finished fifth with a time of 1:07.62, edging out Aussie <a href="/wiki/Tarnee_White" title="Tarnee White">Tarnee White</a> (1:07.63) in a close race by a hundredth of a second (0.01).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> China's <a href="/wiki/Sun_Ye" title="Sun Ye">Sun Ye</a> (1:08.08) and Japan's <a href="/wiki/Asami_Kitagawa" title="Asami Kitagawa">Asami Kitagawa</a> (1:08.43) rounded out the finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100breast_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-100breast-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Earlier in the prelims, Jones opened up her meet by breaking a new Olympic record of 1:05.64, exactly a full second faster than a winning time set by <a href="/wiki/Luo_Xuejuan" title="Luo Xuejuan">Luo Xuejuan</a> in Athens four years earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
