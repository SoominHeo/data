<p><i><b>Only for You</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">獨一無二</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">dú yī wú èr</span></i>) is <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Mandopop" title="Mandopop">Mandopop</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Show_Luo" title="Show Luo">Show Luo</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">羅志祥</span>) eighth Mandarin studio album. It was released on 18 February 2011 by <a href="/wiki/Gold_Typhoon" title="Gold Typhoon">Gold Typhoon (Taiwan)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The album was available for preorder from 28 January 2011 in two limited editions, of 30,000 copies each with serial numbers and photobook: <i>Only For You (Gentry Love Edition)</i> (獨一無二 紳情款款版) and <i>Only For You (Flower World Edition)</i> (獨一無二 花花仕界版). For the first time the first lead track is a ballad "拼什麼" (What Am I Fighting For?).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A further two editions were released by Gold Typhoon: <i>Only for You (10001st Night Celebration Edition)</i> (2CD) on 18 March 2011 with two new tracks and <i>Only for You (MV Collectable Edition)</i> (CD+DVD) on 6 May 2011 with nine music videos and photobook. The Japanese edition, <i>Only for You (Japanese Edition)</i> was released on 20 July 2011 by <a href="/wiki/Pony_Canyon" title="Pony Canyon">Pony Canyon</a>, which included a special bonus track of the Japanese version of title track "獨一無二" (Only You) and a DVD with 10 music videos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Album">Album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_for_You_(Show_Luo_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was marketed with the "拼什麼" (What Am I Fighting For) track, which was rather unconventional, as he often markets an album through a dance piece. The music video for the second lead track "獨一無二" (Only You) was released nine days later. The video consists of dance piece choreographed by <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Evaristo" title="Shaun Evaristo">Shaun Evaristo</a>. It was reported to have costed NT$5 million to create European scenery and to debut the "Anti-Gravity filming technique".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_for_You_(Show_Luo_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at number one on Taiwan's G-Music Top 20 Weekly Mandarin and Combo Charts, and Five Music Chart at week 8 (18 to 24 February 2011) with a percentage sales of 74.6%,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> 48.43%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and 78.8%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> respectively. Suppressing fellow artist <a href="/wiki/Jolin_Tsai" title="Jolin Tsai">Jolin Tsai</a>'s record at G-Music Mandarin Chart of 68.11% for <i><a href="/wiki/Butterfly_(Jolin_Tsai_album)" title="Butterfly (Jolin Tsai album)">Butterfly</a></i> in 2009 and became the highest debut week percentage sales in the chart's seven-year history. As of 3 March 2011 the album has sold 103,628 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tracks, "獨一無二" (Only You) and "拼什麼" (What Am I Fighting For) are listed on the Top 20 Gold Songs of the (January to June) 2011 Global Chinese Golden Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The album is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_albums_in_Taiwan" title="List of best-selling albums in Taiwan">best selling album in Taiwan</a> in 2011, with 150,000 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
