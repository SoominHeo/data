<p>The <b>women's 50 metre freestyle</b> event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 15–17 August at the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_National_Aquatics_Center" title="Beijing National Aquatics Center">Beijing National Aquatics Center</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing,_China" title="Beijing, China">Beijing, China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swimming_schedule_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Swimming_schedule-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Germany's <a href="/wiki/Britta_Steffen" title="Britta Steffen">Britta Steffen</a> blasted a new Olympic record to strike a sprint freestyle double. She posted a time of 24.06, the second-fastest ever in the event, to erase <a href="/wiki/Inge_de_Bruijn" title="Inge de Bruijn">Inge de Bruijn</a>'s 2000 record, and to hold off U.S. swimmer <a href="/wiki/Dara_Torres" title="Dara Torres">Dara Torres</a> in a close race by a hundredth of a second (0.01).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50free_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-50free-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Returning from an eight-year absence, Torres became the first woman in Olympic history to swim past the age of 40.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She established both a personal best and an American record of 24.07 to earn a silver medal and eleventh overall in her fifth Olympics since 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Meanwhile, Australian teenager <a href="/wiki/Cate_Campbell" title="Cate Campbell">Cate Campbell</a> picked up a bronze in 24.17, edging out her teammate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lisbeth_Trickett" title="Lisbeth Trickett">Lisbeth Trickett</a> (24.25) by 0.08 of a second.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Netherlands' <a href="/wiki/Marleen_Veldhuis" title="Marleen Veldhuis">Marleen Veldhuis</a> finished fifth with a time of 24.26, and was followed in the sixth spot by American <a href="/wiki/Kara_Lynn_Joyce" title="Kara Lynn Joyce">Kara Lynn Joyce</a> in 24.63. Veldhuis' teammate <a href="/wiki/Hinkelien_Schreuder" title="Hinkelien Schreuder">Hinkelien Schreuder</a> (24.65) and Belarus' <a href="/wiki/Aliaksandra_Herasimenia" title="Aliaksandra Herasimenia">Aliaksandra Herasimenia</a> (24.77) rounded out the finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50free_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-50free-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Notable swimmers missed out the top 8 final, featuring Sweden's <a href="/wiki/Therese_Alshammar" title="Therese Alshammar">Therese Alshammar</a>, four-time Olympian and silver medalist in Sydney eight years earlier, and Finland's <a href="/wiki/Hanna-Maria_Sepp%C3%A4l%C3%A4" title="Hanna-Maria Seppälä">Hanna-Maria Seppälä</a>, fourth-place finalist in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_100_metre_freestyle" title="Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's 100 metre freestyle">100 m freestyle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
