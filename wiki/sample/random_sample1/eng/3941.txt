<p><i><b>Germany in Autumn</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Deutschland im Herbst</i></span>) is a 1978 West German <a href="/wiki/Anthology_film" title="Anthology film">omnibus film</a> about the 1970s terrorist incidents known as <a href="/wiki/German_Autumn" title="German Autumn">German Autumn</a>. The film is composed of contributions from different filmmakers, including <a href="/wiki/Rainer_Werner_Fassbinder" title="Rainer Werner Fassbinder">Rainer Werner Fassbinder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Kluge" title="Alexander Kluge">Alexander Kluge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Reitz" title="Edgar Reitz">Edgar Reitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volker_Schl%C3%B6ndorff" title="Volker Schlöndorff">Volker Schlöndorff</a>. It was entered into the <a href="/wiki/28th_Berlin_International_Film_Festival" title="28th Berlin International Film Festival">28th Berlin International Film Festival</a>, where it won a Special Recognition award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berlinale_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-berlinale-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
