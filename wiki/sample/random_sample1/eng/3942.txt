<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of German presidents</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>A number of presidential offices have existed in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> since <a href="/wiki/German_Revolution_of_1918%E2%80%9319" title="German Revolution of 1918–19">the collapse</a> of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> in 1918.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Weimar_Republic" title="Weimar Republic">Weimar</a> <a href="/wiki/Weimar_Constitution" title="Weimar Constitution">constitution</a> of August 1919 created the office of <a href="/wiki/President_of_Germany_(1919%E2%80%9345)" title="President of Germany (1919–45)">President of Germany</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Reichspräsident</i></span>). Upon the death of <a href="/wiki/Paul_von_Hindenburg" title="Paul von Hindenburg">Paul von Hindenburg</a> in August 1934 the office was left vacant, with <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> becoming head of state as <i><a href="/wiki/F%C3%BChrer" title="Führer">Führer und Reichskanzler</a></i>. In April–May 1945, <a href="/wiki/Karl_D%C3%B6nitz" title="Karl Dönitz">Karl Dönitz</a> briefly became President upon the <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Adolf_Hitler" title="Death of Adolf Hitler">suicide of Hitler</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West German</a> <a href="/wiki/Basic_Law_for_the_Federal_Republic_of_Germany" title="Basic Law for the Federal Republic of Germany">constitution</a> (<i>Grundgesetz</i>, Basic Law) of May 1949 created the office of <a href="/wiki/President_of_Germany" title="President of Germany">President of the Federal Republic of Germany</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Bundespräsident</i></span>). Since the <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a> in 1990 the President has been the head of state for all of Germany.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East German</a> <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_East_Germany#1949_constitution" title="Constitution of East Germany">constitution of October 1949</a> created the office of <i>Präsident der Deutschen Demokratischen Republik</i> (DDR) ("President of the German Democratic Republic"). Upon the death of <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Pieck" title="Wilhelm Pieck">Wilhelm Pieck</a> in 1960, the office of <i>Präsident der DDR</i> was abolished and replaced by a collective head of state, the <i><a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_East_Germany" title="State Council of East Germany">Staatsrat</a></i> ("State Council"). The <i>Staatsrat</i> was abolished by a constitutional amendment of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Chamber" title="People's Chamber">Volkskammer</a></i> ("People's Chamber") on 5 April 1990. From then until the GDR joined the Federal Republic on 3 October 1990, the president of the <i>Volkskammer</i> also served as the GDR head of state.</p>
