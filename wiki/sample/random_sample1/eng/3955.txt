<p><i><b>Donkey Punch</b></i> is a 2008 British <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror</a> <a href="/wiki/Thriller_film" title="Thriller film">thriller film</a> about a group of English people on holiday in Spain who end up fighting for their lives. The film was written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Olly_Blackburn" title="Olly Blackburn">Oliver Blackburn</a> (as Olly Blackburn) and David Bloom, and stars <a href="/wiki/Nichola_Burley" title="Nichola Burley">Nichola Burley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sian_Breckin" title="Sian Breckin">Sian Breckin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Burke_(actor)" title="Tom Burke (actor)">Tom Burke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Winstone" title="Jaime Winstone">Jaime Winstone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julian_Morris" title="Julian Morris">Julian Morris</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donkey_Punch_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While on a holiday in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mallorca" title="Mallorca">Mallorca</a>, Lisa (<a href="/wiki/Sian_Breckin" title="Sian Breckin">Sian Breckin</a>), Kim (<a href="/wiki/Jaime_Winstone" title="Jaime Winstone">Jaime Winstone</a>) and Tammi (<a href="/wiki/Nichola_Burley" title="Nichola Burley">Nichola Burley</a>) meet four young men, Bluey (Tom Burke), Josh (<a href="/wiki/Julian_Morris" title="Julian Morris">Julian Morris</a>), Sean (<a href="/wiki/Robert_Boulter" title="Robert Boulter">Robert Boulter</a>) and Marcus (Jay Taylor). After spending the day at the resort together, the girls are invited to the men's yacht where they plan to party out at sea. While aboard the boat, they take drugs and the conversation turns to sex, and in particular, types of sexual acts. Bluey describes a sex act called a "<a href="/wiki/Donkey_punch" title="Donkey punch">donkey punch</a>" which involves punching the female in the back of the head while having <a href="/wiki/Doggy_style" title="Doggy style">doggy style sex</a> in order to increase the sexual pleasure for the man.</p>
<p>Marcus, Bluey, Kim and Lisa go to the master bedrooms where they begin having drug-fuelled sex, watched by Josh who, in the knowledge of all involved, lingers furtively in the darkness, recording the action with a camera. Bluey, who is copulating with Lisa, asks Josh to film the action and then decides to let Josh take a turn. Josh and Lisa then have <a href="/wiki/Anal_sex" title="Anal sex">anal sex</a>. Immediately prior to ejaculation and with Bluey's encouragement, Josh donkey punches Lisa but uses excessive force, breaking her neck and killing her instantly. To cover up the incident, the men decide to throw the body overboard while the women want to report it to the authorities, and argument ensues about what to do with the tape. Bluey continually insults Tammi and in a fit of rage, she stabs him in the chest with a penknife, and the women escape in the yacht's <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_tender" title="Ship's tender">tender</a>. However, the girls soon realise that the tender's outboard motor is missing (a cut scene shows it still attached to the yacht). In a fit of despair, Tammi fires a flare, attracting the attention of the men. They quickly locate and pick up the women.</p>
<p>As the men attempt to get the women aboard, threatening them with a shotgun, Kim shoots a second flare directly at Marcus. The flare explodes into Marcus's torso and slowly burns him to death. Josh locks the recaptured women in one of the rooms below. Sean asks Josh to "call in" and request medical assistance for Bluey. However, knowing that Bluey still has the tape that contains footage of him dealing the earlier fatal blow, Josh instead decides to discover its whereabouts by torturing Bluey. He does this by withholding pain numbing drugs from him. Bluey reveals the location of the tape, beneath the bed in the state room. Tammi escapes the room by smashing through the glass door and cutting herself, and overhears Bluey mention where the tape is. She frantically roams the boat trying to locate it. She does this just moments before Josh attempts to retrieve it. Unable to find the tape, Josh returns to Bluey, stepping up the torture by turning his attention to the knife which still protrudes from the wound. Josh ultimately takes this too far and, following Bluey's pained protestations that he has already revealed the location, he twists the knife further into the wound before pulling it out, causing Bluey to die.</p>
<p>Sean tries to bring the situation under control by retrieving a shotgun from Josh. He then tries to calm both Josh and Tammi from a frantic argument about the whereabouts of the tape. Kim notices Sean holding a shotgun with Josh and Tammi visibly upset from an adjacent room. She misinterprets his intentions towards Tammi and Josh and brutally kills Sean with the propeller of an <a href="/wiki/Outboard_motor" title="Outboard motor">outboard motor</a>. After realizing her mistake, she manically commits suicide by jumping overboard, leaving Josh and Tammi as the only ones left. A distraught Tammi decides that she cannot remain on the boat any longer. Josh agrees, and readies the tender. His plan is to leave the yacht, get back to shore and claim that there was an accident. As Josh pushes the tender away from the yacht, Tammi quietly takes hold of the end of the <a href="/wiki/Mooring_(watercraft)" title="Mooring (watercraft)">mooring rope</a> at the stern.</p>
<p>Once the tender has floated some distance from the yacht, Josh pulls his hunting knife from his shorts and points it at Tammi. He demands the incriminating tape from Tammi and, fearing for her life, she obliges, throwing it on the floor of the tender. As a distracted Josh reaches for the tape, Tammi quickly throws the looped end of the mooring rope around his neck. The rope immediately reaches the end of its tether and Josh is wrenched into the sea, snapping his neck and killing him. Tammi, now the sole survivor, fires a distress flare in the hope of being rescued. She then lies down on the tender and morbidly stares up at the night sky, as the raft drifts away into the ocean.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donkey_Punch_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blackburn commented that films including those by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Love" title="Nick Love">Nick Love</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Descent" title="The Descent">The Descent</a></i>, helped pave the way for <i>Donkey Punch</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup> He stated he was inspired by <a href="/wiki/Sam_Peckinpah" title="Sam Peckinpah">Sam Peckinpah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Polanski" title="Roman Polanski">Roman Polanski</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Straw_Dogs_(1971_film)" title="Straw Dogs (1971 film)">Straw Dogs</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rosemary%27s_Baby_(film)" title="Rosemary's Baby (film)">Rosemary's Baby</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Romero" title="George Romero">George Romero</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dawn_of_the_Dead_(1978_film)" title="Dawn of the Dead (1978 film)">Dawn of the Dead</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/John_Carpenter" title="John Carpenter">John Carpenter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer" title="The Philadelphia Inquirer">The Philadelphia Inquirer</a></i> compared Blackburn’s work to films including filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Peter_Berg" title="Peter Berg">Peter Berg</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/Very_Bad_Things" title="Very Bad Things">Very Bad Things</a></i> and director <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Noyce" title="Phillip Noyce">Phillip Noyce</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Calm_(film)" title="Dead Calm (film)">Dead Calm</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stevenrea_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-stevenrea-5">[5]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> additionally compared Blackburn’s work to <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Calm_(film)" title="Dead Calm (film)">Dead Calm</a></i> as well as director <a href="/wiki/Roman_Polanski" title="Roman Polanski">Roman Polanski</a>’s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knife_in_the_Water_(film)" title="Knife in the Water (film)">Knife in the Water</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-robertabele_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-robertabele-6">[6]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> observed that the movie appeared similar to <i>Knife In The Water</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Calm_(film)" title="Dead Calm (film)">Dead Calm</a></i>, but with actors similar to those from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Real_World" title="The Real World">The Real World</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scotttobias_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-scotttobias-7">[7]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_San_Diego_Union-Tribune" title="The San Diego Union-Tribune">The San Diego Union-Tribune</a></i> characterized it as a <a href="/wiki/Slasher_film" title="Slasher film">slasher film</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rex_Reed" title="Rex Reed">Rex Reed</a> of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_York_Observer" title="The New York Observer">The New York Observer</a></i> likened the plot of the film to <i><a href="/wiki/And_Then_There_Were_None" title="And Then There Were None">And Then There Were None</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Agatha_Christie" title="Agatha Christie">Agatha Christie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rexreed_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-rexreed-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donkey_Punch_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Olly_Blackburn" title="Olly Blackburn">Olly Blackburn</a> directed <i>Donkey Punch</i>, which he co-wrote with David Bloom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maher_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-maher-10">[10]</a></sup> He explained that he was in the offices of production company <a href="/wiki/Warp_X" title="Warp X">Warp X</a> discussing television options with company co-founder Robin Gutch when he first thought to make a movie about the topic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup> He and David Bloom discussed the idea and concept with Warp X management and successfully convinced them to go ahead with the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>His total budget for the film was <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washingtoncitypaperinterview_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-washingtoncitypaperinterview-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eyeforfilminterview_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-eyeforfilminterview-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Film" title="Total Film">Total Film</a></i>, Blackburn commented, "I think <i>Donkey Punch</i> is an extreme thriller or an extreme reality-based thriller. The whole point of the film is it’s grounded in reality."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totalfilm_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-totalfilm-11">[11]</a></sup> Blackburn wrote that he made <i>Donkey Punch</i>, "to try to push the genre."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackburnroeg_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-blackburnroeg-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donkey_Punch_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blackburn's production team went through a casting process which took seven months;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totalfilm_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-totalfilm-11">[11]</a></sup> the film stars <a href="/wiki/Nichola_Burley" title="Nichola Burley">Nichola Burley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Burke_(actor)" title="Tom Burke (actor)">Tom Burke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Winstone" title="Jaime Winstone">Jaime Winstone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julian_Morris" title="Julian Morris">Julian Morris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-donkeypunch_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-donkeypunch-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Donkey Punch</i> was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenlit" title="Greenlit">greenlit</a> by Warp X in February 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup> Blackburn chose to look at filming locations in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a> owing to greater availability of sunlight that time of year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Shot_(filmmaking)" title="Shot (filmmaking)">Film shooting</a> for <i>Donkey Punch</i> began in March 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edinburghfestival_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-edinburghfestival-15">[15]</a></sup> Blackburn shot the film in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totalfilm_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-totalfilm-11">[11]</a></sup> over 24 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmmakermagazine_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmmakermagazine-16">[16]</a></sup> Blackburn decided upon South Africa because of its cost benefit, its support for the movie production, and the availability of skilled film technicians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cinematography" title="Cinematography">Cinematography</a> was handled by Nanu Segal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamesverniere_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-jamesverniere-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>During production Blackburn had to deal with actors afflicted by <a href="/wiki/Hypothermia" title="Hypothermia">hypothermia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal_surge" title="Tidal surge">tidal surges</a> on location.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackburnroeg_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-blackburnroeg-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indielondoninterview_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-indielondoninterview-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Donkey Punch</i> was given an <a href="/wiki/Motion_Picture_Association_of_America_film_rating_system" title="Motion Picture Association of America film rating system">R rating</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Motion_Picture_Association_of_America" title="Motion Picture Association of America">Motion Picture Association of America</a> (MPAA) for "a scene of strong sexual content" described as "an aberrant violent act".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tedfry_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-tedfry-19">[19]</a></sup> A different version of the film was unrated by the MPAA; no one under the age of 18 was allowed in by theaters to see this version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-robertabele_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-robertabele-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tedfry_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-tedfry-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donkey_Punch_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Donkey Punch</i> had its world premiere at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It was featured among the non-competition titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The film was Blackburn’s first work to be shown at the Sundance Film Festival,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloodydisgustinginterview_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-bloodydisgustinginterview-22">[22]</a></sup> where it received a positive reception from the audience in attendance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indielondoninterview_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-indielondoninterview-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was subsequently shown at film festivals in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmfeast_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmfeast-24">[24]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_American-Statesman" title="Austin American-Statesman">Austin American-Statesman</a></i> recommended it among the "10 Must-See Movies" at <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Fest" title="Fantastic Fest">Fantastic Fest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmfeast_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmfeast-24">[24]</a></sup> It was promoted at Fantastic Fest with a "'Donkey Punch' Boat Party" in honor of the film featuring a screening followed by festivities aboard a ship at <a href="/wiki/Lady_Bird_Lake" title="Lady Bird Lake">Lady Bird Lake</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmfeast_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-filmfeast-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Donkey Punch</i> was featured in 2008 at the 62nd <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_International_Film_Festival" title="Edinburgh International Film Festival">Edinburgh International Film Festival</a>, where it competed for the Michael Powell Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It was a featured selection at the <a href="/wiki/Sitges_Film_Festival" title="Sitges Film Festival">Sitges Film Festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_International_Film_Festival" title="Melbourne International Film Festival">Melbourne International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Donkey Punch</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> on 10 November 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-michaelgillespie_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-michaelgillespie-4">[4]</a></sup> It was shown on British TV on the Film 4 channel on 14 December 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donkey_Punch_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reported a 49% approval rating with an average rating of 5.5/10 based on 51 reviews. The website's consensus reads, "<i>Donkey Punch</i> begins as a promising thriller, but loses credibility while adhering too closely to genre conventions."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> It received a score of 43 out of 100 at <i><a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> described it as "expertly made and painfully tense; a bracing cold snap blowing in off the ocean."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xanbrooks_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-xanbrooks-31">[31]</a></sup> The film failed to stir up much controversy in the British press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Platell" title="Amanda Platell">Amanda Platell</a> described the film, in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Daily_Mail" title="The Daily Mail">The Daily Mail</a></i>, as "a morally bankrupt tale of teenage group sex, violence, drugs and sadism which left me sickened to the core" concluding that it was "quite simply, the most distasteful, depraved and nihilistic film I have ever had the misfortune to sit through. I freely confess that there were times I felt physically ill simply watching it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-platell_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-platell-33">[33]</a></sup> Elsewhere in the British press <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> called the film "a notable achievement in all departments, handled superbly by its first-time director and largely unknown cast, with a tight, frighteningly plausible script. ... Donkey Punch has a respect for formula but, more importantly, a commitment to doing things differently, and properly. A scary, truly edgy film."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-damonwise_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-damonwise-34">[34]</a></sup> Writing in <i>The Daily Mail</i> Baz Bamigboye said "It's a violent, cracking shocker. In fact, I was so stunned by several scenes that I sat frozen in my seat."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bamigboye_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-bamigboye-35">[35]</a></sup> Jason Solomons wrote in <i>The Mail on Sunday</i> "A stylish mix of <i>Ibiza Uncovered</i>, <i>Lord of the Flies</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Calm_(film)" title="Dead Calm (film)">Dead Calm</a></i> ... I liked Blackburn’s energy and tension and was taken by the ensemble performances – the film has an eye on cult audiences and late night thrills, all of which it delivers with unsavory relish."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jasonsolomon_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-jasonsolomon-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stephen_Holden" title="Stephen Holden">Stephen Holden</a>'s review in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> said "There is no transcendent agent of evil; the enemy is within ... the movie remains above the typical splatter ’n’ scream fest. These careless hedonists are convincing, and the ensemble acting feels believable."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stephenholden_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-stephenholden-37">[37]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aintitcool" title="Aintitcool">Aintitcool</a>'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Harry_Knowles" title="Harry Knowles">Harry Knowles</a> said "Damn I fucking loved it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harryknowles_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-harryknowles-38">[38]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rex_Reed" title="Rex Reed">Rex Reed</a> wrote a review of <i>Donkey Punch</i> for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_York_Observer" title="The New York Observer">The New York Observer</a></i> and called it a "slaughterfest" and "the newest footnote in the history of pretty-girls-in-peril pictures".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rexreed_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-rexreed-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Morris" title="Wesley Morris">Wesley Morris</a> reviewed the film for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a></i>, and commented, "The naughtiness turns nasty, the nastiness turns fatal, and the kids gradually turn against each other. ... And the alive-to-dead pecking order is strictly a horror film convention: The soberer and more virginal you are the better your chances."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wesleymorris_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-wesleymorris-39">[39]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lou_Lumenick" title="Lou Lumenick">Lou Lumenick</a> gave the film two stars in a review for the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> and observed, "You could call <i>Donkey Punch,</i> ... a female revenge movie. But you could just as easily characterize it as fairly well-executed exploitation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loulumenick_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-loulumenick-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Writing in the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Press" title="New York Press">New York Press</a></i>, Eric Kohn said "Such a unique thrill. Director Blackburn develops the scenario with remarkably shrewd finesse and builds to a satisfying spectacle."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erickohn_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-erickohn-41">[41]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i> called it "A Knife in the Water for the era of Ugly Briton, package tour imperialism."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nickpinkerton_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-nickpinkerton-42">[42]</a></sup> In a review which graded <i>Donkey Punch</i> a "B", the <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Herald" title="Boston Herald">Boston Herald</a></i> called the film "dirty, nasty fun", while criticising its lack of originality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamesverniere_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-jamesverniere-17">[17]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer" title="The Philadelphia Inquirer">The Philadelphia Inquirer</a></i> wrote, "<i>Donkey Punch</i> offers a gripping mix of sexual heat and nasty menace."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stevenrea_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-stevenrea-5">[5]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> concluded, <i>Donkey Punch</i> isn't without a certain power as it gleefully turns its careless hedonists into caged, paranoid rats."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-robertabele_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-robertabele-6">[6]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> gave the film a grade of C+ and wrote that the director, "sustains a tense mood of mistrust, sexual rivalry, and shifting alliances", but lamented that "the film goes off the rails in the final third, sacrificing subtle character work at the altar of blood-and-guts survival horror. As mood-killers go, it's like a jab to the back of the neck."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scotttobias_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-scotttobias-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Weekly" title="Philadelphia Weekly">Philadelphia Weekly</a></i> review called the film "an odious little potboiler".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Beach_Press-Telegram" title="Long Beach Press-Telegram">Long Beach Press-Telegram</a></i> called the film a predictable thriller.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> A review in <i><a href="/wiki/The_San_Diego_Union-Tribune" title="The San Diego Union-Tribune">The San Diego Union-Tribune</a></i> concluded, "What began as a fairly interesting girls-gone-wild picture becomes just another gorefest with lingering close-ups of wounds and knives being extracted."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Though the <i><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a></i> called <i>Donkey Punch</i> an "interesting but flawed indie thriller", it determined the final act was "annoyingly routine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Contra_Costa_Times" title="Contra Costa Times">Contra Costa Times</a></i> gave the film a grade of "C" and described it as unoriginal suspense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seattle_Times" title="The Seattle Times">The Seattle Times</a></i> called <i>Donkey Punch</i> a "lurid thriller", and was critical of its plot progression towards the middle of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tedfry_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-tedfry-19">[19]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Kitsap_Sun" title="Kitsap Sun">Kitsap Sun</a></i> described the film as "a nasty, dirty little movie".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kitsapsun_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-kitsapsun-48">[48]</a></sup> Though lamenting the predictability of the film's plot, the review offered an instruction for potential viewers, "Prediction isn’t the point, because you know the final score’s going to be 1. The point is to just sit back, if you’re so disposed and not easily offended, and gleefully watch all the nasty, dirty little business go down."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kitsapsun_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-kitsapsun-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
