<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Sturmscharführer</i></h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><i><b>SS-Sturmscharführer</b></i> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranks_and_insignia_of_the_Schutzstaffel" title="Ranks and insignia of the Schutzstaffel">Nazi rank</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Waffen-SS" title="Waffen-SS">Waffen-SS</a></i> that existed between 1934 and 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELumsden2000109_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELumsden2000109-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcNab200930_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcNab200930-2">[2]</a></sup> The rank was the most senior enlisted rank in the <i>Waffen-SS</i>, the equivalent of a <a href="/wiki/Regimental_sergeant_major" title="Regimental sergeant major">regimental sergeant major</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFlaherty2004148_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFlaherty2004148-3">[3]</a></sup> in other military organizations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rank_usage">Rank usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sturmscharf%C3%BChrer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rank usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sturmscharführer</i> as a proper rank was unique to the <i>Waffen-SS</i> and was not used in the general-SS (the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allgemeine-SS" title="Allgemeine-SS">Allgemeine-SS</a></i>), where the highest enlisted rank was <i><a href="/wiki/Hauptscharf%C3%BChrer" title="Hauptscharführer">Hauptscharführer</a></i>. Members of the <i><a href="/wiki/Sicherheitspolizei" title="Sicherheitspolizei">Sicherheitspolizei</a></i>, when wearing the rank insignia of <i>Sturmscharführer</i> with police collar piping, did so when holding the equivalent police rank of <i>Meister</i> or the investigator's title of <i>Kriminalsekretär</i>. Administratively, such secret police members were not even required to be members of the SS (although many were) and thus the insignia was sometimes worn by non-SS members. Due to SS rank parity regulations, by 1943 most police members were required to hold SS rank, and those with police equivalent rank to <i>Sturmscharführer</i> were often administratively enlisted into the <i>Waffen-SS</i> in order to be ranked as a <i>Sturmscharführer</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sturmscharf%C3%BChrer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The rank of <i>Sturmscharführer</i> was first created in June 1934, after the <a href="/wiki/Night_of_the_Long_Knives" title="Night of the Long Knives">Night of the Long Knives</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcNab200929.2C_30_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcNab200929.2C_30-4">[4]</a></sup> Due to a reorganization of the <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">SS</a>, <i>Sturmscharführer</i> was created as the most senior enlisted rank of the <i><a href="/wiki/SS-Verf%C3%BCgungstruppe" title="SS-Verfügungstruppe">SS-Verfügungstruppe</a></i>, replacing the older <i><a href="/wiki/Sturmabteilung" title="Sturmabteilung">Sturmabteilung</a></i> (SA) title of <i><a href="/wiki/Haupttruppf%C3%BChrer" title="Haupttruppführer">Haupttruppführer</a></i>.</p>
<p>By 1941, the <i>Waffen-SS</i> had become the successor organization to the <i>SS-Verfügungstruppe</i> and <i>Sturmscharführer</i> was established as the most senior enlisted rank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELumsden2000109_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELumsden2000109-1">[1]</a></sup> A <i>Sturmscharführer</i> was typically assigned as the head sergeant of an entire regiment or, in some cases, an infantry division.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Insignia">Insignia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sturmscharf%C3%BChrer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Insignia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sturmscharführer</i> was not the same as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stabsscharf%C3%BChrer" title="Stabsscharführer">Stabsscharführer</a></i>, which was a positional title given to the head SS <a href="/wiki/Non-commissioned_officer" title="Non-commissioned officer">non-commissioned officer</a> of a company. The rank of <i>Sturmscharführer</i> was also not a prerequisite for promotion to <i><a href="/wiki/Untersturmf%C3%BChrer" title="Untersturmführer">Untersturmführer</a></i> and was generally considered as a rank for “career” enlisted SS soldiers, rather than a rank on the path to becoming an officer.</p>
<p>The insignia for <i>Sturmscharführer</i> was two silver pips and two silver stripes worn on a collar patch along with the shoulder boards of a <i><a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a> <a href="/wiki/Feldwebel" title="Feldwebel">Stabsfeldwebel</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFlaherty2004148_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFlaherty2004148-3">[3]</a></sup> As was the case with <i>Waffen-SS</i> enlisted insignia, the collar patch of a <i>Sturmscharführer</i> was edged with black-and-silver twist cord until 1940 when the edging was dropped; like all non-commissioned officers the collar was bordered with 9mm silver-grey braid.</p>
