<p><i><b>Oplegnathus</b></i> is currently the sole recognized genus in the <b>knifejaw</b> family (Oplegnathidae) of marine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perciform" title="Perciform">perciform</a> <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fishes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The largest, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_knifejaw&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cape knifejaw (page does not exist)">Cape knifejaw</a>, can reach a maximum length of about 90 cm (35 in). Knifejaws have teeth fused into a parrot-like beak in adulthood. They feed on barnacles and mollusks, and are <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">fished commercially</a>. They are native to the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> Oceans.</p>
