<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Dolsan</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Dolsan-eup</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">돌산읍</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">突山邑</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Dolsan-eup</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/McCune%E2%80%93Reischauer" title="McCune–Reischauer">MR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">tolsan-ŭp</span></i>) or <b>Dolsan town</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Eup_(administrative_division)" title="Eup (administrative division)">town</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Yeosu" title="Yeosu">Yeosu city</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. It is located on the <a href="/wiki/Dolsando" title="Dolsando">Dolsan island</a>. Yeosu city hall Dolsan office (3rd city hall) is in here.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traffic">Traffic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dolsan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Traffic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dolsan town has no <a href="/wiki/Expressways_in_South_Korea" title="Expressways in South Korea">expressways</a> and 2 <a href="/wiki/National_highways_of_South_Korea" title="National highways of South Korea">National highways</a>. That 2 national highways are <a href="/wiki/National_Route_17_(South_Korea)" title="National Route 17 (South Korea)">No. 17</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_Route_77_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National Route 77 (South Korea) (page does not exist)">No. 77</a>.</p>
<p>It has 3 bridges — <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dolsan_bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dolsan bridge (page does not exist)">Dolsan bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geobukseon_bridge" title="Geobukseon bridge">Geobukseon bridge</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hwatae_bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hwatae bridge (page does not exist)">Hwatae bridge</a>. Dolsan bridge is on <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_Route_77_(South_Korea)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National Route 77 (South Korea) (page does not exist)">national route 77</a>. It connect with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yeosu_peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yeosu peninsula (page does not exist)">Yeosu peninsula</a>. Geobukseon bridge is on <a href="/wiki/National_Route_17_(South_Korea)" title="National Route 17 (South Korea)">national route 17</a>. It connect with Yeosu peninsula, too. Hwatae bridge is on national route 77. It connect with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hwataedo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hwataedo (page does not exist)">Hwatae island</a>.</p>
