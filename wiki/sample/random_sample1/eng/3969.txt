<p>The <b>Battle of Domstadtl</b>, also spelled Domstadt, <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a> Doma≈°ov, was a <a href="/wiki/Battle" title="Battle">battle</a> between <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a> at a Moravian village <a href="/wiki/Doma%C5%A1ov_nad_Byst%C5%99ic%C3%AD" title="Doma≈°ov nad Byst≈ôic√≠">Doma≈°ov nad Byst≈ôic√≠</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">Seven Years' War</a> on 30 June 1758, preceded by a minor clash at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guntramovice&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guntramovice (page does not exist)">Guntramovice</a> (Gundersdorf) on 28 June. Austrians under the command of <a href="/wiki/General_(Germany)" title="General (Germany)">Major General</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ernst_Gideon_Freiherr_von_Laudon" title="Ernst Gideon Freiherr von Laudon">Ernst Gideon von Laudon</a> and Major General Joseph von Siskovits attacked and destroyed a supply <a href="/wiki/Convoy" title="Convoy">convoy</a> bound for the Prussian army <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Olomouc" title="Siege of Olomouc">besieging Olomouc</a> (Olm√ºtz). The Austrian victory saved the city and the Prussian King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_the_Great" title="Frederick the Great">Frederick the Great</a> was forced to leave <a href="/wiki/Moravia" title="Moravia">Moravia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prussian_invasion_to_Moravia">Prussian invasion to Moravia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Domstadtl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Prussian invasion to Moravia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frederick the Great invaded Moravia in the beginning of May 1758 and besieged the <a href="/wiki/Fortification" title="Fortification">fortified city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Olomouc" title="Olomouc">Olomouc</a>. He hoped that the Austrian army would come to help the fortress and the Prussians would defeat them in a big battle at the place of their choice. If the Austrian army did not come, he could conquer the fortress in a short time and use it as a base for defending <a href="/wiki/Silesia" title="Silesia">Silesia</a> and increasing pressure on <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>.</p>
<p>Austrian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Field_Marshal" title="Field Marshal">Field Marshal</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun" title="Leopold Josef Graf Daun">Leopold Joseph von Daun</a> knew the strength of the Prussian army and therefore avoided a decisive clash. Instead the Austrians concentrated on attacking the Prussian supply lines and causing them damage in minor skirmishes. The defenders of the Olomouc fortress also struggled bravely and held much longer than Frederick had expected. Although by June it was on the edge of being taken, with the <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">defensive walls</a> destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Cannon" title="Cannon">cannons</a> in two places, the Prussians desperately needed new supplies to be able to continue the fight.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_convoy">The convoy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Domstadtl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The convoy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frederick was afraid that many separate, small convoys protected by small forces could be easily captured by the Austrians and he therefore decided that one huge convoy guarded by a large, strong force should be sent. The supplies for the convoy were collected in <a href="/wiki/Silesia" title="Silesia">Silesia</a> and at the end of June it arrived in the territory of Moravia.</p>
<p>The convoy was so big that there was no chance it could be kept in secret. It contained about 4,000 <a href="/wiki/Wagon" title="Wagon">wagons</a> loaded with military <a href="/wiki/Materiel" title="Materiel">materiel</a> and accompanied by about 2,500 head of cattle. As it set out on its way, it stretched 45¬†km long.</p>
<p>The convoy was protected by 10,870 <a href="/wiki/Soldier" title="Soldier">soldiers</a>, commanded by <a href="/wiki/Oberst" title="Oberst">Colonel</a> Wilhelm von Mosel. The strongest part was probably <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">cavalry</a> consisting of 1,341 men; further he had eight <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> <a href="/wiki/Battalion" title="Battalion">battalions</a> created from new recruits or healed veterans.</p>
<p>As soon as Field Marshal Daun learned about the convoy, he decided it had to be stopped and destroyed. The task was given to <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Gideon_von_Laudon" title="Ernst Gideon von Laudon">Ernst Gideon von Laudon</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_von_Siskovits&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joseph von Siskovits (page does not exist)">Joseph von Siskovits</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_battles">The battles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Domstadtl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The battles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guntramovice">Guntramovice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Domstadtl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Guntramovice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Laudon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="361" data-file-width="302" height="263" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Laudon.jpg/220px-Laudon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/18/Laudon.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Laudon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Ernst Gideon von Laudon. The battles of Guntramovice and Doma≈°ov were his first big successes. Later he was promoted field marshal.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Laudon awaited the enemy at Guntramovice, a small village in northern Moravia. Siskovits, who had lost his way in woods, was supposed to arrive in two days. This was quite a problem for Laudon, because his four infantry battalions, a <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiment</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dragoon" title="Dragoon">dragoons</a>, a regiment of <a href="/wiki/Hussar" title="Hussar">hussars</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Artillery_battery" title="Artillery battery">artillery battery</a> and a troop of frontier guards had only about 6,000 men. Despite that he decided to attack the Prussians because Olomouc was near and there was no time for waiting. He also knew that five battalions of 20,000 Prussian soldiers commanded by <a href="/wiki/General_(Germany)" title="General (Germany)">Lieutenant-General</a> <a href="/wiki/Hans_Joachim_von_Zieten" title="Hans Joachim von Zieten">Hans Joachim von Zieten</a> were rushing towards the convoy to help Mosel.</p>
<p>The convoy arrived on 28 June early in the morning. The Austrians started shooting at the front wagons. One Prussian battalion forged ahead in order to find out the strength of the enemy, but they were routed by the Austrian artillery. The Prussians also formed artillery batteries on their side of the road and started shooting. They tried to attack the Austrian positions in the hilly terrain several times, but they were always forced back. The fight took about five hours but finally the Prussians started to dominate the battleground and Laudon ordered his men to retreat towards <a href="/wiki/Moravsk%C3%BD_Beroun" title="Moravsk√Ω Beroun">Moravsk√Ω Beroun</a>, which they did without any problems, because Mosel did not have enough cavalry to chase them.</p>
<p>Although Laudon retreated and did not manage to destroy the convoy, his losses were much smaller than the Prussian ones. However, the most precious thing that he gained was time. Nowadays some <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">historians</a> point out that the Prussians had a chance to succeed if they had sacrificed some of the dispersed wagons and immediately rushed to Olomouc. However, neither Mosel nor Zieten, who reached the convoy several hours after the clash, knew about Siskovits' approaching forces, and therefore they decided to devote some time to rearranging the convoy and repairing some damage. They continued on 30 June early in the morning.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doma.C5.A1ov">Doma≈°ov</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Domstadtl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Doma≈°ov">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Meanwhile, the Austrians prepared for a new attack. They chose an open place between Doma≈°ov nad Byst≈ôic√≠ and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nov%C3%A1_V%C3%A9ska&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nov√° V√©ska (page does not exist)">Nov√° V√©ska</a>, which was surrounded by hills and woods, ideal for an <a href="/wiki/Ambush" title="Ambush">ambush</a>.</p>
<p>Siskovits‚Äô troops arrived on the scene first and waited for the enemy in the woods on the left side of the road. Laudon was supposed to come from Moravsk√Ω Beroun later, in the middle of the fight, and attack from the opposite side, thus increasing the chaos among the Prussian soldiers.</p>
<p>First the vanguard consisting of 4,850 soldiers and 250 wagons arrived, but Siskovits let them go. Austrian artillery started the fight when the main body of the convoy was passing, which caused enormous chaos among the wagons. Siskovits‚Äô infantry was fighting successfully with Prussian soldiers despite being outnumbered 3 to 1, and when Laudon‚Äôs troops appeared from the other side the result of the battle was determined. After 7 hours of fight the Prussian convoy was routed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casualties">Casualties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Domstadtl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Casualties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although the total number of fighting Austrian soldiers was approximately 12,000, they lost (at Guntramovice and Doma≈°ov together) only about 600 of them. The Prussian <a href="/wiki/Casualty_(person)" title="Casualty (person)">casualties</a> were much higher, although different sources give different numbers. The Austrians claimed about 2,000 killed, injured or missing soldiers and 1,450 captured (including General Puttkamer and 40 other officers), while Prussians reported only 2,701 killed, injured, missing and captured soldiers altogether, although they admitted that they found it difficult to count them precisely due to the complete dispersion of their troops after the battle.</p>
<p>The victors also seized 2,200 horses, numerous cattle and a major part of the transported materiel. Because many of the wagons were damaged during the battle, they burned everything they were not able to take away with them. Some of the ammunition wagons were blown up as a part of the victory celebrations. An important part of the spoils was 2 million <a href="/wiki/Prussian_thaler" title="Prussian thaler">Prussian thalers</a> (according to some sources 1 million), but the Austrian commanders let their soldiers keep half of it and only the residual part reached the state coffers.</p>
<p>Only 250 wagons from the vanguard escaped, but some of them were captured by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croat" title="Croat">Croatian</a> soldiers near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Svat%C3%BD_Kope%C4%8Dek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Svat√Ω Kopeƒçek (page does not exist)">Svat√Ω Kopeƒçek</a>, only several kilometers north of Olomouc, and as a result only about 100‚Äì200 of them reached their destination.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consequences">Consequences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Domstadtl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Consequences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The importance of the battle is sometimes underestimated. The number of casualties on either side were not as high as in other notable battles of that time, but the loss of the supplies for the Prussian army had severe consequences. It significantly influenced Frederick‚Äôs opinion on whether to continue to besiege Olomouc and shocked the Prussian camp. When Daun finally came to help Olomouc, the Prussians were forced to abandon the siege of the city as the lack of ammunition had made its capture impossible. The Prussian army retreated to <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemia</a>. The Battles of Guntramovice and Doma≈°ov were the turning point in the fight for Moravia.</p>
<p>A memorial called "Black Cross" was built to the north-east of Doma≈°ov in 1858 on the occasion of the 100th anniversary of the battle. There is also a memorial to the soldiers killed in the battle to the south-east of Guntramovice.</p>
