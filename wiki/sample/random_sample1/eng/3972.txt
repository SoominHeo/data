<p>The <b>East Germanic languages</b> are a group of extinct <a href="/wiki/Germanic_languages" title="Germanic languages">Germanic languages</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_languages" title="Indo-European languages">Indo-European</a> language family spoken by <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">East Germanic peoples</a>. The only East Germanic languages of which texts are known are <a href="/wiki/Gothic_language" title="Gothic language">Gothic</a> and its dialect, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crimean_Gothic_language" title="Crimean Gothic language">Crimean Gothic</a>; other languages that are assumed to be East Germanic include <a href="/wiki/Vandalic_language" title="Vandalic language">Vandalic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burgundian_language_(Germanic)" title="Burgundian language (Germanic)">Burgundian</a>, though very few texts in these languages are known. Crimean Gothic is believed to have survived until the 18th century.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Germanic_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the 1st century CE, the writings of <a href="/wiki/Pomponius_Mela" title="Pomponius Mela">Pomponius Mela</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pliny_the_elder" title="Pliny the elder">Pliny the elder</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaius_Cornelius_Tacitus" title="Gaius Cornelius Tacitus">Tacitus</a> indicate a division of Germanic-speaking peoples into large groupings with shared ancestry and culture. (This division has been appropriated<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> in modern terminology about the divisions of Germanic languages.)</p>

<p>Based on accounts by <a href="/wiki/Jordanes" title="Jordanes">Jordanes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Procopius" title="Procopius">Procopius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Deacon" title="Paul the Deacon">Paul the Deacon</a> and others; <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">linguistic</a> evidence (see <a href="/wiki/Gothic_language" title="Gothic language">Gothic language</a>); placename evidence; and archaeological evidence, it is believed that the <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">East Germanic tribes</a>, the speakers of the East Germanic languages related to the <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">North Germanic tribes</a>, had migrated from <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> into the area lying east of the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In fact, the Scandinavian influence on <a href="/wiki/Pomerania" title="Pomerania">Pomerania</a> and northern Poland from period III<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> onwards was so considerable that this region is sometimes included in the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_Bronze_Age" title="Nordic Bronze Age">Nordic Bronze Age</a> culture (Dabrowski 1989:73).</p>
<p>There is also archaeological and <a href="/wiki/Toponymy" title="Toponymy">toponymic</a> evidence that Burgundians lived on the <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Bornholm" title="Bornholm">Bornholm</a> (<a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a>: <i>Burgundaholmr</i>), and that Rugians lived on the <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> coast of <a href="/wiki/Rogaland" title="Rogaland">Rogaland</a> (<a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a>: <i>Rygjafylki</i>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Groups">Groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Germanic_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Groups identified as East Germanic tribes include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bastarnae" title="Bastarnae">Bastarnae</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Burgundians" title="Burgundians">Burgundians</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Goths" title="Goths">Goths</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Crimean_Goths" title="Crimean Goths">Crimean Goths</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gepids" title="Gepids">Gepids</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ostrogoths" title="Ostrogoths">Ostrogoths</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Greuthungi" title="Greuthungi">Greuthungi</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thervingi" title="Thervingi">Thervingi</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heruli" title="Heruli">Heruli</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rugii" title="Rugii">Rugii</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Scirii" title="Scirii">Scirii</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Silingi" title="Silingi">Silingi</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Traditionally the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Langobards" title="Langobards">Langobards</a> were classified as East Germanic, however, the <a href="/wiki/Lombardic_language" title="Lombardic language">Lombardic language</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">Yiddish</a> are now considered by many specialists to be close to <a href="/wiki/Old_High_German" title="Old High German">Old High German</a>, especially its <a href="/wiki/Upper_German" title="Upper German">Upper German</a> dialects, which would make a classification as <a href="/wiki/West_Germanic_languages" title="West Germanic languages">West Germanic</a> rather than East Germanic more sensible.</p>
