<p>The <b>meridian 129° east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Meridian" title="Prime Meridian">Greenwich</a></b> is a line of <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitude</a> that extends from the <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">North Pole</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic Ocean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">Southern Ocean</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> to the <a href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">South Pole</a>.</p>
<p>The 129th meridian east forms a <a href="/wiki/Great_circle" title="Great circle">great circle</a> with the <a href="/wiki/51st_meridian_west" title="51st meridian west">51st meridian west</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, the meridian defines the <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia_border" title="Western Australia border">eastern border</a> of <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>, and the western borders of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Territory" title="Northern Territory">Northern Territory</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">South Australia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia_border" title="Western Australia border">border of Western Australia</a> meets the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Territory_borders" title="Northern Territory borders">Northern Territory border</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Australian_borders" title="South Australian borders">South Australian border</a> at the <a href="/wiki/26th_parallel_south" title="26th parallel south">26th parallel south</a>, at what is known as <a href="/wiki/Surveyor_Generals_Corner" title="Surveyor Generals Corner">Surveyor Generals Corner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
