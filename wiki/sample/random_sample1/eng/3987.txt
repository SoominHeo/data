<p>The <b>Asian Winter Games</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> held every four years for members of the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Council_of_Asia" title="Olympic Council of Asia">Olympic Council of Asia</a> (OCA) which features winter events. The <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Olympic_Committee" title="Japanese Olympic Committee">Japanese Olympic Committee</a> first suggested the idea of holding a winter version of the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games" title="Asian Games">Asian Games</a> in 1982. Their efforts were rewarded when they were finally given hosting rights for the first edition that was held in <a href="/wiki/Sapporo" title="Sapporo">Sapporo</a> in <a href="/wiki/1986_Asian_Winter_Games" title="1986 Asian Winter Games">1986</a>, as the city had the infrastructure and expertise gained from hosting of the <a href="/wiki/1972_Winter_Olympics" title="1972 Winter Olympics">1972 Winter Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>From having only seven member nations of the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Council_of_Asia" title="Olympic Council of Asia">Olympic Council of Asia</a> taking part in the first edition, the number of nations competing in the Winter Asiad has consistently grown. In the <a href="/wiki/2007_Asian_Winter_Games" title="2007 Asian Winter Games">2007 Asian Winter Games</a> in Changchun, 27 out of the 45 members fielded a record number of competitors, while all 45 NOCs sent delegations for the first time ever in Winter Asian history.</p>
<p>Although games in Lebanon in 2009 were considered, they ultimately did not take place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
