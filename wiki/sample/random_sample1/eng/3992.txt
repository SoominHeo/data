<p><i><b>Dōkyūsei</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">同級生</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>classmates</i>)</span> is the first game in the popular <a href="/wiki/D%C5%8Dky%C5%ABsei_series" title="Dōkyūsei series"><i>Dōkyūsei</i> series</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eroge" title="Eroge">eroge</a> <a href="/wiki/Dating_sim" title="Dating sim">dating sims</a> by <a href="/wiki/ELF_Corporation" title="ELF Corporation">ELF Corporation</a>. The original <i>Dōkyūsei</i> (also called <i><a href="/wiki/Nanpa" title="Nanpa">Nanpa</a></i>, meaning <i>pick-up</i>, due to its <a href="/wiki/Batch_file" title="Batch file">batch file</a> name "nanba.bat"), released in 1992.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="OVA">OVA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C5%8Dky%C5%ABsei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: OVA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There was an <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">OVA</a> series made from <i>Dōkyūsei</i>, which was originally a 45-minute one-shot, but the original release was expanded into two half-hour episodes, with two more episodes added, for a total four. The first two episodes were released as <i>End of Summer</i>, and episodes three and four were released as <i>End of Summer 2</i> in the North American market by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SoftCel_Pictures" title="SoftCel Pictures">SoftCel Pictures</a>, an imprint of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ADV_Films" title="ADV Films">ADV Films</a>. A two-episode sequel, entitled <i>Dōkyūsei Climax Files</i>, was also released.</p>
<p><i>End of Summer</i> is about a boy who seeks after a redheaded girl named Mai and along the way runs into difficulties (and trysts) with several other girls, namely, Kurumi, Miho, Misa, and Satomi. It was renowned for its romantic attributes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and quality of animation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The depth of character development has been reviewed as being above average, although the format forced on it by OVA requires some compromises with realism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The film has some classical music.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_system">Game system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C5%8Dky%C5%ABsei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Game system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The gameplay in <i>Dōkyūsei</i> revolves around wandering through different locations in a town, conversing with whatever character you happen to meet. To finish with a girl, the main challenge is to learn the times of day when the girl will be in a location.</p>
