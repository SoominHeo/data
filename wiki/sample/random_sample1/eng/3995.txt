<p>The <b>Southeast Asia Ministers of Education Organization</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (<b>SEAMEO</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental organization</a> of the eleven <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asian</a> countries, which was formed on 30 November 1965 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lao_PDR" title="Lao PDR">Lao PDR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> and the then Republic of <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> SEAMEO aims to promote regional cooperation in the fields of education, science and culture.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southeast_Asian_Ministers_of_Education_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>SEAMEO organised the SEAMEO <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mathematics_Olympiad" title="Mathematics Olympiad">Mathematics Olympiad</a>, a regional competition for young mathematicians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_programs">Joint programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southeast_Asian_Ministers_of_Education_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Joint programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014 SEAMEO announced a joint program with the German Government called "Fit For School", the first phase running over 3 years will concentrate on basic hygiene of pre--school and primary school children, improving sanitation facilities and bringing hand-washing and teeth brushing into school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
