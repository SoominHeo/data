<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Treaty of Amity and Cooperation in Southeast Asia</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <a class="external text" href="http://www.asean.org/?static_post=treaty-of-amity-and-cooperation-in-southeast-asia-indonesia-24-february-1976-2" rel="nofollow">Treaty of Amity &amp; Cooperation in Southeast Asia</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Peace_treaty" title="Peace treaty">peace treaty</a> among <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asian</a> countries established by the founding members of the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations" title="Association of Southeast Asian Nations">Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN)</a>, a geo-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political" title="Political">political</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic" title="Economic">economic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organisation" title="Organisation">organisation</a> of 10 countries located in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Amity_and_Cooperation_in_Southeast_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 24 February 1976, the <a href="/wiki/Treaty" title="Treaty">treaty</a> was signed into force by the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">leaders</a> of the original members of ASEAN,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lee_Kuan_Yew" title="Lee Kuan Yew">Lee Kuan Yew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Marcos" title="Ferdinand Marcos">Ferdinand Marcos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Datuk" title="Datuk">Datuk</a> <a href="/wiki/Hussein_Onn" title="Hussein Onn">Hussein Onn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kukrit_Pramoj" title="Kukrit Pramoj">Kukrit Pramoj</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Suharto" title="Suharto">Suharto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charter_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-charter-2">[2]</a></sup> Other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASEAN_members" title="ASEAN members">members</a> <a href="/wiki/Ratification" title="Ratification">acceded</a> to it upon or before joining the bloc. It was amended on 15 December 1987 by a <a href="/wiki/Protocol_(politics)" title="Protocol (politics)">protocol</a> to open the document for accession by <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">states</a> outside <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and again on 25 July 1998, to condition such accession on the consent of all member states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On 23 July 2001, the parties established the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rules_of_order" title="Rules of order">rules of procedure</a> of the treaty's High Council, which was stipulated in Article 14 of the document.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 7 October 2003, during the <a href="/wiki/ASEAN_Summit#9th_Summit" title="ASEAN Summit">annual summit</a>, a declaration was released that says:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd><i>"A High Council of [the treaty] shall be the important component in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ASEAN_Security_Community&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ASEAN Security Community (page does not exist)">ASEAN Security Community</a> since it reflects ASEAN’s commitment to resolve all differences, disputes and conflicts peacefully."</i></dd>
</dl>
<p>India and China were first, outside ASEAN to sign the treaty in 2003 at Bali, Indonesia. As of July 2009<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Amity_and_Cooperation_in_Southeast_Asia&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, sixteen countries outside the bloc have acceded to the treaty. On 22 July 2009, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State" title="United States Secretary of State">Secretary of State</a> <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a> signed the TAC on behalf of the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> intends to accede as soon as the treaty is amended to allow for the accession of non-states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The treaty has been endorsed by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">General Assembly</a> stating that:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-un.org_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-un.org-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd><i>"The purposes and principles of the Treaty of Amity and Cooperation in Southeast Asia and its provisions for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peaceful" title="Peaceful">pacific</a> settlement of regional disputes and for regional co-operation to achieve peace, amity and friendship among the peoples of Southeast Asia [are] in accordance with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charter_of_the_United_Nations" title="Charter of the United Nations">Charter of the United Nations</a>."</i></dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Principles">Principles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Amity_and_Cooperation_in_Southeast_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Principles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The purpose of the Treaty is to promote perpetual <a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">peace</a>, everlasting <a href="/wiki/Friendship" title="Friendship">amity</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Co-operation" title="Co-operation">co-operation</a> among the people of Southeast Asia which would contribute to their strength, solidarity, and closer relationship. In their relations with one another, the High Contracting Parties shall be guided by the following fundamental principles;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charter_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-charter-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>a. mutual respect for the independence, sovereignty, equality, territorial integrity and national identity of all nations,</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>b. the right of every State to lead its national existence free from external interference, subversion or coercion,</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>c. non-interference in the internal affairs of one another,</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>d. settlement of differences or disputes by peaceful means,</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>e. renunciation of the threat or use of force, and</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>f. effective co-operation among themselves.</dd>
</dl>
