<p>The <b>AFF Championship</b> (ASEAN Football Championship), is a biennial international <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> competition organised by the <a href="/wiki/ASEAN_Football_Federation" title="ASEAN Football Federation">ASEAN Football Federation</a> (AFF), contested by the national teams of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. It was founded as the <b>Tiger Cup</b> after <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Pacific_Breweries" title="Asia Pacific Breweries">Asia Pacific Breweries</a>, makers of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Pacific_Breweries#Tiger_beer" title="Asia Pacific Breweries">Tiger Beer</a>, sponsored the competition from its inauguration in 1996 until the 2004 edition. After Asia Pacific Breweries withdrew as title sponsors, the competition was known as the AFF Championship for the 2007 edition. From 2008, Japanese auto-company <a href="/wiki/Suzuki" title="Suzuki">Suzuki</a> bought the naming rights for the competition, and the competition has therefore been named the <b>AFF Suzuki Cup</b> for sponsorship reasons. The official football since 2014 of the cup has been Mitre - and in 2016 the Delta Hyperseam will be the ball of choice.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AFF_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sports marketing, media and event management firm, Lagardère Sports has been involved in the tournament since the inaugural edition in 1996. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suzuki_Motors" title="Suzuki Motors">Suzuki Motors</a> is the title sponsor of the tournament since 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AFF_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Summaries">Summaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AFF_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Summaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 2004, the knockout stage is played over two legs on a home-and-away format without the <a href="/wiki/Away_goals_rule" title="Away goals rule">away goals rule</a>. However, the away goals rule was put into effect from the 2010 edition.</p>
<p>Since the 2007 edition, there was no official third place match. Hence, there were no official third place and fourth place being awarded. Semi-finalists are listed in alphabetical order.</p>
