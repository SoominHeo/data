<p><b>Dongdaemun Market</b> or <b>Tongdaemun</b> (abbreviated <b>DDM</b>) is a large commercial district comprising traditional <a href="/wiki/Market_(place)" title="Market (place)">markets</a> and shopping centers in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jongno-gu" title="Jongno-gu">Jongno-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddm_doosan_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ddm_doosan-1">[1]</a></sup> A popular shopping and tourist destination, it has been designated a 'Special Tourism Zone' (관광특구) since 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongdaemun_Market&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dongdaemun Market opened in July 1905 in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yeji-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yeji-dong (page does not exist)">Yeji-dong</a> (예지동 <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%A6%AE" title="wikt:禮">禮</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%99%BA" title="wikt:智">智</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B4%9E" title="wikt:洞">洞</a>), whose name means "a neighborhood for learning politeness", so the market was originally called <i>Baeugaejang</i> (배우개장, "market for learning"). It was also called Gwangjang Market (광장시장) as a company of the same name was set up as market management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddm_doosan_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ddm_doosan-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The market was set in a closed structure until the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, when it was completely destroyed. It slowly rebuilt over the years and, in 1959, a building was constructed and the market was revived. In 1998 and 1999, large shopping malls such as Geopyeong Freya, <a href="/wiki/Migliore" title="Migliore">Migliore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Doosan_Group" title="Doosan Group">Doosan Tower</a> were built in the district, and the market was renovated with a modern atmosphere in the traditional market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongdaemun_Market&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dongdaemun Market is near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongdaemun" title="Dongdaemun">Dongdaemun</a> from which it takes its name. The market is divided into five shopping districts—A, B, C, D and a shopping town,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with 26 shopping malls over 10 blocks, 30,000 speciality shops, and 50,000 manufacturers.</p>
<p>The market sells all types of goods but notably <a href="/wiki/Silk" title="Silk">silks</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fabric" title="Fabric">fabric</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clothes" title="Clothes">clothes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shoes" title="Shoes">shoes</a> and leather goods, sporting goods, plumbing and electronics, office supplies, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fortune_teller" title="Fortune teller">fortune tellers</a>, toys and food areas specialising in <a href="/wiki/Korean_cuisine" title="Korean cuisine">Korean cuisine</a>. It also has many pet shops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lifeinkorea.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-lifeinkorea.com-5">[5]</a></sup> It is on the Seoul list of 'Asia's 10 greatest street food cities' for the Korean snack <a href="/wiki/Sundae_(Korean_food)" title="Sundae (Korean food)">Sundae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mandu_(dumpling)" title="Mandu (dumpling)">mandu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>DDM was traditionally a night market and wholesalers once operated from 1:00 am to 1:00 pm. Now, the area is open for 18-½ hours a day from 10:30 am to 5:00 am, with some stores open 24 hours a day, although most close on Mondays and holidays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lifeinkorea.com_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-lifeinkorea.com-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Malls">Malls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongdaemun_Market&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Malls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Retail_shops">Retail shops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongdaemun_Market&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Retail shops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The main street divides Dongdaemun Market into two sections. Section 1 is on the side where Doosan Tower is found, and Section 2 is on the side of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongdaemun_Design_Plaza_%26_Park" title="Dongdaemun Design Plaza &amp; Park">Dongdaemun Design Plaza &amp; Park</a>, the old site of <a href="/wiki/Dongdaemun_Stadium" title="Dongdaemun Stadium">Dongdaemun Stadium</a>. Huge shopping malls in Section 1 basically sell wholesale and retail goods, but mostly deal with general customers and tourists at retail prices. They have facilities like money exchanges and information desks with English-speaking staff. Opening hours are aimed at general customers, from 10 am to 5 am the next day. Major shopping malls are Doosan Tower, Migliore, Freya Town and Hello apM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddm_w21_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ddm_w21-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Wholesale_shops">Wholesale shops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongdaemun_Market&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wholesale shops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Shopping malls in Section 2 sell goods in wholesale and retail, but mainly sell in bulk. Malls here usually open at about 8 pm and close at 8 am or 5 pm the next day. The peak time comes late at night through early morning. Since most shops deal with wholesalers, there are no dressing rooms. Major shopping malls in Section 2 are Designer's Club, Migliore Valley, Nuzzon, Gwanghee Fashion Mall, Jeil Pyeonghwa, and Heungin Stardom. Among them, Jeil Pyeonghwa and Heungin Stardom attract customers in their thirties and forties. Teens and youth in their twenties usually visit Designer's Club, Migliore Valley, Nuzzon and Gwanghee Fashion Mall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddm_w21_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ddm_w21-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
