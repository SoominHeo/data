<p><b><a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Greece" title="Elections in Greece">Parliamentary elections</a></b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> on 4 October 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> An election was not required until September 2011.</p>
<p>On 2 September Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Kostas_Karamanlis" title="Kostas Karamanlis">Kostas Karamanlis</a> announced he would request President <a href="/wiki/Karolos_Papoulias" title="Karolos Papoulias">Karolos Papoulias</a> to dissolve Parliament and call an election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Parliament was dissolved on 9 September.</p>
<p>Voting was mandatory; however there are no sanctions or penalties for not voting.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seats_in_Parliament">Seats in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_Parliament" title="Greek Parliament">Parliament</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greek_legislative_election,_2009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Seats in Parliament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The electoral law for this election specifies that:</p>
<ul>
<li>A party or a coalition of parties elects MPs only if its nationwide tally <a href="/wiki/Election_threshold" title="Election threshold">exceeds</a> 3% of the total valid vote</li>
<li>260 of 300 total seats are apportioned <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">proportionally</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Plurality_voting_system" title="Plurality voting system">first-past-the post</a> party or coalition receives a 40-seat bonus</li>
</ul>

<p>According to the constitutional principle of the <i>dedēlōménē</i>, a party or a coalition needs a parliamentary majority of 151 total seats in order to form a government. In the eventuality that no party or coalition are able to secure 151 seats, another election will be called. For this occasion the electoral law specifies that the leading party will receive a <i>50</i>-seat bonus.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_before_the_election">Events before the election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greek_legislative_election,_2009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Events before the election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Greek_parliamentary_elections_2009_debate.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2931" data-file-width="4397" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Greek_parliamentary_elections_2009_debate.jpg/250px-Greek_parliamentary_elections_2009_debate.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Greek_parliamentary_elections_2009_debate.jpg/375px-Greek_parliamentary_elections_2009_debate.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Greek_parliamentary_elections_2009_debate.jpg/500px-Greek_parliamentary_elections_2009_debate.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>

<ul>
<li>September 11, 2009: "Ecologists Greece" announced their electoral alliance with the Popular Orthodox Rally (LAOS)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>September 16, 2009: A total of 28 parties submitted candidate lists to the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Cassation_(Greece)" title="Court of Cassation (Greece)">Areios Pagos</a>. The court will vet the candidacies' legal standing and officially declare lawful candidates within about a week. In past elections, some candidacies were rejected because the candidates had held certain public offices which they had not resigned in a timely fashion.</li>
<li>September 21, 2009: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Hellenic_Television" title="New Hellenic Television">NET</a> state television, in <a href="/wiki/Simulcast" title="Simulcast">simulcast</a> with major commercial TV stations, broadcast the debate among the leaders of <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">ND</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panhellenic_Socialist_Movement" title="Panhellenic Socialist Movement">PASOK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Greece" title="Communist Party of Greece">KKE</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coalition_of_the_Radical_Left" title="Coalition of the Radical Left">SYRIZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Popular_Orthodox_Rally" title="Popular Orthodox Rally">LAOS</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Ecologist_Greens" title="Ecologist Greens">Ecologist Greens</a>. The party leaders questioned by a team of journalists from the TV stations participating in the simulcast. Questions and responses were rotated and timed. Each party leader was allowed to place one question to one other party leader of their choice directly.</li>
<li>September 21, 2009: The <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Cassation_(Greece)" title="Court of Cassation (Greece)">Areios Pagos</a> announced the parties participating in the elections. 5 of the 28 applications were rejected; namely: "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecologists_Greece_%E2%80%93_Konstantinos_Papanikolas_%E2%80%93_Political_Catharsis" title="Ecologists Greece – Konstantinos Papanikolas – Political Catharsis">Ecologists Greece – Konstantinos Papanikolas – Political Catharsis</a>", "Hellenic Direct Democracy Movement" (<i>Ελληνικό Κίνημα Άμεσης Δημοκρατίας</i> - Giorgos Kokkas), "New Greek Republic" (<i>Νέα Ελληνική Δημοκρατία</i> - N. Laskos), "Where are you, Papadopoulos? – Party of Greek Aliens – Terrorise the Terrorists" (<i>Πού είσαι Παπαδόπουλε – Κόμμα Ελλήνων Αλλοδαπών – Τρομοκρατήστε τους Τρομοκράτες</i>), "Future of the Greeks - Servants of the Greek People" (<i>Μέλλον των Ελλήνων – Υπηρέτες του Ελληνικού Λαού</i> - N. Skopelitis).</li>
<li>September 22, 2009: The two likely Prime Ministers, <a href="/wiki/Kostas_Karamanlis" title="Kostas Karamanlis">Kostas Karamanlis</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Papandreou_(junior)" title="George Papandreou (junior)">George Papandreou</a>, debated for 75 minutes. The debate was divided in 5 sections (the economy, education, administration, the environment, and foreign policy). Both leaders had 2 sessions of 3 minutes and 1 session of 1 minute. The moderator had 1 minute to introduce the subject. The debate was moderated by journalist Maria Houkli, and was simulcast as per the multi-party debate above.</li>
<li>October 3, 2009: It was announced that seven pollsters pooled their resources to conduct a joint <a href="/wiki/Exit_poll" title="Exit poll">exit poll</a>, which was broadcast by all TV and news stations at 7 PM, the time when voting officially ended. The pollsters procured approximately 14,000 randomised responses and were aiming for a ±1.5% margin of error in their earliest predictions. <i>Singular Logic</i>, the IT contractor for the Ministry of Interior, implemented a secure system to obtain initial tallies from approximately 4,000 voting stations by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SMS" title="SMS">SMS</a>. They were thus able to project final results within ±0.2% by 9 PM and within ±0.1% by midnight.</li>
<li>October 3, 2009: Publicised <a href="/wiki/Odds" title="Odds">odds</a> by international <a href="/wiki/Bookmaker" title="Bookmaker">bookmakers</a> were roughly in agreement with the latest predictions as outlined in the tables above. Sports <a href="/wiki/Gambling" title="Gambling">betting</a> is the only form of betting allowed in Greece.</li>
<li>October 4, 2009: Voting commenced at 7 AM and was completed at 7 PM, in 20,937 stations throughout the country. Turnout was estimated at slightly more than 7 million.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-election_events">Post-election events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greek_legislative_election,_2009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-election events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2931" data-file-width="4397" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg/220px-Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg/330px-Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg/440px-Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Prime_Minister_of_Greece_George_Papandreou_-_ceremony_for_the_official_handover_2009Oct06.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Former Prime Minister, Kostas Karamanlis and George Papandreou in the ceremony for the official handover at the Maximos Mansion.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>October 4, 2009: Incumbent Prime Minister and <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">New Democracy</a> party leader <a href="/wiki/Kostas_Karamanlis" title="Kostas Karamanlis">Kostas Karamanlis</a> conceded defeat and stated that he is resigning his offices, and will not stand as candidate at the party convention summoned to elect his successor next month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The 33.5% tally marks a historic low for the party since its founding in 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-first-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-second_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-second-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>October 5, 2009: The Speaker of the outgoing Parliament notified the President of the Republic about the election returns. Immediately afterwards incumbent Prime Minister Kostas Karamanlis tended his resignation to the President.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfi.fr_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-rfi.fr-8">[8]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panhellenic_Socialist_Movement" title="Panhellenic Socialist Movement">Panhellenic Socialist Movement</a> (PASOK) leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Papandreou_(junior)" title="George Papandreou (junior)">George Papandreou</a> was then summoned by the President and given a mandate to form a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_(government)" title="Cabinet (government)">Cabinet</a> as the Prime Minister-designate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> PASOK <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">Member of the European Parliament</a> Stavros Lambrinidis said the result was "remarkable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfi.fr_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-rfi.fr-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>October 6, 2009: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Papandreou_(junior)" title="George Papandreou (junior)">George Papandreou</a> was sworn in as Prime Minister at 11 AM, and moved into the <i>Máximos</i> Mansion, the Prime Ministerial office. The names of the new ministers were announced at 7 PM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It became known that George Papandreou had offered the <a href="/wiki/Ecologist_Greens" title="Ecologist Greens">EcoGreens</a> one of the two positions of Undersecretary for the Environment, but the offer was turned down.</li>
<li>October 7, 2009: The new Cabinet was sworn in at 11 AM. The <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">New Democracy</a> party congress is to convene and elect its new leader at November 7, 2009.</li>
</ul>
