<p><b>Dongseong of Baekje</b> (?-501, r. 479-501<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_monarchs_of_Korea" title="List of monarchs of Korea">24th king</a> of <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Three Kingdoms of Korea</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongseong_of_Baekje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was the son of Gonji, the younger brother of the 22nd king <a href="/wiki/Munju_of_Baekje" title="Munju of Baekje">Munju</a> who returned to Baekje from <a href="/wiki/Yamato_period" title="Yamato period">Yamato</a> Japan in 477 after hearing of the fall of the Baekje capital. Gonji died in that year, and like Munju may have been murdered by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hae_Gu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hae Gu (page does not exist)">Hae Gu</a>. Dongseong appears to have been promoted to the throne by the Jin clan that prevailed over Hae Gu's rebellion. After Samgeun died without children, the throne passed to Dongseong.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reign">Reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongseong_of_Baekje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He worked to strengthen the court's power after the move of the capital from the present-day <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> area to <a href="/wiki/Ungjin" title="Ungjin">Ungjin</a>. He built several fortresses and castles to build out the new capital. He incorporated the local Sa, Yeon, and Baek clans into the court to counter the entrenched aristocracy from the former capital.</p>
<p>Dongseong sent a tribute mission to the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Qi" title="Southern Qi">Southern Qi</a> in 484, reopening Baekje’s ties with southern China after a long hiatus.</p>
<p>He established an alliance with <a href="/wiki/Silla" title="Silla">Silla</a> through his marriage of a Silla noblewoman in 493, and the two countries united in attacking Goguryeo in 495.</p>
<p>In 498, the Baekje army subjugated <a href="/wiki/Tamna" title="Tamna">Tamna</a>, the kingdom on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju-do" title="Jeju-do">Jeju island</a> which had formally accepted Baekje rule twenty-two years before, because it failed to send tributes.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Qi" title="Book of Qi">Book of Qi</a> states that Dongseong sent armies to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liaodong" title="Liaodong">Liaodong</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liaoxi" title="Liaoxi">Liaoxi</a> (요서 遼西) in China to defeat <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> forces.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongseong_of_Baekje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beginning in 499, the country was stricken by famine, but according to the <i><a href="/wiki/Samguk_Sagi" title="Samguk Sagi">Samguk Sagi</a></i> the king was unresponsive. He continued to live an indulgent lifestyle while brigandage spread.</p>
<p>By the end of Dongseong's rule, the local clans of the new capital had eclipsed the traditionally powerful Hae and Jin clans, and even pressured the throne. Dongseong sought to contain them by exiling <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baek_Ga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baek Ga (page does not exist)">Baek Ga</a> to an outlying castle. This caused great resentment, and Baek Ga's forces assassinated Dongseong while he was hunting.</p>
