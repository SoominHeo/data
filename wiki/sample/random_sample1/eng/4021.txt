<p><i><b>The Dong-A Ilbo</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">동아일보</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani">東亞日報</span>, literally <i><b>East Asia Daily</b></i>) is a newspaper in Korea since 1920 with daily circulation of more than 1.2 million and opinion leaders as its main readers.</p>
<p><i>The Dong-A Ilbo</i> is the parent company of Dong-A Media Group (DAMG), which is composed of 11 affiliates including Sports Dong-A, Dong-A Science, DUNet, and dongA.com, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Channel_A_(Korea)" title="Channel A (Korea)">Channel A</a>, general service cable broadcasting company launched in December 1, 2011. It covers variety of areas including news, drama, entertainment, sports, education, and movies 24 hours a day.</p>
<p><i>The Dong-A Ilbo</i> has partnered with international news companies such as <i>The New York Times</i> of the United States of America, <i>The Asahi Shimbun</i> of Japan and <i>The People's Daily</i> of China. It has correspondents stationed in five major cities worldwide including Washington D.C., New York, Beijing, Tokyo and Paris. It also publishes global editions in 90 cities worldwide including <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feminist_movement">Feminist movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Feminist movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By creating “Women’s Section” in its regular production of the paper, <i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> worked to liberate women who had long been forced to stay at home and abide by traditional rules of obedience from the very beginning. Articles such as “The New Woman and Education”, “Liberation of Women and the Nuclear Family” and “Women and Career” were printed to stimulate women’s participation in society and the development of women’s rights.</p>
<p>In 1933, <i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> created the very first women’s magazine called <i>The New Women</i> (later to become <i>Dong-A Women</i>) For the female readers at the time could only read Korean characters (not Chinese characters), all the articles were written purely in Korean. The publication held events such as cooking schools and wives’ picnic providing women a place to socialize and a reason to get out of the house. <i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> also hosted athletic events for women. “Women’s National Tennis Competition” is Korea's and <i>Dong-A</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s oldest contest ever to be held.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Forced_closure:_Erasing_of_Japanese_flag">Forced closure: Erasing of Japanese flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Forced closure: Erasing of Japanese flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1939 when World War II erupted, the Japanese government began a campaign to unify Korea and Japan as a culture. This meant the suppression of much of Korea's cultural identity.</p>
<p>After four attempts to close <i>DongA Ilbo</i> and other numerous occasions of inspection, censorship, seizure and deletion, the Japanese government succeeded in August 1940. <i>The Dong-A Ilbo</i> built Dong-A Broadcasting System. Under the Chun Doo-hwan regime, South Korea's media policy had changed. The regime had closed several radio and TV networks and DBS was forced to give most of its shares to the government. <i>The Dong-A Ilbo</i> gave up DBS in 1980.</p>
<p>The event that made forced closure possible was <i>Dong-A Ilbo'</i>s deliberate obscuration of the Japanese flag in a photograph of the first ever Korean Olympic Gold medalist.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sohn_Kee-Chung" title="Sohn Kee-Chung">Sohn Kee-Chung</a> won the gold medal in marathon at the 11th Summer Olympics in Berlin (1936); however, because Korea was under Japanese rule, his uniform featured the Japanese flag. The article showed pride for the Korean athlete and featured a smudged Japanese flag to promote nationalistic ideas.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognition">Awards and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards and recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_partnerships">International partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> has partnered with internationally acclaimed news companies such as the New York Times and <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a>. They share information including articles and video clips. <i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> also prints global editions in 90 cities such as Washington DC, London, Paris, Frankfurt, etc., and has 22 branches world-wide including LA, Vancouver, Osaka. It also has international correspondents stationed in 6 cities with New York, Tokyo, and Beijing among them. Also, the digital edition of the paper is available in English, Japanese and Chinese.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Partnership:</b>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> (UK)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Asahi_Shimbun" title="Asahi Shimbun">Asahi Shimbun</a></i> (Japan)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/People%27s_Daily" title="People's Daily">People's Daily</a></i> (China)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Izvestia" title="Izvestia">Izvestia</a></i> (Russia)</li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney_Morning_Herald" title="Sydney Morning Herald">Sydney Morning Herald</a></i> (Australia)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Publishing">Publishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Publishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> also has an active publishing sector which produces magazines and books. There are four monthly magazines, two weekly magazines and one annual magazine. The literature sector concentrates on translating and distributing foreign material and also creating domestic content. DongA Books has brought to Korea many international bestsellers and award winning literature as well as creating million sellers on its own.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Magazines:</b>
<ul>
<li><i>Shin Dong-A</i> (Current events magazine)</li>
<li><i>Women's Dong-A</i> (Women's magazine)</li>
<li><i>Dong-A Science</i> (Popular science magazine)</li>
<li><i>Dong-A Science KIDS</i></li>
<li><i>Weekly Dong-A</i></li>
<li><i>Weekend</i></li>
<li><i>Dong-A Annual</i></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><b>Dong-A Books: (examples)</b>
<ul>
<li><i>Interpreter of Maladies</i> by Jhumpa Lahiri (a Pulitzer winning fiction)</li>
<li><i>A Walk in the Woods</i> by Bill Bryson (an international bestseller)</li>
<li><i>Sponge</i> series (Korean content) has sold over a million copies</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_and_multi-media_services">New and multi-media services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: New and multi-media services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> has been investing in many ventures that integrate technology into the method of spreading the news. First was the establishment of DongA.com which is the online version of the paper with much more content. It provides space for discussion and submission by the readers. From the success of the on-line content, the company also started its mobile services allowing readers to seek out the news wherever and whenever they are. With recent partnership with Reuters, <i>Dong-A</i> aims to add multimedia services to its methods. With raw video feeds from Reuters which <i>Dong-A</i> has the right to edit for its own use, DongA.com aims to reach its readers through text, images and video.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_service">Community service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Dong-a_Ilbo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Community service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Dong-A Ilbo</i> has always recognized its responsibility as a public corporation. As stated in Dong-A DNA, humanism is a great part of <i>Dong-A Ilbo</i>. It has a Culture &amp; Sports Operations department (New Project Bureau) that works to raise awareness of different areas in arts and sports as well as promote healthy lifestyles. The company also has many foundations and scholarships for the less fortunate students in the country.</p>

<p>and more</p>
