<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dong_(Chinese_surname)" title="Dong (Chinese surname)">Dong</a>.</div>
<p><b>Dong Yuan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">董源</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Dŏng Yuán</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile"><b>Tung Yüan</b></span></i>, <a href="/wiki/Gan_Chinese" title="Gan Chinese">Gan</a>: dung3 ngion4; c. 934 – c. 962) was a <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painter</a>.</p>
<p>He was born in Zhongling (钟陵; present-day <a href="/wiki/Jinxian_County" title="Jinxian County">Jinxian County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> Province).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CiHai_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CiHai-2">[2]</a></sup> Dong Yuan was active in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Tang" title="Southern Tang">Southern Tang</a> Kingdom of the <a href="/wiki/Five_Dynasties_and_Ten_Kingdoms_period" title="Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period">Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period</a>. He was from <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>, which was a center for culture and the arts.</p>
<p>He was known for both figure and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landscape_art" title="Landscape art">landscape</a> paintings, and exemplified the elegant style which would become the standard for brush painting in China for the next nine centuries. He and his pupil <a href="/wiki/Juran_(painter)" title="Juran (painter)">Juran</a> (巨然) were the founders of the Southern style of landscape painting, known as the Jiangnan Landscape style. Together with <a href="/wiki/Jing_Hao" title="Jing Hao">Jing Hao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guan_Tong_(painter)" title="Guan Tong (painter)">Guan Tong</a> of the Northern style they constituted the four seminal painters of that time.</p>
<p>As with many artists in China, his profession was as an official, here he studied the existing styles of Li Sixun and <a href="/wiki/Wang_Wei_(8th-century_poet)" title="Wang Wei (8th-century poet)">Wang Wei</a>. However, he added to these masters' techniques; he included more sophisticated perspective.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Works">Works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Yuan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>Xiao and Xiang Rivers</i> or <i>Scenes along the Xiao and Xiang Rivers</i>, a painting on silk (49.80 cm by 141.30 cm),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is one of his best-known masterpieces. It demonstrates his exquisite techniques, and his sense of composition. The clouds break the background mountains into a central pyramid composition and a secondary pyramid, by softening the mountain line, he makes the immobile effect more pronounced.</p>
<p>The inlet by breaking the landscape into groups makes the serenity of the foreground more pronounced, instead of simply being a border to the composition, it is a space of its own, into which the boat on the far right intrudes, even though it is tiny compared to the mountains. Left of center, he uses his unusual brush stroke techniques, later copied in countless paintings, to give a strong sense of foliage to the trees, which contrasts with the rounded waves of stone that make up the mountains themselves. This gives the painting a more distinct middle ground, and makes the mountains have an aura and distance which gives them greater grandeur and personality. He also used "face like" patterns in the mountain on the right. A painting attributed to Dong Yuan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <i>The Riverbank</i>, is housed in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Museum_of_Art" title="Metropolitan Museum of Art">Metropolitan Museum of Art</a>, and was a gift from New York financier <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Tang" title="Oscar Tang">Oscar Tang</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twsJanJ13e1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-twsJanJ13e1-5">[5]</a></sup> though some scholars believe it may be a modern forgery by <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Daqian" title="Zhang Daqian">Zhang Daqian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
