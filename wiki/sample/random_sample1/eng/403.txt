<p>The <b>2009 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXXX Gran Premio Santander d'Italia</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 13 September 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Autodromo Nazionale Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It was the 13th race of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. It was contested over 53 laps.</p>
<p>It was the last win for <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> and for team driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. Team-mate and Championship Leader <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> came in second, completing Brawn's fourth (and last) one-two finish of the season, while the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> came third after reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> crashed on the final lap.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Hamilton's crash meant the race officially finished behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, despite the safety car not picking up the leader. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, who finished in fourth place, recorded the first fastest lap of his career, and the first for the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> team.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led the Drivers Championship by 16 points from <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, who led <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull's</a> third placed man <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> by 3 points. Brawn led the Constructors Championship by 23½ points from Red Bull who were a further 48½ points ahead of winners of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Belgian Grand Prix">previous race</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vettel won his maiden Formula One race in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Italian Grand Prix">2008</a> race for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> team, that made him the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_winners" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest driver to win a Formula One race</a>, in the first race won by both the Toro Rosso team since they were formed as <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and by a customer <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> engine. The only other Italian Grand Prix winners in the field are <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and Barrichello. No <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> has won his home Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Scarfiotti" title="Ludovico Scarfiotti">Ludovico Scarfiotti</a> won his only race at the <a href="/wiki/1966_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1966 Italian Grand Prix">1966 race</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> were the only current Italians in the field this year, with <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> replaced as Ferrari's stand-in by Fisichella. Fisichella became the first Italian since <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in <a href="/wiki/1992_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Italian Grand Prix">1992</a> to race for Ferrari at Monza, after terminating his <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> contract. Fisichella was replaced by Force India test driver Liuzzi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Renault decided to use KERS for the first time since <a href="/wiki/2009_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first session of the Italian GP finished with the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> on top with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> first and second fastest, respectively. <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> finished the session in third. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renault_F1" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s Fernando Alonso finished fourth while team-mate <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> finished fifteenth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pr1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pr1-5">[5]</a></sup> Fischella, driving for Ferrari for the first time in his career, finished eighth ahead of team-mate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> who ended the session in tenth. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" title="Toyota F1">Toyota's</a> Trulli and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> were at the back of the pack. Championship leader Jenson Button ended seventh while his Brawn team-mate Rubens Barrichello finished twelfth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pr1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pr1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the second session Force India's Adrian Sutil was fastest, ahead of the two Renaults of Grosjean and Alonso. Hamilton was eleventh with Kovalainen coming in fourth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pr2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pr2-6">[6]</a></sup> Button in a <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a> finished 19th, with only Fisichella separating him from last place. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull's</a> Sebastian Vettel came eighteenth, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in fourteenth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pr2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pr2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The final practice session ahead of Qualifying again saw Sutil fastest, ahead of the Brawn of Jenson Button with team-mate Barrichello in fourth. The two McLarens ended fifth and sixth, with Fisichella's replacement at Force India, Vitantonio Liuzzi, in seventh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pr3_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pr3-7">[7]</a></sup> The two Red Bulls ended the session 17th and 18th. Fisichella ended the session last, with Räikkönen's other Ferrari in twelfth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pr3_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pr3-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1910" data-file-width="2950" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg/220px-Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg/330px-Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg/440px-Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Adrian_Sutil_2009_Italy_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> qualified in a career-best second position, placing a <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> on the front row for the second consecutive race.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The first session, lasting 20 minutes, decided the final five places on the grid. Five minutes into the session Liuzzi ran off the track but did not damage his car and managed to proceed through to the next part of qualifying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Qu2-8">[8]</a></sup> Räikkönen topped the session ahead of Hamilton and Kovalainen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Qu1-9">[9]</a></sup> Both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a> and both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> cars were knocked out along with the Toyota of Timo Glock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Qu1-9">[9]</a></sup> Fisichella made it through to the next session, making it the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> that both Ferraris had made it through to Q2. Grosjean also progressed to Q2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu2_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Qu2-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second session, lasting 15 minutes, decided positions 11 to 15. Five minutes into the session <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>'s BMW engine gave way to leave him in 15th for the race. Seven minutes later, the other BMW of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> appeared to have the same problem and pulled off with engine problems just before the first corner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu2_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Qu2-8">[8]</a></sup> This left Kubica qualifying in 13th. Fisichella was unable to make it through to the final session of qualifying. Button topped the session, with Hamilton in second and Force India's Adrian Sutil in third. After the chequered flag had fallen Liuzzi was the final driver to post a time moving from 13th into the top 10. The other drivers not to make it were Grosjean and Trulli.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu1_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Qu1-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The final session, lasting ten minutes, decided the top ten positions. Early on, Alonso set the fastest time in the first sector, but it was Räikkönen who had the best time overall. Very soon afterwards Hamilton set the fastest lap, while Webber, Alonso, Sutil and Räikkönen traded second place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu2_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-Qu2-8">[8]</a></sup> The chequered flag fell and very few cars were still out, but it was Hamilton who came out on top to snatch pole from Sutil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu1_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Qu1-9">[9]</a></sup> Although Button qualified sixth and his team-mate Barrichello in fifth, they looked well-placed after the fuel loads were released and both were on a one stop strategy, while Hamilton, Sutil and Räikkönen were all on a two stop strategy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Qu3_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Qu3-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> held onto his lead with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in second, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, on a one-stop fuel strategy was overtaken by the similarly fuelled <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawns</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, off the start line, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, through the second Lesmo. Championship contender <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was spun by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> at Della Roggia on the opening lap and was forced to retire. By Lap 15, Kubica had also retired with an oil leak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Before this, on lap 9, Kubica was shown the black flag with orange circle because he damaged his front wing during his contact with Webber, meaning he was summoned to the pits to repair it. To date, this is the last time the black flag with orange circle was used.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Italy_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2399" data-file-width="3845" height="137" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Jenson_Button_2009_Italy_2.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2009_Italy_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Jenson_Button_2009_Italy_2.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2009_Italy_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Jenson_Button_2009_Italy_2.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2009_Italy_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished on the podium for the first time since his win in <a href="/wiki/2009_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Turkish Grand Prix">Turkey</a>.</div>
</div>

<p>The battle for the lead was tactical, with the Brawn cars stopping only once while Hamilton was on a two-stop strategy. Barrichello and Button were able to lap consistently faster than Hamilton despite their heavier fuel load,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Win_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Win-12">[12]</a></sup> meaning when Hamilton emerged from his second pit stop he found himself behind both Brawns in third position. Reigning World Champion Hamilton tried in vain to overtake Championship leader Button over the final few laps before crashing into the barriers at the second Lesmo as he attempted to catch Button.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Win_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Win-12">[12]</a></sup> Hamilton's crash left debris across the race track, meaning the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed despite it being the final lap of the race. The safety car was therefore unable to pick up the leader, Barrichello, before he took the chequered flag as he would take Brawn's eighth win of the season and his third win at Monza.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1585" data-file-width="2661" height="131" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg/220px-Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg/330px-Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg/440px-Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Toyotas_2009_Italy_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The two Toyotas battling for track position.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The safety car ended a race long battle between the similarly fuelled Räikkönen and Sutil. Sutil was able to keep up with the Ferrari driver throughout the race. Both made their second and final pit stop at the same time, Räikkönen coming out of the pits first despite a delay leaving his garage after Sutil drove too fast into his box, knocking over a mechanic, who was unhurt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Despite Sutil's pressure over the closing stages, Räikkönen held to inherit third place. Sutil did have the consolation of claiming the fastest lap of the race, Force India's first in Formula One.</p>
<p>Fernando Alonso finished fifth, ahead of Kovalainen despite the Finn being on the race-winning one-stop strategy. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> finished seventh after working his way through the field from fifteenth on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> took the final point, ahead of Ferrari debutant <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>. Fisichella's replacement at Force India, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, retired with a mechanical problem while challenging for the points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Win_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Win-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_race">Post race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2081" data-file-width="3271" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Italy.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> crashed out on the last lap, mathematically eliminating him from championship contention.</div>

<p>The poor performance from the Red Bull cars, combined with Brawn's 1-2 finish, meant the Drivers Championship became effectively a two-horse race between Button, who led Barrichello by 14 points with four races to go. Vettel was 26 points behind Button with Webber a further 2.5 points adrift. Brawn team boss <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> said after the race he would allow his drivers to compete amongst themselves for the Drivers Championship, as his team was virtually assured of the Constructors Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Barrichello admitted he was relishing the prospect of a straight title fight after playing second fiddle to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> whilst the German won five world championships at Ferrari. A visibly more relaxed Button was magnanimous in defeat and happy to face a battle from within.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Webber admitted frustration at his first non-finish of the year following such a "small" accident, while Vettel insisted his Championship chances were not over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Quotes_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Quotes-16">[16]</a></sup> McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a> did not criticise Hamilton for his last lap crash, stating the accident was a result of the Briton's "never-give-up attitude, his unquenchable desire to fight until the very last metre of the very last lap."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Quotes_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Quotes-16">[16]</a></sup> The focus of the F1 paddock after the race, however, was on the looming <a href="/wiki/Renault_Formula_One_crash_controversy" title="Renault Formula One crash controversy">Crashgate</a> hearing at the FIA <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="World Motor Sport Council">World Motor Sport Council</a> in Paris the following week. This was to determine whether Renault had asked former driver <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a> to crash at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">2008 Singapore Grand Prix</a>, to aid team-mate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> ultimately win the race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> mathematically conceded the defence of his <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" title="2008 Formula One season">2008</a> World Championship following the result of this race as only Jenson Button, Rubens Barrichello, Sebastian Vettel and Mark Webber were mathematically able to win the Championship. <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" title="2007 Formula One season">2007</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> also mathematically conceded the World Championship as a result of this race, while <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" title="2005 Formula One season">2005</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" title="2006 Formula One season">2006</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was already out of contention before this race. As a result, it meant following this race a brand new World Champion was guaranteed to be crowned for 2009.</p>
