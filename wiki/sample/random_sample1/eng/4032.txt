<p>The <b>E233 series</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">E233系</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a commuter and suburban <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple unit</a> (EMU) train type developed by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East) from the earlier <a href="/wiki/E231_series" title="E231 series">E231 series</a> and the <a href="/wiki/E531_series" title="E531 series">E531 series</a> design. The first train was introduced in December 2006 for use on the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Main_Line" title="Chūō Main Line">Chūō Main Line</a>, followed by the E233-1000 series variant in 2007 for use on the <a href="/wiki/Keihin-T%C5%8Dhoku_Line" title="Keihin-Tōhoku Line">Keihin-Tōhoku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Negishi_Line" title="Negishi Line">Negishi</a> lines, the E233-3000 series outer-suburban variant in December 2007 for use on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokaido_Main_Line" title="Tokaido Main Line">Tokaido Main Line</a>, and narrow-bodied E233-2000 series variant for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joban_Line" title="Joban Line">Joban Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Chiyoda_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line">Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line</a> inter-running services. Further variants were built for use on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saikyo_Line" title="Saikyo Line">Saikyo Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Line" title="Yokohama Line">Yokohama Line</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nambu_Line" title="Nambu Line">Nambu Line</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E233_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The E233 series, according to the October 2005 press release, features two identical sets of the main equipment in case of failure. This is the first JR East stock to feature such backup measures. The E233 series provides for better accessibility for the disabled, and is designed to be more comfortable to ride overall than conventional stock. The height between the platform and the train was reduced from the 80 mm (3.1 in) of the 201 and 209 series to 30 mm (1.2 in). Seats are 460 mm (18 in) wide, compared to the 430 mm (17 in) of the 201 series and 450 mm (18 in) of the 209 series. For standing passengers the handle straps have been lowered by 50 mm compared to older train models.</p>
<p>This stock also features an <a href="/wiki/Air_filter" title="Air filter">air filtration</a> system to remove unpleasant smells. It also features <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquid_crystal_display" title="Liquid crystal display">liquid crystal display</a> information screens and automatic announcement system similar to those used on the <a href="/wiki/E231_series" title="E231 series">E231 series</a> rolling stock on the <a href="/wiki/Yamanote_Line" title="Yamanote Line">Yamanote Line</a>.</p>
<p>The stock is the first JR East stock to use full-colour <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LED" title="LED">LEDs</a> for the destination indicators on the sides of the carriages. This is due to the fact that the Chuo Line has numerous types of services, and colour-coding will help passengers board the correct train.</p>

<p>The first 10+5-car E233-3000 series outer suburban set (E01 + E51) for use on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokaido_Main_Line" title="Tokaido Main Line">Tokaido Main Line</a> was delivered from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyu_Car" title="Tokyu Car">Tokyu Car</a> factory in Yokohama to Kōzu Depot in November 2007. This includes two bilevel Green cars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It entered revenue service on 10 March 2008. It is limited to use on the Tokaido Main Line between Tokyo and Atami.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20080310_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20080310-17">[17]</a></sup> The second set (10+5-car set E02 + E52) was delivered in February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20100218_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20100218-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Subsequent sets (originally numbered NT1 + NT51 onward) were delivered to Tamachi Depot from September 2011, entering service from 12 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj333_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-dj333-14">[14]</a></sup> These sets incorporate a number of minor changes, including moving the second reserve pantograph from car 7 to car 3, and the addition of a toilet in car 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan610_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-railfan610-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Takasaki-based 10- and 5-car sets were introduced on <a href="/wiki/Takasaki_Line" title="Takasaki Line">Takasaki Line</a> services between Ueno and Maebashi, replacing <a href="/wiki/211_series" title="211 series">211 series</a> sets, and on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryomo_Line" title="Ryomo Line">Ryomo Line</a> services from 1 September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dj343_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-dj343-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>These are 10-car and 4+6-car sets for use on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keiyo_Line" title="Keiyo Line">Keiyo Line</a>. The fleet consists of 20 10-car sets and four 4+6-car sets, with the first sets entering revenue service on 1 July 2010, replacing the <a href="/wiki/201_series" title="201 series">201 series</a>, <a href="/wiki/205_series" title="205 series">205 series</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/209-500_series" title="209-500 series">209-500 series</a> EMUs previously used.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrr2014_summer_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrr2014_summer-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first E233-5000 series set, set 501, was delivered from JR East's Niitsu factory on 10 March 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In addition to services on the Keiyō Line between Tokyo and <a href="/wiki/Soga_Station" title="Soga Station">Soga</a>, these sets are scheduled to be used on <a href="/wiki/Sotob%C5%8D_Line" title="Sotobō Line">Sotobō Line</a> services between Soga and <a href="/wiki/Katsuura_Station" title="Katsuura Station">Katsuura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uchib%C5%8D_Line" title="Uchibō Line">Uchibō Line</a> services between Soga and <a href="/wiki/Kazusa-Minato_Station" title="Kazusa-Minato Station">Kazusa-Minato</a>, and on <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dgane_Line" title="Tōgane Line">Tōgane Line</a> services between <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cami_Station" title="Ōami Station">Ōami</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narut%C5%8D_Station" title="Narutō Station">Narutō</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan589_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan589-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>These are 8-car sets operated in the <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Line" title="Yokohama Line">Yokohama Line</a> since 16 February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20140217_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20140217-24">[24]</a></sup> As with the earlier Saikyo Line E233-7000 series variant, the new trains use LED interior lighting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast20120410_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast20120410-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>A total of 28 sets were delivered. The first E233-6000 series set, H016, was delivered from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J-TREC" title="J-TREC">J-TREC</a> Yokohama factory in January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmnews20140108_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-rmnews20140108-26">[26]</a></sup> The first set built at the J-TREC Niitsu factory, set H001, was delivered on 22 January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20140123_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20140123-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents">Accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E233_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=48" title="Edit section: Accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Keihin-Tohoku Line E233-1000 series was derailed in a collision with a track maintenance vehicle in the early hours of 23 February 2014 near <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki_Station" title="Kawasaki Station">Kawasaki Station</a> while on an empty stock train operating from Sakuragicho to Kamata.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mainichi20140223_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-mainichi20140223-37">[36]</a></sup> The first two cars of the 10-car train derailed, with the first car ending up on its side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jt20140223_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-jt20140223-38">[37]</a></sup> The train was carrying no passengers, and the driver and conductor escaped with minor injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fnn20140223_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-fnn20140223-39">[38]</a></sup></p>
