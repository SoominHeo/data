<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Identification (biology)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">For other uses, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Identification_(disambiguation)" title="Identification (disambiguation)">Identification</a>.</div>
<p><b>Identification</b> in <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a> is the process of assigning a pre-existing <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> name to an individual <a href="/wiki/Organism" title="Organism">organism</a>. Identification of organisms to individual scientific names (or codes) may be based on individualistic natural body features (e. g.),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> experimentally created individual markers (e.g., color dot patterns), or natural individualistic molecular markers (similar to those used in <a href="/wiki/DNA_paternity_testing" title="DNA paternity testing">maternity or paternity identification tests</a>). Individual identification is used, e.g., in <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wildlife_management" title="Wildlife management">wildlife management</a> or <a href="/wiki/Conservation_biology" title="Conservation biology">conservation biology</a>. The more common form of identification is the identification of organisms to <a href="/wiki/Common_name" title="Common name">common names</a> (e. g., "lion") or <a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">scientific name</a> (e. g., "<i>Panthera leo</i>"). By necessity this is based on inherited features ("characters") of the sexual organisms, the inheritance forming the basis of defining a class. The features may, e. g., be morphological, anatomical, physiological, behavioral, or molecular.</p>
<p>The term "determination" may occasionally be used as a synonym for identification (e. g.),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> or as in "determination slips".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Identification methods may be manual or <a href="/wiki/Automated_species_identification" title="Automated species identification">computerized</a> and may involve using <a href="/wiki/Identification_key" title="Identification key">identification keys</a>, browsing through fields guide that contain (often illustrated) species accounts, comparing the organism with specimens from natural history collections, or taking images to be analyzed and compared against a <a href="/wiki/Training" title="Training">pre-trained</a> <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_base" title="Knowledge base">knowledge base</a> with species information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
