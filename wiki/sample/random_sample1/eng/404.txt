<p>The <b>1st <a href="/wiki/Asian_Youth_Games" title="Asian Youth Games">Asian Youth Games</a></b> was held in Singapore from June 29, 2009 to July 7, 2009 in over 90 sporting events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The plan for the Asian Youth Games was part of Singapore's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_Youth_Olympics_bids" title="2010 Youth Olympics bids">bid</a> to stage the <a href="/wiki/2010_Summer_Youth_Olympics" title="2010 Summer Youth Olympics">2010 Summer Youth Olympics</a> (YOG).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo1-2">[2]</a></sup> The purpose was to allow youths to use the chance to benchmark their performances against youth teams from Asian nations. The Games also provided Singapore with an opportunity to test organisational and logistical capabilities in advance of the 2010 YOG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The organisation of the Games, spearheaded by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singapore_Sports_Council" title="Singapore Sports Council">Singapore Sports Council</a>, cost S$15 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> which was part of the $130 million for YOG.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Torch_relay">Torch relay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Asian_Youth_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Torch relay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1st Asian Youth Games torch relay started on June 28 morning with the <i>Community and Corporate Leg</i>. The flame lighting ceremony was held on June 28 at the <a href="/wiki/Kallang" title="Kallang">Kallang</a> Waterfront. The flame is then carried across the island in buses to arrive at the start of the three routes, named after the Olympic values of Friendship, Excellence and Respect. A total of 70 torchbearers were involved in day one of the relay.</p>
<p>The torch relay resumed on June 29 for the <i>Schools leg</i>, with the flame travelling to 45 schools around Singapore, where over a hundred runners had the honour of carrying the torch.</p>
<p>The relay ended at the Singapore Indoor Stadium where three athletes ran in with the torches and light the <a href="/wiki/Cauldron" title="Cauldron">cauldron</a> as part of the Opening Ceremony celebrations.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theme_song">Theme song</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Asian_Youth_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Theme song">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The theme song entitled <i>Asia's Youth, Our Future</i> was unveiled on March 19, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song carries the message of hope and friendship and was composed by musician <a href="/wiki/Iskandar_Ismail" title="Iskandar Ismail">Iskandar Ismail</a>, and written jointly by Jose Raymond and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hoo_Cher_Liek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hoo Cher Liek (page does not exist)">Hoo Cher Liek</a>.</p>
<p>The singers are <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Hartono" title="Nathan Hartono">Nathan Hartono</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lian_Kim_Selby&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lian Kim Selby (page does not exist)">Lian Kim Selby</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Emblem">Emblem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Asian_Youth_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Emblem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The official <a href="/wiki/Emblem" title="Emblem">emblem</a> of the 2009 Asian Youth Games was unveiled on November 14, 2008 and designed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brainwave_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brainwave Design (page does not exist)">Brainwave Design</a>. The logo, as described by <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Council_of_Asia" title="Olympic Council of Asia">Olympic Council of Asia</a>, represents excellence and victory that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> will bring during the Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Asian_Youth_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Frasia</i>, which means Friends of Asia, is the name of the official <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">mascot</a> for the 1st Asian Youth Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The mascot embodies the values and spirit of the Asian Youth Games. The sprightly lion exemplifies friendship, respect and excellence. It constitutes a spirited representation of young hearts and minds in pursuit of sporting excellence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast">Broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Asian_Youth_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The sports events were broadcast 'live' primarily via the official website through 4 concurrent 'live' streams during the games period. It was the first multi-sport event to receive approval from the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Council_of_Asia" title="Olympic Council of Asia">Olympic Council of Asia</a> to provide broadcast coverage of all the sports events completely via <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Digital_channels&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Digital channels (page does not exist)">digital channels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Singapore's television channel, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MediaCorp_Channel_5" title="MediaCorp Channel 5">MediaCorp Channel 5</a> provided daily highlights of the Games. <a href="/wiki/StarHub_TV" title="StarHub TV">StarHub TV</a> had 4 dedicated TV channels to provide coverage for the Games.</p>
<p>The opening ceremony was broadcast 'live' via the website. In addition, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Video_On-Demand&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Video On-Demand (page does not exist)">Video On-Demand</a> Clips capturing key sporting highlights of the various games were made available.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/NTUC_Downtown_East" title="NTUC Downtown East">NTUC Downtown East</a> originally was the planned Games Village for athletes and officials. However, a decision was made to move the Games Village to central Singapore at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swissotel_The_Stamford" title="Swissotel The Stamford">Swissotel The Stamford</a>. The change was made as <a href="/wiki/NTUC_Downtown_East" title="NTUC Downtown East">NTUC Downtown East</a> does not have enough beds for the athletes. Due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H1N1" title="H1N1">H1N1</a> outbreak, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swissotel_The_Stamford" title="Swissotel The Stamford">Swissotel The Stamford</a> catered the ninth floor to suspect cases in athletes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Opening_ceremony">Opening ceremony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Asian_Youth_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Opening ceremony">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Themed <i>Asia's Youth, Our Future</i>, the inaugural Asian Youth Games opening ceremony on June 29 was held in the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Indoor_Stadium" title="Singapore Indoor Stadium">Singapore Indoor Stadium</a>. More than 1,400 performers from 20 schools and tertiary institutions are taking part in the opening ceremony. The opening ceremony was graced by Singapore Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hsien_Loong" title="Lee Hsien Loong">Lee Hsien Loong</a>. Representatives from the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Council_of_Asia" title="Olympic Council of Asia">Olympic Council of Asia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_National_Olympic_Council" title="Singapore National Olympic Council">Singapore National Olympic Council</a> were also be present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> There were three segments which were showcased: 'Asia's Zest', 'Garden in the City', and 'Spirit Of Evolution'. The AYG cauldron was lit by three Singaporean young athletes, Tao Li (swimming), Remy Ong (bowling), and Jasmine Ser (shooting).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Asian_Youth_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Attempts were made to stage all 26 YOG sports but, due to time and resource constraints, Singapore and the Olympic Council of Asia trimmed it to nine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ssc1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-ssc1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>An athlete from <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> who was participating in the football matches came down with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H1N1" title="H1N1">H1N1</a> flu.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> causing the Group A football preliminary match between Philippines and Chinese Taipei due to be played at Meridian Junior College at 5pm (1700hrs) on June 20, 2009 to be suspended till further notice. The player's condition is stable, according to the AYG2009 website.</p>
<p>Four cases of AH1N1 flu virus were discovered from the Hong Kong booters on June 22.</p>
<p>Malaysia has withdrawn most of its athletes (with the exception of their sailors) due to concerns over the H1N1 outbreak.</p>
