<p><i><b>Dongchimi</b></i> is a variety of <a href="/wiki/Kimchi" title="Kimchi">kimchi</a> consisting of <a href="/wiki/Korean_radish" title="Korean radish">Korean radish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Napa_cabbage" title="Napa cabbage">napa cabbage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scallion" title="Scallion">scallions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jangajji" title="Jangajji">pickled</a> green <a href="/wiki/Chili_pepper" title="Chili pepper">chilli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ginger" title="Ginger">ginger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pyrus_pyrifolia" title="Pyrus pyrifolia">Korean pear</a> and watery <a href="/wiki/Brine" title="Brine">brine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Korean_cuisine" title="Korean cuisine">Korean cuisine</a>. As the name <i>dong</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">hangul</a>: ë; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">hanja</a>: <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%86%AC" title="wikt:å¬">å¬</a>; literally "winter") and <i>chimi</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">hangul</a>: ì¹ë¯¸, an ancient term for kimchi), suggests, this kimchi is traditionally consumed during the winter season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dongchimi is fermented like other varieties of kimchi, but its maturing period is relatively short (2â3 days). Although it can be made at any time of the year, it is usually made during the <i><a href="/wiki/Gimjang" title="Gimjang">gimjang</a></i> season. The northern regions consisting of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamgyeong-do" title="Hamgyeong-do">Hamgyeong-do</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyeongan-do" title="Pyeongan-do">Pyeongan-do</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> are particularly famous for their dongchimi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The clear and clean taste of the watery dongchimi is used as a soup for making <i>dongchimi guksu</i> (ëì¹ë¯¸êµ­ì cold noodle soup made with <i>dongchimi</i>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Naengmyeon" title="Naengmyeon">naengmyeon</a></i>, or served with <i><a href="/wiki/Tteok" title="Tteok">tteok</a></i> or steamed <a href="/wiki/Sweet_potato" title="Sweet potato">sweet potatoes</a> to balance out the rich flavors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ingredients">Ingredients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongchimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ingredients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Korean_radish" title="Korean radish">Radish</a> is the most important ingredient in dongchimi. Whole green or red peppers can be added as decoration but are not required. Leaf mustard, garlics, gingers and leeks, as well as other salted ingredients may be included.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dongchimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>Baechu dongchimi</b> (Cabbage dongchimi, ë°°ì¶ëì¹ë¯¸)</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Korean_radish" title="Korean radish">Radish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Napa_cabbage" title="Napa cabbage">napa cabbage</a> stuffed with ingredients such as julienned radish, Korean chives, red peppers, garlic, and ginger is pickled in brine. Kelp stock is added for deep flavor before fermentation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Daenamu dongchimi</b> (Bamboo dongchimi, ëëë¬´ëì¹ë¯¸)</li>
</ul>
<p>Bamboo leaves are used as a main ingredient, giving the dongchimi a crisp and clean taste while adding texture. The fermentation process takes longer than other varieties of dongchimi due to its lower sodium content but it can also be stored much longer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It is a local specialty of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Jeolla_province" title="South Jeolla province">South Jeolla province</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Gungjung dongchimi</b> (Royal dongchimi, ê¶ì¤ ëì¹ë¯¸)</li>
</ul>
<p>Made from small radish, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuja" title="Yuja">yuja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pomegranate" title="Pomegranate">pomegranate</a>, and Korean pears are added for a fragrant version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are many other variations depending on region and personal taste.</p>
