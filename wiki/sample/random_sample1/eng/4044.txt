<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dong_(Chinese_surname)" title="Dong (Chinese surname)">Dong</a>.</div>
<p><b>Dong Zhuo</b> (died 22 May 192),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> <b>Zhongying</b>, was a politician and warlord who lived in the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Han_Dynasty" title="Eastern Han Dynasty">Eastern Han Dynasty</a>. He seized control of the capital <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a> in 189 when it was in a state of turmoil following the death of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ling_of_Han" title="Emperor Ling of Han">Emperor Ling</a> and a clash between the <a href="/wiki/Ten_Attendants" title="Ten Attendants">eunuch faction</a> and some court officials led by General-in-Chief <a href="/wiki/He_Jin" title="He Jin">He Jin</a>. Dong Zhuo subsequently deposed <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Hongnong" title="Prince of Hongnong">Emperor Shao</a> and instated <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xian_of_Han" title="Emperor Xian of Han">Emperor Xian</a>.</p>
<p>Dong Zhuo rose to power in the Han imperial court and ruled the nation with tyranny and cruelty for a brief period of time. The following year, a coalition of regional officials and warlords launched a <a href="/wiki/Campaign_against_Dong_Zhuo" title="Campaign against Dong Zhuo">punitive campaign</a> against Dong, forcing him to move the capital to <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a>. Dong Zhuo was assassinated in 192 by his foster son <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BC_Bu" title="Lü Bu">Lü Bu</a> as part of a plot orchestrated by Interior Minister <a href="/wiki/Wang_Yun_(Han_dynasty)" title="Wang Yun (Han dynasty)">Wang Yun</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dong Zhuo was born in Lintao, Longxi <a href="/wiki/Commandery_(China)" title="Commandery (China)">Commandery</a> (present-day <a href="/wiki/Min_County" title="Min County">Min County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a>) and was said to be a chivalrous youth who was excellent in horseback archery in his early days. He travelled around the <a href="/wiki/Qiang_people" title="Qiang people">Qiang</a> region and befriended many men of gallantry. When he grew up, he returned and started farming in the countryside, where he incidentally excavated a blade, which had obscure inscription fading from it which said "slash the kings like logging." When he took the sabre to the famed scholar <a href="/wiki/Cai_Yong" title="Cai Yong">Cai Yong</a> for appraisal, the latter asserted to him that it was the blade of the Hegemon-King of Western Chu, <a href="/wiki/Xiang_Yu" title="Xiang Yu">Xiang Yu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As a physically adept man with a good background, Dong became an imperial guard, and joined <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zhang_Huan_(Han_Dynasty)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zhang Huan (Han Dynasty) (page does not exist)">Zhang Huan</a>'s campaign against Qiang rebels in <a href="/wiki/Bing_Province" title="Bing Province">Bing Province</a> as a Major. He was rewarded with 9,000 rolls of fine silk for his performance, all of which he distributed to his colleagues and subordinates.</p>
<p>Dong was sent to quell the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Turban_Rebellion" title="Yellow Turban Rebellion">Yellow Turban Rebellion</a> in the early 180s after a few subsequent promotions but he was defeated by the rebels and demoted. When the <a href="/wiki/Liang_Province_Rebellion" title="Liang Province Rebellion">tribes of Qiang and Hu rebelled</a> with local gentries <a href="/wiki/Han_Sui" title="Han Sui">Han Sui</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bian_Zhang" title="Bian Zhang">Bian Zhang</a> in Liang Province, Dong was reinstated as Knight General and sent to suppress the rebels. During a battle with the Qiang tribes, Dong Zhuo's army was outnumbered and a river sealed his retreat route. To prevent his army from being routed by the enemy, Dong ordered his troops to dam the river and pretend to fish in the reservoir formed. He sent his men to cross the drained lower stream and break the dam in order to thwart any subsequent pursuits by the enemy. Despite failing the campaign, Dong's unit was the only unscathed unit of the Han forces.</p>
<p>Dong Zhuo was henceforth promoted to General of the Van and Inspector of Bing Province. However, he refused to accept his new post as he was unwilling to leave his troops and subordinates back in Liang Province. Realizing the Han authority was declining from its failure in subjugating the Qiang tribe, Dong focused on building his power in Liang. At the time, a Han military officer, <a href="/wiki/Sun_Jian" title="Sun Jian">Sun Jian</a>, suggested to his superior that Dong's arrogance and insubordination to the court warranted a death sentence, but his advice was not heeded.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_to_power">Rise to power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rise to power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Following the death of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ling_of_Han" title="Emperor Ling of Han">Emperor Ling</a> in 189, General-in-Chief <a href="/wiki/He_Jin" title="He Jin">He Jin</a> ordered Dong Zhuo to lead troops into <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a> to aid him in eliminating the <a href="/wiki/Ten_Attendants" title="Ten Attendants">eunuch faction</a>. Before Dong's arrival, He Jin was assassinated by the eunuchs and the capital city fell into a state of turmoil. The eunuchs took <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Hongnong" title="Prince of Hongnong">Emperor Shao</a> hostage and fled from Luoyang. Dong Zhuo's army intercepted the eunuchs and brought the emperor back to the palace.</p>
<p>At the same time, He Jin's half-brother, General of Chariots and Cavalry <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=He_Miao&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="He Miao (page does not exist)">He Miao</a> (何苗), was killed by his subordinates after they accused him of colluding with the eunuchs. He Jin and He Miao's forces were without a leader and came under Dong Zhuo's command when the latter led his ferocious <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Liang_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Liang Province (page does not exist)">Liang Province</a> troops into the city. Dong also instigated <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BC_Bu" title="Lü Bu">Lü Bu</a> to kill the latter's foster father and Imperial Commandant of Capital Guards, <a href="/wiki/Ding_Yuan" title="Ding Yuan">Ding Yuan</a>, when the Commandant disagreed on his proposal to replace <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Hongnong" title="Prince of Hongnong">Emperor Shao</a> with a younger <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xian_of_Han" title="Emperor Xian of Han">Liu Xie</a>. Lü then became Dong's adopted son and trusted aide, and assisted Dong to take total control of the imperial capital of Luoyang. At the onset of his arrival to Luoyang, the powerful gentries and eastern warlords did not wish to see Dong becoming influential, and Dong was aware of that and told his generals to lead troops out of the city at night and re-enter through the front-gate at noon. Thus, the lords perceived Dong as possessing a sizeable army and dared not make a move, and Dong was able to grab power using a series of plots deemed unjust in most Confucians' eyes.</p>
<p>In 190, Dong deposed Emperor Shao and replaced him with the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xian_of_Han" title="Emperor Xian of Han">Prince of Chenliu</a>, who became known as Emperor Xian. Dong declared himself <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_China" title="Chancellor of China">Chancellor</a> and monopolized state power. He was given special permission to carry his sword to the Imperial Court while others were forbidden to do so. This privilege had not been granted to any state official since <a href="/wiki/Xiao_He" title="Xiao He">Xiao He</a> in <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Gaozu_of_Han" title="Emperor Gaozu of Han">Emperor Gaozu</a>'s time. The Chancellor was also allowed to enter the court without removing his footwear. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanguozhi" title="Sanguozhi">Records of the Three Kingdoms</a></i> recorded an incident in which Dong Zhuo led his troops to <a href="/wiki/Luoyang#History" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a> (洛陽城) and ordered them to kill all the male inhabitants and loot the city, under the pretext of eliminating a rebel army. It was said that Dong Zhuo even slept on the emperor's bed and slept with palace maids.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coalition_against_Dong_Zhuo">Coalition against Dong Zhuo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coalition against Dong Zhuo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Campaign_against_Dong_Zhuo" title="Campaign against Dong Zhuo">Campaign against Dong Zhuo</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dong_Zhuo_retreats_to_Chang.27an">Dong Zhuo retreats to Chang'an</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dong Zhuo retreats to Chang'an">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the same year, regional officials and warlords around the country formed <a href="/wiki/Campaign_against_Dong_Zhuo" title="Campaign against Dong Zhuo">a coalition force</a> and launched a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punitive_campaign" title="Punitive campaign">punitive campaign</a> against Dong Zhuo. In response, he sent a detachment to intercept the coalition vanguard led by Sun Jian, and ordered his son-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Niu_Fu" title="Niu Fu">Niu Fu</a>, to develop the fortress of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mei_County,_Shaanxi" title="Mei County, Shaanxi">Mei</a>, where Niu stored 30 years of food supply. After his subordinates, <a href="/wiki/Hua_Xiong" title="Hua Xiong">Hua Xiong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hu_Zhen" title="Hu Zhen">Hu Zhen</a>, and Lu Bu, were defeated by Sun Jian at Yangren, Dong Zhuo sent an eloquent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Jue_(Han_Dynasty)" title="Li Jue (Han Dynasty)">Li Jue</a> to propose a marriage between Sun's son and Dong's daughter, the lobbyist promised Sun Jian that the country could be split between the two families. But the vanguard general rebutted Li Jue strongly, and made further preparation to attack Luoyang. Thus, Dong evacuated everyone in Luoyang and moved them to the new capital city of <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a> (present-day <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>) in the west. Before the relocation, Dong ordered his troops to excavate the tombs of the late Han emperors for treasures and sent his men to seize valuables from the wealthier residents in Luoyang, as well as burn down the palaces and anything that might be useful to the allied forces.</p>
<p>Then, the chancellor assembled his forces in the city and personally led them to ambush the approaching army. When Sun Jian advanced to the imperial graveyard, he was intercepted by Dong, who had been awaiting him. However, Dong Zhuo was unable to suppress his archrival, and was routed during the combat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He then ordered Lu Bu to lead a cavalry force back to the city to halt Sun's progress before he took flight for <a href="/wiki/Mianchi_County" title="Mianchi County">Mianchi</a>. Lu Bu and his knights had gotten back to Luoyang before Sun Jian arrived, but they were unable to hold the eastern third gate (宣陽城門) when the latter attacked, and a fight inside the city was ensured, wherein Lu Bu was defeated once more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Defeat_of_the_coalition">Defeat of the coalition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Defeat of the coalition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After their capture of Luoyang, the allied forces found nothing but a scene of ruins, so they retreated back and awaited further development. Dong Zhuo then sent his skilled generals, Li Jue, <a href="/wiki/Guo_Si" title="Guo Si">Guo Si</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhang_Ji_(Han_Dynasty)" title="Zhang Ji (Han Dynasty)">Zhang Ji</a> to the frontline against the eastern warlords. At the time the alliance was having internal conflicts and did not focus on fighting them, so the senior imperial officer, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhu_Jun_(Han_Dynasty)" title="Zhu Jun (Han Dynasty)">Zhu Jun</a> could only ask his old friend, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tao_Qian_(Han_Dynasty)" title="Tao Qian (Han Dynasty)">Tao Qian</a> to give him a hand in a futile effort to fight the forces of Dong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Tao, despite having a general alliance with Dong, indeed sent 3,000 elite danyang troops to Zhu to battle Dong forces at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhongmu" title="Zhongmu">Zhongmu</a>, where Zhu was totally crushed. Li and his comrades then performed raids around area of <a href="/wiki/Chenliu" title="Chenliu">Chenliu</a> and Yingchuan, wherein <a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a> and the likes were unable to stop them, and many residents there were hijacked and enslaved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The soldiers of the coalition were quite terrified by the creative and insane treatment Dong designed for them, if captured: he would have the captives tied up with fat-soaked clothes, and started a fire from their foot; as the captives' heads were unbound, he could enjoy the screams and facial expressions from them, however, such a hobby was not shared by most of the educated imperial officials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rule_of_terror">Rule of terror</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rule of terror">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since the warlords were far from able to stop him separately, Dong Zhuo's autocracy started to stabilize in the new capital. Two months after he moved the capital, Dong Zhuo revived and held the title of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Perceptor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Grand Perceptor (page does not exist)">Grand Master</a>, an antiquated title once reinvigorated by <a href="/wiki/Wang_Mang" title="Wang Mang">Wang Mang</a>, but was scrapped in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Han" title="Eastern Han">Eastern Han</a>. He also appointed his younger brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dong_Min" title="Dong Min">Dong Min</a> as General of the Left, and gave official posts to several of his kin. Dong Zhuo had also been constantly building up his personal castle in Mei County near Chang'an, where he thought he could outlive the coalition even if the latter worked together again and waged war on him. He also threw lavish banquets, during which savage tortures would be performed on captured enemies as entertainment: he would first cut their tongue so they wouldn't make much noise for the following operations, which were the severing of limbs and removal of eyeballs. The operations required professional skill because the betrayers would remain conscious when they were thrown into boiling oil. Now, a literal "meat-ball" would be rolling around the center of the banquet for all to observe. Dong was quite comfortable with his "masterpiece" and enjoyed his meal as usual, while the rest of the audience were said to have experienced a high degree of discomfort.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Dong would also dish out cruel punishments to his political enemies; when he was in Luoyang, he wiped out the entire Yuan clan within the city, because <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Shao" title="Yuan Shao">Yuan Shao</a> was the leader of the coalition against him. Within two years, thousands of public servants were wrongly accused and executed, and numerous commoners were kidnapped or killed under his tyrannical rule. In order to purchase materials for further development of his invincible Mei citadel, he had bells and bronze statues, among which were nine of the <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Metal_Colossi" title="Twelve Metal Colossi">Twelve Metal Colossi</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> melted and recast into coins. This flooded the market and caused a serious <a href="/wiki/Inflation" title="Inflation">inflation</a>, rendering the currency useless. His venomous policies had greatly reduced Han authority and raised resentment among most gentries and commoners of the time (while the Liang armies adored him like a god).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Downfall_and_death">Downfall and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Downfall and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dong Zhuo was fully aware that his provocative actions and reckless behavior had aroused the anger of many, and he was at a high risk of being assassinated by enemies, so he ordered his strongest warrior and adopted son, <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BC_Bu" title="Lü Bu">Lü Bu</a> as a personal bodyguard, who shadowed him most of the time. When Dong threw a temper, he would hurl a halberd at Lü Bu who would dodge the weapon every time, and Dong's anger would quickly subside. Nonetheless, Lü bore a furtive displeasure for his adoptive father for that. Besides, as Lü had access to Dong's residence, he had been having an amorous affair with one of Dong's chambermaids, and the couple were constantly in fear of being discovered.</p>
<p>In 192, with encouragement from Interior Minister <a href="/wiki/Wang_Yun_(Han_dynasty)" title="Wang Yun (Han dynasty)">Wang Yun</a>, Lü Bu made his decision to kill Dong Zhuo. One morning, Lü greeted Dong at the palace gate with a dozen trusted men led by Cavalry Captain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Su_(Han_Dynasty)" title="Li Su (Han Dynasty)">Li Su</a>. Li stepped forward and stabbed Dong. Dong cried out for Lü Bu to save him, but Lü merely answered, "This is an imperial order," after which he delivered a fatal blow to Dong. It was recorded that Dong's corpse was left on the streets for people to enjoy the sight of a lit corpse: The official guarding the corpse lit a wick on Dong's <a href="/wiki/Navel" title="Navel">navel</a> (belly) and it burned for days with the corpse's fats as fuel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> But a special provision stating anyone who went forth to collect the body would be killed. However, three officials including the famous Cai Yong still challenged the order and were executed. Meanwhile, all the affiliates of Dong clan were put to death, including Dong's 90-year-old mother, who screamed for her amnesty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Dong Zhuo's death, several of his loyalists, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Jue_(Three_Kingdoms)" title="Li Jue (Three Kingdoms)">Li Jue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guo_Si" title="Guo Si">Guo Si</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhang_Ji_(Han_Dynasty)" title="Zhang Ji (Han Dynasty)">Zhang Ji</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fan_Chou" title="Fan Chou">Fan Chou</a>, escaped on the belief that their allegiance to him would be considered as <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a>. Wang Yun, who had taken control of the government, heard their appeal for pardon and said, "Of all those who should be forgiven, they are the exceptions." The four then planned to relinquish their positions and go into hiding. However, an advisor named <a href="/wiki/Jia_Xu" title="Jia Xu">Jia Xu</a> suggested they should take this opportunity to launch a strike at Chang'an since the Liang faction was actually unscathed. The four then roused several-thousand hardcore followers to attack Chang'an. Wang sent <a href="/wiki/Xu_Rong_(general)" title="Xu Rong (general)">Xu Rong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hu_Zhen" title="Hu Zhen">Hu Zhen</a>(former members of the Liang faction) to fight the Liang force en route, but Xu was killed in the first encounter and Hu led his troops to join the rebels and the size of the rebel force was inflated to 100,000 when they surrounded the capital. Lu Bu attempted to break the siege, but was defeated outside the city gate, thus Chang'an fell into the hands of Dong Zhuo's followers and Emperor Xian was taken hostage. State power fell into the hands of the Liang faction once again.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Romance_of_the_Three_Kingdoms">In <i>Romance of the Three Kingdoms</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: In Romance of the Three Kingdoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Romance_of_the_Three_Kingdoms" title="Romance of the Three Kingdoms">Romance of the Three Kingdoms</a></i>, a 14th-century historical novel by <a href="/wiki/Luo_Guanzhong" title="Luo Guanzhong">Luo Guanzhong</a>, was a romanticization of the events that occurred before and during the Three Kingdoms era. Because the real-life Dong Zhuo was already much of a cruel and treacherous character, the novel probably could do little more to accentuate that treachery and cruelty. It did, however, on two occasions deviate from the history.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dong_Zhuo_and_the_three_sworn_brothers">Dong Zhuo and the three sworn brothers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Dong Zhuo and the three sworn brothers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dong Zhuo first appeared as early as late in Chapter 1. Being sent to quell the Yellow Turban Rebellion, Dong Zhuo was defeated by the rebel leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhang_Jiao" title="Zhang Jiao">Zhang Jiao</a> and the battle was turning into a rout.</p>
<p>The three newly sworn brothers, <a href="/wiki/Liu_Bei" title="Liu Bei">Liu Bei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guan_Yu" title="Guan Yu">Guan Yu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Fei" title="Zhang Fei">Zhang Fei</a>, happened to be nearby. They then led their forces out to Dong Zhuo's rescue. Suddenly met with this new opposition, the rebels were swept off their feet and had to retreat.</p>
<p>After returning to camp, Dong Zhuo asked the three brothers what offices they currently held. And they replied that they held none. Dong Zhuo harrumphed and then ignored them. This angered Zhang Fei so much that he grabbed his sword and wanted to kill Dong Zhuo. He was however stopped by his two brothers, who suggested taking their service elsewhere. Thus was Dong Zhuo's life spared and the three brothers went their own way.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dong_Zhuo_and_Diaochan">Dong Zhuo and Diaochan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Zhuo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Dong Zhuo and Diaochan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Perhaps the most popular story about Dong Zhuo was the fictional love triangle involving Dong Zhuo, Lü Bu and <a href="/wiki/Diaochan" title="Diaochan">Diaochan</a>, which eventually led to the death of Dong Zhuo in the hands of his own adoptive son, Lü Bu.</p>
<p>After Dong Zhuo moved the capital to the more strategically sound Chang'an, Interior Minister Wang Yun started to contemplate a plot to assassinate the tyrant by using the petite Diaochan, a song girl who was brought up in his household but whom he had been treating like his own daughter, to plant the seed of dissension between Dong Zhuo and Lü Bu.</p>
<p>Inviting Lü Bu over one night, Wang Yun asked Diaochan to serve wine to the guest. Lü Bu was immediately seized by the girl's beauty. Well aware of this, Wang Yun then promised to marry Diaochan to the mighty warrior.</p>
<p>A few days later, however, Wang Yun laid a feast for Dong Zhuo and repeated the feat. Like Lü Bu, Dong Zhuo could not lift his eyes off Diaochan, who also displayed her prowess in song and dance. Dong Zhuo then brought Diaochan home and made her his concubine.</p>
<p>When Lü Bu heard about this early the next morning, he headed for Dong Zhuo's bedroom and peeped in through the window. There he saw Diaochan sitting up grooming her hair while Dong Zhuo was still asleep. Aware of Lü Bu's presence, Diaochan put up a sorrowful expression and pretended to wipe tears off her eyes with a handkerchief.</p>
<p>A similar incident recurred about a month later, but this time Dong Zhuo woke up in time to see Lü Bu staring fixedly at Diaochan. Lü Bu was then thrown out and forbidden to come into the house.</p>
<p>Then one day, while Dong Zhuo was holding a conversation with Emperor Xian, Lü Bu stole to his foster father's residence and met with Diaochan in the Fengyi Pavilion (鳳儀亭). Weeping, Diaochan pleaded with Lü Bu to rescue her from Dong Zhuo. Placing his halberd aside, Lü Bu held Diaochan in his arms and comforted her with words.</p>
<p>Right then, Dong Zhuo returned to find the duo in the pavilion. The startled Lü Bu turned to flee. Dong Zhuo grabbed the halberd and gave chase. Being too slow, Dong Zhuo could not catch up with the agile Lü Bu. He then hurled the halberd at Lü Bu but the latter fended it off and got away.</p>
<p>After the incident, Lü Bu became increasingly displeased with Dong Zhuo. The displeasure was further inflamed by Wang Yun, who suggested subtly that Lü Bu kill Dong Zhuo. Lü Bu was eventually persuaded.</p>
<p>The conspirators sent Li Su to fetch Dong Zhuo from his castle in Meiwu (郿塢) under the pretense that the emperor intended to abdicate the throne to the warlord. The overjoyed Dong Zhuo then came to the palace gate, where his troops were barred from entering. As Dong Zhuo's carriage neared the palace building, soldiers loyal to Wang Yun escorted Dong Zhuo to the trap they set. Then suddenly a general stabbed Dong Zhuo.</p>
<p>Injured only in the arms, Dong Zhuo then cried out for Lü Bu to save him. Lü Bu walked over and impaled Dong Zhuo's throat with his halberd, proclaiming, "I have an imperial decree to slay the rebel!"</p>
