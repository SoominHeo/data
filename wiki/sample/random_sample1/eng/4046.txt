<p>The <b>Timor-Leste national Under-23 football team</b> (also known as Timor-Leste U-23 or Selecao U-23) represents the <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">Timor-Leste</a> in international <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> competitions in the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the Summer Olympics">Olympic Games</a>, <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_Asian_Games" title="Football at the Asian Games">Asian Games</a>, <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_Southeast_Asian_Games" title="Football at the Southeast Asian Games">Southeast Asian Games</a> and any other under-23 international football tournaments. It is controlled by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_de_Futebol_de_Timor-Leste" title="Federação de Futebol de Timor-Leste">Federação de Futebol de Timor-Leste</a>, the governing body of football in the country. Timor Leste is currently one of the weakest teams in the world. They won their first match on 5 November 2011 against <a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a>, scoring 2-1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timor-Leste_national_under-23_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Football established in East Timor or Timor Leste in the Portuguese Colony era, where many local and the Portuguese Official played for enjoyment. After Portuguese leave the island, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> invaded the Island and took their cultured and Identity. East Timor gained independence from Indonesia in 2000 following more than 20 years of occupation at the end of the Portuguese rule, which resulted in a long running battle against Jakarta-led forces. East Timor Football Association was accepted as an associate member of AFC at the 20th AFC Congress in 2002. Timor Leste U-23 was invited to <a href="/wiki/2005_AFF_U-23_Youth_Championship" title="2005 AFF U-23 Youth Championship">2005 AFF U-23 Youth Championship</a>, this was the first appearances for the team to play Under 23 International Football. Selecao U23 did not achieve success at that tournament. In 2009, Selecao U23 enter the first <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2009_Southeast_Asian_Games" title="Football at the 2009 Southeast Asian Games">Southeast Asian Games</a>. Once again the team loss every matches and it was humiliating for the country. The biggest defeat was <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_national_under-23_football_team" title="Malaysia national under-23 football team">Malaysia</a> scoring 11-0 at the 2009 SEA Games.Timor Leste have major improvement at <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2011_Southeast_Asian_Games" title="Football at the 2011 Southeast Asian Games">2011 SEA Games</a>, when they use oversea players from Brazil and Australia whom their parents are exile from <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_invasion_of_East_Timor" title="Indonesian invasion of East Timor">Invasion</a> at 1975. These players contribute success at the two matches at SEA Games; especially, <a href="/wiki/Murilo_de_Almeida" title="Murilo de Almeida">Murilo de Almeida</a> who score 3 goals. Overall the under 23 team did well and was one of the surprise teams of the SEA Games. The team finished third in their group by recording their first win in the SEA Games and finishing above Laos, Brunei and Philippines. The team finished the SEA games with a goal difference of -4. This was a big improvement form the SEA Games at Laos 2001 where the team finished last in their group with no wins from four games and a big goal difference of -28 and only one goal scored.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kits">Kits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timor-Leste_national_under-23_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Kits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Timor-Leste's traditional home kit is red shirts, black shorts and red or black socks. The away kits have featured white or yellow shirts. In <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2009_Southeast_Asian_Games" title="Football at the 2009 Southeast Asian Games">2009 SEA Games</a>, Timor Leste U-23 wear the same feature as <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>'s Home kit but the away kit is Yellow-Blue-Blue. The kits are currently manufactured by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nike_Inc." title="Nike Inc.">Nike</a></p>
