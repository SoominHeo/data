<p><b>Đồng Hới</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Dong_Hoi.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5a/Dong_Hoi.ogg" title="Dong Hoi.ogg">listen</a></span></span>) is the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_B%C3%ACnh_Province" title="Quảng Bình Province">Quảng Bình Province</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_Central_Coast" title="North Central Coast">north central coast</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>. The city's area is 155.71 km<sup>2</sup> (60.12 sq mi). Population as per the 2013 census was 160,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Official-1">[1]</a></sup> It is served by <a href="/wiki/National_Route_1A_(Vietnam)" title="National Route 1A (Vietnam)">National Highway 1A</a>, the <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi_Railway_Station" title="Đồng Hới Railway Station">Đồng Hới Railway Station</a>, and airport. By road, Đồng Hới is 486 kilometres (302 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Hanoi</a>, 195 kilometres (121 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Vinh" title="Vinh">Vinh</a>, 160 kilometres (99 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Hu%E1%BA%BF" title="Huế">Huế</a> and 1,204 kilometres (748 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Hồ Chí Minh City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It borders <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_Ninh_District" title="Quảng Ninh District">Quảng Ninh District</a> on the west and south, the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> on the east, <a href="/wiki/B%E1%BB%91_Tr%E1%BA%A1ch_District" title="Bố Trạch District">Bố Trạch District</a> on the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quangbinh_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-quangbinh-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nuocnonhuyendieu_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nuocnonhuyendieu-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Đồng Hới has a 12-km-long coastline with white sand beaches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is the closest city to <a href="/wiki/Phong_Nha-K%E1%BA%BB_B%C3%A0ng_National_Park" title="Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park">Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>'s <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Natural Heritage Site</a>, 50 km north.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Archaeological excavation in this area proved that humans lived in what is now Quảng Bình province in the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a>. Many artifacts, such as ceramic vases, stone tools, and china, have been unearthed in Quảng Bình.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nuocnonhuyendieu_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-nuocnonhuyendieu-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1926, French archaeologist <a href="/wiki/Madeleine_Colani" title="Madeleine Colani">Madeleine Colani</a> discovered and excavated many artifacts in caves and grottoes in west mountainous areas of Quảng Bình. She concluded that the <a href="/wiki/Hoabinhian" title="Hoabinhian">Hòa Bình culture</a> existed in this region. Through C14 testing, the artifacts dated back to 10,509 (plus or minus 950) ago. From Quy Đạt township (<a href="/wiki/Tuy%C3%AAn_H%C3%B3a_District" title="Tuyên Hóa District">Tuyên Hóa District</a>) to south-west about 150 m, the Hum grotto contains many stone tools and animal stones from an ancient human community. Inside Khai grotto near the Quy Đạt township, similar artifacts were found, including ceramics from <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%C3%B4ng_S%C6%A1n_culture" title="Đông Sơn culture">Đông Sơn culture</a>. Additionally, artifacts of the Stone Age were unearthed in grottoes in the Quảng Bình region. Owners of these artifacts lived in the caves and grottoes and hunted for their food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nuocnonhuyendieu_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-nuocnonhuyendieu-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Human settlement in Đồng Hới can be traced 5,000 years back. Many relics and remnants have been found in Bau Tro, a lake in the city, most of which date to the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a>. Around 2880 BC, the site of modern Đồng Hới was a territory of the Viet Thuong tribe of <a href="/wiki/V%C4%83n_Lang" title="Văn Lang">Văn Lang</a> during the reign of king <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H%C3%B9ng_V%C6%B0%C6%A1ng" title="Hùng Vương">Hùng Vương</a>. The site was a long-disputed territory between the <a href="/wiki/Champa" title="Champa">Champa kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Vi%E1%BB%87t" title="Đại Việt">Đại Việt</a>. It officially became Đại Việt territory in 1069 after <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_Th%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Dng_Ki%E1%BB%87t" title="Lý Thường Kiệt">Lý Thường Kiệt</a> took victory over Champa as a result of the Đại Việt-Champa War (1069).</p>
<p>The area ceased to be the southernmost of Đại Việt following the political marriage of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n_Dynasty" title="Trần Dynasty">Trần Dynasty</a> princess, <a href="/wiki/Huy%E1%BB%81n_Tr%C3%A2n" title="Huyền Trân">Huyền Trân</a>, to Champa king, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jaya_Sinhavarman_III" title="Jaya Sinhavarman III">Jaya Sinhavarman III</a> (Vietnamese: <i>Chế Mân</i>). Princess Huyền Trân was king <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n_Nh%C3%A2n_T%C3%B4ng" title="Trần Nhân Tông">Trần Nhân Tông</a>'s daughter and king Trần Anh Tông's younger sister. Political matches made to acquire land was a traditional practice by Champa kings. Thanks to this marriage, Đại Việt acquired lands (as dowry) of what is now <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_Tr%E1%BB%8B_Province" title="Quảng Trị Province">Quảng Trị Province</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Th%E1%BB%ABa_Thi%C3%AAn%E2%80%93Hu%E1%BA%BF_Province" title="Thừa Thiên–Huế Province">Thừa Thiên–Huế Province</a> (provinces which were then known respectively as Chau O and Chau Ri or Chau Ly).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the time of the <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BB%8Bnh%E2%80%93Nguy%E1%BB%85n_War" title="Trịnh–Nguyễn War">Trịnh–Nguyễn War</a> (1558–1775), Vietnam was divided into two countries: Dang Trong (South) and Dang Ngoai (North) with the <a href="/wiki/Gianh_River" title="Gianh River">Gianh River</a> as frontier line. Đồng Hới was an important fortress of the southern <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_lords" title="Nguyễn lords">Nguyễn lords</a>. The Đồng Hới Wall (Vietnamese: <i>Thành Đồng Hới</i>) was considered the barrier that protected the Nguyễn lords from the attack by the northern <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BB%8Bnh_lords" title="Trịnh lords">Trịnh</a> family.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_Indochina_War" title="First Indochina War">First Indochina War</a> (between the French and the <a href="/wiki/Viet_Minh" title="Viet Minh">Viet Minh</a> in the 1950s), the Đồng Hới airbase was used by the French to attack the Viet Minh in north-central Vietnam and the Laotian <a href="/wiki/Pathet_Lao" title="Pathet Lao">Pathet Lao</a> army in central and southern Laos. During the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, Đồng Hới was heavily devastated by bombardments from the United States <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-52" title="B-52">B-52</a> due to its location near the <a href="/wiki/17th_parallel_north" title="17th parallel north">17th parallel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_Demilitarized_Zone" title="Vietnamese Demilitarized Zone">DMZ</a> between <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 11, 1965 bombing destroyed much of the city. The <a href="/wiki/Tam_T%C3%B2a_Church" title="Tam Tòa Church">Tam Tòa Church</a>, a Catholic cathedral, was severely damaged. Today the bell tower remains near the town center as a monument.</p>

<p>On 19 April 1972, during a major North Vietnam offensive, a task force of four US ships were sailing off the coast of Vietnam — the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Oklahoma_City_(CLG-5)" title="USS Oklahoma City (CLG-5)">USS <i>Oklahoma City</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Sterett_(CG-31)" title="USS Sterett (CG-31)">USS <i>Sterett</i></a>, the <a href="/wiki/USS_Lloyd_Thomas_(DD-764)" title="USS Lloyd Thomas (DD-764)">USS <i>Lloyd Thomas</i></a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Higbee_(DD-806)" title="USS Higbee (DD-806)">USS <i>Higbee</i></a>. They were attacked by three North Vietnamese MiGs in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi" title="Battle of Đồng Hới">battle of Đồng Hới</a>. In an attempt to surprise the task force, the MiGs came in low, described as getting their feet wet. Despite the official stories, they did not surprise the task force, which had spotted them long before engagement range and were ready to shoot. Two ships, the <i>Oklahoma City</i> and the <i>Sterett</i>, had anti-aircraft missiles, while the <i>Higbee</i> and the <i>Lloyd Thomas</i> were armed with dual purpose 5" guns. All ships were at battle stations.</p>
<p>This city is the narrowest land of Vietnam (around 40 km from the east to the west). After the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Saigon" title="Fall of Saigon">fall of Saigon</a> on 30 April 1975, Quảng Bình province was merged into Bình Trị Thiên province (Bình Trị Thiên is the abbreviation of Quảng Bình, Quảng Trị, and Thừa Thiên provinces). In 1990, Bình Trị Thiên was once again separated into three provinces as it had been before. Đồng Hới then became the capital of Quảng Bình province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Đồng Hới is on the National Route 1A, North–South Railway, and <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_Highway" title="Ho Chi Minh Highway">Ho Chi Minh Highway</a>, at the coordinates 17 21' N and 106 10’ E. This city is in the heart of Quảng Bình Province. It borders <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%E1%BB%91_Tr%E1%BA%A1ch" title="Bố Trạch">Bố Trạch</a> on the north, <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_Ninh_District" title="Quảng Ninh District">Quảng Ninh</a> on the south, the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> on the east and <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_Ninh_District" title="Quảng Ninh District">Quảng Ninh</a> on the west. It is 50 km south of <a href="/wiki/Phong_Nha-K%E1%BA%BB_B%C3%A0ng_National_Park" title="Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park">Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park</a>, 50 km north of <a href="/wiki/Bang_Spa" title="Bang Spa">Bang Spa</a>, 180 km south-east of Cha Lo border gate with Laos. It has a coastline of 12 km. <a href="/wiki/Nh%E1%BA%ADt_L%E1%BB%87_River" title="Nhật Lệ River">Nhật Lệ River</a> is the only river that runs through the city. <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A0u_Tr%C3%B3" title="Bàu Tró">Tro Lake</a> is the source of fresh water. The <a href="/wiki/Nh%E1%BA%ADt_L%E1%BB%87_Beach" title="Nhật Lệ Beach">Nhật Lệ Beach</a> is endowed with fine and white sand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quangbinh_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-quangbinh-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are many ways to get to Dong Hoi from Hue, such as local bus at Hue North Bus Station. There are taxis from city center bus station (6 km). The bus fare is around $8. It takes 3.5 hours to drive to Dong Hoi/Quang Binh Town.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like all provinces in north and north central of Vietnam, there are four distinct seasons in Đồng Hới. In the spring, it is cool, humid and foggy. The average annual temperature is 24.40C, average rainfall varies from 1,300 to 4,000 mm, total sunshine hours per year is 1,786 hours, average annual moisture is around 84%. The city has monsoon climate with three types of wind: south-east (gió nồm), south-west (gió nam), and north-east.</p>
<p>Although the city is by the sea, its climate is subject to drought in the summer. This is largely due to the dry south-west wind from <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Thailand" title="Gulf of Thailand">Gulf of Thailand</a> (this flow of wind is wet but when it passes to Vietnam, <a href="/wiki/Annamite_Range" title="Annamite Range">Annamite Range</a> mountain range keeps its humidity to cause rains in Laos but drought in central Vietnam). It is rainy in the fall but the weather is comfortable with the temperature around 26 to 31 degrees Celsius. In the winter, due to the north-east wind blowing from the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> mainland, the temperature may go down below 18 degrees Celsius, and there may be slight rain. The highest precipitation gets highest level in August (118.4 mm), compared to just 38.9 mm in January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Topography_and_geology">Topography and geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Topography and geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city has diverse topographical and geological features, including hill, mountainous, half mountainous, coastal plains and coastal sand dunes.</p>
<p>The hilly region is in the west, stretching from north to south (in parts of communes: Dong Son, Thuan Duc) with an average elevation of 12–15 m, with total area of 64.93 km², 41.7% of the city total area. Residents here live on agriculture, forestry, farming. The soil in this area is poor in nutrition, infertile and subject to continuous erosion due to its slope of 7-10%.</p>
<p>The half hilly region surrounds a plain with an average elevation of 10 m from north-east - north to north-west – south-west and south – south-east.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This region covers commues and wards of Bắc Lý, Nam Lý, Nghĩa Ninh, Bắc Nghĩa, Đức Ninh, Đức Ninh Đông, Lộc Ninh and Phú Hải with total area of 62.87 km², or accounts for 40.2% of the city total area. Residents here lives on industrial, handcraft, trading and a small percentage lives on farming. This region is not very fertile, subject to alum. However, thanks to the heavy distribution of rivers and lakes, ponds and pools, it's better for agriculture than the hilly region. The plain with the average of 2.1 m, with little slope, accounting for 0.2% of the city area (5.76 km²). Most of the city's commercial, administrative and main streets concentrate in this narrow regions. Coastal sand dune area is on the east of the city, with an area of 21.98 km², making up 14,3% of the total area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tmulti tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:384px;max-width:384px">
<div class="tsingle" style="float:left;margin:1px;width:202px;max-width:202px">

<p>Đồng Hới is endowed with beautiful beaches with fine sand and clean water of Nhật Lệ. The Da Nhay and Ly Hoa beaches (60 km north of the city) are popular tourist destinations. The <a href="/wiki/Bang_Spa" title="Bang Spa">Bang Spa</a> (hot mineral spring) is ideal for those who enjoy spa baths. The city is 50 km south of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Heritage" title="World Heritage">World Heritage</a> <a href="/wiki/Phong_Nha-K%E1%BA%BB_B%C3%A0ng_National_Park" title="Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park">Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park</a> which is ideal for cave and grotto exploration and biological research activities (mainly <a href="/wiki/Phong_Nha_Cave" title="Phong Nha Cave">Phong Nha Cave</a> and, from September 2010, <a href="/wiki/Thi%C3%AAn_%C4%90%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Dng_Cave" title="Thiên Đường Cave">Thiên Đường Cave</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C6%A1n_%C4%90o%C3%B2ng_Cave" title="Sơn Đoòng Cave">Sơn Đoòng Cave</a> are available for visitors).</p>
<p>Đồng Hới is included in former battlefield tours where travelers can visit once-dangerous fields along the <a href="/wiki/Annamite_Range" title="Annamite Range">Annamite Range</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_trail" title="Ho Chi Minh trail">Hồ Chí Minh trail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Đồng Hới is accessible by road (National Highway 1A, Hồ Chí Minh Highway), by rail at Đồng Hới Railway Station on the Hà Nội–Hồ Chí Minh City Railway, or by air from Hà Nội or Hồ Chí Minh City at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi_Airport" title="Đồng Hới Airport">Đồng Hới Airport</a>.</p>

<p>Đồng Hới Airport was put into use on 18 May 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and the first commercial flight was from <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Hanoi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Noi_Bai_International_Airport" title="Noi Bai International Airport">Noi Bai International Airport</a>, followed by air link with <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%C3%A2n_S%C6%A1n_Nh%E1%BA%A5t_International_Airport" title="Tân Sơn Nhất International Airport">Tân Sơn Nhất International Airport</a> from 1 July 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city provides tourists with 98 hotels and guest-houses from 1 to 3-star hotels like Cosevco Hotel, Saigon-Quảng Bình Hotel and the 4-star resort of Sun Spa Resort. The cuisine includes seafood, the traditional "<a href="/wiki/Hot_pot" title="Hot pot">hot pot</a>", Vietnamese and European-style meals. In 2005, the city welcomed nearly 300,000 visitors.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Flagstick broke ground on another new project on February 1 – FLC Dong Hoi Golf Links in Quang Binh Province.</p>
<p>A complex of 10 contiguous <a href="/wiki/Golf_course" title="Golf course">golf courses</a>, three clubhouses, more than 1,000 villas, a large resort hotel, commercial village and convention centre is under construction in seaside area of Dong Hoi and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quang_Ninh_District" title="Quang Ninh District">Quang Ninh District</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_Thuy_District" title="Le Thuy District">Le Thuy District</a>, which is scheduled to achieve the completion of all construction of the first phase by November 2016, with spring 2017 set as the opening date for play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Hòn La bay is 4-km<sup>2</sup>, with the depth up to 15 m, suitable to develop into a deep-water sea port. A port is currently (as of 2006<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>) under construction north of Đồng Hới. When finished it will be able to accommodate ships up to 50,000 metric tons, on a 32ha of land, has a loading capacity of 10-12 million tonnes per year and is expected to facilitate business for Quảng Bình's industrial and economic zones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two industrial parks (north-west Đồng Hới Industrial Park and Hòn La Industrial Park) are under construction and partially available to investors. The city achieved an average economic growth rate of 12.32% during 2010–2013 period. The economic contributors for 2013 were as follows: industry and construction accounting for 35.5%, services 50.9%, agriculture-forestry-fishery 13.6%. GDP per capita in 2015 was $3871,3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_H%E1%BB%9Bi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Đồng Hới is home to <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_B%C3%ACnh_University" title="Quảng Bình University">Quảng Bình University</a>, a multidisciplinary university. There are several schools here, notably <a href="/wiki/V%C3%B5_Nguy%C3%AAn_Gi%C3%A1p_Gifted_High_School" title="Võ Nguyên Giáp Gifted High School">Võ Nguyên Giáp Gifted High School</a>, and Đào Duy Từ High School.</p>
