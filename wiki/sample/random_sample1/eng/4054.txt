<p><b>Döbling</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈdøːblɪŋ]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:De-at_D%C3%B6bling.ogg" title="File:De-at Döbling.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/09/De-at_D%C3%B6bling.ogg" title="De-at Döbling.ogg">listen</a>)</small>) is the 19th <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Vienna" title="Districts of Vienna">District</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i>19. Bezirk, Döbling, Doebling</i>). It is located on the north end from the central districts, north of the districts <a href="/wiki/Alsergrund" title="Alsergrund">Alsergrund</a> and <a href="/wiki/W%C3%A4hring" title="Währing">Währing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GVD_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GVD-2">[2]</a></sup> Döbling has some heavily populated urban areas with many residential buildings, and borders the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Woods" title="Vienna Woods">Vienna Woods</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StatA_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-StatA-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GVD_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GVD-2">[2]</a></sup> It hosts some of the most expensive residential areas such as <a href="/wiki/Grinzing" title="Grinzing">Grinzing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sievering" title="Sievering">Sievering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neustift_am_Walde" title="Neustift am Walde">Neustift am Walde</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kaasgraben&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kaasgraben (page does not exist)">Kaasgraben</a> and is also the site of a large number of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heurigen" title="Heurigen">Heurigen</a></i> restaurants. There are also some large <i><a href="/wiki/Gemeindebau" title="Gemeindebau">Gemeindebauten</a></i>, including Vienna's most famous one, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karl-Marx-Hof" title="Karl-Marx-Hof">Karl-Marx-Hof</a>.</p>
<p>Also located in Döbling is the <a href="/wiki/American_International_School_of_Vienna" title="American International School of Vienna">American International School of Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lauder_Business_School" title="Lauder Business School">Lauder Business School</a> and Q19 Shopping Center.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Döbling is located in the northwest of <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> and spans the slope of the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Woods" title="Vienna Woods">Wienerwald</a> (Vienna Forest) to the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danube_Canal" title="Danube Canal">Danube Canal</a> that make up the border of the district in the east. The Danube forms the border between Döbling and the district <a href="/wiki/Floridsdorf" title="Floridsdorf">Floridsdorf</a>, and the canal forms the border to the district <a href="/wiki/Brigittenau" title="Brigittenau">Brigittenau</a>. At the Gürtel Bridge, over the Donau Canal, the district border splits off and separates Döbling in the south along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%C3%BCrtel,_Vienna" title="Gürtel, Vienna">Vienna Belt</a> from the district <a href="/wiki/Alsergrund" title="Alsergrund">Alsergrund</a>. At the Schrottenbachgasse the district border branches towards the northwest and separates Döbling from the district <a href="/wiki/W%C3%A4hring" title="Währing">Währing</a> along the line Währinger Park-Hasenauerstraße-Peter Jordan Straße-Starkfriedgasse -Sommerhaidenweg. There is then a short border to the district <a href="/wiki/Hernals" title="Hernals">Hernals</a> at the edge of Vienna.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Space_allocation">Space allocation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Space allocation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nearly 32.6% of the Döbling district area is <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">land</a> (compared to Vienna city-wide as 33.3%). To 85.2% involves Housing areas; the proportion of Betriebsbaugebiete, as 2.2% of the district area, is very low (Vienna 7.6%). With a greenspace share as 51.8% (48.3% for Vienna), Döbling is the 5th greenist district of Vienna. It accounts for 14.9% of district land as agricultural land, while the <a href="/wiki/Vineyard" title="Vineyard">vineyard</a> here plays the biggest role around <a href="/wiki/Grinzing" title="Grinzing">Grinzing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nussdorf,_Vienna" title="Nussdorf, Vienna">Nußdorf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sievering" title="Sievering">Sievering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neustift_am_Walde" title="Neustift am Walde">Neustift am Walde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salmannsdorf" title="Salmannsdorf">Salmannsdorf</a>. Further 25.4% of the district is <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forested</a>, plus 5.3% in meadows, 2.7% in small gardens, 2.5% in parks, and 0.9% as sports and recreational areas. The remainder district territory has 11.0% in traffic/transport and 4.6% on water. While the proportion of water is higher in relation to the whole city of Vienna, the proportion on traffic is below.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hills">Hills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Across the large proportion of the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Woods" title="Vienna Woods">Vienna Woods</a> in Döbling, lie numerous hills of Vienna. Many lie on the border with <a href="/wiki/Lower_Austria" title="Lower Austria">Lower Austria</a> and the neighboring districts. The highest summit is <a href="/wiki/Hermannskogel" title="Hermannskogel">Hermannskogel</a> (542 m, 1778 ft; even highest of Vienna) with an outlook tower; however, the symbols of Döbling are the <a href="/wiki/Kahlenberg" title="Kahlenberg">Kahlenberg</a> (484 m, 1588 ft) with an outlook and a radio mast, and the nearby <a href="/wiki/Leopoldsberg" title="Leopoldsberg">Leopoldsberg</a> (427 m, 1401 ft). Other hills in this region are: the Reisenberg, Latisberg, Vogelsangberg, Dreimarkstein and Nußberg. Besides, there are partially built-up elevations in Döbling, such as Hohe Warte in <a href="/wiki/Heiligenstadt,_Vienna" title="Heiligenstadt, Vienna">Heiligenstadt</a>, the Hungerberg in <a href="/wiki/Grinzing" title="Grinzing">Grinzing</a>, and the Hackenberg in <a href="/wiki/Sievering" title="Sievering">Sievering</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Waterways">Waterways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Waterways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the district zone, numerous streams originate, but now are mostly hard-installed or led as stream canals. Originally they all flowed, with the exception of the Waldbach (forest stream), into the Danube canal. Because the catchment areas of the streams lie in the sandstone zone of the Viennese forest, the streams can and were able to grow on a multiple of its normal water quantity. This led again and again to destructive flood waters, especially along the Krottenbach. The Krottenbach was the most important stream in Döbling, and is led practically perfectly as a stream canal. It includes the area behind the federal secondary school Billrothstraße, underground, and through Sievering, joins into the Arbesbach (Erbsenbach) stream, which today in the upper run, still flows openly until Obersievering.</p>
<p>The Nesselbach passes yet to the Krapfenwaldl openly, before it unites underground with the Reisenbergbach stream in <a href="/wiki/Grinzing" title="Grinzing">Grinzing</a>. The Reisenbergbach stream passes openly at the same time yet until shortly before the center of Grinzing. Almost entirely in the open, the Schreiberbach stream passes up to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nu%C3%9Fdorf,_Vienna" title="Nußdorf, Vienna">Nußdorf</a> and the Waldbach stream at Kahlenbergerdorf. Through diverting water, the Döblinger Bach stream has entirely disappeared. This sprang originally in the Cottage and flowed in the Spittelau into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danube_Canal" title="Danube Canal">Danube Canal</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Döbling was first mentioned in 1114 as <i>"de Teopilic"</i>. The name derives from the Slavic <i>* topl’ika</i> ("swampy waters" or "swampy place").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The name "Döbling" relates to the lake of the Krottenbach stream, while further possibility of interpretation derives from <a href="/wiki/Slavic_languages" title="Slavic languages">Old Slavic</a> <i>Toplica</i> ( "warm stream"). Later spellings of the place-name were for example <i>Toblich</i>, <i>Töbling</i> and <i>Tepling</i>. In the formation of the district 1890/92, the name was finally "Döbling", from the largest municipality, <a href="/wiki/Oberd%C3%B6bling" title="Oberdöbling">Oberdöbling</a>, in the incorporated district.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="D.C3.B6bling_in_antiquity">Döbling in antiquity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Döbling in antiquity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The district Döbling had been settled over 5,000 years ago, while the area Döbling-<a href="/wiki/Nussdorf,_Vienna" title="Nussdorf, Vienna">Nußdorf</a>-<a href="/wiki/Heiligenstadt,_Vienna" title="Heiligenstadt, Vienna">Heiligenstadt</a> next to the area <a href="/wiki/Simmering" title="Simmering">Simmering</a>-<a href="/wiki/Landstra%C3%9Fe" title="Landstraße">Landstraße</a> probably represents the oldest settlement area in the Vienna area. Known is that on the <a href="/wiki/Leopoldsberg" title="Leopoldsberg">Leopoldsberg</a> hill, a weapon-bearing village with a fortified tower existed in which the inhabitants of surrounding villages gathered at risk. About the residents of that time, little is known; science refers to them as makers of "Donauländischen" (Danube-land) culture; they were not however <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indo-Germanic" title="Indo-Germanic">Indo-Germanic</a>. These penetrated into the Vienna area until one thousand years later, where the resident population mixed with the immigrant <a href="/wiki/Illyrians" title="Illyrians">Illyrians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> people. The actions of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a>, at the current site of Döbling, is revealed by several findings. So found were: in Heiligenstadt a fortified tower of the <a href="/wiki/Limes" title="Limes">Limes</a> (border wall); in <a href="/wiki/Sievering" title="Sievering">Sievering</a> a <a href="/wiki/Mithraeum" title="Mithraeum">Mithraeum</a> temple was found; and excavations in Heiligenstadt's church revealed a Roman cemetery. In Sievering in Roman times, a great <a href="/wiki/Quarry" title="Quarry">quarry</a> existed, with a large worker settlement. A further means of subsistence of the population was the <a href="/wiki/Vineyard" title="Vineyard">vineyard</a>, which presumably already operated before the Romans arrived. Otherwise, the people conducted agriculture for their own needs.</p>
<p>In Roman times, the garrison town <a href="/wiki/Vindobona" title="Vindobona">Vindobona</a> was established to cover a part of today's 1st District, the <a href="/wiki/Innere_Stadt" title="Innere Stadt">Innere Stadt</a>. Within the boundaries of Döbling a tower of the Roman defence wall, the <a href="/wiki/Limes" title="Limes">limes</a>, remnants of a temple and tombstones from a cemetery have been unearthed. A quarry located in today's <a href="/wiki/Sievering" title="Sievering">Sievering</a> was operated in Roman times and would have been staffed by local quarry men.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_events">Later events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Later events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Throughout the centuries the suburb, due to its strategic location (elevated in respect to the centre of Vienna) was occupied and often looted. The decisive encounter of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vienna" title="Battle of Vienna">Battle of Vienna</a> in 1683 was fought on September 12, between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jan_III_Sobieski" title="Jan III Sobieski">Jan III Sobieski</a> and the forces of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>, commanded by the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Vizier" title="Grand Vizier">Grand Vizier</a> <a href="/wiki/Kara_Mustafa_Pasha" title="Kara Mustafa Pasha">Kara Mustafa Pasha</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_Development">Social Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Social Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the hilly terrain, large forested areas - used as hunting grounds by the nobility - remained between the creeks and villages, spread throughout the district. The topology also attracted <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> growers. This combination increased the prosperity of the suburb, as noblemen built villas and hunting lodges whilst the burghers of Vienna relaxed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heurigen" title="Heurigen">Heurigen</a> wine-gardens. The existing villages expanded, as the population increased, until the district "Döbling" was established at the end of the 19th century, in 1892.</p>
<p>In the following years, Döbling developed as a district for the prosperous middle and upper class. In the period of the <a href="/wiki/First_Austrian_Republic" title="First Austrian Republic">First Republic</a> between World War I and World War II the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_Democrats" title="Social Democrats">Social Democrats</a> also planned and erected many blocks of <a href="/wiki/Public_housing" title="Public housing">public housing</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karl-Marx-Hof" title="Karl-Marx-Hof">Karl-Marx-Hof</a> is one of the largest of these settlements (<i>Siedlungen</i>). The suburb of Döbling had a high percentage of Jewish residents and maintained a synagogue in the district. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reichskristallnacht" title="Reichskristallnacht">Reichskristallnacht</a> this synagogue (like almost all others in Vienna) was destroyed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_preferences">Religious preferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%B6bling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Religious preferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The distribution of religious preferences of the population in the 13th District, in 2001, differed most from the average in Vienna. With 55.7% of residents being Roman Catholic (Vienna: 49.2%), it is the second highest of all districts of Vienna. There are 11 districts of Roman Catholic parishes, the city Deanery 19 images. Also, the percentage of people with Protestant religion reached 6.5%, as one of the highest values of the districts in Vienna. The proportion of people with different religions are 4.0% known to Islam, 3.2% for the orthodoxy. About 23.8% said they had no religious community.</p>
