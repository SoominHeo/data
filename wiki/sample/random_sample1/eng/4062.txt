<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dubai_Marathon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race is held in January each year, the first being run on 14 January 2000. In April 2007, it was announced that the 2008 race would become the world's richest long distance running event in history, with one million dollars offered for a world record and $250,000 for first place for both the men and women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2008 race was won by <a href="/wiki/Haile_Gebrselassie" title="Haile Gebrselassie">Haile Gebrselassie</a> in a time of 2:04:53, making it the second fastest marathon of all time, but not fast enough to claim a world record and the million dollar prize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2012 edition proved to be one of the fastest finishing fields at that point: a record of four athletes ran under two hours and five minutes. <a href="/wiki/Ayele_Abshero" title="Ayele Abshero">Ayele Abshero</a> won in a course record time of 2:04:23 hours, which was the fourth quickest on the all-time lists and the fastest time ever run by an athlete on their marathon debut. The other podium finishers also entered the all-time top ten: runner-up <a href="/wiki/Dino_Sefir" title="Dino Sefir">Dino Sefir</a> became the eighth fastest man with 2:04:50 hours, while <a href="/wiki/Markos_Geneti" title="Markos Geneti">Markos Geneti</a> became the ninth with 2:04:54 hours. Behind them, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Kiplimo_Maiyo" title="Jonathan Kiplimo Maiyo">Jonathan Kiplimo Maiyo</a> became the 13th fastest man with 2:04:56 hours and <a href="/wiki/Tadese_Tola" title="Tadese Tola">Tadese Tola</a> became the 16th fastest with 2:05:10 hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012ed_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-2012ed-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The women's side was also fast: <a href="/wiki/Aselefech_Mergia" title="Aselefech Mergia">Aselefech Mergia</a> ran a course and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ethiopian_records_in_athletics" title="List of Ethiopian records in athletics">Ethiopian record</a> time of 2:19:31 hours to win, becoming the seventh fastest woman ever. For the first time in history, the top three runners of a race all finished under two hours and twenty minutes: debutante and runner-up <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Wangui_Kabuu" title="Lucy Wangui Kabuu">Lucy Wangui Kabuu</a> became the eighth fastest woman with a time of 2:19:34 hours and <a href="/wiki/Mare_Dibaba" title="Mare Dibaba">Mare Dibaba</a> became the 15th fastest woman in 2:19:52 hours. Behind her, fellow Ethiopians <a href="/wiki/Bezunesh_Bekele" title="Bezunesh Bekele">Bezunesh Bekele</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aberu_Kebede" title="Aberu Kebede">Aberu Kebede</a> moved up to the 16th and 17th fastest of all-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012ed_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2012ed-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2013 edition, ran under a heavy fog, was also very fast. The Ethiopian winner <a href="/wiki/Lelisa_Desisa" title="Lelisa Desisa">Lelisa Desisa</a>, won in a sprint finish of the last 200 m with a time of 2:04:45, and led 4 other runners for sub-2:05 runs. In the women's side, Ethiopian <a href="/wiki/Tirfi_Tsegaye" title="Tirfi Tsegaye">Tirfi Tsegaye</a> won in a time of 2:23:23, 16 seconds ahead of her fellow countrywoman <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ehitu_Kiros&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ehitu Kiros (page does not exist)">Ehitu Kiros</a>. The top 4 in the men's side and top 6 in the women's side were all Ethiopians.</p>
<p>In the 2015 edition, <a href="/wiki/Shure_Demise" title="Shure Demise">Shure Demise</a> set a <a href="/wiki/List_of_world_junior_records_in_athletics" title="List of world junior records in athletics">junior world record</a> in the marathon where she finished 4th overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
