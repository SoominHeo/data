<p><b>Alpha Ursae Majoris</b> (<b>α Ursae Majoris</b>, abbreviated <b>Alpha UMa</b>, <b>α UMa</b>), also named <b>Dubhe</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-CSN_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-CSN-10">[10]</a></sup> is (despite being designated '<a href="/wiki/Alpha" title="Alpha">alpha</a>') the second-brightest <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ursa_Major" title="Ursa Major">Ursa Major</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Ursae_Majoris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:155px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dubhe_A_%26_B.jpg"><img alt="Dubhe A &amp; B" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="136" data-file-width="153" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ea/Dubhe_A_%26_B.jpg" width="153"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dubhe_A_%26_B.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Dubhe "A" and faint companion binaries</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Alpha Ursae Majoris forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Big_Dipper" title="Big Dipper">Big Dipper</a> (also known as the Plough or the Great Bear), and is the northern of the 'pointers' (or 'guards'), the two stars of Ursa Major which point towards <a href="/wiki/Polaris" title="Polaris">Polaris</a>, the North Star. Dubhe is about 123 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_year" title="Light year">light years</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> and is a <a href="/wiki/Giant_star" title="Giant star">giant star</a> that has evolved away from the <a href="/wiki/Main_sequence" title="Main sequence">main sequence</a> after consuming the hydrogen at its core. It is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spectroscopic_binary" title="Spectroscopic binary">spectroscopic binary</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Main_sequence" title="Main sequence">main sequence</a> companion <i>α UMa B</i> that has a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> of F0V. The companion star orbits at a mean separation of about 23 <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">astronomical units</a> (AU) and completes an orbit every 44.4 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msc_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-msc-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is another spectroscopic binary 8 arc minutes distant, a 7th magnitude pair showing an F8 spectral type. It is sometimes referred to as Alpha Ursae majoris C, but is separately catalogued as HD 95638.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msc_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-msc-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>α Ursae Majoris has been reported to vary in brightness by about a thousandth of a magnitude. Ten radial oscillation modes have been detected, with periods between 6.4 hours and 6.4 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buzasi_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-buzasi-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although it is part of the constellation of Ursa Major, it does not form part of the <a href="/wiki/Ursa_Major_Moving_Group" title="Ursa Major Moving Group">Ursa Major Moving Group</a> of stars that have a common motion through space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-motz_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-motz-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomenclature">Nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Ursae_Majoris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>α Ursae Majoris</i> (<a href="/wiki/Latinisation_of_names" title="Latinisation of names">Latinised</a> to <i>Alpha Ursae Majoris</i>) is the star's <a href="/wiki/Bayer_designation" title="Bayer designation">Bayer designation</a>.</p>
<p>It bore the traditional names <i>Dubhe</i> and <i>Ak.</i> <i>Dubhe</i> derives from the <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> for 'bear', <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration"><i>dubb</i></span>, from the phrase <i><span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">ظهر الدب الاكبر</span>‎</i> <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration"><i>żahr ad-dubb al-akbar</i></span> 'the back of the Greater Bear'. The rarer <i>Ak</i> means 'The Eye'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allen1899_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-allen1899-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2016, the <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">International Astronomical Union</a> organized a <a href="/wiki/IAU_Working_Group_on_Star_Names" title="IAU Working Group on Star Names">Working Group on Star Names</a> (WGSN)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WGSN_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-WGSN-14">[14]</a></sup> to catalog and standardize proper names for stars. The WGSN's first bulletin of July 2016<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WGSN1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-WGSN1-15">[15]</a></sup> included a table of the first two batches of names approved by the WGSN; which included <i>Dubhe</i> for this star.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindus</a> refer to the star as <i><a href="/wiki/Kratu" title="Kratu">Kratu</a></i>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Saptarishi" title="Saptarishi">Seven Rishis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allen1899_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-allen1899-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">北斗</span> Běi Dǒu, meaning <i><a href="/wiki/Purple_Forbidden_enclosure" title="Purple Forbidden enclosure">Northern Dipper</a></i>, refers to an asterism consisting of Alpha Ursae Majoris, <a href="/wiki/Beta_Ursae_Majoris" title="Beta Ursae Majoris">Beta Ursae Majoris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Ursae_Majoris" title="Gamma Ursae Majoris">Gamma Ursae Majoris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delta_Ursae_Majoris" title="Delta Ursae Majoris">Delta Ursae Majoris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Epsilon_Ursae_Majoris" title="Epsilon Ursae Majoris">Epsilon Ursae Majoris</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zeta_Ursae_Majoris" title="Zeta Ursae Majoris">Zeta Ursae Majoris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eta_Ursae_Majoris" title="Eta Ursae Majoris">Eta Ursae Majoris</a>. Consequently, Alpha Ursae Majoris itself is known as <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">北斗一</span> Běi Dǒu yī, (English: the First Star of Northern Dipper) and <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">天樞</span> Tiān Shū, (English: Star of Celestial Pivot).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_culture">In culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Ursae_Majoris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dubhe is the official star of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_of_Utah" title="State of Utah">State of Utah</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Dubhe_(ID-2562)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="USS Dubhe (ID-2562) (page does not exist)"><i>Dubhe</i></a> was a ship in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>. The Danish National Home Guard Navy ship MHV 806 is also named <i>Dubhe</i>.</p>
