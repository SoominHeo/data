<p><b>Dubí</b> (<small>Czech pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Czech" title="Help:IPA for Czech">[ˈdubiː]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Eichwald</i></span>) is a town in the <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Ast%C3%AD_nad_Labem_Region" title="Ústí nad Labem Region">Ústí nad Labem Region</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Czech_Republic" title="The Czech Republic">the Czech Republic</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Teplice" title="Teplice">Teplice</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ore_Mountains_(Germany)" title="Ore Mountains (Germany)">Ore Mountains</a>, with 7,792 residents. It is an important transit point to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> on <a href="/wiki/European_route_E55" title="European route E55">European route E55</a>, and the border crossing <b>Cínovec</b> is located within the town limits. There is a spa with mineral waters and a china factory there. The railroad line (<a href="/wiki/Most_(Most_District)" title="Most (Most District)">Most</a> -) Dubí - <a href="/wiki/Moldava_(Teplice_District)" title="Moldava (Teplice District)">Moldava v Krušných horách</a>, that passes through the town, was declared a national monument in 1998. After the <a href="/wiki/Velvet_Revolution" title="Velvet Revolution">Velvet Revolution</a>, the town received bad publicity due to rampant prostitution, fueled by the close proximity to Germany, location on a main truck route and low purchasing power in the Czech Republic; municipal authorities have been struggling with this issue with some recent successes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dub%C3%AD&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dubí was first mentioned in the period of 1494–1498 as a village of <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a> miners (in Czech <i>cín</i>, giving the name to nearby <b><a class="extiw" href="https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%ADnovec_(Dub%C3%AD)" title="cz:Cínovec (Dubí)">Cínovec</a></b>). Rapid development started in the 19th century. First, a new road to <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> was built, followed by a spa (1862) and in (1864) A.Tschinkel purchased a mill Buschmühle where he established <a href="/wiki/Porcelain" title="Porcelain">porcelain</a> factory that in 1871 changed name to "Eichwalder Porzellan und Ofenfabriken Bloch and Co." Furthermore, a new railroad (1884) made Dubí a popular holiday spa resort, visited by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johann_Wolfgang_Goethe" title="Johann Wolfgang Goethe">Johann Wolfgang Goethe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Neruda" title="Jan Neruda">Jan Neruda</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A1clav_Talich" title="Václav Talich">Václav Talich</a> and others. its land is very rich.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights_and_spa">Sights and spa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dub%C3%AD&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sights and spa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most important sight in Dubí is Saint Maria's Church, which was built on the order of princes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clary-Aldringen" title="Clary-Aldringen">Clary-Aldringen</a> between 1898 and 1906 as a copy of the <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> church Santa Maria dell'Orto to serve as their family's church.</p>
<p>The first spas in Dubí were built in 1860 under the management of Anton Tschinkel, the founder of a local china factory. In 1862, his first spa (Diana Spa) was opened. The present-day Theresa Spa (Tereziny lázně) with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mineral_waters" title="Mineral waters">mineral waters</a>, recommended to patients after brain and spine surgeries, have been operating since 1879.</p>
<p>The scenic railroad line Dubí–Moldava was declared a national monument in 1998.</p>
