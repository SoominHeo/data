<p>The <b>Dubbi Volcano</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Stratovolcano" title="Stratovolcano">stratovolcano</a> located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Red_Sea" title="Southern Red Sea">Southern Red Sea</a> Region of <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a>. Its peak elevation is 1625 m. There have been four known eruptions. In 1400 <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">lava</a> was determined to have reached the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> while in 1861 ash was thrown over 250 km from the volcano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Two further events were suspected between 1861 and the 20th century.</p>
<p>On June 13, 2011 an ash cloud that had some influence on air travel was attributed to Dubbi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, more accurate satellite imagery later showed that <a href="/wiki/Nabro_Volcano" title="Nabro Volcano">Nabro</a> was the volcano that had erupted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
