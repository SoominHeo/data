<p><b>Doosan Arena</b>, previously known as <b>Stadion města Plzně</b> (Plzeň City Stadium) is a <a href="/wiki/Multi-purpose_stadium" title="Multi-purpose stadium">multi-purpose stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Plze%C5%88" title="Plzeň">Plzeň</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>. It is located in the Štrunc Park near the confluence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C5%BEe_River" title="Mže River">Mže</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radbuza_River" title="Radbuza River">Radbuza</a> rivers. The stadium is currently used mostly for <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> matches and is the home ground of <a href="/wiki/FC_Viktoria_Plze%C5%88" title="FC Viktoria Plzeň">FC Viktoria Plzeň</a>. The stadium holds 11,700 people. It is also known as <b>Stadion ve Štruncových sadech</b> (Stadium in Štrunc Park), named after Emil Štrunc, a former regional chieftain of the <a href="/wiki/Sokol" title="Sokol">Sokol</a> Movement (some people incorrectly think it is named after a famous local footballer <a href="/wiki/Stanislav_%C5%A0trunc" title="Stanislav Štrunc">Stanislav Štrunc</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doosan_Arena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium was opened in 1955 for the regional <a href="/wiki/Spartakiad_(Czechoslovakia)" title="Spartakiad (Czechoslovakia)">Spartakiad</a>. After various construction enlargements, its capacity was 35,000 people, 7,600 of which were seated. In 2002-2003 it underwent reconstruction to comply with the needs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%8CMFS" title="ČMFS">football association</a> and the capacity lowered significantly to 7,425 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_reconstruction">2011 reconstruction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doosan_Arena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2011 reconstruction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2011, work costing approximately 360 million <a href="/wiki/Czech_koruna" title="Czech koruna">Czech koruna</a> got under way to modernise the stadium in line with UEFA criteria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> While the reconstruction was in progress, the capacity of the stadium was reduced to 3,500 spectators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At the end of August 2011, part of the 2011 reconstruction had finished, leaving the stadium with a new capacity of around 8,500.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In December 2011, the reconstruction was finished with a resultant capacity of almost 12,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Plzeň played in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League group stage">2011–12 UEFA Champions League group stage</a>. However, home matches were played at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synot_Tip_Arena" title="Synot Tip Arena">Synot Tip Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> due to the reconstruction. In January 2012, the stadium was approved for use in Plzeň's <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League">2011–12 UEFA Europa League</a> match with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schalke_04" title="Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
