<p><i><b>Doosan Encyclopedia</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean language</a> <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedia" title="Encyclopedia">encyclopedia</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Doosan_Group" title="Doosan Group">Doosan Donga</a> (두산동아). The encyclopedia is based on the <i>Dong-A Color Encyclopedia</i> (동아원색세계대백과사전), which comprises 30 volumes and began to be published in 1982 by Dong-A Publishing (동아출판사). Dong-A Publishing was merged into Doosan Donga, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Doosan_Group" title="Doosan Group">Doosan Group</a>, in February 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncyKorea_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EncyKorea-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Digital_Edition">Digital Edition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doosan_Encyclopedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Digital Edition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EnCyber">EnCyber</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doosan_Encyclopedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: EnCyber">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The online version of the <i>Doosan Encyclopedia</i> is named <b>EnCyber</b>, which is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blend_(linguistics)" title="Blend (linguistics)">blend</a> of two English words: <i>Encyclopedia</i> and <i>Cyber</i>. The company has stated that, with the trademark, it aims to become a center of living knowledge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> EnCyber provides free content to readers via <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">portals</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newsboy_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Newsboy-6">[6]</a></sup> Naver has risen to the top position in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a> market of South Korea partially because of the encyclopedia. It is said that when Naver exclusively contracted Doosan Doonga in 2003, the former paid multi billion <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_won" title="South Korean won">won</a> to the latter in royalties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newsboy_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Newsboy-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weekly_Donga_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Weekly_Donga-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The articles in the encyclopedia aim to educate readers in every age group. It is regarded as a major encyclopedia in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weekly_Donga_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Weekly_Donga-7">[7]</a></sup> As of 2009<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Doosan_Encyclopedia&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, EnCyber is the biggest online encyclopedia of South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doopedia">Doopedia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doosan_Encyclopedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Doopedia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 1, 2010, the digital output 'Encyclopedia' was 'renamed 'doopedia', a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> from the company name 'Doosan' and the English word 'encyclopedia'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zine2010-10_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-zine2010-10-10">[10]</a></sup> On 7 November 2013 were 463,953 items in the 'doopedia' available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Wikipedia" title="Korean Wikipedia">Korean Wikipedia</a> on that day there were 252,830 articles.</p>
