<p><b>Dušan Švento</b> (born 1 August 1985) is a <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovak</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> club <a href="/wiki/SK_Slavia_Prague" title="SK Slavia Prague">SK Slavia Prague</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_football_team" title="Slovakia national football team">Slovakia national football team</a>.</p>
<p>Švento made his international debut for <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_football_team" title="Slovakia national football team">Slovakia</a> in the friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a> on 15 August 2006, under coach <a href="/wiki/Du%C5%A1an_Galis" title="Dušan Galis">Dušan Galis</a>. He scored his first goal in the national team in an away game against <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a> (5:1 win) on 7th October 2006.</p>
<p>During summer 2007, he went to an English club <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> for a trial and to take a look at the facilities there. Seemingly, more English based clubs were interested. However, this interest ended as he damaged his knee ligaments after returning to Prague. He has undergone surgery and has been out for almost a year.</p>
<p>On 16 June 2009, Švento joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Red_Bull_Salzburg" title="FC Red Bull Salzburg">Red Bull Salzburg</a> after signing a three-year contract for a transfer fee of €2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
