<p><b>Dushyanta</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanskrit_language" title="Sanskrit language">Sanskrit</a>: <span lang="sa" xml:lang="sa">दुष्यन्त</span>) is a king in classical <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> literature and mythology. He was the husband of <a href="/wiki/Shakuntala" title="Shakuntala">Shakuntala</a> and the father of the <a href="/wiki/Bharata_(emperor)" title="Bharata (emperor)">Emperor Bharatha</a>. He appears in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahabarata" title="Mahabarata">Mahabarata</a></i> and in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kalidasa" title="Kalidasa">Kalidasa</a>'s play <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Recognition_of_Sakuntala" title="The Recognition of Sakuntala">The Recognition of Sakuntala</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Circa" title="Circa">c.</a> 300 CE). His name is also transliterated as <i>Dusyanta</i> or <i>Dushyant</i>, and means "Destroyer of Evil" in Sanskrit.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="King_Dushyant">King Dushyant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dushyanta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: King Dushyant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mah%C4%81bh%C4%81rata" title="Mahābhārata">Mahābhārata</a></i>, Dushyanta is the son of Ilina and Rathantara.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was king of <a href="/wiki/Hastinapur" title="Hastinapur">Hastinapur</a> and ancestor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuru_dynasty" title="Kuru dynasty">Kuru dynasty</a>. Dushyant met his wife <a href="/wiki/Shakuntala" title="Shakuntala">Shakuntala</a> while on an excursion from his kingdom at the hermitage of sage Kanva. Dushyanta and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sakuntala" title="Sakuntala">Sakuntala</a> had a son named <a href="/wiki/Bharata_(emperor)" title="Bharata (emperor)">Bharata</a> who went on to become <a href="/wiki/Bharata_(emperor)" title="Bharata (emperor)">Emperor Bharata</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Love_for_Shakuntala">Love for Shakuntala</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dushyanta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Love for Shakuntala">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="533" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg/220px-1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg/330px-1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg/440px-1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1940s_Vintage_Hindu_Print_Dushyant_%26_Shakuntala.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Dushyant married Shakuntala</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The story of Dushyanta's encounter, marriage, separation and reunion with his queen, <a href="/wiki/Shakuntala" title="Shakuntala">Shakuntala</a>, has been immortalized in the <i><a href="/wiki/Mahabharata" title="Mahabharata">Mahabharata</a></i> and in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Recognition_of_Shakuntala" title="The Recognition of Shakuntala">The Recognition of Shakuntala</a></i> by the great <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> poet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kalidas" title="Kalidas">Kalidas</a>.</p>
<p>Dushyant meets Shakuntala, who is a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Vishvamitra" title="Vishvamitra">Vishvamitra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Menaka" title="Menaka">Menaka</a>, while on an excursion from his kingdom. Depending on the source,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Dushyant is either the crown prince, or he is waiting to win back his kingdom from an enemy. Either way, he sees Shakuntala in an ashram (hermitage) of <a href="/wiki/Rishi" title="Rishi">Rishi</a> <a href="/wiki/Kanva" title="Kanva">Kanva</a> and falls in love. He and Shakuntala have a <a href="/wiki/Gandharva" title="Gandharva">gandharva</a> marriage there. Having to leave after some time, Dushyant gives Shakuntala a royal ring as a sign of their love, promising her that he will come to her.</p>
<p>But when Dushyant becomes king, he becomes too absorbed for many years in affairs of state. Shakuntala waits and despairs. One day, sage Durvasa visits the hermitage, but Shakuntala, who is too absorbed in her love for Dushyant, forgets to serve him food. In a fit of anger, sage Durvasa curses her, saying that the person she is thinking about will forget her. A shocked Shakuntala begs for forgiveness and the sage, after recollecting his calm, assures her that the person will remember her again when she shows some proof of their acquaintance.</p>
<p>So, Shakuntala sets off to the capital, <a href="/wiki/Hastinapur" title="Hastinapur">Hastinapur</a>, to remind Dushyant of their past love. An accident occurs by which a fish consumes the royal ring, leaving Shakuntala with no formidable proof.</p>
<p>Dushyanta does not recollect Shakuntala, but his memory and love are rekindled when a sage (not the same who cursed Shakuntala) recovers the ring and brings it to the court. Dushyanta weds Shakuntala, who becomes his queen and mother of his son, Bharata.</p>
<p>A different version of the story<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> involves Shakuntala's father, the great and legendary sage <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vishwamitra" title="Vishwamitra">Vishwamitra</a>, who is said to have bowed his head to none. Dushyant's forgetting of Shakuntala was a device invented by the other sages, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vashishta" title="Vashishta">Vashishta</a>, to make Vishwamitra bow. For the sake of his daughter the great sage is said to have bowed before the great king Dushyant to persuade him to accept his daughter. The sages, delighted, immediately brought the memory of Shakuntala into the mind of Dushyant.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bharata">Bharata</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dushyanta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bharata">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="683" data-file-width="484" height="282" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg/200px-Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg/300px-Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg/400px-Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Raja_Ravi_Varma_-_Mahabharata_-_Bharata.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Bharata_(emperor)" title="Bharata (emperor)">Bharata</a> (Sarvadamana), the son of Dushyanta and <a href="/wiki/Shakuntala" title="Shakuntala">Shakuntala</a>. Painting by <a href="/wiki/Raja_Ravi_Varma" title="Raja Ravi Varma">Raja Ravi Varma</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>According to many literary sources, Dushyanta was the father of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Bharata" title="Emperor Bharata">Emperor Bharata</a>, who is generally credited with uniting India under his rule (see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Origin_of_India%27s_name" title="Origin of India's name">origin of India's name</a>). According to the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mah%C4%81bh%C4%81rata" title="Mahābhārata">Mahābhārata</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adiparva" title="Adiparva">Adiparva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bharata_(emperor)" title="Bharata (emperor)">Bharata</a> was born while Shakuntala awaited Dushyanta at her hermitage. A fisherman catches the same fish who had consumed the ring. His wife wants to make curry out of it, while cutting up the fish she finds the ring and takes it to the king. The King then remembers Shakuntala and sets out in search for her.</p>
