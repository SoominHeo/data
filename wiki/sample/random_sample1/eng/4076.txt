<p><b>Doetinchem</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈdutɪnˌxɛm]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Nl-Doetinchem.ogg" title="File:Nl-Doetinchem.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/91/Nl-Doetinchem.ogg" title="Nl-Doetinchem.ogg">listen</a>)</small>; <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Low_Saxon" title="Dutch Low Saxon">Low Saxon</a>: Deutekem) is a city and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipalities_in_the_Netherlands" title="Municipalities in the Netherlands">municipality</a> in the east of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. It is situated along the <a href="/wiki/IJssel" title="IJssel">Oude IJssel</a> (Old IJssel) river in a part of the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Netherlands" title="Provinces of the Netherlands">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gelderland" title="Gelderland">Gelderland</a> called the <a href="/wiki/Achterhoek" title="Achterhoek">Achterhoek</a> . The municipality had a population of 56,436 in 2014 and consists of an area of 79.66 km<sup>2</sup> (30.76 sq mi) of which 0.60 km<sup>2</sup> (0.23 sq mi) is water. This makes Doetinchem the largest town (by population) in the Achterhoek.</p>
<p>On 1 January 2005, a municipal restructuring merged the neighbouring municipality of <a href="/wiki/Wehl" title="Wehl">Wehl</a> as well as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zelhelmse_Broek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zelhelmse Broek (page does not exist)">Zelhelmse Broek</a> area with Doetinchem.</p>

The municipality of Doetinchem consists of:</p>
<p>the city (‘stad’):</p>
<ul>
<li>Doetinchem</li>
</ul>
<p>the villages (‘Wijken’):</p>
<ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doetinchem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="1544" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg/220px-Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg/330px-Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg/440px-Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kasteel_slangenburg.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Slangenburg_Castle" title="Slangenburg Castle">Slangenburg Castle</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It is known from archaeological finds of skulls, pottery shards, and flint arrowheads that the area was inhabited more than 11,000 years ago. These prehistoric hunters were followed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celt" title="Celt">Celtic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Germanic_tribes" title="Germanic tribes">Germanic tribes</a> like the <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Franks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saxons" title="Saxons">Saxons</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> coins have been found and there is also archaeological evidence of the <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a> having plundered the area.</p>
<p>The first reference to the name of Doetinchem comes in a document from the year 838 which mentions a ‘villa Duetinghem’, a settlement with a small church. In 887, there is another mention of ‘Deutinkem’, a fortress with a church which had been given to the then <a href="/wiki/Archdiocese_of_Utrecht_(695%E2%80%931580)" title="Archdiocese of Utrecht (695–1580)">Bishop of Utrecht</a>. The spelling has varied over the centuries, with ‘Duttichem’, ‘Duichingen’ and ‘Deutekom’ being just some examples.</p>
<p>For a long time Doetinchem remained a small place but around 1100 it started to grow and, after suffering several attempts by plunderers, a town wall was built. In 1236, Doetinchem was granted city rights (‘stadsrechten’) by Count Otto II of Gelre and Zutphen, and in return the town provided taxes and soldiers for the Count’s army. Also, the new city council published rules for the city, codified in the ‘Keurboek van Doetinchem’ (Rulebook of Doetinchem), which laid down severe punishments for infringements.</p>
<p>In 1226, Doetinchem faced increasing danger from plunderers, and so the city wall was raised by a metre. There were four barriers in the wall which, being weak points, were replaced over time by four large city-gates known as: the Hamburgerpoort (built 1302), the Waterpoort, the Gruitpoort, and the Hezenpoort. Later a moat was dug around the wall and a rampart was built in front; the city’s central windmill, the ‘walmolen’ (Dutch ‘wal’=rampart, ‘molen’=mill), stands on the remains of this rampart. Despite these defences, Doetinchem was besieged many times and during the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years' War">Eighty Years' War</a> (1568–1648) was besieged and conquered twice. However, eventually the walls became seen as redundant (or perhaps ineffective) and in 1672, they were torn down. However, it was not until the second half of the 19th century that the city-gates and most of the rampart were removed.</p>
<p>From its early years, Doetinchem had been an important marketplace for farmers to sell their wares; the market was held in the central square called the Simonsplein right up until the Second World War.</p>
<p>Doetinchem has had its fair share of disasters. Apart from the sieges mentioned above, in 1527 a large fire destroyed most of the city including the city archives (which means that many earlier dates in the history of Doetinchem are somewhat unreliable), and in 1580 most of the city was killed by plague. There was also occasional flooding. However, despite the fact that Doetinchem is only 10 km (6.2 mi) from the German border, because the Netherlands was not involved in the First World War, Doetinchem saw nothing more than the posting of a few border guards during that time. Even during the Second World War, Doetinchem came off fairly lightly at first; there was only a small German occupying force and the city even escaped the worst effects of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_famine_of_1944" title="Dutch famine of 1944">Hunger Winter</a>. However, some prisoners were executed after being implicated in the shooting death in <a href="/wiki/Putten" title="Putten">Putten</a> of an important German officer by the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_in_World_War_II#Oppression_and_resistance" title="Netherlands in World War II">Dutch Resistance</a> and disastrously, in March and April 1945, the centre of Doetinchem was largely destroyed by Allied bombing which was either intended for nearby German towns or, as some say, was to destroy the German defenses in Doetinchem. Which of the two is true has never really been clarified and there is still some discussion about the true intentions of the bombardment. The city itself was liberated by <a href="/wiki/The_Calgary_Highlanders" title="The Calgary Highlanders">The Calgary Highlanders</a> in 1945 after a brief battle there.</p>
<p>In the decades after the war, Doetinchem grew and in a few years had outgrown its "competitors" in the Achterhoek, namely <a href="/wiki/Doesburg" title="Doesburg">Doesburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Winterswijk" title="Winterswijk">Winterswijk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zutphen" title="Zutphen">Zutphen</a>. The Dutch company, <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">Philips</a>, had a factory for some years in the city. From 2003 till 2005, the city grew enormously as new districts such as <a href="/wiki/Dichteren" title="Dichteren">Dichteren</a> were built, and as Doetinchem incorporates outlying villages such as <a href="/wiki/Wehl" title="Wehl">Wehl</a> into its municipality. In 2011, the city is still growing. By building new district as <i>het Loo</i> and <i>Isseldoks</i>, and the opening of a brand new theatre and cinema, Doetinchem is the biggest growing city in Gelderland.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Significant_structures_in_Doetinchem">Significant structures in Doetinchem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doetinchem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Significant structures in Doetinchem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main church in the central square, St Catherine’s Church (‘Catharinakerk’) was virtually destroyed in the World War II bombing and restoration took from 1948 to 1963. Although originally a Roman Catholic church, it became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed" title="Dutch Reformed">Dutch Reformed</a> in 1591. There are two castles, ‘De Kelder’ (‘The Cellar’) and Slangenburg.</p>
<p>The city has three windmills. In the city centre, there is the already mentioned <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=De_Walmolen,_Doetinchem&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="De Walmolen, Doetinchem (page does not exist)">De Walmolen</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> the bottom of which now houses the city’s tourist office. In Dichteren, there is a mill called <i><a href="/wiki/Aurora,_Dichteren" title="Aurora, Dichteren">Aurora</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> (Latin for ‘dawn’) and to the east of the city a mill called <i><a href="/wiki/Benninkmolen,_Doetinchem" title="Benninkmolen, Doetinchem">Benninkmolen</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> All these mills are open to visitors, usually open to all one weekday morning and at other times by appointment.</p>
<p>A few other important buildings in the city are the <i>Amphion-Theater</i>, the <i>Gruitpoort</i>, a big cultural centre, the <i>Rietveld Lyceum</i>, the biggest high school in Doetinchem and the Tax administration office.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doetinchem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Doetinchem has a professional football team, <a href="/wiki/De_Graafschap" title="De Graafschap">De Graafschap</a> (‘The County’). The home of De Graafschap was completely revamped to transform it into an all-seater arena. The stands are fully covered, with the roof painted in the blue and white of the club.</p>
<p>The city has an indoor swimming pool, Rozengaarde. In April or May, a local evening four-day marathon is organized.</p>
<p>Since 2005 a four-day marathon is organised which starts and ends in Doetinchem and runs through the <a href="/wiki/Achterhoek" title="Achterhoek">Achterhoek</a>. The marathon was held from 23 August to the 26th. Almost 15.000 people took part.</p>
<p>In 2009 the city's volleyballteam took part in the CEV cup playing against <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Haltbank_Ankara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Haltbank Ankara (page does not exist)">Haltbank Ankara</a> from <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doetinchem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Doetinchem has an annual street-theater festival <i>Buitengewoon</i> (Extraordinary), and the City Festival, a big festival with musicacts, theatre, a carnival and fireworks.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_media">Local media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doetinchem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Local media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Newspapers available in Doetinchem include subscription paper "De Gelderlander" and free papers "Doetinchems Vizier" and "Zondag", all published by <a href="/wiki/Wegener_(company)" title="Wegener (company)">Koninklijke Wegener NV</a>. Doetinchem has its own television station called ‘Stadstv’ which is received by cable in the municipalities of Doetinchem itself and the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Hummelo_en_Keppel" title="Hummelo en Keppel">Hummelo en Keppel</a>, and a television station called 'GraafschapTV' which is received in the entire <a href="/wiki/Achterhoek" title="Achterhoek">Achterhoek</a> region.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doetinchem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Doetinchem has been connected to the Dutch national highway system since November 1974, when the Zevenaar-Doetinchem section of Highway 15 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rijksweg_15" title="Rijksweg 15">A15</a>) was opened. This section was extended to Varsseveld in 1984. Today the A15 is called the A18.</p>
<p>Doetinchem is served by rail services from <a href="/wiki/Arnhem" title="Arnhem">Arnhem</a> by the regional train to <a href="/wiki/Winterswijk" title="Winterswijk">Winterswijk</a> with two stations, one in the city centre – <a href="/wiki/Doetinchem_railway_station" title="Doetinchem railway station">Doetinchem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doetinchem_De_Huet_railway_station" title="Doetinchem De Huet railway station">Doetinchem De Huet</a> lying to the west between the suburbs of De Huet and Dichteren. Doetinchem receives four trains per hour to Arnhem (Monday to Friday) and there are always 2 trains per hour between Arnhem and Winterswijk. The rail company is <a href="/wiki/Syntus" title="Syntus">Syntus</a>. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Arriva</a>, who already operates all the busservices, will take over the rails between Arnhem and Winterswijk.</p>
<p>Bus services are operated by <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Arriva</a>.</p>
