<p>The <b>Cephalocarida</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Class_(biology)" title="Class (biology)">class</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Subphylum" title="Subphylum">subphylum</a> <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">Crustacea</a> comprising only 12 benthic <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>. They were discovered in 1955 by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_L._Sanders&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Howard L. Sanders (page does not exist)">Howard L. Sanders</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and are commonly referred to as <b>horseshoe shrimps</b>. They have been grouped together with the <a href="/wiki/Remipedia" title="Remipedia">Remipedia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Xenocarida" title="Xenocarida">Xenocarida</a>. Although a second family, Lightiellidae, is sometimes used, all cephalocaridans are generally considered to belong in just one family: <b>Hutchinsoniellidae</b>. Though no <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossil</a> record of cephalocaridans has been found, most specialists believe them to be primitive among crustaceans.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description_and_anatomy">Description and anatomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cephalocarida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description and anatomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These crustaceans are from 2 to 4 mm (0.079 to 0.157 in) in length, with an elongated body. They have a large head, the hind edge of which covers the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thoracic" title="Thoracic">thoracic</a> segment. They have no eyes, presumably because of their muddy natural habitat. The second pair of <a href="/wiki/Antenna_(biology)" title="Antenna (biology)">antennae</a> is located behind the mouth; in all other crustaceans the antennae are in front of the mouth at the adult stage, and only their larvae have antennae that have the same location as adult cephalocaridans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IZ_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IZ-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The mouth is located behind the large upper lip, flanked by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mandible_(arthropod)" title="Mandible (arthropod)">mandibles</a>. The first pair of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maxilla_(arthropod)" title="Maxilla (arthropod)">maxillae</a> is very small, and the second pair has the same structure as the following thoracic legs: a large basal part, equipped with outgrowths on the inner side, used in locomotion, a forked inner branch and two outer lobes - referred to as the "pseudoepipod" and the "exopod". The structural and functional similarity between the maxillae and the legs may be a sign of primitive organization; the maxillae are not specialized, as they are in other crustaceans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IZ_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-IZ-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Thorax" title="Thorax">thorax</a> consists of 10 segments, and the abdomen bears a <a href="/wiki/Telson" title="Telson">telson</a> but no other appendages.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cephalocarida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cephalocaridans are found from the <a href="/wiki/Intertidal_zone" title="Intertidal zone">intertidal zone</a> down to a depth of 1,500 m (4,900 ft), in all kinds of <a href="/wiki/Sediment" title="Sediment">sediments</a>. Cephalocaridans feed on marine <a href="/wiki/Detritus" title="Detritus">detritus</a>. To bring in food particles, they generate currents with the thoracic appendages like the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Branchiopod" title="Branchiopod">branchiopods</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Malacostraca" title="Malacostraca">malacostracans</a>. Food particles are then passed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anterior" title="Anterior">anteriorly</a> along a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ventral" title="Ventral">ventral</a> groove, leading to the <a href="/wiki/Arthropod_mouthparts" title="Arthropod mouthparts">mouthparts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
