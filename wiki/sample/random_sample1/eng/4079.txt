<p>A <b>sculpin</b> is a type of <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> that belongs to the superfamily <b>Cottoidea</b> in the order <a href="/wiki/Scorpaeniformes" title="Scorpaeniformes">Scorpaeniformes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kane_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-kane-1">[1]</a></sup> As of 2006, this superfamily contains 11 families, 149 genera, and 756 species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sculpins occur in many types of habitat, including <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fresh_water" title="Fresh water">freshwater</a> zones. They live in rivers, <a href="/wiki/Submarine_canyon" title="Submarine canyon">submarine canyons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelp_forest" title="Kelp forest">kelp forests</a>, and shallow <a href="/wiki/Littoral_zone" title="Littoral zone">littoral</a> habitat types, such as <a href="/wiki/Tide_pool" title="Tide pool">tidepools</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kane_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-kane-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sculpins are <a href="/wiki/Benthic_zone" title="Benthic zone">benthic</a> fish, dwelling on the bottoms of water bodies. Their <a href="/wiki/Fish_fin" title="Fish fin">pectoral fins</a> are smooth on the upper edge and webbed with sharp rays along the lower edge, a modification that makes them specialized for gripping the substrate. This adaptation helps the fish anchor in fast-flowing water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kane_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-kane-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Families">Families</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sculpin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Families">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Families include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Abyssocottidae" title="Abyssocottidae">Abyssocottidae</a>: deep-water sculpins (24 species)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Poacher_(fish)" title="Poacher (fish)">Agonidae</a> (47 species)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bathylutichthys" title="Bathylutichthys">Bathylutichthyidae</a> (two species)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golomyanka" title="Golomyanka">Comephoridae</a>: Baikal oilfishes (two species)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cottidae" title="Cottidae">Cottidae</a>: common sculpins (258 species, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Icelidae" title="Icelidae">Icelidae</a>, sometimes regarded as separate)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cottocomephoridae" title="Cottocomephoridae">Cottocomephoridae</a>: bighead sculpins, Baikal sculpins (9 species)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ereuniidae" title="Ereuniidae">Ereuniidae</a>: deepwater bullhead sculpins (three species)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sea_raven" title="Sea raven">Hemitripteridae</a>: sea ravens, sailfin sculpins (eight species)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Psychrolutidae" title="Psychrolutidae">Psychrolutidae</a>: fatheads (40 species)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Grunt_sculpin" title="Grunt sculpin">Rhamphocottidae</a>: the grunt sculpin (one species)</li>
</ul>
