<p><b>Northeast China</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">中国东北</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">中國東北</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Zhōngguó Dōngběi</span></i>) or <b>Dongbei</b> is a geographical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_of_China" title="Region of China">region of China</a>. It also historically corresponds with the term <b><a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a></b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[a]</a></sup> in the English language. It consists specifically of the three provinces of <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jilin" title="Jilin">Jilin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heilongjiang" title="Heilongjiang">Heilongjiang</a>, collectively referred as the <b>Three Northeastern Provinces</b> (东北三省, <i>Dōngběi sānshěng</i>), but broadly also encompasses the eastern part of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[b]</a></sup> The region is separated from <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">Far Eastern</a> <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> to the north largely by the <a href="/wiki/Amur_River" title="Amur River">Amur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argun_River_(Asia)" title="Argun River (Asia)">Argun</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ussuri" title="Ussuri">Ussuri</a> rivers, from <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> to the south by the <a href="/wiki/Yalu_River" title="Yalu River">Yalu River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tumen_River" title="Tumen River">Tumen River</a>, and from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolian_Autonomous_Region" title="Inner Mongolian Autonomous Region">Inner Mongolian Autonomous Region</a> to the west by the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Khingan" title="Greater Khingan">Greater Khingan</a> Range. The heartland of the region is the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_China_Plain" title="Northeast China Plain">Northeast China Plain</a>.</p>
<p>In the context of the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Area_Revitalization_Plan" title="Northeast Area Revitalization Plan">Northeast Area Revitalization Plan</a> by the <a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="State Council of the People's Republic of China">State Council of the People's Republic of China</a>, five <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_cities" title="Prefecture-level cities">prefecture-level cities</a> of eastern <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>, namely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xilin_Gol_League" title="Xilin Gol League">Xilin Gol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chifeng" title="Chifeng">Chifeng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tongliao" title="Tongliao">Tongliao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hinggan_League" title="Hinggan League">Hinggan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hulunbuir" title="Hulunbuir">Hulunbuir</a>, are also formally defined as regions of the Northeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[1]</a></sup> The region is nearly congruent with some definitions of "Manchuria" in historical foreign usage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[c]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another term for the area is <b>Guandong</b> (关东 / 關東, <i>Guāndōng</i>), meaning "east of the Pass", referring to the famous <a href="/wiki/Shanhai_Pass" title="Shanhai Pass">Shanhai Pass</a> between <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a> Province and the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a> Province (and also <a href="/wiki/North_China" title="North China">North China</a>) to the west. This name was also used by the occupying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_colonialism" title="Japanese colonialism">Japanese colonists</a> referring to their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leased_territory" title="Leased territory">leased territory</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dalian" title="Dalian">Dalian</a> after the 1895 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Shimonoseki" title="Treaty of Shimonoseki">Treaty of Shimonoseki</a>, as the <i><a href="/wiki/Kwantung_Leased_Territory" title="Kwantung Leased Territory">Kwantung Chou</a></i> (関東州), which gave name to the occupying <a href="/wiki/Kwantung_Army" title="Kwantung Army">Kwantung Army</a> that was later mobilized to set up the <a href="/wiki/Puppet_state" title="Puppet state">puppet state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manchukuo" title="Manchukuo">Manchukuo</a>.</p>

<p>Northeast China was the homeland of several ethnic groups, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchu" title="Manchu">Manchus</a> (or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jurchens" title="Jurchens">Jurchens</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulchs" title="Ulchs">Ulchs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hezhen" title="Hezhen">Hezhen</a> (also known as the Goldi and Nanai). Various ethnic groups and their respective kingdoms, including the <a href="/wiki/Sushen" title="Sushen">Sushen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xianbei" title="Xianbei">Xianbei</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mohe_people" title="Mohe people">Mohe</a> have risen to power in the Northeast. Many Korean kingdoms have also risen to power in Manchuria, including <a href="/wiki/Gojoseon" title="Gojoseon">Gojoseon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buyeo_(state)" title="Buyeo (state)">Buyeo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Balhae" title="Balhae">Balhae</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yan_State" title="Yan State">Yan State</a> once occupied the <a href="/wiki/Liaodong_Peninsula" title="Liaodong Peninsula">Liaodong Peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Dynasties_in_Chinese_history" title="Dynasties in Chinese history">dynasties</a> in China loosely controlled the southern parts of the region. During the Song dynasty, the Khitan set up the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liao_Dynasty" title="Liao Dynasty">Liao Dynasty</a> in Northeast China. Later, the <a href="/wiki/Jurchen_people" title="Jurchen people">Jurchen</a> overthrew the Liao and formed the <a href="/wiki/Jin_dynasty_(1115%E2%80%931234)" title="Jin dynasty (1115–1234)">Jin dynasty</a>, which went on to conquer northern China. In <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">AD</a> 1234, the Jin dynasty fell to the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongols</a>, whose <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan Dynasty</a> was later replaced by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> in 1368. In 1644, the Manchu conquered the entirety of China and established the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a> (1644–1912).</p>
<p>Northeast China came under influence of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> with the building of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Eastern_Railway" title="Chinese Eastern Railway">Chinese Eastern Railway</a> through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harbin,_China" title="Harbin, China">Harbin</a> to <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a> replaced Russian influence in the region as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a> in 1904–1905, and Japan laid the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Manchurian_Railway" title="South Manchurian Railway">South Manchurian Railway</a> in 1906 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BCshunkou" title="Lüshunkou">Port Arthur</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Warlord_Era" title="Warlord Era">Warlord Era</a> in China, <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Zuolin" title="Zhang Zuolin">Zhang Zuolin</a> established himself in Northeast China, but was murdered by the Japanese for being too independent. The last <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a> emperor, <a href="/wiki/Puyi" title="Puyi">Puyi</a>, was then placed on the throne to lead a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Puppet_state" title="Puppet state">puppet state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manchukuo" title="Manchukuo">Manchukuo</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">atomic bombing</a> of Japan in 1945, the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> invaded the region as part of its declaration of war against Japan. From 1945 to 1948, Northeast China was a base area for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist" title="Communist">Communist</a> <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a>. With the encouragement of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, the area was used as a <a href="/wiki/Staging_area" title="Staging area">staging ground</a> during the Civil War for the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Chinese Communists</a>, who were victorious in 1949 and have been controlling this region since.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northeast_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Northeast China has a total population of about 107,400,000 people, accounting for 8% of China’s total population. The overwhelming majority of the population in the Northeast is <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a>, many of whose ancestors came in the 19th and 20th centuries during a migration movement called "<a href="/wiki/Chuang_Guandong" title="Chuang Guandong">Chuang Guandong</a>" (闖關東, literally "<i>venture into the east of the Pass</i>"). Northeast China historically had a significant Han Chinese population, reaching over 3 million by the end of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a>, but they were subjected to <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_cleansing" title="Ethnic cleansing">cleansing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eviction" title="Eviction">eviction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cultural_assimilation" title="Cultural assimilation">assimilation</a> by the conquest of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchu" title="Manchu">Manchu</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a> , who then set up <a href="/wiki/Willow_Palisade" title="Willow Palisade">Willow Palisades</a> during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Shunzhi_Emperor" title="Shunzhi Emperor">Shunzhi Emperor</a> and prohibited any settlement of Han Chinese into the region. The Northeast then remained sparsely populated until the ban was finally lifted during the late Qing in response to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian</a> incursions into <a href="/wiki/Outer_Manchuria" title="Outer Manchuria">Outer Manchuria</a> (which resulted in the region's annexation by Russia after the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Aigun" title="Treaty of Aigun">Treaty of Aigun</a>), and a large influx of migrant settlers, mainly landless peasants from the nearby <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a> provinces, came in a scale equivalent to the 19th century American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westward_expansion" title="Westward expansion">westward expansion</a>, resulting in the local Han Chinese population to grow over 20 million before the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">Second Sino-Japanese War</a>. After the establishment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a>, further immigrations were organized by the Central Government to "develop the Great Northern Wilderness" (开发北大荒), eventually peaking the population over 100 million people.</p>
<p>Because most people in Northeast China trace their ancestries back to the migrants from the Chuang Guandong era, Northeastern Chinese were more culturally uniform compared to other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_regions_of_China" title="List of regions of China">geographical regions of China</a>. People from the Northeast would first identify themselves as "Northeasterners" (东北人) before affiliating to individual provinces and cities/towns.</p>
<p>Ethnic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchu" title="Manchu">Manchus</a> form the second significant ethnic group in Northeast China, followed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnic_Mongols_in_China" title="Ethnic Mongols in China">Mongols</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_people" title="Korean people">Koreans</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Hui_people" title="Hui people">Huis</a>, as well as 49 other <a href="/wiki/List_of_ethnic_groups_in_China" title="List of ethnic groups in China">ethnic minorities</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Daur_people" title="Daur people">Daurs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xibe_people" title="Xibe people">Sibos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nanai_people" title="Nanai people">Hezhens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oroqen_people" title="Oroqen people">Oroqens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evenks" title="Evenks">Evenks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyz_people" title="Kyrgyz people">Kyrgyzs</a>, etc.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northeast_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Religion_in_Northeast_China" title="Religion in Northeast China">Religion in Northeast China</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northeast_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Northeast was one of the earliest regions to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrialize" title="Industrialize">industrialize</a> in China during the era of <a href="/wiki/Manchukuo" title="Manchukuo">Manchukuo</a>. After the founding of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, Northeast China continued to be a major industrial base of the country. Recent years, however, have seen the stagnation of Northeast China's heavy-industry-based economy, as China's economy continues to liberalize and privatize; the government has initialized the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revitalize_the_Northeast" title="Revitalize the Northeast">Revitalize the Northeast</a> campaign to counter this problem, and established the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Summit" title="Northeast Summit">Northeast Summit</a> to improve policy coordination and integration.</p>
<p>The region is, on the whole, more heavily urbanised than most parts of China, largely because it was the first part of the country to develop <a href="/wiki/Heavy_industry" title="Heavy industry">heavy industry</a> owing to its abundant <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> reserves. Major cities include <a href="/wiki/Shenyang" title="Shenyang">Shenyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dalian" title="Dalian">Dalian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harbin" title="Harbin">Harbin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Changchun" title="Changchun">Changchun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anshan" title="Anshan">Anshan</a>, all with several million inhabitants. Other cities include the <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> making centres of <a href="/wiki/Fushun" title="Fushun">Fushun</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anshan,_China" title="Anshan, China">Anshan</a> in Liaoning, <a href="/wiki/Jilin_City" title="Jilin City">Jilin City</a> in Jilin, and <a href="/wiki/Qiqihar" title="Qiqihar">Qiqihar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mudanjiang" title="Mudanjiang">Mudanjiang</a> in Heilongjiang. Harbin, more than any other city in China, possesses significant Russian influences: there are many Orthodox churches that have fallen out of use since the Cultural Revolution. Shenyang and Dalian, meanwhile, have sizable populations of Japanese and South Koreans due to their traditional linkages.</p>
<p>The rural population of the Northeast is heavily concentrated in the warmer southern part of the area, where very warm to hot summer weather permits crops such as <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">maize</a> and <a href="/wiki/Millet" title="Millet">millet</a> to be grown with high yields. <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">Soybeans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flax" title="Flax">flax</a> are also very important, as are <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">wheat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barley" title="Barley">barley</a>. The region possesses large flocks of <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pig" title="Pig">pigs</a> are abundant in the more densely settled southern part. The northern half of Heilongjiang province is so cold and poorly drained that agriculture is almost impossible; however, the <a href="/wiki/Amur_River" title="Amur River">Amur River</a> (Hēilóng Jiāng, 黑龙江, "Black Dragon River") provides very rich <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a> prospects, and sheep are even more abundant than in southern Heilongjiang province.</p>
<p>Northeast China is the country’s traditional <a href="/wiki/Heavy_industry" title="Heavy industry">industrial</a> base, focusing mainly on equipment manufacturing. Major industries include the <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automaker" title="Automaker">automobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shipbuilding" title="Shipbuilding">shipbuilding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> manufacturing, and <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">petroleum refining</a> industries. The gross regional product of the three northeast provinces totaled <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">¥</a>1.63 trillion in 2002. In recent years, the Chinese government has initialized the "Revitalize the Northeast campaign" to turn this region into one of China's economic growth engines. As of 2015 the region was losing population and the economy, dominated by state-owned enterprises, was stagnant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caixin92415_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Caixin92415-7">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northeast_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two main dialects of <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" title="Mandarin Chinese">Mandarin Chinese</a> spoken in Northeast China. The dialect spoken in the majority of the Northeast is the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Mandarin" title="Northeastern Mandarin">Northeastern Mandarin</a>, which is a very slight variant of the <a href="/wiki/Standard_Chinese" title="Standard Chinese">Standard Chinese</a> but retains sporadic elements from native <a href="/wiki/Tungusic_languages" title="Tungusic languages">Tungusic languages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>, where there are enough differences to give the dialect its own distinctive characteristics. However many residents in the southern fringe of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liaodong" title="Liaodong">Liaodong</a> region (mostly in <a href="/wiki/Dalian" title="Dalian">Dalian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dandong" title="Dandong">Dandong</a>) speak the <a href="/wiki/Jiaoliao_Mandarin" title="Jiaoliao Mandarin">Jiaoliao Mandarin</a>, which is actually a <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a> dialect. Some Northeasterners often jokingly call the Northeastern dialect as the "corny accent" (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">大碴子味</span>, referring to inland regions where <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">maize</a> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Staple_crop" title="Staple crop">staple crop</a>), and the Jiaoliao dialect as the "oystery accent" (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">海蛎子味</span>, referring to coastal areas like Dalian which are famous for <a href="/wiki/Seafood" title="Seafood">seafoods</a>).</p>
<p>Ethnic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchus" title="Manchus">Manchus</a> speak mostly <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" title="Mandarin Chinese">Mandarin</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Manchu_language" title="Manchu language">Manchu language</a> is almost extinct due to widespread assimilation to Han culture over the last four centuries. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnic_Mongols_in_China" title="Ethnic Mongols in China">Mongols</a> tend to be bilingual in their own <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a> tongues as well as Mandarin.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Chinese_cuisine" title="Northeastern Chinese cuisine">Northeastern Chinese cuisine</a> reflects the region's ethnic diversity. Native, Manchu, Northern Chinese, Russian, Korean and Japanese cooking styles all find their traces in Manchurian cooking. One of the distinguishing characteristics of the cuisine is the use of uncooked fresh vegetables. During the long winter season, pickled <a href="/wiki/Chinese_cabbage" title="Chinese cabbage">Chinese cabbage</a>, which is called "<a href="/wiki/Suan_cai" title="Suan cai">Suan Cai</a>", is preserved and used for cooking. In almost every other region of China, vegetables are cooked thoroughly before being eaten.</p>
<p>In general, the culture of Northeast China takes its elements from the cultures of <a href="/wiki/North_China" title="North China">North China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a>, where most of the <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a> migration into Northeast China, known as <a href="/wiki/Chuang_Guandong" title="Chuang Guandong">Chuang Guandong</a> (闯关东), originated, the native <a href="/wiki/Tungusic_peoples" title="Tungusic peoples">Tungusic peoples</a>, and its own innovations.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Errenzhuan" title="Errenzhuan">Errenzhuan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yangge" title="Yangge">Yangge</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jilin_opera" title="Jilin opera">Jilin opera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stilts" title="Stilts">Stilts</a> are popular forms of traditional entertainment in Northeast China. <i>"<a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Cradle_Song" title="Northeastern Cradle Song">Northeastern Cradle Song</a>" (东北摇篮曲)</i> is an example of the folk songs of this region.</p>
<p>Because of its climatic conditions, Northeast China is the base for China's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_sports" title="Winter sports">winter sports</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">Ice hockey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ice_skating" title="Ice skating">ice skating</a> athletes often come from or were educated in Northeast China.</p>
