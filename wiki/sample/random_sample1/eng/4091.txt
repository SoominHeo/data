<p><b>Düren</b> is a town in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, between <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> on the river <a href="/wiki/Rur" title="Rur">Rur</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_era">Roman era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Roman era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celt" title="Celt">Celts</a> inhabited the area now known as Düren before the Roman invasion. The Celts called their small settlement, <i>Durum</i> (meaning <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">castle</a>). After the Celts, other <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">Germanic tribes</a> settled this area. They were conquered by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a> under <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>.</p>
<p>Durum became a supply area for the rapidly growing Roman city of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>. Furthermore, a few important Roman roads skirt Durum (including the road from <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> to <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BClich" title="Jülich">Jülich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tongeren" title="Tongeren">Tongeren</a> and the road from Cologne to <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BClpich" title="Zülpich">Zülpich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trier" title="Trier">Trier</a>). The Romans remained in the area for about 400 years. The name "villa duria" occurred the first time in the <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> <a href="/wiki/Annals" title="Annals">Annals</a> in the year 747.</p>
<p>After the fall of the Roman Empire in the 5th century, the <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Franks</a> settled in Düren. The <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> king Pippin der Kleine (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pippin_the_Short" title="Pippin the Short">Pippin the Short</a>) often visited Düren in the 8th century and held a few important conventions there. Pippin was the father of the famous emperor <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a>. The Franks made of Durum a royal palace, from which the name <a href="/wiki/Palatine" title="Palatine">Palatine</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kaiserpfalz" title="Kaiserpfalz">Pfalz</a> in German) is derived. Charlemagne sojourned a few times there. Jeff was built at the place where, in 1991, the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Anne" title="Saint Anne">Saint Anne</a> Church is located today. Due to the frequent visits of Charlemagne, a few markets sprang up, such as the corn, cattle, wood, chicken, and butter markets, all of which contributed to Düren's development.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages">Middle Ages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Middle Ages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Düren obtained <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">city rights</a> in the early 13th century. Around 1200, the construction of the city wall was started, which includes 12 towers and 5 gates. The gates faced all directions: in the north, the <i>Philippstor</i>) and the <i>Wirteltor</i>, in the east the <i>Kölntor</i> (Cologne gate), in the south the <i>Obertor</i> and in the west the <i>Holztor</i> (wooden gate). There are still ruins of the gates today.</p>
<p>The chiseler Leonhard stole a small box with the relic of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Anne" title="Saint Anne">Saint Anne</a> out of the Mainzer Stiftskirche St. Stephan in 1501 and brought it to Düren. <a href="/wiki/Pope_Julius_II" title="Pope Julius II">Pope Julius II</a> decided on March 18, 1506 that Düren could keep the remains. They were kept in the <i>Martinskirche</i> (church of <a href="/wiki/Martin_of_Tours" title="Martin of Tours">Saint Martin</a>) which was renamed the <i>Annakirche</i> (church of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Anne" title="Saint Anne">Saint Anne</a>) in 1505. (Probably the church was renamed much later, because in the 19th century it was still called sometimes parish church of the holy Martinus). Saint Anne became the <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> of Düren. Every year, the <a href="/wiki/Calendar_of_saints" title="Calendar of saints">saint's day</a> of Saint Anne (July 26) is celebrated for one week with the Anna octavos and the Anna parish fair, one of the biggest folk festivals of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>

<p>In 1642, Düren was embroiled in the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>. Opposing troops destroyed the city. After the war has finally ended in 1648, <a href="/wiki/Bubonic_plague" title="Bubonic plague">plague</a> broke out and caused many lives to be lost. A second plague epidemic broke out in 1665. Due to the various attacks on the debilitated city, Düren was destroyed again in 1679. In this time, the settlement Miesheim was destroyed, never to be rebuilt.</p>
<p>Towards the end of the year 1755 in the area around Düren and <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a> began a series of <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquakes</a>, which reached its peak on February 18, 1756 with an earthquake with the strength 8 on the <a href="/wiki/Mercalli_intensity_scale" title="Mercalli intensity scale">Mercalli scale</a>. The series of earthquakes affected all of Europe, most famously the <a href="/wiki/1755_Lisbon_earthquake" title="1755 Lisbon earthquake">1755 Lisbon earthquake</a>.</p>
<p>The businesses in the area of Düren was affected since the 15th century by the <a href="/wiki/Drapery" title="Drapery">drapery</a> and metal industry. Since the beginning of the 17th century, paper industry had settled here, advantaged by the exceptionally <a href="/wiki/Soft_water" title="Soft water">soft water</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Rur" title="Rur">Rur</a>. Rütger von Scheven built the first <a href="/wiki/Paper_mill" title="Paper mill">paper mill</a> in Düren. In 1812, there were already 17 paper factories, 11 cloth- and blanket factories, one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masticator" title="Masticator">masticator</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Foundry" title="Foundry">iron foundries</a>.</p>
<p>In the year 1794, Düren was occupied by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_revolution" title="French revolution">French revolutionary troops</a>. From 1798 until 1814, Düren was the main city of the same named canton in the arrondissement <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a> of the French <i>Roerdepartements</i> (from the name of the River Rur (Roer) and <i>départment</i>). After the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a> in 1815, Düren was ceded to the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a> and was subsequently administered within the <a href="/wiki/Rhine_Province" title="Rhine Province">Rhine Province</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By 1900, Düren was among Germany's richest cities (with 42 millionaires and 93 factories) and had a population of 27,168. By comparison, less than 5,000 people had lived in Düren a century earlier.</p>
<p>The city of Düren was located on the main <a href="/wiki/Front_(military)" title="Front (military)">fighting front</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> invasion of Germany in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. During 1944 and 1945, the protracted and bloody <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Huertgen_Forest" title="Battle of Huertgen Forest">Battle for Hürtgenwald</a> was fought on Düren's district area, and on November 16, 1944, Düren was completely destroyed by Allied <a href="/wiki/Aerial_bombing_of_cities" title="Aerial bombing of cities">air bombings</a>. Approximately 22,000 people lived in Düren at that time, and 3,000 of them died during the bombing. Those who survived were <a href="/wiki/Emergency_evacuation" title="Emergency evacuation">evacuated</a> to central Germany. Destroyed buildings included the <a href="/wiki/Stadttheater_D%C3%BCren" title="Stadttheater Düren">Stadttheater Düren</a> (1907), designed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jugendstil" title="Jugendstil">Jugendstil</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carl_Moritz_(architect)" title="Carl Moritz (architect)">Carl Moritz</a>.</p>
<p>On February 25, 1945, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Army" title="US Army">U.S.</a> troops crossed the <a href="/wiki/Rur" title="Rur">Rur</a> at Düren. After the war was over in the summer that year, many evacuated people came back to the destroyed city and started to rebuild their homes against the advice of the American troops. By June 1945, the population had risen to 3,806. Most of the architecture in Düren therefore dates from the 1950s.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_points_of_interest">Culture and points of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture and points of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most famous museum of Düren is the Leopold Hoesch Museum. The in 1905 in <a href="/wiki/Baroque_Revival_architecture" title="Baroque Revival architecture">Baroque Revival architecture</a> erected building presents changing exhibitions of contemporary art. Since 1986, can also be seen artworks of the international Biennale PaperArt. Since 2006, is in the former nurses' home of the St. Augustinus Hospital Lendersdorf the Düren Carnival Museum. The most recent museum is the in 2009 founded Stadtmuseum Düren. This museum shows an exhibition of the local history.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatre_and_music">Theatre and music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Theatre and music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The former Stadttheater Düren was opened in January 1907. By the bombing of November 16, 1944 the theatre was almost completely destroyed. Today cultural performances take mostly place at the <i>Haus der Stadt</i>. As a venue for major concerts serves since 2004 the multi-functional <i>Arena Kreis Düren</i>, which has around 2000 seats.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings">Buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the edge of the forest in the Niederau district lies the Schloß Burgau. The <a href="/wiki/Water_castle" title="Water castle">water castle</a> was at the beginning of the 14th century inhabited by the Counts of <a href="/wiki/Heinsberg" title="Heinsberg">Heinsberg</a>. After it was destroyed in 1944, the restoration process lasted from 1979 to 1998. In the Theodor Heuss Park is the in 1892 erected Bismarck Memorial, to commemorate the most famous honorary citizen from Düren. The town hall was inaugurated <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in 1959. It now belongs as an example of the architecture of the 1950s under <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage_management" title="Cultural heritage management">Cultural heritage management</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Worship">Worship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Worship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The most important church in the city is the Annakirche. The church was completely destroyed by bombing in 1944 and rebuilt in the 1950s under the guidance of the architect <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Schwarz_(architect)" title="Rudolf Schwarz (architect)">Rudolf Schwarz</a>. Throughout the city there are 15 other Catholic parish and church communities, including in Arnoldsweiler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The most important Protestant church in Düren is the Christuskirche At its inauguration in 1954 she possessed the highest freestanding <a href="/wiki/Bell_tower" title="Bell tower">bell tower</a> of Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Up to the destruction of the <a href="/wiki/Kristallnacht" title="Kristallnacht">Kristallnacht</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogue</a> was located in the Schützenstraße.On its place is now a stele from Düren artist Rückriem.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emblem">Emblem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Emblem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The emblem of the city of Düren is divided. It shows on the top a red castle, below that, a black eagle and in the lower half a black lion with a red tongue. The black eagle refers to the old history of Düren as a royal city and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Free_City" title="Imperial Free City">Reichsstadt</a>. In 1242–46 Düren was bonded to the dukes of Jülich (later, <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a> was also Duke of Jülich). Their emblem was a lion passant, with open mouth and a red tongue.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Düren has its own radio station (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_Rur&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Radio Rur (page does not exist)">Radio Rur</a>). The station broadcasts on 92.7 and 107.5 MHz, and on cable at 87.5 MHz. There are two daily newspapers (Dürener Zeitung, Dürener Nachrichten) and several weekly papers.</p>
