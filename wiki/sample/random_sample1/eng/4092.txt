<p><b>Durbuy</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[dyʁ.bɥi]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Walloon</a> <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(province_of_Belgium)" title="Luxembourg (province of Belgium)">Luxembourg</a>. On 1 January 2007 the municipality had 10,633 inhabitants. The total area is 156.61 km², giving a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 67.9 inhabitants per km².</p>
<p>The municipality consists of the following <a href="/wiki/Deelgemeente" title="Deelgemeente">sub-municipalities</a>: Durbuy proper, <a href="/wiki/Barvaux-sur-Ourthe" title="Barvaux-sur-Ourthe">Barvaux</a>, Bende, <a href="/wiki/Bomal" title="Bomal">Bomal</a>, Borlon, Grandhan, Heyd, Izier, Septon, Tohogne, Villers-Sainte-Gertrude, and <a href="/wiki/W%C3%A9ris" title="Wéris">Wéris</a>.</p>
<p>In medieval times Durbuy was an important centre of commerce and industry. In 1331, the town was elevated to the rank of <a href="/wiki/City_rights_in_the_Low_Countries" title="City rights in the Low Countries">city</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_I,_Count_of_Luxemburg" title="John I, Count of Luxemburg">John I, Count of Luxemburg</a>, and King of Bohemia.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ourthe" title="Ourthe">Ourthe</a> river flows through the municipality.</p>
<p>Tourism and recreation are its main activities nowadays.</p>
