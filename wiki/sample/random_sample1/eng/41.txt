<p><br/>
Year <b>1160</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCLX</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Friday" title="Leap year starting on Friday">leap year starting on Friday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1160&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1160&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1160&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Almohads" title="Almohads">Almohads</a> conquer <a href="/wiki/Mahdia" title="Mahdia">Mahdia</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italo-Normans" title="Italo-Normans">Normans</a> after an important naval success near the city against Christian reenforcement coming from Sicily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Picard1997_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Picard1997-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>A commercial treaty between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Almohads" title="Almohads">Almohad caliphate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Pisa" title="Republic of Pisa">Republic of Pisa</a> opens the North African ports to Tuscan merchants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Picard1997_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Picard1997-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1160&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Heiji_Rebellion" title="Heiji Rebellion">Heiji Rebellion</a> continues in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Some 500 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minamoto" title="Minamoto">Minamoto</a> rebels opposed to the retired emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go-Shirakawa" title="Go-Shirakawa">Go-Shirakawa</a> carry out a daring raid on the Sanjo Palace.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yasovarman_II" title="Yasovarman II">Yasovarman II</a> succeeds his uncle <a href="/wiki/Dharanindravarman_II" title="Dharanindravarman II">Dharanindravarman</a> as ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Khmer_Empire" title="Khmer Empire">Khmer Empire</a>. Dharanindravarman's son <a href="/wiki/Jayavarman_VII" title="Jayavarman VII">Jayavarman</a> acquiesces to his cousin's succession and goes into exile in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Champa" title="Champa">Champa</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reynald_of_Chatillon" title="Reynald of Chatillon">Reynald of Chatillon</a> is arrested by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1160&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_3" title="February 3">February 3</a> – Emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_Barbarossa" title="Frederick Barbarossa">Frederick Barbarossa</a> takes <a href="/wiki/Crema,_Lombardy" title="Crema, Lombardy">Crema</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> following a cruel siege, as part of his campaign against the independent Italian city-states.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_18" title="May 18">May 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Eric_IX_of_Sweden" title="Eric IX of Sweden">Erik Jedvardsson</a> is murdered, after which his murderer <a href="/wiki/Magnus_II_of_Sweden" title="Magnus II of Sweden">Magnus Henriksen</a> proclaims himself king of Sweden. He is murdered in turn the following year, however. Eric is soon worshipped as a saint. Though never formally canonized by the pope, he eventually becomes the <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> of Sweden.</li>
<li>Large <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> offensive in the <a href="/wiki/Alentejo" title="Alentejo">Alentejo</a> against the Muslims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-picard2000_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-picard2000-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>The city of <a href="/wiki/Tomar" title="Tomar">Tomar</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> by <a href="/wiki/Gualdim_Pais" title="Gualdim Pais">Gualdim Pais</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spital_am_Semmering" title="Spital am Semmering">Spital am Semmering</a> is founded by Margrave <a href="/wiki/Ottokar_III_of_Styria" title="Ottokar III of Styria">Ottokar III of Styria</a>.</li>
<li>A plot of land at <a href="/wiki/Miholjanec" title="Miholjanec">Miholjanec</a> is donated to the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Templar" title="Knights Templar">Knights Templar</a>, who build a monastery in nearby Zdelia; this is the earliest historical mention of the Templars in <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and Hungary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
