<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">2010 FIFA World Cup Group H</h1>

<p><b>Group H of the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a></b> began on 16 June and ended on 25 June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The group consisted of reigning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Football_Championship" title="UEFA European Football Championship">European champions</a> <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overall">Overall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_FIFA_World_Cup_Group_H&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chile and Spain were in the same group in <a href="/wiki/1950_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1950 FIFA World Cup">1950</a>, in a group from which only the Spanish team qualified for the next round. Chile and Switzerland were also in the same group in <a href="/wiki/1962_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1962 FIFA World Cup">1962</a>, when Chile was host and went on to finish in third place. Switzerland and Spain competed in the same group in <a href="/wiki/1966_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1966 FIFA World Cup">1966</a>, but neither advanced to the next round. Finally, Honduras and Spain were in the same group in <a href="/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1982 FIFA World Cup">1982</a>. Four years later, in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">next World Cup</a>, Chile and Spain would meet again in <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Group_B" title="2014 FIFA World Cup Group B">Group B</a>, against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_soccer_team" title="Australia national soccer team">Australia</a>; Chile would upset Spain 2–0 in their second match, eliminating Spain from the tournament.</p>
<p>The winners of this group, Spain, advanced to face <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_Group_G" title="2010 FIFA World Cup Group G">Group G</a> runners-up <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>, while Chile, as group runners-up, faced <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, the winners of Group G.</p>
