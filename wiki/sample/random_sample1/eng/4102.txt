<p><b>Denis Lavant</b> (born 17 June 1961) is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> known for his distinctive face and the physically demanding aspects of the roles he plays, which often involve <a href="/wiki/Slapstick" title="Slapstick">slapstick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acrobatics" title="Acrobatics">acrobatics</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dance</a>, as well as for his long-standing association with director <a href="/wiki/Leos_Carax" title="Leos Carax">Leos Carax</a>. Lavant has played the lead role in all but one of Carax's films. Lavant is also known for his roles in <a href="/wiki/Claire_Denis" title="Claire Denis">Claire Denis</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Beau_Travail" title="Beau Travail">Beau Travail</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Korine" title="Harmony Korine">Harmony Korine</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Mister_Lonely" title="Mister Lonely">Mister Lonely</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denis_Lavant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lavant was born in <a href="/wiki/Neuilly-sur-Seine" title="Neuilly-sur-Seine">Neuilly-sur-Seine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hauts-de-Seine" title="Hauts-de-Seine">Hauts-de-Seine</a>, in France. At 13, he took courses in pantomime and the circus, fascinated by <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Marceau" title="Marcel Marceau">Marcel Marceau</a>. He trained at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Conservatoire" title="Paris Conservatoire">Paris Conservatoire</a> under <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jacques_Lassalle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jacques Lassalle (page does not exist)">Jacques Lassalle</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AC_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AC-1">[1]</a></sup> and began his professional career in 1982 in theatre, acting in Shakespeare's <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Merchant_of_Venice" title="The Merchant of Venice">The Merchant of Venice</a></i>. In 1982 he appeared in the television film <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Ombre_sur_la_plage&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="L'Ombre sur la plage (page does not exist)">L'Ombre sur la plage</a></i>, before playing the minor part of <a href="/wiki/Montparnasse" title="Montparnasse">Montparnasse</a> in <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hossein" title="Robert Hossein">Robert Hossein</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables_(1982_film)" title="Les Misérables (1982 film)">Les Misérables</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AC_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-AC-1">[1]</a></sup> which was entered into the <a href="/wiki/13th_Moscow_International_Film_Festival" title="13th Moscow International Film Festival">13th Moscow International Film Festival</a> where it won a Special Prize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moscow1983_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moscow1983-2">[2]</a></sup> He appeared in several further minor roles before making his breakthrough in 1984 as the lead in <i><a href="/wiki/Boy_Meets_Girl_(1984_film)" title="Boy Meets Girl (1984 film)">Boy Meets Girl</a></i>, playing a depressed, aspiring filmmaker who falls in love with a suicidal young woman. The film marked the directorial debut of <a href="/wiki/Leos_Carax" title="Leos Carax">Leos Carax</a>, with whose films Lavant has been associated ever since.</p>
<p>In 1986, Lavant and Carax worked together again on the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Mauvais_Sang" title="Mauvais Sang">Mauvais Sang</a></i> and again in 1991 on Carax's third film, <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Amants_du_Pont-Neuf" title="Les Amants du Pont-Neuf">Les Amants du Pont-Neuf</a></i>. In both <i>Mauvais Sang</i> and <i>Les Amants du Pont-Neuf</i>, Lavant starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Juliette_Binoche" title="Juliette Binoche">Juliette Binoche</a>. In 1998, Lavant appeared in the iconic <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Glazer" title="Jonathan Glazer">Jonathan Glazer</a>-directed video for the <a href="/wiki/Unkle" title="Unkle">UNKLE</a> song <a href="/wiki/Rabbit_in_Your_Headlights" title="Rabbit in Your Headlights">Rabbit in Your Headlights</a>, and in 1999, he played one of the lead roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Beau_Travail" title="Beau Travail">Beau Travail</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Claire_Denis" title="Claire Denis">Claire Denis</a>. In 2007, he appeared in <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Korine" title="Harmony Korine">Harmony Korine</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Mister_Lonely" title="Mister Lonely">Mister Lonely</a></i>, in which he portrayed a <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Chaplin" title="Charlie Chaplin">Charlie Chaplin</a> impersonator. Lavant, who does not speak English, took an intensive language course in preparation and learned his lines phonetically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His longtime associate Leos Carax appears in a supporting role as the main character's <a href="/wiki/Talent_agent" title="Talent agent">talent agent</a>.</p>
<p>In 2008, Lavant and Carax re-united for the <a href="/wiki/Anthology_film" title="Anthology film">anthology film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Tokyo!" title="Tokyo!">Tokyo!</a></i>, which marked their first work together since <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lovers_on_the_Bridge" title="Lovers on the Bridge">Lovers on the Bridge</a></i> and Carax's first major directing work in nearly a decade. Carax's segment for the film, called "Merde," starred Lavant as a violent monster who lives in the sewers of Tokyo and speaks in a gibberish language, venturing out occasionally to attack passersby.</p>
<p>In 2012 he starred in <a href="/wiki/Leos_Carax" title="Leos Carax">Leos Carax</a>' film <i><a href="/wiki/Holy_Motors" title="Holy Motors">Holy Motors</a></i> where he plays a "chameleonic actor on assignment, ferried around Paris in a white limousine and changing en route from beggar-woman to satyr to assassin to victim."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The film was an entrant at the <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes Film Festival</a>.</p>
