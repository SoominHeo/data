<p><b>Denis Ménochet</b> (born 18 September 1976) is a French <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. Ménochet is known to international audiences for his role as a French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dairy_farmer" title="Dairy farmer">dairy farmer</a> interrogated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazis" title="Nazis">Nazis</a> for harboring <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> in the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Inglourious_Basterds" title="Inglourious Basterds">Inglourious Basterds</a></i>.</p>

<p>Elizabeth Weitzman, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_critic" title="Film critic">film critic</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Daily_News" title="New York Daily News">New York Daily News</a></i>, praised Ménochet's work opposite <a href="/wiki/Christoph_Waltz" title="Christoph Waltz">Christoph Waltz</a> in the opening scene of <i><a href="/wiki/Inglourious_Basterds" title="Inglourious Basterds">Inglourious Basterds</a></i>. Weitzman wrote in August 2009, "The terrific opening, for example, does feature a hailstorm of bullets. What you'll remember best, though, is the haunted silence of actor Denis Ménochet, playing a French farmer accused of harboring <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nydn_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nydn-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
