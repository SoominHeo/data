<p><b>Dragan Mrđa</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic" title="Serbian Cyrillic">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: Драган Мрђа; born 23 January 1984) is a <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League_Division_2" title="J. League Division 2">J. League Division 2</a> club <a href="/wiki/Omiya_Ardija" title="Omiya Ardija">Omiya Ardija</a>. In international competition he has represented <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragan_Mr%C4%91a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragan_Mr%C4%91a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After spending many years of his youth playing with <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star Belgrade</a>, during which he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/FK_Jedinstvo_Ub" title="FK Jedinstvo Ub">FK Jedinstvo Ub</a>, he decided to accept a challenge that came from Belgium and moved to <a href="/wiki/Lierse_S.K." title="Lierse S.K.">Lierse S.K.</a> in August 2005. In the summer of 2006, he was loaned to another <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_First_Division" title="Belgian First Division">Belgian First Division</a> club, <a href="/wiki/S.V._Zulte_Waregem" title="S.V. Zulte Waregem">S.V. Zulte Waregem</a> before moving to Russia to play with <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Khimki" title="FC Khimki">FC Khimki</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vojvodina">Vojvodina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragan_Mr%C4%91a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Vojvodina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After one season in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, he returned to Serbia to join <a href="/wiki/FK_Vojvodina" title="FK Vojvodina">FK Vojvodina</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_SuperLiga" title="Serbian SuperLiga">Serbian SuperLiga</a>. In spite of arriving to the club off of dry spells in Belgium and Russia, he became Vojvodina's captain after a remarkably short period of time due to a spontaneous explosion of good form. His prolific goal-scoring with Vojvodina earned him calls from the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbian national team</a> from 2008, having been called up for a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> and another friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_football_team" title="Austria national football team">Austria</a> which took place the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> By the end of the 2009–10 season, Mrđa had scored 22 goals in 29 league games and was named <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Serbian_SuperLiga#Awards" title="2009–10 Serbian SuperLiga">SuperLiga Player of the Year</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sion">Sion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragan_Mr%C4%91a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After two seasons spent in <a href="/wiki/Novi_Sad" title="Novi Sad">Novi Sad</a>, in summer 2010, he signed a 3-year contract with Swiss side <a href="/wiki/FC_Sion" title="FC Sion">FC Sion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In March 2011 in a match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">FC Zürich</a> he suffered a serious knee injury which kept him out of football for six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Red_Star_Belgrade">Return to Red Star Belgrade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragan_Mr%C4%91a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to Red Star Belgrade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After seasons spent in Sion, Mrđa returned to the club at which he debuted professionally, signing a two-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star Belgrade</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On September 29, 2013, he scored a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> in a 5-0 win against <a href="/wiki/FK_Spartak_Subotica" title="FK Spartak Subotica">FK Spartak Subotica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On November 23, 2013, he scored another <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> in a 4–1 win against <a href="/wiki/FK_Sloboda_U%C5%BEice" title="FK Sloboda Užice">FK Sloboda Užice</a>, becoming Red Star's highest goal-scorer of the half-season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Omiya_Ardija">Omiya Ardija</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragan_Mr%C4%91a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Omiya Ardija">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 1, 2014, Mrđa joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League_Division_1" title="J. League Division 1">J. League Division 1</a> club <a href="/wiki/Omiya_Ardija" title="Omiya Ardija">Omiya Ardija</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Mrđa scored on his debut on 19 July 2014 helping them to earn a 3–3 draw against <a href="/wiki/Sanfrecce_Hiroshima" title="Sanfrecce Hiroshima">Sanfrecce Hiroshima</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He played 18 times for Omiya in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_J._League_Division_1" title="2014 J. League Division 1">2014</a> as they suffered relegation to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League_Division_2" title="J. League Division 2">Division 2</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragan_Mr%C4%91a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mrđa was a part of the Serbian U21 team that made it to the final of the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>, where he scored coming off the bench against the Netherlands U21 side. By the end of the championship he had scored a total of two goals, which was on level with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Babel" title="Ryan Babel">Ryan Babel</a>. Mrđa scored two goals for the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbian national team</a> in a 3–0 friendly win against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> on April 7, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He was then called up by <a href="/wiki/Radomir_Anti%C4%87" title="Radomir Antić">Radomir Antić</a> to Serbia's squad at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>, although Mrđa ended up not playing a single game in the competition.</p>
