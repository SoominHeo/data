<p>The <b>Draconettidae</b>, <b>slope dragonets</b>, are a small family (about 14 species) of fish in the order <a href="/wiki/Perciformes" title="Perciformes">Perciformes</a>. They are found in temperate to tropical waters of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Oceans</a>. They are closely related to, and appear similar to, the fish of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Callionymidae" title="Callionymidae">Callionymidae</a>. They are small fish, the largest species reaching 12 cm (4.7 in) long. Like the callionymids, they are bottom-dwelling fish, and usually <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">sexually dimorphic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EoF_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EoF-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
