<p><b>Drammen</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Buskerud" title="Buskerud">Buskerud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. The port and river city of Drammen is centrally located in the eastern and most populated part of Norway. Drammen is the capital of the county of Buskerud.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are more than 63 000 inhabitants in the municipality, but the city is the regional capital of an area with more than 150,000 inhabitants. Drammen and the surrounding communities are growing more than ever before. The city makes good use of the river and inland waterway called <a href="/wiki/Drammensfjord" title="Drammensfjord">Drammensfjord</a>, both for recreation, activities and housing. No city in the country has received as many awards for environmental and urban development as Drammen: 6 national and 2 international prizes since 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_and_coat_of_arms">Name and coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name and coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> form of the city's name was <i>Drafn</i>, and this was originally the name of the inner part of <a href="/wiki/Drammensfjord" title="Drammensfjord">Drammensfjord</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Fjord" title="Fjord">fjord</a> is, however, probably named after the river <a href="/wiki/Drammenselva" title="Drammenselva">Drammenselva</a> (Norse <i>Drǫfn</i>), and this again is derived from <i>drǫfn</i> f '<a href="/wiki/Wave" title="Wave">wave</a>'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat-of-arms</a> is from modern times. They were granted on 17 November 1960. The arms are blue with a gray/silver <a href="/wiki/Column" title="Column">column</a> on top of a foundation of rocks. A <a href="/wiki/Key_(lock)" title="Key (lock)">key</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Viking</a> <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">sword</a> are crossed in the middle forming an <a href="/wiki/X" title="X">x</a>. It is based upon the old seal dating from 1723 for <a href="/wiki/Bragernes" title="Bragernes">Bragernes</a>, one of the central parts of Drammen. The <a href="/wiki/Motto" title="Motto">motto</a> for Bragernes (in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>) was <i>In Fide Et Justitia Fortitudo</i> (English: in faith and justice is strength), and the items in the seal are referring to this: key = faith, sword = justice, column on rocks = strength.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality of Drammen was established on 1 January 1838 (see <a href="/wiki/Formannskapsdistrikt" title="Formannskapsdistrikt">formannskapsdistrikt</a>). The rural municipality of <a href="/wiki/Skoger" title="Skoger">Skoger</a> was merged with the municipality of Drammen on 1 January 1964 and was transferred from <a href="/wiki/Vestfold" title="Vestfold">Vestfold</a> county to Buskerud county at the same time. The city itself has 66 000 inhabitants,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> making it Norway's ninth largest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Petroglyph" title="Petroglyph">Rock carvings</a> at Åskollen and Austad are 6000 to 7000 years old, and are the first signs of human activity in the area. The largest rock carving at Åskollen depicts a <a href="/wiki/Moose" title="Moose">moose</a>.</p>
<p>Drammen originally consisted of three <a href="/wiki/Market_town#Norway" title="Market town">small seaports</a>: <a href="/wiki/Bragernes" title="Bragernes">Bragernes</a> (on the northern side of the <a href="/wiki/Drammenselva" title="Drammenselva">Drammenselva</a> river) and <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%B8ms%C3%B8" title="Strømsø">Strømsø</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tangen,_Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tangen, Drammen (page does not exist)">Tangen</a> (both on the southern side of the river). For trade purposes, small seaports were placed under market towns. Despite their geographical proximity, Bragernes was placed under <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Christiania</a> and Strømsø under <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B8nsberg" title="Tønsberg">Tønsberg</a>. For this reason, cooperation between the adjacent seaport towns was almost impossible.</p>
<p>In 1662, a merger was proposed to unite Strømsø and Bragernes to form a market town with the name Frederiksstrøm. The proposal was rejected by <a href="/wiki/Frederick_III_of_Denmark" title="Frederick III of Denmark">Frederick III of Denmark</a>. Bragernes received limited market town rights in 1715, and merged with Strømsø to gain status as a single <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cities_of_Norway" title="Cities of Norway">city</a> on 19 June 1811.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Its geographical location made the city favorable for seafaring, <a href="/wiki/Shipbuilding" title="Shipbuilding">shipbuilding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Log_driving" title="Log driving">log driving</a>, timber trade. During 19th century, paper and pulp industries were developed. Large parts of the city were ruined in the great fire of 12–13 July 1866, which led to the reconstruction of the city centre, including the characteristic town square and Bragernes church. The <a href="/wiki/Drammen_Line" title="Drammen Line">Drammen Line</a> (<i>Drammenbanen</i> ) opened in 1872 providing rail service between Drammen and Oslo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1909, Drammen got the first <a href="/wiki/Trolleybus" title="Trolleybus">trolleybus</a> system in <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Trolleybuses_in_Drammen" title="Trolleybuses in Drammen">Drammen trolleybus</a>. The lines ran until 1967. For many years the centre of Drammen suffered from heavy traffic. In 1970, <a href="/wiki/Drammen_Bridge" title="Drammen Bridge">Drammen Bridge</a> with four lanes on European route E18 was built and in 1999 the opening of the Bragernes tunnel (<i>Bragernestunnelen</i>) diverted additional traffic away from the centre of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In recent years, the city centre has seen the introduction of new housing, shopping facilities, restaurants, cafes and bars, as well as a public pathway along the <a href="/wiki/Drammenselva" title="Drammenselva">Drammenselva</a> river.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Drammen observed its 200th anniversary with many citywide jubilee celebrations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Drammen's district heating system was upgraded to use <a href="/wiki/Heat_pump#Water_source_heat_pumps_.28WSHP.29" title="Heat pump">water-sourced heat pumps</a>, drawing on local fjord water, to support population growth in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc-20150310_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-20150310-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Drammen is one of the larger cities in Norway, and lies about 40 kilometres (25 mi) from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> of Norway, <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>. The city centre lies at the end of a <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valley</a>, on both sides of the Drammenselva river, and where the river meets the <a href="/wiki/Drammensfjord" title="Drammensfjord">Drammensfjord</a>. Drammen is also the main <a href="/wiki/Harbor" title="Harbor">harbor</a> for car and fruit import in Norway.</p>
<p>As of 1 January 2007, the population of the urban area of Drammen is 93,006. Drammen is the sixth largest urban area of Norway and occupies territory in five <a href="/wiki/List_of_municipalities_of_Norway" title="List of municipalities of Norway">municipalities</a>: Drammen (with about 61% of the population), <a href="/wiki/Nedre_Eiker" title="Nedre Eiker">Nedre Eiker</a> (23%), <a href="/wiki/%C3%98vre_Eiker" title="Øvre Eiker">Øvre Eiker</a> (8%), <a href="/wiki/Lier,_Norway" title="Lier, Norway">Lier</a> (5%), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%C3%B8yken" title="Røyken">Røyken</a> (3%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>Øvre Sund</i> area, situated along Drammenselva, will be regulated by the municipality in order to restore this area's character. The buildings there are from the 18th and 19th century, and contribute, as well as the river and the brewery, to give the city a special identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2008 Drammen won the prestigious prize for the best city development in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aass_Brewery">Aass Brewery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Aass Brewery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Aass_Brewery" title="Aass Brewery">Aass Brewery</a></div>
<p>Aass Brewery is the oldest surviving <a href="/wiki/Brewery" title="Brewery">brewery</a> in Norway, and has won acclaim for both its <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">beer</a> and its conservative building. Founded in 1834, the brewery's primary products are soft drinks, beer and aquavit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bridges">Bridges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bridges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Øvre Sund Bridge (<i>Øvre Sund bru</i>) - crosses Drammenselva in the center of Drammen <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li>Drammen City Bridge (<i>Drammensbrua bybro</i>) - concrete bridge connectings the two centers of the town, built 1936</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drammen_Museum">Drammen Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Drammen Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Drammen_Museum_of_Art_and_Cultural_History" title="Drammen Museum of Art and Cultural History">Drammen Museum of Art and Cultural History</a></div>
<p>The Drammen Museum of Art and Cultural History includes Marienlyst, a manor house from ca. 1770, museum building from 1930 with the museum's administration, permanent exhibitions and collections, and Lyche pavilion from 1990 with the gallery, temporary exhibitions and museum café, Halling yard, with 5 old buildings, the oldest from 1760s. The museum also includes the two largest preserved like farms in Drammen, <a href="/wiki/Gulskogen_Manor" title="Gulskogen Manor">Gulskogen Manor</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Austad_farm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Austad farm (page does not exist)">Austad farm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drammen_Spiral">Drammen Spiral</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Drammen Spiral">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Drammen_Spiral" title="Drammen Spiral">Drammen Spiral</a></i> is a road tunnel that allows access to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Skansen_Ridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Skansen Ridge (page does not exist)">Skansen Ridge</a>, 600 ft above the town. It opened in 1961 on the site of a former quarry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drammen_Theater">Drammen Theater</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drammen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Drammen Theater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Drammen Theater in Bragernes was built in 1869 and was designed by architect <a href="/wiki/Emil_Victor_Langlet" title="Emil Victor Langlet">Emil Victor Langlet</a>. The theater was the first modern theater in the country. It was designed in a complex Renaissance style with symmetrical facades and round arched windows. After Drammen Theater suffered total destruction by fire in December 1993, a new theater was rebuilt on the model of the original house. It was finished during February 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
