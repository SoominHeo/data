<p><b>Dragonette</b> is a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, formed in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The band consists of singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Martina_Sorbara" title="Martina Sorbara">Martina Sorbara</a>, bassist and producer Dan Kurtz (also in <a href="/wiki/The_New_Deal_(band)" title="The New Deal (band)">The New Deal</a>), and drummer Joel Stouffer.</p>
<p>Dragonette released a <a href="/wiki/Dragonette_EP" title="Dragonette EP">self-titled EP</a> in 2005 before being signed to <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Records" title="Mercury Records">Mercury Records</a> and relocating to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, where they recorded and released their debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Galore_(Dragonette_album)" title="Galore (Dragonette album)">Galore</a></i>, in August 2007 to moderate critical appreciation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fixin_to_Thrill" title="Fixin to Thrill">Fixin to Thrill</a></i>, was released in September 2009. The group's third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bodyparts_(album)" title="Bodyparts (album)">Bodyparts</a></i>, was released in September 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragonette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9308:_Formation_and_Galore">2005–08: Formation and <i>Galore</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragonette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2005–08: Formation and Galore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sorbara and Kurtz met at a Canadian music festival<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and formed a duo named The Fuzz,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> making music together in their basement recording studio for fun. The duo formed Dragonette after these initial recordings, and supported <a href="/wiki/New_Order_(band)" title="New Order (band)">New Order</a> on only their second live performance. In 2005, the band released the six-track <i><a href="/wiki/Dragonette_EP" title="Dragonette EP">Dragonette EP</a></i>, prior to signing a record deal with UK record label <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Records" title="Mercury Records">Mercury Records</a>.</p>
<p>Dragonette relocated to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> to record their debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Galore_(Dragonette_album)" title="Galore (Dragonette album)">Galore</a></i>, released on August 6, 2007, and toured the UK supporting local acts, including <a href="/wiki/Basement_Jaxx" title="Basement Jaxx">Basement Jaxx</a> (with whom Sorbara also recorded the track "Take Me Back to Your House") and <a href="/wiki/Sugababes" title="Sugababes">Sugababes</a> and played headline gigs in small venues across the UK and Canada. British musician Will Stapleton replaced Simon Craig on guitar following the relocation. Dragonette filmed two music videos, one for "Jesus Doesn't Love Me" and another for "<a href="/wiki/I_Get_Around_(Dragonette_song)" title="I Get Around (Dragonette song)">I Get Around</a>". The former video leaked onto the Internet, while the other served as promotion for their first single release. "I Get Around" reached number 92 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. A follow-up single release, "<a href="/wiki/Take_It_Like_a_Man_(Dragonette_song)" title="Take It Like a Man (Dragonette song)">Take It Like a Man</a>", fared no better, failing to enter the top 100 of the UK Singles Chart. "I Get Around" was released in the band's home country of Canada where it outperformed the UK release, peaking at number 57 on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>. After <i>Galore</i> failed to sell as expected, Dragonette parted ways with Mercury Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dragonette received a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award_for_Breakthrough_Group_of_the_Year" title="Juno Award for Breakthrough Group of the Year">New Group of the Year</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_2008" title="Juno Awards of 2008">Juno Awards of 2008</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Kurtz and Sorbara co-wrote and co-produced the song "Grab a Hold" for <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a>'s 2008 album <i><a href="/wiki/Bring_Ya_to_the_Brink" title="Bring Ya to the Brink">Bring Ya to the Brink</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> That same year Will James Stapleton left the band, to be replaced by Chris Huggett.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9311:_Fixin_to_Thrill_and_collaborations">2009–11: <i>Fixin to Thrill</i> and collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragonette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–11: Fixin to Thrill and collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dragonette's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fixin_to_Thrill" title="Fixin to Thrill">Fixin to Thrill</a></i>, was released on September 29, 2009. The album was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award_for_Dance_Recording_of_the_Year" title="Juno Award for Dance Recording of the Year">Dance Recording of the Year</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_2011" title="Juno Awards of 2011">Juno Awards of 2011</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dragonette have collaborated with several <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a> DJs for releases, to varying degrees of success. Although <a href="/wiki/Basement_Jaxx" title="Basement Jaxx">Basement Jaxx</a>'s 2006 song "Take Me Back to Your House" only featured Sorbara on vocals, it was promoted by the band as a Dragonette collaborative. The band have since recorded several tracks with French DJ <a href="/wiki/Martin_Solveig" title="Martin Solveig">Martin Solveig</a>, including "<a href="/wiki/Boys_%26_Girls_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Boys &amp; Girls (Martin Solveig song)">Boys &amp; Girls</a>" in 2009, "<a href="/wiki/Hello_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Hello (Martin Solveig song)">Hello</a>" in 2010, and "Can't Stop" and "<a href="/wiki/Big_in_Japan_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Big in Japan (Martin Solveig song)">Big in Japan</a>" in 2011; all of these tracks appear on Solveig's 2011 album <i><a href="/wiki/Smash_(Martin_Solveig_album)" title="Smash (Martin Solveig album)">Smash</a></i>. Solveig and Dragonette won the <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_2012" title="Juno Awards of 2012">Juno Award</a> for Dance Recording of the Year for "Hello" in April 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2010, the band also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Kaskade" title="Kaskade">Kaskade</a> on "<a href="/wiki/Fire_in_Your_New_Shoes" title="Fire in Your New Shoes">Fire in Your New Shoes</a>" and with <a href="/wiki/Don_Diablo" title="Don Diablo">Don Diablo</a> on "<a href="/wiki/Animale" title="Animale">Animale</a>".</p>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Double_A-side" title="Double A-side">double A-side</a> single containing the songs "Our Summer" and "Volcano" was released digitally on July 27, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OurSummerVolcano_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-OurSummerVolcano-10">[10]</a></sup> On August 3, 2010, Dragonette released the compilation album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mixin_to_Thrill" title="Mixin to Thrill">Mixin to Thrill</a></i> digitally, while a limited-edition CD was sold via the band's online store. The compilation contains three new songs—"Volcano", "Our Summer", and "My Things"—as well as several remixes from the previous year. In 2011, Dragonette worked with <a href="/wiki/Girls_Aloud" title="Girls Aloud">Girls Aloud</a> member <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Roberts" title="Nicola Roberts">Nicola Roberts</a>, co-writing and producing the single "<a href="/wiki/Lucky_Day_(Nicola_Roberts_song)" title="Lucky Day (Nicola Roberts song)">Lucky Day</a>" for her debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella%27s_Eyes" title="Cinderella's Eyes">Cinderella's Eyes</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313:_Bodyparts">2012–13: <i>Bodyparts</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragonette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–13: Bodyparts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 21, 2012, Dragonette announced the title of their third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bodyparts_(album)" title="Bodyparts (album)">Bodyparts</a></i>, which was released on September 22, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The album's lead single "<a href="/wiki/Let_It_Go_(Dragonette_song)" title="Let It Go (Dragonette song)">Let It Go</a>" was released on April 2, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> "Rocket Ship", a track from the album, was made available as a free download on the band's official <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> page.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/Live_in_This_City" title="Live in This City">Live in This City</a>", was released on August 7, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2013, Dragonette recorded a version of "Won't You Be My Neighbour?", the theme song to the American children's television series <i><a href="/wiki/Mister_Rogers%27_Neighborhood" title="Mister Rogers' Neighborhood">Mister Rogers' Neighborhood</a></i>, for use in <a href="/wiki/Target_Canada" title="Target Canada">Target Canada</a>'s first ad campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Royal_Blues">2014–present: <i>Royal Blues</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dragonette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Royal Blues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, Dragonette collaborated with Dutch producer and DJ <a href="/wiki/Mike_Mago" title="Mike Mago">Mike Mago</a> on the single "<a href="/wiki/Outlines_(song)" title="Outlines (song)">Outlines</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The band also released the single "Let the Night Fall" on June 16, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The band's fourth studio album, <i>Royal Blues</i>, was released on November 11, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Royal_Blues_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Royal_Blues-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
