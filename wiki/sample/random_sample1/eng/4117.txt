<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Drest IV</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Drest</b> son of Girom was a king of the <a href="/wiki/Picts" title="Picts">Picts</a> from 522 to 531.</p>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Pictish_Chronicle" title="Pictish Chronicle">Pictish Chronicle</a></i> king lists associate him with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drest_III_of_the_Picts" title="Drest III of the Picts">Drest III</a>. Various reigns, separately and jointly, are assigned to the two Drests, varying from one to fifteen years. After the joint rule, this Drest appears alone in the lists with a reign of five or four years.</p>
<p>Drest is the first of three possible brothers, all called son of Girom, found in the king lists, the other being his successors <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gartnait_I_of_the_Picts" title="Gartnait I of the Picts">Gartnait I</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cailtram_of_the_Picts" title="Cailtram of the Picts">Caltram</a>.</p>
