<p><i><b>Drake &amp; Josh</b></i> is an American television <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Dan_Schneider_(TV_producer)" title="Dan Schneider (TV producer)">Dan Schneider</a> for <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>. The series follows the lives of two teenage boys with opposite personalities, Drake Parker (<a href="/wiki/Drake_Bell" title="Drake Bell">Drake Bell</a>) and Josh Nichols (<a href="/wiki/Josh_Peck" title="Josh Peck">Josh Peck</a>), who become stepbrothers. Both actors previously appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amanda_Show" title="The Amanda Show">The Amanda Show</a></i> along with <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Sullivan_(American_actress)" title="Nancy Sullivan (American actress)">Nancy Sullivan</a>, who plays Audrey, Drake and Megan's mother in the series. <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Cosgrove" title="Miranda Cosgrove">Miranda Cosgrove</a> plays Megan, Drake's mischievous younger sister, and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Goldstein_(actor)" title="Jonathan Goldstein (actor)">Jonathan Goldstein</a> plays Walter, Josh's father. The series' opening theme song, "<a href="/wiki/Found_a_Way" title="Found a Way">Found a Way</a>", is written by Drake Bell and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backhouse_Mike" title="Backhouse Mike">Backhouse Mike</a> and performed by Bell. The series ran from January 11, 2004, to September 16, 2007, totaling 57 episodes in 4 seasons. It also had two TV films: <i><a href="/wiki/Drake_%26_Josh_Go_Hollywood" title="Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood">Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Merry_Christmas,_Drake_%26_Josh" title="Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh">Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drake_%26_Josh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series is focused on a pair of stepbrothers who have opposite personalities. Drake Parker is a popular but immature aspiring musician idolized by his schoolmates and able to court a countless array of girls with ease, whereas Josh Nichols has trouble with dating and popularity. Drake and Josh live in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego, California</a>, with Josh's awkward, unfortunate, geeky father, a weatherman named Walter; Drake's loving mother Audrey; and manipulative and conniving younger sister Megan. The boys are often involved in comedic misadventures and obstacles while also handling teenage situations such as school, dating, and popularity. Over the course of the series, character changes of both Josh and Drake, and their relationship, develop: Josh loses weight and becomes more popular, while Drake matures and becomes more caring towards other people, especially his stepbrother, whom he eventually calls his "brother" instead of "stepbrother."</p>
<p>A notable running joke on the series is the presence of parodies of various well-known brands, TV shows, Films, or people. Such parodies include Mountain Fizz, a parody of <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Dew" title="Mountain Dew">Mountain Dew</a>, the GameSphere, a parody of the Nintendo <a href="/wiki/GameCube" title="GameCube">GameCube</a> and the Gary Coleman Grill, a parody of the <a href="/wiki/George_Foreman_Grill" title="George Foreman Grill">George Foreman Grill</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drake_%26_Josh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drake_.26_Josh_Go_Hollywood"><i>Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drake_%26_Josh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Drake_%26_Josh_Go_Hollywood" title="Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood">Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood</a></div>
<p>The boys are left at home in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a> after Audrey and Walter go on a ten-day cruise. Drake and Josh are told to take Megan to the airport so she can visit her friend in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver, Colorado</a>, but they accidentally send her on a flight to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. Drake and Josh go to Hollywood to get Megan back, end up stopping a multibillion-dollar heist, and booking a guest-spot for Drake on <a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live"><i>TRL</i></a>. The TV film aired on January 6, 2006; according to <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i>, it was the highest rated program on all of cable during its opening week. It currently holds a 78% on Rotten Tomatoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merry_Christmas.2C_Drake_.26_Josh"><i>Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drake_%26_Josh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Merry_Christmas,_Drake_%26_Josh" title="Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh">Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh</a></div>
<p>A new <i>Drake &amp; Josh</i> TV film tentatively entitled "Drake &amp; Josh: Best Christmas Ever" began production in July 2008, and premiered on December 5, 2008. The title was later changed to "<a href="/wiki/Merry_Christmas,_Drake_%26_Josh" title="Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh">Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh</a>". Creator <a href="/wiki/Dan_Schneider_(TV_producer)" title="Dan Schneider (TV producer)">Dan Schneider</a> returned as executive producer, with <a href="/wiki/Marjorie_Cohn" title="Marjorie Cohn">Marjorie Cohn</a> and Lauren Levine co-executive producing. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Grossman" title="Michael Grossman">Michael Grossman</a> directed the feature. The film centers around the boys who are working as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa" title="Santa">Santa</a>'s helpers in a mall when Drake promises a little girl that her foster family will have "The Best Christmas Ever"—and then try to fulfill that promise while a grumpy policeman tries to stop them. While the boys are not enjoying their time, Audrey and Walter are feeling worse on their vacation because they have to stay in a hut while in a hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The TV film was broadcast in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">HD</a> format, unlike the previous TV film <i><a href="/wiki/Drake_%26_Josh_Go_Hollywood" title="Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood">Drake &amp; Josh Go Hollywood</a></i>. It also featured <a href="/wiki/Henry_Winkler" title="Henry Winkler">Henry Winkler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">MMA</a> fighter <a href="/wiki/Kimbo_Slice" title="Kimbo Slice">Kimbo Slice</a>.</p>
<p>The premiere of <i>Merry Christmas, Drake &amp; Josh</i> broke the record of most viewers for the premiere of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_(TV_channel)" title="Nickelodeon (TV channel)">Nickelodeon</a> film with 9.10 million viewers, a title previously held by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ICarly:_iGo_to_Japan" title="ICarly: iGo to Japan">iGo to Japan</a></i>.</p>
<p>Despite the strong ratings, the film is the lowest-reviewed of the two <i>Drake &amp; Josh</i> films, currently holding a 70% on Rotten Tomatoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drake_%26_Josh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the series' run, it received generally positive reception from viewers and critics, holding a substantial 8.8/10 on TV.com, and a high 7.6/10 from reviews on <a href="/wiki/IMDb" title="IMDb">IMDb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> According to creator <a href="/wiki/Dan_Schneider_(TV_producer)" title="Dan Schneider (TV producer)">Dan Schneider</a>, <i>Drake &amp; Josh</i> had its highest ratings in its final season, and ended due to a collective decision from the creative forces behind the show. To this day, <i>Drake &amp; Josh</i> has widely been considered one of Nickelodeon's best live-action shows, if not one of the best Nickelodeon shows of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
