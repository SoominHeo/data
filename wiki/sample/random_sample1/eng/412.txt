<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
<div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Night_of_January_16th_jury_flyer_front.jpg" title="The flyer for the Broadway production"><img alt="The flyer for the Broadway production" data-file-height="1747" data-file-width="1154" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Night_of_January_16th_jury_flyer_front.jpg/100px-Night_of_January_16th_jury_flyer_front.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Night_of_January_16th_jury_flyer_front.jpg/150px-Night_of_January_16th_jury_flyer_front.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Night_of_January_16th_jury_flyer_front.jpg/200px-Night_of_January_16th_jury_flyer_front.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a></div>
<i><b><a href="/wiki/Night_of_January_16th" title="Night of January 16th">Night of January 16th</a></b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Play_(theatre)" title="Play (theatre)">play</a> by Russian-American author <a href="/wiki/Ayn_Rand" title="Ayn Rand">Ayn Rand</a>, inspired by the death of <a href="/wiki/Ivar_Kreuger" title="Ivar Kreuger">Ivar Kreuger</a>, an industrialist and accused swindler known as the Match King. The play is set in a courtroom during a murder trial, and members of the audience are chosen to play the jury. The court hears the case of Karen Andre, a former secretary and lover of businessman Bjorn Faulkner, of whose murder she is accused. The jury must rely on character testimony to decide whether Andre is guilty; the play's ending depends on their verdict. Rand wanted to dramatize a conflict between individualism and conformity. The play was first produced in 1934 in Los Angeles under the title <i>Woman on Trial</i>. Producer <a href="/wiki/Albert_H._Woods" title="Albert H. Woods">Al Woods</a> took it to <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> for the 1935–36 season and re-titled it <i>Night of January 16th</i>. It became a hit and ran for seven months. The play has been adapted as a <a href="/wiki/The_Night_of_January_16th_(film)" title="The Night of January 16th (film)">movie</a>, as well as for television and radio. Rand had many disputes with Woods over the play, and in 1968 re-edited it for publication as her "definitive" version. <li><a href="/wiki/1780" title="1780">1780</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Spanish_War_(1779%E2%80%9383)" title="Anglo-Spanish War (1779–83)">Anglo-Spanish War</a>: The British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> gained their first major naval victory over their European enemies in the war when they defeated a Spanish squadron in the <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cape_St._Vincent_(1780)" title="Battle of Cape St. Vincent (1780)">Battle of Cape St. Vincent</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a></b>, the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organisation" title="Intergovernmental organisation">intergovernmental organisation</a> whose principal mission was to maintain <a href="/wiki/World_peace" title="World peace">world peace</a>, held its first council meeting in Paris.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1942" title="1942">1942</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/TWA_Flight_3" title="TWA Flight 3">TWA Flight 3</a></b> crashed into <a href="/wiki/Potosi_Mountain_(Nevada)" title="Potosi Mountain (Nevada)">Potosi Mountain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, killing actress <a href="/wiki/Carole_Lombard" title="Carole Lombard">Carole Lombard</a> and all of the other 21 people on board.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1964" title="1964">1964</a> – The musical <i><b><a href="/wiki/Hello,_Dolly!_(musical)" title="Hello, Dolly! (musical)">Hello, Dolly!</a></b></i> opened at the <a href="/wiki/St._James_Theatre" title="St. James Theatre">St. James Theatre</a> on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a>, and went on to win ten <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Awards</a>, a record that stood for 37 years.</li>
</ul>
