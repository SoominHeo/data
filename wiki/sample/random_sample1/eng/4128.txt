<p><i><b>Dmitrii Donskoi</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Дмитрий Донской</span>) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armoured_cruiser" title="Armoured cruiser">armoured cruiser</a> built for the Imperial Russian Navy in the early 1880s. She was designed as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commerce_raider" title="Commerce raider">commerce raider</a> and equipped with a full suite of sails to economize on coal consumption. The ship spent the bulk of her career abroad, either in the <a href="/wiki/Far_East" title="Far East">Far East</a> or in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>. <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> was assigned to the Second Pacific Squadron after the Japanese destroyed the Russian ships deployed in the Far East during the early stages of the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a> of 1904–05. The squadron was intercepted by the Japanese fleet in May 1905 in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tsushima" title="Battle of Tsushima">Battle of Tsushima</a> after a lengthy voyage from the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic</a>. The cruiser was not seriously damaged during the initial fighting and tried to continue on to <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a> after the first day's fighting. She was spotted by several groups of Japanese warships the following day and was badly damaged in the resulting combat. Her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_(nautical)" title="Captain (nautical)">captain</a> ordered the crew ashore off <a href="/wiki/Ulleungdo" title="Ulleungdo">Ulleungdo</a> Island and had <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> <a href="/wiki/Scuttling" title="Scuttling">scuttled</a> offshore.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_description">Design and description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_cruiser_Dmitrii_Donskoi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design and description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> was classified as a semi-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armoured_frigate" title="Armoured frigate">armoured frigate</a> and was an improved version of her <a href="/wiki/Sister_ship" title="Sister ship">half-sister</a> <a href="/wiki/Russian_cruiser_Vladimir_Monomakh" title="Russian cruiser Vladimir Monomakh"><i>Vladimir Monomakh</i></a>. The ship was designed with long endurance and high speed to facilitate her role as a commerce raider. She was laid out as a <a href="/wiki/Central_battery_ship" title="Central battery ship">central battery ironclad</a> with her armament concentrated amidships. The iron-hulled ship was fitted with a <a href="/wiki/Naval_ram" title="Naval ram">ram</a> and was sheathed in wood and copper to reduce <a href="/wiki/Biofouling" title="Biofouling">fouling</a>. Her crew numbered approximately 550 officers and men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ship was 306 feet 5 inches (93.4 m) <a href="/wiki/Overall_length" title="Overall length">long overall</a>. She had a <a href="/wiki/Beam_(nautical)" title="Beam (nautical)">beam</a> of 58 feet 1 inch (17.7 m) and a <a href="/wiki/Draft_(hull)" title="Draft (hull)">draught</a> of 25 feet 7 inches (7.8 m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w7_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-w7-2">[2]</a></sup> The ship displaced 5,593 long tons (5,683 t) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deep_load" title="Deep load">deep load</a>. <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> had two three-cylinder <a href="/wiki/Marine_steam_engine#Compound" title="Marine steam engine">compound steam engines</a> driving a single propeller shaft. Steam was provided by eight cylindrical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boiler_(steam_generator)" title="Boiler (steam generator)">boilers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w68_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-w68-3">[3]</a></sup> The engines were designed to produce 7,000 <a href="/wiki/Horsepower#Indicated_horsepower" title="Horsepower">indicated horsepower</a> (5,200 kW), but produced 6,609 ihp (4,928 kW) during <a href="/wiki/Sea_trial" title="Sea trial">sea trials</a> which gave the ship a maximum speed around 16.5 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (30.6 km/h; 19.0 mph). The ship normally carried 900 long tons (910 t) of coal which gave her an economical range of 7,000 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (13,000 km; 8,100 mi) at a speed of 10 knots (19 km/h; 12 mph), but she is known to have loaded 1,625 long tons (1,651 t) in May 1889.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w44_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-w44-4">[4]</a></sup> She was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_rig" title="Ship rig">ship rigged</a> with three masts<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-s9_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-s9-5">[5]</a></sup> and had a total sail area of 26,000 square feet (2,400 m<sup>2</sup>). To reduce <a href="/wiki/Drag_(physics)" title="Drag (physics)">drag</a> while under sail, her <a href="/wiki/Funnel_(ship)" title="Funnel (ship)">funnels</a> were retractable. The highest speed that she made solely under sail was 10.5 knots (19.4 km/h; 12.1 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w44_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-w44-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> was armed with two 30-<a href="/wiki/Caliber_(artillery)" title="Caliber (artillery)">calibre</a> 8-inch (203 mm) Model 1877 guns, on <a href="/wiki/Sponson" title="Sponson">sponsons</a> on the upper deck between the funnels. The fourteen 28-calibre Model 1877 6-inch (152 mm) guns were mounted in casemates on the main deck. Anti-<a href="/wiki/Torpedo_boat" title="Torpedo boat">torpedo boat</a> defence was provided by a number of nine-pounder (42 mm (1.7 in)), four-pounder (34 mm (1.3 in)) and 37 mm (1.5 in) five-barreled revolver <a href="/wiki/Hotchkiss_gun" title="Hotchkiss gun">Hotchkiss guns</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[Note 1]</a></sup> The ship was also equipped with four above-water 15-inch (381 mm) <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_tube" title="Torpedo tube">torpedo tubes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w44_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-w44-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ck7_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ck7-8">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ship's <a href="/wiki/Waterline" title="Waterline">waterline</a> <a href="/wiki/Belt_armor" title="Belt armor">belt</a> was composed of <a href="/wiki/Compound_armour" title="Compound armour">compound armour</a> and extended the full length of the ship. It was six inches thick amidships, but reduced to 4.5 inches (114 mm) at the ship's ends. It extended 2 feet (0.6 m) above the waterline and 5 feet (1.5 m) below.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w7_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-w7-2">[2]</a></sup> Transverse bulkheads 3–4 inches (76–102 mm) thick protected the guns in the <a href="/wiki/Artillery_battery#Naval_usage" title="Artillery battery">battery</a> from <a href="/wiki/Raking_fire" title="Raking fire">raking fire</a>. The sponsons of the 8-inch guns were equally thick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w68_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-w68-3">[3]</a></sup> The protective <a href="/wiki/Deck_(ship)" title="Deck (ship)">deck</a> was 0.5-inch (13 mm) thick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w7_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-w7-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service">Service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_cruiser_Dmitrii_Donskoi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2364" data-file-width="3000" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg/220px-DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg/330px-DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg/440px-DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DmitriyDonskoy1893New-York.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> at the Columbian Exposition, 1893</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Construction began on <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> on 22 September 1880<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[Note 2]</a></sup> at the New <a href="/wiki/Admiralty_Shipyard" title="Admiralty Shipyard">Admiralty Shipyard</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg" title="St. Petersburg">St. Petersburg</a>, although the formal keel-laying ceremony was not held until 21 May 1881. She was launched on 30 August 1882 and completed in early 1885. The ship's total cost was 3,421,468 <a href="/wiki/Ruble" title="Ruble">rubles</a>. The ship was named after <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Donskoy" title="Dmitry Donskoy">Dmitry Donskoy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Moscow" title="Grand Duke of Moscow">Grand Duke of Moscow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>She sailed to the Mediterranean on 8 August 1885 and remained there until she arrived at Port Said on 6 March 1887 en route to the Far East. <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> reached <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>, Japan, on 19 May and remained in Japanese waters for several months. The ship arrived at Vladivostok on 20 July and accidentally grounded on 12 October whilst conducting torpedo practice. Only lightly damaged, she was refloated the following day. <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> wintered in Japan that year and made port visits to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chefoo" title="Chefoo">Chefoo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> in February 1888. She was refitted in Yokohama before she began her return to the Baltic on 20 January 1889. The ship was inspected by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Russia" title="Alexander III of Russia">Tsar Alexander III</a> after her arrival at <a href="/wiki/Kronstadt" title="Kronstadt">Kronstadt</a> on 12 June. She began a lengthy <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/overhaul" title="wikt:overhaul">overhaul</a> in preparation for her next foreign cruise shortly afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w9_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-w9-11">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> began her second foreign cruise on 3 October 1891 when she sailed for the Mediterranean, visiting <a href="/wiki/Brest,_France" title="Brest, France">Brest, France</a> en route. She was reclassified as a cruiser of the first rank on 13 February 1892 and remained in the Mediterranean for another month or so. The ship reached Vladivostok on 29 June, stopping at <a href="/wiki/Aden" title="Aden">Aden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> en route. <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> served as the <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rear_Admiral" title="Rear Admiral">Rear Admiral</a> Tirtov several times during the year. She spent the winter in Yokosuka and Nagasaki before she sailed in early 1893 to America for a goodwill visit to mark the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World%27s_Columbian_Exhibition" title="World's Columbian Exhibition">400 anniversary of America's discovery</a>. In Algiers in March, the ship picked up <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duke_Alexander_Mikhailovich_of_Russia" title="Grand Duke Alexander Mikhailovich of Russia">Grand Duke Alexander Mikhailovich of Russia</a> and became flagship of Rear Admiral Kaznakov who commanded all the Russian ships at the exhibition. <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> reached <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> on 25 April and participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presidential_Review" title="Presidential Review">Presidential Review</a> two days later. She made port visits to <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport, Rhode Island</a> before she arrived back at Kronstadt in early September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w9_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-w9-11">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the ship's lengthy 1893–95 refit, she was rearmed with six 45-calibre six-inch guns, ten 45-calibre 4.7-inch (120 mm) guns, and six 47-millimetre (1.9 in) guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-s9_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-s9-5">[5]</a></sup> Her boilers may have been replaced at this time and her sailing rig was replaced by three pole masts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w7_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-w7-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wilgelm_Vitgeft" title="Wilgelm Vitgeft">Wilgelm Vitgeft</a> was appointed as the ship's captain in late 1895 and <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> began her voyage to the Far East on 10 November. She was one of the Russian ships that occupied <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCshunkou_District" title="Lüshunkou District">Port Arthur</a> in March 1898 and participated in suppressing the <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a> in mid-1900. The ship was ordered home in late 1901.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[10]</a></sup> <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> was refitted again upon her arrival and six of her 4.7-inch guns were replaced by six 75-millimetre (3.0 in) guns and two additional 47 mm guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-s9_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-s9-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the completion of her refit, she escorted a group of seven destroyers and five torpedo boats to the Mediterranean in October 1903 where they were assigned to the Mediterranean Squadron under the command of Rear Admiral Virenius. The Naval Staff decided to reinforce the Pacific Squadron with the Mediterranean Squadron in December, but its departure was delayed by repairs to the <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleship</a> <a href="/wiki/Russian_battleship_Oslyabya" title="Russian battleship Oslyabya"><i>Oslyabya</i></a> after it had grounded. When the Russo-Japanese War began in February, the squadron was in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> and was recalled to the Baltic lest it be caught and destroyed en route by the Japanese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-w2-13">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> was assigned to the cruiser force of the Second Pacific Squadron and departed <a href="/wiki/Liep%C4%81ja" title="Liepāja">Libau</a> on 15 October 1904 bound for Vladivostok with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_1st_Rank" title="Captain 1st Rank">Captain 1st Rank</a> Lebedev in command. En route in the North Sea, she was damaged by friendly fire from seven sister ships in mistake for a Japanese vessel during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dogger_Bank_Incident" title="Dogger Bank Incident">Dogger Bank Incident</a> of 21/22 October. The ship passed the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> on 20 December. Whilst approaching the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strait_of_Tsushima" title="Strait of Tsushima">Strait of Tsushima</a> on 27 May 1905, the Russian force was intercepted by the Japanese in the Battle of Tsushima. The cruiser was assigned to defend the transport ships at the rear of the Russian formation and was not seriously engaged during the day. She became separated from the rest of the fleet during the early evening and attempted to steam north to <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a> through the Japanese fleet. <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> was unsuccessfully attacked by Japanese destroyers and torpedo boats during the night. The following morning, she helped to transfer the badly wounded squadron commander, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice_Admiral" title="Vice Admiral">Vice Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Zinovy_Rozhestvensky" title="Zinovy Rozhestvensky">Zinovy Rozhestvensky</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_destroyer_Buinyi" title="Russian destroyer Buinyi">Buinyi</a></i> to the destroyer <i>Biedovi</i> and then was forced to scuttle <i>Buinyi</i> when the destroyer's machinery broke down. The destroyer's crew as well as some 205 survivors from <i>Oslyabya</i> were transferred to the cruiser before <i>Buinyi</i> was scuttled. As the ship sailed northward, she was spotted late in the day by several groups of Japanese ships and badly damaged in the ensuing combat. Captain Lebedev decided to run his ship aground on <a href="/wiki/Ulleungdo" title="Ulleungdo">Ulleungdo</a>, but the ship anchored instead and all of the men aboard were taken to the island. Roughly 60 men of the ship's crew had been killed and another 120 wounded during the fighting. The next morning, 29 May, <i>Dmitrii Donskoi</i> was scuttled about a mile and a half (2.4 km) offshore, approximately at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Russian_cruiser_Dmitrii_Donskoi&amp;params=37_30_N_130_57_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">37°30′N</span> <span class="longitude">130°57′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">37.500°N 130.950°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">37.500; 130.950</span></span></span></a></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Russian_cruiser_Dmitrii_Donskoi&amp;params=37_30_N_130_57_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">37°30′N</span> <span class="longitude">130°57′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">37.500°N 130.950°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">37.500; 130.950</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>. The survivors were taken prisoner that afternoon by landing parties from the Japanese destroyer <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_destroyer_Fubuki_(1905)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Japanese destroyer Fubuki (1905) (page does not exist)">Fubuki</a></i> and the Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armed_merchant_cruiser" title="Armed merchant cruiser">armed merchant cruiser</a> <i>Kasugu Maru</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup></p>
