<p><b>Dmitry Vladimirovich Andreikin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Дмитрий Владимирович Андрейкин</span>, born 5 February 1990) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> chess <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_(chess)" title="Grandmaster (chess)">grandmaster</a>, world junior champion in 2010 and Russian champion in 2012.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chess_career">Chess career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dmitry_Andreikin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chess career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andreikin won the Under-10 division of the <a href="/wiki/World_Youth_Chess_Championship" title="World Youth Chess Championship">World Youth Chess Championships</a> in 1999.</p>
<p>He tied for 1st–3rd places with <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Chernyshov" title="Konstantin Chernyshov">Konstantin Chernyshov</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alexei_Kornev&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alexei Kornev (page does not exist)">Alexei Kornev</a> at <a href="/wiki/Lipetsk" title="Lipetsk">Lipetsk</a> 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2008, he won the 4th Inautomarket Open in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and tied for 3rd–7th with <a href="/wiki/Rauf_Mamedov" title="Rauf Mamedov">Rauf Mamedov</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Denis_Yevseev&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Denis Yevseev (page does not exist)">Denis Yevseev</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vasily_Yemelin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vasily Yemelin (page does not exist)">Vasily Yemelin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eltaj_Safarli" title="Eltaj Safarli">Eltaj Safarli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chigorin_Memorial" title="Chigorin Memorial">Chigorin Memorial</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2009, he tied for 1st–3rd with <a href="/wiki/Yuriy_Kuzubov" title="Yuriy Kuzubov">Yuriy Kuzubov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rauf_Mamedov" title="Rauf Mamedov">Rauf Mamedov</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chess_tournament#Tournament_categories" title="Chess tournament">category 16</a> SPICE Cup A tournament at <a href="/wiki/Lubbock,_Texas" title="Lubbock, Texas">Lubbock, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He won the 2010 <a href="/wiki/World_Junior_Chess_Championship" title="World Junior Chess Championship">World Junior Chess Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chotowa" title="Chotowa">Chotowa</a>, Poland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In the same year, he tied for 2nd–7th with <a href="/wiki/Alexey_Dreev" title="Alexey Dreev">Alexey Dreev</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Sokolov_(chess_player)" title="Ivan Sokolov (chess player)">Ivan Sokolov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Fedoseev" title="Vladimir Fedoseev">Vladimir Fedoseev</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Areshchenko" title="Alexander Areshchenko">Alexander Areshchenko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Sakaev" title="Konstantin Sakaev">Konstantin Sakaev</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chigorin_Memorial" title="Chigorin Memorial">Chigorin Memorial</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2011, he tied for 2nd–3rd with <a href="/wiki/Emil_Sutovsky" title="Emil Sutovsky">Emil Sutovsky</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a> Open.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In February 2012, tied for 4th–8th with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Khalifman" title="Alexander Khalifman">Alexander Khalifman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maxim_Rodshtein" title="Maxim Rodshtein">Maxim Rodshtein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabiano_Caruana" title="Fabiano Caruana">Fabiano Caruana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hrant_Melkumyan" title="Hrant Melkumyan">Hrant Melkumyan</a> in the 11th <a href="/wiki/Aeroflot_Open" title="Aeroflot Open">Aeroflot Open</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2012, Andreikin won the 65th <a href="/wiki/Russian_Chess_Championship" title="Russian Chess Championship">Russian Chess Championship</a> in Moscow after winning a <a href="/wiki/Fast_chess" title="Fast chess">rapidplay</a> playoff against five other players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Tal_Memorial" title="Tal Memorial">Tal Memorial</a> played in June 2013, Andreikin was the lowest rated player, but he went through the tournament undefeated with eight draws and a win against <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kramnik" title="Vladimir Kramnik">Vladimir Kramnik</a>, which gave him a shared third to fifth place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Chess_World_Cup_2013" title="Chess World Cup 2013">Chess World Cup 2013</a>, held in Norway from 11 August to 2 September, Andreikin finished in second place, losing to Kramnik in the four-game final match 1½–2½.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This result qualified him for the <a href="/wiki/World_Chess_Championship_2014#Candidates_Tournament" title="World Chess Championship 2014">2014 Candidates Tournament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October-November 2014 he scored a major success in the second leg of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIDE_Grand_Prix_2014-15" title="FIDE Grand Prix 2014-15">FIDE Grand Prix</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tashkent" title="Tashkent">Tashkent</a>, winning the tournament ahead of <a href="/wiki/Hikaru_Nakamura" title="Hikaru Nakamura">Hikaru Nakamura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shakhriyar_Mamedyarov" title="Shakhriyar Mamedyarov">Shakhriyar Mamedyarov</a>, Fabiano Caruana and eight other elite grandmasters. His score of 7/11 gave him a performance rating of 2852.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Andreikin won the Hasselbacken Open (on tiebreak from <a href="/wiki/Baskaran_Adhiban" title="Baskaran Adhiban">B. Adhiban</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> Chess Festival<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and the European <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blitz_Chess" title="Blitz Chess">Blitz Chess</a> Championship in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
