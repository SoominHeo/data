<p><b>Deng Zilong</b> (鄧子龍 in traditional Chinese, 1531–1598) was a military commander for the <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a> China. His <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> was "Wuqiao" (武橋) and his nickname was the "Tiger Crown Taoist" (虎冠道士). He was born in the Dengjia (Deng family) village of <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> province. He was most famously remembered for part taking in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_invasions_of_Korea" title="Japanese invasions of Korea">Imjin war</a>'s final showdown, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Noryang" title="Battle of Noryang">battle of Noryang</a>, where he was killed alongside Korean war hero <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-Sin" title="Yi Sun-Sin">Yi Sun-Sin</a>.</p>
<p>Deng first rose to prominence while subduing a rebellion within his home province during the reign of the <a href="/wiki/Jiajing_Emperor" title="Jiajing Emperor">Jiajing Emperor</a>, after that he was assigned to a command post in <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> province.</p>
<p>His most notable accomplishment in his military career was his efforts against the Burmese armies of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taungoo_dynasty" title="Taungoo dynasty">Taungoo dynasty</a> during the reign of the Burmese king <a href="/wiki/Nanda_Bayin" title="Nanda Bayin">Nanda Bayin</a>, who had repeatedly harassed the Southwestern borders of the Ming dynasty during the 1580s-90s. Although Deng's forces were often unruly, and gotten into incidents where they had brawls against other Ming forces and among Each other, he was relieved of his position in 1583, before being reinstated some 7 years after that, though he continued to run into troop discipline problems and was fired and instated several times during his career.</p>
<p>In 1598, he was called upon to join the Ming efforts in Korea, and was given the title of vice admiral of the Ming navy. He part took in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Noryang" title="Battle of Noryang">battle of Noryang</a> at an elderly age, but was killed in action alongside Korean Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-Sin" title="Yi Sun-Sin">Yi Sun-Sin</a>. The Korean court honored him greatly after the war, building temple in his names in Korea.</p>
