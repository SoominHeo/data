<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Deng Chuna</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Deng Chuna</b> (鄧處訥) (died 894), <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> <b>Chongyun</b> (沖韞), was a warlord late in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">Chinese</a> dynasty <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>, who controlled <a href="/wiki/Wu%27an_Circuit" title="Wu'an Circuit">Wu'an Circuit</a> (武安, headquartered in modern <a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>) as its military governor (<i><a href="/wiki/Jiedushi" title="Jiedushi">Jiedushi</a></i>) from 893 to 894, when he was defeated and killed by <a href="/wiki/Liu_Jianfeng" title="Liu Jianfeng">Liu Jianfeng</a>, who took over the circuit.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_service_under_Min_Xu">Background and service under <a href="/wiki/Min_Xu" title="Min Xu">Min Xu</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deng_Chuna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and service under Min Xu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is not known when Deng Chuna was born, but it is known that he was from Shao Prefecture (邵州, in modern <a href="/wiki/Shaoyang" title="Shaoyang">Shaoyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>). In his youth, he became a soldier under <a href="/wiki/Min_Xu" title="Min Xu">Min Xu</a>, an officer from Jiangxi Circuit (江西, headquartered in modern <a href="/wiki/Nanchang" title="Nanchang">Nanchang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>), on a deployment to Annan (安南, i.e., modern northern <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBT186_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NBT186-1">[1]</a></sup> In 881, when Min and his soldiers were returning from Annan, they went through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanzhou_(in_modern_Hunan)" title="Tanzhou (in modern Hunan)">Tan Prefecture</a> (in modern <a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>), and Min expelled the governor of Hunan Circuit (湖南, headquartered at Tan Prefecture) and took over the circuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Min thereafter claimed the title of acting governor and made Deng the prefect of Shao Prefecture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBT186_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NBT186-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Takeover_of_Wu.27an">Takeover of <a href="/wiki/Wu%27an_Circuit" title="Wu'an Circuit">Wu'an</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deng_Chuna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Takeover of Wu'an">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 886, when Tan Prefecture was under attack by <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Yue" title="Zhou Yue">Zhou Yue</a> the prefect of Heng Prefecture (衡州, in modern <a href="/wiki/Hengyang" title="Hengyang">Hengyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>), Min Xu welcomed one Huang Hao (黃皓), an officer under <a href="/wiki/Qin_Zongquan" title="Qin Zongquan">Qin Zongquan</a>, who had declared himself emperor of a new state centered around Cai Prefecture (蔡州, in modern <a href="/wiki/Zhumadian" title="Zhumadian">Zhumadian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>), to help him defend against Zhou's attack. Once inside the city walls of Tan Prefecture, however, Huang assassinated Min. Zhou subsequently defeated and killed Huang, and took over the circuit (which had been renamed <a href="/wiki/Qinhua_Circuit" title="Qinhua Circuit">Qinhua Circuit</a> by that point), eventually being commissioned as its military governor (<i><a href="/wiki/Jiedushi" title="Jiedushi">Jiedushi</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hearing of Min's death and blaming Zhou for it, Deng Chuna mourned Min bitterly, and stated to his officers:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ZZTJ259_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ZZTJ259-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I, along with you, gentlemen, have benefited from the great grace of the <i>Puye</i> [(僕射, i.e., Min)]. Zhou You killed him for no reason. I would like to, with you, gentlemen, expend our prefecture's strength to avenge the <i>Puye</i>. Can we not do it?</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The officers agreed with him. He trained his forces for seven years, and, in 893, in alliance with <a href="/wiki/Lei_Man" title="Lei Man">Lei Man</a> the prefect of Lang Prefecture (朗州, in modern <a href="/wiki/Changde" title="Changde">Changde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>), attacked Tan Prefecture. He captured it and executed Zhou, claiming for himself the title of acting military governor of <a href="/wiki/Wu%27an_Circuit" title="Wu'an Circuit">Wu'an Circuit</a> (as Qinhua had been renamed Wu'an by that point). In spring 894, then-reigning <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Zhaozong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Zhaozong of Tang">Emperor Zhaozong</a> commissioned him full military governor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ZZTJ259_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ZZTJ259-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Defeat_and_Death">Defeat and Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deng_Chuna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Defeat and Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>However, later that year, an army commanded by <a href="/wiki/Liu_Jianfeng" title="Liu Jianfeng">Liu Jianfeng</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ma_Yin" title="Ma Yin">Ma Yin</a> approached Wu'an. Deng sent his officers Jiang Xun (蔣勛) and Deng Jichong (鄧繼崇) with the Shao Prefecture troops to resist the attack at Longhui Pass (龍回關, in modern Shaoyang). Ma persuaded Jiang and Deng Jichong that Liu's success was prophesied and irresistible, and they disbanded their army. Ma took over the uniforms they wore and headed directly for Tan Prefecture. The Tan Prefecture defense forces, believing that these were soldiers from Shao Prefecture, took no precautions. Ma marched directly into the city and the circuit headquarters, capturing Deng Chuna, who was holding a feast, and executed him, allowing Liu to take over the circuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ZZTJ259_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-ZZTJ259-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
